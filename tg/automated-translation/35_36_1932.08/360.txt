Article Title: 매리를 생각하며 (Thinking of Mary)
Author: 李石山
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Thinking of Mary
						I Sŏk-san

This dirty street was merely
beautiful
because Mary was there.

This lonely village was only
joyful
because Mary was there.

Beneath the full moon of May,
like the breast of Venus,
when the white flowers of the acacia
dreamed of a distant land,
Oh! the profound smile
Mary cast then,
I embraced it dearly like a treasure
and aged through the fleeting years.

But Mary and I,
like the parallel lines of an orbit,
are two fates never to converge.
Ah! the endless sorrow
lies in the difference between blonde hair and black hair.

I burn away the endless sorrow in tobacco smoke,
tracing the dream-footprints of Mary’s
distant smile,
I quietly, quietly wander
through the acacia’s shade.<92>

**Summary**

The poem expresses the speaker's deep affection and longing for a woman named Mary, whose presence once brought beauty and joy to an otherwise dreary environment. He cherishes the memory of her smile under a May moon but laments the insurmountable distance between them, comparing their lives to parallel lines that can never meet. The speaker explicitly attributes this eternal separation and "endless sorrow" to the difference between "blonde hair and black hair." From a historical perspective, the poem is interesting for its direct reference to physical (likely racial or cultural) differences as the source of impossible love during the colonial period. It reflects potential interactions, desires, and perceived barriers between Koreans and Westerners (or those perceived as different) at the time.

매리를 생각하며
						李石山
이 더러운 거리는 다못
매리가 잇는 까닭으로
아름다웟다.

이 쓸쓸한 마을은 오직
매리가 잇으므로 해서
즐거웟섯다.

비너쓰의 유방 같은
5월의 보름딸 아래
아카씨아의 힌꽃이
먼 나라의 꿈을 그릴 때
오! 그때 던저준
매리의 그윽한 미소를
나는 보배처럼 고히 안꼬
덧없이 세월을 늙엇다.

그러나 매리와 나는
궤도의 평행선 같이
영원히 합치되지 않는 두 운명
아! 끝없는 비애는
금발과 흑발의 차이에 잇다.

나는 끝없는 비애를 담배에 태우며
멀고먼 매리의
미소의 꿈자최를 더듬어
가만가만히 아카씨아의 그늘을
헤맨다.＜92＞
