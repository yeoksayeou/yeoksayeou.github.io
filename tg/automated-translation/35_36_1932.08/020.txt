Donggwang Mental Test
▲ 1 point per question. 8 points is Excellent, 5 points is Passing, below 5 points is Failing.
▲ Submissions welcome.
(Answers are on page 82)

1.  What day is the Japan-Korea Annexation commemoration day?

2.  What are the names of the athletes of Korean origin participating in this year's Olympic Games?

3.  Where are the respective provincial capitals of South Kyŏngsang Province, South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province, and North Hamgyŏng Province?

4.  What is the translated term for moratorium?

5.  What goods are monopolized by the Monopoly Bureau of Korea (조선의 專賣局)?

6.  What is the approximate total issue amount of recent Bank of Chosŏn notes (朝鮮銀行券)?

7.  What are the four major cities of Manchuria?

8.  Where are the locations of iron mines (鐵鑛) in Korea?

9.  What are two laws concerning Korea's special political criminals (特殊의 政治犯)?

10. What is the formal national name of Russia (露國)?

**Summary**

This article presents a ten-question general knowledge quiz titled "Donggwang Mental Test" from the August 1, 1932 issue of the magazine *Donggwang*. The quiz tests readers on topics including colonial administration (annexation date, provincial capitals, monopoly goods, colonial bank notes), international affairs (Olympics, Manchuria, Russia), and political control (laws regarding political criminals). It reflects the information deemed relevant or important for educated Koreans during the Japanese colonial period. Historians might find the quiz interesting as it reveals the expected knowledge base of the readership, blending awareness of colonial structures (Annexation Day, Monopoly Bureau, Bank of Chosŏn notes, political crime laws) with contemporary international events (1932 Olympics, Manchuria, the formal name of the USSR) and basic geography/economics (provincial capitals, iron mines). The inclusion of questions about the annexation date and laws targeting political dissidents starkly highlights the colonial context.
