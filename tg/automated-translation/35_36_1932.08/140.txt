Magazine Title: 동광 제36호
Publication Date: 1932년 08월 01일
Article Title: 第6官, 紳士들의 體育熱 (The Sixth Sense, Gentlemen's Enthusiasm for Physical Training)
Article Type: 소식 (News/Dispatch)

Whether influenced by the spread of enthusiasm for physical training throughout all of Chosŏn, an eagerness for physical activity has arisen among the gentlemen of Seoul, and one can see respected elders with beards carrying athletic wear. At the Chosŏn Yŏnmugwan (朝鮮演武館) in Susong-dong (壽松洞), under the guidance of instructor Mr. Yi Kyŏngsŏk (李景錫), a training group has been organized and has held its first and second training sessions. Participants include Dr. Yi Kŭngno (李克魯) and Mr. Kim Sŏn'gi (金善琪) of the Korean Language Dictionary Compilation Society (朝鮮語辭典編纂會), along with Mr. Yi Kwan'gu (李寬求) and Dr. Kim Hyŏnjun (金賢準) of Posŏng College (普專), Professor Ok Sŏnjin (玉선珍), storyteller Mr. Kim Chin'gu (金振九), chief editor of the *Tongbang P'yŏngnon* (東方評論) Mr. Paek Kwansu (白寬洙), physician Dr. Yi Kapsu (李甲秀), Doctor of German Philosophy An Hosang (安浩相), and others.

Furthermore, at the special class of the Central Physical Training Research Institute (中央體育硏究所), besides the aforementioned Mr. Paek Kwansu and Dr. Yi Kapsu, Professor Yi Yongsŏl (李容卨) of Severance Medical College (世專), as well as Messrs. Yi Yŏsŏng (李如星), Hong Ch'ang'yu (洪昶裕), Yi Chŏngnae (李晶來), Yi Il (李一), Yi Hŏnyŏng (李軒永) (of Hwimun - 徽文), and various other gentlemen are said to be learning skills they couldn't learn in their youth under the guidance of Mr. Sŏ Sangch'ŏn (徐相天).<30>

**Summary**

This news article from August 1932 reports on a growing trend among prominent gentlemen and intellectuals in Seoul taking up physical training. It highlights two specific groups: one training in martial arts at the Chosŏn Yŏnmugwan under Yi Kyŏngsŏk, and another special class at the Central Physical Training Research Institute led by Sŏ Sangch'ŏn. The article lists numerous notable figures involved, including academics, doctors, editors, and writers, suggesting a burgeoning interest in physical fitness and perhaps nationalistic physical culture among the Korean elite during the colonial period. For historians, this piece is interesting as it documents the participation of specific, well-known Korean intellectuals and professionals (like Yi Kŭngno, Paek Kwansu, An Hosang) in organized physical activities during the 1930s. It reflects a broader social trend and potentially hints at undertones of national consciousness-building through physical culture, a common phenomenon in colonized nations seeking self-strengthening. The specific names, institutions, and the framing of "learning skills they couldn't learn in their youth" offer valuable social history data.

第6官, 紳士들의 體育熱
전조선으로 체육열이 퍼저나가는 영향을 받음인지 서울에는 신사들 중에 체육열이 발흥하야 수염을 버친 나리들이 운동복을 끼고 단니는 것을 볼 수 잇다. 壽松洞에 잇는 朝鮮演武館에는 사범 李景錫씨의 지도 하에 수련단이 조직되어 1회, 2회의 수련을 햇는데 거기 참가한 사람들은 朝鮮語辭典編纂會의 李克魯 박사, 金善琪 군을 위시하야 普專의 李寬求씨, 金賢準 박사, 玉선珍 교수 등이며 野談家 金振九씨, 東方評論 主幹 白寬洙씨, 의사 李甲秀씨, 獨逸哲學博士 安浩相씨 등등이라고.
그리고 中央體育硏究所 특별반에는 前記 白寬洙, 李甲秀씨 외에 世專의 李容卨 교수며 李如星, 洪昶裕, 李晶來, 李一, 李軒永(徽文) 기타 諸氏가 徐相天씨의 지도 하에 어려서 못배운 재주들을 배우고 잇다 한다.＜30＞
