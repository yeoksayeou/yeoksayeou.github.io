Wandering People
						Yi Kyu-wŏn

Wandering People
You must have seen those people on the road.
People staggering, their pale faces lowered,
Wearing bamboo hats, their bodies clad in colorless clothes,
Moving their feet with sighs, heading aimlessly somewhere.

He is Israel's eternal Wandering People (漂泊民).
Like a son of a bygone century searching for history buried in the grave.
From parents without beginning to descendants without end,
He is a pitiful traveler doomed to wander, weeping and weeping.

Sleeping in the forest without a tent,<105>
Eating blades of grass and moistening his throat with spring water,
Following the desolate sound of the wind shaking tree branches,
He collapses powerlessly, sleeps, and awakens.

At times, unknown tears well up in his eyes,
He raises his fists and endlessly pounds the empty air.
Then, with a trembling, tragic voice, he cries out,
"Oh, warriors of my beloved fatherland, where have you gone?"

Ah, upon the newly risen cities of the next day,
Under which nation's flag will you cover your body, and in what national tongue (邦언) will you sing?
Look at the intensely falling light of the setting sun.
The eternal shadow will appear even after this day has passed. (1932.5) <106>
<105-106>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Kyu-wŏn depicts the sorrowful plight of "Wandering People," drawing a parallel with the displaced people of Israel. It describes their aimless journey, impoverished state, deep sorrow, and desperate yearning for their lost fatherland and its "warriors." The poem concludes with a sense of despair about the future, questioning their identity and place under a new order symbolized by the setting sun and an "eternal shadow." From a historical perspective, the poem is significant as an allegorical representation of the Korean people's suffering and loss of sovereignty under Japanese colonial rule. The imagery of displacement, the lament for the "fatherland's warriors," and the uncertainty about future national identity (flag, language) clearly reflect the anxieties and suppressed nationalism of Koreans during the 1930s.

漂泊民
						李揆元
漂迫民
당신은 길 우에서 그 사람을 보앗으리다.
파리한 얼골 숙이고 비틀거리는 사람들을
머리에는 방갓을 쓰고 물색 없는 옷으로 몸을 걸친 후
한숨소리에 발을 옴기며 어디로인지 정처없이 가든

그는 이스라엘의 영원한 漂迫民.
묻엄 속에 든 역사를 찾는 지나간 세기의 아들 같습니다.
시작 없는 어버이 때로부터 끝없는 자손에까지
울면서 울면서 헤맬 가엾슨 여객이외다.

천막도 없이 숲속에서 잠자며＜105＞
풀닢을 뜯어먹고 샘물로 목을 적시나니
나무가지 흔드는 정막한 바람소리 따라
힘없이 쓰러지며 잠자다가 깨여납니다.

때때로 그의 눈엔 알지 못할 눈물이 맞히자
주먹을 들어 허공을 한없이 두두립니다.
그러다가 참혹한 소리 떨면서 부르짖는 말
내 사랑하는 조국의 용사여 너는 어디로 가버렷느냐.

아하 다음날 새로 생긴 도시 우에서
어느 국기를 네몸에 가리며 무슨 邦언으로 노래 할 터인가
저 강하게 떠러지는 석양의 빛을 보라.
영원한 그림자는 이날이 간 뒤에도 나타나겟구나.(1932.5) ＜106＞
