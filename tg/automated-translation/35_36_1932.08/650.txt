Science Fragments, Lindbergh and the Airplane

It is some airfield. An airplane descends and lands.

"Look over there! It's Lindbergh."

People look that way. The airplane's motor has not stopped and continues to run. Lindbergh, with his goggles pushed up onto his forehead, just sits there.

"A normal pilot would stop the motor as soon as they land and go where they need to go. But Lindbergh alone does not do that; he lets the motor run for 15 minutes to cool it down slowly. Everything Lindbergh does is like that. Because he does things that way, he succeeded in crossing the Atlantic. Because he does that, even though he is in the air like that every day, he has never once crashed." <57>

**Summary**

This short piece recounts an anecdote illustrating Charles Lindbergh's meticulous nature. It describes how, unlike other pilots, Lindbergh would let his airplane engine run for 15 minutes after landing to cool it down gradually. The narrator attributes Lindbergh's success in crossing the Atlantic and his perfect safety record to this careful approach. For historians, the article offers a glimpse into the Korean public's fascination with Western technological figures like Lindbergh during the colonial period, highlighting admired traits such as precision and careful methodology, possibly reflecting aspirations for modernity or a commentary on perceived local shortcomings.

科學斷片, 린드벅과 비행기
어떤 비행장이다. 비행기 한 대가 나려와 앉는다.
『저것 보시요. 린드벅이옵니다』
사람들이 그 편을 본다. 비행긔의 모터는 멎지를 아니하고 그냥 돌아가고 잇다. 린드벅은 안경을 이마에 부치고 그냥 앉아 잇다.
『보통 비행사면 착륙하자 곳 모터를 멈추고 저 갈데를 가지요. 그러나 린드벅만은 그러치 아니하오, 모터를 15분 동안 돌려서 천천히 식히는 것이오. 린드벅의 하는 일이 다 그럿습니다. 그러케 하기 따문에 대서양 횡단도 성공햇지요. 그러기 따문에 저러케 매일 공중에 잇어도 한번도 떠러저 본 일이 없지요』 ＜57＞
