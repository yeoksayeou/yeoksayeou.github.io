Magazine Title: 東光 제36호
Publication Date: 1932년 08월 01일
Article Title: (掌編) 조그만 叛逆者 (Short Story: The Little Rebel)
Author: 李無影
Article Type: 소설 (Novel/Fiction)

Mr. B, a veteran of the Chosŏn social movement and current chairman of the Central Executive Committee of the XX Alliance, scratched his graying hair as he blankly watched his twin sons playing catch. Due to his weakened eyesight from over ten imprisonments, which had consumed half his life in jail, Mr. B found it difficult to distinguish the older son from the younger one; the two boys resembled each other that much. Even their school teacher couldn't tell the older from the younger, the younger son would grumble whenever he came home.

"Aigoo, that darn temper. What's so upsetting about looking like your brother that you have to whine like that?"
When Mrs. B, also an officer of the S Women's Friendship Society, appeared, the younger son would throw a tantrum out of spite.
"Hyŏng is hyŏng, and I am me! I'm not the least bit happy to hear I look like my brother!"

The younger son never once wore his clothes properly. Although there was some opposition from the elders in the family, dressing the twins exactly alike made them look even cuter. It was convenient just to buy everything the same, from hats to shoes. Besides their appearance, it was also a considerable help to Mr. and Mrs. B, who couldn't always be tied down at home.
However, the younger son seemed displeased with this.
It was the day before the school picnic to Uidong.
Mrs. B managed to scrape together some money she didn't have and came home with two water bottles the boys had been pestering her for daily. Mr. B's heart was filled with immense joy. The great happiness of raising these two boys well to follow in his footsteps always filled his chest. <93>
"Hey kids! I bought water bottles!"
Mr. B shouted as he entered the gate, staggering slightly with one bottle slung over each shoulder.
Needless to say, the two children rushed out. They dashed like tigers, snatched one bottle each, and began bragging to each other. Mr. and Mrs. B sat watching the two children happily.

After fussing for a while about whose bottle was better, the younger son's lips suddenly pursed tightly. His lips moved, muttering something to himself as he turned away, then he said,
"Father! I'm not taking this!"
And he threw the water bottle down in front of Mr. and Mrs. B. Mrs. B, who had been holding back her temper for some time, began to flare up.
"You little rascal, throwing down what your father bought you!"
"Leave him be. If he doesn't want it, he doesn't want it. This hat is the same, the clothes are the same, the shoes, the pencil case too... I... I don't need any of that stuff! Even if it's worse than hyŏng's, if it's not different, I won't take it! And I'm not wearing these clothes either!"

The younger son whined and threw a tantrum, then abruptly stood up, stripped off his trousers and jacket, went to the warmest part of the ondol floor (아래묵), and sat huddled up. Even when Mrs. B stood over him with a switch, he sat stubbornly without batting an eye.
"Mother is free to beat me to death, but even if I die, I will not wear or eat the exact same things as hyŏng!"
Ignoring Mr. B's attempts to stop her, Mrs. B couldn't make the younger son surrender, even by treating him harshly like catching a rat.

The next morning, the younger son still wouldn't bow his head. He picked up old, rather dirty, sweat-smelling clothes (these clothes were different in color from his brother's), put them on crookedly, and went out. As the Bolshevik Mr. B watched the departing younger son blankly, a certain stirring emotion filled his chest. And so, Mr. B turned to his wife and instructed her, almost like an order:
"From now on, I will buy the things for the younger one. You take care of the older one, and I'll buy things for the younger one. Wouldn't that work?" (End) <94>

**Summary**

This short story by Yi Muyŏng depicts a family scene involving Mr. B, a prominent socialist activist frequently imprisoned, and his identical twin sons. The younger twin fiercely rebels against being treated identically to his brother, rejecting matching clothes and possessions, demanding his own distinct identity. Despite his mother's anger, the boy remains defiant. Observing this, Mr. B, described as a "Bolshevik," feels a sympathetic emotion and decides that he and his wife will buy different items for each son henceforth. The story highlights the theme of individuality clashing with conformity, possibly reflecting broader societal or political tensions in colonial Korea. For historians, the portrayal of a socialist figure ('Bolshevik') sympathetic to individual rebellion within his own family, the mention of frequent imprisonment for political activities, and the depiction of everyday family dynamics (like school picnics and buying matching clothes for twins) against the backdrop of the social movement provide valuable insights into the personal lives and perhaps evolving ideologies of activists during this period.

조선 사회운동의 원로은 현재 XX동맹 중왕집행위원장인 B씨는 히끗히끗해지는 머리털을 긁어 올리면서 쌍동이인 두 아들이 「캣취뽈」 하는 것을 멀끔히 바라다보고 잇엇다. 전후 십여 회의 입옥(入獄)으로 반생을 옥에서 보내인 B씨의 퇴쇠된 안력(眼力)으로는 큰 놈과 작은놈을 분간키 어려울 만큼, 두 아들은 서로서로 닮엇섯다. 날마다 대하는 학교 선생도 큰 놈 작은 놈을 분간치 못한다고 작은 놈은 집에만 오면 쫑알거렷다.

「아이구, 배라먹을 놈에 성미두. 형과 아우가 닮엇다는 게 뭬, 그리 분해서 앙알대니?」
하고 역시 S녀성, 동우회 역원의 한사람인 B씨 부인이 나타설나치면 작은 놈은 기가나서 포악을 하는 것이엇다.
「형은 형이구 나는 나지! 형 닮엇다는 소리가 내겐 조금도 반갑잔어!」

작은 놈은 옷을 입을 때도 한번 탈속하니 입은 적이 없엇다. 집안 어른들의 반대도 잇엇지만은 쌍동이니 만큼 옷을 꼭 같이 입혀 놓고 보면 더 한층 귀여웟다. 모자로부터 신발까지 꼭 같은 것만 사면 그만이다. 외양 뿐더러 항상 가정에 대어달려 잇지 못한 B씨 부처에게는 적지 않은 큰 도음도 되엇든 것이다.
그러나 그것이 작은 놈에게는 못마땅한 모양이엇다.
그것은 학교에서 우이동으로 원족을 가든 전날이엇다.
R씨는 없는 돈을 억지로 만들어 가지고 날마다 졸리우든 물병을 두 개 사 가지고 들어왓다. B씨의 마음은 더없이 즐거웟다. 이 두 놈을 잘 길러서 자기의 뒤를 잇도록 하겟다는 큰 기쁨이 항상 그의 가슴에 채워젓섯다.＜93＞
「예들아. 빨병 사왓다.」
B는 한쪽 억개에 한 개씩 걸고서는 꺼뜩꺼뜩 대문 안을 들어서며 소리를 질럿다.
두 아이가 몰켜나온 것은 말할 것도 없다. 비호같이 달려와서는 한 앞에 한 개식 채가고 서로 자랑을 하는 것이엇다. B씨 부처는 행복스럽게 두 아이를 바라보고 앉엇섯다.

한동안 네게 좋으니 내게 좋으니 하고 떠들더니 갑작이 작은 놈의 입이 빽 톨아젓다. 입을 쫑굿쫑굿 하고 뭬라고 인지 혼자 돌아 앉아서 쫑알쫑알 하더니
「아버지! 난 이것 안가질테유!」
