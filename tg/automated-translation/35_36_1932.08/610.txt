The Sixth Sense, Kim Tong-in's Life on Manuscript Fees

It is a well-known fact that there is no one in the Chosŏn literary world who makes a living solely by writing. Whether as reporters, clerks, or secretaries, the effort to earn a living comes first, inevitably resulting in a situation where not much writing can be done. Nevertheless, it seems there are a few among them who do live solely by writing.

Ch'unwŏn used to make a living through novels, but these days he also works as a newspaperman. Conversely, Mr. Yŏm Sang-sŏp, who used to make a living as the head of the Arts and Culture department at the *Chosŏn Ilbo*, resigned from that position and went through great hardship, but now makes a living by writing a serialized novel for the *Maeil Sinbo*.

Other than them, the record for purely living off manuscript fees decidedly goes to Mr. Kim Tong-in (金東仁). Having squandered his father's inheritance in his twenties, he is now penniless. However, recently rousing himself, he came up to Seoul with his children and beloved wife, and now supports his family by writing for various newspapers and magazines and receiving fees for his manuscripts. In doing so, his hardships are surely beyond the imagination of the esteemed readers. <51>

**Summary**

This article discusses the financial difficulties faced by writers in colonial Korea, noting that few could survive solely on writing income. It contrasts Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwang-su), who supplemented his novel writing with journalism, and Yŏm Sang-sŏp, who turned to serializing novels for the *Maeil Sinbo* after leaving the *Chosŏn Ilbo*. The main focus is on Kim Tong-in, portrayed as the prime example of a writer living purely on manuscript fees after exhausting his inheritance, highlighting the precarious economic situation for authors. For historians, the article offers insight into the economic realities of intellectuals and cultural producers under colonialism, the specific career paths of prominent literary figures, and the role of newspapers like the *Chosŏn Ilbo* and the Japanese-run *Maeil Sinbo* in providing income streams.

第6官, 金東仁의 原稿生活
조선문단에서 文만으로 벌어 먹고 사는 사람이 없는 것은 누구나 다 아는 사실이다. 혹은 기자 혹은 점원 혹은 書記, 먹기 위한 노력이 앞서기 따문에 결국 글도 많이 못쓰는 형편이지마는 그래도 그중에 글만 가지고 먹는 사람이 멫은 잇는 모양이다.
春園은 소설로 생활을 하더니 요새는 新聞人을 겸하게 되엇고 그 반대로 朝鮮日報 학예부장으로 먹고 지나든 廉想涉 군은 그 자리를 내놓은 후 매우 곤란히 자나다가 지금은 每日新報에 장편소설을 쓰고 살아간다.
그 외에는 순전히 원고료로만 살아가는 레코드는 단연 金東仁 군으로 가게 되엇다. 그는 부친의 유산을 20대에 다 버르저 버리고 인제 와서는 無一分의 신세가 되엇는데 최근에 분발하야 그 자녀와 애처를 더리고 상경한 후 각 신문잡지에 글을 쓰고 그 고료로 일가를 유지해 간다. 그러노라니 그 고심은 독자 諸賢의 상상 밖일 것은 물론이다. ＜51＞
