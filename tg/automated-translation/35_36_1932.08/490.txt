Rural View, Post-Rain Scenery
						Song Sun-il
The clear forest after the rain flutters in the fragrant breeze (薰風),
Every leaf a jewel, every branch a birdsong,
The occasionally crying cuckoo (뻑국), what affection does its lament (알욈) know?
Ascending the mountain peak, my head bows low. <102>

Fertile fields stretching a thousand *li* (옥야천리), the green land swells with life and water,
Undulating green waves encircle the wheat and barley fields,
Every grain crop (전곡) dances merrily in the sweet rain,
The bright daylight (백일) ripens, dappled (아롱지여) with golden hues.

Acacia's white flower (힌꼿) bunches hang heavy and abundant,
Swarms of bees (버리떼) gather on sun-drenched branches,
Calling to each other, only the labor song buzzes... "Woong-woong-woong..."
Ah! They are noble (거륵한) people who can live
just by working.

Movement across the whole plain, all are laborers (노역군),
Weeding (김매기), plowing paddies (논가리), this and that...
Young ladies coming and going (들낙날낙) in the fresh mulberry fields (뽕밭새론),
Even the ants on the roadside seek workplaces, east and west,
The white clouds stretched across the vast sky, where do they flow? Whoosh, whoosh, whoosh.
In this green garden where great nature and human life move (움즉이는) hand in hand,
Let the rhythm of creation, the melody of construction resound.
Towards an eternal future with no exploitation (착취) and no domination (지배).

1932.5.31 <103>

**Summary**

The poem by Song Sun-il depicts an idyllic rural landscape after rainfall. It celebrates the beauty of nature revitalized – clear forests, jeweled leaves, singing birds, fertile fields, blooming acacias – and portrays various forms of labor (bees, farmers, young women, ants) as natural, noble, and harmonious parts of this vibrant scene. The poem concludes with a hopeful vision for an "eternal future" free from exploitation and domination, guided by the "rhythm of creation" and "melody of construction." From a historical perspective, the poem is interesting for its idealized portrayal of rural labor and its concluding utopian vision, which subtly echoes socialist ideals ("no exploitation and no domination"). Written during the Japanese colonial period, this positive depiction of Korean rural life and the longing for an autonomous, just future offers a counter-narrative to the harsh realities of colonial exploitation, potentially reflecting nationalist sentiments or socialist aspirations prevalent among intellectuals at the time.

田園眺望, 雨後風景
						宋順鎰
비 개인 맑은 수풀 薰風에 휘날려
잎새마다 구슬이요 가지마다 새소린데
간간이 우는 뻑국 무슨 정을 알욈인지
산상에 오르나니 머리 숙여지도다.＜102＞
옥야천리 푸른 대지 살이 올라 물이 올라
굼실굼실 푸른 물결 밀보리밭 에여 돌고
단비에 너울너울 전곡마다 춤추는데
백일이 무르녹아 황금빛 아롱지여.

아까시아 힌꼿다발 주렁주렁 소담한데
볓쬐는 가지마다 모여드는 버리떼
너도나도 부르나니 노동가만 웅웅웅...
아아! 그들은 일만 하면
살 수 잇는 거륵한 백성이어라.

왼 벌판에 움직임이 모도가 노역군
김매기 논가리 무엇무엇...
싱싱한 뽕밭새론 아가씨들 들낙날낙
길바닥에 개미들도 일터를 찾아 동으로 서으로
창공에 비낀 힌구름도 어데로 흐르는지 훨훨훨.
대자연과 인생이 손잡고 움즉이는 푸른 동산에
창조의 리듬 건설의 멜로디 울려지라.
착취도 없고 지배도 없는 영원한 미래로.

1932.5.31 ＜103＞
＜102-103＞
