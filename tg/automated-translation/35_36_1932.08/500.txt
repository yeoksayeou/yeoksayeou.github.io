One Crossing the Genkai Sea (玄海灘)
						Kim Pong-je (金鳳濟)

Whenever the rough wind, like the sea's lament,
Rattles the glass doors of the ship's reading room, <103>
Amidst the crowd in white clothes (白衣), carrying bundles and gourds, the young man,
As if shaking from a terrifying premonition, avoiding the murderous gaze of the inspector,
Follows the distant horizon with its dark blue line,
Watching the boundless Rough Sea (荒海), where distant ships flicker and port lights sway on the waves.
Cross this sea, leave this land!
A heart determined, strong as iron.
Yet, alas, how the heart crumbles.

Like a splendid altar consoling a soul departing a corpse,
The port's navigation flags of five colors flutter in the wind,
Awaiting the departure time, the massive ship, eyeing the anxious passengers,
Rolls and sways with the playful slap of the waves.
Its vents and funnel roar, wing wing wing! in the wind,
Signaling nine o'clock, the departure time, with a fearsome accompaniment.
Truly, the dreadful chill and gloom only found in a port (浦口)!
Amidst the miserable atmosphere, the sea's roar (wind)! The rough wind! Wrapping his stained collar tighter,
Ah, cross this sea, leave this land!
A heart determined, strong as iron.
Yet, alas, how the heart grows hot.

The young man, in a corner of the third-class cabin,
Strokes the ship's floor, bound with iron nails.
This young ship! This sad hearse (관선)! that returned hundreds of thousands (멫십만) of young men as corpses!
He sees the wretched cargo, brought back as half-mad wrecks from spirited youths.
How many times has he struck the unresisting steel plate with his fist, tearing at his tear-soaked chest?
The loud departure whistle brands his heart deeply. <104>

Ah, departure! Departure!
(The whistle echoed loudly, resonating in the passengers' hearts.)
"Port of Pusan (釜山港), distance your form from me. Avoid letting your sorrowful sounds reach my ears."
In the ship cutting through waves like a galloping herd of white horses,
The young man clutches the steel plate, tearing at his chest.
Cross this sea! Leave this land!
A heart determined, strong as iron!
Yet, alas, ah, how the heart crumbles.

(In the ship crossing the Genkai Sea (玄海灘)) <105>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Pong-je depicts the intense emotional conflict of a young Korean man leaving his homeland by ship, crossing the Genkai Sea (Hyeonhaet'an) from Pusan during the Japanese colonial period. He steels himself with determination ("Cross this sea, leave this land! / A heart determined, strong as iron") but is repeatedly overwhelmed by sorrow and despair ("Yet, alas, how the heart crumbles"). The imagery portrays the ship not just as transport but as a "sad hearse" carrying away the potential of Korean youth, reflecting the grim realities and forced migration under colonial rule. For historians, the poem offers a poignant personal perspective on the psychological toll of colonial displacement, the sense of loss associated with leaving Korea (symbolized by Pusan), the oppressive atmosphere (inspector's gaze), and the critique of the system that turned vibrant youths into "corpses" or "half-mad wrecks." The specific mention of third-class travel and the sheer numbers alluded to ("hundreds of thousands") highlights the scale and socioeconomic dimension of this migration.
