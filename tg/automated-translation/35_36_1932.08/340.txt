From the Boat
						Kim Soyŏp

In the clearly clearing May sky, there are no clouds,
The warm wind only flutters the flag at the boat's bow,
Now, in the quiet day, our small boat,
Bathed entirely in the warm May light,
Is gliding silently over the oil-like spring waves.
Far away, Shanghai (上海) can be seen.
A corner of Huangpu Bund Road (黃浦灘路), rising tall above the water, is visible.
Aboard that giant warship, dozing on the water with its anchor lowered,
Not even the shadows of sailors can be seen.
They must be dreaming now of leisurely midday pleasures
In the arms of pretty women (게집).

Now our boat sounds its steam whistle (汽笛), toot!
Those tall buildings soaring high into the mid-air draw steadily closer.
Now we must again cast our lonely shadows
Onto the Huangpu Bund Wharf (黃浦灘 埠頭), a part of that bustling city.
The battlefield where they grab and tear at each other, bleed, cry, and bark! <89>
We must again step onto the asphalt of the city, the battleground of life.
Oh, grassy hills of Pudong (浦東)! Beautiful canvas of the countryside!
That let us rest upon your cozy bosom!
Compared to that place, the city confronting us before our eyes,
Ah, how much gentler, how much lovelier you are.

1931.5.2. Returning from Pudong (浦東), aboard the D Maru (D丸) <90>

**Summary**

The poem contrasts the tranquility of a boat journey approaching Shanghai with the anticipated chaos and struggle of the city itself. The speaker observes the calm scenery, a distant warship, and reflects on the impending return to the "battlefield" of urban life. The peaceful memory of Pudong is held up as a desirable, gentle alternative to the harsh city. Historically, the poem captures the sensory experience and psychological tension of navigating modern urban spaces like Shanghai in the 1930s, a major treaty port marked by international presence (warship) and intense commercial/social activity. The contrast between the pastoral escape (Pudong) and the city reflects a common modernist theme, potentially heightened by the colonial context where cities could represent both opportunity and alienation.

배우에서
						金沼葉
맑게 개인 5월의 하늘엔 구름도 없고
따뜻한 바람은 오직 배ㅅ머리의 기빨을 날릴 뿐,
지금은 고요한 낮 우리의 탄 적은 배는
왼몸에 따뜻한 5월의 빛을 받으며
기름 같은 봄물결 우로 고요히 미그러저 가고 잇다.
저 멀리 上海가 보인다
물 우에 웃둑 소슨 黃浦灘路의 一角이 보인다.
닷을 나린채 물 우에 졸고 잇는 저 거대한 군함 우엔
水兵들의 그림자 조차 볼 수가 없다
그들은 지금 어여뿐 게집의 품속에서
느긋한 한낮의 환락을 꿈꾸고 잇겟지

지금 우리의 배는 뚜汽笛을 논다
반공에 높이 솟은 저 高樓가 점점 앞으로 닥어온다
이제 우리는 다시 저 번잡한 도시의 한쪽 黃浦灘 埠頭에
쓸쓸한 그림자를 던저야겟다.
서로 주여 뜯고 피흘리며 울르고 짓는 싸움의 터!＜89＞
삶의 戰地인 도시의 아스팔트를 다시 밟어야겟다.
포근한 가슴 우에 우리를 쉬여주던
이 浦東의 풀언덕아! 아름다운 전원의 화폭아!
눈앞에 버치고서 잇는 저 도시의 그곳보다는
아 너는 그 얼마나 부드러우냐 사랑스러우냐.
