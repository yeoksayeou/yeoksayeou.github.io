Sŏhae as a Novelist
						Kim Tongin

Writer Sŏhae, Ch'oe Haksong (Sŏk Yŏnghwa), who died young at thirty.

He is the third one.
Namgung Pyŏk, Na Pin, Ch'oe Haksong – this is the third friend of mine who made literature his profession whom I have lost.

Sŏhae was a unique presence in the Chosŏn literary world.
Before Sŏhae, the writers in our fiction scene were all from the intelligentsia. Although their environments, regions, and financial statuses might have differed, they were all writers who came from student backgrounds, carefully nurtured. As such, the society they depicted was the society they saw, contacted, and experienced day and night. The incidents they portrayed were likewise.
Sŏhae's upbringing itself was different from previous writers. He was someone who experienced countless hardships and bitterness from a young age in a poor environment. He was someone who had gone through all sorts of experiences – as a monk, a wanderer, an opium addict, a laborer, a pitiful existence facing death due to starvation.
Consequently, the society he depicted was entirely different from the society portrayed by Chosŏn fiction writers until then. The society he depicted was a dark society. It was a society where hunger, illness, pain, sin, cruelty,<97> and fear were all mixed together.
In the Chosŏn reading circles, which until then had only seen the lives of the propertied class in novels, the reason they cast eyes of wonder upon Sŏhae and welcomed him with raised hands was because they were captivated by the novelty of the society within his novels. It was also for this reason that the proletarian movement (푸로예맹) welcomed Sŏhae as a leader figure.

However, Sŏhae was merely a novelist. He was not a strictly defined proletarian artist. Because the society he had witnessed and experienced so far was the miserable lowest society, and he hadn't seen other societies, he naturally wrote about such a society, but portraying only that society was not his goal.
As Sŏhae secured an indispensable, valuable place in the Chosŏn fiction scene, his life gradually stabilized, leading him to experience a social life different from the society he had seen before. Simultaneously, his dual personality life unfolded. The dark past and the relatively stable present – sitting here, struggling with his inherent dual personality, waiting for a resolution, for the last 3 or 4 years, he could not write. To take material for novels from the dark experiences of the past, the relatively stable life of the present seemed to interfere, causing a lack of realistic power. To base his novel's material on the stable life of the present, the darkness of the past was too deeply ingrained. Because of this dual personality, he had stopped his pen and was waiting for an opportunity.

His was a refined brush. His was sharp observation. His was orderly composition. His was flawless skill.
I was waiting. I waited for the novels that Sŏhae, possessing such skill, would write after fully assimilating into a different environment than before, after reaching a resolution where he could look down upon both societies.
But then, he passed away without getting the "time" to write them.

In the Chosŏn fiction scene, where there are only novelists from student backgrounds, and the "dark society" they later witness is merely a society seen through the lens of "erudition," thus still insufficient for thoroughly depicting the life of the lowest class, losing Sŏhae, the only one who came from the lowest stratum of life, is an incomparably great loss.
Even without this, in Chosŏn where writers are already scarce, why did Sŏhae have to die so early?
(July 11th) <98>
<97-98>

**Summary**

Kim Tongin laments the early death of his friend, the writer Ch'oe Sŏhae (Ch'oe Haksong), at age 30. He emphasizes Sŏhae's unique position in the Korean literary world as someone who emerged not from the educated elite like other writers, but from extreme poverty and hardship. This background allowed Sŏhae to depict the "dark society" of the lowest class with unparalleled authenticity, captivating readers and attracting the attention of the proletarian literary movement. However, Kim argues Sŏhae was fundamentally a novelist, not just a proletarian artist. He suggests Sŏhae's recent inability to write stemmed from a "dual personality"—a conflict between his dark past and his recently stabilized present life—and expresses deep regret that Sŏhae died before resolving this conflict and producing works encompassing both perspectives. The loss of Sŏhae, the sole voice authentically representing the lowest class, is deemed a huge blow to the Chosŏn literary scene. For historians, the article offers insight into the class dynamics within the colonial Korean literary field, the perceived limitations of intelligentsia writers attempting to portray lower-class life, the internal struggles faced by writers like Sŏhae navigating changing personal circumstances, and Kim Tongin's own perspective on realism and the role of personal experience in literature, distinguishing Sŏhae's innate talent from ideological alignment.

小說家로서의 曙海
						金東仁
삼십세로 요절한 창작가 서해 최학송(석영화)

세 사람 째다.
南宮壁, 羅彬, 崔鶴松, 나의 친한 벗 가운데 文으로 업을 삼는 사람을 세 사람째 잃엇다.

曙海는 조선문단의 특이한 존재엿다.
曙海 이전의 우리 小說壇의 작가들은 모도 인테리 출신이엇다. 그 환경이며 지방이며 재산에는 차이가 잇엇겟지만 모도 고히 고히 길어난 학생 출신의 작가들이엇다. 그런지라 그들의 그리는 사회도 그들이 밤낮을 보고 접촉하고 경험한 사회엇다. 그들이 그리는 사건도 역시 그러하엿다.
曙海는 그 생장부터가 재래의 작가와 달럿다. 빈곤한 환경 아레서 어려서부터 무수한 고생과 쓰라림을 겪은 사람이잇다. 중으로, 방랑객으로, 아편쟁이로, 인부로, 기아 때문에 죽음에 직면한 가련한 존재로 별별 경력을 다 지난 사람이엇다.
따라서 그의 그리는 사회가, 아직것 조선 소설작가들이 그리는 사회와 온전히 달럿다. 그의 그리는 사회는 암흑한 사회엿다. 굶주림과 병과 아픔과 죄악과 잔혹함과＜97＞ 공포가 막 섞인 사회엿다.
아직것 온전히 유산층의 생활만 소설에서 보든 조선 독서계에서 曙海에게 경이의 눈을 던지고, 손을 들어서 맞은 것은 이 소설 안의 사회의 新奇에 고혹되기 때문이엇다. 푸로예맹에서 曙海를 대장 격으로 맞어들인 것도 그 때문이엇다.

그러나 曙海는 일개의 소설작가엿다. 그는 국한된 푸로예술가가 아니엇다. 아직것 견문한 사회가 참담한 최저 사회이고 다른 사회를 보지 못하엿으므로 자연히 그런 사회만 그러치 그 사회만 그리는 것이 그의 목표가 아니엇다.
曙海가 朝鮮 小說壇에 뽑지 못할 귀한 자리를 잡으면서 그의 생활도 차차 안정됨을 따라서 아직것 본 사회와 다른 사회생활을 하게 되엇다. 동시에 그의 이원적 인격적 생활이 전개된 것이엇다. 암담한 과거와 비교적 생활이 안정된 현재, 여기 앉어서 그 내재한 이원적 인격과 다투면서 낙착되기를 기다리는 최근 3, 4년, 그는 집필을 못하엿다. 과거의 암담한 경험으로 소설의 材를 취하자니 현재의 비교적 안정된 생활이 방해를 하여 박진력이 결핍할 듯 하엿다. 현재의 안정된 생활을 긔초로 소설의 취재를 하자니 과거의 암담한 것이 너무도 깊이 백여 잇엇다. 이 이원적 인격 때문에 그는 붓을 중지하고 기회를 기다리고 잇든 것이엇다.

세련된 붓이엇다. 날카로운 관찰이엇다. 정돈된 구상이엇다. 티 없는 솜씨엇다.
나는 기다리고 잇엇다. 이만한 솜씨를 가진 曙海가 이전과 다른 환경에 충분이 융화가 된 뒤에 두 사회를 눈 아레 굽어볼 만한 낙착을 얻은 뒤에 써내는 소설을 나는 기다렷다.
그랫더니, 그것을 쓸 「때」를 얻지 못하고 세상을 떠낫다.

학생 출신의 소설작가만 잇고 그들이 그 뒤 견문한 「암담한 사회」라야 「학식」이라는 렌즈를 통해서 본 사회에 지나지 못하겟으므로, 역시 철저한 최저게급의 생활을 그리기에는 부족한 조선의 小說壇에 잇어서 최저생활자 출신의 유일인이든 曙海를 잃는다는 것은 무엇에 비기지 못할 큰 손실이다.
그러치 않어도 작가가 부족한 조선에서 曙海는 웨 그다지도 일즉이 죽엇나.
(7월 11일) ＜98＞
