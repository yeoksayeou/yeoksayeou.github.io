Haedong Soksohak
						Pak Chaehyŏng, Original Author

When Ch'oe Hyŏng (崔瑩) was sixteen years old, his father Ong (雍) admonished him, saying, "You should regard gold as mere stone." Having heard this once, he never forgot it and upheld it throughout his life. Consequently, his clothing and food were extremely frugal, and his dwelling was exceedingly simple. He looked upon those who wrapped themselves in silk clothes and galloped around on plump horses as less than dogs or pigs. Furthermore, even though he reached the position of general and prime minister and held the nation's military authority (兵權) for a long time, he never sided with the powerful nor accepted wealth from others, and he firmly refused even the reward of private farmland (民田) offered by the state.

U T'ak (禹倬), who held the official post of Cheju, was thoroughly versed in the scriptures and teachings of the sages (聖經賢傳), and was particularly proficient in the Book of Changes (周易). At this time, Chengzi's (程子) Commentary (傳) first arrived, but <68> no one in our country knew it. The scholar shut his door firmly and diligently studied it alone until he understood it, then taught it to his students, whereupon the study of moral principles (義理之學) began to flourish for the first time. (From Koryŏsa [麗史])

Hoehŏn Sŏnsaeng (晦軒 先生) An Yu (安裕), greatly concerned upon seeing the schools decline day by day, proposed to the government that various officials contribute money and rice designated as funds for supporting students (贍學錢) to be used for educational expenses. Furthermore, with the remaining funds, he arranged for envoys to be sent to China to acquire portraits of Confucius (孔子) and his seventy disciples, along with ritual vessels (祭器), musical instruments (樂器), the Six Classics (六經), and the histories of various philosophers (諸子史). (From Kunp'yo Ilballok [羣豹一斑錄])

The above concerns Establishing Education (入敎). <69>

**Summary**

This excerpt from "Haedong Soksohak" presents anecdotes about three historical Korean figures—Ch'oe Hyŏng, U T'ak, and An Yu—as exemplars of moral conduct and dedication to learning and education during the Koryŏ dynasty. Ch'oe Hyŏng exemplifies frugality and incorruptibility, U T'ak demonstrates scholarly diligence in introducing Neo-Confucian thought (Chengzi's commentary) to Korea, and An Yu shows proactive leadership in revitalizing education by securing funding and resources, including materials from China. For historians studying the colonial period, this article is interesting as it reflects the ongoing effort to instill traditional Confucian values and historical pride in Koreans through education, even under Japanese rule. The emphasis on Korean historical figures and their virtues, particularly related to scholarship and integrity, can be seen as a subtle form of cultural preservation and national consciousness-building during a time of external political control. The use of older Korean orthography is also linguistically significant.

해동속소학
						朴在馨 原著
최형(崔瑩)이 나이 열여섯살ㅅ 적에 그 아버지 옹(雍)께서 훈게의 말씀으로 「네가 맞당히 금을 돌과 같이 녀기라」하였음니다. 그이는 한 번 들은 말이언마는 도모지 잊지 아니하고 일평생 그대로 지키었음니다. 그리하여 의복과 음식에는 급직이 검소하고 거처도 초솔하기 짝이 없었음니다. 비단옷을 몸에 감고 살진 말을 타고 훨훨 날치고 다니는 자들을 보면 개와 도야지만도 못하게 알았음니다. 그뿐아니라 몸이 장수와 정승의 지위에 이르며 오래ㅅ동안 일국의 병권(兵權)을 잡고 있었건마는 권세편에 붙이거나 남에게 재물을 받거나 하는 일은 조금도 없었으며 나라에서 민전(民田)을 상으로 주는 것까지도 굳이 사양하여 받지 아니하였음니다.
제주(벼슬이름) 우탁(禹倬)이 성경현전에 무불통달이요. 더욱 주역(周易)에 능통하였음니다. 이때에 정자(程子)의 전(傳)이 처음으로 들어오았는데 우리나라에서는＜68＞ 아무도 이를 알(아)는 이가 없었음니다. 선생이 문을 꼭 처닫고 홀로 *여나 연구하여 알아내어서 생도들에게 가르치매 의리지학(義理之學)이 처음으로 행하게 되었음니다. (麗史)
회헌 선생(晦軒 先生) 안유(安裕)가 학교가 날로 쇠퇴하여감을 보고 크게 근심하여 정부에 건의하여 여러 관리에게 섬학전(贍學錢)이라 명칭하는 돈과 벼를 내어 교육비에 충용하게 하며 또 그 쓰고 남는 돈으로 중국에 보내어 공자와 및 칠십 제자의 화상이며 아울어 제긔(祭器) 악긔(樂器), 육경(六經), 제자사(諸子史)등을 구하여 오았음니다. (羣豹一斑錄)
이상은 입교(入敎)를 말함니라.＜69＞
