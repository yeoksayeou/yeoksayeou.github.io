Admiral Yi Sunsin, an Elder to Respect and Emulate

Who among the people of Chosŏn would not know the name Yi Sunsin (李舜臣)? His valor and meritorious deeds provide much for our youth to deeply respect and truly emulate.

His Birthday Was the Eighth Day of the Third Lunar Month
The Admiral was born 383 years ago, in the year Kiwŏn 3878, which was the first year of King Injong's (仁祖大王) reign, on the eighth day of the third month (this is, of course, the lunar calendar; calculated by the solar calendar, it would be early April), in Kŏnch'ŏndong (乾川洞), Han'yang (漢陽).

A Hero Even From Childhood
When he was young, playing mischief with his friends, they would use wooden sticks as bows, spears, and swords, mimicking soldiers fighting on the battlefield. He himself would act as the general, leading and commanding the other children, which was extraordinary. Is this not where we can discern the special foundation of a great hero to come?

Throwing Down the Brush and Taking Up the Sword
From his grandfather's time, the Admiral's family esteemed literature, and the Admiral himself sat reading books until he was over twenty. Then, having some sort of realization, he threw down his ink brush and resolved to learn martial arts (武藝).

How Great Was His Valor?
When he was twenty-eight years old, while testing his horsemanship at the Hullyŏnwŏn (訓練院), he unfortunately fell from his horse, breaking his left leg and losing consciousness for a time. The many people who witnessed this spectacle were alarmed, shouting, "Aigo! Master Yi (李公) is dead!" But the Admiral abruptly stood up, broke off a willow branch,<70> peeled its bark, wrapped his injured leg with it, jumped back onto the horse, and rode on. The entire crowd of onlookers cheered loudly. Shouldn't the cowards (怯夫) of today, who groan and act as if they'll die from just a tiny thorn under their fingernail, reflect upon the actions of this elder?

Strength Enough to Overwhelm Dozens of Strong Men
Once, when he went to Sŏnsan to visit his ancestors' graves, a stone warrior statue erected before the tomb had fallen over. Dozens of laborers tried to lift it but could not budge it even slightly. The Admiral scolded them, sent them away, and still wearing his formal *topo* coat, effortlessly lifted it, carried it on his back, and set it back in its original place. Everyone present wagged their tongues and marveled.

Infinitely Diligent in Study As Well
As the Admiral grew older, his setting of purpose and cultivation of character (性格) differed from ordinary people. Furthermore, his patriotism (愛國心) was fervent, and he resolved to devote his body to the nation. Thus, although he grew up in a city like Seoul, he hardly ever went outside, determinedly shutting his door to stay inside, immersing himself entirely in reading military strategy books and practicing weaponry.

Passing the Military Examination at Twenty-Nine
At the age of twenty-nine, he finally passed the military examination (武科). Initially, he served as a mere military officer in a minor border town, but later rose to the prestigious position of Susa T'ongjesa (水師統制使), commanding the entire nation's navy, eventually establishing an immortal reputation worthy of special mention (特筆) in the naval history (海軍史上) of both East and West.

Everything Was Orderly
Early on, while serving as a *Pongsa* (奉事) at the Hullyŏnwŏn, Kim Kwiyŏng (金貴榮), who was the Minister of War (兵曹判書), sought to make him a friend. The Admiral declined, saying, "As I am just starting my official career, how can I already set foot in the gates of power (權門)?"

While serving as Palp'o Manho (鉢浦萬戶), the Chwasusa (左水使) Sŏng Pak (成鎛) sent a servant to cut down a paulownia tree in the front yard of the official guesthouse to make himself a *kŏmungo* (zither). The Admiral stated, "Although this may be a small thing, it is not personal property but belongs to the government office (官家); private use (私事) is absolutely forbidden," and despite it being an order from his superior, he ultimately did not obey.

When Yu Yŏn (柳連), who held the rank of State Councilor, requested a fine *chŏnt'ong* (箭筒, quiver), the Admiral replied, "Giving it is not difficult, but if others hear of it, what will they say about Your Excellency receiving it and this humble servant giving it?" and did not grant the request.

Master Yi I (李珥) asked Yu Sŏngnyong (柳成龍) to arrange a meeting. The Admiral said, "Since we share the same surname (同姓), I cannot refuse to meet, but now is the time for personnel appointments (銓衡), so I cannot see him."

Such was the manner in which he maintained his upright and impartial integrity (節操).

Truly a Peerless Hero, A Great Accomplishment
During the Imjin Waeran (壬辰倭亂), as Sugun T'ongjesa, he was greatly active in the southern seas, sinking hundreds of enemy ships (敵艦) and striking fear into the hearts of the enemy. He also invented the Turtle Ship, becoming the progenitor of the world's submarines. This ever-victorious Admiral Yi Sunsin eventually met his end, struck by an enemy bullet, achieving a glorious death and leaving behind a beautiful name that will not fade through eternity.

There is still much to write about Admiral Yi Sunsin. We will leave the stories of his sacred achievements and the Turtle Ship for another opportunity and conclude here for now.<71>

**Summary**

This 1927 article from the magazine *Tonggwang* presents a biographical sketch of Admiral Yi Sunsin, emphasizing his heroic qualities from childhood, his valor, strength, integrity, patriotism, and eventual rise to become the Naval Commander-in-Chief during the Imjin War. It highlights specific anecdotes illustrating his character, such as his resilience after falling from a horse, his immense strength in resetting a grave statue, and his refusal to bend rules for powerful figures. The article concludes by mentioning his invention of the Turtle Ship and his heroic death in battle. From a colonial history perspective, the article is significant for its unabashed praise of a Korean national hero famed for defeating Japanese forces. Published during Japanese rule, such a narrative celebrating Yi Sunsin's martial prowess against Japan and his unwavering patriotism served as a powerful, albeit potentially coded, assertion of Korean national identity and pride, likely resonating deeply with a Korean readership under occupation. The factual error regarding King Injong (stating 仁祖/Injo instead of 仁宗/Injong or 明宗/Myeongjong) is also interesting, perhaps indicating the limitations of historical resources or editing at the time.

높이고 모범할 이순신 어른
이순신(李舜臣)이라 하면 조선사람 되어서 모를 이가 누가 있겠음니까. 그 날램이라던지 그 훈공이라던지 우리 소년들의 가장 공경하며 참으로 모범할 일이 많은 어른임니다.
음녁 삼월 팔일이 탄일 어른은 삼백팔십삼년전 긔원 3878년 곳 인조대왕(仁祖大王) 원년 삼월 초 여들에ㅅ날 (이는 물론 음녁이니 양녁으로 치면 사월 초생일ㅅ것)에 한양(漢陽) 건천동(乾川洞)에 나시었음니다.
영웅은 어리어서 붙어 어리었을 적에 여러 동무들과 같이 작난하며 놀때에 나무막대기를 가지고 활이라 창이라 칼이라 하면서 군사들이 전장에서 사움하는 흉내를 내는데 자긔는 스스로 대장이 되어 여러 아이들을 거느리고 지휘 호령하는 것이 범상하지 아니하였음니다. 장내에 큰 영웅될 바탕은 특별히 여긔에서 알아 볼 것이 아님니까.
붓을 던지고 칼을 잡아 어른은 그 조부ㅅ적붙어 문학을 숭상하였으며 어른께서도 나이 이십여세가 되도록 책 앉아서 글 읽기만 하시다가 이에 무슨 깨달음이 있었던지 붓대를 내어던지고 무예(武藝)를 익히기로 결심하였음니다.
그의 날램이 어떠한가 나이 스물여들살ㅅ 적에 훈련원(訓練院)에 가아서 말 타기를 시험하시다가 불행히 낙마를 하여 왼편 다리가 절골되어 한참은 혼도하여 있었음니다. 이 관경을 본 여러 사람들이 놀라아 「액구 이공(李公)이 죽었다.」고 하며 야단들을 합니다. 어른께서 벌떡 일어나아서 버들가지를＜70＞ 꺾어 그 겁질을 배끼어서 그 상한 다리를 싸아매고 돌우 말게 뛰어올아 달리니 만장 관중이 크게 갈채하였음니다. 지금 세상에 손톱밑에 조고만한 가시만 하나만 들어도 꿍꿍 앓으며 죽을 것같이 알(아)는 겁부(怯夫)는 이 어른의 일을 한번 생각지 않는가.
힘은 장골 수십을 접어 한 번은 션산에 성묘하러 가시었더니 묻엄 앞에 세우어둔 장군돌이 넘어지었는데 역부 수십명이 들어 일으키려 하여도 조곰도 요동하지 아니하였음니다. 어른께서 그 돌을 다 꾸짖어 물리치시고 도포 입은 채로 그것을 덜렁 들어다 등에 지고 본디 있던 자리에 갓다 세우어 두니 여럿이 모도 혜를 내두르며 탄복하였음니다.
공부에도 무한히 열심 어른은 나이 점점 많아감을 딸아 뜻을 세우며 성격(性格)을 수양함이 보통 사람과는 달았음니다. 더욱이 애국심(愛國心)이 열렬하여 나라에 몸 받히기로 결심하였음니다. 그리하여 서울 같은 도시에 생장하였지마는 도모지 밖끗 출입이라고는 없고 꼭 문을 처닫고 들어 앉아서 병법을 읽으며 무긔를 연습하기에 전심하였음니다.
이십구세에 무과급제 나이 스물아홉에 이르어 비롯오 무과(武科)에 올아 처음에는 일개 미미한 변방 고을 군관을 지내었으며 내종에는 전국 해군을 통솔하는 수사통제사(水師統制使)란 영귀한 직함을 가지게 되어 동서양 해군사상(海軍史上)에 특필(特筆)할 불후(不朽)의 공명을 세우기까지에 이르었음니다.
모든것이 규측적이다. 일쯕 훈련원 봉사(奉事)벼슬로 게실 때에 병조판서(兵曹判書)로 있는 김귀영(金貴榮)이 사우 삼으려고 하매 어른께서 말하기를 「내가 지금 첫 벼슬길에 어찌 발서붙어 권문(權門)에 발ㅅ길을 둘까」하고 아주 거절하였음니다.
발포만호(鉢浦萬戶)로 게실 때에는 좌수사(左水使)로 있는 성전(成鎛)이 자긔의 검은고 만들려고 객사 앞뜰에 있는 오동나무를 하인 보내어 비어가려 함니다. 어른께서 「이것이 비록 적은 것일망정 개인의 것이 아니요 관가의 물건인즉 사사의 소용은 절때 불가하다.」하고 그 상관의 명녕이건마는 종시 듯지 아니하였음니다.
정승으로 있는 유련(柳連)이 좋은 전통(簡簡)하나를 청구하매 어른께서 「들이기는 어렵지 않음니다마는 남이 들으면 공의 받은 것과 소인이 들이는 것을 무어라 할 것임니까」 하고 허락하지 아니하였음니다.
율공선생 이이(李珥)께서 유성뇽(柳成龍)을 시키어 한번 보자 하였음니다. 어른께서 「서로 동성(同姓)인 터에 아니 볼 수는 없음니다마는 지금이 벼슬을 전형(銓衡)하는 때니 볼 수 없음니다.」하였음니다.
그 강직하고 공평한 절조(節操)를 지키어감이 대개 이러하였음니다.
과연 절세영웅 대사업 임진왜란에 수군통제사로 남쪽 해상에서 크게 활동하여 적함(敵艦) 수백척을 격침하여 적의 간담을 놀래게 하였으며 또 거북선을 발명하여 세게 잠수정의 원조가 되시었음니다. 이 백전백승하던 우리 이순신 어른은 마침내 적군의 탄환에 맞아서 만고에 갈리지 아닌할 꽃답은 이름을 남기고 영화롭은 죽음을 일우었음니다.
이순신 어른에 대하여 아직도 쓸것이 많음니다. 그 거룩한 공이며 거북션에 관한 이야기에 대하여는 다음날 따로 말할 긔회를 두고 이에는 이만으로써 그침니다.＜71＞
