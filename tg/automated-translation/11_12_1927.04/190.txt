How Much Rice Do We Produce?

Last year's actual rice yield for All Korea (全 朝鮮) was reported as 15,297,442 *sŏk*, which is said to be an increase of 524,340 *sŏk* compared to the year before last. Dividing this by province:

1. South Chŏlla Province (全羅南道): 2,298,746 *sŏk*

2. North Kyŏngsang Province (慶尙北道): 2,213,175 *sŏk*

3. South Kyŏngsang Province (慶尙南道): 2,055,980 *sŏk*

4. North Chŏlla Province (全羅北道): 1,764,054 *sŏk*

5. Kyŏnggi Province (京畿道): 1,595,871 *sŏk*

6. South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠淸南道): 1,452,309 *sŏk*

7. Hwanghae Province (黃海道): 843,607 *sŏk*

8. Kangwŏn Province (江原道): 726,809 *sŏk*

9. North Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠淸北道): 612,924 *sŏk*

10. North P'yŏngan Province (平安北道): 612,649 *sŏk*

11. South P'yŏngan Province (平安南道): 605,315 *sŏk*

12. South Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡南道): 443,167 *sŏk*

13. North Hamgyŏng Province (咸鏡北道): 112,876 *sŏk*

Total: 15,297,442 <67>

**Summary**

This short article reports Korea's total rice production for the previous year (likely 1926, given the 1927 publication date) as nearly 15.3 million *sŏk*, noting an increase of over 524,000 *sŏk* from the prior year. It then provides a detailed breakdown of the rice yield by each of Korea's thirteen provinces, listing them in descending order of production. For historians, this piece offers precise agricultural statistics from the colonial period, highlighting regional production capacities and disparities (e.g., the high yields in southern provinces compared to northern ones). It reflects the colonial administration's focus on tracking and increasing agricultural output, particularly rice, which was a key commodity.
