Magazine Title: 동광 제28호
Publication Date: 1931년 12월 01일
Article Title: 山上曲 (Song on the Mountain)
Author: 요한
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

On the peak soaring between the city and nature,
I climb at dawn, parting the misty path.
The pine fragrance is sweet, the tinted sky's water color is beautiful.
From peak to peak, the morning mist with the morning wind
Soars, exudes, gathers, flows, and passes over.
Ah, only my footsteps resound, resound in the empty sky,
As I climb the mountain peak rising between the city and nature...

Pointed, square, round, and straight lines, oh angles!
Oh, eight-character roofs (팔자집웅) floating like dreams above the mist!
Oh, ancient palace, oh, new department store, oh, advertising tower!
Oh, church cross, oh, temple bell sound, oh, red and blue,
Gray and black tiled roofs, oh, sick thatched roofs!
Oh, electric lights suffocating in the spreading dawn light!
Oh, the scream of the streetcar running fast on its track, oh, the groan of the city!

And on the other side, like an artist's unfinished painting,
Mountains wrapped in mist like a folding screen!
Oh, those outstandingly grown pine trees, oh, these pretty rocks!
Oh, the winding of the river, known only by seeing the tip of a boat's mast!
From this peak to that peak, wishing to select and step over,
Scattering our spirits infinitely vast,
Making our blood vessels run infinitely fast,
A great panorama, ah, land of earth, my homeland!

I stand, upon this mountain.
Looking down upon civilization and nature to the left and right,
Redly, yet more redly, the dew-covered grass and rocks
Are tinted by the morning sun, which I drink into my breath.

Now the earth steps back one pace,
The mountain pulls down its dark blue cloak,
The round, beautiful face of the sun bursts forth... <99>

**Summary**

This poem by "Yohan" describes the view from a mountain peak at dawn, overlooking both a modernizing city and the natural landscape. The speaker contrasts the geometric shapes, specific buildings (palace, department store, church, temple), and jarring sounds of the city emerging from the mist with the serene, expansive beauty of the surrounding mountains, pines, rocks, and river, identified as "my homeland." The poem captures a moment of contemplation, caught between encroaching modernity ("civilization") and enduring nature, culminating in the powerful image of the sunrise. For historians, the poem vividly illustrates the complex feelings evoked by rapid, often imposed, modernization during the colonial period. It juxtaposes symbols of tradition (palace, temple, nature as homeland) with new colonial-era structures (department store, ad tower, streetcar), reflecting the tensions and transformations occurring in Korean society in the early 1930s. The identification of nature with "homeland" (조국) subtly hints at national sentiment.

山上曲
						요한
도회와 자연과 그 두새에 소사오른 봉어리에
새벽에 나는 오르노라, 안개의 길을 헤치고
소나무 향기 향기럽고 물들인 하늘 물색 고운데
봉에서 봉으로 아츰 안개는 아츰 바람과 함께
솟으고 풍기고 뭉치고 흘러서 넘어가는데
아아 내 발소래만 창공에 올리나니 올리나니
도회와 자연과 그 새에 솟은 산봉에 내 오를 적에...

뾰족하고 네모지고 둥글고 곧은 줄이여, 角度여,
안개우에 꿈과 같이 떠오르는 팔자집웅들이어,
옛날의 궁궐이어, 새로운 백화점이어, 광고탑이어,
교회의 십자가여, 절간의 종소래여, 붉고 푸로고
회색과 검은 기와집웅이어, 병들은 초가집웅이어,
퍼지는 새벽빛 속에 숨막혀하는 전등불이어,
궤도를 굿고 가는 전차의 비명이어, 도회의 신음이어.

그리고 또 한편에는 화가의 그리다 채 못그린
한 폭의 평풍같은 안개에 싸인 산들이어,
그 빼어나게 잘아난 소나무여 이뿐 바위여,
배돗대 끝을 보아서 거긴즐 아는 강물의 감돎이어,
이 봉에서 저 봉으로 골라집고 넘고 싶은
우리의 혼령을 무한광대하게 흐터버리며
우리의 혈맥을 무한 빨리 달리게 하는
위대한 파노라마, 아하 흙의 나라, 나의 조국!

나는 섯노라, 이 산우에.
문명과 자연을 좌우에 나려다 보면서
붉게 또 붉게 이슬 맞은 풀밭과 바위를
물들이는 아츰해를 내 호흡에 마시어 드리노라.

이제 지구는 한거름 물러서고
산은 그 검푸를 장옷을 내리 끄을러
벌꺽 솟는 둥글고 아름다운 태양의 얼골... ＜99＞
