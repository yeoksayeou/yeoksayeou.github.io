Publication Date: 1931년 12월 01일

Author: Survey Respondents (고영환, 홍천길, 김환재, 홍효민, 현상윤, 김윤수, 김진섭, 이량, OOO, OOO)
Article Type: 설문 (Survey)

Complaints and Demands Regarding the Newspaper World

The role (役割) of newspapers in Chosŏn cannot be said to have been small. However, is it as we expect? Of course, the newspaper world itself likely has complex circumstances within it, such as various management issues and censorship relations. But we should not yield just because of that. The time has come to accurately judge right and wrong when looking at newspapers. We intend to take the opportunity of hearing the opinions of various gentlemen regarding the following points as a chance to view newspapers critically. (Order of arrival)
1. Complaints about the Chosŏn newspaper world.
2. Demands for the Chosŏn newspaper world.

Make it Reader-Centric
Ko Yŏng-hwan

1. (a) Doing things based on the newspaper company's interests rather than the reader's. For example, not using Hangul consistently, but each person writing it in their own way, etc.
(b) Recklessly publishing articles merely to attract the reader's temporary curiosity, without sufficiently investigating the truth or falsehood of facts. For example, reporting things like the so-called "Wanpaoshan Incident" (萬寶山事件) with exaggeration (針小棒大), thereby unnecessarily enraging the ignorant masses and consequently causing national shame (民族的 羞恥), etc.
2. (a) Expand the page space as much as possible. By doing so, for example, publish more worthwhile articles in columns like the Home section and Arts and Sciences section.
(b) Use terminology as much as possible so that its meaning is understandable to the general public among us. For example, standardize the usage of words like "moratorium" (모라토리움), "observer" (옵서버), *suhae* (手配 - arrangements/wanted notice), *ch'aji* (差支 - hindrance/problem), *yŏk'hal* (役割 - role)... etc., so that even people who only know Hangul and Hanja can easily understand them.

Accurate Reporting
Taejŏn, Hong Ch'ŏn-gil

1. While swift reporting may be the original mission (使命) of the press, although inadequate investigative resources (調査機關) due to financial issues <228> might be one cause, there is perhaps no place where errors in place names and personal names are as severe as in our newspapers. Furthermore, because they often rely solely on telegraphic communications, we see reports where yesterday's fallen figure becomes today's triumphant general (凱旋將軍). Also, incidents like publishing misinformation known to surely incite mass psychology (群衆心理), causing events like the XXX Incident, and thereby inflicting a fatal blow (致命傷) nationally—these are matters of regret (恨事) more than mere complaints.
2. As for demands for the newspaper world, although the objective situation (客觀的 情勢) may not permit it, I wish they could break free from restrained speech and, even if limited to trivial matters (枝葉問題), expose the XX of the oppression (抑壓) and reckless infringement of rights (權利侵害) faced from time to time.

Lower the Price
Kim Hwan-jae

1. The high price (高價).
2. The newspaper companies' principles and assertions (主義主張) not being clear (鮮明).
3. The wording in the politics section (政治面) being too literal (直譯的).
4. Treating local news (地方記事) neglectfully.

Impartiality and Global Activity
Hong Hyo-min

1. Complaints about the Chosŏn Newspaper World
It's awkward to speak of, so let's postpone this for the next opportunity. Just like the London Times, or the *Taemae* (大每 - Ōsaka Mainichi), *Taejo* (大朝 - Ōsaka Asahi).
2. Demands for the Chosŏn Newspaper World
I think newspapers in Chosŏn must first clarify their principles (主義). Next, I think their political and economic views (政見) and policies (政策) should become apparent in the newspaper. Other things would be accurate reporting and impartiality (不偏不當), I suppose. However, when there are principles and assertions, even if it becomes biased (偏, 儻), I don't think it's grounds for dispute (是非). And I hope they would be active globally.

Lacking Intelligence
Hyŏn Sang-yun

1. Lacking Intelligence (聰明)
Newspapers are the eyes, ears, and heart (心臟) of a nation (民族). Therefore, when other nations or peoples want to know the trend (歸趨) of public opinion (輿論) in a counterpart country, don't they consult (鑑하여) that country's newspapers? However, the newspapers in our place now do not seem to be properly acting as substitutes (代行) for the eyes, ears, and heart of our nation.
2. Demands are numerous, but listing the important ones is as follows:
1. Regarding Management (經營上) - Employ high-level reporters (高級記者); employ reporters whose insight (眼孔) can penetrate the hidden meaning (紙背).
2. Regarding Editing (編輯上)
1. Editorial Unity (編輯의 統一). Ensure editorials (社說), short commentaries (短評), and other editorial content are not contradictory (背馳).
2. Reduction (縮少) of the Literature and Arts column (文藝欄) and increase of the Politics and Economics columns.
3. Improvement of Quality (品位向上). Do not publish (不載) articles on vulgar (俗惡的) love affairs or base (野鄙) lewdness (淫蕩記事). <87>
4. Creation (創造) of Public Opinion. Not being satisfied (滿足) merely with reporting, but producing (製作) living material (材料) to arouse (喚起) public opinion.

Just Hoping for Development
Kim Yun-su

Reply (拜復)
I sincerely wish (仰祝) for the continuous development (益益發展) of your company. Regarding your recent inquiry (下問), I have no special (特別) complaints about the Chosŏn newspaper world; I just pray (빌 뿐이올시다) for its daily development.

Concern for Cultural Creation
Kim Chin-sŏp

(1) I don't particularly have complaints. However, I wish newspapers would think more about the principle (原理) that humans grow (成長). In the past, how closely (緊密) did newspapers stand in the position of protecting (庇護) and guiding (指導) us, the foolish and weak (愚弱) nation! But now, we are already not entirely children (童子) desperately seeking lessons. Thus, I greatly fear that the traditional (傳統的) arrogance (傲慢) of the leader (the newspaper) wearing an empty husk (空虛한 皮殼) estranges (踈隔) the populace (民衆).
(2) For that reason, newspapers must now prepare to move precisely into the formative class (形成階級). It is vital (緊要) to guide the populace, but with absolute (絶對) concern (觀心) for cultural creation (文化創造). It is vital (緊要) to indicate (指示) direction (方向) to them, but in a way that gives them the opportunity (機會) for their abilities (能力) to extend and grow (伸長) respectively in that place. Thus, more than anything, a journalistic spirit (저내리스틱한 精神) in a good sense will make today's Chosŏn newspapers even more honorable (名譽).

First, Become Thinkers
Yi Ryang

Chosŏn's newspapers, whatever their path, must have political assertions (主張) to have value (價値).
Therefore, if it is a newspaper run by Chosŏn people today, before considering its existence (存在) or business policy (營業政策), this newspaper must first become a den (巢窟) for Chosŏn's concerned patriots (憂士), Chosŏn's thinkers (思想者), and Chosŏn's activists (運動者). Through this, the newspaper will become a newspaper for Chosŏn people, for members of society (社會人).

New Establishment of the Agriculture Column
OOO

1. My greatest complaint is that the Chosŏn proletariat (無産階級) still cannot possess an independent newspaper capable of expressing its true *purŭjijŭm* (부르지즘 - bourgeoisie?) through its own organ (機關). No matter how much the current newspapers clamor on behalf of the Chosŏn proletariat, it feels like nothing more than a kind of advocacy (代辯).
2. As for demands for the current newspapers: 1. Aim for at least the level of Japan's (日本) "*Taejo*" (大朝 - Ōsaka Asahi) or "*Taemae*" (大每 - Ōsaka Mainichi), and strive thoroughly (徹底) for the purification (淨化) of the page space (紙面) through more refined (洗練) technique (技術) so there are no misprints (誤植), omissions (脫落), or mistranslations (誤譯). 2. Sweep away (一掃) the slovenly content merely meant to fill pages, and publish authoritative (權威) written materials (記載物) that are necessary and appropriate (必要適切) for the lives of the masses (大衆). <88> 3. Dispatch special correspondents (特派員) from each company to places like Tōkyō (東京), Manchuria (滿洲), Shanghai (上海), Vladivostok (海蔘威), etc., which have inseparable (不可離) and significant political relationships with Chosŏn society, to swiftly report important events occurring (起生) in those locations (該地). 4. Expand and enrich the industry column, strictly speaking, the agriculture column (農業欄), more meaningfully, so that the peasant masses (農民大衆), who constitute almost the entire population of Chosŏn, can gain real practical benefit (實益) directly in their lives. (By doing this, a vast number of new readers could also be acquired (獲得) from the peasant stratum). These are what I think.

Guaranteeing Reporters' Livelihoods and Improving Reporters' Dignity
OOO

Since complaints will be converted into demands, for convenience (便宜上), I will briefly answer your question under the single topic (一個題目) of "Demands for the Chosŏn Newspaper World."
First, a demand for newspaper censors (檢閱官) = Please approach your work with an attitude that follows the progress of the times (時代에 順步的 態度).
Second, a demand for newspaper managers (經營者) = Please establish a firm (確乎) economic foundation (經濟的 地盤) for the newspaper companies; at the same time, please fully guarantee the livelihood security (生活保障) of newspaper employees (社員).
Third, a demand for newspaper reporters (記者) = Please maintain the dignity (品位) of a newspaper reporter; accordingly, generally too, I hope all reporters would move forward under a spirit of cooperation (協力) regarding the improvement of newspaper reporters' dignity (品位向上). <89>
<86-89>

**Summary**

This 1931 survey captures criticisms and hopes for the Korean press under Japanese rule. Respondents lamented newspapers prioritizing company interests over readers, inaccurate and sensationalist reporting (notably citing the Wanpaoshan Incident's handling), high prices, unclear editorial stances, poor translation quality, and neglect of local and agricultural news. Demands included reader-centric content, accuracy, lower prices, clearer principles, improved language accessibility, expanded sections (home, arts, politics, economics, agriculture), hiring intellectually capable journalists, ensuring editorial consistency, avoiding vulgar content, and fostering informed public opinion. Calls were also made for reduced censorship, better international coverage (dispatching correspondents), and crucially, improved economic stability for newspapers and better livelihood security and dignity for journalists.

From a historical perspective, the article offers valuable insights into the Korean intellectual climate of the early 1930s regarding media. The explicit mention of the Wanpaoshan Incident highlights contemporary awareness of how media could exacerbate ethnic tensions. The recurring themes of censorship ("拘束된 言論"), the desire for newspapers to act as voices for the nation ("民族의 눈이오 귀요 心臟"), the call for journalistic integrity and financial stability, and the articulated need for the press to engage with both intellectual discourse and the practical needs of the masses (especially farmers) reflect the complex challenges and aspirations of Korean society under colonial constraints. The plea for journalists to be "thinkers" and "activists" underscores the perceived role of the press in national consciousness-raising.

잡지명	동광 제28호
발행일	1931년 12월 01일
기사제목	新聞界에 대한 不平과 要望
기사형태	설문

新聞界에 대한 不平과 要望
朝鮮에 잇어서 新聞의 役割은 컷다고 아니할 수 없다. 그러나 그것은 우리가 期待하는 바와 같은가. 勿論 新聞界는 그 自體 內에 經營上 諸問題와 檢閱關係등 複雜한 事情이 잇을 것이다. 그러나 그러타고 우리는 讓步할 것이 아니다. 新聞을 볼 떄에 옳고 긇은 것을 正確히 판단할 時機는 이제 왓다. 우리는 이제 左記에 대한 諸氏의 意見을 듣는 것을 機會로 新聞을 批判的으로 볼 機會를 삼고저 한다. (順序到着順)
1. 朝鮮新聞界에 대한 不平.
2. 朝鮮新聞界에 대한 要望.

讀者本位로 하라
高永煥

1. (가) 新聞을 讀者本位로 하지 않고 新聞社 本位로만 하는 것 例하면 한글 같은 것도 一定하게 쓰지 않고 各人各樣으로 쓰는 것 等等.
(나) 事實의 有無虛僞를 充分히 調査하지도 않고 다만 讀者의 一時的 好奇心을 끄을기 위하야 함부로 記載하는 것. 예하면 소위 『萬寶山事件』과 같은 것을 針小棒大的으로 報道하야 無知한 群衆을 空然히 激憤케 하고 따라서 民族的 羞恥를 범하게 하는 것 등등.
2. (가) 可及的 紙面을 좀 더 擴張할 것. 그리하야 例하면 家庭欄과 學藝欄 등에 읽을 만한 論文을 좀 많이 실을 것.
(나) 用語를 될 수 잇는 대로 일반 우리에게 그 意味가 통하도록 使用할 것. 예하면 『모라토리움』, 『옵서버』, 手配, 差支, 役割... 등등의 文字를 오직 한글과 한문만 아는 사람으로도 容易하게 알아보도록 定用할 것.
