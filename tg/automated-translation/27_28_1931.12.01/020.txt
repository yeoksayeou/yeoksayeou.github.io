The Recognition of Strength
						Ch'unwŏn

The universe is strength. All things in creation (森羅萬象) are the ever-changing, myriad rhythms of energy (에네르기). Without strength, there is no universe.

War clouds are now gathering in the skies of the Asian continent (亞細亞大陸). There are bugles for advance, orders for assault, and the smoke and roar of cannons arise. This is the manifestation of the strength of nations (民族). It is the sound of the strength of nations clashing.

There is likely nothing that reveals the form of strength as plainly as war (戰爭). Just as wind, water, and thunderbolts (霹靂) most plainly show the strength of the natural world (自然界).

War demands the sound physical strength (體力), brainpower (腦力), and spiritual strength (精神力) of a nation (一民族). The strength to run long distances (長距離) carrying heavy loads, to endure hunger and stay awake for many days and nights, and to withstand wetness and cold—this is the strength of the body; strategy (作戰), science (科學), and machinery (機械)—this is the strength of the brain; patriotism (愛國), unity (團結), obedience (服從), and courage (勇氣)—this is the spiritual strength of the military. A war between two nations (二民族) is ultimately a comparison of the sum total (總合) of these strengths of the two nations.

However, what we precisely lack is this strength. The strength of the body, the strength of the brain, the strength of the spirit. Therefore, we are nameless people crouching behind the curtain, unable to take a role (一役) on today's stage (舞臺) where humanity (人類) is making a grand appearance (總出動 大出演). On the day strength rises within us, the stage of humanity will send us a formal request (鄭重한 出演請求狀) to appear.

Today is the day to cultivate strength.

(Ch'unwŏn)

**Summary**

The author, Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu), argues that strength—physical, mental, and spiritual—is the fundamental principle of the universe, most clearly demonstrated in war. He laments that Koreans lack this collective strength, rendering them insignificant spectators on the world stage dominated by powerful nations. The piece concludes with a call for Koreans to focus on cultivating this national strength to eventually earn their place and recognition in the world. From a historical perspective, this article is significant as it reflects the discourse of national self-strengthening (자강론) prevalent among Korean intellectuals under Japanese rule, influenced by Social Darwinist ideas. Written shortly after the Manchurian Incident, the emphasis on military-related strengths (physical endurance, strategy, obedience, courage) and the framing of international relations as a stage for powerful actors might be seen by historians as aligning with, or at least resonating with, the increasingly militaristic atmosphere of the time, potentially foreshadowing Yi Kwangsu's later controversial path.

힘의 재인식
						春園
宇宙는 힘이다. 森羅萬象은 에네르기의 千變萬化的 律動이다. 힘이 없으면 宇宙는 없다.
亞細亞大陸의 하늘에는 바야흐로 戰雲이 굼틀거린다. 進軍喇叭이 잇고 突擊의 號令이 잇고 砲煙砲響이 일어난다. 이것은 民族의 힘의 발현이다. 민족의 힘과 힘이 마주치는 소리다.
戰爭처름 힘의 형태를 端的으로 낱아내는 것은 없을 것이다. 마치 바람과 물과 霹靂이 自然界의 힘을 가장 端的으로 보이는 모양으로.
戰爭은 一民族의 健全한 體力을 要하고 腦力을 要하고 精神力을 要한다. 무거운 짐을 지고 長距離를 다름질하여 여러 날 여러 밤을 굶고 새우고도 젖음과 치위에 견디는 힘-이것이 몸의 힘이요 作戰과 科學과 機械와 이것이 골의 힘이요, 愛國과 團結과 服從과 勇氣-이것이 군사의 精神의 힘이다. 二民族의 戰爭은 結局 二民族의 이 힘의 總合의 比較다.
그런데 우리에게 正히 없는 것이 이 힘이다. 몸의 힘, 골의 힘 精神의 힘. 그러기 때문에 우리는 人類가 總出動 大出演하는 今日의 舞臺에 一役을 맡지 못하고 幕 뒤에 쭈구리고 안는 姓名 없는 백성이다. 우리에게 힘이 오르는 날 人類의 舞臺는 우리에게 鄭重한 出演請求狀을 보낼 것이다.
오늘은 힘을 기루는 날.
(春園)
