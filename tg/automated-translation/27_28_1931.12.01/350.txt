Article Title: 피로 색인 당신의 얼골을! (Your Face Etched in Blood!)
Author: 毛允淑
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Your Face Etched in Blood!
						Mo Yunsuk (毛允淑)

You are my companion beside me
Wearing a sincere sash on a stark blue pole
You, who freely crushed
The heads plastered with all sorts of gilt decorations (飾金).

In the marketplace where you stand,
Don't drunken women of melancholy and ennui,
Vainly singing of momentary joy,
Peer into your soul?
Oh, the honor of fullness and heroic spirit!
Oh, the crown of history that boasted of its zenith!
What could be of value!
I see your face,
That triangular face (三稜形), burning red with thought and passion!
Your face that will laugh powerfully
Upon the great judgment seat of the people!

My pleasure burning in dream-like colors
Sang songs in the tomb for long ages,
But now that I see you,
I have abandoned the heart that was built upon honor.
Changing my breath in the fragrant powder jar (粉독),
Roughly changing into wave-patterned clothes,
I grasp the hand of you, the rescuer.
Ah - do you see now?
The soul drifting on the flowing ocean!
The death perishing in that flood!

Please do not push me away,
Calling me a woman of temptation.
Do not laugh at my panting breath,
Saying I block the sound march forward.
My beloved! Let us walk
The narrow path of argument and obstinacy
With silence and contemplation. <97>
Through the thicket of division and jealousy,
Please bind me to your strong hand.

Though there is no place to rest on the icy cold field,
Though we may strike against the valley of starvation,
If the flame in your heart lives on,
Will not the ocean's shore or the wilderness's mad wind
Be conquered by the great you?

Smashing numerous incense burners of illusion (蒙幻),
From the bright pipe organ, the brilliant ruins,
Kicking everything away,
Into the dark legs, the strong arms!
Your face etched (삭인) in blood,
I embrace it to my heart and depart. - September 12th, night -<98>
<97-98>

**Summary**

This poem by Mo Yunsuk expresses fervent admiration and devotion towards an unnamed 'you,' characterized by a face "etched in blood," symbolizing suffering, sacrifice, and revolutionary zeal. The speaker renounces superficial pleasures, societal expectations ("gilt decorations," "fragrant powder jar"), and personal comfort to join this figure on a difficult path ("icy cold field," "valley of starvation"). The poem contrasts the perceived emptiness of past joys and societal norms with the profound meaning found in aligning with this figure, who represents strength, justice ("judgment seat of the people"), and the potential for overcoming immense hardship ("conquered by the great you"). From a historical perspective, written in 1931 during the Japanese colonial period, the poem likely uses the figure of 'you' as an allegory for the Korean nation, the independence movement, or an idealized revolutionary leader. Historians might find the intense, almost religious fervor, the rejection of bourgeois comforts, and the embrace of struggle reflective of the desperate yearning for liberation and the ideological currents (nationalism, possibly socialism) influencing intellectuals and artists of the time.

피로 색인 당신의 얼골을!
						毛允淑
당신은 내곁에의 동무지오
시퍼런 창대에 진실한 띠를 띠고
왼갓 飾金의 회칠한 머리를
마음대로 뭇지른 당신이지오.

당신이 밟고 섯는 장터에는
刹那의 歡喜를 헛되이 노래하는
憂鬱과 倦怠의 술취한 게집들이
당신의 靈魂을 엿보고 잇지 않어요
飽滿의 榮譽와 英雄的 氣勢여!
全盛을 자랑하든 歷史의 王冠이어!
무엇이 값잇으랴!
나는 보노라 당신의 얼골을,
思惟와 情熱의 붉게 타는
그 三稜形의 얼골을!
거륵한 民衆의 심판대 우에서
힘차게 웃을 그대의 얼골을!

꿈빛에 타는 저의 快樂은
긴세월 무덤속에서 노래를 불럿으나
내 이제 그대를 보매
榮譽우에 세웟든 그 맘을 버렷나이다.
내음새 나는 粉독에서 呼吸을 달리하고
波紋난 옷가지를 거칠게 바꿔입고
救助者인 당신의 손을 붓드나이다
아 - 그대는 지금 보십니까?
流動하는 大洋에 떠 가는 靈魂을!
저 洪水의 盡滅되어 가는 죽엄을!

나를 誘惑의 게집이라
들리치지 말어주서요
건전한 行進을 막는다 하여
저의 뛰는 呼吸을 비웃지 마서요.
사랑하는 그대여! 우리는
論爭과 固執의 적은 길을
沈黙과 思惟로 걸어갑시다 ＜97＞
分裂과 嫉妬의 덤불새로는
그대의 굳세인 손에 나를 엮어주서요

차듸찬 벌판에 쉬일곳 없고
饑餓의 峽谷에 부디칠지나
그대의 가슴의 불길이 살엇다면
大洋의 岸邊이나 荒野의 狂風도
위대한 그대에게 征服되지 않으리가.

허다한 蒙幻의 香爐를 깨치고
화창한 風琴 찬란한 폐허로부터
모든 것을 박차버리고
시컴은 다리 굳세인 팔 안으로!
피로삭인 당신의 얼골을
가슴에 안고 떠나나이다. -9월 12일, 밤-＜98＞
