In the Night Streetcar
						By Han Hŭkku in America

After midnight had passed,
I got on the Halsted streetcar.
Inside the car,
Laborers returning from work,
A Black man, a Polish woman, an Irish lass.
The Black man, freed from slavery,
Today, again in the iron chains of money...
The Polish woman, freed from Russia,
Today, again in the fetters of money...
The Irish lass in the green skirt,
Today too, her skirt is green...

All yawning,
Nodding off, sitting and dozing.
The streetcar stopped once or twice,
And they all got off.
Among them, I alone remained,
Listening to the sound of the wheels turning a curve.

Closing the door to my room desolately,
I return and lie down in my place.

They cause pain to my eyes,
But who looks after my body!
XXXXX XXX
No matter what...
They have holidays,
They have a place to stand... <98>

XXX XXXX
I don't even have land to stick an awl into—
Ah! I don't even have land to stick an awl into...

Note: Halsted = Chicago's poor district.
1931. 4. 3 - In Chicago - <98>
<98-99>

**Summary**

The poem describes the narrator's experience riding a late-night streetcar in Chicago, observing weary laborers of different ethnicities (Black, Polish, Irish). The narrator reflects on their hardships, tied to economic struggles ("chains of money"), but ultimately contrasts their situations with his own profound sense of rootlessness and dispossession as a Korean in America. While the others, despite their toil, seem to have some place or belonging ("holidays," "a place to stand"), the narrator feels utterly alienated, lacking even the smallest piece of land to call his own. For historians, the poem offers a poignant glimpse into the struggles and alienation faced by Korean immigrants in the US during the colonial period, highlighting themes of labor, poverty, ethnic encounters, and the painful sense of statelessness experienced by Koreans abroad while their homeland was under Japanese rule. The comparison with other marginalized groups underscores the narrator's unique feeling of complete displacement.
