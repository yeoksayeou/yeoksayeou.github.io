**Summary**

This article from the December 1931 issue of *Donggwang* magazine lists the key personnel and their roles within three major newspapers operating in colonial Korea: the Dong-A Ilbo, the Choson Ilbo, and the Maeil Sinbo. It details the presidents, directors, editors, department heads, reporters (including their specific beats like the Government-General or courthouse), correspondents in major cities (domestic and international like Shanghai, Tokyo, Osaka), and business/technical staff for each publication.

For historians, this article provides a valuable snapshot of the structure and staffing of the Korean press landscape during the Japanese colonial period. It clearly shows the hierarchy within these organizations, the designated news beats revealing journalistic priorities, and the different complexions of the papers – the Korean-led Dong-A and Choson Ilbo versus the Maeil Sinbo with Japanese leadership (President Ikeda Hideo) alongside Korean staff, reflecting its role as the official organ of the Government-General. The lists of names themselves are a significant resource for studying the individuals involved in media and potentially politics during this era.
