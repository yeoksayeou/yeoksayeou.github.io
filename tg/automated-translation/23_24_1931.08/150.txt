Magazine Title: 동광 제24호
Publication Date: 1931년 08월 04일
Article Title: 추억 (Memories)
Author: 全佑漢
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

A heart like a skylark singing in the May sky,
That heart like the sound of a flute seeping into the moonlit night,
How many times did I reach out my hand to find it?
To find again that dear heart grown from the soil.

On a quiet moonlit night, treading on the shadows
Of young blades of grass sobbing in the spring breeze,
That heart that counted, counted stars, one for me, one for a star,
Ah, that lovely heart, could I forget it even in dreams?

Oh, it is the heart of my young old days,
That cannot be seen though I call and search,
That cannot be seen again though I cry or laugh,
My dear, innocent heart of bygone days. <35>

**Summary**

This poem, "Memories," by Chŏn Uhan expresses a deep yearning for the lost innocence and purity of childhood. The speaker likens this past self to a singing skylark and a gentle flute sound, recalling moments of simple connection with nature, like counting stars on a moonlit night. The poem concludes with a lament that this precious, innocent heart can never be recaptured, no matter how much the speaker longs for it. From a historical perspective, the poem offers insight into personal sentiments during the colonial period. It showcases a focus on individual nostalgia and the idealized past, perhaps reflecting a turn inward or a longing for a simpler, purer state of being amidst the complexities and hardships of the era, contrasting with more overtly political or nationalistic expressions common at the time.

추억
						全佑漢
5월 하늘에 노래하는 종달이 같은 마음
달밤에 수며드는 피리 소리같은 그 마음을
그 멫 번이나 손을 내밀어 찾아 보앗든고
흙에서 자란 그 귀여운 마음을 다시 찾으려.

고요한 달밤, 봄 바람에 흐늑이는
어린 풀닢 그림자를 밟으면서
별 하나 나 하나 헤고 헤든 그 마음
아아 그 마음 사랑워라 꿈엔들 닞히랴.

오오 그는 나의 어린 옛날의 마음이로다
불러도 찾아도 보이지 안는
울어도 우서도 다시 볼 수 없는
귀여운 옛날의 천진한 내 마음이로다.＜35＞
