Willow Shade
						Chang Kŏn-sik, Texas Company

As someone working in a foreign trading house where five peoples—British, American, Korean, Japanese, and Chinese—collaborate and labor together, I feel there are many uniquely special and intriguing aspects to the atmosphere within the company and in relations with clients. Firstly, we (including several colleagues) never forget the sense of responsibility, like that of athletes representing Korea in an international arena. This is because when we make a mistake or do something dishonorable, those in higher positions tend to make a sweeping judgment, saying "Koreans," rather than referring to individuals like "Mr. Lee" or "Mr. Kim."

Our business counterparts also include Koreans, Japanese, and Chinese, and the characteristics reflected in our dealings differ for each group. Criticizing others is a futile task, but if I were to offer a general critique of our merchants, it would be roughly as follows.

A lack of the principle of "giving up the small to gain the large" (小捨大取主義). This means not putting into practice the common saying, "giving a *doe* (升) to receive a *mal* (斗)." One must sacrifice a lugworm (?) (갯지내) to catch a goby, and give up shrimp for a carp (鯉魚), yet we often seem reluctant to abandon immediate small profits (小利), fighting over them and thus failing to see the large profits (大利) lying deeper. In other words, there are few people who could be designated as truly powerful merchants (豪商).

There is often a tendency towards weak creditworthiness. Of course, for us lacking material resources, unintentional breaches of trust might be unavoidable circumstances, but often, to evade even a momentary difficult conversation (궁박한 면담) stemming from hardship, people make fine-sounding promises and then fail to fulfill them after parting ways. It would be a relief if people just said "it will work" for what will work and "it won't work" for what won't, even if lightning were to strike on the spot.

Flunkeyism (*sadaejuui*, 사대주의) is apparent. Compared to the amount invested, the facilities, plans (設計), and visions (夢想) are often excessive, ultimately preventing successful operation and leading to many short-lived ventures.

I apologize for listing only shortcomings. However, I wish to correct our weaknesses before boasting of our strengths. Of course, among our merchants, there are many superior individuals who could serve as models for others, but generally speaking, we always lament that steadfast and tenacious merchants are few in number.<58>

**Summary**

The author, a Korean employee at the multinational Texas Company in 1931, reflects on the responsibility he feels representing Koreans in such an environment. He critiques contemporary Korean merchants for three main faults: prioritizing small, immediate gains over larger, long-term ones (lacking 小捨大取主義); having weak creditworthiness, often making empty promises to avoid difficult situations; and exhibiting *sadaejuui* (flunkeyism) by creating overly ambitious business plans disproportionate to their investment, leading to failure. While acknowledging exceptions, he laments the general lack of tenacious Korean entrepreneurs. From a historical perspective, the article offers a window into the self-perception and economic anxieties of some Koreans under Japanese colonial rule. The author's feeling of national representation within a foreign firm and his specific criticisms (especially the mention of *sadaejuui* in a business context) provide insight into the social and psychological pressures of the era and perceived obstacles to Korean economic development.

버드나무 그늘
						텍사스會社 張健植
나는 英·米·朝·日·中 5개 민족이 합작 근로하는 외국인 商館에 잇는 몸이 되어 사내의 공기와 거래처와의 관계가 유달리 특수하고 묘미한 점이 많음을 느낌니다. 첫재로 우리는 (몇몇 사우도 포함) 국제 경기장에 조선을 대표하야 나간 선수와 같은 책임감을 잊을 적이 없습니다. 그것은 우리가 무슨 실수나 불명예한 일을 할 때에 웃사람된 이는 「이모, 김모」하는 이보담 「조선사람」이라고 총괄적 평가를 하기가 쉬운 탓입니다.
상업 거래 상대자도 조선인, 일본인, 중국인이 모다 잇어 거래선상에 비치는 특징이 各異합니다. 남의 비판은 부지러운 일이요 우리 상인의 대체적 비판을 한다면 대략 이럿습니다.
小捨大取主義가 缺한 것. 이것은 常語「되(升) 주고 말(斗) 박기」를 실용 안하는 것입니다. 망둥이를 잡으려면 갯지내를 희생시키고 鯉魚를 위하야 새우를 버리는 것이언만 우리는 목전의 小利를 버리기 아까워 싸우되 깊숙히 잇는 大利를 못 보는 감이 많습니다. 다시 말하면 豪商이라고 지명할 사람이 적습니다.
신용이 박약한 경향이 많습니다. 물론 물질적 여유가 없는 우리로서 본의 아닌 불신용이 어쩔 수 없는 사정이겟지만 궁박한 면담을 일시라도 회피하기 위하야 善美한 약속을 남기고 헤진 뒤 이행이 없습니다. 당장에 벼락이 나려도 될 건 된다 안될 건 안된다 햇으면 속이 씨원하겟습니다.
사대주의가 보입니다. 투자액에 비하야 시설과 설계 及 몽상이 과대하야 결국 운전을 못하고 단명하는 예가 많습니다.
단점만 열거하야 미안합니다. 그러나 장점을 자랑하기 전에 약점을 고치고 싶습니다. 물론 우리 상인 중에 다른 이의 규범될 만한 우월한 분도 많습니다만 대체로 보아 우리는 굳센 근기 잇는 상업가가 소수임을 항상 한탄합니다.＜58＞
