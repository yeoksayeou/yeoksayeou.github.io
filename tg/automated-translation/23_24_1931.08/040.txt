If You Call Me, My Lord/Beloved
						Sin Sŏkchŏng (辛夕汀)

Like autumn leaves, dyed yellow,
Fluttering and scattering in the wind,
So shall I go
If You call me, my Lord...

Like the crescent moon silently crossing the ridge
On a night when mist thickly blankets the lake,
So shall I go
If You call me, my Lord...

Like water flowing bend after bend to the sky's edge
Beneath a softly thawed spring sky,
So shall I go
If You call me, my Lord...

Like sunshine seeping into the early spring lawn
Where white herons sing in the blue sky,
So shall I go
If You call me, my Lord...<7>

**Summary**

This poem by Sin Sŏkchŏng expresses an absolute and unconditional readiness to go to "Im" (Lord/Beloved) whenever called. The speaker compares their departure to natural, inevitable phenomena across different seasons—falling autumn leaves, a silent crescent moon, flowing spring water, and gentle sunshine. Each image emphasizes a quiet, unresisting, and perhaps graceful acceptance of the call. From a colonial history perspective, the poem's devotion to "Im" could be interpreted in various ways. While potentially a personal love poem, the ambiguous "Im" could also represent an idealized figure, a lost nation, or a spiritual calling, reflecting sentiments common during a period of suppressed national identity. The poem's gentle yet unwavering tone might subtly convey resilience or longing in the face of external pressures.

그러케 가오리다
임께서 부르시면...

파란 하늘에 白鷺가 노래하고
이른 봄 잔디밭에 스며드는 햇볓처럼
그러케 가오리다
임께서 부르시면...＜7＞
