Oh, Fortress Wall Hit by Stray Bullets, P'aesŏng (浿城) Ancient Battlefield
						By Yi Kyu-wŏn

Rumbling, rumbling, the thunderous sound of guns
Resounds through the quiet castle town, the desolate streets.
The pavilion tower, singing in the clear breeze,
Appeared so solemnly against the northern sky,
But the fierce noise of an unexpected dawn
Shattered this fortress wall completely.

Flowing, flowing, blood like rain
Flows onto the sacred hill beneath the innocent fortress.
The grasses and trees that danced in the loving breeze,
Were solely joyous beneath the northern sky,
But the unexpected red blood of life
Has soaked these trees completely.

The vanished records of battle, where can they be found again now?
Only the pillars of the pavilion tower, like the holes of a hornet's nest (巢蜂),
Remain, hiding their miserable faces.
Though I intently search for the powerful footsteps
Of the ten thousand heroes dreaming of crowning glory,
Oh, people of the past who passed without a trace,
Does only the fortress wall hit by stray bullets know you?

Time passes, and time comes,
Oh, silent testifier that pains the young heart!
If the harsh wind and rain pursue and erode you away,
With what will you speak of bygone days?<11>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Kyu-wŏn reflects on the ruins of P'aesŏng (likely referring to P'yŏngyang), a fortress scarred by battle ("stray bullets"). The poet laments the violence that destroyed the walls and stained the land with blood, contrasting it with the former peace and beauty. The poem expresses a sense of loss for the forgotten history and heroes associated with the site, with the ruined wall serving as the sole, fragile witness, itself vulnerable to the passage of time. For historians, this piece offers insight into Korean sentiments during the colonial period. The focus on a historically significant Korean site (P'aesŏng/P'yŏngyang) damaged by implied modern warfare could reflect nationalist feelings, lamenting the degradation of national heritage and the violence of the era, possibly alluding to conflicts involving Japanese forces or internal strife contextualized within the colonial landscape. The poem's melancholic tone captures a sense of helplessness and loss regarding the past under present circumstances.

流彈맞은 성벽이여, 浿城古戰場
						李揆元
울려 울려 우레같은 총소리
고요한 성 아래 적막한 거리를 울려
맑은 바람에 노래부르는 樓臺는
북편 하늘 우에 엄숙하게도 나타나더니
뜻하지 않은 새벽의 세찬 소리
그만 이 성벽을 깨트리고 말앗고나

흘러 흘러 비같은 피물이
죄 없는 성 아래 거룩한 동산에 흘러
자애로운 바람에 춤추든 풀나무는
북편 하날 우에 호올로 즐겁더니
뜻하지 않은 생명의 붉은 피
그만 이 나무를 적시고 말앗고나

사러진 싸움의 기록. 지금 어디가 다시 찾으리
巢蜂의 구멍 같은 樓臺의 기둥만이
비참한 얼골을 가리고 잇을 뿐일네
게관을 꿈꾸는 1만 영웅아의
힘찬 발자국을 뚜러지게 찾아보나
흔적 없이 지나간 옛사람들이여
유탄 맞은 성벽 뿐이 그대를 알건가

때는 지나가고 때는 오는데
젊은 가슴을 아프게 하는 침묵의 대언자여
모진 비바람이 너 쫓아 썩퉁긴다면
무엇으로 지난 날을 이야기할텐가＜11＞
