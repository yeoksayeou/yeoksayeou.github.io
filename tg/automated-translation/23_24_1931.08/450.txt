Modern March
France's Perfume King

If asked who the richest person in France is, anyone would probably think of Baron Rothschild, the President of the Bank of France. If not him, perhaps it would be the automobile king Citroën, who holds the title of France's Henry Ford.

However, in reality, the person with the most money in France right now is likely the perfume king, Coty. He amassed a fortune of 100 million *francs* purely by selling perfume, and additionally owns two influential newspapers, "Le Figaro" (피가로) and "L'Ami du Peuple" (인민의 친구).

He became such a great wealthy man forty years after going to Paris at the age of eighteen, learning how to make perfume from some pharmacist, shouldering it, and walking around selling it. <100>

**Summary**

This news brief highlights François Coty as potentially the wealthiest man in France, surpassing figures like Baron Rothschild and André Citroën. It notes Coty's fortune was built entirely on perfume and that he also owned two major newspapers, *Le Figaro* and *L'Ami du Peuple*. The article emphasizes his "rags-to-riches" story, starting as a young man learning perfume making and peddling his wares. For historians, this article reflects the Korean interest in Western modernity, capitalism, and individual success stories during the colonial period. It shows the dissemination of information about global figures and economic power shifts, framing Coty's success through perfume as a notable modern phenomenon.

모던行進曲
佛蘭西의 香水王
프란스의 가장 큰 부자가 누구냐하면 아마 누구나 프란스 은행 총재 「로트실드」남작이라고 생각햇을 것이다. 그러치 아니하면 프란스의 핸리 포드라는 칭호를 가진 자동차왕 씨트루안일 것이다.
그러나 사실에 잇어서 방금 프란스의 가장 돈 많은 사람은 아마도 향수왕 코티가 될 것이다. 그는 순전히 향수만 팔아가지고 1억만원의 재산을 뫃앗고 그 밖에 유력한 두 가지 신문 「피가로」와 「인민의 친구」를 소유햇다.
그는 열 여덟 살에 파리로 가서 어떤 약장사한데 향수 만드는 법을 배와가지고 그것을 질머 지고 팔러단닌 지 사십 년에 그런 큰 부자가 된 것이다.＜100＞
