**Body Text Translation:**
Willow Shade
Severance Union Medical College, Ku Yŏngsuk

Is everyone well? Today is Sunday, so I have free time. Being bored and having things to look for, I rummaged through my trunk and happened to discover something. It was the "Song of the Pacific," things I jotted down as they came to mind on the Pacific Ocean.

1. Wave upon Wave
Going, going, they said
Rowing the oars into the boundless sea
The mountains and rivers of the homeland grow distant
Why are the waves of the great ocean so rough?
I intended to cast the storms of life upon you
But thoughts of the home I leave behind
Wave upon wave again
(April 3rd, 400 miles from Yokohama)

2. Seagull
From Ōsaka to Yokohama
The few friends who came out
Cannot come further
What can you not forget, that you follow?
Go back, the day grows dark
Tell them at my home
That I am traveling well.

3. Spring Rain at Sea
Fine spring rain, welcome
Another world upon the water
Regrettable, to see it alone.
Wind, cease
My collar gets wet.
Water abounds and the wind is gentle
Where have all the flowers and bees gone?
(April 6th, somewhere on the Pacific Ocean) <57>

**Summary**

This piece, presented as a poem found in the author's trunk, captures Ku Yŏngsuk's reflections during a sea voyage across the Pacific Ocean in April 1931. It expresses feelings of nostalgia for her homeland (Korea), the emotional turbulence of departure likened to ocean waves, loneliness, and observations of nature at sea. For historians, this article offers a personal perspective from an educated Korean woman (affiliated with Severance Union Medical College) traveling abroad during the Japanese colonial period. It highlights the complex emotions—sadness of leaving home mixed with the implied purpose of the journey (likely study or emigration)—experienced by Koreans navigating opportunities and constraints under colonial rule.

버드나무 그늘
						世富蘭偲專門學校 具永淑
다 안녕히 게십니까. 오늘은 일요일이라 한가합니다. 심심도 하고 찾아볼 것도 잇어 트렁크를 뒤젓더니 우연히 무엇을 발견을 하엿습니다. 이것은 「태평양의 노래」 태평양에서 생각나는 대로 적어 넣엇든 것이외다.

1. 파도에 파도
간다 간다더니
무변대해로 노를 저어
고국 강산은 멀어지는데
대해양의 파도는 웨 이리 심해
인생의 풍파 네게 띠려 햇드니
남기고 가는 집 생각
파도에 또 파도
(4,3, 횡빈서 400마일)

2. 갈메기
대판서 횡빈까지
나왓든 멫멫 친구
더 못 오거든
너는 무엇을 못잊어 따르느냐
도라가라 날 저문다
전하여라 내 집에
나 잘 가더라구

3. 해상봄비
세우봄비 반갑다
물 우에 딴 세상
아깝다, 혼자 보긴.
바람아 끄치라
내 옷깃 젖는다.
물 많고 바람 고운데
꽃과 벌들은 다 어디 갓느냐
(4,6, 태평양 어느 곳) ＜57＞
