Research on the Korean Language and Script

Thoughts of the Editor

The Chosŏn script is the only treasure we possess. With it, the task of enabling all people, high and low, to read becomes easy, and consequently, it serves as an unparalleled, excellent tool (plow) for pioneering our path forward and building our culture.

However, looking at the writings of those who use the script now, this person writes this way, and that person writes that way, lacking any uniformity, which confuses the reader and causes followers to lose their way.

Our magazine has some awareness of this issue and attempts to write according to the principles advocated by grammarians, but it is regrettable that this often cannot be fully realized due to the constraints of printing press facilities.

The reason for publishing several studies related to this herein is to further seek the proper way to write in this area. We intend to publish articles related to this not only in this issue but also in subsequent issues. Therefore, we plan for this magazine, at least, to employ writing that is rational and systematic in the future.

Though this may seem like a small matter, we know it is something that will be of great consequence in the distant future.<44>

**Summary**

The editor of *Donggwang* magazine expresses pride in the Korean script (Hangeul) as a vital national asset for cultural development and widespread literacy. However, the editor laments the lack of standardized orthography, which leads to confusion. The magazine commits to adopting a more systematic and rational writing style based on grammarians' principles, despite practical challenges, and plans to publish further research on the topic, recognizing its long-term importance for the nation. From a historical perspective, this article is significant as it reflects the burgeoning efforts towards language standardization and orthographic reform in Korea during the Japanese colonial period. It highlights the awareness among intellectuals of the need for a unified written language as a cornerstone for national identity, cultural preservation, and future progress amidst external pressures. Historians might find the explicit link between standardized script, cultural construction, and national future, alongside the practical difficulties mentioned (printing limitations), of particular interest.

조선말과 글의 연구
편즙긔자의 생각
조션 글은 우리가 가진 오직 하나의 보배외다. 이로써 능히 높고 낮은 모든 사람을 글 보게하는 일이 쉬히 되고 딸아서 우리의 앞 길을 개척하는대 우리의 문화를 건설하는대 다시 없는 좋은 쟁긔가 되는 것임니다.
그런데 이제 그 글을 쓰는 사람의 글을 보면 이이는 이렇게 쓰고 저이는 저렇게 쓰어 한가지로 통일됨이 없어 보는 자로 하여금 뒤숭숭하게 하며 딸아오는 자로 길을 잃게 함니다.
우리 잡지에서 약간 여긔 대하여 깨달음이 있어 문법학자들의 주장대로 쓰어 보려고 시험하나 항상 인쇄소의 설비 관게로 원만히 되지 못함은 유감이외다.
이제 여긔 관한 연구를 멫편 실리는 뜻은 더욱 이 방면으로 맞당히 쓸 길을 찾자함이외다. 이번 호뿐 아니라 호를 딸아서 여긔 관한 글을 올리려 함니다. 그래서 장래에 잇서서는 적어도 이 잡지 하나는 합리적이요. 또 규모가 세운 글을 쓰려고 생각함니다.
이것이 적은 문제 같으나 먼 장래에는 크게 상관될 일인 줄 앎니다.＜44＞
