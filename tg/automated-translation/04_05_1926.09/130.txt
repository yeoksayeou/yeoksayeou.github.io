Lonely Dusk
						Kim Ansŏ

Time urges me on here,
Aimlessly entangled,
Thoughts adrift on the water,
Unable to grasp
The human heart.

Whispering heart to heart
In the early summer night wind,
That too has now flowed away,
All that remains is "thought."

When I look back upon myself
In the passing time,
Alone, I lose myself.

To a sorrow less even than
That of the autumn leaves
Destined to fall when their time comes.
-1923. 6. 20

Withering Self

The bloomed flower
Pointlessly leans over;
The spring days
Of sixteen have darkened.

Like a fragment of snow in the South Country,
Melting as it lands,
The dreams of seventeen
Were also brief.

While the tune of the greens gatherer's song
Makes the heart weep,
The springtime
Of eighteen quietly sets.
-1925. 4. 2

Lonely Day (Unfinished Draft)

Raindrops fall softly, softly,
When the garden balsam flower
Bowed its red head,
From the neighbor girl beyond the wall,—

"The days gone by
Are boundless
Hope.

Your sweet
Words
Fade like a dream.

No matter how sad rumors
May circulate from lips,
Your..."

Her sad song being sung
Mingled with the pitter-patter
Of raindrops, was heard.
1925. 4. 2<43>

**Summary**

This article presents three poems by Kim Ansŏ (金岸曙), all centered around themes of loneliness, the passage of time, and the ephemeral nature of youth and moments. "Lonely Dusk" reflects on drifting thoughts and the inability to hold onto feelings or time. "Withering Self" poignantly marks the swift passing of youth through the ages of sixteen, seventeen, and eighteen, comparing dreams to melting snow. "Lonely Day" captures a melancholic moment, overhearing a sad song about lost hope and fading words amidst falling rain. From a historical perspective, these poems, written in the mid-1920s under Japanese colonial rule, express a deep personal sense of sorrow, transience, and isolation that could reflect the broader cultural and national mood of the time. The use of older Korean orthography is also of linguistic interest.

孤寂한 黃昏
						金岸曙
歲月은 나를 예거니
하음 업시 설끼는
물 우에 떠도는 생각
붓잡을 수도 업는
사람의 맘이여.

첫 여름의 밤 바람에
맘과 맘을 소삭인 것도
只今은 흘러 가고
남음이란 「생각」밧게 업거니.

예고 가는 歲月의
나의 몸을 돌아본 때엔
혼자서 나는 나를 잃노라.

때가 되면 떨어질
가을 입사귀의
그것만도 못한 그 설음에.
-1923. 6. 20

지는 몸
핀 꼿은
한갓되히 기울고
열 여섯의
봄날은 저믈엇슴니다.

내려선 꺼지는
南國의 조각 눈이라.
열 일곱의
꿈도 짤밧슴니다.

나물 군의 노래 가락이
가슴을 우니는 새에
열 여듭의
봄철은 고요히 짐니다.
-1925. 4. 2
