Journey to Puyŏ
						Kim Sŏlkang

What traces remain on the old fortress site of Panwŏlsŏng?
Is the evening rain on Mount Pusosan the tears of court ladies?
Oh, roosting crows (宿鴉) of Nakhwaam, I wonder if you know.

Below the shade of Nakhwaam, the Sabisu River’s waves ripple.
Where have the three thousand court ladies, like flowers, gone?
Alas, I wonder if the crying cold crows (寒鴉) are their spirits.

Jackdaws cry and fly away at the sound of Koransa Temple’s bell.
Visiting the Stele of the Three Loyal Subjects (三忠碑), I meditate on old affairs,
And in the pine forest of Ch'ŏnjŏngdae, I shall beat my chest and weep.

Whether the clear breeze at Subukchŏng or the descending geese at Kuryongp'yŏng,
The ferry crossing at Kyuamjin is scenic and leisurely.
Fisherman in the evening glow, how about being my companion?

Paengma River flows, flows over twelve bends and more.
Give water to this land, pour new life into it!
Upon the crumbled old fortress site, I wish to build a new house.<79>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Sŏlkang is a melancholic reflection on a journey to Puyŏ, the last capital of the ancient Paekche kingdom. The poet visits historical sites like Mount Pusosan, Nakhwaam, Koransa Temple, and the Paengma River, evoking the tragic legend of the falling court ladies and mourning the kingdom's demise. The poem blends sorrow for the lost past with a concluding stanza expressing a strong desire for the land's renewal and the building of a "new house" on the ruins, hinting at hope for a revitalized future. From a colonial history perspective, the poem's lament for a lost Korean kingdom, juxtaposed with the final call for rebirth and rebuilding on "this land," is noteworthy. It reflects a common theme in colonial-era Korean literature: recalling past national glory and tragedy while subtly expressing aspirations for future restoration, potentially interpretable as national consciousness under Japanese rule.

夫餘行
						金雪崗
半月城 옛 성터에 남은 자최 그 무언고
扶蘇山 저믄 비는 궁녀들의 눈물인가
낙화암 저 宿鴉들아 너는 알까 하노라.

낙화암 그늘 아레 泗泚水 물껼 친다
꽃같은 삼천 궁녀 그 어드매 갓는고야
어즈버 우는 寒鴉가 그 넋인가 하노라.

皐蘭寺 종소리에 갈가마귀 울고 간다
三忠碑 찾아가서 옛일을 묵상하고
天政臺 松林 속에서 가슴 치고 울리라.

水北亭 청풍이나 九龍坪 落鴈이나
窺岩津 배나드리 운치 잇고 한가롭다
夕照에 고기 낙는이 날과 동모 어떠리.

白馬江 흘러흘러 열 두 구비 더도 흘러
이 땅에 물 주어라 새론 생명 부어주라
문허진 옛 성터 우에 새로운 집 짓과저.＜79＞
