**Body Text Translation:**
Humor

Honest Love
A woman deeply smitten with her husband (greeting him warmly as he returns from an outing at a summer resort): "You're back now! How delightful! We just heard a rumor that some fool fell off a cliff, and I was sure it was you!"

Clueless Person
Editor: "The content you wrote is fine, but it would be better if you wrote it more simply. So that even an ignorant person can understand it."
Writer: "What part couldn't *you* understand?"

Big Eater
In a certain village, young people gathered and held an eating contest. One among them ate three bowls of rice, ten *keun* (unit of weight, approx. 600g) of beef, and besides that, vegetables and what-not, gobbling it all down. Anyway, he became the unanimous 100 percent victor. Several people escorted this winner to his home, but as they reached the gate, the man lowered his voice and said to his friends, "Gentlemen, I have a favor to ask. Please, whatever you do, don't tell my wife today's story. If you tell her, I won't get any dinner tonight."

Indeed a Lawyer
A certain butcher ran furiously into a lawyer's office.
"Sir, if a dog stole meat, is the dog's owner responsible or not?"
Lawyer: "Of course, they are responsible."
Butcher: "Really! Well then, your dog just stole 50 *jeon* worth of meat, so what are you going to do?"
Lawyer: "If you pay just 50 *jeon* more, the appraisal fee will be settled."

A Religion That Can Be Used
Pastor: "Oswald, you offer prayers every night, don't you?"
The reply: "No, sir. I only do it when it's useful; what use is there in doing it every night?"

Saying One Thing and Then Another (一口二言)
The judge asked the female defendant her age.
"I am thirty."
"You've been saying you're thirty for three years now, what do you mean?"
"Yes, I am not the kind of person who says one thing today and another tomorrow."

Face (面相)
Once upon a time, a tiger came and carried off a man's father-in-law. The son-in-law, who frantically chased the tiger with his brother-in-law, saw the father-in-law's head, which the tiger had mostly devoured and left behind, and said to his brother-in-law,
"Father-in-law is playing *janggi* (Korean chess)."
"What do you mean?"
"He's already lost (ji-set-guna - sounds like 'checkmate' or 'lost the game')."
"What does *that* mean?"
"Only the face (myeonsang - 'face,' but also 'bishop' piece in Janggi) is left."

Sympathy (同情)
A certain woman applied to become a secretary, but her face wasn't pretty, and she looked quite old.
"By the way, how old might you be?"
"Yes," she replied, blushing slightly. "Since being born into this world, I have seen eighteen happy summers."
"Is that so? To think of the unhappy life you will lead from now on..." the gentleman sympathized.

The Groom's Duty
Future Mother-in-law: "Our child can do everything! She plays the dulcimer, the piano, and sings exceptionally well. She draws well too, and they say she skis all the time in winter. Lately, she even knows how to fly an airplane. Oh! And when did she learn to drive a car? By the way, what are *you* good at?"
Future Son-in-law: "Yes, I can do needlework and cook rice. I think I probably know how to sweep the *kudŭl* (underfloor heating) too. It seems I'll have to do it if no one else does!"

Memory
Wife: "It was a rainy day like this, wasn't it? The day you told me you loved me..."
Husband: "That's right. Ah, little did I know that day would become a terrible day for me..."
Wife: "?"

Then Tell Her Not to Wait
A country fellow, having come to London for the first time, said to a policeman,
"Look here. I've gotten separated from my wife just now. If she happens to pass by here, please tell her to wait a bit."
Policeman: "But how would I know who your wife is?"
Countryman: "Ah, that's true. Then please tell her not to wait."

An Examinee's Mind
It was time for the physical examination after the oral test finished. After measuring weight and height, it was the hearing test.
Examiner: "Can you hear this watch?"
Examinee: "Yes, I hear it clearly."
Examiner: "Take three steps back. Now, can you still hear it?"
Examinee: "Yes, of course I can."
Examiner: "You certainly have sharp ears! This watch has been dead for two weeks already."<41>

**Summary**

This article presents a series of twelve short jokes or humorous anecdotes from 1931 colonial Korea. The jokes cover themes like marital relationships (often depicting wives as shrewish or husbands as fearful/resigned), misunderstandings arising from literal interpretations or lack of common sense, professional stereotypes (lawyers, editors), social interactions (job interviews, dating), and encounters with authority or testing situations. They employ wordplay (like the *janggi* joke), irony, and situational humor.

For historians, this collection offers glimpses into everyday social attitudes and humor during the colonial period. The jokes reflect gender roles (e.g., the demanding mother-in-law, the wife controlling dinner, the age-sensitive female job applicant), professional stereotypes that were likely prevalent, anxieties (like failing exams), and perhaps a subtle commentary on urban vs. rural life (the London joke). The language itself, with its older spellings, is a valuable artifact of the time. The presence of Western elements like pianos, cars, airplanes, and London indicates cultural influx and awareness.

솔직한 사랑
남편에게 와짝 반한 녀자(피서지에서 남편이 어디 놀러 갓다가 오는 것을 반가이 마즈면서)「인재 오십니까! 참 기뼈! 우리는 지금 웬 바버가 절벽에서 떠러젓다는 소문을 듣고, 나는 꼭 그것이 당신인 줄 알앗서요!」

눈치 없는 사람
편집자 「글 쓰신 내용은 괜찬은데 좀 더 쉽게 쓰시면 좋겟소. 미욱한 사람이라도 알아볼 수 잇게.」
작가 「당신은 모를 데가 어대요?」

대식가
어떤 시골에 젊은 사람들이 모여서 음식 많이 먹기 내기를 하엿다. 그 중에 한 사람이 밥 세 그릇과 소고기 열 근, 그 밖에 채소니 무엇이니 하야 처먹는대, 여하간 만장일치로 100퍼센트의 승리자가 되엇다. 여러 사람들은 이 승리자를 자기 집까지 바라다 주엇는대 문 밖에 다다라서 이 자가 말소리를 낯후며 동무들에게 하는 말이 「제군, 부탁할 것이 잇는데, 제발 우리집 안사람 보고는 오늘 이야기를 말게. 그 말를 하면 저녁밥을 못 얻어먹겟으니.」

과연 변호사
어떤 고기장사가 화가 나서 변호사 사무소에 달려왓다.
「선생님, 개가 고기를 물어 갓으면 그 개 임자는 책임이 잇습니까. 없습니까.」
변호사 「물론 책임이 잇지오.」
고기 장사 「그래요! 그러면 이제 당신네 개가 50전자리 고기를 훔처 갓으니 어떠케 할테요?」
변호사 「당신이 50전만 더 내면 감정료는 셈이 됩니다.」

써 먹을 수 잇는 종교
목사 「오스왈드, 자네는 매일 밤 기도를 올리지?」
그 대답 「아닙니다. 소용 잇을 때나 하지, 매일 밤 무슨 소용이 잇어요」

一口二言
재판관이 여자 피고에게 나이를 무럿다.
「설흔살입니다.」
「그대는 3년 동안이나 같이 설흔살이라고 하니 웬 말이야?」
「네, 저는 오늘 이러케 말하고 내일 저러케 말하는 사람은 아닙니다.」

면상
옛날 한 곳에 범이 와서 장인을 물어 갓다. 처남과 같이 불이나게 범을 쪼차간 사위가 범이 다 뜨더 먹고 남은 장인의 머리를 보고 처남보고 하는 말이
「장인 장기를 두시는구나.」
「그것이 무슨 말이오?」
「벌서 지셋구나.」
「그건 또 무슨 말이오?」
「면상만 남앗구나.」

同情
어떤 여자가 비서가 되겟다고 지원하야 왓는데 얼굴도 이뿌지 못한데 나이도 많아 뵈엇다.
「그런데 네일양 나이가 멫이신지오?」
「네」하면서 양은 얼굴을 약간 붉히며 대답하얏다. 「저는 이 세상에 나서 행복스러운 여름을 열여덟 번 보앗서요.」
「그럿습니까. 이제부터 당신이 불행한 생활을 할 것을 생각하면.」하면서 신사는 동정하얏다.

신랑의 할 일
장래의 장모 「우리집 아이는 무엇이고 다 해요! 양금도 하고 피아노도 치고 성악도 썩 잘 한답니다. 그림도 잘 그리고 겨울에는 스켓이 일수라고들 해요. 요새는 비행기도 부릴 줄 안다고. 오라! 자동차 운전은 배운 지가 원젠데요? 그런데 당신은 무엇을 잘 하십니까?」
장래의 사위 「네, 저는 바느질도 하고 밥도 질 줄 압니다. 구둘 쓸 줄도 알 껏 같습니다. 아마 내가 하지 않으면 않되겟으니!」

기억
부인 「이러케 비오는 날이엇지오? 당신이 나를 사랑한다고 하신 날은...」
남편 「그럿소. 아 그 날이 나에게는 무서운 날이 될 줄이야...」
부인 「?」

그러면 기다리지 말라고
처음으로 「런든」에 온 시골 작자가 순사보고
「이거 보세요. 내가 지금 내 여편네하고 길이 어깃낫는데 혹 여기를 지나가면 좀 기다리고 잇으라고 해 주십시오.」
순사 「그러치만 어떠케 당신의 마누란지 알겟습니까.」
시골 사람 「참 그러쿠만요. 그러면 기다리지 말라고 하십시오.」

受驗者의 마음
구술 시험이 끝나고 신체검사를 할 때다. 중량과 신장을 다 재고 청력 시험인데
시험관 「이 시계 소리가 들립니까?」
수험자 「네 분명히 들립니다.」
시험관 「세 발자구만 뒤로 물러서시오. 자 지금도 들립니까?」
수험자 「네 들리고말고요.」
시험관 「참 귀가 밝은데! 이 시게가 죽은 지가 벌서 두 주일이나 되엇는데.」＜41＞
