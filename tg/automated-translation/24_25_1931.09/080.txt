Magazine Title: 동광 제25호
Publication Date: 1931년 09월 04일
Article Title: 관에 새긴 노래, 동지는 남아 잇다 갈 테이면 잘 가거라 (Song Carved on the Coffin, Comrades Remain, If You Must Go, Go Well)
Author: 李揆元
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

A sad day has come. A history written in the blood of a crumbling century.
The solemn dirge (輓歌) is buried in a tragic atmosphere,
As countless mourners (弔喪者) lament together.

The gaze that burned with devotion (殉情), the brilliant eloquence (輝辯) that rent the heart,
The face that succumbed to fatigue, the arms and legs that still ran about,
All this fragrant body of yours, this will of yours,
It was true then, that you fell, unable to speak.

Oh, breaking heart, do not be startled.
Where can we find again yesterday's street saint?
Ah, the closed eyes, the sealed lips,
The unmoving body, the corpse lying in the coffin.

Comrade, why did you go? Speak! Oh, heaven, oh earth,
Do you pretend not to know of so many things?
Today too, the armored cars of the armed cavalry
Stand guard, escorting the crushed corpse.

On the day you died, leaving behind the lingering regrets of your ambitions,
Your wife, who cried until her voice was hoarse, ran out to the street with her hair undone.
And the young child who called for his father,
Crying blood, was bound in chains.

My true comrade, my corpse,
With what shall I mourn you on this day?
Only, until my ten fingernails break, I carve upon the coffin wall (棺壁):
『Comrades remain. If you must go, go well.』<12>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Kyu-wŏn is a lament for a deceased comrade, likely someone involved in resistance or activism during the Japanese colonial period. The speaker expresses deep sorrow and anger over the comrade's death, remembering their passionate life and dedication. The poem vividly contrasts the comrade's silenced body with the ongoing oppression symbolized by armored cars and the suffering of the bereaved family (wife and child). Despite the grief, the final lines carved onto the coffin express defiance and solidarity, asserting that the cause continues ("Comrades remain") even in the face of loss. For historians, the poem offers a poignant glimpse into the emotional toll of colonial oppression, the strong bonds among activists ("comrades"), the brutal reality of suppression (implied arrest/death, family suffering, military presence), and the resilient spirit of resistance persisting even through death.

관에 새긴 노래, 동지는 남아 잇다 갈 테이면 잘 가거라
						李揆元
슬픈 날이 왓다. 묺어지는 세기의 피로 쓴 역사.
엄숙한 輓歌는 비장한 분위기에 파묻혀
수많은 弔喪者가 통공을 하는구나.

殉情에 타오르든 시선 가슴을 쩌개 놓든 輝辯
피곤에 못이겨 하든 얼골 그래도 뛰어 단니든 팔과 다리
이 모든 향기로운 네 몸이 네 뜻이
말 못하고 쓰러저 버렷다 함이 참말이엇구나.

메어지는 가슴아, 놀라지 마라.
어젯날 거리의 성자 어디가 다시 찾으리
아하 감은 눈이여 다므른 입쑬이어
움지기지 않는 몸이어. 관 쏙에 누은 시체여.

동무야, 웨 갓느냐, 말 좀 해라 하늘아 땅아
그러케 많은 일을 모르는 척 한단 말이냐.
오늘도 무장기병대의 철갑 자동차는
무즈러진 시체를 호위하면서 느러섯다.

네가 품은 뜻의 미련을 남기고 죽든 날
목 쉬게 울든 안해는 머리 푼 채 거리로 뛰어 갓다.
그리고 아버지를 부르든 어린 자식은
피 물고 부르짖다가 쇠사슬에 얽히웟다.

진실한 동무야, 나의 시체여
내 이 날 무엇으로 그대를 弔喪할껀가
다만 열 손톱이 끊어지도록 棺壁에 새기나니
『동지는 남아 잇다. 갈 테이면 잘 가거라.』＜12＞
