Article Title: 平和鳥 (Peace Bird)
Author: 宋順鎰 (Song Sun-il)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Peace Bird
						Song Sun-il

Birds playing in the deep mountains,
All are peace birds.
Even when the cruel prankster
Aims his gun barrel,
They know no fear,
Only beautiful songs...

Where people go,
I wish only for peace,
But singing until now,
They bleed and die.
Useless for medicine,
And not for eating...

Thoughtless prankster,
Please put away your gun barrel.
Though I cannot be like Saint Francis,
Who preached so eloquently,
Please, at least do not startle
The peace birds of the deep mountains.
1931.8.4. At Sudo Hermitage <26>

**Summary**

This poem contrasts the innocent "peace birds" living undisturbed in the mountains with the senseless violence inflicted upon them by a human "prankster" with a gun. The poet laments that these birds, symbols of peace and harmless beauty, are killed for no reason, as they are neither edible nor medicinal. The speaker expresses a desire for peace among humans and pleads for the birds to be left alone, invoking Saint Francis as an exemplar of peaceful coexistence with nature. For historians, the poem offers insight into the period's atmosphere, expressing a poignant longing for peace and a subtle critique of violence and thoughtlessness, potentially reflecting anxieties under colonial rule. The gentle plea against disturbing the "peace birds" could symbolize a broader desire to protect innocence and tranquility in a troubled time.

平和鳥
						宋順鎰
깊은 산에 노니는 새
모도 다 平和鳥.
모질은 장난꾼
총때를 겨누어도
무서운 줄 모르고
아름다운 노래만...

사람의 가는 곧에
平和이어 바라리만
이재껏 노래타가
피 흘리고 죽다니
약에도 못쓰고
먹지도 않을 것을...

부질없은 장난꾼
총때를 걷어 주오.
날새게도 설교하든
성「프란쓰」는 못 되어도
깊은 산 平和鳥
놀래지나 말아주오.
1931.8.4. 於修道菴＜26＞
