Article Title: 아들아! 무서워 마라 (Son! Do Not Be Afraid)

Son! Do Not Be Afraid
						Hwang Sunwŏn

On a deep night when a storm shook the earth (地動치는),
As the father held his son on his back
And waited for the mother,
Through the courtyard enveloped in a dark curtain,
A bizarre object passed.
Oh, Father! Let's go inside.
Isn't a fearsome demon hiding in the front yard,
Spying on us?
Son! Do not be afraid.
That is merely a cat's mischief.

But the child, gripped by terror,
Clung tightly to his father's back.
While worrying about the mother not coming,
What his hand suddenly felt
Was the father's feebly beating heart.
Oh, Father! Look at this.
Why is your chest nothing but bones,
And why aren't the blood vessels, which should beat strongly, beating?
Son! Do not worry.
Inside, only living blood pulses.

The father, still carrying his son,
To find the mother <81>
Into the pouring rain,
Walked forward,
When suddenly, the father's legs trembled powerlessly.
Oh, Father! I understand.
Please put me down from your back.
I am not afraid of anything.
Son! Beloved son!
Carry me, I want to be carried on your sturdy back. <82>

**Summary**

The poem depicts a conversation between a father and son during a stormy night. Initially, the father reassures his frightened son, dismissing perceived threats. However, the son soon senses the father's own fear and physical weakness – his frail body and trembling legs. This realization prompts the son to declare his own bravery and, in a poignant reversal of roles, the father asks his son to carry him. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, might interest historians as a potential allegory. The father's hidden fear and frailty despite his protective stance could symbolize the weakened state of the older generation or the nation itself under colonial rule, while the son's emergent strength and willingness to support the father could represent hope placed in the younger generation or a desire for renewed national vigor.

아들아! 무서워 마라
						黃順元
폭풍우가 地動치는 깊은 밤에
아버지가 자식을 업고
어머니를 기다리고 잇을 때
암흑한 장막이 휩싸인 뜰 가운데로
기괴한 물건이 지나갓다.
오 아버지! 들어갑시다.
무서운 악귀가 뜰 앞에 숨어
우리들을 엿보고 잇지 안씁니까.
아들아! 무서워 마라
그것은 고양이의 장난 뿐이다

그러나 자식은 공포에 싸이어
어버이의 등에 밧삭 붙어서
아니오는 어머니로 걱정할 때에
언 듯 자식의 손에 감촉된 것은
어버이의 긔운 없이 뛰는 심장이엇다
오, 아버지! 이것 보세요
어찌하여 가슴은 뼈만 남앗고
높이 뛰어야 할 혈맥이 뛰지 않겟습니까?
아들아! 염려마라
그 속에는 산 피만이 뛸 뿐이다.

아버지는 자식을 업은 채로
어머니를 찾으려＜81＞
쏘다지는 소낙비 속으로
앞으로 거러갈 때
문득 아버지의 두 다리는 힘없이 떨렷다.
오, 아버지! 알앗소이다
저를 아버지 등에서 내려 주소서.
이 저는 아모 것도 무섭지 안습니다.
아들아! 사랑하는 아들아!
나를 업어라, 억세인 너의 등에 업히고 십다.＜82＞
＜81-82＞
