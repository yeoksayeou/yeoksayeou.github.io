Faint Chirping of Waiting Insects
						By Yi T'ak

An Evening Scene (夕陽卽事)
"I'll finish weeding the last furrows, you go on inside,"
Voices drift affectionately from the field's edge at sunset.
Soon, blue smoke rises from the house below the mountain.

To My Elder Brother (己兄에게) (Hearing of the turmoil in North Manchuria 北滿)
Storms rage on all sides; how fares your house, my brother?
Day darkens on the vast plains; your steps must be pitiable.
Rather than burn with anxiety, I wish I could bring your daughter here.

When I sigh, you are already in tears;
When you beat the rhythm, I naturally dance.
The past seems like a dream, hazy like mist.

Can a heart once white as snow become like mud?
A breast that burned like fire has cooled like ashes.
Touching upon the old days now and then, I shed tears alone.<98>

Impromptu (卽事)
On the path over the knoll, beneath the willow, how lovely the small child!
Stopping their play-cooking, they bow their head as if understanding.
"How old are you?" "Seven," they reply.

The rice seedlings transplanted just the other day, already above the ridge,
Whisper together like an affectionate couple weeding.
Surely, they discuss in advance the things to come after departure.

On the Way Up Yongmun Mountain (龍門山 오르는 길에) (Looking at obscured Panrobong Peak (番爐峰) hidden in mist)
Coming to see the longed-for one, you spread out a field of mist.
Spreading, then clearing again, clearing, then spreading anew.
My heart grows ever more anxious, as if the solemn visage might soon appear.

Trial Translation (試譯)
Crossing the stream, I find flowers of unknown name.
Plucking a flower to hold, I cross another stream.
Before I knew it, I had arrived at your home.

(Original Poem 原時) Spring Day (春日) By Gao Qi (高啓) of Tang (唐)
Crossing water, again crossing water, Seeing flowers, again seeing flowers.
On the spring wind road along the river, Unknowingly, I arrive at your house.<99>

**Summary**

This collection of short poems by Yi T'ak presents vignettes of rural life, personal reflection, and concern for family amidst turmoil. The poems capture scenes like evening farm work, children playing, and the changing landscape, alongside deeper emotions of longing, anxiety about a brother in turbulent North Manchuria, nostalgia for past closeness, and the anticipation of seeing a mist-shrouded mountain peak. The final piece is a translation of a classic Chinese poem, linking the Korean poetic practice to the broader East Asian tradition. For historians, the poem explicitly mentioning the "turmoil in North Manchuria (北滿)" in 1931 provides a personal, emotional glimpse into how events in Manchuria, a region of significant conflict and migration involving Koreans during the colonial period, affected individuals and families within Korea. The sense of separation and anxiety is palpable.

候蟲微吟
						李鐸
夕陽卽事
남은사래 내매오리 그댈랑 들어가소
석양 밭머리에 다정하게 들리놋다
이윽고 산밑집에서 푸른 연기나더라

己兄에게(北滿의 소란함을 듣고)
사방에 풍우치니 님의 집 어이된고
광야에 날점으니 님의 걸음 애처럽다.
차라리 애태우느니 님의 딸어 예고저

내가 한숨지면 님은 벌서 눈물이라
님이 장단치건 나는 절로춤이러니
지난일 꿈결이런 듯 안개자국 합니다.

눈같이 히던마음 진흙같이 된다말가
불같이 타던가슴 재같이 식엇고야
예나를 때로 만지며 홀로 눈물 집니다.＜98＞

卽事
둔덕길 버들아래 어여쁠손 어린아이
솟굽질 하다말고 안다는 듯 머리숙여
네나이 몇살이더니? 일곱이요 하더라

엊그제 옴긴 모가 어느결에 두렁위를
다정한 부부인듯 기음매며 속삭인다.
아모도 갈뒤엣일을 미리의론 하고녀

龍門山 오르는 길에(안개에나 숨기는 番爐峰 바라보며)
그리던님 뵈려오니 안개밭을 늘이시네
늘이다 되걷히고 걷히는듯 다시늘여
莊嚴相의 하마 뵈올 듯 가슴더욱 졸려라

試譯
시내를 건너서니 이름모를 꽃이로세
꽃을 딸어예니 또한내를 건넛고야
어느덕 다 달어보니 님의 집에 왓서라

(原時) 春日  唐 高啓
渡水復渡水 看花還看花
春風江上路 不覺到君家＜99＞
