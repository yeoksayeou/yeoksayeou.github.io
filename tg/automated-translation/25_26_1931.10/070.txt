How Should We Utilize the Pacific Conference? How Must Koreans in Manchuria Live?
						Various Individuals (In Order of Manuscript Arrival)
Special Feature
Undoubtedly, one of the matters of greatest interest among Chosŏn's intellectuals today is the issue of Koreans in Manchuria (在滿同胞 問題). We have already repeatedly discussed this issue in our pages, but we believe there is a need for even more vigorous debate going forward. Therefore, we present this special feature to gather the esteemed opinions of various experts as a reference for that discussion. Additionally, the Pacific Conference issue has become a point of contention, triggered by the outcry of opposition from the Chinese civilian side. Since October is the month the conference is scheduled, it undoubtedly draws our interest. Thus, we have collected observations from various figures on these two questions:
1. How should we utilize the Pacific Conference?
2. How must Koreans in Manchuria live?
We express our gratitude to the many respected figures who sent replies amidst their busy schedules.

Song Chin-u, President, Tong'a Ilbo Sa

I. International Advancement
If the Pacific Conference is an international and national gathering, Chosŏn, by its rights as a nation (民族), has the right to participate fair and square in that conference, and indeed should participate. If it is a gathering of states, then the question of participation does not even arise, but when we participate as one nation (民族), we need to use every opportunity to make our culture and everything else known internationally.
II. The Urgent Task is an Organized Body
The primary way for Koreans in Manchuria to survive is the formation of a solid organization by the Koreans in Manchuria themselves. This organization should, of course, be established within the scope permitted by Chinese law (中國國法), and through this organization, they must strive to achieve all necessary demands for the survival and development of the Korean nation (朝鮮民族) in Manchuria from the Chinese government and other relevant governments. Only then will the Nationality Law (國籍法) become an issue, but above all, creating an organization that the Koreans in Manchuria themselves can establish is most necessary. (Responsibility for text lies with the reporter)

Author

I. The Conference OOOO. [Likely 'excludes' or 'limits']
Since the Pacific Conference is a conference body of strong nations (强大民族) and weak nations (弱小民族), it is inherently difficult for it to protect the unique interests of weak nations. Furthermore, since the Kyoto Conference, it has been based on a state standard (國家標準), not a national standard (民族標準), and denying speaking rights to weak nations (植民地人 - colonized people) <13> testifies to the conference's reactionary turn. Therefore, it goes without saying that we cannot place great expectations on this conference. However, as the conference has emphasized humanitarianism and has some track record in the past, it seems quite necessary to at least make Chosŏn's voice heard. To make the situation of Koreans known globally, utilizing all opportunities is advisable.
II. Acquiring Naturalization Rights is Absolutely Necessary
Regarding the issue of Koreans being pushed out of Manchuria (排滿同胞問題), firstly, the nationality issue (國籍問題) (naturalization rights issue - 歸化權 問題) must be resolved. To extinguish the urgent fire of expulsion and oppression, one cannot bear the name of being an imperialist vanguard (帝國主義前鋒). Especially for the vast majority of Koreans residing in the interior of Manchuria (滿洲奧地), naturalization (歸化) is an absolutely necessary condition. This has already become public opinion (與論化), but there needs to be stronger and more earnest assertion and campaigning. Secondly, there is the economic issue of alleviating the poverty of Koreans in Manchuria; this seems achievable only when the grand unity (大同團結) of Koreans in Manchuria is presupposed. That is, plans can only be accomplished by awaiting the establishment of a Korean autonomous body (朝鮮人自治機關), so I believe the grand unity of Koreans in Manchuria themselves is the most urgent task (先急務).

Yi In, Lawyer

I. Disapprove of a Representative Conference
I cannot approve of the so-called Pacific Conference which represents capitalism as it has been conventionally. I wish there were an institution that reviews and studies everything from the most liberal standpoint, transcending all things.
II. Formation of an Autonomous Territory is Necessary
Neither the issue of naturalization (入籍) nor the right to lease land for commerce (商租權) can be resolved. If an autonomous territory (自治領) were established through a general vote of the residents, they would be able to live.

Yi Sun-t'ak, Professor, Yŏnhŭi College (延專敎授)

I. Oppose a Narrow-Minded Conference
If the Pacific Conference does not grant Chosŏn the independent status of a representative dispatching country (代表 파견국), there will be issues with delegate selection, and the activities of selected delegates will also be greatly restricted. Consequently, even if many facts about Chosŏn are fabricated (虛構), they cannot be properly clarified. In that case, it might be better for us to take a stance of opposing the Pacific Conference and even launch an opposition movement.
II. Naturalization Rights are the Prior Issue
In a word, Koreans in Manchuria must obtain naturalization rights (歸化權). If they fail to obtain this right, even after several hundred years, they will always tread the same path, the problem will not dissipate, and the lives of Koreans will be like water weeds (水草). Therefore, they must push forward to obtain this right. For this, I believe a committee (會) should be formed, and that committee should lobby the Japanese government on one hand, and the National Government (國民政府) or Chinese representative bodies (中國代表機關) on the other.

Professor, Sungsil College (崇專 敎授)

I. Need for Global Advancement as a Nation
Since the Pacific Conference itself is a private organization (私的 機關), if possible, we, as one nation on the Pacific Rim, should participate to learn about others' issues and also inform them about our issues. <14>
II. Grand Unity is Necessary
(a) Koreans in Manchuria should, as much as possible, naturalize (入籍) in China and enjoy all rights enjoyed by Chinese citizens, starting with land ownership rights (上地所有權).
(b) It is said that they should unconditionally discard differences in principles and assertions (一節 主義 主張의 異同) and realize grand unity (大同團結) as Koreans. Of course, due to limited space, I will wait for a future opportunity to state specific opinions.

Sŏ Sang-il, Taegu

The Prior Issue is a Central Organization in the Homeland
Replying to your company's two major survey questions, they are timely issues. If I were to speak about these two issues collectively, whether utilizing the Pacific Conference or enabling Koreans in Manchuria to survive, the fundamental prior issue is whether a solution beyond idealism, containing execution, can possibly be stated only based on the existence or absence of a national central organization (國民的 中心機關), which is the collective body of public pressure from the twenty million masses in Chosŏn (在鮮 이천만 민중). Chosŏn's elders and leaders need the determination and resolution to first foster an authoritative national central force (國民的 中心力) capable of utilizing the Pacific Conference and saving the Koreans in Manchuria.

Chosŏn Ilbo Sa

I. Exposure of the Representative Body
The question "How should we utilize the Pacific Conference?" is merely viewed as an objective issue from the other side, rather than a subjective one for us. This is because we originally did not expect any political significance from the Pacific Conference.
Looking at the original form of the Pacific Conference, it originates from the Institute of Pacific Relations (太平洋問題硏究會, also called 太平洋國交討論會 or 太平洋國際學會, translations of the original name 'The Institute of Pacific Relations') headquartered in Hawaii (布哇). It gathered representatives from various countries along the Pacific Rim with the stated purpose of "studying the conditions of the Pacific peoples and thereby promoting the improvement of their mutual relations." Under this objective, three sessions have already been held, and the fourth was scheduled to open in Hangzhou (抗州), China, from October to November this year. Originally, a research institution's function differs from a political one, being nothing more than a desk-bound banquet of discussions among a few scholars. However, this Pacific Conference is truly deformed (畸形的), not purely a gathering of scholars pursuing truth, nor a gathering of politicians. It merely attempts some unnatural harmonizing function in the buffer zone between XX-ism [likely Imperialism] and weak nations. Therefore, they initially organized it by national units (民族單位), but realizing it was an opportunity for XX-ist [Imperialist] exposure, they declared the abolition of the national unit system. The third conference held in Kyoto in 1929 opposed the national unit and adopted the state unit (國家單位), thereby stripping membership rights from nations in XX-ist [Imperialist] territories, eloquently proving this point. Of course, the fourth conference not only maintained the previous policy but also inevitably adopted the refusal of national rights (民族權) as its guiding principle for the future, thus harboring an inherent conflict of interest that cannot be reconciled. Therefore, in China, which is in a semi-colonial state (半植民地狀態), except for some lukewarm Christians with self-contradictory views of salvation, the Kuomintang (國民黨), along with the Asia Culture Association (亞洲文化協會), the Oriental Affairs Research Society (東方問題硏究會), and many other groups, initiated a fierce movement opposing the Pacific Conference. They attacked the Chinese representative, Mr. Ch'en Li-yen (陳立延氏 - likely romanization of Chinese name), with the stigma of "XX-ist [Imperialist] running dog (走拘)," and Mr. Yü Jih-chang (余日章氏 - likely romanization of Chinese name) even resigned as a director of the Pacific Conference. The Oriental Affairs Research Society in Peiping (北平) sent its chairman, Mr. Fang Chi-sheng (方濟生氏 - likely romanization of Chinese name), south to campaign in various places. He joined forces with various organizations in Nanjing (南京), Hangzhou (杭州), and Shanghai (上海) to oppose the conference and even demanded the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (外交部) ban the Hangzhou opening. Mr. Fang stated the reasons for opposition as follows:
"The Pacific Conference was originally organized by Christians, the XX unit [likely vanguard or tool] of XX-ism [Imperialism], and gradually <15> became a complete mouthpiece for XX-ists [Imperialists]. To prevent the voice of the weak nations, who constitute the vast majority of the population along the Pacific Rim, they refused participation by representatives of weak nations. They changed the initial regulations defining membership eligibility simply as 'nation' (民族) to 'state' (國家), allowing only independent states to participate, thus exposing its XXXX [likely Imperialist] nature. Towards weak nations, they obfuscate with XX [likely paternalistic or oppressive] theories and representing their XX [likely exploitative] rationality is the mission (便命) of the Pacific Conference. This not only hinders the XX [likely liberation or progress] of the Chinese nation but is also a great harm to all Eastern nations and even world peace."
If the Chinese debunk it thus, we can have no expectations for this conference, and there is no reason why our demand for utilization would be accepted. Only on the day when a genuine assembly is held where Koreans can truly speak without, as Lenin said, being "XX's [Imperialism's] horses," will we be able to recognize its significance.
II. If Nothing Else, At Least National Understanding
Compared to the Pacific Conference issue, the second question, "How must Koreans in Manchuria live?" is truly a pressing issue for us. As this issue has been discussed from various angles for a long time, stating my humble opinion would just be redundant. However, the basic condition for a nation to live happily requires, first, the establishment of that nation's XX rights [likely sovereign/self-determination rights]. Second to that is the establishment of genuine systems within that nation. And the establishment of internal national systems is profoundly affected by the presence or absence of the primary basis, national XX rights. If Russia were not externally an autonomous state (自主國), neither the revolution nor the Five-Year Plan could have been achieved. However, the XX [likely loss] of our XX rights [sovereignty] has XXXX [paralyzed?] our entire existence. Those unfortunate individuals, unable to sustain themselves due to political XX [oppression?] and economic XX [exploitation?], drifted to Manchuria like rootless duckweed (浮萍草). Citizens of autonomous states (自主國) are emigrated with state power, receive subsidies for pioneering virgin land, and obtain guarantees for life and property, but what is the situation for XXX [colonized people]? The recent clash between Koreans and Chinese (朝中人 충돌사건) that erupted within Chosŏn was not simply triggered by the Wanpaoshan Incident (萬寶山事件) but was the action of crowds enraged by the news of expulsions and mistreatment of compatriots in various parts of Manchuria, which had been arriving one after another for years. Chinese authorities, who had already been secretly plotting the expulsion of Koreans as one method for recovering national sovereignty (國權回收), are now trying to establish a firm plan using this incident as an opportunity. According to a recent news report, one clause among the countermeasures against Japan (對日辦法) proposed and concluded in the Legislative Yuan testifies to this. That is, land, houses, and money should not be lent to Koreans or Japanese, and what has already been lent must be recovered within the shortest possible period, and those who secretly comply (은밀에 付하는 者) will be punished. This signifies the actualization of expulsion beyond the propaganda stage and is highly noteworthy. Henceforth, the problem of Koreans in Manchuria (在萬 同胞問題) will become even more difficult. Particularly since Chosŏn, although historically significant, is seen from the perspective of diplomacy with Japan as having an inseparable connection with Japan, the huge wave of anti-Japanese sentiment (排日) indirectly affects Koreans in Manchuria as well. However, the key to resolving the issue cannot be sought from or relied upon XXXXXXXXX [likely Japanese authorities or imperialism]. We must directly engage with the Chinese people (中國民), understand each other, and clearly make them recognize the distinct reality of our nation. We must make them recognize that the vast majority of us are not running dogs (走狗) of XX-ism [Imperialism] or XX Party [likely Communist Party?] but are simple farmers legitimately demanding their minimum right to exist. This is an urgent method that general intellectuals must take upon themselves as their greatest mission. Next, the naturalization issue (歸化問題), discussed from the perspective of common law theory, is also important. Although naturalization is nothing more than a greatly shameful act from the perspective of national relations, it is undoubtedly one method for people without sovereignty (自主權) to maintain their existence. It is truly lamentable to think that even this shameful act of acceptance (羞恥的 甘受行動) cannot be done solely by our own will.

Kim Chang-hwan, Tong'a Ilbo Sa

I. Only Opposition Remains
Although representatives of the Korean nation (朝鮮民族) participated in the 1st and 2nd conferences, they saw no particular effect, <16> and at the 3rd conference, their qualification to participate was even revoked on the grounds that they were not an independent country. In any case, this conference was established for the benefit of powerful states, with the XX [likely US and UK or Japan] countries leading it. In China, the Kuomintang (國民黨) and various other groups are fiercely opposing this conference, calling it XX-ism [Imperialism] that XX [oppresses] the oppressed nations. Even though they are a semi-independent country, they seem to have keenly felt the sorrow of weak nations at this conference. Therefore, for us, the Korean nation, the question of how to utilize this conference is not even an issue; since we are being utilized, I believe it is necessary to clearly state our stance of opposition (排擊).
II. The Fundamental Solution is Acquiring Naturalization Rights
Wherever even a few Koreans settle together, there (Omission - Original Text: 中略-원문) This causes great pain to the Chinese people today, who are fervent about revising unequal treaties and abolishing extraterritoriality (治外法權). Thus, Korean immigration inevitably becomes a thorn in their side. Also, due to a few elements (멫 分子), there seems to be a strong tendency to view Koreans as the vanguard (前哨) of OOOO-ism [likely Imperialism] and reject them. Therefore, we Koreans should try our best not to receive (Omission - Original Text: 中略) even if there is some interference and persecution (驅迫) from the Chinese side, but rather obey well, resolve misunderstandings, and strive to promote friendship. On the Japanese government side, some argue that resolving the issue of commercial leasing rights (商租權問題) is a prerequisite for solving the Korean problem, but there is no reason for the Chinese side, fervent about extraterritoriality, to accept this. And even if they did accept it, it would provide no convenience whatsoever to Koreans engaged in farming in the interior (奧地). Then, the only remaining fundamental solution is the acquisition of naturalization rights (귀화권 획득). That is, the issue of Koreans naturalizing in China (中國에 入籍). No matter how noisy and complicated the problem is, the National Government (國府), realizing that they cannot expel all Korean immigrants, seems to have considered this naturalization rights issue as a fundamental countermeasure. However, this involves the issue of dual nationality (2重國籍 問題). The Japanese Nationality Law (日本國籍法) must be revised. This is a difficult problem.

Kim Kyŏng-chae, Author

I. Rather than Utilizing, I Oppose it.
II. Naturalization and Formation of Autonomous Bodies
The issue of Koreans in Manchuria will likely move in tandem with the overall issue concerning the Far East. However, as passive strategies for now:
1. Naturalize in China.
2. Koreans in Manchuria should form autonomous bodies focusing on industry and education.

Paek Kwan-su

I. Membership Rights are the Issue
Our position regarding the Pacific Conference is such that, according to the so-called constitution of that conference, we likely cannot obtain full membership rights. Therefore, whether we participate in the conference depends on the decision of the conference's steering committee (理事會). Thus, for us to utilize that conference, we must first revise its constitution, or if participation is decided by unanimous agreement of the steering committee which convenes one week before each general meeting, then I believe we must resolutely attend.
II. The Issue of Koreans in Manchuria
I will give this special consideration and speak about it later. <17>

Yi Mu-gi, Hamhŭng

I. As an Opportunity to Arouse Public Opinion
It is necessary to be active directly and indirectly.
II. Mutual Friendship and Naturalization
Since Manchuria is the territory of a different people (異民族), the tolerance of that people and the protection of the authorities are necessary. The first strategy should be to promote mutual friendship (互相親和), and the final policy would be naturalization (入籍).

OOO, Professor, Yŏnhŭi College (延專 敎授)

I. Introduction of Our Current Situation
Since the Pacific Conference should not be overestimated originally, just provide materials to introduce our current situation and make it public.
II. Grand Understanding Among Ourselves
Regarding Koreans in Manchuria, recently the Northeast [Chinese] side seems to be using divisive tactics (骨肉計), and the South Manchuria Railway (滿鐵) side distinguishes between the protected zone along the railway line (沿線保護區) and the area outside the line (線外區). Ultimately, it seems preferable to view these two areas separately. Thus, it appears that the Koreans in one area, the Nationalist Party (民族黨) side and the compatriots in the railway zone, are barely the subject of discussion regarding Koreans in Manchuria. Regarding this, I think the means of naturalization (入籍手段) might be acceptable for now. Taking it a step further, if leading figures in Manchuria could gather in places like Shanghai and achieve a grand understanding (大同的 理解) among themselves, there might be possibilities for various strategies.

Pak Wan, Ch'ŏndogyo

I. International Introduction of Chosŏn's Situation
Whatever the nature of the Pacific Conference gathering, our purpose is surely to try utilizing it for our objectives, right? In that sense, having representatives based on the nation (民族本位) attend to publicize all our current irrational circumstances to the politicians and scholars of various countries, striving to make them understand and sympathize with us, is also necessary. However, to utilize it, we must first strive for the attendance of national representatives.

II. National Cooperation
1. XX's [Japan's?] extraterritoriality must be abolished, and the dual nationality problem must disappear.
2. Communists and Nationalists must cooperate from the same national standpoint and cease factional strife (派爭).

Chosŏnŏ Hakhoe (Korean Language Society)

I. The Pacific Conference
Is not a matter of concern for us.

II. Special Establishment of a Korean Department
For Koreans in Manchuria, (1) the nationality issue must be resolved.
(2) Koreans should have local autonomous bodies (地方自治機關), and Chinese government offices (中國官廳) should specially establish a Korean department (朝鮮人部) to facilitate mutual communication. <18>

Yi Hyŏng-jae, Masan

I. Useless for Weak Nations
Conferences like the Pacific Conference are based on the interests of XX-ists [Imperialists], so they likely offer no practical benefit whatsoever to weak nations. Moreover, since they do not grant us Koreans equal rights, I believe the issue of utilization does not arise.

II. Naturalization Issue is the Most Urgent Task
The issue of Koreans in Manchuria is indeed a grave matter, but for us lacking political and economic sovereignty, I feel there is no other way but to rely on the protection of Chinese officials and civilians (中國官民). To receive their protection, I believe resolving the naturalization issue (入籍問題) is the most urgent task.

Ch'oe Wŏn-sun, Kwangju

I. Understanding the Situation of Various Countries
Whenever permitted, accurately conveying our current situation to the personnel of various countries to ensure their understanding, while also understanding the situations of those nations, it is advisable for many individuals proficient in languages and possessing insight to participate in that conference. Especially since the political and economic agendas of independent nations are scheduled for this conference, I earnestly hope for the attendance of many individuals.
II. Unified Organization and Naturalization
1. We must first have a unified organization there and undergo training under fixed control.
2. Naturalize in China (provided the law allows leaving Japanese nationality - 日本國籍).
3. Build an economic foundation for living (taking a long-term perspective).

Ham Sang-hun, Tong'a Ilbo Sa

I. Assertion of Membership Rights
It is absolutely unacceptable for the Pacific Conference, aimed at the cultural development of the peoples of the Pacific Rim, to recognize membership rights based on political units like sovereign states or autonomous dominions. It was already a mistake for the Chosŏn representative to sit idly by when such a charter was established, but even now, using the membership rights obtained at the first meeting as a shield, we should assert our membership rights.
II. First, a Large Organization
First, the Koreans in Manchuria themselves should form a large organization (團體). With this as the center, they should demand wholesale naturalization (一切 歸化) from the National Government (國民政府) and the Northeast Authorities (東北當局). In places where many live densely clustered, they should demand autonomy (自治). From the homeland (內地), another large organization should negotiate with the Chinese Government and Authorities (中國政府 及 當局) to enable detachment from Japanese nationality and allow becoming naturalized Chinese citizens (中國 歸化民).

Kim Tong-jin, Tong'a Ilbo Sa

I. A Support Organization for XX-ism [Imperialism]
Given that one of the important items on the agenda for this year's Pacific Conference is the political, economic, and social problems of non-independent peoples residing on the Pacific Rim, yet this conference has decided not to permit independent participation by representatives of the Korean nation, it is a support organization for XX-ism [Imperialism]. Therefore, participation by Chosŏn, a colonial nation, would likely yield little gain, and consequently, there might be no way to utilize it. <19>
II. Displaying the Strength of Koreans in Manchuria
Koreans in Manchuria have no choice but to display transcendent strength (超絶的 力量) and carve out their own path through their own efforts. Their situation has become exactly like that of the Jews in ancient times (往昔의 猶太人 처지), so extraordinary effort is required.

Song Pong-u, P'ip'ansa (Critique Publishing Company)

I. The Pacific Conference should not be utilized but rejected (拒斥).
II. There is a way for Koreans in Manchuria to live, but I will not speak of it.

Yi Chŏng-byŏn (?)

I. Propaganda of Chosŏn's Unique Culture
It is almost no exaggeration to say that the Pacific Conference and the Chosŏn issue have almost no relation. However, does that mean it cannot be utilized? Perhaps not.
(1) As it is an international gathering, participate as much as possible to impress the consciousness of the Korean nation's existence upon the Pacific nations.
(2) Publicize the unique culture of the Korean nation (朝鮮民族의 고유문화) to display the nation's value.
II. Only Naturalization Exists
There is only naturalizing in China (中國에 入籍). Koreans in Manchuria must fight for the Government-General (總督府) to quickly implement the Nationality Law (國籍法). You ask how Koreans can become Chinese? It may be regrettable. But since they went to Manchuria seeking life XXXXX [despite hardships?], they must live first. What use is there in clinging to such a homeland (故國) that says 'I don't care if you starve to death'? Is there a law somewhere stating that Koreans must not become Chinese? To be loyal to Chosŏn, one should naturalize in China in the sense of implanting Korean influence (朝鮮人 세력) in Manchuria.

Hyŏn Sang-yun

I. If the Initiators Lack Representative Status
It seems there are groups in China now who call the Pacific Conference a mouthpiece for XX-ism [Imperialism] and absolutely oppose participation, but perhaps we Koreans cannot even do that. China's refusal to participate in the Pacific Conference would be quite regrettable, but Chosŏn's refusal to participate would cause no reaction whatsoever. Moreover, last time they stated Chosŏn would not be considered a formal representative. In my opinion, it might be wiser for us to use such opportunities instead to advance into international conferences and express our national assertions (民族的 主張) to the extent possible. While asserting that we initiated the conference and thus naturally have representative status, we should also insist that other non-sovereign nations residing on the Pacific Rim be permitted to participate, propose research into the various national issues that possess inherent conflict among sovereign states, and expose the sore spots of XX-ism [Imperialism].
II. Establishment of a Permanently Neutral State
It might be a wise strategy for Koreans in Manchuria (在滿 朝鮮人) to unite as one group (一團) and simultaneously, in the same written language (同文), propose the establishment of a permanently neutral state (永世中立國 建立) to both Japan and China (日中 兩國). China will likely oppose it first, but since we have prior settlement rights (先住權) and the merit of land reclamation (開墾의 功勞), we cannot unconditionally withdraw. Also, Japan will likely not permit this, so constructing a buffer zone (緩衝地帶) to avoid Japanese invasion would be beneficial for China. It may seem like a far-fetched opinion (迀濶한 소견), but depending on the determination and effort of the Korean people, might its realization not be impossible? <20>

Sŏl Ŭi-sik, Tong'a Ilbo Sa

I. Let's Just Watch
At one time, we in Chosŏn attended with considerable expectations, but the world well knows the results were unimpressive. It's not useful to us, but even if it were useful, what special means could there be in a conference that lacks binding power over participating states or nations? Let's just set it aside (置之度外) and watch. If we were forced to utilize it minimally, perhaps publicize "Chosŏn's True Situation (朝鮮의 實情)"? As a break-even proposition (밋저야 本錢格)-.
II. Abandoning Mutual Trampling
The issue is too vague. Also, as your company's inquiry seems not to want lengthy discussions, I will list a few "tedious items."
1. First, get out of the situation of being caught between rocks.
2. Recognize China's sovereignty (主權) and abide by its national laws (國法).
3. Abandon mutual trampling (自相踐踏) and strive for mutual concession and cooperation (互讓協同).
Even doing this doesn't guarantee living well. Still less can one guarantee living well without doing this.

Kim Myŏng-sik, Osaka

I. Culturally, Perhaps
The Pacific Conference is a target worthy of utilization, and since it can be utilized, it seems no one would object to utilizing it. However, the Pacific Conference lacks content suitable for political utilization. Cultural and academic exchange is not without meaning, but that kind of leisurely academic pursuit of the idle class (有閑階級의 消遺的 學術) likely has no connection with us, so I have never had any interest in such a conference.
II. One Thing is Establishing an Autonomous Zone
Regarding the issue of Koreans in Manchuria (在滿 朝鮮人問題), there is an independent position for Koreans. From this position, this standing must be guaranteed, and the current calamities (禍難) must be rescued. However, the roots of these calamities run deep, so there seems to be no ingenious solution.
Above all, Koreans must now guard against actions lacking rational deliberation (理性的 思慮), agitated by sentimental prejudice (感傷的 偏見). Also, they must reject (排擊) those groups who, entrenched in certain policies, cause the majority of Koreans to suffer persecution shifting from A to B (怒甲移乙의 박해). Furthermore, they must support China's movement to abolish extraterritoriality (治外法權 철폐운동) and fight Japan over the issue of civil registration/nationality (民籍問題). Thus, the issue of Koreans in Manchuria boils down to the anti-XXXX [likely anti-imperialist] movement. No, this very issue must become the forefront (尖端) of that movement. **Summary**

This 1931 survey in *Tonggwang* magazine gathers opinions from prominent Korean intellectuals, journalists, lawyers, and educators on two critical issues: the utility of the upcoming Pacific Conference (Institute of Pacific Relations meeting) and the survival strategies for Korean migrants in Manchuria. Regarding the Pacific Conference, most respondents are skeptical or outright hostile, viewing it as dominated by imperialist powers (often referred to as XX主義) and unlikely to benefit weak or colonized nations like Korea, especially after losing national representation rights. Some see a limited opportunity for publicizing Korea's plight, while others advocate boycotting it. On the Manchurian issue, respondents universally recognize its gravity. Common suggestions include seeking Chinese naturalization (歸化) despite the perceived shame, forming strong, unified Korean organizations (大同團結, 自治機關) for self-protection and negotiation, improving relations with the Chinese populace to counter anti-Korean sentiment fueled by viewing them as Japanese vanguards, and demanding rights within the Chinese legal framework. Some propose more radical ideas like establishing autonomous zones (自治區) or even a neutral state. The issue of dual nationality and the need for Japan to revise its laws is frequently mentioned as a major obstacle to naturalization.

This article offers a valuable window into Korean elite perspectives during Japanese colonial rule. It reveals deep disillusionment with international bodies perceived as tools of imperialism, alongside urgent concerns about the precarious situation of Korean migrants caught between Chinese nationalism and Japanese expansionism. The emphasis on naturalization, autonomy, and pan-Korean unity highlights the complex strategies considered for national survival and identity preservation in the absence of sovereignty. The frequent, though censored, references to "imperialism" (XX主義) and the critique of Japan's role show the underlying anti-colonial sentiment. Historians would find the range of proposed solutions, the direct engagement with international relations from a colonial perspective, and the candid discussion of identity (Korean vs. potentially becoming Chinese citizens) particularly interesting.

잡지명	동광 제26호
발행일	1931년 10월 04일
기사제목	太平洋會議는 어떠케 利用할까 在滿同胞는 어떠케 해야 살까
필자	諸氏(原稿 到着順)
기사형태	설문

太平洋會議는 어떠케 利用할까 在滿同胞는 어떠케 해야 살까
						諸氏(原稿 到着順)
特輯
오늘날 朝鮮의 識者間의 가장 관심하는 일의 한가지가 在滿同胞 問題임에 틀림이 없다. 이 문제에 관하야 우리는 이미 누차 紙上에서 토의를 거듭햇거니와 앞으로 일층 활발하게 토론할 필요가 잇다고 생각한다. 이에 諸大家의 高見을 들어 그 토론의 一參考가 되게하고저 하야 이 특집을 시험한다. 또 太平洋會議의 문제도 中國民間側의 排擊聲浪이 發端이 되어 좌우간에 시비의 초점이 되어 잇거니와 10월달은 회의의 개최기니만치 우리의 흥미를 이끄는 점이 없지않다 할것이다. 그리하야 우리는
1. 우리는 태평양회의를 어떠케 이용할까
2. 在滿同胞는 어떠케 해야 살까
의 두 가지 문제로 諸家의 관찰을 수집해 보앗다. 奔忙 중에 답을 보내주신 여러 어른들께 감사하는 바다.

東亞日報社 社長 宋鎭禹

一. 國際的 進出
태평양회의가 국제적 민족적 결합이라고 하면 朝鮮은 그 민족의 권리로 正正堂堂하게 그 회의에 참가할 권리가 잇고 또한 참가해야 하겟습니다. 그것이 국가적 결합이라고 하면 참가여부 문제도 없거니와 우리가 한 민족으로 참가할 때에는 온갓 기회를 이용하야 우리의 문화와 모든 것을 국제적으로 알릴 필요가 잇습니다.
二. 急先務는 組織體
在滿同胞의 살길은 第一에 在滿同胞 스스로의 견고한 조직체의 결성입니다. 그 조직체는 中國國法이 容許하는 범위 안에서 만들 것은 물론이고 이 조직체를 가지고 中國政府와 기타 관계정부에 대하야 在滿 朝鮮民族의 생존과 발전에 필요한 모든 요구를 달성하도록 힘써야 되겟음니다. 그때에 비로소 國籍法도 문제될 것이지마는 무엇보다도 在滿同胞 자신이 할 수 잇는 조직체를 만드는 것이 가장 필요합니다. (文責在記者)

著述家

一. 會議는 OOOO한다.
태평양회는 强大民族, 弱小民族의 회의기관인 것만큼 약소민족 독자의 이익를 옹호키는 본질상 곤란한 것, 그남아 京都會議 이후로는 民族標準이 아니요 國家標準이 되어 弱小民族(植民地人)에게 ＜13＞ 발언권을 주지 않게 된것은 회의의 反動化를 증거한다. 故로 이 회의에 큰 기대를 못가질 것은 두말할 필요가 없다. 그러나 同會議는 人道主義를 高調하야 과거에 몇몇가지 事跡이 잇어서 온터인즉 朝鮮의 소리를 들려주는 것만은 퍽 필요할 것같다. 朝鮮人의 처지를 세계적으로 알리기 위하여는 모든 기회를 이용함이 可하다.
二. 歸化權獲得이 絶對로 必要
排滿同胞문제는 첫재 國籍問題(歸化權 問題)가 해결되어야 할 것, 驅逐壓迫의 急한 불을 끄자면 帝國主義前鋒이란 이름을 쓰고 잇어서는 될 수 없다. 특히 최다수 滿洲奧地 住居 朝鮮人을 위하여는 歸化가 절대로 필요한 조건이 된다. 그것은 이미 與論化되어 잇는 터이나 좀더 强烈 且 熱心한 주장과 운동이 잇어야겟다. 둘재로는 在滿同胞의 빈곤을 완화키 위한 경제문제이니 이는 在滿朝鮮人의 大同團結이 전제되고야 실현될 것같다. 즉 朝鮮人自治機關의 확립을 기다려 비로소 劃策이 성취될 바 임으로 在滿同胞 자신의 大同團結이 先急務인 것을 믿는다.

辯護士 李仁

一. 代辯會議는 不贊成
종래와 같은 資本主義를 대변하는 所謂 태평양회의는 찬성 못하겟습니다. 모든 것을 초월하고 가장 자유스런 입장에서 檢討考究하는 기관이 잇엇으면 좋겟습니다.
二. 自治領의 結成이 必要
入籍, 商租權, 兩問題도 해결이 못됩니다. 居住民 총투표로 自治領을 들엇으면 살게 될 것입니다.

延專敎授 李順鐸

一. 偏狹한 會議에는 反對
태평양회의에 朝鮮을 대표 파견국으로 독립한 자격을 주지 안는다고 하면 대표선출에도 문제이어니와 선출한 대표의 활동도 많이 제한을 받을 것입니다. 따라서 朝鮮에 관한 많은 사실이 虛構된다고 하더라도 당당히 밝히지를 못할 것입니다. 그러면 차라리 우리로서는 태평양회의를 반대한다고 태도를 세우고 반대운동까지라도 하는 것이 좋을가 합니다.
二. 歸化權이 先決問題
一言으로 蔽하면 在滿同胞는 歸化權을 얻어야 합니다. 만일 이 권리를 얻지 못한다면 今後 幾百年을 가더라도 늘 동일한 轍을 밟아서 문제는 발산되지 아니하야 朝鮮人의 생활은 水草같을 것입니다. 그러면 이 권리를 얻도록 돌진해야 할터인데 이를 위하야는 한 會를 모아가지고 그 會가 한편으로는 日本政府에 운동하고 他便으로 國民政府 又는 中國代表機關에 운동해야 할 줄로 압니다.

崇專 敎授

一. 民族으로서의 世界的 進出이 必要
태평양회의는 그 자체가 私的 機關임으로 될 수 잇으면 우리도 太平洋沿岸의 한 민족으로 참가하야 다른 사람의 문제도 알아보고 우리의 문제도 그들에게 알리어줄 필요가 잇다고 생각합니다. ＜14＞
二. 大同團結이 必要
(가) 在滿同胞는 될 수 잇는대로 中國에 入籍하야 上地所有權을 爲始하야 諸般 中國國民의 향유하는 권리를 향유할 것.
(나) 一節 主義 主張의 異同을 무조건으로 버리고 朝鮮人으로서의 大同團結을 실현할 것이라고 합니다. 물론 구체적 의견을 말슴하기는 紙面이 유한함으로 후일을 기다리겟습니다.

大邱 徐相日

先決問題는 內地의 中心機關
拜復 貴社 2大 設問은 시기에 적절한 문제이온 바 이 두 문제를 일괄하여 말슴할 것 같으면 태평양회의를 어떠케 이용하든지 在滿同胞를 어떠케 살리든지 하는 것은 그 근본적 先決問題가 오로지 在鮮 이천만 민중의 大衆的 압력의 結成體인 0민적 中心機關의 有無如何에서만 해답을 말할 수 잇는 가능성 이외에는 실행을 내포하지 아니하지 아니한 이상론이 아닐가 합니다. 朝鮮의 선배나 지도자는 태평양회의를 이용할, 在滿同胞를 살릴, 권위잇는 國民的 中心力을 먼저 조성할 각오와 결의가 필요합니다.

朝鮮日報社

一. 代辯機關의 暴露
「태평양회의를 우리는 어떠케 이용할까?」 하는 문제는 주관적 문제보다도 對岸의 객관적 문제시 됨에서 지나지 안습니다. 우리는 원래 태평양회의라는 것에 아모 정치적 의의를 기대하지 않엇든 것임으로.
