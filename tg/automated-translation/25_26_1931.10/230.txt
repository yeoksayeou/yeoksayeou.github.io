Article Type: 소식 (News/Dispatch)

110,000 War Widows Remarry
According to a recent survey, it is said that 110,000 women who became widows due to the war have remarried. However, as a result, 110,000 unmarried young women have become unable to marry. In England, the number of 'old maids' aged 38-45 who missed their chance to marry due to the war is a staggering 650,000. This is according to the recent census. These old maids are now spending lonely lives, their only crime having worked diligently in munitions factories or war support organizations during the Great War.

Rooftop London
On the rooftop of Bush House (풋시·하우쓰) in London, the capital of England, it is said there are over 300 female office workers using their lunch break to sunbathe and exercise. The instructor is retired General Delcy (델씨). They are trying to recover the health and youth lost in dim offices through deep breathing and jumping exercises.

Laughing or Crying? The Divorced Xuantong Emperor
The three hundred years of glory of the Qing Dynasty (淸朝) have turned into an old dream, and the Xuantong Emperor Puyi (宣統帝 漙儀氏), who was living a sad life as the deposed emperor in the Japanese Concession (日本 租界) in Tianjin (天津), has once again run into the strange rough waves of society with a divorce suit from his favored consort. Noble Consort Hesim (貴妃 헤심氏), at the flower-like age of 25, cites the intense surveillance and restraint by the eunuchs (太監) serving the deposed emperor as the reason for the divorce, demanding freedom. Does the deposed emperor, feeling the bleak autumn wind, laugh or cry at this world?

Lecture Meeting Hosted by This Magazine's Branch a Success: P'yŏngyang Branch Reporter's Diary

From 8 PM on August 22nd, at the Paeksŏnhaeng Memorial Hall (白善行紀念舘) in P'yŏngyang (평양), as the first project since the establishment of our branch office, we invited Mr. Yi Kwangsu (李光洙), editor-in-chief of the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報社), Ms. Kim Yun'gyŏng (金允經) of Paehwa Girls' High School (培化女高普), and Mr. Chu Yohan (朱耀翰), the chief editor of our company (本社 主幹), to hold a lecture meeting, which was a great success, filled to capacity even before the scheduled time. <73>

**Summary**

This news section from October 1931 compiles several short reports. It discusses the social consequence in Britain of 110,000 war widows remarrying, allegedly preventing 110,000 younger women from marrying, and highlighting the plight of 650,000 older unmarried women who worked during WWI. Another piece describes female office workers exercising on a London rooftop. A third report covers the divorce of the former Chinese Emperor Puyi by his consort Wenxiu (referred to phonetically as Hesim) while living in the Japanese Concession in Tianjin. Finally, it reports on a successful lecture event organized by the magazine's P'yŏngyang branch, featuring prominent Korean intellectuals Yi Kwangsu, Kim Yun'gyŏng, and Chu Yohan. For historians, this article offers a window into the international news and social issues (post-war demographics, women's changing roles, the fate of deposed royalty) being reported in colonial Korea. The P'yŏngyang lecture report is particularly interesting as it shows continued cultural and intellectual activity by Koreans under Japanese rule, featuring well-known figures associated with Korean literature and education, hosted by a Korean publication's regional branch.

전쟁 과부 11만 재혼
최근의 조사에 의하면 전쟁때문에 과부가 되엇다가 재혼한 여자가 11만이나 된다고. 그러나 그 결과로 시집 못 가게 된 미혼 처녀가 11만. 영국에는 전쟁으로 인하야 혼기를 노치고 38세-45세의 노처녀 된 수가 무려 65만 명이나. 된다고 하는 것이 최근의 호구조사 이 노처녀들은 다 大戰 당시에 군수품 공장이나 전쟁보조기관에서 열심히 일해 주든 죄로 지금은 적적한 생애를 보내고 잇다.

屋上倫敦
英京 倫敦에 잇는 풋시·하우쓰 屋上을 보면 점심시간을 이용하야 外光을 浴하면서 休操를 하는 여사무원 孃이 300여인이나 된다고 한다. 선생은 퇴역장군 델씨. 그들은 어듬컴컴한 사무실에서 잃은 건강과 청춘을 심호흡과 跳躍運動으로 회복할라고 하는 것이다.

웃으시나 우시는가 이혼 당한 宣統帝
淸朝의 삼백 년 영화도 옛날 꿈으로 화하고 廢帝의 슬푼 생애를 天津 일본 租界에서 보내시든 宣統帝 漙儀氏는 寵妃의 이혼소송이라는 야릇한 세파에 다시금 부드치게 되엇다. 귀비 헤심 씨는 25라는 꽃 같은 나이 廢帝를 모시고 잇는 太監들의 감시와 구속이 심하니 자유를 달라는 것이 이혼의 이유. 때는 가을 추풍낙막을 느끼시는 廢帝는 그 세상을 웃으시나 우시는가.

본지사 주최로 대강연회 성황 평양지사 일기자
