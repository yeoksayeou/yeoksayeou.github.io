Those Seen in Court, the Silent Ch'oe Yun-suk
						By Hong Chong-in

Following the Kwangju Student Incident, around January 14th of last year, there was a plan to stage a demonstration by all female students in Kyŏngsŏng, one day ahead of the male middle school students. When the male student group requested a one-day postponement so that male and female students could demonstrate simultaneously on the same day, the female student group, gathered somewhere in Kahoe-dong, refused this. Surprised by this plan, the male student side hastily advanced their own plans that night, and on the morning of the 15th at 9 AM, the male and female student bodies throughout Kyŏngsŏng initiated the second student Manse incident.

Around early March, six members from the vanguard of the city's female student demonstrators at that time stood in the Kyŏngsŏng District Court for a public trial. Even before the trial, there was much talk about the six young female students (though one of them, Hŏ Chŏng-suk, was older). People discussed who was attractive, who was an athlete, who spoke well, and so on. At that time, Detective A from S Police Station, who was in charge of investigating the female student incident, shared this story: "People talk about this one and that one, and indeed, there are some genteel young ladies among them. However, there is one fearsome woman among them. That is Ch'oe OO. During interrogations, you get to know a person's character. Those who readily answer questions, even saying things they don't need to, are not an issue, but nothing is as difficult as dealing with someone who remains completely silent. When you have to ask, citing precedents from the case, 'Isn't it like this and this?' and only get a 'Yes' or 'No,' dealing with that person is truly agonizing. There’s no way to get a handle on them. That Ch'oe was truly difficult. Isn't her physique quite sturdy too? Just like her appearance, she has very few weaknesses. If there's one woman to watch out for in the future, it's her. She will certainly amount to something..." When I heard this back then, I listened idly.

Indeed, the time came to reconsider these words. Just last month, in August, I went to the Higher Affairs Section of S Police Station, where that detective worked, regarding some matter. Suddenly, I opened the door to the detective's office and saw a young woman in a corner of the empty room, her hair disheveled, hand on her head, gazing out the window. Seemingly lost in thought, she glanced back at me briefly, then nonchalantly turned back to look out the window again. She looked familiar; she had to be Ch'oe Yun-suk, who, a few days earlier, was reported to have traveled to Hamgyŏng North and South Provinces as a liaison (레포) for the Communist Party rebuilding incident.

At the female student incident trial in early March last year, Kim Mo, who was questioned first, explained the sequence of events clearly. Another, Yi Mo, said, "I made the red flag because the color was pretty. I won't do it again..." Yet another, Song Mo, spoke with a composed attitude but, towards the end, seemed overcome by some desolation (寂寞), appearing to force back tears. Hŏ Mo, having been considerably trained in social movements, likewise did not deviate. Among them, Ch'oe Yun-suk, just as the detective had said, was silent. "Yes," "No"—only these two words. She did not express the opinions she held. Her silent, composed demeanor at that time certainly showed weight. The impression of the "Silent Ch'oe" comes to mind once again when thinking about her recent incident. <34>

**Summary**

This memoir by Hong Chong-in recounts his impressions of female student activists tried for a demonstration in Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) in early 1930, focusing particularly on Ch'oe Yun-suk. He contrasts Ch'oe's resolute silence during the trial and prior police interrogation with the more forthcoming or emotional responses of other students like Hŏ Chŏng-suk, Kim Mo, Yi Mo, and Song Mo. The author recalls a detective's assessment of Ch'oe as a uniquely formidable individual destined to achieve something significant, an observation seemingly validated by Ch'oe's later involvement in communist activities. The article offers insight into the character and resolve of student activists, particularly women, during the colonial period, the nature of police interrogations, and the connections drawn by contemporaries between student nationalism and subsequent involvement in more radical political movements like communism. Historians might find the contemporary perspective on Ch'oe Yun-suk's demeanor and the police assessment particularly interesting, along with the mention of the specific demonstration's planning details.

법정에서 본 그들, 무언의 崔允淑
						洪鍾仁
광주학생사건의 뒤를 이어 작년 1월 14일인가 京城의 남학생(中學)들 보다 하로 앞서서 全 京城의 여학생 「데모」를 이르킬 계획을 하야 남자 측 學生團에서 하로만 연기하여 같은 날에 남녀 학생이 동시에 「데모」를 이르키자고 청하엿을 때 嘉會洞 某處에 모이엇든 女學生團에서는 이것을 거절하엿다. 남학생 측에서는 이 계획에 놀래어 그 밤으로 밭삐 계획을 진행시키여 15일 오전 9시를 기하여 전 경성 남녀 등 학생층은 제2차의 학생만세사건을 이르키엇다.
이때의 시내 여학생 측 데모의 前衛分子 중 여섯 명이 3월 초순엔가 경성법원 공판정에 서게 되엇다. 공판 전부터 여섯 명 나 어린 여학생(나 들은 許貞淑 한 명도 잇지만)에 대한 이야기가 높앗다. 누구는 용모가 어떠코 누구는 운동선수고, 누구는 말 잘하고... 등등, 그때에 여학생 사건을 맡아 취조한 S서의 A형사는 이런 이야기를 하엿다. 「누구라 누구라 하지만 물론 인물도 얌전한 색씨가 잇다. 그러나 그 중에도 무서운 여자가 한 명 잇다. 그것은 崔OO이다. 취조하는 때면 사람의 품을 다 알 수 잇는데 그 중에 묻는 말을 겁석 겁석 잘 대답하되 안 해도 좋을 말까지 하는 것은 문제도 안되지만 전혀 말을 안 하는 것같이 괴로운 것은 없다. 이러 이러하지 안느냐고 사건의 전례를 들어 물어야 겨우 응, 이라던가 아니요 라던가 하는 사람 그 사람에게만은 참 괴롭다. 손을 대일 도리가 없다. 그 崔만은 참 곤란 하엿다. 체격도 여무지지 않드냐? 그 모양으로 그 여자에게는 빈틈이 별로 없다. 이후라도 주의할 여자는 그 여자밖게 없다. 반드시 한사람 구실할 것...」이런 말을 들을 때 그때는 무심히 들엇섯다.

과연 이 말을 다시 생각할 때가 왓섯다. 바로 지난달 8月 未 어떤 일로 그때의 그 형사가 일하는 S서 고등계에 갓섯다. 문득 형사실 문을 열엇더니 빈 방 한 모퉁이에 젊은 여자 한 명이 흥크러진 머리에 손을 대이고 창 밖을 내다보고 잇엇다. 무엇인가 생각하고 잇는 듯 하든 그는 한번 흘끔 돌려다 보고는 천연스러히 또 창 밖을 내다보고 잇섯다. 언젠가 본 듯한 그 여자 그는 멫 츨 전에 공산당 재건 사건의 「레포」로 咸南北 等地로 단녓다는 崔允淑에 틀림없엇다.
작년 3월 초순의 여학생 사건의 공판에 맨 첫 번 訊問에 나섯든 金某는 전후 사건의 경과를 잘 알어 듣게 이야기 하엿다. 또 李某는 「붉은 기빨은 빛갈이 고와서 만들엇섯지요. 다시는 안 그러마요...」 또 宋某는 천연한 태도로 말하다가 끝으로 어떤 寂寞이 솟앗던지 눈물을 억지로 막는 듯 뵈엿다. 許某는 사회운동에도 많이 단련되엇을 만큼 역시 脫線이 없엇다. 그 중에 崔允淑은 과연 그 형사의 말대로 말이 없엇다. 「녜」, 「아니요」 두 말뿐. 자기의 가젓다는 뜻을 말하지 않엇다. 그때의 무언의 침착한 태도는 확실히 무게를 뵈엿다. 「無言의 崔」의 인상은 최근 잇은 그의 사건을 생각할 때 다시금 머리에 떠올라 온다.＜34＞
