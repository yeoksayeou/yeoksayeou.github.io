Article Title:	그 꿈을 깨우면 어떠케 할까요? (What if I wake it from that dream?)
Author:	辛夕汀
Article Type:	시 (Poem)

What if I wake it from that dream?
						Sin Sŏkchŏng

Mother-
Mountain birds live in that forest, don't they?
The bird flying in the darkening sky,
how does it find that forest?
Even the clouds, on the quiet sky's
blue path, wander...
Mother! When I was alone by the river at sunset,
playing, building sandcastles,
just like you call me from under the ginkgo tree,
in the forest across the foggy, hazy river,
does the empty nest in the seeping moonlight
wait for the late-coming mountain bird?

Mother-
On the forest path touched by the distant sky's red glow,
the dreamlike silhouettes of returning people sway,
and even the murmuring waves on the white sandbank <94>
listen quietly to the cowherd's flute.
What story is the evening wind telling,
that the blades of grass on the hill nod their heads?
When I fall asleep on your lap, Mother,
if that wind goes searching through the forest
and wakes the small mountain bird from its endlessly deep
dream, what should I do?
-1931. 8. 3- <95>

**Summary**

This lyrical poem, written from the perspective of a child speaking to their mother, uses imagery of nature at dusk—birds returning to their forest nest, the sunset, fog, wind, and grass—to pose innocent questions about the world. The central concern revolves around the sleeping mountain bird and the child's worry that the wind might inadvertently wake it from its "endlessly deep dream." The poem evokes feelings of innocence, wonder, and a gentle empathy for nature. From a historical perspective, the poem, published in 1931 during the Japanese colonial period, is notable for its focus on nature, family, and inner emotional life, rather than overt political themes. Historians might find interest in this type of lyrical expression as a form of cultural production during a time of restricted expression, potentially representing an inward turn or a subtle assertion of Korean sentiment through nature imagery and traditional themes like motherly affection.
