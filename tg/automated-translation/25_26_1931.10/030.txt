**Body Text Translation:**
Sino-Japanese Relations in Manchuria and Mongolia and Their Prospects
						Kim Il-sŏng
I
Recent anti-Japanese movements in China, particularly concerning policies towards Manchuria and Mongolia (Man-Mō), primarily aim first, to diminish the power of Japanese railways in Manchuria, which constitutes the so-called Manchurian-Mongolian Railway Problem; second, to demand the recovery of administrative rights over Dalian, Lushun, the Anp'ung Line, and the South Manchuria Railway's (Mantetsu) attached lands, as well as the withdrawal of stationed Japanese troops, which is the generally known Extraterritoriality Negotiation Problem; and third, to interfere with Korean farmers' land cultivation and leasing, strictly regulate land sales, and refuse entry to newly arriving Koreans, which is the already controversial Expulsion of Korean Immigrants.
At a meeting of the Chinese Nationalist Government held on July 29th, it was stated: "Japan's attitude towards Man-Mō is a matter of life and death, thus we are confronting it with serious determination, and both China and Japan must be prepared even for war centered on Man-Mō. Looking at international relations now, the earlier the time comes, the more advantageous it is for Japan, and the later it comes, the more advantageous it is for China. Therefore, for the time being, we should endure patiently and act prudently (隱忍自重), striving to expel Japanese influence through covert methods (陰性的 방법), doing our utmost not to give any pretext, and wait for the right time."
According to a report from the T'onghwa County Affairs Committee in West Jiandao (Sŏkando): "The Japanese government's primary means to bring Man-Mō into its grasp is to relocate a large number of Koreans to Man-Mō; the second means is to have these Koreans naturalize (歸化) into China, and they are currently planning its realization; the third means is to use these naturalized Koreans to seize real power in Man-Mō and thereby act according to the circumstances (臨機相應)." As this report comes from T'onghwa County in West Jiandao, where the largest number of Korean farmers reside, one can understand the nature of the Korean Expulsion problem. On top of that, there is the pretext (口實) that Koreans are becoming communized (赤化) (though such tendencies are not entirely absent), and is there not the Mitsuda Agreement (三矢協定) as a weapon?
There have been roughly nine major anti-Japanese movements in China. The first occurred in March of Meiji 41 (1908) when the Kōbe Tatsuma Shōkai's steamship, the Daini Tatsu Maru, loaded with firearms and ammunition ordered by a Macao gun shop, was about to arrive in Macao when it was suddenly captured and plundered by a Chinese warship. Although the Japanese government protested and the incident was resolved peacefully, an anti-Japanese movement arose in China, accusing Japan of trying to foster internal rebellion in China by helping revolutionaries smuggle weapons. This movement, centered in Guangdong and Hong Kong, continued for eight months throughout South China. The second was triggered by the Sino-Japanese Treaty (日淸條約) concluded alongside Japan's declaration of free action regarding the Anp'ung Line reconstruction issue. Chinese students studying in Tokyo were the first to rise up, demanding the recovery of rights <2> and interests (利權), followed by a fierce anti-Japanese movement throughout Manchuria. The third was motivated by the famous Twenty-One Demands (21個條). It spread throughout China and continued for over half a year. Students were central, mobilizing even the lower middle class, and as a result, the Twenty-One Demands were effectively buried (有耶無耶). The fourth occurred when China's demands at the Paris Peace Conference—such as the abrogation of the Sino-Japanese treaty and the direct recovery of the German leased territory in Shandong—were not met. This caused a movement that spread not only throughout China but also to Southeast Asia (南洋). The fifth arose when the lease period for Russia's Lushun and Dalian expired. China attempted to recover Lushun-Dalian (旅大) immediately. When Japan rejected China's notification of the invalidity of the Sino-Japanese treaty, a movement for recovering national sovereignty and boycotting Japanese goods began. It was most intense not only in North China but also along the Yangtze River (長江) area, continuing for five months. The sixth was a large-scale anti-foreign (排外) movement triggered by issues including the Shanghai spinning industry and the Municipal Council (工部局) problem. Workers and students formed the core force. Although it initially started as anti-Japanese, it shifted to an anti-British movement in Southern China. The impact was enormous. Consequently, movements like anti-foreignism and recovery of national sovereignty saw a surprising expansion and strengthening of their organizations. Moreover, it was significantly different from previous similar movements, with economic struggle being its main focus (主眼). The seventh occurred when the Nationalist Government's Northern Expedition army was advancing northward with fierce momentum. Japan dispatched troops (出兵) to the Shandong area under the pretext of protecting its residents (居留民). This led to a clash with the Northern Expedition army, resulting in the so-called Jinan Incident (濟南事件). Using this as an opportunity (機), an anti-Japanese movement arose. Fueled by opposition to the Shandong troop dispatch and the Jinan Incident, a fierce anti-Japanese movement persisted from June 1928 until the autumn of the following year (翌年), instigated by the Kuomintang or the Nanjing Government. The eighth is the recent Wanbaoshan Incident (萬寶山事件).
This outlines only the prominent anti-Japanese movements in China during the 30 years since Japan acquired special rights and interests in Man-Mō; besides these, there were many smaller, localized anti-Japanese movements. What we must particularly note here is that anti-foreign movements in China have almost always been anti-Japanese movements, indicating the extent to which Japan's presence conflicts with China's growth and development. Looking back at the trajectory of these movements, they initially began as vague movements of resistance by an oppressed people.
However, around the time of the Twenty-One Demands, they presented economic conditions as terms of struggle, such as boycotting Japanese goods (日貨不買), refusing customs clearance procedures for Japanese goods, not using Japanese currency, and obstructing the loading of cargo onto Japanese ships, with students forming the core. Then, during the Shanghai Incident (sixth instance), workers became the vanguard, supported by students, and other general economic organizations, merchants, and industrialists followed suit. The movement gained significant strength, resulting in two major achievements: the recovery of leased territories (租借地) and tariff autonomy (關稅自主). Thus, the previously conceptual resistance movement of an oppressed people gradually transformed into a political movement, which then re-transformed into an economic struggle. Furthermore, this is clearly evident in the current Wanbaoshan Incident, where the Shanghai Special City Party Branch announced a declaration to boycott Japanese goods, and the Shanghai Chamber of Commerce and the City Party Branch separately and independently planned anti-Japanese actions, advocating purely economic measures. This shows that the leading group (主動部) is in the hands of the industrial associations (實業團).
Through such a course (經路), the anti-Japanese movement in China has grown and advanced.

II
Now let's examine what Japan's rights and interests (利權) in Man-Mō are. Listing only those manifested in treaties or contracts (契約):
First, regarding Land:
1. Lease rights for the Kantōshū (關東州租借權)
2. Rights concerning the Neutral Zone (中立地帶)
3. Administrative rights for the Mantetsu Attached Lands (滿鐵附屬肢의 行政權)
4. Right of Land Lease and Business in South Manchuria (南滿土地商租權)
5. Right of Residence and Travel in South Manchuria (南滿에 居住往來權)
6. Rights concerning Korean residence North of the Tumen River (圖們江北에 朝鮮人居住에 관한 權利), etc.
Second, regarding Tariffs (關稅):
1. Right concerning tariff exemption at Dalian Port (大連港關稅免除)
2. Special privilege concerning the establishment of Dalian Maritime Customs (大連海關)
3. Special privilege concerning reduced tariffs at the Anp'ung land border (安奉陸境關稅輕減)
Third, regarding Mines and Forests (鑛山森林): <3>
1. Prospecting (試掘) and Mining Rights (採掘權) for South Manchurian mines
2. Mining Rights for Fushun-Yantai Coal and Iron (撫順煙臺炭鐵 採掘權)
3. Rights concerning loan contracts (借款契約) for forests and gold mines in Jilin and Heilongjiang provinces (吉林黑龍 兩省)
4. Mining along the Anp'ung Railway line and the Mantetsu main line (安奉鐵道 沿線 及 滿鐵幹線 沿線)
5. Joint management rights (合辦權) for the Benxihu Coal and Iron Company (本溪湖煤鐵公司)
6. Joint management rights for logging (採木合辦權) on the right bank of the Yalu River (鴨綠江), etc.
Fourth, regarding Railways (鐵道):
1. Mantetsu Management Rights (滿鐵經營權)
2. Right to prohibit Mantetsu Parallel Lines (滿鐵並行線 禁止權)
3. Anp'ung Railway Management Rights (安奉鐵道 經營權)
4. Right to station troops for railway line protection (鐵道線路保護駐兵의 權)
5. Mandated Management Rights for the Jilin-Changchun Railway (吉長鐵道, 委任經營權)
6. Loan rights (借款權) for railways such as Xinmin-Fengtien (新奉鐵道), Jilin-Hoeryong (吉會鐵道), Kaiyuan-Jilin (開吉鐵道), Changchun-Tao'an (長逃鐵道), Sipingjie-Tao'an (四逃鐵道), etc.
From this, one gets the sense that not only Kantōshū and the Mantetsu Attached Lands, but also the 77,000 *li* of the Eastern Provinces (Dongxisheng - 東西省, referring to the Three Eastern Provinces) are Japan's quasi-territory (準領土). First, the investment amount (投資額) is over 1.4 billion yen, more than one division (師團) of troops is stationed (駐屯), and the railways within Japan's sphere of influence, besides Mantetsu's main and branch lines totaling over 1,100 km, include Chinese government-managed (官辦) lines financed by Japanese loans: Sipingjie-Tao'an Railway (四逃鐵道) at 430 km, Tao'an-Ang'angxi Railway (洮昻鐵道) at 224 km, Jilin-Changchun Railway (吉長鐵道) at 127 km, Jilin-Dunhua Railway (吉敦鐵道) at 210 km. Additionally, Sino-Japanese joint ventures (中日合辦事業) include the Jinfu Railway (金福鐵道) at 102 km, Xicheng Railway (溪城鐵道) at 24 km, and Tiantu Railway (天圖鐵道) at 111 km. Therefore, the total length of Japanese-affiliated railways exceeds 2,300 km. Compared to the Chinese system's 1,000 km and the Sino-Russian joint venture's (中露合辦) over 1,700 km, Japan's share is vastly superior (月等). This is why Man-Mō is called Japan's special zone of rights and interests.
However, these rights and interests are facing a crisis (危機に瀕하고 잇다) day by day. The Fengtien Warlords (奉天軍閥) grew up under Japan's shadow, and consequently, respecting and always protecting Japan's interests was the tacit understanding of mutual dependence. After they came under the command (麾下) of the Nanjing Government, there has been a significant change in Man-Mō's policy towards Japan. All negotiations previously conducted with the Fengtien Warlords were transferred (옴기엇고) to Nanjing. As the Nanjing Government has declared the abolition (撤廢) of unequal treaties and the recovery of rights and interests, and is steadily implementing this today, politically, the issues of recovering Lushun-Dalian and abolishing the right to station troops (駐兵權) have arisen. Economically, the issue of land lease rights (商租權問題) is being dragged out vaguely (어름어름하야), attempts are being made to impose taxes (課說 - likely typo for 課稅) on Japanese production facilities in Man-Mō, Korean farmers are being pressured, and measures like the encirclement policy (包團策) against Mantetsu, the backbone (根幹) of Japan's Man-Mō management, are most noteworthy. Originally, when Japan inherited the rights to Mantetsu from Russia, the absolute prohibition of constructing parallel lines (並行線) to Mantetsu was also a right inherited from Russia. However, with the opening of China's Datong Line (打通線), that principle was broken (파쇠되고). Railways like Fengtian-Hailong (奉海) and Jilin-Dunhua (吉敦), once trusted as Mantetsu feeder lines (培養線), have now connected with Jingfeng (京奉), Jilin-Hailong (吉海), Sipingjie-Tao'an (四洮), Datong (打通), etc., encircling (包團) and attacking (擊攻) Mantetsu. Furthermore, if the currently planned Eastern Main Line (東大幹線)—starting from Huludao (葫蘆島), passing through the Fengtian-Hailong and Jilin-Hailong lines, and extending (뻐치려는) to Wuchang, Yilan, Tongjiang, and Suiyuan—the Central Main Line (中部幹線)—extending from Tongjiang via the Datong Line, Tao'an line, etc., to reach (거처서) Heihe (黑河)—and another Western Main Line (西部幹線)—passing through Chifeng (赤峯) to reach Kolun (哥倫)—are realized, and the currently under-construction (築港) Huludao Port is completed (완성되는 날이면), Mantetsu will inevitably face further decline and fall (衰殘零落). Indeed, wasn't it a fact that Mantetsu already suffered immense damage from being encircled by Chinese railways in the past two years? Facing this, Japan seems to be abandoning (放棄) its previous Mantetsu-centric policy (滿鐵 一元論) and attempting to realize the Jilin-Hoeryong Railway (吉會鐵道), adopting a two-main-line strategy (2大幹線主犾� - likely typo for 主義 'juyi' meaning 'doctrine' or 'principle') as its countermeasure. The preferential loan rights (優先借款權) for Japanese capitalists, secured against local taxes for railway construction in South Manchuria and Eastern Inner Mongolia, were offered jointly to the International Consortium (國際借款團) at the Washington Conference (華盛頓會議) some years ago. Among land-related rights (權益), the South Manchurian Land Lease Rights (南滿商租權) still lack a detailed agreement (細目協定). Thus, Japan's rights in Man-Mō have already begun to tilt (기우러지고 잇다).

Looking into the business conditions (營業內情), it's said that almost all ventures are performing poorly (企業 不成績), even more sluggishly than Japan's [domestic economy?]. Mantetsu, which seemed perennially prosperous (萬年景氣), saw its dividend (配當) drop from 11% in fiscal year [Showa] 4 (1929) to 8% in year 5 (1930), and it looks set to fall to 6% in year 6 (1931). Consequently, its affiliated companies, as well as other general Japanese investment enterprises, are reportedly all operating at a loss (缺損). While the general economic downturn has an impact (影響도 잇오나), the sharp fall in silver prices (銀價의 폭락) and the gradual emergence (檀頭 - likely typo for 擡頭, 대두) of Chinese capital, leading to competition (競爭), are cited as reasons.

IV
China's industrialization has been a major policy since the Kuomintang era. As the movement to recover national sovereignty achieved some success and tariff autonomy (關稅自主) was realized, it began to progress at a rapid pace (急速한 템포로).
Here too, the Factory Act (廠法) was established, putting pressure on foreign enterprises. Furthermore, while foreign enterprises are suffering immense blows from the drop in silver prices (銀價의 低落), China is seizing the opportunity (機) to promote the exclusion of Japanese capital (日資排斥), employing policies to shrink Dalian Port (大連港의 衰縮策を弄하야), posing a great threat (脅威) to Japanese enterprises in Man-Mō. The Shanghai Industrial Group (上海 實業團) is taking the lead, raising freight charges (運賃) and imposing heavy taxes (重稅) on products from foreign factories, and announcing plans for excluding Japanese capital and a five-year plan. Although these may not be implemented immediately (直刻), they represent a trend that cannot be taken lightly (輕視). Moreover, Chinese commerce and industry are growing (增長) with sharp momentum. Consequently, as the products of late-industrializing countries (後進工業國) always start with crude manufacturing (粗工業), Japan, which holds the most dominant position in the Chinese market, will naturally be the most affected, as most Japanese products were in crude industries (粗工業). Looking at the current performance in Manchuria, it is true that the Japanese, being more advanced, still hold an advantage (優勢) in management. For instance, in spinning (紡績), while the Chinese-managed Liaoning Spinning Mill (遼寧紡績廠) competes with Japanese affiliates like Manchuria Spinning (滿洲紡績), Manchuria Fufang (滿洲福紡), and Naigai Wata's Jinzhou Factory (內外綿金州工場), the Japanese trio's equipment exceeds 110,000 spindles, whereas Liaoning's is said to be 30,000 spindles. In the flour milling industry (製粉業), the production (産額) of Japanese affiliates exceeds 30-40,000 bags, while the Chinese side accounts for only about 30%. In the soybean oil industry (豆油業), although China has shown remarkable growth recently, it still only reaches 30-40% of Japanese influence. In tobacco (煙草), Japanese, Anglo-American, and Chinese interests are engaged in fierce competition (角逐戰). In matches (洋火), Japanese, Chinese, and Swiss (瑞西) interests were vying for supremacy (爭霸戰), but currently, Chinese factories are shutting down one after another (休業工場이 속출하는 現勢이다).
Thus, according to the *Man-Mō Yōran* (滿蒙要覽) published by Mantetsu, Japan's investment (投資高) in Man-Mō amounts to over 1.4685 billion yen. Comparing this to the 804 million yen invested in Korea (朝鮮) and 360 million yen invested in Taiwan (臺灣), one can understand how significantly Man-Mō is valued by Japan as a capital market and source of resources (資源地임을 알겟다). <6>

**Summary**

This 1931 article by Kim Il-sŏng (金一成) analyzes the increasingly tense Sino-Japanese relationship centered on Manchuria and Mongolia ("Man-Mō"). It details China's multi-pronged anti-Japanese strategy targeting Japan's railway dominance, demanding the return of administrative rights (Dalian, Lushun, Mantetsu lands), and notably, expelling Korean immigrants whom Japan used as a tool for expansion. The author traces the history of anti-Japanese movements, noting their evolution towards organized economic boycotts. The piece meticulously lists Japan's extensive treaty-based rights (land, tariffs, mining, forestry, railways) and massive investments (nearly 1.5 billion yen, spearheaded by the South Manchuria Railway and its affiliates), contrasting this with China's countermeasures like building encircling railways and promoting domestic industry, which threaten Japan's established interests amidst a global downturn and falling silver prices.

From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is significant for several reasons. It provides a contemporary Korean viewpoint on the eve of the Mukden Incident, explicitly stating Japan's policy of using Korean immigrants as the "first measure" to control Manchuria, directly linking Japanese expansionism with Korean migration. It highlights the resulting Chinese backlash and the precarious situation of Koreans caught between competing powers, facing expulsion fueled by Chinese nationalism and implicitly linked to anti-Japanese sentiment. The mention of the Mitsuda Agreement (三矢協定) related to policing Koreans underscores the complex jurisdictional and security issues surrounding the Korean population in Manchuria. Historians would find the detailed breakdown of Japanese investments and enterprises, the analysis of Chinese counter-strategies, and the framing of the Korean immigrant issue within the larger Sino-Japanese geopolitical struggle particularly valuable.

滿蒙의 日中關係와 그 展望
						金一成
一
최근 中國의 排日運動에는, 그중에도 滿蒙에 대한 정책에는, 첫재는 滿洲에 잇서 日本鐵道의 세력을 減殺하려는 것인바 그가 이른바 滿蒙鐵道問題란 것이요 다음은 大連, 旅順, 安奉線及 滿鐵의 부속지 行政權의 회수, 日本 駐屯軍의 撤退 등을 요구하는 것이니 그가 世所謂 治外法權 交涉問題이고 셋재는 朝鮮農民의 토지경작 토지 租貸를 간섭하고 土地賣却을 엄중히 取締하며 그리고 새로이 移來하는 朝鮮人의 입국을 거절하는 이미 物議가 되어 잇는 朝鮮人 移民驅逐이다.
7월 29일에 개최 되엇든 中國 國民政府의 會合에는 「日本이 滿蒙에 대한 태도는 死活問題이니 만큼 중대한 결의로써 그에 당하고 잇음으로써 中日 兩國은 滿蒙을 중심으로의 전쟁이라도 각오하여야 한다. 이제 國際關係를 보니 그 시기가 일으면 일을사록 日本에 유리하고 늦으면 늦을사록 中國에 유리함으로 당분간은 隱忍自重하야 陰性的 방법으로써 日本 세력의 구축을 도모하고 극력것 구실을 주지 않도록 하야 시기를 기다릴 것이라고」 햇다.
西間島 通化縣 當務委員會報告에 의하면 「日本政府는 滿蒙을 自己 掌中에 엿는 제일 수단으로 多數한 朝鮮人을 滿蒙에 移住케 하고 제이 수단으로는 그들의 朝鮮人을 中國에 歸化케 하고저 목하 그 실현을 劃策 중이고 제삼의 수단으로는 이들 歸化한 朝鮮人을 이용하야 滿蒙의 실력을 잡게하고 그리하야 臨機相應한 태도에 出하려는 것이라고」 햇다. 그가 朝鮮人의 農民이 가장 많이 살고 잇는 西間島 通化縣의 보고이니 朝鮮人 驅逐問題가 그 어떤 것임을 알 수 잇겟다. 그우에 朝鮮人은 赤化하고 잇다는 口實과(그런 경향이 없지도 않으나) 武器로는 三矢協定이란 것이 잇지 않은가.
中國의 排日運動에는 이제 그 큰 것만이라도 大凡 9回이엇으니 제1회는 明治 41년 3월에 神戶辰馬商會의 기선 第二辰丸이 澳門銃砲店의 주문을 밭고 그 銃器彈藥을 가득이 싯고 澳門에 도착하려할 때에 돌연히 中國의 軍艦에 捕獲掠奪되어섯든바 日本政府는 항의하야 일은 무사히 해결되엇으나 中國은 日本은 革命黨에게 무기의 密輸入을 도모하야써 中國의 내란을 助長하는 것이라고 排日運動이 일어나고 그가 廣東, 香港을 중심으로 하고 南中國 일대에서는 8개월간이나 계속되엇섯고 제2회는 安奉線改築問題에 대하야 日本의 自由行動宣言과 아울러 이루어진 日淸條約이 動因되어 東京에 유학하는 중국학생이 먼저 일어나서 利權의 회수를 ＜2＞ 부르짖자 계속하야 滿洲 全圓의 排日운동이 熾烈해섯고, 제3회는 例의 유명한 21個條의 문제를 動因으로 한 것으로 그는 中國 全土에 뻐치어 반년이상을 게속하야 排日운동이 잇엇는바 학생이 중심이 되고 소시민층까지 동원하야 그 결과는 21개조로 하야금 有耶無耶에 묻어버리엇으며, 제4회는 巴里講和會議에서 日中條約의 破棄 山東獨逸租借地의 직접 회수 등의 中國의 요구가 관철되지 못할 때에 그것을 원인으로 하고 中國의 全土는 물론이요. 南洋에까지 뻐치엇고, 제5회는 露西亞의 旅順, 大連의 租借期限이 만료되엇음으로 中國은 이때에 곳 旅大를 회수하려 하엿고 日中條約 無效通告에 대하야 日本은 그를 거절한 까닭에 國權回收 日貨排斥운동을 이르키엿는바 北中國은 물론이고 長江 일대에서 가장 猛烈하엿섯고 그가 게속하기를 5개월간이엇으며, 제6회는 上海紡績能業을 비롯하야 工部局問題 등을 원인으로 하고 대규모의 排外운동이 잇엇으니 노동자와 학생이 중심 세력이 되슖� 처음은 排日에서 비롯한 것이 南部中國에서는 排英운동으로 전환하엿섯다. 그리하야 그 타격은 막대한 것이엇으며 따라서 排外은동 國權回收 등의 운동이어서 그 조직의 擴大强化도 놀랠만 하엿거니와 종래의 그런 類의 운동과는 훨신 색다른 것이어서 경제투쟁이 그 主眼이엇으며, 제7회는 國民政府의 北伐軍이 맹렬한 기세로 北上하고 잇을때에 日本은 居留民의 보호라는 구실에서 山東일대에 出兵케되자 그가 北伐軍과 충돌하야 所謂 濟南事件이 생기고 그를 機로하야 排日운동이 일어낫으니 그는 山東의 출병반대와 濟南事件으로 1928년 6월에서 그 翌年 秋季에 이르기까지 國民黨 또는 南京政府의 선동에서 맹렬한 排日운동이 잇섯고, 제8회는 今回의 萬寶山事件인 것이다.
이것은 日本의 滿蒙에 대하야 特殊利權을 갖게 된 후 30년간에 中國의 排日운동의 그 현저한 것만을 뵈인 것이니 그밖에도 細小한 지역의 細小한 排日운동은 그때가 많앗다. 그러고 여기에 우리가 특히 주목할 것은 中國의 排外운동이라면 언제나 排日운동이엇다는 것이니 그만큼 中國의 成長發展에 日本의 그와 相衝하고 잇음을 알겟다. 그리고 그 운동의 자최를 도려다 보면 처음은 다만 被壓迫民族의 反撥운동이란 그런 막연한 운동이엇으나
21個條約 當時에 와서는 日貨不買, 日貨通關手續拒絶, 日本紙幣의 不使用, 日本船舶의 貨物積載妨害 등으로 학생이 그 中樞가 되어 經濟條件을 투쟁의 조건으로 제시하엿엇고 그가 다시 上海事件(제6회) 당시에는 노동자가 前衛가 되고 학생이 호응하며 그 외의 일반 경제단체와 상공업자가 그에 追從하게 되니 운동은 크게 힘을 얻게 되고 그 결과는 租借地의 奪回 關稅의 自主란 2大 收穫을 얻게 된 것이엇다. 이리하야 종래의 觀念的이든 被壓迫民族의 반발운동이 점차 정치운동에 전환하얏고 그가 다시 경제투쟁에 再轉하얏다는 것이다. 더욱이 그가 今回의 萬寶山事件에 와서는 上海의 特別市黨部가 掛日貨의 宣言을 발표하자 上海市商會와 市黨部가 별개로 독립적으로 排日게획을 하고 전연히 경제적 행동만을 표방하고 일어난 것 등으로 보아 그를 명백히 알 수 잇고 그 主動部는 實業團의 손에 잇다는 것이다.
이러한 經路에서 中國의 排日운동은 성장하고 진전되어 왓다.

中國의 공업화는 國民黨 이래의 大政策으로 國權回復 운동이 어느 정도의 성공을 보게되고 關稅自主가 실현되면서 急速한 템포로 그가 진전을 보게 되엇다.
여기에서도 廠法을 設하고 外人의 기업을 압박하며 더욱이 銀價의 低落으로 外人의 기업이 막대한 타격을 받고 잇는 동안에 中國은 그 機를 타서 日資排斥운동을 하며, 大連港의 衰縮策을 弄하야 滿蒙의 日人企業에 크게 위협을 주고 잇다. 上海 實業團이 주동이 되어 外人工場의 제품은 運賃를 올리고 重稅을 부과하며 日資排斥, 5個年計劃 등을 발포하고 잇으니 그가 直刻으로 실시됨은 아니라도 결코 경시치 못할 경향이고, 그리고 中國人의 商工業은 날카로운 기세로 增長하고 잇다는 것이다. 따라서 後進工業國의 産品은 언제나 粗工業에서 비롯하고 잇음으로 중국시장에 가장 우세를 가지고 잇는 것은 日本이나 日本産品은 그 대부분이 粗工業이엇음으로 이제 가장 영향을 보게 되는 것도 日本일 것임은 물론이겟다. 현재 滿洲에서의 그 성적을 보면 아즉은 日本人이 先進인 만큼 그 경영에 잇어 優勢를 삼고 잇는 것이 사실이니 爲先 紡績에서 볼지라도 中國人 경영의 遼寧紡績廠이 日本系의 滿洲紡績, 滿洲福紡, 內外綿金州工場 등과 대립하고 잇으나 機械設備에 잇어 日系 3社는 11만錘가 넘고 遼寧은 3만錘라고 하며 製粉業에도 日本系의 産額은 3,4만袋가 넘으나 中國側의 그는 3割가량에 지나지 못하고 豆油業도 近年에 와서 중국이 비상한 기세로 增長하고 잇으나 역시 일본 세력의 3,4할에 지나지 못한다 하며 煙草는 日系와 英米系 中國系가 서로 角逐戰을 하고 잇고 洋火는 日本 中國 瑞西의 적이 쟁패전을 하고 잇든 바 중국측은 휴업공장이 속출하는 現勢이다.
