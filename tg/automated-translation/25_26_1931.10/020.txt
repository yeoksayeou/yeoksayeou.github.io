Preparation of the Foundation
						Ch'unwŏn

A political movement is the synthesis of all strengths. This is especially true in its incipient stages. A political movement certainly does not succeed merely by having large numbers like a disorderly crowd (烏合之衆). (Line omitted in original text) A political movement is the "powerful" movement of a firm union, gathered under a definite principle, of "powerful" individuals. Therefore, a political movement is the synthesis of all strengths. Thus, where there is no strength, there is no political movement. Still less can there be an incipient political movement. If one were to say there is, it would likely be just the empty talk of saying "we're doing it, we're doing it." And it would likely just be an insipid laughingstock that makes a lot of noise saying "we're doing it, we're doing it" and then quits. Among a populace that just says "we're doing it, we're doing it," there is never a truly powerful movement.

Strength is indestructible (不滅). It does not arise from nothing (無), nor does small become large. There is only the most powerful utilization of strength by employing products of intellectual power like levers (槓杆) or gears (齒輪). Strength that did not exist does not come into being just because one shouts, "We have strength!"

In Chosŏn, for over 20 years, the sound of "we're doing it, we're doing it" has been quite loud. However, there were not many who gave much consideration (顧慮) to the sincere movement to cultivate the strength to be able to act. "When could we cultivate strength while delaying for months and years (遷延歲月)? Let's just go forth with what we have"—this was the conscious or unconscious slogan (標語) of the majority of our nationalist activists (民族運動者) over the past 20 years. What has been the result? Only noise was heard; little in the way of deeds was seen.

Now is the time for the people of Chosŏn to awaken to preparation. It is time to abandon cutting down the trees that already exist and instead plant even one tree. When will the trees grow if we plant them now? However, unless the time comes when the trees we have cultivated with our own hands have grown, our architectural designs will forever be empty documents on paper.

Then, what is this preparation of strength?

(1) Cultivating the necessary number of skilled individuals (技術者) (political, industrial, educational, etc.) among the 10 million people of Chosŏn to manage national life autonomously.
(2) Uniting and training such skilled individuals under the unified principles and discipline (規律) of the national movement.
(3) Giving the general populace literacy, common sense (常識), and civic (公民的) training.
(4) Concentrating the financial power (財力) sufficient to manage the great tasks mentioned above.

Whatever the future political form of the Chosŏn nation may be, whatever the principle of social organization may be, the preparation of strength mentioned above is absolutely the foundation. Can one claim to think about the path forward (進路) for the Chosŏn nation while denying this? (Ch'unwŏn) <1>

**Summary**

Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu) argues that effective political movements, especially nascent ones, require tangible strength built through systematic preparation, not just large numbers or enthusiastic pronouncements. He criticizes the past two decades of the Korean nationalist movement for prioritizing immediate action without adequate groundwork, resulting in noise but few achievements. He outlines four key areas for preparation: cultivating skilled experts in various fields, unifying and training these experts under a common ideology, educating the general populace (literacy, common sense, civic training), and accumulating the necessary financial resources. This foundational work, he insists, is essential regardless of Korea's future political structure.

From a historical perspective, this article is significant as it clearly articulates the "gradualist" or "cultural nationalist" approach advocated by figures like Yi Kwangsu during the colonial period. It reflects the internal debates within the Korean independence movement regarding strategy—contrasting this emphasis on capacity-building with more radical or immediate confrontational approaches. Historians may find it particularly interesting as an example of arguments that, while framed as pragmatic preparation for self-governance, were later criticized by some as accommodating or even collaborating with the colonial regime's constraints by delaying direct political action.

基礎의 準備
						春園
政治運動은 모든 힘의 종합이다. 그것이 草創的인 때에 더욱 그러하다. 政治運動은 결코 烏合之衆으로 名數만 많다고 되는 것이 아니다.(1行 中略-원문) 政治運動은 「힘 잇는」 개체들의 일정한 주의 밑에 모여진 굳은 단결의 「힘잇는」 움지김이다. 그럼으로 政治運動은 모든 힘의 종합이다. 그럼으로 힘이 없는 곳에 政治運動은 없다. 하믈며 草創的인 政治運動은 잇을 수 없다. 잇다하면 그것은 「합네, 합네」하는 부인 말뿐일 것이다. 그러고 「합네, 합네」하고 떠들기만 하다가 고만두는 슴거운 웃음거리뿐일 것이다. 「합네. 합네」하는 민중속에 정말 힘잇는 운동은 없는 법이다.
힘은 不滅이다. 無에서 생기지도 아니하고 小가 大되는 것도 아니다. 오직 槓杆이라든지 齒輪같은 知力의 産物을 이용하야 힘을 가장 힘잇게 이용함이 잇슬뿐이다. 「우리는 힘이 잇다!」하고 떠든다고 없던 힘이 생기는 것이 아니다.
朝鮮에는 20餘年來로 「합네, 합네」하는 소리가 자못 컷다. 그러나 할 수 잇는 힘을 길우자는 정성된 운동에는 많이 顧慮하는 이가 없엇다. 「언제, 遷延歲月하고 힘을 길우랴, 잇는대로 가지고 나가자」하는 것은 지나간 20년의 우리네 民族運動者 다수의 意識的, 또는 無意識的 標語엿엇다. 그 결과는 어떠한가 한갓 소리가 들릴 뿐이오 일이 보인 것이 적엇다.
이제는 朝鮮人도 준비에 자각할 때다. 이미 잇는 나무를 찍어서 없애는 것을 버리고 한개라도 나무를 심을 때다. 이제 나무를 길우어서 언제? 그러나 언제든지 우리 손으로 길운 나무가 자란 때가 아니고는 우리의 建築設計는 영원히 紙上空文이다.
그러면 그 힘의 준비란 무엇이냐.
(1) 1천만 朝鮮人 중에 自主的으로 민족생활을 경영하기에 필요한 數爻의 技術者(政治的, 産業的, 敎育的 등등)를 양성하는 것.
(2) 그러한 기술자들이 民族運動의 통일된 主義와 규율 밑에 단결되고 훈련되는 것.
(3) 일반민중에게 文字와 常識과 公民的 훈련을 주는 것.
(4) 이상의 대사업을 경영할만한 財力을 집중 하는 것이다.
朝鮮民族이 장래에 가질 政治的 形式이 무엇이든지 사회조직의 원리가 무엇이든지를 물론하고 이상에 말한 힘의 준비는 절대로 기초가 되는 것이다. 이것을 부인하고도 朝鮮民族의 進路를 생각한다고 할 수 잇을가.(春園) ＜1＞
