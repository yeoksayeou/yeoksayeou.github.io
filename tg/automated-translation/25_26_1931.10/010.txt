Announcement

As we publish the October issue during a time when the turbulent state of affairs changes like a revolving lantern (走馬燈), there are inevitably many crude points. Fortunately, Mr. Kim Ilsŏng's (金一成) masterful work, the essay "Japan-China Relations in Manchuria and Mongolia (滿蒙) and Their Outlook," is, considering the times, truly something that must be read.

The special topics for this issue are "Utilizing the Pacific Conference" and "A Way Forward for Our Compatriots in Manchuria (在滿同胞)." Due to the rapid changes in the situation, it seems eight or nine times out of ten that the Pacific Conference will be aborted, but it is not being permanently suspended. Once the situation reaches a resolution, it will surely reopen, so it goes without saying that it should be kept in mind for reference. Furthermore, the subsequent issue of our compatriots in Manchuria will undoubtedly become even more critically relevant from now on, so there is a need to read it carefully (熟讀).

Among the pieces edited this time, those that could not be published due to unavoidable circumstances are the full texts of "Critiques of Personalities in Extraordinary Times (非常時人物論)" (Yi Kwangsu, 李光洙), "Yi Su Hŭng as Seen in Court (法廷에서 본 李壽興)" (Hong Chongin, 洪鍾仁), "On Colonies (植民地論)" (Yi Yang, 李亮), and within the article "Recollections of That Time (그 當時의 追憶)," the full texts of "1919" (Yi Yongsŏl, 李容卨) and "OO Women's Association (OO婦人會)" (Hwang Aedŏk, 黃愛德). We sincerely apologize to both the authors and the readers.

We hope for the enthusiastic support of everyone for the "All-Chosŏn (全朝鮮) Middle School Boys and Girls Literary Competition" announced in this issue. We request that all male and female students send in their finest works (力作). Please promote and recruit participants for the competition at each school, coordinate with Korean language (朝鮮語) and composition teachers, form teams if possible, select committee members, and send only the very best works. Also, schools wishing to participate must certainly do so starting from the first round (deadline: end of October). This way, the chance (機) of winning will be complete.

As currently planned, we intend to publish next month's issue as the "Special Issue on the General Trend of the Storm Clouds in the Far East (極東風雲)." We intend to commission writings from experts in various fields to delve into the causes and effects of the current trends. However, as the times are what they are, it is difficult to promise yet to what extent we will have the freedom of publication, but we intend to do our best.

Besides that, we intend to put more effort (用力) into features like "Monthly Critique of Personalities (人物月旦)" and "Short Critiques of Personalities (人物短評)," and aim to draw out original and interesting material. While we intend to avoid vulgarity (野卑), we strive further towards popularization and making things interesting.

The times are unfortunate; who knows what the future holds? Both you, esteemed readers (諸位), and we face a tomorrow that is equally difficult to predict (予測). We can only pray that you remain well (建在).

October 1, 1931
Tonggwang Company Staff (東光社 一同)

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the October 1931 issue of *Tonggwang* acknowledges the difficulties of publishing amidst a rapidly changing political situation ("turbulent state of affairs"). It highlights key articles on Manchuria-Mongolia (by Kim Ilsŏng), the potentially aborted Pacific Conference, and the plight of Koreans in Manchuria. The editors apologize for being unable to publish several articles, including pieces by prominent figures like Yi Kwangsu, likely due to censorship or sensitivity given the "unavoidable circumstances." They promote a nationwide student writing competition and announce plans for the next issue focusing on the "Storm Clouds in the Far East," while expressing uncertainty about the "freedom of publication." The piece ends on a somber note, reflecting the shared anxiety about the unpredictable future during this period. For historians, the article reveals the editorial constraints (censorship), the preoccupation with Manchuria following the recent Incident (September 1931), the attempt to engage youth through competitions, and the general atmosphere of political tension and uncertainty in colonial Korea. The mention of unpublished articles by specific authors offers clues into potentially sensitive topics of the time.

謹告
분분한 시국이 走馬燈과 같이 변하는 때에 10월호를 발행하게 되매 여러 가지로 跙악한 점이 많이 잇습니다. 다행히 金一成 선생의 역작인 『滿蒙의 日中關係와 그 展望』이란 논문은 시절에 비추어 불가불 一讀의 문자라고 생각합니다.
본호의 특별화제는 『太平洋會議의 利用』과 『在滿同胞의 活路』임니다. 시국의 급변으로 태평양회의는 十上八九 유산이 되는 것 같습니다 마는 영구히 중지되는 것이 아니요 시국이 일단락이 되면 다시 열릴 것이니까 참고로서 알아둘 것임은 물론인가 합니다. 그리고 다음의 재만동포문제는 물론 금후로 일층 중대한 관계가 잇을 것이니까 熟讀의 필요가 잇습니다.
금번 편집한 중에서 부득이한 사정으로 실리지 못하게 된 것은 『非常時人物論』(李光洙) 『法廷에서 본 李壽興』(洪鍾仁) 『植民地論』(李亮) 등 全文과 『그 當時의 追憶』이란 기사 중 『1919년』(李容卨) 『OO婦人會』(黃愛德) 등 全文이외다. 필자와 독자께 아울러 사죄하옵니다.
이번호에 발표된 『全朝鮮男女中等學生誌上競技大會』에 제군의 열열한 지지가 잇기를 바랍니다. 남녀학생 제군의 力作을 보내주시기를 바랍니다. 학교마다 경기에 참가할 사람을 선전모집하고 또 朝鮮語와 作文 선생과 연락하며 될 수 잇으면 팀을 작성하고 위원을 선정하야 가장 역작만을 보내주십시오. 그리고 참가할 학교는 반드시 제1차 경기(10월 말일 기한)부터 하십시오. 그러야 우승의 機가 완전합니다.
來月號는 지금 계획대로는 『極東風雲의 大勢號』로 발간코저 합니다. 각방면 전문가의 집필을 위촉하야 시국의 동향의 원인결과를 캐어보고자 합니다. 오직 시국이 시국이니만치 어느 정도까지 게재의 자유가 있을는지 아직 장담키는 어렵습니다마는 우리는 최선을 다해 보고저 합니다.
그리고 그 밖에는 人物月旦과 人物短評 등에 더욱 用力하려 하며 기발한 흥미제재를 끌어내려 합니다. 野卑는 피하려들지마는 통속화와 흥미화에는 일층 노력하고저 합니다.
시절이 불운하니 앞날이 어떨는지요. 諸位나 우리나 다같이 예측키 어려운 내일을 맞게 됩니다. 부대 건재하기를 빌뿐임니다.
1931년 10월 1일
東光社 一同
