Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Gangseollu Pavilion
						Chŏn Pongje

Like a shell cast off by a ghostly presence (幽露),
The grey mural painting (碧畵像) is stained by the stone marks thrown by countless travelers.
In a wretched atmosphere where the dark blue ceiling's dragon head (龍頭) stares, biting its red "tongue,"
The heart, clutching an aching chest, looking around the utterly empty hall—.

In the heavy and fearful spiritual aura (靈氣),
Gazing at the gaping cracks (龜烈) resembling tortoise shells on the dizzyingly high pillars,
Like the shadow of a rusted, bent long sword (長劒)!
Like the scar left by the sword thrown by a warrior falling with a final cry!
Ah, the heart, clutching an aching chest, looking around the utterly empty hall— sketching the outline (輪廓) of indignation's shadow (憤影) with stifling breath, amid the stench (惡醜) of cold coughed-up blood (解血) in a low pressure (低氣壓) like a scream (高喊), looking at the rotten pillars, like pallbearers (상여꾼) flusteredly carrying a funeral bier (葬輿) loaded with a corpse (송장) gone cold and limp, heading towards the grave <95>
The heart, clutching an aching chest, looking around the utterly empty hall—.

O image of yellowish-ocher dark brown,
Like the color of death collapsed in anger!
O face smiling a bitter smile with grey eyes filled with killing intent in countless bullet marks!
Ah! The heart turning away, trying not to see the sight
Like the empty shell of a corpse collapsed helplessly—.
The heart turning away, scattering bitter ashes (灰)
Into my chest, like a tomb of "Thunder"—.
-Strolling through Gangseollu (降仙樓) with Yu An, Kyu Wŏn, Namgung, brothers- <96>

**Summary**

This poem by Chŏn Pongje describes a visit to the decaying Gangseollu Pavilion. The speaker observes the stained walls, damaged dragon carvings, cracked pillars, and generally desolate atmosphere with a heavy, aching heart. The pavilion's ruin is depicted through dark and violent imagery – rust, blood, corpses, bullet marks, anger, and bitterness – creating a sense of oppressive decay and suffering. For historians, the poem offers insight into the bleak atmosphere and suppressed emotions possibly felt under Japanese colonial rule, using the dilapidated traditional structure as a metaphor for a wounded nation or culture, marked by violence (bullet marks) and enduring hardship with barely contained rage (bitter smile, indignation's shadow).

降仙樓
						全鳳濟
회색빛  碧畵像은 幽露의 버서던진
허울같이
뭇 나그네의 던진 돌자리로 얼룽지고
검푸른 천정의 龍頭가 붉은「혀」를 물고 응시하는 비참한 분위기속에서
앞은 가슴을 안고 휑 비인 堂안을 휘돌아보는 마음을-

무겁고 두러운 靈氣속에서
아득히높은 기둥의 헤적히벌린 龜烈을 바라보나니
녹쓰른 휘여진 長劒의 그림자처럼!
마즈막 부르지즘을 남기고 쓰러지는
용사의 던진 칼자리처럼!
아하 앞흔 가슴을 안고 휑비인 堂안을 휘돌아보는 마음을 -무더운 호흡에 憤影의 輪廓을 그리며 식은 解血의 惡醜를 高喊 같은 低氣壓속에서 힘없이 식어버린 송장을 실은  葬輿를 메고 허둥거리며 무덤을 향하는 상여꾼같은 썩은 기둥을 바라＜95＞
아푼 가슴을 안고 휑 비인 堂안을 휘돌아보는 마음을-

분노에 쓰러진 죽엄의 빛같은
황토흑갈색의 영상이여!
수없는 탄환자리에 살기뻣친 회색의 눈동자로 쓴 웃음을 웃는 얼굴이여!
아하! 힘 없이 쓰러진 송장의 허울같은
꼴을 안보러고 도라서는 마음을-
『우뢰』의 무덤같은 나의 가슴속에
쓰린 재(灰)를 뿌리며 도라서는 마음을-
-柳岸·揆元·南宮·兄과 降仙樓를 거르며-＜96＞
