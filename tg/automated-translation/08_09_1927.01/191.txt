**Summary**

This article from the January 1927 issue of *Tonggwang* magazine presents the first list of members of the Tonggwang Society (東光社, Tonggwangsa), based on applications received up to February 15, 1926. It organizes members by province and city/county within Korea (Chosŏn), and also includes members residing outside Korea, specifically in Tokyo, Japan, and China. The list concludes with a request for corrections and information about the dispatch of membership certificates. For historians, this membership roster offers a valuable snapshot of the geographical reach and composition of a significant Korean intellectual or social organization during the colonial period. The inclusion of individuals from various regions, institutional members like a seminary library and a girls' school alumnae association, female members (like Hong Esŭt'ŏ), and overseas Koreans in Japan and China highlights the diverse network associated with the Tonggwang Society in its early stages.
