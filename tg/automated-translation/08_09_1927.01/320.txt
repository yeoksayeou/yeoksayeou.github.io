**Body Text Translation:**
To Young Friends on New Year’s Day

Grow, flourish,
Grow without end,

Says the distant, great life
As its roots spread and branches extend,

The day blessings are heard once more
Is called Sŏul (New Year's Day).

Part 2
We’ve climbed one step higher,
We can see even farther now,

Whereabouts our true life
Might reside,

Believing it is right ahead, and then ahead again,
Let us go forth.

Part 3
Building a new life, striving onward,
We who have no time to cry endlessly,

Moment by moment, connection by connection,
It is Sŏul (New Year) and again Sŏul (New Year),

If even by days it would be far,
Need we speak of years?

From "Paekp'al Pŏnnoe" (108 Afflictions) <74>
<76>

**Summary**

This three-stanza poem, addressed to children on New Year's Day, encourages ceaseless growth, striving, and looking forward to the future ("our true life"). It emphasizes continuous effort ("connection by connection," "New Year and again New Year") and perseverance despite the long journey ahead, suggesting there is no time for despair. Attributed to Han Yongun's "Paekp'al Pŏnnoe," the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, likely carries undertones of national hope and resilience. Historians might find the poem's subtle call for progress and perseverance, framed for the next generation within the context of recurring 'New Years' (potentially symbolizing cycles of struggle and hope), indicative of indirect expressions of nationalist sentiment and cultural endurance under colonial rule.

기사제목	새해에 어린 동무에게
기사형태	시

새해에 어린 동무에게
느셔라 부르셔라
그지 업시 자라거라,

하고 먼 큰 목숨이
뿌리뿌리 버드실 제,

복 한번 다시 듯는 날
서울이라 함니다.

其二
또 한층 오라섯네
더욱 멀리 내다 뵈네,

우리의 참 목숨이
어대만치 잇삽던지,

맨 아페 다시 그 아피
겐 줄 알고 갑세다.

其三
세 목숨 짓고 저어
끈히 울 틈 업는 우리

시마다 이엄이엄
서을이오 또 서을을

날로도 멀겟삽거든
해로 말슴하리까.
