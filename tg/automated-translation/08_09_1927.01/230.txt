Magazine Title: 동광 제9호
Publication Date: 1927년 01월 01일
Article Title: 南大門 (Namdaemun Gate)
Author: 金麗水
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

**Body Text Translation:**

Namdaemun
						Kim Ryŏsu

Seoul is a happy capital city.
For it embraces Namdaemun in its breast.
Like a young man holding his beloved,
With joy and reassurance,
Its heart must be completely full.

During the ten years I lived through hardship,
What hope could I have possibly lived for? Yet,
Silently guarding Seoul in the morning mist,
I have lived looking upon the single Namdaemun.

The people of this capital are also like that.
When they, holding chests seemingly bursting with resentment,
Stumble from street to street, their steps faltering,
Who could have possibly comforted them?
There was no one, only Namdaemun alone existed.

When I, abandoned by all happiness,
Clenching red fists and crying out for death, ran about,
Namdaemun spoke to me in profound quietness:
"Endure and prepare! Now the promised day comes!" - <15>

Friend, if you too would open the door of your heart,
You would hear the profound voice of Namdaemun.
"O people who have lost hope, weary of waiting,
Prepare in deep emotion! The promised day is near."

When I quietly close my eyes and see Namdaemun,
There, gathering at the altar and offering prayers,
I have seen many poor and unfortunate souls.
"Come, Giant, oh come, Giant!
Promised day, come quickly!"

Today too, like my beloved Poksun,
I strolled beside Namdaemun in the early morning.
Watching over our ancestors, us, and also protecting our descendants,
Possessing the mercy of parents and the dignity of a prophet,
I could not leave its presence for a long time.
-1926- <16>
<15-16>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Ryŏsu portrays Namdaemun, the southern gate of Seoul, as a profound symbol of solace, resilience, and hope for the Korean people, likely during the Japanese colonial period. The poet describes the gate as a comforting presence, offering strength to the suffering populace and the poet himself. Namdaemun is personified, speaking words of encouragement, urging endurance and preparation for a "promised day," interpreted as a day of liberation or restoration. The gate is depicted as a sacred site where the desperate pray for the arrival of a "Giant," a messianic figure or event, to bring about this promised future. The poem concludes by emphasizing Namdaemun's enduring role as a guardian connecting past, present, and future generations.

For historians, this poem is valuable as it reflects the psychological state and nationalistic sentiment among some Koreans under colonial rule. The personification of Namdaemun as a source of comfort and prophetic hope against oppression, the longing for a "promised day," and the invocation of a "Giant" savior offer insight into coping mechanisms, resistance expressed through symbolism, and the blend of despair and messianic expectation prevalent during that era. The use of older Korean spellings and grammar is also linguistically significant.

南大門
						金麗水
서울은 행복스러운 都城이외다
그는 그의 가슴에 南大門을 안엇스니
사랑하는 사람을 안은 젊은 사나이와 가티
즐거움과 든든함으로
그의 마음은 하나 가득할 것이외다

내가 고생살이 10년을 하는 동안에
무엇을 바라고서 살엇사오리까마는
새벽 안개 속에 묵묵히 서울을 지키고 잇는
南大門 하나를 바라보고 살어왓사외다

이 都城의 사람들이 또한 그러하외다
그들이 鬱憤하여 터질 듯한 가슴을 안꼬
거리에서 거리로, 비틀거리는 발길을 옴길 때
누가 그들을 慰勞하여 주엇사오리까
업사외다, 오직 南大門 하나이 잇슬 뿐이외다.

내가 모든 행복으로부터 버림을 밧고
붉은 주먹을 쥐고 죽엄을 부르지즈며 뛰어 다닐 때
南大門은 그윽한 중에 나에게 말하엿사외다
『참고 준비하라! 이제 약속한 날이 온다!』고- ＜15＞

친구께서도 만약 마음의 문을 열으신다면
南大門의 그윽한 말소리를 들어시리다
『기다림에 지처 소망을 일허버린 백성들이여
감격한 중에 준비하라! 약속한 날이 가까웁다』는

내 고요히 눈을 감고 南大門을 볼 때에
그 곳에 祭壇 모우고 기도들이는
가난하고 불행한 만흔 목숨을 보앗사외다
『巨人이 오소서 거인이 오소서
약속한 날 어서 오소서』

오늘도 나는 나의 사랑하는 福順어와 가티
이른 아침에 南大門의 겨틀 거닐엇사외다
우리 조상과 우리를 보고 또 우리 자손을 지킬
어버이가튼 慈悲와 예언자가튼 위엄을 가진
그의 아플 오랫동안 떠나지 못하엿사외다
-1926- ＜16＞
