Hooray! Hooray! Posŏng

Posŏng College (普成전문학교), possessing a long history of 30 years, has come to see a new dawn (曙光) thanks to the benevolent donation (喜捨) from the clan (門中) of Messrs. Kim Kijung (金祺中) and Kim Kyŏngjung (金暻中).

(Top) Scene of the memorial service (追悼會 光景) for the school founder (校主), Mr. Yi Yongik (李容翊氏) (March 17, Kwangmu 11 [1907])
(Middle) Founder of Posŏng College, the late Duke Ch'ungsuk (忠肅公), Mr. Yi Yongik (李容翊氏)
(Bottom) Successive principals (歷代교장) (from right) Yu Sŏngjun (兪星濬), Ko Wŏnhun (高元勳), Hŏ Hŏn (許憲), Pak Sŭngbin (朴勝彬)

(Top) Korean students (조선학생) from 30 years ago, scene of the Posŏng College first anniversary ceremony (창립 일주년기념식 광경) (photographed April 3, Kwangmu 10 [1906])
(Bottom) Posŏng College during its Law and Commerce School (法律商業學校) era

**Summary**

This short news article from June 1932 reports that Posŏng College (now Korea University), a Korean institution with a 30-year history, faced a brighter future due to a significant donation from the Kim Kijung and Kim Kyŏngjung clan. The article includes several historical photos: a memorial service for the founder Yi Yongik (1907), a portrait of Yi Yongik, a photo of past principals, a picture from the school's first anniversary (1906), and an image of the school during its earlier phase as a Law and Commerce School. For historians, the article is valuable as it highlights the precarious existence of private Korean educational institutions under Japanese colonial rule and the crucial role of private philanthropy, particularly from traditional clan structures (*munjung*), in sustaining them. The images provide rare visual documentation of the early years of a major Korean university and its key figures.
