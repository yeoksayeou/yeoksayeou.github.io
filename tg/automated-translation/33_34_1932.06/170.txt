Publication Date: 1932-06-02
Article Title: 鍾路夜話 (Chongno Night Talk)
Author: 壁耳子 (Pyŏkija)
Article Type: 세태비평 (Critique of the Times)

**Chongno Night Talk**
By Pyŏkija

A. City slicker living in Seoul
B. Country bumpkin living in the countryside

A. When did you arrive?
B. What does it matter when I came? I'm here. How's the fun in Seoul? Anything special going on?
A. You could say there is, and you could say there isn't. How could there not be anything special going on in Chosŏn, the center of all things political, economic, social, educational—all the "-icals"?
B. So, what's the biggest topic of gossip in Seoul these days? Tell me step by step. Even if a country bumpkin comes to Seoul and stays for just one day, it's customary to bring back some Seoul news.
A. Look over there. How does it feel looking down from high up in this four-story Western-style building on Chongno intersection? That building across the street was originally built as Kolburan's (골부란) electric company. Thirty years ago, at that time, top-knotted sightseers clicked their tongues, saying it was the grandest building in Chosŏn. But even looking at it today, though it's not that big, doesn't it look quite pretty and sturdy? It became the Chongno Police Station, and these days it's become the Simple Insurance Audit Section. When you think about the interrogation records concocted inside there, and the number of people who passed through that detention room, it still feels terrifying somehow.
B. Indeed. The fame of Kolburan's company building was overshadowed by the three-story Western-style Youth Hall (YMCA) right next to it, and now this building we're sitting in, the Christian Literature Society building, boasts four concrete stories and an elevator, so it seems Kolburan's relic has considerably lost its authority.

**Department Store Ballad**

A. Well, looking at it this way, in Seoul, the center of our Chosŏn, and right in its heart, the Chongno area, does it seem like the big-nosed people (Westerners) have taken all the prime spots for large buildings? Kolburan's electric company, then the Youth Hall built with Wanamaker's (와나메커) money, and then this Christian Literature Society. With this lineup, how can the faces of Chosŏn people not flush with pride? That's why the Tonga Department Store building absolutely must become the pride of the Chongno intersection. Although its floor space may be small, it's four Western-style stories high, complete with an elevator, steam heating, a restaurant, and a rooftop garden—meticulously set up like a model department store. No matter what anyone says, it must be raising the spirits of Chosŏn people tremendously.
B. So, did Tonga Department Store build that building themselves?
A. I don't know the details, but I heard one of the Min family built it, and they rented it for a monthly rent of one thousand wŏn.
B. Goodness! One thousand wŏn is two years' worth of food for a country fellow. You mean they can pay that every month and still make ends meet?
A. Whether they'll make a profit or not, we'll have to wait and see. But I went in once, and it seemed difficult for Chosŏn people to do business there. Since it just opened, sightseers were flooding in like waves, making it hard to even move. But looking around, most were either kids with shaggy hair, or folks in turumagi robes dripping with grime, or else friends who couldn't even afford a turumagi crowded the place. The smell of sweat and dirt hit your nose sharply. Setting aside the matter of atmosphere, those customers barely seemed capable of spending even ten chŏn. Yet, if you go to Chongjaok or Samwŏn, there are many customers who look like they could spend a bit of money. Besides, over there, not only Japanese customers but also many Chosŏn customers go, but do Japanese customers even show their faces here? It's disadvantageous this way and that. If Chosŏn people want to see profit in business, they should definitely advance into Chin'gogae. That's what I think.
B. Anyway, even having something like this is quite something.
A. Hey, watch your words. A country bumpkin saying things like that could get you into trouble. The other day, a mother and daughter supposedly went to inspect the XX Ilbo newspaper office and made a remark like, "This is quite something," and now the reporters at that newspaper are badmouthing her, calling her an arrogant bitch.
B. It *is* quite something, but even to a country bumpkin's eyes, a couple of unfortunate things are, firstly, it's too small, and trying to fit in too many display cases (진렬장) has made the customer walkways narrow, which is a big mistake.<41> It would have been better if they had reduced the display cases and made it more spacious. And on the very bottom floor, it looks like a jewelry store and a watch shop rented space separately, but partitioning the place off vertically like that was a huge blunder. Leaving it wide open would give the department store a better look, make the watch and jewelry shops seem more impressive, and attract more customers, wouldn't it?
A. As a friend said, it seems the only place in Tonga that might make a profit is the restaurant. Of course, it's an imitation of others, but it's well done. It's a bit disagreeable that the Chosŏn food, which is supposedly "temporarily under preparation," isn't available yet, and they still have things like bento (벤또) and sushi (스시). But two pieces of bread and a cup of coffee (카피) for ten chŏn is cheap. For a gentleman's dignity, it's awkward to eat just ten chŏn worth and leave, but what can you do when funds are short?
B. Hwasin Department Store is giving away a house, they say. I should try to win that thing.
A. With the appearance of Tonga, Hwasin also seems to have felt compelled to at least clean up its appearance, putting out full-page advertisements (전폐지 광고) in various newspapers to commemorate its incorporation as a joint-stock company and launching a major publicity campaign. Giving away a house as a prize (경픔) is also proof that they're using their heads, even if it's imitation. It's said that Pak Hŭngsik's hand was involved behind the scenes in the establishment of the joint-stock company. If you add Hwasin to Sŏn'gwang Printing and Sŏnil Paper, you have to say it's quite respectable for the business of a young man not yet thirty.
B. I heard a rumor in the countryside that Pak Hŭngsik might be getting involved in newspapers too. Is that true?
A. Who knows? Since Mr. Pak runs a paper company (지물회사), he must have deep connections with various newspaper companies. There was even an incident where he seized a rotary press as collateral for unpaid paper costs (조이값 외상), so perhaps he might naturally get drawn into that field. But rumors like this are extremely dangerous. They can cause unexpected harm to unrelated people, so let's drop it.

**News of the Newspapers**

B. Speaking of newspapers, what's the reason for the Chungang Ilbo suspending publication?
A. Who can know the inside story? Based on what I've heard, they originally started with thirty thousand wŏn. They had been paying salaries well, but a while ago, they supposedly called in several members of the editorial staff and asked them to work without pay. This resulted in the resignations of people like Ryu Kwangryŏl and Pae Sŏngnyong. After that, they apparently couldn't pay wages for a month, leading to a strike disturbance. Besides that, there are many rumors (류언비어)—that there's secret strife (암투) between Business Manager Kim Ch'ansŏng, Editorial Department Head Kim Namju, and President Ro Chŏngil, or that the president has lost favor within the company—but who knows which story is true? Anyway, it's a fact that a strike occurred, even involving a hunger strike (헝거스트라익), and it was resolved by giving the workers about three hundred wŏn. It's also a fact that during this time, they submitted a notice (휴간게) for a two-month suspension of publication.
B. I heard that Tonga Ilbo's Business Department Head Sŏ Ch'un became head of the Research Department, and Research Department Head Kim U'p'yŏng became head of the Business Department. What's the inside story on that?
A. Inside story? There are at least three theories about the inside story. Some say this was favoring Sŏ Ch'un, as the Research Department head position is a ladder to becoming Editor-in-Chief. Others say it was a demotion (좌천) for Kim U'p'yŏng, and still others say it was a demotion for Sŏ Ch'un. However, as far as I know, none of these three theories are accurate, and the real reason won't be known unless we hear it from President Song Chinu.
B. I saw in the newspaper that at the Chosŏn Ilbo, Yu Chint'ae became President, Kwŏn Tŏkkyu became Acting Editor-in-Chief, and Han Kiak became Managing Director. Are there no other changes besides those?
A. The former Acting Editor-in-Chief, Ri Sŏn'gŭn, couldn't stand it after that incident concerning the consumption of relief funds (례의 구제금 소비사건) and took a job at Songdo Higher Common School and ran off (내빼고). Apart from that, they say about ten people were purged (도태) at once across the business and editorial departments. Because of this, the purged individuals circulated a statement (성명서). Amidst this, arrest warrants (구인장) were issued by the prosecutor's office (검사국) for the president and the business manager regarding that incident (례의 사건), and they ended up being imprisoned (입감하게 되니). So it seems a reform of the executives couldn't be avoided.
B. There's a rumor that a whirlwind (선풍) is blowing at the Maeil Sinbo, isn't there?
A. There are rumors that Vice President Pak Sŏgyun is going to Kando as consul, and that certain individuals named Ri and Ch'oe are joining the company. Social Affairs Department Head Chŏng Inik is said to be going to Pongch'ŏn (Fengtian) to run a newspaper, and Editor-in-Chief Ri Iksang is supposedly heading to some other field. But these are all still just rumors (풍설), so who knows until they become reality?
B. Enough about newspapers. Tell me a bit about the movements of so-called controversial figures (문제인물).
A. Is there any reason for controversial figures to exist in a Chosŏn without controversies? Well, let's have a glass of ice water (어름냉수) and talk slowly... (Continued in next issue)<40>

**Summary**

Two acquaintances, one from Seoul and one visiting from the countryside, discuss recent happenings in Seoul, specifically Chongno. They talk about the changing cityscape, noting new multi-story buildings like the Christian Literature Society building and the Tonga Department Store, which overshadow older landmarks. They critique the new Korean-owned Tonga Department Store for its small size, cramped layout, and less affluent clientele compared to businesses in the Chin'gogae area, while also mentioning competitor Hwasin Department Store's modernization efforts led by the young entrepreneur Pak Hŭngsik. The conversation then shifts to gossip about the newspaper industry, detailing the financial troubles and strike at the Chungang Ilbo, personnel shuffles at the Tonga Ilbo, and a purge and arrests at the Chosŏn Ilbo, along with rumors surrounding the Maeil Sinbo and Pak Hŭngsik's potential entry into the newspaper business.

This dialogue offers historians a glimpse into urban development debates in colonial Seoul, the challenges faced by Korean businesses competing in a modernizing economy, the perceived social stratification among consumers, and the volatile, gossip-filled, and potentially politically pressured environment of the Korean press during the 1930s. The specific details about buildings, business strategies (like giveaways), and newspaper personnel issues provide concrete social and economic history insights.

鍾路夜話

A. 서울사는 시굴띠기 B. 시굴사는 시굴띠기
A. 자네 언제 왓나?
B. 언제 온건 알아 무엇하나. 왓네. 서울재미가 어떤가. 별일 없나?
A. 없다면 없고 잇다면 잇지. 조선이 정치적 경제적, 사회적, 교육적, 모든 「적」의 중심진데 어째 별일이 없겟나.
B. 그래 요새 서울에 제일 말성거리 되는 사건이 무엇인가. 차레차레 알려주게. 시굴띠기가 서울와서 단 하로를 잇다 나려가도 서울소식을 좀 무역해 가지고 가야 되는 법이니까.
A. 자, 저것 보게. 종로 네거리 4층 양옥에 높이 앉아 나려다 보는 맛이 어떠한가. 저 맞은 집이 본래는 「골부란」의 전긔회사로 지은 집일세. 삼십년전 그 당시에는 조선제일의 굉장한 집이라고 상투쟁이 구경꾼이 혀를 홰홰 내저엇다 데마는 오늘 보아도 집이 그리 크지는 못하나 꽤 이뿌고 튼튼하게 생기지 안엇나. 그것이 종로경찰서가 되엇다가 요새는 간이보험감사과로 되엇네그려. 저 속에서 꿈여낸 취조서 긔록, 저 류치장속으로 드나든 사람의 수효를 생각해보면 어떤지 지금도 무시무시하다니까.
B. 올해. 골부란의 회사집의 명성이 바로 그 옆 청년회관 3층 양옥 때문에 눌리우더니 인제 우리가 앉은 이집 즉 예수교서회 빌딩이 콩크리트 4층에 엘레베터를 모섯으니 인제 골부란의 유적도 그 권위가 매우 손상된 샘일세그려.

백화점 타령
