Short Poems Celebrating Spring
						Kim Ansŏ
1
The water has reached the early rice paddy.
Has the water reached your heart?
In the mercury-like white water (水銀같이 하얂 물에), the spring breeze gathers.

The spring breeze dances.
It sways and dances.
Has spring entered your heart? The spring breeze dances.

In the mirror-like clear water, the Mountain lies down to sleep.
The mountain lies down to sleep.
My beloved lies down to dream.

2
"Spring fills heaven and earth, blessings fill the home (春滿乾坤 福滿家라)."
Oh man, say no more.
Though spring fills the sky (하눌空中) above,
In my heart, all blessings are gone,
My heart flutters in the restless spring breeze.

3
Jihwaja, oh my heart, the spring wind blows.
Like a top (*p'inggu* 핑구), please spin round and round.
My heart is heavy, there's no way for it to lift.
Like a top, oh my heart, please spin round and round.
-1932. 3. 14- <53>

**Summary**

This series of short poems by Kim Ansŏ adopts a folk song style (民謠體) to explore the feelings evoked by spring. The verses contrast the vibrant imagery of nature awakening—water filling rice paddies, reflections, dancing breezes, sleeping mountains—with the poet's internal state of melancholy and restlessness. Despite the traditional association of spring with blessings and joy, the poet feels empty and heavy-hearted, wishing for the carefree abandon of a spinning top. For historians, the poem offers a glimpse into personal sentiment during the Japanese colonial period. The juxtaposition of nature's beauty with inner turmoil, and the subtle subversion of the traditional "blessings" trope, might reflect the underlying anxieties or suppressed national spirit of the time, expressed through lyrical and traditional Korean forms.

賞春小詩
						金岸曙
1
올벼논에 물 다앗소.
자네맘에 물 다앗소.
水銀같이 하얂 물에 봄바람이 어려노오.

봄바람이 춤을추오.
건들건들 춤을추오.
자네맘에 봄들엇소. 봄바람이 춤을추오.

거울같이 개인물에 山이눕어 잠을자오.
山이눕어 잠을자오.
님이눕어 꿈을꾸오.

2
「春滿乾坤 福滿家라」
이사람아 말을마소.
하눌空中 봄이차니
이내맘엔 福다 가고
싱숭생숭 봄바람에 맘이날데.

3
지화자 이내맘아 봄 바람분다.
핑구처럼 핑핑핑 돌아주렴아.
이내맘 무겁고야 들뜰길없다.
핑구처럼 이맘아 핑핑돌렴아.
-1932. 3. 14- ＜53＞
