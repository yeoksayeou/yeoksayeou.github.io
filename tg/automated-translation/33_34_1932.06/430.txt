**Summary**

This short article presents the answers to a mock or possibly real "Modern University Entrance Exam" published in the June 1932 issue of *Tonggwang* magazine. The answers cover Korean history (Dangun calendar year, promulgation of Hangul), geography (Korean provinces, cities in Manchuria with Korean populations, international locations), contemporary political ideology (Sun Yat-sen's principles), and practical knowledge (train fare height standard). For historians, this piece offers insight into the general knowledge expected of educated Koreans during the colonial period, touching upon Korean identity (Dangun calendar, Hangul), awareness of the Korean diaspora in Manchuria, knowledge of international affairs (Turkey, Switzerland, China via Sun Yat-sen), and practical aspects of modern life under Japanese rule (standard measurements). The specific geographical focus on Manchurian border regions (Hunch'un, Wangch'ing, Yŏngil, Hwaryong, Kirim, Hoeryŏng) is particularly notable.
