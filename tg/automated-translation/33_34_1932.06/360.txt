Magazine Title: 동광 제34호
Publication Date: 1932년 06월 02일
Article Title: 땅파는 사람들 (People Digging the Earth)
Author: 露園
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

People Digging the Earth
						Rowŏn

Under the sun beating down like spring
Today too, the laborers
Are digging the earth.
The pickaxe
High
When they lift it into the air
When it strikes with a thud—
Look! On their two iron-like forearms
The bulging Muscles (筋肉)
(Following 6 lines omitted - original text)
-1931, 8, Observing the Road Reconstruction Work- <84>

**Summary**

This short poem by Rowŏn, published in 1932, depicts laborers digging earth under a harsh sun during road construction work observed in August 1931. It focuses on the physical exertion of the workers, highlighting the moment the pickaxe strikes the ground and the bulging muscles on their arms. The poem offers a glimpse into the lives of manual laborers during the colonial period in Korea. For historians, it provides a contemporary artistic representation of labor, potentially reflecting social conditions and the physical demands placed on workers during infrastructure projects under Japanese rule, although explicit critique is absent in the provided excerpt.
