Article Type: 시 (Poem)

On New Year's Eve
						Yi Ch'o-jŏk

On the last night of the twelfth lunar month
I wish heavy snow would sweep in

Pouring down, coming, piling up (˜¤고), and coming again
Covering the brick house in the village across, the shortcut in the back alley
And even my heart
I wish it would bury everything

Then, on the path of a pure white (純白) heart
Leaving clear, vigorous footprints
Forcefully shaking off the clinging, lingering attachment (未練)
Wouldn't I stride forward powerfully into the new steps of a new day?

On the last night of the twelfth lunar month
I wish heavy snow would sweep in <82>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Ch'o-jŏk expresses a strong desire for purification and renewal on New Year's Eve. The speaker wishes for heavy snowfall to completely cover the past—the landscape, the village, and even their own heart—erasing everything. This cleansing would allow them to step forward into the new year with a pure heart and renewed vigor, free from lingering attachments or regrets (未練). From a historical perspective, the poem's intense longing for erasure and a fresh start might reflect broader sentiments of yearning for change or escape from the difficulties and constraints of life under colonial rule in 1932 Korea. The imagery of burying the old and striding into the new could resonate with desires for national or personal renewal.
