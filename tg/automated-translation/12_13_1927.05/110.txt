Bride and 5 Other Pieces
						Yohan

Bride

If it were not for you,
What use is this black hair?
If today had not come,
What use is this fair face?
I have offered this one body,
Do with it as you please.

Song (1)

To offer to you,
I sing this song.
Though my thoughts are young,
And the words may be clumsy,
I have offered all my devotion.
Please do not refuse it.

Song (2)

I sing and sing again,
And send it off to you,
But mountains and rivers block the way.
Might you perhaps hear it?
If the spring breeze enters your lapels,
Consider it my soul.

Letter

On a day the sun shone and the wind was calm,
I climbed Namsan,
And the flowers bloomed by the spring rain
Greeted me gladly.
I think again
Of those words of yours.

Balsam

The balsam tree in the garden,
Whose shoots grew well in the spring rain,
On every single branch,
Red flowers have bloomed.
I wonder if the tree planted in your heart
Has also blossomed.

Falling Flowers

As rain falls and wind blows,
The blooming flowers will all fall.
They are flowers destined to fall once,
So should I lament their falling?
But they were meant to be shown to you,
So I hold them dear. <33>

**Summary**

This collection comprises six short poems by Yohan, likely expressing themes of love, devotion, longing, and connection through nature. The poems use simple, lyrical language to convey deep emotion, such as total self-offering ("Bride"), attempts to communicate despite separation ("Song 2"), finding solace and memory in nature ("Letter", "Balsam"), and the poignancy of beauty meant for another ("Falling Flowers"). From a historical perspective, these poems are interesting examples of lyrical expression during the colonial period. While overtly personal, the themes of longing, devotion despite obstacles, and finding meaning in nature could potentially be interpreted by historians as subtle expressions of national sentiment or cultural identity persistence in a suppressed environment, using traditional poetic sensibilities.

신부 외 5편
						요한
신부
당신이 아니더면
검은머리 무엇하며
오늘이 없었더면
고운 얼골 무엇하리
이 한몸 바첫사오니
하실대로 하시라

노래(1)
그대에게 들이려고
이 노래 부르노니
어린 생각이라
말이야 됐건말건
정성껏 다 들이었오이다
마다하지 마소서

노래(2)
부르고 또 불어서
당신께 부치어도
강산이 막혔으니
행여나 들으서리
봄ㅅ바람 옷깃에 들거던
내 넋인줄 녀기소

편지
해나고 바람 잔날
남산에 올았더니
봄비에 핀 꽃이
반가히 맞더이다
그러한 당신의 말슴을
다시 생각 함니다

봉사
봄비에 순 잘았던
뜰 위에 봉사남은
가지가지마다
붉은 꽃 피었어라
님 맘에 내 심긴 나무도
꽃피온가 하노라

낙화
비오고 바람 부니
피던 꽃 다 지것다.
한번 질 꽃이오니
진다고 한하랴만
당신께 뵈려던 것이니
그를 아껴 함니다.＜33＞
