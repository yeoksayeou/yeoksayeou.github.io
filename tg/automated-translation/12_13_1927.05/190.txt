Haedong Soksohak
						Pak Chae-hyŏng, Original Author

Son Sun (孫順) was a person from the township of Moryang (牟梁). His father had died, and because his house was poor, he supported his elderly mother by working as a hired hand at others' houses. Son Sun had one young son who, at every mealtime, would snatch away and eat everything meant for the grandmother. Son Sun discussed this with his wife, and carrying the child on his back, went to the mountains to dig a hole in the ground and bury him there. But unexpectedly, he found a stone bell (石鐘) there. He then said to his wife, "The reason we obtained such a marvelous object today is largely thanks to this child, so burying him is not the right thing to do." And he returned home with the stone and the child. Then, he hung the stone bell from the house's crossbeam and struck it. The sound reached even the royal palace. King Hŭngdŏk (興德王) heard it and said, "From the western village comes the sound of a bell, beautiful to the ear. How truly strange." He immediately sent a retainer to investigate its origin. Upon learning the story, he said, "In ancient times, when a man named Kwak Kŏ (郭巨) <68> was about to bury his child, Heaven gave him a gold cauldron (金가마). Now, as Son Sun was about to bury his child, a stone bell emerged from the ground. These events, past and present, correspond with each other." He then bestowed upon Son Sun a house (一區) and granted him fifty sŏm of grain annually. (Tonggyŏngji 東京誌)

Pinyŏngja (不寧子) held the rank of Chunggun (中軍, General of the Center Army) in the country of Silla. During the reign of Queen Chindŏk (眞德王), the country of Paekche invaded. Kim Yu-sin (金庾信) led the troops to block them, fighting to the death until his strength was exhausted. He said to Pinyŏngja, "Only after it turns cold does one know that the pine and cypress wither last. Today's situation is urgent; if not you, who else could possibly, at this moment, make a great effort, step forward, and inspire the hearts of the entire army?" Pinyŏngja replied, "Among the many people here now, you entrust these words specifically to me. How well must you know me to do so? I can only repay this by dying." He came forward and said to his servant Hapchŏl (合節), "Today, I shall die, above for the country, and below for my friend (知己)." Then, holding his spear sideways, he exerted all his <69> strength, fought, and died. His son Kŏjin (擧眞) said, "Seeing my father die, how could merely preserving my own life be considered filial piety?" and he too fought and died. The servant Hapchŏl said, "When heaven has collapsed, what else is there to do but die?" and he also fought the enemy soldiers and died. Seeing this, the entire army was greatly moved, their courage surged, and they attacked and defeated the Paekche army. (Samguksa 三國史)

King Sinmu (神武王) had a son whose face was handsome, so the King loved him very much and named him Hodong (好童). The Queen, worried that the status of the eldest son might pass to him, tearfully approached the King and falsely accused, saying, "Hodong attempts to behave improperly towards me." Someone asked Hodong, "Why do you not try to explain this matter?" Hodong replied, "If I were to explain, I would only reveal my stepmother's wickedness and cause my father distress." And with that, he stabbed himself in the neck with a knife and died.

Lady Sin of Yŏngsan (靈山辛氏) was the daughter of Nangjang (郞將) Sa-ch'ŏn (斯천). When Japanese invaders (倭賊) plundered Yŏngsan <70>, Sa-ch'ŏn fled with his family. As they were trying to cross Myŏlp'o (蔑浦), the Japanese invaders pursued them, shot Sa-ch'ŏn dead, captured Lady Sin, and tried to take her with them, drawing a sword and threateningly advancing towards her. Lady Sin shouted and rebuked them, saying, "You scoundrels killed my father; you are enemies with whom I cannot live under the same sky (不共戴天). I would rather die here than follow you scoundrels and live!" She then grabbed the enemy's neck and kicked him. The enraged enemy finally killed her. Lady Sin was twenty years old at the time. When this deed became known to the court, a monument was erected to record her actions. (Yŏji Sŭngnam 輿地勝覽) <71>

**Summary**

This article presents four historical anecdotes from Korean history intended to exemplify Confucian virtues. The stories feature Son Sun (filial piety rewarded), Pinyŏngja and his son and servant (loyalty and sacrifice in battle), Prince Hodong (filial piety through self-sacrifice to avoid family conflict), and Lady Sin of Yŏngsan (chastity and defiance against invaders). These tales, drawn from historical records like the *Tonggyŏngji*, *Samguksa*, and *Yŏji Sŭngnam*, emphasize traditional moral values. From the perspective of Korean colonial history (published in 1927), this article is interesting as it promotes traditional Korean narratives and Confucian ethics—filial piety, loyalty, chastity, sacrifice—which could be interpreted either as a subtle assertion of Korean identity and heritage or potentially align with Japanese colonial narratives that sometimes emphasized shared East Asian Confucian values, albeit often to legitimize their rule or depict Koreans as needing guidance back to proper tradition. Historians might find the selection and framing of these specific stories useful for understanding the cultural and ideological currents among Korean intellectuals during the colonial period, particularly the use of history to teach morality. The apparent historical inaccuracy in the Hodong story (attributing it to King Sinmu of Silla instead of King Taemusin of Koguryŏ) is also noteworthy.

해동속소학
						朴在馨 原著
손순(孫順)은 모량(牟梁)고을 사람이외다. 아버지는 죽고 집은 가난함으로 남의 집에 품팔이 하여 늙은 모친을 봉양하고 지냄니다.손순이 어린 아들 하나가 있어 끼니 때마다 어머니 잡수실 것을 죄다 빼앗아 먹슴니다. 그 안해와 의론하고 아이를 업고 산으로 가아서 땅을 파고 거긔다 묻어버리려 하였더니 뜻밖에 거긔서 석종(石鐘)을 얻었음니다. 그러고 그 안해더러 말하되 「우리가 오늘 이러한 신긔한 물건을 얻게 된 것은 대개 이애을 덕븐인즉 묻는 것이 도리가 아니라.」하고 돌우 아이를 더리고 집으로 돌아오았음니다. 그리하여 그 석종을 집 들보에다 달어두고 치어 보았음니다. 그 소리가 대궐에까지 들리었음니다. 흥덕왕(興德王)께서 들으시고 「어-서녁 마을에서 종소리 나는데 듯기에 아름답구나. 참 이상한걸.」하시고 곳 신하를 보내어 그 유래를 들어 알으시고 말씀하시되 「옛적에 곽거(郭巨)란 사람이＜68＞ 자식을 묻으려 할 때에 하늘에서 금가마를 주시었는데 이제 손순이 자식을 묻으려하매 땅에서 석종이 생기었다 하니 전후의 것이 서로 부합한 사실이 되는구나. 하시고 손순에게 집 일구(一區)를 나리어 주시고 해마다 곡식 오십 섬씩 주시었음니다.(東京誌)
비녕자(不寧子)는 신라 나라의 중군(中軍) 벼슬로 있었음니다. 진덕왕(眞德王) 때에 백제 나라가 들어오아 침노하였음니다. 김유신(金庾信)이 군사를 거느리고 막는데 죽도록 싸움하여 힘이 다하였음니다. 비녕자에게 말하되 「춥고난 뒤에야 솔과 잣이 뒤에 마르는줄 안다 하였으니 오늘의 일 급한지라 자내 아니면 뉘 능히 이때 한번 크게 분발하여 앞서 나아가아 왼 군사의 맘을 격녀시킬 이가 있겠는가.」 하니 비녕자가 대답하되 「이제 하고나 많은 사람들 중에 유독히 내게 이러한 말씀을 부탁하시니 나를 얼마나 알았기에 이렇게까지 하십니까. 나는 다만 죽는 것으로나 이를 갚을 밖에 없음니다.」하고 나아오아서 그 종 합절(合節)더러 「오늘은 내가 위로 국가를 위하여 아레로 지긔(知己)를 위하여 죽으리라.」하고 곳 창을 비끼어 들고 힘을＜69＞ 다하여 싸우다가 죽었음니다. 그 아들 거진(擧眞)이 말하되 「아버지 죽는 것을 보고 그래도 살겠다고 구구히 생명보존이나 하려는 것이 어찌 효도일까 보냐」하고 싸우다가 죽었음니다. 종 합절이 말하되 「하늘이 문어지었는 바에 죽지 않고 무엇하리오.」하고 또 적병과 싸우다가 죽었음니다. 윈 군사들은 이를 보고 크게 감격하여 용긔를 크게 펼치어 백제의 군사를 치어 파하였음니다.(三國史)
신무왕(神武王)께서 아들을 낳았는데 얼굴이 이뿌게 생기었음으로 임검께서 매우 사랑하여 이름을 호동(好童)이라 하였음니다. 왕후가 장자의 명분이 그리고 돌아갈까 염녀하여 임검을 보고 울며 어쭙되 「호동이 내게 대하여 무례한 행동을 하려고 함니다.」고 이렇게 참소하였음니다. 어떤 이가 호동에게 「웨 이 일을 변명하려고 않는가.」 호동이 대답하되 「내가 만약 변명하고 보면 어머님의 악함을 들어내며 아버지의 근심을 끼치게 될 뿐이라.」하고 그만 칼로 목을 찔어 죽었음니다.
영산신씨(靈山辛氏)는 낭장(郞將) 사천(斯천)의 딸이외다. 왜적(倭賊)이 영산을＜70＞ 도적하매 사천이 식구를 더리고 피란하여 멸포(蔑浦)를  건느려 할 때에 왜적이 뒤를 좇아오아 사천을 쏘아 죽이고 신씨를 잡아서 더리고 함께 가자하며 칼을 빼어 들고 앞에 달기어 들어 위협하였음니다. 신씨가 소리를 지르며 꾸짖어 말하되 「너놈들이 내 바비를 죽이었으니 불공대천(不共戴天)의 원수라. 차라리 외에 죽을 찌언정 너놈들을 딸아가아 산단 말이냐.」하고 드디어 적의 목을 움키어 쥐고 발길로 차니 적이 분로하여 마침내 죽이었음니다. 신씨는 그때 나이 이십세라. 이 사실이 조정에 들리게 되어 비를 세우어 그 사적을 긔록하게 하였음니다. (輿地勝覽) ＜71＞
＜68-71＞
