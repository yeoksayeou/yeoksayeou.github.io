**Body Text Translation:**
Strawberry and 1 Other Poem
						Chŏng Pyŏngsun

Strawberry

On the grassy patch beside the mountain path
Strawberries have ripened
One berry, then another
Bright red strawberries

Once it was sweet potatoes
Sometimes potatoes
Now this land
Gives strawberries

Spring Stream

From the mountain, spring water
Through the cracks in the rocks
Flows
Flowing and flowing
Persistently over the black rocks
In the quiet mountain
It flows, singing
-1926, From an old manuscript- <33>

**Summary**

This publication features two short, simple nature poems by Chŏng Pyŏngsun. The first, "Strawberry," observes strawberries growing where staple crops like sweet potatoes and potatoes once grew. The second, "Spring Stream," depicts water persistently flowing from a mountain spring through rocks. From a historical perspective, the poems offer a glimpse into the natural imagery appreciated during the period. The mention of strawberries replacing other crops might subtly reflect changing agricultural practices or land use, even on a small scale. The use of older Korean spellings (e.g., 열렷네, 샛밝한, 샘몰) is also of linguistic interest to historians studying the evolution of the language during the colonial era.

딸기 외 1편
						鄭昞淳
딸기
산길옆 풀밭에
딸기가 열렷네
한알 또 한알
샛밝한 딸기

한때는 고구마
어느 땐 감자
지금은 이땅이
딸기를 주네

샘몰
산에서 샘물이
바위 틈으로
흐른다
흐르고 또 흘러
검은 바위 하도록
고요한 산에서
노래하며 흐른다
