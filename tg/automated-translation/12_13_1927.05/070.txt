Opinions of Various Notables Regarding Tonggwang

Tonggwang's 1st Anniversary Ch'oe Namsŏn, Kyemyŏngsa

Although a year can be a short period, if used meaningfully, it can also be a time that creates great value. Tonggwang's past year seems to have been precisely such a meaningful period.

Without needing further words, it is strikingly recognized by everyone's eyes how great an achievement Tonggwang has shown in the encouragement, refinement, purification, and deepening of the Chosŏn spirit (朝鮮心), which is currently the most urgent yet weakest aspect in Chosŏn. Readers' memories are fresh, requiring no renewed mention, of how many pages (페지), effort, topics, and content were dedicated to the promotion of the "Chosŏn Self" (朝鮮我), and how this was a distinctly outstanding feature among fellow magazines.

Furthermore, the banners Tonggwang advocated, such as group training (團體的 訓練) and the promotion of the spirit of strenuous effort (力行精神), were all our golden rules (金科玉條) at present. I believe this is one aspect of the edifying influence (薰化) of Teacher An Tosan [An Ch'angho], whom I know has a deep connection with Tonggwang. Recalling how I assisted with the arrangements of the Ch'ŏngnyŏn Haguhoe (Youth Study Association) for this spirit the year before last, I sincerely wish (心祝) that these precious seeds newly sown by Tonggwang will quickly yield abundant fruition.

I know Tonggwang's purpose is undoubtedly great. I also know that the past year's work, both in its ideals and achievements, must have been just the beginning of its duty. I hope that the fervent sincerity and firm will of all you members will demonstrate ever-increasing vigor and vigilance (乾惕) in spreading and realizing Tonggwang's principles, which can be called the highest correct view for Chosŏn today, achieving a geometric (幾何級數的) rate of cumulative progress in the coming year and infinite anniversaries (無窮週年) thereafter.
I believe the eternal life of Chosŏn flows alongside the Tonggwang spirit.

A Restorative Tonic for the Sick Yi Chongnin, Ch'ŏndogyo Wŏlposa

It is regrettable that I have not yet had the chance to fully read the contents of Tonggwang. Therefore, I lack the knowledge to comment on or express expectations for the journal. However, I imagine that the journal's principles are qualitative (質的) rather than quantitative (量的), and practical (實際的) rather than emotional (氣分的). Today, our process (過程) is moving from the quantitative to the qualitative, from the emotional to the practical.

Our future must also proceed in this manner. However, <26> looking at our current press circles (though perhaps not the fault of the press itself), it seems journalism is done for its own sake, rather than work being done for the sake of journalism. For a patient suffering for long years (年久歲深), whose energy is deficient and pulse is floating (氣虛脉浮 - metaphor for weakness), unable to move (動彈不得), would it not be a safer bet (萬全策) to try a restorative tonic (保元劑), even if slow (遲鈍), rather than using a temporary stimulant that risks extinguishing even the faintly flickering (耿耿히 斷續하는) hope? Also, isn't it rather (돌이어) more moral to promote good deeds (善) and provide a model (效則) than to expose evil (惡) and tell people not to act thus? This is my expectation for Tonggwang, and indeed my earnest hope for the press world in general.

In Lieu of Congratulations Kwŏn Sangno, Pulgyosa

Continue Well As You Are Hong Pyŏngsŏn, Ch'ŏngnyŏnsa

I approve of your magazine's great efforts in academic fields and its lack of bias (偏僻되지 안는 것) in principles. Your meticulous care (用意周到함) in Hangul usage is very good, but perhaps writing a bit more popularly (通俗) for the illiterate class would be better? I hope you continue well as you are in the future. (Interview)

Establish a Direction for Cultivation Suitable for the Future Yi Tonhwa, Sin'gan'gansa

Tonggwang, like its name [Eastern Light], has indeed added a light to our Chosŏn nation, an ancient Eastern country. Thus, Tonggwang's guiding spirit, setting self-cultivation (修養) as its goal, can be seen as having chosen a particularly appropriate path (指路) for the youth of Chosŏn who are in a transitional period (過渡期). However, just as there is old and new in all principles (事理), there must also be a strict distinction between old and new in cultivation. For instance, if the pinnacle (極致) of character is the perfect development of wisdom, virtue, and physique, wouldn't there be a distinction between cultivation suitable for the past and cultivation suitable for the future, even within wisdom and virtue? My <27> hope for Tonggwang lies here. Please guide us powerfully with a cultivation policy more suitable for the future.

Heighten the Spirit of Struggle Yi Sŏnghwan, Chosŏn Nongminsa

Focusing on Science and Cultivation is a Distinctive Feature Yi Sanghyŏp, Chung'oe Ilbosa

Despite being a magazine under the Publication Law, Tonggwang's focus on science and cultivation (修養) to advocate for character building is seen as radiating a distinctive color (異彩) in the magazine world. Furthermore, the absence of vulgar language in its articles is good. I hope it expands further in the future and makes many contributions to the populace. (Interview)

Focus on Natural Science An Chaehong, Chosŏn Ilbosa

In a difficult place like Chosŏn, (12 characters deleted - original text) I first want to praise (推稱) your steady efforts. Also, focusing on academic and cultivation issues, especially striving to elucidate (闡明) and cultivate Chosŏn's native culture, is a prominent point and known to be very necessary in Chosŏn at present. Taking the lead (率先) in correcting Chosŏn's standard script (正音) is seen rather as a means (方便) for the elucidation and cultivation of native culture, so I won't praise it separately. And although one cannot cover everything, if possible, it might be good in the future to introduce natural science in a popular (通俗的) and interesting way. This would open new avenues not only for progressive youth but also for the leisured (有閒) reading class, which still tends towards a passive standard (消造本位). It is commonly recognized that half of the Chosŏn people's survival movement lies in their education aspect, and half of the education movement lies in advancing towards science.

Wish for More Focus on Interest and Popularization Song Chinu, Tong'a Ilbosa

Seeing the serialization in every issue of all the novel (塹新) moral concepts (觀念) and new knowledge about science that could form the basis for building a new society, there was always a solemn (嚴肅) atmosphere through the pages. Among them, the writings where Teacher San'ong [likely An Ch'angho] instructed the general compatriot youth were particularly seen as frankly disclosing (披瀝) the sincerity and loyalty (誠忠) the teacher practiced for decades. Thus, it came to radiate a unique color (一種 異彩) in the press world. However, in my humble opinion, it seems overly biased (偏重되어) towards a solemn mood, feeling somewhat like a textbook and lacking (缺如) in the aspect of interest. Wouldn't it be even better if, from now on (인저붙어는), articles that attract interest were added to the existing mood, and it were made more popular (通俗化) so general readers could understand it better? <28>

Become a Friend and Leader of the Masses Ch'oe Wŏnsun, Tong'a Ilbosa

Before speaking of Tonggwang's mission, I first pay respect to Tonggwang's fortune (運數). To have passed one year without major changes in such a harsh and perilous place is a fact demonstrating the effort and skill of those in charge of its management. Merely having such figures and effectively displaying its power as the light of the East is enough to say Tonggwang has come with good fortune. I pray and hope that Tonggwang, struggling against fierce wind and rain in a difficult place, meets the new spring and that its power as the eastern light of the spring garden, aiding and stimulating the budding of our creativity, grows even greater. On this occasion, I wish to add one more word: I hope Tonggwang firmly maintains (益益 堅固히 지키어서) the popularizing (通俗的) reach it has kept, becoming a friend and leader to even more of the masses.

Do Not Change Your Dignified Attitude Yi Iksang, Chosŏn Ilbosa

In Chosŏn's current situation, it is generally observed that magazines inevitably cannot continue. In the last five or six years (這間), the magazine world can be said to have experienced constant flux (興亡盛衰가 無常하였다). However, that Tonggwang has safely reached its first anniversary amidst such precarious views (危殆視) shows how great the efforts of the Tonggwang members must have been.
For our cultural enterprises, newspapers and magazines, the less understanding the general public has of the enterprise itself, the more desperate struggle (惡戰苦鬪) those affiliated (提携된) with the enterprise must endure. What I always expect from Tonggwang is that Tonggwang itself moves beyond mere journalism (쩌나리쯤) and always strives for culture. Since it is a fantasy (空想) in Chosŏn to think that managing a magazine could somehow turn a profit or even break even (收支가 相殺), how much more dignified is it to thoroughly realize this and offer material and spiritual resources out of a spirit of sacrificing everything for culture, compared to scrambling not to suffer even a small loss and making an ugly scene (추태)? In this sense, I hope Tonggwang continues unchanged in its existing (在來) attitude. Of course, it is desired that there should be great development in its content.

Dissatisfied with the Focus on Ancient Chosŏn National Culture Kim Kijin, Chung'oe Ilbosa

I have had the opportunity to encounter your magazine every month since the first issue. Although I lacked the leisure for careful reading (精讀), recalling my general impression, the first two or three issues felt vague (漠然), like a hygiene magazine published by a province or prefecture. The cover illustrations of stars and sunbeams were unpleasant. However, the cover has improved now.
Regarding the content, I have held the hasty conclusion (속단) that it was thoroughly (徹頭徹尾) promoting spiritualism – though perhaps that's a hasty conclusion, as it's a judgment made without reading the entire content. And I wonder if Tonggwang <29> isn't edited (編輯되) in a way that still allows me to hold this hasty conclusion.
I always express respect for the sufficient effort dedicated to finding, correcting (바루잡고), and developing the Chosŏn script. I also express respect for providing space for promoting health and hygiene ideas and popular science lectures.
I don't know within what scope it would be good to state my hopes. Hopes are not something that can be spoken so freely. However, to mention just one thing: I always feel dissatisfied when I see your magazine putting its main effort (주력) into the excavation (採掘) of ancient Chosŏn national culture. Isn't the simple introduction of buried (파아묻히어 있는) myths or legends, or scenery and customs (風物) revealed in historic sites (古蹟) and artifacts (唯物 - likely relics/artifacts), merely material for research data for the modern Chosŏn populace? Moreover, because the introduction always accompanies a kind of excitement, it sounds like praise (禮讚) for old sites, objects, and customs. The style (양식) of ancient Chosŏn national culture likely doesn't differ much from what we with our shallow learning (淺學) imagine, as is a common fact of human history. It seems natural that we cannot be satisfied with mere verification of things we can easily guess or already know. If our scholars were scholars of brave inquiry (勇敢한 探究), I believe they couldn't stop at such introductions. – This statement might seem directed at some of your magazine's contributors (寄稿家), but regardless, I ask of your magazine: By what method should readers gain a proper understanding of ancient Chosŏn national culture? – Please strive to present (提示) that method.
It is pleasing that a "New Poetry Movement" section was added starting this month. I hope that in the future, space will be allocated (할애하여) to accommodate literary discussions, pouring main efforts into that area as well. Next, I wanted to speak about Tonggwang's movement spirit, but doing so would require, for convenience, delving into Chu Yohan's rebuttal-like (辨駁文) open letter (공개문) to general society published in the April issue of "Chosŏn chi Kwang," and consequently (딸아서), as there will likely be opportunities later, it seems it would become too long, so I will stop here.

Try Continuing for 5 Years Yu Chint'ae, Chosŏn Kyoyuk Hyŏphoe

All magazines are good, but the magazine that continues for a long time will gain glory in the future. To do so, endure, be diligent (부즈런하고), and strive courageously. Tonggwang too, as much as possible, should economize on expenses, and all staff members (사원) should endure sacrificially and strive hard. If you continue just as you are now for 5 years, you will gain glory in the future. (Interview)

Magazine Management is Just Difficult Kim Mirisa, Chosŏn Yŏja Kyoyukhoe

Although I receive Tonggwang, I haven't had the leisure to read it all thoroughly. However, I know the content is quite substantial. Of course, I know that articles with substance and strength should be published more in the future, but what can be done given our environment? But since I know well that magazine management only involves feeling constrained and spending money, how difficult must it have been even for just that one year? I have nothing but a word of consolation. (Interview)

Satisfied with the Current State Yi Kwanyong, Sin'ganhoe

I am also one of those who have continuously read Tonggwang with affection for the past year. Whenever I receive it, even before looking at the splendid cover, I examine the table of contents with an irrepressible feeling of "What interesting articles are published this time?" Then, usually without disappointment, I see listed (나열된) good educational (敎養的) articles that can elevate the spiritual life of youth, and <30> articles containing practical knowledge that cannot be learned (학습할 수 없는) through school subjects. Since there seem to be no other means besides these two for cultivating (敎養시키는) youth conceptually (觀念으로), I believe it will receive much welcome (환영) from our youth circles as a "cultivation institution (修養機關)." There might be some regret that politics and current affairs issues (時事問題) are lacking, but this stems from circumstances that cannot be helped until the current Newspaper Law is fundamentally revised. I am satisfied with Tonggwang in its current state, so I have no opinion on what to do in the future. However, I would like to add one thing here. To simplify (平易化) academic matters like Tonggwang does, I believe adding more illustrations (揷畵) would receive greater welcome from general readers.

Strive for Methods to Unite Sin Hŭng'u, Chung'ang Kidokkyo Ch'ŏngnyŏnhoe

I think the format (體裁) and content are excellent. But spending money and effort and receiving only 30 chŏn probably means a considerable financial loss. It would be good if the readership increased and advertising revenue came in well so it could continue smoothly, but I worry it might not. Also, while everyone knows unity (단합) is necessary for Chosŏn people, achieving unity seems difficult. Even if one side sacrifices its own opinion and makes concessions (양보) out of respect (존중) for the other side's opinion, if the other side instead considers it a weakness and tries to insult (侮辱) and trample on it, conflict will inevitably arise. It is necessary to somehow devise (講究) methods to unite through mutual concessions. I hope Tonggwang expands its scope further to gain the freedom to discuss (論及) politics and current affairs, becoming a major national institution in the future. (Interview)

Promote Great Unity Kim Yŏngsŏp, Chung'ang Yebedang Pastor

[The following 13 lines deleted (original text)]

Hope for the Establishment of Principles Pak Hŭido, Chung'ang Yuch'iwŏn

Among the magazines in Chosŏn, I am most pleased with the spirit of Tonggwang. However, I hope the color (色彩) becomes richer (濃厚), articles are well-selected, and the principles are established (확정). (Interview)

Favorable Towards its Principles Kim Hwallan, Ihwa Chŏnmun Hakkyo

Unlike other ordinary magazines that recklessly (함부로) publish articles just to win readers' favor (歡心), Tonggwang has a definite goal and advances according to its principles, towards which I feel favorable. I hope you strive even harder according to your principles in the future. Regarding the format (體裁), it is undoubtedly good, but from my perspective, using large characters in the upper column (上欄) and small characters in the lower column (下欄) seems psychologically dissonant and uncomfortable to look at. (Interview)

Respond to the True Demands of the Era and the Masses Yi Kŭnjong, Purip Tosŏgwan Chongno Pun'gwan

Anything that conforms (順應) to the true demands of the era and the common wishes of the masses will surely prosper and its strength will be great. Therefore, upon celebrating Tonggwang's future, I believe Tonggwang was born from the demands of our peninsula (半島), and at the same time, I hope it achieves eternal development by conforming to these demands in the future as well. Our demand is to strive for (圖謀) self-fulfillment while achieving happy liberation from all unfairness and inequality. Tonggwang! You were born for this, so struggle hard (奮鬪) for this as well. Then your life will be eternal and glorious. <31> <26-31>

**Summary**

This article compiles opinions from various Korean intellectuals and figures associated with different organizations (publishing houses, religious groups, educational institutions, social movements) on the first anniversary of the magazine *Tonggwang* in 1927. Contributors generally praise the magazine for its focus on "Chosŏn spirit," self-cultivation (修養), group training, practical effort, science, and moral uplift, seeing it as a unique and valuable publication in colonial Korea. Specific praise is given for its careful use of Hangul and its dignified tone. Criticisms and suggestions include desires for more popularization and accessibility for the masses, greater inclusion of natural science, more content appealing to reader interest (rather than being overly solemn or textbook-like), clearer articulation of its principles, and potentially broader coverage including literature or even politics (though limitations are acknowledged). Some contributors express concern over the magazine's financial viability and the difficulty of cultural work under colonial conditions, while others offer encouragement for its continued perseverance. The contributions reveal a shared concern for national consciousness, cultural development, education, and practical self-strengthening among Korean elites during the colonial period. Historians might find the range of perspectives, the emphasis on specific themes like "cultivation" linked to figures like An Ch'angho (referenced as "An Tosan" and "San'ong"), the discussions around language use (Hangul), the balance between traditional culture and modern science, and the explicit mentions of censorship (deleted lines) particularly valuable for understanding the intellectual currents, cultural strategies, and constraints faced by Koreans under Japanese rule in the 1920s.

東光의 1周年      啓明社 崔南善

1년이 짜르다면 짜른 동안이지마는 有意義하게 쓰면 퍽 큰 가치를 만드는 동안도 될 수 있음니다. 東光의 지난 1년 동안 같음은 正히 그러한 意義의 세월이라 할 것임니다.
다른 말 할 것 없이 시방 朝鮮에 있어서 가장 緊切하면서 가장 微弱해 있는 朝鮮心에 대하여 그 振作과 淬勵와 淨化와 深化에 東光이 어떻게 큰 効績을 나타내었는지는 아무의 눈에던지 두드러지게 인식된 점임니다. 어떻게 많은 「페지」와 힘과 題目과 내용을 「朝鮮我」의 發揚에 쓰었는지 또 이것이 어떻게 동무 잡지들 중에서 卓然한 면목이었는지는 독자의 기억이 새롭은 바에 새삼스럽은 擧似를 요하지 아니할 것임니다.
또 團體的 訓練 力行精神의 鼓吹 등 東光의 표방한 旗幟는 어느 것이고 시방 우리의 金科玉條 아닌 것이 없었으니 이는 東光으로 더불어 옅지 아니한 關繫를 가지신 줄 아는 安島山 선생의 薰化의 일면인 줄 생각하고 前年에 이 정신을 위하여 靑年學友會의 周旋을 한가지 하던 일을 회상하면서 東光으로 인하여 섀로 뿌리어지는 이 귀중한 種子가 하로바삐 많은 結實로 갚아지기를 못내 心祝하고 지냄니다.
東光의 목적은 무론 위대하실 줄 앎니다. 또 지난 일주년쯤의 일은 그 理想에서나 實績에서나 의무에의 신날이실줄 앎니다. 願하건대 여러분 同人의 뜨겁은 誠力과 굳은 뜻이 朝鮮 今日의 最高 正見이라 할 東光의 主義를 擴布하고 실현하는 上으로 갈쓰록 健旺과 乾惕을 나타내시어 오는 일주년 내지 無窮週年에서 항상 幾何級數的 累進率을 발휘하시옵소서.
久遠 朝鮮의 생명은 東光 정신의 가에서 흐름을 나는 믿슴니다.
