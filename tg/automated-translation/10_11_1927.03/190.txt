A Children's Historical Tale: The Filial Piety of Chiŭn

This happened about a thousand years ago. During the reign of Queen Chinsŏng (眞聖女王) of Silla, there was a woman whose filial piety towards her parents became renowned throughout the nation; her name was Chiŭn (知恩). Living in Han'gibu (韓岐部) in the Silla capital, she lost her father early and devotedly supported her widowed mother alone, remaining unmarried even past the age of thirty, determinedly staying to care for her mother.

Chiŭn supported her mother daily by doing day labor at others' houses, but at that time, a severe famine struck, making it impossible to survive no matter what she did. After much thought, Chiŭn could find no other way but to sell herself, becoming a female servant in the house of a wealthy person in the village, and thus obtained grain. During the day, she would go to that house to work, and at night, she would return home to cook rice for her mother.

After some time, her mother found Chiŭn's situation very strange and asked her, "My dear Chiŭn, I find this odd. Before, even eating rough rice cakes (게떡) tasted good and my heart was at peace, but these days, although I eat good rice, it doesn't taste good and my heart is uneasy. What is the reason?"

Hearing these words, Chiŭn lowered her head and said, "Indeed, since I couldn't earn enough and there was nothing to eat, I had no choice but to sell myself as a female servant to a certain house to obtain grain and cook rice for you, Mother," her eyes brimming with tears. When her mother heard this, she cried out,

"Because I have lived so long without dying, I have caused you to sell yourself like this!"

and began to wail. Chiŭn also wailed along with her. At that moment, two disciples of Hyojong (孝宗), who held the position of Kuksŏn (國仙 - Hwarang leader), were passing by and saw this. They asked why they were crying. Chiŭn explained the entire situation, saying, "The reason I have remained at home unmarried, though now over thirty, is to support my mother. But now I have caused my mother such distress. What can I do to put my mother's heart at ease?" and sobbed sorrowfully.

The disciples of Hyojong returned and reported this to their teacher, Hyojong. Hyojong felt pity for her and sent one hundred sŏm (bags) of grain to Chiŭn. He also sent grain to Chiŭn's master's house <56> to ransom Chiŭn's freedom. When Hyojong's parents heard this story, they felt particular sympathy and sent clothes to Chiŭn. Hyojong's one thousand disciples each collected one sŏm of grain, gathering a thousand sŏm in total, and sent it to Chiŭn. This matter eventually reached the royal palace, and the King learned of it. Praising her astonishing devotion, he bestowed upon her five hundred sŏm of grain and granted her a fine house. Furthermore, he dispatched soldiers to guard the house, preventing thieves from entering. Thus, the village's name was changed to Hyoyangni (孝養里 - Village of Filial Support) to commemorate her filial piety. <57>

**Summary**

This article recounts the story of Chiŭn, a woman from Silla during Queen Chinsŏng's reign, renowned for her extreme filial piety. Living in poverty and facing a famine, she sells herself into servitude to feed her elderly mother. Her mother eventually discovers this sacrifice, leading to mutual sorrow. Their plight is witnessed by disciples of the Hwarang leader Hyojong, who, moved by the story, helps ransom Chiŭn and provides aid, inspiring his disciples and parents to contribute as well. The King also hears of her devotion, granting her grain, a house, and protection, and renaming her village Hyoyangni in her honor. From a colonial history perspective, the promotion of traditional Confucian values like filial piety (효성) through such historical tales could be seen as aligning with certain aspects of Japanese colonial cultural policy, which sometimes emphasized traditional virtues to encourage social stability and obedience, albeit within a framework ultimately serving colonial interests. The focus on a Silla-era story reinforces Korean national history, yet the emphasis on self-sacrifice and acceptance of hardship might resonate with colonial-era narratives encouraging endurance.

少年史話 지은의 효성
한 천년 전 일임니다. 신라 진성여왕(眞聖女王) 시절에 부모께 효도로써 이름이 일국에 높이 들어난 여자 하나가 있으니 이름은 지은(知恩)이라 함니다. 신라 서울 한기부(韓岐部)에 살아서 일쯕 아버지를 여이고 홀로 그 어머니를 지극히 봉양하여 나이 삼십여 세가 넘도록 시집도 아니 가고 꼭 어머니를 모시고 있었음니다.
지은이 매일 남의 집에 품팔이 하여 어머니를 봉양하는데 마침 이 때 크게 흉년이 들어 아무리 하여도 살아날 수가 없게 되었음니다. 지은은 생각다 못하여 자긔 몸을 팔아 그 동리 어느 큰 부자의 집에 게집종이 되고 곡식을 얻어 오았음니다. 낮이면 그 집에 가아서 일을 하고 밤이면 돌아오아 어머니께 밥을 짓어(지어) 들임니다.
얼마 뒤에 어머니가 지은의 일이 하 이상하게 생각되어 지은을 보고 『이 애 지은아 내가 이상하다. 전날에는 게떡을 먹어도 맛이 있고 맘이 평안하더니 요새는 좋은 쌀밥을 먹건만 맛이 없고 맘이 좋잖으니 웬일이냐』
지은이 이 말을 듯고 머리를 숙이며 『과연 제가 잘 벌 수는 없고 먹을 것은 없는 고로 할 수 없이 어느 집 여종으로 팔리어 곡식을 얻어다가 어머님께 밥을 짓어(지어) 들인 것임니다.』하고 눈물이 그렁그렁하였음니다. 그 어머니가 그 말을 듯더니
『내가 나이 많도록 죽지 아니 하고 살아 있어 너를 이렇게 몸을 팔도록 하는 구나』
하며 통곡함니다. 지은도 딸아서 통곡함니다. 이 때에 국선(國仙)으로 있는 효종(孝宗)의 제자 두 사람이 지나다가 이것을 보고 웨 울(우)느냐고 물었음니다. 지은이 그 사연을 일일이 말하며 『내가 지금 나이 설흔이 넘도록 시집도 아니 가고 집에 있는 것은 내 어머님을 공양하기 위함인데 이제 어머님의 맘을 저렇게 상하게 하였으니 어찌하면 어머님의 맘을 편안하게 할 수 있을까.』하고 흑흑 느끼어 울었음니다.
효종의 제자들이 돌아 가아서 그 선생 효종에게 여쭙었(여쭈엇)음니다. 효종이 불상히 녀기어 곡식 백섬을 지은에게로 보내어 주고 또 지은의 주인 집에 곡식을＜56＞ 보내어 지은의 몸을 속냥시키었음니다. 효종의 부모께서도 이 말을 듯고 특히 동정하여 의복을 지은에게로 보내었음니다. 효종의 제자 천명이 각히 곡식 한 섬씩 걷우어 천섬을 모아서 지은에게 보내었음니다. 이 일이 마침내 대궐에까지 들어 가아서 임검님께서 알으시고 그 놀랍은 뜻을 칭찬하시어 곡식 오백섬을 나리어 주시며 좋은 집 한 채를 내어 주시고 또 군사를 보내어 그 집을 수직하여 도적이 못 들게 하였음니다. 그리하여 그 동리의 이름을 효양리(孝養里)라 하여 효성을 표장하였음니다.＜57＞
