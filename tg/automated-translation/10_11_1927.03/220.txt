Magazine Title: 동광 제11호
Publication Date: 1927년 03월 05일
Article Title: 平壤崇實學校 校歌, (P'yŏngyang Sungsil School School Song,)
Author: 平壤 朴潤元 (Pak Yunwŏn of P'yŏngyang)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

1. Moranbong Hill rises and turns to sit,
Where the waters of the Taedong River wind down and embrace,
The multi-storied building soaring among the white clouds is
Union Sungsil School.
Sungsil Sungsil Union Sungsil
Sungsil Sungsil Union Sungsil
Sungsil Sungsil Union Sungsil
Manse Manse Manse! (Hurrah! Hurrah! Hurrah!)

2. On Mansudae, the color of the old pines was verdant,
On Nong'aksan, the color of the rocks is like white jade.
The fine scenery of this picturesque, wondrous landscape
Is a myriad of forms (萬千象).

3. Becoming deeply engrossed in all studies of astronomy and geography,
Forgetting the passing tides (time),
At the sound of the bell ringing according to the hour,
Let us come to our senses.

4. Wondrous is the origin (淵源) of our school's founding;
Its foundation was laid upon the Rock of Ages (萬世磐石).
The spring water of life gushing forth from within
Spreads throughout society.
<57>

**Summary**

This text presents the school song of P'yŏngyang Sungsil School, written by Pak Yunwŏn in 1927. The lyrics celebrate the school's scenic location near Moranbong Hill and the Taedong River in P'yŏngyang, encouraging diligent study and alertness signaled by the school bell. It emphasizes the school's strong foundation, likening it to the "Rock of Ages," and its mission to spread beneficial influence ("spring water of life") into society. For historians, the song is interesting as it reflects the ethos of a prominent Christian mission school during the colonial period, blending pride in Korean geography (Moranbong, Taedonggang) with Christian symbolism ("Rock of Ages," "spring water of life") and a focus on modern education ("astronomy and geography") aimed at societal contribution. The reference to "Union Sungsil School" (合成崇實學校) points to its history of merging educational institutions.
