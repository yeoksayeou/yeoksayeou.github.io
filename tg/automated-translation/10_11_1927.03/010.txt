Magazine Title: 동광 제11호
Publication Date: 1927년 03월 05일
Article Title: 봄맞이 (Welcoming Spring)
Author: Not Specified
Article Type: 문예기타 (Other Literary Works)

Spring has come.

On the mountains, the piled snow melts, and on the river (江), the thin ice thaws. In the front yard, the willow tree's buds open, and on the back hill, the plum blossoms form buds. Sparrows that were huddled in their nests fly out, and bugs that were shrunk in the soil crawl out. Thus, all things meet their time and become vibrant and active (生動活躍).

Each individual (各個) self (自我) has opened its eyes. The effort of creation and the dawn of resurrection are full. It is the spring that makes what was prostrate rise, what was pressed down break free, what was crumpled straighten out, what was hidden reveal itself, and what was dead come alive.

All things have come alive. Everything has risen up. They guarded their own lives; they cultivated their own strength to live. They did not yield at all to the harsh tyranny (暴威) of winter and fought against the fierce snowstorms. Even if their outer forms seemed dead, they did not betray that firm vow to keep only the life force (生命力) within stored away (潛蓄).

The awaited new opportune moment (機運) has returned. Saying "Ah, this is it!", they all lifted their heads at once. They stir. They rise. Now the time has come to fully display the self (自我) just as it is, without the slightest deviation, without the worry (念慮) of perhaps falling.

The light of spring has spread. Every branch of the trees, every clump of grass is the light of spring. In that brilliant (爛熳) spring scenery (春城), the time will come when trees, plants, and flowers will compete in loveliness and boast of their beauty. Ah, spring, whose joy and delight are thus boundless!

Friend, let us all come here together and welcome spring. <1>

**Summary**

The article is a lyrical celebration of spring's arrival, depicting the reawakening of nature after winter. It emphasizes the themes of resilience against hardship (winter's "tyranny"), the innate "life force" within all beings, the emergence of the individual "self," and the seizing of a new "opportune moment" for life and expression. For historians studying the Korean colonial period, the article is interesting as a potential allegory. The endurance through harsh winter and the joyful embrace of spring's rebirth could symbolize the Korean people's perseverance under Japanese rule and the hope for national liberation or cultural revitalization, with the emphasis on "self" (自我) and "life force" (生命力) reflecting underlying nationalist sentiment or a focus on inherent potential.

봄맞이
봄이 오았다.
산에는 쌓인 눈 녹고 가람(江)에는 살얼음 풀린다. 앞뜰에 버들나무 눈이 트고 뒤ㅅ동산에 매화꽃 봉오리 맺는다. 보금자리에 곱송기리었던 참새 날아 나아오고 흙속에 옴추리었던 버러지 기어 나아온다. 이렇듯 온갓은 다 때를 만나아 생동활약(生動活躍)한다.
각개(各個)의 자아(自我)가 다 눈을 뜨었다. 창조의 노력과 부활의 서광이 가득 차았다. 엎디었던 것이 일어나고 눌리었던 것이 벗어나고 쭈구리었던 것이 펴어지고 숨었던 것이 낱아나고 죽었던 것이 살아나게 할 봄이다.
온갓은 다 살았다. 모도 일어 나았다. 제 목숨을 제가 지키었고 제 살 힘을 제가 길었다. 겨울의 혹독한 폭위(暴威)에도 도모지 굴하지 아니하고 사납은 눈바람과 싸움하여 오았다. 겉모양은 다 죽었더라도 오직 그 속에 있는 생명녁(生命力)만은 잠축하(潛蓄)여 있거라 하던 그 굳은 맹서를 저버리지 아니 하였음이다. 기다리던 새 긔운(機運)은 돌아오았다. 옳다구나 하고 모도 한꺼번에 머리를 치어다. 움즉인다. 일어난다. 인저는 조고만한 어기어짐도 없이 행여라 넘어지리라는 염녀도 없이 꼭 자아(自我) 그대로를 여실히 발휘할 시긔가 되었다.
봄빛이 펴이었다. 나무 가지가지 풀 폭이 폭마다 다 봄빛이다. 저 난만(爛熳)한 춘성(春城)에 초목화훼가 꽃답음을 다투며 곱음을 잘랑할 한창 좋은 때가 올 것이다. 아아 이렇듯 기쁘고 질겁음이 한이 없을 봄!
동무여 다 같이 이리 오아 봄맞이 하자.＜1＞
