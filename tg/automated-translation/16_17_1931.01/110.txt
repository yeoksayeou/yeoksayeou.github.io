Korea

Rural Problems - Price Collapse of Rice, Rural Panic Gradually Worsens, Rice Grain Law Application Issue

Entering the autumn season, Korea's biggest problem was the drop in rice prices.
Although the second forecast for the Korean harvest as of November 1st, announced by government authorities on November 7th, showed a decrease of 46,742.2 sŏk (unit of volume) compared to the estimate, it still showed a surprising increase of 5.5 million sŏk compared to the actual yield of the previous year, an unprecedentedly large harvest.

[Table: Comparison of 2nd Rice Harvest Forecast and Previous Year's Actual Yield]

This bumper crop was not a phenomenon limited to Korea; it was common throughout Japan, Taiwan, China, etc., resulting in an unprecedented surplus of rice production. Since September, rice prices continued a steep decline, and in November, futures rice caused a commotion by falling below the 12 wŏn per sŏk mark. In rural areas, the price for one kŭn (unit of weight) of unhulled rice (正租) fell to a disastrous level below 4 chŏn in some places, but there were effectively no transactions.

Stemming from this grave situation, roughly three trends emerged: one was the movement to prevent the catastrophic fall of rice prices, another was the movement for emergency relief for rural villages, and yet another was the movement criticizing the rice production plan.

To prevent the plunge in rice prices, the government appears to be considering (1) exporting old rice currently held under purchase according to the Rice Grain Law (米穀法) overseas and newly purchasing rice, (2) providing low-interest funds for Korean rice to regulate the monthly export volume, and (3) applying Article 1 of the Rice Grain Law to Korea, Karafuto (Sakhalin), and Taiwan to carry out purchases there as well.

The export of husked rice overseas faced a setback due to the Chinese government's import ban, but plans are being made to export to Hawaii and the South Seas, and there are even proponents suggesting promoting the food value of rice eventually even in Europe and America.

The monthly regulation of rice exports, of course, aims to control rice prices in the Japanese market. For this purpose, a rice warehousing company was established in November with Hamnam Provincial Governor Matsui Bōtarō (松井房太郞) as president. Additionally, the establishment of a so-called Rice Control Company, centered around Fujii Jittarō (藤井實太郞), is being planned. Authorities announced the interest rates for rice stored in agricultural and commercial warehouses on November 17th and 18th. For agricultural warehouses, the rate is uniformly 1.5 ri per day; for commercial warehouses, for new grain intended for export to Japan, to be released after March 1st and stored for over 3 months by then, the rate is 1.3 ri per day, and for others, 1.8 ri per day. However, regarding the most crucial provision of 12.7 million wŏn in funds, negotiations with the government have not progressed, so an emergency measure was established for now, with the Bank of Chosen providing rediscounting.

While this funding for rice aimed at regulating monthly exports can be utilized by large landowners and rice merchants, and consequently fatten the bellies of Rice Warehouse Company-type privileged companies, it offers no benefit whatsoever to general small farmers and poor peasants. Therefore, on the one hand, the argument for purchasing polished rice, especially unhulled rice, in Korea gained momentum. Finally, at the joint council meeting on the revision of the Rice Grain Law held on the 21st, the Ministry of Colonial Affairs (拓務省) proposed applying Article 1 of the Rice Grain Law to Korea and Taiwan, that is, allowing rice purchases in Korea and Taiwan as well.

Due to these various countermeasures, the rice price has barely avoided further decline. However, since most small and poor farmers would have already been forced to sell their rice at the fallen prices, it is clear that even if the purchase plan is realized<24>, the beneficiaries will be the large landowners and rice merchants who sell in the spring.

Therefore, in rural areas, which include many small and poor farmers, alongside measures for rice prices, policies for emergency relief from rural hardship are desperately needed. Movements arose for paying water utilization association fees in kind or delaying payment, delaying debt payments, and even demands for paying taxes in kind or delaying them.

Furthermore, simultaneous with the movement to delay water utilization association fees, the weaknesses of poorly managed associations were exposed, leading to proposals such as dissolving existing water associations, halting those under planning, or extending the annual repayment period for funds. Subsequently, demands arose for the temporary suspension or major revision of land improvement and rice production increase plans. Although authorities notified each region that there would be absolutely no change to the rice increase plan from the standpoint of food policy, under the pressure of reality, they were forced to hold a Land Improvement Project Council meeting starting November 20th to devise countermeasures.

Related to the disastrous fall in rice prices and the impoverishment of rural villages, the decline of the middle peasant class in Korea and population trends are drawing the attention of knowledgeable people.

Koreans in Manchuria - Conflict with Japanese Officials in Kando, Detainees in North Manchuria Not Yet Released, Resurgence of Land Non-Sale Sentiment

The suffering of fellow Koreans residing in Manchuria continues.
It is said that Chinese officials strictly prohibited the use of the name Puk Kando (北間島 - North Jiandao), deeming it humiliating. In Longjing (龍井), Júzǐjiē (局子街), and other areas, direct action by communist groups led to the mass resignation of leaders of people's associations (民會), resulting in their eventual demise. The 29 elementary schools and 28 village schools (書堂) established by the Government-General are also facing threats to their existence. A clash between Japanese and Chinese officials resulted in casualties on both sides, leading to a temporarily tense situation due to the dispatch of police officers by the Government-General. However, the incident was subsequently transferred entirely to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and all police officers dispatched by the Government-General were withdrawn, bringing the matter to a close for the time being.

After the destructive activities of August 1st, some arrested compatriots were executed by firing squad, while others released are still detained as follows. It is reported that they face hardship due to inadequate prison facilities and treatment.
Sŏng of Kirin Province (吉林省城): Sin Il-yong (辛日鎔) and 2 others; Taitun, Kirin (吉林大屯): Hwang Chong-man (黃鍾萬) and 2 others; Ning'an County (寧安縣): An Kŭn-gyu (安根奎) and 4 others; Binzhou County (賓州縣): Han Chin (韓震) and 12 others; Fuyu County (扶餘縣): Yi U-juk (李友竹) and 7 others; Wuchang County (五常縣): Im Pok-sun (林福順) and 1 other; Jiaohe (蛟河): Han Il-gwang (韓日光) and 13 others; Total 48 people.

The tendency to view Koreans with hostility in connection with the fervor for recovering national rights has traditionally been seen in Manchuria. However, recently spurred by the Sino-Soviet conflict and the destructive activities of communist groups, the movement advocating not selling land to Koreans seems to be resurging. Documents reportedly related to the expulsion of Koreans<25> include the following:

1.  Reportedly an order from the Kirin Provincial Police Department (吉林省 警務處).
    In Hunchun (琿春), Yanji (延吉), Helong (和龍), Wangqing (汪淸) counties, Koreans hold land ownership rights for 9 out of 10 plots. Therefore, our government has previously encouraged them to naturalize (入籍) to prevent future dangers. However, Japan does not approve Korean naturalization. Also, the Oriental Development Company (東洋拓殖會社) secretly provides funds to Koreans, enabling them to purchase land, and then uses opportunities to take over the land ownership. Thus, Korean land ownership is a major problem. Furthermore, since Koreans are under Japanese extraterritoriality, when disputes arise with our people, they go to our courts if it benefits them, but if it's disadvantageous, they go to the Japanese consulate and act oppressively to win. If this situation continues, it is unknown when we might lose our national territory. Therefore, from now on, selling land to Koreans is strictly prohibited, and efforts should be made to recover already sold land as much as possible.

2.  Reportedly an instruction from Chang Tso-hsiang (張作相), Chairman of the Kirin Provincial Government, and a communication from the Beiping-Tianjin Economic Committee (北津經濟委員會).

    Instruction
    Hebei (河北), Shaanxi (陝西), and Shandong (山東) provinces originally have large populations and insufficient arable land. Compounded by natural disasters and warfare, the number of poor people increases daily, truly reaching hundreds of thousands. In Liaoning (遼寧), Kirin (吉林), and Heilongjiang (黑龍) provinces, foreigners live interspersed, and over a million Korean farmers have already migrated and reside there, yet the migration of Korean farmers increases year by year. Leaving them as is poses a major contradiction to our national economic life. Therefore, from now on, the migration of Korean farmers into the three eastern provinces must be prevented, and concurrently, a policy for expelling Korean farmers already within these provinces must be established. Simultaneously, migrating the poor from Hebei, Shaanxi, and Shandong provinces is the fundamental policy for our nation's poverty relief.

    Beiping-Tianjin Economic Committee Communication
    Our committee, with the cooperation of your provincial government, has achieved excellent results in the already planned first, second, and third phases of migration in the 123rd district, for which we express our sincere gratitude. From this autumn through next spring, we plan the second phase of migration, relocating 50,000 poor people from Hebei, Shandong, and Shaanxi provinces who have suffered from years of natural disasters and warfare. We hope for your continued assistance. The realization of our committee's plan primarily aims to prevent the residence of foreigners and the migration of Korean farmers into the three eastern provinces, and to execute their expulsion, while simultaneously developing the resources of the three eastern provinces to improve and advance the economic life of the impoverished populace. We request that your provincial government deeply understand our committee's purpose and thoroughly inform the administrative offices and garrisoned military police under your jurisdiction of our intent...

A workaround was devised to have the central executive committee<26> act on behalf of the representative assembly's decision-making authority. The plan to revise Korea's local administrative system to make Provincial Councils (道會), Municipal Councils (府會), and Town Councils (邑會) deliberative bodies has generally passed the Ministry of Colonial Affairs (拓務省) according to the original draft. It is said that the qualification for suffrage will be set at payment of 5 wŏn or more in taxes, including school fees and school association fees. This slightly increases the number of eligible voters, but it is noteworthy that the number of Japanese voters increases relatively significantly.

Other
In North and South Kyŏngsang Provinces, the temporary confiscation of people's cotton gins (繰綿機) and looms (織造機) under the guise of joint storage to improve the results of cooperative cotton sales caused much controversy, being criticized as looking after the interests of yarn manufacturers rather than the people's interests.
The Electric Power Control Committee meeting was held in November, and the Ordinance for the Korean Chamber of Commerce and Industry was promulgated to take effect from November 25th. This year marks the 30th anniversary of the opening of railways in Korea.
The Southern and Northern Methodist Churches in Korea have decided to unite, holding a plenary committee meeting to conclude the long-pending issue. A strong movement towards unification is also underway among the New, Old, and Sariwŏn factions of Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎).
The 484th anniversary ceremony of the creation of Han'gŭl was held on November 19th.

World
Japan - Prime Minister Hamaguchi Shot, Budget Meeting Difficulties, Proletarian Party Merger Movement
On November 14th, around 9 a.m., Prime Minister Hamaguchi was shot with a pistol by a young man at Tōkyō Station, sustaining serious injuries to his lower abdomen. The perpetrator was immediately arrested; he was a member of a right-wing group and boasted that it was to overcome the depression. Mr. Hamaguchi immediately underwent emergency surgery and received blood transfusions, and it seems his life will likely be saved.
Due to this incident, Foreign Minister Shidehara assumed the duties of acting prime minister. Although internal strife among the Egi, Adachi, Wakatsuki, and Yamamoto factions within the Minseitō party is reported, there seems to be no significant political impact yet.
The budget for the next fiscal year is currently being drafted. On one hand, there is the realization of the austerity policy and using the surplus from military budget reduction to fund tax cuts. On the other hand, there are strong demands for supplementary funding from the Army and Navy, especially the Navy, leading to considerable difficulties. However, ultimately, the military cliques' power is not what it used to be, and the decision was made for a total budget of 1.448 billion yen,<28> a naval replenishment plan of 391 million yen, and tax cuts amounting to 134.11 million yen.
Among the proletarian political parties, after the dissolution faction's strife within the Labor-Farmer Party (勞農黨), there are reports of proposals for a grand merger centered around the Masses Party (大衆黨). The major revolt of Taiwanese indigenous people and the Tōkyō student strike incident, which were once widely reported, are gradually calming down.

China - Chang Hsüeh-liang Goes to Nanjing, Fourth Central Executive Committee Plenary Session Held, National Assembly to Convene Next May
The Nanjing government, having seen the pacification of the internal conflict with the complete surrender of the Yan (閻) and Feng (馮) forces, convened the plenary session of the Kuomintang Central Executive Committee for post-victory arrangements in Nanjing starting November 12th. Chang Hsüeh-liang (張學良), who assumed the post of Deputy Commander of the Army, Navy, and Air Force, took this opportunity to visit Nanjing with his wife and received a lavish welcome. The resolutions of the 4th Plenary Session were:
1.  Convene the National Assembly (國民會) starting May 5, 1931.
2.  Centralize administrative and financial power by reducing the authority of the old party branches in each provincial government.
3.  Regarding the disposition of the Yan and Feng armies, naturally, but also organize the military forces including the four northeastern provinces and consolidate ruling power under the center, establishing the form of unified national military authority. However, under the understanding of obeying central orders, de facto military and administrative authority over the four northeastern provinces is entrusted to Mr. Chang Hsüeh-liang.
4.  Establish one Chief Councilor and 12 to 16 councilors in the government, renaming the former National Government Council (國民政府會議).
    Rename the former Administrative Council (行政院會議) to State Council (國務會議), headed by the President of the Executive Yuan (行政院長). The Vice President of the Executive Yuan and committee chairmen may attend meetings of the National Government Council and Legislative Yuan (立法院) as needed.
5.  Merge the Ministry of Agriculture and Mining (農工部) and the Ministry of Industry and Commerce (商工部) into the Ministry of Industry (實業部).
6.  Streamline personnel through these name changes and others, refreshing the ministries.
7.  [Item 7 missing or misnumbered in original - likely skipped, jump from 6 to 8]
8.  Grant amnesty on New Year's Day next year, reducing sentences or releasing political prisoners.
9.  Abolish the Likin tax (釐金) and other similar surtaxes from New Year's Day next year, prohibiting extensions in any province.
Chiang Kai-shek (蔣介石) concurrently assumed the position of President of the Executive Yuan, replacing the late Mr. Tan Yankai (譚延闓). It is also said that Chiang's election as president, to be chosen at the National Assembly next May, is doubtless, leading some to compare Chiang to Kemal or Mussolini.
On the other hand, there are theories that the Reorganizationist faction (政造派 - likely 改組派, 'Kaizupai' or 'Gǎizǔpài') will cease its anti-Chiang movement and rejoin, seizing the opportunity of the National Assembly's convocation. The communist forces' invasion of Jiangxi is intensifying, and it is said Chiang Kai-shek will soon mobilize to suppress them.
Taking the opportunity of the Debt Consolidation Conference, envoys from various countries went to Nanjing. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs reportedly decided to purchase the Legation Quarter in Beiping (北平) and provide sites in Nanjing, suggesting the pending issue of moving the legations to Nanjing will be realized. It is an undoubted fact that the Nanjing government, having achieved a phase of internal pacification, will soon adopt a hardline stance externally to rally public sentiment. Its future trajectory warrants attention.<29>

Other - India Round Table Conference Opens, US Democratic Party Makes Great Strides, Riots in Spain
The India Round Table Conference opened in London on the 12th. The fact that the Indian National Congress party was not invited speaks to a flaw in the conference itself, but at the meeting, Indian representatives unanimously demanded dominion status.
Prior to this, the British Imperial Conference concluded without making any concrete decisions on the free trade issue. Within the Conservative Party, there was a confrontation between leader Baldwin and the Beaverbrook faction's imperial trade group, but it ended temporarily with Baldwin's victory.
In the United States, due to the unprecedented economic crisis, confidence in current President Hoover and the Republican Party has fallen. In this autumn's general election, the Democratic Party, suppressed for the past 10 years, made great strides, achieving near parity in both houses of Congress. This leads to predictions that the Democratic Party will likely have the upper hand in the next presidential election.
Simultaneously with the advance of the Democratic Party, which holds a relatively favorable view towards Philippine independence, the fact that the giant of those islands, General Aguinaldo, intends to launch a fierce campaign in the US mainland regarding the independence issue is a noteworthy fact.
The Soviet Union's Five-Year Plan is reportedly proceeding smoothly. Germany, with 3 million unemployed, is said to be seeing the gradual rise of extremist nationalist and communist factions. The riots in Spain seem to stem from unemployment issues and the maneuvers of disgruntled politicians against the dictatorship. (Written November 20th)<30>

**Summary**

This article from January 1931 provides a snapshot of major political and economic events in Korea and globally over the preceding month (late 1930). In Korea, the dominant issue is the severe agricultural crisis caused by a bumper rice harvest and subsequent price collapse, leading to rural hardship and debates over government relief measures (rice purchases, loans, warehousing) and the controversial colonial rice production increase policy. It also covers the ongoing difficulties faced by Koreans in Manchuria due to anti-Korean sentiment and official policies, political activities including the Sin'ganhoe leadership change amidst government suppression (arrests, trials of communists and nationalists), and the introduction of revised local governance structures favouring Japanese residents. Internationally, it notes the shooting of Japanese PM Hamaguchi, China's consolidation under Chiang Kai-shek after internal conflict, the India Round Table Conference demanding dominion status, the impact of the Great Depression on US politics (Democratic gains), and unrest in Spain.

From a historical perspective, the article is valuable for its contemporary account of the devastating impact of the global economic depression on colonial Korea's agrarian economy and the colonial government's inadequate, landlord-biased responses. It vividly portrays the plight of Korean farmers and the precarious situation of Korean migrants in Manchuria facing discrimination and expulsion threats fueled by Chinese nationalism and Japanese expansionist policies (via the Oriental Development Company). The details on political suppression (Sin'ganhoe restrictions, communist trials) and the subtle ways colonial policy disadvantaged Koreans (e.g., revised suffrage rules) offer direct insights into the realities of Japanese rule in this period.

內外大觀, 過去 一個月間 朝鮮과 世界의 政治的 經濟的 動向의 客觀的 記錄
						朱耀翰
朝鮮
農村問題 - 米價慘落 農村恐慌 漸次 深刻해 갈 뿐, 米穀法 適用問題
秋期에 드러서면서 朝鮮의 가장 큰 문제는 米價의 低落이엇다.
11월 7일에 官邊으로부터 발표된 11월 1일 현재 朝鮮수확 제2회 豫想高는 비록 예상보다 46,742.2석이 감소되엇다 하나 아직도 전년도 實收高에 비하야 550만석의 增收라는 놀라운 數字를 보엿으니 稀有의 대풍이라 아니할 수 없다.
米收穫高累年對照

米2회 수확예상고와 전년 實收高 비교
이 풍작은 朝鮮 안에 현상이 아니요. 日本, 臺灣, 中國 등지를 통하야 공통한 현상으로 米의 생산은 未曾有의 잉여가 되어 9월 이래로 米價는 滔滔한 落潮를 계속하야 11월에는 期米가 石에 12원대를 割下하는 소동을 이르키는 正租一斤에 대하야 농촌에서는 4전이하라는 참담한 가격으로 낙하한 곳도 잇으되 매매가 없는 형편이엇다.
이러한 중대한 사실을 원인으로하야 대체로 세가지의 경향이 나타낫스니 하나는 즉 米價慘落 방지의 운동이요 하나는 農村救急의 운동이며 또 하나는 産米계획에 대한 비판운동이다.
미가의 폭락을 방지하기 위하야 정부에서는 (1) 미곡법에 의하야 買上所有中인 古米를 해외로 輪出하고 새로이 쌀을 買上할 것과 (2) 朝鮮米에 低利자금을 융통하야 월별로 移出高를 조절할 것 및 (3) 朝鮮, 樺太, 臺灣 등지에도 미곡법 제1조를 적용하야 買上을 행할 것을 고려하는 모양이다.
현미의 해외輪出은 中國정부의 輪入금지로서 1頓挫을 받앗으나 布哇, 南洋으로 輪出을 계획하며 終當에는 歐米에까지라도 米의 食糧價를 선전하자는 제창자가 잇다.
米移出의 월별조절은 물론 日本시장에서의 미가를 조절한다는 것으로 이를 위한 미곡창고회사도 咸南지사 松井房太郞을 사장으로 하야 11월 중에 성립이 되엇고 또 藤井實太郞을 중심으로하야 소위 米조절회사의 설립을 劃策하는 중에 잇다. 당국에서는 농업창고 及 상업창고에 입고할 쌀에 대한 금리를 11월 17,8양일에 발표햇다. 즉 농업창고는 일률로 日步 1전 5리, 상업창고에 대하야서는 日本에 移出을 목적으로 한 新穀에 限하야 3월 1일 이후에 출고하며 그때까지에 재고 3월 이상이 될 者는 日步 1전 3리, 기타는 1전 8리로 되엇다. 그러나 가장 요긴한 1,270만원의 자금의 융통에 관해서는 정부와의 교섭이 진보되지 아나 우선 朝鮮은행으로 하야금 재할인하는 형식으로 응급책을 세웟다.
이상의 월별 移出조절을 목적으로 한 米資융통은 대지주 及 米商에게는 이용할 수가 잇고 따라서 米倉會社類의 權會社는 배부르게 할지언정 일반소농 及 빈농에게는 하등의 이익이 없는 것이라 하야 一方에서는 朝鮮에서의 白米買上論 특히 正租의 매상론이 括頭하든 바 마침내 拓務省에서는 21일에 열린 미곡법개정에 관한 연합협의회 석상에서 미곡법 제1조의 朝鮮, 臺灣적용, 즉 朝鮮, 臺灣諸地에서도 米를 買上할 수 잇도록 하자는 제안을 하게 되엇다.
이러한 諸對策으로 인하야 米價는 겨우 더 낙하됨을 면하고 잇는 상태에 잇스나 이미 細農,빈농으로서는 낙하된 가격으로 부득이 처분한 자가 전부일 것이매 매상안이 실현된다＜24＞ 하드라도 그 혜택을 입은 자는 春季에 매출하는 대지주 及 米高뿐일 것은 명료한 일이다.
그러므로 細農, 빈농을 다수 포함한 농촌에 잇어서는 미가대책과 동시에 농촌의 窮境을 응급구제할 방침이 絶阧되게 된다. 혹은 수리조합비의 현물납부, 納付延期의 운동, 채무의 延期운동, 심지어는 납세에 대한 현물납부 혹은 延期의 요구까지 發하게 되엇다.
또 수리조합비의 延期운동이 이러나는 동시에 불량한 수리조합의 약점이 허위에서 폭로되어 혹은 旣設水組의 해산계획중의 것의 중지, 혹은 자금 年賦기간의 연장 등이 제창되고 이어서는 토지개량, 산미증식계획의 일시중지 혹은 대 개정을 요구하게 되엇다. 당국에서는 식량정책의 입장에서 증미계획의 변경은 절대로 없다고 각 지방에 通達햇지마는 현실의 압력하에는 부득이하야 11월 20일부터 토지개량사업 협의회를 열고 대책을 강구중이다.
미가의 慘落, 농촌의 피폐에 관련하야 朝鮮 중농층의 전락과 인구의 추세가 식자의 주목을 끄을고 잇다.

8. 明年元旦을 期하야 釐金 기타 유사부가세를 폐지하고 각 省에 연기를 不許함.
故 譚延闓씨 대신으로 행정원장을 蔣介石이 겸임하게 되며 明年 5월 국민회의에서 선거될 대통령도 蔣씨의 당선이 無疑하다 하야 蔣을 「케말」이나 「뭇솔리니」에 비하는 사람도 잇다.
一方 政造派에서는 국민회의의 소집을 기회로 反蔣운동을 중지하고 합류하리라는 설도 잇고 공산군의 강서침략은 더욱 맹렬하야 近近 蔣介石이 출마하야 이를 토벌하리라 한다.
책권 정리회의를 기회로 각국 公使가 南京행을 햇는 바 외교부에서는 北平의 공사관구역을 매수하고 南京에 기지를 주기로 하엿다 하니 현안이든 공사관의 南遷도 실현될 것이다. 南京정부가 대내평정을 一期로 民氣를 환기하기 위하야 조만간 對外强硬으로 出할 것은 無疑의 사실인 바 今後 그 추세가 주목된다.＜29＞

其他  英印圓卓會議 開催, 米國 民主黨 大躍進, 西班牙의 暴動
英印 원탁회의는 12일부텨 英京에서 개최되엇다. 印度의 국민회의파가 초청되지 안는 것은 회의의 자체의 결함을 말하는 것이나 其席上에서 인도의 대표자는 衆口一聲으로 자치령의 지위를 줄 것을 제창하얏다.
이에 先하야 개최한 英帝國會議는 자유 質易문제에 대하야 何等 구체적 결정을 짓지 못하고 헤여젓다. 보수당 내에서 볼드윈 수령 對 비버부룩 일파 제국통상파의 대항이 잇섯스나 볼드윈의 승리로 일단락을 지엇다.
米國은 未曾有의 경제공황으로 「후-버」現대통령 及 공화당에 대한 信望이 떨어지어 今秋의 총선거에 잇서서 과거 10년간 눌리엇든 민주당이 대약진을 보이어 상하양원을 통하야 瓦角의 형세를 呈햇스므로 벌서 차기 대통령 선거에 민주당이 우세일 것을 豫斷하게 되엇다.
比律貧 독립문제에 대하야 비교적 호의를 가진 민주당의 약진과 동시에 此島의 巨人 「아기날드」장군이 독립문제를 가지고 米본토에 猛運動을 이르키려는 것은 주목할 사실이다.
쏘베드 연방의 5년계획은 順調의 진행을 보이고 잇다하며 獨逸은 300만의 실업자를 가지고 국수, 공산 양극단파가 점차 대두할 형세에 잇다한다. 西班牙의 폭동은 역시 실업문제와 독재에 대한 不平政客의 策動에 依함인 듯 하다. (11월 20일 記)＜30＞
