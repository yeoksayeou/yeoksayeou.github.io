International Meetings and the Activities of Koreans
						One Reporter

Today, the world is one family, and only those who live internationally truly live. For the white-clad people of Chosŏn (朝鮮), located on a small peninsula in East Asia, although they possess a cultural history of 40 million people, not many days have passed since they awoke from the delusion of isolationism (쇄국의 迷夢), so opportunities to stand shoulder-to-shoulder with other peoples on the international stage and be active have been very scarce.

Moreover, as Chosŏn is not an independent sovereign nation today, it goes without saying that it cannot officially attend political international conferences. It is true, however, that in private gatherings related to academia, religion, culture, etc., which do not place importance on national borders, Chosŏn can occupy a place as a cultural group (一文化群) possessing a unique culture. Yet, it seems an undeniable fact (不誣의 사실) that even these opportunities have not been fully utilized, and we have not yet reached the stage of making our existence known and contributing significantly to world culture.

Recently, it is something worth celebrating (慶賀할 만한 일) that our efforts are gradually advancing somewhat in this direction, and repeating the news from this area is likely not meaningless.

The submission of documents in the name of Chosŏn representatives to the Paris Peace Conference and the Washington Conference was the voice of the Chosŏn people, who had been silent since the Hague Incident, resounding in the ears of international figures after a long time. However, regardless of the influence and results, it is certain that these cannot be called instances where Chosŏn people participated and were active.

Perhaps the first formal participation of Chosŏn people in gatherings of an international nature in recent times includes the Newspaper Reporters' Convention held in Washington D.C. in the West, and the Congress of the Peoples of the East, as well as the Communist Party and Communist Youth League Congresses held in Moscow in the East. As for religious assemblies, there was an instance where a Chosŏn representative was chosen as a Korean for the Methodist General Conference. More recently, the late Wŏllam Yi Sang-jae (月南 李商在) and his party attended the World Student Christian Federation conference held in Beijing, and Ms. Kim P'il-lye (金弼禮) participated in the YWCA conference held in India. The World Missionary Conference in Jerusalem the year before last and the Buddhist Conference in Hawaii last summer were of even greater global significance. Besides these, Sunday school conventions, YMCA conventions, and Endeavor Society conventions also belong to this category.<60>

Regarding academic and professional organizations, Chosŏn members have also attended the Institute of Pacific Relations (commonly known as the Pacific Conference), which is still fresh in our memory, heading the list, as well as the Pan-Pacific Surgical Congress and the Pan-Pacific Women's Conference hosted by the Pan-Pacific Union. There were also the International Council of Nurses, the World Engineering Congress, and the International Bar Association meeting. It seems there was also attendance at the Pacific Commercial Conference.

Now, let us hear about the details and impressions from the members or representatives who participated in such gatherings. Please forgive and read on if some are omitted due to unavoidable circumstances.

Institute of Pacific Relations, with Problems Still Unresolved, Currently Deadlocked over Constitutional Amendment Issue

The Institute of Pacific Relations (太平洋關係研究會), commonly called the Pacific Conference, was first convened in Hawaii (布哇) in 1925. The second conference was also held in Hawaii, the third in Kyoto (京都), and the fourth was scheduled to be held in Hangzhou (抗州), China, this autumn.

The purpose of this conference was to gather influential private citizens and scholars from each nation along the Pacific Rim in one place to promote mutual understanding and jointly study various social issues. The host of the first conference was the American YMCA. Since the Korean YMCA was also a member of the international federation, a formal invitation arrived, and the attendees for the first conference were:
From America: Sŏ Chae-p'il (徐在弼), Yun Hwal-lan (尹活蘭)
Korean YMCA: Sin Hŭng-u (申興雨)
Korean YWCA: Kim Hwal-lan (金活蘭)
Tonga Ilbo: Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹)
Chosun Ilbo: Kim Yang-su (金良洙)
Yonhui College: Yu Ŏk-kyŏm (兪億兼)
Posŏng College: [Name missing in text]
These individuals attended.

At this conference, a resolution was adopted to make the conference permanent rather than temporary, leading to the creation of the aforementioned name, the Institute of Pacific Relations. It was decided to form a central council by selecting one representative from each national group. At this point, unexpectedly, a major issue arose regarding whether Chosŏn could also appoint a council member.

Prior to this, at the very beginning (劈頭) of the opening ceremony, each national member group was to give an opening speech. The Chosŏn members consulted each other, and based on the results, Mr. Sin Hŭng-u drafted and read a statement, the gist of which was to portray the spiritual and material conditions of Chosŏn factually. This opening speech was welcomed with applause. However, subsequently, when the divisional discussion sessions opened, a fierce debate happened to arise over a certain issue between Chosŏn member Sŏ Chae-p'il and Japanese member Tōhon (頭本), who were participating in the same division. This marked the beginning of the problem.

Consequently, Mr. Tōhon argued that since Chosŏn was not an independent nation, it could not be allowed to participate with independent qualifications. In response, representatives from China, the United States, and others argued that since the nature of this conference was not a diplomatic official meeting but a private gathering, it was appropriate for participation based on unique cultural units (各 特異한 文化를 單位로).<61> The debate went back and forth (甲論乙駁), but ultimately, when the Japanese committee members threatened withdrawal, the constitution was inevitably established restricting membership to sovereign nations or self-governing dominions.

The second conference was held in Hawaii in the autumn of 1927. At that time,
Chosun Ilbo: Paek Kwan-su (白寬洙)
Ewha College: Kim Hwal-lan (金活蘭)
Yonhui College: Yu Ŏk-kyŏm (兪億兼)
These three individuals attended and submitted a protest against the aforementioned charter, proposing a motion for amendment.

In response, the headquarters sought a compromise with a proposal that delegates from colonies could be invited with the approval of their ruling country. However, the Chosŏn committee members rejected this, stating that this restriction would effectively make the attendance of Chosŏn representatives impossible.

As the third conference was held in Kyoto in the autumn of 1929, the Japanese chapter negotiated with the Chosŏn committee members to attend as part of the Japanese delegation. When news of this spread, various social organizations in Chosŏn passed resolutions opposing it and recommended withdrawal from the Institute of Pacific Relations.

Thus, the Chosŏn committee members finally replied that they would not attend until the charter was amended, leading to a situation effectively amounting to withdrawal. The flustered (狼狽한) headquarters of the Institute then extended an invitation to attend and explain the charter amendment proposal submitted by the Chosŏn committee members. Consequently,
Yun Ch'i-ho (尹致昊)
Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹)
Kim Hwal-lan (金活蘭)
Yu Ŏk-kyŏm (兪億兼)
These individuals rushed to Kyoto (馳往하야) mid-conference as guests. At the Council meeting, Mr. Yun Ch'i-ho explained the basis for Chosŏn's demand to participate as a cultural unit in a lengthy speech, and on the spot, upon the motion of the chief Japanese delegate, Dr. Nitobe (新渡戶博士), it was adopted unanimously by the Council.

However, a fierce opposition movement reignited in Japan against this resolution. The leaders were the aforementioned Tōhon and Count Soejima (副島伯爵), the former president of the Keijō Nippō (京城日報). They launched a fierce attack against Dr. Nitobe for his speech at the Kyoto conference, which seemed self-critical of Japan's foreign policy towards China, while simultaneously attacking the Council's decision regarding the participation qualifications of Chosŏn members.

It is said that amending the charter of the Institute of Pacific Relations requires a 60-day notice period to each chapter, after which the central Council must pass it by unanimous vote. Based on this article, the Japanese chapter council demanded a re-vote on the participation of colonial representatives. Therefore, the issue was deferred to the central Council meeting scheduled for last summer. However, that council meeting could not be held due to circumstances, and now it awaits the resolution of the council meeting to be held in October 1931. Thus, one can only see it as hopeless that a formal invitation will be issued for the fourth conference to be held in Hangzhou, China, in the autumn of 1931.

Consequently, Dr. Shotwell (「쇼트웰」 박사) of the United States, who intended to establish a separate institution for the study of Korean rural villages through the Institute, seems likely to have his plan put on hold (留案) until this issue is settled.

As the conference repeats, its importance is increasingly recognized, and the number of participating world-renowned scholars and politicians grows. Even at the first conference, prominent figures such as the current U.S. Secretary of the Interior, Mr. Wilbur (「윌버」씨), Mr. White (「화이트」씨), editor of the Emporia Gazette, and Dr. Jeremiah (「제레마이아」 박사), a professor at Johns Hopkins University with deep knowledge of East Asian issues, attended. From the Chinese side, figures like Ku Tzu-jen (顧子仁) and Ch'en Li-t'ing (陳立庭) participated. At the second and third conferences, Dr. Nitobe from Japan and<62> Dr. Yü Jih-chang (余日章博士) from China attended, and Lord Hailsham (「해일삼」卿) from Britain also participated. Among the many important issues, the abolition of extraterritoriality in China and the Manchurian problem attracted everyone's attention.
It is said that Koreans devoted all their efforts to fighting for the right to attend and have not yet had the opportunity to submit research materials on important Chosŏn issues.

International Council of Nurses and Pan-Pacific Surgical Congress - First Attendance at Both

In 1929, Korean footprints were imprinted (足跡이 印박히엇다) for the first time in two meetings related to medicine.

The Pan-Pacific Surgical Congress (汎太平洋外科醫大會) was held from August 14th to 24th in Honolulu, Hawaii, hosted by the Pan-Pacific Union. Its purpose was to gather surgeons from the Pacific Rim in one place to foster friendship and exchange surgical knowledge. Participating members came from:
Australia (濠洲)
Canada (가나다)
China (中國)
Japan (日本)
Chosŏn (朝鮮)
South America (南아메리카)
New Zealand (뉴질란드)
North Africa (北아프리카) [Note: Likely an error in original text, possibly meant another region]
Mainland USA (米國本土)
In addition, members of the Hawaii Medical Association participated. From Chosŏn, an invitation came from the host organization to Severance Union Medical College (세브란스 의학전문학교), so Dr. Yi Yong-sŏl (李容卨), a professor of surgery at the college, participated. Also, Dr. Kang Hae-ryong (康海龍) from Pyengyang Union Christian Hospital (平壞 기독병원), who happened to be on his way to America, attended. Several Korean doctors residing in Hawaii also participated.

It was a large gathering with 265 participating members and nearly 200 submitted papers, and there were daily welcoming banquets hosted by officials and civilians.

Dr. Yi Yong-sŏl gave a lecture on the topic "Amoebic Abscess" (아미바성 濃癢). This is likely the first time research by a Korean doctor was presented at such an international meeting.

Koreans residing in Hawaii warmly welcomed the two doctors. When people from various countries marched in procession showcasing their national characteristics, Korean women also participated wearing traditional Korean attire (純 朝鮮服), adding exotic color (異彩를 발햇다).

As this was purely an academic organization, the Chosŏn chapter was duly recognized without the troublesome border issues seen elsewhere, and participation as a member of the Council was achieved. Currently, the Chosŏn chapter council member is Dr. Yi Yong-sŏl, and the secretary is Dr. Kang Hae-ryong. The second congress is scheduled to be held again in Hawaii in August 1932. At that time, it is hoped that an even larger number of Korean members will participate, making significant contributions to the medical field while widely publicizing the existence of Korean culture.

The International Council of Nurses (국제간호부대회) was held from July 1st to 13th, 1929, in Montreal, Canada (北米 「가나다」의 「몬트리알」). The purpose of this council is to carry out nursing work uniformly, and it has been established for many years. Since 1925, Chosŏn had been negotiating to join but could not achieve it. Finally, the year before last,
Severance Hospital (世富蘭偲病院): Yi Hyo-gyŏng (李효경) <63>
East Gate Women's Hospital (東大門婦人病院): Yi Kŭm-jŏn (李金田)
These two individuals, along with a Western nursing staff member, Ms. Schepping (「셉핑」여사), totaling three people, attended representing Chosŏn.

As experts from this field (斯業界) worldwide gathered, although all attendees were women, the proceedings of all matters were as agile (敏活하고) and academic as any men's meeting. As an example, looking at the various issues discussed at the conference reveals how broad the scope of nursing work is:
The need to teach psychological nursing methods within nursing subjects
Nursing education and the economic concept of nursing
Nursing ethics
Public health nurses and social work
Nurses as citizens
Maternal protection
Red Cross work
Laws related to nursing
Government and nursing education
Nurse training centers and recreation
Duties of rural nurses
And dozens of other topics.

Ms. Schepping reported on the recent state of nursing in Chosŏn. The issue of Chosŏn's formal admission could not be resolved because the council's constitution was based on sovereign nations as units, and they returned without a resolution, it is said.

First Formal Admission - International Missionary Council - Trend Towards Indigenous-Centered Policy Heightens

From March 24th to April 8th, 1928, six representatives from Chosŏn attended the International Missionary Council held on the Mount of Olives in Jerusalem, the Holy Land. Among them, excluding two missionaries, four were Koreans. This was the first time (효시) Koreans participated in this meeting, and it was also the first time Chosŏn formally joined this council. The names of the Chosŏn representatives are:
Chŏng In-gwa (鄭仁果), Yang Chu-sam (梁柱三), Sin Hŭng-u (申興雨), Kim Hwal-lan (金活蘭).

Among the 240 representatives sent from 50 national units worldwide, about half were missionaries and about half were native people. Many attendees from distant places like Africa, India, and other smaller countries added exotic color. Among the 37 global committee members elected, one person from Chosŏn was included, and Mr. Yang Chu-sam was elected as the first executive committee member.

A noteworthy event regarding this meeting was that while the composition of this Council had traditionally been mostly missionaries, it was gradually becoming indigenous-centered. At this meeting, native people already occupied about half the positions, and they are expected to occupy even more in the future.

Attendees included Dr. Weigle (「와이글」박사) from Yale University, Dr. Hocking (「헙킨스」박사) from Harvard University, and Dr. Butterfield (「버터필드」박사), who had deep knowledge of rural issues. General sessions were held in the morning, and divisional meetings in the afternoon. While people from various countries prayed in their own languages, prayers in the Korean language also resounded, it is said.

The British representative expressed shame regarding the British government's mistreatment of natives in Africa.<64> Regarding the issue of excluding East Asian immigrants, American representative Dr. Mott (「못트」 박사) declared that America could not hold its head up until this issue was resolved. Representatives from India and China clarified that changing British policy in India and abolishing unequal treaties in China were preconditions (선결 조건) for promoting missionary work, gaining unanimous agreement.

It is said that one of the major gains of this conference was the resolution that the future direction of Christian missions would firstly center on indigenous clergy (本國人 本位) and secondly prioritize rural development. Based on this resolution, it is said that in Chosŏn too, Koreans are increasingly becoming central, and emphasis is being placed on rural issues.
As it is purely a religious circle, this is one of the meetings where Koreans can confidently engage in activities.

World Engineering Congress - 800 Papers Submitted - Professor Kim Pong-jip of Yonhui College Attends

Professor Kim Pong-jip (金鳳集), from the Mathematics and Physics Department of Yonhui College (延禧專門 數物科), attended the World Engineering Congress and the World Power Conference held in Tokyo for six days starting October 29, 1929, as a formal member. This is likely the first time a Korean has shown his face at such an industrial academic conference. It is said there were also several students studying engineering in Tokyo who attended as associate members. Following in the footsteps of our ancestors who boasted of crafts and inventions, it can be said that the first step towards contributing to the new scientific civilization was laid here.

This Engineering Congress was hosted by the Japanese chapter of the World Engineering Congress, and the Power Conference was hosted by the Tokyo Sectional Meeting of the World Power Conference. The event was thriving, with 500 attendees just among the regular members, and 800 papers were submitted to the Engineering Congress.

According to Professor Kim Pong-jip, who focuses on the electrical field, the focal points of discussion, especially in electricity, were:
Television (「텔레비쥰」)
Shortwave Radio (短波長 無線電)

Attendees came from 24 countries: Austria (墺國), Australia (濠洲), Belgium (白耳義), Canada (加奈多), China (中國), Czechoslovakia (첵코슬로박키아), Denmark (丁抹), Finland (芬蘭), France (佛蘭西), Germany (獨逸), Great Britain (英國), Netherlands (和蘭), Italy (伊太利), Dutch East Indies (蘭領印度), Norway (諾威), Poland (波蘭), Romania (루마니아), Philippines (比律賓), South Africa (南亞), Sweden (瑞典), Switzerland (瑞西), Russia (露西亞), USA (米國), and Japan (日本). Among them were Mr. Sperry (「스페리」씨) from the electrical engineering world and many other world-renowned scholars.
The next conference is scheduled to be held in the USA. It is said that by then, Koreans should also be prepared to submit substantial papers.<65>

**Summary**

This 1931 article reports on the increasing, albeit limited and often fraught, participation of Koreans in various international conferences (academic, religious, professional) during the late 1920s. It details specific events like the Institute of Pacific Relations (IPR) meetings, where Korean delegates fought for independent representation against Japanese opposition, the Pan-Pacific Surgical Congress and International Council of Nurses, where Koreans participated formally for the first time, the International Missionary Council which signaled a shift towards indigenous leadership, and the World Engineering Congress. The article highlights both the challenges faced by Koreans due to their colonial status (lacking sovereignty for official political participation) and their persistent efforts to gain recognition as a distinct cultural group and contribute internationally.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant for documenting the struggles and aspirations of Koreans on the international stage during the Japanese colonial period. It reveals the direct impact of colonial politics on non-political international interactions, particularly the conflict over representation rights within the IPR, showcasing Japanese efforts to subsume Korean identity. Furthermore, it provides valuable insights into the early international networking activities of Korean intellectuals, professionals, and religious leaders, and the specific issues (like national status, cultural recognition, professional development) they prioritized.

國際會合과 朝鮮人의 活動
						一記者
今日은 세계가 一家로 국제적으로 사는 자만이 참으로 사는 것이다. 東亞의 一小半島로 이들 朝鮮의 백의 민족, 비록 4천만의 문화사를 가젓다고 하나 쇄국의 迷夢을 깨트린지 멫 날이 못되니 국제 무대에 타민족과 어깨를 나란히 하야 활동할 기회가 매우 드믈엇섯다.
더구나 오늘은 독립한 주권국이 아니매 공식으로 정치적 국제 회의석상에 참석 못함은 두말할 것도 없는 일이다. 오직 학술, 종교, 문화 등 국경에 主重하지 안는 私的 회합에 잇어서 만은 특이한 문화를 가진 一文化群으로 한자리의 지위를 차지할 수 잇는 것이 사실이다. 그러나 그것이나마 기회를 충분히 이용치 못하고 우리의 존재를 인식시키며 세계문화에 큰 공헌을 하기까지에 아직도 이르지 못한 것은 不誣의 사실일까 한다.
近者에는 점차로 우리의 노력이 이 방면으로 조금식 앞서가는 것만은 慶賀할 만한 일이거니와 이 방면의 소식을 되푸리함이 무의미한 것은 아닌가 한다.
巴里講和會議와 華盛頓會議의 朝鮮代表名義의 서류가 날아 들어간 것은 海牙事件 이후에 침묵하든 朝鮮人의 소리가 오래간만에 국제인의 고막을 울린 것이엇으나 그 영향과 결과는 어찌햇든 朝鮮人이 참가 활동한 것이라고 못 일컬을 것만은 확실하다.
아마 朝鮮人으로서 국제 성질을 띤 회합상에 정식으로 참가한 것은 최근에 잇어서는 西에는 米京서 열린 新聞記者大會가 잇고 東에는 露京에서 개최된 동양민족대회 及 共黨, 共靑大會 등이 잇을 뜻하다. 종교적 집회로서는 감리교대회에 朝鮮 대표가 朝鮮人으로 뽑힌 일이 잇엇섯거니와 최근은 北京서 열린 세계기독교학생대회에 故 月南 李商在씨 일행이 참석한 것과 印度서 열린 여자청년대회에 金弼禮여사가 참가한 일이 잇고 재작년 예루살렘의 전세계 기독교 대회와 去夏의 布哇의 불교 대회 등은 세계적 의의가 더욱 큰 것이엇다. 그 밖에 주일학교대회, 청년회대회, 勉勵會大會 등도 이 부류에 속하는 것이다.＜60＞
학술 단체 及 직업 단체로서는 아직도 우리 기억에 새로운 태평양회의라 통칭하는 태평양 문제연구회를 筆頭로 범태평양연맹 주최의 범태평양외과의대회, 同婦人대회 등에도 朝鮮 회원이 참석해섯고 국제看護婦대회, 국제공업대회, 국제변호사대회 등도 잇엇다. 태평양상업 회의에도 출석한 일이 잇엇는 듯하다.
이제 이런 회합에 참가햇든 회원 혹 代表諸氏에게 그 내용과 감상을 들어보기로 하자. 부득이한 사정으로 간혹 빠진 것이 잇는 것은 용사하고 읽기를 바란다.

布哇在住同胞들은 兩씨를 크게 환영햇으며 각국 인민이 자국의 특색을 가지고 行列를 할 때에 朝鮮 여자들도 순 朝鮮服을 입고 이에 참가하야 異彩를 발햇다 한다.
순전한 학술 단체인 이 회합이니만치 다른 것같이 까다로운 국경 문제도 없이 당당하게 조선지부가 승인되고 理事會의 일원으로 참가가 되엇는데 현재 조선지부 이사는 李容卨씨요 서기는 康海龍씨라 한다. 1932년 8월에 제2차 대회를 다시 布哇에서 열기로 되엇는데 그때는 일층 다수의 조선회원이 참가하야 의학계에 많은 공헌을 하는 동시에 조선문화의 존재를 넓히 알리기를 바란다.
국제간호부대회는 1929년 7월 1일부터 13일까지 北米 「가나다」의 「몬트리알」에서 개최되엇다. 이 會의 목적은 간호부의 사업을 통일적으로 진행하잠에 잇고 성립된지 해가 오래다. 1925년부터 朝鮮에서도 이에 가입하려고 교섭하얏으나 현실을 못하고 잇다가 재작년 비로소
世富蘭偲病院     李효경＜63＞
東大門婦人病院   李金田

전세계의 斯業界의 대가들이 집합하니만치 출석자 전부가 여자지마는 모든 일의 진행에 남자의 會合 이상으로 敏活하고 학술적이엇다. 시험으로 同大會에서 토의된 각종 문제를 들어보면 看護 事業의 범위가 얼마나 광범한지를 알 수가 잇다.

처음으로 正式加入 - 萬國基督敎聯合公議會  本國人 本位의 政策高潮
1928년 3월 24일부터 4월 8일까지 성지 예루살넴 橄欖山에서 열린 만국기독교연합공의회에 조선서 6인의 대표가 출석하엿다. 그 중 두 사람 선교사를 제하고 네 사람은 朝鮮사람이엇으니 조선사람이 이 회합에 참가한 것도 이것이 효시요 또 朝鮮이 정식으로 이 회의에 가입한 것도 이번이 처음이엇다. 조선 대표의 氏名은
鄭仁果, 梁柱三, 申興雨, 金活蘭 諸氏다.
전세계 50개 민족 단위에서 派送한 대표 240명 중 약 半分은 선교사요 약 반은 본국인이엇으며 멀리 아프리까, 印度, 기타 소국 등의 출석자가 많아 異彩를 발햇다. 전세계 위원 37명을 선출하는 가운데 朝鮮서도 일인이 참가하게 되어 梁柱三씨가 처음으로 실행위원에 당선되엇다.
이 회합에 대하야 特記한 사건은 在來이 同公議會의 구성 분자가 대부분 선교사들이든 것이 점차로 본국인 본위로 되어가 이 회합에서 이미 약 반분의 지위를 본국인들이 차지햇고 장차는 더욱 많이 차지하게 될 것이다.
출석자 중에는 「예일」대학의 「와이글」박사 「하바드」대학의 「헙킨스」박사 등이며 농촌 문제에 연구가 깊은 「버터필드」박사 등이 출석햇으며 오전에는 전체 회의를 개최하고 오후는 분과 회의를 열엇는데 각국인이 각기 자기말로 기도를 하는 중에 朝鮮말로도 기도가 울리어 젓다고 한다.
영국의 대표는 「아프리까」에서 英정부가 土民을 학대하는데 대하야 스스로＜64＞ 부끄러운 뜻을 표시하며 동양 이민 배제 문제에 대하야 미국 대표 「못트」 박사는 미국이 이 문제를 해결키 전에는 얼굴을 들 수가 없다고 선언햇다. 印度에 在한 영국정책의 변개, 중국의 불평등조약폐지는 선교사업을 진흥하는 선결 조건인 것을 印度와 중국 대표가 선명하야 만장의 동의를 얻엇다.
今後 기독교의 선교방침은 첫재로 敎役者를 本國人 本位로 할 것과 둘재로 농촌의 진흥을 제1의로 할 것을 결의한 것이 이 대회의 큰 소득 가운데 하나이 잇다 한다. 이 결의에 의하야 朝鮮에 잇어서도 朝鮮人은 일층 本位삼게 되엇으며 농촌 문제에 注重하게 된 것이라 한다.
純然한 종교계니 만큼 朝鮮人이 마음 놓고 당당한 활동을 할 수 잇는 회합 중의 하나이다.
