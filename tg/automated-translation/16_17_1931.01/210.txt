**Greeting the Year Sinmi**

Sinmi and Sinmi [New Taste]       Ch'ae P'il-gŭn, Sungsil Professional School (崇實專門大學校)

I
Viewed solely as characters, the two characters 辛未 (Sinmi) are not particularly auspicious. Many words incorporating the character 辛 (sin), such as *sinsan* (辛酸 - bitterness), *sin'go* (辛苦 - hardship), *sinch'o* (辛楚 - pain), and *sillal* (辛辣 - acrimony), are unpleasant. Similarly, many words incorporating the character 未 (mi), such as *mian* (未安 - feeling sorry), *miryŏn* (未練 - lingering attachment), *misang* (未詳 - unknown), and *misuk* (未熟 - immature), are unfavorable. However, we should not engage in superstitious pronouncements based on divination through yin-yang and numerology (陰陽 術數), claiming misfortune because the characters are bad or good fortune because they are good. No matter how fine the characters we choose for names and titles (名號), not everyone receives only happiness; conversely, there are numerous real-life examples of foreigners who, despite having names derived from animals or unfavorable words, became great entrepreneurs.
Since this is spoken playfully, neither theoretical arguments nor enumeration of facts is necessary. Wishing for the pronunciation "Sinmi" to remain unchanged while improving the meaning of the characters, I intend to arbitrarily add strokes. By adding 八斤 (p'al gŭn - eight pounds/axes) to 辛 (sin) and 口 (ku - mouth) to 未 (mi), transforming them into 新味 (Sinmi - new taste), I wish to reflect upon this a little.

II
Ah - Sinmi [New Taste], truly one cannot but call it a good word. Without a new taste (新味), our lives cannot continue joyfully even for a single day or moment. We humans, who maintain our physical existence through metabolism (新陳代謝) and pursue moral cultivation through daily renewal (日新 又日新), demand a new taste above all else. While biological evolution involves a conservative lineage called heredity on one side, it occurs because there is also a creative condition called variation on the other side. However, up to the animal kingdom, there has only been objective history, lacking subjective history. When it comes to us humans, we consciously and self-determinedly create new history. In other words, humans contain self-causality (自己 原因性) within, enabling them to perform active (能動的) actions beyond passive (所動的) ones.
We Chosŏn people, no matter what, have lacked the cultivation of creativity. We imported Chinese characters (漢文) from China (支那) and added new sounds (新音), reading 天 as *hanŭl* Ch'ŏn and 地 as *tta* Chi, but we made no particular changes to their meaning or application. Whether it was Confucianism following the interpretations of Ch'eng-Chu (程朱) or poetry and prose following the style (步調) of Li-Han (李韓), mere imitation was considered the greatest task. Even in recent years, in the study of natural sciences, it is still just imitation. If we continue like this, we will always be busy chasing others without respite (奔汨 無暇). To <51> escape (脫却) this situation (境城), we must have something creative.

III
Let us greet the year Sinmi with a new taste (新味). Let us greet the year Sinmi with new meaning (新意味), new spirit (新氣味), new pleasure (新趣味), and new interest (新興味). Whether for individuals or society, in the field of business or education, let us greet the year Sinmi with a new taste everywhere. Let us leave behind the insipid taste (萵味) and head towards a new taste. Let us not lean too heavily only on Euro-American (歐米) civilization, but let us try to establish (建稱設) our own unique, new-tasting civilization. This must surely be the reason why this journal's name is Tonggwang (東光 - Eastern Light). Light comes from the East. Since civilization is also a kind of light, let us illuminate the world with a civilization of new taste from our East.
As we greet this year Sinmi, let us produce a new taste from our perspective and our standpoint. Let us not excessively boast about past successes or excessively regret (悔恨) past wrongdoings or anything else from the past. If we exaggerate the past too much, we float in emptiness (浮虛) and cannot produce a new taste, and if we regret the past too much, we fall into despair and cannot create a new taste. Let us emanate a new taste from within ourselves, firmly establish ourselves on the path of self-strengthening (自强), broadly seek measures for self-advancement (自進), and thereby bring about a new taste in all aspects of our society. It is a coincidence that Sinmi (辛未) and Sinmi (新味) have the same pronunciation (音韻) and similar character shapes (字形). However, if conscious effort arises from this coincidental motivation, it should be possible to obtain the necessary good results, which can also be called an expression of a kind of creativity. In any case, as we greet the year Sinmi, let us set out on the path of new taste.

I Don't Hold Great Expectations           An Chae-hong, Chosŏn Ilbo Sa (朝鮮日報社)

Those who speak of Chosŏn's most recent modern history often mention the era of the Taewŏn'gun's (大院王) regency. This is because it is thought that the historical fate of modern Chosŏn stood nearly at the crossroads of success or failure then. And during his regency, in the third year of Kojong (高宗), Pyŏngin (丙寅 - 1866), there is the thought that if Chosŏn had seized the opportunity for opening the country and advancing, absorbing the new culture of the West (泰西), and achieving self-reliance and self-strengthening (自立 自强) through the "Foreign Disturbance" (洋亂) when French (佛蘭西) warships unleashed cannon fire in the waters off Kanghwa (江華). Five years after the Pyŏngin Foreign Disturbance, there was another Sinmi Foreign Disturbance (辛未 洋亂 - 1871), referring to the incident when American (美國) warships unleashed cannon fire in the same Kanghwa waters; it is believed that year too was a good opportunity to establish a policy of opening the country and advancing. As the coming new year is Sinmi, these thoughts resurface for me. It would not be an exaggeration to say that one feels Chosŏn has plummeted from the ninth heaven (九天) down to the ninth earth (九地) during these 60 years.
Looking back at the past 60 years, it is good to further gather and tighten one's mind and will for this year. People of Chosŏn with passionate blood should have two kinds of resolve: the urgent one, "Even now, if it's good to throw my body away, I will throw it away..." and the gradual one, "But even if I stumble a hundred times, I wish to serve the people of Chosŏn." The urgent and the gradual may seem contradictory, but in reality, they likely stem from the genuine pure passion (純熱) of sincere service (至誠 奉仕). In this sense, while thinking bitterly (쓰라리게) about the traces of the past where we stumbled and slid, we must nevertheless proceed together on the path of service, both most quickly and step-by-step.
The year Sinmi! 1931, I do <52> not hold better expectations for our Chosŏn. That should rather be considered wisdom and truth. The inactive attitude of the gentlemen, Confucian scholars, and intellectuals gasping in the stifling atmosphere doesn't seem likely to change significantly towards vigorous advancement, and the local administrative system reform currently under consideration will probably end without causing any major changes either. Furthermore, it seems exceedingly difficult to expect that the predicament (難境) of the cutting-edge youth who are hurriedly striving will likely transform into a new stage (一境地) on its own. While China, Japan, and the entire world will certainly experience some political changes (政變) and shifts in their situations, unless there are unexpected incidents, it appears that the general state of affairs, characterized by mutual friction and rivalry amidst ordinariness, will persist. Although considerable disputes can be anticipated in matters like Sino-Russian relations (中露關係), Anglo-Indian relations (英印關係), or the German people's reparations issue (賠償金 問題), I hold no great expectations for this new year called Sinmi. If I have any hope for myself, it is merely to ask that Chosŏn people concern themselves more with, plan for, and strive towards Chosŏn's real problems (現實 問題) through concrete means (具體的 手段), thoroughly (두실르도루).

Concentrate Total Strength on the Unification of the Front        Lawyer Yi In

Having passed the year Kyŏngo (庚午 - 1930), filled with hardship, suffering (艱難 苦痛), melancholy, and anguish (憂愁 煩悶), though it was time like gold and jade, it is unequivocally both relieving and immensely regrettable. Our life's battle scars (戰跡) from the past year are dripping with fresh blood (鮮血이 淋漓) and the wounds (瘡痕) are clearly visible (歷歷하다). Economically, politically, ideologically, in everything. Thus, everything ended up in chaos, inversion, paralysis, and exhaustion (混亂 顚倒 痲痺 枯渴).
Shaking off the old calamities (災厄) and tribulations (患難) on the Kyŏngo New Year's Eve (除夜), let us turn to a renewed spirit (心機) for a fresh start (更始 一新), greet the new year filled with light and hope, and concentrate our total strength (總力量) on the unification (統一) among all the fronts (戰線) of our chaotic and desperately urgent (窮急) lives. We must break through (打開) the destitute and urgent (窮塞 急迫) situation in industry, trade, and education. In breaking through this destitute situation, sincere unity (團結) is more urgent than anything. Living in a common consciousness (共通한 意識), we must, individuals uniting with individuals, and organizations uniting with organizations, concentrate and devote (集中 傾注) the total strength of our whole bodies and souls to the unification of the same consciousness and front. Great effects (功効) manifest (顯現) from sincere collective bodies (集合團).

Only Joyful       Kim Tong-hwan, Samch'ŏlli Sa (三千里社)

The vitality of the Oriental Development Company (東拓), Irrigation Cooperatives (水利組合), the Industrial Bank (殖産銀行), and Financial Cooperatives (金融組合) will likely increase with each passing year, but our fortunes (歸運) cannot keep pace, so my heart aches more with every new year. However, looking at various points, the general trend will not disappoint us, so my feeling upon greeting the year Sinmi can only be called joyful (깃브다).

Let's Fight Cheerfully with Confident Hope
Yi Chong-su

Is it only the young men? Even young students are groaning in prison, so homes are filled with sorrow, and farmhouses blessed with abundant harvests paradoxically face severe decline, filling the countryside with sighs, complaints, and dissatisfaction. Facing our current situation (現狀) as we greet the new year Sinmi, groundless joy (깃븜) <53> and baseless optimistic attitudes are futile.
However, we should not always groan and self-destruct in misery and despair (落望). Let us summon new courage in the new year and continue the hard struggle (苦鬪). Within that struggle, let us be confident of shining hope and bravely break through (突破) our path. In this sense, let us greet (맞융하자) the new year joyfully with new courage.

Five Hopes
Ch'oe Sŭng-man
Christian Youth Association Federation (基督靑年聯合會)

In the year Sinmi,
1. I hope each person becomes "thorough" (철저) in their view of life.
2. I hope people stop deceiving superficially (얼렁얼렁 눈가리고 야옹하는 태도), stop lying, be "truthful" (참되고) and "honest" (정직), be trustworthy, and live like individuals of character.
3. I hope people devote their "whole heart and sincerity" (성심, 성의) to their assigned tasks.
4. I hope the spirit of "cooperation" (협조) and "service" (봉사) becomes abundant.
5. I hope people do not lose heart in tribulation (患難) and persecution (逼迫), but endure patiently (꾸준이 참고 견대며) and advance firmly. <54>

**Summary**

This collection of short essays from the January 1931 issue of *Tonggwang* presents reflections by several Korean intellectuals (Ch'ae P'il-gŭn, An Chae-hong, Yi In, Kim Tong-hwan, Yi Chong-su, Ch'oe Sŭng-man) on greeting the year Sinmi (辛未). Ch'ae P'il-gŭn creatively reinterprets the characters "Sinmi" (辛未) as "Sinmi" (新味 - new taste/meaning), urging Koreans to move beyond imitation, particularly of Chinese and Western models, and cultivate originality and a uniquely Korean "new taste" in all spheres. An Chae-hong expresses pessimism, recalling past missed opportunities for modernization (1866 and 1871 disturbances) and seeing little prospect for significant positive change in the coming year, advocating instead for realistic, concrete efforts focused on Chosŏn's problems. Yi In calls for unity and concentrating all efforts to overcome the current economic and social crises. Kim Tong-hwan acknowledges hardship but finds reason for cautious optimism. Yi Chong-su advocates for continued struggle with renewed courage despite the grim reality. Ch'oe Sŭng-man lists five hopes for personal conduct: thoroughness, honesty, sincerity, cooperation/service, and perseverance.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing of the diverse moods and priorities among Korean intellectuals during the Japanese colonial period. It highlights the deep-seated desire for national self-strengthening and cultural originality (Ch'ae's "new taste") contrasted with a pragmatic, almost resigned realism about the oppressive political and dire economic conditions (An's pessimism, Yi In's call for unity amidst crisis, Kim's acknowledgement of hardship despite colonial enterprise growth, Yi's mention of imprisonment and rural decline). The reference to the 1866 and 1871 "Foreign Disturbances" reflects a continued grappling with historical turning points and the path not taken towards independent modernization. The explicit call to move beyond imitating China and the West, and the invocation of "Tonggwang" (Eastern Light), underscores a nascent cultural nationalism seeking its own path. Historians might find the varying degrees of hope and despair, the focus on creativity versus pragmatism, and the specific social/economic concerns mentioned (imprisonment, rural poverty, colonial financial institutions) valuable for understanding the intellectual climate of 1930s colonial Korea.

辛未年을 맞음
辛未와 新味       崇實專門大學校 蔡弼近

一
문자로만 본다 하면 辛未의 兩字는 그다지 신통하지 못하다. 辛酸이니 辛苦니 辛楚니 辛辣이니 辛字를 넣어가지고 된 말들 중에 滋味롭지 못한 것이 다수다. 未安이니 未練이니 未詳이니 未熟이니 未字를 넣어가지고 된 말들 중에도 좋은 것이 적다. 그러나 문자가 좋지 못함으로 불길하리라든지 문자가 좋음으로 길하리라든지 陰陽 術數의 운명론으로 미신적 언사를 弄할 것은 아니다. 우리가 아무리 좋은 글자로 名號를 짓지마는 다 행복만을 받는 것이 아니요, 외국인은 이따금 동물의 이름이나 좋지 못한 말로 氏名을 지어 가지고도 위대한 사업가가 된 실례가 적지 아니하다.
이러코 저러코 유희적으로 말하는 것이니까 이론적으로만 할 것도 아니요 사실을 열거할 것도 아니다 辛未라는 발음만은 변하지 않고 문자의 의의만은 개선되기 위하야 임의로 자획을 첨가하고저 한다. 辛에는 8斤을 添하고 未에는 口를 加하야 新味로 변하야 놓고 좀 생각하야 보고자 한다.

二
아 - 新味, 참말 좋은 말이라고 아니 할 수 없다. 新味가 없이는 우리 인생이 일일 일시라도 유쾌하게 생활을 계속할 수가 없다. 신진대사로 신체의 생존을 유지하고 日新 又日新으로 도덕의 수양을 영위하는 우리 인생은 무엇보다도 新味를 요구하는 것이다. 생물의 진화도 일면에 유전이라는 보수적 계통이 잇지마는 딴면에 변이라는 生新적 조건이 잇기 때문에 되는 것이다. 그러나 동물계까지는 객관적 역사만 잇엇지 주관적 역사가 없는 것이다. 우리 인생에게 이르러서는 자기 의식적으로 자기 결정적으로 새로운 역사를 창작하는 것이다. 다시 말하면 인생은 자기 원인성을 內含하야 所動적 행위 이상에 능동적 행위를 할 수 잇는 것이다.
우리 朝鮮 사람은 아무리 하야도 창작력의 함양이 부족하엿다. 한문을 支那에서 수입하야 가지고 新音(색음)을 부가하야 문자를 하늘 天하고 읽고 地字를 따 地하고 읽엇지마는 그 의미에든지 활용에든지 특이한 변화를 시킨 것은 없다. 유학이면은 程朱의 해석대로 詩文이면은 李韓의 步調대로 그저 모방한 것이 가장 큰 일이엇다. 近年에 와서 자연과학을 학습하는 편으로도 아직 그저 모방하는 것뿐이다. 이러케 나아간다 하면 우리는 언제든지 타인을 追隨하기에 奔汨 無暇할 수밖에 없을 것이다. 이 境城에서＜51＞ 脫却하고저 하면 우리는 창작하는 것이 잇어야 하겟다.

三

朝鮮의 最近代史를 이아기하는 이가 大院王의 執政 시대를 많이 말한다. 그것은 현대 朝鮮의 역사적 운명이 그대로써 거의 그 성패의 기로에 섯든 것을 생각하는 것인 까닭이다. 그리고 그 집정중인 高宗 3년 丙寅에 佛蘭西의 함대가 江華 바다에서 砲火를 터트리든 『洋亂이란 것으로 朝鮮이 개국 진취하야 泰西의 新文化를 섭취하고 또 자립 자강할 기회를 지엇더라면 하는 생각을 가지고 잇다. 丙寅 洋亂을 지난 지 5년에 辛未 洋亂이 또 잇엇으니 그것은 미국의 함대가 똑같은 江華 바다에서 砲火를 터트리든 일을 가리침이요 그 해로도 개국 진취의 정책을 세울만한 좋은 기회이엇다고 믿는 것이다. 오는 새해는 辛未年이나 나는 이런 생각이 되살아난다. 이 60年 동안에 朝鮮이 九天의 우에서 九地의 알에로 뚝 떠러젓다고 느끼워하는 것은 쓸대없는 일이라고 하여도 과언은 아니겟다.

辛未年! 1931년이라고 나는 더＜52＞ 좋은 기대를 우리 朝鮮에게 갖지는 안는다. 그것은 도리어 총명이요 진실이어야 할 것이다. 답답한 분위기 속에 헐덕이고 잇는 紳士, 儒生, 및 識者層의 사람들의 무위한 태도도 그대지 동떼게 약진적으로 변하리라고 보이지 안코 요새 걸려잇는 지방제도 개정이란 것도 아마 이러타는 큰 변동은 못 일으키고 말을 것이다. 또는 급하게 서들어서 애쓰고 잇는 尖端적인 청년들의 難境도 아마 스스로 一境地에 전환시키는 일은 여간 기대키 어려울 것이다. 중국과 일본과 및 전세계가 다소의 政變 및 그 국면의 推移쯤은 반듯이 잇겟지만 의외의 사변 같은 것은 몰라도 대체를 평범한 가운대에 상호간에 비비고 승강이하는 국면을 지속할 것이라고 보인다. 中露관계 같은 것 英印관계 같은 것 혹은 독일국민의 賠償金 문제 같은 것 등이 상당한 분규가 잇으리라고는 예상할 수 잇으나 나는 이 辛未年이라는 새해에 아무 큰 기대를 가지는 바는 없다. 다만 나 자신으로 희망이 잇다면 朝鮮人이 보담 더 朝鮮의 현실 문제를 구체적 수단에 의하야 두실르도루 관심, 계획, 및 노력함이 잇기를 구할 뿐이다.
