照明彈
						前硝子

Among my elders (尊長), there is one like this.
"Hey there, where did you study?"
He asks this whenever he meets a young person.
"I studied in Sŏul and also went to Tonggyŏng."
Usually, an answer like this comes out.
"Hey, studying is useless too. My son went to America and came back, and now he does *chige pŏri*, *chige pŏri*."
This old man's son indeed went to America, even received a degree, came back, and is now running a transport business with a freight truck. Since it's hauling cargo and receiving money, it is indeed just *chige pŏri* (A-frame carrier work) made slightly modern.

"So-and-so studied in Japan but still..."
"I heard so-and-so even went as far as Germany..."
The evaluation of these intellectuals imported from abroad (外方輸入의 신지식게급) is handled by weighing them against the monthly salaries they receive. When our trusting parents see that these individuals, who spent one, two, three, even ten years throwing away hundreds of wŏn across the sea every month, cannot recoup even a tenth of that "investment amount" (投資額), it seems they cannot help but harbor doubts about this new knowledge.

Regarding this phenomenon, interesting facts are discovered from various perspectives.
In the old days, the reason our ancestors learned scholarship was to pass the state examination, become *yangban*, and live comfortably without labor. The ideology of *kwanjon minbi* (reverence for officials, contempt for the common people) is still deeply embedded in our blood. The clamor for study! study! is entirely aimed at obtaining a diploma to secure a respectable job—a salaried position. Employment, employment, employment—this is the chorus sung by the thousands upon thousands of students leaving the school gates. Thus, if one fails to secure a "respectable" and "dignified" position that compensates for the investment amount during their student days, their "character" (人格) and "talent" (才能) immediately come under suspicion by their parents, wife and children, relatives, colleagues, and juniors. I saw a friend from the countryside who failed the lawyer's examination, tried to get hired at a certain newspaper company, and when that also failed, threw himself into the Han River. I also saw someone who graduated from university and worked as a traveling salesman for a store, but when asked, would just say he was unemployed, his face turning red. He thought the shame of being unemployed was less than the shame of working for a store.

The surplus (剩餘) of the intellectual class is a worldwide phenomenon. An American said that in his country, until three years ago, it was difficult to find competent teachers for schools even with high salaries. Competent people were ambitious and went into business, which involved hard work but offered large gains (收獲), and thus were less likely to apply to the field of education, which was relatively stable but lacked great potential for advancement. However, in the last two or three years, this situation has completely changed. Now, to secure a decent teaching position at a decent school, one must overcome many competitors.
In Chosŏn, this trend is, of course, severe. It would not be an exaggeration to say that Chosŏn is beyond full (滿員), whether in education, business, government offices, or private enterprises. It's partly because business isn't growing, and (One line omitted - original text)

Those people in Chosŏn who own even a plot of land (땅마지기) should contribute some money. Create jobs.
And whatever is left over, there must be a way to export it abroad needlessly. Manchuria and mainland China are vast stages. The South Sea Islands (Namyang Kundo) are fertile ground.

Koreans, who wouldn't roast even bran chaff (겻붙은) even if starving to death, accordingly look down on money. Being upright (淸廉) is the pride of the scholar (*sŏnbi*), and being ignorant of money is the first condition of integrity. This is the chronic illness of the Korean people. Everyone wants to become a scholar who doesn't know how to earn money and dislikes taking up the abacus (수판) to make money. A European exchange student started a fabric store (布木廛). His family has enough to eat.
"Tsk, tsk, crazy person," say those who criticize.
"He probably doesn't feel ashamed," say those who sympathize.
However, Koreans are also people who know money as the highest unit of all value.
"That person's salary is..." comes the exclamation of admiration.
"Well, it's better than being idle..." comes the sneering remark.
Koreans who are ignorant of money, Koreans who know money is paramount. This is a kind of "paradox" (파라독스). A "paradox" is always the truth. <61>

**Summary**

The author critiques the Korean attitude towards education and employment in 1931. Traditionally valued scholarship aimed at securing government posts (*kwanjon minbi*), but modern education, often obtained abroad at great expense, is now primarily seen as a means to a high salary. However, a surplus of educated individuals struggles to find "respectable" jobs that justify the "investment," leading to disillusionment among families and social pressure causing despair or shame for those taking up manual or commercial work. The author notes the contradiction: Koreans traditionally disdain commerce (*sŏnbi* ideal) yet simultaneously judge worth by salary. He suggests creating domestic jobs and exporting surplus intellectuals/labor to regions like Manchuria and Southeast Asia. For historians, the article vividly portrays the socioeconomic pressures on the educated class in colonial Korea, the clash between traditional values and modern capitalist/colonial realities, the early signs of graduate unemployment, and the nascent idea of outward migration/labor export as a solution.

이 노인의 아들은 과연 미국가서 학위까지 맡아 가지고 와서는 화물자동차로 운수업을 하고 잇다. 짐을 날러주고 돈 받는 것이니 하기는 지게버리가 조곰 신식으로 된 것 뿐이렷다.

『아무개는 일본류학생인데두... ... ...』
『아무개는 독일까지 갓다 왓다더라마는』
이들 외방수입의 신지식게급의 평가는 그들이 받고 잇는 월급과 저울질하야 다루게 된다. 한 달에 백원 이상 수백원식을 바다 건너편에 내버리기를 일년·이년·삼년·십년까지 하고 온 이들이 그 「투자액」의 십분지 일도 거누지 못하는 것을 볼 때에 아무리 믿기 잘하는 우리네의 부형들이라도 이 신지식에 대한 의심을 아니 가질 수 없는 모양이다.

이러한 현상에 대하야는 여러 가지 방면으로 흥미잇는 사실이 발견된다.
옛날 우리 조상들이 학문을 배운 것은 그것으로 과거에 급제해서 양반이 되어 노동 아니하고 놀고 먹자는 생각이엇다. 관존민비의 사상은 여태껏 우리 피에 깊이 박여 잇다. 공부! 공부! 하고 떠드는 것은 온전히 졸업장을 가짐으로 훌륭한 취직자리 - 월급자리를 얻고저 하는 것이다. 취직 취직·취직·이것은 교문을 나서는 수천 수만 학생의 합창하는 노래다. 그래서 만일 그가 재학당시의 투자액을 보상할만한 「훌륭」하고 「점잔」은 자리에 취직을 못한다 하면 그의 「인격」과 「재능」은 그의 부모와 처자와 친지와 동료와 후배에게 그만 의심을 받게 된다. 나는 어떤 시굴 친구가 변호사 시험에 낙제하고 모 신문사에 입사운동을 하다가 그도 안되니까 한강에 빠진 사람을 보앗다. 또 나는 어떤 사람이 대학을 졸업하고 어떤 상점 외교원을 하면서 누가 물으면 그저 놀고 잇노라고 얼굴이 벍애서 대답하는 것을 보앗다. 그는 놀고 잇는 부끄럼이 상점에 단니는 부끄럼보다 덜하다고 생각한 것이다.

지식계급의 잉여는 전세개적 현상이다. 미국사람의 말이 자기 나라에서 삼년 전 까지도 학교에 능률잇는 교사를 구하려면 많은 월급을 주고도 얻기 어려웟다고 한다. 능률잇는 사람은 야심이 많아 힘이 들어도 수획이 많은 사업으로 가고 비교적 평안해도 크게 진취할 가능성이 없는 교육게를 덜 지원하기따문이엇다. 그러터니 이 수 삼년래로 이 형세는 아주 변햇다. 인제는 상당한 학교에 상당한 교수지위를 구하려면 많은 경쟁자를 이겨야 된다고.
조선은 이 경향이 물론 격심하다. 교육게나·실업게나·관청이나·개인 사업이나를 물론하고 조선은 만원 이상이라함이 과언이 아닐 것이다. 사업이 일지 안는것도 까닭이어니와 (一行略 - 원문)
