기사제목	冶匠手, 冶爐를 바라보는 靑春魂의 詩 (A Blacksmith's Hand, Poem of a Youthful Soul Gazing at the Forge)
필자	李揆元(詩), 全鳳濟(컷트)
기사형태	시 (Poem)

Like a lump of fire from the Earth's core, in the crimson forge (冶爐),
Strong molten iron (鐵) bubbles and flows.

Born of fire and raised in fire, the old blacksmith's hand (冶匠手),
With a heart hot as a fire spirit (火魂) and muscles sturdy as iron pieces (鐵片),
Strikes forth the strong rhythm of the iron hammer (쇠마치).
Even in the blazing August sunlight, even under the frigid December waning moon.

Occasionally, from his two eyes sunk in fatigue (疲困),
A man's (丈夫) noble tears fall onto the back of his veined hand.
He thinks sad thoughts of his son who did not return from the battlefield (戰地).
Was his heart not fervent enough? Were the weapons (武器) not strong enough?

Amidst the hazy gloom veiled in soot (煤煙),
He stops the hammer (마치) that was moving and prays solemnly:
May my son's passion become red like the forge fire (冶爐火),
May my son's will become hard like an iron piece (鐵片).

When he looks at the molten iron (鐵) flowing powerlessly,
He gives a faint smile to the forge's flame, like a red heart.
When he looks at the iron strip (鐵쪽) sagging limply,
He boasts to himself of the greatness of the hammer (마치), like a steadfast will.

Like a lump of fire from the Earth's core, in the crimson forge,
Strong molten iron bubbles and flows.<101>

**Summary**

This poem depicts an elderly blacksmith working intensely at his forge, his labor juxtaposed with his grief over a son lost in battle. He finds parallels between his craft and his hopes for his son's spirit—praying for his passion to be like the forge fire and his will like tempered iron. The blacksmith draws strength and pride from his work, seeing the hammer as a symbol of steadfast will against the weakness of merely molten metal. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects the industrial labor conditions of the colonial era and potentially carries subtle nationalist undertones through the themes of sacrifice (the son lost in war), strength, and the fervent desire for a strong will and passion, possibly hinting at a longing for national resilience. The focus on heavy industry (the forge) also aligns with the period's modernization efforts under Japanese rule.

丈夫의 거륵한 눈물이 힘ㅅ줄서린 손등으로 떨어진다.
戰地에서 돌아오지않는 아들의 슬픈 생각을 함이라
마음이 뜨겁지 못하엿던가 武器가 굳세지 못하엿든가.

그는 煤煙이 서리운 몽롱한 가운데서
움직이든 마치를 멈추고 엄숙하게 기도하나니
바라건대 아들의 정열이 冶爐火같이 붉어지기를
바라건대 아들의 의지가 鐵片과 같이 굳어지기를.

그는 능력없이 녹아흐르는 鐵물을 바라볼때면
붉은 마음같은 冶爐의 불ㅅ길을 빙그레 우서주고
힘없이 느러지는 鐵쪽을 바라볼때면
굳센 의지같은 마치의 위대함을 스스로 자랑한다.
