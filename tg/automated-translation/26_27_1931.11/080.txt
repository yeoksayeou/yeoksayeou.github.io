Magazine Title: 동광 제27호
Publication Date: 1931년 11월 10일
Article Title: 어머니의 다지는 말슴, 옵바보내는 밤에 (Mother's Firm Words, on the Night Brother Leaves)
Author: 趙宗玄 (Cho Chonghyŏn)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Keep your thoughts deep and don't speak carelessly.
Once words leave your mouth, you can no longer act.
Truly, people who work earnestly are quiet.

Don't tell me the words inside you,
And let's not me tell you the words inside me.
Who knows what will become of the place where mother and daughter sit.

Though you may say all sorts of things, one thing you must not say,
Keep it even more unknown from parents, siblings, and spouse.
I believe it is right to just keep it within oneself.

Do you reveal deeply held intentions just to make them known?
They come out unexpectedly while speaking.
Wherever you go, live with your mouth shut.
-September 3, 1931-<17>

**Summary**

This poem presents a mother's stern advice to her daughter, emphasizing secrecy, caution in speech, and stoicism, particularly on the night the daughter's brother ("oppa") is leaving. The mother counsels against revealing inner thoughts, even between mother and daughter or other close family members, advocating for silence as a virtue and a means of maintaining control. From a historical perspective, the poem's intense focus on guarding one's words and maintaining secrecy could reflect the anxieties and dangers perceived by Koreans living under Japanese colonial rule in the 1930s. Historians might find it valuable as an expression of personal coping mechanisms, the perceived need for discretion in a potentially hostile environment, and the emphasis on inner fortitude within the family unit during that period.

어머니의 다지는 말슴, 옵바보내는 밤에
						趙宗玄
마음만 깊이 먹고 함부로 말을 마라
입박에 말이 나면 그때부터 못하느니
참으로 일하는 사람 좀해 말이 없니라.

네 속에 잇는 말도 내에게 하지 말고
내 속에 잇는 말도 네에게 하지 말자
모녀의 앉은 자리가 어이 될 줄 아느니.

온갓말 다 하여도 한가짓말 안할 것은
부모 형제요 부부게는 더 모르게
저혼자 품고만 감이 올을 줄로 아노라.

깊이 갊은 뜻을 알리려고 알리느냐
말을 하다가 무망간에 나오느니
어데로 가든지간에 입다물고 지내리.
-31·9·3-＜17＞
