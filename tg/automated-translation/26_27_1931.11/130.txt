Income and Expenditure of Laborers (勞働者收支)

How can one clearly understand the income and expenditure situation of laborers? This is no easy task. Firstly, there should be no omissions regarding types of occupations. Secondly, the number of people surveyed should be at least about one-tenth of the total number of laborers. Thirdly, the survey period must not only cover the four seasons but also extend until average figures for various statistical years, including years of good harvest, prosperity, and depression, can be obtained. However, we have not yet discovered survey statistics obtained under such rational conditions. Therefore, let us first examine a glimpse of the situation based on the investigation conducted by the Railway Business Related Investigation Committee in July of Shōwa 3 (1928), although it is insufficient.

This investigation selected 38 types of occupations and surveyed the personal income and expenditure of 426 Koreans, 123 Japanese, and 64 Chinese, totaling 613 individuals. Compiling a table with average figures by occupation type and ethnicity yields the following: <29>

According to the above, the average daily wage for each of the 38 occupations for Korean laborers is 2 Won 2 Chŏn 3 Rin, ranking second among the ethnic groups. However, the percentage of consumption relative to the daily wage is:
--------- Wage / Consumption

Korean Laborer / 100 / 61%
Japanese Laborer / 100 / 55%
Chinese Laborer / 100 / 43%

As shown, because they rank highest [in consumption percentage], we can find that their actual amount [saved] is 22 Chŏn 1 Rin less even than Chinese laborers whose wages are lower.

One interesting point drawn from the table above is the investigation into what kind of sentiments laborers themselves hold towards organizations like trade associations. According to the table, the vast majority of laborers hope for such trade organizations. Among them, it is particularly interesting that Korean and Chinese laborers, whose labor conditions are relatively poorer, hope for them even more. Out of the 613 people surveyed, 444 were those who hoped for or approved of such organizations. The remaining 153 were indifferent, but considering that there was not a single objector, even among Japanese laborers (Note), what else could this be but a clear statement of the overall anxiety of the laboring class within Korea?
(Note) The original Railway Business Related Investigation report divided responses into Hopeful, Neutral (평범), and Indifferent, but for convenience, I have combined Neutral into the Indifferent category, resulting in two categories: Hopeful and Indifferent.

Thus far, we have compared the daily income and expenditure of laborers by ethnicity. However, because this sought the net income based on the occupational average of wages received and daily consumption, without accounting for days of unemployment, there is a significant difference from the actual monthly income of laborers. Therefore, we must now examine their total monthly income and expenditure.

Because wages for unemployed days are deducted from the monthly income and expenditure of laborers, we find that their actual income is not 30 times the daily actual income, but considerably lower.

The average monthly actual income by occupation type for Korean laborers is 11 Wŏn 57 Chŏn 8 Rin, which is 11 Wŏn 35 Chŏn 1 Rin less than the Japanese figure of 22 Wŏn 92 Chŏn 9 Rin, and 13 Wŏn 31 Chŏn 7 Rin less than the Chinese figure of 24 Wŏn 89 Chŏn 5 Rin. Thus, it is natural that the misery of Korean laborers is more than double that of other laborers. Although Korean laborers' wages may be comparable to those of Chinese laborers, they cannot avoid having the smallest actual income because their unemployment rate is the highest among all laborers within Korea. If this were calculated not by occupational average but, as mentioned above, by subtracting total consumption from total wages and then dividing by the total number of laborers, it is doubtful that the average monthly net income of a single Korean laborer would not be 'minus' (마이너쓰). To give one example, among the 'Ordinary Laborers' (普通人夫), who constitute the largest group among Korean occupations, an investigation of the monthly net income of 104 individuals found that 71 had an average income deficit of 4 Wŏn 77 Chŏn 9 Rin, while only 33 had an average surplus of 3 Wŏn 1 Chŏn 4 Rin, resulting in an overall average income deficit of 2 Wŏn 30 Chŏn 6 Rin per person. (Calculated based on the Railway Business Related Investigation). Therefore, even if the 'plus' (풀너쓰) net income of skilled laborers (熟鍊勞働者) is added to this, because they are a small number (小數), it is clear that the overall net income will be significantly lower than the net income based on the occupational average mentioned above. <32>

**Summary**

This 1931 article analyzes the income and expenditure of Korean, Japanese, and Chinese laborers in colonial Korea, based on a 1928 railway worker survey covering 38 occupations. It highlights that while Korean laborers had the second-highest average daily wage (after Japanese, before Chinese), their high consumption rate (61% of wages) and significantly higher unemployment rates resulted in the lowest actual monthly net income, often falling into deficit, especially for ordinary laborers. The article also notes a strong desire among laborers, particularly Koreans and Chinese, for trade organizations, with no surveyed worker opposing the idea. For historians, this article offers valuable quantitative (albeit limited, as the author acknowledges) and qualitative insights into the economic hardships, ethnic disparities in wages and living standards, high unemployment, and emerging labor consciousness among Korean workers under Japanese colonial rule. The explicit mention of calculation methods and data sources (Railway Business Related Investigation Committee) adds to its research value.

勞働者의 一個月收支＜31＞

(표 생략 - 국편)
勞働者의 一個月 收支는 失業日의 賃金이 공제되어잇는 까닭에 그 實際所得額은 一日實際所得額의 30배가 아니며 그보다 훨신 떠러저 잇는 것을 발견한다.
朝鮮 勞働者의 月業種別平均實際收入은 11圓57錢8厘로서 日本人 22圓92錢9厘보다 11圓35錢1厘가 적으며 中國人 24圓89錢5厘보다는 13圓31錢7厘가 떠러지는 셈이니 朝鮮人 勞働者의 비참한 것도 다른 노동자보다 배 이상에 달할 것은 물론이다. 조선 노동자는 賃銀에 잇어서 비록 中國勞働者와 比眉할만하다 하지마는 朝鮮內 다른 어느 노동자보다도 실업률이 가장 높음을 따라 그 실제소득은 가장 소액임을 면치 못하엿다. 만일 이것을 업종평균으로 따지지 않고 상술한 바와 같이 賃銀總額에 消費總額을 減한 뒤 勞働者 總數로 除하여 본다면 朝鮮勞働者 1인의 月平均實得은 『마이너쓰』 안될 것이 의문이다. 그 一例를 들자면 조선 勞働者 業種중 최다수를 점한 『普通人夫』 중 그 104인의 一個月實得 調査에 71인이 평균 4圓77錢9厘의 수입부족이요 겨우 33인이 평균 3圓1錢4厘의 剩餘가 잇을뿐으로 통트러 一人 평균 2圓30錢6厘의 수입부족을 告케 되엇다는 것이다. (鐵道業務關係調査에 의하야 採算함) 그런즉 거기다가 비록 熟鍊勞働者의 『풀너쓰』 實得을 加한다 할지라도 그것이 小數이기 때문에 全體로 보아 그 實得이 상술한 업종별 평균의 實得보다는 무척 低下될 것이 명백하다.＜32＞
