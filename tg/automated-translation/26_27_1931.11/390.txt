**Body Text Translation:**
A Stern Critique of Family Tragedy
						By Various People

1.  In a household with many young children, if the wife commits adultery, what attitude should the husband take?
2.  If the husband is incompetent, should the wife separate or divorce him?

These kinds of things happen all the time. It might be pointless to argue this way or that about such abstract problems, but since these tragedies occur daily, and especially now when a Certain Recent Incident (某某事件) has become a topic of conversation both in the capital and the provinces, finding out the opinions of Various People on this matter would not be meaningless. Here are their opinions (in no particular order).

Separation is Also Fine
Kim Kyŏngsuk

1.  If viewed from a situation with absolutely no constraints regarding living conditions, the problem is extremely simple. If the wife commits adultery, divorce is the natural course. However, the actual, concrete problem will likely not be that simple. Look! Consider how much our society's laws presuppose adultery. The fact that a child born during marriage is considered the husband's child, etc., proves this. And there are countless cases where, pressed by livelihood, a husband pretends not to see his wife's unwilling adultery. In short, there cannot be a uniform answer to the question; the resolution to the problem of adultery can only be found in the resolution of fundamental social system problems.
2.  If not separating interferes with social activities, then separation is perfectly fine. However, separating because the husband is incompetent is something to be done after resolving oneself to break the marriage (婚). Why? Because the condition of a husband's incompetence is not a legally recognized condition for separation, yet cohabitation cannot be forced either. (Interview)

Emotions Won't Allow It
Yi Kwangsu

1.  Living together with a wife who has committed adultery, unless there are special circumstances, I believe that for Korean people today, emotions would not permit it. Once a woman commits adultery, living together has already become impossible.
2.  If a man or woman feels they cannot live together, divorce is the only option, but trying to justify oneself by pinning all sorts of defamatory labels (陋名) like the husband being incompetent and so on onto the other party is unacceptable. (Interview) <74>

Love is the Issue
Hŏ Yŏngsuk

1.  If a wife doesn't want to live with her husband, she should get a divorce and then have relations with another man; why commit adultery? Divorcing a wife who slips out the back door because there is no love is unavoidable.
2.  If there is no love, divorce is fine, but saying one cannot live together because, despite there being love, one cannot earn enough to feed two children and the husband besides, makes no sense. The issue is less about the husband's incompetence and more about whether love exists or not. If there is love, she should live with him, earning a living as best she can, just like before. If there is no love, she should stop making excuses and just say she cannot live with him; that would be right. (Interview)

Why Quibble Over Private Life?
Yu Sanggyu

1.  Adultery or whatever, as long as it has no relation to social life, there is no need to talk about the private lives of two individuals. Aren't there plenty of such people in Korean society now? And even if we were to speak only to the extent that an individual's sexual life affects society, one cannot speak uniformly without knowing the specific, complex circumstances. We must first praise their social activities rather than discussing their private lives.
2.  The issue of separation or divorce because the husband is incompetent is also not much of an issue. Why is there a need to expose the private life of someone who has no social activities whatsoever? And if an incompetent husband becomes an obstacle to her social activities, there is no problem whatsoever with her separating or divorcing as she pleases. (Interview)

If You Don't Like It, Divorce     Doctor of Medicine Paek Injê

1.  Viewed from the old morality, committing adultery makes one a slut (잡년), what else? Japanese people seem to love their family or children more than individuals, so even if the wife commits adultery, they sometimes continue living together, apparently. But if I were in that situation, of course, it would be divorce.
2.  Separating because the husband is incompetent ultimately means you don't want to live together, so if you don't want to live together, divorce is the natural course. There's nothing to argue about. (Interview)

It's No Longer an Issue     OO Girls' High School 4th Year Kim O-hŭi

This kind of problem is already not a problem. The new morality of the new era has already resolved it all. That the responsibility for chastity must be exactly the same for men and women, or that the freedom of divorce should be recognized, or that systems which fail to guarantee women's individuality are irrational – these are things we all know. It seems there is much controversy regarding the stance recently taken by a Certain Lady (某女士), but those are stale arguments stemming from the tyrannical male-centered moral code and the submissive psychology of women unknowingly bound by that morality.
We actively support the actions of the Certain Person (某氏). That she could carry it out individually is due to her special circumstances. How many of us are unable to do so and still suffer under the iron heel (鐵蹄下) of men? We hope the day comes soon when we can achieve socially what she has achieved individually. <75>

The Family is a Mutual Responsibility
Yi Yunjae

1.  The family is a mutual responsibility. When demanding chastity from a woman, the man also has the duty of chastity (貞操). Demanding chastity only from the woman is wrong. For the sake of the children, one should wait for the unfaithful wife's repentance. However, if she ultimately cannot mend her ways, divorce is natural.
2.  A Certain Lady (某女士) I know engages in many social projects. Her husband is a drunkard. Thus, the home is a chaotic mess (난장판). Even so, the Certain Lady endures it patiently. If the man is incompetent and the woman also cannot earn a living, then separating is natural. If either one (even the woman) can earn a living, then living together is natural.

Conflict Between Reason and Emotion
Ham Sanghun

1.  What to do with an adulterous wife? Of course, logically, if there is leeway to wait for a man's profligacy to be repented, then a woman's mistake should also be forgiven (容赦), and one should wait for her reform (改過遷善). Especially if there are many children, one should do so for their sake. However, emotionally speaking, even I would find it difficult to do so. Of course, that is because feudal consciousness (封建意識) has not been eradicated...
2.  Regarding an incompetent husband, if he unfortunately became disabled and incompetent after marriage, if the wife has the ability to support (扶養) him, trying her best with devotion would be an expression of true marital love. Otherwise, if after marrying, she finds the husband was originally incompetent and unable to manage property/assets (治産), then leaving him as soon as possible is better. If children have been born... well, that's a big problem. Under the current capitalist system (現資本制度), that is a big problem... (Interview) <76>
<74-76>

**Summary**

This 1931 survey in *Dong-gwang* magazine gathers opinions from various Korean intellectuals and a student on marital crises: a wife's adultery with children involved, and separation/divorce due to a husband's incompetence. Responses range from traditional views demanding divorce for adultery (Yi Kwangsu, Paek Injê) to more complex considerations involving love (Hŏ Yŏngsuk), economic necessity (Kim Kyŏngsuk), mutual responsibility (Yi Yunjae), the conflict between reason and emotion (Ham Sanghun), and prioritizing social contribution over private life (Yu Sanggyu). A female student (Kim O-hŭi) strongly advocates for modern views, gender equality in chastity standards, divorce rights, and supports a contemporary "Certain Lady" who likely defied traditional norms, framing it as a step towards women's liberation from male oppression.

From a historical perspective, the article is significant as it captures the turbulent social transition in colonial Korea, showcasing the clash between entrenched Confucian patriarchal values and emerging modern ideas about gender equality, individual autonomy, and the nature of marriage, influenced by Western thought and social change. The differing viewpoints, especially the stark contrast between older male intellectuals and the young female student, highlight the generational and ideological divides concerning women's rights, divorce, and sexual morality during a period of rapid, though constrained, modernization under colonial rule. The reference to a specific contemporary incident (likely involving a 'New Woman' figure) underscores the real-world relevance and contentiousness of these debates.

別居도 無妨
金景淑

1. 생활조건에 하등의 부자유가 없는 경우로 보면 문제는 극히 간단하다. 간통하는 妻면 이혼하는 것이 당연하다. 그러나 현실적 구체적 문제는 그러케 간단치 않을 것이다. 보라. 우리의 사회의 법률이 얼마나 간통을 전제로 하엿는가를. 혼인중에 생산한 아이는 그 남편의 아이로 간주한다는 등은 이것을 증명하는 것이다. 그리고 생활에 쪼끼어 그 妻의 본의아닌 간통을 번이 보면서도 못 본체 하는 경우가 부지기수다. 요컨대 문제의 대답은 일률적으로 되지 않을것이니 간통. 그 문제의 해결은 근본적 사회제도문제의 해결에서만 얻을 수 잇다.
2. 별거를 하지 않아서 사회적 활동에 방해가 된다 하면 얼마던지 별거해도 좋다. 그러나 남편이 무능력하다고 벌거하는 것은 婚을 각오한 후의 일이다. 왜 그런고 하면 남편의 무능력이라는 조건은 법률상 벌거를 용인하는 조건이 아니요 그러타고 동거를 강제할 수 없는 일인 까닭에.(談)

感情이 不許
李光洙

2. 남자나 여자가 살지 못하겟으면 이혼 할 수 밖에 없지마는 자기를 정당화하기 위하야 남편이 무능력하니 마니 온갓 陋名을 상대편에 씨우는 것은 안될일이오.(談)＜74＞

사랑이 問題

1. 안해가 자기 남편과 살기 싫으면 이혼을 하고 다른 남자와 관계를 하지 간통을 왜 해요? 사랑이 없어서 뒷문으로 나드는 안해를 이혼하는 것은 할 수 없는 일입니다.

私生活에 是非가 웬 일
劉相奎

1. 간통이고 무엇이고 사회생활에 아무 관계없는 한 두 사람의 사생활을 가지고 말할 필요도 없다. 지금 조선사회에는 그런 사람이 얼마던지 잇지 않은가? 그러고 만일 개인의 성생활이 사회에 영향을 주는 한도내에서 말한다 하여도 그 구체적 복잡한 사정을 모르고 일률로 말 할 수는 없는 것이다. 우리는 개인의 사생활을 논의하는 것보다 먼저 그의 사회적 활동을 찬양하지 않으면 안된다.
2. 남편이 무능력하다고 별거니 이혼이니 하는 문제도 별로 문제가 되지 안는다. 아무 사회적 활동이 없는 사람을 갓다가 그의 사생활을 들출 필요가 무엇인가?
그러고 만일 그가 사회적 활동을 하는데 무능력한 남편이 방해가 된다면 마음대로 별거나 이혼하는데 아무 상관이 없다.(談)

싫으면 離婚     醫學搏士 白麟濟

1. 舊도덕으로 보면 간통을 하는 것은 잡년이지 무엇이오? 일본 사람들은 개인보다 가정이나 어린애를 더 사랑하는 까닭에 안해가 간통을 하여도 그냥 사는 수도 잇나 봅디다마는 내가 그런 처지에 잇다고 하면 물론 이혼이지오.
2. 남편이 무능력하다고 별거하는 것은 결국 살기 싫다는 말이니 살기 싫으면 이혼하는 것이 당연하지오. 시비할 것도 없습니다.(談)

벌서 問題가 아니다     OO女高 4학년 金O姬

이따위 문제는 벌서 문제가 아닙니다. 새 시대의 새 도덕은 이미 다 해결짖고 잇는 것입니다. 남녀의 정조에 대한 책임이 꼭 같아야 된다든가 또는 이혼의 자유를 인정한다든가, 여성의 개성을 보장치 못하는 제도는 불합리하다든가 하는 것은 우리가 다 아는 것입니다. 최근 某女士가 취한 태도에 대하야 말성이 많은 모양입니다 마는 그것은 횡폭한 남성본위의 도덕률에서 그리고 그 도덕에 不知 不覺중에 얽어매운 여성의 屈從心理에서 나오는 케케묵은 소리들입니다.
우리는 某氏의 행동을 적극적으로 지지합니다. 그가 개인적으로 단행할 수 잇은 것은 그의 특수한 경우를 인함입니다. 그러치 못하고 아직도 남성의 鐵蹄下에 신음하는 우리가 얼마나 많습니까. 그가 개인적으로 성공한 것을 우리는 사회적으로 성취할 날이 어서 오기를 바랍니다.＜75＞

家庭은 雙方의 責任
