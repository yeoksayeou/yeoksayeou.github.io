Magazine Title:	동광 제27호
Publication Date:	1931 November 10
Article Title:	豫言?, 第二次世界大戰!!, 원제 大戰이 일어나나?, 그 相對國은 어느나라? (Prediction? World War II!!, Original Title: Will the Great War Occur?, Which Countries Will Be the Opponents?)
Article Type:	설문 (Survey)

Prediction? World War II!!, Original Title: Will the Great War Occur?, Which Countries Will Be the Opponents?
World War! The world's attention is focused on the Second World War. 1. Will the Great War occur, and 2. Which countries will be the opponents? Predicting this might be a futile endeavor, but what we want to know is indeed this, and what draws our interest is truly this. When looking at the entirety of the world's situation, it is not impossible to make such predictions. Didn't General Hindenburg (힌델붉), the former supreme commander of Germany during the Great European War, predict that the Second World War would break out on May 1, 1932? Then, what are our predictions indeed? We believe the predictions of various experts on this matter will give us something more than just interest.
We extend special thanks to all who sent replies amidst their busy schedules. (Manuscripts arrived in order) <2>

War in 1934, Protagonists Japan, China, America, Russia
Chosŏn Ilbo President An Chae-hong (安在鴻)

1. The Second World War will break out in 1934.
2. The opposing camps will be the entire Chinese continent and its northern regions, and the Pacific Ocean. Japan (日), China (中), America (米), and Russia (露) will be the main actors. Britain (英), Germany (獨), France (佛), and Italy (伊) will play supporting roles.

Within 10 Years, Japan and China
Lawyer Kim Yong-mu (金用茂)

1. It is difficult to assert exactly which year the Second World War will occur, but I hypothesize a world war within the next 10 years.
2. I believe Japan and the Republic of China will be the main protagonists when it breaks out.

In Less Than 10 Years, Vertically a Class War, Horizontally a War of Powers
Chosŏn Ilbo Yi Sŏn-gŭn (李瑄根)

1. While the Imperialist Powers seem to be trying to avoid war on the surface, in reality, war is unavoidable, and it is a fact that they are engrossed in preparations. When will it break out? I am not a prophet, so I do not want to determine the timing to a specific year. However, it seems likely to be in the not-too-distant future. At this rate, it probably won't exceed 10 years.
2. The opposing camps will be, vertically, a fight between capitalist countries and socialist countries. Horizontally, it will be a fight among the capitalist countries themselves. Regarding the latter, the Germany (獨)-France (佛) relationship in Europe (歐洲), and the Japan (日)-America (米) relationship in the East (東洋) are not looking good.

After 10 Years, Japan vs. China and America
Posŏng College Professor Ok Sŏn-jin (玉璿珍)

1. After 10 years.
2. Japan vs. China and America (米).
Attitudes of various countries during the Great War:
Germany (獨逸) will be neutral, Italy (伊太利) will join Japan (日), Britain (英國) will be neutral, Russia (露國) will actively pursue global communization (赤化運動), France (佛國) will join America (米) and China (中).

Within 10 Years, Britain and ?
Kyŏngsŏng Ko Yŏng-hwan (高永煥)

1. Even if the so-called "Manchurian Incident" dispute between Japan (日) and China (中), which is currently the issue, is resolved amicably without dragging on for more than a year, it seems an undeniable fact that a Second World War must inevitably explode within 10 years. (However, predicting the exact year and month the Second World War will definitely explode seems like a dangerous task beyond the ability of this writer, who is not Yi T'o-jŏng (李土亭) or Shao Kangjie (邵康節).)
2. In the future Second World War, one of the opposing sides will probably be Britain (英國). <3> I wish to leave the identity of the other fighting nation to the imagination of the wise readers.

This Winter or Next Spring, Japan and America as Main Combatants
Tonga Ilbo Ham Sang-hun (咸尙勳)

1. Seeing Zhang Xueliang (張學良) concentrating troops in Jinzhou (錦州) and Jiang Jieshi (蔣介石) entrusting the chairmanship of the Nationalist Government to another and going to Henan Province (河南省), a militarily vital location, it is clear that China intends to fight Japan. In Japan too, many discuss the incompetence of the Wakatsuki (若槻) cabinet, and internal and external troubles have piled up, so I think it will inevitably be replaced by a militarist cabinet before long. Then, it is clear as day (明若觀火) that war will break out between Japan and China. However, whether this Sino-Japanese War will lead to a world war is highly noteworthy; firstly, America's attitude. While writing this, I have not yet heard reports on American public opinion regarding the Jinzhou attack, so it is difficult to assert definitively, but the League of Nations Council meeting opening on the 14th will surely take up the Jinzhou bombing as an issue. I believe America will not overlook it either. If America begins to interfere, Japan is unlikely to respond meekly, so I wonder if the Japan-America clash, which I usually thought would occur due to China's declaration of abrogating the 21 Demands in 1936, might be brought forward and start this time. I declare that a Japan-America clash will cause a world war. A decisive battle between Japan and China might be imminent (目睫面). I believe the Japan-America clash will occur this winter or next spring.
2. If Japan and America go to war, Britain, which has similar interests in China as Japan, cannot remain silent. Britain may dispatch troops to protect its colonies along the Chinese coast and the Pacific. It cannot but become a war against China and America. France (佛國), which also has colonies and interests in Indochina (印度支那), China, and the Pacific, will also be unable to maintain a spectator's attitude.
France's participation in the war cannot but become an opportunity for Italy and Germany for immense territorial expansion and recovery. Italy has been in conflict with France over the Tunisia (튀니티) issue and the Africa (亞弗利加) issue, so Mussolini (뭇솔리니) is unlikely to miss this golden opportunity, and Germany, with its National Socialist Party (國粹社會黨) trying to overthrow the current government through revolution, will inevitably enter a reactionary phase soon and cannot but participate in the war. The Italy-France and Germany-France wars are a reenactment of the European war. Poland (波蘭), Czechoslovakia (첵크·슬로바키아), Yugoslavia (유고·슬라비아), and Romania (羅馬尼), which support the Treaty of Versailles (벨사이유 조약), may join France. Austria (墺地利), Hungary (匃牙利), Bulgaria (勃牙利), Albania (알바니아), Greece (希臘), etc., which seek to revise the Treaty of Versailles, cannot but help Germany and Italy and fight against the French side. When the capitalist empires are engulfed in the flames of war, the Soviet Union (쏘련) will not be left alone in peaceful industrial development; Britain, citing incitement of colonies, and France, citing incitement of revolution, cannot but have neighboring Poland and Romania attack the Soviet Union and drag it into the world war.
The possibility of war breaking out was rather high in Europe (歐洲), but it seems this is being reversed, and it looks like it will break out first in the East (東洋).
Although I believe a world war will break out due to the Japan-China conflict, even if this is avoided by some chance, the Germany-France, France-Italy, and France-Russia relationships are worsening by the moment, and I wonder if we might see war from next spring. When the starting point is in Europe, there is no room for doubt that the camps will repeat as described above.

Within 10 Years, Japan and America Will Fight
Hyŏn Sang-yun (玄相允)

1. The cause of the outbreak will lie in the clash between Japan and China centered on Manchuria (滿蒙), but even this time, in principle, China lacks the strength to resist, so it shouldn't become a war. However, if due to the incitement of the Chinese people's hostility, some outrageous unexpected incident occurs in some area, Japan will not overlook it, and if so, it might perhaps become a war. But from the perspective of war philosophy, war <4> will break out only when the opposing country, China, gains enough strength to confront Japan, so naturally, it seems it will take about 10 years.
The central camps of the war will be Japan and America, and the war will mainly take place in the Far East and the Pacific; it seems unlikely to occur on the European mainland (歐洲本土). Therefore, France (佛), Russia (露), Germany (獨), and Italy (伊) do not seem likely to be drawn into the vortex (渦中) of war. However, if France, Russia, Germany, and Italy also rise up (崛起) due to the war debt (戰債) and reparations (賠償) issues from the First World War and colonial issues such as Annam (安南), it will be truly serious. If that happens, Germany and Italy will contend with France, and Japan will seek powerful nations capable of facing Britain and America for its own side, so it is questionable which side, Japan or the other, France and Russia will join.

By Next Year at the Latest
Yi Pong-su (李鳳洙)

1. The prelude is now sounding in Manchuria (滿洲). I wonder if a major explosion won't happen by next year at the latest.
2. One side will bundle together Japan (日本), Britain (英國), and France (佛蘭西) as main countries, along with smaller nations like Poland (波蘭), Czechoslovakia (체크슬로바키아), Romania (루마니아), etc. The other side will have America (米國), Germany (獨逸), Italy (이태리) as main countries, along with China (中國) and smaller nations under British and French oppression like Turkey (土耳其), Central Asia (中央亞細亞), etc. Russia's (露西亞) attitude is the most noteworthy point, but it seems unlikely to participate directly for quite some time.

Sinsaengsa Yu Hyŏng-gi (柳瀅基)

Because I am an advocate of non-war (非戰爭論者), I do not even think about a Second World War.
From now on, it will be an ideological war (主義的 戰) rather than a war of weapons (武器的 전쟁).

Within 3-4 Years, China-America vs. Japan-Britain
Tonga Ilbo Kim U-p'yŏng (金佑枰)

Your company's questions (1) In which year will the Second World War break out? (2) Which countries will be the opposing camps at the time of outbreak? are, given the times, likely to be interesting, I think. Thus, I will try to write down roughly what comes to mind.

Anyone concerned with the current international political situation will undoubtedly say that the present age is indeed, truly, an era of precariousness (風雨飄搖時代). In various Western countries, the turmoil in the global financial world, originating from Germany's financial distress, finally led to Britain's unavoidable suspension (停*) of the gold standard. Furthermore, the drastic changes in the currency systems and financial structures of various European countries have caused fundamental anxiety in the financial circles of America, which was recognized by itself and others as a major global investor nation. Not only that, but the plight (窮狀) of rural villages due to the crash in agricultural product prices is indescribable (不可形言). Thus, Mr. Hoover is said to be establishing a large $500 million financial corporation as a stabilization measure for the American financial world, while simultaneously trying to promote a recovery trend in the international economy, and at the same time advocating for arms reduction in various European countries. Interpreting such a global economic panic politically means that the various imperialistic antagonisms are becoming acute. Of course, the tariff policies, dumping (廉賣), low interest rates (低利), shifts in power alliances (勢力의 離合), new offensive-defensive military alliances (新攻守軍事聯盟), armament build-ups (軍備의 充實), and war preparations (戰爭準備) currently being undertaken by the so-called great powers are facts, but who could possibly predict when the Great War will break out? Merely based on conceptual and common-sense reasoning, one might say that the entire world will inevitably be divided into two major camps, <5> and consequently, it is difficult to predict who the members of these two camps will be.
The current Japan-China issue has already become delicate (떼리케잇트) and is under the world's watchful eye. In short, Manchuria is said to be the economic and strategic base (地盤) of XX Imperialism. Through this, XX Imperialism obtains its vital nourishment. Not only that, but it is of vital importance to nations seeking to acquire rights and interests in China. If XX Imperialism arms itself, establishes hegemony in East Asia (東亞), and comes to dominate Manchuria, American capital could not but be called a castle built on sand (砂上 高厦). The Nine-Power Treaty regarding China explains this. Furthermore, looking at the relationship between Britain and America, it is becoming increasingly acute regarding the establishment of new market spheres of influence. That is, the struggle between the two countries centers on acquiring export markets for goods and capital. This is particularly true in South America (남아메리카), old Chinese colonies (中國古殖民地), and protectorates (保護國). If that is the case, should a Sino-Japanese war occur, it seems likely that a great war between China-America versus Japan-Britain will break out. As for the timing, judging by the current situation, America is not moving, and it seems the current unstable state will continue for 3-4 years.

Within Several Years, Japan-America and the Two Classes
Mun Il-p'yŏng (文一平)

1. The worsening Japan-China relationship will eventually provoke the Second World War. If soon, it might even happen within a few years.
2. Centered on the struggle for hegemony (爭覇) between Japan (日) and America (米), Russia's (露國) participation in the war seems likely to drive the world to divide into two major camps, imperialist countries and socialist countries, and fight. <6>

**Summary**

This 1931 survey article from the magazine *Tonggwang* gathers predictions from prominent Korean intellectuals (journalists, lawyers, academics) about the timing, participants, and causes of a potential Second World War. Most respondents foresee a major war within the next 10 years (some predict much sooner, even 1932 or 1934), frequently citing the Manchurian Incident and escalating Japan-China tensions as the likely trigger in Asia. Key predicted matchups include Japan vs. China, Japan vs. the USA, and broader conflicts drawing in European powers (UK, France, Germany, Italy) and the Soviet Union, often aligning along existing political (Versailles Treaty supporters vs. revisionists) and emerging ideological lines (capitalism vs. socialism/communism). The predictions heavily feature Japan, China, the USA, and Russia as central players, particularly in an anticipated Pacific theater. From a colonial history perspective, the article is fascinating as it shows educated Koreans under Japanese rule actively analyzing global geopolitics and forecasting a conflict where Japan, their colonizer, is expected to be a central belligerent. Their diverse predictions, anticipating conflicts between imperial powers, ideological clashes, and specific alliances (sometimes placing Japan against the US/China, sometimes with Britain), reflect the complex international anxieties of the era and offer insight into how colonial subjects perceived the shifting world order and the potential vulnerabilities or ambitions of the Japanese Empire.

세계대전! 세상의 이목은 제2차 세계대전으로 집중하엿다. 1. 원제나 大戰이 일어나며 2. 그 상대국은 어느 나라가 될까. 이것을 예언하는 것은 부질없는 일일지 모르나 우리의 알고 싶어하는 것은 실로 이것이며 우리의 흥미를 끄는 것도 실로 이것이다. 세계의 모든 情勢를 종합하야 볼 때에 그 예상을 못할 바도 아니니 歐州大戰의 독일의 총사령관이든 「힌델붉」 장군은 제2차 세계대전은? 1932년 5월 1일에 발발할 것이라고 예언하지 않엇는가. 그러면 과연 우리의 예상은? 여기에 대한 諸家의 예언은 우리에게 흥미 이상의 무엇을 줄 것이라고 믿는다.
