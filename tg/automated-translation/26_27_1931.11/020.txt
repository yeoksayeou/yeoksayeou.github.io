Hymn to Power
						Ch'unwŏn

Power!
Today's beauty lies in power.
Rather than the powdered white face,
The sun-tanned dark one,
Rather than skin like silk strands,
Sinews like lumps of iron!
Cast aside refinement,
It is authority, it is toughness!
Oh people, today we seek power.

Power!
Today's righteousness lies in power.
Rather than refined etiquette,
The trustworthiness that does what it says,
Rather than polite humility,
The guts to stand up for one's own!
Quickly lift your weak hands!
Clench both fists tightly!
Oh people, today we seek power.

Power!
Today's glory lies in power.
Tear down the towers offering prayers
And build gun emplacements for cannons!
What use are the white clothes of peace?
Wear the red uniforms of the troops
And arm yourselves completely, body and mind!
Oh people, today we seek power.

Power!
When there is battle between infantry and artillery,
When airplanes are said to drop bombs,
When we see the trucks transporting munitions,
When the ultimatum is issued, is issued,
Our hearts pound with unspeakable excitement.
Ah, the endless troops bearing guns and swords!
Oh people, today we seek power.<1>

**Summary**

This poem by Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu) is an ardent call for embracing power—physical, resolute, and military—as the supreme value of the modern age. It contrasts traditional Korean aesthetics and ethics (refined beauty, politeness, peace) unfavorably with symbols of strength (tanned skin, sinews, military might). The poem explicitly advocates for militarization, urging people to discard peaceful ways, build armaments, wear military uniforms, and find glory in warfare and military technology. For historians, this 1931 poem is significant as it starkly reveals the growing influence of militaristic and potentially fascistic ideologies in colonial Korea. Coming from a prominent intellectual like Yi Kwangsu, it exemplifies the ideological currents of the time and perhaps foreshadows his later, more overt pro-Japanese collaborationist stance, reflecting a desperate or calculated turn towards perceived strength amid colonial subjugation.

힘의 찬미
						春園
힘!
오늘의 미는 힘에 잇다.
분바른 흰 것보다
볕에 걸은 검은 것,
비단결 같은 살결보다
쇠뭉치 같은 힘쭐!
얌전을 집어치어라,
위엄이다, 억세임이다!
사람아 오늘은 힘올 찾는다.

힘!
오늘의 의는 힘에 잇다.
세련 된 예절 보다
한다면 하는 믿어움성,
인사ㅅ성 잇는 겸양보다
제 것을 버티는 배씸!
유한 손을 빨리 들어라!
두 주먹을 불꾼 쥐어라!
사람아 오늘은 힘을 찾는다.

힘!
오늘의 영광은 힘에 잇다.
긔도 올리는 탑을 묺고
대포를 거는 포대를 쌓아라!
평화의 흰 옷은 다 무엇이냐,
병대의 붉은 복장을 입고
몸과 맘을 모다 무장하여라
사람아 오늘은 힘을 찾는다.

힘!
보병 포병의 접전이 잇다 할 때에
비행긔가 폭탄을 던지다 할 때에
군수품 수송하는 차를 볼 때에
최후통첩이 난다 난다 할 때에
우리 가슴은 말 못할 흥분으로 뛴다.
아아 총메고 칼 찬 끝없는 병대어!
사람아 오늘은 힘을 찾는다.＜1＞
