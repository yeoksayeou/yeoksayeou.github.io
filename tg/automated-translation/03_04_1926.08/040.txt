Truth
Falsehood is on the surface, and truth is deep within. (Goethe)

Falsehood is most successful when it uses truth as bait. (Colton)

Falsehood is cowardly. Truth is brave. (Ballou)

Falsehood is easy, but truth is difficult. (Eliot)

Truth in the heart is better than truth on the lips. (Proverb)

Failure is the school that tempers truth and makes it stronger. (Beecher)

Truth is strange. Stranger than fiction. (Byron)

Truth may weaken, but it never perishes. (Proverb)

Truth is the body of heaven, and light is its shadow. (Plato)

Truth shines brighter when it wears plain clothes. (Pope)

There is nothing stronger than truth, and nothing stranger than truth. (Webster)

The greatest truths are usually simple. (Malesherbes) <15>

**Summary**

This article is a compilation of aphorisms and proverbs centered on the nature of truth ("참") versus falsehood ("거즛"). It draws quotes from various Western thinkers (Goethe, Colton, Ballou, Eliot, Beecher, Byron, Plato, Pope, Webster, Malesherbes) and includes traditional proverbs. The recurring themes emphasize that truth is profound, brave, difficult, enduring, powerful, often simple, and ultimately superior to falsehood, which is superficial, cowardly, easy, and deceptive. For historians, this piece reflects the intellectual currents among Korean elites during the colonial period, particularly their engagement with Western philosophical ideas and universal concepts like 'truth'. The selection of quotes emphasizing truth's strength and endurance might subtly reflect a desire for underlying truths or national identity to persevere despite the difficult political reality of colonization.
