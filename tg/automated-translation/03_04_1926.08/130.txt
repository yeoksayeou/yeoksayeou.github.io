Article Title: 과학세계 (Scientific World)

Article Type: 소식 (News/Report)

Scientific World
						One Reporter

Is the Earth shrinking, or is science conquering nature and shortening the Earth's distances? This question is not without interest. However, it's not that the Earth is shrinking, but that science has developed to make it seem so. Previously, we spoke of trains, steamships, and telegrams in this context, but these have already fallen behind; now, this world is one where every minute and second counts in the competition for time.

Recently, an airship manufactured in England is scheduled to fly to India in 4 days. Major d'Oisy (도아지少佐) of France (佛國), who previously flew from Paris (巴里) via Japan (日本), is reportedly now negotiating with Germany (獨逸) and Russia (露西亞) for a plan to comfortably fly between Paris and Tokyo (東京) in 4 days. There is also news that a newspaper reporter from the United States (米國) is planning a round-the-world trip in 21 days.

The distance between Tokyo and Paris is about 20,000 *ri* (里) in Korean measure (朝鮮 里數). Since Major d'Oisy is said to plan landings at several locations en route—Moscow (莫斯科), Irkutsk (일크츠크), and Peking (北京)—the travel speed will be approximately 890 *ri* per hour.

A German (獨逸) airship that departed from "Bdense" (「쁘덴세」 - likely Bodensee/Lake Constance) on the border of Germany and Switzerland (瑞西) at 5:30 PM on October 12, 1924, arrived in New York (紐育) at 9:20 AM on the 15th of the same month. The elapsed time was 79 hours and 15 minutes. The distance between the two places is about 5,000 miles (哩), and this was the recent record for long-distance flight at that time.

Recently, there is the radio (라듸오), which allows people to communicate without any wires in the air and listen to things like music from the United States (米國) while lying down in their rooms. Furthermore, they are trying to send even photographs (사진아지) using this. Humankind's activities conquer nature, shrink the Earth, and aim to reduce it from a vast, immense existence (廣大無過) to a ball (一球 - likely typo for 지구, Earth) held in the palm (掌上).

In this arena where science is developing like this, isn't it pitiful that we (Koreans), in slang terms (속어), can't even present our calling card (名銜)? It's truly dumbfounding! <45>

**Summary**

This 1926 article marvels at contemporary advancements in science and technology, particularly in aviation (long-distance flights by airships and airplanes reducing travel times drastically between continents) and radio communication (wireless voice and potentially image transmission). The author uses examples like planned flights between Europe and Asia, a record transatlantic airship journey, and radio broadcasts to illustrate how technology is "shrinking the Earth." The article concludes with a lament, expressing a sense of dismay and backwardness that Koreans ("we") are not participants in this scientific progress, unable even to "present their calling card" in this rapidly modernizing world. For historians, the article reveals Korean awareness of global technological developments in the 1920s and a palpable sense of national frustration or inferiority due to perceived exclusion from this progress under colonial rule. It highlights the aspirations for modernity and the anxieties about lagging behind that were prevalent during this period.

과학세계
						一記者
지구가 축소하느냐 과학이 자연을 정복하어 지구의 거리를 가깝게 함이냐 하는 문제도 자미가 업지는 아니한 문제다. 그러나 지구가 축소됨이 아니라 과학이 발달되어 이러함이지. 전에는 기차 기선 전보로 이런 말을 하엿스나 이것들은 벌서 뒤떨어젓스며 지금에는 1분 1초로서로 시간 다툼을 하는 이 세상이다.
최근에 영국서 제조한 비행선은 印度를 4일예 날을 작정이고 巴里에서 日本을 경유하여 비행하여왓던 佛國의 도아지少佐가 편히 巴里 東京간을 4일에 비행할 계획으로 방금 獨逸과 露西亞에 교제중이라 하며 米國의 一新聞記者가 세계일주를 21일간에 계획한다는 報가 들린다.
東京과 巴里간은 朝鮮 里數로 약 2만여里인데 도아지少佐는 中路 莫斯科 일크츠크 北京의 *個所에서 착륙할 터이라 한즉 1시간에 行程은 약 8백 90里가 될 것이다.
지난 1924년 10월 12일 오후 5시 30분에 獨逸과 瑞西의 국경인 「쁘덴세」를 출발한 獨逸의 비행선이 同 15일 오전 9시 20분에 紐育에 도착하여 소요시간은 79시 西 15분이라 하며 兩地의 거리가 약 5천哩인데 이는 장거리비행의 최근기록이엇섯다.
최근에는 공중에 선 하나 업시 서로 말을 통하며 米國서 하는 음악가튼 것을 방중에서 누어 듯는 라듸오가 잇고 또 이로 사진아지 박으러고 하니 인류의 활동은 자연을 정복하며 지구를 축소하여 廣大無過한 존재로부터 掌上의 一九까지 이르자 한다.
이러케 과학이 발달되는 마당에 우리는 속어로 名銜도 못 들여노흐니 가련한 일이 아니냐 참 긔막힐일새!＜45＞
