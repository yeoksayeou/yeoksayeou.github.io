How Many Years is Cowpox Vaccination Effective?

Smallpox (天然痘) is a disease that originally came from India (印度), having first occurred there 2,000 years ago. That we do not fear this disease so much now is thanks to the grace of Dr. Jenner (젠나선생). However, receiving vaccination (種痘) once does not mean one is safe for life. The effectiveness of vaccination (種痘) varies somewhat depending on a person's constitution and the vaccination itself. On average, it is reliably effective for 2 or 3 years, but after 10 years, the effect disappears, so it is safe to do it once every 5 years. During a smallpox (天然痘) epidemic, even if one received it last year, one feels uneasy if not vaccinated again. For young children (아니), spring and autumn are the best times. <45>

**Summary**

This short article from 1926 informs readers about the effectiveness duration of the smallpox vaccine (cowpox vaccination, 우두). It states that while the vaccine, credited to Dr. Jenner, significantly reduced the threat of smallpox (which originated in India), its protection is not lifelong. The article advises that immunity lasts reliably for 2-3 years, wanes significantly after 10 years, and recommends revaccination every 5 years for safety, or even annually during outbreaks. It also suggests spring and autumn as optimal vaccination times for children. From a historical perspective, this piece highlights the dissemination of Western medical knowledge (vaccination) and public health guidance in colonial Korea during the 1920s. It reflects the contemporary understanding of vaccine efficacy and the efforts to manage infectious diseases through regular preventive measures.
