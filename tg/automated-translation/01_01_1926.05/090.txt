Footprints
						Yohan

Spring's arrival has entered the hometown house where my beloved is,
The sound of the wind from Gangnam pierces my heart at the guest window.
Seeing the familiar handwriting, I am both glad and sorrowful.

Though I haven't seen you, upon meeting, you feel familiar;
Though I haven't waited, you are the beloved I have long yearned for.
Has Heaven sent my beloved to me?

Having seen you coming and going, I cannot say all the words I wish to say.
My heart is full, yet there is no way to convey it.
Will we meet in the future when Heaven comes?

Though the road ahead is busy, can you not linger?
This body, bound by small matters, resents the time.
Though I endure, I cannot let go of the hand I once held.

News from across the sea makes your face seem vivid.
Though the day you left feels like yesterday, it's been over a year since we were separated.
Let us not forget the great thought, the shared purpose.

Spring sun is also late; the apricot tree behind the courtyard,
April, when we picked and ate them standing, is tomorrow.
Is my aged grandmother well?<19>

As the willows turn green, my spirit also wanders far.
It has already been ten years since spring passed at home.
Where are the friends I played with in childhood now?

The clouds floating in the east are dyed crimson,
As if flowing on the river, their faces reddened.
As the wind kicks up the waves, golden ripples arise.

On the willow branch that knows spring, laughter sings,
Through the clouds and willows, radiance stretches out.
As the evening sun sets, it flashes again.

Grass grows and the moon shines bright, I miss my beloved terribly.
Washing my hair in the wind, cleansing the grime with the sound of water,
Shall I try to cool my somewhat fevered breast?

When birds descend on the millet field and cicadas cry in the chestnut trees,
Pushing through the overgrown reeds, I go searching for my beloved's grave;
Only the tombstone sits covered in moss, green and green.

The hot sun after the long rain passes over the study room,
Coloring red the grass grown on the grave.
Why is the wind blowing through the pine grove so sad?

Since you left the egg behind, the remaining burden is heavier.
When I let out a deep sigh, even in the evening wind,
It seems the grave would speak if it had a mouth. (Above, Spring/Summer of '22)<20>

The world goes its way, and I go mine.
Living as I please, what is there to fear?
Life blooms and wilts, so what's there to fear?

Wait, wait, wait for the unknown beloved.
Since the waiting me doesn't know, who else would know, but
Regardless of knowing or not, I will wait until death.

Going mad under the moonlight, waiting for the beloved who won't come,
The cruel crescent moon has already set.
Oh, flame burning in my heart, light up this midnight.

As the clouds passed by and looked down upon the world,
How must the way people live have appeared?
They have covered the bright moonlight of the autumn night.

Coming and going on life's path, we met by chance,
But having met once, I can never forget again.
Why tell me to forget what I insist I cannot forget? (Above, draft from July 1920, corrected by Teacher C)

Though I tried to extinguish the fire igniting in my heart, I could not;
As two fires become one, that flame burns even stronger.
Oh, human, I shall light your altar with this flame. (Old work)<21>
<19-21>

**Summary**

This collection of poems by Yohan, titled "Footprints," explores themes of longing, separation, memory, and the passage of time. The speaker expresses deep yearning for a beloved person and their hometown, recalling past moments and reflecting on the changes brought by time and distance. Nature imagery (spring, willows, moon, setting sun, gravesite) is used extensively to mirror the speaker's emotions. The verses, noted as being written at different times (1920, 1922) and one edited by a "Teacher C," convey a sense of enduring waiting, unresolved sorrow, reflections on life's transient nature, and a final, intense dedication of passion. From a historical perspective, the poem is interesting for its use of early modern Korean orthography and its focus on personal, lyrical expression during the colonial period. While not overtly political, the themes of separation, longing, loss, and enduring hope could resonate with the broader national experience of the time, offering historians insight into personal sentiments and literary practices under colonial rule.

발자취
						요한
님 계신 고향 집에 봄ㅅ발이 드럿거니
강남의 바람소리 객창에 사모친다
낫 닉은 글시를 보오니 반갑고도 서러라

뵈온 적 업것마는 만나매 낫 닉으며
기둔 적 업것마는 오래 그린 님이오라
하눌이 나의 님을 보내신가

가다오다 뵈앗스매 하올 말슴 다 못하네
가슴에 가득컨만 젼할 길 업스오라
훗날에 하늘이 오면 맛나올가

가는 길 밥브지만 머물지는 못할건가
적은 일에 매인 몸이 시간을 원망한다
참아도 한번 잡은 손 노치 못해

바다 건너 오는 소식 그대 얼골 방불하다
떠난 날 어제 갓것만 막힌지 해가 넘엇네
큰 생각 가튼 한 뜻을 닛지 말기

봄해도 느젓고나, 뜰 뒤에 살구나무
선채로 따서 먹던 사월달이 래일이다
늙으신 한머님께선 탈 업슨가＜19＞

버들이 푸르르니 몸 역시 다 나간다
집에서 봄 지난지 어느덧 십년이라
어린 적 놀든 친구들 지금 어듸

동녁에 뜬 구늠은 주홍에 물드럿고
강 우에 흐르는 듯 얼골을 붉혓셔라
바람이 물결을 차니 금빗 놀이 닐더라

봄 아는 버들가지에 우슴이 노래하고
이랑 구름 버들 속으로 광채가 뻐치엇다
석양이 넘어가면셔 다시 번쩍

풀 자라고 달 밝으니 님 생각 무척 난다
바람에 머리 깜꼬 물 소리에 때를 씨서
적이나 더운 가슴을 식혀 볼가

조 바테 새 나리고 밤나무 맴이 울 제
욱어진 새리 헤치고 님 무덤을 차저 가니
비석만 익기 안저서 푸룻푸룻

장마 끗 더운 해가 서재를 넘어 가다
무덤에 자란 풀을 벌거케 드렷는데
솔 바테 부는 바람은 어이 그리 슬픈가

알 두고 가섯스니 남은 짐 더 무겁네
석양에 바람 마져 간 한숨 하올 적에
무덤이 입 잇섯더면 말슴할 듯(이상 22년 春夏)＜20＞

세상은 세상대로 나는 나대로
맘대로 사는 터에 무엇 무섭겟소
피는 듯 지는 인생이니 무서울 게 무에요

기두르오 기두르오 모를 님 기두르오
기두르는 나 모르니 누군던 알겟소만
그만두 알건 모르건 기두르다 죽게

달비체 미처나가 안올 님 기두를 제
야속한 초생달달이 어느덧 너머 갓소
가슴에 타는 불길아 이 밤중을 밝히렴

구름이 지나가다 세상을 구버 볼 제
사람들 사는 꼴이 엇더케나 뵈엇던지
가을 밤 밝은 달빗을 가리워 노핫소

일생길 가다오다 우연히 만낫건만
한번 만나보니 다시는 못 잇겟네
구테어 못 잇는다는 걸 이즈랄 건 무어요(이상 1920년 7월 습작, C선생의 첨삭한 것)

가슴에 붓는 불을 끌래도 못 껏더니
두 불이 하나 되니 그 불길 더욱 셰라
사람아 너의 제단을 이 불길로 밝히리(舊作)＜21＞
