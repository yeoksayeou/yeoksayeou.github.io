Characteristics of the Camel

When we say camel (yaktae), you probably all know it as the beast (chŭmsaeng) with two humps on its back found in the zoo. That camel is called the ship of the desert. Why is that? It is because the camel's characteristics are perfectly suited for traveling through the hot sandy deserts (moraebat).

Firstly, its feet are suitable for walking on sandy ground, its eyes and nose are suited for blocking the desert's harsh (mopsŭn) winds, and because it can store much (manhi) water in its large hump and stomach, it can live for a long time without drinking water, making it perfectly fit (matchi) for the waterless desert, doesn't it?

Not only that, but it is strong (manhasŏ), carries much luggage, and is said to be able to travel four hundred *ri* in a day (haro). Because it has grown up in the desert for a long time, when a harsh wind is about to blow, it lifts its head, sniffs ('k'ŭngk'ŭng'), and dashes off, allowing people to prepare for the wind in advance; it is also said to be good at finding (ch'annŭnda) water sources.<11>

The Camel and its Young<11>

**Summary**

This article describes the unique adaptations of the camel that make it suitable for desert life, earning it the name "ship of the desert." These features include feet designed for sand, eyes and nose protected against wind and sand, the ability to store water in its hump and stomach for long periods, great strength and endurance (reportedly covering 400 *ri* daily), and instincts to anticipate sandstorms and locate water.

From a historical perspective, this piece is interesting as an example of educational content disseminated in Korea during the early colonial period (1926). It introduces natural science and geography concepts (specifically about camels and deserts, likely exotic subjects for many Koreans then) in relatively simple language. The older Korean orthography (e.g., '즘생', '잇서서', '맛지', '하로에') is also characteristic of the time before widespread language standardization, providing linguistic data for historical Korean studies.

駱駝의 특성
약대라 하면 동물원에 잇는 등에 혹 두 개 달린 즘생인 줄 다 알겟지오. 그 약대를 사막 다니는 배라고 함니다. 엇재 그러느냐 하면 약대의 특성이 사막 모래밧 더운 데로 다니기에 꼭 합한 까닭임니다.
첫재 그 발이 모래터를 거러 가기에 적당하고 또 눈과 코가 사막의 몹슨 바람 막기에 합하며 그 커다란 혹과 위에는 물을 만히 저축하여 둘 수 잇서서 오래 동안 물을 마시지 안코도 살 수 잇스니 물 업는 사막에는 꼭 맛지 안습니까.
그뿐 아니라 힘이 만하서 짐을 만히 지며 하로에 사백리나 갈 수 잇담니다. 오래동안 사막에서 자라난 까닭에 몹슨 바람이 불려고 할 적에는 머리를 들고 코를 킁킁하며 뛰어 감으로 사람들이 미리 바람을 방비할 수 잇스며 또 물길을 잘 찻는다 함니다.＜11＞
