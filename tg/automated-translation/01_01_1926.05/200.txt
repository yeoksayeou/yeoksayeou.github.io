Song of the New Youth

Offering All

To save
Our pitiful compatriots,
Having sworn an oath and set our purpose,
O patriotic men and women!
For the nation's affairs,
Our lives are like mere weeds.
What is there to cherish?
Offer all.

Dust gathered makes a great mountain (T'aesan);
Whether little or much,
We just do our utmost sincerity.
Our success
Lies not in empty words
But in actual practice.
The power of truth and the sincerity of truth—
Offer all.<41>

Advance

O men and women of loyalty and righteousness, who have firmly resolved!
Annihilate the dark, demonic world,
And to open a bright new heaven and earth,
Let us advance and fight.

Advance, advance, let us all advance together!
Advance until the great victory is won.
Erect the triumphal arch conspicuously
And let the victory song resound loudly.

Wear the helmet of substance on your head,
Wrap the armor of forceful action around your body.
Block the front with the shield of loyalty and righteousness,
And draw the sword of courage.

Cold and hunger, what are you?
Hardship and persecution, what are you?
We, the valiant warriors fighting the great battle,
What shall we fear and hesitate for?

Arise, O brave multitude!
Let us advance, and advance, and advance again.
If we uphold what is right and go straight forward,
There will be nothing we cannot overcome.<42>
<41-42>

**Summary**

This two-part poem, "Song of the New Youth," is a fervent call to action for young Korean patriots. The first part, "Offering All," urges complete self-sacrifice, emphasizing sincerity and practical action over mere words for the sake of liberating their "pitiful compatriots." The second part, "Advance," uses militant imagery (helmets, armor, shields, swords) to exhort the youth to fight against the "dark, demonic world" (implicitly, colonial rule), overcome hardships like cold, hunger, and persecution without fear, and march forward relentlessly to achieve victory and build a "bright new heaven and earth." From a historical perspective, the poem vividly reflects the intense nationalism, spirit of resistance, and yearning for liberation among Korean youth during the Japanese colonial period in the 1920s. Historians would find the poem's emphasis on total commitment, collective action ("다함께 나가"), militant resolve, and the blend of traditional values (loyalty, righteousness) with a modernizing drive for a "new world" particularly revealing of the era's ideological currents.

비란과 핍박아 네가 무어냐

무엇을 두려 주저하랴

일어나자 용감한 무리들아
