Modern University Entrance Examination
1 point per question. 8 points or more: Honors. 5 points or more: Pass. Below 5 points: Fail.
Submissions Welcome = We welcome submissions from Readers. Please attach the answers as well.

1. How many independent nations are there in the whole world?
2. How many "Ŭp" (towns) are there in Chosŏn?
3. Which is heavier, kerosene for lighting or water?
4. Lausanne, where the Lausanne Conference is held, is a city in which country?
5. What does the word "Hegarinda" mean?
6. The names of the two great leaders ("big heads") of the Fascists.
7. What is the approximate budget amount for the Government-General of Chosŏn in recent years?
8. What is the title of the supreme leader of the Nationalist Government (國民政府)?
9. The names of three world-renowned Indians.
10. What changes did the Treaty of Magwan (馬關條約) bring about for Chosŏn?
-(Answers on page 36)-
(Questions 1, 2, and 3 among these problems were submitted by Toramsaeng of Chaeryŏng.) <5>

**Summary**

This article presents a ten-question "Modern University Entrance Examination" quiz published in the Korean magazine *Tonggwang* in 1932. The questions cover a range of topics including global geography and politics (independent nations, Lausanne Conference, Fascist leaders, Indian figures, Chinese government), colonial Korean specifics (number of towns, Government-General budget, impact of the Treaty of Shimonoseki), and basic science (density of kerosene vs. water). It sets grading criteria and invites reader submissions. For historians, this quiz offers insight into the general knowledge expected of educated Koreans during the colonial period, reflecting awareness of both international affairs and the realities of Japanese rule (e.g., questions about the colonial budget and the treaty that significantly impacted Korea's path towards colonization). The mix of global and local questions highlights the complex informational landscape accessible to readers at the time.
