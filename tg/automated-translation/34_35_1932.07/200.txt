**Body Text Translation:**
Letters from Prison by Yang Kŭnhwan (梁槿煥의 獄中書信)

It is rumored that he will be released from prison on August 11, 1933. I had the opportunity to personally meet Yang Kŭnhwan (梁槿煥), who assassinated Min Wŏnshik (閔元植) – a representative of the National Association (국민협회) who went to the Japanese Imperial Diet (日本帝國議會) to campaign for suffrage under the policy of extending domestic rule – at the Tokyo Station Hotel, bravely attempted to flee to Shanghai (上海) via Nagasaki (長崎), but was arrested by Japanese authorities (日本官憲) and received a life sentence (無期). The time was 2:00 PM on June 2nd, and the place was Kokwan (小管) Prison under Tokyo Prefecture (東京府下). Twenty minutes after requesting the visit, Yang Kŭnhwan, clad in blue prison clothes with a childlike face flushed like a peach blossom, came out and exchanged firm handshakes with the visitors Ch’oe Sŭngman (崔承萬), Hong Sŏn (洪宣), Kim Ŭngt’ae (金應泰), and myself. Yang was very eager to hear news from the outside world through us, and his desire to know about social conditions seemed particularly strong (濃厚하게 뵈엇다). The first thing he asked us was, "I heard a major incident occurred in Shanghai on April 29th. Has it been revealed who did it?" When told it was a man from Ch’ungch’ŏng Province (忠淸道) named Yun Ponggil (尹奉吉), he smiled faintly and said, "So there are people like that from the Samnam (三南) region too," while simultaneously showing an attitude of great pride as if originating from Haesŏ (海西). He continued, "I thought only people from Hamgyŏng (咸鏡), P'yŏngan (平安), or Hwanghae (黃海) provinces were like that," showing an expression as if he had gained insight into regional character (地方氣風). Next, he spoke of his concern about whether his mother would live until his release from prison, followed by worries about his daughter Chŏngja (貞子). Then, turning to us, he introduced his various writings (著書) and expressed concern, more than for himself, for three other Koreans currently in prison. He was much healthier than people in the outside world, and his attitude, filled with hope for the future, <62> left a great impression on us. Furthermore, he declared that he would definitely work for Korea (朝鮮) in the future and even encouraged us to awaken the despairing Korean youth. Quoting the philosopher Socrates' words, "Man is the masterpiece among God's creations," he earnestly requested that we devote much effort to helping the Korean youths Kim Pongnam (金福男) and Yi Sang’i (李相伊), who are suffering in prison like himself. Not only that, he made a very forceful request: if his autobiography (自敍傳), novel, poems (詩), and other writings could be published, the manuscripts have been sent to Mr. Song Chin’u (宋鎭禹) at the Kyŏngsŏng (京城) Dong-A Ilbo Company (東亞일보사), so please consult with him and help Kim Pongnam (金福男) (22), who is set to be released this December, by giving him even a hundred Won so that he does not wander (流浪) too much in the world and can take up a good enterprise. <63>

**Summary**

This 1932 news report by Chu T’aedo details a prison visit to Yang Kŭnhwan, the assassin of Min Wŏnshik, who was expected to be released the following year. Yang appeared healthy and hopeful, inquiring eagerly about outside news, particularly the Shanghai bombing by Yun Ponggil. He expressed regional pride, concern for his family, and deep solicitude for fellow Korean prisoners Kim Pongnam and Yi Sang’i. He also mentioned his own writings, hoping their publication could financially aid Kim Pongnam upon his release, and reaffirmed his commitment to working for Korea. For historians, the article provides a valuable glimpse into the mindset of an imprisoned Korean independence activist during the colonial period, revealing his continued nationalist fervor, awareness of ongoing resistance (like Yun Ponggil's act), concern for comrades, and practical plans for the future, including leveraging his writings. His comments on regional characteristics associated with resistance are also noteworthy.

明年 八月에 出獄할 梁槿煥 訪問記
						東京 朱泰道
梁槿煥의 獄中書信

1933년 8월 11일에 出獄하게 되엇다고 소문에 전하는, 국민협회대표로 日本帝國議會에 內地廷長主義에 叅政權운동을 갓든 閔元植을 東京스테슌 호텔에서 암살하고 용감히도 長崎를 經由하야 上海로 다러나려다가 日本官憲에게 체포되어 無期의 刑을 받게 되엇든 梁槿煥을 親히 면회하게 되엇다. 때는 6월 2일 오후2시 장소는 東京府下小管 형무소인데 면회를 請한지 20분만에 혈색이 紅桃같은 童顔을 가지고 靑服을 입고 나온 梁槿煥은 찾아간 崔承萬, 洪宣, 金應泰, 諸氏와 같이 내게도 굳은 악수를 교환하엿다. 梁은 外部의 소식을 우리를 통해서 대단히 듣기를 원햇으며 더욱이 사회사정에 대해서 매우 알고저 애쓰는 形色이 퍽 濃厚하게 뵈엇다. 우리들에게 그는 第一 먼저 묻는 말이 4월 29일 上海에서 大사건이 일어낫다는데 누구가 그랫는지 알게 되엇느냐하는 말이엇다. 그는 忠淸道人 尹奉吉이라는 사람이라고 하니까 빙긋이 웃으면서 三南사람도 그런 사람이 잇어하며 한편으로 대단히 海西출신이라고 자랑하는 듯한 태도가 뵈이엇다. 그러면서 나는 咸鏡이나 平安이나 黃海사람만이 그런 줄 알엇더니 하면서 지방氣風에 대해서 깨달은 듯한 표정을 뵈웟다. 다음에 하는 말이 自己母親이 내가 出獄할 때까지 생존해 게실 수가 잇을까하는 말과 다음에 自己딸 貞子에 대한 걱정이엇다. 그리고 우리들을 향해서 自己著書에 대해서 여러 가지로 소개를 하며 현재 獄中에 잇는 朝鮮사람 세사람에 대해서 自己일 以上으로 걱정을 하고 잇엇다. 그는 세상에 잇는 사람들 보다 훨신 건강햇으며 미래의 희망을 품고잇는＜62＞ 태도은 우리로 하여금 큰 느낌을 가지게 하엿다.  그리고 自己가 장래에도 반드시 朝鮮을 위하야 일을 하겟노라고하며 실망하는 朝鮮청년을 깨우치라고 우리들을 향하야 격려까지 해주고 잇엇다. 철학자 쏘크라데스의 「사람은 하나님의 만드신 걸작중의 걸작이라」는 말을 하며 自己와 같이 獄中에서 고생하는 朝鮮청년 金福男
李相伊를 위하야 많은 힘을 나누어 주라고 간절한 부탁을 하고 잇엇다. 그뿐아니라 현재 自己가 쓴 자서전 소설, 詩 其他 등을 출판할 수 잇으면 현재 京城 東亞일보사 宋鎭禹氏에게 원고를 보내두엇으니 의논해서 今年12월에 出獄키로 결정한 金福男(二二)에게 한 백원이라도 주어서 세상에서 너무 流浪치 않고 좋은 사업을 붙들도록 도아주게 힘써달라고 하면서 대단히 힘잇는 부탁을 하고 잇엇다.＜63＞
