Baby Born of May
						Yi Taeyong

Baby born of May
Baby born of May, the lovely sunlight
Grows among the willow leaves.
Twinkling its many eyes,
It grows among the willow leaves—.

Baby born of May, the lovely wind
Dances in the ripening barley field.
Rippling like the ocean waves,
It dances in the ripening barley field—.

Baby born of May, the young calf
Sleeps upon a bed of green grass.
Dreaming joyful and beautiful dreams,
It sleeps upon a bed of green grass—.

Baby born of May, our baby
Rejoices in mother's embrace.
With plump cheeks full of smiles,
Rejoices in mother's embrace.
―A song given to my beloved daughter Hŭita―

Deep Thoughts

Philosophy is like the seabed
The secret within the waves that leap day and night
An enlightened state of mind (心境) that has forgotten all greed (利慾)
The truth of quiet stillness, unmoved by surface ripples (波紋)
Philosophy is like the seabed—. <94>

Art is like the wilderness (曠野)
The fragrance (香趣) of the earth changing with the seasons
The brilliance (光彩) of the wind shining with the seasons
A treasury of life inexhaustible even when pioneered
Art is like the wilderness—.

Religion is like the blue sky (蒼空)
Infinite activity within quiet silence
The unchanging suggestion (示唆) of strength and love
The eternal, imperishable symbol of salvation (救援)
Religion is like the blue sky—.

Paths appearing in the mind's eye (心眼) on a quiet night
Too many diverging branches, where should I go?
This heart wanders, unable to decide (的定)
The burden placed on a short life is too heavy
I fear repeating the fruitless days gone by—. <95>

**Summary**

This article presents two poems by Yi Taeyong. "Baby Born of May" is a lyrical celebration of spring's vitality, drawing parallels between the new life in nature (sunlight, wind, a calf) and the poet's infant daughter, Hŭita. "Deep Thoughts" offers philosophical musings, comparing philosophy, art, and religion to the seabed, wilderness, and sky, respectively, before concluding with a personal expression of existential doubt and the fear of an unfulfilled life. From a historical perspective, these poems are interesting as examples of personal and philosophical lyricism published during the Japanese colonial period. While not overtly political, the first poem's gentle focus on nature and family could be seen as a quiet assertion of enduring values, while the second poem's anxieties about life's path might subtly reflect the constraints and uncertainties faced by Korean intellectuals under colonial rule. The daughter's name (喜多), potentially read as the Japanese 'Kita', also reflects the era's cultural blending or imposition.

五月이 낳은 아기
						李大容
五月이 낳은아기
五月이 낳은 아기 고운 해볓은
버드나무 잎새에서 자라납니다.
그 많은 눈동자를 반작이면서
버드나무 잎새에서 자라납니다―.

五月이 낳은 아기 고운 바람은
익어가는 보리밭에 춤을 춥니다.
바다의 물결처럼 넘실거리며
익어가는 보리밭에 춤을 춥니다―.

五月이 낳은 아기 어린 송아지
푸른 잔디 침상에서 잠을 잡니다.
즐겁고 아름다운 꿈을 꾸면서
푸른 잔디 침상에서 잠을 잡니다―.

五月이 낳은 아기 우리 아기는
엄마의 품안에서 기뻐합니다
복스러운 두볼가득 웃음띄우고
엄마의 품안에서 기뻐합니다.
―愛女 喜多에게 주는 노래―

沈想
철학은 海底와 같다
밤낮을 날뛰는 물결속의 비밀
모든 利慾을 잊어버린 깨다른 心境
표면의 波紋에 動치 않는 靜潛의 眞理
철학은 海底와 같다―.＜94＞
예술은 曠野와 같다
때를 따라 변하는 흙의 香趣
때를 따라 빛나는 바람의 光彩
개척하여도 다하지 않는 생명의 보고
예술은 曠野와 같다

종교는 蒼空과 같다
고요한 침묵속에 無窮한 활동
힘과 사랑의 변치않는 示唆
悠久히 滅치않는 救援의 상징
종교는 蒼空과 같다―.

고요한 밤 心眼에 떠오르는 길

的定못하고 헤매이는 이 마음
짜른 인생에 지운 짐 넘우 많으니
일욱없든 지낸 날을 거듭할까 두렵다―. ＜95＞
