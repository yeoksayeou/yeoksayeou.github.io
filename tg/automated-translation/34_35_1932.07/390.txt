Magazine Title: 東光 第35號
Publication Date: 1932년 07월 03일
Article Title: 매듭진 마음 (A Knotted Heart)
Author: 三峯
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

On the unseen thread-end of my heart,
I tried to tie the passing spring breeze.
The wind slipped away, and only a knot remained,
A knot that won't untie, no matter how I try.

In a corner of my heart, a clear, fresh spring wells up.
In that spring water, countless stars (星) glitter and shine.
The moon, round like a tray, reflects upon it.<95>
But the twinkling starlight is still too scarce,
The pale moonlight is still too cold.
Yearning, yearning, I yearn for the warm sunlight, I yearn for the ardently burning sun. <96>

**Summary**

This poem by "Sambong" (三峯) uses metaphors of a persistent knot and unsatisfying celestial light to express feelings of inner entanglement and unfulfilled longing. The speaker ties a knot in their heart trying to hold onto something fleeting (spring breeze), leaving an irresolvable tangle. A clear spring reflects stars and the moon, but their light is insufficient and cold, leading to a desperate yearning for the warmth and intensity of the sun. From a colonial history perspective, the poem's themes of unresolved internal knots and the longing for warmth and bright light (the sun) over cold, distant light (moon, stars) could be interpreted as a subtle expression of the speaker's frustration with their current state and a yearning for liberation, hope, or a more vibrant existence under oppressive circumstances. The contrast between the desired "warm sunlight" and the insufficient "pale moonlight" is particularly evocative.

매듭진 마음
						三峯
뵈지도 않는 내마음의 실마리에
지나가는 봄바람을 붙들어 매엇드니
바람은 빠저가고 매듭만 맺혓고나
풀어도 풀어도 풀리지않는 그 매듭

내마음 한구석에 새맑안 샘물이 솟아나네
그 샘물에 뭇별(星)들이 빛의어서 반짝이네
쟁반같이 둥근 달이 비쳐주네＜95＞
그러나 반짝이는 별빛, 넘어도 적어
헤쓱한 달빛 넘어도 차거워,
그리워 그리워 따뜻한 해빛이 그리워 이질이질 타는 해가 그리워. ＜96＞
