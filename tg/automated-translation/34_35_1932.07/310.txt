Publication Date: 1932년 07월 03일
Article Title: 荒海를 거느는 사공아 (Oh Boatman Crossing the Rough Sea)
Author: 黃順元
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Oh Boatman Crossing the Rough Sea
						Hwang Sunwŏn
Ink-black clouds fly wildly through the air <91>
Lightning and thunder, cursing the universe, shake heaven and earth.
Now a fierce storm will rush in
And the billows lined up on the vast expanse of waves will rage.
Oh boatman, boatman holding the tiller
Will you go forward even on this sea without a lighthouse
Or will you turn the bow, chased away?
Deep in the night, the heart of the boatman gazes only at the compass that has lost its direction.

The traces of tears shed by ancestors,
Befriending the reed forest nodding in the gaunt wind,
The wounded old place where they flowed, flowed down,
Comes before the eyes of the young boatman crossing the rough sea.
Will you too, following the high-dancing water spray, shed worthless tears, shed them?

No, no. From between the boatman's teeth, clenched until they could break,
Hot breath, as if melting metal and stone, leaks out,
And in the eyes looking up at the ash-colored sky,
The burning light of suggestion (暗示) has been found.
The solemn face is darkened, brushed by the typhoon (颱風),
And the outstretched forearm, tormented by the raging waves, has hardened like an iron plate.
What now could be fearful or hindering to him?
It has been long since even the tears of defeat (敗北) dried on his cheeks.

If so, young boatman, trustworthy worker,
Turn your head quickly and look.
The sea that has already caught and taken away numerous comrades (同志), and will take more,
The sea where fierce sea creatures wait with mouths wide open
For the boatman's strength to fail,
Is it not splashingly raising the great earth (大地) to mock you?
Oh boatman crossing the rough sea, hot-blooded youth,
Quickly, swiftly prepare to fight the wind and waves (風波).
Lower the sail and weigh the anchor. Look only ahead again and grasp the tiller firmly.
And let us advance. Piercing through these raging waves (怒濤), this fierce wind (狂風), forward, forward—.
- 1932. 6 - <92>

**Summary**

This poem by Hwang Sunwŏn depicts a young boatman navigating a treacherous sea during a violent storm. Urged not to succumb to despair or the sorrows of the past, the boatman finds inner strength and determination. The poem calls for courage and resolute action against overwhelming adversity, symbolized by the storm and the waiting "sea creatures." The boatman represents the youth or the people facing hardship, exhorted to prepare actively and push forward despite the dangers. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, likely serves as an allegory for the Korean situation. The "rough sea," the "storm," and the "sea creatures" can be interpreted as the oppressive colonial rule and its dangers, while the boatman embodies the resilience and fighting spirit of the Korean people, urged towards national consciousness and resistance ("prepare to fight," "advance... forward, forward"). The mention of "comrades" taken by the sea reinforces this potential political undertone.

그대도 높이 춤추는 물굼이를 따라 값없는 눈물을 뿌리려는가 뿌리려는가―.

아니다 아니다. 앗아지도록 악문 사공의 이ㅅ사이로는
金石을 녹힐듯한 뜨거운 입김이 새어 나오며
재빛 하눌을 치어다 보는 눈동자에서는
