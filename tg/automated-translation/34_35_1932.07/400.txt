Children's Poem Collection
						Yi Hou
Transcriber's Foreword = Master Yi Hou is a child who turned four this year. The several poems presented here are words that occasionally slipped out eccentrically from Master Yi's mouth, which his mother carefully noted down. I cannot know if these can be called poems, but feeling great interest in the pure expression of his childlike innocence and his genius descriptions, I am transcribing and publishing them. Transcriber Yi Sŏkhun notes.

Mother's Thoughts
Mother
Lying down alone
What are you thinking about?
Huh?
Thinking pretty thoughts?
Thinking delicious thoughts?
Huh? Mother

Travel
I tell you, trains are delicious
Rickshaws are delicious too
Cars are delicious too
And carriages are delicious
They're all delicious
I always want to ride them

(Note)= About a month ago, Master Yi traveled with his mother to the Sinŭiju and Andong County areas. At that time, he rode trains, carriages, rickshaws, and cars. Master Yi likes such things quite a bit.

Soyang River
Yesterday, on the holiday
With Uncle
We went to see the Soyang River
There was so much water
That's all my pee
The water, like my suit,
Is prettily blue, I tell you
Salt boats (Uncle called them salt boats)
Were sitting on the river, many of them
Inside the boats were pots for cooking rice
And gourds too...
I like sitting on Mother's lap

(Note)= The Soyang River is a beautiful river in Ch'unch'ŏn.

I Hate Flies
(While pushing away a fly that couldn't fly properly and was just crawling clumsily after being swatted imperfectly with a fly swatter...)
Eek! You little brat! (i nom saekki)
Get out of here! I hate looking at you!
Go outside!
You little brat stopping me from sleeping,
Making me annoyed (songhwa sikinŭn)!
I really hate you!

(Note)= "i nom saekki" is the P'yŏngan Province dialect for "ija식" (this brat/bastard). Although Master Yi lives in Ch'unch'ŏn, because his parents are from P'yŏngan Province, he mixes the dialects of each region. "songhwa" is also P'yŏngan Province dialect. <96>

**Summary**

This article presents a collection of "poems" by a four-year-old boy named Yi Hou, transcribed by his mother and submitted for publication by Yi Sŏkhun. The poems reflect the child's simple observations and feelings about his mother, travel experiences (riding trains, rickshaws, cars, carriages), a visit to the Soyang River, and his annoyance with a fly. The foreword emphasizes the innocence and perceived "genius" in the child's spontaneous utterances. For historians, the article offers insights into early 20th-century Korean perspectives on childhood creativity, parenting practices (mothers documenting children's words), internal travel within colonial Korea (Sinŭiju, Andong, Ch'unch'ŏn), and the linguistic environment, showing the mixing of regional dialects (P'yŏngan dialect) even in a child living elsewhere. The use of "delicious" to describe vehicles is a particularly charming example of childish expression.

代筆者附言=李虎羽君은 今年 네 살되는 어린아이다. 여기 발표하는 수편의 詩는 李君의 입으로 잇다금 엉뚱스레 새여나온 말을 그 어머니가 注意해서 적어 두엇든 것이다. 이것을 詩라고 할 수 잇는지는 알 수 없으나 순진스러운 그 童心의 流露에 그 천재적인 形容에 크게 흥미를 느끼엇음으로 내가 代筆하야 發表하는 것이다. 代筆者 李石薰記

어머니의 생각
어머니
혼자 눠서
무슨 생각해
응?
이쁜 생각해
밋잇는 생각해
응? 어머니

여행
난 기차가 맛잇단 말야
人力車두 맛잇어
자동차두 맛잇구
마차두 맛잇어
다 맛잇어
늘 타구 싶어

(註)=李君은 月餘前에, 自己어머니와 같이 新義州, 安東縣 방면을 여행하엿다. 그때 기차, 마차, 인력차, 자동차 등을 타고 댕긴 것이다. 李君은 그런 것들은 여간만 좋아하지 않는 것이다.

昭陽江
어적게 공일날
삼춘하구
昭陽江 구경을 햇다우
물이 흠뻑 많아
그것 다 내 오즘이야
물이 내 양복처럼
파라케 이쁘단 말야
소곰배(삼촌이 소곰배라구 그래)가
많이 강에 앉아 잇어
배안엔 밥하는 솥하구
바가지두 잇드라...
난 어머니 무릎에 앉는게 좋아

(註)=昭陽江은 春川에 잇는 아름다운 江이다.

파리가 미워
(파리채로 빗맞아서 날지는 못하고 엉기엉기 기는 파리를 밀어 내면서... ...)
엑! 이 놈새끼
저리나가! 보기싫어
밖으루 나가라우!
남, 잠못자게
송화시키는 놈새끼
막 밉단 말야

(註)=「이놈새끼」는 「이자식」의 平安道 方言이다. 李君은 春川서 살지만 自己부모가 平安道人인 까닭으로, 各其 지방의 方言을 섞어서 쓰는 것이다. 「송화」도 平安道 方言이다. ＜96＞
