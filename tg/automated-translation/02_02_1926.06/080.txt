Magazine Title: 동광 제2호 (東光 第2號)
Publication Date: 1926년 06월 01일
Article Title: 최대의 비행기 (The Largest Airplane)
Author: None specified
Article Type: 잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)

The world's largest airplanes are in Britain and the United States; under the wings of Britain's Cavalier-type airplane, about 20 people can comfortably shelter from the rain.<17>

**Summary**

This short article notes that Britain and the United States possess the world's largest airplanes. It specifically mentions a British "Cavalier-type" airplane, highlighting its immense size by stating that around 20 people can stand beneath its wings to avoid the rain. From a historical perspective, this piece reflects the interest in and awareness of modern technological advancements, like aviation, within colonial Korea. It shows that information about global developments, particularly concerning Western powers like Britain and the US, was reaching the Korean readership, feeding a fascination with modernity during a period of limited national sovereignty.

최대의 비행기
세계 최대 비행기는 영국과 미국에 잇는데 영국의 캐발로형의 비행기는 20여명의 사람이 날개 밋헤서 비를 넉넉히 것는다.＜17＞
