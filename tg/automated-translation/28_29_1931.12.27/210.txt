**Body Text Translation:**
The 10 Great Athletes Born of Chosŏn, Sokol Column
Nominated by Various Gentlemen

A Strong Spirit Comes from a Strong Body
Against the contemporary trends of luxury and decadence, the youth of Chosŏn must now cultivate a spirit that is strong, sturdy, substantial, bold, and courageous. A strong spirit comes from a strong body; the worker's body must first be robust. It is under this spirit that we establish this Sokol column. The name "Sokol" is borrowed briefly from the name of the national physical exercise movement of the newly risen European nation of Czechoslovakia. We hope to contribute, at least through these pages, to this movement which cultivates the habit of health, trains a strong body, and fosters a bold spirit, and we hope that, stimulated by this, the actual movement itself will also greatly flourish.

For the promotion of physical education and health, this magazine has newly established the Sokol column. As the first step, we have already announced several times that we will select the 10 great athletes born of Chosŏn through a vote by all our readers across the land. Now, to assist with that vote, we requested various gentlemen who are authorities in the world of sports to select "the 10 most outstanding individuals, male or female, among the athletes recently born of Chosŏn," and we introduce their selections below exactly as received, thereby publicly recommending them.

**Summary**

This 1931 article from the magazine *Tonggwang* announces the creation of a "Sokol Column," named after the Czech national fitness movement, dedicated to promoting physical health and a strong spirit among Korean youth. As its first initiative, the magazine is conducting a reader poll to select the "10 Great Athletes Born of Chosŏn." To guide the readers, the article presents nominations for the top 10 athletes (male and female) solicited from 14 sports authorities, listing the combined tally and each authority's individual ballot with the athletes' respective sports. For historians, the article is significant as it reflects the adoption of Western physical culture movements (like Sokol) for nationalistic purposes ("strong spirit from a strong body") within the constraints of Japanese colonial rule. It also provides a snapshot of popular sports (baseball, track/marathon, tennis, boxing, soccer) and celebrates specific Korean athletes like Yi Yŏngmin, Kim Ŭnbae, and Kim Pongnim as national figures during this period.
