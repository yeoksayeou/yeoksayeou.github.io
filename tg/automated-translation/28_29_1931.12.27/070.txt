The Fundamental Meaning of the State and the Freedom of the Populace
						By Kim Pyŏngno

What is the comprehensive foundation for the establishment and existence of the state? Is it religion? Is it power? Is it justice? These merely represent parts of the fundamental facts concerning the existence of the state and cannot fully elucidate the entire fundamental concept. Therefore, to unify the whole truth and explain concretely the fact of its establishment and existence in any state system, one must inevitably speak of the free activity of the populace.

Since all social phenomena exist based on the psychology of the individual, the foundational concepts regarding the establishment, existence, and development of the state cannot be investigated apart from the individual. Then, what is the essence of the individual? When discussing the fundamental concept, one must inevitably say it is the subject of free activity. Any theory contrary to this cannot be recognized as having any value.

So-called free activity means freely displaying the self's capabilities based on self-awareness, thereby producing some result in the external world. This freedom of activity, without being hindered by natural laws of causality or theories of human destiny, utilizes natural phenomena to freely arouse the volitional power for activity. This is precisely why the essence of humankind must be called the free activity of the self.

The existence of this free activity is neither imagination nor fantasy; it is the primary, certain, and immovable fact that becomes the source of human life, which no one can deny. Therefore, although humans cannot help but harbor doubts about any reality or knowledge in social life, the fundamental fact of free activity permits no doubt for anyone. As such, since the self-awareness of humankind and its activation cannot be denied or doubted, this becomes the starting point for research in the political sense and the social sense, and at the same time, it determines the final reason for the establishment, existence, and development of the state.

The result of each individual recognizing the existence of free will based on self-awareness and activating it is free activity. What triggers this activity is not social reality or natural causality, but purely the freedom of the self. The sense of freedom based on this gives rise to the concept of responsibility towards others, but needless to say, this too is not subject to any external control but is purely a result of the self's freedom. Because the self is free, a sense of responsibility arises; because there is a sense of responsibility, responsible actions arise; and by affirming that responsibility, one stimulates free activity in society. Therefore, even if humans assume themselves to be the subjects of free activity, if the accompanying sense of responsibility is absent and one consequently hesitates to face that responsibility, the self's free activity will naturally be obstructed. In short, the development of freedom activates the individual self's sense of responsibility, and the development of the sense of responsibility strengthens the power of free activity. Regardless of the nation or society, if humankind's free activity is discouraged and consequently the individual self's sense of responsibility becomes non-existent, there is no way to investigate the meaning of the state's establishment, existence, and development apart from this. <21>

**Summary**

This article by Kim Pyŏngno argues that the true foundation of any state lies not in religion, power, or abstract justice, but in the "free activity" of its people (the populace). This free activity stems from individual self-awareness and free will, which are undeniable facts of human existence. Crucially, this freedom is inherently linked to a sense of responsibility towards others. The development of freedom fosters responsibility, and a strong sense of responsibility, in turn, empowers free activity. Without this dynamic of individual freedom and responsibility, the state loses its fundamental meaning and basis for existence and development. From a historical perspective, this article is significant as it was written during Japanese colonial rule. Kim Pyŏngno's emphasis on the "populace's freedom" as the core of the state, penned by a future key figure in post-liberation Korea, can be seen as a subtle assertion of inherent rights and the basis for self-governance, even when such ideas were suppressed. Historians may find it interesting for its use of philosophical discourse to champion liberal ideals related to individual agency and the legitimacy of the state under an authoritarian regime.

國家의 根本義와 民衆의 自由
						金炳魯
國家의 成立 存在의 總括的 基礎는 무엇일가요.  宗敎이겟습니까?  權力이겟습니까?  正義이겟습니까?  이것이 모다 國家存立에 關한 根本的 事實의 一部를 表徵함에 不過한 것이요 全的 根本觀念을 闡明할 수 없습니다.  그러므로 全的 眞理를 統一하야 어떠한 國家制度에 잇어서도 그 成立과 存在의 事實을 具象的으로 說明하랴면 民衆의 自由活動이라고 할 수밖에 없습니다.
무릇 社會現象은 個人의 心理에 基因하야 存在하는 것이므로 國家의 成立 存在 發達에 關한 基礎觀念이 모다 個人을 떠나서 究明할 수 없는 것입니다.  그러면 個人의 본질은 무엇일가요.  그 根本的 觀念을 論함에는 自由活動의 主體라고 말할 수밖에 없습니다.  이에 反한 如何한 理論이 잇더라도 何等의 價値를 認할수 없습니다.
所謂 自由活動이라 함은 自我의 自覺에 基因하야 自由스럽게 自我의 力量을 發揮하야 外界에 어떤 結果를 生케 함이니 이 活動의 自由는 自然의 인과법칙이나 人類의 定命理論에 拘碍됨이 없이 自然現象을 利用하야 自由로 活動의 意力을 惹起하는 것이니 이것이 곳 人類의 本質은 自我의 自由活動이라고 할 수밖에 없는 所以외다.
이 自由活動의 存在는 想像도 아니요 空想도 아니요 確實不動한 人類生活의 根源이 되는 第一段의 事實로서 누구던지 否定할수 없습니다.  그러므로 人類는 社會生活에 잇어서 어떠한 現實에나 어떠한 智識에 對하여서도 疑念을 아니 가질 수 없으나 自由活動의 基本事實은 그 누구에게나 疑念을 許치 아니한 배입니다.  이와 같이 人類의 自我인 自覺과 및 그 發動은 이것을 否定 又는 懷疑함을 不得함으로 政治의 意義에 잇어서나 社會의 意義에 잇어서나 國家가 그 硏究의 始點이 되는 同時에 이것이 國家의 成立, 存在, 發達에 最終理由를 決定하는 것이 되겟습니다.
個個의 人類가 自我의 自覺에 依하야 自由意思의 存在를 認하면서 그것을 發動케하는 結果가 自由活動이니 이 活動을 惹起하는 것은 社會의 現實性이나 自然의 因果律이 아니요. 純然한 自我의 自由입니다.  이에 基因한 自我의 自由스러운 感覺이 他我에 對한 責任觀念을 生케하는 것이나 勿論 이것도 何等外界의 支配를 受함이 아니요 自我의 自由의 結果뿐입니다.  自我가 自由로우므로 責任感이 生하고 責任感이 有하므로 責任事實이 生하고 그 責任을 肯定하므로 社會에 잇어서 自由스러운 活動을 振作하게 되는 것입니다.  그럼으로 人類가 自由活動의 主體됨을 自任할지라도 이에 伴한 責任感이 없고 따라서 그 責任에 當하기를 躊躇하면 自我의 自由活動도 自然히 阻止될 수밖에 없습니다.  要컨대 自由의 發達은 個我의 責任感을 發動케 하고 責任感의 發達은 自由의 活動力을 强大케 하는 所以외다.  어떠한 民族 어떠한 社會를 勿論하고 人類의 自由活動이 沮喪되고 따라서 個我의 責任感動이 無함에 至할진대 이것을 떠나서 國家의 成立, 存在 및 發達의 意義를 究明할 바이 없는 것입니다. ＜21＞
