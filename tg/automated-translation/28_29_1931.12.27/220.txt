First, Health, Starting with a Worker's Strong Body, My Health Method
						By Various People

Whatever work we do, health is absolutely necessary. Only with a healthy body and a sound mind can we tackle any task vigorously. However, health cannot be obtained in a single day; needless to say, daily, unceasing effort and discipline are required. As we greet the New Year, it is a most pleasant thing to be able to publicly share the unique health methods of various figures from all walks of life. A worker's body must first be strong. Let us resolve, with the New Year, to hold the greatest concern and pay meticulous attention to making our bodies strong.
(No particular order)

Peace of Mind and Body   Kim Yŏ-sik, Principal of Hyŏpsŏng Industrial School

1.  Meals... I have a fixed quantity for three meals daily (morning, noon, evening) and am most careful not to overeat. For about 20 years now, I have continued to practice abstinence from alcohol and tobacco, true to the name. I avoid, as much as possible, foods with strong stimulants like strong coffee, leaf tea, and hot peppers.
2.  Conduct... I adhere to the monogamous system established through an old-style, early marriage. Enduring the ridicule of being a 'foolish husband,' I still maintain so-called "male chastity" and live a life of sacrificial moderation.
3.  Rest... I make it a habit to sleep for about 8 hours at night.
4.  Exercise... Whenever I have the chance, I breathe fresh air and make an effort to walk for over 30 minutes daily.
5.  Mind and Body... My innate constitution is somewhat weak, but for the past 20 years or so, <83> I haven't felt any particular suffering from illness. However, in the last 3-4 years, unavoidable anxieties and anguish concerning public and private matters have constantly tormented my mind and body, and my health has already suffered damage. Therefore, I have further realized that the conditions for health require not only physical self-discipline but also obtaining peace of mind.

Sleep When I Want To Sleep   Yi Il, Lawyer

I was born with a naturally healthy constitution, so I don't get sick even if I push myself unreasonably hard. However, I have not tried reckless experiments to deliberately make myself ill. If I were to state my health method:
1.  Sleep when I want to sleep,
2.  Eat when I want to eat, forget worries, and strive to live like wood and stone, avoiding strong emotions as much as possible.

Exercise for Over 5 Minutes   Yi Kwang-su, Tong-A Ilbo Company

I (生) don't really have anything that could be called a health method. If I were to pick out something resembling a health method from my life after illness, it would be:
1.  Sleeping more than 9 hours.
2.  Not going out at night.
3.  Eating meals regularly at home.
4.  Quitting alcohol and tobacco.
5.  Doing exercises for more than 5 minutes daily.

Keep the Mind Cheerful   Kim T'ak-wŏn, Director of Hansŏng Clinic

1.  Keeping the mind always cheerful without strain.
2.  Taking an interest in one's work.
3.  Getting sufficient sleep.
4.  Occasionally taking walks in the suburbs.

Not Worrying About Trifles   Sŏ Ŭn-suk, Ewha College

1.  Paying special attention to clothing, food, and housing as the seasons change.
2.  Avoiding skipping meals as much as possible.
3.  Lying down to rest when even slight fatigue is felt, except for unavoidable tasks.
4.  Avoiding trivial worries, or trying not to dwell on them.
5.  Trying to seek new things and not remember unsettling things from the past.
6.  Seeking pleasant things.
7.  Consulting a doctor when symptoms of illness seem present.
8.  Trying to find opportunities for frequent contact with nature.

Conserving Energy   An Chae-hong, Chosŏn Ilbo Company

1.  I maintain a set routine with the principle of conserving and concentrating energy. My principles are no smoking, <84> no drinking, and no miscellaneous vices, and I have lived like a single man for a long time.
2.  When I have leisure time, I take pleasure in mountain climbing and walking to refresh my spirits. In Keijō (Seoul), I have climbed mountains like Inwang, Paegak, Kŭmhwa, and Chongnam many times each, and I have also climbed Kwanak and Samgak mountains.
3.  I savor transcendental writings such as those of Jesus, Buddha, Laozi, and Zhuangzi, making sure to resolve mental obstructions as much as possible.

Wholesome Recreation   Pak Wŏn-gyu, Coach at Sinsŏng Middle School

1.  Living a regular life.
2.  Keeping one's residence clean and ensuring good air circulation in the bedroom.
3.  Not being lazy about daily outdoor exercise.
4.  Paying attention to food selection and eating with enjoyment.
5.  Not using stimulants (especially alcohol, tobacco).
6.  Resting for 15 minutes after meals.
7.  Engaging in wholesome recreation.
8.  Always keeping a cheerful mind.
9.  Discarding things harmful to the body and adopting beneficial ones.

Appropriate Exercise   Kim Sin-sil, Ewha College

1.  Getting a health check-up from a doctor once a year.
2.  Having a fixed daily sleep schedule.
3.  Paying attention to food nutrients.
4.  Getting appropriate exercise daily.
5.  Paying attention to clothing according to the season.

Petty Worries and Sexual Excess are Forbidden   Sim Hun, Creative Writer

Height 5 ch'ŏk 7 ch'on 3 pun, weight 16 kwan 400 monme. With lung capacity enough to fill an air bladder and more in a breath and a half, I must have a healthier body than the average person. Perhaps it's thanks to my parents who 'created' me when they were full of vigor in their thirties. I once had surgery for a boil, but I still have no acquaintance with minor illnesses. I had a bad habit of sometimes drinking excessively, but recently I practice drinking moderately on occasion, cultivating the discipline of 'not reaching the point of disorder.' As for cigarettes, even if I smoke, one pack a day is more than enough.
I have no special health method. Every summer, I start cold water baths, but I'm the lazy type who forgets to continue once the weather cools. However, if you were to specifically ask me for a health method:
1.  Mentally, not getting anxious over trivial matters and not making personal enemies around oneself.
2.  Physically, after becoming sexually aware, not engaging in excessive sexual indulgence, unnatural lust (藍色), or lechery. <85>

A Cheerful Life   Kil Myŏk-sŏk, Female Doctor

1.  Feeling satisfaction in daily life and working diligently.
2.  Engaging in appropriate exercise and maintaining a proper health regimen.
3.  Getting sufficient sleep time.

Sunbathing and Walking
Hyŏn Sang-yun

1.  Cold water rubdown. Every morning upon waking, I do this. Since I do it indoors, I arrange beforehand to have someone bring the water before I get up.
2.  Sunbathing. Whenever I have a spare moment, I take off my hat and go outside to do this. However, I don't remove clothes like jackets, just loosen my coat strings and roll up my sleeves slightly so the sunlight is absorbed well.
3.  Walking. I walk for an average of about 2 hours daily.
4.  Sleep and Rest. I strive to do these two things well as much as possible.
5.  Nutritious Food. I try, but my finances often don't permit it well.

An Afternoon Walk   Chang I-uk, Principal of Sinsŏng Middle School
Recently, I haven't had a health method that I practice with particular effort. Upon receiving your inquiry, I have decided anew on the following three things and intend to practice them.
1.  Go to bed at 10:30 PM and wake up at 6:30 AM as much as possible.
2.  Prohibit overeating at regular meals and reduce snacking.
3.  Take an afternoon walk.

Three-Stage Deep Breathing   Yun Paek-nam, Novelist

1.  Perhaps thanks to being unable to drink alcohol, I wake up at 6 AM no matter how late I went to bed the previous night. And I perform my unique three-stage deep breathing twice daily.
2.  Regarding food, if only I have the money, I eat quite a lot of various things, almost to the point of extravagance. However, I regularly consume garlic once a day and obtain considerable effect in protecting my stomach.

Two Big Laughs   Ch'oe Nŭng-jin, Coach at Sungsil College

I. Food.
1.  Vegetarian diet in the summer season.
2.  Meat diet in the winter period, but substituting meat with peanuts, chestnuts, sesame oil, etc., as much as possible.
3.  Absolutely avoid foods harmful to the body (alcohol, tobacco, coffee, etc.).
4.  Drink plenty of water.
II. Get plenty of sleep.
1.  Go to bed before 10 PM.
2.  Sleep for less than 20 minutes after lunch.
III. Wear warm clothes, but do not wear underwear that could harm the skin or obstruct air circulation <86>.
IV. Exercise. Sweat once daily.
V. Strive to laugh heartily 2-3 times daily.
VI. Make sure to have a bowel movement once daily.

One Glass of Cold Water   Kim Wŏn-ju, Maeil Sinbo Company

I. Every morning, I drink one glass ("koppŭ") of cold water.
II. I originally like exercise, so I always skate in winter and play tennis in summer whenever I have time. Thanks to this, I have become healthy and rarely catch colds.

Absolute Rest for Half a Day or More Per Week   Ku Yŏng-suk, Dean of Severance Union Medical College

As someone with a congenitally weak constitution who has gone through a serious illness in recent years, the method by which I preserve even this much health is:
I. Not using alcohol or tobacco.
II. Going to sleep early after evening to rest the brain, which has been troubled all day.
III. Waking up early in the morning and going up a small hill for breathing exercises for over 5 minutes.
IV. Being careful about overeating.
V. Walking for over 30 minutes daily.
VI. Eating one piece of fruit daily.
VII. Taking absolute rest for half a day or a full day once a week.

"My Health Method" Is   O Kŭng-sŏn, Vice President of Severance Union Medical College

I. Rise early and go to bed late, and
II. Eat moderately and take medicine.

Calisthenics Every Morning
Korean Language Dictionary Compilation Committee

I. Always doing an appropriate amount of both mental and physical labor.
II. Living a regular life, especially sleeping and waking early.
III. Doing calisthenics or taking a walk every morning.

Eat and Sleep Regularly   Yi Yong-sŏl, Professor at Severance Union Medical College

I. Striving to eat and sleep regularly as much as possible.
II. Absolutely prohibiting tobacco and alcohol.
III. Whenever there is an opportunity, using walking as exercise instead of riding. <87>

One Excursion Per Week   Yi P'a-ha, Tong-A Ilbo

I. Sleep time: Bed at 11, rise at 5.
II. Meals: At fixed times.
III. One short-distance excursion per week.
IV. One long-distance trip per month.
Other appropriate exercise according to the season.

A Cheerful Mind   Yi Yŏng-min, Athlete

I. Abstinence from smoking and drinking.
II. Maintaining a constantly cheerful mental state through an enjoyable life with hobbies.
III. Observing general hygiene rules for sleep and meals.
IV. Improving skills through sincere practice under proper methods and rules. <88>

**Summary**

This 1931 article from *Tongkwang* magazine compiles personal health advice from various prominent Korean figures of the colonial era. Common themes include maintaining regular schedules for sleep and meals, moderation in diet (avoiding overeating, stimulants like alcohol, tobacco, coffee), incorporating physical activity (walking, specific exercises, sports), ensuring adequate rest, and managing mental well-being (avoiding stress, cultivating cheerfulness, engaging in hobbies or appreciating arts/philosophy). Some mention specific practices like cold water rubs, sunbathing, deep breathing, consuming garlic, or regular medical check-ups. The advice blends traditional sensibilities with modern hygienic concepts.

From a historical perspective, the article provides a snapshot of the health concerns and practices among the Korean elite during Japanese colonial rule. The emphasis on the "worker's" (일꾼) health could reflect nationalist undertones, linking individual well-being to collective strength. The diverse backgrounds of the contributors (education, law, journalism, medicine, arts, sports, business) highlight the reach of modern health discourse. Mentions of specific locations (Keijō mountains), institutions (Ewha, Severance, Sungsil, newspapers), and contemporary issues like "male chastity" or the stress of "public and private matters" (potentially hinting at colonial pressures) offer valuable social context for historians.

우선 健康, 일꾼의 몸튼튼부터, 나의 健康法
						諸氏
우리는 무슨 일을 하던지 健康이 絶對로 必要하다. 健康한 몸과 健全한 精神으로 야만 무슨 일이던지 힘잇게 할 수 잇다. 그러나 健康은 1日에 얻을 수 잇는 것이 아니니 日日의 不絶한 努力과 修鍊이 必要함은 勿論이다. 이제 新年을 當하야 各界諸氏의 獨特한 健康法을 公開할 수 잇는 것은 무엇보다 愉快한 일이다. 일꾼의 몸은 튼튼부터. 우리는 新年을 期하야 몸을 튼튼히 하도록 最大의 關心과 細密한 注意를 가지기로 하자.
(順序無順)

心神의 平和   協成實業校長 金麗植

1. 食事... 每日 3時(朝, 午, 夕)의 定量이 잇어 過食치 않기를 가장 注意하며 約 20年前부터는 名實相符의 禁酒斷煙의 實行을 繼續中이고 强度의 커피, 葉茶, 苦椒 等 刺戟이 甚한 食物을 可及的 忌避합니다.
2. 性行... 舊式 또한 早婚으로 成立된 1夫1婦制를 遵守하고 拙丈夫의 嘲笑를 들으면서도 아직까지 所謂 「男子의 貞操」를 保有하고 犧牲的 節制의 生活을 합니다.
3. 休息... 夜間 約 8時間의 睡眠을 慣性합니다.
4. 運動... 機會잇는 대로 新鮮한 空氣를 呼吸하고 每日 30分 以上의 行步를 勵行합니다.
5. 心神... 나는 先天的 稟質이 좀 弱합니다마는 過去 約 20年동안은 ＜83＞ 特別한 病苦를 느끼지 아니하엿으나 그러나 最近 3-4年來로는 公私에 關한 不可抗克의 不安과 煩憫이 나의 心神을 恒常 괴롭게 하는 가운데 나의 健康은 발서 損傷을 當하엿습니다. 그러므로 健康의 條件은 肉體上 攝操와 아울러 心神上 平和를 얻지못하면 아니 될 것을 더욱 覺悟하엿습니다.

자고싶은 때 자고   辯護士 李一

나는 先天的 健康한 體質을 가지고 낫으므로 여간 無理를 하야도 病이 아니 납니다. 그러나 억지로 病을 내여 보려고 無法한 試驗을 하야 보지 않앗사오며 나의 健康法을 말하자면
1. 자고 싶은 때에 자고,
2. 먹고 싶은 때에 먹고 煩悶을 잊어버리고 感激을 될 수 잇는 대로 避하야 木石과 같이 生活하기를 힘쓰기외다.

5分以上 軆操   東亞日報社 李光洙

生은 別로 健康法이라고 할만한 것을 가지지 못하엿습니다. 病後의 生活에서 健康法이라 할 것을 추린다면,
1. 9時間 以上 睡眠.
2. 夜間外出 아니하는 것.
3. 食事를 家庭에서 規則的으로 하는 것.
4. 酒草를 끊는 것.
5. 每日 5分 以上 體操하는 것.

4. 時時로 郊外散步하는 것.

3. 耶, 佛, 老, 莊 等 超脫的인 文章을 翫味하야 되도록 心神의 碍滯를 풀어 버리기로 留意합니다.

適當한 運動   梨花專門 金信實

3. 飮食榮養素에 對한 注意.
4. 每日에 適當한 運動.
5. 節季따라 衣服에 對한 注意.

細鎖한 焦慮와 過色은 禁物   創作家 沈薰

身長 5尺 7寸3分, 體重 16貫 400匁. 한 呼吸半이면 風枕을 채우고도 남는 肺量을 가젓으니 凡人 以上의 健康體일 것입니다. 아마 우리父母가 30餘歲의 血氣가 方旺하실 때에 創作해 주신 德澤인가 합니다. 腫氣로 外科手術을 받은 적이 잇섯으나 잔病하고는 아직 交際가 없습니다. 때로 過飮을 하는 좋지못한 버릇이 잇엇으나 近來에는 應時小酌하되 不及亂의 修養을 하는 中이요 담배는 피워도 하로 한 匣이면 남습니다.
特別한 健康法은 없습니다. 여름에는 年年히 冷水浴을 始作햇다가는 날이 선선해지면 잊어버린 듯이 繼續하지 못하는 게름뱅입니다. 그러나 구태여 남에게 健康法을 무르신다면
1. 精神的으로는 細鎖한 일에 焦慮하지 않고 身邊에 私敵을 만들지 안는 일.
2. 肉體的으로는 性을 안 以後에 過色, 藍色, 貪色을 하지 않는 것. ＜85＞
