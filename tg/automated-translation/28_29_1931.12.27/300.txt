Magazine Title: 東光 제29호
Publication Date: 1931년 12월 27일
Article Title: 斷章 (Fragment)
Author: 岸曙
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Poverty is fertilizer, do not worry.
Thanks to the fertilizer, the fruit grows.

At the end of the whip, even cries hold their breath,
But within lies solemn power and blood.

They say only a battered tree becomes sturdy;
Strike it, this person, it is steel.

Though harsh beatings draw blood and sweat,
Within the tears, Rebellion cries out.<116>

**Summary**

This short poem by An Sŏ uses metaphors of hardship—poverty as fertilizer, whipping revealing inner strength, a battered tree becoming sturdy steel—to assert that suffering ultimately cultivates resilience and power. The final line explicitly mentions "Rebellion" emerging from tears, suggesting that enduring oppression breeds defiance. For historians, the poem offers insight into the spirit of endurance and veiled resistance among Koreans during the colonial period. The transformation of suffering into strength and the final, direct mention of rebellion, even metaphorically, reflect common themes in the literature of the era, potentially navigating censorship while expressing national sentiment.

						岸曙
가난은 거름일세, 걱정을 말라.
거름 德에 열매는 자라는 것을.
채직끝엔 울음도 숨을 죽이나
그속에는 힘과 피 엄숙한 것을.
부다낀 나무라야 딴딴하다고
따려보게, 이사람, 鋼鐵인 것을.
모진 매에 피땀을 흘리긴해도
눈물속엔 反抗이 웨치는 것을.＜116＞
