Article Title: 朝鮮農村問題解剖, 主로 農業政策에 對하야 (Analysis of the Chosŏn Rural Problem, Mainly Regarding Agricultural Policy)

Analysis of the Chosŏn Rural Problem, Mainly Regarding Agricultural Policy
						By Im Pyŏngch'ŏl

(1) Rice Production Increase Plan
1. Land Improvement Projects
2. Agricultural Improvement Projects
(2) Upland Crop Production Increase Plan
(3) Owner-Farmer Creation Plan
(4) Tenancy Dispute Mediation Law

In this extremely limited space, I wish to briefly introduce mainly the rural policies among the Chosŏn rural problems, and along with this, discuss my own views and those of general society.

(1) Rice Production Increase Plan
The most fundamental and problematic aspect of Chosŏn's agricultural policy is the Rice Production Increase Plan. This plan was established in Taishō 9 (1920), when Governor-General for Political Affairs Shimooka and others were advocating for Industrialism-First. Initially, the results of this plan were poor, leading to the establishment of the Renewed Rice Production Increase Plan from Taishō 15 (1926).

This plan consists of (1) Land Improvement Projects and (2) Agricultural Improvement Projects.
(1) The Land Improvement Project aimed to improve 350,000 *chōbo* (町步) over 12 years starting from Taishō 15 (completion in 14 years) (irrigation improvement 185,000 *chōbo*, land category conversion 90,000 *chōbo*, reclamation and land reclamation from the sea 75,000 *chōbo*), requiring 303.25 million yen to be procured through government subsidies and bank financing.
(2) The Agricultural Improvement Project involved spending 37 million yen for commercial fertilizer application funds on a total of 740,000 *chōbo* (existing paddies and land to be improved), and 3 million yen for other agricultural improvement funds such as increasing green manure and compost. Through this, an increased yield of approximately 8.2 million *sŏk* (石) was intended.

Such a grand plan has now reached a difficult situation where it must be changed, halfway through its planned duration.
The Ministry of Finance (大藏省) has decided to change this plan, reducing the government-mediated funds from 8 million yen to 3.9 million yen, and the National Treasury subsidies from 4 million yen to 2 million yen. Consequently, the financing from the Industrial Bank (殖銀) and the Oriental Development Company (東拓), which was equal to the government-mediated funds, will also be halved. Thus, the famed Land Improvement Project will proceed with its annual expenses halved to 10 million yen.

So why did such a national grand plan have to be changed? Setting aside complex reasons, the overproduction of rice led to a sharp fall in rice prices, causing an agricultural crisis, and the rural villages suffered from anemia. On top of that, debts increased and water association fees (水組費) became excessive, causing the rural villages to finally collapse with no room for recovery. The 8,000 cases of seizure of standing rice crops (立稻差押), rural debt amounting to 500 million yen, and landowners abandoning their rights to irrigated land prove this. Given this situation, even though the water association fees are backed by strong authority <6> only about 50% could be collected, so how could the projects proceed?

Looking back at the past, we can see that even if the land improvement funds were halved, the complete success of the project cannot be expected. We believe that its fundamental flaws must be corrected.
(1) Do not force the establishment of water associations where they are unnecessary.
(2) Plan the designs thoroughly.
(3) Abandon the construction-first principle and prioritize the farm household economy.
(4) Provide funds at low interest rates with extended repayment periods.
(5) Do not make the personnel costs of the association excessive.
(6) Do not focus solely on rice paddies (畓).
These are some points.

Regarding agricultural improvement, as discussed above, the allocated low-interest funds for this year (Shōwa 6 - 1931) were 10,837,270 yen, but the actual loans amounted to 7,360,150 yen, a ratio of merely 67% of the allocated amount.
Originally, the allocation was severely insufficient compared to the total number of farm households in Chosŏn, and yet even the allocated amount was barely half utilized – what a severe contraction this is. Furthermore, the loan conditions are harsh, utilized only by landlords or the middle-farmer class and above. The poor farmers who most need agricultural improvement merely hear the sound of money, how contrary this is to the fundamental spirit.

(2) Upland Crop Production Increase Plan
The Upland Crop Production Increase Plan is attracting attention as a plan to invest a total of 3.8 million yen over 12 years starting from Shōwa 6 (1931), aiming for an annual increased yield of 10.6 million *sŏk* of miscellaneous grains (barley, beans, millet), valued at 115 million yen.
Since this plan is in its first year, it is difficult to discuss its performance yet, but the plan itself must be considered appropriate for Chosŏn, which focuses too much on rice paddies. However, rather than just barley, beans, and millet, which are extensions of rice, there is a need to actively encourage more practical supplementary foods like potatoes, sweet potatoes, and corn, as well as others like peanuts, other fruits, and furthermore, various types of livestock farming.
That is, barley, millet, etc., are not much different from increasing rice production and will merely contribute to the agricultural crisis; it does not go beyond a plan to supplement rice exports.
Also, within the plan, in the 200 counties and islands designated as major upland crop cultivation zones, one person per county is assigned for guidance, and the Agricultural Experiment Station employs 1 engineer, 2 assistant engineers (技手) (subsidy 1,000 yen per person, salary 6,000 yen, travel expenses 400 yen), totaling 4 personnel. 1 Such excessive personnel costs will likely lead to the same failure as seen with the water associations. Rather than nominal goals, providing practical benefits like seeds for useful crops or breeding stock for livestock free of charge or at cost would be truly beneficial to our rural villages.

(3) Owner-Farmer Creation Plan
The proportion of owner-farmers indicates the soundness or ruin of a rural village. In a country like Denmark (丁抹), 95% are said to be owner-farmers. However, in our Chosŏn villages, pure owner-farmers constitute 18%, while tenant farmers make up 45% of all farm households. This shows the weakness of our rural villages.
For several decades, the dissolution of owner-farmers has intensified. According to the Government-General's survey in Shōwa 4 (1929), there were 21,326 large landowners, 83,179 medium landowners, 507,384 owner-farmers, 885,594 owner-tenant farmers, 1,283,471 tenant farmers, 92,710 tenant/slash-and-burn farmers, and 34,337 pure slash-and-burn farmers.
Comparing this to the previous year, large landowners increased by 599, medium landowners decreased by 654, owner-farmers decreased by 3,599, and owner-tenant farmers decreased by 8,784 households. From these numbers, we can <7> intuitively grasp the fact that over 12,000 households combining owner-farmers and owner-tenant farmers fell into ruin within one year, becoming tenant farmers, i.e., extremely poor farmers.
In the face of such reality in Chosŏn, the Government-General has only recently announced the Owner-Farmer Creation Plan. The main points of this plan are as follows:
(1) Create 2,000 owner-farmer households annually, establishing approximately 20,000 owner-farmer households over 10 years. (2) The method involves arranging low-interest funds of 1.6 million to 2 million yen annually, lending 800 to 1,000 yen per household. (3) Enable each household to own an average of 5 *tanbo* (段步) of land, (4) Provide a subsidy of 15 to 20 yen per household, and (5) Have them repay the principal and interest in annual installments over fifteen years.
Firstly, regardless of the content, one cannot but support the creation of owner-farmers. However, as mentioned above, with over 12,000 households annually treading the path of inevitable ruin, creating 2,000 owner-farmer households per year is merely like trying to stop a flood with a wooden plank.
We refrain from prematurely judging whether the Owner-Farmer Creation Plan will balance its budget as expected and indeed succeed in turning that number into owner-farmers without seeing the actual results. However, more than anything, the scope of its implementation—specifically, which farmers, to put it concretely, their ethnicity and what level of poverty—becomes the focus of attention. This is a question for tomorrow.

(4) Tenancy Dispute Mediation Law
Year after year, tenancy disputes in Chosŏn show a trend of increasing both quantitatively and qualitatively. This is likely in direct proportion to the impoverishment of rural villages.
In Shōwa 5 (1930), the number of tenancy disputes was 720, with over 12,000 participants. Compared to the year before last, this shows an increase of 303 cases and over 7,100 participants. The causes of these disputes were changes in cultivation rights (作權) in 489 cases and high rent rates in 67 cases.
It is regrettable that there is still no tenancy law capable of controlling landlords who wield tenancy rights as their sole weapon.
Recently, the Legal Affairs Bureau (法務局) is said to be preparing a Tenancy Dispute Mediation Law. It will likely take time until it becomes a complete law. We believe its enactment will have an impact on Chosŏn's rural villages.
The contents of the law are not yet known, but some parts mentioned include setting the rent uniformly at 50%, and reducing the litigation costs for tenancy disputes so that tenants can assert their rights in court.
Above all, landlords and their agents (舍音) arbitrarily raise rents, impose various expenses, and levy taxes as tactics to change cultivation rights.
A provision regarding cultivation rights is necessary for tenants who have no choice but to submit to such harsh conditions. Thus, a minimum term of about 10 years should be given, providing a nature of permanent tenancy (永小作), so that farmers can put down roots and live securely. Any law that seeks only to prevent tenancy disputes without protecting the security of livelihood is no more than a wooden plank trying to stop a flood.
Also, regarding rent, even if 50% is based on old customs and local strongmen (土豪), it does not allow tenants to sustain their livelihood. It should be stipulated clearly that the rate should be lower, and at the same time, land taxes and various expenses should be borne by the landlords.
Reducing litigation costs is also a good thing. Just as physical illnesses are treated for free, treating the illnesses of tenants' livelihoods for free is also good. However, we fear the example where, just because the fee is low or non-existent, the illness is treated carelessly.

In conclusion, open a path for farmers to live, give them farmland (農土). We only insist on this.
November 28, 1931 <8>

**Summary**

This 1931 article by Im Pyŏngch'ŏl critiques the Japanese Government-General's agricultural policies in Korea, specifically the Rice Production Increase Plan, the Upland Crop Production Increase Plan, the Owner-Farmer Creation Plan, and the proposed Tenancy Dispute Mediation Law. The author argues that these policies, particularly the Rice Production Increase Plan, have largely failed due to flawed planning, excessive costs (especially personnel and water association fees), harsh loan conditions favoring wealthier farmers, and a fundamental misunderstanding of rural economic realities, ultimately exacerbating rural poverty, debt, and the decline of owner-farmers into tenancy. He criticizes the scale of the Owner-Farmer Creation Plan as woefully inadequate and expresses skepticism about the effectiveness of the proposed Tenancy Dispute Mediation Law if it doesn't address core issues like secure cultivation rights and fair rent levels. The core demand is for policies that genuinely provide farmers with secure livelihoods and access to land. For historians, this article offers a valuable contemporary Korean perspective critical of colonial agricultural policies, providing specific data points (debt figures, land seizure cases, farmer statistics) and detailing the perceived failures and social consequences of programs like the Rice Production Increase Plan and the proposed solutions being debated. It highlights the growing agrarian crisis and tenant-landlord tensions in the early 1930s.

朝鮮農村問題解剖, 主로 農業政策에 對하야
						林炳哲
(一) 産米增産計劃
一. 土地改良事業
二. 農事改良事業
(二) 田作增殖計劃
(三) 自作農創定計劃
(四) 小作爭議調停法

極히 制限된 紙面에서 나는 朝鮮農村問題中 主로 農村政策에 對하야 簡單히 紹介하고 아울러 나와 및 一般社會의 見解를 論하고저 한다.

(一) 産米增産計劃
朝鮮의 農業政策 中 第一 根本이 되고 第一 問題가 되는 것은 産米增殖計劃이다.  이 計劃을 세우기는 下岡政務總監 等이 産業第一主義를 부르짖든 大正 9年이다.  最初에 이 計劃은 성적이 불량하야 大正 15年부터 産米增殖更新計劃을 세우게 되엇다.
이 計劃은 (一) 土地改良事業 (二) 農事改良事業이다.
(一) 土地改良事業이란 大正 15年 以降 12個年 (完成 14個年)에 35萬町步 (灌漑改善18萬5,000町步, 地目變換九萬町步, 開墾干拓7萬5,000町步)를 改良하는데 3億325萬圓을 政府의 補助 及 銀行에서 調達케 하는 것이요.
(二) 農事改良事業이란 旣成畓과 改良할 土地 合 74萬町步에 販賣肥料施用의 資金 3,700萬圓과 其他 綠肥堆肥의 增産 其他 農事改良資金 300萬圓을 쓰는 것이다.  그리하야 約 820萬石의 增收를 얻으려는 것이다.
이러한 大計劃이 그 豫定年數의 半期를 當하는 今日에 와서 變更하지 않으면 안 될 難境에 處하얏다.
大藏省으로부터 이 計劃을 變更하야 政府의 幹旋資金 800萬圓을 390萬圓으로 國庫補助金 400萬圓을 200萬圓으로 주리게 되엇다.  이 같이 政府의 幹旋資金과 同額이든 殖銀 及 東拓의 融資도 同樣으로 半減되게 되엇으니 이름높든 土地改良事業도 年經費를 半減하야 1,000萬圓으로 事業을 進行하게 되엇다.
그러면 어찌하야 이 같은 國家的 大計劃을 變更하지 않으면 안되게 되엇든가?  複雜한 理由는 그만두고라도 米의 所出이 過剩되어 米價暴落으로 農業恐慌을 이르키어 농촌은 貧血症에 걸리엇다.  그런데다가 負債는 늘어나고 水組費는 過重하야 農村은 마츰내 回復의 餘地없이 쓰러지게 되엇다.  立稻差押八千件, 農村負債 五億圓, 水組地主作權抛棄 等이 이를 證明하고잇다.  이러한 現狀임으로 强權을 ＜6＞ 가진 水組費이지 마는 그 五割밖에는 건지지 못하엿으니 어찌 事業을 進行할 수 잇으랴.
우리는 過去를 비치어보아 비록 土地改良資金을 半減하엿다고 事業의 完全을 企할수 없음을 알 수 잇다.  마땅이 그 根本的 缺陷을 고치지 않어서는 안될 줄 믿는다.
(1) 水組를 不必要로 하는 곳에 强制的 創立을 말 것.
(2) 設計計劃을 充分히 할 것.
(3) 工事第一主義를 버리고 農家經濟를 爲主로 할 것.
(4) 資金은 低利로 期限은 늘일 것.
(5) 組合人件費를 過大히 말 것.
(6) 畓(논)만을 爲主하지 말 것.
等이다.

農事改良에 對하야서도 以上에서 論하얏거니와 今年 (昭和 六年)에 配定된 低資는 1,083萬 7,270圓인데 이에 對한 貸付實績은 736萬150圓으로 配定額에 對한 比率은 겨우 六割 七分이다.
원체 그 配定이 全朝鮮農戶에 比하야 甚히 적은 것인데 그에다 配定額도 겨우 半을 넘엇다 하니 이 얼마나 甚한 縮小이냐.  그 나마도 貸付條件이 苛酷하야 地主나 中農以上의 階級이 利用할뿐으로 農事改良을 가장 必要로하는 貧農은 돈소리만 돗는 셈이니 이 얼마나 根本精神에 어그러지는 일인가.

(二) 田作增殖計劃
田作增殖計劃은 總經費 380萬圓을 들이어 昭和 6年부터 12個年 繼續事業으로 每年 雜穀 (麥, 豆類, 粟)1,060萬石, 價格1億1,500萬圓의 增收를 얻고저하는 計劃으로 注目을 끄을고 잇다.
이 計劃은 計劃初年이라 그 성적여하를 아직 논하기 어려우나 計劃만은 畓을 너무 爲主하는 朝鮮에 잇어서 適切한 것이라고 할 수밖에 없다.  그러나 米의 延長物인 麥, 豆類, 粟만을 하지 말고, 보다도 實用的補助食物이 될 감자, 고구마, 옥수수 같은 것이나 그外에 落花生, 기타 果樹같은 것이며, 또 한거름 나가서나 各種畜産을 積極的 獎勵할 必要를 느낀다.
즉 麥, 粟 等은 米의 增收나 別差가 없어서 農業恐慌의 一助力物에 不過할 것이요 移出米增加를 補助하는 計劃에서 지나지 안는다.
또한 計劃中에 田作栽培主要地帶 200郡島에 各郡1人을 두어 指導케하며 農事試驗場에는 技師1人, 技手2人 (1人當補助1,000圓, 俸給6,000圓, 旅費400圓) 雇用 4人이다. 1 이러한 人件費의 過大는 水組에서의 失敗와 마찬가지로 될 것이다.  차라리 名 目的을 떠나 實際益으로 實益이 잇는 作物의 種子, 家畜의 原種을 無代 或은 實費로 提供하는等 事業이 우리農村에 實益이 될 줄 믿는다.

(三) 自作農創定計劃
自作農의 多少로 써 그農村의 健實性과 破滅相을 알 수 잇다.  丁抹같은 나라는 9割 5分이 自作農이라 한다.  그런데 우리 朝鮮農村은 純自作農이 1割 8分이요 小作農이 全農家의 4割 5分이니 이로써 우리農村의 貧弱을 알 수 잇다.
수십년 以來로 自作農의 分解作用은 더욱 甚하다.  昭和 4年度 總督府調査에 依하면 大地主는 2萬1,326戶 中地主는 8萬3,179戶, 自作農은 50萬7,384戶, 自作兼小作은 88萬5,594戶, 小作農은 128萬3,471戶, 小作兼火田民은 9萬2,710戶, 純火田民은 3萬4,337戶이다.
이를 그 前年에 比하면 大地主 增599, 中地主 減654, 自作農 減3,599, 自作兼小作 減8,784戶이다.  이 數字에서 우리는 ＜7＞ 直覺的으로 自作農과 自作兼小作農을 合하야 1年間 1萬2,000餘戶가 沒落하야 小作 卽 極貧農化해 간다는 事實을 알 수 잇다.
이 같은 朝鮮의 現實에 잇어서 近日에야 겨우 自作農創定計劃을 總督府에서 發表하게 되엇다.  이제 이 計劃의 대요를 들면 다음과 같다.
(1) 每年 2,000戶의 自作農을 創定하야 10年間 約 2萬戶의 自作農을 創立한다.  (2) 그 方法은 每年 160萬圓 내지 200萬圓의 低利資金을 融通하야 每戶 800圓 乃至 1,000圓을 貸付한다.   (3) 每戶 平均 5段步의 土地를 所有케하고, (四) 每戶當 十五圓 乃至 二十圓 補給金을 주고, (五) 十五個年에 元利金을 年賦還償케 하리라는 것이다.
爲先 內容 如何를 둘재로 하고 自作農創定을 贊成할 수밖에 없다.  그러나 우에서 든 것과 같이 年年히 1萬2,000餘戶가 餘地없는 沒落의 過程을 밟고 잇는 今日에 잇어서 每年 2,000餘戶의 自作農創定은 洪水를 널쪼각으로 막으려는 셈에서 不過하다.
우리는 그 自作農創定計劃이 豫想과 같이 收支가 맞어 果然 그만한 數를 自作農으로 할 수  잇을가는 實蹟을 보지 않고 먼저 斷言하기를 避하거니와 무엇보다 그實施의 範圍 卽 어떠한 農民에게 具體的으로 말하면 民族別 如何와 어떤 程度의 貧農에게냐가 注目의 的이 된다.  이 問題는 明日의 問題이다.

(4) 小作爭議調停法
해를 거듭할사록 朝鮮의 小作爭議는 量的質的으로 增加의 趨勢를 보여 준다.  이는 농촌의 貧窮化와 正比例가 될 것이다.
昭和 5年中 小作爭議件數는 720件이요 參加人數는 1萬2,000餘人이다.  이것을 再昨年것에 比하면 件數 303件, 人數 7,100餘人의 增加를 보여 주고 잇다.  그리고 그 爭議의 原因은 作權移動이 489件, 作料高率임에 因한것이 67件이다.
小作權을 唯一의 武器로 하야 橫行하는 地主를 制御할만한 小作法이 아직까지 없음은 遺憾이다.
최근 法務局에서 小作爭議調停法을 마련한다고 한다.  아직 完全한 法律이 되기까지는 時日을 要 할 것이다.  이것이 制定되면 朝鮮農村에도 및을 影響이 잇을 줄 믿는다.
同法의 內容은 아직 알 수 없거니와 그 一部만을 들면 小作料는 一律로 5割로 할 것, 小作爭議의 訴訟費用을 적게하야 裁判所에 訟하야 小作人의 權利를 主張하도록 하는 것이다.
무엇보다도 地主들과 舍音들은 作權移動策으로 作料引上, 諸費用負擔, 課稅 等을 맘대로 한다.
이런 어떤 苛酷한 條件에도 服從치않을수 없는 小作人에게 作權에 關한 規定이 必要하다.  그리하야 적어도 10餘年의 限을 주어 永小作의 성질을 가지게 하야 農民들로 하야금 뿌리를 박고 安全한 生活을 할 수 잇도록 하여야 할 것이다.  生活安全을 保護치 않고 小作爭議만을 막으려는 어떠한 法은 洪水를 막으려는 널쪼각에서 지나지 못한다.
또한 作料에 잇어서도 5割은 舊習慣을 土豪한것이라 할지라도 이는 小作人의 生活을 持支할수없다.  보다 以下로 하는 同時에 地稅, 諸經費負擔等을 地主들의 負擔으로 하여야 할것等을 明確히 規定해야 할 것이다.
그리고 訴訟費用을 적게 한다는 것도 좋은 일이다.  肉體의 病을 無料로 보아주는 것 같이 作人들의 生活의 病을 無料로 보아주는 것도 좋은 일이다.  그러나 料金이 적다고 或은 없다고 病을 허술히 보아주는 것 같은 예를 우리는 두려워한다.

要컨대 農民에게 살길을 열어 주어라, 農土를 주워라, 우리는 이것을 主張할 뿐이다.
