Magazine Title: 동광 제29호
Publication Date: 1931년 12월 27일
Article Title: 연약한 사공아 어서 지나가거라. (O Weak Boatman, Pass Quickly.)
Author: 李揆元(詩), 全鳳濟(커트)
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

O weak boatman, pass quickly.
						Yi Kyu-wŏn (Poem), Chŏn Pong-je (Cut/Illustration)

Through the listless reed fields like a shot soldier,
A feeble boat, as if carrying the soul of a young widow, flows on.
Why is the boatman's intermittent song such a sad tune?
The heart watching from the riverbank plummets as if going mad.

In the old stories of the past, in the long-ago tales,
What precious flower blossoms once bloomed on this hill?
If they bloomed, why couldn't they bloom long, withering before time passed?
O, the riverside dusk that severs the nerves, making one shudder anew.

O grandfather, O father, who used to sing vigorous songs of youth.
Your children, full of wishes, row the boat singing sad songs.
Though they followed the roaring current of the distant past,
They have become weak boatmen, rowing feebly onward.

Do not mock us, O flowing current, O frolicking fish.
And you, undecaying stones, O sandbanks,
The waterway passed in old times was vigorous with strong boatmen,
But the boat brushing past now follows the tearful path of a boatman whose passion has cooled.

However, however, those spiritless days will come before long,
Our new sons shall row this river singing brave songs.
O water, flowing onward, connecting the blood vessels with the sick heart of civilization.
It is indeed the majestic waterway of my beloved sons—

Flow on, flow on, through the pale reed fields,
Where does this river end? Flow on to the very last port.
The sons seeking a brave waterway shall rise up behind you,
O weak boatman carrying tears, pass quickly.
=At the Taedong River= <119>

**Summary**

This poem uses the metaphor of a weak boatman navigating a river, explicitly identified as the Taedong River, to lament the present state of decline and sorrow compared to a more vigorous past symbolized by grandfathers and fathers. The boatman and the surrounding landscape reflect a sense of listlessness and grief. Despite the prevailing sadness and weakness, the poem concludes with a strong sense of hope, envisioning a future generation ("our new sons") who will navigate the same river with courage and strength, reclaiming a "majestic waterway." For historians, the poem offers insight into the feelings of disillusionment and suppressed national spirit under Japanese colonial rule in the early 1930s. The contrast between the weak present and the strong past, coupled with the fervent hope for a revitalized future generation, reflects common nationalist sentiments expressed through allegorical means during that period.

연약한 사공아 어서 지나가거라.
						李揆元(詩), 全鳳濟(커트)
총맞은 병사같이 맥없는 갈밭을 거처
청상과부 혼백 싫은 듯한 힘없는 배가 흘러를 간다.
끊젓다 닞는 사공의 노래 웨 이리도 슬픈 곡존가
강변에 앉어 바라보는 마음 미칠듯이 곤두박질하는구나.

지나간 옛니아기속에 오랜 옛니아기속에
얼마나 보배롭든 꽃송이가 이 언덕에 피엇든가
피엇거든 웨 기리못피고 세월도 가지전에 시드럿나
새삼스럽게 몸서리치나니 신경을 끊어놓는 강변의 저므름이여.

청춘의 기운찬 노래 부르든 하라버지여, 아버지여.
당신의 바람많든 자식들은 슬픈 노래부르며 배를 젓습니다.
까아만 지난날의 우렁찬 물줄기 따럿으나
연약한 사공이 되어 힘없이 노저어 갑니다.

비웃지 마라 흘으는 물결아, 뛰노는 고기야.
그리고 썩지 않는 돌멩이야, 모래언덕아,
옛날의 지나가든 뱃길은 강한 사공으로 줄기찻거든
지금에 스처가는 배는 피식은 사공의 눈물겨운 길이로구나.

그러나 그러나 맥빠른 그날은 멀지 않어 오리니
우리들의 새아들이 용감한 노래부르며 이 강을 저어가리라.
문명의 병든 심장으로 혈관을 이으며 달려가는 물.
내 사랑하는 아들의 위엄잇는 뱃길이어늘-

흘러가라 흘러가라 혈색없는 갈밭을 거처
이 강 끝다은데가 어디냐 마즈막 포구까지 흘러가거라
용감한 뱃길 찾는 아들은 네 뒤에서 일어나리니
눈물실은 연약한 사공아 어서 지나가거라.
=大同江에서= ＜119＞
