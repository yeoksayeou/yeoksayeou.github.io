Article Title: 물소래 (Sound of Water)

Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Sound of Water
						毛允淑

Deep night—
The distant sky dozes in milky fatigue,
And the morning star, as if to steal away dreams, is brilliant.
A night anxious lest the heart’s precious dream be forgotten,
The faint sound of water flowing by the ear.

When a pitiful dream-path made the heart ache,
The murmuring sound of water gently washed my heart,
A night when the heart clears and the head lightens.
Tonight, following the water's sound, where shall I go?

A heart that wept in confinement, lost direction in lingering attachment,
Unable to escape from numerous fetters.
This night, devoid of any strength, I open the window,
Tonight, following the water's sound, shall I leave this house?

The water heard moments ago must have flowed endlessly,
That stream, rubbing against stones, ever freshly, clearly flowing,
Let heavy worries, hateful pains, all flow away together.
Tonight, following the water's sound, shall I flow away coolly?

A body lifted from the pillow opens the window.
The mountain village sleeps, the dream-path distant,
On tear-streaked, lovely cheeks, moonlight follows, the mind agitated.
Tonight, following the water's sound, shall I step over this room's threshold?
(Dawn when I heard the sound of water at Sambang (三防)) <79>

**Summary**

This lyrical poem by Mo Yunsuk expresses a deep yearning for release and purification, symbolized by the continuous sound of flowing water heard during a sleepless night. The speaker feels confined by worries, regrets ("lingering attachment"), and unnamed burdens ("numerous fetters"). The water's sound offers solace, a sense of cleansing, and tempts the speaker with the possibility of escape, of flowing away from pain and confinement into a state of freedom and clarity. The poem concludes on a note of hesitant desire, wondering whether to cross the threshold and follow the liberating sound. From a historical perspective, while deeply personal, the poem's themes of confinement, yearning for escape, and the desire for purification could resonate with the broader Korean experience under colonial rule. The expression of internal turmoil and the longing for liberation, even framed personally, might offer historians insight into the subtle ways individuals navigated the emotional landscape of the era.

물소래
						毛允淑
깊은 밤―
먼 하늘은 우유 빛 피곤에 조을고
꿈을 빼서갈 듯 새ㅅ별은 영롱도 하여라.
가슴 속 고은 꿈을 닞을까 조바심 친 밤
귀ㅅ가로 흘러가는 그윽한 물소래.

애처런 꿈길이 가슴 복판을 앞으게 하든 때
쇨쇨 물소래는 내 가슴을 고히도 씿엇나니
가슴은 맑고 머리는 가벼워지는 밤.
내 이 밤에 물소리 따라 어대로 가버릴까?

구속에 울고 미련에 방향을 놓지든
수많은 굴레에 못나가든 맘.
아모 맥 힘도 없는 이 밤에 창문을 열고
내 이 밤에 물소래 따라 이 집을 떠날까?

아까 듣든 물은 벌서 끝도 없이 흘럿으리
돌을 부벼 새로 새로 맑게 가는 그 줄기
무건 근심 싫은 아픔 다 같이 흘러 가자.
내 이 밤에 물소래 따라 시원히 흘러 볼까.

벼개에서 들어진 몸 창문을 여네.
산촌은 잠들어 꿈길이 아득한데
눈물지는 가연 뺨에 달빛 좇아 마음 산란.
내 이 밤에 물소래 따라 이 방 턱을 넘어 볼까.
(三防서 물소래 듣든 새벽) ＜79＞
