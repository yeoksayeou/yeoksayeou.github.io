Magazine Title: 동광 제38호
Publication Date: 1932년 10월 01일
Article Title: 北部江原紀行 (Travelogue of Northern Kangwŏn)
Author: 李石山
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

(In June 1931, I traveled through the highland regions of northern Kangwŏn Province. This place forms a unique landscape.)

1. In the Middle of the Highlands
On the highlands bloom
nameless flowers.
The "Singosan T'aryŏng"
of the women digging bellflower roots
fades away on the blue wind.
Ah, a traveler captivated by the flowers—
June twilight!

2. Yi Royal Household Ranch
A.
In the blue wind of the highlands,
amidst waves of yellow sunlight,
horses freed from their bridles run and play.
As if about to leap into the sky,
they run and play freely.

B.
The horses grow vigorously well
in the wide, grassy fields!
For what purpose are these horses raised?

3. Sep'o Street
When the winter sun sets<68>
in the blizzard,
a gray street, shrunken.
Is it a Canadian (카나다) gold placer mining town?
Summer morning, five-thirty,
this street asleep in the rain—
Is it a decadent southern fishing village?

On a day cleared of rain,
stepping out onto the roadside,
the sun seems close enough to touch.
As I let the clear wind blowing
across a corner of the vast highlands wash my chest,
I wonder if right above the sky is my hometown
and feel a surging homesickness.

4. Highland Girl
When passing by,
a face humbly lowered.
Turning back to look from the corner after passing,
the simple highland woman.
Is it shame
for her own fate, like a red highland flower
blooming and fading meaninglessly?
Is it shyness?

5. Nangok Mechanized Farm
Though it's mid-June,
the acacia have only just bloomed.
Swarms in the sunny spot (?) (야지의 떼)
on the green clover lawn
are intoxicated by the flower scent,
and the windmill grinding wheat turns all day.
Field after field after field...
Soybeans·Oats (燕麥)·Potatoes·Soybeans·Oats (燕麥)·Potatoes...
Countless white dots of farmers weeding the distant fields
shimmer under the fiercely pouring sunlight.

(Ah! Lost in this picturesque rural landscape filled with an exotic (異國) atmosphere, I was about to stroll on in silence, but the faint, resentful gazes of the white-clad farmers starved for day labor stung my heart, and I hastened my steps and departed hurriedly.) <69>
<68-69>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Sŏksan describes a journey through the highlands of Northern Kangwŏn Province in 1931. It portrays scenes of natural beauty (flowers, twilight), modern enterprises (Yi Royal Household Ranch, Sep'o town, Nangok Mechanized Farm), and encounters with local people (women singing, a shy girl, farmers). The poem juxtaposes the picturesque or "exotic" scenery, including modern developments, with the underlying hardship and resentment of the Korean farmers, culminating in the author's uneasy departure from the mechanized farm. For historians, the poem offers a glimpse into the landscape of colonial Korea, showing the presence of Japanese-influenced or managed enterprises like the ranch and mechanized farm alongside traditional life. The final note explicitly contrasting the "exotic" view with the impoverished farmers' gazes is particularly revealing of the social tensions and the uneven impact of modernization during the colonial period.
