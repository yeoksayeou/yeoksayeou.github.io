STUDENT LITERARY SCENE

Twilight Comes Without a Sound
Kyŏngsin School, Kim Sŏngdo (金聖道)

The sound of planting rice seedlings has stopped.
The cicada on the roadside tree has also shut its mouth.
Beneath the zelkova tree in the middle of that field
Naked bodies gather.
Cigarette smoke dances.
A clamor of some sort can be heard.

Above the long, slender rice seedlings
The wind that fluttered has fallen asleep.
The wide field has become a dark blue plain. <60>
Breath is held.
Walking the path between the paddies,
A farmer carrying a farming tool (광이) on his shoulder
Looks as if he is walking on the rice seedling bed.

Up in the sky
Dark and reddish clouds
Why? Do they look so desolate?
Like the face of a wife looking at her husband
A hired hand bachelor
With his dark upper body bare
In the bean field furrow he is weeding
On this side of the new road!
One short one remains.
The shortest furrow, the finishing furrow (끌고랑).

An old man in a bamboo hat who was grazing [a cow]
Pulls forcibly on the lead rope of the cow that wants to graze more.
Helplessly, the cow is led away.
Following the sound of plodding footsteps
Twilight gathers.
Without a sound.
—(Looking at the Plains of my Hometown)— <61>

Cold Spring (冷泉)
Sungjung School, Cho Chongswan (趙宗纘)

From a crevice in the blue rock, emitting a cold aura,
Spring water like clouds wells up.
Trickle, trickle, it wells up.
Young girls clad only in undergarments
Scoop it with gourds and splash the water.
Ah, is this our place to escape the heat?
The sunlight beats down, the earth is a lump of fire,
Yet they stand shivering.
Ah—the sweat dries up completely.
(July 10th)

Coconut (椰子열매)
Hwawŏn Middle School, Cho Mok (趙黙)

On a quiet moonlit night in a foreign land
On the shore where no people are,
A coconut drifted from a southern isle
Sways lonely in the shallows.

On the island where you grew, <62>
Tonight the moon must be shining brightly (皎皎히),
And the shadow of the branch you hung from
Must be dancing on the ocean waves.

Like the swaying coconut's heart,
My heart too, riding the moonlight,
Runs aimlessly over the sea.

Standing on the seashore,
Trying to figure out the way back,
My heart is just filled with helplessness.
1932.7.

Summer by the Stream
Chang Sŭngdu (張承斗)

Two, three, four bare pebbles
Stick their faces out above the water,
Looking up at the sky,
Sighing;
Even the bellies of the playing fish
Shine white,
Summer by the stream
Seems to have arrived. <63>

A young maiden who had taken off her blouse
And was washing her breasts,
At the sound of a frog, *plop*—
Jumping into the water,
Flusteredly covers her breast with her blouse;
Summer by the stream
Seems to have arrived.
1932.8.9

Idle Thoughts (偶想)
Pak Nohong (朴魯洪)

*Whirr!* The bird flew away.
Where is it going?

In the tree's shade, a few ants
Carry something away.

The sky is blue,
The trees are green.

Again, the heart walking the hill
Is a soul that has lost its love. <64>
Like a patch of cloud in the summer sky.
1932.7

Stream Water (내ㅅ물)
Sinsŏng Middle School, Kim Yehran (金禮煥)

The stream flows.
The stream flows.
Through the dim shade,
It flows endlessly.

Carrying the full sound of insect cries,
Passing through the forest,
The flowing stream water,
Where is it going?

The stream flows.
The stream flows.
My aimless heart
Wanders on the forest path.
August 10th

(Selector's Comment)
I don't know why there are so many poems. Please send in many short travel essays (紀行小品), letters (書翰文), critiques (論平), etc. (September) <65>

**Summary**

This section of "Tonggwang" magazine presents six poems written by students from various schools in 1932. The poems primarily focus on observations of nature and rural life, often imbued with a sense of gentle melancholy, longing, or quiet contemplation. Themes include twilight descending on a farming village, the relief of a cold spring in summer, reflections inspired by a coconut washed ashore, vignettes of summer by a stream, fleeting thoughts on nature and loss, and the ceaseless flow of a stream mirroring the poet's wandering heart. The editor's note at the end laments the overabundance of poetry submissions and requests more prose, such as travelogues, letters, and reviews. For historians, this collection offers a glimpse into the literary expressions of Korean youth during the colonial period. The focus on nature, introspection, and rural scenes, rather than overt political commentary, might reflect the constraints of the era or a common youthful preoccupation, yet the underlying tones of longing ("Coconut") or aimlessness ("Stream Water") could subtly hint at broader sentiments of displacement or uncertainty. The editor's plea for different genres also sheds light on the editorial direction and perceived imbalance in student submissions at the time.

學生文壇
황혼은 소래도 없이
儆新 金聖道

논메기 소래가 그첫다.
길갓 나무에 매암이도 입 다물엇다.
저 들 한 가운데 정자나무 밑엔
발가벗은 몸들이 모여든다.
담배 연기가 춤을 춘다.
와글와글 무슨 소래가 들려 온다.

길쭉길쭉한 모 우에서
알랑거리든 바람도 잠이 들엇다.
넓다란 들은 검푸른 평지가 되엇다. ＜60＞
숨이 죽엇다.
논 사이 길을 걷고 잇는
광이 멘 농부
말이 모판 우로 걷는 듯이 뵈인다.

하늘 우엔
검고 또 붉으스레한 구름들
웨? 그다지도 쓸쓸하여 뵈이나
님 바라보면 안악네의 얼골 같이
시컴은 웃통을 벗은
머슴사리 총각이
신작로 이편에서
김매는 콩밭 고랑!
짤막한 것 하나 남엇다.
끌고랑 제일 짧은 고랑이.

풀 뜯어 먹이든 삿갓 쓴 노인
더 뜯어 먹을랴는 소 잇가리를
억지로 끄집어 당긴다.
할 수 없이 소가 잇끌려 간다.
타박타박 발자최 소리 따라
황혼은 몰려 온다.
소래도 없이.
―(故鄕 平野를 바라보면서)― ＜61＞

冷泉
崇中 趙宗纘

푸른 바위 틈에서 찬 기운을 뿜으며
구름 같은 샘물이 솟아 나온다.
촐촐촐 솟아 나온다.
속옷만 두른 소녀들
바가지로 퍼서는 물을 낓는다.
아 이곳이 우리의 피서지인가.
해빛이 나려 쪼여 대지는 불떵이인데
오들오들 떨고 서 잇다.
아- 땀이 버썩 잦는고나.
(7월 10일)

椰子열매
花園中學 趙黙

고요한 이국의 달밤
인적이 없는 바다 가에는
남쪽 나라 섬에서 흘러 온 야자 열매가
외로이 여을에 흔들리고 잇어라.

네가 자라난 섬에도 ＜62＞
이 밤 달이 皎皎히 떠 잇고
네 달렷든 가지의 그림자
바다 물결 우에 춤추리라.

출넝이는 야자 열매의 가슴 같이
내 맘도 달빛을 타고
바다 우으로 보람없이 닫네.

바다가에 서서
돌아갈 길 헤아리니
가슴 막막만 하여라.
1932.7.

시내ㅅ 가의 여름
張承斗

앙당한 돌맹이 두 세 넷
물 우에 얼골 내 놓고
하늘 처다보며
한숨 지으며
뛰여 노난 고기에 배때기까지
하얗게 빛나니
시내까에 한 여름
찾어왓나 봅니다.＜63＞

저고리 벗어 놓고
젖가슴 싲든 시악씨
개고리 풍덩―
물 속에 뛰여 드는 소리에
황당이 저고리로 가슴 가리우니
시내까에 한여름
찾어왓나 봅니다.
1932.8.9

偶想
朴魯洪

푸르릉! 새는 날라 갓습니다.
어디로 가는지요?

나무 그늘에 개암이 몇 마리
무엇을 물고 갑니다.

하늘은 파-랗고
나무는 퍼렇습니다.

다시 언덕을 걷는 마음
임 잃은 넋입니다.＜64＞
여름 하늘에 구름짱 같으니.
1932.7

내ㅅ물
信聖中學 金禮煥

냇물이 흐른다.
냇물이 흐른다.
어득한 그늘 새로
끝도 없이 흐른다.

버레우름 가득 실고
숲속을 지나
흐르는 시냇물
어디로 가는고.

냇물이 흐른다.
냇물이 흐른다.
지향없는 내 마음
숲길에 헤매는데.
8월 10일
