Article Title: 여름이 가나보다, 가을을 그리는 마음 (Summer Seems to Be Leaving, A Heart Longing for Autumn)

Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Summer Seems to Be Leaving, A Heart Longing for Autumn
						Sŏl Chŏng-sik

On the village marker post (標柱), dragonflies alight side-by-side
Now, even the paulownia leaves must intend to grow no more—
In the backyard behind the shed, the clustered persimmons ripen, and
The jujube tree, snagged with a girl's hair ribbon, bears fruit abundantly
Standing alone, touched by frost, their color reddens.

White clouds like the fur beneath a goat's ear
Bloom, and when the day clears
Is the sky merely high?—Even if scooped out with a large basin (함지), scooped out endlessly
It wells up unknowably, soaring—this sky right above the forehead.

Mingled with the regrettable, regrettable painted moon (畵月)
The long harbor inlet's sound is lovely with the high tide
And lovelier still, marvelously dyed by the setting sun (落照).
When they dismantled the tents on this hill, packing their summer bundles,
Those girls mimicking the charms of Greece (希臘)
Hitching up the thin clothes wrapped around their midriffs,
Hide away from the streets
And today too
The Weather Forecast (天氣豫報), its white flag, flutters far off.

(When I return to my beloved woods and fields)
Gently, the sun sets
Wind seeps into both breasts.
Tens of thousands of high and low insect cries
Pierce the undergrowth and vines,
And when the cat that went somewhere crawls back home
Grandmother
Gathers the spread-out, dried mulberry tobacco leaves into her skirt hem.

The oxcart, slowly
Winding the departing summer evening onto its two wheels,
Winds its way back from the distant rear valley.
A young man sits askew on the edge,
Quietly keeping rhythm
With the cowbell,
As they round the bend of the hill path,
The dog (삼사리) runs out from the brushwood gate, barking
To greet the young master, the reaper from the labor exchange (품아시 베아리꾼) —
Oh, at times like this
Grandfather too comes down from the lookout hut in the southern fields (南畝).
(1932.8.10) <78>

**Summary**

This poem by Sŏl Chŏng-sik captures the transition from late summer to early autumn in the Korean countryside of 1932. It evokes a nostalgic and serene atmosphere through rich sensory details: ripening fruit, the high clear sky, dragonflies, the sounds of insects and cowbells, and the quiet activities of rural life like harvesting and gathering dried leaves. The poem contrasts this traditional pastoral scene with fleeting images of modernity or external influence, such as beachgoers mimicking Greek styles and a weather forecast flag, grounding the idyllic portrayal within its specific time. For historians, the poem offers valuable insight into the ambiance of rural Korea during the colonial period, showcasing the persistence of traditional agricultural practices (like *p'umasi*) and communal life alongside subtle markers of contemporary change and cultural awareness. The precise dating adds to its documentary value.

여름이 가나보다, 가을을 그리는 마음
						薛貞植
마을어구 標柱에는 나란히 내려앉은 잠자리
이제는 오동잎도 더는 자라지 않으려니―
헛간 뒷마당에 다롱다롱 여무는 감과
처자의 댕기 걸린 대추나무 함뿍 되는 열매가
호올로 서리 맞어 그 빛이 붉어간다.
염소의 귀밑 털 같은 하이얀 구름
피어 날이 개이고 보면
그 하늘 높다 뿐이랴―함지로 퍼내이고 퍼내어도
끝 모르게 괴여 소사 오름이 이마작의 하늘이다.

아수운 아수운 畵月이 어울린
긴 포구는 밀물로 소리 곱고
더 곱게 落照로 희한하게 물든다.
이 언덕에서 천막을 허러 그네, 여름 봇짐을 싸질머 질 때
希臘의 그 아양을 본 뜬 게집들
반 허리에 휘감은 엷은 옷을 치키며
거리에서 숨어들고
이 날도
天氣豫報, 힌 깃밝은 멀리 나부낀다.

(내 사랑하는 숲과 드을로 도라가 보면)
뉘엿이 해 저물어
두 가슴으로 새어드는 바람.
수만 줄기 높낮은 버레 우름
숲풀과 덩쿨에 사모치고
어데 갓든 고양이 집으로 기여들 때
할머니는
널어 말린 뽕잎 담배를 치마자락으로 걷어 드린다.
소수레는 천천히
두 박휘에 이가는 여름 저녁을 감으로 감으며
먼 뒷골에서 예돌아든다.
젊은이는 끝단에 비껴 앉어
소방울에 고요히
장단을 놓으며
언덕길을 굽이돌제
품아시 베아리꾼 젊은 주인을 마지하는
삼사리는 싸리문 밖으로 내다르며 짖나니
오 이러할 때
南畝에 할아버지도 원두막에서 내려 온다.
(1932.8.10) ＜78＞
