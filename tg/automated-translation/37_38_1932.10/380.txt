Magazine Title: 동광 제38호
Publication Date: 1932년 10월 01일
Article Title: 마을의 여름밤 (Village Summer Night)
Author: 張萬榮
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

It is a summer night.
The summer sky is high and dark.
In the sepia-colored sky, small stars
are scattered here and there, looking like they might spill, and they twinkle.

It is when a gentle breeze shakes the tree leaves.
In the front yard, the womenfolk have spread out mats
and are busy fulling cloth.
Ah, it is a summer night.

Mosquito-repelling mugwort smoke rises in plumes,
and the children, listening to grandmother's old stories,
fall asleep just like that, snoring softly—
A peaceful village! It is a summer night.<68>

**Summary**

This poem by Chang Man-yŏng depicts a serene and idyllic summer night in a Korean village. It evokes a sense of peace through imagery of the starry sky, the gentle breeze, women working at traditional crafts like fulling cloth, burning mugwort to repel mosquitoes, and children falling asleep to grandmother's stories. For historians, the poem offers a glimpse into the idealized depiction of traditional rural life during the colonial period. It highlights specific cultural practices (mugwort burning, cloth fulling, storytelling) and showcases the Korean language usage and orthography of the early 1930s, potentially reflecting a desire to preserve or celebrate Korean culture amidst external pressures. The absence of overt references to the colonial situation itself might also be significant, suggesting either a focus on timeless aspects of life or a form of subtle escapism.

마을의 여름밤
						張萬榮
여름의 밤이외다.
녀름하늘은 높고 깜하외다.
쎄삐아빛 하날에는 작은 별들이
쏟아질 듯 이곳 저곳 헽어저 반듯입니다.

선들선들 바람이 나무닙을 흔들 때외다.
앞뜰에는 안악네들이 장석을 깔고
다드미질 하기에 분주하외다.
아 여름의 밤이외다.

모기쑥이 뭉게뭉게 피여오르고
아해들은 할머니의 옛이아기에
그대로 잠이 들어 콜콜거리는
평화로운 마을! 여름의 밤이외다.＜68＞
