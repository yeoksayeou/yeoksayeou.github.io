Clutching a Handful of Soil
						Kim Soyŏp

Having shed blood and sweat the whole year round,
All that remains in autumn is the soil scattered on desolate, empty feet—
Sowing seeds on a warm spring day full of hope,
Applying fertilizer and weeding the slash-and-burn field (화전) under the scorching sun,
How could I have known that on this late autumn day today, I would come holding just a handful of soil!

For whom did I till the fields?
For whom did I shed blood and sweat?
Father, weeping, clutching a handful of soil on a high autumn day!
The grains that covered this wide field yellow, the waves of gold,
Into whose warehouse have they been snugly stored away!

On the field as the sun sets, Father weeps alone, clutching the soil.
Caw, caw, the crows, fearing the coming winter, head south...
(Written 1932.2.5) <355> <77>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Soyŏp depicts the profound despair of a Korean farmer in the autumn of 1932. Despite a year of arduous labor—plowing, sowing, fertilizing, and weeding—the farmer finds himself left with nothing but a handful of soil, symbolizing his dispossession and poverty. He laments the loss of the harvest, questioning who ultimately benefited from his toil while he is left weeping in the empty field as winter approaches. For historians, the poem offers a poignant glimpse into the harsh realities faced by tenant farmers under Japanese colonial rule, highlighting themes of exploitation, the extraction of agricultural wealth, and the resulting sense of hopelessness and injustice prevalent in rural Korea during that period.

흙 한줌 쥐고
						金沼葉
온 일년 피땀 흘리고,
가을에 남은 것은 쓸쓸한 빈발에 흩어진 흙뿐―
희망이 가득 찬 따스한 봄날에 씨를 뿌리고
불볓이 나려 쪼이는 화전에 거름을 주고 김을 맬 때
어찌 늦은 가을 오늘에 한 줌의 흙을 쥐고 올 줄을 알엇스랴!

누구를 위해 발을 갈엇고,
누구를 위해 피땀을 흘렷노?
하늘 높은 가을 날 한 줌의 흙을 쥐고 우는 아버지!
이 넓은 벌판에 누렇게 깔렷던 나달은, 황금의 파도는
누구네 창고로 알쭌히 담어 갓나!

해지는 벌판 우에서 아버지 혼자 흙을 쥐고 우네.
까아까아 갈가마기는 오는 겨울이 무섭다고 남쪽으로 가는데...
(1932.2.5 作) ＜355＞ ＜77＞
