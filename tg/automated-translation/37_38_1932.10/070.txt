Article Type: 소식 (News/Information)

Ch'ŏndogyo Kyŏngdogwanjŏng and Chidogwanjŏng Yi Chong-nin
Pen name Hwangsang (凰山), also Poam (普庵). Born February 12, 1883.
Original Domicile: Sŏsan County (瑞山郡), South Ch'ungch'ŏng Province (忠南). Current Residence: 175 Sŏngbuk-ri (城北里), Koyang County (高陽郡).
Father: Farmer. Eldest son. Married at age 17. Wife: Pak Chŏng-ja (朴貞子), 34. Two sons, one daughter.

「Education」 1902: Studied the Six Styles of Technical Writing (工科文六體).
1904: Studied composition under Yi Sŏl (李偰) and Kim Pok-han (金福漢).
1908: Entered Sŏnggyun'gwan (成均館) to study Classical Studies (經學).
1908: Sŏnggyun'gwan Doctorate (成均博士).

(Experience) 1911: Chief Editor (主筆), Taehan Hyŏphoe Hoebo (大韓協會會報).
1912: One of the founders (創立者) of the Taehan Minbo (大韓民報).
1913: Entered Ch'ŏndogyo (천도교) and became Manager (主幹) of the Ch'ŏndogyo Wŏlbo (天道敎月報).
1923: Published the XX Newspaper (XX신문) up to issue 7, then imprisoned for 3 years. After release, managed magazines such as Chosŏn Mulssan Changnyŏhoe (朝鮮物産獎勵會), Minuhoe (民友會), Mumyŏnghoe (無名會) (Advocacy for Freedom of the Press - 言論權威 伸長), Kaebyŏk (開闢), Sin Yŏsŏng (新女性), Ŏrini (어린이), Sanŏpkye (産業界), Changsan (獎産), Chung Sŏng (衆聲).

Organizational Affiliations. (Past) Chief Editor, Taehan Hyŏphoe Hoebo; Chief Editor, Taehan Minbo; President, Ch'ŏndogyohoe Wŏlbo Sa (天道敎會月報 社); President, Kaebyŏk Sa (開闢 社); Chairman, Mumyŏnghoe; Chairman, Chosŏn Reporters' Convention Committee (朝鮮記者大會委員長); Chairman, People's Convention Committee (民衆大會委員長); Chairman, Sin'ganhoe Kyŏngsŏng Branch (新幹會京城支會委員長). (Present) Ch'ŏndogyo Kyŏngdogwanjŏng (敬道觀正) and Chidogwanjŏng (知道觀正); President, Tongilbo Sa (同日報社長); Chairman of the Board, Chosŏn Mulssan Changnyŏhoe (朝鮮物産獎勵會 理事長).

Works: Monghak Ich'ŏnja (蒙學二千字), Monghak P'ilsŭp (蒙學必習), Munjang Ch'ebŏp (文章體法), Sinp'yŏn Todŏkkyŏng Yŏk (新編道德經譯), Sinp'yŏn P'ilsŏ Yŏk (新編必書譯), Man'ganghong (滿江紅) (creative work), Hongnuji (紅淚地) (creative work), Insaeng kwa Sinang (인생과 신앙), Siksaruji P'yŏnjipchung (式辭類聚 編輯中), Aedok Kan'i Chip (愛讀簡易集). Favorite readings: Ch'oe Ch'ŏng (崔呈), Laozi Zhuangzi (老子壯子), Sun Wu Ping Fa (孫吳兵書). Hobby: Smoking (喫煙).

**Summary**

This article provides a concise biography of Yi Chong-nin (李鍾麟), a prominent figure in early 20th-century Korea, highlighting his dual leadership roles within the Ch'ŏndogyo religion (Kyŏngdogwanjŏng and Chidogwanjŏng). It details his background, education in classical and technical studies, extensive career in journalism (founding newspapers, editing magazines), involvement in nationalist and cultural organizations (Taehan Hyŏphoe, Chosŏn Mulssan Changnyŏhoe, Sin'ganhoe), and a three-year imprisonment related to his publishing activities. The article also lists his published works, readings, and personal details. For historians, this profile is valuable as it showcases the interconnectedness of religion (Ch'ŏndogyo), journalism, nationalism (Sin'ganhoe, 物産獎勵會), and political dissent (imprisonment) during the Japanese colonial period, embodied in a single influential individual. His leadership positions in both religious and secular organizations, particularly those promoting Korean products and press freedom, illustrate the multifaceted nature of Korean resistance and cultural preservation efforts.
