Magazine Title: 동광 제38호
Publication Date: 1932년 10월 01일
Article Title: 새벽 하늘 (Dawn Sky)
Author: 孫貞華
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Redly, redly, the burning dawn sky
Also ignites in the youth's heart.
The crackling, crackling, rising bellows fire.
It is a fire that cannot be extinguished.

In the sky where dawn breaks at daybreak,
Brilliant light stretches and flows.
Ah, it is an unspeakably great dawn sky.

The youth who climbed the mountain
Looks upon the firelight that seems to want to burn the sleeping streets
And knew the flames arisen in their own heart were redder. (33.7.28) <69>

**Summary**

This poem, "Dawn Sky," uses the vivid imagery of a burning red dawn to symbolize the passionate, inextinguishable spirit within youth. The speaker compares this inner feeling to a bellows fire and suggests it surpasses even the brilliance of the dawn itself, especially when observing a "sleeping" city. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period (published 1932, potentially composed 1933), could be interpreted allegorically. The dawn might symbolize hope for a new beginning or national liberation, the "sleeping streets" the dormant state of the nation under colonial rule, and the youth's inner fire the burgeoning nationalist fervor or desire for change that surpasses even the promise of dawn. Historians might find the intense, fiery imagery directed towards youth and juxtaposed with a "sleeping" city indicative of the era's underlying tensions and aspirations for awakening.

붉게, 붉게, 불타는 새벽 하늘은

이륵, 이륵, 이러나는 풀무불을.

새벽녁 동트는 하늘에는

잠든 거리를 불태우려는 불빛을 바라보고
자기의 가슴에 이러난 불길이 더 붉은 것을 알엇다. (33.7.28) ＜69＞
