The Sensation of Drizzling Rain
						By Yi Ŭngsu

A drizzling rain,
giving such a tickling sensation to the face,
fills the sky.
It is a summer night...
Friend who scolds romance and sentimentality,
let me enjoy this rain just for tonight.

Why does the world's confusion trouble my mind so?
What tangled lines (亂線) of emotion
make my face flush so hot?

Oh, the tickling sensation of the drizzling rain...
My blood-rushed face gradually cools,
and it seems the boiling within my skull slightly subsides.
Oh, the affectionate whisper of the pitch-dark night!
My clothes grow pitifully damp, and upon my head
the sweet dew (甘露) of the cosmos settles heavily. (End) <77>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Ŭngsu expresses the speaker's desire for solace amidst personal turmoil and worldly confusion. On a summer night, the speaker embraces the physical sensation of a gentle drizzle, finding it soothing to their "hot face" and "boiling mind." They ask a critical friend to allow them this moment of sentimental escape. The poem contrasts internal agitation with the calming influence of nature. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, offers a glimpse into personal introspection and the seeking of refuge in nature as an escape from possibly oppressive external realities or the general "confusion" of the times, even if not explicitly political. The focus on individual feeling and sensory experience might be seen as a quiet form of resistance or simply a coping mechanism in a restrictive era.

보슬비의 感觸
						李應洙
몹시도 얼골에 간지러운 감촉을 주는
보슬비가 하눌에 차 잇습니다.
때는 여름밤이고...
『로맨』과 감상을 叱責하는 친구여
오늘밤만 내게 저 비를 즐기게 하여 달라.

세상의 혼란은 이다지도 내 뇌를 괴롭힘니까.
무슨 감정의 亂線이
이다지도 내 얼골을 확근거리게 합니까.

간지러운 보슬비의 감촉이어...
血潮진 내 얼골은 차차 시켜가고
끓든 골속이 조금 식기는 것 같습니다.
컴컴한 밤의 다정스러운 속삭이이어!
옷은 측은이 젖어오고 머리에는
우주의 甘露가 듬슥이 내려 앉엇습니다. (끝) ＜77＞
