Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Early Autumn in the City Streets
						Kim Si-ch’ang

An advertisement received on Chongno Street—
What’s this again. Haha. Buying Gold Ingots (金塊) at High Prices (高價買入).

Bear with it, bear with it. As for the flowerpots,
The flowers won't live even a few days.

In a dusky corner,
Young lumpen lads spat at a schoolgirl.

An old gentleman, smoking in the cushion of his private car, was running away somewhere.

An opium addict staggering along in thin clothes.
The apron of a waitress kicking the door of a cabaret (酒場).
The neon flickers (Oh—like the veins of a sick person (病子))
Jazz dies— <68>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Si-ch’ang depicts scenes from the streets of Seoul (specifically Chongno) in early autumn, 1932. It captures fragmented images of urban life: blatant advertisements for buying gold, fleeting beauty (flowerpots), street harassment, the wealthy escaping in cars, addiction, and the nightlife represented by cabarets, waitresses, neon lights, and fading jazz music. The poem juxtaposes elements of modernity and commerce with social decay and weariness, creating a melancholic atmosphere. For historians, the poem offers a glimpse into the social fabric of colonial Seoul, showing the stark contrasts between wealth and poverty, the presence of Western cultural imports like jazz and cabarets alongside social problems like addiction and harassment, and the general mood of a city undergoing rapid, perhaps unsettling, changes under colonial rule.

市井初秋
						金時昌
종로 거리서 맏은 광고―
이게 또 뭐야. 하하. 金塊를 高價買入이라.

아스시오 참으시요. 화분일랑
몇 날 꽃이 살껏도 못 되오니.

어둑시근한 모통이에서
젊은 룸펜군들이 여학생에게 침을 뱉엇다.

녕감마님은 자가용의 큿숀에서 담배를 피우며 어데인가 다러나고 잇엇다.

훗옷을 입고 들먹이며 가는 아편쟁이.
카바레(酒場)의 문짝을 차는
웨이트레스의 에푸론.
네온은 끔뻑이고(오―病子의 靜脈과 같이)
쟈즈는 죽고― ＜68＞
