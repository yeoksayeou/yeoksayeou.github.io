A New Detector

If someone says a new detector has been invented, you might laugh and say, "Oh, another one!" It has become a habit to tinker this way and that with the same type of detector and sell it as new, so even when called a new detector, people don't believe it. However, the detector mentioned here is completely different from those that have existed until now.

First, what is a detector? It is a machine that inspects the radio waves of wireless telephony. Radio waves have alternating current (交流) oscillations, where the wave goes down once and then up once, alternating. However, the receiver (수신긔) that receives wireless telephone signals is different; it only receives waves going in one direction to achieve reception. To change alternating current (交流) to direct current (直流), a detector must be used to block the current flowing in one direction. Beginners in radio (라듸오) all know that machines performing this function include the coherer (코히러), the crystal detector (광석 검파긔), and the vacuum tube (진공관).

Now, the newly emerged detector is completely different. Copper oxide (酸化銅) is spread on a piece of copper, and then a piece of lead (鉛) is placed on top, after which it is tightly fastened with a screw (라사 못). The copper oxide is made by heating the copper sheet (銅片) in the air, allowing the air to oxidize the copper. After this is done, the copper oxide on one side is scraped off with a file. When tightening with the screw, it is, of course, necessary to insulate the copper and lead with a non-conductor (不導體).

This detector was presented by a person named L.O. Grondahl (L.O.그란들) at the American Physical Society (미국 물리학회). Its distinctive feature, unlike other detectors, is that it operates even at very low voltages and also works with very small currents.

Not only that, but an interesting point is that it might work not only with copper and lead but also with other metals. Therefore, those with an interest in invention may experiment in various ways. Just keep in mind as a principle <45> that it uses two kinds of metal and one kind of metal oxide.

Machines and Farming

[Image Caption] Radish (무우) pulling machine: This machine pulls, trims, and stacks radishes by itself.

An era has come where farming is done from seeding to harvest without a single human hand touching the crops. Moreover, the amount of farming one person can do has increased manifold, and it seems the day is coming when what one person used to plow in two days can be done in ten or twenty days.

The land is America (미국), and the tools are steam (증긔) and gas engine vehicles (까스 긔관차). There's an old Korean saying about a puppy that digs with its front paws, sows with its back paws, and covers with its tail, but the steam motor-driven "Universal" cultivator (유니버살 경작긔), although it can't sow seeds, first turns over the soil with a large spade, and then rotating hoes underneath break it up smoothly, creating a perfect field (바틀). The most developed are the harvesting machines (추수하는 긔게); while cutting the grain, they simultaneously thresh it, putting it either into sacks or storing it in tanks. Even pulling this through the field is now done by automobile. The leftover straw (지프) after threshing was originally a headache for harvesters, but recently, harvesting machines used in the Kansas (칸사스) region are equipped with machines to spread the straw and machines to screen it. By applying such machines, immense labor is saved, and agricultural efficiency is increased. <46>

**Summary**

The text consists of two main parts. The first introduces a new type of radio wave detector invented by L.O. Grondahl, utilizing copper, copper oxide, and lead. It highlights its novelty compared to existing coherers, crystal detectors, and vacuum tubes, emphasizing its ability to function at low voltages and currents. The second part discusses the rapid mechanization of agriculture, particularly citing examples from America (the "Universal" cultivator, advanced harvesters used in Kansas). It marvels at how steam and gas engines are replacing manual labor, drastically increasing farming efficiency from plowing to harvesting. For colonial history, these articles reveal a keen interest among Koreans in contemporary Western technological advancements in both electronics (radio) and heavy industry (agricultural machinery) during the 1920s. They reflect an awareness of global trends and implicitly contrast modern, efficient methods (associated with America) with potentially less developed local practices, showcasing the era's aspirations and the perceived gap in technological development.

새로운 검파긔
새로운 검파긔가 발명되엇다하면 『응 또 낫구먼!』하고 웃으시리다. 가튼 타입의 검파긔를 가지고 이리 뜻고 저리 뜻어 새것이라고 파는 것이 습관이 되어서 새로운 검파긔라 하여도 밋지를 안케 된 것이오. 그러나 여긔 말하는 검파긔는 지금까지 잇던 것과는 아주 딴판이오.
대체 검파긔가 무엇인가. 무선 전화의 전파를 검사하는 긔게니 무선 전파는 그 파동이 교류(交流)가 되어 파동이 한 번은 나려가고 한 번은 올라가 서로 엇박귀게 되는데, 무선 전화를 밧는 수신긔는 이와 달라 오직 한 편으로 가는 파동을 바다야 수신이 되오. 교류(交流)를 직류(直流)로 변하는대는 검파긔를 사용하여 한 편으로 흐르는 전류는 막아버려야 됨니다. 이런 일을 하는 긔게로 「코히러」도 잇고 광석 검파긔도 잇고 진공관도 잇는 것은 「라듸오」의 초학자는 다 아는 것이오.
인제 새로 난 검파긔는 그와 아주 달라 구리조각 우에 산화동(酸化銅)을 펴고 또 그 우에 납(鉛) 조각을 노흔 후 라사 못으로 꽉 잡아맨 것이오. 산화동은 그 동편(銅片)을 불에 달와서 공긔로 하여금 동을 산화하게 하여 만들고 그러케 된 뒤에 한 편은 줄로 산화동을 쓸어버림니다. 라사못으로 조일 때는 물론 동과 납에서 부도체(不導體)로 절연을 시킴이 필요하오.
새 검파기의 구조
은칠한곳은 절연한 부도체요 점은 것은 산화동이라

이 검파긔는 「L.O.그란들」이란 이가 미국 물리학회에서 발표한 것인데 그 특색은 다른 검파긔와 달라 아주 나즌 전압에서라도 작용하며 또 아주 적은 전류에서도 되는 것이오.
그 뿐 아니라 재미잇는 일은 비단 동이나 납뿐 아니라 혹 다른 금속으로 하여도 될 수 잇스니 발명에 취미를 가진 사람은 여러가지로 실험하는 것이 가하오. 다만 원측으로＜45＞ 마음에 두어야 할 것은 두 가지 금속과 한 가지 산화 금속을 쓰는 것이오.
