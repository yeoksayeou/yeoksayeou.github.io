Denmark's Rural Villages and Education, The Great Achievement of Mr. Grundtvig, Implementer of National Education in a Tiny Island Nation of Northern Europe, an Earthly Paradise
						By Pang Tae-yŏng

Comparing Denmark (丁抹) to Chosŏn (Korea), its area is one-fifth, and its population one-sixth. It is also a country with extremely few natural endowments; the entire nation, formed of over 500 islands, is almost entirely barren, poor soil. Not only that, but dense fog fills the air for over 90 days a year, and the average number of perfectly clear days seen is merely over 50 per year, so livestock must be raised indoors in barns (廐舍) for nine months of the year.

About 50 years ago, when Denmark faced extraordinary political and social difficulties, a great man named Mr. Grundtvig (그란드 윗치) emerged. He advocated for an astonishing system of Folk High Schools (國民高等學校) to provide vocational preparation for the young men and women residing in rural villages, fundamentally restoring the nation's fortunes. As a result, the level of national wisdom and virtue (國民智德) became among the highest in the world, and today, this small Nordic nation has earned the honor of being Europe's leading producer. This is because its products are distributed in markets worldwide as the most trusted, high-quality goods. Therefore, the Western powers are modeling their systems after it, striving to reform their own rural education systems based on the Folk High School. I (余) am originally neither a farm manager nor an educator. However, my concern (憂心) for our Korean people is second to none. After reading the Danish inspection report by Dr. Harold W. Foght (하를드 따불유 포드), an expert in rural education dispatched by the US government to research Danish rural areas, I first came to admire Denmark. Further interest was sparked by other books on Denmark, leading me to firmly believe that the permanent path to survival (活路) for our Korean people, currently cornered, lies solely in learning from Denmark.

Thus, when I explained my decision to my respected teachers and close friends (恩師知己), I fortunately received great agreement and support (贊助). Despite being a layman (門外漢) in both rural matters and education, I left Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) on May 25, 1926, crossed Siberia (西伯利亞), passed through Moscow (莫斯科) and Riga (리가), glimpsed the small nations of Latvia (라트비아) and Lithuania (리트아니아), crossed the Baltic (빨틱) Sea, and traveled to inspect various regions of my destination, Denmark.

While a layman's brief research investigation will inevitably have many unsatisfactory points, if among the readers I can fortunately find individuals who, recalling the traces of sweat (汗) and blood (血) from the Danish people's desperate struggle (奮鬪) to cherish (愛護) their natural resources amidst the rough waves of the North Sea (北海), thereby restoring their national fortune to become preeminent (冠) in the world today, and are inspired (觸) by that spirit to rise up (蹶起) in sympathy (共鳴) for our Chosŏn rural development movement, it would be a happiness for our Chosŏn.

Denmark, One-Sixth the Size of Chosŏn

Amidst the rough waves and fierce sea winds of the Baltic Sea (빨틕 海) in Northern Europe (北歐), the Jutland (유틀란드) peninsula (also known as Little Scandinavia (小스칸듸나비아)) protrudes from North Prussia (北普魯西), along with three main islands and countless, tiny islands like scattered sesame seeds, over 500 of them, floating and sinking like a cluster of stars in the night sky. This is Denmark, the world's model agricultural nation, regulating Europe's food supply. The total area of the mainland is 43,017 square kilometers (2,790 square *pangri* (方里)), about one-fifth the size of our Chosŏn (14,312 square *pangri*). Its population is 3.39 million; if we consider Chosŏn's population to be 20 million, it is a small country less than one-sixth the size of Chosŏn.

Looking at the topography, the Jutland peninsula protrudes from the Schleswig-Holstein (시레스윗츠, 홀스다인) region of Germany's northern territory, connected to the European continent. The main territories include Zealand (셀란드) island where the capital Copenhagen (코펜하-겐) is located, Funen (펜), Lolland-Falster (롤란드팔스타-), and Bornholm (뽄홈) islands in the center. Additionally, the Faroe (페-아오) Islands and Greenland (그린란드) are in the northern ocean (北洋), and Iceland (아이슬란드) is far away, serving as its sole colony. It is an archipelagic nation formed from countless, over 500 islands windingly inletted (蜿蜒히 만입된 곳에) in the North Atlantic (북대양). However, several of these islands are sandbars (沙洲) with no economic value. In short (要컨대), Denmark belongs to the low-lying region at the northern end of Northern Europe (北歐羅巴), with an altitude of only about 95 feet (呎), and the severity of its land uplift is unparalleled in the world. Its so-called colony, Greenland, near the North Pole, has a large area of 80,000 square *pangri*, but most of it is perpetually covered in pure white snow (白雪이 皚皚) and unusable barren land, so its population is merely 13,000. Also, the islands of St. Thomas (센트로마스) and St. John (센트긔욘) in the West Indies (西印度) were reportedly ceded (讓與) to the United States of America (北米合衆國) in 1917 for about 50 million dollars. As a result of the Treaty of Versailles (벨사유 강화조약), the 398,000 *chōbo* (町步) of the Schleswig peninsula, previously taken by Germany, were returned to Denmark. About 75% of the total area, roughly 3.2 million *chōbo*, is farmland (農耕地); 9% is barren land (不毛地), namely heath (히-스), wetlands (濕地), and sandy areas (沙地); 9% is forest zone (三林地帶); and the rest belongs to urban areas (市街), parks (公園), and roads (道路).

Denmark, Lacking Natural Blessings

Denmark's climate belongs to the cold zone, but due to the moderating effect of the maritime climate, there isn't extremely fierce cold air. Looking at the temperature, it ranges from 5-6 to 8 degrees Celsius nationwide. Winter is minus 5 to 1 degree Celsius, Spring is 4-5 to 6 degrees Celsius, Summer is 14.5 to 16 degrees Celsius, and Autumn is 7 to 9 degrees Celsius. Wind blows unceasingly (間斷업시) from the ocean, so cold and gloomy northwest winds are common.

The annual rainfall is 615 millimeters. Dense fog occurs 90 to 100 days a year. Even in the capital, Copenhagen, said to be most blessed by nature (天惠), one cannot see sunlight (日光) throughout the year as in our Chosŏn; aggregating the whole year, only 60-70 days are clear (晴天). Therefore, considerable difficulty is felt in cultivating crops, and livestock are raised indoors for 7-8 months of the year. During the summer months of June and July, the daytime (晝間) is longest. Around June 23rd or 24th each year, sunrise is at 2:30 AM, and sunset is around 1:00 AM the next day, presenting the spectacle (奇觀) of nighttime being barely 1.5 hours long. Furthermore, it is said to be no exaggeration that the Scandinavian (스칸듸나비아) peninsula has <29> virtually no night during summer, it is so short.

The Land is Infertile and the Climate Unfavorable

The soil quality is mostly light soil (輕質土), and its fertility (地味) is extremely poor (瘦瘠). The topsoil (地殼) is glacial clay (堆石粘土) mixed (混) with calcareous (石灰質) gravel (礫石). The northern region, in particular, has many sand dunes (砂山) and peat bogs (泥炭地). The area of these peat bogs is about 12.5 million square kilometers (this unit seems incorrect, likely meant smaller units or a typo), about half of which is excavated (採掘) and supplied (供) for fuel (燃料).

Originally, Denmark is a great wilderness (大荒蕪地) formed during the Ice Age (氷河時代). Coal (石炭) and swamps/marshes (沼澤) are scattered (散在) across all its islands. Liffe (?) island (리프도) is said to have risen from the sea level to become an island, and deposited sandy soil surrounds all the seabeds of northern Denmark.
In short, the entire land of Denmark can only be described in one word as unfortunate (박복한) land, not blessed by nature. Therefore, anyone would be surprised that Denmark, with such poor soil and unfavorable climate, has become a model agricultural nation yielding excessive harvests. However, it is said that the Danish people, who received no favor regarding natural blessings or soil quality, conquered this nature, united nationwide (擧國一致) to utilize science, added their blood and sweat (血汗) to it, and achieved this solely through strenuous effort (力行). Human power (人力) flies through the air and dives into the sea; truly, there is nothing fearsome in the world. Compared to this, we Korean people are a nation that has received many heavenly blessings (天惠). The mineral veins (鑛脉) in the northwestern region, the fish and shellfish (魚貝) of the East, South, and West seas, and the fertile soil (沃土) of the three southern provinces – truly abundant resources (財源). We should not just lament (恨) that they are not in our hands now, but follow the wide-ranging principles of science and expect results from unceasing effort, shedding blood and sweat. We Korean people must surely take Denmark as our teacher (스승) and radiate (放射) light (光明) ourselves.

Danish Education System
Characteristics of the Folk High School

Education employs compulsory education (義務敎育), implemented since 1839, with the compulsory age range being from 7 to 13 years old. Elementary education (初等敎育) highly respects the child's freedom (自由), decidedly avoids uniform (劃一的) education, and trusts the character of the school principal, leaving matters to them. Therefore, even among elementary schools (小學校), the standards vary. The children of Colonel Tyussŭ (듀쓰 大佐), where I was boarding (寄宿), also commuted to a private elementary school (私立小學校) far away, despite many elementary schools being nearby. In most elementary schools, the majority (多大數) of teachers (敎員) are male (男敎員); female teachers (女敎員) are extremely few. The management (管理) of elementary schools falls to the village/neighborhood school board members (洞里 學務委員). These members are usually recommended by city or village/neighborhood representatives (洞里總代), and it is common for the village church (敎會) pastor (牧師) to hold the position concurrently (兼任). Half of the elementary school teachers' salaries (給料) is paid (支辨) by the village/neighborhood, and half by the national treasury (國庫). The number of elementary school teachers is one in small places but increases according to the local area size. In local village schools (地方 村落 學校), teaching occurs 3 days a week; in county/town schools (郡邑 學校), 5 days a week. The hours are over 21 hours in county/towns and 18 hours in villages. If a child is absent without reason (無故히 缺席), a fine (罰金) is imposed (課), showing how strict compulsory education is.

Although an increase is seen like this, the vast majority (大部分) are accommodated in public elementary schools (공립소학교), and non-enrolled children (不就學 兒童) numbered only 643. By 1922, this decreased to 370, showing that compulsory education has been fully disseminated (보급).

Since tuition fees (受業料) are collected (徵收) only in private elementary schools, wealthy farmers (富有한 豪農) worry (杞憂) that the free teaching (無料敎授) in public schools might be unkind (不親切) towards children and send them to private schools. Therefore, private elementary schools tend to have many children from relatively wealthy families, giving an aristocratic (귀족적) feel. Besides this (이 밧게), there are also several thousand children taught at home (自家) by wealthy people (富豪) who specially hire (雇聘) teachers.

General Danish people have an extraordinarily high regard (觀念이 비상히 만흠) for education, so violators of compulsory education laws are reportedly rare. The number of elementary schools nationwide is 4,509, of which 34 are national (國立), 3,825 are county/township/village-run (郡面洞立), and 650 are private (私立). When parents wish to transfer (轉校) their child from one school, they must notify (通知) the relevant school district (該校區) one week in advance and simultaneously notify the authorities (官憲) of the place they are moving to (移住). Such a strict child tracking method (嚴密한 아동 追跡方法) has produced outstanding results (卓越한 成績) for compulsory education. Also, as mentioned earlier, when a child is absent, the teacher investigates the reason (內容). If there is no reason according to the regulations (規則上), it is passed to the school board (學務위원회) for thorough investigation. If it is found to be due to the child's habitual negligence (習慣的 怠慢) or the parents' responsibility (責任), the following fines are imposed on the parents (兩親) or guardians (保護者):
First month after enrollment: 1 day absence = 12 øre (우오) (1 øre is our 5 *rin* (厘))
Second month after enrollment: 1 day absence = 25 øre
Third month after enrollment: 1 day absence = 50 øre
Fourth month after enrollment: 1 day absence = 1 krone (크로나) (50 *jeon* (전))

The committee (위원회) and school collect these fines without the slightest neglect (조곰도 태만히 하지 아니하고) and with utmost strictness to absolutely instill the child's responsibility and the duty of compulsory education, and to cultivate a national character (國民性) that prevents absence without permission (無斷히 休業하지 못하게).

Middle school (중학교) is attended by children aged 11 to 15, with a study term (受業期限) of 4 years. The language course (語學科) teaches English, German, and Swedish (瑞典語). There are 97 provincial (?) middle schools (주립 중학교) and 404 private middle schools (사립 중학교). Middle school graduates enter high school (고등학교); the ages 17 and 19 are most numerous. Languages taught include Swedish and French (佛語), and advanced mathematics (高等數學) is also assigned. There are 34 national high schools (국립), 11 provincial (?) (주립), and 9 private (사립). These high schools serve as preparation for university entrance, similar to Japan's (日本) school system (學校制度). <31>
The entrance examination system (입학시험제도) first exists when completing high school and entering university, which differs from our East Asia (東洋).

There is only one university (대학교) in Copenhagen. This university has a considerably long history among European (구라파) universities, founded (創設) in 1478, and women can also enter freely (任意로). There are 60 professors (敎授) and over 40 other staff (職員) managing over 3,200 students (學生). Departments (學科) are divided into five: Theology (神學科), Science and Engineering (理工科), Medicine and Law (醫療法科), and Liberal Arts (文科). The university offers lectures very freely (極히 자유롭게 解放) with no tuition fees (無受業料). Degrees (學位) are conferred (授與) immediately after graduation, but those wishing to become specialists (專門家) must continue research (研究) for another 6-7 years and pass an examination (試驗).

Those unable to enter university can enroll in government-run specialized schools (官立 專門學校). There are 250 veterinary (獸醫), agriculture and forestry (農林), and industrial arts (工藝) specialized schools nationwide, with an enrollment of 25,000 students.

Schools with a specialized nature include 21 normal schools (師範學校) with 1,600 students, 90 commercial schools (商業學校) with 14,400 students, plus arithmetic schools (算術學校), industrial schools (工業學校), and many other private vocational schools (私立實業學校). And there are Denmark's famous Folk High Schools (國民高等學校) or equivalent agricultural schools (農業學校) and home economics schools (家庭學校).

Generally, these Folk High Schools are the great source (큰 源泉) that made Denmark's current industry prosperous (隆盛케한). I intend to write about this again in a separate section (別項), but they are generally private schools that provide supplementary education (補習敎育) to rural youth (農村青年) aged 18 to 25.
Youth who have graduated from elementary school and are engaged in practical farming (實施 農事) in rural areas, or laborers engaged in agriculture with considerable savings (貯蓄), receive education at these Folk High Schools. There are dormitories (寄宿舍) within the schools to accommodate students (生徒). During the winter term (冬期), generally from November to the end of March the following year, they live communally (共同生活) in the dormitories and receive moral education/discipline (訓育) from this principal (*淑中 - text seems corrupt here, likely meant 'principal'). During the summer term (夏期), women's courses (女子 講習) are opened for 3 or 5 months, teaching entirely practical subjects (實用的 學科). Besides that, various workshops/short courses (各種의 講習會) are opened as needed (臨機), all utilizing the farming off-season (農閑) in rural areas to cultivate practical general knowledge for citizens (國民常識).

Home economics schools (家庭學校) are established entirely for women (女子). There are 14 nationwide. Utilizing the Folk High Schools, students can enroll in two terms (期): November to March of the following year, and May to September. They also have dormitories to accommodate students from afar (遠方). The main subject is cooking (割烹科), and sewing (裁縫), etc., are taught. It is said that Danish home cooking methods (丁抹人의 家庭料理法) being the best (冠) in the world is due to this kind of education.

These Folk High Schools and home economics schools, during the short periods they teach in winter or summer, never just instill human intellect (人智) based on textbooks (敎科書). Rather, based on Danish history and literature (文學), they nurture the national character (國民性) from a religious perspective (宗敎方面), teach spiritual/mental learning (精神的 學問), and guide (指導) the learners (學習者) to awaken voluntarily (自發的으로 각성케하여) and embody/master (體得) a pure "Christian life" (크리스찬, 라이프). This is the main policy (大方針) of this education. Indeed, as a result, it has produced remarkable actual results (顯著한 實績) among the people and built the foundation (基礎) of today.
Opportunity often knocks on the door, but the foolish person doesn't know how to open it. (Danish Proverb (丁抹俗談)) <32>

**Summary**

This article by Pang Tae-yŏng expresses deep admiration for Denmark, highlighting how a small, resource-poor Nordic nation transformed itself into a prosperous agricultural powerhouse through national unity, hard work, and, crucially, education. Pang contrasts Denmark's harsh natural conditions with Korea's potential abundance, urging Koreans under Japanese rule to emulate the Danish spirit and particularly its Folk High School system, founded by N.F.S. Grundtvig. He details the Danish education system, emphasizing the practical, character-building, and nation-fostering aspects of the Folk High Schools, which offered supplementary education to rural youth during farming off-seasons, as the key to Denmark's success and a potential model for Korea's own development and path to survival.

From a colonial history perspective, the article is significant as it reflects a common trope among Korean intellectuals seeking models for national strengthening outside the Japanese colonial framework. Praising Denmark's self-reliance and educational achievements served as an indirect critique of Korea's subjugated state and a call for self-motivated national progress, focusing on rural development and education as tools for empowerment and fostering national identity. The specific focus on Grundtvig's Folk High Schools points to the search for alternative educational philosophies centered on national spirit and practical skills relevant to the largely agrarian Korean society.

丁抹農村及敎育, 北歐의 蕞爾一小島國로써 地上의 樂園 國民敎育實施者 그란드 윗치 氏의 偉業
						方台榮
丁抹을 조선에 비교대조하여 볼진대 면적으로 5분의 1이요. 인구로 6분의 1이요. 또 天惠的 禀受가 극히 적은 나라이니 5백 有餘의 島嶼로 성립된 전국은 거의 不毛에 가까운 惡土다. 뿐만 안이라 1년 중 90여일은 濃霧가 가득하여 완전한 晴天을 보는 날이 년평균 50여일에 지나지 못함으로 가축을 먹임에 년 9개월 동안은 廐舍내에서 기르는 상태다.
距今 50여 년 전에 정치적으로 사회적으로 비상한 곤란을 조우하엿슬 때에 丁抹에 한 위인 인「그란드 윗치」씨가 나서 농촌에 거주하는 남녀아동에게 직업적 준비를 공급하는 可驚할 제도의 국민고등학교를 제창하여 근본적으로 국운을 만회하니 그 결과로는 國民智德의 수준이 세계중에 가장 놉게 되어 現今은 北歐의 一小國에 구라파 제 1의 생산국인 영예를 博得하게 된 것이다. 곳 그 생산품이 세계 각 시장에 나가서 가장 신용잇는 우량품으로 分配되는 까닭이다. 그럼으로 구미 열강이 모범하여 그 국민고등학교를 본 바다. 자국의 농촌교육제도에 개혁을 행하고저 노력하고 잇는 중이다. 余는 본래 농사경영자도 아니요 또 교육자도 아니다. 다만 우리 조선민족에 대한 일편의 憂心은 남에게 뒤지지 안는 바이다. 당초 미국 정부로 부터 丁抹 농촌 조사연구에 派遣되엇던 농촌교육의 대가 「하를드 따불유 포드」 박사의 丁抹 시찰보고서를 讀하고 비롯오 丁抹을 동경하게 되엇스며 또 다른 丁抹에 관한 서적에 의하여 더욱 흥미를 가지게 되엇슴으로 現今 막다른 골목에 入한 우리 조선민족의 영구적 活路는 오직 丁抹을 배움에 잇다고 굿게 미덧던 바이다.
딸아서 余의 먹은바 마음을 恩師知己에게 訴하엿더니 다행히 큰 동의와 찬조를 힘입어 농촌에나 교육에나 다 일개의 문외한임을 不顧하고 1926년 5월 25일 경성을 떠나 西伯利亞를 횡단하고 莫斯科로부터 「리가」를 경유하여 라트비아, 리트아니아 각 소국을 瞥見하고 「빨틱」海를 渡하여 목적지 丁抹 각 지방을 시찰 여행하엿던 것이다.
문외한의 단적 연구조사가 본래부터 불만한 점이 만히 잇슴을 면치 못할 것이나 余는 독자 중 다행히 丁抹사람이 北海의 荒波 가온대에서 天然의 자원을 愛護함에 필사적으로 노력 분투한 결과 今日에 국운을 만회하여 세계에 冠코저하는 그 汗과 血의 治蹟을 상기하고 그 정신에 觸하여 우리 조선 농촌 진흥운동상 공명 궐기하는 人士를 得할 것 가트면 우리 조선을 위하야 행복이라 할 것이다.

조선의 6분 1되는 丁抹
北歐 「빨틕」海의 거칠은 파도와 사나운 해풍 가운대 北普魯西에서 돌출한 「유틀란드」 반도 (일명 小스칸듸나비아)와 3개의 島嶼 及 깨알가튼 5백여의 무수한 島嶼가 夜天의 별떨기가티 浮沈하엿다. 이 곳 세계의 모범적 농업국이요 歐洲의 식량을 조절하는 丁抹國이다. 본토의 총 면적이 43,017평방「킬로메터」 (2,790방리)니 우리 조선(14,312방리)에 비하여 약 5분지 1이요 인구는 339만이니 우리 조선의 인구를 2천만이라하면 조선보다 6분지 1도 채 못되는 한 적은 나라다.
地勢로조차 보건대 歐洲대륙에 接續한 독일 北領土 「시레스윗츠, 홀스다인」지방에 돌출한 「유틀란드」반도와 首府「코펜하-겐」이 잇는 「셀란드」도, 중앙에 잇는 「펜」 「롤란드팔스타-」도, 「뽄홈」도 등이 그 중 큰 領地요 그 외에 「페-아오」도 「그린란드」도가 北洋에 잇스며 「아이슬란드」도가 멀리잇서 그의 유일한 식민지가 되엇스며 또 북대양에 蜿蜒히 만입된 곳에 수업시 만흔 5백여의 島嶼로 성립된 집성도서국이나. 그 중에 몃 개 島嶼는 하등의 경제적 가치가 업는 沙洲니 요컨대 丁抹은 北歐羅巴 北端 저지역에 속하여 해발 약 95呎에 불과하며 그 지역의 隆起가 또한 세계에서 그 比를 보지못하게 심한 나라이다. 북극 가까이 잇는 그의 소위 식민지인 「그린란드」島는 그 면적이 8만방리나 되는 큰 면적을 가지엇스나 항상 白雪이 皚皚하여 小部分밧게는 이용되지 못하는 불모의 地임으로 주민이 1만3천에 지나지 못한다. 또 西印度에 잇는 「센트로마스, 센트긔욘」島는 1917년에 약 5천만불의 갑스로써 북미합중국에 讓與하엿다 한다. 벨사유 강화조약의 결과 옛날 독일에게 빼앗기엇던 「시레스윗츠」반도의 39만8천 町步는 丁抹에 회수되엇다. 전면적의 약 75% 즉 약 3백 2십만 町步는 농경지요 9%는 불모지니 곳 「히-스」 濕地 沙地가 그것이요 9%는 삼림지대며 기타는 시가, 공원, 도로에 속하엿다.

천연의 혜택을 입지못한 丁抹
丁抹의 기후는 한대에 속하엿스나 해양성 기후의 조절로 그다지 맹렬한 한기는 업다. 이제 그 온도를 보건대 전국을 통하여 섭씨 5, 6도로부터 8도 이내니 冬期는 섭씨 영하 5도로 내지 1도요 春期는 섭씨 4, 5도로 내지 6도요 夏期는 섭씨 14도 반으로 내지 16도요 秋期는 섭씨 7도로 내지 9도 間이다. 風은 해양으로부터 間斷업시 불어오는 고로 대개 한랭음울한 서북풍이 만타.
雨量은 1년간에 615 미리 米突이요 1년 중 90일 내지 100일은 濃霧가 襲來한다. 天惠的으로 가장 만흔 혜택을 입엇다는 수도 「코펜하-겐」도 우리 조선과 가티 日光을 1년 중 늘 볼 수는 업고 1년을 통산하여 겨우 6, 70일이 晴天이라한다. 그러함으로 농작물 배양상에 不少한 곤란을 느끼며 가축도 1년 중 7, 8개월은 廳舍내에서 사육한다. 夏期 중 6, 7 兩個月은 晝間이 가장 長한 때니 매년 6월 23, 4일경은 日出이 오전 2시 반이요 일몰이 또한 오후를 경과한 1시 경이니 곳 夜間이 겨우 1시 반에 지나지 못하는 奇觀을 呈한다. 더욱이 「스칸듸나비아」반도는＜29＞ 夏間에는 夜가 전혀 업다고 하여도 과언이 아니리만치 短하다한다.

토지는 瘠薄하고 氣候는 不順
토질은 대개 경질토로 地味가 극히 瘦瘠하다. 地殼의 토질은 堆石粘土에 石灰質을 多含한 礫石이 混하엿다. 특히 북부지방은 砂山과 泥炭地가 만타. 그 泥炭地의 면적이 약 1250만 「킬로미터」나 되는데 약 半分은 채굴하여 燃料에 供한다.
원래 丁抹은 빙하시대에 된 대황무지다. 석탄, 沼澤은 그 모든 島嶼에 산재하고 「리프」도는 해면에서 융기하여 도서로 된 것이라하며 또 퇴적한 砂土는 북부 丁抹의 모든 해저를 圍繞하엿다한다.
이제 丁抹 全土를 통틀어서 다만 一言으로써 천혜를 입지못한 박복한 토지라 할 수 밧게 업다. 그런즉 이러한 薄土와 불순한 기후를 가진 丁抹로써 모범적 농업국이 되고 過大한 收穫을 내인다함은 누구던지 놀라지 아니할 수 업슬 것이다. 그러나 天惠的으로나 土質上으로나 아무 귀여움을 밧지 못하게 된 丁抹民族은 이 자연을 정복하여서 擧國一致로 과학을 이용하고 이에 血汗을 더하여 오직 力行한 결과라 한다. 空中을 비행하고 海中을 잠행하는 人力, 참 세상에 무서운 것이 업다. 우리 조선 사람은 이에 비하면 天惠의 만흔 복을 바든 민족이다. 서북지방에 鑛脉과 東西南 3海의 魚貝와 3남 지방의 沃土, 과연 풍부한 財源이다. 이제 내 손에 업슴만 恨치 말고 광범한 과학의 원리에 조차서 오직 부단의 血汗을 뿌리어 노력의 결과를 기대할 것 아닌가. 우리 조선 사람은 반듯이 丁抹로 하여곰 스승을 삼아 광명을 스스로 放射하여야 할 것이다.

國民高等學校의 特色
교육은 의무교육을 쓰는데 1839년부터 실시되어 7세로부터 13세까지가 의무교육의 년한이다. 초등교육은 극히 아동의 자유를 존중히 하여 단연히 획일적 교육을 취하지 아니하고 또 그 학교 교장의 인물을 신임하여 맛기어 두는 고로 가튼 소학교라도 각각 그 정도가 다르다. 余가 기숙하여 잇던 듀쓰 大佐의 집 자녀들도 그 근처에 만흔 소학교가 잇슴에 불구하고 遠方에 잇는 사립소학교에 통학을 하게한다. 각 소학교에서 다대수의 교원은 대개 男교원이요 女교원은 극히 소수임에 지나지 못한다. 소학교의 관리는 洞里 학무위원이 맛게 되는데 그 학무위원은 대개 市 또는 洞里總代들의 추천으로 洞里 교회의 목사가 겸임하는 것이 보통이다. 그리고 소학교 교원 급료의 절반은 洞里로서 절반은 국고로서 支辨하며 소학교의 교원은 적은 곳에 1인이나 그 지방 면적에 딸아서 증가되며 지방 촌락 학교에서는 1주 3일간, 郡邑 학교에서는 1주 5일간을 敎授하고 그 시간은 郡邑 21시간 이상, 촌락은 18시간 이며 아동이 無故히 결석하면 벌금을 課한다하니 얼마나 의무교육의 엄격함을 알 수가 잇다.
