잡지명	동광 제15호
발행일	1927년 07월 05일
기사제목	길ㄴ밤 (Long Night)
필자	餘心
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Long Night
						Yŏsim (餘心)
Characters
Ponghun (봉훈): Around 40 years old (Aviator)
Chŏngsun (정순): Ponghun's wife
Yŏnghun (영훈): Ponghun's younger brother (Aviator)

Time: Contemporary
Place: A small village on the border of China (中國) and Russia (露西亞), not far from Harbin (할빈).

Act One
Stage: A bedroom decorated in Western style. On the right side of the room is an entrance door, and on the left is a wide window draped with white curtains. On the lower center of the opposite wall is a fireplace (火場 - 난로), and above it hangs a portrait of Lenin (레닌). On the right side of the wall hangs a map of Korea (朝鮮地圖) with red lines drawn here and there, and on the left side, two long hunting rifles (산양총) are crossed and hung. There is a desk with medicine bottles and books, plus a sofa, a couple of chairs, etc.
Chŏngsun is lying in bed below the window, and beside her, Ponghun sits on the sofa, gazing vacantly out the window.
Chŏngsun (Putting her arm out over the blanket): What are you looking at so intently? Oh, it's hot!
Ponghun: Spring has come. Spring has come. Look, there's a heat haze already.
Chŏngsun: Where? Help me sit up, please.
Ponghun: How is your body feeling?
Chŏngsun: Well, it's okay. Please help me sit up. Has spring come? Let me see the spring, please help me sit up.
Ponghun: Will it be alright for you to get up?
Chŏngsun: I feel much better today. It feels like I'm completely healed.
Ponghun helps Chŏngsun sit up, puts a fur coat on her, then gently lifts her, settles her carefully on the sofa, and pulls up a stool, sitting diagonally beside her.
Ponghun (Gazing intently at Chŏngsun): Chŏngsun's hair has fallen out so much. That hair! The hair that Ponghun fell in love with... Chŏngsun pays no attention to Ponghun's words and just looks out the window.
Chŏngsun: Look—over there, on that mountaintop, the snow is still there! As if it will never melt... That heat haze is shimmering, crawling over the white snow... Azaleas must be blooming in the homeland, and peach blossoms too...
Ponghun: The peach blossom season has already passed. Peach buds would have formed by now.
Chŏngsun: Is that so?... Ah! But I can smell peach blossoms like this!... <61> Once, when our little one broke off a branch of peach blossoms from the back hill garden, Father spanked him!
Ponghun: Why bring up old stories all of a sudden?
Chŏngsun: And another time, I went up the back mountain with Grandmother, and the azaleas were blooming so profusely. I ate so many that I got completely intoxicated. I was sick for several days after that.
Ponghun: Do you remember that time? That spring in P'yŏngyang (평양), when we were hiding in the back room of Elder Yi's house, you wanted to see azaleas so badly that I disguised myself, went out into the street, and bought flowers for you. You held those flowers and wouldn't let go?
Chŏngsun: That time was still joyful, wasn't it! Chirping like skylarks. And unaware of the world, but so passionate...
Ponghun: That was already eight years ago. Time flies so fast. But—
He looks up at the map hanging on the wall.
Chŏngsun: Still, childhood was better. When I was young, I loved flowers so much, but when I went to Seoul (서울) for middle school and all that, even when spring came, I didn't really appreciate flowers much. Dragged along by friends, I went to see the cherry blossoms in Uidong (우이동) and sometimes went to see the flowers at Ch'anggyŏngwŏn (창경원), but that was just for the fun of wandering around noisily, not really to see the flowers... Then, truly, when I was chased by that detective and couldn't even see the outside world, hiding for months, it was really suffocating. And how I longed to see flowers! Still, I saw azaleas for the last time then! Now, I'm afraid I might die without seeing azaleas even once more.
Ponghun: Again, again, saying such things.
Chŏngsun: Why, does it displease you to hear it? What will you do, Ponghun-ssi, when I die?
Ponghun: I'll die too.
Chŏngsun: Really?
Ponghun: Of course.
Chŏngsun: Ho ho, you fool.
Ponghun: Why?
No answer. A moment's silence.
Chŏngsun (In a voice as if lost in thought): How are all those kids doing now? Back then, we insulted each other, hated each other, and were jealous... That was all fun. If only I could see those kids just once more... Don't you remember sitting on the earthen floor (봉당), saying, "Polish the beans, let the tree color come out!" (콩닦아줄겐 나무색이 나라!), sweeping the dusty corner of the yard (마당귀ㅅ댁이) with the palm of your hand? Didn't you play like that when you were young, Ponghun-ssi?
Ponghun: Why not? Making kimchi with leaves (나무색이) and making sticky rice cakes (찰떡) with mud.
Chŏngsun: And putting mud pies (흙밥) on the bridge of our noses.
Ponghun: Ha, ha, ha, if only I had seen you back then, Chŏngsun, with your maiden pigtails dangling, making kimchi from weeds under a broken bowl shard!
Chŏngsun: And I often went with friends to dig wild chives (달레) in the common fields (보통벌). If we went out for a while, we'd dig a whole large basket (바구미) full. So, when returning in the evening, it was customary to give a handful to the boatman (배사공) instead of the fare (배선가).
Ponghun: Seeing you sit there talking about old times (녯소리), Chŏngsun, you look just like some fifty-year-old woman (노상한). <62>
Chŏngsun (In a faint voice): Why wouldn't I have aged!
Ponghun: The young grow old, the old die, and again the young grow old, and during that time, the world moves forward step by step! But when will we ever... He looks up at the map again.
Chŏngsun: Don't you want to fly just once, with the propeller roaring loudly over the skies of Seoul (서울 하눌)?
Ponghun (In a voice close to despair): When? What time ever?
They both sigh.
The curtain slowly falls as—
Chŏngsun: I hear the sound of a skylark singing! The scent of peach blossoms...

Act Two
Same room. It is night, and the window is completely covered by a thick curtain. The fire in the fireplace (火場) is burning brightly, and the electric lamp is covered with a bright shade, making the room reddish (빩앟다). Five o'clock in the morning.
Chŏngsun groans continuously. Ponghun sits on the stool beside Chŏngsun's bed, looking down at her.
Chŏngsun (Turning over): Still not sleeping (주시네)? Am I being too noisy (씩크럽지오)?
Ponghun: Don't worry about me, just steel your heart.
Chŏngsun: Life is truth. Death is also existence. It's useless to try to deceive reality and existence! I sense now that the time has come (이르럿다).
Ponghun: Please, steel your heart!
Chŏngsun: I am not afraid at all. Nor am I sad. Those who must go, must go!
Ponghun: You mean you'll go alone first, without even flying over the skies of Seoul? What fun will it be for me to fly around alone?
Chŏngsun: Well, thinking of that, I regret it too! ... But if I have a soul... ho ho, Ponghun-ssi, you deny the existence of the soul (령혼)... I don't know either. You'll probably laugh again, calling it woman's superstition, but if there happens to be such a thing as a soul, my soul will always follow (딸아) Ponghun-ssi. Wherever Ponghun-ssi goes, it will sit upon the wing of that airplane and protect Ponghun-ssi.
Ponghun: Chŏngsun! Chŏngsun!
Chŏngsun: Has Yŏnghun-ssi returned?
Ponghun (Looking up at the clock): Well, it seems things must have gone wrong. If things had gone right, he would have returned already. Even if he took the plane that returned in the middle of last night (재밤쫑), he should have been back by now!
Chŏngsun: Don't worry (념녀마세요). Success! Success! Why is it so difficult!... But listen. I believe Yŏnghun-ssi will definitely return successfully this time. As the proverb says, "A mouse tells you," I don't know why, but I have a firm belief that he will succeed. This belief, this 'confidence' (콘피덴쓰), cannot be doubted... I feel like I can hear (뜻는) the sound of the propeller piercing the dark sky right now.
Ponghun: Chŏngsun, don't get so excited!
Chŏngsun: How can I not be excited! Ponghun-ssi, the night was long. It was a lo-o-ong, lo-o-ong night. But after the long night, morning comes. The sun rises. It surely comes.
Ponghun: Please, don't get so excited.
Chŏngsun: I am joyful. The joy of staying awake all night nestled in Ponghun-ssi's arms <63> to greet (마지) the morning, this joy cannot be bought even with gold... Ponghun-ssi! Do you still love me? If so, come here and help me sit up and hold me, please? The sun is about to rise! I want to greet the sunrise in your arms!
Ponghun, with a despairing look, sits up on the bed, raises Chŏngsun's upper body, and holds her in his arms. He strokes her hair with one hand.
Chŏngsun: This hair! Ponghun-ssi, you said this hair was precious. Please cut (버히세요) this hair. Before you bury me, cut (버혀) this hair and spread it under the airplane seat. My hair will keep my love warm there and follow my love for thousands of miles, tens of thousands of miles, wherever you go.
Ponghun: Chŏngsun, why are you like this tonight...
Chŏngsun: Because the end (마그막) has come. Because the time for the lo-o-ong night to end has arrived! Whenever our wish is successfully fulfilled, please visit my grave just once. Come to my grave and tell me, 'Chŏngsun, you couldn't see it, but the X you believed in has been achieved. I have brought you azaleas now. Dance in the underworld. Dance for the twenty million brethren,' please tell me that...
Silence.
Chŏngsun: Hold me tighter, please. Has Yŏnghun-ssi arrived?
Ponghun: No, he hasn't come yet... Perhaps it all went wrong (모도 글었나 보오).
Chŏngsun: No! No! He will surely come. He cannot not come. Ah, I see it now. I see a sound, a light, breaking through the dark sky.... Look, Ponghun-ssi, look—there, morning is dawning (새임니다)..
Chŏngsun passes away (운명한다). Ponghun, in despair, shakes the body.
Ponghun: Chŏngsun, Chŏngsun!
He kisses the corpse, lays it down, covers the face (얼골) with a white sheet (홋이불), then steps aside and looks into (들어다 본다) the fireplace (火場).
Ponghun: Ah, this darkness! This despair! Why does that flame burn? ... The flame within my heart, why is this flame trying to die out now! Chŏngsun, Chŏngsun, after the long night, morning comes! Ah! Does morning truly come? Will sunlight enter my dark heart too?..
He collapses onto the sofa, burying his head in his hands.
Ponghun: Ah! I doubt... I doubt....
Silence.
The sound of a propeller is heard. Ponghun stands up as if crazed.
Ponghun: Is it true? Is it true!
The propeller sound grows louder. Ponghun rushes over and throws open (열어젓긴다) the black curtain that covered the window. The bri-i-ight (훠-s한) morning air rushes in (들이쏜다).
Ponghun (Spreading his arms): Ah, he came! He really came!
Yŏnghun's voice: Hyŏngnim, I'm back!
The first rays of the morning sun shine through the window, illuminating (빛외어) Chŏngsun's bed.
Ponghun (Turning to look at the bed): Chŏngsun, you were a prophet. You knew. Ah! Morning has truly come. The lo-o-ong, lo-o-ong night has passed forever! Going to Chŏngsun's bed, Ponghun says, Chŏngsun, look at the morning you believed in! Look at the sunlight! He reveals (들어 내인다) Chŏngsun's face.
The dazzling morning sunlight shines upon (들이 빛왼인다) Chŏngsun's peaceful face.
The door opens, and Yŏnghun enters, still in his flight suit.
Yŏnghun: Hyŏngnim, success... Mansae, Mansae!
Ponghun: Oh! Chŏngsun sees (보훈다) the morning too! Mansae!
The curtain falls. -End- <64>

**Summary**

The play "Long Night" depicts the final hours of Chŏngsun, the wife of an aviator named Ponghun, set in a village near the China-Russia border. While Chŏngsun lies dying, reminiscing about her past in Korea and longing for home, she and Ponghun anxiously await the return of Ponghun's brother, Yŏnghun, also an aviator, from a critical mission. Chŏngsun maintains faith in Yŏnghun's success and the coming of a metaphorical "morning" despite Ponghun's despair. She passes away just as dawn breaks, coinciding with the sound of Yŏnghun's returning plane. Yŏnghun enters announcing the mission's success, and Ponghun declares that Chŏngsun, too, witnesses the arrival of the long-awaited "morning." The play uses the metaphor of the long night ending and dawn arriving to symbolize hope for Korea's future, likely liberation or revolution, tied to the aviators' clandestine activities hinted at by the map, border setting, and the mission's secrecy. For historians, the play is interesting for its portrayal of exiled Korean activists (implied), the use of aviation in their efforts, the setting near the Soviet border (with Lenin's portrait suggesting ideological leanings or context), the strong nationalist undercurrent expressed through longing for Korea and the final triumphant "Mansae," and the blending of personal tragedy with national hope during the colonial period.

第一幕

정순(팔을 이불 우로 내노흐며) 무엇을 그리 내다보고 게서요? 에고 더워!」
