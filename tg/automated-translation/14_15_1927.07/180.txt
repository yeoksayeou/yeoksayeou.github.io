Severance Medical School Dean O Kŭng-sŏn
Pen name: Haegwan (海觀). Born October 4, Kae'guk 486 (1877). Born in Kongju.
Original Domicile: Kyŏngsŏng-bu, Chukt'am-jŏng 2-chōme 58. Current Residence: Same.
Father: Farmer. Wife: Yi Hyŏn-jin (李玄眞). Married 1891. Two sons, three daughters.

1909: Graduated from Kyŏngsŏng Paejae Haktang.
1902: Graduated from Central College.
1907: Graduated from the University of Louisville.
1908: Doctor at Kunsan-bu Christian Hospital (耶蘇敎病院).
1912: Doctor at Mokp'o-bu Christian Hospital (耶蘇敎病院).
1913: Professor at Kyŏngsŏng-bu Severance Medical School (世富蘭偲醫學專門學校).
Current Position: Dean (學監) at the same school.
Director (理事) of the incorporated foundation Kyŏngsŏng Poikwŏn (京城保育院).

Traveled to Canada (加奈多) and the United States (美國). Favorite reading: Medical documents. Religion: Christianity.

**Summary**

This article provides biographical details about O Kŭng-sŏn (Oh Kyung-sun), the Dean of Severance Medical School as of 1927. It lists his birthdate (1877), birthplace (Kongju), education (Paejae Haktang, Central College, University of Louisville), career path (doctor in Christian hospitals in Kunsan and Mokp'o, professor and dean at Severance), family details, and affiliations (Kyŏngsŏng Poikwŏn). It also notes his travels, reading interests, and religion. For historians, the article highlights the role of Western-educated Koreans and Christian institutions (Paejae, Severance, mission hospitals) in modernizing Korean medicine and society during the colonial period. O Kŭng-sŏn's career trajectory exemplifies the path taken by some Korean elites who engaged with Western knowledge systems under Japanese rule.
