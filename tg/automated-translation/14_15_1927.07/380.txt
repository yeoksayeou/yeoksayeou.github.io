Haedong Soksohak
						Pak Chaehyŏng, Original Author

(38 lines of original text omitted here)

Mulgyeja was a person from the Silla kingdom. He had an upright heart and harbored great ambitions. During the reign of King Naehae, when the P'osang p'alguk invaded, the king of the Kara Kingdom sent his son Noriŭm to request aid. Mulgyeja performed great meritorious deeds, but because he was not well-regarded by Noriŭm, his deeds were not recorded. Someone said, "Your merit is so great, isn't it frustrating that it remains buried?" Mulgyeja replied, "What is there to resent?" and also said, "Boasting of military exploits and seeking fame is not what a person of fundamental principle does; one should only strive diligently. Why did you not mention this to the king?"

Later, in the battle at Kalhwa Fortress, he achieved great merit, but his deeds were entirely unrecorded. At this time, he said to his wife, "In the duty of a subject, offering one's life when the country is in peril and forgetting oneself when there is turmoil is called loyalty. In this battle at Kalhwa Fortress, I did not offer my life or forget myself, so it should be called disloyalty. Being so disloyal, if this shame extends even to my ancestors, how can it be called filial piety? Having failed in both loyalty and filial piety, with what face can I brazenly stand and walk in the world?" He then shaved his head, took his kŏmun'go (검은고), and entered Sach'e Mountain, never to return.<66>

<65-66>

**Summary**

The article recounts the story of Mulgyeja, a man from Silla during King Naehae's reign. Despite achieving significant military merits in battles against the P'osang p'alguk and at Kalhwa Fortress, his contributions were overlooked and unrecorded, partly due to not being favored by Noriŭm, the son of the allied Kara king. Believing that seeking recognition was improper and later feeling he had failed in his duty of loyalty (by surviving) and thus also filial piety, Mulgyeja renounced the world, shaved his head, and became a recluse in Sach'e Mountain. From a colonial history perspective, this retelling of a Silla-era story emphasizing Confucian virtues like humility, loyalty, and filial piety, even in the face of perceived failure or lack of recognition, could be seen as an effort to promote and preserve traditional Korean values and historical identity during a period of Japanese rule. It implicitly contrasts these ideals with the pursuit of fame or reward.

해동속소학
						朴在馨 原著
(此間 38행 삭제-원문)
물계자는 신라나라 사람이외다. 맘이 곧곧하여 큰 뜻을 품엇음니다. 내해왕 시절에 포상팔국이 침노하매 가라국 임검이 그 아들 로리음을 보내어 구원하였음니다. 물계자는 큰 공이 있으되 로리음에게 잘 뵈지 못하여 공을 기록하지 아니하였음니다. 어떤 사람이 말하되 「자네의 공이 이렇게 큰대 그냥 묻어두니 답답하지 아니한가」 물계자 대답하되 「무엇 원망할 것이 있겠는가」하고 또 말하되 「훈공을 자랑하고 명예를 구하는 것이 근본지사의 하는 일이 아니니 다만 힘쓸 뿐이라 함을 웨 임검에게 말슴하지 아니하였는가」하였음니다. 그 후에 갈화성 싸움에 큰 공이 있었으나 도무지 공을 긔록하지 아니하였음니다. 이 때에는 그 안해더러 말하되 「신하된 도리에 나라가 위태하였을 쩨 목숨을 받히고 난이 있을 쩨 몸을 잊어버리는 것이 충성이라 하겠거늘 이번 갈화성 싸움에 목숨을 받히거나 몸을 잊어버리거나 한 일이 없었은 즉 불충이라 할찌요. 이렇게 불충하고서 누를 선조에게까지 및이게 되면 어찌 효라 할것인가. 이미 충성과 효도를 못하였거늘 무슨 면목으로써 뻔뻔하게 세상에 서어 다니리오」하고 머리를 깎고 검은고를 가지고 사체산에 들어가아서 돌어오지 아니하였음니다.＜66＞
