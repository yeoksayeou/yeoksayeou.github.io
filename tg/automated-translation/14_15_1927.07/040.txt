Your Face and 1 Other Poem
						Kim Nanch'on (金蘭村)

Your Face

On the eastern hill, a jade-like face,
My mother suspects that moon
Might be your face.

Looking and looking again, still unable to forget,
When I ask, “Are you not my beloved?”
That chilly moon gives a faint smile and says,
“I do not know you,”
And stealthily hides behind the clouds.

Dream

In a peaceful flower garden,
When the birds are singing songs,
My beloved, whom I believed would never return,
Greets me with a smiling face.

However, that is but a momentary illusion
Born of longing for my beloved.
When I wake from that pitiful dream,
I cry alone in this world of parting.<9>

**Summary**

This entry features two short poems by Kim Nanch'on. The first, "Your Face," describes the speaker's longing for their beloved ("님"), projecting this longing onto the moon, which ultimately denies recognition and disappears. The second poem, "Dream," portrays a fleeting reunion with the beloved in a dream, only to awaken to the painful reality of separation and solitude. Both poems express deep yearning and the sorrow of loss or absence. From a historical perspective, the intense longing for an absent "님" and the theme of separation ("리별의 세상") could potentially be interpreted allegorically, reflecting the Korean people's feelings of loss and yearning for sovereignty or a lost past during the colonial period. The stark contrast between the idealized dream and the harsh reality highlights a sense of disillusionment potentially relevant to the era.

						金蘭村
님의 얼굴
동산에 옥같은 얼골
내 어미는 저 달을
님의 얼골 아닌가 의심함니다.

보고 또 보고 그래도 못잊어
『나의 님이 아님니까고』 물어보니
쌀쌀한 그 달은 생긋 웃으며
나는 당신을 몰라요』 하고
살며시 구름 속으로 숨습니다 그려.

꿈
평화스런 꽃동산에서
새들이 노래를 부를 때
다시 안 오리라고 믿었던 님은
웃는 낯으로 나를 마저줌니다.

그러나 그것은 님을 그리는
순간의 환상입니다.
애처럽운 그 꿈이 깨고 보면
리별의 세상에서 혼자 움니다.＜9＞
