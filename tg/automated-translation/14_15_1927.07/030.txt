Magazine Title: 동광 제15호
Publication Date: 1927년 07월 05일
Article Title: 歸省三章 (Three Stanzas on Returning Home)
Author: 李殷相
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Sad Changes

When children grow tall enough to see over shoulders,
And the village elders have vanished without a trace,
Even the familiar old path lies buried beneath a big house?
Ah! How transient! What sad changes are these?

Old Mountain

Pine trees, Korean pine trees (잦나무), chestnut trees—
On the mountain where they stand, treading upon the grass—that old mountain,
I have come and stood here again, Oh! How desolate!
Only the chirping sound of birds in the forest remains.

Twilight by the Sea

O evening tide, swirling around the moss-covered rocks,
Do you wet the hem of the skirt of the maiden digging for oysters?
In the eyes of the maiden rising, I am a young boatman;
Now it is twilight over the hometown sea.<9>

**Summary**

This three-stanza poem by Yi Ŭnsang reflects on the bittersweet experience of returning home ("Gwisŏng" 歸省). The speaker observes the inevitable changes: children have grown, elders have passed away, and familiar landscapes like old paths are altered by development. Visiting the "Old Mountain" evokes a sense of desolation despite its familiar trees, with only birdsong remaining. The final stanza shifts to the sea at twilight, where the speaker, identifying as a "young boatman," observes a girl gathering oysters, capturing a moment of nostalgic beauty tinged with the passage of time. For historians, the poem offers a personal glimpse into the sentiments of Koreans experiencing modernization and change during the colonial period. The themes of loss, altered landscapes, and nostalgia reflect the disruptions and transformations occurring in Korean society, even in seemingly personal contexts like returning to one's hometown.

설은 變遷

저녁 밀물이여, 이끼 덮인 바위를 돌아
굴 캐는 처녀의 치마 끝을 적시느뇨.
일어서는 처녀의 눈에 나 젊은 사공이여
지금은 고향ㅅ바다의 황혼이로다.＜9＞
