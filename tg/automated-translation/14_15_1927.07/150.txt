Magazine Title: 동광 제15호
Publication Date: 1927년 07월 05일
Article Title: 집으로 가는 女學生들에게 - 家事를 돕자, 夏期休暇特輯 (To the Girl Students Going Home - Let's Help with Housework, Summer Vacation Special Feature)
Author: 京城女子高等普通學校 林順芬 (Kyŏngsŏng Girls' Higher Common School, Im Sun-bin)
Article Type: 문예기타 (Literary etc./Miscellaneous)

Since we girl students (女子) face what could be called an excessive burden from the daily curriculum at school (學校), being chased by preparation and review for various subjects (學科), there is no time for observing or assisting with housework (家事). Therefore, during the summer vacation (夏期休暇), we should devote ourselves entirely to this area. Especially for students who came from the provinces (地方) and live in boarding houses (下宿) or dormitories (寄宿舍), being separated from housework (家事) normally, they should exert even more effort in this regard (點).

After waking up in the morning and doing a cold water rubdown (冷水摩擦), review your studies when it's cool. In the afternoon, as much as possible, take outdoor strolls (屋外 散步) with your mother and sisters (母姉) or younger siblings (弟妹), pull weeds and water the flower beds (花壇), and wipe the rooms and floors with a cloth. This not only cultivates the sacred habit of diligence (勤勞) but also serves the purpose (目的) of exercise (運動). That is, I think this is the best method (方法), considered killing two birds with one stone (一擧兩得). In other countries, during the summer vacation (夏期休暇), people do a lot of bathing (水浴) and sea bathing (海水浴), and besides that, things like tennis (庭球), but as mentioned earlier, given our situation and especially this state where we haven't shed old customs (因襲), it's impossible even if we wanted to, so I won't ask for more (注文).

Also, since the vacation itself is rest (休養), I believe there is no need (必要) for special consideration (考慮).<22>

**Summary**

This article, written by Im Sun-bin of Kyŏngsŏng Girls' Higher Common School for the July 1927 issue of *Donggwang* magazine, advises female students returning home for summer vacation to dedicate their time to learning and helping with housework. The author argues that schoolwork leaves little time for domestic duties, especially for boarding students, making the vacation an important opportunity to cultivate diligence through chores like cleaning, gardening, and assisting family. While acknowledging recreational activities like swimming or tennis common in other countries, the author dismisses them as impractical for Korean girls due to prevailing social conditions and customs, suggesting simple chores and walks suffice for both work and exercise.

From a historical perspective, the article reflects the societal expectations placed on educated young women in colonial Korea, emphasizing domesticity even alongside modern education. It highlights the perceived burden of the school curriculum, the situation of students from rural areas studying in the city (Kyŏngsŏng), and the limitations on female activities due to conservative social norms ("因襲"). The contrast drawn with activities in "other countries" underscores a sense of Korea's constrained reality under colonialism and tradition.

집으로 가는 女學生들에게 - 家事를 돕자, 夏期休暇特輯
						京城女子高等普通學校 林順芬
우리 女子들은 學校에서 매일 배오는 과정의 負擔이 과중하다 할 만한 고로 여러 가지 學科에 예습과 복습에 쫓기어 家事의 見習이며 또는 助力을 할 틈이 없음으로 夏期休暇에는 이 방면에 전념하여야 할 것이며 특히 地方에서 온 學生들은 下宿 혹은 寄宿舍에서 지내는 고로 평시에 家事에 떠러지어 있음으로 일층 더 이 점에 전력을 쓰어야 할 것이다.
아침에 일어나서 冷水摩擦을 한 후 서늘한 때에 복습을 하며 오후에는 될 수 있는 대로 母姉나 혹은 弟妹들과 같이 屋外 散步를 하며 또는 花壇의 풀뽑기와 물을 주며 방이며 마루에 걸레를 치는 것이 勤勞의 신성한 습관을 길으게 될 뿐 아니라 또한 運動의 目的이 되지 않을까 한다. 즉 이것이 一擧兩得이라고 생각하는 가장 좋은 方法일까 한다. 남의 나라에서는 夏期休暇에 水浴 海水浴을 많이 하고 그 외에 庭球같은 것도 하나 아까 말함과 같이 우리의 정도라던지 더욱 因襲을 벗지 못한 이 현상으로 할랴야 할 수 없음으로 더 注文하지 않는다.
또 휴가 자체가 즉 休養인 고로 별로 考慮할 필요가 없을 줄로 안다.＜22＞
