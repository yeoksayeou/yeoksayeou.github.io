Hangul Inquiry

(Question) Regarding the particles "ŭro" and "e" attached after nouns, looking at the grammar books written by various teachers or expressions in the *Tonggwang* magazine, while "ŭro" and "e" are used after all other final consonants, why specifically are "mŭro" and "mae" used only after the "ㅁ" final consonant? (From Mr. Paek Il-gwang, Sinŭiju)

(Answer) In "Chosŏn Malbon" (Korean Grammar), both "ŭro" and "e", and "mŭro" and "mae" are called *kyŏtssi* (곁씨, auxiliary words/particles) or *maimt'o* (매임토, relational particles). According to their usage, they are further divided: "ŭro" and "e" are called *kipŭmt'o* (깁음토, complement particles), and "mŭro" and "mae" are called *kkumimt'o* (꾸밈토, modifier particles). Complement particles are used after *momssi* (몸씨, substantives/nouns), and modifier particles are used after *ssŭmssi* (씀씨, predicates/verbs/adjectives). For example, in "Write the letters *with a brush* (붓으로 글씨를 쓰오)" and "I *go to school* (나는 학교에 가오)," the particles "ŭro" and "e" are attached after substantives like "put" (붓, brush) and "hakkyo" (학교, school). In "*Because* it is raining, I could not go (비가 오므로 가지 못하였소)" and "*Upon reading* the text, it becomes interesting (글을 보매 재미가 나오)," the particles "mŭro" and "mae" are attached after predicates like "o" (오, come/rain) and "po" (보, see/read). There is no instance where particles like "mŭro" and "mae" are used only after the "ㅁ" final consonant of a noun, as in your esteemed question. If something was written that way, it would be a misprint (誤植).<59>

**Summary**

This article is a reader question-and-answer segment addressing a specific point of Korean grammar: the usage of postpositional particles "으로/에" versus "므로/매". The respondent clarifies that "으로/에" follow nouns (substantives) to indicate instrumentality, direction, etc. (complement particles), while "므로/매" follow verb/adjective stems (predicates) to indicate cause/reason or sequential action (modifier particles). The answer refutes the questioner's observation that "므로/매" are specifically used after nouns ending in "ㅁ", stating such usage would be a typographical error. From a historical perspective, this piece is interesting as it shows active public discussion and efforts towards standardizing Korean grammar and orthography during the colonial period. The use of specific, possibly Chu Si-gyŏng-influenced, grammatical terminology (깁음토, 꾸밈토, 몸씨, 씀씨) and older spellings provides insight into the state of Korean linguistics and language education in the late 1920s.

한글 問議
(問) 名詞 밑에 달리는 「으로」와 「에」에 대하여서 여러 선생의 著述한 文典을 보거나 또는 東光誌에 표현한 것을 보면 다른 받힘 밑에는 모도 「으로」, 「에」로 쓰면서 何特 「ㅁ」받힘 밑에서만 「므로」, 「매」로 씀니까.(新義州 白日光氏)
(答) 「조선말본」에는 「으로」와 「에」, 「므로」와 「매」를 다 겻씨(助詞의 매임토(關係吐)라 하였으며 그것을 그 쓰임에 딸아 다시 난우어 「으로」와 「에」를 깁음토(補語吐)라 하고 「므로」와 「매」를 꾸밈토(修飾吐라 하였음니다. 깁음토는 몸씨(體言) 아레에 쓰이고 꾸밈토는 씀씨(用言) 아레에 쓰이나니 例를 들면 『붓으로 글씨를 쓰오』 『나는 학교에 가오』 하면 「붓」 「학교」따위의 몸씨 아레에 「으로」 「에」의 토가 달리고 「비가 오므로 가지 못하였소」 『글을 보매 재미가 나오』하면 「오」 「보」 따위이 씀씨 아레에 「므로」 「매」의 토가 달림니다. 貴問과 같이 名詞의 「ㅁ」받힘 아레에서만 「므로」 「매」따위를 쓰는 일은 없음니다. 만일 그렇게 쓴 것이 있다면 그는 誤植이겠음니다.＜59＞
