**Castle Ruins and 1 Other**
						By Yi Ŭngsu

**Castle Ruins**

From the day I received this life,
I build the castle ruins.
The castle ruins where my spirit will dwell.
Each morning, I peek at the castle ruins,
Wondering if yesterday's work wasn't in vain.
No, in last night's rain,
One side collapsed.

The road ahead is long and distant...
Though I am rushed, building again and again,
It seems I will just live and perish this way on this path.

**Night by the Sea**

This quiet night by the sea,
A light rain makes it weep.
Sprinkling on the young man's cheeks,
Making his blue soul weep.
This quiet night by the sea,
That rain makes it weep once more.

In the falling streaks of rain,
Gazing at the distantly soaked lighthouse beam,
I stand blankly counting the old days,
My heart, too, becomes soaked in tears and rain. <10>

**Summary**

The article presents two poems by Yi Ŭngsu. The first, "Castle Ruins," uses the metaphor of constantly rebuilding crumbling ruins to depict a life of persistent, perhaps futile, effort in pursuit of a spiritual or personal foundation. The second, "Night by the Sea," evokes a mood of melancholy and nostalgia, where the speaker reflects on the past amidst rain and the sea, feeling sorrowful. From a historical perspective, the poems might reflect the sense of struggle, loss, and the weight of the past experienced by Koreans under colonial rule. The themes of unending effort against setbacks and quiet sorrow could resonate with the political and cultural climate of 1927 Korea.

성터를 외 1편
						李應洙
성터를
목숨 받는 그날붙어
성터를 쌓노라.
나의 령 깃드릴 성터를
아츰마다 성터를 엿보노라
하룻 일이 헛일이 않었던가고
안이랴 간밤 비에
한판은 문어저

앞길은 멀고 멀거늘...
거듭 쌓고 거듭 쌓어 배바빠하나
이리하다 이 길에 살어질 것뿐.

바다가의 밤
바다가의 고요한 이 밤을
실비가 울니네
젊은이의 두 뺨에 뿌리어
프른 혼을 울리더니
바다가의 고요한 이 밤을
또 다시 그 비가 울리노나

나리는 줄기줄기 빗발에
멀니 젖은 등대물을 바라며
녯날을 멀금이 세고 섰으니
마음도 눈물에 비에 저저 오도다.＜10＞
