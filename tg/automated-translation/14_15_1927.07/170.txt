**Body Text Translation:**
Yonhi College Dean Yu Ŏkkyŏm
Born October 23, 1896. Native of Kyŏngsŏng.
Original Family Registry: 107 Che-dong, Kyŏngsŏng-bu. Current Residence: Same.
Father: Farmer. Wife: Yun Hŭisŏp.

1916: Graduated from the Regular Division (普通部) of the private Dōshisha (同志社) in Kyōto, Japan.
March 1919: Graduated from the First Division, Kō Group (第一部 甲流) of the Governmental Third Higher School (官立 第三高等學校) in Kyōto, Japan.
1922: Graduated from the Law Department (Law Course, specializing in English and French Law) of the Faculty of Law (法學部) at Tōkyō Imperial University (東京帝國大學), Japan.
Same year: Studied law under Dr. Makino Eiichi (牧野英一) at the Graduate School (大學院) of Tōkyō Imperial University, Japan (1 year).
1923: Registered on the roll of lawyers at the Prosecutor's Office of the Kyŏngsŏng District Court (京城地方法院).
Same year: Registered on the roll of lawyers at the Prosecutor's Office of the Tōkyō District Court (東京地方裁判所), Japan.
Same year: Registered on the roll of patent attorneys (特許辨理士) at the Tōkyō Patent Office (東京 特許局), Japan.
Same year: Commissioned teacher (囑託 教員) at the private Chungang Higher Common School (中央高等普通學校) in Kyŏngsŏng.
Same year: Professor at the private Yonhi College (私立 延禧專門學校) in Koyang-gun.
Same year: Commissioned teacher (囑託 教員) at the private Kyŏngsin School (私立儆新學校) in Kyŏngsŏng.
1924: Vice Dean (副學監) at the private Yonhi College in Koyang-gun.
Same year: Dean (學監) at the same school.
Same year: Founder of the private Ŭllo School (私立恩露學校) in Sihŭng-gun.
1925: Lecturer at the private Ewha College (私立 梨花專門學校) in Kyŏngsŏng.
Same year: Director on the Board of Directors (理事) of Ewha Haktang (梨花學堂).
1926: Acting Principal (校長 臨時代理) of the private Yonhi College in Koyang-gun (3 months).

Director (理事) of the Seoul Central YMCA (京城中央基督敎靑年會).
Auditor (監事) of the Maintenance Foundation (維持財團) of the Seoul Corporate Body Central YMCA (京城財團法人 中央基督敎青年會 維持財團).
Member of the Pacific Problem Research and Investigation Association (太平洋問題硏究調査會). Member of the Korean Affairs Research and Investigation Association (朝鮮事情硏究調査會). Member of the Federation of Korean YMCAs (朝鮮基督敎青年會聯合會). Secretary-General (幹事長) of the Boy Scouts Korea General Federation (少年斥候團朝鮮總聯盟). Committee Member (委員) of the Joseon Sports Council (朝鮮體育會).
Religion: Christianity. Traveled to China, Hawaii (布哇). Favorite Reading: Bible, Biography of Lincoln. Hobby: Travel. Preference: Listening to music (音樂叅聽). <24>

**Summary**

This article provides a detailed biographical profile of Yu Ŏkkyŏm (兪億兼), the Dean of Yonhi College (later Yonsei University) as of 1927. It outlines his birth details, prestigious education culminating in law degrees from Kyōto Third Higher School and Tōkyō Imperial University, and his subsequent legal registrations in both Korea and Japan. It charts his rapid career progression within Korean educational institutions, particularly Yonhi College, alongside roles at Chungang, Kyŏngsin, and Ewha schools. The article also lists his extensive involvement in various Christian, social, research, and sports organizations, including the YMCA, Boy Scouts, and Joseon Sports Council. For historians, this piece offers valuable insight into the trajectory of a highly educated Korean elite during the colonial period. Yu's ability to gain qualifications within the Japanese system (university, law registration) and immediately leverage them within prominent Korean private institutions like Yonhi and Ewha, while also participating actively in civil society organizations, highlights the complex landscape navigated by Korean intellectuals and administrators under Japanese rule. His affiliations suggest involvement in both modernizing efforts and potentially nationalist-adjacent activities within the permitted boundaries of the time.
