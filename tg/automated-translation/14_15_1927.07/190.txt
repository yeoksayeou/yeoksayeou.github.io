**Body Text Translation**
Principal of Posŏng Higher Common School, Ch'oe Myŏnghwan

Pen Name: Kan'gang (干岡). Born May 8th, 493rd year of Kaeguk (開國) [approx. 1884]. Born in Kyŏngsŏng.
Original Domicile: 16 Ch'ungsin-dong (忠信洞), Kyŏngsŏng Prefecture. Current Residence: Same.
Father: [Name not provided]. Wife: Kim Sigyŏng (金時卿). Married 1904.

1907: Graduated from Tōkyō Prefectural First Middle School (東京府立第一中學校), Japan.
1911: Graduated from Tōkyō Higher Normal School (東京高等師範學校), Japan.
Same year: Dean (學監) at Kyŏngsin School (儆新學校) in Kyŏngsŏng.
1912: Teacher at Kyŏngsŏng Posŏng Higher Common School (普成高等普通學校).
Current Position: Principal of the same school.

Travelled to China. Favorite Reading: Natural science documents (自然科學 書類). Religion: Buddhism. Hobby: Hunting (狩獵).

**Summary**

This article provides a brief biographical profile of Ch'oe Myŏnghwan, the principal of Posŏng Higher Common School in Kyŏngsŏng as of 1927. It details his birthdate (approx. 1884), education in prestigious schools in Tōkyō, Japan, his marriage, and his career progression from dean at Kyŏngsin School to teacher and eventually principal at Posŏng Higher Common School. It also notes his pen name, travel to China, interest in natural sciences, Buddhist faith, and hobby of hunting. For historians, this profile highlights a member of the Korean elite educated in Japan who attained a position of leadership within the colonial education system at a prominent Korean private school. His background and interests provide a glimpse into the life of an educated figure navigating the complexities of the colonial period.
