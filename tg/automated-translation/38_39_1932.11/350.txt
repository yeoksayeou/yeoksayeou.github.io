愛人에게 보내는 冊子 (A Booklet to Send to a Loved One)
The reader's autumn has returned once again. On clear-weathered, high-skied days, when seeking autumn while strolling through mountains and fields, or when quietly facing the lamplight on an autumn night, what kind of booklet will you take in hand? Everyone likely has booklets they particularly love, but wouldn't publicly revealing everyone's favorite books to the whole world also be a meaningful event for the reading world!
This is the reason we pose the question, "What is a booklet you would like to recommend to a loved one or a close friend?" and introduce the answers below.
(In order of manuscript arrival)

Nietzsche's *Zarathustra*

Yi Kwan'yong, Doctor of Philosophy

You mean something generally readable, not specialized, right?
I recommend Nietzsche's *Zarathustra* without hesitation. Although I have read it repeatedly over fifteen years, I have not yet grown tired of it. Its philosophical content is extremely individualistic and aristocratic, (3 lines omitted - original text) and especially its powerful writing is so forceful that the reader cannot maintain the attitude of a reader but cannot help but be drawn along.<78>
There is a Japanese translation by Mr. Ikuta Chōkō, but it wasn't well done. And perhaps it couldn't be done well. I have seen the English translation and the French translation as well; *Zarathustra* appearing in the English translation is just like a Puritan pastor preaching, and the one appearing in the French translation seems like the admonitions of Eastern hermits. Although the name was imported from Persia (波斯), the deep emotion and grand thoughts bursting forth in powerful words would not reveal the full flavor of magnificence unless they were the words of the German superman wandering in the Alps (알프山中). I do not know how a Chinese translation would be, but there are those who say that if it were to be translated, Chinese text (漢文) has the possibility of being rendered more closely than Japanese, English, or French text. How about those without knowledge of German (獨文 素養) trying to buy and read a Chinese translation?

Yi Kwangsu's *Yi Sunsin*

Chu Yosŏp, Sintonga (新東亞)

In *Yi Sunsin*, one can see the courage, righteous spirit, cowardice, factional strife, irresponsibility, lethargy, incompetence, in other words, an enumeration of all the strengths and weaknesses of the Korean people (朝鮮民族). It is a living history. *Yi Sunsin* is the dissection room of the Korean people, and its protagonist, Yi Sunsin, is an indicator for the aspiring youth of Korea (朝鮮靑年) and a symbol of the regenerating nation (民族).
(Price 2 Won, Published by Taesŏng Sŏrim 大成書林)

Julio Baghy's *Kkoduk Kaksi yŏ* (Oh, Marionette?)

Kim Ŏk, Poet

Just because a book is loved does not necessarily mean it is a good book (良書). Why is that? Because whether it is loved or not depends on an individual's culture (敎養) and taste (趣味). Just as one doesn't say, "This side dish (반찬) is tasty, so you must eat it too," but rather, "This side dish is tasty to me," in that sense, I will write down one volume from among the books I love. Whether a writing is moral (道德) or immoral (不道德) will also, of course, be determined according to culture and taste. This is also why A and B have different standards for good books.
One. The book's name (어름) is the cynical (히니꾸롭은) title "Let's Dance, Marionette" (꼬둑각씨여 춤을 추자). The author is Julio Baghy,<79> the publisher is Munhak Segye Sa (文學世界社) of Budapest, Hungary, and the price is approximately 1 Won 50 or 60 Chŏn. In this book, especially in short stories like "Just Be Human" (다만 사람이거라), I discovered the "unpretentious, truly human person" (거줏없는 사람답은 사람). The discovery of "human" is a precious thing.

Asano Kenshin's *Atheism and the Anti-Religion Movement*

Pak Irhyŏng, Critic

My recent life (生活) does not afford me the leisure (餘裕) to "most love" any particular book. However, to comply with your company's request, if I were to introduce one among the newly published books (新刊書籍) I have recently read through (通讀), I would pick this book. "The criticism of religion (宗敎) is the premise (前提) of all criticism." Marx said this in his preface to the critique of Hegel's Philosophy of Law (法律哲學批判序說). If I had a loved one and sent this book as a gift (선물), the words I would say to them then would be excellently substituted (代身) by this one phrase from Marx.

The content (內容) of this book, *Atheism and the Anti-Religion Movement* (その史的 展望 - Its Historical Perspective), begins with a preface on the necessity (必要性) of the anti-religion movement in capitalist society (資本主義社會), the first part (前篇) covers the historical development (史的 發展) of anti-religion theory (理論), and the latter part (後篇) covers the historical development of the anti-religion movement (運動)! It follows this order (順序), and while there is a feeling that the description (記述) is too conceptual (槪念的), if one pays attention (注意) only to the few lines (數行) about the true nature (正體) in the so-called "Conclusion" (結論), it will be a considerably (相當히) good companion (伴侶) for beginners (初步者).
(Price 50 Chŏn. Publisher: Ōyū-kaku (大雄閣), 31 Nishi-Edogawa, Koishikawa-ku, Tōkyō (東京小石川區西江戶川三一). Postal Transfer Tōkyō 66927 振替東京66927)

Yi Chungho (ed.), *Views on the History of Writing Systems and Revolution Theory*

OOO (Author's name missing or illegible)

The anguish (煩悶) of new China (新中國), with its many writing systems (文字), is further rousing (奮起) its ambitious youth.<80> This book (本書) discusses the superiority or inferiority (優劣) between world writing systems (世界文字) and Chinese characters (中國文字), the origin and development (起源發達) of writing systems, and the historical destiny (歷史的 運命) for abolishing (廢止) Chinese characters (漢字). It also almost (거위) comprehensively covers (總網羅) discussions (議論) on what to use instead of (代身) Chinese characters: the Roman alphabet (羅馬字), National Language Romanization (國語羅馬字), National Phonetic Alphabet (國音字母), Esperanto (에스페란토), or a newly created script (新製文字)! These are theses (論文) concerning China's writing system. I urge (勸한다) the academic world (學界) of Korea (조선), where research fever (硏究熱) for Hangeul (한글) is flourishing (隆盛), to certainly give it a read (一讀).
(Published and issued by Puk Munhwa Haksa (北文化學社). Price: One Silver Dollar 大洋壹元)

Yi Yŏsŏng, Kim Seyong (Co-authors), *Statistical Research on Korea*

Kim Kyŏngjae, Critic

I would like to recommend (推천) *Statistical Research on Korea* (數字朝鮮硏究). The reason (理由) is that Korean people (朝鮮사람), especially we Korean youth (朝鮮靑年), try to know and somewhat (多少) know the situations (사정) of Japan (日本), America (米國), Russia (露西亞), etc., but are ignorant (어두운) of the situation (情勢) in Korea (朝鮮). However, the aforementioned *Statistical Research on Korea* is concise and clear (簡明), allowing one to understand (解得) the various situations in Korea at a glance (一瞥).

O. Henry's *Short Story Collection*

Im Pyŏngch'ŏl, Tonga Ilbo Sa (東亞日報社)

I want to gift (선물하고 십습니다) this book to my beloved friend. Since I am reading this book now, I want to talk about (이야기 하고 십습니다) what we savored (맛본 것) after reading each work (作品식).
O. Henry's Short Story Collection "A Service of love and other Stories." Published August 20, Shōwa 7 (1932). Anglo-American Modern Literature Series (英米近代文學叢書). Shun'yōdō (春陽堂), 8, 3-chōme, Tōri, Nihonbashi-ku, Tōkyō City (東京市 日本橋區通3丁目8番地). (Price 1 Won 50 Chŏn 定價1圓50錢)

O. Henry is one of the world's six great short story writers (短篇作家), and in his works, one can find a bright and warm (밝고도 따듯한) heart (마음). Differently (달리) from the mood (情調) of Poe or Chekhov, or the description (描寫) of Hardy, he expressed (表現) life's (人生) Humor and Pathos through changes (變化) in the Story. His writing style (作風) can be seen in his early short story (젊은 短篇) "After Twenty Years." It gives the feeling (感) of looking out at the world (世上) as if through a small peephole (문구멍).<81>

*Home Encyclopedia*

Kim Tongin, Novelist

Even among friends, the books one wants to recommend (소개하고 싶은 책) would differ depending on the person's environment (환경), age (나이), wealth (貧富), wisdom (賢愚), occupation (직업), physical strength (體力), etc., so it is difficult (어렵습니다) to answer collectively (一括하여).

If I were to recommend one book (冊) to my most beloved wife (안해) right now, I would choose (골라 보겟습니다) one volume of the *Home Encyclopedia* (家庭百科全書).

Takahashi Kamekichi's *Anatomy of the Japanese Zaibatsu*

(Author's name missing, possibly continued from Kim Tongin or another contributor)
Since recently (近來) I have only been rummaging through (뒤지고 잇는) statistical documents (統計書類), I haven't discovered (發見치 못하엿사오나) any particular book (冊) to recommend (推薦). However, books I read with interest (興味) until recently (요전까지) were:
Ōuchi Hyōei's *Introduction to Public Finance* (財政學槪論)
Yanaihara Tadao's *Taiwan Under Imperialism* (帝國主義下의 臺灣)
Takahashi Kamekichi's *Anatomy of the Japanese Zaibatsu* (日本財閥의 解剖)
They were very good reference books for probing into things like Ionva(? 이온바)'s Capitalist National Economy, Imperialism within XX Territory, the content of Dictatorial 'Konzern' (獨裁的 『콘체룬』), etc. Since they are all books borrowed and returned (빌려보고 돌닌 冊), I don't remember (記憶) the publishers, and the prices (定價) seem to have all been around 2 Won (2圓 假量).

*Book of Changes* and Emerson's *Essays*

Hyŏn Sangyun, Principal of Chungang Kobogyo (中央高普校長)

For Chinese classics (漢籍), I want to recommend the *Book of Changes* (周易). That book compares yin and yang (陰陽) to the superior man (君子) and the petty man (小人), and is fully loaded (滿載) with lessons (敎訓) on removing (除去) yin and supporting (붓드는) yang, which is extremely (大端히) good for our introspection (內省) and self-cultivation (修養).

As for Western books (西洋書籍), I would like to recommend Emerson's essays. Every phrase and word (句句言言) is a lesson (敎訓) and a sharp pointer (頂針). The original text (原書) is available in various collections (各種 叢書), and there is a Japanese translation (日本譯) by Togawa Shūkotsu. <82>

Nakajima Hanjirō's *Trends in Personalistic Pedagogy*

Kim Yun'gyŏng, Paehwa Yŏgo (培花女高)

Mr. Nakajima deliberately visited professors at various German universities for over a year, researching (硏究) these current educational trends (敎育思潮), and first imported (輸入) them to Japan. Today (오늘에 잇어서는), it has become common knowledge (常識化) to the point where anyone (누구던지) can easily say that the purpose (目的) of education is "making a human-like human" (사람답은 사람을 만들음), embracing personalistic educational principles (人格的 敎育主義). However, still (아직도), especially (特히) in our society (社會), because this goal (目標) of education has not been certainly (確實이) recognized (認識되지 못하엿기 때문에), efforts (勞力) are made not to make people human-like (사람답게) but to make them into machines (機械). While the educational system (敎育制度) is likely the cause (原因) for the most part (大部分), educators (敎育者) themselves (自身) do not strive for cultivation (陶冶) with great (偉大) personality (人格) but play the role of a machine (機械노릇) to the students (被敎育者). Because of this, the impersonal behavior (非人格的 行動) of educators occasionally (가끔가끔) meets with resistance (反抗) from the students. From this point (이점), I especially want this book to be widely read (넓히 읽히고 싶습니다). Many authoritative (權威) philosophers (哲學者) and educational scholars (敎育學者) like Kant, Eucken, Budde, Kastner, Foerster, Linde, Itschner are central figures (中心 人物) of this theory (學說). Most of my thoughts (생각), that is, my view of life (人生觀), were obtained (얻은 것) from such people. This book roughly introduces (대강 소개한 것) their theories. (Published by Dōbunkan (同文舘), Kanda (神田), Tōkyō 東京)

Eddington's *Space, Time and Gravitation*

Anonymous (失名氏)

1. In the field of science (科學方面),
I want to advise (勸告) reading Eddington's "Space, Time and Gravitation." This book introduces (紹介) Einstein's "General Theory of Relativity" (一般相對性理論), and those with knowledge (素養) of higher mathematics (高等數學) will be able to read it. If by chance (萬一에) someone without knowledge of higher mathematics wants to learn the outline (槪要) of the Theory of Relativity, reading Einstein's "General and Special Theory of Relativity" (一般及特殊相對性理論) would be best. This book is made to be understandable by anyone (누구나 알 수 잇게 맨든 冊 - allgemeinverstaendlich), but even this cannot be read without knowing algebra (代數學). Why do I recommend it (웨 勸하느냐고요)? Because Korean people (朝鮮사람) neglect (等閑히 하니) science, let's correct (校訂) that harm (弊), and above all ( 무엇보다도), we must not fall behind the times (時代에 落伍되지 말어야지오).

2. In philosophy (哲學), Kant's "Pure Philosophy" (純正哲學 - likely Critique of Pure Reason) and Bergson's "Matter and Memory" (matiere et memoire - 物質과 記憶).
3. In social science (社會科學), Durkheim's "De la Division du Travail sociale" (社會分業論).

Kawakami Hajime's *Outline of Economics*

OOO (Author's name missing or illegible)

Characteristics (特徵): The explanation (解說) is generally (大體로) accurate (正鵠), and the writing (文章) is plain (平易) and fluent (流暢).
Content (內容): It consists of two parts (上下 二篇). The upper part (上篇) explains the summary (大要) of proletarian economics (프로레타리아 經濟學), and the lower part (下篇) describes a glimpse (一班) of the historical development (歷史的 發展) of bourgeois economics (부루주아 經濟學). Generally speaking (大體로 말하면), it collects (收綠) analyses (解剖) based on Marx's (맑쓰) *Das Kapital* (資本論), and can be seen as Mr. Kawakami's recent masterpiece (力作).
(Economics Complete Works (經濟學 全集) First Volume (第一卷). Price 1 Won (定價 1圓). Published by Kaizōsha (改造社)).<83>

**Summary**

This 1932 article from the magazine *Tonggwang* surveys prominent Korean intellectuals (philosophers, writers, critics, educators, journalists) on which books they would recommend to a loved one or close friend. The recommendations cover a wide range of genres: philosophy (Nietzsche, Kant, Bergson), literature (Yi Kwangsu's historical novel, O. Henry, Julio Baghy), social science (Marx, Durkheim, economics texts on Japan and Korea, anti-religion tracts), science (Einstein, Eddington), pedagogy, classical Chinese texts (*Book of Changes*), and practical guides (*Home Encyclopedia*). Each respondent provides their reasoning, often reflecting personal interests, perceived needs of Korean society (understanding Korea statistically, fostering national spirit, promoting scientific literacy, critiquing religion, educational reform), or appreciation for specific literary or philosophical qualities.

From a historical perspective, the article is revealing about the intellectual currents among the Korean elite during the colonial period. There's a clear engagement with both Western and Japanese thought (often via Japanese translations or scholarship), alongside a concern for Korean identity and progress (Yi Sunsin novel, statistical study of Korea, Hangeul research). The inclusion of Marxist-influenced texts (Asano Kenshin, Kawakami Hajime) alongside classical philosophy and modern science highlights the diverse ideologies competing for attention. The commentary on translation quality (Nietzsche) and the discussion about Chinese character reform also point to contemporary linguistic and cultural debates. The recommendations reflect a desire among these intellectuals to guide the reading habits of Koreans towards self-improvement, national awareness, and modern knowledge, despite the constraints of colonial rule.

잡지명	동광 제39호
발행일	1932년 11월 01일
기사제목	愛人에게 보내는 冊子
기사형태	설문

愛人에게 보내는 冊子
讀者의 秋는 다시 돌아 왓습니다. 日氣淸朗하고 하늘 높은 날 가을을 찾아 山野로 散策할 때, 또는 조용히 가을 밤 燈火를 대할 때에 어떠한 冊子를 손에 잡을가. 여러분은 各其 愛好하시는 冊子를 가지섯겟지마는 이제 諸氏의 愛讀書를 滿天下에 公開함이 또한 讀書界에 뜻잇는 일이 아닐까!
「사랑하는 사람 또는 親한 친구에게 勸하고 싶은 冊子」
는 무엇인가 하는 設問을 들어 그 解答을 左에 紹介하는 所以입니다.
(原稿到着順)

니이췌著  짜라투스트라

哲學博士 李灌鎔

專門的이 아니고 一般的으로 愛讀할만한 것 말슴이겟지요?
나는 서슴치 않고 「니잇췌」의 「짜라투스트라」를 勸합니다. 열다섯해동안 두고 두고 屢讀하엿것만 아직 실증이 안낫습다. 그 思想內容이 極端的으로 個人主義的 貴族的이나 (3行略 - 원문) 特히 그 雄文은 讀者가 讀者의 態度를 維持할 수 없고 다만 끌리어 가지 아니치＜78＞ 못하도록 힘이 많습니다. 日譯은 生田長江氏의 것이 잇는데 잘 되지 않엇습디다. 또 잘될 수도 없겟지요. 英譯도 보고 佛譯도 보앗는데 英譯에 나타나는 「짜라투스트라」는 똑 淸敎徒 牧師의 說敎하는 것 같고 佛譯에 나타나는 것은 東方處士들의 訓戒하는 것 같애서 이름은 波斯에서 輸入햇으나 깊은 感動과 雄大한 思想이 힘 잇는 말로 북바처 나오는 것은 알프山中에 徘徊하는 獨逸 超人의 말 아니고는 모든 壯美의 맛이 나타나지 않을 것입니다. 漢譯은 어떤지 모르나 만일 飜譯한다면 日英佛文보다 도리어 漢文이 近似하게 맨들어 늘 可能性이 잇다는 사람이 잇으니 獨文 素養 없는 이는 漢譯을 사보시면 어떠할른지.

李光洙著  李舜臣
新東亞 朱耀燮

「李舜臣」에서 朝鮮民族의 勇氣, 義氣, 卑怯, 黨爭, 無責任, 無氣力, 無能, 다시 말하자면 왼갓 長點과 短點의 羅列을 볼 수 잇습니다. 그는 한 개의 산 歷史입니다. 「李舜臣」은 朝鮮民族의 解剖室이요 그 主人公인 李舜臣은 向上하려는 朝鮮靑年의 一指標요 更生하려는 民族의 象徵입니다.
(定價2圓 大成書林發行)

율니오.바기作  꼬둑각씨여
詩人 金億

書籍이란 愛讀한다고 그것을 반드시 良書라 할 수가 없는 것이외다. 웨 그런고하니 한 個人의 敎養과 趣味에 따라서 愛讀되고 아니되는 것이기 때문이외다. 그 반찬이 맛이 잇으니 자네도 먹어야 할 것이 아니요 이 반찬이 내게는 맛이 잇데 하는 意味로 나는 나의 愛讀하는 冊 中에서 한 卷은 적어 놓습니다. 道德不道德의 文書란 것도 또한 敎養과 趣味에 딸라서 斷定될 것은 勿論이외다. 甲乙이 良書에 대한 標準이 다른 것도 이 때문이지오.
一. 冊 어름은 「꼬둑각씨여 춤을 추자」한 「히니꾸」롭은 表題외다. 作者는 「율니오.바기」,＜79＞ 發行所는 헝거리아 부다페스트의 文學世界社, 定價는 約 1원 5, 60전 가량이외다. 이 冊에서 그 中에도 「다만 사람이거라」와 같은 短篇에서 나는 「거줏없는 사람답은 사람」을 發見하엿기 때문이외다. 「사람」의 發見은 貴한 일이외다.

淺野硏眞著  無神論과 反宗敎運動
