기사제목 世界戰爭과 大衆生活 (World War and the Lives of the Masses)
필자 文一東
기사형태 논설 (Editorial/Opinion Piece)

World War and the Lives of the Masses
						By Mun Il-dong

Is War a Conflict of Peoples?
The bourgeoisie sees war as a conflict between one people (民族) and another people. This bourgeois view of war, through all its propaganda organs like newspapers and magazines, is deeply ingrained even among the petit bourgeoisie, unawakened workers, and the peasant masses. Such a view of war inevitably leads to the following conclusion: "When one people rules over another through war, the ruling people as a whole can live much happier lives, both economically and culturally." In other words, war brings happiness to the entire nation. This kind of [censored] arises more widely the more a nation is in economic difficulties. Due to the recent worldwide Great Depression, we are now witnessing examples of this phenomenon right before our eyes.

However, such "hope," at least for the workers and peasants, is nothing more than a single [censored]. War absolutely does not arise from a simple conflict between peoples, as the bourgeoisie propagates; rather, in capitalist society, it is an inevitable product arising from the capitalists of each country seeking to expand or maintain their ruling power (the power of exploitation of workers by one capitalist). In other words, war is a means to reproduce the production relations that cause the war itself. Therefore, war sometimes brings great profits to the capitalists. (Omitted from original text)

Post-[censored] War Commodity Prices <12>

Let's take the [censored: likely Sino-Japanese] War as an example. The [censored] War was an unavoidable event that arose as [censored: likely Japanese] capitalism, squeezed between the imperialist great powers, took its first steps of development, seeking to maintain and expand its capitalism. [Censored: Japanese] capitalism, victorious in this war, not only consolidated its [censored: likely dominance] over [censored: likely Korea] by completely detaching [censored: Korea] from [censored: likely China] and solidified its foothold for advancing onto the continent, but the huge reparations and [censored: likely concessions/territory] also contributed to the further development of [censored: Japanese] capitalism. However, through the [censored] War, the [censored: likely worker and peasant] masses of [censored: Japan] saw no [censored: benefit] whatsoever. (4 lines omitted from original text)

Immediately after the war, commodity prices showed a drastic surge, linked to the issuance of war bonds and the increase in convertible note issuance due to the surge in corporate fund lending by the [censored: likely Bank of Japan]. While this brought increased profits to the capitalists on one hand, on the other hand, it only brought hardship (生活의 XX) to the [censored: workers] and salaried employees. Not only that, but the post-war expansion of government finances—due to the accelerating increase in army and navy expansion costs, increases in corporate subsidies, etc.—inevitably resulted in an increased burden on the [censored: masses]. Therefore, the development of [censored: Japanese] capitalism and the accumulation of capital after the [censored] War amounted to nothing more than an accumulation of [censored: hardship/poverty] for the [censored: workers and peasants].

The Lives of the American Masses During the World War
We can see a similar example on an even larger scale in the 1914-18 World War. It was an inevitable product arising from the confrontation between the newly emerging German (獨逸) imperialism and other imperialist great powers, but we know well how much the workers and peasants suffered throughout the war and post-war period. In the World War, for the sake of the capitalists who [censored: exploit] them, 10 million [censored: workers and peasants] (the majority of soldiers were [censored: workers and peasants]) died in battle, and 12 million [censored: workers and peasants] were wounded. Not only that, but immense wealth vanished like smoke, and the severing of world economic connections and the collapse of currency values brought about indescribable deterioration in the lives of the masses, ultimately leading to the revolutionizing of the people in various countries, and with the establishment of the Soviet Union (사벳트同盟), turned world capitalism into a capitalism entering a process of [censored: decline/crisis].

The ones who benefited most from this Great War were American (米國) capitalism and Japanese (日本) capitalism. Taking advantage of the European powers being preoccupied with the flames of war, American capitalists and Japanese capitalists could monopolize the markets of colonies and semi-colonies that slipped from their hands and reap enormous profits. The rapid development of American and Japanese capitalism was, so to speak, a gift from the World War. However, what did the [censored: workers] and peasants of [censored: Japan] and America gain from the development of [censored: Japanese] and American capitalism? Their gain was only the worsening of their lives. Taking [censored: Japan] as an example, the abnormal currency inflation within the country and the great development of export munition production materialized as a sharp rise in domestic prices; while wages for labor doubled from 1914 to 1919, prices rose approximately 3.6 times. This, needless to say, indicates an absolute deterioration in [censored: working-class] life. This worsening of [censored: working-class] life rapidly promoted the [censored: political] awakening of the domestic [censored: working] class, finally leading to the outbreak of the Rice Riots (米騷動), Japan's first [censored: political? mass?] [censored], in August 1918. In America (米國) too, the impact of the rapidly developed capitalism during the World War on the [censored: working] class was no different from [censored: Japan]. <13>

Therefore, the World War, regardless of victor or vanquished nations, only gave enormous profits to a small number of large capitalists and munitions industries, while only bringing [censored: suffering/hardship] to the [censored: workers and peasants].

Capitalists' Profits, Masses' [censored: Suffering]
Such was the impact of the World War on the [censored: workers and peasants] during the war, but did it bring any particularly good influence after the war? We have not heard from any corner of the world that the Great War brought good, happy effects to the masses. Needless to mention the Great Depression that swept the entire world, starting with Japan, following the end of the Great War, what did the so-called Industrial Rationalization (産業合理化) movement, aimed at rebuilding the capitalist economy ruined by the Great War, give to the masses? Extension of working hours, creation of mass unemployment, intensification of [censored: labor exploitation], lowering of wages—all these were the sole gifts that Industrial Rationalization gave to the masses.

So, did this Industrial Rationalization finally restore the entire system of the capitalist economy and ultimately provide even a little stability to the lives of the masses? Of course, no. What followed Rationalization was the current world economic crisis. It is unnecessary to speak now about the impact this current world Great Depression is having on the masses. Here, it suffices to state the following fact: That the 1914-18 World War and the current world Great Depression, which is the inevitable result of the so-called Industrial Rationalization movement aimed at rebuilding the capitalist economic mechanism [censored: destroyed] by that Great War, are again preparing for a confrontation among the imperialist powers, or rather, a [censored: challenge/provocation] by the imperialist powers against the Soviet Union (사벳트同盟), a Second World War. This Second World War has been showing partial symptoms since last year.

Therefore, in capitalist society, war is nothing more than a process that reproduces the production relations that caused the war, expands and reproduces all the contradictions contained therein, and thereby prepares for yet another new war. While it gives enormous profits to a few capitalists, it only brings [censored: suffering/hardship] to the [censored: workers and peasants].

The Next Great War and Commodity Prices
Therefore, the impact of the coming Great War (some symptoms are already present) on the workers, peasants, and the masses will by no means deviate from this example. Not only that, but the impact of war, the [censored: hardship/suffering] in the lives of the masses, will be further expanded and reproduced. Why? Because the expansion of the theater of war and the swelling of war expenditures will be incomparably greater than even during the First World War. The swelling of war expenditures will inevitably lead to increased issuance of war bonds and a great flood of state currency—we will see a flood many times, tens of times greater than the flood seen in Germany during the last World War. A single piece of bread will exceed astronomical figures, reaching super-astronomical figures; we will see an unprecedented surge in prices. Consequently, while it will bring enormous profits to a few capitalists and the munitions industry, the lives of the [censored: peasants] will become tens of times more [censored: miserable] than now. Peasants do possess some goods, grain, to sell. However, it is a principle that agricultural products rise much more slowly than industrial goods when prices go up, and fall many times more rapidly than industrial goods when prices go down. Therefore, in the end, the one or two mal, one or two seom of grain that the peasant can sell may not even be enough to buy a single hoe (farm tool) or a foot of thread (糸). <14>

Furthermore, war not only drives the worker-peasant masses into [censored: hardship/poverty] through price inflation, but the swelling of military expenditures inevitably leads to increased issuance of taxes and an increase in the [censored: exploitation/burden] of the [censored: worker-peasant masses] through increased taxation. This will become a burden (짐) that peasants absolutely cannot endure without [censored: resisting/rising up?].

The Misguided View of Waiting for War to Break Out
The impact of war on the masses, as described above, will be no different, although there may be some difference in degree, whether one is in a belligerent nation or not. If the coming war is a challenge by the imperialist powers against the Soviet Union (this is extremely likely), the losses for the [censored: worker-peasant] masses will not only be economic but they will also face political persecution (政治的 迫害) they have never seen before. Therefore, for them, war becomes the greatest enemy not only economically but also politically.

Nevertheless, the unawakened masses, unaware that the current Great Depression is a result of the previous Great War, harbor a "hope" that some war might break out again. This, of course, arises from the misguided view influenced by the war perspective of the capitalists and landlords who stand to gain enormous profits from war. It is because they do not know that war does not give [censored: benefits] to the masses, only [censored: suffering/hardship]. Of course, war will inevitably break out regardless of whether the masses merely think about it, hope for it, or not. At that time, the path for the masses will solely be the question of how they can eliminate that war, which only brings them [censored: suffering/hardship], and the production relations that give rise to it. <15>

**Summary**

This 1932 article by Mun Il-dong argues that war, contrary to bourgeois propaganda portraying it as a national conflict bringing collective benefit, is an inherent product of capitalism. It contends that wars are instigated by capitalists seeking to expand or maintain their power and profits, while the working masses (workers and peasants) bear the costs through death, injury, economic hardship (inflation, taxes), and post-war crises like depressions and exploitative "rationalization." Using examples like a censored war (likely Sino-Japanese or Russo-Japanese), World War I, and the experiences of Japan and the US, the author demonstrates that war consistently worsens the lives of ordinary people, regardless of whether their nation wins or loses. The article concludes by warning against a looming Second World War, potentially targeting the Soviet Union, which would bring even greater suffering and political persecution to the masses, urging them to understand war's true nature and question the system that produces it.

From a historical perspective, this article is significant as an example of Marxist or socialist critique circulating in colonial Korea during a period of intense Japanese imperialism and global economic crisis. The explicit class analysis, the identification of capitalism and imperialism as root causes of war, and the sympathetic mention of the Soviet Union (albeit cautiously) reflect the ideological currents challenging the dominant colonial and capitalist narratives. The heavy use of "[censored]" (XX) highlights the strict censorship environment under Japanese rule, forcing writers to use coded language or face suppression when discussing politically sensitive topics like specific wars involving Japan, exploitation, class struggle, and potentially the Soviet Union or communist ideas. Historians might find it valuable for understanding the dissemination of anti-capitalist and anti-war thought in colonial Korea, the nature of censorship, and the intellectual responses to global events like World War I and the Great Depression.

戰爭은 民族의 衝突인가
부루주아들은 戰爭을 한 民族과 民族과의 衝突이라고 본다. 이 부루주아 戰爭觀은 그의 모든 宣傳機關 新聞, 雜誌 等을 通하야 小부루주아 未覺醒 勞働者 農民 大衆사이에까지 뿌리깊이 박혀잇다. 이와 같은 戰爭觀은 必然的으로 다음과 같은 結論을 짓게 한다. 『一民族이 戰爭을 通하야 他民族을 支配할 때에는 支配民族은 全體的으로 經濟的으로나 文化的으로나 훨신 幸福스런 生活을 할 수 잇다』는 다시 말하면 戰爭은 그 國民全體에 幸福을 준다는. 이러한 XX은 그 國民이 經濟的 困境에 잇으면 잇을수록 廣汎히 일러난다. 今般 世界的 大恐慌으로 말미암아 우리는 지금 이러한 現象의 實例를 目前에 보고 잇다.
그러나 이러한 「希望」은 勞働者農民에 限하야서는 다만 한 개의 XX에 지나지 못하는 것이다. 戰爭은 決코 부루주아들이 宣傳하는 바와 같이 單純한 民族과 民族과의 衝突에서 일어나는 것이 아니요 資本主義社會에 잇어서는 各國資本家가 그의 支配力(一資本家에 의한 勞働者의 착取力)을 伸張 或은 維持하려는 데에서 일어나는 한 必然的 産物이다. 다시 말하면 戰爭은 그 戰爭을 發生케 하는 生産關係를 再生産하려는 한 手段인 것이다. 그러므로 戰爭이란 때로는 資本家에게는 크다란 利益을 준다. (中略 - 원문)

XX戰爭直後의 物價＜12＞

一例를 XX戰爭에 들어보자. XX戰爭이란 帝國主義的 列强間에 끼어서 그 發展의 最初階段을 밟게 된 XX資本主義가 그의 資本主義를 維持하고 X張하려는 데에서 일어난 한 不可避的 事實이엇다. 이 戰爭에 勝利를 얻은 XX資本主義는 XX을 XX로부터 完全히 離脫시키므로서 그의 XX에 대한 XXX을 XX하고 다시금 大陸進出의 地盤을 굳게 하얏을 뿐만 아니라 巨大한 賠償金과 XX은, XX資本主義의 더한層 發達에 貢獻한 바 잇어섯다. 그러나 XX戰爭에 의하야 XX의 XXXXXXX民은 아모러한 XX을 보지 못하얏섯다.(4行略 - 원문)

戰爭直後 物價는 戰時公債의 發行과 XX銀行의 企業資金貸出의 激增에 의한 兌換券 發行增加에 關連하야 激烈한 騰貴를 示하얏다. 이러한 것은 一方資本家에게는 利潤增大를 주엇으나 他方XXXXX 俸給生活者에게는 다만 生活의 XX를 주엇슬 뿐이엇다. 그 뿐 아니라 戰後 政府財政의 膨張-陸海軍進擴弱費의 加速度的 增大, 企業保助金의 增加 等에 依한은 必然的으로 XXXX民의 負擔增大로 歸結되지 않으면 아니되엇다. 그러므로 XX戰爭 以後의 XX資本主義의 發達, 資本의 蓄積은 XXXXX에게는 XX의 蓄積에 지나지 못하얏다.

世界大戰時의 米國大衆生活
