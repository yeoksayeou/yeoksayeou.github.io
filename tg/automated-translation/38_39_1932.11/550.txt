Article Title: 聖書三代 外 五篇 (The Bible, Three Generations, and Five Other Poems)
Author: 張瑞彦
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Autumn Ode

Moon, swallow me
How did Yi T'aebaek say life tasted?
A night I want my body swallowed whole by the moon
Because it's a quiet night, past midnight
I take the gramophone and stroll in the public cemetery.

The sound of a snake slithering—
Dry leaves rustle—

The autumn wind is born from the snake's hole
A wind that has devoured the poisonous snake's flesh and urine
When the chilly wind softly flutters
Falling leaves shed tears, rustling down

In the grave I now sit upon
Which hero lies asleep?
The moonlight, on the tips of rough grass
Puts old dreams to sleep.

The sound of the gramophone scattering over the graves in the night sky
Is the thin melody of the elegy
The sigh of which corpse?
The weeping of which young person who died suffering from some terrible illness?

Moonlit Night

A thief, intending to climb over the wall, peeks from the forest, sees the moon
And thinking there's a policeman (without a sword) in the moon, cannot steal anything and weeps on this moonlit night.
Oh, this moonlit night, when I somehow want to steal anything at all
And forcibly give it to that thief.

Lumpen and Sunday <118>

In front of the bulletin board of a suspended newspaper company
Stands staring all day long at an old extra edition
Oh, dreadful boredom!
If only I too had a truly genuine Sunday.

The Bible, Three Generations

Grandfather
Enshrined the Bible on the altar and offered rites day and night.
Father
Read the Bible and offered prayers on moonlit nights.
The son
With a hungry gut, clutches the Bible under his arm and circles restlessly before the pawn shop door.

July Noon

Seeking the shade of the chestnut tree
The wind has closed its eyes.
On the white granite that the old stonemason was chiseling yesterday noon
Lies a lizard
It being a sultry summer day
The noon scene is quieter than night.

*** Seeking the shade
*** Blazing balsam flowers.
Under the sunlight
That shines down dazzlingly bright
Only the blue eyes
Of a lizard trying to escape are watching
It being a sultry summer day
The noon scene is quieter than night.

Barber's Spring

Spring fairies
Come to get their hair bobbed.

The cat dressed in crimson satin dozes
Into the glass window steals
The colors of a prism, covering it like a veil (面紗).

The old stove, smoking a darkly dead cigarette butt
Lies collapsed, weakly.

The goldfish in the fishbowl
Follows its dream towards the Dragon Palace (龍宮), towards home
And the young barber, standing in a field
Snips at the cloud-like grass with scissors

Your silent song, do not cease
Oh, skylark dozing in the mural. <119>
<118-119>

**Summary**

This collection of six poems by Chang Sŏ-ŏn presents vignettes of modern life, alienation, and sensory experience in 1930s Korea. Themes include melancholy contemplation ("Autumn Ode"), ironic observation of social disparity ("Moonlit Night"), urban ennui and unemployment ("Lumpen and Sunday"), generational shifts and economic hardship eroding religious tradition ("The Bible, Three Generations"), the stillness of nature ("July Noon"), and the quiet, dreamlike atmosphere of a modern setting ("Barber's Spring"). From a historical perspective, the poems are interesting for their depiction of modern sensibilities blending with traditional imagery (gramophone in a cemetery, Bible generations ending at a pawn shop), reflecting the social and economic anxieties of colonial modernity. The mention of a suspended newspaper, a "lumpen" figure, and the pawn shop points to the precariousness and hardships faced by Koreans during this period.

秋賦
달아 나를 삼켜다고
李太白이 살맛은 어떠 하드냐
나의 몸을 달님에게 통자로 삼키우고 싶은 밤
고요한 밤이기에 子正이 넘어서
留聲器들고 共同墓地에 산보를 하네.

배암 기는 소리-
가랑 닢은 바삭-

가을 바람은 배암 구멍에서 생긴 바람
毒잇는 배암의 살과 오줌을 먹어 삼킨 바람
싸늘한 바람이 가만히 나부낄 때
落葉닢은 우수수 눈물 흘리고

내지금 앉어 잇는 무덤속엔
어느 英雄이 잠들어 잇노
달빛은 거츤 풀 끝에
옛꿈을 잠재우고 잇네.

밤 하눌에 흐터지는 무덤우에 留聲器소리
에레지의 가느다란 곡조는
어느 송장의 한숨인고
어느 몹슬 병을 앓다 죽은 젊은 사람의 울음인고.

달밤
담을 넘어 들어 가려든 盜賊놈이 숲속에서 엿보고 잇는 달을 보고는
달속에 巡査(칼차지 않은)가 잇다고 물건을 못 훔치고 울고 잇는 달밤.
나는 어떠케 해서라도 무엇이든지 훔처다가
그 盜賊놈에게 강제로 주고 싶은 오 달밤.

룸펜과 日曜日 ＜118＞

休刊한 新聞社 揭示板앞에서
묵은 號外한장을 왼종일 드려다 보고 섯네
오 지독한 倦怠여!
내게도 참 眞情 日曜日이 잇엇으면.

聖書三代
하라버지는
聖書를 祭壇우에 모시고 밤낮으로 제사를 올렷습니다.
아버지는
聖書를 읽으며 달밤에 祈禱를 올렷습니다.
아들은
굼주린 창자에 聖書를 옆에 끼고 典當舖 문 앞을 빙빙 돌고 잇습니다.

七月의 正午
밤나무 그림자를 찾노라니
바람은 눈을 감엇슴네.
어젯낮 늙은 石手쟁이가 쪼코 잇든
하얀 花崗石 우에는 도마뱀 한 마리
무더운 여름날이어니
正午의 풍경은 밤보다 고요하다.

*** 그림자를 찾노라니
*** 불붓는 봉선화 꽃.
쨍쨍이 눈부시게 나려쪼이는
日光밑에
도망가려는 도마뱀의
파란 눈만이 엿보고 잇는
무더운 여름날이어니
正午의 풍경은 밤보다 고요하다.

理髮師의 봄
봄의 妖精들이
단발하러 옵니다.

자주공단 옷을 입은 고양이는 졸고 잇는데
유리창으로 숨여드는
프리즘의 彩色은 面紗를 덮어 줍니다.

늙은 暖爐는 감아케 죽은 담배불을
빨며 힘없이 쓰러저 잇습니다.

어항속에 금붕어는
龍宮으로 고향으로 꿈을 따르고
젊은 理髮師는 벌판에 서서
