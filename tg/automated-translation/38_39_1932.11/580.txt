Road Improvement and Farmers     P'ayŏngsaeng

Regardless of the province, farmers are commonly suffering due to the road improvement issue, but I believe our T'aech'ŏn X-myŏn is suffering considerably more than other regions.
The principle should be to do this work during the farmers' off-season (農閒期), but in reality, they proceed according to their own convenience, paying no heed to the farmers' circumstances. Ordering them to constantly work on the roads without even leisure time for farming – where else could there be such a frustrating situation?
Moreover, aren't we Chosŏn people in a situation where, even after farming year-round through spring, summer, autumn, and winter, shedding beads of sweat without a moment's rest, we end up with nothing to eat? Isn't this why men *** leave behind their deeply cherished native land (故國山川), shedding tears *****, heading down an endless path of wandering beyond ***** into the vast plains (曠野) of North and South Manchuria (南北滿洲) and struggling in the cold winds of West *****?
If great effort (功力) was put into laying gravel on the road, soil should have been spread on top right away, but it was left for a year. Now, during the busiest season, they order the soil to be spread, but they don't even come to see the soil spreading. So, there are places where gravel was laid and places where it wasn't, places where soil was spread and places where it wasn't, creating a road of 3 or 4 layers, which is actually worse than before. Never mind providing convenience for traffic (交通上 便利), why is it causing hindrance instead?
If the order came from the Province or the County, it would be (Omitted - original text) but this road work is being done under the direction of the township office (面所) and township officials (面職員). Why make such a complex road, a two-lane road (二間道路), where so much grass grows in the middle that children bring cows morning and evening to graze on the grass in the middle of the road? Is that a pleasant sight?
In other townships, they aren't doing anything, let alone roadwork, but our township reportedly received an award this year for being a "Superior Township" (優良面). Perhaps they want to appear in the official gazette once more for being a "Superior Township"? Isn't it fine not to receive such awards?
Also, matters concerning roads are the affair of the township office (面所), so what concern is it of the gentlemen officers (나린님네) from the police substation (駐在所)? Is it for killing time? Or did the township officials perhaps treat them to drinks and request (付托) their involvement?
What relation do the policemen (巡査) have with roadwork, commenting on whether it's done well or poorly and striking the common people indiscriminately?

Defense of Machine Civilization     Pak Chisu, Inch'ŏn

Machines, produced through human toil and research, are in turn conquering humans. Then, should humans be conquered by the machines they painstakingly researched and produced? Or should they destroy and discard those machines? Is there no other ingenious plan besides these? It seems we can save humanity not by adopting passive policies like Gandhi's (깐디) machine-breaking theory, but through machine completion and reform of the machine factory system. Gandhi's machine-breaking theory was unconsciously advocated from a mindset that opposed and cursed (咀呪) the civilization of advanced nations. <125>
***** If Gandhi had consciously ***** advocated machine *****, we can only see Gandhi's machine ***** as stemming from Gandhi's lack of understanding of machines.

Let us turn back the pages of history books and examine (考察) the significance of the origin of machines.
It was autumn in a farming village, when rice harvested was pounded in a mortar morning and evening for meals. One clever (怜悧) farmer found it extremely bothersome (괴롭게) to pound rice morning and evening for just one meal. That farmer thought, couldn't he pound several months' worth of food in a day or a few days and store it? After having this thought, the farmer couldn't get it out of his head (머리에서 떠날 때가 없엇다). However, the research just wouldn't progress (좀처록 硏究가 나서지를 아니 하얏다). Several years passed with this research. One day, he went out to the yard and stood there. Right in front of where the farmer sat, water was flowing swiftly down the stream. The farmer watched intently as the water flowed swiftly down. A little further down, the water suddenly dropped. The spot where the water fell was hollowed out by the force of the falling water. Seeing the hollowed-out spot caused by the falling water, the farmer realized the great power of water. He thought, couldn't he utilize this power of water to create some tool (道具) that would be convenient for us?
After thinking and researching in this manner for many months and years, he finally discovered (發見) a brilliant ingenious plan (一大光明의 妙案): the machine called the water mill (물방아). After researching and manufacturing the water mill and using it for several years, another farmer researched and manufactured a water wheel (水車), using the rotating power of the water wheel to pound rice for food faster than the water mill. What could be the significance of that farmer's invention of the water mill and water wheel? There likely isn't any other significance. It was solely to lessen his own (human) labor (勞力) that he invented those machines.
Is the modern system of machine civilization incapable of reform? Machine civilization has an easy potential for reform. We misunderstand that modern machine civilization has reached its peak (尖端的 地境).
However, we must know that the present is merely a transitional period (過渡期) for machine civilization.

Agricultural Diversification
Yi Sŭnghwi

Raw silk (生糸), which sold for around 450 wŏn during the spring silkworm cocoon (春蠶繭) selling season, suddenly broke through 1,000 wŏn this autumn, so the silk industry (蠶業界) will surely experience great prosperity (大盛旺) going forward. Just how indispensable the silk industry (蠶業) is to Japan (日本) can be known by investigating its exports (輸出品), and this crucial silk industry had suffered a major blow for the past 6 years, bringing the sericulture sector in Chosŏn almost to the point of ruin (滅亡할 地境). However, the government still encouraged (獎勵) sericulture. This was undoubtedly because sericulture had some profit (利益) compared to other industries. But what attitude (態度) did Chosŏn sericulturists take? They dreamed of the time over ten years ago when one kwan (一貫) sold for 14 wŏn or even 6 wŏn, only recognized the current crash in cocoon prices (繭價暴落), and failed to compare it with other industries, thus advocating the abandonment of sericulture (養蠶廢止). And so, they dug up (캐버리고) all the so-called ideal mulberry fields (桑園), completed with hardship over 5 or 6 years, and now soybeans (大豆) flourish (茂盛).
Did they really calculate the precise ratio (精確한 比例) between soybeans and sericulture? Then, now that cocoon prices have rebounded (反騰) and ordinary crops remain sluggish (不振) as before, they probably regret it. What use is there in regretting, saying, "If only I had kept it for one more year (올한해만 더 두엇드라면)"? Just as the saying goes, "Diligent farming originates from diversification (精農은 多角形으로부터 生한다)," monotonous agriculture is one of the causes of rural stagnation. Sericulture is one example, and cotton (棉花) cultivation is another. Because the price of cotton fell disastrously last year, not many people cultivated cotton this year. Indeed, hasn't cotton rebounded incomparably compared to last year? The need for rural diversification (農村多角化) is felt even more keenly (切實히). <126>

**Summary**

These three short articles from a 1932 Korean magazine discuss pressing issues of the time. The first details farmer complaints about poorly managed, burdensome road construction projects imposed by local authorities (likely under Japanese colonial direction), highlighting the disruption to farming, poor quality of work, and unwelcome police involvement. The second piece defends technological progress, arguing against anti-machine sentiments (like Gandhi's) by framing machines as tools to reduce human labor, citing historical examples, and viewing contemporary machine civilization as a transitional phase needing reform, not rejection. The third article advocates for agricultural diversification, criticizing farmers who abandoned sericulture and cotton farming due to short-term price drops, only to miss out on subsequent rebounds, arguing diversification is key to rural economic stability.

From a historical perspective, these articles offer valuable glimpses into Korean society under Japanese colonial rule. The first article provides direct testimony of the hardships faced by farmers due to colonial development projects, including forced labor mobilization (corvée labor was common), bureaucratic inefficiency, and police oversight extending into civil matters. The second reflects intellectual engagement with global ideas (Gandhi, modernization) within Korea. The third illustrates the economic precarity of colonial agriculture, subject to volatile markets possibly influenced by Japanese economic priorities, and highlights local responses and debates about farming practices. Historians could find the specific grievances about roadwork, the discussion of emigration, the critique of local administration cooperating with police, the engagement with modernization theories, and the detailed account of agricultural economic fluctuations particularly interesting.

機械文明擁護     仁川 朴智秀

人間이 苦心 硏究 産出하야 논 機械가 도리어 人間을 征服한다. 그러면 人間이 苦心 硏究 産出시키여 논 그 機械에게 征服을 받아야 옳은가? 또는 그 機械를 破碎하야 바리어야 옳은가? 이 外에 또 그 무슨 妙案은 없을 것인가? 우리는 깐디의 機械破碎論과 같은 消極的 政策을 쓰지 아니하고 機械完成과 機械工場制度改革으로써 人類를 匡濟할수 잇을 것 같다. 깐디의 機械破碎論은 先進國家의 文明을 敵對하고 咀呪하는 心中에서 無意識的으로 機械破碎論을 主張한 것이다.＜125＞
***** 아니하고 깐디가 意識的으로 機 ***** 主張하얏다면 우리는 깐디의 機**** 은 깐디의 機械認識不足에서 나온 *이라고 볼수밖에 없다.

우리는 歷史冊의 페지를 거슬러 올라가서 機械創始 意義를 考察하야 보려 한다.
農村에서 가을에 秋收하야 벼을 朝夕으로 절구에 찌어서 糧食을 할 때다. 한 怜悧한 農夫는 朝夕으로 벼를 찌어서 한끼의 糧食을 하는 것을 대단히 괴롭게 여기엇다. 그 農夫는 몇 달 먹을 糧食을 하로에나 或은 몇칠에 찌어서 두고 糧食할 수 없을까 하는 생각을 하얏다. 그 農夫는 그 생각이 든 後로부터는 그 생각이 머리에서 떠날 때가 없엇다. 그러하나 좀처록 硏究가 나서지를 아니 하얏다. 이 硏究로 몇年이라는 歲月은 흘러갓다. 하로는 뜰에 나갓다가 뜰에 서게 되엇다. 바로 農夫가 앉은 앞 개울에서 물이 철철 흘러 내려 갓다. 農夫는 그 철철 흘러 내려가는 물을 有心히 보고 잇엇다. 거기서 좀 내려가서 물은 갑자기 떠러지게 되엇다. 그 물이 떠러지는 자리는 물의 떠러지는 힘으로 오목하게 패엿다. 農夫는 물이 떠러저서 오목하게 패인 힘을 보고 스스로 물의 힘이 重大하다는 것을 깨달앗다. 저 물의 힘을 利用하야 그 무슨 道具고 우리에게 便利하게 만들어 볼 수가 없을가 하는 생각을 하얏다.
그 모양으로 여러 달과 해를 두고 생각하고 또 硏究한 結果 마침내 一大光明의 妙案을 發見하게 되엇으니 즉 물방아라 하는 機械가 그것이다. 물방아를 硏究製造하야 몇年을 使用하는 가운대서 또 다른 農夫가 水車를 硏究製造하야 水車의 廻轉하는 힘으로 물방아보다 더 速하게 벼을 찌어서 糧食하게 되엇다. 그 農夫의 물방아와 水車發明의 意義가 그 무엇일까? 別다른 意義는 없을 것이다. 오즉 自己(人間)의 勞力을 덜기 위하야 그 機械를 發明하얏다는 것일 것이다.
現代의 機械文明制度는 改革할 餘地가 없는 것인가? 機械文明은 容易한 改革性이 잇는 것이다. 우리는 現代의 機械文明이 尖端的 地境에 到達할 줄로 誤解한다.
그러나 오즉 現代가 機械文明의 過渡期라는 것을 알아야 한다.

農業多角化
李承徽

春蠶繭 販賣時에 450圓쯤 하든 生糸가 今秋에는 突然이 千圓을 突破하얏으니 앞으로 蠶業界는 大盛旺을 이를 것이다. 대저 蠶業이 日本에 얼마나 없지 못할 産業인가는 그 輸出品을 調査하면 알려니와 그 重大한 蠶業이 6年以來의 大打擊을 받아 朝鮮의 養蠶界는 거이 滅亡할 地境에 이르럿섯다. 그러나 政府는 亦是 養蠶을 獎勵하얏다. 그것은 勿論 養蠶이 他産業과 比하야 多少의 利益이 잇엇음은 틀림없는 事實이다. 그러나 朝鮮養蠶家는 어떠한 態度를 取하얏든가. 그들은 거금 拾餘年前 一貫에 14圓 乃至 6圓하든 때를 꿈꾸며 現在 繭價暴落만을 알고 他産業과 比較해 보지 못하얏으므로 養蠶廢止를 主唱하얏다. 그리고 5, 6年을 辛苦하야 겨우 完成된 所謂 理想的 桑園을 모조리 캐버리고 目下 大豆가 茂盛하다.
그들이 果然 大豆와 養蠶의 精確한 比例를 내어 보앗든가? 그러다가 現今 繭價는 反騰하고 普通 農作物은 如前히 不振하니 아마 그들은 後悔할 것이다. 「올한해만 더 두엇드라면」 하고 後悔하는 무슨 所用이 잇으랴. 「精農은 多角形으로부터 生한다」는 말과 같이 單調한 農業이야말로 農村不振의 原因의 하나이다. 養蠶도 그러하려니와 棉花栽培도 그러타. 昨年 棉花代가 可히 말할수 없이 暴落되엇기 때문에 今年에 棉花栽培한 사람이 얼마되지 안는다. 實로 棉花는 昨年과 比할 수 없이 反騰하지 안햇나. 農村多角化는 一層 切實히 느껴진다.＜126＞
