New Book Introductions, Reading Room

Yi Kwangsu, Author; 『Yi Sunsin』

This novel is so widely known that there is hardly any need for a fresh introduction. It was written upon commission from the *Tong'a Ilbo* at a time when memories of this great man were evoked nationwide, triggered by last year's issue concerning the land attached to Yi Sunsin's tomb (位土 問題). This book, which could be called a biography despite being a novel, is a large volume of over 600 pages in quarto format (四六版).

Traces of the author's effort to portray Yi Sunsin more as a man of character than as a brilliant general are visible in this novel. How is Yi Sunsin idealized in this novel?
(1) He transcended the factional disputes (色論) of the time and was solely faithful to the duties entrusted to him.
(2) Although he was falsely accused several times due to the slander (謅訴) of petty men (小人), he neither made excuses nor harbored resentment.
(3) Through his magnanimity (厚德), he won the admiration and loyalty (悅服) of soldiers, the people, superiors, and famous generals.

The author places stronger emphasis (力点) on these few points than on his brilliant victories or his wisdom and courage in repelling large enemy forces with a small army. To emphasize this, the author thoroughly depicts the petty men (小人輩) of the royal court (朝廷) at the time. They are all portrayed as a cowardly group, habitually engaging in false incrimination and slander (誣陷中傷), filled with jealousy (猜忌心), and only good with their words. It is unknown to what extent this portrayal is close to the truth (眞), but such exaggeration through contrast is commonly used (恒用) by novelists to achieve effect (効果).

While Yi Sunsin's character in this novel is undoubtedly based on references to historical records (史乘), there are also many traces where it aligns with the author's own thoughts, clothed in those ideas and idealized. That is, being taciturn (寡言沈黙), transcending criticism and praise (毁譽褒貶), and confronting the secret machinations (陰害) of petty men with the power of non-resistant (無抵抗) character can be seen as embodying the author's view of a great man.

In the *Sad History of King Tanjong* (端宗哀史), we saw the "goodness" (善) of King Tanjong and the Six Martyred Ministers (死六臣) absolutely exaggerated, and likewise, the "evil" (惡) of King Sejo and Chŏng Sin (鄭申) and others also absolutely exaggerated. Similarly, in 『Yi Sunsin』, only Yi Sunsin stands toweringly tall, giving the impression that among the remaining civil and military officials (文武百官), common people, and soldiers (人民軍卒), there is not a single admirable person. In this sense, this novel falls into the "hero-worshipping" (英雄崇拜的) archetype. It is a work with a Romantic (浪漫主義的) tendency rather than Naturalistic (自然主義). And consequently, it can be called a popular (通俗的) work.

Discussing it in terms of novelistic interest (小說的 興味), what first seems somewhat lacking is that only Yi Sunsin is idolized (偶像化) in such a manner, and there is a failure to intersperse (点綴) supporting characters (輔佐人物) among his subordinates (部下) who could play the role of secondary protagonists. Furthermore, the complete lack (缺如) of a female protagonist or romantic elements between men and women is another point. It might have been interesting if there had at least been a female spy (女子密探). The author might protest that this could not be done since it is a biography, not a novel, but the reviewer (評者) insists it is appropriate to view this entirely as a novel, and since it is a novel, the lack of embellishment (潤色) is considered a flaw (缺點).

In any case (何如間), the author's intention (意圖) to provide an object of faith (信仰) to Chosŏn, which lacks great men, to Chosŏn, which does not know its own heroes, can be said to have been fully achieved (達成). Adding criticism to the writing style (筆致) would be rather superfluous (蛇足); in terms of format (體裁), the Western-style cover (洋裝表紙), illustrations (揷畵), title calligraphy (題字), etc., are groundbreaking (劃期的) for the Chosŏn publishing world. Even a portrait (影幀) is included as a frontispiece (屛畵). The fixed price (定價) of two Won is definitely not expensive for this book. (Price 2 Won, Taesŏng Sŏrim [大成書林], Kasai-chō [笠井町], Kyŏngsŏng, Postal Transfer Kyŏngsŏng 12328) (D)

Kim Ŏk, Author; 『Esseup'erant'o Soksŏng』 (Revised Edition)

Mr. Kim Ŏk has put out another <127> booklet titled 『Esseup'erant'o Soksŏng』 (Esperanto Rapid Course) for a two-week workshop. It need hardly be said again that Mr. Kim Ŏk is a leading figure (重鎭) in Chosŏn's Esperanto linguistic circles (語學界), and his zeal (熱誠) for the Esperanto language is truly astonishing. He has striven to establish (設置) Esperanto columns in newspapers, magazines, etc., held workshops (講習會) several times (數次), published standalone books (單行本), become a principal member (主要한 一員) in the publication of the Esperanto magazine 「Ra. Boch'yo」, and whenever meeting friends (親故), has emphasized (高調) that Esperanto is the easiest language to learn among all world languages and that it has practical value.

Esperanto is no longer in the publicity (宣傳) stage but already in the practical application stage (實用期). Esperanto is so widespread (普及) that there is hardly any kind of book unavailable in Esperanto regarding science, religion, economics, politics, or entertainment, and there is no excellent literature from various countries around the world that has not been translated into Esperanto. "Seeing cultures compete to introduce themselves through Esperanto, we keenly (切實) feel the need to learn this language as quickly as possible." This is the reason (所以) the author is enthusiastic about Esperanto.

『Esseup'erant'o Soksŏng』 is a revised and enlarged edition (改正增版) created because the author felt dissatisfied (不滿) with the previous 『Esseup'erant'o Soksŏng』 he wrote, based on the practical experience (實地經驗) gained from workshops. Adding poetry and prose poems (散文詩) to the practice exercises (練習問題) is something hard to imagine were it not for the poet (詩人) Kim Ŏk.

This booklet is not for absolute beginners (初學者) but is made for workshop use or for persons with a grounding (素養) in foreign languages. This booklet is recommended for those with foreign language knowledge who wish to learn Esperanto in the shortest possible time (短期間) through diligent study. This booklet is recommended for those who wish to understand poetry in Esperanto in a short time. (Price 50 Chŏn, Published by Tonggwangdang Sŏjŏm [東光堂書店], Postal Transfer Kyŏngsŏng 16121, Chrysanthemum format [菊版], 50 pages)
For beginners, there is Kim Ŏk's 『Esŭp'erant'o Tan'gi Kwangjwa』 (Esperanto Short Course). (Price 50 Chŏn, Published by Hansŏng Toso Chusik Hoesa [漢城圖書株式會社]) (L)<128>

**Summary**

This article from the November 1932 issue of *Tonggwang* reviews two new books. The first is Yi Kwangsu's biographical novel *Yi Sunsin*, praised for its portrayal of Yi Sunsin's idealized character (transcending factionalism, enduring slander without resentment, possessing magnanimity) but criticized for its hero-worship, lack of compelling supporting characters, absence of romance, and romantic rather than naturalistic style, though its intent to provide a national hero figure is acknowledged. The second review covers Kim Ŏk's revised Esperanto textbook, *Esseup'erant'o Soksŏng*, highlighting Kim's dedication to promoting Esperanto in Chosŏn and the language's growing practical utility worldwide, recommending the book for those with prior language experience seeking rapid learning. For historians, the reviews offer insights into the cultural milieu of colonial Korea: the nationalistic interest in historical figures like Yi Sunsin (and the specific lens, emphasizing character over just military prowess), the debate between romantic/popular literature and realism, the active Esperanto movement as a potential avenue for international connection and alternative cultural expression, and details about the publishing industry (publishers, pricing, book formats).

新刊紹介, 讀書室
李光洙 著  『李舜臣』
이 小說은 너무도 넓리 알려진 것이매 새삼스러이 紹介할 必要가 없다. 去年의 李舜臣 位土 問題를 契機로 이 偉人에 대한 記憶이 全民族的으로 喚起된 때에 東亞日報의 依囑으로 써내린 것이 이것이다. 小說이면서도 傳記라고 할 이 冊은 四六版 600餘頁의 大冊이다.
作者는 이 小說에서 李舜臣을 名將으로서 보다도 人格者로서 나타내고저 努力한 痕跡이 보인다. 李舜臣은 이 小說에 잇어서 어떠케 理想化되어 잇는가.
(1) 그는 當時의 色論에 超越하고 오직 自己의 맡은 任務에만 忠實하엿다.
(2) 그는 小人의 謅訴에 의하야 여러번 無實의 罪를 입엇으되 辯明도 아니하고 怨妄도 아니 하엿다.
(3) 그는 厚德으로서 兵士, 人民, 上官, 名將等을 悅服케 하엿다.
이런 멫 가지 点을 作者는 그의 善戰이라든가 小軍으로 大敵을 물리치는 智勇보다도 더 힘잇게 力点을 두엇다. 이것을 强調하기 위하야 作者는 當時의 朝廷의 小人輩들을 餘地없이 그리내엇다. 全部가 卑怯하고 誣陷中傷을 일삼고 猜忌心이 가득하고 입만 살아 잇는 무리로 되어 잇다. 이 描寫가 어느 程度까지 眞에 가까울는지 모르나 小說家로서 効果를 얻기 위해서는 이런 對照의 誇張은 恒用 쓰는 것이다.
이 小說의 나라는 李舜臣의 人格은 勿論 史乘에 나타난 것을 參照하야 된 것이려니와 그中에는 다시 著者의 思想과 一致되고 맞는 그 思想으로 옷 입혀 理想化한 痕跡이 많다고 할 것이다. 卽 寡言沈黙하고 毁譽褒貶에 超越하며 小人의 陰害을 無抵抗의 人格力으로 대한다는 것이 著者의 一偉人觀을 俱現한 것이라고 볼수 잇다.
端宗哀史에서 우리는 端宗과 및 死六臣의 「善」이 絶對的으로 誇張되고 世祖와 및 鄭申 等의 「惡」 역시 絶對的으로 誇張된 것을 보앗거니와 「李舜臣」에 잇어서도 「李舜臣」하나만이 우뚝 솟고 其餘의 文武百官이며 人民軍卒까지도 하나도 잘난 놈은 없는 것 같은 感想을 준다. 이 意味에서 이 小說은 「英雄崇拜的」 典型에 드는 小說이다. 自然主義보다도 浪漫主義的 傾向을 띤 作品이다. 그리고 따라서 通俗的 作品이라고 할 것이다.
小說的 興味로서 論한다고 하면 우선 좀 不足하게 생각되는 것은 李舜臣만이 그와 같이 偶像化되고 그 部下의 第2의 主人公 노릇을 할만한 輔佐人物을 点綴하지 않은 것이다. 그리고 또 다음은 女主人公 또는 男女의 로만쓰的 材料가 全혀 缺如한 것이다. 하다못해 女子密探이라도 하나 잇엇드면 興味가 잇엇슴즉 하다. 作者는 이것이 小說이 아니요 傳記인 以上 그리할 수 없다고 抗議할는지 모르나 評者로서는 이것을 어디까지든지 小說로 보는 것이 可하다고 主張하며 旣爲 小說일진대 潤色이 不足함이 缺點이라고 생각한다.
何如間 偉人을 아니가진 朝鮮, 自己의 英雄을 모르는 朝鮮에게 한個의 信仰의 對象을 주려하는 作者의 意圖는 充分히 達成햇다고 할 것이다.
그 筆致는 批評을 加함이 도리어 蛇足일 것이요 體裁에 잇어서 洋裝表紙에 揷畵, 題字 等이 朝鮮出版界로서는 劃期的이다. 影幀까지 屛畵로 넣어 잇다. 二圓이라는 定價가 決코 비싸지 않은 冊이다. (定價2圓, 京城笠井町 大成書林, 振替京城 12328番) (D)
