Article Title: 短詩數篇 (A Few Short Poems)
Author: 金杚白
Article Type: 시 (Poetry)

1. This Heart

Couldn't this heart
be put inside that tree?
Even into space,
the freedom of my heart
would not be taken away.

Couldn't this heart
be submerged in that water and flow away?
Even into the water's depths,
the advance of my heart
would not be blocked.

2. Sent to Elder Brother Kyuwŏn <87>

If you
were to die in your sickbed,
I
would become a crow.
Sadly, sadly, I will cry.

If you
were to die on the battlefield,
I
would move your dead body.
Never forgetting, always.

Therefore,
O you, my dear friend, be at ease.
As long as I am alive,
always be at ease.

3. The Joy of the Vow - Hearing PSN's Words -

Aiming the stars and the blue water
at your heart,
O you who vowed.
I am joyful,
and also glad.
Even after I die,
the stars will twinkle. <88>
The water will flow.

4. Fragmentary Thoughts

Mother
was not bound.
But when she spoke of being bound,
you all blocked your ears.

Mother
did not starve.
But when she explained her hunger,
you all walked away.

Ah, ah, this
strikes my heart.
Thump, thump, it strikes.
Endlessly, it strikes. <89>

**Summary**

This collection comprises four short poems by Kim Olbaek. The first poem expresses a yearning for the heart's liberation, wishing it could merge with nature (a tree, water) to escape confinement. The second is a pledge of unwavering loyalty and remembrance to a friend named Kyuwŏn, promising care even in death. The third celebrates a vow made by someone (inspired by "PSN"), finding solace in the enduring nature of stars and water. The final poem laments the indifference of children who ignore their mother's unspoken suffering and constraints. From a historical perspective, the poems, written during the Japanese colonial period, might reflect contemporary feelings of confinement and a longing for freedom (Poem 1), the importance of personal bonds and loyalty in difficult times (Poem 2), and perhaps a metaphorical commentary on the misunderstood sacrifices of a maternal figure, potentially representing the older generation or even the nation itself (Poem 4).

1. 이마음을
이 마음을 저 나무에
집어 넣을 수 없을까.
空間속까지
내 마음의 自由를
빼앗지 않을테니.

이 마음을 저 물속에
잠가 흘너갈 수 없을까.
물 속까지
내 마음의 나아감을
막지 않을테니.

2. 揆元兄에게 보냄. ＜87＞

그대가
병상에서 죽어진다면
나는
가마귀가 되겟소이다.
슯히 슯히 울게요.

그대가
싸흠터에 죽어진다면
나는
죽은 시체를 옴기겟소이다.
언제든지 잊지 않구요.

그러므로
그대여 나의 그대여 안심하라
내가 살아 잇을 때까지
언제든지 안심하라.

3. 맹서의 기쁨-PSN의 말을 듣고-
별 그리고 푸른 물을
가슴에 겨누며
맹서한 그네여.
나는 기쁘노라
또한 반갑아노라.
내가 죽은 후라도
별은 빤작이리라.＜88＞
물은 흐르리라.

四. 斷想
어머님은
얽매이지 않엇다.
허나 얽매 잇다는 것을 말할제는
너이들이 귀를 막든구나.

어머님은
굶지 않엇다.
허나 굶는다는 것을 설명할제는
너이들은 떠나가든구나.

아 아 이것이
나의 가슴을 치네.
퉁퉁 치네.
끝없이 치네. ＜89＞
＜87-89＞
