My Day
						Ch'unwŏn

8 AM: Wake up. Go to the toilet. Exercise for about 10 minutes. I don't know what style it is, but I swing my arms and legs while singing a song.

Brush teeth, wash face, wash feet. I've developed a habit of washing my feet when going to bed and waking up.
To make the children laugh, I do a round of dancing as exercise. This dance imitates everything I've seen, regardless of whether it's Western dance, Korean dance, Ch'oe Sŭnghŭi's (崔承喜) dance, monk dance, sword dance, shaman dance, or mask dance (山臺舞). Eat breakfast. Since I only have one kidney and also lack appetite, I avoid meat as much as possible and eat vegetables. I like any kind of fruit and eat heartily.

10 AM: Go to the newspaper company. I dislike buses, but I take the bus for convenience. Every day I vow not to take this unclean, ugly, jerky, and lowly bus, yet I take it every day.

At the newspaper company, I check the attendance of colleagues, look at the night duty log, check incoming mail, read the major newspapers, write one installment of my novel, sometimes write rambling essays (橫說竪說), write editorials (社說) if unavoidable, go in and out of the printing plant several times, get called into the president's office several times, read the editorials, rambling essays, and other major articles on each page to see if there's anything that might draw criticism or cause confiscation (押收), check each page's ledger (臺帳), see several visitors, make and receive several dozen phone calls, stamp my seal on payment slips numerous times, occasionally hold editorial meetings, occasionally listen to colleagues' complaints, and occasionally get summoned to the Police Bureau (警務局). Then, when the rotary press starts running and the newspaper comes out on time (before 4:20 PM), my day's work ends with satisfaction.

Then, I take the bus I hate and come home. If I have business, I stop by Tonggwangsa (東光社). I usually arrive home around 5:30 PM. When they hear the sound of me opening the main gate, my 5-year-old and 6-year-old children run out onto the wooden floor shouting "Daddy!" and cling to me.

Then I wash my hands and feet, eat dinner. If the phone doesn't ring constantly due to incidents like confiscation (押收), deletion (削除), or extra editions (號外) at the newspaper company, I sing and dance with the children, give them horsey rides, and explain pictures until 9 PM. Then I take charge of putting the 5-year-old to sleep by singing a lullaby. The time after that is my own, during which I read the newspaper, read books, daydream, chat with my wife, and write manuscripts. Go to bed around 11 PM. I have difficulty finding time for haircuts and baths. Visits to friends have almost entirely ceased.
This is how I live. <35>

**Summary**

The author, Ch'unwŏn (Yi Kwangsu), describes his mundane daily routine in 1932. He details his morning rituals, his commute via a disliked bus, his demanding work at a newspaper company involving writing, editing, administrative tasks, and dealing with potential censorship, and his evenings spent with his young children before dedicating late hours to personal reading and writing. From a historical perspective, the article offers a glimpse into the life of a Korean intellectual and editor during the Japanese colonial period. Particularly revealing are the casual mentions of fearing newspaper confiscation or deletion and being summoned to the Police Bureau (警務局), highlighting the ever-present pressure and scrutiny from colonial authorities on the press. The blend of modern work life (newspapers, buses) with family activities and the underlying anxiety about censorship provides valuable insight into the era.

나의 하로
						春園
午前 8時 起床. 便所에 가고. 體操 約 10分. 무슨 式인지 모르거니와 노래를 부르면서 팔다리를 내어두르는 것.

이 닦고 세수하고 발 씿고. 잘 때와 일어날 때에 발 씿는 버릇이 생겻다.
아이들 웃기노라고 體操 삼아 춤 한바탕 추고. 이 춤도 洋舞 韓舞 崔承喜舞踊 僧舞 劍舞 巫舞 山臺舞 할 것 없이 눈에 본 것은 다 숭내 내는 것. 아츰밥 먹고. 腎臟도 외쪽밖에 없을 뿐더러 嗜好도 없어서 아모조록 肉食은 避하고 菜蔬를 먹고 果實은 무엇이나 좋아하고 끓음하게 먹고.

10時에 新聞社에 가고. 뻐스를 싫여 하지마는 便利 때문에 뻐스를 타고. 날마다 이 不潔하고 醜하고 까불고 낮고 한 뻐스를 안타자고 맹세하면서도 날마다 타고.

新聞社에 가서는 同僚들의 出席狀況을 보고 宿直簿를 보고 書信 온 것을 보고 主要한 新聞을 보고 小說을 한回 쓰고 或時 橫說竪說을 쓰고 不得已하면 社說도 쓰고 工場에 멫 번 들락날락 社長室에 멫 번 불리어 가고 社說 橫說竪說 其他 各面의 主要記事를 읽어 혹시 是非 들을 것이나 없나 혹시 押收當할 것이나 없나를 보고 各面 臺帳을 보고 來客 멫 분을 보고 電話 멫 十次 주고 받고 그리고 傳票에 圖章을 멫 번 찍고 間或 編輯會議를 하고 間或 同僚의 不平을 듣고 間或 警務局에 불리고. 그리고 輪轉機가 돌아 新聞이 제 時間(午後 4時 20分 以前)에 나오는 것을 보고 나면 滿足으로써 내 하로의 職業이 끝이난다.

그리고는 곳 타기 싫은 뻐스를 타고 집으로 오고. 일 잇으면 東光社에 들르고. 집에 오면 대개 5時 半 前後. 내가 大門 여는 소리를 들으면 5歲兒 6歲兒가 「아빠!」하고 마루로 뛰여 나와 매어 달리고.

그리고 손 씿고 발 씿고 저녁 먹고 新聞社에 押收 削除 號外 等 事故가 잇어 電話가 無時로 울지 아니하면 아이들과 노래하고 춤추고 말되어 태워 주고 그림 설명하다가 9時가 되면 5歲兒 자장가 불러 맡아 재우고 그리고 난 뒤가 내 時間이어서 新聞도 보고 讀書도 하고 空想도 하고 夫妻間에 談話도 하고 原稿도 쓰고. 11時 前後에 就寢. 理髮沐浴할 餘暇가 없어서 困難. 親舊訪問은 거이 全廢.
이러케 살아갑니다.＜35＞
