기사제목 人生 (Life)
필자 朴永山
기사형태 시 (Poem)

Life
						Pak Yŏngsan

Life is like a ship.
Like a ship disappearing aimlessly
into the fog of the vast sea.

Though it goes and cannot return,
still the ship just keeps going.

The ship that departed without even a trace,
the fog swallows it eternally. <92>

**Summary**

This poem by Pak Yŏngsan compares life to a ship that sails into the vast, foggy sea, never to return. It emphasizes the aimless, irreversible, and ultimately consumed nature of existence, vanishing without a trace into eternity. From a historical perspective, the poem's themes of aimlessness, disappearance, and being swallowed by an overwhelming force (the fog/sea) could reflect the feelings of uncertainty, loss of direction, or lack of agency experienced by Koreans under Japanese colonial rule in the 1930s. Historians might find the poem's melancholic tone and imagery indicative of the prevailing mood and existential concerns during that period.
