**Body Text Translation:**
Song of the Young Farmer
						Kim T'aeo

Oh, listen, young sir!
I am a young farmer of this land.
Nineteen years old now—a young farmer I am.
I lost my beloved parents as a suckling infant<89>
And grew up amidst tears and sighs.
In poverty and injustice! In this sunless darkness,
This body has lived like a cow or a horse.

Sometimes I went up the mountain and came back having chopped trees.
Sometimes I went out to the fields, dug the earth, and sowed seeds.
Sometimes I took up the plow and tilled the land barefoot.

In winter, I slept curled up like a shrimp in an ice-like cold stone room,
And in summer, I stayed up all night on rough stones.
From the age of nine, I cursed the world.

At the stern commands of the cruel master X, fat like a pig,
I cannot tell how much I trembled in fear.

Though for ten years of my servitude, with endless devotion,
I drew out blood and sweat each year to create the fragrance of autumn,
Have I ever freely touched even a single grain of rice?

In my ten years of hardship, the only treasure that remains
Is this iron-like body (肉體) and steel-like frame (鋼鐵).
Now, this body's anger has pierced every joint,
And hot, surging breath boils up in my chest.
(Following 6 lines omitted - original text)<90>
<89-90>

**Summary**

The poem "Song of the Young Farmer" by Kim T'aeo portrays the harsh life of a nineteen-year-old farmer who was orphaned young and endured a decade of servitude under a cruel master. He recounts his endless toil – logging, farming, plowing – and suffering – cold, hunger, fear – without reaping any personal benefit from his labor. Despite the hardship, he emphasizes his enduring physical strength ("iron-like body," "steel-like frame") and a growing, deep-seated anger against his exploitation. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this poem is significant as it vividly depicts the plight and resentment of tenant farmers or indentured laborers, a common reality under Japanese rule. The farmer's narrative of exploitation, endurance, and rising anger reflects the socio-economic tensions and burgeoning resistance sentiment within the oppressed rural population during that period, offering historians insight into the lived experiences and emotional landscape of the colonized peasantry.

少年農夫의 노래
						金泰午
오오 여보오, 젊은 그대여!
나는 이 땅의 少年農夫외다.
지금이 열아홉살-젊은 農夫로소이다.
나는 젓먹이로 사랑하는 부모를 여이고＜89＞
눈물과 한숨으로 자라난 사람이외다.
가난과 억울! 햇빛없는 이 어둠속에서
이 몸은 소와 말같이 살아왓나이다.

때로는 산에 올라 나무를 찍어왓더이다.
때로는 들에 나가 땅파고 씨뿌렷더이다.
때로는 쟁기들고 맨발로 땅을 갈엇더이다.

겨울에는 어름장같은 랭돌방에서 새우잠을
자고
여름에는 거츠른 돌우에서 밤을 새웟나이다.
아홉 살때부터 나는 世上을 咀呪하엿나이다.

도야지처름 살찐 잔인한 주인X의 추상같은
호령에
나는 얼마나 무서워 떨엇는지 모르겟나이다.

내가 머슴사리 10年에 끝없는 정성으로
해마다 피땀을 뽑아 가을의 香氣를 이루건만
벼알 하나라도 맘대로 건드려나 보더이까?

내가 고생사리 10年에 오직 남아 잇는 하나의 보배는
이 무쇠같은 肉體와 鋼鐵같은 뼛대외다.
지금에 이 몸의 분노는 뼛마디마다 사모첫고
불룩거리는 더운 호흡은 가슴에 끓어오르나이다.
