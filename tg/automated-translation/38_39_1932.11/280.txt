Seeking Miss Yi XX, the First Generation Korean Nun
						ABC

That the first generation nun had emerged since the establishment of a convent (修道院) in Chosŏn piqued my curiosity. Thus, one autumn afternoon, with the gentle breeze fluttering my collar, I decided to visit the Chŏngdong Convent (貞洞 修道院), tucked away down a narrow street off Kwanghwamun-t'ong (光化門通).

Passing through the Korean-style main gate (大門) and entering the convent where the virgins (童貞女) live, I asked the gatekeeper if I could see a nun. "Secular people (俗人) are not permitted to meet them," the old man replied, dismissing me with a single phrase as if he hadn't heard properly, and refused outright. That seemed right. It is not strange at all that we, who fight against reality in this dusty world (塵世), cannot meet face-to-face with those whose companions are the Sign of the Cross (十字聖號) and whose partners are the Holy Scripture (聖書).

However, wanting somehow to hear about her background and life, I first met the Mother Superior (修女長). Her appearance—standing there in her white habit, changed according to the season, ears covered by a black veil (미사보) to avoid worldly chatter (雜談) and block external temptations, a cross (十字架) hanging around her neck—showed the sacred atmosphere of one who has transcended the chaotic secular world (混沌한 俗世), having vowed poverty (淸貧), chastity (童貞女), and obedience (忍從) to God. Her calm demeanor and the holy light appearing on her face gave a unique impression that could only be found in them. She did not seem inclined to speak at length with me, whose life is different,<68> but pretending not to notice, I persistently asked a few questions.

When this Anglican Church (聖公會) was first propagated (布敎) from America (美國) to Chosŏn (朝鮮), it began in Inch'ŏn (仁川) and spread particularly to the northern regions (北鮮地方). Here and there, grand cathedrals (宏壯한 聖堂), similar to Catholic churches (캐더릭 처취), were built with high crosses, and the sound of hymns praising "Our Lord" (吾主)—as they call Him—resounded loudly, accompanied by a solemnity (嚴肅) and discipline (規律) not found in modernized Christianity.

Consequently, seven years ago from now, in 1925, this convent was established here, gathering nuns whose purpose was ascetic practice through contemplation (黙想) and labor (勞働), spending their entire lives with God. Thus, while five people have sought refuge within this convent's flower garden (花林) so far, that Miss Yi XX was chosen among them as the first generation nun is the first page of precious history made in this house.

About 10 years ago, when she had just passed the age of twenty, she and C whispered of love on this hill and that, following the spring haze. Singing songs of youth amidst the cotton-like clouds and fragrant flower scents, they were unfortunately perhaps unlucky in love. Lamenting the love they could not achieve as desired, the two simultaneously made a vow on the same path: one decided to become a priest (神父), and the other a nun (修女). In this way, the sorrowful dream of youth was purified (淨化) through prayer and the Sign of the Cross (十字聖號), and thus began a holy life.

As the faint dawn breaks and birds chirp on the branches at five in the morning, with the tolling cathedral bell proclaiming "Be holy," the monotonous yet new daily life of these virgins begins.

These women, like farm girls (農女) holding hoes and weeding—each having come into this house for some reason—perhaps their dreams each night might become confused, perhaps distracting thoughts (雜念) might seep into their contemplative minds. The Sign of the Cross on their chests, and the prayers offered with pictures and bell sounds, may seem somewhat distant from the vibrant society of the present day, but for them, it is the path of happiness bestowed by fate. I wanted to pray that their lives might be blessed and radiant, at least on that path. (End) <69>

**Summary**

The article reports on the author's visit to the Anglican Chŏngdong Convent in Seoul to find Miss Yi, reportedly the first Korean nun of that order. After initially being denied entry to see any nun, the author interviews the Mother Superior. The piece provides background on the establishment of the Anglican Church in Korea, starting from Inch'ŏn and spreading north, noting its solemnity compared to other Protestant denominations. It recounts the founding of the convent in 1925 and highlights Miss Yi's significance as the first Korean nun there. The author relates the poignant story of Miss Yi choosing the monastic life around the age of twenty after a failed romance, making a pact with her beloved (who decided to become a priest) to dedicate their lives to God. The article concludes by describing the nuns' disciplined daily routine starting at dawn and reflecting on their secluded, prayerful existence as a chosen path of happiness, despite its distance from secular life.

For historians, this article offers a glimpse into the early presence and practices of the Anglican Church in colonial Korea, particularly its monastic tradition, which might be less commonly associated with Protestantism. It highlights the personal motivations, including heartbreak and romantic pacts, that could lead young Koreans, especially women, to choose religious vocations during this period. The comparison drawn between Anglican and Catholic churches in terms of their imposing structures and solemn rituals is also noteworthy, suggesting contemporary perceptions of different Christian denominations. The reporter's struggle to access the nuns underscores the tension between worldly curiosity and the intended seclusion of monastic life.

第一世 朝鮮人修女 李XX孃을 찾음
						ABC
朝鮮에 修道院이 생긴 以後 처음으로 第一世 修女가 낫다는 것이 내 好奇心을 자아내엇다. 그래서 나는 첫 가을 어느 날 午後 산들한 바람에 옷깃을 날리며 光化門通 한 좁은 길을 잠깐 피해 들어가 잇는 貞洞 修道院을 찾기로 하엿다.
朝鮮式 大門을 지나 童貞女들이 살고 잇는 修道院으로 들어 서서 문직이에게 修女 보기를 청하니 「俗人들은 만나 보지 못합니다」 하고 그 老人은 들은척 만척 一言에 拒絶하고 만다. 그러켓다. 塵世에서 現實과 싸우는 우리들은 「十字聖號」로써 벗하고 「聖書」로써 짝하는 그들을 正面으로 만날 수 없다는 것이 조곰도 怪異할 것이 없다.

그러나 어떠케 해서든지 그의 來歷과 生活을 듣고 싶은 나는 먼저 修女長을 만낫다. 時節을 따라 바꾸어 입은 그들의 힌 禮服과 俗世의 雜談을 避하고 또한 外界의 誘惑을 막으려 검은 「미사보」로 귀를 싸고 목에는 十字架를 걸고 섯는 그의 姿態는 淸貧 童貞女 忍從으로 神알에 맹세하고 混沌한 俗世를 超越한 聖스러운 氣分을 보여 준다. 그 沈着한 態度와 顔面에 나타나는 거륵한 빛은 다만 그들에게서만 찾을 수 잇는 特異한 印象을 준다. 生活이 다른 나와는 길게 이애기 하기를＜68＞ 즐기지 않는 모양이엇으나 나는 그런 것은 짐작도 못하는 듯이 추근추근히 멫마디 물엇다.

처음에 이 聖公會란 것이 美國으로부터 朝鮮에 布敎될 때 仁川으로부터 비롯하야 特히 北鮮地方으로 퍼지자 이곳저곳에 十字架를 높이 단 캐더릭 처취와 近似하게 宏壯한 聖堂을 세우고 現代化한 基督敎에서는 찾을 수 없는 嚴肅과 規律이 아울러 「吾主」(그들이 부르는 그대로)를 찬양하는 노래소리가 높이 울리게 되엇다.

또한 따라서 지금으로부터 7년전 1925년에 이곳에 修道院을 세우고 神과 더부러 一生을 마치며 黙想과 勞働으로 修道를 目的하는 修女들을 모으게 되엇다. 그리하여 지금까지 다섯 사람이 이 修道院의 花林 속으로 찾어온 동안 그 中에서 李XX孃이 처음으로 第一世 修女로 뽑히게 된 것은 이 집에서 생긴 貴重한 歷史의 第一頁이다.

지금으로부터 10餘年 前 그의 나이 스물을 막 넘긴 그때 봄아지랑이를 따라 이 언덕 저 언덕으로 C와 같이 사랑을 속삭이며 솜같이 피어오르는 구름과 향기로운 꽃냄새에 靑春의 노래를 부르는 그들은 不幸하게도 사랑의 幸福이 엷엇든지 뜻대로 못 이룰 그 사랑을 원망하는 同時에 두 사람은 같은 길에 盟誓를 세우고 하나는 神父로 하나는 修女가 되자고 結議하엿다. 이리하여 애닯은 靑春의 꿈은 祈禱와 「十字聖號」로써 淨化시키어 비로소 거룩한 生活은 展開된 것이엇다.

먼동이 트자 새 소래가 나무가지에 짖어귀는 아츰 다섯時 「거륵하라」 울려 오는 聖堂 종소리와 함께 單調하면서 새로운 그날그날 그들 童貞女들의 생활이 시작되는 것이다.

호미를 들고 기심을 매든 農女의 그들. 다 각각 무슨 한가지 事情으로 이집 속으로 찾어 와서 밤마다 맺는 꿈이 행여 어지럽게 할나 黙想하는 그 머리에 행여 雜念이 슴여 들나 가슴의 十字聖號 그리고 그림과 鍾소리에 올리는 祈禱는 現代의 活社會와는 얼마쯤 그 사이가 멀다할 것이나 그들 自身에게는 運命的으로 賦與된 幸福의 길인만큼 나는 그 길에서나마 그 生命이 복되고 빛나소서 하고 빌고 싶엇다. (끝)＜69＞
