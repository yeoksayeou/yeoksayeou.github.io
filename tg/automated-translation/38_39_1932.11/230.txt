(Excerpt from Yi Tonhwa, *Sin'ingan*, October issue)
Even in the psychological state when we hold a great hope (希望) and wish to achieve (達하고저) that hope, one must approach with calmness (沈着), composure (冷靜), and attention (注意), striving to the utmost (臨勳盡料), and slowly (徐徐) await the opportunity (其機).

(Excerpt from *Pip'an*, October issue)
Regarding today's newspapers, without exception, there are none that have not become extremely reactionary (反動化) in their substance (實質). They are already no longer entities advocating for any particular ideology (意識의 主張體).

(Excerpt from *Man'guk Puin*, Inaugural Issue)
New Women of Chosŏn (朝鮮의 新女性) - you must recognize the reality (現實) of Chosŏn more clearly... and decisively carry out the historical task (歷史的 課題) assigned (賦課된) to you from your unique social position (獨特한 社會的 立場).<45>

**Summary**

This article presents three excerpts from different contemporary magazines reflecting on various aspects of Korean society in 1932. The first advocates for patience, strategy, and composure when pursuing significant goals. The second offers a sharp critique of the press, deeming it universally reactionary and devoid of ideological substance. The third issues a direct call to the "New Women" of colonial Korea, urging them to understand their reality and fulfill their specific historical mission. From a historical perspective, the excerpts reveal contemporary intellectual concerns, including critiques of the media landscape under colonialism (likely implying censorship or co-option) and the burgeoning discourse surrounding the role and responsibilities of modern women ("New Women") in shaping Korea's future.
