**Body Text Translation:**
Film Communication, The Ownerless Ferryboat
						Chu Yŏngha

Having left his hometown due to drought (旱災) and flood damage (水害), Ch'unsam, after coming up to Kyŏngsŏng with his wife,<114> was a person living day by day pulling a rickshaw (人力車). However, due to livelihood difficulties, Ch'unsam abandoned his wife who had betrayed him, left Seoul with his young daughter, returned again to the countryside (農村), and became a ferryman, living happily, though poorly, with his only daughter. However, after 15 years passed, because a railway bridge was built here, the days of the ferryboat were truly beyond description.

Furthermore, when he thought of his beloved daughter, between whom and a technician (技手) who had come for this construction (工事) work the flower of love was blooming, wanting to leave his embrace, he felt like he would go mad. Finally, Ch'unsam fights with the technician, gets rid of him, and then, like a madman, vanishes under the wheels of the train (汽車) of Civilization (文明) that trampled all his happiness – it is a tragedy (悲劇).

In terms of content (內容), there is little to find beyond its box-office value. However, through this considerable work of eight reels (八卷), we must have been astonished by the director's refined skill in meticulously and quite realistically depicting [the story] relying only on the camera until the end, borrowing merely a few title cards (字幕), and by the screen (畵面) flowing with fluid beauty (流動美). In our film world (영화게), which has not possessed a director worthy of the name, the newcomer Mr. Yi Kyuhwan truly makes us anticipate tomorrow.

In terms of acting (Action), the sincere attitude (眞摯한 態度) of Mr. Na Un'gyu, who appeared as Ch'unsam, was good. However, there is a sense that he is still repeating the madman of 'Arirang' [and] 'Beongeori Samnyong' [Deaf Man Samnyong]. Ch'unsam's wife, Miss Kim Yŏnsil, also showed seasoned skill (老練한 솜씨), but the most excellent was the pure face (純眞한 얼골) and truly innocent acting (演技) of Miss Mun Yebong.<115>

**Summary**

This article reviews the 1932 Korean film "Imja ŏmnŭn narupae" (The Ownerless Ferryboat). It summarizes the plot: a man named Ch'unsam, displaced from the countryside to Seoul and back again, becomes a ferryman. His life is disrupted first by poverty and marital betrayal, and later by modernization (a railway bridge destroying his livelihood) and his daughter falling for an engineer, leading to a tragic climax where he kills the engineer and commits suicide under a train. The reviewer, Chu Yŏngha, dismisses the plot as having only commercial value but highly praises the director Yi Kyuhwan's sophisticated visual storytelling and minimal use of title cards. While Na Un'gyu's lead performance is deemed sincere, it's criticized as repetitive of his previous roles, whereas Mun Yebong's acting as the daughter is lauded as outstanding. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for its commentary on the nascent Korean film industry, highlighting the emergence of skilled directors like Yi Kyuhwan and reflecting anxieties about modernization (symbolized by the train and bridge) destroying traditional livelihoods and ways of life during the colonial period. The explicit identification of the train with destructive 'Civilization' (文明) offers a glimpse into contemporary critiques.

映畵通信, 임자없는 나루배
						朱永夏
旱災 水害로 말미암아 고향을 떠나온 春三은 자기 안해와 더부러 京城에 올라온 후＜114＞ 人力車 버리로 그날 그날을 살아가는 사람이엇으나 생활난으로 인하야 春三은 자기를 배반한 안해를 버리고 어린 딸과 서울을 떠나 다시 農村으로 도라와 배사공이 되어 단 하나인 딸과 가난하나마 幸福스럽게 지내엇다. 그러다 15년이 지나간 후 이곳에는 철교가 놓인 까닭에 나루배의 세월은 아조 말이 아니엇다. 뿐만 아니라 이 工事에 와잇든 技手 한사람과 자기가 사랑하는 딸사이에 사랑의 꽃이 피여 자기의 품을 떠나려는 딸을 생각할 때 그는 미칠 듯 하엿다. 이윽고 春三은 技手와 싸와 그를 없새고 자기는 미친대로 자기의 온갓 幸福을 즛밟은 文明 汽車박휘 밑에서 사라저 간다는 悲劇이다.

內容에 잇어서 흥행적 가치밖에는 별로 찾을 것이 없다. 그러나 우리가 이 영화에 잇어서 팔권(八卷)이나 되는 적잔은 작품을 통하야 치밀하게 또는 그만치나 핍진하게 그려내되 겨우 몇 개의 자막(字幕)밖에 빌지 않고서 끝까지 카메라만을 가지고 描寫해논 감독의 그 세련된 수완과 류동미(流動美)가 흐르는 畵面에 놀애엇으리라. 감독다운 감독을 못가진 우리 영화게에 신인 李圭煥君은 실로 명일을 기대케 한다.
Action에 잇어서 春三으로 나온 羅雲奎君의 眞摯한 態度가 좋앗다. 그러나 『아리랑』의 미친 사나히 『벙어리 三龍이』를 아직도 되푸리한 감이 잇다. 春三의 안해 金蓮實孃도 老練한 솜씨를 보여 주엇으나 가장 훌늉한 것은 文藝峰孃의 그 純眞한 얼골과 천진 그대로의 演技이엇섯다.＜115＞
