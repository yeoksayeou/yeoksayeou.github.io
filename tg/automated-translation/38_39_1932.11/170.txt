An Ideal Study Method Taught by Psychology
						O Ch'ŏnsŏk

Is There Value in Being Faithful to Schoolwork?
"There's no need to try so hard at studying. One can achieve great success even without good school grades!"
Recently, we often hear such assertions being made among students or the general public. Is this really true? Is there truly no positive relationship between school grades and success in the real world?

Modern psychology, in order to provide a clear answer to this question, has created quite a few statistical tables. These statistical tables uniformly show the same trend. Now, taking one representative statistical table to examine the general situation, we can find the relationship between school grades and success after entering society for individuals engaged in the legal and religious fields among graduates of Oxford University in Britain (英國), as shown in the table below.
School Grades  Percentage of Individuals Successful After Entering Society
The table above clearly indicates that there is a significant positive relationship between school grades and success after entering society. In other words, the number of individuals who graduated with top honors and achieved success after entering society is more than 4 times greater in the legal field and nearly 8 times greater in the religious field compared to the number of successful individuals who entered society without obtaining a degree. What does the investigation above teach us?
1. It shows that graduates with good grades generally have more opportunities for success after entering society.
2. Even if success in school or society largely depends on our innate talent (才能), it emphasizes that the responsibility to develop this talent to its maximum potential lies with us.

Ultimately, the above affirms the necessity of effort and speaks to the value of being faithful to one's schoolwork.

How Should One Study?
An unwise method of studying means a waste of much time and energy. Modern psychology offers several helpful suggestions in this regard.<36>
1. Concentration of Attention
The most fundamental element of studying is the concentration of attention. Without concentration of attention, no matter how much effort or how long the time, one cannot achieve perfection in studying. So, what is the method for concentrating attention?

a. Adjustment of the Physical Environment
(1) First, avoid cluttered places and choose a quiet one. If possible, find a private room far from the street and close the door. However, going outdoors to find a quiet place is not advisable, as there are many other conditions there that can distract the mind (精神).
(2) Remove objects that distract attention from the study room. Keep the desk tidy. Sit with your back to the window to avoid external temptations.
(3) Arrange the lighting, temperature, desk, seating, clothing, etc., in the study room so they do not impede studying. Keep the body healthy.
(4) If possible, always study in the same place, at the same seat, and at the same time.

b. Adjustment of Mental Attitude
(1) After deciding to study, discard distracting thoughts and begin immediately. The saying "Starting is half the battle" is appropriate here.
(2) When facing studies, set clear goals (目標) and have a plan in mind for what you will achieve in a certain amount of time.
(3) Cultivate the habit of understanding what you are studying while memorizing. Rote memorization is wasteful.
(4) Organize the items you intend to memorize under appropriate outlines (綱目) before memorizing. One experiment showed that memorizing the same items took 14 minutes and 3 seconds when done randomly, compared to 9 minutes and 11 seconds with organized memorization, demonstrating the great necessity of organization.
(5) It has been psychologically proven that memorizing the entire text at once is more economical (經濟的) and lasting than dividing it into several parts for memorization. If the text to be memorized is long, it should be divided into large sections and handled accordingly.
(6) When memorizing certain items, it is more effective to review them somewhat, then leave them for a while to forget, and then review again, rather than trying to memorize everything on the spot at once.
(7) Occasionally recalling parts of what has been reviewed is effective for correcting shortcomings and making memory permanent (永久).<37>

**Summary**

This article by O Ch'ŏnsŏk argues against the notion that academic effort is unnecessary for life success. Citing statistical evidence allegedly from Oxford University graduates, it asserts a strong positive correlation between good grades and later success in professional fields like law and religion. The author emphasizes the importance of maximizing one's innate talent through diligent study. The second part offers practical study advice based on contemporary psychology, focusing on concentrating attention through environmental control (quiet space, tidy desk, consistent routine) and mental discipline (setting clear goals, starting promptly, understanding before memorizing, organizing material, using whole-learning and spaced repetition techniques, and practicing recall). From a historical perspective, the article reflects the adoption and promotion of Western psychological principles (specifically, behavioral and cognitive efficiency techniques) in colonial Korea as tools for self-improvement and achieving success in a modernizing society. It highlights a desire among Koreans for practical methods to navigate and succeed within the structures available to them, framing academic achievement and disciplined effort as key pathways.

心理學이 가르치는 理想的 工夫法
						吳天錫
學科에 忠實할 價値가 잇나?
「工夫란 그리 애쓰고 할 必要가 없는 것이다. 學校成績이 좋지 못하고도 훌늉한 出世할 수가 잇다!」
近者에 우리는 가끔 學生들 사이에서나 或은 一般 사람들 사이에서 이러한 斷言이 나리워짐을 듣는다. 果然 그런가? 참말 學校에서의 成績과 實社會에서의 成功 사이에 아모런 積極的關係가 없는가?

現代心理學은 이 疑問에 明確한 對答을 提供키 위하야 不少한 統計表를 만들어 보앗다. 이러한 統計表들은 一率도 같은 傾向을 보이는데 이제 代表로 一統計表를 들어 一般을 살펴보면 英國 옥스퍼드 大學生으로 法界와 宗敎界에 從事하는 人士의 學校成績과 出世後 成功과의 關係가 下表와 같음을 찾을 수 잇다.
學校成績  出世後 成功한 人士의 百分比
上記表는 學校成績과 出世後 成功과의 사이에 顯著한 積極的 關係가 잇다는 것을 明示한다. 換言하면 最優等成績으로 出世後 成功한 人士의 數는 學位를 얻지 못하고 出世한 人士로 成功한 人數보다 法界에 잇어서 4倍强, 宗敎界에 잇어서 8倍弱이 된다는 말이다. 以上 調査는 무엇을 敎示하는고 하니
1. 好成績卒業生은 出世後 一般的으로 成功할 機會를 많이 가젓다 함과
2. 비록 學校나 社會에서의 成功이 大部分 우리의 先天的 才能에 依한다 할지라도 이 才能을 最大限度까지 發揮할 責任은 우리에게 잇다 함이다.

以上은 結局 努力의 必要를 認定하며 學科에 忠實할 價値가 잇다 함을 말하는 것이다.

工夫를 어떠케 할까?
슬기럽지 못한 工夫 方式은 많은 時間과 精力의 浪費를 意味한다. 現代心理學은 이에 몇 가지의 도음 되는 暗示를 提供한다.＜36＞
1. 注意의 集中
工夫에 가장 基本的인 要素는 注意의 集中이다. 注意의 集中 없이는 제 아모리 많은 努力 제아모리 긴 時間을 가지고도 工夫의 完美를 걷을 수가 없다. 그러면 注意를 集中시키는 方法이 무엇인가?

ㄱ. 物質的 還境의 調節
(1) 위선 煩雜한 곳을 避하야 종용한 데를 擇할 것. 可能하면 길거리를 멀리한 獨房을 求하야 문을 닫을 것. 그러나 閑寂한 곳을 찾는다고 野外로 가는 일은 贊成할 수 없다. 무릇 거기에는 精神을 散漫케 하는 다른 條件이 많은 까닭이다.
(2) 注意를 迷亂케 하는 物件을 工夫室에서 除去할 것. 冊床 우는 單潔하게. 窓을 등지고 앉어 外界의 誘惑을 避할 것.
(3) 工夫室의 光線, 溫度, 冊床, 坐席, 衣服 等을 工夫에 障害가 없도록. 身體는 健康하게.
(4) 可能하면 工夫는 늘 같은 곳 같은 자리에서 같은 時間에.

ㄴ. 心的 態度의 調節
(1) 工夫를 決心한 다음에는 雜念을 버리고 곳 始作할 것. 「始作이 半」이란 말이 여기에 適合한 말이다.
(2) 工夫를 對할 때에 明確한 目標를 세워 一定한 時間에 무엇을 成就하겟다는 心算을 가질 것.
(3) 工夫하는 바를 解得하여 가며 記憶하는 習慣을 기를 것. 盲目的 記憶은 浪費的이다.
(4) 記憶하랴는 事項을 適當한 綱目 下에 組織하야 가지고 記憶할 것. 一實驗에 依하면 같은 事項을 記憶함에 無秩序로 記憶하는데 14分 3秒가 걸렷음에 反하야 組織的 記憶에는 9分 11秒가 걸리엇다는 것을 보아도 組織의 必要가 큼을 알 수 잇다.
(5) 記憶하랴는 글의 全篇을 一時에 全體로 記憶하는 것이 그를 여러 部分으로 나누어 가지고 記憶하는 것보다 經濟的이요 永久的이라 함이 心理學的으로 證明되엇다. 記憶할 글이 長篇인 경우에는 그것을 큰 部分으로 나누어 가지고 取扱할 것이다.
(6) 어떤 事項을 記憶할 때에 그것을 一時에 그 當場에서 해 버리랴 함보다 얼마쯤 復習한 뒤에 얼마동안 버려두고 잊어다가 다시 復習하는 것이 有効하다.
(7) 때때로 復習한 바를 部分的으로 回想해 보는 것이 缺點을 깁고 記憶을 永久히 하는데 有力하다.＜37＞
