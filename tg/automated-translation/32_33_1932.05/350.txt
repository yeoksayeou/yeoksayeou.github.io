Magazine Title: 동광 제33호
Publication Date: 1932년 05월 01일
Article Title: 불을 질러라 (Set a Fire)
Author: 皮千得
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Set a Fire
						P'i Ch'ŏndŭk

Set a fire to the dry grass!
Burn away the withered blades!

When the dead grass catches fire
The pale hill burns red
When spring comes and new shoots sprout on the old site
The burnt field turns green

Set a fire to the dry grass!
Burn away the withered blades! <12>

**Summary**

This short poem by P'i Ch'ŏndŭk (Pi Cheon-deuk) uses the imagery of burning dry grass in a field to symbolize renewal. The act of destruction (burning the old, withered grass) is presented as necessary for new life (the green shoots of spring) to emerge. From a historical perspective, the poem, published in 1932 during the Japanese colonial period, could be interpreted allegorically. While ostensibly about nature's cycle, the theme of clearing away the old and dead to make way for vigorous new growth might subtly resonate with aspirations for national renewal or change amidst the oppressive colonial reality, even if not overtly political.
