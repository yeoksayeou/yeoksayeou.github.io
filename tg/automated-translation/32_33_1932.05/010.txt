Sixth Anniversary of Founding
						Chu Yo-han

It has already been six years since Tonggwangsa (東光社) was founded. With one table, one chair, one writing brush—using these as our weapons, we have strived to play one beat, one part of Chosŏn's marching song. It was truly a fierce and bitter struggle. Even now, the struggle continues, and hardship still lies ahead.

Nurturing *Tonggwang*, sustaining *Tonggwang*—at times, it felt as though this was our sole objective. Of course, that is a miscalculation. If, through *Tonggwang*, we have offered even a finger's worth of effort to the project of Chosŏn's cultural reorganization, we are satisfied. If, through *Tonggwang*, we have struck a chord on the heartstrings of Chosŏn's youth, then our mission is fulfilled. Let us add a handful of soil to our garden. Let us sprinkle a drop of sweat upon our land. In this way, let us await our autumn of harvest.

(1) Strongly, truly, courageously, faithfully.
(2) Under the conviction that our work can only be pioneered by our own strength.
(3) Others before myself. Society before the individual.

May *Tonggwang* become a beacon of this spirit. May it become a pioneer of this spirit.

Keeping our ideals high, doing our best in the present circumstances, we will forge ahead bravely until the time we fall, and for 60 years, 600 years, our upright brush will not bend.

Burying all the past, along with its dissatisfactions and errors, we intend to advance once more with a new leap forward. Let all who seek to win the new era support *Tonggwang*. (Chu Yo-han) <1>

**Summary**

This editorial by Chu Yo-han commemorates the sixth anniversary of the founding of *Tonggwang* magazine. He reflects on the difficult struggles faced since its inception with minimal resources, emphasizing the magazine's mission to contribute to Korea's cultural renewal and inspire the youth. Chu calls for perseverance, self-reliance ("our own strength"), placing collective good over individual interests, and maintaining high ideals while working diligently. He urges readers striving for a new era to support the magazine, positioning *Tonggwang* as a pioneering force that will continue its mission steadfastly into the future. For historians, the article offers insight into the cultural nationalist sentiment prevalent among some Korean intellectuals during the colonial period, highlighting the emphasis on self-strengthening (자력갱생), the role of youth, the hardships of publishing under Japanese rule, and the forward-looking, albeit struggling, spirit aiming for national regeneration through cultural means. The call for progress "by our own strength alone" is particularly notable.

創社 六週年
						朱耀翰
東光社를 創設한지 於焉間 六週年이다. 테불 한 개 의자 한 개 붓 한 자루― 그것들을 武器로 해 가지고 우리는 朝鮮의 行進曲의 한 拍子, 한 파트를 演奏하려고 애써 왓다. 그야말로 惡戰苦鬪엿다. 지금도 苦鬪의 계속이요 앞으로도 苦鬪가 남아잇다.
東光을 키우는 것, 東光을 維持하는 것―때로는 그것이 우리의 유일한 目標인 것 같이 感覺된 때도 잇다. 물론 그것은 誤計다. 東光을 통하야 朝鮮의 文化的 再編成工事에 한 손까락의 노력이라도 提供햇다 하면 우리는 만족이다. 東光을 통하야 朝鮮靑年의 心琴에 한 絃의 우름을 울렷다 하면 우리의 使命은 이룬 셈이다. 한 줌의 흙을 우리의 동산에 북돋으자. 한 방울의 땀을 우리의 땅 우에 뿌리자. 이리하야 우리의 收獲의 가을을 기다리자.
(1) 강하게, 참되게, 勇氣잇게, 믿음잇게.
(2) 우리의 일은 오직 우리의 힘으로만 開拓할 수 잇다는 信念下에.
(3) 나보다도 남을. 個人보다도 社會를.
東光은 이러한 精神의 등불이 되소서. 이러한 精神의 先驅者가 되소서.
理想은 高處에 두고 目下의 最善을 다하아 가다가 거꾸러지는 때까지 勇往邁進, 60週年 600週年으로 우리의 곧은 붓을 휘지 아니할 것이다.
모든 과거를 그 不滿과 過誤와 함께 장사하고 또 한 번 새로운 飛躍으로 우리는 進取하려 한다. 새로운 時代를 戰取코저 하는 이는 다 같이 東光을 支持하라. (朱耀翰)＜1＞
