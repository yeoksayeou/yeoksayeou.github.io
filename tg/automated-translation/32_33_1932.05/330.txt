Introducing Newcomers to the New Poetry Scene
						By Chu Yohan

It is truly delightful to welcome new workers to Chosŏn's new poetry movement. We wish to introduce here several colleagues whom we have encountered through *Tonggwang* (東光) and who we believe deserve recognition in the current poetry scene. Of course, their poetic works have already been widely published in various journals and are subject to reader criticism, so introducing them anew might perhaps be a nuisance (累) to the individuals themselves, but it seems not without meaning to introduce them upon the publication of this special literary issue.

Mr. Song Sun'il (宋順鎰) is one who has shifted from lyricism in the style of short songs (小曲風) to become a more realistic pastoral poet. He depicts the reality of rural life, still virgin territory (처녀지) among Chosŏn poets, with a grievous (침통한) touch (필치).

Mr. Yi Ŭngsu (李應洙) is an intellectual (이지적) poet. Possessing much humor and satire, he holds a very unique ground among us. His songs bear a philosophical beauty, and his philosophy is adorned with wit (기지). We welcome this poet with a smile.

Ms. Mo Yunsuk (毛允淑) is one of the rare female writers, like the Morning Star (曉星), and at the same time possesses a very robust style (필치). She expresses through her songs "activeness" (적극성), the most respectable quality among all that the term "modern woman" symbolizes (표상하다).

Mr. Yi Kyuwŏn (李揆元) is a poet of passion. He expresses his burning sense of justice and anger with the most effective metaphors. He is a poet who spews flames like a volcanic crater or bellows (풀무).

Mr. Chŏn Pongje (全鳳濟) is also a poet with the air of an ultra-modern (超近代的) painter, extolling (구가하다) strength, rhythm, intense emotion, and intense color. He possesses a state of mind that is the antipode (對蹠) to the poets of old who held worlds of pity and contemplation (冥想).

Mr. Hwang Sunwŏn (黃順元) also sings songs of strength. He too is a poet called by the new era. However, his rhythm is not rough (거츨지않고) but smooth (順하며); not discordant, but harmonious (調和). He possesses not a superficial but an inherent (內在的) robustness (强健).

While introducing these newcomers, almost all unknown (未知) to me by face (面識), through this publication, I hope for strict (엄밀한) criticism from gentlemen of all fields (사계제씨).

Also, I must add a note (添記할 것은) that these poems were by no means specially requested contributions from them, but were published arbitrarily (임의로) from pieces we had received, and therefore, this fact means they cannot be considered their representative works (대표작). <2>

Become a Tower of Eternity (and Two Other Poems)
- Commemorating the 5th Anniversary of *Tonggwang* -

Mo Yunsuk

As the pitch-black night deepens silently on the East street
The last (마즈막) cries of the perishing populace are uttered
Through the ash-gray of darkness's lost round dance
O brilliant dawn light (曙光) piercing (뚜르고) and scattering (흐터지는)!

O populace who sang (노래튼) the opium of anguish and weariness!
Open your hands that covered (덮든) the orchard of life with black gauze
And behold the flames rising (떠오는) from the clear Eastern sea
Don't the surging waves laugh along with the dancing palm trees?
This new morning, the dark streets have brightened (밝엇나니)

O soul of the decayed street! Stop staggering
Do not the portraits of majestic dynamism and established theory
Descend upon you riding the red light of the eastern (동녁) sky?
Virgin of the World! Take my hand

Stop your feet drifting towards the splendid dance party (戀舞會)
Don't glare askance, saying it's walled off by an earthen fence (土塀)
Sing a song for our house's feast
Filled brimming with the joy of birth.

Carrying the noble passion radiating (뻐치는) from the essence
O Tower of Eternity! traversing the cloud paths of the world like a brilliant rainbow
Let your eyes shine
The new mission will weigh heavy on your shoulders (억개).

That Smile
Even when a strong wind in the frosty mist
Shakes your delicate heart
Or when untimely clouds swirl around your clear eyes
Please don't break (깨치지 말어주서요) that pure smile.

Even if monkeys in the field lure (꾀이거나) you
Or all the beauty of the world tempts you
That unforgettable smile you sent me
Please always, always cherish it (간지해 두서요).

Under the Spring Sky
The thrilling spring energy comes seeking (찿어와) my heart too
Making me recall again you whom I had forgotten (잊엇든)
My cold heart beats to the spring song
And warm tears well up (소사납니다).

Every year when the spring wild geese shake the sky
My cursed flame blooms (피어나) once more
The old days rise through the bewildering flute sound
Grabbing hold (휘여잡고) of the willow branch, I sing an unskilled song (서튼노래).
(From Old Manuscripts) <3>

Rural Landscape (and One Other Poem)

Song Sun'il

Beautiful rural landscape—upon the green earth
All sorts of things (가추가추) pour forth...
In every garden plot (터받마다), young zucchini and green peppers
Grow plumply awaiting their tending owner
Corn is already (벌서) ripe, its yellow beard flying.

The five grains form waves (물껼치고) on the vast field
In the breeze scattering (흣날리는) the scent of rice stalks
Ripe fruits break from their stems and fall, plop plop plop
Even the calf, fattened on grass, leaps about in its own excitement.

The utmost rural beauty is enchanting the more one sees it (볼사록)
When I deeply ponder this, achieved through blood and sweat
I place my hand on my chest and bow my head to pray (지도다).

If one were to say (하올진대) their livelihood is comfortable
The beautiful rural landscape would become (되올것이) a "Utopia"
But since the unrighteous hordes, like the cannibal souls of "Corsica" (골식카),
Attack, aiming for their lives (덤비거니)
Where is this ownerless rural landscape to go (어데이드뇨)?—

When the platinum-thread sun blazes fiercely upon the earth
These robust ones advance, brushing off beads of sweat
If they all just washed their hands and turned away

Even the fertile fields would become wormwood patches overnight
But this era's high civilization—its strange system—
Tries to place (씨우려하고) a crown of thorns
And attacks to brand a fiery mark upon their burning chests.

Oh, civilization seduced (호리인) by "Satan"!
If even a beast of burden cannot have a net placed upon it (구물을 시우지 못하려든)
These people who built the beautiful rural landscape with blood and sweat
Why (어찌랴) are they told to just erect monuments of defeat before hungry ghosts (餓鬼)?
(1931. 7. 11)

Disharmony (不同調)
Past the one sighing deeply enough to sink the earth, turning his back on the cotton cloth store
A silk garment hem brushes past, avoiding him.
Before the starving masses fighting (싸호는) death,
"The warehouse is too small, I fear thieves"—this too is called worry
And yet they say the worries are the same.
Which (엇드란) mathematician produced such an equation?
(Following 6 lines omitted - original text)

The Study of Respect (and Two Other Poems)

Yi Ŭngsu

One (者) who does not have even one person to respect
Shows that their inner world is still empty.
It is from having elders to respect that people
Take the first step in cultivation (修養) and character (人格).

A person who does not know how to respect others
Knows neither others' strengths nor their own weaknesses;
Respecting others is respecting oneself
And simultaneously hating one's own imperfection.

Humans estranged from the morality of respect
Think "I" alone am unique in the world;
Exaggeration and conceit are their way (道)
Slander (誹謗) and suspicion (猜疑) are their virtue (德).

As I see it, Chosŏn people
Know how to hate successful people
But do not know how to respect great compatriots.
Let us Chosŏn people also undertake the study of respect.

Poem of the Northern Sky (北天)
When the evening song whispers (소군소군) from beyond the torn paper of the door and window
My disheveled head, having opened the brushwood gate and stepped out with arms folded (사빈채),
My gaze tonight too rushes towards that Northern Sky (北天).
As the setting sun crosses the western (西켠) sky
The fully burning heart in my deeply sunken (가러앉은) chest
Rushes towards that Northern Sky, rushes towards the Northern Sky, the direction of the wild geese (기럭이).

The evening wind holding (먹음은) sorrow (서름)
Whirls through the reed leaves (갈대닢) in the field
And brushes the back (등터리) of a lost calf
The fallen (너머진) shadows of a masterless people (百姓)
Also writhe chaotically in this desolate field.

Oh—why is it that every morning (아츰마다) and evening I so painfully
With the earnest prayer of a devout believer
Gaze at that Northern Sky and shed tears (눈물지움은)?

It seems a sacred sound is heard (들리는듯 하고나) from the Northern Sky
It seems a holy sound spreads endlessly (퍼저오는 듯) from there
And it seems a beautiful light, an auspicious glow (瑞光), flashes (번뜩이는 듯하고나).

Oh—thus I became a poet murmuring of the Northern Sky
I, who should be a poet of this land, gaze upon that country beyond the north
And have made it my business (일삼어오다) to plead ardently with heart-rending verses.

Who says the light is from the East?
For me, for my people
The light will come (오리로니) from the North, the light from the North
My faith is in that Northern Sky, and I have become (되엇고나) a poet of the Northern Sky. <5>

Confusion (混亂)
Time is alarmingly busy to the point of making one shudder
Why on earth (어찌자고) is the world so noisy (떠들석하고) and chaotic, such turmoil (들볶음일가)?
Old folks rake up old stories (녯말) of the Three Kingdoms period (三國時節) and the Warring States era (戰國)
Young folks repeat (되푸리한다) the shallow dreams of world wars
If you go out to the crossroads (十字街路), the madness of the last days, like radio static (雜音),
Snatches away (빼어갓고) people's spirits to the ninth heaven and runs away (달아나는대)
There are people collapsing (쓸어지는) from tinnitus and dizziness
From fainting to collapse, from powerlessness to suicide, to death
The scene just resembles (방불타) a battlefield with myriad troops galloping
A woman who was weeding a field in the village
Listens, pail in hand, to the distant noise
Even a flock of mountain birds flying (나러가든) in the sky, startled in panic (황겁),
Drags (끄을고) their shadows into the distant sky
At this time, my chest is about to burst, filled with cries I reluctantly suppress
And words I want to speak.
But being a pitiful (안타까운) body that possesses the sad right
To break my pen and become mute
I gaze vacantly (먼안이히) at the distant sky where the mountain birds flew
And let out the sigh of a giraffe (麒麟) stretching its neck long.

Draw the bowstring forcefully towards "his" spiritless chest - A song calling to the sleeping Earth -

Hwang Sunwŏn

Saying that when the day of bloodshed comes, after wrapping oneself in the flying flag (기폭)
One would shoulder even a broken rifle stock
And must come out (나오야될) vigorously—
Have you forgotten (잊어느냐) those words now? Have you lost your strength?
Or have you been robbed (빼아꼇느냐) of your racing blood?
Holding the blood-stained past of bitter bygone days to the heart
Shuddering at the thought of that time, I cry out (외치나니)
It is my wish (바람이다) that the heart of a man becomes firmer and hotter.

The ghoulish heaps of bare bones
Weep at the foot of ruined castle walls, beside graves (墓畔)
And the crowd seeking sunlight (해ㅅ빛) in the lightless street
Has become a mass of blood from knife wounds!
O lightless eyes! O powerless mouths!
O hands unable to hold a trumpet! O ankles unable to walk!

Ah, wanderers in the atmosphere (雰圍氣)
Will you then just sit and await death?
Are you trying to extinguish (사라트리려느냐) even your firm will?
If even a drop of oil remains (남어 잇거들랑) between your bones
Gaze upon the Earth and shout (웨처라) until your throat bursts! Match (마처라) your steps!
And towards his spiritless chest
Forcefully draw (당겨라) the bowstring! Draw it!
Until the blood from the struck heart gushes up (용소슴처서) and startles him awake.

This Street Holding Gray Light (and One Other Poem)

Chŏn Pongje

This street holding gray light
Pants (헐덕어리며), exhaling bitter breaths
All sounds from the tavern—fights (싸홈), screams, groans—
Flow into this street like a muddy stream (흐린내)—

The dim lights, like the eyes of one weeping
Disorder (어즈럽히며) the shadows of skeleton-like men
With the gloomy wind
And the sound of weeping comes from somewhere—

Through the broken lattice window of the palace, like a worn-out funeral bier (葬轝),
A cold wind shivers (부르르), dry leaves (마른닢) fly in
And no one awakens the terrifying "sleep" of the young beggar in the darkness—

A madman who came running (다라온) from somewhere
Wept bitterly on this street, then hugged (부더안고) a telephone pole and vomited blood
Ah, the staggering "merchant," countless times even in one day (하로도),
On this street, on this street
Flounders—sobbing (느껴울며)—
- 1931. 9. In P'aesŏng (浿城) -

Crematorium (火葬場) - O body burned by fire, O skeleton turned to ash -

When the dead (死骸) and the living spirits (生靈) gather (뫃여) weeping
To burn the sad corpse (시톄) into eternity (永劫)
How the sad humans, harboring (품은) souls of anxiety,
With eyes of curse
Have gazed (하엿든가) intently at this crematorium

Crematorium! Crematorium!
Swallowing (삼치고) the corpse like a shadowless object with fire
O smoke rising (비앗는) towards the blue sky, O smoke of the crematorium!

Crematorium! Crematorium!
O body (육톄) burned by fire! O skeleton turned to ash!
Now you will only fade away (스러질뿐이로구나) like a dream into sad smoke

Oh—Crematorium, whose loser of life are you sending into eternity today?
When did the skeleton of a great hero
Powerlessly turn to ash in this crematorium?
Today too, sad smoke rises.

In the endlessly blue sky
It dances wildly (란무) with fearsome shapes
Ah, when did the skeleton of a great hero
Powerlessly turn to ash in this crematorium?
- 1932. 1. At Pukch'ang Cemetery (北倉墓地) - <7>

The Person Treading Sulfur on the Vast Northern Plain (萬里北原) (and Three Other Poems)

Yi Kyuwŏn

On the Vast Northern Plain (萬里北原), the sun soars up (소사오른다).
Far, far away, piercing the horizon, the red sun soars up.
The young masses, who suffered (시달리던) in darkness, rush out like clouds
Into the middle of the rough Great Plain (大野) to meet the ascending morning sun (旭日).

How much the sorrowful pulse had cooled (식어불엇든가) in the suffocating room
How much the vivid breath that sought (찿든) the light had wandered away (떠돌아갓든가) as impure sounds
But, but, when the exhilarating mass of the sun soars up from the Vast Northern Plain
The throb of the blood-leaping young heart (염통) rises higher than the roar of an atomic bomb!

If, if you came leaping (뛰여나왓거든) out to the field to find red life
What means this molten rock (岩液) of sulfur overflowing (끓어넘는) in this Great Plain?
The blaze of molten rock (岩漿) flowing out like a mineral spring (鑛泉), bursting (터치며) the castle heart (城心)
Is the desolate Northern Plain about to collapse like crushed corpses (屍體)?

When legs like steel rush (몰려갈 때) to meet the golden wings of the sun
The earth's core (地心) explosion pours out molten lava (溶岩液), making the strong steps falter (앉히는구나)
Yet, falling down (어프러젓다) and rising, shouting mighty cries, when they run (뛰여갈 때)
The universe is buried in red flames (紅焰), the field is swept up (휩쌓이나니) in black smoke (黑煙)
Even if our bodies melt (녹아도) in boiling sulfur, we are sons of the sun raised in heat.
We will offer (祭祀하러니) the vital flame (氣焰) stored in our lungs with the pure heart (純情) of the morning sun
Boil (끓으라), sulfur! Burst, earth's core! And flow, red molten lava!
We shall harden forever into young fossils in this field.

On the Vast Northern Plain, the sun soars up.
As the earth's core's splitting hastens, the golden light (光明) becomes brighter (밝어지나니)
O strong people of the Great Plain, treading the molten sulfur!
Truly, truly, may your lives shine (빛나라) from the sun, boil (끓으라) from the molten lava.

Old Battlefield (古戰場) - Elegy of Ruins (廢墟) -

Where a million skeletons (骸骨) hold rusted swords
And countless throngs of ghosts (幽靈) play flutes (불며노는)
The old battlefield, the old battlefield, ah, the old battlefield of ruins—

Where crows cry sorrowfully (설게) on dry tree branches
And sad winds cry out (소래질르는) across the rough field
O tearfulness of mournful reminiscence (懷古), O heartache!

History's blood-stained records praise this ground
The strong forearm of a man (丈夫) has veins standing out on this ground
But what can be done (어찌하리) about the cries of the wronged souls? <8>

Don't you see (안느냐) the antics of the mad skeletons?
With the strength that held swords at the front lines yesterday (어제ㅅ날)
Aren't they rushing (달려오지안느냐) to pierce through (꿰여뚫으러) today's painful rule?

Listen quietly! To the weeping of the deranged (실ㅅ성한) ghosts
With the agony (痛哭) of parting (나뉘든) from mother, wife (안해), and young children
They cry out (소래지르나니), cursing the cruel target of humanity.

Sad, sad is the remaining tragedy of the old battlefield!
I will close my eyes (감ㅅ고) and not see (보지않겟네) that fearsome dance of skeletons.
I will cover my ears and not hear (듣지않겟네) that dreadful weeping of ghosts.

The Hero's Crown (冕琉冠) Being Auctioned - Poem Sent to a Certain Heroist -

At the street auction house (競賣場)
The golden crown (黃金冕琉冠) that adorned a hero's iron head is being auctioned.
Having gathered dust (몬지오르며) for several centuries (멫世期) on a curio shop display stand
Among rusted (녹쓰른) metal, it alone proudly shone—

Once, in a palace where a million subjects danced
He must have stood (섯으련만) firmly upon history wearing this crown
But the victor, glorious with great medals (勳章) of honor,
Finally escaped (다라낫나니) naked into the grave.

Behold! Today's ownerless laurel wreath (桂冠)
As it won't fit (언치지 않을진대) upon a skull (頭蓋骨) like rotten wood
O hero lying more powerlessly than the grass upon the grave!
Foul-smelling sighs must pass through (드나들리라) your crushed (바서진) windpipe.

As the blood-stained crown appears on the auction block
The inlaid red jewels (紅寶石) shed (떠러트리나니) sad tears
When exchanged (바꾸일 때) for gold into an anonymous museum (無名의 博物舘)
It is sold along with the victor of old.

Ah, hero of yesteryear who received toasts from power!
O rusted crown less splendid than a crown of thorns!
Today's crowd, turning away sneeringly,
Sets its value as a single treasure (寶物).

The flowers on the grave will bloom and fade year after year.
Even after your body turns to earth and then again to water—
But, but, the crown you left behind
Will surely convey (전할터이지) sad stories to passersby.
- 1932. 2. 7 -

The Furnace (冶爐) Whose Charcoal Has Cooled
Father, I am now sitting blankly beside the collapsed furnace.
Why (웨) do tears fall (떠러지는지) on this sturdy man's face (얼골)? I don't know.
Your son, who must stand up bravely with a resolute heart,
Is sitting here shedding tears more worthless than maggot- (버러지) rotted water... <9>

Father, the more I think, it feels like the blood circulating in my chest is drying up (말러붙는).
The place I sit now, isn't it where mighty hammers once flashed (번쩍이든데) like lightning?
And wasn't it the greatest smithy that forged this nation's spirit,
Where red-hot iron lumps were flying about (휘날리고 잇든) like bullets (彈丸)?

But Father, now the furnace remains (남엇서요) only white ash like a cremation site.
And on the tools hanging on the pillars, only the lesion (부시럼) of verdigris (동록) remains,
And where many washed their sweat back then, yellow rainwater lies pooled (고여잇습니다).

When my powerless sigh creates ripples on the smelly water
How can the dead blood pooled in my heart not stir?
And when my blankly sitting shadow falls upon the crumbling wall
How can I not picture the sinewy man of bygone days?

Father, after you passed away, the charcoal fire in the furnace also died down.
It was because the children, who once learned the will of steel (鋼鐵), battered by wind,
Threw away (내던졋든) their hammers, seeking to escape the suffocating coal smoke (煤煙);
And finding the sweat-inducing (땀맻이는) flames painful, they left the side of the bellows.

Father, Father, how your strengthless (맥푸러진) fist must tremble in the grave
And how the voice lingering in the void strikes (나리는지요) my own body like a painful whip!

But, but Father, I will surely return to the sound smithy
I have realized (깨달엇으며) I was the son of a builder, an old husbandman (丈夫) of labor (勤勞)
And have found out (찾어내엇습니다) that I am the body of the bravest one
Who bestows (베프는) the teaching of life to the weak populace (民衆) for myriad ages.

Father, as time passes, your face with its full beard appears (서립니다) before my eyes.

If I waste (없애버린다면) my precious youth in weeping (우름), what will become (될까요) of the masterless smithy?
In the end (나종에는), even this house with only pillars remaining will be torn down (헐리고) completely
And who will convey (전하겟읍닛가) the story that this was the workplace of strong men of old?

Father, the X Country will shine fully amidst the black smoke (煙氣).
Youth will certainly (정영코) grow with the resounding sound of the steel hammer.
The most vital history and tradition, and health, and the flowers in the garden
Will surely sprout (순돋아나겟지요) vigorously within the reopening smithy.

I am looking for (찾어보고 잇습니다) the shadows of bygone days, each and every one (낯낯이).
Searching for the sweat beaded like iron pellets on the old sinew-veined (힘ㅅ줄서린) arm
Touching (맍어보며) the shoulders of the strong men around whom muscles wrap
Searching for the iron fragments like volcanic bombs (火山彈) bursting out (터저나는) of the red flames (紅焰).

Ah Father, return to the earth with peace of mind.
The legacy (遺業) you bequeathed (끼처준) will certainly not become (않되고) ruins but a strong fortress wall (城壁).
And the sound of hammers in the silent (정막한) room will rise steadily
And the charcoal fire (수ㅅ불) in the furnace will ignite (붙을 것입니다) majestically
The charcoal fire in the furnace will ignite majestically.
1932. 1. 25 Night, P'aesŏng (浿城) <10>

**Summary**

Chu Yohan introduces six new poets—Song Sun'il, Yi Ŭngsu, Mo Yunsuk, Yi Kyuwŏn, Chŏn Pongje, and Hwang Sunwŏn—to the readers of *Tonggwang* magazine in 1932. He briefly characterizes each poet's style: Song's pastoral realism, Yi Ŭngsu's intellectual satire, Mo's active female voice, Yi Kyuwŏn's fiery passion, Chŏn's ultra-modern intensity, and Hwang's harmonious strength. The accompanying poems showcase these traits, exploring themes of rural life versus exploitation, social critique, national yearning (veiled as the "Northern Sky"), urban decay, historical reflection, critique of hollow heroism, and calls for strength and renewal. From a historical perspective, the article is valuable as it captures a moment in the development of modern Korean poetry during the colonial period, showcasing the diverse voices emerging despite censorship. The poets' engagement with social reality, national identity (often expressed indirectly, like Yi Ŭngsu's "Northern Sky" possibly symbolizing Manchuria or a lost northern homeland, or Yi Kyuwŏn's call to rebuild the "smithy"), and the desire for strength and action reflect the anxieties and aspirations of Koreans under Japanese rule.

新詩壇에 新人을 紹介함
						주요한
조선의 신시운동에 새로운 일꾼을 마지함은 실로 유쾌한 일임니다. 우리가 동광을 통하야 접촉하여온 몇낯의 동무들 중에서 현시단에서 지위를 인정받아도 좋다고 생각하는 멫분을 여기 소개하고저 합니다. 물론 그들의 詩作은 이미 많이 각 지상에 게재되어 독자의 비판을 받고 잇는 것이 사실이매 이제 새삼스러이 소개한다는 것이 當人에게는 도리어 累가 될는지 모르나 이번 문예특집호를 발간함에 잇어서 그들을 소개함이 무의미가 아닐듯합니다.
宋順鎰군은 小曲風의 서정으로부터 좀더 현실적인 田園詩人으로 전향한 분입니다. 그는 아직까지 조선시인 중에서는 처녀지인 농촌생활의 현실면을 침통한 필치로 묘사합니다.
李應洙군은 이지적 시인입니다. 유모어와 풍자를 많이 가진이로서 우리들 중에 매우 독특한 경지를 가젓습니다. 그의 노래는 철학미를 띠엇고 그의 철학은 기지로써 장식되어 잇습니다. 우리는 미소로서 이 시인을 마지합니다.
毛允淑군은 曉星같이 드믄 여류작가의 한분인 동시에 매우 건장한 필치를 가젓습니다. 그는 현대적 여성이란 글자가 표상하는 모든 것 중 가장 존경할만한 기질인 「적극성」을 그의 노래로서 표현햇습니다.
李揆元군은 정열의 시인입니다. 그는 타오르는 정의감과 분노를 가장 효과적인 비유로서 나타내엇습니다. 분화구와 같이 또는 풀무와 같이 화염을 토하는 시인입니다.
全鳳濟군도 힘과 리듬과 강렬한 감정, 강렬한 색채를 구가하는 超近代的 화가의 風이 잇는 시인입니다. 가련한 세계, 冥想의 세계를 가진 옛날의 싱니과 對蹠되는 심경을 가젓습니다.

숫불식은 冶爐
아버지 저는 지금 묺어진 冶爐곁에 우득허니 앉어잇습니다
웨 이러케 튼튼한 사내얼골에 눈물이 떠러지는지 모르겠어요
반듯이 강한 마음을 먹고 용감하게 이러나야할 당신의 아들이
버러지 썩은 물보담도 값없은 눈물을 흘리고 앉엇는지...＜9＞

아버지 생각할수록 가슴을 싸고도는 피ㅅ물이 말러붙는 것 같습니다.
지금 제가 앉은 곳은 그전날 힘센 마치가 번개같이 번쩍이든데 아닙니까
그리고 불다른 쇠ㅅ덩어리가 彈丸처럼 휘날리고 잇든
이나라의 정신을 싼 가장 위대한 대장깐이 아니엇습니까

그런데 아버지 지금은 冶爐는 火葬터처럼 白灰만 남엇서요
또는 기둥에 걸려잇는 연장우에는 동록의 부시럼만 쓸고
그때에 여럿이 땀씿든 자리에는 누런 누런비ㅅ물이 고여잇습니다.

저의 힘없는 嘆息이 냄새나는 물우에 波紋을 낼 때
어찌 염통에 고인 죽은 피가 흔들리지 않겟습니까
그리고 우득허니 앉은 그림자가 떠러진 벽우에 서릴 때
어떠게 지나간날 억센 筋肉의 사니히를 그려보지 않겟서요.

아버지 당신이 돌아가신 뒤에 冶爐의 숫불도 삭엇든 것입니다.
일즉이 鋼鐵뜻을 본받고 바람맞은 자식들이
숨막히는 煤煙을 피하고저 마치를 내던졋든 것이며
땀맻이는 火焰이 괴로워 풀무곁을 떠나갓든 것입니다.

아버지 아버지의 맥푸러진 주먹이 무덤속에서 얼마나 떨며
허공에 서린 음성이 얼마나 앞은 채ㅅ직을 제몸에 나리는지요

그러나 그러나 아버지 저는 건전한 대장깐으로 다시 돌아오겟지오
勤勞의 늙은 丈夫인 建設者의 아들이엇음을 깨달엇으며
억만년 지날때까지 약한 民衆에게 삶 가르침에 베프는
가장 용감한 이의 몸임을 찾어내엇습니다.

아버지 시간이 지날ㅅ수록 당신이 수염많은 얼골이 눈앞에 서립니다.

저의 귀한 청춘을 우름으로 없애버린다면 면 主없는 대장깐은 어찌 될까요
나종에는 기둥만남은 이집조차 영영 헐리고
옛날 억센 사내들의 일터란 말을 누가 전하겟읍닛가

아버지 X國은 거믄 煙氣속에서 완전히 빛나겟습니다
청춘은 떨쳐나는 쇠마치소리에 정영코 자라나겟습니다
가장 생명잇는 歷史와 傳統과 그리고 건강과 뜰의 꽃포기는
다시 문열리는 대장깐 속에서 무럭무럭 순돋아나겟지요.

저는 지나간 날의 그림자를 낯낯이 찾어보고 잇습니다
힘ㅅ줄서린 늙은 팔둑에 철알같이 매인 땀을 찾어보며
筋肉을 휩싸고 돌아가는 힘센 사나히들의 억개를 맍어보며
紅焰속에 터저나는 火山彈같은 鐵片을 찾어봅니다.
