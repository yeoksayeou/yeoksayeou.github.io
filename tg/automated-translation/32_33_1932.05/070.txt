**Body Text Translation:**
From Dublin, the Capital of Ireland
						Yi Chŏngsŏp (李晶燮)

Was it the first autumn of 1927? I visited Mr. De Valera [Éamon de Valera], leader of the independence faction, at the Fianna Fáil (피안나페일) Headquarters in Dublin (떠블린), the capital of Ireland (愛蘭). A female secretary, seemingly brimming with charm, came out and said, "You've come again as promised. However, Mr. De Valera is extremely busy right now due to the general election. It's impossible for him to see anyone, but since you are a rare visitor from afar, he says he will grant a brief audience. Please keep your words brief..." thus setting a time limit defensively from the start. Presently, Mr. De Valera came shambling in; he was, after all, the former President of the Irish Republic and the current President of the major party, Fianna Fáil (피안나, 페일). How could there not be a sense of tension? At a glance, he was of medium height, with a relatively slender body topped by a relatively small skull (頭蓋骨), achieving 100 percent harmony in physique. His forehead bore many wrinkles (皺); these were likely not the wrinkles of worldly dust (띄끌주름) from struggling on the streets (街頭) for fine clothes and good food (好衣好食), nor the wrinkles of heartache from disappointment with a beauty, fallen into the depths (深底) of sorrow (悲哀). No, they were certainly not such vulgar (俗된) wrinkles. (3 lines omitted) His eyes behind his eyeglasses (眼鏡) sparkled with wisdom (智慧), and the lines (線) descending from both sides of his nose to the corners of his mouth were so powerfully and deeply etched, as if cut by a knife, expressing an indomitable (百折不屈) determination (決心). His mouth was very wide, suggesting he was an orator (雄辯家). In terms of calmness (沈着) and prudence (穩健), he presented the appearance of a confident (堂堂) statesman (政治家); in terms of combativeness (鬪爭性), challenging 'come at me if you dare' (뎀빌테면 뎀비라), he was a steadfast (꿋꿋한) [3 characters omitted]; and in his attitude of slyly evading commitment (꽁문이를 살살 빼는) so as not to bear responsibility for his words (言責) even to us, foreigners and private individuals (私人), he gave the impression of a first-rate (一流) diplomat (外交家). In the general election (總選擧) at that time, De Valera's party suffered a crushing defeat (一敗塗地), and Cosgrave [W. T. Cosgrave] (코스그레-브) of the Conservative Party (保守黨) seized and held political power (政權). But as fortune (運數) is destined to turn and turn again, in the recent general election, Fianna Fáil (피안나, 페일) was victorious, and Mr. De Valera was inaugurated as President of Ireland. Less than 24 hours after the new cabinet (新內閣) was formed, he granted special amnesty (特赦) to 20 political prisoners (政治犯), and subsequently issued a statement (聲明) declaring the deletion of the clause requiring an oath to the British Empire (英帝) from the Irish Constitution (憲法), etc. [3 characters omitted] His entire being, with the agility (機敏) and boldness (大膽) of a statesman, is projected anew onto the screen of my mind (腦幕).<27>

**Summary**

The author, Yi Chŏngsŏp, recounts his 1927 meeting with Éamon de Valera, the Irish independence leader, in Dublin during a busy election period. He describes de Valera's physical appearance and character, noting his sharp intellect, determination etched on his face, oratorical skills, and diplomatic cautiousness. Yi contrasts this past meeting, where de Valera's party subsequently lost the election to Cosgrave, with the recent 1932 election where de Valera won and became President, immediately taking bold actions like releasing political prisoners and moving to remove the oath to the British Crown from the constitution. For historians of Korean colonial history, this article is interesting as it shows a Korean intellectual keenly observing and reporting on another nation's struggle for independence and sovereignty against a major empire (Britain), drawing implicit parallels or lessons for Korea's own situation under Japanese rule. The focus on de Valera's leadership qualities and bold anti-colonial actions (like removing the oath) would have resonated strongly with Korean readers yearning for their own independence.

愛蘭 首都 떠블린에서
						李晶燮
그것은 1927年 첫 가을이엇든가 愛蘭의 首都 「떠블린」의 「피안나페일」 本部에 獨立派 首領 「데발레라」씨를 차젓다. 愛嬌가 절절 흐르는 듯한 女秘書가 나오드니 「約束대로 또 오섯습니다그려. 그런데요 데발레라씨는 지금 總選擧 중이어서 매우 바뿌십니다. 만나뵐 수 없는 것이지만 遠來의 珍客이라기 잠간 會見하시겟답니다. 말슴은 간단히...」하면서 시간의 防禦線을 미리부터 친다. 이윽고 데발레라씨가 터덜거리고 들어오는데 어쨋든 會經愛蘭共和國 大統領이요 大政黨 「피안나, 페일」의 現任總裁이시다. 어찌 緊張味가 없엇을 소냐. 얼른 보니 中키에 比較的 가느다한 몸도 比較的 조그마한 頭蓋骨을 얹엇으니 體格으로의 調和味는 100퍼센트엿다. 이마에는 많은 주름(皺)이 잇으니 이 주름은 好衣好食하기 위하야 街頭에서 奮鬪한 物慾의 띄끌주름이 아닐 것이며 美人에 失意하야 悲哀의 深底에 빠진 마음 苦痛의 주름도 아닐 것이다. 아니다 결코 그런 俗된 주름이 아니다. (3行 畧) 眼鏡 너머의 兩眼은 智慧로 번적어리며 코 兩側오로부터 口邊으로 내려온 줄(線)은 어떠케나 힘잇게 깊이 패이엇는지 마치 칼로 버인 듯 하야 거기에는 百折不屈의 決心이 表現되엇으며 입은 몹시 넓어 그는 雄辯家이오 하는 듯 보엿다. 沈着하고 穩健한 方面으로는 堂堂한 政治家도 뎀빌테면 뎀비라는 鬪爭性의 面貌로는 꿋꿋한 (3字 略)요 外國人인 그리고 私人인 우리에게까지 言責을 지지 말기 위하야 꽁문이를 살살 빼는 態度로는 一流의 外交家라는 印象을 주엇다. 이 때의 總選擧에 「데발레라」의 黨이 一敗倒地하야 保守黨의 「코스그레-브」가 政權을 잡어 오드니 運數는 元來 돌고 또 돌기로 되어 잇는지라 금번 總選擧에는 「픊�안나, 페일」의 勝利와 함께 「데발레라」씨가 愛蘭의 大統領으로 就任하엿다. 新內閣이 成立되어서 24시간도 못 되어 政治犯 20명을 特赦하엿고 다음에 愛蘭 憲法에서 英帝에게 宣誓하는 件을 削除한다는 聲明을 發한 등 (3字 畧) 政治家로의 機敏과 大膽에 그의 全體가 새삼스럽게 나의 腦幕에 映寫된다.＜27＞
