Setting Sail
						Ho Chegwang

Brother! Hurry, get on the boat
The water will soon be gone. Isn't today neap tide?
Draw water deep into the barrels
Who knows when we'll get to water again?
Don't hoist the sail; the Hanu wind is foul.
It seems it will hurry things indeed!
It looks like another storm has risen in the front inlet.

Look at the rushing tide!
Where is this multitude all rushing from?
Someone is following behind.
Eugh! That dark, dead face, blue
lips with bared teeth, unable even to cover
its filthy flesh, floundering!
Ah! This sky-reaching breath sound, this senseless, spirit-lost "Ah! Ah!" cry.
Somewhere, weeping breaks out.
"Hey! Help!" Don't you hear that voice, over there?

Why is the zelkova tree on the hill writhing so?
Look there! The house’s thatched roof (집웅) has flown right off.
Do the grains of sand weep all night? Two egrets have already gone in. Their throats are choked, they're saying something!
It doesn't sleep, hey! This wind. It seems it's not an ordinary breath.
Listen quietly, doesn't the wind's windpipe pound as if to burst? Can't you see the writhing blood vessels in the Dongpan pine grove?
Hey! This seething sound! This sound!
The wind is mustering its force, behind that sound ridge
Aren't blades flashing, over there?
It runs. Look there! Towards where the tide rushes
The wind runs, holding a blade.

Brother! In this sad and fearful time, we must set out on the road.
Weigh the anchor. The island people must be waiting long. All must be lying down, starving. Some crying in pain. Ah! The sight of them starving to death comes to my eyes.
Shouts ring out from afar.
A fearsome fight has broken out.
Your boat must pass through that bleeding place, right?

Even if blood spatters fiercely onto the deck, yes,
do not stop rowing your pole.
The island people will hear this shouting, and weep again
Could they possibly think your boat will arrive?
Look at your grain (穀食) on the road.
They will not be sad that it's grain not fully loaded. You too have starved for three days!
Until the last mouthful of buckwheat paste
goes down the throats of those gasping with closed eyes, you must guard the boat.
While the water in your barrels does not run dry
You must reach the island.
- 1932.2 - <13>

**Summary**

This prose poem, "Setting Sail," depicts a desperate and urgent departure by boat amidst a storm and chaos. The speaker urges his brother onto the boat to deliver grain to starving islanders, emphasizing the grim conditions—a pursuing, death-like figure, a raging storm damaging homes, and the sounds of distant conflict. The journey is fraught with danger, passing through a "bleeding place," yet the mission to bring relief, however meager, to the suffering islanders is paramount, even as the boatmen themselves are starving. From a historical perspective, the poem vividly portrays the extreme hardship and desperation likely faced by many Koreans during the colonial period. The imagery of starvation, environmental fury, and implied violence ("fearsome fight," "bleeding place," "wind holding a blade") could reflect the socio-economic pressures, natural disasters, or perhaps even veiled commentary on the oppressive conditions of the time, making it valuable for understanding the affective atmosphere and lived experiences of the era.

出帆
						胡濟光
아우야! 어서 배로 올라라
물이 다 써겟다. 오늘이 조금이 아니냐?
창자에 물을 듬북 길어 올려라
언제 물에 오를지 알 수 잇느냐.
돛은 달지 말아라 한우 바람이 고약타.
기이 보챌 것 같고나!
앞개에 또 노대가 일엇나 보다.

밀려 오는 물살을 보아라!
어대로 다 쪼껴 오는 무린가 보다.
뒤에 누가 따르는 게다.
에구! 저 시컴언이 죽은 얼굴, 파-란
입술에 살여문 이빨, 더러운 살덩이도
못가리운 채 헤우적이누나!
아! 이 하눌 닿은 숨소리, 엄없이 넋잃은 「아! 아!」하는 고래기.
어대서 울음이 난다.
「야! 살려라!」하는 소리가 들리지, 저쪽으로?

당재 느트이 왜, 저리 굼틀 거릴까?
저봐라! 집에는 사슬넝(집웅)이 발각 날아 낫다.
모래알이 밤새 울지? 왜가리가 벌써 두 마리 채 들어갓다. 목이 메어 무슨 소리 하는데!
자지 안누나 야! 이 바람이. 네사로운 숨길이 아닌가 부다.
가만 듣어라, 바람 그 숨통이 터질 듯 뛰지? 동판 솔밭에 저 굼틀거리는 핏대가 보이지?
야! 이 들끓는 소리! 이 소리!
바람이 울력해 오누나, 저 소리재 뒤로
칼날이 나들지 저-기?
달린다. 저봐라! 물살 쪼껴오는 곳으로
바람이 칼들고 달린다.

아우야! 이 슬프고 두려운 때에 길을 떠나야 하겟고나.
닻을 감아라. 섬 사람들이 오래 기달리고 잇겟다. 모두 굶어 눕엇겟다. 더러는 아퍼 울고. 아! 굶어 죽는 꼴이 눈에 어린다.
멀리서 아우성을 친다.
무서운 싸움이 벌려 젓고나.
네 배는 저 피흘르는 속을 지나가야 하겟지?

뱃장에 핏발 사납게 설레 들어도, 네,
삿대 젓기를 놓지 말아라.
섬 사람들은 이 아우성을 듣고, 또 울겟다
네 배가 오리라고야 생각 할 수 잇겟니?
길 우에 네 나달(穀食)을 보아라.
그득 실찌 못한 나달이라고 그들이 서여하지 않으리라 너도 사홀을 굶엇구나!
눈감은 채 할딱이는 그들의 올대로
모밀밈 한 술이 넘어 갈 때까지 너는 배를 지켜라.
네 창자에 물이 마르지 않을 동안에
섬에 다아야 하겟다.
- 1932.2 - ＜13＞
