The Rebirth of Nongae
						Kim Tongin

Chinju Ch'oksŏngnu Pavilion... Who among those who ascend Ch'oksŏngnu would not remember the righteous kisaeng Nongae? Nongae is dead. But if she were to live again... Novelist Mr. Kim Tongin's sharp pen will leap like Nongae's righteous spirit.

Chapter of Self-Sacrifice

Chinju Castle (晋州城) had fallen.
This Chinju Castle, which had been so sturdy during the Imjin War that Magistrate Kim Simin (判官 金時敏), with only a few thousand soldiers, had repelled a hundred thousand Japanese troops, had also fallen.
This time, the defending army numbered over 60,000. Magistrate Sŏ Wŏllye (牧使 徐元禮) and Righteous Army Leader Kim Ch'ŏnil (倡義使 金千鎰) commanded 60,000 soldiers, awaiting the arrival of the Japanese troops. And they were complacent. Previously, even with a few thousand weak soldiers, they had been able to repel 100,000 enemy troops, so how much more so now with an army several tens of times larger than then? There was no reason why this army could not repel the enemy. Ample soldiers, ample provisions – from any perspective, Chinju Castle did not seem likely to fall.
When Nongae (論介), the beloved mistress of Chinju Magistrate Sŏ Wŏllye, dared to stick her nose into military affairs, all the commanders wanted to execute her immediately, calling her words the presumptuous talk of a bewitching woman.
—Previously, because the army was small, the soldiers knew their commander, and the commander cared for his soldiers, so even with a few thousand troops, they could repel a hundred thousand. But now is different from then. You must not think of that time, when a single command from the general spread throughout the entire army, the same as now. The 60,000 soldiers now do not know who their commander is, and the commander also does not know which are his subordinates. To try and block the elite <16> Japanese troops with this army lacking unity is unreasonable talk. A foolish girl raised in the demimonde (花柳界) – what would she know? But even she can clearly see the lack of unity in the soldiers' hearts.
With clear eyes lifted fearlessly, looking around at the assembled generals, Nongae spoke with a fervor in her tone that could not easily be broken.
However, there was no way such words from a mere girl could enter the ears of the generals whose hearts had become arrogant.
Righteous Army Leader Kim Ch'ŏnil found Nongae's bold opposition most offensive. And he immediately ordered soldiers to take Nongae out and behead her.
Had it not been for her title as the beloved mistress of Chinju Magistrate Sŏ Wŏllye, Nongae would likely have suffered a wrongful death there. Because she held that title, other commanders intervened, soothing Kim Ch'ŏnil's anger, and barely managed to save her from death. Instead, it was decided to expel Nongae from Chinju Castle.
Nongae was expelled from Chinju Castle. As she was being expelled, Nongae cast her eyes one last time towards her husband – Sŏ Wŏllye, who had loved her dearly.
—This is our final farewell. How must your humble concubine feel, leaving Your Excellency behind in this castle where there is no way to survive? Only fight bravely. Fight and fight again, and offer your life for the country. Your humble concubine, too, will never cherish her life when it comes to the country. Enjoying the promise that our two souls, offered for the country, will meet again soon in the afterlife, your humble concubine departs.—
Looking at Sŏ Wŏllye with tear-filled eyes, Nongae left behind Chinju Castle, where she was born and raised, where her parents and ancestors were born and raised, and stepped outside the castle gate.

The Japanese troops arrived.
The battle began.
However, the result was just as Nongae had predicted. The disunited army acted each according to their own whims. The commanders, not knowing which soldiers were their own, each gave orders recklessly. Through the gaps, the Japanese troops climbed the walls, opened the gates, and surged into Chinju Castle like a tidal wave.
Sŏ Wŏllye, Kim Ch'ŏnil, and all the other commanders were slaughtered by nameless Japanese soldiers without even having had a chance to fight properly.
—Thus, Chinju Castle finally fell.
To an outsider's eye, it might have been a spectacle, but to those involved, it was a heart-wrenching event.
Chinju Castle burned fiercely. Nongae, who had previously been expelled from Chinju Castle and was hiding in the house of some relative (a farmer) outside the castle, watched the burning Chinju Castle under a blood-red sky. Chinju Castle – which had boasted its prosperity until just a few days ago – seemed to have fallen without much sound of bows and arrows (弓矢) and without the battle continuing for long – that Chinju Castle was now burning. According to those who had barely escaped with their lives, not a single civil or military official inside the castle was left alive; all were slaughtered by the enemy troops. Then, Nongae's own husband, Sŏ Wŏllye, must surely have died in battle. Under those blazing flames, his uncollected body would now have turned into a handful of ashes. Her parents who bore her, raised her, and disciplined her, her beloved younger siblings – all would now have turned into a handful of ashes. Thinking of these things, rather than resenting the generals who had not listened to her loyal advice (忠諫) and brought about this situation, rather than trembling at the great tragedy of the nation's ruin, Nongae simply hated the immediate enemies, the Japanese generals and soldiers. She hated them so much that even tearing out their livers and chewing on them would not feel satisfying.
Standing vacantly in the yard, gazing at the distant burning Chinju Castle, tears of grief flowed endlessly, endlessly from Nongae's eyes. When she pictured the scene of her husband Sŏ Wŏllye, who was taciturn and gentlemanly though not necessarily gallant, and her elderly parents <17> and siblings being trampled to death by the cruel enemy soldiers, Nongae trembled with rage, unable to bear it.
Thus, enmity towards them fiercely flared up in Nongae's heart.

The next day, Nongae appeared near the enemy camp. With oiled hair, lips adorned with rouge, and her body dressed in the most splendid clothes, Nongae's appearance was such that even a woman might fall for her.
Having failed in her attempt to examine the corpses chaotically strewn within the burnt castle – some decapitated, some with severed arms or legs – one by one, searching for her beloved husband or parents and siblings, Nongae concealed her bitter heart deeply and wandered near the enemy camp.
Mogokuchon Rokujo (毛谷村六助), a subordinate general of Katō Kiyomasa, happened to come out on some business and bumped right into Nongae.
Nongae saw Rokujo. For a moment, a sharp expression of anger and resentment threatened to surface in Nongae's eyes. But Nongae firmly suppressed it. Nongae, whose eyes had widened upon encountering Rokujo, calmly closed them and then opened them again. When she did, Nongae's eyes held a coquetry that could melt even iron.
Rokujo, who had intended to just walk past Nongae, stopped his feet, halted by these strangely captivating eyes. And he stared at Nongae as if to pierce through her.
Meeting Rokujo's piercing gaze, Nongae did not flinch. And for a while, she looked back at Rokujo. After letting her final glance slowly move from his chest down to his feet, she bowed her head slightly and prepared to leave. However, before Nongae could turn away, Rokujo called out to her. It was Japanese, so the exact meaning was unclear, but the calling sound was distinct. Nongae turned her body, which had been about to turn away, back towards Rokujo.
『XXXXXX』
Words unintelligible to Nongae came again from Rokujo's mouth. Nongae smiled slightly. And she shook her head side to side, indicating she didn't understand.
Rokujo touched his waist. He took out a portable ink-and-brush set ("ヤダテ"). He took out paper from his breast pocket. And he came closer to Nongae.
『What is your name?』
Rokujo wrote this in Chinese characters on the paper. Nongae received the paper and brush.
『Chinju kwan'gi Nongae (진주관긔 논개 - Chinju official kisaeng, Nongae)』
Thus, the brush talk (筆談) began.
『Age?』
『Between fifteen and twenty-five. Think as you please, General.』
『Who do you think I am?』
『I do not know who you are, but I think you are a famous general from Japan.』
『Why?』
『The dignity of a famous general is apparent.』
『Shall we go into my camp for a short rest?』
『I know that a military camp is not a place for a woman to enter.』
『Shall we go to your house?』
『At my house reside my parents who regard the Japanese army as enemies.』
『Then, is there nowhere we can talk for a bit?』
『...』
『Tomorrow (July 22nd) there will be a banquet at Ch'oksŏngnu Pavilion (矗石樓). Will you come that day and perhaps add to the banquet's excitement?』
Nongae looked up at Rokujo's face. Forcibly concealing the venomous glare that kept trying to emerge, Nongae's heart felt like tearing as she looked at Rokujo's face with flowing coquetry.
After looking at Rokujo's face for a while, Nongae lightly bowed her head, indicating she would go. Then she took her leave of Rokujo.
On her way back after parting from Rokujo, Nongae looked back at Rokujo several times, as if her heart lingered. Rokujo stood stiffly rooted to the spot, watching Nongae go. <18>
Thus, Nongae and Rokujo had their first meeting.

『Shinshū』
This clearly meant Chinju (晋州).
At the victory celebration banquet at Ch'oksŏngnu Pavilion—the drinks had flowed considerably. The word 『Shinshū』 repeatedly came from the mouths of the Japanese generals. And then they would point towards Chinju Castle, still emitting black smoke as it burned, and laugh cheerfully.
『Nongae!』
『Yes?』
『Komungo』
It was a command for Nongae to play the kŏmun'go. The smell of sweat emanated from the bodies of the generals who prided themselves on martial prowess (武). The perfume from the kisaengs made that sweat smell even more nauseating. There was also a strong smell of alcohol.
It was a summer day. The sound of the Long River flowing below could be heard splashing. Reflected light sparkled up to the Ch'oksŏngnu Pavilion. Amidst this, generals boasting of their capacity for strong drink (斗酒) pressed forward. The dispirited official kisaengs, trembling in body and mind, were engrossed in pouring drinks and singing.
Only Nongae was serving the generals with flowing coquetry.
『Nongae. Have a drink.』
Nongae accepted without refusal and drank.
『Nongae. Sing a song!』
Nongae sang without hesitation.
『Nongae. Dance.』
Nongae danced without reservation.
Among them, Rokujo, who had become utterly infatuated with Nongae due to their brief meeting outside the camp the day before, did not leave Nongae's side for even a moment.
『My Nongae. My Nongae.』
He brought his face with its sideburns, unkempt from not having been able to wash properly in the camp, close to Nongae's and tried to charm her with some unintelligible sounds.
Phew! Phew! It was truly difficult to endure the smell of sweat and foul odors assaulting her nose while maintaining a smile. But Nongae endured it all. And she was just watching for an opportunity. There was a dagger in her breast. Poison was also prepared. She was waiting for the chance to use either one, suppressing all her emotions and expressions.
However, there were no openings in the bodies of the Japanese generals, trained in martial arts (武) since childhood. There was no opportunity for even the smallest action.
At one point, a certain warrior came and sat before Nongae. His back was turned towards Nongae. Nongae looked around. Everyone was absorbed in drinking, and no one was paying attention this way. Nongae's eyes sharpened. Nongae tapped her chest. And she tried to grasp the hilt of the dagger inside her jacket flap. But at Nongae's unusual breath sound, the Japanese general swiftly turned around. It was difficult even for Nongae to deceive the general's eyes, which stared at her face as if to pierce it, with a smile. After forcing a slight smile, and seeing his gaze still fixed on her face, she made a coquettish gesture towards the general and said,
『Why are you looking at me like this? Then I'll go over there,』 and left the spot.

Leaving that spot, Nongae descended the stairs below Ch'oksŏngnu Pavilion. Then she circled the pavilion once and came around to the riverbank.
Between the water and the pavilion, there was a rock about two arm's lengths wide. Nongae went and stood on that rock. And she looked down at the water. The water was still flowing downstream with a splashing sound. She looked back at Chinju Castle. Chinju Castle was still burning, emitting black smoke. She looked up at the pavilion. Up in the pavilion, they were still lost in music, dance, and drink. As she surveyed this scene from different angles, blood seemed to surge <19> into Nongae's eyes again.
『Nongae. You now—』
It was the voice of the deceased Magistrate Sŏ. Nongae jumped in fright. What should she do? What must she do? The river water still flowed with a splashing sound. Chinju Castle was still burning even now.
Nongae slowly turned towards the pavilion.
『Nongae』
Looking up, Rokujo stood out on the railing. Suppressing the tears that threatened to emerge again, Nongae smiled faintly.
Rokujo looked down from the pavilion. Nongae looked up at Rokujo on the pavilion. Flowing coquetry again adorned Nongae's face. Above the pavilion and below the pavilion—the eyes of the two people met and held each other for a while.
Rokujo raised his hand. And he gestured for Nongae to come up. But Nongae shook her head side to side. Then she gestured with her eyes for Rokujo to come down. Rokujo pointed downwards with his finger. Nongae nodded her head.
『It's quiet here』
Nongae's eyes spoke thus.
Rokujo's figure disappeared from the railing. The sound of staggering footsteps came from that direction. The footsteps drew nearer. But Nongae stood still as if she didn't notice. Her heart was now extremely agitated... It was difficult, by any means, not to show this agitated feeling on her face. With her head bowed low, Nongae stood there as if planted.
『Nongae』
Rokujo's voice sounded right behind Nongae. Large hands covered Nongae's two eyes. She could sense his dirty, side-burned face gradually approaching.
Nongae whirled around. She raised both hands and wrapped them around Rokujo's neck. And she stepped back a couple of paces. Rokujo, harboring no suspicion at Nongae's passion (?), followed Nongae dazedly. The place where they stood was the edge of the rock; one misstep meant falling into the water.
Nongae gradually tightened the arms wrapped around Rokujo's neck. Rokujo's head gradually came closer towards Nongae's face. Nongae closed her eyes tightly. And she pulled Rokujo's head even closer.
Nongae smelled the scent of alcohol from Rokujo's mouth. She heard his panting breaths. She also felt Rokujo's sideburns brushing against her soft face. After knowing that Rokujo's mind had thus lost its composure, Nongae suddenly seized Rokujo's neck. Simultaneously, Nongae's foot forcefully kicked Rokujo's legs.
The next moment, the two bodies disappeared from atop that rock (now called Ŭiam (義岩 - Righteous Rock)).
A large ripple formed in the river water flowing below the rock and gradually, gradually widened.

Thus, at a precious age, Nongae departed this world.
Nongae's soul, having left this world, went straight up to Heaven (天上). However, the gatekeeper, having reviewed Nongae's record of conduct (行錄) during her time on earth, did not open the gate for Nongae.
「You are a woman who killed a person. Go to Hell (地府).」 Thus, Nongae was turned away from there.
Turned away from there, Nongae this time went to Hell. But Hell also did not accept Nongae.
「Madam, you are one who offered her life for righteousness (義) – why have you come here instead of going to Heaven? This place punishes only wicked and lowly (낮본) people – we absolutely cannot admit a noble person like you, Madam.」
These were the words of Hell's gatekeeper.
—Thus, Nongae's soul, having lost its place in both Heaven and Hell, wandered aimlessly, adrift in the realm of the dead (幽明界). (To be continued) <20>

**Summary**

This fictional story by Kim Tongin retells the famous tale of Nongae, a kisaeng in Chinju during the Japanese invasions of Korea (Imjin War). After Chinju Castle falls due to the arrogance and disunity of the Korean commanders, despite her earlier warnings, Nongae witnesses the destruction and death of her husband (the magistrate) and family. Filled with hatred for the Japanese invaders, she uses her beauty to infiltrate a victory banquet held by the Japanese general Mogokuchon Rokujo at the Ch'oksŏngnu Pavilion. Feigning affection, she lures the drunken general to a cliff edge overlooking the river, embraces him tightly, and pulls them both into the water, sacrificing her life to kill the enemy commander. The story ends with her soul being rejected by both Heaven (for killing) and Hell (for her righteous sacrifice), leaving her to wander in the afterlife.

From a historical perspective, the story is interesting as a piece of colonial-era Korean literature (1932) romanticizing a historical act of resistance against a past Japanese invasion, published under Japanese rule. It reflects nationalist sentiments and the veneration of figures like Nongae as symbols of defiance. The portrayal of Japanese soldiers and commanders as arrogant and brutal, contrasted with Nongae's calculated sacrifice and righteousness, would resonate with contemporary Korean readers experiencing colonial occupation. Historians might find the specific details (like the invented name Mogokuchon Rokujo, the depiction of the generals' council, the dialogue) less factual but valuable for understanding how historical narratives were adapted and utilized for cultural expression and potentially subtle resistance during the colonial period.

論介의 還生
						金東仁

晉州矗石樓……矗石樓에 오르는 사람 그 누구가 義妓 論介를 追憶치 않으리오. 論介는 죽엇다. 그러나 그가 살아온다면……小說家 金東仁씨의 筆鋒은 論介의 義魂과 같이 動躍할것입니다.
投身篇
진주성(晋州城)은 함락되엇다.
임진란 때에 판관 김시민(判官 金時敏)이 겨우 수 천의 적은 군사로 십만 왜병을 물리친 만치 튼튼하든 이 진주성도 함락이 되엇다.
이번에는 지키는 군사가 6만이 넘엇다. 목사 서원례(牧使 徐元禮)와 창의사 김천일(倡義使  金千鎰)이 6만의 군사를 거느리고 왜병이 오기를 기다리고 잇엇다. 그러고 그들은 마음 놓고 잇엇다. 이전에 수천의 약졸로도 능히 10만의 적병을 물리쳣거늘 하믈며 이번에는 그때보다 수십 곱이 되는 군사가 아니냐, 이 군사로 적병을 못 물리칠 까닭이 없다. 넉넉한 군사 넉넉한 량식 어디로 보던지 진주성 뿐은 함락될 듯 싶지 않앗다.
진주목 서원례의 애첩 논개(論介)가 대담히도 군정(軍政)에 주둥이를 디밀 때에 모든 장사들은 요망한 게집의 참람된 말이라고 당장에 베려 하엿다.
-전에는 군사가 적엇으므로 군사는 장수를 알고 장수는 군사를 사랑해서 능히 수천의 군사로도 십만 대군을 물리쳣거니와 지금은 그 때와는 다르옵니다. 장수의 한 마디의 호령이 전군에 퍼질 그 때와 지금과를 같이 생각해서는 안되옵니다. 6만의 군사는 지금 누가 자기네의 장인지를 모르고 장수는 또한 어느 것이 자기의 부하인지 모르는 통일 없는 이 군사로서 정예한＜16＞ 왜병을 막으려는 것은 당치 않은 말슴이외다. 화류게에 자라난 무식한 게집애 - 무엇을 알으리까만 통일 안 된 군심 뿐은 넉넉히 볼 수가 잇습니다.
명낭한 눈을 저픔없이 치 뜨고 모여 앉은 장성(將星)들을 둘러보며 이러케 말하는 논개의 어조에는 능히 꺽기 어려운 열성이 잇엇다.
그러나 마음이 교하게 된 장성들의 귀에 이러한 소소한 게집의 말이 들어갈 리가 만무하엿다.
창의사 김천일이 논개의 당돌한 반대를 제일 괘씸하게 보앗다. 그러고 당장에 군사를 시키어서 논개를 내어다가 베이려 하엿다.

『농게』
육조의 목소리가 논개의 곳 뒤에서 낫다. 커다란 손이 논개의 두 눈을 덮엇다. 구레나릇의 더러운 얼굴이 차차 접근되는 것도 짐작되엇다.
논개는 홱 돌아섯다. 양 손을 들어서 육조의 목에 감앗다. 그러고 두어 걸음 물러 섯다. 육조는 이 논개의 정열(?)에 아모 의심도 품지 않고 덜레덜레 논개에게 끌려 갓다. 그들이 선 곳은 바위의 끝 한 발만 그릇하면 물에 떨어질 곳이엇다.
논개는 육조의 목에 감은 팔을 차차 당기엇다. 육조의 머리가 차차 논개의 얼굴을 향하여 가까히 왓다. 논개는 눈을 힘잇게 감앗다. 그러고 육조의 머리를 더욱 가까히 끄을어 당겻다.
육조의 입에서 나는 술내음새를 논개는 맡앗다. 씩씩이는 숨ㅅ소리를 들엇다. 육조의 구레나릇이 보드러운 자기의 얼굴을 스치는 것도 알앗다. 이리하여 육조의 마음이 철을 잃게 되는 것을 안 뒤에 논개는 와락 육조의 목을 낚우어 채엇다. 동시에 눈개의 발은 힘잇게 육조의 다리를 찻다.
다음 순간 두 몸뚱이는 (지금 의암(義岩)이라 닐컷는) 그 바위 우에서 살아졋다.
바위 아래서 흐르든 강물에는 커다란 파문이 하나 생겨서 차차 차차 넓어 갓다.

이리하여 아까운 나이에 논개는 이 세상을 떠낫다.
이 세상을 떠난 논개의 혼은 곳 천상(天上)으로 올라 갓다. 그러나 논개의 재세 중의 행록(行錄)을 뒤적이어 본 문직이는 논개를 위하여 문을 열어 주지 않앗다.
「너는 사람을 죽인 게집이다. 지부로 가거라」 이리하여 논개는 거기서 쫓겨 낫다.
거기서 쫓겨난 논개는 이번은 지부로 갓다. 그러나 지부에서도 또한 논개를 받지를 않앗다.
「낭낭은 의를 위해서 목숨을 받히신 이 - 웨 천상으로 가시지 않고 이곳으로 오섯습니까? 이 곳은 고약하고 낮본 인종만 벌하는 곳 - 낭낭 같은 존귀한 분은 도저히 들일 수가 없소이다.」
지부의 문직이의 말은 이것이엇다.
-이리하여 천당과 지부에 그 갈 곳을 잃어버린 논개의 혼은 유명게에서 정처 없이 흐늘흐늘 헤매고 잇엇다.(게속)＜20＞
