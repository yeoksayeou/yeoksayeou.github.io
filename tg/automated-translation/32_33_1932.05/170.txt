**Body Text Translation:**
Round Table Conference, Urgent Protest Letter
						To Yu-ho in Germany

Chu Yo-han!

My writing has appeared again in the February issue of Tonggwang. Perhaps you are short on manuscripts. Wasn't that an English letter I sent to you privately? What is the point of translating it and publishing it as if I gave permission to Tonggwang? Moreover, by adding "습니다" (sŭmnida) to the sentence endings, you've made it look exactly like my writing style, so won't readers think I wrote it? That translation is quite different from what I meant. I never meant "Origin of the Nation" (國家의 起源). And the new term "Germans People" (께르만즈 國民) sounds strange even to me. Besides that, mentioning the Japanese people (日本國民)! I never meant the Japanese people. And translating Race as "種族" (chongjok - ethnic group) seems a bit weak. (Perhaps I added a note in Hanja suggesting 種族).

I am one of those who try to translate European technical terms as accurately as possible. (Ultimately, it would be most appropriate to translate Volkstums and Rasse, which National Socialist Adolf Hitler (民族社會主義者 아돌프 히틀러) brings up, as 民族 (minjok - nation/people) and 人種 (injong - race), wouldn't it?)

Mr. Chu, anyway, I've truly run into a lot of trouble because of my writing published in Tonggwang. Suddenly <42> it seems I've also been included among the theorists like Messrs. XXX, XXX. I absolutely refuse to become such an ignorant theorist. It's true that I am ignorant, but on the other hand, I am reluctant to theorize while forcibly protecting that ignorance.

Mr. Chu!

Shouldn't you also look out for my affairs a little? You might do such improper things thinking of your magazine, but on the other hand, what happens to me? Furthermore, what's the point of publicly disclosing that I am currently planning to write a book, or that Professor Mannheim (you wrote this as Maunhain (마운하인), didn't you?) said it was suitable for a doctoral dissertation? Doctor! Doctor! I am not one who particularly respects such Doctors (博士). People go to America (米國) or come to Germany (獨逸) and become Doctors. But those dissertations are mostly about Korea (朝鮮), which the examiners don't know well! In fact, those Doctors really don't know much. Hearing myself called Doctor, I am truly afraid that I myself might become such a rotten Doctor. A few days ago, a friend of mine from some country in the East (東洋) became a Doctor. A Doctor of Economics! But the dissertation he wrote was about a certain city in his own country. Here, dissertations of this kind are welcomed for obtaining materials. Thus, one obtains a Doctorate! But that Doctor wasn't particularly impressive. Most Doctors from the East are like this. Never mind the Doctors, Mr. Chu, anyway, I feel quite bad. Tonggwang now sounds to me like nothing but a troublesome magazine.

February 29, 1932 <43>
<42-43>

**Summary**

To Yu-ho, writing from Germany, lodges a strong protest with Chu Yo-han, the editor of Tonggwang magazine. He is upset that a private English letter he sent was translated and published without proper consent or accurate representation of his intended meaning, particularly regarding terms like "nation" and "race" (contrasting his preferred translations with those used for Hitler's concepts). He resents being labelled a "theorist" and criticizes the publication of personal details about his academic plans. To expresses disdain for the academic title "Doctor," observing that many Asians acquire PhDs abroad, particularly concerning topics about their home countries unfamiliar to examiners, resulting in superficial qualifications. He worries about becoming such a "rotten Doctor" himself and concludes that Tonggwang has become a source of trouble for him.

From a historical perspective, this article offers a fascinating glimpse into the anxieties and intellectual concerns of a Korean intellectual studying in Europe during the colonial period. To Yu-ho's engagement with European political terminology (Volkstums, Rasse, National Socialism, Hitler), his critique of academic standards and the pursuit of PhDs (especially concerning knowledge production about colonial Korea for Western academia), and his assertion of intellectual independence against perceived misrepresentation by a Korean publication are particularly revealing. Historians might find interest in his views on identity (民族 vs. 人種), his critique of the value placed on foreign degrees within the colonial context, and the dynamics between expatriate intellectuals and homeland publications.

圓卓會議, 緊急抗議文
						在獨逸 都宥浩
朱耀翰君!
東光 2月號에 내 글이 또 나타낫구려. 아마 原稿가 없기도 한가 봅니다. 그것은 내가 君에게 私私로히 한 英文書信이 아니엇소? 그것을 翻譯해서 내가 東光所許로 한게라고 하야 發表할 것이 무에 잇소? 게다가 語尾에 「습니다」를 부처 바로 내 文體를 만들어 놓앗으니 讀者가 그것을 내가 쓴거라고 알께 아니오. 그 翻譯文을 내가 意味한 바와는 아조 다르오. 나는 「國家의 起源」을 意味한 일이 없엇소. 그리고 「께르만즈 國民」이란 새 述語는 내게도 別거로 들립니다. 그 밖에 日本國民 云云! 나는 日本國民을 意味한 일이 없소. 그리고 Race를 種族이라고 譯한 것은 좀 弱하다고 생각됩니다. (아마 내가 種族이라고 漢字로 註를 단 듯 하오)
나는 歐文의 術語를 될 수 잇는 限에서 가장 正確히 翻譯할려고 애쓰는 사람 중의 하나이오. (民族社會主義者 아돌프 히틀러의 일으는 Volkstuns과 Rasse을 民族과 人種이라고 譯하는 것이 결국 至當하겟지오)
朱君, 如何間 나는 東光에 실린 나의 글로 해서 참말 말성이 많아젓습니다. 어느듯 ＜42＞ 나도 XXX, XXX 諸氏의 理論家중에 들어 버린 듯 합니다. 나는 그런 無識한 理論家가 되기를 절대로 拒絶합니다. 내가 無識한 것은 사실이오나 그러나 나는 그 反面에 그 無識을 억지로 保護해 가며 理論하기를 끄려 합니다.
朱君!
나의 일도 좀 돌보아 주어야 되지 안소? 君은 君의 雜誌를 생각해서 그런 안될 일도 하지만 그 反面에 나는 어떠케 됩니까. 게다가 내가 지금 책을 著作할려고 하느니 만하임 (이것을 마운하인이라고 썻습디다 그려) 敎授가 博士論文에 適合한 게라고 햇다느니 하는 것을 公開할 것이 무엇입니까? 博士! 博士! 나는 그런 博士를 그리 尊敬하는 者 아니외다. 米國에 가느니 獨逸에 왓느니 해서 博士가 됩니다. 그러나 그 論文이란 大槪 試驗官이 잘 모르는 朝鮮에 관한 것! 사실 그 博士님네는 정말 아는 것이 별로 없습니다. 博士소리를 내게 들려 놓으니 정말 내 자신도 그런 썩은 博士가 될가바 두렵소외다. 몇칠前 東洋 어느 나라에서 온 나의 벗 한 분이 博士가 되엇습니다. 經濟學博士! 그러나 그가 쓴 論文은 자기 나라 어느 都市에 관한 것이엇습니다. 여기서는 材料를 얻기 위하야 이런 종류의 論文을 歡迎합니다. 그래서 얻게 되는 博士! 그러나 그 博士가 별로 씨원치 않엇습니다. 東洋人의 博士중에는 이런 博士가 大部分이외다. 博士는 그만두고, 朱君, 如何間 꽤 미안합니다. 東光이라면 인제 내게는 말성거리 雜誌로밖에 안들립니다.
1932年 2月 29日＜43＞
＜42-43＞
