Article Title:	世界一週寫眞畵報(20), 巴里의 獎忠壇 「팡테옹」 (World Tour Photo Pictorial (20), Paris's Changch'ungdan, the "Panthéon")
Author:	李晶燮
Article Type:	기행문 (Travelogue)

World Tour Photo Pictorial
						I Chŏngsŏp

Standing on Sŭp'ŭllo Street (쓰-플로 街) and looking due east, a magnificent and imposing building soars high into the empty sky; this is the famous "P'angt'eong" (Panthéon). Initially a temple, it was converted during the French Revolution into a tomb for burying the remains of great citizens. One might call it the Changch'ungdan (獎忠壇) of Chosŏn (朝鮮).

It is a modern Greek-style building, whose majestic dome is supported by dozens of large Corinthian (고린트) style columns. Below the building's pediment (破風), the large characters "Aux grands hommes la Patrie reconnaissante" (To great men, the grateful Fatherland [偉人에 대하야 感銘하는 祖國]) are engraved, seemingly encouraging all French people who see these words to become someone whose bones will be buried in this tomb after death. On the pediment is a masterpiece relief (浮彫) by Tangje-rŭ (David d'Angers); the relief depicts the Fatherland distributing awards to its great citizens.

Who are these great citizens? To quickly pick out a few, they range from writers like the founder of Romanticism, Pikt'o Yugo (Victor Hugo), and the giant of Naturalism, Tchora (Zola), to politicians like Mirabo (Mirabeau) and Choresŭ (Jaurès); Russ'o (Rousseau) and Polt'e-rŭ (Voltaire), who greatly influenced the outbreak of the French Revolution; President K'arŭno (Carnot), who prompted theories of the universe's demise with his "Study on the Power of Fire"; the mathematician Mongji (Monge); the master chemist Perŭt'olle (Berthelot); Rap'ŭllasŭ (Laplace), who made astronomy a complete science; K'wibi'e (Cuvier), the founder of comparative biology and paleontology; the medical master Piswa (Bichat); and all the famous military figures whose bones are interred in this tomb. Burial (埋骨) in the P'angt'eong requires the approval (協贊) of Parliament. Therefore, among politicians, unless one was a statesman who, despite being a party politician, transcended party politics and fought thoroughly with patriotism (愛國主義), one cannot become a guest of this building. Because this is truly so, in recent times, only the politician Choresŭ (Jaurès), famous as a patriotic socialist, has had his bones buried in this building. It is said that becoming a member of this tomb upon death is the highest ideal and supreme glory for the French people.<44>
<44-44>

**Summary**

The article describes the Panthéon in Paris, a grand neoclassical building originally a church but repurposed after the French Revolution as a mausoleum for distinguished French citizens. The author, I Chŏngsŏp, compares it to Korea's Changch'ungdan shrine. He details its architecture, the inscription honoring great men, and lists notable figures interred within, including writers (Hugo, Zola), philosophers (Rousseau, Voltaire), politicians (Mirabeau, Jaurès, Carnot), scientists (Monge, Berthelot, Laplace, Cuvier, Bichat), and military heroes. Burial requires parliamentary consent, emphasizing state recognition and patriotism, exemplified by the recent interment of the socialist Jaurès. The article concludes that burial in the Panthéon represents the highest honor for French citizens. From a colonial Korean history perspective, the comparison to Changch'ungdan is particularly striking, implicitly highlighting concepts of national heroes, state-sanctioned honor, and patriotic remembrance, themes resonant for Koreans under Japanese rule who lacked national sovereignty. The emphasis on "patriotism" (愛國主義) and the process of national recognition could serve as both an inspiration and a poignant reminder of Korea's own situation.

世界一週寫眞畵報
						李晶燮
「쓰-플로」 街에 ‚˜서서 正東을 바라보면 壯快하고도 威嚴堂堂한 建物 하나가 虛空中天에 우뚝히 솟아 잇으니 이것이 유명한 「팡테옹」이다. 처음에는 寺院이엇지만 佛蘭西革命期에 와서 위대한 市民의 遺骨을 埋葬하는 墳墓로 方向을 轉換하엿으니 말하자면 朝鮮의 獎忠壇이라고나 할까.
近世 希臘式 建物로서 壯嚴한 圓天蓋는 수십개의 「고린트」式 大柱로 支持되어 잇으며 建物의 破風 下部에 Aux grands hommes la Patrie reconnaissante (偉人에 대하야 感銘하는 祖國)이라는 큰 글字를 새겨 부처 이 글 보는 모든 佛人에게 마치 너도 죽걸랑 뼈를 이 무덤에 무칠 사람 되라고 激勵하는 것 같앗다. 破風에는 「당제-르」의 傑作인 浮彫가 잇으니 그 浮彫는 祖國이 위대한 市民에게 賞을 노나 주는 光景인 것이다.
위대한 市民은 그 누구누구인가 얼른 몇 개만 주서 쓰기로 하면 浪慢派의 始祖 「빅토·유고」, 自然派의 巨頭 「쪼라」와 같은 文人을 爲始하야 「미라보」와 「죠레쓰」같은 政治家며, 佛國革命을 誘發함에 큰 影響을 준 「룻쏘」와 「볼테-르」며 「火의 勢力에 대한 硏究」로써 宇宙滅亡論을 일으키게 한 大統領 「카르노-」, 數學者 「몽지」, 化學大家 「베르톨레」, 天文學을 완전한 科學으로 맨든 「라플라쓰」, 比較生勿學과 古生物學의 建設者 「퀴비에」, 醫學大家 「비솨」 등 科學者로부터 유명한 軍人의 모든 뼈가 이 墳墓안에 安葬되어 잇다. 「팡테옹」에의 埋骨은 議會의 協贊을 얻어야 한다. 그러므로 政治家로서는 政黨政治家이면서도 오히려 政黨을 超越하야 徹頭徹尾 愛國主義로 奮鬪한 政治家가 아니면 이 建物의 손이 될 수 없다. 이 실로 이러하기 때문에 近來에는 愛國社會主義者로 유명한 政治家 「죠-레쓰」만이 그 뼈를 이 建物에 묻은 것이다. 죽어 이 무덤의 一員이 되랴는 것이 佛國人의 最高 理想이요 至上榮光이란다.＜44＞
