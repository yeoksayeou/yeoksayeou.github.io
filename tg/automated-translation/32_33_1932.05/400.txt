Magazine Title: 東光 제33호
Publication Date: 1932년 05월 01일
Article Title: 꽃에 물주는 뜻은 (The Meaning of Watering Flowers)
Author: 吳一島
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

The Meaning of Watering Flowers
						O Il-to

The meaning of giving water to the flower
Is to say, "Bloom when spring comes."

Others say:
How can flowers bloom upon this dry land?

But I go out to the garden and
Give water to the flower.
When the spring breeze of a loving mother (慈母) blows,
Behold! The red flower blooming abundantly on that branch.

On one small stalk of flower,
The meaning of giving water
Is so that leaves may grow when summer comes.

Others say—
How can leaves grow upon this barren (패라운) land?

But I water the flower
Every day without rest.
When the sky's nourishing (젓) rain falls in summer,
Behold! The green leaves filling that branch.

On one small stalk of flower,
The meaning of giving water
Is so that fruit may be borne when autumn comes.
Others say—
How can fruit be borne upon stone and sand?

But I
Give water to the flower.
When the golden autumn sun shines,
Behold! The yellow fruit ripened and hanging on that branch.

On one small stalk of flower,
The meaning of giving water
Is so as not to go against the heart of the Beloved (님).

Though You did not come when the flowers bloomed,
Though You did not come when the leaves unfurled,
Surely You will come when the fruit is borne?

Today too, I go out to the garden
And give water. Give water to the flower.
(End) <14>

**Summary**

This poem by O Il-to expresses persistent hope and dedication through the simple act of watering a flower on barren land. Despite others' doubts about whether flowers, leaves, or fruit can grow in such conditions, the speaker continues watering, driven by the belief in eventual fruition coinciding with the seasons (spring flowers, summer leaves, autumn fruit). Ultimately, the act is motivated by a desire not to disappoint a longed-for "Beloved" (님), who the speaker hopes will finally arrive when the fruit ripens. From a historical perspective, the poem, written in 1932 during the Japanese colonial period, resonates with symbolism. The "barren land" can be interpreted as the oppressed state of Korea, the act of watering as a metaphor for maintaining hope and nurturing national spirit despite hardship, and the anticipated arrival of the "Beloved" alongside the harvest as a yearning for liberation or a brighter future for the nation. Historians might find the poem's gentle yet steadfast tone and its potential allegorical meaning reflective of Korean cultural and nationalist sentiments under colonial rule.

꽃에 물주는 뜻은
						吳一島
꽃 물 주는 뜻은
봄 오거든 꽃피라는 말입니다.

남들이 말합니다.
마른 이 땅 우에 어이 꽃 필까

그러나 나는 뜰에 나가서
꽃에 물을 줍니다.
慈母의 봄바람이 불어 오거든
보옵소 담뿍 저 가지에 피는 붉은 꽃을

한 포기 적은 꽃에
물 주는 뜻은
여름 오거든 잎 자라나라는 탓입니다.

남들이 말하기를-
가을 오거든 열매 맺으라는 탓입니다.
남들이 말하기를-
돌과 모래 우에 어이 열매 맺을까

그러나 나는
꽃에 물을 줍니다.
황금의 가을볓 쪼일제
보옵소, 저 가지에 익어 달린 누런 열매를.
패라운 이 땅 우에 어이 잎 자라날까

그러나 나는 날마다 쉬지 않고
꽃에 물을 줍니다
여름 하눌 젓 비가 나리거든
보옵소, 가득 저 가지에 피는 푸른 잎을.

한 포기 적은 꽃에
물주는 뜻은
한 포기 적은 꽃에
물주는 뜻은
님의 마음을 아니어기랴는 탓입니다.

꽃필 때에도 안 오섯으나
잎 필 때에도 안 오섯으나
열매 맺을 때에야 설마 아니 오실까.

오늘도 나는 뜰에 나가서
물을 줍니다. 꽃에 물을 줍니다.
(끝) ＜14＞
