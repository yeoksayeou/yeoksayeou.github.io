**Body Text Translation:**
African Natives' Murder Festival

African natives (土人), even today, have not corrected their savage evil customs, and there are instances where they offer living virgins as sacrifices (祭物) to snakes (배암). Among them, there is a kind of religious ceremony worshipping a snake god (蛇神) called "Abi" (아비). It is said that the rites and rules (規律) for this religion's sacrificial ceremonies are extremely strict and secret, making it impossible for outsiders to know their inner workings (內幕).

Recently, a certain white man living in Dahomey City (따허매城) in the continent of Africa, after painstaking effort to learn the natives' secrets, managed to discover (探知) the location of the natives' sacrificial ceremonies. While waiting for the right time (時機), one night he heard the loud sound of drums. Knowing this was the signal (暗號) for the natives to gather for a sacrifice, he secretly went to the location, climbed a tall tree, hid, and watched their movements.

A Dance Party in the Dark Night (暗夜)

In that place, there was a thicket (叢林) with skeletons (骸骨) and beautiful women; this place is called the "Juju" (주주) grove and is considered the sacred ground (聖地) where the Abi snake god resides. The open space (空地) in front of it is the place for the sacrificial ceremony. Upon hearing the drumbeat once, natives from the surrounding villages (四面 鄕村) all rose with expressions of fear and anxiety (恐懼 不安 態狀) and gathered there together. This was a special sacrifice being held because one native had mistakenly killed a small snake, thereby angering (震怒) the Abi snake god, and it was said that disaster (災禍) would soon (將次) follow. Shamans and fortune-tellers (卜術), wearing masks and painted all over their bodies, adorned with the <53> skulls (頭骨) and bones of the dead, led the crowd. Behind them stood drummers (鼓手), armed soldiers (武裝兵), and several dozen beautiful young women. At the very back stood several officiants (祭官) dressed in long white robes (白色長衫), looking dignified and imposing (威儀堂堂), as they gathered at the ceremonial ground. First, a group of young men danced wildly until they collapsed from exhaustion (氣盡脉盡). Then, an officiant lit a fire and made the one who committed the sin pass through the middle of the fire. The chief officiant (主祭者) caught the blood of a sacrificed sheep in a vessel and had the young girls dance. At this time, the girls trembled as if death was right before them.

The Sacrifice of a Beauty

This was because one among them had the fate of having no choice but to die. The chief officiant took a mouthful of blood, went before the dancing girls, looked at each one, and then spat the blood onto the face (얼골) of one girl. The other girls all dispersed (헤여저), leaving only this one unfortunate girl, whose weeping was truly unbearable to watch. Then, they dug pits in the ground, placed the girl's feet into the pits, and buried her up to her knees in mud. They then bound (結縛) her arms so she could not move (요동). The crowd moved far away and produced a desolate (凄凉한) drum sound. An officiant recited incantations (呪文) for a while, then left, leaving only the girl. After a short while, a large snake (大蛇), 14 or 15 ch'ŏk (尺 - approx. 4-5 meters) long, emerged from the grove (林中) and coiled around the girl's body. The girl cried and cried until she finally died. Could there be such a cruel (慘酷한) thing? The snake did not eat her corpse (屍軆) but returned into the thicket (숲풀). After this, it is said that the Abi god's anger subsided, and they were thus able to avoid (免하게) calamity (災殃).<54>

**Summary**

This 1932 article recounts a second-hand story, allegedly from a white man in Dahomey, Africa, about witnessing a secret human sacrifice ritual. The ritual involves appeasing a snake god named 'Abi' after a native accidentally killed a snake. A young woman is selected during a ceremony, partially buried, bound, and left for a large snake to kill, purportedly satisfying the god and averting disaster. From a historical perspective, the article is interesting as it reflects the sensationalist and often stereotypical portrayal of African cultures prevalent in colonial-era narratives disseminated in places like Korea. It highlights how information (or misinformation) about distant 'exotic' lands was filtered through Western perspectives and presented to a Korean audience, potentially reinforcing notions of 'civilization' versus 'savagery' common during that period. Historians might find it useful for studying colonial mentalities, the reception of ethnographic (or pseudo-ethnographic) accounts, and the construction of 'the other' in early 20th-century Korean print media.

아푸리카 土人의 殺人祭
아푸리까 土人種은 오늘까지도 야만의 習惡을 고치지 몯하고 삶 處女를 祭物로 돌여 배암께 祭祀하는 일이 잇다. 저들 중에 「아비」라고 하는 蛇神을 崇拜하는 一種의 宗敎式이 잇으니 그 敎의 祭祀하는 禮儀와 規律은 심히 嚴하고 秘密하여 그들의 內幕을 外人은 알 수가 없다 한다.
最近 아푸리까洲 따허매城에 와서 사는 白人 某씨가 土人들의 秘密을 알기 위하야 苦心한 결과 土人들의 祭祀하는 場所를 探知하여 가지고 時機를 기다리고 잇던 차에 하로는 밤중에 북소리가 요란한 것을 듣고 이것이 土人들의 祭祀하려 모이자는 暗號인 줄 알고 곳 몰래 그 場所에 가서 높은 나무에 올라가 숨어서 저들의 動靜을 엿보앗다.

暗夜중의 舞踏會
그곳에 한 叢林에 骸骨과 美女가 잇는대 이것을 「주주」 林이라 하고 거기를 아비 蛇神이 잇는 聖地라고 한다. 그 前面에 잇는 空地가 즉 祭祀하는 場所인대 四面 鄕村 土人들이 한 번 북소리를 듣더니 모두 恐懼 不安한 態狀로 일어나 그곳에 一齊히 모인다. 어떤 土人 하나가 적은 뱀 하나를 잘못 죽여서 아비 蛇神을 震怒하게 하여 將次 災禍가 잇으리라 하야 이번은 특별히 하는 祭祀이엇다. 무당과 卜術들이 탈을 쓰고 온 몸에 그림을 그리고 죽은 사람의  ＜53＞ 頭骨과 뼈들을 둘러 차고 群衆의 잎장을 서고 그 뒤에는 鼓手와 武裝兵과 젊은 美女 수십명이 서고 맨 뒤에는 白色長衫을 입은 威儀堂堂한 祭官 數人이 서서 祭場으로 모여 왓다. 몬저는 젊은 남자 한 떼가 氣盡脉盡하야 저절로 꺼꿀어지기까지 춤을 한바탕 추고 나서는 祭官이 불을 퓌우고 罪지은 者를 그 불 가운대로 지나가게 하고 主祭者가 羊을 잡아 그 피를 그릇에 담아 들고 少女들로 하여금 춤을 추게 하는데 이 때에 그 少女들은 방금 죽음이 앞에 닥친 것 같이 벌벌 떨엇다.

美人의 犧牲
그 중에 하나는 죽을 수 밖에 없는 運命을 가진 때문이다. 主祭者가 피를 한 입 물고 少女들 춤추는 앞에 가서 일일히 보다가 한 여자의 얼골에 피를 뿜으니 다른 여자들은 다 헤여저 가고 오직 이 한 不幸한 여자만이 남아 잇어 痛哭하는 것은 참아 볼 수가 없엇다. 그리고는 땅에 구덩이들을 파고 그 여자의 두 발을 그 구덩이에 넣게 하고 진흙으로 무릎까지 묻고는 다시 두 팔을 結縛하여 요동하지 못하게 하고 여러 무리들은 멀쩍이 가서 凄凉한 북 소리를 내고 祭官이 한참 呪文을 외오다가 그 여자만 두고 다른 대로 가고 만다. 얼마 안 되어 長이 14, 5尺되는 大蛇가 林中으로부터 나와서 그 여자의 몸에 감기니 그 여자는 울고 울다가 그만 죽어버리고 말앗다. 이런 慘酷한 일이 어대 잇으랴. 그 뱀이 그 屍軆는 먹지 않고 돌우 숲풀 속으로 들어갓다. 이러고나서는 아비神의 怒가 쉬엇기때문에 災殃을 免하게 되엇다 한다.＜54＞
