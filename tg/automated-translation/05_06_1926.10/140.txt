Article Title: 故國을 보고시퍼, 徐載弼 博士의 書信에서 (Wanting to See the Homeland, From a Letter by Dr. Sŏ Chae-p'il)
Author: 徐載弼 (Sŏ Chae-p'il - as source of the letter)
Article Type: 소식 (News/Tidings)

Having received "Tonggwang" (동광) and seen your writing in it, I took great interest, thinking it seemed like a 'movement for national physical remodeling' - Reporter's Note. I believe your work is very important. Making the people's bodies strong is the first step in cultivating the strength of spirit and economy. In times like today, when one must constantly move body and mind without a moment's rest to maintain one's position, a weak body is a great obstacle.

(9 lines omitted here - original text)

I intend to write another piece and send it before I pass away, but of course, I know I won't be able to write everything I wish - if it's to be published in a magazine. (That writing has already arrived at our office and is scheduled to be published in the next issue - Reporter's Note)

(Omitted - original text)

I truly wish to set foot on my homeland's soil once before I get too old. But it seems that might be a hope I cannot fulfill. I would be very grateful if you could always keep me informed about all matters concerning Chosŏn (조선). The only way to know the true situation is through private letters. (That is all) <36>

**Summary**

This article presents excerpts from a letter written by Dr. Sŏ Chae-p'il (Philip Jaisohn) expressing his reaction to an article in the magazine "Tonggwang". He praises the idea of a movement to strengthen the Korean people's physique, deeming it crucial for spiritual and economic power in the modern era. Sŏ Chae-p'il expresses a deep longing to visit Korea before he gets too old, though he feels it's an unlikely possibility, and requests continued updates via private letters, as they are his only source for the true state of affairs in Chosŏn. For historians, the letter highlights the emphasis placed on physical strength as a component of national revival by early Korean modernizers, Sŏ's poignant personal desire to return to his homeland despite his long exile, and the reliance on private correspondence for uncensored news from colonial Korea.

故國을 보고시퍼, 徐載弼 博士의 書信에서
「동광」을 밧고 거긔 쓴 그대의 글을 보고 『민족적 육체 개조운동』인 듯 - 記者註 크게 흥미를 가지엇소. 나는 그대의 사업이 메우 중요한 것으로 아오. 국민의 육체를 튼튼케 하는 것이 정신의 힘과 경제의 힘을 기르는 첫걸음이오. 오늘 가티 한 때도 쉴새 업시 몸과 맘을 움즉이어야 자긔의 지위를 보전할 수 잇는 이 때에는 약한 육체는 큰 장애가 되는 것이요.
(此間 9행 삭제-원문)
나는 다시 한 편을 써서 돌아가시기 전에 보낼 터이나 물론 맘대로 다 쓸수는 업슬줄 아오 - 잡지에 발표되려면. (그 글은 이미 본사에 도착 되엇는데 다음호에 실릴 예정- 긔자 註)
(中略-원문)
나는 나이 넘우 만하지기 전에 꼭 한 번 고국땅을 밟고 십소. 그러나 그것은 바라지 못할 소망인가 보오. 조선에 대한 모든 사정을 늘 긔별하여 주시면 매우 감사하겟소. 진상을 아는 것은 사사론 편지 박게 업슴니다. (이만) ＜36＞
