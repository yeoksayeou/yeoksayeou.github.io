Magazine Title: 동광 제6호
Publication Date: 1926년 10월 01일
Article Title: 江西 「三墓」에서 (At the "Three Tombs" of Kangsŏ)
Author: 崔南善
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

In the middle of a plain approximately 10 *ri* west of Kangsŏ County, P'yŏngan Province, three ancient tombs from the Koguryŏ period stand like the legs of a tripod. Within these tombs, magnificent murals representing the refined techniques of the late Koguryŏ era were discovered, estimated to be works from around 1,400 years ago. Along with murals from several other ancient tombs in this vicinity, they are considered important masterpieces among the oldest extant paintings in East Asia.

Part One
Deeply buried in the earth,
Did you weep and lament?
On the day when all things revealed
And shining disappeared and were gone,
Only you, buried as if abandoned,
Remain standing alone.<52>

Part Two
Within the palace of Art,
You became its pillars,
Allowing our magnificent cauldron (鼎)
To bloom upon the world.
Oh remnant, though nothing else is left,
How could there be any deficiency?

Part Three
Lowering both eyes,
As I stand solemnly,
Sounds arise from every line (線),
Clashing together like thunder.
Does the body not tremble?
Even the spirit melts away.<53>
<52-53>

**Summary**

The text introduces the historically significant Koguryŏ-era Three Tombs in Kangsŏ, known for their ancient murals. The accompanying poem by Ch'oe Namsŏn contemplates these tombs. It laments the loss of past glories but finds resilience in the enduring presence of the tombs (Part 1), celebrates them as artistic pillars that showcase Korean brilliance to the world (Part 2), and expresses profound awe at the powerful impact of the murals (Part 3). For historians studying the colonial period, this piece is interesting as it reflects a nationalist sentiment focusing on ancient Korean cultural achievements (Koguryŏ murals) as a source of pride and enduring spirit, implicitly contrasting the grandeur of the past with the subjugated present. Ch'oe Namsŏn's engagement with ancient history served to bolster national identity during a time of external domination.

江西 「三墓」에서
						崔南善
平南 江西郡의 西方 약 10리許 평야의 中에 高句麗 시대의 古墳 3基가 鼎立하여 잇고 그 古墳의 中에서 高句麗 下葉에 精練한 技術을 대표할 훌륭한 벽화가 발견되니 대개 일천 사백년 전 경의 作으로 추정되는 것이요. 이 근처에 잇는 다른 몃 군대 고분벽화와 한 가지 현존 동양 최고회화의 중요한 일품이라 하는 것이다.

其一
흙 속에 기피 들쩨
울며 설다 햇슬랏다
들어나 빗나던 것
다 살아저 업는 날에
버린듯 파 무든 너만
남아 홀로 잇고녀.＜52＞

其二
藝術의 대궐안에
네라 잇서 발이 되어
거륵한 우리 소츨(鼎)
世界 우에 피여나니
남아야 아무것 업다
구차할 줄 잇스랴.

其三
두 눈을 나리깔고
엄숙하게 섯노라니
금(線)마다 소리잇서
우뢰가티 어울리매
몸 아니 떨리시는가
넉도 녹아 가도다. ＜53＞
＜52-53＞
