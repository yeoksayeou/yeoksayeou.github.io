Magazine Title: 동광 제6호
Publication Date: 1926년 10월 01일
Article Title: 수양동우회 노래 (Song of the Self-Cultivation Comrades Society)
Author: N/A
Article Type: 시 (Poem/Song Lyrics)

1
With sound character and firm unity,
To lay the foundation of a new Chosŏn's culture,
The sons and daughters of Chosŏn gather and unite,
How great is the principle! Our Suyang Tonguhoe.

O mountains and rivers of Chosŏn, O people of Chosŏn,
Cast off sorrow and rejoice!
Our group has risen, has risen!
To save Chosŏn, to make Chosŏn shine.

2
Let's be true, let's work, let's be faithful, let's be strong!
Pledging to offer our whole bodies and whole minds to the nation,
The sons and daughters of Chosŏn gather and unite,
How lofty is the spirit! Our Suyang Tonguhoe.

3
Virtue, body, and wisdom—these three are our great studies.
Pledging to pursue lifelong allied training,
The sons and daughters of Chosŏn gather and unite,
How long is the path of training! Our Suyang Tonguhoe.

4
The love of comrades is unchanging!
With one heart for righteousness, what task is impossible?
The sons and daughters of Chosŏn gather and unite,
How firm is the unity! Our Suyang Tonguhoe.
Tune=(Star-spangled Banner)＜3＞

**Summary**

This article presents the lyrics for the "Song of the Suyang Tonguhoe," a Korean nationalist organization founded by An Ch'angho. The song emphasizes the goals of the society: building a new Korean culture based on strong individual character (truthfulness, diligence, faithfulness, strength, virtue, physique, wisdom) and collective unity. It calls on Koreans to unite, cast off sorrow, dedicate themselves to the nation, and cultivate themselves through lifelong training and unwavering fellowship to "save Chosŏn" and "make Chosŏn shine." For historians, the song clearly reflects the nationalist aspirations and self-strengthening ideology prevalent among Korean intellectuals during the Japanese colonial period. The explicit mention of cultivating "Virtue, Body, and Wisdom" (덕 체 지) points to An Ch'angho's specific educational philosophy, and the adoption of the tune of "The Star-Spangled Banner" is a fascinating detail, perhaps suggesting an admiration for American ideals or simply using a well-known, rousing melody.

수양동우회 노래
1
건전한 인격과 공고한 단결로
새 조선 문화의 긔초를 세우려고
조선의 아들 딸 모이어 오아 뭉치니
주의도 클시고 우리 수양동우회

조선의 강산아 조선의 사람아
슬픔을 버리고 기쁘어 할찌어다
우리 무리 일어나았네 일어 나았내
조선을 살리려 조선을 빛내려

2
참되자 일하자 믿브자 힘있자
왼 몸과 왼 마음 나라에 받히자고
조선의 아들 딸 모이어 오아 뭉치니
정신도 높고나 우리 수양동우회

3
덕 체 지 세 가지 우리 큰 공부를
맹서ㅎ고 일생에 동맹 수련 하자고
조선의 아들 딸 모이어 오아 뭉치니
수련도 길도다 우리 수양동우회

4
동지의 사랑은 변함이 없구나
의 위한 일심이 못할 일 무엇이냐
조선의 아들 딸 모이어 오아 뭉치니
단결도 굳어라 우리 수양동우회
곡조=(Star-spangled Banner)＜3＞
＜3＞
