**Body Text Translation:**
Suspension
						By Chŏng Pyŏng-sun

Today is the tenth of this month!
Even after finishing his morning meal, Ilho, his school start time having arrived, stood blankly on the wooden floor because he didn't have his monthly tuition fee.
“Tomorrow is the deadline. If you don’t pay by tomorrow, you won’t be able to study and will be kicked out!”
The angry voice of Teacher XX, who had made the announcement during yesterday morning's assembly, still hadn't faded from his ears. More than that, for Ilho, his eyes were terrifying. Those eyes, glaring with pitch-black pupils opened wide as if to say, "You brat!" Even now, more than feeling sorry for not having the tuition money, the shuddering fear of those eyes made it impossible for him to leave.
His mother, carrying out the dining table, saw her son still standing there. Setting the table down on the wooden floor, she glared at him for a moment, quite displeased.
“You brat, you ate your meal, why aren't you going to school, just standing there like that?” she snapped.
Her pale, copper-colored eyes, set wide on her sparrow-egg-like, freckled cheeks, clearly showed her quick temper.
These mother's eyes, like Teacher XX's, had the power to press down on others, but Ilho wasn't that afraid of his mother's eyes. No matter how much she threatened, he wouldn't even blink, and if she occasionally tried to lay a hand on him, he had a habit of flinching and resisting like a praying mantis in early autumn, as if asking, "Would I lose to my own mother?" This time too, deeply displeased by his mother's words, he stuck out his lip about a handspan and remained silent.
“Are you still going to stand there like that?”
His mother shouted once more, as if finding him infuriating.
“Give me the tuition money. Then I’ll go to school.”
The son shot back bluntly.<61>
“The tuition money, well, tell them you’ll bring it after your father comes.”
“Always ‘after father comes.’ Have we ever given it earlier than others, even once?”
“Is it because I have it and won’t give it?”
“I don’t know. Give me the money so I can go to school.”
“Has this child lost his mind or what? Where am I supposed to get money I don’t have? Why this fuss every day?”
His mother, her anger flaring up, clenched her fists tightly as if about to strike her son right then and there.
As his mother moved menacingly, the son stepped back a couple of paces.
The son suddenly grabbed his book bundle, threw it down, and turned away.
“The teacher said not to come to school without the money. Why pick on only me? What sin have I committed…” he said, covering his face with his hands. His voice was already half-sobbing.
“You wretched child, whose manners are these? Where are you throwing your book bundle? Isn't the teacher human too? Can't you just plead with him to wait three or four days?”
The mother, to preserve her maternal dignity, hadn't yet calmed her anger, but her spirit had already deflated considerably.
— Ilho's words are perfectly reasonable! What sin is his? It's my sin for not being able to provide the money!
The mother deeply regretted making her innocent child cry unnecessarily. Now, feeling ashamed herself, she spoke in a gentle tone,
“Honey—. Don’t cry. Your father is definitely coming in three days, they say. I’ll surely give it then. Don’t cry, hurry and go to school and come back.”
Ilho, the more he was comforted, the sadder he became, sniffling and sobbing intermittently. His mother, too, seemed to be barely holding back tears welling up in her eyes.
“What on earth are they fussing about day and night with that child in that house?”
The voice of the landlady from the inner room could be heard muttering.
From the side of the lettuce patch, a hen clucked, calling her chicks. A couple of tiny chicks scurried away.<62>

In truth, there was no way Ilho's father could return in three days, and even if he did, there was no chance of getting money. It had already been three months since he left his wife and child in this nostril-sized room rented for two won and fifty jeon, and went to Pusan to work as a laborer. According to a friend who went with him, heard about half a month ago, he couldn't find a job right away in Pusan either and spent his days sighing, eventually having no choice but to work as a servant at some inn. The one won his husband supposedly sent was received then, used to buy a measure of barley, and they had been getting by on two meals a day since. From tomorrow morning, there wouldn't be a single grain of rice or a piece of firewood left, so how could there be any thought of educating her son? However, being a woman's heart, she thought that if he studied even one more day, some way might appear, so even without the tuition money, her intention was to endure grimly until he was kicked out. Because of this tuition money, conflict had arisen between mother and son for nearly four or five days. The reason Ilho's mother had vented her anger and complained to Ilho earlier was also due to her own frustration. When she finally saw her son crying pitifully, how could a mother's heart feel otherwise? She couldn't help but feel her bones ache and her nose sting.

Twenty minutes had already passed since the bell for the upper grades rang at OO School.
Ilho's small figure, wearing a black student cap and carrying a blue book bundle, appeared in front of the second-grade classroom. He had run, thinking he might be late, but as soon as he entered the school gate, thoughts of School! Tuition money! Teacher XX’s eyes! made his heart pound continuously, like a criminal appearing in court. What should he do? Should he turn back? He wanted to leave again.
In the first-grade classroom, students were collectively repeating after the teacher, “Ka-kya kŏ-kyŏ,” reciting the letters. The buzzing sound from all around was like being near a beehive.
From the classroom over there, a loud cough sounded, and it seemed someone was looking out.
Almost instinctively, Ilho flung open the classroom door and stepped inside. As if someone had pushed him from behind.
All the students' gazes turned towards this newcomer. Teacher XX, who had been writing something <63> on the blackboard, looked this way, chalk still in hand.
He glared at Ilho, his peculiar eyes wide open. Ilho stood with his head bowed low, awaiting his fate as if pleading to be put out of his misery.
“Did you bring the tuition money today!”
His words, snapping off sharply at the end, were like nothing less than a death sentence.
Ilho’s heart sank. In a voice as faint as a mosquito's buzz,
“Um, my mother said in three days…” Before he could even finish speaking, the teacher, as if he understood perfectly and needed to hear no more, commanded,
“What do you mean, three days? No! No! Get out!”
His anger flared as he shouted the order, gesturing towards the outside with his stick.
Glancing up quickly, Ilho saw a few students looking at him and smirking foolishly.
Ilho, feeling sorrowful, indignant, and embarrassed, didn't even know how he rushed out of that spot. Anyway, he was standing in the schoolyard in front of the office.
Outside the main gate, another student ran up, fists clenched, panting heavily. He was also a second-grader.
His face beaming, he glanced sideways at Ilho and asked,
“Why are you standing there? Didn’t they give you tuition money at home?”
Then he bounded into the entrance hall with thudding steps. In his hand was paper money.
Ilho stood there blankly, lost in thought. Like a fool.
Something like a hazy fog seemed to shimmer palely across the whole world, while the warm sunlight gently kissed the earth. On the far side of the schoolyard, a field of bright green barley lay spread out.
From afar, a train whistle blew— *ttee*— and a train came rushing along, puffing out clouds of black smoke, *poo-pa-poo-pa*.
The dismissal bell rang.
Who might have seen Ilho's pitiful figure as he walked listlessly out of the school gate?—(The End)—
Morning, April 2nd, 1926 <64>

**Summary**
This short story depicts a day in the life of a young boy named Ilho who faces expulsion from school because his family cannot afford the monthly tuition fee. His mother, struggling with poverty after his father left for Pusan seeking work, cannot provide the money. Ilho endures his mother's frustration and the humiliation of being scolded and sent away by his teacher in front of his classmates. The story ends with Ilho leaving the school grounds after the dismissal bell, his education abruptly halted due to his family's financial hardship. For historians, the story offers a poignant glimpse into the economic struggles faced by ordinary Koreans under Japanese colonial rule, particularly the difficulties in accessing education due to poverty. The portrayal of the teacher's harshness and the family's desperate situation highlights the social pressures and lack of opportunity prevalent during that period.

그는 노긔 충천하여 호령하면서 막대로 박글 가리치는 모양이다.
눈껼에 얼른 치어다보니 멧멧 학생이 자긔를 보고 싱겁게도 힛죽 웃는 듯 하다.
일호는 설고 분하고 점적한 김에 자긔도 어더케 그 자리에서 뛰어 나왓는지 모르겟다. 하여간 그는 사무실 압 운동장에 서 잇섯다.
대문 박게서 학생 하나가 주먹위을 불끈 쥐고 숨을 헐헐덕덕 쉬면서 뛰어 온다. 그도 이학년 학생이다.
그는 희색이 만면해서 일호를 슬쩍 치어다 보면서
『너는 웨 거긔 섯니? 집에서 월사금 안 주던?』
하더니 현관으로 쿵쿵쿵 뛰어들어간다. 그의 손에는 지전ㅅ장이 들이어 잇섯다.
일호는 정신업시 머-ㅇ하니 서 잇다. 마치 등신갓다.
왼 누리에 무슨 안개 가튼 것이 보야케 까물까물하는데 따뜻한 해ㅅ발이 살근살근 대지를 입마추고 잇다. 운동장 저쪽에는 샛팔안 보리 바티 즐편하게 깔리어 잇다.
멀리서 긔적 소리가 뛰-하며 긔차가 검은 연긔를 물큰물큰 내 뿜으며 푸파푸파 달아 온다.
하학종이 울엇다.
맥 업시 교문을 나가는 일호의 애처럽은 모양을 누구가 보앗으리라.-(完)-
