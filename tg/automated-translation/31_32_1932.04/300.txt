**Body Text Translation**

New Year's Eve Serenade and 13 Other Pieces
						Kim Tong-myŏng

New Year's Eve Serenade

"Time" is a river,
Its flow is such, its speed is such.
But its destination is the sea of "forgetfulness" (이즘),
So I gather pieces of my song to make a boat,
Moor it on the riverbank,
And load all sorrows, all regrets—can even dreams remain?
After loading everything,
With a heavy heart (愁心) as extra cargo,
I send it floating midstream.
Ah, my boat, may you go in peace.

Moon

Awakening at the third watch (三更),
Through a crack in the torn paper door,
A white face gazes at me.
Delighted, I sit up
And gaze back with all my heart,
And that face shines even brighter.
Hoping against hope,
I briefly strain my ears,
But have those lips, sealed for eternity (千古),
Forgotten how to part?

Cup

My dear,
Must I drink this cup again?
The cup you gave me yesterday, its color was blue,
But today's, why is it so red?
No matter, bring out all the wine you have,
Let me taste every kind.<115>
Life is but a moment,
How long can drunkenness last?

Hardship on the Road (行路難)

How can I go on this road?
On the eighty-ri path through sandy fields,
My body, weary as can be,
Barely made it through half a day
On a muddy path where harsh rain comes and goes.
Oh, look at this!
A mountain pass as high as the sky
Stands tall, blocking the way.

Ah, how can I cross it, this pass?
My shoes are already worn out,
My feet are blistered too.
But the road ahead
Grows ever more rugged.
How many ri are left ahead?
Difficult is this road,
How can I go on?
When will I ever arrive?

If the World Were the Sea

If the world were the sea,
Life is rowing a boat.
Then, are all hardships (苦難)
Raging waves (怒濤)? Or dense fog (雲霧)?
No matter, love is the lighthouse (燈臺) beam.
If only love exists,
Even if the dark night comes,
There will be nothing to fear.

I Too Will Seek

Long ago, a certain person
Saw the universe even in a grain of sand,
And another person
Found great truth (眞理) even in a falling apple.
Now before me,
A storm (暴風雨) of misfortune (不幸) rages.
Surely, even within this,
There must be something precious.
That's right, I too will seek,
Rolling up both my sleeves.<116>

I Will Go My Own Way

People's appearances,
Eyes are different, noses are different.
Their natures are also thus,
And language, actions, beliefs, thoughts,
All are thus;
How could their paths be the same?
Hey there, friends,
Don't meddle in others' affairs,
Just hurry along the path you must take.
Whether the world is this way or that way,
I will go my own way.

To a Certain Friend

Friend, let us forget and move on together.
If we understand what life is,
There is no need for me to blame you,
Nor for you to resent me.

In any case, my fault was trusting friendship (友情) too much,
And your fault was taking friendship too lightly.
But friendship is simple and life is complex;
What is the use of asking now who was right or wrong?

Friend, let us forget and move on together.
But don't feel sad tomorrow
Thinking I cannot give you my old heart.
Such is life; what can I do?

Sitting Before the Mirror

Looking at my face reflected in the mirror,
When did another fine line appear on my forehead?
Thinking it's the vanguard of approaching old age,
I feel endlessly sad, as if I've lost a precious treasure.
But looking back at the growing children,
I wish to grow old quickly.

Life is Fleeting (人生은 暫間이니)

Life is fleeting; is it like a fire in the fields?
If so, then all sorrows and pains, also joys and pleasures,
Are ultimately fleeting moments within the fleeting,
Ah, my dear one, is there any need to distinguish between misfortune (不遇) and success (榮達)?<117>

Lament (탄식)

Oh, my lady (따님),
You are white as snow.
And you are also cold as snow.
But if I had a love as warm as the June sun,
No matter how cold your heart may be,
Could it possibly not melt?
But alas, my youth is now more than half withered.
Since the love blooming upon it cannot help but wither alone,
Oh, my lady, I spend my youth upon cooling passion (情慹)
And gaze upon your face, white as snow.

Twilight (黃昏)

Twilight. Like a bride,
Fluttering shining coattails,
Following the fragrant gentle breeze,
When its footsteps, drunk on peace and dreams,
Quietly arrive,
The sea sings softly to welcome it,
And the islands, mountains, and also the rocks offer silent prayers (黙禱).
At this time, I too bow my head before it for a moment,
Participating in the solemn worship (禮拜).

Thinking of a Friend

Separated by distant waters, distant clouds,
Already one year and half a year have passed.
Why is there no news?
Are you angry that I don't write often?
That couldn't be the reason,
Or perhaps you've made new friends
And your heart is devoted there?
But old affection shouldn't be discarded.
Ah, whatever the reason, I pray only that you are not ill.

Lament of Wounded Wings (傷한 날개의 탄식)

Drying wet wings in mother's embrace,
Nurturing young wings in mother's love,
One fine morning, with good wings, I set off on the long road to Kangnam (江南).

Flying freely high in the mid-sky (半空中), heading south,
Crossing a river in one flight, crossing a mountain in another,
The 90,000-ri high sky, being far, is even more welcome.<118>

Wind, enter my embrace, my body feels lighter!
Cloud, stay there, let's cross that peak together!
Flapping wings gracefully, the path already traversed is 1,000 ri.

Passing small fields, there is another rugged mountain (뫼).
Crossing it in one breath, there is an even higher mountain there.
Just then, rain clouds, like frost upon my eyes?

Putting all strength into my body to soar into the mid-heaven,
Alas! My wings are soaked by the cruel rain.
Heavy as a thousand, ten thousand catties, how can I fly over this mountain?

Beating the wind with wet wings, how can I spread my pinions?
Though my spirit alone surges towards the sky (天空),
Does my body fall to the ground like a fallen leaf?

Folding wounded wings on a broken branch,
Looking back, the road ahead is vast, ten thousand ri.
When will these wings heal to fly the remaining path?<119>
<115-119>

**Summary**

This collection of 14 poems by Kim Tong-myŏng explores themes of the passage of time, the hardships and fleeting nature of life, sorrow, regret, love, friendship, aging, individuality, and perseverance. The poems often employ natural imagery (river, moon, sea, storm, twilight, wings) and express personal, lyrical, and frequently melancholic sentiments. From a historical perspective, these poems, published in 1932 during the Japanese colonial period, focus primarily on individual emotional landscapes and existential reflections rather than overt political resistance or social commentary. This inward turn might reflect a common response among artists seeking expression in personal themes amidst external political constraints, making the work interesting to historians studying the cultural and psychological atmosphere of colonial Korea. The final poem, "Lament of Wounded Wings," while personal, uses allegory (a difficult journey south, setback by rain) that could potentially be interpreted as reflecting broader national or personal struggles under colonialism, though it remains ambiguous.
