Musings of a Dreamer
						Sangha

There is a rivalry between materialism and idealism. One is a son, the other a daughter, of theory attempting to explain the fundamental reality of the universe.
The bourgeois class and the proletarian class stand in opposition. One is a son, the other a daughter, of the era's process organizing the current state of society.
Emotion and reason wrestle. They must be a son and a daughter of the contradiction-that-is-not-a-contradiction weaving the mystery of human psychology.

Nietzsche says,
"I am one who leaps from mountain to mountain..." This is thoroughgoing individualism, the highest possible form of aristocratism.
The words of Jesus, "Knock, and it shall be opened unto you..." are said to be thoroughgoing populism, the highest possible form of philanthropy.
But Nietzsche also grieved over human suffering, and Jesus also grieved over human suffering.
No one can stop the setting moon, except perhaps the sleep-talking emperor Qin Shi Huang... but they say Qin Shi Huang also died.
There is no living person who cannot eat, except perhaps Old Man Pak who starved to death... but they say Old Man Pak also died – because starving to death is also dying.

A man in formal attire rides in an automobile. His solemn demeanor is the epitome of a gentleman. It's said that that night, he got drunk, struck a Kisaeng (妓生), and had to pay 350 Won for the Kisaeng's medical treatment for three weeks.
A swarthy man appears at the night market every night. The way he peers into this shop and that, no matter how sympathetic you are, marks him as a thief. That night, he returns home and lays out goods before the eight eyes of his old mother, wife, son, and daughter:
Apples
Sweet potatoes
A shirt
A hat
Soybean candy
Chestnut sweets
As a reward for taking others' things without paying, he gets to go to the "villa" (別莊 - prison) for six months.
This society maintains the dignity of a gentleman for 350 Won, yet sends the main pillar of a household to rot in prison for merely 3 Won 50 Chŏn. <91>

When Germany was still an Empire, during the final battles with the Allied forces, how many hundreds, how many thousands of beautiful souls died in battle daily for the laws of the state (do you say for the country?)? It was on that very Western Front reported as "no change." If death in battle is also homicide, who committed this murder? The very murderer who committed this murder judged robbers. Judged adulterers. And oh! Did not the murderer send murderers to the execution block (斷頭臺)?

Kaptori and Ŭlpoki are fighting in a secluded alley. The neighborhood kids, discovering this, issue verbal "extras" (號外) to Kaptori's house, to Ŭlpoki's house...
Kaptori's father hits Ŭlpoki's father. Ŭlpoki's father kicks Kaptori's father. This again gets reported in an interesting extra, spreading from this neighborhood to that...
Kaptori's friends. Kaptori's father's friends. Ŭlpoki's friends. Ŭlpoki's father's friends. Friends of no one, with nothing better to do. Thus, the secluded alley becomes, literally, a mountain and sea of people (虫山虫海).
Kaptori:
"What's our fight got to do with them!"
Ŭlpoki:
"What's our fight got to do with them!"
Neighborhood friends:
"What do you mean, what's it got to do with us! You just keep fighting without listening to us trying to stop you! You wicked kids"... Such comical extras are repeatedly distributed. Oh! The childish (小兒病的) struggles in these restless days! Oh! The World War that is said to be coming!

If Edison hadn't invented electricity, X wouldn't be stealing electricity every night. If humans hadn't discovered society, would the great thieves trying to steal society have been anything more than thieves who steal pot lids (솟둑겅) or sacks of rice? Just for the single reason that society was carelessly created (맨둘엇다), how numerous are the thieves who prey on society? Thieves who prey on capitalist society, thieves who prey on bureaucratic society (官僚社會), and among them, the cute thieves are the lumpen (룸펜) beggars running an obstacle course through the employment society network. If the witty Proudhon were alive today, wouldn't he erase his words "What is property? It is theft!" and instead say, "What is society! It is arson-robbery (火賊)?"

The night of the XX-sponsored Grand Concert (音樂大演奏會).
Like a shaman gaining supernatural power (神通力) and grasping the ritual pole, the musical notes jumped sideways and flew diagonally. Beethoven danced on the piano, and Wagner conducted the XX Orchestra (管絃隊). The grand bourgeoisie and petit bourgeoisie, who suffer no direct blow to their livelihood even after buying 2-Won white tickets and 1-Won blue tickets, unable to forget this one night's pleasure (享樂), bow their heads, prop up their chins, close their eyes, gaze at the ceiling, and sink gently, gently into so-called mystical ecstasy (陶醉). A female tenor in mourning clothes, as if representing all the world's vocalists, presses her hand to her small chest, ready to sing "Jocelyn's Lullaby" (죠스란의 子守歌) for the encore, while singing "Drigo's Serenade" (도라고의 쎄레나드). This person and that person in the audience weave fantasies of a mirage (蜃氣樓) that would vanish with a puff, where celestial maidens (仙女) dance and angels sing from heaven.
This very night. On this very land.
Our Old Man Hwang (黃령감), with his daily wage (日給) of 70 Chŏn, buys foxtail millet and firewood. That should be enough, but he also buys one head of *piut* fish (비웃) as an extra treat, returns not to a public hall but to the servants' quarters (行廊房), and says:
"Mother, eat plenty."
"You have some too, dear."
"Big Tong, little Suna, eat slowly. *Piut* has many bones."
A bowl of mixed-grain rice. One head of *piut*. A medium bowl of kimchi. Two bowls of scorched-rice water. After this sacred meal (聖餐), like Holy Communion, this dinner party (晩餐會) ends, and packed tightly like bean sprouts, they drift off to sleep.
From here a *poong*, from there a *poong* - what is commonly called farting (放氣), actually an impromptu poem (卽興詩) symbolizing health, serves as the overture. This night, in this room too, not a bourgeois concert but a proletarian concert, not a concert selling 2-Won white tickets and 1-Won blue tickets, but a domestic concert is held.
*Kool kool kool* – the sleeping song (就寢曲) preparing for tomorrow's battle is performed by the baritone nose of Old Man Hwang.
*Tchul tchul tchul tchol tchol tchol* – such accompaniment (伴奏) is handled by the small intestine (小腸) and large intestine (大腸).
*P'alttakung p'alttakung* – when the bright red heart beats the small drum (小鼓), *tŏktŏktŏk tŏktŏktŏk* – the hands scratching bedbugs keep the rhythm (拍子).
Even after the bourgeois concert ends by midnight <92>, the proletarian concert continues. Oh, enduring proletarian concert held every night, may you receive many blessings!

The military advance bugle (進軍喇叭) flows sideways past the fields of XX. Entrusted (付托) with the command to love thy neighbor (隣人), man and yet another man, born in the very image (形相) of God, form an endlessly long parade of murderers (殺人囚). Does he not know that when he draws his sword and stabs another man born in the very image of God, and after the moment passes when that slain man's old mother, young wife, and little children are weeping, his own old mother, young wife, and little children will have to weep, clutching his memory (追憶)?
For the State!
For the Nation!
Having vaguely believed in such orthodox (正統的), excessively orthodox morality, by the time he becomes a philosopher who discovers the new truth (眞理) that "XX is not for us," it will be after he discovers that his soul, his flesh—so dearly valued by his mother, wife, and little children—lies fallen diagonally into a cannon's hole.
Why would being for the state be wrong?
Why would being for the nation be wrong?
If, that is, that state had been for him, and that nation had been for him.

There is the profession of liquor seller. It seems like a newly emerged profession. It probably isn't an occupation passed down from antiquity like the *piut* seller or tofu seller. Because it hasn't been long since water became enslaved to private property (私有財産).
I don't know if there's a poem about the priceless sun and the ownerless air, but setting aside (姑捨) everything else, the sun and air have been believed to be fair common property (共有財産). However, recently, we can prove by comparing the complexion of a young gentleman on summer vacation with that of a fabric store (布木店) clerk that the sun and air too seem to have awakened to class consciousness (階級意識) and are withholding fair distribution (分與). As unemployment is so severe these days, it seems the sun has ideas and the air also has ideas, wishing to bestow (寄贈) upon this society new professions that make commodities of themselves. Namely:
Sun seller.
Air seller.
However, I know, though I wish I didn't, that the time when sun sellers and air sellers emerge is the time when the entire past world falls into ruin (沒落).

He is a philosopher (哲學家). He is a poet. But he is a beggar rummaging through trash cans. His philosophy is thus. His poetry is also thus.
The Creator (造物主) erred and made the world, and humans erred and made Lord Money. If even imperfect humans have senses (感覺), emotions (感情), and also will (意志), why would the Almighty Lord Money lack senses? Lack emotions? Would he lack will? Thus, Master Money, possessing keen hearing (聽覺) and delicate (纖細) emotions, already broke off relations (絶交) long ago with me, who used to curse him as 'money-bastard, money-bastard'. Born by mistake into a world made by mistake, he mistakenly possesses a mouth, mistakenly possesses a stomach; not eating, he gets hungry; being hungry, he cannot live, so just like a medicine-selling poor student seeking out some Korean millionaire, he visited His Excellency (閣下) Money's gate yesterday, the day before, and today too, countless times, carrying 100 percent failure.
Isn't there the price of rice?
Isn't there also the price of firewood?
And oh! The things displayed in the pub's "show window."
Ribs
Mung-bean pancakes
Sweet rice cakes
Fish
Pork
Pork
But all these are slaves of money. Slaves of the money-bastard who is my irreconcilable enemy (不俱戴天의 怨讐). My eyes spin. The earth and sky dance dizzily.
From within his dimming mind, he barely manages to pull out the consciousness,
"Still, I am a man," and with his last ounce of energy, he curses.
"Ack! You heartless money-bastard—" <93>

Here is a slave who hates to work.
Here is a tiny rebel (反逆兒) trying to destroy (破壞) the great truth (眞理) of the universe that "he who does not work shall not eat" (though this truth is often applied only to the proletariat).
Here is a miserable failure (敗慘者) of life who spent his irreplaceable youth thoughtlessly for their work, not his.
Here is a laborer (勞働夫) weary (싫증난) of work, who only knew aging as something else, now panting through the latter half of life where wrinkles appear and white hair increases.
Just as a maiden cherishes and protects her virginity (處女性) for that day, that night, he now sees that he had prepared his youth—whether he harbored Napoleon's grand ambitions (雄圖), Lenin's revolution, or dreamed of the human love of Sakyamuni (釋迦) and Jesus—for them, his enemies (怨讐).
Could exploitation (搾取) exist within the category of sanctity? Could oppression (壓迫) exist? Could unfairness (不公平) exist? If his labor (勞働), which encompasses (包含) all this injustice (exploitation, oppression, unfairness) (for them), is also labor... based on this logic, he ended up denying (否認) the old saying "Labor is sacred."
If "He who does not work, shall not eat" is the truth, how can the existence (存生) of those guys who live without working be proven? Does it mean those guys live without eating?
If "Only those who work can eat" is a plausible principle, how can the fact of us, who work yet cannot eat, be proven?
Thus, he doubts all the old truths. However, even for him, who must doubt everything, the following one fact was a truth transcending doubt (懷疑):
"I am hungry. Therefore I am."
(Postscript) (附記)
These are excerpts from daily records. What unity (統一) could there be? <94>

**Summary**

This collection of vignettes by "Sangha" presents a biting critique of social and economic inequalities in 1930s Korea. Through philosophical musings and contrasting anecdotes, the author highlights the hypocrisy of societal values, the vast gulf between the bourgeoisie and the proletariat, the injustice faced by the poor, the dehumanizing nature of war fought for abstract ideals, and the commodification of basic necessities. The piece questions established truths about labor, property, and societal structures, culminating in a declaration of existence rooted in physical hunger rather than abstract thought. For historians, this article offers valuable insights into social consciousness and intellectual dissent during the Japanese colonial period. It vividly portrays class disparity, critiques the contradictions of capitalism and state power from a Korean perspective, and reflects a growing skepticism towards traditional morality and established institutions, hinting at emergent radical thought.

空想家의 隨筆
						想何
唯物論과 唯心論의 相兢이 잇다. 宇宙의 根本實在를 解釋하려는 理論의 한 아들이요 理論의 한 딸이다.
부르주아階級과 푸로레階級이 대립하고 잇다. 社會의 如是相을 組織하고 잇는 時代過程의 한 아들이요 時代過程의 한 딸이다.
感情과 理性이 씨름한다. 人間心理의 神秘性을 질삼하는 矛盾아닌 矛盾의 한 아들이요 한 딸일 것이다.

니체는
「나는 山에서 山으로 뛰어 넘는 사람...」이라고 한다. 철저한 個人主義요 以上 더 잇을 수 없는 貴族主義다.
「두드리라 열어 주실 것이오...」 한 예수의 말은 철저한 民衆主義요 以上 더 잇을 수 없는 博愛主義라 한다.
그러나 니체도 사람의 괴롬을 괴로워 하엿고 예수도 사람의 괴롬을 괴로워 하엿섯다.
넘어가는 달은 못 넘어가게 할 사람이 없다. 잠고대軍인 秦始皇 밖에야... 그런데 秦始皇도 죽엇다 한다.
살아잇는 사람으로 먹지 못할 사람이 없다. 굼주려 죽엇다는 朴첨지 밖에야... 그런데 朴첨지도 죽엇다 한다. -굼주려 죽은 것도 죽은 것이기 때문이다.

禮服 입은 사나이가 자동차 타고 간다. 嚴謹한 태도가 紳士의 典型이다. 그가 그 밤에 술먹고 妓生처서 妓生 3週日間 治療費 一金 350圓也를 물엇다 한다.
식검은 사나이가 밤마다 夜市에 나타난다. 이 가게 저 가게를 끼웃거리는 樣이 아모리 同情하여도 盜賊놈이다. 그가 그 밤 그 집에 돌아와 老母 안해 아들 딸 여덟 개 눈초리 앞에 내여논 物品
사과
고구마
샤쓰
帽子
콩사탕
밤과자
남의 것을 돈 안주고 가젓다는 賞으로 그는 6개월 동안 別莊으로 가게 된다.
350圓이면 한 紳士의 體面을 維持하여 주는 이 社會면서도 不過 3圓 50錢이 한 집의 큰 기둥을 監獄에 보내 썩히는 이 社會다.＜91＞

獨逸이 아직 帝國이엇을 때 聯合軍과 마지막 싸움을 싸울 때 하로에 몇백 몇천 아름다운 魂들이 國法을 위해 (나라를 위햇다고 하느냐?) 戰死햇다. 異狀없엇다는 바로 그 西部戰線서다. 戰死도 他殺이면 이 殺人罪는 누가 犯하엿느냐? 이 殺人罪를 犯한 바로 그 殺人犯이 强盜를 審判하엿다. 淫行을 審判하엿다. 그리고 오! 殺人犯이 殺人犯을 斷頭臺에 올리지 않엇나?

甲乭이와 乙福이가 으슥한 골목서 싸움 싸운다. 이것을 探知한 洞里 아이들이 입으로 號外를 發行하며 甲乭이 집에 乙乭이 집에...
甲乭이 아버지가 乙福아범을 친다. 乙福아범이 甲乭아범을 찬다. 이것이 다시금 興味잇는 號外에 실려 이 洞里서 저 洞里로...
甲乭이 親舊. 甲乭아범의 親舊. 乙福이 친구. 乙福아범의 친구. 아모의 親舊도 아닌 일없는 親舊. 이러하야 으슥한 골목쟁이는 말 그대로의 虫山虫海.
甲乭
「우리 싸움에 相關이 뭐람!」
乙福
「우리 싸움에 相關이 뭐람!」
