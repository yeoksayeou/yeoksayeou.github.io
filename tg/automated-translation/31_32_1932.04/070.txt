Magazine Title: 동광 제32호
Publication Date: 1932년 04월 01일 (April 1, 1932)
Article Title: 受難의 中國은 어디로, 破産? 再建? (Where is Suffering China Headed, Bankruptcy? Reconstruction?)
Author: 李晶燮, 許憲, 宋鎭禹, 元世勳, 高永煥, 咸尙勳, 金章煥 (Yi Chŏng-sŏp, Hŏ Hŏn, Song Chin-u, Wŏn Se-hun, Ko Yŏng-hwan, Ham Sang-hun, Kim Chang-hwan)
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Discussion)

Where is Suffering China Headed, Bankruptcy? Reconstruction?
What are the impacts of the Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents?
How will foreign policy unfold?
The Kuomintang's dictatorship?
The feasibility of international administration?

[Image Caption] Sculpture commemorating Sun Yat-sen, the Father of the Chinese Nation, owned by the Paris branch of the Kuomintang.

Presently, China is once again facing an unprecedented period of chaos. Externally, difficult problems arise one after another, and internally, disputes do not cease. Is not the path of the 400 million masses the path of the general trend in the East, and the path to world peace? It is also a fact that the movements of the world powers, burdened by unprecedented economic depression, are revolving like a whirlwind, particularly centered on China's future. Where is China truly headed? <20>

1.  What are the effects of the Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents on China?
2.  Will China's foreign policy lean towards pro-American, pro-British, pro-Russian, or pro-Japanese?
3.  Will the Kuomintang's rule continue? Or will it become the era of the Nationalist Youth Party or the Communist Party?
4.  If things go wrong, might it face the fate of international administration or partition?

Regarding these issues, we intend to hold a discussion within the scope of freedom available to us. The answers of various gentlemen to the four questions above are published here. (In order of manuscript arrival)

Emphasis on the National Unity Movement
Yi Chŏng-sŏp

1.  The recent Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents will have an enormous impact on China's path forward. Firstly, looking at it spiritually, it seems the national sentiment of the Chinese people has received a more intense stimulation (刺戟) than in any previous historical period. This is not to say that this incident is the most significant among all incidents in Chinese history, but rather because it is a major incident since the introduction of new culture, or since the realization of a unified government, and because the incident unfolded even in Shanghai, which holds a position dominating the entire Yangtze River basin, indeed all of China. Having received such intense stimulation of national sentiment, the Chinese people will likely engage in significant self-reflection regarding the kind of fratricidal (骨肉的) internal strife seen in past political movements. A movement for national unity will naturally be emphasized.
    Since the Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents, not only Japan's liberal newspapers but even the Social Masses Party have thoroughly embraced national essentialism (國粹化), and Japan as a whole has leaned further to the right. Therefore, I speculate that China's future political movement will also lean further to the right.
    Also, due to this incident, the Chinese people will likely try to discover considerable truth in the view of the state famously held by the German government-sponsored scholar (御用學者) Treitschke: "The state is power" (國家는 力, Der Staat ist Macht).

Pro-Americanism is a Given Fact

2.  It is expected that they will decisively adopt a pro-American policy. In this incident, <21> although the United States took no active measures for China, it is undeniable that they made considerable sympathetic efforts. It is clear that the United States, a financial imperialist power, will adopt a pro-China stance as much as possible in the future, both for its economic advance into the East and in consideration of a potential emergency with [Censored: likely Japan], while China, besides political unification, needs industrial development above all, requiring the import of American capital. Also, for foreign policy, relying on the United States, which holds the greatest influence in the international political situation, is very advantageous, so they will try to pursue friendly relations with the United States as much as possible.
    A pro-British policy is unlikely to be easily realized. The reason China adopted a somewhat pro-British policy during the recent incident was firstly as a reaction against Japan, and secondly to try to develop the various circumstances within the League of Nations advantageously for itself through Britain. This was a temporary policy. Britain also tried to gain the fisherman's profit through the skillful Minister Lampson by taking advantage of the Sino-Japanese conflict. However, once the incident reaches a conclusion, China's movement to recover its rights and interests from Britain will reignite, so the future of Sino-British relations is observed to be likely not smooth.
    In this incident, Russia maintained an observer's attitude, consistently exercising self-restraint and quiet observation. Therefore, there is no reason for China to suddenly adopt a pro-Russian policy. However, if Worker-Peasant Russia's famous industrial plan steadily progresses and it gains international economic power, there might be a prospect of adopting a pro-Russian policy, but isn't this unrelated to the current incident?
    A pro-Japanese policy? One can only say it is hopeless. Unless Sino-Japanese relations are restored to the state before September 18th last year (Statu quo ante bellum), considering the current situation, this is entirely hopeless, and talk of a pro-Japanese policy for China is out of the question.

Chiang Kai-shek's Rule Continues <22>

3.  The Kuomintang government will continue as before. Currently, apart from Chiang Kai-shek, there seems to be no figure capable of controlling and bringing order to China. As long as Chiang Kai-shek holds political power, China will continue to be governed by the Kuomintang government. A Communist Party era? Unthinkable. If a Communist government were realized in China, it would have to be when Russia's power reached a level capable of fighting against the various capitalist nations like the US, Britain, Japan, and France. Otherwise, if a Communist government were realized in China, wouldn't it be annihilated by the capitalist countries like Japan, Britain, and the US, which have the most significant interests in China?

4.  China will neither be internationally administered nor is there any chance of partition. There is currently no basis (건데기) for such an outcome. (March 11th)

Cooperation with the United States?
Hŏ Hŏn

1.  The recent Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents, internally, will inevitably lead to the realization of a dictatorial government centered around warlords as a temporary measure to deal with the extraordinary wartime and post-war situation. Externally, the most dangerous and anachronistic secret diplomacy will actively and furtively flourish.

2.  China's future foreign policy, predicated on the traditional strategy of "using barbarians to control barbarians" (以夷制夷策), will cooperate with the United States, which is as clear as day (明若觀火). Moreover, China has recently been striving through the deliberative body of the League of Nations, the so-called "court of public opinion" (輿論의 法廷), to enable the enforcement of the economic sanctions stipulated in Article 16 of the League Covenant (規約). Even if the League of Nations intended to enforce this, if the United States, possessing the world's greatest economic power, remains indifferent, the implementation of Article 16 would be entirely impossible. However, the fact that the United States, which has not joined the League of Nations, is cooperating with the League regarding the China issue with a more proactive attitude than League members suggests that some secret agreement may already exist between the US and China.

3.  As long as the Chinese Kuomintang advocates for "governing the state by the party" (以黨治國), the party should be above the government and guide it. However, dictatorial politicians centered around warlords will first diminish party authority (黨權) and expand government power, effectively placing the party under the government. Simultaneously, the mass movement, which has been the lifeblood of the party, will be completely abolished, leaving not even its form intact, thus eliminating even the slightest (小毫) need to dissolve the Kuomintang. However, it should be added here that China has a powerful Communist army whose actions often attract public attention. If the [Censored: Sino-Japanese?] conflict takes a long time to reach a fundamental resolution, or if a Second [Censored: World War?] erupts centered on the Far East, then even if the Chinese proletariat lacks universally developed class consciousness and has not shed its national character unsuitable for realizing Communism, the possibility of a [Censored: revolution?] similar to [Censored: Russia?] after the Great European War occurring is high.

4.  China, largely due to its own responsibility, has already become a US colony and a common [Censored: market?] for the capitalist powers. Consequently, even if it maintains the appearance of an independent and sovereign nation politically, economically, unless it achieves the goal of an industrial revolution, it lacks the qualifications of a modern, fully independent nation. Therefore, even if it cannot escape the invasion of capitalist imperialist foreign powers in an economic sense, viewed from the interpretation of the Washington Treaties (華府 諸條約) and the League of Nations Covenant, it will certainly not suffer the fate of international administration or partition in a political sense. However, limited to the Manchuria-Mongolia (滿蒙) region, even though the new state of Manchukuo has recently emerged, when a fundamental resolution is reached between Japan and China in the future, I believe an international free zone will be established in Manchuria-Mongolia under Chinese suzerainty, securing the right of residence for people of all nations. <23>

Kuomintang Government Continues As Is
Song Chin-u

1.  China suffered immense losses in terms of human life, finances, and various other aspects due to the recent Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents, but I think the spiritual aspect is different. China had many internal conflicts, such as the confrontation between the Kuomintang, the Guangdong faction, and the Communist Party, friction (軋礫) among warlords, and other issues that were major problems for unification. However, due to this incident, I believe it has spurred a heightened sense of self-awareness and will consequently have a significant impact on China's path forward.

2.  China will surely be able to consolidate its foundation through its own strength. Politically, it will likely maintain a pro-American and pro-British tendency.

3.  The Kuomintang government will continue. Why? Because if the Communist Party were established, it would be an enemy to all capitalist nations, so they wouldn't allow it to happen. Also, I don't think the Nationalist Party will suddenly seize power. The Kuomintang government, with its 40-50 year history, will likely continue, and it might even take on the characteristics of the Nationalist Party.

4.  This issue will be decided by the attitude of the Chinese people. If the Chinese people become self-aware, unite, and handle the situation well, international administration or partition will not happen. Otherwise, it's uncertain what fate they might face. (Interview, Reporter Mun Kwi-jae)

National Consciousness Strengthens Further
Wŏn Se-hun

1.  What impact will the two major incidents in Manchuria and Shanghai have on China's national path? The common observation would be that they will have an inauspicious, negative impact. No matter how much the Republic of China made internal strife its business, having barely achieved formal unification under the Kuomintang regime and reached a point where constructive work could begin, it encountered an unprecedented external calamity and suffered a major blow. Of course, this cannot be called a smooth, positive influence on its path. However, as the saying goes, there is no pure profit or pure loss in the world, and there is a sense of "the fortunes of old Sai's horse" (塞翁得失). The separation and independence of the four northeastern provinces (東北 4省), a crucial eastern and western hub with a population of 30 million and an area one-eighth of all China, and the sacrifice of tens of thousands of armed soldiers and billions in assets without yet achieving tangible results – viewed from any angle, this is an immense loss. <24> However, in terms of territory (Original: 地方面으로는) [Omitted in original - 중략] there are also aspects. To elaborate, even the streams of warlords and politicians who seemed to regard internal strife as their sole heavenly mandate, and the unscrupulous merchants (奸商) and ignorant farmers (愚農) who disregarded the rise and fall of the nation and the survival of the people, having thoroughly experienced the baptism (洗禮) of this incident, have gained a great [Censored: awakening?]. This must be seen as a [Censored: event?] that strongly imparted a national or [Censored: ethnic?] consciousness to the Chinese people. Furthermore, externally, considerable [Censored: gains?] have been harvested in the international arena. Although there are points where they suffer [Censored: losses?] from the imperialist powers, gaining [Censored: support?] generally, starting with the weaker nations, and thus not being isolated and unaided diplomatically, must also be seen as a major [Censored: benefit?] for national survival. Moreover, even if Manchuria has separated and become independent, considering the [Omitted in original - 畧] of that independent state, and also considering the people of the new state of Manchukuo, since almost all of them are of the Chinese ethnicity, [Omitted in original - 畧].
    Regarding the Shanghai Incident, whatever changes may occur in the future, it is unlikely to reach the point of ceding territory or paying war reparations. Thus, for China, which experienced internal strife every spring and autumn, this cannot be called such a great [Censored: loss?]. In short, the two major incidents in Manchuria and Shanghai are extremely [Censored: negative?] and [Censored: detrimental?] to China, while at the same time, this [Censored: negativity?] and this [Censored: detriment?] paradoxically provide considerable awakening and impetus both internally and externally, resulting in a positive influence on the national path. China is merely undergoing a kind of fated process that it inevitably had to face at some point to engage in national life in the true sense.

[Image Caption] Leaders of the 19th Route Army that resisted the Japanese Army. From right: Jiang Guangding (蔣光鼎, General Commander), (one person skipped), Cai Tingkai (蔡廷楷, Commander), Dai Ji (戴戟, Shanghai Garrison Commander).

Pro-American and Allied-with-Britain Policy

2.  Which way will China's future foreign policy lean? Of course, they would wish to adopt a pro-American, allied-with-Russia policy. However, such a foreign policy would merely be their subjective hope. Ultimately, it will likely gravitate towards a pro-American, allied-with-Britain policy. Being friendly with both the US and allied with Russia is impossible, just as one cannot be friendly with both the League of Nations and the Third International simultaneously. Therefore, it will eventually come down to choosing friendship with one of these two camps. And given China's current situation, any regime other than the Communist Party will lead China towards a pro-American policy. Consequently, due to the relationship between Britain and the US, China's policy of alliance with Britain will naturally follow suit. If the Communist Party establishes its regime in China, then it wouldn't just be pro-Russian; the entire situation in East Asia would change. However, since it hasn't reached that point yet, it's not an issue. As for whether it will be pro-Japanese, apart from the new regime in Manchuria, no warlord or politician would adopt this. It wouldn't be permissible in domestic policy, and the same holds true for foreign policy. In the future, China will strive even harder <25> for friendship with the Anglo-American bloc.

The Kuomintang is Also Nationalist

[Image Caption] The Shanghai International Settlement under the defense of the International Volunteer Corps.

3.  The current Kuomintang regime is a regime of nationalists. It is true that discontent and dissatisfaction have arisen among bourgeois liberal nationalists because the regime adopts a Soviet or Italian-style one-party dictatorship system. Therefore, for example, figures like the intellectual Hu Shih (胡適) and out-of-favor warlords or politicians, collaborating with youth who hold grudges against the Kuomintang, seem to be increasingly active in plotting to overthrow the Kuomintang regime recently. It is said that the Kuomintang authorities are also trying to appease old bureaucrats and warlords to mitigate this atmosphere. However, the Kuomintang authorities will likely be unable to fundamentally alleviate the discontented atmosphere, and the plot to overthrow the Kuomintang's dictatorial regime under the name of the Nationalist Party will also not easily achieve its goal. Therefore, the Kuomintang regime will likely continue for some time yet. Based on this reality, these two factions will likely seek a path of compromise for some time. The only entity possessing fundamentally divergent principles and latent or overt power capable of confronting and overthrowing the Kuomintang regime is the Communist Party. In other words, in present-day China, only the Kuomintang and the Communist Party exist as entities capable of contending for and maintaining political power. Therefore, if the Kuomintang regime collapses, the one to replace it will be the Communist Party. However, China's internal situation and the international political situation do not yet permit China to become this party's domain. Nevertheless, speaking of the actual state of China even now, one cannot say that China is under the complete rule of the Kuomintang; rather, it could be said that a certain portion of rule is being delegated to the Communist Party.

Substance is Already Joint Management

4.  The question of whether China might face international joint management or partition (爪分) in the future is not unfounded. However, even present-day China cannot be called a China governed completely independently by the Chinese. In other words, it wouldn't be wrong to say that present-day China is under a disguised form of international joint management. <26> The powers will likely not adopt a more overt form of joint management than the current one. Even while manipulating and controlling China as they currently do, they can achieve the substance of joint management with ample room to spare while putting forth the fine-sounding name of not harming China's independence. Considering China itself, it has reached a point where it voluntarily accepts joint management to that extent. The recent incidents in Manchuria and Shanghai have made this even more so. The measures China has taken towards the League and the United States since the incidents occurred prove this with room to spare.
    Next, regarding the question of partition, if this were the world before the Great European War, it might be an issue. However, in today's world, even if such talk is occasionally heard, it likely won't become a realistic problem. The powers' policy towards China has greatly changed since the Great War; namely, they view China as an object for economic [Censored: exploitation?] and not as an object for territorial plunder. Therefore, present-day China, while perhaps already undergoing disguised international joint management internally, has reached a stage where it will not suffer territorial partition. Even if one or two powers were to speak of territorial ambitions, they would ultimately fail.

The Choleric Chinese People
Ko Yŏng-hwan

1.  The Chinese people, generally seen as possessing abundant continental magnanimity, actually seem to contain a large amount of choleric temperament (膽汁質). Therefore, even when faced with slightly shameful or insulting events, they don't act hastily. To others' eyes, they may seem foolish like a bear or sly like a snake, possessing extraordinary patience and tolerance. Thus, it seems likely they will try to skillfully utilize even events like the recent Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents to bring forth (齎來) an effect that turns misfortune into fortune for their path forward. This is not just my speculation; recent news indicates that the Nationalist Government, in order to resist Japan's attack over the long term, plans to excavate tunnels (隧道) approximately 2,000 li long with a budget of about 50 million yuan. Starting from Pukou (浦口), they plan to go north along the Jinpu (津浦線) and Longhai (瀧海線) railway lines to Xuzhou (徐州), and west through Xuzhou, Kaifeng (開封), Zhengzhou (鄭州), to Luoyang (洛陽). Also, the fact that the Nanjing (南京) and Guangdong (廣東) governments have compromised and are cooperating since the Manchurian Incident occurred serves as evidence supporting this.

2.  In the current state, China, being weak, will naturally try to become close to any foreign country that shows sympathy and a willingness to help, even if only slightly. Under the present circumstances, it is difficult to say they will adopt a pro-Japanese attitude. Since Britain is preoccupied with the India (印度) issue for the time being, it seems difficult to predict a pro-British stance. Also, as long as the Nationalist Government faction holds power, thinking they would become pro-Russian seems difficult. Therefore, regardless of their underlying intentions, it seems easiest for them to become close to the United States, which subtly shows the appearance <27> of being most sympathetic towards China.

3.  Although the internal situation may change constantly, for the time being, I think it will remain the era of the Kuomintang.

Partition is Impossible

4.  China probably won't face international administration or even partition. Of course, judging by the intentions of the European and American powers, they might be relishing the current Sino-Japanese conflict as a once-in-a-thousand-years golden opportunity. That is, their intention seems to be, regarding the Sino-Japanese conflict, to adopt the attitude shown since the incident began: convening meetings in scenic spots by Lake Geneva under fine-sounding names like the League of Nations Council, the League of Nations Assembly, or Conferences of Statesmen. They compete with their three-inch tongues (三寸舌, i.e., eloquence) to grab headlines with special typefaces in the world's newspapers, or perhaps practice diplomatic dancing as a form of digestive exercise for their bloated sense of fullness. Meanwhile, they send routine letters of advice to the Japanese and Chinese governments urging them to "resolve all disputes peacefully by any means necessary," while observing the trend (趨移) of the overall situation with eyes that miss not a drop. Once the Chinese people have suffered quite severely, and the Japanese military side has also become somewhat exhausted and developed a sense of war weariness (倦怠症), then, as if they have no choice but to rise up for the sake of "justice" and "humanity," or for "world peace" and "human happiness"—things they abuse or misuse like their patented products—they will carefully maintain their respective countries' international prestige. Then, with the resolve and attitude of not shying away even from armed sanctions, they will decisively begin to interfere. Later, seizing another opportunity, their intention seems to be to advocate for internationally administering China or even partitioning it outright.
    However, today's world is entirely different from the world of the 18th or 19th century. Therefore, no matter how "disunited" or "disorderly" China may be, I am firmly convinced (確信) that it cannot be so easily partitioned or subjected to international administration according to the ambitions of a few powers (regarding China as a whole).

Partition is Impossible
Ham Sang-hun

1.  It is a fact that general public morale has been dejected (沮喪) by the general retreat of the 19th Route Army. If the atmosphere in the League and the United States becomes favorable to China in the future, public morale might revive [Censored: revive?]. Otherwise, trust in the current government will fall to the ground, potentially leading to situations like the past attack on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and the division of the country by various strongmen will begin anew.

2.  They might try to be pro-American, but if the US remains ultimately cool, they might adopt a pro-Russian policy.

3.  The Nationalist Party is still weak, and due to interference from the imperialist powers, it seems it is not yet time for the Communist Party to take power. Political power will likely go to someone under the name of the Kuomintang.

4.  International administration or international partition probably won't happen, right? Why? Because while certain countries <28> are unwilling (不願) for China to unify, other countries desire China's bourgeois national unification.

A Kind of Stimulant
Kim Chang-hwan

1.  It will promote the establishment of national sovereignty and the completion of a national defense force. At the very least, it served as a great stimulant (刺戟劑) for instilling a national consciousness spiritually into the general populace.

2.  It will lean towards pro-British/American, especially pro-American. Pro-Japanese is of course impossible. Pro-Russian is not feasible due to unfavorable foreign relations and the avoidance (忌避) of Communism. The reason it leans more towards pro-American than pro-British is that the United States is a supporter of the Nine-Power Treaty, which stipulates the "territorial integrity of China," and the Kellogg-Briand Pact, which "condemns recourse to war for the solution of international controversies." Furthermore, America is a country capable of supplying the funds necessary for China's development.

3.  The Kuomintang government will continue. The Nationalist Party emerged out of discontent with the Kuomintang's dictatorship under Chiang Kai-shek and is gradually gaining influence. It seems Zhang Xueliang (張學良) holds favorable views towards it, but he lacks the power to seize control. The Communist Party's seizure of power is also a matter for the distant future.

4.  It will not suffer the fate of international administration or partition. This can be inferred from the fact that when Japan proposed establishing neutral zones in Shanghai, Tianjin (天津), Hankou (漢口), Qingdao (靑島), Guangdong (廣東), Beiping (北平), etc., the United States opposed it, stating that it implied international re-partitioning and violated the Nine-Power Treaty. <29>

**Summary**

This 1932 article from the magazine *Tonggwang* presents commentary from several Korean intellectuals (Yi Chŏng-sŏp, Hŏ Hŏn, Song Chin-u, Wŏn Se-hun, Ko Yŏng-hwan, Ham Sang-hun, Kim Chang-hwan) on the future of China following the Manchurian and Shanghai Incidents. They debate China's potential path (reconstruction vs. bankruptcy), its likely foreign policy alignment (mostly predicting pro-American leanings, dismissing pro-Japanese or immediate pro-Russian shifts), the endurance of the Kuomintang (generally expected to continue, despite challenges from Communists or other Nationalists), and the possibility of international administration or partition (generally deemed unlikely in its overt forms, though some note a de facto economic co-management). The contributors largely agree that the incidents, while damaging, served as a strong stimulus for Chinese national consciousness and unity against external threats, potentially strengthening the nation in the long run despite immediate losses.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is significant as it showcases Korean intellectuals engaging with major international events and analyzing geopolitical shifts, particularly concerning their large neighbor, China, during a period of intense Japanese imperialism. Their discussions reflect an awareness of global power dynamics (US influence, League of Nations limitations, Soviet potential) and internal Chinese politics (Kuomintang vs. Communists, warlords). Historians might find interesting the generally sympathetic, though analytical, view towards China's plight, the assessment of Japan's actions, the predictions about US policy, and the underlying discourse on nationalism and state power, all expressed within the constraints of colonial censorship (evident in the bracketed omissions like OO, XX).

中國의 國父라하는 孫文을 紀念한 彫刻 巴里國民黨支部所有
現下의 中國은 다시 未曾有의 混亂期를 當面하고 잇다. 對外的으로 難問題가 疊出하고 對內的으로도 紛爭이 不息한다. 4億萬大衆의 가는 길은 곳 東洋大勢의 가는 길이요 世界平和의 가는 길이 아니냐. 未曾有의 不景氣를 질머진 世界列强의 動態는 특히 中國의 前途를 중심으로 旋風的 回轉을 하고 잇는 것도 사실이다. 中國은 과연 어디로 가는가.＜20＞

國民一致運動의 强調
李晶燮

1. 금번 滿洲 及 上海事件은 中國의 進路에 莫大한 영향을 미칠 것입니다. 첫재로 精神的으로 보아 中國民의 民族感은 以前의 그 어느 歷史期에서 보다도 가장 강렬한 刺戟을 받은 것 같이 보입니다. 그것은 이번 事變이 中國歷史가 가진 모든 事變 가운데 가장 중대한 事變이라는 의미에서 하는 말이 아니라 新文化가 輸入된 以來, 또는 統一政府가 실현된 以來의 一大事變이라는 것과 楊子江流域은 물론, 全中國을 지배하는 地位에 잇는 上海에까지 事變이 展開되엇다는 것이 그 이유인가 합니다. 이와 같이 강렬한 民族感의 刺戟을 받앗으므로 中國民은 장래의 政治運動에 잇어서 從來와 같은 骨肉的 內爭에 대하야 크게 반성함이 잇을 것입니다. 國民一致運動이 당연히 강조될 것입니다.
滿洲, 上海事變 以來 日本의 自由系 新聞은 물론, 社會民衆黨까지 깨끗이 國粹化하야 日本은 전체로보면 보다 더 右傾化하여 젓습니다. 그러므로 中國의 今後 政治運動도 보다 더 右傾化하리라고 추측합니다.
또 이번 事變으로 말미아마 中國民은 獨逸의 御用學者로 유명한 『트라이취케』의 『國家는 力』 Der Staat ist Macht이라는 國家觀에 대하야 상당한 眞理를 발견하려 할 것입니다.

親米는 旣定事實
2. 정해 놓고 親米定策을 취할 것으로 봅니다. 이번 事變에 잇어서＜21＞ 米國이 中國을 위하야 積極的 행동을 취한 바는 없어도 상당히 많은 同情的 노력을 하엿든 것만은 누구나 否認못할 것입니다. 金融帝國主義의 米國이 經濟的으로 東洋에 진출함에 잇어서 또는 OO과 一朝有事時를 考慮하야서라도 今後 될 수 잇는대로 親中主義를 취할 것이 분명한 동시에 中國으로서는 政治的 統一 외에 무엇보다도 産業의 啓發인 바 이에는 米國의 資本을 輸入하여야 겟고 對外政策으로는 國際政局에 가장 큰 세력을 가진 米國을 업는 것이 매우 유리함으로 米國과 에데까지 親善關係를 圖하려 할 것입니다.
親英定策은 容易히 실현 못 되리라 봅니다. 그것은 今番 事變 중에 中國이 어느 정도쯤 親英定策을 쓴 것은 첫재는 日本에 대한 反動이요, 둘재는 英國을 통하야 國際聯盟의 諸情勢를 自國側에 유리하게 展開시키자는 데서 그러한 一時政策을 취한 것이요 또 英國은 日中의 충돌을 기회로 하야 手腕家 『랭프쏜』公使로 하여금 漁夫의 利를 취하려든 것이엇으나 事變이 一段落을 告하게 되면 英國에 대한 中國의 權益回收運動이 再燃할 것임으로 英中關係의 장래는 圓滑치 못할 것으로 관찰하려 합니다.
이번 事變에 잇어서 露國은 傍觀的 태도를 취하야 그야말로 自重靜觀으로 一貫하엿으니 中國이 새삼스럽게 親露政策을 취할 理가 없겟지요. 다만 勞農露西亞의 유명한 産業計劃이 着着 進展되어 經濟的으로 國際勢力을 얻게 되면 親露政策을 취할 可望이 잇을 것이나 이것은 이번 사건과는 관계없는 일이 아니겟습니까.
親日政策이요? 可望없는 일이라고 할 밖에 수 없겟지오. 그도 日中의 관계가 작년 9月 18日 以前 狀態(Statu quo ante bellum)에로 恢復된다면 몰라도 現狀으로 보면 이것은 全然 無望이라는 限에서 中國의 親日政策 云云은 안 될 말입니다.

蔣介石 天下 게속＜22＞

3. 依然 國民黨政府가 계속될 것입니다. 지금에 누구니누구니해야 蔣介石 以外에는 中國을 收拾統制할 인물이 없다고 봅니다. 蔣介石이 政權을 잡고 잇는 限에서 中國은 依然 國民黨 政府가 다스리게 되겟지오. 共産黨天下? 안될 말입니다. 만일에 中國에 共産黨政府가 실현된다면 그것은 露國의 세력이 米, 英, 日 佛 등의 諸資本國을 상대로 능히 싸울만한 정도에 이르러야 할 것입니다. 그러지 않고 共産政府가 中國에 실현된다면 그는 中國에 가장 많은 利害關係를 가진 日, 英, 米 등 諸資本主義 國家에게 撲滅을 당하지 않을까 생각합니다.
4. 中國이 國際管理되지도 않겟고 分割될 理도 萬無입니다. 지금에 잇서서 그러케 될 아무 건데기가 없습니다. (3月 11日)

米國과 提携乎
許憲

1. 今番 滿洲 及 上海事變은 對內的으로는 戰時 前後의 非常時機에 처하는 一時的 방편으로서 반드시 軍閥을 중심으로 한 獨裁政治가 실현될 것이요 對外的으로는 가장 危險性을 가진 時代錯誤의 秘密外交가 積極的으로 暗中飛躍할 것이라고 본다.
2. 中國 今後의 對外政策은 傳統的인 以夷制夷策을 前提로 하야 米國과 提携할 것은 明若觀火다. 况 최근 中國은 國際聯盟의 議事機關 즉 『輿論의 法廷』을 통하야 聯盟規約 第16條에 정한 經濟的 制裁의 강행을 가능케 하려고 노력한다. 비록 國際聯盟이 此를 강행코저 할지라도 世界最大의 經濟力을 有한 米國이 此에 無關心하면 第16條의 실행은 全然 不可能할 것이다. 그런데 國際聯盟에 참가치 아니한 米國이 對中問題에 관하야 도리어 聯盟國 以上의 飛躍的 態度로 聯盟과 협력하는 것은 발서 米中間에 어떤 密約이 성립하지 아니 하엿는가 한다.
3. 中國國民黨은 以黨治國을 標榜하고 잇는 이상에는 黨이 政府의 上에 잇어서 此를 指導하여야 할 것이다. 그러나 軍閥을 중심으로 한 獨裁政治家는 먼저 黨權을 縮小하고 政府의 權力을 擴大하여 도리어 黨을 政府의 下에 存置할 것이다. 그와 동시에 黨의 생명으로 하야 오든 民衆運動은 全然히 廢止되어 그 形骸만도 殘存치 못할 것인즉 귀태여 國民黨을 解散할 필요가 小毫도 없을 것이다. 그러나 玆에 附言할 것은 中國에는 强大한 共産軍의 세력이 잇어서 그 행동이 往往 世人의 주목을 끄으는 일도 적지 아니하다. 만일 日中 XX이 根本的 解決에 이르기까지 長久한 時日이 걸리거나, 혹은 第2XXXX이 極東을 중심으로 하야 爆發된다면 中國의 無産階級이 비록 階級的 意識이 普遍的으로 발달되지 못하엿다 할지라도 또한 共産主義실현에 不適當한 國民性을 脫却하지 못하엿다 할지라도 歐洲大戰 後 XXXX과 同樣의 XXXX이 실현할 可能性이 많으리라고 본다.
4. 中國은 대부분 자신의 責任上 벌서 米殖民地가 되고 말엇으며 또한 列國資本主義의 共同 XX場이 되엇섯다. 그 결과 政治上으로서는 아직 獨立自主의 國家의 體面을 보존한다 할지라도 經濟上으로서는 産業革命의 목적을 달성하지 못하면 아모리 하야도 現代的 완전한 獨立國家의 資格이 없다 할 것이다. 그러므로 經濟的 의미에 잇어서 資本帝國主義的 外國勢力의 侵畧을 免치 못한다 할 지라도 政治的 意味에 잇어서 華府 諸條約 及 國際聯盟 規約 解釋上으로 보아서 결코 國際管理 또는 分割의 운명을 당하지 않을 줄로 본다. 단 滿蒙 地方에 限하야는 최근에 滿洲 新國家가 비록 출현되엇다 할 지라도 後日에 이르러 日中間에 根本的 해결 될 때에는 滿蒙은＜23＞ 中國의 宗主權 下에 國際自由地帶를 設定하야 各國人의 居住自由權을 獲得하리라고 본다.

國民黨 政府 依然 繼續
宋鎭禹

1. 中國은 이번 滿洲事件과 上海事變으로 인하야 人命과 財政上 기타 여러가지로 巨大한 損失을 보앗지마는 精神上으로는 그러치 않다고 생각합니다. 中國은 國民黨과 廣東派, 共産黨과의 대립, 軍閥間의 軋礫 기타 여러가지로 內爭이 많고 統一上 큰 문제엿섯는데 이번 사건으로 말미아마 一層 緊張한 自覺을 이르키고 따라서 中國進路에 많은 영향을 주리라고 믿습니다.
2. 中國은 中國 자신의 힘으로야 그 基礎를 鞏固히 할 수 잇겟지오. 政治上으로 보면 역시 親米, 親英의 傾向을 가질 것입니다.
3. 國民黨政府가 계속될 것입니다. 왜 그런고 하면 만일 共産黨이 성립된다 하면 그것은 資本主義國家 전부의 敵이니까 그러케 되게 두지 않을 것이요 國家主義黨이 갑작이 權力을 쥐게 되리라고도 생각 안됩니다. 역시 4,50年의 歷史를 가진 國民黨政府가 계속될 것이요 그것이 國家主義黨의 色彩를 띠게 될 지도 모릅니다.
4. 中國國民의 態度如何에 의하야 결정될 문제입니다. 中國國民이 自覺하고 一致團結하야 善處하면 國際管理나 分割이 안될 것이요 그러치 않으면 如何한 운명에 이를지 모를 것입니다. (談, 文貴在 記者)

國家觀念이 益鞏固
