Magazine Title: 동광 제32호
Publication Date: 1932년 04월 01일
Article Title: 너와 나 (You and I)
Author: 김여제
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

The righteous have no bread
And youth has no love
Ruffians emerge in broad daylight
Masked petty men strut about rampantly
Who will save this cursed land?
O youth of Chosŏn!
It is you and I

Everywhere sighs are heard
Faces are full of sorrow
Of failure and despair
Do you hear the pitiful laments?
Who will save our pitiful compatriots?
O youth of Chosŏn!
It is you and I

We are the masters of destiny
We must find our own way to live<119>
Standing in adversity, laughing greatly
With boundless hope and courage
Who will bring about that day of glory?
O youth of Chosŏn!
It is you and I.<120>
<119-120>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Yŏ-je paints a bleak picture of contemporary Korean society, lamenting injustice ("righteous have no bread"), lost hope ("youth has no love"), rampant corruption ("ruffians," "masked petty men"), and widespread suffering ("sighs," "sorrow," "laments"). Despite this despair, it issues a powerful call to the youth of Korea ("Chosŏn"), identifying them ("you and I") as the masters of their own fate and the only ones capable of overcoming adversity ("laughing greatly" in hardship) and bringing about a "day of glory" for the nation through courage and self-reliance. For historians, the poem vividly reflects the oppressive atmosphere and socio-economic hardships of colonial Korea in the early 1930s, while simultaneously showcasing the resilient nationalist spirit and the strong emphasis placed on the younger generation as agents of national salvation and future hope.
