**Body Text Translation**
If
						By Pak-i, Translated by Kim An-sŏ

If you wish to live, kill your Soul (魂).
Cast away all the Heart's (맘) Aspirations (熱望) too.
And walk through this Life (人生) as an ignoramus.
Facing the Agonies (苦惱) of this Life alone with a Smile (微笑).

If you wish to seek your own Happiness (幸福) on this Earth (地上)
As a person free from care,
Cast the ice of the North Pole (北極) into your heart,
And then feel no Emotion (感動), nor have any Hope (希望).
-1932, 3, 7-

(Appendix) Although I tried to capture the Gist (大意) from the original poem in the S-language (에쓰語 原作詩), I am only Ashamed (汗顔) before the Original Author (原作者). I cannot Suppress (禁) the Three Sighs and Nine Laments (三歎九恨), feeling as though I tried to hold Sunlight (日光) in the palm of my hand.-An-sŏ<127>

**Summary**

The poem "If" advises a path of emotional detachment and suppressed desire as a means to survive life's hardships and find a semblance of personal happiness. It suggests killing one's soul, discarding aspirations, and embracing ignorance and emotional coldness ("North Pole ice") to navigate life's pains with a forced smile. The translator, Kim An-sŏ, adds a note expressing profound humility, feeling inadequate in capturing the essence of the original Esperanto poem ("S-language," likely Esperanto given Kim's known work) and lamenting the loss of its brilliance in translation. For historians, this piece is interesting as it reflects a potential mood of resignation or a survival strategy emphasizing emotional suppression, possibly resonant with the difficulties of life in colonial Korea. The translator's note also highlights the active cultural exchange through translation (specifically Esperanto literature) during this period and the translator's own artistic anxieties.

만일에
						바기(作), 金岸曙(譯)
만일에 그대 살고 싶건 魂을 죽이라.
맘의 熱望도 모다 던저 버리라.
그러고 모르숭이로 이 人生을 걸으라.
이 人生의 苦惱를 혼자서 微笑로 맞으며.

근심없는 사람으로 그대만의 幸福을
이 地上서 구하고 싶거들랑,
北極 어름을 그대 맘에 던지라,
그러고는 感動도 되지 말고 希望도 말으라.
-1932, 3, 7-
(附記) 에쓰語 原作詩에서 大意를 따노라고 하엿으나, 原作者에게 汗顔될 뿐이외다. 日光을 손바닥에다 잡아 놓은 듯한 三歎九恨을 禁할 길이 없을 뿐이외다.-岸曙＜127＞
