Magazine Title: 동광 제22호
Publication Date: 1931-06-01
Article Title: 오를까 내릴까 今秋米價豫想, 아직도 繼續下落 -米價引上策은 失敗히엿다.- (Will it Rise or Fall? This Autumn's Rice Price Forecast, Still Continuing to Fall - Rice Price Hike Measures Have Failed.)
Author: 金得鎭
Article Type: 논설 (Editorial/Commentary)

1
When considering the rice price problem, we first need to note that the rice price is not determined by its producers, but rather by others not involved in production—merchants, landlords, etc.
"The price of these products is sold without relation to the value of production. This is because merchants arbitrarily set the price of the products, taking advantage of the farmers' urgent need for cash during the rent payment period" (Engels).
In fact, Chosŏn farmers sell at the time of year when the rice price is lowest—during the harvest season—to make various payments such as rent, taxes, water fees, debts, and fertilizer costs. Not only that, but local purchasing merchants use various deceptions to snatch rice from the farmers' hands at unbelievably low prices (歇價).
The production cost of rice for tenant farmers within Chosŏn, even calculated conservatively, would be 30 wŏn per sŏk (in Japan, it's 34 wŏn for tenant farmers - Imperial Agricultural Association (帝國農會) Survey). They sold it off around December last year at the market price of 12 or 13 wŏn, and now, not a single grain of rice likely remains in the farmers' hands. Therefore, what this autumn's rice price will be is, of course, not a major concern for the farmers—the poor farmers who make up the majority—but only a critical issue for the large landlords and merchants who have stored thousands or tens of thousands of sŏk in warehouses, waiting for an opportune moment. Consequently, the various spokesmen for the landlord class are still concerned "for the farmers" that the rice price is not soaring and are indignant at the "sleep state worse than nothing" of the Chosŏn Agricultural Association.

2
Last year's rice crop was an unprecedented bumper harvest. Chosŏn's actual yield was over 19,183,000 sŏk, an increase of over 5,482,000 sŏk compared to the previous year. Japan's actual yield was 66,882,000 sŏk, an increase of over 7,320,000 sŏk compared to the previous year. Combined, it showed a massive increase in harvest of about 13,000,000 sŏk.
With the five grains being abundant, in the old days, villages would have overflowed with songs of peace and plenty (擊壤歌). However, in reality, to save [people] from the "enormous damage due to the good harvest," the authorities devised "emergency measures," and newspaper editorials reported on the "miserable state of farming villages." <3>
This kind of rice panic, of course, has its fundamental cause in overproduction relative to the consumption power of the masses, which has been extremely constricted under capitalist exploitation. If all people in Chosŏn ate their fill, assuming an average annual consumption of 1 sŏk 5 tu per person, 30 million sŏk would be needed per year.
So, how is a mere output of 19 million sŏk an "excess"? Is there not rather a shortage of over 10 million sŏk?
Thus, while socially, Chosŏn's rice production is still infinitely insufficient, under the current production methods, a slight increase in harvest immediately appears as excess, leading to "enormous damage due to a good harvest."
Within this contradiction—and this contradiction becoming ever sharper at the current stage—lies the essence of the severe "famine of abundance (豊年饑饉)."

3
The rice price, which had already begun to gradually fall from August and September last year in anticipation of a bumper crop, plummeted by about 50% by January this year compared to the same month the previous year. Even after that, the Inch'ŏn futures market price lingered in the 14-15 wŏn range.
Rice price fluctuations within Chosŏn always follow the rise and fall of rice prices within Japan, the major producer and consumer of rice. Therefore, the supply and demand situation within Japan and the government's policies immediately ripple through to the rice price within Chosŏn.
Looking at the supply and demand situation within Japan this year based on figures announced by the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry (農林省):

Let's glance at the "countermeasures" for disposing of the surplus rice of over 10 million sŏk. However, this surplus rice intended for disposal is, of course, not an absolute surplus. It is a "surplus" that arises because there is a vast impoverished populace unable to buy even a single hop of white rice throughout the year, and these are "countermeasures" designed to raise the rice price and further deny consumption to the masses. The measures are as follows:
1. Government purchase of 3 million sŏk in two installments.
2. Dumping government rice overseas.
3. Raising the tariff on foreign rice from the previous 1 wŏn per 100 kŭn to 2 wŏn, restricting foreign rice imports into both Japan and Chosŏn.
4. Providing low-interest funds to landlords to prevent panic selling (賣急).
5. Restricting the inflow of Chosŏn rice into Japan.
6. Restricting the import of Manchurian millet into Chosŏn.
All these measures to raise or prevent the fall of rice prices were the result of landlords, wealthy farmers, etc., "running frantically" "to save the farmers from destitution." However, as previously stated, since tenant farmers and small owner-farmers had already sold off all their harvested rice during the harvest season, the benefits of the rice price hike measures are received only by a small number of landlords and wealthy farmers.
Originally, the financial bourgeoisie (금융 부르조아지-) inwardly desired a fall in rice prices. An increase in rice production and a sharp drop in rice prices are ideal conditions for carrying out rationalization and the most blessed event for the pursuit of profit. However, these financial bourgeoisie cannot completely disregard the interests of the landlords and wealthy farmers. <4> The landlord class in Chosŏn and Japan still maintains its feudal power rooted in various regions to this day, making it difficult to practically strengthen the dominance of finance capital without securing the support of the landlord class, the holders of this power. Therefore, they attempt rice price hike measures and tolerate the minor sacrifices resulting from them. In fact, that sacrifice is merely throwing a portion of their vast monopolistic special profits to the landlord class.

4

According to the figures above, the rice price did indeed recover somewhat in February and March. The government purchases started in February and March (after farmers had sold everything off at the lowest prices in December and January), along with various other emergency measures, can be seen to have had some effect. However, the difference between the lowest price in January and the March price is only about 1 wŏn. Such a difference is not unique to this year. Looking at the rice price index over several decades, the index for March is generally over 10% higher than that for January every year. (Refer to the Rice Compendium (米穀要覽) published by the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry)
Therefore, it can be seen that the numerous countermeasures were only effective in preventing the rice price from falling further, but did not succeed at all in raising the rice price. Since the market price in February and March, when the effects of the rice price countermeasures should have been most pronounced, did not rise at all, there is no factor suggesting any improvement in the future trend. Not only that, but due to the bumper crop of Siamese rice, its import—despite efforts to block foreign rice imports with high tariffs—is unexpectedly large, and the plan to dump government rice in China (支那) has become almost impossible due to opposition from the Nanjing (南京) government.

5
The existence of Chosŏn rice, with its 5.5 million sŏk increase in harvest, further threatens the Japanese rice price. Even in normal years, the export of Chosŏn rice to Japan amounts to six or seven million sŏk annually, so in a year like this, if left unchecked, the Japanese rice price would fall even more disastrously. Finance capitalists welcome the fall in rice prices, but they cannot ignore the interests of landlords and wealthy farmers. Thus, the Chosŏn rice countermeasure—import restriction—was implemented. However, on the other hand, the interests of the landlords in colonial Chosŏn cannot be entirely disregarded either. Therefore, on one hand, 15.8 million wŏn in low-interest funds were procured from the Ministry of Finance Deposit Bureau (大藏省 預金部) to provide loans against grain stored in agricultural and commercial warehouses, and on the other hand, attempts were made to block the import of Manchurian millet (滿洲粟) to compel farmers to consume white rice.
However, these measures also proved futile. While the low-interest financing was advantageously used by some landlord rice merchants, tenant farmers and owner-farmers lacked the means to utilize it. Consequently, the rice sold off all at once flooded the market like a deluge, with over 5.55 million sŏk entering Japan from November to the end of April alone. Furthermore, landlords, who would normally have sold off their autumn harvest rice by now, are all still holding onto it as unhulled rice (正租), waiting only for the price to rise. <5> Therefore, from now on, the massive amount of landlord rice, which accounts for over 30% of Chosŏn's rice production, will flood the market as soon as the price seems even slightly favorable, inevitably causing the rice price to fall further. Such anarchy (無政府性) is the essence of capitalism.

6
This short article was intended to end here, but new material was reported in today's newspaper. Although the planned page count has been exceeded, I wish to briefly discuss it below. It was announced that the amount of rice remaining within Japan as of May 1st is 36,039,000 sŏk. Based on this figure, the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry has newly estimated the rice supply and demand forecast for the half-year from May 1st to the end of the rice year (November 30th this year) and announced it as follows:

Foreign Rice Import Estimate 300
Total 41,339
Demand

Calculated Surplus Rice 12,339
Let's compare this supply and demand forecast table with the old forecast table (which was for the entire year) announced last January, also by the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry, as discussed above.
The remaining rice amount of just over 36 million sŏk is excessively low compared to general expectations (the Exchange circles were expecting 38 million sŏk), but even assuming it is an accurate figure, the surplus rice at the end of this rice year, compared to the previous estimate of just over 11 million sŏk, has not decreased at all even after half a year has passed, and it seems the surplus rice will only continue to grow.
Furthermore, the old forecast table projected the annual inflow of Chosŏn rice at 8 million sŏk and foreign rice imports at 250,000 sŏk, and implemented various measures to prohibit or restrict imports/inflows beyond that. The new forecast table itself sufficiently explains that these measures completely failed in less than half a year. Specifically, since 5.5 million sŏk of Chosŏn rice had already flowed in during the first half-year, they were forced to estimate a further inflow of 4 million sŏk for the second half. For foreign rice, the half-year import estimate was set at 300,000 sŏk, adding 50,000 sŏk even to the previous full-year import estimate. One can imagine how large the supply of foreign rice was in the first half—despite the implementation of high tariffs.
The consumption estimate in the old forecast table was calculated based on the Shōwa 4 (1929) standard of 1 sŏk 1 tu 1 sŭng 4 hop per person per year. However, the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry finally had to admit that the consumption power of the masses had sharply declined since then. Thus, the new forecast table calculates the per capita consumption at 4 tu 5 sŭng 4 hop—that is, 9 tu 8 hop per person per year.
Thus, on one hand, there is a rapidly increasing massive surplus of rice, and on the other hand, the consumption power of the masses is drastically decreasing. Therefore, a collapse in rice prices is an unavoidable fact going forward. And it is absolutely impossible that the increase in mass consumption due to the sharp drop in rice prices will overcome the panic again. Farmers who had been groaning under a chronic agricultural depression for over 10 years have had their consumption power reduced almost to zero by this "bumper harvest famine (豊作饑饉)," and the lives of the broad working class have been utterly ruined by unemployment, underemployment, and wage cuts. Therefore, the rice panic will henceforth gain further torrential force and continue until capitalism XXXXXXX. (May 11, 1931) <6>

**Summary**

This 1931 article by Kim Tŭkchin analyzes the severe drop in rice prices in Korea despite a bumper harvest. It argues that government price support measures failed, primarily benefiting landlords and merchants, not the impoverished tenant farmers who sold their rice early at low prices. The author attributes the crisis ("famine of abundance") to capitalist contradictions: overproduction relative to the drastically reduced purchasing power of the Korean masses. Updated government forecasts showing persistent surpluses and decreased consumption estimates further support the prediction of continued price decline. The article employs a Marxist critique, highlighting class conflict (farmers vs. landlords/merchants, finance capital vs. landlords) and the structural flaws of the colonial capitalist system. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into the "rich harvest famine" phenomenon in colonial Korea, the specific mechanisms and failures of Japanese economic policies regarding rice, the severe impact on Korean farmers' livelihoods, the perceived conflict between finance capital and landed interests, and an example of contemporary critical analysis of the colonial economy using Marxist concepts. The use of specific figures on production, consumption, and policy details provides concrete data points for economic history.

오를까 내릴까 今秋米價豫想, 아직도 繼續下落 -米價引上策은 失敗히엿다.-
						金得鎭
1
米價問題를 고찰할 때에 우리들은 먼저 米價는 그 생산자가 결정하는 것이 아니고 오히려 생산에 관여치 않은 타인―상인, 지주 등―이 결정한다는 것을 주의할 필요가 잇다.
『此等 생산물의 가격은 생산의 가치와 관계 없이 판매된다. 소작료 決濟期에 농민이 急切이 현금이 필요함을 奇貨로 상인들은 임의로 생산물의 가격을 정하는 까닭이다』(엥겔스).
사실 朝鮮 農民들은 추수기를 當하여 소작료, 租稅, 水稅, 債務, 肥料代 등의 여러 가지 지불을 위하여 1년 중 제일 米價가 저락한 시기에 팔게 된다. 그 뿐만 아니고 지방 매출 상인배들은 여러 가지 欺瞞을 다하여 말할 수 없는 歇價로 미곡을 농민의 수중에서 빼앗고 잇다.
朝鮮內의 소작인들의 産米 생산비는 적게 산정하여도 每石 30원(日本서는 소작농에 34원- 帝國農會 調査)은 될 것이다. 그것을 작년 12월경의 시세 12, 3원에 팔아뿌리고 지금 농민의 수중에는 한 알의 쌀도 남지 않엇을 것이다. 그러므로 今秋의 米價 如何가 농민―그 太半을 점령한 빈농―들에게는 그리 큰 관심거리가 아님은 물론이고 다만 창고에 數千 石, 數萬 石을 쌓아 놓고 호기를 엳보고 잇는 대지주, 상인들에게만 중대한 문제가 된다. 고로 지주계급 代辯家 諸氏는 아직도 「농민을 위하여」米價가 高騰치 않음을 심려하며 朝鮮農會의 「不若無의 睡眠狀態」를 분개하고 잇다.

2
작년의 米作은 未曾有의 大豊이엇다. 朝鮮米 실수량 1918만3천여 石, 前年에 비하여 548만 2천여 石의 증가요 日本의 實收量 6,688만2천 石, 前年에 비하여 732만여 石의 증가, 합하여 1,300만 石 가량의 大增收를 보앗다.
五穀이 豊穰하니 옛날 같앗으면 村村에 擊壤歌가 橫溢하엿을 것이다. 그런데 사실은 「풍작으로 인한 막대한 피해」를 구하기 위하여 당국에서는 「應急策」을 강구하고 신문의 論調는 「농촌의 慘狀」을 보도한다. ＜3＞
이러한 미곡 공황은 물론 자본주의적 착취하에 극도로 위축된 대중의 소비력에 대한 생산 과잉이 그 근본적 원인이다. 朝鮮 사람이 모두 배불리 먹는다면 當 1인 1년 소비량을 평균 1石 5斗로 잡고도 1년에 3천만 石이 필요하다.
그런데 불과 1,900萬石의 산출이 무엇이 「과잉」인가. 오히려 1천여만 석이 부족하지 아니한가.
이와 같이 사회적으로는 朝鮮의 産米는 아직 한량없이 부족한 것이 사실인데 現 생산방법 하에서는 若干한 增收가 곳 과잉으로 나타나고 「농작으로 인한 막대한 피해」를 받게 된다.
이러한 모순―그러고 현 단계에 이르러 더욱 尖銳化하여 가는 모순 가운데 심각한 「豊年饑饉」의 본질을 알 수 잇다.

1,000餘萬 石의 過剩米 처분을 위한 「대책」을 一瞥하여 보자. 그러나 이같이 처분하려는 過剩米는 절대적 과잉이 아닌 것은 물론이다. 1년을 두고 1合의 白米도 살 수 없는 거대한 빈곤 대중이 잇기 때문에 생기는 「과잉」이고 米價를 騰貴케 하여 대중의 소비를 더욱 거절하는 「대책」이다. 그 대책은 이러하다.
1. 前後 2회로 300萬石을 정부에서 買上.
2. 정부미를 해외로 投賣.
3. 외국관세를 종래 百斤에 1원이든 것을 2원으로 인상하여 日本, 朝鮮 全體에 外米輸入을 제한.
4. 低利 資金을 지주들에게 융통하여 賣急을 방지.
5. 朝鮮米의 日本에 移入을 제한.
6. 朝鮮에 滿洲粟 수입을 제한.
이러한 모든 米價引上 내지 低落防止策은 「농민의 궁핍을 구하기 위하여」 지주, 부농 등이 「狂奔」한 결과엿다. 그러나 소작농, 소자작농들은 旣述한 바와 같이 벌서 추수기에 收穫米를 다 팔아뿌리엇으므로 米價 引上策의 恩澤은 오직 小數地主, 富農들만 받게 된다.
원래 금융 부르조아지-는 米價의 低落을 내심으로 퍽 원하는 바다. 産米의 增收와 米價의 激落은 合理化遂行上 절호의 조건이요 이윤의 추구를 위하여 가장 축복할 만한 일이다. 그러치마는 그들 금융 부르조아지-는 지주, 부농의 이익을 ＜4＞ 全然 돌보지 안을 수 없다. 朝鮮과 日本의 지주계급은 今日토록 의연한 봉건적 세력을 각 지방에 扶植하고 잇음으로 이러한 세력의 保持者인 지주계급의 지지를 획득하지 않고는 금융자본의 지배력을 현실적으로 강화케 하기 어렵다. 그러므로 그들은 米價引上策을 기도하고 그로 인한 약소한 희생은 참고 잇다. 사실, 그 희생이야말로 그들의 막대한 독점적 특별 이윤의 일부를 지주계급에게 던저 주는 것이다.

550萬 石의 增收를 본 朝鮮米의 존재는 日本米價를 一層 더 위협하고 잇다. 平年에도 朝鮮米의 日本移出은 1개년에 六七百萬 石이므로 금년 같은 해에는 그대로 방임하여두면 日本米價는 더욱 慘落할 것이다. 금융자본가는 米價의 低落을 기뻐하나 지주, 부농의 이익을 돌보지 안을 수 없다. 그리하여 朝鮮米 對策-移入 제한이 실시되엇다. 그러치마는 또 한편으로 植民地 朝鮮의 지주의 이익도 全然 무시하여서는 아니된다. 그러므로 일방으로 1,580만원의 저리자금을 大藏省 預金部로부터 융통하여 농업창고, 상업창고의 貯穀에 대하여 貸付하게 하고 또 일방으로는 滿洲粟의 수입을 阻止하여 농민들에게 白米의 소비를 강제하려고 하엿다.
그러나 이러한 대책도 徒勞에 지나지 못하엿다. 低資融通은 일부의 지주 米穀商들에게는 유리하게 이용되엇으나 소작농, 자작농들은 그것을 이용할 여유가 없으므로 일시에 放賣한 米穀은 홍수같이 시장에 넘치어 日本으로 이입된 것만 하여도 11월부터 4월말까지 555만여 石이나 되엇다. 또 지주“¤은 그 秋收米를 평년과 같앗으면 벌서 다 팔앗을 것이나 누구나 아직 正租대로 쌓아 놓고 米價의 상등만 ＜5＞ 기다리고 잇다. 그러므로 이후부터는 朝鮮産米의 약 3할 이상을 점령한 巨量의 地主米가 조금이라도 시세의 유리함을 보면 즉시 매출이 殺到하여 米價를 一層 低落케 하고야 말 것이다. 이러한 無政府性이 자본주의의 본질이다.
