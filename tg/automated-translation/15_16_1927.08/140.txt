Principles of This Technique's Basic Characters

While it is true that the languages of the world's nations differ vastly according to their peoples, they can be fundamentally divided into two types: 1) Ideographic scripts (意符文) and 2) Phonetic scripts (音符文). Thus, it is a fact that human pronunciation has been devised based on some form of shape or sound. For instance, in our Chosŏn, there is "Ka Kya" (Phonetic script); in Japan, "Iroha" (Phonetic script); in China, "Tiāndì Xuánhuáng" (天地玄黃) (Ideographic script); and in Europe and America, "ABC" (Phonetic script). Based on such differing forms, shorthand techniques also had to be devised from national standard characters or ethnic languages so that the shorthand speed could approximate the speed of pronunciation. However, examining the original plans of existing shorthand characters from various countries reveals they are almost all devised from a single circular shape, as if by agreement. Of course, we are latecomers compared to them, but there is absolutely no need for imitation. Therefore, the unique pride of our "Hangeul" adds a touch of 'gilding the lily' [i.e., enhancing something already excellent]. If the original plan from which the basic characters of this technique were derived is illustrated below, it looks much like an umbrella shape.

Original Plan Diagram

If this is further broken down into consonants and vowels, as illustrated, the vowels are arranged as follows: for simple vowels (ㅏ ㅓ ㅗ ㅜ ㅡ ㅣ), four short lines (ㅏ ㅓ ㅗ ㅜ) centered around the dot (`) in Figure 1, plus the inner arc (ㅡ) and outer arc (ㅣ) appearing on the right angle in Figure 2 are prepared. The remaining four long lines (ㅑ ㅕ ㅛ ㅠ) in Figure 1 are prepared as diphthongs. Since diphthongs are combined sounds of "ㅣ" and simple vowels, they are represented by long lines twice the length of the simple vowels.

For consonants, a single circle (360 degrees) is divided into four equal parts (90 degrees), and except for "ㅅ ㅇ ㅎ," they are represented by arcs relative to the right angle. However, since the aspirated consonants (ㅊ ㅋ ㅌ ㅍ) are formed by combining "ㅈ ㄱ ㄷ ㅂ" with "ㅎ," variations of "ㅈ ㄱ ㄷ ㅂ" are prepared by adding a partial form of "ㅎ" to "ㅈ ㄱ ㄷ ㅂ." The remaining consonants are simple consonants (ㅅ is a variation of ㅁ, ㅇ uses the original Hangeul form as is, and ㅎ is formed from the central large semicircle and a half-straight line).

As described above, the basic characters of this technique are distinguished by the size (large, small), length (long, short), and angle inclination of two circular shapes (large and small), straight lines (直), horizontal lines (橫), vertical lines (縱), chords (弦), and arcs (弧). Furthermore, a dot-adding method (加點法) is used to achieve greater clarity, so refer to all the points of caution described below.

Examples of Basic Characters for Shorthand

Below are examples of basic characters for shorthand, followed by essential points of caution concerning them. <48>

Examples of Basic Characters for Shorthand

1.  For the 'Ah' series (아 행) only, use the vowel characters as they are.
2.  For the first character of each series like 'Ka, Na, Ta, Ra...' (each series' first level, upper part), use the consonant character as is, but always write it in the first form (一種形).
3.  For the second character of each series like 'Kya, Nya, Dya, Rya...' (each series' first level, lower part), write it similarly to 'Ka, Na, Ta, Ra...' also in the first form (一種形), but always attach a dot to the right side.
4.  The so-called arae-a (ㆍ) character should be completely abolished.
5.  The "ㅅ" or tense s (된 시옷), like ㄲ ㄸ ㅃ ㅉ, should be written as paired sounds. For instance, since ㄲ is two "ㄱ"s and ㄸ is two "ㄷ"s, use a dot in this sense: for ㄲ, attach a dot (`) to the beginning of ) to make the shape of ",", and for ㄸ, attach a dot (`) to ( to make the shape of "‘".
6.  When consonants like ㄱ ㄴ ㄷ... combine with vowels like ㅏ ㅑ... to form one syllable, write them in the second form (2종형).
7.  When consonants like ㄱ ㄴ ㄷ... change into final consonants (받침 글자), write them in the third form (3종형).
8.  Consonants like ㄷ ㅈ ㅊ ㅋ ㅌ ㅍ should also be used as final consonants (받침 글자), just like ㄱ ㄴ ㅂ ㄹ ㅁ ㅅ. (This means using the entire 'Ah' series [vowel symbols] as relationals, the reason being to save effort and also for convenience in complex writing (複文).)

Explanation:
1.  Dan (段 - Level/Row): The "Ka Kya," "Na Nya," "Ta Dya"... of each series constitute the 1st level; "Kŏ Kyŏ," "Nŏ Nyŏ," "Tŏ Tyŏ"... the 2nd level; "Ko Kyo," "No Nyo," "To Tyo"... the 3rd level; "Kŭ," "Nŭ," "Tŭ," "Rŭ," "Mŭ," "Pŭ"... the 4th level; and "Ki," "Ni," "Ti," "Ri," "Mi," "Pi"... the 5th level. The upper part (上) of each level is designated as 'upper' and the lower part (下) as 'lower.'
2. **Summary**

This article excerpt details the principles behind a new Korean shorthand system developed by Ŏm Chŏng'u in 1927. It explains how the basic symbols for vowels and consonants are derived from geometric shapes (circles, arcs, lines) inspired by, but distinct from, foreign systems and adapted specifically for Hangeul. The system uses variations in size, length, angle, and the addition of dots to represent different sounds, including simple vowels, diphthongs, basic consonants, aspirated consonants, tense consonants, and final consonants (batchim), with specific rules outlined for their application. The author emphasizes the unique suitability of Hangeul for creating an efficient shorthand and mentions plans for a training institute. From a historical perspective, this article is interesting as it reflects Korean efforts during the colonial period to modernize and systematize their own language tools, asserting cultural identity and practicality through Hangeul even while acknowledging foreign influences. The development of a distinctly Korean shorthand system highlights adaptation and innovation within the constraints of the era.

本術 基本文字의 原理
세계 각국의 언어는 그 민족에 딸아 각양 각색으로 판이한 것은 사실이나 그 근본은 이중으로 난울 수 있으니 1은 意符文이요 2는 音符文이다. 이와 같이 인류의 발음은 어떠한 형상 혹은 음향에 의지하여 案出한 것은 사실이다. 곳 우리 조선에서는 「가갸」 (音符文) 일본에서는 「イロハ」 (音符文) 支那에서는 「天地玄黃」 (意符文) 歐米에서는 「ABC」 (音符文) 등으로 이와 같이 各異한 형상에 근본하여 온 것임으로 속기술도 각히 國定 문자 혹은 民族語에서 속기력이 발음의 속도에 가급하도록 案出하지 않으면 안되게 되었다. 그런데 각국 기성 속기문자의 原案을 考案하면 거의 다 의논한 것처럼 一圓形에서 案出되어 있다. 물론 우리는 그들에 비하여 後進이나 그러나 決ㅎ고 모방할 필요가 없다. 그럼으로 우리 「한글」의 독특한 자랑은 금상첨화의 감이 不無하다. 좌에 本術의 기본 문자를 割出한 原案을 도시하면 흡사히 한 傘形과 같이 뵈인다.
原案圖形
이것을 다시 子 母音으로 細別하면 도시와 같이 모음은 1圖의 점 (`)을 중심으로 한 단선 4개(ㅏ ㅓ ㅗ ㅜ)와 2圖의 직각상에 現한 內弧(ㅡ) 外弧(ㅣ)를 합하여 단모음(ㅏ ㅓ ㅗ ㅜ ㅡ ㅣ)을 마련하고 其餘 1圖의 長線 4개(ㅑ ㅕ ㅛ ㅠ)는 중모음으로 마련하였으되 중모음은 「ㅣ」와 단모음의 합음인 까닭에 단모음의 倍大長線으로 표시하였다.
그리고 자음은 1원 (360도)을 4등분 (90도) 하여 「ㅅ ㅇ ㅎ」을 제하고는 직각에 대한 호로써 표하였다. 그런데 혼성 자음(ㅊ ㅋ ㅌ ㅍ)은 「ㅈ ㄱ ㄷ ㅂ」와 「ㅎ」의 합음으로 成한 것임으로 「ㅈ ㄱ ㄷ ㅂ」에 「ㅎ」의 부분형을 합하여 「ㅈ ㄱ ㄷ ㅂ」의 변형적을 마련하였다. 그리고 其餘는 단성자음(ㅅ는 ㅁ의 변형으로 ㅇ은 「한글」의 원형 그래도 ㅎ는 중앙의 대반원과 반직선으로) 으로 마련하였다.
이상과 같이 本術의 기본 문자는 2개의 원형(대 소)과 直, 橫, 縱, 弦, 弧 등의 大小, 長短, 角度의 경사를 구별하여 표시하고 더욱 加點法을 사용하여 일층 명료를 기하였은 즉 下述의 모든 주의를 참고하라.
