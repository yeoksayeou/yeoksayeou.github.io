Article Title: 琵琶記(戱曲) (The Lute Song (Play))
Author: 明代 高則誠(原作), 朝鮮 梁白華(譯補) (Ming Dynasty Gao Zecheng (Original Author), Chosŏn Yang Paekhwa (Translator/Supplementer)) [Note: The text later corrects the author to Gao Ming.]
Article Type: 희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

The Lute Song (P'ip'agi)
Original Author: Ming Dynasty Gao Zecheng, Translator/Supplementer: Chosŏn Yang Paekhwa

Synopsis up to the Previous Issue
Cai Yong (蔡邕) of the Han Dynasty, courtesy name Bojie (伯喈), was born in a time of sage rule and possessed talent for governing, but due to his aging parents, he abandoned his ambitions for high office. He newly married Zhao Wuniang (趙氏 五娘), and they enjoyed family harmony with conjugal bliss and parental well-being. At this time, an imperial summons for talented individuals reached the county, and the Prefect reported Cai Yong's name. Yong wished to fulfill his filial duty to his aging parents and firmly declined. However, due to the urging of his neighbor Elder Chang (張太公) and his father's strict command, he entrusted all matters to Elder Chang, bid farewell to Zhao Wuniang after only two months of marriage, and made the thousand-li journey to the capital (京師). There, he took the examination, passed, and won the highest honor of Zhuangyuan (壯元).
At that time, Prime Minister Niu (牛太師) was renowned for his learning and virtue. Having lost his wife early, he had one daughter who was gentle, virtuous, and deeply cherished by the Prime Minister. Following an imperial decree, he sought to take the Zhuangyuan Cai Yong as his son-in-law.
However, Yong, being a filial son and loyal to his old ties, wished to resign from his post and return to his country home. But his petition was not granted.

Act 17: Relief from the Charity Granary (第17齣 義倉賑濟)
Characters:

Village Headman (里正 - Ijŏng)
Community Leader Yi (李社長 - Yi Sajang)
Grain Distribution Official (放糧官)
Clerk (書吏)
Blind Man (盲人)
Deaf Man (聾者)
Zhao Wuniang (趙五娘 - Cho Onyang)
Father Ch'ae (蔡公 - Ch'ae Kong)
Elder Chang (張太公 - Chang T'aegong)

[Village Headman enters, singing]
Being a Village Headman is truly hard. Called here and there for labor duty (赴役), busy day and night every day. And on top of that, if the officials embezzle from the grain storehouse (官員이 쌀庫를 積奸하면), there won't be a single grain left, and I'll be beaten to death.
As the Village Headman governing the people, I have one method. I dress shabbily in worn-out shoes, a torn hat, and ragged clothes. When I go into the county seat, I bow until my waist <72> touches the ground. But when I return to the village, I suddenly become arrogant. When receiving government grain (官糧), I use a very large measure. When selling salt, I use a faulty scale and weigh it quickly. When assigning labor duty (夫役), I exempt the wealthy and powerful, but call upon the poor and weak, doing as I please. Truly, things in this world don't go as intended. Even if I cheat five or ten nyang (兩), expenses of fifty or a hundred nyang arise, and now all my fields and lands have passed into others' hands, with not even a little bit left. What's vexing is that the village Magistrate (使道) and clerks (吏屬) hound me in various ways, frequently knocking off my hat and beating me until I'm swollen and yellow. I've even thought of hanging myself or drowning myself, but I haven't been able to do that either. Today, an official is coming out to check the granary (積奸), but there isn't a single grain of rice. I'm sure to get beaten severely again. At that time, I plan to shout, "Magistrate, please investigate clearly! I am not the Village Headman! Please do not wrongly beat the people!"
[From offstage] Then who are you, old brother?
Village Headman: I am the fool in this play (演劇).
[From offstage] Don't reveal the whole truth like that.
Village Headman: But what should I do? I've been stealing grain from the granary (倉) to feed my wife (계집) and children, but today they are checking it (積奸), and there's not a grain of rice in the granary (倉). There's nowhere to borrow from, and even if I sell all my household goods (세간) and my wife and children, I can't possibly make up the amount. There's no other way; I must go consult with Community Leader Yi (李社長). - Turning this way and that, I've somehow arrived at Community Leader Yi's house. - Community Leader Yi! Community Leader Yi! Are you there?
Community Leader Yi: Who is calling?
Village Headman: Good grief, putting on airs. - Come out!
[Community Leader Yi enters, singing]
As the community leader (사장), I oversee the official granary (官倉), and my whole family lives by eating granary rice (倉쌀).
Village Headman: Goodness, you say your family eats granary rice? What will you do if it's discovered?
[Community Leader Yi singing]
The Village Headman will be beaten first, so I'm not afraid even if it's discovered.
Village Headman: Do you think you'll be fine with just that?
[Community Leader Yi singing]
First the Village Headman gets hit, then the Community Leader.
I am eighty years old this year. Only three family members live in my house, but since becoming the community leader (사장), I haven't had a peaceful day. I have to go to the county seat to post notices (告牌), encourage farming among the people, repair ditches (開川 修築), maintain roads (길 治道), and even prepare ropes for binding sheaves in the fields. If called for a wedding (婚姻), I must attend. Because I am the eldest (年長), everyone respects me and calls me Sajang. When lawsuits (呈狀) arise, I'm even better than the county clerk (吏房). If the plaintiff (原告) presents me with two or three nyang (兩) of silver (銀子), the defendant (被告) will send 10 or 20 geun (斤) of pork (猪肉). In such cases, I just muddle through writing the judgment (題辭) and that's that. But while I was out overseeing the irrigation work every day, I didn't know disaster had struck my home. My eldest son is utterly unfilial, my daughter-in-law (며누리년) ran away, and my youngest son is so mischievous, always tearing up my headscarf (頭巾). I'm about to burst with anger. Ah well, shall I sing a marketplace tune (場打令) to vent my frustration? <73>
Village Headman: How does it go?
Community Leader Yi: Oh dear, you heard that? Well then, I have no choice but to sing one. You provide the response.
Village Headman: I will.
Community Leader Yi: "To a filial person, a filial child,"
Village Headman: "Eŏyŏra chiŏra, Yŏnhwarak (蓮花落 - Lotus Flower Ballad)!"
Community Leader Yi: "To an unfilial wretch, an unfilial child,"
Village Headman: "Eŏyŏra chiŏra, Yŏnhwarak!"
Community Leader Yi: "If you don't believe me, look at the water dripping from those eaves (簷下),"
Village Headman: "Eŏyŏra chiŏra, Yŏnhwarak!"
Community Leader Yi: "Drip, drip, drip, each drop falls in the very same spot."
Village Headman: "Eŏyŏra chiŏra, Yŏnhwarak!"
Community Leader Yi: Let's stop now.
Village Headman: If you hadn't suggested stopping, Your Honor (영감님), I might have sung all night.
Community Leader Yi: By the way, why did you come looking for me?
Village Headman: Sajang-nim, today the government office (관가) is opening the granary (倉) to give aid to the famine-stricken people (饑民), but there's no rice in the granary. What should we do? Therefore, you and I together must replace however much is needed.
Community Leader Yi: Hey, what are you talking about? You stole all the rice, so why are you telling me to replace it? That was all your doing, so even if the officials come, it's none of my concern. I'll just stay home and play with my grandson. This is what's called, "Closing the gate, I care not for the moon before the window; I leave the plum blossoms to manage themselves (閉門不管窓前月, 一任梅花自主張)."
(Community Leader Yi exits)
Village Headman: Oh, what should I do? Community Leader Yi washes his hands of it and leaves, and the officials will be here soon. What's the best course of action? - Oh, the sound of the officials' procession (喝道ㅅ소리) is getting closer. What can I do but face the beating?
(Grain Distribution Official and Clerk enter)
[Grain Distribution Official singing]
Received the court order (朝命) to relieve the famine (흉년).
[Clerk singing]
Whipping the horse, we hastened to arrive here.
Village Headman: I am the Village Headman (里正).
[Clerk singing]
Quickly go open the granary (倉) and give grain to the pitiful people. Do not dare cheat in the distribution (出納).
Grain Distribution Official: Village Headman, bring the ledger (出納簿).
Village Headman: Here is the ledger.
Grain Distribution Official: (Reads) Previous balance (前在) 29 sŏk (石), Received (收納) 36 sŏk, Distributed (出納) 19 sŏk, Current balance (現在) 46 sŏk. - Hey, open the granary! - Huh, where are 46 sŏk in this granary (倉)?
Village Headman: They are here, they are here, Magistrate (使道)!
Grain Distribution Official: Look here. Get a signed statement (票) from the Village Headman confirming the amount is definitely here, and quickly call the people to give them rice.
Village Headman: "My heart is as hurried as an arrow, my two feet as swift as flight (一心忙似箭, 兩脚疾如飛)."
(Village Headman exits)
Grain Distribution Official: Look here. That fellow lied. Where is the rice in the granary (倉)?
Clerk: Then we shall make the Village Headman compensate for the shortfall.
Grain Distribution Official: Then do so.
[Blind Man enters, singing]
My stomach is shriveled, my eyes are dark too. Not a bit of money <74> do I have. The cries of my little child are unbearable to hear. The Magistrate (使道) has come out to give aid to the starving (긔민), so I too will go get some and save my family.
(The Blind Man mistakenly kneels and clings to the Clerk.)
Blind Man: Magistrate (使道님), please save me!
Clerk: The Magistrate is over there.
Grain Distribution Official: What is your name, and how many are in your family?
Blind Man: My surname is Qiu Ge (邱哥), my given name is Yisi (乙巳). I live in Sangdae Village (上大村), and my family consists of 3,070 people.
Grain Distribution Official: What lies are these? Where could you have so many family members?
Blind Man: I report to the Magistrate. In the children's calligraphy practice book (筆法冊), there is the phrase, "Shang da ren, Qiu Yisi, hua san qian qi shi shi (上大人, 邱乙巳, 化三千七十士)." [Note: This is a nonsensical phrase from a beginner's calligraphy text.]
Clerk: He's talking nonsense.
Grain Distribution Official: How many family members do you really have?
Blind Man: Myself, my wife, and two children.
Grain Distribution Official: Give him enough.
Clerk: Here is the share for four people.
Blind Man: Thank you, Magistrate. This truly is, "If one knows no shame for a day, one cannot endure hunger for three days (一日不識羞면 三日不忍飢)."
(Blind Man exits)
[Deaf Man enters, singing]
Enduring hunger day after day is truly difficult. In my house, five or six family members have collapsed from starvation. Yesterday, I pawned my wife's (마누라) skirt (치마), and today I sold my daughter-in-law's (며누리) trousers (고쟁이). But the Magistrate (使道) has timely come out to give aid to the starving (긔민), oh how good, how good it is.
Magistrate (使道님), please save me!
Grain Distribution Official: What is your name, and how many are in your family?
(The Deaf Man cannot hear, so the Grain Distribution Official asks again.)
Deaf Man: My surname is Da Ge (大哥), my name is Bhikkhu Monk (比丘僧). I live in the Jetavana-Anathapindika Garden (祇樹給孤獨園), and my family consists of 1,250 people.
Grain Distribution Official: What nonsensical talk is this? Where could there be so many?
Deaf Man: I inquire of the Magistrate. In the Amitabha Sutra (彌陀經), it is said, "In the Jetavana-Anathapindika Garden, with a great Bhikkhu sangha of 1,250 monks (祇樹給孤獨園에 與大 比丘僧 千二百五十人으로 俱하시다)." [Note: He is citing the opening lines of the sutra, listing the Buddha's assembly.]
Clerk: Buddha's words, snake's heart (佛口蛇心). [Meaning hypocritical]
Grain Distribution Official: How many really?
Deaf Man: In my house, there are two daughters-in-law (며누리), three small children, and myself, making six family members in total (모도).
Grain Distribution Official: Oh, give him [enough].
Clerk: Here! Share for six people. Take it.
Deaf Man: Thank you, Magistrate. As they say, "Today, having received your help, I am saved from falling into the mire (今日得君提掇起하야 免敎人在汚泥中이옵니다)."
(Deaf Man exits)
[Zhao Wuniang enters, singing]
My fate (命途) is harsh (崎薄), and now even famine strikes? Swallowing my shame (붓그러움), hiding my tears, I go before people to beg (구걸). My heart aches, wondering if my parents-in-law are waiting.
"When encountering danger on the road, it's hard to turn back; when matters come to a head, one is not free (路逢險處難廻避요. 事到頭來不自由로다)." Since childhood, I grew up within the women's quarters (閨門), so I don't know the roads well. But today, hearing that the government office (관가) is distributing grain (곡물) to aid the starving (긔민), I intend to get a little to give to my parents-in-law.
(She sees the Grain Distribution Official.)
Grain Distribution Official: Who are you, and what have you come for? (75)
Zhao Wuniang: I report to the Magistrate (使道). My name is Zhao Wuniang (趙五娘), and my father-in-law (시아비) is called Ch'ae Chonggan (蔡從簡). However, my husband (지아비) has gone away and is not home, so I came to get a little rice to keep my parents-in-law alive.
Grain Distribution Official: Where has your husband gone that a woman like you (녀편네) has come to ask for rice?
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
My husband is currently staying in the capital (서울).
Grain Distribution Official: Who else is in your house?
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
Only my elderly parents-in-law (싀부모님) are there.
Grain Distribution Official: Do you have no brothers-in-law?
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
There are no siblings (同氣) at all.
Grain Distribution Official: If there are no brothers, who looks after the parents?
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
It is all I who serve (밧들어 들이다) them.
Grain Distribution Official: In that case, it must be very difficult for you. Women are not supposed to go outside the house carelessly, so why didn't you send a man to get the rice?
[Zhao Wuniang singing sorrowfully]
Everyone is dying; who would help me? Magistrate, please listen. The saying that women do not leave the women's quarters (閨門) is idle talk from peaceful times (太平한 시절).
Grain Distribution Official: How many family members are in your household?
Zhao Wuniang: Only three.
Grain Distribution Official: Hey, give her rice.
Clerk: There is no more rice now.
[Zhao Wuniang singing, weeping]
If there is no rice, I cannot go home either.
Grain Distribution Official: Why do you say you cannot go home?
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
Magistrate, please listen. How can I bear to see my parents-in-law starve? Is my fate (八字) truly so cruel?
Grain Distribution Official: Listen. The reason the rice in this granary (倉) has already run out is that the original count (石數) was incorrect and deficient. Also, this woman's situation (情境) is pitiful. Go quickly, arrest the Village Headman, bring him here, and make him replace the missing amount (물어노케 하여라).
Clerk: Yes, sir. "Even if he runs to the heaven of Yama (燄摩天), I shall ride the clouds beneath my feet and surely catch him (假饒走到燄摩天이라도, 脚下騰雲須趕上호리다)."
(Clerk exits)
Zhao Wuniang: Magistrate, please, somehow give me even a tiny bit (죡음아치라두) of rice so I can sustain my parents-in-law's lives.
Grain Distribution Official: I will arrange something for you.
(Clerk enters, having arrested the Village Headman)
Clerk: I caught him as easily as catching a turtle in a jar (독속에 자라).
Grain Distribution Official: Village Headman! The shortage of rice in this granary (倉) is because you stole it. Confess the truth immediately.
Village Headman: Magistrate. I cannot confess. As the old saying goes, "If you gain in the east, you lose in the west." You cannot make me replace it (물어노흐라고는 못하십니다).
Grain Distribution Official: You wicked fellow! No matter how carefully you managed the measuring bowls (되) for intake and outflow, the difference could not be this large. Hey! Give this man 40 lashes (40度).
Village Headman: Please spare my life! I will confess (自服).
Village Headman: (Reads the confession - 供招文) "Declarant (招狀人): Surname Mao (貓 - Cat), Name Li (貍 - Raccoon dog). Age over 30 <76> this year. No particular illnesses, but suffers from leucorrhea (白帶). Regarding the deficiency (欠虧) in government grain (官糧) and the corruption (情弊) in the charity granary (義倉), there is nothing suspicious about it. When the grain ripens, the gates are opened to receive it. After receiving, each person takes their share home; there is no practice of storing it in the granary (倉). Therefore, there can be no ledger (帳目) of income and expenditure. Even if a tiny amount (조곰아치) exists, it is just sent to private houses. When an official sends someone to check the granary (積奸), this rice is put into the granary (倉) to deceive them. The people above and below just get money, and that's it; they never ask if the granary (倉) is full or empty. If an honest official (청렴한 관원) comes, I temporarily hide myself (影子를 숨기고), borrow 10 k'ang (扛) [pole-loads] from the house to the east, and take 5 ki (箕) [basketfuls] from the house to the west. Then there is grain in the granary (倉), so outsiders do not know the secret behind it. This has been the annual custom (年年의 상례), carried out like a game (작난가티). This year, due to the unexpected drought (旱災) and the distribution of rice (施米), the secrets of heaven (天機) were inadvertently revealed. Even if reported to the thirty-three heavens (三十三天에 奏聞), this is not the Village Headman's crime."
Grain Distribution Official: Why not?
Village Headman: (Reads) "Those who use checking the granary grain (倉穀積奸) as a pretext to take money (금전) shall become horses and mules. This is not a lie. I hope you will dispose of this matter justly."
Grain Distribution Official: Look here. Take this fellow into custody (押領) and make him replace the grain (물어노케 하여라).
Clerk: This is what they call, "Fearing the law brings daily joy; deceiving the public brings daily sorrow (懼法朝朝樂이요 欺公日日憂라)."
(Clerk leads Village Headman away under arrest (押送). Re-enters with him in custody (押領).)
Clerk: "Even if human hearts were like iron, how could they escape the law, which is like a furnace (假使 인심이 철갓다 하드래도 어떠케 관법이 爐가튼 것을 버서나랴)?" Reporting. The Village Headman has replaced the rice (물어노핫습니다).
Grain Distribution Official: Then give it to that woman.
Zhao Wuniang: Thank you, Magistrate.
(Clerk gives [rice] to Zhao Wuniang. Village Headman watches furtively (倫觀).)
Village Headman: (Aside, facing away) Take it if you want. I'll just snatch it back on the way.
Zhao Wuniang: "Thank you, gracious official, for your decisive help (謝得恩官有主維합내다)."
Village Headman: "[I'll] just ensure disaster befalls you on the way (只敎中途有災危호리라)."
Grain Distribution Official: "If those in power are reluctant to act kindly (當權苦不行方便이면),"
Clerk: "It's like entering a treasure mountain and returning empty-handed (如入寶山空山回니라)."
(Grain Distribution Official, Clerk, Village Headman exit)
Zhao Wuniang: Everything happens according to predetermination (前定). Today I came to get rice and found the Village Headman engaged in wicked deeds, and there was no rice. If the Magistrate hadn't ordered the Village Headman to replace it, I wouldn't have been able to get rice to take back to my parents-in-law. "Getting a spoonful when hungry is better than getting a mal [unit of volume] when full (배고풀 때에 한술을 엇는 것이 배불을 때에 한말을 엇는 것보다 낫다는 것)" – this is it.
(As Zhao Wuniang is about to exit, the Village Headman enters)
Village Headman: (Blocking Zhao Wuniang) "Benefactors meeting see each other more clearly; enemies meeting glare more fiercely." I've been waiting for you. Give back that rice from before.
Zhao Wuniang: The Magistrate gave me this rice, so I cannot give it to you.
Village Headman: What? If you hadn't pleaded like that before the Magistrate, why would he have ordered me to replace it (무러노라겟소)? That rice was bought by selling my wife (녀편네) and household goods (세간), so I cannot give it to you.
(Tries to snatch it)
Zhao Wuniang: Please don't be so forceful (억택). Please consider my family's difficult situation with pity (가엽시).
Village Headman: What? You talk about pity? <77>
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
My husband went away and hasn't returned. My elderly parents-in-law have starved from yesterday to today.
Village Headman: What concern is it of mine if your parents-in-law starve?
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
I came out to get rice; my elderly parents-in-law are at home, just waiting for my return. Thinking of my old parents-in-law, please forgive me [let me keep the rice].
(Zhao Wuniang bows to the Village Headman.)
Village Headman: Don't bow. Why are you bowing? In this situation, what can I do? Now, hand over the rice (쌀 돌우내우).
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
Village Headman (里正님), Village Headman. Please take pity (불상히 녀기소서). This rice is the lifeline (명맥) of my parents-in-law. If you insist on taking it away (아서를 가시려 하시거든), then please take the clothes I am wearing instead.
(Zhao Wuniang starts to take off her clothes.)
Village Headman: No, no. You'll be cold too.
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
Better that my body suffers the cold (추음); I must save the remaining lives of my parents-in-law.
Village Headman: Hey there. Stop it. Your words, born from such filial piety (효심), are too pitiful to bear hearing. It doesn't matter, just take it and go.
Zhao Wuniang: Then I am truly grateful.
(The Village Headman pretends to leave but hides himself - 쉼긴다.)
Zhao Wuniang: Ah, Heaven (하누님) and Earth (땅님). Thank you. Goodness, the Village Headman has gone that way too. I must hurry home.
(The Village Headman suddenly appears, snatches the rice from Wuniang, and exits)
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
How cruel, how heart-wrenching (긔막혀라)! The food has been snatched away. My parents-in-law are waiting only for my return. Even if they do not blame me, as a daughter-in-law and child, what kind of duty is this? If my parents-in-law starve, the blame will fall on my husband (지아비게). With what face can I meet my husband's gaze (낫 대하올까)?
Since I must die sometime anyway (이차비), it's better to die quickly. Goodness, here is an old well (움물). Alright. Let me drown myself here.
(About to jump into the well)
[Singing]
As I leap to throw myself into the well, I reconsider, and everything seems difficult. When my husband left me, he earnestly charged me (천만당부) with caring for his parents. If I die, won't my husband be lonely? And won't my parents-in-law resent me from beyond?
[Father Ch'ae enters, singing]
My daughter-in-law (며누리애) went out and hasn't returned; the waiting person's eyes are about to fall out (눈하마 빠지겟네).
(Father Ch'ae trips on something and falls. Zhao Wuniang helps him up.)
[Father Ch'ae singing]
Why did you delay your steps here, making me wait and worry so much?
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
Father (아버님), Father. Please listen to me. The food I obtained by begging to serve you (낫진지 해들이랴) has been cruelly snatched away by someone else.
Father Ch'ae: What do you mean? <78>
Zhao Wuniang: As I was bringing the rice I received, the Village Headman snatched it away midway.
Father Ch'ae: Oh, is that so? Heaven help us (하누님 맙시사)!
[Father Ch'ae weeping, singing]
Why is my fate (八字) so wretched? Tears just flow endlessly. Perhaps I am destined to starve to death in the end. Better to quickly depart for the Yellow Springs (황천길) and relieve you of this painful worry.
Daughter-in-law (며눌애), listen. Listen, daughter-in-law. Your old mother-in-law, who has only a short time left, please take good care of her (아모쪼록 잘 밧들어 섬기어라) for me.
Oh, here is an old well (움물). Let me jump in and die.
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
Father, if you were to pass away (돌아를 가오시면), how could my body bear the grief (긔막힘이)? If you also pass away, Father, how would Mother survive? If both of you pass away at once (一朝에), how could I live on alone?
Father. No matter how hard life is, I am deeply saddened and earnestly plead with you (구지구지 諫합내다).
[Father Ch'ae singing]
You pawned all your clothes for food, and the jewelry boxes (노리개 그릇) are empty too. Even if I live for a short while longer, how long can this last? Your efforts will all be in vain (허사). When there are no clothes and food, even filial piety is difficult (어련게니). It would be better if you and I parted ways sooner (허여짐만 못하도다).
(Father Ch'ae tries to jump into the well. Zhao Wuniang saves him.)
[Elder Chang enters carrying rice. Singing]
It's not a year of abundance but famine, and officials come out to give aid to the starving (飢民). Looking from afar, an old man is sighing constantly over there. What's the matter? Let me go and ask in detail.
Oh, I wondered who it was! It's old Mr. Ch'ae and Zhao Wuniang.
[Singing]
What are you doing here?
Zhao Wuniang: Sir. How can I possibly tell you everything? Today, hearing that the official was giving aid to the starving (飢民), I went to get some to relieve my parents-in-law's hunger (싀장하신 것). But the Village Headman (里正) did wrong, and the rice ran short. Fortunately, the Magistrate (使道) ordered the Village Headman to replace it (물어노라고) and gave that rice to me. As I was bringing it back, the Village Headman lay in wait (장마지를 하엿다가) midway and snatched the rice I had. So, although I was angry, there was nothing I could do, and I came back empty-handed. When my father-in-law heard this, he said he would drown himself in the well, and I am just now trying to dissuade him (諫하는 판이옵니다).
Elder Chang: Hey now, that was unnecessary. I also went to the government office (관가) just now to get rice. I intended to share this with you. Why did you go alone and suffer insults from those fellows without even discussing it with me?
[Singing]
I'll chase down that wicked fellow and give him a piece of my mind (흠벅 욕을 뵈이리라).
Zhao Wuniang: That person is already far away. <79>
Father Ch'ae: Please, Elder Chang (太公). Let it be. What good would it do for us to punish such a lawless fellow? - But we have been starving for several days (몃칠) and cannot endure it.
[Elder Chang singing]
Hey now. Don't worry so much. I have this rice I received. Let's divide it in half (半식) and take it home.
Zhao Wuniang: How can we accept what you received, Your Honor (영감님)?
[Elder Chang singing]
Don't refuse so much; please take this (바드시요). Let's cook rice for both households for a short while.
Zhao Wuniang: Then, shamelessly, I will accept it (염치불고하고 밧겟습니다).
Elder Chang: What are you saying? When the Scholar (秀才 - referring to Ch'ae Yong) went to the capital (서울), he earnestly entrusted (신신부탁) his parents to my care. In such a famine, should I just stand by and watch while you struggle alone? The old saying goes, "When helping people, help those in urgent need." Don't hesitate; quickly take this and go save your parents-in-law. Anyway, you go home first. I will slowly follow behind with your father-in-law.
[Zhao Wuniang singing]
Nothing is left in the house; they only rely on me. How can I hesitate in the hardship of saving my parents-in-law?
[Chorus - 合唱]
Suppressing tears, enduring hunger, this suffering is truly beyond words.
[Father Ch'ae singing]
I would have died already, but my daughter-in-law saved me. A life soon to end, how can I repay this kindness?
[Chorus - 合唱]
Same as above.
[Elder Chang singing]
I cannot bear to hear those words. Don't speak so grievously. I live next door to your house; how could I live comfortably alone and pretend not to see your starvation?
[Chorus - 合唱]
Same as above.

Correction Note regarding the Author of "Pipa Ji" (琵琶記)
For a long time, the original author of this Pipa Ji was known as Gao Zecheng (高則誠) of the Yuan Dynasty (元代). However, it has now been discovered that he is Gao Ming (高明) of the Ming Dynasty (明代), and thus a correction (訂正) is made here.
Gao Ming (高明) (Ming 明), native of Pingyang (平陽人). Courtesy name Zecheng (字則誠). Passed the imperial examination in the Zhizheng era of the Yuan (元至正進士). Appointed Recorder (錄事) of Chuzhou (處州). Later served in the Signatory Office (辟氶署像). Afterwards, sojourned in the Yili Society (鄞櫟社). Emperor Taizu (太祖) heard of his fame and summoned him. He declined due to old age and illness and returned home. His work, Pipa Ji (琵琶記), is considered the crown of Southern Drama (南曲之冠). He also wrote the Rou Ke Zhai Ji (柔克齋集). (Note on the author of Pipa Ji: Ancients often considered it to be Gao Shi (高拭). Jiang Yiyao's (蔣一堯) Yaoshan Waiji (堯山外記) states the author of Pipa Ji was Gao Shi (高拭), courtesy name Zecheng (字則誠). Zhu Yizun's (朱彛尊) Jingzhiju Shihua (靜志居詩話) cites this and further adds that the Hanxu Yu (涵虛于) [likely a collection or catalog] lists Gao Shi but not Gao Ming. Wang Shizhen's (王世貞) Cangyuan Zhiyan (藏苑巵言) also says, 'Southern Drama Gao Shi Zecheng (南曲高拭則誠) surpassed all before and after.' This shows that Ming people uniformly regarded Zecheng as Shi. However, the Yuan edition of Zhang Xiaoshan's (張小山) Beiqu Lianle Bu (北曲聯樂部) in three volumes has prefatory inscriptions by Haisu Feng Zizhen (海粟馮子振) and [?]shan Gao Shi (*山高拭). This is the Gao Shi in the Hanxu Yu Qu Pu (涵虛于曲譜 - Hanxu Yu Song Collection). The Wenzhou Prefecture Gazetteer (溫州府志) states: Gao Ming (高明), courtesy name Zecheng (字則誠), sojourned at the Shen family tower (沈氏樓) of the Li Society (櫟社) in this region, where he wrote Pipa Ji. Thus, the author of Pipa Ji was clearly Gao Ming of Yongjia (永嘉), and cannot be confused as the same person.) (Source: Zhongguo Renming Da Cidian - 中國人名大辭典 - Great Dictionary of Chinese Names) <80>

**Summary**

This excerpt from Act 17 of "The Lute Song" depicts the trials of Zhao Wuniang during a famine. While attempting to secure relief grain for her starving parents-in-law, she encounters corrupt local officials (the Village Headman and Community Leader Yi) who embezzle supplies. Although a higher official orders the Headman to replace the stolen grain and gives it to Wuniang, the Headman ambushes her and steals it back. Despairing, Wuniang contemplates suicide, and her father-in-law, upon hearing the news, also attempts to end his life. They are saved by the intervention of their kind neighbor, Elder Chang, who shares the grain he received. The act highlights themes of extreme filial piety, official corruption, desperation caused by famine, and neighborly kindness.

From a colonial history perspective, the publication of this classic Chinese play, translated into Korean in 1927, is noteworthy. It reflects continued cultural engagement with China despite Japanese rule. The strong emphasis on Confucian values like filial piety (Zhao Wuniang's devotion) might have been seen as reinforcing traditional ethics, potentially aligning with or subtly critiquing aspects of colonial modernity. Furthermore, the depiction of corrupt local officials exploiting the populace during hardship could have resonated deeply with Korean audiences experiencing their own forms of official abuse and economic difficulty under the colonial administration, offering an indirect social commentary. The detailed scholarly note correcting the author's identity also indicates a serious intellectual engagement with Chinese literary history among Korean translators/intellectuals of the period.

前號까지의 梗槪
漢의 蔡邕은 字는 伯喈라 聖朝의 時에 태어나 經世의 才를 품엇지마는 父母가 늙으신 까닭으로 靑雲의 뜻을 단념하고 새로히 趙氏 五娘을 娶하여 夫婦和順 父母康寧으로 一家의 團樂을 즐겨하였다. 이때 고을에 招賢하는 글ㅅ발이 이르러 太守가 蔡邕의 이름을 報하니 邕은 늙으신 父母에게 효성을 다하고저 하여 이를 固辭하엿스나 隣家의 張太公의 권고와 父親의 嚴命으로 萬事를 張太公에게 부탁하고 新婚 2개월의 趙五娘과 작별하고 千里의 行을 作하여 京師에 이르러 應試에 及第하야 壯元의 榮冠을 엿다.
當時 丞相 牛太師는 學德이 一世에 뛰어났섯다. 일즉이 부인을 여희고 한 딸이 잇는데 溫柔貞淑하여 太師의 鍾愛가 비상하든 차 聖旨를 밧들어 壯元 蔡邕을 사위로 마즈러 하엿다.
그러나 邕은 孝子요. 또 舊에 厚함으로 벼슬을 고만두고 시골집으로 돌아 가려하엿다. 그러나 그 上疏는 允許가 되지 안하엿다.

네 옷은 죄다버서 잡혀를 먹구서 노리개 그릇도 텅비고 말핫스니 내 설사 잠간동안 산다를 하야도 이것이 어떠케 오래를 가을소냐. 네 마음, 쓰는 것도 다 허사가 되리로다. 옷과 밥이 업슬 때는 효성도 어련게니 너와 나완 진즉에 허여짐만 못하도다.
(蔡公이 움물로 뛰어들려한다. 趙五娘이 구한다.)
「張太公이 쌀을 지고 등장. 唱」
풍년이 아니라 흉년이 들어서 관원이 나와서 飢民을 주노나. 멀리로 바라보니 늙으니 하나가 거긔서 짜꾸만 탄식을 하고 잇네. 무슨 일로 그리하나 가서 자세 물어보쟈.
어 난, 누군가 햇더니 蔡노인과 趙五娘이구료.
「唱」
그대들은 여긔서 무엇을 하시는가.
趙五娘. 여봅시요. 이로 말슴을 어찌 다 여쭙겟습니까. 제가 오늘 관원이 飢民을 준다는 말을 듯고 가서 좀 어더다가 부모님의 싀장하신 것을 면해 들이고자 하엿더니 里正이 못된 짓을 하여 쌀이 부족하겟지요. 그런데 다행이 使道께서 里正에게 물어노라고 호령을 하서서 그 쌀을 나를 주섯서요. 그래 내가 그 쌀을 가지고 오는데 도중에서 里正이 장마지를 하엿다가 내 가진 쌀을 빼아서 갑니다그려. 해서 나는 분하나 하는 수 업서 그냥 왓는데 아버님께서 이 말슴을 드르시고 움물에 빠저 죽는다고 하시기에 제가 못하시게 지금 諫하는 판이옵니다.
張太公. 여보오, 그건 공연히 그랫구료. 나도 지금 관가로 쌀을 어드러 갓섯소마는 이건 당신하구 노나 먹으랴고 그랫는데 그런 일을 나에게 의논도 아니하고 혼자가서 그 놈들에게 욕을 본단 말이요.
「唱」
그 못된 놈 내 쪼차가 흠벅 욕을 뵈이리라.
趙五娘. 그 사람은 벌서 멀리 갓습니다.＜79＞
蔡公. 여보시요 太公. 그만두시요. 우리가 그 까진 무도한 놈을 탄해서 뭘 한단말이요.- 그런데 우리는 몃칠을 굶어서 견댈수가 업소.
「張太公 唱」
여보시요. 그러케 걱정을 마오시오. 이 내가 어더온 쌀이랑 그 잇스니 둘이서 한 半식 노나서 가읍시다.
趙五娘. 영감님께서 어더 오신 것을 어떠케 밧습니까.
「張太公 唱」
그다지 사양말고 이것을 바드시요. 잠시동안 두 집의 밥지어 먹읍시다.
趙五娘. 그러면 염치불고하고 밧겟습니다.
張太公. 그게 무슨 말슴이요. 秀才가 서울에를 갈 때에 부모님을 내게다 신신부탁을 하엿는데 이러한 흉년에 당신만 혼자 애를 쓰게하고 내가 가만히 보고 잇단 말이요. 옛말에도 사람을 구할 때에 급한 것을 구하라 햇스니 어려워 말고 어서 가지고 가서 부모님을 구해 들이시요. 엇쨋든 당신은 먼저 집으로 가시요. 나는 아버님과 뒤딸하 천천히 가겟소.
「趙五娘 唱」
집안에 남은 것은 도모지 업는데 다만지 이 나만 바라고 게시도다. 부모님 구하랸 맘 고생 어찌 사양할가.
「合唱」
눈물을 억제하고 주림을 참으니 이 고생 참으로 말할 수 업구야.
「蔡公 唱」
내 벌서 죽엇슬걸 며누리가 구하엿네. 얼마안야 죽을 목숨 이 은혜를 어찌 갑나.
「合唱」
同上
「張太公 唱」
그 말 참아 못 듯겟네. 너모 괴탄 말으시요. 그대 집 이웃에 내 살아잇스니 이 나만 어찌서 편안히 지내고 그대의 주리는 걸 모른체 하오리까.
「合唱」
同上.

高明 (明) 平陽人. 字則誠. 元至正進士. 授處州錄事. 辟氶署像. 後旅寓鄞櫟社. 太祖聞其名. 召之. 以老病辭還. 所著琵琶記. 爲南曲之冠. 又有柔克齋集. (按作琵琶記者. 古人多以爲高拭. 蔣一堯堯山*外記. 謂作琵琶記者乃高拭. 字則誠. 朱彛尊靜志居詩話引之. 復云涵虛于** 有高拭而無高明. 王世貞藏苑巵言亦云. 南曲高拭則誠. 遂掩前後. 是明人均以則誠爲拭也. 然元刊張小山北曲聯樂部三卷. 前有海粟馮子振. *山高拭題詞. 卽涵虛于曲譜中之高拭. 溫州府志. 高明. 字則誠. 旅寓邦之櫟社沈氏樓. 因作琵琶記. 是作琵琶記者. 自爲永嘉之高明. 不容混爲一人也.)(中國人名大辭典)＜80＞
