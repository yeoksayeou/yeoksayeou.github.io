**Body Text Translation:**
This Year's World of Science, Nature

Discovery of the Pathogen for Cancer
Dr. Gye and Mr. Bernard of Britain (英國), after researching cancer for many years, succeeded in discovering its pathogen and announced the process leading up to the discovery at the British Association for Science (英國 科學協會) on August 29th of last year. According to their announcement, Mr. Bernard photographed the microbe isolated by Dr. Gye using a method of passing ultraviolet light (紫外光線) through the side of a microscope (顯微鏡), magnifying the minuscule so-called filterable pathogen (瀘過病源軆) 3,000 times to capture it on film, leading to this discovery. It is expected that due to this major discovery, the treatment methods for cancer will greatly advance in the future.

Excavation of Nangnang Tombs
Last summer, rare fragments (破片) of lacquerware (漆器) from the Three Kingdoms Period (三國時代) were excavated from the Nangnang Tombs (樂浪古墳) located in the outskirts of our P'yŏngyang (平壤). The lacquerware is an ancient artifact (古物) from 3,000 years ago. However, as it is a valuable item where even the year name (年號) and the maker's signature (署名) remain clearly visible, it attracted the attention (主意) of archaeologists (考古學者). In September of the same year, saying "Look at this!", a group (一團) composed of doctors and nobles (貴族) from Tokyo (東京) organized the Nangnang Tomb Excavation Team, came over (渡來) to Chosŏn (朝鮮), and discovered many valuable relics. It is said that many historical facts will be clarified (闡明) following further detailed (仔細히) research on these items.

American's Mongolian Expedition
Mr. Andrews (안드류스氏), leader of the American Asia Expedition Team (美國亞洲 探險隊), who has been engaged in the Mongolian (蒙古) expedition (探險)事業 for the third time since March of last year, returned safely to Beijing (北京) on September 16th of the same year. According to his account, the distance traversed (踏破) by the party was 5,000 miles (哩). In the Mongolian interior, they witnessed (目擊) herds of several hundred wild asses (驢馬), groups of tens of thousands of reindeer (馴鹿), discovered 15,000 items including stone tools (石器) and jade (璧玉) ornaments (裝飾物) of ancient humans, and found skulls (頭蓋骨) of humans even more ancient than the Aryan race (아리안族) and over 40 fossilized (化石) dinosaur (恐龍) eggs (卵). Among these, the skulls of sand dune (砂丘) dwellers and relics from that era, along with skulls of horned animals (有角動物) belonging to ungulates (有蹄 動物), are expected to become extremely beneficial materials for research in anthropology, biology, and other sciences.
Furthermore, the party planned to engage in the fourth Mongolian expedition this year, adding paleontologist Dr. Matthew (매듀博士) and geologist Mr. Berkey (덱그윗츠氏), preparing 8 expedition automobiles, and departed Beijing (北京) on April 15th. It is believed they will return having obtained many valuable materials this time as well.

Success in Arctic Exploration
1926 was a year when the fervor for Arctic exploration (北極探險) reached its peak. First, Major Byrd (뻐드少佐) of Britain [Note: Byrd was American], supported by the wealthy British [Note: American] Mr. Ford (포드氏) and Mr. Rockefeller (락키펠러氏), departed New York (뉴욕) with a party of 46 on April 5th, reached Spitsbergen (스피츠벨겐), left Kings Bay (킹스베이) by airplane on May 9th, reached the North Pole (北極), circled (旋回) over it three times, and returned (歸還) safely to Kings Bay (킹스베이). Next, the veteran (耆宿) of the exploration world, Mr. Amundsen (아문젠氏) of Norway (놀웨), with the help of Colonel Nobile (노빌레 大佐), the captain of the Italian (이탈리) airship (飛行船) Norge (놀게號), departed Rome (로마) on April 7th, arrived in Spitsbergen (스피츠벨겐) on May 7th, made preparations at Miller (밀러), left Kings Bay (킹스베이) at 10 AM on May 11th, arrived over the North Pole (北極) at 1 AM on the 12th, and subsequently crossed (橫斷) it heading towards Nome (노-ㅁ), Alaska (알라스까). Although there was concern as news ceased thereafter, they arrived at Teller (텔러), Alaska (알라스카) at 2 AM on the 15th, successfully completing the Arctic crossing. According to his announcement, north of 82 degrees 30 minutes North latitude, there are no signs (形迹) of animals (獸類) inhabiting (棲息), and no land (陸地) could be seen at the North Pole. An Australian (濠洲人), Mr. Wilkins (윌킨스氏), also headed north from Point Barrow (포인트 배로), Alaska (알라스카) on April 9th, but later returned to Fairbanks (폐어뱅크스), Alaska (알라스카), having been unsuccessful.<34>

**Summary**

This 1926 news article covers four major scientific and exploration achievements from the previous year (1925) and ongoing efforts in 1926. It reports on the discovery of a potential cancer pathogen by British researchers Gye and Bernard, the excavation of 3,000-year-old lacquerware from Nangnang tombs near P'yŏngyang by a Japanese team, the extensive findings of Roy Chapman Andrews' third American expedition in Mongolia (including ancient human remains, dinosaur eggs, and vast wildlife sightings), and successful Arctic explorations by air, specifically Major Byrd's flight over the North Pole and Amundsen and Nobile's trans-Arctic airship crossing in the Norge, alongside Wilkins' unsuccessful attempt. For historians of colonial Korea, the section on the Nangnang Tomb excavation is particularly interesting. It highlights Japanese archaeological activities on the peninsula, mentioning the involvement of "doctors and nobles" from Tokyo, and uses slightly informal or critical language ("이것 봐라 하고," "하이들이"), potentially hinting at a Korean perspective or subtle resistance towards the Japanese appropriation and study of Korean heritage during the colonial period. The framing suggests awareness and perhaps resentment of these external activities concerning Korean historical sites.

잡지명	동광 제8호
발행일	1926년 12월 01일
기사제목	今年의 科學世界, 自然
기사형태	소식

今年의 科學世界, 自然
癌의 病源體 發見
英國 「지」-博士와 「버나드」氏는 多年間 癌에 대하여 硏究하여 오던 중 그 病源体 發見에 成功하여 작년 8월 29일에 英國 科學協會에서 發見하기까지의 經路를 發表하엿다. 그 發表한 바에 의하면 「지-」博士의 分解한 病菌을 「버나드」氏가 顯微鏡 여프로 紫外光線을 통하는 방법으로 攝影하고 微小한 소위 瀘過病源軆를 3,000배나 늘이어 사진을 박아서 이것을 발견하게 되엇다는데 이 일대 발견으로 인하여 今後 癌의 治療法은 크게 進展되리라고 期待된다.

樂浪古墳發掘
우리 平壤 郊外에 있는 樂浪古墳으로부터 작년 여름에 珍奇한 三國時代의 漆器 破片이 發掘되엇다. 그 漆器는 3,000년 전의 古物이다. 그러나 年號와 製作者의 署名까지 똑똑히 볼 수 잇게 남아 잇는 귀중한 것임으로 考古學者의 主意를 이끄는 바 同年 9월에는 이것 봐라 하고 東京으로부터 博士이니 貴族이니 하이들이 一團으로 樂浪古墳發掘隊를 組織하여 가지고 朝鮮에 渡來하여 만흔 갑진 遺物을 발견하엿다. 今後 다시 이것을 仔細히 硏究함을 딸아서 만흔 史實이 闡明하게 되리라고 한다.

美人의 蒙古探險
작년 3월부터 제3회째나 蒙古探險事業에 종사하고 잇는 美國亞洲 探險隊長 「안드류스」氏 一行은 同年 9월 16일에 무사히 北京에 돌아왓다. 그의 말한 바에 의하면 一行의 踏破한 거리는 5,000哩, 蒙古內地에서 幾百頭의 野生하는 驢馬의 群, 數萬頭의 馴鹿의 群等을 目擊하고 古代 人類의 石器와 璧玉의 裝飾物 等 15,000個를 發見하고 아리안族보다도 더욱 古代 人類의 頭蓋骨과 40有餘의 恐龍의 알(卵)의 化石 등을 발견하엿다는데 그 중 砂丘上 거주자의 두개골 밋 그 時代의 遺物, 有蹄 動物에 속한 有角動物의 두개골 등은 인류학 생물학 其他 科學 硏究에 非常히 有益한 材料가 되리라고 한다.
또한 一行은 今年에 다시 제4회 蒙古探險에 종사할 양으로 化石學者 「매듀」博士 地質學者 「덱그윗츠」氏를 加하여 探險用 自動車 8台를 豫備하여 가지고 4월 15일 北京을 出發하엿다는데 今回에도 귀중한 재료를 만히 어더가지고 돌아오리다고 밋는다.

北極探險成功
1926년은 北極探險熱이 絶項에 달한 해니 먼저 英國의 「뻐드」少佐는 英國 富者 「포드」씨와 「락키펠러」氏의 後援으로 一行 46명이 4월 5일 뉴욕을 出帆하여 「스피츠벨겐」에 이르러 5월 9일 飛行機로 「킹스베이」를 떠나 北極에 이르러 그 위를 세 번 旋回하고 무사히 「킹스베이」에 歸還하엿고 다음에는 探險界의 耆宿인 놀웨의 「아문젠」氏는 이탈리의 飛行船 「놀게」號에 그 船長 「노빌레」 大佐의 도음으로 4월 7일 로마를 떠나 5월 7일 「스피츠벨겐」에 도착 「밀러」에서 準備를 하여 가지고 5월 11일 朝 10時 「킹스베이」를 떠나 12일 오전 1時 北極의 頭上에 도착하고 이어서 이를 橫斷하며 알라스까의 「노-ㅁ」으로 向하엿스나 其後 消息이 업서 근심되다가 15일 오전 2時 알라스카 「텔러」에 도착하여 北極 橫斷에 成功하엿다. 氏의 發表에 의하면 北緯 82度 30分 以北에는 獸類가 서식하는 形迹이 업고 북극에는 陸地를 볼 수 업다 한다. 濠洲人 「윌킨스氏」도 4월 9일 알라스카 「포인트 배로」에서 北向하엿스나 其後 알라스카 「폐어뱅크스」에 돌아와서 不成功하다.＜34＞
