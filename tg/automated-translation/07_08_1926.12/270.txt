**Body Text Translation:**
T'aep'yŏngho Lake is located in the southwest corner of Peking's Inner City (內城) and is part of what was formerly the Prince Ch'un Mansion (醇王府) of the Ch'ing dynasty, now part of Republic University (民國大學). In Korea, it would be merely a small pond, but with its grassy banks, it serves as a fine canvas for reminiscing about the rise, fall, and splendor of the Ming (明) and Ch'ing (淸) dynasties.

Autumn at T'aep'yŏngho Lake
						Chŏng Tong, Peking National University

Sunset at T'aep'yŏngho Lake

The sun in the west,
Hidden by the tall old city wall,
Does not show its final face;
Red clouds are illuminated by the afterglow.

Taking one step, then another,
Walking along the grassy path beneath the wall,
When I see the still-green willows of early autumn
Reflected in the blue lake,<72>

The sorrow (비레) in the cracks of earth
Speaks of the autumn
Of the old dynasty.

On the low-lying grassy field (底地),
A bored young man
Sings a familiar tune,
And in another corner,
A groom whose horse has grazed the grass
Is listlessly led away by his horse.

Wild shrubs emerging
From crevices in the wall
Flutter in the evening breeze,
Expressing the rise and fall of successive dynasties (累朝).
(9. 3)<73>

T'aep'yŏngho Lake After Rain

After the rain clears,
While white clouds still linger,
I go towards the lakeside,
And the autumn wind blows.

Young children
Playing in groups
Right below the tall city wall—
It reminds me of a picture
I saw in childhood.

Following the wind,
When I look at the grassy field below the path,
The tree leaves are still green,
But here and there, the tips of the grass have turned yellow.<74>

Fine ripples on the water,
Smiling like a faint smile,
Willow branches hanging straight down
Leave the sky and flow separately.
(9. 5)

Morning at T'aep'yŏngho Lake

Mist clings to distant trees,
As the sun rises in the blue eastern sky,
I walk along the bank of T'aep'yŏngho Lake.

Morning dew on blades of grass,
Like stars in the evening sky,
Sparkles here and there.
The young grass patch right by the water's edge,
Like a seedling bed in an early summer field,
Is tenderly, freshly green.
(9. 16)<75>

Autumn at T'aep'yŏngho Lake

Alone, braving the wind,
I look straight ahead—
Upon the yellow lawn,
Yellow leaves are falling.

The weeds in the wall's crevices
Shake their withered stalks
Limpingly,
While the yellowing pagoda tree
Stands solemnly and quietly,
Pondering something.

In the empty space above the lake,
Solitude (孤寂), Misery (悽慘), Suffering (苦痛)—
The Suffering (苦痛), Misery (悽慘), Solitude (孤寂)
That withers the leaves and lives—
Collides here and dashes there.
(Age 24. 10. 27)<76>

**Summary**

This collection of four poems by Chŏng Tong describes T'aep'yŏngho Lake in Peking during different times in autumn 1926: sunset, after rain, morning, and late autumn. The poet observes the natural scenery—willows, grass, water, wind, dew, falling leaves—and juxtaposes it with remnants of the past, such as the old city wall and the former Prince Ch'un Mansion, reflecting on the rise and fall of the Ming and Ch'ing dynasties. A sense of melancholy, boredom, and deepening solitude permeates the verses, culminating in a feeling of pervasive suffering in the final poem. For historians, the work offers a glimpse into the life and sentiments of a Korean intellectual studying in China during the colonial period. The setting in Peking, the references to Chinese history (明淸盛衰), and the expressed feelings of listlessness (無聊한 청년) and suffering (孤寂 悽慘 苦痛) provide context to the experiences and perspectives of Koreans abroad during this era, possibly reflecting broader sentiments felt under Japanese rule.

太平湖의 가을
						北京國民大學 丁東
太平湖는 北京 內城 西南角에 잇는데 前淸 醇王府 現今 民國大學의 한 부분이다. 우리나라 가트면 小池에 불과하나 湖畔에 풀바티 잇서 明淸의 盛衰 榮華를 回憶하는데 조흔 한 幅의 그림이다.

太平湖의 夕陽
서편에 잇는 해는
노픈 故城에 가리워
최후의 얼굴을 뵈이지 안코
붉은 구름이 餘光에 비최어 잇다.

한 걸음 두 걸음
풀우 城미틀 걸어가며
프른 호수에 비최인＜72＞

아직 프른 첫가을 버드나무를 볼쩨
흙틈에 비레가 옛날 王朝의
가을을 이야기 한다.

底地의 풀바테
無聊한 청년이
입에 익은 곡조를 부르고
한편 구석에
풀 뜻긴 馬夫가
힘 업시 말에 끌려 간다.

성 틈에서 나온
버러진 灌木이
夕陽微風에 날여
累朝의 盛衰를 形容한다.
(9. 3)＜73＞

비 뒤 太平湖
비 개고
힌구름 아직 잇슬쩨
湖畔으로 가는데
가을 바람이 부도다.

어린 아이들이
노픈 바로 城미테
떼지어 노는 것
어려서 본
그림 생각이 나도다.

바람 조차
길미테 풀바틀 볼쩨
나무니픈 아직 프른데
일면 풀끄티 누럿도다.＜74＞

가는 물ㅅ결
토인 미소 가티 웃을쩨
바로 드린 버들가지
空中 버리고 따로 흐르도다.
(9. 5)

아침 太平湖
머-ㄴ 나무에 안개 끼고
프른 東天에 해 도들쩨
太平湖畔을 걸어간다.

풀닙사귀에 아침이슬
저녁 공중에 별가티
여긔 저긔에 반짝인다.
물바로 가에 어린 풀밧
초여름 들에 모밧가티
살 보드랍게 새프르다.
(9. 16)＜75＞

太平湖의 가을
홀로 바람을 물읍쓰고
눈 아플 바라보니
누른 잔듸 우에
누른 니피 떨어진다.

城 틈에 잡초는
말은 줄기를
힘업시 흔드는데
누러가는 둥구나무는
엄숙하게도 조용하게
무엇을 생각하고 잇다.

湖水 우 빈 공간에는
孤寂 悽慘 苦痛
니플 말리우고 사는
苦痛 悽慘 孤寂이
이리 부드치고 저리 처 간다.
(24세. 10. 27)＜76＞
