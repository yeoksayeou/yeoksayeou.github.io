Special Company Announcement

Establishment of New Postal Transfer Account
For the convenience of our esteemed readers (諸位), in addition to the existing postal transfer account (振替口座), we have newly established "Kyŏngsŏng No. 4" (京城四番). We hope you will make much use of it. (Sending money via postal transfer is the safest, incurs lower fees, and offers the convenience of attaching correspondence.)

New Year's Special Enlarged Issue
Our New Year's issue will be a special enlarged issue (特大號) with a particular increase in page count. Accordingly, the regular price will be around 50 Chŏn. However, for subscribers paying directly in advance (先金) for three months or more, it will be calculated at the previous price of 30 Chŏn. Do not miss this opportunity; becoming an advance subscriber is advantageous.<5>

**Summary**

This announcement from the magazine *Tonggwang* informs readers about two main points: the creation of an additional postal transfer account ("Kyŏngsŏng No. 4") for easier payments and the upcoming New Year's special enlarged issue. The special issue will cost more (50 Chŏn vs. the usual 30 Chŏn), but existing subscribers who pay for three or more months in advance can get it at the old price. This piece reflects practical business operations of a Korean magazine during the colonial period, showing its efforts to facilitate reader payments through the Japanese postal system (postal transfer accounts, referencing Kyŏngsŏng/Seoul) and using subscription incentives to secure readership and funding. Historians might find the specific mention of the postal transfer account number and the pricing details useful for understanding economic and publishing practices of the time.
