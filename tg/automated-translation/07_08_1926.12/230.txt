Magazine Title: 동광 제8호
Publication Date: 1926년 12월 01일
Article Title: 思鄕 (Homesickness)
Author: 曺雲
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Homesickness
						Cho Un

Homesickness

Now another autumn
Swallows fly far away
Wild geese return
But where is my Hometown?
Looking up at the sky
My heart only deepens.<63>

Though not truly a wanderer
Why this homesickness (鄕愁)?
Am I a thirsty boatman (砂工)
Upon the vast blue waves (萬頃滄波)?
A thousand *li* away, a single nest
Must be waiting for me.

What if it's a thousand *li*?
What if it's ten thousand *li*?
What if it's a perilous valley (險谷)
Even monkeys fear to tread?
Even if it's a road I cannot finish
How could I bear not to go?
... Autumn, Year of Byŏngin [1926], from my Hometown...<64>

**Summary**

This poem by Cho Un expresses a profound sense of longing for the speaker's hometown. Using imagery of migrating birds and the vastness of nature, the speaker contrasts the cyclical return of seasons and animals with their own displacement and yearning. Despite the potential distance or dangers involved in the journey, the poem culminates in a strong resolution to return home, framing it as an unbearable necessity. From a historical perspective, the poem, written during the Japanese colonial period, powerfully reflects the feelings of displacement, loss, and intense longing for 'home'—whether a physical place or a broader sense of belonging and sovereignty—that many Koreans experienced during that era. The determination to return despite obstacles could resonate with the desire for national restoration.
