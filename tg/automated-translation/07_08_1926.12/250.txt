Article Title: 農家의 밤 (A Farmhouse Night)
Author: 鄭昞淳
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

A Farmhouse Night
						Chŏng Pyŏngsun

A Farmhouse Night
A dark night, a humble hut
Mosquito-repelling smudge fire, tobacco pipe fire
The old man twists straw ropes
The young man weaves hemp sandals.

Working without words
That father, that older brother
Even the short summer night
Passes without words.

Dusk (黃昏)
The evening sun sets
Dark smoke still rises.
Mountains and sea shrouded in smoke
In the eastern sky, quietly twinkling
A single, bright blue star! <68>

Incoming Tide (밋물)
The incoming tide
Swish— Swish—
Sings.
Befriending the children
Gathering clams barefoot
Every day, Swish— Swish—
It sings. <69>

**Summary**

This collection includes three short poems depicting serene rural and coastal scenes. "A Farmhouse Night" portrays the quiet diligence of a farming family working late into a summer evening. "Dusk" captures the tranquil beauty of twilight with smoke rising over the landscape and a single star appearing. "Incoming Tide" personifies the tide, singing alongside children playing on the shore. From a historical perspective, these poems offer a glimpse into the quiet, traditional rhythms of Korean rural life during the colonial period, focusing on nature and familial labor rather than overt political or social commentary. Historians might find the depiction of enduring traditional activities and the focus on simple, peaceful imagery interesting, potentially contrasting with other contemporary narratives of hardship or resistance.

農家의 밤
						鄭昞淳
農家의 밤
어둔 밤 오막살이
모긔ㅅ불 담배ㅅ불
늙은이는 새끼 꼬고
젊은이는 신삼네.

말업시 일하시는
그 아부지 그 형님
여름 밤 짧은 밤도
말업시 가신다네.

黃昏
저녁 해는 넘어가고
깜한 연긔 아직 인다.
연긔에 싸히는 산과 바다
동쪽 하늘에 가만히 반짝이는
새팔안 별 한아!＜68＞

밋물
밋물이
솨- 솨-
노래한다.
발 벗고 조개 줍는
아이들을 벗삼아
날마다 솨- 솨-
노래한다.＜69＞
