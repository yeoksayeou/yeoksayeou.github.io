**Body Text Translation:**
Sorrow in Autumn
						Kim Namju

Sorrow in Autumn

O wind, blowing wind.
Do not shake the tree branches.
They are leaves that will fall even if left alone
Do not torment them so much.<69>

Saying farewell to the swallows
Not many days passed
Met with a counterattack again
Ah, wild goose, to you
Humans offer a welcome
With the tip of a gun.

Every night descends
The white frost; the suffering blades of grass
Cry tormented.
As they fall asleep
Do you whip them again?

Occasionally, dew descends
Making the departing leaf
Yearn again for this world.
Knowing it will be even more painful.<70>

Since early spring
All the friends have bloomed
O chrysanthemum, you alone
Truly, where are you attached?
The time has come.
Bloom freely now.

On the blue night, cold frost descends.
The chrysanthemum will surely tremble
The wild goose sleeping among the reeds
Will startle awake
In my dream tonight
Will someone clad in blue
Come looking for me?<71>
<69-71>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Namju captures the melancholy and harsh beauty of autumn. It uses images of wind stripping leaves, migrating geese being hunted, frost harming grass, and the late-blooming chrysanthemum to convey themes of transience, suffering, endurance, and a faint, perhaps apprehensive, anticipation. The natural world is depicted as vulnerable to harsh forces, mirroring the fragility of life. Historians might find the poem's tone and imagery reflective of the atmosphere in colonial Korea. The suffering endured by nature (leaves, geese, grass) could be seen as an allegory for the hardships faced by the Korean people under Japanese rule, while the resilient chrysanthemum blooming late amidst the frost might symbolize enduring national spirit or hope against adversity. The final stanza's dream of a visitor in blue adds a layer of ambiguous yearning or anxiety.

필자	金南柱
기사형태	시

가을을 을픔
						金南柱
가을을 을픔
바람아 부는 바람아.
나무ㅅ가지를 흔들지 말아라.
두어도 떨어질 니피어니
넘우 괴로히 하들 말어라.＜69＞

제비를 하직하고
몃날이 못가
다시 반격 마즌
아아 긔럭아 네게
사람은 총끄트로
대접을 하는구나.

밤마다 나리는
하이 한서리 괴로운 풀니플
시달피 운다.
잠들어 가는 그를
채쪽질을 또 하느냐.

간간히 이슬이 나려
가는 풀닙에게
이 세상을 도루 그립게 한다.
그것이 더욱 괴로울 것을＜70＞

일흔 봄날로부터
동무들이 다 피엇거니
국화야 너 여러
참 어긔 달렷니
때 왓다.
네 훨훨 피어라.

푸른 밤에 찬 서리 나린다.
국화는 떨리리라
갈때에 잠던 기럭이
놀라 잠깨일 걸
이 밤의 나의 꿈엔
푸른 옷 닙은이
차자 오려는가.＜71＞
＜69-71＞
