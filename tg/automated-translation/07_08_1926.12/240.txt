발행일	1926년 12월 01일

필자	劉道順
기사형태	시 (Poem)

Tree
						Yu To-sun

Tree
A bird sits on the tree and makes a sound.
Though having leaves is a difficult meaning,
With sunlight and wind,
It is green and sways.
A bundle of joy,
The tree stands naturally on the earth.

If one truly knows it is autumn,
All things appear blessed.
Among them,
Even as leaves desperately struggle and fall,
The tree holds life within
And is silently, eternally venerable.<65>

Though its body is deeply pierced and falls
By the wicked plow,
The tree has never uttered a word of anger,
Not even a sound to itself, throughout all time.
Thinking of this,
I held back tears at its gentleness.

Old thoughts
In the yard of breaths coming and going,
A cherished life
Departed unknowingly.
Did time follow along?
Seeing the first snow, old thoughts arise.

On the reddish mounds,
The cold moonlight embraces fallen leaves
And falls asleep.<66>
From my lips, a whistle
Near the window does not cease.

A single fly
Not budging—
In the stillness of this heart,
The cast large shadow,
What shall I call it?

Only from the village where the river is joyful,
That sound comes.
Oh, heart of a thief, on which road
Were you startled?
In opium-like old memories,
My heart is like a new groom.<67>
<65-67>

**Summary**

This poem by Yu To-sun uses the image of a tree to explore themes of endurance, the cycles of nature, inner strength, memory, and quiet suffering. The tree stands through seasons, endures hardship (symbolized by the plow) with silent dignity, and evokes reflection on the passage of time and lost life. The latter stanzas shift to a more personal, introspective mood, connecting the speaker's memories and present stillness to the natural world, ending with a complex feeling likened to a "new groom" amidst "opium-like old memories." For historians of colonial Korea, the poem offers insight into the introspective and nature-focused sensibilities prevalent in literature of the period. The tree's quiet endurance despite suffering ("wicked plow") could be subtly interpreted as reflecting the Korean situation under colonial rule, finding solace and a model for resilience in nature rather than direct political expression.

나무
						劉道順
나무
나무에 새가 안자 소리를 한다.
닙달린 것도 어려운 뜻이려니와
해ㅅ빗과 바람이 이서
프르고 흔들리니
한뭉치 즐거움어
나무로 따우에 절로 섯다.

가을인줄을 참되이 알면
만물이 다- 복스러워 보인다.
그 중에도
니피 뚝뚝 발버둥처도
나무는 안에 생명을 두고
묵묵히 길이 거륵타.＜65＞

간악한 쟁기에
몸이 푹푹 찍기워 넘어저도
나무는 노염 한마디
혼자 소리로도 고금에 업섯스니
생각코
그 유순에 눈물을 먹음엇다.

옛생각
드나드는 숨결 마당에
아끼던 인생이
저도 모르게 갓다.
세월도 딸아 갓는가
첫눈을 보니 옛 생각이 솟는다.

등뻘건 두던 우에는
찬 달빗이 락엽을 껴안고
잠이 들엇고＜66＞
내 입에는 회파람이
창문ㅅ가에서 끈지 안는다.

파리 한 마리
달삭 안하는
이 맘의 정막에
드리운 큰 그림자는
무어라 이를까.

강물만 즐거운 동리에서
저 소리가 온다.
도적의 맘이여 어느 길에서
놀랫는가.
아편 가튼 옛 생각에
내 맘은 새신랑 갓다.＜67＞
＜65-67＞
