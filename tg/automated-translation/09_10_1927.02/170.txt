Magazine Title: 東光 제10호
Publication Date: 1927년 02월 01일
Article Title: 近詠三章 (Three Recent Verses)
Author: 梁柱東
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

1
Over mountains and across waters,
I set out on the road to see you, my dear.

Because your place is at the foot of the mountain,
I climb and search the mountain paths, coming from afar.
Because your place is by the sea,
I cross the sea, coming from afar.

Ah, today too, searching for the lost you,
I wander in this nameless village.

2
With empty hands, my dear, do not blame me,
Though I come after a long time, without even one gift...

"Though poor you may be, your heart is rich,
The heart with which you came is more precious than a gift."

Hearing my wife's earnest words of comfort,
Tears well up in my eyes before I know it.

3
My beloved's eyes,
Clear like the autumn sky—
My spirit boundlessly into that vast, tranquil space
Flies away, is drawn away.

But whenever I gaze upon you,
Endless lonely thoughts again like clouds
I see drifting by your eyes,
Ah, perhaps the end of love is ennui.
(Byeongin Year, 12th Month, At Yanggok) <55>

**Summary**

This three-part poem by Yang Chudong expresses themes of longing, reunion, and existential doubt about love. The first stanza depicts a strenuous search for a beloved person across landscapes. The second describes a touching reunion where the speaker arrives empty-handed but is comforted by his wife's understanding, valuing his presence over material gifts. The final stanza shifts tone, admiring the beloved's clear eyes but seeing a shadow of loneliness, leading to a melancholic reflection on whether love ultimately ends in weariness or ennui. From a historical perspective, while not overtly political, the poem's themes of searching, hardship (implied poverty in stanza 2), and underlying melancholy might reflect the personal struggles and perhaps a sense of disillusionment prevalent among intellectuals during the Japanese colonial period. The use of older Korean orthography is also of linguistic interest.

近詠三章
						梁柱東
1
산 넘고 물 건너
내 그대를 보려 길 떠낫노라.

그대 잇는 곳 산 미티라기
내 산ㅅ길을 토파 멀리 오노라.
그대 잇는 곳 바다ㅅ가라기
내 바다를 건너 멀리 오노라.

아 오늘도 일허진 그대를 차즈려
이름 모를 이 마을에 헤매이노라.

2
뷘손이외다, 그대여 탓하지 마소,
오래ㅅ만에 오아도 선물 하나업시...

각난은 할망정, 마음은 가멸으오니,
오신 그 마음 선물보담 보배롭지오.

안헤의 정성스러운 위로ㅅ말 듯고
어느듯 내 눈에 눈물 고이다.

3
사랑하는 그대의
가을 한울과 가티 해맑은 눈-
나의 령은 가업시 넓고 고요한 속으로
날아 가도다, 빨려 가도다.

그러나 내 그대를 바라볼 때마다,
끗업는 쓸쓸한 생각 다시 구름과 가티
그대의 눈ㅅ가에 떠듦을 보나니,
아 사랑의 끄튼 아마도 권태이런가.
(丙寅 12月, 於陽谷)＜55＞
＜55＞
