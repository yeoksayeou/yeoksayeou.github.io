Field at Twilight
						Yu To-sun

Yesterday, the sad sound of rain
I climbed the embankment alone
Today, following the fog, following the twilight
I sought the field

The stream winding below the mountain, at the field's edge
Suddenly the evening star (長度星) has fallen and sunk
Like an empty boat made naturally of two fallen leaves (落葉)
Carrying the twilight, it departs from this field.

The savory smell of earth rises
This land, dug up and seeded
Whose is it?
I stand with an uneasy heart.

Over there, the moon rises above the mountain
The lonely branches of field trees are greyish
The solitude (孤寂) of the twilight field
Makes tears well up in my unspeakable heart.
(December 7th)

Snow

Heaven, in clouds, in sorrow
Over the front mountain, back mountain, even the fields, all
Scatters tears.
Vigorously, equally it scatters.
What would be rain in summer
Has blossomed as snow in winter.

Beneath heaven's blue light
Upon this sacred land, covered in snow and lying down
When the sound of a gunshot rings out, another country's dog runs.
Upon this snow, like heaven's old heart
Oh brother who received the hawk (鷹) [or: brother beaten by the hawk/falconer], what are your thoughts?
(December 7th)

**Summary**

These two short poems by Yu To-sun, published in 1927, use natural imagery to evoke feelings of melancholy, displacement, and critique. "Field at Twilight" describes the desolate beauty of a field at dusk, pondering the ownership of the land with an "uneasy heart." "Snow" portrays snow as heaven's tears covering a "sacred land," but sharply contrasts this purity with the sound of a gunshot and "another country's dog," ending with a poignant question to an oppressed "brother." From a historical perspective, the poems are significant for their reflection of the Korean experience under Japanese colonial rule. They subtly (in the first poem) and more overtly (in the second) express national sorrow, a sense of loss of sovereignty ("Whose is this land?", "another country's dog"), and hint at the oppression faced by Koreans ("brother who received the hawk").

黃昏의 들
						劉道順
어제는 비소리 구슬픈 소리
내 혼자 두언에 올랏고
오늘은 안개 딸아 黃昏 딸아
들을 차잣소

산아레 들끄틀 감도는 시내ㅅ물
어느새 개밥별(長度星)이 떨어져 잠겻고
落葉이 두닙 절로 된 뷘 배로
黃昏을 싯고 이 들을 떠나가오.

구수한 흙냄새 떠올라 오오
파 들추고 씨뿌린
이 땅은 누구의 것이요
나는 마음 켕기는 하노를 가지고 셋소

저긔 산우에 달이 뜨오
들나무 외롭은 가지는 회스름
黃昏의 들 孤寂은
말못할 마음에 눈물만 괴이게 하오
(12月 7日)

눈
하늘이 구름에 수심에
압산 뒤ㅅ산 들까지 전부
눈물을 뿌리오.
싱싱히 똑가티 뿌리오.
여름이면 비일 것이
겨울이메 눈 피엇소.

하늘의 파란 빗 미테
눈 덥고 누운 이 거룩한 땅 우에
총 소리가 나자 딴 나라의 개가 뛰오.
하늘의 늙은 마음가튼 이 눈 우에
매(鷹) 바든 형제여 생각이 어떠하오.
(12月 7日)＜67＞
