						Yuan Dynasty Gao Dongjia (Original Work), Joseon Yang Paek-hwa (Translation and Supplementation)

Act 4: Ch'ae Kong Forces the Examination
Characters Appearing
Ch'ae Ong
Chang T'aegong (Ch'ae Kong's neighbor)
Ch'ae Kong
Ch'ae P'a

[Ch'ae Ong enters, singing]
As the spring waters warm and peach blossoms float, about to turn into fish in an instant, the year for the state examination (科擱) has returned. An imperial edict (詔書) came down summoning people to the Ministry of Rites (禮曹), and notices seeking talented individuals (招賢) circulate in the county, but with both parents (兩親) so dear, how can I leave their side (膝下)?
"Good things in this world are not sturdy; colored clouds easily scatter, and glazed glass is brittle." I originally found contentment in honest poverty (淸貧), intending only to strive for filial piety (孝道). However, because the court (朝廷) is holding the state examination this time, and my county recommended me to the higher authorities as a candidate (應試者), someone already came from the county to summon me. So, I initially declined, citing my parents' advanced age (年老). But that person will probably come again tomorrow. I intend to firmly decline once more. As they say, "Human honors are not as valuable as heavenly honors (人爵不如天爵貴); how can worldly success (功名) compare to the renown of filial piety (孝名)?"

[Sings again]
Setting my mind on scholarship, I've read ten thousand volumes (卷 書), but worldly success (功名) was never my wish from the start. My only worry is my parents' advanced age (年老); who would even dream of going to the examination hall (科場)? Even if I were to attain the high office of a Duke or Minister (九棘三槐, 公卿), how could I abandon my mother and father (萱花椿樹)? Oh Heaven, Heaven, this single point of utmost sincerity (一點至誠), this feeling in my heart (衷情), to whom can I speak?

[Chang T'aegong enters, singing]
Living as neighbors, we help each other; when something comes up, we come and tell each other.

Ch'ae Ong: Chang T'aegong, are you coming?
(They see each other)

[Chang T'aegong sings]
Hey, listen to me. The examination (科擧) day is near, so quickly prepare your travel gear (行裝) and set off on your journey.

Ch'ae Ong: Please listen, sir. I cannot go because my parents (兩親) are elderly (年老).

[Chang T'aegong sings]
Haha, hey, listen to me. A son thinks of his old parents' solitude (孤單), but parents wish for their child's glory and honor (榮貴). If you don't go now in your youth, when do you intend to go?<91>

Ch'ae Ong: What the venerable sir says is very correct. However, there is no one to attend (侍奉) my father and mother, so how can I go?

Chang T'aegong: If you say you won't go, let's see what your parents (兩親) say when they come out. They will surely tell you to go. – Ha, speak of the devil, your father is coming out now.

[Ch'ae Kong enters, singing]
Time hurries on, scattering snow on my head. Living out my remaining years (餘生) while upholding honest poverty (淸貧), how fortunate that I have one bright son (聰明). Now, all I wish for is that he quickly enters the path of officialdom (벼슬 길); I have no other desire.
(Ch'ae Kong and Chang T'aegong meet)

[Ch'ae Kong sings]
My son, listen. The Sage King (聖上), by imperial decree (詔勅), broadly calls for the wise and worthy (賢良). Scholars from all directions (四方) are going up to the capital. You too should quickly go up to the Ministry of Rites (禮曹). Hurry and prepare for your journey.

Chang T'aegong: The venerable mother is also coming out.

[Ch'ae P'a enters, singing]
I am eighty years old; my eyes are dim and my ears are deaf too. My children haven't amounted to much, just this one son. Sixty days ago, he married (娶妻), and just as I was about to see [grandchildren? unclear], that wretched old man, for the sake of useless fame and profit (名利場), insists on sending our only son away. Ah, thinking about this makes me so angry I can't stand it.
(They see each other)

Ch'ae P'a: My son, it's only been two months since you married (娶妻). Why are you becoming so thin? If you go for three years like this, you'll be nothing but skin and bones.

Chang T'aegong: Ah, Madam, if you say such things, it damages the relationship between husband and wife (內外 誼傷).

Ch'ae Kong: My son, the examination (科擧) day is near. The county has already recommended you, and I know that with your level of scholarship (學問), you won't fail (違不). Why haven't you prepared to leave?

Ch'ae Ong: Father, it's not that I don't want to go. It's because Father and Mother are elderly (年老), and there is no one in the house to attend (侍奉) you.

Chang T'aegong: Venerable sir, Madam, you must send the young master off this time, one way or another.

Ch'ae P'a: Look here, as you know, this is our only child. How can we send him away?

Ch'ae Kong: What kind of talk is that? Are all the people going to take the examination (科擧) this time only those with many siblings (兄弟)?

Ch'ae P'a: Look at this old man. Sir, you are currently blind, deaf, and can't even get around properly. If you send that boy away and something happens, what will you do? Who will look after you? If you can't eat, you'll starve; if you can't dress, you'll freeze. Don't you know that?

Ch'ae Kong: What do women know? If that boy goes up to the capital this time and does well, obtaining an official post (벼슬), the family name (家門) will shine. Why shouldn't we send him?

Ch'ae Ong: Father's words are correct, but I cannot go.

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
How much longer do my aged parents (父母) have ahead of them? The time to serve and support them is probably today.<92>

Chang T'aegong: If you go this time, you will certainly wear the official's robe (官服).

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
That's not true. Even if I wear the blue robe (藍袍 - scholar's robe), it's not as good as the five-colored robe (五綵衣 - worn by Lao Laizi to amuse his parents, symbol of filial piety). If I go, I might obtain a high position (貴한 자리), but perhaps my parents (兩堂) won't live to see that glory.

[Ch'ae Kong sings]
My son, listen. Are the scholars gathering at the examination ground (科場) only those without parents?

[Chang T'aegong sings]
Hey, listen to me. You are a man; if you have ambitions reaching the clouds (凌雲之志), don't hesitate like this.
If you don't go this time,
[Sings]
Isn't the long hardship and ten years of study under the blue lamp (十載靑燈) all in vain? This journey follows your parents' wishes. Hey, don't betray your parents' will.

[Ch'ae P'a sings]
Our reliance for a hundred years rests solely on this one child. You wretched old man, do you think he has many brothers?

[Ch'ae Kong sings]
It's hard to speak of that boy's heart, but I know his inner thoughts clearly.

Chang T'aegong: What is the reason?

[Ch'ae Kong sings]
He finds it hard to abandon the affections of the women's quarters (閨房之恩愛) and cannot leave for the distant journey to the horizon (天涯).

Ch'ae Ong: That's not it.

Ch'ae Kong: You have read books, so let me tell you a story.
[Sings]
Do you know of the wife of Great Yu (大禹), Lady Tushan (塗山氏), or not? Just four days after marriage, she parted from her husband.
You have been married (娶妻) for two months already,
[Sings]
Why can't you part like this?

Chang T'aegong: Ha, are you really like that?

Ch'ae Ong: Absolutely not, how could that be?

[Chang T'aegong sings]
If you are lost in new affections (新情) and only think of the women's quarters (閨房), you will likely ruin the great path of the roc (大鵬 - symbol of great ambition).

[Ch'ae P'a sings]
If we are to speak of that boy's heart, it's not because he misses his wife (안해). It's because he wants to serve his parents with filial piety (孝誠). When did Zeng Shen (曾參 - disciple of Confucius known for filial piety) ever take the state examination (科擧)? Human wealth and honor (富貴) are given by Heaven. If Heaven intends to give it, it will naturally come even if unsought.

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
Father, please listen to Mother's correct words.

Ch'ae Kong: What? Mother's words are right, and Father's words are wrong? So, you really disobey your parents because you can't forget your wife (처)?

Ch'ae Ong: (Kneeling towards the sky) Ah, Heaven! If I am not leaving because I cannot forget my wife,
[Sings]
May Heaven please observe the unfilial sin of this Ch'ae Ong.

Ch'ae Kong: (Angrily) You wicked brat! I tell you to go take the examination<93> to elevate our family (家門) and bring renown to our ancestors' names, yet you brat just keep making excuses this way and that.

Ch'ae Ong: Father, I dare not disobey. It's just that with Father and Mother being elderly, there's no one to attend (侍奉) you. If, by chance, something were to happen, firstly, others would say the son was unfilial, abandoned his parents, and went seeking an official post (벼슬). Secondly, they might say Father was thoughtless to send his only son far away. Hearing such talk would be sad (슬혀서), so I cannot follow your command (말씀).

Ch'ae Kong: Whether you listen to my words or not is up to you, but tell me, what is filial piety (孝)?

Ch'ae P'a: What a wretched old fool. Haven't you learned what filial piety is even at eighty? Wearing mourning clothes (喪服) with hemp garments is filial piety.

Ch'ae Kong: Be quiet, what do you know?

Ch'ae Ong: Father, please listen. "For all who are sons, in winter keep them warm and in summer cool (冬溫夏凊); settle their bedding at dusk and inquire after them at dawn (昏定晨省), asking if they are too hot or cold (燠寒), scratching where they itch (搔其疴癢); when they go out or come in, support them (出入則扶持之); when asked what they desire, respectfully present it (問所欲則敬進之). Therefore, when parents are alive (父母在), one does not travel far (不遠遊); when going out, one does not change direction (出不易方); and when returning, one is not late (復不過時)." The ancients spoke of filial piety thus.

Ch'ae Kong: What you speak of are all minor aspects (小節), not great filial piety (大孝).

Ch'ae P'a: You senile fool. You want that boy to perform great filial piety (大孝) before you even die? Then all the more reason you shouldn't send him off to the examination (科擧).

Chang T'aegong: Your words are incomprehensible.

Ch'ae Kong: My son, listen. Hmm, "Filial piety (夫孝) begins with serving one's parents (始於事親), continues with serving one's ruler (中於事君), and culminates in establishing oneself (終於立身). Body, hair, and skin (身體髮膚) are received from parents (受之父母), so not daring to injure them (不敢毁傷) is the beginning of filial piety (孝之始也). Establishing oneself and practicing the Way (立身行道), making a name for posterity (揚名後世) to bring glory to one's parents (以顯父母), is the culmination of filial piety (孝之終也)." Therefore, "When the family is poor and parents are old, not serving in office for a salary (不爲祿仕)" is unfilial. If you attain an official post (벼슬), your parents' goodness will be revealed; isn't that great filial piety (大孝)?

Ch'ae Ong: Father's words are correct. However, I do not know if I will attain office or not if I go this time. If I fail to pass the examination (及第), I will end up serving neither parents nor ruler, failing in both.

Chang T'aegong: Hey, that's not it. "Learn when young (幼而學), act when mature (壯而行). To cherish treasure [talent] and confuse the state [not serve] is called inhumane (懷寶迷邦謂之不仁)." Confucius's (孔子) constant busyness, Mozi's (墨翟) chimney having no time to get sooty, Yi Yin (伊尹) working as a cook, Baili Xi (百里奚) being sold for five ram skins (羊皮) – all were about practicing the Way according to the times to benefit the world and pacify the people (濟世安民). As the old saying goes, "Having mastered civil and martial arts (學成文武藝), sell them to the imperial house (貨與帝王家)." With talent (재조) and learning (학문) like yours, is there any reason you wouldn't get an official post (벼슬)?

Ch'ae P'a: Venerable sir, you just keep saying nice things to persuade our boy to go take the examination, but let me tell you an old story. Listen.

Chang T'aegong: Please do.

Ch'ae P'a: Long ago, Squire Li (李員外) living in the East Village had one son. After teaching the son a little bit of writing, Squire Li constantly nagged him to seek office (求仕). The son couldn't stand it anymore and went to the capital to look for an official position. But since no one looked after him, he became a beggar wandering the streets. Then one day, seeing a prime minister pass by, the beggar prostrated himself on the ground, bowed, and begged to be employed.<94> The prime minister then said, "Alright, I will make you the head of the Relief Agency (養濟院), so supervise your parents." The son was happy and agreed. But no matter how much he became the head of the Relief Agency, how could he supervise his parents? He returned to his hometown and found that his parents, with no one to look after them, had become destitute and were living in the Relief Agency. However, the parents were overjoyed to see their son and said they did well to send him to the capital. "Today you've come as our director, so the other folks here won't dare look down on us," they said. So, venerable sir, the reason you urge our son a thousand times to go to the capital and take the examination is also so that he can become the head of the Relief Agency, ensuring we won't be looked down upon by others, right?

Chang T'aegong: (Smiling) Madam's beggar story is quite long.

Ch'ae Kong: My son, hurry and prepare to leave.

Ch'ae Ong: It's not difficult for me to leave, but who will look after Father and Mother?

Chang T'aegong: Don't worry about that. As the old saying goes, "Spend a thousand coins to buy a neighbor and eight hundred to buy a house (千錢買隣 八百買舍)." Since I live nearby, if anything at all happens at your house, I will help with all my strength. So leave with peace of mind.

Ch'ae Ong: If you do that, how could I be anything but grateful? Please, look after them well. If I achieve even a little success on this trip, I will never forget this kindness (恩惠). Then, I must get my travel preparations ready.
[Sings]
I thank you for your deep kindness, venerable elder. I ask again that you look after everything. Even if I pass the examination (登科) this time and return home wearing silk clothes (비단옷), I fear my aged parents might not see it.

[Ch'ae Kong sings]
We two old folks just wish for you to quickly bring honor to the family (家門). That is our only desire.
You seem worried that there's no one to support us, but when you return with an official post (벼슬),
[Sings]
With grand feasts of three sacrificial animals and five tripods (三牲五鼎 盛饌), you'll serve us morning and evening (朝夕供饋). Isn't that better than now, eating only beans and water? If you return home in silk clothes,
Even if I die,
[Sings]
My spirit (魂靈) will finally rejoice.

[Chang T'aegong sings]
Since I live nearby, how could I begrudge such trivial effort? But why haven't you quickly sought "Ten years under the window, no one asked (十年窓下無人問), but one success makes the name known throughout the world (一擧成名天下知)"? If today you "Do not return to your hometown in brocade clothes (若不錦衣歸故里), who will know you read ten thousand volumes (誰知隬讀萬卷書)?"

[Ch'ae P'a sings]
Taking out the pearl from upon the hall (堂上珠 - metaphor for a precious child) one morning, upon whom shall I rely in my old age? "Enduring the parents' death from hunger and cold (忍將父母饑寒死) to gain the child's fame and profit (博得孩兒名利歸)." Even if you return home in silk clothes, your ruined name and conduct will never be restored.<95>

[Exit Poem]
(Ch'ae Kong) "Hurriedly prepare travel gear to go to the examination hall (急辦行裝赴試闡);"
(Ch'ae Ong) "How can I disobey my parents' stern command (父母嚴命怎生違)?"
(Ch'ae P'a) "If he ranks first on the Dragon and Tiger List (一擧首登龍虎榜);"
(Chang T'aegong) "In ten years, he'll reach the Phoenix Pond (十年身到鳳凰池 - imperial court)."

Act 5: Farewell Instructions at Namp'o
Characters Appearing

Cho O-nyang
Ch'ae Ong
Ch'ae Kong
Ch'ae P'a
Chang T'aegong

[Cho O-nyang enters, singing]
As the spring dream fades away meaninglessly, I face the mirror, my dark hair (綠雲) disheveled. I hear my husband is going to the capital; another sorrow of parting has arisen.

[Ch'ae Ong enters, singing]
Following my father's stern command, I set off on the road. How can I bear this unforgettable affection?

[Duet]
When people not strangers part one morning, the difficulty of separation, the sorrow of parting is great.

Cho O-nyang: Listen, husband. It matters little that we part after being husband and wife for only two months, but don't you think of your eighty-year-old parents with white snow (白雪) scattering on their heads? You are just caught up in worldly success (功名) and forget the thought of supporting them. Is that really okay?

Ch'ae Ong: Look, how could my heart be happy leaving my parents' side (슬하)? However, Father keeps insisting, and he doesn't listen to my words. What can I do?

Cho O-nyang: Yes, I understand.
[Sings]
You read books hoping to become the top scholar (狀元), but perhaps your learning (學問) is not deep.

Ch'ae Ong: How do you know my learning is not deep?

[Cho O-nyang sings]
Listen, hear my words. Have you forgotten the Classic of Filial Piety (孝經) and the Book of Rites (曲禮)?

Ch'ae Ong: I haven't forgotten them.

[Cho O-nyang sings]
Cooling in summer and warming in winter (夏凊冬溫), and not traveling far while parents are alive (父母在不遠遊) - you say you haven't forgotten?

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
I tried to remonstrate (諫) in a hundred ways.

Cho O-nyang: What did old Master Chang T'aegong say?

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
That old man strongly urged me on.

Cho O-nyang: Going or not going depends on your heart.

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
He severely reproached my great sin, leaving me utterly without words for defense (變明).

Cho O-nyang: What sin do they say you have?

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
That I cannot forget new affections (新情), disobey my parents' words, love only my wife,<96> and refuse to go to the capital.

[Cho O-nyang sings]
How pitiful Father is! Doesn't he keep his only son beside him?
Listen, where is Mother now?

Ch'ae Ong: She is in her room.

Cho O-nyang: Then let's go together and speak to her.
(Cho O-nyang starts to go, then stops)

Ch'ae Ong: Why aren't you going?

Cho O-nyang: Let's not. If I go with you,
[Sings]
They might think that I, I am not virtuous and bewitched my husband. Ah, what should I do about this wrongful accusation?

[Duet]
For Father's sake, tears flow. For Mother's sake, tears flow. Why can't we forget and miss them so?

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
Father doesn't listen to my remonstrances (諫). Truly, being a son is difficult, so difficult.

Cho O-nyang: Listen, you shouldn't resent Father like that.

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
How could I dare resent him? But they are lonely and old, and if I leave right away, who will support them? That's why I worry.

[Duet]
Same as above.

Ch'ae Ong: Ah, Father and Mother are coming. Look, wipe your tears.

[Ch'ae Kong, Ch'ae P'a enter, singing]
We must bid farewell to our departing son. May the fish transform into a dragon (龍), soar high in the blue clouds (靑雲), and break off a branch from the laurel tree in the Moon Palace (月宮桂樹 - symbol of passing the exam).

Ch'ae Kong: My son, have you finished packing your luggage (行李收拾)?

Ch'ae Ong: Yes, I have finished.

Ch'ae Kong: If you've finished, why haven't you left?

Ch'ae P'a: Oh, you frustrating old man. When that boy leaves, there will be no one in the house, so doesn't he have a few words of instruction for his wife (처)?

Ch'ae Ong: The reason I haven't left yet is that I wanted to see old Master Chang T'aegong when he comes, entrust Father and Mother to him properly, and then leave with peace of mind.

Cho O-nyang: Oh dear, Master Chang T'aegong is coming.

Chang T'aegong: "Sword in hand, facing a cask of wine (仗劍對樽酒), ashamed to show the face of a wandering son (恥爲遊子顔). My aspiration lies in worldly success (所志在功名), why lament the parting (離別何足嘆)?"

Ch'ae Ong: Listen, old sir, I am leaving now. However, there is no one at home but my elderly parents and my wife (처). And my wife is a woman and knows nothing, so please look after all matters well. Also, if anything runs short, please help supplement it. I received Father's permission for this yesterday, so I am saying this again. If I achieve even a little advancement (發身) in the future, I will certainly not forget this kindness (恩惠).

Chang T'aegong: Hey, if you accept someone's request, you must see it through to the end. Also, once a word is spoken, it cannot be taken back, can it? I already promised you yesterday, so after you leave, I will look after them to the best of my ability.

Ch'ae Ong: Thank you so much.<97>

Ch'ae Kong: Since the venerable sir (太公) has promised so firmly, is there any chance he would break his word (食言)? Leave with your mind at ease.

Ch'ae Ong: Then, Father, Mother, I will leave now.
[Sings]
I am leaving now, so please don't worry too much. I will surely return within this year (今年). I only hope and pray you remain well.

[Duet]
There will be a day when I return to see my parents (兩堂). There will be a day when I return to see my parents (兩堂).

[Ch'ae Kong sings]
Don't worry about household matters at all. Just strive for glory and honor (榮貴) and return. If your name appears first on the list (榜),
[Duet]
Send news quickly. Send news quickly.

[Ch'ae P'a sings]
My beloved son departs on a long journey. The old mother left behind is truly lonely. At parting, I took needle and thread and stitched his clothes again tightly. Lifting my eyes, I gaze – the passes and mountains (關山) are so distant. Alone and desolate, I stand leaning against the gate.

Ch'ae Ong: Mother, don't be too upset.

[Ch'ae P'a sings]
How can my heart possibly be happy?

[Duet]
Write letters often so we don't worry too much.

[Cho O-nyang sings]
There are so many words I want to say in my heart.

Ch'ae Ong: What words do you have so many of? If you have something to say, say it now.

[Cho O-nyang sings]
What's the use of saying it? It will only choke me up.

Ch'ae Ong: What is choking you up?

[Cho O-nyang sings]
The marital bond (夫婦恩情) is severed after sixty days. Who is there to support the eighty-year-old parents?

Ch'ae Ong: Madam, you resent me.

[Cho O-nyang sings]
Even if I don't want to resent, how can I not resent?

[Duet]
Same as above.

[Chang T'aegong sings]
Though I may be lacking, living nearby, we will discuss all matters together. Listen, hear my words. Even if you go on this journey reluctantly, what great thing could happen?

Ch'ae Ong: I worry about how my elderly parents will fare after I leave.

Chang T'aegong: Rest assured about that.
[Sings]
I will come morning and evening to look after your parents.
(Ch'ae Ong grieves.)

[Chang T'aegong sings]
A true man is not without tears (丈夫非無淚), but does not shed them at parting (不灑別離間).

[Duet]
A family member leaves, tearing out my five viscera (五臟).
(Ch'ae Ong kneels before Chang T'aegong)

[Ch'ae Ong sings]<98>
Please look after my elderly parents.
(Chang T'aegong helps Ch'ae Ong up.)

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
If my body attains honor (榮貴), I will repay this kindness (恩惠).

[Cho O-nyang pulls Ch'ae Ong's back, singing]
What's the use of honoring the son, when the parents are entrusted to others? How can I bear this role? Only tears pour down.

[Duet]
Same as above.

[Ch'ae Kong sings]
Don't grieve too much about leaving home. My heart is also no less heavy (쓸임). My son, listen. Do you think I send you off because I like it? It's only because I wish for your glory and honor (榮貴).

[Ch'ae P'a sings]
Not much road remains ahead for us. Hurry and break off the laurel branch in the Moon Palace (月宮桂樹).

[Duet]
On what day will we reunite (團聚) again? On what day will we reunite again?

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
Listen, Madam. Hear my words. Look after our aged parents (*親 - likely 兩親, parents) well. When they are hungry, urge them to eat more rice (진지); when they feel cold, change their clothes.

[Cho O-nyang sings]
Serving parents-in-law is a daughter-in-law's duty (道理). Even without your words, I know to do so. But you are their descendant (子孫); if you leave today, when will you return?

[Duet]
Same as above.

[Ch'ae Kong sings]
My son, somehow return early and don't make people wait too long.

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
When I return home, perhaps my parents (兩堂) will be even older.

[Duet]
How can my heart be at ease? Tears keep flowing.

[Cho O-nyang sings]
How can I express all this resentment?

Ch'ae Ong: What resentment?

[Cho O-nyang sings]
Try being in my position; it's difficult, so difficult.

[Ch'ae Ong sings]
If you want to resent me, resent me to your heart's content. But please, don't neglect my parents (父母).

[Duet]
Same as above.

[Duet]

**Summary**

These excerpts from the play "Pipa ki" depict the scholar Ch'ae Ong being pressured by his father and neighbor to leave his elderly parents and wife to take the crucial state examination in the capital, despite his strong reluctance rooted in filial piety. After much debate about the true meaning of filial duty versus achieving success (and thus honoring parents), he agrees, securing a promise from the neighbor to care for his parents. Act 5 shows the emotional farewell between Ch'ae Ong, his wife Cho O-nyang, and his parents, emphasizing the wife's burden and the parents' hopes and anxieties. Act 6 shifts focus to Prime Minister U, who seeks a top-scoring scholar as a husband for his daughter, rejecting other suitors. He also instructs his daughter on proper female conduct and household management, setting up a potential future intersection with Ch'ae Ong's story.

From a historical perspective, the play's adaptation in 1927 Korea is interesting. It reflects the enduring influence of classic Chinese literature and Confucian values like filial piety (孝) and the importance of state examinations (科擧) for social mobility (立身揚名), even during a period of significant social change under Japanese colonial rule. The tension between personal feelings/family duty and societal expectations/ambition provides insight into traditional values being negotiated or reasserted. The detailed portrayal of family dynamics and the specific language used (including older Korean forms and Hanja) offer linguistic and cultural insights into the period.

廳直.  (등장) 『珠幌斜連雲母帳에 玉鈞半捲水晶簾을. 輕煙裊裊歸香閣이요, 月影騰騰轉書簷을』 나는 별 사람이 아니라 牛丞相 府中에 잇는 청직이 올시다. 몃칠 전에 대감께서는 參內를 하시더니 무슨 일이 게신지 이때지 歸宅을 아니 하심니다. 그로해서 계집 하인들은 매일 뒤 화원에서 놀기만 하더니 오늘은 대감께서 돌아오신단 말을 듯고 하나도 나오지를 아니 하엿슴니다. 나는 사랑을 치어 노코 대감 나오시기만 기대리고 잇슴니다. 애구 이거 웬일이냐 할멈이 하나 들어온다. 무슨 일일까.
「媒婆 甲 登場 唱」
내 남의 중매 노릇 이골이 아조 낫다. 가진 말로 구슬리어 赤姥繩 매저주고 움푹한 박 먹는구나. 그리다가 거짓말이 綻露 한 번 되는 날엔 애구머니 경이란 팟다발 가티 친다.
廳直.  여긔 웨 들어왓서.
媒婆 甲.  저는 張尙書宅에 혼인 중매를 들자고 왓지요.
廳直.  응 그건 좀 어려울 걸.
媒婆 甲.  웨 어려워요.
廳直.  대감께서 허락 잘 안하실테야.
媒婆 甲.  이번 이 혼사는 대감께서 필연코 허락하서요.
廳直.  가만 잇서. 또 노파 하나 오네.
「媒婆 乙 登場 唱」
어렵고도 어려워라 중매 노릇이 어려워라 얌전한 색시를 그리도 차저도 공연히 헛수구에 달이품만 파누나.
媒婆 甲.  빌엉맹이 마누라 여긘 웨 왓소.
「媒婆 乙 唱」
엇불사 한 걸음 느진 것이 탈이로다.＜101＞
廳直.  마누라, 무슨 일로 왓서.
媒婆 乙.  여봅시요 저는 樞密使님 댁과 통혼하시자고 왓지라오.
廳直.  그 일이면 지금도 말햇거니와 좀처럼 안 될걸.
媒婆 乙.  그건 어째서요.
廳直.  대감께선 골르기를 몹시 하시니까.
媒婆 乙.  그건 염려업지라우. 제가 말씀하는 혼인은 꼭 되지라우.
媒婆 甲.  난 오래 이 댁 아페서 중매 노릇하구 살앗는데 이번 이 혼인을 저 빌엉뱅이 마누라에게 빼겨선 안 될걸.
媒婆 乙.  뭐 어째, 이 밸랑뱅이 마누라 중매를 들려거든 가문이 서로 맛는 집을 차자 다리어라구, 댁 아페서 살면은 당자가 거지라두 괜챤흔지라구.
廳直.  이거 떠들지들 말허-대감께서 돌아오셔. 저 편에 가들 잇서.
「牛丞相 登場 唱」
闕下로서 나아오니 哀袍의 御香이 아직도 마추이네. 문에는 劍戟이 엄숙히 버리이고 모래를 깐 길에는 車馬가 분비이네.
허엿케 머리에는 서리ㅅ발 날리는데 3公의 몸으로 功업슴이 브끄러웨. 집안을 돌아보니 적막도 하오구녀 제사를 밧드를 아들 하나 그 업구야.
나는 오랫동안 공무로 해서 집에를 돌아오지 못하엿는데 청직아 지금 안에서 떠든게 누구냐.
廳直.  『有事면 不敢不報요 無事면 不敢亂傳이엇다.』 지금 두 노파가 청혼을 하러 왓슴니다.
牛丞相.  그러면 그 둘을 이리로 불러라-너희 둘은 무엇하러 왓느냐.
媒婆 甲.  저는 張尙書 댁에서 보내서서 청혼을 왓슴니다.
媒婆 乙.  저는 李樞密 댁에서 보내서서 중매로 왓습지라오.
牛丞相.  어떤 사람이구 상관업지마는 학문이 잇서 當世의 장원이 될 사람이라야만 딸을 줄러이구 그러치 아니하면 안될 터이다.
媒婆 甲.  대감께 여쭈옴니다. 제가 말씀하온 신랑은 사주쟁이 말에 금년에는 장원한다구 하옵니다.
媒婆 乙.  대감께 여쭈옴니다. 이 중매의 말하는 신랑은 명운이 조치 못한 사람이지라우. 단지 제가 말하는 신랑은 관상쟁이 말에 이번 과거에는 꼭 장원을 한다구 하옵지라우.
(매파 갑과 매파 을이 서로 싸운다.)
牛丞相.  어, 이 무례한 것들, 어대서 감히 이리하노. 이 애 청직아 그가 지고온 단자는 뭘 썻던지 상관 업스니 찌저 버려라. 그리고 둘을 매달고 열암온 箇식 때려라.
(청직이 둘을 때린다.)
牛丞相.  냉큼 그 매파를 내쪼차라.
廳直.  장원 곳 아니어든 혼인은 얼신 말 말아라.
媒婆 甲.  『열암은 개 매 흠벅 먹엇슬 뿐일세.』
媒婆 乙.  『마즌 것이 급기야 소득이로군.』
(청직, 매파 갑, 매파 을, 퇴장)
牛氏 丞.  『光陰은 似箭催人老하고 日月은 如梭趲少年이로다.』 나는 喪配를 하고 다먼 딸자식 하나 잇슬 뿐인데 그도 벌써 나히가 차기는 하엿지마는 혼처는 아직 정하지를 아니 하엿지. 그런데 우리 딸애는 성격이 부드럽고 모든 일에＜102＞ 숙성하니 만일 富貴家 자제를 주엇다가는 그 애를 버릴 터이요 학문잇는 군자에게 보내줄 것 가트면 현부라는 칭찬을 들을 터이니 그것이 조흘 줄 알어-내가 몃칠동안 집에를 돌아오지 안햇는데 들으니 게집 하인들이 그 새 매일 뒤 화원에서 작난치고 놀앗다 하니 이것은 우리 딸애가 단속을 잘못한 까닭이야. 古人도 말하기를 『欲治其國이면 先齊其家라』하니 응, 딸애와 할멈과 惜春을 불러서 한 번 타일러야 하겟다.
(牛氏, 老女, 惜春, 등장)
「牛氏 唱」
깁숙고 종용한 방에 가 들어안저 얌전한 여자라 단장을 아니한다. 심심파적 수를 노아 긴긴 해를 지내고 글 읽고 그림 그려 봄이랑 보내며 妝臺를 대하는 일 아조도 드물다. 絳羅帳 들이워 고은 꼿 가리워 蜂蝶이 함부로 차저 오게 못한다.
「老女 惜春 唱」
우읍고 우읍구야 저 다친 창안에 얼마나 혼자서 쓸쓸히 게오신가.
牛丞相.  이 애야 閨門을 나지안는 것이 부인의 덕이란다. 너도 인저는 장성을 하여서 아까 매파가 구혼까지 왓더라. 지금은 네가 내 딸자식이지마는 장래는 다른 사람의 안해가 될 몸 아니냐. 그런데 내가 수일 잇지 안는 동안에 너는 할멈과 惜春을 뒤 화원에 내보내어 놀게 하여 저의 할 일은 모두 내번저 두게 햇다 하니 그게 무에란 말이냐. 이것은 죄다가 네가 단속치를 못한 까닭이다. 혹시 그것들에 조치 못한 일이 생기면 네 이름에도 상관되는 것이 아니냐.
牛氏.  아버님, 참 잘못하엿슴니다. 이 후부터는 잘 단속을 하겟슴니다.
牛丞相.  (노하야) 할멈. 그 나희가 되어 가지고, 집안일을 마타보는 몸으로 게집년들을 꾀어 가지고 노는 것이 무에야.
老女.  저는 모름니다. 이 惜春이가 모도 그랫담니다.
惜春.  쇤네는 모름니다. 이 마누라가.
牛丞相.  이 두 못된 년이 서로 미는구나. 두다려 주워야 하겟다.
牛氏.  (꿀어 안저) 아버님 고정하십시오.
牛丞相.  일어나거라.
