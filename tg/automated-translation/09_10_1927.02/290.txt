Magazine Title: 동광 제10호
Publication Date: 1927년 02월 01일
Article Title: 봄은 오건만 (Though Spring Comes)
Author: 洪又石
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Though spring comes every year
With the new year,
Why can you, my beloved who left once,
Not return?
Remembering my beloved,
Separation from my beloved is unbearable.
When the Rose of Sharon (無窮花) blooms on Mt. Taebaek (太白),
Will you perhaps come then?

Since my beloved who should come does not,
I shall go out searching.
Whether crossing mountains
Or traversing waters,
Whether rough or deep,
Without resting, I will search
And bring my beloved back.<67>

**Summary**

This poem by Hong U-sŏk expresses a deep sense of longing for a "beloved" (님) who does not return, even as spring arrives year after year. The speaker contrasts the cyclical return of nature with the absence of the beloved. Ultimately, the speaker resolves to actively seek out the beloved, regardless of the hardships involved, mentioning significant national symbols like Mt. Taebaek (太白) and the Rose of Sharon (無窮花). For historians, the poem is interesting as it likely uses the "beloved" (님) as an allegory for Korea's lost sovereignty under Japanese colonial rule. The yearning for the beloved's return, tied to national symbols, reflects the suppressed nationalist sentiment and hope for independence prevalent during that era, expressed through metaphorical language to evade censorship.
