Sŏnch'ŏn Myŏngsin School Song

A welcome dawn shines within our peninsula,
Our Myŏngsin School, founded on the rock of ages.
We stand firmly upon beautiful truth,
Let us truly learn the teachings of our loving teachers.

We, the Paedal people, have entered the stage of activity,
Let us cultivate wisdom and knowledge, uniting soul and spirit.
The bright light of Myŏngsin will shine forever on our peninsula,
Ten thousand years (Hurrah)!
(Submitted by the Sŏnch'ŏn Branch)
(School/Association Song Collection: A small reward will be presented for adopted submissions) <34>

**Summary**

This article presents the school song for Myŏngsin School located in Sŏnch'ŏn. The lyrics express pride in the school, founded on "truth," and urge students to learn diligently from their teachers. It emphasizes the students' role as members of the "Paedal Minjok" (an archaic term referring to the Korean people) stepping onto the "stage of activity," encouraging them to cultivate wisdom and knowledge for the future of the Korean peninsula, hoping the school's "bright light" shines forever. From a colonial history perspective, the song is interesting for its affirmation of Korean identity ("Paedal Minjok," "our peninsula") and the emphasis on education ("truth," "wisdom," "knowledge," "bright light") as a source of hope and progress during a period of Japanese rule. The call for song submissions also points to community involvement in shaping school identity.
