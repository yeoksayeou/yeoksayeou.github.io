Changes Resulting from Linguistic Habits
By Yi Pyŏnggi

Language is constantly changing. It changes continuously according to time or region. However, it does not necessarily change to conform to phonetic principles or grammatical rules. Here, changes that do not align with phonetic principles nor follow grammatical rules, found in old language (古語), contemporary language (今語), and regional dialects (地方語), are categorized into four types: Transformation of Vowels and Consonants, Reduction of Vowels and Consonants, Vowel Gemination and Addition of Vowels/Consonants, and Transformation of the Original Word.

1. Transformation of Vowels and Consonants
There are cases where vowels change among themselves and consonants change among themselves. These are divided into Vowel Transformation and Consonant Transformation, with examples provided below.

(2) Consonant Transformation

**Summary**

This article by Yi Pyŏnggi meticulously documents various types of sound changes in the Korean language based on usage ("habit"). It categorizes these changes into transformations (vowel/consonant swaps), reductions (elimination of sounds), additions (insertion of sounds), and semantic shifts accompanying phonetic changes. The author provides extensive examples drawn from Old Korean, contemporary standard Korean of the 1920s, and various regional dialects (Chŏlla, Kyŏngsang, Ch'ungch'ŏng, Hamgyŏng, etc.), illustrating phenomena like vowel harmony shifts, palatalization, consonant weakening/strengthening, deletion, and epenthesis.

From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article is significant as a work of Korean linguistics conducted by a Korean scholar during Japanese rule. Its detailed focus on the nuances, history, and regional variations of the Korean language itself, using Korean linguistic terminology and older Hangul orthography, represents an academic assertion of Korean linguistic identity and scholarship. Historians might find the systematic classification and wealth of dated linguistic examples (including archaic forms and dialectal variations from 1927) invaluable for studying the evolution of the Korean language and the state of linguistic research within Korea during the colonial period, a time often marked by language assimilation policies.

言語는 자꾸 변하는 것이다. 時代나 地方을 딿아 자꾸 변하는 것이다. 그러나 그것은 반듯이 音理에 맞게 또는 規則이 바르게 변하는 것만이 아니다. 이에 音理에도 맞지 않고 規則도 바르지 않게 변한 것을 古語에나 今語에나 地方語에서 찾어 네 가지로 낞워 子母音의 轉換, 子母音의 줄음, 母音의 겹침과 子母音의 덧붙임, 原語의 轉變이라 하여 말한다.

1. 子母音의 轉換
母音은 母音끼리 子音은 子音끼리 서로 바뀌어 나는 것이 있으니 이를 母音의 轉換, 子音의 轉換 두 가지에 낞워 그 例를 돌어 적으면.
