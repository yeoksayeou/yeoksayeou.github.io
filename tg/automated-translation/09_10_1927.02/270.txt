Longing for a Friend/Loved One
						Kim Nanch'on

Snow falling like large flakes,
on the riverside, gently, gently
piles up and piles up, it does.

When I met my beloved last year,
large snowflakes fell then too.
This year, though snow falls,
why does my beloved not come?

With a foolish heart hoping you would come,
I look up at the snowy sky;
my beloved, my beloved, dressed in white mourning clothes,
gently, gently descends, it seems.

With a foolish heart hoping you would come,
I sit on the snow-piled ground and listen;
my beloved's, my beloved's footstep sounds,
gently, gently are heard coming, it seems.
(January 1927) <67>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Nanch'on expresses deep longing for an absent beloved, triggered by the falling snow which reminds the speaker of a past meeting. The speaker waits foolishly, imagining the beloved descending like the snow, dressed in white mourning clothes (suggesting death or absence), and hearing their footsteps approach gently on the snow-covered ground. From a historical perspective, the poem reflects personal sentiment during the colonial period. While not overtly political, the imagery of the beloved dressed in white (소복) could be interpreted metaphorically, perhaps hinting at loss or mourning related to the national situation, though its primary reading is one of personal bereavement or separation. The use of older Korean orthography is also characteristic of the era.

벗을 그림
						金蘭村
함박 가티 나리는 눈은
강ㅅ가에 삽붓삽붓
싸이고 또 싸임니다 그려.

작년에 님 만날 때
함박눈 오더니
눈 오는 금년엔
님은 어이 안 오누.

오시리라는 철업는 마음
눈 오는 하늘 치어다 보니
우리 님은 우리 님은 소복 입고서
삽붓삽붓 나려옴니다 그려.

오시리라는 철업는 마음
눈 싸인 땅우에 안저 들으니
우리 님의 우리 님의 발자취 소리
삽붓삽붓 들러옵니다 그려.
(1927年 1月)＜67＞
