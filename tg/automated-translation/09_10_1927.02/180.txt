Article Type: 시 (Poem)

**Body Text Translation:**
Seagull
						U-i-dong-in from Tokyo

Splashing, splashing, wave-beating
Upon the wide sea
The bird wandering in a flock
Is the homeless seagull.

Because it is a sad body without a home
Whether morning or evening
Floating upon the sea's waves
It cries sorrowfully.

The swallows from Kangnam (south of the river)
Have built nests in the lighthouse
But the seagull with no place to build a home
Cries day and night, being too sad.

Splashing, splashing, wave-beating
Upon the wide sea
The bird wandering in a flock
Is the homeless seagull.
(March 1926) <55>

**Summary**

This poem uses the image of a homeless seagull endlessly crying on the vast sea as a central metaphor. Contrasted with swallows that have built nests, the seagull represents profound sadness and rootlessness. Written by a Korean residing in Tokyo ("U-i-dong-in from Tokyo"), the poem likely reflects the feelings of displacement, sorrow, and lack of belonging experienced by Koreans under Japanese colonial rule, particularly those living away from their homeland. Historians might find this poem significant as an expression of the emotional burden and sense of alienation prevalent among Koreans during the colonial period, especially those within Japan itself.
