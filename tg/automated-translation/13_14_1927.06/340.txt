<62> Chosŏn Ilbo Director Paek Kwansu
Pen Name: Kŭnch'on. Born January 28, 1891. Born in Koch'ang.
Original Family Register: Tŏksan-ni, Sŏngnae-myŏn, Koch'ang-gun, Chŏllabuk-do.
Current Residence: 12 Pongik-tong, Kyŏngsŏng-bu.
Father: Farmer. Wife: An Chin'gyŏng. Married [Year missing]. Three daughters.

1910: Graduated from Yŏngho Hakkyo in Kunsan.
1915: Graduated from Kyŏngsŏng Chŏnsu Hakkyo.
1921: Graduated from Meiji University in Tokyo, Japan.
1910: Teacher at the private Pehwa Hakkyo in Chungwha-gun, P'yŏngnam Province.
1915: Secretary at the Chungang Kidokkyo Ch'ŏngnyŏnhoe (Central YMCA) Hakkan (Educational Hall) in Chongno, Kyŏngsŏng.
1916: Teacher at Kyŏngsŏng Chungang Kodŭng Pot'ong Hakkyo (Kyŏngsŏng Central Higher Common School).
1924: Director at the Chosŏn Ilbo Sa (Chosŏn Ilbo Company).

**Summary**

This brief biographical notice from 1927 details the background of Paek Kwansu, a director at the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper. It lists his birthdate, place of origin, family details, education (including studies in Kyŏngsŏng and Tokyo), and career progression from teacher to YMCA secretary to his role at the newspaper. For historians, this entry provides a snapshot of the educational and career path of a Korean elite figure during the colonial period, highlighting connections between education (both domestic and in Japan), Christian organizations (YMCA), and the burgeoning Korean press under Japanese rule. His position at the influential Chosŏn Ilbo makes him a noteworthy figure in understanding Korean media and intellectual life at the time.
