A Boy from a Fishing Village Becomes Britain's Prime Minister, The Tumultuous Half-Life of MacDonald
						By Songsaeng (頌生)

According to recent correspondence from Britain, the British people are said to be greatly concerned, worried about the health of the former Prime Minister, Mr. Ramsay MacDonald, and the current Prime Minister, Mr. Baldwin. These two men represent opposing poles in their policies; one is the leader of the Conservative Party, and the other is the leader of the Labour Party. However, the British people well know that both men are indispensable leaders, and that had only one existed without the other, it would have been difficult to rescue post-war Britain from its decline (쇠퇴리).

A life as tumultuous as MacDonald's is rare indeed; born in a fishing village (一어촌), he rose to become the prime minister of a nation. His childhood was one of impoverished living under the care of his grandmother (祖母 侍下). However, possessing a fierce desire for reading, he avidly read the collection at the village library. At age 20, he went out to London, working as a shop clerk for a monthly salary of 12 shillings and a half while diligently attending night school. As a result, he was able to enter a certain chemistry laboratory as an assistant. He studied day and night (晝夜) intending to become a scholarship student (校費生), but consequently harmed his health. Through the kindness of a certain Member of Parliament, he became a secretary with an annual salary of 75 pounds (磅).

From this time, he began contributing articles to newspapers, and within several years, he became the chief editor of a Labour newspaper (一勞動新聞). In 1902, he enhanced his literary reputation (文名) by authoring a book about the Boer War.
From 1906 to 1918, he was elected as a Member of Parliament (代議士), representing the Leicester region. During the World War in 1914, he was offered a position as a cabinet minister (閣員) in the national unity cabinet, but he bravely opposed the war and refused. Consequently, he was even imprisoned as a war opponent and eventually suffered defeat in the 1918 election. However, faith in him gradually increased, and after the war (戰後), he became the Prime Minister of Britain.

Not a few (不少하다) post-war international problems were resolved directly or indirectly through his efforts. One of his colleagues, Mr. Thomas, said:
"All the achievements of Locarno, the increased efficiency of Geneva due to Germany's admission (獨逸의 可入), the success of the London Conference and the Dawes Plan (다우스案) are but a part of his political skill. I firmly believe that someday there will be a day when we welcome this boy from Lossiemouth (로씨마움) again as the Prime Minister of Our Nation (吾國)." ＜15＞

**Summary**

This article details the remarkable life story of Ramsay MacDonald, emphasizing his rise from humble beginnings in a Scottish fishing village to become the British Prime Minister. It highlights his dedication to self-education despite poverty, his entry into politics, his controversial opposition to World War I which led to imprisonment and electoral defeat, and his eventual post-war comeback to lead the Labour Party and the country. The piece portrays both MacDonald and his Conservative counterpart, Baldwin, as crucial figures for Britain's post-war recovery, and quotes a colleague predicting MacDonald's return to power. From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article's focus on a figure achieving high office from poverty in Britain, a major world power, might have resonated with aspirations for social mobility. Additionally, the detailed reporting on British politics reflects the keen interest Koreans had in the affairs of global powers during this period.

漁村의 少年이 英國의 宰相, 波瀾많은 「맥도낼드」의 半生
						頌生
최근의 英國 통신에 의하면 英國民은 前수상 「램시 맥도날드」氏와 現수상 「볼드원」氏의 건강이 염려된다 하여 크게 근심 중에 있다 한다. 이 두 사람은 그 정책에 있어서 양극단을 대표하니 하나는 보수당의 수령이요 하나는 노동당의 수령이다. 그러나 英國民에게는 이 두 사람이 다 없지 못할 인도자는 그 중 하나만 있고 하나이 없었던들 戰後의 英國을 그 쇠퇴리에서 구출하기 어렵었을 것임을 잘 아는 까닭이다.

「맥도날드」와 같이 파란 많은 일생도 드믄 일이라 할 것이니 그는 一어촌의 탄생으로 일국의 재상까지 된 것이다. 그의 幼時는 祖母 侍下의 貧苦生活이었다. 그러나 그는 맹렬한 독서욕의 소유자로 촌 도서관의 장서를 탐독하였다. 20세에는 「런던」에 出하여 12실링 반의 월봉을 받고 점원 노릇을 하면서 야학에 精勤한 결과 여떤 화학 실험실에 조수로 들어가게 되었다. 그는 校費生으로 공부를 하려고 晝夜 전공한 결과, 건강을 해하였더니 어떤 국회의원의 호의로 연봉 75磅의 서기가 되었다.

이 때붙어 그는 신문지에 기고하기 시작하여 수년 내에 一勞動新聞 주필이 되다가 1902년에 「보어」전쟁에 대한 책을 저술하여 文名을 높이었다.
1906년 붙어 1918년까지 그는 「레이세스테」지방을 대표하여 代議士로 선출되었으며 1914년 세계대전 時에는 거국 일치 내각에 閣員되라는 교섭을 받았으나 그는 용감히도 전쟁을 반대하고 이를 거절하였다. 딸아서 그는 전쟁 반대자로 투옥까지 되고 마침내 1918년에는 선거에 패배을 당하였으나 그에 대한 신념은 점점 증가하여 戰後에는 英國의 수상이 되었다.

戰後의 국제문제 해결은 직접 간접 그의 힘으로 된 것이 不少하다. 그의 동료 중 1인인 「토마스」氏는 왈
『「로카노」의 모든 성적, 獨逸의 可入에 의한 「제네바」의 능율 증진 「런던」회의와 「다우스」案의 성공등은 그의 정치가적 수완의 일부분 뿐이다. 내가 확실히 믿기는 언제던지 다시 이 「로씨마움」의 소년을 吾國의 총리대신으로 영접할 날이 있을 것이다.』 ＜15＞
