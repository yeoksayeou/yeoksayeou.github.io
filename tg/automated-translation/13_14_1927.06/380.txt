Entering the Fire for Her Mother-in-Law
Pak Cho’s wife, Lady Im, served her mother-in-law, fulfilling her duties as a daughter-in-law to the utmost. As Pak Cho was an official residing in Seoul, Lady Im lived alone at home, attending to her mother-in-law. In the middle of the night, a fire broke out in the house. Everyone in the household ran out to save their own lives, but the mother-in-law, being old and sick, remained lying down and could not get up. Lady Im rushed in, embraced her mother-in-law, and was coming out when she hit a stepping stone and fell. At this time, the wind grew fiercer and the force of the fire became extremely intense, so she shielded the fire with her body, getting her head and back burned. However, she did not let go of her mother-in-law and stayed put. A strong servant saw this, was greatly impressed, and immediately rushed in, carried the mother-in-law and daughter-in-law (姑婦) out on his back, and fortunately, they were rescued. (Yŏji Sŭngnam) <64>

A Heart Loyal to the King Even After Death
Kim Hujik was a man from the time of King Chinp'yŏng of Silla. When the king only enjoyed hunting, Hujik earnestly remonstrated with him, but he wouldn't listen at all. When Hujik was dying, he told his son, "As a subject, I failed to correct the king's faults. When I die, please be sure to bury me by the side of the road the king takes for hunting." Later, his son did as instructed. One day, as the king came out to hunt, a voice was heard on the road, saying, "Your Majesty, please do not go. Your Majesty, please do not go." The king turned and asked about it, and an attending retainer respectfully replied, "This is the tomb of Kim Hujik," and then recounted everything he had said when he was dying. The king shed tears profusely and said, "The teacher remonstrated with loyalty during his life, and even after death, he does not forget and loves me so. If I do not mend this habit, with what face could I see the teacher in the underworld (地下)?" And for the rest of his life, he never went hunting again. The world called this "tomb remonstrance" (墓諫). <65>
<64-65>

**Summary**

This excerpt from "Continuation of the Haedong Elementary Learning" presents two didactic tales illustrating Confucian virtues. The first recounts the story of Pak Cho's wife, Lady Im, who bravely rescued her elderly mother-in-law from a house fire at great personal risk, embodying extreme filial piety. The second tells of Kim Hujik, a Silla official whose loyalty to King Chinp'yŏng extended beyond death; his spirit, speaking from his strategically placed tomb, successfully persuaded the king to abandon his excessive hunting. For historians, these stories are interesting as they showcase the continued promotion of traditional Confucian ethics (filial piety, loyalty to authority) during the Japanese colonial period. Publishing such tales could serve to reinforce social order and respect for hierarchy, potentially aligning with, or being tolerated by, colonial authorities seeking stability, even while rooted in pre-colonial Korean history and values.

시어머니 위해 불에 뛰어들어
박조(朴隨)의 안해 임씨(林氏)가 시어머니를 섬기매 며느리의 도리를 극진히 하였음니다. 박조 버슬아치로 서울에 머물고 임씨는 홀로 집에 있어 시어머니를 봉양하였음니다. 밤ㅅ중에 집에 불이 나아서 왼집 사람들은 모도 각기 제 목숨을 살려고 뛰어 달아나되 시어머니는 늙고 병들었음으로 그냥 눕어 있어서 일어나지 못하였음니다. 임씨가 뛰어 들어가아서 시어머니를 안고 나아오다가 섬돌에 받히어 엎더지었음니다. 이때 풍세 더욱 사납고 화력이 몹시 맹렬함으로 그는 몸으로 불을 가리우어 머리와 등이 불에 더이었음니다. 그러나 그는 시어머니를 놓지 아니하고 그냥 있었음니다. 힘센 종 하나가 그를 보고 크게 감복하여 곳 뛰어 들어가아 그 고부(姑婦)를 업고 나아오아 다행히 구원을 얻었음니다.(輿地勝覽)＜64＞

죽어서까지 임군을 위하는 맘
김후직(金后稷)은 신라 진평왕(眞平王)때의 사람임니다. 임검께서 사냥다니기만 즐기매 후직이 극진히 간하되 도무지 듯지 아니 하였음니다. 후직이 죽을 때에 그 아들더러 말하되 『내가 남의 신하되어 가지고 임검의 잘못을 바루우지 못하였으니 내 죽거든 임검께서 사냥다니시는 길ㅅ가에 꼭 묻어 달라』 하였음니다. 그 후에 아들이 그대로 하였더니 어느 날 임검께서 나나오아 사냥할 새 길에 무슨 소리가 들리어 『임검님이시어 가시지 말으소서. 임검님이시어 가시지 말으소서』 함니다. 임검께서 돌아보고 물은 즉 모시는 신하가 여쭙되 『이는 김후직의 묘임니다』 하고 이내 그가 죽을 때에 하던 말을 죄다 아뢰었음니다. 임검께서 눈물을 줄줄 흘리며 말하되 『선생이 생시에 충성으로 간하더니 죽어서까지 잊지 아니하고 이대도록 나를 사랑하는고나. 내가 만일 이 짓을 고치지 아니하면 무슨 면목으로써 선생을 지하(地下)에서 보리오』 하고 한 평생 다시 사냥 다니는 일이 없었음니다. 세상에서 그를 묘간(墓諫)이라 하였음니다.＜65＞
