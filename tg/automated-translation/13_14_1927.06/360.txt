The Moon and Stars
						Pak Ch'anmyo

This happened nine years ago.
One night, the year I turned five, the moon and stars were twinkling in the sky.
I was in the yard looking at them with my mother and older sister. My sister asked me, "Which do you think is bigger, the moon or the stars?"
I immediately answered, "The moon is bigger," but my sister said, "No. The stars are bigger," and then said many other things.

But I clung to my mother, crying, "Mommy, sister is a liar. The moon is bigger than the stars, but smaller than your face." My mother held me and
said, "That's right, baby. Mommy's face is bigger than the moon, and bigger than the sun too." (End)＜65＞

**Summary**

This short piece recounts a childhood memory from when the author was five years old. The author recalls a conversation in the yard with their mother and older sister about whether the moon or the stars are bigger. The child insists the moon is bigger than the stars but smaller than their mother's face. The mother lovingly affirms the child's perspective, saying her face is bigger than both the moon and the sun. From a historical perspective, this piece, published in a Korean magazine in 1927 during the Japanese colonial period, offers a glimpse into everyday life and familial affection. Its simple, innocent theme might reflect a focus on personal experience amidst broader societal changes, and the language provides examples of early 20th-century Korean orthography and expression.

달과 별
						박찬묘
이것은 아홉 해 전의 일임니다.
내가 다섯살 먹던 해 어느 날 밤인데 하늘에는 달과 별이 반작이었음니다.
나는 어머님과 언니하고 뜰에서 구경을 하고 있었음니다. 언니는 나보고 뭇기를 『너는 저 달과 별이 어는 게 더 크냐』 하였음니다.
나는 바로 대답하기를 『달이 더 크지』 하였더니 언니는 『아니다. 별이 더 크단다』 하고는 무슨 말을 많이 하였음니다.

그러나 나는 어머님께 매이어 달리며 『엄마 언니는 거짓말쟁이. 달은 별보다 더 크고 엄마의 얼굴보다는 작은데』 하였음니다. 어머님은 나를 안으시며
『그렇다. 아기야 엄마의 얼굴은 달보다도 크고 해보다도 크다』 하시였음니다.(끝)＜65＞
