**Body Text Translation:**
Dong-A Ilbo Chief Editor, Song Chin-u

Pen name: Koha. Born May 8th, Kaeguk 499 (1890). Born in Tamyang.
Original Domicile: Kyŏngsŏng-bu, Won-dong 74. Currently resides at the same address.
Father: Farmer. Wife: Lady Yu. Married in Kwangmu 7 (1903). 1 Son.

1915: Graduated from the Faculty of Law, Meiji University, Tokyo, Japan.
1916: Principal, Kyŏngsŏng Chungang High Common School (3 years).
1921: President, Dong-A Ilbo (3 years). Current position: Chief Editor, the same company.

Traveled to Hawaii (布哇). Enjoys reading books on religion (宗數) and history (歷史書類).

**Summary**

This biographical entry from 1927 profiles Song Chin-u, the then-chief editor of the prominent Korean newspaper Dong-A Ilbo. It outlines his key personal details, including his birthdate (1890), birthplace (Tamyang), education (Meiji University Law Faculty, 1915), family information, and career progression from principal of Chungang High Common School (1916) to president (1921) and chief editor of the Dong-A Ilbo. It also notes his travel to Hawaii and his interest in reading religious and historical texts. For historians, this piece offers a contemporary snapshot of a major figure in the Korean nationalist movement, highlighting his elite education in Japan, his leadership roles in significant Korean institutions (press and education) under colonial rule, and his personal interests, providing context to his activities and influence during this period.
