<9> Chosŏn Ilbo Chief Editor An Chae-hong (安在鴻)
Pen name: Minse (民世). Born November 30, 500th year of the Founding Era [1891]. Born in Chinwi.
Permanent Address: 646 Turyŏng-ri, Koph'o-myŏn, Chinwi-gun, Kyŏnggi-do.
Current Address: 75 Kongp'yŏng-dong, Kyŏngsŏng-bu [Seoul].
Father: Farmer. Wife: Ms. Yi (李氏). Married in the 9th year of Kwangmu [1905]. 2 sons, 1 daughter.

1914 Graduated from the Department of Politics and Economics, Waseda University (早稻田大學), Tokyo, Japan.
1915 School Inspector (學監) at Kyŏngsŏng Chungang Kodŭng Pot'ong Hakkyo (Central Higher Common School) (2 years).
1924 Chief Editor (主筆) at the Chosŏn Ilbo company (조선일보사).

General Secretary (總務) of the Sin'ganhoe (新幹會). Traveled to China.
Favorite Reading: History and the classics (子書). Hobby: Mountain climbing.

**Summary**

This brief biographical entry from 1927 outlines key details about An Chae-hong (安在鴻, pen name Minse), the chief editor of the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper. It covers his birthdate (1891), birthplace (Chinwi), education (Waseda University), career milestones including school inspector and his current role at Chosŏn Ilbo, family information, his position as General Secretary of the Sin'ganhoe, his travels to China, and his personal interests in reading history/classics and mountain climbing. For historians, this article provides a contemporary snapshot of a prominent Korean nationalist intellectual and journalist during the colonial period, confirming his influential position in both media (Chosŏn Ilbo) and nationalist organizations (Sin'ganhoe) by 1927.
