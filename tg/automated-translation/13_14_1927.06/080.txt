Chungoe Ilbo Managing Editor Min T'aewŏn
Pen names: Ubo. Puch'unsanin. Born December 28, Kae'guk 503 (1894). Born in Sŏsan.
Original family register: Sinjang-ri, Ŭmam-myŏn, Sŏsan-gun, Ch'ungch'ŏngnam-do. Current address: 136 Tangju-dong, Kyŏngsŏng-bu.
Father: Farmer. Wife: Yi Yongt'ae. Married in 1909. One son, one daughter.

1914: Graduated from Kyŏngsŏng High Common School.
1914: Reporter at Maeil Sinbo Company (6 years)
1924: Graduated from the Department of Political Science and Economics, Waseda University, Tokyo, Japan.
1924: Social Affairs Director, then Political Affairs Director at Tonga Ilbo Company.
1925: Managing Editor at Chosŏn Ilbo Company.
1926: Managing Editor at Chungoe Ilbo Company.

Writings: Musoet'al (Iron Mask), Pup'yŏngch'o (Duckweed), etc. Translated novels and Oh Kogungŏsa (嗚呼古筠居士).
Hobby: Baduk (Go)
Contemporary Who's Who (3) <19>

**Summary**

This document is a biographical entry for Min T'aewŏn, the Managing Editor of the Chungoe Ilbo newspaper as of June 1927. It details his birth date (1894), birthplace (Sŏsan), family background, education (Kyŏngsŏng High Common School, Waseda University), career progression through major Korean newspapers (Maeil Sinbo, Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo, Chungoe Ilbo), notable writings and translations, and personal details like his hobby (Baduk). For historians, this profile offers a concise snapshot of a prominent figure in Korean journalism during the colonial period. It highlights the career path of an educated Korean navigating the media landscape, including working for the Government-General's mouthpiece (Maeil Sinbo) before moving to nationalist papers (Tonga Ilbo, Chosŏn Ilbo) and then the Chungoe Ilbo, reflecting the complex environment for intellectuals and journalists at the time. His education at Waseda University also points to the common practice of studying in Japan for aspiring elites.
