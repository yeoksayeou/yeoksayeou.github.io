Norway's Great Poet Who Spoke Thoroughly of Truth, Facing His Centennial: Henrik Ibsen
						By Paek Min

Henrik Ibsen was born 100 years ago from now (1828), on March 20th, in a small southern town called Skien in the country of Norway (놀웨). Although his was a family of considerable renown in that town, it is said that his paternal line included much foreign blood, such as German (떠이취), Scottish (스콜랜드), and Danish (덴막), which is very interesting when compared to his life, which ended as a man of the world.

When he was 8, his father went bankrupt, the family dispersed, and Ibsen became an apprentice (從弟) to an apothecary (악제사) in the town with hardly any formal schooling. While studying on his own there, preparing to take the medical school examination at Kristiania (크리스티아나) University, he was moved by history while memorizing Latin (라텐문) and created a three-act verse play.

From this time, he also wrote lyrical poetry and published writings sympathetic to the revolutionary thought of the time, gaining some literary fame. At the age of 24, he was invited to the newly established Norwegian National Theater in the city of Bergen (베르겐) as a writer and stage director. This period was a training time for Ibsen, the great playwright of the future, to gain knowledge about the stage.

In 1857 (age 30), he left Bergen and took the position of general manager at the Norwegian Theater in Kristiania (크리스티아나), the capital of Norway (놀웨). Financially, he was not well-off at all; moreover, the theaters were divided between the so-called "Foreign Culture Faction" and the "National Culture Faction," destroying themselves through useless competition. Also, Ibsen's major works were not welcomed by the public. Instead, the optimistic and youthful Bjørnson (뵤른손) received great acclaim from the general public as the literary representative of young Norway. Greatly troubled by this, Ibsen left his home country in 1864 (age 37) and entered a life of wandering abroad. Thus began a nomadic life of 28 years in total, until he returned to his home country again at the age of 65.

The tragedy *Brand* (쁘란드), written in Rome (로마), received a great welcome, earning enthusiastic praise such as "The emergence of a world-class poet." However, the verse play *Peer Gynt* (페아 균트) two years later suddenly became the focus of attack.

After that, whenever Ibsen's works appeared, praise and criticism were divided, but he paid no heed and continued to publish prose social dramas such as *A Doll's House* (人形의 家), *Ghosts* (幽靈), and *An Enemy of the People* (民衆의 敵). Among them, *A Doll's House* became a major stimulus regarding domestic issues and the problem of women's liberation, making Ibsen's name immortal (不杇ㅎ지함에 이르럿다).

In 1891, this old writer returned to his native mountains and wrote symbolic poetic dramas befitting an old man, before passing away amidst worldwide mourning on May 23, 1906, at the age of 78. At the poet's resting place, an obelisk (方尖碑) stands tall, as if symbolizing the life of this great writer who taught us to abandon compromise and pursue truth. <14>

**Summary**

This 1927 article commemorates the upcoming centennial of Norwegian playwright Henrik Ibsen (born 1828). It outlines his life, from his difficult youth and early struggles in the Norwegian theatre scene to his long self-imposed exile, during which he wrote internationally acclaimed but controversial plays like *Brand*, *Peer Gynt*, *A Doll's House*, *Ghosts*, and *An Enemy of the People*. The author, Paek Min, highlights Ibsen's unwavering commitment to truth and his impact on social issues, particularly women's liberation via *A Doll's House*, concluding with his death and legacy. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the article is significant as it shows Korean intellectuals engaging with modern European literature and its themes of social critique, realism, and individualism. Publishing a piece on Ibsen, known for challenging societal norms and advocating for truth ("眞理에 徹底"), could be seen as an indirect commentary or aspiration within the constrained environment of colonial Korea, particularly the discussion of *A Doll's House* resonating with emerging discussions on women's roles.

眞理에 徹底를 說한 놀웨의 大詩人, 百年祭를 當한 「헨릭 입센」
						白民
「헨릭 입센」은 지금붙어 100년전(1828년) 3월 20일 「놀웨」國 南方 씬이라는 적은 고을에서 났다. 그 고을에서는 상당히 명망이 있는 가문이었으나 그 父系로는 「떠이취」, 「스콜랜드」, 「덴막」등 외국인의 혈통이 많았다 함은 세계인으로 終始한 그의 일생과 비교하여 매우 흥미 있는 일이다.

8세에 아버지가 파산하여 일가가 분산하고 「입센」은 별로 학교교육도 없이 고을의 어떤 악제사의 從弟가 되었다. 거긔서 自修하면서 「크리스티아나」대학 의과 시험을 치르려고 「라텐」문을 암송하다가 그 역사가 感한 바 있어 3막의 운문극을 창작하였다.

이 때붙어 서정시도 짓고 또 당시의 혁명사상에 공명한 글도 발표하다가 약간의 文名을 어얻 24세 時에 「베르겐」市에 새로 설립된 「놀웨」 국민극장에 작자 겸 무대감독으로 초빙되었다. 이 시대는 후일의 위대한 극작가인 「입센」이 무대에 관한 지식을 얻는 수련 시기였다.
1857영(30세)에 「베르겐」을 떠나 「놀웨」의 國都 「크리스티아나」의 「놀웨」극장 총무로 취임하였다. 물질상으로는 조곰도 유족하지 못하였고 더구나 극장들은 소위 「외국 문화파」와 「국민 문화파」의 2者로 대립하여 무익한 경쟁으로 자멸하고 또 「입센」의 역작은 세간의 환영을 못 받고 돌이어 낙천적이요 청년인 「뵤른손」이 청년 「놀웨」의 문학적 대표자로 일반 국민의 大갈채를 받게 되어 「입센」은 크게 번민한 결과 1864년(37세)에 본국으로 떠나 외국 漂浪生活로 들어갔다. 65세에 다시 귀국하기까지 전후 28년의 유랑 생애가 시작된 것이었다.

「로마」에서 쓴 비극 「쁘란드」는 대환영을 받아 『세계적 시인의 출현』이라는 열광적 찬사를 얻게 되었으나 2년 후의 「페아 균트」라는 운문극은 홀연히 공격의 초점이 되었다.
그 뒤로는 「입센」의 작품이 출현할 때마다 讚貶이 상반하였으나 그는 이를 개의하지 아니하고 연속하여 「人形의 家」 「幽靈」 「民衆의 敵」등의 산문 사회극을 발표하였다. 그 중에도 「人形의 家」는 가정문제와 여성 해방문제에 대한 일대 자극제가 되어 「입센」의 이름을 不杇ㅎ지함에 이르럿다.

1891년에 이 老작가는 다시 故山으로 돌아와 노인답은 상징파적 시극을 쓰다가 1906년 5월 23일 78세로 세계적 애도 속에서 서거하였다. 시인의 安息之地에는 方尖碑가 타협을 버리고 진실을 추구하기를 가르키던 이 위대한 작가의 일생을 표상하는 것처럼 屹立하였다.＜14＞
