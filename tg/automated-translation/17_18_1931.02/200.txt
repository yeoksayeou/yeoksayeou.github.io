Accounts of Experiences in Choosing a School
						Various Seniors
A single experience is more valuable than a hundred theories. Believing that hearing the actual stories of respected seniors, who possess valuable experiences regarding the major concern of students—choosing an advanced school—is the most worthwhile path, we sent out the following questions. We believe their responses will be greatly informative for all of us. First and foremost, let us read the seniors' experiential accounts, and then engage in deep contemplation and earnest deliberation.

Questions
One. Which school did you graduate from?
Two. What were your reasons for choosing that school?
Three. Do you now think that choice was a good one? Or do you regret it as a mistake?
Four. What are the reasons? Above

Responses (In order of arrival)
Intending to be a Soldier, Ended up in Law
Yŏnhŭi Specialized School, Yu Ŏkkyŏm
One. Tokyo Imperial University, Faculty of Law, Department of Law.
Two. To briefly explain the path that led me to Tokyo University (東大 - Tōdai): When I was 12 (in 1907), one autumn evening, my late father (先考) asked, "What do you intend to study in the future?" Without hesitation, I respectfully replied (仰答), "I wish to become a soldier and serve as a cornerstone (柱石) for the Three Thousand Li Rivers and Mountains (三千里江山)." He immediately gave his ready consent (快諾). After consulting with the late Mr. No Paengnin (盧伯麟), the principal of Yŏnsŏng School (硏成學校) in Samch'ŏngdong (三淸洞), Hansŏng (漢城) at the time (equivalent to an Army Cadet School; its former site is now used by the Serum Manufacturing Institute - 血淸製造所), it was tentatively decided that I would enter Yŏnsŏng School in the spring after graduating from Kyesan Elementary School (桂山小學校) at the age of 13. However, Yŏnsŏng School, which I intended to enter after graduating from Kyesan School, was fated to close, and thus my sweet dream of becoming a soldier turned into an ephemeral dream (一場春夢). My late father again asked what field I wished to pursue. I respectfully replied that I would go to the United States (米國) to study law, and simultaneously received approval. However, several other elders within the family (堂內) suggested it would be better to complete university education in the East (東洋) before studying abroad (遊外), so that too became an ephemeral dream. Nevertheless, my determination to study law remained unchanged (不變). Therefore, <76> if one intended to major in law in the East, there was no choice but to select Tōdai, which was number one in the East and identical (同一) in name and reality (名實).
Three. I still believe that choice was a good one.
Four. I think it is most appropriate to choose any field of study based on one's individual interests and special skills (長技).

Women Too Need Higher Education
Ihwa Specialized School, Sin Ŭigyŏng
One. I finished Sendai's Tōhoku Imperial University (仙臺東北帝大).
Two. Korean women, emerging from a more miserable state (慘狀) than anyone else, must learn for the sake of their own future in this current situation. Not only that, but as women who will be responsible for the family, the foundation of the nation and society, a higher level of culture/refinement (敎養) is necessary as much as possible. I think of this learning in two ways: general education that reaches all women, and concurrently, special (特殊) education. Some argue that higher education for women is unnecessary, insisting only on general education, but until the day such an argument alone prevails, this human society will be an incomplete/disabled (不具) society, and the future for women will remain dark. It was from the motive (動機) of believing in the necessity of higher education for women that I went to Sendai. And the reason I continued and finished is primarily here as well.
Three. I believe that at the level of higher education, co-education (共學) is preferable. In the East, one must attend co-educational institutions to grasp the deep flavor of academics, and it also provides an opportunity (機會) to fully understand men in preparation for the future era of work. Not only that, but compared to studying at a women's school, there is much more stimulus (刺戟) in various aspects, naturally leading to more enthusiasm for academic research.
As I see it, Japanese education seems to be education close to solving the problem of "how to live." Western (西洋) education seems to be education that enables the solving of the problem of "how to live well." Of course, current society needs both types of education, but for me, seeing the problem of "how to live" as the urgent task (急先務) and prerequisite issue (先決問題), I went to Sendai.
Four. I think this choice was well made.

A Matter of Pure Coincidence
Sungsil Specialized School, Yang Chudong
One. Waseda University (早稻田大學).
Two. To enter Waseda University, one first had to graduate from Waseda Higher Academy (早稻田高等學院). My entrance into Waseda First Higher Academy (早稻田第一高等學院) was entirely coincidental (偶然). The day after (翌日) I blindly crossed over to Tōkyō (東京), I heard Waseda Higher Academy was holding its entrance examination, so I completed the application procedures (受驗手續) that day and took the exam (應試). Initially, I had graduated from Chungdong School (中東學校) in Kyŏngsŏng (京城), but at that time, the crash course (速成科) was an unprecedented program that completed the entire 5-year middle school curriculum in barely one year. So although I completed the course (業은 畢하엿으나), I lacked confidence in my academic ability (學力), especially my English proficiency which was poor (貧弱). Thus, while I applied to Waseda Higher Academy, I actually took the test half-jokingly, thinking, "What's the point?" But luckily, I passed (入格) and ended up studying (修學) there for six years. Well, perhaps it wasn't entirely coincidence. I recall hearing rumors (風聞) at the time that Waseda University had many outstanding professors in the literature department, and I also felt considerable yearning (憧憬) for it being a campus of free research. Once I actually entered, however, there were many points of disillusionment (幻滅).
Three. It wasn't particularly good, nor was there anything to regret. In my youth, I worshiped (崇拜) the school's name more than its content (내용) or real ability (실력), but as I gradually matured, I realized that any school, in short (요컨대), depends on one's own ability; it's up to how one studies (저 공부할 탓인 줄). I also saw through (看破) the fact that so-called school study is ultimately nothing more than a game (노름) to obtain a single diploma (졸업장), and grasped where (那邊) the meaning of modern schools lies. Right before graduation, I had thrown aside school subjects like novel translation reading and was absorbed in (몰두) the sociological study of art.
If I have any regrets, it's perhaps regretting not specializing in social sciences and studying pure literature instead, but it's unavoidable (無可奈何). <77>
Four. The reason is simple. Without social scientific research, even literature cannot be ultimately understood. Also, it's because I believe that the little bit of literary knowledge I possess could have been sufficiently acquired as common sense even if I hadn't specialized in literature.

Character Influence
Ŭnnyul, Kim Kyŏngha
I finished Sungsil University (崇實大學), the predecessor of the current P'yŏngyang Sungsil Specialized School (平壤崇實專門學校). The reason I entered and finished that school was that its principles (宗旨) and management (經營) aligned with my goal of majoring in theology (神學) and engaging in church work (敎會事業). Also, I admired (欽羡) and revered the various figures produced by that school, which sparked a desire to enter it. Furthermore, the missionary (宣敎師) who supported (後援) my school expenses also approved of my intention. Thinking back now, I believe entering that school at that time was the best thing I ever did among all my good deeds. The reason is that I recognize (自認) I grasped (透得) the foundation for the desire to do the work of a pastor—which I now consider the best work in the world and serve (奉職) in with gratitude—at that school. Additionally, the many eminent (高明) professors at that school not only taught academics well but, more importantly, their kind care (愛護) intertwined (엉키어) teacher and student (師弟간), providing much character influence (人格感化). Therefore, if I were ever given the fine opportunity to live as a student again, I would not go to another school but would seek out my alma mater, Sungsil University, once more.

Easy to Maintain Livelihood
Ch'ŏngju, Pak Ch'ŏn'gyu
One. I finished Kyŏngsŏng Imperial University (京城帝國大學), Faculty of Medicine.
Two. The reason I chose that school was to learn a specialized skill necessary for social life, and because it seemed the easiest (容易) way to maintain (維持) the economic independence (經濟獨立) of an individual or a family within the current environment (環境) of Chosŏn (朝鮮) society.
Three. I still think that choice was a good one.
Four. Because I can realize (實現) what I mentioned in the second answer.

A Lesson from Past Failure
Hwimun Kobo, Yi Il
One. I finished Law School (法學校) and Seminary (神學校).
Two. Around the time I entered Law School, the fervor for heroes (英雄熱) was quite high among Tōkyō exchange students. Swept up in that fervor, I vaguely (茫然) thought knowing law might be necessary to become a hero, so I studied it, then gave it up. Later, when I entered Seminary, I had considerable anguish (煩悶) and a fair amount of resolve. Disappointed after studying law, I decided to live a sacrificial (犧牲的) life, rescuing lives more ignorant (無智), weaker, and more pitiful (可憐) than my own. With the outstanding (秀常 - likely typo for 殊常 meaning unusual/special) thought of becoming a religious figure, casting aside so-called (所謂) personal success (成功), property, and fame, I decided to spend a life of service (奉仕). I spent five years in the religious school, returned to my homeland (故國), started well on the intended path, but then began to run off onto a side road (橫路), which is my current state.
Three. I don't think either choice was particularly wrong, but I lament (恨歎) that my circumstances (境遇) did not permit it and that I myself was weak.
People who finish commercial school and dedicate their lifetime (平生) to commerce, people who finish normal school and spend their lifetime in education – such types are happy people, and I consider them successful individuals unknown to others (남모르는). What I have gone through may not serve as a reference (参考) for my juniors (後進), but perhaps the overturned cart in front serving as a warning to the cart behind (前車之覆轍은 後車之戒) could be material? Even that might be more than I deserve (過分).

Politics and Economics
Tong'a Ilbo, Ham Sanghun
One. I finished Waseda University (早稻田大學), Department of Politics and Economics.
Two. After finishing middle school, I intended to go to high school, but I rested for two years due to illness (病). Seeing that preparing for entrance exams would take too much time, I chose a place that was easy to enter. While attending the preparatory course, I intended to study history or sociology, but a certain senior (某先輩) studying engineering (工學) told me to think about post-graduation life. I interpreted this as advice to study something that makes finding employment (就職) easy, so I chose that department.
Three. Now, I realize even more that the senior's words were correct. (Remainder omitted)

Interested in Politics
Sin T'aeyak
One. I graduated from Waseda University (早稻田大學), Department of Politics and Economics, and then graduated again from Chūō University (中央大學), Department of Law.
Two. My personality (性格) originally had an interest in the political sphere. Also, my family education in youth and social life in middle age fanned these flames, so choosing a school suitable (적당) for that led me to Waseda University. Thinking that law is most necessary for real social life and that Chūō University was the most suitable school for it, I graduated from there again.
Three. I still think it was a good choice.
Four. The reason is that it wasn't particularly (別로) different from my expectations. However, one thing to briefly mention here is that the Waseda Political Science department has completely changed in substance (實質) compared to when we were students. For law, among private schools (私學), Chūō University is perhaps still the best.

Women and Economic Independence
Tongyang Women's Hospital (東洋婦人病院), Yi Tŏkyo
One. I graduated from Tōkyō Women's Medical Specialized School (東京女子醫學專門學校).
Two. The reason I stubbornly finished this school was the awakening (覺醒) that from now on, women must absolutely become economically independent.
Three. I do not regret choosing medicine. Rather, there are many times I feel fortunate.
Four. The reason I feel fortunate is that, although lagging behind in everything, I am happy that in our society where hygiene concepts are particularly weak (薄弱), I can work, even with my meager strength, for women with weak bodies. Another thing is that because I have a profession, even after my husband went into exile (亡命), although I cannot live an affluent (潤澤) life, I feel fortunate to this extent that I do not suffer great hardship in making a living.

Newspaper Research
Tong'a Ilbo, Sŏl Ŭisik
Regarding my experiences before middle school, there's nothing to say, and even with experiences after that, I don't have the kind of "reference material" your company requires. My student life immediately after middle school ended in less than a year, and since then, I've mostly been loitering (꾸물거리다) in the real society (實社會). Thanks to a newspaper company, I did leave the real society for about three years under the pretext (名目) of "Newspaper Research," but I myself don't know if that qualifies as complete student life. Anyway, I'll try to write as asked.
One. I attended Nihon University (日本大學) in Tōkyō, but I don't have a diploma, so it's graduation without proof.
Two. The pretext was "Newspaper Research," but in reality (實狀), I wanted to study sociology. Nihon University was the only place with an independent (獨立된) sociology department. Also, there was a "Newspaper Society" (新聞學會) <79> where one could study newspapers and actually publish one, so I thought it was killing two birds with one stone. Besides this:
(a) For someone like me who didn't follow the direct path (順路), it was extremely convenient in various aspects like admission, study (修學), and commuting (通學).
(b) Course selection was free, and time allocation (配定) could be adjusted to one's own circumstances (형편).
(c) Almost all lectures (講座) within the campus were generally open, making auditing (聽講) other lectures easy (allowing utilization of spare time arising from professor absences, etc.).
(d) Since there was no school year system (學年制), one could arrange the sequence of subjects to be taken over three years and the number of subjects per year according to one's circumstances. In sum, the school regulations (學則) suited my circumstances and intentions.
Three. I still think that choice was a good one.
Four. The reason is the same as the reason I chose that place when I entered.

I Regret It
P'yŏngyang, Kim Sŏng'ŏp
Regarding your company's questions, I am a person unqualified (未備) to answer. However, I will briefly state my experience.
One. I entered Posŏng Law School (普成法律學校) but dropped out (퇴학) midway.
Two. Since I didn't finish school, there's nothing to say about the reason for graduating.
Three. I regret choosing the Law School.
Four. The reasons for regret are as follows:
Studying at a specialized school means establishing the foundation (基本) for future effort and activity (活動) in that field in social life. Therefore, the chosen major must match one's personality (性格) and interests. Being bound (구속되다) by the difficulty of entrance exams (入學難) and forcing oneself (억지로) to attend a specialized school one does not desire (志望) is absolutely forbidden for someone planning for the future. Thus, the reason I still regret it to this day is that the subject of law did not suit my personality and interests.

Studying in Switzerland
Kim Tong-sŏk
I studied abroad (留學) at the National University in Zurich (쥬리히), Switzerland (瑞西). Among the numerous (하고많은) schools, the reason I ended up there stems from hearing much about the place from Mr. Yi Kwanyong (李灌鎔). At that time, many went to the United States (米國), where the possibility (可能性) of working one's way through school was considered high, but few people studied abroad in Europe (歐洲). I particularly wanted to go to Europe. Since Europe at that time was immediately after the Great War (大戰卽後 - WWI), everything was unsettled and in a pathological state (病的狀態). There was concern that if things went wrong, going to Europe might cause more harm (弊害) to someone young like me. Thus, I ended up going to Switzerland, which was said to be relatively (比較的) stable (安定). Switzerland is not only in the middle of Europe, but its learning/scholarship (學術) is also said to be central to European culture. More importantly, because everything is neutral (中立), they are said to maintain a fair (公正) attitude (態度) in academic research. Another reason was that famous exiles from around the world gathered there, offering the benefit of getting to know such people somewhat closely. Furthermore, Switzerland is known as the "World Park" (世界公園) for its scenery (風景), which was also considered good. Hearing only these merits (長處), I chose Switzerland and departed for that place. I didn't know if it was true or not when I went, but after visiting Germany (獨逸) several years later, I realized it was truly great good fortune (天幸) that someone as young as me hadn't come here [Germany] first. So, I kept my school registration (學籍) only in Switzerland and studied only there. I still hope that many of our students will study abroad in Switzerland. As I thought when I went, Switzerland can truly be called the center of European culture. At the university I attended, people from over 30 countries were registered. Also, there is no interference (干涉) whatsoever in academic research, and absolute freedom (絶對自由) in expressing thoughts (思想發表), making such a place rare (드물다). Switzerland's scenery is beautiful <80>, making it good for traveling around with books. Also, there are indeed many exiles from various countries, allowing close association (交際). International conferences (國際會議) are often held in Switzerland, so opportunities to attend (參席) arise occasionally, which is also good. Moreover, since Switzerland is a neutral country, public opinion (여론) can be considered relatively fair, allowing one to receive European public opinion more or less (다소간에) directly (바로).

Choose Practical Courses
Taegu, Sŏ Sangil
Your company's "Advanced School Choice Issue," aiming to guide the tens of thousands of graduates across all of Chosŏn (全朝鮮) in selecting advanced schools, is a timely initiative.

The advanced schools in question include those for advanced general education (普通敎育) and those for advanced specialized education (專門敎育). The topic (命題) here concerns advanced schools for receiving specialized education. Generally, if specialized education can be divided (녾아) into education for interest (趣味敎育) and vocational education (職業敎育), we must first receive vocational education rather than education for interest. We are in a situation (處地) and environment where we cannot but receive it. Then, in choosing a school for this vocational education, it is necessary to first consider (考察) what kind of occupation is suitable for our societal objective conditions (客觀條件), or suitable for each individual's subjective conditions (主觀條件), or fitting (可合) for each individual's personal talent (天才), and make the choice based on that determination (전제로 할 필요가 잇다고 한다).
Since we are Koreans, only vocational education that corresponds (당응한) to the objective conditions of Chosŏn society, after clearly recognizing (認識) and grasping (파악) them, will lead to a future for the intended occupation. As we know, isn't Chosŏn an agricultural nation from a geographical viewpoint (地理的 見地)? To build (건설) a world-ideal (世界的 理想) agricultural nation like Denmark (丁抹), agricultural and forestry schools (農林學校) are solely needed. Whatever else may be said, the first path to revival (更生) for the Korean people, currently struggling in this state of demise, lies only in a group of sound (건전한) youths (靑年群), having finished agricultural and forestry schools, returning to the farming villages, deeply planting the roots of life (生의 뿌り) in the farmland (農土), and simultaneously guiding and rescuing (救濟) the peasant masses (農民大衆) still weeping under the shackles (桎梏) of feudal remnants (封建的 遺制). Next is to accurately discover and recognize the talent of the Korean people. From a historical viewpoint (歷史的 見地), aren't Koreans an artistic people? If it's an undeniable fact that the talent of Koreans, inherited through 4,000 years of history, possesses artistic creativity (創作性), shouldn't we maximize the display (발휘) of technical talent (技工的 天才) harmonious (調和) with that national character, aiming to contribute (공헌) to world culture as Koreans through technical skill like Switzerland (瑞西)? Doesn't this necessitate technical schools (技工學校)? It's not that other specialized education like commerce (商), medicine (醫), law (法), literature (文), etc., isn't necessary, but in terms of specialized (專門的) unique education (特殊敎育) looking ahead (전망하는) to the future of Chosŏn and Koreans, agricultural/forestry schools and technical schools are particularly crucial (절실하다). To reiterate, the development (발전) of the economic life of Chosŏn and Koreans depends entirely (전연히) on agricultural/forestry production and handicraft/technical industry and textile industry (纖維工業) production. This holds true whether in the age of capitalism (資本主義) characterized by commercial production or the age of socialism (社會主義) using the production of wealth (富의 生産) as a means (手段); our economic revival (復活) can only be found here. Besides this, since Chosŏn is a peninsula surrounded by sea on three sides (三面環海), maritime education (海事敎育) should be necessary. As the present is the age of electrification (電化時代) and the future is the age of aviation (航空時代), aren't electricity and aviation education also necessary? Aren't all these practical courses (實科) both vocational education and the fundamental education (根本敎育) for the economic revival of Koreans? To the tens of thousands of graduates across Chosŏn, how about choosing such vocational schools (實業學校)? Some might say, in today's Chosŏn where Koreans have suffered economic ruin (破滅), even if one finishes a roundabout (迂遠) vocational school, what way (方途) of application (應用) could there be in Chosŏn where the environment doesn't permit it? However, having learned (學得) a skill, one of the human abilities indispensable for human survival (生存), there should be no reason why one cannot survive. Indeed (모름지기), this is the skill and power (動力) that will save us who have fallen into death. Whether for pioneering (開拓) one's own distant (久遠) future or for the long-term plan (百年計) of national revival, is there any shorter path (捷徑) than stepping back and weaving a net (退而結網) instead of just envying fish by the deep pool (臨淵羡魚)? <81> Do not laugh at (笑하다) the roundabout path, nor lament the adversity (逆境). That roundabout path is the shortcut; that adversity is the ordeal (시련). (Remainder omitted - original text)

The Wind of Freedom

One. I finished the Specialized Division, Department of Politics and Economics at Tōkyō Waseda University (東京 早稻田大學 專門部 政治經濟科) in Taishō 12 (1923).
Two. There's no special reason. Among private schools, it had a very free atmosphere, and there were many new/progressive professors (新進敎授), making it convenient (便宜) for receiving guidance, so I finished there.
Three. Of course, I still think it was suitable, and I have no particular regrets.
Four. Within the scope (범위) permitted by our current economy, studying in Tōkyō is the shortest path (捷徑). Believing there's nothing more or less than that, that's why.

Comparing with Social Circumstances
Kongju Yŏngmyŏng School, Hwang Insik
One. Graduated from the Secondary Education Department, Teachers College, Columbia University, New York City, United States of America (北美合衆國 紐育市 콜럼비아 大學師範學校 中等敎育科).
Two. Because I had an interest (취미를 懷한 故) in guiding youths aged sixteen or seventeen, whose future prospects (前途) are distant/vague (杳然) and whose psychological (心理上) comprehension ability (理解力) and physical (身體上) physiological state (生理狀態) are flourishing (方盛).
Three. The chosen subject is good as far as it goes. However, looking back (回顧) at the circumstances (形便) of our society, which is constantly changing (변천하여 가는), I feel some regret.
Four. Deeply considering the actual situation within Chosŏn, what is needed is not high-level knowledge or science like in civilized Western countries, but vocational education (實業敎育), namely agricultural, commercial, and industrial school education (農商工學校敎育), to guide the entire nation wandering (방황하고 잇는) on the death line (死線) towards a path to survival (活路). The choice of an advanced school depends on each individual's personality (個性) and interests, but my sincere advice is that numerous (다수한) young graduates should gather (운집하야) in various vocational schools (實業學校) and, with an industrial spirit (産業精神), restore (회복케 함에 在하다 합니다) the households (門戶) that have been declared bankrupt (破産宣告).

Agricultural and Forestry School
Taegu, Song Kich'an
I am someone who has only finished a 4-year common school and hasn't seen any higher school, so I lack the qualification to answer about school choice. However, if you insist on asking which school I would go to now if I were to enter one, I would unhesitatingly point to an Agricultural and Forestry School (農林學校). In the future, I would also send my younger siblings or children there, as long as there isn't a major gap (懸隔) in their personalities. The reason is very simple. Whatever one says, Chosŏn is an agricultural nation, so future prosperity or further decline hinges entirely on how the rural problem is resolved.

Several Regrets
Nyŏngbyŏn, Ch'ae Ubyŏng
One. I finished the Literature Department (文科) at Yŏnhŭi Specialized School (延禧專門學校).
Two. The reasons for choosing Yŏnhŭi's Literature Department are as follows:
First, I thought my aptitude (素質) was unsuitable (不適) for technical fields. Therefore, I never even dreamed of Higher Technical School (高工) or Higher Commercial School (高商). However, unlike common misconceptions about the Literature Department, I didn't intend to become a writer.
Second, I hoped to study abroad (外國留學). Although one could say it's possible after finishing any school, Yŏnhŭi, in particular, had many foreigners and seniors who had studied abroad, so I thought it would be helpful for studying foreign languages (外國語 學修), various introductions (諸般紹介) related to studying abroad, and procuring funds (辦費) for school expenses (學資) – like scholarships ("스칼라쉽"), for instance.
Third, I had the intention of majoring in history (歷史科). It wouldn't be an exaggeration to say Yŏnhŭi's Literature Department was practically a History Department (史學科). Starting with Western History, there were the most history hours, including East Asian History, History of Philosophy, History of Education, History of Literature, etc.
Fourth, if I were to mention it, perhaps the school expenses? Although both are Literature Departments, Kyŏngsŏng University (城大) required 6 years including the preparatory course, while Yŏnhŭi was 4 years. Furthermore, at Yŏnhŭi, there were some ways to work one's way through school. <82>
Three. Therefore, I decided on Yŏnhŭi from my third or fourth year of middle school and have not regretted it since graduating.
Four. However, there are several regrets (멫가지 遺憾). These are:
1. Changing the course (코-쓰) I initially started and settling on the Education Department (敎育科) – and even then, not being able to continue majoring in it.
2. The content of the Literature Department at my alma mater at that time was not very complete (完備) – I don't want to point out specifics.
3. Being tied down (얽매어) by matters other than a student's duty (當務) during my time there, wasting (낭비한) much of the considerable period of four years.

Emphasizing Engineering
P'yŏngyang, O Chŏngsu
I (記者 - Reporter/Writer, used self-referentially) finished the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (北美 매세추셋쓰 工業大學 - M. I. T) in North America. The reason for choosing that particular school among many engineering schools (工學校) is simply that I chose a famous (名聲높은) school, but the main point (要點) of the question is probably "Why did you choose an engineering school?"
Two. Reason for choosing an engineering school. The proportion (比例) of subject choices in our student community was very different 15 years ago compared to now. In recent years, the number of engineering students (工學生) is not less than (不下하지마는) second or third compared to other departments, but 14 or 15 years ago, engineering students were almost non-existent. The rarity (稀貴) of engineering students among us was the main reason I chose engineering. After passing through Shanghai (上海) and landing (下陸) in the United States, I was greatly envious of others' material civilization (物質文明). Even with my young thoughts at that time, I realized that for our nation's rise (勃興), the completion (完成) of ourselves (自體) was the urgent task (急先務), and I thought national wealth and power (富强) would be absolutely impossible if we ignored industry. Amidst this, the state of our student community, both at home and abroad (海內外), neglected (等閑히 하엿습니다) engineering. To satisfy the vigorous (勃勃) heroic spirit of youth, engineering was definitely not the way. (At that time, I thought heroes were necessarily politicians, and politicians necessarily had to study law or political science).
A sense of duty (義務感), thinking "Our society's demand is industry," overwhelmed (압도시키) the heroic spirit, so I began preparing for engineering school entrance from my middle school days.
Three. Reason why I still think that choice was good.
Seeing the thought that industry cannot be ignored spreading more widely among our people day by day confirms that choosing an engineering school was not a mistake. More than others, we are a people facing a life-or-death problem (死活問題). We must live first, so the bread issue must be solved first. Materially speaking, in solving the bread issue, those possessing technical skills are comparatively superior. I am by no means saying that just studying engineering makes everything go smoothly. On the contrary, even if we have extraordinary engineering skills and experience (經歷), opportunities to utilize (活用) them freely are scarce. Not only is capital (資本) lacking (缺乏) in our society, but there is also the tragedy (비극) that because trust/harmony (信調 - unusual term) among ourselves is weak, capitalists and technologists do not cooperate (協同) well. It is a fact that even graduating from engineering faces job-finding difficulties (就職難), and it is also a fact that even capable engineers cannot freely utilize their skills. At the same time, the time when our nation will increasingly benefit from the power of industry is drawing closer day by day, and it is also an undeniable fact that the scope (餘地) for utilizing industrial technology is gradually expanding (擴張).

**Summary**

This 1931 article from *Tonggwang* magazine compiles advice from prominent Korean seniors on choosing advanced schooling (specialized schools or universities). Respondents, graduates of institutions in Korea, Japan, the US, and Europe, share their reasons for choosing specific schools and fields (law, medicine, literature, economics, engineering, theology, education, biology, social science) and reflect on whether their choices were successful. Common themes include the importance of matching studies to personal interest and aptitude, the perceived differences between Japanese ("how to live") and Western ("how to live well") education, the necessity of practical/vocational training (especially agriculture, engineering, commerce, medicine) for personal economic independence and national revival in colonial Korea, and the challenges faced by Koreans, including limited opportunities and economic hardship. Several contributors express regret over pursuing non-practical fields or highlight the difficulties even skilled graduates face in the colonial context.

From a historical perspective, the article vividly reflects the anxieties and aspirations of the Korean elite under Japanese rule. The strong emphasis on practical skills (engineering, agriculture, medicine) reveals a pragmatic approach to survival and nation-building within colonial constraints. The debates around studying in Japan versus the West (including neutral Switzerland), the specific mention of difficulties faced by women (Sin Ŭigyŏng, Yi Tŏkyo), and the acknowledgement of limited opportunities even for the highly educated (O Chŏngsu) offer valuable insights into the socio-economic realities and intellectual currents of 1930s Korea. The explicit connection made between education choices (especially vocational) and national economic revival (Sŏ Sangil, Hwang Insik, Song Kich'an, O Chŏngsu) underscores the nationalist undercurrents within seemingly personal decisions.

學校選擇體驗談
						先輩諸氏
백가지 理論보다 한 가지 体驗이 더욱 귀하외다. 우리는 學生諸君의 一大 苦憫問題인 上級學校選擇에 대하야 귀중한 체험을 가지신 先輩諸氏의 實際談을 들어 봄이 가장 값잇는 길이라 하야 다음과 같은 물음을 보내엇습니다. 그들의 回答은 다 우리들에게 크게 參考가 될 것인 줄 압니다. 무엇보다 먼저 先輩의 體驗談을 읽고 뒤에 沈思熱慮하십시다.

設問
一. 先生은 어느 學校 出身이십니까.
二. 그 學校를 택하신 理由가 무엇입니까.
三. 지금 그 選擇이 잘 되엇다고 생각하십니까. 혹은 잘못되엇다고 後悔하십니까.
四. 그 理由는? 以上

回答(到着順)
軍人되려다가 法律로
延禧專門 兪億兼
一. 東京帝國大學法學部法律學科.
二. 東大에 가게된 經路를 간략히 말하면 이럿습니다. 12歲時(1907년) 어느 가을 저녁에 生의 先考께서 「너는 장래에 무슨 工夫를 하려느냐」물으시기에 躊躇치 아니하고 「軍人이 되어 三千里江山에 柱石이 되여 보고저 합니다」라고 仰答하엿더니 즉석에 快諾하시고 당시 漢城三淸洞에 잇는 硏成學校(陸軍幼年學校에 해당함. 그 古址는 現今 血淸製造所로 사용함) 校長 故盧伯麟氏와 상의하신 결과 13세되는 春期에 桂山小學校를 졸업하면 硏成學校에 입학하기로 내정 하엿섯습니다. 桂校를 졸업하면 입학하려든 硏成學校는 閉校될 運命에 이르럿섯음으로 軍人되려 하든 단 꿈은 一場春夢이 되고 말앗습니다. 다시 生의 先考께서 지원하는 학과를 물으시기에 米國에 渡往하야 法律學을 공부하겟습니다 라고 仰答하고 동시에 응락을 받앗섯으나 堂內 다른 어른 몇분께서 大學敎育가지 東洋에서 받고 遊外함이 좋다 하섯기 때문에 其亦 春夢이 되고 말앗습니다. 그러나 法律學을 공부하겟다 하든 결의만은 不變하엿습니다. 그럼으로＜76＞ 東洋에서 法律學을 전공하자하면 名實이 同一하고 東洋 제일인 東大를 아니 선택할 수 없엇습니다.
三. 지굼도 그 선택을 좋앗다고 생각합니다.
四. 무슨 學問이든지 각개인의 趣味와 長技에 의하야 선택함에 지당하다고 생각합니다.

女子도 高等敎育
梨花專門 辛義卿
一. 仙臺東北帝大를 마추엇습니다.
二. 누구보다도 더욱 慘狀에서 살아 나아가는 朝鮮女性은 그 자체의 장래를 위하야 배우지 아니하면 안될 이 現象에 잇습니다. 뿐만 아니라 國家와 社會의 근본인 가정을 맡을 女性으로서는 될 수 잇는대로 보다 높은 敎養이 필요하외다. 나는 이 배흠을 두 가지로 생각하는데 女性全體에 및는 一般的 敎育과 동시에 특수한 敎育 이것이외다. 혹은 말하기를 女性의 高等敎育이 불필요하다 하고 일반적 교육만을 주장하나 이러한 주장만이 성립되는 날까지 이 人類社會는 不具의 社會, 女性의 前途는 여전히 暗黑일 것이외다. 나는 女性의 高等敎育을 어대까지 필요로 생각하는 動機에서 仙臺로 갓습니다. 또 게속하여 마친 리유도 여기 주요히 잇겟지오.
三. 高等敎育 정도까지 가서는 共學이라야만 좋을 줄 압니다. 東洋에서는 공학을 하여야 學問의 깊은 맛을 얻을 수가 잇고 장래 事業時代準備로도 충분히 男性을 이해할 수 잇는 機會가 됩니다. 그 뿐만 아니라 女學校에서 工夫하는 것 보다 여러 가지에 刺戟이 많아서 자연히 學問硏究에 열심하게 됩니다.
내가 보는 대로는 日本의 敎育은 「어떠케 살가」의 問題를 해결하는 데에 가까운 敎育이요. 西洋의 敎育은 「어떠케 하면 잘 살가」의 問題를 해결케 하는 敎育인 듯 합니다. 물론 現社會는 이 두 가지의 敎育이 다 필요합니다마는 나로서는 急先務로 어떠케 살가의 문제를 先決問題로 보는데서 仙臺를 가게 되엇습니다.
四. 이러한 선택은 잘 한 것이라고 생각합니다.

전혀 偶然의 일
崇實專門 梁柱東
一. 早稻田大學.
二. 早大에 입학을 하려면 먼저 早稻田高等學院을 畢業하여야 하는데 내가 早稻田第一高等學院에 入學을 하게 된 것은 전혀 우연이엇습니다. 덮어놓고 東京을 건너간 翌日에 早高에서 入學試驗을 본다 하기로 그날로 受驗手續을 하고 應試하엿습니다. 당초에 나는 京城서 中東學校를 졸업하엿으나 그때 速成科가 근근 1個年동안에 中學 5個年 全課程을 果修하는 前無後無의 速成科엿음으로 業은 畢하엿으나 學力에 自信이 없엇고 특히 英語實力이 貧弱하야 早高에 應試는 하면서도 其實은 웬걸되리하고 작난삼아 처 보앗든 것입니다. 그랫더니 요행으로 入格이 되어서 끝끝내 6년간을 修學하게 되엇습니다. 하기는 전연히 우연이랄 순 없지오. 當時 早大에 文科敎授로 優秀한 이가 많다는 말을 風聞에 들엇고 또 自由硏究의 學園이라기에 적지 안은 憧憬조차 가젓든 생각이 납니다. 막상 들어가본 즉 幻滅되는 점이 많앗습니다.
三. 좋앗달 것도 없고 후회할 것도 없엇습니다. 少年時代에는 學校이름을 내용이나 실력보다 더 崇拜하엿으나 차차 철이 듦에 및어서 學校는 어느 學校든지 요컨대 제 實力本位요. 저 공부할 탓인 줄 깨달앗고 또 所謂 학교공부가 결국은 卒業狀 一張 얻기 노름에 불과한 것, 내지 현대 학교의 意義가 那邊에 잇는가를 看破하엿습니다. 졸업 바로 전에는 學校科目인 小說譯讀 같은 것은 집어치우고 藝術의 社會學的 硏究方面에만 몰두하고 잇엇으니까요.
後悔가 잇다면 그저 社會科學을 전문하지 안코 純文學工夫를 한 것이 좀 후회나는가 합니다마는 無可奈何입니다.＜77＞
四. 理由야 간단하지오. 社會科學的 硏究가 없이는 文學조차도 窮極的 理解를 할 수가 없으니까요. 또 다시 내가 가진 文學의 智識, 그 쥐꼬리만한 것 쯤은 文學을 專門하지 안앗드라도 넉넉히 常識으로 獲得하엿으리라 믿는 까닭입니다.

人格感化를
殷栗 金京河
나는 현재 平壤崇實專門學校前身인 崇實大學을 마치엇습니다. 그 學校에 들어가 그 학교를 마친 리유는 그 학교의 宗旨와 經營이 장차 神學을 전공하여 가지고 敎會事業에 종사하려는 나의 목적과 일치함으로써요, 또는 그 학교에서 배출된 여러 人物들을 欽羡하고 崇拜하는 맘이 일어나 그 學校入學을 지망하든 중 나의 學費 後援하는 宣敎師도 내 뜻을 찬동함으로써 이엇습니다. 지금도 생각하면 그때에 그 學校에 들어간 것이 나의 잘한 일 중에도 제일 잘 한 일이라고 생각합니다. 그 까닭은 내가 지금 世上事業중에 제일 좋은 사업으로 인정하고 또 내가 감사함으로 奉職하는 牧師일 볼 맘의 기초를 그 學校에서 透得하엿다고 自認하는 것이외다. 또는 그 學校에 高明하신 여러 敎授들이 學問도 잘 가르처 주려니와 더욱 친절한 愛護가 師弟간에 엉키어 人格感化를 더 많이 주든 까닭입니다. 그럼으로 나는 이제 다시 學生生活할 好機會를 만난다면 다른 학교로 가지 안코 또한 母校인 崇大를 찾아갈 맘이 잇습니다.

生活維持容易
淸州 朴天圭
一. 京城帝國大學 醫學部를 마추엇습니다.
二. 그 學校를 選擇한 이유는 社會的 生活에 필요한 한 가지의 專門的 技術을 배우고 또 그것이 現下 朝鮮社會의 환경에 처하야 一個人이나 一家族의 經濟獨立을 維持하기에 가장 容易한 줄로 생각된 때문이외다.
三. 지금도 그 선택을 좋앗다고 생각합니다.
四. 第二問에서 말한 것을 實現할 수 있기 때문이외다.

後車之誡

工學을 注重
平壤 吳楨洙
記者는 北美 매세추셋쓰 工業大學(M. I. T)을 마추엇습니다. 많은 工學校중 특히 그 學校를 택한 이유는 간단히 名聲높은 學校를 택하느란 것에 불과하겟지마는 問題의 要點은 「어찌하야 한 工學校를 선택하엿나뇨」일 줄 압니다.
二. 한 工學校를 택한 理由. 우리 學生界의 科目選擇의 比例가 15년 전만도 지금과 많이 달랏습니다. 近年은 工學生數가 他科門學生 수에 비하야 第二三位에 不下하지마는 十四五년 전은 工學生이 거이 없다 싶이 되엇습니다. 우리 중 工學生 稀貴한 것이 記者의 工學을 택한 重要理由외다. 上海를 거처 米國下陸을 해 놓고 보니 남의 物質文明狀態가 심히 부러웟습니다. 그 時 어린 생각에도 우리 民族의 勃興에는 우리 자체의 完成이 急先務임을 각오하엿으며 工業을 無視코는 民族의 富强이 절대 不能일 줄 생각하엿습니다. 그런 중 海內外 우리 學生界의 狀態는 工學을 等閑히 하엿습니다. 少年時代에 勃勃한 英雄心을 만족하게 함에는 절대로 工學은 아니엇습니다.(其時 생각에는 英雄은 반드시 政治家요. 政治家는 반드시 法律이나 政治學을 공부한 者야 되는 줄 안 고로).
「우리 社會의 요구는 工業이다」하는 일종의 義務感이 英雄心을 압도시킴에 따라 中學時代부터 工學校 入學準備를 하얏습니다.
三. 지금도 그 선택이 좋앗다고 생각하는 理由.
工業을 無視할 수 없다하는 생각이 日日 우리 民族중에 더 넓히 퍼짐을 보드라도 工學校를 택한 것을 錯誤없음을 알 것이외다. 남달리 우리는 死活問題에 迫到한 민족이외다. 살아놓고야 볼 노릇이니 빵 문제를 먼저 해결해야 되겟습니다. 物質的으로 들어가 빵 문제를 해결하는데는 아무래도 技術의 所有者가 比較的 優勝합니다. 工學만 공부하면 만사가 다 享通한단 말은 결코 아닙니다. 반대로 우리는 아무리 비상한 工學의 技術과 經歷이 잇다 하야도 맘대로 利用할 기회가 적습니다. 우리 社會에는 資本이 缺乏할 뿐 外라. 우리끼리의 信調가 흐린 까닭에 資本家와 技術家가 잘 協同되지 안는 비극까지 잇는 터입니다. 아무리 공학을 卒業하야도 就職難을 당할 것도 사실이요. 아무리 능한 技師라도 맘대로 技術을 活用치 못할 것도 事實인 동시에 우리 민족이 工業의 힘을 점점 더 입을 때도 나날이 더 가까와 오며 工業技術을 活用할 餘地가 점점 워더 擴張됨도 부인치 못할 사실인 줄로 인정하는 바올시다.
