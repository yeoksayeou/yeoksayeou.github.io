Article Title: 새벽 (Dawn)
Author: 金泰午
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Dawn
						Kim T'aeo

Friend,
Even in this field where fierce winds (狂風) blow
And only darkness (暗黑) fills the space,
The distant dawn breaks quietly.
Cock-a-doodle-doo, cock-a-doodle-doo,
The rooster hastening the dawn
Crows loudly and strongly.

Friend!
Beyond the horizon (地平線),
Tearing apart the black curtain (帳幕),
The distant dawn breaks brightly.
Clenching both fists tightly,
Keeping step with the rooster's crow,
Let us march forward vigorously and powerfully.

October 8, 1930, Dawn<38>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim T'aeo uses the metaphor of dawn breaking after a dark night filled with harsh winds to express hope and encourage action. Addressing a "friend," the speaker points to the rooster's crow and the arriving light as signals to clench their fists and march forward with strength and vigor, leaving the darkness behind. From a colonial history perspective, the poem is significant for its likely allegorical representation of the oppressive Japanese colonial rule (darkness, harsh wind) and the nascent hope for liberation or national awakening (dawn, rooster's crow). The call to "march forward vigorously and powerfully" can be interpreted as a subtle encouragement for collective action or resistance during a period of intense suppression.

벗이어
모진 狂風 불고

먼동이 고요히 트입니다
꼬기요, 꼬기요,
새벽을 재촉하는 닭은

벗이어!
地平線 넘어로
검은 帳幕을 찢어 트리며
먼동이 환하게 트입니다
두 주먹 불끈 쥐고
닭 소리에 발을 마추어
긔운차게 힘잇게 나아갑시다.
