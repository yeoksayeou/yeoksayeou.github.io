O Great Power! Rise Up, My Song Sung from the Heart, Kneeling Sincerely at the First Dawn of the New Year
						Kim Hae-gang (金海剛)

It has been long since this land turned its back on the sun.
Will it forever remain breathless,
with the heavy, unopenable bolt drawn across?
Will it build a cold tomb and be done?
Unable to beat its chest freely even once, unable to stomp its feet even once—

The sun rises and passes. It sets and rises.
Day after day. Month after month—
Year after year,
Has even one of the things believed in, hoped for, resolved, and prepared for each year come to pass as intended?
As time goes by, only the heart tightens, only the breath constricts—

The valleys and the villages and markets clinging to the edges of this land,
Will their strong pulse ever beat loudly one day?
The desire to break off a piece of time,
which runs on without stopping,
running from eternity to eternity,
and bury an inextinguishable lump of [fire?], sets the whole body aflame—

The stuffiness of this day, pressed down by heavy layers of clouds,
The hearts standing on the gallows where life cannot be guaranteed,
How could even their breathing be whole?
How could possessing a body be [free?]?
The desire to rip the layers of clouds to shreds and scatter them, to lift the globe and throw it down, pours out fierce flames—

Oh, Great Power! Rise up.
When I swing my fist, does even a small wind rise?
When I strike the wall, is there even a small echo?
O Great Power that will grab the obstructing bolt, push it aside, and bring in the light!
Rise up.
O Great Power that will sweep away all sleep-talking, and thus ring out a great sound like a spring dawn! Rise up!

Earnestly, we roll our sincerity, like heaven, gathering the hearts of the multitude into one mass.
Oh, Great, Great Power! Rise up in this land.
Strike the flank of the fleeing sun,
Pour out fiercely burning flames,
And thus, O Great Power that will breathe [life?] into the valleys and markets of this [nation?] whose blood vessels are hardening! Rise up. 1932. 1. 1 <81>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Hae-gang, published in January 1932, is a fervent plea for a "Great Power" to emerge and revitalize Korea, depicted as a land long shrouded in darkness and oppression ("turned its back on the sun," "heavy bolt," "gallows"). The poet expresses deep frustration with the passage of time yielding no progress and the stifling atmosphere preventing freedom of expression or action. The poem calls for a radical force to break through the constraints, bring light (liberation), and revive the nation's dying pulse, unifying the people's hearts in this desperate longing. For historians, the poem vividly reflects the despair and yearning for independence among Koreans during the height of Japanese colonial rule. The explicit metaphors of imprisonment, suffocation, and death, coupled with the urgent, almost desperate call for a messianic "Great Power," underscore the psychological and political conditions of the time. The censored words ('XX') themselves are artifacts of the era's strict censorship, hinting at forbidden concepts like nationhood, freedom, or resistance.

큰 힘이어! 솟아나소서, 동트는 새해 첫새벽에 정성스러이 무릅꿀고 앉아 마음으로 부르든 나의 노래
						金海剛
이 땅은 태양을 등진 지 오래 외다.
열 수 없는 무거운 빗장이 가로질린 그대로
기리 숨이 잠겨 버리고 마오리까.
싸늘한 무덤을 짓고 마오리까.
시원하게 가슴한번 처 보지 못 하옵고 발 한번 굴러보지 못 하옵고-

해는 돋고 지나이다. 지고 돋나이다.
날에 날. 달에 달-
한해 또 한해,
해마다 믿고, 바라고, 다지고, 벼르든 그 일이 한가지나 뜻대로 이루어짐이 잇어 왓나이까.
갈수록 가슴만 매여질 뿐이오니, 숨통만 죄어들 뿐이오니-

이 땅의 골작이와 변두리에 들어 붙은 마을과 저자는,
어느 날이나 굵은 맥박이 크게 뛰어 보오리까.
멈춤이 없이 달아나는,
영겁에서 영겁으로 달아나는
때의 한 토막을 툭 부러트려, 꺼짐 없는 XX덩어리를 묻어 보고 싶은 마음, 왼 몸을 달구옵거든-

무거운 구름장이 덮어 누르는 이 날의 답답함,
목숨을 기약할 수 없는 교수대 우에 올라선 마음들,
호흡인들 온전 하오리까.
몸 가지긴들 XX로우리까.
구름장을 짝짝 찢어 허트리고, 지구덩이를 번쩍 들어 떠다 곤지고 싶은 마음, 확확 불길을 쏟거든-

오오 큰 힘이어! 솟아나소서.
주먹을 휘저으오니 적은 바람이나 일더이까.
벽을 치오니 적은 반향이나 잇더이까.
가로질린 빗장을 잡어 제치고 광명을 저다놓을 큰 힘이어!
솟아나소서.
모든 잠꼬대를 휩쓸어 버릴, 그리하야 봄 새벽 같은 큰 소리 울려 줄 큰 힘이어! 솟아나소서!

오즉 무리의 마음을 하나로 덩어리지어 하눌 같은 정성을 굴리읍나니
오오 크나 큰 힘이어! 이 땅에 솟아나소서.
달아나는 태양의 엽갈비를 내질러,
활활 거리는 불길을 쏟아트릴
그리하야 혈맥이 굳어가는 이 X의 골작이와 저자에 호흡을 XX처 줄 큰 힘이어! 솟아나소서. 1932. 1. 1 ＜81＞
