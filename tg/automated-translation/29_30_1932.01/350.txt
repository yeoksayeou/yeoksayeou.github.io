Travelogue First Place
Morning at Tan'gun Tae
Chaejyŏng Myŏngsin Third Year, Yi Sangjun

Our party, having arrived at Paegyŏpsa Temple on Kujŏngsan Mountain, spent the night with the round autumn moon of sorrow (愁月). In the early morning, plucking maple leaves shining beautifully in the sunlight, we are climbing the mountain, seeking Tan'gun Tae (檀君臺), the trace of our ancestral grandfather from half-ten-thousand years ago. Though our legs are weary from the hundred-*li* journey, without anyone saying the path is rough or the mountain high, literally with solemn attitudes, we search for the long-yearned-for Tan'gun Tae, pushing through *mŏru* vines (머레넝쿨), into the *tarae* vines (다래넉꿀), following our persimmon wood staffs (고양나무 단장), climbing and climbing still.
Two or three mountain birds startled from their perch in a pine tree flew fluttering into the maple leaves upon seeing us, and the wind made a rustling sound, sending chills down our spines. The beautiful morning sun, crossing the mountain ridge, poured its light generously into the deep valley of the vast Paegyŏpsa, as if it were Muryng Towŏn (武陵桃源). The long mist that had gathered and spent the cold night also scattered, strand by strand. The sounds of birds and the stream flowing from the dusky valley seemed to praise the autumn morning of Tan'gun Tae with the music of nature.
Dragging our trembling legs, relying on the grass barely clinging to the rocks, by the time we stood upon the Terrace (臺), the clock's short hand already pointed to eight. On the high layered rock of Tan'gun Tae, I intended to sit on the Uncarved Stone Seat (無毛石椅) where our ancestral grandfather, who endured all the wind and frost of over 4,600 years, used to sit peacefully. However, the stone seat bathing fully in the sunlight felt infinitely sublime, and it seemed as if our ancestral grandfather <97> was sitting there with a peaceful face (얼골), so I dared not lift my head.
Ah, my Ancestor, no, the revered grandfather of the White-Clad People (白衣人)! Are you still praying for the lives of your descendants? Ah, loving grandfather, this descendant has come seeking you, not minding the hundred-*li* distance. Please grant our wish... let us too live freely like others and beautifully, beautifully protect this land of three thousand *li*...
Thus, I pleaded before the formless shadow. Kissing the faint traces where "Tan'gun Tae" was written, I made a tearful farewell (惜別) amidst the distant sound of the assembly bugle call. On the way to Sahwangbong Peak after breakfast (朝飯), I looked back at Tan'gun Tae from afar, promising to return someday, and moved my feet onward.

Poem Second Place
What Happened Today
Kyŏngsŏng Kobo Second Year, Ham Hyangsu

A tulip beneath the wall
Bloomed anew this morning.
On the willow by the well,
Today, for the first time, the oriole's song was heard.

This is
In our yard,
What Spring did today!

Samgil's father's house
Was finally seized, you know.
For not being able to repay Elder Chang's money—

Mr. Song, who works at the Tobacco Factory across the river,
Was apparently taken to XX
For discovery of a strike conspiracy (스트라익密謨 發覺).

This! In our village spring day,
What happened in just today.

From today, school start time is delayed by ten minutes, they say.
For some reason these days,
Many people are late.

Yi-kun from the mulberry paper house (뽕펫집)
Cannot come to school from today, you know.
Because his monthly tuition (月謝金) is six months overdue!

This is our village spring day,
What happened in just today!

Poem Third Place
Heart of Iron - A Young Man's Poem Seeing a Lump of Iron -
Musun (Fushun) Middle School Fourth Year, Kim Ch'angsik

Iron is the Earth's sinew (筋肉).
- It is the Earth's strength.

Fearsome blood boils in the iron Heart (心臟).
- It is the clotting of strong Will-Blood (意志血).
Iron's body is made of Stillness (靜寂).
- Once a promise (約束) is made, it is Silence (沈黙).

Iron's Unity (團結) cannot be broken,
The iron heart knows only Comrades (同志).
Iron is the blood of strong Will (意志).
For Unity and Will, it even enters the sacrificial flames of the Furnace (冶爐).

Iron is the Prime Mover (原動力) of strength,
The chest of Fighting Spirit (覇氣).
And it was the arm of the Strong (强者),
In Battle (戰鬪), it is the final Victor (勝利者).

Iron becomes harder the more Hardship (苦楚) it endures,
It is unyielding to the very end.
Iron knows no Failure (失敗) in Destruction (破壞), <98>
In Construction (建設), it is the strength of a firm Foundation (基礎).

Iron is the Will of the young.
The heart of iron is the strength of the young.

Poem Fourth Place
Love of the Universe
Kyŏngsin School Fourth Year, Kim Sŏngdo

I saw the love of the Universe.
On a spring day, sunbathing at noon,
The smile of a smiling flower,
I saw.
The love of the Universe, I saw.

I heard the love of the Universe.
On a summer day, deep beneath the mountain,
The song of a singing stream,
I heard.
The love of the Universe, I heard.

I tasted the love of the Universe.
In late autumn, picking fruit,
Delicious fruit, I tasted.
The love of the Universe, I tasted.

Poem Honorable Mention
Falling Snow
Sinsŏng Middle School Fourth Year, Kim Yehan

Dusk on the mountainside,
White snow (힌눈) is falling (나리네).
Wordlessly, silently,
White snow is falling.

Countless white butterflies (힌나비)
Flutter and fly (날으네).
Tail to tail,
They dance in pairs.

Has my heart, before I knew it (어느듯),
Become snow (눈되엇는가)?
Far away in the sky,
It flies away (날어 가노나).

Poem Honorable Mention
When the Red Sun Spreads its Wings - Song Sent Amidst Dense Fog -
Sungsil Middle School Fourth Year, Kim Chogyu

On a morning when slick fog covers the world,
Unburdening my bursting heart into the morning fog,
Turning my pupils, like rising red Flames (火焰), towards the sky (하날),
Until blood vessels gather (서리여) and clumps of blood seem to pop out (튀여나오도록),
I sing a powerful song towards the sleeping Life (生命) of this people.
Friend, press the Keyboard (鍵盤) along with the song.

The song trembling from my Vocal Cords (聲帶) this foggy morning
Is not the sad Dirge (輓歌) sung by those carrying a Corpse (屍體),
The song sung towards the sleeping Life of this people this morning
Is not the Decadent (데까단) song sung by a fragrant Decadent Poet (頹廢詩人).
This is the mighty song of XX welling up deep from within the chest,
A hot and vigorous cry like the red molten iron of a Smelting Furnace (熔鑛爐).
Friends, you too kick off your blankets and break the Silence (沈黙)!

On this hazy morning, like eyes that have lost their Spirit (靈氣),
This song sent through the Dense Fog (濃霧),
Even if only a single unaccompanied note flows out,
When dawn breaks unseen beyond the clouds,
The siren, like the Ferocity (發惡) of a savage beast, will Accompany (伴奏) it—
Even if the song sung this morning flows out thin like a mosquito's buzz on a summer night,
When the red sun spreads its wings in the eastern sky,
The roar that will arise (니러날) from the Dense Fog will join it in Chorus (合唱).
Friend, lift your head, rise up, keep Time (拍子), and sing the song!

Selection Commentary (選評)
Travelogues (紀行文)
Although there were many travelogues, none were worth publishing [Note: contradicts the selection of one 1st place winner above; perhaps means 'few were worth publishing' or refers to the rest]. A travelogue is not about listing everything one hears (듯고) and sees during a trip, large or small, without any selection. One must select the most significant, most interesting, and most necessary things to convey from all observations and write them down.
There is no need to write about the car stopping and starting, checking into an inn, eating meals, and sleeping.
Also, while writing down impressions (感想) is not impossible, it is better to do this suggestively as much as possible. Directly using exclamations (感嘆詞) has no effect. One should present concrete facts (具體的 事實) and let the reader admire them on their own. For example, seeing Kŭmgangsan (金剛山) and exclaiming, "Truly magnificent!" is the most clumsy (拙劣) method of expression. Describing the magnificent scene so vividly that the reader can see it clearly and attaching no exclamation is the most effective way.
To write a good travelogue, one must first cultivate the power of observation (觀察). Discovering what ordinary people do not see and presenting it in writing will make everyone want to read it. Listing station names and the locations of historic sites (古蹟) is not a travelogue. Unfortunately, most of the submissions this time were of that kind.

New Poetry and Sijo (新詩와 時調)
This time, there were no outstanding works. Therefore, deciding the rankings was very difficult. If the poetic idea (詩想) was unique, the expression (表現) was lacking; works with balanced expression had overly mundane (平凡) ideas. The majority were works of the "Moonlit Night," "Autumn," or "Winter Night" variety. It seems they just wanted to try writing a song, but lacking sudden material, they simply depicted what caught their eye. Of course, for practice writing (習作), one must start this way. However, if one reads many works by poets old and new (古人 今人), one might be able to obtain inspiration (靈感) beyond such common material. The first step (첫거름) in entering literary works (文藝作品) lies in extensive reading (多讀).
In any case, it seems many wish to try writing poetry. However, lacking proper guidance (指導), they are charting a chaotic course (亂脈). For that purpose, starting next month, we plan to establish a special New Poetry section (新詩欄) in this magazine (本誌) and help directly and indirectly.
It is a pleasure to have discovered several hidden writers (作家) in this Student Works Competition. It seems there will be an opportunity to write about them again later. <100>

**Summary**

This article from the January 1932 issue of *Tongkwang* magazine announces the second installment of winners for its first student works competition. It includes the winning travelogue about visiting Tan'gun Tae, a site associated with Korea's mythical founder, and several poems ranked from first to fourth place, plus two honorable mentions (two others were omitted). The editorial committee provides commentary, noting a general lack of outstanding submissions. They critique the travelogues for being mere listings of events rather than insightful observations and the poems for often relying on clichéd themes and lacking originality, though balanced expression sometimes occurred. The editors encourage aspiring writers, especially poets, to read more widely and announce a new poetry section in the magazine to offer guidance.

From a historical perspective, the article is interesting for several reasons. The travelogue's focus on Tan'gun Tae reflects a persistent national consciousness and search for historical roots among students during the colonial period. The poem "What Happened Today" provides a stark glimpse into social realities: crippling debt leading to property seizure and the suppression of labor activism (mentioning a strike organizer taken to "XX," likely prison or police custody). The poem "Heart of Iron," with its themes of strength, unity, will, and sacrifice, could be interpreted as reflecting nationalist or perhaps socialist ideals prevalent among some youth. The editorial commentary reveals the standards of literary criticism and the efforts to foster literary talent among students under constrained circumstances.

第一回學生作品競技發表
이 작품을 발표함에 잇어서는 비록 等級에 들어간 作品이라도 수준 이하의 것이면  略하고 그 대신 等級에 아니든 選外라도 수준 이상의 것이면 실리기로 햇다. 그리하야

紀行文 一等
檀君臺의 아츰
載寧明新三年 李常俊

九月山 貝葉寺에 온 우리 일행은 가을의 둥근 愁月과 함께 한 밤을 새이고 이른 아츰 햇발에 곱게 빛나는 단풍닢을 뜯으며 半萬年前 우리 祖上 하라부지의 뒷자최 檀君臺를 찾어 山上으로 오르고 잇나이다. 百里길에 시달린 우리의 다리나 누구하나 길이 험타 山이 높다 말도 없이 文字 그대로 嚴肅한 태도로 오래도록 그리고 그리웟든 檀君臺를 찾어 머레넝쿨을 헤치고 다래넉꿀 속으로 고양나무 단장을 앞세이고 오르고 또 오르나이다.
잦나무에 짖드렷든 산새 두세마리 우리를 보고 놀라 포드득 날아 楓葉속으로 숨어 버리고 바람은 바스삭 소리를 내여 우리들의 마음에 소름을 쭉 끼치게 하나이다. 아츰에 고운 해도 건는 산등승이를 넘어 멀리 武陵桃源이 아닌가 *만한 貝葉에 깊은 골짝이에 그 빛을 아깝없이 부어주더이다. 밤새 뭉치여 찬밤을 새인 긴 안개도 한 줄 두 줄 산산히 헤여지고 어슥한 골짝이에서 흘러나오는 새소리와 내-ㄴ물 소리와는, 自然의 音樂으로 檀君臺의 가을 아츰을 찬미하는 듯 하오이다.
푸들 푸들 떨리는 다리를 끄을고 바위에 간신히 붙어잇는 풀포기에 무거운 몸을 의지하여 臺에 올라설 때에는 이미 시계의 短針이 여듧시를 가르치더이다. 나는 檀君臺 높다란 層岩우에서 4,600여년의 온갓 風霜을 다 겪은 우리 祖上 하라부지께옵서 安座하여 게시든 無毛石椅에 앉으려고 하엿나이다. 그러나 햇빛을 마음껏 받는 石床이 무한이도 崇嚴하고 祖上하라부지께옵서 ＜97＞ 평화스런 얼골로 앉어게시는 듯 하여 나는 감히 머리를 들지 못하고
아 나의 祖上 아니 白衣人의 꼽고조 하라버지 이때껏 後孫의 삶을 위하야 기도하시나잇가? 아 사랑많으신 하라부지 이 後孫이 百里길을 머다 않고 찾어와 원하옵노니 우리도 남과 같이 자유롭게 살며 이 三千里江山을 곱게 곱게 지키게 하여 주세요...
이와 같이 無形의 그림자 앞에 억지 썻나이다. 檀君臺라고 쓴 희미한 자최와 입을 마추고 멀리 들려오는 集合의 호각소래와 같이 눈물의 惜別을 하엿뀀˜이다. 朝飯후의 思皇峰가는 길에 다시금 檀君臺을 멀리 바라보며, 後日을 期約하고 발을 옴기엇나이다.

選評
紀行文
紀行文이 수효는 많으나 쓸 만한 것은 없다. 紀行이라는 것은 旅行중에 듯고 보는 것을 大小不拘하고 아모 선택없이 나열하는 것이 아니다. 모든 觀察한 중에서 가장 意義가 잇고 가장 興味가 잇고 가장 알려줄 필요가 잇는 것을 선택해서 적어 놓아야 할 것이다.
車가 머물고 떠나고 여관에 들고 밥먹고 잠자는 것까지 쓸 필요는 없다.
그리고 感想을 적는 것도 無不可나 이것은 될 수 잇는 대로 暗示的으로 함이 좋을 것이다. 그리고 感嘆詞를 直接으로 쓰는 것은 아모 효과가 없다. 具體的 事實을 들어서 讀者로 하여금 스스로 讚嘆을 하게 해야 될 것이다. 가령 金剛山을 보고 「참으로 위대하다」하고 감탄하는 것은 가장 拙劣한 表現方法이다. 그 위대한 光景이 讀者의 눈에 훤하니 보이도록 描寫하고 아모 感歎詞도 부치지 안는 것이 가장 효과가 잇는 것이다.
紀行文을 잘 쓰려고 하면 먼저 觀察의 능력을 길러야 하겟다. 보통 사람이 보지 못하는 것을 발견해서 그것을 글로 내어 놓아야 모든 사람이 한번 읽고 싶어할 것이다. 停車場이름과 古蹟의 所在名을 열거하는 것이 기행이 아니다. 이번 作品에는 불행히 그런 종류가 대부분이엇다.

新詩와 時調
이번에는 特出한 作品이 없엇다. 그러니까 等級을 정하기가 매우 어려웟다. 詩想이 독특하다고 보면 표현이 부족하고 표현이 均衡이 된 作品은 想이 너무 平凡하다. 「달밤」이니 「가을」이니 「겨울 밤」이니 하는 종류의 작품이 太半이다. 그냥 노래를 지어 볼터인데 갑자기 材料가 없으니까 눈에 띄우는 것을 그대로 그려본 것이다. 習作으로는 물론 이러케 시작할 수밖에 없다. 그러나 만일 古人 今人의 작품을 많이 읽고 나면 그런 평범한 재료 이상의 靈感을 얻을 수 잇을 것이다. 文藝作品에 入門하는 첫거름은 多讀에 잇을 것이다.
좌우간 詩를 지어 보려는 이가 많은 모양이다. 그러나 적당한 指導가 없어 亂脈을 정한다고 하겟다. 그것을 위해서 本誌上에 來月부터 新詩欄을 特置하고 직접 간접으로 도와드릴까 한다.
