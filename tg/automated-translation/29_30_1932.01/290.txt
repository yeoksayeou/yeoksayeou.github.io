**Body Text Translation:**
Condolence Poem and 1 Other
						Ryubin (Author), Kishi (Translator)

Condolence Poem - Original Esperanto Poem -

You have returned, O brave fighter,
From the proletarian battlefront, you have returned.
Therefore, we honor you with song,
You, your greatness, your sacred deeds.

The enemies of virtue struck you down,
Scattering your warm blood here and there,
Cruelly celebrating this slaughter, devils looking down on you, sneering.

But your blood is a banner,
Fluttering towards victory, towards the sun.
Until the first shining day comes
For the downtrodden proletariat.

We commemorate you, we shed tears,
But we do not weep at the grave of you, the martyr.
We only fight bravely, taking you as our model,
We too shall fight until we die like men.

Having seen a good example in you,
Tears of sorrow have never clouded our hearts.
Our path only becomes brighter as we go,
We only know progress, we do not know yesterday.

If our time comes soon,
We shall gladly die for the cause.
We believe - that soon the "Aurora" (오로라)
Will shine upon Mother Earth.

Do we know how to yield at all?
Are the tears, an ocean, still insufficient?
Does the blood, shed like a waterfall, still flow?
We suffer and are tormented, robbed of both mind and body.
But for the final struggle, our will burns with fiery passion.

The road is long, covered thick with stones and obstacles,<82>
So many sacrifices follow one after another!
But nothing can block our advance,
We only march forward ceaselessly, freely and bravely.

Do not lament the many dear sacrifices,
The more the tyrant's rough footsteps trample us,
Our numbers grow, and we resolve firmly to die
Rather than be crushed under those heavy, pressing footsteps.

The wicked exploiters cruelly strike down our bodies,
Nail us to the cross, and torture us with the flames of hell,
But our bodies grow stronger and our spirits harden.
Behold, who can stand against us or block our path ahead?

Bleeding, we cry out, run wild for now, O tyrants!
Torment us, kill us. Threaten us as you please.
We know well that your guns and swords are useless,
Soon the red storm will sweep you all away.

(Translator's Note) Compared to the grandeur and emotional impact of the formal style of the original Esperanto poem, the free verse form and vocabulary of the translated poem are often inadequate. This is due to the translator's lack of skill and also circumstances that prevent a complete transfer. Read the original text. Especially for things like poetry, one cannot taste the true flavor without the original. This "Ryubin" is also somewhat known as a proletarian poet. He has a poetry collection, "Ruga Aŭroro (새벽노을)". 1931.12.22. Translator <83>

**Summary**

This article presents a Korean translation of an Esperanto poem by Ryubin, a proletarian poet. The poem, titled "Condolence Poem," is a fiery revolutionary piece dedicated to a fallen "fighter" of the proletarian cause. It eulogizes the martyr, condemns the oppressors ("enemies of virtue," "tyrants," "exploiters"), and expresses unwavering resolve to continue the struggle for liberation, fueled by sacrifice and the belief in an imminent victory symbolized by the "Aurora." The translator's note acknowledges the limitations of the translation and encourages reading the Esperanto original, mentioning Ryubin's reputation and his collection "Ruga Aŭroro." For historians, this piece is significant as it demonstrates the circulation of international proletarian literature and revolutionary ideology (specifically via Esperanto) within colonial Korea during the 1930s. The poem's militant tone and the translator's cautious note about not being able to fully convey the original possibly hint at the political sensitivities and censorship prevalent at the time.

吊詞 외 1편
						류빈(作), 岸曙(譯)
吊詞 -에쓰페란토 原作詩-
그대는 돌아갓네, 勇敢한 鬪士여,
프로레타리아 戰線에서 그대는 돌아갓네.
그러길래 우리는 노래로 그대를 빛내나니
그대를, 그대의 위대한 것을, 그대의 거륵한 行爲를.

惡德의 원수들은 그대를 넘어터리고
그대의 덥은 피를 여기 저기 휘뿌리며
잔혹하게도 이 殺生을 祝賀하면서 악마같이 그대를 내려보며 비웃거니.

그러나 그대의 피는 旗빨인 것을,
勝利를 앞에, 해를 두고 펄덕이는.
즈려 밟힌 프로레타리아들에게
빛나는 첫날이 오기까지는.

우리는 그대를 기루노라, 우리는 눈물 흘리며,
殉敎者인 그대의 무덤에서 울지 안노라,
우리들은 그대를 本삼아 용감하게 싸흘뿐,
우리들도 사내답게 죽기까지 싸호랴노라.

그대에게서 좋은 本을 보앗으매
哀愁의 눈물은 우리 맘을 흐린적없네.
우리의 길은 갈사록 더욱 밝을 뿐,
우리는 前進을 알 뿐, 昨日을 모르노라.

만일에 오래지 않아 우리의 때가 온다면
우리는 일을 위해 기뻐서 죽으랴노라,
우리는 믿노라, - 오래지 아니하야 「오로라」가
大地인 어머니의 우에 빛날 것을

아모런들 굽힐 줄 아는가.
눈물은 바다연만 오이려 부족타는가,
피는 폭포로 흘린 것을 아직도 나오는가,
우리는 시달리며 괴롭아 하노라, 맘과 몸을 모다 뺏기고.
그러나 나종의 씨름을 위해 우리의 意志는 불길의 情熱에 타는 것을.

길은 멀고 돌과 장애물이 가뜩히 덥혀＜82＞
하다 많은 희생은 뒤를 이어 나는고야!
그러나 우리의 前進을 막을 것은 하나도 없네,
우리는 끊임없이 나아갈 뿐이노라, 자유로히 勇敢하게.

귀엽은 희생이 많이난다 恨을 말라,
暴君의 거츤 발길이 우리를 즈러 밟으면 밟을사록,
우리의 수는 늘어가며, 굳은 결심에 죽으랴노니
눌르는 무겁은 그 발길에 깔리우기 보다는.

몹쓸은 搾取者들은 잔혹하게도 우리의 몸을 넘어터리고
우리를 十字架에 못박으며, 지옥의 불길로 拷問을 하나
우리의 몸은 갈사록 힘이 나며 精神은 굳어지나니,
볼지어다, 누구라서 우리를 대서며 앞길을 막을 것이랴.

피흘리며 우리는 부르짓노라, 미처 뛰놀라 暴君들이어,
우리를 시달루라, 죽이라. 그대들은 맘대로 위협을 하라.
우리는 잘 아노라, 그대들의 총과 劒은 쓸데 없을 뿐,
오래지 안아 붉은 暴風雨가 그대들을 휘말아 갈 것을.
