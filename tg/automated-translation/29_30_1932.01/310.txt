Article Title: 松都紀行 (Songdo Travelogue)
Author: 李鐸
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Sŏnjukkyo Bridge
Wordlessly, I touch the stone stained with my lord's (님) blood.
It seems a warm vapor still permeates my hand,
The water flowing beneath the stone sounds choked with grief.<83>

Manwŏltae Terrace
Having endured hardship (찰설음), my emotions have grown numb.
The autumn grasses on Manwŏltae Terrace appear commonplace.
I wonder if those who wept here in the past were merely sentimental.

Ch'aehadong Valley
How clear the water is, and are the stones (돌아니) not wondrous!
Everything that meets the eye is a picture.
As the beautiful scenery steals my heart, I forget old sorrows (예ㅅ시름).

Hwadam Pond
I composed this imagining Hwadam, which I could not see from beside the stone pond of Ch'aehadong Valley, and record it under this title.

I scan all around, upon the rock beneath the tree.
It is as if I can almost see my master (님) strolling there.
Clouds linger on the pond; perhaps it was him, I think.
1931.10.10 <84>
<83-84>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi T'ak describes a journey through Songdo (modern Kaesŏng), visiting historical sites associated with the Goryeo Dynasty. The poet reflects on the tragic loyalty of Chŏng Mong-ju at Sŏnjukkyo Bridge, feels detached amidst the ruins of the Manwŏltae palace, finds solace in the natural beauty of Ch'aehadong Valley, and imagines the presence of the scholar Sŏ Kyŏng-dŏk at Hwadam Pond. The poem juxtaposes feelings of historical sorrow and numbness with an appreciation for enduring natural landscapes. For historians, the poem offers insight into how Korean intellectuals in the colonial period engaged with their national history and heritage sites. Visiting places symbolizing Goryeo loyalty (Sŏnjukkyo) and scholarly tradition (Hwadam) can be interpreted as a quiet affirmation of Korean identity and cultural memory in the face of Japanese assimilation policies.

善竹橋
님의 피 배인 돌을 말없이 만지노라.
아직도 더운 김이 하마손에 스미는 듯
돌아레 흐르는 물은 목메인 듯 하여라.＜83＞

滿月臺
찰설음 겪어나니 感情이 무되것다
滿月臺 가을풀이 예사로이 보이고녀
예와서 우시던 이는 興이런가 하여라

彩霞洞
물도 맑을씨고 돌아니 奇異한가
눈에 드는 것이 모도다 그림이라.
美景이 맘을 앗으니 예ㅅ시름을 몰라라.

花潭
彩霞洞 石潭가에서 보지도 못한 花潭을 연상하고 지은 것을 本題로 적노라.

나무밑 바위 우를 두루두루 살피노라
님의 거니는 양 눈에하마 띄우는 듯
구름이 못에 어리니 행여긴 듯 하여라
1931.10.10 ＜84＞
