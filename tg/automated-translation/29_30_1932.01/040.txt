The Manchurian Incident and the Chinese Revolution
						By Pak Wŏn-il

This short article was completed on December 3rd of last year. Not only has more than a month passed since then, but now that the year has changed, it is not the author's intention to release this text to the world as it is, ignoring the circumstances that have changed and unfolded moment by moment. However, due to unavoidable circumstances, not a single character could be revised or added, so I ask the readers for their understanding. Chiang Kai-shek's resignation, the Canton faction's new government, the increasingly tense situation in Manchuria and Mongolia, the shifting attitudes of Britain, America, and France, Chang Hsüeh-liang's withdrawal from Chinchow, the Japanese army's entry into Chinchow, the activities of the Chinese Communist Army, etc. – now that all these situations have become clear, it can be said that forecasting the future has become easier. Consequently, this essay, constructed with such insufficient data, may seem unnecessary already. However, as it is an unavoidable fact that occurred as one link in the development process of the Chinese Revolution, I believe it will facilitate, not hinder, the future outlook. This is where the author's intention lies, and if this short piece has any significance, I believe it lies here.
- 1932, 1, 4 -

Setting aside the question of timing, that a futile yet fierce conflict for the resolution of the Pacific problem – which is the current global problem – would unfold on the stage of China, the so-called Balkan Peninsula of the East, particularly Manchuria, and that it would become the prelude to the Second World War, is something anyone who does not deliberately turn a blind eye to world affairs would no longer doubt. Indeed, things have gone as far as they can go, and consequently, the problem has rapidly become complicated. This is precisely the conflict between Japan and China, triggered by the bombing of the South Manchuria Railway line on September 18th last year, staged in Manchuria. Wherever the urgent clouds of war travel—south, north, east, west—thunder erupts and storms arise, and now the crisis has permeated every corner of the globe.

Here, is it wind? Or rain? What is the attitude of the League of Nations? What is the course of the United States? What are the movements of Red Russia? Public speculation varies widely. Of course, observing the trends of the world's imperialist nations like Britain, America, France, Japan, Italy, etc., centered around the League of Nations, and the Nationalist Government, and considering the movements of Red Russia as a proletarian state, is indispensable for a comprehensive grasp of the problem. However, merely looking at the phenomena expressed on the surface cannot grasp the core of the overall problem of the third stage of capitalism. The internal situation within each country will again become more serious through international problems, so this situation must again be grasped within the international chain.

That the conflict between Japan and China is not merely a Japan-China relationship, but a Japan-US relationship, a Japan-Russia relationship, and further, a Japan-Britain, Britain-France, Japan-France global relationship, and simultaneously a relationship between imperialist states versus the Soviet Union, is known to everyone. However, the question of where the historical development of the capitalist world will turn, triggered by this
Prelude to the Second World War
, is the sole <6> core and conclusion of the problem that must be understood. Therefore, rather than the question of today's nations' attitudes, tomorrow's attitudes, etc.—in other words, whether a world war will explode and unfold or subside—it should be recognized as the question of how the global revolutionary process is progressing. Our humble intention in discussing the Manchurian Incident and the Chinese Revolution lies here. This is because it is not only a comprehensive grasp of the China problem but also a comprehensive grasp of world history. Why? Because the China problem will only be resolved through the Chinese Revolution, and the resolution of the China problem is the key to the resolution of the world problem.

The Manchuria problem is, of course, not limited to Manchuria; it is a problem of all of China and the entire world. Its significance in the process of the Chinese Revolution certainly did not begin today. It holds an important position in terms of natural conditions, as well as economic and political conditions. However, its current global significance emerged after the First World War, and especially in recent years, the problem has rapidly become complex. As long as Manchuria was politically isolated from China's central government, major contradictions did not manifest. As the growth of the indigenous Manchurian bourgeoisie and the commonality of interests with the central Chinese bourgeoisie grew, Chang Hsüeh-liang's allegiance to the center, the extension of central power into Manchuria, and consequently, the advance of American financial capital into Manchuria, etc., inevitably led to conflict with Japanese interests.

On the other hand, we see the growth of the Chinese Revolution; the connection of Manchurian workers and peasants as the main body of the Chinese movement to the entire Chinese Revolution also occurred after the Great War, as mentioned below. Furthermore, defined by natural, economic, and political conditions, they most easily achieved international confluence with movements in Japan, Korea, the Soviet Union, etc. Despite all obstacles, they were directed towards the same path; in this respect, the trajectory of the Manchuria problem becomes ever more crucial.

The Chinese Revolution, which temporarily showed signs of stagnation, has developed rapidly in the last two or three years. It has expanded comprehensively and strengthened through struggles whenever domestic or foreign incidents occurred. In this short essay, we intend to briefly examine how the process is advancing, triggered by the Japan-China conflict. Therefore, we must first glance at the main revolutionary process of recent years.

While the Nanking Government clamored for central unification, running east and west, campaigning south and north, and on the other hand, fiercely pushed for the abolition of extraterritoriality, appearing most revolutionary in its phenomenal form, it was merely a
Government of Landlords and Capitalists.
To consolidate its own power amidst the scramble of all imperialisms, it did no more than play one imperialism off against another—in reality, being a loyal servant—and in return, merely fulfilled its duty of suppressing workers and peasants for those imperialist powers.

The Kuomintang's Nanking Government turned against the Chinese revolutionary process in 1927. They broke ties with the Chinese Communist Party in July 1927, and by April 1928, Pai Ch'ung-hsi's coup d'état in Shanghai, Li Chi-shen's party purification movement in Kwangtung, and large-scale white terror against the Communist Party were carried out. Since then, they have suppressed all popular movements, even cracking down on demonstrations commemorating the Kuomintang itself, established by the Nanking Government.

Despite such suppression, the Chinese revolutionary forces grew moment by moment; they expanded rapidly amidst all natural, political, and economic difficulties, especially during the global depression. The notable results are the formation of the Red Army and the construction of soviets. Due to various circumstances, let's briefly look only at the recent <7> development of the soviet struggle.

The beginning of the rural soviet struggle in China was the Nanchang Uprising. The Nanchang Uprising erupted just before the collapse of the Wuhan government, specifically on June 21, 1927 (*error in original text, should be August 1, 1927*), when Ho Lung, Yeh T'ing, and Chu P'ei-te under Chang Fa-k'uei occupied Nanchang and organized a provisional government with Communist Party members. Subsequent major movements include the so-called Four Provinces Autumn Harvest Uprising carried out in Kwangtung, Kiangsi, Hupeh, Hunan, and Kiangsu regions, the Changsha Uprising, etc., and the famous establishment of the Haifeng, Lufeng, and Kwangtung soviets in December of the same year. In June 1928, the 6th National Congress of the Chinese Communist Party was held, and in May 1930, the First National Congress of Soviet Area Representatives was held in Shanghai, where the political program, land law, labor law, etc., were decided and announced, as is widely known. Since then, the organization of the Red Army and the movement to establish soviet political power in its areas of activity have developed rapidly. Looking at the current situation:

Kiangsi - The entire province except for the Nanchang area, i.e., 60 out of 81 counties, are soviet areas, and the Hsiachiang county area downstream from Chi-an on both banks of the Yangtze River is a Red Army zone.
Hupeh - About 40 counties are soviet areas; the activity zone of Red Army guerrilla units extends to within 10 li (approx. 5km) of the Wuhan suburbs.
Fukien - 7 western counties, 2 southern counties, and 4 northern counties are soviet areas.
Kwangtung - The East River, West River, and North River vicinities are historical soviet areas. Although suppressed after the Canton Uprising, its foundation remains firm, and fierce struggles are unfolding in 16 East River counties and 7 Chiungyai counties.
Kwangsi - Over 20 western counties are under the influence of the soviet movement.
Hunan - The region with the most intense white terror, but the eastern and western soviet areas have been maintained for 3 years and are rapidly developing towards the south.
Honan - The area bordering Hunan and Anhwei is a historical region of peasant struggle. Recently, the differentiation of the Red Spear Society has progressed, and the soviet area centered around Shangch'eng has expanded.
Szechwan - Recently, the "Divine Soldiers" movement opposing warlord taxes occurred and is gradually merging with the Red Army.
Anhwei - Red Army guerrilla warfare is expanding throughout the western part.
Kiangsu - Ihsing, Chiangyin, and Wusih were revolutionary areas in 1927; now small struggles are being carried out.

Thus, the soviet movement is not only in South-Central China but is gradually advancing northward. In the north, spontaneous uprisings are expanding, and parts of the Big Sword Society and Small Sword Society are said to be gradually escaping exploitation by the exploiting class.

Next, let's glance at the struggle organization in Manchuria, the source of the current problem.
After the Peking-Hankow Railway strike in February 1922, the Dairen Chinese Engineering Society (大連中華工學會) - a night school and mutual aid organization for workers - was founded at the end of the same year. In 1924, organization expanded to the Manchuria Dockyard, Dairen Machinery, Onoda Cement, Fukushima Spinning, Dairen Kiln Elements, Dairen Electric, and other factories, developing into a revolutionary labor union affiliated with China's only Red Labor Union, the "All-China Federation of Trade Unions" (中華全國總工會). In February 1924, representatives were dispatched to the 2nd Congress of the National Railway General Union held in Chengchow, Honan. Over the years, they managed to organize all Chinese workers in the Dairen area, but received a dissolution order from Japanese authorities and subsequently lost their legal existence. In July 1926, the Central Department of the Chinese Communist Party organized the Party's Dairen Regional Committee, and by May 1927, they had acquired over 500 party members. However, after 54 were arrested for leading the Fengtien Hemp Mill strike, they entered a temporary state of stagnation. But in 1929, they regained strength and carried out propaganda activities in places like the Fushun Coal Mine and Fengtien. In May 1930,
[Missing text/group name] was absorbed into the party's local organization, leading to the continuous outbreak of major uprisings in the Chientao (間島) region, which became widely known. Thus, it progressed from spontaneous occurrence to organized development. The maturation of objective revolutionary conditions, especially <8> the increase in political contradictions, the generalization and broadening of mass struggles spurred by the 1928 economic crisis, gradually formed the prerequisites for further organization. During the period of struggle that moved northward from South Manchuria along the industrial centers, one trigger that accelerated it was, above all, the Chinese Eastern Railway issue. Due to this incident, peasants in the North Manchuria region came under the significant political influence of the Soviet Union, and peasants along the Chinese Eastern Railway are said to be almost entirely organized. Also, in the Kkakkang (砍杠) region of the Changpai Mountains, there are reportedly 500,000 to 600,000 organized peasants. Consequently, recently, the establishment of soviet political power in Manchuria has become the immediate central focus of the struggle, becoming a force guiding the victory of soviet power throughout China, while simultaneously weakening imperialism and contributing to the victory of the international proletariat.

As described above, the true revolutionary movement in China, while externally undermining the economic and political foundations of the imperialist bourgeoisie, internally overthrows the rule of feudal warlords, landlords, local gentry, and comprador classes who directly assist those imperialists. It also overthrows the national bourgeois regime, which, while having partial objective contradictions with imperialism, has completely compromised with it and is merely an instrument for suppressing workers and peasants, thereby removing the basis for imperialist rule and resisting the anti-Soviet attacks by imperialism and the Kuomintang reactionaries.

Therefore, they not only diligently oppose and expose imperialism and the Nationalist Government daily but also gain the masses through these struggles and rush towards the goal of true problem-solving. How the revolutionary process will unfold triggered by the current Mukden Incident, i.e., the Japan-China conflict, holds significant meaning for world history. Consequently, this Manchurian Incident defines the task of the Chinese proletariat—nay, the proletariat of the entire world.

The progression of the issue centered on the League of Nations, that is, the reversal of the major powers' attitudes and the stance of the Nationalist Government representatives, indeed the attitude of the Nationalist Government itself—what impact will these generally have on the Chinese masses? They reveal their essence all too clearly. The attitude of the major powers centered on the League of Nations, sometimes soft, sometimes hard, only prolongs the issue while causing its intensification. Considering the domestic situation of any country, attacked by the third-stage crisis and facing political crisis stemming from economic crisis, their attitude is not strange at all. By separating the Japan-China conflict from the Kellogg-Briand Pact, whatever treaty, etc., thereby acknowledging its rationality, while continuing interference in the Sino-Japanese issue through investigation committees, international police, buffer zone establishment, etc., sometimes opposing, sometimes compromising, the conflict in Manchuria meanwhile becomes increasingly critical moment by moment. This, of course, fails to bring about a resolution; it merely aims to overcome their own fatal crisis by gaining a share of the unearned income, that is, an appropriate re-division, at the expense of Japan and China. In short, the League of Nations is undergoing its final trial before the world's masses through this incident. While on one hand, they conceal imperialist actions and prepare for coordination against their common enemy by repeating
Deceptive Resolutions and Empty Discussions,
thereby promoting imperialist intervention in China, on the other hand, they absolutely cannot overcome the conflict of interests among themselves; rather, it gradually sharpens. The attitudes of various countries during the Tientsin Incident, the Chinchow Incident, and regarding the establishment of a buffer zone convey this situation. All relationships in the current capitalist world expose unprecedented fragility and the threatening uncertainty of the boundary between peace and war, vividly demonstrating the infinite deception of pacifist phrases—in other words, how imperialist forces <9> manipulate the interests of the world and the fate of all nations.

The Chinese masses and the world masses have come to understand the League of Nations roughly as described above. What impact, then, has the attitude of the Nationalist Government had on the Chinese populace? Since China is in a semi-colonial state, they have partially objective conflicting interests with imperialism. However, ultimately, they have maintained their own base through imperialism and consequently assisted imperialism, as mentioned earlier. Now, they could not help but expose their feudal bourgeois reaction, their degeneration into spokesmen for world imperialism, and their powerlessness under broad daylight. They proved what the reign of the militarist-landlord clique and Kuomintang politics ultimately brings. The arguments of the Chinese representative Shih Chao-chi (施肇基) at the League of Nations did not represent the interests of the Chinese masses in any way; merely representing the Nationalist Government, he kept pace with the major powers, advocating temporary pacifism and appealing to the international powers for a resolution of the Sino-Japanese problem. His recent proposal to establish a buffer zone is merely an attempt to use one imperialism to check another. Even if successful, it would amount to nothing more than offering a peaceful re-division of China as compensation, which is as clear as daylight. Furthermore, they try to compromise with imperialism at the expense of the entire laboring masses of China. How empty Chiang Kai-shek's embellished words and boasts were is proven by his actual actions. Chang Hsüeh-liang disarmed before the occupier and preached non-resistance. However, the gathering storm leaves no room for his resurgence. Awakening to the fact that beautiful non-resistance was but a dream, while under final judgment, he seems to have taken up arms again as a measure to maintain his base, but alas, how can the lament of being too late be helped? The entire policy of their Nationalist Government was merely war against the laboring masses and surrender to imperialism, only serving to degrade the Chinese people nationally. Their arrows were instead directed, directly or indirectly, at the masses; corpses littered the wilderness, and wasn't the Tientsin uprising suppressed by them?

The Chinese masses will now easily realize that famine, poverty, degeneration, imperialist oppression, intervention, internal division, etc., are
The Result of the Policies of the Kuomintang, the Party of the Anti-Revolutionary Bloc.
They will intuitively feel that the only force capable of ending the yoke of world imperialists on the laboring masses of China is certainly not the Kuomintang—which shoots workers participating in anti-imperialist demonstrations, executes Communist Party members organizing strikes in imperialist factories, and invites all imperialists to battle against the Chinese Soviets and their Worker-Peasant Red Army—but the victory of the worker-peasant revolution achieved under the leadership of the Chinese Communist Party.

Therefore, triggered by this incident, the laboring masses organized under their banner, fighting against the warlords within their own country and foreign imperialism, will surely be able to accelerate the realization of their demands. On the one hand, Mukden was occupied—government, arsenal, headquarters, army, all—in less than a few hours. Subsequent events exposed the powerlessness of the warlords and the Nationalist Government, regardless of who they were. Furthermore, fueled by the anti-Japanese movement ignited throughout China in connection with this incident—which is not just anti-Japanese but an anti-all-imperialism movement—the work of the Chinese Revolution is facilitated. Consequently, based on all current experiences, it is not difficult to understand that the revolutionary movement in Manchuria will expand and strengthen, capable of rallying new masses. As soon as the incident broke out, strong resistance from anti-Japanese masses arose in Peking, Shanghai, Hankow, and other places. The Nanking government authorities scrambled to suppress them. <10> On September 20th, the Joint Association of Academic Groups met in Peking. On the 21st, the Joint Association of Academic Groups Against Japan was formed in Peking, Tientsin, etc., organizing student-centered anti-Japanese groups. On the Mid-Autumn Festival, September 26th, demonstrations were held in various places. In Harbin, an anti-imperialist league middle school student demonstration was planned, and a Chinese Communist Party Mid-Autumn Festival demonstration was carried out, while leaflets were distributed. The anti-Japanese citizens' rally planned for the Mid-Autumn Festival in Peking, Tientsin, etc., was held outside the T'aiho Gate on the 28th, with about 50,000 attendees who carried out a demonstration. This was the largest since the Northern Expedition and indicates that the Public Security Bureau and the city party headquarters had completely lost control over the popular movement. In Hankow, on September 23rd, the Anti-Japanese Association took the lead in organizing the National Salvation Volunteer Corps. Students at the Wuchang Military Academy raised slogans like "Attack Tokyo!" and their fervor became intense. Especially after being abandoned by the League of Nations, the anti-Japanese movement worsened extremely. On the 28th, a complete market strike was carried out, and leaflets calling for economic severance with Japan were distributed throughout the city. In Shanghai, the Kuomintang Shanghai City Party Headquarters held an emergency committee meeting on September 21st, established principles for anti-Japanese national salvation work, and passed an outline for organizing student groups. On the 21st, under the auspices of the Anti-Japanese Association, a conference of representatives from all circles was held. It was decided to reorganize the Anti-Japanese Association into the Anti-Japanese National Salvation Association and make it the main body of the anti-Japanese movement. The Anti-Japanese National Salvation Citizens' Rally held on September 26th reached 50,000 attendees and passed important resolutions for demonstrations. Among them, the petition to the Kuomintang government to organize a National Volunteer Corps and provide it with weapons is noteworthy. Arming the citizens is not only a self-defense method in times of emergency but also a leftist tactic based on the experience of the Shanghai citizens' government era in February and March 1927. It allows us to infer how the popular movement has expanded and, triggered by this, the nature of the Chinese Communist Party's activities. Particularly, the surge in the student movement during this anti-Japanese campaign signifies the Kuomintang headquarters' inability to control students. They attacked Nanking's Foreign Minister Wang Cheng-t'ing (王正廷) and expressed themselves through petitions to Chiang Kai-shek. More recently, several thousand volunteer troops organized by male and female students from Shanghai came to Nanking, petitioning for a declaration of war against Japan, while 8,000 students gathered from various places stormed the Nationalist Government, roaring demands to bring out Chiang Kai-shek, filling the vicinity with a menacing atmosphere (November 25th). University students in Peking stopped classes and resolved to declare war on Japan, while about 500 students rushed to the Shun-ch'eng Mansion (順承府), urging Chang Hsüeh-liang to take active measures and immediately supply weapons to the student army (November 10th).

The actions of the Red Army, which had temporarily retreated due to Chiang Kai-shek's suppression, have now launched an offensive in light of the current incident. The Shanghai Mainichi (Japanese newspaper) reported at the end of September the collapse and general retreat of the Nanking army, new victories for the Red Army, and the threat to the northward-advancing government forces. The Shanghai Nippo reported the critical situation in Nanchang.
Furthermore, huge strikes of a political nature broke out in factories everywhere. Peasant volunteer groups carried out attacks against Chinese police and the transport of military supplies for the occupying forces in various parts of Kirin and Fengtien provinces. Revolutionaries are constantly distributing leaflets for propaganda, agitation, and exposure. On the October 7th anniversary, demonstrations by workers and students in Shanghai were carried out, leading to clashes with police forces. On November 8th, the Tientsin Incident erupted. The China Press reported that over 1,000 rioters in Tientsin attacked the city government, the Public Security Bureau, and the central station of the Tientsin-Pukow Railway line, also cutting electric wires to plunge the entire city into darkness. Recently, as Chang Hsüeh-liang's powerlessness became apparent, anti-Chang movements arose in the Tientsin-Peking area. In response, Chang Hsüeh-liang decided to carry out a major coup d'état. Due to Chang's suppression of anti-Hsüeh-liang figures like Han Fu-ch'ü (韓復渠), Sun Ch'uan-fang (孫傳芳), and Tuan Ch'i-jui (段祺瑞), the situation in North China <11> grew tense. Moreover, the recent popular uprising was directed against imperialism on one hand, and against Kuomintang officials on the other. Therefore, it cannot but hold historical significance in the current offensive warfare of world imperialism against the Chinese Revolution. The Kuomintang's surrender to imperialism, the schemes between Chiang Kai-shek, Wang Ching-wei (汪兆銘), and foreign oppressors against the Chinese Soviets and the laboring masses, became clear before their eyes. When the Tientsin uprising occurred, the garrison troops of various countries were dispatched, led by the Japanese garrison troops and military police. This popular uprising in Tientsin is indeed
A Bell Toll and Simultaneously the Advancing March of the Entire Chinese Revolution Northward.
The next day, the 9th, provisional martial law was declared in Peking. Shanghai and Hankow were also shaken, and the government declared a special state of emergency martial law, while the concession authorities and army showed unusual tension. Riots in the Tientsin area continued to erupt afterwards, and mutinies are occurring within the Tunhua army, the Kirin army, and other various military units.

Thus, recently, the Chinese Revolution has advanced northward from South-Central China, gradually establishing soviet areas. The peasant masses of Manchuria are raising the banner of revolutionary uprising against the Chinese warlords and Japanese imperialism. Despite the fact that the power of Chiang Kai-shek and Chang Hsüeh-liang has completely fallen to the ground, leaving them with no ability to resist, Japan resolutely opposes immediate or time-limited withdrawal. The reason given is that the rampant banditry disrupts life, property, and order. The powerless Nationalist Government, in response, has already ceased its offensive to attempt to regain its own power, while also abandoning even the demand for immediate or time-limited withdrawal. Recently, Chang Hsüeh-liang, feeling insecure, maneuvered to halt the Chinchow offensive. Chiang Kai-shek had Finance Minister Sung (宋) attempt to procure 12 million yuan in military funds, but was reportedly refused by the local banking consortium, suggesting their fate will end here. Representative Shih at the League of Nations is said to be requesting the dispatch of a joint force of Britain, America, France, and Italy to Chinchow to establish a buffer zone. Their true intentions and essence become starkly clear here. In short, Chiang, Chang, and the Kuomintang are appealing to world imperialism for rescue from the tide of the worker-peasant masses. The Chinese masses intuitively recognize this and are marching towards their path to survival. In other words, these hundreds of millions of the laboring class will unite in struggle to save themselves. This becomes the focal point for answering the question of what kind of development this will bring to the entire Chinese Revolution.

Finally, it was reported that the Nationalist Government, disappointed by the attitudes of the League of Nations and the United States after the incident occurred, turned to a pro-Soviet policy as part of their usual opportunistic containment strategy. Moscow's plenipotentiary representative was said to express willingness to make concessions, and it was even reported that the Chinese Communist Party would be officially recognized as a legal political party. Regardless of the certainty of this report, the 7th National Congress scheduled to be held at the end of this year is truly noteworthy.

Above, we have briefly glanced at the movements that appeared on the surface. Besides these, we must not forget that significant plots were exposed in the Manchuria area during this incident, leading to the arrest of 40 Japanese revolutionaries, or the continuous occurrence of anti-Japanese proclamation incidents in various places, starting with the righteous struggle outside X City. Especially noteworthy is the unprecedented support from the international proletariat for the Chinese revolutionary process.
- 1931. 12. 3 - <12>

**Summary**

This 1932 article by Pak Wŏn-il analyzes the Manchurian Incident not merely as a Sino-Japanese conflict but as a pivotal event accelerating the Chinese Revolution and exposing the deep-seated contradictions of global imperialism. The author argues that the incident reveals the powerlessness and reactionary nature of the Nationalist (Kuomintang) government, which compromises with imperialism while suppressing the true revolutionary forces – the workers and peasants led by the Communist Party and establishing Soviet areas. The League of Nations and Western powers are depicted as self-interested actors maneuvering for advantage amid China's turmoil. Pak predicts the incident will galvanize the Chinese masses, strengthen the Communist movement (particularly its northward expansion), and ultimately contribute to the global proletarian struggle against imperialism, highlighting the interconnectedness of the Chinese Revolution with world historical developments. From a Korean colonial history perspective, the article is significant as it shows a Korean intellectual analyzing regional power dynamics and revolutionary movements with a clear Marxist lens. It reflects the influence of international communist thought among some Koreans under Japanese rule and provides a contemporary Korean viewpoint on the failures of both Chinese nationalism (Kuomintang) and international diplomacy (League of Nations), positioning the Communist-led revolution, linked with the Soviet Union and international solidarity, as the only viable path for China and implicitly relevant to other oppressed nations.

滿洲事變과 中國革命
						朴元一
이 小稿를 畢한 것은 客年 12月 初3日이다. 그 後 1個月 以上이라는 時日을 經過하엿을 뿐 아니라 해가 뒷치게 된 오늘에 더욱이 刻刻으로 變하고 展開되어간 事態를 無視하고 이 글을 그대로 世間에 내놓는 것은 筆者의 本意가 아니나 할 수 없는 바뿐 事情으로 1字의 修正加稿를 못하니 讀者諸位는 理解하여 주시오. 蔣介石의 下野, 廣東派의 新政府, 漸急한 滿蒙의 風雲, 豹變하는 英米佛의 態度, 張學良의 錦州撤兵, 日本軍의 錦州入場, 中國共X軍의 活躍 等 모든 事態가 明瞭하게 된 오늘에 와서는 今後의 展望을 쉬웁게하는 바가 잇다고 하겟다. 따라서 이러한 不充分한 資料로서 構成된 論稿는 벌서 必要치 않을 것 같다. 그러나 中國XX의 發展過程에서 일어난 1環의 經過로서 看過치 못할事實인 同時에 그것은 今後의 展望을 쉬웁게 할지언정 阻害치 않을 줄 믿는다. 이 點에 筆者의 本意는 잇는 것이며 小稿의 意義도 잇다면 잇을 줄 믿는다.
- 1932, 1, 4 -

時期의 早晩은 別問題로 하고 東洋의 발칸 半島라고 이르는 中國의 天地, 더욱이 滿洲를 舞臺로 太平洋問題의-그것은 곳 現下 世界的 問題이다-解決을 爲한 부질 없는 그러나 激烈한 衝突이 演出될 것이라는 것에 對하여서 그리고 곳 第2次 世界大戰의 前哨戰이 될 것이라는 것에 對하여서는 世界의 動靜에 故意로 눈감어 버리려는 者가 아니면 벌서 何等의 疑心을 갖지 않을 것이다. 果然 그것은 갈데로 갓고 따라서 問題는 急速度로 複雜化하게 되엇다. 이것이 곳 滿洲를 舞臺로 지난 9月 18日의 滿鐵線爆破를 한 개의 契機로 한 日中의 衝突이며 그것은 南으로 北으로 東으로 西로 急한 戰雲이 往來하는 곳마다 雷聲이 發動하고 暴風雨가 일어나니 이제야 全世界의 구석구석에 危機는 彌滿하여 젓다.
여기에 잇어 風이냐? 雨냐? 國際聯盟의 態度는? 米國의 去趨는? 赤露의 動向은? 하고 世人의 推測은 區區하다. 勿論 國際聯盟을 中心으로 英米佛日伊 等 世界 帝國主義國家와 國民政府의 動向을 보며 푸로레타리아國家로서 赤露의 動向을 考察함은 問題의 全般的 捕捉에 잇어서 不可缺의 하나일 것이다. 그러나 다만 우리는 그 現象形態로서 表面에 表現된 그것만을 보아서는 資本主義 第3期의 全般的 問題의 核心을 把握할 수 없다. 各 國內의 內部的 情勢는 또다시 國際的 問題를 通하야 深刻化할것이니 이러한 情勢를 또다시 國際的 連鎖에서 把握하여야 된다.
日中의 衝突이 다만 日中의 關係가 아니고 日米의 關係이며 日露의 關係며 그것은 다시 日英, 英佛, 日佛의 世界的 關係인 同時에 帝國主義國家對 蘇聯의 關係인 것은 누구나 悉知하는 바이다. 그러나 資本主義 世界의 歷史的 發展은 이러한
第2次大戰의 前哨戰
을 契機로 하여 어데로 轉換될 것인가? 하는 問題가 오즉 문제의 ＜6＞ 核心이며 終局인 것을 알어야 된다. 그러므로 今日의 諸國의 態度 來日의 그것 等等 다시 말하면 世界戰爭은 爆發展開될 것인가 靜息될 것인가의 問題보다는 世界的 XX過程은 어떠케 進展되어 가는가? 하는 問題로서 認識하여야 될 것이다. 吾人이 滿洲의 事變과 中國革命을 論하는 微意는 여기에 잇다. 그것은 中國問題의 全般的 把握일뿐 아니라 世界歷史의 全般的 把握인 까닭이다. 웨 그런고 하면 中國의 問題는 中國의 革命을 通하야서만 解決될 것이며 中國問題의 解決은 곳 世界問題의 解決의 鍵인 까닭이다.

滿洲問題가 오직 滿洲에 局限된 問題가 아님은 勿論, 中國全體의 問題이며 世界全體의 問題이다. 中國XX過程에 잇어서 그것이 重大性을 가지게 된 것은 決코 今日에 와서 始作된 것이 아니다. 自然的 條件에 잇어서 또는 經濟的 政治的 條件에 잇어서 重要한 地位를 가지고 잇는 것이다. 그러나 그것이 世界的으로 現下의 重大性을 가지게 된 것은 第1次 大戰 後의 일이며 特히 最近 數年來로 問題는 急速度로 複雜하여진 것이니 滿洲가 政治的 으로 中國의 中央政府에서 隔離되어 잇는 동안에는 그다지 큰 矛盾은 發露되지 않엇섯다. 滿洲의 土着뿔주아지의 成長 乃至, 中央의 中國뿔주아지와의 利害의 共通性이 成長됨을 따라 張學良의 中央에의 歸順, 中央勢力의 滿洲에의 延長, 따라서 米國財閥의 滿洲에의 進出 等等은 必須的으로 日本權益과의 衝突를 招來하게 되엇섯다.
이 反面에 잇어 우리는 中國XX의 成長을 보나니 滿洲의 勞働者 農民이 中國運動의 主體로서 全中國XX에 連結된 것도 또한 以下에 말함과 같이 大戰 後의 일이다. 그뿐 아니라 그들은 自然的 經濟的 政治的 條件에 規定되어 日本 朝鮮 蘇聯 等等의 運動과 國際的 合流를 가장 容易하게 한 것이엇다. 그들은 모든 障碍가 잇엇음에도 不拘하고 同1한 進路로 向하여지게 되엇나니 이 點에 잇어서 滿洲問題의 去趨는 더욱더욱 重大하여진다.
一時 沈滯의 氣味를 가지든 中國의 革命은 最近 數3年來로 急速的 發展을 하엿다. 그것은 國內的으로나 國外的으로나 事變이 일어날 때마다 鬪爭을 通하야 全幅的으로 擴大되고 强大되엇나니 이 小論에 잇어서 吾人은 日中衝突을 契機로 中國의 過程은 어떠케 進展되고 잇는가를 簡單히 살펴보랴고 한다. 그러므로 우리는 爲先 數年來의 主XX過程을 一瞥하여야 된다.
南京政府가 中央統一을 부르짓고 東奔西走 南征北伐을 하여가면서 一方 治外法權 撤廢에 猛進하니 現象形態로서는 가장XX的 인 것같이 보이는 것이나 그는
地主 及 資本家의 政府
로서 自己勢力을 鞏固키 爲하야 모든 帝國主義의 角逐場裡에서 1帝國主義로 他帝國主義를 牽制-其實은 忠僕-함에 지나지 못하며 그 代償으로 그들 帝國主義勢力에 對하야 勞働者 農民의 抑壓이라는 職責을 다하여 옴에 不過하엿든 것이다.
國民黨 南京政府가 中國의 XX過程에서 背反한 것은 1927年의 일이다. 그들은 1927年 7月에 中國XX黨과 絶緣하엿고 1928年 4月에 이르러서 上海에 잇어서의 白崇禧의 구테타, 廣東에 잇어서의 李濟琛의 淸黨運動, XX黨에 對한 大規模의 白色테로가 敢行되엇섯다. 爾來 그들은 모든 民衆運動을 禁壓하엿나니 甚至於 南京政府 自身이 制定한 國民黨紀念의 示威運動의 類까지도 取締하여 왓섯다.
이러한 抑壓에도 不拘하고 中國XX勢力은 刻刻으로 成長되엇나니 그것은 모든 自然的 政治的 經濟的 危難, 더욱이 世界的 恐慌에 際하야 急速度로 擴大되엇섯다. 그 顯著한 것은 赤軍의 編成과 쏘벳트의 建設이라고 하겟다. 여러 가지 關係로 다만 最近의 ＜7＞ 쏘벳트鬪爭의 發展만을 一瞥하여 보고저 한다.

中國의 農村 쏘베트鬪爭의 發端은 南昌暴動이다. 南昌暴動은 武漢政府 崩壞의 直前에 勃發되엇나니 卽 1927年 6月 21日 張發奎部下 賀龍葉挺 及 朱培德*德 等이 南昌을 占領하고 XX黨員으로서 臨時政府를 組織한 事件이다. 그後 主要한 運動으로는 廣東, 江西, 湖北, 湖南, 江蘇地方에 遂行된 所謂 4省秋收運動, 長沙暴動 等이며 同年 12月의 有名한 海豊 陸豊 及 廣東쏘베트樹立 等이 잇엇다. 1928年 6月에 中國XX黨 第6回大會가 잇엿고 1930年 5月 上海에서 第1回 全國쏘베트區域 代表者 大會가 開催되엇나니 그 席上에서 政網, 土地法, 勞働法 等을 決定發表하엿음은 世人이 共知하는 바다. 그 後로 紅軍의 組織과 그 活動地域에 잇어서의 쏘베트政權確立運動은 急速度로 展開되엇나니 이제 그 現狀을 보건대
江西-南昌一帶를 除한 全部 卽 81縣 中 60縣이 쏘벳트區域이며 長江 兩岸 吉安下流의 浹江縣一帶는 赤軍地帶이다.
湖北-約 40縣이 쏘베트區域, 武漢郊外 10里內外까지 赤軍遊擊隊의 活動地帶.
福建-西部7縣 南部2縣 北部4縣이 쏘베트區域.
廣東-東江 西江 北江附近은 歷史的 쏘區域. 廣東暴動 後 抑壓을 받앗으나 그 基礎가 儼存하고 東江16縣 瓊崖7縣은 激烈한 鬪爭을 演하고 잇다.
廣西-西部20餘縣이 쏘베트運動 影響 下에 잇다.
湖南-白色테로와 가장 激烈한 地域이나 東部西部의 兩쏘區는 3年間 支持되어 南部에 向하야 急激發展하고 잇다.
河南-湖南 安徽에 接境한 地方은 農民鬪爭의 歷史的 區域이다. 最近 紅槍會의 分化가 遂行되어 商城을 中心으로 쏘區域이 擴大되어 잇다.
四川-最近 軍閥의 稅捐賦課에 反對하는 神兵運動이 發生하야 漸次 赤軍과 結合되고 잇다.
安徵-西部一帶에 赤軍遊擊戰擴大中.
江蘇-冥興 江陰 無錫은 1927年의 革命區域 지금은 小鬪爭이 遂行되고 잇다.
如斯히 하야 쏘베트運動은 非單 南中에 뿐 아니라 漸次 北進하고 잇나니 北部에 잇어서는 自發的 暴動이 擴大되어 大刀會 小刀會의 一部가 漸次 搾取階級의 利用에서 脫出하고 잇다 한다.
다음에 今番 問題의 策源地인 滿洲에 잇어서의 鬪爭組織을 一瞥하여 보자.
1922年 2月의 京漢鐵道 罷業 後 同年 末에 와서 大連中華工學會-勞働者의 夜學 及 相互扶助機關-가 創立되엇고 1924年 滿洲독그, 大連機械, 小野田세멘트, 福島紡績, 大連窯素, 大連電氣, 其他工場에 組織이 擴大되어 中國 唯一의 赤色勞組 「中華全國總工會」에 加盟한 XX的 勞組에까지 發展하엿다. 1924年 2月 河南鄭州에 開催된 全國鐵道總工會 第2會大會에 代表를 派遣하고 年來에 와서 大連 一帶의 全中國人 勞働者를 組織할 수 잇엇으나 日本官憲에 依하야 解散命令을 받고 그後 合法的 存在만은 잃어버렷섯다. 1926年 7月에 中國XX黨中央部는 黨大連地方委員會를 조직하고 27년 5월에 와서 5백여명의 黨員을 獲得하엿으나 奉天製麻罷業指導로 因하야 54名의 被檢을 본後 一時 沈滯狀態에 잇엇으나 1929年에 들어 勢力을 挽回하야 撫順炭鑛, 奉天等地에서 宣傳活動을 하여왓섯다. 1930年 5月에

XX主義에 對한 國民黨의 降服 中國쏘베트 及 勤勞大衆에 對한 蔣介石, 汪兆銘과 外國 壓迫者와의 計略은 그들의 眼前에 明白케 되엇나니 天津暴動이 일어나자 各國의 駐屯軍은 XX의 駐屯軍과 憲兵隊를 先頭로하야 出動되엇든 것이다. 이 天津의 民衆暴動이야말로
鍾소리인 同時에 全中國XX의 北進하는 前進曲일 것이다. 翌9日에 北平에는 臨時戒嚴令이 나리고 上海 漢口 赤是 動搖되어 政府는 非常特別戒嚴令을 發布하는 一方 租界當局 及 陸軍隊는 異常한 緊張을 보엿다. 天津地方의 暴動은 그 後도 連發되엇을 뿐 아니라 敦化, 吉林軍 其他 各軍隊內에서는 兵變이 일어나고 잇다.
如斯히하야 最近에 와서는 中國의 XX은 南中으로부터 北進하야 漸漸 쏘베트區域을 建設하고 잇으며 그들 滿洲의 農民大衆은 XX暴動의 旗를 中國의 軍閥과 XX의 XX主義에 對하야 抗立하고 잇는 것이니 蔣介石이나 張學良의 勢力이 全然 따에 떠러저 何等 對抗할 能力이 없음에 不拘하고 日本은 卽時 撤兵 乃至 期限附 撤兵을 斷然 反對하며 그 理由로 匪賊의 橫行이 人命財産 秩序를 攪亂함에 잇는 것이며 勢力을 잃은 國民政府는 이에 對하야 벌서 攻勢를 阻止하야 自身의 勢力挽回를 圖하는 同時에 卽時 撤兵 乃至 期限附撤兵 要求까지도 抛棄하는 것이다. 最近에 와서 張學良은 身邊의 不安을 느끼고 錦州攻勢 阻止를 策動하며 蔣介石은 宋財政部長으로 하여금 1千2百萬圓의 軍費調達을 하게 하엿으나 當地銀行團으로부터 拒絶을 當하엿다고 하니 그들의 運命은 이에서 그칠 것이다. 國際聯盟의 施代表는 英米佛伊의 4聯合軍을 錦州에 特派케 하야 緩衝地帶의 設立을 要求한다 하니 그들의 本意가 那邊에 잇는 것이며 그들의 本質이 如何한 것인가?는 여기에서 明痀½케 될 것이다. 要컨대 蔣張及 國民黨은 勞農大衆의 潮流가운데서 救援받기 爲하야 全世界XX主義에 呼訴하고 잇는 것이니 中國의 大衆은 直覺的으로 그것을 認識하는 同時에 그들의 活途에 邁進하고 잇을 것이다. 다시 말하면 그들 數百千萬의 勤勞階級은 그들 自身을 救하기 爲하야 鬪爭에 結合될 것이니 이것이 中國XX全體에 어떠한 展開를 볼 것인가 하는 問題를 解答하는 焦點이 된다.
最后로 今回의 事變이 일어나자 國際聯盟과 米國의 態度에 失望한 國民政府는 例의 그들의 機會主義的 牽制策으로 親露政策에 나아갓다고 傳하엿다. 莫斯科의 莫全權은 讓步의 意를 表하는 同時에 中國共産黨을 合法政黨으로 公認하리라고까지 報導되어잇다. 이 報導가 얼마나한 確實性을 가지는가와는 別問題로하고라도 今年末을 期하야 開催될 第7回全國大會는 참으로 注目되는 바이다.

以上에서 吾人은 表面에 나타난 運動을 大略 一瞥하엿다. 以外에도 今回의 事變에 잇어서 滿洲一帶에서 重大한 劃策이 發露되어 日本XX主義者 40名이 被檢되엇다는 것이며 또는 X市外의 X義鬪爭을 爲始하아 各地에 反X檄文事件이 連發된 것을 잊어서는 아니 된다. 特히 大書할 것은 中國XX過程에 잇어 國際的 푸로레타리아트의 空前的 聲援이다.
