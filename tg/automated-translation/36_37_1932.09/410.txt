**Body Text Translation:**
Household Affairs
						Kim Ki-jin

On the ash-gray roof, gourd flowers bloom white,
Into the collapsed mud wall base, pumpkin blossoms crawl,
A gaunt dog curls up before the flat stone step,
Shivering in the pouring rain shower.

Is there a child here whose sallow veins
Even move?
What games are being played?

In the kitchen, the dishes, making mouse-like sounds, nod off with clicks,
Here and there, gourds have fallen over, always yawning,
And the cauldrons, tilted askew on their trivets,
Only groan and groan!

Here, there is a ghost called household affairs
Crying whee whee whee whee, jwol jwol jwol jwol!

O master(?), wake from your nap!
Isn't rain leaking through the ceiling? <95>

**Summary**

This poem by Kim Ki-jin paints a bleak picture of extreme poverty and neglect in a rural Korean household. Using imagery like gray roofs, collapsing walls, a shivering dog, listless children, and anthropomorphized kitchen items that seem to groan and sleep, it evokes a sense of decay and desolation. The poem culminates by personifying the hardship itself as a crying ghost and urging the indifferent "master" to wake up to the leaking roof, symbolizing perhaps a call to awareness amidst pervasive misery. For historians, the poem offers a stark, evocative glimpse into the harsh living conditions and potential despair experienced by segments of the Korean population during the Japanese colonial period, reflecting the economic hardships and social atmosphere of the time.

살림사리
						金基珍
재ㅅ빛 집웅에 박꽃은 희게 피고,
쓰러진 을바지에 호박꽃은 게여들어,
파리한 개 한 마리 팡돌 앞에 꼬브리고,
쏘다지는 소낙비에 떨고 잇노라.
어린이 여기 잇어 누런 피ㅅ줄이
움지기나 하며,
무삼 작란을 하시노잇가?
부억에 그릇은 쥐소리 하며 깨닥깨닥 졸고,
군데군데 박아지는 넘어저 하품이 매양이며
소동깨 비껴쓴 가마가마는
꿍꿍 앓기만 하노라!
여기 살림사리란 귀신이 잇어
회회회회 죌죌죌죌 울고 잇노니!
주인이어(?) 낮잠을 깨이라
천정에 비새지 안느냐.＜95＞
