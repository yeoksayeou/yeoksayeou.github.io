Article Title: 綠原에 누어서 (Lying Down on a Green Field)
Author: 李大容
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

Lying Down on a Green Field
						Yi Taeyong

Lying on the boundless grassy field
I gaze at the boundless sky

In the grassy field, the scent is blue/green
In the sky, the wind is blue/green<95>

My vision turns blue/green, when I close my eyes
My breath turns blue/green, when I breathe

I realize youth, in that blue/greenness
I saw a new dream, in that blue/green light

Blue/green power, in sky and earth
Overwhelms like waves

Blue/green spirit, in mountains and all fields
Resounds like thunder<96>

**Summary**

This poem by Yi Taeyong depicts a speaker lying in a vast green field, completely immersed in the natural surroundings. The overwhelming presence of "blue/green" (푸르다) symbolizes the vitality of nature, youth, and burgeoning dreams. The senses are saturated with this color, leading to a feeling of powerful connection between the self, the sky, and the earth, culminating in an expansive sense of energy likened to waves and thunder. From a historical perspective, the poem's intense focus on youthful vitality and the power drawn from the Korean landscape (sky, earth, mountains, fields) during the colonial period could be interpreted as a subtle expression of national spirit and resilience, finding strength and hope in nature amidst oppressive circumstances.
