Song of August - A Poem Sent to the Sun
						Hwang Sunwŏn

Limp, limp, the August sun softening the earth,
A gloomy crisis where not a single leaf stirs,
The sound of yawning from building-like landlords (마림같은 삘딍)
Comes searching even here, filled with the smell of soil.

From farming villages, from cities, from fishing villages,
The outcry of the people, tightly packed and pouring out.
What is this scream, trampled down to the very core, what could it be?

Like molten metal (쇠ㅅ물) in a fiercely boiling forge (冶爐),
With the remnants of blood vessels formed in a man's spirit (영롱),
Fingers that had been raised powerfully pressed the keyboard,
Climbed onto the screen and shouted a cry,
But the cry of blood-stained youthful spirit (청춘혼)
Provides accompaniment more sorrowful than a dirge (輓歌).

Oh, August sun, melt us.
Oh, magnificent sound (雄宏한 음향), overwhelm us.
We, the true workers, will startle you
With the solemn song of August, which has traded sleep.
August 1932 <95>

**Summary**

This 1932 poem by Hwang Sunwŏn uses the oppressive heat of the August sun as a metaphor for the suffering and stifled atmosphere experienced by the Korean people. It depicts the collective anguish ("outcry," "scream") rising from all corners of society, suggesting deep societal distress. Despite attempts to express this pain powerfully ("pressed the keyboard," "shouted a cry"), the result is more sorrowful than a dirge. The poem ends with an invocation to the sun and a "magnificent sound" to bring about transformation, promising a powerful "solemn song" from the awakened "true workers." For historians, the poem offers insight into the atmosphere of despair and suppressed anger under Japanese colonial rule. The imagery of heat, suffocation, and the collective but unheard cries reflects the socio-political climate, while the final lines hint at a nascent desire for resistance or awakening, framed not as direct political action but as a powerful, transformative "song."

8월의 노래 - 태양에게 불러보내는 詩
						黃順元
흐늑흐늑 대지를 물쿠는 8월의 태양,
나무닢 하나 까닥이지 않는 음울한 위기,
마림같은 삘딍의 하품 소리는
흙냄새 가득찬 이곳에도 찾어오나니.

농촌에서 도회에서 어촌에서.
꽉꽉 메여 나오는 겨레의 아우성 소래
진두까지 짓밟힌 비명은 무슨 일일까, 무슨 일일까

이글이글 끌른 冶爐의 쇠ㅅ물처럼
장부의 영롱에 서리운 피ㅅ줄기의 남어지로
힘잇게 올렷든 손꾸락은 건반을 눌럿으나,
영상에 올라 고함을 첫으나
피묻은 청춘혼의 부르짖음은 輓歌 보다도
더 애끗게 반주하는구나.

8월의 태양아 우리를 녹켜라
雄宏한 음향이어 우리를 넘어치라
우리는 참 일꾼은 조음을 바꾼 8월의
장엄한 노래로 너히를 놀래줄 것이다.
1932년 8월 ＜95＞
