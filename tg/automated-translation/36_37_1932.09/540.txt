Sighs on a Summer Afternoon
						Yi Ŭng-su

With the fierce heat of the midsummer dog days (삼복지경), where red flames (紅熖) have devoured the world,
Whichever place, whichever house, whichever room one looks into,
Like unravelled cotton spread across the whole land (全地), like the four seas (四海),
Bodies soaked in fatigue lie collapsed unattractively—
It is a summer afternoon filled with lassitude (倦怠) and lethargy (無氣力).

At this time, lying powerlessly in my room, letting various daydreams (空想) fly,
Suddenly (홀연이) there was a thought that heavily pierced my heart,
And abruptly (돌연이), I picked up my brush and fan and went down below the courtyard.

These living corpses stretched out in lassitude and lethargy
Reminded me of today's Chosŏn.

The children and the old, the women and the men of this land,
Who should be tense all twelve months of the year,
To me, they all appear
Like the living corpses stretched out on this summer afternoon.
Sitting in the flower garden, pulling out blades of grass, I laid a deep sigh upon the flower petals.

I do not trust the person who speaks out midway,
I truly do not trust them.
But then again, are the hidden ones not too much nihilists (허무주의자)?<104>

**Summary**

The poem by Yi Ŭng-su depicts the oppressive lethargy of a hot summer afternoon, where people lie around like "living corpses." The speaker explicitly links this scene of exhaustion and powerlessness to the state of contemporary Korea (Chosŏn). Sitting amidst nature, the speaker laments this condition and expresses distrust towards those who compromise ("speak out midway") and ponders if those who remain silent have succumbed to nihilism. For historians, the poem offers insight into the atmosphere of despair and helplessness felt by some intellectuals under Japanese colonial rule, using the metaphor of summer heat to critique the socio-political climate. The closing lines hint at the internal debates and anxieties regarding resistance and collaboration or quietism.

여름대낮의 嘆息
						李應洙
紅熖이 세상을 먹어버린 삼복지경의 모진 더위로
어느곳 어느집의 어느방을 보아도
풀린솜같이 전지에 널린 사해같이
피로에 젖은 몸뎅이가 모양사납게 쓰러저잇는
권태와 무기력으로 가득찬 여름의 대낮이로다.

내 이때 방안에 힘없이누어 가지가지의 공상을 날리다가
홀연이 가슴을 묵직이 찌르는 생각이 잇어
돌연이 붓채를 들고 뜰아래로 내려갓도다.

이권태와 무기력에 느러진 산 송장들이
내게 오늘의 조선을 연상케 하엿노라.

일년 열 두달 언제나 긴장해 잇어야 할
이땅의 어린이나 늙은이, 게집이나 사나이
내게 그들의 모두는
이 여름대낮에 느러진 산송장들 같이 보이여
꽃밭에 앉어 풀뿌리를 뽑으면서 깊은 한숨을 꽃닢 우에 끼여얹엇습니다.

중도에서 떠드는 사람을 나는 안 믿노라,
나는 실로 그들을 않믿노라.
그러나 또 감추인 사람은 너무 허무주의자가 아닌가.＜104＞
