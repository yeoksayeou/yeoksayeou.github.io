Magazine Title: 동광 제37호
Publication Date: 1932년 09월 01일
Article Title: 中國現代特輯(2) 말괄량이 全1幕 (Special Feature on Modern China (2): The Tomboy, Complete 1 Act)
Author: 中國 歐陽子倩(作), 梁白華(譯) (China, Ouyang Ziqian (Writer), Yang Paek-hwa (Translator))
Article Type: 희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Special Feature on Modern China
China, Ouyang Ziqian (Writer), Yang Paek-hwa (Translator)

Setting: The reception room of an upper-middle-class home.
Characters:
Ch'en Shen-chih (陳愼之) (30 years old)
His wife, Yü Su-hsin (于素心) (24 years old)
His father, Yi-li (以禮) (55-56 years old)
His mother, O-ssi (吳氏) (Originally Yi-li's concubine, 48 years old)
His newly acquired concubine, Wang-ssi (王氏) (16-17 years old)
His younger sister, Chih-hsiang (芷祥) (25 years old)
His paternal aunt (45-46 years old)
Female servant
Old maidservant
Male servant

(The curtain opens. Yi-li and his wife O-ssi are sitting facing each other, talking. Yi-li is reading a newspaper, and O-ssi is smoking a water pipe.)

Yi-li: (Scoffs.)
O-ssi: What are you laughing at?
Yi-li: I really don’t understand what these people are saying these days. Even less so what they are doing.
O-ssi: What’s the use in knowing that? I don’t read the newspaper, nor do I want to know.
Yi-li: Even when things are erupting right before our eyes?
O-ssi: Ah! I thought my dear husband was laughing about matters of state!
Yi-li: How can I manage matters of state when I can’t even manage my own household! Humph, the main house, the small house, it's a laughingstock everywhere!
O-ssi: (Scoffs) Well, we'll just have to see how things turn out—. She has her own house but doesn't live in it, instead coaxing her husband to go live outside. She must find her father-in-law and mother-in-law bothersome. But honestly, when did we ever treat the daughter-in-law of this house the way we were treated when we lived with our in-laws? We treated her like a guest, but she still says she doesn't like it. The daughter-in-law of the house<117> says she wants to live separately. Is she lacking clothes? Lacking food? What is she lacking that she acts this way? Becoming a daughter-in-law in someone else's house means looking after household affairs, serving the parents-in-law, raising children, what else is there?
Yi-li: It’s about wanting to have affairs, pursue things like love, and seek things like freedom.
O-ssi: Freedom? What lack of freedom does the young mistress of this house have? If she wants to play mahjong, she can. If she wants to go amuse herself at the "Great World" (「대세계」) or "New World" (「신세계」), she can. What else could she want? She just acts too crazy, playing the fashionable 'high collar' (하이카라) type. Love too. What parents-in-law are obstructing her? (Laughs) If she wanted to raise her child well, wouldn't it be better to live together?...
Yi-li: She's just following the times and setting up a separate household. The couple, without knowing why, are just following the trend. That's what's ridiculous! Nowadays, they imitate Westerners, needing to embrace wildly in wide courtyards with those they fancy to feel satisfied! The day they start running around naked in the streets won't be far off.
O-ssi: Lately, it seems Shen-chih doesn't cherish her that much either.
Yi-li: Let him do as he pleases. Fortunately, it wasn't arranged by the parents, and she'll bear children. Let him do as he likes.
O-ssi: That girl of his, you mean. Is he bringing her today?
Yi-li: Since there's a daughter-in-law and he's seen a grandchild, I should forbid him from taking a concubine, but the daughter-in-law can't manage her affairs, and the situation is urgent, so let him do as he pleases.

(Female servant enters.)
Female Servant: The Aunt and the Young Lady have arrived.
O-ssi: Oh.
(The Aunt and Chih-hsiang enter together. An old maidservant follows, carrying gifts in a paper box.)
Aunt: Older Brother! Sister-in-law!
Chih-hsiang: Father! Mother!
Yi-li, O-ssi: Have you been well? Sit down!

(They exchange greetings. Yi-li looks at the gifts.)
Yi-li: Why did you spend money again?
Aunt: There wasn't anything particularly good.
Chih-hsiang: If Father doesn't eat it, I'll eat it instead—. Why isn't Older Brother here? Is he busy with bank work?
Yi-li: The bank is closed today.
Aunt: He must have gone out with the Young Mistress?
O-ssi: Is there any need to ask?... Who says you are as virtuous as our Aunt? It would be nice if she were.
Aunt: What's virtuous about me? It's just that I've aged a bit, so even if I wanted to have an affair, I couldn't. (All laugh.)
Yi-li: Now, tone down that kind of talk about affairs. Just hearing about it gives me a headache. (Looking at Chih-hsiang) You shouldn't learn from your older brother's wife!
Chih-hsiang: How could someone like me follow her example?
Yi-li: Right. Is your husband doing well?
Chih-hsiang: He's fine. He's always at home and doesn't go out much.
O-ssi: Is the girl your husband brought home obedient?
Chih-hsiang: She’s good enough, I suppose. Men can't be trusted. It's fortunate enough if they take a concubine and don't cause trouble outside. I hear Older Brother is bringing home a concubine too.<118> Is it true?
(All laugh and feign ignorance. O-ssi says something to the Aunt. Chih-hsiang asks, "What?")
Aunt: He's bringing her today; we didn't know.
Chih-hsiang: If Sister-in-law finds out, will she remain quiet?
O-ssi: Your older brother says he's bringing someone from outside because his wife doesn't manage things well and doesn't know how to serve her elders. This is all just an excuse born from being infatuated with this 'new style' and 'civilization.'
Yi-li: (Sighs deeply.)
O-ssi: Isn't there a saying? "When children grow up, they don't listen to their parents." We old folks have no choice but to let them do as they please. What luck do people like us have to deserve someone to attend to us? We wouldn't dare entertain such useless thoughts, but since your older brother has already made his decision, he's bringing her today. So, if your sister-in-law finds out, there will surely be a huge uproar with crying and screaming— but crying and screaming would earn her criticism for not being like a civilized person! So, it would be best if we could prevent her from making a scene. Therefore...
Yi-li: That's enough. Her complaining is useless. And if she understands reason, she won't make a scene. It's been customary since ancient times for a man to have three or four wives. Your mother was originally... (O-ssi signals him to stop.) So she gave birth to and raised you children; isn't it the same!
O-ssi: What's the use of telling her that?
Yi-li: Perhaps. But parents might as well be the instigators.
O-ssi: Now, listen to me... The reason I wanted everyone to come early today is, Older Sister, your husband also took a concubine, didn't he? (Turning to Chih-hsiang) Too, your husband also recently took a concubine. So, everyone has experience. Later, if you see the Young Mistress, Older Sister, please explain the situation simply. You too, from the side, persuade her gently so she understands. If she listens and compares herself to others and clearly realizes the situation, won't things resolve peacefully without fuss? I just didn't say anything beforehand because I was afraid she would find it bothersome.
Aunt: If it were about attending a wedding feast, maybe, but persuading the Young Mistress? I don't want to!
Chih-hsiang: Auntie dislikes it too, so I dislike it even more. (A voice from outside shouts, "The young master is arriving.")
O-ssi: Shen-chih is returning. (Shen-chih appears, dark-complexioned with a mustache, carrying a leather bag and a short cane, wearing glasses and Western clothes, looking imposing.)
Shen-chih: Oh my! Auntie, Sister, you came too.
Aunt, Chih-hsiang: We came to congratulate you!
Shen-chih: Congratulate me for what!
Chih-hsiang: Older Brother is playing innocent. Aren't you bringing the new person today?
Shen-chih: Don't talk nonsense. What new person? I'm just buying a servant girl to attend to Father and Mother.
Aunt: Stop it. Why drag Father and Mother into your own affairs?
Chih-hsiang: (Pointing at Shen-chih) Didn't Older Brother say he would *never* take a concubine?
Shen-chih: The past is the past, and the present is the present. Before, I was a student; now, I am the assistant manager of the bank. Soon, I'll be the manager. Therefore, if I don't take a concubine, I can't be considered a modern man. Haha! (Half-jokingly, trying to cover up his <119> fault, he laughs.)
Chih-hsiang: What will you do if Sister-in-law gets angry?
Shen-chih: Please, Auntie and you, talk to her gently.
Aunt, Chih-hsiang: We don't know anything about it.
Shen-chih: Please, please. (Nods his head.)

(A servant brings candles and lights them.)
Shen-chih: Why are you lighting those?
Servant: The car will arrive soon.
Shen-chih: (Looks at his watch) Isn't it still early?
Aunt: Your heart must be pounding.
Shen-chih: Such words.
(A male servant and female servant enter hurriedly, saying, "She's here. She's here.")
O-ssi: Chih-hsiang, go out and see.
Aunt: I'll go too.
O-ssi: There's no need for that.
(Chih-hsiang doesn't go out but waits at the doorway. The female servant leads three people in. Chih-hsiang tells the newcomer to bow first to Yi-li, then to O-ssi, then to Shen-chih. Then O-ssi has her bow to the Aunt and Chih-hsiang. Chih-hsiang and the others offer congratulations to Yi-li, O-ssi, and Shen-chih.)
Yi-li: (To the concubine) Oh, now that you've come to our house, it's different from elsewhere. Serve your husband according to propriety. If there's anything you don't know, ask Mother first. Work hard, speak little, and behave well. Then everyone will cherish you.
(The concubine keeps her head bowed. Just then, it is announced that the Young Mistress has arrived. Everyone's expression changes. They usher the concubine inside and remove the red candles. Su-hsin enters, holding a child. Behind her, an old maidservant carries a bundle wrapped in a cloth. As she enters, everyone looks at each other and smiles. Su-hsin finds it suspicious but pays it no mind. O-ssi takes the child, cooing, "How cute, how cute." Everyone says something in turn. Shen-chih takes out a Luzon cigar and smokes it. The female servant and the old maidservant look at each other unnaturally. Su-hsin takes the bundle, pulls out a pair of shoes and a set of clothes, and speaks to O-ssi.)
Su-hsin: Mother, I made these for you. I don't know if they'll fit.
O-ssi: Anything you make is wonderful. Thank you so much. (Very kindly)
(At this time, Yi-li remains. The Aunt looks at the shoes and praises them, "What neat craftsmanship!" Shen-chih takes Chih-hsiang's hand and shakes it as a sign of respect, indicating she should speak well to Su-hsin.)
Su-hsin: Did Mother eat that fish sometime ago?
O-ssi: It was truly delicious. You are really filial. And so well-behaved. Making clothes, making shoes, sending side dishes, and at home, you look after the child and read books. It's good to rest a little. And if you don't have time, there's no need to come here every day. You come thinking of us, but... (She says this while fussing over the child again.)
Su-hsin: It wasn't that busy. It took me half a month to make one garment; what's so neat about it! (All laugh.)
Aunt: I wish I had such talent. It would be fun if you could teach me a little foreign language.
(Yi-li calls O-ssi, saying, "Come and see." O-ssi understands and quickly<120> goes inside. The Aunt follows, saying, "I should go rest over there too." Shen-chih yawns as if to leave, saying, "I have to write a letter." Su-hsin signals him with her eyes to stop, saying, "I have something to ask you." Shen-chih stops stiffly. Chih-hsiang runs off. Shen-chih and Su-hsin look at each other and smile.)
Su-hsin: What's going on?
Shen-chih: What's going on with you?
Su-hsin: There's nothing going on with me. Why does everyone look at me like they're hiding something?
Shen-chih: That's because Su-hsin is being suspicious. Old people think differently from us. That doesn't matter at all. As long as we, the couple, live contentedly, that's enough.
Su-hsin: Between the two of us, no problems have ever arisen until now, right?
Shen-chih: That's only natural. Ours wasn't arranged by our parents. Thinking about it, there probably isn't a marriage in the world as perfect as ours. When we first met at the art exhibition, we hit it off after just a few simple words. And though there were some difficulties, we married easily. Our affection reached its peak. Even though we're married, it's like we're not. Even now that we have a child, it's like before our honeymoon. Every day when I'm working outside, I always want to come home. When I come home and see you, it feels just like the first day of our honeymoon. That's because our love has 쌓이고 쌓여서 (piled up and piled up) and is different from others'. (Su-hsin seems deep in thought.) (Shen-chih takes the child and says.) This is a memento of our love... Ah, the memento of love... a treasure...
Su-hsin: (Takes the child) It goes without saying that I love you. After promising myself to you, I dedicated my body and soul entirely to you.
Shen-chih: My body and soul are also completely given to Su-hsin!
Su-hsin: It's just that we are too happy, and I worry that we might incur the jealousy of creation (조물) and suddenly face misfortune!
Shen-chih: Rest assured. Our hearts have been tempered a thousand, ten thousand times. Whether the form changes or not, the spirit will always be the same!
Su-hsin: You speak of changes in form, but I don't understand the meaning. The power of the current environment is immense, so it's not so easy for one person to overcome the environment. I just worry that being dominated by the environment, changes in form might simultaneously lead to changes in spirit?
Shen-chih: Su-hsin, do you doubt me?
Su-hsin: How could I possibly doubt you? Doubting you is the same as doubting myself. It's just...
Shen-chih: What?
Su-hsin: It's just that when I was studying at school, having not encountered real society (실사회), my heart was truly pure. Then, after graduating and coming out into the world to work, I came into contact with everything, and on top of that, received some pressure from customs, gradually changing from my initial mindset. As this went on, it naturally began to affect various matters!
Shen-chih: Su-hsin, why do you say such things? I don't understand. (Appears uneasy but forces a smile.)
Su-hsin: If you don't understand, that's good! (Shen-chih places his hand on Su-hsin's shoulder. Su-hsin holds the child with one hand and holds Shen-chih's hand with the other.)
Shen-chih: Anyway, a thousand words boil down to one. Whatever happens, I am me. I won't change my principles. I won't change my beliefs!<121>
Su-hsin: (Shen-chih takes the child. Su-hsin leans her head on Shen-chih's shoulder, silent for a moment. Shen-chih pats Su-hsin's back.)
Shen-chih: Now, let's stop... Su-hsin, what has touched you again to make you say such things!
Su-hsin: (Lifts her head, looking like she might cry or laugh, sighs) Eh... When love reaches a certain point, there's a fear that unexpected things might happen. I'm truly afraid of that. That's what I fear the most!
Shen-chih: What are you afraid of? Are you still afraid even if I guarantee it?
Su-hsin: I also guarantee that I will trust you completely.
Shen-chih: (Takes a jeweled necklace from his pocket) Let me give Su-hsin a token of guarantee.
Su-hsin: (Smiling) Even if you didn't buy such things, I've never hinted for them! (The child cries. She scolds it.)
Shen-chih: It's just a little fun. This is the result of my labor. I'm using your body to present it. (Puts the necklace on Su-hsin and looks at his watch) Ah, I need to write an important letter. It seems Auntie and Sister have something to say to you! (Chih-hsiang peeks through the crack in the door. Shen-chih signals towards her.) (Su-hsin paces back and forth, seemingly thinking about something. Chih-hsiang and the Aunt push each other, reluctant to come out. They finally emerge reluctantly. Once out, they laugh and push each other again.) (They enter and speak to Su-hsin, all very formally.)
Su-hsin: Auntie, Sister. (They continue laughing.) Why are you laughing like that? Did something funny happen?
Aunt: It's nothing. Fools laugh easily.
Su-hsin: Auntie, you said your head hurt, are you feeling better? (Chih-hsiang takes the child.)
Aunt: Better. Thank you!
Su-hsin: I heard the little house (concubine) is showing signs of pregnancy (태기)?
Aunt: So they say. That's one worry (한시름) relieved.
Chih-hsiang: Auntie must have been heartbroken before too!
Aunt: Thinking about it now, I was quite foolish. What was there to be angry about? It's common at that age for men to cherish women; it's something unavoidable. If he takes a concubine, you can't confront him directly. It only damages the marital relationship and causes unpleasantness. It's better to half-listen and make the other party feel sorry. By the way, your uncle (아젓씨) is doing quite well these days. The little house always comes and helps with chores. I let her do it, and now that there's a concubine, I just hope he doesn't run around causing trouble outside. (To Chih-hsiang) They say the new little house at your place is well-behaved too.
Chih-hsiang: Yes. It's fine. No matter how capable one is, managing household affairs is difficult. As long as we maintain appearances decently, that's enough. In a big family like ours, would it do any good just to be jealous?
Aunt: If the marital relationship is good, what does one concubine matter? Even if he took ten or nine, could they occupy even half the place of the legal wife (정실)? Besides, I'm old, but for young and pretty women like you, why wouldn't your husband like you?
Su-hsin: Is being liked by men the only thing that matters for a woman in this world!
Aunt: It's not just about being liked by men, but... men are picky, and it's incredibly difficult for a woman to earn a reputation for being virtuous among others.
Su-hsin: (Smiling) People like Auntie and Sister are truly virtuous, aren't you?
Chih-hsiang: Oh, listen to her! Sister-in-law is the truly virtuous one.<122> (Note: Original page number missing, inferred based on sequence)
Aunt: That's absolutely right.
Chih-hsiang: Where else could you find such a fine couple as Older Brother and Sister-in-law? Besides, Sister-in-law is a school graduate, so even if Older Brother has some shortcomings, he'll be influenced by Sister-in-law's character. And,
Aunt: Absolutely!
Su-hsin: Don't say such things, Sister. And what were you about to say just now?
Chih-hsiang: Oh, nothing, just talking about how well our Older Brother is doing.
Aunt: Shen-chih is a decent person too. The Young Mistress must know that.
Su-hsin: (Annoyed) What's there to know or not know.
Aunt, Chih-hsiang: (Laugh loudly.)
Su-hsin: Did someone really arrange a concubine for the child's father? Is it true?
Aunt, Chih-hsiang: Where did you hear that?
Su-hsin: I just sensed it from everyone's expressions.
Aunt: Nothing like that happened. Just speaking hypothetically... if he were to take a concubine, what would you do? (As if expecting some reaction. Chih-hsiang just keeps laughing.)
Su-hsin: What would I do? What could I do?
Chih-hsiang: Sister-in-law, wouldn't you be angry?
Su-hsin: I wouldn't be angry.
Aunt: I wasn't originally going to say this, but since you asked, I have to tell you. You're a kind-hearted person, so it should be alright to tell you?
Su-hsin: I'll have to find out eventually anyway. Auntie, please tell me directly.
Aunt: He's already brought her home.
Su-hsin: (Greatly shocked, but deliberately remains calm) Yes! She's already here? That's ridiculous. What does it matter if the child's father takes a concubine? It's not like I forbade him, so why did he have to deceive me!
Aunt: That's why they asked us to explain it nicely to you. (Smiling) He probably felt bad about it. It was difficult to tell you face-to-face, and he felt very sorry— that just shows he has a conscience!
Su-hsin: How ridiculous. What's there to feel sorry about or not? Just doing things that way isn't right.
Chih-hsiang: Father and Mother's idea is that you should act as though you acquired her for Older Brother's sake.
Su-hsin: (Struggling intensely to suppress her anger.) I'm not that kind. I can't do that... (Looks at the necklace.)
Chih-hsiang: Between yourselves (사기지차), Sister-in-law, what are your thoughts?
Aunt: (Smiling) She won't stay silent, I bet. (Chih-hsiang moves closer.)
Chih-hsiang: Give him a piece of your mind! (Watches Su-hsin stealthily.)
Su-hsin: (Scoffs) If it's something he likes, what can I say! The new person must be that kind of woman!
Aunt: Probably so. Shen-chih works at the bank. People from that sphere visit him. And socializing nowadays tends to be like that too! But his elegant ways (풍치좋게) are also your good fortune (복).
Chih-hsiang: Everyone envies Sister-in-law's fortune (분복).
Aunt: But for a young man living in a bustling place, where can you find someone who truly maintains such self-control? (Focuses her attention.)
Su-hsin: There's no need to mention this to the child's father, but since she has come, she can't be hidden forever. How about we meet her? Please call her. Firstly, the child's father will be pleased. Secondly, both Father and Mother <123> will be relieved. Auntie, Sister, what do you think? (The Aunt and Chih-hsiang are greatly surprised.)
Aunt: Really?
Chih-hsiang: I never thought Sister-in-law could be so kind.
Su-hsin: It's not about being kind or not. What else can be done?
Aunt, Chih-hsiang: Doing that is good!
Su-hsin: Then, Auntie, Sister, please tell Father, Mother, and the child's father that since she arrived today, we should all have a formal meeting (상우례)!
Aunt: I'll tell them.
Su-hsin: I'll go prepare some gift for the formal meeting and come back. (Exits.)
(The Aunt and Chih-hsiang look at each other in amazement.)
Aunt: What do you think? See!
Chih-hsiang: Let's tell Father and Mother it turned out like this! (Shen-chih enters.)
Shen-chih: How did it go?
(Chih-hsiang exits with a strange expression.)
Aunt: You really lucked out today!... Your wife is truly kind!
(Shen-chih laughs. Yi-li enters with O-ssi and Chih-hsiang. The female servant and old maidservant follow.)
Yi-li: Then that's good. It should be that way!
O-ssi: This is a rare occurrence. It's your fortune. The Young Mistress is truly kind. Have them meet right away. (Chih-hsiang looks shy from afar, and Shen-chih laughs. Shen-chih gives Chih-hsiang an angry look.)
Aunt: They have to meet eventually. I'll go call the young lady (애기씨)!
Yi-li: Naturally, you must call the new girl and have them formally meet.
Chih-hsiang: I'll call her! (Exits. Returns with Shen-chih's concubine, Wang-ssi.)
Shen-chih: Well, I should get going! (Starts to leave but hears Su-hsin calling.)
Su-hsin: Hey there, wait a moment! (Shen-chih looks extremely awkward. He just bows his head, unable to properly lower it, nor force a smile, standing stiff as a board (붓백).)
Yi-li, O-ssi: Chih-hsiang, tell the new person to bow to her elder sister-in-law!
Chih-hsiang: Yes. (Starts to lead Wang-ssi forward.)
Su-hsin: Stop it. Why are you doing that? (To Wang-ssi) I am a person just like you. I don't know you at all, so why should you bow to me?
Everyone: It's proper etiquette!
Su-hsin: No! (To Wang-ssi) How did you come here?
Wang-ssi: I didn't come of my own free will. The master (서방님) of this house brought me!
Su-hsin: I know that too. He deceived you with money and brought you here.
Wang-ssi: I don't know.
Su-hsin: Please rest assured. No one will harm you. (To Shen-chih) What did you say before? What did you always say to me? What did you just tell me moments ago? Didn't you oppose polygyny (일부다처제)? Didn't you advocate the sanctity (신성) of romantic love (연애)? Didn't you claim to be a key figure advocating women's liberation (여자해방)? Didn't you make it your principle (신조) never to deceive? Didn't you advocate for the abolition of selling women (폐창), saying that powerless (무대한) women cannot be oppressed with money (금전)? Can you still not take off that mask of justice and humanity (정의인도)? Today, you have proven yourself to be false (허위) from head to toe! (Shen-chih laughs.) You have nothing to be triumphant (득의양양) about. Laughing or crying cannot hide your falsehood.<124> I was completely deceived by you. Why couldn't I see your weaknesses when we first started seeing each other? You deceive others and feel triumphant about it. And you abandon me and go deceive others again. What more is there to say? First, release her. Return her indenture contract (몸판 문서) to set her free, and also give her 2,000 won so she can live independently. (Everyone is silent. Shen-chih just keeps smiling.)
Yi-li: (Greatly angered) Is that reasonable talk! Where is the principle that allows the main wife to persecute (핍박하는) the husband into chasing away the minor wife? No matter how jealous you are, you can't do this in front of everyone! From today, the parents are on your side, what more could you possibly lack!
Su-hsin: I have made up my mind. You shouldn't try to threaten me by pinning such a heinous accusation on me. If you all refuse to listen to what I say, I will kill this child. (Pulls out a small knife and holds it to the child's neck. Everyone tries to snatch it away.) Go ahead, try to take it. My knife will fall immediately. Now, what will you do? It depends on one word! (Everyone frowns and signals to Shen-chih to placate her for a moment.)
Shen-chih: (Reluctantly takes out Wang-ssi's indenture contract and a check (수형), giving them to Wang-ssi.) Alright, alright, I'll do as you say! (To Wang-ssi) I'm giving this to you, so do whatever you please with it! (Turning back to Su-hsin) Is this better? (To Wang-ssi) You can go rest in the back now!
Everyone: That's fine! That's fine!
Su-hsin: Stay right there! (To Wang-ssi) Rip up that document. (Wang-ssi tears it, and Su-hsin snatches the pieces and tears them further. Wang-ssi looks frightened.) You stand there. I will thoroughly vent all the grievances I hold today. I will take responsibility! (Everyone is greatly surprised. Yi-li just sighs, and O-ssi just placatingly says, "Stop now," "Stop now." Su-hsin speaks again to Wang-ssi.) No matter what, you cannot escape his grasp. Even if you leave, it will amount to nothing. Follow me now. I will provide you with a proper education so you can become independent. I will treat you like my own younger sister. From now on, I will ensure you are never deceived by a man again! I will guarantee (담보할) your situation now. But I have my own matter to attend to! (To Shen-chih) Let's separate now. Please write two copies of the divorce papers (이혼장). Sign one and give it to me, and I will sign the other and give it to you. (Shen-chih hesitates.) Don't pretend (가작하지마시고), just write it readily (선 듯 쾌쾌히)!
Aunt: You are a good couple; if you have something to say, just say it all. Why do things like this?
Shen-chih: If Su-hsin insists on separating, I have no choice. I'll write it! (Gets paper and writes.)
Chih-hsiang: Older Brother, why are you doing this? This is all just a momentary outburst; why are you taking it so seriously? What about Father and Mother? (Tries to stop him.)
Yi-li: Ugh! Do as you please! Parents are just bothersome things now. All useless items! (Exits.)
O-ssi: I don't know! (Cries. Chih-hsiang tries to console her.)
(Shen-chih finishes writing the divorce papers and gives them to the female servant to take to Su-hsin. Su-hsin signs, and they each take one copy.)
Su-hsin: Now it's done! Thank you. (To Wang-ssi) Look here, rest assured.<125> I will definitely do this without fail (위불없이)! I will always help you, so come with me. I will definitely make you into a useful person. (Wang-ssi looks very troubled but seems resigned.) I am taking this child with me.
Shen-chih: You can't do that!
O-ssi: How can that be allowed?
Yi-li: (Entering from inside) Taking the child! What ridiculous talk is this? The child is a descendant of the Ch'en family. If you stay here, you are the child's mother, but since you are divorced, you are a complete outsider. How can you take the child? You cannot take the child. No matter what, you cannot!
Su-hsin: (Pointing at the child) The child is not your private property (사유물). The child is common property (공유물) of the nation and the world. I cannot leave a promising future citizen (유위의 국민) in such a household. To be raised under such deceitful (기만) paternal authority (부권), receiving such deceitful education, creating a sinful youth (죄악청년)! It is a mother's sin to let a clean, flawless (틔가 없는) child be tainted by sin. If I foresee a bad future, it would be better for the child to die cleanly at the mother's hands right now! (Raises the knife as if to cut. Everyone is greatly shocked.) (Su-hsin laughs.) How could I possibly kill my child? My treasure? I have no time for useless talk now. (To Wang-ssi) Come, sister, let's go! (Pulls Wang-ssi and exits. Su-hsin throws the necklace at Shen-chih, saying.) The guarantee of love! (Wang-ssi follows helplessly.)
Everyone: (Look at each other) What a terribly unruly woman (말괄량이)!

**Summary**

This one-act play, translated from Chinese in 1932, depicts the confrontation between U So-sim, a modern, educated woman, and her husband, Jin Sin-ji, who secretly takes a concubine (Wang-ssi) despite their supposedly modern, love-based marriage. Pressured by family and societal norms, Sin-ji attempts to maintain traditional privileges under a veneer of modernity. So-sim discovers the deception, denounces her husband's hypocrisy regarding love, marriage, and women's rights, demands he free the concubine, secures a divorce, and defiantly leaves with both her child and the newly freed Wang-ssi, whom she promises to educate and protect. The play vividly portrays the clash between patriarchal traditions (concubinage, filial duty) and emerging modern ideals (individualism, women's autonomy, companionate marriage) in 1930s East Asia. For historians, this piece is valuable as it reflects the social tensions and transformations occurring in Korea during the colonial period, particularly concerning changing gender roles, the struggles of educated 'New Women', the critique of patriarchal hypocrisy, and the cross-cultural exchange of modern ideas (here, a Chinese play addressing issues resonant in Korea).

(칼로 자르랴고 한다. 모다들 크게 놀란다.) (素心이 웃는다.) 내 참아 어떠케       어린애를 죽여요? 나의 보배를? 나는 인제 쓸데없는 말할 새가 없어.(王氏를 향하야)       자 동생 우리 갑시다! (王氏를 끄을고 퇴장. 素心은 목걸이를 愼之에게 팽겨치며 말한       다.) 사랑의 보증품이라고! (王氏는 하는 수 없어 따라 퇴장.)
모다들  (얼골을 서로 보며) 참 지독한 말괄량인걸!
