Old News from Newspapers

Yŏ Unhyŏng After Release from Prison
Yŏ Unhyŏng, who had been weaving nets at Taejŏn Prison, was suddenly released on parole four months before completing his sentence. His family, who had gone for a visit, were reportedly surprised by the unexpected news. The released Yŏ is currently staying at his younger brother's (季氏댁) house in Kahoe-dong, Kyŏngsŏng. Perhaps due to the hardships of prison (옥고), his hair is sparsely gray, but his radiant eyes and resolute voice remain unchanged, though it seems he will have to start growing his Kaiser mustache (가이젤 수염) again from scratch. Speaking of Yŏ, he is the person who gained renown overnight back in the Kimi year (己未年, 1919) when, while in Shanghai, he was invited by then Minister of the Army Tanaka Giichi and Minister of Colonial Affairs Koga Renzō to cross over to Tokyo, where he was reportedly treated like a state guest (X빈 대우, likely 國賓待遇) and met with prominent figures both in and out of government (朝野名士).
When asked about that time, he says there were negotiations where Prime Minister Hara Takashi suggested that if Korea were given autonomy (자치), Yŏ should come over to Kyŏngsŏng, confer with the 33 signers [of the 1919 Declaration of Independence], and attempt an autonomy preparatory movement. Of course, he firmly rejected this and went back to Shanghai, only to become imprisoned (囹圄) the year before last and was serving his sentence.

Mr. An Chaehong and His Sense of Responsibility
According to Yi Chongnin's assessment (평), Minse (民世) An Chaehong is a person with a very deep sense of responsibility. Setting aside what grounds this assessment might have, Mr. An, since his release from prison, has declared his sense of responsibility, saying, "The responsibility to restore the Chosŏn Ilbo to its original state (원상회복) rests with me."
"However," he was heard lamenting (한탄하드라고), "even just the internal relationships (내부관계) are triangular (삼각, i.e., complex), so resolving (수습) the situation is not easy (용이치 않다고)." He also reportedly revealed (吐露) his deep feelings (감개), saying that once it is restored to its original state, he firmly intends (심산) to retire to the countryside (田園에 歸臥). <5>

**Summary**

This news article reports on the recent early parole of Yŏ Unhyŏng from Taejŏn Prison in 1932 and his current condition. It recounts his past fame from a 1919 trip to Tokyo where Japanese officials, including Prime Minister Hara Takashi, allegedly offered Korean autonomy if Yŏ collaborated, an offer he rejected. The article also covers An Chaehong's release from prison and his expressed determination to restore the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper, despite acknowledging the complex internal challenges, after which he plans to retire. For historians, the article offers insight into the post-prison activities and attitudes of key nationalist figures. Yŏ Unhyŏng's recollection of the autonomy discussion with Hara Takashi provides a potentially significant detail about Japanese colonial policy considerations or tactics in 1919. An Chaehong's situation highlights the difficulties faced by Korean intellectuals and nationalists in reviving cultural or media institutions under colonial surveillance and control.

新聞舊聞
출옥 후의 呂運亨
大田刑務所에서 그물을 뜨고잇든 呂運亨은 돌연히 형기 4개월을 앞두고 가출옥이 되엇다. 면회 갓든 가족도 의외의 소식에 놀래엇다 한다. 출옥한 呂는 목하 京城 嘉會洞 그의 季씨댁에 기류하고 잇는데 옥고로 인함인지 백발이 드문드문 햇지마는 그 광채 잇는 눈과 다부진 음성만은 여전하고 가이젤 수염은 인제부터 다시 길러야 될 모양이다. 呂로 말하면 己未年 당시에 上海에 잇다가 당시 육군대신 田中義一과 척무장관 古賀廉造의 초청으로 東京으로 건너가 X빈 대우로 朝野名士와 맛나보앗다 하야 일약 명성을 날린 사람이다.
당시의 이아기를 물으면 原敬 총리대신이 조선에 자치를 줄 터이니 呂가 京城으로 건너와 33인과 회의를 하고 자치준비운동을 해보라고 교섭이 잇엇다고 한다. 물론 그는 이것을 단호 거절하고 上海로 다시 갓다가 재작년에 囹圄의 몸이 되어 복역 중에 잇든 것이다.

安在鴻씨와 책임심
李鍾麟 씨의 평을 들으면 民世 安在鴻 씨는 책임감이 매우 깊은 사람이라 한다. 그것은 무엇을 보고 그러시는 것인지 두어두고 安씨는 출옥 이후 가르되 「조선일보」을 원상회복시길 책임이 나에게 잇다」고 책임심을 발표하엿다.
「그러나 내부관계만도 삼각이니 수습이 용이치 않다고」 한탄하드라고. 그리고 원상회복이 되면 자기는 단연 田園에 歸臥할 심산이라고 그의 감개를 吐露密하엿다 한다. ＜5＞
