Folk Song: Night Laundry
						Han Kil-lim

The Han River has so much water, its two banks overflow,
But in poor Seoul life, why is even water precious?
*Ttodak ttodak swiwich’a hewich’a!*
Life is hard, this night laundry!

Chongno's crossroads are bright as midday with electric lights,
But this alley, this stream, is dark without even the moon.
*Ttodak ttodak swiwich’a hewich’a.*
Ah, how pitiful, this night laundry!

I must quickly wash these clothes, dry them all this evening,
Tomorrow morning, wearing these clothes, he goes out to earn a living.
*Ttodak ttodak swiwich’a hewich’a!*
Sleepiness comes too, this night laundry! <96>

**Summary**

This poem, written in the style of a folk song, depicts the laborious life of a poor woman in 1930s Seoul who must do laundry late at night by a dark stream. It contrasts the abundant water of the Han River with the difficulty of accessing water for the poor, and the bright electric lights of the main street (Chongno) with the darkness of the back alleys where she works. The repetitive sounds of washing clothes underscore the toil, driven by the necessity for her husband ("he") to have clean clothes for work the next day. For historians, the poem vividly illustrates the harsh realities of urban poverty during the colonial period, the stark contrasts between modernization and the lives of the marginalized, and the gendered nature of domestic labor under economic hardship.

민요 밥빨래
						한길님
한강엔 물도 많어 두 언덕이 철넝이네
가난한 서울사리 물도 웨이 귀하다나
또닥또닥 쉬위차 헤위차!
살기도 어려워이 밤빨내질이야!
종로에 네거리는 전등불이 한낮같다.
이골목 이 개천은 달도 없이 어둡고나.
또닥또닥 쉬위차 헤위차.
아쉽기도 하다 밤빨래질이야
이 빨래 얼는 빨어 움´ 저녁에 다말려야
낼 아츰 이옷입고 그이 버리 나간다네
또닥또닥 쉬위차 헤위차
잠도 나오누나 밤빨내질이야!＜96＞
