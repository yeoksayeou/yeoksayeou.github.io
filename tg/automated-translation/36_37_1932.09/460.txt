Summer Rain
						Arang (啞娘)

When the pleasant summer rain
Dances down upon the flowerbed
I wake from a dream
A refreshing dream
Like the soft sound heard, *sabak sabak*,
When slowly walking across a pearl desert, a beautiful dream
When the sound of rain striking the iron sheet
Reminds me of that beautiful music
Made as I left my footprints
Upon the desert pearls
Like slowly passing through the piled pearls
I go under the falling, falling summer rain.

As the relentlessly falling raindrops
Give a strange sensation
To my disheveled hair
To my heavy brow
To my deep heart, and eternally wander
The meditating luster of the rain
On a summer day
Upon all the endlessly hazy colors
Folds its wings and sleeps. <96>

**Summary**

This poem by Arang describes the experience of being woken by summer rain. The sound evokes a recent dream of walking on a pearl desert, creating a synesthetic link between the rain and the dream's texture. The speaker then immerses themselves in the rain, feeling its touch physically and emotionally, leading to a state of hazy meditation where the rain itself seems to settle into sleep upon the muted colors of the summer day. From a historical perspective, the poem offers a glimpse into lyrical expression during the colonial period. Its focus on sensory experience, introspection, and finding a deep connection with nature (the rain) might reflect a common tendency among intellectuals and artists of the time to turn towards personal feeling and the natural world as spaces relatively untouched by the pressures and restrictions of colonial rule. The melancholic yet serene tone could be indicative of the complex emotional landscape of the era.

여름비
						啞娘
즐거운 여름비가
화단에 춤추며 나릴 때
나는 꿈에서 깬다
쌋듯한 꿈
진주사막을 천천히 걸어갈 때
사박사박 들리는 소레와도 같은 고흔 꿈을
철판을 뚜다리는 비ㅅ소레가
사막의 진주 우에
내가 발자최를 남기며 지은
그 고웁든 음악을 연상시킬 때
나는 쌓인 진주 사이를 천천히 지나듯이
나리고 또 나리는, 여름비 아레로 간다.
쉬지 않고 나리는 빗방울이
나의 흐터진 머리에
나의 무거운 이마에
나의 깊은 심장에
이상한 감각을 주며 영원히 헤메일 때
묵상에 잠긴 비의 윤광은
여름날
한없이 몽롱한 모든 색채 우에
나레를 졉고 잠든다. ＜96＞
