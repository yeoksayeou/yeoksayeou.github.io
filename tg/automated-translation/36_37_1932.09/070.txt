**Body Text Translation:**
An Ch’angho and Lawyers
It was reported in the newspapers that Tosan An Ch’angho (島山 安昌浩), currently detained in Sŏdaemun Prison, asked his relatives not to hire a lawyer for him. When Kyŏngsŏng lawyer Kim Pyŏngno first visited him in prison and offered his services as counsel, An declined as previously stated. Later, when Yi In met with him and brought up the issue of legal representation, he still refused. Yi In then suggested that even if formal court defense was declined, someone should at least handle the procedural paperwork. An finally agreed to allow Yi to submit the notice of appointment of counsel (辯護屆) on the condition that he would not engage in courtroom arguments.

Wŏn Seh’un’s Activities
Mr. Wŏn Seh’un, who was involved in the movement abroad for many years before being caught by the law in Peking (Beijing) and serving time in Sinŭiju Prison, recently settled (卜居) in Kyŏngsŏng. He brought his elderly mother to live with him and was practicing filial piety for the first time in his life. A short while ago, he traveled to inspect the Manchurian region and has also been nominated as the chairman of the newly formed writers' association. Mr. Wŏn, who has published authoritative essays in various places under the pen name "Ch'ungok" (春谷), will certainly be adequate as chairman, although, being a resourceful man, the position might not fully satisfy him.
After returning from Manchuria, Mr. Wŏn concluded, "The Koreans in Manchuria are all going to die," his eyes glistening with tears of worldly concern (憂世).
He currently resides in a village 30 *li* outside the East Gate (Tongdaemun) and occasionally comes into Kyŏngsŏng.

Kim Ŏk Compiling an Encyclopedia Entry
An Sŏ Kim Ŏk (岸曙 金億) recently received a request from the Universal Esperanto Association in Hungary to compile an entry about Korea for an encyclopedia they are preparing. He is currently visiting authorities in this field (斯界) to request their contributions. His plan is that once they write their parts in Korean, An Sŏ will translate them into Esperanto before sending them off. <7>

**Summary**

This article presents brief news updates on three prominent Korean figures in 1932. An Ch'angho, imprisoned, reluctantly agrees to procedural legal assistance but refuses courtroom defense. Wŏn Seh'un, recently released from prison after activism abroad, settles in Seoul, becomes chairman of a writers' group, and expresses deep pessimism about the fate of Koreans in Manchuria after a visit. Kim Ŏk is undertaking the task of compiling and translating information about Korea into Esperanto for an international encyclopedia. For historians, the article offers glimpses into the legal strategies of detained independence activists, the post-prison lives and continued engagement of former activists, the grim assessment of the Korean situation in Manchuria during a period of intense turmoil, and early 20th-century Korean efforts to engage internationally through cultural projects like Esperanto.

新聞舊聞
安昌浩와 변호사
西大門형무소에 수용된 島山 安昌浩가 변호사를 대지 말라고 그 친족에게 부탁햇다는 것은 신문에도 발표되엇거니와 京城 변호사 金炳魯씨가 처음으로 옥중에서 면회하고 변호를 자청햇드니 전언대로 사절, 그 후에 다시 李仁 씨가 면회하고 변호문제를 꺼내니까 여전 사절하는 것을 그러면 법정변호만은 그만두고 수속상 처리만이라도 맡은 사람이 잇어야 한다하야 법정변론을 아니할 조건부로 辯護屆를 맡는데 성공햇다.

元世勳 씨의 動靜
다년 해외에서 운동에 종사햇다가 北京서 법망에 걸려 新義州監獄에서 복역하고 출옥한 元世勳씨는 저간 京城에 卜居하고 그 노모친을 모시어다가 난생 처음 효도를 연습 중이시더니, 요 얼마 전에는 滿洲방면 시찰을 다니어 오시고 또 새로 생긴 문필가협회의 위원장으로 추대를 받앗다. 「春谷」이란 호로 당당한 논문을 각지에 게재하여온 氏는 위원장으로 부족은 없을 것이다. 물론 기지가 잇는 氏로서는 그 자리가 도리어 맘에 안드실 것이나.
氏는 滿洲를 갓다 오시더니 결론을 「滿洲 잇는 조선사람은 다 죽겟소」 하고 나리시고 그 憂世의 눈에 눈물이 번득인다.
목하 동대문외 30리에 촌거하시면서 가끔 京城으로 들어오신다.

金億군의 백과사전 집필
岸曙 金億군은 최근 헝가리에 잇는 만국에스페란트협회로부터 백과사전을 편찬하는데 조선에 관한 것을 집필해 달라는 요구가 잇어서 斯界의 권위자들의 문을 두다려 집필을 의뢰 중인데 그이들이 조선문으로 써주면 이것을 岸曙가 다시 에스페란트로 번역해서 보낼 작정이라고 한다.＜7＞
