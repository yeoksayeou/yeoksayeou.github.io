Magazine Title: 동광 제37호
Publication Date: 1932년 09월 01일
Article Title: 無言에 돌아간 그대여, 弔哭하는 이가 잇든구나- (O, you who returned in silence, there is one who mourns you—)
Author: Unknown
Article Type: 시 (Poem)

O, you who returned in silence, there is one who mourns you—
Within the walls stained with father’s blood and soaked with mother’s tears, after the coffin of death has departed,
To greet yet another death, a chilling scream, as if to seize the enemy’s throat, fiercely rings out.
Those who will bury their lives in the vicious jaws of death
May come as Pok Man-bin, or across the Hwanghae Sea.
The life that entered within the walls is revealed through death,
And death causes a tomb to rise upon the earth.
Even to the crying bird gliding across the sky,
Only a sorrowful eulogy, sunk in silence, is offered.
Unknown to hometown, to parents, even to wife and children,
You went to your death, embracing a searing sorrow in your heart.
If only the tears overflowing the nine-fold gut and surging in the liver would not remain in this world full of suffering.

But there is one who commemorates your death.
In that heart, torn by the flame of your pure passion,
In that head, pledged with red blood to your selfless spirit,
In those arms and legs, along with thick pulses, resolute tears are formed.

Then, O you who returned to silence, you who vanished into muteness (緘口),
Do not grieve that the life received under the shield of salvation, weeping and laughing at the receding shore of life in your living days, could not be completed.
Do not lament that the life bound by the command of creation, clinging to and crying out for a life driven by pure passion, could not be fulfilled. (Rest omitted - original text) <97>

**Summary**

This elegy mourns an individual who died silently, possibly as a martyr or victim under oppressive circumstances hinted at by mentions of "enemy" and sacrifice. The poem contrasts the deceased's unknown, silent passing with the fervent promise of remembrance and continued struggle by those left behind, who are inspired by the departed's "pure passion" and "selfless spirit." From a historical perspective, the poem is significant for its portrayal of grief, sacrifice, and suppressed resistance during the Japanese colonial period (1910-1945). The imagery of blood, tears, enemies, and silent, unacknowledged death reflects the harsh realities faced by Koreans, suggesting potential anti-colonial sentiment expressed through coded, mournful language. Historians might find the poem useful for understanding the emotional and psychological landscape of Koreans under colonial rule, particularly the way loss and resistance were articulated in literature.

기사제목	無言에 돌아간 그대여, 弔哭하는 이가 잇든구나-
기사형태	시

無言에 돌아간 그대여, 弔哭하는 이가 잇든구나-
아버지 피로 물들고 어머니 눈물로 저진 담우에는 죽음의 관이 떠난 다음 또 하나의 죽엄을 맞기 위하야
원수의 숨통을 가로삼킬 듯이 모저락서리운 비명을 떨치고 잇나니
죽음의 악살스러운 입아구니에 목숨을 장사지낼 사람은
복만비니 또는 황해바다를 넘어서 오리다
담우안으로 들어간 목숨은 죽음으로 밝기고
죽음은 땅우에 무덤을 쏘게 할재
하늘 우로 미끄러저가는 우름짓는 세에도
무언에 잠긴 구슬픈 조사를 올리기만
고향도 부모도 모르게 심지어 처자도 모르개
쓸아리는 애사를 가삼에 품은 채 죽어가시니
구곡에 넘치고 간장에 용소슴치는 눈물만이라도 괴롬많흔 세상에는 남구지 않으람이다.

그러치만 그대의 죽음을 조상하는 이가 잇노라
그 가슴에는 그대의 순정에 불꽃으로 찌저짓고
그의 머리에는그대의 無我한 정령에 붉은 피로 맹세햇나니
그 팔과 다리에는 굵은 맥박과 아울러 굳셋 눈물을 짓는 것이다.

그러면 무엇에 돌아간 그대여 緘口에 사라진 그대여
생전에 기우러저가는 생명의 포구에 울고 웃다가
구조의 방패 받은 생명을 다 못한 것을 구슯퍼하지 말라
순정에 잇기센 생명을 부어잡고 소리치다가
창조의 명령바친 명을 못이은 것을 애닯허하지 말라.(하략-원문) ＜97＞
