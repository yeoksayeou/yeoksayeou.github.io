남은 말슴 (Concluding Remarks)

Having finished the September issue, we feel refreshed. By the time the printing is done and it reaches your hands, the lingering summer heat (殘暑) will probably not be so severe. We hope you are all well despite the heat.

Whether existing magazines or newly launched ones, the Chosŏn magazine world is currently in a state of great flourishing. And among them, one trend is that of aiming to be "popular" (『大衆的』).

The trend towards being "popular" (『大衆的』) can easily come to mean low-brow taste. Our magazine alone intends to swim against the surging current of the times (滔滔한 世流) and firmly maintain the standard of the cultural front (문화전선).

This month's editing has also been done according to this standard. We only hope that those comrades who understand this point and share this intention will become our readers.

Therefore, we do not intend to pay heed (掛齒코저 아니합니다) to criticisms such as *Donggwang* being too staid, or *Donggwang* having too few pages. We believe the mission entrusted to *Donggwang* is to maintain the standard first established until the very end, even if it eventually becomes only ten pages.

However, this is not to say that we entirely disregard plainness and amusement. If you look at the editing of this issue, you will be able to see the traces of how we tried to arouse interest and how we tried to make things plain and easy to understand.

Please support us to the end. Please promote us widely.

Donggwangsa Editorial Department, signing off <128>

**Summary**

This editorial postscript from the *Donggwang* magazine's September 1932 issue expresses the editors' relief at completing the issue and addresses the contemporary Korean publishing scene. They note the proliferation of magazines and a dominant trend towards "popularization" (大衆的), which they fear leads to lowered standards. *Donggwang* explicitly positions itself against this trend, vowing to uphold a high "cultural standard" even at the cost of readership or page count, appealing only to readers who share their intellectual commitment. While asserting this high-minded mission, they also reassure readers that they haven't entirely ignored accessibility and reader interest, pointing to efforts in the current issue. They conclude with a plea for continued support and promotion. For historians, this piece is interesting as it clearly articulates the magazine's self-perceived role and identity within the competitive colonial media landscape. It highlights the tension between mass appeal and intellectual rigor ("cultural front") debated among Korean intellectuals under Japanese rule, reflecting concerns about cultural direction and quality in a constrained environment.

기사형태	사고·편집후기

남은 말슴
9월호를 끝내고 나니 새풍이 나는 듯 합니다. 인쇄가 다 되어 여러분 손에 갈 때는 아마 殘暑도 그리 심하지는 않겟지오. 여러분 더위에 만안하시옵니까.
본래 잇는 잡지, 새로 나는 잡지, 지금 조선 잡지계는 대번창 상태입니다. 그리고 그중에 한가지 경향은 『대중적으로』라는 一경향입니다.
『大衆的』으로라는 경향은 자칫하면 저급 취미를 의미하게 되기 쉽습니다. 본지만은 滔滔한 世流에 역류하야 문화전선의 수준을 고수코저 합니다.
이달의 편집도 이 수준에서 되어 잇습니다. 오직 이 점을 이해하고 이 뜻을 같이 하는 동지들께만 독자가 되어 주기를 바랍니다.
그러므로 동광은 너무 점잔타든가, 동광은 페지 수가 적다든가, 그런 비평을 우리는 掛齒코저 아니합니다. 동광의 가진 사명은 나종에 단 열페지가 되드라도 처음 세운 수준을 끝까지 유지함이라고 생각합니다.
그러타고 평명과 취미를 전혀 돌보지 않음이 아니외다. 이번 호의 편집을 소시면 어떠케 흥미를 이르키며 어떠케 평이화 하려한 자취를 아실 수 잇을 것입니다.
여러분 끝까지 지지하십시오. 넓리 선전해 주십시오.
