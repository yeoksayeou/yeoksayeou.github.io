Answers to the Monthly General Knowledge Quiz
(The questions are on page 3.)

1. Ibsen.

2. Collusion among bidders in an auction to predetermine the bidding price and avoid competition.

3. 218.

4. Kyŏngsŏng, P'yŏngyang, Pusan.

5. Ottawa city, Canada.

6. Abbreviation for the National Socialist German Workers' Party (國粹社會主義 獨逸勞働黨), led by Hitler.

7. The theory of relativity.

8. Dancer.

9. Plant.

10. Meaning 'modern'. <31>

**Summary**

This short piece provides the answers to a monthly general knowledge quiz published in the magazine "Tonggwang". The answers cover a diverse range of topics including European literature (Ibsen), economic terms (bid rigging), specific numbers (likely related to a previous question), Korean geography (major cities), international geography and politics (Ottawa, Canada), European politics (Hitler's Nazi party), science (theory of relativity), arts (dancer), biology (plant), and vocabulary (modern). For historians, this list offers a glimpse into the kind of general knowledge considered relevant or expected among the Korean readership of this magazine in 1932. It reflects an awareness of international figures, political movements, scientific concepts, and major Korean urban centers during the colonial period.
