Great Exploration of the Secrets of Married Life
						Green Spectacles

Until a Woman Writer Divorces

There was a virtuous woman who graduated from a church girls' school, studied in the United States, returned, became the wife of a wonderful gentleman (who was a teacher at a private vocational school), created a happy and peaceful family, and lived pleasantly in a Western-style household. She went to church without fail, so she had some young male acquaintances, and because it was a Western-style household, young men visited more than a few times, but she never felt any emotional turmoil and always greeted and respected her husband, who went to school by rickshaw every day, and lived peacefully for six years. But for some reason, she suddenly abandoned her husband and even got a divorce, and then moved from place to place seeking young men at will, and as much as she and her family were famous, the rumors spread loudly and remain a source of suspicious gossip to this day.

"The teacher who was her husband has an incomparable personality and temperament, but unfortunately, he has a rubber leg, so how could a young woman stand it?"

Everyone attributes the reason for the divorce to the husband's rubber leg in a single sentence, but the reality was not just that.

When she graduated from school and immediately got engaged, she knew that her husband's leg was disabled, and she also knew that his first young wife had run away because of that leg, so she had lived truly respecting him for six long years. She associated with many modern boys (modern-boys) who attended church, but she remained devoted to her husband without any change of heart and lived without any complaints.

However, the beginning of that happy life being eaten away was when a young man from the church she associated with <98> encouraged her to launch a women's magazine and put her in charge as the editor. What the literary youth of the church were thinking, I don't know, but they gathered four or five graduates from Owha Academy (Owha hakdang) to start a women's magazine, and they thought it would be convenient to use it as an office and that they would have a lot of free time, but it was something they started without figuring out the printing costs, so they put her in charge as the editor because they also wanted to figure out how to pay for the printing costs. So the first issue of the magazine was quickly sold out, letters from readers poured in every day, and preparations for the second issue were busy, and now her once leisurely life suddenly became more complicated and busy than her husband's simple teaching life.

Until then, she didn't even know that she had any talent or interests of her own, and she just thought that it would be enough to stay at home and take care of her husband, so she heard news from the outside world through her husband's mouth, and she followed her husband's opinion and obeyed him in matters of the household, but from then on, she heard news from the outside world sooner than her husband, and she had to make household decisions based on her own opinion and circumstances. And on the other hand, the young men she met at church and the people on the street had just seen her as someone's wife and treated her ordinarily, but from now on, they saw her not as someone's wife but as Ms. So-and-so, who publishes So-and-so magazine, so they naturally looked at her twice or three times, and men who hadn't visited before gradually started to visit. As things turned out, instead of cleaning the house, setting the table, and waiting with bated breath for her husband to return from school, she impatiently waited for a man to come and discuss magazine matters with her because of her busy work. When she heard a sound at the door or footsteps, she was happy and ran out thinking it was a man who would help her with the magazine, but when she saw that it wasn't him but her husband, she even showed a slight hint of disappointment. Instead of saying, "Why are you so late today? The side dishes I made are all cold," she said, "Oh, are you so hungry? I was so busy that I didn't eat lunch and I'm still working, so please wait a moment. I'll finish writing this manuscript and then I'll set the table." Her husband, a kind middle-aged gentleman, was happier than anything else to hear his wife's name become more famous day by day and to hear praise for his wife from everyone he met everywhere he went. But the attitude and warm affection towards him were definitely not the same as before and were gradually turning cold, which was a sorrow and worry he couldn't complain to anyone about. He had not only agreed to the start of the magazine but had even approved of it, but it was a painful thing to bear that his young wife's heart was moving away from him even a little because of that work. As much as he had personality, well-rounded thoughts, and understanding, he didn't show it at first, but as the days went by and his wife's business grew and became busier, he felt more and more hurt <99> and eventually said, "Wouldn't it be better to entrust the complicated work to someone else if possible?" and "Since it's so complicated, shouldn't we think about declining visits from men who come without any special business?" But doing so only made his wife's heart, which was increasingly absorbed in her work, move further away. Gradually, she began to think of her husband as interfering in her work, and she came to know her husband as someone who didn't understand her work, like someone from a distant village. Then, due to various circumstances, the magazine was discontinued, and the complicated work disappeared, but her heart, which had moved away from her husband, did not return to its original place. She became determined to succeed as a female writer, and as a result, she gained more interest and benefit from conversations with young literary men than from conversations with her husband. As things went on like that, she came to think that her husband and she were not fit as a spouse, and she often dreamed of taking walks with young literary men. She hadn't yet made another man or found a clear lover, but at this time, the happiness of that peaceful family was already broken beyond repair. His wife dreamed different dreams, and her husband couldn't shake off his uncomfortable and lonely heart, so he lost interest in everything and couldn't put his energy into teaching, and naturally, the reputation among the students gradually began to worsen. The kind, unfortunate middle-aged professor, after much agonizing over trying to write a great life story, suggested that he and his wife leave Joseon (Joseon) and go to the United States again to live relying on each other alone. But his wife's heart still didn't turn back, so she said, "I can't speak English and how can I endure it if I go to America first? If you go first, I'll go all the way to Japan and study English for about a year and then follow you to America." The kind husband believed her and went to Japan with her, saw her into her dormitory, and then went to America alone.

In the meantime, the husband heard rumors that his wife was in love with a young writer and spending time with him at a hotel, so he crossed the Pacific Ocean to find his wife, but inevitably ended up divorcing, and the husband quietly went down to the countryside with another wife, and the wife couldn't find a place to settle down and just kept moving around.

The husband is a pitiful person, and the wife is a pitiful person who has a valuable talent but walks the path of a harsh fate. It is not for us to say here who is right and who is wrong, but only to write as it is that there was a couple in our neighborhood who walked such a path. <100>

Ripple Between Foreign-Born Couples

There was a young new family who had finished studying in Japan, married a woman they had been engaged to in Tokyo, lived in a clean nine-room tiled house, bought a pump organ, bought a phonograph, installed a radio, and even built a bathtub in the shed, and who came home from work at the Guri (Guri) company in the evening and were too busy having fun to visit friends. The woman had studied music a little and art a little, but she had stopped when she got married. The man had enough property to live on, but there was nothing to do in Seoul, and it was uncomfortable to find a good job, so he ended up working for a large company that he hadn't expected, but because the couple was very modern, they never slacked off in their efforts to avoid feeling bored with their life and to deepen their affection for each other. They were diligently trying not to fall into the "ennui of married life" that everyone falls into sooner or later, which was actually a good thing and something everyone should do. The husband never forgot to come home with a story to tell at the dinner table, and the wife also prepared a story to amuse her husband with different side dishes for dinner. And when there wasn't enough to talk about or when it ended quickly, they put a phonograph next to the dinner table and brought up music stories while listening to it. And they listened to the radio, and when they lay down in bed, they made a promise to each tell a story. Short old stories about couples, of course, and stories about couples in domestic and foreign novels were good material. In this way, they had new fun every day, and new affection sprang up every day like a fountain.

But after a summer and then a winter passed, there was nothing they could do. The material for the stories was completely depleted, and even if there was any, it didn't taste as sweet as it used to, and only boring and dull thoughts came out of the wife's mouth as she stayed at home. For some reason, the "baby" that should have been didn't come, and the simple life dragged on, so as much as the beginning of their life was unusually fun, the complaints of boredom now were unusually deep. Because she was bored, the woman began to read novels. The man also thought that it was better to read novels than to just sit still and have useless fantasies when she felt bored at home while he was at work, so he sometimes went to Jingogae (Jingogae) and picked out novels for her. And every night, he lay in bed and listened to stories about the novels she read. And they never forgot to go to the movies two or three times a week. And when they came back, the wife <101> became the heroine of the movie and the husband became her lover, and it became a habit to imitate the movie for a while before going to bed. And they didn't hide this from their friends when they came to visit. Every time, the wife would say without embarrassment, "But does this actor (pointing to her husband) know how to exercise? Can he row a boat? No matter how good the actress is, it's boring because the partner doesn't match."

Having her read novels to avoid fantasies and showing her movies to avoid boredom unfortunately had the opposite effect. The more she read novels with twists and turns, the more she disliked her ordinary and monotonous life. So sometimes she cried like the heroines in the novels she read, and when she cried, she dreamed of a strange man coming and holding her shoulders and comforting her. Sometimes, as she had seen in the movies, she dreamed that she was tricked by a bad guy and dragged all the way to Jeju Island (Jeju-do), unable to escape or resist, and that a man who she didn't even know was her lover came and defeated two or three of the villains with his fists and carried her in his arms and put her on a boat and brought her back. But when she woke up from the dream and looked at her husband lying next to her and looked at his arms, no matter how much she looked, he wasn't the good man she saw in her dream, and his arms weren't strong and reliable like that. As this happened often, she became a person who dreamed of novels and dreamed of movies as they were.

When her husband came home from work and asked, "Did you get any letters today?" she would reply, "Yes." "From where?" "From my old lover, where else?" "Did you even have an old lover?" "Well, he came a few days ago," she would sometimes say even when friends were around. She said it with a smile, but anyone with a sense of humor could guess how far the woman's fantasy life had gone.

The thought that she wanted to live a life with twists and turns, or even with ups and downs, was a thought that was unknowingly buried in the woman's heart. Then, two men newly came to visit her house, one of whom was an amateur theater enthusiast who had studied theater in Japan and had even studied jujutsu, so he had strong arms, and his words were all theatrical, which was strange and funny, and his walk was like that of an actor, and he sometimes talked about how he had "beaten up the guys who were harassing women all by himself," which was quite exhilarating. Every time she heard such stories, the woman remembered the time when she and her husband had gone to the Han River (Han-gang) railroad bridge before they got married and had heard the lewd words of bad guys until her face turned red and had returned as if being chased away without being able to say a word, and she saw again the face of her husband, who had said, "It's embarrassing, let's walk a little faster."

After that, when her friends had left, the wife referred to the theater enthusiast as a "slovenly, dirty man" when talking to her husband. But even so, <102> when it came time to go to the theater, she couldn't help but say, "Oh, it would be fun if he came with us," and when her friends came to visit, she would say, "Why isn't he coming today?"

After that, the husband began to speak ill of the theater enthusiast even in other settings. Everyone who went to that house had the thought that "something turbulent will happen soon," and they watched with a mixture of anxiety and interest.

But according to what a friend who recently visited said, the precious scenery of that house was broken. When asked why, the wife said nothing and only said, "I don't know, he came home drunk and broke it like that last night."

It is not a good thing for a young woman to just read novels and sit around doing nothing, so a few friends are now suggesting that the man either change his job or that the woman also get a job, or that they fundamentally change direction in order to escape the ennui...

Divorced and Living Together Again....

While the son of a wealthy family was studying in Tokyo, his parents-in-law discussed with him to allow his daughter-in-law to attend school. Because everyone who studied in Japan came back and complained that their wives were uneducated and talked about divorce, they had their young daughter-in-law (who had graduated from elementary school before) attend school in advance. But no matter how much they sent her to school, he couldn't start a new romance with his wife, whom he had known since childhood, and once his heart had left, it couldn't return, and even more than that, he found a better match that suited his reason, so there was nothing he could do. So, taking the opportunity of her finishing a girls' high school in Gyeongseong (Gyeongseong) and coming to Tokyo to study with her husband, the two sat down and agreed to divorce. The woman had also finished high school, so she didn't say, "I'll die and be a ghost in your house" and drag on, but said in a tone of "If you really don't like me, how can I live?" So her friends even envied her situation, saying that it would be great if they could get a divorce by mutual agreement because the woman was so understanding.

However, one of the man's friends, an humanitarian who admired Tolstoy and Arishima Takeo (Arishima Takeo) and an artist who studied stage art, sat next to the couple who had sat down to divorce and opposed their divorce.

First, the wife had finished girls' high school and even came to study in Tokyo <103>, so she had no shortcomings as a modern wife.

Second, even if you get married to a woman who is blown away by love or something, can you be sure that that marriage will be happy? He gave various reasons and conditions and earnestly advised them not to divorce in a reasonable and understanding manner. It wasn't like a stubborn old man with no understanding or a flattering attitude in favor of his in-laws, but the reasonable advice of a friend with the deepest understanding and the newest ideas sounded plausible, so the wife, who had wanted it from the beginning, but the man also abandoned his intention to divorce and took his wife's hand again firmly and bowed his head in thanks to his friend. And they combined the two separate boarding houses they had arranged into one. For the first four or five days, like a couple who had fought and reconciled and lay in the same bed, they had things to talk about and there was some interest, but after ten days and into the fifteenth day, they returned to their old feelings. The man tried to hold on to his heart, which was running away from his wife, saying, "This won't do," but the more he tried, the faster his heart ran away. The thought that he was keeping a woman he couldn't help with forced sympathy came first, and the more he looked at her, the more foolish and uneducated she seemed. The wife also always felt that her husband's heart was not with her, so she couldn't bring up anything to discuss or lean on or trust him. On the other hand, the person who truly sympathized with and understood her was the artist who had reconciled them. Her husband was someone who disliked and didn't care for her, and that man knew the situation well and was the most well-rounded and naturally trustworthy among the modern youth. All of the husband's friends who came to visit were on the husband's side, which was a different world from her, and only that artist seemed like her husband.

Less than two months later, the husband declared that he would divorce. The wife left for a trip to the hot springs with the artist's wife.

But after that, the man married another woman, but the artist was not a long-time sympathizer and left on his own. It was later heard that she had married another good man somewhere.

A Shadow That the Wife Can't Forget

There is a neat and gentle woman named P who graduated from Omyung (Omyung) Girls' School, completed the teacher training course, and was a female teacher at a regular school near Seoul, and is now a nanny at a kindergarten. She had been admiring and loving a young man named S, who was a rare and trustworthy person among young men, since her student days until the beginning of her career as a female teacher, <104> but he seemed to be a bit difficult and lacked the ability to be friendly and fun.

Then, a young man named K, who had been to Shanghai and graduated from Tokyo and worked for a newspaper company, appeared and proposed to her with various good conditions, so P married K. Just a few months after the marriage, K quit the newspaper company and started working for a newly established company, receiving a better salary than at the newspaper company, and the woman quit being a teacher and started working as a kindergarten nanny, so the couple's income became considerable.

However, in less than two years, K became like a man who had forgotten his wife, frequenting courtesans, drinking alcohol, and exchanging letters with other women at the church, so that they were almost divorced for a new family.

The story that the woman named P confessed to her close friend is quite worthy of attention.

She married K in the first place because she was deceived by lies, and also because she thought that he would be more cheerful and fun than Mr. S, so she thought that such a person would be good for married life, but once she got married, she realized that he was so giddy and like a child that she couldn't rely on him for the rest of her life, and she couldn't even trust him for a moment, so she immediately regretted it and couldn't settle down. She whipped her own heart, saying, "This won't do," but K's insincere and frivolous behavior grew day by day, and if she looked at him silently, he looked exactly like a clueless child playing around, so she couldn't dream of respecting him as a husband and lived like soothing a child, but it would have been better if she had just stayed that way, but the more frivolous her husband's words and actions became, the smaller and unforgettable, and S appeared in her eyes. "If I hadn't married Mr. K and married Mr. S, what would it have been like?" When she was alone in her room, S's silent face appeared in her eyes, and when she was at home watching K's giddy behavior, S's face suddenly appeared, and when she was lying alone in the middle of the night, and even when K was lying next to her, it suddenly looked like S, she said. He has since married another woman and had a plump baby, and there were times when she even thought that she might have given birth to that baby if she had married him. And when there was a difficult problem between the couple, at home, or among relatives, she felt so hopeless and frustrated that she sat next to K, who was scratching his head and fidgeting, saying, "What should I do? What should I do?" and thought, "If it were Mr. S, he would have come up with a way to handle this calmly and smoothly without any worries..." "If it were Mr. S" "If it were Mr. S," she regretted it and often cried alone in the end. Unlike when they went for a walk or trip and played around for a while, she regretted so much that she wanted to pinch and tear her own heart when she thought that she had chosen the wrong husband to live a difficult life in this difficult world. So she doesn't feel precious or want to respect her husband, <105> so how can she serve K with joy coming from her heart? She says that when she sits at the table or lies in bed, she only thinks about what it would be like if this were Mr. S instead of K, so how can she force herself to have good feelings? She knows it's wrong and that she shouldn't do that, but no matter how hard she tries, her feelings don't get better, and therefore she doesn't make him happy and affectionate.

So naturally K feels dissatisfied with his wife, can't find interest in family life, and can't see any fun, so since he's already giddy, he can't stand it for even a moment and ends up going to courtesan houses and churches, and it seems reasonable that he does so. She knows that it is her sin to be someone else's wife and not serve him or comfort him well and make him seek fun outside the house, but she says that she doesn't say the words "let's break up" first and just leaves it as it is and waits for it to be resolved on its own.

A New Wedding Every Week

Now that we've only talked about unhappy families, I'll introduce an extremely peaceful and happy married life and stop there.

At the end of an alley in Bukchon (Bukchon), Seoul, there is a clean nine-room thatched house built in a semi-Japanese style, and a tall, white-haired gentleman in a suit with a hisashikami hairstyle that was popular ten years ago but has disappeared, accompanied by a lady in shoes, always walking together, who is the talk of the neighborhood and a neat old gentleman Mr. K whom those who know him assume.

He is over 60 years old, and the old woman must also be 60 years old, but they know the secret to getting along as well as they did when they were young and have been living without any uncomfortable times or any feelings of ennui, which is that they have been living in separate rooms and working on different tasks throughout their lives, but they have been sleeping in the same bedroom one day a week, and on that day, they especially *stop working and take a bath, get a haircut, put on powder, and comb their hair with the same feeling as a bride and groom going to a wedding, and then come home and have a fun conversation while eating dinner specially prepared more than usual like at a feast (even if it's simple, specially prepared), eat it late, clean the room cleanly, and in winter put up a folding screen and in summer put up a bamboo blind, decorating the room like on the wedding day, and go to bed early, and they have been doing this without fail until now, when they are over 60 years old. In this way, they are always living a new married life and always trying not to forget the feelings of their honeymoon, so they have come to this day without knowing much ennui.

"Always recall the honeymoon!" is what everyone tells every couple. "Visit the places you traveled to during the courtship period before the wedding and stay at the hotel you stayed at then" is a clever way to avoid the ennui of married life, so in this regard, the old couple has done a very good thing. <106>

Various Ups and Downs in Married Life

Mr. Jang (Chang), who had been in England for a long time, was very worried about how to reorganize his family when he returned home while on his way back. He thought that in order to reorganize his family, he should first reform his wife, so he thought that the order would be divorce and then welcoming a new wife, but that was the act of a frivolous person, so he thought that he should not divorce but reform his old wife's brain, and on the way home, he made an effort to give his wife, who had only been in the room, a new breeze by having her comb her hair in a Western style and ride in a car with him, traveling around to hotels and resorts, but the more he did so, the more she seemed to feel suffocated, and in just three months, he brought in a courtesan as a concubine. This is not a problem because the man originally had a lot of wandering nature and didn't do it with serious consideration, but it was a fact that became a topic of conversation.

Mr. H finished O Law School (O gwa) at Waseda (Waseda) in Tokyo and came out to start a new family with a new woman and was living a fun life, but a woman named M, who was a classmate of his wife's, often came to visit and began to learn English from Mr. H. In the meantime, Mr. H's wife gave birth to a baby and became unable to go out freely, so she became increasingly suspicious of the relationship between her friend and her husband who came to learn English. So, they even had arguments about it several times. The honest man made another good excuse and declined the woman's visits, so the family peace was restored. But the wife again began to suspect that the two men and women were continuing their close relationship outside the house, so she kept her thoughts to herself and was full of jealousy and dissatisfaction. But the man had no guilt in his heart from the beginning, so he lived indifferently. But the young wife's jealousy and dissatisfaction eventually turned into resistance, and she became close to another man who didn't even frequent the house. So the couple, who had been living in arguments for four or five months, broke up. The husband took the baby and sent it to his parents' house in the countryside... It is extremely important to be careful not to cause baseless suspicion between couples.

Mr. K's wife, a young gentleman, was a good wife who had graduated from a private girls' high school even though she was old-fashioned, and had come to Mr. K's house upon marriage. But she thought, like all former wives, that the husband should be respected and that if she served her parents-in-law well, that was enough as a wife. And after having two sons and one daughter in seven years of marriage, she was so physically and mentally exhausted every day from supporting her parents above and taking care of young people below that she was unable to devote all her energy to comforting her husband's heart and body. But she never dreamed that it was wrong <107> and believed that supporting her parents well, taking care of young people well, and respectfully respecting her husband was the right way to go. But from the point of view of what her husband, a modern man, wanted from his wife, she was completely full of shortcomings. He wanted her to accept his will well in every matter and to always be by his side to caress and soothe his tired soul when he came home. He wanted her to put effort into doing her makeup for him, to sit next to him and talk to him, and to go for walks and watch plays together, and he wanted her to give him sufficient satisfaction in their sexual life. But the wife was just a respectful and polite friend, and she didn't know how to play the role of a "modern wife" beyond that.

Mr. K himself fully understood that his wife couldn't cling to him alone because she was serving her parents-in-law and had many young people to take care of. So he always thought that he was the one being understanding in demanding more.

But understanding is just understanding. The loneliness and dissatisfaction that could not be filled in his youth did not disappear with understanding and only grew larger. Still, the wife didn't know what else to do. In the meantime, the man ended up seeking the things that could not be filled by his wife from another woman, and gradually the man's heart ran away to the new woman.

Koreans, both in the past and now, were too ignorant of the way of the couple (夫婦道). Because they didn't know how to seek a more intimate fusion and didn't make efforts to increase their love for each other, they quickly sighed at the ruin of their marriage and lived in families like dead ashes even if they didn't get divorced. We should pay more attention to the issue of love between couples and the way of the couple (夫婦道) and we should also have knowledge of sexual life. <108>

女文士 離婚하기까지

일즉이 어느 교회 녀학교를 졸업한 녀자로서 미국 류학하고 도라온 (사립 전문 학교의 선생님으로 게신) 훌늉한 신사의 부인이 되야 리해 만흔 평화한 가뎡을 니루어 반양식 살림에 자미나게 자미나게 사는 부덕잇는 부인이 잇섯다. 례배당에도 빠지는 법 업시 다니엿스니 젊은 남자 교제가 업지 안이하엿고 반양식 살림이니 남편보다 젊은 청년의 래방도 그리 적지 안이하엿섯것만은 그는 조곰도 마음의 동요를 밧는 일이 업시 날마다 인력거로 학교에 다녀오는 남편을 잘 마지하야 잘 공경하면서 6년 동안이나 평화롭게 지내엿섯는대 엇전  인지 그는 돌연이 남편을 바리고 뒤미처 리혼까지 한 후에 이리로 저리로 젊은 제비를 차저 마음대로 옮아 다니게 되야 그와 그의 가뎡이 유명하엿던 만큼 소문이 요란하게 퍼저서 이날까지 의심스러운 소문 꺼리가 되어잇다.
『남편 되는 선생의 인격이나 성정이야 더할수 업시 조치만은 불행히 다리 하나가 고무다리이니 젊은 녀자로서 될 수 잇는 일이겟소?』
하고 누구던지 한마듸 말로 리혼하게 된 까닭을 남편의 고무다리에만 미러버리고 말지만은 실상은 단순히 그것뿐만이 안이엿다.

역시 일본 류학 마치고 도라와서 동경서 약혼해 둔 녀자와 결혼을 하야 아홉간짜리 깻긋한 개와집에서 살면서 풍금 사다 노코 류성긔 사다 노코 라듸오 매노코 헛간에 목욕통까지 꾸며 노코 구리개(黃金町) 회사에서 저녁때 도라와서는 친구 방문도 할 줄 모르고 자미보기에 밧분 젊은 새 가뎡이 잇섯다. 녀자는 음악 공부 조곰 미술 공부 조곰하다가 결혼하면서 그만 두어버린 이엿다. 남자는 자긔 먹을 재산은 잇지만은 서울 와서 놀고 잇슬 것도 업스나 조흔 자리는 거북한 일이 만허서 뜻하지 안이한 어느 큰 회사에 다니는 터인데 부부가 아조 샛뜻한 모던-부부인 만큼 살림에 권태(倦怠)를 늣기지 안케 하노라고 두 정분을 더욱 도탑게 하노라 하고 노력하기에 결코 게으르지 안엇다. 누구나가 조만간 모다 빠저드러가는 『결혼 생활의 권태』에 자긔 두 사람뿐 만은 빠지지 안이하려고 부즈런히 그 방면으로 노력을 하는 것이니 이것은 실상 잘하는 일이요 누구나가 그러해야 할 것이다. 남편은 집에 도라오기까지에 반듯이 밥상에 안저서 할 이약이 꺼리를 작만해 가지고 가기를 닛지 안코 안해 역시 저녁 밥상의 색다른 반찬과 함께 남편을 즐겁게 하여 줄 이약이 꺼리를 작만해 노코 기다리엿다. 그리고 이약기 꺼리가 부족하거나 속히 끗난 때에는 밥상 엽헤 류성긔를 노코 드르면서 음악 이약이를 꺼집어 내엿다. 그리고 라듸오를 듯고 자리에 누어서는 반듯이 한사람이 한가지씩 이약이를 하는 약속을 실행하엿다. 짤막한 옛날의 내외(夫婦) 이약이는 물론이요 내외국 소설에 나오는 부부의 이약이는 조흔 재료엿다. 이리하야 그들은 날이 밧귈사록 밧귀는 새로운 자미가 잇서서 날마다 새로운 정이 샘믈 솟듯 하엿다.
그러나 한녀름이 지나고 또 한겨울이 지나니 별수가 업섯다. 이약이 재료도 미천이 아조 떠러젓거니와 잇서서 듯는대야 처음가티 단맛이 업고 그저 지리하고 심심스러운 생각만이 집에 드러안저 잇는 안해의 입에서 하폄이 나오게만 하엿다. 엇전 일인지 잇슴즉한 『아기』는 잇지 안코 단순한 살림이 길다라케 느러지니 처음 생활이 남다르게 자미잇섯던 만큼 지금와서 심심스런 한탄이 남달이 깁헛다. 심심하닛가 녀자는 소설을 닑기 시작하엿다. 남자도 자긔가 회사에 가 잇는 동안 집에서 권태를 늣길 때에는 공연히 가만히 잇서서 쓸데업는 공상을 하는 것보다는 소설을 닑는 것이 조타고 가끔 진고개에 가서 소설을 골라다 주엇다. 그리고는 밤마다 자리에 누어서 소설 닑는 이약이를 안해에게 듯고 듯고 하엿다. 그리고 일주일에 두 번 세 번씩은 활동사진 보러가기를 닛지 안엇다. 그리고 도라와서는 안해는<101> 활동사진의 녀주인공이 되고 남편은 그 애인이 되어가지고 활동사진 흉내를 한동안하고 잠자리에 드는 것이 버릇이 되엿다. 그리고 이러한 일도 친구가 차저오면 숨기지 안코 이약이하엿다. 그럴 때마다 안해도 붓그럼 업시 『그러치만 이 배우는(남편을 가르치면서) 운동을 할 줄 아나요. 뽀-트를 저을 줄 아나요. 녀배우가 암만 조와도 짝이 안마즈닛가 싱거워요』이런 말을 한다.
공상을 말라고 소설을 닑히고 심심해하지 말라고 활동사진을 보이는 일이 불행히 그 반대의 성적을 나타내엿다. 곡절 만코 파란 만흔 소설을 닑으면 닑을스록 자긔의 평범하고 단조한 생활이 점점 더 실혀젓다. 그래서 각금 자긔가 닑은 소설 속의 녀주인공가티 울기도 하고 울면은 모르는 남자가 와서 억개를 집고 다정하게 달내여 주는 꿈을 꾸게 되엿다. 엇던 때는 활동사진에서 본 것처럼 자긔가 낫분 놈에게 꼬임을 바더 제주도 섬에까지 껄니여가서 도망도 못하고 발악도 못하고 잇느대 자긔도 모를 자긔의 애인이라는 남자가 쪼차와서 서너 놈의 도당을 주먹으로 물니치고 자긔를 폭 안어다가 배에 싯고 도라오는 꿈도 꾸엇다. 그러나 꿈이 깨여서 엽헤 누은 남편을 보고 그 팔둑을 보면 아모리 보아도 꿈에 보는 그런 조흔 남자도 안이요 그럿케 긔운찬 미덤즉한 팔둑이 안이엿다. 이런 일이 자조 잇스면서부터 그는 소설을 꿈꾸고 활동사진을 고대로 꿈꾸는 사람이 되엿다.
회사에서 도라온 남편이 『오늘 혹시 어데서 편지 안이왓습뎃가?』하고 무르면 『왓서-』 『어데서』 『우리 녜전 애인에게서 왓지 어데서와-』 『왼 애인이 잇기나 잇섯나』 『흥 요 몃칠 전에도 왓다가 갓는데』 이런 종업는 이약이들을 친구들이 잇는데서도 각금 하게 되엿다. 말은 우슴의 말로 하는 것이로되 그 녀자의 공상 생활이 어느 뎡도까지 간 것을 눈치 잇는 사람은 짐작하엿다.
곡절 만흔 더러 파란 잇는 생활을 해보앗스면 하는 것이 녀자의 가슴속에 자긔도 모르게 잠겨 잇는 생각이다. 그런대 새로히 그 집에 놀너오게 된 남자가 두 사람 잇는데 그 중의 한사람이 일본에서 연극 연구하던 흥미객이요 유술까지 공부한 일이 잇서 팔 긔운이 세이고 굿세인데 말하는 것이 모다 연극뎍이여서 괘사스럽고 자미잇고 거름 거리도 활동 배우 갓고 각금 어데 놀너갓다가 『녀자 히야까시한 놈들을 자긔 혼자서 두들겨 주엇다』는 이약이를 자못 통쾌하게 하고 하고 하엿다. 그런 이약이를 드를 때마다 녀자는 결혼 전에 둘이서 한강 텰교에 갓다가 낫분 놈들의 빈성거리는 소리를 얼골이 빩애지도록 듯고도 아모 말 한만듸 못하고 쫏겨오듯 도라오던 일이 생각나고 『창피하니 좀 빨니 거러갑시다』하던 남편의 얼골이 다시 처다 보엿다.
그 후로 친구들이 도라가고 업는 때 안해는 남편에게 말할 때 그 연극 연구 남자를 가르처 『조촐하지 못한 지저분한 남자』라고 하엿다. 그러나 그러면서도<102> 연극장에 가게 될 때는 『아이그 그이가 왓뜨면 가티 갓스면 자미잇슬 터인데』하는 소리가 자긔도 것 잡을 새 업시 나오고 나오고 하엿다. 그러고 친구들이 놀러가면 안해의 입에서 『그이는 왜 오늘 안 오세요』하는 소리를 하게 되엿다.
그러는 후로부터 다른 좌석에서도 그 남편되는 남자가 그 연극연구가를 조치 안케 말하는 일이 자조 잇게 되엿다. 『암만해도 무슨 파란이 미구에 생기지』하는 생각을 그 집에 가는 친구마다 가지게 되야 한 불안과 흥미를 어울러 가지고 보게 되엿다.
그런데 최근에 차저간 친구의 말을 드르면 그 집의 어엽분 톄경이 깨여저 잇드라 한다. 왜 그랫느냐고 무른즉 아모 말이 업시 안해가 『모르지요 술을 잔뜩 먹고 와서 어적게 그럿케 깨뜨려 노앗스닛가요』할 뿐이드란다.
하는 일 업시 젊은 녀자가 소설만 닑고 안젓는 것은 조치 못한 일이니 근본으로 남자가 직업을 갈던지 동시에 녀자도 직업을 가지게 하던지 그야말로 근본뎍으로 방향을 전환식히는 일이 권태에서 버서저나는 일일 것 갓다고 권하겟다는 친구가 지금 두세 사람이 나잇스나 엇지 될넌지.......

離婚햇다가 다시 살다가....

엇던 부호의 아드님이 동경 류학을 하고 잇는 동안에 그 본택에서는 사돈집과 의론하야 며느님을 학교에 다니게 하엿다. 일본 유학만 하고 오면 저마다 안해가 무식해서 갑갑하다고 리혼설을 내이는 판이라 미리미리 나 어린 며느님을(전에 보통학교 졸업은 하엿스닛가) 학교에 다니게 한 것이엿다. 그러나 아모리 학교에 보낸대도 어릴 때부터의 자긔 안해와는 새로 련애를 시작할 수도 업거니와 거긔서 한번 떠난 마음이 다시 도라갈 수도 업고 또 그보다도 더 자긔의 리성과 맛는 조흔 배우자감이 작고 눈에 뜨이니 엇저는 수가 업섯다. 그래서 경성에서 어느 녀자고등학교를 맞추고 남편 따라 류학하러 동경에 온 것을 긔회삼아 둘이 마주안저 합의 리혼을 하기로 하엿다. 녀자도 고등학교까지 맛친지라 『나는 죽어도 당신의 집 귀신이요』하고 느러지지를 안코 당신이 정히 실타면 실흔 것이야 엇지 살겟소』하는 투로 대하엿다. 그래서 친구들은 녀자 쪽에서 그만큼 리해가 잇스니 합의 리혼을 하면 아조 훌륭하겟다고 그 헝편을 부러워하는 사람까지 잇섯다.
그런대 그 남자의 친구 중에 한사람 톨스토이와 유도무랑(有島武郞)을 사숙하는 인도주의자요. 동시에 무대예술을 연구하는 예술가가 잇서서 리혼을 하기로 마조안즌 부부의 엽헤 안저서 두 사람의 리혼을 반대하엿다.
一 부인이 녀자고등까지 맞추고 동경 유학까지<103> 오섯스니 현대뎍 안해로서의 조곰도 부족한 뎜이 업슬 것.
二 련애니 무어니하야 바람에 뜨인 녀자와 새로 결혼을 한 대야 그 결혼이 반듯이 행복할 것이라고만 밋을 수 잇느냐는 등 여러 가지 리유와 조건을 드러서 조리잇게 리해 만흔 태도로 리혼을 말라고 간절히 권고하엿다. 아모 리해 업는 완고 노인이나 처가 편의 애걸하는 태도가 안이라 가장 리해 깁고 가장 새로운 생각을 가진 친구의 조리 잇는 권고라 듯고 보니 그럴 듯 하야 안해는 본래부터 바라든 일이지만은 남자도 리혼할 뜻을 단연히 버리고 다시 안해의 손을 굿게 잡고 친구의 압헤 머리를 숙여 감사하엿다. 그리고 두 군데 하숙을 따로 뎡하고 잇던 것을 한데로 합첫다. 싸홈햇다가 화해하고 한 자리에 누은 부부가태서 처음 四五日은 서로 이약이 할 일도 잇고 별다른 흥미도 업지 안엇스나 열흘이 지나고 보름째 드닛가 다시 전 마음으로 도라가고 말엇다. 『이래서는 못쓰겟다』고 남자는 안해에게서 떠러저  다라나는 마음을 붓잡아 매이려고 하엿스나 그런 애를 쓸수록 마음은 더 밧비 다라낫다. 『하우불이』엇지 할 수 업는 녀자를 내가 억지의 동정으로 다리고 잇거니 하는 마음이 더 압서서 보면 볼스록 녀자가 미련해 보이고 무식해 보엿다. 안해 역시 어느 때던지 남편의 정이 자긔에게 잇지 안이하거니-하게 되야 무슨 일이라도 터러노코 의론하거나 탁 안겨지지도 미더지지도 안엇다. 그러는 한편으로 자긔를 진정으로 동정해 주고 리해해 주는 사람은 그 화해 부처 주든 예술가엿다. 남편은 자긔를 실혀하고 돌보아 주지 안는 사람이고 그이는 사정을 잘 아러주고 현대 청년 중에 보기듬을게 원만하고 저절로 미덤즉 하엿다. 차저오는 남편의 친구 모도가 자긔와는 딴 세상인 남편의 편이요. 다만 그 예술가뿐만이 자긔 남편인 것 가티 생각되엿다.
두 달이 채 지나지 못해서 남편은 리혼하기를 선언하엿다. 안해는 예술가의 안해를 따라 온천으로 려행을 떠낫다.
그러나 그 후 남자는 다른 녀자와 결혼을 하엿스나 예술가는 오래인 동정자가 안이엿던지 저 혼자 갈대로 가 버렷다. 그 후에 풍편에 들으니 어데서인지 다른 조흔 남자와 결혼을 하엿다고 드럿다.

안해가 못 잇는 그림자

O명 녀학교를 졸업하고 사범과를 마치고 서울 갓가운 어느 보통학교의 녀훈도로 잇다가 지금은 어느 유치원 보모로 잇는 P라는 조촐하고 얌전한 녀자가 잇다. 그는 학생 시대로부터 녀훈도 다니던 처음까지 사모하고 사랑하던 S라는 청년이 잇섯는데 그가 청년 중에는 드물게 보는 진중한 사람이고 미덤직한 이엿스나<104> 도리혀 조곰 어렴스럽고 사근사근 자미나게 구러주는 점이 부족한 것 갓기도 하엿다.
그런데 상해도 가 잇섯고 동경 류학을 마치고 나와서 어느 신문사에 다니는 K라는 청년이 생겨서 그가 여러 가지로 조흔 조건을 들어 구혼(求婚)하는 바람에 P는 K와 결혼하엿다. 결혼한 지 불과 몃 달에 K는 신문사를 그만두고 어느 새로 세워진 회사에 다니면서 신문사보다 나흔 월급을 밧게 되고 녀자는 훈도를 그만두고 유치원 보모로 다니게 되여서 두 사람의 수입은 상당하게 되엿다.
그러나 단 두 해가 차지 못하여서 K는 안해를 니저버린 사람처럼 기생을 친한다 술을 마신다 다른 례배당 녀자와 편지질을 한다하야 신 가뎡치고는 거의 리혼한 사이처럼 되어잇다.
그 P라는 녀자가 그 친한 동무에게 하소연하듯 고백한 이약이는 자못 귀를 기우릴 만한 뎜이 잇다.
