別건곤 第63號
1933년 05월 01일
連載探偵小說, 湖底의 秘密, 原名-눈푸른 處女 (Serial Detective Novel, The Secret of the Lake Bottom, Original Title - The Blue-Eyed Maiden)
소설 (Novel)

連載探偵小說, 湖底의 秘密, 原名-눈푸른 處女
모-리스. 루부랑(원작자), 尹成學(번역자)

Serial Detective Novel, The Secret of the Lake Bottom, Original Title - The Blue-Eyed Maiden
Maurice Leblanc (Original Author), Yun Sŏng-hak (Translator)

1
In the splendid Seoul of March, as the warm spring rain falls, the youthful Ri U-do (李愚道), full of life like the lush green grass sprouting, strolled leisurely through the city.

A dapper gentleman, not too tall, with a frame that was neither too stout nor too thin, he appeared all the more charming and refined.

His skin looked intelligent and refined, his strong-looking forehead and broad chest exuded masculinity, and he wore a modern, tailored suit that fit him perfectly. Anyone, even a stranger, could easily surmise that he was a representative modern man.

As he happened to pass by the park, he unexpectedly noticed a gentleman walking in the same direction, following a woman.

What had seemed like a mere guess quickly turned into a certainty. There is nothing as amusing and curious as watching a man follow a woman.

"Ha, that fellow is following a woman. How amusing! Should I follow him then...?"

Driven by curiosity, he soon began to follow the man who was following the woman.

A man following a woman, and another man following that man. One might call it a triangular relationship!

The three of them walked through the bustling streets of Seoul, each maintaining a suitable distance.

The strange man maintained the attitude of a perfect gentleman so as not to be noticed by the woman.

Ri U-do was, at least, an experienced observer in such matters. He also pretended to know nothing, subtly passing by the many pedestrians, thinking, "Somehow, I must catch a glimpse of their faces."

The strange man's back view showed a striped suit, a black fedora, hair slicked back with oil, broad shoulders, and a tall stature. Turning to see his face, he was handsome, with a mustache that spread out in a figure eight (八字), with glossy skin, appearing to be about thirty years old. He had an energetic gait and tried to appear refined, but there was a vulgar air about him. He wore a few rings on his fingers and smoked a cigar.

"Now, I must see the woman's face," Ri U-do thought, quickening his pace. The woman was slender, and her delicate steps immediately brought to mind English ladies. She had beautiful curves and ankles that seemed soft and fragile, and her face was so beautiful that it evoked thoughts of a beauty who could bring down a kingdom. Her eyes, shining like stars, sparkled even more beneath her golden hair.

Many passersby glanced at her as they passed, but she seemed to pay little attention to them.

"Oh! She's a beauty like an angel," the young Ri cried out involuntarily.

"Truly a splendid classical beauty. Too good for the lecherous fellow following her... But what is his purpose? Is he a jealous detective (刑事)? A heartbroken would-be handsome man? Or a lustful fiend (色狂魔) wandering in search of love? Yes, he probably isn't far from that."

"Even if I can't say for sure, he's not far off in that regard. Or perhaps he has money and women and is simply driven by instinct, overflowing with sexual desire."

The woman walked gracefully toward the wide plaza in front of the opera house (극장) filled with carriages. As soon as she entered, a carriage blocked her path. Seeing her way blocked, she effortlessly reached out her beautiful arm and grabbed the horse's rein. The horse, startled, stopped. But it was the horse's owner who was angered. The horse's owner stepped out of the carriage and shouted, "What are you doing! This is dangerous!" But as soon as he shouted, he cried out, "Ah!" In an instant, the woman's fist had struck the horse owner's nose, and bright red blood dripped onto the ground. People gathered, and police officers ran over, making a fuss, but the woman paid no attention to the horse owner clutching his nose and turned lightly, walking away as if nothing had happened. In front of the Youth Association (靑年會), two children were fighting.

Without asking any questions, the woman grabbed the two children by the scruff of their necks and tossed them aside as if throwing away kittens, then threw them two coins.

"Heh, heh, heh, what a fearsome woman!" Ri U-do exclaimed, his eyes widening.

And so, when they reached Jongno intersection (종로 네거리), the woman entered a tea room (차점). The young Ri watched her sit down in a chair.

The man who had been following the woman did not enter the tea room itself but took a seat in a corner where the woman could not see him.

The woman ordered tea and four pieces of bread, and began to eat with her snow-white teeth.

Many people seated nearby occasionally glanced in her direction, but she seemed unconcerned. She ordered another four pieces of bread.

At that moment, a woman sitting at a table a little further away caught Ri’s attention.

She also had blonde, golden hair like an English woman and wore clothes that, while not particularly flashy, were fashionable in Seoul, with matching colors.

Beside her were three children in tattered clothes. The children were receiving sweets from the woman and eating them.

The children did not know the woman and seemed puzzled and apprehensive about receiving sweets from a stranger, but they were also delighted and chattering about something.

"An intelligent face and a lovely figure," Ri thought, completely captivated by the woman. He turned his head and looked at the woman he had been following earlier. She was also a beauty.

She was so lovely and elegant that it was astonishing. But the blue-eyed woman (he had decided to call her the blue-eyed woman) was even more so than the English woman.

She paid the bill and stood up with the children. Should he follow this maiden? Or should he stay and follow the English woman? Which one should he choose? Blue eyes? Golden eyes? There was no time to think for long.

He quickly got up and paid the bill, then hurried out of the tea room. In the end, the temptation of the blue eyes triumphed. He came out of the tea room and was shocked.

He saw the blue-eyed maiden standing and talking to the man who had been strangely following the English woman since earlier. But they were not speaking calmly; they both seemed agitated, as if they were arguing. At first glance, it seemed that the man was preventing the woman from passing.

"How amusing. A good opportunity. I will take advantage of this and step between them to decide the outcome," he thought, quickly approaching. Just then, a car stopped in front of the tea room.

As soon as the gentleman stepping out of the car saw this scene, he struck the man who was talking with his cane.

"Ah!" the struck man exclaimed in surprise, taking a step back.

"What are you doing! You bad fellow!"

The gentleman, who was of modest height and appeared to be about fifty years old, raised his cane again, threatening to strike.

"Don't talk to my daughter! I am her father. You rude fellow! I cannot forgive a fellow like you!"

The two men glared fiercely at each other.

The woman grabbed her angry father's arm, trying to force him into the car.

"It doesn't matter. This fellow!"

"What!" The man, unable to contain his anger, rushed forward, pushed the maiden aside, and snatched the old man's cane. He was about to strike with the cane when suddenly, a man appeared between them.

His right eye was twitching nervously, and he had a cigarette in his mouth. It was none other than the young Ri. In fluent language, he said,

"Excuse me, but could I get a light..."

The man who had been fighting was dumbfounded and stood there blankly,

"Why are you interfering? I don't have one!"

"No, I saw you smoking earlier," he pressed.

The man tried to push Ri away. But strangely, his hands and his entire body were as if covered with glue, and he could not move. Involuntarily, his head lowered, and

a groan escaped his lips.

"Ugh..." he strained with all his might, his face turning red, trying to break free, but Ri, who was holding his arms, simply smiled leisurely and said, still,

"Excuse me, but a light... If you don't lend me a light, I'll be very..." The onlookers all laughed.

After being ridiculed for a while, Ri released his arm.

The assailant pushed through the crowd and ran off. But by then, the car carrying the blue-eyed maiden and her father was already far away, barely visible.

"I've only opened the prologue," Ri muttered, watching the man follow the car.

The young man looked in the direction of the English woman. She, too, had been watching the street drama and was still mingling among the onlookers. He immediately began to follow the woman.

Ri was in the midst of the past, the future, and the essence of life. His past was filled with strange endeavors and adventures. Perhaps the future would not stray far from that path. But the present was without purpose or occupation. Just when the time was right (when a man is in his thirties), it is a woman who decides our fate. Indeed, after the blue-eyed maiden had vanished in an instant, he followed the English woman, seeking the path of his destiny. After a while, when he looked around, he found that he had somehow returned to following the English woman that the man had initially been pursuing. The triangular relationship continued.

2
The English woman continued to stroll leisurely through the bustling streets.

She entered a hotel.

The pursuer stopped.

After standing for a moment, he walked a few steps, bought a pack of cigarettes, went into the hotel, spoke with the doorman (門番) for about thirty minutes, and then came out again.

Ri also went to the doorman and asked about the English woman.

As soon as he asked, the English woman appeared and quickly got into a car. She was carrying a bag. Perhaps she was going on a trip?

"Hey, driver, follow that car," Ri said, getting into the car next to him.

The English woman got out of the car at the station at eight o'clock and immediately went into the dining room to eat. Ri also sat at a table a little further away. After finishing her meal, she was smoking a cigarette.

At about nine thirty, the boy (뽀-이) came with a ticket. It seemed that she was going to take the maximum express at nine thirty.

"Hey, boy," Ri said, taking out a bill and calling the boy.

"If you tell me that woman's name, I'll give you five won (원)..."

"Paek Hwa-son (白花孫) is her name."

"Where is she going? You know, right?"

"She bought a ticket to Pongch'ŏn (奉天) and is in car number five (五號車) now."

Ri made up his mind. He bought a ticket to Pongch'ŏn and hurried onto the platform (푸랏트호-ㅁ).

There were not many people on the platform. And she was already on the train.

In car number five, only two people were sitting by the window. Ri kept his distance from the train, strolling along the platform and buying newspapers and magazines.

Soon, the train whistle sounded.

He hurriedly got on the train that was starting to move and took a seat in the third car.

The English woman was sitting alone by the window. Involuntarily, she looked up and saw Ri, a strange passenger who had no luggage or bags, but she did not seem to find it particularly odd and opened the chocolate (초코레-트) box on her lap and began to eat a large piece of chocolate.

After a while, the conductor (車掌) came and checked the tickets and then left. The train was speeding across the vast plain.

The vivid traces of splendid Seoul gradually disappeared as the train sped away. Ri aimlessly flipped through the newspaper, but he found it dull. He felt bored.

He moved his seat closer to her. The English woman, not understanding that the passenger seemed to want to say something to her, kept her mouth shut and looked away. It seemed that she would not respond at all, so he decided to say something himself. In fact, it was common for the playful man to do such things, and he said in a very polite tone,

"I'm terribly sorry... It's rude and impolite to say something like this so suddenly, but I have something important to say regarding your personal affairs, if you don't mind. What do you think!"

The woman, still touching the chocolate, looked away and said curtly,

"If it's something simple, go ahead."

"Listen, madam..."

"I am not a madam. I'm a maiden."

"Yes, is that so? Well, today I happened to notice a very strange man following you all day. And that man..."

Before he could finish speaking, the woman barely raised her face and looked back, saying,

"You are being very rude. Have you forgotten the manners of a Chosŏn (朝鮮) gentleman? You don't seem to be employed to monitor the people following me..."

"No, it's just that the man was acting so strangely..."

"If it's about him, I know him well. He's a man named Ma T'aek (馬宅) whom I met a few years ago, and he is at least polite, and even if he follows, he does so from a distance, so it's no matter. And besides, he is not on this train."

The cold words coming from her cherry-red lips swept through Ri’s chest.

"I am very sorry! Madam! But you are being too harsh. I have nothing to say."

"That's right. Then get off at the next station."

"I am very sorry, but I have business in Pongch'ŏn, so I cannot obey your command."

"You only had business there after you found out I was going there."

"No, I saw you at the tea room, and that's when I decided to go." Ri raised his face.

"I don't think so. You had your eye on that pretty blue-eyed maiden, and if there hadn't been a fight, you would have followed her. Since that didn't work out, you followed me. And you came with that man you call strange, from the hotel to the station restaurant."

"I am amazed that you paid so much attention to my every move."

"Of course, I know everything, even the blinking of your eyes."

"Really? Then do you know my name?"

"You are Ri U-do (李愚道), the explorer (探險家) of Tibet and Central Asia." Ri was startled.

"I am truly amazed. But how did you find out that..."

"I didn't investigate anything. But if I see a woman alone in a train compartment without any luggage, not even a bag, anyone would observe her. And just now you took out your business card and tore off a piece of paper. I only saw your business card. So I thought of Ri U-do's exploration stories in the newspaper these days. It was a very simple observation."

"Truly too simple! I am amazed by your keen insight."

"Yes, that's right. I always say that I have good insight."

"But you seemed to be only looking at the chocolate box, but somehow..."

"I don't use my eyes to see, and I don't need to think to guess."

"Guess? What do you mean?" "I mean that your real name is not Ri U-do."

"How could that be?"

"If not, what is the reason for the letters H.V on your hat? You wouldn't have borrowed a friend's hat..."

His unflinching words left Ri speechless. He had started the conversation, but now he was at a loss for words, which greatly lowered his pride.

"Then what does H.V mean?..."

"Yes. It's just that your initials being H.V are quite rare. From the moment I saw those letters H and V together, I thought of a name that I remember well."

"That name is?..."

"It has nothing to do with you, and you don't know him."

"Arsène Lupin, isn't it?"

Ri slapped his knee and laughed heartily.

"Haha, so you're saying I'm Arsène Lupin."

화려한 서울의 춘삼월 따뜻한 봄빗흘 바더 푸릇푸릇이 도다 오르는 고흔 잔디와 가티 생(生)에 열중한 절문 청춘의 부드러운 가삼과도 가티 청년 리우도(李愚道)는 한가한 마음으로 시가(市街)를 건일고 잇섯다.
키는 그리 크도적도 안한 아담한 신사 그다지 뚱뚱하도 야위도 안한 알마즌 테격 그것이 더한층 긔잇고 강긔잇게 보인다.
그 모든 것에 영민하고 단아하게 보이는 피부가 터질 듯이 힘잇서 보이는 두억개 넓직한 가삼의 남성미 그런 중에도 최신 류행의 줄선 양복을 몸에 꼭 맛도록 입은 그 남자 아모리 처음보는 사람일지라도 현대 대표적 신식남성의 한사람이란 것을 잘 추단할 수가 잇다.
그가 맛츰 공원 압흘 지날 때 불의에 자긔와 가티 거러가는 신사가 엇더한 녀자의 뒤를 딸은다는 것을 발견햇다.
그럴덧하든 추칙은 발서 어느 사이에 확실한 사실이 되고 마랏다. 남자가 녀자의 뒤를 딸른 것을 구경하는 것가티 자미잇고 우수운 일은 업다.
「야- 녀석이 녀자의 뒤를 딸아간다. 자미잇구나! 그러면 나는 저자의 뒤를 딸어볼가. ...」
그는 만흔 호긔심에 끌녀 곳 녀자의 뒤를 쫏튼 남자의 뒤를 쫏기시작햇다.
녀자의 뒤를 따른 남자, 남자의 뒤를 따른 남자. 말하자면 이것이 삼각관계라고 할까!
세사람이 다 각긔 적당한 거리를 띄워가며 번화한 서울 시가를 거러갓다.
이상한 남자는 녀자가 눈치를 채이지 못할만치 가장 신사인 태도를 일치안엇다.
리도우는 적어도 그런 방면에 경험자이엿다. 그는 또한 아모것도 몰으는체 하고 만흔 행인을 슬적슬적 지나치며 「엇더케 하든지 남녀의 얼골을 조곰 보야야겟다」하고 생각햇다.
그 이상한 남자의 뒷모양은 줄진 양복에 검은 중절모 모자 밋헤는 머리는 기름을 발너 광채가 나고 역시 억개는 넓직한대 키는 훨신 크고 도라선 정면을 보니 아조 얼골이 잘생겻고 수염은 팔자(八字)로 버러젓스며, 윤택잇는 살빗, 나희는 한 삼십세 가량이나 되어보엿다. 활발스러운 거름거리 억지로 점잔은체 하는 태도 그러나 어대인지 상스려운 풍채가 뵌다. 손에는 두셋의 반지(指環)을 끼고 입에는 여송연을 피우고 잇섯다.
「이번에는 녀자의 얼골을 보아야겟다」하고 리우도는 다리를 빨니 옴겻다. 녀자는 키가 호리호리한데 삽분삽분거러가는 거름거리는 대번에 영국부인들을 련상할만하다. 어엽븐 곡선미(曲線美) 그리고 만지면 부서질 듯이 연(軟)해 보이는 동글고도 볼통한 두복상씨(踝) 그리고 얼골은 경국의 미를 생각할 만치 고옵고도 아름다웟다. 샛별가티 반짝이는 두 눈동자가 금실머리 밋헤서 더 한층 반짝이고 잇섯다.
오고가고 하는 만흔 사람들이 겻을 지날 때마다 흘금흘금 처다보앗스나 그녀자는 처다보는 행인들에게 별로 유의하지 안는듯이 보엿다.
「오오! 천사가튼 미인이다.」하고 리청년은 불식중 속으로 부르지젓다.
「참으로 훌늉한 고전적(古典) 미인이다. 뒤를 따른 여드럼장이 한테는 너무나 과한 미인이다.... 그러나 저자가 엇떠케 할 적정인가? 질투만흔 나리님(刑事)인가? 실연한 자층 미남일가. 그러치 안으면 사랑을 주으랴고 도라단이는 색광마(色狂魔)일까? 그러타 과연 그가튼대서 별로 버서나지 안으리라.」
「꼭 맛는다고는 할수 업서도 하여간 이 방면에서 멀지 안타. 또는 돈이 잇고, 여자가 잇고, 다만 본능적으로 성욕에 넘쳐 도라단이는 놈인 듯하다.」
그녀자는 마차가 들어찬 오페라(극장) 압 널은 마당으로 삽분삽분＜56＞ 거러들어갓다. 막 들어가자말자 한채의 마차가 녀자이 압길을 막엇다. 압 길을 막은 것을 본 녀자는 두말 업시 고흔 팔을 느려 말 곱비를 잡업다. 곱히를 잡힌 말(馬)은 놀나 멈춧섯다. 그러나 성낸 것은 말 임자이엿다. 말 임자는 곳 마차에서 나려왓다.
「이게 무슨 짓이야! 큰일 나겟네!」하고 욋치자 말자 말 임자는 「악」 소리를 첫다. 어느사에 그 녀자의 주먹은 말 임자의 코를 후려갈겨 시뻘건 피가 땅에 두닥두닥 떨어젓다. 왕래하든 사람들은 끄러모이고 순사들이 달녀와서 이런이 저런이 하고 야단을 하나 그 녀자는 코를 움켜쥐고 잇는 말 임자는 도라보도 안하고 몸을 가볍게 돌녀 아모치도 안한 듯이 빨리 거러갓다. 청년회 압헤서 두 아해가 싸옴을 하고 잇섯다.
그 녀자는 뭇는말도 업시 댓듬 두 아해를 덜미를 움켜쥐며 맛치 고양이색기나 집어던지는 듯이 던지고는 금전 두푼을 내던저 주엇다.
허허허 참 끔직한 녀자다! 하며 리도우는 눈이 둥그래젓다.
그리하야 종로 네거리에 왓슬 때 그녀자는 엇던 차점에 들어갓다. 리청년은 녀자가 의자에 걸어 안는 것을 바라보앗다.
그 녀자를 미행하여 온 그 남자는 식당 한 가운대는 들어가랴고도 하지 안하고 녀자가 못볼만치 한편 구석에 자리를 잡고 잇섯다.
그 녀자는 차와 빵 넷쪼각을 주문하야 백설갓치 흰니로 먹기 시작햇다.
사방에 들너안즌 만흔 사람들이 각금 시선을 그녀자의 편으로 돌엿스나 그 녀자는 예사이엿다. 또 다시 빵 네조작을 주문햇다.
그때 거기서 조곰 떠러저 잇는 탁자 압헤 안즌 한 녀자가 리청년의 주의를 끌엇다.

아해들은 알지도 못하는 녀자에게 과자를 어더 왼 영문인줄 몰으고 겁도 나는 듯하나 질거운 듯이 무엇이라고 짓거리고 잇섯다.

너무나 어엽버서 놀날만치 단려하다. 그러치만 눈푸런 녀자는(그는 혼자서 자긔 마음대로 눈푸린 녀자라고 지엇다) 영국녀자 이상이다.

그는 곳 일어서 세음을 치루자말자 빨니 차점을 나섯다. 결국 푸른눈 편의 유혹이 승리를 하고 마랏다. 그는 차점을 나와 깜짝 놀낫다.
눈푸린 처녀가 앗가부터 이상하게 영국 녀자의 뒤를 딸어오든 그 남자하고 서서 무슨 말을 하는 걸 보앗다. 그러나 서서 순리로 말하는 것이 아니고 두 사람이 다 흥분되야 언쟁이라도 하는 듯 햇다. 언뜻 보건대 그 녀자가 지나가랴는 것을 남자가 못가도록 막은 듯하다.
『자미잇지. 조흔 긔회다. 이것을 리용하야 두 사람의 사이에 들어가 승패를 결정해 주리라』하고 빨니 갓갑게 거러갈 때 막 한채의 자동차가 차점 압헤 머물넛다.
차안에서 나려오든 신사가 이 광경을 보자말자 곳 단장으로 말하고 섯는 남자의 모자를 후려처 갈겻다.

「미안합니다만 불을 조곰 ... 불도 조곰 빌녀주시지 안한다면 참 ...」한다 사방에 들너서서 구경하는 사람들은 모다 깔깔 우섯다.
한참 조롱을 하다가 리청년은 그의＜57＞ 팔을 노아 주엇다.
궐자는 만흔 구경군을 헤치고 쫏차갓다. 그러나 발서 그때에는 눈푸런 처녀와 그 아버지를 태운 자동차는 잘 보이지도 안을 만치 멀니 다라나고 잇섯다.

「그럴 리가 잇습니가?」
「그러치 안으면 당신의 모자 우에 쓰인 글자가 H.V인 것은 무슨 리유입니가? 친구의 모자를 빌녀쓰고 온 것은 안일터인대 ...」
조금도 서슴지 안는 그의 언변에는 리청년도 말히 막힐 만치 되엿다. 처음에 자긔가 먼저 말을 끄내여 가지고 도로혀 말이 막힐만치 밋진다는 것은 적지 안케 그의 자존심을 낫추엇다.
「그러면 H.V의 의미는? ...」
「네. 그것은 당신의 성명의 머리글자가 H.V인 것이 만히 업는 드문 글자인 것을 잘 암니다. 저는 그 H자와 V자의 합친 것을 본 후부터는 불식중 내가 잘 기억하고 잇는 성명을 하나를 생각하게 되엿습니다.」
「그 성명은? ...」
「별로 당신과 관게가 업슬뿐 아니라 당신은 몰으는 분입니다.」
「알세-ㄴ 루팡이지요.」
리청년은 무릅을 치며 쾌활하게 껄걸 우섯다.
