最近 合走男女氏 列傳, 봄바람에 날아간 모-던 男女세상 (Biographies of Recently Eloped Couples: The Modern Men and Women's World Gone with the Spring Breeze)	雙S	잡저 (Essay)

The title is "Biographies of Recently Eloped Couples." Perhaps, unless you know, dear readers, you won't readily grasp the meaning just by looking at the title. Let's start by explaining the title here.

To interpret "hapju (合走)" as "joining together and running away" doesn't quite convey the meaning. In the countryside, when a young woman from the front house and a bachelor from the back house fall in love and run away together, it's said they "matched and ran away." The word "hapju" signifies that. That is, a certain Mr. Mopo or Mr. Gentleman and a certain Miss Doggeol or Miss Maiden, while in love, find the surrounding circumstances unfavorable, yearn for a free world, and disappear. These men and women have "eloped (合走)." In Japanese, wouldn't that be called "kakeochi (駆け落ち)?"

This year, with the spring breeze, as many as three couples we know well have "eloped (合走)."

Out of sympathy for their difficult situations, I intend to tell the readers about their inside stories on their behalf. This is the meaning of this "Biographies of Recently Eloped Couples."

K Miss and S Mr. Who Went to Shanghai by Airplane

K Miss.

Seeing the title, some of you will probably guess who it is.

Elegant like a queen, plush like a rich man's daughter-in-law, glamorous in her extravagance, and tight-lipped... that was K Miss who frustrated the bachelors of the capital for a while.

While she was at XXX and XXX, various rumors were heard. That she went to P'yŏngnam somewhere to visit a handsome man, or that she was in talks for marriage with a teacher at a school...

Someone sarcastic even saw Mr. R taking a morning stroll on the back hill of Chungang School <18> and said,

"Why don't you go and greet Miss K from early in the morning like that?" But anyway, it's true that the two, R and K, like a pair of mandarin ducks, happily frolicked around the Pebi-Kolpu area in Chinkogae.

However, Miss K herself denied the engagement rumor with Mr. R. Mr. R prevaricated and didn't give a clear answer.

Then, last summer, news like a bolt from the blue fell upon Seoul from a summer vacationer who returned from Sŏkwangsa.

"Miss K came to Sŏkwangsa, and I don't know who her companion was, but he was quite tall, had a dark complexion, and was quite handsome."

"Then isn't that Mr. R she's supposed to be engaged to?" they asked.

"I know Mr. R well..." they replied.

The young man who went on summer vacation to Sŏkwangsa with her was S Mr.

S Mr... He is a son of a wealthy family from C in Kyŏngsang Province. They say he probably owns tens of thousands of bushels.

From then on, all sorts of rumors were heard.

"Miss K is marrying Mr. S, and they agreed to give her 500 sŏk (石, unit of measurement for grain)" they said.

"They are setting up a very grand household in Seoul,"

and

"They are setting up a household in Taegu,"

was also reported in the news section.

"Mr. S is married, but he illegally married Miss K first, and later he will send away his first and second wives, and then she will advance to become the first wife."

Among these rumors, I don't know which one to believe, but while those stories were going around, before we knew it, the two, K and S, went out to Yŏŭido, took a plane, passed through Japan, and went to Shanghai.

They had their wedding and honeymoon by airplane.

Now in Seoul, Miss K's mother, who is said to live in a 3,000-won house bought by her son-in-law, lives in the same rented room she was with Miss K, with ten bags of rice and XX won, waiting for them to "study" and return in glory.

I hope the troubles at Shanhaiguan end quickly so that the two can return more easily.

The Poet R Mr. and R Miss Full of Regret

The reason why love is often a tragedy is that the two people in love may not be in a position to advance to the next stage of love, which is marriage.

For example, they are in love, but they can't satisfy the demands of a Miss who wants money, or she is already married.

Is this elopement case of R Mr. and R Miss because Mr. R is married?

Mr. R is an affectionate poet. He is a victim of customary marriage, not falling into the trend of young Koreans in their 30s today.

He tried several times to get a divorce, but couldn't.

"Why does a guy with a wife, good or bad, have an affair! It was wrong to start with,"

some might scold.

But if you end up having an affair even when you have a wife you love, how can you force the youth in the prime of their lives to kill their emotions and stay confined to the old moral framework of having a wife in name only, without the pleasure of love? That can't be done in 1933.

Mr. R had an affair.

He knew well that love is the prelude to marriage. So he tried even harder to get a divorce. But what could he do when it didn't work out!

His parents wouldn't listen, and the other side especially wouldn't listen...

The relationship between Mr. R and Miss R became known to Miss R's family.

Miss R's family approved of their marriage. But the approval came with a condition. That is,

"R, you must completely divorce your current wife first. Then I will let you marry my daughter."

And if he couldn't get a divorce, he was not allowed to contact Miss R from then on.

How frustrating could this be! He can't get a divorce... if he can't get a divorce, he can't even meet his lover...

But the love of youth melts even iron. Such surveillance or restrictions are nothing more than a cup of water poured on a blazing palace.

Mr. R and Miss R made a decision.

"Let's break free from this awkward and troublesome environment and run away somewhere to die."

So, one night in April, the two boarded the Kyŏngwŏn Line. <19>

Where were they going?

If they took the Kyŏngwŏn Line, there's nowhere to go but Kando... No. The two went to Sŏkwangsa and spent some time in a honeymoon that was sweeter than honey, doing underground activities. Of course, they didn't have a formal wedding.

We don't know what the news is after that yet, but I, like the readers, am curious.

The Musician A Mr. and Miss K Are Said to Be Going to Beijing

Our beloved tenor A Mr.

He is a vocalist who has received much love from our people in concerts, records, and on the radio, and whom we consider a great source of pride, Mr. A.

Mr. A has been a professor at XX Women's Professional School, as the world already knows.

Meanwhile, Miss K, who graduated from the music department of the same XX Women's Professional School and has great promise in the music scene...

This pair of young men and women suddenly disappeared from our sight around mid-April.

This news caused a strange shock when it was reported in the newspapers, and for a while, the story of these two, A and K, became the central topic of conversation wherever young men and women gathered in Seoul.

Artists are inherently susceptible to emotions. Both A and K are artists.

From the time Miss K was a student, the glances they exchanged were unusual.

Some people noticed this to some extent, but few knew that the relationship between the two had become so deep.

Their love blazed. Naturally, the two should become a couple.

From Miss K's perspective, there are no obstacles. But Mr. A is not the same.

He has a wife and children with her. He has a proper family.

He is someone else's husband, someone else's father, and the world does not easily allow a man to become another woman's husband.

Moreover, Mr. A is a teacher at XX Women's Professional School, where a religious atmosphere overflows. An illegal marriage like those commonly seen in the world is absolutely out of the question.

But if the two give up and separate, their burning passion will not allow it.

After much thought, the two came up with a final plan.

Mr. A obtained leave from the school, saying he was going to Harbin for a vacation, and the two joined hands and set off for Beijing, toward a land of freedom.

But did they really head to Beijing?

Thus, this spring breeze has decorated the history of love on this land like a flower. <20>
