잡지명	별건곤 제29호
발행일	1930년 06월 01일
기사제목	朝鮮歷史日誌 (Korean Historical Diary)
기사형태	잡저 (Miscellaneous writing)

Korean Historical Diary
April
1st (Old March 3rd)
◇ Great hunt at Nangnang Plain (custom of Koguryŏ)

2nd (Same 4th)
◇ Yiadong Count Kim Ŭng-ha died a martyr in the deep river (Kwanghae 11th year, Kimi)

3rd (Same 5th)
◇ Ch'a Osan Ch'ŏn-ro died (Kwanghae 7th year, Ŭlmyo)
◇ Scholar Hong Ik-han died a martyr in Shenyang (Injio, Chŏngch'uk)
◇ The 1st Regiment of the Royal Guard Infantry was organized

4th (Same 6th)
◇ Changgungdan Ritual held (Kwangmu era)

5th (Same 7th)
◇ Ch'oe Yŏng-un Che-u founded Ch'ŏndogyo (天道敎)

6th (Same 8th)
◇ Yi Ch'ungmu-gong Sun-sin was born (Injong Ŭlsa)
◇ Regulations on Decorations were promulgated (Kwangmu 4th year)

7th (Same 9th)
◇ The Catholic missionary Zhou Wenmo and other prominent figures such as Yi Ka-hwan, Yi Sŭng-hun, and Chŏng Yak-hyŏn were executed, and Dasan Chŏng Yak-yong was exiled to Kangjin (Sunjo 1st year, Shinyu)
◇ Detailed rules of the Japanese Immigration Protection Law were promulgated (Yunghŭi 2nd year)
◇ Assisted Qing to attack Mao Wenlong of Kado (Injio Chŏngch'uk)

8th (Same 10th)
◇ Envoy Kim Ki-su departed from Pusan for Japan (Yi T'aewang Pyŏngja)

9th (Same 11th)
◇ The Japan-Qing Treaty of Shimonoseki was ratified (Yi T'aewang 32nd year, Ŭlmi, Kaeo 504)
◇ A major earthquake occurred (Kwanghae 3rd year, Kyehae)

10th (Same 12th)
◇ Injio's Restoration (Kwanghae 15th year, Kyehae)
◇ Detailed rules of the Forest Law were promulgated (Yunghŭi 2nd year)

11th (Same 13th)
◇ Remnants of Paekche, based in Turyang, Isŏng, and Sŏsŏng, resisted the Silla-Tang forces (Silla Muryŏl Wang Kyŏngsin)
◇ Two regiments of the Royal Guard Infantry were organized (Kwangmu 11th year)

12th (Same 14th)
◇ Kim Il-gyŏng's faction, including Yi In-jwa and Chŏng Hŭi-ryang, initiated a rebellion in Yŏngnam (Yŏngjo 4th year, Musin)

13th (Same 15th)
◇ Yi In-jwa captured Ch'ŏngju, and Military Commissioner Yi Pong-sang, Vice Commander Hong Rim, and Camp Commander Nam Yŏn-nyŏn died as martyrs (Same as above)
◇ Lady Kang was given death by poison (Injio 23rd year, Ŭlyu)

14th (Same 16th)
◇ The sixteen-foot statue of Hwangnyongsa Temple was cast (Silla Chinhŭng Wang Hongje 3rd year, Kapo)
◇ Lunar eclipse occurred (Sukjong Kisa)

15th (Same 17th)
◇ Yejong sat in Saru and had civil servants compose poems about peonies (17th year, Imjin)

16th (Same 18th)
◇ Ungju Military Governor Kim Hŏn-ch'ang rebelled, calling the country Chang'an and establishing the era name as Kyŏngwŏn (Silla Hŏntŏk Wang 14th year, Imjin)
◇ Yi Ch'un-o submitted a memorial impeaching Sin Ton and was demoted to Changsa (Koryŏ Kongmin Wang 15th year, Pyŏng-o)

17th (Same 19th)
◇ Emperor Chongzhen of Ming committed suicide (This is Emperor Chongzhen, Injio Kapshin)

18th (Same 20th)
◇ The Grand Penal Code was newly promulgated (Kwangmu 9th year)
◇ The letter of Tsushima Island Governor of Japan, So Yoshitada, arrived again (Yi T'aewang Imshin)
◇ Queen Consort Chongsun's tomb was posthumously designated as Sŭrung (Sukjong Kimyo)

19th (Same 21st)
◇ King Ŭijong of Koryŏ held a banquet at Puyŏngnu in Sŏgyŏng (西京浮碧樓)

20th (Same 22nd)
◇ Red bandit ships invaded various ports in the West Sea (Koryŏ Kongmin Kyŏngja)
◇ Regulations on Foreign Travel Tickets were promulgated (Kwangmu 11th year)
◇ Implementation of the disposal of state-owned forests (Yunghŭi 2nd year)

21st (Same 23rd)
◇ King Kim Suro of Karakguk passed away (aged 158)

22nd (Same 24th)
◇ Koguryŏ forces heavily defeated Sui forces at the Liao River, killing their general Maikch'ŏljang (Yŏngyang Wang 23rd year, Imshin)
◇ The King of Koryŏ, Simyang, built Man'gwŏndang in Yanjing (Kyeshuk)

23rd (Same 25th)
◇ Government troops heavily defeated Yi In-jwa's army at Chuksan (Yŏngjo Musin)

24th (Same 26th)
◇ The Jin ruler Aguda sent a letter addressing him as a brother (Koryŏ Yejong Chŏngyu)

25th (Same 27th)
◇ Yu Kyŏng and others executed Ch'oe Ŭi and restored royal rule (Koguryŏ Kojong Mu-o)

26th (Same 28th)
◇ The case of Hŏ Kyŏn (so-called Kyŏngshin Great Treason) began (Sukjong 6th year, Kyŏngshin)

27th (Same 29th)
◇ Sui invaders besieged Liaodong (Koryŏ Yŏngyang Wang Imshin)

29th (Same April 1st)
◇ Chŏng Il-tu Yŏ-ch'ang was given death by poison at the Chongsong exile site (Yŏnsan Kapja)
◇ Yi Ch'ungmu-gong was slandered, dismissed, and joined the army as a common soldier (Sŏnjo Chŏngyu)

30th (Same April 2nd)
◇ Yi T'aejo fell from his horse in Haeju and returned to his residence (Koryŏ Kongyang Wang Imshin)
◇ Chief Recruitment Officer Hong Kye-hun headed to Chŏnju to persuade the Tonghak Party (Yi T'aejo Kap-o)

May

1st (Lunar April 3rd)
◇ Five American warships arrived at Inch'ŏn Port (Yi T'aejo Sinmi)

2nd (Same 4th)
◇ Chŏng P'oŭn Mong-ju was killed by Cho Yŏng-gyu at Sŏnjukgyo Bridge (Yi T'aejo 1st year, Imshin)
◇ The Japanese in Samp'o started a rebellion and invaded Chep'o (Chungjong Kyŏng-o)
◇ Chargé d'affaires to the UK, Yi Han-ŭng, committed suicide in sorrow and anger (Kwangmu 7th year)

3rd (Same 5th)
◇ Government troops were defeated by Japanese soldiers in Ungju (Chungjong Kyŏng-o)
◇ The public trial of Yi Chae-myŏng, who stabbed Prime Minister Yi Wan-yong, began (Yunghŭi 4th year, Kyŏngsul)

4th (Same 6th)
◇ Eastern Nüzhen offered 117,000 arrows of K'osi (Koryŏ Hyŏnjong Kyŏng-o)

5th (Same 7th)
◇ Sui Emperor Yang Guang invaded Liaodong but was defeated and returned (Koguryŏ Yŏngyang Wang Kapsul)
◇ Yi Yŏ-song moved troops from P'yŏngyang to Kaesŏng (Sŏnjo Kyesa)

6th (Same 8th)
◇ Yi T'ae-wang entered the Kirim Society (Kwangmu 6th year, Imjin)

7th (Same 9th)
◇ Yŏngyu submitted a memorial on righteousness (Chŏngjo Imja)

8th (Same 10th)
◇ Chungjong sat in Kŭnjŏngjŏn and personally tested the literati (Chungjong Kimyo)
◇ Yun Kwan defeated the Nüzhen and returned in triumph (Koryŏ Yejong 3rd year)
◇ The Korea-Russia Treaty was abolished (Kwangmu 8th year)
◇ Yi Chae-myŏng was sentenced to death (Yunghŭi 4th year)

9th (Same 11th)
◇ P'u Pun-nu defeated the Xianbei state and made it a vassal state (Koguryŏ Yuri Wang Imja)
◇ Crown Prince Ch'ŏn went to Yuan (Koryŏ Kojong Kimi)
◇ Bank of Japan notes were first used in Inch'ŏn and Pusan

10th (Same 12th)
◇ The Queen issued an edict promoting female education (Yunghŭi 2nd year)

11th (Same 13th)
◇ Pusan Military Commissioner Chŏng Pal died a martyr (Sŏnjo Imjin)
◇ The Korea-America Treaty was exchanged (Yi T'ae-wang Kye-mi)

12th (Same 14th)
◇ Tongnae Magistrate Song Sang-hyŏn died a martyr (Sŏnjo Imjin)
◇ The Korea-England Treaty was exchanged (Kae 493)

13th (Same 15th)
◇ The people of Iksan caused unrest due to the Tugyŏlsa (Ch'ŏljong Im Sul)

14th (Same 16th)
◇ An Yun-dŏk led troops from three provinces to pursue and suppress the Japanese in Samp'o (Chungjong Kyŏng-o)
◇ The Korea-America Treaty was concluded (Kae 491)

15th (Same 17th)
◇ Japanese soldiers invaded and captured Miryang, and Pak Chin escaped to report it

16th (Same 18th)
◇ Government troops defeated Hong Kyŏng-nae's rebel army and recovered Chŏngju Castle (Sunjo Imshin)
◇ The Chogyŏng Altar was built in Chŏnju (Kwangmu 3rd year)
◇ Regulations on the return of shrine lands were promulgated (Kae 490)

17th (Same 19th)
◇ Scholars O Tal-je and Yun Ch'ŭp-nyang died as martyrs in Shenyang (Injio Chŏngch'uk)
◇ Ŏ Chung-gun Chae-yŏn fought American soldiers on Kanghwa Island (Yi T'ae-wang Sinmi)
◇ The 1st Regiment of the Royal Guard was organized (Kwangmu 2nd year)

18th (Same 20th)
◇ Ch'oe Yun-dŏk met with Yi Man-ju and combined forces (Sejo Muja)
◇ Government troops recovered Hansŏng (Sŏnjo Kyesa)

19th (Same 21st)
◇ Queen Hyŏndŏk (Queen Kwon of Munjong), Sorŭng, was restored (Chungjong Kyeshuk)
◇ The Yuan Marshal of the Jin Dynasty, Yu Kŏ-ha, came to surrender (Koryŏ Kojong Chŏngch'uk)

20th (Same 22nd)
◇ General Kwak Chae-u, the Red-Clad General, raised a righteous army (Sŏnjo Imjin)
◇ The Korea-France Treaty was exchanged (Kae 495)

21st (Same 23rd)
◇ Inspector Yi Yo-hŏn pacified Hong Kyŏng-nae's troops and returned with his army (Sunjo Imshin)
◇ Local Financial Association Regulations were promulgated (Kwangmu 11th year)

22nd (Same 24th)
◇ The Queen visited the Suwŏn Sericulture Farm (Yunghŭi 3rd year)

23rd (Same 25th)
◇ Pak T'ae-bo, O Tu-in, and others submitted memorials opposing the deposition of the Queen and were interrogated (Sukjong Kisa)

24th (Same 26th)
◇ Ch'oe Yun-dŏk defeated Yi Man-ju at P'ajŏ River (Sejong Imja)

25th (Same 27th)
◇ Sin Rip died in battle at T'angŭmdai in Ch'ungju (Sŏnjo Imjin)
◇ Scholar Yi U of Yŏngnam was summoned to Hŭijŏngdang (Injio Imja)

26th (Same 28th)
◇ News arrived that An Yun-dŏk had suppressed the Samp'o Japanese Rebellion (Chungjong Kyŏng-o)
◇ Kim Pang-gyŏng suppressed the Sambyŏlch'o Rebellion (Koryŏ Wŏnjong Kyeshuk)
◇ The Tonghak Army captured Chŏnju (Yi T'ae-hwang Kap-o)

27th (Same 29th)
◇ Kim Hak-pong Sŏng-il passed away in the Chinju military camp (Sŏnjo Kyesa)
◇ Kim In-mun passed away in Tang (Silla Hyosog Wang Kyesa)

28th (Lunar May 1st)
◇ Sŏnjo resided in Kaesŏng (Imjin)
◇ Yi Sun-sin launched warships for the first time (Same as above)

29th (Same 2nd)
◇ The Equal Tax Law was implemented (Yŏngjo Sinmi)
◇ Queen Inhyŏn Min was demoted to her private residence (Sukjong Kisa)

30th (Same 3rd)
◇ Japanese soldiers invaded and captured Kyŏngsŏng (Sŏnjo Imjin)
◇ Qing warships arrived at Asan (Yi T'ae-wang Kap-o)
◇ Korea-Japan Fishing Zone Regulations promulgated (Kwangmu 8th year)

31st (Same 4th)
◇ Sent the monk Yuk-yŏn to start firing roof tiles in Kanghwa (Koryŏ Ch'ungyŏl Wang Chŏngch'uk)

June

1st (Lunar May 5th)
◇ King Kungye of T'aebong was born (Silla Hŏnan Wang Muin)
◇ Sin Kak engaged in battle with the Japanese army at Yangju Haehyŏn and achieved victory (Sŏnjo Imjin)
◇ The so-called Military Police Auxiliary were recruited from today (Yunghŭi 2nd year)

2nd (Same 6th)
◇ Qing soldiers landed at Asan
◇ Implemented the Gyŏljŏn Law and the Salt and Fish Boat Tax Law (Yŏngjo Sinmi)

3rd (Same 7th)
◇ Admiral Yi Sun-sin burned and destroyed twenty Japanese ships at Okp'o

4th (Same 8th)
◇ The Korea-Russia Trade Treaty was agreed upon (Yi T'ae-wang Kapsin)
◇ The Cabinet System was promulgated (Kwangmu 11th year)
◇ The Tonghak Army was defeated by the government army and retreated from Chŏnju (Yi T'ae-wang Kapsin)

5th (Same 9th)
◇ Koguryŏ troops made a major attack on Pukhansansŏng but retreated without success (Silla Muryŏl Wang Shinyu)

6th (Same 10th)
◇ On this day, rain fell, which was called T'aejong Rain

7th (Same 11th)
◇ Yu Kŭng-nyang died in battle at Imjin (Sŏnjo Imjin)
◇ King Ch'ungyŏl welcomed the daughter of Kublai Khan, Emperor Shizu of Yuan, to be his queen (Koryŏ Wŏnjong Kapsul)

8th (Same 12th)
◇ Granted special permission for Han River and Daedong River hydroelectric business (Kwangmu 10th year)
◇ The County Magistrate Appointment Order was promulgated (Yunghŭi 2nd year)

10th (Same 14th)
◇ The Korea-France Trade Treaty was agreed upon (Kae 495)
◇ The Police Bureau was abolished (Yunghŭi 4th year)

11th (Same 15th)
◇ Boundary markers were erected on Paektusan Mountain (Sukjong Imjin)
◇ The Command Headquarters and various military camps were abolished (Yi T'ae-wang Ŭlmi)

12th (Same 16th)
◇ When Sim Chŏng became the Minister of Justice, on this day, earthquakes struck eight provinces, causing city walls and houses to collapse (Chungjong Muin)
◇ Yi T'aejong was born in Hamhŭng Gwiju-dong (Koryŏ Kongmin Wang Chŏngmi)

13th (Same 17th)
◇ The Korea-Austria Trade Treaty was established (Kae 510 Sinch'uk)

14th (Same 18th)
◇ Commander of the Northeast Military Region, Yu In-u, recovered Ssangsŏng and other states (Koryŏ Kongmin Wang Pyŏngshin)

15th (Same 19th)
◇ Kolpul-an sold the Electric Railway Company (predecessor of the Kyŏngsŏng Electric Company) to Japan

16th (Same 20th)
◇ A Japanese envoy arrived (Silla Adalla Wang Kapin)
◇ The Korea-Belgium Trade Treaty was agreed upon (Kae 493)

17th (Same 21st)
◇ The Conscription Act was promulgated (Kwangmu 11th year)
◇ A memorial service for 160 patriotic figures of the Reform Party between Kapsin and Kapchin was held at the Training Institute (Yunghŭi 2nd year)

18th (Same 22nd)
◇ Yi T'aejo retreated from Wihwa Island (Sin U 14th year)
◇ The Hague incident involving Yi Chun, Yi Sang-sŏl, etc., was discovered (Kwangmu 12th year)

19th (Same 23rd)
◇ The Ministry of Finance was placed under the advisory of a Japanese financial advisor (Kwangmu 9th year)
◇ The Immigration Regulations and Mining Law were promulgated (Same 10th year)

20th (Same 24th)
◇ Yi T'aejo passed away (T'aejong Muja)
◇ Chŏng Chae-hong, the principal of Inch'ŏn Yŏnghwa School, wrote Eight Transformation Songs and a will in resentment of the current situation and committed suicide at the welcome party for Pak Yŏng-hyo at the Training Institute (Kwangmu 11th year)
◇ The Royal Guard was newly established (Yi T'ae-wang Ŭlmi)

21st (Same 25th)
◇ Due to the proposal of Pae Kŭng-nyŏm, King Kongyang was deposed and imprisoned in Wŏnju (Yi T'aejo 1st year, Kapsin)
◇ Japanese stamps and postcards were first used in Korea (Kwangmu 9th year)

22nd (Same 26th)
◇ King Muryŏl of Silla personally invaded Paekche, leading Kim Yu-sin and others (Kyŏngshin)

23rd (Same 27th)
◇ Regulations on the sale of textbooks were promulgated

24th (Same 28th)
◇ The Office of Meritorious Subjects was first established (Kwangmu 3rd year)
◇ The Law on the Utilization of State-Owned Uncultivated Land was promulgated (Same 11th year)

25th (Same 29th)
◇ Yi Sun-sin heavily defeated Japanese soldiers at Noryang (Sŏnjo Imjin)
◇ King T'aejo of Koryŏ passed away (Same King 26th year, Kemyo)

26th (Same 30th)
◇ Detailed rules for the implementation of state-owned uncultivated land reclamation were promulgated (Kwangmu 11th year)

27th (Lunar June 1st)
◇ Yi Sun-sin crushed Japanese warships with turtle ships in Saryang, achieving a great victory

28th (Same 2nd)
◇ The Korean Language Research Institute was established

29th (Same 3rd)
◇ General Ch'oe Yŏng was exiled to the County of Kopong (Koyang) (Sin U 14th year)

30th (Same 4th)
◇ Yi Saek was imprisoned in Ch'ŏngju Prison but was released due to a flood and natural disaster (Koryŏ Kongmin Wang Kyŏng-o)
◇ The transfer of judicial power and the abolition of the military department were agreed upon (Yunghŭi 3rd year)

31st (Same 5th)
◇ Mongol soldiers invaded (Koryŏ Kojong Pyŏngshin)
◇ In Tang crossed the Amnok River and destroyed Pasap'u (Kongmin Pyŏngshin)

◇樂浪原에서大獵하다(高句麗風俗)
