별건곤 제30호
1930년 07월 01일
(創作) 歸鄕(下) (Creative Work: Homecoming (Part 2))
兪鎭午 (Yu Chin-o)
소설 (Novel)

Yu Chin-o
12
-Is she there?
I cheerfully climbed the stairs and flung open S's door. But neither S nor Sadako was there. Sadako might have gone to someone else in the meantime, but where could S have gone?

Anyway, I decided to wait for S to return. I had to meet S to find out where Sadako was.

On S's desk were reference books and old newspapers, scattered haphazardly, which he was using for his thesis. I sat down at the desk and picked up the manuscript of his paper, which had been pushed aside. "How can colonies and semi-colonies under imperialism be XX - especially based on the realistic problems of XX?" The long-titled paper was almost finished. I thought about the contrast between S's neat appearance and his meticulous writing. In fact, S was clever. There were no contradictions in his words, and once he opened his mouth, he wouldn't stop, examining every aspect in detail without missing anything. His thesis was of that kind.

- But - I thought, flipping through the thesis. - Unfortunately, S is a man who has lost his strength due to heartbreak.

Perhaps there was some jealousy in my thoughts about S's brilliant brain. Also, an unpleasant feeling about the fact that Sadako was closer to S than anyone else. But anyway, S was definitely that type of person.

Unlike S usually, clothes he had taken off when going out were scattered around the room. I put the manuscript back on the desk and looked around the room, and I happened to see a woman's clothes in the corner, also thrown haphazardly. It was definitely Sadako's jacket. The one she wore the night we left for Tokyo together from the countryside. <159> My suspicion grew to infinity in an instant. Unpleasant feelings turned into anger, emitting red flames.

- Where have they gone!
But no matter how long I waited, neither S nor Sadako returned. Nine o'clock, ten o'clock, eleven o'clock... Time passed slowly and mercilessly in no time. What on earth was going on? Of course, when I entrusted Sadako to my fellow students in Tokyo and went to the countryside, I had some degree of awareness. But what I firmly believed beyond all that was that my fellow students were not just fellow students, and that Sadako was also not just my sweetheart or "de facto wife." What if they shook my conviction?

Whether Sadako was in love with someone or S took Sadako away from me was not a significant issue. If they didn't break the bond as comrades... But could such a relationship actually be established? Could the love affair with S - assuming that Sadako was in love with S now - perhaps be the first step in Sadako's depravity?

A little after twelve o'clock, I heard the sound of the front door sliding open. Then I heard the cheerful footsteps of two people coming up the stairs. I reflexively closed my open eyes.

- I don't like Lillian Gish at all.
- Then do you like Mary Pickford?
The two people opened the door while talking about the popular movie stars of the time.
- Ara!
That was Sadako's surprised voice.
- Oh - isn't that Mr. Kim?
- Huh?
I opened my eyes like someone waking up from sleep and looked at the two people.
- What's going on? You waited in vain last Saturday. Did you have something urgent to do?
Sadako, wearing a brown new suit, was standing in the middle of the room without sitting down. The suit didn't seem that expensive to look at, but it looked very neat, perhaps because brown was the fashionable color at the time. I uttered something different from what I was thinking.
- Yeah, I was a little busy, so I couldn't come. But it's worth being busy. Wait and see. See how much fruit the seeds I sowed will bear in a little while.
- We are "seed sowers." - Sadako said, looking at my eyes that were only scanning her appearance up and down - Tonight, I won the S Prize, so I went to see a movie at the M Hall. It was really fun.
I felt so much anger that I couldn't stand it anymore.
- That suit? <160>
I cast a sharp gaze. Sadako looked at S's face as if appealing for something.
- This suit? I bought it for her. She couldn't get through the winter with just one set of clothes she brought from the countryside. I thought a suit would be cheaper than a kimono, so I bought this. How about it? Does it suit Sadako-ssi well? Huh? It suits her very well.
I had more questions I wanted to ask, but I held back. What was I doing? What right did I have to ask such things?

That night, Sadako and I waited until late without going to sleep, waiting for S to fall asleep. But S didn't fall asleep easily, tossing and turning repeatedly.
- Sadako-jjang, do you sleep in this room even when S sleeps alone?
- Yes, so what?
Sadako caressed me much more skillfully than before. At least, that's how it seemed to me. S and Sadako, K and Sada C? P? I couldn't sleep because of the unpleasant images that kept popping up. If you think about it, it was obvious.

13
The N-jeong Paper Mill Labor Union held its founding ceremony publicly on February 11, choosing the holiday of Foundation Day. Many policemen attended the ceremony, but it ended safely under the control of "Asano."
But after that ended, our work became even busier. We worked day and night, taking every opportunity to gather and aim for the formation of occupational labor unions in each factory in N-jeong and the establishment of the All-Japan Labor Union N-jeong Branch.
In the meantime, I went back and forth to Tokyo more frequently than before whenever I had time. But the more I did, the more Sadako seemed to change, and my agony deepened accordingly. However, I had never heard rumors or witnessed Sadako doing anything wrong. It was just that her behavior and speech were losing the simple sincerity of a country girl day by day and becoming sleek from being immersed in the city, which was clearly visible on the outside. At that rate, it would be difficult for Sadako to become a good worker. "Asano"'s misunderstanding might not be a misunderstanding but a correct assessment.

Sadako didn't pay much attention to the process of forming a labor union, which I tried to tell her interestingly. Instead, she asked me to go for a walk to places like "Ginza" or "Asakusa." When I refused,
- How can a person just work and live? Shouldn't you play sometimes?
And she would pout her usual lips. I listened to her request several times and walked around the bright streets of "Ginza," where "parks" were already filled, <161> and looked into places like the park theater in "Asakusa." By then, Sadako had almost completely become a woman who enjoyed the bustle of the city. She looked at the colorful items displayed in the glass windows of the Ginza shops and hummed a verse of a popular song.
- ねえ, 여보세요 (Nē, yoboseyo. Hey, look). Isn't that pretty?
- What?
- That, that I'm talking about.
At the tip of Sadako's pointing finger, a colorful Japanese woman's sash was shining.
- What's so great about that! Isn't it because one woman wears that sash that hundreds of female workers strip themselves bare to weave it?
- That's true, but...
Several times, I thought about ending the increasingly unpleasant relationship with Sadako. But I was still an animal of lust. Sadako's body had developed much better than when she came from the countryside, and accordingly, her face and figure didn't fail to excite a twenty-three-year-old man.
- Sadako! Why are you becoming more and more reckless? しつかり (Shikkari. Behave yourself)!
- Why, what's wrong with me? 호... (Ho...). You're just being silly.
- It's not being silly. From now on, don't just play around like that and go to work at a factory.
- Who said I wouldn't go? There has to be a position. Get me a position.
- Anyway, leave S's or M's house! - I was imagining the two people most unpleasantly at that time.
- 호... (Ho...). You're just being jealous for no reason. Do you think of me as such a woman?
February passed, and March came. March passed again, and April came. Finally, everything we had planned in N-jeong was done. In fact, several people had been dispatched from the headquarters to various places, but there was no other place that had succeeded like N-jeong. I heard words of praise from the headquarters. My fellow students praised my activities. But my heart was heavy. How should I deal with Sadako's problem? Even when the warm wind blew in April and flowers bloomed, it didn't feel like spring had come at all.
Days of depression came and went.

14
It was almost the end of April when I left the paper mill in N-jeong and returned to Tokyo. Now, I couldn't live carelessly like before. I went to my former teacher, Mr. T, and first went to work as a deliveryman for a soy sauce store, and Sadako decided to go to a cotton-fluffing factory in "Fukagawa." We rented a small room in the back alley of "Fukagawa" and started living together.

We went out in the early morning and came back in the evening. I didn't intend to make Sadako stop working at night from the beginning. We were going to find time at night to do our own work somehow.
But our livelihood was not very satisfactory. Sadako now openly expressed her dissatisfaction with the difficult life.
- How long will we have to suffer like this?
- Until we are happy while we live. Didn't you know this from the beginning?
- But who knew it would be like this? 아이구 신신찬어 (Aigu sinsinchan'eo! Oh, my miserable self!)!
Sadako's lips were pouting again.
- Sadako! Then what do you want to do? Is it because we are interested in this work or because it is right that we are doing this work now? Aren't we doing this because we can't help but do it?
- Who doesn't know that? It's just that it's so hard to live. You carry soy sauce bottles around day and night, and I inhale cotton fluff day and night. Is that a sign that we will live happily?
Sadako knew enough to reason, but she still repeated these small complaints. Eventually, it became a habit, and her lips were always pouting day and night. My forehead was also furrowed as soon as I entered the room. Even so, the world of politics unfolded every day under the dim five-candle lamp as the night deepened. Sadako's long, closed eyebrows trembled faintly like petals of a poisonous flower.
Sometimes S came to visit. S talked about how his previously published thesis had caused a huge wave of controversy. He brought fruit and also helped with money. But he usually gave those things on days when I wasn't home. I was very busy working with my fellow students from Joseon at that time, so there were many nights when I wasn't home. When I came home late on such days, fruit peels were scattered haphazardly on the torn "tatami," and Sadako was sleeping soundly in the middle of the room like a fallen soldier. When I went to sleep, pushed the fruit peels aside, and covered myself with a blanket next to her,
- Ara! 오가에리 (Ogaeri! Welcome home)!
And then she would open her eyes wide.
- What is this messy state!
- Mr. S bought fruit and came, waited for you, and couldn't wait any longer, so he left. Why, is it wrong to eat a piece of fruit at the end of a day of hunger? <163>
I immediately feel the urge to slap Sadako's cheek. But then Sadako's long eyebrows and red lips speak. In just a few minutes, I fall into a deep depression and then into weary sleep.

15
As each day passed, I felt more deeply that I had to part with Sadako. Now, I had no face to meet her older brother. Now that things had come to this, how should I justify bringing Sadako to Tokyo?
How could I prove that I didn't deceive a naive country girl who longed for the bustle of the city with sweet words and bring her to Tokyo?
- It's a mistake. Everything is my mistake.
Even so, I didn't think about going to "Asano." Wait a little longer. Then Sadako will be okay somehow.

In early May, at that time, the work we had planned was finally becoming concrete, so I took the train every night and gathered with my comrades at K's boarding house in "Soshigaya" to discuss it until late. I continued to do that for about a week.
That night as well, I was extremely tired and took the last train with a red light on it and returned to our den in "Fukagawa." The fruit peels were still scattered in the room, and Sadako had collapsed soundly. Somehow, her skin seemed very rough.
- It's a gift from working at the factory.
I became very sentimental and held Sadako's hand. I was newly surprised to feel that her palms had become thick and her nails were calloused haphazardly. But at the same time, I saw something unexpected. On the ring finger of her left hand, there was a humble but red gemstone ring!
I shook Sadako roughly to wake her up. She opened her eyes wide and then, as usual,
- Ara! 오가에리 (Ogaeri! Welcome home)!
I suppressed my slightly trembling voice and opened my mouth in the most solemn way.
- Where did you get this ring?
Sadako touched the ring like lightning and clearly showed a look of surprise on her face.
- Why are you doing this? Why is it a problem that I'm wearing this?
- Who bought it for you?
- Don't look at me with such scary eyes. I don't like it.
Suddenly, Sadako started to cry, sobbing. Then, no matter how much I asked, she didn't say a word. In one corner of my heart, a feeling of pity for her arose like a storm. But on the other hand, I heard a voice saying, "This is an opportunity! Don't miss the opportunity and liquidate your life!" What should I do? <164>
- Did S buy it for you?
- No. I guarantee that. Mr. S didn't give it to me.
- Then where did it come from?
Sadako had stopped crying, but she still didn't answer.
- What did you swear the night you left the countryside? What did we say to your older brother's misunderstanding?
- But please think about it. I really wanted to have something like this!
Sadako's answer was definitely telling her true feelings. Her growing environment was her environment. Her age was her age. Whatever she was thinking when she first came to Tokyo, who could guarantee that there was no corner in her heart that longed for the city? But at that time, I didn't have room to think about such things one by one. The unpleasant thoughts that had accumulated over the past few months burst out at once. Not only that, but Sadako's life was definitely on the path to depravity. What could I do to stop her from being in love with another man? But he had been rapidly clouding his consciousness since I went to N-jeong. Now, even if he reluctantly attends the research group, he has no enthusiasm at all. Sometimes he makes mistakes that are in the wrong direction, which rather confuses our meeting. He makes my life sluggish and directly or indirectly controls my activities. Decide! Decide!
- Sadako! If that's really the case, then we have no choice but to break up. I believed that you would become a good worker for us. But you are no different from the daughters of the half-baked, depraved proletariat, so we have no choice but to break up cleanly right here and now! You go wherever you want to go! I will go wherever I want to go. You go and get a gemstone ring, a gold ring, or a silk dress.
My voice became more and more excited and loud as it went to the end. Sadako is sleeping.
- It was my mistake that I misjudged you and brought you to Tokyo. But I have repeatedly agonized over my mistakes enough to make up for them.
I got up from my seat. Suddenly, Sadako rushed and grabbed my pants.
- Please forgive me. Please forgive me. I was wrong. I won't do it again. Please don't go. Please sit down.
- There's nothing to be wrong about. Whatever you've been doing where I can't see you or who that ring came from, those things don't matter. Anyway, you are not helping my work and are hindering it. That's all.
I shook her off and jumped out of the room. I heard Sadako crying, "Wa-". I was wrapped in a joy that felt like I had liquidated my mistakes, a demonic pleasure at my cruel actions, and a guilt complex as if I had committed some sin, and my toes felt as heavy as lead. <165>
- But there's no choice! What's done is done.

In late May, I was XX and returned to my homeland. The day before I was XX, I visited "Asano" after a long time. If I think about it, perhaps there was some pre-, sentimentality at that time. "Asano" greeted me with a very unpleasant look.
- I hear you abandoned my sister now. How about it? Was it no longer fun?
He is still misunderstanding!
- Is that all you're thinking? It's a pity that you don't understand it that way!
- What did I say in the first place?
He looked sharply into my eyes. My chest ached.
- Still, I think I acted in a way that I wasn't ashamed of in my conscience.
- Conscience, huh! - Then he showed a bitter look and said - If you think about it, it's no one's fault! Anyway, do you know what happened to your Sadako now?
I didn't know what had happened to Sadako after that. Three days after the fight and breakup, I went to our den, but Sadako was already gone.
- I heard from S that she is now quietly making a "love nest" with the son of a bourgeois called "Kondo-" -
According to "Asano"'s story, the relationship between Sadako and "Kondo-" had started quite a while ago. I found out that the suit incident sometime was actually bought by "Kondo-," and S had said that he bought it himself to protect Sadako's secret.
- S came the other day and told me that while you were running around like crazy, S was also quite misunderstood, so he scratched his head.
My face flushed. I grabbed "Asano"'s hand.
- Asano! Forgive me. For unnecessarily suspecting S-gun, you, and other fellow students.
- I guessed Sadako's path from the beginning. There's nothing for you to be wrong about either. But think about it. What made that woman do that? We can't just see it as a small mistake of a young girl!
I returned to my hometown. Sitting in a solitary room of less than one pyeong, looking at the blue sky of my hometown through the iron window, a stream of past thoughts mixed with pain and anger came to mind. Whenever that happens, I think.
- What made that woman do that?
Isn't the path that Sadako took her first step on a path that the daughters of the proletariat in all countries are falling into? - 30. 4 - <166>

-S잇나?
나는 경쾌하게 층다리를 올나가 S의 방문을 열어 젓치엇다. 그러나 그곳에는 S도 사다꼬도 업섯다. 사다꼬는 그동안 또 다른 사람에게로 갓는지도 몰으지만 S는 어듸를 갓슬가.
하여간 나는 S의 도라오기를 기다리기로 하엿다. S를 맛나야 사다꼬의 잇는 곳도 알 수 잇는 것이다.
S의 책상에는 그가 쓰고 잇는 론문의 참고서와 헌 신문 가튼 것이 란잡하게 흣터저 잇섯다. 나는 책상 압헤 안저 한편으로 치어 논고의 원고를 들어보앗다. 「제국주의 하의 식민지급 반식민지는 여하히 하야 XX될 것인가 - 특히 XX의 현실적 제 문제에 립각하야」라는 긴 제목을 가진 그 론문은 거의 끗헤 니르럿섯다. 나는 S의 말숙한 몸맵시와 그의 밋긋한 글시를 대조해 생각하엿다. 사실 S는 재사엿섯다. 그의 말에는 모순이 업고 한번 입을 열면 끈칠 줄을 몰으고 모든 방면을 자상하게 뻬놋치 안코 고찰하는 것이엿다. 그의 론문도 그러한 종류의 것이엇다.
- 그러나 - 하고 나는 론문을 뒤적어리며 생각하엿다. - 앗갑게도 S는 실련을 당해 힘이 업는 사람이다.
나의 그 생각 속에는 얼마간 S의 총명한 두뇌에 대한 싀긔가 잇섯는지도 몰은다. 또 사다꼬가 다른 누구보다도 S와 각갑게 지내는 사실에 대한 불쾌한 감정도. 그러나 하여간 S는 그런 타입의 사람임에는 틀님업는 것이다.

- 이들이 어듸를 갓슬가!
그러나 아모리 기달녀도 S도 사다꼬도 도라오지 안이하엿다. 아홉시 열시 열한시... ... 시간은 느리게 또 어느 결에 사정업시 지나갓다. 대관절 이 일이 엇지된 일일가. 물론 사다꼬를 동경에다 더구나 사내 동모들에게 맛겨버리고 싀골로 갈 때에는 나도 엇던 정도의 깨다름이 잇섯든 것이다. 그러나 그 모든 것을 초월하야 내가 굿게 미덧든 것은 나의 동모들이 단순한 동모가 안이고 사다꼬가 역시 단순한 나의 스위트허-트 또는 「내연의 처」가 안이라는 것이엇다. 만일 그들이 나의 이 확신을 흔든다면?
사다꼬가 누구하고 련애를 한다거나 또는 S가 나에게로부터 사다꼬를 빼서간다거나 그것은 그리 중한 문제는 안인 것이다. 만일 그들이 동지로서의 매즘을 깨트리지만 안이 한다면 그러나 이러한 관게가 실제로 성립될 수 잇슬가? S와의 련애 - 지금 사다꼬가 S와 련애하고 잇다고 가정하고 - 가 혹시 사다꼬의 타락의 제일보가 되지는 안이할가?
열두시가 조금 지낫슬 때 현관문을 드르륵 여는 소리가 낫다. 이어 층다리를 올나오는 긔운 조흔 두 사람의 발소리가 들엿다. 나는 떳든 눈을 반사적으로 스르르 감엇다.

- 아라!
