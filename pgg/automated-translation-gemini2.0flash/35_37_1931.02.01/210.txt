Job Hunting Front Has Anomalies!
Pale Cultural Reserve Force

There are 20 million unemployed people worldwide. 20 million unemployed!
Who made them unemployed?
How many unemployed people are there in Korea?
Korea is far behind advanced capitalist countries in its stage of social development. However, the unemployment rate is higher than in any other capitalist country, and Korea's unemployed population is close to 23 million.

However, the fact that there are 20 million unemployed people in the world and that almost all of Korea's farmers and laborers are unemployed refers to the true proletariat.
In addition to this proletariat (narrow sense), there is a large unemployed class, namely the intellectual class.

Regardless of the right or wrong of it, the fervor for learning has already become very high in Korea.
The "patriots" taught the people that "knowing is living well."
Many common schools and training centers were established, as well as middle schools and vocational schools.
Korea's poor people dug up the land and gave up meals to send their children to school—hoping they would live better.
However, Korea's culture was too barren and poor to absorb the intellectual groups that had already been cultivated.
The number of farmers' children who finish common school and enter secondary schools is only a few percent. <84>
Again, the number of those from that small percentage who finish secondary school and go on to vocational school or university is also only a few percent.
So, where do those who have only finished common school, possessing only elementary knowledge, go?
Where do most of the middle school graduates with only a little common sense, and most of those who cannot find jobs after finishing vocational school or university, go?
The glorious afterglow of the intellectual class of the feudal era has been mercilessly trampled upon by liberalism and faded into white, and in capitalist culture, they too are left empty-handed, becoming mere laborers who say, "Give me food."
Moreover, unlike the proletarians who wield hammers and try to XX XXXXX, they have no tools, no energy, and only decorate themselves with pale faces and hungry looks.
Therefore, we call them the pale cultural reserve force.
Like the proletarians, they are all slaves of the capitalists. XX is like that, and those who discuss the world are like that, scholars are like that, and everyone is like that, even down to a single bank clerk.
Like the industrial reserve army, the cultural reserve army grows without limit. However, the modern production system does not allow them to be absorbed as they grow. Rather, it strangles all industries. As a result, the unemployment of laborers increases, and as cultural institutions decrease, the cultural unemployment also increases.
Inevitably, like the industrial reserve army, they have become lost wandering the streets in search of food.
On the streets, or among the intellectual class who are still unaware of the ways of the world, various tragicomedies are played out here in order to obtain jobs. Here are a few examples.

Six Copies of a Literary Scholar's Resume
A confidential letter arrived to Mr. XXX, who occupies a considerably important position in XXXXX. The letter stated:
"I am graduating this spring. My family is waiting with bated breath, as if I will bring back a star from the sky when I graduate. In a society where crowds are clamoring, raising blackened hands to find even a scrap of food, it makes me cry to think that even someone like me has to join them for the sake of eating, but what can I do if I don't jump in like that and end up with cobwebs in my mouth? (omitted) If, by some stroke of luck, you could arrange a position for me..."
Enclosed were six copies of his resume.
Looking through the resume, it shows that he graduated from common school and is scheduled to graduate from XX university in Japan this year. <85>
And his major is literature. That is, he is a literary scholar (文學士).
Literary scholar! Literary scholar! Hungry literary scholar! Who made them hungry?

Carrying a Blanket
Mr. Song Jin-u (宋鎭禹), president of the Dong-a Ilbo (東亞日報), shook his head wearily and told the following story.
The person who came to see him was Mr. X, with whom he had little close acquaintance, having only met him once by chance.
Mr. X did not bring any of the usual introductions or letters of recommendation, but came to directly negotiate, the cutting edge of the job-hunting war.
Of course, it would be good if Song could introduce and employ hundreds, thousands, or even tens of thousands of people and help them find work, but Song simply did not have the power to do so.
So, Song explained in various ways that his circumstances did not allow it and gave a difficult refusal. Then, that very night, Mr. X came to Song's house carrying a blanket.
He intended to be fed and housed until he got a job or until Song helped him get one. Stunned by Mr. X's extremely cutting-edge "job-hunting" tactics, Song could only stand there speechless. In fact, he had no choice but to let him stay.
The night passed.
He thought he would leave in the morning, but there was no sign of him doing so.
A day passed. Still, he did not leave. And he kept quietly pleading.
Two days, three days...ten days passed. Still, he did not go. Eleven days, twelve days, and finally fifteen days passed.
It was only after fifteen days that Mr. X gave up. He had no choice but to retreat, but he was a person worthy of being called a brave fighter who had entered the skirmish of the job-hunting war.

New Interpretation of Sitting and Waiting for Death (坐而待死)
When people are in such a difficult situation that they cannot live, they often use the phrase "sit and wait for death" (坐而待死).
The meaning is that rather than sitting and waiting to die, they stand up and try something. That is, if they are going to die anyway, they will use their last strength and wait for the result.
However, these days, people who have set out to "find a job" are using the meaning of this "sitting and waiting for death" differently.
That is, they are waiting to die while sitting, but they are not waiting for themselves to die, but for "someone else" to die. <86> The "someone else" is a person who currently has a job. Therefore, when that person dies, that position will naturally become vacant. Then, it will be the turn of the person who has been sitting and waiting. Here is an example.
Mr. XXX, who is currently at XXXXXXXXXX, went to XXXXX for recuperation last summer.
Then, he received a letter from a classmate from his university days asking when he would return, saying that there was something urgent to discuss as soon as he returned.
Later, when Mr. X returned to Gyeongseong (京城), Mr. 厥, who had written the letter saying he had something to discuss, came to see him. He came and asked for a position at XXXX.
"As you know, I haven't been able to get a job, so it goes without saying that it's difficult to make a living, and also, because I'm just lazing around, I'm not even looking like a person..."
"Well... I would recommend you if there was a vacant position, but first of all, there is no position, and what power do I have?"
"Well... I saw in the newspaper the other day that two people at XXXX had passed away..."
Ah! What kind of heart did Mr. 厥 have that he waited for someone else to die and saw it as an opportunity, but what else is this if not sitting and waiting for death! No matter how much you stand up and struggle, there is no other way. Now it is sitting and waiting for death. Modern style sitting and waiting for death.

On the way out after obtaining the above statistical figures, I discovered an episode.
An old woman who looked to be about 50 and seemed to have just come up from the countryside.
She looked dazed and called out to me,
"Hey there," she called. Thinking she was asking for directions, I turned around.
"Yes?"
"Is this the-what is it-the place that finds people places to live?"
She clearly seemed to be looking for the Personnel Consultation Center. I replied that it was and was about to turn away when she called out again,
"Hey there."
"Yes?"
"If I talk here, will they find me a place to earn a living?"
"Well... try going inside."
"I heard in the countryside that if you just come and talk, you can get a job right away."
"I don't think it will be right away... Don't you have any sons or daughters in the countryside?"
"Why? Yes, I have a son."
"Then why don't you live with your son? Why did you come up here?"
"Because I heard that you can earn a good living here..."
"What does your son do?"
"He's a merchant."
"Then it can't be that difficult, can it?"
"Well, we just barely get by."
"Then why don't you just keep barely getting by... Did something upset you?"
Although she denied it, an unmistakable look of desolation floated across her face.
"Isn't your husband around?"
"He died last year."
"Did you fight with your daughter-in-law?"
"............."
"So your son took your daughter-in-law's side, and you got angry and upset, so you came to Seoul?"
"How do you know all that?"
"Even so, go back down. It may not happen right away even if you talk here, and even if it does happen, it's not as good as being with your children, no matter how bad it is."
The old woman hesitated for a while, but still headed towards the Personnel Consultation Center and went inside.
Who makes her become an "old mother" (오마니)? <88>

조선의 실업자는 얼마나 되는가?
조선은 그 사회적 발전단계에 잇서서 선진 자본주의국가들에게 멀니 뒤저 잇다. 그러하지만 실업자의 비율은 달은 어느 자본주의국가에 보담도 크나 조선의 실업군은 2300만에 각가웁다.
그러나 세계에 2천만의 실업자가 잇고 조선의 농민과 노동자의 거의 전부가 실업자라 하는 것은 진쌈 푸로테타리아를 말하는 것이다.
이 푸로레타리아(狹意) 이외에 한 큰 실업군의 층이 잇스니 즉 지식계급의 그것이다.

그 시비는 고사하고 이미 이후 조선에는 향학열이 벗적 놉핫다.
「志士」들은 「아는 것이 잘 사는 것」이라고 민중에게 가르켜주엇다.

농민의 자제 중에 보통학교를 맛치고 그 이상인 중등학교에 입학하는 수는 실로 멧％도 다 되지 못한다.

어릿어릿하다가 나를 보고
「여보시우」하고 부른다. 길이나 뭇는가하고 나는 도라섯다.
「네?」
「여긔가 적-무엇이냐 저-사람잇슬데 마련해주는데요?」

「여보시우」하고 부른다.
「네?」
「여긔가서 말을 하면 버리할데를 마련해쥬나요?」
「글세...들어가보십시요그려」
「시골서 듯기에는 와서 말만하면 당장 된다고 그립데다」
「그거 당장은 안될걸요...시골은 아들도 딸도 다 업슴니까?」
「웨요 잇지요 아들이 잇지요」
「그러면 아들하고 가치지내지 뭘하러 올나오섯소?」
「그래도 여기를 오면 버리가 조타고해서...」
「아들은 무얼함니까?」
「장사하지요」
「그러면 그닥지 어렵지는 안켓슴니다그려?」
「웨요 그저 근근히 살어가지요」
「그러면 그대로 근근히라도 지내시지.. 아마 무슨 화나는 일이 잇섯나봄니다그려?」
아니라고 하기는 하지만 그 얼골에는 숨길 수 업는 적막한 빗치 떠돈다.
「영감은 안게시우?」

「그래 아들도 며누리편만 들고 그래서 화도 나고 섭섭도 해서 서울로 오섯구려?」
「그말을 다 어터케합니까?」
「그러터래도 내려가십시오. 여긔가서 말을 햇자 곳 될지도 모르고 또 된다하더래도 조컨낫건내 자식한테 부터잇는것만 못함니다.」
