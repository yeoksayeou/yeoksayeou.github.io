On a Female Student's Face
Horyangdang In

Ladies and gentlemen, I will tell you a story without sin or fault... a true story.

Mr. K - he is a rather mature person for his young age. He is quiet, fond of contemplation, avoids other people's squabbles or causing trouble, and is, so to speak, a scholarly type of person. Even so, he is very fond of alcohol. However, his personality does not change from his usual self when he drinks.

There are many people who, even if they are as gentle as a bride with a clear mind, turn into a dog when they get drunk, but not our Mr. K.

Therefore, he never makes any mistakes even when he drinks.

However, Mr. K, by chance, made an unexpected mistake after drinking. Perhaps it was fate!

One day, Mr. K went out with several friends.

His body, tired from several days of work, was refreshed to be able to relax in the clean air outside.

So, Mr. K seems to have drunk a little more than his limit. Rather than exceeding his usual amount, perhaps he got drunk more easily because he drank after not drinking for a long time. However, he got on the streetcar without any problems. As soon as the streetcar started moving, he suddenly felt nauseous.

But what could he do? He held it in.

The streetcar shook more. The nausea worsened. His mind became dizzy.

Now, even Xiang Yu could not have endured it any longer. Everything in his stomach rushed up his throat.

But - but, who was sitting in front of Mr. K at that time? Who received that offering? It was a neatly dressed female student.

Ladies and gentlemen!

Now that things have come to this, even though it was an unavoidable incident, the fact that "a drunk man threw up on a female student's face in the streetcar..." was unfolding before my eyes!

What a great mistake and rude thing it was.

If Mr. K had a bad drinking habit, it might be understandable, but he certainly does not.

However, the result was so significant. However, one fortunate thing was that the female student did not show any displeasure or dislike; she simply washed her face and brushed off her clothes.

What a kind and dignified young lady!

I am not trying to make Mr. K's mistake the content of the story. I want to turn it into a harmless joke and praise the young lady's gentle heart. This is all there is to the story.

<136> The Story of Lingering in Front of a Geisha House
Baengneung

K and S were both quite drunk. When they said goodbye to the geisha H, whom they had been playing with at the entrance of T House (T館門), it was already past midnight.

The two parted ways in north and south directions - K towards the alley in I-dong (里門) and S towards Jongno (종로).

They greeted each other affectionately as they parted.

"Sleep well."

"Yeah, you too."

"Why don't you take a taxi if you can!"

"No, it's okay... Do you think I'm drunk?"

"You might not be drunk, but I'm still worried..."

"Don't worry about me; you should find a way to get a ride."

"No, it's okay."

"Well then."

"Okay, then let's meet at the club tomorrow."

Thus, the two parted ways.

K raised his overcoat collar, buried his neck, and walked out of the dark alley in I-dong (里門), onto the main street of Sadong (寺洞). The weather was very cold, and only drunken revelers staggering like a "zigzag (갈 之) character" were sparsely visible on the streets.

K stood at the entrance to Nagwon-dong (樂園洞) for a moment, lost in thought. A sweet potato vendor was making a loud cry to promote his wares.

K changed his direction from going home and headed down to Jongno (종로), then entered the alley next to Seungdong (勝洞) Presbyterian Church. Right behind Seungdong (勝洞) Presbyterian Church was H's house, where they had been playing tonight.

Meanwhile, S headed towards Jongno (종로). The wide, empty street, as if abandoned by its owner, only had rickshaws carrying geishas returning from restaurants and cars with snow falling on them passing by occasionally.

S changed his mind from going home to the west and slowly moved his feet to the east.

Then, from behind, he heard a soft voice saying, "Where are you going?" He turned around and saw H passing by in a rickshaw.

S smiled faintly and moved his feet.

Before long, H's rickshaw disappeared into the narrow alley leading from Jongno (종로) to the back of Sadong (寺洞). S went through the alley next to the YMCA (청년회관) and headed towards the back of Sadong (寺洞).

As a result of exercise and time, S arrived at a point only about six steps away from H's house behind Seungdong (勝洞) Presbyterian Church.

Then, from the alley coming out of the Hohae Inn (湖海려관), a black shadow emerged. It was definitely K.

The two recognized each other even in the darkness.

The two hesitated, trying not to reveal their thoughts, and walked towards each other.

But the words that came out were:

"Is that you?"

"Is that you?"

After asking each other this, they tried to hide their facial expressions and embarrassment, and then again,

"Where are you going?"

"Me? Well... I'm just going somewhere... What about you?"

"Me? Well... I'm just going somewhere..."

"Then go ahead."

"Yeah, you too."

The two passed each other.

S walked past the Hohae Inn (湖海려관) and the newly built P's hospital (P의 병원), then entered the Insa-dong (인사동) alley and headed north. On the way, he entered the I-dong (里門) alley again, passed in front of T House (T館) where they had been playing earlier, and headed towards Jongno (종로). He carefully looked around to avoid running into K, but he was nowhere to be seen.

Meanwhile, K passed in front of H's house and reached the side of the YMCA (청년회관) in the back alley of Insa-dong (인사동). There, he was afraid of running into S, so he went around the back of the YMCA (청년회관), entered I-dong (里門) again, and headed towards H's house again. This time, K and S reached the front of the door, thinking there was no way. However, presumption kills people. They met again.

"What's going on?"

"What about you?"

"I'm...?"

"I'm...?"

"Hehehe..."

"Hahaha..."

"Damn you!"

"You bastard!"

"Hahaha"

"Hehehe"

"Let's go."

"You're right."

"You're right."

"Goodbye."

"Yeah, you too."

The two parted ways. An hour later, the two met again. Another burst of laughter erupted. And then, the two headed towards the pub in Donggwan (동관).

Geisha's Love Story Chronicle
Gohan

The rumor that geishas adore actors has been around for a long time. Of course, actors were not the only people geishas adored. In the old days, after gangsters, it was new-wave actors, then newspaper reporters, musicians, writers, and then car drivers - there was even a golden age for technical school students, and there were even cases where geishas fell for film narrators (活動寫眞辯士), influential people, and detectives. It's already old news, but when Ahn Chang-nam (안창남) succeeded in a homeland visit flight and the Dong-A Ilbo (동아일보사) was thrilled and made a lot of money from celebratory advertisements, a geisha named K, who was invited to the Shikdowon (식도원) welcoming party, was so taken with that manly son that she even chased Ahn Chang-nam (안창남) to the outhouse and

"Please allow me just one kiss."

She was once rejected after pleading. There was also a time when baseball players and soccer players were the object of geisha ladies' affections. In this way, even if geishas did not have money or were a bit unattractive, the objects of men who fell in love with their popularity, their fame, and even their appearance and chased after them always changed with the times, but the traces of this never disappeared.

As a result, geishas who cut their hair, geishas who ran away, geishas who fell into a pit, geishas who fought with their parents and drank poison, and all sorts of tragicomedies followed one after another, providing stories for that era and place. Even these days, most car drivers and narrators have geisha lovers. Na Un-gyu (라운규) was also one of the people who received much admiration from geishas, and Hong Sun-tae (홍순태), a young driver who is now living a solid life in the countryside - in his prime when he was good at speeding and getting over Ojom Pass (오좀 고개), it was common to see three or four geishas following him around.

"Mr. Lee (리선생), the moon is bright tonight, so let's go for a ride in the city."

She pleads with a customer who has fallen for her at a restaurant and follows her around.

"Okay, let's do that." The customer readily agrees, pleased with the request of the geisha he loves.

"Boy, call a car."

The customer happily gives the order. A geisha with ulterior motives quietly goes to the office and asks the staff to call the car of the driver she adores.

The customers who were playing together take the geisha and get into the car. Before anyone knows it, the geisha is sitting in the driver's seat. <139>

"Hey, Ok-guk (옥국), come and sit in the back."

Not knowing what's on her mind, the gentleman in the back calls her to come to the back, thinking of her as his lover.

"No, it's okay. I get dizzy when I go over Ojom Pass (오좀 고개), so I can't ride in the back."

She answers naturally, and one hand is already resting on the driver's knee.

In this way, the gentlemen in the back, paying their own money, often became extras in the love march of the geisha and the driver they had fallen for.

Oh, the story has gone too far astray. The story, which was supposed to make the readers laugh, has become long without even a snort. However, I have been hesitant to write this because it is supposed to be a so-called true story (實話), so I have led the readers here. The protagonist of this joke, Mr. Ahn (안00), is now a central figure in his hometown after giving up his acting career, and is a good role model for the young people of the region, so I cannot use his name. However, most people involved in the theater know this story, and even those who don't will say, "Oh, it's him!" after hearing the whole story.

It was more than ten years ago - when the new wave of Joseon (조선) and theater was at its peak, our Mr. Ahn (안씨), although he grew up in a fairly well-off family, had such a deep interest in theater that he became an actor. He was slender, had a deep voice, and could move his eyes and mouth in a unique way, so it was not difficult for him to become a popular actor. His popularity grew day by day, and when he went to Pyongyang (평양) to perform, one night after the performance, a friend of Mr. Ahn (안씨) living in Pyongyang (평양) came to him and said,

"A certain geisha wants to meet you, so let's go to the Japanese restaurant Moranbong (모란봉우)."

He grabbed Mr. Ahn (안씨)'s arm and took him there. Mr. Ahn (안씨) was also young at the time, and he was curious because a beautiful woman was looking for him, so he went along.

He went up to Moranbong (모란봉) and arrived at "Omagijaya (오마기쟈야)," and the friend who acted as the matchmaker went in first. Then he came out and took Mr. Ahn (안씨) in. Because it was a Japanese restaurant, they first entered the next room where the beautiful woman was waiting, with a paper door in between.

He could hear the beautiful woman whispering. Mr. Ahn (안씨), who had a good character, was

"Who could it be?"

He was filled with anticipation as he imagined all the famous Pyongyang (평양) geishas he knew and enjoyed the approaching joyful moment.

"Bashi-shi! Bashi-shi!"

He heard the sound of the paper door quietly opening. Mr. Ahn (안씨) focused on the side where the paper door was opening with a tense attitude.

The door opened and a beautiful woman entered, but it wasn't that a beautiful woman was entering. Instead, the paper door was pulled back, and Mr. Ahn (안씨) was peeked at one more time.

The focus of the black pupils flashed through the slightly opened gap in the paper door, and then - bang!

The opened paper door suddenly slammed shut. Mr. Ahn (안씨) was suspicious and anxiously waited for the waiter when he heard loud voices between the matchmaker and the geisha in the next room.

"How can you bring someone else and call him Mr. Ahn (안씨)?"

He heard the geisha's angry voice.

"What nonsense are you talking about? If there were two Mr. Ahns (안씨) in that troupe, I might have mistaken him, but I clearly brought the Mr. Ahn (안씨) who sang Bongnam (봉남) in your ear last night, so who else could it be?"

He heard the friend's scolding voice. Hearing this, Mr. Ahn (안씨) finally realized his situation.

"It would be easy to say that I was mistaken for someone else."

He thought and quietly came out through the back door without saying a word and returned to the inn.

Even up to here, it's not funny. But now, at the end, something funny happens.

Mr. Ahn (안씨) was a dark and pockmarked man.

However, he had a skillful makeup technique. He put cotton on his sunken cheeks to make them look plump, and he put powder on his face to hide the pockmarks and dark complexion. When he went on stage, he was a dignified man.

The geisha lady, who did not know this and only saw the face on the stage, was completely smitten, and when she saw his real face, how could she not say that he was mistaken for someone else?

This is the end of the story. I laughed a lot when I heard this story from Mr. Ahn (안씨). However, I don't know if the readers will laugh or not. If you don't laugh, it's not Mr. Ahn (안씨)'s fault. It's my fault for not telling it in an interesting way.

The Story of Being Drunk in the Army Barracks
Yongsan In

It was during the Lunar New Year last year. After playing a yunnori (擲柶) match with A Village (A町) and W Village (W町), I was drunk at a Chinese restaurant in D Village (D町). <141> As I bid farewell to my friend and staggered home, a friend wearing a Japanese robe (도데라) looked at me with a face full of drunkenness and said,

"Anikki eraigeigidane (아니끼 에라이게이긔다네)." He wanted to tease me as if he had met a close friend. Since I wasn't in a hurry, I wandered around with him for about twenty minutes. He and I, who were strangers who didn't even know each other's names, became comrades only as members of the Drunken National Party (酒國國民黨員) because of our drunkenness. Then he insisted that I go to his house. I refused, but he held on tight and wouldn't let go. At that time, he called a taxi that was passing by and forced me to get in, and I couldn't figure out where he was turning or how he was going, but I was able to come to my senses a little in the cold wind. Then he stopped the car at a desolate doorstep and told me to get out as if he was dragging me out. I said I would go in this car, but he didn't listen at all and dragged me into the house. At that time, even in my drunkenness, I realized that his house was not an ordinary house. He dragged me up the stairs, staggering, to the third or fourth floor, and entered an eight-mat room after passing through a six-mat room. Then he took out a 되 (traditional Korean unit of volume) bottle of alcohol from the closet and said that guests and hosts don't eat snacks, so let's drink straight alcohol. I was already about to die from drunkenness, and being forced to drink straight alcohol, I couldn't refuse, so after drinking a few glasses, my eyes spun around and I couldn't come to my senses as if I had been hit with a hammer. Even so, I still had the idea that I was in someone else's house, so I tried to get up and leave before making a mistake, but he grabbed me and told me to finish the rest of the alcohol, and when I tried to shake him off, I fell and broke three sliding glass doors between the six-mat and eight-mat rooms, and then I ran down and broke the glass in the front door (玄關) on the third floor. I vaguely knew that friends wearing nemaki (네마가만) were coming out from all directions, but after that, my drunkenness completely turned around and I was in a state of unconsciousness...?

After a while, I woke up because my arm hurt, and it was bright daylight, and it seemed to be six in the morning. I was being dragged by a military policeman in front of the 000 Officer's Dormitory. After dragging me for a while, he couldn't figure out what to do with me, so he called a passing car, put me in it, and took me somewhere, and it seemed like I was being imprisoned in a dark room with white walls on all sides and only a hole that looked like a door. I wasn't fully awake from my drunkenness, so I shouted and knocked on the door in the detention center, and when I kicked it, a friend wearing Korean clothes came in and beat me indiscriminately, asking why I was making so much noise, so I took the lid of the toilet in the detention center and tried to hit him with it, asking why he was hitting me. The two of us had a great fight like this, but I was a drunk man and he was a sober man, so how could I resist? Even so, the uniformed people <142> came in and kicked and beat me indiscriminately, and I lost consciousness and fell...

As I gradually woke up from my drunkenness, I felt thirsty and couldn't stand the dirt, so I got up and sat down and tried to remember the events of last night...

Around nine in the morning, the door opened and they told me to come out. I went to the water tap to wash my face and asked for some hot soup to get rid of my hangover. Then, around eleven o'clock, they brought me two bowls of kake udon (가게 우동), so I quickly ate them up without even taking off the lids, but I was still not full, so I took out a one-won bill that I had hidden in my back pocket and gave it to the udon delivery man and asked him to bring me another bowl and buy me a pack of cigarettes, winking and gesturing. After about fifteen minutes, he brought the udon and secretly gave me a pack of Pigeon (비종) cigarettes and a match, and handed me the remaining money. I turned around and acted innocent, took the udon with one hand and the cigarettes with the other, put them in my pants pocket, and ate the udon. Then, a person from the special higher police came and took me away for questioning. He asked me for my address, name, and occupation in his usual routine, and then asked me about the facts. So, I remembered the faint memories and told him the facts as they were. Then he said, how could I, a stranger, do such a thing with Izumi (泉) officer (士官), who was a close friend, and because I had invaded the army barracks, destroyed equipment, and committed violence, I would be punished under the army criminal law, and then he said that if that was the case, he would call Izumi (泉) officer (士官) to find out the facts and take the best measures, and then he asked if the statement was correct and told me to sign and seal it.

After the interrogation was over, I came back into the detention center and was now waiting for Izumi (泉) to come. I was feeling stuffy and bored, so I looked out the window and puffed on the poor cigarettes.

I waited for him to come all day, but he didn't come, and he didn't come the next day either. Around eight o'clock at night, the door opened and a woman wearing Japanese clothes who looked to be around twenty years old came in and begged me to send her home because she was innocent. However, the military policeman didn't say anything and just put the woman in the room and locked the door. I lay down and stared blankly at the woman. It was not another person, but Sadako (사다꼬), a waitress in C Cafe (C카페). So I asked her what was wrong, and she said that she had been arrested on suspicion. So I asked her what she was suspected of, and she said that a private named Mori (森) in the 00th regiment had deserted, and she was suspected of instigating and helping Mori (森) to desert. However, she said that she had never done such a thing, and although she had known Mori (森) for a long time, they were not particularly close, and she had been with her brother in P place (P라는 곳) for about two weeks before Mori (森) deserted, and she had only returned this morning, but she was being unfairly detained like this. <143>

While we were talking about this, the sound of shoes was heard around eleven thirty, so we stopped talking and remained silent. Then the door opened, they called Sadako (사다꼬) out, and when I saw that she didn't come back in, it seemed that she had been acquitted.

After Sadako (사다꼬) left, I felt even more lonely. But what could I do with my freedom lost? There was nothing I could do but lie down and try to sleep, but my eyes wouldn't close and only became clearer. Then, somehow I fell asleep, and when I woke up the next morning, it seemed to be around nine o'clock. After I washed my face, they told me to go to the supply room (수부실) because they were cleaning the room I had been in. So I went to the supply room (수부실) and was warming myself by the stove when a brush peddler came in and told the military police there to buy brushes. Then the people there were making a fuss and commotion, choosing brushes. At that moment, I thought of 물실회긔 (not sure what this means). I quickly told the brush peddler to send a letter to my house, wrote down the address, and earnestly asked him to do so. While I was doing this, it was almost noon when Izumi (이지미) came. The person who had interrogated me took him and came back to the supply room (수부실) after about thirty minutes.

Then I greeted Izumi (이지미) anew, and he apologized for making me suffer because of him. And after talking about this and that, around five in the afternoon, a squad leader called me to the front and gave me a long lecture, and told me to pay for the broken glass. Then Izumi (이지미), who was next to me, said that it was his fault and that he would pay for it, and after bowing politely, he said goodbye to the military police squad. When I came out of the military police squad, the weather was so cold and my body was trembling as if I was shaking like a shaman's flag. Izumi (이지미) and I went for a while and entered C Cafe (C카페), where Sadako (사다꼬) was, and after having a drink and dinner, I finally felt alive and energetic. After coming out of the cafe and saying goodbye to Izumi (이지미), I went straight home. When I got home, there was a commotion. I had been gone for three or four days without saying a word, so they had searched all the places I could have gone and sent a telegram to my hometown.

This day was supposed to be the day of the second chuksada (척사) competition, but because I, who was the main person, was absent, S and P, who were good at playing yunnori (윳), couldn't participate because they were searching for me. And other people took their place, but in the end, they were defeated. We were defeated because I, S, and P were absent. But yunnori (윳) is a matter of fate, so it is impossible to know, but it is an undeniable fact that our side's morale was broken and we were defeated because we were absent, which everyone acknowledged. (End)

To the Room, Saying I Won't Do It Again
Kim Yeongpal

This year, I am a 60-year-old woman, and for some reason, my eyes were swollen, and it was obvious that I had been crying last night. Even so, I still had some sadness left, so I sat at the end of the veranda, looking down at the ground, sighing from time to time, and crying. Yes, crying teardrops like beads, lamenting.

"What am I going to do living any longer like this! That rascal is making me suffer so much."

Yes - that rascal is making me suffer so much - so it is obvious that she is saying that it is better to die because she does not need to live any longer since there is no one to rely on because her son or grandson does not even look at her and is causing trouble. But if you listen to her lamenting words, it is true that he causes trouble and does not come home. When I hear the words filled with envious language, I realize that she is not talking to her son or grandson.

"He must have gone to that woman's house again. If I could, I would grab that woman by the hair and drag her out to the main street and say, 'Let's die together,' but I'm so angry. I'm so angry. But if I meddle, both of us - Aigu -"

Like this, she mumbles to herself and continues to shed tears miserably. At that time, I thought, "Aha, I see." So, that ignorant old woman has found a young man and is jealous because the man is having an affair. In his eyes, that woman looked like Yang Guifei (양귀비) or Xishi (서시), or even more than that, but anyway, he had no interest in anything and only thought about that woman's appearance, so as the days went by, he became so infatuated that he eventually became ill.
