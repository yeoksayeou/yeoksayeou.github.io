기사제목	特號實話 人間的 殘忍性의 極致, 女子四十八人虐殺事件, 近代 犯罪史上 驚異!! (Special Edition True Story: The Height of Human Cruelty, The Massacre of 48 Women, A Shock in Modern Crime History!!)
기사형태	소식 (News)

It was 1908, when the European Great War that shook the world was at its peak.

At that time, Germany was like a living hell, exactly like a scene of carnage. All the men had gone to the battlefields, and the remaining people were full of hunger and discontent. In fact, the tiresome war had made them feel weary, and they became despondent, turning into animalistic and violent beings. They roamed the streets and parks, committing daytime kisses, robberies, and sexual assaults. Revolutionary plots were constantly emerging here and there, and Germany's public order was barely maintained by martial law (戒嚴令).

Among them, Berlin, the capital of Germany, was a more chaotic scene of carnage and a more frightening hell.

At this time, in Berlin's most prosperous areas, the Friedrichstraße (푸리-루리히街) and near the Büttam Platz (쁫탐·뿌랏츠), women were continuously disappearing.

Lujie (루제), the widow of Lieutenant Colonel Brantestain (뿌란테스타인 中佐), who had fought gloriously in the battle of Pillan, went out leaving her two children at home and disappeared. And her eldest daughter, six-year-old Mireya (미리야), was taken away by someone.

However, in the chaotic living hell that was Berlin, this was not considered a big deal, and it was simply seen as succumbing to the temptation of the golden demon (黃金魔). But then, six-year-old Mägdariene (막따리네), who lived on Polta Street (뽈타街), went out to Friedrichstraße (푸티-투리히街) in the evening to buy something and disappeared. A beautiful saleswoman from a famous department store on Büttam Street (쁫탑街) disappeared on her way home. A female teacher and student from a certain school on Büttam Street (쁫탑街) vanished, a young nurse from a hospital on Klaus Street (크라우스街) went missing, a typist from a store disappeared, and the young daughter (令孃) of a wealthy man living on Belturen Street (뻴투렌街) vanished without a trace.

Although these strange disappearances occurred in succession, the people of Berlin, whose nerves were extremely numb, did not think much of the fact that young women were disappearing from the streets in the evening, assuming that they had gone to a nightclub (나잇트 俱樂部) in the basement and were indulging in debauchery.

Nightclubs (나잇트 俱樂部) were formed because general entertainment facilities were closed due to the war, and people who had become rich due to the war wanted to enjoy pleasures in a free zone (自由境) beyond the reach of the police, forming clubs in basements and creating a pleasure market (XX시장). There were several of these clubs in Berlin at the time, and their existence was publicly known in secret and secretly existing in public.

Therefore, even when a series of strange incidents occurred in which more than a dozen women disappeared, they did not suspect that a major incident was lurking behind it, but simply thought that they had been lured into such secret clubs by hunger and the temptation of gold.

Even the police authorities at the time saw that revolutionary movements had broken out in countries such as Bulgaria and Hungary, and the people of Berlin were tired of the war, so there was a looming crisis where a revolution could break out at any moment. They thought that preventing revolution was a more urgent issue than investigating these erotic incidents, and they devoted all their energy to it. They did not take this strange incident seriously, thinking it was the work of enemy spies, and strictly forbade the press from reporting on the incident, conducting secret investigations.

However, almost every day, cries of "My younger sister hasn't come back since last night" and "My daughter hasn't come back for three days, please find her" tormented the Berlin police. What was even stranger was that most of the men in the Berlin police had become soldiers and gone to the battlefields, so they naturally used women as a majority of their informants (密偵). Naturally, female informants used all their means to investigate the incident, but three female detectives (녀탐정) who had worked the hardest on this case suddenly disappeared. At first, the authorities expected that they had gone into hiding somewhere to use their skills, but in the end, all three did not return.

As a result, the Berlin police, who had not taken it seriously until now, decided to conduct a thorough investigation into this incident, triggered by the disappearance of the female informants, and mobilized the criminal division, security division, and police affairs division to conduct an emergency investigation throughout the precinct.

Restaurants, cafes, hotels, and flophouses (룸펜下宿) were thoroughly investigated. As a result, more than eighty people, including vagrant boys and other witnesses, were summoned and questioned, but none of them seemed to be related to the incident. So this time, they investigated merchants who easily appealed to women's vanity, but found nothing here either. Again, they raided gambling dens in slum areas and arrested more than 50 people, but they were all unrelated to the strange incident.

Despite conducting emergency investigations day after day, not only did they not find anything, but the police authorities, who had not obtained any evidence about the disappearance of dozens of people so far, were very anxious. Under normal circumstances, this would not have been a prolonged case, but it occurred during the Great War that determined the fate of Germany, so the investigation and everything else did not go as planned.

One day at dawn, a boatman (뱃사공) ran to the police station and reported, "There's a body on the riverbank now..." The detective team immediately rushed to the scene, and the body was that of a naked woman, and that woman was the body of Gijera (기제라), one of the female detectives who had disappeared three weeks ago.

The gold watch she always carried on her body was unlikely to be left on her body, but even her engagement ring, her only comfort, was gone, and even the gold teeth (金齒) that peeked out adorably between her red lips had been pried out. Her usual glamorous physical beauty had also been bloated by the water like a cold rubber doll, and it was a gruesome corpse that was unbearable to look at.

The next day, the authorities started another emergency investigation, and at that time, they finally discovered a bizarre object: twenty-six women's clothing were found at a secondhand clothes shop in Inpaliten Street (인파리텐街). It had been bought from an old man, and it was valuable evidence for this case. In other words, the authorities called the families of the missing people to identify them, and they cried and wailed, grabbing clothes they recognized, saying, "Oh, this is it!" "This is what my daughter was wearing!" This created a tragic scene.

Among them were the clothes of Mrs. Eruje (에루제), the widow of the Lieutenant Colonel, the clothes of Mägdariene (막딸네나), and the clothes of the female detective Lee-ta (이-타), who had gone missing a month ago. In any case, it was confirmed that all twenty-six articles of clothing belonged to the victims, but the clothes of Gijera (지제라), whose body had been found in the river, were not found.

There was blood on the skirts of Mägdariene (막딸네나) and the wealthy young lady (富豪 令孃) from Beltuten Street (뻴투텐街).

And when they put their hands in the pockets, they found a few bills, which helped the investigators realize that the perpetrator was not motivated by money, but was a cruel pervert (變態性慾者), giving the investigation authorities a glimmer of hope.

Meanwhile, the investigation team tracked down the old man who had sold the items four days ago and found that he was Ru-man (루-만), an old station worker (老驛夫) living on August Street (오-커스트街) north of the Friedrichstraße (푸리-투리히) - Storase (스트라-세) station. However, when Ru-man's house was raided, Ru-man had already passed away three days prior.

When they investigated the cause of Ru-man's death, they found that he had died suddenly of a heart attack in the station worker's office (驛夫室) three days ago. It was also confirmed that he was a faithful station worker who had worked for 30 years at the Friedrichstraße (푸리-투리히) - Straß (스트라-세) station, and according to the neighbors, he not only did not have the potential to commit a violent crime, but he had always lived a very upright life. It seemed that he had no relatives, but a friend of his came to help with various arrangements at the funeral. However, there was no way to know who that friend was, and therefore, there was no way to know what secrets Ru-man had, since he was not the person involved.

So, this strange incident, which had seen a glimmer of hope, once again fell into a fog.

About six months ago, a bachelor who had returned from America had started a butcher shop in an alley near Krausen Street (크라우젠街), close to Friedrichstraße (푸리-투리히街) and Büttam Street (쁫탑街). He was about 40 years old, and his impression was unpleasant with a large crescent-shaped scar on his left cheek. He did not interact with his neighbors and just silently ran his business, but he always seemed to be wary of his surroundings.

He didn't have many customers at his store, but on Tuesday and Friday nights, a sidecar (싸이드카-) would always come to his house and whisper something, leaving something behind. The next day, there would always be lamb (羊肉), with blood dripping from it, hanging and being sold in front of his house. Rumors began to spread that there was some secret between him and the sidecar (싸이드카-) at night, other than the fact that he was trying to make a profit by selling unchecked lamb (無檢査 羊고기).

Moreover, even though he was a bachelor, there were times when the voices of women could be heard from inside his house in the middle of the night, and the next morning, there was no sign that a woman had been there the night before, which led to even stranger suspicions towards him.

With this information, the female detectives (녀탐정) paid attention to him and did not neglect their vigilance, and reported to the investigation headquarters that "all the secrets are hidden in that lamb (羊肉)". So, the police immediately arrested the owner, Helman (헬만).

After questioning and investigating him thoroughly for several days, he was eventually released due to insufficient evidence.

Once again, a man confessed that he was the perpetrator. However, that man was a French spy who boldly confessed in order to spy on the internal affairs of the Berlin Police Department.

In any case, more than half a year had passed since this rare and brutal incident occurred, and several months had already passed since the investigation began, but it remained an unsolved mystery.

In this living hell (大戰 末期) of wartime Berlin, the female detective Angera (앙게라), for the sake of her colleague's soul and for the general women of society, made a firm resolution to catch this terrifying criminal no matter what, and went out every evening to search for places where crimes were likely to occur.

Every night, in a dark and secluded place, the young Angera (앙게라), who was only twenty-two years old, hid herself and kept a close watch. After doing so for three weeks, she discovered a major piece of evidence.

That is, while she was hiding in the dark shade of a tree on the Spree (스푸레이) Riverbank, where the body of the female detective (女탐정) had been found the other day, a black shadow came out of the woods towards the riverbank with a large bundle on its back as dawn approached.

Angera's hand instinctively gripped the pistol in her pocket and watched the strange person's actions closely. It was not a man, but unexpectedly a woman. But the woman acted as if she was about to jump into the river, but suddenly changed direction and went down along the river.

Angera followed her, still hiding. The strange woman went down about 7 or 8 blocks along the riverbank, stood blankly by the river, looked around, threw the bundle she had been carrying on her back into the river, and ran away without looking back.

Angera was still secretly following the strange woman. The strange woman wandered around and entered the house of the butcher Helman (헬만).

Upon receiving this report, the authorities mobilized all their resources and began their operation. A handful of women's hair was found on the riverbank where the strange woman had stood, and as a result of searching his house, truly horrifying evidence was found in the basement. And it was found that the strange woman was Helman (헬만) in disguise, and at the same time, he was found to be a violent pervert (變態性慾者).

Helman (헬만)! He was originally born in a slum in Berlin and grew up in an unfortunate environment. At the age of fifteen, he worked as a houseboy (하우스·뽀이) in the house of a captain of a ship sailing to America in Hamburg (함벅크) City. The family consisted only of the captain and his wife.

Every evening when the captain returned from sailing, he would always argue with his wife in the middle of the night. One evening, after an argument, the captain accidentally killed his wife. However, it was difficult for the captain alone to dispose of his wife's body, so he woke Helman (헬만), who was sleeping, and threatened him to carry the body to the basement with the captain. Then he gave him some money and smuggled him to America.

Afterward, the captain returned and reported him as the murderer of his wife. The investigation reached America. Here, his personality gradually became self-destructive and gradually developed a violent character.

Years later, he hid in the Montana Mountains (몬테나 山地) in America and lived as a shepherd for more than ten years. During that time, his youth was enjoyed by female sheep (牝羊), and he became addicted to the taste of perverted sexual desire by killing those female sheep.

When the European Great War broke out, he suddenly set foot on the land of his native Germany. The women crying from hunger due to the war easily satisfied his sexual desire. When he seduced women, he always disguised himself as an old woman (老女), lured young women, and when he had the chance, he would drug them and drag them to his house. Then, he would use a strong anesthetic, lay them on the bed, satisfy his sexual desire, kill the women with a hammer, and take advantage of the fact that he was a butcher to skin them. He would mix the meat with the meat he sold and throw the rest into the river. He committed truly heinous and shuddering atrocities. And he took satisfaction and interest in it, massacring 48 women in a row.

This can be said to be the product of his unfortunate past life and his bestial shepherding life. It is no exaggeration to say that it is a sample of human cruelty in modern major crimes throughout the world.

Germany is keeping this incident a secret for the sake of its wartime honor, but it is said that Helman's (헬만) torture instruments are on display in the Berlin Police Museum along with the murder weapon used by Wolsto, who killed 40 people. <32>
