Dissolute Women's Word Parade
						OYES
If I had been born a woman in America, with my looks, I would have been the daughter-in-law of Carnegie, or at least, if I were a little more worldly, I would easily have become a Jeolla governor (全羅監使), as my face is suitable for such a position, but I have one flaw.

It is that, despite my plump physique, I am good at making sharp remarks.

I know very well that the person is not particularly hateful or useless, but once I pick up a pen, I start spouting words that are sharper than this steel pen I am using now, and sting more painfully than rose thorns.

Perhaps knowing this flaw, the reporter from Kaebyoksa (開闢社) who came to ask for this manuscript begged me ten, twenty, a hundred times—please, don't use any thorny cynicism (ヒニク), but write in a round, vague, cotton-like way, tickling without hurting.

I answered that I would, but can one change one's habits...? We'll see...

On the other hand, I should perhaps repent. Now that I am over 30, my back is bent, and my hearing is failing, it is pointless for an old bachelor (?) like me to only make sharp remarks to others—especially to flower-like women (色씨)—or else I might not escape becoming a bachelor ghost, or worse, I might suffer a tragic death (非命慘死) in an "erotic-terror" attack based on the red-and-white terror tactics (赤白테로전술) that are so popular these days. Who can guarantee otherwise?

Enough with the prologue, now I must get to the main topic, which is "Dissolute Women's Word Parade."

It seems I must capture all the "dissolute women" and put them on display like mannequins. No matter how much I repent and try to write with a soft, round brush, or even a broad one like the Governor-General's seal, I will still be cursed out for the title and the nature of the article. Ah, well, if an old bachelor can't get married, he might as well be cursed out by women to his heart's content.

Madam P, who opened a large hemorrhoid clinic (肛門病院) in XXX...

Looking into her background, her family runs the famous Kang Yakju (康藥酒) brewery. They made a considerable amount of money from the secret recipe of this Kang Yakju, and even now, golden bars (but since gold exports are prohibited, there are no gold bars in the house, but bank accounts, deposit books, and land deeds) are rolling around in the safe.

She got married, but her husband was a complete philanderer and vagrant... His main occupation was drinking, his side job was being a gigolo, and his tertiary occupation was making human waste (人糞製造).

In terms of fate, this is the ultimate misfortune.

However, the wife, having graduated from XX Girls' School in the days of cow-dung hair (쇠똥머리) (she is now a new modern woman, but was an old modern woman then), could not stand to see this.

So, the wife divorced him and went back to Seoul to finish XX Girls' School, then went to Tokyo and entered a medical college to major in proctology (科).

Then she returned to her hometown and worked as an assistant (副手) at XX Hospital before opening her own clinic recently.

After telling this story, some might say, "Her husband was a wanderer, so she divorced him, studied further, became a doctor, and started her own business(!), so what is wrong with that, and why is she a dissolute woman?!"

But that alone does not make her dissolute. It is just that there is a sweet, peach-blossom-like attraction interwoven like tangled silk threads, and that is what makes her dissolute.

Readers might misunderstand the word "dissolute," but this writer does not attach any subjective moral value to it, and even thinks that if men can be dissolute, there is nothing particularly wrong with women being dissolute. I am saying this in advance.

Putting that aside, some might ask, "Did you see P-ssi being dissolute with your own eyes?" But that is a question that is not only wise but also foolish, because do you have to see everything with your own eyes to know and believe it! If so, all records would have to be denied.

Undoubtedly, she is dissolute, but the madam is quite attractive.

She is as buxom as anyone, has 100% shapely legs (脚線美), speaks fluent Japanese (ah, that was not a physical attribute), has kind eyes, and although she is an old hag of thirty-four, she insists she is twenty-seven (but this twenty-seven was the same last year and will be the same next year).

So, with that body, that youth, and that money, wouldn't she have some thoughts about being a little dissolute?

If you ask, "Are you married?" she will answer frankly, "Yes, I am single."

If you say, "You can be single even if you are married," she will say, "Yes (hesitantly)...." That alone is 100% proof of her dissolute nature and qualifications.

The time was X month of 19XX.

The place was the boarding house of artist K in XXX.

K was lying down, either contemplating a novel or daydreaming.

At that moment, the door burst open and in rushed Ms. S, also an artist by profession (?), but she is forever Ms. S.

Ms. S rushed in and immediately complained of hunger. It is common for artists to be hungry in Joseon, then and now.

"Oh, I'm starving."

"Why, couldn't you eat again?"

"It's not that I couldn't eat again, but I still haven't eaten."

"Well, that's true!"

"Buy me a bowl of ox bone soup (설넝탕)라도 한 그릇."

Fortunately, K had enough money for ox bone soup in his pocket (or perhaps he was planning to cheat her by promising to pay tomorrow), so he ordered a bowl of ox bone soup from the landlady.

When K sat back down, look at what S did. She pulled down the bedding and lay down in the lower part of the room, saying she would sleep there.

I knew S's behavior was like that from the start, but the man was so XXXXX by the woman that something was bound to happen.

To borrow the narrative style of old novels, S's dissolute behavior was generally like this.

Now she has gone to China and is completely down on her luck.

A woman who graduated from the Tokyo Nanyang (南洋) Music School and the Beiyang (北洋) Music Department and is now teaching Western music at the Kyongsong (경성) Dongyang (東洋) Business School...

With a face so thin that Yanggeumchae (양금채) would come and cry, she always wears a fabricated, lonely expression and says, "I'm so lonely (サビシイ해)! I'm so lonely (サビシイ해)!"

"Let's go somewhere. Let's dress up."

She never keeps her mouth shut.

Let's look into the background of this Mrs. M.

She married the son of a wealthy family and went to Tokyo, where she majored in Beiyang music at the aforementioned Nanyang Music School. But for some reason, she had a bad relationship with her husband and completely separated from him, refusing to fulfill her duties as a wife. Of course, her husband's family paid her 70 won per month for tuition, and her husband was studying modernology (考現學) at some school, but because his wife, M, treated him so coldly, he spent his days drinking and prostituting himself.

Eventually, they finished their studies and returned to Joseon and divorced!

After the divorce, the wife, unable to bear the loneliness (サビシイ) she always talked about, or perhaps because she had rejected her husband so coldly, became an expert in intense lesbian relationships.

Some might jump in and say, "What is dissolute about divorcing a husband she hates and having lesbian relationships?" But I consider lesbian relationships to be a form of sexual indulgence, so I am sorry that I have to include her in that category. (To be continued) <33>

그것은 그와가티 풍더분한 체격에도 얼니지 아니하게 뽀족한 소리를 잘하는 것이다.
