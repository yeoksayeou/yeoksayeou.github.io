The International Snake Exposition Continues

Serong (蛇龍) is the name of a Korean folk song. Sama (蛇矛) is the name of a weapon; in the History of Jin (晋書), it is written that Chen An held a ten-foot-eight-inch sama in his right hand, and during the Three Kingdoms period, Zhang Fei also possessed a ten-foot-eight-inch sama. Sa-ui (蛇醫) is another name for sugung (守宮), a term used in the southern Chu dialect. Serpentinite (蛇紋石) is a mineral formed by the alteration of olivine and hornblende; it is named so because its texture resembles snake skin. (Also known as warm stone (溫石).) Snake mother (蛇*용母) is a reptile, similar to lizards but of a different genus. Snake grape (蛇葡萄) is a perennial vine. Snake 含草 (Snake 含草) is the name of a grass; its common name is small dragon tooth (小龍牙), and another name is purple-backed dragon tooth (紫背龍牙). There is an interesting story about this grass: long ago, a farmer saw a snake that was injured, and another snake brought this grass and applied it to the wound, and immediately the wound healed, and the snake was as good as new. The farmer then picked the grass and applied it to human wounds, and it had a miraculous effect, hence the name Snake 含草 (Snake 含草). Snake belly pattern (蛇腹紋) is a type of crack pattern found on ancient zithers (古琴); there is a saying in an ancient poem, "The zither is covered with snake belly patterns." (Also known as snake 蚹.) Snake yellow (蛇黃) is the yellow soil found in snake nests during the winter; it is used in medicine. Snake yellow is also found with cow yellow (牛黃) in the snake's belly and used in medicine. Snake slough (蛇蛻) is the shed skin of a snake and is used in medicine. There is also a story about snake slough: In the Jin dynasty, in a sealed armory, an unexpected pheasant crowed, which many found strange. The scholar Zhang Hua said that it must be a transformation of a snake, and when they opened the door, there was indeed a snake slough where the pheasant was. (History of Jin (晋書)) Snake horn (蛇角) is a precious name; in the Annotations to the Classic of Mountains and Seas (山海經注), it is explained as blue rhinoceros horn (碧犀). Snake strawberry (蛇苺) is a perennial herb, a type of raspberry (覆盆子) (commonly known as snake strawberry (뱀딸긔)). Snake bed seed (蛇床子) is a perennial herb used in medicine. Snake venom sore (蛇漠瘼) = The saliva (延味) of the pit viper (蝮蛇) is called snake venom (蛇漠), and sores that arise from contact with grass, wood, or stone tainted with snake venom are called snake venom sores (蛇漠瘡) (commonly known as stone sickness (돌쇅이)). Besides these, there is a Silla monk named Sabok (蛇福) (contemporary with Wonhyo (元曉)) in human names, and place names such as Saryang (蛇梁), Sasan (蛇山), and Sado (蛇島) are numerous, but these are omitted here.

7. A Panoramic View of Ancient Records of Snakes

Seungsa (勝蛇) is a type of dragon (龍類) that can create clouds and rain, and Mang (蟒) is the king snake (王蛇), called the king because it is the strongest and largest among snakes (爾雅). Basa (巴蛇) swallows an elephant and takes three years to excrete its bones (山海經). Pit vipers (蝮蛇) are most venomous in the autumn months, and when they release their venom, grass and trees die instantly (博物志). Sulyeon (率然) is a snake from Mt. Chang (常山); if you strike its head, its tail will come, and if you strike its tail, its head will come, and if you strike its middle, both head and tail will come (孫子). The centipede (蚿) envies the snake and says, "I walk with many feet, but you are better than me even without feet" (莊子). Dae (帶) is a small snake, and centipedes (蛆螂) like to eat its eyes (莊註). When a centipede (蜈蚣) sees a snake, it can kill the snake with its breath (抱朴子). There are many snakes in Bu-gu (負丘), but when the Yellow Emperor (黃帝氏) brought realgar (雄黃), the snakes scattered (同上). The year of the snake is called Sanyeon (巳年); in a poem by Li Shangyin (李商隱), it says, "In the fifth month of the year of the snake (蛇年建午月), I returned from Liang to Qin (我自梁還秦)." In the Zuo Zhuan (左傳), inner snakes fought with outer snakes in the south of Jeong (鄭南中); this is called snake demon (蛇妖). When Sun Suk-o (孫叔敖) was young, he killed a two-headed snake, and Emperor Gaozu of Han (漢太祖) cut a large snake while crossing Daeseok (大澤) at night. An old woman cried, "My son was the son of the White Emperor (白帝), but the son of the Red Emperor (赤帝) has cut him down" (史記). Emperor Sui (隨帝) treated a wounded snake and saved its life, and later the snake brought him a pearl, which was one inch in diameter and shone so brightly that it was like the bright moon at night, so it was named Sui Pearl (隋珠), or Bright Moon Pearl (明月珠), or Sui Snake Pearl (隋蛇珠). As Liu Yuxi (劉禹錫) wrote in a poem, "I bid farewell to the thatched house while holding the snake pearl (自握蛇珠辭白屋)." During a campaign, Emperor Taizong of Tang (唐太宗) took a nap and was awakened by a snake chasing a mouse, thus avoiding harm from enemy soldiers, which others found amazing. When Xia Yu (夏禹氏) drilled through Dragon Gate Mountain (龍門山), a god with a human body and snake face came and set up the Eight Trigrams (八卦) on a golden plate and gave him a jade tablet to measure the twelve hours and measure heaven and earth. That god was Fuxi (伏養氏), who first drew the Eight Trigrams (八卦). Du Yu (杜預) and Ju Sun (周詢) transformed into snakes when they fell asleep drunk, and Chae Guntam (蔡君譚) of Dongje (東齊) transformed into a snake during his illness, so he was called Snake *. It is called painting a snake and adding feet (畵蛇添足) to do useless things, and harmful things are called snake pigs (蛇豕). As the Zuo Zhuan (左傳) says, "Burying pigs and snakes (封豕葬蛇) preserves the upper country (存食上國)," and crawling is called snake walk (蛇行). In the Gukchae (國策), Su Jin's (蘇秦) elder sister said, "crawl like a snake (蛇行葡伏)." Li Bai's (李白) poem says, "There are many dragons and snakes in Daeseok (大澤多龍蛇)," Chen Hushan's (陳后山) poem says, "The forest is noisy with sparrows pecking at snakes (林喧雀啅蛇)," Su Dongpo's (蘇東坡) poem has the line, "Thunder god chases clouds to catch yellow snakes (雷公*雲捕黃蛇)," and Liu Zongyuan (柳宗元) has "Edict on Forgiving Pit Vipers (宥蝮蛇文)" and "On Catching Snakes (捕蛇說)." And the story of the snake in the Christian scriptures, which many people know well these days, seems too trivial and absurd, so I will omit it. The Book of Poetry (詩) says, "Only snakes and vipers are auspicious for women (惟虺惟蛇는 女子之祥이라)," and the Book of Changes (易) says, "Dragon and snake insects exist to preserve themselves (龍蛇之蟲은 以存身也라)," and Yu Shin's (庾信) "Letter Thanking Emperor Ming of Zhou for Silk and Cloth (謝周明帝錫絲布等啓)" says, "The snake that repays the favor in the Han River is a one-inch pearl (漢水報德之蛇는 明珠一寸이라)," and the Samdonggye (叅同契) says, "The male does not live alone, and the female does not live in solitude, so the Black Tortoise (玄武) [turtle and snake] intertwine and support each other (雄不獨處하며 雌不孤居하야 玄武龜蛇가 蟠糾相扶라)," and the Huainanzi (准南子) says, "The * snake roars male in the sky and the female roars female in the wind, transforming into refined essence ( *蛇는 雄嗚天上風하며 雌嗚天下風하야 化成精至라)," and the Notes on the Book of Rites (儀禮註) says, "Sages are likened to dragons, and gentlemen are likened to snakes (聖人은 喩龍하고 君子 喩蛇라)," and Zhuangzi (莊子) also says, "Without praise or blame, one is a dragon and one is a snake (無譽無皆는 一龍一蛇라)," and Cao Zhi's (曹植) "Letter to Yang Deok-jo (與楊德祖書)" says, "Everyone says they hold the pearl of a divine snake (人人이 自謂握靈蛇之珠라)," and Dongminggi (洞冥記) says, "Red Pine Master (赤松子) took phoenix sunflower grass for three years and rode a yellow snake into the water to obtain a pearl, the color of which was Jurchen gold (三年間에 服食鳳葵草하고 乘黃蛇入水하야 得珠하니 其色이 女眞金이라)," and Ronheng (論衡) says, "The divine snake rides the sky (神蛇乘天이라)," and the Classic of Mountains and Seas (山海經) also says, "West of Gaemyeong (開明) there are phoenix wind beautiful birds, all carrying snakes and treading on snakes (有鳳風孌鳥하니 皆載蛇踐蛇라)," and Ungye Yuui (雲溪友議) says, "Tang Minister Li Shin (李紳) was initially poor and went to Cheonggung Hermitage (天宮精舍) in Yeomcheon (剡川) and took a nap with his books. An old monk saw a black snake climbing the front Li tree to eat the fruit. The monk said, 'I am afraid that its venom will reach people,' and slowly drove it down. The snake looked toward the east and entered Li Gong's arms and suddenly disappeared. Gong was startled, and the old monk asked, 'Did the scholar see anything in his dream (秀才鄋中에 所見이 無한가한대)?' Gong replied, 'I dreamed of climbing a Li tree and being harassed by a monk.'", and in Duryang Japbyeon (杜陽雜編) it says "At the beginning of Gae-seong (開城), a yellow snake came out of the palace treasure house and wandered around the courtyard. The palace official held up a coral bead and struck the snake, and the bead was lost. Later, the storekeeper searched the warehouse and found both the yellow snake and the coral bead." In the Biography of Bowoo in the Book of Later Han (後漢書寶武傳), it is said, "Bowoo's (武) mother gave birth to Bowoo and a snake together, and the snake disappeared into the forest. When the mother died and was buried, a large snake suddenly came, struck the coffin with its head, and tears and blood flowed together, and the bowing and bending looked like a gesture of mourning. After a while, it left. People called it a sign of the Bo clan (寶氏의 祥)," and in the Erya (爾雅) it is said, "The gallbladder of the bearded snake (髥蛇) turns every day, being near the head in the first ten days of the month, in the heart in the middle ten days, and in the tail in the last ten days. In the customs of the southern people, they take its gallbladder to fill medicinal ingredients, and they sew the wound with thread, and if they encounter a later capturer, they show their gold wound to prove that they have no gallbladder." Also, its nature is promiscuous, so the capturer may call it Hongnyeoja (紅娘子), or if it is covered with a woman's * clothes, it will lie prostrate and motionless, subject to being dragged away. In Osak Seon (五色線) it is written that "Sun Sa-myak (遜思邈) saw a wounded little green snake and saved it, and later a young man in white came down from his horse, bowed in thanks, and led him to a castle. There, there was someone like a king, and he offered him light silk, pearls, and gold, but he did not accept them and took thirty prescriptions from the Dragon Palace." (From this point to here is a contribution from Ahn Jong-won (安鍾元).)"

8. First Aid Room for Snake Bites

Before discussing first aid for snake bites, I would like to mention a few ways to repel snakes. Snakes like cleanliness and dislike yang energy, so it is important to keep the house dry and avoid realgar (石雄黃) and Cheon-gung (川芎). In the past, when Emperor Hwang (皇帝氏) went to Bu-gu (多蛇之地), a place with many snakes, he followed the words of Gwangseongja (廣成子) and wore realgar. In Joseon, women and children wore realgar on their tanggi (당긔) or headgear, and in rural farmhouses, they planted Cheon-gung near the sauce jars. They also avoided bamboo resin (댓진) and centipedes (蜈蚣), so farmers carried them when going to the fields. If unfortunately bitten by a snake, quickly tie the affected area tightly with rope or hair (near the joint) to prevent the venom from circulating in the bloodstream. If the snake is attached to the person and does not fall off, pouring warm urine on it will make it fall off immediately. It is of course good to immediately incise the affected area and draw out the venom, and it is okay to suck it out with your mouth, because snake venom is a type of protein that is digested and rendered harmless when it meets stomach or intestinal fluid, so it is safe to absorb it with your mouth. After that, of course, you should seek a doctor and receive inpatient treatment, but among common remedies, applying a poultice of sheep's sorrel (양渧菜, *soru-jangi*) after pounding it is immediately effective. This is because when I was young, I was bitten by a poisonous snake and reached a critical point, but a monk from Geumgangsan (金剛山) came and taught me that remedy, and when I tried it, it was immediately effective, and others have tried it several times and all have had good results. Also, pounding and applying Asarum sieboldii (細辛, *jipsin-namul*) is especially effective. However, since I am not an expert, I am only speaking from experience.

9. The Great Flourishing Store

The person most suitable for the manager of this store, considering their experience and trustworthiness, would probably be a snake catcher (깍적이, *ggakjeogi* = *ttangkun*). The story of the snake catcher was already published in detail in this journal after the organizer explored his digging holes, so I will omit it here and briefly introduce the story of selling snakes. The snakes that these snake catchers catch are mainly pit vipers (蝮蛇), gold snakes (金蛇), and black snakes (烏蛇). Pit vipers are generally 50 jeon wholesale and 1 won to 2 won 50 jeon retail. Gold snakes are 1 won wholesale and 2 won to 4 or 5 won retail. Black snakes have the best color among snakes, so they are 2 won wholesale and 5 or 6 won retail. White snakes are the most expensive, costing up to 30 or 40 won retail (last year's price). The best times to catch snakes are May, June, September, and October. They are not caught well in July and August. Pit vipers gather regularly in May, June, August, and September, like some kind of competition season for humans, so the "snake catchers" take advantage of that time to hunt pit vipers. Guangdong (廣東) is the best place in China for snakes, and since snakes are often used for food there, there are often places in the city that sell snake medicine. In Japan, Shiga (滋賀) and Ehime (愛媛) prefectures have the most snakes. About 300,000 striped snakes, 200,000 pit vipers, and 2,000 black snakes are caught nationwide each year, and the average daily income of a snake catcher is about 4 or 5 won. In Joseon, Gyeonggi (경기) province has the most, followed by Chungcheong (충청) and Jeolla (전라), and although the export volume is not accurate, it is about 200,000 to 300,000 snakes, and the export destinations are Japan and China.

10. A Stage for Ten Thousand People to Laugh

When it comes to the entertainment of this exposition, anyone might easily think of introducing the snake dances of Indian or South Sea natives. However, that is too disgusting to even talk about, so instead, I will tell a funny story about snakes and close the exposition. In the past, there was a member of the Yi clan royalty named Paseongnyeong (坡城令), who was so infatuated with women that he absolutely trusted whatever a woman said as if it were the word of a god, and even if a woman farted, he thought it smelled fragrant. Once, he heard that Namwon (남원) in Jeolla province was famous for its courtesans, so he went there and spent several months intimately with a courtesan. When he had to return to Seoul, he could not bear to part with the courtesan, and he held her hand and cried and cried. The courtesan, seeing that his attitude was too excessive, pretended to cry and whispered, "Since you love me so much, even if I die and become a gullyong (구렁이, large snake), I will come to find you again." He took the courtesan's words as the truth and took them to heart. At that time, there was a magistrate of Gongju (공주) named Jeong Hui-hyeon (鄭希賢), who always ridiculed Paseongnyeong's (坡城令) actions in his heart. Once, when that Yi person came to Gongju (공주), Magistrate Jeong (鄭) decided to play a trick on him. He caught a large gullyong (구렁이) and put it under the mat, leaving only the tail slightly exposed, and then sat him down on that spot. After a while, the magistrate cleared his throat and, pointing to the tail of the gullyong (구렁이), pretended to be surprised and said, "Oh my, what is this!" When Yi saw the tail of the gullyong (구렁이), he thought that the courtesan from Namwon (남원) had died and come back as a gullyong (구렁이), and tears fell from his eyes like rain. He said, "Oh, you poor thing. You died because of me. How could I have known you felt this way? Even though you were a courtesan, you were the most virtuous and loyal woman in the world." He cried for a long time, then took off his underwear and carefully wrapped the gullyong (구렁이), buried it near the guest house, held a memorial service, and even erected a monument, which made everyone laugh and became a topic of conversation in the capital and countryside for a long time. Everyone! What do you think? If you misunderstand the words of a courtesan, you might end up crying over a dead gullyong (구렁이) like this Yi person. Be careful. Shh... (closing) <157-159>

萬國蛇類博覽會 續開
蛇龍은 高麗 俗樂名이오. 蛇矛는 兵器名이니 晋書에 陳安이 右手에 丈八蛇矛를 잡엇다는 것이 그것이오. 또 三國時에 張飛도 丈八蛇矛를 가젓섯다. 蛇醫는 守宮의 一名이니 南楚方言이오. 蛇紋石은 鑛物이니 橄欖石과 角閃石 등의 분해로 변성한 것으로 其紋이 蛇皮와 가틈으로 이름을 그리 지엿다. (일명 溫石) 蛇*용母는 爬虫類니 도마뱀과 同類異屬이요. 蛇葡萄는 다년생 蔓草요. 蛇含草는 草名이니 俗名은 小龍牙오. 또 일명은 紫背龍牙다. 이 풀에 대하야는 자미잇는 이약이가 한아 잇스니 녯날에 엇던 농부가 본즉, 뱀 한 마리가 被傷하엿는데 다른 뱀이 이 풀을 물어다가 그 상처에 붓친 즉시 상처가 암으러서 뱀이 여전이 잘 감으로 농부가 그 풀을 뜨더서 사람의 상한데에도 붓치니 神效가 잇슴으로 인하야 蛇含草라고 이름하얏다 한다. 蛇腹紋은 古琴의 斷紋이 有한 자이니 古詩에 琴漫蛇腹紋이란 것이 그것이다. (亦名蛇蚹) 蛇黃은 冬 중에 뱀의 보금자리 치고 잇던 黃土니 약용에 쓰고 또 蛇腹 중에 牛黃과 가티 잇는 것을 蛇黃이라고도 하야 약에 쓴다. 蛇蛻는 脫皮한 것이니 약용에 쓴다. 이 蛇蛻에도 또한 이약이가 한아 잇스니 녯적 晋나라 때에 밀폐한 武庫 중에 난데업는 꿩이 울무로 여러 사람이 이상이 녁이엿더니 박물학자 張華가 말하되 그것은 반듯이 蛇의 化物이라 함으로 문을 열어 본즉 꿩이 잇는 곳에 과연 蛇蛻가 잇섯다고 한다. (晋書)蛇角은 寶名이니 山海經注에 碧犀라 解하고 蛇苺는 다년생초니 覆盆子의 일종이오(속칭 뱀딸긔) 蛇床子는 다년생초니 약용에 쓴다. 蛇漠瘼=蝮蛇의 침(延味)을 蛇漠이라하고 蛇漠이 무든 草, 木, 石에 접촉하야 生한 瘡을 蛇漠瘡이라 한다.(속칭 돌쇅이) 其外에 人名으로는 新羅僧의  蛇福이 잇고(元曉와 同時) 지명으로는 蛇梁, 蛇山, 蛇島 등이 허다하나 玆에 略한다.

8. 蛇咬의 應急治療室
뱀에 물인 응급치료법을 말하기 전에 먼저 驅除法을 몃가지 말하겟다. 뱀은 潔氣를 조와하고 陽氣를 시려하는 까닭에 무엇보다도 집안을 燥강하게 할 것은 물론이고 石雄黃이나 川芎을 忌하는 까닭에 녯적에 皇帝氏가 負丘(多蛇之地)를 갈때에도 廣成子의 말을 좃차서 雄黃을 차고 갓섯고 朝鮮에서도 자래로 부인 소아들이 당긔나 頭盖에 石雄黃을 달고 다니며 시골 농가에서는 川芎을 장독간에다 심으고 또 댓진(烟竹진)과 蜈蚣(지네)을 忌함으로 농군이 풀밧에 갈때에는 그것을 바로고 가긔도 한다.그리고 不幸히 뱀에게 물인 때에는 急急히 피해처를 노끈이나 머리털(다리꼭지)가튼 것으로 잔득 매여 毒液이 血液에 流通되지 안토록 하고 뱀이 사람에게 붓터서 떠러지지 안커던 더운 소변을 찌언지면 즉시 떠러진다. 피해처는 즉시 절개하고 毒血을 뽑아내는 것이 조흔 것은 물론이고 입으로 빨어내도 관계치 안으니 그것은 蛇의 毒液은 일종의 白質로서 胃液이나 腸液을 만나면 消和되야 無害物로 되는 고로 입으로 흡수하야도 무방한 所以다. 그 다음에는 醫師를 차저서 入院治療를 할 것은 물론이어니와 常藥中으로는 羊渧菜(소루장이)로 浮黃찜질(항아리에 소루장이를 두드려 늣코 보통 부황찜질하덧 함)즉일 神效가 잇다. 이것은 필자 자신이 어렷을 때에 독사에 물니여서 가장 急險한 시기에까지 이르럿는대 금강산 노승이 와서 그 약을 가르처줌으로 시험한 즉, 즉시 神效가 잇섯고 또 다른 사람도 여러번 試用하야 다 得效한 것이다. 또 細辛(집신나물)을 두드려븟처도 특효가 잇다.그러나 나는 전문가가 안인 까닭에 그저 경험상으로만 말할 뿐이다.

9. 天下大繁昌의 賣店
이 賣店의 주임은 러력으로 보나 신용으로 보나 아마 『깍적이=땅꾼』이 제일 적임자일 것이다. 땅꾼에 관한 이약이는 언제인가 이 주최자가 그 掘穴을 탐사하야 본지에 자서이 발표하얏기로 略하고 여긔에는 뱀의 賣買하는 이약이를 잠간 소개하랴 한다. 이 땅꾼들의 잡는 뱀은 주로 蝮蛇, 金蛇, 烏蛇 등인데 蝮蛇는 도매로 대개 50전 소매로 일원 내지 2원50전이고 金蛇는 도매 1원, 소매 2원 내지 4,5원이고 烏蛇는 蛇 중 색태가 제일 조흠으로 도매 2원 소매 5,6원이고 白蛇는 最貴한 자로서 소매 3,40원까지 간다고 한다.(작년 표준) 뱀의 제일 잘 잽히는 때는 5, 6, 월 9, 10월이오. 7, 8월은 잘 잽히지 안는다 한다. 蝮蛇는 5, 6월과 8, 9월이 사람으로 치면 무슨 대회시기와 가티 정기로 모혀드는 일이 잇기 때문에 『땅꾼』들은 그때를 이용하야 蝮蛇 산양을 한다. 중국에서 뱀이 맨키로는 廣東이 제일인데 그곳에서는 자래로 뱀을 식용에 쓰는 고로 시가에서 蛇藥를 파는 곳이 종종 잇고 일본은 滋賀, 愛媛兩縣이 제일 만흔데 전국에 일년에 잡는 것이 縞蛇가 약 30만匹 蝮蛇가 20만필, 鳥蛇가 2천필인데 뱀잡이꾼의 일일 평균 수입이 약 4,5원 된다 하며 조선은 경기가 제일 만코 其次는 충청, 전라인데 輸出數는 자섯치 못하나 약 2,30만匹이 되고 輸出地는 일본 及 중국이라 한다.
