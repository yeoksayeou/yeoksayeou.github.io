별건곤 제19호
1929년 02월 01일
夢見春香記 (Dreaming of Ch'unhyang)
八角亭 李道令
문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

"Have a dream. And meet Ch'unhyang."

To meet Ch'unhyang even in a dream is more welcome than meeting Chang Fei or Kwan Un-chang. However, for someone who averages 24 hours a day and only sleeps three or four hours on a good day, and even then, cannot close their eyes without the blessing of "flourishing virtue (가루 못진 德)," to say, "Take this opportunity and have a dream, force yourself to have a dream," is enough to make one angry.

But meet Ch'unhyang! Ch'unhyang! It is Ch'unhyang. Having borrowed the spirit of Mt. Chiri and received the essence of the Chŏksŏng River, her body was born in Namp'yŏng ŭp, but the world calls her an unparalleled beauty (絶代佳人)!

The thought alone is somewhat enticing.

However, upon further reflection, is not this Ch'unhyang a woman who not only possesses the beauty of Nokchu but also harbors the literary talent of Sŏl T'a'o in her heart and takes the etiquette of Mokran as her example, becoming the eternally virtuous woman (貞烈婦) whose name is renowned as Sŏng Ch'unhyang? How could she, if I, a mere humble scholar (寒士) in this mundane world, were to traverse the distant and secluded path of ten million ri, deign to acknowledge me, let alone bestow upon me even a single frown or smile (一嚬半笑)?

Bah! If she were to come in my dreams, how could I lie down and wait for sleep that does not come, and how could I believe I could meet her in a dream that cannot be dreamed? <108>

However, every morning when I enter the editorial office,

"Did you dream last night?"
"I have a vague recollection of a dream, but..."
"Ah - a big problem. All the other manuscripts are finished."

And the day after that,

"Did you meet her last night?"
"I did meet someone, but it was a preposterous Mottogi..."

The atmosphere of the editorial room, urging on others' dreams, seems to have become more impatient than my own nerves.

"You need to sleep to dream, and you need to dream to see your beloved..." If this were that kind of beloved, meeting and conversing with them in dreams without such waiting would not be so extraordinary, but to wait with anticipation, like a burning star, to meet Sŏng Ch'unhyang, the lover of the ancient magistrate Yi, whom I have never even seen in a dream, as if she were a nameless nobody of the present day, either tonight or tomorrow night, is more absurd, incomparable, and utterly ridiculous than the wild fantasies of "journalism"!

I wrote in large letters with the chief editor's pen on a paper pasted near my head: "Heaven and Earth, Gods, Sun, Moon, Stars, Kuan-yin, all Buddhas, Five Hundred Arhats, Four Seas Dragon Kings, Eight Divine Generals, and Lord of the City (城主*王님), I pray! I pray for a dream meeting with Sŏfang Chŏngsa Sŏng Ch'unhyang. May the Gods of Heaven and Earth be moved and grant my wish, I beg, I beg." Every time I went to sleep, I took a ritual bath and offered sincere prayers, but the shadow of Ch'unhyang, whom I longed to meet, never appeared, and instead, masked figures and men in conical hats, whom I had never imagined in waking life, would jump out and block my path with six-sided clubs, or the wretched Mottogi would leap out from some corner and grab my hand, begging for affection. Bah! Is it my fate not to see Ch'unhyang? Or is it Ch'unhyang's fate not to see me? If I am determined to see Ch'unhyang, I may not die a natural death but instead meet a violent end at the hands of some evil spirit. I give up. I give up completely.

The editorial department, having no other choice, also gave up on "Dream Meeting with Ch'unhyang," and although it is regrettable, the long-awaited editing has come to an end. Last night, there was a party at some yŏjŏng (요정, courtesan house), with one room being filled with the sounds of "pour it, drink it."

I declined the alcohol that I cannot drink, but as the cups kept coming, I became completely drunk, and even though it was just a room, the sounds flowing in were those of kiseng, and the excitement rose as I saw and did not see things.

Having nowhere to vent this excitement, I looked around and saw a painting of a peach blossom garden on the wall. I do not know whose hand created it, but each branch was full of blossoms, each bloom a smile, and the sound of owls seemed to echo in my ears, and butterflies came and went before my eyes. In the winter room, the spring scenery was resplendent. Just as Chuang Chou became a butterfly and the butterfly became Chuang Chou, I became one with everything and entered the Taoist Arcadia (武陵桃源). It was said that the retired courtesan Wŏlmae conceived Ch'unhyang from a single peach blossom; <109> today, I have accidentally achieved the Ch'unhyang dream that I had so earnestly desired from a painting of a peach blossom garden.

My body became the wind, and the wind became my body, and I arrived at a place. Is this the Western Paradise (서방정토)? Is this the world of utmost bliss (극락세계)? Fragrant flowers and jade-like herbs were blooming, and a tall pavilion soared amidst radiant colorful clouds.

I happily arrived at the gate and, straightening my clothes, called out, "Come here!" using the words of the mundane world to find the owner. A young boy suddenly bowed before me. Even though I had come to see Ch'unhyang, I could not, out of courtesy, fail to seek out Yi Taegam first, so I asked, "Is Taegam here?" He replied, "He has just left on official business and is not here." I thought to myself, "Yi Mongnyong is still serving as an aide even in Okgyŏng," and instead thought it was a good opportunity to meet Ch'unhyang, so I did not hesitate and said, "To tell you the truth, I am not yet a celestial being, but a man of the mundane world living in Hanyang, Chosŏn, Haedong. I have not come to see Taegam, but I have come after enduring countless hardships as if performing a hundred days of sincerity to see Lady Sŏng, so tell the Lady of my intentions."

In the flower garden beneath the stairs, white flowers seemed to be ever-blooming, and the faint sound of wind chimes truly made it seem like a celestial realm. Following the boy who came out again, I passed through the flower garden, climbed the stone steps, and before I knew it, I had entered a room.

The interior was surprisingly simple. A few old paintings hung on the walls, and a few ancient books were piled on the mother-of-pearl dressing table and writing desk.

As I looked at the mother-of-pearl dressing table, I could not help but picture in my mind the scene when Ch'unhyang parted with Yi Toryŏng:

"What use is it for Ch'unhyang, who has no husband, to decorate herself and keep house?" And smashing the mirror, dressing table, and Four Treasures of the Study.

And when I looked at the painting on the wall of four or five white-haired old men playing chess, the pitiful scene of Ch'unhyang's mother bringing the beggar son-in-law Yi Toryŏng and arriving at the prison gate came to my mind:

"Ahga, Ch'unhyang-ah! Ahga, Ch'unhyang-ah!" Ch'unhyang startled awake and said, "Who is it that seeks me? Who is it that seeks me? Does Ch'ŏbu Hŏ Yu, who washed his ears in Kisan Chŏnsŏu Pyŏlgŏn'gon, seek me? Do the old men of the Shangshan Sa'ho seek me to play chess..."

I do not know what aspects of Ch'unhyang the world finds beautiful and admirable, but I interpret Ch'unhyang's beauty and Ch'unhyang's greatness in this way.

Ch'unhyang is the first woman in our country to engage in free love, and she regarded gold, honor, and even her own life as mere weeds for the sake of love. That is the beauty of Ch'unhyang. And she resisted the unjust system of that era to the end, enduring thousands of hardships, and never yielding even once, even if it meant becoming a soul under the rod. That is the greatness of Ch'unhyang. If "Juliet" in the West took a sleeping pill for her lover "Romeo" and lay in the tomb, how easy a task that would be compared to the trials Ch'unhyang endured! Therefore, how passive was "Juliet"! Truly, Ch'unhyang is a beautiful woman and a great figure throughout history, both East and West. Even though Yi Toryŏng, whom she had so longed for, came to her in the guise of a most wretched beggar, she was not at all displeased, and furthermore, she was concerned that her coarse mother might mistreat her husband, so she instructed Hyangdan and her mother: <110>

"Mother, listen to me. As soon as it is light, light the lamps in the Puyo'ngdang where we two made our vows, spread out the quilt and pillows where we two slept, choose a few rolls of silk from the third layer of the cabinet in the next room to make clothes for my husband, prepare his hat and manggŏn (망건, horsehair headband), and put his everyday necessities in the purple case, and make T'aesahye (太史鞋, shoes) with jade-colored bottoms and purple trim, and borrow 2,000 nyang from the songjwa (成座首, successful candidate) of the hyanggyo (향교, local confucian academy) to manage the household and make sure there is plenty of rice and meat on alternate days so that the family does not go hungry, and make sure Mother eats well too. If you tell people that I am not home and you get angry and make him uncomfortable, how can he feel at ease coming from so far away? Knowing my temperament, if you mistreat him, I will be an unfilial daughter and commit suicide, so please treat him well."

"Hyangdan, are you there? Make sure your master is comfortable and well-fed, and if he complains about anything, it is entirely up to you. Serve him sincerely before and after breakfast, and send a messenger to Master Yi at Tongmunbat to get medicine and brew two doses a day. I know your heart, so I will not ask for more."

Ch'unhyang truly loved Yi Toryŏng lovingly. She showed for all time "what love is." She showed for all time "what loyalty is." And she showed for all time "what injustice is."

Oh! Beautiful Ch'unhyang! Brave Ch'unhyang! Our proud Chosŏn Ch'unhyang! I muttered to myself in this way. Then, startled by the sound of the sliding door, I realized that Ch'unhyang was already in the room, and I hurriedly stood up. Ch'unhyang bowed politely to me and then offered me a seat, sitting down herself as well. What surprised me most was that she was still a young woman, like a flower bud, not yet thirty years old, and that her clothes were as simple as the interior, made of light silk like a dragonfly's wings. Her snow-white skin and flower-like face, which could not be hidden, were such that even a begonia in the morning sun could not compete with her beauty, and even a lotus wet with dew would be ashamed. It was no wonder that the new magistrate refused the posts of Miryang and Sŏhŭng and came to Namwŏn to find Ch'unhyang, and I thought that it was not unreasonable for him to take Ch'unhyang and say, "The former magistrate Toryŏng touched your head, but you are not yet grown, so it is fortunate. Dress yourself prettily and serve me from today."

"You have come such a long way..."

"Yes. The reason I have come such a long way, disregarding all difficulties, is that you surely know that the relationships between men and women in our world have become unspeakably depraved. Although there are more scholars and education for both men and women has developed more than in the time of Lady Sŏng, and people call themselves cultured and say that we live in a civilized age, when I quietly observe and think about what is happening, there is certainly no progress when it comes to human beings. People trust a single contract more than they trust each other, and a husband trusts his wife and a wife trusts her husband less than they trust a broken flower or a hundred-carat diamond on a finger. What can we call human progress? Thinking about it, I feel overwhelmed with despair, and every time I feel despair, I naturally long for elders like Lady Sŏng, and even though I know that the worlds of the living and the dead are different, I have come here, presuming upon your kindness. I hope you will not find fault with me." <111>

"Thank you. But it is rather presumptuous of you to think of me, who has nothing to boast of for future generations, in that way and to come to me."

Ch'unhyang smiled slightly and fiddled with the jade ring on her left ring finger with her silk ribbon-like fingers.

When I looked at the jade ring, the tearful tragedy of their past came to my mind again. At the scene where Yi Toryŏng parted with Ch'unhyang, he stroked the silk brocade and gave a mirror to Ch'unhyang, saying, "If the pure heart of a man is like a mirror, how can it change even after ten million years?" Ch'unhyang received the mirror and put it in her bosom, and taking off the ring she was wearing, she gave it to Yi Toryŏng, saying, "I wish you, sir, may be as constant as this jade ring and may never be separated like this ring," and wiped away her tears with her sleeve. Even to this day, the two of them must be whispering to each other about their romance, which will never disappear, from that single ring.

Ch'unhyang continued to speak.

"Since you say that, I myself, whenever I sit with Taegam, have lamented the depraved nature of the young men and women of Chosŏn. Those who call themselves thinkers, talking about the masses and discussing society, how can their personal lives be models even for beasts? They say that cutting their hair is for efficiency in work and that not observing distinctions between men and women is to avoid restrictions in sports, but what is the efficiency in work of those new women? Excuse me, but is there such a thing as efficiency in work that prevents them from being dragged around by this man and that man? And what are the restrictions in sports? Are they saying that by not observing distinctions between men and women, there are restrictions on having a new man every day? When you look closely, they are thorough in their theories, and their words and actions are consistent, which is admirable to some extent. However, in their dirty sexual desires, they are as willing to compromise unconditionally as perverted sex offenders, and their so-called ideas change color every day, with the ideology of yesterday being right and the ideology of today being left, along with the movement of their male lovers. Who are they guiding? Who will follow them? It is not that there are no warriors among them. However, I, Ch'unhyang, being an old-fashioned woman, cannot fundamentally agree with their actions. In today's human society, as I said earlier, the reason why civilization has not become the culture of human beings themselves is because it has not become the civilization of both men and women. Hasn't it all been developed for the sake of men? Therefore, truly enlightened women should not cut their hair and come out to engage in male attire movements, nor should they unknowingly assist in the male-dominated civilization, but should let women return to being women themselves, which is the true cultural movement of humanity. Just as there is the sun and the moon in the sky and there is a distinction between day and night, the distinction between men and women should be clearly distinguished in terms of lifestyle and lifestyle content. Furthermore, when you look at the end of the so-called new-style women these days, they are reluctant even for their comrades and friends to come to visit them from the day they manage to start a family after going from Kim Nam to Yi Nam. <112> And when you look at it, haven't they been engaged in a quest for men and indulgence in men rather than a liberation movement? What about Ibsen's Nora? What about Kollontai's Vasilisa? But woman is a woman by nature, and I want to say one thing to you new women and to women politicians around the world: "Please thoroughly recognize what a woman is before you act." These days, I also know here, but Kollontai, who brought out Genia after Vasilisa, is truly a clever woman. I don't even know if the women of Chosŏn these days—especially those who call themselves new-style women—know what Vasilisa is or what Genia is before they go around. All they are running around with bloodshot eyes, desperate to find a new male comrade. There is an old saying that a boastful woman is unchaste (衒女不貞). Since there has never been a woman who knows everything who is not unchaste, we should first hope that there are no boastful women rather than hoping for chaste women. Look at the women whose names were mentioned for a while. They are so attached to the names they had that they are always worried about how they will end up selling themselves for a few pennies. Excuse me, but <113> they would rather take off their underwear and go to the pawnshop... Since they are like this, what more can be said about the kisaeng society? The Namgang River in Chinju must still be flowing today, but how can its clear waters ever wash the hair of a woman like Nongae again? When I look down on the kisaeng of Chosŏn today, I sometimes find it funny and sometimes pitiful. Whether their faces are beautiful or ugly, there is nothing to complain about, but why can't they dress like kisaeng? They suddenly tie their waists with Japanese women's 'koshimaki' cords and carry leather 'opera bags', and all they look for is 'anooruko' to sing, which makes me feel sorry for them. I think that kisaeng have disappeared from Chosŏn a long time ago. Not only kisaeng but also P'ungnyu Hyŏbyong (風流俠容, romantic knights) have disappeared, and I don't think there is even a Kyŏndal (乾達). Those who go to Myŏngwŏlgwan or Sikdowan all seem to have come out after working as 'banto' in Japanese shops, and why do they enjoy 'oyokobushi' so much... Now that I have come this far, I have said many rude things. Please forgive me." Ch'unhyang blinked her starlike eyes a few times and hid her jade-like teeth under her lips.

"Indeed. Everything has lost its color and virtue, and in our world today, it is as if we are meeting foreigners who do not understand each other, and there is no etiquette or decency. Now that Lady Sŏng knows so clearly about the situation in our world, do you have any particular requests for young men and women like us?"

"What request would I have? Since you say so, if I were to say just one thing based on my experience, it would be that it is for the best life for any man or woman, for the morals of society in general, and for having the highest form of love, it is to have one ring for life (一生一環). Just because rings are common these days does not mean that a man can buy dozens of rings in his lifetime, but that he should buy only one ring in his lifetime. And just because a woman can receive several rings in one night does not mean that she should receive more than ten rings, but that she should swear on her life and receive only one ring in her lifetime. I am only conveying this one word."

Ch'unhyang lowered her eyebrows and sat down, fiddling with the jade ring on her hand again.

"Thank you. And one thing I would like to know is what happened to the servant Pangja (房子) and Hyangdan afterwards?"

"Pangja and Hyangdan seemed to be in love with each other from the time they were in the Lower World, so I discussed it with Taegam and immediately arranged their wedding. Now they are also living happily in Okgyŏng, reminiscing about the old days."

At that moment, the sound of "gee-oh-dee-kay" rang out from somewhere. Thinking that there was a "radio" even in the celestial realm, I snapped to my senses and realized that it was all a dream. I jumped up and shouted, "I dreamed! I dreamed!"

"What! A dream? About Ch'unhyang?" "Yes, a Ch'unhyang dream."

The table roared with excitement! And everyone raised their glasses for "Ch'unhyang Dream Forever." <114>

「꿈을 꾸시오. 그리고 春香이를 맛나 보시요.」
꿈에라도 春香을 맛나보라는 것은 張飛나 關雲將을 만나보라는 것보다는 고마운 말이다. 그러나 일일 평균 24시간에 잘 자는 날이라야 세시간 네시간, 그것도 「가루 못진」德이 안이면 눈을 붓처 보지도 못하는 사람에게 고틈을 타서라도 꿈을 꾸시요, 억지로라도 꿈을 꾸시요, 하는 것은 화가 난다면 화도 낼만 한 일이다.
그러나 春香을 맛나보라! 春香을! 春香이다. 智異山 靈을 빌고 赤城江에 정을 바더 그의 몸이 南原 一邑에 태여낫스나 세상이 그를 부르기를 絶代佳人이라 하는 春香이를!
그것 한번 마음이 솔깃하여지지 안는 것도 안이다.
그러나 일을 다시 생각해보면 그 春香이란 게집은 綠珠의 색을 가진 미인일 뿐만 안이라 薛濤의 문장을 그의 가슴에 품엇스며 목란의 예절을 본을 바더 만고의 貞烈婦로 일홈 놉흔 成春香이가 안인가. 나 가튼 속세의 일개 寒士로 幽界의 먼먼 길을 不遠 멧 천만리하고 차저 간들 그가 무슨 명목으로 나를 대하며 그 엇지 一嚬半笑인들 나에게 던질소냐!
예라 제가 꿈에 오면 내가 보지 안오는 잠을 엇지 누어 기대리며 꾸어지지 안는 꿈을 엇지 그를 맛나 보려니 밋을소냐＜108＞
그러나 아츰마다 社에 들어가면
「엇저녁엔 꾸엇소?」
「무슨 꿈을 꾸엇는지 생각이 암암하니...」
「하- 큰일 낫군 다른 원고들은 다 끗이 낫는데.」
또 그 이튼날
「엇저녁엔 맛낫소?」
「맛나기는 맛낫소만 당치안은 莫德이를...」
남의 꿈만 재촉하고 안젓는 編輯室의 공기도 나의 신경보다 못하지 안케 조급하여진 것갓다.
「잠을 자야 꿈을 꾸지 꿈을 꿔야 님을 보지...」하는 이러한 님이라면 이럿틋 기다리지 안터래도 꿈속에 맛나 情話함이 그리 珍事는 안이렷만, 그야말로 꿈에 한번도 본 적 업는 고대 李宰相의 애인 成春香을 당치 안은 현세의 한낫 무명 필부로 오날 저녁에나 맛나볼가 래일 저녁에나 맛나볼가 하고 星火가티 기다림이란 「쩌나리즘」의 狂想이란 것보다 그 얼마나 맹랑 無比하고 엉터리 無敵한 天外之寄想이랴.
머리마테다는 주필로 대서하야 써붓치기를 「天地之神 日月星辰 觀音諸佛 五百羅漢 四海龍王 八府神將 城主*王님 비나이다. 西方精士 成春香과 夢中會見을 비나이다. 天地神明 感動하사 所願成就伏乞伏乞」이라 하엿고 잘 때마다 목욕재계하고 지성으로 발원기도하엿스나 맛나고저 하는 春香 그림자도 오지 안코 생시엔 뜻도 하지 안엇든 탈 쓴 놈 벙거지 쓴 놈들이 뛰여나와 륙모방맹이로 길을 막고 수작하는 것이 안이면 萬苦 薄色莫德이 가튼 것이 어느 구석에서 뛰여나와 손을 잡고 願情하는 꿈들이다. 예라! 春香이가 나를 못볼 팔자이냐. 내가 春香이를 못볼 팔자이냐. 그여히 春香을 보고말녀들다는 내가 내 命에 죽지 못하고 엇던 악귀에게 횡사할지 몰으겟다. 단념이다. 단단념이다.
편집국에서도 하릴 업시 「夢中春香會見記」만은 단념하고 말엇스니 유감은 유감이나 오래 애쓰든 편집은 일단락하라. 어제 저녁에는 어느 요정에 한 방은 미러 부어라 먹어라 한는 판이엿섯다.
나는 못먹는 술에 사양은 하엿스나 도라가는 잔에 한잔 두잔 바든 것이 곤드레 만드레 하게 되데다 房은 저房이라 하더래도 흘너오는 소리가 기생은 기생이라 물건은 보고 못보고 흥은 날대로 일어낫다.
이 흥을 쏘다 노흘 곳이 업서 두리변거리다가 壁上에서 一幅桃林을 바라보게 되엿스니 엇던 화공의 솜씨인지는 알 수 업스되 가지마다 열닌 꼿이 송이마다 우슴이라 부얼의 우는 소리 귓속에 은은한 듯 双去双來 蝴蝶들은 눈압헤 분분한 듯 冬室 壁上에 춘향이 爛爛한지라. 莊周가 蝴蝶되듯 蝴蝶이 莊周되듯 玄玄無我하여 武陵桃源에 들엇스니 그야말로 退妓月梅가 一枝桃花로 春香을 孕胎하엿다더니＜109＞ 오날에 나는 一幅의 桃林圖에서 지성하든 春香夢을 偶然得成한 것이다.
내몸이 바람되여 바람이 내몸되여 어느 한곳에 다다르니 이곳이 서방정토라 하는 덴가. 이곳이 극락세계라 하는 덴가. 珙花瑤草가 芳暢한데 燦然한 彩雲속에 高層一閣이 소사 잇다.
나는 快然히 문전에 당도하여 옷깃을 바로 잡으며 「이리 오너라.」하고 속세에서 하는 말로 주인을 차젓더니 동자 한 아희가 어느듯 내 압헤 揖하고 섯는지라. 春香을 보러왓다할지라도 예의상으로 이대감을 먼저 찻지 안을 수 업서 「대감긔서 게옵시냐?」하고 물은 즉 「맛츰公故에 드러가옵시고 안게심니다.」한다. 나는 속으로 『李夢龍은 玉京에 와서도 輔國노릇을 하나.』하고 도리혀 春香을 맛나보기 조흔 긔회라 하여 서슴지 안코 말하엿다. 나로 말하면 사실인즉 아직 仙人이 안이라 海東 朝鮮 漢陽에 사는 俗戒 人間으로 대감을 뵈오려 온 것도 안이요, 忠烈夫人 成氏마마를 뵈옵자고 百日致誠을 하다십히 萬難을 不顧하고 떠나온 사람이니 그 뜻을 마마님끠 아뢰여라 하엿다.
階下의 화단에는 백화가 常春인 듯 은은한 풍경소리 果是 仙境이라 할만하다. 다시 나온 동자의 뒤를 따라 화원을 도라 石階를 올나 나는 어느듯 어느 방안에 들어섯든 것이다.
실내 裝置는 의외에 수수하니 古畵 멧폭이 壁上에 걸니엿고 자개 體鏡과 문갑우에 고서 멧길이 싸혀 잇슬 뿐이다.

그리고 벽 우에서 백발 노인 사오인이 바둑 두고 안젓는 그림을 바라볼 때 春香母가 걸인사위 李道令을 다리고 獄門前에 당도하야

세상에서는 春香이를 엇더한 점을 아름답다 하며 엇더한 점을 장하다 하는지 알 수 업스나 나는 春香의 아름다운 것과 春香의 장한 것을 이와 가티 해석한다.
春香은 우리나라에서 누구보다도 제일 먼저 자유연애한 처녀이며 사랑을 위하여선 황금도 명예도 목숨까지도 草芥視한 것이 春香의 아름다운 것이며 그 시대의 그 불법한 제도와 끗끗내 반항하여 千幸萬苦를 一身에 바드며 杖下의 魂이 될지언정 百折에 一屈이 업슨 것이 춘향의 장한 것이다. 서양에 「쭈리엣트」는 情男 「로미오」를 위하여 催眠藥을 먹고 墓內에 가 누엇섯스나 春香이가 밧는＜110＞ 고초에 빅인다면 그 얼마나 용이한 일이며 따라서 「쭈리엣트」는 그 얼마나 소극적이엿스랴. 실로 春香은 고금동서를 통하여 아름다운 게집이며 장한 인물이로다. 그럿케 고대하든 李道令이 자기의 모-든 기대를 끈을 만치 초초한 걸인의 행색으로 차저왓것만 그는 조곰도 불쾌한 미련이 업섯슬 뿐만 안이라. 도리혀 걸걸한 모친의 말슴이 털끗만치라도 자기 남편을 박대할가 하여 그는 香丹이와 자기 모친에게 무어라 당부하엿나.

『香丹이 게 잇나냐. 서방님 寢需丹節 안녕하시고 불평하신 것은 전혀 네 하기에 달녓스니 조반 전후 지성으로 供饋하고 동문밧 李주부께 和題내다 藥을 시여 하로 두첩씩 대리여라. 네마음 내가 알고 더 부탁 안이한다.』
春香은 李道令을 과연 사랑답게 사랑하엿다. 그는

春香은 약간 북그러운 우슴을 지으면서 그 명주고름 가튼 손꼬락으로 자기의 왼편 무명지에 낑여 잇는 玉指環을 만적만적 굴녀보고 잇섯다.
