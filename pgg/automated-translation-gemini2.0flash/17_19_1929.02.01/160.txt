기사제목	學校와 家庭의 時急問題 性敎育 實施 方策 (The Urgent Problem of Schools and Homes: Measures to Implement Sex Education)
기사형태	논설 (Editorial)

Before the Springtime Engine Starts
A Joint Meeting of Boseong High School Faculty

This issue of sex education is indeed a significant one. Some may say that it is still premature in our homes and society, but they are absolutely mistaken. It is more a matter of being too late than too early. How can we neglect the issue of sex (性), which leads one's self down the path of prosperity or ruin? We have been blindly following traditional Eastern morality, and its harmful effects are not insignificant. Whenever the topic of sex is brought up, it is dismissed as vulgar. Because of this, during the springtime when the eyes of sex open and the sprouts of affection grow, the lack of understanding about sex darkens the hoped-for future and leads to a life of ruin. How many such cases are there? The reason for this is that, at home, we are raised with the principle of "boys and girls should not sit together after the age of seven" (男女七歲에 不同席式) and, at school, we are not taught anything about sex. Because of a lack of sex education, young people do not understand the opposite sex and, when sexual desire awakens, they cannot control their momentary impulses and rush in without knowing what they are doing. Whose fault is this? It is the fault of the parents at home and the educators at school for not providing an understanding and knowledge of sex.

Our homes only hide such matters in the back room and do not teach them openly, thus fostering only malicious curiosity, which eventually leads to various tragedies. Rather, this is like instilling sex education with malicious intent, ruining one's life. What about in society? If you bring up the topic of sex, you are labeled with the unsavory name of "disturbing public morals," and if you write about this issue, the police circle out important passages. Seeing this, it is clear that parents at home and leaders in society are looking at the issue through jaundiced eyes and with disordered thoughts. How can the young men and women who are being educated by them not be in danger? As an educator, and in general, I deeply feel the need for sex education and advocate for its implementation. First, at home, mothers and fathers should provide their children with knowledge about sex from a young age—not at seven or eight, of course, but from right before the onset of puberty. They should show them models and simply explain the basics, or show them specimens of sexually transmitted diseases made by Ar田또락크 (Arita Torakku) company and explain them simply so that they become accustomed to it from an early age. When sexual desire awakens, they will not be completely controlled by their emotions but will have an understanding and a kind of fear of it, thus gaining the ability to restrain themselves.

Of course, at school, teachers should thoroughly teach about sex during physiology class and instill moral values related to it during ethics class. It is especially dangerous during middle school, when students begin to experience puberty. Due to a lack of understanding about sex, they cannot control their momentary impulses, and while trying to satisfy their desires, they suffer mental loss and physiological harm. From my experience, I have seen students who were quite academically capable and had good complexions in their first and second years become academically inferior, pale, and physically weak around the ages of 16 and 17. Although I have not actually examined or tested them, I surmise that it is because they cannot control their momentary impulses due to sexual arousal. Knowing this and neglecting sex education is abandoning the true purpose of education. I am not sure if it would be a good idea to have a separate subject dedicated to sex and have lectures solely on sexual matters at a set time, but I suggest mainly teaching about it during physiology class. For example, teachers should teach in detail about how much nutrition is contained in one sperm so that students always pay attention to the issue. Furthermore, this should be given more importance in girls' schools. If teachers feel uncomfortable saying the names of certain things, they can use euphemisms, but they must ensure that students understand them to a certain extent. Moreover, women must be taught about hygiene from a health perspective, even more so than about physiology. <54>

What is education? We know how to talk and eat from the time we are born, but education teaches us what our mouths are like, what kind of words we should say, how we should eat, and what happens when we eat, so that we can use that knowledge. If we leave out this critical issue that can ruin or beautify a life, where is the purpose of education? One education researcher said that it would be enough if middle school education only taught students not to masturbate (手淫). <55> It may seem like an exaggeration, but it is a very pertinent point.

It is difficult to say how to implement sex education in general society, but it would be very helpful if those who have studied this issue would constantly share their knowledge and caution about sexual matters through writing and speaking, and show detailed and frightening specimens of sexually transmitted diseases. I heard that when one of my friends went abroad to study, he would go to Ar田또락크 (Arita Torakku) company whenever he felt an urge, and the sight of the terrible specimens would drive away his thoughts, allowing him to restrain himself. To summarize what I have said so far, first, make sex a commonplace topic in the home before the onset of sexual urges, while also instilling a sense of fear. At school, teach about sex carefully and thoroughly during physiology class so that there are no harmful effects, thus creating healthy brains and bodies. In general society, handle the issue with care and have researchers or experienced individuals provide guidance in various ways so that all the harms caused by this issue disappear.

First, through Books
Lee Sang-hyeop of the Jung-oe Daily News

It seems that we Koreans have very little knowledge about sex. Although humans cannot live without sex, lacking knowledge about it is like eating food without knowing anything about it. Just as a child who does not know about food eats recklessly and becomes ill or poisoned, potentially risking their life, if a person lacks knowledge about sex, various illnesses and other harms can arise, with their negative impact extending to individuals, families, and even the nation. To give a simple example, students of both sexes may suffer from neurasthenia due to masturbation (自瀆), women may develop serious illnesses due to improper postpartum care or diet during menstruation or pregnancy, men and women may contract venereal diseases due to unclean intercourse, or families may face significant financial difficulties due to excessive childbearing. Furthermore, public morals, blind love, and all these things ultimately originate from a lack of sex education. Because Korean education has traditionally been detached from real life and has only valued empty words and formalities, those who claim to be respectable people pretend to be virtuous and do not even mention relationships between men and women. The unnatural and contradictory concept of so-called Eastern morality still remains, and many people seem to find sex education strange. However, it cannot necessarily be considered Eastern morality. Among the words of the great sages and wise men in Confucianism, is there not the saying "love virtue as you love beauty" (好賢을 如好色이라)? And is not "preferring virtue to beauty" (賢賢호대 易色이라 하지 안엇습닛가) also a similar idea? These words can also be seen as related to sex education in today's terms. Whatever the past may have been, it is necessary to implement sex education as soon as possible. <56> However, since there seems to be a lack of understanding in homes and schools, it would be good to publish books about sex as an immediate remedy so that people can read them and gain knowledge. I have a book titled "Studies in the Psychology of Sex" written by a Westerner, and its contents are very interesting and contain much useful information. It would be good if someone would translate and publish it.

Through Conversation at Home
Kim Beop-rin of Buddhist JeonSu School

This issue of sex education is only a problem in Korea, where various forms of education lag behind others. In the West, it is not an issue at all in society or schools. Because parents in those countries routinely talk to their children about sex in a matter-of-fact way from an early age, most boys and girls have some knowledge of sex by the time they are in middle school. In Korea, when adults are talking about sex and there is a chance that minors might hear them, they avoid it as if they were talking about some great sin, but Westerners seem to deliberately talk about it. At first glance, talking to young men and women about sex might seem inappropriate, but that is not the case at all. Sex is not just about romantic relationships (痴情關係). It includes the principles of how humans are born, growth, development, marriage, menstruation (經度), birth control, and other general issues related to men and women. Not teaching such important and relevant things is a major flaw in common sense, and the harms that arise from this lack of common sense are not insignificant. The downfall of young men and women is mostly caused by curiosity and temptation. If they had sufficient knowledge about sex, they would never fall due to momentary curiosity or temptation. For example, if they were told what a licensed quarter (遊廓) is like, that they are likely to contract terrible sexually transmitted diseases if they go there, or what kind of harm masturbation (自瀆) can cause to their health, and if young men and women were made to think of these things as very bad, no one would engage in such behavior, even if they were encouraged to do so, let alone if it were forbidden. Also, women can develop uterine diseases or infertility because they do not know about menstruation (經度) and therefore do not take proper care of themselves. I wonder if the reason why even chaste virgins have uterine diseases is not due to this lack of knowledge about sex. In any case, the need for sex education is beyond question, and as for the method of implementation, it would be good for parents to talk about it at home whenever they have the opportunity.

Too Early
An Hyeong-jung of Ewha Girls' High School

From the beginning, I do not know if it is because of our Korean people's moral values, but the parents in the home have only been concealing the problem of sex. Up until now, there has never been a case where a mother or father taught their children about it, and because of this, talking about this issue was considered something that only vulgar or uneducated people did. Because there is no education on this, women regard the changes that naturally occur physiologically as a disease, causing them to have unnecessary fears and causing great harm to their mental state. Furthermore, they cannot even tell their parents about the phenomenon and hide it, so it is impossible to know how many suffer from unforeseen illnesses. However, this is not their own fault but the fault of their parents. They must provide a sufficient understanding of sex, whether from a moral, physiological, or hygienic perspective, in the home. But until now, and even still, it is considered proper etiquette or morality to not even allow children of marriageable age to express their opinions on the important issue of marriage in their lives. Because they are forced into arranged marriages around the age of 14 or 15, or even as young as 10, they must absolutely obey their parents. They do not have the intellect or thoughts to express their opinions, but if they were to express their opinions or argue about their marriage, it would be considered a terrible and absurd thing. Since they have been bound by such home education and customs, even if they have the most honest attitude when discussing the topic of sex, others will look at them with a certain amount of suspicion. Considering that the parents have such morals and etiquette, and that the general public has such ideas, it seems too early to address this issue. If sex education were to be implemented in schools, students would of course want to learn about it out of curiosity. However, it would be especially inappropriate for male teachers to teach this subject in girls' schools, and there would be inconveniences in teaching. Furthermore, it is doubtful whether female teachers can effectively guide the emotional and lively young women and achieve good results.

It is a fact that male-female relationships will become more common, and it is also a fact that the harms related to them will increase. However, the reason why young men and women fall into miserable traps as a result of not controlling their passion and emotions is largely due to a lack of knowledge about the opposite sex and the inability to critically assess the opposite sex. In the end, it is because there is no sex education. However, in implementing this sex education, I think it is too early to implement it in schools, as mentioned above, due to various reasons, so I have no ideas on how to proceed. For now, it would be good to promote the need for it in magazines and newspapers to change the general public's perception, and then implement it in schools when the time comes. Even now, it would be good for parents to teach their children at home whenever they have the opportunity so that they can treat the issue of sex normally.

Definitely at School
Ju Yo-han of the Dong-a Daily News

Sex education is a truly necessary issue. Our newspaper has also been thinking about addressing this issue for some time, but we have not been able to muster the courage because the general public does not understand it yet. It is very necessary and I believe it will have a positive effect in practice for your magazine (貴誌) to take the lead in addressing this issue this time. It is absurd that people cannot live without sex but do not study the issue of sex and even seem to dislike talking about it. We study the sun, Mars, and other celestial bodies that are very far from us, or the reproduction of lower animals, and everyone is absorbed in learning about them, and we even teach them as regular subjects in school, but we completely neglect the issue of sex, which is absolutely necessary for humanity. It is very contradictory. The need for sex education is beyond question, but in implementing it, it would be best if the educators in schools first understood it and taught it as a subject, judging the age and grade level of the students. Of course, it is also necessary to educate through conversation or writing at home or in society, but it would be best to implement it in schools to give students actual effects and spread it widely.

As People of Noble Character
Nam Sang-chan of Jinmyeong Girls' High School

In Korea, which is steeped in old morality, people still consider the issue of sex to be something that must be concealed, so talking about it is dismissed as vulgar. If schools were to teach this, it would be seen as a great perversion, and if sex education were to be implemented in girls' schools, the parents would immediately bring their daughters with a letter of withdrawal and cause a commotion. Of course, it is very necessary to provide education to a certain extent from a physiological or hygienic perspective, and it is true that understanding must be instilled. However, because there are still many who have very different ideas from us, it seems difficult to implement in girls' schools, although it may not be the case in boys' schools. In fact, there is a greater need for it in girls' schools than in boys' schools, and it is essential to provide them with understanding and knowledge in that area, but it seems a little too early to do so. It seems that it will be difficult no matter what because the mothers' thoughts have not yet reached that point, let alone the fathers' thoughts. Moreover, if teachers suddenly explain sex on the podium, when they have never heard or learned about sex from their mothers or teachers, they may interpret the intended sex education as a kind of curiosity and in an excessive direction, which may have a negative impact. Rather than proposing the implementation issue suddenly now, it seems that there is no other way than to foster their noble character and, at the same time, instill a little simple understanding.

I cannot say more as a female educator on whether to implement this education until the general public has a little more understanding of it.

From the Home
Choe Gyu-dong of Joongdong High School

Regardless of the era or country, when civilization weakens and declines, morality inevitably decays and sexual issues become disordered. In recent times, it seems that morality has decayed in our Korea, whether or not civilization has weakened, and that issues of chastity and sex have become very disordered, especially among young men and women, but also among the general public. There are many places to look for the causes, but I think this is the biggest cause.

For thousands or hundreds of years, women have been kept locked up in their rooms (閨門) and have had no way of knowing any men other than their fathers and brothers, and men have sat apart from women after the age of seven (男女七歲에 부동석을 외우고 안젓든 남자가). Thanks to the influx of new civilization and new trends, so-called male-female relationships have become frequent, and those who only knew the opposite sex as the opposite sex have suddenly met and played together. There was nothing but curiosity in other areas.

It is no surprise that this has resulted because they originally lacked an understanding of sex. In the end, it is because there is no sex education that they lack composure and ordinariness when facing the opposite sex, and because they cannot control their momentary emotions and instincts, they end up ruining their lives.

Leaving aside other foreign countries, this issue of sex education has already arisen in Japan, but after the indecent incident of Dr. Ōno (大野醫學博士) some years ago, it has been further advocated socially and has become a matter of discussion among educators and scholars. Afterwards, it is said that one girls' school implemented sex education and the results were good, but I do not know to what extent they are talking about the results, but it seems that the question of whether to implement it generally is still an issue.

In any case, this issue of sex education must be carefully considered, especially by educators. Boys' schools are of course important, but in girls' schools, they should handle various kinds of knowledge about sex in the most respectful way and ensure that students have a thorough understanding. Especially for young men and women who are of marriageable age or in puberty, this is a very dangerous time for sexual issues, so I feel the need for it more acutely. However, while I have these thoughts, I do not have any ideas on how to implement it, and I am very hesitant because the general public's habitual perception is that the issue of sex goes beyond the bounds of reason. However, I always use my time to talk to upper-class students of marriageable age about understanding marriage and the position or duties of men and women. Also, I have not yet told my daughter at home, but I tell my son about the concept of marriage, romance, and understanding of the opposite sex whenever necessary. However, I am not sure if it is still possible to set aside a separate subject in school and dedicate it purely to sex education. Rather than that, it would be best for parents to kindly tell their children at home, and for schools to provide understanding and knowledge of sexual issues morally, physiologically, and hygienically during ethics class or physiology class whenever there is an opportunity. Furthermore, I would like to reiterate that I feel the need for it even more in girls' schools. And the current guidelines for male-female relationships should also be based on this sex education. <60>

學校와 家庭의 時急問題 性敎育 實施 方策
春機發動機前에
普成高普具中會

이 성교육문제는 과연 중대한 문제임니다. 혹 엇던 이는 아즉 우리의 가정에서나 사회에서나 시기상조이라는 말을 하는 사람도 잇지만 그는 절대로 잘못된 생각입니다. 尙早라는 것보다도 尙晩의 감이 잇습니다. 一身을 흥망의 길로 끄을고 가는 이 性문제를 엇지 등한히 할 수가 잇겟습닛가. 우리는 재래의 동양의 도덕을 맹종하여 오기 때문에 그 폐해가 적지 안습니다. 성문제를 집어내면은 야비한 것으로 돌이고 말어 버려서 성의 눈이 뜨고 情의 싹이 트는 춘정기에 이 성에 대한 이해가 업기 때문에 희망을 두엇든 압길이 캄캄해지고 일생의 파멸을 사는 일이 얼마나 만습닛가. 그것은 다른 까닭이 안이라 늘 가정에 잇서서는 男女七歲에 不同席式의 가정훈화를 바더오고 학교에 와서도 더구나 그에 대한 지식이란 배호지 못하야서 즉 성교육을 밧지 못하야서 이성이라는 것은 더구나 알지 못함으로 한참 성욕의 눈이 틀 때에 일시적 감정을 자제하지 못하고 물인지 불인지 모르고 덤벼드는 까닭임니다. 이것이 누구의 허물일가요. 가정에 잇서서는 그의 부모 학교에 잇서서는 그의 교육자가 성에 대한 이해와 지식을 너허주지 아니한 까닭이겟습니다.
우리의 가정은 그런 문제를 뒤ㅅ구멍으로 숨겨만 두고 내놋코 가르처 주지 안이함으로 惡意의 호기심만을 길러주어 나종에 여러 가지 비극을 연출하게 만드러 줌니다. 도리혀 이것은 일생을 그릇치는 악의에 잇서서의 성교육을 식혀주는 것임니다. 사회에서는 엇더한가요. 성문제를 끄집어내서 이약이를 하면 풍기문란이란 향기롭지 못한 일홈을 붓치고 혹＜54＞ 이 문제에 대하야서 글로써 내이면은 중요한 구절을 경찰은 똥그램이를 처내이게 되니 이러한 것을 보면 가정에 잇서서 그 부모나 사회에 잇서서는 그 지도자들이 먼저 난잡한 생각과 색안경을 대이고 보는 것이니 그들에게 교육을 밧고 잇는 청춘남녀가 엇지 위태하지 안켓습닛가. 나는 교육자로써 뿐만이 아니라 일반적으로도 이 성교육에 필요를 절실히 늣기며 그 실시를 주장함니다. 첫재는 가정에서 어머니나 아버지가 자녀가 아즉 어렷슬 때 - 그야 7,8세 때에야 안될 말이지만 春期 발동기 卽前부터 이에 대한 지식은 너허 주고 혹은 모형 가튼 것을 보히고 간단하게 그에 대한 이해를 할만콤 가르처주며 혹은 有田또락크 상회에 만드러 노흔 성병의 표본 가튼 것을 보혀주며 성의 대한 것을 알여주는 한편으로 그러한 무서운 것도 보혀서 그 설명을 간단히 하여 어려서부터라도 심상하게 녁이도록 하고 성욕의 발동기에 가서도 함부로 감정의 지배를 밧지 안코 이해를 가진 한편 그에 대한 일종 공포심도 갓게 하며 자제하는 마음을 가지게 할 것입니다.
물론 학교에 잇서서도 生理시간 가튼 때 좀 철저하게 가르키고 修身시간에라도 그에 대한 도덕관념을 너허줄 것입니다. 더구나 중학생시대에는 때가 춘정을 알게 되는 때임으로 더구나 위험함니다. 성문제에 대한 아모런 이해가 업슴으로 일시적 충동을 억제하지 못하야 그때 그때의 만족을 채워가는 동안에 정신적 손실과 생리적 장해를 밧게 됨니다. 그는 내가 경험하는 바이지만 1학년 2학년 때에는 學力도 상당하고 혈색도 조튼 사람이 16,7세쯤 되여서는 學力이 열등하여지고 혈색이 납버지고 신체가 허약해지는 것을 보면 실지로 진찰을 하엿거나 검사는 하여보지 안이하엿더라도 미루어 생각하면 성적 발동으로 한때의 충동을 억제하지 못하는 까닭인 줄 암니다. 이러한 것을 알면서도 그에 대한 교육을 등한히 하는 것은 교육의 本意를 저바린 것입니다. 특별히 성에 대한 과목을 따로히 하야서 일정한 시간에 순전히 성문제만을 가지고 교수를 한다든가 하는 것은 엇덜른지 모르겟스나 주로 해서 生理시간에 가령 사람의 精蟲 하나에 양분이 얼마나 잇다는 것이라도 좀 자세하게 가르켜서 항상 즉접 문뎨에 주의를 하도록 할 것이며 더구나 여학교에서는 더욱 중요시를 하여야 할 것입니다. 혹 그 名詞를 말하기에 불편을 늣기게 되면, 갑이니 을이니 하는 명칭을 부처서라도 반듯이 어느 정도까지 이해를 식혀야 할 것이며 더구나 생리라는 것보다도 여자에게는 위생학적으로 꼭 교수를 하여야 하겟습니다.
