기사제목	無産者의 大厄日, 금음難! 月給難! (The Great Unlucky Day for the Proletariat, Difficult to Get Money! Difficult to Get Paid!)
필자	春坡
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Critique)

The Great Unlucky Day for the Proletariat, Difficult to Get Money! Difficult to Get Paid!
						Chunpa

It is noon on the scorching, stifling August 30th. As the break bell rings, the machines, typesetting, plate-making, proofreading, bookbinding, hesitation, and even the errand boy and cleaner all become quiet at once, as if entering a place of midnight rest.

They gather,쿵쾅거리고, in the upper floor communal rest area (dining hall), saying things like, "How's my rice?" "What's your side dish like?" "So-and-so's wife is good at cooking," "So-and-so's daughter-in-law is not so good at cooking..." They chatter like this, giving, taking, chewing, and swallowing, truly in a laborer-like manner, very hungry and bustling. After eating as if they had caught a fly 둑겁이, they fill their stomachs halfway with cold water and, after lighting up a "막코" cigarette each, they open the window and look out at the complex city, each sighing and lamenting:

"Some guys have lucky fates and live in grand mansions, enjoying freedom and pleasure, but this wretch is living a factory worker's life in the sweltering heat..."

"Who's riding in the rickshaw in this heat, and who's pulling it? The world is truly unfair."

"The train is whistling to go, and I can only hold my lover's hand and shed tears... Hey, Tol-i-kun, that train probably has couples going to Inch'ŏn Wŏlmidŏ or Yusŏng hot springs, right? We can't even go to Noryangjin once; the world is truly unfair."

"Aigoo, those damned bastards are already here waiting. Hey, damned bastards..."

"Who are you talking about? Who...?"

"The creditors, of course. Look outside the door. Four or five of them are already here. Aigoo, those damned bastards, if they have to wait until five o'clock, their legs might hurt a little..."

A little while later, they each received an envelope. Amidst joy and trepidation＜55＞, as they opened their envelopes, some stood blankly, seemingly disappointed, staring silently at the machine gears, while others seemed surprised and smiled brightly, and a quick-tempered complainer threw his envelope onto the work saying, "Is this all I get for half a month's labor!" And some weak young men couldn't speak, couldn't get angry, and just puckered their lips and sobbed.

A little later, when their feelings had calmed down, they became curious about each other's paychecks, glancing at each other's envelopes, and a conversation like this began:

"Hey, how much did you get?"

"Don't even ask. I'm ashamed, frustrated, and speechless. Only 15 won 50 chŏn."

"You're still richer than me. I only got 15 won 25 chŏn. I'm speechless..."

"Did you borrow a lot?"

"Not much. I borrowed 3 won 75 chŏn."

"Still, you don't have a big family, so it's nothing to worry about. What's there to worry about with only two mouths to feed?"

"Two mouths! Don't even mention it; I'm dying from the debt. Rice costs 4 won 50 chŏn, and I'm two months behind on rent. The landlord is probably waiting outside the door. On top of that, my wife doesn't know anything and nags me day and night to buy her some fabric. Jeez! I can't stand it."

"They say if a dry leaf rustles, even pine needles rustle; if I were you, I wouldn't worry. I truly don't know how to live; I feel so lost. I have seven mouths to feed. And I'm over 100 won in debt, so how can I survive? There are probably at least five or six creditors waiting outside the door to see me. I really don't know how I'll fend them off. If there were a back door, I'd run away..."

"Why are there five or six creditors? What, what did you borrow so much for?"

"Aish, my friend, with a poor household and so many family members, how can I not be in debt? Let me tell you everything: rice costs money, firewood costs money, water costs money, electricity costs money, side dishes cost money, monthly wages of 30 won, coal costs from last winter, and that damned liquor costs money (I shouldn't have drunk that then in anger) - how much is that alone? I'm speechless. Above all, that coal merchant and the monthly wage collector are truly like malaria; how am I supposed to deal with them...? I absolutely have to buy my mother a pair of rubber shoes this time..."

"It's truly pitiful, but just live as you can. What else can you do? They won't kill you... But those section chiefs probably make at least 60 or 70 won, right?"

"Of course, they probably make close to 100 won..."

"Ugh, it leaves a bitter taste in my mouth..."

They were talking like this, lamenting their fate and worrying about their livelihood. Amidst anxiety, fear, weariness, and 怠症, they reluctantly did the work they didn't want to do.＜56＞ The biggest and most difficult problem in their minds was, above all else, the problem of 債鬼退治 (getting rid of debt collectors).

Looking out the company's main gate, don't be surprised. The 債鬼떼 (gangs of debt collectors) who came early to 미리미리 to devour these poor workers were as big as a decent country marketplace.

A coal merchant resembling a thief, a monthly wage collector resembling a bailiff, a 色酒家 (house of prostitution) husband resembling a 0사퇴물, a rice merchant like a country old man, a firewood seller truly like a 나무ㅅ군, a young 선줄집 bachelor with greasy grime, an old 内外酒店 (inn) woman with a face like an 餓鬼 eating and shitting, a pimp like an old shaman... So many 債鬼 (debt collectors) of all kinds had gathered. Some walked back and forth with their hands behind their backs, some squatted down on the 仮家 head and smoked, some peered intently into the cave of society like a hunter trying to catch a large animal, some women stood inside other people's gates and waited, some men stood behind electric poles and waited, and some chewed on 호떡 (Korean sweet pancake) because they were hungry.

"Time doesn't go by. I wish it were five o'clock already. I'll grab that bastard as soon as he comes out and take even his clothes this time. If he lies again this time, I'll feed him 양떡 (Western-style rice cake) so he looks good. The time's not here yet; come out quickly."

They were each waiting for, aiming for, and scheming against those workers, preparing their means and methods, like cats eyeing rats in a trap.

The fateful five o'clock came. The bell signaling the end of work rang. Whether they liked it or not, they couldn't not go out. On ordinary days, they hated night work, but on this day, they wished there was night work. But because it was payday and they wanted them to take their pay and go home to their parents, wives, and children, buy meat, buy liquor, buy noodles, and buy rice cakes and have a good time, they didn't make them work night shifts that day. Whether there was a tiger or a cat, they couldn't not go out. They took the money in their envelopes, put a few won in their inside pockets, a few won in their notebooks, a few won in their wallets, entrusted a few won to a trustworthy friend (to pick up tomorrow), and left a few won in the envelope to 防塞 (prevent) the 債鬼 (debt collectors), and then, like calves going to the slaughterhouse, they lowered their heads and slipped out the main gate in worry and fear.

Ah! These damned 債鬼떼 (gangs of debt collectors)! Like hungry tigers finding a flock of rabbits, they pounced on them from all sides. Some pretended to be friendly, some pretended to be kind, some pretended to be gentle, putting on false smiles, saying, "Lee-sŏbang, you're coming out now. Kim-chusa, it's been a while. Poktong-i, you're coming out now, Sutong-a, did you meet your mother?" and they each acted 친체 (intimate).

"Pay me everything this time. Only half again this time..." "It's not even enough for shoes." "When did I eat your rice?" "When did I take your coal?"... A commotion broke out. One side made a fuss, one side begged, one side was full of豪氣 (spirit), one side伏地謝罪 (prostrated themselves in apology). It was truly a pitiful and bitter scene.

I had only just realized that for laborers, 직공 (factory workers), i.e., 無産者 (the proletariat), payday was an even greater pain and an unbearable hardship.＜57＞

無産者의 大厄日, 금음難! 月給難!
						春坡
찌는 듯 삼는 듯 아조 혹독키 더운 8월 30일의 정오이다. 휴식 종소리 땅강땅강 나자 기계도 植字도 整版도 校正도 製本도 躊字도 심지어 급사 소제부까지 일시에 夜半 안식의 자리에 나드는 것 가티 고요해진다.

『빗쟁이들 말일세. 저- 문밧좀 내다보게. 발서 사오명이나 와서섯네. 엑기 망할 자식들 다섯시까지 기다릴내면 다리가 좀 압흘나... ...』

얼마 후에 그들은 봉투 하나씩을 밧엇다. 희열 또는 恐縮＜55＞ 한가운데서 봉투를 뜻는 그들은 혹은 낙심한 듯 멍하니 서서 기계 박휘만 물그럼히 처다보고 혹은 의외인 듯 깃버서 희죽 웃기도하며 성미 팔측한 불평자는 「반달 노동의 보수가 겨우 요고람!」하고 일상 우에 봉투를 던지는 놈도 잇고 엇던 유약한 소년은 말도 못하고, 성도 못내고 입을 빗죽거리며 울먹울먹하고 잇다.

『가랑닙이 벗석거리닛가 솔닙까지 벗석거린다고 자네가트면 나는 걱정도 안하겟네. 나야말로 엇더케 사러갈지 실로 발길이 망연하네. 글세 밥 숫까락 드는 식구가 일곰일세. 게다가 빗이 100圓이 넘으니 엇더케 설겟나? 문밧게 나보려고 온 빗장이가 적어도 五六名은 될 것일세. 잇다가 엇더케 방패막이를 할지 실로 딱해죽겟네. 뒷문이나 잇스면 다러나지나 안켓나......』

『되고말고 근 100圓씩 되리......』
『흥 입맛이 써서......』
그들은 이런 말을 주고 밧고 하면서 신세타령 살님 걱정을 하고 잇다. 불안, 공포, 염증, 怠症의 중에 손이 풀니고＜56＞ 맥이 풀녀서 하기 실은 일을 억지로 하고 잇다. 그들의 머리ㅅ속에는 무엇보다 債鬼退治問題 그것이 제일 難問題엿다.

회사 정문 압흘 내다보니 놀나지마라. 이 불상한 직공들을 뜻어먹기위하야 午正때붓터 미리미리 먼저와 기다리는 債鬼떼들이 어지간한 시골장판 만하다.

『시간도 안이도 간다. 어서 다섯 시가 되야라. 이놈을 나오는 즉시로 붓들어 가지고 이번은 옷이라도 벗겨가지고 가겟다. 이번도 또 거짓말을 하면 보기 좃케 洋떡을 하나 먹이랸다. 시간이 안되얏나 어서 나오나라.』

문제의 다섯 시는 왓다. 작업 중지의 종은 울엇다. 그들은 좃커나 실커나 안이 나아갈 수는 업섯다. 평일에는 夜業이 귀찬터니 이날은 夜業이라도 잇스면 조흘 듯 하얏다. 그러나 월급날이라고 월급 타가지고 가서 부모처자 마조 안저, 괴기 사고, 술 사고, 국수 사고, 떡 사고하야 자미잇게 지내라고 이날은 夜業도 안이 시킨다. 그들은 호랑이가 잇든 고양이가 잇든 하여간 안이 나아갈수 업섯다. 봉투 속에 든 돈을 꺼내여 몃 圓은 속 줌어니에 녓코 몃 圓은 수첩 속에 녓코 몃 圓은 돈지갑에 녓코 몃 圓은 신용잇는 동무이게 맛기고 (내일 찻기로 하고) 그리고 債鬼에게 防塞이나 하려고 단 몃圓을 봉투에 남겨가지고 屠獸場으로 가는 송아지 모양으로 머리를 숙이고 憂愁恐怖의 중에 슬그머니 정문 밧글 나섯것다.

노동자, 직공, 卽無産者는 월급날이 이럿케 더 만흔 고통, 못 견댈 난관인 것을 나는 비로소 보고 아럿다.＜57＞
