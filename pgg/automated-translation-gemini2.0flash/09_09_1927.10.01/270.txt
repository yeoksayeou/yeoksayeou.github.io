발행일	1927년 10월 01일
기사제목	無學大師 新京城 求景, 놀내지마라! 五百年 漢陽國都를 指定한 無學大師가 五百年後 今日에 서울구경을 하고 갓다. 案內는 天外君 (Don't be Surprised! Muhak Daesa Visits the New Gyeongseong; Muhak Daesa, Who Designated Hanyang as the National Capital Five Hundred Years Ago, Visited Seoul Today After Five Hundred Years. Guided by Cheongoegun)
기사형태	문예기타 (Miscellaneous Literature)

Cheongoegun. You may not recognize me, but I am the monk Muhak who helped Yi Taejo designate Hanyang as the national capital 500 years ago. Don't be suspicious just because I am a monk; just follow me.

Ah! Is that so? I have long heard of the Great Master's esteemed name, but I never dreamed I would see you here like this. Is this a divine manifestation, or are you truly here? If it is not a divine manifestation, how could the Great Master of 500 years ago appear in this way?

If you believe well in Buddha, become enlightened, and transform into a Dharma body, you can manifest yourself even after 5,000 years, not just 500 years. Those who have not reached enlightenment would not know this...

How on earth did you deign to descend to this lowly place? Please, come in and rest your legs first, and then we can talk slowly.

Have I been in the Lotus Dharma World for nearly 500 years? I became weary and curious about what was happening in the lower realm, so I came down for a brief excursion and inspection. Just like common people go on autumn trips... So, I came to Anbyeon Seokwang Temple, where my karmic connection is deepest, and stayed for a week. Then, I went to Geumgangsan to see Yujeom Temple, Singye Temple, Jangan Temple, and Pyohun Temple. Passing through Geumseong, Gimhwa, Cheorwon, and Yangju, I slept at Doseon Temple last night and briefly visited Hwagye Temple on my way here. Oh, my, it's not easy; I am very tired because it is a secular world and a metropolis... But has it changed so much in 500 years? It truly feels like a sea change. Iron horses roar everywhere, telegraph and telephone lines are like spider webs, electric gas creates a city that never sleeps, trams and automobiles race each other, four- and five-story Western-style houses rise high, and the people have almost turned into black-headed monkeys. How could there be such a great transformation? The world has changed beyond recognition; this is unimaginable even in the Lotus World. Moreover, there are airplanes, submarines, and recently, radios or wireless telegraphs, which allow you to hear words from all over the world, thousands of miles away, just by holding a small machine. It is truly a bizarre and strange world. Back then, 500 years ago, there was nothing like this. The world has an unceasing principle of evolution... <90>

You must be thirsty, so please have a cup of tea and speak slowly. What would you like for lunch?

Lunch? I don't need to eat. There's already a world where people say there's too much food, so why would I add another bowl? I don't need to eat, so don't worry about me. I'll have a cup of tea... It has a savory taste, like barley tea.

Yes, it is barley tea. We also have coffee tea, milk tea, black tea, and all sorts of other teas, but we are proletarians, so we drink barley tea like this. It's cheap and tastes good.

If it is cheap and tastes good, that's all that matters... Ah, but has it changed so much in 500 years? If Taejo Daewang (태조대왕) were here, he would be even more deeply moved than I am. The mountains and rivers remain the same, but where have the royal descendants gone?

No matter how moved you are, please don't say such things. They will arrest you. Of course, as the Great Master who designated the base of the national capital, you must be even more moved. But please, don't say such things. If you make a mistake, your Lotus Dharma body will become a prisoner of hell.

Arrest me? Who?

There are things that arrest you if you say such things. Just know that and say something else.

Haha, coming to the secular world brings so many restrictions. Oh, dear! When I first came to Seokwang Temple, someone claiming to be a reporter asked for my name, saying that a Great Master they didn't know had come. Before several hours had passed, a policeman or something came and made a fuss. I told him that even after living for over 500 years, this was the first time I had been treated like this, and he just laughed and ran away. I suppose it will be the same when I go to Geumgangsan. But now, are you trying to intimidate me by saying I'll be arrested if I say those things? I am someone who will not be bound by anyone. Only if Sakyamuni Buddha or Yi Taejo himself came would I perhaps be swayed out of affection; otherwise...

Great Master, "When in Rome, do as the Romans do." Please be careful in advance so you don't regret it.

Haha, you young fellow, how can you live in this harsh world if you are so afraid? What a cowardly boy!

I led the Great Master up to Sangsang Peak (상상봉) on Bukak Mountain (북악산).

But wasn't this Hanyang capital actually determined by Jeong Do-jeon (정도전), even though you are said to have chosen it? There are varying accounts of this, so please tell us in detail what happened back then, since you are here. <91>

Hmm! It wouldn't be wrong to say Jeong Do-jeon (정도전) determined it. Well, I'll tell you the truth in detail. To tell this story, I need to briefly explain my background.

I was originally a monk in Anbyeon Seolbong Mountain (안변설봉산). One day, a grand figure named Yi Seong-gye (이성계) came and asked to study with me. One evening, he had a strange dream and begged me to interpret it. When I heard the whole story, I thought it was strange that he went into a house and came out carrying three rafters (橡). After thinking about it for a while, don't be surprised, it was a dream about becoming king (because carrying three rafters horizontally on his back forms the character 王). And he also dreamed of "ten thousand chickens crowing at once." After thinking about this, it was clear he would achieve a high position (because the sound of a chicken, "kkokkoo," sounds like "high position"). So I explained at length that it was a very auspicious dream and that he would become very noble, a dream of becoming king. He was delighted and bowed to me, repeatedly begging me to help him to the end. So, I became close to Yi Seong-gye (이성계), and later, just as I had interpreted the dream, Yi seized the Goryeo Dynasty and ascended to the throne. That was 536 years ago, Wangsin (왕신), right? After ascending to the throne, he found the existing capital inconvenient and decided to establish a new one, asking me to select a new capital base for royal prosperity. So I accepted his request and divined the geography here and there. Eventually, when I came to this place called Hanyang (한양), the energy of Samgak Mountain (삼각산) was good, the Han River (한강) flowed broadly, Bukak Mountain (북악산) served as the rear guard, Jongnam Mountain (종남산) as the front guard, Inwang Mountain (인왕산) as the right wing, and Naksan Mountain (낙산) as the left wing. The structure of the mountains and water was certainly promising for 500 years, if not for ten thousand years. So I decided to designate it as the capital. But then I thought to myself that it would be better to build a temple in such a beautiful land and aim for the prosperity of Buddhism rather than a royal capital. So I secretly had a great ambition, stopped the Hanyang (한양) capital relocation plan, and went south to Gyeryong Mountain (계룡산). After seeing all of Gyeryong (계룡) front and back, I chose a site within the capital and argued that it was a good base for establishing the capital. Yi Taejo (이태조), who always followed my word, immediately went to Gyeryong Mountain (계룡산) and decided to build the base for the capital. But at this time, Jeong Do-jeon (정도전), who had extraordinary wisdom and talent (a founding contributor), saw through my ambition and began to advocate against the Gyeryong Mountain capital, while also arguing for the relocation to Hanyang (한양). Yi Taejo (이태조) was half-believing and half-doubting, wondering whether to believe my words or Jeong Do-jeon's (정도전) words, even though the construction had already started. Do-jeon (도전) came up with a plan to secretly bury a stone in the Gyeryong Mountain palace construction site with the inscription "The foolish monk Muhak (무학) mistakenly entered Jeong's (정씨) land," and also had someone disguised as the Gyeryong Mountain spirit go up to the mountain at midnight and loudly shout, "This place is Jeong's (정씨) land in 500 years. Yi Seong-gye (이성계) has come here by mistake; return north to Hanyang (한양), for that is your land." After hearing this, Do-jeon (도전) reported to Taejo (태조), "The mountain spirit is instructing us like this, so why insist on human will and ruin the great undertaking? Please relocate to Hanyang (한양) immediately." Then, a laborer working on the construction site found a stone tablet and presented it to Taejo (태조), which read, "The foolish monk Muhak (무학) has mistakenly entered Jeong's (정씨) land." <92> Consequently, Taejo (태조) followed Do-jeon's (도전) words and decided to relocate to Hanyang (한양) immediately. I failed. I was outwitted by Do-jeon (도전). So, feeling resentful and determined to seize Hanyang (한양) and build a temple there, I came up with another plan and argued that the capital should be established in Cheongnyangni Nanyeo, now Wangsimni. Those words prevailed. Yi Taejo (이태조) could not refuse my words again and decided to establish the capital there. Knowing this, Do-jeon (도전) strongly opposed it, saying that the back was open and the front was weak, making a great fuss. Eventually, he devised another plan to write the three characters "Wangsimni" (왕십리) on a stone and bury it near the palace construction site. Eventually, this was discovered... As a result, all my plans were ruined. What could I do? In the end, Hanyang (한양) was decided upon according to Do-jeon's (도전) insistence. I thought there was no other way; it was the fate of the Yi family. I decided to put my heart into helping as best I could, and I designated the Heunghwamun (흥화문) palace as the formal palace site. Here, Do-jeon (도전) opposed again, saying, "That place is Gan Jwason Hyang (乾坐巽向) and cannot be used. The ruler must face south to govern!" He insisted on the current site of Gyeongbok Palace (경복궁) below Bukak Mountain (북악산). I strongly opposed the Gyeongbok Palace (경복궁) site, saying that the back was too steep and the Ganseulbang (乾戌方) (Changui Gate (창의문) sill) was broken, so there would be many misfortunes and strange events in the palace due to the Ganseul wind (乾戌風). But in the end, I couldn't withstand the cunning Do-jeon (도전)... Didn't my words come true? How many misfortunes and strange events occurred in that palace afterward? Taejo (태조) and Taejong (태종) fought, and then Sejo (세조) and Danjong (단종) fought. Then, there was the Wangjin (왕진) great fire. Recently, Empress Min (민비) was brutally murdered. In the end, didn't it consume the Yi family? After that, everything I did failed, so I thought, "The world is like floating clouds; it's better to quickly return to Buddha," and I went down to my old hermitage on Seolbong Mountain (설봉산). Yi Taejo (이태조), as a reward for my interpreting his auspicious dreams, renamed my hermitage Seokwang Temple (석왕사) and greatly expanded the temple with state funds. That's how things were back then. People today may laugh at my ambitious actions.

Is that so? I understand well. From now on, let's sightsee in Seoul... But let's not ask about or answer anything regarding the Japan-Korea annexation or the political relationship between Japan and Joseon (조선). They will arrest you if you say such things.

Even if we are quietly talking on this mountain, they will arrest us? You are such a cowardly descendant... How severe must the oppression be for you to constantly say "they will arrest us"?

Great Master, it doesn't matter how much of an elder you are. Come on, let's go sightseeing in Seoul...

(The Muhak Daesa's (무학대사) Night View of the City and Impressions will be in the Next Month's Issue) <95>

<90-95> The red brick building in front of the Government-General (총독부) is the Gyeonggi Provincial Office (경기도청). The Korean-style tile-roofed house across from it, which was once one of the Previous Six Ministries (前六曹), is the Law Special School, and the white brick building across from that is the Communications Bureau (遞信局). The house above is the Joseon Infantry Barracks. That Deoksugung Palace (덕수궁대궐) is where Gojong Tae Emperor (고종태황제) resided until his later years, and after he passed away in Mumo Year, it is now under contract to be sold to the Japanese. That Heunghwamun Palace (흥화문대궐), which the Great Master personally laid the foundation for, is now Gyeongseong Middle School for Japanese people. That tall, slender building is the radio broadcasting station, and that building with the red flag fluttering is the Labor-Russia Consulate. That is Ewha Girls' School, and behind it is Baejae School. That tall building on Euiju Street (의주통로) is the Independence Gate (독립문) (completely independent according to the Treaty of Shimonoseki after the Gapo Japan-China War (甲午日淸戰爭後馬關條約에 의하야 完全獨立)), and behind it, the house surrounded by red walls is the Seodaemun Prison (서대문형무소), known as the hell on earth. Looking south, that place with smoke billowing and sounds ringing is the Namdaemun Train Station (남대문정차장), and beyond that, all the way to the Han River (한강), is the Japanese settlement, New Yongsan (新龍山), and across from that is Old Yongsan (舊龍山). In New Yongsan (新龍山), there is the 20th Division stationed, the Railroad Bureau, and the Gas Company. We'll look at those in detail later, but that thing that looks like an eagle spreading its wings is Sungnyemun (숭례문), also known as Namdaemun (남대문). Coming inside, the magnificent stone structure is the Bank of Joseon (조선은행), and across from that is the Gyeongseong Post Office (경성우편국), right next to it is the Commercial Bank, and next to that is the troublesome Industrial Bank of Joseon (식산은행). And that place below Jongnam Mountain (종남산) is so-called Jeungo Gae (즌고개), which is entirely a Japanese village, and the tall, pointed building on Jonghyeon (종현) is the Catholic Church, and in front of it, the round, flat, and tall building is the Oriental Development Company (동양척식회사), which is also a Joseon (조선) 000. The belfry in Jongno (종로) is still keeping its solitary vigil. The site of Wongaksa Temple (원각사터) had been a park for a while, but for some reason, it is now in a state of confinement. That long brick building near Jongno (종로) is the YMCA, and next to it, the house with the clock on the roof is the house where they will arrest you... That is Donhwamun Palace (돈화문대궐), or Changdeokgung Palace (창덕궁), where the last emperor of the Yi Dynasty (이조), Sunjong Hyo Emperor (순종효황제), resided until he passed away last May. That place with the dense trees is the Great Shrine (大廟), and behind it, the Heunghwamun Palace (홍화문대궐) where the Great Master once resided is now a zoo. Across from that, the tall building is the Government-General Hospital, and to its left is Gyeongseong Imperial University (경성제국대학). Behind the university is the Higher Commercial School, and in front of the university is the Higher Technical School. That entrance to Seongbuk-dong (성북동), the former site of Bukgwanwang Temple (북관왕묘), is now Boseong Higher Common School (보성고등보통학교), run by Buddhism. They are also building a Buddhist Professional School there. That is Heunginjimun (흥인지문), or Dongdaemun (동대문), and the tram road in front of it leads to Cheongnyangni (청량리). That thing crawling across, like a grub, is the tram going to Wangsimni (왕십리), where the Great Master established his second site. That is the road leading to Jangchungdan (장충단), and that place with the black smoke is Tteokseom Waterworks Bureau (뚝도수도국). There is the Sicheolri (수철리) public cemetery, and going south, there is also a public cemetery in Itaewon (이태원)... Now that you've seen this much, you've seen the general outline, so let's go down and gradually look around.

Oh, my, it's really amazing. But what is that red house on the hillside?

That is the Central Higher Common School (중앙고등보통학교).

And that house?

That is Hwimun Higher Common School (휘문고등보통학교).

And that house?

That is Gyeongseong Girls' Higher Common School (경성여자고등보통학교). <94>

And that?

That is Byeolgung (별궁), but it's useless and just left empty.

And that?

That is the Sicheon Church (侍天敎堂).

Oh, there are so many houses. How many households live in this Hanyang (한양) city?

The total population is 342,600 people, of which about 245,000 are Koreans and about 84,000 are Japanese. There are 70,628 households, of which about 55,000 are Korean households and about 15,000 are Japanese households.

Oh dear, so many Japanese have come. How can we be so easily taken over?

What can we do? It's fate, it's fate.

And are the fortress walls still the same as when we built them?

No, sir. Most have naturally deteriorated due to wind and rain, and others have been demolished artificially for transportation convenience, so there is not much left of the fortress walls now. Seodaemun (서대문) and Seosomun (서소문) disappeared long ago, and Bukmun (북문) and Namsomun (남소문) have also deteriorated. The only gates remaining are Namdaemun (남대문), Dongdaemun (동대문), Dongsomun (동소문), Changuimun (창의문), and Sigu Gate (屍口門), I think.

But we said back then that if Namsomun (남소문) were breached, licentiousness would spread greatly in Hanyang (한양) and morals would be disordered. Is that not related?

Well, sir. Perhaps that's why the morals of men and women in Hanyang (한양) are getting worse and worse. General intellectuals are very worried that even boys and girls as young as ten are having affairs and love affairs and doing strange and chaotic things. Perhaps it's because Namsomun (남소문) has unleashed licentiousness, so there are many houses of prostitution near Namsomun (남소문), such as Sinmachi (新町) and Namiki Street (並木町). Perhaps that is one proof.

Okay, I've heard a lot and seen a lot. Let's go down slowly. You must be hungry, so let's go down and have something to eat, and then go sightseeing at night again...

『天外君. 나를 몰느겟지 나는 500년 전 李太祖를 도아 漢陽國都를 정하든 僧 - 無學일세. 僧俗이 다르다고 嫌疑치 말고 마저주게.』
『아! 그러심닛가 대사의 尊名은 임의 드른지 오래 엿삽거니와 이제 여긔서 이럿케 뵈올 줄은실로 몽상도 못하얏슴니다. 대관전 환령이 심닛가 생시이심닛가 환령이 안이시면 엇지 500년 전 대사가 이처럼 現身을하시겟슴닛가?』
