발행일	1927년 07월 01일
기사제목	朝鮮映畵界 唯一의 花形女優 一問一答 申一仙孃과의 問答記, 履歷·出演經驗·結婚問題·希望 (A Q&A with Shin Il-seon, the Only Leading Actress in the Korean Film Industry: Her Background, Acting Experience, Marriage Plans, and Hopes)
기사형태	대담·좌담 (Interview/Dialogue)

A Q&A with Shin Il-seon, the Only Leading Actress in the Korean Film Industry: Her Background, Acting Experience, Marriage Plans, and Hopes
Time: June 20, Evening
Location: Gwanhun-dong, Gyeongseong
People: Shin Il-seon and the Reporter

Reporter: "I'm a bit embarrassed to ask, but please understand and answer well."
Shin: "Yes."
Reporter: "I'm not asking what I or the magazine want to know, but what those who love your movies would want to know, so I might ask some strange questions later."
Shin: "(Just smiles)"
Reporter: "Well then, how old are you really?"
Shin: "Sixteen years old."
Reporter: "Where were you born?"
Shin: "Seoul. I was born in Gyeonji-dong, Seoul."
Reporter: "What did you do before entering the theater world?"
Shin: "I went to O-deok Girls' Common School. I entered the first grade at the age of ten, finished the first semester of the fourth grade at the age of thirteen, and during the summer vacation, I ended up in the theater world, so I quit school."
Reporter: "What kind of relationship and motivation led you to step into the theater world?"
Shin: "Didn't the Joseon Art Troupe (朝鮮藝術團) come into existence three years ago? It was organized when Mr. Kim Mun-pil, who does magic, came out. At that time, my older brother was an executive in the Joseon Art Troupe."
Reporter: "Yes, I understand. So?" <98>
Shin: "Well, that summer, the actors of the troupe supposedly had a 'strike' because they didn't agree with the executives. So, my brother, as one of the executives, put me in as an actress as a temporary solution."
Reporter: "How old were you then?"
Shin: "Thirteen years old. I entered the Joseon Art Troupe at the age of thirteen and followed them on a tour of the entire country."
Reporter: "What did you do while following them?"
Shin: "I participated in plays, but in addition to plays, I sang solo wherever I went and received a lot of applause. I was always good at singing songs at school."
Reporter: "What play did you first do and what role did you play when you joined the art troupe?"
Shin: "I played the role of a wildcat in the musical 'Wildcat (狸) and Rabbit (톡긔)'."
Reporter: "So how did you end up moving into the film industry?"
Shin: "After the Joseon Art Troupe finished touring all of Joseon and returned, another 'strike' broke out, so my brother started a separate Bando Art Troupe (半島藝術團), and I became a member of the Bando Art Troupe and toured the western part of Joseon this time. After finishing the western tour and returning, the Bando Art Troupe was dissolved, and a new Dongban Art Troupe (東半藝術團) was formed, which later became the Haedong Art Troupe (海東藝術團), and I became a member of the Haedong Art Troupe."
Reporter: "It changed a lot."
Shin: "Yes, it changed very often. My brother suffered a lot because of that."
Reporter: "What was your motivation for moving into the film industry?"
Shin: "When the Haedong Art Troupe went to Hamheung, Hamgyeong Province, to perform, I met a group of members from the Towol Society (土月會) there, and Lee Gyeong-son (李慶孫), who was among them at the time, saw me for the first time and discussed my work with my brother. After coming to Seoul, he introduced me to the Joseon Cinema Company (朝鮮키네마회사), and I immediately appeared in 'Arirang'."
Reporter: "Haha, so 'Arirang' was your first film. 'Arirang' was really good."
Shin: "Quietly bows her head and smiles."
Reporter: "Starting with 'Arirang,' how many films have you appeared in so far?"
Shin: "After 'Arirang,' there was 'The Crown of the Phoenix (봉항의 면류관),' then 'The True Identity of the 괴인 (怪人의 正體),' then 'Field Mouse (들쥐),' and now 'Goldfish (금붕어),' which is currently being filmed."
Reporter: "You've done both plays and movies, which do you find more 재미 (jami, interesting)?"
Shin: "It's more 재미 (jami, interesting) to act in a play right away. But when I put in all my effort into filming a movie and see the first screening, I feel very happy and good."
Reporter: "Which do you think you will be more successful in, plays or movies?"
Shin: "Movies."
Reporter: "During filming, when -<99> doing what kind of role - do you feel the best?"
Shin: "Hehe, are you asking all those things? (Laughs)"
Reporter: "Fans want to hear those things, so I'm asking even though it's embarrassing."
Shin: "The best time is when I'm acting a scene like meeting my older brother after a long time."
Reporter: "There are more scenes where lovers meet in movies, so it's the same as saying you feel good when you do those scenes."
Shin: "(Bowing her head) That's right."
Reporter: "When do you feel the worst?"
Shin: "When I have to act in a scene where a man who is not my lover tries to approach me when I'm alone as a virgin."
Reporter: "He's just a bad guy in the movie, but he's actually a close person you always spend time with, so why do you dislike it so much?"
Shin: "Even so, I have to create a sense of reality while acting, so I guess that's why."
Reporter: "When is it the most difficult?"
Shin: "It's the most difficult and tiring when I'm grabbed by a bad person and try to shake them off and run away. For example, in 'Arirang,' there's a scene where the landlord's servant (played by Mr. Ju In-gyu) tries to attack me. It's the most tiring to try to shake him off while filming that."
Reporter: "Are there times when you make mistakes?"
Shin: "Why wouldn't there be? First of all, if the weather gets cloudy while filming, I worry that the picture won't be clear, and I lose interest. And when I'm upset or in a bad mood because of something else, I can't do it no matter how hard I try."
Reporter: "That makes sense. They say your best expression is smiling happily, but what is the most difficult expression?"
Shin: "Being surprised. But I look in the mirror beforehand, sit down, and think about when I was actually surprised before, and if I practice, I can do it."
Reporter: "Which of the films you've appeared in so far do you think turned out the best?"
Shin: "The Crown of the Phoenix."
Reporter: "There was a rumor that The Crown of the Phoenix was not a good film."
Shin: "The film as a whole wasn't a good film, but I'm saying that in terms of my acting performance."
Reporter: "Which scene in which film do you think had the best expression so far?"
Shin: "There's nothing particularly good, but in my opinion, the scene in 'The Crown of the Phoenix' where I'm fantasizing alone seems to be the best. Have you seen that film?"
Reporter: "I haven't seen that film. Which do you think was the biggest failure <100> among the ones you've done?"
Shin: "In 'Field Mouse,' there's a scene where I'm standing alone and crying, and my expression when I meet the student wasn't good at all."
Reporter: "While filming various films, there must be times when you reluctantly do things you don't like."
Shin: "Of course. First of all, that's how it was when I was filming 'The True Identity of the 괴인 (怪人의 正體)'."
Reporter: "Why? For what reason did you dislike it?"
Shin: "The actors I was working with didn't match well. And the role I played at that time didn't suit me, so I reluctantly filmed it."
Reporter: "Then, what kind of role do you think is most suitable for you?"
Shin: "It's a bit funny, but a playful, cheerful character suits me. Maybe it's because I'm young."
Reporter: "This is a bit out of order, but when you are embracing the neck of the male actor playing your lover, pressing cheek to cheek, and shedding tears in a love scene, what do you feel? Do you feel good? Or do you dislike having to do that?"
Shin: "It doesn't matter whose cheek it is. I think to myself, 'What would I do if I were really in love with someone and faced this situation?' and naturally, my heart becomes filled with sadness."
Reporter: "So that's why your sad expression comes out so well."
Shin: "That's right. When I'm performing on stage, there are many times when I really cry and shed tears."
Reporter: "That must be a good thing. Now, please tell me which Western movie you've seen recently was the best."
Shin: "Oh, what was the name of that movie? It's about a country girl who comes to the city and becomes an actress and falls in love with the director."
Reporter: "Are you talking about the film 'Through the Haze of the Comet (彗星雲霧를 뚤고)' that was shown at Dansungsa (단성사)?"
Shin: "Yes, yes, that's the one. It's the best. The story is good, and the actors' performances are good."
Reporter: "What kind of movies do you generally like the most?"
Shin: "I like comedies the most."
Reporter: "Who is your favorite foreign actor <101>?"
Shin: "For women, it's Corinne Griffith, who starred in 'The Savvy Flapper.' For men, don't laugh, it's the comedy actor Reginald Denny."
Reporter: "While filming movies, you might get hurt by accident."
Shin: "Of course. I've already almost died twice. While filming 'The True Identity of the 괴인 (怪人의 正體),' I crawled up to Doseonam (도선암) on Bukaksan Mountain (북악산) and jumped off a high rock, but I messed up and my head was injured, and I became unconscious. The scar is still on my forehead. And while filming 'Goldfish' this time, I went to the Han River (한강) and fell into the water and almost died, but luckily I survived."
Reporter: "How did you fall in? There are even absurd rumors that you deliberately fell in to commit suicide."
Shin: "Why would I pay attention to such silly rumors? It was a scene where lovers were talking by the river. I was sitting on a rock by the river, but I lost my footing and fell in with only a cry of 'Aigoo, Mother! (아이그머니)' I didn't even have time to think, 'I'm going to die now.' But I still remember flailing my arms in the water."
Reporter: "That's a really dangerous situation. Now, let's stop talking about movies and ask about something else that might be uncomfortable."
Shin: "What is it?"
Reporter: "You're only sixteen years old, so it's early, but haven't you thought about marriage at all?"
Shin: She lowers her head and smiles.
Reporter: "It's something that you'll have to face someday, so you must have thought about it at least a little bit. And it's what your fans want to know the most."
Shin: "I'm only going to be an actress until I'm twenty."
Reporter: "And what will you do after you turn twenty? Are you saying you're going to get married?"
Shin: "That's right."
Reporter: "If you get married, what kind of man will you marry?"
Shin: "Aigoo (again lowers her head and smiles). Really."
Reporter: "Even if it sounds funny, I have to write those things to get praised by the fans. What kind of man do you think you will marry? Even if it doesn't necessarily happen that way, just what you think right now. For example, simply put, will you marry someone like an artist? Or will you cut off all ties with artists and marry a man in another field?"
Shin: "(Lowers her head deeply) An artist."
Reporter: "I guess so. Of course..."
Shin: "(Her head lowers even further.)"
Reporter: "You have a good talent for singing, so do you have any plans to study music?"<102>
Shin: "Why wouldn't I? I'm definitely going to study music."
Reporter: "Who is your sponsor who truly cares for you and tries to guide you well?"
Shin: "Only my older brother. And Lee Gyeong-son and Na Un-gyu."
Reporter: "Do you receive letters often?"
Shin: "Yes."
Reporter: "How many do you get? What kind of letters are they usually?"
Shin: "I've already received over 300 letters. My brother handles and reads all the letters. I get thank-you letters from women. The letters from men are mostly just dirty love phrases."
Reporter: "Do you reply to them?"
Shin: "What reply?"
Reporter: "In Joseon, actresses are considered to be unscrupulous, without anything to hold onto, and just cheap and dirty people. What do you think about that?"
Shin: "They're saying that because they don't know. As long as I'm not like that, it's enough. That's why I always go to the theater with my brother and don't hang out with other colleagues."
Reporter: "What is your favorite sport? Is it tennis? What is it?"
Shin: "The parallel bars (鐵棒)."<103>
Reporter: "Why parallel bars for a young lady?"
Shin: "I still like it."
Reporter: "What kind of books do you read at home?"
Shin: "I read the art magazine (예술삽지) and Joseon History (조선력사) while learning from my brother."
Reporter: "You must go to the theater often, so which narrator (변사) do you think is the best right now?"
Shin: "Well, I think Kim Yeong-hwan is better so far."
Reporter: "Do you eat three meals or snacks?"
Shin: "I eat three meals. I sometimes eat snacks."
Reporter: "What do you particularly like?"
Shin: "Ginkgo nuts and bananas (laughs again)."
Reporter: "Oh, I've troubled you for too long. Let's stop now. Is there anything else you want to say?"
Shin: "I don't have anything else to say. Just one thing I want to ask of everyone is that after watching my movies, please review the good and bad points, and I would be grateful if you could send me letters directly."
She was too gentle for an actress, so I felt sorry to ask her more questions.
Her older brother is Shin Chang-un.<103>

仙 『왜 업겟슴닛가 첫재 박히다가도 날이 흐려지면 사진이 선명하게 못 될 생각하고 흥이 나지 안음니다. 그러고 공연히 딴 일 노라도 마음이 상하거나 긔분이 달너질 때는 죽여라 하고 안되여요』
記 『그럿켓슴니다. 당신이 제일 잘 하는 표정은 깃버서 웃는 표정이라 합데다 만은 제일 힘드는 표정은 무엇인가요.』
仙 『놀내는 거야요. 그러나 미리 거울을 드려다 보고 안저서 전에 실지로 놀내본 때의 일을 생각해 가면서, 작고 련습하면 되기는 됩니다.』
記 『당신이 이때 까지 출연한 것 중에 당신 마음에 제일 잘 되엿다고 생각되는 것이 무슨 사신임닛가』
仙 『봉황의 면류관 이야요.』

仙 『사진은 전톄로 잘 된 사진이 못되여요 그럿치만 저는 저 한 사람의 연줄성적으로 보아 하는 말슴이야요』
記 『이 때 까지 출연한중에도 당신 마음에 제일 표정이 잘 된 것이 무슨 사진의 어느 장면임닛가.』
仙 『별로 잘 된 것이 어데 잇슴닛가 마는 제 마음에는 봉황의 면류관에 혼자서 공상(空想)하는 곳이 제일 잘 된 것 갓해요 그 사진을 보섯슴닛가?』
記 『그 사진은 못 보앗슴니다. 당신 마음에＜100＞ 하든 중 재일 실패 햇다고 생각되는 것은?』
仙 『들쥐에 왜 혼자 울고 섯는데가 잇지 안음닛가 그 때 학생을 맛날 때의 표정이 제일 못 되엿서요.』
記 『여러가지 사진을 박히는 중에는 더러 마음에 실흔 것을 억지로 억지로 하는 때가 잇겟지요』
仙 『잇고 말고요 우선 「괴인의 정테」(怪人의 正體)를 박힐 때에 그랫서요』
記 『왜 엇전 리유로 실혓슴닛가?』
仙 『출연하는 이들이 짝이 안 맛고요. 그런데다가 그 때에 내가 맛흔 역이 내게는 맛지도 안는 딴 청의 것이닛가 실흔 것을 억지로 박혓지요.』
記 『그럼 당신 생각에 당신에게 제일 뎍합한 노릇은 무엇 일까요.』
仙 『좀 우습지만 까불까불 유쾌하게 뛰노는 인물이 제게는 마저요. 아마 나희가 어려서 그런가 바요.』
記 『좀 순서가 밧귀엿지만 련애하는 장면을 박히노라고 애인 노릇하는 남자 배우의 목을 얼사 안고 뺨과 뺨을 마조대고 눈물짓는 형용을 할 때에 당신의 마음이 엇덧슴닛가. 좃슴닛가? 그런 일을 하게 되는 것이 마음에 실혀짐닛가.』
仙 『그것이 어느 배우의 뺨이거나 그런 분간은 업서요. 속으로 내가 정말 누구와 련애를 하다가 이런 경우를 당하면 엇저나 하고 생각하게 되고 그러 하닛가 저절로 마음이 비창해저요.』
記 『그래 저래 비창한 표정이 저절로 잘 되겟네요』
仙 『그럿탐니다. 저는 무대 우에서 연극을 할 때에도 정말 설허서 눈물을 흘니고 운 때가 만하요.』
記 『그건 조흔 일이겟지요. 그런데 이 번에는 최근에 구경하신 서양사진 중에 무엇이 제일 좃튼가 말슴해 주십시오.』
仙 『아이고 그 사진 일홈이 무엇이드라 시골처녀가 도희지에 나와서 녀배우가 되어 가지고 감독과 련애하는 것 말슴이야요.』
記 『단성사에서 상영한 것『헤성운무를 뚤고』라는 사진 말임닛가?』
仙 『녜녜- 그것이 제일 조와요. 내용도 좃코 배우들의 연출도 조와요.』
記 『당신이 제일 조와하는 사진은 대개 엇던 종료의 것임닛가.』
仙 『희활극이 제일 조와요.』
記 『당신이 가장 조와하는 외국배우＜101＞는 누구임닛가.』
仙 『녀자 치고는 『사치한 계집애』하던 코-린무-어 고요. 남자는 웃지 마세요 희극배우 레지날드 떼니야요.』
記 『활동 사진을 박히다가 잘못하야 몸을 닷치는 일도 잇겟지요.』
仙 『잇고 말고요. 그 동안에도 벌서 두 번이나 죽을 번 하엿담니다. 「괴인의 정톄」라는 것을 박힐 때 북악산 도선암까지 긔여 올나가서 놉다란 바위에서 뛰여 나리다가 잘못되야 머리가 터지고 인사불성이 되엿섯슴니다. 그 때에 험집이 지금껏 이마 우에 남어잇서요. 그리고 이번 「금붕어」를 박힐 때에 한강에 나갓다가 물에 빠저서 한 동안 죽엇다가 요행으로 살아 낫지요.』
記 『엇저다가 빠젓슴닛가. 세상에는 당신이 자살하려고 일부러 빠젓다는 당치도 안흔 소문까지 잇스니요.』
仙 『그 까짓 허풍선이 소문이야 종잡을 것 잇슴닛가. 애인과 애인이 강가에서 이약이하는 장면이라나요. 강가에 바위 우에 안젓는데 발을 헛드듸여서 그만 아이그머니 소리 한 마듸 밧게 못하고 풍덩바저 드러갓슴니다. 그려 그래도 이제는 죽엇고나 하는 생각 까지는 낫서요. 그러고 지금도 물 속에서 두 팔을 휘젓든 생각 까지는 남니다. 』
記 『참말 위태한 일이외다. 그런데 인제는 사진에 관한 이약이는 그만 두고요 다른 일을 좀 거북한 것을 뭇겟슴니다. 』
仙 『무슨 말슴이서요.』
記 『아즉 열여섯살 이시라닛가 일르기도 하지만은 결혼문뎨에 관해서 엇더케 생각하는 일이 업슴닛가?』
仙 『아이그』하고 고개를 풀숙이면서 웃는다.
記 『아모 때 당해도 당하고야 말 문뎨닛가 거긔에 대해서 아모 생각도 전혀 업지는 안켓지요. 그러고 당신의 영화를 사랑하는 팬들이 제일 알고 십허하는 말임니다. 』

記 『결혼을 한다면 엇던 남자와 결혼을 하게 될까요.』
