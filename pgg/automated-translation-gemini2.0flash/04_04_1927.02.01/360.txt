別건곤 第4號

1927년 02월 01일
女流名士의 家庭問題合評會記를 읽고 卽興詩의 生活改造論 시집 안간 處女의 結婚觀 (Reading the Records of the Symposium on Family Problems of Famous Women, An extempore poem on the Theory of Life Reform, and the Views on Marriage of Unmarried Women)
대담·좌담 (Dialogue/Symposium)

A: At the beginning of the New Year, a symposium of famous women on family problems was held at Gukil-gwan, what did you think? Do you know about it?

B: Yes! It became an article and was published in the New Year's issue of the Chosun Ilbo newspaper.

A: Then you must know the details of the symposium well. Who gathered and what did they discuss?

B: Almost all of the famous women of Chosun were selected and gathered. There were representatives from various night schools, female doctors, women's youth associations, and other prominent figures. Kim Won-ju or someone like that was also included, which was a unique feature of the gathering. As for the content of the symposium, it was about family problems, so it was obvious, wasn't it? Food, clothing, and shelter, marriage, love, childcare, things like that...

A: Oh my. That must have been significant. The people who gathered were notable, and the issues were plausible. Were there any noteworthy opinions?

B: Well, what can I say? From my perspective, it was like some poets who, after getting drunk, looked at the flowers blooming before their eyes and said, "Let's each write a poem!" and just wrote extemporaneous poems as they came to mind. If it's a symposium of famous people, and especially if they are discussing a serious issue under the current state of Chosun, they should have thought about it carefully and seriously, or at least shown some traces of having paid attention to it. But they just repeated the same old things that everyone already knows, and threw them out there as they came to mind. What else could that be but an extemporaneous poem? They should have considered and commented on family problems from a realistic perspective, but they just talked about them romantically and extemporaneously. In short, they mixed up real-world problems and extemporaneous poetry.

A: What are you saying? I can't understand what you're saying at all! Why is there so much extemporaneous poetry? I can't even understand what you're saying. Was everything they said as difficult as you make it sound?

B: No. Why are you pretending not to know, whether I'm telling the truth or not? If you're really curious, you should read it yourself.

A: Come on. When would I have time to read the whole thing? You've already read it, so tell me the general outline of the overall review. Surely there must have been some worthwhile opinions?

B: Yes, of course. Not everything was an extemporaneous poem. There were many good arguments.

A: Then wouldn't it be necessary to distinguish between the good and the bad?

B: Well, how can I remember everything that those many people said?

A: Even if you can't remember everything, surely you remember something about who said what, and what you felt about it?

B: It's annoying... and how would I deal with the criticism?

A: Come on. There's nothing wrong with speaking the truth and facing criticism. It would be unkind to overlook what's wrong.

B: Alright, alright. But the most annoying thing was Kim Ae-seok's bragging about her own family. During the symposium on food, clothing, and shelter, whenever someone made a good suggestion, she would say, "Yes, we do that in our house," and show off her own home. It seems like she has money and lives a fairly Westernized life. But how can poor Chosun people not live with their sons, grandsons, mothers-in-law, and daughters-in-law all crammed into one house? How can they only pay attention to nutrition and cook special meals, wash dishes only with soapy water, have a fireplace in the reception room, sleep with a hot water bottle in bed, have a bathroom in their house, and bury a ten-foot-long toilet? This person only thinks about herself and doesn't care about anyone else's situation. If that's the case, she's not qualified to discuss family problems in Chosun. Should she go to a meeting of upper-class women in America or France?

A: That's a problem. It's true that if we're talking about improving families in Chosun, considering the current situation, those things are all difficult. People can't even afford two meals a day and are starving on the streets, so what more is there to say? Were there any other opinions from other people? <114>

B: There were many opinions, but Kim Seon said that the current family system needs to be changed and economic problems need to be solved in order to improve food, clothing, and shelter. Shin Albert also said so, as did Kim Hwal-ran and Lee Hyeon-gyeong, but they just said it vaguely. They didn't say how to change the family system or how to solve economic problems. It seems like they avoided it because it's a fundamental issue that might be controversial, but there's no need to do that. I think the solution to family problems ultimately depends on the courage of educated women. There's no room for compromise or harmony today. If something is right and a rational action, they must rush forward without clinging to the old ways. If they just worry about appearances and do things half-heartedly, it will be the same forever. What kind of improvement is that? But women and men lack that courage. However, if Choi Eun-hee and Seong Ui-gyeong get married, they will take good care of their households, respect their husbands, and raise their children well. Kim Yeong-sun and Kim Won-ju said that it would be good if they bought all their kimchi and everyday meals, which surprised them and made them jump up and down. They said that their husbands and children should be fed with their own hands, with sincerity, so that the family has a warm feeling. That's true. I've seen it myself, and I thought that Western women all lived comfortably, but I saw that the husband of a Western missionary was buying meat and vegetables with his own hands, and his wife, who was the principal of a girls' school and a missionary's wife, was still going into the kitchen to cook every morning and evening. It all depends on the person. If a person is substantial and there's a sense of life and peace between husband and wife or in the family, then there's no excuse for not having time or effort. Also, Ryu Yeong-jun spoke from his medical experience, Shin Albert spoke as an elderly person, and Son Jeong-gyu spoke as an experienced educator, so they all had a lot of practical experience. They didn't just take it for granted that Son cleaned the dishes with kimchi soup at Seokwang Temple, which was truly attentive and admirable.

A: Besides that, was there anything else to talk about?

B: Why not? Choi Eun-hee said that using light green or jade colors when plastering walls is mentally comforting, especially in a single person's bedroom. And Seong Ui-gyeong made fun of men wearing Korean pants and jackets, saying they looked like tailless birds. Those are all extemporaneous poems, you see! Seong Ui-gyeong said that housewives should put their heart into food to make the family thrive, and she shouted "Hurray!" to Choi Eun-hee's opinion that her husband and children should be fed with her own hands. But when it came to clothing, she said that men not wearing Korean socks made things easier for women... and she made a hateful remark. She opposed Ryu Yeong-jun's opinion that Korean clothes should be worn with Korean socks, so isn't that too much of a contradiction? This person may be good at cooking, but it seems like she doesn't know how to make socks. Or perhaps she's good at sophistry, and she's just uttering sophistry like reciting an extemporaneous poem. Also, some people said that it's better to dye clothes at home because it's economical, while others said that it's better to have them dyed at the dye shop. Some people said that it's better to do laundry at home, while others said that it's more convenient to leave it to the laundry shop because it doesn't get damaged. Some people said that it's better not to iron clothes, while others said that what would they look like if they didn't iron them? Some people said that Western clothes should be abolished, while others said that it couldn't be done. There's no way to keep up. If female students were to memorize all of that, it would be as difficult as memorizing a chemical formula, and they would be shaking their heads in frustration. And there's no conclusion about what's good or bad. <115>

A: Alright! Let's move on to another topic. The symposium on marriage, love, and childcare must have been quite interesting, considering how much there was to say about food, clothing, and shelter.

B: Wait a minute. It's hard to think of anything. There were so many different opinions that I don't know who said what.

A: Come on. You should have paid more attention to that. Don't be so irresponsible.

B: Who knew I would be talking to you like this? But I would never write such irresponsible extemporaneous poetry. Anyway, with regard to marriage, Hyeon Deok-sin said that it should be based on love, Lee Deok-yo said that it should be a union of personalities, Gil Jeong-hui said that love marriages are dangerous and require the supervision of experienced parents, and Yu Gak-gyeong said that love should arise from marriages arranged by parents. What do you think? Let me hear your opinion.

A: Well, I'm confused. I don't have enough experience or knowledge about it. But it's all a matter of degree. If men and women have reached a certain age, have knowledge and character, then a love marriage would be good, but the so-called free marriages of modern men and women seem dangerous to me. As Gil said, the supervision of experienced parents is necessary, and as Yu Gak-gyeong said, it would be good if love arose from marriages arranged by parents, but that's also a problem. How many experienced parents are there today? And if love arises from a marriage arranged by parents, that's good, but what if it doesn't?

B: That's right. Love is a problem, and so is supervision. But it's true that a married couple needs to have some love in order to live together, so even if there are some problems, it's still better to leave it to the former. You have to experience some successes and some failures to find the right path.

A: But what was the conclusion there? <116>

B: What conclusion? But it seems that most people thought that love marriages were good, judging from the fact that they said that their hearts should be in tune. Choi Eun-hee was the most forceful in her argument. She said that there should be no conditions for marriage, and that she would marry a cart driver if she liked him for some reason. But I don't know if women really have that courage and awareness. The problem is that liking part. If she really likes him, would she be able to love and marry a cart driver? Of course, it's possible. But I hope that such a person will catch her eye. Especially for women, and even more so for modern women who have received so-called high school education, all they see is silk clothes, powder boxes, dressing tables, perfume bottles, two-story houses, and cars, so how can they have time to turn their eyes to a cart driver? I wonder if Choi herself has such qualities? Oh dear, those are the worries of older people like Shin Albert and Kim Mi-ri. Shin sincerely asked why women who were so passionate about love in the beginning would grab their friends' wrists after getting married and say, "Don't get married, live alone." And Kim earnestly asked, "So-called famous men get married but don't register their marriage, and then they casually kick their wives out with some excuse. Don't fall for seduction," as if she were giving advice to elementary school students.

A: So, what answers came out to Shin's question? What opinions were given to Kim's request?

B: Kim Won-ju said that the reason for the poor marital relationship after marriage is that they only saw each other's good points during the dating period, but after marriage, they discover each other's bad points while living together. Lee Deok-yo said that it's because of men's tyranny, and that men can't abandon their customs and try to force women to obey them, and then they apologize in the end. Seong Ui-gyeong then teased Ahn Jae-hong and said, "Do men really apologize that quickly?" And she said that she can't have a love affair because there are no men who have a proper love affair, so it's dangerous. Also, Ryu Yeong-jun said that husband and wife should obey each other, and Hyeon Deok-sin said that if they love each other, they will naturally obey each other. The theories are all good. But this is how I see it. At least those who participated in this symposium, who have received specialized or higher education, are an exception. But I think that so-called modern women who have received secondary education—who are, after all, representatives of modern women in Chosun—are more tyrannical than men. But it's not tyranny, it's just stubbornness and unreasonable demands. When they're dating, they say that they understand, trust, embrace, and endure everything. Just try getting married. No, even before getting married, as soon as they get a little closer and become less shy, they start making material demands to show off and satisfy their vanity. Spiritual love, understanding, trust in personality! Forget it <117>. And they pretend to know everything, even though they don't know anything. They're good at finding freedom and equality that they've only heard about, and they're good at treating people coldly, ignoring them, and snubbing them. Try teaching them something they don't know. If they were a calf, you could pierce their nose and lead them around. They're quick to catch on, and they trust pretty faces. If they're young, they act cute to flatter you, and if they're older, they say, "Do you think I can't find another husband if not you?" If they don't get what they want, it's great luck. Men's tyranny! That's an old story. Do modern women have any respect for men? There are plenty of examples. Just look at our friends M, S, C, K, and Z. Is that all? It seems like almost everyone who lives with a modern woman is tired of the modern woman's ways. Then the women will say, "He's only seen those kinds of women." "So-and-so is gentle, and so-and-so is agreeable, but he's just saying that because he's looking at women with a conventional eye and despising and enslaving them." You see, I'm a worshiper of women and a believer in female superiority, but if they act like that, there's no need to worship and respect them even half a penny's worth. Choi Eun-hee said sarcastically, "Men like women to be uneducated because it's easier to use them like servants," but in fact, it's better to know nothing and obey well than to be half-educated and act presumptuously. So, you might think that I'm saying that it would be better if everyone didn't learn anything and became uneducated, but if you're going to learn, learn properly, and if you're going to know, know properly, and take charge of your rights, your love, and your home. So, in the end, it's a matter of knowledge, character, and degree, so there are many teachers and seniors there, and they need to teach and enlighten them well. They have to make people. If they just make dolls every day, marriage and family will be a failure. Failure, failure. Oh, and the marriage registration issue! That's just childish. Kim Mi-ri warned them not to fall for seduction, and Park Gyeong-sik said that it was because the women were foolish. He asked why they didn't register the marriage. And he said that the social system that allows such men is bad. Seong Mi-gyeong said, "If there's such a man, kick him out!" and spoke boldly. What do you think?

A: What do you mean, what do I think? Of course, it's the man's fault.

B: It's the man's fault. Women have no responsibility at all? You see, why are women so stupid? They use the law as a standard for whether or not to register the marriage. In reality, what power does it have in the essence of marriage and couples? So, if it's right to register the marriage, then of course it's the proper procedure for a legitimate married couple to register their marriage immediately after getting married <118>, but the procedure alone cannot guarantee anything permanent for a married couple. So, they get married and register their marriage because they're infatuated with love, money, or looks and status. But what guarantee does the marriage registration provide if they fall out of love, run out of money, or something goes against what they wanted or demanded? Well, it might serve as a warning to those who act recklessly and treat marriage and love as a game or entertainment, but there's no other way than for a decent man to meet a decent woman. There's no other way. Look at those old-fashioned couples who have their marriage firmly registered. There are even more divorces there. It only gives more pain to both sides. And if there's a deep love and they become a couple, can they be so easily kicked out just because they didn't register their marriage? There are even those who say that they can't stop even if they're socially ostracized if their hearts are in tune. So, it's not a big problem. If there's a man who's not registering the marriage because he's going to kick her out with that as an excuse, then it's true that he already has some bad character or behavior. If she marries him blindly without knowing that, then it must be because the woman is attracted to some vanity or her own behavior is bad. And if there's really love and it's right to register the marriage, shouldn't everyone go through that procedure? So, instead of just looking for love from the beginning, it's necessary to get to know each other deeply and see if they're decent people before dating and getting married. What do you think? About my opinion.

A: Of course, since it's your opinion, it's right from your point of view. But they must have their own opinions too.

B: You're saying something obvious again. There's no point in saying that. But you see, there's also this saying that's like an extemporaneous poem or a rapid-fire statement. About the conditions for marriage, some people say that there are no conditions and that it's enough if their hearts are in tune, while others say that the first impression should be good and then their hearts should be in tune. Can marriage really work out well unconditionally? But those who say that are all Misses. Then are they all living as old maids because they don't have someone who suits their hearts? It's sad to be an old maid, but it's a little inappropriate to say this. But doesn't not suiting their hearts already become a condition? When their hearts don't suit each other, it's because there's always some dissatisfaction, such as that the person already has a wife, is poor, is old, doesn't have a high position, or has an ugly face. Isn't that a condition? Is there a law that has no conditions? But anyway, it's a big problem. Only here do they hide the name, but the words of a single teacher who is a professor at a women's vocational school... The conditions for marriage are: 1. Emotions, 2. Understanding, 3. Health, 4. Economics. Since they can't see all of these, old maids can only close their eyes and grab whatever they can get. That's what she said. I think it's a little irresponsible for a vocational school professor who is teaching others to say that.

A: Oh! That's not just a matter of being a vocational school professor, it's something to think about...

B: Rim Sun-bun said that it's best to have a military husband because they don't change their minds once they've made a decision, so you can trust them. That makes sense. It seems that there are a lot of men who do bad things and wander around. I thought only women were like that. And Hyeon Sin-deok said that it would be good to educate women about sex, but Gil Jeong-hui opposed it because it's dangerous. However, since there are many cases of women falling for seduction, having marital dissatisfaction, or experiencing health problems because they are too ignorant about sex, it would be good to do it in the end. Oh my, I've been babbling on my own. I'll stop saying things that will make people glare at me.

A: Come on. You're leaving out the childcare issue. How can you do that?

B: Oh dear, let's stop. The childcare issue is actually important, but maybe it's because there are so many old women and single women there, but what they said was also trivial. Kim Ae-seok is probably waiting for a baby, even though she doesn't have any children yet, and she has a separate room for the baby. Kim Won-ju said that if there's a place where she can entrust and raise her child, she'll give it away. Shin Albert and Kim Seon were surprised. That's all. (End)

잡지명	별건곤 제4호
발행일	1927년 02월 01일
기사제목	女流名士의 家庭問題合評會記를 읽고 卽興詩의 生活改造論 시집 안간 處女의 結婚觀
기사형태	대담·좌담

女流名士의 家庭問題合評會記를 읽고 卽興詩의 生活改造論 시집 안간 處女의 結婚觀
甲 신년 첫머리에 여류명사들의 가정문제합평회가 國一舘에서 열니 엇더라고 그래 자네도 그것을 아는가?
乙 응! 그것이 기사가 되야서 朝鮮日報 신년호 紙上에 발표되얏데.
甲 그러면 자네는 그 합평회의 내용을 자세히 알겟네그려. 그런데 도대체 모이기는 누구누구가 모이고 합평 한 것은 무엇무엇일넌가?
乙 출석한 인물이야 朝鮮의 여류명사로는 거의 추려서 모엿데. 각 야학교 여교원측으로 여의사측으로 각 女子청년회측으로 기타 유지로도 몃분이고 해서 하여간 여류인물로서 남이 알 만한 이는 다 모엿는데 그 중에도 金元周씨인가 누구인가 하는 분까지 끼인 것이 그 회합의 한 특색이엇고 합평의 내용이야 가정문제이닛가 물론 뻔한 것 아닌가? 의식주, 결혼, 연애, 육아문제 가튼 것이지 뭐...
甲 엑키. 그럼 상당하겟구먼. 인물들이 그만큼 모이고 문제가 그럴듯해 그려. 그래 무슨 볼 만한 의견들이 잇던가?
乙 글세 엇더타고 할고. 하여간 나 본 바로는 어느 시객들이 술잔이나 취한 김에 눈 압헤 피인 꼿을 보고서 「자! 우리 시 한수씩 짓세」하고는 즉흥시를 되는대로 써 논 폭 밧게는 안되데. 아마 적어도 명사의 합평회요 더구나 朝鮮현하의 중대한 문제를 가지고 토의한다면 미리 좀 신중히 또는 심각하고 진격한 태도로 생각하고 평소에 적어도 거긔에 상당히 유의해 오던 무슨 흔적이 좀 <113>뵈여야 할 터인데 그저 밤낫하는 소리 누구나 다 아는 소리를 되는대로 집어 내 던저 바렷스니 그것이 즉흥시가 아니고 무엇인가? 그러나 가정문제만 하야서는 현실적 입각하에서 진실하게 고찰하고 논평하여야 오를 것인데 그것을 갓다가 낭만적으로 즉흥시적으로 말해 바렷서. 하여간 현실문제하고 즉흥시하고 뒤범벅을 만드러 바렷단 말이여.
甲 엇더케 하는 말인지. 자네 말은 도모지 알어 드를 수가 업네그려! 웬 즉흥시가 그러케 만히 드러가나. 나는 위선 자네 말부터 못 알어 듯겟네. 그래 그 말한 것이 자네말처럼 그러케 모다 어렵더란 말인가?
乙 아니야. 이 사람이 왜 이러케 생청을 떼여 거짓말인가 오른말인가 자네도 정 궁금하거던 그것을 한번 읽어보게그려.
甲 이 사람아. 그럼 언제다 새찹이로 읽겟나. 자네는 기왕 읽어 보앗다니 말이지 대강 총평의 개략이라도 좀 드러보세그려. 그러구 개중에는 상당한 의견도 잇서슬 것이 아닌가?
乙 암. 하기야 그럿치 다 즉흥시야 되겟나. 물론 조흔 주장도 만테.
甲 그러면 조코 나즌 것을 좀 갈나서 말할 필요가 잇지 안한가?
乙 그야 이 사람아. 어대 그만한 사람의 수다한 말을 내가 지금 다 기억하고 잇서야지.
甲 다는 기억 못하더래도 대강 누구의 말이 엇더터라고 자네가 기억하고 늣긴 것이 잇슬 것이 아닌가?
乙 귀찬한걸...또 그 욕을 엇터케 어더먹게.
甲 그것이야. 오른 말을 하고 욕 어더 먹는 것은 할 일 업지 잘못된 것을 보고 그대로 넘어 가는 것이 도리여 불친절한 것일셰.
