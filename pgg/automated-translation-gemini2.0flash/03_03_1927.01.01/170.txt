別乾坤 第3號
1927년 01월 01일
現代珍職業展覽會 (Exhibition of Unique Modern Professions)
잡저 (Miscellany)

Exhibition of Unique Modern Professions

A Person Who Lives by Eating Snakes
Look at this. There's a job where you live by eating snakes! Among snakes, the *salmosa* (殺母蛇, pit viper) is said to be good for nourishing the body and medicinal for treating phlegm, so many people boil them and eat them. They say that they start this work after Hansik (寒食, Cold Food Day) every year because snakes start to come out and people start looking for them. They say that if you see a *salmosa*, you press its neck with a piece of wood and grab its head firmly so it can't bite you. It's a disgusting job. The peak season is July, and on a good day, they can catch more than 30 snakes. They keep the caught snakes alive and sell them for about 7 or 8 won per *mut* (묶, ten snakes). If they're lucky and meet a rich person, they can get 20 won per *mut*. Even if they don't sell well, they sell at least 300 won worth in a year, and with that, even as a widower, they can take care of two or three orphans.

They also sell *salmosa* caught in the summer during the winter. They put them in a box and leave a bowl of cold water for them to drink. They say they grow to be about the height of a child, and the big ones are about the height of an adult. They say that it takes three hours to boil them well.

They are called "Ggakjeongi," (깍정이, a derogatory term), and there are five groups in Kyŏngsŏng (京城, Seoul), two in Chemul (仁川, Incheon), and one in Suwŏn (水原, Suwon) who can't make a living without doing this.

A Person Who Lives by Hitting Things
Haha—this is a person who lives by hitting things. Blacksmiths also hit things, and Buddhist monks also hit things, but those aren't unique jobs, so they probably weren't exhibited at this exhibition. This is a job where you hit a *ttaktakyi* (딱딱이, clapper) when everyone else is asleep. I don't know if it exists in the countryside, but it's a new job that has appeared in Seoul.

Every night, twice (these days, three times a night because it's the end of the year), they hit two pieces of wood together, making a *ttak-ttak* (딱- 딱) sound, and shout, "Is there anyone sleeping with the gate open?" "Is there any house sleeping without being careful about fire?" They patrol each neighborhood like policemen. That's the job of this *ttaktakyi*.

The *ttaktakyi doga* (都家, headquarters) has a sign that says "Chongno Chagyonghoe" (鍾路自警會, Jongno Self-Defense Association) at 3 Toryŏm-dong (桃廉洞, Doryeom-dong).

They divide Kugyŏng Iibok (구리개이복, presumably a neighborhood) into fourteen sections, with four *tongni* (洞里, neighborhoods) per section. One person is responsible for one section (four neighborhoods) and patrols every night. Their monthly salary is 16 won 50 jŏn, so it's a job to pity. But don't despair yet. If they collect the 10 jŏn per month from each house well, they get a bonus of 15 jŏn per won, which isn't a small amount. And if they collect and submit more than 60 won per month, they get a special bonus of 1 won 50 jŏn. And if they patrol well without missing a single day in a month, they get a bonus of 1 won 50 jŏn. So, if you add it all up, it's not a small amount. But even if you add it all up, it's only about 30 won.

If they get cold while patrolling in the deep night, they go into a police box like it's a friend's house and warm themselves by the police officer's brazier. And if they meet a friend who's doing a little bit of stealing, they take them to the police box, get a stamp from the police officer, and take it to the Self-Defense Association, where they get a bonus of 1 won. The *ttaktakyi* also has a busy job behind the scenes.

A Person Who Lives by Catching Birds
If you go up to Samchŏng-dong (三淸洞, Samcheong-dong) or the back of Ch'wiun-jŏng (翠雲亭, Chwiun-jeong) in Seoul early in the morning for a pre-breakfast walk, you'll see old people, young people, students, and gentlemen coming up from the dark dawn and taking deep breaths on this peak and that valley, or exercising with their chests bare, or shouting to practice their speeches, or rubbing themselves with cold water. It's a whole world that lazy people can't even dream of. But among them (on the mountain or under the pine trees), there's someone who's not a gentleman or a student, some ragged person who's not shouting or rubbing themselves with cold water, but just squatting and looking around. That's the unique job to show at this exhibition. "What is that person doing squatting there for so long?" If you go closer, they don't say anything, don't move, just sit there and look up at the sky. "Hey, what do you do? You've been sitting under that pine tree like that every morning..." If you ask, they say, "Yes, I'm a bird catcher," and look up at the sky again as if annoyed. If you look at the pine branches, wondering how they catch birds without a gun or bow, you'll see small birdhouses hanging on the pine branches, and two small birds chirping inside. And if you look closely, you'll see a long stick sticking out crookedly like a rotten branch. They put a sticky substance called *kkŭnkkŭni* (끈끈이, birdlime) on that stick, so the birds mistake it for a tree branch and come to rest on it, getting stuck to the birdlime. And the bird in the birdhouse is there to lure other birds, so they put it in the birdhouse and hang it on the stick. They say that birds always have a path they fly on, and if you find that path and set it up, other birds hear the sound of the bird in the birdhouse and come and get caught. It seems that birds also fall for the *miin-gye* (美人計, honey trap). They say that they catch four or five birds a day while sitting with their arms crossed, and if they take them to a bird dealer in Chin-gogae (진고개, presumably a place), they get 50 or 60 jŏn per bird, so they make 3 won a day. As for the types of birds, there are *kongsae* (콩새, Brambling), *p'atsae* (팥새, Eurasian nuthatch), *solsae* (솔새, Goldcrest), and *pak-sae* (박-새, Titmouse). They say that they use 10 jŏn worth of birdlime for 2 or 3 days, so they earn 8 or 9 won with a 10 jŏn investment.

A Person Who Lives by Catching Ghosts
There's a saying, "They eat the wind and poop clouds," but there are also people who live by catching ghosts. They are in the same spiritualist (崇神人) association, but *mudang* (巫堂, shaman) and the blind are on opposite paths.

The *mudang* makes a living by appeasing and comforting ghosts, while the blind make a living by catching and chasing them away. There are almost 300 people in Kyŏngsŏng who carry a staff, beat a drum, and go around looking for customers who want them to catch ghosts, so it would be worrying if there were ghosts. They would starve if people knew there were no ghosts...

Under the pretense of being blind, they sit surrounded by young women and old ladies, waving their blue eyeballs like phlegm and beating a drum, shouting nonsensical things like, "When was heaven created? When was earth created? When was man created? When was the ghost created?..." and showing off all the natural slyness they were born with. They earn at least 3 or 4 won at a time, and sometimes more than 20 won. The world seems bright, but in reality, it's dark.

They chase away non-existent ghosts, and even blind people can sit comfortably and earn money, so isn't it quite clever?

Anyway, as far as jobs go, it's a unique job worthy of being exhibited at this exhibition. But while the blind person is just trying to make a living, the women who are so engrossed in the ghost-catching that they pee in their underwear should be brought here and made to sit at this exhibition.

Hahaha...

A Person Who Lives by Looking for Fights
It would be fortunate if you didn't get beaten up just by looking for fights, but here's a great job on display.

When they meet a friend, they first ask how they are, but as soon as they meet, they put aside greetings and ask, "Is there any case (事件)? If there is a case, please let me know." That's the person who lives by looking for fights. Asking "Is there a case?" means "Is there a fight?"

What kind of work do they do that they're looking for fights? It's because they quit being a lawyer.

Doesn't a lawyer have to look for fights and take on cases to make a living? Whether it's a money fight, a women's fight, a land fight, or a reputation (名譽) fight, they need fights to happen often and take them on, or else they'll have to close their office. But a lawyer can't go around shouting like a tofu seller or a bean sprout seller, "Is there any fight? Is there any case for trial?" So they get their law firm employee (secretary) to find cases or, if not, they beg someone with a wide network of contacts to "Please find me a case. I'll give you 20% of the income..." Why only 20%? If the lawyer is short on cash, they'll beg to give 30%, 40%, or even 50%. So, to get that commission, they go around looking for cases to hand over to the lawyer. That's what it means to have quit being a lawyer. So, the first thing they ask when they meet someone is "Is there a case?" It's a unique job that makes everyone see them as someone with a fight on their hands. <51>

A Person Who Makes a Living with Their Mouth
There are so many kinds of jobs... There are people who make a living with their mouths and people who make a living with their throats.

Lawyers, newspaper and magazine advertising staff, pastors, and street vendors all make a living with their mouths, and in music, vocalists (聲樂家) make a living with their throats. But those aren't unique jobs, so they can't participate. Instead, it's the auctioneer in a fancy building on Chongno street in Seoul who shakes a bell and matches the rhythm of a story to his throat, shouting at the top of his lungs,

"Cheap! Cheap! 10 jŏn! 10 jŏn! Now! 15 jŏn! 15 jŏn! Now—even cheaper, 13 jŏn! 13 jŏn!" It's the job of the auctioneer who auctions off goods to earn a living with their throat.

Even if they shout so hard that their throat bursts, it wouldn't be a unique job if they were the owner of the business, but the owner of the business is separate, and they just get paid a daily wage for shouting out 10 jŏn and 20 jŏn. Isn't it unique?

The income of these auctioneers is usually divided into three categories: first class, who receive 1 or 2 won per day; those who receive a commission (割引) from the sales price depending on the number of items they sell; or those who sell one or two carts of goods and receive 20% or 30% of that. They say that there are 20 people in Seoul who just sell their voice at auction houses like this.

A Person Who Lives by Scouring Off Dirt
If a person isn't dirty, or if everyone takes a bath only at home, the family of the bathhouse owner would starve. Is there a more unique job than living by scouring off dirt (때, ttae)?

Whether the price is high or low, they heat the water with coal from morning and sit stolidly in the middle of the men's and women's baths, praying that even one more naked person will come. That's the daily routine of someone who lives by scouring off dirt. Unlike other businesses, since the water is already heated, it's a loss if there are few naked people taking off dirt and a profit if there are many. "These days, so few people are taking off dirt... If so few people take off dirt, how can we make a living?" Such laments come out every summer.

Usually, 160 or 170 men and women come in a day. The dirt (때, ttae) that 160 or 170 people take off and leave behind must be amazing when you collect it. And how amazing would it be if you collected it for a year? Who says it's dirty? It's someone else's livelihood...

Still, perhaps because Koreans are refined, only 5 or 6 of the 60 or so bathhouses in Seoul are run by Koreans, and more than 50 are the livelihood of Japanese people.

These days, with schools on vacation, the students who go to the countryside take a bath in Seoul before they leave, so the bathhouse business is booming. But it's not as good as the dirt (때, ttae) that people take off at the end of the year.

A Person Who Lives by Dealing With Poop
Don't pinch your nose because it stinks. There are many brothers who live by dealing with poop. It is understandable when a farmer touches their own waste in order to use it on their fields, but there are countless people who make it their job to carry buckets of poop (똥, ttong) from house to house from morning till night. Just 10 years ago, these people were addressed with honorific titles, but times have changed and now they must carry poop buckets to survive.

Even in this cold winter, it is difficult to get hired if you don’t have breakfast before dawn, and even then, after carrying a poop bucket from 8 AM, shouting “I’m here to remove the poop-” (“똥치러 왓소-”), they can only straighten their aching backs and go home when it gets dark. It is undeniably hard labor. And yet, they only earn 90 jeon a day. The people who collect the poop from the buckets and transport it by cart are considered the highest class, but even they earn only 95 jeon. Those who push a cart on the streets are considered the lowest class and earn only 70 jeon.

It truly evokes pity to think about those who start before dawn in this cold weather, breaking up the frozen poop with a tool called a *hogo* (호고) and carrying it to the cart until evening for only 90 jeon.

A Person Who Lives by Sweeping
Black, black, black, black... A person with a black face, black clothes, black lips, and even black eyes. There are quite a few people who live by sweeping chimneys and flues.

The office door of a school or company opens slightly, and if a black, ghostly head peeks in without a word while you’re sitting alone, you might scream! But since there are many people sitting together, you don’t get startled but just look up.

“Hehe, do you want me to sweep your chimney? I’ll do it cheap.”

Even doing it cheap costs 20 jeon to sweep a stove chimney once. If you meet someone who insists on haggling, it’s 15 jeon.

How many houses need their chimneys swept? But still, they usually earn 2 won a day, and if they’re lucky, they earn more than 3 won, so you can’t look down on a black person.

When they’re walking around sweeping chimneys and there’s no chimney work, they shout on the main street, “I’ll sweep your flues- I’ll clean your furnace. Only 10 jeon! You’ll use less wood, the room will be warmer, and if you heat just one lump of coal, it’ll be so warm you’ll burn your cheeks even on the coldest days.” So, winter is a cozy season for them.

What do they do in the summer? They each do other work in the summer, but there are many who just live off what they earned in the winter.

A Person Who Lives by Being Obnoxious
Even for an exhibition of unique jobs, this is truly a bizarre job... So, what does it mean to live by being obnoxious? Is it like a wife who sees her husband’s concubine? That’s obnoxious, but it’s not how they make a living. Then, are you talking about someone who just goes around as someone else’s concubine? That’s not a job either.

The person who lives by being obnoxious and is displayed here is an actress who plays villainous women in *sinp’a* (新派) plays. How’s that for a plausible exhibit? There are generally two types of young women who appear in plays: one is the protagonist of a tragedy who is either abandoned or widowed and wrings tears from the audience, and the other is a woman who plays a stepmother, a concubine, or the third person in a love triangle. They need to be good at acting out the *yangjaemul* (洋才物, presumed to be melodrama) with a pale face and pinched lips, acting obnoxiously while screaming "You'll have to see me die!" The more applause they get from the audience, the higher their salary goes, so this obnoxious money-maker studies co-wife fights every day. There were obnoxious people in the Lim Sŏng-gu group, who have been dead for a long time, and in the Kim To-san group, but perhaps there weren't any women who were good at being obnoxious in that group, so handsome men put on makeup and did it. A woman like Kim SoO in the Kim SoO group is considered a representative among those who make a living by being obnoxious. However, even *sinp’a* play groups can’t even afford a bowl of *chang-gukbap* ( presumed to be a type of noodle soup) when they go on tour in the countryside, and they fill their stomachs with two *hotteok* (호떡, Korean pancake) and collapse in the back room of the theater, which is like a beggar's house, and spend the night. If they happen to stay in an inn, a pretty obnoxious actress is often held as collateral for the cost of food. So, as soon as they get off the stage, they’re in a pitiful situation.

A Person Who Makes a Living in the Dark
A person who only makes a living in the dark... it’s on display in a dark place.

Don't assume that someone who makes a living in the dark is a thief. Even thieves need to work under electric light or with a flashlight. But here's a job where they work more diligently than anyone else in the dark, but run away as if something terrible has happened as soon as it gets light. What is on display here is a film narrator (*pyŏnsa*, 辯士) for silent movies. In the past, they had to come out like Sŏ Sang-ho in his prime, matching the rhythm of the military band with butt dances and blowing a rickshaw horn, making faces under the electric light to be popular, but now that's become more modern. They don't have time to show their face under the electric light, and only after the lights go out do they come out quietly and move their mouths busily.

They sit still, engaging in romance, becoming pessimistic, getting motivated, running away in cars, and chasing in airplanes, moving their mouths quickly. When they reach the point of a big fight, they can't control the inspiration that rises within them, so they jump up and punch and kick in the dark, acting out a scene by themselves, which is cool, but only people with quick wits can focus on the film and see it.

They wear high-collar Western suits, like silent movies, hang out with *kisaeng* (妓生, female entertainers), and seem proud to others, but in reality, they have many bitter experiences because it's a job that's only busy in the dark. The worst thing is that they ruin their eyes if they're a narrator for a long time. To be a narrator, they have to start as an apprentice, paying for their own food and expenses. If they have talent, they can become a full narrator in just a few weeks, but if they don't have talent, they can stay as an apprentice for 2 or 3 years, which is a sad thing to hear. Still, if they become a full narrator, they can earn from 40 won to 150 won, so it's a decent living for a nighttime job. <55>

A Job That Goes Between Heaven and Hell
A job that goes between Heaven and Hell… You might easily mistake it for the job of a Christian pastor. However, when you find out what it is, it is actually the complete opposite: it is the job of a procurer ("*ttujaengi*", 뚜쟁이), a broker in the human flesh market, which is hard for us to talk about. Looking at it one way, the " *ttujaengi*" brokers good relationships for many people, so they might go to Heaven after death, but considering their crimes, there is no place for them to go but Hell. Their lives are truly bizarre. They don't invest any capital or put in any effort like other jobs. They just entice women from good families or match up vagrant youths through sweet talk and profit from the reward after they facilitate illicit relationships between men and women. The *ttujaengi* are also divided into upper, middle, and lower classes depending on their skills, methods, and relationships, and their compensation is not fixed. If they meet a naive and free-spending person, they can earn tens of won at once, but usually, it is less than ten won. Sometimes, they get to wear clothes and shoes. If they are lucky, they can earn money from both sides, but if they are unlucky, they have to pay the so-called “flower money” (花代) themselves. Not only that, but if they handle things wrong, they get cursed, slapped, and even end up in the police station. Even if they skip dinner, they sometimes get to eat fancy dishes and *sŏllŏngt'ang* (설넝탕, ox bone soup) thanks to others, and they sometimes ride a human-powered rickshaw (人力車) when they are busy running errands. It is impossible to know how many men and women do this job in Seoul alone. There are *ttujaengi* who specialize in other people's concubines, *ttujaengi* who specialize in respectable women, and even *ttujaengi* who specialize in female students these days. As for their background, the men are mostly those who have wasted money or indulged in gambling and have fallen into ruin, and the women are those who have wandered around as other people's concubines and have had their brains ruined by carnal desire and cannot survive without preying on others. How many of these groups of demons are there in Seoul alone? Among jobs, it is truly a unique job. <56>

뱀 잡아먹고 사는 사람
이것보시요. 뱀으로 먹고 사는 직업이 잇소구려! 뱀 중에도 살모사(殺母蛇)는 몸 보하는 데에도 좃코 담든 데에 약이 된다하야 고아먹는 사람이 만흔 고로 살모사를 잡어다가 팔아서 먹고 산다는구려...... 해마다 한식(寒食) 후면 뱀이 나오기도 시작하고 먹는 사람들이 찻기도 시작하는 고로 한식만 지내면 버리를 시작한다는대 살모사를 보기만하면 나무때기로 목을 눌르고 손으로 그묵을 꼭 잡아쥐면 뱀이 목을 돌리지 못하는 고로 물리지 안는다고요. 딴은 징글징글한 직업이지요. 뎨일 만히 잡히기는 7월이 한창인대 잘 잡는 날은 하로에 30여 마리씩 잡기도 한담니다. 잡은 것은 모다 산채로 두엇다가 한뭇(열 마리)에 보퉁 7, 8원씩 밧고 팔고 엇저다가 운수가 조와서 돈 만흔 사람을 맛나면 한뭇에 20원도 밧는다는데 못 팔아도 1년에 300원어치는 파는 고로 그걸로 홀아비 살림일망정 의지가지 업는 불상한 아해를 두어 사람 쯤은 다리고 살아간담니다.
녀름에 잡은 살모사를 겨울에도 파는데 괴짝 속에 너어두고 랭수(冷水) 한 그릇을 떠서 노아두면 그 물을 먹고 삿다고 함니다. 대개 아해 키로 한 길은 되고 큰놈은 어른의 키의로 한 길이 된다는데 끌는 물에 너어 고을 때는 세 시간이면 잘 고아진다나요.
『깍정이』 라는 반갑지 안흔 층호를 밧아가면서 이런 짓이라도 안하면 먹고 살 수 업는 친구가 지금 경성에 다섯 패 제물(仁川)에 두 패 수원에 한 패가 잇다 함니다.

뚜드리고 먹고 사는 사람
하하-이건 뚜드리고 먹는 사람임니다. 뚜드리기야 대장쟁이도 뚜드리고 부처님 제자도 뚜드리지만 그거야 진긔한 직업이 될 것 업스닛가 이 뎐람회에는 출품이 안 된 모양이지요. 이것은 남들이＜48＞ 모다 잠자는 때에 딱딱이를 뚜드리는 직업이야요. 시골에는 업는지 몰라도 서울에는 전에 업던 것이 새로 생긴 직업임니다.
밤마다 두 차례씩(요사이는 섯달 금음께라고 하로밤에 세 차례씩) 딱- 딱- 하고 나무토막 두 개로 마조 뚜드리면서 『대문 열어놋코 자는 사람은 업는가.』 『불조심 안 하고 자는 집은 업는가.』 삷히면서 동리마다 순경을 도는 것이 이 딱딱이의 직업이람니다.
딱딱이 도가(都家)는 종로 자경회(自警會)라는 문패를 도렴동 3번디에 달고 잇슴니다.
구리개이복(北)을 한 구역을 네 동리 씩으로 하야 모다 열 네 구역에 난호아 가지고 한 사림이 한 구역(네 동리씩) 책임을 지고 밤마다 도는데 한 달 월급이 16원 50전씩 이라니 동정할 직업이지요. 그러나 아즉 미리 락망은 하지 마시요. 그 외에 집집에서 한 달 10전씩 밧는 돈을 잘 밧아오면 1원 밧는데 15전씩 상금을 주닛가 그돈이 적지안코 또 한 달에 60원 이상을 밧어 밧치면 특별 상금 1원 50전을 주고 그러고 또 한 달에 한 번도 안 빠지고 잘 돌앗스면 상금으로 1원 50전을 주닛가 모다 합치면 그리 적지 아니하담니다. 그래야 모두 합처야 30여 원 밧게 못 되는 것이지만은요.

연통을 쑤시며 도라다니다가도 연통버리가 업스면 큰길거리로 『방고레 쑤시시요- 굴돌 소제하오. 한 번에 단 10전! 나무 덜 들고 방 땃듯하고 석탄 한 덕이만 되우면 아모리 치운 날도 볼짝이 데을 만큼 덥습니다.』 하고 외우고 도라다니는 고로 겨울철 한철은 오붓한 버리가 됨니다.
녀름철에는 무얼하느냐고요? 녀름에야 각각 딴 버리를 하지만 겨울에 번 것만 가지고 녀름에는 놀고 먹는 이도 흔히 잇슴니다.

강짜로 먹고 사는 사람
아모리 진긔한 직업 뎐람회기로 참말 괴상한 직업도 다 잇지......그래 강짜로 먹고 사는 직업이 무어란 말슴이요. 시앗 본 마누라가 그러탄 말인가요? 그것은 강짜는 강짜지만 그걸로 먹고 사는 것은 아니지요. 그러면 남의 자근댁으로만 도라다니는 사람 말슴인가요? 그것도 직업까지 되지는 못하지요.
여긔 출품된 강짜로 먹고 사는 사람은 신파연극에 따라 다니면서 독부(毒婦) 노릇하는 배우임니다. 엇덧슴닛가 그럴둣한 출품이지요? 연극에 나오는 젊은녀자 중에는 대개 두 가지 종류가 잇는데 한 가지는 소박을 맛거나 과부가 되거나 하야 구경꾼의 눈물을 짜아내는 비극의 주인공이고 또 한 가지는 계모노릇 첩 노릇 삼각련애에 셋재 노릇하는 녀자임니다. 눈가장이를 파르둥둥하게 하고 입끗을 염으지게 모아 가지고 『나 죽는 것을 보고야 말테요?』 하고 발악을 하면서 양재물 연극을 앙큼하게 잘해야 구경꾼이 손벽을 치고 구경꾼이 손벽을 만히 처주어야 월급이 올라가는 까닭에 이 강짜로 먹고 사는 버리꾼은 날마다 하는 공부가 시앗 싸홈 실디 견학이시람니다. 죽은지 오래 된 림성구 일행에도 잇섯고 김도산 일행에도 이 강짜버리꾼은 잇섯스나 그패에는 강짜 잘 하는 녀자가 업섯던지＜54＞ 얼골 고흔 남자가 분을 바르고 하엿섯고 김소O 일행에 마호O 갓흔 녀자는 강짜로 버리하는 중에도 대표라고 할만 함니다. 그러나 신파연극 일행도 시골 흥행을 나가서는 장국밥 한 그릇도 못 엇어먹고 호떡 두 개에 주림을 채우고 독각이집 갓흔 연극장 무대 뒤ㅅ방에 쓰러저서 밤을 지내는 것이 보통이요 엇저다가 려관방에 묵엇다가는 밥갑에 쪼들니여 어느때던지 얼골 반반한 강짜 배우가 몸뎐당을 잡혀 잇게 되는 때가 종종 잇스니 무대에서만 나려오면 딱한 신세이지요.

어두운데서 버러먹는 사람
어두운데서만 버러먹고 사는 사람......딴은 출품 어두운데다 진렬해 노앗군요.
어두운데서 버러먹는다고 도적선생이라고 짐작해서는 모씀니다. 도적 도선생도 실상 일을 할 때는 뎐긔불 밋혜서 하거나 회중뎐둥이라도 가지고야 함니다. 그러나 어두운데서 부즈런히 누구보다도 부즈런히 일을 하다가도 밝아만지면 큰일난듯키 쫏겨 도망하는 버리 여긔 출품된 것은 활동사진 변사임니다. 전갓흐면 한창 때의 서상호 모양으로 군악대 장단에 궁등이짓을 맛처 인력거 라팔을 뿡뿡거리고 춤을 추며 나아와 뎐긔불헤서 어리광을 부려야 인ㅅ긔가 놉하지지만은 지금은 그것도 개화가 되여서 뎐긔불 켜놋코는 얼골을 내밀어 볼 틈이 업서지고 따르릉하고 불이 꺼진 후에라야 슬그먼이 나와서 부즈런히 입을 놀입니다.
가만히 안저서 련애를 하고 비관을 하고 분발을 하고 자동차로 도망를 하고 비행긔로 추격을 하느라고 입설을 밧브게 놀리다가 대격투를 하게 되는 사연에 이르면 스스로 니러나는 감흥을 닉이지 못하야 벌덕 니러서서는 어둔 속에서 혼자 주먹질을 한다 발길질을 한다 자긔몸으로 일장 연극을 하면서 설명을 하는 것도 멋잇는 일이지만 눈치빨른 사람 아니면 사진에 취해서 그 구경은 하지 못함니다.
하이카라 양복닙고 활동 사진 조아하는 기생 친하고 남이 보기에는 억개가 읏슥해 보이지만 실상은 어둔데서만 부즈런한 직업이니만큼 쓰라닌 일도 만탐니다. 뎨일 변사 노릇을 오래하면 눈을 버리개 되는 것이 쓰라는 일이요. 변사 노릇을 하자면 먼저 자긔밥 먹고 자긔 돈 쓰면서 견습을 해야하난대 재조잇는 사람이면 두어 주일동안만 하고 곳 본변사가 되지만 재조가 업스면 2년 3년을 해도 견습으로만 묵는다니 듯기에도 딱한 일임니다. 그래도 본 변사가 되기만 하면 40원으로크터 150원은 밧을 수 잇다니 밤ㅅ버리치고는 상당은 하지요.＜55＞

天堂·地獄 오고가는 버리
天堂·地獄 오고가는 버리라닛가 잘못하면 야소교의 목사임의 버리로 알기 쉬울 것이다. 그러나 알고보면 실상은 그와 정반대로 우리 사람으로서는 참아 말하기도 어려운 人肉市場의 仲介業者 「뚜쟁이」를 이름이다. 「뚜쟁이」는 一方으로 보면 여러 사람에게 佳緣을 매저주닛가 죽어서 天堂으로 갈 뜻도 하지만은 그의 罪惡으로 말하면 地獄으로 가지 안코는 갈 곳이 업슬 것이다. 그들의 생활이야말로 참으로 奇奇怪怪하다. 다른 직업처럼 무슨 미천을 드린다든지. 무슨 努力을 드리지도 인코 다만 甘言利說로 良家의 婦女를 꾀여내기도 하고 浮浪靑年을 適合하기도 하야 男女間 서로서로 불의의 교제를 하게한 후 그의 報酬로 얼마식을 어더먹고 산다. 뚜장이도 역시 그의 수단 방볍과 교제 여하에 의하야 상등도 잇고 중등 하등의 구별이 잇는 것은 물론이어니와 그 보수도 역시 일정치가 안타. 어수록하고 돈 잘 쓰는 사람을 만나면 한 번에 몃 십원도 생기지만은 보통은 십원 이내가 만타. 엇던 때는 옷가지도 어더 입고 신발커리도 어더 신는다. 그러다가 운수가 조흐면 좌우편에서 몃원식 다 어더먹고 운수가 불길하면 보수는 커영 소위 花代라는 것을 자긔가 무러노키도 한다. 그뿐이냐 깻댁 잘못하면 욕도 먹고 뺌도 맛고 또 경찰서에 가셔 변도밥 신세도 끼친다. 저녁밥은 굴물지언정 남의 덕분의 淸料理 접시와 설넝탕 그릇은 각금 어더 먹고 밧부게 심부름할 때에는 팔자업는 人力車도 각금탄다. 서울만 하야도 이러한 버리 하는 남녀가 몃몃인지 알 수 업다. 남의 첩 전문의 뚜쟁이도 잇고 여염부인 전문의 두쟁이도 잇고 근래에는 심지어 녀학생의 뚜장이도 잇다. 그의 출신으로 말하면 남자로는 대개 전일에 돈양이나 쓰고 오슞�푼이나 하다가 패가타락된 자들이요 녀자로는 남의 첩으로 도라다니다가 춘화로 골이 다 되야 남의 덕에다 솟을 글지 안으면 살 수 업는 자들이다. 이 악마의 무리들이 서울에만 하야도 과연 몃 사람인가 직업 중에는 참으로 珍職業이다.＜56＞
