잡지명	別乾坤 第69號
발행일	1934년 01월 01일
기사제목	約婚女의 惡魔性(探偵小說) (The Demonic Nature of the Betrothed Woman (Detective Novel))
필자	崔流帆
기사형태	소설 (Novel)

The Demonic Nature of the Betrothed Woman
						Choe Ryufan

1. The Gruesome Corpse of the Renowned Beauty

In the relatively secluded S village, about ten li (里) away from Gyeongseong, a horrific incident occurred that sent shivers down one's spine just hearing about it.

The sound of a car siren blared, and constables with sabers and officials in Western-style suits got out, suddenly displaying a grand spectacle usually only seen in the city.

Crowds flocked from all directions, not to see these officials, but rushing like an avalanche toward a certain spot outside the village. The reason for the commotion in this quiet village was there: a terrifying and dreadful thing lying there.

That terrifying and dreadful thing was Gyeongsuk, the only daughter of the wealthiest landowner in this village. Why call someone else's precious daughter a "thing"? Because she was a corpse. Her insides were exposed, torn and ripped by wild dogs, and white bones were visible among the red muscles—a truly gruesome death.

What cause and effect led to such a tragic end for this renowned beauty of S village? Her face was so mangled that it was unrecognizable; she was only identified as Gyeongsuk by her clothes and belongings. Even the fiercest dogs cannot devour a living person. She must have been murdered, and her body hidden in these bushes, where the dogs found it and attacked.

The first discoverers were Kim Yeongho, the son of the head of this district (免狀), and his friend, Yi Manse, a detective novelist (探偵小說作家) staying at a relative's house in S village. Early that morning, as they were taking a walk near the railroad tracks (鐵道線路) outside the village, they saw dogs growling and eagerly devouring something in the bushes below the tracks. There was also a strange smell. At that moment, Yi Manse's sixth sense (第六感) kicked in, and he chased the dogs away and jumped down there.

There, something like chunks of beef were wrapped in soft clothing. The two were startled. Testimonies from Yun, who guarded the chapel nearby, and his daughter proved it was Park Gyeongsuk.

As the matter became clear, someone rushed to Gyeongsuk's house, another went to report to the police station, police calls went back and forth, and people with tense expressions gathered at the scene from every house. Soon, a police car arrived, causing an unprecedented commotion in this S village.

2. Kim Yeongho, the Betrothed Man

After a thorough on-site investigation, the body was transported to Gyeongseong for an autopsy (解剖). To investigate those involved, the reception room of the local elementary school was temporarily borrowed, and Gyeongsuk's parents, household staff, employees, visitors, the discoverers Kim Yeongho and Yi Manse, and Yun, the chapel caretaker, and his daughter Sunhui (順姬) were summoned in turn.

The questioning took quite some time, but apart from a letter found by the victim Gyeongsuk's mother, nothing particularly significant was obtained.

"This was in Gyeongsuk's desk drawer. It was lying on top of the ledger, just like this. She must have gone out after receiving this letter." The mother said, handing over an unsealed envelope without a stamp.

"Did you ask your family who delivered this letter to your daughter?" Judge M, the preliminary judge (豫審判事), asked in a low voice.

"Yes, I asked, but they all say they don't know. Maybe she received it directly when she went outside."

"Ah, I see. Then, do you have any idea who might have written this letter?"

"No. You might say this because she's my daughter, but she would never exchange letters with strange men. The man who wrote this letter is definitely not someone she knew before <48> and she probably went out because she was enticed by the letter." The content of the letter was extremely simple.

Tonight at seven o'clock
I will be waiting under the gingko tree. Please come. Do not tell anyone and come alone. There is something important to discuss.
From K

"Do you recognize this handwriting?"

"I don't know it at all."

"Is it true that Gyeongsuk was engaged to a man named Kim Yeongho?" Judge M said this because the "K" in the sender's name matched the initial of Kim Yeongho's name (金), and he thought it reasonable that she responded to her fiancé's request if it was a letter from him.

"Yes, we thought so too, so we asked Yeongho, but he said he had no reason to send a letter and that he was in Seoul at that time... And first of all, his handwriting is not that bad, and if he wanted to meet Gyeongsuk, he could come to our house instead of sending a letter." This must be some bad guy imitating Yeongho's handwriting and luring her out."

The questioning between the judge and the victim's mother did not go any further. And the judge felt the need to question Kim Yeongho, whom he had questioned first, once more. The police chief and everyone else shared the same opinion. When Kim Yeongho was shown the letter in question, he gave the same answer as Gyeongsuk's mother.

"You said you were in Gyeongseong last night. That is a definite alibi (現在不在證明). You must have visited someone in Gyeongseong. I'm not particularly suspicious of you, but because the case is serious, it's necessary to check." The preliminary judge asked as if it were nothing.

"I didn't visit anyone in particular. I didn't meet anyone on the street either," Kim Yeongho replied, looking troubled.

"Then, what did you go to buy? The clerk or owner of the store would remember you."

"I didn't buy anything. I just went to Seoul because I wanted to and strolled around Jongno and Honmachi. The only thing I bought was a pack of cigarettes."

"That's a bit difficult then." The judge, thinking deeply about something, looked at the other person's face, and then, as if realizing something, said,

"That won't be a problem. You must have taken a shared taxi (乘合自動車) to and from Gyeongseong. Surely the driver would remember your face. If we ask the driver, we'll find out everything." The judge said, his face brightening, but unexpectedly, a look of embarrassment appeared on Kim Yeongho's face. He turned pale and stammered. The judge wore a subtle smile around his lips, but his gaze, sharp enough to pierce the other person's heart, watched Kim Yeongho's expression.

"It's a coincidence. It's a difficult situation." Kim Yeongho muttered strangely, looking at the person standing behind Judge M as if asking for help.

There stood Yi Manse, the detective novelist and Kim Yeongho's friend, looking sympathetic. The reason he was attending this interrogation, especially behind the judge, was that he had a prior acquaintance with Judge M, who had allowed him to observe.

The judge hoped to get some reference material from Yi Manse, and Yi Manse had a good opportunity to observe a real crime case. He had been questioned once by the judge, who was an acquaintance, as a witness to the incident, but he did not leave after it was over and remained in his seat.

However, hearing Kim Yeongho being questioned about the car to and from Gyeongseong, changing color, and muttering strangely, Yi Manse could not help but worry.

He had roughly guessed Yeongho's <49> difficult situation. He must have gone to Gyeongseong last night to meet his lover (戀人). Yeongho was trying to fabricate an alibi (現場不在證明) to hide it.

"You didn't take a shared taxi, did you?" The judge, suspicious of the other person's hesitation, asked, pressing for an answer.

"I didn't take a car," Yeongho said, looking distressed, and for some reason, his face turned red. The sudden reddening of his pale face drew the attention of many people.

"You must think I'm lying. But I'm telling the truth. Coincidentally, I didn't take a car last night. When I went to the terminal (發着所), the last car to Gyeongseong had already left, and since there were no cars, I had no choice but to walk. Unlike a train, it only takes about forty minutes if you go the short way."

"You said earlier that you went to Gyeongseong without any purpose, just because you wanted to go. Do you have the courage to walk ten li or so without any purpose?" The judge's questions became increasingly urgent. "Yes, people from outside the area think nothing of ten or twenty li. People here usually walk even if they have business in Gyeongseong to save on car fare." Yeongho, who said that, is the son of a local wealthy man. He didn't look like a young man who could casually walk ten or twenty li.

"Then, how did you come back? You didn't walk both ways, did you?"

"Yes, I walked back as well. Because it was late."

"Then your alibi naturally disappears. Doesn't that mean there is no proof that you were not in this S village the night the crime occurred?" The judge's attitude seemed to become increasingly cold.

"I myself find this incident coincidental. It would have been good if I had met someone I knew on the way, but I didn't meet anyone." Yeongho said, lamenting his misfortune.

"But even if I don't have an alibi, that forged letter shouldn't make me a suspect, haha..." He forced an awkward laugh from his unease.

"You call it a forged letter, but there's no proof that it's really a forged letter." The judge replied coldly, as if implying something.

"Even if it doesn't match your handwriting, you could deliberately change your handwriting."

"Why would I need to do such a thing? Me?"

"No, I'm not saying you changed it, just that you could have. ... Alright, you can go now. I might suddenly have more questions to ask."

As Yeongho left, Judge M secretly spoke to the police chief, and a plainclothes detective, receiving orders from the chief, set out somewhere.

3. The Straw Doll Murder Case

"Mr. Yi! This concludes one phase. It's not as interesting as a novel." <50>

"Concludes one phase? Are you going to dismiss me now? Isn't the case just beginning to progress?"

"Hahaha... There's no way. There's no more investigating to do today. We'll know the results of the autopsy tomorrow. That's when the case will progress."

"You're very enthusiastic. Shouldn't you leave it to the chief?"

"Well, it seems like it'll be a bit interesting, so I'm planning to investigate it myself."

"Does the judge suspect Kim Yeongho?"

"No, I'm not suspecting him. But assuming such a thing and starting is extremely dangerous, like you always write in your novels. If I were to suspect, I'd suspect everyone. You might even suspect me of having suspicions." The judge said jokingly, shaking Yi Manse's shoulder.

"Are you free now? I have something to show you, so let's take a walk together to the chapel here." Yi Manse, not accepting the other person's joke, said what he had wanted to say earlier.

"You mean to go to Yun's place? What's there that you want to go see?"

"There's a large effigy (제웅)."

"There's an effigy?" The judge looked at Yi Manse's face in surprise.

"I was going to tell you when we were inspecting the scene, but I didn't."

"Ah! Is that effigy related to this case?"

"Anyway, let's go see it once. It might be the key to solving this case."

Judge M couldn't genuinely listen to this absurd remark, but he had no reason to refuse Yi Manse's suggestion. He said, "Novelists are always like this," and followed Yi Manse out of the elementary school gate.

When they arrived at the chapel, Yun and his daughter greeted the two, terribly afraid that they were being investigated again.

"Elder! I came to see that effigy you were talking about." When he said this, Yun widened his eyes and said,

"Yes, is that what you mean?" and led them to the shed. Opening the shed door, they saw a human-sized doll made of straw lying in a corner where coal and firewood were piled up.

"What is this? Isn't it a scarecrow?" the judge said, dumbfounded.

"It's not a scarecrow. Where would you find such a fine scarecrow? It's quite scary. It's made to ward off evil (방자하기 위하야 만든 것입니다)."

"Then, what does this effigy-like thing have to do with this murder case?"

"It's difficult to say definitively what the relationship is. But I can say for sure that there's a connection. Elder! Please tell this gentleman about when you first saw this effigy."

"I think it was five days ago in the morning. I had something to do and was passing by the railroad tracks... right where Gyeongsuk's body was."

"Right near where Gyeongsuk died," Yi Manse interjected. <51>

"Yes, but Gyeongsuk's body was right below the embankment of the road, while this effigy was about ten feet away from the road in a field of grass."

"With a knife stuck in its chest."

"Yes, right here. This knife was stuck in its chest."

"It's an effigy made to ward off evil... But the fact that it was abandoned near the scene of the murder four days before the murder, could there be some hidden meaning?"

"I don't know!" Judge M could not ignore this coincidence (between the doll and the human) of the two murder cases. More than that, the body of the effigy with a chest wound stimulated him in a strange, indescribable way.

"I thought it was just the work of local pranksters and went to take it away to burn it," Yun said.

"Then you didn't tell anyone about this effigy story?"

"No. Who would have dreamed that it would be a precursor to this incident? Actually, one person did see it. None other than Gyeongsuk. She deliberately came to my house the day or two after I found it. My talkative daughter mentioned it in passing, and she asked to see it." You never know what will happen in this world. Who would have known that that young lady would meet such a gruesome death like this effigy?"

"Oh, did Gyeongsuk often come to your house?"

"No, she didn't come often. She said she had something to give to my daughter that day, and she brought it."

Hearing this detailed story, Judge M asked the guard to take care of the effigy when the police came, and left the house.

"It can only be seen as a coincidence. It must be the work of local pranksters, as Yun said. It's ridiculous to think that the culprit tested the murder with the effigy before committing it, and it's also foolish to leave it in the same place."

The realistic preliminary judge could not affirm the detective novelist's mysterious words.

"If you think that way, it might seem unrelated to the crime. Furthermore, isn't it interesting that Gyeongsuk came to see the effigy?"

"No, she might not have come to see it on purpose. According to Yun, Gyeongsuk didn't have any particular business to see Sunhui, so the real purpose of her visit might have been to see the effigy."

"You're having strange fantasies. But the real problem is not something as skillful (奇術) as that." Preliminary Judge M dismissed Yi Manse's opinion as fantasy, but who knows what that fantasy might mean. (To be continued) <52>

約婚女의 惡魔性
						崔流帆
1. 評判美人의 慘殺體
경성시내에서 약 십리쯤 떠러진 비교적 한적한 S촌에 듯기만 하야도 몸서리가 저절로 처지는 참혹한 사건이 발생하얏다.
자동차의 싸이렌소리가 요란히 들녀오더니 샤벨들찬 경관들이 나리고 세비로 양복을 입은 관리들이 나리고하야 갑작이 도회에서만 볼수 잇는 어마어마한 살풀경을 보여 주엇다.
이곳 저곳에서 우루루 몰려드는 군중들은 이 관리들을 보랴는 것이 아니고 동리 박 어느 지점(地點)을 향하야 눈사태가티 몰녀가는 것이엿다. 그곳에는 이 한적한 동리에 소동을 이르킨 원인 즉 무섭고 끔직스러운 한물건이 가로 노혀 잇는 까닭이다.
그 무섭고 끔직스러운 한 물건이라는 것은 이 촌에서 첫재둘재를 다토는 지주(地主)의 무남독녀 외딸인 경숙(慶淑)이엿다. 외? 남의 귀여운집 규수를 한 물건이라고 불늘가? 그것은 그 녀자가 한 개의 시체가 되어 잇섯든 까닭이다. 아니 들개들에게 뜻기고 찍기여 하로 밤사이에 내장이 드러나고 연붉은 근육사히로는 흰 뼈까지도 앙상하게 보이게 될만치 참혹한 죽엄을 한 까닭이다.
이 S촌에서 평판 잇는 미인이 무삼 인과관게가 잇서서 이러한 비참한 최후를 이바지하게 되엿나? 얼골은 누구인지 분별할 수 업게 진익이며 젓고 오즉 그 녀자의 입은 옷과 가진 물건으로써 경숙이라는 것이 판명되엿다. 암만 사나운 개들이라도 산사람을 잡아먹을 수는 업다. 누구에게 살해를 당한 뒤 이 덤불속에 숨겨둔 경국이의 시체를 개들이 발견하고 덤벼든 것이 틀님 업슬 것이다.
최초의 발견자는 이곳 면장(免狀)의 아들인 김영호(金英鎬)와 그의 친구로 S촌 친척집의 두류하고 잇든 탐정소설작가(探偵小說作家)인 리만세(李萬世) 두 사람이엿다. 그들이 아츰 일즉이 동리박 철도선로(鐵道線路) 엽흐로 산보하고 잇슬 때 선로 언덕아래 덤불속에 개들이 으르렁 거리며 무엇인지 열심히 뜨떠먹고 잇는 것을 보앗다. 그리고 이상한 냄새가 나는 듯 하얏다. 이때 리만세는 제육감(第六感)이 동하야 개를 쫏차 버리고 그곳으로 뛰여나려갓섯다.
그곳에는 쇠고기 덩이가튼 것이 부드러운 옷에 싸인채 듸울고 잇섯다. 두 사람은 깜작 놀낫다. 부근에 례배당을 직히고 잇는 윤첨지와 그의 딸의 증언으로 그것이 박경숙이란 것이 판명되엿다.
일이 이러케 되자 경숙이 집으로 사람이 급히 뛰여 가고 주재소로 고발하라가고 경찰전화가 왓다갓다 하고 집집이 서는 긴장한 표정을 한 사람들이 현장으로 현장으로 몰녀드럿다. 그리고 얼마 안되여서 경찰자동차가 도착하야 이 S촌에는 전무 후무한 소동이 이러난 것이엿다.

2. 約婚男子 金英鎬
면밀한 현장조사가 끗나고 해부(解剖)를 하기 위하야 시체는 경성으로 운반하야 갓다. 관게자의 취조를 하기 위하야 임긔처치로 이곳 보통학교 응접실을 빌니고 그곳에 경숙이의 량친을 비롯하야 그 집하인 고용인 출입하는 사람들 발견자의 김영호와 리만세 례배당직이 윤첨지와 그의 딸 순희(順姬)가 순차로 불니여 드러갓다.
취조는 시간이 퍽 걸니엿지만 피해자 경숙 어머니가 내여 노흔 편지한장 외에는 별노 신통한 것을 엇지 못하엿다.
『경숙이 책상 설합속에 이러한 것이 잇섯습니다. 지금는거와 가티 맹우에가 노혀잇섯습니다. 아마 그 애가 그 편지를 밧고 나간 것이 틀닙업습니다.』 어머니는 이러케 말하면서 우표가 부터잇지 안은 봉토를 내여 노앗다.
『누가 이 편지를 따님에게 전하얏는지 댁 사람들에게 무러 보시엿습니까?』 예심판사(豫審判事)인 M씨가 나즉한 목소리로 물엇다.
『네. 무러보앗지만 모다 몰느겟다고 합니다. 그 애가 문박게 나갓슬 때 즉접 전하엿는지도 몰느겟습니다』
『아아 그런가 봅니다. 그러면 로인께서는 누가 그 편지를 하엿는지 짐작을 하시겟습니까?』
『아니요. 딸자식이니깐 그러케 말한다고 하시겟지만 그에게 잇서서 외간남자와 편지질하는 일만은 업습니다. 이 편지를 한 남자도 결코 전부터 아는 것이＜48＞ 안이고 아마 그 편지의 선동을 바더서 나간 것이겟지요』 그 편지의 사연은 극히 간단한 것이엿다.

오날밤 일곱시
은행나무 아래에서 기다리고 잇겟습니다. 꼭 오십시오. 아모에게나 말슴하시지 말고 혼자 오서야 합니다. 중대한 사건을 의른한 것이 잇서서 그럼합니다.
K로부터

『이 필적을 아시겟습니까?』
『도모지 몰느겟습니다』
『경숙양은 김영호란 사람과 약혼한 것이 사실입니까?』 M판자는 이러케 말하얏다. 편지 발신안의 『K』란 것이 김영호란 김자의 머리글자의 일치하고 약혼자의 편지라고 하면 그 남자의 요구에 응종한 것도 무리는 안이라고 새악한 까닭이다.

판사와 패해자의 모친과의 문답은 그 이상 더나가지 안엇다. 그리고 판사가 맨 먼저 취조한 김영호를 한번 더 심문하야 볼 필요를 늣기엿다. 경찰서장을 비롯해서 모다 가튼 의견을 가지엿섯다. 김영호는 문제의 편지를 보이니깐 악가 경숙이 모친이 말한거와 대동소이의 대답을 하엿다.
