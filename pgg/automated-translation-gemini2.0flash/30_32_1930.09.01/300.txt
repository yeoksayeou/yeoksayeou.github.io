Faces of Korean Prominent Figures from a Physiognomic Perspective
						Bae Sang-cheol

Song Jin-u, Still Not Free From Calamity

At this juncture, with the prevalence of civilized science, some might scoff, "What nonsense is this physiognomy (觀相法) and phrenology (骨相學) in 1930?" Many will laugh before even reading this article, but in truth, once understood, it is quite fascinating. However, I do not intend to delve into a lengthy discussion. In this heat, where the temperature soars to 95.6 degrees and then plummets, sweating profusely, there are two main reasons why a protracted argument for its fascination is impractical (if a scholar were to write it, it would be a scholar contrary to the narrow-minded). First, writing after sweating all day is detrimental to a scholar's health (unless there is makgeolli or ice cream). Second, if the scholar sweats, the reader might become like Jang Bi (begging forgiveness for the rude comparison), afraid of sweating, and might not read the entire article. Thus, the scholar's erudition would not be widely known, hence the preference for brevity (economy). Even this is quite long, but in a world full of freedom, writing is free and reading is free. Let's consider this minor issue settled! If you find the noise unpleasant and resist, you can simply choose not to listen because seeing you avert your gaze is bearable. Without further ado, let's proceed to the phrenological critique!

You might dismiss it as superstition. Although I graduated from a vocational school and have some knowledge of science, this is science beyond science... Upon hearing the words of a scholar blending falsehood and truth, old man (古下, pen name) President Song (宋社長) (no relation to the traitorous son-in-law) with a smile of <100> complete understanding said, "Even a modern airplane can be seen as superstition by those who don't understand its organization." Ah, with that, the scholar felt emboldened, taking it as permission to open his mouth. But at that moment, he realized that the words of his grandmother, who had passed away the day before, flashed through his mind when she first saw an airplane in the sky: "How can people do such things? They must be using sorcery and capturing empty spirits!"

Let that be as it may, let's briefly examine the characteristics appearing in Song Jin-u's (宋鎭禹) phrenology.

Is he a notable figure? Is Song Jin-u a notable figure (人物) from a phrenological standpoint?

The definition of a notable figure may be limitless, but upon examining his eyes (日月, left and right eyes), spirit (神氣), eyebrows (眉), voice (聲音), and build (體局), it seems that if he cannot be a carp (鯉魚), he could at least be carp roe (鯉魚子), far surpassing ordinary crucian carp (鮒魚) or minnows (송사리류). (That is, metaphorically speaking.) To put it bluntly, Song Jin-u is not a minnow. Had he lived in the old days (the era of smoking with white tiger pipes), he would have effortlessly attained the position of Minister of State (崇政大夫) or Left State Councilor of the State Council (議政府 左贊成). If he had been born in the turbulent times of the late Silla and early Goryeo dynasties, wielding a mace, a crowbar, and a club, charging recklessly, annihilating enemies with a resounding cry and returning with drums beating after a hidden ambush (一聲梛子響處에  伏軍이 大起하야 적을 섬멸하고 북을 치며 도라오는 羅末麗初에 태엿던들), he would have likely become a meritorious subject who assisted in stabilizing the nation (推忠定難定社壁上三韓功臣), a Grand Preceptor of the Threefold Merit (三重大匡檢校太師), and the Lord of Yeoryang County (礪良縣, the old name for Yeosan, leading the scholar to speculate whether his clan is the Yeosan Song clan (礪山 宋氏)) (開國侯), a matter of retrieving something from a bag (如探囊中之物). But...
=麻姑三桑何處在, 魯連氣像掛東天=
Looking at his bright and refined forehead (彩霞月角明秀學堂廣大學堂) in detail, his unwavering determination will easily surpass a thousand setbacks (千折不屈), proving his ambition.

Sir (先生) (oh, the scholar calls himself 'sir' to others... since I've already opened my mouth...), your eyes are like an eagle's eyes (鷹目), highly regarded in phrenology, destined to command high office and wealth. However, your mouth is small, and you are likely to get caught in the eagle's net after surpassing Mount Heng (衡山) in the South (or perhaps it's the North!) (南岳衡山). You will have to go to the authorities (田中, and if you still don't know, detention) often to pull weeds.

The Five Peaks (五岳), Four Rivers (四瀆) (the mouth, one of the Four Rivers, is weak), Six Ministries (六府), and Three Talents (三才) and Three Stages (三停) are all good, very good, but look here, your cheekbones (顴骨) have quite a bit of 㥘氣 (inauspicious energy). It has a subtle depression as if it's been burned by fire and also resembles the color of slightly dried tangerine peel (陳皮). This is a terrible 㥘氣 and ill omen (厄殺), so be mindful and be careful to get past your many forties and fifties, after which that unlucky star will disappear.

Your heart is very kind, and your temperament is quite restless. Your stubbornness is considerable, and your decisiveness is remarkable. Once you befriend someone, it's hard to lose their favor. Your heart contains the Pacific Ocean (太平洋), so it's only natural that your chest will burn from the steamship smoke.

The horizontal lines (橫紋) on your forehead (天庭) foretell misfortunes at the ages of sixteen or seventeen, nineteen, twenty-one, twenty-eight or twenty-nine, thirty, thirty-five, thirty-seven, and thirty-nine. You will spend your parents' wealth <101> with your own hands (自手) and then rebuild your fortune with your own hands (自手).

Looking at the breadth and length (廣短) of your philtrum (人中), your womb (子宮) doesn't seem very fertile, so you should expect a late-born son (晩得의 男) or look forward to glory. Your face is generally red, like metal thrown into the fire. You must undergo thorough training to succeed, but it is inevitable that you will face adversity. From the age of fifty, you will receive the support of the earth element (土運), where all things are mutually reinforcing, yin and yang harmonize, and a great destiny that shakes heaven and earth will come. (Eh-hey)

Looking at the chain-like structure (聯珠格) of your ears (城廊), you won't be able to care for both parents for long, so you either already lost your parents early (早失父母) or one of them will pass away early. Since you have sparse and disconnected eyebrows (踈斷眉), you may have to adopt a son (양자를 안가면 양자를 할 數) (or maybe not)!

Your phrenology is generally of a superior configuration (上相格), but even after forty-two, the unlucky star will occasionally raise its head and wag its tongue until you reach fifty. You may not live a long life, but you will live past seventy, and there will be a time in your life when you sing the song of knocking on a basin (叩盆의 歌) under the moon (月下), which will happen before you turn fifty. I want to write in more detail, but I couldn't pick up the brush during the day because of the heat, and I started writing during the slightly cooler night, but those wretched mosquitoes (暮起) and bedbugs (虛竹) are invading my bare toes, so I can't write long. Whew, it's hot, damn it.
=采陽何日*將展, 楚阜三年鳳不飛=
-弓滿定穿楊, 登樓醉一場. 醉中有失錯, 於我有何妨-

Han Yong-un, Retreating from the Precipice

First, let me explain what "retreating from the precipice" (懸岸撤手) means. The term "retreating from the precipice" comes from Buddhist scriptures. It might seem disrespectful to bring up difficult Buddhist scriptures to Venerable Manhae (萬海居士) Han Yong-un (韓龍雲) , who is 52 years old, 15 years older than me, and considers anything not Buddhist to be heterodox, but it is because he only considers 佛道 to be the right way. "Retreating from the precipice," to put it succinctly, means that one must let go of what one is holding onto even on top of a hundred-foot cliff (百尺危岩의 上에서) (where there is deep blue river water below) (and if you let go, you might fall and die) which is said to be the courage of a man. You should understand with just this much. I will not explain it further.

However, Han Yong-un's face shows that he has such courage. His forehead and eyes, and the line at the right corner of his eye (右魚尾, right eye's corner) all indicate his perseverance and bravery. However, at first glance, he appears impoverished and wretched (窮狀이 께제하게 낫하나 보이고) (in reality), and there is nothing particularly striking about his face. He doesn't seem like someone who could become a political figure, let alone a village schoolteacher (村訓長ㅅ감), and he only appears to be a monk or someone destined for a solitary and poor life (孤寒之相) (they are the same thing). The fact that he was among the 33 signatories of the Declaration of Independence (己未年에 33인에 든 것은) is beside the point. In Buddhism, he seems fit to stand at someone's gate, striking a wooden clapper (木柝) and <102> begging for alms while chanting Buddhist scriptures (念佛乞粮). Not only does he look that way in my eyes, but most people would view him the same way, but surprisingly, there is a black mole resembling an asterisk (*) like the star Altair (牽牛星의 黑子) on his right neck, and the borderland (邊城) is sturdy and high, resembling the poem "The Yellow River rises far beyond the white clouds, An isolated city stands amidst myriad peaks" (黃河遠上日雲間 一片孤城萬仞山.) (聖人不相), clearly indicating that he will command many people and live to the age of 88.

The scar from a knife (刀痕) on the upper left ear (左耳上의 頭中) speaks of past misfortunes, silently expressing the sorrows of his 27th and 28th years.

His forehead (天庭) is slightly round, his philtrum (印堂) is flat, he has a three-character line (三字의 紋), and his chin (地閣) is slightly pointed, suggesting an inclination towards Buddhism and some desire for fame. However, I predict that no matter how hard he tries, worldly fame is all fleeting clouds. "Literary talent should be valued like the clouds" ("文章當貴總如雲"). Therefore, even if he enters politics and becomes active, Manhae himself will never succeed.

However, because his frame is so extraordinary, his posthumous fame will be great. But unless he rides the winds and clouds, his name will be buried after his death. That is because I divined the place where he sits (坐處). (I consider myself quite adept at Zhouyi and Six壬) He may or may not be the reincarnation of Seosan Daesa (西山大師). Around the age of 70, he will be widely known for his benevolence (仁聲遠聞).

I want to write more, but now the bedbugs are really stinging me, and the drink I had is wearing off, so I feel dizzy and my throat is scratchy, so I really can't write anymore.
=人心欣欣欣欣際, 世事非非是是間. 欣慼是非皆外置, 此身何處不春風.=

Yi Sang-hyeop, a 70-Point Passer

Before meeting Mr. Yi Sang-hyeop (李相協), whose pen name is Hamong (何夢), I had evaluated his pen name first. What kind of dream (夢) is this? What in the world is this dream? It is truly a peculiar pen name. As far as pen names go, this is the first one that meets my standards.

But upon meeting him, I wondered, what kind of face (相) is this? I struggled quite a bit to grasp his build and form, but because I am a scholar, I confidently classified him as having a "bizarre face" (古怪之相), the fourth-best among the four auspicious faces in the Eight Methods of Observing People (觀人八法四大吉 相中 第四位), scoring around 70 points in the Book of Physiognomy (相書). (Although his face is different from Chunwon's (春園의 얼골), the way I grasp the spirit and build is truly unfathomable). It's not just that he is exceptionally intelligent (총명이 過人 할 것만이 특점이 안이다). His wisdom is also exceptional (지혜도 過人), his skill is exceptional (능정도 過人), his social skills are exceptional (交際術도 過人), his ambition is exceptional (志槪도 過人), his hardships are exceptional (苦生도 過人), and his glory will be exceptional (榮華도 過人). This impulsive and quick-tempered nature (퍼르르하고 불가티 급하게 이러나는 性ㅅ갈은) rather than a calm and orderly one, signifies a masculine spirit (男子的 기상), which is enough to startle others, and his fondness for the bizarre and his seething ambition (勃勃하는 풍운의 야심) will indeed cause him to look at the world and (except for someone like me) laugh dismissively, saying, "How can a swallow know the ambitions of a swan?" (燕雀安知鴻鵠志랴?)

There is a firm determination in his eyes (눈에 굿은 뜻이 잇스니), so he will rarely reveal his intentions even to his wife and children (妻子의게라도 자기 뜻은 좀처럼 공개해 주지 안을 것이오). A certain stubbornness that makes him go his own way can also be seen as his distinctive trait.

Mr. Hamong (何夢), remaining in obscurity, does not have the face to simply create a Hamong Park (하몽공원 그릇만 할 골상은 결코 안이다). This is the difference in physiognomy between him and Chunwon (春園). I was taken aback (狼狽) to find that his fame had already spread as far as it could go. Even when he is 80, he will still have the heart to challenge (抗心), thinking, "How dare these rats be so presumptuous!" (「鼠輩安敢如此」리오). Therefore, he also has the makings of a late bloomer (晩達의 格), so the fame he achieved in the past with his brush (筆) will only serve as the foundation for his future achievements (신 삼으려고 집축인 것 밧게 못 될 것이다). I'm writing this as if I'm getting a free drink from him. Smile (微笑).

Mr. Hamong (何夢) has a "The time has come to ride the winds and clouds in the Jade Hall" (「得際風雲在玉堂」 格) configuration. However, the Jade Hall is definitely not just a position like a junior scholar (校理). When the muddy Yellow River (黃河) becomes clear, there may be a day when he writes a sweet poem (甘吐). But...

If he asked me what kind of official position he would have held in the past, I would answer that he would have been something with the middle character of his name ( 그의 일홈字中 가온대 글자는 할ㅅ걸 하고 답하리라). (Seeing this, I am also a sharp writer).

His lifespan is also quite long, and he seems likely to live beyond seventy (稀年은 시럽시 더 살 모양). Around the age of 48 or 50, he seems poised for extraordinary activity (非當한 활동을) (Eek! Note, note...), leaving success aside for now.

As for wealth... He should be an official who fans himself with a feather fan, but he will also quietly hoard sacks of rice (쌀섬이나 얌전이 싸코) and keep quite a few crowned figures (冠쓴 놈) (paper money (紙錢)) detained in a golden cabinet (金櫃)! You have good sons, and you don't have to worry about going to the authorities (밧가운대) from now on. Your wife is also good, and you might have even considered having a mistress, but if you haven't done so yet, there's nothing you can do. That's all.
=草堂春夢裡, 天地復唐虞.=是又何夢?

						裵相哲
刦運未脫의 宋鎭禹
시럽슨 놈 과학문명이 발달된 이때 더욱 1930의 今日 이게 무슨 수작이여. 觀相法이니 骨相學이니란...하며 우슬것이다. 이 글을 채 보기도 전에 일반은 우슬 것이다 만은, 實은 알고만 보면 반할ㅅ것이다. 허나 長論코저 안는다. 이 95,6度를 쑥 올나 갓다 내려 갓다 하는 더위에 땀을 철철 흘니며 반할 이유를 長論的으로(博士가 쓴다면 博士는 狹士의 반대로 어든 博士요)대체 두 가지로 무모함이 나타나기 때문이다. 一日 땀 빼고 쓰자면 박사 衛生에 損(막걸니나 잇스면 혹 모르지만) 二日 박사가 땀 뺄제야 독자는 張飛-ㄴ가(乞赦 險口之罪)땀 날ㅅ가 무서워 全文을 죄다 못볼 터이닛가 박사의 博學을 博聞식히지 못함이 損해서 짤막짤막 主義(에크머니)를 취하려는 것이다. 이만만 해도 길긴 길지만 허나 자유가 만은 세상에 쓰기도 자유 읽기도 자유닛가. 요만한 것은 다시 문제 외로 하자! 그 소리가 듯기 실타고 뿌리댄다면 안듯는 꼴이 보기 실타고 노리고 볼 수 잇기 때문에 말이다. 여러 말 할 것 업시 骨相評室로 드러가자!
미신이라고 우스시겟지오. 저도 전문학교 낫이나 단여서 과학도 꽤 안담니다 만은, 과학 이상의 과학이기 때문에...거짓말 참말로 혼합된 박사의 말에 古下(號) 宋社長 어른(非婟賊之丈)ㅅ께서는＜100＞ 徹笑를 띄운 낫흐로 『現代式飛行機도 그 組織 된 내용을 모르는 이에게는 미신으로 볼 수 잇지 안슴닛가?』 올아, 이만 하시면 박사의 開口를 용납해 주신다는 한 단서여 해서, 박사는 대번 신명이 낫것다. 허나 그 순간, 전ㅅ날 도라가신 할머니께서 비행기가 공중에 뜬 것을 처음 보시고 「사람이 엇지 그래늬 그것이 요술을 피워 虛神을 잡어 너흔 게지」 하시든 말슴이 뇌리에서 電起함을 깨다럿다.-
그는 그럿타고 하고 宋鎭禹씨의 골상에 나타나는 바를 조금만 그저거려 보자.
인물이냐? 宋鎭禹씨가 骨相學法上으로 보아 人物이냐?

인물이란 과연 한정이 업는 것이겟지만 그의 日月(左右目)과 神氣, 眉, 聲音, 體局을 검진해 본다면 마치 鯉魚가 못 된다면 딸치(鯉魚子)라도 될 것이지 보통의 鮒魚나 송사리류는 훨신 초월한 것이다.(비유한다면 그럿탄 말이다.) 똑바로 말한다면 송사리는 안인 송진우씨이다. 녯날(白虎吸煙時代)만 갓해서 그가 立朝事君케 된다면 崇政大夫 議政府 左贊成 하나는 무난히 할 것이오. 만약 소시랑, 고물개, 몽둥이 가지고 좌충우돌하며 一聲梛子響處에  伏軍이 大起하야 적을 섬멸하고 북을 치며 도라오는 羅末麗初에 태엿던들 推忠定難定社壁上三韓功臣 三重大匡檢校太師礪良縣(礪山의 古號니 氏가 礪山 宋氏-ㄴ가 하고 박사가 추측한 것)開國侯하나는 如探囊中之物할ㅅ번 햇다. 하지만...
=麻姑三桑何處在, 魯連氣像掛東天=
그의 彩霞月角明秀學堂廣大學堂 하나하나 해부적으로 보면 철석가튼 뜻이 百折不屈은 고사하고 千折不屈도 무난 할 것이니 장부의 志槪임을 알 수 잇다.
先生(에크 박사도 남보고 선생이라나...기왕 입밧ㅅ게 냇스니...)의 눈은 鷹目이여 相法에 아조 신용잇고 高官大爵을 막드리 울녀 먹을 격이여. 허나 입이 적어서 기럭이가 南岳衡山(北岳일는지도 모르지!) 鷹宕門을 넘ㅅ다가 鷹網에 얽히는 격이니 밧가운대 (田中 그래도 모른다면 증역) 여러번 가서서 잡초를 뽑을 수가 여간 만치 안쿠려!
五岳四瀆(四瀆 중의 一인 口는 점병이지만) 六府三才三停이 모다 조킨 썩 조흐나 이거 봐요 顴骨에 㥘氣가 여간欠 안이구려. 오목자목한 듯 고하고 불에 듸어 부속부슥한 것도 갓고 陳皮(귤껍덕이) 덜 말엿슬 때의 빗도 가튼 것이 기막힌 㥘氣요 厄殺이니 단단히 정신차려서 만흔 둘까지만 넘기면 그 후는 밧가온대 數가 아조 업서질 것이다.
心德은 대단히 착하고 성품도 상당히 燥한 편이며 고집이 여간하지 안해 보이고 용단이 기맥키며 누구를 한번 거상에 벗게만 보면 어지간해서는 다시 그 눈에 드리기가 어렵ㅅ고 胸中에 太平洋이 드러 안젓스니 汽船煙氣에 가슴 탈ㅅ것도 정한 이치이다.
