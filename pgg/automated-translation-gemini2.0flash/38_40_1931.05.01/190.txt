Our Female Reporter's Record of Exposing Delinquent Gentlemen

On the first day of the Changgyeongwon night cherry blossom viewing, almost at quitting time, I received an order from the editor-in-chief to "go to Changgyeongwon on the first night of the night cherry blossoms." It wasn't just to go and see the sights, but to get some kind of article, but haven't night cherry blossom articles from Changgyeongwon been regurgitated to the point of nausea in newspapers and magazines? So, I couldn't just write a plain flower story, and it didn't seem like there would be anything that special...
It was frustrating, but I couldn't just refuse to go and be rebellious.

Arriving at the entrance of Changgyeongwon around 8 o'clock, the wave of people was already wriggling like a school of fish in a vast sea.
Being a woman, I squeezed through the crowd of men who were deliberately pushing and shoving, and barely managed to get inside.
The flowers weren't in full bloom yet, and the lights weren't that impressive. In my opinion, the Changgyeongwon night cherry blossoms are becoming more tiresome every year. Nevertheless, I pity the citizens of Gyeongseong who consider this the only pleasure ground... paradise... of a spring night and flock to it like this. Moreover, people who came all the way from distant provinces, spending a lot of money... I can't help but laugh at the people who only get to see the fallen flower petals because they encountered rain.

Since I was already there, I went around the grounds once.
Men, women, old people, young people, children, Chinese people, Westerners, Koreans, prostitutes, female students, whores, good-for-nothings, salaried workers, landlords, students, gentlemen, soldiers, policemen, and whatnots in colorful and various clothes... In this way, their number transcended description, and they streamed along in droves.
I distinguished these many people into two kinds of races.
The eating race...
The flirting race (hiyaqasi injong)...
The eating race is the Japanese. All those who occupy a spot at every nook and cranny and eat are Japanese. Yes, even though they are island people, they have enough leisure in their lives to enjoy themselves like this.
The flirting race is the Koreans.
Students who flock together in groups and can't keep their mouths shut when they see a woman...
Mostly, so-called Western-style gentlemen (?) who wander around alone or in pairs, rolling their eyes.
Good-for-nothings wearing caps and baggy pants, roaming around other people's shoe stores, barbershops, and bicycle shops, cursing at passersby...
If my body could gain weight from people's glares and words, I would have been riddled with a hundred thousand holes in just one or two hours of wandering around that night.
What kind of twisted mind makes them insult and flirt with passing women for no reason? What's the fun in that?
Even if they were to flirt, it would be much more interesting if they did it with more humor, more class, and more gentility.
But the flirting they do... "Did you come to sell yourself!" "Ugh, you lewd bitch!"... the vulgar and base way they act is worse than animals. In fact, animals never act so vulgarly and basely when they are with their "mate."

After walking around once, I sat down on a bench under a tree on one side of the wide yard by the pond to rest my legs.
After sitting for a while, a Western-style man who had been pacing back and forth in front of me, glancing sideways, came and sat next to me, saying, "Oh, my legs hurt!" Another of his cronies followed and sat down too. One was tall and the other was short and chubby, and strangely, I found the appearance of bulldogs on both of their faces. Their pouting lips, their upturned noses, their puckered faces—how could they look so much like bulldogs!
I guessed why they had come to sit next to me.
"What time does the night cherry blossom viewing end?" I asked.
I don't know how fortunate this question must have sounded to them. They were trying to pick me up, and here I was, a woman, making the first move!
So, the short man in the middle chimed in eagerly,
"Well, I'm not sure, but maybe until 11 o'clock." He scooted closer to me. As if not to be outdone, the man at the end asked,
"Did you come alone?"
"Yes, I came alone."
I answered like this and watched their reactions. They exchanged a look. I asked again,
"Are you from Seoul?" The two men's eyes grew rounder and rounder at my unexpected behavior, and the short man said, "Well, my hometown is Sincheon in Hwanghae Province, but I live in Seoul now." And the tall man said politely, "I'm originally from Seoul."
A little later, the tall man ran off to the store and bought two mandarin oranges (dear readers, please bear with me even if you burst out laughing). He came running back, panting, and offered me one.
I refused, but now the short man asked,
"What school do you go to?" I quickly replied,
"I go to the X department of XXXX school."
"Really!? My younger sibling goes to that school too... What's your name?"
"Kim Sun-ok." I made up a name on the spot and then asked, "You said your younger sibling goes there, who is it?" Then the man smiled sheepishly and said,
"Well, they quit now." He mumbled.
"Oh... really? By the way, excuse me, but what's your name?" I asked again.
The tall man quickly handed me a business card that said Shin XX-su (申X洙), and the short man honestly gave his name as Choe XX-uk (崔X旭).
I took the business card and chuckled inwardly.
"I'll excuse myself now," I said and stood up.
The two men stared blankly at my face and then at each other, as if struck by lightning, and then stood up as well. And then they followed me side by side—like two faithful dogs.
I went to the entertainment hall to see the entertainment, and they still followed and stood behind me side by side. When I went outside, they still followed.
When I got on the streetcar, they scrambled to follow and got on, pretending not to notice me. The way they pretended not to notice me...
Another episode happened inside the streetcar.
A man who had met me once at a friend's house and again on the street, and who kept saying he would come to my boarding house, recognized me and greeted me happily. When I tried to punch my ticket, he said,
"Oh, why are you doing this? I'll punch it for you." And he punched two tickets.
Why are the men of the world so affectionate and oblivious to the value of money! The streetcar was going straight to Seodaemun. I got off the streetcar at Jongno intersection.
The two men frantically got their tickets from the ticket booth and followed me onto the Anguk-dong streetcar.
I thought of dragging them around all night, but I figured this was enough material for an article, so I headed for my boarding house in Gwanhun-dong. The two men frantically followed me, and as I was about to enter my house, the short man called out, "Mr. Kim!"
"Yes, what is it?" I turned around as the gate was being opened. The tall man was pacing around at the corner of the street.
"Well... I'm sorry, but... how about we go to a Chinese restaurant for a bite?"
"Thank you, but it's late..."
"But just for a little while."
"There's no need to do that..."
"But just for a moment..."
At this moment, the gate opened. I said, "Excuse me," and went inside. <31>
