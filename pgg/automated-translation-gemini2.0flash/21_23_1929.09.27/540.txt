매니큐-어·껄 (MANIQURE GIRL) (Manicure Girl)

희곡·시나리오 (Play/Scenario)

Manicure Girl
By P. Wilde (U.S.)

Manicure Girl

Detective
Hotel Room

A dignified middle-aged man is sitting in an armchair. He looks very rich, dressed in new clothes made of fine fabric. A thick Luzon cigar is in his hand.
A pretty manicure girl is diligently filing the man's nails at a table set between them.

Man (after a while) Does the cigarette smoke bother you?
Manicurist Not at all! Good tobacco has a good smoky flavor. Some customers smoke Five Centers, but I really hate that kind of cigarette smoke.
Man Do customers who smoke such cheap cigarettes come to this kind of hotel? I thought they would at least smoke Havana cigars...
Manicurist Not really. Most customers don't buy cigarettes and add that money to their hotel bill.
Man It's not that... isn't this hotel expensive?
Manicurist Oh my, how can you say that, sir? You are said to be worth 5 million won!
Man But I still don't know how much my fortune is. It hasn't been long since I became rich. Until about half a year ago, the only things I owned were a toothbrush and a bill collector.
Manicurist Should I put rouge on your nails?
Man Well! As you wish! (Suddenly remembering) Hmm! Since I was young, my goal was a gold mine, but even after discovering a copper mine, I couldn't get copper, and I failed at gold mining, and silver mining also went wrong. All I had then was hunger, solitude, and cold. I slept in the open air with ferocious poisonous snakes and wildcats as my companions! At that time, I was disappointed in everything, thirsty, and even thought about dying! <159>
Manicurist I heard that you finally made a fortune. People say you have 5 million won in cash savings?
Man Yes, that's right! With oil...
Manicurist People's fortunes are like that.
Man What? Fortune? Suffering to death in the mountains for 30 years - after seeking for 30 years - do you call that fortune? (Extending his right hand) Look at this! Is this the hand of a lucky man?
Manicurist (Holding his hand tightly) Then it would be good to get married now. I know a woman who can make you helpless in a minute.
Man What nonsense are you talking about?
Manicurist ! (Laughing) Ruby! My name is Ruby.
Man Oh! Ruby! That's a good name. But I already have a wife and two young children.
Manicurist ! (Disappointed) Yes?
Man My wife is separated from me and is well-regarded in society, but I made her suffer for 30 years, so I can't say anything even if she gets along with other people!
Manicurist (Suspiciously) But she must be old by now.
Man (Dreaming) She always looks only twenty years old to me... It's no wonder she's involved with other men.
Manicurist Still, you must be upset inside.
Man What upset? My right arm would fall off before I would criticize my wife.
Manicurist Oh, I've finished filing your nails.
Man (Taking out a bill from a thick wad of bills) Here, 5 dollars, you don't have to give me change. (The woman doesn't receive it.) It doesn't matter, just take it.
Manicurist I don't take such things.
Man What? Is 5 dollars not enough?
Manicurist That's right.
Man (Taking out the wad of bills) Then how much should I give you?
Manicurist 100,000 dollars.
Man What? Say it again?
Manicurist 100,000 dollars in cash.
Man Why are you joking like this!
Manicurist If I joke, will you laugh? Did I tell you while laughing?
Man All right. That's right. But 100,000 dollars for filing nails! That's a bit expensive.
Manicurist Still, isn't it cheaper than losing an arm because of your wife?
Man What are you talking about?
Manicurist Shall I tell you? We've been alone together with the door closed for over half an hour... It's already against hotel rules.
Man I don't know what the rules are.
Manicurist (Locking the door) Moreover, locking the door - even now.
Man So what are you going to do.
Manicurist And I'm going to attack you!
Man You crazy woman?
Manicurist But if I say that, who wouldn't believe it?
Man But no matter what, it doesn't seem like I attacked you.
Manicurist (Suddenly tearing her clothes) Here. How about now? Can you still say no? (A pause) Come on! It's better for you to hand over the money! Otherwise, shall I tell your wife? How funny! Being caught by the police in a hotel room and going to court to be tried for insulting another man's girl, and even going to jail, the newspapers will make a fuss and the world will hear about it! Well, your wife has been with you for 30 years, so she will trust you. But - if she sees these scandalous details in the newspaper as I described them, and if she finds out that you were caught by the police, will she still believe you then? Hahaha. What woman would live with a man who does such despicable things as her husband? (Threateningly) Give me the money now!
Man Are you finally going to make fun of me?
Manicurist Why would I make fun of you?
Man All right. Do as you please. I won't give you a penny... Do you think I'll fall for an underdog like you! Hahaha. (He slowly raises his cigar and puffs smoke.)
Manicurist What? Humph. Underdog! You cursed me well! You won't be able to stand it without giving me money, so watch. (Shouting) Help me - Help me - (Tearing her clothes to shreds) Help me! Oh, help me - (Overturning the table and scattering the manicure tools.)
Man (Sitting still) Shout louder.
Manicurist Help me. Oh, help me!
Detective (Knocking on the door) Open the door - open it quickly.
Manicurist Help me!
Detective Open the door quickly.
Manicurist This guy locked the door. (The woman goes in front of the man, waving the key) You despicable bastard! Are you not going to give it to me? You won't give me the key. Ah, oh, this. (Eventually, she puts the key in the hole and turns it.)
Detective (Kicking down the door and running in) What's going on? Huh? (The customers in the other rooms peek in.)
Manicurist (Madly) This guy called me to file his nails, and then he locked the door and forced me -
Detective Huh? This is not an easy situation!
Manicurist You despicable bastard! Come on - what are you going to do now?
Detective (Coming to the man's side) I'm sorry, but let's go to the police station together.
Man (Sitting in the chair, not moving) Wait a moment. What does this woman say I did to her?
Manicurist Hey! You despicable bastard. You shouldn't try to take advantage of others....
Man I have been sitting still for half an hour. (Holding up his cigarette hand and showing it to everyone) Look. The cigarette is lit. And look at the ash, which is two inches long. This ash will fall off if it moves even a little. (Curtain) <162>

매니큐-어 손톱에 연지칠을 해드릴가요?

매니큐-어 사람의 운수란 그래요.
男 머? 운수요? 山에서만 30년동안을 죽을 고생을 하고 - 求한지 30년만에 - 그것도 운수라고 할까? (오른손을 내밀며) 좀 보아! 이게 운수 조흔 놈의 손일가?

매니큐-어 ! (우스며) 루비-! 제 일홈이 루비-람니다.
男 응! 루비! 조흔 일홈이로군. 그러나 나는 벌서 안해가 잇고 어린애가 둘식 잇는데-

매니큐-어 (의심스러운 듯) 그러나 벌서 나희가 만흐섯을 걸요.
男 (꿈을 꾸는 듯키) 그는 나에게는 언제든지 스므살 밧게 안되여 보히닛가.... 머 다른 사내들과 이러쿵저러쿵하는 것도 무리가 안이지.
매니큐-어 그래도 녕감께서는 속으로는 강짜가 나실 걸요.

매니큐-어 그럼요.
男 (지전 뭉치를 꺼내 들고) 그럼 얼마나 줄가.

매니큐-어 一時拂로 10만弗.
男 이거 왜 시럽시 이러노!
