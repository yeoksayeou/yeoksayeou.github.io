Statistics of Various Fields and Institutions in Gyeongseong
						Editorial Department

Gyeongseong, being the capital of Joseon, is where all cultures, both domestic and foreign, are concentrated. Today's Gyeongseong is not the Gyeongseong of 20 years ago, and this year's Gyeongseong is not last year's Gyeongseong; next year's Gyeongseong will also be quite different from this year's. It would be interesting to know the various statistics of Gyeongseong, which is developing year after year, even if by the hands of others, and it would also be helpful to know what the 320,000 people in 72,000 households in the current administrative districts of 101 towns (町) and 85 villages (洞) do, what they eat, and how they move around. Let's examine this total population by internal and external nationality. (The statistics cited here are mostly from a survey conducted by the Gyeongseong Prefecture (京城府) as of the end of September of Showa 3 (1928), but the population as of the end of June of Showa 4 (1929) is 321,843.)

Transportation

Next, how do these 300,000 residents move around?
Looking at the number of people getting on and off at Gyeongseong Station (京城驛) every day throughout the year, an average of over 1.5 million people leave Gyeongseong and over 1.3 million people arrive. (The above statistics are mostly as of the end of Showa 3 (1928).) There are over 120 streetcars (電車) and over 660 employees (從務員), and excluding government and private cars, there are only 66 cars for Korean businesses and 149 cars for Japanese businesses, totaling 250 cars, that operate for hire in the city, with a total of about 250 drivers. In addition, there are 44 local buses (地方乘合自動車) operated by Koreans and 26 operated by Japanese, totaling 70. The municipal city buses (府營시내승합자동차(뻐스)) have 40 vehicles, with 32 drivers and 48 pretty female bus conductors (여차장) who call out for "step on right". Besides these, there are 92 motorcycles (自動自轉車), 12,832 bicycles <138>, 1,184 rickshaws (人力車), 3 horse-drawn carriages (乘合馬車), 5,411 carts (荷車), and 599 ox-drawn carts (荷牛馬車). (The above is as of June, Showa 4 (1929).)
These many transportation means carry over 320,000 residents from dawn till midnight. Then, what are these residents doing so busily? Do they all have jobs? How many are seeking jobs, and how many are losing jobs? First, let's look at the statistics of the job placement office (직업소개소).

In the days when they didn't know streetcars and cars, they ate well and dressed warmly. But why are there so many people starving to death today, enjoying the benefits of civilization? The civilization of Seoul is the civilization of the Zitao (紫桃) plum. Even if the inside is eaten by worms, only the outside is shiny. Leave the starving people to starve and just watch the outside, whether the inside is rotten or not. What do the people who struggle and get a job do for a living? Let's look at the general facilities of the economy.

Companies, Factories

There are 24 agricultural companies, 211 commercial companies, 54 industrial companies, 4 mining companies, 1 fishery company, 10 banks, 8 financial associations (금융조합), 18 transportation companies, 1 company for electricity and gas (전기及瓦斯), and 83 other companies, totaling 542. The total capital of these factories is 28,755,000 won, and the total number of workers working in these factories is 12,285 men and 3,771 women, totaling 16,056, moving like machines inside the factories.

General Sanitation

First, there are 4 national hospitals, 1 public hospital, and 195 private hospitals, totaling 200 hospitals and clinics (病醫院). There are 43 Korean doctors and 74 Japanese doctors working for the government, and 97 Korean doctors, 98 Japanese doctors, and 18 foreign doctors in private practice, maintaining the health of the residents. Next, looking at the amount of water supplied by the waterworks (水道), which cannot be stopped even for a moment, the total amount is 6,776,015 cubic meters, with a daily maximum of 23,147 cubic meters and a daily minimum of 11,961 cubic meters, and the daily water supply capacity is 20,300 cubic meters. If this water supply is cut off once, all the residents will have no way to get water. This water supply holds the lives of the residents. People who do not receive the benefits of this water supply or are negligent in hygiene are likely to contract infectious diseases. Other Miscellaneous Items

Next, let's look at the scale of disposal of the countless unsanitary materials (不潔物) in the prefecture, which is a bit off-topic.
There are 68 public restrooms (共同便所), and the amount of unsanitary materials carried out is 21,092,065 geun (貫) of garbage (鹿芥) and 472,324 seok (石) of excrement (屎尿), and the number of laborers (人夫) working to carry this out every day (cumulative total) is 137,183. Although they say they clean everything evenly, I haven't seen a single clean public restroom, which may be the fault of the authorities, but it is also the fault of the residents. No matter how splendid a city is in any country, if it cannot keep even one public restroom clean, you can already tell the level of its citizens. Even though people can live a little longer, they lose their precious lives by neglecting hygiene <141>. Then, the cemeteries (묘지) that take the corpses are managed by the prefecture:
Korean cemeteries: 4 locations, 401,280 pyeong (坪)
Japanese cemeteries: 2 locations, 151,254 pyeong
In addition, there are two crematories (火葬場), and 3,257 corpses were cremated during Showa 2 (1927).

The life of urban dwellers (都市民) is born, eats, works, gets sick, and dies in the midst of the ten thousand zhang (丈) of red dust (紅塵). They toil all day long and return to their homes, which they call havens of rest. However, their numbed nerves cannot find solace in the monotonous home alone. Their heads are very tired. So they seek comfort. They rather feel the loneliness of the heart. So they seek pleasure. With the above figures, you cannot know our lives, but only the lives of the entire Gyeongseong, where the economic power of foreigners occupies most of it. And then, there is something like this to know our inner lives more than this.
Pawnshops (典當舖): You can know the lifestyle of the neighborhood by looking at the items that come into the pawnshops. If you go to the pawnshops in the lower district and upper district (아래대와 우대의 전당포에), there are many clothes, and if you look around the central district (중부 근방), there are many gold and silver ornaments. If you see someone bringing in ragged clothes or even blood-stained underwear to get money, you can know their lifestyle without even following them, and you can know what kind of life the person who brings in gold and silver ornaments to get money lives. Then, if you look at the amount of money going in and out of the 151 pawnshops currently managed by Koreans in Seoul, the figures will vary depending on the location, but if you assume an average of 1,000 won per month per house, it will not be wrong. Then, if one house averages 12,000 won per year, the average total of the 151 houses in the prefecture is a large amount of over 1.8 million won circulating in the pawnshops in one year. However, this is only the money going out from Korean pawnshops. Now, if you look at the Japanese ones, there are about 80 pawnshops scattered throughout the prefecture that are concerned about Korean finances, and if you average this, one place lends 24,000 won per year, and the total of 80 places is about 1.9 million won. The items they bring in to get money are mostly clothes, and then gold and silver, and the pawnshops with lots of gold and silver ornaments are said to be in Korean residential areas. Now, although I do not compare the lives of these two groups of people based on this, you can know how much better their lives are than ours.
I wonder if you know the contents of Gyeongseong's livelihood with this much, so finally, I have compiled a total number of criminals in Showa 3 (1928) in this busy place by thoroughly searching the five police stations in the prefecture. I hope you will appreciate the hard work. However, it would be too troublesome for the readers to write down even the detailed things, so I will only write down the main crimes very simply so as not to harm the reader's eyesight.
In addition to this, I am sorry that there are some more important things missing as it seems to bother the reader's head too much to write down even the detailed things. <143>

典當舖 典當舖에 드러오는 물건을 보면 그 근방 그 동리의 생활상을 알 수가 잇다. 아래대와 우대의 전당포에를 가보면 의복류가 만코 중부 근방을 보면 금은패물이 만타, 헌털 벵이나 심하면 피무든 속옷을 가지고 와서 돈을 어더 가는 사람을 보면 따라가 보지 안이하여도 그 사람의 살님사리를 알 수가 잇고 금은이나 패물을 잡히고 돈을 어더가는 사람은 엇더한 생활을 하는 것을 알 수가 잇는 것이다. 그러면 서울 안에 현재 朝鮮人 경영만 151개處의 전당포에 들고 나는 액수를 엿보면 그 소재지에 따라서 수자가 不一할 것이로되 한 집의 평균 한달에 千圓을 잡으면 틀님업다. 그러면 한 집에서 1년 평균 1만2천圓이라면 府內 151개處의 평균합계를 보면 1년간에 전당포의 덕으로 돈의 융통되는 것이 180여만圓이나 되는 큰 액수이다. 그러나 이것은 朝鮮 사람의 전당포에서만 나아가는 돈이다. 이제 日本 사람의 그것을 보면 府內에 散在하야 朝鮮 사람의 금융까지 염려하야 주는 전당포를 합하면 80여 곳이라 하며 이것을 평균을 치면 한 곳에서 1년에 2만4천圓의 대출을 하고 80개處의 총계를 보면 190여만圓이나 된다. 이들이 들고 가서 돈을 어더 쓰는 물건은 대개가 의류요 그 다음이 금은이요 금은이나 패물이 만키는 朝鮮 사람 사는 곳에 잇는 전당포이라 한다. 이제 이것으로 미루어 보아 이들의 양쪽 사람의 생활을 견주어 생각하지는 안으나 저들의 생활이 우리네의 생활보다 얼마나 나은 것을 알 수가 잇다.
이만해도 京城의 살님사리의 내용을 알엇슬가 하니 끄트로 이 복작복작어리는 속에서 昭和3년 중에 얼마나 한 범죄자가 잇섯는가를 府內 다섯 경찰서를 달달 두저서 꿈여 노은 총계이니 애쓴 보람이나 잇게 보아 주엇스면 한다. 그러나 그 역시 자세하게 하랴면 머리털개나 시여야 할 터이고 독자의 안력이 상할가 하여 극히 간단하게 주요한 범죄만 적어 보앗다.
이 외에 세세한 것까지 기재하기에는 너머나 독자의 머리를 식그럽게 할 듯하야 이만 끗을 막으나 좀더 중요한 것이 빠진 것이 업지 안은 것을 미안해한다.<143>
