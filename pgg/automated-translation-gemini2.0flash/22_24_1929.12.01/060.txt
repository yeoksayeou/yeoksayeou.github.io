Record of a Visit to Hangul Master Kim Tu-bong, Record of Visits to Prominent Figures Abroad
						Lee Yun-jae
I arrived at the Shanghai pier at 1 PM on August 8th. Taking a carriage, I entered the French Concession, passed through Xiafei Road, which is like Jongno in Seoul, and turned onto Avenue MacSear蒂Elo to find the Wonchang Company (元昌公司). I was glad to meet Mr. Jo, the owner, and after exchanging greetings, I decided to find Mr. Kim Tu-bong with him.

Going a little to the west from there, we reached Wangzhi Road (望志路), the village where Mr. Kim Tu-bong's house was located. As we approached the house, he seemed to know I was coming and came out to the street to greet me with a warm handshake. The family lived in a room on the second floor of a Western-style house. As we could guess, most Koreans who have fallen into exile overseas do not live comfortably, but Mr. Kim, being simply a scholar, struggles with poverty, finding love and comfort only with his wife and five-year-old daughter, spending his days as if in a flash of lightning for ten years.

Mr. Kim had just returned from a trip to Changsha (長沙), Hunan Province, a few days ago, and he seemed to be still suffering from the summer journey, with a tired look on his face. Returning to the room, he offered me a seat, smiled, and said to me:
"I have heard through hearsay and newspapers that you are working hard in many ways."

"Not at all. Hearing you say that, I feel ashamed for not having done anything."
Starting with these words of greeting, he asked:
"Did you have any particular difficulties coming this time?"

"No, I had no difficulties at all. The sea was extremely calm, and the diligent protection of the police at each border..."
"Yes, I suppose so. They look at Shanghai with unnecessary strangeness."

"Kwon (Tŏk-kyu), Ch'oe (Hyŏn-bae), Shin (Myŏng-gyun), Chang (Chi-yŏng)... all the brothers are well."
"Yes, they are all well. And they are working very hard for 'Hangul'."

"Yes, I've heard a lot about it. I am very grateful."
Mr. Jo, who had come with me, said he was busy and left for home. The two of us sat facing each other and continued the conversation.

"You must have suffered a lot, having been overseas for so many years."
"What do you mean? Those in the homeland are the ones suffering. I live freely here, so what is there to suffer?"

"What do you do to pass the time now?"
"I have been in charge of Inseong School (仁成學校, a Korean elementary school in Shanghai) for several years. I have the title of principal..."
"That must be fun. How many staff members do you have?" <13>

"There are five or six male and female teachers."
"How many students?"
"About a hundred."
"Is the school's maintenance secure?"

"What do you mean? We are doing it with the weak strength of our expatriates, so how can maintenance be secure?"
"Our expatriates' strength is not small."
"That's right. The fact that the school has grown this much in the 10 years since its founding is entirely due to the strength of the expatriates. And there are about a thousand Koreans residing in Shanghai. There are hundreds of children alone, and if we send them to Chinese elementary schools, they will receive Chinese education and forget their mother tongue and only speak Chinese. How can they fulfill their role as Koreans? For this reason, we cannot but put more effort into the school. But look at this. Can such a thing happen?"
He took out a printed sheet from the desk. It was a statement written in the name of the school's board of directors. I took it and read it.

"Ha, there are those who oppose the school."
"Yes, a few days ago, communist people circulated a statement denouncing our Inseong School (仁成學校聲討文). This is a statement to clarify that."
"Ah, is that so? Why would they denounce the school? That's strange."

"Well, they said the reason was that we did not lend the school's classrooms for their meetings, but as stated in this statement, it is not appropriate to use a sacred educational place for general meetings, and it is unbearable to cultivate such bad habits of political struggle in the minds of innocent children. Also, if we arbitrarily do things that violate the peace of the concession while being based in someone else's concession, and attract the attention of foreign officials, it may cause disadvantage to the school, so we did not allow them to hold meetings. But they are denouncing us, which is truly frustrating."
"If they see this statement, they will have some idea. There must be many other difficulties as well. But how much basic capital do you have?"

"How can we have basic capital? Only the monthly donations from expatriates and the monthly tuition fees from students are the basic assets. We must somehow secure some basic capital. So, this time, with the opportunity of the Korean Magic Troupe coming here, we rented the clubhouse of a French person under the auspices of Inseong School and held a one-night variety show, but due to the high costs, the income was far from enough, resulting in a large deficit. Misfortune always strikes those who are already down, haha." <14>
"Then the maintenance of the school depends entirely on your efforts."

"Not at all, I am going to resign as principal. I lack sincerity and have other things to do."
"Then the school work is in trouble. If someone like you cuts off relations with the school."
"No. If someone more capable takes over, the school will do better. And even if I resign from the principal's job, I will still help indirectly."

"Then what are you managing?"
"It's hard to say yet. And I am going to try to publish the Korean dictionary (朝鮮語辭典) that I have been working on for 5 or 6 years."
"A dictionary? I saw your letter and knew that you have been putting infinite effort into compiling the dictionary (辭典編纂). How is it going?"

"Would you like to see a rough draft? Come this way."
He went up to the dusty attic. He pulled out a few "card" pages full of dust and showed them to me, saying,
"Look at this. It's like this for now."
"Is everything organized?"

"If everything was organized, what worries would I have? My life is not secure, so where can I put this down and work on it? So far, I have only focused on vocabulary collection (語彙蒐集) and explanation."
"How much more do you need to do to finish the organization?"

"If I work for just one more year, the organization of this will be roughly complete. And if I add more ancient words (古語), dialects (方言), and new words (新語)... that are still lacking here, it might become a dictionary."
"Where will you publish this when it is all drafted (脫稿)?"

"I will entrust it to anyone who says they will publish it. I will waive any reward or manuscript fee and just let the book be published."
"That is very kind of you. Even though you are putting in so much effort, you say such things..."

"I heard that they are compiling a dictionary somewhere in the homeland, but how is that going?"
"The Kyemyŏng Club (啓明俱樂部) is doing it."
"What kind of place is the Kyemyŏng Club (啓明俱樂部)?"

"Like its name, it is a club. It is a club for the wealthy class, focusing on self-cultivation and entertainment."
"How did they start compiling a dictionary there?"
"Some enthusiastic people started it."
"Who is writing it?"

"Initially, Ch'oe Nam-sŏn, Chŏng In-bo, Im Kyu, Pyŏn Yŏng-ro, Yang Kŏn-shik, Han Ch'ŭng, and I, a total of seven people, were doing it."
"Ah, were you doing it too?"
"Yes, I have been doing it until now."
"Have all seven of you been doing it all along?" <15>

"We started compiling it in June of the year before last. Within a few months, one person after another dropped out, and now only Im Kyu and Han Ch'ŭng remain."
"Then you also quit?"
"Yes, I quit a few days ago."

"How much has been done?"
"You could say a lot has been done, but we only collected vocabulary and did not organize it, and we did not even touch the many Chinese idioms (漢文熟語) and academic terms (學藝語), so it will still take a considerable amount of time to become a dictionary. Even so, based on the current situation, it seems that we will continue for another month or two at most and then stop."
"Ha, it is truly lamentable that our work is always so unfinished."

"How did you decide on the grammar (文法) to use in the dictionary?"
"Yes, it is difficult to talk about grammar (文法) simply. I have been pondering this for decades. ..." (Here, there were several hours of questions and answers, but since it is a grammatical theory, I will publish it separately and omit it here - original text)
For the next two weeks, while staying in Shanghai, I exchanged many opinions on the Korean dictionary (朝鮮語辭典) issue. I hope for Mr. Kim's health and the completion of his work. <16>
