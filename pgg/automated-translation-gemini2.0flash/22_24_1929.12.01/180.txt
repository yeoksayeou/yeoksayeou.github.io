잡지명	별건곤 제24호
발행일	1929년 12월 01일
기사제목	五錢한푼 銅錢서푼, 年末賞與金타령 (Five jeon one pun, three pun in copper coins, Year-end bonus lament)
필자	壬寅生
기사형태	회고·수기 (Recollections/Memoir)

30 percent, which I believed in like I believe in Jesus, turned out to be only 18 percent... I'm so dumbfounded that I can't even speak.
18 percent of 52 won is 9 won 36 jeon. Adding this month's salary, it totals 145 won 60 jeon.
After deducting the 25 won of provisional payment, 10 won left over from the month before last and 15 won from last month, I'm only left with 120 won and 60 jeon.

Holding the salary envelope containing 120 won and 60 jeon, I sat down and racked my brain, wondering how to pay off the pile of debts as high as a mountain. A slip of paper suddenly appeared on the table. <40> 9 won 15 jeon to the sugar shop. I only paid half last month, so that's probably right.
I can't bear to not pay this out of embarrassment. When I hand over a 10 won bill and receive 85 jeon in change,
I hear, "Mr. Kim, business is good."
It's a Chinese tailor.
I can put up with talking about rice cakes in front of a ghost, but I can't bear to show money to a Chinese person and not pay. Moreover, I'm four months behind on the monthly installment, so I have to pay at least two months' worth. 30 won.
"Sanjū en komaru komaru (Thirty yen, I'm in trouble, I'm in trouble)."
"I'm only collecting it because you came early."
"Demo watashi... Ne, Kim-jusa, mō 10 en...(But I...Hey Kim-jusa, another 10 yen...)"
"Urusai na! (Shut up!)"
"Urusai nai, ne, Kim-jusa. Sorechiya ato wa itsu? (Don't be noisy, hey Kim-jusa. When is the rest then?)"
"Tsugi no nenmatsu sa (Next year-end)."
"Jōdan otssharu na... Raigetsu ne (Don't joke... Next month, right?)"
"Rainen da yo (Next year)."
"Hihi, Kim Jusa is a good person."

Having been deceived many times when fighting with the landlord, I didn't wait at the * but went directly to the office. <41>
I can get away with only paying two months' rent out of the three months' worth, which is 36 won, but I haven't paid for rice for several months, so if I pay less, I won't be able to get credit next time. I have to pay all 42 won and 76 jeon.
After paying the rent and rice, the envelope feels empty.
Let's see, how much is left? 10 won and some change!

No matter how I try, it won't work. I have to go to the Accounting Department and make a scene.
The slick Accounting Manager doesn't listen to me.
"Just give me 30 won."
"30 won? You have 120 won in your hand, and you're asking for another provisional payment?"
"I only have 10 won left."
"What will you use it for?"
"I don't know... Just give me 30 won more."
"I can't. If it were up to me, I'd give you as much as you want, but..."
"Please, save a person."
"Take 10 won."
"10 won isn't enough."<42>
"I can't give you any more."
"Just 20 won."
"I can't... There's no money."

I barely managed to get 10 won, so now I have 20 won.
After giving 13 won to the man from the lumberyard, I have about 7 or 8 won left.
There are still so many things to pay for... My child's tuition is three months overdue, which is 1 won 50 jeon. I pawned my wife's winter clothes, promising to redeem them by the end of the year and even make her a new jeogori (저고리, Korean traditional jacket) as a bonus. The electricity bill is over 2 won, and there's the hygiene fee, night watch fee, and oh, the year-end party fee. My shoes are worn out, and I need to buy a new dress shirt. Tonight, if all else fails, I have to go to the Brothers Chueotang (추어탕, Loach Soup) restaurant and have a drink.
Even 100 won more wouldn't be enough.

Mr. P is walking ahead with slumped shoulders.
"How much do you have left?"
Mr. P takes out his salary envelope from his pocket and crumples it up.
I grabbed Mr. P and went to a sukiyaki restaurant. After shaking out everything and counting, I had one 5-jeon coin and three copper coins left. <43>
But without any worries, I roamed around, causing trouble all night long.
And somehow, I made it home--with 5 jeon one pun and three copper coins left. <44>
