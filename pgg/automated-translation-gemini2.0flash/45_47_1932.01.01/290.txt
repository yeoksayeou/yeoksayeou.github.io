別乾坤 第四十七號
1932년 01월 01일
反作春香傳 春香이는 정말 美人이엿더냐, 薄色고개의 한 傳說 (An Anti-Chunhyang Chronicle: Was Chunhyang Really a Beauty? A Legend of Baksaek Pass)
風流郞 (Pungnyulang)
문예기타 (Literature, Other)

Chunhyang! Chunhyang! Whether it is said who is the most beautiful and who has the most famous name in Joseon, there is probably no one who knows this Chunhyang as well, whether young or old, learned or unlearned, because her name has been widely spread through novels, songs, or plays. People may not know the name of Yi Ch'ungmu-gong (李忠武公), who revived the dying Joseon people, but there is no one who does not know Chunhyang's name, and people may not know King Sejong (世宗大王), who created Hangul to awaken the ignorance of the Joseon people, but there is also no one who does not know this Chunhyang. The power of beauty is truly great, and the life of art is truly eternal. It is ridiculous to question whether the Chunhyang in the novel actually existed or not. It also seems useless to argue now whether Chunhyang, whom the world has been calling the most beautiful woman and a virtuous woman, was actually beautiful or not. However, the world considers Chunhyang as a real person, and some people even go to Namwon (南原) to investigate historical sites, using the local gazetteer (邑誌) to find Chunhyang's house and Chunhyang's tomb, which is ridiculous. Last summer, the kisaeng (妓生) of Namwon took the opportunity of the county office repairing Gwanghallu (廣寒樓) and built even Chunhyanggak (春香閣), just as the kisaeng of Jinju (晋州) built a shrine for Uigiron Gae (義妓論介), and even held ancestral rites in the spring and autumn. Therefore, it doesn't seem entirely useless to write about whether this Chunhyang was a beauty or not. Whatever the case, everyone in this world, of course, knows Chunhyang only as the most beautiful woman in the world and a loyal woman of all time, just as it is written in the novel. However, in the vicinity of Namwon, a strange legend, which is the complete opposite, has been circulating until now.

According to the legend, Chunhyang was originally not a beauty but the ugliest woman in the world. The era is the same as the era of King Sukjong of the Yi dynasty that appears in the Tale of Chunhyang, and it is also true that she was the daughter of the government kisaeng (官妓) Wolmae. However, her face was the complete opposite of Chunhyang in the Tale of Chunhyang, the ugliest woman of all time. Her nose was like a dysentery pot, her eyes were like a pig's eyes rolling on a hillside, her hair was like a short broom, her hands were like the feet of an itch mite, her neck was like a turtle's neck, and her body was as big as a mortar. Moreover, she had suffered severely from smallpox, so her face was blackened and scarred, like the 곰보 (gombo) in Park Chun-jae's (朴春栽) gombo ballad, like a hail-beaten ash heap <40>, like cow dung after the rainy season, like a cicada's back sitting on a jujube tree, and like a toad's buttocks. Anyone who looked at her once would immediately get rid of a decade-old malaria. Therefore, no matter how old she was, over 20 and approaching 30, no one would even look at her, and having an affair was out of the question. However, human instinct cannot be helped. One day, while doing laundry in the 으천강 (Ŭch'ŏn-gang) in front of Gwanghallu (廣寒樓), she happened to see the son of the local magistrate (使道), Sŏwŏr Yi Doryeong, who had come to Gwanghallu to play. She was seduced by his handsome appearance and lost her mind. She returned home and refused to eat or sleep, covering her head and lying down. As the saying goes, a disease caused by not seeing one's beloved cannot be cured until one sees them. Chunhyang's illness, caused by being charmed by Yi Doryeong, could not be cured by any medicine. Several days passed, and she used all kinds of medicine, but there was no improvement, and her illness gradually worsened. Whether she was handsome or not, the love of a mother was no different from others. Chunhyang's mother was always worried, and finally, she asked Chunhyang about the cause of her illness. Chunhyang, in that situation, confessed the truth without any deceit. However, since Chunhyang was originally ugly, no one else would do anything for her. Moreover, how could she even talk to the son of the local magistrate (사ㅅ도)? However, Chunhyang's mother, who had experienced all sorts of things in the pleasure quarters as a veteran kisaeng (妓生), suddenly came up with a plan. It was none other than to bribe Bangja, who was closest to Yi Doryeong, to lure Yi Doryeong to her house. The method was, of course, the same as in the Tale of Chunhyang. She lured Yi Doryeong to Gwanghallu and had Hyangdan (香丹), one of her pretty and clever maidservants, dress up nicely and swing on a swing near Gwanghallu, pretending to be Wolmae's daughter. After Yi Doryeong came to her house, she had Hyangdan use all her charms and tricks to get Yi Doryeong drunk. She had him sleep in Hyangdan's room, and in the middle of the night, Hyangdan quietly slipped away to another room and had Chunhyang sneak into the room instead. In the midst of drunkenness, people are blind to beauty. Yi Doryeong was drunk and could not distinguish between Hyangdan and Chunhyang in the dark. Moreover, Yi Doryeong, who had been completely captivated by Hyangdan, would have mistaken even a log or a chest for Hyangdan in his drunken state. He had no time to examine her closely. Thus, he spent a sweet dream with Chunhyang for one night. When Yi Doryeong woke up the next morning, he found that Hyangdan, whom he had seen the night before, was gone, and unexpectedly, he was lying next to the ugliest woman he had ever seen in his life. Yi Doryeong was startled and thought to himself, "Is this a ghost or a person? How did I end up sleeping with this woman? Where did Hyangdan, whom I was playing with and sleeping with last night, go?" He muttered to himself, "Strange things happen in the world," and, like someone who had been bewitched by a goblin, he felt dazed and tried to get up and leave.

It was at this moment that Chunhyang's mother, knowing this would happen, was waiting outside the door. She prostrated herself on the ground and said, "Please forgive my deadly sin, sir." After confessing all the details, she added, "Now that things have come to this, if you love Chunhyang forever, I will consider it an unforgettable favor and repay you even after death. But if not, please give me some token of remembrance, as the saying goes, 'Even one night of sleep builds a ten-thousand-mile wall.' I will cherish the keepsake forever as if I were serving you, sir, and wait for a future opportunity." Yi Doryeong was initially angry and resentful that he had been deceived by Bangja and Chunhyang's mother. He remained silent for a while, like someone who had been in a fight. However, upon further reflection, he felt sorry for Chunhyang's situation. Even if she was the ugliest woman in the world, she had become deathly ill because of him and had even resorted to such means to save her life. He could not help but feel sympathy for Bangja and Chunhyang's mother as well. So, he thought to himself, "I cannot live with Chunhyang forever, but what is the harm in giving her a small keepsake, as men often do?" He took out a silk handkerchief <41> from his sleeve and gave it to her as a token, then left abruptly.
Not long after, the magistrate was transferred, and Yi Doryeong also came to Seoul with his father. If Chunhyang had been a beauty and Yi Doryeong had been in love with Chunhyang, there would have been a tragic scene like the farewell scene at Orijeong in the Tale of Chunhyang. However, since the relationship between Yi Doryeong and Chunhyang was not as described above, Yi Doryeong left without saying goodbye to Chunhyang on the day he left. Yi Doryeong left so heartlessly, but Chunhyang, on the other hand, yearned for him with a one-sided love and became deathly ill. Like a bat tasting honey in a dream, she briefly met Yi and even obtained his keepsake. Although she had temporarily fulfilled her lifelong wish, her lingering feelings were even more ardent than before. She longed to meet him again someday. Unexpectedly, she heard that Yi Doryeong had gone to Seoul and was utterly disheartened. She thought to herself, "If I had been born beautiful like others, even if we were separated for a while, he would look for me later, or I could go to Yi myself. But since I was unfortunately born the ugliest woman in the world, Yi will not look for me again. Moreover, since I have forcefully made myself intimate with him, I cannot marry anyone else. It would be better to die cleanly on the day Yi Doryeong leaves." On the very night that Yi Doryeong left, she went to Gwanghallu and hanged herself with the silk handkerchief that Yi Doryeong had given her. Her parents, as well as the villagers, felt sorry for her. They buried her on Imsil Pass (任實고개, Imsil Pass is the road from Namwon to Seoul), where Yi Doryeong had gone. From that time on, people called the pass Baksaek Pass (薄色고개), and that Baksaek Pass is the Bakseok Pass that appears in the Tale of Chunhyang. Later, Yi Doryeong beautified the name and changed Baksaek (薄色, ugly color) to Baekseok (甎石, brick stone).

Chunhyang was not killed by others, but she committed suicide in despair because the Creator had mistakenly assigned her to this world and she could not live a happy life like others. As the saying goes, "The resentment of youth can fill the Eastern Sea, and the body can turn into a Mangbuseok (望夫石, a rock statue of a woman waiting for her husband)." Her pitiful resentment, like a cloud mass rising from Jirisan (智異山), never dispersed. On bright moonlit nights or rainy days, her sorrowful spirit would wail mournfully near Gwanghallu or near Baksaek Pass, where her tomb was located. Even the strongest man would feel his hair stand on end and shivers run down his spine when he heard her wailing. From then on, whenever a new magistrate came to the district, the hooves of the horse he was riding would stick to the ground while crossing Baksaek Pass, and the magistrate would faint and die on the spot at the sight of Chunhyang's hideous appearance. After this happened four or five times, Namwon Prefecture became like a deserted town, and no one wanted to go there as a magistrate. The government was very worried.
As these rumors gradually spread throughout the capital, Yi Doryeong could not help but hear them. Since he was the cause of such unfortunate events, how could he not feel uneasy? He always thought that if he had the chance, he would go there himself and put an end to such things, whether he lived or died, as the saying goes, "The one who tied the knot must untie it" (結者解之). Then, one year, he took the civil service examination and, as in the Tale of Chunhyang, passed with the highest honors (狀元, changwon). When he was appointed as a local official by royal order, Yi Doryeong volunteered to become the magistrate of Namwon. The king knew that the place was not good and tried to dissuade him, wanting to spare him such a fate. However, Yi Mong-ryong (李夢龍) insisted on going, so the king especially admired his courage and sent him off. Yi Doryeong was going there not for the sake of his official position but to appease Chunhyang's vengeful spirit. More than anything else, he intended to compose Chunhyang's biography (whether the current Tale of Chunhyang is exactly that or not, it praised her as the most beautiful woman in the world, the complete opposite of her real appearance) and a memorial address (祭文) with his fluent writing. On his way to his post, he stopped in Jeonju (全州) and summoned Kim Ŏk-sŏk (金億石), who was famous as the best singer in Jeolla Province at the time. He had him practice singing the Tale of Chunhyang (春香傳) that he had composed, setting it to music, before heading to Namwon.
When his entourage reached Baksaek Pass, the hooves of the horses stuck to the ground as before. Yi Doryeong got off his horse and shouted loudly into the sky, "Yi Doryeong, the husband of Chunhyang, the most beautiful woman in the world and a loyal woman of all time, is coming as the magistrate of Namwon. If Chunhyang has a spirit, come before me!" Suddenly, a song of love (사랑가) was heard in the air, and the hooves of the horses were released. Yi Doryeong, still dismounted, slowly walked over Baksaek Pass (it seems that the song Yangsando (梁山道) "slowly walking over Baksaek Pass" (천천이 緩步하야 薄色재를 넘는다는) refers to this scene). He went to Chunhyang's tomb and performed a memorial service (祭祀) with the prepared offerings and memorial address. Then, he had the entertainer (광대) he had brought sing a Chunhyangga (春香歌) song with a strong voice. The mountains echoed the song, and the sound rose to the clouds. Even the crows of Jirisan (智異山) flew over and danced. How could Chunhyang's resentment not be appeased? After Yi Doryeong took office, he changed the name of Baksaek Pass to Bakseok Pass (甎石峴), and he also regretted his past misdeeds and changed the names of Gwanghallu (廣寒樓) to Gwanghallu (狂漢樓, madman's pavilion) and Ojakgyo (烏鵲橋, magpie bridge) to Ojakgyo (誤作橋, mistaken bridge). This may or may not be true, but such riddle-like stories remain among the elders in the vicinity of Namwon to this day. <42>

춘향이! 춘향이 조선에서 누가 제일 잘나고 누가 제일 이름이 만히 낫느니 하야도 이 춘향이 처름 소설로 노래로 또는 연극으로 이름이 넓이 전해 남녀로소 유식무식 할 것업시 누구나 잘 아는 인물은 업슬 것이다. 다 죽게 된 조선 사람을 다시 살게한 리충무공(李忠武公)의 이름은 몰나도 춘향의 이름은 모를 사람이 업고 조선 사람의 무식을 깨워 주기 위하야 한글을 창작한 세종대왕(世宗大王)은 몰나도 이 춘향이는 또한 모를 사람이 업슬 것이다. 미인의 힘이란 참으로 위대하고 예술의 생명이란 정말 장원한 것이다. 소설에 나온 춘향이를 실제에 잇느냐 업느냐 의문을 가지는 것도 우서운 일이요. 또 이때까지 세상 사람이 천하 미인이요 렬녀라고 떠드는 춘향이를 지금와서 그가 미인이엿느냐 아니엿드냐고 하고 론난하는 것도 역시 실업슨 일 갓다. 그러나 세상 사람들은 춘향이를 꼭 실재의 인물로 생각하고 심지어 어떤 사람은 남원(南原) 땅에 가서 고적(古蹟)조사를 하는 때에 음지(邑誌)를 가지고 춘향의 집터를 찻고 춘향의 무덤을 차진 우서운 일도 잇섯고 작년 여름에는 남원의 긔생(妓生)들이 군청에서 광한루(廣寒樓)를 수리하는 긔회를 타서 맛치 진주(晋州)의 긔생들이 의긔론개(義妓論介)의 사당을 지여 놋텃이 춘향각(春香閣)까지 지여 놋코 춘추제향을 지내기 까지 하게 된 바에야 이 춘향이를 미인이엿느냐 하는 말을 쓰는 것도 과히 실업슨 일은 안일 것 갓다. 그것이야 엇지 되엿던 아즉것 이 세상에서는 누구나 물론하고 춘향이는 그 소설에 씨워잇는 것과 맛찬가지로 천하의 미인이요 만고의 렬녀로만 안다 .그러나 남원 부근에는 그와는 정반대 되는 한 이상한 전설이 지금것 떠돌고 잇다.
