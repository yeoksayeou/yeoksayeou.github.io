Remarriage of Divorced Men and Women - A Divorced Couple Meets Again and Remarries Abroad
By Bang In-geun

My friend K had a very thorough love marriage. His partner, S, was a fourth-year student at a girls' high school. K was a second-year student at a vocational school. It's generally the case that if a female student is just a little smart and pretty, she gets "reserved" before graduating high school, and S had been selected by several men in her third year, with the competition being quite fierce, but K emerged victorious. S was a very gentle and affectionate woman, and K was a cheerful and lively man. They got engaged and promised to marry each other. Men with wicked, devilish natures, knowing full well that S was engaged, tried to snatch S from K's hands. K, aware of the high risk, hastily got married. Even if they hadn't, they were both too affectionate and weak-hearted to wait until graduation.

After the marriage, they truly had a sweet home. They continued their happy life, saying, "Flowers, let's laugh together; birds, let's sing together." Some of us friends envied their home greatly and found it fun to visit occasionally. <160>

K graduated from vocational school and started working for a company. As he finally had a regular income, S gradually furnished the house with household items, decorating it. It was decorated so artistically and aesthetically that anyone who came would feel refreshed. While her husband was at work, S enjoyed the pleasures of being alone at home, preparing delicious side dishes with care, cleaning the rooms, the veranda, and the yard, arranging flowers in vases, and spending her days with the sole hope of her husband's return. If life could continue like this, it would be wonderful, but a terrible storm descended upon them.

One day, S, who was waiting for her husband, grew anxious as evening passed and turned into night, and he still hadn't returned. She looked at the dinner table and repeatedly placed the stew on the brazier and took it off. Even after midnight, he didn't come. This was the first time this had happened since they got married, so S was very worried, felt a sense of dread, and became suspicious, sitting alone in the empty house, burning with anxiety. The next day, she called the company, but they said he had left yesterday and hadn't returned yet. S felt as if the sky was falling. She returned home and was crying when K's friend came and unintentionally told her a sad story. <161>

It was that K had been caught with his comrades while participating in a secret society's 00 movement (00운동) and was arrested. S cried and trembled. Afterward, K was sentenced to five years and eight months and began his prison life. S was a lonely person with no parents or relatives. The immediate hardship of life weighed on her more than longing for her husband. The bitter experience of humanity, which she was facing for the first time, was too harsh for her weak self. One by one, her belongings and clothes piled up in the pawnshop, and eventually, she was forced to move into a small rented room due to unpaid rent. She searched for a job, but no one was willing to hire her. S, having run out of tears, only sighed and focused all her energy on making a living day by day. Moreover, the world seemed so desolate and uninteresting. Somehow, a loafer smelled this out and began to use his tactics. That loafer was the son of a wealthy family. S initially paid him no attention and didn't give him any thought, but she eventually succumbed to his persistent advances. S became that loafer's concubine. The society was partly to blame for ruining S in this way, as were we, K's friends. Of course, S's fault is undeniable. Anyway, S lived a luxurious life, albeit with suffering. K was always in her mind and heart, reproaching her. However, that gradually faded, and the current comfortable and interesting life, the brilliant light of gold, gradually blinded her eyes and numbed her. Since things had come to this, she decided to live with this man forever, so she pestered the man, saying she didn't want to be a concubine. The man said he would divorce his wife if she divorced her original husband, so three years after K went to prison, she filed for divorce. As it was legally impossible for someone in prison to refuse a divorce request, K was divorced by S. K felt the urge to break out of the prison bars and kill S, his blood boiling with this desire several times a day. Having lost everything, he eventually wanted to die. But a man shouldn't die because of a woman! Instead, he decided to devote himself completely to society! With this resolve, his sentence was reduced by six months from five years and eight months, and after five years and two months, he emerged into the beloved and hated land and sky of Joseon as a free man. Although he was released, the question was where to spend his first step and first night. He keenly felt a sense of disillusionment that made him want to return to prison. Some friends met him at the prison gate and took him away in a car, which he appreciated but also felt uncomfortable with. S, hearing the news of K's release, felt both fear and joy. And an inexplicable flood of tears poured out, and she hated herself. She felt very sorry for K and realized the enormity of her sin, and at the same time, she wanted to see K and missed him. After thinking about it day and night for several days, she urged the man to go to a foreign country. The man didn't listen and only scolded her. S inquired about the inn where K was staying and went to find him one night. S <162>

As soon as she entered, she fell to her knees before K and just cried. K considered beating her, killing her, but instead, he made up his mind to be even crueler and told her to leave as if nothing had happened. His low voice had a sharp edge like a blade. K, not paying attention and not even responding, turned away, and without realizing it, tears welled up. His heart felt like it was bursting. You ugly bastard! He mentally punched the man and ordered S to leave as sternly as possible. S left. After that, S came every night to beg for forgiveness and then left. But K remained unchanged. Eventually, he moved the inn. She found him again. K wanted to escape to some country, because the more he met S, the more he felt the danger of his heart being drawn to her! You dirty woman! Even so, he couldn't stop missing the old days and feeling affection. This was because the traces of sincere remorse were evident in her swollen eyes from crying and her slightly thinner body. However, when he saw S in her rustling silk clothes, he was filled with thoughts of "concubine! dirty woman!" and felt nauseous. A broken pearl! K felt it was a waste. After that, S was nowhere to be seen. K felt relieved but also curious. Several months later, K accidentally met S in a back alley of Jongno (종로). <163> She was completely different from before, and her clothes were dirty. And her appearance was haggard. K was puzzled and asked. S's reply was that she had abandoned that man, given up her life as a concubine, and was pioneering a new life by earning her own living as a female factory worker. She said very sincerely and solemnly that she had repented of everything from the past, corrected herself, and decided to serve as a member of society. K was also moved by that. However, S didn't even mention living together again as before, and she didn't seem to have any such thoughts, as if she had completely given up. That made K feel even more pitiful and touched. And they parted that day. They didn't even ask each other for addresses, so there was no way to meet again. However, a black shadow following K from afar saw K entering a small house at the end of Sogyeok-dong (소격동) and turned away. Let's save S, let's restore the old life, then it will be more fun. What can't be forgiven because a weak woman did that for a moment? There is my fault and society's fault too – moreover, S regrets everything and has become a new person... K was tormented in his mind by imaginations of hide-and-seek. But K's strange pride and strong personality didn't allow it, and he went to Namdaemun (남대문) train station to leave for another country to do "work." As he passed through Aeo-gae Tunnel (애오개굴) on the northbound train, K's heart felt like it was collapsing, and he wanted to jump out again and run to S. Ah – let's throw everything away! I will penetrate humanity with an iron will and do "work" tomorrow! He buried his head in his arms, squeezed his eyes shut, and tried to forget all thoughts. After some time, he felt someone come and sit next to him. When he turned around, S's smiling face appeared. It wasn't a dream, it was reality.
"What's going on?"
"I'm going to follow you and work together."
"How did you know?"
"What do you mean? Don't you know I've been following you every day without fail!"
Their ice broke.
They went to a foreign country. They had never had a honeymoon after getting married, but they went on a grand honeymoon and went to a foreign country, where they pretended not to know anything from the past and had a new wedding ceremony in front of many people. I heard that rumor several years ago, and after that, I heard that they had a happy family and even had a son, and they both worked there, but for the past few years, there has been no news. This is like a novel but it is a fact, and since they are not in Joseon, I wrote without hesitation, and even if they see this article, I believe they will not blame me too much, and it is a request from "Byeolgeon-gon-sa (別乾坤社)" to write a story of "divorcing and living together again," so please understand that I cannot help but use you as material. However, I couldn't write everything I wanted to write in detail. -I pray that there will be light on your path. And I look forward to meeting you again.- <164>
