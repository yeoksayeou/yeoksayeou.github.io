Romance of National Flowers of the World

Thistle (Scotland)

The thistle signifies authority and independence. In the old days, when the "Danes" invaded the country in the dark of night, one of the enemy soldiers stepped on a thistle and cried out loudly from the thorns. The Scottish army, hearing this, was able to defend against the invasion, thus avoiding a national disaster. The country, considering this a blessing, especially revered the thistle and made it their national flower.

Poppy (India)

The poppy is called "pospi" in India. Ancient and modern Indians alike favor its beautiful blossoms. They especially believe the flower is the god of grain harvest, and the general public reveres it as their national flower, as it signifies comfort.

Water Lily (Egypt)

The water lily signifies simplicity. In Egypt, it is called "ninpaa." "Ninpaa" is the Latin word for goddess, so Egyptians consider the water lily a goddess of the water and continue to use that name. The shape of the flower, like the Korean lotus, is simple and refined, differing only in its smaller size.

Violet (Greece)

The ancestors of Greece are called "Ion." "Ion" was originally the daughter of a king, "huiduenja," who, because she was marrying a beautiful shepherd boy, the god of the wild worried that she would anger "Aporo," so he transformed "Ion" into a violet. So, "Ion" is not an ancestor but a violet. The people of Greece worship Ion as they would an ancestor, and its meaning is humility.

Lily (Italy)

In the ancient heavenly realm, among the gods, there was a cute and sturdy little child named "Haa Gikyuritju," son of "Jjubita," the father of all gods. The mother goddess "Jju-no" treated him with boundless abuse because she was not his birth mother. However, the innocent "Haa Gikyuritju," unaware of this, one day sucked hard on "Jju-no's" breast. At that moment, "Jju-no" felt a surge of affection she had never felt before, and milk poured out of her chest like a waterfall, rising to the sky and becoming the Milky Way, and falling to the ground and becoming the white lily. This flower symbolizes purity, and in Italy and Spain, it is used to decorate altars to the Virgin Mary.

Narcissus (China)

In the old days, during the age of the gods, there was a handsome young man with clear eyes and a refined appearance named "Na-shisseusaseu." A certain girl fell in love with him at first sight, and she suffered endless agony, but the boy was as cold as a stone and did not even look back. One day, "Nashisseusaseu" went to a spring to drink water after hunting mountain goats, and by chance, the girl was also there playing by the spring. The girl, who had always admired him more than anyone else, was happy to see him and tried to strike up a conversation, but when he didn't give a single reply, the girl burned and burned her heart until she fell ill and died. Not long after, a clear and beautiful white flower bloomed by the spring. That was the narcissus. It signifies self-respect.

Cornflower (Germany)

In the old days, Queen Louise of Prussia loved this flower very much, so the German people still generally revere it as their national flower. It is also called "Miido" flower. Queen Louise has been dead for a long time, but the cornflower still blooms every year, so the lament, "Year after year, the flowers resemble each other, but year after year, the people are different," is something that any German person who encounters the flower cannot help but feel.

Iris (France)

The iris became the emblem of the French royal family, so in the old days, ordinary people could not pick the flowers freely, but after the revolution, the general public could pick them as they pleased. The blessings of the revolution extended even to the flowers!

Cherry Blossom (Japan)

According to ancient mythology, a beautiful woman named Konohanasakuya-hime (木花開耶姬), born between a mountain god and a field god, married Ninigi-no-Mikoto (瓊瓊杵尊). The groom's room was filled with clouds and mist, making it impossible to enter freely, so the woman's father had her ride the clouds and mist into the sky and go to the summit of Mount Fuji, where she scattered the seeds of the "sakura." These seeds propagated and spread throughout Japan. The cherry blossom signifies nobility and beauty, and the fact that it blooms and falls at once symbolizes the national character of Japan.

Rose (England)

The rose signifies affection. In the old days, thousands of years ago, a beautiful woman was to be burned at the stake in the city of "Bethlehem." No matter how much brushwood was piled around her and set on fire, it would not burn. The people watching all thought it was strange. It was because the beautiful woman had prayed to God, pleading for her innocence and purity. Not long after, the beautiful woman transformed into a beautiful white rose. Therefore, roses were initially all white, but later, when "Cupid," the god of love, was having a banquet in the sky with all the gods, he accidentally dropped a wine glass, and the wine fell on a white rose, turning it into a yellow rose, according to legend.
And also, when the goddess of love, "Venus," was rushing to save the endangered beautiful boy "Adonis" one day, she tripped and was pricked by the thorns of a rose. Red blood poured from her snow-white legs and sprinkled on the white rose, which is why the white rose turned into a red rose, according to legend.

Persia loves the tulip, America loves the mountain azalea, Ireland loves the shamrock, Spain loves the orange blossom, Peru loves the sunflower, and China, besides the narcissus, has always cherished the peony as its national flower. And in the West, it is customary to present wreaths or bouquets of flowers for auspicious or inauspicious occasions, and they are also widely used as decorations for women. The meanings vary depending on the flower: rose signifies youth, aster signifies friendship, yellow rose signifies affection, lily signifies purity, water lily signifies simplicity, balsam signifies cleanliness, honeysuckle signifies attachment, narcissus signifies foolishness, broom signifies domestic virtue, pomegranate signifies amity, laurel signifies victory, peach blossom signifies affection, marigold signifies anguish, orange blossom signifies chastity, and violet signifies prudence. <47>

世界各國 國花 로맨스
薊(蘇格蘭)

薊는 權威獨立을 의미함이니 녯날에 『데인』人이 暗夜에 同國을 침략할 때에 敵軍중 一人이 薊를 밟고 가시에 찔니여서 크게 소리를 침으로 蘇格蘭軍이 그것을 알고 防禦하야 國家의 禍를 면하얏슴으로 同國이 그것을 德으로 생각하고 특히 敬愛하야 國花를 삼게 되엿다.

罌粟(印度)

罌粟은 印度에서 『뽀쓰삐』라 칭하니 미려한 그 꼿은 古代印度人이나 지금의 印度人이나 다 嗜好한다. 그 꼿은 특히 穀物收穫의 神으로 信하야 일반 국민이 國花로 경애하는데 安慰를 의미함다아.

睡蓮(埃及)

睡蓮은 淡白을 의미함이다. 埃及에서는 此를 『닌파아』라고 칭하니 『닌파아』는 羅典語에 女神인 故로 埃及人은 睡蓮을 水中女神으로 생각하야 그 名辭를 仍用한 것이다. 그 꼿의 形態는 조선의 蓮과 가티 淡하고 淸秀하나 다만 그 形體의 적은 것이 다를 따름이다.

菫菜(希臘)

希臘의 祖先을 『이온』이라 칭한다. 『이온』은 원래 『후이된 자』 국왕의 왕녀로서 牧羊하는 美少年과 결혼하기 때문에 野神은 그가 『아포로』에게 觸怒가 될가 염려하야 『이온』을 菫菜로 幻出하게 하얏다 한다. 그러고 보면 『이온』은 祖先이 안이라 菫菜다. 希臘의 人은 이온을 祖先과 가티 崇拜하니 그 의미는 謙讓의 의미다.

百合(伊太利)

太古天界의 神中에 百神의 父가 되는 『쮸비타』의 아들 『하아기큐리쯔』라 하는 귀엽고도 튼튼한 어린 아기가 잇섯는데 母神 『쮸-노』는 그의 生母가 안인 까닭에 무한한 학대를 하얏다. 그러나 천진난만한 『하아기큐리쯔』는 그것을 알지도 못하고 하루는 『쮸-노』의 젓을 힘껏 빠럿다. 그 순간에 쮸노는 전에 업던 애정이 소사나며 가슴에서는 폭포가튼 젓이 쏘다저서 天에 올나가서는 銀河水가 되고 땅에 떠러저서는 白百合이 되엿다고 한다. 이 꼿은 純潔을 상징한 것이니 伊太利와 西班牙에서는 聖母의 祭壇에 粧飾을 한다.

水仙(中國)

녯날 神人時代에 『나-시쓰사스』라 칭하는 眉目淸秀한 한 美少年이 잇섯는데 엇던 소녀는  그 소년을 한 번 보고 사랑의 불꼿이 타올나서 무한한 고민을 하얏스나 소년은 돌과 가티 아주 냉정하야 돌아보지도 안엇다. 엇던 날에 『나시쓰사스』는 산양을 하다가 피곤하야 물을 먹으러 泉邊으로 갓더니 마츰 그 소녀가 또한 泉邊에 놀너왓섯다. 소녀는 그를 평소에 누구보다도 사모하던 터이라 한 번 보고 반갑게 말을 붓첫스나 一言의 返辭가 업슴으로 소녀는 가슴을 태우다 태우다 못하야 그만 병이 들어 죽엇섯다. 얼마 안이 잇다가 그 泉邊에서는 淸秀하고 아리따운 白花가 피엿다. 그것이 곳 水仙花엿다. 自尊의 의미.

矢車菊(獨逸)

녯날 普魯斯의 루이 황후는 이 꼿을 비상히 사랑하얏기 때문에 독일사람들은 지금까지도 일반이<46> 國花로 경애하는데 一名은 『미이도』花라고 칭한다. 루이황후는 죽은 지 벌서 오랫스나 矢車菊은 해마다 해마다 依舊하게 되니 年年歲歲 花相似하되 歲歲年年 人不同이라는 歎感은 그 꼿을 대하는 독일사람으로서 누구나 금치 못할 것이다.

花菖蒲(佛蘭西)

花菖蒲는 佛國王家의 紋章이 된 까닭에 녯날에는 보통인민은 함부로 꼿지를 못하얏스나 혁명후에는 일반국민이 마음대로 다 꼿게 되엿다. 革命의 恩澤은 꼿에까지 보급!

櫻花(日本)

고대의 신화에 의하면 山神과 野神間에 生한 木花開耶姬라는 미인이 瓊瓊杵尊에게 시집을 갓섯는데 그 신랑의 방에는 雲霞가 가득하야 마음대로 드러갈수가 업슴으로 미인의 아버지는 그로 하야곰 雲霞를 타고 공중으로 나러가서 富士山 絶頂에 臨하게 하고 『사구라』의 종자를 撒布케 하엿더니 그 종자가 繁殖하야 日本 全國에 퍼젓다고 한다. 櫻은 高尙優美를 의미하는 동시에 一時開 一時落하는 것이 일본의 국민성을 表徵함이라 한다.

薔薇(英國)

장미는 애정을 의미함이다. 녯날 몃천년 전에 『베쓰레헴』市에서 한 미인이 화형을 당하게 되엿는데 그 주위에다 아모리 柴草를 쌋코 불을 질너도 꺼지고 타지 안음으로 구경하던 사람들이 모다 이상히 생각하얏다. 그것은 그 미인이 자기의 無罪潔白한 것을 신에 祈禱訴願한 까닭이엿다. 얼마 안이하야 그 미인은 아리따운 白薔薇花로 幻形하얏다. 그럼으로 장미가 최초에는 모다 백색이엿더니 그후 엇던 때에 『큐-빗트』라는 愛의 神이 하늘에서 萬神과 가티 연회를 할 때에 잘못 실수하야 술잔을 떨어트리엿더니 그 술이 백장미에 떠러저서 황색장미가 되엿다는 신화가 잇다.
그리고  또 愛의 여신 『뷔-나스』가 엇던 날에 미소년 『아도니스』의 위급한 것을 구하랴고 급히 달녀가다가 넘어저서 장미의 가시에 찔이엿더니 눈빗 가티 흰 다리에서 붉은 피가 쏘다저서 백장미에 뿌려즌 까닭으로 백장미는 또 赤薔薇로 변하엿다고 전하야 오는 신화가 잇다.
