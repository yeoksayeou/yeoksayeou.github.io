家庭兩極記, 家庭爭議 紙上審判
第一號 法廷
사건 남편의 처에 대한 불충실과 배신에 대한 안해의 抗爭

Two Extremes of Family, Paper Trial of Family Dispute
Courtroom No. 1
Case: Wife's Resistance to Husband's Infidelity and Betrayal

원고 안해
피고 남편

Plaintiff: Wife
Defendant: Husband

裁判長 피고는 원고의 남편이라지.
被告 녜 그럿소.
裁判長 그러면 안해에게 대해서 불충실한 점이 만헛다나＜94＞ 사실인가.
被告 엇던 점을 들어 불 충실이라 하는지 지적하기 전에는 알 수 업는 일이요.
裁 응. 피고의 말에 대해서 원고는 지적할 만한 사실이 잇는가.
原告 녜. 잇구 말구요. 우리는 적어도 연애결혼을 한 사람들이야요. 그럼으로 두 사람의 의사와 인격을 존중하야 가정에 잇서서는 절대로 평등 권리를 가지고 아모리 사소한 일이라도 서로 합의해서 처리하기로 결혼 당초부터 단단히 언약을 하엿담니다. 그래서 처음 얼마동안은 자미잇는 생활을 햇세요. 그때 나는... ...
裁 간단히 간단히!
原告 녜. 그래도 이 말은 해야해요... ... 그때 나는 은행이 취직하고 잇섯세요. 그래 빨내 가튼 것도 세탁소에 주는 것 외에는 일요일이나 휴일 가튼 틈을 타서 두리서 하고 밥 짓는 것도 아침에는 내가 짓고 저녁에는 자긔가 짓기로 해왓섯세요.
裁 흥 그래 그것이 잘 실행되엿든가.
原告 녜. 처음에는... ...
裁 얼마동안이나?
原 한 두어달 동안 계속 되엿지요.
裁 그런데 지금은 실행이 못된단 말이지?
原 녜. 그래요. 처음 약속은 전부 다 깨트러지고 말엇세요.＜95＞ 그리고 별별 이유를 다 부처서 나의 직업을 그만두게 하드니만 그 뒤로는 아주 그만 구식 부녀와 맛찬가지 대우를 하는구먼요. 나종에는 밥짓고 빨내하는게 여자의 천직이라는 둥 안이 꼬운 케케묵은 소리까지 해이끼(예사로)로 느려놋는군요. 그리고 자긔가 저녁밥을 지어야 할 날은 으레 술이 취해서 눗게 돌아옴니다 그려. 그리고 요새와서는 어데 마음 둔 곳이 잇는 모양이야요. 기생 외입을 하나봐요... ... 나는 인격을 짓밟히고 사랑까지도 희미해진 부부생활에는 더 참을 수가 업서요.
裁 응. 그래서 여러번 충돌이 되엿단 말이지. 그런데 아즉도 남편을 사랑하는 마음은 잇는가.
原 영 업진 안치요. 단지 그의 태도와 언행에 따라서 자연 차차 식어 지는듯해요. 엇절 수 업지 안어요. 난 그가 정 그런 태도를 곳치지 안으면 이혼할 각오까지도 하고 잇서요.
裁 응. 현대 신여성으로는 당연한 항변이야. 그럼 원고의 진술에 대해서 피고는 전부 이를 시인하는가.
被 사실에 잇서서 다소 시인할 점도 잇스나 나로서는 이유가 잇는 일이요. 오즉 원고가 가정이란 것을 잘 이해하지도 못하고 頑迷하게도 남녀평등 동등권리 등만을 주장하기 따문에 생기는 폐단이지요. 밥 짓는 것만 말하드래도 처음에는 그의 無邪氣한 유희심을 물니칠 수 업서서 사실 저녁밥은 내가 짓기로 햇지요. 또 안해를 사랑하는 마음으로 빨내도 갓치 한 일이 잇섯소. 그랫더니 혹 연회 가튼 것이 잇서서 눗게 돌아올 때가 잇스면 자긔 차레가 안이라고 성이 털끗까지 올나서 밥을＜96＞ 짓지안코 노리고 안젓지요. 또 무슨 카로리가 잇는 음식을 먹어야 하느니 뷔타민A.B.C.가 만히 들엇느니 적게 들엇느니 하야 자양과 조리법에 두통이 날만치 머리를 썻다는 양요리를 나는 도저히 먹어낼 수가 없섯소... ... 사랑과 미각은 혼동할 수 업스닛가요.. ... 그래서 너무 권리만 찻고 모든 일에 긔막히는 이유와 설명을 붓처서 박아지를 글그니 이건 가정이 안이라 무슨 남녀 투쟁장 갓지가 안켓소. 정말 난 이런 류의 신여성에는 실망할 수 밧게 업섯소이다.
裁 잘 알엇서. 그럼 피고는 원고와 이혼할 생각이 잇는가.
被 그런 생각까지는 업소. 좀더 지나면 그가 자기의 잘못을 깨달게 될 줄 미드닛가요.
原 내가 무슨 잘못이 잇길내? 그럼 아주 그만 당신의 노예가 되고 말게요. 그걸 말이라고 하나!
裁 원고는 가만히 잇서! 자 그러면 이제 두 사람의 진술한 바에 의하야 직석판결을 나릴 터이니 조용히 들으라. 첫재 원고로 말하면 매우 새로운 사상을 가진 신여성인 모양인데 남녀 평등을 주장하는 것은 조흔 일이요 당연한 바이나 부부생활의 엇던 것임을 잘 이해하지 못하고 잇는 모양이다. 부부간이란 인격과 인격이 융화하여야 하는 것이며 부단히 사랑을 창조하야 스사로 두 사람의 安息所인 가정을 만들어야 한다. 그러자면 천직이 안이라도 各其性徵과 長技에 따라서 가정 안에 분업이 생겨야 할 것이다. 원고는 부질업시 新思想에만 머리가 완미해저서 이 점을 깨닷지 못한 모양이다. 그러나 그러함에는＜97＞ 결코 구도덕적 강제와 압박이 잇서서는 안된다. 만일 피고의 진술에 거즛이 업다하면 제 차레가 안이라고 밥을 짓지 안코 노리고 안젓다는 것은 도저히 나로서는 이해할 수 업는 일이다. 그러나 피고는 상당한 사회적 지위도 잇고 지식 계급의 新靑年으로써 그 점에 잇서 안해를 납득 식히지 못하고 번번히 충돌을 하엿다는 것은 사랑으로 지도해 주고 깨처주기 전에 실망부터 먼저하야 가정을 비우고 다른데서 사랑을 추구한 것을 증명하는 바이다. 그러고 원고가 결혼한 뒤 그리 큰 이유가 업시 직업을 버린 것은 스사로 자긔 지위를 낫친 것이니 큰 실책이다. 이러한 점을 살펴서 특히 승패를 나리지 안코 삼 개월 간 별거생활을 하되 원고의 생활비는 피고가 부담하기로 판결을 언도하는 바이다. 만약 그 삼 개월 동안 피차에 남의 夫요 妻되는 처지로서 불미한 점이 생긴다면 그때는 청구에 의해서 다시 엄격한 판결을 내릴것이다.

Judge: The defendant is the plaintiff's husband, I believe.
Defendant: Yes, that is correct.
Judge: Then, is it true that you have been very unfaithful to your wife? <94>
Defendant: I cannot know unless you specify what aspects you consider unfaithful.
Judge: I see. Does the plaintiff have any specific facts to point out regarding the defendant's statement?
Plaintiff: Yes, of course I do. We are at least people who married for love. Therefore, we promised firmly from the beginning of our marriage to respect each other's intentions and personalities, to have absolutely equal rights in the home, and to handle even the smallest matters through mutual agreement. So, for the first while, we had an enjoyable life. At that time, I...
Judge: Briefly, briefly!
Plaintiff: Yes. But I have to say this... At that time, I was employed at a bank. So, besides giving things like laundry to the cleaners, we would do it together on Sundays or holidays, and we had agreed that I would cook in the morning and he would cook in the evening.
Judge: Hmm, and was that well executed?
Plaintiff: Yes, at first...
Judge: For how long?
Plaintiff: It continued for about a couple of months.
Judge: And now it is not being executed?
Plaintiff: Yes. That's right. All the initial promises were completely broken. <95> And making up all sorts of reasons, he made me quit my job, and after that, he treated me just like an old-fashioned woman. Eventually, he even casually spouts out outdated nonsense that cooking and doing laundry are a woman's natural calling. And on the days when he should cook dinner, he invariably comes home late, drunk. And these days, it seems like he has someone else on his mind. It seems he is seeing a courtesan... I can no longer endure a marital life where my personality is trampled on and even love has faded.
Judge: I see. So, there have been many conflicts. But do you still have feelings of love for your husband?
Plaintiff: It's not entirely gone. It just seems to fade gradually according to his attitude and behavior. I can't help it. I am even prepared to divorce him if he doesn't correct that attitude.
Judge: I see. That is a natural protest for a modern new woman. Does the defendant admit to all of the plaintiff's statements?
Defendant: There are some points in the facts that I can admit, but I have my reasons. It is only because the plaintiff does not understand what a family is and stubbornly insists on gender equality, equal rights, etc., that these problems arise. Speaking only of cooking, at first I could not resist her innocent playfulness, so I agreed to cook dinner. Also, I did the laundry with her out of love for my wife. But when there were occasions such as parties where I came home late, she would get angry, saying it was not her turn, and would refuse to cook. <96> Also, she would insist on eating foods with so many calories, or whether there was a lot or little vitamin A, B, C, that I could not bear to eat the Western food that she had racked her brains over for nutrition and cooking methods... Can't we separate love and taste?... So, she only sought rights and added incredibly detailed reasons and explanations to everything, it was not a home but a gender struggle. I can only be disappointed in this type of new woman.
Judge: I understand. So, does the defendant have any intention of divorcing the plaintiff?
Defendant: I don't have such thoughts. I believe that she will realize her mistakes after some time.
Plaintiff: What mistakes do I have? Then I'll just become your slave. How can you say such a thing!
Judge: Plaintiff, be quiet! Well then, I will now deliver a summary judgment based on the statements of both of you, so listen quietly. Firstly, the plaintiff seems to be a new woman with very new ideas, and it is good and natural to advocate for gender equality, but it seems that she does not fully understand what a marital life is. Marriage should be a fusion of personalities, and a home, which is a haven (安息所) for both people, should be created by constantly creating love. To do that, division of labor should occur in the home according to each person's characteristics (各其性徵) and strengths (長技), even if it is not their natural calling. It seems that the plaintiff has not realized this because she is foolishly obsessed with new ideas. However, <97> there should never be moral coercion or pressure in doing so. If there is no falsehood in the defendant's statement, then it is incomprehensible to me that she would refuse to cook, saying it is not her turn. However, the defendant also has a considerable social status and is a new youth (新靑年) of the educated class, and the fact that he has repeatedly clashed without convincing his wife in that regard proves that he abandoned his home and sought love elsewhere, giving up before guiding and enlightening her with love. Also, the fact that the plaintiff quit her job after getting married without a good reason is a major mistake, as she lowered her own status. Considering these points, I will not particularly declare a winner or loser, but I order a three-month separation, with the defendant to bear the plaintiff's living expenses. If any unsavory incidents occur during those three months while they are in the position of being someone else's husband or wife, then a stricter judgment will be made again upon request.

第二號法廷
事件 안해의 부정에 대한 남편의 離婚 訴訟
原告 남편 證人 어멈
被告 안해 K란 두 사나희

Courtroom No. 2
Case: Husband's Divorce Lawsuit Against Wife's Infidelity
Plaintiff: Husband Witness: Omeom
Defendant: Wife and Mr. K

裁判長 원고는 원고의 안해인 피고가 원고의 친구 K란 사람과 간통햇다는 무슨 물적 증거를 가젓는가?＜98＞
原告 내가 싀골 가고 업는 동안에 K가 방에 차저와서 내 처하고 단두리 눗도록 이야기하고 간 일이 잇섯고 또 한번은 두리서 가티 나갓다가 술에 취하야 얼골이 빨게진 K가 자정이 지나서 내 안해를 다려다 주드라는 사실을 어멈이 보앗다합니다. 고러고 K가 내 안해에게 한 편지가 여긔잇소.
裁判長 그 편지를 자저오라... ... 그러나 이것은 그저 전에 가서 실례가 만헛다는 인사의 편지에 지나지 못한게 안이냐. 이걸로는 간통의 증거가 되지안어!
어멈은 원고의 안해가 K하고 두리 가치 나가는 것과 단두리 안저서 이야기 하는 것을 보앗는가. 거즛말하면 징역을 보낼테야! 바로 말해.
어멈 녜요. 보구 말구요. 서방님이 싀골 가실때 일부로 일느시고 가섯는데 그럼 그런걸 보고 가만 잇겟세요. 아씨가 전에는 그럿치 안터니 이즘 정말 난봉이 낫나봐요.
裁 식그러! 그런말 하는게 안이야! 알엇스니 물너가! 그러고 피고 이리나와 응- 피고는 악가 한 원고와 어멈의 말에 대해서 이의가 업는가? K와 간통한 사실이 잇섯는가?
被告 절대로 그런 일이 업슴니다. 재판장이여! 너무나 억울한 말이 올시다. 어멈은 내 남편에게 돈을 바더 먹고 남편의 ＜99＞긔미를 맛치려는 계책이야요. K는 내 남편과 형제와 가치 친한 친구야요. 그 남편이래서 잇고 업고간에 각금 놀너 오시는 분이야요. 공교하게도 내 남편이 싀골에 가고 업슬때 그이가 놀너와서 좀 오래 이야기하고 간 일은 잇섯세요. 그러고 한 번은 남편이 업슬땐데... ... 그때도 어대 볼일이 잇서 나가는데 오후 두시에는 꼿 돌아오겟스니 가티 야외로 놀너가자고 단단히 약속을 해 놋코는 일곱시가 지나도 돌아오질 안는구먼요. 옷까지 가라입고 긔다리는데 혼자 어대가서 잘 노는 가부다 생각하니 분해 못 견듸겟는데 맛츰 K씨가 차저 왓서요. 그래서 화ㅅ김에 차나 한 잔 마시러 가자고 두리서 잠간 나갓다 온 일 밧게는 업서요. 그것이 무엇이 잘못이야요. 엇제서 간통이란 말얘요. 남편의 친한 친구하고 가티 이야기를 좀 햇기로 산보를 햇기로니 그것이 그만 부정한 행동이 된단 말임닛가.
裁 조용히 말해. 너무 그리 흥분될 것은 업서. 잘 처단해 줄 터이니... ...K는 이 일에 대해서 할 말이 업는가?
K 나는 내 친한 친구의 부인을 욕되게 할 행동을 하리 만치 타락된 사람은 안이요. 그러고 이번 이러한 불미한 소송을 제기한 친구의 태도는 확실히 우리 사히의 친분과 나의 명예에 도전의 화살을 던진 것으로 밋슴니다. 그러고 그가 오늘 날 이러한 태도를＜100＞취하기에까지 이른 것은 반다시 엇더한 목적 아래서 술책을 弄한것이 나 안일가하오.
裁 거긔 대한 증거가 잇는가?
K 물적 증거는 업스나 그가 엇던 여성에게 마음이 기우러저 잇다는 것을 그와 친한 탓으로 내가 잘 알고 잇스며 또 현재의 자기 부인에게 대한 불만... ...그것도 주장미에 대하야... ...을 종종 호소한 일이 잇섯지요.
被 정말이야요. 요새는 그런 눈치가 확실히 보혓서요. 내가 미우면 정당하게 갈너질 일이지 이런 허망한 모욕을 하는 것은 정말 분해 죽겟서요. 그것이 엇지 신사의 태도야요.

Judge: Does the plaintiff have any material evidence that the defendant, who is the plaintiff's wife, committed adultery with the plaintiff's friend, Mr. K? <98>
Plaintiff: While I was away in the countryside, Mr. K came to the room and talked to my wife alone for a long time, and once, Omeom saw Mr. K, with a flushed face from being drunk, bring my wife home after midnight. Also, here is a letter from Mr. K to my wife.
Judge: Bring that letter... But isn't this just a letter of greeting saying he was very rude the other day? This is not evidence of adultery!
Omeom, have you seen the plaintiff's wife go out with Mr. K or sit and talk alone? If you lie, you'll be sent to prison! Tell the truth.
Omeom: Yes, I have seen it. When her husband went to the countryside, he deliberately told me when he was leaving, so how could I stay still after seeing that? Madam used to be different, but these days, she seems to be running wild.
Judge: Be quiet! Don't say such things! I understand, step back! And you, defendant, come here. Defendant, do you have any objections to the words of the plaintiff and Omeom? Have you committed adultery with Mr. K?
Defendant: Absolutely not. Judge! This is such an unfair statement. Omeom took money from my husband and is trying to match her husband's mood (긔미). Mr. K is a close friend of my husband, like a brother. He is a person who comes to visit sometimes, whether my husband is there or not. Coincidentally, my husband was away in the countryside when he came to visit and talked for a long time. And once, when my husband was away... I was going out on business that day and promised to be back by 2 PM so we could go on an outing together, but he didn't come back even after 7 PM. I was dressed and waiting, and I couldn't bear the thought of him having fun somewhere by himself, so Mr. K happened to come by. So, in a fit of anger, I suggested we go out for a cup of tea, and we just went out for a while. What is wrong with that? Why is it called adultery? How can it be considered immoral behavior just because I talked or took a walk with my husband's close friend?
Judge: Speak quietly. There's no need to be so agitated. I will handle this well... Mr. K, do you have anything to say about this matter?
Mr. K: I am not so depraved as to do anything to disgrace my close friend's wife. And I believe that my friend's attitude in bringing this disgraceful lawsuit is definitely throwing an arrow of challenge at our friendship and my honor. And I can't help but wonder if he has been using some kind of trick (술책) under some purpose to get to this point today. <100>
Judge: Do you have evidence for that?
Mr. K: I have no material evidence, but as a close friend, I am well aware that his heart is leaning towards another woman, and he has often complained about his current wife... especially about her insistence (주장미)...
Defendant: That's right. I've definitely noticed that lately. If he hates me, he should just separate properly, but I'm really angry that he's making such absurd insults. How is that the attitude of a gentleman?
Judge: Okay, be quiet! That's enough listening for now. I will make a ruling, so everyone come forward. As a result of questioning Omeom in a separate room, it was revealed that the plaintiff gave Omeom 5 won when he went to the countryside, and the facts of this case do not constitute sufficient evidence to reach adultery. To immediately consider it an act of infidelity for a close friend to come and talk to his wife or take a walk with her while he is away is a delusion that only a Confucian scholar steeped in old morality could think of. It comes from a distorted interpretation close to jealousy (嫉妬心) stemming from the lack of a solid friendship between friends and the lack of true love and understanding for his wife. Or, as Mr. K and the defendant said, it could be a scheme (計略) to reject his wife because he has fallen for another woman. Looking at the plaintiff's attitude towards this dispute, it is clear that both of these psychologies are at play. And those who are wives should consider their husband's face and treat his close friends with special hospitality and goodwill. It seems that the defendant's attitude towards Mr. K comes from this. Today, Korean youths (朝鮮 靑年) seem to have new ideas about marital relations, but they still try to apply imprisonment (監禁主義) only to their wives and have a tendency to retain the relics of the slave and feudal eras, the desire for possession. This should at least be a shame (羞恥) for new youths (新靑年) in the new era. If he had respected his wife's personality and trusted his friend's friendship, he would never have come to this situation. In this sense, I dismiss this lawsuit and order the plaintiff to sincerely apologize to the defendant and Mr. K and to respect his wife's personality and become a couple with strong trust in each other on a foundation of love that absorbs the spirit of the times. And Mr. K should not visit that wife while her husband is away until such trust is established between the plaintiff and defendant. If you don't have that sense, you are likely to cause discord in other people's homes. Leave! <102>
