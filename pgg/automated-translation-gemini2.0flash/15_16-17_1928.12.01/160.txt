The Thing I Liked Most in Foreign Countries, The Thing We Can Immediately Emulate
						Kim Pŏmnin

The Most Missed Library
Kim Pŏmnin

The thing that impressed me most when I went to France was the people's enthusiasm for reading. In Chosŏn, you rarely see people reading newspapers or magazines on trains or trams, but in France, there are many people who take books and read them even in trams, trains, parks, and other entertainment venues. When traveling, they would rather forget cigarettes and walking sticks than forget their books. Because the people of that country have such enthusiasm for reading, there are also many public and private libraries. It goes without saying that there are many in large cities like Paris, but even in very poor rural villages—like mountain hamlets in Chosŏn where people eat only potatoes and corn—there is always a library. To put it another way, there are more libraries than makkeolli (濁酒) houses in Chosŏn. What is even more impressive is that even gray-haired old men in their 60s and 70s go to the library every day and read books diligently, no different from young people. For some reason, reading ability in Chosŏn seems to be still weak. Even if there are people who want to read, it seems they cannot because there are no libraries. Although there are several libraries in Seoul, it seems that there are not many libraries in the countryside, even in large cities. Just as our bodies need nourishment to maintain life, shouldn't our minds also have nourishment to live? Books are the nourishment of our minds. Just as the wealth of a nation is judged by its financial power, the civilization of a nation is judged by its reading ability. I think it is most regrettable and shameful that Chosŏn people are not only weak in electric power, but also weak in reading ability. There are many things we need to establish, but I think the establishment of libraries is most necessary. I hope that many libraries will be established in rural villages rather than in government or public institutions, and more in rural villages than in cities.
<68>
Even if it cannot be on a large scale, how delightful would it be if even small-scale libraries were established everywhere? Of course, it is difficult to manage this individually, so it would be good if religious organizations or youth groups took the lead in establishing even simple ones.

I Want to Implement the British Family System in Chosŏn
Pak Sŏgyun

It is undeniable that the various customs that have been passed down in traditional Chosŏn families will change rapidly or gradually as our lives become more globalized, but as the need for Chosŏn people's families to be reorganized practically and usefully is so urgent, it is a fact that general interest in this issue is also growing.

The traditions that have been maintained within Chosŏn's families, in other words, the so-called old families that include all of Chosŏn's old moral codes, are now shaking from their foundations and are collapsing too rapidly in some ways. Because new norms to replace the collapsing old moral codes have not yet been systematically established, those who find chaos and disorder there cannot see any other new light, and the shaken foundation of the family has even affected society in general, and the morals of individuals in social life have lost their authority, which is the current phenomenon.

There are many very complex reasons for the social loss of moral authority in present-day Chosŏn, but the fact that solid new norms have not been established in the family is no small contribution. Of course, establishing our own way of self-governance in social life is the most direct route and the axis we should naturally seek, but at the same time, in our family life, we must quickly move away from the old ways and build a solid foundation for family life by establishing practical norms.

In the past, the moral codes we followed had a source of origin. This can be said to be the source of Eastern morality in some sense, not just in Chosŏn. It also had a unified social power to enforce that moral code. However, whether the origin and social power of the moral codes we had are suitable for the inherent needs of humanity is not questioned now, but today we have neither that origin nor that social power. Therefore, we cannot avoid temporary transitional changes as there is no control as before, but instead, we can say that we have the good fortune to create a unique new tradition most suitable for our national life with the background of free thought.

It seems that the recent issue in family reform lies in the methodology. However, in my opinion, while it is certainly necessary and good to find and improve better things one by one, it seems that a more fundamental consideration of the basis or origin of family moral codes is an urgent issue for us. We have something extremely venerable and precious called the "Chosŏn mind (朝鮮心)," so there is no doubt that the main stream of our family and social moral codes will naturally originate from this Chosŏn mind unconsciously or semi-consciously, but this is a very abstract concept in some sense, and therefore it is extremely difficult for it to be pervasive in each person's life consciousness as a truly refined reality, and it will certainly require considerable time, explanation, and faith to express its value as a reality.

I will not now discuss the basis or origin for the new establishment of family moral codes that will come in Chosŏn, but I will only try to define "our attitude" toward family problems. There have always been thoughts that deny the existence of the family since ancient times. Perhaps there are not a few people in Chosŏn today who doubt the reason for the existence of the family. In the same way, in improving our families, there is no fixed direction in the basis of moral codes, and as the social control imposed from above is also very weak, there will be ten people, ten colors (십인십색), and a hundred people, a hundred colors (백인백색) in their opinions and in their implementation. To put it more concretely, there are people who live in Japanese-style houses called improved houses or cultural houses, people who live in half-Western, half-private makeshift houses, and people who live in small-scale housing expos that combine Chosŏn, Western, and Japanese styles, and there is nothing that has not been influenced by so-called foreign ideas, whether good or bad, in our words, clothes, actions, and other things. This is an unavoidable problem we face and also an extremely natural path. These days, there are some Chosŏn students walking around in Japanese clogs, and there are people who live in Japanese clothes, and even the smallest utensils in the house are like exhibits in a variety of exhibitions, and there is nothing that does not feel chaotic in anything related to family problems. Seeing these phenomena, some people are so at a loss that they are very pessimistic about the future. However, as mentioned above, we have no other way to avoid such chaos in our situation, and on the other hand, I believe that only after this chaos will a lasting new tradition of the family be established. Therefore, our attitude towards this problem is simply that each person should do as they please. In other words, unless what someone else does directly harms other individuals or social life, let us take an absolutely tolerant attitude and abandon the intention to interfere even in our minds.

There may be various factors that have made the British people have such a great national character, but among them, I believe that the family, which the French mocked by saying that there is nothing in England but sports and family, treasures the driving force of the British people. To the Englishman, his home is his castle. It is the territory where he exercises absolute sovereignty. Therefore, the Englishman insists on absolute sovereignty over his family and at the same time fully allows the same attitude to others' families. This has become a common phrase that the Englishman often does not say, "Mind your own business." It means "Do your own work." or "Don't interfere in other people's business." This attitude that comes from the family is a great factor in the British national character and gives a driving force to spread out as it pleases.

We talk too much. We are too sensitive to the dust in other people's eyes. Let us make our homes our castles. In other words, let us close our eyes to other people's business. Let everyone do as they please. Even so, I believe that we will be unified in the Chosŏn mind, just as the British remain British. Because we are free and not bound by anything, I believe that we will create a unique tradition. If we continue to maintain this attitude, we will bring a unique tradition to our society more quickly.

What I Saw and Learned in Denmark
Sin Hŭngu

I have traveled to various countries in Europe and America several times, but in the past, I only traveled for church work, so I did not pay much attention to what their lives were like other than things related to the church, and therefore I did not know much. However, recently, our Christian church has been paying great attention to the Chosŏn rural problem, so I also paid some attention to it, and again this summer, I inspected about 20 countries in Europe and America with Mr. M某 (Momo-ssi) as a special envoy of the church. I learned and felt many things everywhere I went, but what impressed me the most was going to Denmark and seeing how developed the agriculture of that country was. As everyone knows, Denmark is a small country located at the northwestern end of Europe, but when it comes to the development of agriculture, even the most powerful and civilized countries in Europe and America would yield first place. Of course, this is because the people of that country have been inherently diligent and hardworking and have devoted themselves to the improvement and development of agriculture, but fundamentally, it seems to be because the people who guide the farmers of that country first taught them the truth of life above all else. Humans are animals with the strongest desire for life, but if they do not realize the truth of life, they cannot pioneer the path of life, and at the same time, they will simply become discouraged and fall, and they will not be able to achieve their sacred desires. Denmark is also a Christian country, and the leaders of the farmers are also believers in Christ, but they believe that Christianity and the Danish nation must be safe in order for Christianity to develop well, so they always shout out the truth of life as individuals and as a church, and because the general public has the idea that everyone must live well, agriculture has also developed in that way.

I was most impressed by the fact that the people of Denmark realized such a truth of life, rather than the development of agriculture in Denmark. Our Chosŏn has been an agricultural country since ancient times, and the authorities say that "agriculture is the foundation of the world (農者는 天下之大本이니)" and focus on promoting agriculture, and the people also know that they cannot live without agriculture, and those who work in society say that Chosŏn must protect and develop farmers well, and they put a lot of effort into rural lectures, farmer magazines, and tenant farming, but above all, I think we should teach the truth of life to the farmers—no, to the general Chosŏn people. When I travel around foreign countries and arrive in Pusan or Ŭiju, I feel really frustrated when I see the lives of our Chosŏn people. It is a sinful thing to say, but the anxious thought that I have anew is, "Why don't our Chosŏn people know the truth of life?" Even if our environment is like this, if we each know the truth of life, isn't there a way for us all to live well? I think it was a big mistake to take our village problems lightly. I think our Christianity is far behind Ch'ŏndogyo in that respect. No matter what the teachings are, we are all Chosŏn people, so we must all study and pioneer the way for Chosŏn people to live.

Four Things About German Living
Yi Sŏngnyong

During my time in Germany, there were many things in their lives that I thought were good and right. Among them, I especially believe that the things that we can bring into our lives with little time and effort, if we only have the will, are cleanliness, organization, orderly behavior, and raising young children.

Chosŏn people have never known how to organize since ancient times. Therefore, if you go into a Chosŏn person's home, the furniture is scattered here and there like fallen leaves in autumn. What was lying on the floor in the morning is placed on the shelf in the evening, and there is no fixed place, so it rolls around here and there. Therefore, it takes a lot of time to find one item, and many items are damaged.

In contrast, German people, from young children to the elderly, are consistent in organizing and arranging items. They know how to put things back in their place once they have used them. Therefore, the home of a German person is always neat and tidy, which does not make the viewer uncomfortable. And unlike us, they do not waste time unnecessarily or worry. It does not cost money, it does not take effort, and it can be done if you only have the will, so we should imitate the work of organizing.

German people do a major cleaning of their homes once a week. They always sweep and wipe the floors and verandas in the morning and evening, but especially once a week they do a major cleaning. Therefore, if you go into a German person's house, you cannot get a speck of dust even if you touch the cracks in the door with your fingertip. Therefore, even if the room is not decorated ornately, it is clean and tidy, so the mind is always refreshed and the body is greatly benefited. When we look at our homes, there are more than one or two things that need to be fixed, such as furniture and house systems, but this is something that can only be done if you have money, so we cannot start easily, but we should sweep the room often or dust the furniture, or do a major cleaning once a week like the German people to keep it clean. Keeping things clean does not necessarily mean buying good items, but it means keeping the items clean even if they are not good, so this is as important as eating.

Order is even more necessary for many people living in one place. A home is a collection of individuals. Society is an organic body of homes. We may not know about the primitive era of long ago, but today we cannot live apart from the home and society. Therefore, the spirit of respecting order is more important than anything else.

German people practice orderly behavior well. They have a budget for everything. Therefore, they do not see foolish mistakes or unexpected failures. Even when they visit someone, they ask in advance what time they can meet and come at the exact time they agreed to meet and talk about the main points of the matter from the beginning. Compared to this, our lives are too irregular. Everything from the time we sleep, the time we eat, and the time we go out is irregular. Therefore, the impact of this on individuals, families, and societies is truly great, including losses to individuals, understanding to families, and societies not becoming right. We must strive for orderly behavior from now on.

The thing that needs to be fixed right away, even from today, is the way we raise young children. When German people do not do something right, they scold them or rebuke them, but they never hit them with their fists or sticks like Chosŏn people do. If you hit them, it seems that there is an effect at the time because the pain reaches the body, but without knowing it, a violent temperament develops in the personality of the young child. If a young child does not listen even if you scold them, it is said that the best way is to starve them or make them kneel down so that they realize their own mistakes. Therefore, those who have children in Chosŏn should think carefully about this point and not only focus on sending them to school, but also pay attention to how parents teach young children at home.

The best thing to do is to do what you think is right, so if you do not do it, it is of no use. The things written above are things that can be done, so I hope that you will do them as they are.

Especially Exemplary Child Education
Kim Hamna

When I was in the United States, I personally experienced their lives and thought that there were many things that were good, but the first thing we should imitate is to pay attention to raising young children.

It is not that our Chosŏn people do not love their children. Everyone wants to feed them delicious food, make them good clothes, and see them grow up better than others.

Chosŏn people think that their responsibility as parents is to make clothes, feed them, and send them to school. American people also make delicious food and good clothes for their children and want them to grow up well. In this respect, we are no different.

However, American people give more importance to young children in various ways than we do, and they study, consider, and are cautious in detail for them in addition to feeding and clothing them.

American people have a firm idea that they think of young children as citizens who will improve, develop, and lead the society of the future.

Because of this, they are careful not to do anything that is harmful to young children.

Chosŏn people think that drinking alcohol and smoking are bad things and teach their young children the same, but in reality, they drink alcohol and smoke in front of the children. In short, Chosŏn people think that school education is enough for child education, but it cannot be said that school education alone is complete in terms of becoming a person.

The place where children are most often together is the home, so the place where we should pay attention to child education is the home. The home is a place led by parents, so parents should be teachers before anyone else.

Therefore, adults should be careful in every move they make.

American people first of all do not lie to young children. Secondly, when they scold them, they never use words that will have a negative impact on the child's mind or emotions, and they make them understand the right and wrong of the matter. Thirdly, they recognize the rights of young children even within the home, and if the children's claims are beneficial to the children themselves, they abandon their own opinions and obey the children's claims. These things have a great relationship to the cultivation of individuality, belief, and self-esteem.

I cannot list everything that is done in the home or at school, but above all, we must strive to learn not only to love young children, but also to respect them, and we must truly realize that we must honor children, no matter what point of view we see them from, whether it is the home, society, or nation, and we must have a much more civilized spirit than the spirit we have had toward children in the past. I felt this keenly when I was in the United States, and I would like to recommend it to you all at this opportunity.

The Diligence and Thrift of the Chinese People
Sŏ Sech'ung

While I was in China, the things I liked most were the diligence of the people of that country (except for the idle upper class) and their great sense of thrift. You may have seen the Chinese laborers working in Chosŏn. They work diligently from morning to night without knowing the hardship, sweating profusely. They do it the same way, whether it is their own work or someone else's work. It is the same in agriculture, it is the same in industry, and it is the same in any other business. In particular, commerce is not only their innate talent, but also because they are diligent, there is no country in the world that their footprints have not reached. In addition, the people of that country are full of thrift and value every penny as much as their own lives. Even a ragged hawker has savings of several tens or hundreds of won. Moreover, because the people of that country have a strong sense of self-respect and self-esteem, they never change their national clothes or food according to the customs and habits of the country they are in, no matter which country they are in. I do not know if this will be a cause for the slow development of China, but compared to our people, who wear Japanese clothes when they go to Japan, Chinese clothes when they go to China, and Western clothes when they go to the West, and whose language, behavior, and everything else is foreignized, how much more solid and stable is their character?

What I have said above is a brief description of the national character of the people of that country, but even in terms of lifestyle, the Chinese people, first of all, eat "tteok (떡)" (rice cakes) as well as rice, unlike Chosŏn people (China also eats a lot of rice in the south), so the price is low, the cooking process and table setting are not complicated, and they do not value breakfast like Chosŏn people, but value lunch and dinner, and breakfast is simple, so it is convenient to do work in the morning and there is a lot of time benefit, and the clothes are also strong in quality and use dyed fabrics, so they do not waste time and money on washing and sewing like Chosŏn people, and even if the houses in Chosŏn are large, there are only many empty rooms and few rooms where people actually live, so they are not very useful, but Chinese people do not live in heated floors (ondol, 溫突) and make the walls suitable for cold protection, so they use less fuel, and as long as they have a bed like a flat bed, they can live in any room. Therefore, there is not as much housing difficulty as in Chosŏn. It is not that we must imitate Chinese things, but if we improve our lives, it would be good to refer to these things.

Things I Want to Imitate in the American System
Kim Up'yŏng

At the request to introduce anything that I have seen and thought about through foreign school life that can be applied to our way of life, I would like to discuss things that can be used as materials for trial and consideration with the readers.

It is no exaggeration to say that almost nothing in our social life is established or advanced according to our wishes. In such a dire situation, we who experience various difficulties cannot help but feel that even in our family life there is irrationality and emptiness, along with cursing the irrationality. It is not a small feeling that it is like a poor man's song of life or complaining about his situation. If we were to transcend the dust and dirt of the floating world and laugh everything off and just leave it alone, we might have at least some passive satisfaction, but if not, everyone cannot help but feel that our lives are poor.

I envy the prosperous cities of the American people, the orderly and splendid quality of the streets, the universities standing on grassy hills, and the incredibly large factory systems. However, such a life will not be limited to the American people. If we also have the opportunity to freely try the time, labor, capital, and perseverance that they use, our skills and grades may even be superior. However, let us leave aside such an easy and advantageous assumption and ask what can be imported and applied to our current situation that can be implemented immediately?

I would say that it is the family budget system, which is the basis of the simple and orderly family life of the American people. The United States is the country with the most developed modern machine civilization, so their lives are simple and orderly, but if you think a step further, it is because they utilize the principle of "teamwork" in their family life. We lack awareness of "teamwork." Let's utilize the principle of "teamwork" first. In other words, let us utilize the principles of division of labor, unity, and control.

The goal is to simplify our lives and cultivate good habits of orderly living, thereby saving unnecessary time and effort.

In our families, the division of labor is weak in terms of whether the husband is the housewife or the housewife is the husband in terms of their functions, that is, in terms of housework. Therefore, there is no unity in life, there is a lack of control, and it becomes a disorderly and unnecessarily complicated thing.

Therefore, in order to have a well-organized life like the families of the American people, I think that we should first establish and implement a family budget system in our families. The idea is that the husband and wife should divide all of the husband's income equally and share the total family budget set by the husband and wife. In other words, to give a numerical example, let's say that the average income of a family is 150 won per month and the total family budget for one month is 100 won. Then, divide the income of 150 won equally between the husband and wife, 75 won each, and share the family expenses of 100 won, 50 won each, and use the remaining 25 won for each spouse's own use. The reason for dividing the family income equally between the husband and wife and having them bear the family expenses equally is that (1) the husband and wife's sense of responsibility becomes stronger and they have a sense of material independence, the income of a family is not entirely the reward for the husband's efforts alone. The wife helps the husband to work efficiently by taking care of household chores such as cooking, washing, childbearing, and education. Therefore, the wife has the right to half of the income. In this way, the husband's sense of responsibility to the wife and the wife's sense of responsibility to the husband become stronger, and at the same time, the husband's so-called sense of the wife being a high-class maid disappears, and they express their mutual existence of personal dignity.
(2) It prevents extravagance on the part of the husband or wife and allows them to have thrifty and frugal habits. (3) Therefore, it is possible to teach children a life of moderation. (4) It is because the good habit of practicing the division of labor, unity, and control from family life is applied to social life.

Such a way of life can be implemented regardless of the amount of family income. Living improvement does not necessarily mean building a so-called cultural house or a two- or three-story Western-style house, which is popular these days. Even if we live in a single thatched house, let's first realize the family budget system.

金法麟

獨逸 살림 네 가지
李星鎔

내가 독일 가서 잇는 동안 그네들의 생활 가온대에서 됴라고 생각하고 올흔 것이라고 인정한 것은 여러 가지가 잇다. 그 중에도 특히 우리가 적은 시간과 만치 안은 노력으로 마음만 잇스면 우리의 살림에 옴겨다 노을 수 잇다고 생각한 것은 청결과 정돈과 규측뎍 행동과 어린 아희 기루는 것이라고 간주한다.
됴선 사람은 자고 이래로 정돈할 줄을 모른다. 그럼으로 됴선 사람의 가뎡안에 들어가 보면 가구가 맛치 가을날 락엽 가티 여긔 저기 흣터저 잇다. 아츰엔 방바닥에 노혀 잇든 것이 저녁엔 선반에 언치워 잇다든지 하야 일정한 장소가 업시 이러저리 굴러 단이는 형편이다. 이럼으로 물품하나를 차즐야면 만흔 시간을 허비하게 되며 물품도 적지안케 파손되는 것이다.
이와 반대로 독일 사람은 어린 아희를 비롯하야 늙은이에 니르기까지 물품이 나기구 정돈하는 일은 일치되여 잇다. 그네들은 한번 사용한 물품은 꼭-제자리에 가저다 노을 줄 안다. 그럼으로 독일 사람의 가뎡안은 항상 정연하야 보는 사람으로 하여금 불쾌케 하지 안는다. 그리고 우리네와＜73＞ 가티 쓸데업시 시간을 허비하지 안코 마음 씨우는 일도 적다. 돈도 안 들고 힘도 안들고 마음만 잇으면 행할 일이니 우리는 곳 정돈하는 일을 본떠야 할 것이다.
독일 사람은 일주일에 한번식은 가내 대소제를 한다. 아츰과 저녁에 방안이나 마루 가튼데는 늘-쓸고 닥고 하지만 특히 일주일에 한번식은 꼭 대청결을 한다. 그럼으로 독일 사람의 집안에 들어가면 문틈 가튼 것을 손끗으로 만저보아도 몬지 하나 뭇는 법이 업다. 그럼으로 방안은 화려하게 장식하지 안엇서도 깨끗하고 청결하야 마음이 항상 상쾌하고 딸아서 몸에 큰 유익을 엇게 된다. 우리의 가뎡안을 살펴볼 때에 가구라더니 집제도 가튼 것을 곳처야 할 것은 하나 둘이 안이지만 이는 첫재로 돈이 잇서야 할 일이니 손쉬웁게 착수치는 못하나 방안을 자주 쓴다던지 물걸네를 친다든지 독일 사람 가티 한 주일에 한번식 대소제를 하야 청결케 하여야 할 것이다. 청결케 한다는 일은 반다시 조흔 물품을 장만하여야 하는 것이 안이라 물품은 됴치 못할지라도 그 물품에 더러운 것이 무더잇지 안토록 하는 것이니 이 일은 먹는 것 다음으로 가는 귀중한 일이라고 할 것이다.
규측이라는 것은 만흔 살마이 한 곳에 모혀사는데 더욱 필요한 것이다. 가뎡은 개인이 모힌 것이요. 사회는 가정의 집화된 유긔톄이다. 머-ㄴ 녯날 원시시대면 모르지만 오늘에 잇서서는 가뎡과 사회를 떠나 살어갈 수는 업다. 그럼으로 규측을 존수하는 정신은 무엇보다도 긴요한 것이다.
독일 사람은 규측뎍 행동을 잘-리행한다. 그들은 모든 일에 예산을 가지고 잇다. 그럼으로 그들은 어리석은 실수와 뜻하지 안은 실패를 보지 안는다. 그들은 남을 방문할 적에라도 만나 줄 시간을 미리 물어 가지고 만나자고 한 그 시간에 꼭 와서는 일의 요건부터 니야기한다. 이에 비하야 보면 우리네의 살림은 너머 불규측하다. 자는 시간 밥 먹는 시간 외출하는 시간이 일정치 안은 것을 비롯하야 여러 가지가 모다 불규측하다. 그럼으로 인하야 개인에게 손실되는 일가뎡 이해를 닙는 일 사회가 올케 되지 못하는 일등 실노 그 영향은 큰 것이다. 우리는 일노부터 규측뎍 행동을 힘써하여야 할 것이다.
오늘부터라도 곳 곳처야 할 일은 어린 아희 기르는 법이다. 독일 사람은 어린 아희가 잘못하면 그것을 타일는 다던지 꾸지람은 할지언정 됴선사람가티 주먹으로나 막대기로 때리는 법은 절대로 업다. 매질을 하면 몸에 압흠이 밋치니까 일시는 일의 효과가 나는 것 갓지만 모르는 동안에 어린 아희의 품성에 횡포한 성질이 생기게 된다. 어린 아희가 타일너도 말을 안이 들으면 밥을 굶긴다던지 무릅을 꿀면서 자긔의 잘못한 것을 스사로 깨닷도록 하는 것이 데일 조흔 법이라고 한다. 그럼으로 조선사람으로 자녀를 둔 이는 이점을 잘-생각하야서 학교 보내는 일에만 뜻을 두지 말고 집에서 부모가 어린 아희 가르치는 법에 유의하여야 할 것이다.
올흔 일이라고 생각하면 곳 행하는 것이 데일이니 행함이 업스면 이는 아모러한 소용이 업게 된다. 우에 적은 멧가지의 일은 실행할 수 잇는 것인즉 곳 그대로 실행하기를 바라는 바이다.

特히 模範할 子女敎育
金合羅

내가 米國 가 잇슬 때에 그들의 생활을 몸소 체험하여 보고 조타고 생각한 일은 여러 가지 가 잇스나 우선 우리들이 본떠야 할 일은 어린 아희 기루는 일에 유의하여야 할 일이다.
우리 朝鮮사람도 저녀를 사랑하지 안는 것이 안이다. 맛난 음식을 멕이고 조흔 의복을 지여 입히고 남보다 잘 자라는 것을 누구나 企望한다.
朝鮮사람들은 옷 해입히고 밥 해멕여서 학교에 나보내면 부모의 책임이다.- 되는 줄만 안다. 米國사람들도 자녀의게 맛잇는 음식과 조흔 의복을 해입히고 잘 자라나기를 바란다. 이 점에 잇서서는 우뤀¬네와 다를 것이 업다.
그러나 米國人은 우리네보다 어린 자녀들을 여러 가지 방면으로 중대시하고 이들을 위하야 먹고 입히는 외에 깁히 연구하며 사려하며 謹愼한다.
米國人들은 어린 자녀를 오는 압날의 사회를 향상식히고 발전식히고 주장할 국민으로 생각하는 관념이 굿게 백혀 잇다.
이것으로 인하야 어린 아동에게 대한 일체의 행동은 아동에게 유해되지 안토록 삼간다.
朝鮮사람은 술 먹고 담배 먹는 일은 납븐 일이라고 생각하고 또는 어린 자녀에게도 그와 가티 가르키면서도 실제에 잇서서는 아희들 보는데서 술 먹고 담배 먹고 한다. 一言으로 하면 朝鮮人은 아동교육을 학교교육만으로 만족하게 생각하나 사람되는 범절에 잇서서 학교교육만으로는 완전하다고 퓀  수 업다.
아동이 제일 만히 부졉하여 잇는 곳은 가정이니 아동교육에 주의하여야 할 곳은 가정이다. 가정은 부모가 주장하는 터이니 부모가 누구보다도 먼저 선생이 되어야 할 것이다.
그리하야 장성한 이들은 一動一靜을 삼가고 주의하여야 하겟다.
米國人은 첫재로 어린 아해에게 거짓말을 하지 안는다. 둘재로 타일늘 때에는 어린 아해의 정신에나 감정에 악영향을 줄 언사는 절대로 사용치 안코 그 일의 可否善惡을 해득하도록 한다. 셋재로는 가정 내에서도 어린 아해들의 권리를 시인하야 어린 아해들의 주장이라도 아동자체에 이로운 것이면 자기네의 의견을 버리고 어린 아해의 주장에 복종한다.＜75＞ 이런 일은 個性의 신념과 자존심 함양에 지대한 관계가 잇다.
가정 내에서나 학교에서는 하는 일을 일일이 들어다-말할수 업스나 우리는 무엇보다도 어린 아해들을 사랑하지만 말고 존경하는 일을 힘써 배우고 아동을 가정이나 사회나 국가로나 엇더한 견지로 보던지 존귀하여야 할 것임을 참으로 깨닷고 아동에 대한 종래에 가젓든 정신보다 휠신 文明한 정신을 가저야 할 것을 在米時 나는 절실히 늣기고 아울너 니 기회에 여러분에게 권하다.

米國人制度中 模倣하고 십흔 것
金佑枰

외국 학창 생활을 통하여 余 가본 것 생각한 것에서 우리의 생활제도에 응용하면 실행될 만한 그 무엇이든지 잇스면 소개하라는 言託에 의하야 讀者諸賢과 가티 試考의 재료가 될 만한 것을 논하랴 한다.

우리의 사회생활의 一五一什에 우리의 의사대로 시설 혹은 진전되는 것은 殆無하다 하야도 과언이 아니다. 如斯히 참담한 경우에서 여러 가지의 곤란을 경험하는 우리는 너머나 그 불합리한 것을 咀呪하는 一便으로 가정생활에 잇서서도 불합리와 공허하다는 것을 늦기지 아늘 수 업다. 흡사히 가난뱅이의 살님 타령이나 신변 타령과 如한 感이 不少하다. 그야말로 浮世의 塵穢氣를 超脫하야 모든 것을 一粲에 붓치고 고만 둔다하면 찰아리 소극적 만족이나마 잇슬 듯하나 不然하다면 누구나 우리의 생활이 빈약하다는 것을 구비구비 아니 늣길 수 업다.
彼 米國人의 융성한 도회, 시가의 정연하고도 화려한 品이라든지 芳草동산 우에 屹立한 대학이라든지 보기에도 어마어마한 대공장 제도라든지 모다가 부럽다. 그러나 如斯한 생활이 彼 米國人에게만 국한된 것이 아닐 것이다. 우리도 彼等이 비용한 그 시간 노동 자본 및 인내력을 자유로 試할기회가 잇다면 기술이라든지 성적은 오히려 우승할는지도 모를 것이다. 그러나 如詞히 과히 힘드지 안는 유리한 가설은 姑舍하고＜77＞ 現下 우리의 처지로서 수입하야 응용하면 즉시라도 실행할 만한 것은 무엇일가?
余는 彼 米國人의 간단하고 규범잇는 가정생활의 근거가 되는 가계제도라 하겟다. 米國은 현대기계문명이 가장 발달된 국가로서 彼等의 생활은 간단하고 규범잇다 하겟스나 一步를 진하야 생각하야 보면 彼等은 가정생활에 『틤웍』“Team Work"의 원리를 활용하는 까닭이다. 우리는 『틤웍』에 대한 의식이 부족하다. 우리는 『틤웍』의 원리부터 활용하자. 즉 분업 통일 및 통제의 원리를 활용하자는 것이다.
우리의 생활을 간단화하며 질서 잇는 생활의 善習慣을 양성함으로 無用한 시간과 노력을 경제하자는 말이다.
우리의 가정에는 대개는 그 직능에 잇서서 남편이 주부인지 주부가 남편인지 말하자면 가사관계에 잇서서 분업선이 미약하다. 그럼으로 생활에 통일이 無하고 통제력을 缺한 그것이 되어 질서 업시 공연히 복잡하기만 하다.
그럼으로 余는 米國人의 가정과 如히 규모 잇는 생활을 하기 위하야 우리의 가정에서도 무엇보다도 먼저 가계(Family Budget) 제도의 수립을 실행하얏스면 하는 생각이다. 남편의 수입을 전부를 부부간에 등균이 분배하야 부부간에 작정된 가계총액을 相伴하게 부담하자는 것이다. 즉 數字例를 擧하면 이러하다. 일가정의 수입이 每箇月 평균 150원이라고 하고 1개월 가계의 총액은 1백원이라 하자. 그러면 수입인 150원을 부부간에 75원식을 분배하야 1백원의 가계비용을 50원식 분담하고 그 餘의 25원식을 부부 각자의 이용에 충당하자는 것이다. 如斯히 일가의 수입을 부부간에 균일히 분배하야 가계를 균등하게 부담하자는 것이 (1) 부부의 책임감이 강하게 되는 동시에 부부간에 물질적 독립감을 가지게 되는 것이다. 일가의 수입이 전혀 남편 1인의 노력에 대한 報酬가 아니다. 처가 炊事 세탁 자년생산 및 교육등 가사를 정리함으로 남편이 능률잇게 활동하게 되는 것이다. 그럼으로 처가 그 수입의 반에 대한 권리를 所持하얏다고 하는 것이다. 이리하야 夫가 婦에 대한 것과 婦가 夫에 대한 책임감이 강하게 되며 따라서 부부각자의 물질적 독립감을 가지게 되는 동시에 남편이 처에 대한 그 소위 高等下女感이 업게 되며 互相 인격적 존엄의 존재를 표현하게 되는 事.
(2) 夫나 婦의 濫費를 방지하는 동시에 검소와 저축적 습관을 소지할 수 잇는 事. (3) 따라서 자녀에게 절제적 생활의 교훈이 가능한 事. (4 )一家庭생활부터 분업 통일 및 통제의 생활을 勵行하랴는 善習慣으로 사회생활에까지 응용하게 되는 때문이다.

如斯한 생활제도는 일가 수입의 다소를 불구하고 실행하기 가능한 것이다. 반드시 생활개선이라 하야 그 소위 근일 유행하는 문화주택이나 2,3층 양옥을 건축한 것만이 아니다. 單間 茅屋에서 생활할지라도 먼저 家計제도부터 실현하자.＜78＞
