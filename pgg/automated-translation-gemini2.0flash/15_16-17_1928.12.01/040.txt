잡지명	別乾坤 第16·17號
발행일	1928년 12월 01일
기사제목	孫逸仙과 袁世凱의 地下面談記, (=黃泉探訪記에서) (Sun Yat-sen and Yuan Shikai's Underground Interview, (From the Record of a Visit to the Underworld))
필자	金振九
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

Sun Yat-sen and Yuan Shikai's Underground Interview,
Kim Jin-gu

In Donghwawon, Pyongyang. After drinking 36-degree Pyongyang soju all night at the Chinese pavilion, I suddenly became a newly invented human bird. Wings sprouted from both armpits. How could a human-insect who only ate cooked food be so light? My body became a light swallow, and riding on an invisible thread, I floated higher and higher. Instantly, I crossed the Yellow Sea westward, briefly ascended to Yueyang Tower to admire the autumn moon over the lake, glanced at the ancient relics of Boyi and Shuqi on Shouyang Mountain, mourned the two widows at the Huangling Temple, and arrived at the Qin Emperor's Mausoleum on the ridge of Lishan Mountain. The saying, "How many events, past and present, are all entrusted to jokes" would be useless here. Under the ambition of "passing it down to the Second Generation, Third Generation, even ten thousand generations, without end," outwardly, to block the fierce and strong Northern Xiongnu, he scraped together the blood and bones of a million people to build the Great Wall. Inwardly, to eliminate those smart and theoretical scholars, he "burned books and buried scholars alive," and to eradicate stubborn and heroic popular unrest, he confiscated all weapons, even iron tools from the common people. Sitting high in the Afang Palace, indulging in every pleasure of the wine pool and meat forest, even the great hero Qin Shi Huang, armed with bamboo spears and wooden knives, was crushed with bare hands and met a miserable fate of being destroyed in the Second Generation by the mob of Chen Sheng and Wu Guang. Alas! Alas!! Qin Shi Huang, who was once a hero of his age, has now become a desolate mound of earth!

The whistling of autumn pines is mournful, the roaring of tigers and leopards echoes through the mountains, and the herdsmen's songs resound through the valleys. Even though I have wings, my chest suddenly felt cold, and my hair stood on end. There was nowhere to escape. In that instant, stepping backward, my feet slipped and I fell down a cliff ten thousand feet high. For some reason, I couldn't run, and I couldn't even fly.
<11>
I don't know how long I fell, terrified, into a ravine, exhausted. After about an hour, the clear sound of a jade flute melody floated from afar. Even in my dazed state, this clear tune touched my auditory nerves and brought a pulse of life to my exhausted body. I half-opened my heavy eyes and looked around. In the dark ravine, the rustling autumn wind invaded me. After the sound of the jade flute stopped abruptly, the surroundings were desolate and returned to a melancholy atmosphere. Where on earth is this place? At the age of thirty, I have visited heaven and hell, private detention centers that are human hells, and all sorts of hardships and sufferings, leaving no place unvisited. But the environment is strange, and the air is eerie. This is the first time I have seen such a hell. What are the three thousand worlds, the five directions, and the eight divisions? Now that I am in this situation, let's fight with all my strength. I firmly resolved, cast aside my fear, shook off my weariness, and headed towards the light. I couldn't even breathe deeply, and I crept along like a squirrel, trying not to make any noise. When I reached one spot, something suddenly struck my head. My mind went blank, and I collapsed on the spot. Regaining my senses, I looked up, and suddenly, the cave became a world of light. My eyes were too dazzled to see. Under the flags and spears, five hundred Arhats and eight guardian deities lined up, bowing their heads to me again. Still prostrate, I asked, "Tell me, where is this place?" The guardian deity replied in a commanding voice, "Shh! This is the Yellow Emperor's Temple!" I retorted, "Then let me see it. Why shouldn't I, who was born into this world, be allowed to see the Yellow Emperor's Temple?" I shouted rebelliously. The guardian deity chuckled and said, "You cannot enter. This is a place where only Han Chinese can enter, especially not someone of your Hu lineage..." Hearing these words, I became so enraged that I shouted, "You call me Hu! To call our Baedal people, descendants of sacred ancestors, Hu is an absolute insult! Let's duel! Duel! Let's duel!" I unleashed my Korean spirit. The guardian deity smiled faintly and said, "You, a mere commoner, duel? What duel..." I became so furious that the fire reached the top of my head. "You fool! You ignorant fool! You call me Hu, but your people have always been conquered by our race. Why don't you know that, even in modern history, you were all subdued by the Liao, Jin, and Qing? You idiot! Even so, did you ever come to our land and rule and exterminate our people for even a hundred years? Aren't you ashamed, you ignorant Han Chinese nobleman?" As I shouted these words, my speech became frantic, and I was like a madman. The air inside seemed to be greatly disturbed. "Shh—who is making such an impudent noise? Seize him immediately and await orders!" A stern command came from inside the palace. The Arhats lined up on both sides opened their scarlet mouths and rolled their bulging eyes, pouncing on me like eagles catching sparrows, and dragged me before the殿下 (jeonha, Your Majesty). I was caught with varying degrees of force, but my anger was fierce, and my chest heaved. "What kind of vulgar person are you, daring to defile this sacred immortal realm?" I raised my head and looked at the殿上 (jeonsang, the royal dais), declaring, "I am a descendant of the altar, who came to see the underworld. I have accidentally trespassed on this strange and strict border, so punish me according to my crime." I spoke with courage. An old scholar in formal attire, standing to the right of the seat, meticulously scrutinized my face, then came down to the殿角 (jeongak, corner of the hall), grabbed my shoulder, and said, "Mr. Kim! Aren't you Mr. Kim, whom I briefly met in Kobe, Japan, five years ago?" His voice was very warm and gentle. Looking closely, it was Sun Wen, whom I had met in Kobe for about 20 minutes, under the introduction of Toyama Mitsuru, to hear the news of the Eastern dignitaries, when I went to Shanghai on my way to Beijing to meet with the two giants (Zhang Zuolin) at the National Representatives Conference in the 11th month of the 甲子 (gapja) year, winter, and stayed in Kobe for a week to have some talks with the Japanese representatives. How delighted I was! I grabbed his wrist with both hands and exclaimed, "Mr. Sun!" Meeting such a teacher unexpectedly in the harsh underworld was like meeting Buddha in hell.

The Two Heroes' Heartfelt Conversation

Sun: "Mr. Kim! How did you come to a place like this?"

Me: "I came here by accident, and now I am in this state. As a vulgar person of the mortal world, my crime of defiling the immortal realm deserves ten thousand deaths, but I especially beg for your benevolent disposition."

Sun: "What disposition? The underworld is no longer like the old feudal times. It is now completely open. We have been working our hardest to democratize even this much with these thousands-year-old antiques."

Me: "Teacher, you are truly the great benefactor of people's liberation. You dedicated your whole life to liberating the 40 million Chinese people during your lifetime, and now you are working to reform the underworld after your death. Truly, truly, you are an absolute great man through both the living and the dead. But from what I have experienced, the dignity is very strict."

Sun: "That's why I'm worried. I still have those 3,400-year-old military officials like Meng Ben, Niao Huo, Fan Qiang, and Zhang Da, who haven't shed their old shells yet. But looking only at this underworld, people like Chiang Kai-shek and Feng Yuxiang, who are somewhat accustomed to the new trends, should come and become gatekeepers soon. Even though this is the Yellow Emperor's Temple, and when I first raised the revolutionary army, I advocated destroying the Manchus and reviving the Han, that was just a temporary weapon to overthrow the then 朝廷 (chongjeong, imperial court) (Manchu). <14> Now, is there any reason to have racial discrimination or border issues? Especially to you, an Eastern aspiring youth... Come here! Sit here! Although it's a seat at the end, sit here for a while and watch. You can't sit here for long..."

He grabbed my wrist and kindly guided me. After quietly settling down, I looked around the殿堂 (jeondang, main hall). In the center, facing forward, the Yellow Emperor Xuanyuan sat solemnly and majestically. To the left and right, revolutionary heroes who once swept through an era, such as Hwang Heong, Song Gyoin, Tang Caichang, Li Yuanhong, and Cai E, sat in a row. The music of ancient instruments filled the air, and the atmosphere of the underworld was newly refined. The phoenix danced, and the trees flourished. This kind of elegant and refreshing atmosphere is something we can never taste in the human sea of suffering. Soon, a strange sound came from the sky, and a military official gently descended on the backs of twin cranes. Until now, the atmosphere of harmony suddenly turned into a atmosphere of肅殺 (suksall,肅殺), of sternness. The air became extremely tense. Hwang Heong, who was sitting on the堂上 (dangsang, the platform), gnashed his teeth and drew his sword, striking the殿角 (jeongak, corner of the hall) and shouting like thunder. The military official who landed in the courtyard bowed respectfully and said, "Please, General Hwang, calm your anger a bit. We were once irreconcilable enemies when we were in the human world, but today, after completely repenting of our past mistakes, we cannot come here and settle old scores. I have come down here today because I see you all gathered here, enjoying yourselves peacefully, and I am envious, as you are all successful and I am a failure. I want to apologize to you all for my past mistakes."

Looking closely, it was clearly Yuan Shikai, whom I had seen many times in photographs, when he was about to become the Hongxian Emperor. His words, accompanied by tears, were sincere in every sentence. His words and expressions undoubtedly showed repentance. Sun Wen, watching silently, comforted Hwang Heong, who was glaring with anger, saying, "That's right! 'How many events, past and present, are all entrusted to jokes,' that's all it is. Why should we think of old grudges now? Come up here quickly."

He ran down to the hall barefoot, grabbed Yuan's wrist affectionately, shook hands warmly, and then led him to the hall. He shook hands with everyone in turn, without any reservation, and gave him a seat in the ranks. The atmosphere in the hall was extremely quiet for a moment. It became so quiet that not even a cough could be heard. Soon, breaking the silence in the hall, a sobbing sound was heard. Everyone looked in that direction. Yuan Shikai's face was covered with a handkerchief. Cai E, who was quick to speak, pursed his lips as if he was very displeased.

"Why so suddenly now..."

Song Gyoin, who was sitting next to him, still hadn't resolved his old grudges. <14>

"That obstacle to the revolution! A lump of treacherous and cunning wisdom!"

He shouted loudly. Sun Wen, who was famous for being smooth in all situations, covered Song's mouth with his hand and earnestly dissuaded him with his eyes and hands. Li Yuanhong, who was known for his well-roundedness, whispered into Song and Cai's ears,

"Hey, that's too much. Don't pursue a desperate foe. Now that he has completely repented of his past mistakes and is shedding hot tears because of his conscience, why keep chasing him?"

The atmosphere in the hall returned to silence. Soon, from Sun Wen's solemn mouth came the words,

"Look, Mr. Yuan! Why is there so much to repent about? It was one time then, and this is another time now. At that time, there was no one in the Chinese world who could match you in prestige or trust, so you became a slave to arrogance, self-respect, and ambition without having time to consider the overall situation. There seems to be nothing to be so upset about! "

He consoled him with gentle words and soothing tones. From among the seated, a Taoist with the spirit of an immortal stepped forward.

"That figure is ultimately a traitor to the people! His crime did not end in China but extended far to 朝鮮 (Joseon, Korea). He listened only to treacherous words and recklessly and violently lured Daewongun (大院君), a hero of the East overflowing with patriotic fervor, and imprisoned him in Baoding, creating a world of hens in Joseon. Three years later, he brutally suppressed the Gapsin Coup (甲申改革), creating a dark world of conservative Sadae, committing all sorts of harsh actions. He nipped the new sprouts of the Joseon masses in the bud, who were awakening to a new spirit with the crowing of the dawn rooster coming from the West, rubbing their eyes, and seeking freedom as the dawn broke. How can his crime be expiated?"

He listed the charges one by one, and the tears of sadness disappeared somewhere. After wiping away his tears, he straightened his appearance and confessed the past honestly, as if deeply moved.

"When I first set foot on Joseon, following Wu Changqing at the age of 24, the Min clan (閔族) was declining and Daewongun's reign was in power, so I thought this would not do. As a result, it was upon the recommendation of me and Kim Yunsik (金允植) and Eo Yunjung (魚允中) that I devised a treacherous plan to have Daewongun come aboard a Qing warship, and it was my courage that resolutely carried out what the Easterners could not easily do... And then, seeing that the 궁중府中 (gungjung bujung, the royal court) of Joseon had returned to darkness, I felt happy. Liu Da-zhi, an old young man, fueled the fire from behind, awakening the new elite of Kim Ok-gyun (金玉均), Park Yeong-hyo (朴泳孝), and Seo Gwang-beom (徐光範). I couldn't let this go, so I secretly received the backing of Li Hongzhang (李鴻章) and disguised soldiers as merchants, and suppressed the first beacon of independence and reform with a large army to avoid a life of servitude. No trace of such elements remained, and no matter how clever they were, women were women, so I held them tightly in my grasp and danced at will. My reputation and trust became brighter day by day. And then, during my 12 years in Korea as駐韓 (juhan, stationed in Korea), I got rid of 5 or 6 Japanese ministers (Hanabusa Yoshitada, Inoue Kaoru, Komura Jutarō, Ōishi Masami, Ōtori Keisuke), but those guys were not worth mentioning. I just wanted to ride the favorable wind and pursue glory and fame, so I exhausted all my flattery to Li Hongzhang and the Empress Dowager. If sincerity touches heaven, the Empress Dowager's favor was extraordinary! In such a smooth path to glory, what was the world situation, and what was the point of insisting on consistency? This is the major cause that ruined my life!"
<15>
His words faded. Hwang Heong, whose martial spirit was uncontrollable, thrust his fist under Yuan's chin and shouted,

"You cunning rogue! After tens of thousands of people's blood and tears were united to achieve the great cause of unification for over a dozen years, how dare you suddenly appear and ignore the will of the people, saying that you would become the Hongxian Emperor and that your son, Ke-ding, would become the crown prince, and that you would enshrine the eight concubines, including Lady Yin (印夫人), whom you stole from Joseon, as imperial concubines? Your face was so disgusting that I couldn't bear to look at it."

He clicked his tongue. Yuan Shikai, the great hero who once dominated the Central Plains and tried to command the world, had become a weak woman sitting in this place. A blush, as if dyed with vermilion, overflowed his entire face. Tang Caichang, who was only witty, patted his hands and laughed, as if he was overly embarrassed.

"General Hwang's words are too harsh. Yuan's restoration movement was an error of the times. It is plausible to reproach him for ignoring the will of the people, but what does it matter if he appoints a crown prince or enshrine an imperial concubine? After going to Joseon and seeing Pyongyang's famous courtesan, Yin Hyang-ran (印香蘭), he was so captivated and terrified, falling head over heels for her voluptuous figure, neglecting all military affairs. That's the true nature of a hero. Why shouldn't he enshrine her as an imperial concubine, let alone appoint a crown prince?"

His tone was so humorous that the entire hall burst into laughter. Yuan Shikai also smiled. The gloomy atmosphere of the hall suddenly became harmonious. As if sweeping away the walls of the east and west rooms, a pair of children came forward to present fine wine and divine fruit. Cups and dishes were set out, and lively conversations ensued. The dignified Sun Yat-sen raised his glass, offered it to Yuan, and said earnestly,

"Once I met Mr. Yuan, we had an unreserved exchange of feelings, but you all have been so harsh that I have swallowed all my words! We several people poured out our hearts and took on all sorts of hardships, and after achieving the great cause with the blood of many comrades, we entrusted it to you with the unanimous agreement of the public because you still had trust among the officials and people. But in the end, your actions turned into a reactionary movement, and our enterprise was undermined by a single basket of earth that you threw at the nine-fathom tower. How can we not be resentful? If we had realized the Three Principles of the People after the Nanjing Conference and shook hands firmly, the Chinese world would have been settled long ago without seeing the past dozen years of internal treasury and foreign invasion, and it would have become a national power that could compete with the world powers by now. But in the end, you tried to satisfy your individual ambitions, failed to do so, and only created warlords who were dividing the country, so that the deep-rooted evils have not been easily resolved, and foreign insults have become increasingly severe, and the face and dignity of the 40 million people of the great nation have fallen into a ditch. Although the past cannot be chased, my bones ache when I think of my homeland. However, now there is no need to think about such things, and everything seems to be progressing smoothly, so our doctrine based on the people will always be victorious, sooner or later. There is no need to say such things, but it is just a 술席聞話 (sulseok munhwa, words heard at a drinking party). I am just telling you this and that. Please don't take offense."
<16>
Yuan: "..." (This section of ten lines has been deleted - original text)

Sun: "Who is without fault? It is valuable to correct it, and it is enough if you have repented of your past mistakes that much. It is difficult for people to avoid being controlled by the environment, so you were also controlled by the environment at that time. Without mentioning the past, aren't we now excellent comrades without any reservations? From now on, let us support our great cause for the ancient nation and help us greatly. When Chiang Kai-shek arranges for the meeting, he is quite capable. Seeing that he skillfully manipulates both Lü (Lv Zhiyi) and Feng (Feng Yuxiang) to organize a perfect Five-Yuan system, his capacity and ambition do not end with just being a military man, which is so admirable in my heart... But it cannot be said that it is still perfect and flawless. Shouldn't we, the predecessors, constantly look after you from the underworld? Mr. Yuan!"

Hearing these words, Yuan rushed up and embraced Sun, rubbing his cheek against his, and crying loudly with the tears of a man. Indeed, only great men were gathered here. And in the face of Sun Yat-sen's earnest conversation, Yuan's chest had burst open, and he could not contain the tears of 感泣 (gamgyeop, 感泣), of emotion coming from his conscience. There was no one among the gathered heroes who watched this scene without shedding a tear of compassion. (This section of 12 lines has been deleted - original text) Soon, Yuan, who had wiped away his tears, silently rose, went down to the階下 (gyedan, the base of the stairs), bowed to the seated crowd, and boarded the back of a crane. Sun Yat-sen, who was reluctant to let him go, asked,

"Where are you going now?"

"I will briefly visit our old home, 河南圭塘 (Henan Guitang), and look over the 圭塘詩集 (Guitang Poetry Collection) (Yuan's poetry collection) edited by my second son, Ju-mun, and then go to heaven or hell! With so many sins, I can't go to heaven, so I'll go to hell!"

As soon as he finished speaking, he disappeared without a trace. The atmosphere on the stage was filled with a sense of desolation. I also rose and bid farewell with 長揖不拜 (jangipubae, a deep bow), and Sun Wen and Hwang Heong followed me out, shook hands warmly, and gave me affection.

Sun: "Don't just envy China's things too much! Soon, XX will also XXX."

Hwang: "I only entrust you with these eight characters: 不折不屈 勇往邁進 (bujulbulgul yongmaengmaejin, unwavering, unyielding, courageous, and advancing)."

The two men simultaneously tapped my shoulder and gripped my wrist tightly. Startled by the pain, I stretched out my legs and kicked over the liquor table, shattering it with a crash! "Oh—this guy sleeps harshly..." Opening my eyes wide, I realized it was just a 南柯一夢 (namgaeilmeng, a fleeting dream).

Damn it, I almost became a ghost in the underworld because of this Pyongyang soju. The house is still Donghwa Gwajeong (東華科亭). On the walls, there is only the couplet of Sun Yat-sen's遺訓 (yuhun, last words): "革命尙未成功同志伋湏努力 (gyeongmyeong sangmi seonggong dongji gipsu noryeok, the revolution has not yet succeeded, comrades must continue to work hard)." <17>
