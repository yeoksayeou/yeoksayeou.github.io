A Sincere Request to Unmarried Men and Women
My Marriage Confession - Advice to Unmarried Virgins -
Pak O-hui

Beloved younger sisters!
I am writing this to share my failed past with you, hoping to serve as a mirror so you don't repeat my mistakes.
Unmarried virgin... just the words themselves evoke the beautiful memories of my past and resonate with me.
Especially when I see you, walking lightly on the streets filled with the spring air, your cheeks blooming with innocence as if you're proud of being virgins, I feel like I'm meeting my younger self again, and I smile wistfully as I watch you from behind.
If you walk close to the streetcar tracks, I feel like rushing over to lightly tap your shoulders and warn you to be careful of the streetcar, and if a car recklessly speeds behind you, I worry that you might get hurt.
Beloved younger sisters!
Your eyes are clear and pure, and your smiles are without sorrow, only filled with happiness.
I pray that you remain the eternal owners of those smiles and eyes. <12>
May your eyes not be clouded by fog like mine, and may your smiles not be mixed with desolate echoes like mine.

Thinking about it, it was already ten years ago. When I was only eighteen years old...
Back then, I didn't know sadness or pain like you do now, and I saw the world through the clear eyes of a virgin, and I laughed happily with an unblemished smile.
That was probably the golden age of my life.
Taking that time as a dividing line, my life began to decline pitifully, like the sun setting in the west, after a sudden burst of joy quickly faded away at the peak.
I said, "a sudden burst of joy." That was nothing other than falling in love with a man named S, who is now my husband.
It happened and disappeared so quickly that I call it a sudden burst of joy.
Beloved younger sisters!
When you meet and interact with young men,
"That person is a young member of the opposite sex - a man."
"I am also a young member of the opposite sex - a woman."
If you have such thoughts, you will not be able to properly observe that man.
In other words, you should not have preconceived notions of sexual distinction (性的區別의 先入感).
Because of this preconceived notion of sexual distinction, you either underestimate or overestimate the man.
If you underestimate him, you won't approach him further, but if you overestimate him, it means you have already taken a step closer to him.
I made this initial mistake (第一步). If I hadn't had preconceived notions of sexual distinction when I first met S, or even if I had, if I had underestimated him (of course, I'm not telling you to look down on others), I wouldn't be suffering so much now.
S seemed like a very good man. As a result, my face flushed more than usual when a virgin meets a man, and as I briefly looked up at him again, my eyes met his, which were smiling gently.
From then on, his value only increased. The more I saw him, the more I liked everything he did, and I felt happy and joyful.
His words were much more memorable than the lessons (學課) that teachers explained until their throats were sore, and they all sounded like the truth.
Moreover, he seemed to know so much, and he spoke so eloquently about literature, as if he knew all the names and works of the writers who had ever lived in this world, as well as their contents.
His friends looked outstanding next to him, like disciples attending a great Buddha, and his ambitions seemed like they would reach the sky in the future.
His occasional melancholy moods were as attractive as the temptations of spring.
And his physique seemed very robust.
Of course, this was my observation of him, and all my feelings had already been directed towards him.
More than anything, what captivated me was his delicate emotions and moods, which stemmed from his poetic nature.
He was sentimental and resentful, yet strong, oppressive yet soft as wool. <13>
Beloved younger sisters!
If you are in a relationship, I urge you to allow at least one year between dating and marriage.
I'm not saying don't date.
Dating is good. It's the pride and privilege of youth.
However, dating and marriage are different. Dating is preparation for marriage. Therefore, it's good to learn from dating, but there's no law that says you must marry just because you've dated.
Generally, dating obscures reason. To clear this obscured reason, I suggest allowing a year between dating and marriage. If you have chosen an unsuitable partner due to dating, you will have enough time to discover the unsuitability during that year.
If there is nothing unsuitable, then you can marry at that time; if there is, then break it off.
Beloved younger sisters!
I acted too rashly.
I opened my heart to S within a month of meeting him, and we held our wedding ceremony two months later.
After the intense romance passed and we entered married life, the tension eased, and life became ordinary, like a rubber balloon that was stretched taut when filled with air but loses its strength and droops when the air is released, leaving only unsightly wrinkles.
Consequently, S's true nature was revealed to me (and S probably discovered my true nature then too).
The kingdom of praise that I had discovered in him and dreamed of within him was miserably destroyed.
His delicate poetic nature turned out to be an artistic (?) expression of his inherently mischievous and eccentric personality.
The addition of decadent sentiments to this mischievous and eccentric nature was what made him seem so sentimental and resentful.
The "melancholy mood" that had seduced me was frustration at not being able to freely express his individualism due to ideological oppression. In other words, he was a modern Hamlet.
His ideals of saving the world (濟世의 理想) and his claims of the impossibility of XX that he uttered before my eyes every day were ultimately just "grumbling" that revolved around the lips of a frustrated seeker of fame, which seemed like a strong personality to me at the time, and what seemed soft as wool was actually a powerless softness like taffy in May and June. His knowledge was too unsystematic. He was too frail for his physique.
Beloved younger sisters!
S was such a person. There are many such S's before your eyes.
I urge you to allow time between dating and marriage.

Beloved younger sisters!
If you have an unhappy marriage, I urge you to tear up that empty document as soon as possible and bravely escape that sacrifice.
Getting married once is by no means the end of the road in life.
I have been living a tomb-like married life with S for the past ten years.
Ugly me, pressed down by misguided notions, cannot escape this place.
But beloved younger sisters!
You are people who are already ten years "ahead of the times" (時代의 압).
The notions that a first marriage or chastity is the end or life itself do not threaten you.
I am not telling you to treat marriage or chastity like straw.
I am only saying don't be slaves to outdated customs.

Beloved younger sisters!
You are the masters of a new generation. Light lies before you.
Of course, I am not telling you to devote your entire lives to vulgar eroticism. <15>
What I am saying only pertains to a part of your individual lives.

The Unemployed are Unqualified
Man Nosaeng

The issue of marriage is one of the most important issues in human life. Whether viewed personally or socially, the rise and fall of a person depends on how well they resolve this issue. However, since this is mostly driven by instincts, it cannot be controlled uniformly by any morality or law, so the morality or law regarding this issue varies with the times or countries. Also, the fact that most lawsuits appearing in law are related to sexual issues shows that this is the most complex of many social problems.
In advanced civilized countries, the doctrine of supreme love (戀愛至上主義) is advocated, and so-called love marriages (戀愛結婚) are very popular, but in Russia, a free marriage system has already been established, and legally, a notification to the registry office (法所) is all that is needed to marry and divorce freely.
In the rising class (新興階級), class marriage (階級結婚) is advocated, saying that proletarians must only unite with those who have a thorough proletarian consciousness (푸로意識), and in the United States, a so-called fraternal marriage (友愛結婚) has recently been advocated and seems to be promoted in Japan as well. This mainly uses production as the standard, so before production, men and women are free to unite and separate without any responsibility between them, but once the union of the two sexes leads to production, there is an obligation to marry. And of course, once married, there is an absolute responsibility between the two for life, which is no different from the modern system.
I am not trying to criticize the pros and cons of all the above systems in this article, but whatever system of marriage is used, there are special circumstances for Koreans in Korea. We are not people who have enough to eat and wear. Or, no matter where we go or what we do, there are not many cases where one person can earn enough wages to feed five, six, seven, or eight family members. Therefore, men and women who want to marry must have jobs that can share the joint responsibility. And even those who have somewhat sufficient living abilities are like a candle in the wind under the huge economic power. Most have a fate of destruction. Our assets are only as strong as the special skills of each individual. We must have a job that sells our skills, even for a small salary, according to our abilities. Moreover, we cannot always think that our lives will be comfortable. If any misfortune befalls either spouse, if the family has been living off the job of one person, the misfortune of that one person is the same as ending the lives of the entire family. "I can't do anything because I'm tied to my family (家庭에 매여서)" is something we hear frequently, but if such "ties to the family" hinder everything for us, how much of a loss would that be overall? Even if there are people who struggle without looking back at such families, how many people can we count who turn over the entire society, and even if we count these few people, what is there to say about their value in society, but how irresponsible is it to their families? We will strive to quickly awaken modern so-called new women (新女性) who make the misdiagnosis that they have solved all their problems in life once they get married,
"People who do not have a job between men and women are not qualified to marry."
"Even if you live as a single person for the rest of your life, do not have an irresponsible marriage that cannot guarantee your livelihood."
I think it is necessary to put up slogans and impose social sanctions.

Three Conditions
Ko Yŏng-han

Write an article to unmarried people who are about to get married, based on what married men and women have experienced in their married lives—a very interesting story. I would like to offer the following three conditions regarding this.
1. Economic Conditions
2. Understanding and Love
3. Physiological Conditions

1. Marriage is a combination of two sexes, putting the spiritual aspects aside as a separate issue and observing it coldly from a biological perspective. This combination of two sexes inevitably leads to the creation of a family, the birth of sons and daughters, and after the birth of children, they must be raised and educated well. This is probably the common form of marriage, so if you want to fulfill your responsibilities after marriage, first of all, it is essential to resolve the economic conditions (經濟的 條件), so I want to tell anyone who wants to get married to prepare to make a living first.
Just as the courtesans (花柳) say, "You can't live on love alone," no matter how deep the love between a couple, they cannot live without money, so there is no need to say how many marital breakdowns and family tragedies occur due to economic relations. Therefore, if you are a person who sees reality straight, you must think about economic marriage (經濟結婚) before free love (自由戀愛) or free marriage (自由結婚).

2. Also, understanding and love are essential conditions that must be present in married life. Understanding is easy to say but difficult in practice, and love seems common in the world, but in reality, true love is rare. Understanding and love include various meanings such as compromise, forgiveness, and sacrifice, and without these, you cannot continue a smooth married life. No matter how understanding and loving a couple is in the world, they cannot help but sometimes cause domestic disputes. In such cases, couples with deep understanding and love will find ways to reach a smooth resolution, but for couples who do not have that, it becomes a direct or indirect cause of various tragedies. Suspecting each other and fighting is worse than not getting married, so if you want to get married, you must first think about understanding and loving your partner well.

3. Living conditions (生活的 條件) are absolutely necessary in married life. First of all, when choosing a spouse, you must know their nature, and you must also see if their physical conditions are equal to yours. You must also consider various factors such as the presence or absence of other diseases, age, health, and heredity. Couples who are combined ignoring the physiological conditions (生理的 條件) will never get good results, and as a result, various life tragedies or even crimes occur, which are not uncommon examples in the world.
What I have said above is what I have always thought and experienced frankly, and I believe that if you think carefully about those three conditions, you will not make many mistakes when getting married. <18>

결혼문제는 인간생활에 잇서서 가장 중대한 문제의 하나이다. 개인적으로 보나 사회적으로 보나 이 결혼문제를 잘 해결하고 못하는데서 그의 消長이 달녓다고도 하겟다. 그러나 이것은 인간의 본능에서 소사 나오는 衝動이 대부분을 占함으로 一律로 어떤 도덕이나 法律로 制御할 수 업슴으로 이에 대한 도덕이나 法律이 시대를 따러서 혹은 국가를 따러서 各樣各色인 것은 볼 수 잇스며 또는 法律에 나타나는 訴訟事件의 대부분이 性問題에 關聯된 것으로 보아 許多한 사회문제중에 가장 복잡성을 띄고 잇는 것은 看破할 수 잇다.
선진문명국에서는 戀愛至上主義가 唱導되야 所謂 戀愛結婚이 大流行性을 가지고 잇지만은 露西亞에서는 이미 자유결혼제도가 確立되야 法律上으로도 法所에 通知書 한장이면 자유로 결혼을 할 수 잇고 離婚도 할 수 잇게 되엿다.
新興階級에서는 階級結婚이 提唱되여 포로는 푸로意識이 徹底한 사람끼리 結合치 안으면 아니된다고 하고 米國에서는 近日에 友愛結婚이라는 것이 唱導되여 日本에도 宣傳되는 모양인데 이는 主로 생산을 標準하자면 生産前에는 理性間에 自由로 結合하고 分離하는 것은 何等 彼此間에 책임이 업스나 한번 兩性의 결합으로 말미암아 생산을 하게 되는 때에는 반듯이 결혼을 할 의무가 잇다는 것이다. 그리고 물론 한번 결혼한 以上에는 兩者間에 일생을 통아야 絶對의 責任이 잇는 것은 現代制度와 달을 것이 업다.
이상 모든 制度의 長短點을 들어 批判하랴는 것은 이글의 本意가 아니려니와 何如든 어떠한 制度의 결혼을 하든지 朝鮮에서는 조선사람으로서의 特殊한 사정이 잇다. 우리는 먹을 것이 넉넉하고 입을 것이 充分한 사람이 되지 못한다. 또는 어듸 가서 무슨 일을 하든지 한 사람이 벌어 가지고 5,6식구나 7,8식구를 먹여 살닐만한 賃金을 어더내는 수가 업다. 그럼으로 결혼하랴는 남자나 여자는 반듯이＜16＞ 다 各其 共同責任을 질만한 職業을 갓지 안흐면 아니된다. 그리고 다소 裕足한 생활능력이 잇는 사람이라 할지라도 거대한 經濟勢力미톄는 風前의 燈火이다. 대부분이 破滅의 운명을 가지고 잇다. 우리의 재산은 각 개인의 특수한 기능밧게는 튼튼한 것이 업다. 자기의 所長대로 薄俸이나마 技能을 팔어먹는 직업을 가저야만 되겟다. 더욱이 우리 일생은 언제나 安逸하리라고 생각할 수 업다. 夫婦間에 누구든지 어떠한 불행이 잇슬때에 만일에 한 사람의 직업으로 그 집 食口가 살어나갓다하면 그 한사람의 不幸은 즉 全食口의 생명을 끗는 것이나 한가지다. 『家庭에 매여서』 무슨 일을 할 수 업다는 것은 瀕瀕히 듯는 소리지만은 할것만흔 우리로서 이러한 『家庭에 매여서』 每事에 障害가 된다면 全體的으로 얼마만한 損失이랴 만일에 이러한 가정을 돌아다 보지 안코 분투하는 사람이 잇다할지라도 이러한 사람이 전사회를 들추어서 멧사람이나 꼽을 수 잇스며 이 꼽히는 멧사람이라 할지라도 사회적으로 보아 價値 云云할 일이지 그 家庭에 대하야는 얼마나 無責任한 일이랴. 近代 所謂 新女性이 결혼만하면 자기의 일평생 모든 문제를 해결한 것가치 생각하는 誤診를 하로밧비 깨우처 주기에 노력할 것이며,
『男女間에 職業을 갓지 안은 사람은 結婚할 資格이 업다.』
『一生을 獨身으로 지낼지라도 生活도 保障못할 無責任한 結婚은 하지마라.』
하는 標語를 내걸고 사회적으로 制裁할 필요가 잇다고 생각한다.

旣婚남녀가 자기의 결혼생활에 잇서서 체험하야 어든 바를 가지고 장차 결혼하랴는 未婚者에게 보내는 글을 써라―매우 자미잇는 이약이임니다. 나는 이에 대하야 다음과 가튼 세가지의 조건을 들겟슴니다.
1. 經濟的 條件
2. 理解와 사랑
3. 生理的 條件

1. 결혼이라는 것은 精神的 方面에 대하야는 別問題로 하고 이것을 生物學的으로 냉정히 관찰할진대 兩性 旣男女의 結合에 지나지 안는 것이니 兩性의 결합에는 필연적으로 가정을 일우게 되고 아들딸을 낫케되고 또 子女를 나은 뒤에는 그들을 잘 길느고 교육하지 안으면 아이됨니다. 이것이 아마 결혼에 대한 공통적 형태일 것이니＜17＞ 결혼한 뒤에는 책임을 다하자면 첫재로 經濟的 條件의 해결이 업서서는 안될 것이니 그럼으로 나는 누구던지 결혼을 하랴거든 먼저 먹고 살 준비부터 하여 노은 뒤에 하라고 하고 십슴니다.
『情만 가지고도 살지 못한다』는 花柳*의 格言과 마찬가지로 아모리 사랑이 깁흔 부부라 하여도 돈이 업시는 도저히 살아가지 못하는 것이니 經濟關係로 부부생활의 破綻 家庭悲劇등이 얼마나 만히 이러나는 것은 구터여 여긔에 말할 필요도 업는 것임니다. 그럼으로 實現을 똑바로 보고 사는 사람이면 自由戀愛니 自由結婚이니 하기 전에 먼저 經濟結婚이라는 것을 생각하지 안어서는 안될줄 암니다.

2. 또 그 다음 부부생활에 잇서 理解와 사랑이라는 것은 업서서는 안될 必修 條件이니 理解라는 것이 말로는 쉬우되 事實에 잇서서는 어렵고 사랑이는 것이 세상에 흔한 듯하나 其實 참된 사랑이라는 것은 드문 것임니다. 理解와 사랑속에는 妥協, 容恕, 犧牲등 여러가지 의미도 포함되여 잇는 것이니 이것이 업시는 부부생활을 원만히 繼續하여 갈 주가 업는 것임니다. 세상에 아모리 理解와 사랑이 깁흔 부부라 하여도 혹 때로는 가정 爭議를 이르키지 안는달 수가 업슴니다. 이러한 경우에 理解와 사랑이 깁흔 부부이면 원만한 해결을 어들길이 만흐되 그러치 못한 부부사이에는 그것이 여러가지 비극을 연출하는 直接, 間接의 원인이 됨니다. 서로 疑心하고 싸홈하고 이것은 결혼안한 것만 갓지 못한 일이니 결혼을 하랴거든 먼저 배우자를 잘 理解하고 사랑할 것부터 생각하지 안어서는 안될줄로 암니다.

3. 부부생활에 잇서 生活的 條件이라는 것은 반듯이 필요한 것임니다. 먼저 배우자를 택할 때에 그 사람 形質도 알아야 하는 것이오 또 肉體的 條件이 자기와 均等한가도 보아야 하는 것이며 其他 疾病의 有無 年齡關係 健康與否, 遺傳性의 有無등 여러가지를 생각지 안어서는 안됨니다. 生理的 條件을 無視하고 結合된 부부는 결코 조흔 결과를 엇지 못하는 것이니 그것이 원인으로 여러가지 인생비극과 또는 犯罪까지 이러나는 일도 세상에 드문 例가 안임니다.
이상에 말한 바는 平素에 내가 매양 생각하고 또 경험한 바를 솔직히 말한 것이니 그 세가지 조건만 잘 생각하야 결혼을 한다 하면 별로 蹉跌이 업스리라고 밋슴니다.＜18＞
