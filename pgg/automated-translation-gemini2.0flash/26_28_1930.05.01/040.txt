잡지명 별건곤 제28호

발행일 1930년 05월 01일
기사제목 今春卒業 모던 男女의 結婚理想 (Marriage Ideals of Modern Men and Women Graduating This Spring)
기사형태 문예기타 (Miscellaneous Literature)

This spring, we greet a large number of newcomers who are leaving the school gates with the academic knowledge they have accumulated over a long period of time in the halls of learning and with great ambitions, and are entering society as workers.

From the moment these newcomers leave the school gates, the first problems they all face are jobs and marriage.

Most of the issues regarding jobs have been resolved, but the issue of marriage is something they will have to rectify themselves in the future.

What kind of ideals do they have about marriage?

Understanding Husband
An Pok-hui, Dental Medicine Professional School

I haven't even thought about marriage yet, but well, it's a problem that will come sooner or later.

A spouse? If we understand each other—I guess that would be the central condition—we wouldn't make unreasonable demands like, "It should have been like this or that." <19>

Of course, since I'm a doctor, I think it would be nice if the other person had the same profession. And I think it would be good if they had a cheerful personality. I don't care about their financial situation.

I think it would be good if the man was a bit older.

As for the wedding ceremony, my family is still old-fashioned, so it will be done in the old style. That's because my family is like that, but I think the old style has a classical flavor, even though it's a bit cumbersome.

I don't think it matters whether the other person is divorced or a first-time marriage. If we discriminate against that, people who want to remarry will never get married, right?

Modern Woman is a Big No-No
Eun Jung-gi, Dental Medicine Professional School

Well, now that I'm going to be able to earn a living, I guess I should get married? ... ...

I'm a guy who makes a living pulling teeth, but I want my wife to be someone who can take care of the household—so, no modern women. <20>

If the cost of pulling several teeth a day goes into stocking silk, that won't do.

I'd like her to be nineteen or twenty years old.

As for the ceremony, I'm not a child of God, so I can't go to church. The old style is like a clown show, and there's something called a modern wedding these days. I might choose that. If I find out there's a better way, I'll do it that way.

I Want a Female Teacher
C, Yonhi Professional School

I've heard that literature students have a lot of romantic luck, but I've never even been in love.

I've finished school, and I wish I could get married, but I don't really feel like it. I don't know why, but getting married (I have to get married) makes me sad—like a mother sending her daughter off to get married.

Well ... if I were to choose a spouse, I would marry a female school teacher.

And she shouldn't be too pretty. If she's too pretty, she'll rely on that and look down on me, who's not good-looking.

I'd prefer someone older than me.

I think the feeling and taste of an older woman taking care of a younger husband is really good.

As for the ceremony, I'll do whatever she wants, but if I had my way, I'd go to church because I'm a Christian.

Resilient Women
Ha In-yong, Law Professional School

I haven't even thought about that yet. ...

Well, a spouse ... I'd like an active, resilient woman. Modern? No way ... look at the way I'm made. Would a modern woman suit me? ... <22>

I think the best would be a gentle woman with a resilient, active spirit.

I think the right age would be around my age, or a little younger, around twenty-one or twenty-two.

The ceremony ... well ... I may look classic on the outside, but I'm quite modern on the inside. How can I just repeat what others have done? If I ever get married, I'm going to invent the most cutting-edge, outlandish wedding ceremony.

But why are you asking? Are you trying to arrange a marriage for me?

Country Girls
S. K. Y, Advanced Industry

People say that humans need food and sex, so now that the bread problem seems to be solved, the sex—marriage problem—is coming up before me. But I haven't really thought about it deeply.

If I had my way, I would look for a country girl ( 시골 處女 ) as a wife.

These days, those arrogant girls are always saying this and that, but a country girl is the best for a businessman like me—that is, as a housekeeper ( 살님ㅅ군 ).

If she's honest, gentle, and looks blessed, that's perfect.

I've dated female students for a while, but maybe it's because I'm not modern, but our spirits didn't match at all.

In fact, women in this period of liberation are mostly arrogant, and their insides are empty like rubber balloons.

As for age, there probably aren't any women over 20 in the countryside, so she'd probably be under 20.

The ceremony? I'm not a Christian, so I'm embarrassed to go to church, and a modern wedding ( 社會結婚 ) seems awkward. I think a Korean style ( 朝鮮式 ) with plenty of classical flavor would be good.

Originally, a ceremony should be solemn.

But this is all my ideal now (and as an ideal, I'll try to make it a reality), but it's hard to know if it will really work out that way.

100% Modern Woman

R, Boseong Professional School

A spouse?

That question is already contradictory.

When young people are in love, do they choose someone who fits their ideal of marriage?

No!

Yes, yes, if I were to choose ...

It would be like this. I would choose a 100% modern woman.

Of course, I'm not talking about a pathological modern woman. It's a completely healthy modern woman.

She should be clear, cheerful, refreshing, and always able to live a bright life like a morning day, without any gloomy feelings in her life.

The best age is from 18, 19 to 21 years old. <24>

The ceremony?

The ceremony is not even a problem.

I deny religion and all traditional customs.

So, I would rather die than go to church and worship a non-existent God or do a goblin dance like the old Korean style ( 朝鮮舊式 ).

And there's something called a modern wedding ( 社會結婚 ) these days, but that's also a scam.

Should I have Hwang Seok-u's forest wedding ( 黃錫雨의 林間結婚 )? Haha. ... ... That's a joke. I won't hold a ceremony.

I'm quite a rebel, aren't I?

My Ideal Marriage
KCH, Pharmacy Professional School

If there is someone who has all the conditions I idealize and whom I can have a good feeling for, I will have a normal relationship with that person for more than a year. After finding out the person's strengths and weaknesses, if I am confident that I can supplement their weaknesses, and if I develop a love that can cover their weaknesses, I will get married.

Marriage is the biggest part of a person's life, so I will think carefully and make it perfect. I don't think I can decide in such a short period of time.

It's hard to set a marriage age. If there is no target, I might live as a bachelor for the rest of my life. From a medical point of view, it's good for a woman to be over 25 years old, and people who value beauty say that it's ugly to have an old bride. So, should I take the middle ground and say 22 or 23 years old? I think it would be good to improve the old style for the wedding ceremony.

During our relationship, I will first see how much understanding they have, and I will also observe their etiquette towards others, especially their behavior towards the rich and the poor.

Regarding education, regardless of whether they graduated from school or are self-taught, it's enough if they have plenty of common sense and know-how. I don't particularly want a writer, but they should understand literature and have the writing skills to express what they think.

As for personality, I like a manly nature. A cheerful but silent nature, a romantic but thoughtful person. I prefer a complainer to a carefree person.

As for appearance, I don't know if it's because I'm trying to supplement my own shortcomings in my partner, but I want a handsome man.

I don't particularly care about the type of occupation, but they should have a respectable, stable job. <25>
