기사제목	不景氣는 언제까지 繼續될가??, 不景氣檢討 大座談會 (When will the recession end??, A roundtable discussion reviewing the recession)
기사형태	대담·좌담 (Dialogue/Roundtable Discussion)

When will the recession end??, A Roundtable Discussion Reviewing the Recession
Attendees
Seo Ch'un, Head of the Economics Department, Dong-A Ilbo
Bae Seong-ryong, Head of the Economics Department, Chosun Ilbo
Hong Seong-ha, Professor, Boseong College
Son Bong-jo, Professor, Yonhi College

From Our Company
Cha Sang-eop
Chae Man-shik
Park No-a

November 11, Evening

Cha: I am very grateful to all of you for attending despite your busy schedules. The reason I invited you here tonight has already been explained, so I won't repeat it. It would be best to get straight to the point and start discussing the main issue. This is not a lecture or any other kind of formal gathering, so please feel free to smoke and enjoy the fruit while you speak.

What is the cause of this recession?

Chae: It's not just our Joseon, but the whole world is talking about the recession, and we, who are already in a poor economic state, are especially anxious. I'm very curious to know when this recession will end. First, I'd like to know what the causes are, in order.
Hong: Well, I've come, but... I think those two gentlemen (referring to Mr. Bae and Mr. Seo) know better. My thoughts are a bit different. From my perspective, the recession can be explained simply. Economic panic is an inevitable result of today's capitalist economic system, so the cause lies in the system itself. The conclusion will come out soon enough.
Chae: Since tonight's roundtable is for the general public, please speak as plainly as possible, and don't hesitate to ask silly questions.
Hong: Anyway, those two (again pointing to Mr. Bae and Mr. Seo) know more about that. Hehe, please say something. (Both smile but don't answer)
Son: Why do you keep passing the buck? Haha.
Hong: Well, specifically, there are many causes, but today's economic panic is not accidental but inevitable. If we trace the important causes, we have to go back to before the Great War, which would be too technical and hard for the general public to understand.
Chae: Then what are the most immediate causes?
Hong: The most immediate cause is the agricultural panic in North and South America, which led to the stock market crash on the New York Stock Exchange. Since the United States has so much wealth that it dominates the world, it directly affected Europe. After the Great War, there was the German panic of 1926-27, the British General Strike of 1927, and the agricultural panic of 1928. Economic panic spread broadly, and this time the panic swept from the United States to Europe. As for Japan's panic, there are many other inevitable causes, but the most important is the recent disruption of Japan's raw silk trade. Japan's raw silk exports amount to 890 to 900 million won, and the main trading partner is the United States, so this year's exports are significantly lower than last year's.
Chae: Other teachers, please say something too.
Hong: If we look at Joseon alone, we might say that the main cause of the recession is the plummeting rice prices, but that's just a partial factor added to the larger panic.
Son: What I see is just a repetition of what everyone knows, and the two teachers probably have more detailed statistics or theories based on reality about the causes and realities of the recession, but in my opinion, in 1920, or the 9th year of Taisho, the production cycle entered a somewhat stable period in a relative sense. As Mr. Hong said, under the current capitalist economic system, the industrial cycle inevitably goes through a panic process. Looking at the United States alone, in 1923, thanks to the European war, the power of financial capital was greatly expanded, and production developed significantly, including automobile manufacturing, construction, installment sales, and lending financial capital to European powers. The financial capital was not brought in from Europe in the form of money, but in the form of American products. From 1923 to August and September of last year, the production side of the United States expanded beyond measure. That expansion led to the stock market crash in October of last year, revealing the clear causes of the panic. However, the fundamental cause lies in the fundamental contradiction of the capitalist economic system, in which the general consumption power does not keep up with the development of production power. So, the panic in the United States, which began with the stock market crash this year, spread to Europe, especially affecting the United Kingdom. In Japan, it affected the raw silk trade, and in Africa, it affected gemstones. Since the United States accounts for half of the world's wealth, the panic in the United States can be seen as a world panic. In Europe, Germany experienced a panic even before the potential elements of panic developed in the United States. In other words, the extreme development of the chemical industry led to a situation where consumption power could not keep up with the expanded production power, resulting in a bottleneck in general purchasing power. In France, there was an agricultural panic in 1928, but the panic came most slowly to industrial production. Recently, in England, the United States, Germany, and France, there has been a tendency to use monopolistic policies to raise prices and try to recover the economy. Each country is fiercely competing to seize the global market through huge monopolistic enterprises, but they are preventing each other with tariff barriers. However, the fundamental problem lies in the inability to digest the products, which is a flaw in the capitalist economic system, as you have already said.
As for Japan, since it is part of the global economy, it has also been affected by the global economic panic. During the Great War, the industrial sector expanded greatly, including the iron and steel industry and the chemical industry. After the gold export ban, thanks to that, it grew and developed further, becoming an industrialized nation. While production power continued to develop, the monetary aspect of finance increased, and the element of panic was brewing in the process of preventing prices from falling. In other words, the cause of the panic was that consumption power could not keep up with the extremely expanded production power. Therefore, when the financial panic hit in Showa 2 (1927), the industrial sector, which had been expanding until then, suffered a great <66> blow, and financial capitalists finally earnestly demanded the lifting of the gold embargo. However, of course, that was not the direct cause, but merely the external expression of the panic element that was inherently contained within, through the lifting of the gold embargo. If we compare the prices in June of last year with the current prices after the lifting of the gold embargo, they have fallen by about 20%. In order to prevent this decline, monopolistic policies were used for coal, the steel industry, and printing paper, but there was no way to reverse the fundamentally 기울어진 (inclined) trend of the panic. Other industries such as textiles, sugar, and cement did not use monopolistic policies and used temporary preventive measures against price drops, so they did not fall drastically overall, but in general, Japanese prices have fallen by about 60-70% since the lifting of the gold embargo. Now, in Joseon, the fundamental problem lies in agricultural production rather than industrial production, and our lives are at stake. The current global panic has spread to agriculture, and 570 million bushels of wheat are piled up unsold. Rice, which Joseon people depend on for their lives, is not originally a global product, and this year's harvest is expected to increase by 5 million bags, so in Joseon, where industrial production is extremely small, the direct cause of the panic has come entirely from agricultural products. However, the two teachers will speak in more detail when we get into the specifics. No matter who speaks, it's all similar...
Hong: If we talk about the causes, we inevitably have to explain the global economic situation before and after the war in detail, but that would be too boring for the readers and would go against the initial request. Haha... (Picks up a pear and eats it). Of course, Joseon is an agricultural country, so industrial production is extremely rudimentary, so agricultural production will be the most affected by the recession, and it would not be an exaggeration to say that industrial production has not been greatly affected. And since this panic is not just in Joseon, but global, even if this year were not a year of abundant harvest, the panic would be inevitable... it would just be adding insult to injury, and the lives of Joseon people would be ruined.
Chae: Mr. Bae, why haven't you said anything?

The Relationship Between Abundant Harvests in Joseon and Japan

Bae: (Standing up and smiling) Well, if there were a famine in Joseon, the amount of rice going to Japan would be reduced, but since Japan also has an abundant harvest, it is clear numerically that we are suffering a loss proportional to the increased production of Joseon rice.
Hong: If there were a famine in Japan and an abundant harvest in Joseon, the rice price would not fall.
Bae: If that were the case, of course, it wouldn't fall. But fortunately, this year's abundant harvest means that we can eat a little better for the time being. Anyway, if we can't sell it, we can eat it. <67>
Hong: That's right. The lives of ordinary people are much better. Now, even if you try to hire a nanny, there aren't any. And aren't fewer people going to North Gando?
Bae: People don't go to Gando easily. Only when they are completely ruined and unable to live here do they pack up and leave.
Chae: Then what if Japan has an abundant harvest and Joseon has an average harvest?
Hong: Of course, that would be better. If that happened, the grain price would rise, and overall life would be better. But what do you think, Mr. Seo?
Seo: Well, it's just like that. There's nothing special.
Bae: In fact, the highest price of polished rice in October of last year was 30 won, and the lowest was 20 won, but this year the highest is 16 won and the lowest is 14 won. Last year's actual rice production in Joseon was 13.79 million bags, and the lowest price was 27 won, so even though rice production increased by 5-6 million bags, the income of Joseon people has decreased.
Son: When there is a famine in Joseon, rice paddies that cannot utilize irrigation suffer almost complete crop failure, causing partial ruin. But this year, Japan has an unprecedented abundant harvest, so small farmers in Joseon are taking a huge hit.
Small farmers account for about 80% of all farmers in Joseon, so the impact this year is enormous. Even if a bag of rice costs 5-6 won, we can still make a living, but there are always debts for fertilizer, seeds, and labor, and there is no way to repay them until we sell the rice. But if we convert a bag of rice to the market price, it's only worth 5-6 won, so the overall impact on small farmers is quite large. Last year was still just a partial ruin, so it wasn't so bad, but this year, we are being seized every day due to various debts.
Bae: So, if we compare the last two years, we can't talk about the recession in Joseon without mentioning the issue of Japan's lifting of the gold embargo. The impact of the gold embargo on the economy cannot be expressed numerically, but as a result, Japanese prices have completely lost their equilibrium, with some rising by about 30% and others falling.

What Impact Will the Panic Have on Joseon?

Chae: Now that we've talked about the causes enough, will this panic cause a general social ruin in Joseon? Or will it end in a partial ruin limited to one class, and will the capitalist class be able to achieve new development and profits? Since capitalism has not yet developed in Joseon, what will happen?
Hong: We can't talk about Joseon separately. However, Joseon can be seen as being in the industrial revolution stage, so even if it is not a panic in the cyclical process, if we consider the global economic process, we can't say that Joseon will not be affected just because it is lagging behind.
Chae: Mr. Seo, why have you been quiet from the beginning? <68>
Seo: Well, if I talk like this, the order doesn't match, and I can't say what I want to say.
Hong: (Picking up a banana and handing it over) Here, peel this and start. Haha. (Everyone laughs)
Seo: If I were to talk about the economic panic, it would take at least 3-4 hours, so I can't possibly say everything here. As I said before, I can only say it simply, and it might be similar to what I've already said. The Japanese financial panic of Showa 2 (1927) ruined small and medium-sized financial businesses and created large financial businesses, and this panic has ruined small and medium-sized manufacturers. In terms of prices, some have fallen by 20% or even 23%, and others have fallen by 10% or 15%, with agricultural products falling the most.
Looking at rice and cocoon prices, they have fallen by 50-60% compared to last year. Spring cocoons that cost 7 won 50 jeon per kan last year have fallen to 3 won 50 jeon to 1 won 80 jeon this year, which is terrible. The reason is that Japan's raw silk exports, which amount to 800-900 million won a year, were almost entirely exported to the United States, but as the whole world has become so depressed, even the rich in the United States are naturally using less luxury goods. So, as demand decreased and raw silk didn't sell, the recession in the United States inevitably led to a recession in Japan. In addition, China's silver prices fell, and Japan's exports to China and India decreased. On top of that, there was an unprecedented abundant harvest, so the impact of the gold embargo and the abundant harvest caused the price of Joseon agricultural products to fall without fail. However, in terms of commerce and industry, it is different from agriculture in that production can be limited, prices can be adjusted to some extent, raw materials can be bought cheaply, and wages can be lowered, so there is a way to provide some relief, but agricultural products cannot do that, so they are the first to be hit. The prices of cocoons and rice have fallen the most in Joseon right now, but since the only thing Joseon people can do is agricultural production, there is nothing we can do. However, to some extent, the propaganda and planned intentions of businessmen and industrialists also contributed to the psychological impact on farmers.
Since industrial products are arranged so that they don't fall further, isn't it inevitable that only the prices of rice and cocoons, which are important agricultural products for Joseon farmers, will fall so much?
In terms of actual receipts this year, it has increased by about 160 million won compared to last year, but the prices of agricultural products have been covered up and have fallen by more than 50%, which is terrible.
Cha: What will be the impact of this current recession? Will an entire society be ruined?
Seo: Not everyone will be ruined. Even if small capitalists go bankrupt, large capitalists will prosper rather than be greatly affected. If rice prices are cheap, they can wait until they rise before selling. Only those who sell rice to eat, dress, and pay off debts right away will be ruined. Anyway, even if there are millions of bags of surplus rice this year, rice prices will rise next year.
However, one thing Joseon people should distinguish is 모미킨유우 (momi kinyuu, unhulled rice financing) and 코메키유우 (kome kinyuu, rice financing). We can only live if the price of unhulled rice goes up rather than the price of rice, so unless we use a policy to raise the price of unhulled rice from November, when the new grain starts to circulate, to January of the following year, it is useless to do anything after March or April. At that time, we have to buy rice at a high price. Anyway, Joseon people can only live if the price of agricultural products rises, so the Governor-General's office is also implementing a policy to raise the price of unhulled rice. However, even if the price of unhulled rice falls to this point and the price of rice rises a lot next spring, it will only benefit the rich, and it will only make it more difficult for small and medium-sized farmers to live.
Chae: Can we expose the inside story of increased rice production?
Seo: Well, talking about it is like scratching an itch from outside the boot (隔靴搔痒)...
Just as small industrialists are ruined by the panic in the industrial sector, small farmers are also ruined by the agricultural panic. Even if this recession recovers, under the current capitalist economic system, the period of prosperity is extremely short, and the recession is long and broad. This is an inevitable trend as various industries develop overall. However, if the industrial cycle in capitalist industrial countries recovers as before, some of the unemployed will become factory workers, and general consumption power will also improve, so the economy will improve somewhat, but even if general industrial countries recover, it will be difficult for our 80% of farmers to become factory workers. Anyway, Joseon people can only live if the panic of agricultural products such as rice, beans, and millet passes. Originally, industrial companies can create trusts to agree on prices and adjust price declines to some extent, but agriculture has special characteristics, so even if the quality or production rate is above average, it can never keep up with the ratio of industrial products.

What If We Temporarily Halt Rice Production Policies?

Chae: What if we suspend the rice production policy for a certain period?
Hong: Well, who is the increased rice production for? There must be a reason to suspend or limit it. Isn't the reason clear? If they solve the food problem with it and use the labor force to buy goods, that's all they care about. Why would they care what happens? If we look at the irrigation associations (水利組合), they lend low-interest funds to increase rice production without waiting for rainwater, but in reality, the land in the irrigation associations dries up on its own, so there's nothing more to say.
Seo: Exposing the contents of increased rice production is not just about that. Increased rice production is ultimately just a superficial excuse, so I predict that there will be many opponents next year.
If we take 160 근 (geun) per bag of rice and set the base <70> price at 12 won, it should cost 7 jeon 5 rin or 6 rin per 근 (geun), but this year it only costs 3 jeon 5 rin per 근 (geun), which is terrible. If we set the break-even point at 7 won per bag of rice and approve the irrigation associations, we can't possibly manage the irrigation associations with this year's grain prices without changing the original policy. Everyone will go bankrupt.
Hong: Well, it's not like we didn't know that irrigation associations were harmful from the beginning.
Seo: Yeah. Still, if things go well, we can get tricked. Also, the tenant farmers can earn some money for a while by digging ponds and building embankments. And the landlords get tempted by the promise of low-interest funds. Hehehe. But now even financial institutions won't get excited. Because the results are like that...
Chae: Isn't it because they overestimate the costs compared to the actual expenses and do something in the middle?
Bae: That's part of it. Originally, the planned approval for irrigation associations was 30,000 jeongbo, but only 5,000 jeongbo have been completed so far. Anyway, the rice production increase policy has always failed rather than produced an abundant harvest.
Hong: But from a top-down policy perspective, it may not be a failure.
Bae: Well, that's right.

What Measures Are the Authorities Taking?

Bae: Then, considering the current situation, what measures can be taken? Even if the government buys up 5 million bags, this year's production will increase by 6 million bags, so the combined total will be 10 million bags, so the impact won't be great.
Hong: The impact is certainly great. Overall, there will always be rice left over during the new grain circulation period. So, if there is old rice left over, the price of new grain will inevitably fall. Anyway, unlike industrial products, agricultural products cannot limit production, so the decline phenomenon cannot proceed at the same pace as industrial products. Even if it becomes an average harvest and the price of industrial products rises, the recession will be the same. In any case, we cannot talk about the price cycle for agricultural products, and even if it becomes an average harvest every year from now on, it will be difficult to recover like last year.
Bae: The Governor-General's office previously announced measures for rice prices, stating that it was not a rice price increase policy but a monthly export adjustment. Rice price adjustment is the same as export adjustment, which is a ridiculous statement.
Seo: The rice price policy is not a monthly export adjustment, which has a subtle meaning (妙理). It's just playing with numbers. Hahaha.
Chae: What effect would it have if we used the Agricultural Association Warehouse or Rice Warehouse system to lend about 80% of the money for the stored rice?
Hong: People who have a lot of rice may benefit somewhat, but if they don't have much, they'll have to pay interest and storage fees later, so there won't be anything left over. I can't support that. <71>
Son: Small farmers only have a few bags of rice left over. And if you look at what they bring to the market, some even have only a few mal, but would they block it in the warehouse just because the grain price is cheap? What would they do?
Bae: That's right. Even if they lend at low interest, grain merchants are very reluctant to lend for one or two bags. So, they can't even get the raised price.
Hong: That's right. And there's always someone in the middle who introduces and negotiates, and that person takes a cut for the introduction fee. There are brokers and wholesalers.
Bae: In such cases, rice merchants station people to find them. Anyway, small farmers are only exploited one way or another.
Chae: That's right. Let's get to the main point. Can't we find a way to break through the recession?
Hong: We can't possibly find that here. It's simple to say, but... In capitalist countries with established foundations such as Japan, England, France, Germany, and the United States, even if the economic panic is taking its final steps, they can still use hard work policies, so even if there is a difference in the length of prosperity and recession, they can still cycle. However, Joseon is being systematically used according to Japan's prosperity and recession, so what can we discover here?
Son: According to a statement by Westminster Bank, there will be some recovery next year. According to Dr. Holton, if we limit production and clean up the inventory from the end of this year to next year and achieve a balance, production will become lively again. Also, since economic panic is a fundamental contradiction under the current capitalist economic system, it is true that we will achieve balance momentarily, but even if we cannot achieve prosperity, we will at least reach a state of order.
In Joseon, only small and medium-sized farmers are being affected, while general merchants, industrialists, and financiers are not being greatly affected. The prices of general industrial products have not fallen much compared to the price of rice.
Bae: That's right. In the case of industrial products, wholesale prices have fallen, but retail prices have not fallen much. Originally, general retail prices fall 2-3 months after wholesale prices fall.
Son: That's right. However, Joseon people don't know the signs of rising and falling prices and are ignorant of changes in the financial world, so even if wholesale prices fall, they don't know and buy expensively. While they are buying expensively, the wholesale prices that had fallen rise again, so they always end up buying expensive goods.
Bae: In the United States, this is the first time they are experiencing a panic, so they are 정신을 못차리고 (unable to come to their senses) and making a fuss, but in Europe, they seem to be used to it.
Hong: Of course. Because they experience it all the time.
Bae: Anyway, they can't even handle the domestic inventory and are trying to advance internationally, so they can never limit production.
Son: That's right. Things like Hilferding's international planning policy and the creation of international trusts are fundamentally impossible to abolish competition. Limiting production is difficult overall.
Hong: What's wrong with creating international trusts? <72> What happens when interests conflict?
Son: Right now, things like the Volga International Trust, syndicates, and cartels are strategically influencing the economy.
Hong: The joint front of capitalists will soon be fractured by the proletariat, and they will inevitably be in opposition due to conflicting interests.

How Long Will This Recession Last?

Chae: Then, in what direction is this recession developing?
Bae: This current panic is said to be a destruction process in the global context, but it doesn't seem to be recognized as a whole in Joseon like it was last year. It's like a cold on a tuberculosis patient. Hahaha.
But when will it end? That's the question. It will also have a big impact on the industrial rationalization problem.
Hong: A lot. Industrial rationalization is ultimately about rationally XXing workers and making them more and more poor. If we keep going like this, even if we produce cheap and excellent products, there will be no one to buy them, which is a big problem... No matter how much rich people buy, sparrows can't swallow ten grains of rice at once. Anyway, the big problem is that we can't adjust production, we only lower production costs and increase the quantity, so the consumption power of the dispossessed disappears.
Son: Ultimately, industrial rationalization will ruin small and medium-sized merchants and industrialists, and only large merchants and industrialists will remain.
Chae: What if the Japanese cabinet changes? Will the economy improve somewhat?
Hong: The policy will change, so it will have some impact, but not a very big impact.
Son: Anyway, the inherent elements will be expressed by taking advantage of the moment.
Hong: That's right. The problem lies in fundamental healing. It's a bit difficult, though...
Chae: What measures will be needed in the future?
Seo: If there is a change in the Japanese cabinet and the Seiyukai or another party forms a cabinet, they will abandon passive policies and use active policies. First, they will use bond issuance, and by starting railroad construction and civil engineering projects to relieve the unemployed (even if they don't reimpose the gold embargo), the general purchasing power will be strong. The economy will recover.
Hong: But that's just a stopgap measure. It won't last long.
Bae: Whether the Seiyukai cabinet uses bond issuance or relieves the unemployed, they will inevitably use austerity policies.
Seo: They don't have to use it. Isn't the fundamental problem purchasing power? By the end of industrial rationalization, production increases extremely in one place, <73> and production decreases in another place. As prosperity and recession turn, wars break out.
Hong: Anyway, prosperity is temporary.
Bae: Purchasing power must increase.
Son: If prosperity passes for a moment, the recession will be even greater.
Seo: Everything is premised on purchasing power. If there are no unemployed people in the country and everyone is working, they will all eat.
Bae: But if we look at the phenomena in industrial countries, there is no place to handle all the production even if we try to export it.
Cha: Anyway, a war must break out and we must seize new territories.
Everyone - Laughing - That's right.
Seo: But the economy seems to be improving somewhat temporarily next year.
Hong: That's right. It may improve temporarily.
Seo: Right now, capitalist countries around the world are desperately looking for undeveloped countries. Only then can they dispose of domestic products and somewhat alleviate the recession. One way for places like Japan to promote their own scenic spots widely and use foreign tourist attraction policies. The goal of making Nikko or Geumgangsan a world park is all for that purpose.
Hong: It's foolish for the rich to sell rice cheaply. Isn't there a lot that can be made with rice? If they made something like 풀 (pul, paste) first, it would be more profitable than selling rice as it is. Temporary unemployment relief is also possible.
Seo: Japan controls the rice price, and the United States controls the raw silk. This year, Japan has an abundant harvest, so it has lost a strong consumer of rice, and the United States has a panic, so raw silk is not exported at all, which has a big impact on Japan, so both are sick. If only there were a famine in Japan and an abundant harvest in Joseon, it would be very good, but it is worst when both Japan and Joseon have an abundant harvest. Still, it's better than when only Japan has an abundant harvest and Joseon has a famine. Recently, Osaka Asahi wrote that Joseon and Japan had abundant harvests in Taisho 9 (1920), and Japan had an average harvest and Joseon had an abundant harvest in Showa 2 (1927), so they speculated that Japan and Joseon would both have famines next year. Hahaha. That's superstition.
Bae: Oh, I should buy rice.
Everyone Hahaha
Bae: If there is a famine atmosphere, there will definitely be a change in the rice price.
Seo: Anyone who sells millet in a year like this is a fool. Since the price of millet and rice are about the same, why would they eat millet that they don't want to eat? Even though there are 6 million bags of increased production, if they can't sell the rice because the price is so low, they can at least eat it, so half of it will disappear within this year.
Hong: It's foolish to eat millet, but in fact, millet swells when cooked, so poor people eat it.
Son: Foreign rice coming into Joseon included Annam rice, Rangoon rice, Taiwan rice, and Manchurian millet, but <74> it will decrease somewhat this year.
Hong: By the way, how is the number of 남부여대군 (nambu yeodae gun, groups of men carrying burdens and women carrying children) going to North Gando this year compared to last year?
Bae: Much less than last year.
Hong: If there are fewer, it means that life is better. They only pack up and leave when they are about to die.
Seo: That's right. This recession (in Joseon) is a problem for those who can sell rice. People who don't have rice can at least eat it, so it's better than a famine.
Chae: The Japanese government is planning to start private relief projects from next year and has set a budget of 70 million yen. If that is realized, will purchasing power increase and will there be some activity?
Hong: That's a 3-year plan.
Seo: It's better than nothing. If they start civil engineering projects, they can earn a living while working on those projects. But it's just a temporary fix.
Hong: Until we remove the fundamental contradictions, no matter what policies we use, the root of the disease will remain.
Cha: --Quickly checking the tired expressions on everyone's faces-- What time is it? I think you have time elsewhere... I think we've talked enough about breaking through the recession, so should we raise a toast?
Seo: That's the best thing I've heard so far. Hahaha.
Everyone... Hahaha
Chae: Instead of drinking alcohol to break through the recession, should we have a cup of tea and get up? Haha. Thank you for your hard work. (Written by Roa at 9:30 PM) <75>

이 不景氣의 原因은 어데서 왓는가?

蔡  비단 우리 조선뿐 안니겟지만 세상이 하도 不景氣 不景氣 하닛가 남달니 빈궁한 경제 상태에 잇는 우리이니 만치 위선 갑갑한 푼수로 언제까지 이 不景氣가 계속될는지...퍽 궁금한 일인데요...위선 순서를 따러서 그 원인이 어데잇는지 그것을 먼점 이약이해주섯스면 함니다.
洪  글세요. 오라시니 오긴 왓슴니다만은... 그런<64> 것은 아마 저 두분 先生 (裵, 徐 兩氏를 가르치면서)이 잘 알고 게시겟지요. 내 생각은 좀 다르닛가요. 내 입장에서 不景氣를 본다면 간단히 말할 수가 잇서요. 즉 經濟恐慌이란 오늘날 資本主義 經濟制度 밋헤서는 필연적으로 따르는 것인 만치 그 원인은 그 組織과 제도 자체에 잇는 것이닛가 결론은 곳 나올 것 갓슴니다.
蔡  그런데 오늘밤 이 座談은 일반 독자를 상대로 하는이만큼 말슴도 될 수 잇는대로 平易하게 해 주시려니와 우리들도 당치안은 우수ㅅ광스러운 질문을 할 것임니다. 그리 아시고 말슴해 주시지요.
洪  엇잿거나 그 방면에 관한 것은 저 두분 (또 한번 笑指 裵, 徐 兩氏) 이 잘 아실 걸이요. 허허 말슴 좀 하시지요. (兩氏 微笑不答)
孫  뭘 작구 미루기만 하십닛가. 허허.
洪  글세 구체적으로 말한다면야 그 원인으로 여러 가지를 들 수 잇는데 오늘날 이 經濟恐慌이란 결코 우연이 안이고 필연이닛가 그 중요 원인을 遡究한다면 不得已 大戰前까지 더듬어 올너가야 하겟는데 그러자면 전문적이 되겟고 또 그러고 일반 독자들이 알어듯기 쉽게 하라는데 허허.
蔡  그럼 가장 갓가운 원인은 무엇일가요.
洪  갓가운 원인으로는 米國 뉴욕取引所 주식 폭락을 이르킨 南北米洲의 農業恐慌이 이러나고 보니 그 富力이 거진 全世界를 지배하고 잇는 米國이니 만치 그것이 직접 全歐羅巴에 영향이 밋치게 된 것인데 大戰後 1926년 7년도 獨逸의 恐慌, 7년도 英國의 總同盟罷業과 同 28년의 農業恐慌, 이럿케 經濟恐慌이 全幅的으로 버더저 나오든 것이 이번에는 米國에서 歐羅巴로 恐慌의 旋風이 넘어간 것이지요. 그런데 日本의 恐慌을 말한다면 다른 여러 가지 필연적 원인도 잇겟지만은 최근 日本의 생사 무역에 지장이 생겻다는 것이 가장 중요한 점이겟지요. 현재 日本의 생사 수출액만 하드래도 8억 9천만 원 내지 9억만 원인데 그 무역 상대국이 米國이니 만치 금년은 작년보다 엄청나게 감소된 수자를 보히고 잇슴니다.
蔡  다른 선생님들께서도 좀 말슴해 주시지요.
洪  朝鮮만 單獨으로 떼여서 본다면 不景氣의 중요한 원인이 주장 米價暴落에 잇다 하지만은 그것은 대체의 큰 덩어리 恐慌에따 雪上加霜으로 덥처진 一部的 素因에 지나지 못하겟지요.
孫  내가 보는 바는 결국 다 아는 것을 되푸리 하는데 지나지 못하겟고 또 不景氣의 원인과 현실에 대해서는 두분 선생께서 統計나 혹은 현실에 立脚한 이론이<65> 더 자세하겟지만은 내 생각 갓해서는 1920년 즉 大正 9년에는 生産循環過程이 상대적 의미에서 다소 安定期에에 들어갓다고 볼 수 잇슴니다. 洪先生의 말슴과 가티 현재 資本主義 經濟制度에 잇서서 産業循環過程은 필연적으로 恐慌過程을 밟게 되는데 米國만을 보드래도 1923년에는 歐洲戰爭의 덕택으로 金融資本의 세력을 가장 만히 **게 되여 특별히 생산이 발전되엿다고 볼 수 잇는데 내용으로는 자동차 제조업이라든지 건축 토목이라든지 月賦販賣制度 혹은 歐洲列强에 대해서 金融資本을 대출한 것이라든지를 들 수 잇슴니다. 그러고 그 금융자본도 歐羅巴에서 돈으로 가저간 것이 안이라 米國 생산품을 가저간 점에 잇서서 1923년부터 작년 8, 9월까지는 米國의 생산 방면이 말할 수 업시 膨脹되엿다고 볼 수 잇는데 그와가티 팽창되엿든 것이 작년 10월에 와서 주식 가격이 폭락되면서부터 恐慌의 원인을 명확히 들어내엿다고 하겟슴니다. 그러나 그 근본 원인이 어데 잇느냐 하면 생산력이 발달되여가는데 따라서 일반 소비력이 거긔에 따라가지 못한다는 資本主義 經濟組織의 근본 모순에 잇지안는가 생각함니다. 그래서 금년도에 들어가와 주식 가격이 폭락됨으로부터 생긴 米國의 恐慌이 歐羅巴로 넘어가서 특히 英國에 큰 영향을 끼치엇고 日本에 잇서서는 생사 무역에 영향을 끼첫고 아푸리카에는 寶石에 영향을 끼치엇는데 그것은 米國의 富力이 세계의 半部를 차지하고 잇는이만치 米國의 恐慌은 곳 世界恐慌으로 볼 수 잇는데 歐羅巴에 넘어가서 獨逸은 米國에 잠재적으로 恐慌의 요소가 발전되여 나오기 전부터 恐慌이 생겻다고 보겟슴니다. 즉 화학공업이 극도로 발달됨으로 말미암아 소비력이 그 팽창된 생산력을 따라가지 못하게 된데서 일반의 購買力에 그 ユキツマリ가 생긴 것이고 佛蘭西에서는 1928년에 농업 공황이 잇섯스나 공업 생산에 잇서서는 공황이 가장 더듸게 왓다고 보겟슴니다. 그런데 英, 米, 獨, 佛을 물론하고 최근에 와서는 獨占政策을 써서 물가를 인상하야 景氣를 만회코자 힘쓰는 경향이 보이며 거대한 독점적 기업을 計*하야 세계적으로 시장을 爭奪하고자 각국 간에 競爭이 대단히 심한 모양인데 관세 장벽으로 그것을 서로 防止하고 잇지만은 근본 문제는 생산품을 소화할 수 업다는 資本主義 經濟組織의 결함에 잇다는 것은 여러분께서 임이 말슴한 바임니다.
日本으로 말하면 세계 경제의 一輪으로 볼 수 잇는이만치 역시 세계적 經濟恐慌의 영향을 밧게 된 것인데 大戰 당시에 대단히 팽창되엿든 공업 부문 즉 제철업이라든지 화학공업이라든지 그 외에 여러 가지 산업이 金 수출 금지 후 그 德으로 더구나 성장 발달되야 工業國化 해바렷고 그와가티 생산력이 계속적으로 발전하는 가운데 금융 방면으로 貨幣가 증대하야 물가가 低落됨을 방지해 오는 과정에 잇서서 공황의 요소를 기루고 잇섯다고 보겟슴니다. 다시 말하면 생산력이 극도로 팽창한데 대해서 소비력이 거긔 따라가지 못하는데 공황의 원인이 잇섯든 것입니다. 그래서 昭和 2년에 금융 공황이 닷치자 엿헤까지 팽창되엿든 산업 부문이 크나큰<66> 打擊을 입게 됨애 금융 자본가들은 드듸어 金解禁을 切實히 요구하게 된 것입니다. 그러나 물론 그것이 직접의 원인은 되지 못하고 다못 내재적으로 포함되여 잇든 공황의 요소가 金解禁을 통해서 외부적으로 表現한데 불과한 것이지요. 작년 6월과 金解禁 후의 현재와의 물가를 비교해 본다면 약20%는 떨어젓는데 여긔 대해서 그 低落을 방지하게 위하야 석탄이라든지 製鐵業이나 洋紙에 대한 독점 정책을 써 보기도 햇스나 근본적으로 기우러지는 공황의 대세에 역행할 재조가 업섯고 其外에 紡織業이나 砂糖, 세멘트 가튼 공업에는 독점 정책을 쓰지안코 가격 저락에 대한 일시적 방지책을 써서 전폭적으로 크게 떨어지지 안엇지만은 일반적으로 볼 때 金解禁 이후로 일본 물가가 6, 7分 가량은 떨어진 형편입니다. 그런데 朝鮮은 공업 생산보다 농업 생산에 근본 문제가 매여 잇고 우리들의 생명을 달고 잇는 터인데 시방 세계 공황은 농업에까지 밋처서 小麥만이 5억 7천만 븟셀의 滯荷가 그대로 싸혀 잇다 함니다. 그런데 朝鮮 사삼들이 목숨을 달고 잇는 쌀은 원래 세계적이 못되는 데다가 금년에는 5백여 만석의 增收를 보게 되니 공업 생산품이 극히 微小한 조선에 잇서서는 공황의 직접 원인이 순전히 농업 생산품에서 왓다 하겟슴니다. 그러나 專門에 들어가서는 두분 선생께서 더 자세히 말슴하시겟지요. 아무가 말하드래도 비슥 비슥하지요만...
洪  원인을 말하게 된다면 不得已 戰前 戰後의 세계적 경제 상태를 전문적으로 설명해야겟는데 그리자면 독자가 시로도란 점에서 처음 요구에 버서날게고 하하...(배 한쪽을 집어 자신 후에) 물론 조선은 농업국이니 만치 공업 생산은 극히 유치하고 보니 농업 생산에 가장 만흔 不景氣의 타격을 입을 것이고 공업 생산에는 그리 큰 타격이 업섯다 해도 過言이 안이겟지요. 그리고 이번 공황이 결코 조선만이 안이라 세계적인만콤 금년이 풍년이 안이드라도 공황은 면치 못할 것이닛가...그저 雪上加霜으로 조선 사람의 생활만 破滅을 당할 뿐이지요.
蔡  裵先生님은 웨 아무 말슴도 안허심닛가.

朝鮮과 日本의 凶豐關係

裵  (몸을 이르켜 微笑하면서) 글세요. 조선에 만일 흉년이 젓다고 하면 일본 나갈 쌀이 적어질 터인데 일본에도 풍년이 들고 보니 그런 점으로 본다면 조선 쌀이 增收된 비율에 따라 그만한 손해를 입고 잇다는 것은 數字的으로 명백한 일이지요.
洪  日本에 만일 흉년이 들고 朝鮮에 풍년이 들엇다면 쌀갑이 나리지 안켓지요.
裵  그럿케 되면 물론 나리지 안켓지요. 그런데 何如間  금년에 풍년이 들고 보니 당분간 먹기에는 좀 나흔 모양이지요. 엇젯든 팔아 못쓰면 먹기는 할 터이닛가.<67>
洪  그러치요. 細民의 생활은 훨신 나허젓지요. 爲先 어멈 한아를 구하려도 사람이 업스닛가. 그리고 北間島 가는 사람이 적지 안슴닛가.
裵  여간해서 間島가지 안슴니다. 전체적으로 沒落이 되어서 정 살수 업서야 털고 일어서지요.
蔡  그러면 日本에 풍년이 들고 조선에 평년작이면 엇덜가요.
洪  물론 낫겟지요. 그래되면 穀價가 올너가느니 만큼 전반적 생활이 나어지겟지만 徐君 생각은 엇덧소.
徐  글세 그럿치 뭐 별게 잇서.
裵  사실 작년 10월 달 正米 갑이 최고 30원이고 최저 20원 이든 것이 금년에 와서는 최고 16원 최저 14원으로 떨어젓스닛가요. 작년도 조선 産米 實收가 1,379만 石이고 최저 가격이 27원 이엿스닛가 쌀로는 5, 6백만 石이 增收되엿다 하지만 조선 사람의 수입은 줄은 세음이지요.
孫  그런데 조선에 흉년이 들 때는 水利를 이용하지 못하는 田畓은 全滅에 갓가울만치 減收됨으로 一部的 沒落이 생기지만은 금년가티 일본이 未曾有의 풍년이고 보니 조선의 小農들은 전적으로 대 타격을 밧게 되는 게지요.
조선에 소농이 전 농민의 8할 가량이나 되는이만치 금년에 밧는 타격은 여간한게 안임니다. 벼 한섬에 5, 6원을 하드래도 풍년이니 만치 위선 糊口는 해가지만은 肥料代다 苗種代다 일꾼들 품싹이다 하야 負債업는 사람이 업고 또 벼를 팔기 전에는 그것을 보상 할 길이 업는데 벼 한섬을 시장가격으로 환산하면 불과 5, 6원밧게 안되고 보니 소농들이 전면적으로 밧는 타격은 如干 큰 것이 안임니다. 작년은 그래도 一部的 몰락에 지나지 못하엿스니 그리 심하지는 안엇지만은 금년에는 여러 가지 負債로 하야 매일 差押을 당하고 잇는 형편이닛가요.

孫  그럿슴니다. 그런데 조선 사람들은 물가가 올으고 나리는 기미를 잘 몰으고 재계의 변동에 어둡기 때문에 도매갑이 떨어저도 그것을 몰으고 빗싸게 사는 동안에 떠러젓든 도매가격이 다시 올너가고 보니 언제든지 빗싼 물품만 사 쓰게 되는 게지요.
裵  지금 米國에서는 공황이 처음이닛가 정신을 못차리고 떠들지만은 歐羅巴에서는 예사로 알고 잇는 모양입듸다.
洪  그럼요. 늘 당하닛가요.
裵  하여간 國內滯荷도 다 처리하지 못하고 국제적 진출을 하려하닛가 늘 생산 제한을 못하게 되는 게지요.
孫  그럿치요. 힐포린의 국제적 계획책이라든가 국제트라스트란 것을 만들녀는 것이라던지 하여튼 근본적으로 경쟁을 撤廢할 수가 업스닛가. 생산 제한이 전면으로 어려운 일이겟지요.
洪  국제트라스트를 만들면 엇전다나요.<72> 서로 이해가 상반하는 데야 엇저나요.
孫  시방 발가의 국제적 트라스트라든가 신듸케-트, 카-텔 가튼 것이 계획적으로 경제계에 영향을 주고 잇는데요.

蔡  그러면 이 불경기는 엇더한 방향으로 발전이 되고 잇나요?
裵  이번 공황이 세계적으로 보아서 파멸 과정이라고 하지만은 조선에 잇서서는 작년처럼 전체적으로 인정되지 안는 모양이지요. 말하자면 肺病患者에게 感氣가 더친 셈이나 될가요. 허허허.
그런데 언제 끗이 날는지 그것이 疑問이지만 産業合理化 問題에도 영향이 만켓지요.
洪  만치요. 産業合理化란 결국 노동자를 합리적으로 XX해서 점점 더 가난방이를 만들자는 것이닛가 그대로 나가다가는 나종에 아모리 생산품을 갑싸고 훌륭하게 만들어 낸대도 살 사람이 업서질 것이닛가 큰일이지... 돈잇는 놈이 아모리 만히 사들인대도 참새가 한 입에 쌀알 열 개는 못 삼키는 법이닛가요. 하여튼 생산 조절을 못하고 생산비만 낫추고 量만 늘우고 보니 무산자들의 소비력이 업서지는게 큰 문제겟지요.
孫  결국 産業合理化로 말미암아 중소 상공업자들은 몰락할 것이고 대상공업자들만 남게 될 것이지요.
蔡  가령 일본서 內閣이 밧귄다면 엇덜가요. 다소 경기가 조와질가요.
洪  정책이 덜너질 것이니 그만큼은 영향이 잇겟스나 별로히 큰 영향은 업겟지요.
孫  하여간 내재적 요소가 그때 그때의 모맨트를 타서 발현될 것뿐 이지요.
洪  그럿치요. 문제는 근본적 療治에 잇는게지요. 좀 어려워서 그럿치...
蔡  압흐로 엇더한 대책이 필요할가요.

裵  購買力이 늘어야지요.
孫  호경기가 잠시 지나면 불경기는 더 크게 들어날 터이지요.
