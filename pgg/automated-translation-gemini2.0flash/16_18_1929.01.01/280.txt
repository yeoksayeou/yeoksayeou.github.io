1929년 01월 01일
總角心情, 안타가운 心境 老總角의 告白 (Boyhood Sentiment, Heartrending Emotion: An Old Bachelor's Confession)
馬面生
문예기타 (Miscellaneous Literature)

I am thirty-two years old this year and still a bachelor. In the old days, when even determining the traces of a baby in the womb was a busy affair, if one were not a eunuch or a ginger merchant bachelor, it would be unheard of to be single until this age. Even if people ridiculed or mocked me, I would have nothing to say, being an incredibly old bachelor.

However, in this world where everyone is too busy trying to make a living, heads buried and rushing about, and in this chaotic situation where everyone is consumed with fulfilling their own desires, how could anyone understand or even listen to the sorrowful circumstances and long sighs of an old bachelor like me! On long winter nights, sleep won't come due to resentment, and on my lonely pillow, I only let out the unspeakable sighs that seem to fill the window frame. I'd rather, with a reckless heart, confess the resentment I've buried in my heart until now and appeal to the many lonely old bachelors and old maids who can't sleep like me.

I am not impotent, nor do I lack eyebrows, have a crooked nose, or a broken leg. I am not a ginger merchant or a leech. Outwardly, I may not be considered a handsome man, but when I look at my own face in the mirror, I seem reasonably presentable. Whether it's because I've been indifferent to women, ignorant of the secrets of love, or have set my ideals for a woman too high, I don't know. In any case, it is true that I have lived a lonely life in inns until today. Therefore, I haven't felt particularly pressured by life or other inconveniences, but the only thing I long for and feel heartbroken about is women. However, there is no specific woman in mind. Unable to be satisfied with lukewarm love from the same sex, I am like a child shivering in the snowy streets, wishing someone would give me an overcoat or jacket to cover me. My heart longs to crawl into a warmer place than a silk blanket under the heated floor in a cozy room, or into the warm bosom of my mother. However, I always embrace my full heart and wander aimlessly through dreamland, constantly shivering. When I meet a like-minded friend, it's always about drinking. After sending my friends back to their nests where they are awaited, I wander the streets aimlessly until late at night, keeping the fading intoxication as my companion. When I open the creaky door of my lonely room in the inn, lit only by a dim electric light, it feels as if I am entering a silent cemetery on Pukmang Mountain (北邙山), and a cold wind blows, causing my heart to sink. The old, patched quilt seems to resentfully stare at me. My family knows about this life, and there have been people who have earnestly advised me over the years, but for some reason, their advice doesn't feel welcome; it feels like they are trying to drag me into misfortune. I dismiss the important matter of marriage with a bitter smile.

If a virtuous gentleman were to hear these words, he might spit and turn away. But I don't know what to do with my melancholy heart. Clutching my bursting heart, I've tried to soothe my troubled mind by going to the pine forest in Ch'ŏngnyangni (청양리) or the clear waters of the Han River (한강수) more than once or twice. There have also been many times when I struggled to suppress my instincts. However, if my desire for women were solely based on instinctive desires and the pursuit of pleasure, I wouldn't have lasted this long. It's not because I'm too picky about the woman I want to marry. When I see an attractive woman on the street or anywhere else, I send a lightning-fast glance without losing face, applying my aesthetic sense to compare her to my ideal level. But beauty alone doesn't captivate me. So, it's neither this nor that. I'm just afraid of marriage because it's such an important matter. However, in my lonely room, when I long for the image I saw during the day in my dreams, and when I feel inexplicably yearning and affectionate towards someone, I yearn for married life and have impatient thoughts like a fire. But then, when I think of the headaches and various pains of married life and family life that my friends suffer, I am startled, and all thoughts of wanting to marry vanish without a trace. I return to the free life of a bachelor and have been going back and forth like this until now. However, I don't believe that marriage is a rule that people must follow, and that the meaning of life lies there. I also want to imagine a beloved woman in my mind and have a lover who passionately loves only me in this world. But I still don't know what a woman is like. I don't know if a virgin's heart is like the tongue of a venomous snake or the soft breast of silk. But there is no way to find out, and no one to tell me. To simply live like this is to be an unfortunate old bachelor. <161>
