From the Ruins of Hwaseomun
Roa

In the ruins of an ancient castle where even human traces have ceased,
Even the wandering clouds shed tears.
Where have all the green tiles that covered it gone?
Is it only weeds that grow rampant?

On the four pillars of arm-span circumference, ornate with dancheong (丹靑, traditional Korean decorative coloring),
And on every beam, blue dragons and yellow dragons coiled,
Even the stone walls that blocked bullets have crumbled,
And the remaining letters on the signboard weep, embracing the earth.

The shepherds riding cows along the sunset path
Used to shade themselves with parasols and leisurely smoke, moving with grace,
Do they not know the king's lament is a thing of the past?
Only the tilted headstones remain where the supports once stood.

Hwaseomun, tell me. Why have you become like this?
For five hundred years, trying to serve one king,
Investing money, sacrificing bodies, living and dying,
Now that the nation is lost, what is the point of you guarding?
-1930. 9. 30- (On the way to Suwon) <152>

華西門의 廢墟에서
						로아
인적조차 끈허진 古城의 폐허에
떠도는 구름도 눈물지우네
덥혓든 청개와 다 어데 가고
잡풀만 길길이 자랏단 말가

아람도리 네기둥에 단청올니고
들보마다 청룡황룡 구비치더니
총알 막든 돌담마자 허무러지고
현판에 남은 글씨 흙안고 우는고나

석양길 소타고 자나는 목동들은
일산밧고 연타시고 자로거동하시압든
님금님 비탄을 녯일이라 모르는가
지지대 섯든 고개 비ㅅ돌만 남엇거늘

화서문아 말해라. 네 웨 이 꼴 되엿는가
오백년 나려 오며 님군 한분 모시자고
돈바치고 몸바치고 죽어 살어 왓건만
나라 일흔 백성되니 네직힌들 뭘하느뇨
-1930. 9. 30-(水原갓든 길에) ＜152＞
