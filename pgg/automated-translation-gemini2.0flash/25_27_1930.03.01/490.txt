Fourth Lecture: Queen Min's Autocracy and the Imo Incident

Daewongun ruled for 10 years, breaking old practices, reforming the system, and filling the granaries (倉*), implementing many facilities, but his methods were too drastic, causing the subjects, high and low, to have a sense of anxiety (憂懼의 念). Furthermore, the requisitions for repairing the palace and the abolition of academies (書院) caused the loss of public sentiment and incited Confucian scholars. In the third year of King Gojong, he married the daughter of Min Chi-rok of Yeoju, making her the queen. This Queen Min, possessing a shrewd and intelligent character, gradually employed her elder brother, Min Seung-ho, and his faction after entering the palace, preparing to confront Daewongun without negligence (不怠). Meanwhile, there were petitions for royal rule from Yi Hang-no and Choe Ik-hyeon, leading Daewongun to retire from politics in the tenth year of Gojong (Gyohae year) as an inevitability (不得已에 至하엿다).

What arose with Daewongun's retirement was Queen Min's autocracy (專權). Moreover, when the first son was born in the eleventh year of Gojong (Gapseul year) and was appointed as crown prince the following year, the national power returned to the Min clan centered around Queen Min, forming virtually a Min clan's world (閔氏一族의 천하) for 23 years from the 10th to 12th years of Gojong. Daewongun fiercely opposed Queen Min during this period. With these two major forces as the center, the political situation in Hanyang could not find its course amidst overlapping waves, and the Imo Military Rebellion of the 19th year of Gojong can be called the first act of its curtain.

Prior to this, after Queen Min seized power, she changed all the people and policies of the Daewongun era, valuing Park Gyu-su, Yi Choe-eung, Min Gyu-ho, Min Tae-ho, and Min Yeong-mok, and reviving the academies. At that time, a box of bombs came to Min Seung-ho's house, annihilating his family. This was attributed to the Daewongun side, and Daewongun's illegitimate son, Yi Jae-seon, was framed for treason and killed, reaching the peak of Daewongun's resentment (忿恨). Furthermore, the government at the time abandoned Daewongun's isolationist policy and concluded the Treaty of Amity with Japan in February of the 13th year of Gojong, resulting in frequent communication with Japan. In addition, a new faction of enlightenment emerged in the court, and its power increased daily. In March of the year the Imo Incident occurred, the existing Five Military Commands (五營) were abolished and replaced with Two Military Commands (二營). At the same time, Lieutenant Horimoto was invited from Japan to provide new-style training to a separate unit of about 100 soldiers, and a military academy was established. It was natural for the old-style officers and soldiers to be dissatisfied. This resentment (懣) of Daewongun and his faction, the general suspicion and jealousy (疑嫉) of the people towards the Queen's policy and the relationship with Japan, and the soldiers' grievances became the atmosphere that led to the Imo Incident.

The Actual Events of the Imo Military Rebellion: In July of that year (June 9th by the lunar calendar), the Seonhyecheong paid one month's salary to the old-style soldiers, which had not been paid for 13 months. The distribution was unfair, and it was said that sand and gravel were mixed in it. The soldiers rioted and beat the storehouse officials. When the chief officer, Min Gyeom-ho, tried to punish the ringleaders, about 5,000 soldiers rose up together, attacking the homes of the Min clan, releasing the prisoners, and burning the Japanese Legation outside Seodaemun. At the same time, they killed Lieutenant Horimoto of the Training Academy and wept to Daewongun the next morning, entering Changdeok Palace and killing Min Gyeom-ho and several high officials. They searched for Queen Min, but she had already fled, so they were unsuccessful. At this time, Daewongun entered the palace, reorganized his own power, and announced Queen Min's death. However, Queen Min, who had actually sought refuge in the Janghowon area, secretly sent a person to Gojong to convey her intention to request assistance from Qing China. Gojong immediately requested troops from Qing China. Li Hongzhang, considering it a timely opportunity, provided 5,000 soldiers by land and sea, sending Wu Changqing and Ma Jianzhong to enter the capital on July 11th by the lunar calendar. On the 15th, Wu Changqing arrested Daewongun and sent him to Tianjin, later transferring him to Baoding Prefecture. Similarly, Japan, whose minister Hanabusa had fled, arrived in Incheon with four warships and about one battalion of soldiers, concluding the Treaty of Jemulpo (signed by Yi Yu-won and Kim Hong-jip on the Joseon side) on the day Daewongun was captured, bringing the situation to a conclusion. Due to this military rebellion, Japanese and Qing troops were stationed in the capital under the pretext of protecting the legation, and Joseon entered into their midst, laying the groundwork for the Gapsin Coup in the near future and the Sino-Japanese War in the distant future.

Fifth Lecture: The Gapsin Coup and the Treaty of Tianjin

The Origins of the Enlightenment Party: The Enlightenment Party was a faction that sought to promote the Restoration in Joseon by modeling after the Meiji Restoration of Japan. Its power began to form with the Treaty of Amity with Japan in Byeongja year. Now, if we briefly look at the recent relationship between Japan and Joseon, as mentioned earlier, Japan had already communicated with foreign countries during the shogunate era and conveyed its intentions to us. Also, in the first year of Meiji, it sent envoys to announce the restoration of the monarchy and the Restoration, requesting diplomatic relations. However, at this time, it was the era of Daewongun's isolationism and exclusion of foreigners, so it was not accepted. Later, when Queen Min gained power and opposed Daewongun's policies, she secretly prepared to trade with Japan. In September of the 12th year of Gojong, the Japanese warship Unyo-go, heading towards Qing China, anchored off the southern coast of Ganghwa Island to seek freshwater. The Ganghwa Fortress shelled it. The Japanese government, using this as a pretext, sent Kuroda Kiyotaka and Inoue Kaoru, along with some land and sea troops, to question the reasons for the repeated rejection of its national letter and the Unyo-go shelling incident, demanding the establishment of amity and trade. Although there was some opposition in the court, with the support of the Min faction led by Park Gyu-su and Oh Gyeong-seok, the Japan-Korea Treaty of Amity was concluded in Ganghwa Prefecture on February 26th. This was the first time in modern times that the path to Japan was opened. The content consisted of 12 articles, stating that Joseon was an independent state that consulted on all matters, and that Joseon would open the three ports of Busan (opened in the 13th year of Gojong), Wonsan (17th year of Gojong), and Incheon (20th year of Gojong) 20 months later. As a result of this Ganghwa Treaty, Hanabusa Yoshimoto came to reside in Gyeongseong (present-day Wangranjeong) in November of the 17th year of Gojong (Gyeongjin year) as a chargé d'affaires.

As friendly relations with Japan were established, the power of the Enlightenment Party, which had been on the same side from before, gradually increased. In May of the 18th year of Gojong, Park Jeong-yang, Eo Yun-jung, Hong Yeong-sik, and a dozen other high-ranking officials and gentlemen were selected to inspect Japan. In August of the same year, many conservatives were sacrificed due to Yi Jae-seon's treason. Furthermore, the subsequent reform of the military system further strengthened the position of the Enlightenment Party. It should be noted that the conservative faction before the Imo Incident refers to the Daewongun faction. Thus, when the Imo Military Rebellion occurred, the Enlightenment Party temporarily concealed its traces. However, with the dispatch of Japanese troops and Daewongun's transportation to Tianjin, its power was quickly restored. In August of the Imo year, Park Yeong-hyo, Kim Ok-gyun, and Seo Gwang-beom were dispatched to Japan as envoys and attendants. What should be remembered here is the oppression of Qing China and the changes in Joseon's political sphere after the Imo Incident. Now, if we briefly examine the relationship between Joseon and Qing China, it spans 259 years from the time Joseon established a suzerain relationship with Qing China in the 15th year of Injo (Jeongchuk year, 2nd year of Qing Chongde, 1637) until that relationship was practically severed by the Treaty of Shimonoseki between Japan and Qing in Byeongsin year, the 33rd year of Gojong. In recent times, due to the invasions of England and France and the southward advance of Russia since 1960, Qing China had little leeway to think about Joseon due to the many internal affairs and diplomatic challenges. Moreover, diplomatic problems arising in Joseon added to the difficulties in diplomatic relations. Therefore, when French missionaries were killed in Gyeongseong in 1866 or when the United States pressed Joseon to establish diplomatic relations with the S.S. General Sherman incident in 1973, or when Japan advocated the conquest of Korea the following year, Qing China responded by saying, "Joseon is not a vassal state of China" or "It follows the Chinese calendar, but declaring war and making peace is the freedom of that country." Afterward, Qing China, seeing that Joseon lacked true independence and that Japanese power was invading after the treaty with Japan in Byeongja year, changed its attitude and waited for the opportunity. Using the request for assistance during the Imo Military Rebellion as an opportunity, it dispatched a large army to suppress it. Then, it stationed Yuan Shikai with 2,000 soldiers, taking over Joseon's political and military power. Although high officials such as Hong Sun-mok, Kim Byeong-guk, Jo Nyeong-ha, and Kim Hong-jip were in the court, the actual affairs were mainly handled by Qing officials Yuan Shikai and Ma Jianchang, as well as the German advisor, Mollendorff. Li Hongzhang, in order to check Japan, also advised on trading with distant European and American countries, resulting in the conclusion of trade treaties with the United States in May of 1882 (Imo year) and with England and Germany in the following year, 1883 (Gyemi year).

The Independence Party and the Sadae Party: As Qing China's power grew in this way, the previous factions of the Enlightenment Party and the Conservative Party underwent a transformation. The traditional Conservative Party, which supported Daewongun, disappeared with Daewongun's downfall. The faction centered on the Min clan, which was previously on the same line as the Enlightenment Party, became attached to Qing China's power, forming a faction called the Sadae Party (事大黨). At the same time, the Independence Party (獨立黨) faction, which sought to obtain Joseon's restoration and independence by getting close to Japan, was established. Hong Yeong-sik, Kim Ok-gyun, Park Yeong-hyo, Seo Gwang-beom, Seo Jae-pil, and Yun Ung-ryeol were among its members. Although their numbers and power were inferior to the Sadae Party, they became a significant force. The discord between the two factions became increasingly severe, and in the 21st year of Gojong (Gapsin year), Qing China became embroiled in a conflict with France over the Annam issue. When the news reached Gyeongseong in August of the same year that the Qing navy had been defeated by the French army, the Independence Party, in consultation with the Japanese Minister Takezoe Shinichiro at the time, launched a major movement on December 4th of the same year (October 17th by the lunar calendar). This was the Gapsin Coup.

The Facts of the Gapsin Coup: Hong Yeong-sik (son of Chief State Councilor Hong Sun-mok, 30 years old that year), Kim Ok-gyun (34 years old), Park Yeong-hyo (24 years old), Seo Gwang-beom, and others, who had formed a party and watched for an opportunity from early on, conspired with a group of military academy students in advance. Taking the banquet at the Postal Bureau that opened from 6 PM on this day (October 17th by the lunar calendar) as an opportunity, they planned to kill the high officials coming out in surprise from the fire set near the Byeolgung around 10 PM at night all at once, but only managed to wound Min Yeong-ik, who came out first. As a second measure, Kim, Seo, Park, and others immediately entered Changdeok Palace and claimed that Qing soldiers were causing chaos and that there was an imminent crisis. They conspired with a company of Japanese soldiers by royal order to guard the royal palace. Again by royal order, they summoned and killed nine leaders of the Sadae Party, including Min Yeong-mok, Min Tae-ho, and Jo Nyeong-ha. That night, they organized a new government, with Hong Yeong-sik, Kim Ok-gyun, Park Yeong-hyo, and Seo Gwang-beom occupying all the state affairs. On the afternoon of the following day, they declared the expansion of national power and the innovation of the political world. However, on the third day, the 6th, Yuan Shikai, seeing the downfall of the Sadae Party, led a large army and entered through the Eight Gates (Dongbang Seoninmun). The soldiers of the Left and Right Camps inside the gate joined together, attacking the government side and the Japanese soldiers together. Kim Ok-gyun, Park Yeong-hyo, and others, realizing the wrongfulness of the situation, tried to turn the tide while holding onto the king until the very end, but were killed by the soldiers. The King entered the Qing military camp and organized a new government with Shim Sun-taek, Kim Byeong-si, and Kim Hong-jip. After returning to the palace 10 days later, Kim Ok-gyun's reform ended in just 30 days. Kim Ok-gyun was later lured from Japan to Shanghai in March of Gap-o year and was killed by the assassin Hong Jong-u.

In this upheaval, the Gyeongseong crowd burned down the Japanese Legation and killed a major. For this reason, Japan sent Inoue Kaoru the following January to conclude the Treaty of Hanseong, the contents of which were mostly compensation for damages. Since then, Japanese and Qing troops were in confrontation in Gyeongseong, and the situation was not reassuring. Japan sent Itō Hirobumi to Tianjin to conclude the Treaty of Tianjin with Li Hongzhang. The contents consisted of three articles:

1. Within four months from the date of signing, Japan and Qing China will both withdraw their troops from Joseon.
2. Encourage the King of Joseon to train soldiers to maintain public order themselves, and neither Japan nor Qing China shall send instructors.
3. In the future, when there is a disturbance or major incident in Joseon, Japan and Qing China shall notify each other before dispatching troops, and shall withdraw their troops as soon as the matter is concluded.

Based on this, both countries withdrew their troops together. From Joseon's perspective, this was truly a time to make independent efforts. However, the reactionary conservative forces after Gapsin did nothing other than enjoy the old-fashioned power and only led to the uprising of the masses by the Donghak Party and again saw the intervention of Japan and Qing.

Sixth Lecture: The Uprising of the Donghak Party and the Sino-Japanese War

After King Gojong's ascension to the throne, there was a period of Daewongun's internal reform, and after Gapseul, there were political parties of Enlightenment and Conservatism, Independence and Sadae, causing incidents like the Gapsin Reform. However, these were all just temporary disturbances within the inner circles of Gyeongseong and had little impact on the actual lives of the general public. Moreover, after Gapsin, the followers of the enlightened progressives were persecuted, and the government's autocracy, centered around the Min clan with the backing of Qing and Russia, further increased the suffering of the lower classes. As mentioned above, in the late Joseon Dynasty, a kind of sudden and temporary people's movement began to arise. During the reign of King Cheoljong, Donghak emerged, equipped with new truths and purposes that responded to the changing times, gaining the support of many common people. In the early years of King Gojong's reign, its leader, Choe Je-u, was killed, and its activities were subsequently prohibited. However, that which emerged in response to the times could not be forbidden by human power, and the situation over the next 30 years fostered its development. In the years 28 and 29 of Gojong, that is, between the years of Imjin and Gyosa, Donghak spread throughout the regions of Gyeongsang, Jeolla, Chungcheong, Gyeonggi, Gangwon, and Hwanghae. Its vigorous new power became a threat to the yangban, local magnates, and officials of the time. In response, these new and old forces confronted each other in various regions, leading to mass movements in Gongju and Jeonju. Even a sit-down demonstration by tens of thousands of people in front of Gwanghwamun occurred. However, the government, centered around the Min Yeong-jun, that is, the Min Yeong-hwi faction, at the time, only knew that there was a struggle for worldly affairs and watched the breath of foreign soldiers, and besides that, they did not know that there was anything else. They simply ordered Jeolla Governor Kim Mun-hyeon and Gyeongsang Governor Yi Yong-jik to appease and disband them. Later, they appointed Eo Yun-jung as the ambassador of reconciliation for the two lakes (Jeolla and Chungcheong Provinces) to carry out reconciliation, but there was no other consideration or consolation.

In March (late February by the lunar calendar) of the 31st year of Gojong (Gabo year, 1894), a group of Donghak followers led by Jeon Bong-jun and Kim Gae-nam killed Go-bu Magistrate Jo Byeong-gap and raised the beacon fire of protecting the country and comforting the people (除暴救民). First, the entire region of Jeolla and Gyeongsang responded, Gyeongsang Province Kimhae Magistrate Jo Jun-gu was expelled from the region by the local people, and the magistrates of various counties either surrendered or were sacrificed, and the situation greatly advanced. In May, the government appointed Hong Gye-hun, the former Jeolla Military Commander, as the commander of the two lakes, giving him 800 soldiers and directing him towards Gunsan. However, when the Donghak army advanced on Jeonju in the last ten days of that month, Governor Kim Mun-hyeon fled early, and the so-called government army, without any intention to fight, fled everywhere. Seeing this, Hong Gye-hun immediately submitted a memorial to the government, pleading for the inevitability of requesting a large army from Qing China. The government (Min Yeong-jun) immediately informed Yuan Shikai, and Yuan Shikai pleaded to Li Hongzhang, and about 1,500 Qing soldiers led by Ye Zhichao and Ye Shicheng arrived in Asan on June 6th. Qing China, while dispatching troops to Joseon in this way, notified Japan on the same month on the 7th, with a public document attached. Japan, while rebuking Qing China for violating the promise of prior notification in the Treaty of Tianjin, also decided to dispatch troops under the name of protecting residents. Minister Oshima, who had returned home at the time, returned to Gyeongseong on the 10th, leading 400 sailors, and later on the 13th, about 3,000 Japanese soldiers entered the capital. As the troops of both countries were dispatched, the Donghak Rebellion was suppressed, but as soon as the suppression ended, the conflict between Japan and Qing began, and on August 1st, the two countries declared war on each other.

The Donghak Party was subsequently defeated by the combined attack of Hong Gye-hun and Seoho Border Patrol Commander Yi Won-hoe, losing Jeonju, but again joined with the Son Ui-am army in the north, occupying Gongju. However, after being attacked by the combined forces of the three countries' armies, it was completely dispersed by the end of the year. What should be considered in this Donghak Rebellion are (1) that in the movement itself, unlike the Gapsin Enlightenment Party movement, which was a clash of political views between reformist and conservative factions within the same ruling class, it was a popular movement in which the common people, groaning under the dual oppression of politics and economy, attempted a fundamental reform of politics and society, (2) that the result shook the foundations of Joseon's traditional society, prompted the emergence of a reform government, and made both high and low taste the new atmosphere of change, and (3) that it laid the near cause of the Sino-Japanese War, changing the situation in East Asia.

Seventh Lecture: The Gabo Independence and the Eulmi Coup

Daewongun's Return: After the Gapsin Coup, the withdrawal of troops by Japan and Qing was carried out, but Qing stationed Yuan Shikai to monitor the government's every move. The government side, centered around the Min faction, was dissatisfied with Yuan and had already approached the Russian Minister Weber, who had entered the capital in June of the 21st year of Gojong and had signed the Korea-Russia Trade Treaty. Seeing this, Yuan Shikai hated Queen Min and the royal palace; it was October of Eulmi year. After Daewongun's return, Yuan further manipulated the government's internal and external affairs, even preventing the dispatch of overseas ministers at the end of the 24th year of Gojong. When Russian Minister Weber returned home in the spring of the 27th year, Qing's power became even greater, but it could not distance itself from the power of the Min clan.

The Gabo Reform: In fact, due to the Donghak Rebellion, Japan and Qing dispatched troops in May and June of Gabo, Qing advocated mutual troop withdrawal with Japan, and Japan insisted on the reform of Joseon. Eventually, Japan proposed the Five Articles of Reform to the government through Minister Oshima on July 3rd, holding a meeting at Noin-jeong on the 15th, and then forcing the severance of diplomatic relations with Qing. Yuan Shikai fled, and the winds of East Asia were imminent. Seeing this, Jo Ui-yeon, Yu Gil-jun, Gwon Yeong-jin, Kim Ga-jin, and others of the Enlightenment Party conspired with Minister Oshima and invaded Gyeongbok Palace with Daewongun on July 23rd (June 21st by the lunar calendar), exiling Min Yeong-jun, Min Hyeong-sik, Kim Se-gi, and others of the Min clan party to remote areas on charges of corruption. On the 26th, they established the Temporary Military and State Affairs Office, appointing Kim Hong-jip as president and appointing 17 people, including Min Yeong-dal, Kim Hak-u, Yu Gil-jun, and Park Jeong-yang, as council members to plan the innovation of state affairs. The fact that 208 laws and regulations were resolved here in about three months until its abolition on February 17th shows how urgent the reform was at that time. The high officials under the new government system revised at the time were Prime Minister Kim Hong-jip, Minister of the Palace Yi Jae-myeon, Minister of Internal Affairs Min Yeong-dal, Minister of Foreign Affairs Kim Yun-sik, Minister of Finance Eo Yun-jung, Minister of Justice Yun Yong-gu, Minister of Public Works Seo Jeong-sun, Minister of Education Park Jeong-yang, Minister of Military Affairs Yi Gyu-won, Minister of Agriculture and Commerce Eom Se-yeong, and Police Commissioner An Gyeong-su.

As the Sino-Japanese War progressed, the government made the Japan-Korea Alliance on August 26th, and at the end of November, Inoue Kaoru came in place of Minister Oshima, intensifying negotiations with the government. Daewongun was retired, and Park Yeong-hyo and Seo Gwang-beom, the exiles of Gapsin, were brought back as ministers of internal affairs and justice, respectively, thus forming a coalition cabinet of Kim Hong-jip and Park Yeong-hyo. The crack in the reform government arose from here.

In any case, the reform of state affairs progressed steadily. On January 7th of the 32nd year of Gojong (December 12th of Gabo by the lunar calendar), Gojong reported the reasons for the reform of state affairs and the independent sovereignty to Tae-myo and declared the Fourteen Articles of Hongbeom. On the 11th, the government requested to refer to the King as Grand Monarch, and sent ministers to foreign countries, showing the appearance of Joseon's sovereignty both internally and externally.

Alas! The Dissolution of the Reformist Cabinet: As Park Yeong-hyo returned and became the Minister of Internal Affairs, he wanted to form a central cabinet. Queen Min also used Park to remove Kim Hong-jip, Eo Yun-jung, and Jo Hui-yeon, who were political enemies at the time, and wanted to revive her own power. In response, Park first dismissed Jo Hui-yeon of the Military Affairs Department in May of that year (Eulmi year). Enraged by this, Kim Hong-jip and Yu Gil-jun resigned together, and eventually the moderate Park Jeong-yang cabinet came to power, taking orders from Park in everything. Minister Jeongsang of Japan, who was relatively enthusiastic about the Joseon Restoration, did not get along with Park and returned in June. In response, Queen Min immediately began her activities, establishing the Special Promotion Officer system in the palace to build her own faction, gradually distancing herself from Park. Seo Gwang-beom and Kim Ga-jin also disliked Park's dictatorship and resigned from their positions. Furthermore, unexpectedly, the Park Yeong-hyo conspiracy theory emerged, and Park fled back to Japan in early July. The momentum of reform that had been trying to establish itself was thus ended, and the Min faction and pro-Russian faction regained power, eventually causing the coup on the 10th of that year, and the great task of restoration came to an end. The harm of factional strife is truly great.

The October Coup of Eulmi: As a result of the Sino-Japanese War, Japan reaped the fruits of victory with the Treaty of Shimonoseki in April of Eulmi year, but soon faced the intervention of Russia, Germany, and France, inevitably returning the Liaodong Peninsula on May 5th. Russia's power suddenly pressed down on the Far East. The former Minister Weber returned to Gyeongseong, and Inoue went and Miura Goro came in September of the same year, but the situation in the government changed completely, and the era of the pro-Russian faction was about to arrive. The fate of the Enlightenment Party, backed by Japan, was like a lamp before the wind. Seeing this, Gwon Yeong-jin, Jo Hui-yeon, Kim Ga-jin, and others conspired with Japanese Minister Miura and the Ronin group, and on October 8th (August 20th by the lunar calendar) at dawn, led Daewongun under the vanguard of Japanese soldiers, entered the palace, dismissed the pro-Russian brothers Yi Beom-jin and Yi Yun-yong, and reappointed Kim Hong-jip. In addition to being in charge, Jo Hui-yeon was appointed as the Military Affairs Minister, Yu Gil-jun as the Internal Affairs Minister, and Gwon Yeong-jin as the Police Commissioner. Kim Yun-sik, Seo Gwang-beom, Jeong Byeong-ha, and others were appointed to various high positions, and the aim was to continue the Gabo Reformation and carry out a Great Restoration. However, Queen Min was assassinated in this chaos. Upon receiving this report, Japan confined Minister Miura and 40 others to Hiroshima to avoid raising international issues. At the same time, the government announced Queen Min's state funeral in November and, fearing the boiling public opinion, dismissed Jo Hui-yeon and Gwon Yeong-jin from their positions. Also, the use of the solar calendar (designating November 17th of the 504th year of the founding of the country as January 1st of the 505th year and starting to call the year Geonyang) and the issuance of the haircut order, as well as the establishment of schools, greatly offended public sentiment. Along with the resignation of Lee Do-jae of the Department of Education, righteous armies rose up in various places, and there was no way to manage the situation. In the midst of this, on February 11th (December 28th by the lunar calendar), the King suddenly moved to the Russian Legation, Park Jeong-yang became the acting prime minister, and Yi Gye-yong became the police commissioner. Kim Hong-jip and Jeong Byeong-ha were killed in front of the Police Department, and Eo Yun-jung was killed in Yongin. Others (Minister of Internal Affairs Yu Gil-jun, Minister of Military Affairs Jo Hui-yeon, Minister of Justice Jang Bak, Gwon Yeong-jin, Yi Du-hwang, U Beom-seon, Yi Beom-rae, Yi Jin-ho, etc.) fled, and the pro-Russian faction's Yi Beom-jin and others seized power, declaring the revocation of the haircut order and the abolition of all reform matters after Gabo. The Restoration Movement of Joseon, which seemed to be successful, came to an end with this. At the same time, the struggle between Daewongun and Queen Min, who had been mutually hostile since Gapja, ended with the Queen Min disturbance in Eulmi, and Daewongun passed away at the age of 79 in Musul year, the 2nd year of Gwangmu.

第四講 閔妃의 專政과 壬午軍變
大院君은 執政10년에 舊弊를 破하고 제도를 整하고 倉*을 充케하는 등 幾多의 施設을 행하엿스나 그 방법이 넘우 矯激하야 臣民上下로 하여금 憂懼의 念을 갓게 하얏고 더욱 궁전을 修키 위한 誅求와 書院의 撤廢와 가튼 것은 한便으로 민심을 일케 함이 되는 동시에 儒生의 激昻케 함이 되엿다. 이러한 중 高宗 3년에는 驪州閔致祿의 女를 듸려 王妃를 삼게된 바 이 機警明敏한 성격을 소유한 민비는 궁에 드러온지 해를 지내며 가만히 자기의 兄되는 閔昇鎬 一派를 등용식히여 대원군과 대립하는 준비를 不怠하엿다. 이러는 間에 李恒老
崔益鉉등의 親政奏請이 잇서 고종10년(癸亥)에 大院君은 정계를 은퇴하는 不得已에 至하엿다.
대원군의 은퇴와 한가지로 니러날 것은 민비의 專權이다. 더욱 高宗11년 甲戌에 元子가 탄생되고 니려 그 翌年에 세자로 책봉되자 일국의 정권은 그대로 민비를 중심삼는 閔族一黨에게 도라가 自高宗10年至12年의 23년간을 宛然한 閔氏一族의 천하를 형성하엿다. 대원군은 이 期에 잇서 虎視耽耽으로 민비와 대립한 바. 이 兩大勢力을 중심으로 한양의 정국은 波瀾重疊에 그 진로를 붓잡지 못하고 말앗스니 高宗19년의 壬午軍變은 그 幕의 第一이라할가.
이에 先하야 閔后가 집권하며 대원군시대의 人與政을 모조리 변하야 朴珪壽
李最應
閔奎鎬
閔台鎬
閔泳穆 등을 중용하고 서원을 부활식혓스며 때에 마츰 閔昇鎬의 집에 爆彈一箱이 와서 一家를 전멸한 바 이를 大院側의 소위라 하야 대원군의 庶子 李載先을 逆謀로 모라서 죽인바 대원군의 忿恨은 其極에 달하엿스며 加之而當時 정부는 대원군의 鎖國政策을 排하고 高宗 13년 2월에 일본과 修交條規를 결하야 일본과의 通交가 빈번할뿐 아니라 조정에는 개화파가 신생하야 其勢가 日增하는 중 壬午年變이 닐던 그 해 3월에는 在來의 五營을 폐하고 二營을 두는 동시에 일본으로부터 堀本中尉를 請하야＜140＞ 別로 100여명의 一隊에 新式의 훈련을 주고 다시 士官學校를 設한 바 구식의 士官兵丁이 불만을 가젓슬 것은 물론이다. 이러한 대원군 及 其一派의 忿 懣民族의 惠政及交日에 대한 일반의 疑嫉, 병사의 불평등은 壬午의 사변을 니르키는 분위기가 되엿다.
壬午軍變의 實事 이해 7월(陰6월 9일)에 宣惠廳에서는 其間 13개월을 지불치 못하던 舊式軍卒의 1개월분 급료를 지불하엿는데 其分配가 不公하고 또 거긔에는 砂礫을 석것다 하야 병사들은 폭동을 니르켜 庫吏를 亂打하엿다. 堂上 閔謙鎬는 그 주모자를 벌하려한 즉 營兵 약5천은 한가지로 니러나 閔氏諸家를 襲하고 罪囚를 놋코 서대문외의 日公館을 불놋는 동시에 훈련원의 堀本中尉를 죽이고 翌日 새벽에는 대원군에게 泣訴하고 昌德宮으로 드러가 閔謙鎬외 몃 大官을 자살하고 민비를 搜索하엿스나 그는 벌서 다라낫슴으로 不果하엿다. 이때 대원군은 宮中에 드러가 자기중심의 세력을 更植하고 민비의 喪을 發하얏스나 사실로 長湖院方面에 請避한 민비는 가만히 고종에게 사람을 보내여 淸國에 淸援할 뜻를 通하얏슴으로 高宗은 곳 淸國에 請兵한 즉 이때의 李鴻章은 時不可失이라 하야 水陸兵 5천을 주어 吳長慶 馬建忠등으로 하여금 陰7월 11일에 入京하야 同15일에 吳長慶은 대원군을 잡아 천진으로 보내엿다가 後에 保定府로 移囚하얏스며 일본은 淸國과 同樣으로 一段逃避한 花房公使가 軍艦四隻과 陸兵 약 1개 大隊를 거느리고 인천에 니르러 대원군이 붓잡히던 그날에 제물포조약(조선에서는 李裕元 金宏集)을 체결하야 一段落을 告하니 이 軍變으로 인하야 公使館保護의 名으로 일본군대와 청국군대가 아울너 경성에 주둔케 되야 조선이 비로소 그 와중에 드는 동시에 갓가히는 갑신정변 멀니는 日淸전쟁을 니르키는 원인을 지엿다.

第五講 甲申政變과 天津條約
開化黨의 生成由來 개화당은 일본의 명치유신을 則하야 조선의 유신을 促成하려는 一便인바 丙子의 대일수교와 더부러 其勢를 조성하게 되엿스니 이제 일본과의 최근 通交關係를 一瞥하면 우에서 暫述한 바와가티 일본에서는 일즉히 막부시대에 외국과 통상하고 其意를 우리에게 전하엿고 또 明治元年에 使들 보내여 왕정의 복고와 유신을 告하여 통교를 청하엿스나 이때는 대원군의 鎖國排外時代이라 역시 불응하엿스며 後 閔妃가 세력을 잡으며 대원정책에 反하야 그윽히 일본과 통상할 준비를 하던중 高宗 12년 9월에 日軍 艦雲楊號가 청국을 향하던 도중 淡水를 求코저 강화도 남단에 投錨하엿슬때 江華砲臺에서 이를 砲擊한바 일본정부는 이를 언지로 若于의 수륙병과 아울너 黑田淸隆井上馨을 보내여 다년 자기의 국서거절 이유 及 同雲楊號砲擊事件을 詰하고 修好通商을 要한바 조정에서는 일부의 반대가 잇섯스나 朴珪壽 吳慶錫을 위시한 閔一派의 찬동이 잇서서 2월 26일 江華府에서 日韓修好條規를 結하니 이것이 근세에 잇서 일본과 길을 열은 처음이며 그 내용은 12款으로 成한바 조선은 자주국으로써 만사를 상의한＜141＞ 것과 또 조선은 20개월후에 부산(고종 13년 개항), 元山(고종17년), 인천(고종 20년)의 三港을 開한다는 등이엿다. 이 강화조약의 결과로 고종17년 庚辰11월에 花房義質이 代理公使로 비로서 경성(現王然亭)에 來駐하엿다.
이와가티 일본과의 수호가 되며 그 전부터 한便이 되여 오던 개화파의 세력은 漸增하는 중 고종18년 5월에 朴定陽
魚允中
洪英植 등 大官紳士十數名을 선발하야 일본을 시찰케한 事와 同年 8월에 李載先 逆謀로 守舊派가 만히 犧牲된 事와 또 니여서 兵制가 개혁된 事등은 일층 개호파의 형세를 크게 함이 되엿다. 대개 壬午이전까지의 수구파라하면 대원군파를 指함임을 주의하자 이리다가 임오군변이 닐며 一時는 개화파가 迹을 隱하엿스나 일본의 出兵 대원군의 天津押送과 한가지로 其勢는 적히 회복되야 壬午 8월에는 朴泳孝
金玉均
徐光範등이 修信使 及 隨員으로 일본에까지 파견되여섯다. 그런대 여긔에서 기억할 것은 壬午 이후 청국의 抑壓과 조선정치 분야의 변천이니 이제
朝鮮對淸國의 관계를 暫瞥하면 조선이 仁祖15년 丁丑(淸崇德 2년 1637년)에 청국과 宗主의 관계를 매진 이후 고종 33년 丙申 日淸間의 馬關條約후에 의하야 그 관계가 실지로 단절되기까지 凡259년이다. 그런중 최근 1960년이래 英佛의 침입과 露國의 南進 등으로 內治外交에 多端하야 조선을 생각할 여유가 업슬뿐더러 조선에서 생기는 외교문제는 도로혀 국교상 一難을 加함으로 1866년 경성에 佛宣敎師가 쥑이올때나 1973년 癸酉 미국이 쎄맨號 사건으로 조선에 통교를 迫할때나 翌年 일본이 征韓論을 주창할때에 청국은 「조선은 중국의 속국이 아니라」고도 하고 혹은 「중국의 正朔을 奉하나 宣戰媾和는 그 國의 자유에 잇다.」는 등의 회답을 하엿다. 그후 조선이 자립의 實이 업고 丙子에 일본과 수교이후 일본의 세력이 침입함을 본 중국은 곳 태도를 변하야 그 시기를 기다리던 중 壬午軍變時의 請援을 기회로하야 곳 대군을 出하야 이를 鎭하고 니여 袁世凱로 하여금 2천의 兵을 갓고 駐留케하며 조선의 정치과 병권을 아울너 거두어 조정에는 비록 洪淳穆
