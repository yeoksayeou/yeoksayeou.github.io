To a Comrade, from a Comrade
My Regret, Your Regret (Among Reporters)

P.C. Saeng

Mr. K, it's been almost ten years since you and I, under the guise of so-called reporters, have been aimlessly wandering in this muddy stream of transition! How much of this world's sweet and bitter taste have you experienced during that time? Or how much profit and loss have you seen? I, who am not you, cannot easily know the quality of your possessions, but if I confess frankly, everything is zero, and at the same time, my entire past is full of regret. This is a problem to be addressed not only to the environment of the times but also to myself.

Mr. K, what use is it to speak of regret now, but whether you and I were bound by our livelihoods or by the trends of the times, we are still carrying the sign of something showy and unsubstantial, true or false, and when I think of all the comrades around us and all the comrades of tomorrow who will be wandering in the same track, I cannot help but say something. As birds of a feather flock together, I cannot help but say something to you, Mr. K. Therefore, I cannot tell you about my regrets and your potential regrets. Please understand this as a matter of friendship.

Mr. K, truly, all of my past is filled with regret. First of all, it is a true confession that I never had any qualifications to carry the sign of a reporter. As you have experienced, a news organization (whether it be a newspaper or a magazine) is the ears and eyes of society and the mirror of the times, so shouldn't a reporter, as a member of that organization, also be the ears and eyes of society and the mirror of the times? Isn't that what a reporter does by taking on this role? In other words, isn't the reporter's duty to observe every good, evil, beauty, and ugliness, hidden or visible, big or small, of that era and society, report them truthfully under strict criticism, and become a standard and mirror for the general public?

Then, Mr. K, what qualifications should one have and how should one act to fulfill this duty? As you have also experienced and felt, shouldn't one have a lot of knowledge, a lot of experience, be bright in appreciation, and strong in criticism? Shouldn't one possess a personality that is diligent, honest, thorough, meticulous, calm, and decisive, in addition to such knowledge?

But, Mr. K, I didn't have any of that. Truly, I didn't even know the meaning of being a reporter and jumped in blindly, driven by the trends of the times. It's a shameful thing to say, but you probably weren't much different from me.

Mr. K, I, who was almost a zero in terms of knowledge and personality, have been acting like a so-called reporter for eight or nine years, so how shameful do you think that is? My conscience is tormented, and how great a sin have I committed against society? Even if it's not a direct comparison, I don't think your self-reproach is any less than mine.

Even so, if I had been faithful to my duty, I wouldn't have had so much regret, but I wasn't faithful either. I should have been diligent, but I was more often lazy; I should have been honest, but I was more often false and fabricated; I should have been thorough and meticulous, but I often only skimmed the surface; I couldn't be calm when I should have been calm, I couldn't be kind when I should have been kind, I couldn't be decisive when I should have been decisive, I couldn't be composed when I should have been composed, I confused public and private matters, reversed big and small matters, couldn't distinguish between good and evil, couldn't tell beauty from ugliness, was swayed by a glass of alcohol, 넘어갔고 by a single envelope of money, 용서하고 by a single praise, 굴하고 by a single threat, and even sacrificed my principles for green robes and red skirts, so how shameful do you think that is? It's a shameful thing to say, but if you think about it, you probably have many reasons to feel like throwing a bonfire on your face.

Mr. K, since we are in an impartial position, let me speak frankly. Let's throw away hypocrisy and be faithful to our duties until the day we die. That was six or seven years ago. Why did you insist on saying "yes" alone to the 0氏00葬問題 (the funeral problem of Mr. 00) that the world denied? Did you get some drinks from his subordinate at that time? How shameful is that to think about now? And listen, Mr. K. That was probably six or seven years ago too. When one of your own kind went to the West and got even a minor position in some meeting, why did you make such a national celebration out of it for three or four months? Did that person give you some drinks after returning home? That must be something to feel ashamed about too.

I probably don't remember things from too long ago. Just a year ago. Why did you write such a big and special article about the 000事 (000 event) that the world hates, and earn the criticism of the world? You shouldn't do that either. Let's leave that aside. Recently, why did you write a special article on the first page of the second section of a newspaper, which is a public instrument of the people, for three consecutive days about a certain movement article sponsored by your own company? Did you like the scent of women's perfume so much? A reporter who holds a public pen in a public instrument of the people cannot do that.

If I were to talk about it, wouldn't there be more? Even those so-called three thieves (三賊) are sold for a cup of alcohol and a cigarette, introducing 行何處出 (where they went), and those who everyone calls Black Master (黑大監) 극구頌德 (sing praises to the extreme) for a ring or an envelope of money. Well, in this world where even important articles from important organizations are ignored and the birth of a colleague's son is written about in a big and special way, there is no need to talk about reporter ethics.

Mr. K, from now on, appeal to your conscience and be faithful to your duties with a fair attitude. And thus redeem all the regrets of the past. Don't blame me for speaking recklessly. I have shared all my regrets with you out of friendship.

My Hope (Among Lawyers)

Kim Byeong-ro

Whether we have power or not, we are people in the legal profession whose duty is to protect and promote the rights of the general public. If there is anything that benefits the people, we should act and argue fairly within the legal standpoint or legal scope to the fullest extent, and we should eliminate illegal things to the fullest extent.

However, as the saying goes, "one hand cannot clap," it will be difficult to carry out that task well with the power of only one or two people. Moreover, we Koreans have special circumstances. Although the number of people in the legal profession may not be said to be many, if we combine Seoul and the provinces, there are also a large number of people.

If we unite and take legal action, the effect will be great. To put it simply, because people in the provinces do not know the law well, they suffer unfair damage and pain in terms of property, reputation, or physical well-being, but they do not know how to make any complaints. Even if they do know, because of their circumstances, they only worry and complain secretly in some corner. Not only the parties themselves but also those who are responsible for overseeing and supervising many people often misinterpret and misapply the law, causing unnecessary pain to others and violating their rights. If we had an organization that could unite us to always pay attention to and monitor whether such facts exist, promote the interests of the people, warn those who use the law, and propose and advocate for the enactment of laws that will benefit the general public in the future and the improvement of past incomplete laws, I think it would be more necessary than anything else. In addition, I also think it is necessary to publish a legal newspaper or legal magazine as an organ to comment on legal knowledge and legal issues facing the general public. (Omitted below)

Two Requests (Among Doctors)

Park Chang-hun

It is very meaningful and good to give words that you want to say to someone in the same situation, and it is more gratifying than anything else to be able to say something to a fellow comrade on this occasion, but because I am too busy, I cannot speak in detail and instead replace it with these two short requests, and it is only unfortunate that I cannot express all the meanings I want to say.

1. Although being a doctor is a profession, of course, it should not be too professionalized.
Since the origin of medicine is simultaneous with the creation of human history, it is a necessary part of human life. Therefore, a doctor engaged in medicine must have a great awareness. In other words, human life, which is the most spiritually valuable among living things, is different from inanimate machines, so if a doctor treats a patient like a mechanical engineer treats a machine, it would be an unforgivable sin. At least, one must adhere to human ethics and medical ethics. Although it has not been very long since Western medicine was imported into our country, the degree of respect for fellow practitioners is still weak, so the responsibility must be borne by our fellow practitioners themselves, and furthermore, efforts to restore the credibility that has been sluggish so far and to improve it even further are absolutely necessary. Isn't it embarrassing that people now tend to see medicine as a business and doctors as merchants?

2. One must be kind, courteous, and full of ambition and confidence.
The medical sage Hippocrates famously said, "God (nature) cures the disease, and the doctor nurses the disease." However, he meant that the doctor does not cure the disease with his own power, but just as a machinist uses the power generated by the heat energy of the machine itself to control the car on the track, the doctor cures the patient by borrowing the power of nature and nursing the patient (monitoring and controlling the progress of the car in the same way as controlling a locomotive). In other words, it is not that modern medicine and the power of medicine cure the disease, but that they only lead the course of the disease that nature makes with medicine and drugs by borrowing the power of nature. A famous doctor is only a famous nurse, and a bad doctor is one who spoils even the disease that nature would cure. And the more severe the patient's illness, the more polite and kind you should be. When the patient gets better, you should rather say to the patient, "Thank you for getting better," because a doctor's humility must come from such a mind, otherwise it is false humility, and if that kindness does not truly come from the brain, it is false kindness, and at the same time, such a doctor is not a doctor as a doctor, but becomes a doctor as a merchant. In this time when the economic state of Koreans is almost ruined, if you distinguish between 厚遇 (favor) and 薄待 (disfavor) by looking at the patient's status or money (assuming that you are a doctor as a merchant) without understanding and sympathizing with life and illness, you will know in advance what the end will be. Therefore, I think that my comrade must always have the determination not to engage in medicine without having a considerable ambition and confidence in medicine that will be renewed daily. If a mechanical engineer makes a mistake in fixing a machine due to a lack of skill and experience, the loss will be only a small amount, but if a doctor makes a mistake and ruins a disease, the sin is small even if he goes to the underworld and receives it.

Ten Thoughts (Among Female Teachers)

Son Jeong-gyu

1. Let's live faithfully, even if it's not rewarding.
2. Let's live with a sacrificial spirit in everything we do.
3. Let's seek knowledge widely and always reflect on our position.
4. Let's not think of teaching as a way to make money. Let's think of it as something that requires us to spend money.
5. A teacher who only knows books seems to be far from experience, and an experienced teacher seems to be ignorant of theory. In other words, we must always be aware of our shortcomings and study.
6. Let's not express our feelings too much when we are upset. People can understand enough and be influenced enough.
7. Let's aim for the adequacy of character rather than the superiority of academics and guide accordingly. Both are good - it would be even better if it were 적의 (appropriate).
8. We female teachers should especially observe the inner circumstances of each student as much as possible. There are many unfortunate situations where they have no place to discuss their problems.
9. Let's have a connection of hearts while teaching subjects.
10. When you are lonely, reading is the best companion, but even if that is not good, let's change the type of books. Even inappropriate books can be very touching.

가튼 처지에 잇서서 가튼 사람에게 하고 십흔 말을 주는 것은 대단히 의미잇고도 조흔 일이라 이런 긔회에 가튼 동지에게 한 말을 하게 된 것이 무엇보다도 깃분 일이나 너무 밧분 시간에 자세한 말슴을 하지 못하고 이 짜른 두 가지 부탁을 말슴으로 대신하는 것이라 말하고 십흔 바 뜻을 다하지 못하는 것만이 퍽아나 유감입니다.
1. 의사가 職業的임은 물론이지만 너무 직업화해서는 안될 것입니다.
의술의 기원이 인류 역사의 刱始와 동시인 만큼 인류 생활에 필요한 일부분이 되는 동시에 따라서 醫業에 종사하는 의사는 반드시 큰 자각이 잇지 안이하면 안될 것입니다. 즉 생물중에 가장 靈貴한 人生은 무생물의 기계와 다른 즉 의사가 환자를 대할 때 機械工業家가 機械를 대하는 것처럼 한다면 그야말로 용서치 못할 죄악일 것이니 적어도 인류 도덕과 의사 도덕을 준수해야만 될 것입니다. 서양의술이 우리 나라에 수입된지 時日이 그러케 짤지 안치만 아직도 일반으로 서양의술을 그러케 신뢰치 안이하야 同業者에 대한 敬愛의 度가 희박함은 그 責을 맛당히 우리 동업자 스스로가 지지 안이하면 안될 것이요 한거름 더 나아가서는 지금까지 不振하던 신용을 회복식히며, 더 한층 향상식힐 노력이 절대로 필요할 것입니다. 세상 사람이 지금 醫業을 한 商業으로 보며 醫師를 한 商人으로 보는 경향이 잇슴은 엇지나 붓그러룬 일이 안이겟습니까?
2. 남에게 親切丁寧하고도 포부와 자신이 잇서야 만 할 것입니다.
醫聖 힙포크리테스가 揚言한 격언에 『神(自然)은 病을 곳치고 醫師는 病을 看護한다.』라고 하엿스나 그는 즉 의사의 힘으로 병을 곳치는 것이 안이라 마치 機關手가 機關車自體의 熱에네리기로 말미암이 생기는 동력이라는 놈을 잘 이용하야 궤도상에 車를 조종하는 것과 가티 의사가 病人을 곳침은 자연의 힘을 빌어서 病人을 간호(機關車를 操縱하는 것과 맛찬가지로 병의 경과를 監視 조종함)함에 지나지 못하는 것입니다. 즉 현대의 의술과 약의 힘으로 병을 치료식히는 것이 안이라 자연의 힘을 빌어 가지고<29> 의술과 약으로 병의 경과를 자연이 만드는 경과 내로 잇그러 주는데 불과한 것이니 즉 名醫라는 사람은 名看護員에 불과한 것이요 惡醫라는 사람은 자연이 곳처 줄 병도 잡치는 사람입니다. 그리고 환자가 병이 심하야 질사록 더욱 丁寧親切히 해야 할 것이요, 병이 낫거던 도리어 환자에게 대하야 『당신의 병이 나어서 감사하다.』고 치사를 해야 할 것이니 그러면 의사의 恭遜이라는 것이 이런 심리에서 울어 나온 恭遜이 아니면 그는 그짓 恭遜이요, 그 친절이 참으로 腦裡에서 울어 나온 것이 안이면 그는 그짓 친절인 동시에 그런 의사는 의사로서의 의사가 아니요, 상인으로서의 의사가 되고 말것입니다. 황차 조선사람가티 경제 형태가 거의 파멸된 이때에 인생과 병고에 대한 이해와 동정이 업시(상인으로서의 의사라 가정하면)손님의 지위나 금전을 보고 厚遇, 薄待의 차별을 붓친다 하면 그 끗치 엇지 될가를 미리 알 것입니다. 그럼으로 나는 동지에게 늘-日新될 의학에 상당한 포부와 자신을 갓지 안코는 의업에 종사치 안켓다는 결심이 잇서야 할 것이라고 생각합니다. 機械工業家가 자기의 기술과 경험이 부족하여 일개 機械를 잘못 곳첫다면 그 損은 불과 얼마 안이 되겟지만 의사가 잘못하야 병을 그릇친다면 그 죄는 黃泉에까지 가서 밧는다 하더래도 오히려 적다 할 것입니다.
