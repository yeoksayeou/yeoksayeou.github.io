Has Employment Increased? How Was the Life of Koreans This Year? A Comparison of Last Year and This Year from Various Perspectives
Personnel Consultation Office, Kyŏngsŏng Meiji-chō

Have the unemployed increased, or have the employed increased? I visited the Personnel Consultation Office in Meiji-chō, Seoul, to get a glimpse of the hardship of the world, and the bitter numbers they showed me were as follows:

Job Openings Koreans Male 1,936 people Female 1,168 people
Job Seekers Koreans Male 5,513 people Female 1,175 people
Employed Koreans Male 1,047 people Female 93 people
(The above is the statistics for the last year)

From January to the end of October this year:
Number of Job Openings Koreans Male 1,299 people Female 1,160 people
Number of Job Seekers Koreans Male 4,607 people Female 1,143
Number of Employed Koreans Male 990 people Female 864 people

Employed by Age Group Men Approximately 70% are 16 to 20 years old
Approximately 20% are 21 to 50 years old
Approximately 10% are 12 to 15 years old
Women Approximately 20% are 12 to 20 years old
Approximately 60% are 21 to 40 years old
Approximately 20% are over 40 years old

Job Seekers by Age Group Men Most are 16 to 25 years old
Women Most are 25 to 40 years old

Job Types Men Approximately 30% are domestic servants, approximately 40% are in shops, approximately 10% are in factories and mines, and approximately 20% are in miscellaneous jobs.
Women Approximately 60% are domestic servants, approximately 20% are in shops, and approximately 20% are in miscellaneous jobs.

Job Types Men Most are domestic servants and in shops
Women Also the same

Employed by Education Level Men Approximately 50% are graduates of common schools (普通學校),
Approximately 10% are graduates of secondary schools (中等學校)
Approximately 30% are dropouts from common middle schools (普中)
Approximately 70% can read (解字者)
Approximately 30% have higher specialized education or above
Women Education level not counted

Employed by Education Level Men Most are graduates of common schools (普校) and middle schools (中)
Women Most can understand Japanese (日語解得者) <178>
