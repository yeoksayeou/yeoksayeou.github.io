Article Type: 회고·수기 (Reminiscence/Memoir)

A Chastised Illegitimate Child
崔秉和

1
"What in this world will save me? My heart, fallen into injustice and dried up, cannot be saved even by Christ or Shakyamuni. Find someone to save me if you can. Huh..."
I slammed the empty bottle down, wanting to find someone to fight. Then, sighing,
"Bring me liquor!" I glared at the waitress.
"You're quite drunk already. Please stop," the waitress said, startled, probably not wanting to deal with a drunk.
"Don't worry. Even if I get drunk, won't my liver take care of it?" I said, showing her my wallet. The day after drinking like this, I always fall into terrible agony. Even lying in bed, I'm consumed by unbearable anxiety. Everyone in the world looks like an enemy to me. They look like terrible demons. So, I drink to forget. Being drunk gives me some courage and relief.

Who created this kind of human being? Who plunged me, a young man full of youthful passion who came to the city, into this abyss of injustice and corruption? It's not women. It's not alcohol. It's not the vampires lurking in the city. There's only one person in this world. It's my father. My father!
The father I longed for for 20 years pushed his son into a deep pit from which he could never emerge. That was my father's last gift to his son.
I am an illegitimate child!
Born like everyone else in this world, the word "illegitimate child" placed upon my head is a strong, cold iron chain that I can never escape throughout my life.

"Kyŏng-uk! Who is your father?"
It was one spring day when I was in the fifth grade of elementary school. After school, as I entered the office with the classroom diary, Miss Kim, my female teacher, asked me. Suddenly, with a strange feeling, I looked at Miss Kim and answered,
"My father has passed away."
I had always wondered why I didn't have a father and felt lonely, so I had asked my mother about it.
"Your father passed away when you were two years old," my mother said with a sad look, bowing her head. I had always honestly believed those words.
"Oh, is that so?" Miss Kim said lightly.
"You don't seem to know anything. I don't even know if your father is still alive. I think he might be."
"Yes? Is my father alive?" I asked, my eyes widening.
"I don't know for sure, but I think he might be."
"How did you know about my father, teacher?"
"Well! Isn't it because you're an illegitimate child?"
"Teacher! What does 'illegitimate child' mean? Please tell me," I asked with a sincere attitude, stepping close to the female teacher, as if I were about to cling to her knees. Seeing the ripples of a small stone thrown carelessly unexpectedly widening, Miss Kim began to feel somewhat uneasy and fearful.
"No. It's nothing. What I said about your father passing away is definitely true."
"Teacher! Please tell me what the word 'illegitimate child' means."
"It's nothing. Don't ask anyone about that word. And it's late, so go home quickly," she said, forcibly retracting her words, but her face was very pale with regret.
A stain of ink fallen on clean white paper can never be made as white as before, no matter how much you wipe and wash it away. The small ripples of the water falling into my childhood heart spread endlessly across the sea of my mind.

2
"Mother—I heard that Father is alive?" I asked my mother. My mother, who was sewing, dropped the cloth as soon as she heard these words, her face suddenly turning as pale as a dead person's.
"Who said that?" My mother's voice trembled greatly.
"Miss Kim said so. She said my father is probably alive, and that I'm an illegitimate child. What does 'illegitimate child' mean?"
"That...even that the teacher told you!" My mother trembled, biting her lip. She seemed to have something to say, but she couldn't speak properly because she was holding back tears.
"Your teacher has a loose tongue." Along with this resentful voice, tears that had been held back streamed down her pale cheeks.
"Mother, what does 'illegitimate child' mean?" Unable to fathom my mother's heart, I kept asking in a small voice.
"Is it true that I'm an illegitimate child?"
"Yes. You are an illegitimate child!"
My mother said in a voice as if spitting blood, as if she had given up all hope.
"It's not that I wanted to hide it forever, but I thought I would tell you when the time came, but you heard it before that time came. Since the teacher said that, what can I do? I'll tell you without hiding anything."
My mother said, wiping away the tears that flowed incessantly.
"How can I say it so that you can understand? An illegitimate child means that you were born before your father and mother got married, and your father passed away from illness before we could report it to the government (申告). So, your father can't be alive in this world. Do you understand now?"
"Yes," I replied, but with my mind only 11 years old, it was difficult to fully understand the meaning of the word "illegitimate child."
"Then, Sun-bok is also an illegitimate child like me?" Sun-bok, who studies in the same class, lost her father early.
"Yes, that child is also an illegitimate child." My mother, cornered by the answer, was very distressed.
"But you shouldn't tell this to anyone," my mother said, emphasizing it several times. If my mother had been a person of gentle character, she would have gone to argue with Miss Kim immediately, but my mother, who was naturally timid, didn't think of that and only cried in her place.
However, after several years and graduating from that elementary school, the meaning of "illegitimate child" became clear, and I secretly believed that my father was indeed still alive somewhere, just like Miss Kim had said.
The thought of, "I really want to meet my father once," surged up, and I asked my mother sternly several times, but each time, my mother only looked at my face with tearful eyes and shook her head.
"No. Your father really passed away. If he were alive, I would do anything to let you meet him, but..."
My mother looked truly sincere when she said this. Thus, I couldn't help but believe in my father's death.
I was born before marriage, and my father died before I could complete the entry procedures (入籍手續), so I had no choice but to remain an illegitimate child forever. Believing my mother's words, the painful determination only made my heart ache.
As I was about to graduate from elementary school, I thought,
"I am an illegitimate child. This word that is tiresome to even see and hear will never disappear from my name. Then, I should study hard and become great!"
As the longing for my father deepened, the red blood of youth turned its direction toward academic fervor (向學熱). However, my mother didn't have enough money to send me to study in Seoul. She tried to dissuade me from going to Kyŏngsŏng (Seoul) several times, but that only added fuel to the fire, and I insisted on going to Kyŏngsŏng more and more stubbornly.
"I hate to accept the humiliation of being an illegitimate child and rot in this countryside. I'll definitely succeed and return, so please let me go to Seoul," I begged her countless times every day, and eventually, my mother had no choice but to allow it.
We were a mother and son with no relatives in Kyŏngsŏng. As someone who couldn't afford to study abroad, I had no choice but to work my way through school.<53> As a result, my mother made various arrangements, and after a month, with the help of someone, I was placed in the home of Mr. Min (閔氏), a bank manager (銀行支配人) living in Sŏrin-dong, Kyŏngsŏng.

3
Mr. Min was a gentle man who appeared to be about 50 years old. His wife was also very kind, so I was very happy.
Their only daughter, a young woman named Bong-hŭi (鳳姬), who was eighteen years old this year, resembled her parents and seemed very sweet. The manager would sometimes take me out when he had errands to run and buy me delicious food. I felt a happiness no different from being under my mother's care in the countryside, and I advanced to the third grade of XX Commercial School. I was 18 years old at the time.
And the love that sprouted between Bong-hŭi, the daughter of the homeowner, and me was already crossing the line that should not be crossed.
"After graduating from commercial school and getting a job, I'll propose marriage. Until then, we must keep it absolutely secret. Otherwise, I'll be a guy who doesn't know the kindness (恩義) of the homeowner,"
I told Bong-hŭi this, and every time we met alone, I carefully watched out for other people's attention. However, secret affairs are quickly revealed, and the couple found out about our relationship. The couple's shock and anger were immense. However, the wife said,
"What's done is done. I don't want my daughter to be disgraced, and Kyŏng-uk isn't a frivolous guy, so let's make them a couple," and she tried to soften her husband's anger. However, the manager jumped up and scolded his wife.
"Even if he's trustworthy, I can't allow a couple who have done such a dirty thing while deceiving their parents. I'm going to kick him out right now." The gentle attitude he usually had was gone, and now he was a person without an ounce of compassion.
When I received this expulsion notice,
"Ah! It's because I'm an illegitimate child..." More than anything, this word that I couldn't forget even if I tried, was the first to ring in my head. It was a cursed word that I had forgotten for a while since coming to Seoul.
"Is it because I'm an illegitimate child that you won't allow this marriage?" I said to the manager in defiance, giving up on everything.
"What? An illegitimate child..."
I don't know what meaning it gave him, but the manager's eyes showed considerable dismay. And after trembling his lips for a moment,
"What does it matter if you're an illegitimate child? That's not the issue. Your behavior is too much. Leave this place immediately," he shouted, clenching his fists as if he were about to hit me.
That evening, with a small bag, I was kicked out of the Min's house and wandered aimlessly. As I was walking down the Kwanggyo Bridge,
"Kyŏng-uk! Kyŏng-uk!" I heard someone calling me from behind, so I turned around and saw the manager, Min, running up, out of breath.
"I have something to tell you. Let's go together," he said in a much softer voice than before.
We took a taxi. We got off at the entrance of Honmach'i (本町) and went into a Japanese restaurant (料亭) whose name I didn't know clearly. I just followed him without saying anything.
"Come on! Sit here." His overly kind demeanor made me suspicious. And inwardly, I thought he had followed me to approve the marriage, just like his wife had said.
"I thought you thought I was stubborn and ununderstanding because I made such a fuss and got angry a while ago. There's a deep reason for that..." After saying this, Min lowered his head. And on his face, which he raised again, there was an expression of resolution and anxiety.
"Kyŏng-uk! That reason is... you are actually my son..."
After barely saying these words, he lowered his head again.
"Yes? Is that true?"
I felt like my head had been hit hard with something, and my head spun!
"Yes..." Min admitted, still lowering his head. And in a voice as if something heavy was pressing down on his chest, he continued speaking painfully, still lowering his head.
"I abandoned your mother and you for 20 years. It was my position that I couldn't help but do so. But I never forgot about you both until today. I couldn't openly say that you were my son, but I was looking for a chance to somehow lighten my sin. At that time, your mother wrote a letter to my brother, saying, 'I want to send my son to Seoul to study, but I don't have any money. Please find a way for him to study. I won't ask for anything else.' So, my brother asked me to take responsibility for you until you graduated from school and got married. And it was decided that we would keep the fact that we were father and son a secret until you got married.
"But I never knew things would become so serious. If I had revealed it to you earlier, you wouldn't have fallen into incest (亂倫)," Min said, sobbing uncontrollably as if he couldn't bear it.
"I have been doing things only according to my own desires. I have been handling things to my advantage and made you commit a terrible sin. It doesn't matter to me. I think of it as retribution for the sins I committed in the past and accept it, but when I think about the sin of making my children, you and Bong-hŭi, fall into incest, my heart feels like it's breaking. It feels like it's being cut with a knife. Kyŏng-uk! Forgive your foolish father..." Min said, crying, dropping thick tears.

4
Incest (亂倫).
Having learned this for the first time, I felt like I had been hit hard on the head with a club, and I staggered as if I were about to fall, barely leaning against the wall to keep from falling.
Is this man, the father (아버지) I had longed for for 20 years, the selfish man who had lived with us for nearly three years while keeping the secret for the sake of his own reputation, the man who finally made his own child fall into incest?
I felt doubtful. I wished it was a dream. But what could I do about this reality? Tears of indignation welled up, and I couldn't see well. My whole body trembled.
"Are you still able to set foot in society as a human? What are you going to do with this child who has unknowingly fallen out of the path of humanity?"
I pressed Min with a voice mixed with sobs.
"I have nothing to say. Please forgive me. Instead, I'll do anything for you. I'll send Bong-hŭi off to get married without telling her anything. I'll take care of you as much as I can. Please forgive me," Min said, resting his head on the table and looking at me with pleading eyes, not even wiping away the tears that were flowing.
"No. I can't forgive you. You said you would do anything for me. You're going to send Bong-hŭi off to get married as she is. Isn't everything for your own sake? Do you think everything will be resolved with that? How are you going to erase the mark (刻印) imprinted on our hearts after falling into incest? What in this world will illuminate my bleak future? My life has become a mess. What are you going to do about it?"
"That's why I'm begging you like this. It's my fault that you fell into incest. If you blame me even more, I'll have no choice but to commit suicide (自殺). Please forgive me. It's my lifelong wish."
"If there's no other way, it would be good for you to commit suicide," I said, extremely agitated.
"Yes... You're telling your father to commit suicide."
"Of course."
"Even though I begged you for forgiveness like this..."<55>
"If my anguish from incest could be extinguished with a few words and some material things, I would gladly forgive you. You won't commit suicide. As long as you're alive, as long as I'm alive, I will resent and curse the man called 'father'. Just remember that!" Unable to bear the resentment and anger, I stood up abruptly.
"Wait, wait, there's one more thing I want to say," I pushed away Min, who was frantically trying to hold me back, and ran outside.
It's already been a couple of months since that incident. Having lost hope, I couldn't make up my mind, so I couldn't commit suicide, and I barely endured the agony by getting drunk on alcohol. If I don't have money, I'll do anything to earn it. And then I get drunk. But when I sober up, the terrible agony of being in incest pricks my whole body like needles.
Right now, I am only cursing my father and endlessly waiting for the day when alcohol will destroy my heart.<51>

『이 세상에서 무엇이 나를 구해 줄테냐? 불의(不義)에 빠지고 마른 나의 가삼 속은 그리스토도 석가여래도 구해 낼 수 업다. 구해 줄 놈이 잇다면 어듸 구해보라. 응...』
텅비인 술병을 깨저라하고 내려 놋코 싸홈 상대자나 차저보랴는 듯이 나는 벌덕 이러낫다. 그리고 한숨을 쉬면서
『술을 가저오너라!』 하고 녀급(女給)의 얼골을 노려보앗다.
『약주가 몹시 취하섯는데요. 인제 고만 잡수시지요』 술주정 밧기를 끄리는지 녀급은 깜작 놀난 듯한 얼골노 제지하엿다.
『염녀마라. 암만 취하기로서니 간조를 아니 할가바서 그래』 하고 나는 돈지갑을 꺼내보엿다. 이 술이 깨인 이튼날에는 나는 언제든지 무서운 고민에 빠지고 만다. 자리 속에 드러누어 잇는 것까지도 참을 수 업는 불안에 싸히고 만다. 이 세상의 모든 사람이 나의 눈에는 적으로 보인다. 무서운 악마 가티 보엿다. 그래서 나는 이것을 이저바리려고 술을 마신다. 술에 취하고 잇는 동안이 나에게 얼마큼 용기와 안심을 주는 듯하엿다.
이러한 인간을 누가 맨드러 노앗느냐? 청춘의 피가 끌어 도회지에 올나 온 나를 이러한 불의와 타락의 구렁텅이로 처미른 사람이 누구냐? 그것은 녀자도 안이다. 술도 아니다. 도회지에서 꿈틀거리는 흡혈귀(吸血鬼)도 아니다. 이 세게에 오즉 한 사람 박게 업다. 아버지다. 아버지다!
20년 동안을 그리워하든 아버지는 두 번 다시 떠올나 올 수 업는 깁흔 구덩이 속으로 자기 아들을 떼미러 너엇다. 나의 아들에 대한 아버지의 최후의 선물이 이것이엿다.
나는 사생아다!
이 세상의 다른 사람과 가티 태여낫스면서도 나의 머리에 언처진 사생아라는 문자는 나의 일생을 통해서 한 때도 떠날 수 업는 굿세이고 차듸찬 쇠사슬이다.

『경욱아! 너의 아버지는?』
보통학교 5학년에 진급한 어느 해 봄날 일이엿다. 방과 후 교실일지(敎室日誌)를 가지고 사무실에 드러갓슬 적에 녀자선생님이신 김선생님께서 무러보시엿다. 나는 불연듯 야릇한 감정을 가지고 김선생님의 얼골을 치여다 보면서
『아버지께서는 도라가시엿습니다』
하고 대답을 하엿다.
아버지가 안게신것을 항상 이상스럽게 생각하고 늘 고적하게 지냄으로 어머니에게 무러본 때가 잇섯다.
『아버지께서는 네가 두 살 되든 해에 도라가시엿단다』 어머니는 얼골에 쓸쓸한 빗을 띄우고 고개를 숙으리시엿다. 나는 그 말삼을 어듸까지든지 정직하게 밋어 왓든 것이엿다.
『응- 그러냐』 김선생님은 가벼히 말삼하시엿다.
『너는 아모 일도 몰느는 모양이로구나. 너의 아버지는 사라 게신지도 몰느겟다. 나는 그러케 생각이 든다』
『넷- 저-의 아버지가 사라 게서요?』 나는 눈을 크게 뜨고 반문을 하엿다.
『확실히 몰느겟지만 그러케 생각이 든단 말이다.』
『선생님께서는 엇더케 우리 아버지를 아시엿습니까?』
『아니! 너는 사생아이니깐 그럿치』
『선생님! 사생아라는 것은 무슨 뜻입니까? 가르켜 주세요』 나는 녀선생님 압흐로 밧짝 닥아서서 그의 무릅헤 매달닐 듯이 진정한 태도로 무럿다. 아모럿케 생각지도 안코 훠- 내던진 적은 돌의 파문이 뜻박게도 그＜52＞ 범위가 넓어지는 것을 보고 김선생님은 다소 불안과 공포를 늣기기 시작하시엿다.
『아니다. 아모 말도 아니다. 너의 아버지는 역시 도라가시엿다는 말이 틀님업는 말이다』
『선생님! 사생아란 말이 무삼 말인지 아르켜 주세요』
『아모 말도 아니다. 그 말을 아모에게나 뭇지 마러라. 그리고 시간이 느젓스니 얼는 집에 가거라』 하시면서 강압적으로 그 말을 취소하시엿스나 김선생님 얼골에는 후회로 말미암아 몹시 창백하시엿습니다.
깨끗한 흰종희에 떠러진 먹은 그것을 암만 닥고 씨서 낸다 하드라도 이전과 가티 희여질 수는 업는 것이다. 동심(童心)에 떠러진 작은 물의 파문은 나의 마음 바다에 끗업시 퍼저가고 잇섯슴니다.

2
『어머니- 아버지가 사라 게시다는데?』 하고 나는 어머니에게 엿주어 보앗다. 바누질을 하고 게시는 어머니는 이 말을 드르시자마시자 옷감을 떠러트리드니 별아간 얼골빗이 죽은 사람과 가티 창백하야 지섯습니다.
『누가 그런 말을 하든』 하는 어머니의 음성은 몹시 떨니엿습니다.
『김선생님께서 그러케 말삼하시든데요. 너의 아버지는 필경 사라 게시리라고 그리고 어머니 저는 사생아라지요. 사생아란 무슨 말이요』
『** 그것까지도 선생님이 말해 주시든!』 어머니는 손을 벌벌 떠르시면서 입살을 깨무시엿다. 무엇인지 할 말삼이 게신가 본대 넘어 나우는 눈물을 참으시느라고 말삼이 잘 안나오시는 모양이시엿다.
『너의 선생님은 입도 가벼우시다』 이러한 원망하는 말소래와 함께 참엇든 눈물이 창백한 *협에 주르르 흘너내리엿다.
『어머니 사생아란 무슨 말이요?』 어머니의 심중을 삷히지 못하는 나는 작고만 재처 무럿다.
『내가 사생아란 말이 정말이요?』
『그럿타. 너는 사생아란다!』
어머니께서는 모든 희망을 내버린 것와 가티 피를 토하는 듯한 음성으로 말삼하시엿다.
『언제든지 숨겨 둘냐는 것이 아니고 때가 오면 너에게 일너 주리라고 생각하엿는데 아즉 그 때가 오기 전에 벌서 너는 듯게 되엿다. 선생님이 그런 말삼을 하시엿스니 할 수 잇늬? 너에게 속이지 안코 들녀주마』

『엇더케 말하면 네가 네가 아라 드를는지. 사생아라는 것은 아버지와 어머니가 혼인하기 전에 네가 태여난 것이고, 그리고 관청에 신고(申告)도 하기 전에 너의 아버지는 병으로 도라가시엿단다. 그러니깐 너의 아버지는 이 세상에 사라 게실 리치가 업다. 이제 알겟늬?』

3
민씨는 년세가 50세쯤 되여 보이는 온순한 사람이엿다. 그의 부인께서도 퍽 친절하게 하야 주엇슴으로 나는 몹시 깃버하엿다.

그리고 이 집주인 딸 되는 봉희하고 나 사이에 싹터나온 사랑은 넘어서는 안 될 울타리를 두 번이나 넘고 잇엇든 때엿다.

란륜-.
비로소 이것을 알게 된 나는 곤봉으로 머리를 몹시 마진 것 가태 비틀비틀 씨러질 듯한 것을 겨우 벽에 기대여서 씨러지지는 아니하엿다.

『물논입니다』
『이가티 너에게 용서를 비럿는데도...』＜55＞
『몃 마듸 말과 약간의 물질노 란륜이 된 저의 고민이 소멸된다면 저는 깃버서 용서하야 드리겟습니다. 당신은 자살은 못하실 것입니다. 당신이 사라 잇는 이상 내가 사라 잇는 이상 나는 아버지라는 사람을 원망하고 저주합니다. 그것만은 잘 긔억하야 두십시요!』 비분, 격노에 더 참을 수 업는 나는 벌떡 이러낫섯다.
『잠간만 잠간만 한 마듸 더 할 이야기가 잇다』 황급히 나를 붓드는 민지배인을 뿌리치고 박그로 뛰여나왓다.
그런 일이 잇슨 뒤에 벌서 두어 달이 지나갓다. 희망을 이러바린 나는 마음이 굿지 못하야 자살도 못하고 알콜에 취하야 겨우 고민을 참고 순간순간을 지나왓다. 돈이 업스면 아모 일이나 또 하지. 그리하야 술에 취하고 만다. 그러나 술이 깨이면 란륜에 빠진 무서운 고민이 전신을 바눌로 찔느는 것이엿다.
지금의 나는 아버지를 저주하면서 알콜이 나의 심장을 파렬 식혀 줄 날을 하염업시 기다리고 잇슬뿐이다.＜51＞
