April Fool's Assembly, The Person Who Tells Lies
Lovers Who Cannot Meet
Ungch'o

I wrote letters several times to Mary Astor, an actress living in Hollywood across the Pacific Ocean, hoping to get a personally signed bromide print.
I thought a reply would come, but no. Two months after I sent the first letter, a pen pal unexpectedly came from America. It was definitely from Mary Astor, and the content was so friendly that I couldn't help but dance. Even though I received a reply, I couldn't just stay still. I thought, "Great, good," and wrote another letter. I asked a friend to type a love letter with a slightly more intense color than before, and I even put in my own photo, which I modified to make myself look better. Then another reply came. It was even more enthusiastic than the last one. It called me an unknown lover (未知의 愛人), said my facial features were more beautiful than "Ramon Novarro's," and at the end, it said she missed Korea and would put good picture postcards in her jewelry box. Anyway, there was nothing sarcastic, so I was dying of joy. We exchanged letters twice a month, and we became very passionate. From then on, we always wrote sweet things like "To my unknown lover" and "To my love living in the world" at the beginning of our letters. Then, last Christmas, she even sent me a pocket watch. In return, I knitted three or four Taegeuk fans and dancing shoes made of Wanggul (I invented them) and sent them. Mary had never seen those shoes before, and they were comfortable and modern. Anyway, they became a big hit and a big trend in Hollywood, but they say they make them with thread there because they don't have the material (Wanggul).
Due to space constraints, I can't write more details, but she wants to meet me in Hawaii around the fall of next year. And then, we will either bring her to Korea or I will go to America with her. One of the two will be decided.
Ah, but what kind of fate is this? I don't even have the streetcar fare to go to the station, let alone Hawaii. How can I meet her if I don't have the money? No matter how much we love each other, can I say that I can't meet her because I don't have travel expenses?...

Ten Won War
Smiling

K Bank tellers ABC received a special allowance of 10 won as a reward for five days of overtime work.
It was extra income outside of their salary. They were "ultra modern" (올트라 모뽀) who liked to play and enjoy pranks, so they pondered over the problem of how to spend this 10 won that they had obtained so easily.
They couldn't divide it in cash because of their pride, so they decided to go to a café (카페-) together with the 10 won.
So far, the decision to go was made, but they couldn't agree on where to go.
A insisted on Rakwon Café (락원 카페-), where he was a regular customer. B insisted on Angel Café (엔젤카페-), which he frequented. C insisted on Mokdan Café (목단카페-), which he frequented.
They argued for an hour about going to their favorite places, but in the end, they couldn't compromise and reluctantly decided to go to their respective favorite places and divide the 10 won equally.
But when they divided 10 won equally, it came out to 3 won and 33 jeon, with 1 jeon left over. Normally, they would give it to one person, but they were stubborn and insisted that since they were going their separate ways, they should divide the remaining 1 jeon equally. In the end, they bought another item with that 1 jeon, but instead of sharing the item, they divided it into cash and went their separate ways. <46>

Big Disturbance Over a Female Student's Baby
Ch'ŏng'o

This is a very secret story. It was a Sunday night last summer, and there were hundreds of people taking a walk in the park. All the benches were taken by people who had arrived earlier, so people couldn't sit down and dragged their aching legs around. Suddenly, someone came and said, "There's a big spectacle over there! Is the world going to end? A female student with braided hair has given birth to a baby in the park!" The crowd heard the news and shouted, "Where? Where?" and rushed here and there. Eventually, even the police officers from the police box came to investigate the matter and searched all the bushes, suspecting all the female students passing by. But the truth was that it was a baseless rumor. It was a meaningless lie. Then why did that story come out? The truth is that the person who first told the story was walking around, and his legs were aching. When he went to sit on a bench, someone else had already taken it, so he made up that lie so that the person sitting down would leave, and he could take the bench.

My Son, Give Him Back!
P'akchin

A noisy and boring spring street. In the middle of Chongno (종로) intersection, late at night - the other day, a strange couple was grabbing each other's collars and rolling around, fighting so hard that their heads were about to explode. All the police officers from the Chongno Police Station rushed out, and streetcars and cars couldn't pass, and the passersby couldn't go their way. While stuck there, they watched the fight, forming a large crowd of ten or twenty people. Dozens of police officers rushed in and managed to break up the fight. It turned out that the man was a eunuch (內侍) and the woman was a bob-haired beauty. They fought so fiercely that the man's testicles were kicked, and blood was flowing down his pants, and the woman's hair was pulled so hard that more than two ja (자) of hair was pulled out, and the gold hairpin that was worn was bent into a "ㄱ" shape. Only then did the police officer ask why they were fighting. It wasn't a big deal. The man and the woman got married on April 32nd of this year and lived happily together until the woman accidentally developed a uterine disease and went to the university hospital for surgery. After removing her uterus, she suddenly became pregnant and gave birth to a beautiful baby boy in one day. The two of them lived happily and enjoyed the baby's antics until the child turned five and could barely crawl and walk. Then the woman had a change of heart, took the child, and ran away. After searching and searching, the man happened to meet her on the street today, and the fight was so intense because the man was telling the woman to return his son, but she refused. <47>
