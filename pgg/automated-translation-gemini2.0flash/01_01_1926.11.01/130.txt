A Word from Each Bug

Ignorant humans, trying to imitate me, waste precious money practicing flying, only to make mistakes and lose their lives. How absurd! *Ssaeng-a! Ssaeng-a!* You fake *ssaeng-a*! What's the use of military aviation? (Dragonfly)

You who sleep soundly through the long nights of the ninth and tenth months without weaving even a foot of cloth, what will you do but go naked in the snowy, cold winds of the eleventh and twelfth months? *Ingssalssang! Ingssalgang!* (Cricket)

During the hot fifth and sixth months, heartless humans hated me like an enemy, carelessly using fly traps (捕蠅器) and fly poison (捕蠅藥). Now that I'm gone, their children's hair stands on end, and they wail, complaining their hands and feet are cold. Truly pitiful. Will they still not think of me now? (Fly)

Just put a lid on a round, spinning coil, and it's a multi-story Western house (洋屋). My home is like this, yet those so-called patriots (志士) and literati (文士) don't even have a single room of their own and are chased around by monthly rent (朔月貰). They too are pitiful. Moon room (月房)! Moon room! (Snail)

Those rich fellows, whose bellies bulge like Namsan mountain from constantly sucking the blood of the poor (貧民), make a huge fuss calling me a bloodsucking bug (吸血虫) or evil bug (惡虫) if I bite them just once, putting up tin shields and spraying insecticide (除虫藥). Do they feel no shame in their conscience? Hither slither! Thither slither! (Bedbug - 貧代)

That fashionable loose woman (蕩女子) with the framed hair, dashingly dressed in a French layered jacket and a Hansan ramie skirt, strutting about seeking her lover (情夫) in the afternoon sun after rain, briefly appears to have the refined air of an immortal like me. But if you investigate her inner workings, all sorts of behaviors are revealed. Stop it—don't—don't do that—Selling sex! Selling sex! (賣淫! 賣淫!) (Cicada)

Humans accept it as normal when people catch other people by setting nets with the law, but when spiders catch insects by setting nets with silk (絲), humans often think it wicked and interfere greatly. It seems there is originally no justice (正義) or public opinion (公論) in human society. Prohibited here! Prohibited there! (Spider)

Humans, who cannot distinguish things nearby (咫尺) in the middle of the night if they don't use nature like electricity or oil, boastfully claim, "Among all creation, only humans are most noble" (萬物之中 惟人最貴). Seeing this, humans are truly ridiculous animals. Won't they envy me when they see me? Flashing here! Flashing there! (Firefly)

City people who live in idle comfort (遊衣遊食) thanks to farmers, yet look down on them, are truly hateful (可憎) and deplorable (可惜). If you don't want to starve to death either, hurry out quickly, even for a day, and run along the ridges between fields and through the grass like me. Return to the soil and farm (歸土農)! Return to the soil and farm! (蟋蟀 - Grasshopper) <76>

**Summary**

This article presents a satirical critique of early 20th-century Korean society through the imagined voices of various insects. Each insect observes and comments on human follies: the dangers of mimicking technology (aviation), laziness, hypocrisy regarding hygiene, the struggles of intellectuals versus the wealthy, moral decay (promiscuity), questionable human justice compared to nature's ways, human arrogance despite limitations, and the exploitation and disdain of farmers by city dwellers. The piece uses the insects' perspectives to highlight social inequalities, moral failings, and the contradictions of modernization under colonial rule. For historians, the article offers valuable insights into contemporary social commentary, anxieties about Westernization (aviation, housing), class tensions (rich vs. poor, intellectuals vs. wealthy, city vs. country), changing gender roles and morality ("new women"), and a critique of human hubris, all framed within the specific context of 1920s Korea. The call to "return to farming" (歸土農) reflects agrarian values and possibly critiques the social dislocation caused by colonial economic policies.

一蟲一言
무지한 인간들은 내 흉내를 내느라고 악가운 돈버리고 飛行 연습하다 가서 깟댁하면 실수하야 생명까지 빼앗기니 가소롭기 짝이 업다. 쌩아! 쌩아! 요假쌩아! 軍用飛行 무엇하랴 (蜻變)

九十月 긴긴 밤에 벼 한자도 안이쨔고 잠만 쿨쿨 자는 넌은 동지 섯달 雪寒 風에 헐벗짠코 무엇하랴 잉쌀쌍! 잉쌀강!-(促織)

五六月 더운 때에 무정한 인간들이 웬수가티 나를 미워 捕蠅器 捕蠅藥을 함부로 사용터니 내가 업는 요새이는 제 자식의 머리털이 한울로 올나가며 손발이 치워라고 앙앙대고 울어대니 참으로 가련하다. 이제도 저 이들이 내 상각을 아니할가 (蠅)

돌아 팽팽 매다지에 뚝겅만 덥흐면 몃 몃 層 洋屋이다. 요내 집도 이러한데 소위 志士니 文士니 하는 놈들은 제 집 한 간이 별로 업시 朔月貰ˆ¿으로 쪽겨만 다니니 그 역시 가련하다 달방(月房) 달방 (蝸牛)

밤낫으로 貧民의 피만 빠러 먹고 배가 南山 가티 불은 부자 놈도 내가 한번 저를 물면 吸血虫이니 惡虫이니 하고 야단법석을 함부로 치며 양철홈도 대고 除虫藥을 뿌리니 그 놈에게는 良心의 부끄러움이 조곰도 업슬가 이리 살살! 저리 살살! (貧代)

불란사 겹저고리에 한산 모시 치마를 멥시잇게 떨처입고 雨後 斜陽에 행똥거리고 情夫를 차저 돌아 다니는 틀에머리 蕩女子는 잠간보면 나와 가튼 仙風道骨의 態가 잇다만은 내막을 조사하면 별별 소행이 다 잇구나. 아서라-마러라-네 그리 마러라-賣淫! 賣淫! (蟬)

법으로 그물을 처서 사람이 사람을 잡는 것은 尋常히 넉이지만은 실(絲)로 그물을 처서 벌어지를 잡아 먹는 것은 사람놈들이 흔이 惡하게 생각하야 방해를 만히 하니 인간 사회에는 원래 正義 公論이 다 업는 것 갓다. 이리 엄금! 저리 엄금! (蜘蛛)

전기나 석유 등의 자연을 이용치 못하면 밤즁에는 지척도 不辨하는 인생들이 자칭왈 萬物之中에 惟人最貴라고 입으로만 떠드는 것을 보면 사람이란 참으로 우순 동물이다. 나를 볼때에 그들이 불어워 하지나 안을가. 여긔서도 번젹! 저긔서도 번적! (螢)

농민의 덕으로 遊衣遊食하면서도 농민을 천시하는 도회 사람들은 참으로 可憎하고 可惜하다. 너희들도 굴머죽지 안으려거든 하루라도 밧비 나와 가티 논두렁으로 뛰고 풀 속으로 단여라. 귀토농(歸土農)-귀토농! (蟋蟀)＜76＞
