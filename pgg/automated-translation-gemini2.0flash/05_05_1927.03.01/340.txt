별건곤 제5호
1927년 03월 01일
海內 海外에 헛허저 잇는 朝鮮女醫師 評判記, 해마다 늘어가는 그 수효 잇다금은 해외에서도 활동 (A Critical Biography of Korean Female Doctors Scattered at Home and Abroad, Their Numbers Increase Every Year, Sometimes Active Overseas)
소식 (News)

A: Mr. X! Do you know how many female doctors there are in Korea?

X: Why do you ask? Are you trying to compile some statistics?

A: It's not about compiling statistics, I'm just asking. Living in Korea and not knowing such a thing is a bit outside the realm of common sense. And it's something people in the world are curious about.

X: Then why specifically ask about female doctors when there are plenty of male doctors?

A: Everyone usually knows about male doctors. But the number of female doctors isn't that high, and people are curious about how they're active. Is it just because the word "female" is attached that you're asking? If you interpret everything that way, there's nothing to say.

X: Everyone usually knows about male doctors, you say. How do they know? Huh! They might know the number, their origin, their specialty, and where they practice. But the medical profession is full of secrets. Is there anything more to say? In Japan, there was a so-called doctor who drugged and violated a virgin, and there was a abortion specialist (落胎 專門醫) who performed over a hundred abortions in one town, which was all over the news. Fortunately, such things haven't happened in Korea yet, but in the dark corners of the medical profession, some people criticize doctors for charging six, seven, or even ten times the original price (原價) for medicine, calling it "new medicine." As an outsider, I can't fully understand it, but it's true that there are things I can't know. <70>

A: This is off track (脫線). Answer my question.

X: Okay. But I don't know the details. I'll tell you what I know. Ask me one by one. Hmm, the number! I saw in a newspaper that there are only eighteen female doctors in Korea.

A: Are they all Korean women?

X: Well, maybe not all of them are Korean women.

A: If they're all Korean women, that's a considerable number more than I thought.

X: What do you mean considerable? It's only because people are rare in Korea. Try saying eighteen female doctors out of ten million women. Westerners would laugh.

A: That's true. How do you get the qualification to be a female doctor?

X: First, you have to graduate from a legally recognized school or pass the medical exam. As with everything else, most people seem to be from schools.

A: Of course. Exams aren't that easy anywhere. Which school has produced the most Korean female doctors?

X: Most of them are graduates of Japanese women's professional schools. And about seven or eight are from the Ch'ongdokbu Medical Professional School. There are also graduates of Chinese medical schools and some who are studying at American medical schools, and about four or five who graduated from pharmacy schools and obtained pharmacist (藥劑師) qualifications.

A: Who are the ones who graduated from women's medical school in Japan? I know a few - Hŏ Yŏng-suk, Hyŏn Tŏk-sin, Ri Tŏk-yo, Ryu Yŏng-jun, Kil Chŏng-hŭi of Tongdaemun Women's Hospital, Chŏng Cha-yŏng - are there any others?

X: Yes, I know a couple more. Chŏn Hye-dŏk, who is at the Incheon YMCA Women's Hospital, and Song Bok-sin and Han So-je, who are studying in America.

A: Ah, Song Bok-sin, the one who caused so much trouble among the students when she was studying in Tokyo, and even Ryang Kŭn-hwan almost punched her? Is she a medical school graduate?

X: I don't know about that, but I heard she graduated from medical school in Japan and went straight to America, and recently I heard she even got a degree.

A: That's admirable. It's truly gratifying that a Korean woman has gone overseas and even obtained a degree. Her efforts must have been considerable. And it's truly gratifying that Korean women are developing like that.

X: What is? When it comes to female doctors, Kim Ae-hŭi, who was born in P'yŏngyang, is the best. She even went to Beijing, China, to graduate from medical school and returned to <71> work at the P'yŏngyang Kihŭl Hospital, and is now studying in America. In P'yŏngyang, the Kim Ae-hŭi was known as one of the three sisters, and her older brother suffered greatly during the March 1st Movement and eventually died. Her younger sister also tried to help her brother and died as well. Now, Ae-hŭi is alone overseas, focusing on her studies, but their spirit and activity were far superior to that of men. And Kim Ryŏ-un, who graduated from Kyŏngsŏng Pharmacy School as the first graduate, went all the way to Chinahan'gu (支那漢口) as a single woman and started a pharmacy business, which is quite an achievement in the business world.

A: Really? It seems that there are surprisingly many talented and active female doctors.

X: You could say they're better than the stuffy old teachers.

A: Who are the graduates from the Government-General's school?

X: There's An Su-gyŏng, who is at the Tongdaemun Women's Hospital, Kim Hae-ji, who is at the P'yŏngyang Kihŭl Hospital, and Kim Yŏng-hŭng, who worked at the Incheon Women's Hospital for many years and is now said to be practicing privately in P'yŏngyang! These three must be the veterans (元老). Then there's Kim Yŏng-sil, who is at the Sŏngjin Chedong Hospital, Ko Su-sŏn, who is at the Kaesŏng YMCA Hospital, and Yun Bŏm-yŏng, who was a teacher and school doctor at Taegu Shinmyŏng School and is now practicing in Yŏngdŏk, Kyŏngsangbuk-do, with her husband as a public doctor and his wife as a female doctor, who graduated two years ago, and I think two or three graduated last year, but I can't remember their names.

A: The Government-General's school uniquely used a co-educational system to produce several female doctors, but since two years ago, they haven't even allowed women to audit classes, so the system is gone? What's the reason?

X: Well, there's a rumor. I don't know the details, but maybe the effects weren't that good after all. Maybe co-education just didn't work? When Ko Su-sŏn and Yun Bŏm-yŏng were attending school, if one of them was absent, the other would go, but it was awkward to sit alone among so many men and study, so they would take their books and go back home. So it seems that co-education doesn't have a bright future.

A: That's not unreasonable. For women who have only been in the house until now, and especially if they're learning medicine, there must be times when they feel embarrassed and uncomfortable due to physiology, anatomy, and hygiene. And the pharmacist is a bit different from the doctor, but since we're on the topic, what are the pharmacy school graduates doing?

X: There were three graduates in the first class! Kim Sun-bok, who is working as a pharmacist at <72> Tongdaemun Women's Hospital and is putting more effort into the women's movement and occasionally gives speeches and writes articles, and Cha Sun-sŏk, who was at the Hamhŭng Jaehye Hospital until recently and is now going to Seoul to start a private practice, and Kim Ryŏ-un, who I mentioned earlier, is very active in Hankou, but I'm wondering if she was scared by the anti-British movement (反英運動). There were three graduates last year, and all three went to study in Japan.

A: Who is the most highly regarded female doctor?

X: How can you know who is highly regarded? Most of them don't have their own private practice and are helping Western doctors or male doctors at other hospitals, so it's hard to know their skills, and it's hard to realize their potential even if they have it. Anyway, the well-known ones in Seoul are Hŏ Yŏng-suk, Ri Tŏk-yo, Hyŏn Tŏk-sin, and Ryu Yŏng-jun. Hŏ has long severed ties with medicine, so there's nothing to say. Hyŏn is at the Tongdaemun Women's Hospital, Ryu is at the T'aehwa Clinic, and Ri is at the Government-General's Hospital. Only Chŏng Cha-yŏng has a main office in Suŭn-dong and a branch in Ch'ŏngyŏp-jŏng, and she goes around as an internal medicine, pediatric, obstetrics, and gynecology doctor, busy with examinations and treatments in the morning at the main office and in the afternoon at the branch.

A: The reason why Hŏ, Hyŏn, and Ri became known to the world was not because of their medical skills or medicine, but because their husbands were writers and newspaper reporters. And Ryu is a well-known P'yŏngyang native who has traveled all over China, studied in Japan, and is busy as a T'aehwa doctor and a doctor at Ewha College, and also publishes many articles in newspapers and magazines, so that's how she became known, not because of her medical expertise. Anyway, Ryu's activities are something to celebrate in terms of the women's liberation movement.

X: Of course, in this day and age, shouldn't women have professional awareness and a rebellious spirit? It would be even more gratifying if the liberation movement raised its voice from the medical profession. But hey! Hyŏn, Ri, and Hŏ would be upset if they heard that their husbands made their names known to the world. That's just coming from male superiority (優越感).

A: A married couple is one body, so if the wife's name becomes known because of the husband, and the husband's name becomes known because of the wife, there's no need to force a distinction between high and low.

X: That's fine if you interpret it that way, but is it easy to interpret it that way?

A: How much do female doctors earn? And do they earn well? <73> If they open their own practice, men seem to do well, and especially since children and female patients would go to them, wouldn't they earn well?

X: It doesn't seem that way. If they earned well, wouldn't there be more people opening their own practice, or wouldn't they quit after opening one? If you have the qualifications as a female doctor, it's safest to work at a hospital and earn at least seventy or eighty won. A: Do you know why Hŏ Yŏng-suk closed her practice?

X: I can't know the details of other people's lives, but first of all, she was well-off, and she didn't enjoy it, and she didn't earn much, so she closed her practice.

A: Anyway, it's a good thing. If these people work in various fields and make efforts to spread hygiene awareness in our Korean homes, which is still lacking, their grace and contribution will be greater than any other endeavor, won't it?

X: That's right. I agree. - End

A X군! 조선에 녀의사가 얼마나 되는지 아는가?
X 그건 왜 무러, 무슨 통게표를 꾸미랴나?
A 왜 통게표 꾸밀랴만 그런 것 뭇나. 아모러나 조선 살면서 그런 것도 모른다면 좀 상식에 버서진다고 볼 수 밧게 업지. 그리고 세상에서도 좀 궁금해 하는 일이야.
X 그러면 왜 하필 녀의사를 뭇는가 남의사도 만흔데.
A 남의사야 보통으로 세상 사람이 다 아는 것 아닌가. 그러나 녀의사야 말로 수효도 그러케 만치 못하고 엇더케 활동하는지 그게 궁금해하는 바란 말이지. 하필 녀자라는 명사가 붓기 때문에 뭇는 것인가? 세상일을 그러케만 해석한다면 할말이라고는 하나도 업지.
X 남의사는 세상이 보통으로 안다고. 알기는 엇더케 알어. 응! 수효가 몃이고 어듸 출신이고 무슨 과(科)전문이고 어대서 개업햇다고 그런 것이야 알 수 잇겟지. 그러나 의사게야말로 수수꺽끼 가튼 비밀이 다 만타네. 더 말할 것 잇나. 일본서는 소위 박사라고 하는 친구가 마취약을 써 가지고 량가 처녀를 릉욕햇다가 들녀나서 증역까지 산 이가 잇고 락태 전문의 (落胎 專門醫) 가 잇서서 한 고을에서 백여명 락태 식힌 일이 잇다고 한 참 신문에 떠들지 안헛나. 조선서는 아즉 그런 일이 업서서 다행이지만 어둑한 곳은 의사게여서 양약이니 신약이니 해 가지고 약갑슬 원가(原價) 의 육칠배 혹은 십배식도 바더 먹는다고 비난하는 사람도 만테. 그런 말을 문외한으로 안저서는 다 대종할 수가 업지만 하여간 알 <70> 수 업는 일이 잇슬 것도 사실이야.
A 이 사람 그것은 탈선 (脫線) 일세. 나 뭇는 말이나 대답하여 주게.
X 그러케 하게. 그러나 나인덜 어대 자세히 알어야지, 아는 대로는 말하여 줄 것이니 차레차레 무러주게. 응 수효! 저거번 어느 신문에 난 것을 보닛가 조선 내에 잇는 녀의사가 전부 십팔인이라고 하엿데.
A 그게 다 조선녀자일가?
X 글세 아마 다 조선 녀자는 아니겟지.
A 그게 다 조선 녀자라고 하면 그래도 생각하던 바 보다는 수효가 상당히 만흔데.
X 만키야 무엇 만흔가? 하도 사람이 귀한 조선이닛가 그럿치. 일천만 녀성중에 녀의사 십팔인이라고 해보게. 서양사람 가트면 코우슴을 칠 것일세.
A 그야 그래. 그런데 녀의사는 엇더케 해야 자격을 엇는구?
X 첫재 법령으로 개정한 학교를 맛치거나 의사 시험에 합격한 이라야 되지. 그런데 어듸나 일반이겟지만 대개는 학교출신이 만흔가 보데.
A 그야 그럿켓지. 시험이란 원래 어듸 그러케 쉬운 것인가? 그러면 조선 녀의사로는 어느 학교의 출신이 제일 만흔고?
X 물론 일본 녀자의 전문학교 출신이 대부분이지. 그러고 총복부 의학전문학교출신이 아마 한 칠팔인 되나 보데. 그러고 중국 의학교 출신도 잇고 미국 의학교 재학중인 분도 약간 잇고 그 외로 약학교를 졸업하고 약제사 (藥劑師) 자격을 어든 분도 역시 사오인 잇는 모양이지.
A 일본 가서 녀자 의전을 맛치신 분은 누구누구인가 ? 나 아는 분도 몃 분 잇긴 잇네마는. 저 -허영숙씨, 현덕신씨, 리덕요씨, 류영준씨, 동대문 부인병원의 길정희씨, 정자영씨- 그런데 그 외에 또 잇나.
X 응 나 아는 중에도 두어 분 더 잇서 .지금 인천 야소교부인병원에 가 게신 전혜덕씨하고 미국가서 지금 류학하는 송복신씨하고 한소제씨가 잇지.
A 아하 송복신씨, 한참 동경재학 시대에 류학생들의 물의가 분분하야 량근환군의 주먹까지 왓다갓다 햇다는 그 분 말인가. 그 분이 의전 출신이던가?
X 나는 그것까지는 몰나도 하여간 일본서 의전을 맛치고 바로 미국으로 가더니 요세 드르닛가 학위까지 어덧다는 말이 잇데.
A 갸륵한 노릇일세. 아모러나 조선 녀자도 해외에까지 나아가서 학위까지 엇는데는 그의 고심도 적지는 아니하엿겟지. 그러고 조선의 녀성이 그러케 발전하여 나간다는 것이 참으로 깃분 일이야.
X 그게 무엇인가. 녀자 의사게 인물로 말하면 그래도 평양 출생의 김애희씨를 칠 것일세, 일즉 중국 북경까지 가서 의학교를 맛치고 도라와서 <71> 평양긔흘 병원에서 일도 보고 지금은 역시 미국류학중이지만 평양에서도 김애희씨라면 처녀 삼형제라고 평판이 자자하야 그 형은 긔미 운동 때에 천신만고를 하다가 필경 황천의 손이 되고 그 동생 역시 형을 구완하랴 애를 쓰다가 따라서 죽고 지금은 애희씨 혼자 만리 해외의 외로운 몸으로 오직 학업에 힘쓸 뿐이나 그들의 의긔와 활동이란 남자보다도 훨신 우승한 점이 잇섯다네. 그러고 경성약학교를 제 일회로 졸업한 김려운씨라는 이는 녀자 단신으로 지나한구 (支那漢口)까지 나가서 약종업을 개시하엿다는데 상당히 실업게로 활동하는 인물이라고 아니할 수 업느니.
A 그런가. 그러면 의외로 녀의사게야말로 인물과 활동이 상당히 잇는 셈일세 그려.
X 말하자면 골샌님 선생님네 보다는 나은 셈이라 하겟지.
A 총독부의 학교출신으로는 누구누구인가?
