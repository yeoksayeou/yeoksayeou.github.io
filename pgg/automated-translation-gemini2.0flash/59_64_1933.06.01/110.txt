If we had a million won, how would we use it? Those who don't know a million won
						B Reporter (Investigation)

Madam An, the Odd-Jobber

Reporter: If you had a million won, how would you use it?

An: How much is a million won?

Reporter: How much is a million won? Do you know how much a hundred won is?

An: Yes, I know what a hundred won is.

Reporter: A million won is ten thousand times that hundred won.

An: Then it must be quite a lot?

(The woman does not seem very surprised. She just vaguely knows it's a lot.)

Reporter: Quite a lot... So, if you had a million won, how would you use it?

An: My goodness! How could such a large sum of money come to me?

Reporter: Well, no, I mean if you were to have it, hypothetically.

An: Haha… I don't know… I'd quit my odd jobs.

Reporter: Of course… What would you buy first?

An: I need to get some medicine for my young child who is always sick. <24>

Reporter: And then?

An: I'd buy a phonograph. Hohohoho…

Madam Choi, the Rice Cake Seller

Choi: Buy some good rice cakes!

Reporter: How much is one rice cake?

Choi: One jeon each… Eleven for ten jeon…

Reporter: How much do you make a day?

Choi: What do you mean make? I just do this to survive.

Reporter: But you do it because you make at least a little something, right?

Choi: If I do well, I make about six or seven nyang a day.

Reporter: If you had a million won, how would you use it?

Choi: What is a million won?

Reporter: Money! Money... If you had a million won, how would you use it?

Choi: How much is a million won?

Reporter: It's a hundred won multiplied by ten thousand.

Choi: Aigoo… That's a lot! Where does such money exist?

Reporter: Well, if you, ma'am, were to have that million won, hypothetically.

Choi: Oh, don't talk nonsense! Why don't you buy some rice cakes?

Reporter: I will buy rice cakes… But please answer the question.

Choi: I'd marry off my son.

Reporter: And keep selling rice cakes?

Choi: If I didn't do this, I'd starve.

Reporter: Even with a million won?

Choi: As if a speck of a million won will ever appear!

(The reporter bought ten jeon worth of rice cakes)

Reporter: How much do you make selling ten jeon worth?

Choi: One jeon.

Reporter: What do you use that one jeon for?

Choi: I save it to buy rice to cook for dinner… Huyu! <25>

Oh-bok, the Ice Cream Seller

Oh: Ice cream…

Reporter: Hey, ice cream!

Oh: Yes, how much do you want?

Reporter: I'm not going to eat ice cream…

Oh: Hehe, you're too kind… Would you like a ten-jeon one?

Reporter: Hey, if you had a million won, what would you use it for?

Oh: A million won? If I had a million won…

Reporter: A hundred ten-thousand-won bills.

Oh: Hehe… How could such money ever come to me!

Reporter: Well, if you were to have it, hypothetically.

Oh: If I had it, it would be great.

Reporter: Yes, it would be great… but how would you use it?

Oh: I'd have so many things to use it for.

Reporter: What would you use it for first?

Oh: I'd buy a machine that automatically turns the ice cream container.

Reporter: And then?

Oh: No, no, I should quit selling ice cream.

Reporter: And then?

Oh: I'd open a rice store.

Reporter: Why?

Oh: So I wouldn't starve even if I didn't have money.

Reporter: What next?

Oh: I'd open a lumber store too.

Reporter: And?

Oh: A side dish store too.

Reporter: And?

Oh: I'd build a house too.

Reporter: And?

Oh: Hehe… Hehe, I'd buy a suit like the one you're wearing, teacher. Hehe… Please buy some ice cream.

Reporter: How old are you?

Oh: Nineteen. <26>

Reporter: Wouldn't you go to Pyeongmokjeong (병목정)? If you had a million won?

Oh: Hehe… I would… Please buy some ice cream.

Mr. Oh, the Fish Seller

Reporter: Hey, croaker seller.

Oh: Yes.

Reporter: If you had a million won, how would you use it?

Oh: What? A million won… Hehe… Don't talk nonsense and buy some croaker. They're jumping fresh.

Reporter: I'll buy fish later… Tell me about how you'd use a million won. Do you know how much a million won is?

Oh: Hehe, of course I know.

(He says this confidently, but he doesn't really have a clear understanding of a million won.)

Reporter: Then… what would you buy first?

Oh: There's a fish store for sale in Jingo-gae (진고개) right now. I'd buy that.

Reporter: So you'd become the owner of a fish store?

Oh: Yes.

Reporter: You have a million won, why would you do that?

Oh: That's a really big business. I can easily make a hundred won a day.

Reporter: What next?

Oh: That day, I'd gather all my friends and treat them to a big feast.

Reporter: And?

Oh: And… I'd just spend it here and there.

Kim-tong, the Pear Seller

(He's a pear seller from Wangsimni (왕십리). He's young, but he comes from a long line of pear sellers.)

Reporter: Hey, how much do you make carrying that load?

Kim: If I do well, I make about twenty or thirty jeon.

Reporter: How would you feel if you had a million won right now?

Kim: A million won? You mean money? <27>

Reporter: Yeah, if you had ten thousand hundred-won bills right now.

Kim: Ten thousand hundred-won bills… Aigoo… That's a lot.

Reporter: Yeah.

Kim: Hehe… Who would give me that much money?

Reporter: Well, if you were to have it, hypothetically…

Kim: That would be great.

Reporter: Yes, it would be great… but what would you use it for first?

Kim: I can't do anything without money, but if I had money, there's nothing I couldn't do, right?

Reporter: (Speechless for a moment) Well, you can't do anything without money, but what's the first thing you'd want to buy with that million won?

Kim: There are so many things I want to buy. I'd buy a car… and…

Reporter: And?

Kim: And… Hehe… I'd have to see it to know.

Mr. Lee, the Rickshaw Man

Reporter: Hey, if you had a million won right now, how would you use it?

Lee: What? That's too sudden a question.

(He seems confused, not understanding what I'm saying.)

Reporter: Money, I mean. If you had ten thousand hundred-won bills right now, how would you use it?

Lee: (Looks up at me without a word. He seems to suspect I came from room 8 of the Government-General Hospital (總督府病院) (now room 8, though)) Where does such money exist?

Reporter: Well, if it did?

Lee: What kind of luck would it take to get such money?

Reporter: Well, if you were to have it…

Lee: I'd have so many things to use it for.

Reporter: How?

Lee: A lot.

Reporter: What would you use it for first?

Lee: I'd buy all the cars in Seoul and smash them to pieces.

Reporter: (Feigning surprise) Why? <28>

Lee: Because those things are making it impossible to make a living.

Reporter: But if you had a million won, you wouldn't be pulling a rickshaw, right?

Lee: Oh, that's true.

Reporter: Nothing else?

Lee: I'd buy each of my buddies a rickshaw.

Reporter: And?

Lee: I'd have a courtesan and drink and do everything.

Mr. Moon, the Errand Man

Reporter: Where did the master go?

Moon: He went out after having breakfast.

Reporter: How many people are in your family, Mr. Moon?

Moon: Three.

Reporter: Three. Do you live off the mistress?

Moon: Yes.

Reporter: If you had a million won right now, how would you use it?

Moon: What is a million won?

Reporter: Money, I mean... If you had ten thousand hundred-won bills...

Moon: Aigoo! Where does such money come from!

Reporter: Well, if you were to have it, hypothetically.

Moon: Hehe… Well… I don't know… How would I use such a large sum of money?

Reporter: Still, you'd have to use it, right?

Moon: That's right.

Reporter: Then?

Moon: Then I'd get a… get a water pipe installed in this house's kitchen.

Reporter: Why?

Moon: Hehe… It's nothing grand. This old man's back hurts so much from fetching water morning and evening that I can't live like this.

Reporter: But if you had a million won, you'd be richer than this household. Would you still be doing errands for this house then?

Moon: Hehe. That's true, but… Then maybe I'd buy my wife some summer clothes - some of that common rayon these days - give her some nice clothes? Hehe…

Reporter: Is there anything you want, Mr. Moon?

Moon: What would I want? I'd just take a train straight back to my hometown and farm. <29>
