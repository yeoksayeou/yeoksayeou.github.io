別乾坤 第64號
1933年 06月 01日
各界男女 逢變 誌上 座談會 (Roundtable Discussion on Mishaps by Men and Women from All Walks of Life)
대담·좌담 (Dialogue/Roundtable)

各界男女 逢變 誌上 座談會

Participants:
Pyŏn Yŏng-ro, Sŏl Ŭi-sik, Hŏ Yŏng-suk, Kang Agŭn (female), Yŏm Sang-sŏp, Yun Ik-sŏn, Yi Tae-un, Yun Sŏng-sang, Sŏ Ch’un, Ch’a Sang-ch’an
Host: Ssang S

Ssang S: E- Everyone, thank you so much for attending despite your busy schedules. If you have cigarettes, take them out and smoke. Actually, I was going to invite you to a Myŏngwŏl-gwan (明月館) kind of place, but this is a roundtable about embarrassing mishaps, and since you all generally enjoy drinking, I thought it would be a shame if you got too drunk and had an embarrassing mishap, so I invited you here on the manuscript paper.
Now, enough with the small talk, let's each tell at least one story about an embarrassing mishap, as the title suggests. If there's someone who does a particularly good job, I might take them aside and treat them to a drink later.
Pyŏn: Ugh, I hate alcohol.
Sŏ: Then I'll go first... Can you even call yourself a man if you can't drink?
Yŏm, Ch’a, Yi: (In unison) That's right.
Yun (Sŏng), Hŏ, Kang: That's not true, that's not true.
Sŏl: When I see people drinking, they don't seem human...
Ssang S: Oh, why are you all like this? Just because we're talking about alcohol, you think I'm going to bring out a makgŏlli (막걸리) bowl or something? No way! What kind of situation do you think this is... Alright, Sŏ Ch’un, please tell your story.
Sŏ: Yes, I will. But I have to get the prize you're giving out later... It must have been about three years ago. <4>
I drank a lot of alcohol. I was drunk. It's gone now, but it was at the Asia Cafe in Insadong. So I asked the girl there to call me a taxi and I went home.
Hŏ: How vulgar!
Sŏ: Don't interrupt, listen to the rest. So I went home and tried to pay the fare, but I didn't have any money... I had no choice but to give him my business card, but he wouldn't take it.
Sŏl: Is Sŏ Ch’un's business card that impressive?
Sŏ: Just listen... This driver wouldn't take my business card, so he took me to the police box. What good would it do to argue at the police box... In the meantime, I urinated inside the police box... What an embarrassment!
Everyone: (Laughs loudly. "How awful!" is heard.)
Sŏ: But you see... the officer asked why I wasn't paying the taxi fare, and when I said I didn't have any money and showed him my empty wallet, my seal fell out. When the officer picked it up and saw it, his attitude suddenly changed. He told the driver that I was an editor at the Dong-A Ilbo and that I wouldn't cheat him out of a won, and ordered him to take me home immediately.
Everyone: (Laughs loudly.)
Pyŏn: I'll tell a story about being embarrassed while drunk too. Now, I run away at the sight of a liquor company chimney, but years ago, wasn't I, Pyŏn Yŏng-ro, famous for drinking a lot and being a drunkard? One day, I went to visit a friend and we drank until our noses were crooked. The thing is, I somehow crawled into the mosquito net where my friend's wife was sleeping!
Sŏ: Oh, heavens!
Pyŏn: No, no... I was sleeping and the mosquitoes were biting me, so I must have crawled in without thinking. I quit drinking after that.
Sŏ: What a coward. To chase away your heroic nature over something like that!
Ssang S: Sŏl Ŭi-sik, please tell us your story.
Sŏl: I don't have any interesting stories. Because I don't drink... If I had to say something, it would be about being embarrassed after going to Yŏngdosa (靈導寺)... It was the day after the late King Yi's funeral rites. I had been up all night preparing special editions...
Sŏ: During your time as the social affairs director?
Sŏl: Yes... And with Han In-bong, Ch’ae Man-sik, Pak Man-sŏ, and a few others, we went to Yŏngdosa to eat temple food. With two kisaeng (妓生) in tow... So we went and drank and sang—the friends who went with me were drunk and singing, but no matter how much I told them to stop, they wouldn't listen. While we were having fun, a man came in with a very disgusted look on his face, asked for a glass of cold water, and left. I thought nothing of it... but as we were coming back in the dark and passing through the pine forest near Yŏngdosa, Han In-bong, who was walking with a kisaeng, ran into a group of thugs. It looked like there were about 20 of them, while we were only five or six, so there was no chance of winning. When I tried to stop the fight, one of them slapped me hard across the cheek. Oh... I was so angry I could have died. If I hadn't thought about the consequences, I would have beaten one of them half to death, but I couldn't do that because of my reputation, so I just cried in frustration.
Sŏ: You really like to cry!
Yi: In that kind of situation, you should follow Ch’a Sang-ch’an's example.
Sŏ: How?
Yi: Once, Ch’a Sang-ch’an was drunk on Kwanch’ŏl-dong (貫鐵洞) street and deliberately bumped into someone passing by. The other person got angry and stood there, ready to make a scene, so Ch’a quickly took off his hat and said, "This man is weak. Please don't hit me"... The other person was so dumbfounded that he laughed and left.
Yun (Sŏng): Truly a Ch’a genius... If you act so cleverly, you'll never get hit. Aigoo! Really.
Ch’a: What is improvisation, after all? If someone suddenly faces an unexpected mishap, there's nothing they can do. More than that, I got a lot of flak from the students of a certain vocational school because of an article I wrote in Pyŏlgŏn-gŏn (別乾坤).
Yi: You mean the time you got grabbed by the collar and beaten?
Ch’a: I wasn't beaten, but those people swore at me without even asking what happened and even threatened to attack me. What an embarrassment!
Sŏ: It would have been better if you had been beaten up a little then.
Ch’a: Even if someone hadn't intervened, I might have been beaten up. What can you do near the courthouse?
Hŏ: Tell us about the Tongdaemun (東大門) police station incident.
Yun (Sŏng): Everyone knows about it... A young man dressed neatly suddenly proposed to me on the street in broad daylight. What could be more embarrassing than that... So I had no choice but to take him to the Tongdaemun police station. I later heard that he had even graduated from university in Japan.
Hŏ: It was in the newspapers too.
Yun (Sŏng): Yes, it was in the Chosŏn Ilbo, where I work, but it said that a certain madman harassed a noblewoman on the street.
Ch’a: It's easy to be a noblewoman if you're Sŏng-sang.
Yun (Sŏng): Why do you always have to pick on people with every word you say?
Ch’a: Why would I pick on you? I didn't even touch you.
Hŏ: I don't know if you'd call it an embarrassment, but I was really scared... It was when I opened my practice at my new house in Tangju-dong (唐珠洞), so it was a long time ago. But back then, there weren't many female doctors in Korea.
Sŏ: A single red flower in a green bush.
Hŏ: Oh, I'm not going to talk if Sŏ continues to act like that.
Sŏ: No, no, just keep going.
Hŏ: Anyway, one day, someone came—it was very late at night—saying that there was an urgent patient and asking me to come. Without thinking, I prepared for a house call and followed the person in a rickshaw to a very rich man's house. I went into the sarangbang (사랑방) as I was guided, but there was no patient. A young man who seemed to be the owner greeted me. I thought the patient was a woman and was inside, so I sat down for a moment while he went to guide me there, but the owner started flirting with me. When I asked who was sick, he said he was feeling ill. The healthy fellow!... And then he tried to grab my wrist! Aigoo—I don't know how I got out of there, but I think I aged ten years.
Sŏ: So you're saying you can only live to ninety now?
Hŏ: Who said I'd live to be a hundred?
Sŏ: But wasn't there also that time when the poet XXX followed you from Tokyo and chased you in Chongno...?
Hŏ: Yes, XXX. That's embarrassing, let's not talk about it.
Yŏm: I was internationally embarrassed while drunk.
Pyŏn: That's a strange way to be embarrassed.
Yŏm: I got drunk and went to the British Consulate... I went and knocked on the locked door and shouted, and a servant came out. I asked him for a glass of cold water. The man was so dumbfounded that he asked if I knew where I was. So I asked if this wasn't the British Consulate.
Everyone: (Laughs loudly.)
Yun (Ik): It was a long time ago, but I was taking my wife for a walk in Ch’ŏngnyangni (淸凉里) when I had a big mishap. It's not that strange to walk around with your wife now, but back then, if a man and woman walked together, people assumed they were having an affair and would tease them... Without knowing any better, we were walking through the pine forest when dozens of men, like street thugs, came running and started swearing at us. They looked like they were going to beat us if we said anything wrong. I had a really bad time.
Yi: I'll tell you a story about pooping my pants.
Ch’a: Ugh—that's smelly.
Yi: No—it's all washed now. Don't worry.
Sŏ: Excuse me for asking so bluntly, but how does someone end up pooping their pants?
Yi: At the Kaktŭkpa (갓득빠).
Sŏ: Oh, my!
Yi: I went to the Venus and had to pee. So I went to the bathroom to pee, but then a little bit of the big one (feces) came out with the little one, what could I do?
Everyone: (Laughs loudly.) <6>
Yi: So what could I do! I came back out and there were customers. And then Kim Myŏng-sun (金명순) came over and told me to sit next to her. How could I sit next to her? What if the smell went straight into her nose? And the seat was so awkward that I couldn't even get up. How could I manage without getting caught, I was so scared.
Ch’a: Since that story came up, there was a friend of mine named Yi Tu-sŏng (李斗星), who was the publisher of Kaebyŏk (開闢)—he's dead now—he had diarrhea in the summer and went out on a boat ride on the Han River. On the way back, he pooped his pants on the streetcar. Then, he got off in front of Kyŏngsŏng (京城) Station and went into the first-class restroom and washed his soiled underwear. That's all good and well, but when he got back on the streetcar and stood up, everyone in the car started laughing and whispering. When he looked back, there was red blood on his ramie durumagi (두루맥이)... He was so surprised that he panicked, but then he realized that the water from his washed pants had soaked into the red velvet of the streetcar and dyed it red.
Kang: I'll tell a story about embarrassing someone else rather than being embarrassed myself.
Sŏ: That's good too.
Kang: A student from a certain vocational school was writing letters and persistently following me around. One day, I ran into him on the street and he greeted me happily and started talking to me. So I smiled back and led him to a window sill... Even when the new maid opened the window behind him, he remained calm and didn't even turn around. He must have been drunk with the happiness of succeeding in love. So I quietly poured a bucket of dirty water on his head.
Everyone: (Laughs loudly.)
Ssang S: Well—I'm busy, so I can't listen to your nonsense any longer. I'll stop here, so if anyone's thirsty, go buy a bowl of makgŏlli (막걸리) with your own money. <7>

사회  雙 S

雙 S: 에- 여러분 밧부실텐데 이러케 만히 출석해 주서서 대단히 고맙습니다. 담배는 다각기 가지고 오신 것이 잇거든 내노코 피우십시요. 실상은 명월관 가튼데로 모시려고 햇스나 이 좌담회가 봉변망신한 좌담회요 또 오신 여러분이 대개 술을 질겨하시는데 그러케 대접을 하엿다가 또 술을 만히 취해서 봉변이나 망신을 하신다면 미안한 일이겟고 해서 이러케 원고지 우에다 초대를 한 것입니다.
인제는 잔말은 그만두고 제목대로 다 각기 망신이나 봉변하든 이야기를 하나 이상씩 해 주십시요. 혹 그 중에 잘 하시는 분이 게시면 잇다가 도라갈 때 살작 따가지고 선술이나 한 잔 대접할지 모르겟습니다.
卞: 에구 술이라면 나는 실혀
徐: 그러면 내가 하지… 세상에 술 못먹는 것도 사내자식이람!
廉, 車, 李: (일시에) 올소.
尹(聖), 許, 姜: 그런 법은 업서요 업서요.
薛: 나는 술 먹는 사람을 보면 사람갓지가 아니해…
雙 S: 아-니 왜들 이러시요? 술이야기를 작고만 하면 막걸니 잔이라도 내올 줄 알고 그러지만 천만에! 이게 어느 판이라고… 자- 서춘씨부터 어서 이야기를 하시요.
徐: 네, 하지요. 그러나 잇다가 선물로 주는 상을 내가 바더야 하오… 그게 어느 때든지 아마 한 3년 되엇스리다.＜4＞
술을 담북 먹엇지요. 취햇지요. 지금은 업서젓지만 인사동 잇는 아세아 카페-에서 그랫습니다. 그래 거기 잇는 게집애더러 택씨-를 불너달래서 타고 집으로 가지 아니햇겟소.
許: 원 승거워!
徐: 잔말말로 그 담을 들어봐요. 그래 집에로 가서 차삭을 줄랴고 하니까 돈이 업겟지… 할 수 업시 명함을 주엇더니 아니밧는단 말이야.
薛: 서춘이 명함이 그리 장해서?
徐: 그래도 들어봐요… 이 운전수군이 기어히 명함을 밧지 아니하고는 나를 돌우 실어가지고 파출소로 갓단 말이지 파출소에 가서 옥신각신 시비를 하나 소용이 잇나… 나는 그런중에 파출소 안에다 소변을 보앗지요… 그런 망신이라니!
一同: (크게 웃는다. 아이 망칙해라. 소리도 들닌다.)
徐: 그런데 말이지… 순사가 웨 자동차를 타고 차삭을 아니 주느냐길네 돈이 업다고 지갑을 털어보이는데 도장이 떨어지니까 순사가 그것을 집어 보더니 갑작히 태도가 변해가지고 운전수더러 하는 말이 이 양반은 동아일보사 편즙국이신데 그까지 돈 1원 잘너먹을 어른이 아니시니까 어서 댁으로 모서다 드리라고 호령호령을 하겟지.
一同: (크게 웃는다.)
卞: 나도 술먹고 망신한 이야기나 하지. 지금은 술은 양주회사 연돌만 보아도 달어나지만 년전이야 술 만히 먹고 주사 잇기로 유명한 이 변영로가 아닙니까? 한데 어느 날 어느 친구를 차저가서 권커니 작커니 밤이 깁도록 코가 비트러지도록 먹은 것 까지는 관찬햇는데 글세 술이 어떠케 취햇든지 친구부인이 자고 잇는 모기장 속에를 기어들어 갓구려!
徐: 엑기 천하에!
卞: 아니야 아니야… 잠을 자다가 모기가 물고 하니까 정신업시 기어들어 갓든 모양이야. 나는 그 뒤로 술을 끈헛서.
徐: 졸장부다. 그것쯤 가지고 호걸을 내쫏츤 셈이니!
雙 S: 설의식씨 이야기줌 하시지요.
薛: 내야 머 재미잇는 이야기가 잇나요. 술을 아니 먹으니까… 잇다면 영도사에 나갓다가 봉변한 이야기나 할가요… 그게 그러니까 리왕께서 도라가시고 성복이 지난 그 이튼날 입니다. 여러 날 호외준비로 줄곳 밤을 새우고 해서…
徐: 사회부장 시절이로구먼?
薛: 네… 그런데 지금은 업지만 한인봉
채만식, 박만서 외에 멧사람과 영도사로 절밥을 먹으러 갓지요. 기생도응 둘 데리고… 그래 가서 술을 먹고 소리를 하고- 소리는 갓치간 친구들이 취해 가지고 불넛는데 아모리 말녀야 들어먹어야지요. 그래 한참 노는 판인데 웬 사람 하나가 장히 뇌꼴스런 눈쌀을 해 가지고 그 집에 와서 냉수 한 그릇을 어더먹고 갑듸다. 그저 그런가부다고 햇는데 어둠컴컴해서 도라오느라고 영도사 압 송림을 지내느라니까 압헤서 기생을 끼고가든 한인봉이가 쌈패에 걸녓지요. 보니까 저편은 한 20명이나 되는데 우리는 오륙명이니 될 말이요. 내가 쌈을 말리려고 나서니까 어느 놈이 댓자곳자로 뺨을 한 대 보기조케 붓친단 말이지. 허허… 분해서 꼭 죽겟서요. 뒷일을 아니 생각하면 어느 놈 하나를 반죽엄을 식혀노켓지만 체면에 그럴 수도 업고 그래 혼자 분해서 울엇지요.
徐: 도대처 울기를 조아하는구먼!
李: 그런 경우에는 저 차상찬씨 뽄을 따요.
徐: 어떠케?
李: 한 번 관철동길에서 술이 취해 가지고-차상찬씨가-지나가는 사람을 일부러 툭 첫더라나요. 그러니까 그 사람이 골이 나가지고＜5＞ 떡 버틔고 서서 그야말로 테로를 할 눈치가 보이니까 차씨는 얼는 모자를 버서들고 하는 말이 이 사람은 약질이올시다. 매다 꼰지지는 마십시요… 하더라고 그러니까 그 사람은 하도 어이가 업서 픽 웃고 가버리더래요.
尹(聖): 과연 차천자식이로군… 그러케 살살 알은거리니까 어대가 매도 아니 마질거야. 아이그! 참-.
車: 임시응변이 다 무엇입니까. 사람이 별안간 뜻박게 봉변을 하랴면 별 수 업지요. 나는 그것 보담도 별건곤에 기사 쓴것 때문에 모 전문학교 학생들한데 참 톡톡히 욕을 보왓지요.
李: 멱살 잡히고 매마즌 일 말이지요?
車: 매야 아니 마젓지만 그 여러 사람들이 그저 말 세마듸 하고는 불문곡직하고 함부로 욕을 하면서 육박까지 할뜻이 덤비는데 그런 망신이라니!
徐: 그 때 그저 매나 듬신 좀 마젓스면 시언햇슬텐데.
