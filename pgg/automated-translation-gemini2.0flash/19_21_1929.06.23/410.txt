Article Title	Adventures of the Existing Brave Man's Youth, Sweeping the Outlaw's Den with One Whip=The Divine Power and Strategy of Flying Tiger General, -Yun Yŏng-ryŏl During His Time as Ansong County Magistrate- (Adventures of a Brave Man's Youth)
Author	Kuisŏksan'in
Article Type	Memoir/Essay

The existing brave man's youthful adventure of blood boiling and flesh throbbing, the Flying Tiger General's divine might and valor in sweeping away the bandit's nest with a single whip, -Yun Yŏng-ryŏl during his time as Ansong County Magistrate-
						Kuisŏksan'in

If you start from Jongno intersection in Gyeongseong and head north, when you reach Anguk-dong intersection, look east, and go north alongside the Byeolgung's eastern wall, you will see Anguk-dong Church towering on your left. Directly across from that church is a magnificent large house. People passing in front of it ask,
"Whose house is this? It must be a rich man's house?"
"Who is it? Let's look at the nameplate..."
"The nameplate says Yun Ch'i-so, but who is he?"
"Oh, you don't know! He's Yun Ung-ryŏl's elder brother and Yun Yŏng-ryŏl's son!"
"Yun Yŏng-ryŏl? Are you talking about Yun Yŏng-ryŏl who was the police chief (p'ododaejang)?" <86>
"That's right! Exactly!"
They exchange these questions and answers as they pass by. The old master of this house, Yun Yŏng-ryŏl, is now one of the wealthiest men (大福之人) in Korea (longevity, wealth, nobility, many children and grandchildren) and the object of envy for people in the world. Two years ago, he spent a large sum of money to celebrate his hwegollye (回婚禮, 60th wedding anniversary) in such a grand and extravagant manner that Japanese newspapers called him the "Matsukata Masayoshi of Korea" for quite some time. Although he is such a blessed man, few people seem to know that in his youth, he faced dangers and hardships in battlefields where arrows and stones rained down and in killing fields where swords flashed, enduring difficulties that others could not easily overcome.

Okjŏng Pass Grand Drama

Throughout his life, there are many episodes, but if you hear about the episode from his 20s, especially during his time as Ansong County Magistrate, everyone will feel great satisfaction and be endlessly amazed.
In the old days, the ranks of county magistrates (kunsu) were differentiated into hyŏn'gam (縣監) and hyŏnllyŏng (縣令) depending on the size of the county, and even among county magistrates, there was a distinction between civil and military officials depending on the topography and customs of the county. Because Ansong County in Gyeonggi Province was a strategically important place surrounded by dangerous mountain passes, the custom at the time was that only military officers could become county magistrates. Ansong, located in the southeastern part of Gyeonggi Province, was a major hub (雄洲), and it was also famous for its developed production and manufacturing (rice, brassware, etc.) since ancient times, being a prominent commercial and industrial market in the three southern provinces. Moreover, due to its location, it was a crucial point (咽喉之地) connecting Chungcheongbuk-do and parts of Gyeongsangbuk-do to Gyeongseong, so the transport of taxes and tributes to the capital passed through this land in spring and autumn, crossing the dangerous Okjŏng Pass (玉政峙) on the border with Jincheon in Chungcheongbuk-do. For this reason, Ansong, Juksan, Jincheon, and Ŭmsŏng were known as places with many bandits (불한당, outlaw gangs), but there was probably no place with more bandits than Okjŏng Pass. In any case, Okjŏng Pass was the headquarters of bandits from Tokyo (Seoul), Gyeonggi, and North Chungcheong.

Therefore, many military officers (hoban, 虎班) had come as Ansong County Magistrate, but with only a few government soldiers or police officers, they usually ended up being surrounded and fleeing in disgrace by the bandits, causing great headaches for the authorities at the time. Then, Yun Yŏng-ryŏl, at the young age of 25, assumed the seal of Ansong County Magistrate and confidently entered Ansong territory, spurring his horse. He must have known that Ansong was the headquarters of bandits and had a firm resolution about it, because three days after his inauguration, after inspecting the officers, police, and clerks, he devoted himself to military training day and night. By adding training upon training for several months, using Sun-Tzu's military strategy and Zhuge Liang's battle formations, he prepared for war with well-equipped and disciplined troops and then began the campaign to eradicate the bandits.
The bandit gang, like snakes and scorpions and jackals and wolves (蛇蝎 豺狼), the outlaws experienced in suppressing government troops, constantly scouted the new county magistrate's character and operational plans. Having learned in advance that he was focusing on military training, they built strongholds (山寨) and practiced tactics, preparing for a major battle. They were like a gang of Chinese bandits and horse thieves (土匪 馬賊), not just a small gang of robbers.

Yun not only had extraordinary courage but was also as agile as a flying tiger (飛虎), easily leaping over tall buildings and deep rivers. Furthermore, his beloved horse was a thousand-li steed (千里駿驄), so when facing the enemy, it was like Guan Yu's Red Hare (赤兔馬) in the past, so fast that the Hebei generals Yan Liang (顔良) and Wen Chou (文醜) had no time to prepare before their heads fell where the blade flashed. Therefore, people at the time called him the Flying Tiger General.
His sharp spirit and his formation looked as if he intended to pierce and destroy the general headquarters at Okjŏng Pass, so he first sent secret agents to scout the mountain terrain and then led villagers (良民) who had been harmed and captured as guides, surrounding Okjŏng Pass for 40 li in 10 layers. He himself became the vanguard and charged into the bandit's den. One of the officers advised him, "Please spare your precious body and avoid the vanguard," but shouting repeatedly, "How can you catch tiger cubs without entering the tiger's den (不入虎穴 安得虎子)?" he single-handedly launched an attack. How adventurous was this? However, his spirit seemed to pierce the clouds, and his bravery seemed to devour dragons and tigers. Who could block his swift swordplay? Wherever his sword and spear flashed, the heads of bandits rolled, and wherever his horse turned, the enemies of the people trembled in fear. Despite the rugged mountain fortress, he endured wind and rain of blood with spears and stones, fighting hundreds of battles. Since the bandit army was an untrained rabble (鳥合之衆), they lowered their flags, held their spears and swords upside down, and fled as the best option of the 36 strategies, but who could escape the net of the officers and police? Not even one bandit was left, and they were all tied up with ropes. In this way, the total headquarters of the bandits who were like snakes and scorpions and jackals and wolves! The source of anxiety and terror, threat, plunder, slaughter, and ruin to good people was wiped out under the single whip of the Flying Tiger General.

Danger! Great Danger!!

After sweeping away the main den (本窟), the remaining bandits were easily annihilated by a few dozen officers and police without Yun's personal involvement, and within a year, there was no trace of bandits. As a result, not only Ansong but also the neighboring towns of Juksan, Jincheon, and Yangsŏng could sleep peacefully with their heads high and doors unlocked at night.
One day, he went on a business trip to a remote mountain village about five or six li away and was unable to return, so he had to stay overnight at a wealthy man's house. Although he was suppressing unjust bandits, he had killed so many people that he was always uneasy, so he always made sure that his lodging was not easily known and surrounded it with police as a fortress before feeling safe enough to stay overnight. That night, as usual, the front, back, left, and right of the house were guarded by police, and he was preparing to sleep alone in the middle of the main room, with a long sword and dagger placed at his bedside. Suddenly, the door opened slightly, and a large man (一大漢) – a seven-foot giant with eyes stretched to his ears – strode in, drew a seven-shaku (尺, unit of length) sword with a glittering blade from his waist, and said,
"Are you the guy, Yun Yŏng-ryŏl!"
Yun slightly relaxed his tense body and said,
"Yes! I am Yun Yŏng-ryŏl!"
"Ha! Today is the day you die! My father was the chief of the bandits, and you captured and killed him last year, so how could I, as his son, just stand by! I've been secretly plotting to take your life for a year, and thanks to divine help, I've met you here tonight! This is the last moment of your life!"
His tone was a solemn death sentence. The scene of him standing close by, pointing the tip of his sword at Yun's forehead, was filled with murderous intent. Hero without a place to use his skills (英雄 無用 武之地), a crisis, a crisis! Where else could there be such a crisis? However, his nature was such that he became more calm in such a crisis. He nodded and said,
"Oh! That makes sense! But did you come alone? Or with others?"
"I came alone! Do you think I need several people to take the life of someone like you!"
"Really? Then who is standing behind you?"
No matter how bold a warrior he might be, he had clearly come alone, so hearing that someone was standing behind him, how could he remain calm? Although he had entered boldly, he was afraid of making a mistake, knowing that Yun's defenses were strong, so he instinctively turned around. In that moment, Yun grabbed the dagger that was placed by his pillow and threw it like lightning, and the bandit fell to the ground with a thud. Quick thinking! This was truly greater agility than Cao Cao (曹操).

Second Grand Drama

Once, he had urgent business and went to Jincheon (鎭川) in Chungcheong Province and was returning. He stopped at the Jaeyang (長楊) marketplace to have dinner. Suddenly, a man with a pale face, covered in sweat, and gasping for breath ran into the tavern. People rushed to help him, and after he managed to catch his breath, they asked him what had happened. He said that he had gone to the Ansong market to buy goods, but it was late, so he stayed overnight and was returning to Jincheon today. While crossing Okjŏng Pass, he was suddenly captured by bandits who jumped out from between the rocks. They dragged him up the mountain (등마루로), stole all his money, and hung him upside down from a pine tree. He escaped while they went into the tavern to drink. Hearing this, Yun seemed to have thought of something. He quickly finished his dinner and asked the owner for a straw mat (myŏksŏri, 藁席) and a bundle of hemp rope (ma sŭm). He disguised himself as a merchant and told the police officers, "Follow me about two hours after I leave." He left and went to a pebble field (石礫) below Okjŏng Pass. He filled the straw mat with stones, each about the size of his fist, tied it tightly, and carried it on his back. In the dim moonlight of dusk, he slowly climbed the pass. When he reached the top of the pass (where there is still a tavern now), there were two or three taverns where travelers rested and gambled. That night, the bandits were probably having a great time drinking and gambling with the stolen money. As Yun was sweating and carrying the heavy load, one of the bandits who came out and was staggering saw him and stared blankly without saying anything before rushing back into the room. Yun pretended not to notice and continued down the pass for a while. Then, from the top of the pass, he heard a shout, "Hey you! Stop right there!" And then the sound of thousands of soldiers rushing down the mountain echoed. Yun quietly placed the straw mat on a rock by the side of the road, untied it rustling, and grabbed stones in both hands. He responded to the first shout with "pang!" and one bandit fell face down. The bandits who were chasing him fell one by one, and their foreheads "thudded" as they rolled down the mountain. In this way, the gang of several dozen bandits was wiped out in just a few tens of minutes. Who knew that Yun was so good at throwing stones? The violent bandits only thought that Yun was carrying a load of money (金錢) and chased him, but they were all knocked down by his stones and captured by the police officers who were following him. This was probably the grand finale of Yun's bandit suppression during his time as Ansong County Magistrate, and a great adventurous drama (대활극) during his energetic twenties. (End) <90>

피끌코 살뗑이 뛰노는=現存 壯士 靑春時代의 冒險談, 不汗黨巢窟을 一鞭에 掃蕩한=飛虎將軍의 神威勇略, -安城郡守時代의 尹英烈翁-
						龜石山人
경성 종로 네거리에서 북부로 치다라서 안국동 네거리 당도하거든 동쪽으로 내다라서 別宮 동편 담을 끼고 다시 북으로 북으로만 곳장 올라가고 보면 왼편으로 안국동 예배당이 웃둑 솟아 잇다. 그 예배당에서 바로 마즌편으로 宏傑한 큰 대문집이 닥처온다. 그 압흐로 지나가는 사람들.
『이 건뉘 집인가? 어느 부잣집인가붸 그려?』
『누군가? 문패를 보게나 그려...』
『문패는 尹致昭라구 부터 잇네만 그는 도대톄 누구란 말이야?』
『압다 이 사람아! 尹雄烈의 伯氏 尹英烈의 아들일세 그려!』
『윤영렬이라니? 포도대장하든 윤영렬이 말인가?』<86>
『올치 올치 똑바로 마젓네! 마젓서...』
이러한 문답을 주거니 밧거니 하면 지나간다. 이 집의 老主人 公尹英烈翁은 지금 와서는 朝鮮안에서 두 사람도 업는 大福之人으로 (壽富貴多子孫) 세상 사람의 欽慕의 관역이 되어 잇스며 재작년에는 거액의 자금을 드려서 회혼례를 엇지나 굉장 뻑은 하게 치두웟든지 일본신문에서는 조선의 松方正義라고 한참동안이나 떠드러 댓다. 이러케 팔자 조흔 大福之人이지만 그의 靑少 시대에는 矢石이 雨下하는 戰場에서 劒戟이 閃閃한 살육장에서 남의 격거내기 어려운 위험과 고난을 치루워 온 사람인 것을 아는 사람은 적은 것 갓다.

玉政峙大活劇

그의 일생을 통해서 별의별 일화가 만치만은 그의 20當年의 일화=그 중에도 安城郡守시대의 그것을 한 번 들어보면 누구나 다-大痛快를 늣기게 되며 감탄을 마지 안케 될 것이다.
녯날 郡守級에는 縣監縣令의 구별이 군의 대소에 딸아서 다르며 또 다가튼 군수 중에도 군의 地勢와 人情을 딸아 文武의 구별이 잇는 것이엿다. 그런데 경기도 안성군은 險嶺重關을 낀 요세지임으로 무관이라야 군수를 하게 된 것이 그 때의 관제이엿다. 안성이란 경기도 동남단에 잇는 雄洲이며 또한 녜로부터 생산 제조업(米穀 유기 등)의 발달로 유명하야 삼남에 屈指하는 무역, 공업 시장임을 딸아 居民의 생활이 풍요한 그 우에 교통으로 보아서 충청북도와 경상북도 일부로서 경성으로 통하는 咽喉之地임으로 春秋로 稅納의 運搬 上京이 이 땅을 밟게 되며 忠淸北道 鎭川과의 接界에 옥정이ㅅ 고개 (玉政峙)라는 險嶺을 넘게 되는 것이다. 이러한 관계로 安城 竹山 鎭川 陰城하는 곳은 不汗黨 (强盜團) 만키로 일음난 곳이지만 그 중에도 옥정이ㅅ고개처름 도적놈 만이 난 곳은 업슬 것이다. 엇잿든지 옥정이ㅅ고개라는 데는 東京畿 北忠淸의 불한당의 총본영이엿섯다.
그럼으로 안성군수는 호반(武官)이 만히 해나려 왓섯지만 약간의 관병이나 捕校개로는 정복은 커냥 도리어 賊軍에게 포위 도망의 추태를 연출한 것이 상례엿스며 때의 당국자들의 큰 두통꺼리엿섯다. 그리다가 尹英烈운 25세의 약관으로 안성군수의 印授를 차고 得意揚揚 安城 경내로 말골비를 춧석이며 호기 잇게 부임햇다. 그는 물론 안성이 불한당의 총본영인 줄도 알고 갓슬 것이며 여긔 대한 어떠한 確乎不拔한 결심이 잇섯든지 부임후 3일 만에 장교 포교 사령 등의 점검을 마친 후에 그 날로부터 晝夜兼行 군사교련에 精硏하엿다. 孫武의 병서와 제갈의 진법으로 몃 달동안 조련에 조련을 加하야 兵精精足의 戰備를 정돈한 연후에 賊窟 소탕 불한당 근절의 행군을 시작한다.
蛇蝎 豺狼가튼 强盜團은=官軍 토벌에 노련한 不汗黨들은 쉴 새 업시 신임 郡守의 인물과 작전 계획을 探偵하여서 이미 군사 교련에 전력을 기우리는 줄 미리 알은지라 저의들도 山寨를<87> 당당히 얼거노코 전술을 연습하야 일대 결전을 예비하엿다. 中國의 土匪 馬賊과 가튼 떼이며 여간 조고마한 强盜團은 아니엿다.
尹은 담력이 過人할 뿐만 아니라 날내기 飛虎가티여 高樓巨閣이나 大川深溝가튼 것은 예사로 훌쩍 훌쩍 뛰어넘는 勇力이엿스며 그 우에 그의 애마는 千里駿驄이라 賊軍과 臨陣對敵할ㅅ 적에는 녯날 관운장의 적토마가 河北 名將 顔良 文醜의 밋처 정신을 차리고 준비를 할 겨를도 업시 칼날이 빗나는 곳에 목이 벌서 떠러지든 그러한 神足을 가젓섯다. 그럼으로 때의 사람들은 그를 가르처 飛虎 將軍이라고 일커럿다 한다.
