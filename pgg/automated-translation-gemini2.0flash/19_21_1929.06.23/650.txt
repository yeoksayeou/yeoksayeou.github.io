別건곤 제21호
1929년 06월 23일
初夏隨筆 金衣公子 (Early Summer Essay: The Golden-Robed Young Master)
李殷相 (Yi Eun-sang)
문예기타 (Literature and Others)

Was it as a mountain, was it as a river, from somewhere, without a path, the spring that had come seeking, has again, as a mountain, has it gone as a river, has gone without a path.

◇◇

"Nightingale"—"Willow"—"Green Shade"—
Just calling those names, their fragrance and abundance seem to embrace my body, weary from the dusty world—that serenity, that sanctity, that mystery……

◇◇

It is said that Tang Minghuang (唐明皇) saw a nightingale in his forbidden garden (禁苑) and called it "Golden-Robed Young Master (金衣公子)." How rich, noble, joyful, and peaceful that name is!
Was it only the nightingales in that dreamlike forbidden garden that were like that? Did it only appear that way to that person, Minghuang?

No, to anyone, at this place, the nightingale in the green shade cannot but be seen as a "Golden-Robed Young Master."
Oh, the happiness of enjoying that, now and always, unchanging! <158>

◇◇

Why did the old people, seeing a nightingale playing happily among the willow leaves—
The willow becomes thread
The nightingale becomes the shuttle
In ninety-three springs
Weaving out my sorrow (omitted below)
Sing of this! That is also that person's emotion and sorrow, and it is not appropriate to judge right or wrong, but the willows dancing as willows, and the nightingales singing as nightingales, that alone must be their pure joy that cannot be disturbed. That eternal joy!

◇◇

There is a song among the Tang Dynasty's merchant tunes (商調曲):
Do not wake the nightingale
Do not let it sing on the branch
For I cannot fulfill my dream
How can I go to the land of Liaoxi (遼西) (Anonymous Yizhou Translated)

A woman in an empty room (空閨) sings, thinking of her husband, a conscript, far away in Liaoxi (遼西戌). But I, in place of that sleeping nightingale, want to sing this song:
Shut that woman's mouth
Do not let her chatter under the tree
The joyous dream of meeting my love
Lest it be broken

Yes! In the willows! Nightingale! Whether sleeping or awake, what does it matter? That serenity, that sanctity, that mystery only empties people…(× day, from Diary) <159>

						李殷相
山으로서 왓는지, 江으로서 왓는지, 어대서인지, 길업시, 차자왓든봄이 다시 山으로하여 갓는지, 江으로하여 갓는지, 길업시가버리엇다.
◇◇
『꾀꼴이』—『버들』—『綠陰』—
그이름들만 불러보아도 그향긔로움 복스러움이 紅塵에 시달리우는몸을 부드안아 주는것갓다—그昇平함, 그聖潔함, 그神秘로움이……
◇◇
唐明皇이 그 禁苑속에서 꾀꼴이를보고『金衣公子』라고 불렷더라한다. 그이름이 얼마나 富하고 貴하고 즐겁고 太平한가.
그속가튼 禁苑속의 꾀꼴이들만이 그러하엿든가. 明皇이란 그사람에게만 그러케 보이엇든가.
아니다, 누구에게나, 이대서나, 綠陰속의 꼴이야말로『金衣公子』로보이지 안흘수업다.
매나 이제나, 변함업시 누리는 그 幸福됨이어!＜158＞
◇◇
녯사람은 어이하여 버들닙속에서 즐거히노는꼴이를보고—
버들은 실이되고
꾀꼴이는 북이되어
九十三春에
내는니 나의시름(下略)
리라 놀애하엿든고! 그도 그사람의 情이요 슬픔이니 是非할배야 아니언만 버들은 버들로 춤을추고, 꾀꼴이는 꾀꼴이로 놀애하는 그것만은 건디릴수업는 저이들의 깨끗한 즐거움일것이다. 그 永遠한즐거움!
◇◇
唐의 商調曲中에 이런놀래가잇다.
꾀꼴이 일깨워서
가지우에 울리질마라
꿈일우지 못하리니
遼西땅에 어이가리(無名氏伊州歐譯)
空閨의계집이 멀리 遼西戌에 가잇는 征夫를생각하고 부른놀애다. 그러나 나는 그잠든꾀꼴이를 대신하여 이런놀애를 불러주고십다.
저게집 입을막아
나무알에 들게마라
님맛난 즐거운꿈
행여나 깨어질세라
그러타! 버들속에! 꾀꼴이야! 자든 깨엇든 무슨관게랴 그昇平, 그聖潔, 그神秘로움이 사람을 비우슬이니……(×日, 日記에선)＜159＞
