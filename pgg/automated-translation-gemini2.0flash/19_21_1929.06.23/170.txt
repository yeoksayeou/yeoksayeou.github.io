기사제목	청춘의 꿈, 닷는 꿈 (Dreams of Youth, Pursuing Dreams)
필자	柳光烈
기사형태	문예기타 (Miscellaneous Literature)

Dreams of Youth, Pursuing Dreams
						柳光烈

Write about the dreams of youth! It is both a joyful and a sorrowful thing. Even this dark and gloomy world, where dust covers the sky and the whistling wind of evil spins, looks beautiful to youth, like a garden blooming in the bright sunlight. Before tasting the bitter taste of the world, it looks as sweet as a ripe fruit. This is because they only have hope and do not know disappointment, they only know possibility and do not know impossibility.

Youth! What a hopeful word. All the courses of life start here. At the same time, there are many splendid dreams. Now, I will recall one by one the dreams of my past youth and write this article. Now that I am thirty-two years old, I am not yet past my youth. However, I have lost the purity and peace of mind to dream the dreams of youth that I write about here.

Because I grew up in the countryside, my dreams were not dreamed under the electric lights of the city. I was caught up in endless dreams while wandering in the forest. At that time, I was a child of seven or eight years old, so I did not set a specific goal, but simply wanted to become a great man, as my parents had said. I wanted to become a prominent person in this world. And secretly, I would ask and answer myself, "If I become a famous person - if everyone praises me - then I will answer like this." I whispered this too long. When the sound was heard by my older sister, she asked me, "What are you whispering about?" I denied it, saying, "No," but I felt ashamed as if my heart had been exposed. <37>
As I grew up, the dream of becoming a great man continued. However, the vaguely defined object of the great man gradually became the historical figures I dreamed of. And after I was thirteen or fourteen years old and gradually opened my eyes to the matter of sex, I also dreamed of spending my life with one of the most beautiful women in the world. But these various dreams were just dreams. Every time I faced reality, I realized that my thoughts were nothing more than a kind of splendid fantasy.

I still have some youth left. I don't know if I'm still dreaming these dreams. There is a saying in an old person's writing, "Life is all a spring dream (人生이 都是一場春夢이라)." I may still be dreaming a long, long dream of life. Marx told us to start all theories from "self-evidence (셀푸에비렌스)." But even that self-evidence is difficult to know. Now, I don't know if the fact that I am holding a pen and writing this article is a "white and bright (白明)" fact. But that, too, may be a dream.

I want to dream again. After graduating from some industrial school, I was holding a test tube with the thought of giving happiness to the Koreans. However, the test materials that I used every month cost a huge amount of money. As I could not escape from my living expenses and test fees, I was in despair and had to stop, but there was someone who sympathized with this. So, with that person's financial support, the research was completed, and I started some manufacturing industry. By applying some chemical processing to ordinary materials, excellent products demanded in the world market were produced. They built a huge factory and produced on a large scale, earning huge profits. The money saved from this income amounted to more than 20 million won in a few years. Half of this money was set aside to establish the Korean People's University (朝鮮民立大學), and eminent scholars from around the world were invited to cultivate young Korean talents, and on the other hand, a chemical industry research institute was established to conduct research on this, and the manufacturing industry in Korea flourished as a result of the research of various scholars, and on the other hand, overseas students were dispatched to absorb the advantages of Eastern and Western civilizations.

The dream runs away in another direction. I took a small amount of travel expenses and ended up in some tropical region of the South Seas (南洋). For a while, I was stunned, but I plucked up my courage and started working as a laborer on some Westerner's farm. After a few years of diligence, I had some savings in my hands. With that, I started a small farm and planted "coffee (카피)" and other things needed in various markets in the East. The farm gradually expanded and became a vast farm. A large number of workers were employed to produce and send products to various ports such as Hong Kong and Shanghai. In this way, I obtained wealth of more than 3 million won. With that money, I came to Manchuria and tried to secure a path to happiness for the Korean people with our compatriots in Manchuria.

The dream runs away in another direction. Looking at the setting sun over the vast Manchurian plains, I was in despair for a long time. But how could the course of life end in despair? I searched for our compatriots and talked to them as far as my sincerity reached, collected the scattered hearts, and hurried to establish an economic <38> foundation first. And after gathering the will of our compatriots and negotiating with the Chinese authorities to secure a place to settle down first, we cultivated strength in each direction. And I tried to promote the happiness of the Korean people.

The dream runs away in another direction. I entered some university in a certain country's market. Every day, as I learned new truths, I would think of my desolate homeland when I was tired, and leaning on the window, I was deeply moved. I don't know what it is, but outside the door, tens of thousands of men and women, overflowing with joy, pass by like a lake. It was new news sending a clear wind over the globe. This news must be conveyed to the highlands. A clear wind must blow. (Eight lines omitted - original text)

The dream runs away in another direction. I want to dream in a more solid direction. I tirelessly brought together my comrades. It was a collection of all natural and social scientists in Korea. When I dreamed this dream, literacy had been disseminated to all Koreans, regardless of gender, so I devoted myself to disseminating science. As a result, factory chimneys stood tall in the cities, and electrification and mechanical power were disseminated in the countryside. In the position of semi-agriculture and semi-industry, we opened up markets in vast markets such as Shandong (山東) in China and Manchuria.

The dream runs away in another direction. I got married to a peerless beauty and returned to my hometown. And there was a farm that was neither too big nor too small. Behind it, the green mountains were covered with dense pine forests, and in front, clear streams flowed. In the garden, flowers of various colors bloomed in all four seasons, and fish leaped happily in the stream. In spring, peach blossoms and apricot blossoms were in full bloom, and the flowers fell in the gently blowing wind and flowed down the clear stream. In the water, the fish tried to swallow the sweet and fragrant flowers, and there were days when swallows from Gangnam (江南) came to pick the flowers in the fields. On long spring days, butterflies fluttered in the garden, mandarin ducks danced, and there were days when swallows chirped for a long time as if singing the leisure of spring on the rafters. In front of the yard, beautiful spring grass, orchids, and ganoderma lucidum were lush, and there were days when I whispered about the eternity of the universe and the impermanence of life, with my lovely wife half-rolling up the bead curtain and looking at the setting sun. There were also days when red apricot rain fell softly outside the window, and the five-watch bell shattered the lingering dream of the head. Then, in summer, on the blue barley field in front of the gate where the green shade was thick, swallow chicks flew long, and there were days when the pomegranate flowers in the South Garden bloomed red. Yellow plum rain fell outside the window, and there were days when the oriole sang in the shade of the zelkova tree. On long summer days, the slowly moving sunlight entered the south-facing veranda, and there were days when the lotus leaves covered the pond in front of the gate in blue. There were days when I strolled on the veranda and watched the rain scene when black clouds covered the sky and rain feet like hemp slanted against the front mountain. As the intention of full sky rain became more gloomy with the setting sun, there were days when a woodcutter played the flute and drove the cow back. Then, in autumn, there were nights when the autumn wind, I don't know where it came from, shook the full mountain grass and trees, making a rustling sound. There were also nights when the bright and clear <39> autumn moon rose high and the stream in front of the gate became strangely cold. There were nights when I couldn't sleep lying on my pillow, and I wondered whose heart the wild geese crying once or twice in the cold autumn frost and wind were cutting.
There were nights when I deeply felt the impermanence of life as the round moon rose brilliantly on the paulownia tree in front of the gate. There were days when the moon was bright and the autumn bugs cried sadly, and my wife scratched her head because the iron for the iron burned out as she ironed new clothes. Then, in winter, even though the sky was covered with wind and snow, there were nights when I brewed fragrant tea over a brazier in a warm room. There were quiet nights when the moonlight was bright and the north wind drove the snow, and a branch of plum shadows slanted on the desk. When the setting sun shone beautifully on the west window, there were days when I held my lovely wife and quietly asked if she loved me, and she touched the gold hairpin behind her neck with her slender hands and lowered her small head. As I lived like this, the years flowed like water, and when I died, would my tomb be in a sunny place with white sand and green pines? Would my wife wear mourning clothes and come and cry and sprinkle flowers and tears on my grave? Oh my, this dream is too romantic to be used.

Let's dream more realistically. I go to the farm and sow seeds with the skylark singing high in the sky in the spring, and fertilize the mulberry trees with my wife, and on the one hand, I raise silkworms. I work in the rice paddies in the spring and summer, and my face becomes like a black person in the blazing sun. "Let me eat my forehead sweat and live!" When I wake up in the morning, I give the chickens feed and play with the cows that give good milk. In the fall, the crops ripen in the rice paddies and the fruits ripen in the orchard. I gather this with joyful laughter and a full heart.

The dream runs away in another direction. The green mountains are layered and the green waters are **. I was in some mountain village thatched house. A young boy in blue clothes drove a cow and passed by playing the flute. When I wake up in the morning, I listen to the clear air and the sound of birds, and at night, I listen to the sad sound of the cuckoo crying in the empty mountain moonlight. And when I die, only wild grass and the indifferent wind and rain will be scattered on a piece of North Mang Mountain (北邙山一塊)! The passerby basking in the sunset - who would want to know the owner of this tomb? Worldly affairs are all flowing water and empty clouds!

Heo heo - this dream is leaning towards "nihilism (늬희리슴)" again. But is there anything in human affairs that does not return to nothingness? It is only that a minuscule life in the infinite universe is leafing through the books of philosophers in an attempt to gain some cooperation.
Youths with rosy cheeks and beautiful hair! How splendid dreams of happiness you dream! How you fight, covered in blood, to realize this dream! How you deal with each other with resentment, curse, and hatred!
On some youthful day, I was on a deserted island, and the moonlit sea was shining brightly, and I could not sleep because of the loud sound of the waves that no one was watching! On some youthful day, I slept in a deep mountain hermitage, and at dawn, I heard the old monk knocking on the wooden bell and drawing out the mournful sound for a long time,
"Three thousand six thousand worlds... countless beings..." and I cried as I listened to the sutra chanting! That's right - how many dreams of youth have countless beings had since the history of mankind <40>! (End) <41>

청춘의 꿈, 닷는 꿈
						柳光烈
청춘의 꿈을 적어라! 그것은 질거운 일인 동시에 또한 슬푼 일이다. 몬지가 하눌을 덥고 악마의 호리 바람이 핑핑 도는 어둠 컴컴하고 음산한 이 세상도 청춘에는 밝은 날 빗알에 꼿핀 동산 가티 아름다워 보히는 것이다. 『소태』 가티 쓴 세상의 맛도 맛보기 전에는 감미도는 과실 가티 아름다워 보히는 것이다. 이것은 희망만 잇고 실망이라는 것을 모르며 가능만 알고 불가능이라는 것을 모르기 때문이다.
청춘! 얼마나 희망의 찬 말이냐. 인생의 모든 행로는 여긔서 『스타트』를 시작하는 것이다. 동시에 화려한 꿈도 만흔 것이다. 이제 나는 지나간 과거의 청춘의 꿈을 하나씩 다시 불러이르키어 이 글을 적으려 한다. 이제 내 나히 삼 십이 되엇스니 아즉도 청춘이 아닌 것은 아니다. 그러나 내가 여긔에 적는 청춘의 꿈을 다시 꾸어 볼 만콤 맘의 순진과 평화를 일흔 것이다.

나는 어려서 鄕村에서 자랏기 때문에 나의 꿈은 都會의 전등불 알에서 꾼 것이 아니다. 樹林이 무성한 산에서 逍遙하면서 끗업는 꿈에서 붓잡히엇섯다. 그 때는 나히 칠팔 세의 어린 때이라 이러타 할 목표를 세운 것이 아니라 그저 부모가 말슴하시든 바 偉人이 되려 하엿던 것이다. 이 세상에 웃뚝한 사람이 되려 하엿든 것이다. 그리고 속으로 『내가 유명한 사람이 되어-모든 사람이 나를 칭찬하게 되어-그러면 나는 이러케 이러케 대답하리라 하는 것을 自問自答하엿섯다. 이 소래를 너무 내가 길게.』 속살 거리다가 그 소래가 누의의 귀에 들리어 누의에게 『너는 무엇을 그러케 속살 속살하니.』하고 물어서 나는 『아니.』하고 부정하면서도 나의 가슴을 꾀둘려 보힌 것가티 붓그러웟섯다.<37>
차차 자라면서도 이 위인이 되겟다는 꿈은 그대로 계속하엿다. 다만 윤곽이 분명치 못하던 위인의 대상이 차차 역사상에 나타난 그들을 목표로 꿈꾸게 되엇다. 그리고 십 삼사 세가 되어 性에 대한 눈을 차차 뜬 후에는 세상에 有數한 미인과 한 번 일생의 伴侶를 지어 보겟다는 꿈도 꾸엇섯다. 그러나 이 여러가지 꿈들은 역시 꿈이엇다. 만흔 현실에 다닥칠 때마다 나의 생각은 모다 일종의 화려한 공상에 지나지 못하던 것을 알앗다.
나는 아즉도 청춘이 남앗다. 지금도 이러한 꿈을 꾸고 잇는지 모르겟다. 녯 사람의 글에 『인생이 都是一場春夢이라.』는 말이 잇다. 나는 역시 길고 긴 인생의 꿈을 꾸고 잇는 지 모르겟다. 맑스는 우리에게 모든 이론을 『셀푸에비렌스』에서 출발하라 하엿다. 그러나 그 셀푸에비덴스도 알기 어려운 일이다. 이제 내가 『펜』을 잡고 이 글을 쓰는 것은 『白明』한 사실인지 모르겟다. 그러나 그 역시 꿈인지도 모르겟다.

나는 다시 꿈을 꾸려한다. 나는 어떤 공업 학교를 졸업한 후 조선인에게 행복을 주겟다는 생각으로 시험관을 붓잡고 잇섯다. 그러나 매월 드는 시험재료는 거액의 돈이 드는 것이다. 나는 나의 생활비와 시험비에 헤어나지 못하야 비탄을 하여 중지치 아니치 못할 때에 마츰 이를 동정하는 사람이 잇섯다. 그래서 그 사람의 재력으로 연구가 완성되어 가지고 어떤 제조 공업을 이르키엇다. 그것은 평범한 재료를 가지고 어떤 화학적 가공을 하면 세계 시장에서 요구하는 훌륭한 물품이 생산되는 것이다. 이것을 거대한 그 공장을 만들고 대규모로 생산하야 거대한 이익을 어덧다. 이에서 들어온 수입으로 저축한 돈이 몃해 동안 2천만 원이상이 되엇다. 이 돈의 반분을 띄이어 朝鮮民立大學을 세우고 세계의 有數한 학자를 초빙하야 조선의 청년인재를 양성하고 한편으로는 화학 공업 연구소를 두어 날로 이에 대한 연구를 하고 여러 학자의 연구 결과로 조선의 제조 공업은 왕성하여지며 또 한편으로는 해외 유학생을 파견하야 동서문명의 長處를 흡수한다.

꿈은 다시 별다른 방향으로 다라난다. 나는 근소한 路費를 가지고 南洋 어느 熱帶地方에 가서 떠러젓다. 한참동안은 망연자실 하엿스나 용기를 내어 가지고 어떤 서양인의 농장에서 고용을 살게 되엇다. 몃해 동안 근검으로 수중에는 多少의 저축이 잇게 되엇다. 그것들 가지고 소규모의 농장을 시작하야 『카피』와 기타 동양 각 시장에서 필요한 것을 심엇다. 그 농장은 차차 확대되어 방대한 농장이 되엇다. 다수한 노동자를 두고 생산을 하야 香港 上海 등 각 항구로 보내엇다. 이리하야 3백만 원 이상의 富를 어덧다. 그 돈을 가지고 滿洲로 와서 滿洲에 잇는 우리 동포와 함께 조선사람의 행복의 길을 차지려 하엿다.

꿈은 다시 다른 데로 달려간다. 광막한 滿洲평야에 넘어가는 紅日을 보며 나는 한참동안 비탄하엿다. 그러나 어찌 인생의 행로가 비탄에서 긋치랴. 나는 나의 정성이 닷는 데까지 우리 동포를 차저 다니며 이약이 하야 분산된 인심을 收拾하며 먼저 경제적<38>기초를 세우기에 급급하엿다. 그리고 우리 동포의 뜻을 모아 中國官憲에게 교섭하야 먼저 安堵할 수 잇게 한후 각 방면으로 실력을 양성하엿다. 그리고 조선 사람의 행복을 도모하려 하엿다.

꿈은 다시 다른 방면으로 다라난다. 나는 어떤 나라 저자에 어떤 대학에 들엇다. 날마다 새로운 진리를 배우다가 피곤할 때이면 황막한 故園을 생각하며 창에 의지하여 감개가 자못 깁헛섯다. 무슨 말인지 문 밧게는 환희에 넘치는 수만 남녀 군중이 호수 가치 지나간다. 그것은 지구우에 맑은 바람을 보내는 새 소식이엇다. 고원에도 이 소식을 전하여야 겟다. 맑은 바람이 불어야 겟다.(八行略-원문)

꿈은 다른 방향으로 다라난다. 나는 좀더 착실한 방면에 꿈을 꾸려한다. 나는 불철주야하야 동지를 紏合하엿다. 그것은 조선의 모든 자연 사회의 과학자를 모흔 것이엇다. 이 꿈을 꿀 때에 조선인 전체에는 남녀를 물론하고 문자 보급이 되엇슴으로 과학 보급에 전력하엿다. 이리하여 도시에는 공장의 烟突이 林立하고 농촌에는 電化와 기계력이 보급되엇다. 半農半工의 지위로서 中國 山東 滿洲 등 광대한 시장에 판로를 열엇다.
