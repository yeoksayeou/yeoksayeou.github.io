Once upon a time, in a place called Milano in Italy, there was a lecherous young soldier (軍人) named Grald, who was exceptionally trustworthy in financial matters and never missed a payment deadline.

Therefore, whenever he needed money, many merchants trusted him and provided it as needed.

Among these merchants was a wealthy one named Gaspare, who had a beautiful mistress (情婦).

Grald had always desired this woman and, seizing an opportunity, approached her. Contrary to his expectations, she readily agreed on the second proposal. However, being greedy, she demanded 200 gold coins as the price for her favor.

Grald hesitated to abandon the plan because of this audacious woman's request, but then he decided:

"No, I'll play a trick on this shameless woman who only knows money."

Grald calmly agreed to her demand.

Blinded by lust and greed, the woman never suspected the hidden scheme and promised:

"Soon my master will be leaving for Genoa for business, so let's meet then."

After the promise, Grald immediately went to Gaspare and pleaded, "I urgently need 200 gold coins, please lend them to me." Because he had always shown exceptional creditworthiness in financial matters, Gaspare readily agreed and provided the requested gold coins.

Twenty-three days later—after the master had left for Genoa—the woman immediately sent a messenger to Grald, saying:

"Bring the promised gold coins."

She made thorough preparations in a detached building in the middle of a beautiful garden and waited for Grald.

As promised, Grald arrived. However, strangely, he brought an unknown man with him, which made the woman frown in puzzlement.

But the sly Grald pretended not to notice and said:

"This is my closest friend, and we happened to meet in front of your house. He admired your garden and asked to see it, so I brought him along," he introduced the man, then took out a wallet from his pocket and handed 200 gold coins to the woman.

"Well, take this money. And when Gaspare returns, please give it to him directly."

The woman found Grald's words a bit strange, but she assumed he was just being cautious because of the man he had brought along.

"Very well. I have received the 200 gold coins. I will certainly give it to him when the master returns."

Grald said nothing more and, after touring the garden with his friend that day, he returned. From the next day, he visited the woman whenever he had the chance.

One day, when Grald visited the woman, Gaspare had returned. According to his plan:

"Ah, Mr. Gaspare, thank you so much for the other day. I suddenly asked, but you trusted me so much and lent me the money. Fortunately, the problem was resolved without the money, so I came to return it without even touching it, but you had already left on business, so I entrusted it to your wife. Anyway, I am endlessly grateful for your kindness in helping me with my urgent matter, whether the money was used or not."

Hearing these unexpected words, Gaspare's ears perked up, and he looked at the woman sitting next to him and scolded:

"Why haven't you told me anything about this until now? Your behavior has been suspicious lately..."

The woman was stunned by this sudden remark and didn't know what to do. Since there was a witness when she received the money, she couldn't deny the truth, fearing what Grald might say next.

"Oh... that money? I certainly received it. But I just forgot to tell you!"

Gaspare, who knew nothing, apologized to his guest for his wife's carelessness.

Thus, Grald triumphantly returned home. - End -

Mistress and Goose (情婦와 鵞鳥)

Once upon a time, in the city of Cairo in Egypt, there lived a naive man who believed his extremely lewd wife (게집) to be the most virtuous woman in the world.

This man kept two plump geese (鵞鳥) in his house. One day, while the husband was out, his mistress (情夫), who had come to visit the woman, looked at the geese playing in the yard and murmured:

"Those look deliciously plump. I wish I could eat them cooked."

The geese were precious to the homeowner, who fed them himself every morning and evening, so the woman hesitated slightly.

However, she wouldn't hesitate in front of her most beloved lover. The shameless woman readily agreed.

"Is that so? It's not difficult at all. I will fulfill your wish by tomorrow."

The woman confidently replied and sent her lover away. That night, she opened her mouth while having dinner with her husband, who had returned from his outing.

"Why are you so stingy?"

"What are you talking about all of a sudden?"

"What do you mean, what am I talking about? Think about it. Since you brought me here as your wife (녀편네), have you ever invited guests and treated them to anything?"

"Well, not really, but does that make me stingy?"

"Hmph, you might think so, but do you think others think that way?"

"Alright! Is it that difficult? Let's invite guests right away tomorrow."

"Oh, thank you so much! That would be great! Well then, let's catch the geese we raise, and you go to the market and buy other ingredients. I'll make delicious dishes and show off my skills..."

The couple agreed.

The next morning!

The husband got up and caught the two geese he had carefully raised with his own hands and went to the market to buy ingredients.

Meanwhile, the woman immediately cooked the geese that her husband had caught and sent them to her lover, who arrived at the appointed time.

Unaware of this, the husband bought everything he needed at the market and came home with a friend.

The woman immediately said to her husband:

"Why did you only bring one guest? How can I show off the dishes I'm making?"

The husband thought she was right.

"That's true. Then I'll go and bring one more person."

The husband went back out.

The woman took the opportunity to run to the guest room, found the guest, and said breathlessly:

"I'm very sorry to be rude, but you've been deceived by my husband. He said he invited you for lunch today, but actually, he intends to..."

She deliberately hesitated without finishing her sentence. The guest was startled and jumped up, asking, "Yes, what are you going to do to me?"

The woman looked around cautiously and whispered:

"Actually, the purpose of inviting you today is not for lunch, but to knock you unconscious with anesthesia and carry out some terrible plan."

Hearing this, the guest's face turned pale, and without a word, he ran out of the house and down the street. However, as if in exchange, the husband arrived with another friend. As soon as the woman saw her husband, she ran out with a red face and complained:

"What kind of person are you? Didn't you have any other people to bring, so you brought a thief (도적놈) as a guest? ... I'm so dumbfounded that I can't even speak!"

"What are you talking about again?"

"What do you mean, what am I talking about? He stole the goose dishes I worked so hard to make and ran away. Oh dear... look over there, see if I'm lying!"

The husband was even more surprised by her words and looked in the direction the woman was pointing. He saw the friend he had brought running away without looking back. He was so dumbfounded that he chased after him, shouting:

"Hey, wait a minute! We don't need two now, so leave at least one!"

The fleeing friend:

"You idiot, I only have one life, did you think I had two!" - End -
