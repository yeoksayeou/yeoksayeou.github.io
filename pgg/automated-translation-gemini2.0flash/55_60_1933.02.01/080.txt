Informal Essay, The Appearance of Publicly Established Cafes
						Sŏk Mun-ryang

Recently, "cafés" have sprung up too much, and due to "ero-service," scandalous problems have been constantly arising here and there. As a result, the authorities have been deeply contemplating and researching, and what they have come up with is the "publicly established café."

Thus, a signboard as formidable as a police station or county office, "Publicly Established Hanyang Café (公設漢陽카페-)," was proudly erected on a corner of Chongno intersection, the busiest area in Seoul.

Three café enthusiasts, their eyes wide with this ingenious invention, were the first to step into this "café."

Door: "Hey, hey, what are you here for?"

A: "What? What am I here for... Hey—I guess the system is a bit different here... Well—I came to buy some coffee."

Door: "Then take this door pass (門鑑) and go to the reception (受付) to verify your identity."

B: "This is quite bothersome."

Reaching the reception, there was a female clerk sitting there.

C: "Excuse me—we came to buy some coffee..."

Female: "Did you come alone?"

C: "No. There are three of us."

Female: "Then hand over the door pass (門鑑). And fill out this application form (新入書) in detail, writing your address, name, occupation, age, etc., and affix your seal."

A: "This is too much. Affixing a seal just to have a cup of coffee... But I don't have a seal, so what should I do?"

Female: "If you don't have a seal, you'll have to put your fingerprint."

B: "Fingerprint! This is just like being led to a public trial... Well, is it okay to stamp it like this?"

Female: "Are you only going to have coffee?"

C: "Well, what should we do? After going through all these procedures, it would be a bit unfair just to have coffee... Ah, I'll just have some whiskey out of spite."

A, B: "Then we'll do that too..."

Female: "However, our rule here is that we don't sell more than three glasses of whiskey per person. So, please keep that in mind when you order. Now, please write down everything you're going to have."

C: "You want me to write it again?... Well, I've written it all down..."

Female: "Good. But are all of you in good health?"

A: "None of us are sick."

Female: "If there's even a slight problem with your health, you must have a physical examination first."

C: "This is just like an entrance exam."

Female: "You don't have any pistols or other weapons, do you?"

B: "Hey, we're not gangsters, so you don't have to worry about that."

Female: "Then take this and turn left; there's a service counter (給品所) reception (受付) there, so hand it over there."

A: "This is truly an elaborate procedure... At this rate, will we even be able to have a cup of coffee today?... Well... let's just go where they told us to go. Okay, this seems to be the place... Oh, there's a female clerk sitting here too... Hey, here you go."

Female: "Yes... But are you all the same people as written here?"

B: "That's right. We're all the people written here."

Female: "Are you sure?"

C: "Who would be crazy enough to come here as someone else when we're drinking whiskey? Just give us something to drink quickly."

Female: "Alright—then go into that room. After that, if you drink coffee there, we'll call you one by one, so please come out then."

A: "Then where do we drink the whiskey?"

Female: "Whiskey will be served separately, one person at a time, in this room."

B: "So, we can't even drink together..."

Female: "Yes. That's absolutely not allowed. It's easy to get excited if two or more people drink alcohol together, and mistakes can easily happen, so according to our rules here, we have to drink separately!"

Female: "Oh dear... but this is a bit too much!"

Female: "Now, I'll call out your names, so please wait in that smoking room.... Mr. Kim Polo (金保羅氏) from An'guk-tong! Mr. Kim Polo (金保羅氏)!"

B: "Hey, Kim (金) is calling you."

C: "Yes. I'm Mr. Kim Polo (金保羅氏)."

Female: "You're Mr. Kim Polo (金保羅氏)?"

C: "Yes. That's right."

Female: "Then why didn't you answer the first time I called you and made me call you two or three times? Perhaps your ears are a bit bad. Should I refer you to the otolaryngology department (耳鼻科)?"

C: "Not at all, my ears are perfectly fine."

Female: "I'll let you have whiskey, so come this way."

As C entered the room he was guided to, the door closed from the outside, and the metal clicked shut.

C: "Uh oh, this looks like they're not giving me alcohol but locking me in a cell... ..."

At that moment, a waitress (女給) came in with whiskey and snacks and poured the drink.

C: "Alright, alright, that's more like it; a café is still a café after all... But hey, why is this whiskey so weak? It looks like they added water!"

Waitress: "No, we didn't add water; we just slightly reduced the alcohol content so that it's harmless to your body."

C: "So this is 3 won for three glasses... Okay, that's fine, but miss, let's shake hands."

Waitress: "That is absolutely not allowed according to our rules here."

C: "What's wrong with bending those rules a little?... Are you afraid you won't get a tip?"

Just as he was about to grab the waitress's wrist, a police officer, who had entered unnoticed, stood behind him, glaring.

Officer: "No, no, you can't engage in such behavior that disrupts public morals here. Quickly drink your pre-ordered alcohol and leave..."

C: "Yes, yes, yes."

Officer: "I'll forgive you because this is the first time, but if you do that again, I'll immediately put you in detention."

C: "Yes, yes, you're absolutely right... Ah ah, this is truly the first time in my life that I've drunk alcohol under the watchful eye of a police officer!"

Thus, after the group of three had all undergone such indignities, as they were about to leave through the gate, the gatekeeper who was there asked,

"What did you have?"

C: "Don't even mention it. I drank three glasses of whiskey, but it was so weak that I came out as sober as can be."

Gatekeeper: "Ha, that's the highest regulation in this café; there's no way you'd get drunk."

Everyone, what do you think? What if a café like this (though it may be established before long) were to appear? That's what I'm saying. <19>

漫談, 公設 카페 出現
						石文亮
최근에 와서 『카페-』가 너무 만히 생기여 『에로. 써비쓰』 때문에 이곳 저곳에서 풍긔상 좃치 못한 문제가 속출하는 고로 당국에서 여러 가지로 고심 연구한 결과 안출해 낸 것이 『公設카페-』이다.
이리하야 맛치 경찰서나 군청의 간판가치 무시무시한 『公設漢陽카페-』의 간판이 서울에서도 제일 복잡한 종로 네거리 한 모퉁이에 당당히 걸리게 되였다.
이 긔발한 안출에 눈이 둥그러진 카페-광 세사람이 제일착으로 이 『카페-』에 발을 드려노앗다.
門 『여보 여보 무엇하러 왓소』
A 『뭐? 무엇하러 왓느냐고... 얘- 여긴 식이 좀 다른가보다 ... 네- 커피를 좀 사먹으려고 왓소 』
門 『그럼 여기 이문감(門鑑)을 가지고 수부(受付)에 가서 신임을 하시요』
B 『이건 꾀 싯그럽구나』
수부에까지 이르러 보니까 그곳에는 녀사무원이 안저 잇다.
C 『저- 커피차를 좀 사먹으러 왓는데 ...』
女 『혼자 오섯습니까?』
C 『아니요. 셋이 왓소』
女 『그럼 그 문감을 이래 내노시요. 그리고 여기 잇는 이 신입서에 일일히 주소 성명과 직업 연령 등을 자세히 쓰고 도장을 찍으시요.』
A 『이건 너무 지독하구료. 커피 한잔 먹는데 도장까지 찍고 ... 그런데 도장을 안가젓스니 어떠케 하려오.』
女 『도장이 업거던 지장이라도 찍어야 합니다.』
B 『지장! 이건 맛치 공판의 인도와 갓수려 ... 자아 이렇케 찍으면 되오』
女 『잡숫는 것은 커피 차 뿐입니까?』
C 『글세, 어터케 할까 이러케 만흔 수속을 하고 커피차 만으로는 좀 억울하고 ... 에라 나는 홧김에 휘스키나 좀 먹으려네』
AB 『그럼 우리도 그렇게 하지 ...』
女 『그런데 휘스키는 한사람 압헤 석잔 이상은 팔지 안는것이 이곳의 규측입니다. 그러니 미리 그러케 짐작하시고 잡숴야 합니다. 자아 그럼 여기다 잡술 것을 일일히 적으십시요.』
C 『아니 또 적어요. ... 자아 이러면 됏소 ...』
女 『좃습니다. 그런데 당신들은 모다 몸이 건강하십니까?』
A 『병자라고는 한사람도 업소』
女 『만약에 조곰이라도 몸에 고장이 잇는 분은 먼저 신체검사를 하지 안으면 안되겟는데요.』
C 『이건 바로 입학시험 갓구려』
女 『피스톨움´나 기타 흉기가튼 것은 안가지섯겟지요.』
B 『여보 우리는 『깽』이 아니니 그런 염려는 안해도 좃소』
女 『그럼 이것을 가지고 거기서 왼쪽으로 꼬부라지면 급품소(給品所)의 수부가 잇슬테니 거기다 내노시요.』
A 『이건 참 굉장한 수속일세 ... 이거 이러다가 오늘 안으로 어듸 커피 차나 한잔 먹고 가겟나 ... 엥 ... 하여간 일너준 데로 가볼밧게. 올치 여긴 모양인데 ... 아니 여기도 녀사무원이 안저 잇네 그려 ... 여보 자아 엣소』

B 『그럿소. 모두 본인들이요』
女 『틀림 업습니까』
C 『휘스키 먹는데 대신 올 밋친놈이 어대 잇단 말요. 얼는 물이나 먹게 해주시요.』
女 『자아- 그럼 그 방으로 들어가십시요. 그리하야 그곳에서 커피를 잡수시면 한사람씩 부를 터이니 그때 나와 주시요.』
A 『아니 그럼 휘스키는 어대서 먹는단 말요』
女 『휘스키는 이쪽 방에서 한사람씩 따로따로 불너서 공급합니다.』
B 『아니 세사람이 한꺼번에 먹지도 안쿠 ...』
女 『녜. 그것은 절대 안됩니다. 두사람 이상이 알콜을 가티 마시면 흥분되기도 쉬웁고, 또 간혹 실수가튼 것도 생기기 쉬운 까닭에 이곳 규측에 의하야 한사람씩 따로 마시도록 되여 잇습니다!』
女 『오라 딴은 ... 그러나 좀 심한걸!』
女 『자아 지금부터 이름을 부를터이니 그쪽 흡연실에서 기다려＜18＞ 주십시요. ... 안국동 사시는 金保羅氏! 金保羅氏!』
B 『여보게 金군 부르네.』
C 『녜. 金保羅氏는 나요.』
女 『당신이 金保羅氏요.』
C 『네. 그럿소.』

C 『온 천만에 귀가 낫블니가 잇소』
女 『휘스키를 잡숫도록 할터이니 이리오시요.』
C가 안내하는 방으로 드러가자 방문이 밧그로 닷처지며 덜그럭 하고 쇠가 채워젓다.
