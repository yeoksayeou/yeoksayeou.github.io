**City Slicker and Country Bumpkin**
By Pak Tal-sŏng

**Time:** A night in Mid-Autumn
**Place:** The men's quarters (사랑방 - Sarangbang) of a country farmhouse
**Characters:** Yun Tusŏp, a city guest (京客 - Kyŏnggaek) around 30 years old, Kim Sŏngch'il, the master of the farmhouse, and 1 or 2 other villagers (村夫 - Ch'onbu)

**First Night**

**Master:** "Being the countryside, the lodging and food etiquette are inconvenient, what are you going to do? Please forgive us. This is how we live day and night in the country. It's dirty, but please sit on this cushion." (Sweeps the floor with a broom and takes down a worn-out red cotton cushion from the shelf and lays it down.)
**City Guest:** "It's quite alright. Please don't trouble yourself. It's not as if I don't know the country situation." (Extinguishes the cigarette he was smoking, most genteelly.)
**Master:** "How is the taste of country Soju (소주)? Quite rough, isn't it? How is it compared to Seoul's Yakju (약주 - refined rice wine)?"
**City Guest:** "Since the basic ingredients are coarse, how could it compare to Yakju? But it's fine. I'm naturally a heavy drinker, haha."
**Master:** "Please tell us some stories about Seoul. They say Seoul is very nice. About how many tens of thousands of households are there?"
**City Guest:** "About fifty thousand households, I suppose."
**Master:** "Gosh, that's quite a lot. What do all those households do to make a living? There aren't any farming households, right...? They say Seoul people all just play and eat."
**City Guest:** "Just because they are Seoul people, can they all just play and eat? Ten million met... [He trails off]"
**Master:** "Then what do they do to live? Those 50,000 households... Perhaps because our perspective is narrow, but we think everyone would starve without farming."
**City Guest:** "Are farming occupations the only way people make a living? There's commerce, industry, salaried life, labor, all sorts <50> of things, aren't there? Seoul people work even more than country folk, except for the rich..." (Another top-knotted country gentleman enters. Following him, a young man around 20 with a high-collar [haik'ara - ハイカラ] hairstyle also enters.)
**Master:** "Please greet each other. This gentleman is Supervisor Yun (윤주사 - Yun Chusa) who came from Seoul."
**Villager 1:** "Ah, is that so? Welcome. I am Yi Haksu, who lives in this village." (Bows his waist in a half-bow.)
**City Guest:** "Is that so. I am Yun Tusŏp." (Puffing out his chest with a slightly arrogant attitude.)
**Villager 2:** "I am Kim Yongju, living in this village. Welcome." (Appears a bit arrogant compared to the other villager.)
**City Guest:** "Yes. Hello. You must know my name."
**Master:** "We were just listening to stories about Seoul. How often do we get to host a gentleman from Seoul in a remote place like this? Let's listen our fill to Seoul stories. It might be tiring for you, but..."
**Villager 1:** "I heard you arrived earlier, but being a country person, I was busy with work and shy, so I only came to see you now. Please tell us many stories about Seoul."
**City Guest:** "What special stories could there be? Ask whatever you like. I'll answer what I know."
**Villager 1:** "We're country bumpkins, what do we know to ask? Please just tell us whatever you know."
**City Guest:** "... ..." (Thinking, "What story should I tell?" He just puffs away at his cigarette.)
**Villager 1:** "Just how many ri (里) wide is the capital, Seoul? It's quite large, isn't it?"
**City Guest:** "Large indeed. It's about a 30-ri circumference."
**Villager 1:** "They say there's nothing you don't buy in Seoul. They even buy water to use, is that right?"
**City Guest:** "Absolutely. Is it just water? They even buy every single thing down to a fire poker stick or a piece of cleaning rag."
**Villager 1:** "Goodness, how do they buy all that...? We wouldn't last a day. To live in Seoul, you must need a lot of money. How much does it cost per month?"
**City Guest:** "Well, there's no set amount. It differs depending on the household management. If the family is small, it costs less; if the family is large, it costs more. And then there's the high-collar [haik'ara] lifestyle and the frugal lifestyle..." (As if thinking, "He asks all sorts of things." As if the level of questions from the country folk is bothersome, appearing somewhat annoyed, showing an increasingly condescending attitude.)
**Villager 1:** "Then what do all those people do to make a living?"
**Master:** "That's what I asked earlier too. What do they do to live? It seems like people like us couldn't survive even a day... They must all be like ghosts..."
**Villager 2:** "Uncle, really... What do they do for a living? They live by sucking off country people. It might be rude to say this with a guest from Seoul present, but Seoul people, simply put, live by skinning the backs of country bumpkins." (His face turns red, speaking strongly with a trembling voice.)<51>
**Master:** "Hey, what kind of talk is that? Do you think all Seoul gentlemen are like that? Perhaps the gentry who held official posts like governors or county magistrates during the old days of power were like that." (Looks at the Seoul guest as if asking forgiveness for the villager's rude words.)
**City Guest:** "This gentleman's words have some truth to them. City dwellers generally end up consuming what rural people produce. In return, things also come from the city to the countryside. It's reciprocal. Supply and demand are balanced. This gentleman seems to have had some trouble with Seoul people, haha."
**Villager 2:** "What connection would a country bumpkin have with Seoul to have trouble or not? It just seems plausible when I think about it."
**Villager 1:** "This fellow brings up unrelated things and causes trouble. Let's quickly hear another story. (Lifts the corner of his mat, spits forcefully, then looks at the Seoul guest) By the way, in Seoul, there's no coarse grain rice [조밥 - chobap], right? They say they only eat meat side dishes at every meal."
**City Guest:** "... ..." (Frowns as if thinking, "He asks all sorts of trivial things," and just smokes his cigarette intensely.)
**Villager 1:** "In Seoul, they say the person in the back house doesn't know the person in the front house, and the person in the front house doesn't know the person in the back house. Is that true?"
**Villager 2:** "Uncle, you ask all sorts of things. That's true even if you go to our small town center, which is the size of a palm. Do townspeople all know each other? Let's hear about current affairs. (Clears his throat with an 'ehem') By the way, has Chang Tso-lin (張作霖) become President? What in the world is happening with those guys... ..."
**City Guest:** "I saw talk of President Chang Tso-lin in the newspaper, but it seems he hasn't become president yet."
(10 lines deleted here)
**Master:** "Hey, this gentleman left Seoul a month ago, how would he know if you ask such things? He's just like us..."
**Villager 2:** "Well, since he travels widely, wouldn't he know better than us? I was just so curious."
**City Guest:** "How much is a kama (가마 - sack) of rice in this village now?" (Abruptly changes the subject)
**Villager 1:** "At the last market day, they said one mal (말 - unit of volume) of top-grade rice was 4 Won 10 Chŏn. The price of rice seems to be rising a bit."
**City Guest:** "No, rice prices usually fall in autumn, why would they be rising?"
**Villager 1:** "It always rises around this time. Since the new grain hasn't come out much yet... it needs a little more time..."
**City Guest:** "A bundle of firewood..."
**Master:** "A bundle of firewood seems to be 80 Chŏn. For one bundle of eight smaller bunches..."
**City Guest:** "Gosh, that's cheap. How big are the firewood bunches?"
**Villager 1:** "About the size of a large soybean stalk bundle... In Seoul, they call something inexpensive 'ssada' (싸다 - cheap), right?"
**Villager 2:** "That's what they say. They call inexpensive things 'ssada' and expensive things 'pissada'<52> (비싸다 - expensive), and going to buy something 'p'allŏ kanda' (팔러 간다 - going to sell).. Good grief, strange folk..." (This person resorts to curses at the drop of a hat.)
**City Guest:** "Anyway, 80 Chŏn for a bundle is cheap. In Seoul, it would cost at least 2 or 3 Won..."
**Villager 2:** "I started asking earlier but stopped... where in Kyŏngsang Province (慶尙道) is Pak Yŏl (朴烈) from?"
**City Guest:** "Sangju (尙州), they say?" (As if extremely annoyed, as if wishing they would leave soon, forcefully expressing fatigue with a reluctant answer.)
**Villager 2:** "But..."
**City Guest:** "I walked about 30 ri, so I'm quite tired. Ehem." (Fakes a yawn, showing signs he wants them to leave. The top-knotted country gentleman still doesn't get the hint, while the high-collar haired youth, though understanding the hint, deliberately acts mischievous. Just then, the table with drinks comes in.)

**Second Night**

**City Guest:** "If you'd like to try living in Seoul, why don't you? Roughly how much property do you own?" (In the kindest tone)
**Master:** "What property is there to speak of? Altogether, it might be worth about 10,000 Won." (At the words '10,000 Won', the Seoul guest's mouth waters.)
**City Guest:** "Gosh! 10,000 Won is substantial property indeed! With that, if you go to Seoul, you could live without doing anything, just playing and eating... I'll arrange things well for you. If you want to move to Seoul, you should. You could join the OO Club too... haven't you seen the newspapers lately?" (Moves closer to the master, sits right next to him, and whispers in his ear. Then moves back a step and sits.) "Since the thought has occurred to you, why not just decide? Isn't it frustrating for a person to live their whole life in a country corner like this and die old? You should taste city life too."
**Master:** "Well, that's what I was thinking too... If my family were to live in Seoul, how much would it cost per month?"
**City Guest:** "How many people are in your family?"
**Master:** "Well, altogether, there are only 5 or 6 people. My elderly mother, my wife, my three children, that's all."
**City Guest:** "Then 50 Won a month would be sufficient. There are people with families of over 10 members living on 10 or 20 Won..."
**Master:** "Really... how can I manage to draw out 50 Won to spend each month?"
**City Guest:** "Good grief, such a narrow-minded person!" (Feigning anger as if getting mad) "Why spend it? Doesn't money bear interest (색기 - saekki, lit. 'offspring')? With just 10,000 Won, you can keep the principal intact and just live by playing. Just do as I say."
**Master:** "How? No matter how I think about it, being a country bumpkin, it feels like I won't be able to live if I go to Seoul."
**City Guest:** "I've never seen such a frustrating fellow. Where is there a fool who can't live with money? Good grief, really! Just do as I say."
**Master:** "How? Please tell me."
**City Guest:** "You said you have about 10,000 Won worth of property, right?" (Sits up straight, quietly) <53> "Alright - let's say about 1,000 Won for arranging housing, moving expenses, and furniture. With 8,000 Won in cash, why couldn't you live? Even at just one percent monthly interest (일분변 - ilbunbyŏn), isn't that 80 Won a month? Even just depositing it in a bank or financial cooperative would yield enough for monthly living expenses. If you engage in money lending, or property speculation, or even just open a side dish store, couldn't you secure an income of 100 Won a month?"
**Master:** "Well, I'd have to try it to know."
**City Guest:** "Such a frustrating person! Did anyone learn how to do things while still in the womb? Just leave everything to me. I'll somehow make sure you can live..."
**Master:** "... ..." (Stares at a cockroach crawling on the plastered wall, thinking about something.)
**City Guest:** "Seoul is truly wonderful. You'll know once you live there... Streetcars, automobiles, rickshaws, bicycles - wherever you want to go, you don't get a speck of dirt on your feet. Telegraph, telephone, electric lights, gas, etc. - you can talk to someone 10 or 100 ri away while sitting down, and the night is brighter than day. You won't have to see those sorts of oil lamps before you die. There's a rice shop and firewood seller right outside your door, and a side dish shop and butcher shop right next door. If you just keep one clever servant (해랑이 - haerangi), living is like being an immortal sage. Occasionally take a walk to places like the Han River Railway Bridge (漢江鐵橋) or Ch'ŏngnyangni (淸凉里), maybe call out 'Hey you!' (誰也某也 - Suya Moya) to a friend you happen to meet and share a drink... If you live one life like that, that's enough. What more could you wish for? Hahaha..." (Praises Seoul until his mouth is dry to stir the master's heart.)
**Master:** (Smiling faintly) "Could someone like me really have the fortune to live playing like that..."
**City Guest:** "Gosh, what an impossible fellow... Then forget it. Who's forcing you? It's because you showed such interest, that's why! Humph." (Snaps dismissively.)
**Master:** "No, it's just that having only lived in the country, I worry I might be a bit... bewildered! ... Then I trust you completely, Supervisor Yun. Like an older brother (親兄님 - Ch'inhyŏngnim)."
**City Guest:** "What did I tell you? Just trust me. Is it just a little help? We'll be living together for life." (Pleased as if he succeeded, as if he caught a big fish and put it in his creel, overjoyed.)
**Master:** "You must be thirsty, have another drink... Hey! Eldest child!" (The City Guest lights a cigarette, the master opens the door and goes out.)

**Third Night**

**City Guest:** "Alright! Then I'll be heading up [to Seoul] tomorrow. So, first, try to get about 1,000 Won. I'll go first and make all the arrangements..."
**Master:** "How can I suddenly get 1,000 Won? In the country, I can only manage 100 or 200 Won. And I'd have to borrow that too."
**City Guest:** "Couldn't you manage about 500 Won? It needs to be around 500 Won..."
**Master:** "500 Won! That's difficult. Where is there money in the country... If I try hard, maybe about 300 Won might be possible..."<54>
**City Guest:** "Well then, we have no choice but to do what we can. You can get about 300 Won by tomorrow, right? I can take it with me, right?"
**Master:** "Well... Let me try to arrange it." (Blinks his eyes, calculating this way and that.)
**City Guest:** "Couldn't you manage about 50 Won more? I have something I need to use it for urgently... (Shrinks his neck as if finding it very awkward to bring up) I need to get travel expenses to go up too..."
**Master:** "Don't worry about travel expenses. You shouldn't say such things."
**City Guest:** "Travel expenses aside, I need to get a set of clothes. Being away from home is so tedious..." (Strokes his clothes smoothly.)
**Master:** "Indeed, your clothes have gotten a bit dirty. Let's arrange for you to get a set in town (邑 - Ŭp)."
**City Guest:** "Then tomorrow I'll head into town first. You arrange the money and come into town tomorrow evening or the morning after next. I'll go first, prepare the clothes, and send some letters to Seoul..."
**Master:** "Well. Let's try to do that. From the village, I can probably only get about 100 Won. It seems the rest will have to come from town."
**City Guest:** "Is there any trustworthy place in town?"
**Master:** "At the very least, couldn't I get 100 or 200 Won if I go to the financial cooperative? I don't know about big money, but..."
**City Guest:** "Then let's go together tomorrow morning."
**Master:** "No, no. Someone is supposed to come tomorrow... I'll go early the morning after next. If you're anxious, please go ahead first tomorrow morning."
**City Guest:** "Let's do that then. Then could you give me just a little bit, whatever amount? I don't have a single penny in hand..." (His face reddens, looking very awkward.)
**Master:** "Then shall I give you about 50 Won to take with you?"
**City Guest:** "If you would..."
**Master:** (Takes out a money pouch with a chain attached from his vest pocket, rummages through it, and takes out five 10-Won bills) "Here - take this. No need to worry about it in the morning."
**City Guest:** (As if overwhelmed with gratitude, kneels on one knee) "Yes, thank you. Then I will head out early tomorrow morning. Elder brother (형님 - hyŏngnim), please come the day after tomorrow." (As soon as he receives the money, he immediately calls him 'elder brother'.)
**Master:** "Shall we have another drink?"
**City Guest:** "Well. I drank too much last night..."
**Master:** "We need something to eat with it... shall I catch a chicken?"
**City Guest:** "Goodness, let's just drink with whatever we have. Why go through the trouble of preparing that now?"
**Master:** "Trouble? What trouble? It'll be quick." (Stands up and goes out. The city guest pulls over the wooden pillow, plops down, lies back, stares at the ceiling, and smiles faintly as if he has achieved a great success.)

**Fourth Night**
(The young man named Kim Yongju, who came on the first evening, enters.)<55>
**Kim:** "Uncle, are you here?"
**Master:** (Opening the door to the inner room) "Ah, nephew? Sit there. I'm eating."
(After a short while, he comes out holding a tobacco pipe.)
**Master:** "Have you had dinner?"
**Kim:** "Yes... Where did you go today?"
**Master:** "To Councilman Yi's house across the way..."
**Kim:** "Why there?"
**Master:** "I had some business to attend to." (His expression changes as if some secret is about to be exposed.)
**Kim:** "I hear you're having some other thoughts, uncle."
**Master:** "Other thoughts? What's that? What are you talking about?"
**Kim:** "No, there was talk that you're moving to Seoul. What is there to hide from family? Please tell me the truth." (Speaks somewhat forcefully, staring intently at the master's face.)
**Master:** "Who did you hear it from? Words have no feet yet travel a thousand ri (言無足而千里), news travels fast... It's true that I've been thinking about it."
**Kim:** "Uncle, you've been fooled by that Seoul fellow Yun! You're heading for big trouble! Don't you know he's a notoriously infamous vagrant (浮浪子 - purangja)? Uncle, even with eyes... do you know who that guy is? Old Man Kim (김선달 - Kim Sŏndal) in town, his son was ruined by that guy, and Min Usik (민우식) of Yanggwimyŏn (양귀면) was also deceived by him and lost 10,000 nyang (萬兩)! You didn't recognize a complete vagrant (알부랑자 - alburangja)..."
**Master:** (Eyes wide, startled, but still shaking his head as if not believing it) "No, how could that person be like that? He seemed like a very respectable person. Nephew, you must have mistaken him..."
**Kim:** "Good grief, listen to me! If you don't believe my words, go into town and ask Old Man Kim. Uncle, really! How can you be so uninformed?"
**Master:** (Still frowning as if not believing) "How could that person be like that? I firmly believed he was a respectable man... His speech and behavior, no matter how I looked, didn't seem like a vagrant's..."
**Kim:** "The more his speech and behavior seem respectable, the more likely he's a vagrant! Good grief, so frustrating! Anyway, let's go into town together tomorrow morning. Where did that fellow say he was going?"
**Master:** "He went into town."
**Kim:** "Did you give him some money?"
**Master:** "I gave him 50 Won."<56>
**Kim:** "Oh no! Good grief, really! Why give even a single penny to that guy? Feeding him, serving him drinks... oh, it makes me furious! Uncle, you've aged, but you've aged in vain. Not recognizing a thief! That guy has probably already skipped town far away (뺑손이를 첫겟소 - ppaengsonirŭl ch'ŏtkkesso)."
**Master:** (Eyes wide) "Are you absolutely sure? He really didn't seem like that kind of person..."
**Kim:** "Look, just come with me into town. Let's go right now, even in the middle of the night. Maybe that guy is hiding in town..."
**Master:** "... ..." (Clasps his hands and stares only at the ceiling.)
**Kim:** "Let's say that person is indeed smart and respectable. What is Seoul life for someone in your position, uncle? Seoul? It's the perfect place to ruin your family and yourself (敗家亡身 - p'aegamangsin). What's Seoul anyway? Seoul? Uncle, if you go to Seoul, what will you do for a living?"
**Master:** "... ..."
**Kim:** "Don't think about anything else, just stay firmly planted here in the countryside. Seoul? Even the so-called movers and shakers of the world find it hard to live in Seoul, so what is Seoul to a capable and good person like you? Like straw shoes finding their match (집신에는 제 날이 제격이라구 - chipsin-enŭn che nar-i chekyŏgiragu), the countryside is the right place for a country bumpkin... You just lost about 50 Won."
**Master:** "... ..."
**Kim:** "Get some sleep. Let's go together in the morning. I'm too angry to bear it." (Stands up and leaves. The Master lets out a deep sigh and taps his pipe.)<57>

**Summary**

This play script, "City Slicker and Country Bumpkin," depicts the interactions between Yun Tusŏp, a seemingly sophisticated man from Seoul, and Kim Sŏngch'il, a relatively prosperous but naive farmer, along with other villagers. Over several nights, Yun entices Kim with tales of Seoul's wonders and convinces him to sell his property and move to the city, offering to manage the transition. Yun manipulates Kim into giving him money upfront. However, Kim's sharp-witted nephew, Kim Yongju, reveals Yun is a known swindler who has ruined others, shattering Kim's dreams and preventing financial disaster, though not before Yun escapes with 50 Won.

From a historical perspective, the play highlights the stark urban-rural divide and economic anxieties in 1920s colonial Korea. It reflects the allure of Seoul as a modern metropolis contrasted with the perceived naivete and vulnerability of rural populations. The depiction of the city guest as potentially predatory, aiming to exploit the farmer's assets, touches upon themes of economic precariousness, the risks of migration and investment, and mistrust between city and country dwellers. Historians might find the portrayal of rural aspirations, the specific economic details (property value, living costs, interest rates, scams), and the social commentary on class, regional differences, and gullibility particularly interesting for understanding the social dynamics of the period. The references to contemporary figures like Chang Tso-lin and Pak Yŏl anchor it firmly in its time.
