**Body Text Translation:**
Each Person, Each Color (Part 5)
Yuktang Ch'oe Nam-sŏn

Speaking of the tastes (嗜好) of Mr. Yuktang Ch'oe Nam-sŏn (六堂 崔南善氏), they span various fields. Among books (書籍), he naturally likes old history books (古書籍), and he also has a great fondness (趣味) for *sijo* poems. Not only does he avidly read many good *sijo* by old masters (古人), but he also enjoys composing *sijo* himself, often writing them while on the road and muttering them like the rambling sounds of an old widow.

And while snacking is common for non-drinkers (不酒客), Yuktang is particularly fond of snacking compared to other non-drinkers, so things like *hottŏk*, roasted chestnuts, Japanese beans (왜콩), and broad beans (호콩) constantly pop out of his pockets. In other words, one could say his pockets are stuffed full with *sijo* manuscripts, *hottŏk*, fruit, and the like. Perhaps his gradually becoming plump is also thanks to his fondness for snacking.

Also, when he speaks, it is his custom (恒例) to first close his narrow eyes and smile. A characteristic feature of his writing is that it is always long, like the Yangtze River (萬里長江). In the old days, he used to wear *mit'uri* (straw sandals) and heard the nickname (別號) "Ch'oe Mit'uri," but now he is said to have switched to rubber shoes. <74>

**Summary**

This short piece profiles the personal habits and tastes of Ch'oe Nam-sŏn (pen name Yuktang), a prominent Korean intellectual. It highlights his love for historical texts and *sijo* poetry (both reading and writing), his particular fondness for various snacks which he carries in his pockets, his habit of smiling before speaking, his lengthy writing style, and a past anecdote about his footwear earning him the nickname "Ch'oe Mit'uri." From a historical perspective, the article offers a glimpse into the everyday life and personality of a major figure during the colonial period, humanizing him beyond his intellectual contributions. The mention of specific snacks and the shift from traditional *mit'uri* to modern rubber shoes reflect the changing material culture in Korea during the 1920s.

各人各色(其五)
육당 최남선
六堂 崔南善氏의 嗜好로 말하면 여러 방면이엿다. 書籍 중에도 역사의 古書籍을 조와함은 물론이고 또 시조에 多大한 취미를 가저서 古人의 조흔 시조를 만히 애독할 뿐 안이라 자기도 시조 짓기를 조와하야 路上에서 각금 시조를 지여가지고 늙은 과부의 니알는 소리 모양으로 웅얼웅얼한다. 그리고 군것질은 不酒客의 보통 잇는 짓이지만은 六堂은 다른 不酒客보다도 특히 군것질을 잘하야 그의 주머니 속에서는 항상 호떡, 군밤, 왜콩, 호콩 등 屬이 쏙쏙 나오것다. 다시 말하면 그의 주머니 속에는 시조원고에다 호떡, 果物등을 잔뜩 싸서 채워 잇다고 할 수 잇다. 그의 몸집이 漸漸 뚱뚱하야 가는것도 아마 그 군것질 잘 하는 덕인지도 알수 업다. 그리고 그는 말할 때 이면 가는 눈을 감고 먼저 웃는 것이 恒例이다. 그의 글은 萬里長江 모양으로 항상 긴 것이 한 특색이라 할 수 잇다. 녜전에는 미투리를 신고 다녀서 崔 미투리의 別號를 듯더니 지금은 고무신으로 밧귀엿다고.＜74＞
