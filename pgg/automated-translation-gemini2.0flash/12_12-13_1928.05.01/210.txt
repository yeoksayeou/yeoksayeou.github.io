별건곤 제12·13호
1928년 05월 01일
내가 자랑하고 십흔 朝鮮 것-家族制度는 世界에 模範 (Things About Korea I Want to Brag About - The Family System is a Model for the World)
崔誠愚 (Essay)

It seems quite out of step with today's thinking to boast about the family system at a time when it is fundamentally being denied. However, we even boast of historical things that disappeared thousands of years ago, so how can we completely deny a system that still exists today? Regardless of whether this system aligns with current ideas, when comparing it to the era when the family system was practiced in the past—or even today, some countries in the East that still use the system—the Korean family system can truly be called a model for the world. For example, we deeply and sincerely revere our ancestors, performing ancestral rites and protecting tombs even for ancestors from dozens or hundreds of generations ago. Also, towards those of the same clan (同族), there is a saying that "those of the same surname and origin are as close as relatives for a hundred generations (同姓同本은 百代之親)," and "those who were in the same house a hundred years ago (百年前 一室之人)" are especially loved and cherished like close brothers or uncles. And the genealogical system (族譜制度) of Koreans is a beautiful custom that is unmatched. The order and arrangement (昭穆) are so well-organized that the lineage is not disordered even for hundreds of generations, and when we say "So-and-so's family, so-and-so's clan (某家 某族)," everyone immediately knows each other well. This will be a great reference material for scholars and historians who study clans in later generations.

<47>

There are inevitable drawbacks to the end of it, but no system is absolutely perfect forever. Even the socialist system (社會主義制度) that the whole world resonates with and praises now will eventually have its problems and naturally decline. Even now, among those who advocate the same new ideology, the anarchists (無政府主義者) are already rejecting the socialist system, aren't they? With that in mind, there is no need to mention the side effects of any system. We can only talk about whether it fits or doesn't fit, whether it's good or bad, at the time when the system exists. Anyway, the family system of our Korea in the past was well-developed in every way and was therefore a model for the world.
