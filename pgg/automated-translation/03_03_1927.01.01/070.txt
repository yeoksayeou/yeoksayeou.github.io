발행일	1927년 01월 01일
기사제목	朝鮮에서 活動하는 海外에서 도라온 人物評判記, 어느 나라가 제일 잘 가르처 보냇는가? (Critical Biographies of People Returned from Overseas Active in Korea, Which Country Sends People Best Taught?)
필자	YYY
기사형태	소식 (News)

朝鮮에서 活動하는 海外에서 도라온 人物評判記, 어느 나라가 제일 잘 가르처 보냇는가?
						YYY
The new year is approaching, and the old year is going. "Oh! Time flies!" is too cliché a lament. There must be something to discuss. There is no freedom in everything, and society is too desolate. Can this be called a human society? Let's take a look at the year-end street scene. As I was about to dust myself off, get up, and put on my hat, troublemaker Mr. B rushed in, shaking his muddy shoes and making a fuss as he opened the door. "He must have had a drink somewhere! He looks excited..." I said to myself. Mr. B stepped forward and said,

"Where are you going? Stay here. I have something to tell you. Wait a minute."

He was flustered. I was bored too. Meeting troublemaker Mr. B was like finding a piece of meat for a hungry tiger. I smiled and said,

"What do you have to say? You must have visited a *tachinomi* (立ち飲み, standing bar) after getting some year-end manuscript fees."

Mr. B suddenly became serious and sincere, his face changing.

"I had a few drinks, but do you think of me as a drunkard? Anyway..."

He waved his hand. "Speaking frankly in my drunkenness,"

"Hey, why do Koreans never finish anything? Always *yongdusawi* (龍頭蛇尾, dragon head, snake tail)! Look at the so-called students who returned from overseas. Is there anything they do? When they first come back, they make a fuss about writing articles in newspapers or introducing something, but after a few days, they disappear without a trace. Is there anything special about studying abroad?" He suddenly brought up the story of foreign students. "Okay, let's do it," I said, grabbing Mr. B and deciding to listen to a general review of people who returned from overseas. Although I couldn't get detailed reviews of each person, I heard a general story about the people who returned from overseas.

Y Well, you can't blame just them. The whole social phenomenon is like that, so what can they do? At first, they don't know and only talk about where they used to be and try to do something, but when they actually do it, it's different from what they thought, so they naturally become disillusioned and give up. Do you think they want to do that? And to be honest, leaving aside foreign students, what about those who grew up in this country, know the situation here well, and gained knowledge here? It's the same in the end.

B Oh, come on! You have a lot of good feelings for foreign students! You should become a devout Christian and go to study in the United States with the church's recommendation! And your family has some wealth, so it would be best to go to study in the United States.

Y Why? Do you have a grudge against studying in the United States? If the church recommends it and I can afford it, I would go study anywhere, not just the United States...

B Hey, don't even talk about studying in the United States. What's the point of going there and seeing how well others live, calling it the Land of Freedom and Paradise on Earth? And if they had done something good, it would be nice, but they make a fuss about studying abroad and being professors at specialized schools, and then they cause women's problems and make a fuss in the world. Wasn't that all because of students who studied in the United States?

Y Huh! Why do you say that it was only students who studied in the United States and professors at specialized schools? It's just that those people were in such positions, so people talked about it. Why, is there no such thing elsewhere? Even Dr. Ishihara Jun (石原純博士), who was a professor at Tohoku University (東北大學) in Japan, had a love affair with Ms. Hara Asajo (原阿佐緖女史) and had to resign from his job. Even that shows that such problems exist everywhere among humans. You're seeing it through colored glasses, but even among students who studied in the United States, there are people like Sin Hŭng-u (申興雨氏), who has considerable trust in our youth community, right? But since you're talking about it, I'll say it. There seem to be the most students who study in the United States, but when they come back, no one knows what they're doing.

B That's right. Still, Sin Hŭng-u (申興雨氏) is, at any rate, a representative figure among students who studied in the United States. It's up to each person's opinion and situation to engage in religious affairs, so I can't necessarily criticize that. But anyway, he seems to be a sincere person who tries to do his best in everything from start to finish. What else is there to say? They just bow their heads in religious schools, thinking they have nothing to do with the world, singing songs of heaven and thinking that it's enough to secure a livelihood. What else do they do?

Y Well, there are some who have opened medical practices. There are also some who write.

B There are a few doctors. But they usually work at church-affiliated hospitals and rarely open their own practices. And don't even talk about writing. Anyone can write that kind of thing without studying in the United States.

Y Hey, do you want to get kicked by those people for saying that?

B Huh! Well, if I'm short on strength, lack the power, and can't beat them, then I'll just get beaten. But anyway, if students who study in the United States continue like this, they'll decline soon. What reputation will they have? Even now, in reality, they are not as active socially or even personally as other students who are fewer in number. How can the future of students who study in the United States not be bleak?

Y But what about other students? A few students from Germany have come back. What else?

B German students! Don't even mention it. They are not that few in number, but there isn't a single one who is useless. They always do their part. In reality, it hasn't been that long since students from Germany started coming back to Korea, but even if their reputation isn't that high, everyone knows their names, which shows how abundant their activities are, right? Ch’oe Tu-sŏn (崔斗善氏) was the first to come back and became a leading figure in the education world. Yi Kwan-nyong (李灌鎔博士) alone has a considerable reputation in the press, ideological circles, and academia. Kim Chun-yŏn (金俊淵氏) is also on his way, and although he doesn't have such a prominent name in the press, he always seems so gentle and well-behaved that he doesn't seem like he would cause any trouble. And Yun Ch’i-hyŏng (尹治衡氏) also came back from there and got a doctorate in Japan, and Yi Sŏng-nyong (李星鎔博士) even brought a Western wife and opened a hospital, right? <21> Oh, Dr. Chŏng Sŏk-t’ae (鄭錫泰博士) is individualistic, but the way he acts is very agile. First, he built a new internal medicine and venereal disease clinic in a famous teahouse alley in Seoul and opened it. Just look at how he is active in various fields. No student can keep up with his small body flitting around like he's flying. And Dr. Yi Kap-su (李甲秀) also seems to have opened a huge new hospital in front of Posŏng High School (普成高等普通學校) in Susong-dong (壽松洞). I saw an advertisement in the newspaper the other day, and he's promoting it a lot. Anyway, I agree with the German students! I agree. A person should have the pleasure of being active unless they are a dead object. Isn't that right?

Y That's right. I agree, but you're praising them too much. Do you think you'll get free treatment at their hospitals if you get sick?

B What are you talking about? I don't have money, but would I go to them for free treatment? But what value is there in being half-assed? Still, it's the German spirit, so I like the rough-and-tumble approach. That's how there's some energy. If an individual is small and does that, the society will naturally have that energy.

Y That's right. There's nothing worse than a society without energy. But in general, I hear that it's very difficult to get a doctorate in Germany because academics are so advanced, but everyone who comes back to Korea seems to have a doctorate. It seems like their talents are extraordinary.

B Well, isn't it possible if you try? A doctorate is something people do. If you have some talent and put in a little more effort to study, you can do it, right?

Y So, according to you, the German students have the best record among students who studied abroad. But there must be others, right?

B Yes! There are probably a few students who studied in France. First of all, Bachelor of Literature Yi Chŏng-sŏp (李晶燮氏) just came back, didn't he? And Kim Chae-ŭn (金在殷氏), who writes travelogues in the *Tonga Ilbo* (東亞日報) supplement, probably came back from France.

Y What are they doing?

B I don't know. They seem to have just come back. If it's France, they'll probably be active in the arts.

Y Is there anyone else? I heard that there are quite a few students who studied in France, but I haven't heard much about them.

B Well? I also heard that after the war, Germany had severe economic difficulties, with the value of the mark falling and prices rising, so many people moved to France because tuition was expensive. But I haven't heard of anyone who came back yet...

Y Hey, isn't there someone who came back from England? <22>

B What's there to ask? Min Yŏng-hwi Daegam's (閔泳徽大監) son, Kyu-sik (奎植氏), graduated from Cambridge University (캠푸릿치大學) with a degree in mining or something a long time ago, but what's there to say about that? He has some position at a bank, and he's living a dandyish life in a new brick house he built at the foot of Mt. Pukak (北岳山) across from Samch’ŏng-dong (三淸洞). And Pak Sŭng-ch’ŏl (朴勝喆君) was actually in Germany a lot, but it would be okay to say that he came back from England. He teaches geography and history at Chungang High School (中央學校) and also teaches a few hours at a specialized school. It's a pitiful thing to think about. When students who study abroad come back to their home country, they become middle school teachers, newspaper reporters, missionaries, and interpreters! It's pathetic!

Y Hey, you have to learn even if it's small. What else can you do? What about students who studied in Japan and China?

B Hey, you crazy bastard! You're asking all the bitter questions. So-called reporters are all like that.

I hesitated here and stopped talking. Anyway, I am very grateful to Mr. B for providing me with this material. <End> <23>

B君은 별안간에 의외로 엄격하고도＜19＞ 진실한 태도가 얼골빗에 나터나며

하며 손을 홰홰 내두른다. 『취중에 진담한다고
얘 여보게 그런데 朝鮮 사람들은 왜 무슨 일을 하나 끗치 업다나. 그저 龍頭蛇尾여 龍頭蛇尾! 爲先, 해외에서 도라온 소위 留學生들을 보게. 무엇하나 하는 것이 잇던가?
처음에 도라올 때는 아조 그럴드시 신문에 글ㅅ줄이나 무엇 소개도 하고 한다고 떠들다가는 그저 몃칠 지내면 희지부지 踪跡이 渺然하게 되데그려. 그러니 무엇 해외 留學生이라고 別한 것이 잇던가?』 하고는 의외로 외국 留學生 이약이를 끄낸다. 『그러면 게뎨좃타』 하고 B君을 붓잡고 해외에서 도라온 인물의 總評을 한 번 드러보기로 內定하엿다. 그리하여 일일히 細評까지는 못하엿지만 하여간 槪括的이나마 해외에서 도라온 인물들의 이약이를 듯게 되얏다.

B 엇다 이 사람! 유학생에게 무던히 好感을 갓고 잇네 그려! 자네도 耶蘇敎篤信者나 되여 가지고 교회의 주선으로 米國留學이나 가보게 그려! 그러고 자네 집부터도 재산 푼이나 잇스닛가 米國留學 가기는 제일좃치.
