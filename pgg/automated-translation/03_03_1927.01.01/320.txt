필자	波影
기사형태	문예기타 (Literature and Other)

抱腹絶倒 양초귀신
						波影

They say fortune comes to a smiling home, so in the new year, let me tell a funny, funny story that will make your sides ache.

A long time ago, a rather naive country gentleman came to Seoul for sightseeing. He saw candles (蠟燭) for the first time, which made the whole room bright when lit. Amazed, he bought many of them and went back to his village. He visited every house in his village, boasting about his trip to Seoul and giving each house three of the wondrous candles as a souvenir of his trip.

The villagers received the unfamiliar objects, but they didn't know what they were or how to use them, which made them very frustrated. However, they were too embarrassed to ask the person who had given them, so they didn't ask. Instead, they went from house to house, asking each other, but no one knew what the slender, long, white things were.

Finally, five young men with topknots took the objects in their hands and went to the most famous teacher in the village's schoolhouse (書堂) to ask.

"Teacher! Song seobang, who lives behind the hill, bought these in Seoul and gave them to every house as a souvenir. Did you get any of these at your house?"

"Yes, of course. I got nine of them at my house."

"Yes, you got especially many, Teacher... We don't know what they're called or what they're used for. That's why we came to ask what they're used for."

"Can it be that there are people who don't know such a thing? Die. You should die. It would be right to die..."

"Yes, even if we die, we want to know for sure before we die, so please tell us."

"Even the most ignorant person knows it's for boiling soup. People in Seoul boil soup with it."

"Huh? They boil soup with it? Is it delicious?"

"Of course, it's delicious... If it weren't delicious, would people in Seoul eat it...? It's delicious, makes you fat, and is very excellent."

"What exactly is it that makes it so delicious and good for the body?"

"It's made from baegeo (白魚), a fish that lives in the water and is dried."

"There are many strange fish. What is this pointy thing (심지) in front?"

"That's a fish that originally doesn't have eyes... That's why it's even more precious. Isn't that pointy thing its mouth?"

"What is this hole at the bottom?"

"That's its anus, what else could it be?"

"Yes, yes, I understand. Hearing you speak, it really does seem like dried fish... Seoul people eat all kinds of strange fish."

"Isn't that why Seoul is good?"

"So, how do you make porridge with this?"

"Huh? Are you so ignorant? It's frustrating! You boil water, slice this thinly with a knife, put it in, and season it with soy sauce."

"Is it really that delicious?"

"Of course, it's delicious... Well, since we're at it, let's boil it at my house today and you can try it."

The teacher should have just said he didn't know, but he pretended to know and told his family to boil water, add soy sauce and chopped scallions, and then thinly slice the candles and add them. He brought out six bowls.

"Now, try it. You'll be amazed if you just taste it..."

"Well, eating such good food will shock my insides."

"Don't talk nonsense and just eat. I ate it when I went to Seoul last year <91> and this is the first time I'm eating it today."

But when the five men tried to eat it, they saw that the soup was made with candles and was covered in shiny oil.

"Oh dear, what is this strange oil floating on top? What is it?"

"Ah, those country bumpkins only say ignorant things. The better the soup, the more oil there is. Doesn't beef soup have a lot of oil when it's well-boiled...? Baeggeo soup also has a lot of oil, so you get fat when you eat it. Didn't I say from the beginning that it makes you fat?"

They were afraid of being criticized as country bumpkins if they said anything more, so they endured the smell and swallowed it down. After eating, their throats felt clogged and sore.

Finally, unable to bear it, they said,

"Oh dear, does all Seoul food make your throat hurt like this? It hurts so much I'm going to die."

"Huh? Of course, a commoner's throat would hurt when noble food goes down. Be quiet and eat."

The others couldn't say anything and sat there with their mouths wide open and hissing because their throats hurt. The teacher was in even more pain than the others, but he was too embarrassed to open his mouth and just fidgeted.

Just then, Song seobang, who had actually bought the candles in Seoul, came to the house. The others were so happy to see him.

"Oh, it's perfect that you came. We boiled the baegeo you gave us today and our throats hurt so much we're going to die. Is it supposed to hurt?"

Song seobang was surprised and his eyes widened. "It's not something you're supposed to eat..."

The others heard that it wasn't supposed to be eaten and

"Oh dear, we're in big trouble. We ate something we shouldn't have because it was called Seoul food."

They made a fuss.

"Who said it was something to eat and did such a foolish thing?"

"Who else? The teacher here almost died boiling it into soup."

The teacher's face turned as red as a beet and he just looked down at the floor.

"That's not baegeo, it's something you light. Look, I'll light it..."

He struck a match and lit the wick, which they had called the fish's mouth. The whole room lit up brightly, and when the others saw it,

"Oh dear, we ate fire."

They jumped around like crazy people, saying that fire would light up in their stomachs and asking what they should do. They were jumping up and down as if fire was rising in their stomachs, saying

"Oh my, fire."

"Oh dear, what if my stomach burns?" <92>

They cried out in a way that sounded like crying. But the teacher, who was sitting with his face bowed down, was more afraid than anyone else. The more he thought about it, the more anxious he became at the thought of a spark of fire in his stomach. Suddenly, he shouted, "Let's jump into the water before the fire rises in our stomachs!" He took the lead and ran out, took off all his clothes behind the village stream, and jumped into the water with a splash, leaving only their necks above the water and submerging their bodies in the water to prevent the fire from rising.

It was a brightly moonlit night, and a late-night traveler (旅客), who was already afraid to walk alone by the stream, was startled by the chattering sounds coming from the stream. He looked closely at the moonlight and saw only people's heads floating in the stream like watermelons.

"Aha, those are dokgabi (도깨비, goblins)... I heard that dokgabi are afraid of cigarette fire." He quickly lit a cigarette and struck a match to light the cigarette. The teacher and the men, who were barely staying in the water to prevent the fire from rising in the candles in their stomachs, were afraid that the match would light the candles in their stomachs.

They screamed without knowing whether they would live or die.

"Hey, that guy is striking a match to light the candles in our stomachs, so let's all submerge our heads in the water! We're in big trouble!"

They all submerged their heads and faces in the water.

The traveler didn't know what was going on and, seeing that the dokgabi heads like watermelons had disappeared from the stream,

"I guess dokgabi are most afraid of cigarette fire..." and passed by. (The End) <93>
