요것만은 알어둘 것, 汽車 타는 데도 常識이 든다 (Things to Know: Common Sense for Riding the Train)
잡저 (Miscellaneous)

Here are some things to know, common sense is also needed when riding a train.

When trains were first introduced in Joseon, there was a high-ranking official who, with the same mindset as when he used to ride in a palanquin with his servants, became furious when the train didn't wait for him and left. He reportedly flared his nostrils and shouted, "Are there such impudent fellows?" He lashed out at everyone from the stationmaster and conductor to the train driver and station staff, causing a commotion. This story lingered in our ears, but now, thinking about it, we laugh and wonder if there could be such a foolish official.

However, even in today's enlightened world, not everyone is necessarily smart. Sometimes, someone receives a telegram about a parent's critical illness at their hometown and rushes onto the train without even buying a ticket. They pretend to be nonchalant until the ticket inspector discovers them. After that, they get a candy from the conductor and feel utterly deflated. Or, a gentleman has money in his pocket but ends up standing stiffly in a crowded third-class cabin all night because the sleeping car is fully booked. In the past, people could walk five hundred or a thousand li to their destinations in five or ten days, so there wouldn't have been any problem. But nowadays, even a bookworm who stays cooped up in his room, only reading Confucian texts, might end up relying on the fast-moving black train once a year if he's lucky. Therefore, it seems necessary for everyone to know these tips.

When boarding a train without a ticket

First of all, one never knows what might happen. Sometimes, you might be in a rush and miss the train because you couldn't buy a ticket in time, which is truly frustrating. If it doesn't matter much and you can take the next train, it's fine. But imagine receiving a telegram about your parent's critical illness and being able to see them before they pass away if you took that train, but missing their death because you took the next one. A filial son would feel like spitting blood and would regret it for the rest of his life. What should you do in such a situation? If you're really in a hurry, it's okay to jump on the train without a ticket. However, if you rush too much and fall, injuring your forehead, that would be a major incident. Also, if you board the train and pretend to be nonchalant until you get off at your destination, you'll end up eating a bad candy and acting like a fool. Moreover, since you rode the train without a ticket, they won't know where you boarded, and you'll have to pay double the fare from the initial station as a penalty. Therefore, it's crucial to establish proof that you boarded at the station printed on the ticket. You should quickly inform the ticket seller at the station or the conductor of the train when you board. If you can't do that, you must take measures to prove that you boarded at that station by any means possible.

Can you get a refund for a purchased ticket?

However, sometimes you might buy a ticket and enter the platform but suddenly have to cancel your trip. What happens to the purchased ticket in such cases? That's the question. If the reason for not being able to travel is due to a sudden illness or the railway authority's fault, such as the train being full, you can get a refund or extend the validity of the ticket. But if the cancellation is due to the passenger's circumstances, you'll have to bear the loss alone.

How to use an express ticket?

Express ticket! Some people know it well, but many don't, and sometimes they fail to use it properly. They might think the fare is the same to any destination, but in reality, it's divided into four categories based on class and distance, as shown in the table.

Also, the validity of an express ticket is two days from the date of issue. After that period, it cannot be used again. If you get off midway, the original ticket is unaffected, but for the express ticket, even if you haven't reached the distance specified, it becomes invalid beyond that point.

Sleeping Car Information

Sleeping cars are sold four days prior to the train's departure, and the fare is per night. The sleeping car ticket specifies the train number and seat number. If you don't board the train at the departure time, no matter how much you regret the money, it's useless. You can get a refund before departure, but it's not as flexible as regular tickets and express tickets. Therefore, only half the amount is refunded to cover such risks. It is best to purchase a sleeping car ticket in advance for long journeys and depart on the designated train at the specified time.

What if you don't have a ticket?

This time, it's about cases where you don't have a ticket. If you boarded without a ticket from the beginning, you were prepared for it, so there's no point in being cautious. The issue arises when you buy a ticket from Kyŏngsŏng to Taegu (大邱) but suddenly change your plans and decide to go to Pusan (釜山) while on the train. Of course, everyone knows that you should ask the conductor for an extension and pay the additional fare. However, it's a big mistake to assume you can just pay the extra fare anytime and pretend to be nonchalant! If you go as far as Miryang (密陽) or Samnangjin (三浪津) thinking you can pay the difference there, you'll have to pay double from Taegu to that point as a penalty. And if you continue to the final destination of Pusan, you'll end up looking like a fool, wasting money, and losing everything.

What about children's fares and handling luggage?

If you're traveling with just a small handbag, stuffing a few items inside, there won't be much of a problem. But if you're traveling with your family or a lot of luggage, it's common to have four children and two or three pieces of luggage. In such cases, it's helpful to know these things. For children aged four to fourteen, the regular fare or sleeping car fare is always half of the adult fare. For luggage, you can check up to 50 kŭn for third class, 70 kŭn for second class, and 100 kŭn for first class for free. However, if you exceed that limit, you'll have to pay the specified fee. Also, there's no charge for luggage for one day after it arrives at the designated station, but after 24 hours, you'll have to pay a considerable storage fee.

Caution is the best policy

The world is becoming enlightened, transportation is becoming more frequent, and people's lives are spilling out onto the streets. Even newly married couples who used to stay indoors are becoming modern, and people with straw shoes, topknots, rubber shoes, and braided hair are showing their faces at the station. Everything is better safe than sorry, so knowing these things will be less regrettable than just fixing up your appearance.

그러나 세상이 밝어진 오늘이라고 반드시 다 똑똑하란 법은 업서서 잇다금은 시골집 부모 병환의 위급하다는 뎐보를 밧고 차표 찍을 새이도 업시 차ㅅ간에 뚸여 드러가서 시침이를 떼고 안젓다가 차표 조사하는 때야 겨우 발견되야 되지 안흔 차장 알에 눈깔 사탕을 어더 먹고 코가 쑥-빠지는 사람 돈을 주머니에다 두고도 침대가 만원되야 비좁은 3등실에 가 뼛뼛이 서서는 밤을 새는 신사가 적지는 아니하다. 전날 가치 사람마다 오백 리 천리 되는 곳을 닷새나 열훌이나 두고 타박타박 제발로 제각기 거러다닌다면 문제될 것도 업지만 시골 방안에 드러뭇처 공자왈 맹자왈을 찻고만 안젓던 골서방님도 신수 조흐면 1년 만에 한 번 쯤은 핑핑 닷는 거믄 긔차에게 신세를 낏치게 되는 시대이닛가. 요런 요령만은 누구나 다 가치 알어둘 필요가 잇는가 하다.

차표 찍지 안코 차에 올나탄 때
위선 첫재 사람이라는 것은 어느 때 무슨 일이 생길지 모르는 법이라 차가 방금 떠나랴 하는데 차표를 못 찍어서 어름어름 하다가 차시간을 놋치고는 울드시 긔막힌 때가 잇는 것이다. 그것이 어지간해서 고다음 떠나는 차로 가도 관게치 안흘 것 가트면 모르지만 부모의 병환이 위중하다는 뎐보를 밧고 고 시간에 갓스면 운명하는 부모에게 림종할 수가 잇섯는데 고 다음 시간에 갓기 때문에 림종을 못 하엿다고 해보라. 그야말로 효성잇는 자식이 피를 토하랴고 할 것이요. 일생에 잇치지 못할 후회일 것이다. 고런 때에는 엇더케 할 것인가? 정 밧브면 차표를 찍지 말고 그대로 막 뚸여 드러가서 타도 좃타. 그럿타고 너무 급히 서들다가 잡바저서 이마나 깨여지면 그도 일대 사건, 그러고 차에 드러가서 시침이를 떼고 안젓다가 하차할 때에야 정거장에서 그대로＜126＞ 내주면 맛 업는 눈깔사탕 먹고 반편 노릇하고 게다가 안 찍은 차표 가지고 차 탓스닛가 어대서부터 탄 것인지 알 수 업다 하며 처음 탄 정거장서부터 온 차싹의 갑절을 벌금으로 밧처야만 된다. 그러니 그런 때에는 차표에 씨워 잇는 정거장서부터 탄 것이 분명하다는 증거를 세우는 것이 무엇보다도 필요함으로 거긔서 탈 때에 그 정거장의 표 찍는 곳이나 또는 그 차 차장에게 얼는 말하여둘 필요가 잇고 또는 그럿케 할 수 업는 때라도 반드시 무슨 방법으로던지 그 정거장에서부터 탓다는 증명이 되도록 수단을 취하여야만 할 것이다.

한 번 산 차표는 다시 무를 수 업는가.
그러나 차표를 찍어 가지고 홈에 드러가서 별안간 려행을 중지하게 되는 때가 잇다. 그런 때에는 산 차표가 엇지될 것인가. 그것이 문제이다. 그런 때에는 그 타지 못하게 된 원인이 급히 병이나서 엇절 수 업는 때나 혹은 만원되야서 탈 여지가 업다던지 하는 텰도 당국의 책임으로 된 때는 그 료금은 도로 차질 수도 잇고 또는 차 타는 긔간을 연장식힐 수도 잇스나 만일 승객의 형편으로 해서 중지하는 때는 할 일 업시 혼자 손해가 되고 마는 것이다.

급행권은 엇더케 쓰는 것인가?
급행권! 이것도 아는 사람은 잘 알지만 모르는 이도 적지 아니하야 잇다금은 실패하는 수가 만타. 그래 료금은 어대까지고 가튼 줄로 생각하나 실상은 그럿치 아니하여 등급과 거리를 따라서 네 가지로 구별되야 잇는데 표로 쓰면 이러하다.

그리고 급행권온 사용하는 긔간이 발행한 날부터 이틀 동안임으로 그 긔간을 지나면 다시 사용할 수가 업고 도중에서 하차하게 되면 원차표는 관게가 업지만 급행권에 한하야서는 거리가 한정된 곳까지 못 밋처 갓더래도 그 압흐로는 무효가 된다.

차표 가지지 아니한 때는?
