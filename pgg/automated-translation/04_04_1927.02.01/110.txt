별건곤 제4호
1927년 02월 01일
珍職業展覽會續開 (Exhibition of Rare Professions Continued; Miscellany)

People Who Live by Telling Stories

Should I call them liars or truth-tellers? Anyway, regardless of whether there is an incident or not, whether it is someone else's or their own, whether it concerns the times or society, they string together idle talk based on conjecture, sometimes assumptions, or seemingly factual or like a lie, and gather it with an iron pen (鐵筆) onto a manuscript paper, and sell it to newspapers, magazines, or bookstores to make a living. These people are called novelists or creators.

Whether a friend fails in love, or they have some dissatisfaction or complaint, or a war breaks out somewhere, or a fire occurs, they incorporate these facts to some extent and artfully depict the environment and events with various imaginations and conjectures and publish it to the world to make a living, which is this profession.

What is in this person's head day and night? "How can I portray that fact as a novel and publish it to the world?" That's it. For example, if a friend fails in love with a certain beauty and dies from taking rat poison in anger, they don't just extract the outline like a news article on page three, but they skillfully add idle talk to the place, background, characters, and events to make it seem like a real fact. Sometimes, they subtly embellish their own experiences as if they were talking about someone else's. In this way, they rack their brains day and night, wear out their pens, and sacrifice manuscript paper. As a result, they receive ten won, twenty won, one hundred won, two hundred won, or even one thousand won, several thousand won, depending on the value, level of credibility, or length of the manuscript. They make a living as storytellers for life, so it cannot be denied that it is a remarkable and rare profession (珍職業).

People Who Make a Living with the Dead<40>

This was more than a rare profession (珍職業); it was a bizarre profession (怪職業). Ordinary people dislike even visiting the sick, let alone corpses, but somehow this person follows corpses like a fox and makes a living. There are "enshrouders" (歛軍) and burial teams, but they are all strange professions.

In large cities like Seoul, there are one or two such people in every neighborhood who make a living doing this full-time.

When someone dies in the neighborhood, regardless of whether they are male, female, young, or old, they habitually visit the house (or are called to go). First, they take the deceased's shirt or underwear and go out into the yard and shout, "Return! Return! Return!" starting with the soul-calling (復) (if it's the time of death). And when it's time for enshrouding (歛), they take silk (明紳) or hemp cloth (벼) or paper (조희) and go into the place where the body is, and they sit on the rotting, smelly, and fluid-leaking corpse, change its clothes, and tie it with twelve knots. They also usually put it in the coffin and load it onto the bier. In this way, they receive three or four won, up to five or six won for enshrouding (歛) an adult, and one won or two won for enshrouding (歛) a child (depending on the family's circumstances, it varies).

Anyway, this person sees this as their profession, so they wait for someone to die in the neighborhood to make a living. When someone dies, instead of feeling sorry, they are secretly happy, saying, "That's great. Another meal," which is common. There are all sorts of bizarre professions in the world! But it's also unavoidable to make a living.

People Who Carve and Eat

There are people who hold a small knife and sit hunched over day and night, carving and eating away at wood, bone, crystal, or stone. Should we call them sculptors? Seal engravers? In any case, how meticulous and frustrating is the way they work. It cannot be excluded from the category of rare professions (珍職業).

Look. Even when writing, they must write backwards (左書). Even when using a knife, they can't use swords or kitchen knives; they use small and sharp angles suitable for gutting minnows. And after writing four or five characters in red ink (赤書) on the tip of a small, pointed bone like the tip of their little finger, how much effort, eyesight, and hand strength does it take to carve it out stroke by stroke?<41> Their knees are numb, their eyes sting, and their heads are dizzy.

A regular seal (本圖章) costs fifteen to twenty jeon, a square seal (角圖章) costs thirty to forty jeon, and crystal or ivory costs one won to one won fifty jeon. Anyway, the price is not fixed depending on the quality of the item, but the daily income is said to be four or five won, up to seven or eight won if they do well, and one or two won even if they don't. And like the other day when the year changed, it was said to be one hundred won a day. Due to the reorganization of office managers in banks, companies, and public organizations. And in case there is a habit of forging seals by unscrupulous gangsters, they say they must keep a register of original seals to register with government offices.

Of course, this profession requires permission from the government. If you do it without permission, you will go to the police station. Village teachers, don't just focus on earning pocket money; pay close attention.

People Who Sell Their Legs and Eat

There are so many different jobs in the world. There is nothing that people don't sell to make a living, selling their brains, eyes, tongue tips, and fingertips, and there are also people who sell their legs to make a living. They move their legs there when told to go there, and they move their legs there when told to go there, which is usually called walking. Or sometimes called debt collectors (債傭軍).

But to make a living doing this, you must be good at walking. And you have to be strong-willed. They take on the power of attorney from a creditor and go to a debtor to collect the debt, so how can a weakling do it?

The walking fee is thirty or forty jeon per ten li, depending on the times. This walking fee is always borne by the debtor. And the debtor also bears the cost of lodging, shoes, and lunch. If the debtor encounters a walking army (보행군) several times, they will become unable to live. That's why you can't live with other people's debt. There is also the saying that debtors are sinners, but this walking army (보행군) is more dignified than a police officer who goes to arrest a major criminal. Avoid incurring debt as much as possible. Even if you incur debt, don't encounter the walking army (보행군). Oops - if you meet the walking army (보행군), you might get slapped in the face.

People Who Guard the Toilet and Eat

People who make a living with feces, people who make a living with corpses, people who guard the toilet<42> and eat, these wouldn't be rare professions (珍職業), but they are not ordinary professions (普通業). Ordinary people can't help but go to the toilet when their bladder is about to burst and their bowels are about to tear. Even when they go, they cover their noses and frown and come out after a short while. But there are people who guard this toilet, which is famous for its filth, day and night, all year round, and make a living. This is not common in private homes, but it exists even in some government offices. It exists in the famous Namdaemun Station in Seoul. There is a paid toilet there where it costs three jeon per use. There is always a toilet attendant there to monitor and collect money. But that money goes to the station's income, and the wage is a measly thirty jeon per day. Not only the paid toilet, but also the free toilet behind the third-class waiting room has a toilet attendant. Not just one, but two or three people monitor the men's and women's toilets. They don't wear high-collar (하이카라) suits, but they wear suits that are better than the average office worker's uniform and shoes, and very handsome people guard the toilet and eat. This is probably to maintain the cleanliness or prevent contamination of the station, but more than that, it is for the convenience of many men and women. Anyway, a toilet attendant seems like a rare profession (珍職業).

People Who Make a Living in the Water

Land animals are those who cannot even make a living on land and are frantically running around, so how can they make a living in the water, which is absurd? If it's not a lie, it must be about amphibians like frogs or marine creatures. But how can it be that there are people of the highest order among land animals, living underwater?

That is the famous female divers (海女) of Jeju Island in Korea. They spend more time underwater than on land in spring, summer, autumn, and winter. Unlike ordinary fishermen who live with boats or nets or such equipment, they dive naked with diving goggles on their eyes, a sickle (비창) in their hands, a float (등박) on their backs, and a net (망사) around their waists, in groups of three or five, swimming effortlessly in the vast ocean, no matter how deep or dangerous, and dive into the water for ten or five minutes to pick up shellfish, abalone, seaweed, sea cucumbers, catch fish, and find pearls. Women who are good at it live in the water all day or half a day, and even those who are not good at it spend two or three hours. Sometimes they sit on rocks to rest, or they prepare a boat and go out and sit on the boat to eat. But even in the water, they have clear vision and breathe freely like on land.

These female divers (海女) catch fish and use their income to feed their husbands and children.

There is no one whose fate is as good as the husbands of female divers (海女).<43> So there is a saying that all widowers should go to Jeju Island. But these female divers (海女) live in the water naked all year round, so their skin is very dark. They are similar to black people (黑人種). And because they live fighting the waves, their bodies are very strong.

People Who Make a Living as Executioners

Those who know what an executioner (망난이) is know, but there may be some who don't. In the old days of Korea (朝鮮), the death penalty executor was called an executioner (망난이). A butcher of large animals was called a large executioner (개망난이), and a butcher of small animals was called a small executioner (소망난이). There are many strange and bizarre occupations in the world, but there is no occupation as bizarre as a person killing people and making a living.

There are various positions in prison, including wardens, guards, doctors, and chaplains, but among them, the death penalty executor (死刑執行手), that is, the so-called executioner (망난이), also has a position. When a murderer, robber, or someone who poisoned their husband receives a death sentence from the court, the prison takes custody of the death row inmate and executes the death penalty on a certain date. At the time of the death penalty execution, wardens, prosecutors, doctors, and chaplains are present. After a physical examination by a doctor, the prosecutor asks for a final statement or thoughts, the chaplain preaches to repent of their crimes and become a good person in the afterlife, and the warden orders the execution, they cover the death row inmate's face with a cloth and drag them to the gallows. And the executioner (집행수) grabs the device of the gallows once. Then, unknowingly, the machine snaps and kicks, and the death row inmate hangs in the air with a rope around their neck. After five or ten minutes, they are completely dead. Then, they pull up the body and, in the presence of the prosecutor and warden, require another examination by the doctor, and after confirming that they are completely dead, they immediately hand over the body to the bereaved family. If there are no bereaved family members, the prison disposes of it freely.

In the old days, when executing death row inmates, the executioner (망난이) would perform a sword dance and cut off the head with a sword, or they would cut off the head of the death row inmate with something like a noose before they could even realize it.<44>

잔사설로 먹고 사는 사람

거짓말쟁이라 할가 實말쟁이라 할가 엇잿든 사건이 잇고 업고간에 남의 것이고 제것이고 시대상이고 사회상이고 間에 추측으로 혹 가정으로 혹은 사실답게 혹은 거짓말답게 잔소리를 느러 노아가지고 그것을 철필이란 갈키로 원고지란 그릇에 거더 모아가지고 신문잡지사나 書冊肆에 파러가지고 먹고사는 사람이 잇슴니다. 이것을 소설가라고도 하고 창작가라고도 함니다.
엇던 친구가 연애를 하다가 실패를 당햇다든지 혹은 자기가 무슨 불평이나 불만이 잇다든지 어대 무슨 전쟁이 낫다든지 무슨 火變이 낫다든지 그런 사실을 다소 참작해 가지고 여긔다가 여러 가지 가상과 추측으로 환경과 사건을 교묘히 그려여며 세상에 공개해 가지고 먹고 사는 것이 즉 이직업임니다.
이 사람의 머리ㅅ속에는 밤낫 무엇이 잇는고 하니 「엇더케 하면 그 사실을 소설로 그려내여 세상에 공개하노?」그것임니다. 가령 엇던 친구가 엇던 미인과 연애를 하다가 실패를 당하고 화김에 쥐 잡는 약을 먹고 죽엇다 하면 그것을 신문 삼면기사와 가티 그러케 줄거리만 잡아내지를 안코 장소나 배경이나 인물이나 사건이나에 두루 그럴듯하게 교묘히 잔소리를 붓치여 정말 사실인 거와 가티 그려 놋슴니다. 자기가 당한 일도 다른 사람의 것을 말하는 듯이 슬적 그러케 꾸미는 일이 잇슴니다. 그리하야 밤낫 머리를 썩이고 붓끗를 시달리고 원고지를 희생시킴니다. 그리하야 가치의 유무 또는 신망의 고하 또는 원고의 장단에 따라 십원, 이십원, 백원, 이백원, 내지 천원, 멧천원까지 바다먹슴니다. 평생을 이러케 잔소리군으로 버러먹으니 직업 중에도 톡톡한 한 珍職業이라고 아니 할 수 업슴니다.

죽은 사람으로 버리하는 사람<40>

이야말로 珍職業 이상의 怪職業이엿다. 보통 사람은 송장은커녕 病人한테도 가기를 실혀 하는데 이 사람은 엇더케 된 셈인지 여호 모양으로 송장만 따라단이며 버러 먹고 살것다. 거긔에는 「歛軍」도 잇고 埋葬軍도 잇스나 다 해괴한 직업이다.
서울가튼 대도회에는 동리마다 이런 사람이 하나둘식 잇서서 전업으로 이것을 해 먹고 산다.
이웃에서 남녀노소간 누가 죽엇다 하면 의례로 그 집을 차저간다(안가면 불러간다) 가서는 위선 死者의 적삼이나 속옷을 가지고 마당에 나와 「復!」 「復!」 「復!」하고 초혼부터 불러주고(운명 당시면 말이다) 그리고 歛할 시간이 되면 明紳나 벼나 조희 等屬을 가지고 시체 잇는 곳으로 드러가 붓고 썩고 냄새나고 물 흐르는 시체를 타고 안저 의복을 밧궈입피고 열두묵기를 묵거 놋는다. 그리고 관에 너키도 그가 하고 상여에 실기도 그가 보통한다. 이러케하야 대인의 歛에는 삼사원 내지 오육원을 밧고 소아의 歛에는 일원 혹 이원을 밧는다.(주인형편 따라 不一)
이 사람은 엇잿던 이것이 직업인지라 먹기 위하야 이웃에서 사람죽기만 기다린다. 누가 죽엇다 하면 불상타하기커녕 「올타 되얏다. 또 먹엇다」하고 은근히 깃버하는 것이 常套이다. 세상에는 해괴한 직업도 다 잇지! 그러나 먹고 살기 위하야서는 또한 엇절수 업는 일이다.

홉여파고 먹는 사람

밴대칼을 들고 밤낫 꼬부리고 안저 나무고 뼈고 수정이고 돌이고 간에 작구 홉여 파내고 먹는 사람이 잇슴니다. 이것을 조각가라구 할가요. 도장쟁이라고 할가요. 여하간 고하는 꼴악산이란 엇지도 周密스럽고 갑갑한지요. 珍職業축에 안너흘 수 업슴니다.
자 보십시오. 글씨를 써도 類 달리 左書를 써야 됨니다. 칼을 써도 검이나 식도가튼 것은 어림도 업고 송사리 배 따기 알마즌 적고도 날카라운 각도를 씀니다. 그리고 그 색기손가락 끗가튼 적고도 뾰죽한 뼈다귀 끄테다 넉자고 다섯자고 赤書를 하고 나서 그것을 획수대로 차근차근 홉여파 내는 것이야말로<41>얼마나한 정력과 안력과 手力이 들겟슴닛가. 무릅이 저리고 눈알이 쏘다저 나오고 머리가 뗑하지요.
本圖章 한 개에는 십오전 내지 이십전 角圖章 한 개에는 삼십전 내지 사오십전 수정이나 상아에는 일원 내지 일원오십전 엇잿든 물품의 好不好에 따라 갑은 일정치 안으나 하로 수입이 잘하면 사오원 내지 칠팔원이고 못된다 해도 일이원은 넉넉하다 함니다. 그리고 요전과 가티 년호가 밧귈때는 一日 百金 이엿다 함니다. 은행, 회사, 공공단체의 사무장 개각으로 해서요. 그리고 혹시-불량협잡배의 僞圖章버릇이 잇서서 관공서에 등록키 위하야 반듯이 도장원본을 成冊해 둔다 함니다.
이 직업은 물론 관청의 허가가 잇서야 됨니다. 허가 업시 하다가는 경찰서로 가게 됨니다. 촌서방님들 닙돈 버리에만 한 눈을 팔지 알고 크게 주의하십시요.

다리파러 먹고 사는 사람

세상에는 별 직업이 다 만슴니다. 머리를 팔로 눈깔을 팔고 혀 끗을 팔고 손끗을 팔고 두루 안파러 먹는 것이 업는데 다리를 파러 먹고 사는 사람도 잇슴니다. 여긔 가거라 하면 그리로 다리를 놀리고 저리 가거라 하면 또 그리로 다리를 놀려 가지고 먹고 사는데 이것을 보통 부르기를 보행이라 함니다. 혹은 債傭軍이라기도 하구요.
그런데 이 노릇을 해 먹으랴면 무엇보다 길을 잘 가야 됨니다. 그리고 속이 억세야 됨니다. 엇던 채권자의 권리위임을 마타 가지고 엇던 채무자에게로 가서 빗을 바더 오는 것이니 여간 유약자로야 되겟습닛가.

뒤ㅅ간 지켜주고 먹는 사람
