기사형태	대담·좌담 (Dialogue/Symposium)

Company Staff's New Year's Party on the Magazine Page
Location: Headquarters Editorial Bureau
Date: November 7
Attendees: Ch’a, Shin, Kim, Kim, Song, Yi

Ch’a: Because of the recession, we can’t go to a Yorijip [Korean-style restaurant], so let's just have our year-end party on the page. Everyone, come closer to the stove.
Shin: There's no other way!
Ch’a: Even last year, we were banging around at a Yorijip, but what about this year?
Kim: A year-end party at a Yorijip is usually what poor people do, so there must be some this year.
Song: Year-end parties aren't limited to rich people, are they? There's no way poor people can't have one, right?
Ch’a: Of course. We can buy some peanuts and crack them open while we chat. There's no way we can't do it just because we don't have money.
Paek: The format will be different from a year-end party for rich people. Like this one on the page. (All laugh)
Song: Teacher! The year-end parties in the city and in the countryside are probably different. I hope there will be some discussion about that.
Ch’a: Good. Mr. Song, please tell us a story first.
Song: In my hometown, Shinch’ang, North Ch’ŏng (北靑新昌), when there was a year-end party hosted by the Youth League and Kŭnuhoe [women's association], a lot of women from the fishing village gathered. What was different from what I saw in Seoul was that on that night, the women from the fishing village stepped forward in front of the men and told their feelings and various stories from the past year, and it was very moving.
Shin: That must have been.
Yi: It must be different from Seoul or the southern provinces.
Ch’a: Speaking of year-end parties, I still remember it, more than ten years ago, when I was at school, Kim Pyŏng-O (金OO) and of course, all the teachers! We went to a Yorijip and drank a lot of alcohol, and then we put the most drunk Mr. Kim in a rickshaw and sent him home, and we broke up. Around midnight, the rickshaw puller came running, trembling, saying that the guest he was carrying had died, so come and see. What happened was that the rickshaw puller took Mr. Kim and went up to the top of Sajik Pass, but no matter what, he couldn't find the house! Back then, it was very cold! The rickshaw puller was about to die! The rickshaw puller didn't know, but later he saw that his friend was crouched down like this (huddling his shoulders) and frozen stiff... (All laugh) So, to the rickshaw puller, it looked like a dead person! You don't know, but if you put him in a warm room, he would often die. So, I laid him down in a cold room and massaged him until he woke up. Even now, when I meet Mr. Kim, I laugh inside, thinking of him as the man who died and came back to life at the year-end party!
Kim: I've been to two or three year-end parties, and we're very worried when the sound of wooden pillows being turned comes out.
Shin: That's true! Me too.
Ch’a: Smile! (To Yi) Tell us a story.
Yi: I don't have one.
Taek: Let's stop talking about year-end parties...
Ch’a: Well, let's eat Tteokguk [rice cake soup] first.
(Brief break)
Taek: We've eaten, so let's continue.
Ch’a: Shall I tell you a story about being chased by a creditor? A certain person was about to die from the pressure of creditors at the end of the year, so he snuck into a Yorijip at night to hide. He ordered alcohol. He called a gisaeng [female entertainer]! He was having a good time when another creditor came running. He said, "You're not paying your debts and you're coming here to eat and call gisaeng?" He was speechless, but because he was a debtor, he led him into the room and made excuses, saying that he was supposed to meet someone here and that he would have money when that person came, but he hadn't arrived yet, so let's have a drink together. But naturally, the time passed and it was already one o'clock in the morning. The person suddenly embraced him and bowed politely, asking "Have you been at peace during the year? I will somehow pay off the debt I couldn't pay last year within this year." The creditor was speechless. But there was nothing he could do but say, "Please give it to me quickly when you have the money," hahahahaha (All laugh)
Kim: There was a person who burned a Heunngwŏn Line bond certificate, but later, even if he thought of getting it back, he wouldn't be able to get it...
Ch’a: He can't get it back. Because there's no proof, how can he?
Shin: When you burn it, it's with the intention of not getting the money back, so even if it becomes a legal problem, if you insist on that, you won't be able to win.
Song: Last year, it was passed on to the prosecutor's office in January...
Kim: All I can think about is Tteokguk.
Song: When I was at Sŏdaemun (Police Station), they would give us rice on the first day of the new year. So we protested. We said, "Where is there a place that gives rice on the first day of the new year?" We stayed hungry all day and didn't eat rice, and we protested, so they had no choice but to bring us cold Tteokguk, but we couldn't eat it either. It was sour and full of pebbles...
Ch’a: It's not Tteokguk, it's Pebblguk! (All laugh)
Kim: In our days, we were transferred to prison on the first day of the new year, and what was funny was that we said "Shinnyen omedeto-" [Happy New Year] to each other in the courtroom, and everyone received two or three years...
Song: It's so strange. When I was riding in the prisoner transport car, I was so envious of the children on the street wearing colorful new clothes and playing. I felt like I wanted to be released and play with them right away.
Taek: You were that envious?
Song: Of course. Back then.
Ch’a: That must have been.
Kim: When I was in prison, there was a prisoner who had seven previous convictions, and I was in the same factory with him for three months! But he followed me so well, he would somehow steal soap and wash my washcloth every day...
Ch’a: How old was he?
Taek: Why are you asking? (All laugh)
Kim: They gave us rice cakes for two or three days during the New Year, but this friend didn't eat the rice cakes and left them...
Ch’a: What a sweet romance!
Kim: He would hold it under his armpit and secretly give it to me. But for some reason, I didn't feel like eating it, so... <20>
Ch’a: You should be grateful and eat even a pile of poop.
Kim: I took it and passed it on to the person next to me. The person next to me passed it on to another person, thinking of their relative. That person also passed it on to another person. I don't know how many times we did this, but in the afternoon, the rice cake came back to me. It was so funny.
Taek: In prison or in war, same-sex love is already a perversion.
Ch’a: Let me tell you another funny story. A certain bride got married on the last day of the year, and she had a son in the first month of the new year. So what happened? The family was in an uproar. But look at what the bride said. "I've seen all kinds of strange families. Is it such a big deal that I had a child two months after I got married?" When the father-in-law heard this, he was speechless and laughed out loud. So the bride said, "If I had known that my father-in-law would be so happy, I would have brought one with me!" (All laugh)
Paek: When we send off the old year and welcome the new year, what are the psychology of men and women like?
Song: If you're going to single them out like that, go ahead and do it. Distinguish between men and women.
Cha: That's not what I meant.
Shin: A sensitive remark.
Taek: Mr. Paek, please speak first.
Paek: From my experience, every time I welcomed the new year in the past, instead of thinking that I should study more, I thought about how I could have a romance in the new year.
Ch’a: I'm already over forty, but I don't think it's a pity to get older! I'm happy about the new things that are happening in the world, and I'm not pessimistic. I want to know the facts that are happening in the world more quickly.
Song: Mr. Ch’a is getting younger.
Ch’a: The way the world is going is very interesting.
Song: I hate getting older.
Ch’a: Why? Are you afraid your beautiful face will change?
Song: Oh, teacher, really! It's not that, it's just that all women are like that.
Paek: Men feel the same way. Not only do they hate getting older, but they also feel disappointed...
Taek: I don't know about Mr. Ch’a's way of thinking, but I don't feel as frustrated as I do when I get older.
Ch’a: Look at us when we were young. There are many Confucian scholars (儒林) in the countryside, right? When we joined in the conversation and said something, they would say, "What do young kids know?" and it was very frustrating! I always thought, "I wish I was a year older so I could become an adult."
Kim: I don't feel anything special about welcoming the new year. Other people seem to be quite happy, but for some reason, I feel the same.
Ch’a: How do you feel when another woman is prettier than you? Mr. Song!
Song: Hohoho... It's good.
Taek: I don't think so.
Ch’a: Men don't feel envious or jealous when another man is more handsome than them, but when they go to a wedding and see a beautiful bride, they don't think "Oh, the groom is so lucky!" but rather "What a pity for the bride!"
Taek: That's right. If the bride and groom's looks are very different, I might even feel like congratulating them, but if the groom is much less attractive than the bride, I feel sorry for her rather than pity.
Shin: That's usually the case. What do you think, Mr. Yi?
Yi: I don't know! (All laugh)
Kim: A woman who is nothing special looks much prettier when she dresses up like this... (mimics)
Song: The story is going sideways.
Ch’a: The new year and the honeymoon! They're both new.
Taek: What is your heart like when you see a pretty woman?
Kim: I don't know what a beauty is.
Taek: Well, if you think she's pretty, that's all that matters.
Shin: That's right.
Kim: When I see a woman that other people say is pretty, I feel resentful.
Ch’a: But a pretty person is pretty to everyone.
Taek: There may be slight differences depending on how people see her, but there are no drastic differences.
Paek: When I see a pretty woman, I think about how I can make her mine. I don't know if it's a psychology that comes from the traditional feudalistic psychology or the family system.
Song: Men think of women as objects and that's where the desire to possess them comes from?!
Taek: No. It's just a different expression. What if instead of saying "my object," you said "my wife"?
Song: Still the same!
Ch’a: When I see a pretty woman, I think, "Was there ever such a woman in the world?" I don't have any ambition, but I want to know everything about her, like what kind of family she comes from, where her address is, and what her personality is like. I can't feel comfortable unless I know, and I feel frustrated if I don't.
Song: It's because you see women as products.
Kim: We don't get any stimulation even when we see a beauty.
Taek: Really?
Paek: There must be a permanent love bond rather than beauty, and there must be ideological resonance...
Taek: No way! No matter what, beauty is the first condition.
Shin: Then the heart is the second condition.
Kim: If someone says she's pretty, I hate her.
Ch’a: As the saying goes, "Even if it's ugly, the most beautiful thing is my baby"... hahaha
Paek: The standard of beauty has also changed. From the beauty of a young master or a noblewoman to the healthy beauty of a factory worker...
Ch’a: But even if she's a factory worker, you have to pick and choose among them, right? If you say you like someone who is dark and ignorant, then Jinmiryeok is the beauty of the world... (All laugh)
Taek: The scheduled time is up, so I'm sorry, but let's close the year-end party. I hope you all receive a lot of blessings in 1932. (Closing) (Taek, Stenography) <21>

車, 또 우수운 이야기 하나 하지. 엇던 색시가 섯달금음날 싀집을 갓는데 정월초순에 아들을 덜컥 낫겟다. 그러니 엇떠케 됏소. 그래 집안이 란가가 돼서 야단이 낫지. 그런대 색시가 한다는 소리 좀봐. 별놈의 집구석을 다 보겟네. 싀집온지 이태만에 애 하나 난 것이 이러케도 말성인가 햇겟다. 시아범되는 자가 이 소리를 듯더니 엇처구가 업서서 껄껄 우섯지, 그러니까 색시가 또 한다는 소리가 싀아버니가 저러케 조왓할 줄 아럿더면 진작 하나 노아가지고 왓슬걸! 하더라지. (一同 笑)
白, 묵은 해를 보내며 새해를 맛는데 잇서서 남자의 심리와 녀자의 심리는 어떠하겟습니까!
宋, 서로 그러케 꼬집어 내기를 한다면 해 보서요. 남자 녀자를 구별해서.
者, 그런 말이 아니지요.
申, 과민하신 말슴.
澤, 백선생부터 말슴합쇼.
白, 그런데 경험으로 보아서 과거에 새해를 마질 적마다 공부를 더 좀 해야 하겟다 하는 생가보다도 새해에는 어떠케 해서 련애를 좀 해봐야 하겟다 하는 생각이 납니다.
車, 나는 내 나히가 벌서 사십이 넘엇지만 나이 먹는 것이 악갑다고 생각키지를 안어! 세상에 새로 이러나는 일이 반가울지언정 비관이 안된단 말이야. 세상에 이러나는 사실을 좀더 속히 알고 십흐니까.
宋, 차선생님은 젊어가시니까.
車, 세상되여가는 것이 여간 자미잇지 안커든요.
宋, 나먹는 게 여간 실치 안어요.
車, 왜요. 고흔 얼골이 변할까 바선가요.
宋, 아이, 선생님도 참! 그런게 아니라 녀자들 모도가 그래요.
白, 남자도 역시 그러치요 나먹는 것이 실을 뿐더러 실망이 되고...
澤, 차선생 모양으로 압니마는 버서지지 안엇지만 나이먹는 게 원통하기까지는 안하든데.
車, 우리 어렷슬적 좀 봐. 지방에 유림(儒林)들이 만이 잇지 안소. 이야기들하는 틈벅에 끼여서 무어라고 하면 젊은 애들이 뭘 안다고 참견을 해 하는데 여간 분한 게 아니엇거든! 나도 얼핏 한 살이라도 더 먹어서 언제나 어른이 되누 햇단 말이야.
金, 나는 신년을 맛는다 해야 별 늣김업서. 남들은 꽤 조키도 한 모양이나 나는 왼일인지 심상하단 말이야.
車, 딴 녀자가 자기보다 엡불 때는 그 마음이 엇던가요. 송선생!
宋, 호호호...조치요 뭘.
澤, 그럴리야 잇겟다구요.
車, 남자들이야 다른 남자가 자기보다 인물이 곱다고 해서 부럽거나 시기하는 생각은 업는데 흔이 결혼식 가튼 데 가서 고흔 색시를 보면 에-그 신랑 복 만타! 하는 생각보다 색시가 앗갑다는 생각이나!.
澤, 그래요. 신랑신부가 인물이 어상반하면 오리려 축복하는 생각도 나나 한편 신랑깜이 웬체 색시만 못하면 앗갑다는 것보다 가여우니까.
申, 의레껀 그런 모양이야. 리선생 어떳소.
李, 몰라요! (一同笑)
金, 아모것도 안인 녀자가 꿈여노면 한결 곱게 보여 뭐 이러케 뒤집어쓰고...(흉내낸다)
宋, 이야기가 요꼬미찌로 드러갓서요.
車, 신년이나 신혼이나! 신자야 일반이지.
澤, 엡븐 녀자를 볼 때의 마움성은?
金, 엇떤 것이 미인인지 모르겟드군.
澤, 그야 자기가 입부게 봣스면 고만이지.
申, 그러치.
金, 나는 남들이 엡부다는 녀자를 보면 도리혀 얄밉게 보여.
車, 그러나 엡븐 사람은 누가 보던지 엡부니까.
澤, 사람이 보기에 따라 다소의 차이는 잇슬지언정 엉뚱한 차이는 업는 것이야.
白, 나는 엡븐 녀자를 볼 때 엇떠케 해서나 내 물건을 만드럿스면하는 생각이 납니다. 재래봉건적 심리나 가족제도에서 나온 심리인지는 모르나.
宋, 남자들이 녀자를 물건으로 생각하고 소유욕에서 나온 것이지요?!
澤, 아니지요. 표현이 달라서 그러치 내 물건이라 하지 말고 내 안해라던지 하면 엇대요.
宋, 그래도 그러치요 뭐!
車, 나는 엡븐 녀자를 보면 세상에 저런 녀자가 잇섯나? 하는 생각이 나고 내게 아모 야심은 업지만 공연이 그 녀자에게 대한 것을 알고 십흔 성격이나 그여코 가정이 엇떠며 주소가 어듸며 성격이 엇던 것을 아라야만 속이 시원하지 모로면 갑갑증이 나니까.
宋, 녀자를 상품으로 보니까 그래요.
金, 우리는 미인을 보아도 아모 자극이 업서.
澤, 그럴까?
白, 미인보다도 영구한 사랑의 결합이 잇서야지 사상적으로 공명되고...
澤, 안될말! 아무리 해도 미는 제일조건이야.
申, 그래 마음은 제이 조건이야.
金, 남이 곱다 하면 나는 미워.
車, 이미저미해도 우리집 간난에 미가 제일이라더니...하하하
白, 미의 표준도 변해젓서. 말하자면 귀동자나 귀부인의 미에서 공장로동자의 건강미로...
