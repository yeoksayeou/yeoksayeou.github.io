鳳凰臺에 올나서 (Ascending Bonghwangdae)
로아
시 (Poem)

Having Ascended Bonghwangdae

						Roa

Ascending to the ancient site of Bonghwangdae, covered in fallen leaves,
Embracing the shadow of the waning moon beyond the ancient tomb,
The sound of crickets echoes, and I, too, wept.

Night, the night sea, this lawn soaked in dew and tears, the lawn of old,
Upon it, writhing cripples, the disabled… how can I bear to see such a sight? I closed my eyes.

From Chang'an's myriad households, the sound of bugles and horses' hooves resound,
An unfortunate prince wanders all night around Anapji Pond!
Tears of blood fall on the tattered hem of his old monk's robe.

Who can I grasp and tell the old news to! Severing my thoughts, I open my eyes,
In the silver moonlight, myriad wool houses gleam,
Like the vengeful spirit of a giant, the pampas grass sways,
And I stand listening to the sorrowful sound of joy flowing from the house of courtesans (妓家).

Oh, to whom shall I entrust this land? <110>
Why are these drowsy people only sleeping? What dreams are they dreaming!
The grain is gone, and only fallen leaves remain in this autumn, this night,
Unable to bear it…
I wanted to cry out and wake them!
(From Gyeongju, among old drafts) <111>
