						第四隊 微笑, 靑年, 三石
Before 12:10 PM, we added Mr. Samseok to our team, bolstering our strength and courage, and set out towards Suchang-dong, as directed by the editor. Although it was the most unfamiliar and awkward place in Seoul for us, we couldn't defy the editor's strict order and reluctantly embarked on the journey, so things were unlikely to go well from the start.

First, we couldn't find the clapper (딱딱이) along the way and went through all sorts of hardships. Eventually, we managed to meet him near the Naejadong alley, almost at the end of our first round.

However, we couldn't blame anyone, so we treated the clapper to drinks at a nearby establishment as a gesture of greeting. Then, we returned to Suchang-dong via Doyeom-dong, eagerly awaiting the second round. Thus, we completely failed the planned first attempt.

A Midnight Woman's Cry
2:23 AM.

The group failed so miserably on the first attempt, and dragging our collapsing legs, we passed through Suchang-dong and Sajik-dong. When we reached the edge of Pirundong, in front of Paehwa Girls' School, we were suddenly startled by the sound of a young woman sobbing.

"A midnight cry! Moreover, a woman's cry! This must have a story, a major incident!"

Thinking this, we rushed to a small thatched house built facing west on a high foundation, to the front room by the gate.

It had been so long since we'd encountered an incident, so even the sound of crying felt greatly welcome (due to our commitment to duty). We ran there, but for some reason, our hearts raced as if we were committing a great sin as we approached the house's gate.

However, we couldn't turn back now, so we forced ourselves to change our minds and pressed our ears to the gate, holding down our pounding hearts with both hands.

"...Hoo-hoo-hoo... What sin have I committed to be kicked out in the middle of the night... Uh...uh...uh... Why am I being kicked out..."

This was the woman's sharp voice of resistance, mixed with sobs.

"...Well, you're being told to leave because I'm tired of living with you. Why are you nagging so much..." This was the angry man's blunt, exposed voice. However, his voice sounded too nonchalant and sarcastic compared to the woman.

"What do you mean? You're telling me to leave because you're tired of living with me, so what's with the nagging... Why am I a rock, just for sex...? You brought me here when it was good to live with me, and now you're telling me to leave because you're tired of living with me... What kind of law is that...? I won't leave, even if I die..."

"Oh, you won't leave... You really won't...? Leave now, while I'm telling you nicely... Before I embarrass you in front of the whole neighborhood by dragging you out..."

"Try hitting me if you dare... Do you think I'm scared of you and will just say 'yes, yes' and run away without any sin or reason...? Aren't you afraid of heaven..."

"Oh, this woman still has a mouth on her..." he seemed to be rushing at the woman.

So, the woman also seemed to be confronting the man, not staying still.

Inevitably, a scene of chaos unfolded. -Sound of clothes tearing- Sound of hitting and kicking- Sound of dishes breaking- The woman's voice screaming, "Kill me! Kill me!"

"You're still not leaving and just running your mouth... A man tells you to leave, so you should leave. What's with a woman being so impudent..."

The man's hateful voice, threatening with a contradictory sense of superiority. Thus, this sound shook the deep night and the still air.

...The fight grew more intense by the minute- no one was mediating- and it seemed like the situation would really become dangerous if it continued any longer. However, we couldn't just jump in- we were really frustrated.

At that moment, the neighbor's gate creaked open- "Oh no, we'll be in big trouble if we're caught."

We moved as quickly as swallows and stuck to the wall of the house below, watching the situation. Fortunately, there was no reflection of electric lights in that spot, so we felt somewhat relieved, but our hearts were pounding.

Soon, a woman who looked to be in her 40s came out of the gate, hurriedly tying her skirt strings, and went straight into the house where the fight was happening.

"Aha, she's probably going in to stop the fight," we thought to ourselves, feeling relieved. After some time, as expected, the woman came out with the young woman, whose clothes were torn and hair disheveled, supporting her, and went into the gate they had come from.

The woman must be an elder in the neighborhood, trying to reconcile the couple and taking the woman to her house.

Seeing that, we didn't need to stay any longer and turned our steps. We didn't know when the fight had started or why they were fighting, and the reason was vague, so we couldn't help but feel somewhat ambiguous. But we assumed that there wouldn't be a big reason or a good motive for a domestic fight, and we just turned away. And in our hearts, we sincerely hoped that the two of them would reconcile soon, changing our direction and quickening our pace.

An Old Woman's Sad Tale
2:42 AM!

The group of three returned along the winding road of Naejadong and emerged again in front of the brightly lit and wide Sajik Gate. The traces of pedestrians had long disappeared from the streets, and the chilly wind, seeping into the bones in the early morning air, only scattered the fallen leaves and light trash on the ground beneath the faint electric lights.

As per the clapper's usual route, we rounded the Governor-General's residence and entered a narrow dead-end alley to the north.

At that moment, the clapper, who was walking ahead, was startled by something and stepped back, so we, also surprised, peered ahead.

Unexpected! Unexpected!

There, a woman in blue was crouching under the eaves of someone else's gate, dozing off. The clapper glanced back at us and lightly shook the woman's shoulder, saying, "Hey! Hey!" to wake her up.

Finally, the woman opened her eyes, looked at us, and, startled, jumped up. She seemed embarrassed, rubbing her eyes and not raising her head. However, the clapper, whether he knew it or not, spoke bluntly in his usual tone.

"Who are you and why are you sleeping here?"

Because of his gruffness, the woman lowered her head even further and couldn't say anything.

So, as an emergency measure for the investigation, Mr. Cheongnyeon (靑年君), from our group, spoke in his usual soft and friendly voice. "I don't know who you are, but why are you sleeping in a place like this in the middle of the night?"

Asked like that, the woman couldn't help but answer, finally opening her mouth. However, she was still speaking with her head lowered.

"Because I have nowhere to sleep..."

She couldn't finish her sentence.

"Nowhere to sleep? Do you mean you don't have a home?" This time, Mr. Samseok (三石君) asked.

"No. I do have a home, but it's someone else's house now, so I can't go in, and I'm sleeping here."

"Someone else's house? Why were you kicked out of the house?"

Mr. Cheongnyeon (靑年君) continued to question.

"No. That's not it either... Since you're asking so kindly, I'll tell you the details. My hometown is Anseong, and my livelihood was difficult, so I came to Seoul a few months ago. Through someone's introduction, I've been working as an old woman (オマニ, *omani*, 下女 - a maid) at the residence of a Japanese person named 00, providing lodging and meals, and sending a small amount of money to my husband in the countryside. But a few days ago, I suddenly became ill and couldn't move, so I asked the owner for permission and went to my relative's house in Suwon for rest. Today, I seemed to have recovered, so I was coming back, but since I didn't have anything to ride, I had to walk. By the time I arrived, it was almost one o'clock. I couldn't wake up the owner, as I wasn't an important guest, and I suddenly had nowhere to go, so I had no choice but to sleep here, thinking I'd go there tomorrow morning."

As she said this, tears welled up in her eyes.

The group listened to her tearful plea, holding their breath.

"But..."

Mr. Cheongnyeon (靑年君) said.

"...But if you sleep outside in this cold weather, won't your illness get worse? Even if it's a little uncomfortable for the owner, why don't you try knocking on the door?"

"No. I have to do it this way, even if my illness gets worse... If I were to knock on the door, they might open it. But if I sleep comfortably like that for one night, I might receive a tragic dismissal notice tomorrow... The owner is certainly the type to do that."

"Then..."

Mr. Samseok (三石君) said.

"...We'll go to that house and explain the situation so that nothing bad happens to you."

"No. You can't do that. That might actually make me more unhappy, so if you really care about me, please don't do that."

As if by agreement, the group clicked their tongues. And in the silent gaze of the three men, there was clearly a question:

"Then, what should we do with this poor woman?"

"Anyway, shouldn't we go to the police box and discuss it?"

This was the clapper's suggestion.

"There's no other way..."

We gave this vague answer and looked at the woman. She, again, said in a pleading voice, "Even if we go to the police box, please make sure the police officer doesn't go to that house... If you don't promise that, I won't go with you."

She shook her head as if expressing her determination.

"Of course, we'll make sure to explain that. Anyway, let's go together."

We said this and asked the clapper to take the woman.

We wanted to go too, but we were most concerned that our true colors would be revealed, so we decided to wait from afar.

About 20 minutes later, we were relieved to hear from the returning clapper that the woman would be allowed to sleep inside the police box.

Anyway, it's a pitiful situation.

How unfortunate it is that the woman is trying to endure that dangerous overnight stay for fear of losing even that lowly job, which is inevitably difficult, by going to someone else's house, especially a foreigner's house.

However, this is not just one woman's immediate predicament. We must realize how many Korean women are in even more difficult situations.

How can we just listen to this fact as a passing story and forget about it?

After that, there were no particular incidents, and it was late, so we thanked the clapper and parted ways, it was 3:20 AM.

『잘데가 업다니 집이 업다는 말슴입닛가?』 이번에는 三石君이 물엇다.
『아니요. 집이 잇기는 잇서도 남의 집이 되서 못드러 가고 여기서 잔답니다.』
『아니 남의 집이 되서 못드러가다니... 왜 그집에서 내쫏겻나요』
靑年君의 계속 質問이다.
『아니요. 그럿치도 안탑니다... 이럿케 작고 물어주시니 자서한 말슴을 하지요. 제의 고향은 저 安城인데 살님이 구차해서 요몃달 전에 서울로 와서 누구의 소개로 바로 요압 00이라는 日本사람의 官舍에서 오마니(下女)노릇을 해주고 宿食을 하며 한달에 얼마되지 안는 돈을 바더서 싀골 남편에게 보내주는터인데 요몃칠 전브터 우연히 병으로 꼼짝을 할 수가 엽는고로 주인에게 간청을 하고 始*郡에 잇는 親戚의 집에 가 靜養을 하고 잇*가 오늘에야 병이 나은 듯하기에 ** 드러오노라고 드러온 것이 아모것도 타지 안코 거러오는 거름이되야 새로 한시나 되게 도착이 되엿스니 도도한 손님도 안닌 내가 자는 주인을 께여 이르킬 수도 업고 * 갑작이 차저갈 집도 업서서 할 수업시 **에서 자고 내일 아츰에 차저 드러갈 생각을＜37＞ 하고 잇섯든 것임니다』
이럿케 말하고 그의 두눈에는 눈물이 맷치엿다.
일행은 이 눈물겨운 하소연을 숨소리까지 죽이고 죄다 듯고 나서
『그럿치만...』
靑年君의 말이다.
『...그럿타고 이럿케 치운 날 한데서 자면 병이 더 덧치지 안나요. 주인에게 좀 거북하드라도 문을 여러달내 보지요』
『아니요. 병이 더 덧치더라도 나는 이럿케 해야해요... 萬若에 문을 여러달나면 여러주기야 하겟지요. 그러치만 오늘밤 하로를 그럿케 편하게 자고나면 내일로 당장 나는 실직의 悲慘한 宣告를 바들는지 모른답니다... 주인의 심정이 족히 그럴 인물인걸요』
『그러면...』
三石君의 말이다.
『...우리가 지금 이길로 그 집에 가서 잘 말을 하야 아모일이 업도록 하여 드리지요』
『아니요. 그것은 안돼요. 그것은 나를 도리혀 불행하게 만드러 주시는 일이 될는지 모르니 정말 나를 위해주시려면 제발 그일만은 고만 두어주십시요』
일행은 약속이나 한듯이 일히히 입맛을 다시엿다. 그리고 세사람의 말업시 쳐다보는 시선속에는 분명히
『그러면 이 가여운 여인를 엇더케 할까』
하는 의문이 포함되엿잇섯다.
『여하간 派出所에 가서 議論을 해보는 것이 좃치 안을가요』
이것은 딱딱이의 提案이다.
『그럴 수밧게...』
우리는 이럿케 막연한 대답을 하고 그 여인을 바라보앗다. 그는 다시 哀願하는 말소리로 『파출소로 가드라도 제발 巡査가 그 집에는 가지 안토록 좀 잘 말슴을 하여주서요... 만약 그것을 약속하시지 안으면 저는 따러가지 안겟서요』
하고 자기의 결심을 표명하려는 듯이 고개를 흔드럿다.
『물론 그것은 잘 말슴해드리겟슴니다. 여하간 갓치 가십시다』
우리는 이럿케 말하고 딱딱이에게 부탁하야 그 여인를 다리고 가게 하엿다.
우리도 갓스면 좃켓지만 우리의 본색이 드러날 것이 제일 念慮스러워서 먼-곳에 서서 하희를 기다리기로 하엿다.
약 20분이나 지난 후 단여오는 딱딱이에게 그 여자는 파출소 안에서 재워보내겟다는 말을 듯고 겨우 안심이 되엿다.
여하간 가여운 일이다.
남의 집에 가 더욱이 異國人의 집에 가서 말할 수업시 고생스러운 그 賤한 職業이나마 떠러질까바 그 위험한 노숙을 하려는 그 여자의 신상이 얼마나 불상하냐.
그러나 이것은 그 여자 하나의 當面된 苦境이 아니다. 朝鮮여자로서 오히려 그보다 더 難境에 처해 잇는 이가 얼마나 만흔 것을 아러야한다.
우리는 엇더케 이 사실을 귀ㅅ결에 들니는 한 지나가는 이약이로 듯고 바려바릴 수 잇슬 문제이랴?
그 후에는 별반 사건도 업고 또 시간도 늣고 하야 딱딱이에게 致謝를 하고 各其 헤여지니 오전 3시 20분이엿다.＜38＞
