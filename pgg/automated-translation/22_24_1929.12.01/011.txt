Snow
						Kim Ip

The snow falls, rustling and dense,
Indistinctly showing the white shadows of the mountains and rivers.

It deliberately blows against me with the slanting wind,
Who knows what the heavens and the earth are playing at?

A lonely crane by the stream is sorrowful and speechless,
Forcibly approaching the old woman at the shop, I ask for wine.

The cold crows at the end of the branches are frozen and cannot fly,
Vaguely, drunk I lie down and forget about returning home. <5>
