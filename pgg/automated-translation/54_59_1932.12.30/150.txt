Magazine Title	別乾坤 제59호
Publication Date	1932년 12월 30일
Article Title	新年劈頭 막걸리 大將 선술집 巡閱記 (New Year's First: A Makgeolli General's Tavern Inspection Journal)
Author	從軍記者 裵爾賓 (From the Battlefield Reporter, Pae Ěl-bin)
Article Type	회고·수기 (Reminiscence/Memoir)

They call me the Makgeolli General, but to be honest, I'm a general who's less than even a common soldier. Instead of a gold medal, I wear tattered clothes, and for the past ten years, I've been wearing a black silk overcoat, stained white from the collar to the hem with makgeolli. You could say this is the Makgeolli General's medal. When people see a gold medal, their eyes widen and they bow their heads, but the Makgeolli General's makgeolli medal makes everyone wrinkle their noses. Even the Minister of Home Affairs turns up his nose. No matter how insignificant it seems, it's not like a pan'im (判任), ch'ik'im (勅任), or ch'in'im (親任) official's path, but it's something earned through considerable struggle and effort. All you talented people and beggars of the world, I hope you understand and respect this.

Since I've started talking, I'll tell you the story of how I became the General, a tale that made people shake their heads, calling me "General, Teacher, General, General," from the first moment I got that title.

Even before I became as famous as I am today, I had a certain reputation, but after the "Makgeolli Barrel Incident" (막걸리통사건) at "Ilsamshil" (一三室), I became known throughout the land. There might be people who don't know Geumgangsan (금강산), but there isn't a single person walking around Seoul's Jongno (서울종로) who doesn't know the amazing rumors of this man, no, this General.

What kind of incident was it? One day, I felt uneasy, and more than wanting to drink, I felt like I needed to drink makgeolli to melt the ice in my heart. I lay down from noon, wrapped in blankets and pillows, sighing for something to happen. Then, out of nowhere, a gust of wind blew, and rain poured down like a waterfall. Damn it! I was in despair. My wife's earrings, which I'd been meaning to pawn, were now out of reach, and the hope I'd been clinging to, the hope that a friend who'd go to heaven after death would come and say, "Feeling hungry? Let's go for a drink!" was shattered. I pulled the blanket over my head, bit my lip, and tried to calm down before falling into a daze. Suddenly, I heard someone shout, "Hey, you!" It was the Minister of Home Affairs waking me up, and the children were shouting, "Daddy, Daddy!"

I went outside and saw one of my close friends, someone who could drink as much as me, had brought someone to see me. I was overjoyed. It felt like makgeolli was already going down my throat.

I immediately realized they'd come to buy me a drink, and based on my experience, I knew what kind of person the unknown friend was and what would happen next.

After exchanging greetings, the three of us braved the wind and rain and headed to Jongno. On the way, we passed several makgeolli houses where the bubbling makgeolli was being poured, but I resisted their offers to have just one drink each. In fact, only those with our experience would understand, but when you're dying for a drink and think you're about to have a great time, you become cautious and humble.

We arrived in Jongno and entered "Ilsamok" (一三屋). The original name of this place was "Samilok" (三一屋), but somehow the name got twisted to "Ilsamok." Anyone who drinks makgeolli and knows a bit about the history of March 1st (三月一日) would guess why it was called "Samilok." Whatever the modern name, the taste of the alcohol and the quality of the side dishes were once good compared to other places, but nowadays it's nothing like it used to be. And there are so many rats running across the side dish plates that it's not a pleasant sight. But my appetite was set, and as I stood there trembling, waiting for the side dishes to cook, I could hear the rain and wind gradually die down, and the drunken ramblings of other customers from both sides. We were the only ones there at first, but gradually more people started to gather, and it became lively, with the smell of cooking and smoke filling the room.

"Shall we eat?"

"Yes."

As we got into the swing of things, after we'd each downed five bowls of makgeolli, I said, "Hey, I don't think I can have more than a few more," and the server said, "Judging by the looks of you, you won't be able to do more than eight." But when our group all surpassed ten bowls and showed no signs of stopping, she didn't say anything but looked quietly surprised. And why wouldn't she be? I am now someone who has acquired the position of General, and my two friends, they are now undeniable authorities in the world of makgeolli, more than qualified to be colonels. Suddenly, these words burst out in front of us:

"Do you have the guts to drink three more bowls of makgeolli?"

"Of course, that's nothing!"

"Ah, that's the courage of Napoleon!"

Looking closer, I saw they were just some ordinary people, a group of three. But what's so great about drinking three bowls of makgeolli? What's so Napoleon-like about it? Even drinking twenty bowls is nothing to brag about, and if you brag about it, then what about me, the General? Hearing this, I felt a surge of unpleasantness, like dealing with a rude child.

"Pour the alcohol!"

"More makgeolli?"

"Yes!"

All eyes were on us. But we continued to drink steadily. Makgeolli, unlike other alcohols, has a strong competitive spirit and also creates a lot of foolishness. One large bowl fills you up, and after three or four, your stomach swells like a drum, and you get scared that you can't drink any more. But at times like that, if you just set your mind to it, you can do anything. But who can have another bowl? Who but me, the great me? That's why even after drinking three bowls, I was thinking presumptuous things like "the courage of Napoleon?" That's where the competitive spirit comes in. When the server, with a look of alarm as if saying "you can't possibly," asked, "More makgeolli?" and we readily agreed, the people who had the courage of Napoleon were embarrassed.

"Should we pour makgeolli too?"

One of them asked.

"Yes,"

the madam said as she poured the alcohol. The server who was like a flower bud earlier was gone, and now an old madam had come instead.

"No, no, I'll just have yakju - I'll have yakju,"

said one of them, the one who had ordered makgeolli.

"Hey, what kind of cowardly act is that? Can't a man drink one more bowl of that liquor? If you have the guts, then go ahead."

So the three of them simultaneously lifted the heavy bowls of alcohol. They drank with what seemed like a brave and daring adventure, and after succeeding, they laughed loudly and triumphantly.

"Really, makgeolli is a man's drink. Even if women eat all the food men eat in this world, when it comes to makgeolli, no matter what, they have to step aside."

Their offensive behavior was unbearable for our Makgeolli General group, but their words were pleasing. In fact, makgeolli is a man's drink, and even if you search every corner of the world, there's no other food that women can't eat. Thinking this, we unconsciously ordered three more bowls of makgeolli, and our group began drinking again. I believe this would have looked impressive to others, like the actions of a general's group. After we had several drinks in a very ordinary manner, some people might think I'm lying or exaggerating in a makgeolli-like way, but it's not an exaggeration or a lie. Our group drank fifteen bowls per person, a total of forty-five bowls for all three. A great number. The noise around us grew louder, and now everyone started looking at us with respect, as if they were ready to act like mice in front of a cat. That night, we drank countless times, and a big fight broke out. Some people respected and feared our group, but others were jealous and hated us. So, some people called us "Makgeolli Generals" as a title of respect, but <49> some people despised us and called us "Makgeolli Barrels," which led to a battle. Even after the fight, we drank again. Of course - I was the first to be recognized, and from then on, we decided to use the title "Makgeolli General" instead of "Makgeolli Barrel," and I was officially awarded the General's Medal among the three of us. It was a truly memorable day.

○

Now, I'll briefly introduce the taverns in Seoul that I frequent. I already talked about "Samilok," no, "Ilsamok," but the place that I, the General, like the most is a secret, so I won't reveal it carelessly. Next, if you go up to Sogyeok-dong (소격동) past Gyeonggi High School (제일고등보통학교), there's "Mindae Tteok House" (민대떡집). What I like about it is that, unlike other places, it's a makgeolli-focused tavern, and the side dishes are old-fashioned: pig intestines (sundae, 순대), and bindae-tteok (빈대떡) grilled thick like siru-tteok (시루떡), made with pig fat and chunks of pork. It's a specialty. Of course, if you just want to say "tavern," there are places in Jongno that are several times bigger and have more abundant side dishes, truly delicacies, but those are just "fancy taverns," and their connection to makgeolli isn't that strong, so they don't appeal to me. If a reader goes to this "Bindae-tteok House" and offers their parents a piece of sundae or bindae-tteok, or even a bowl of makgeolli, they will surely be a filial son.

If we're talking about fame, "Hyeongje Tavern" (형제주점) outside Dongdaemun (동대문) is the first that comes to mind. As a friend said,

"It's even been in the newspapers!"

There's no need to say more. But the regrettable thing about Hyeongje Tavern is that it's not very clean around Dongdaemun. It's not that the other things aren't clean, but the bathroom is too dirty and grotesque. If you're drunk, it might not matter, but since I'm a General, I don't often get drunk, and seeing such a grotesque scene makes me feel heavy and uneasy rather than disgusted. But when the cold wind blows and I think of ch'ut'ang (추탕), I feel like I should go to Hyeongje Tavern, and the newspaper ad saying "Hyeongje Tavern's ch'ut'ang is good. Everyone come!" is a refreshing change from the usual ads that make you frown, and it naturally makes me want to get on the streetcar and go there.

Another unique place is "Erosseobiseu" (에로써비스) or something like that. I don't know, but it's a fact that everyone who pours the alcohol, cooks the side dishes, and serves the soup is a girl in the store, about seven or eight of them bustling around. If that's unique, then it's unique. If you want to see it, ask at the Hwanggeumjeong (황금정) police box. It's right across the street.

The biggest places are on the way to Namdaemun (남대문), just past the market, where there are two places that look similar and face each other. Both are quite large. But their business policy is focused on restaurants, or rather, rice houses, so they're not as interesting.

For gifts, "Daeheung Tavern" (대흥주점) in Jongno would be suitable. Their soup is the most famous. There are even people who can't live without Daeheung Tavern's galbi soup (갈비국), so you can imagine. I, the General, am actually quite fond of it.

And above it is "Mongno Tavern" (목노술집). The name is "Mongno Tavern." It probably has the longest history. I am deeply moved when I see that the girl who used to pour alcohol when I was still a runny-nosed kid is now the madam.

And across the street is "Bupyongnu" (부벽루), but for some reason, the General isn't friendly with it.

Also, next to the Western-style restaurant "Baekhapwon" (백합원) is a nameless tavern. It looks shabby from the outside, but if you go inside and look at the side dishes, you'll be tempted. It's famous for yukhoe (육회). It's nothing like some cheap dishes.

Since we're talking about side dishes, there's a place that gives out side dishes as a bonus. Where is it? It's across from "Changmunsa" (창문사) alley in Seodaemun (西大門).

In the Seodaemun area, Songwǒldong (松月洞) sundae (순대) house is famous, and if you go out to Mapo (마포) and go into Hyeonjeo-dong (현저동) in the middle, there are many good places, but I'll tell you more about that next time. Also, there's a place that's famous for its chapssal (찹쌀) makgeolli, but I've fought with them several times because they add saccharin (사카링).

I have a lot more to say about "Ch'unwǒnok" (春園屋), about our taverns, about the delicious and nutritious side dishes, about how you can eat makgeolli all year round without getting fat, and about how to calculate a month's worth of food based on the side dishes, but because I've exceeded the length of the manuscript requested by Gaebyǒksa (開闢社), I'll stop here and go be a General at my secret makgeolli house." <50>

막걸리대장 막걸리대장해도 사실 지고본다면 총대멘병정만도못한대장이다. 금치훈장대신에 지지궁상을 해가지고바지저고리 근십년동안을 걸치고단긴 검정라단두루마기우으로 동정서부터 단지 주―ㄱ막걸리자욱이허여케낫다. 말할테면 이것이 명색 막걸리대장의 훈장이다. 금치훈장을 보면 세상사람이 두눈이후둥그레지고그압헤고개를 숙이게 되지만 이 막걸리대장의 막걸리훈장에는 남녀노소가모다 눈살을 흐리고 우선우라내무대신(內務大臣)이 박아지를 극는 아무리 허수하게보이는벼슬이나 판임관 칙임관 친임관의 경로와는못하다할망정 상당한 분투노력으로 어더걸린게어늘 만천하재사걸인이시여 마히 현명한리해가잇스며 동시에 존경하는마음이 잇스라.
이왕말문이 열린판이니 내가 이 대장어되기지의력사 사람들이 헤를내두르고 대장나리 선생님 대장대장하고 첫밧들든순간의 일을 들리여주기로한다.
내가오늘처럼 이러케크게 유명하여 지기원에도 상당한 명성과 정평는잇섯지만은 지금부터 이야기할려는 「一三室」의 「막걸리통사건」이후로는 그야말로 천하에 일홈놉은 우리금수강산의 「금강산」을 몰으는사람은 잇서도서울종로로 신발뒤금치달퀴며돌아다니는 인사치고는 이사람 아―니이대장각하의 굉장한 소문을 아지못하는 양반은 엄게되엿든 것이다.
그사건이 어한것이엿드냐하면 하로는 마음도 시원치못한데다가 먹고 십다느니보다도 먹고나야만 마음속이어름덩이풀리듯할 막걸리한사발을 못먹어 속이씻리듯하야 요고 이불덥고 벼개비고 해가 한오정부터드러누어서 이지라고 한숨을쉬며잇스려니 난데엄는 일진광풍이불더니만 폭포수쏘다지듯 비가 퍼부엇다 엠병을할! 이러구보니인제는 절망하엿다. 임대것망성거리고잇든 안해의남바귀를 전당잡혀햇든것도 이러케날새가 변하고보니 욕심에 리운 결심이 한풀기고 속으로 은근히바라고바라서 그야말로 일루의 희망을 부치고잇든 어느죽어서 천당갈친구가 차저와서
「출출한대 자네 막걸리나 한잔하러가세!」하는 장면을 몽상하고 잇든 것이 고만 여지엄시 여지고 마럿다. 이불을 푹뒤집어쓰고아주 입술을 물며 단렴을 하고나서 아케어섬푸렷하게잠이드럿는대 벼란간
「얘 이자식아!」
하고 외치는소리가 들렷다. 뒤밋처우리내무대신이 나를 우고 일변 어린아이들이 「압바압바」를 부르며 야단이엿다.
내다보니 나와두리서 술잘먹기로 웬만치치는 참그러구두 친밀한동무의 한사람이 누구를 데리고 나를 차저왓든 것이다. 나는 순간 뭇척반가웟다. 하여간금방 막걸 리가 목구녕으로 넘어가는듯하엿스니 두말할나워가엄지 안은가
그만치 언듯보아 그들두사람이나를 술사주러왓구나하고 즉각적으로 달엇스며 한동시에 오늘지의 경험으로 술친구가 데리고온그몰으는 친구가 어한종류의 사람이며 장차무슨일이전개되리라는 것을 지래짐작햇기문이엿다.
우리는 인사를 마친다음 내가 예상햇든바와가티 세사람은 바람불고 비퍼붓는일긔를 무릅쓰고 종로통으로 내려섯다. 오다가도중에서 비지만부내려섯다. 오다가도중에서 비지만부글부글여노코잇는 사발막걸리집에서 그들돌이몃번이나 한잔식만하고서내려가자는 것을 나는 정신을 가다듬을겸 사양아닌사양을 햇든 것이다. 사실  우리만한경력을 못가진사람은 알수엄는 심정이지마는 그러케주리를 틀게술이먹고십허죽겟다가도 올치지금부터 술을 굉장하고 야단스럽게먹을판이로구나하고 생각을 먹게되는 순간부터는 어지술이그러케지는 그립지안타느니보다도 어지간히조심하는마음이 생겨서 알수엄는겸손을 부리게 되는법이만흔 것이다.
종로통을 당도하야 「일삼옥」으로드러섯다. 이짐은 본일홈이「三一屋」이엿섯는데 그후어케되여서 집일홈이 구로 「一三屋」이되여버렷든 것이다. 막걸리사발을 기우리며다니는 친구래도 三月一日이라는 날사의력사를 손톱만치만알어도 「삼일옥」으로된 닭을 짐작하리라밋는다. 현대 일홈운 그러타고하고 술맛이라든지＜48＞ 안주의 설비도 한참에는 다른데비하야 별로손색이엄드니 요즈음은 전에다대면 말아닌판이요  쥐가만허서 안주접시위로 신장로를 내고다니는 것을 볼 그리반갑지는못한것이엿다. 그러치만 구미가 잔기든판이라 덜덜거리며 붓자마서라 몃곱행이를먹고 뒷짐을 턱집고 버틔고서서 안주익을나 바라고잇스려니 비오는 소리며 바람일른소리가 어느듯치고 저-편 이편에서술취한친구들의 주정부리는 소리가들려오고 하엿다. 우리외에는 사람이하나도엄든 것이 차차모이기시작하고 들석해젓스며 안주익는냄새와연긔가 그야말로 실내에 애애하엿다.
「드시죠-」
「네-」
이러케 우리는 더욱더 어우러지는 판인데 아우리가 막걸리다섯사발식을 넘기며
「에이 인제 뭐몃잔못하겟는걸-」

「참-막걸리라는 것은 남자의 음식이지 이세상에서 남자먹는 음식을 여자가 다먹어도 이막걸리에 와서만은 제아무리뭣해도 질차레지-」
