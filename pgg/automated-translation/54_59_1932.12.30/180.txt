長篇講談 滄海力士(第十二回)
月明山人 述, 金奎澤 畵

Originally, Hu Yaja, after being attacked by a tiger in Cheoljukwon, initially lost consciousness but regained his strength. He found that one leopard was carrying him, and another was leading the way. Arriving at a place, they put him down. Hu Yaja thought, "Are they going to eat me here?" and lamented in his heart, saying:

"Once I pass this moment, my bubble-like body and smoke-like soul will be digested in the cruel tiger's intestines, and I will forget all the aching, stinging, sadness, and fear. But my parents and siblings will constantly cry out my name in a pathetic voice and call all the gwanposu and chonposu (官捕手, 村砲手 - official and village hunters) to hunt the fierce tigers of Odaesan. Even if they catch all the fierce tigers of Odaesan, how will they know that my flesh and bones have disappeared inside which tiger?"

Thinking like this, two streams of tears blurred his vision in the morning. Suddenly, two leopards darted out from the forest and fought with the two leopards. He lamented again, saying:

"My fate is originally to be food for tigers and leopards. Since I'm going to die anyway, what difference does it make whether it's a leopard or a tiger? But logically speaking, it is right for the one who contributed to eat."

He sat idly by, waiting for the outcome of the battle. Indeed, the leopard was defeated and died, and the leopard picked up the leopard's corpse and dug a hole with its two feet to bury it. Yaja was moved and said, "They say Joseon is a country of etiquette; it is indeed true. Even such an animal buries the corpse of its enemy."

Now, he was just waiting for the moment of his death. The leopard carried him again and climbed up a steep cliff. On top of the cliff was a hut, and in front of the hut, an old woman was holding a lamp in one hand and an iron staff in the other. She scolded the leopard, and the leopard dared not resist. It threw Yaja down and ran away. The old woman embraced Yaja and laid him down in the hut. At this time, Yaja fainted again and became unconscious. The old woman gave him baekbitang (白沸湯 - plain boiled water) to drink and massaged his limbs, and he finally came back to life. She boiled and fed him acorn porridge, and he barely drank a spoonful. Pearl-like tears fell like rain from his ears, and he regained consciousness. At this time, Yaja's cloud-like hair was scattered, and his silk skirt was torn to shreds. Yaja roughly gathered his hair and clothes and asked the old woman:

"Where is this place, and who are you, old woman? What kind of power do you have that you can command fierce beasts and save people?"

The old woman replied:

"This place is Jawoonam (慈雲庵) in Odaesan, and I am an old woman named Soo Orang (손오랑). Originally, I was not a human but a monkey who cultivated the Dao for over seven hundred years and transformed into a human. I could not bear to see animals harming people, so I have often run barefoot to save them. Tonight, I knew that a princess was being attacked by a tiger, so I waited outside the door."

Yaja got up and bowed, saying:

"How do you know me, old woman?"

The old woman smiled and did not answer, stroking Yaja's face and saying:

"Her face resembles Haja (하자) Princess, but Haja has vitality in her eyes and eyebrows, while the princess has a purple star shining in her eyes and eyebrows."

Yaja was even more surprised and asked:

"Where have you seen my sister Haja?"

The old woman smiled again and did not speak. Yaja asked again:

"What sins did I commit in my previous life that I had to suffer such a tiger attack?"

The old woman said <54>

"This is the princess's fate. The military strategist Pyo Byeon (표변) intends to use the princess in a beauty trap to plot against Changhae (창해), so the Odaesan spirit sent a fierce tiger to disrupt the marriage. Also, there is a relationship between the princess and the crown prince of Changhae."

Yaja suddenly remembered the Hamakpyeong battle and asked:

"What is the Bokmagyeong (伏魔鏡 - Demon Subduing Mirror)?"

The old woman said:

"Why do you ask about the Bokmagyeong?"

Yaja replied:

"I don't know if you've heard of it, but in the Hamakpyeong battle, Bu Pyeonghyeon Wongong (부풍현원공) was advancing triumphantly when the enemy general chanted the Bokmagyeong, and Wongong was captured. Is the Bokmagyeong that effective?"

The old woman laughed and said:

"That matter was something your sister Haja knew very well but did not say. Originally, Bu Pyeonghyeon Wongong was killed by Yo Dongnangja (요동낭자) and Yeo Minong (여민옹) in such and such a month and day, and the Wongong who appeared at Hamakpyeong was a fake Wongong. What Bokmagyeong is there?"

Yaja finally felt like she was waking up from a deep dream.

One day, Son Orang took Yaja up to a high peak, looked southeast, pointed to an island scattered in the middle of the sea, and said:

"There is a place in the sea where a Seonjin (선진 - enlightened person) can play the role of a tyrant. Do not listen to Byeon and return to tell your father. If you try to plot against Changhae, you will inevitably face disaster."

That night, Son Orang chanted a few incantations, and suddenly two leopards came and knelt on the ground. She immediately ordered them to protect the princess and return her to her homeland. The two tigers carried Yaja as before towards Geumnan Cave (금난동). This time, Yaja did not lose consciousness, but she could not remember the mountains and rivers she passed, only the sound of the wind in her ears. Before dawn, she found herself falling into the Cheoljukwon pond.

At this time, Seo Jin-in and his wife had invited dozens of shamans to perform a gut (굿 - shamanic ritual) in Cheoljukwon for Yaja's spirit. A shaman with a good voice shook a bamboo branch and sang, "Seo Jin-in's thousand pieces of gold, Useok Jin-rang's ten thousand pieces of gold, this body like white jade is buried without a grave in the deep valley of Mancheop Cheongsan. Alas, how pitiful is my fate," making everyone lament. Seo Jin-in and his wife couldn't stop crying. Hu Yaja, regaining her senses from the bottom of the Cheoljukwon pond, went to her parents and bowed. Her family was shocked and ran away, and the shamans were also frightened and fled. Helpless, she went to her room, only to find that the golden bedding and mirror had been burned, leaving only the bed. Waiting for the dawn, she tidied her clothes and went to see her parents, telling them everything she had suffered.

Seo Mok (서목) sent an envoy to Changhae again to propose marriage, but Yaja strongly dissuaded him, saying:

"Marriage is the beginning of human relations and the source of all blessings, but Military Strategist Pyo Byeon intended to use a beauty trap, causing me to suffer the punishment of the mountain spirit. Also, the words of Son Orang, a divine person of Jawoonam, are such and such. Please give up all thoughts of marriage and seek to leave Changhae and enter the sea."

Seo Mok was silent and said nothing.

Useok Jin-rang said:

"Yaja is eighteen years old, and marriage is late. It is right to marry Oh Ja-won Orang (오자원오랑)."

Yaja dissuaded again, saying:

"The people of Changhae despise our people because they do not forbid consanguineous marriages. I have heard the mocking words of the Changhae people: 'No virgin goes out of the gate, and no magpie comes into the fence.' I hate to hear those mocking words."

Seo Mok heard those words and thought for a while, then said:

"The son of Deputy Minister Jeo Sam-rang (저삼랑), Jeo Ae-rang (저애랑), is very handsome and capable. He is a suitable candidate for son-in-law."

He immediately called Jeo Sam-rang to arrange the marriage, but Yaja said:

"Although Jeo Ae-rang is handsome, his face is full of peach blossom energy, indicating that he is licentious and will surely fail."

Seo Mok said:

"Then tell me who is a suitable young man."

Yaja said:

"There is no suitable person. Originally, all the myriad worries of life arise from the day of marriage. Why buy worries that you don't have? I will never marry and will serve my parents for the rest of my life."

At this time, Changhae's relative Lee Gong-hwa (이공화), hearing that Hu Yaja had survived the tiger attack, introduced Pyo Byeon, as the royal marriage would be broken, to marry his son. Pyo Byeon said to Seo Bok:

"Lee Gong-hwa is Changhae's Buwongun (부원군 - father-in-law of the king), the most powerful in the court and the most trusted by the king. If we make a relationship of mutual assistance, wouldn't it be difficult to plot against Changhae?"

Yaja, hearing those words, raised her eyebrows like swords and scolded Pyo Byeon in a clear voice:

"Is the only plan hidden in Military Strategist Pyo's heart a beauty trap? Lee Gong-hwa, a relative of Changhae, is a traitor who receives bribes from the enemy. I would rather die at the hands of a fierce tiger in Odaesan than become the daughter-in-law of a corrupt official."

Finishing her words, she drew a dagger to kill herself, but Seo Bok grabbed her hand to stop her. Pyo Byeon was greatly ashamed and retreated.

Changhae Traitors Receive Bribes from the Enemy, and Yeo Bang's Soldiers are Disbanded

Seo Bok called Pyo Byeon and said:

"Changhae has compensated for the national debt and returned the prisoners, but they have not returned Won Il-rang (원일랑) and his wife because they are waiting to return the Eighteen Heavens (십팔동천). The situation is not favorable, so I want to abandon this place and enter the sea."

Pyo Byeon said:

"Military strategy says that if you advance, there must be a place to take, and if you retreat, there must be a place to defend. Now, if we head aimlessly into the vast sea and the enemy general Yeo Bang (여방) ambushes us from behind, it would be like seeking our own destruction. In my opinion, we should have the King of Changhae strip Yeo Bang of his military power and then slowly look for a place to defend and enter the sea."

Seo Bok said:

"Where is there a place to defend?"

Pyo Byeon said:

"If you go southeast for seven hundred li by water, there is an island called Usando (우산도). That island is very vast and sufficient to defend. It originally belonged to Changhae but has not been under Changhae's control since it was developed. It is better to take that island first and make it our base."

Seo Bok said:

"Is that island uninhabited?"

Pyo Byeon replied:

"The island has many people and abundant resources. It originally belonged to Changhae, but Changhae has not been concerned about it since its development."

Seo Bok said:

"I entrust everything to you. Do your best to avoid failure."

Pyo Byeon bribed Changhae traitors Do Jeon-jak (도전작) and others to persuade the king to strip Yeo Won-su (여원수)'s military power and appoint Yeo Bang as a gakgan (閣干 - high official rank) to manage internal affairs and return the prisoners Won Il-rang and his wife. Seo Bok was overjoyed.

Yeo Won-su received the decree to disband his soldiers and made a great determination to report to the king, saying:

"It is reckless to disband the soldiers before returning the Eighteen Heavens."

But the king did not listen and said:

"If they do not return the Heavens, what difficulty is there in recruiting soldiers again?"

Yeo Won-su could not advise again and withdrew.

He opened up the stored grain and supplies one by one and comforted the soldiers, saying:

"I cannot express how I feel to be separated from you, who have shared life and death for the sake of the country. However, I cannot disobey the king's orders. Each of you should return home to comfort your parents, wives, and children and spend your days joyfully."

"Yeo Bang has not repaid the debt to the country, so I cannot return to my hometown with you."

Before he could finish speaking, two streams of tears fell on his military uniform, and the soldiers' cries shook Hamakpyeong all at once.

On this day, a great feast was prepared, and the generals were gathered to say farewell. Won-su said:

"In the past, you were bound by military law and careful because of your ranks. But today, as we disband the soldiers, what is military law or rank? Each of you should drink to your heart's content and say everything in your heart without hiding anything."

He personally held a cup and offered it in turn. No-yak-su (노약수) was not happy and said:

"Today, when there is no restraint, waiting for the commander's cup is also a restraint."

He picked up an entire wine jar and drank freely until he was drunk. He drew his sword and danced, singing:

"Our parents, Cheonsin Mago (천신마고 - heavenly gods and goddesses),

Bore and raised No-yak-su

Eight-foot tall with clear features

I couldn't use all my courage and was wronged in the war, losing both ears.

If I return to the underworld and my parents ask, what shall I answer?"

Finishing the song, he threw down his sword and laughed loudly to the sky. Won-su said sadly, "General No's song is truly moving. Today, as we part ways, let us each speak of our future and wishes."

Hong Cheon-bi (홍천비), Hong Mal-rok (홍말록), No Deung-hae (노등해), and Song Jo (송조) and a few others replied in unison:

"We will stay with you, Commander."

Pi Tong (피통) said:

"I wish to return to Gudu Mountain (구두산) and become the Guduswaesu (구두쇄수 - water master of Gudu) again."

Yeon Min-ong (연민옹) reported:

"I will hide in the mountains with my mother and wait for my father to return."

Heo Bi (허비) and No-yak-su were drinking and did not speak. Won-su asked them repeatedly. Heo Bi said:

"I was originally a hunter. On winter days, when the snow was falling and the morning wind was stinging my ears, I would carry a bottle of alcohol on my waist and an iron bar on my shoulder and enter the deep mountains. I couldn't catch a single rabbit or hare, but I met a big mountain tiger. I threw the iron bar and hugged it with both arms, rolling around and around. In the end, I struck the decisive blow with my fist, drew my dagger, cut open its belly, drank a ladle of hot blood, lit a fire on a rock, grilled the meat, and drank the whole bottle of alcohol. That refreshing pleasure, I thought there was nothing else to want. I happened to become a general under you, defeating the enemy general and seizing the enemy's flag, which would have been another pleasure, but that's all. I wish to return to the mountains and become a hunter again."

Won-su clapped his knees and sighed, then asked No-yak-su. Yak-su said:

"No-yak-su was originally a boatman. When the weather was bad, I would do my best to safely transport urgent travelers. The boat fare was given generously, and the wine was poured generously. When there were no travelers, I would get drunk and fall asleep in the boat, not knowing where the waves were carrying me. Suddenly, I would wake up to the sound of 'Boatman, boatman!' and see some official wanting to cross the river. If I was even a little slow, I would be slapped and scolded countless times, but even then, the boat fare was never discussed. At that time, I hated and envied the officials."

Won-su burst into laughter and offered him a cup of wine. Yak-su accepted it and continued:

"I have become a great general under you, so I can say that my wish to envy officials has been fulfilled. But I think that being an official is not something an ignorant person should do."

Won-su said:

"Who dares to mock your ignorance, General?"

He replied:

"The relatives Lee Gong-hwa and Yu Rap (유랍) call me a 'black-skinned, white-intestined' (흑질백장) and a 'sunpungle' (순풍뢰). Because of my ignorance, I didn't know what they meant. I asked a learned person, and they said that 'black-skinned, white-intestined' means that the body is black and the intestines are white, meaning that not a drop of ink has entered my intestines. 'Sunpungle' means that the wind passes smoothly through the empty space, which is mocking the fact that I have no ears. How can I bear to hold office while receiving such mockery?"

He got up, said farewell, picked up his sword, and left. That day, the soldiers all parted ways in sorrow.
