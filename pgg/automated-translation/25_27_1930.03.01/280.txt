Impressions Before and After Meeting "Imagination"

Mr. Pak Hui-do
Pang Chong-hwan

I have heard the name of Mr. Pak Hui-do since I was sixteen or seventeen years old. Because he wasn't a writer, there was no way to know him through the pages of a book, and because I wasn't a Christian, I never had the opportunity to meet him at any gatherings. However, almost everyone who told me about their financial difficulties or discussed their worries would say, "I consulted with Mr. Pak Hui-do," or "Thanks to Mr. Pak Hui-do's arrangement, things turned out well like this..." So, although I didn't know what he looked like or what his voice sounded like, I thought he was someone who was active in various fields and enjoyed arranging things for others, especially for young people and students. I thought he had a cheerful personality and was very sociable. Therefore, to be honest, when someone mentioned to me about finding a job, and I couldn't give a satisfactory answer, I sometimes told them to go see Mr. Pak Hui-do. Because I didn't know him, I couldn't introduce them, but I simply told them to go see him. Later, when I heard that he was among the 33 people in the Kimyo Independence Movement (己未年) and was in charge of arranging things, especially for the student side, I nodded to myself, thinking that it was consistent with what I had heard.

I only got to meet the teacher directly in recent years. The teacher first spoke to me on the phone regarding school matters, so I heard his voice first, and anyone could guess that he was not a conservative scholar or someone with an inflexible personality like someone carving a seal with a knife or wearing Japanese wooden clogs (日本 나막신).

Even after meeting him, I was pleased that he didn't disappoint my imagination at all with his refreshing tall height, the healthy physique that a tonic salesman would want to take a picture of, a face without any cramped or stuffy corners to be found, a hearty and savory voice, and an unpretentious manner.

Ms. Chong Chong-myong
Pak No-a

"Oh - Mr. Chŏng? If you mess with her, you'll get slapped and not even know what happened. Anyway, that woman is liberated!"
"Oh, is it Lady Chŏng Gom-bo (鄭곰보)? The pockmarked Lady Chŏng? Haha... Still, she's a veteran in the women's movement."

When Ms. Chŏng Chong-myŏng was mentioned, I had heard people who knew her make light comments while laughing several times. Each time, as someone who hadn't met her directly, I imagined, based on the word "pockmarked (곰보)" that flowed from their mouths and the attitude of laughing and dismissing it with a single smile (一笑에 附하는 태도), that her appearance had been marred by smallpox (마마) and that, while sharply rebuffing men's old-fashioned advances or playful (戱弄的) behavior towards women, she might not be a delicate and graceful young woman but still have a round, auspicious face, even if it was slightly marred, and a sturdy physique like a man. I even longed to meet her once, but we could not meet easily because our lives were different. Then, about five or six years ago, when I visited Mr. Sin Ch'ŏl (辛鐵), a classmate and comrade, in Takejo-cho (竹添町), I unexpectedly found Ms. Chŏng Chong-myŏng, his friend and lover. Ms. Chŏng was lying down, facing the wall, as if she was uncomfortable. At the time, I had not heard any news that the two were living together, so I thought to myself, 'I wonder what kind of woman she is, or if they are close friends?' I quickly glanced at her slightly disheveled hair and slender figure, neither too fat nor too thin, and sent Mr. Sin a meaningless smile, seemingly trying to find traces of amorous fortune (艶福의 자최) on his stern and joyful face, but his expression did not move at all. As I was about to withdraw my awkward smile, I met the face of the woman who turned over with a groan ("으응-"). At that moment, my face flushed red as if I had been embarrassed, and my heart seemed to drop. However, in the next moment, I felt inexplicably relieved and felt ashamed of the groundless greed of men who, upon hearing "woman," immediately imagine and expect so-called beauties that can sink fish and fell geese (沉魚落雁) and the grace of a hibiscus (芙蓉의 자태). At that time, I learned that she was Ms. Chŏng (鄭女史) and saw Mr. Sin gently stroking her stomach over her skirt, asking in a kind tone, "Are you still in pain? Is your stomach still cramping?", and I felt an inexplicable emotion. The uneasy expectation that had been thinking about beauty conditions on top of the word "pockmarked (얼것단)" completely disappeared. However, when I thought that Ms. Chŏng was already over thirty years old, had a grown son, had a midwife's license (産婆의 免許狀), was the head of the Women's Poor Students Assistance Association (女子苦學生相助會會長), a member of the Women's Friendship Association (女性同友會委員), and the chairman of the Kŭn'u Society (槿友會委員長), and had been steadfastly maintaining her principles and fighting hard (惡戰苦鬪) since her early aspirations, I felt a friendly feeling as if I were meeting a family aunt. Ms. Chŏng, regardless of gender, is never awkward in front of anyone, is good at joking with her skillful eloquence (口辯), and sometimes her sentimental (쎈티멘탈한) emotionalism flows from her mouth. It is said that once, she showed her white arms like white fish (白魚) to several friends and said while patting them with her own hands, "Well, even if my face is marred, I have physical beauty (肉톄미)!" causing much laughter, whether it was a lament or a boast.

Mr. Kim Ki-t'aek
Ch'ae Man-sik

Among the names that I associated with the name Kaebyŏksa (開闢社) in the past, the most absurd association I made, thinking about it now, was with Mr. Kim Ki-t'aek.

I thought he would be tall and slender, look modern (not a handsome man), be grumpy, like to drink, and be a boisterous person who likes to have fun.

It was only last October that I met Mr. Kim Ki-t'aek for the first time.

Until then, I had only thought that Mr. Kim was in charge of the practical affairs of Kaebyŏksa (開闢社).

When the person introducing him said, "Mr. Kim Ki-t'aek, who used to work with us at Kaebyŏksa (開闢社) for a long time and is now working at the Ch'ŏngnyŏndang (青年黨) (Ch'ŏndogyo)," I couldn't help but look at him again.

The fact that he was no longer in charge of the practical affairs of Kaebyŏksa (開闢社) was also unexpected, and his appearance was completely different from what I had imagined.

He wasn't short, but he was broad, with a wide face and a wide mouth, and his eyes were round and blank, making him look somewhat timid...

And when I sat down to talk with him, he was like a person deeply immersed in religious faith (宗敎信仰), speaking very kindly (상냥하듸 상냥하고), and his expression and small gestures showed a calmness like that of a philosopher.

I haven't had a deep acquaintance with Mr. Kim yet, so I don't know the other aspects hidden inside, but that was my first impression.

Mr. Song Chin-u
Kim Wŏn-ju

As a young man with poor writing skills, it seems presumptuous for me to write this, and on the other hand, I am very sorry if it might be disrespectful to Mr. Song (宋先生).

Moreover, I met Mr. Song Chin-u when I took my first steps as a reporter to conduct an interview, so how could I have had the time and composure amidst the busy schedule and anxious mind to carefully observe Mr. Song's character and demeanor?

Therefore, I would like to state in advance that writing about Mr. Song's impression here is merely a light and superficial attempt, just like seeing someone passing by on the street and thinking, "Oh, that's Mr. Song Chin-u! Let me take another look to see what kind of person he is!" and then the rickshaw carrying Mr. Song quickly runs away, and you miss the chance. "Oh, right, I saw him briefly and he was like this," like writing about that fleeting impression. I had heard the name Song Chin-u (宋鎭禹), the president of the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報), several years ago. However, it was only recently that I began to imagine what kind of person he might be.

I had a vague idea that he would be of medium height, have a stout body, wear a white overcoat (두루막이), have a worried face contemplating current affairs, and have a white-streaked beard. Then, as I mentioned above, I came to see him when I was planning an interview, but I failed on the first visit, couldn't meet him on the second, and finally got to meet him on the fourth attempt, which gave me another kind of premonition. I thought he must be a very strict and stubborn old man who wouldn't even talk after a glance. I was worried about how to go in and talk to him.

However, when I finally met him, he was not the stubborn old man I had imagined.

He was not tall, wore a Western suit, and had no beard on his face. He did not show any condescending attitude towards a young person, and he did not waste a single word from beginning to end.

And although I was a stranger, he kindly and meticulously taught me as if a teacher was guiding his disciple's future or as if a father was thinking about his daughter's future. He answered every question without seeming reluctant, and he seemed to never go beyond a certain boundary while answering in a way that was not too sharp. Mr. Song (宋先生) twitched one side of his eye and face every time he spoke, which was one characteristic I didn't miss.

That's all I have to say about my impression of Mr. Song.

金起田氏

前에 開闢社라는 일흠과 한가지로 연상하는 멧분의 일흠 가운데 지금 생각하면 제일 엉터리업는 연상을 한 것은 金起田씨다.

宋鎭禹氏
金源珠

소견이 어리고 붓이 것츤 나로써 이글을 쓰기에는 넘우나 당돌한 듯하며 한편으로 宋先生에게 실례나 되지 안을가하야 먼저 미안한 생각이 넘치게 만타.
더구나 宋鎭禹씨를 맛난 것은 내가 기자생활 첫거름에 인터뷰-를 갓슬때 이니 밧분 시간과 조리는 마음에 어느 틈에 침착하게 氏의 성격과 풍채를 살필 여유가 잇섯스랴.
그리하여 미리 말하여두노니 여긔에 氏의 인상을 쓰는 것은 다만 길거리에 지나가시는 이를 보고 오- 저분이 宋鎭禹씨야! 엇떠한 분인지 다시 한번 자세히 보랴고 할때에 氏를 태운 인력거가 훠- 다라나 버림으로 앗차 니러버렷다. 오-올치 얼는 보닛가 이러한 분이엿다하는 그 찰라의 인상을 쓰듯이 극히 가비얍게 피상만을 쓰고저한다. 東亞日報 社長 宋鎭禹라는 일흠을 들은지는 벌서 몃해 전이엿섯다. 그러나 그가 엇떠한 분일가-하고 환상을 그려본 것은 최근 일이니-
키는 큰중키 부대한 몸에 흰 두루막이를 닙고 時事를 근심하는 묵어운 얼골에 흰줄섯긴 수염이 만히 낫스리라고 대강 막연하게 생각하고 잇섯다. 그러다가 정작 보게된 것은 이상에 말한 바와 가티 인터뷰-를 계획하든 때인데 첫번가서 실패하고 두번가서 못맛나고 세번가서 연긔 네번만에야 면회를 하게 되엿스니 그때가 나에게는 또 한가지 예감을 주웟다. 한번 흘겨 보고는 말도 안할 퍽도 엄하고 완고한 로인인가부다. 어떠케 들어가서 이약이를 할가-하고 마음이 저윽히 조리웟섯다.
그러나 마츰내 면대하고보니 이때까지 생각하고잇든 그럿케 완고한 할아버지는 아니엿섯다.
크지 안은 키에 양복을 닙고 얼골에는 한울기 수염도 업섯다. 연소한 사람을 대하되 조곰도 경멸뎍 태도가 업섯고 처음부터 나종까지 말세 한 마듸 헛놋치 안엇섯다.
그리고 처음 보는 사람이것만은 선생이 뎨자의 압길을 지도하듯이 아부지가 딸의 장래를 생각하듯이 친절하게 차근차근히 가르처주엇섯다. 무엇이든 뭇는 말에 꺼리끼는 양이 보이지 안토록 별로 모나지안케 대답을 하면서도 결코 어느 범위를 넘어가지 안는듯 하엿다. 宋先生은 말끗마다 한Ž¸쪽 눈과 얼골을 매번 끔칠끔칠하엿스니 이것은 니치지안는 한가지  특징이엿다.
