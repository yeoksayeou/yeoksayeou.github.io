잡지명	別乾坤 第9號
발행일	1927년 10월 01일
기사제목	變裝出動 臨時OO되여본記, 사주쟁이가 되야 늙은이 젊은이의 신수를 보아주어 (Disguised Outing: A Record of Temporarily Becoming an OO, Becoming a Fortune-teller and Reading the Fortunes of Old and Young)
필자	D記者
기사형태	소식 (News)

Setting out on the first road
The world has become much brighter than before, they say. Thank you very much. Then, that means the world is more civilized now than it was before, right? Alright, there are trains and streetcars and automobiles now that weren't there before. Of course, there's telegraph, and there's telephone. And that's not all, there's even radio. What's that, an elegantly dressed gentleman in a Western suit! Oh my, look at that modern girl, a new-fashioned female student.
Ha! It's become very civilized. It's all science and civilization, machine civilization, new-fashioned civilization, civilization of inventions. They built Western-style houses, they invented experimental machines. And the roads are much wider, making it much more convenient to travel than in the old days. Political thought has developed, economic thought has developed, scientific thought has developed, religious thought has developed. It's a very good world. It's a bright world. The ancestors who passed away underground can't see this bright world, so they must be sighing so hard in the dark earth that the earth collapses twofold.
Listen, you are Korean, aren't you? Of course. We are also civilized Koreans like everyone else.
That's right. We are also civilized people. If there is any uncivilized aspect, we must run to a civilized place as quickly as possible, even if it's just one hour, no, one minute, one second.
But the world is such that one side is bright while the other is extremely dark, and one side is high while the other is extremely low. The sun is blazing on the south side of the mountain, but there are chunks of ice on the north side. Bright light is brilliant in front of the lamp, but darkness is blocking the back of the lamp.
The world talks about civilization and progress, but on one hand, there are still close-cropped topknots, long tobacco pipes, high horsehair hats, flowing mourning clothes, doormat-like hats, wide bean sleeves, palanquins carried by two or four people, bridal coronets, and headbands remaining as they are. The world shouts for scientific civilization and the destruction of superstition, but in Korea, Chong Doryeong (鄭道令) of Gyeryongsan (鷄龍山) is still alive, and pastors and elders perform miracles of Jesus coming back to life by touching his dead body, and in broad daylight, the Isame Dantai (異體同體) of Sungshin Association (崇神組合) openly exert their power in the capital. Female students and new-fashioned mistresses embroider silk cushions and go before Buddha to pray, pray, pray for a good husband and a jade-like son. Moreover, in rural villages and uncivilized places, what's so strange about having a firm belief that praying to the kitchen god, praying to the ancestral god, and praying on the street are the truths for having a long life, wealth, and many sons, and that reading the Okchubalhaenggyeong (玉樞八衡經) and commanding the gods and ghosts will cure diseases, as it is an unchanging principle of heaven and earth for all ages? If you bury your ancestor's bones in an auspicious site, your descendants will be emperors and kings, and if your four pillars are good, you can lie down and receive an overflowing amount of wealth. Even if it means scraping the blood and oil of others, if they live well and sell the entire land, just filling their own bellies, they are praised and admired for being blessed, having good fortune, and having the energy of the grave. A guy who doesn't know how to deceive others is rather an idiot. If parents get sick, they say it's because of disturbing the earth or wood, so they cook rice, make rice cakes, pray, and chant scriptures to get better, and if their offspring are scarce and their young son wants to divorce, then they perform a mountain grave ritual. They say you live by sweating, you live by being diligent, you live by working, you live by striving? Nonsense. You just live well if you are blessed. You just live well if you are born with good fortune. You just do well if you write the grave well. What do you say?
It's a very developed thought.
Readers, what do you think? About my preface. Thinking about it, it's disheartening and even suffocating. All of Joseon's days have passed while hoping for fortune, believing only in the four pillars, and talking only about Chong Doryeong. Even while feeling their fading blood vessels, they only read the Okchubalhaenggyeong, and after death, they wrap the corpse and look for an auspicious site for contemporary fortune and a site for kings and generals. It's pitiful.
Readers! Will there ever be a time when we suddenly awaken from a deep dream, and everyone's eyes flutter open at the sound of "Heaven punishes evil," like a refreshing spirit?
The preface has become long. It would be fortunate if you could find in it the meaning of my intention to explore in disguise.

To Pagoda Park for Qualification Training
Fortune-teller! Just hearing the word, it seems very easy, but it's a fact that some basic knowledge is required. The basic knowledge may not be that difficult, but anyway, it's necessary to know how to read fortunes, and it seems that you need to learn some tricks to make a living.
However, I also, back in my younger days, thanks to my parents, thanks to the village school teachers, thanks to the neighborhood teachers, knew things like the Eight Trigrams, the Six Lines, the Prior Heaven Numbers, the Posterior Heaven Numbers, the mutual generation and mutual destruction of the Five Elements, determining the month by pushing the Wolgeon (月建) with "Byong-in Head in Gapgi Year," "Muin Head in Eulgyeong Year," and setting the hour with "Gapja is born at night in Gapgi," "Byongja is born at night in Eulgyeong." Now, it has all become someone else's business, but still, the faint memories remain, so if I look at a book once, <31> I can understand it again, and it's not just boasting, but in a very peaceful time, I even spread out the Jami Cheonseo (紫微全書) and looked at the 12 palaces. Because I had that experience, if I just looked at the Tanghwa Saju (唐畵四柱) once, I could become a decent fortune-teller, and if I hadn't escaped from the village school and run away to school, I might have become a real, genuine fortune-teller today.
However, "it's better to be safe than sorry," so in addition to knowing how to read fortunes, it is necessary to know how to deceive with plausible but absurd words, so I expected that there would definitely be fortune-teller friends gathered in Pagoda Park, and I went there around 1 p.m. that day after receiving the editor's order to go straight to the capital.
And indeed, at the octagonal pavilion stone seats, one friend leaning against this pillar, and another friend leaning against that pillar, two people were sitting there, with things like dog gallbladder pills and mountain root fragments scattered around them, and old document books like Dang-saju, Dan-si, Sin-su, Jae-su-jeom, and Haeja-jeom spread out left and right, trying to advertise medicine. They were trying to explain fortunes, trying to observe the appearance and expressions of the other person to read their mood. They had to see if it was right or wrong and adjust their answers accordingly. A yellow beard, a pale face, and a very busy appearance. I focused my attention only on the explanations that the teacher made while reading the mood and adjusting, and watched intently.

First Day's Earnings
Humans are animals that seem incredibly shrewd but are actually incredibly foolish. They live a lifetime saying "I know myself," but they don't even know what will happen in the next hour, let alone tomorrow, and they wander around aimlessly, constantly being deceived. They live hoping for luck. They get deceived like this, wondering if today will be better, if tomorrow will be better, if this year or next year, if the middle or end of their lives, if their children's generation or their grandchildren's generation will be better. That's why they work hard and are active. If they knew for sure that they were destined to suffer all their lives, then poor people who work so hard every morning and evening that they sweat profusely, but can't even afford three meals a day, and who starve more than they eat, or sick people who don't see any effect from medicine or acupuncture, would all commit suicide. But even while suffering, they work again when the day breaks and work when the sun rises. Even though it's bitter and painful, sick people still take medicine again and get acupuncture again. What is the reason? Because they hope for luck in tomorrow, the day after tomorrow, next year, the end of their lives. Rather than hoping for luck, that is the duty and responsibility of people. Striving and being active for tomorrow, for the future, is what we must naturally do as long as we live in this world. There's no way a persimmon will roll into your mouth while you're lying still just because you have good fortune and were born with good luck, and there's no way a wad of money will jump into your room while you're sitting still. <32>
However, many of the friends who gather here seem to be hoping for persimmons to roll in on their own and dreaming of wads of money jumping in on their own. Everyone comes asking what they should do to start a business, how to take care of their children who have been lost, and 20 jeon coins fly to the fortune-teller. This fortune-teller is truly a blessed person, a person with good fortune. But the friends who ask for their fortunes to be told don't realize that the fortune-teller's effort is to make a living by talking.
Next to them, friends with ox-like frames who look like they could catch a tiger were lying down and snoring like thunder, creating a separate world. What good is it for these people to have fortunes of kings and generals, or fortunes of a thousand seok in middle age and ten thousand seok in old age? Did So Daeseong only sleep while waiting for his time, and did Woryong Seonsaeng (臥龍先生) only plow the fields while waiting for Samgo Choryeo (三顧草廬)? Maybe so. But these people only get barked at by dogs in front of this house and that house in the evening and only rely on the gates of big houses and banks, so it's pitiful. Of course, who knows what will happen in the world. If that person has good fortune, who knows if they might become wealthy and reach a high position in their later years, even if they are currently wearing a gourd and carrying a bundle? No way, that's a dream. Even if it's a dream, it's a dream from centuries ago, from the time when tigers smoked tobacco.
Anyway, I have no choice but to do my job. After spending half a day observing the speaking skills and reading the mood (more than reading fortunes), I returned home that day.

Meeting the Hunchback Teacher by Chance
The next day, I went to the company and got 1 won and 2 jeon for confidential expenses <33> and returned to my boarding house well past lunchtime, but the actual performance began from then on.
In this kind of business, being "high-collar" is forbidden, having a smooth face is forbidden, and wearing suits, hats, and shoes is all forbidden. I borrowed hemp clothes from the landlord, wore a worn-out vest, tied a tobacco pouch to my pipe, put on a loose-fitting jacket and vest, borrowed a tanggeon (蕩巾), borrowed a gat (갓), borrowed glasses, and put on rubber shoes. From any point of view, I looked like a fortune-teller candidate who couldn't lose even one point out of a hundred. The landlord couldn't straighten his back, wondering what kind of mischief I was up to.
I wanted to go straight to Anguk-dong (安國洞) intersection, but I was afraid that someone would recognize me, so I went over the mountains and hills, passed through Chuseongmun (추성문), and took a streetcar from Hyoja-dong (孝子洞) terminal. As soon as I got off at Dongdaemun (東大門) pier, I slipped out under the gate and looked around.
Readers in Seoul must have seen a middle-aged man with a dark face, always wearing a tanggeon (蕩巾), a hump on his ear with coarse hair like pine needles growing out of it, and another hump the size of a bean on the space between his eyebrows, reading Tang Saju (唐四柱) books and explaining fortunes in a hoarse voice every time they passed by the public restroom next to Dongdaemun (東大門). This strange man is my teacher.

Spending a Night at the Hunchback Teacher's House
I immediately paid 20 jeon and asked him to tell my fortune first, and he seemed to be working hard to string together nonsense and forced explanations, and every now and then, he kept asking if it was correct. I smiled and flattered him, saying, "How can you be so right?" The people who had gathered nearby had almost disappeared, and the sun was almost setting, so I took advantage of the fact that my four pillars said that I was destined to make a living by writing and said a word.
"Listen. My fortune says that I will make a living by writing, but as you can see, I am so ugly, what kind of writing would I do? And in today's world, you have to study modern learning to make a living, but I don't even know a word of Japanese, so how can I make a living? And as my fortune said, I lost all the money and land I had in my early years, and I'm coming to the city from the countryside for the first time, and I only have 100 won in my pocket, but that's like taking a bite out of a dried persimmon, how long will it last? So, I was wondering if I could learn how to read fortunes from you and find a way to make a living. What do you think?"
Maybe it was because my words were plausible, or maybe it was because he was tempted by the mention of 100 won,
"Of course, that's good. But it will take at least a year to learn that."
He said, agreeing on one hand but dragging his feet on the other. <34>
"Can that be true? I already know the general method, and I can learn it in three days. Anyway, I will follow you from today and learn from you."
And I immediately addressed him as "teacher."
The hunchback teacher didn't refuse so much and seemed to agree.
"Then let's go together."
And he stood up. Seeing that he came out wearing a hemp jacket, I guessed that his house was not too far away, and after going to a tavern and buying him a drink, we finally arrived at a tiny shack near the end of Chungsindong (忠信洞), and he invited me into a room the size of a fist. Thinking about spending the night under a kerosene lamp with bedbugs and no electricity was not very pleasant. However, in order to fulfill my mission, I would not avoid even worse things than this, so I decided to endure it. The teacher and disciple, who were also guests, ate dinner quite happily (the rice was not very good, of course), and while talking about this and that, he said that there was a place that specialized in drawing and selling these Tang Saju (唐四柱) books, and it cost about 20 won to buy one, and if you did well, you could earn about 2 won a day, but if you were unlucky, you wouldn't even earn enough for a drink, and even then, you would be chased away if the police saw you. He also freely revealed secrets such as explaining by looking at the person's appearance and reading their mood and making excuses this way and that way, and saying that if you meet an old person, you should say that they suffered a lot in their youth, and if you meet a young person, you should flatter them by saying that their middle and later years will be very good, and you won't make any big mistakes, and I felt a little sorry but was secretly pleased.

Half a Day as an Assistant, Half a Day as a Debut
Under the lamplight, taking advantage of the fact that the teacher was asleep, I took out the book and read the method of reading fortunes, and indeed, it was not much different from what I had thought, so I thought, "This is good enough to pass," and spent the night fighting with bedbugs. In the morning, I got up and boasted to the teacher that I had almost learned how to read fortunes, but the teacher seemed to be half-believing and half-doubting, but I didn't care.
Late in the morning, I took the fortune-telling tools instead of the teacher and went back to the public restroom next to Dongdaemun (東大門), sat down, and just stared at the people coming and going, but I was more worried about being discovered by someone I knew than about people not coming to see fortunes. And sure enough, around 11 a.m., a friend of mine was either going to Cheongnyangsa (淸涼寺) or going to Yeongdosa (永導寺), I don't know, but she got off the streetcar with a bundle and was strolling around the gate waiting for the Cheongnyangni (淸涼里) bound streetcar. Thinking that I was in trouble, I turned my face away and ran straight into the public restroom and into the toilet stall, not the urinal, and sat there stiffly even though I didn't need to defecate, until I heard the Cheongnyangni (淸涼里) streetcar come and go. It must have taken about 10 minutes. I was ashamed and my nose was a little uncomfortable from the smell of feces.
When I came out, it seemed that someone had been caught, and the hunchback teacher was explaining very enthusiastically. After telling the fortune of another person, the midday siren rang over the high roof of Heunginmun (興仁門), as if to tell the lazy people in Gyeongseong (京城) to wake up. After buying 20 jeon worth of red bean porridge sold under the gate and eating it together,
"Teacher! I will try to solve fortunes from the afternoon, so please watch my skills."
I said, making the teacher nervous. The teacher, with a face that seemed both surprised and laughing, said mockingly,
"You're just going to reveal your true colors by saying it's not right."
And he reluctantly gave up his seat. Now, let's see the fortunes of those who fall into my hands!

The First One Caught: A Sexagenarian
The net has been cast. I began to look for people coming and going, wondering what kind of male destiny (乾命) or female destiny (坤命) would be caught. At first, I secretly wished that a female student or a new-fashioned mistress would be caught, as it would be a good source of material for an article, but that was a vain hope. After an hour, two hours passed, and now I was just waiting for anything to be caught, even a crab or a crawfish, and unfortunately, a 61-year-old man, who had passed his sixtieth birthday in April of this year, approached me. He was wearing a tanggeon (蕩巾) under his self-made gat (갓), and a brand-new silk durumagi (두루마기), and he was carrying a small cane. Judging from his appearance, he must have spent some money and enjoyed women in his youth, and no matter how I looked at it, he didn't seem very calm. Even though he was sixty-one years old, his skin was still fresh, and he looked like a middle-aged man in his fifties. At first, I was flustered, wondering what to do, but first, I wrote down his birth date and the four pillars in my notebook, found each palace, pointed at the pictures with my finger, and started explaining, and I gradually became bolder. Putting down all of the old man's life here would only dirty the page, so let's just write down the outline.
The world is fine as long as you can get away with lies that sound plausible. It's a great thing for the people who look at fortunes to admit that fortunes are not always correct, so it is great news for a bum like me. In the alternation of heaven and earth, the sun sets in the west, so his father passed away first. Although it is said there are three geese, one goose flies alone, so he has three siblings but they are scattered in different directions. I showed him the picture of geese flying, two down and one up, and said that in the eastern garden, peaches and plums are everywhere in the spring breeze, so he has endless luck with wives. In the spring rain in the front yard, two orchids bloom late, so he will have two sons late in life. He has no family business and will make a fortune by himself, but he will have many successes and failures in middle age, but he will make a fortune in business and enjoy happiness in his later years. <36> He had a big illness at the age of two or three, but after that, he has been very healthy, and since he has passed 59, he will live past eighty. I was chattering away nonsensically, and the old man seemed to be captivated and admiring how well it matched. Finally, he opened his mouth and objected that everything else was plausible, but the "father passed away first" part was not correct. Showing weakness is failure! I replied that if his father didn't die first, then his mother must have passed away before he was ten years old, or she must have died in some kind of accident, to make up for that.
That was a pretty good first success. I was quite confident, and the hunchback teacher seemed very surprised. The 20 jeon coin slipped into my pocket.
While I was explaining like this, onlookers gathered around. Another person was caught.

The Fortune of a Gentleman in a Suit
This time, a gentleman I had been hoping for, wearing a suit, carrying a stick, and wearing a straw hat, rushed in. As a gentleman skilled in the art of living, he first asked the fee, and when I answered 20 jeon, he demanded that I tell his fortune for 10 jeon, a 50% discount. How can there be a world without usury, but the gentleman's heart, trying to haggle even here, was very irritating. Since my main business is not this, there would be no big loss if I sold at a special price, but I was worried that it would affect our teacher's living, so I said, "It's just one lifetime for a gentleman to see his fortune, what is 10 jeon? If there is sincerity, there is value," quoting a phrase and staring him down. The gentleman's answer was,
"Then I'll give you 20 jeon, so please tell my fortune well." What if it's good, what if it's bad? You carry your life on your own shoulders, so both happiness and unhappiness are your own fault. Thanks to your parents, you went to Gyeongseong (京城) for study, and then to Tokyo, and sold all the land, paddies, and cattle. You graduated without really graduating, your desires are burning, but your strength is lacking, you want to look stylish, you are thinking about female students and wives, you want to live a new-fashioned life, but you can't go back to the countryside and farm, and you can't do manual labor because of your face and strength. You can't scam because there's no one to be scammed, it's a pitiful situation. How many gentlemen and new-fashioned friends are wandering around the wide streets of Gyeongseong (京城), dreaming vain dreams and lamenting their fortunes? I felt the need to throw a strong bomb right here.
Let's skip the parents palace, the siblings palace, and the children palace for this gentleman, born in the year of Jeong-yu (丁酉), 31 years old. But we can't leave out the wife palace for a young gentleman. "The pleasure of the spouse palace is unfavorable in the first marriage, so the candle shines again on a paulownia tree on an autumn night." If there is no harmony between husband and wife, there will inevitably be a loss of a spouse. The gentleman's smiling face <37> showed an expression of, "Are you a ghost or a dokgabi (도깨비), how can you know so well?"
But look at the wealth palace and the body palace. "It is difficult to keep the family business in youth, so where is the profit? It is neither farming nor labor. If you keep your heart and guard your place, you will have five blessings until the end of your life." In his youth, he will lose a lot of the family business, and writing or commerce will not be very profitable, but no matter what, farming is the most suitable. Look at this. He is standing with a janggi (將棋) piece in his hand, and there are four or five stacks of grain in front of his house, aren't there? You can deceive the world, but you can't deceive your fortune. Since it is written here, what can you do? It is said that "before 30, you will inevitably fail eight times, and in the years of Yu and Sul, your wealth will greatly increase," so I asked if he had suffered any harm due to fire (the character for "eight" (八) is a decomposition of the character for "fire" (火)), and I showed him a picture of a person trapped in fire. Readers, don't laugh too much. This gentleman nodded his head and confessed that he almost died in the Tokyo fire years ago. But since your wealth will greatly increase in the years of Yu and Sul, rest assured and keep your place, and from now on, the years of Im-sin, Gye-yu, Gap-sul, and Eul-hae will come, won't they? At that time, you will see what kind of fortune you will have and live well in your old age.
I don't know if it was right or not. Anyway, it was about the past, and we will have to wait and see about the future. Anyway, this gentleman was very satisfied, paid the full price of 20 jeon without saying a word, and, watching to see if anyone was looking, slipped away towards the Dongdaemun (東大門) gate and onto the streetcar road.

The Final Gift: The Traffic Policeman's Kick
For the third time, a worker was caught. There's no need to say anything, I was explaining enthusiastically that he would suffer a lot and have many misfortunes in his early and middle years, but his fortune would be very good in his later years, when I didn't even realize that a traffic policeman with a blue badge on his arm was slowly approaching. The plank holding the fortune-telling books was kicked away. "Get lost."
That was enough to understand. Why did the opportunity have to be so bad, like hitting a rock with a hoe? Why did the traffic policeman have to pass by just when I was plying my trade like a nail? I thought I was going to be arrested and was preparing to present my press card, but the policeman didn't scold me severely and turned back to cracking down on the rice cake vendors under the gate, his eyes red. As I was gathering my books, he suddenly turned around and kicked the plank again. "Get lost, you son of a bitch!" I was afraid of getting slapped if I hesitated. I quickly packed up the books, gave them to the hunchback teacher, went around the corner of the restroom, gave him 1 won and 40 jeon,
"Thank you for your trouble. I'll see you again later. I have something to do inside the gate, so I'll see you again in a few days. This isn't much, but use it to buy a drink."
And I ran away to the Dongdaemun (東大門) terminal. The hunchback teacher stood there for a while like a dog watching a chicken fly away before turning around. It was 5 p.m.

Is the world so clever? Or are we so foolish? We have been deceived so much, but we don't know how to wake up. We have been deceived a little and a lot, and we have been deceived in every way, but still, we don't know how to be clever? How many years have passed since Chong Doryeong (鄭道令) proclaimed that he would ascend to the throne in Gyeryongsan (鷄龍山)? The friends who dreamed of taking the civil service examination again with poems and prose when the capital was established in Gyeryongsan (鷄龍山) are almost all centenarians, and the road to the afterlife is not far away. Aren't the descendants who buried their ancestors' bones in the Land of Kings and Generals and the Land of Contemporary Fortune and just looked up at the sky now beggars and vagrants, filling the valleys and wandering north and south? Aren't the people who had shamans perform rituals and begged Buddha for blessings now the most incurable patients among the sick and the most lowly among the lowly, receiving scorn and abuse from others?
The means of deceiving the world with nonsensical superstitions is like this. Fortune-tellers, diviners, shamans, pansu (blind geomancers), they are all like that. Can't even one person - just one person, one house - just one house, break free from the dream of superstition and live based on the foundation of science? Isn't there anyone who wants to build a new structure on the foundation of modern civilization, rather than relying only on the four pillars, fortune, and wealth?
I earnestly wish that to the readers and put down this pen. (The End) <39>

						D記者

『요보십시오. 내 사주가 문필로 벌어먹을 팔자라 하지만 보시는 바와가티 사람이 이러케 못생겻스니 무슨 문필이 잇겟소. 그러고 지금 세상은 신학문을 하여야 벌어먹고 사는데 日本말 한마듸 모르고 하니 무슨 수로 벌어 먹겟소. 그러고 악가도 사주에 그랫습듸다마는 초년에 돈냥 논 섬지기나 잇던 것을 다 업세고 시골서 처음으로 뛰여 올나오니 수중에는 돈 100圓이나 잇지만 그까짓것 꼭감ㅅ대 빼먹기갓지 얼마가겟소. 그러니 지금게제 조케 선생에게 이것 보는 법이나 배워가지고 엇더케 生道를 뭇드러 볼가 하는데 엇더켓슴닛가?』
수작이 그럴듯해 그랫던지 돈ㅅ 100圓이나 잇다는 바람에 회가 동하여 그랫던지
『그것 물론 조치요. 그러나 그것을 배우랴면 적어도 일년을 걸녀야 할터인데요.』
하며 한편으로는 찬성을 하면서도 한편으로는 한자락을＜34＞ 끌고 느러진다.
『그럴 수가 잇슴닛가. 대강 방ㅅ수는 전부터 아닛가 늘 잡고 사흘이면 배우지요. 하여간 오늘부터 선생님을 따라가서 배워보겟슴니다.』
하고 단번에 선생님 尊號를 밧처 올녓다.
혹부리 선새도 그러케 사양치는 안코 무방하게 되얏다는 드시
『그럼 가티 가십시다.』
하고 니러선다. 된저고리바람으로 나온 것을 보면 이 선생의 집 역시 그러케 머지 아니한 곳에 잇는 줄을 짐작하고 도라 선술집에 가서 한 잔 먹여가지고는 급기야 가고보니 忠信洞 막바지 산 밋 거의 가서 게딱지 만한 오막사리로 드러가더니 주먹만한 방안으로 나를 불너드린다. 빈대도 빈대려니와 전등도 업는 석유등잔 밋헤서 하로ㅅ밤 지날일을 생각하니 그리 마음이 유쾌치는 못하엿다. 그러나 맛흔 사명을 다하기 위하야는 이보다 더한 일이라도 피치 아니할 터이어던 이것즘이야 하고 꾹 참기로 결심을 하엿다. 사제겸 주객 두 사람이  꽤 團樂하게 저녁밥을 먹고(밥은 물론 그러케 조흔 밥이 아니엇지만) 이 니약이 저 니약이 하는 동안에 이 唐四柱冊을 전문으로 그려파는데가 잇는데 한 권 사랴면 한 20圓든다는 말, 하로에 잘하면 한 2圓 버는 때도 잇고 재수업스면 술 한잔 갑도 안 생긴다는 말, 그나마도 순사가 보면 쫏겨다닌다는 말, 설명 할때는 그 사람의 생긴 맵씨와 눈치를 보아서 이로 저로 둘너대야 한다는 말, 그리고 늙은이 맛나거든 젊어서 고생 만히 하엿다고 하고 젊은 사람 맛나거던 中分後分이 대단히 좃타고 추어주면 큰 틀님은 업다는 말 가튼 그런 비결이 함부로 막 쏘다저 나오는데는 내가 도리어 좀 미안하고 속으로는 깃벗다.

한나절은 조수, 한나절은 出身
등잔불 밋헤서 주인선생의 잠든 츰을 타서 책을 끄내 가지고 보는 방법을 죽내려 한번 보니 과연 생각하던 바와 별로 틀닐 것이 업는 지라 『이만하면 합격이다.』하고 빈대와 싸워가며 하로ㅅ밤을 지나고 아침에 니러나서 주인 선생에게 보는 방법을 거의 깨달엇다는 자랑을 하니 선생은 반신반의하는 모양이나 내게야 무슨 상관이 잇스랴.
아침 후 느직-해서 문서 보통이를 선생님 대신에 들고 다시 東大門 맛 공동변소 엽에 나와서 자리를 잡고 안저서 오는 사람 가는 사람만 노려보고 잇는데 나는 四柱보러오는 사람이 업는 것보다도 아는 사람 알에 발견 될가하는 것이 더욱 마음을 조렷다. 아니나 다를가 오전 11 시나 하야 어느 아는 친구 한분이 淸涼寺를 나가는지 永導寺를 나가는지는 몰나도 동부인을 해가지고 전차에서 내리여 淸涼里行 전차를 기다리느라고 성문알에로 거닐며 스틕을 회회 내두르고 잇섯다.  이것 탈 낫다하고 얼골을 돌니고는 단거름에 공동변소로 뛰여 드러가서 소변ㅅ간도 아니요 大便ㅅ간에 드러가서＜35＞ 똥도 마렵지 안흔 것을 우둑허니 안저서 淸涼里띠 전차가 와가지고 떠나가는 소리를 듯기까지는 아마 한 10분 걸닌 모양이엇다. 창피도 창피려니와 똥냄새에 코가 좀 거북하엿다.
나와서보니 맛침 한 사람이 걸닌 모양이라 혹부리 선생은 가장 열심으로 설명을 하는 중이다. 고 다음으로 또 한 사람을 보아주고 나니 京城안 게으른 사람에게 정신을 차리라는 드시 午正의 기적이 興仁門 놉흔 지붕을 넘어 들녀왓다. 성밋헤서 파는 팟떡을 20錢어치를 사다가 둘이 요긔하고는
『선생님! 오후부터는 제가 한번 풀어보겟스니 제 수단이엇던가 한번 구경하십시오.』
하고는 선생의 간을 써늘케하여 주엇더니 선생은 과연 놀나는 듯 웃는 듯한 얼골로
『공연히 안 맛는다고 본색을 드러내고 십허서』
하고는 빈정대면서 못 익이는 척하고 자리를 양도해 준다. 인제부터는 내 손에 걸닌 자의 사주평이나 해보자!

첫번에 걸린 還甲老人
그믈은 별녀 노핫다. 엇던 乾命이 걸니랴느냐 坤命이 걸니랴느냐하고 오는 사람 가는 사람을 물색하야 보기 시작하엿다. 처음에는 엇던 여학생이나 신식부인이 하나 걸녓스면 기사의 재료가 상당히 되겟다 하고 마음으로는 은근히 바라고 잇섯스나 그는 헛바람이엇다. 한 시간이 지나고 두 시간이 지나고 보니 게나 가재나 하고 무엇이던지 걸니기만 기다리고 잇는 판에 팔자사납게도 61세, 금년 4월에 환갑이 지나갓다는 늙은이 하나가 대든다. 自笠으로 탕건을 눌너스고 샛듯한 모수 두루마기에 조그만 단장을 거더 집흔품이 보아하니 젊어서 돈냥이나 써보고 게집개나 주무러보고 아모리 보아도 그리 점찬치는 안코 나히는 환갑이라하여도 아직도 피부가 싱싱한 품이 한 50내외라고 햇스면 알맛게 생긴 중노인이다. 처음에는 엇지할까 하고 가슴이 덜넝하다가 위선 생년월일 사주팔자를 수첩에 적어노코 各宮을 차저가며 그림을 손가락으로집고 설명해 내려가는 중에 차차 대담하여저 가게 되얏다. 일일이 그 노인의 평생을 여긔에 버려놋는 것도 공연히 지면만 더럽히는 것이니 대강만 적어두자.
그짓말이라도 그럴듯하게만 넘어가면 세상은 그만이다. 사주라고 다 맛는 법이 업다는 것도 의레 보는 사람이 인정해주니 이런 버리하는 친구에게는 고마운 노릇이다. 乾坤消息에 日落西山하니 父先亡格이요 雖云三雁이나 一雁이 孤飛하니 형제는 셋이나 各分東西할 격이라 하며 기걱이가 둘은 알에로 하나는 우로 날너가는 형상 그린 그림을 집허뵈이고 東園桃李에 到處春風이니 마누라 복은 한이 업시만코 庭前春雨에 二蘭이 晩抽하니 아들은 늦게라야 둘이 태우고 재수는 世業이 업시 赤手成家하되 중년에 성패가 만하엿스나 상업으로 成家하야 말년의 향락을 누리겟고＜36＞ 몸은 이삼 세 때 큰 병으로 厄이 잇섯스나 그 후로는 매우 튼튼하고 壽는 59세를 넘엇스니 八十上壽를 하겟다고 한참 주책업는 소리를 떠드러 주엇더니 이 노인이 무엇에 홀닌드시 빙긋빙긋 잘 맛는다는 것을 탄복하는 듯하다가 최후로 입을 열어 다른 것은 다 그럴듯하게 맛는데 父先亡하나가 안 맛는다고 不服을 申立한다. 약점을 뵈야서는 실패라네! 그는 父先亡이 아니시면 열 살 안에 어머니가 도라가섯거나 도라가서도 무슨 변사를 하섯거나 그러케 수를 때우는 법이라고 넘겨첫다.
그만하면 최초의 성공이 무던하엿다. 나도 꽤 자신이 나고 혹부리 선생님도 아조 놀나는 모양이다. 돈 20錢은 내호주머니로 긔어드러왓다.
이러케 설명하든 판에 구경ㅅ군이 찍둘너 섯다. 또 한 분이 걸녓다.

양복신사의 사주
이번이야말로 바라고 원하던 신사 한 분이 양복을 잡숫고 스틱을 잡숫고 麥藁모자를 쓰고 덤빈다. 처세술에 능난한 신사인 만큼 먼저 수수료를 믓더니 20錢이라는 대답에 5 할인으로 10錢에 보아달나는 청구다. 외누리 업는 세상이 어대잇스랴마는 여긔에도 깍정이 짓을 하겟다고 덤비는 신사의 심정이 무던히 얄미웟다. 내의 本장사가 이것이 아닌 바에 特價廉賣를 하기로 큰 손해가 갈 것은 업지만 우리 선생의 뒷버리에 영향이 밋처서는 그도 미안한 일이라 저만 냥반이 일평생 사주을 보는 데 돈 10錢이 무엇임닛가. 不誠이면 無物이라고 그만한 정성이 잇스면 그만한 갑시가 잇는 것이지요 하고 문자를 한마듸 인용하며 턱 벗터 보앗다. 신사의 대답은
『그러면 20錢 다 줄터이니 잘이나 보아주우.』 잘 보면 어덧코 못보면 엇덧탄 말이냐, 네 평생을 네가 질머젓스니 불행도 행복도 다 너 할 탓이다. 부모 덕분으로 경성유학이니 동경유학이니 하느라고 잇던 논 마직이, 소ㅅ발이나 다 팔어먹고 졸업도 하는 둥 마는 둥, 욕망은 불타오르고 뒷전의 힘은 부족하고 맵씨는 내고십고 여학생 마누라 생각은 나고 신식 살님은 하고 십고 시골 도라가 농사 짓기는 죽어도 못하겟고 막버리 노동은 체면, 체력에 관게되야 못하고 사기하랴니 속는 놈이 업고 딱한 노릇이다. 넓으나 넓은 京城市街에는 헛꿈을 꾸고 도라다니는 사주팔자만 한탄하는 양복 친구, 신사 친구들이 얼마나 만흘고 여긔에야말로 단단한 폭탄을 하나 던저 줄 필요를  절실히 늣기엿다.
31세 丁酉生되는 이 신사의 四柱評은 여긔에 父母宮도 그만두자, 兄弟宮도 그만두자, 子宮도 그만두자. 그러나 젊은 신사에게 妻宮을 빼여 노아서는 아니된다. 配宮之樂은 初娶不利라 梧桐秋夜에 華燭이 再明하니 내외간에 화합치 못하시면 필연코 상처수가 게심니다. 신사의 빙긋이 웃는 얼골에는＜37＞ 『네가 귀신이냐 독갑이냐 엇저면 그러케도 知之如神이냐』하는 표정이 나터낫다.
그러나 財宮身宮을 보십시오. 소년호기에 세업을 難守라 利在何處오 非農則工이로다. 安心守分하면 五福終世하리라. 젊어서는 세업을 만히 업셀 수요 문필이나 상업으로는 대단히 이롭지 못하나 아모리 하여도 농업이 제일 적당합니다. 이것을 보십시오. 이 그림에 장기를 잡고 섯고 집압헤 노적눌이 넷이나 다섯이나 쌔여잇지 안슴니가. 세상을 다 속여도 팔자는 못 속임니다. 여긔에 이러케 나타난 바에야 엇지 함닛가. 30이전에는 必敗八人이요 酉戌之年에는 財數大通이라 하엿스니 30이전에 무슨 화재(八人은 불 火ㅅ자 解字한 것)로 욕 보신 일이 업느냐고 하며 불ㅅ속에 쌔인 사람 그린 그림을 펼처 뵈이니 독자 제군! 너무 웃지를 마라. 이 신사 냥반 고개를 그덩그덩 하며 年前 東京火災때에 하마하더면 죽을 번 햇다는 고백이다. 그러나 酉戌之年에 재수대통하니 안심하시고 분수를 직히시면 이 압흐로 壬申 癸酉 甲戌 乙亥년이 오지 안슴닛가 그때에는 무슨 재수를 보아 말년을 잘 살 신수올시다.
마젓는지 안 마젓는지는 모른다. 지나간 일은 하여간, 장래 일은 두고 보아야 안다. 엇잿던 이 신사는 만족 대만족한드시 깍자던 수수료도 두말안코 호가대로 20錢을 내 주고는 다른 사람이 처다보는가 안보는가 주의하여 가며 슬멍슬멍 東大門 성문밋흐로 하야 전차길로 사라지고 마럿다.

최후의 선물은 교통순사의 발ㅅ길
셋재ㅅ번에 노동자 한 분이 또다시 걸녓다. 두말 할 것이 무엇잇나, 한참 신이 나는드시 초년 중년에는 만흔 고생과 만흔 수액을 당하나 末分에 가서는 대단히 팔자가 조켓다는 설명을 하는 판이데 팔뚝에다 푸른 표를 두른 교통순사 한분이 슬멍슬멍 닥아서 온 줄도 나는 몰낫다. 사주책 노힌 널파땍이가 엇던 발ㅅ길에 툭 채엿다.『고라박아.』
그만하면 알ㅅ知字다. 굉이에 마듸닷키로 이러케도 기회가 낫부단 말이냐. 하필 내가 못처럼 行術하는 지금이야 말고 교통순사가 지나갈 필요야 무엇잇나. 나는 잡혀 갈 줄 알고 속으로 기사의 명함 내밀 준비를 하고 잇는 판인데 순사는 그러케 심히 굴지 안코 도로 도라서더니 성문밋 떡장사 전을 取締하느라고 눈알이 붉은 모양이다. 주침 주침 하며 책을 싸는 판에 휙 도라서더니 널판땍이에 발ㅅ길이 또 한번이다. 『고라박아ㅅ 이 겟쯋다리 (이 자식가라닛간)』어름어름 하다가는 따귀 맛는 판이다. 거듬거듬 책을 싸서 혹부리 선생을 주고 조곰 변소 모퉁이로 도라가서 돈 1圓 40錢을 내여주며
『너무 수고를 만히 낏첫슴니다. 후일에 다시 뵙지요. 문 안에 가서 볼일이 잇스니 몃칠 후에 다시 뵙겟슴니다. 이것 얼마 안 되지만 갓다가 술ㅅ잔 갑이나 하십시오.』하고 東大門종점의 정류장으로 줄행랑을 치니 혹부리 선생은 닭 쫏던 개 울 처다보는 격으로 한참 섯다가 도라서는 모양. 때는 오후 5시.

세상은 넛즛이 이럿다 깁흔 꿈을 깰 수는 업는가
세상이 약어서 그러냐? 우리가 어리석어서 그러냐? 속기도 만히 속앗건만 깨다를 줄을 모른다. 적게 속고 크게 속고 속을 것은 다 속앗건만 그래도 약은 꾀가 아니 나는 고나? 鄭道令이 鷄龍山에 등극한다고 떠든지가 벌서 몃몃해가 지나갓느냐. 鷄龍山에 도읍되면 風月詩賦로 다시 과거를 뵌다고 꿈꾸던 친구들이 거의 백수가 되야 황천의 길이 멀지 안흐냐. 王侯將相之地와 當代發福之地에 조상의 뼈다귀를 뭇고 한울만 처다보고 안젓던 자손들이 인제는 乞人漂客으로 邱壑에 塡死하고 남북으로 流離하는 오늘이 아니냐. 무당에게 굿을 식히고 붓처님께 복을 빌던 인물이 지금은 病客 중에도 痼疾의 病客, 천인 중에도 가장 천인이 되야 남의 게 끄림을 밧고 학대를 밧지 안느냐.
얼토당치 아니한 미신으로 세상을 속이는 수단은 이러하다. 사주쟁이, 점쟁이, 무당, 판수 할 것 업시 모다가 그럿타. 한 사람 - 단 한 사람, 한 집 -  단 한 집이라도 미신의 꿈을 깨트리고 과학의 토대우에 근거를 두고 살 수는 엄는가? 사주팔자, 신수, 재수에만 의뢰치 말고 현대문명의 地盤우에 새 건설을 하랴는 동무는 엄는가?
나는 독자 제군에게 그것을 간절히 빌어두고 이 붓을 놋는다.(完)＜39＞
