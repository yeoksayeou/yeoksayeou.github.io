Six Folk Songs,
My Hometown (Rhyme)
Kim An-seo

My hometown is Hwangp’o in Kwaksan
The waves are high on the boat carrying the spring song
I used to pick azalea blossoms and play on the back hill
All I long for is now a dream.

Even a thousand-ri road is only forty or fifty ri in my dreams
P'yongyang is a place I used to stop by and enjoy on my way
Last night I couldn't go again while going
How can I deal with this frustrating wandering heart?

Leaving my hometown in Ori Village, Hwangp'o
Why did I stop on the way?
Even if I go, the dream path is reckless
I can’t see the white sail that is vivid in my eyes. <128>

The falling flowers are flying in the wind today
The lark sings of the shimmering spring day
The land route is a thousand ri, my heart has no place to rest
I wander this day searching for my hometown.
(P.S. The author notes that this is something in between a folk song and a poem.)

In Posan

If you return

Here is the mouth of the river
And outside is the sea
Going with the tide is the road to P'yongyang
Going against the tide is the South Sea.

I don't know

It's May
Pak No-a

It's May, it's May
It's May when flowers fall and leaves grow
I am a servant in someone else's house, do I have time?
There's no time for plowing and weeding.

The round sun rises in the morning
My sorrow comes every night
I hold the girl from the neighborhood in my dreams
The east window is bright, I moan on my pillow. <131>

Strawberry Picking Girl

Pak No-a

Strawberries are red
Ripening on the back hill
The crimson skirt flickers
In my eyes.

Strawberry picking girl
Let me taste it
Is it unripe and bitter?
Or is love bitter?

Horse Song
Ri Ŭn-sang

How many horses
Eat that grass every year and grow?
As the spring breeze fills the field
The scent of new grass is fragrant
Yalli yalli yallaseong
Yallari yalla. <132>

Can it be that
The horses that used to run and play are now sick and lying down?
Now with which horses
Shall we go out and fight?
Yalli yalli yallaseong
Yallari yalla.

Let's raise foals again
And make them into warhorses
If they are tall and have strong legs
Won't they kick even rocks?
Yalli yalli yallaseong
Yallari yalla.

Untie the reins
Of the one who is full of flesh
The sound of him crying to go
Doesn't it split the mountains and rivers?
Yalli yalli yallaseong. <133>
