Modern Fortune House
Civilized People and Superstition

In America, famous for producing new stories, this time a great miraculous, mysterious, and religious story has emerged. It is at the "Holy Cross" Cemetery in "Maten," Massachusetts. It is said that a Catholic monk, "Patrick Power," who was buried there 60 years ago, had his tombstone merely brushed against, and a person who had been ill for 12 years was miraculously cured. More than twenty million patients and spectators flocked there from all over the place, almost causing the desolate cemetery to burst and creating unprecedented chaos. Whether this is an expression of the increasingly cruel curiosity of Americans, a kind of diversion, or a result of a deeply rooted religious sentiment, the fact remains that civilized people, while mocking superstition, have a mischievous interest in seeking new stimuli from it! How can such civilized people have the courage to mock the primitive barbarians who worshipped reproductive organs!

Jobau and Fake World

It is surprising that even old-fashioned Korean women who only know how to part their hair in the middle and slick it back so that it hangs neatly, possess a fashionable hat. If a woman wears a hat, it sounds extremely modern, but in reality, it is a kind of headgear with an extremely old-fashioned model that is uniform (perhaps this will become a modern trend in the future). This is the so-called jobau (조바우) that women in Seoul wear often, and even the name sounds frivolous. And when you see a woman wearing this, no matter how beautiful her face is, you can't help but feel dissatisfied, and it doesn't seem like I'm the only one with this quirk. Up to this point, you can offset this with the cold of Seoul and the somewhat belated but proud "Korean women also wear hats," and you can even take a step further and feel affection, but when you carefully observe the women sitting next to you on the tram, you can't help but feel disgust rather than affection! The reason is:
The jobau's (조바우) fabric is artificial silk.
The jobau's (조바우) tassel (black silk thread) is made of glass golden beads.
Moreover, the gold hairpin visible at the end of the jobau (조바우)... (However, the price is 25 jeon (錢), and the weight is surprisingly heavy).

At the Cafe

One night at the Cafe Baron.
Jazz flows from the gramophone like the sound of a bull.
The shoulders of the waitresses are swaying. Their buttocks are wiggling. The disgusting rhythm of the flesh, like electrically-operated dolls, unfolds here and there.
"All right, let's dance!" A young man with neatly combed hair and a smart suit suddenly stands up, stretches out his arms, and rushes at the waitress, hugging her waist. <141>
"Ara! Chotto matte, chotto matte, atashi honto odore nai wa yo!" (Oh! Wait a minute, wait a minute, I really can't dance!) The girl's cooing voice, as she twists her body and pretends to be breathless, is melting!
However, in the end, they cling to each other as if stuck together like loaches, rubbing their buttocks against each other between the narrow tables, but it is pitiful. The steps don't match. Social dancing is suffering a great disgrace. Two excited young men grab hands and dance the Charleston? Because they are flailing their feet like mad horses, the dust fills the entire room, making it hard to breathe. A Russian youth sitting next to them watches the scene and, seemingly intrigued, suddenly gets up and joins in. It is a trio dance. However, the young Russian's dance is authentic, so it lacks ostentation and is graceful and dignified. Even when he swings his feet freely, it fits the rhythm and does not deviate from the form. After the chaotic dance ends, the two Korean youths shout triumphantly in Japanese:
"Dōshitemo warera sanning gumida ne" (By all means, we are a trio) How pitiful that they did not realize that the Russian youth (actually a Finn) meant "Watch and learn from me" in his drunken spirit.
Still, they get up a few times and look around at the musicians Hong 00, Choe 00, Hong 00, gentlemen, who are dancing gracefully and leaving, making gestures like circus performers while saying:
"Head A Cut Time See You Tomorrow Night"
In this way, they seem to think that they are the highest-class gentlemen, modern people, socialites, and noble sons of Korea. Oh, not at all! Ha ha! They say that if a crow-tit tries to walk like a stork, its legs will be torn!

The World Seen by a Blind Woman After Gaining Sight

This is the story of a blind woman living in Nebraska, America. This woman was born blind and, after graduating from a school for the blind, suddenly regained her sight at the age of eighteen (nineteen or twenty in Korean age).
"I was sitting in front of the radio, listening intently. Then, suddenly, something like an electric spark flashed on my forehead and went behind my head, and then suddenly my eyes could see..."
Doctors have published various theories about this miraculous event, but I cannot introduce them all here. Let me tell you the story of how the young woman, who regained her sight at the age of twenty, was confused by the world she saw for the first time and made absurd mistakes.
Although she regained her sight, she was not at all accustomed to it, so the first thing she could not distinguish was distance. She would stretch out her arm to grab the moon in the sky or the clock hanging on the high ceiling, or she would mistake the table in the middle of the room for being attached to the far wall. Also, sometimes:
"The flowers in the vase on that window and the chestnut tree standing on that mountain look the same size when viewed from here like this. But why is the chestnut tree bigger than the flowers?" she would ask others.
Because of this, she felt more uncomfortable when she had her eyes open, so when she went out, she would deliberately close her eyes and walk with her familiar cane.
"People in red and white clothes"
When she first saw pigs sleeping soundly inside a fence, the woman is said to have made this funny remark. Also, when she saw someone laughing, she was startled by the white teeth showing under their lips and ran away.
Every tree with spreading branches looked like a person dancing with their arms raised high.
When she first saw "Muffy," the black puppy she had cherished so much before she gained her sight, the woman was terrified and startled. When she was blind, everything was black. But when the woman first came into contact with light, she became afraid of the color black and feared that the black puppy would make her blind again, so her heart pounded.
Well, maybe it is more comfortable to be blind and not see all the unpleasant and disgusting things in the world!

Advocates of Shamans

Eradicating illiteracy! Breaking superstition! This is our only slogan and alarm bell these days. Many leaders and pioneers have devoted their heart and soul and made desperate efforts to achieve this movement. However, the elders of Joseon, steeped in long-standing ignorance and customs, instead regarded this as an unacceptable reckless and perverse act or a deplorable Japanese practice, and merely used the authority of their ancestors to scold them and maintained their conservative stubbornness. As a result, there arose conflicts between the old and the new, emotional discord, and domestic strife.
Now, speaking of shamans (巫女), one aspect of superstition, these were women who had grasped the secret art of divination and healing for the good and bad fortune of Joseon people for hundreds of years and manipulated and dominated them by keeping them under their skirts.
Thus, ignorant Joseon people (especially women) go to shamans even for serious illnesses, stomach aches, eye diseases, or leg pains. They do not know that terrible germs are eating away at blood cells and destroying cell tissues in the patient's body. Instead, they are deluded by the shamans' false and absurd words, saying that the wood spirit has moved, that the vengeful spirit of the dead so-and-so is displeased, or that a goblin has taken root in the apricot tree. How many people are pitifully robbed of their money and even their lives by the shamans who beat drums, clang gongs, stick paper money on their foreheads, and dance wildly!
It is truly heartbreaking and brings tears to my eyes.
However, these days, even those who blindly believe in shamans are gradually realizing the futility of superstition and that just as there is rice to feed the hungry, there are doctors and medicine for the sick. However, the sound of gutgeori (굿거리) (shamanistic rituals) continues to be heard every day in the heart of Seoul, the capital of Joseon, so the situation is still far from reassuring. Moreover, another strange incident has occurred because of a shaman.
It is said that in OOO Village (OOOOO里) in Hamnam (咸南), Chongpyong County (定平郡), the young people had established a labor night school and were educating poor children. Some time ago, when a shaman came to the house of OOO in the village to perform a gutgeori (굿거리), the night school officials obstructed it. Angered by the fact that night school student O (O君) refuted the shaman's falsehoods and had an argument with his father, eight or nine village elders formed a group, attacked the aforementioned night school, beat the instructors, and expelled the students. When we hear such news, we once again examine the sentiments of those who turn their backs on the times and futilely lament and struggle, and we do not spare even a bit of sympathy, thinking about their remaining future. We cannot but feel no small amount of concern when we think about the children who will be colored by their prohibitions and misguided guidance of the times. We do not know how great their obstruction and hindrance are in eradicating our bad habits and ignorance and pioneering a new life that is in line with the spirit of the times. Anyway, they should shut their mouths and retire to the back room, or quickly abandon this dirty world and become gentle spirits in the netherworld, and discard their trivial and bothersome old-womanish concerns. <143>
