기사제목	안잠재기가 본 모보·모껄 新家庭 風景 (An Jamjaegi's View of a Modern Husband and Wife's New Family Scenery)
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Commentary)

An Jamjaegi's View of a Modern Husband and Wife's New Family Scenery

Oh, my. My mistress is quick-tempered and modern, but her heart is very kind.
And the master is even kinder.
The baby is just over a year old now. My mistress knows when the sun sets but doesn't know where it rises. She should have gotten up before sunrise.
In the morning, I get up at the crack of dawn, put fire in both stove holes of the inner room, and cook the rice before she even gets up.
Only when the master is about to leave for the bank does he wash his face and come into the inner room, and then she'll finally call me to bring the rinsing water to the inner room so she can brush her teeth with a toothbrush and then have breakfast with all the fixings.
How would I, a servant, know what's modern? If she woke up a little earlier, she could tell me how to do this and that, tasting and seasoning as she made it, but instead, she just lies in bed, calling me out ten or twenty times, telling me to do this and that. I try my best, but how can it turn out well? And then she just gets annoyed, saying I have no skill or sense.

Both of them hardly eat breakfast at all. The master eats about half, but the mistress barely manages a couple of spoonfuls, and instead eats a lot of raw eggs and milk.
So, even if I put aside rice for myself, I have no choice but to eat what they leave behind. I can't very well put less rice in my own bowl because I don't like cold rice.
That's how I live on cold rice. It might be alright in the summer, but how can I endure it in the cold winter, not just for a day or two?
Truly, if you only have money and want to live a modern life, there's nothing to envy in the world.
In the old days, no matter how much money or gold a young woman had, when she got married, she had to take care of her parents-in-law and manage the household with servants, didn't she?
But does my mistress have parents-in-law to serve? Does the master treat her strictly? On the contrary, the master takes care of the mistress.
Look at this. After breakfast, the master goes to the bank, and the mistress takes a long nap in the warm part of the room. She sleeps until noon, and then finally washes her face. And washing her face is such a fuss.
She heats some kind of iron skewer and singes her perfectly good hair. She shaves her mustache with a razor. In the old days, I saw women pluck out facial hair with tweezers, but this is the first time I've seen a modern mistress shave her mustache.
And she usually does it herself, but on Sundays, the master does it for her.
When the master gently shaves her face, the mistress rests her head on his lap and sleeps.

She singes her hair and shaves her face, and then puts powder on her face. I couldn't use that many things in my entire life. There are so many different kinds. She dabs on this colorful bottle and that one, rubbing them in, and then changes her clothes completely.
And she has so many clothes. She tries on this one and that one. It's quite a spectacle.
Then, she talks with her friends until sunset, eats some kind of Western food (I couldn't eat it even if I died, but she leaves it and tells me to eat it), orders food and sings, and plays something called a phonograph, making a "boom-boom" sound.
Honestly, I can't stand that sound day or night, it hurts my ears. But it's worth four thousand yang, I think.
If she has friends visiting, they play at home, and if not, the mistress goes out to play. On those days, I'm as good as dead because of the baby.
Once she goes out, whether the master comes home or the baby cries, whether it's midnight or dawn, she doesn't come back as if she's forgotten.
Then, the couple fights.
When they fight, it's as if they're about to break up right then and there.
Sometimes, the mistress packs her bags and tries to go out, but I get scared and follow her, stopping her. Then, she pretends to give in and comes back inside.
The mistress goes out and plays as she pleases, while the master can't move an inch.
If he happens to be late coming from the bank, she grabs him and interrogates him. Sometimes, I feel sorry for the master.

On Sundays, after the master shaves the mistress's face, they go to the bathhouse together.
On that day, I don't cook dinner. They go out and eat somewhere else. I forget what it's called, but they eat dinner that costs 150 yang per person.
They sleep separately, with the master in the other room and the mistress, the baby and I in the inner room.
But she always leaves her place empty and pretends not to hear the baby crying.
Wouldn't it be better if she just let me have the other room? Once, her mother-in-law and father-in-law came to visit. They were very old people.
Judging by their behavior, it seemed like they were seeing their daughter-in-law for the first time.
Oh, even the modern mistress tried her best to act like a daughter-in-law in front of her parents-in-law, but it just didn't suit her.
Wearing a short skirt and showing her legs, which were barely covered, she bustled in and out of the kitchen, the inner room, and the other room. It was so funny.
The mother-in-law, not knowing how hard she was trying, said, "Dear, I don't mind you living in a modern way, but while your father-in-law is alive, please wear longer skirts and cover your legs a bit. It's embarrassing for me to see." The mistress didn't say anything at the time, but she took it out on the master later.
Still, the mother-in-law cherished her daughter-in-law and wouldn't let go of her grandson for a moment.
Whenever the baby cried, she would tell her daughter-in-law to breastfeed him, and then she would hold him and kiss him, smiling happily with her wrinkled face.
When they left, they gave her and her grandson some silk fabric – later I heard that our master was an only child. But the mistress opposed taking care of his parents.

These days, we have a new and astonishing sight at our house. They bought a phonograph. And some man who looks like a banjo (he gets a monthly salary of 1,500 yang, which is ten times more than me) comes every day to play the phonograph and teach the mistress how to dance. How scandalous.
Isn't dancing something that only courtesans and entertainers do?
And that's not all. She learns to dance by hugging that man and whirling around the room.
It's so scandalous that my face feels hot with shame.
Then, when the master comes home from the bank, he joins in and they make a fuss about learning to dance.
And it would be fine if they just danced, but these days, they bought something called mahjong (麻雀) or something, with bamboo sticks attached to the bones, and they spend all night playing it.
Every night, ten or twenty people gather at the mistress's house and occupy the large inner room, making "poong, poong" sounds. What is it anyway?
I have no choice but to be kicked out to the other room, where I huddle and spend the night.
I'm at my wit's end.
Are all modern people who have gone to school like this? If being modern is good, there should be some good in it, but it seems like it's just about wasting precious money and living fancy.

The master receives 2,500 yang a month, but they spend more than 10,000 yang.
I wondered where they get all the money, but it turns out the mistress had money that she brought with her when she got married. So, they keep it in savings and take it out little by little, like picking dried persimmons.
I don't know how they will manage when they run out of that money. Please don't tell my mistress that I said this.
I get paid three won a month and eat cold leftovers while being told to throw it away, but I need to have somewhere to go if I get kicked out. <29>

안이야요. 우리 주인아씨는 성정이 급하고 또 신식이 돼서 그려치 맘은 퍽 착하신 이야요.

애기는 인제 겨우 돌이 지나갓서요. 우리 주인아씨는 해가 서쪽으로 지는 줄은 알어도 어데서 떠올으는지는 모르세요. 아침에 해가 뜨기 전에 이러나 보기를 햇서야지요.
아침에는 내가 첫새벽에 일어나서 안ㅅ방 두 아궁이에다 불을 너코 밥을 다 익혀 놋투록 안이 일어나세요.
그러다가 서방님이 은행소에 가실 시간이 되야서 세수를 하고 안ㅅ방으로 건너오시면 그제야 나를 식혀서 안ㅅ방으로 양치물을 떠 드려다가 솔로 양치질을 하구 나선 겨을 만상을 해서 아침을 자세요.
찬가튼 것도 내가 뭘 신식을 아나요. 좀 일직 일어나서 이건 이러케 하고 저건 저러케 하라고 식히기도 하고 간도 보아서 만들지 안쿠 이건 이불 속에 드러누어서 열ㅅ 번 스무 번 나를 불너내여 이래라 저래라 식히기만 하니 나는 죽을 애를 써서 하느라고 하지만 그게 잘 되어야지요. 그러구 날더러만 솜씨가 업느니 주변머리가 업너니 짜증만 내세요.

아침밥은 두분 다 통히 안이 자세요. 서방님은 그래도 절반 가량은 자시지만 아씨는 두어 숫갈도 다 못 자시고 그 대신 생닭알이네 우유네 하는 것을 만히 자십듸다.
그러니 나는 내 목스로 밥을 퍼 노앗다가도 할 수 업시 그이들이 남긴 밥을 먹어 치어야지 어터케 해요. 내가 식은 밥뎅이가 먹기 실타고 진지 그릇에다 진지를 적게 퍼놀 수는 업고.
그래저래 나는 식은 밥으로만 살어가지요. 그것도 여름이면 모르지 다만 치운 겨울에 그것을 하루 이틀이 안이고 터 엇케 견대냄니까.
참 돈만 잇고 신식살님만 하기로 들면 세상에 브러울게 업서요.
전 세상가트면 아모리 돈은 말고 금이 잇더래도 젊은이가 시집을 오면 시부모를 봉양하고 이대로 하인을 거느리고 살음사리를 해야 안이해요?
그런데 우리 댁 주인아씨는 시부모가 잇슴니까. 서방님이 그리 엄하게 구십니까. 되려 서방님이 앗씨를 모시고 지내지요.
이거 보세요. 글세 아침을 그러케 지내고 나서 서방님이 은행소를 가시고 나면 아씨는 뜨뜻한 아랫목에서 한 잠을 늘어지게 자지요. 실컷 오정이 지내두룩 자고 나서는 그제야 겨우 세수를 함니다. 세수가 또 엇저면 그러케 요란스런지요.

그것도 연헤날이나 앗씨가 손수하시지 공일날이면 서방님이 해 주신담니다.

참말이지 나는 밤이나 나제나 그 놈의 소리 때문에 귀가 압허 살수가 업서요. 그래도 그것이 갑이 四천량 이라든가요.
