別乾坤 第21號

1929年 06月 23日
別의別乾坤 (Extraordinary Miscellany)
雜著 (Miscellany)

◇南米의 지렁이(蚯蚓)
우리나라에잇는지렁이야 제아모리크대야 五寸쯤밧게안되지만 南米에가면크로케―쓰라는 三四尺乃至는 五尺以上이나되는 큰지렁이가잇다.

◇倫敦의 煤煙
크기도 世界一이요 煤煙이만키로도 世界一은 倫敦이다 겨울이되면은 더욱만혀서하로한에 사람이마시는 煤煙의 量은 約五百兆億이나된다.

◇世界一의 高價郵票
묵은 郵票는 世界各國에서 珍重히녁이지만 그中에도 英領이니아―에서 七十年쯤 前에 發行한 一센트(三錢) 郵票는 時價六萬五千圓이나된다 아마世界에이보다 더갑만흔 묵은 郵票는업슬것이다.

◇世界第一큰 公園
世界第一큰 公園은 米國國立에로―스튼公園인것은 누구나잘알것이다 東西三百二十里南北二百九十里餘 그가운대에는 一萬尺이나되는 山도잇고 間歇泉이 百 警邇溫泉이 三千六百이나잇고 周圍가 百餘里이나되는 湖水도잇다.

◇永久的理髮
아이랜드의 다부린市에잇는 어느理髮所에 料金表를써부쳣는데
一 紳士의 上等毛髮웨이프
金二圓五十錢
一 永久的毛髮웨이프
金十錢
다만긴 時間을 참고견뒤일 勇氣가잇고 他人의 批評을상관안을 覺悟가 必要

◇地下都市
루―마니아에는 地下都市라는것이 잇다 예날부터 地下의 소곰(鹽)을 파내기위하야 작고파드러간것이 인제는 廣大한 都會가되엿다지금은호텔도잇고 電車도잇고 敎會도 運動場도잇고 湖水와 蓮못도잇서 一大文化都市가되엿다나.

◇미트러퍼리스(映畵)의 出現
機械文明의 世上이다 米國陸軍에서는 刺桐裝置로 來襲하는 飛行機의 高度를 測定하야 탕하고 航空機射擊砲를터치는 人造人間이 完成되여서 試驗의 結果好成績이라고

◇진듸물과 戰爭
요전에 日本中央線 ■尻驛附近에 진듸물의 大軍이나타나 汽車의 進行을방헤하야 人夫들과 진듸물과는 一大激戰이이러낫섯다.

◇動物의 絶食時間
動物은 늘무엇이고 먹어야사는것이다 그러나어느동안지는 안먹고도 넉넉히산다 이제 動物에따러얼마동안을 絶食하고도살수잇는가를보자―개고리는 三百六十日間 개(犬)三十日間―사람은十二日間―새(小鳥)는 九日間―빈대는千二百日間―물고기는(魚)千日間―배암(蛇)는八百日間―거북(龜)은五百日間

◇銳敏한 臭覺
아푸리카의 未開야만人은 내음제를맛는힘이굉장하야서 개보다도더예민하다 一마일밧게잇는냄새쯤은 얼는안단다.

◇괴상한 禮法
인사에도별별식이다만타! 蒙古의혀(舌)를날늠거리는인사와라프란트에서는 코를비비는禮法! 이것은벌서알엇지만!안다곤이란섬(島)에서는 남의얼골에대고 입김을혹부는것이 禮法이고 힘잇게볼사록 最敬禮라니 참별건곤이야!＜133＞

◇ South American Earthworms (蚯蚓)
The earthworms in our country are only about five *ch'on* at most, but in South America, there are earthworms called "Crookes" that are three to four *ch'ŏk*, or even over five *ch'ŏk* long.

◇ London's Smoke
London is the world's number one in size and also in the amount of smoke. In winter, it becomes even more so, and the amount of smoke a person breathes in a day is about 500 trillion.

◇ The World's Most Expensive Stamp
Old stamps are treasured in countries around the world, but among them, a one-cent (three *chŏn*) stamp issued about seventy years ago in British Guiana is worth 65,000 won at current prices. Probably there is no older stamp more valuable than this in the world.

◇ The World's Largest Park
Everyone knows that the world's largest park is Yellowstone National Park in the United States. It is 320 *ri* east to west and over 290 *ri* north to south. In the middle of it, there is a mountain that is 10,000 *ch'ŏk* high, 100 geysers, 3,600 hot springs, and a lake with a circumference of over 100 *ri*.

◇ Permanent Hairdressing
In a barbershop in Dublin, Ireland, a price list was posted:
One gentleman's high-class hair wave: 2 *wŏn* 50 *chŏn*
One permanent hair wave: 10 *chŏn*
However, courage to endure a long time and the resolve not to care about others' criticisms are necessary.

◇ Underground City
There is something called an underground city in Romania. From ancient times, small holes were dug to extract salt (鹽) from underground, and now it has become a vast metropolis. Now there are hotels, trains, churches, sports fields, lakes, and lotus ponds, so it is said to be a major cultural city.

◇ The Appearance of Metropolis (映畵)
It is a world of mechanical civilization. In the U.S. Army, an artificial human being has been completed that measures the altitude of airplanes attacking with thorn devices and fires anti-aircraft guns, and the test results are said to be good.

◇ War with Aphids
Recently, a large army of aphids appeared near ■jiri Station on the Japanese Chūō Line, obstructing the progress of trains, and a great battle broke out between the laborers and the aphids.

◇ Animals' Fasting Time
Animals always need to eat to live. But they can survive without eating for a certain period of time. Now, let's see how long each animal can survive without food: Frog: 360 days, Dog (犬): 30 days, Human: 12 days, Bird (小鳥): 9 days, Bedbug: 1200 days, Fish (魚): 1000 days, Snake (蛇): 800 days, Turtle (龜): 500 days

◇ Acute Sense of Smell
The uncivilized savages of Africa have a tremendous power to smell odors, even more sensitive than dogs. They can recognize a smell from a mile away.

◇ Strange Etiquette
There are so many different ways to greet people! The Mongols wag their tongues, and in Lapland, they rub noses! I already knew that! On Andagon Island (島), blowing breath on someone's face is etiquette, and the stronger you blow, the more respectful it is! It's truly an extraordinary miscellany! ＜133＞
