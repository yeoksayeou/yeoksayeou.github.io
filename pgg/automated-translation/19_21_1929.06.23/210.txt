발행일: 1929년 06월 23일
기사제목: 靑春의 자랑 나의 寶物 (The Pride of Youth, My Treasure)
기사형태: 설문 (Questionnaire)

The Pride of Youth, My Treasure

“Life gets younger.” Life’s vitality must lie in youth, and youth must have invaluable treasures. Ten people, ten colors! A reporter set out to find these different treasures depending on the area of expertise and interview young professionals. (Reporter)

A Newspaper Reporter's Treasure

Kim Tu-baek

As one nameless writing laborer, I don’t think I have anything to present as “My Treasure as a Newspaper Reporter.” Even if I did, I would be embarrassed to say it myself before someone else acknowledged it.

In my four years as a newspaper reporter, I often found "waste" (廢物) in my cultivation as a reporter, but I don't remember ever finding something that I could boast as a treasure that others could not emulate.

With many hesitant thoughts, when I first took on the title of newspaper reporter, the seniors in this field told me many requirements necessary for this profession. Thorough observation, quick action, fair thinking, accurate judgment, etc., are the attitudes of a reporter when encountering an event, and subjective composition, concise technique, clear writing, and popular style are the most important techniques for newspaper articles. Even now, I think that if I had all these qualities, I could have succeeded as a distinguished newspaper reporter, and I would have something to present as my treasure. When I face an event or write an article, I often get too absorbed in it, so I don't know whether it is good or bad at the time. After the article I wrote and worked on appeared in the newspaper, I read it over and over again and thought about the course of my work. Only after this process would I conclude that I had worked without fault in this case, and feel satisfied that this article was not embarrassing to me. More often than not, I felt more ashamed to reveal that I had written this article than satisfied. If someone praised my article, I would often feel embarrassed to the point of blushing. <46> I wouldn't dare to say I have any treasure. However, I know that I have one characteristic necessary for a newspaper reporter that no one else knows: I believe in it myself. Not being satisfied with my still incomplete self is the only creed that keeps me enduring this profession. Because I am not satisfied, I put in some effort. Will there be someone who will say that this effort is my treasure? I, a still nameless newspaper reporter, have effort. Healthy body - constant effort - can this only be my treasure?

Decorative Brain

Painter Chang Bŏk

If you simply ask what a painter's treasure is, it's difficult to answer right away. Because if you say "painter," their ideals and perspectives will differ depending on their specialized field, so it's difficult for me alone to say what a painter's treasure is. But if I were to say it quickly, a painter's treasure is what they see with their eyes and draw with their hands, so perhaps eyes and hands could be called treasures. However, simply seeing with your eyes and drawing with your hands doesn't make you a painter, and what a so-called painter draws doesn't necessarily have artistic value.

I originally have an interest in murals and want to dedicate my entire life to them. Therefore, it is difficult for me to speak about the treasures of other types of painters, let alone find them. But what is a painter's treasure - from my point of view -? I would say it is a decorative brain. There are different schools and styles of painters, such as futurism, expressionism, and realism, but I think that a painting should neither be detached from reality nor be artistically valuable as reality itself. Some artists separate art and reality, saying that art has nothing to do with reality and exists somewhere in the air. However, art detached from reality is ultimately just useless effort, and drawing reality as it is also has no artistic value. <47> If you ask what a painter's treasure is, it may seem like I'm dodging the question, but you need to add ideas that come from your head to something that exists and decorate it. To say it again, at least a painter as an artist must have a decorative brain.

A Pianist's Treasure

Kim Yŏng-hwan

A pianist’s treasure is their hands. Everyone needs hands, but for a pianist, their hands are more precious than anything else. Because these hands are treasures, they take special care and cherish them.

It goes without saying that they must always be healthy, but they can only play ping-pong as exercise. It goes without saying that their hands must always be soft and nimble, and they must wear gloves even in summer. Wearing gloves in summer is not to make their hands beautiful, but to keep their skin soft because the skin on their hands becomes rough when exposed to the sun.

However, it's not good if the hands are too soft. That's why the sound is different even when a man and a woman play the same song.

There are several books on these treasure hands as part of the music curriculum, so it's impossible to say everything in one word. But hands are hands, and all the spirit must be focused on the fingertips, and all the heart must be gathered at the fingertips.

People think that being a musician is very easy and luxurious, but they worry about their instruments more than a precious metal merchant worries about their merchandise, and they cherish them as much as their lives. In the West, pianists insure their fingers for hundreds of thousands of won, but there are no insurance companies in the East that recognize the treasure of musicians to that extent. But anyway, fingers are that precious to pianists.

Sacrificing Everything for the Vocal Cords

Vocalist An Ki-yŏng

Without asking, a vocalist's treasure is their vocal cords above all else. <48> However, just as everyone cherishes the vocal cords they have, they cherish and value those vocal cords as a treasure and take extra care.

A vocalist's life is their vocal cords. Therefore, they change their entire personality and tastes and sacrifice everything for this life and treasure. Can I tell you about the unique hardships that are different from how people usually take care of their vocal cords?

The most terrifying thing for a vocalist is a cold. Just as someone who owns a gold ingot, which they consider to be their entire asset and entire life, is always afraid of thieves, the most terrifying thing for a vocalist is a cold. Therefore, they fear and take precautions in a different sense than other people who take care not to catch a cold for their health.

Even the words they say in ordinary times must be musical, and they must not make illogical sounds. If they are a baritone, they must speak in the tone of a baritone, and if they are a tenor, they must speak in the tone of a tenor. They must always be happy, even when they are not in a good mood. They can't even cough freely. Do you know how much extra effort they have to make to keep their treasure like a treasure? Even when playing and laughing joyfully on New Year's Day, they can't shout as they please. For example, if a couple is fighting, they can't shout even if they are angry, and they have to force themselves to eat food they don't want to eat. After all, it's harmful to them if they shout or don't eat when they're angry.

They can't eat whatever they want either. Of course, everyone knows that they can't eat anything irritating, but they can't eat anything that leaves powder. Sometimes they feel sluggish and want to eat something spicy, but they are afraid to do so for the sake of their vocal cords. Alcohol and cigarettes are of course prohibited. What's strange is that singers can't eat walnuts.

Also, they must sleep a certain amount of time every day. When they weren't singing, they would stay up all night reading novels that they liked, but now they have to put down the book and go to bed even if they are enjoying it. Therefore, vocalists dedicate their entire lives to their vocal cords. <49>

Above All, Composure

Female Aviator Yi Chŏng-hŭi

What's the point of asking what an aviator's treasure is? It's the airplane, right? But the real treasure is something else. The real treasure is elsewhere than in machines that can be made by human power.

The body! A healthy body is a treasure and happiness for everyone, not just aviators.

If you ask which part of the body is the most precious, I can't immediately pick just one because an aviator can't function if only one part is perfect. Therefore, instead of singling out one thing as the treasure of an aviator, I will tell you what you need to be to become an aviator, even if it deviates from the question a bit.

You must be tall. For no particular reason, you must be tall. There are many people in Japan who are trying to become aviators but fail the first test because they are short. Since I've started talking about parts, let's talk about the eyes.

Even a one-eyed or cross-eyed person can become a politician or writer, but an aviator's eyes must be perfect. When flying five or six thousand meters, or even ten thousand meters or more, you can find your direction with a compass, but you determine your route by looking at the rivers, mountains, railroads, and islands below. Therefore, you can't be nearsighted or farsighted.

Then there are the ears. Ms. Kim Mi-ri of Kŭnhwa School would only hear thunder if it struck right next to her, but she is still enthusiastic about education and takes care of all her business despite having ears like a cliffside. But if an aviator has even slightly impaired hearing, their life is already over. If something goes wrong with the engine and the sound is strange, it's difficult to hear because of the sound of the propeller, but if something goes wrong with any part of the engine, you must quickly recognize the sound and make an emergency landing. <50> What would happen if you couldn't hear it and just kept flying? Therefore, keen hearing is also a treasure that determines life! To say it this way, the nose, which can smell the burning of the engine and know whether something is wrong, would also be a treasure that determines life. I can't say which treasure is more or less important because it's not like I have philanthropy or a fair mind. Mentality and courage are also treasures of an aviator, but since I'm talking about the treasure of an aviator, I'm assuming that all of these things are perfect. If I had to pick one treasure, I would ultimately pick composure. Even if your nose is perfect and your ears are perfect and you know that something is wrong somewhere, you will lose your life in an instant if you lose your composure. People often die horribly in crashes, and they inevitably lost their composure. Even if something goes wrong during flight, the engine rarely breaks down to the point of complete destruction, so you should remain calm and find a place to make an emergency landing. Think about it. Could you survive if you panic and rush around in the air? This is just my opinion, so other aviators may disagree!

A Cartoonist's Three Treasures

Cartoonist Kim Kyu-t'aek

Since there are no cartoonists in Korea who specialize in cartoons yet, I don't have any special treasures to present as a general artist, especially for cartoons. But if I had to present something, I would say the following few things based on my experience.

First, wit and humor. Wit and humor (酒落과 滑稽) are essential to cartoons, so sensitivity to wit and humor is necessary for the subject matter.

Second, imagination. Wit and humor lose their life through explanation. Therefore, the charm of cartoons lies in expressing what cannot be described in words with pictures, so a rich imagination is necessary for composition and creation. <51> Third, common sense. Everyone agrees and demands common sense, not just artists, but especially cartoonists, because the factual material of cartoonists involves all aspects of society. Therefore, cartoonists need the accumulated common sense of novelists, as well as abundant visual common sense.

For Us Violinists, Fingers Are a Treasure

Violinist Ch'oe Ho-yŏng

If anyone denies that a violinist's treasure is their fingers, I will roll up my sleeves and fight them.

However, if you ask a pianist, they will also say that their fingers are their most precious treasure. But pianos have fixed keys, so you just have to follow them and hit them. Violins, on the other hand, have no fixed intervals, and you have to press the strings with your fingers to make the right sound, just like a clown performing tricks on a tightrope. If you make even a small mistake, you will fail or fall off the rope and lose your name instantly. If you press the seemingly empty strings wrong, the sound will change, and your reputation as a violinist will disappear in an instant. But the skill of a person is so mysterious that it is captured properly.

The violinist's fingers! How precious they are. Those fingers make strange sounds wherever they are pressed, making people happy or sad, or expressing sad feelings!

Since those fingers are treasures, they are treasures that cannot be bought for any amount of money. But if it's a treasure, it should be something that only you have, not something that everyone has, like a mute or a deaf person. And if anyone can become a violinist with practice, what's so great about it? <52> But the skill to cherish the fingers that others also have as a treasure and to regard them as life is also a great treasure.

The fingers that are the treasure of violinists! For these fingers, they can't exercise as they please, let alone eat spicy food, and they have to kill their hobbies and interests to keep their fingers soft. They take care of their hands almost the same way as pianists or violinists.

But I'm a pseudo-musician, so I do whatever I want and don't take any precautions. But if you want to do it properly, there are many difficult things.

Slender Legs Are a Dancer's Treasure

Ch'oe Sŭng-hŭi

I have never found anything that I would call a "treasure" in myself, nor have I ever dreamed of it being anywhere in my body. Therefore, it is difficult to answer immediately when you ask what my treasure is.

However, if you were to find a "treasure" in me, I cannot think of it apart from dance.

The reason is that I am currently studying dance at the Ishii Baku Dance Poetry Research Institute, and I also want to dedicate my life to dance art in the future. Dance is, needless to say, an art that tries to express something through physical movement. The most important part of the physical movement is, of course, the mind that controls all movement, but if you think about it internally, you can't help but count the legs first.

Therefore, if you leave aside the most important mental function and brain in dance art, the legs (足) can be said to be the most precious treasure for dance artists.

Furthermore, in terms of a woman's physical beauty, the legs play the most important role in constructing the overall "beauty." In Europe and America, beautiful legs are considered the most important thing.

Also, the fact that famous dancers and movie actresses have their legs insured for huge amounts shows how precious legs (足) are to women's physical beauty or to dancers and actresses.

The "beauty" of legs is first of all health, and it is represented by being naturally straight and slender.

However, depending on the lineage, race, or living <53> conditions, it is difficult to be born with beautiful legs. It is probably even more difficult than being born with a beautiful face.

Therefore, recently, various sports and other physical exercises have been used to correct the shape of the legs throughout the world.

Also, there is such a correction method in the basic exercises of new dance, and in the basic exercises of the Dance Poetry created by Mr. Ishii Baku, and also in the recently completed "Expression Movement for Children," there is a correction method for the shape of the legs as part of the basic exercises.

Fortunately or unfortunately for myself, I inherited a large physique and health from my parents, but I am not confident in the "beauty" of my legs. I usually pay a lot of attention and focus on exercises other than basic exercises (such as jump rope), but I hate it when they get thicker than necessary. And of course, I pay attention to food, but there is no way to eat to make the shape of the legs beautiful.

Therefore, in the true sense, to become a great dance artist, I always wish I had a beautiful body and beautiful legs when I look back on my poor talent and strength! I don't think this desperate wish will be fulfilled. Looking at it this way, I may not have anything that I can call a "treasure," but I have only mentioned that the "treasure" of a dancer is their legs. <54>

新聞記者의 寶物

金枓白

무명한 文筆 노동자 중의 한사람인 나에게 『新聞記者로서의 나의 寶物』이라고 내여 노흘 만한 것이 잇슬 것갓지 아니하다. 설사 잇다하여도 다른 사람이 인정하야 주기 전에 내 자신이 말하기는 쓱스러운 일이다.
사 년 남짓한 신문기자 생활에 나는 흔히 나의 수양중에 기자로써의 『廢物』은 발견한 때가 잇섯지만은 이것 만은 다른 사람이 추종할 수 업는 보물이라고 自矜할 만한 무엇을 발견하야 본 기억은 업다.
여러 가지 躊躇하는 생각으로 내가 처음 신문 기자 名啣을 가지고 나설 때에 斯界의 선배 여러분은 이 직업에 필요한 여러 가지 요건을 만히 일너 주엇다. 透徹한 관찰 敏活한 동작 공평한 心思 정확한 판단 등은 사건에 접하는 기자의 태도이요 주체적 구상 간명한 기술 똑똑한 글시 통속적 필법 등은 신문기사에 가장 중요한 기술이라고 들은 듯하다. 지금 생각에도 이 모든 점을 구비하얏스면 내가 당당한 신문기자로 출세할 수도 잇슬 것갓고 이것이 나의 보물이라고 내여노을 거리도 잇슬 것 갓다. 나는 무슨 사건을 대할 때나 기사를 쓸 때에 흔이 거기에 너무 열중하는 까닭에 그 당시에 잘 되고 못 되는 것을 모른다. 내가 활동하야 쓴 기사가 신문에 나타난 이후에 여러번 여러번 그것을 닑어 보고 또 활동하든 경과를 생각해 본 뒤에 이번 사건만은 귀감이 업시 활동을 하얏다. 이 기사만은 별노 붓그럽지 아니하다고 스스로 만족할 때보담 남에게 이것이 내가 쓴 기사라고 내여노키가 붓그러울 만콤 수집을 만콤 불만을 늣기는 때가 만앗다. 혹 남이 나의 무슨 기사를 칭찬하여 주는 이가 잇스면 도리혀 얼골이 붉어질 만콤 듯기가 거북하엿든<46> 것이 前例이엿다. 이러한 나에게 무슨 보물이 잇다고 내 스스로 말을 하랴. 그러나 신문기자에게 필요한 성격 한 가지가 나에게 잇는 것을 나는 아모도 몰내 나 스스로 밋는 점이 잇다. 아직 未成한 自我에 만족치 안는 것 이것이 내가 지금 이 직업을 내여 노치안코 견듸는 다만 한 가지 신조이다. 만족치 못함으로 다소간이라도 노력이 따른다. 이 노력이 혹 나의 보물이라고 말하여 줄 사람이 잇슬는지? 아직 無名한 一個 신문기자인 나에게는 노력이 잇다. 건강한 몸-부단의 노력-이것은 나에게만 보물이 된다고 할가?


裝飾的頭腦

화가 張勃

그저 화가의 보물이 무엇이냐 무르면 얼는 대답을 하기가 어렵슴니다. 왜 그러냐하면 화가라고 하면 제 각각 그 전문하는 부문에 따라서 이상과 見地가 다를 것임으로 나 혼자서 무엇이 화가의 보물이라고는 말하기에 어렵슴니다. 그러나 얼는 말하자면 화가의 보물이라면 눈으로 보고 손으로 그리는 것이닛가 아마 눈과 손이 보물이라고 하여도 되겟지요. 그러하니만 눈으로 본대로 손으로 그린다고 화가가 아니요 소위 화가가 그리여 노은 것이라고 반드시 예술적 가치가 잇는 것이 아님니다.
나는 원래가 벽화에 취미가 잇고 또 그것을 위하여서 전 생활을 밧치고저 함니다. 그럼으로 다른 流의 화가의 보물은 말을 하기는 고사하고 차자 내이기에도 어렵슴니다. 만은 화가- 나의 見地에서 잇서서-의 보물이 무엇이냐 하면 장식적 두뇌하고 하겟슴니다. 화가에도 流와 派가 다 각기 달너서 혹은 미래파니 표현파니 사실주의이니 여러 가지 派流가 잇스나 엇제든 그림이라는 것은 실재를 떠나서도 아니되고 실재 그것으로 만도 예술적으로 가치가 업는 것이 아닌가 생각슴니다. 혹은 예술과 현실를 각각 분리식이여 예술이라는 것은 현실과는 아모 상관이 업고 어느 공중에 떠서 잇는 것으로 말하는 예술가도 잇스나 그러나 현실을 떠난 예술은 결국 쓸 데 업는 수고에 지나지 못하고 현실 그대로 실재 그대로를 그려내인 것이 역시 예술적 가치가 업다는 것은 우에도 말하엿스나 화가의 보물은<47>무엇이냐고 무르면 좀 딴청일지 모르나 어느 현재한 것에 나의 머리속에서 우러나오는 생각을 더하야 장식을 하여야 할 것임니다. 한 번 다시 말하면 적어도 예술가로서의 화가는 장식적 두뇌가 업서서는 안 될 것임니다.

피아니스트의 寶物

金永煥

피아니스트의 보물은 손임니다. 누구나 업서서는 안이 되는 손이지만 피아니스트에게 잇서서는 무엇보다도 손이 보물이올시다. 이 손이 보물인 만치 남달리 애써가며 위하고 위함니다.
몸이 늘 건강해야 만 할 것은 물논이려니와 운동으로는 핑퐁박게 못함니다. 늘 손이 부드러워야하고 민활하여야 할 것은 말할 것도 업거니와 녀름에도 장갑을 끼고 단여야 함니다. 녀름에 장갑을 끼는 것이 손을 곱게 하기 위하는 것이 아니라 볏에 기을면 손의 피부가 뻣벗해지닛가 보드랍게 하기 위하야 그러는 것임니다.
그러나 손이 너모 부드러워도 덜 좃슴니다. 남자와 여자가 똑가튼 곡조를 彈秦하드라도 그 음이 다른 것은 그 까닭임니다.
이 보물인 손에 대한 책이 음악과의 課程으로도 몃 권식이나 되는 까닭에 엇더타고 한마듸로 말 할 수가 업슴니다. 그러나 손도 손이려니와 손끄테 온 정신이 몰녀야 하고 온 마음이 손끄트로 뭉쳐야 합니다.

또한 요새 새로운 무용의 기본 연습 중에도 그러한 교정법이 잇고 石井漠선생의 創案한 舞踊詩의 기본 연습 중에도 또 최근 완성된 『어린이를 위한 표현 운동』에도 기본 연습의 일부로 주의 깁흔 다리의 형체미의 교정법이 잇슴니다.
나 자신으로는 幸인지 不幸인지 남달니 큰 체구와 건강을 兩親에게 바덧슴니다만은 다리의 『美』로는 과연 자신이 업슴니다. 평소에 무던히 주의하여 기본 연습 이외의 운동(가령 줄넘기)에 마음을 쓰고 잇스나 잘못하면 필요이상으로 굴거지는 데는 질색이올시다. 그러고 음식물에도 물론 주의를 하지만은 그러타고 다리의 모양을 아름답게 하는 식양법이 잇슬 까닭은 업지요.
