1933년 06월 01일
湖底의 秘密(第2回), 原名 눈푸른 處女 (The Secret of the Lake Bottom (Part 2), Original Title: Blue-Eyed Maiden)
모-리스 루부랑(作), 尹成學(譯) (Maurice Leblanc (Author), Yun Seong-hak (Translator))
소설 (Novel)

The Secret of the Lake Bottom
						Maurice Leblanc (Author), Yun Seong-hak (Translator)

Ri Cheong-nyeon hid his surprise and said naturally,
"However, if I were Arsène Lupin in his glorious position, I would never have done such a foolish thing in front of you today. Rest assured on that point. However, it is a pleasure to be mocked by you as the phantom thief Arsène Lupin, the gentleman thief who once startled the whole world, even as just a mere Ri Do-u. Haha."
The woman held out a chocolate box.
"Would you like one? It's a consolation for your defeat. And I must excuse myself."
"No, it seems there's more to say."
"There isn't,"
the woman replied.
Ri Cheong-nyeon said a few more words and got up. He had been completely defeated by the woman tonight.

The name Ri Do-u was an alias, and far from just Europe, Arsène Lupin, the chivalrous thief (俠名) whose name echoed throughout the world, was a hero indeed.
The phantom thief Arsène Lupin, who cared nothing for emperors, royalty, aristocrats, or the wealthy.
If any strange fact fell into his hands, he would solve the eternal riddle. If it caught his eye, Arsène Lupin, with his clear wisdom and strange means, would be active even in the most hellish situations.

When heinous thieves arose and spilled the blood of cruel demons (妖魔無殘), when innocent people shed tears in vain and lovely beauties suffered in dire straits (窮境), Arsène Lupin, the passionate chivalrous thief (怪俠盜) who powerfully confronted tens of thousands of murderous devils (殺人魔鬼) with his bare hands, miraculously striking down the wicked and helping the innocent, Arsène Lupin, hiding under the alias Ri Do-u, seeking a life of tumultuous events from a life of mundane nothingness, had unintentionally followed a beautiful woman he met on the street, even started a conversation, and failed.
Lupin freely imagined dreamlike scenarios. In his illusion, it had already spread like the cold of May, becoming a chill and a blue wave (碧波). It became so much that he could hardly distinguish whether it was the eyes of that Englishwoman or the eyes of a Parisian girl.
Thinking about it, the Parisian girl seemed to have smiled at him. A smile, a smile... He also wore a smile and fell deeply, deeply asleep...
He was intoxicated by the joyous feeling of a pleasant journey and forgot his usual caution about a tense life. And so, he let his guard down and fell deeply asleep.
That was a mistake. A night train with few passengers was not something to be trusted. He trusted it. That was a mistake.
Therefore, he didn't notice that the door of the fourth car (第四號車) quietly opened and three villains, masked and dressed in shiny, long capes, entered.
He made another mistake. He forgot to turn off the lights in the car before going to sleep. If he had only turned off the lights and gone to sleep, since it would be dark, the thieves would have had to turn on the side lights to do anything.
If that had happened, Arsène Lupin, the gentleman thief who didn't overlook even the mountains and trees, would surely have opened his eyes.
Thus, he heard no sound, saw no shadows, and slept deeply.

One masked man stood guard at the doorway with a pistol (拳銃) in his hand. The two villains exchanged a password and each pulled out a club from their pockets. The two villains attacked two passengers sleeping in the corner.
A command (命令) like whisper was heard. But even if that low, mumbling sound had been unintentional, how could Arsène Lupin, of all people in the world, not have opened his eyes!
He startled, opened his eyes, and stretched his arms and legs freely. That was the moment. That was another mistake. Already, something was aiming between his eyebrows and coming down to strike.
"Ack!"
As soon as he screamed, he pressed his chest and clutched his neck. A black shadow flickered before his eyes and passed towards the woman's side.
Like a person drowning in the dark night, he lost control of his limbs. He was bound all over, and like a wild horse (馬), he was gagged with gravel and had a sack placed over his head. The wad of bills in his wallet was, needless to say, stolen.
"It's going well,"
a whispering voice said.
"But this is unexpected. There's quite a bit to use. What did you do with that? Did you tie it up?"
"He thrashed about so much that his eyes went blurry."
"But he doesn't seem completely dead."
Then a violent noise, moaning, and the sound of a desperate struggle shook the car. After a while, a scream was heard... a woman's scream...
"You thieves! Damned devils!"
she barely managed to shout in a mosquito-like voice.
"Hey, this one's attacking... this one's biting... but do you know that girl?"
"Of course. She's the one I told you about."
"Gag her so she can't speak!"
After that, the moaning sound became barely audible. The scream turned into a wail, and the wail turned into a painful moan. But the woman fought to the end.
Lupin sensed in his dreamlike state that the woman was resisting with her last strength. But somehow, it was coming to an end.
At that moment, a low voice was heard from the doorway. It seemed to be the one standing guard.
"Wait a bit? Take your hands off for a moment! You haven't killed her yet, have you?"
"Yeah, I don't know what happened! Let's get rid of her quickly."
"Wait... quiet... hey... damn..."
The two villains went out. It sounded like they were arguing about something in the doorway.
Lupin, who had begun to move his body slightly, heard the villains whispering at this moment,
"Oh, coming this way... came to that car... quickly, quickly... the conductor will come."
It seemed the two villains were crossing over to the car with the two passengers.
At this moment, Lupin was already loosening the ropes and moving his mouth to get the gravel out.
The moaning of the Englishwoman next to Lupin was fading, barely audible like a mosquito. Lupin struggled with all his strength to untie the ropes.
If he hesitated, he wouldn't be able to save that Englishwoman. Every moment felt like three autumns. But the ropes were hardly coming undone.
However, he barely managed to remove the sack from his head. The woman was tied up and knocked over on the floor.
A pistol shot was heard in the distance. The three masked villains were fighting with the two passengers. After a while, one of the villains ran out, rattling something small.
Several minutes later, the train slowed down. It seemed to be the opportunity the villains were waiting for.
Lupin finally despaired. However, he struggled to untie the ropes and, enduring the pain, shouted with all his might through his gravel-filled mouth.
"Come to your senses! Come to your senses... I'll rescue you soon... please come to your senses. Yes!"
The villain seemed to be slitting the woman's throat and breaking the bone behind her neck.
The woman cried out with a painful moan, "Eum!" and fell over, startled. Thus, her upper body leaned towards Lupin. He was suffocating, making a wheezing sound in his throat. But even in that state,
"Please... please listen to me... and pick it up... please pick it up..."
"What are you saying? Yes?"
Lupin asked.
"Take out my wallet... the documents inside... my father doesn't know anything... please... please..."
She shook her head, unable to bear it, and passed away... The train stopped.

Lupin, the hero of the era, was at a loss for what to do about this incident, which was like a nightmare.
In fact, it was far more transcendent than he had imagined. The sound of people talking outside the car grew louder. Railway workers from the nearest station had chased after them, bringing laborers who were working on the tracks at that time.
A noisy commotion arose. They were looking for the car where the alarm had sounded. The conductor untied Lupin's ropes, listened to his explanation, opened the window of the doorway, and blew a whistle.
"Hey, it's here! It's this car!"
Then he said to Lupin,
"Is the woman dead? That young lady!"
"Yes... she was killed with her throat slit. Not only that, it seems the two passengers who were riding in that corner are also dead."
The station workers jumped in through the doorway. In the car at the very end, two bodies were lying. There were no signs of disarray. Nothing on the shelves. No bags or luggage.
At this time, the station workers tried to open the door to the right of the conductor, but the door was locked from the inside.
Judging by this, the villains must have used the outer door. Several people crowded in through the outer door, which was not locked. The car where the tragedy had occurred was filled with people.
Right at this moment, a man stepped forward, shouting in an imposing and loud voice.
"Do not touch the items, equipment, corpses, or anything else! ... No, leave that pistol as it is! It is a very important piece of evidence... and everyone in the room except the staff, get out! Also, separate this car and let the others depart! Stationmaster, let the train depart!"
Even in the midst of the chaos, he was directing and ordering as if he were a leader, the most composed and gentlemanly of them all. Perhaps he was a man accustomed to leading and commanding many people.
Lupin glanced at the man and was startled. The man was the strange gentleman Ma Taek (馬宅) who had initially followed the Englishwoman, argued with the blue-eyed woman, and wouldn't even lend a light for a cigarette.
He stood at the entrance of the murdered woman's car, chasing away the onlookers.
"Stationmaster!"
he shouted loudly.
"Stationmaster! I know it's a bother, but please keep a close watch. And gather the station staff. Also, call the nearest police station from here. Call a doctor too. Send an urgent message to the prosecutor's office. It's a big case!"
"The laborers working on the tracks saw the shadows of the thieves and are chasing after them now. If they cross the tracks, there is a small forest. There is a large road to Seoul from that forest, and they are chasing near that area. And if they catch the thieves, they are to bring them here."
He ordered powerfully with an authoritative attitude.
Lupin was even more surprised. Therefore, he gradually regained his usual composure. What kind of person was the strange gentleman? With what authority did he take such an overwhelming attitude? What secrets did he hide beneath his keen and shining eyes?
There was no one left in the car. Only the conductor remained. Lupin wanted to go to the conductor's side, but Ma Taek stopped him. Ma Taek didn't seem to recognize him, so he spoke.
"Why are you stopping me? I was here, so I'm going back to my seat."
"The scene of the tragedy is entirely under the jurisdiction of the police. No one is allowed to enter or leave without permission."
At that moment, the conductor, who was standing nearby, interjected.
"He is also a victim. He was tied up and even had gravel in his mouth." <54>
"Oh, I'm sorry, but..."
Ma Taek stubbornly refused to listen.
"It's an order."
"An order?"
"It's my order."
Lupin folded his arms and showed a rebellious attitude.
"However, sir, with what right are you saying such things? I believe I have no obligation to obey someone with no authority who is directing and ordering."
Ma Taek smoothly, or rather with the most solemn attitude, took out a business card from his pocket.
"Ma Taek - Special Secret Investigator, Ministry of Home Affairs"
If he was a special secret investigator from the Ministry of Home Affairs, there was no room for argument. He had no choice but to stay put.
"Regarding this case, I am directing and commanding everything as a result of consulting and understanding with the stationmaster. In other words, it is because I recognize it as necessary for my duties."
Lupin was a little upset, but he couldn't say anything. Lupin hadn't thought much about the name Ma Taek until now, but he suddenly remembered the time when Ma*, with his extraordinary insight and quick actions, had gained fame in a major case he was involved in before.
He was at least a first-class detective. "A big shot has stepped up," Lupin thought.
"This is my great blunder (一大失策),"
he regretted inwardly.
Lupin, with his abundant sense of justice, wanted to be active by the side of the Englishwoman and carry out her last wishes, but he was so excessively startled and bewildered by the masked blue-eyed woman that he had spent too much time in a daze.
But now there was no other recourse. "But you handsome devil or lecher, I'll see you soon! What reason did a guy like you have for being on this train, and how are you involved with these two women? I'll find out every detail..." But until then, he would pretend not to know and remain hidden.
Having made up his mind, he listened to the sound of "Detective" and, with an attitude as if he were very embarrassed,
"I didn't know anything and was rude. Please forgive me. Also, I'm not always in Seoul and am always traveling abroad, but I have heard much about you. So, in that earring (耳飾) case..."
Hearing those words, Ma Taek seemed to gain even more strength.
"Yes. I was involved in that earring case. Somehow, it went well on its own. But in today's case, I want to show off my skills as clearly as possible to the extent of my abilities. That's why I'm thinking of investigating it thoroughly on my own before the police and prosecutors arrive..."
"Of course. But if you need me, I can postpone my trip by a day and stay here today."
"Thank you. Please do so."
The conductor once again stated what he had witnessed as much as possible and prepared to depart. The passenger car where the damage had occurred was switched to another track, and the rest departed.
Ma Taek began his investigation. In order to keep Lupin, Ri Cheong-nyeon, away from the scene, he ordered him to go to the station and bring the sack that the victim had been wearing. Lupin didn't say a word and did as he was told, walking down from the train and peeking through the glass window.
Indeed, Ma Taek was standing up the murdered Englishwoman's body and reaching into her travel coat to find something.
There was a reddish leather-covered bundle hidden in the inside pocket of the coat. He took out the bundle and opened it. There were documents inside. He began to read them intently.
Lupin was only looking at the back of Ma Taek's head over the glass window, so it was only a presumption that "this is it."
"I'll get you, you damn bastard. Lupin will catch you in the end... Those documents were already entrusted to me. How could I hand them over to anyone else! Let's see what you're up to!"
He muttered these words under his breath and ran to the station. He brought the sack as he was told. And he brought the stationmaster's wife and mother to take care of the woman's body.
He heard from Ma Taek that they had seen the two villains running into the forest and were now surrounding the forest.
"Is there any way to identify them?"
he asked.
"There's no way to know. But one of the criminals is a cripple, and later they found a woman's shoe among the trees. They say it's a woman's shoe!"
"Is there anything else?"
"No, there's nothing else."
People carefully laid the woman's body down.
Lupin bid a final farewell to the beautiful, unfortunate woman who had been his companion for one night. And in his heart, he said, "I will definitely avenge your death. Even if I couldn't protect you and save your life, I swear I will avenge your death. Rest assured and sleep deeply, deeply forever."
At the same time as he swore this, he thought of the blue-eyed woman. And thinking of the mysterious and incomprehensible woman and their reunion, he swore again and again to hate and take revenge. As he did this, he stroked the eyes of the unfortunate young woman and closed them. And he covered her pale face with a white cloth.
"This woman was truly lovely," he said.
"Do you know this woman's name?"
"How could I know such a thing? I don't know,"
Ma Taek replied in a slightly flustered tone.
"But there's a travel ticket here..."
"No, we can't open it without the police being present."
Ma Taek said as he cut the belt.
"But it's strange that the villains didn't steal this."
"Could there be documents inside..."
"Until the police arrive..."
he said again.
"Even so, from you, the thieves stole everything, but from this woman, it seems they didn't take anything... no watch, no diamond ring, no necklace... no pearls on her wrist..."
Lupin told the story of this incident. He earnestly testified as if it were a great pleasure to help this famous detective and discover the truth.

However, he didn't say a word about the fact that a woman was among the villains.
Ma Taek listened intently, asked two or three questions, told one of the station workers to keep a close watch, and went to inspect the car where the two passengers had been murdered, guided by another station worker. The two victims looked very similar, as if they were brothers. Both had slightly rough faces, and their eyebrows were dark and thick.
And they were wearing the same gray suits and ill-fitting pants. Among them, the younger man had been shot between the eyebrows, and then a fatal blow to the neck.
With a very calm attitude, Ma Taek investigated for a long time, then examined the bodies without moving them from where they had fallen and covered their faces with white cloths.
"Detective!"
Lupin said, cleverly using Ma Taek's esteemed pride.
"You must already know the truth. You seem to be very confident... What will happen in this case after all? Can you tell me a little?"
"It's okay to tell you..."
Ma Taek took Lupin to another car and began to talk.
"The police officers and doctors will be here soon. Then it will be okay to tell you my opinion and a summary of my initial investigation results."
"Don't chirp, you damn bastard,"
Lupin scoffed inwardly.
"There's no one more reliable than me for a guy like you. Heung-heung..."
He took an attitude of being truly embarrassed by the unexpected trust. Ma Taek became increasingly pleased with himself.
"Sir, please sit down for a moment. In fact, there are still some kinds of contradictions (矛盾), and there may be room for consideration regarding the details, but leaving that aside, if I were to rely on my own thoughts, I think two extremely important starting points have clearly emerged. First of all, the young Englishwoman who was next to you was murdered because she was mistaken for someone else. You may think it's strange to say such a thing, but I have definite evidence. Initially, taking advantage of the slowing of the train, the villains in that car (I remember seeing them from afar, and there were definitely three of them) first attacked and robbed you. And then they attacked the woman next to you and tried to kill her by slitting her throat... Then, suddenly, they abandoned it and invaded that car over there... Why did they suddenly change their minds and go to another car?... What was the reason? ... It's because they mistook the person. Because the woman was wearing a blanket, they mistook her for the two passengers they had been planning to rob. So, when they attacked, it was a woman. After thinking they had failed, they frantically roamed the doorway with a desperate force, and the car where they found the men they were aiming for was that car over there. But the two passengers resisted. Because time was running out, they ended their lives with a single gunshot and ran away, taking the bags, luggage, and even hats."
Lupin was quite surprised by this reasoning. It was not just conjecture but a brilliant insight. Detective Ma Taek indicated the course of events with surprising logic.
"Secondly..."
the detective, whose heart had softened due to the other's compliance, continued to speak. He handed Lupin a small, beautifully crafted silver case.
"This is what I found under the cushion (쿳숀)." <56>
"Isn't it a tobacco case?"
"Yes, it's an old-fashioned tobacco case... But this is a cigarette case. There happen to be seven cigarettes in it... These are cigarettes... What do you think? Isn't this a woman's cigarette..."
"Men may smoke such things, I don't know,"
Lupin said with a smile.
"Was it only men..."
"I believe it's a woman's item."
"There's no way it could be."
"Then smell the scent of this case."
Ma Taek held it out under Lupin's nose.
Lupin smelled it and nodded.
"Ah- indeed... indeed... a woman's scent... It seems like the cigarette case was put in a pocket together with a handkerchief, powder, or perhaps perfume... It's definitely for women. It has a special scent."
"Then what will happen?"
"What will happen? I don't know. The two men who were murdered here and the two men who attacked, killed, and ran away..."
"Can't we say one man and one woman?"
"Eh- a woman... then one of the villains is a woman?"
"Yes, what about looking at this woman's cigarette case?"
"How can I say that? I don't think there's enough evidence."
"I have other evidence as well."
"What is it?"
"These shoes I was talking about... These shoes that were found in the forest, what do you think? Seeing this, doesn't it confirm the second important point I'm making? That one of the criminals is a woman?"
Ma Taek's insight seemed to be mocking Lupin. Even Lupin unconsciously groaned.
"Yes, it's truly insightful. I admire you. You're truly wonderful! I'm impressed! You..."
and he changed his tone again.
"Is that all? Is there anything else that was discovered?"
"Hahaha,"
Ma Taek laughed.
"You'll naturally know if you just keep watching!"
"Then will you be investigating all night tonight?"
"Yes, I'll just be working hard until those two fugitives are arrested. If they only do as I instruct, they'll be caught soon!"
Lupin listened to Ma Taek's eloquence, pretending to be obtuse and making a face as if the details of the incident he was explaining were not what he thought. And in the end, yawning and stretching,
"Well- Detective! Please do your best... Actually, I'm so tired because I was so surprised that I need to get some sleep for an hour or two..."
"Ah, you can sleep,"
Ma Taek said.
"You can sleep wherever you want... Go and sleep in that car over there... I'll make sure no one wakes you... I need to do some reasoning about the case too, and then I need to get some sleep."
Lupin immediately climbed into bed and closed the curtain. Would Lupin, the great thief of the era, be sleeping peacefully in such an emergency? <61>

「이번 사건에 대하야 내가 일체를 지휘하고 명령하고 잇는 것은 역장과 상의하고 량해를 어든 결과야. 즉 말하자면 나의 직무상 필요하다고 인정하고 잇는 때문이야」
루팡은 속이 좀 상하엿지만 아모 말도 못하고 잇섯다. 마택의 일흠을 루팡은 지금까지 별로 생각하고 잇지 안엇스나 문든 전번에 관게한 큰 사건 중에 마*이 비상한 명찰을 가지고 쾌완(快腕)을 저어 명성이 자자하든 일을 생각하엿다.
적어도 일류명탐정이엿다. 큼직한 자가 나섯고나 하고 루팡은 생각하엿다.
「이것은 나의 일대실책(一大失策)이다」
하고 그는 속으로 후회햇다.
영국부인의 겻헤잇서 활약하고 그 최후의 유언을 실행하고저 함에는 협의심(協議心)이 풍부한 루팡이엿지만 그 복면한 눈푸른 녀자 때문에 너무나 과도히 놀나고 정신이 휘황하야 망연히 시간을 만히 넘겨버렷다.
그러나 지금은 다시 구처가 업다. 그러나 야이 미남자인지 색마인지 이자식 지금에 곳 보자! 너와 가튼 자식이 엇재서 무슨 리유로 이 긔차에 탓섯스며 엇지하야 이 사건에 뛰여들어 두 녀자의게 관게한 것을 갈피갈피 세세히 차저낼 터이니… 그러나 그때까지는 몰으는 체하고 숨어 잇슬 터이다.
이러케 자긔 마음을 결정하고는 탐정나리님이라는 소리를 듯고는 아조 황공한 듯한 태도로
「저는 아모것도 몰으고 실례하엿습니다. 용서하십시요. 또 저는 늘 서울에 잇지 안이하고 언제든지 외국에만 가서 잇습니다만은 선성은 익히 들엇습니다. 그래 그 귀거리(耳飾)사건에도…」
그 말을 듯든 마택은 더욱 힘이 나는 듯 하다.
「응. 그 귀거리사건에 관게한 일이 잇섯습니다. 그것은 엇전일인지 제절로 좀 잘 되여갓습니다. 그러치만 오늘 사건은 그보다도 더 나의 힘 자라는 데까지는 선명하게 수완을 흔들어 보고저 합니다. 그러기에 순사들이 오기 전에 검사도 오기전에 나의 단독으로 잘 조사해 보려고 생각합니다…」
「그러고 말고요. 그런데 만약 선생께서 필요할 것 가트면 나의 려행을 하로 늣추어서 오늘은 이곳에 체재하여도 좃습니다」
「고맙습니다. 꼭좀 그러케 하여 주시요」
차장은 다시 한번 자긔의 목격한 바를 잇는대로 공술하고 출발하게 되엿다. 피해가 잇든 객차는 달은 선로서 밧구어 너어놋코 그 외에는 출발하엿다.
마택은 조사에 착수하엿다. 그는 루팡인 리청년을 현장에 두지 안으려는 수단으로 그를 식혀 정거장에 가서 피해자가 덥허쓰고 잇든 푸대를 가지고 오라고 하엿다. 루팡은 두 말도 안이하고 식히는 대로 긔차에서 내려 긔차 엽흐로 걸어가며 유리창 밧그로 엿을 보앗다.
과연 마택은 살해당한 영국녀자의 시체를 일으켜 세워놋코는 그 녀자의 려행왜투에 손을 너어 무엇인지 찻고 잇섯다.
외투의 안 포켓트에 감추어 두엇든 불그스름한 가죽에 싼 뭉치가 잇섯다. 그는 그 뭉치를 그내여서 열어보앗다. 그 안에는 서류가 잇섯다. 그는 열심으로 그것을 읽기 시작햇다.
루팡은 유리창 넘어로 마택의 등더머리만 바라보앗슬 뿐임으로 「이갓흐리」라고 추정함에 지나지 못햇다.
「이 망난이가튼 자식 볼 날이 잇스리라. 최후에는 이 루팡이 너를 잡을터이니… 그 서류는 발서 나에게 멧긴 것이다. 나 이외에 또 엇던 자에게 내주고야 될 말이냐! 이 자식 어듸 보자!」
입안엣 소리로 이가티 중얼거리며 정거장으로 뛰여갓다. 그는 식히는대로 푸대를 가지고 왓다. 그리고는 부인의 시체를 치우기 위하야 역장의 부인과 모친을 다리고 왓다.
그는 마택에게 악한 두 사람은 산림 가우데로 달아나는 것을 보고 지금 산림을 포위중이라는 말을 들엇다.
「달니는 알만한 도리가 하나도 업습니까?」
하고 물엇다.
「아모 도리도 업소. 그러치만 범인 한 놈은 절눔바리라는데 그 후 나무사이에 부인 구두짝을 주어서 그런데 그것이 부인구두짝이라나!」
「그 외에는 아모것도 업단 말입니까?」
「네 아모것도 업습니다」
사람들이 녀자의 시체를 고히고히 눕혓다.
루팡은 아름다웟든 하로 밤의 벗 박명하고 불행한 그 녀성의 시체에 최후의 리별을 고햇다. 그리고는 가슴 가운데 「당신의 원수는 내가 꼭 갑허들이겟습니다. 가령 내가 당신의 주위를 직히고 당신의 생명의 급함을 구원할 수는 업섯다고 할지라도 나는 맹서하고＜55＞ 당신의 원수를 갑허들이겟습니다. 안심하시고 영원히 깁히 깁히 잠들어 주시요」
그는 이와 가티 맹서하는 동시에 눈푸른 녀자를 생각햇다. 그리고 불가해의 수수꺽기인 녀자와 다시 맛남을 생각하며 미움과 복수하자는 마음을 몃번이나 몃번이나 맹서햇다. 이가티 하며 박명한 젊은 처녀의 눈을 내리 쓰다듬어 감기여 주엇다. 그리고는 창백한 얼굴에 흰 수건을 덥헛다.
「이 녀자는 참으로 어엽벗섯습니다-」하고 그는 말햇다.
「당신은 이 녀자의 일흠을 아십니까?」
「어떠케 그런것을 알 수가 잇서야지. 나는 모릅니다」
하고 마택은 약간 낭패한 듯한 어조로 대답햇다.
「그러치만 여기 려행권이 …」
「안이 그것은 경관들이 입회하지 안은 이상에 절대로 열어볼 수가 업지」
하고 마택은 혁대를 잘나매면서 말햇다.
「그러나 이것을 악한들이 흠처가지 안은 것은 이상하오」
「이 안에는 서류가튼 것이 들어 잇슬가요…」
「경관들이 오기 전에는…」
하고 그는 재차 말햇다.
「그러타고 하드래도 당신 당신에게서는 강탈하여간 도적들이 이 녀자에게서는 아무것도 빼서간 것이 업는 듯 하구려… 시게라든지 다이야몬드반지라든지 목거리라든지… 손목에 달린 진주라든지…」
루팡은 이번 사건의 이야기를 말하여 주엇다. 그것도 이 유명한 탐정에게 조력해 주어서 진실한 내용을 발견하게 되는 것이 얼마나 질거운 일이라는 듯한 얼골을 하여 가지고 절실하게 공술하여 주엇다.

그러나 그는 악한들 사이에 녀자가 끼엿드란 말은 손톱만치도 입박게 내지 안엇다.
마택은 열심히 듯고 잇다가 두서너 마듸 질문을 한 뒤 역원의 한 사람에게 잘 직히라고 하고는 달은 역원의 안내를 바더 두 려객이 살해당한 차실을 검사하러 나갓다. 피해자가 두 사람은 형제인 듯이 얼골이 아조 비슷하엿다. 두 사람이 다 얼골이 조금 상쓰러운데 눈섭은 시컴엇고 국직하다.
그리고는 가튼 잿빗나는 양복에 좃치 못한 바지를 입고 잇섯다. 그 중에도 젊은 사람은 양미간에 탄환을 바덧고 그 다음에는 목을 마진 것이 치명상이엿다.
아조 침착한 태도로 마택은 오래ㅅ동안 조사를 하고 잇더니 넘어진 채 잇는대로 움지기여 보지도 아니하고 신체검사를 하고나서 흰 수건으로 얼골을 덥헛다.
「탐정님!」
하고 루팡은 마택의 존대한 자존심을 교묘히 리용헤 가며 말햇다.
「벌서 진상을 아시게 되엿겟지요. 네 보아하니 아조 충분한 자신이 잇는 듯 합니다 그려... 대관절 이번 사건은 엇지 될 일이오니까? 조금 들녀 주실 수 업겟습니까?」
「그것은 말하여도 관게업지만…」

「경찰관들도 곳 올터이고 의사도 올터이겟지요. 그러면 나의 의견도 이야기 하고 또 나의 최초의 조사결과 대요도 이야기하여도 좃습니다」
「이 망난이 가튼 자식 지저귀지 말어」
하고 루팡은 속으로 조소햇다.
「너가튼 자식에게는 나보다 더 신용할만한 자는 업겟지. 흥흥…」
그는 의외의 신임(信任)에 진실로 황송한 듯이 굽실숩실 태도를 취햇다. 마택은 점점 양양자득햇다.
「여보. 잠간 안즈시요. 사실은 아즉까지 다소간 어떤 종류의 모순(矛盾)도 잇고 자세한 점에 대해서는 고려할 여지도 잇겟스나 그것은 그만 그대로 두고 나는 나만의 생각에 의할 것 가트면 극히 중요한 두 가지의 출발점이 명백하게 나타낫다고 생각합니다. 위선 첫재는 말입니다. 당신의 겻헤 잇섯다고 하는 그 젊은 영국녀자는 다른 사람인 줄 알고 살해를 당한 것이요. 즉 당신은 그러한 말을 할 것 가트면 응당 이상히 생각할는지도 알 수 업지만 나는 확실한 증거를 쥐고 잇소. 처음에 긔차의 속력이 늣추어 짐을 리용하야 저쪽 차실에 잇든 악한들은(나는 그 놈들을 멀리서 본 것이 생각나는데 확실히 세 놈이 잇섯든 터인데) 위선 당신을 습격해서 강탈햇지요. 그리고는 겻헤잇는 녀자를 습격하야 목을 잘너서 죽이려고 햇지요… 그러다가 갑작이 그것을 버려두고 저쪽 차실로 침입햇구려… 그 곳이란 말이요 엇지하야 급작히도 마음을 가라 다른 차실에 갓슬가? … 무슨 리유일가? 하면 그것은 사람을 잘못본 것이지요. 녀자가 담뇨를 덥허스고 잇섯기 때문에 그놈들이 벼르고 잇든 온 두 려객이라고 잘못본 것입니다. 그리하야 습격하고 보니 그것이 녀자이엿습니다. 실패햇다고 생각한 뒤에 비호가튼 힘으로 구처업시 황망히 랑하를 돌아단여 목적햇든 남자를 차진 것이 저쪽 차실입니다. 그런데 두 려객은 저항햇섯습니다. 시간이 급박해 오니까 총 한 방으로 생명을 끈어놋코는 가방도 행리도 모자까지 흠켜가지고 다라난 것이지요」
루팡도 이 추리에는 적지안케 놀낫다. 추칙 뿐만 아니라 명찰이다. 마택탐정은 놀낸만한 론리에 의하야 사건의 경과를 지시한 것이다.

루팡은 마택의 능변을 들으면서 일부러 멍뚱한 체도 설명하는 사건의 내용이 자긔 생각으로는 그러치 안타는 듯한 얼골을 하여 가지고 잇섯다. 그리고 나종에는 하품을 하고 기지게를 켜면서
「자- 탐정님! 부대 잘하여 주시요… 사실은 너무나 놀낫슴으로 아조 피곤하게 되엿스니 한 두어시간만 잠을 좀 자야 할 터인데…」
「아 주무서도 좃치요」
하고 마택이는 말햇다.
「어듸서든지 당신 자고십은대로 자도 좃소… 저족 차실에 가서 자시요… 아모도 깨지 못하도록 할 터이니… 나도 조곰 사건의 추리만 될 것 가트면 잠을 좀 자야할 터이니까」
