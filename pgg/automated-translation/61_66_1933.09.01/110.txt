기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir·Essay)

A Story of Fooling a Tail
						Yun O-wol

It was already a year ago, in spring. The drizzling rain of April was making the closed flower buds smile more and more each day!

It had been a while since the azaleas had bloomed, but it was mid-April, not long after the cherry blossoms had appeared.

Just hearing the words "cherry blossoms in full bloom" made young men and women flutter about like butterflies flitting around flowers without a care. Now that I think about it, I guess I was one of them.

It was on the night of April 20th that I went with three friends to see the cherry blossoms at Changgyeongwon (昌慶苑), which were in full bloom every year. From the first day, the streetcar was crammed so full of people that it felt like it was squeezing human flesh, picking it up, putting it down, and about to burst, but we rolled from Seodaemun to Changgyeongwon, packed together like sardines.

From the entrance of Changgyeongwon, it was so crowded that you couldn't even put one foot down; it was a sea of people (人山人海). Whether I was intoxicated by the beautiful flower scent or by the people, I don't know what I was intoxicated by. Now that I think about it again, I can't help but laugh. Whatever crowd it was that had come to find something, to get something, as I was pushed by the people, the flowers I had come to see were nowhere to be found. Only the black, seething waves of people (人波) caught my eye.

<24>

Pushed by the flowing crowd, our group arrived at the place where the gisaeng (妓生) were dancing against the backdrop of a wide lawn. There were so many people that you couldn't see anything just standing there, so the three of us were craning our necks trying to see when four or five students, I don't know what school they were from, were standing in front of us like sticks, blocking our view. Big, tall guys wearing what looked like five-jeon spring coats and holding sticks the size of clubs. I was so annoyed that we punched them in the back of the head and moved to another spot, stood there for a while, and then when we turned to go somewhere else, I stepped on someone's foot. I couldn't help but step on someone's foot, I stepped on someone's foot so hard I didn't even know whose foot it was.

I was so surprised that I looked up, and it was one of those guys who had been causing trouble earlier. What do you think happened then?

The modern girl (moppo; モボ) who was wearing a belt around her waist stood there looking at me and smiling. It was clear that the man had deliberately stuck his foot out. Anyway, stepping on someone's foot was rude, so I apologized.

"I'm sorry."

"Oh, you're too kind."

I had a feeling that something was fishy, so we pretended not to notice and slowly walked toward the pond.

"Did you step on someone's foot on purpose?" This kind of sarcastic remark gradually got closer. We knew we were in trouble, and we laughed at their appearance and walked even more slowly. When we sat down by the pond, they followed us and stood next to us, as if they were guarding something.

Our faces were reflected in the subtle ripples of the light, and the night waves were rippling gently.

After sitting there for about ten minutes, one of them said, "Hey! Let's take a break too," and sat right next to us, complaining that his foot hurt a lot. I was so annoyed that I widened my eyes and sent him a sharp glare.

"Oh, scary - those ladies!"

Then, he called his friends, "Come on over here and sit down." I was so dumbfounded and embarrassed that I jumped up and said, "You'd be less shameless if you just took off that hat you're wearing on your head and walked around."

The person whose foot I had stepped on had a white mark on his hat. It said XX or XXX student or something like that. He seemed to be the leader (大將) of his group, and the others seemed like his subordinates (部下). The way they acted like they were missing a screw whenever he told them to do something was really a waste of their physique.

Again, we went to the movie theater, dodging the crowds so they couldn't see us. The funniest thing was that when our group hid in the crowd and watched, they would push through the crowd, looking around to find us. It was the best sight and the most ridiculous thing to see.

But they were such persistent guys that they found where we were standing and pretended to be watching the movie. Then we pretended not to notice and went somewhere else. They followed us to the death. We dragged them around like we were walking a dog, and they followed us relentlessly. Maybe they had some kind of plan, so we decided to give them a first test (一次試驗) to see how far they would follow us. We didn't even have time to get out, so we made a full circle and came back out the gate, and they followed us in a line.

We got on the streetcar. They followed us onto the streetcar as well, saying, "We had a great time tonight. It was a ridiculous show, and all the boys in the back were fun..."

<25>

As the streetcar began to move, our group stood huddled at the entrance and bought tickets for Seodaemun. We were thinking, "Let's give them a taste of their own medicine," and waited for the streetcar to enter Jongno. We got off in front of the Dongdaemun Police Station, caught the Seodaemun-bound streetcar coming from Dongdaemun, and watched their expressions. As expected, they followed us and got on the streetcar. "Okay, come on," we thought, and sat down naturally. When we got to Hwashin Department Store, we pretended to get off and looked at them out of the corner of our eyes. They also stood there looking at us, but when they saw us sitting down, they went back to their seats.

After a while, when we got to Gwanghwamun Street, we said to each other as if we were going to Hyojadong,

"Isn't this Gwanghwamun Street? Let's get off."

As we prepared to get off, those pretty boys (ppaetppoi; べっぴん) jumped off the streetcar before it had even stopped and followed the Hyojadong-bound streetcar. It was so funny. "Don't get your hopes up," we said, and looked out the window. It was quite a sight.

They looked back and, realizing that it was funny to them too, waved and then discovered that we were still sitting on the streetcar and desperately jumped back on the departing Seodaemun-bound streetcar.

The way they were panting like crazy people was both pitiful and funny. It wasn't like the innocent mischief of 17- or 18-year-old boys; it was impossible to see them as true human beings (人間視). No matter how much they wore flashy Western-style suits (洋服) on the surface and wore square hats instead of round hats, their true worth (眞價) was no better than a dog's value.

We got off the streetcar at Seodaemun Samjeongmok. They persistently followed us off the streetcar, just like our servants (侍下).

We went straight up the hill and entered CW-dong. When we entered a friend's house in K-dong instead of going home, they stood at the gate like sentries, watching our every move and waiting for us to come out.

We locked the gate and went inside, and then we quietly peeked through the gate to see if they were gone or still standing there. We could hear their breaths through the cracks in the gate. The night was getting deeper and deeper, and we had to go home, but we didn't know what to do. So we woke up the servant (行랑아범) of that friend's house, took a club, and went out in the middle of the night, making a fuss about how someone was peeking into the wooden house and trying to break into the house (家宅侵入), turning the whole village upside down.

At that time, they couldn't reveal their motives (動機) within the law (法), so they just tapped each other on the back of the head and scattered.

<26>

After that, I would occasionally run into them on the street. I would laugh inwardly, but I would pretend not to notice and walk past them with a swagger. They would take off their hats and bow politely, as if they had always been friendly. Thinking about it, they were persistent to follow us, but our group was even more persistent to mess with them.

It was just like being possessed by a spirit (돌씨). It was just around this time last year that it was funny, charming, and refreshing, like rain on a sunstroke-stricken heart.

It's a hot summer night with no breeze. I went to Hwashin Department Store (和信商會) to buy cosmetics (化粧品) after dinner. I went with my friend P because it was vacation time and we were going to do various "things" (gaimono; 買物) together.

When we entered Hwashin, our feet stopped first at the cosmetics department (化粧部) at the entrance. We were standing there picking out perfume from the display (陳列), wondering which one was better, when a couple of young men in suits (紳士服) suddenly appeared and came to our side. They pointed to the perfume bottles we were choosing and said, "That one is good, let's buy one too," interrupting the clerk's words.

They followed us as we went upstairs.

When we went to the embroidery department (手藝部) to buy a few skeins of embroidery floss (jasil; 刺繡絲) and were picking out floss for a project, those two young men came to our side and pretended to be looking around. "Oh, I don't know the name of the thread, but my wife told me to buy some thread..." said one of the young men wearing a mushroom hat (mekomoja; マコ帽). They kept following us wherever we went, pretending to buy something but not actually buying anything.

I came out to Jongno (鍾路) with my friend and took the streetcar to Honmachi-dori (本町通). First, we got off in front of the Chosen Bank (鮮銀) and went into Mitsukoshi (三越). As we were coming out after buying stationery on the third floor, those two young men were waiting in front of the door. I was so dumbfounded that we laughed inwardly, memorized their faces, and decided to see how far they would go. If they did anything wrong, we would take care of them in the end (最後), so we walked slowly toward the Gyeongseong Municipal Government (京城府). When we walked slowly, they walked slowly too, and when we walked fast, they followed us fast, making small talk.

"E School is already on vacation, right? It's very hot. You must be free now, let's go have some shaved ice (氷水) together."

For some reason, they were being very kind. As they followed us, we could hear them whispering and chuckling, so we came up with a plan. We put on a mask (假面) and acted as if we didn't know them and were suddenly friendly to them. I secretly sent my friend R to the police station (派出所) to report them, and I walked alone, asking the two young men for their addresses and names.

<27>

At that time, those two young men were overjoyed and thought that we loved them, so they said in happy voices,

"I'm Kim Jin-yeong (金鎭英) (fake name) and I live in Hwadong (花洞)."

"I'm Shin Wan-seong (申完成) (fake name) and I live in Changseongdong (昌城洞)."

Before we knew it, we had passed Gwanghwamun and entered a big street. They had been following us for well over 30 minutes, about an hour and a half.

The young men were happy to talk to us and asked questions one after another.

"Where do you live?" they asked for my address. At that time, I joked, "I live in Sajikdong (社稷洞), let's go together," and they got even more excited and started talking.

"Are you going on vacation (避暑) this vacation?"

"Yes, I'm thinking of going to Wonsan (元山)."

"Then we'll meet there. Hahaha."

As we lured them and got them almost completely out of the Yazugae (야주개) neighborhood, I saw my friend R and a police officer (巡事) in the distance, waiting for me while talking at the Naejadong Police Station.

At that time, I sped up and headed towards them with the young men. I was trying to subtly lead them, saying that I had an appointment, but they didn't know anything and thought that we were going to my house. They were foolish and pitiful.

My friend R was already winking at me. The police officer was also strolling around. When we arrived almost in front of the police station, the two young men saw my friend R standing there and hesitated, as if they felt guilty about something. But I pretended not to notice and tried to pass in front of the police station.

The police officer (巡事) who was waiting there called the two young men over. At that time, they must have felt guilty (良心의 가책) because they were shaking and bowing their heads.

The police officer asked them about their motives (動機) and reasons for following me from Jongno (鍾路) to Honmachi (本町) to Naejadong (內資洞), and then slapped them in the face.

People gathered from all directions as if they had received urgent news (急報). Some people thought, "Those guys are getting what they deserve," and stood there watching the spectacle. The two young men were detained (留置) at the Jongno Police Station for three days (三日間) for following a woman for more than 30 minutes (三十分以上), according to the law (法). That night, the woman's unique hypnosis (催眠術) achieved a victory outside the law (法外의 승리). (The End)

그만하여도 벌서 1년이 지난 봄입니다. 나리는 세우(細雨)는 입다문 꼿봉오리를 날로 웃겨만 주는 사월의 봄날!
진달내꼿 피인 제는 오래엿것마는 사구라 피인 제 얼마 아니되는 사월 중순이엿습니다.
「사구라 한창이라」는 말만 드러도 젊은 청춘 남녀들은 물색 업시 꼿 우에 뛰노는 봉접과 가티 날뛰는 판입니다. 지금 생각하니 나도 한 목 끼엿든가 봐요.
바로 4월 20일(四月 二十日) 밤 연연히 열리는 창경원 야생(昌苑園夜櫻) 구경을 세 동무와 가티 갓습니다. 전차(電車)는 첫날부터 끔직끔직하게 인육(人肉)을 집어 생켯다 배앗텃다 터질듯건만 가티 기름을 짜면서 서대문서부터 창경원(昌慶苑)까지 굴러갓습니다.
창경원 입구(昌慶苑 入口)서부터 발 하나 드려놀 수 업시 인산인해(人山人海)를 이루엇습니다. 내 뉘할 것 업시 아름다운 꼿냄새에 취하여서 갓는지 사람에 취(醉)해 갓는지 대체 무엇에 취해 갓는지를 이제 다시금 생각하니 헛우슴박게 나오지를 안습니다. 무엇을 차즈러 간 무엇을 어드러간 무리엿든지 사람에 밀리여 드러가니 보러 온 꼿은 보이지 안코 시커멋케 밀리는 人波만이 와글와글 눈을 오로지 빼앗고 말엇습니다.
류동(流動)되는 인파(人波)에 밀리여 우리 일행은 넓은 잔듸밧을 배경하고 춤추는 기생(妓生) 댄쓰하는 곳에 이르럿습니다. 엇지도 사람이 만턴지 그냥 서서는 아무 것도 보이지 안음으로 우리 셋은 발도듬을 하면서 그것을 보려고 기우리려 하는데 어느 학교 학생인지는 모르나 네다섯의 사각모(四角帽)짜리가 압흘 턱 가려 작대기처럼 서 잇습니다. 키덜이 큼직큼직한 것들이 거긔다가 제일이 멧푼자리 스푸링콧인지 입고 들고 손에는 몽딍이만한 스틱을 집고 섯습니다. 하 속상해서 우리는 뒷통수에다 주먹질을 하고 자리를 옴기여 서서 한참 보다가 막다른 곳으로 갈여고 몸을 돌리려 할 때 발 미테 또 발이라 골라 드딜 수 업시 내 발은 누구의 발인지도 모르게 꽉 발벗습니다.＜24＞
그래 깜짝 놀라 얼골을 처다보니 말성부리는 앗가 그 군(軍)들 중의 하나엿습니다. 그 때 엇더케 되엿겟습니까?
단장을 허리에 턱 걸처 집고 선 모뽀는 나를 대고 서서 빙그래 웃습니다. 일부로 그 男子는 발을 내 밀엇든 것이 거기에 판명(判明)되엿든 것입니다. 하여간에 남의 발을 발벗든 것이 실례(失禮)는 실례엿슴으로 잘못햇다고 인사를 햇습니다.
「실례햇습니다」
「아이구 천만의 말슴을 다 하십니다」
엇지 눈치가 수상해 보임으로 우리는 시침을 뚝떼고 연못으로 슬슬 거러 갓습니다.
「슬그머니 남의 발을 발고?」 이러한 빈정거리는 소리가 차츰차츰 갓가와 옵니다. 이제 고질을 맛낫다 하고 우리는 그 꼴들이 하 우서서 더 천천히 거러가 연못가에 안저 잇스니까 그것들도 뒤따라와 그 엽헤 웅게중게 무엇을 직히는 덧이 서 잇습니다.
불빗의 교묘한 여울에 우리의 얼골은 빗치고 밤잔 물쌀은 잔잔히 여울저 잇섯슴니다.
이러케 한 10분 가량 안저 잇스니까 그 즁에 하나가 「예! 우리도 점잔이 쉬자쿠나」하면서 우리 바로 엽헤 안지면서 발이 몹시 압흔데, 하고 지긋게 굴읍니다. 하 밉상이라 눈을 동그러케 떠 날카로운 시선을 보내엿더니
「아이구 무서워- 그 아가씨들!」
그러면서 예예 이리로 다와 안즈라고 저의 동모들을 끕니다. 하도 어이가 업고 도로혀 우리가 챙피스러워 벌덕 이러나면서 「숫재 머리에 쓴 모자나 버서버리고 댕겻스면 체신이나 안 붓그럽지」 하엿습니다.
그 중에 발 발핀 사람은 모자에 흰 마크가 붓처 잇섯는데 XX인지 또 XXX학생인지 그럿습니다. 그는 저의 一行 즁에는 대장(大將) 노릇을 하고 그 나머지는 부하(部下) 가티 보엿습니다. 그가 하자는대로 그 나머지는 씰게 빠진 체를 하는 꼴이야 참으로 덩지가 아까워 보엿습니다.
또 다시 사람 만흔 곳을 헤처 눈에 안 뵈도록 빨리 활동사진(活動寫眞) 하는 곳으로 가니 또 그 패들이 따라옵니다. 제일 우서운 것은 우리 일행이 사람 만흔 틈에 끼여서 숨어서 보면 우리들을 찻느라고 사람떼를 허처가며 기웃거리는 것이 제일 장관이엿고 꼴볼견이엿습니다.
그러나 엇지들 극성패들인지 우리들이 선 곳을 차자와 시침을 딱 띄고 구경을 하는 척하고 섯습니다. 그 대에 우리도 시침을 띄고 다른 곳으로 갑니다. 죽어라 하고 따라옵니다. 올 때로 오라고 개끌고 댕기듯 아무데나 막 끌고 가면 지긋지긋하게 쫏차 옵니다. 아마도 그들은 무슨 게획이 잇는 못양이라 어데까지 따라오나 일차시험(一次試驗)을 보려고 나올 시간도 못되여 한 박휘 일주하여 문으로 되집허 나왓더니 그들도 죽 일렬을 지어 따라 옵니다.
전차를 탓습니다. 그들도 따라 전차에＜25＞ 올으면서 「참 오늘 밤 구경 잘햇다. 참 꼴불견 모던 껄 뒤에는 모던 뽀이 자미잇던 걸...」
전차는 움즉이기 시작할 때 우리 일행은 입구(入口)에 뭉켜 서서 서대문행으로 표를 찍엇습니다. 우리는 속으로 어데 점 골탕을 먹어바라 하고 전차가 어서 종로번선으로 드러가기만 고대(苦待)하여서 동대문경찰서 압헤서 나려 東大門으로부터 굴러오는 西大門行을 잡어타고 그들의 꼴만 주목(注目)하고 잇스랴니 역시(亦是) 뒤따라 와 탑니다. 오냐 오너라 하고 천연스럽게 안젓다가 화신상회 압헤 와서 우리 일행은 일부로 내리는 척 눈치를 보엿습니다. 그들 역 우리만 바라보고 서성거리더니 우리 안즌 것을 보고 도로 자리에 앗젓슴니다.
얼마쯤 오다가 광화문통(光化門通)에 와서는 우리들끼리 효자동(孝子洞)이나 가러 타는 것처럼
