별건곤 제61호
1933년 03월 01일
今春 卒業한 아들과 아버지 對話 (Dialogue between a son who graduated this spring and his father)
曺山江
세태비평 (Social Critique)

This Spring's Graduate Son and Father's Conversation
						Jo San-gang
Dongseok, who finished XXX vocational school, tried to find a job in Seoul, but the world was not like before, so he could not get a job easily, so he had no choice but to go down to his hometown.
The following record is a conversation with his father.

"Didn't you plant cotton this year?"
"How could I have time? I don't have any money..."
"What about the oiled paper? You put down straw mats? Straw mats not only get dirty easily, but they are also not good for hygiene because of the dust. Oiled paper is better for both economic and hygienic reasons."
"After you left, who would take care of the oiled paper well? There were holes here and there, and the dust was flying everywhere, so I couldn't stand it, so I took it off. And is there a problem with hygiene or anything in a place like this? Old people lived to be eighty or ninety in worse places..."
"Oh, it's frustrating. I can't get used to seeing an oil lamp after seeing only electric lights. Buy a lamp and turn it on. Like this..."
"That's all a matter of money. Don't you know that I'm ruined from putting you through school?"
"...But the village looks worse than before. I saw it while coming in..."
"There's nothing to say about the village. Look at our house first. This house, which used to be the most famous in this township, is in this shape, so what can I say about other houses?"
"There seem to be a lot of empty houses?"
"There are five empty houses in this village. They all ran away at night..."
"Why did they run away? Did they commit crimes?"
"They ate all the potato seeds that the county office gave them to plant because they were starving, so how could they live? They don't have good fields to farm, and they don't have anything to eat, so they have no choice but to run away. They are ashamed, so they have no choice but to run away at night..."
"So they can't even earn enough to eat?"
"You're still not awake. Isn't this the third year of famine since you went to Seoul? Even if there is a good harvest, sharecroppers can't make it for half a year after paying the rent, and there has been a famine, so how could they have lived?"
"Then how did they live during that time?"
"You should have known even if you read the newspaper. Haven't you seen the newspaper? Now, the mountains in the highland villages are all red. They peeled off everything, including tree roots, grass roots, and tree bark. Some people even washed and ground jo-house (좁-집, probably a type of wild grass) to make gruel, but they died because they were constipated. There were so many people who died like that. There was even a case where a whole family was wiped out after begging for a mal (斗) of millet and cooking rice after not seeing millet for six months. Didn't you see it on the way here? People's faces are still yellowish... Then, last year, they started talking about a great bumper crop, but when it came time for sprouting (發穗긔), the sea breeze came and the rice all withered... But last year's harvest was not as bad as the previous year's, so it was a little better, but already many people have run out of food. In the midst of all this, you graduated. But what's going on in Seoul these days? I heard that there's a riot going on."
"It's all rumors. There may be such rumors because of the Jeolha (熱河) issue, but even if there is a problem, there will be no problem for Korean farmers."
"Won't the money dry up even more?"
"There's no problem. If that happens, the price of rice will go up, so farmers will be a little better off."
"One way or another, we need to make more money..."
"There's a lot more money now. Gold used to cost five won, but now it costs nine won each. First of all, German marks, which used to be 45 jeon each, now cost one won each. So, one won and fifty-five jeon has increased."
"Isn't that less? We have to pay nine won each to buy something that we used to buy for five won."
"No, sir. There's more money and the goods are the same, so won't the price of goods go up? First of all, a bag of rice used to cost six won each, but now it costs more than ten won, so doesn't the money increase?"
"That's right. But do we have rice to sell? And we can't live without buying even a box of matches, so we need money... This is a big problem. By the way, I mortgaged a small plot of land behind the house to pay your graduation fee of two hundred won. I mortgaged it on the condition that I would pay it back soon..."
"You mortgaged it? The fifty majigi (마지기, a unit of land area) of rice paddies in Seongan are still there, right?"
"Is there any reason for that to be there? I already sold it. Look here. Your tuition was an average of thirty won per month, but let's say it was three hundred thirty won per year for eleven months. Let's subtract thirty won and calculate only three hundred won. Five years of middle school and three years of vocational school - eight years totals three times eight is twenty-four, two thousand four hundred won, right? Is that all? How much money did you spend unknowingly on your travel expenses or suits? So where did that money come from? I had no choice but to sell the rice paddies..."
"Then it's a big problem."
"What's the big problem? Since you've become that much, you can make it up again, right? But did you find a job before you came here?"
"...Is it that easy to get a job?"
"Is it that difficult for a vocational school graduate to get a job!"
"It's completely different from before. Before, it wasn't a problem to get a job even if you only graduated from middle school, but now, every year, the total number of vocational school graduates, including university and high school graduates, is one thousand in Joseon. So more than four-fifths of them can't get a job. Not only that, but all companies and stores are being hit hard, so they are all laying off people. Where can they hire people to get a job?"
"Then why did you study? Studying doesn't even earn you a living..."
"No, sir. Studying is about gaining knowledge, and it's not about making money. When talking about the level of civilization and the level of the people in any country, we look at the level of knowledge of the people. If all the people in that country have a university-level education, then that country is that civilized, and if they can't even reach the level of ordinary school, then that country is that uncivilized. So, the reason why any country builds schools to train young people is not just to make money. It's about knowledge."
"I don't want to hear that. I don't know anything, but I think that civilization and everything else is only for eating and wearing. I know that the more civilized you are, the more money you need. Railroads, airplanes... everything requires money."
"That also comes from knowledge. Money is a secondary issue."
"I don't want to hear anything. Can you study when you're hungry?"
"Anyway, the country that has learned the most and invented the most has the most money."
"That's right. What you say is right. Invent something quickly every day and make a lot of money. I don't want anything more. Just as much as you wasted..."
* *
The next evening, D went to his father's room because of the divorce problem that he had always left as homework.
"Father, I have something else to ask you."
"What is it? Don't tell me you need money?"
"No, sir..."
"Then what is it?"
"I'm going to send Ok-sun's mother back to her parents' house."
"Oh, that's a good idea. It's been a long time since that girl has been home. She must want to go. Let her go home once. And you should go with her."
"No, sir. I'm not sending her like that."
"What are you going to do to send her..."
"No, sir."
"Then how..."
"I mean I'm sending her away for good."
"You're going to get divorced?"
"Yes!"
"For what crime?"
"It's not that she committed a crime..."
"According to the seven grounds for divorce (七去之惡, chilgeojiak), you can only abandon your wife if she has committed a crime... Especially if she has even had a child..."
"That's old morality..."
"Oh, there's such a law in the new morality? So what's the reason?"
"Whatever business you do, your wife must have enough knowledge to help you by a third. When your husband is away, she must be able to read and handle urgent letters and be able to reply. Or, in modern times, men and women go out into society and socialize together a lot. To do that, she doesn't have to be beautiful, but she shouldn't be so ugly that people don't want to look at her. But my wife doesn't have that. So I have no choice but to get a divorce..."
"Is that enough reason to get a divorce?"
"Yes."
"Then that's great. Let's get a divorce as soon as possible. First of all, I have to divorce your mother first. Your wife at least knows Hangeul, but your mother doesn't even know Hangeul, and her face is so wrinkled that I can't stand looking at her anymore. Now it's great. Let's go tomorrow and get divorced together, father and son..."
"You can't do that."
"There's no such law?"
"Yes, you can't!"
"Then you can't either."
