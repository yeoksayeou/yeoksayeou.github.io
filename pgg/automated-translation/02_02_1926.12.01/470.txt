누구의 罪? (Whose Sin? / Novel)

英 로바드 마-길(원작), 北極星(번역)

Whose Sin?
						By Young Robad Ma-Gil (Original), Bukgeukseong (Translation)

An incompatible adopted son, an attractive young woman who is the son's lover, and a lawyer who came to pay off a debt—in the place where these three people were wide awake, the rich old man was stabbed to death. A murder as sudden as lightning. Yet, none of the three are accomplices, and despite being present, they don't know the culprit. What? Strange? Who is the culprit, and what is the reason? The detective slowly opened his mouth.

Lawyer Henry-Batd had come with money to retrieve the collateral to pay off this house's debt, which was more expensive than a widow's inheritance, but the old man, using the excuse that the deadline was three days past, was greedy to swallow the collateral.

Despite repeated pleas, the old owner, without even looking at the pile of bills on the desk, muttered reproachfully, <115>

"Hmph, return the collateral? Where would anyone say such nonsense?"

As a lawyer, he had seen all sorts of extortionists and fraudulent cases, but he was so taken aback because he had never seen a usurer so incredibly greedy.

"Only two or three days late, and I've been paying the interest meticulously; isn't that too unreasonable?"

"Don't make such excuses!" The old man shouted, pounding the desk as if wielding great power.

"If you had come before the deadline, I would have returned the collateral without a word... ... Not two or three days, even if it's just one day past the deadline, the collateral, no matter how valuable, becomes my property. Since such a valuable thing has become mine, do you think I'd take a little more interest and return it?"

The lawyer's face changed as if he couldn't bear it any longer. But he barely managed to control himself and

"I understand," and cut off the conversation.

"If you understand, be gone." The old man said in a hateful tone, as if throwing it away, then turned around and, with a compass (兩脚規) on the desk, started drawing a circle.

Unable to retrieve the collateral and only subjected to insults that a gentleman could not bear, Lawyer Henry stood dumbfounded, like a dog chasing a chicken, looking around the room.

The room had no decorations; the five electric lights on the ceiling above the old man were lit dimly to save money, and the lamp in the corner row had never been turned on due to the old man's frugal economic views.

On one wall, ancient and rare weapons—long swords and old spears—were hung as decorations like treasures, suggesting that this house once belonged to someone of considerable status, but the rug on the floor was worn and torn with large holes, clearly indicating that the house had been seized due to debt, the good rugs removed, and electric lights sparingly used, with the worn-out rug laid down.

For a while, the old man diligently turned the compass, drawing a circle and measuring its dimensions, then lifted his face, folded the compass, and stuck it in his waistcoat pocket like a fountain pen before abruptly standing up. At that moment, the door opened, and an attractively dressed young woman entered.

"Sir, it's 7:15. Dinner is ready."

"What?" The old man glared at the young woman with a mean look. The young woman, wearing thin clothes on a chilly day, cautiously approached the fireplace, looking fearful.

"Hey, what's that shiny thing stuck in your hair, huh?" <116>

The attractive young woman's face flushed red and then pale at the old man's vulgar words in front of a guest, and she pulled out a hairpin that resembled a 송곳 (songgot, awl) from her hair. The head of the pin had a jewel embedded in it.

"Ronald oppa bought it for me as a birthday present."

"You wench!" The old man frowned and shouted angrily. "That ungrateful Ronald bought it for you? Hmph, bring that fellow here."

Soon, Ronald, the old man's adopted son, entered, summoned by the young woman. He was a tall, handsome, and fine young man.

"Father, why did you call me?"

"Um. I saw you two in the backyard the other day, and you kissed her then."

"Yes..." The young man replied calmly and bravely, continuing, "I love her. She loves me too. We were going to tell you that we would get married whenever we had the chance."

"What? You rascal, marriage?" The old man exclaimed as if something terrible had happened, pounding the desk with his fist. In the process, red ink spilled and flowed onto the desk.

"You don't appreciate the grace of me raising you and are trying to grow up quickly to embezzle my property."

"Grace, grace, but what great grace is there? Are you talking about the grace of treating me like an enemy because I'm an adopted son and only making me work?" The young man... became agitated and retorted angrily. "And you talk about property day and night, but you can't take property to the afterlife." The old man was furious to the point of losing his mind.

"Good, you rascal, say anything! I will rewrite the will. Even after I die, I won't give you or that woman a single penny."

"Do as you please," the young man said with a snort.

"Ah, now we feel like we've shrugged off a heavy burden. Then we'll leave this house right away, even tomorrow. Right now, let's pack our bags and go. I'm going to pack." As he walked away, he turned back and said, "Oh, could you lend me that old sword? To cut the string for packing." He went to the old man's desk, picked up the sharp sword, and headed towards the door.

While waiting for the unexpected family fight to end, Lawyer Henry, out of boredom, fiddled with the tip of the old spear hanging on the wall, and the young woman stood by the fireplace, her face pale, trembling with the pin in her hand.

"Hey, you rascal!" The old man called out to Ronald, who was about to leave with the sword. <117>

"I will rewrite the will. Now 40,000 pounds is not your money. You want me to die quickly, don't you... Not just you, but everyone wants me to die, I know that. First of all, if I die, that lawyer Henry will be happy too, the cook will be relieved, everyone who owes me money will be happy, right? But do you think I'll die? Do I look like I'm going to die?"

The old man laughed in a ghostly voice. But then, suddenly, the light went out.

In the darkened room, only the embers of the fireplace glowed red. Crackle, crackle! Suddenly, the sound of someone walking was heard. Then, thud! A falling sound, a sharp cry. Ronald quickly turned around and turned on the electric light near the door.

When the light came on brightly, it turned out that the three people were still standing in the same spot as before the light went out. Who had fallen, who had cried out? Where was the old man? Looking closely, the old man's face was pale, kneeling under the desk, holding his chest with both hands, and bright red blood was flowing out like a spring between his hands.

As the three people's eyes widened, the old man collapsed with a groan.

When the lawyer and the young man rushed to lift him up, it was already impossible to do anything. In just two or three seconds of darkness, his heart had been stabbed with a sharp object. ... ...

2

Detective Ko Bold and a doctor rushed over in response to Lawyer Henry-Batd's phone call.

The detective listened carefully to the statements of the lawyer and young man Ronald, and then began to search the room.

"Where is the murder weapon?" Detective Ko Bold muttered. Indeed, the murder weapon, which should have been present in this strange and terrifying crime, was nowhere to be seen.

"It's a strange thing, what could have been used to kill him?"

"The wound is deeply embedded at three inches." The doctor began to explain. "Judging from the amount of blood that spurted out, it is clear that the wound was inflicted by stabbing the connection point of the heart and the aorta (大動脈) with a sharp instrument. The instrument is very sharp and pointed."

At that moment, Ronald's eyes were caught by the pin in the young woman's hand.

"It couldn't be suicide, could it?" The detective muttered, seemingly frustrated.

"Based on the nature of the wound, the suicide theory (說) doesn't hold. And if it were suicide, the murder weapon would be here, wouldn't it?" The doctor denied. <118>

"How could someone be killed so quickly in just 2-3 seconds while several people are standing in the same room? It's very strange, no matter how I think about it."

At that moment, Ronald took a couple of steps forward.

"There's no need for further investigation. I killed him."

"You?" The detective, the doctor, and even the lawyer asked in surprise.

"Yes, I killed him." A look of anguish appeared on his face as he replied. "As you know, the deceased is my adoptive father. We were arguing with each other here until just now. Because he hated me and said he would rewrite the will, I stabbed him without thinking when the lights went out."

Everyone was speechless and their eyes just widened. The detective was so taken aback that he couldn't open his mouth.

"This is the murder weapon. I was going to use this sword to tie up the luggage, but when the lights went out..." He held out the sword he was holding. "Look at this. There's blood on the tip of the sword." His lips trembled. The young woman's face turned completely pale as if she were about to faint.

"It's true. There's no need for further investigation, arrest me."

The detective stared at Ronald's face for a long time without saying anything, then looked at the lawyer and the young woman and said,

"Please go to the room over there for a moment. I have something to ask this young man." He sent them to the next room and then spoke to Ronald in a very kind tone,

"Why are you making such a suspicious confession?"

"Suspicious confession?" The young man was startled.

"Firstly, you're confessing falsely. We'll know if we investigate the blood on that sword first."

The doctor immediately smeared some of the blood on the sword onto paper and looked at it.

"This isn't blood; it's red ink," he said.

"My guess was right." The detective smiled and then said,

"And Ronald-gun was going to leave with the sword, so he would have been standing near the door, right? But how could he have gone all the way to the side of the desk and turned on the electric light in just 2-3 seconds? Isn't that right?"

"No. I threw the sword from near the door," Ronald still insisted that he was the culprit.

"Then it's clear now that you're lying. Your father was facing the desk, so his back was to the door, but what kind of trickery would cause a sword thrown from the door to go over his shoulder and then turn around and stab him in the chest? ... ... Aren't you making such a false confession to hide the real culprit?" <119>

Ronald couldn't answer, bit his lip, and his face turned pale as he bowed his head deeply.

"The person you're willing to sacrifice yourself to protect is certainly not the lawyer. If so, then it must be your lover, the young woman. ... ... Well, let's move this corpse upstairs and then question that young woman in detail. ... ..."

3

"When the lights went out, you were standing closest to the deceased old man, right?" The detective began his harsh interrogation of the young woman.

"Are you saying that I killed him?" The young woman trembled.

"It is my duty to suspect, so I am suspicious of everyone. You were the only one who could have stabbed the old man in the chest and returned to your place in just 1-2 seconds of darkness, as you were standing only a step or two away from the old man, right?"

The young woman couldn't say anything, bowed her head, and began to cry. At that moment, the pin stuck in her bowed head caught the detective's eye.

"Please show me that pin stuck in your hair." The detective's voice was stern. The young woman pulled out the pin and handed it over.

"Could you kill someone with this?" At the end of the detective's words,

"Yes, you can kill someone with it. You can definitely kill someone. But there's no trace of blood on it." The doctor replied.

"Well, it would have been washed off. Let's look at it with a microscope."

At that moment, the young woman almost fainted and barely managed to lean on the edge of the fireplace with her left hand. Fortunately, she didn't collapse, but cried out, "Oh my!" and flailed her right hand.

"What's wrong?" The doctor asked with wide eyes.

"This is my sprained wrist, and it hurts so much that it feels like it's breaking because I leaned on the fireplace and put pressure on it. Really, look, my pretty wrist is swollen and even turning blue."

"How did it get sprained?" The doctor grabbed her wrist and asked.

"I sprained it the other night when the deceased old man scolded me and I fell over while being hit. The old man sometimes hits me."

The doctor looked at her wrist for a while and then said,

"That's right, this wasn't sprained today. Look, Ko Bold-ssi, this young woman is also innocent. This young woman was standing to the right of the deceased old man, so if she had stabbed him, her right hand would have had to have strength, but she can't even hold the murder weapon with her right hand like this." He said loudly.

"You absolutely can't hold it?" The detective asked again.

"Absolutely."

"Then she's innocent. Now only the lawyer is left."

4

"The deceased old man was miserly and quite hated by others, right?" The detective began questioning Lawyer Henry-Batdo, who had been brought into the room.

"Yes, that's right."

"Then you must have hated him a lot, too, right?"

"That old man is hated by everyone. He is inherently a dirty and vulgar person. Firstly, he is a miserly and greedy person who engages in wicked deeds, unjustly seizing the possessions of unfortunate people through usury." The lawyer spoke as if giving a speech.

"Yes, that must be it." The detective continued, "Where were you standing when the lights went out?"

The lawyer walked deliberately to the other side of the wall where the old spear was hanging and stood proudly. "I was standing here."

"Yes, about ten steps away from the old man." The detective's eyes flashed. "Then you didn't move an inch from that spot?"

"Of course not."

"But even if you didn't move from that spot, you could have taken that old spear and stabbed him, couldn't you? Just take one step forward and thrust it, and you could have stabbed him right in the chest, couldn't you?"

"Eek, don't say such absurd things... such, such."

"No. There were only three people present at the scene. But as a result of the initial investigation, both of them have been completely cleared of suspicion. But only you have enough motive to kill him, and you also have the murder weapon, and you could have easily stabbed him in that short amount of time." The detective jumped up and went to the wall, looked closely at the tip of the old spear, and then shouted,

"Hey, there's blood on it!!" He turned to the doctor and said,

"Please take a look at this. Is this on the tip of the spear definitely human blood?"

"Yes, it looks like blood. But we won't know for sure until we examine it in detail, so let's take this spear down and examine it in detail." As the doctor said, the detective rushed to remove the spear from the hook. But the spear was firmly fixed to the wall with decorations and could not be removed unless the screws were turned and the decorative nails were removed. He realized that the lawyer could not have removed it in a short amount of time. <121>

"Then what is the blood on this spear tip?" The detective, dumbfounded, muttered.

At that moment, the lawyer held out his hand, showing a wound with blood flowing from it, and explained, "The blood must have gotten on it from this hand. I was bored earlier and was fiddling with this spear tip when suddenly the lights went out and it became dark, so I scraped the spear tip with my hand and caused bleeding."

5

"It's a really strange case. Three people were clearly watching, but how could someone have committed the crime in the blink of an eye? It's like a ghost." As the doctor said this while coming out of the room, the detective finally expressed his own opinion.

"If all three people in this room are not suspects, then I think the only person who knows the truth about this murder is the dead old man himself."

The detective went upstairs and re-examined the corpse lying on the bed.

"Could it be said that the great spirit (神靈)'s sword of justice (正義) stabbed him?" The detective came down from the second floor and spoke in front of everyone gathered.

"The most outstanding trait of the deceased old man was only his miserliness. So in the end, he was stabbed to death because of his miserliness."

"Yes, but who killed him?" Among the people standing there with wide eyes, the lawyer asked.

"Please listen to my story slowly. If he hadn't worn worn-out shoes... ... And if he hadn't worn such worn-out socks... ... And if he hadn't laid down such a badly worn old rug and had laid down a new rug, he wouldn't have died yet and would have lived longer. Listen carefully. He had a compass stuck in his sock pocket."

"Yes, yes, I saw it." The lawyer said.

"If I had known that earlier, I would have known the culprit immediately without asking you all impertinent questions... The truth is, the culprit who stabbed the old man to death was none other than the dead old man himself. What? The incident is very simple. When he stuck the compass in his sock pocket, one leg of the compass got caught in the torn part of the sock pocket, causing the two legs to open on their own. And the open end happened to be right above the old man's heart, and suddenly the lights went out, so as a naturally miserly old man, he instinctively thought, 'What if someone takes this pile of money?' and rushed to grab the pile of bills on the desk, but his worn-out shoe soles got caught in the torn seam of the rug, and he leaned his waist against the corner of the desk and <122> fell flat, so one leg of the open compass stabbed his heart. So he grabbed his chest and knelt down."

Everyone was relieved and their faces brightened.

"That's right. Exactly right." The young man shouted.

"And when he completely fell over and rolled around, his jacket and vest became tautly stretched, so one leg of the compass automatically came out of his heart. And after that, as he stumbled and rolled around, the two legs of the compass fell through the hole and disappeared between the lining and the outside of the vest, so we didn't see it when we first examined the body. When I examined the body again just now, I scratched my fingertip on the compass point, and that's how I discovered the compass." He laughed heartily.

After that, it is, of course, certain that every issue was joyfully resolved for everyone. -(End)- <123>

						英 로바드 마-길(원작), 北極星(번역)
뜻안 맛는 어븟아들, 아들의 애인인 어엽븐 처녀, 빗갑흐러온 변호사 이 세 사람이 눈을 뜨고서 잇는 그 자리에서 돈 만흔 로인은 찔려 죽엇다. 눈깜작할 동안에 번개가티 생긴 살인사건이다. 그러나 세 사람의 공범도 아니요. 세 사람이 그 자리에 잇고도 범인을 모른다. 긔? 괴? 범인은 누구, 까닭은 무엇, 탐뎡은 천천히 입을 열엇다.
변호사 헨리-밧드는, 과부집 대돈변보다도 더빗싼 이집의 빗을 갑흐려고 돈을 가지고 담보물을 차즈려왓스나 긔한이 사흘지낫다는 핑계로, 늙은이는, 담보물을 그냥 삼켜 버릴 욕심이엿다.
여러번 졸라도 고개한번 끗덕이지안턴 주인로인은 책상우에 노힌 지전뭉치는 본테도＜115＞ 안하고 꾸짓듯이 중얼거렷다.
「흥, 담보를 도로내이라니 그런 잠ㅅ고대 가튼 소리를 하는 사람이 어대잇나」
변호사인만큼 별별협잠군과 별별사귀사건을 만히 보앗지만 이럿케 엄청나게 욕심을 부리는 빗장이는 처음보는 터이라, 넘어 어이가 업섯다.
「2,3일 느젓다고, 이럿케 연테리자까지 깍듯이 해왓는데 그건 넘어 무리한 말슴 아님닛가.」
「그따위 수작은 하지말어!!」 로인은, 무슨 큰 권세나 부리는 듯이 책상을 땅치면서 소리질넛다.
「긔한전에 가서왓드면 아무말안하고 담보물을 내여 주엇지... ... 2,3일 아니야 단 하루라도 긔한이 지낫스닛가 담보물은 아모리 갑만흔 것이라도 내 물건이 된 것아닌가. 그것을 그럿케 갑만흔 것이 내 물건이 되엿는데 그까짓 리자좀 더 밧고 도로 내여줄 듯 십은가?」
변호사는 그만 참지못할 듯키 얼골빗이 변하엿다. 그러나 간신히 마음을 가려안처가지고
「잘 알앗소이다」 하고 말을 끈엇다.
「알앗스면 고만이지.」 내여 던지듯이 미여운 소리를 하고는 빙그르 도라안저서 책상우에 잇는 컴파쓰(兩脚規)의 다리를 벌려가지고 무언지 둥근그림을 그리기 시작하엿다.
담보물을 찾기는 고사하고 도로혀 신사로 당할수 업는 모욕만 당한 변호사 헨리-는 닭쫏든 개 모양으로 벙벙히 서서 방안을 둘러보앗다.
방에는 아모장식도 업시 늙은이가 안저 잇는 머리우 텬정에 다섯 개 달린뎐긔등이 돈앗기느라고 단하개만 켜저 잇고 한편 구석 란로우에는 잇는 등도 로인의 인색한 경제관렴으로 켜본적이 업섯다.
한편 벽에 녯날시대의 진긔한 무긔- 긴칼 녯날 창가튼 것들-가 보물가티 장식으로 걸려 잇는 것을 보면 이집이 전에는 상당한 행세하는 이의 집이 엿든 것가튼데 방바닥에 깐 양탄자는 해여지고 헐어서 구멍이 펑펑 뚜러진 것을 보면 이집도 빗으로 빼아서 가지고 조흔 양탄자를 치우고 인색스럽게 뎐긔도 만히 안켜고 양탄자도 다해여진 것을 깔아 노코 잇는 것이 분명하엿다.
한동안 렬심으로 컴파쓰를 돌니여 둥근 금을 것고 그 치수를 재우고 잇던 로인은 얼굴을 들더니 컴파스를 접어서 족기 웃주머니에 만년필처럼 꼿고 벌덕 니러섯다. 그때에 방문이 열니고 어엽브게 생긴 처녀가 드러왓다.
「아젓씨 닐곱시 15분임니다. 저녁 진지 잡수세요」
「무어야?」 늙은이는 심술구진 눈으로 미운사람 가티 처녀를 흘겨보앗다. 쌀쌀스럽게 치운날 얇은 옷을 닙은 처녀는 겁나는 듯이 주츰주츰 벽란로 엽흐로 갓가히 갓다.
「에그 너 그 대가리에 꼬즌게 빤작빤작 하는게 그게 무어야 응?」＜116＞
어엽븐 처녀는 로인이 손님잇는 앞헤서 쌍스럽게 하는 소리에 얼골빗이 붉으락 푸르락하면서 머리에서 송굿가티 삐즉한 핀을 뽑아 들엇다. 그 핀의 대가리에는 보석이 물려 잇섯다.
「로날드 옵바가 제 생일날 선물로 사준거야요.」
「이년!!」하고 로인은 얼골을 찡기면서 골난 소리를 질넛다. 「그 은혜도 모르는 로날드란 놈이 사주엇서? 흥 그놈을 이리 불러오너라.」
이윽고 처녀에게 불리여 로인의 어붓아들 로날드가 드러왓다. 그는 키가 후리후리한 잘생긴 훌륭한 청년이엿다.
「아버지 왜 부르섯슴닛가.」
「음. 내가 어적게 너의들이 뒷마당에 가티 잇는 것을 보앗는데 너는 그때 이 애하고 입을 맛첫지.」

「녜- 피는 피갓슴니다. 그러나 자세 됴사해 보지 안으면 모르겟스닛가 이 창을 떼여 내려 가지고 자세 보기로 합시다.」 의사의 말대로 탐뎡은 달겨들어 창을 고리에서 벗겨나리려 하엿다. 그러나 그 창은 굿게굿게 장식을 박어 벽에 박어 논 것이고 나사를 틀고 그 장식 못을 빼여낸 후가 아니면 끄낼 수 업섯다. 그리하야 변호사가 잠간동안에 그것을 빼여내여 쓰지 못하엿슬 것을 알앗다.＜121＞
「그럼 이 창끗에 무든 피는 엇전것일가.」 어이가 업서 입이 벙벙하든 탐뎡이 중얼거렷다.
병호사는 그때 자긔손을 내여밀어 피가 흐른 험집을 보이면서 「이 손에서 피가 무덧나보니다. 앗가 심심하여서 이 창끗을 주물너 보고 잇는데 별안간 뎡등이 꺼저서 캄캄해 진고로 그때 손으로 창끗을 몹시 숫처서 피가 난 것임니다.」고 설명하엿다.

「참말 이상한 사건인 걸요. 세 사람이 눈을 뻔하게 뜨고 엇는데 엇던 놈이 고 눈깜짝 할 동안에 그 범행을 하엿겟소잇가 귀신이 아니고야.」 방에서 나오면서 의사가 이러케 말하닛가 그제야 탐뎡은 처음 자긔의 의견을 말하엿다.
「이 방에 잇든 사람 세 사람에게 혐의가 모다 업다고 하면 이 살인사건의 진상을 알 사람은 죽은 늙은이 당자 밧게는 아모도 업다고 나는 생각함니다.」
탐뎡은 이층으로 올라가서 침대위에 뉘여 노흔 시톄를 다시 한 번 됴사하엿다.

「위대한 신령(神靈)의 정의(正義)의 칼이 그를 찔른것이라고 할까요.」 탐뎡은 이층에서 나려와서 모든 사람이 모여섯는 압해서 이럿케 말을 내엿다.
「도라간 로인의 가장 뛰여난 특질은 다만 인색한 것 그것뿐이엿슴니다. 그래 결국 그 인색 때문에 찔려 죽은 것임니다.」
「그 그래 누가 죽엿단 말임닛가.」고 모다 눈이 둥글해 섯는 중에서 변호사가 물엇다.
「천천히 내 이약이를 드러주십시오. 만일 그가 바닥 해여진 구두를 신지안엇드면. ... ... 그러고 그런 해여진 족기를 닙지 안엇드면. ... ... 그러고 그럿케 몹시 해여진 헌양탄자를 깔지 말고 새 양탄자를 깔고 잇섯던들 그는 아즉 죽지 안코 더 살앗슬 것임니다. 자세 드르십시오. 그는 족기주머니에 컴파쓰를 꼿고 잇섯슴니다.」
「녜 예 나도 보앗슴니다.」 변호사가 말하엿다.
「그것을 진작 알엇드면 여러분께 실례되는 심문을 안하고도 즉시 범인을 알엇슬 것을 ... 실상은 늙은이를 찔너 죽인 범인은 곳 다른 사람이 아니고 죽은 늙은이 그 자 엿슴니다. 무얼요 사건은 아조 간단함니다. 그가 콤파쓰를 족기 웃주머니에 꼬즐 때 족기 주머니속이 해여진 것인고로 콤파쓰의 한다리가 그 해여진 데 걸려서 두다리가  저절로 버러저 잇젓슴니다. 그런데 그 버러진 끗이 맛츰 로인의 가슴 심장우에쯤 잇섯는데 별안간에 뎐등이 꺼지닛가 원래 인색한 늙은이라 본능뎍으로 「엇던 놈이 이 돈뭉치를 가져 가면 엇저나 하고 책상우에 잇는 지젼뭉치를 집어 웅키려고 달겨드는데 해여진 구두바닥이 해여진 양탄자 험집에 걸려서 책상구텅이에 허리를 걸치고＜122＞ 폭 업드진고로 다리 버러진 콤파쓰의 한다리가 심장에 쿡 박힌것임니다. 그래 그는 가슴을 쥐고 꿀어업드린 것임니다.」
여러 사람은 그만 소생되야 얼골 빗이 밝아젓다.
「그럿슴니다. 꼭 그럿슴니다.」고 청년이 웨첫다.
「그런데 그가 아조 업드러저 뒹굴적에 그이 웃저고리와 족긔가 팽팽하게 켱겨지게 된고로 콤파쓰의 한다리는 저절로 심장에서 빠저나왓슴니다. 그러고 그 후에 작고 뒹굴때에 콤파쓰는 두다리가 한데 해여진 구멍으로 빠저서 족긔 거죽과 안 사이로 드러가 버린고로 처음 우리가 시톄를 됴사할 때에는 보지 못햇든 것임니다. 지금 다시 시톄를 됴사할 때에 내가 그 콤파쓰 끗에 찔려서 손끗을 걸켜민고로 비로소 콤파쓰를 발견한 것임니다.」하고 시원스럽게 우섯다.
그후 모든 문뎨가 모든 사람에게 깃겁게 해결되엇슬 것은 물론이다. -(끗)-＜123＞
