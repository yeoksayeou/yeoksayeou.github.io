Apology and Notice
						Pyŏlgŏn'gon Editorial Department

Due to offending the authorities' taboo (當局의 忌諱에 觸하야), the sections "Literary Scene Rumors" (문단풍문) and "Sugumun" (수구문) were subjected to deletion and could not be published. Although this is deeply regrettable for our readers, we will ensure they are published next time, so please wait and see what kind of articles appear.<10>

**Summary**

This brief notice from the Pyŏlgŏn'gon Editorial Department informs readers that two planned sections, "Literary Scene Rumors" and "Sugumun," were censored ("subjected to deletion") by "the authorities" because they touched upon forbidden topics (忌諱). The editors express regret and promise to publish the content in a future issue. For historians, this text is a direct piece of evidence illustrating the censorship faced by Korean publications under Japanese colonial rule in the 1920s. It highlights the constraints on freedom of the press and the editorial challenges of navigating sensitive topics while trying to keep readers informed or entertained.
