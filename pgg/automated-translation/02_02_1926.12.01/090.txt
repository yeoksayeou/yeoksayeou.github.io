People on the Street

Elder Yi Sangjae
This morning, while strolling along the main street of Kyodong to get material for my cartoon notebook, I saw an old man walking slowly, wearing a Nakayama-style hat (中山모자), leggings (행전), a thick scarf wrapped snugly, and holding a cane leisurely. Drawn to the Nakayama-style hat and old-fashioned leggings, I quickly sketched a picture, and lo and behold, it was none other than the Elder Yi Sangjae, whom we deeply respect. At the venerable age of 78, walking so soundly on such a cold day! Seeing the elder's gait, there surely isn't anyone who wouldn't momentarily think of his 78 years of steadfast struggle (健鬪). The elder passes through Kyodong street once every day to visit the Youth Association Hall (靑年會館) on Chongno.

Madam Yu Kakyŏng
Even if you curse me as a delinquent youth for targeting women passing by with my cartoon notebook, I can't help it, as it's a strict order from the honorable editor-in-chief to draw at least one woman; it's not my responsibility. Behind Tapkol Park in Sŏul, a charming female student walks jauntily, wearing a violet mantle (만ㅅ도)! The violet color draws people's eyes as much as anything. Holding up my cartoon notebook, I see—who else but Ms. Yu Kakyŏng, president of the Young Women's Christian Association (녀자 긔독청년회장)! Perhaps because she resembles her father, she is so petite (톄소하신지); looking at her shoes, they seem like cute baby shoes. Although she has borne two children, I have never seen her accompanied by them, and seeing her like this on the street, she looks like a 17 or 18-year-old female student. (Please forgive the impertinence) <25>

**Summary**

This article presents two short, informal sketches of prominent figures encountered on the streets of Seoul in 1926, drawn by a reporter for their "cartoon notebook." The first depicts Yi Sangjae, a respected elder nationalist figure, noted for his vigor despite his age. The second portrays Yu Kakyŏng, president of the YWCA, described playfully as looking youthful and petite despite being a mother. From a historical perspective, the article offers a glimpse into the daily lives and public perception of significant Korean social and nationalist figures during the Japanese colonial period. The casual observational style, the mention of organizations like the YMCA (Youth Association Hall) and YWCA, and the commentary on fashion (Nakayama-style hat, violet mantle) provide valuable social context for historians studying colonial Seoul.

路上의 人
李商在 老人
漫畵노트로 기사를 엇으려고 아츰에 校洞 큰 길로 거니르노라닛가 中山모자에 행전치고 목도리 퉁퉁히 둘으고 집행이 풀업시 집고 천천히 거러오는 노인 한 분 中山모자 옛날 행전에 마음이 끌려 번쩍하고 한 장 그리고 보니 우리의 존경하야 마지안는 李商在 老人이시다. 78이나 되는 高齡으로 이럿케 치운날이 건전한 거름거리! 노인의 거름거리 보고 그의 健鬪 78년 일을 잠시라도 생각지 안는 이가 업슬 것이다. 노인은 매일 한 차례 校洞길을 거처 鐘路 靑年會館을 들는다.

兪珏卿 女史
漫畵노트를 들고 지나 다니는 여자를 노린다고 不良靑年이라고 욕을해도 할 수 업는일 편즙장 각하가 꼭 여자 한 분을 그려오라는 엄명이닛가 내 책임은 아니요. 서울 탑골공원 뒤로 보라ㅅ빗 만ㅅ도를 닙고 아장아장 거러오는 앙징한 녀학생 한 분! 보라ㅅ빗만큼 남의 눈을 만히 끈다. 漫畵노트를 드리대고 보니 나-는 누구라고 녀자 긔독청년회장 兪珏卿씨! 딴은 아버님 달머서 엇지로 톄소하신지 구두를 보면 귀여운 아기구두 갓다. 아가를 두 사람이나 나으셧것만은 다리고 다니는 것은 본 일이 업고 이럿케 길에서 보면 17,8의 여학생 갓해 보인다.(실례 용서합시오)＜25＞
