Human Emotions in Many Aspects A Record of Unrequited Love -
A Sword Fight After Love Ends
Seongbukdongin

Commotion! Where else could there be such a commotion? Over a love affair, young men and women either punch each other at home, or go to the Han River Railroad Bridge and try to commit suicide out of despair, "hesitating after seeing the 'I Don't Know' bulletin board and being caught by a police officer" and escorted back home, which is also a commotion, but the commotion of a young woman in her twenties, undressed and with her hair disheveled, holding a shiny dagger (双光) in her chest and a calculation sheet in one hand, running to a guesthouse, which is located in the middle of Gyeongseong (京城) and not in a remote alley, shaking a deeply sleeping guest, yelling and cursing, waking up guests in the next room, is the biggest commotion among the commotions of unrequited love. It happened five years ago in the fall, and the main character was Hong Yun-deok (洪允得) (pseudonym), who had studied at a university in Tokyo as a temporary feminist (一時 女流主義者); and the man who was trained by her was a young man named Ryu Guk-gwang (柳國光) (pseudonym), who had also studied in Tokyo for a while but had returned after going to Shanghai due to some reason and was wandering around without a job. Originally, Hong was not very beautiful, but she had considerable talent and was passionate and full of resentment (多情多恨), so she had many relationships with young men while studying in Tokyo, and her popularity was not small. She was not a promiscuous or depraved woman, but, as mentioned above, she was passionate and resentful and also revered Marxism, so she did not sanctify the chastity or morality of a past husband (過夫) and had the idea of leaving her emotions and sexual instincts to freedom to the extent that they were expressed, so while living as a loving couple with a young man whose principles, age, and knowledge matched hers, she also had love affairs intertwined in double or triple ways with other men. Among those men, a young man named Ryu received the most love. Because the young man was five or six years younger than her, she outwardly designated him as a brother and sister (義男妹) for the sake of good hearing and even took him to her house. The popular song, "Whoever they are, they say they are siblings, but when they meet alone, they turn off the electric lights..." was about these two siblings. In the spring of six years ago, Hong Yun-deok graduated and became a female office worker in a company in Seoul. As the saying goes, "Where the needle goes, the thread follows," the young man Ryu Guk-gwang also came to Seoul following Hong's footsteps. While living like that for several months in secret love, Hong's original lover, a young man named Y, was imprisoned for a long time due to a certain incident (某 事件). Hong, who usually loved Ryu more than Y, loved Ryu even more as things turned out that way. It was not just a temporary love, but a passionate and true love that dreamed of an eternal marriage. To do so, she thought that it was necessary to educate and improve Ryu more than anyone else, considering her future or Ryu's future, rather than just holding Ryu in her arms and loving him only in front of her eyes like a cat, and she scraped together the money she had saved from her meager salary and sent Ryu to study in Shanghai, truly raising him to be her husband.

And she sent him 25 or 30 won every month from her salary for tuition. Originally, her living was not sufficient, and she had to take care of the house and support her original lover in prison while living on a meager salary, so Hong's agony was truly indescribable. Regardless of whether it was good or bad, if you look at it only from the point of view of love, everyone would admire Hong. However, as the saying goes, misfortune follows fortune, the young man Ryu became ill due to water and soil incompatibility (水土不服) in just a few months and could not stay in Shanghai and just returned to Seoul. The excuse was water and soil incompatibility, but in fact, it may have been because he could not overcome love. That was something even Hong did not know. Ryu was also in Hong's house after coming to Seoul. To others, they were supposedly brother and sister, and love was a secret love known only between the two of them. At first, she thought there would be no problem, but as the saying goes, even the cheapest musk smells, and in just a few months, several people noticed it, so Hong, even though she could not overcome her burning love and had such a relationship with Ryu for a while, could not just stay silent considering her status or the honor of her original lover in prison. So, after thinking this way and that, she came up with a plan, which was a wicked plan to pass on the disgraceful thing to another woman. At that time, a female student named Choi Ok-sun (崔玉淳) (pseudonym) often visited Hong's house. She had a pretty face and was very talented, especially in literature, and she was attending a certain girls' high school in the city. Her father was a pastor at a certain church (某레배당), so she had known Hong's house well since the old days (because Hong was originally a Christian), so the female student also called Hong "aunt" and often visited. Until then, Choi, who was purely innocent, believed in Hong as much as her aunt, but how could she have dreamed that Hong would sacrifice the innocent Choi to a wandering young man to hide her faults? At first, Hong, of course, did not have other ambitions and thought of Choi as her niece or sister, but as things turned out that way, she inevitably came up with such a plan while feeling guilty in her heart. So, when Choi came to her house, Hong treated her more kindly than usual and praised Ryu with sweet words, creating opportunities for the two to meet often, and on the other hand, she also told Ryu, "It is still a secret how you and I became related, but if this continues for a long time, rumors will surely spread and I will be criticized. If you break off your relationship with me at this time and fall in love with Choi, it will not only be a happiness for you, but even if we continue our secret relationship from time to time, it will not be as passionate as before, so please love Choi well." Ryu also initially came to approach Hong and fell in love, but in terms of age or anything else, it would not be very special to continue the love, and Choi, whom Hong introduced, was more than enough for him in terms of appearance, age, or talent, so he was most satisfied. In this way, Ryu and Choi formed a bond of love through Hong's bridge. At first, since the love was introduced by Hong, the two young men and women usually met at Hong's house, and Ryu still continued his previous relationship with Hong, taking advantage of secret opportunities, but as the love deepened between them, they became tired of showing their passionate love whispers in Hong's eyes, and from then on, the two met elsewhere, and in the end, Ryu even moved his quarters to a guesthouse in Nakwon-dong (樂園洞) and spent every day with the woman, not even showing his shadow to Hong. (Of course, it was because Hong had a lot of curses and jealousy toward the two of them.) At first, Hong thought of her status and inevitably gave her love to others, but as the saying goes, even if you throw away the food because you are tired of it, you feel pain in your stomach when you see others eating it. No matter how much she introduced it first, she felt unpleasant in her heart that the two were in such passionate love and kept her away. As for Ryu, she felt that she had raised him and loved him while spending all the expenses, so she thought it was more than anything else to treat her like that, saying, "New love is good, but how can you forget the old love?" She sent people several times to call him, but he did not come for this and that reason, so Hong could not bear the anger and made a final decision that night, took out the calculation of tuition and other expenses she had given to Ryu, and went to Ryu's guesthouse with a knife as a threat and caused such a big commotion. At that time, Ryu begged and begged, so it ended there, but if the words had gone out a little unpleasantly, Ryu would have received a great deal of training with that calculation, whether he could afford it or not, or his hair would have been cut with a knife. The story after that is also very complicated, but since I am only writing about the commotion of unrequited love here, I will stop writing here.

Advocate of Non-Resistance's Love Commotion
Yi Il

Love itself inherently possesses the quality of being a one-act play, with the male and female actors being the parties involved in this love play. Because it is a play, from opening to closing, the ending will be resolved as a tragedy, comedy, farce, or drama.
The outline of the love play that I am writing here is just one of the ordinary ones, but the ending is a tragicomedy that is difficult to name, with a slight taste of a middle-ground drama.
Exactly 15 years ago, a Korean youth named K was studying in Tokyo, Japan. Now, studying in Tokyo is so common that it is even more inferior to being ordinary, but at that time, when there were not many schools or facilities made by others in Korea, it was worthy of considerable respect. The young man named K above was originally from Incheon (仁川), graduated from P School (P學堂) in Gyeongseong (京城), and immediately went to study in Japan. He was only 21 or 22 years old, and after spending 1 or 2 years preparing for admission in Tokyo, he enrolled in the Department of Literature at W University (W大學) and was diligent in his studies as a student who was more than pure, and he had a humble nature of pretending not to know what he knew. He did not associate much with his peers and, in other words, hated commotion and loved solitude, so he had many poetic tendencies. However, since K grew up in a devout family where his entire family, even the servants, had to become believers to be at ease, Christianity was unconsciously deeply rooted in him, so going to church on Sundays was a very important item in his daily life that he could never miss. In Tokyo, there was a church called "Korean United Church (朝鮮聯合教會)," which was founded by Presbyterians and Methodists in collaboration, so K never missed it on Sundays for a year. For that reason, he had considerable trust from the pastor of the church, and he was not only one of the deacons among the believers, who numbered less than a hundred, but also one of the important figures among them. Not so long ago, only 15 years ago, there were not many Korean female students in Japan, not much more than 20 or 30, and most of those women were believers, so women accounted for dozens of the less than a hundred believers, and among those women, there were 2 or 3 deacons. Among those female deacons, there was a woman named S. She was also from Incheon, graduated from E School (E學堂) in Gyeongseong, came to study in Japan, and was enrolled in T Music School (T音樂學校). She could not be said to be beautiful, but she would not be left out of one in ten, and she also grew up in a Christian family and studied at a Christian school, so she unconsciously acknowledged Christianity like K mentioned above, and it was an important part of her life that had more meaning than studying abroad, attracting the attention of many men in the church where only men gathered, fully made up on Sundays, and moreover, she became the organist of the church because she was attending music school. She met a shower of gazes from various believers every week from when she went to play the organ to when she came back to her seat after playing the organ.
However, one strange thing to say is that K and S were both from Incheon, both Christians, and therefore attended the same church, and P School (P學堂) and E School (E學堂) in Gyeongseong were not far apart, so they must have met on the road sometimes, and since Incheon was not far from Seoul on Sundays or other days, they must have met several times on the train when traveling back and forth from home to school and from school to home, and they must have had opportunities to meet closer as officials at the church every Sunday, but it is strange that they had only greeted each other with their eyes until then without talking or greeting each other in front of others, but that was the fact.

<105>

And one more thing I want to say is that K at that time did nothing but read Tolstoy's novels and collections, neglecting his school studies day and night, so he had nothing in his head but human service and non-resistance, and he did not even dream of enjoying life or having love, and he even decided that after graduating from school, he would live a life of human service while begging.
Then one day, the pastor of the church came to visit early in the morning, and of course, seeing him come like this, the meaning would be different from a normal visit to a believer. Seeing K, who had just finished breakfast and was about to sit down, the pastor took out a letter from his suit pocket and handed it to him, saying, "Take a look at this. Something terrible has happened." K asked what was wrong and weakly received the letter and saw that the envelope was in a woman's handwriting, and the sender on the back of the envelope was signed S. Feeling some kind of instinctive fear, he opened the letter and read it. It was filled with 7 or 8 sheets of writing paper, and the content was a letter asking the pastor to tell her parents the reason and circumstances because she had received an unjust misunderstanding from others and would not see the sky of Korea again. K, who had seen it all, also became flustered and examined the pastor's expression. "This letter came this morning with the first mail. It was written and sent last night. I met S several times during that time, and she seemed a little different than before, but I thought it was some minor worry or that she was not feeling well, but I never expected this to happen. What should we do? If she really commits suicide due to her short temper, it will be a big problem. I am on my way to S's inn now, but since K's house is right on the way, I stopped by, but I can't go to school, so let's go together since K is from the same hometown as S and has a relationship with her," he said, urging him, and without time to say whether he should respond or not, the pastor and K went to S's inn. When they arrived, S had packed her books, clothes, etc. to be sent to her home country by transportation, her face was pale, she was wearing clothes carelessly, without makeup, and sitting at her desk as if she had just woken up in the morning. She reluctantly greeted the pastor and K into the room and only gave a silent nod and sat down again without energy. The landlady, who was doing something in the kitchen, wiped her water-stained hands on her apron and came into S's room, greeted them, and said that she was worried because S had not said anything and had not eaten for two days, saying that something big had happened, and asked them to comfort her well so that her worries would disappear, and then went out again. The pastor or K, of course, sat silently for 2 or 3 minutes like people who could not speak, but the pastor still spoke first in a comforting tone, "S! I read your letter and understood the details. I received the letter this morning and came urgently, and I am grateful to God for meeting S. What is the matter?" he said, showing a relieved expression and ending his words. Then K also mustered up courage and said, "S and I are from the same hometown in Korea, and since our schools in Gyeongseong were not far from each other, we should have been close for a long time, but the reason why we have been living so casually is that I am not a very cheerful person, but I must have known it in my heart for over 10 years. I have heard about various things from the pastor, but isn't there a saying that 'time is the wisest judge'? As people live, how can they not be misunderstood or feel wronged? We have come here to learn to be the light in the darkness, so we must bear the minor anger and look forward to the big things in the future. I am saying this without knowing the details, so it will be insufficient, but please endure everything and continue your studies," K felt himself getting excited.
Anyway, after this happened, S did not commit suicide and went back to school, and the pastor returned home with his family after his two-year term expired two months later.
After that, as Sympathy acts lone, ordinary love that is common in the world arose between K and S. One thing that can be said to be a special feature of this love is that K and S decided that they would dedicate their lives to human service and not get married as a result of their love, build a house, and start a family.
Two years later, K graduated from school first and returned to Korea, and S remained in Tokyo to finish her studies, which had one year left. The year after K returned to Korea, World War I ended and the Peace Conference was held at the Palace of Versailles, and after US President Woodrow Wilson advocated some kind of self-determination (自決主義), some kind of movement arose in Korea, K became a prisoner for five months, and after five months, he saw the clear sky again thanks to the grace of some kind of final acquittal. Of course, what he could not forget even for a moment during the five months in prison was his lover K in Tokyo. On the day of his release, he immediately sent a telegram to Tokyo twice to inform her of his safe release, but there was no reply. Therefore, he became so worried about her safety that he suspected that she had committed suicide and went to Tokyo in three days and searched for S's hidden house for two days, only to find that S had been cohabiting with a young man named H for several months. Tolstoy's follower of non-resistance, K, who saw this scene, took the wrists of the two men and women, S and H, and prayed to God for their future and returned to Korea.

소란! 소란하니 이런 소란이 또 어듸잇스랴. 실연 문제로 청춘남녀가 집안에서 서로 주먹질만 하던지 혹은 한강철교에 가서 비관자살을 하랴다가 「좃도오맛지란 게시판을 보고 머뭇머뭇하다가 순사에게 붓잡혀서』제집으로 호송되는 것도 한 소란은 소란이지만은 심야 3경에 경성시내에서도 제일 한 복판이요 여관으로도 그리 구석지지 안은 큰 골목 첫재 집인 여관에 옷 벗고 머리를 풀어 헷친 20내외의 젊은 여자가 가슴에는 双光이 閃閃한 단도를 품고 또 한 손에는 무슨 計算書를 들고 달녀 와서 깁히 잠든 손님을 잡아 흔드로 노코 고함을 치며 辱之罵之하야 엽헤 방에 잇는 손님까지 잠을 깨우치게 한 소동이야말로 실연의 소란 중에는 큰 소란이다. 때는 다 지금으로부터 5년 전 가을 문데의 주인공은 一時 女流主義者로 東京에 가서 某대학까지 업을 맛치고 도라온 洪允得(假名)이란 여자이요, 그 여자에게 단련을 밧는 남자는 또한 동경에 잠시 류학을 하다가 중도에 무슨 바람이 부럿던지 상해까지 갓다가 도라와서 무직 무업으로 도라다니는 柳國光(假名)이란 청년이엿다. 원래에 홍씨는 그리 미인이라고는 할 수 업스나 재질이 상당하고 또 多情多恨한 까닭에 동경 유학중에도 여러 청년들과 교제도 만헛고 따러서 인기도 적지 안엇섯다. 그는 무슨 방탕한 여자라던지 타락된 여자는 안이엿지만은 우에 말한 바와 가티 위인이 다정 다한하고 또 맑스 주의를 숭봉하니만치 過夫의 貞操觀, 道德觀을 그다지 신성시하지 안코 자기의 감정과 성의 본능을 엇던 程度까지 發作되는 그대로 자유에 맷기겟다는 관념을 가지고 잇섯기 때문에 자기와 主義, 年齡, 學識등이 걸마즌 엇던 청년과 사실상 사랑하는 부부의 活을 하면서도 다른 남섊�과도 또한 사랑의 실마리기가 이중 삼중으로 얼키고 또 얼키게 되엿다. 그런 여러 남성 중에는 柳 某란 청년이 가장 사랑을 만히 바덧섯다. 그 청년은 년령이 자기보다 5,6세가 연하가 되는 관계로 외면상＜103＞ 남 듯기 조케 의남매를 정하야 가지고 자기 집에 다러다 두기까지 하얏다. 『誰也某也 다뫼한덴 누의 동생하더니 단두리 만나서는 電氣燈만 끈다... ...』는 俗謠는 이 두 사람의 남매를 두고 한 노래라 하야도 틀임이 업는 사실이엿다. 그리다가 6년 전 봄에 홍윤득은 졸업을 하고 서울에 도라와서 엇던 회사에 여사무원이 되엿다.  眞所謂 바늘 간 곳에 실이 따른다고 유국광이란 청년도 홍씨의 뒤 밋처서 서울로 오게 되엿다. 남 모르는 사랑 속에서 그럭저럭 몃 달 동안을 지내는 중 홍씨의 원래 연인인 Y란 청년은 某 事件으로 장기의 감옥생활을 하게 되엿다. 평소에도 Y보다 柳某를 더 사랑하던 洪씨는 일이 그와 가티 되매 더욱 柳某를 사랑하게 되얏다. 다만 일시적 사랑이 안이라 영구한 백년의 가약까지도 꿈을 뀌는 열열한 참된 사랑이엿다. 그리함에는 柳某를 품속에만 안어 두고 고양이 가티 목전의 사랑만 하는 것보다는 자기의 장래를 보나 또는 柳某의 장래를 보나 柳某를 보다 以上으로 교육을 식히고 인격을 향상 식히는 것이 필요하다 생각하고 眞所謂 길너 남편 삼는다는 격으로 자기의 박봉 속에서 다소 저축 하엿던 돈을 박박 글거서 노비를 만드러 가지고 柳씨를 상해로 유학을 보냇섯다.
그리고 학비는 자기 월급 속에서 每朔25,6원 내지 30원식 보내주엇다. 원래에 살임도 넉넉지 못하고 박봉생활을 하면서 집 살임하야 감옥에 잇는 원 애인의 감옥 바라지를 하랴닛가 홍씨의 고심이야말로 참으로 형언할 수 업섯다. 사실의 잘하고 못한 것은 별 문제로 하고 사랑이란 그 점으로만 본다면 홍씨에 대하야 누구나 感心할 만 하엿다. 그러나 호사다마로
