甲己之年의 二大痛快
羅龍煥

Exhilaration, you ask? It seems there wouldn't be many exhilarating things for Joseon people, no matter who they are. In the situation of Joseon people being pushed and chased around like this, what exhilaration could there be? I haven't felt much exhilaration yet, and I'm looking forward to exhilaration in the future.

However, while it wasn't an unparalleled exhilaration, there were times when I gained some exhilaration. As I said once before in Byeol Geon-gon, when the Gaewha Party (開化黨) opened, turning the Daedong River into the Gyeongmucheong (警務廳; police headquarters) and knocking down corrupt officials, that was more exhilarating than anything. The so-called commoners and lowly people who had suffered oppression and disdain rose up all at once, established a proper organization, arrested and interrogated the Gwan찰 G군su (觀察郡守; governor and magistrate), and threw them into the river. That momentum was truly exhilarating.

Before that, when the late King Lee (李太王) issued a decree on the free expansion of the Donghak Army (東學軍), we proclaimed it to the 13 provinces without going through the Ministry of Law, and then we took out and burned the documents accusing Great Teacher Choi (大神師) of being an evil heretic and sentencing him to death, and the documents accusing Teacher Haewol (海月神師) of disturbing the state with his unorthodox ways and sentencing him to death. Walking freely under the blue sky and white sun while chanting "Long live the Donghak Army" was the most exhilarating thing. And after that, there was another great exhilaration, but I can't talk about this exhilaration. That time was truly exhilarating, but...

내가 經驗한 第一痛快
崔南善

There are times when you feel exhilarated when you understand what you've been most frustrated about, or when you understand something you thought you could never easily understand, but that can be different for each person. <38> I have been trying to understand Joseon and especially the very foundation of Joseon, but because there are originally few documents and rare artifacts, and because Joseon's history is like virgin territory, I have only been wandering in the wilderness day and night, unable to grasp anything, and I have had frustrations that others do not have in matters that others are not frustrated about. For example, from the meaning of the word "Joseon" itself, to the origin of the Joseon people, to the origin of Joseon culture and its unique points, I have been immersed in such darkness that others may have been living normally, but I have been living in frustration for a long time, thinking that I cannot live without knowing even these basic issues. Also, regarding Dangun (檀君) and Gija (箕子), who appear at the very beginning of Joseon history, whether they are passively accepting people or those who pretend to be clever and try to deny them, from our point of view, they are all absurd, and it seems impossible to obtain a solution that satisfies reason, so I have been living with great concern. I have been agonizing over these issues in various ways for more than ten years, and I have not been able to obtain affirmation from others, let alone a reassuring answer for myself, so I have been lamenting my own stupidity more than anything else. So, while going back and forth between small gains and great disappointments, I spent useless time, but now, almost ten years ago, due to the events of Giminyon (己未年; 1919), I cut off other relationships and lived in solitude, and naturally I had the opportunity to exclusively organize what I had learned from books and thoughts until now, and I searched for a unified and fundamental aspect of knowledge about Joseon, whether awake or asleep. After doing so for only a year and a half, on August 24th of Gyeongsin year (庚申年; 1920), at dawn, while performing Jeongjwa practice (靜坐法; seated meditation) as usual in the prison window of Gyeongseong Prison (京城監獄), I thought about how there are many mountains, great mountains, and famous mountains in Joseon with names containing the character "bright" (白), and also similar place names in the legends of national founders such as Dangun (檀君) and Haeburu (解夫婁), and as a result of deducing, inducing, classifying, and synthesizing various things based on that, I learned that there was a great principle called "brightness" (밝) in ancient Joseon, and based on that, myths were created, and one of the twins of myths became history and the other became religion, and at that time, it was as if daylight came out of the dark night, and I saw a new light in the whole world. I don't know what would be appropriate to describe the psychology at this time, but it was clear that it contained an exhilaration beyond what is commonly called exhilaration. <39> After holding onto this "brightness" (밝), even if I don't know about the state of ancient Joseon, there is something that seems to be known even in the unknown, and I know that a great cultural system existed centering on Joseon, and this is actually a significant fact and an essential aspect in the history of human culture, but until now, the academic world has been neglecting it, and on the one hand, I think it is strange, and on the other hand, I think that it is my mission to govern this land that has been abandoned by the academic world, even with insignificant power, and I am always living a kind of satisfying life.

내가 생각하는 痛快二三
韓龍雲

Meeting Han Yong-un (韓龍雲), whose upright character and pungent arguments provide coolness even in the sweltering summer heat, whenever I meet him, he says:
"Exhilaration! I have never seen anything exhilarating in my life. How can I talk about exhilaration when I have never experienced it?"
The reporter then asked, "Even among the books you've read...?"
He pointed to the Water Margin (水滸誌) on his desk and said, "If it's that kind of exhilaration, there's plenty of it in this book. It's exhilarating that Wu Song (武松) killed a tiger on the mountain, and it's exhilarating that Lu Zhishen (魯智深) went to where the Zen monks were meditating and smeared pig meat on their faces."
Master Han Yong-un (韓龍雲師) is a disciple of Sakyamuni (釋迦牟尼), who strictly prohibits killing, but he says that killing a tiger is exhilarating, and although he himself has done a lot of Zen meditation, he says that smearing pig meat on the faces of Zen monks is exhilarating, which demonstrates the beauty of his free satire and humor.
"Besides that, are there any Buddhist practices...?"
When asked, the Master replied again, "During the Imjin War (壬辰倭亂), when the Great Master Samyeong (四溟大師) met the Japanese general Kiyomasa Katō (淸正), whose sword light covered the sun and killed lives like mowing grass, Kiyomasa asked, 'Do you have any treasures in your country?' The Great Master said, 'We have treasures.' 'What are the treasures?' 'Your head is the treasure.' Kiyomasa was surprised and asked, 'What do you mean?' The Great Master calmly said, 'If we bring your head, our country will give a thousand gold coins, so what else could be a treasure?' That's a bit exhilarating, but compared to people in recent times who sacrifice others for their own sake and step over the corpses of those who have been shot to death, and gladly die, it's not that exhilarating."
Finally, "Really, in the Master's own experience?"
The Master, gradually expressing his true self from humor, said, "The only somewhat exhilarating thing in my life was when I gave a speech at the 00 Hall years ago, but I can't talk about that!"
And a piece of sorrow covers his bright face. (Reporter)

朝鮮近代史中에 통쾌한 일
權悳奎

Looking at modern history rather than old days, I find the Great Prince Regent's (大院君) abolition of Seowon (書院; private academies) very exhilarating. <40> Around that time, Confucianism and Confucian scholars were seen as frightening idols by the general public, but after the Great Prince Regent took power, he abolished the Seowon like lightning. When some people criticized this, the Great Prince Regent said,
"I cannot leave the dens where they collude by selling out the sages of the past. Even if Confucius were to be reborn, there would be nothing he could do."
So, he rejected the memorials and uproar of thousands of Confucian scholars who gathered in Gyeongseong from all over the country to oppose this with a single shout, drove out hundreds of Confucian scholars to Mapo (麻浦) with soldiers, and finally carried out his initial intention. Whether it was to destroy pro-Chinese thought, to eradicate the dens of the yangban (nobility), or to shatter yangban thought, it was a great exhilarating event.

博士論文 맛치던 날
朴昌薰

I think the most exhilarating time was when I completed my doctoral dissertation. When I graduated from Gyeongseong Medical College (京城醫學專門學校) and got a job as a doctor, it could be said that there was almost no enthusiasm for academic research among our Joseon colleagues. At that time, I (if I say this, there may be some readers who will scold me for being conceited) started research by immersing myself in the hospital research lab, using my spare time outside of the time I spent in diagnosis. At that time, some of my Joseon colleagues even ridiculed me as a madman, because
First, I had shallow talent and a dull brain, or perhaps others underestimated my capabilities too much.
Second, it was impossible for a Joseon person who graduated from a vocational school to expect to write a doctoral dissertation or submit a paper at that time.
Third, the external environment made it difficult to deeply desire this.
However, I completely disregarded these various things and tried to move forward without being daunted. So, I started writing my doctoral dissertation, which was a small part of my expectations, and I almost enthusiastically sat in the same place for 8 days and nights, forgetting to eat and sleep, and finally completed it before anyone else. At this time, I felt a kind of indescribable exhilaration.

罪 버슨 두 女人
金炳魯

As a lawyer, there are often exhilarating results, whether viewed from the perspective of legal opinions or from the perspective of resolving the grievances of the parties involved. <41> To give an example, this happened when a certain prestigious family was falling into ruin. There was a house belonging to Lee Yong-sik (李容植, pseudonym), a panseo (判書; high-ranking official) from the Old Korean era. His eldest son had died early, and he had two grandsons. The elder was Lee Hyeong-gu (李兄求, pseudonym), and the younger was Lee Je-gu (李弟求, pseudonym). And Hyeong-gu's wife was Kim Jin-ok, and Je-gu's wife was Park Cha-ok (朴次玉, pseudonym). Now, Lee Hyeong-gu, the grandson, was, as often happens with children of prestigious families, completely unproductive and dissolute, engaging in debauchery. So, if he left the estate of a thousand seok (石; unit of volume) of land inherited from his grandfather as it was, he would have squandered it all in just a few days and be left empty-handed. Therefore, by unanimous agreement of the clan, all of Lee Hyeong-gu's land was entrusted to his younger sister-in-law, Park Cha-ok, and the ownership was transferred and registered in Park Cha-ok's name. Then, because Lee Je-gu, Park Cha-ok's husband, was also dissolute and there were rumors that he was forging his wife's seal and selling the land, Lee Hyeong-gu and Park Cha-ok consulted again and decided to cancel the trust and transfer the ownership to Lee Hyeong-gu's wife, Kim Jin-ok. The two men were both branded as dissolute and untrustworthy, and the bizarre phenomenon occurred where land yielding a thousand seok of harvest was transferred and registered to the two women. However, before completing the transfer registration, Lee Je-gu indeed forged his wife Park Cha-ok's seal and sold land worth more than 100,000 won to Jang Gwang-han (張狂漢, pseudonym), a usurer, for 50,000 won, and received 20,000 won of that money. It was said that only 20,000 won, but after deducting the bribes and cuts for the middlemen, he didn't even get that much. Jang Gwang-han, from the beginning, knew perfectly well that Lee Je-gu was selling the land without Park Cha-ok's consent, but he filed a lawsuit against the four people—the landowner Lee Hyeong-gu, his sister-in-law Park Cha-ok with whom he had a trust agreement, Lee Je-gu who sold the land as Cha-ok's husband, and Hyeong-gu's wife Kim Jin-ok who received the second sales contract—for false entry in official documents and fraud. However, in the preliminary examination, it was recognized that Lee Je-gu had sold Lee Hyeong-gu's land entrusted to Park Cha-ok without Park Cha-ok's consent, so only Lee Je-gu was handed over to the trial, and the other three were acquitted. In the midst of this, Lee Hyeong-gu even suffered the tragedy of dying in prison. After that, Lee Je-gu was convicted in the trial while not being detained, and fled to Shanghai (上海). But Jang Gwang-han again filed a civil lawsuit seeking to cancel the registration of ownership transfer to Park Cha-ok and Kim Jin-ok. In the first trial, Park Cha-ok and Kim Jin-ok lost, and they appealed. During the trial, it was discovered that the transfer registration of ownership between Park Cha-ok and Kim Jin-ok was based on a sale, which was incorrect, so Park Cha-ok again created a notarized deed stating that she was giving the land to Kim Jin-ok free of charge. However, after that, it was discovered that the person who should have owned the land, Lee Hyeong-gu, had died, so the ownership now went to Lee Hyeong-gu's biological mother. So, because it was invalid to create a notarized deed between Kim Jin-ok and Park Cha-ok without the consent of Lee Hyeong-gu's biological mother, Park Cha-ok, Kim Jin-ok, and Lee Hyeong-gu's biological mother created another notarized deed stating that Park Cha-ok was giving the land to Kim Jin-ok free of charge with the consent of Lee Hyeong-gu's biological mother. However, the black-hearted Jang Gwang-han claimed that Park Cha-ok and Kim Jin-ok had made a false statement to the public official when creating the notarized deed, causing the notarized deed to contain false information, and filed another criminal lawsuit for forgery of official documents. However, when Park Cha-ok and Kim Jin-ok confessed to the fact that they had created the two notarized deeds mentioned above, the opinion of the judge and prosecutor in the first trial was that
Regardless of who the land ownership goes to, whether the transfer registration of ownership between Park Cha-ok and Kim Jin-ok is genuine or not,
The act of creating a notarized deed of gratuitous transfer when there was no transfer of ownership between Park Cha-ok and Kim Jin-ok at the time of creating the notarized deed alone constitutes the crime of forging an official document, so they were convicted. In the second trial as well, the opinions of the presiding judge, the two associate judges, and the prosecutor were the same as the first trial, and the same judgment as the first trial was made. However, at that time, as the lawyer, I was confident in arguing that the legal interpretation of the first trial judge and prosecutor (2 people) and the second trial judge and prosecutor (4 people), a total of 6 people, was unfair, so I immediately filed an appeal. The gist of the reason was that if the land belonged to Lee Hyeong-gu and was entrusted to Park Cha-ok, and then the trust was canceled and the ownership transfer procedure was carried out from Park Cha-ok to Lee Hyeong-gu's wife Kim Jin-ok in accordance with Lee Hyeong-gu's opinion, then even if a notarized deed was created afterwards, no crime would be established. Now, the High Court indeed recognized my reason for appeal and overturned the second trial judgment. As a result of the fact-finding trial, Park Cha-ok and Kim Jin-ok were found not guilty, and they were able to see the blue sky and white sun again in the criminal case, and they also won in the civil case. The parties involved were elated, and I was also very elated to have argued with the six judges from the first and second trials and achieved the final victory.

적은 痛快
朴八陽

-"You are a newspaper reporter. Then, there must have been at least one time in your life as a reporter when you felt exhilarating. So, please write about that."
After receiving this request from the Byeol Geon-gon editor, I thought, "Well-" and thought about it again. Not just once, but carefully.
-Indeed, have I ever felt exhilarating during my life as a reporter? Was there really "even once?"-
-"No."
That would be my honest confession. That's how much our profession is an unrewarded profession. Responsibility. Tension. Overwork. <43>
-These are our tasks, but in return, we get nervous breakdowns, indigestion, and forgetfulness.
-These are the rewards that come to us.
But that doesn't mean that there can be no pleasure or flavor in my profession, which I feel attached to. There is some. There is as much as there can be. -But I doubt whether there is anything that can really go as far as "exhilaration."

Reporting on the movements of the oppressed class as they are-of course, within the scope permitted by the authorities-is one of my pleasant things. Our pain lies in not being able to report facts that should be reported to the public. Therefore, when we can report the movements of the oppressed class to a certain extent within the permitted range, we feel some pleasure. This can be said to be the natural feeling of someone who has a sense of being one of the oppressed.
But pleasure and exhilaration are different. It's just pleasure, not exhilaration. Only by taking a step further from there do we reach exhilaration.
There are times when a part of the oppressed class-or a member of it-does not engage in normal resistance, but shouts out a "hysterical" sound of resistance in an outburst. Also, there are times when sudden acts of violence occur in response to unfair oppression. Actions arising from so-called "righteous indignation"! It has an exhilarating beauty for anyone.
If you read the history of the Russian social movement in the 19th century, the speeches and actions of many defendants who did not share the same views as the government in court have a certain exhilarating beauty, regardless of our understanding of them.

I do not want to talk about the specific details of such exhilarating events that I have reported and experienced because I anticipate that it will be related to censorship. However, when reporting on the trial of an "anarchist" who demands the use of honorifics to the judge and refuses to be questioned, stand, or swear an oath, or on the trial of a socialist who demands equal status with the judge by saying that he will not answer if he is called "you" (お前), I feel a kind of unspeakable exhilaration. It may be my useless youthful courage, but what is exhilarating is still exhilarating.

新聞記者로의 痛快
柳光烈

There are various exhilarating things in the life of a newspaper reporter, and I will choose one of them to write about. It was when I was working for the Donga Ilbo (東亞日報) and was assigned to cover the court as a field reporter.
At that time, the issue of Bun 참봉 (分參奉) was causing quite a stir, and there were rumors that someone among the aristocrats had taken bribes and someone had taken money, and it was said that Yoon Deok-yeong (尹德榮) himself had received money and bribes and made people into Bun 참봉, and Yoon Deok-yeong's name was in the newspapers every day. However, even though the noise was so great, there was no actual evidence, and the police or the court did not even call Yoon Deok-yeong once, so the newspaper reporter was truly sounding a drum without knowing the truth. So, the editor-in-chief in the company said,
"We've written this much in the newspaper, so now we must not miss the article about Yoon Deok-yeong being summoned to the prosecutor's office..."
And gave a warning. After that, I went near the prosecutor's office every day and watched to see if Yoon Deok-yeong was being called or not. One day in the afternoon, I was about to pass the door of the prosecutor's office when the clerk of the prosecutor's office was answering the phone.
"Yes-yes-please come around ten o'clock tomorrow morning. Are you coming by palanquin (步轎)? Yes-yes-it doesn't matter. Yes-I don't know! It doesn't matter if you come by rickshaw (人力車)."
At this time, I quickly realized with the so-called sixth sense of a newspaper reporter,
"That's it! Maybe Yoon Deok-yeong will be summoned at ten o'clock tomorrow."
And I came into the company, took the photography team, and waited in ambush at the gate of the courthouse. Around ten o'clock, a rickshaw covered with curtains came in. At that time, the photography team was a Japanese person named Yamazaki (山埼). As soon as he saw the rickshaw, he quickly said,
"Kore daro (これだろ - Is this it?)."
I couldn't answer quickly.
"Sa (さあ - Well)."
At this time, the rickshaw came straight in like an arrow without hesitation, and when the curtains were lifted and he got off, Yoon Deok-yeong came out. Failure...stomping his feet, Yamazaki (山埼) said, "Kore da (これだ - This is it)." Yamazaki (山埼) hurriedly adjusted the "lens" of the camera and aimed, but the shadow of Yoon Deok-yeong, looking around before and after and entering the hallway with shortness of breath, was missed. "Frustrating!" I said, "Kore da no yu-noni (これだの言うのに - Even though I said it was this)..."
Yama: "Kore da to yu datte...sakini yu! Degure nage reba (これだと言うだって...先に言う！出遅れなければ - Even if you say it's this...if you had told me earlier! If I hadn't been late...)"
Yoon Deok-yeong, who went up to the second floor, was questioned for about an hour in the reception room by Prosecutor Kawanishi (川西), who was in charge of the Bun 참봉 issue at the time, and came out. This time, Yamazaki (山埼) stood close to the gate, determined not to miss it, and with his big head and big eyes, he looked around in all directions, fearing that he would be caught by the eyes of newspaper reporters or the newspaper's photography team, and took a flash photo of him as he was leaving, panting for breath. And that day, in the evening edition, the photo that had been developed with great difficulty was published in three columns with the headline
"Yoon Deok-yeong, the focus of attention, finally summoned to the prosecutor's office."
It was very exhilarating, as if I had accomplished something I had been eagerly waiting for. <45> The next day, each newspaper in Joseon competed to reprint it, and each newspaper in Japan also reprinted it, and it was even reprinted in English newspapers. There was an exhilarating feeling that I had provided the first material to all the news agencies and all the newspapers.

Brother R,

痛快한 小說 몃 가지
朴英熙

"Please write about what novel you think is the most exhilarating and what it is like." This was the thing that Byeol Geon-gon (別乾坤社) asked me when we met. So, I have no choice but to make time and try to write this leisurely introduction.
However, when I pick up the pen, I am reminded of some difficulties. That is because I have not been fortunate enough to see even the cover of a book called a novel lately, so I have not had time to read novels. And what I have seen is all from a long time ago, so my memory is blurry, and also, has there been anything that we have seen in our lives that is exhilarating? It has become so much that it is a leisurely task, but not an easy one. And because I have moved all the novels, even scraps, in my bookcase to the antique shop, the level to discover this exhilaration is vague. So, I have no choice but to squeeze out what I think is still faintly in my head and write it down.
Sometime-it seems like more than 3 years ago-a friend of mine named Y showed me a booklet with a red cover and asked me to read it, saying, "It's really exhilarating, take a look!" That book is not a novel. It is a play. The name is "German Man Hinkemann (獨逸男 힌케만)", and the author was Ernst Toller (에른스트·토르리-), who Germany produced after the war. The content of the book is about what happened after World War I, so the protagonist Hinkemann went to the battlefield and fought for the capitalist country, but unfortunately, he was castrated. In other words, he lost his genitals to a bullet. He returned from the battlefield and could not endure poverty, so he went through various hardships, and then he went on stage. In fact, the play he performed was exhilarating. Eating live rats (鼠) in front of the crowd, how tragic would the sight of the dripping blood be! And he has a violent outburst cursing the world and humanity. Unfortunately, I don't have the book in my hand, so I can't introduce the passage, but anyway, that scene was exhilarating. And the tragedy at the end caused by the sexual impulses of the castrated Hinkemann and his wife-all of this tragedy is the tragedy of the proletariat who became slaves on the front lines of the 00 class. Some critics say that Toller's (토르라-) ideology is humanitarian. But anyway, that scene alone had an exhilarating taste.
And the 3rd act of "R.U.R (인조노동자)"-Original Name R.U.R-which I introduced in the Gaebyeok (開闢) magazine before, was also exhilarating. The revolt of the slaves in the human slave factory against the capitalist factory owners is truly one of the exhilarating things. In novels, there was an exhilarating scene in the novel "The Jungle (쩡글)" that I saw last winter. "The Jungle (쩡글)" is a book by Sinclair (씽크레아) in the United States. A Lithuanian worker wanders and drifts, and comes to the capitalist country, the United States, with his lover, and experiences the hardships of the tyrannical American capitalist system, and exposes the dark side of this capitalist system. The original is a huge book of more than 400 pages, so I cannot say everything one by one, but now I will try to introduce only one scene. Joris (요리쓰), the protagonist, a worker-and his wife, Ona (오나), are people who work in a factory. With the cunning tactics of the female foreman of Ona's factory, one worker violates Ona's chastity. Joris, who knows this, runs to the factory to take revenge. The brief quote is as follows:
-Ona said to her husband, crying.
"It was two months ago. At that time, he told me to come to his house-to that house. And he told me to stay in that house. He told us not to work. That night he made me go to that house. I told you too. I was going to be in the factory. But one night it snowed so I couldn't go home. And the car broke down yesterday. What ruined us was this little thing. I was going to walk, but I couldn't. I didn't want to tell you. -Omission- That guy started to get tired of me. Soon he separated me. He said that I was pregnant and getting uglier-he said that to me twice yesterday. And last night-he kicked me. Come on! You kill that guy and you kill that guy and let's die---"
