I was originally healthy, so I did judo and the parallel bars quite a bit in my student days, and for 23 years after being discharged from the military in November of the year before last, I didn't even go to the doctor or know anything about medicine, and my weight was about 62.2 kilograms. But last year, in April, I was afflicted by an unknown illness on the evening of the 16th, which I cannot forget. I was on my way home from the company with a friend.

I met a close friend from my student days, had dinner together at a nearby restaurant, and when I returned to my boarding house, the landlady poured me some tea. I was reading a book, so when I remembered and drank it, it felt like my insides were tearing apart because it was so full. But I kept reading, and I fell asleep without even realizing it. When I woke up, my lower abdomen was aching terribly. It still hurt the next morning, but I forced myself to go to work. Around noon, the pain in my lower abdomen was gone, but this time I had a pain in my left chest and felt dizzy, so I left work early. The landlady, worried about me, brought a thermometer and an ice pack. When I took my temperature, it was 39.8 degrees.

The village doctor (生醫) that the landlady secretly called diagnosed me with "Rassel" in my left lung. As May began, there was no sign of improvement, so on the 3rd, I went to 00 Internal Medicine in Shibuya-ku and was diagnosed with "pulmonary infiltration (肺浸潤)." When I asked what pulmonary infiltration was, they said, "It's an early stage of tuberculosis, and you'll probably get better if you rest for about half a year." So I immediately sent a telegram to my hometown to wait for my brother to come up and discuss it. I was resting absolutely, but by early July, I wasn't getting any better, and on the morning of the 11th, I coughed up a blood clot the size of a large bean. So I discussed it with my brother again and decided to move. On the 18th, after being farewelled by my concerned seniors and friends, I rented a small house in a coastal area of Shizuoka after 4 hours. But for some reason, the land I chose for recuperation was a land of fear for me. The night after I arrived, in a land where I didn't know anyone,

I Suffered from Bloody Sputum for 24 Hours
Suddenly, I had a major hemoptysis. Along with a weak cough, frothy, bright red blood was splattered on the white mat.
I had heard about hemoptysis many times and read about it in books—the complicated feelings of fear and despair when I experienced it myself—only those who have experienced it can understand. But thanks to the kind treatment of the village doctor, I mostly stopped bleeding after about a week, and with the doctor's advice, I decided to return to my hometown when I could walk on my own.
I got a hemostatic injection from the doctor and returned to my hometown on August 2nd, in the middle of summer. This long journey started a fever as I had expected, but from this time on, I took "Tanzai Wakamoto (綻劑와까모도)" on my brother's recommendation. As a result of that and rest, the fever started to go down at the end of August, but the phlegm still had blood mixed in.
As the fever subsided somewhat, I started nutritional therapy and tried air therapy and sunbathing by repairing the warehouse (庫) in the backyard, but that also caused a lot of bloody sputum and hemoptysis, so it ultimately failed. As a result, I even forgot to take "Tanzai Wakamoto (綻劑와까모도)."
But from the evening of September 7th to the dawn of the 8th, I had difficulty breathing, and eventually I was suffocating. Although it was night, I sought an examination from Dr. M, who used to be a military doctor, and he said it was concurrent peritonitis (腹膜炎倂發). Alas, all is lost! Thinking about whether the day I would be dragged away by the god of death would be today or tomorrow, I wandered on the line of death for another two weeks. However.

Even in This Dying State
The desire to become strong again made the blood in my dying body boil. This was the first day I promised to be reborn. It was "Tanzai Wakamoto (綻劑와까모도)" that is now on the medicine shelf. I put ten pills in my mouth in one breath. At the time, I didn't expect this cheap "Tanzai Wakamoto (綻劑와까모도)" to be my savior. I took it with some doubt about its effectiveness, but only out of respect for my brother, thinking that I couldn't not take it.
I still had a fever after taking it. Even after 10 days, the fever didn't go down. I became somewhat self-destructive and took 12 tablets each, as many as 50 times a day. The next morning, I had several bouts of diarrhea.
After that, at some point, the fever went down, I felt a little refreshed, and I remembered that I hadn't wanted to eat because my stomach was upset, so I ate all three pieces of bread (팡) that my sister had bought. Everyone in the family was surprised. Then my brother bought me a thousand pills of "Tanzai Wakamoto (綻劑와가모도)," so I started taking it continuously with extraordinary energy and conviction, but unfortunately, I caught a cold. I thought that if I caught a cold, my fever would go up, and if my fever went up, I would have hemoptysis, but this time, thanks to the power of "Tanzai Wakamoto (綻劑와까모도)," I had no bloody sputum, let alone hemoptysis.
The high fever gradually dropped to 37.2 degrees, and the diagnosis on October 3rd said that the ascites (腹水) had also decreased. The progress from then on was truly amazing, and on the 9th, the ascites was completely gone, and they said that I could read or take a walk if I didn't have tuberculosis (肺結核). So I can sit and eat, and the fever rarely goes over 7 degrees. Moreover, my pulse on the evening of the 8th was 71. This was the lowest it had been since the onset of the disease. During hemoptysis and high fever, it had gone up to about 114, but I had been counting 95 to 110. (Omitted)
October 11th—I was twice as happy on this day than before. That's because the "Rassel" was gone. The sound of lung disease and "Rassel"—I don't think I need to explain what the disappearance of "Rassel" from that opposition means.
