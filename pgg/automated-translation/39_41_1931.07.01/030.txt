Modern Figures in Photographic Art

Yi Yong-ik, originally from Kilju, North Hamgyong Province, was a prominent pro-Russian figure during the late Korean Empire. He held high positions such as Minister of Military Affairs and Chief of the Royal Household (Naejangwon gyeong 內藏院卿), and he dedicated himself to education, establishing Bosung College and elementary schools. After the Russo-Japanese War, the pro-Japanese faction led by Song Byeong-jun gained power, and the pro-Russian faction declined. He wandered in Shanghai and Russian territory and eventually died in Vladivostok. He is the main figure in the so-called First Bank and 900,000 won incident that is currently being discussed in newspapers. Yi Jong-ho is his adopted son.

近代人物寫眞畵報
李容翊씨는 元(咸北吉州人) 韓末 親露派 거두로 軍部大臣 또는 內藏院卿의 大職을 지내고 교육에 뜻을 두어 普成專門 中學 小學 등을 설립하얏다. 日露戰爭後 宋秉崎 一派의 親日派가 정계의 세력을 잡고 親露派가 몰락되며 그는 上海로 露領으로 방랑하다가 海蔘威에서 遂히 객사하얏스니 요새에 신문상에 떠드는 소위 第一銀行과 90만원사건이란 그 사건의 주인공이오 李鍾浩씨는 그의 양자이다.
