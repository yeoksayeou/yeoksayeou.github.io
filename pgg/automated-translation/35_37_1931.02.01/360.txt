잡지명	別乾坤 第37號
발행일	1931년 02월 01일
기사제목	(희곡) 米價大暴落(1막 2장) (Play) Rice Price Crash (Act 1, Scene 2)
필자	蔡萬植
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Screenplay)

Cae Mansik

Characters:
Kim Jusawa - Young Landowner
Kim's Father
I Sŏbang - Kim's Servant
Hong Sŏbang - Kim's Servant
Four or Five Farmers
Chŏnsbang Owner (전ㅅ방) - Shop Owner
Chinese Coolie (쿠-리-) - Kim's tenant farmer cultivating vegetables

Time:
Late Autumn, 1930

Place:
A rural village not far from a small city in Jeolla Province

Scene 1

A large warehouse covered with corrugated iron is seen in the front, and a small general store (chŏnsbang, 전ㅅ방) is to the right. The warehouse door (right next to the general store) is wide open, and a large scale is placed in front of the door. On the scale head are a large abacus (珠盤) and a so-called "judgment passbook (判取)" (P'ant'wi, 판취) (a type of bankbook).

To the left, three or four piles of rice are stacked, and Hong Sŏbang, with a pencil tucked behind his ear, leans lazily against a pile of rice sacks, basking in the sunlight.

In the general store, three rice containers (궤짝) are placed diagonally in front, displaying rice, barley, and millet. Besides that, dried pollack, straw shoes, tobacco (three nyang), cheap soju, and sweets are placed separately. The general store owner sits behind the rice container, with his side turned towards the money chest, smoking a tobacco pipe.

The area in front of the warehouse is a wide road that runs from left to right.

Around 2 PM, the curtain rises, and the stage remains unchanged for a moment. Soon after, Farmer (1) enters from the left, carrying a sack of rice on his back.

Farmer (1) (Approaches the scale, looks around, sets down the sack) How much is it per keun (근)?<157>
Hong Sŏbang (Reluctantly gets up, walks towards the scale) Top quality is three jeon six ri.
Farmer (1) (Setting down the rice sack, aside) Gee whiz! (To Hong) So how much is it per sack?
Hong Not even six won.
Farmer (1) (Aside) Gee whiz! It's fourteen or fifteen mal (말)! Can't you give me a little more? (Takes down the rice, puts it on the scale, and unties the bag)
Hong (Picks out a handful of rice)
Farmer (1) It's top quality rice.
Hong (Picks up a grain of rice and chews it) Three jeon four ri.
Farmer (1) (Surprised) Huh? You said top quality is three jeon six ri. Is this not top quality?
Hong It's glutinous, but not quite ripe.
Farmer (1) Still, give me five ri more.
Hong Can't do it.
Farmer (1) (Hesitates)
Hong What are you going to do? Take it back if you don't like it.
Farmer (1) Just look at it a bit more generously.
Hong I told you, I can't.
Farmer (1) (Scratching the back of his head) Just weigh it as it is.
Hong (Adds weights to the scale, takes them off, puts them back on, and weighs the rice) Ninety-three keun.
Farmer (1) (Earnestly stares at the scale)
Hong (Writing in the ledger) Go pour it in the warehouse.
Farmer (1) (Puts the rice sack on his back, enters the warehouse, comes out with the empty sack, and puts it on the scale)
Hong (Looking at the weight) Four keun. (Writing) Ninety-three keun minus four keun equals eighty-nine keun (writes). You understand?
Farmer (1) Yes.
Hong (Using the abacus) Three won and two jeon six ri (writes it down, then takes off his overcoat and takes money from a large money pouch tied to his waist) Here - three won, two jeon, and for the six ri (goes into the warehouse, comes out with a bundle of tobacco (석냥) and hands it over) - six ri in this.
Farmer (1) (Counts the money carefully)
Hong (Returns to his original spot and leans back in his place)
Farmer (1) (Holding what he received in his hand, first puts the sack on his carrying frame and goes to the general store) How much is millet? (Takes a handful and examines it)
General Store Owner One hwan fifty-three jeon.
Farmer (1) Isn't the price of millet coming down?
General Store Owner I'm not the one setting it.
Farmer (1) (Ties the sack around his waist) Give me one mal (말). Make sure you give me a full measure.
Hong Sŏbang (Comes to the front of the general store, seemingly bored)
General Store Owner (Measures the millet with a measuring container) <158>
Farmer (1) (Spreads the sack open, holding one end of the opening in his mouth and both hands apart, waiting for the general store owner to pour the millet in, speaking unclearly because of what he is holding in his mouth) Don't skimp too much.
General Store Owner (Pours millet into the sack)
Hong Sŏbang With rice prices so cheap, you're selling rice to buy millet?
Farmer (1) (Tying the opening of the sack) Still, there's a taste to adding millet to the rice.
Hong No matter how much millet you add, how can it be as filling as rice?
Farmer (1) Still, it fills your stomach when you eat it. (To the general store owner) How much is a bottle of kerosene?
General Store Owner Twenty jeon.
Farmer (1) Tch, that hasn't come down either! Give me a bottle. (Takes a dirty beer bottle hanging on the carrying frame and gives it to the general store owner)
General Store Owner (Takes the bottle and pours kerosene into it)
Farmer (1) (Taking it) How much are the dried pollack?
General Store Owner Four jeon each.
Farmer (1) Give me five and a piece of paper and a bundle of tobacco.
General Store Owner (Picks up each item as requested)
Farmer (1) How much is it all together?
General Store Owner (Using the abacus) Two won thirteen jeon.
Farmer (1) (Gives the three won he was clutching in his hand)
General Store Owner (Opens the money chest and gives change)
Farmer (1) (Takes the change, puts the purchased items in the rice sack he sold, and hangs them on the carrying frame. He greets the two men and exits to the left.)
Hong Sŏbang Tch, those pigs! Selling rice at such a cheap price to buy millet!
General Store Owner Still, they must have their reasons.
Hong Sŏbang What reasons could they have? They're so full of crap, it's coming out of their throats.
General Store Owner (Suddenly) I wonder why Master Kim (Kim Jusawa) is buying up so much rice when the market price keeps falling?
Hong Tch, do you think he has less sense than we do? Just watch. In a few months, the price will skyrocket. There are plenty of good ways to make money. With something like the Rice Low-Interest Financing (米穀低資融通), you can deposit the rice in the financial association (금융조합) or the agricultural association warehouse and get money at a very low interest rate. Then, when the price goes up, you just sell it off.
General Store Owner So, the world is only for those with money. <159>
Farmer (2) (Enters from the right carrying a sack of rice on his back. Sets it down in front of the scale and sighs.)
Hong (Walks towards the scale)
Farmer (2) How much is it?
Hong Top quality is three jeon six ri.
Farmer (2) Huh? They say it's four jeon over at XX!
Hong Do those XX guys buy rice with money, or do they dig up the ground to pay?
Farmer (2) Still, that's what they say.
Hong Well, then, carry it all the way to XX!
Farmer (2) (Taken aback) Ah, no, what…
Farmer (3) (4) (Enter from the left carrying a sack each)
Farmer (2) Did you brothers bring a sack each?
Farmer (3) (Sets it down by the scale) Yes, did you come to sell rice?
Farmer (4) (Sets it down by the scale) Did Elder Choe (Choe Sŏbang) come?
Farmer (3) How much are they saying?
Farmer (2) Three jeon six ri for top quality, they say.
Farmer (4) They say it's four jeon over there.
Farmer (2) I don't know.
Farmer (3) Whether it's four jeon or eight jeon, if we wait a little longer, the price might go up, and if we deposit it with the financial association, they say they'll give us a low loan and hold onto it until the price goes up much higher.
Hong Huh, that's for hundreds of seok (석), not one or two sacks.
Farmer (2) That's true.
Farmer (4) And if we wait until then, we have to be able to live while being tormented by debt. They're saying they're coming to seize our assets any day now.
Farmer (2) I borrowed fifty won from the financial association, and I brought a sack hoping to pay some of it back, but I'm afraid I won't even be able to do that.
Farmer (3) I've already been pressured several times by Master Kim's household because of the ammonia fertilizer bill...
Farmer (2) (To Hong) Can you take a look at the quality first?
Hong (Examines Farmer (2)'s rice sack, picks up a handful, chews on it, and reconsiders) Three jeon two ri.
Farmer (2) (Surprised) Three jeon two ri?
Hong The rice is rough and also not well matured.
Farmer (2) (Feeling uneasy, thinks)
Farmer (3) Take a look at ours too.
Hong (Examines it in the same way as before) About the same - three jeon two ri.
Farmer (3) (Disappointed, doesn't say anything)
Farmer (4) What the hell!
Farmer (2) (Looking at Farmer (3) and (4)) What are you going to do?
Farmer (3) I don't know. <160>
Farmer (2) Let's just sell it as is and go.
Farmer (3) (Clicking his tongue) Let's do that.
Farmer (2) (Looking at Hong) Please make sure you measure it well.
Hong I will.
Farmers (2,3,4) One person holds the carrying frame, and two people lift the rice sacks one by one, placing them on the scale and untying the bags. They then proceed in the same manner as with "Farmer (1)," weighing the rice and receiving payment. The farmers take the money they received for the rice, pick up their empty carrying frames, and exit dejectedly to the left.

Scene 2

A part of the garden in front of Kim Jusawa's reception room. A grand house is visible in the background. To the left is a pond. A passageway runs between them. A black-painted middle gate is on the left in front. As the scene opens, Kim Jusawa is standing stiffly between the flower beds, facing the pond, lost in thought.
Father (Enters from the right of the stage and comes down the garden path.)
Kim (Takes his hand and steps aside)
Father With the price of rice falling so much, how are you going to handle the rice money (볏돈) you lent out?
＜Note＞ Rice money (벼ㅅ돈) is when farmers borrow money from landlords or moneylenders in August or September before the harvest, promising to repay it with rice at a set price per seok (석) once the harvest comes in.
I am already in big trouble. At first, I thought it would be at least three won per sack this fall, so I lent out five hundred seok (석) at ten won each, totaling five thousand won. But now, one sack doesn't even fetch six won...
Father (Smacks his lips) And yet you're still buying up all the rice?
Kim If I buy it now, the price will go up soon, and if it doesn't, I can store it in the agricultural association or financial association warehouse and get a low-interest loan, so there's no need to worry.
I Sŏbang (Entering through the middle gate with several newspapers and letters in his hand)
Father Still, the way you guys do things is always so risky – it worries me. (Exits through the middle gate)
Kim (To I Sŏbang) Did you call?
I Yes, they're saying it's four jeon two ri.
Kim Hmm… It's up one ri from yesterday.
I Yes.
Farmer (5) (Enters through the middle gate carrying a sack of rice)
Kim What's this?
Farmer (5) (Setting down the frame and gasping for breath) It's the rice money (벼ㅅ돈) I borrowed in early autumn.
Kim (Frowning as if annoyed) Hey, you people! You kept asking and asking to borrow rice money, and I gave you ten won per sack, but now it's not even worth six won, so I'm going to be ruined.
Farmer (5) That's what I'm saying. If we had known the rice price would be so cheap, we wouldn't have borrowed so much...
Kim (Looking at him) Go and have them weigh it properly. (Asking) Not even one nyang less than one hundred and ninety keun (근).
I Yes. (Looking at Farmer (5)) Bring it. (Gives the newspapers and letters to Kim)
Farmer (5) (Carries the sack of rice and follows him to the right, exiting behind the stage)
Kim (Opens the newspaper and looks at it. After looking for a while, aside) Huh?! They're doing it again?! These bastards! Huh! (Reads the newspaper carefully)
I Sŏbang, Farmer (5) (Reappear on stage)
Farmer (5) (Coming to Kim's side) Goodbye.
Kim (Perfunctorily) Yeah.
Farmer (5) (Exits through the middle gate)
Kim (After reading it all, looking at I) Are there bastards like this? The Chinese guys, I mean. (Hands the newspaper to I to read) Are they treating poor Koreans like this?! Huh?!
I (Takes the newspaper and tries to read it)
Kim (Hurriedly) Go quickly and tell the man who just came that I said he shouldn't buy even an inch of cotton cloth from the Chinese from now on... Go and tell him, quickly, quickly.
I (Puts down the newspaper and runs)
Kim (To his back) And call that Wang bastard. (Picks up the newspaper again and reads it)
I (Appears a little later with the Chinese coolie)
Kim (Glares at the Chinese man)
Chinese Man (Showing his yellow teeth and bowing) I come.
Kim Now there's no work, no nothing. Pack your bags and go back to your country.
Chinese Man (Startled and stammering) Huh? We do nothing bad. We do nothing wrong.
Kim (Pointing to the newspaper) Look at this, you bastard! In your country, they treat our Koreans like this...
Chinese Man We know nothing of this. We good people.
Kim (About to scold him) Don't talk nonsense, you bastard.
Chinese Man (Frightened) We good people. Yes.
Farmer (3,4) (Enter through the middle gate carrying empty frames. Seeing Kim) Hello.
Kim (Turning around) Yeah.
Farmer (3) (Takes out the money he received for selling the rice from his purse and offers it to Kim)
Kim (To I) Take that. (To Farmer (3)) Is that the ammonia fertilizer bill?
I (Comes to receive it) <162>
Farmer (3) (Scratching the back of his head) Yes... I'm sorry, but it's eleven won, so if you could take this for now, I'll pay the rest of the sack in a few days...
Kim (Getting angry) What?
Farmers (3,4) (Flinch)
Kim Do you think I stole ammonia and gave it to you?!
Farmer (3) How could you do that?
Kim Then what are you doing? The deadline was already a month ago, and now you're only bringing half of it?!
Farmer (3) I'm just really sorry. The harvest was good, but the rice price is so cheap that after paying for this and that, there's nothing left to eat. We'll probably starve to death this winter. There's nothing else we can do.
Kim I don't care if you live or die! I'm about to die too! No, if you don't bring the remaining eleven won before sunset today, I'll have your assets seized tomorrow. (Storms off to the reception room) I Sŏbang.
I Yes.
Kim (Turning back) First, take that bastard's eleven won and tell him to bring the rest before sunset today.
I Yes. (Snatching the money from the farmer) Go and get it ready.
Kim No forgiveness. What do I care if you live or die. (Exits to the right)
Farmers (3,4), Chinese Man (Stand stiffly, staring blankly without any emotional exchange)
-＜Curtain＞ <163>

제1장
함석으로 둘너지은 큰 창고가 정면으로 보이고 그 우편으로 조고만한 전ㅅ방이 잇다. 창고는 문이(바로 전ㅅ방 엽흐로 낫다) 훤히 열니엇고 문압흐로는 큰 저울이 노혀잇다. 저울머리에는 굴다란 珠盤과 소위 「判取」라는 通帳이 노혀잇다.
左手로 조곰 당겨서는 벼가 서너이 노혀잇고 홍서방이 귀우에 연필을 끼인 채 벼ㅅ섬에 늘펀히 기대어 안저서 해ㅅ볏츨 쪼이고 잇다.
전ㅅ방에는 압흐로 조선쌀 살액 좁쌀해서 궤ㅅ작이 비스듬히 세 개가 노혀잇고 그밧게 명태 집신 담배 석냥 왜소주 과자 가튼 것이 각기 노혀잇다. 전ㅅ방 주인은 살궤 뒤에 잇는 돈궤엽해 쪽을 트리고 안저 담배ㅅ대로 담배를 피우고 잇다.
창고압흔 좌우로 통한 넓운 길이다.
오후 2시쯤 막이 열니며 무대는 잠시 아모 변화가 업다가 조곰 후에 농민(1)이 가마니에 담은 벼를 지고 左手로 등장.
농민(1) (저울엽흐로 각가히 와서 두리번 두리번하다가 띄여노고) 한근에 얼마씩해요?＜157＞洪서방 (내키쟌케 이러서서 저울엽흐로 거러오며) 극상에 삼젼육리.
농민(1) (벼ㅅ짐을 밧처노흐며 傍白) 거참! (홍더러)그래 한섬에 얼마ㅅ골이요?
홍 육원이 다 못가지.
농민(1) (傍白) 거참! 여나문 말되는데 금이나 좀 노아보시우(벼를 내려서 저울우에 놋코 맥기를 풀어허친다)
홍 (벼한줌을 집어내본다)
농민(1) 벼야 극상이지요.
홍 (벼한알을 집어 톡 씹어복)삼젼사리.
농민(1) (놀내여) 예? 극상에 삼젼육리라면서 이벼가 극상이 못돼요?
홍 몽글기는 한데 좀 덜맛넛서.
농민(1) 그러치만원(間)오리나 채서주시오.
홍 안돼.
농민(1) (주저한다)
홍 엇절테야? 실커든 도루가지고가고.
농민(1) 조곰만 더 보와주시우.
홍 안된대도 그래.
농민(1) (뒤통수를 극적극적하닥)그대로 달어주시우.
홍 (저울추를 더 노앗다가 집어내어다 올녓다 내렷다하며 벼를 달고나서) 아혼세근.
농민(1) (저울눈을 지성으로 굽어다본다)
홍 (通帳에다 적으며)광에다 쏫고와.
농민(1) (볏가마니를 등에 지고 창고로 들어갓다가 가마니를 들고나와 저울우에 놋는다)
홍 (근수를 보고나서)네 근. (적으면서) 아흔세 근에서 네 근을 빼니까 여든아홉근(적는다) 응 알것지?
농민(1) 예.
홍 (珠盤을 노아가지고)삼원이전육리(적고나서 두루막이를 거더치고 허리띄에 찬 큰 돈주머니에서 돈을 끄낸다)자-삼원하고 이전 그리고 육리는(창고로 드러가서 석냥 한갑을 가지고나와)육리는 이놈으로.
농민(1) (자세하게 세여밧는다)
홍 (다시 전자리에 가서 전대로 안는다)
농민(1) (바든 것을 손에 든 채 지게에 가마니를 먼저 한편억개에 걸치고 전ㅅ방으로 간다) 좁쌀 얼마씩이요?(한줌을 뒤여서본다)
전ㅅ방 주인 일환오십삼전
농민(1) 좁쌀갑슨 아니내리나요?
전ㅅ방주인 낸들아오.
농민(1) (허리에찬 자루를 매여들고) 한말만 주시우. 말이나 잘 주시우.
홍서방 (심심한 듯이 전ㅅ방압흐로 온다)
전ㅅ방주인 (말로 좁쌀을 된다)＜158＞
농민(1) (자루를 펴서 아궁이 한끗을 입에 물고 두손으로 짝버려 전ㅅ방주인이 좁쌀을 부어주기를 기다리며 입에 물은 것이 잇서 분명치 안케) 넘우 깍지마시우.
전ㅅ방주인 (자루에 좁살을 붓는다)
홍서방 쌀갑시 이러케 헐한데 벼를 팔어서 좁쌀을 사먹어?
농민(1) (자루 아궁이를 동여매이며) 그래도 밥이 느는 맛으로.
홍 밥이 아무리 눈다지만 어터케 쌀밥만큼 든든허우?
농민(1) 그래두 먹는때는 배가 불으지요(전ㅅ방주인엑)석유한병에 얼마요?
전ㅅ방주인 이십전.
농민(1) 체-그것도 아니내려! 한병주시우(지게에 매달엇든 때무든 맥주병을 듸여다 전ㅅ방주인을 준다)
전ㅅ방주인 (병을 바더서 석유를 너어준다)
농민(1) (바더들고) 명태는 얼마씩 바더요?
전ㅅ방주인 사전씩.
농민(1) 다섯 마리만 하고 희연한 봉지하고 석냥 한통하고 주시우.
전ㅅ방주인 (달나는대로 일일이 집어다준다)
농민(1) 도통 얼마지요?
전ㅅ방주인 (珠盤을 노아본다) 이원십삼전.
