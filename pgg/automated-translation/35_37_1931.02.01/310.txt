閻羅國 審判傍聽記 (Record of Listening in on the Judgement in the Land of Yama)
金振九
문예기타 (Literature Miscellaneous)

Record of Listening in on the Judgement in the Land of Yama
						Kim Chin-gu

(Omitted) I became intoxicated with New Year’s Toso wine, my mind refreshed. Lying sprawled out, patting my full belly, I hummed a divine song, entering a heart-pleasing, celestial realm. Suddenly, my body transformed into a butterfly, ascending into the heavens! Passing by the foot of Pugak and Samgak mountains, and quickly passing Myohyang mountain in September, I arrived at the sacred region of Paektu. Two celestial officials were sitting beneath pine trees amid strange rocks. I bowed deeply and asked their esteemed names. One old man was Taesang Nogun (太上老君), and the other was T’aeŭl Chin’in (太乙眞人). They raised their staffs and led me to the Jade Capital (玉京).

Riding on the back of a blue crane, traversing the rainbow bridge, pushing aside the clouds and mist, I followed T’aeŭl Chin’in (太乙眞人) to the palace. The twelve great gates were like iron fortresses. The gatekeepers, with ghostly faces and blue bodies, grabbed me and asked for some favor.

It seems that even in this country, there are private favors and bribes. As a penniless wanderer, no matter how much I thought, I couldn't come up with a penny for a favor. After much thought, I took off my overcoat and gave it to them. They accepted it with a sneer and opened the twelve gates. The pearl palaces and jade towers were dazzling, and the rare flowers and plants were in full bloom. Five hundred celestial officials and fairies in golden crowns and jade bodies stood in the east and west pavilions. The Jade Emperor (玉皇上帝), wearing a Tongch’ŏn jade crown and a robe with sun and moon patterns, sat high on the throne, commanding his officials.

Taesang Nogun (太上老君) went before the Emperor, bowed deeply, and said,
“I have brought a human being who admires the Emperor and ceaselessly sings divine songs. I am honored to request a special audience.”

The Emperor smiled gently and granted permission for the audience. I carefully performed four bows.

“With my body stained with the sins of the mortal world, I have defiled the Jade Capital, a crime worthy of ten thousand deaths. Yet, you bestow such favor upon me. I am deeply grateful.”

The Emperor showed a benevolent expression on his face and said,
“Your words are commendable. Since you have come all this way, listen to the Yama’s judgment and return, but never leak the secrets of heaven.”

As he finished speaking, he summoned Judge Ch’oe (崔判官 (珏)) and ordered permission for me to observe the Yama's judgment. The judge gestured with his hand and led me to the Yama's palace. Taking one step inside, it was completely different from the Jade Capital. A chilling atmosphere enveloped me. Compared to the warm, spring-like air of the Jade Capital, it was like the harshness of autumn frost and winter snow. King Yama (閻羅大王), wearing a fire crown and holding a white jade scepter, sat high on a golden dragon throne, and beneath him, divine generals with dragon faces and beast bodies were arranged on both sides. Under the banner were arranged flags, spears, swords, and various instruments of punishment such as axes and cauldrons. It was a terrifying and desolate sight that made one's hair stand on end. I asked Judge Ch’oe (崔判官)
“Has King Yama (閻羅大王) been the same person since the creation of heaven and earth?”

The judge shook his head and said,
“No, the King is replaced every five hundred years of human time. The current King is Kim Ch’i (金緻), the father of Kim Paek-gok (金栢谷), who ascended to heaven after being treated to milssu (密水) while serving as the governor of Kangwŏn Province to Samch’ŏk. He became the King after the former King, Yun Kwan (尹瓘) of the Koryŏ Dynasty.”

“Then, who orders the replacement?”
“Why do you ask such a foolish question? It is by the order of the Jade Emperor (玉皇上帝). Who else would order it?”

I took a seat in a corner of the hall as guided and observed the scene. After a while, the sound of “Waiting! Coming!” (待令去來) rang out loudly, and the doors opened. The daily messengers (日直使者) entered, holding badges, followed by the monthly messengers (月直使者) wielding iron whips. Behind them, five hundred arhats (五百羅漢) with demon-faced tattoos dragged in human sinners in groups of ten, bound in chains, and made them kneel before the court. <131>

Judgement of the Wealthy

The daily messenger (日直使者) knelt before the court and said,
“We have brought in ten wealthy human sinners at once.”

Judge Ch’oe (崔判官) unfolded his writing tools and questioned each of the ten sinners. After questioning and confirming, King Yama (閻羅大王) raised his voice and said,
“You became a wealthy man (富家翁), the foremost of the five blessings in the human world, enjoying pleasures that others could not. With your abundant wealth, what merit, what good deeds, what virtues did you perform? Were you loyal to the country? Were you filial to your parents? Were you fraternal with your siblings and harmonious with your relatives? Did you offer food to the hungry and perform the merit of saving lives? Did you give clothes to the naked and perform the merit of reaching paradise? Did you build houses along the roads for travelers to stay? Did you build bridges over rivers and launch ships on the seas, performing the merit of crossing waters? Did you provide medicine and treatment, performing the merit of saving lives? Did you provide water? Did you chant Buddhist prayers?
Did you build shrines to honor the wise ancestors and set an example for future generations? Did you establish schools to educate your descendants? Did you build factories to relieve unemployment? Did you create media outlets to raise public awareness? Did you support social organizations to suppress the strong and support the weak? Did you care for the elderly and the orphans? If you did all these things, you will go to the paradise, to the lotus platform, to achieve divine nobility, to become the Commander-in-Chief of the Three Armies, or the Prime Minister of the country.
If you only know how to sit high in tall buildings and grand mansions, enjoying golden goblets and beautiful women, then go to hell!” (秋霜) It was a frosty command.

One. Official Power and Local Tyrant (官權土豪). An old man was called out and made to stand on a platform. The King's accusation and sentence were announced at once. “You, with the power of your ancestors and the prestige of a noble family, enjoyed the blessings of a county magistrate (郡守), a minister of the court, and a Poguk (輔國), taking advantage of your position to falsely accuse, beat, and imprison humble and powerless local people, extorting money and land from them, becoming a millionaire with twelve gold calves (金松아치 열두마리). You will be sent to Kŏmsan Hell (劍山地獄)!”

The old man was bound with chains, stabbed with swords and spears, and thrown into the cold and desolate hell. He screamed and his limbs were torn apart like skewered meat.

Two. Merchant of Self-Interest (利權商人). Another gentleman was seized.
“You spent money without helping the unfortunate or contributing to the public good. You bribed powerful people to get rich and gain official titles, enjoying eight beautiful concubines and spending your days gambling. You will be sent to Centipede Hell (蜈蚣=지네 地獄)!”
Soldiers with knives and axes seized the gentleman by the scruff of his neck and threw him into the dark hell. Red-backed centipedes crawled out, clinging to his body and sucking his blood. <132> It was a spine-chilling sight.

Three. High-Interest Loans (高利貸金). Another person was seized.
“You charged high-interest loans to the poor with your ancestors' money. When the due date passed, you used your sons, grandsons, and great-grandsons to enforce false seizures, forced executions, rice field seizures, and eviction procedures, relying on the courts. You drove the poor to become beggars, wandering to the north and south. You were cruel and did everything you could. You will be sent to Viper Hell (毒사 地獄)!” He was thrown into a dark pit, where poisonous snakes coiled around him, biting him relentlessly. It was a gruesome scene.

Four. Tenant Farmer Exploitation (소작인착취). A large landowner wearing a tanggon (탕건) on his head and holding a long pipe was dragged out, his manggŏn (망건) and tanggon (탕건) removed, his pipe broken, and he was forced to kneel.
“You monopolized thousands of ri of fertile land on the plains and exploited tenant farmers like slaves, collecting land taxes, gambling taxes, and additional levies. You indulged in wine pools, meat forests, and mountains of delicacies, without ever contributing to social causes. You will be thrown into the Oil Cauldron (油鑊)!”

His clothes were stripped off and he was thrown into the oil cauldron. The sound of human flesh being fried was unbearable to witness.

Five. Labor Enrichment (勞力致富). Another person was seized.
“You worked diligently with your own blood and sweat, discovering mines and fisheries, becoming a millionaire through hard work. This is commendable, but you did not use that money for social or national projects. You just hoarded it for yourself. You will be designated as a pig.” His clothes were removed, and a mask was put on him, transforming him into a pig. He entered the pigpen, grunting.

Six. Fraudulent Acquisition of Wealth (詐欺取財). A slickly dressed man in a suit was seized.
“You shameless and cunning daylight robber, you deceived the sons of foolish wealthy men, squandering their family fortunes and spending it on wine, women, and pleasure. Your intelligence and cunning have saved you from human hell, but you cannot escape the Yama's judgment. Know this and do not complain.” His nose was pierced and he was locked in an iron cage.

He begged and pleaded, but there was no hope of escape.

Seven. For Ancestral Fortune (爲祖先부호). An old man was called forward and made to kneel before the court. The King personally offered him a white jade chair and had him sit down. “You have been diligent and frugal, becoming rich. You honored your ancestors by building shrines, holding ancestral rites, and erecting statues, enlightening the path for your descendants. You will become a noble celestial official (一流仙官) and serve by the side of the Jade Emperor (玉皇上帝).”
The man, his face full of joy, put on a golden crown and jade robes and ascended to the Jade Capital.

Eight. Educator (교육가). Another gentleman was called forward and seated on a silver chair.
“You used your ancestors' inheritance to build schools and factories, educating your descendants and relieving unemployment. Your virtuous deeds are commendable. You will be granted eternal life and boundless blessings (無量大福)!” <133>

Nine. Philanthropist (慈善家). An old woman with white hair and a gentle demeanor was called forward and seated on a gold cushion.
“You toiled with your own body and hands, gathering wealth, not knowing how to be greedy. You dedicated your wealth to public projects, charitable works, debt relief, and rent reduction. You only did good deeds. You will be reincarnated as the wife of the president of the world.”
The old woman danced with joy, guided by a fairy, and went out through the gate.

Ten. Relief Worker (구휼사업가). Another old woman was called forward and seated on a gold cushion.
“You gathered helpless old people and created nursing homes. You gathered orphans without parents and created orphanages. You gathered the blind and the deaf and established schools for the blind and deaf, providing vocational education. You gathered lepers and created leper hospitals. You will be reincarnated as a man and become the leader of a party, and be elected president.”
The woman stepped out of the gate, boarded a blue crane, and departed for the human world.

After the judgment was finished, the instruments of punishment were put away. The tall soldiers were dismissed, and the celestial officials and fairies gathered. Wine and peaches were prepared, and I was invited to the court. At first, I declined several times, but then I thought, why not? Well! Having been born into this world, it would be a chance to participate in a banquet in the Jade Capital (玉京) and drink a cup of wine offered by a fairy's hand. I sat on a wide golden cushion and tasted one cup, then two. The taste was strange, and the feeling was special. Celestial songs were sung, and the five tones and six laws were complete. I could not resist the festive mood and sang the representative Korean folk song "Kaechina Chingching" (캐지나칭칭). The laughter from left and right shook me awake. I opened my eyes wide and saw the three musketeers of R.K.B.

"Hey, even when you're sleeping, it's 'Kaechina Chingching'?"

Having witnessed such an incredible sight, after being repeatedly asked not to leak the secrets of heaven, I decided to simply publish the judgment of the wealthy, even if it meant going to hell. <134>

一. 官權土豪. 한 노인 불러내어 동틀 우에 올려세우고 대왕의 論告, 판결문이 한거번에 言渡된다. 너는 각 조상 질머지고 일류 양반의 위력으로 郡守, 관철대신, 輔國, 인간의 영복을 너혼자 누리면서 寒微하고 세력업는 지방부호 잡어다가 업는 죄명만 드러서달 고치고 매고때려 項鎖, 足鎖, 오라, 주리, 못견듸게 죄형하야 돈을 뺏고 땅을 빼서 금송아치 열두마리 百萬長者되엿스니 너는 검산지옥으로 보내노라.
그 노인을 오라사슬 올거다가 칼 창 꼬자 노흔 차바람 쓸쓸한 지옥에다 집어던지인다. 비명을 냅다치며 四肢六體는 산적 꾀이듯 나려꾀인다.
二. 利權商人. 또한 신사 잡아내어
너는 돈을 쓰되 불상한이 살려주고 여러사람의 공익 되는데는 한푼 돈을 쓰지 안코 권력자의 불알글거 뇌물주고 기부해서 부자되고 官爵어더 八仙女妾을 두고 賭博遊樂일을 삼엇스니 너는 蜈蚣=지네 지옥으로 너흐리라.
刀斧들은 羅卒들이 그 신사의 덜미를 집허서 컴컴한 지옥으로 집어너흐니 등어리 뻘건 百足蟲이 꿈틀끔틀 기어나와 사지육체 척척부터 피를 쭐쭐 빨아먹는다.＜132＞ 보기에도 소롬이 쭉쭉 끼친다.
三. 高利貸金. 또 한 사람 잡어내어
너는 父祖의 남긴 금전 빈민에게 고리대금, 기한을 넘기면은 아들은 邊, 孫子 邊 曾손 邊가지고 假差押, 强制執行, 立稻差押, 明渡手續ㄷ, 재판소 신세만 지고 빈한한 사람은 죄다 날몸둥이만드러서 流離丏乞, 北國南方으로 헛터지게 가혹잔인 못할 짓이 업섯스니 너는 독사 지옥으로 보내노라. 캄ㅂ캄구렁에 집어너으니 長蟲毒蛇 홰홰 칭칭 둘러감고 우둑우둑 깨무는 꼴은 처참한 광경이다.
四. 소작인착취. 머리에 탕건쓰고 장죽 손에 들은 대지주를 집어내어 망건탕건 벗기고 담배때 와락빼서 뚝뚝 꺽거내버리고 냅다 꿀려노은 후에
너는 평원광야 沃土千里 할가로 독점하고 소작인은 노예로 부리면서 地稅, 賭稅, 兼徵하고 그 우에 삼중사중으로 奸策的착취를 자행하야 酒池肉林과 山珍海味에 파뭇치면서도 사회사업 한번 안코 그냥 이리 드러왓스니 너는 油鑊에 너으리라.
그 사람 몸을 벗겨 기름 가마에 집어던지니 버지직버지직 인육 『댐부라』되는 광경, 目不忍見 볼 수 업다.
五. 勞力致富. 한 사람 잡어내어
너는 네피와 네땀으로 극력 근면해서 광산 어장 발견하야 일확천금 長者가 되엿스나 그 점은 가상이나 그 돈을 가지고 사회, 국가의 대사업을 하지 안고 그냥 우물주물 네 혼자만 드러왓스니 도야지로 점지한다. 그 사람의 옷을 벗겨 탈을 가러 씨우닛가 도야지로 되엿구나 도야지 우리속으로 꿀꿀거리며 드러간다.
六. 詐欺取財. 말속한 양복쨍이 한 사람 잡어내어
뻔뻔스럽고 간교한 自晝强盜 가튼 놈아 어리석은 부호의 자식을 꾀어내여 그 사람의 祖父業을 탕진식히고 그것을 가지고 好酒美女의 유락으로 소비하엿스나 네 지혜 영리하야 인간지옥 면햇지만 염라신부에서는 면할 일이 바이업다. 그리알고 원망마라 그놈의 코를 꾀여 철강속에 가두엇다.
빙빙돌며 애걸하나 나올 길이 가망업다.
七. 爲祖先부호, 한 노인 불너내어 당하에 꿀려노니 대왕 친히 白玉椅子내여주며 그 자리에 안친 후에 너는 근면저축, 부자가 된 두에 선현을 숭경하야 사당지어 祭享을 올리고 동상건립, 후손의 압길을 개명햇슨 너는 一流仙官의 귀한 몸이 되어 옥황상제 侍側을 하게 하노라.

九. 慈善家. 白髮風身 넉으럽게 된 한 노파를 불러내어 금방석에 안친 뒤에
