잡지명	別乾坤 第五號
발행일	1927년 03월 01일
기사제목	地下國訪問記, 所謂 富 所謂 貧者 諸君에게 (A Visit to the Underground Kingdom: To All of You, the So-Called Rich and the So-Called Poor)
필자	먹적골 許生
기사형태	문예기타 (Literature and Other)

From my belly, a rumbling sound just emanates, but the rich ignore it, debt collectors come and go, and though I fear what people might say, I cannot kill myself, I have no means, so I cannot commit fraud, I hate prison, so I cannot steal, I don't even have a pistol, so I cannot threaten anyone, I have nothing to pawn, so I cannot get a loan. How can this state of poverty be good?

"When one is poor, even following a chick's rear end will cure a cold," so I will go to see Golden King Hŏ Saeng and listen closely to his secrets of wealth. Maybe my empty belly will at least feel full. And I will listen to his stories of impoverished days. To see if it gives me comfort. Yes, I will do that. <48>

Should I seek him in the underground kingdom? Or should I seek him in the heavenly kingdom? There are no reports of him being dead, nor are there reports of him being alive, so where should I seek him? Is he playing baduk with Ch'ŏksongja, or enjoying the moon with Yi T'aebaek? Is he on Piro Peak of Kŭmgang Mountain, or on the highest peak of Halla Mountain? Is he enjoying himself in the East Sea or in the South Sea? Is he still living in his humble cottage in Mŏkjŏktong, or is his whereabouts totally unknown?

Aha! It seems he might be there. Let's look for him there. The master originally said that wealth and status are like floating clouds (富貴如浮雲), and fame is like worn-out shoes (功名如弊履), so he would not be in this mundane world. Furthermore, that place is one of the places that the master personally pioneered, one of the places where he earned ten thousand gold pieces and then scattered them away, a matter of the Samshinsan, one of the isles where true men reside, so he surely must be there. That is, either Kŏje Island in the South Sea or Halla Mountain on Cheju Island, especially around the crater where blue cranes cry and white deer leap. Let us seek him there.

"I am extremely presumptuous to trespass on this sacred place as a mundane person, but I have come without regard for the thousand li because of an urgent matter... I carry the burden of my depression over my poverty, the suffering of all my propertyless comrades, and all the injustices and inequalities of this world of the rich and have come before you, master. Please tell me about your days of poverty, and please tell me about your days as the Golden King. Please tell me in detail how you gathered your wealth and how you spent it. And please tell me the ways to behave towards all the poor and the rich of the world. Tell me specifically, comparing how one should act as a poor person and how one should act as a rich person with your own life as an example."

After a long silence, the master slowly opened his mouth and gave a lecture, and indeed, my heart and mind opened wide.

"Indeed, you are a lowly being from the mundane world. Why do you fear poverty so much, and why do you envy wealth so much, as a life that comes empty-handed and goes empty-handed...? I have lived a life of freedom in poverty and wealth, holding the universe in my own hands. My days of poverty, you ask? At that time, I lived in a humble cottage in a place called Hanyang Mŏkjŏktong, making it my nest, reading day and night. My wife barely made a living doing needlework for others. Because I was so hungry, she wept and pleaded with me, saying, 'When hunger and cold reach the bone (飢寒이 到骨), what good does it do to read day and night?' So I smiled and said nonchalantly, 'My reading is still not proficient,' and my wife became furious and rebuked me, saying, 'Neither trading nor manufacturing, how can you not be a thief?' So I said, 'Alas - the ten-year promise ends in seven years (十年期約이 七年에 終矣로다),' and I closed my book and went out into the streets. I looked for a man named Pyŏn, famous for being rich, and asked him for ten thousand gold pieces in a single breath. Pyŏn must have seen something in me, because he granted my request immediately, despite the objections of his servants and guests. So I took that money and went to Ansong and traded in dried fruits such as jujubes, chestnuts, pears, and raisins. Because there was a nationwide shortage of fruit (果物이 絶乏), prices began to rise,<49> and I made ten times my money in a single go. So, wanting to try something bigger with that money, I went to Cheju Island and traded in horsehair (馬갈(갈기)). Again, I made ten times my money. So, with that money, I obtained a large empty island with the introduction of a certain sailor, and gathered thousands of bandits (賊徒) from Pyŏnsan, scattering grain and goods to start development. Perhaps because it was a newly developed land, the crops grew so well that in a few years (不幾年) I earned tens of millions of bags of grain. At that time, there was a great famine in Japan, and they ordered foreign grain, so I loaded ten thousand bags and sold them, earning a million in gold. At this point, I thought, 'Alright, I've tasted money now. The experiment is over. I should return and live peacefully within the bounds of my fate.' So I said to the people, 'I am leaving,' and threw five hundred thousand in silver into the sea, distributed the rest to all the people, and took only one hundred thousand in gold to the capital to repay Pyŏn, but Pyŏn refused to accept it. I threw it down forcibly and returned home, only to find my wife holding a memorial service for me, saying it was the day I had left.

From then on, I became friends with Pyŏn, and one day, through Pyŏn's introduction, Ŏyŏngdaejang (禦營大將) Yi Wan came to see me, wanting to discuss the state of Namhan and talking about a northern expedition, asking for my opinion. So, I doubted the sincerity of those who sell their names and positions in the current times (時俗賣名賣官), and at first, I was aloof and cold, but he persistently pleaded, 'Please tell me your opinion.' So I gave him about three or four suggestions. But he said that they were all difficult and he could not carry out a single one. Finally, I suggested that he gather the heroes of the nation, have them cut their hair and wear barbarian clothes (胡服), and send them to Manchuria as students or merchants, wandering among the officials and commoners to investigate their strengths and weaknesses and make connections with influential figures, and then slowly plan a great undertaking to wash away the national shame (國恥), but Yi Taejang, upon hearing this, sighed and said, 'Our nation's scholar-officials (士大夫) value propriety, so who can adapt to barbarian clothes and hairstyles (胡服辮髮)?' and said that this too was difficult. So I became angry and, in a single breath, said, 'Scholar-officials? What useless corpses! Pŏn Ogi (樊於期) did not hesitate to cut off his own head for personal vengeance, and Cho Mulling (趙武靈) did not feel ashamed to wear barbarian clothes for the sake of strengthening the nation. Now, while talking about national shame, you are reluctant to wear barbarian clothes and hairstyles, so what will you do?' and shouted, 'Cut off the head of that false heir apparent (虛僞가세자) first!' and looked around for a sword, and he became so ashamed and frightened that he ran away. Afterwards, he came to me several times, requesting my advice on national affairs, but because there were no men of valor, I foresaw that a great event would be ruined (大事 誤矣), and also because wealth, status, and fame are like floating clouds, I set fire to my house because I was tired of the bustle of the mundane world and came to this place unnoticed, befriending the white clouds and flowing water for three hundred long years, singing the praises of heaven and earth." He narrated his entire life, and mundane people could not even dream of his great character. He then spoke of how to behave towards all the poor and the rich of the world, and I was so deeply moved that I did not want to leave his side. "Although it may sound boastful, if you experience poverty as I have, accumulate wealth as I have, and spend money as I have, you will clearly see the ups and downs of life in the palm of your hand. What is money after all? But I don't mean not to eat, <50> not to wear clothes, and not to live. I am not denying money. However, there are so many who cry and die for money, and so many who laugh and dance for money, which is ridiculous. What about the basic necessities of life? How can I deny that? The world is ignorant of economic problems, which are the center of everything. But how can there be such petty lives that cry for a penny, laugh for a penny, live and die, kill and steal? Of course, it's the evil of OOOO. I strongly support OOOO, as the recent news tells. It will already be so. I am against OOOO to the end. Isn't that why the world is so chaotic? I only gathered money temporarily, experimentally, in a possessive way, and you can tell just by seeing that I earned ten thousand gold and then threw it away. (Omission - Original text)

The world is not the private world of an individual (私世上). Therefore, everything is like that. It is right to eat together, work together, live together, and return to public ownership together. I wonder if all the rich and the poor of the world know this." (Omission below) - Written by Pak Dori. <51>

뱃 속에서 쪼루룩 소리는 그냥 나는데, 富者놈은 본체만체 빗쟁이는 들낙날락 그럿타고 是非가 두려우니 자살도 못하고 수단이 업스니 사기도 못하고, 감옥이 실흐니 절도도 못하고 딱총 좃차 업스니, 위협도 못하고 전당물이 업스니 빗도 못 엇고 엇더면 이 궁한의 신세가 조탄 말이냐?
「궁한 때는 병아리 뒷 꽁문이만 따라단녀도 잔 기침이 업서진다 」고 에라 黃金王 許先生이나 차자 보고 그의 치부술이나 슬컷 드러보자. 헛배라도 부르겟지. 그리고 그의 빈한시절 니약이도 드러보자. 위안이 되나 안 되나 에라 그리하기로 하자-.<48>

지하국으로 차즐가? 천상국으로 차즐가? 죽엇단 말도 업고 살엇단 말도 업스니 어듸로 차즐가? 赤松子와 바둑을 노는지 李太白과 玩月을 하는지? 금강산 비로봉에 게신 지 한라산 제 일봉에 게신지? 동해에 노시는 지 남양에 노시는지? 一間斗屋으로 墨積洞에 그대로 안저 게신 지 그 종적을 도저히 알 수가 업다.
올치! 그 곳에 게실 듯하다. 그 곳으로 차저 보자. 선생이 본래 富貴如浮雲을 말하얏고 功名如弊履를 말하얏스닛가 이 속계에는 잇지 안을 것이다. 더구나 그 곳은 선생이 몸소 개척한 곳의 하나이요, 만금을 어덧다가 만금을 훗터버린 곳의 하나이요, 삼신산의 일이요, 진인이 상주하는 해도의 하나이니 그 곳에 분명 게실 듯하다. 즉 남해의 거도 제주의 한라산 그 중에도 청학이 울고 백녹이 뛰는 분화구 좌우안일 듯 하다. 그리로 찻기로 하자.

「속계 천인이 선경을 범함은 극키 황송하오나 긴요한 일로 천리를 불구하고 왓사오니 더럽다 마시고 밧어주십시오... 저는 제 貧寒에 대한 抑鬱과 모든 無産동무들의 苦情과 또는 모든 부자 세상의 모든 불공불평을 한 짐 걸머지고 선생님 전에 왓슴니다. 선생님의 빈곤시절을 말슴해 주십시오, 그리고 선생님의 황금대왕 시절을 말슴해 주십시오. 엇더케 모으시고 엇더케 쓰신 것을 자세히 말슴해 주십시오. 그리고 세상의 모든 빈자부자에 대한 처세법을 말슴해 주십시오. 빈자로는 엇더케 해야 될 것과 부자로는 엇더케 해야 될 것을 선생의 일생에 대조하야 구체적으로 들녀 주십시오.」
선생은 침묵 良久에 천천히 입을 열어 일장을 설파하시는데 과연 흉금이 通開해진다.
