A Guide to Women's Occupations: How Many Places Are There to Work If You Can't Afford to Study Abroad?

Typical Female Teachers

Perhaps the first thing that comes to mind when thinking about Korean new women awakened to careers is the female teacher. Nowadays, even in good elementary schools, whether in Seoul or the countryside, there are usually two or three female instructors. In girls' high schools, you can see the faces of at least three or four female teachers. It is most appropriate for women, who are naturally responsible for educating children, to engage in this profession. Whether as a permanent career or a temporary one before marriage, there is no job as common or beneficial for young women as this one. However, for a young woman to be among many men and teach other people's children is not easy. Her actions and words must be very careful, and the responsibility is significant. Therefore, considering the importance of the responsibility, it can be said that this is the highest position for women. To become a secondary school teacher, it is best to graduate from a women's higher normal school, except for special subjects. For specialized subjects, graduating from a vocational school is also good. To become an elementary school teacher, graduating from the practice course of the Gyeongseong Normal School or a provincial normal school qualifies you. You can also obtain a license by passing the official teacher examination, or a graduate of a girls' high school can be employed as a contract teacher and obtain instructor qualifications after one or two years. The starting salary for secondary school teachers is seventy or eighty won, and it can gradually increase to over one hundred won. The starting salary for elementary school teachers is forty or fifty won, and it gradually increases with promotions. In any case, this is the safest, most convenient, and common job for women, but one must always remember the responsibility of teaching others.

Female Doctors with High Income

This is not as common, but the number of Korean female doctors is relatively high, and their profession is noble and their income is high. It is no exaggeration to say that it is the best of women's occupations. Doctors have long been considered to practice medicine (仁術) to widely relieve the suffering of the people (廣濟). It is a very interesting and suitable profession for women who are kind, gentle, and meticulous, but it requires considerable intelligence and extraordinary effort. As everyone knows, doctors have to make house calls even in the middle of the night if there are patients, and they have to deal with all kinds of patients every day, so it is an impossible profession unless one is physically strong. To qualify as a female doctor, one must graduate from a school prescribed by law or pass a statutory examination to be able to practice or work. However, skill is gained through experience, so even after graduating from school, it is necessary to refine one's skills by becoming an assistant at another hospital or with a famous doctor for two or three years. In Gyeongseong alone, besides Jeong Ja-yeong (졍자영), who has a private practice, the number of people working in leading hospitals and local hospitals is increasing every year. Many people also go abroad to study and further their research. Most of those who are qualified have graduated from women's medical schools in Japan or the Government-General's Medical School. There are still no female doctors who have achieved great success, but if skilled female doctors come out and show their performance (實績), the general public will trust them as much as men. Unlike school teachers, this is a job that can be done even while staying at home, so it is relatively convenient even after marriage. Specializations such as midwifery, gynecology, and pediatrics are most suitable. As it is a profession that saves the poorest and most unfortunate people, it is by no means a lowly occupation. The starting salary for qualified people working in hospitals is seventy or eighty won, and the income varies depending on the number of patients if they open their own practice, but it can be said that the income is considerable.

Interesting Women Journalists

The term "woman journalist" is quite a new profession for Korean women. It has only been three or four years since women journalists set foot in the media! Choe Eun-hui (최은희) of the Chosun Ilbo! Heo Jeong-suk (허정숙), currently studying in the United States! Heo Yeong-suk (허영숙) of the Dong-A Ilbo, Kim Gyeong-suk (김경숙) and Park Gyeong-sik (박경식) of Jeonsin Women, and Hwang Sin-deok (황신덕) of the Jung-oe Ilbo—these few were the first to occupy this position. As a result, each newspaper's family section and women's section were newly created, and the voices of women were raised considerably. Accordingly, the number of applicants for these positions is gradually increasing. Depending on one's personality and interests, it is a fairly interesting profession, but those who lack patience and activity are unqualified. Since skill appears based on common sense and experience, it is somewhat difficult for young people around twenty years old, so it is more appropriate for those who are at least twenty-five or twenty-six years old. Of course, it is not possible to become a journalist unless one has considerable talent and knowledge in writing or an interest in it. Moreover, the number of positions available is very small, and in the already weak Korean press, there is only one person per company, which requires more effort. There is no special institution for training journalists. You have to go to a newspaper or magazine company and gain experience through your own hard work. You have to go to places you don't want to go and meet people you don't want to meet, and you have to write articles you don't want to write. The amount of work is more than what people usually think. However, for those who have talent and can find it interesting, there is no job more fun than this. The general requirements for journalists are to be familiar with all aspects of society, to have clear judgment, and to have a lot of common sense. Although academic ability is not that necessary, graduates of Korean women's high schools may feel somewhat lacking, but they can demonstrate their skills depending on their talent and effort. In any case, to succeed as a journalist, you need to accumulate at least five or six years, or even ten years, of experience. Therefore, it is essential to be physically strong, patient, and hardworking. As for income, even in Japan, there are female journalists who earn from around 100 won to 200 or 300 won, but in Korea, it is impossible to even dream of that. You can only give 40 or 50 won, or maybe 60 or 70 won at most.

Kindergarten Teacher

(Qualifications) Graduate of Kindergarten Normal School
(Age) No particular restriction
(Monthly Income) Starting salary of 40 won, over 50 won or 60 won with experience
(Nature of the Job)
"On the pillow where the kind child sleeps,
The mother sits alone, sewing pants.
Even as she sews, the night does not deepen."
Singing songs like this with young children, playing with toys, and running around with them from morning till evening, and not thinking it dirty to wipe the runny noses of young children and wash their faces is the usual job of a kindergarten teacher (保姆).
Whether one is sick, troubled, tired, or cannot rest for a moment, one must run around and sing songs. Even when one has one's own lovely and cute son or daughter, there may be times when one feels dislike, and even when one has one's own sibling or nephew, there may be times when one feels annoyed. How much more so when one has to play the role of a mother to children, no matter how dear and cute they are. The mental and physical pain is not small. However, no matter how much they cry, fuss, wet themselves, run their noses, or kick and make a fuss, when one hears and sees the lovely children, like birds and flowers, singing with cherry-like lips and fluttering their soft hair like chicks and rabbits in the wind, the annoying and troublesome thoughts melt away like spring snow, and one feels so happy and joyful that tears come to one's eyes. <102>

Midwife

(Qualifications) Graduate of the Government-General's Medical Department or Severance Hospital's Department of Midwifery
(Age) Age is not important as long as you have the skills
(Monthly Income) Usually 40 or 50 won on average (minimum)
(Nature of the Job)
"No matter how much your stomach hurts, please calm down and come to your senses," saying this, and being dragged out of bed in the middle of the night or at dawn when others are sleeping, and listening patiently to the painful cries of a woman in labor who has been unable to give birth for days, twisting and turning, and shuddering, and helping the mother give birth safely is the job of a midwife. However, if she is fortunate enough to have a smooth delivery, that's one thing, but if, after struggling for two or three nights without being able to give birth, she witnesses the death of the mother and child, or only the young child, or only the mother—a young life dying without even seeing the world—she immediately wants to take down the midwife's sign, regardless of money or job.
However, on the other hand, when she thinks about her job having the important responsibility of assisting in childbirth, and that she personally brings a new seedling, that is, the young life of a newborn (新生), out of the mother's womb and creates a human being in this world for the first time, she experiences endless joy and pleasure. And the income of midwives generally depends on the wealth (貧富) of the family, and it is not a daily occurrence, so the monthly income cannot be fixed. They receive from ten won to forty won per delivery.

Nurse

(Qualifications) Graduate of the Government-General Hospital, Severance Hospital Nursing Department, or Dongdaemun Women's Hospital Nursing Training School
(Age) Fifteen to thirty years old
(Monthly Income) Starting salary of 15 won or more, less than 30 won in public hospitals, starting salary of 20 won or more, up to 40 won in private hospitals. <103>
(Nature of the Job)
"Oh, no matter how much it hurts, you have to bear it," saying this, and spending every morning and evening, 365 days a year, in a hospital filled with the smell of medicine, wearing thin, snow-white clothes in summer or winter, walking around with scissors and tweezers (짝게), and touching the pus and blood coming from the rotting flesh of patients, wincing and covering their noses, and always dealing with all kinds of patients, whether they like it or not, is the usual job of a nurse. Sometimes, when dealing with a seriously ill patient (重病者), they cannot sleep well at night and have to spend the night listening to the groans of the patient that they cannot bear to hear. Occasionally, they also have to close the eyes of others when they pass away (臨終). However, in the midst of all this, they say that there is nothing more pleasant and joyful than seeing a patient they have nursed and cared for fully recover (全快) and leave the hospital.

Radio Station Announcer

(Qualifications) Graduate of a girls' higher common school
(Age) Sixteen to forty years old
(Monthly Income) Trainee starting salary of 30 won, 50 won or more and 60 won as an "announcer"
(Nature of the Job)
"JODK! This is the Gyeongseong Broadcasting Station. We will now begin broadcasting."
Like this, from nine o'clock in the morning to nine o'clock at night, the job of an "announcer" is to stand in front of the "microphone" (broadcasting mouth) and tell the general radio (wireless telephone) listeners what they are going to do. At first glance, it seems like a very easy and comfortable job, but there are hardships there that others do not think about. Of course, since it is not a job that physically strains the body, there is less physical fatigue or pain, but instead, there is no small amount of mental fatigue and pain. From morning to night, they speak into the "microphone" without any reaction (反響), but they don't know if anyone is listening to their voice, or if they are laughing, liking, or disliking it. This makes them feel very frustrated and uncomfortable. However, when they think that a single word they say spreads not only throughout Korea but also to foreign countries and reaches the ears of hundreds of thousands of people, it becomes interesting and joyful, but also a great responsibility, so they cannot help but be anxious.

Telephone Operator

(Qualifications) Graduate of a girls' common school
(Age) Fifteen to twenty years old
(Monthly Income) Starting salary of 24 won or more, up to 50 won
(Nature of the Job)
"Moshimoshi, Nambang! Nambang!" (모시모시 남방! 남방!) Every day from morning till night, wearing a black uniform and with a receiver (受話機) on their ear, they ask in a lovely voice whenever the signal lights flicker, and mediate between telephone subscribers so that they can talk to each other. This is the job of a telephone exchange operator. People who do not know the situation may think that it is not that difficult to simply sit down, ask for the number, and quickly connect it. However, like the "announcer" at the broadcasting station, this job seems easy but has hidden pain and sorrow. People who have been to a telephone exchange know this, but those who have not been there do not know the situation and, if the operator answers a little late or connects them late, they swear at them, calling them names they cannot utter. From morning to night, they sit in front of the switchboard and, no matter how quickly they move their hands, they cannot keep up with the demand. They are scolded by customers and, if they look away even for a moment, they are scolded by supervisors. At times, they clutch their chests and want to cry, feeling wronged and angry. However, on the other hand, when they think about how they quickly connect people to the hospital when their lives are in danger, or how they quickly connect people to the fire department when there is a fire, preventing each dangerous situation from occurring, they realize that their responsibility is important and they feel most joyful and happy.

一般的인 女敎員

아마 조선의 신녀성으로서 직업에 눈 뜨게 된 최초의 것이 무어냐 하면, 적어도 이 녀교원을 가르처 말하지 아니할 수 업슬 것임니다. 근일에 와서 보통학교 중에도 조흔 학교이면 서울 이거니 시골이거니 반드시 녀훈도의 의자가 둘이나 셋은 반드시 자리를 잡게 된 만큼 일반화가 되고 녀자 고보에도 적어도 삼사인 씩은 녀 교유의 얼골이 뵈입니다. 엇재서나 자녀를 교양하는데는, 선천적으로 책임을 갓고 잇다고 해도 조흘만한 녀성이 이 직업에 종사하는 것이야말로 가장 적당한 일로서 영구적으로나 또는 젊은 녀자의 결혼하기전 일시적 직업으로 해서나 이 직업처럼 일반적이고 또는 장래에 유익한 것은 업슬 것임니다. 그러나 젊은 녀자의 몸으로 여러 남자의 틈에 끼여 만흔 남의 자녀를 가르치기란 그러케 용이한 일이 아니여서 일언 일동의 처신이 참으로 어려울 것이요, 책임이 역시 중대할 것임니다. 따라서 책임이 중대한 만큼 부인의 지위로 해서는 최고의 위치에 잇다고 보아도 가할 것임니다. 자격으로는 중등교원이 되랴면 특별한 과목을 제한외에는 보통으로 녀자 고등 사범을 맛치는 것이 제일 상책이고 전문 과목으로는 전문 학교를 맛처도 조흘 것이며 보통 학교 교원은 관립 경성 사범학교의 연습과나 도립사범의 규정한 줄신이면 물론 자격이 잇스나 법졍한 교원시험에 합격하야서 어들 수도 잇고 혹은 녀자 고보 출신이면 그대로 촉탁 교원에 채용되야 한 이삼년 잇다가 훈도자격을 엇는 수도 잇다 함니다. 수입으로는 중등 교원이면 초급이 칠팔십원으로 차차 백여원 까지 되기도 하며 보통 교원이면 초급 사오십원으로 차차 승급하여 올나간다 함니다. 어느 방면으로 보던지 녀자로는 가장 안전 편리하고 보통 일반적이나 그만큼 남을 가르친다는 책임이 잇는 것을 언제나 이저서는 안 될 것임니다.

收入 만흔 女醫師

이것도 그러케 일반적은 아니지만, 조선 녀자로서는 수효도 비교적 만코 또는 직업이 고상하고도 수입이 만흠<100>은 부인 직업중에 제일 위라 하여도 과언이 안닐 것임니다. 의사는 녜전부터 중생을 광제 (廣濟) 하는 인술(仁術) 이라고 한 말과 가티 인자성이 만코 친절성이 만코 일에 조밀한 부인으로는 매우 흥미잇고도 적당한 직업이나 상당히 머리가 조흔 사람인 동시에 비상한 로력이 드는 것임니다.
다 아시는 바와 가티 의원은 밤ㅅ중이라도 환자가 잇스면 왕진도 하야 되고 날마다 별의별 환자들을 드다름으로 몸이 튼튼치 안코는 도저히 할 수 업는 직업임니다.
녀의의 자격으로는 물론 법령으로 졍한 학교출신이 나 혹은 법졍(法定)의 시험을 맛쳐야만 개업할 수도 잇고 근무할 수도 잇스나 역시 경험에 인하야서 실력이 붓는 것임으로 학교를 졸업한다 하더래도 이 삼년간은 다른 병원이나 명의의 조수가 되야 수완을 련마할 필요가 잇다 함니다.
경성 안에만 하여도 개인으로 개업한 졍자영씨외에 유수한 병원에 근무하는 분과 지방병원에 근무하는 분이 해마다 늘어오고 외국으로 류학가서 연구를 더욱 츙실히 하랴는 분도 만흐며 자격을 갓고 잇는 분으로는 대개 일본 가서 녀자의학 전문학교를 맛첫거나 총독부 의학전문학교를 졸업한 이가 만타 함니다. 아즉 의사로 하야 훌늉한 성공을 한 분은 업스나 수완잇는 녀의가 만히 나와서 실적 (實績)을 뵈여주면 일반 사람의 신뢰도 남자나 가티 생길 것이요, 다른 직업에서 볼 수도 업는 노력이 필요한 대신에 학교 교사와도 달너서 집에 잇스면서도 할 수 잇는 직업임으로 결혼 후라도 비교적 편리하며 전문으로는 아모려나 산파, 부인과, 소아과가 가장 적당할 것이요, 가장 가난한 사람 불행한 사람을 구제하는 직업인 만큼 사업으로 보아서도 결코 천한 것이 안임니다.
수입은 자격 잇는 이로 바로 병원에 근무하게 되면 초급이 칠팔십원이라 하며 개업하면 환자의 수에 따라서 달늘 것이나 아무려컨 수입도 상당하다고 볼 수 밧게 업슴니다.

興味 잇는 婦人記者

『부인 긔자』하면 조선 녀자로서는 꽤 새로운 직업이어서 실지로 부인 긔자가 언론 긔관에 발을 드려 논지도 불과 삼사년! 조선 일보의 최은희씨! 현재 미국 류학중의 허정숙씨 동아일보의 허영숙씨, 전신녀성의 김경숙씨, 박경식씨, 중외일보의 황신덕씨, 이 몃분이 처음으로 이 자리에 의자를 점령하게 된 것임니다, 그래 각 신문의 가정란 부인란도 새로 생기게 되고 녀자들이 부르지지는 소리도 상당히 놉하저 왓슬 뿐 아니라 따라서 거긔에 대한 지원자도 차차 늘어오는 모양임니다. 각기 자긔의 성격이나 취미에 따라서는 상당히 흥미잇는 직업이나 아모려컨 인내성과 활동이 적은 사람이면 자격이 부족하다고 볼 수 밧게 업슴니다 . 상식과 경험에 떠라서 수완도 나터나는 것이닛가 이십세 전후의 젊은 분에게는 다소 곤란함으로 적어도 이십오육세 이상된이라야<101>만 상당할 것임니다. 더구나 문필에 상당한 재조와 소양이나 또 는 거긔 대한 흥미를 가진 특별한 사람이 아니면 될 수 업는 것은 물론이고 게다가 수용이 비상히 적은 데다가 갓득이나 빈약한 조선 언론게의 긔관에 잇서는 한사에 한 사람이라는 것도 분발증대 분발 이겟지요.
특별히 긔자 양성하는 긔관이 잇는 것도 아니요, 신문사나 잡지사에 드러가서 자긔가 고생해가며 경험을 쌋는 것이라 가기 실흔 곳에도 가야 되고 대하기 실흔 사람이라도 대해야만 되고 쓰기 실흔 글이라도 써야만 되닛가 그 수고로 말하면 그러케 보통 생각하는 바보다는 이상되는 것이 만슴니다. 그러나 재능이 잇고 흥미를 가질 수 잇는 분이면 이것처럼 자미잇는 일은 업슬 것임니다.
