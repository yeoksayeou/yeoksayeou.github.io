별건곤 제33호
1930년 10월 01일
專制國王의 白色奴隷, 新家庭生活破綻記, 新女性들 그는 왜 시집살이를 못하나. (Despot King's White Slave, A Record of a New Family Life Rupture, New Women - Why Can't They Endure In-Law Life?)
一記者
문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

I was returning home after taking my sister, who studies in Seoul, to my hometown after a long time. While waiting for the express train to Seoul in the first and second class waiting room at Daegu Station, I happened to witness an unusual scene.

"So, is a daughter-in-law not a child? Why do you ruin my life like this and then try to drive me out? I don't understand. Settle this and then leave!"

A young woman, presumably the daughter-in-law, wiped away tears with a handkerchief, stomping her shoe-clad feet and, without any hesitation, loudly rebuked an old man (probably her father-in-law), who appeared to be around 60 years old, stout, and swarthy, sitting blankly beside her. The gazes and interest of the people gathered in the waiting room were instantly drawn to this old man and young woman.

The old man, sucking on a long pipe, seemed unable to bear the shame, occasionally clearing his throat, getting irritated, and trying to appease her with soft words, looking utterly helpless. Their conversation, the young, modern daughter-in-law's unreserved grievances, continued for quite some time, drawing the interest of the waiting passengers. I silently imagined her situation and so-called fate, merely watching until a journalist's instinct suddenly arose, and I wanted to grab the woman and investigate the details of her situation. I was worried that the daughter-in-law might follow the old man, but fortunately, only the old man left on a southbound train, and the weeping daughter-in-law remained behind.

In such a case, having already revealed her situation to some extent in front of others, approaching her is easy. We decided to leave on the night train, and after giving my sister a few instructions, I sent her to approach the woman in my place. My sister readily accepted my request and went to the woman. After exchanging a few words, my sister led the hesitant woman to the second-floor restaurant, and I returned to the waiting room to await her report. What follows is the story my sister heard from her. She said the woman was very outgoing.<133>

"Of course, it was a love marriage. He's from Miryang, and I'm from Daegu. We met while studying in Seoul. He defied his family's arranged marriage, even after they had set a date, and amidst the opposition of his elders, we had a simple wedding in Seoul.

After that, my family faced difficulties, and my husband, still a student, couldn't support both of us. So, we decided that I would go to his family's house until he graduated and could live independently, and I would endure life in his family home until we could start our own household. My husband's family was wealthy and of noble lineage in Miryang. There were his parents, his grandparents, both over 70, his siblings, and his sisters-in-law, making it a very large household. Even if a pebble fell in the yard, his grandfather and all the servants would know, and even a pinecone rolling in the wind couldn't do so without the family's knowledge. Every day, my clumsy housekeeping was repeated under that terrible watchfulness. At first, my mother-in-law seemed to love me.

'A student daughter-in-law isn't so bad. Even though she's like a *yanggalbo* (洋갈보), she's quite good at taking care of her parents. She has good manners too.'

I heard her say things like this when a neighbor or relative visited.

At first, not knowing any better, I endured all the absurd things, the things that rubbed me the wrong way, the unpleasant things, and the bothersome things, unconditionally obeying. I became like a fool or an idiot in a temple. When my sister-in-law said something, I accepted it; my sister-in-law, with the authority of the 'firstcomer' (先參者), would unravel her complaints, saying this is how it is, that is how it is, and I listened meekly. So, I took over the duties of my sister-in-law and served my mother-in-law and father-in-law their meals morning and evening.

Even though they had servants, receiving the meal from their daughter-in-law's hands was their pleasure and their face-saving. But for me, it was a great ordeal. Carefully carrying the meal, I would enter and kneel before them until they finished eating. After offering the scorched-rice tea (숭융), I would finally carry the meal back, stepping backward respectfully, and only then could I sit down with a sigh and pick up my spoon. I couldn't help but feel a growing sense of rebellion: why should I be trapped again in the shackles of old morality and live like a slave? Of course, that feeling would inevitably show in my behavior. The more it did, the more my in-laws' attitude changed. From the beginning, they had many regrets and anxieties but couldn't completely refuse their son's wishes and had let me in. Now that I was acting like a 'new woman' (新女性), I was definitely out of their favor. Scorn, hatred, and malicious scrutiny to find fault spread through the eyes of everyone in the house, from my in-laws to my sister-in-law, my other sister-in-law, and even the servants. I was isolated and helpless. But the more that happened, the more I despised them.

'What are you all? You're so petty. Are these even people?' I would comfort my troubled heart alone. I had the powerful support of my husband, my thousand-army strength. Even if ten million of those fading humans became my enemies, I wouldn't be afraid.

As time passed, April arrived, and the biggest ancestral rite (祭祀, *chesa*) of the year was approaching. On the day of the ancestral rite, the women of the house gathered to <134> beat the dough, steam the rice cakes, prepare fried dishes, and make *jeonya* (전야, savory pancakes). Each woman displayed their years of accumulated skill, and I was somehow assigned to help with the *jeonya*. While they were frying, I was just whisking eggs and flour when a young woman sitting next to me said in a friendly tone, 'Oh, Seoul lady, try frying one. Let's see your skills. Ho ho.' Her words sounded like she was belittling me, saying, 'Can you even fry *jeonya*, you?' and I couldn't bear it. So, in a somewhat agitated tone, I said, 'Well, even if I'm incompetent, I can certainly fry this. I've even fried omelets and *tempura* (덴뿌라) at school. Like this...' At that, everyone burst out laughing, shaking their shoulders. I felt so humiliated that I couldn't contain my anger. I grabbed the arm of the woman who was laughing the loudest and asked her seriously why she was laughing at me. It was all clumsy, but facing such a situation, I didn't know what to do. She said, feigning innocence, 'Oh, you made us laugh. Saying you've fried omelets and *tempura* at school... Ho ho ho.' Another round of laughter erupted. I couldn't stand being there any longer, so I threw down the bowl of egg and flour mixture, went to my room, and cried. Those uneducated country women seemed to enjoy vulgar jokes. So, that night, while others stayed up all night preparing food and doing dishes, I stayed in my room with a headache and didn't go out. Then, that night, something finally happened. But it was so unexpected. It must have been a little past midnight. Everyone had gone to bed to rest until the first rooster crowed, and only the sound of the servant cleaning up the dishes could be heard faintly.

Unable to sleep with so many thoughts, I was dozing off when I felt a cold draft coming from somewhere. What could it be? I looked at the door and, oh my! The sliding door (미다지, *midaji*) was opening very carefully, little by little, silently. I lay still, trying to calm my fluttering heart, waiting to see what would happen. Finally, the door opened boldly, and a man slipped in like a ghost. I couldn't bear it any longer, so I jumped up with a look of great surprise before he could even touch me. His face also showed considerable embarrassment. He was a playboy from the village, a distant uncle of my husband. He must have been left behind that night as a ritual officer (祭官). I was not properly dressed, so I hugged the blanket and scolded him politely.

'Excuse me. Why are you sneaking into my room in the middle of the night?' Instead of answering, he gave a very awkward and embarrassed, pleading smile around his lips. What a brazen fellow. He looked at me with passionate eyes for a while, then, without thinking of leaving, he tried to pull this trick on me.

'What's the matter? I came to comfort you, risking everything. If you really don't want it, I can't force you, but seeing your expression... Heh heh, it's all just young people's passion. Everyone in the house is asleep. No one will know.'

Then he immediately tried to hug me. I wanted to slap that rascal right then and there, but I barely restrained myself and said, 'Please, don't even think such dirty thoughts and get out of here before you're disgraced,' pushing him out with my hands and letting out a long sigh. That's how the noblemen (량반놈) behave! Is there such a dog who would try to seduce his nephew's wife (족하 며누리)'s weakness? I was disgusted. That night, I couldn't sleep at all. No matter what, I couldn't endure that life! In-law life! The outdated thoughts, customs, and words of the heads of the family (싀부모) held absolute authority and controlled everyone in the house, so even a slightly deviating word or action was already considered an unforgivable sin.

It was an absolute monarchy where the feelings and lives of the subordinates were not acknowledged at all. Only those who became fools or simpletons and obeyed were filial sons and daughters. Oh, let me tell you the next story. The next day, my father-in-law called me in, gave me a lecture on ethics and morality, and then declared in a stern tone:

'I cannot keep in my house a wicked and cruel woman like you, who hates honoring ancestors and cannot harmonize with relatives and the clan. So, I will buy you a house, and until you and your husband discuss how to handle it, stay there. And I know you have some affairs, but I won't say more about it. It would be a disgrace to our family, so know that.' So, what happened? It was as if I was bearing all the blame alone. But what good would it do to argue with that ignorant old man for months? I retorted sharply and sent a long letter to my husband, who was at a technical school in Seoul, and came down to my parents' house in Daegu. I trusted my husband's love so much that my father-in-law, mother-in-law, and sister-in-law were not a problem. If they bought me a house and provided food, how fortunate would it be to live alone until my husband graduated and returned? But it was just honeyed words to drive me out. My husband, whom I trusted more than my life, must have heard something from my father-in-law or been threatened in some way (perhaps he was told that if he lived with that woman, he wouldn't be allowed to be a scholar or inherit a penny). The distance between our hearts grew further and further. And now, more than half a year has passed since I left, and they haven't even provided a room, let alone a house. Now I'm disappointed in my husband too. Who would trust and live with a man who abandons his innocent wife because he's attracted to his parents' wealth? I'm planning to sue them. Today, I put on a play, enduring the shame, just to embarrass that old man. There must be many unfortunate women like me in the world. How infuriating is that!<136>

專制國王의 白色奴隷, 新家庭生活破綻記, 新女性들 그는 왜 시집살이를 못하나.
						一記者
내가 서울서 공부하는 누이를 디리고 오래간만에 고향에 가다가 도라오는 길이엿다. 대구정거장 1,2등 대합실에서 서울 나가는 급행차를 기다리고 잇섯슬 때 거기서 우연히도 예사롭지안은 광경을 보게 되엿다.
「그래 메누리 자식은 자식이 안이란 말이여요. 왜 내 신세를 이러케 망처노코는 영 내몰내라는 게야요. 모르겟서요. 결말을 지여주고 가세요.」
며누리인 듯한 신녀성은 손수건으로 연방 눈물을 씨스면서 구두신은 발을 토당토당 굴너가며 엽헤 사람이 잇거나 말거나 조곰도 거리낌업시 놉흔 소리로 엽헤 망연히 안져잇는 검으테테하고 비대하게 생긴 나히 60이나 되여 보이는 로인을 (아마 시아비겟지) 노려보고 호령인지 애소인지를 하는 것이엿다. 대합실에 모여 안즌 사람들의 시선과 흥미는 삽시간에 이 늙은이와  젊은 녀자에게로 몰렷다.
로인은 장죽에서 담배를 갓가수로 빨면서 창피해 못견듸겟다는 듯이 간간히 헷기침도 해보앗다가 짜증도 내여보앗다가 가만한 말로 달내도 보앗다가 안질부질을 못하는 모양이엿다. 그들의 문답안이 그 젊은 신식 며누리의 기탄업는 하소연은 연해 대합인의 흥미를 끄을면서 한참동안이나 게속이 되엿다. 나는 속으로 그 녀자의 당한 사정과 소위 운명이라는 것을 가만히 상상해 보면서 구경만 하고 안저 잇다가 문득 직업상(긔자로써) 제륙감이 동하야 그 녀자를 붓들고 자세한 사정내용을 캐보고 십흔 생각이 낫다. 그리하야 며누리가 로인을 따라가면 엇저나 하는 불안이 적지 안엇스나 다행이 로인만 남행차로 떠나고 울든 며누리는 혼자 뒤떠러젓다.
이러 경우에 발서 자기 처지를 어느 정도까지 제입으로 중인의 압헤 발설해버린 이상 그 녀자에게 접근하기는 용이한 일이다. 우리는 밤차로 떠나기로 하고 누의에게 몃가지 주의를 식여서 그 녀자 압해 나를 대신하야 내세웟다. 누의는 상양하게 내뜻을 바다 그 녀자 압흐로 나아갓다. 인사를 하고 무에라 무에라 몃 마듸 주고 밧고 하더니 주저하는 그 녀자를 잇굴고 누이가 이층식당으로 올라가는 걸 보고 나는 대합실에 도라와서 하회를 기다리고 잇섯든 것이다. 이 다음에 적는 것은 누이가 그에게서 들은 이야기다. 그는 성격이 퍽 활발한<133> 녀자이드란다.
「물론 련애 결혼입니다. 그는 밀양 사람이고 나는 대구인대 서울서 공부하는 동안에 엇더케 알게되엿지요. 그래서 그는 자기집에서 날ㅅ자까지 밧다십히 하야 정해노은 혼처를 물리치고 여러 부형의 반대속에서 서울서 간략하게 결혼식을 하엿담니다.
그런 뒤로는 우리집이 곤란하고 남편되는 사람 역시 학생의 몸으로 두 사람의 생활이 지탕해 갈 수가 업슴으로 할 수 업시 우리 두 사람이 의론한 결과 싀집에 드러가서 그가 학교를 졸업하고 독립 생활을 하게되고 살림을 날 때까지 싀집사리를 하기로 햇든 것임니다. 남편의 집은 밀양서 부자요 양반이엿습니다. 시집에는 시부모가 잇고 싀할머니 내외분까지 7旬이 넘는 로인이 잇서 싀숙이며 올케며 하야 집안이 여간 번다치가 안엇슴니다. 마당에서 돌 한아가  떠러저도 싀할아버지 이하 싀동생 하인들까지 죄다 알게되고 바람에 솔방울 한 개 구르는 것도 집안 사람 몰래는 구르지 못하는 무서운 경게와 감시하에 하로 하로 의 서투른 살림이 반복되여 갓슴니다. 처음에는 싀어머니께서는 나를 퍽 사랑하는 것 가탓슴니다.
「학생 며누리도 괜찬트라. 양 갈보갓기는 해도 부모위하는 게 제법이거든 행실도 잇서.」
이웃집 일가 할머니가 왓슬 때 신식며누리 칭찬이라고 이런 말하는 것을 멧 번들은 일이 잇습니다.
처음에는 나도 멋도 모르고 모든 우수운 일 비위틀리는 일 아니꼬운 일 성가신 일을 꾹 참고 무조건으로 복종을 하엿습니다. 절에 간 색시처럼 바보가 되고 등신이 되여 올케가 뭐라하면 뭐라는 대로 맛동세가 선참자(先參者)의 권위를 가지고 험란을 빼물고 이것은 엇덧코 저것은 엇덧코 하면서 느러놋는 것도 달게 들엇슴니다. 그리하야 나는 맛동세가 하는 일을 마터 조석으로 싀어머니와 싀아버니 밥상을 들게 되엿슴니다.
하인이 잇서도 밥상만은 며누리 손으로 밧는 것이 그들의 락이요 체면이겟슴니다. 그러나 그것은 나에게 잇서서 여간 큰 고역이 안이엿슴니다. 밥상을 조심조심 들고 드러가서 식사를 다 마칠 때까지 그 압헤 꿀어 안젓다가 숭융을 올닌 다음에야 다시 밥상을 들고 뒷거름질 처서 공손히 들고 나와서야 간신히 피곤한 손으로 숫갈을 들고 안즈면 한숨이 저절로 나왓슴니다. 내가 무엇때문에 다시 옛날 도덕의 굴레속에 사로잡혀서 노예 생활을 해야 하는냐 하는 반항심이 날로 복바처 올라오는 것을 엇지할 수 업섯슴니다. 그러한 마음이 내 행동거지에 나타나지 안을 도리가 잇섯겟슴니까. 그럴사록 싀부모의 눈치는 달라젓슴니다. 처음부터 만흔 후회와 볼안을 가지고도 자식의 하는 일이라 전연 거절하지는 못하고 들여노앗슬 터인데 그것이 차차 신녀성 행세를 시작하니 발서 눈밧게 난 것은 사실임니다. 경멸과 증오 험점을 차저내고자 하는 악의적 감시가 싀부모 맛동세 올케녀 하인들까지 왼집안 사람의 눈을 통하야 내 신변에 흐터젓슴니다. 나는 고립무원한 처지에 빠진 셈이엿슴니다. 그러나 그럴사록 내가 그들을 경멸하는 정도도 심하여 젓슴니다.
「네까진 것들이 다 뭐냐. 시시하다. 저것들이 다 사람인가.」이러고는 혼자 어지러운 내 마음을 스스로 위로하엿슴니다. 나의게는 오직 남편이라는 천만군의 힘다운 응원이 잇섯기 때문이외다. 그 따위 멸망해 가는 인간들이야 千萬사람이 다 원수가 된대도 무서울 것이 업섯슴니다.
