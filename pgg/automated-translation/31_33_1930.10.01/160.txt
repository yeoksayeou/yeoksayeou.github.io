기사제목	現實에서 빗처본 中等敎育 改良方針 問題 (The Problem of Improvement Plan for Secondary Education Viewed from Reality)
기사형태	설문 (Survey)

The Problem of Improvement Plan for Secondary Education Viewed from Reality
1. The Problem of Adding Practical Extracurricular Time
2. The Problem of School Excursions
3. The Problem of Opening School Buildings

The current higher common schools (高等普通學校) in Korea have, in fact, become institutions for preparing for entrance to higher-level schools. The general parents recognize it as such, and the students also take it for granted, and the teachers or authorities also show an attitude of recognizing it as such unconsciously without any other thoughts or doubts on this point. However, looking at the number of students who go on to higher-level schools after graduating from higher common schools, only a small number of 20 to 30 percent of the total go on, and we are well aware of the phenomenon that the remaining majority either stay in rural villages in frustration or mix with the unemployed in the city and lead a miserable life of the lumpen intelligentsia. However, here is a problem that we must consider: the majority of higher common school graduates who cannot go on to higher-level schools and become a group of dropouts (they think of themselves that way when they cannot go to higher-level schools or get a job) only then realize their ignorance keenly. The knowledge they have learned is not specialized knowledge, so they cannot become technicians, and the entrance preparation education they have received formally and mechanically, away from the actual circumstances of society, is of no use in real society. There was no reason to welcome ignorant backroom gentlemen who had no money or knowledge of the situation. Here, the energy and tension they had spent five years studying hard are released, and their ambition for a bright future is broken, and they become disappointed people who cry in the sorrow of disillusionment.

How can we disregard and overlook this problem? Therefore, we must change the educational policy to the extent possible, even if passively, to eliminate and correct such harms, so that the higher common schools, which have a significant role in Korea in many ways, can become true educational institutions that do not deviate from our lives and expectations, and to prevent the majority of intelligent people who will come out into our society in the future and fulfill a great mission from falling into frustration and pessimism. In this sense, we visited each secondary school principal along with a few other issues and listened to their countermeasures and opinions. And even if this time is limited to raising the issue, we believe that the actual effect will be revealed as social interest in this matter increases several times. -Editor-

Fundamental Treatment is Necessary

Jeong Dae-hyeon, Principal of Boseong Higher Common School (普成高普校長 鄭大鉉)

It is a fact that the higher common schools in modern Korea are exactly the same as Japanese middle schools, and they are in a situation similar to that of a preparatory school for higher-level schools. In the early Meiji Restoration (明治維新) in Japan, there was a need to rapidly import Western civilization and great efforts were made to develop a new culture, so the secondary school education system was established according to that purpose, and as a result, it is causing considerable harm today. Moreover, today, general society <25> evaluates people based on a single diploma and treats them differently, so parents or students who send their children to secondary schools have no time to look back at their own situation and circumstances and inevitably aim for the highest institution of learning.

As the social system is like this and the educational system cannot deviate from that scope, school authorities have no choice but to respond to the demands of parents and students like riding a tiger, even knowing the flaws and dissatisfaction. For example, even if the school adds extracurricular time to teach practical courses or invites famous figures from society once a week to listen to lectures on the realities of society, students or the majority of parents will not have any interest in it, and they know very well that it will not produce that much result.

That is because almost all of us are aiming for university until the graduation period is approaching and the path after graduation is definitively decided, so it is inevitable that we are indifferent to subjects that are not directly related to entrance preparation, and also because we think of not being able to go to higher-level schools due to our economic circumstances as a kind of shame, so even after the graduation period, students who cannot go to university and go to vocational schools or return home are full of frustration and only secretly confess their situation to the teacher in charge, and this is by no means their fault. The fundamental problem lies in the defects of the social system.

Therefore, I think that no matter what passive improvement measures are taken under this social system and this education ordinance, they will ultimately not produce any practical effect.

2. Regarding school excursions, I believe that it will be more effective to choose the destination well and go with the teacher leading, as in the traditional way, in terms of the meaning of obtaining group training and the point of experiencing friendly feelings between students and teachers. And it seems that there is nothing more deeply impressed in student life than school excursions. <26>
If, on the other hand, students are given freedom to travel arbitrarily for a certain period of time, not only will many people not go because they are not interested, but most of them will either return to their hometowns and laze around or waste money on uncontrolled entertainment. It is a fact that middle school students do not yet have the passion or cultivation to have a deep interest in researching the historical sites or customs of a particular region in a specialized way.

3. It is good to open the school building socially. We do not have suitable buildings to carry out various social education projects. In this situation, since the purpose of the school itself is different, there is no way to do anything during ordinary times, but during winter or summer vacations when the school building is empty, it can be used for such social projects as much as possible. We are willing to provide it at any time if it is a socially meaningful project. It would be a bit different if we were to leave a large house unoccupied, but there is no reason not to use this large building for good work. However, we regret that although our school has used it as a venue for workshops related to the school several times, it has not yet been used for the sincere educational work of social people.

Social Circumstances are Conveyed During Teaching Time

Choe Gyu-dong, Principal of Jungdong School (中東學校長 崔奎東)

In the sense that middle school students cannot be completely unaware of social affairs because Korea is in a special situation, or to the extent of preparatory knowledge for them to become useful soldiers in the real society in the future, we are making efforts to let them know about the real social affairs we are facing in the geography, history, and ethics classes, and also to have them make simple comments on social issues that arise from time to time. <27> However, I do not know to what extent the effect has been achieved. I cannot estimate how much more remarkable the effect would be to add courses that are practically beneficial to social life or to invite social experts to listen to lectures on the realities of society, but since we are in a position where we are severely restricted in that regard, I only regret that we do not have the power to break the regulations and actively establish our beliefs. And the so-called higher common school education is treated differently from Japanese middle schools in light of the Korean situation, which means that practical courses such as agriculture, industry, and commerce are taught, but students' attention and interest in these subjects is extremely distant.

In this situation, if we focus on practical subjects again, it will only waste the students' energy and create dissatisfaction. Moreover, the original purpose of higher common schools is to cultivate the personality and character for students to develop their own path and become self-reliant in the future, and to teach them common sense, but today, due to the fierce survival competition, society values a diploma of authority rather than a person's personality, talent, and character, so higher common schools have become like preparatory institutions for higher-level schools, just like Japanese middle schools, and therefore, those who cannot go on to higher-level schools and fall into real society (the majority) are in fact more useless than graduates of other short-term vocational schools and are treated as a group of unemployed people who are worse than hermits, so it is inevitable that contradictions arise.

And what we keenly feel is that students these days lack sincerity. They have a kind of academic vanity of vainly longing for higher education without considering their parents' economic power, and when that is not realized, they often become frustrated and disappointed and join the ranks of the dropouts. There is no trust between teachers and students or between colleagues, and even when looking at the attitudes and movements of students towards social science research, which has been frequently discussed these days, they only have an extremely vague overview of the actual situation and problems of how exhausted the rural villages in Korea are, how severe the political oppression we are receiving is, and how miserable our lives are, and they tend to agree with a kind of popular psychology and make noise from the border line, satisfying themselves. By doing so, they seem like patriots and revolutionaries, but in fact, they are cowardly, lack trust, lack a lively spirit, and lack a sense of truth, so they do not seem like students of the old days at all. How can we not lament!

2. School excursions are something that middle school students cannot expect to gain more from, and from the point of view of group training or promoting intimacy between teachers and students, it is a very good thing. Also, when going to Gyeongju (新羅古都) to admire Seokguram (石窟庵) or Bulguksa (佛國寺), even if they do not go into deep historical research, how much of a deep impression and emotion it will give them to see and know that we also have great art worthy of boasting to the world.
For now, we have no choice but to have a suitable person lead and select a suitable place in the traditional form.

3. If the purpose of using the school building while students are resting does not greatly contradict the main purpose of the school and does not deviate from the regulations, we will always agree to use it for projects that are beneficial to society.
And if we do summer private lectures, I believe that even the school teachers will not hesitate to help as much as the situation allows.

To What Extent Do You Intend to Implement It?

Kim Dong-hyeok, Principal of Baejae Higher Common School (培材高普校 金東赫)

Regarding the fact that higher common schools cannot be used as institutions for preparing for entrance to higher-level schools, we have always been racking our brains as the direct party involved, and it is always discussed during staff meetings, but we have not yet come up with any specific countermeasures. We have many doubts about the effectiveness of adding more practical courses or adding extracurricular time to focus on that area, and in fact, the burden on students is too great, so we have not yet tried it except for what is stipulated, but in the past, senior classes have repeatedly requested to remove courses that are not directly necessary for entrance to higher-level schools and increase the number of necessary subjects, but the school authorities have said that it is absolutely impossible and have refused. However, our school is a Christian-related school, so it is very convenient to invite social figures to listen to lectures on various social issues. We have done that several times in the past, and we have received a lot of attention from the authorities, but it seems to be effective to some extent, so in the future, we plan to invite Shin Heung-u (申興雨), a Christian, to listen to lectures on social affairs or our rural situation during morning prayer time to avoid undue attention from the authorities for the time being.

2. School excursions can only be taken by middle school students in groups led by a teacher, and it is important to choose the destination well above all else. It is not bad to go to famous scenic spots such as Mt. Kumgang (金剛山) to admire the beautiful scenery, shake off the mundane dust with the mysterious and profound spirit, and cultivate the spiritual food of the mind and body by tasting reverent feelings and sacred emotions, but I want to bring out the effect of school excursions in the sense of guiding and instructing students' attention and interest to the aspects that are directly related to our real life, such as what the social situation will be when we accept them several times, and what the local customs and lifestyles are like. As a result of fully discussing this point, we have decided to set the destination for the 5th grade as the northwestern Gando (間島), which has a deep relationship with Koreans. When they see the miserable life of the wanderers scattered in the wide Manchurian fields, or when they see the bloody battles of the seniors who are constantly fighting against various hardships and dangers, they will deeply feel something tremendously different from the feeling of entertainment or sorrowful indignation they feel in front of scenic places or the ruins of dead historical sites. However, since lower grade students have less understanding and are younger, it would be better to limit them to outings, so our school only sends 5th graders on school excursions and instead takes the remaining lower grade students on outings.

3. How meaningful would it be to use the school building for socially beneficial projects during vacation time than leaving it empty? Even if we suffer some damage to goods by lending out the school building, it would be a very small sacrifice, wouldn't it? It would be good to use it for eradicating illiteracy or for summer lectures during the time the school building is empty.

I Hope for the Support of Social Volunteers

Yi Yun-ju, Principal of Hwimun Higher Common School (徽文高普校長 李潤柱)

I fully agree with the idea of making middle school students aware of real social affairs, eliminating the harm of making higher common schools like institutions for preparing for higher-level schools, adapting to the social situation in Korea, and creating schools that meet the true needs of the people, and I am always worried about it.
However, we, who are powerless, do not have the power to break through the strict restrictions and numerous restrictions assigned to us and establish new beliefs. I hope that general social volunteers who have sufficient understanding, sympathy, and concern about that point will lend us their strength, and I also hope that we will receive constant and passionate encouragement. Indeed, there is probably no one in a more difficult situation than educators in Korea. <31>
2. It would be good to continue school excursions in the traditional form. It will be a good opportunity for teachers and students to open their hearts to each other and have a pleasant gathering, and I believe that the impressions and gains from the trip will also be significant. Even when looking at the vivid travelogues of the students, such things are well revealed.
3. We cannot lend the school building as a meeting place. Aside from the fact that the purpose of using the school building is different, it is easy to become messy when lending it to the general public, and there is a risk that goods will be damaged, as well as the concern that it may be used for purposes that are greatly contrary to school education, so our school has decided not to lend the school building to others.

No Special Good Measures

Bak Seung-bin, President of Boseong College (普成專門學校長 朴勝彬)

I am aware that there are considerable flaws in the current higher common school education in Korea when viewed in light of the real situation in Korea, but I have not yet thought deeply about that point because I am not directly involved and I am preoccupied with my work. However, under this social system and this education ordinance, even if we take some passive palliative measures, the effect will not be very new.
2. School excursions can only be done in the traditional form for middle school students, and I think it would be good for teachers to first fully research and make efforts on the choice of destination and the points that will attract the students' attention when they go to that place.
3. It is certainly meaningful and we also agree to provide the school building while it is not used for social projects, but <32> the circumstances are different for each school, even if they are the same school. Our school has never been asked to borrow it, perhaps because our school building is not very good.

Teach Industrial Arts Materials

Kim Ryeo-sik, Principal of Hyeopseong Industrial School (協成實業學校長 金麗植)

The main reason why I changed the higher common school course to the industrial school system after taking charge of this school was that I keenly realized that the higher common schools in Korea are too far from our real situation. Graduates of industrial schools still have some skills and simple knowledge to make a social living, so they are in a considerably more advantageous position in social life than graduates of higher common schools who have received education purely focused on preparing for entrance to higher-level schools. Therefore, I may be just engaging in armchair theory alone, but I wonder if it would be better to teach industrial courses appropriately to the extent that there is no major obstacle to entrance to higher-level schools (currently, graduates of industrial schools can also enter higher-level schools). To be more specific, I wonder if it would be better to classify students who will go into real society and students who will go to higher-level schools from the 3rd year of higher common school and teach them in a format similar to specialized courses. Of course, the system and regulations are the system and regulations, but the self-awareness of parents and students is also necessary. It is only in vain that we aim for and long for higher-level schools with a kind of academic vanity without considering our own economic situation or talent, and in the end, only more frustrated young people will increase. Therefore, we must make higher common schools a complete educational institution in Korea. At least, higher common schools these days are institutions that are too far from our lives and environment and have little connection to them.

2. I am absolutely opposed to school excursions. However, even in our school, I am sending senior students to the Dalian area every year because I cannot overcome the precedent and the consensus of other teachers, but I am secretly opposed to it. I highly doubt how much effect that trip, which wastes so much money and time and forcibly collects travel expenses from students as if it were a subject, regardless of individual intentions or needs, only in the name of the group, has. Moreover, the attitude of students during school excursions is extremely lax, and after returning from school excursions, they are aimlessly excited, neglect their studies, and I have seen many students who ultimately produce bad results. Therefore, I believe that it is a much more advantageous method to go on outings to nearby suburbs according to the season, whether it is from the point of view of the harmonious gathering, the point of refreshing the mood, or the cost relationship.

3. It seems that the general public dislikes lending school buildings to other people or organizations because of various liability issues that arise. In fact, those in charge often have very difficult situations. However, it would be a great loss, especially in the impoverished Korean society, to leave large buildings such as school buildings idle. Therefore, I think it is most appropriate for the school authorities to directly manage social projects to the extent that the school can do it with more sincerity. If we first run projects such as night schools or workshops directly from the school to the best of our ability, the liability issues regarding the school building will disappear, and the effect will be much better. <33>

잡지명	별건곤 제33호
발행일	1930년 10월 01일
기사제목	現實에서 빗처본 中等敎育 改良方針 問題
기사형태	설문

現實에서 빗처본 中等敎育 改良方針 問題
1, 實益的課外時間附加 問題
2, 修學旅行 問題
3, 校舍解放 問題

現下 朝鮮에 잇는 高等普通學校는 事實上 上級學校 入學準備過程의 機關가티 되어 잇다. 一般 學父兄들이 그러케 認定하고 學生들이 또한 그것을 當然하게 생각하며 敎員이나 當局者들이 亦 然 이點에 대하야 別다른 思慮와 疑念이 업시 意識無意識間에 그런한 機關으로 自認하고 나아가는 態度를 보히고 잇다. 그런데 高等普校 卒業生으로 上級學校에 나아가는 學生數를 보면 全數의 2割 乃至 3割의 少數에 지나지 못하고 남어지 太半數는 失意하야 農村에 蟄伏하거나 그럿치 안으면 都市의 失業群에 석기여 룬펜 인태리의 慘憺한 生活을 하게 되는 現象임을 우리는 잘 알고 잇다. 그런데 여긔 우리가 생각하지 안으면 안될 問題로 上級學校로 나아가지 못하고 一群의 落伍者(上級學校에도 못가고 就職도 못할 때 스사로 그럿케 생각한다.)가 되여 實社會로 훗터지는 大多數의 高普卒業生들은 그 때야 비로소 自己네들의 無智를 痛切히 깨닷게 된다는 것이다. 배운 바 學識은 專門智識이 못되매 아무런 技術者도 될 수가 업슬뿐 안이라 全然<24> 社會의 實事情을 떠나 公式的 機械的으로 바더온 入學準備敎育은 實社會에서는 何等의 所用處가 업섯다. 돈업고 事情 몰으고 無識한 뒷방 서방님들을 歡待해 줄 까닭은 업섯다. 여긔서 그들은 5個星霜을 刻苦勉勵해온 精力과 緊張이 풀니고 靑雲의 志도 꺽기어 새삼스래 幻滅의 悲哀에 우는 失意人이 될 뿐이다.
이것이 엇지 우리가 等閑視하며 看過할 問題이랴. 그리하매 吾人은 비록 消極的이나마 可能한 程度에서 敎育方針을 變改하야 그리한 弊害를 除外匡正하여서 여러가지 意味로 조선에는
重大한 役割을 가지고 잇는 高普校로 하야금 우리의 生活과 企待에 버서나지 안은 참된 敎育機關이 되게 하며 將次 우리 社會에 나와서 크나큰 使命을 다할 多數한 聰俊를 失意와 悲觀에 빠지지 안케 하여야 할 것이다. 이러한 意味에서 우리는 다른 두어가지 問題와 아울러 各中等學校長을 歷訪하고 거긔대한 對策과 意見을 들어 본 것이다. 그러고 이번은 비록 問題의 提唱에 긋친다 하드래도 數次 여긔대한 關心이 社會的으로 擴大될사록 實際的效果가 들어날 것을 밋고 오로지 徒勞가 안임을 깃버한다.-編輯者-

根本的療治가 必要

普成高普校長 鄭大鉉

現代 朝鮮의 高等普通學校가 日本 中學校와 꼭 가티 되여 잇는데 上級學校의 準備學校와 가튼 處地에 빠저잇는 것은 事實이다. 日本은 明治維新 初에 西歐의 文明을 急遽히 輸入할 必要가 잇섯스며 또한 新文化啓發에 多大한 努力을 햇기 때문에 中等學校敎育制度가튼 것도 그 目的에 따라서 制定된 것이엿슴으로 오늘날에 와서는 적지 안흔 弊害를 낫코 잇는 것이다. 더구나 오늘날에 와서 一般社會는<25> 한張의 卒業狀으로서 人品을 評定하고 待遇와 取扱을 달니하게 되니 中等學校에 子弟를 보내는 學父兄이나 學生들이 自己의 處地와 事情을 돌아볼 餘暇도 업시 最高學府를 目的하고 나아가게 되는 거은 또한 避치 못할 情勢라 하겟다.
이와 가티 社會制度가 그러하고 敎育制度가 또한 그 範圍를 버서나지 못하매 學校當局者로서는 그 缺陷과 不滿을 알면서도 騎虎之勢로 學父兄과 學生들의 要求에 應從할 수 밧게 업는 것이다. 假令 學校에서 課外時間을 附加하야 實益過程을 敎授한다든지 一週日에 一次式社會의 名士를 招聘하야 社會의 實情에 대한 講義를 들여준다 하드래도 학생들이나 大多數의 學父兄들은 아무런 興味도 가지지 안을 것이며 그러니만치 그만한 實積을 낫하 내이지 못할 것을 잘 알고 잇다.
그것은 現在 우리들의 거이 全部가 다 卒業期가 迫到하야 卒業後의 進就가 決定的으로 決定되기까지는 無非大學을 目的하고 잇는 터인즉 직접 入學準備課題가 아인 學課에 대해서 冷膽한 것은 엇절 수 업는 일이며 또한 自己의 經濟形便이 許諾지 안이하야 上級學校로 나아가지 못하는 것을 一種의 羞恥로 생각하는 고로 卒業期에 이르러 그 去就가 作定이 된 뒤라도 大學으로 가지 못하고 專門學校나 혹은 歸鄕하는 學生들의 失意滿滿하야 겨우 擔任先生에게 內密히 自己의 事情을 고백하는 터이니 이것은 결코 그들의 過責이 안이다. 根本問題는 社會制度의 缺陷에 잇는 것이다.
그럼으로 이 社會制度와 이 敎育令아래에서는 엇더한 消極的 改善方策을 세운다 하더래도 結局 아무런 實際的 效果를 낫하 내이지 못할 것이라고 생각한다.

2, 修學旅行에 대하여서는 다른 方式보다는 從來의 方式대로 目的地를 잘 選擇하야서 敎師가 引率하고 가는 것이 團體的 訓練을 엇는 意味로나 生徒互相間에나 師弟間에 親和한 情을  맛볼 수 잇는 點을 보아 效果가 만흘 줄 밋는다. 그리고 學生生活 가운데 修學旅行가티 깁히 印象에 남는 것은 업는 모양이다.<26>
만일 그러치 안코 學生 個個人에게 自由를 주어 어느 時日동안 任意로 旅行을 하게 하고 보면 첫재 興味가 업서 하야 가지 안을 사람도 만흘 뿐 안니라 大部分은 故鄕에나 돌아가서 閒遊하던지 無節制한 遊興에 金錢을 浪費하는 結果밧게는 더 되지 못할 것이다. 中等5年의 程度로서는 아즉도 專門的으로 한 地方의 史蹟이나 人情風俗에 깁흔 興味를 가지고 硏究해 보고자 하는 熱誠도 素養도 가지지 못한 것이 事實이다.

3, 校舍를 社會的으로 解放하라는 것은 조흔 말이다. 우리는 여러가지 社會的 敎化事業을 해나가는데 적당한 建物을 가지지 못하엿다. 이러한 처지에 잇서 學校自體의 目的이 다른이 만치 普通때는 엇지할 道理가 업다 하드래도 冬期나 夏期休暇가튼 때 校舍가 비여잇는 동안에는 얼마든지 그러한 社會的 事業에 利用할 수 잇슬 것이다. 우리는 社會的 意義잇는 事業이라 하면 어느 때든지 欣然히 提供할 생각이다. 曠安宅而不居라면 좀 意味가 다르지만 이 가티 宏大한 建物을 두고 조흔 일에 利用하지 안을 까닭은 업다. 그러나 우리 學校에서도 몃 번 學校關係의 講習會場으로 利用한 일이 잇슬 뿐 아직껏 社會人들의 誠意잇는 敎化事業에 利用된 일이 업슴을 오히려 遺憾으로 생각하는 바이다.

2, 修學旅行은 中學生으로써 더 만흔 收穫을 바랄 수 업는 것이고 團體的 訓練이라든지 師弟間이나 生徒互相間의 親密을 圖하는 점으로 보아 대단히 조흔 일이고 또 가령 新羅古都에 가서 石窟庵이나 佛國寺를 求景할 때 그리 깁흔 史的深究에까지 들어가지 안트라도 우리들도 세계에 足히 자랑할만한 偉大한 藝術을 가젓섯다는 것만이라도 눈 압헤 보이고 알여 준다는 것이 얼마나 그들에게 깁흔 印象과 感銘을 줄 것인지 몰으는 것이다.
아즉은 從來의 形式대로 適任者가 引率하고 適當한 곳을 選定하야 가는 수 밧게 업겟다.

3, 校舍가튼 것을 學生들이 쉬는 동안이면 그 利用하는 目的이 學校의 主旨에 크게 背馳되지 안코 規定에 버서나지 안는以上 언제든지 社會에 有益한 事業을 위하야 利用하겟다면 언제든지 應諾할 것이다.

實業敎材를 敎授하라

協成實業學校長 金麗植

내가 이 學校일을 맛터 보게 된 뒤로 高等普通學校發程을 變改하야 實業學校制로 한 것도 그 主要目的이 朝鮮의 高等普通學校가 우리들의 現實情과는 넘우도 距離가 멀다는 것을 切實히 깨다른 까닭임니다. 實業學校卒業生들은 그래도 社會生活을 營爲하는데 多少의 技術과 簡單한 智識이 잇슴으로 純全히 上級學校入學準備에 注力한 敎育을 밧고 나온 高普出身보다는 社會生活에 잇서 적지안이 有利한 處地인 것은 事實임니다. 그럼으로 나는 혼자 卓上空論에 지나지 못할는지 몰으지만 上級學校入學에 큰 拘碍가 업는 限에서(現在 實業學校出身도 上級學校에 入學이 된다.) 實業課程을 適當하게 課하는 것이 엇덜가 한다. 더 具體的으로 한다면 高等普通學校 3年後부터라도 實社會로 나아 갈 學生과 上級學校에 갈 學生들을 分類하야서 專門科와 가튼 形式으로 工夫를 식혓스면 엇덜가 한다. 그것은 勿論 制度와 法規도 法規려니와 學父兄이나 學生들의 自覺이 必要한 것이다. 自己의 經濟形便이라든가 才質을 돌보지 안코 부질업시 一種의 向學的 虛榮心으로 上級學校만을 目標하며 憧憬하고 잇서서는 나종 失意의 靑年만 더 늘어갈 뿐이다. 그리하야 高等普通學校를 完全한 朝鮮의 敎育機關으로 만들어야할 것이다. 적어도 요사이 高等普通學校는 우리들의 生活과 環境에서는 넘우나 距離가 멀고 緣分이 적은 機關인 까닭이다.

2, 修學旅行에는 나는 絶對로 反對이다. 그러나 우리 學校에 잇서서도 前例와 다른 敎員들의 衆議에 못닉여 할 수 업시 高級學年學生들을 年年히 大連方面으로 보내고 잇기는 하나 속으로는 反對하고 잇다. 그와 가티 多大한 費用과 時間을 虛費하야 强制로 하다십히 學生들에게 修業科나 맛찬가지로 旅行費를 徵收하여 個個人의 意思나 要求에 버서나 오즉 團體의 名目으로서 恒例的으로 하는 그 旅行이 얼마만한 效果를 가저 오는지가 퍽 疑問이다. 그리고 더구나 學生들의 修學旅行때 가지는 態度란 극히 放漫的이여서 修學旅行을 단여온 뒤에는 空然히 마음들이 들떠서 學業을 怠慢히 하고 氣分이 解弛되여 結局 좃지 못한 結果를 내는 學生을 만히 보앗다. 그럼으로 나는 이러한 修學旅行보다는 철을 따라 갓가운 郊外에 遠足을 가는 것이 그 團欒한 편으로 본다든지 氣分이 淸快해지는 점으로 본다든지 費用關係라든지 모든 점으로 보아 훨신 有利한 方法인 줄 밋는다.

3, 校舍는 다른 사람이나 團體에 빌여주고 보면 여러 가지 責任問題가 생기는 까닭에 一般이 실혀하는 모양이다. 事實 局에 當한 사람으로는 處地가 매우 困難한 때가 만타. 그러나 學校校舍와 가치 宏大한 建物을 그냥 놀여 둔다는 것은 貧弱한 朝鮮社會에서는 더구나 큰 損失일 것이다. 그럼으로 좀더 誠意잇게 學校로서 할 수 잇는 程度의 社會事業이라면 學校當局에서 直接으로 經營하는 것이 가장 合當한 일인 줄 생각한다. 夜學이라든지 講習會가튼 事業부터 힘자라는 데까지 爲先 學校에서 直營해 본다면 校舍에 대한 責任問題도 업서질 것이며 效果도 훨신 優秀할 것이다.<33>
