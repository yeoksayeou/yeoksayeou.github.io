기사제목	各學校體操先生 夫人訪問記 (Visits with the Wives of Physical Education Teachers at Various Schools)
기사형태	소식 (News)

Visits with the Wives of Physical Education Teachers at Various Schools

Mrs. Ch’oe Yu-dong of Chungdong School, Yi Yŏng-gi, a woman of the wise mother and good wife type
What shall I compare her to, but a cloudless sky? A person who comes down to the yard without hesitation, with a joyful demeanor that shows no hint of worry, saying, "Who is it? To this humble place!" How graceful she looks! Her face, with its clean complexion and rosy cheeks, seems to boast of the health of youth! One could easily sense that she enjoys the happiness of family life.

She got married at the age of 19 (Mr. Ch’oe was 21). Before the reporter could string together a long sentence, the conversation with her flowed with a familiar and affectionate flavor.

"It seems the students think of my husband as very strict, but conversely, he is unusually gentle at home. My nine-year-old daughter counts down the time for her father to come home as soon as she returns from school. Sometimes, she can’t stand it and runs to Chungdong School. Every time he has to go out at night, she invariably cries, saying, 'How can I sleep alone without Father?'" Every word of the wife sounded pleasant.

Since her husband returned from Japan, he has been in charge of both the Judo Club and the Tennis Club, so he works so hard day and night that he is completely oblivious to household matters, and he doesn't even have time to read at home. However, his wife handles all the market shopping and bargaining at fabric stores with her monthly allowance. It seems that his wife takes a lot of weight off of her husband's shoulders.

Even though they are always busy with matters inside and outside the house, they take the time to prepare lunch boxes at least once a week and go on outings to the mountains, fields, the Han River, or the theater with all the children. To maintain such a relaxed lifestyle, the husband and wife are always researching new and easy ways to manage their lives. The husband often wears a simple tracksuit with a raincoat to school, saying, "This is fine—it's simple and good!" and when setting the table, they make sure to have a couple of nutritious dishes.

In the end, the reporter was surprised when he was handed the wife's name written in her own hand. How wonderful the Chinese characters looked!

Mrs. Han Chin-hŭi of Posŏng School, Yun Kye-ŭn, an enlightened mistress of the house
If you say Kangmugwan (講武舘) in Nakwŏn-dong, everyone knows it’s a judo hall, and anyone interested in exercise knows that the person who goes to this judo hall is the physical education teacher at Posŏng School. Right next to the judo hall, busy managing the household and taking care of the guests who flock to the judo hall, the teacher's wife, with her neatly styled hair and refreshing tone, was a vibrant and modern woman.

The husband gets up early in the morning and usually goes for a run around the city, and on Sundays, he goes on picnics with the children. On days when he is too tired or when it is rainy, he usually reads at home, while the wife devotes herself to domestic affairs, saying that she never goes out. However, she makes time to read newspapers and magazines at night.

As the reporter listened attentively to the stories, one thing stood out as particularly interesting.

"The children take after their father and love exercise. I think it's because they constantly hear and see it. Of course, the child who goes to Posŏng School is like that, but even the little ones imitate judo moves and sometimes fall down, but even then, they manage to mimic it quite well! 'Are you talking about me?' I can't do exercise, but when I see the teachers and judo youths vigorously doing their thing, I sometimes want to imitate them. But where would I find the courage? Sometimes, something funny happens. When my husband jokingly pretends to attack me with a judo move, I can't move a muscle and tremble all over. Women also need to learn judo as an emergency measure." The wife's tone was very amusing.

The husband and children wear Western-style clothes, but on top of that, they wear plain clothes as much as possible, and they only eat three meals a day without any snacks. However, after exercising, they always drink a lot of sugar water, and the children, following their father, think they can exercise well just by drinking sugar water, so they constantly beg for it, which annoys the wife a lot. The wife's face showed a humorous smile. (She married Mr. Han at 24, she was 22).

Mrs. Yi Hŭi-sang of the Women's Commercial School, Ch’oe, flawlessly handling domestic affairs
She spends the whole day sewing and mending the torn clothes and socks of her six sons, and she plans the day's work with purpose. She says that she hardly has time to sleep, except when she is sick.

Her eldest son, who started attending Hwimun School this year, is enthusiastic about the parallel bars, and the younger children are engrossed in rough play, so taking care of them is a lot of work for the wife. Of course, the husband is always busy because he is also in charge of Chongsin School, so he only has time to sleep while he is at home. Therefore, the wife takes care of everything, both difficult and easy.

"What fun can there be for someone who lives such a hard life like me?" The wife's weary voice had a lively and welcoming tone, however. "But my husband is very easygoing, but when he comes home after having some drinks, it's what you might call a happy family. He not only brings sweets or fruit to make the children happy, but he also says all sorts of things. Even things he doesn't usually say to me—'We have to work hard to raise these children well, don't we?' Those words are a great comfort to me in my hardship."

As the reporter listened to these meaningful words, a profound thought came to mind, and he wished the wife health and happiness. (The wife was 18, Mr. Yi was 21 when they married).

Mrs. Sŏ Sang-ch’ŏn of Hwimun School, Mrs. X
The reporter went far beyond Hwadong Pass and quietly looked at a house with a parallel bar in the middle of a large and scenic yard.

The gentle woman who was spreading red peppers in the yard seemed very weak, but she was the wife of the famous Mr. Sŏ, who was known for his health and strength.

When the husband returns from school in the evening, he gathers workers who are exhausted from irregular lives and those who suffer from poor health and leads them in physical training and mental exercises, including parallel bars and various other exercises. Among them, there are students who are particularly interested in exercise, and even their five-year-old child joins in boldly.

In the summer, the husband and wife usually take the children to the Han River, and the cute five-year-old son insists on going by bicycle, and usually, the father walks while the younger children ride bicycles.

The wife told the story with a joyful tone, saying that the child seems to be following in his father's footsteps, whether he is climbing to the top of a tall tree in an instant, playing in the river, or doing club exercises.

The reporter could see the wife's clear understanding of exercise, as she not only taught her young children sternly but also paid attention to every detail when asking them to pick up something or do something in their daily lives, and praised them with a joyful expression after they boldly did as they were told.

"I was originally very sickly, but after following my husband's teachings and trying to live a regular life as much as possible, I have recently gained some strength. I still look very weak on the outside," she said, showing a glimpse of great success. One could see that she was the wife of a man who was a strongman (力士) but didn't act like one, a big drinker (大飮家) but didn't act like one, and who read books on physical education in his spare time.

Mrs. Kim Kŭn-myŏng of Paehwa School, Pak Kŭm-bong, a modern housewife with many hobbies
"Since you asked me to talk about our family life, let me first tell you about what my husband likes. I think I'm living with him because I'm starting to like what he likes more and more.

My husband always takes the young child in his arms and goes for a walk early in the morning or after dinner. He always asks me to go with him. We always go to Sajik Park (社稷公園), which is the closest and best place to play. We frequent it as if it were our backyard. Sometimes we take longer walks to theaters, concerts, or outside the city. On Sundays, many students visit, so we go to church together in the morning and then he spends the day reading magazines. My husband always cherishes music more than exercise. His favorite is Paolo Tosti. He also plays the cornet and pump organ well. Even when I'm busy with housework, he asks me to play the pump organ, so I can't even wipe my wet hands and I have to play it. During the short time he comes home for lunch, we sometimes have small concerts and sports days at our house.

My husband especially wants the house to be clean and tidy. He's too meticulous. So even though we have a simple household, I'm always struggling. I feel like I'm becoming more and more nervous. I'm sorry I can't tell you any special stories. Oh, dear."

Mrs. Pak Ch’ang-ha of Chungang School, Mrs. Cho, a benevolent lady
"Mr. Pak who lives below Ch'wihunjung (翠雲亭)!" When the students hear that, they always say, "Oh, the dance teacher!"

Anyone would wonder what kind of person the wife who lives with such a talented man is.

She is a very benevolent person. Even when her husband gets angry at home because something doesn't go his way, she listens silently. She always says, "If I had been born a modern woman, I would have learned to dance well and made my husband happy," and she always tells her daughter who goes to elementary school, "You should learn to dance well."

Just as her husband leads a cheerful life at school, he is also very active in doing housework and cleaning. He not only helps with household chores but also entertains guests and keeps the house tidy. Therefore, the wife is always busy trying to please her husband without any particular job. She truly lives a disciplined life. (The wife was 16, Mr. Pak was 14 when they married).

Mrs. Kim Su-gi of Paejae School, Ch’oe Yong-ae, a housewife skilled in household affairs
At first glance, the wife looked very gentle.

The fact that the household goods, which were not very large, were neatly arranged seemed to quietly reveal her gentleness. Furthermore, seeing how carefully she thought before speaking, one could see how cautious she was.

"Because I live with my mother-in-law, I can't go out even though I'm a modern person, more so than old-fashioned people. My husband travels far and wide, even at night..." The wife's words contained a hint of subtle dissatisfaction. "Now that you mention it, it's amazing. My husband came from Japan this spring and just started teaching, so he must be several times busier, but he didn't even take a single day off during summer vacation! He said he was going to Wŏnsan for swimming. And what's outside of Tongdaemun compared to outside of Sŏdaemun? And after coming from school, he goes to Kangmugwan (講武舘) in Nakwŏn-dong again to do judo, and he often comes back late."

"You mean at home? Even at home, he's very strict even though he's young. He doesn't joke at all. So it's very difficult. I try to anticipate his needs and prepare his favorite fruits and sweets, but I don't ask him every time."

While she was telling me the details, she added, "My mother-in-law and I are always at home physically, but our minds are always wandering around, being careful. Because my husband spends his time doing dangerous exercises, we are always worried, and we are also concerned about our daughter who goes to kindergarten... She is very mischievous..." Her words of worry still ring clearly in the reporter's ears.

Mrs. Chang Sŏk-lyun of Kyŏngsin School, Mrs. Chŏn
Before the reporter could even step into Paegokchŏm (百屋店) at the entrance of Susong-dong, a clerk asked, "What can I get for you?" The reporter suddenly felt sorry. "I'm here to see the teacher's wife—is she here?" The reporter's eyes glanced at a neatly decorated room in one corner of the store.

A woman who was originally elegant was busy combing her hair.

"Oh, I'm embarrassed. I just finished combing my hair," said the woman, who was indeed Mrs. Chang. "I thought I would open a small confectionery to earn some money and relieve boredom, but it hasn't been long, so it's not very interesting and I'm always busy. I was much more relaxed when I was teaching at school before. The children who go to school and the young ones at home all wear Western-style clothes like the teacher. I also have two people to help me with the housework, but I'm always rushed."

"Please, be comfortable. I'm sorry," said the reporter, and as if she truly heard the reporter's words, she hurriedly oiled her hair and neatly combed it back, continuing to speak.

"Oh, in the past, I used to go to Honmachi (本町) and night markets at night, and on Sundays, I would take my husband out for picnics, but since opening the store, I haven't been able to enjoy that. My husband also does most of his reading at school because it tends to be chaotic at home. When he comes home, he just plays happily with the children. The children follow their father and are afraid of me. That might be different from other families. Also, my husband reads the Tonga Ilbo (東亞日報), while I read the Osaka Mainichi (大阪每日)—that might be a little different, too... The Chosŏn Shinmun (朝鮮新聞) is too boring."

The wife's face showed a look of longing for the days when she lived with Mr. Chang in Japan. She seemed to be living a busy, fun, and peaceful life. The reporter gratefully accepted her kind words, "I'll see you again soon," and turned away. (She was 19, Mr. Chang was 27 when they married). (End)

各學校體操先生 夫人訪問記
中東學校 崔祐東氏夫人 李永琦, 賢母良妻타입이신 분
구름 한 점 업는 한울이라고 랄가. 근심의 빗치란 일호도 뵈올 수 업는 깃붐에 찬 태도로 『누구신지? 이 루추한 곳에!』 하시면서 뜰아래로 서슴서슴 나려오시는 분! 엇지도 어지러보히시는지! 깨끗한 바탕에 화긔가 도시는 얼골이 젊은이의 근강을 한껏 자랑하시는 듯! 가뎡생활의 행복함을 즐기시고 게신 것을 넉넉히 엿볼 수 잇섯다.
19세(崔氏 21세) 때 결혼하섯단다. 기자가 길다란 말 늘어노흘 사히도 업시 부인과의 담화는 어트듯 익숙하고 다정한 맛이 떠돌앗다.
『학생들사이에는 우리집 선생님이 대단히 엄하시다는 평판인 듯 하지만 그와 반대로 집에서는 유난하게 부드러우심니다. 9살 먹은 큰딸은 학교에 단녀오기만 하면 아바님 드러오실 시간만 손꼽고 안젓는 걸이요. 엇던 때면 고만 못 견대서 중동학교로 다라나버리는 걸이요. 늘 밤에 나가시게 될 적마다 「아바지 업시 엇더케 혼자 자-?」하면서 울기가 일수임니다.』하시는 부인의 말삼하시는 바 모도가 유쾌하게 들니엿다.
선생님이 日本에 갓다 오신 후부터는＜102＞ 柔道部와 庭球部까지 맛하보시게 되어서 주야 피곤해 애를 쓰시다십히 하심으로 집안 살님에는 전혀 캄캄하게 지낼 뿐 아니라 댁에서 한가히 독서하실 시간이란 조곰도 어드시지 못하실 형편이라 한다. 그러나 부인께서 한 달 용돈을 가지시고는 시장매매나 오복졈 흥정갓흔 것을 다-맛하본다는 말삼. 부인께서 선생님의 힘을 만히 더러 드리시는 모양이다.
이러케 두 분이 안밧일에 항상 다사하게 지내는 중에도 일주일에 적어도 한차레식은 변또를 귀려드시고 아해들과 총줄동을 해서 산에나 들노 혹은 한강 갓흔 곳과 극장 갓흔 곳으로 흥미 만히는 시간을 취하신다 한다. 그만큼 여유잇는 생활을 하시기 위하야 선생님과 부인께서는 늘 경편하게 살도리를 새로히 새로히 연구해 나가시게 하신다 한다. 선생님도 번쩍하시면 갓든하게 운동복우에 렌코-트 하나만 걸치고 이레도 괜찬어-간단하니 좃치 하시면서 등교할실 때가 만흐며 식사차리시는 것도 자양분 만은 음식을 두어가지씩 색가러 잡수실 뿐이라고 한다.
마즈막으로 긔자에게 써주시는 부인의 성함을 손에 쥐일 때 과연 놀난 바가 잇섯다. 한문으로 쓰신 글시가 엇지도 훌륭해 보엿는지.

普成學校 韓眞熙氏夫人 尹게은씨, 開化하신 안主人
樂園洞의 講武舘이라 하면 누구든 柔道場인줄 모를 사람이 업슬 것이고 또 이 유도장으로 나려가시는 분이 바로 보성학교 체조선생님이신 것도 왼만콤 운동에 유의하는 남녀는 다-짐작하고 잇다한다. 그런대 유도장 바로 엽채에서 집안 살님하시랴 날마다 뒤숭숭한 유도장으로 모혀드는 손님들의 모든 뒤치개질하시랴 항상 부즈런한 생활을 계속 하시고 게신 선생님 부인 언진 머리에 씩씩하게 차리신 그 모양과 시원스러운 말삼투가 어대로 뵙든지 활발하신 현대녀성의 긔분이 풍부한 분이엿다.
선생님은 새벽 일즉이 니러나셔서 대개 시내 일주를 하시며 또 일요일이면 아해들과 원족을 가시지만 너무 피로가 심하신 날이나 우긔가 잇는 날 갓흐면 댁에서 독서하시는 것이 보통이고 부인께서는 평생 나가시는 날이란 업다고 하실 만콤 안일에만 진력하신다 한다. 그러나 밤 갓흔 때 신문잡지쯤은 손에 드러볼 틈을 억지로라도 맨드러 보신다고-
긔자는 이야기 저 이야기 한참동안 정신 드려듯고 잇든 중에도 무엇보다도＜103＞ 흥미를 늣기게 된 말삼이 잇섯다.
『아해들가지 아바님을 달머서 운동이라면 조와서 펄펄 뛰지요. 시시로 듯고 배호고 하다 십히 하닛가 그런지요. 보성학교에 단니는 아해는 말할 것도 업지만 어린 아해들까지 유도하는 흉내를 내다가는 골코골코 하지요만은 골타가는 눈결에 그럴듯하게 흉내를 내고 말게되여요! 「나말삼이에요?」나는 운동 갓흔 것 하지는 못함니다만 학교여러분 선생님들께서나 혹은 유도군 청년들이 긔운차게 쿠드렁쿠드렁하는 양을 보고 섯스면 좀 흉내나마 내보고 십허질 때가 만슴니다. 그러나 어듸 용긔가 나야지요? 혹간 우수운 일이 잇서요. 선생님께서 작란삼아 유도법으로 별안간 넘겨버리실 듯이 내게 달녀들실 때 갓흔 때면 꿈적도 못하고 벌벌 떨 때가 다-잇서요. 녀자도 비상수단으로 쓸만한 유도쯤은 배화둘 필요가 잇계서요.』 부인의 말삼투가 대단히 자미잇게 들녓다.
