Walking the Quiet Night Streets
						Ha Yun

Walking the quiet night streets,
Though drunk, with cautious steps,
Walking the quiet night streets,
Lest a hand lying on a stone might be startled,
Though drunk, with cautious steps.

Autumn gone, winter approaching,
Where has the summer's bed gone?
Even the sound of footsteps is chilly this night,
A late-crying autumn bug is a sad friend,
May your weary dreams not be startled.

Tick-tock, the world's sound approaches,
Passing through the quiet night streets,
When will I come again if I pass this street?
This body has a long way to go,
Urging the path ahead without rest.

In winter, I must go to the riverbank,
The last thirty sights to see,
Longing in my heart but unable to go,
Can I abandon the path to stay?
Passing through the quiet night streets.
(1930.8.29) <150>
