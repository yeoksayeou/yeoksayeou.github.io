잡지명	別乾坤 第10號
발행일	1927년 12월 20일
기사제목	메리치잡이, 江原道 襄陽郡, 地方色 (Anchovy Catching, Gangwon Province Yangyang County, Local Color)
필자	襄陽 朴寬玉
기사형태	문예기타 (Miscellaneous Literature)

It's just the anchovy season, so I want to tell a story. Anchovies are abundant in Yangyang, Gangwon Province, along the coast, and I want to talk about what we do in our region.

It happened a few days ago. After eating breakfast, while I was playing in the yard, I heard an urgent sound from afar. I listened carefully and realized it was someone frantically shouting:

"Seine netters, come out, come out!"
"Seine netters, come out, come out!"

I am also one of the seine netters, so I thought, "Let's go out, let's go out!" At the order of the chief (長官), the seine netters all went to the seaside to load the seine net onto the boat. The commander was signaling to come out quickly by waving a blue flag towards the sea. Our men were loading the seine net while singing a boat song (欸乃聲).

"Seine netters, get your spirits up, aeng-e!"
"Fish are playing in the offshore waters, aeng-e!"

After loading all the seine nets, the commander said, "Row!" and we rowed, setting the net with one end on this side and the other on that side. The commander then waved a red flag towards the land again. It was a signal to pull the seine net quickly. The seine netters were dripping with sweat, pulling with all their might, and the seine net came out eight-tenths of the way to land. From the boat, a white flag was raised, meaning they had eaten the fish and their tails were long. The commander quickly waved the blue and red flags, ordering us to scoop the fish onto land. As soon as the order fell, we picked up the fish-scooping dippers and scooped, and another sound came out:

"With a rake, e-yang-e!"
"With a rake, e-yang-e!"

As we scooped, another strange thing happened. The men and women, young and old, of Andong Village (安洞里) came out to pick up fish with their wicker baskets. More than the men, the women, wearing only their underskirts (속고의), were very active picking up fish. As one of the seine netters, I tried to chase them away by throwing sand, but they wouldn't leave. Whether they got hit by sand or fell into the water, they didn't think about their lives and enjoyed picking up fish.

As if we had done something great, talk of food came out. The thought of makgeolli (막걸리, Korean rice wine) came to mind, and the sound of "eo-chu-eo, eo-chu-eo" was constantly heard from here and there.

The chief told us to bring a jug of makgeolli to drink if we were cold. We were happy, and getting a bowl of makgeolli with anchovy soup (멜치국) was truly a great pleasure.

Is this also a characteristic of our fishing village? <161>
