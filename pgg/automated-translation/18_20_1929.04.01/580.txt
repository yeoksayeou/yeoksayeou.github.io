필자	金秉濟 (Kim Pyŏng-je)
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

A Beggar Gathers 5,000 Won and Finds His Former Wife Who Remarried 10 Years Ago
						Kim Pyŏng-je
It happened more than 20 years ago. In Yŏnghŭng, near the coast, there lived a young couple named Kim Sang-nok (a pseudonym) and Ch'oe Pok-sun (a pseudonym) in a small, cramped mud hut where they spent their days. It was around the time of the Lunar New Year, and they were constantly worried about food and clothing, leaving them no peace of mind. On top of that, the debt collectors were relentlessly pressing them, causing them to lose weight and become anxious. Pok-sun, with a stomach empty for three days, lay down, worrying about their livelihood. Eventually, she fell into a troubled sleep. When she woke up, what was this? Her husband, who had been sleeping beside her, was gone. At first, she thought he had gone to relieve himself, but as an hour, then two, passed, and the day grew brighter, there was still no news. Then, fear suddenly arose. She worried that he might have been attacked by some animal and began searching everywhere. She looked in the back yard, front yard, outhouse, and stable, but could not find him anywhere. <168> Even if he had been dragged away by a tiger or wolf, there would be traces, but there were none. If he had been a performer of some sort, she would have thought he had gone to a performance, but he had nothing to do with such things. He didn't frequent bars or brothels, and if he had to go somewhere for business, he would have told her. There was no trace, no sign, no hint of where he had gone. What could have happened? Pok-sun searched the lower and upper villages several times, but eventually exhausted herself and returned to the empty hut, crying. She lay down, got up, and tried everything, but to no avail. Furthermore, even when her husband was there, they had been starving for three days. Now that he was gone, things were even worse. She spent that day in tears, and from the next day, she went from house to house, doing chores like pounding grain, washing clothes, sewing, fetching water, and tending the fire, earning a living by the day. Several months passed like this. As spring passed and they entered the harsh summer, she could no longer continue in that village, which was a farming community. She had no choice but to leave.

About ten *li* (里) from the village towards the coast, there was a salt pan (鹽田) run by a headman named Kim Chwa-su (金座首). Ch'oe Pok-sun eventually made her way to Kim Chwa-su's house. She stayed there comfortably for about ten days. One day, Kim Chwa-su called Pok-sun and said,

"Originally, I was going to keep you here for a long time, having you do kitchen work and passing the days. But there are circumstances, so you should leave my house and go somewhere else."

Pok-sun was surprised to hear this and wondered if she had done something wrong. She looked at Kim Chwa-su and said,

"I don't know why you are saying such things. I have been trusting you like a relative since I lost my husband. I am very surprised to hear you say that. I would like to know the reason."

Kim Chwa-su, with a look of sympathy on his face, said worriedly,

"I know your situation. During the summer, when it's time to make salt, all sorts of disorderly people (烏合亂民) gather here. They know you are alone in my house and are asking me to be a matchmaker. I can't possibly talk to you about such things, considering your behavior and my reputation. I have refused several times, but there are so many requests that I can't refuse them all, and I can't agree to be a matchmaker. It's just too bothersome, so I have no choice but to ask you to leave my house."

Pok-sun was simple-minded and had a strong sense of chastity. Even though her husband had disappeared without a trace, she was determined to remain faithful to him, believing that he was still alive and might come looking for her someday. She would hold his hand again, even if it meant living in poverty. She was struck by a bolt from the blue. For a while, she was dumbfounded and speechless. After thinking about it carefully, she realized that she couldn't search for her husband because she didn't know where he was. If she traveled alone, she would be harassed by men. She thought about throwing herself into the sea to maintain her chastity, but she worried about her missing husband's future. Given the circumstances, what should she do? She decided that she had no choice but to sacrifice her chastity and stay alive, hoping to meet her husband someday. If she could explain her situation and he would take her back, she would follow him. If not, she would kill herself. With this resolution, she looked at Kim Chwa-su with a calm expression and said,

"Sir, if you are asking me to leave because of these circumstances, I have nowhere to go and cannot live with another man. Please have pity on me and let me stay in your small house."

Kim Chwa-su initially refused, but after Pok-sun repeatedly pleaded, he agreed, considering her situation. From that day on, Pok-sun became part of Kim Chwa-su's household.

Time, unburdened by human judgment or consent, allowed Pok-sun to endure ten years of a life that was not of her own choosing, a life lived as if in a dream. One day, Kim Chwa-su was taking a stroll along the street at sunset when he saw a beggar sleeping near a willow pavilion. Suddenly feeling pity, he went to the beggar, woke him up, sat him down, and said, "The sun is setting, and you haven't prepared anything for dinner. Why are you sleeping like that?" The beggar thanked Kim Chwa-su and then rose, bowed, and said (apologies, but I have something to ask you. It happened ten years ago. A man named Kim Sang-nok, who lived in a village about ten *li* from here, disappeared without a trace. I heard that his wife became a concubine to a man named Kim Chwa-su here. I want to know if she is still in his household. I would be grateful if you could tell me if you know anything about it.") Kim Chwa-su was very surprised to hear these words from the beggar. He wondered how he knew so much about the situation and why he was asking. The beggar replied, "The truth is, I knew that man very well. I was passing through the area recently and heard about what happened. I thought you might know something about it. If you do, I would like to see his wife, Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi)." Kim Chwa-su thought to himself that the beggar might be Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi)'s husband, Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿). However, since he hadn't forcibly taken another man's wife, there was no reason to be concerned, even if he was the husband. Furthermore, even if something untoward had happened, there was no way to avoid it since he already knew and was asking about it. So, he readily replied that he knew where she was and even knew her face. He would arrange for them to meet. He returned home and told Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi) the whole story. <170>

Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi) was shocked to hear this and suspected that it was her husband. She rushed out to find him. Sure enough, sitting under a willow tree, looking at the setting sun, wearing a tattered coat and a rush raincoat (속새오쟁이) beside him, the beggar was indeed Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿), her husband who had disappeared without a trace ten years ago. For ten years, Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi) had wanted to cry, resent, and express all the pent-up frustrations in her heart if she ever met her husband. But when she met this beggar husband, all those feelings vanished. She was dumbfounded and stared at him for a long time before saying,

"You are still wandering around like that?"

The beggar husband sighed deeply and said,

"A man with a bad fate can't help it, no matter where he goes. No matter how long it's been, nothing changes. I've been wandering around like this for ten years, which felt like a single day. I was on my way back to my hometown, since it's where I used to live. I heard that you had remarried. I came to see you and talk to you. Without further ado, you have been living comfortably with Kim Chwa-su (金座首) for ten years. I have come back in this state. Now, you can choose to follow me or Kim Chwa-su (金座首). You can decide as you wish. I just wanted to hear your answer." He looked at Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi). At that moment, all the pent-up frustration, resentment, and bitterness that had been building up in Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi)'s heart for ten years erupted at once. She fell to her knees before the beggar husband and wept bitterly for a long time. Finally, she calmed down and said,

"Tell me, am I enduring all this shame until now, so I can talk about everything that happened while we were apart and follow you if you take me back, or throw myself into the sea and die if you don't? Is your asking me to do as I please a test of me? If you don't think I'm dirty, I will follow you anywhere."

Then, her husband comforted Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi), saying that all the suffering was his fault. He said that Kim Chwa-su (金座首) was their great benefactor and that they should express their gratitude to him. That night, they went to Kim Chwa-su (金座首)'s house and thanked him for everything. The next day, Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi) and her husband returned to their old village. They stayed at a friend's house for a few days, eating whatever they could get. One day, they heard that a large estate (田庄) in the village was for sale. Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿) asked a friend to help him buy the estate. The friend said, "How can you buy such a large estate when you've come back to your hometown as a beggar?" Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿) said, "It's not like that. On my way back to my hometown, I met a friend in Wŏnsan (元山). <171> I told him about my situation, and he said he would buy me a good estate if there was one available. He said he would provide the money, so don't worry about it and just negotiate the price of the estate." The friend didn't really believe him, but he decided to check it out, since Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿) kept asking him. He spent several days negotiating the price of the estate, finally agreeing on 5,000 won. He told Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿) that he had to pay the full amount in ten days and to prepare accordingly. The friend was still suspicious and kept an eye on Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿)'s movements. Since the money was coming from someone in Wŏnsan (元山), he would have to go to Wŏnsan (元山), but he just stayed there nonchalantly. The friend found it ridiculous and frustrating, so he went to Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿) and said, "Are you trying to make me look unreliable? The money is coming from your friend in Wŏnsan (元山), so you need to go to Wŏnsan (元山). Tomorrow is the deadline, and you haven't even left yet. What's going on?" Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿) calmly smiled and said, "As long as the money is paid on time, it doesn't matter. Why are you worrying so much? Don't worry," and continued to linger. The next day, the landowner and the friend came to collect the money for the estate. Then, Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿) took out the only item he had brought with him from his hometown ten years ago, the rush raincoat (속새오작이). From the bundles of banknotes inside, he counted out 5,000 won. The landowner and the friend were shocked, and Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi) was even more surprised. She couldn't believe that he had been traveling as a beggar with such a large sum of money, and that he hadn't mentioned it for over a month since they had met. She was amazed that he had been testing her heart.

After sending the two men away, Kim Sang-nok (金尙祿) took Ch'oe (崔) 씨 (ssi)'s hand and spent the night telling her the story of how he had run away ten years ago, leaving her sleeping, the story of how he had worked as a laborer in Haeju (海蔘威) to earn money, and the story of how he had left Haeju (海蔘威), throwing away all his other clothes and wearing only the clothes he had worn when he left, carrying only the old rush raincoat (속새오작이) as he searched for his hometown, and how he had been testing his wife's feelings. <172>

지금부터 20餘年年 일이 올시다. 永興땅 어느 해변 갓가히 족고만 달팡이집 가튼 土窟 속에서 그날그날을 보내든 金尙祿(가명)과 崔福順(가명)이란 내외 두 젊음 사람이 잇섯슴니다. 陰曆세 밋히 되어서 먹을 양식 걱정 닙을 의복 걱정에 정신 찰일 사이도 업는데다가 債鬼들의 성화가튼 督促에야 살이 빠지고 간이 조라 붓흘 지경이 되엇슬 때임니다. 福順이는 사흘이나 굶은 배를 타라쥐고 살님사리 걱정으로 이궁리 저궁리하고 누엇다가 그래도 怨謦의 잠이 들어서 한잠 자고 니러나니 이게 웬일임닛가? 겻헤서 자든 남편이 간데 업겟지요 처음에는 소페 보러 나간 줄 알고 잇다가 차츰 한 시간 두 시간 지나서 날이 히원이 밝아도 종시 消息이 업섯슴니다. 그담에는 겁이 왈칵 니러나서 항시 무슨 짐승의 봉변이나 당하지 안엇나 하고 이리저리 차저 보기 시작하엿슴니다. 뒤 뜰 압 뜰 변소간 오래뜰 할 것 엄시 암만 차저 보아도 도저히 알길이 업섯슴니다.<168> 虎狼이에게 물려가도 자최는 잇슬 터인데 자최라고는 아무 것도 업고 본래 雜技나 하는 사람 가트면 雜技판이나 갓거니 하겟는데 雜技라고는 겻헤도 안가는 사람이요 술집 기생집은 말 할 것도 업스며 무슨 볼일이 잇서서 어듸를 단여 올랴고 가슬것 가트면 녀편네에게 갓다온다는 이약이는 하고야 갈 터인데 도모지 아무 자최도 흔적도 긔척도 업시 업서젓스니 이것이 웬일일가? 福順이는 알엣마을 웃마을 두  번 세 번 곱돌면서 찻다찻다 못하야 고만 氣盡脈盡하여서 쓸쓸한 빈움 속에 도라와 울다간 생각하고 생각하다간 울면서 누엇다 니러낫다 아무리 애를 써도 별 수가 업섯슴니다. 더구나 남편이 잇슬 때에도 사흘식이나 굼는 형편에 남편까지 업서젓스니 이제는 더할 수 업시 되엇슴니다. 그 날 하로는 그럭저럭 눈물로 지나 보내고 그 잇흔날부터는 이 집 저 집 도라 단이면서 절구질 빨내질 바누질 물질기 불때기 이런 일 저런 일을 하여주며 입이나 엇어먹고 하로 잇흘 지난 것이 벌서 數3朔이 되엿슴니다. 그리자 봄철은 다 지나고 窮夏를 당하게 되엿스니 그 洞里도 역시 농촌이라 그러케라도 더 지나갈 수가 업서서 이제는 不得己 딴 곳으로 가게 되엇슴니다.
그 촌에서 해변으로 한 10리 가면 소곰(鹽)굿는 잠판이 잇고 잠판 가운데는 金座首라는 판주 영감이 잇섯는데 崔福順이는 그럭저럭 흘너 간 것이 이 金座首 집에까지 가게 되엿슴니다. 그래서 한10여일 동아 맘 노코 몸 편히 잘 잇노라니까 하로는 金座首가 福順이를 불너 하는 말이
내가 당초에는 자네를 내 집에다 오래두고 부억일이나 식히면서 돌의자 남의지로 지나려고 하엿드니 딱한 사정이 잇스니 不得不 내 집에 잇지 말고 다른 곳으로 가는 것이 조켓다. 고 함니다. 福順이는 뜻박게 이 말을 듯고는 자긔가 무슨 잘못한 일이나 잇서서 그러나 하고 한참! 으로 생각하다가 金座首를 처다보고
무슨 일로 그런 말삼을 하시는지 알 수 업사오나 저는 남편 일고 외로운 몸이 당신을 親戚처럼 밋고 잇삽드니 千萬 뜻박게 그런 말삼을 하시니 그 緣由를 알고 십슴니다.
