Song of the Markets of the Eight Provinces - Gaksari
						Gaksari
It is ignorant to regard the market song as merely the idle chatter of beggars. The market song is originally a song of the markets of each province, and even though it is a short verse, it contains special aspects of the local products, sentiments, and customs of each region, so there is no reason not to savor it.

"Gaksari's" First Lament

Eolssiguna, doing well.
Pumba, doing well.
Gaksari who came last year.
Didn't die and came again.
Ah, this fellow, even if he is
A son of a high minister or a judge,
Even if he refused the governors of the eight provinces,
Sold for a penny
And met as Gaksari.
Jirigu, jirigu, doing well.
Pumba, pumba, doing well.
Who is your teacher?
Doing better than me.
Did you read the Book of Poetry and the Book of Documents?
So learnedly doing well.
Did you read the Analects and Mencius?
Doing the main points well.
Did you drink cold water?
So refreshingly doing well.
Did you drink rice water?
So thickly doing well.
Did you drink oil?
So smoothly doing well.
If you can't see the master carpenter,
You take off your winter clothes.
Jirigu, jirigu, doing well.
Pumba, pumba, doing well.
The seated ring is a copper ring,
The standing ring is a door ring,
The jumping ring is a dog ring,
The flying ring is a cuckoo,
The wearing ring is a jacket.
Jirigu, jirigu, doing well.
Pumba, pumba, doing well.
One-legged goblin,
Two-legged crow,
Three-legged wooden owl,
Four-legged donkey,
The eating mouth is ogre (口).
Jirigu, jirigu, doing well.
Pumba, pumba, doing well.

Pumba, pumba, doing well.

장타령을 걸인의 허튼 수작으로만 녁이면 무식한 짓이다. 장타령이란 원래 각도 시장타령이니 짧은 구절일망정 지방지방의 특산물, 인정, 풍속의 특수한 뎜이 나타나 잇서 吟味할 갑이 업지 안흔 것이다.
