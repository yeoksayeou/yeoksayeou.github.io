Discussion on the Issue of School Excursions
A Matter to Consider Before Abolition

Ch'ŏn Yun-shik, Principal of Chŏngsin Girls' School

"Because another school is going, we have to go too. Because that school went to such and such a place, we have to go there too!" Isn't this the recent trend, focusing only on the formality of school excursions? To reiterate, "Because boys' schools and commercial schools are going to Antong County (安東縣) or the South Manchuria (南滿洲) area, we have to go too!" I think it's a big mistake for ordinary girls' schools to go on trips there.

I think it is necessary to let them go to places that suit their station – like Kyŏngju or Puyo for girls' schools.

One Gain, One Loss

Yun Ye-bin, Principal of Kyŏngsin School

I do not want to say whether school excursions are good or bad, or discuss their pros and cons. In other words, school excursions have both advantages and disadvantages for us, so if we only think about the advantages, there are disadvantages, so it is difficult to regard them as absolutely good, and if we only think about the disadvantages, there are advantages, so it is difficult to say that they are absolutely bad. If we look at the point of losing money and spending class time, we can only think of it as harmful, but in terms of actually seeing what we learned in history, geography, and natural history, it is difficult to say it is not necessary, so I do not have an opinion on whether school excursions are good or bad. If you value money and time and say that school excursions are unnecessary, then they will be unnecessary. If you spend money and time, but actually see and hear a lot and get great results in cultivation, then that makes sense too.

However, it is very necessary to consider the circumstances and the times for everything, so it would be difficult to put forward the opinion that even if you cannot buy textbooks or pay monthly fees, you should prepare travel expenses and go on a trip. It is also not acceptable to say that you can spend a lot on tuition but cannot pay for school excursions. If you proceed with a plan to obtain effects within the extent that you will not receive serious <114> influence by doing this, it is undoubtedly beneficial and should be encouraged. However, it is also difficult to make things as they are in words.

Finally, there is something I want to recommend to my school's students and the general students. Using winter vacation or summer vacation, prepare something like a pouchkau (幕) and go on a walking trip with like-minded comrades to explore famous places and see various things. Because you travel on foot, firstly, your body is healthy, secondly, the cost is low, and thirdly, you are not limited by the schedule, so you can observe everything in detail.

This has been my long-cherished wish for years. I have not been able to fulfill it due to various circumstances, but this year I am going to have our school students try it, and I am thinking of putting forward school teachers to directly guide this work.

Beneficial in Three Ways

Yi Yun-ju, Principal of Hwimun High School

If I express my personal opinion, I think school excursions should be strongly encouraged and are a good thing. There are some people who usually criticize and oppose school excursions, but their reasons for opposition are mostly about money. The living conditions of Koreans are weakening day by day, but despite this, the school authorities collect more than 10 won per person every year and force students to do this, so it is difficult to fathom the intentions of the authorities. Some people say that going on a school excursion does not bring any special effects and is just taking students out to eat and ride cars and spend money, and some people even say such harsh things.

There are many reasons why I approve and support school excursions, but I will mention only a few important ones.

First, school excursions allow students to actually see and hear what they have learned in each subject at school, which is very helpful to students in a subtle way. For example, if you take students from the southern provinces to the northern provinces, show them the beautiful Kumsu River (錦繡江山) in P'yŏngyang, show them the different customs, and let them visit historical sites of thousands of years, what could be wrong with that? It is not worth questioning spending a few won. <115>

Secondly, students and teachers can have informal friendships. Usually, there is a strict gap between teachers and students, so there is no opportunity to be close, but during school excursions, they share lodging and meals in the same room, so the bond between teachers and disciples naturally deepens, and I think this is a beautiful thing in today's academic world.

Thirdly, Korean students are usually buried in thatched houses, so the air is naturally unclean and it has a considerable impact on their mental health. Therefore, going out to the vast nature as a group is really good for the body and mind. For example, when you climb a scenic mountain and look out at an endless field, your mind is broadened and when the world comes into view, a difficult-to-name sense of courage wells up in my mind.

Therefore, I hope that once a week, within the scope that does not interfere with class time, teachers will lead students on outings to cultivate their mind and body.

In any case, I think that school excursions should be there, even if it costs money, and I do not hesitate to say so.

Meaningless Travel is Unnecessary

Ch'oe Jin-sun, Principal of Chungang School

School excursions are a required extracurricular activity. It is especially indispensable for history, geography, and natural history, as it allows students to see the various things they have learned in class. There is an old saying, "Seeing once is better than hearing a hundred times," and it is truly effective in learning as they get to see the real thing, not just imperfect specimens, after studying in a narrow classroom.

However, various schools in our society hold school excursions as an annual event, and I think it should be opposed if they go in groups like country people sightseeing in Seoul, wandering around the streets and looking at strange things.

We must prepare for school excursions before going. For example, if you are going to a place with many historical sites related to history, you must have preparatory education on history, and if it is a place that is helpful for geography, you must have relevant teaching. Otherwise, if you just say, "Such and such place is a famous scenic spot, let's go there this year," and leave vaguely, it will only waste money and time.

Currently, most of the school excursions conducted in general schools <116> are done vaguely without preparatory education. I do not know if it is necessary to abolish this type of school excursion immediately, but I believe that it should not be abolished from a fundamental educational point of view.

Another significant advantage of school excursions is that they provide an opportunity for teachers and disciples and students to become close without any gaps.

Unlike in the old days, today's schools are not like private schools, so they are not places where only children from the same village gather to study. Even looking at the small schools in Seoul, you can see that the students come from all over the 13 provinces (道). Therefore, even though they are close friends studying in the same room, they become quite estranged. Moreover, there should be an unbreakable relationship between teachers and students like that between parents and children, but that is not the case. This regrettable point can be broken down on the occasion of school excursions.

And those who oppose school excursions by citing the disadvantages mostly raise the issue of money, which is a foolish thing to throw away a hundred for the sake of one. If you do not spend about 10 won per person per year on school excursions, will the family feel the smoothness and abundance of the economy? Absolutely not. Just like you can't save money for a manggeon (網巾) even if you cut your hair, that 10 won will not be saved separately. It would be difficult to spend even 10 won if you don't have it, but it is not something to make a fuss about like those who oppose school excursions.

In any case, I assert that school excursions are indispensable for students in terms of physical education or mental cultivation.

Make it More Meaningful

Ch'oe Tu-sŏn, Principal of Chungang High School

Until a few years ago, there was no issue of continuation or abolition of school excursions. However, recently, the issue has become quite problematic among school authorities and students internally, and the public opinion of the general public is also not insignificant externally. Our school did not go on school excursions the year before last, and it was suspended last year. However, it is not because we completely deny the need for school excursions. The year before last, we couldn't go due to the school's strike (盟休) incident, which you all know well, and last year it was suspended because we couldn't collect the school excursion fees, which should have been collected from January of that year. Collecting a lot of money in a lump sum is a problem, but the bigger reason is that all the compatriots (同胞) of Chosŏn <117> are feeling extreme hunger and poverty due to the drought in the south and the flood in the north. How can we consume so much material and go, even if it is a school excursion? For the above reasons, it was suspended last year and this year, and only field trips to nearby places are planned for this spring for the entire school year.

In any case, I find it difficult to express my opinion on the necessity of continuing or abolishing the issue of school excursions. That is because my saying whether this issue is good or bad is like expressing a part of our school's opinion to the outside world before our school itself decides. Even if our school decides to hold school excursions as an annual event in the future, we will try to make it more meaningful and have practical results than the traditional school excursions, and even if we abolish it altogether, we will try to gain a smooth understanding from parents and students. One of the causes of the student unrest last year was the issue of school excursions. Anyway, it is difficult for me to speak unilaterally before a concrete plan regarding school excursions comes out in our school, and if a plan is established regarding this issue in the future, we will announce it to the general public.

Our School Has Already Abolished It Completely

A-p'yŏn Sŏl-lo, Principal of Paejai Higher Common School

Rather than expressing my ideals, opinions, or arguments regarding the issue of school excursions, I will talk about what my school is doing because it believes it is right.

Our school thought that school excursions were not very necessary, so it even abolished them completely for about two years. When we abolished the annual event all at once, the students seemed lonely, and the school authorities seemed to have acted too extremely. After extensive consultation and deep consideration, we decided to have only the fifth graders in the graduating class go on one school excursion a year, and we have been implementing it ever since, and we are seeing very good results.

This is not only limited to the students' families, but it is also an issue to be viewed in the overall situation of Korean society. In terms of the economy, the strength of Koreans is extremely weak, so it is an undeniable fact that it is impossible for people in the provinces to afford to send their children to study in Kyŏngsŏng (京城) and other places. Wouldn't it be a good thing for the educational authorities in Chosŏn to take care of the parents who are always having difficulty paying for their children's tuition so that they do not have to bear even a small additional burden?

Therefore, our school receives 3 won per year for travel expenses, so when they reach the fifth grade, 15 won has been accumulated, and all the fifth graders are taken to see the scenic Kŭmgangsan (金剛山), which can be proudly presented to the world. <118>

If you were to go to Kŭmgangsan as an individual, you would need at least 100 won, and moreover, to see all of Kŭmgangsan, you would have to explore it as if you were on an expedition because the mountains are rugged.

In this respect, I think it is very advantageous to use the student period.

School excursions are usually said to be indispensable extracurricular activities, but in reality, they do not achieve the same effect, and students often behave badly during the trip, which causes considerable damage not only to the students but also to the school's reputation.

The fact is that school excursions do not achieve the same beneficial effects as the money and class time spent, so I think it is good to conduct school excursions to a very limited extent.

School Excursions Becoming Luxurious

Kim Pal-bong

Going on school excursions in spring and autumn is an annual event for each school. If you ask whether going on school excursions is good or bad, we answer that it can be both good and bad.

The reason why school excursions can be good is that (1) they provide an opportunity to broaden knowledge of geography, (2) history, and (3) human affairs, and to gain a lot of practical knowledge, and (4) they are effective for health because they provide a temporary change of mood in school life. Next, the reasons why school excursions can be bad are that (1) they provide an opportunity to spend money freely and recklessly, (2) they provide an opportunity to increase curiosity about playful activities in a bad way rather than practical knowledge due to insufficient guidance and supervision, and (3) they generally cause a considerable economic blow to the families of parents because the parents are poor and the students are forced to squeeze out a considerable amount of school excursion fees, and (4) therefore, students without travel expenses cannot go and only students who have travel expenses can go. Therefore, even if the trip is beneficial as a whole, it is a fact that school excursions tend to become a kind of luxury and ostentation in student life because only students from wealthy families can go, and they are already becoming luxurious.

In short, if school excursions, as they are currently conducted in each school, cost a lot of money and gain more bad knowledge than good knowledge, then they are undoubtedly bad even for students from wealthy families. If most of the time, they are just going to P'yŏngyang, Kyŏngju, or Kaesŏng from Kyŏngsŏng and ending up getting some air, then it would be more economical to take a train <119> and go to Ch'ŏngnyangni or the Han River and come back.

In short, the problem lies with the school authorities and parents. Those in charge should study harder on how to get closer to nature and at the same time acquire much more practical knowledge about geography, history, human affairs, climate, and social conditions, and strive to bring the current luxurious "school excursions" in a good direction.

If the Method Cannot Be Improved, Abolition is Preferable

Yi Sŏk

Whatever the matter may be, I think it is unacceptable for the means to achieve the purpose to be evil, even if the purpose is good. Therefore, I think the same applies to the issue of school excursions that your company is asking about. In other words, is it meaningless to visit scenic spots and historical sites to relieve the students who are struggling day and night to accumulate the merits of studying hard (螢雪의 功) once a year and to gain knowledge of geography and natural history? However, if it places an excessive burden on the students or causes them immense pain in order to achieve that purpose, wouldn't it be better to abolish the practice? In general, the lives of ordinary Koreans are not affluent, so even when they send one son or one daughter to school and pay the regular expenses, most of them suffer extraordinary pain and distress in their household finances. How much more so if they have to bear an unexpected burden? Even the actual cost of 5 to 10 won required for a school excursion is not a small amount, and if they have to prepare some private expenses, clothes, and other travel equipment on top of that, it would be a huge hardship for the parents, and the regret and sorrow of the student himself, who has no choice but to drop out of the crowd, is truly indescribable. Even if I were to talk about my own experience, about 14 or 15 years ago, when I was enrolled in a certain local agricultural school, the school decided to have all of our class go on a school excursion to the National Industrial Exhibition (共進會) being held in Kyŏngsŏng. Unfortunately, my family was poor, so I was the only one who could not afford the necessary expenses, and I had no choice but to drop out. At that time, I resented my family's poverty for no reason, and I even cursed the school authorities and the National Industrial Exhibition. Perhaps because I became depressed because of this, it seemed that all the students in the class despised me after that, and until graduation, I could not get rid of the feelings of bitterness and resentment in my chest. And to give a final example, 2 or 3 years ago, after I enrolled one of my children in a certain ordinary school, I tried not to show him any hardship as much as possible, as I had suffered from the hardship of school expenses when I was young. But at some point, he asked me to buy him a set of athletic clothes when he was going on a field trip nearby, but I did not have enough money to respond to his request at that time, and after that, I missed the opportunity. A few months later, unfortunately, the child died. What kind of regret would I not have? The greatest regret was that I had not bought him the athletic clothes at that time, so one day I bought a set of athletic clothes and went to the child's grave and shed tears. This is the heart of a proletarian student and the suffering of a proletarian parent. How much more so if a weak woman experiences such a situation during her student days and faces such a situation in her mother and sister's era? It would be difficult to describe her suffering. In short, school excursions are not bad in themselves, but if even a few students' parents are suffering such hardship, I think it is absolutely necessary to abolish them. However, as I said before, it is not entirely a loss, and there is also some benefit to some students, so rather than abolishing it, I think it is necessary to revise the method of implementing it. Of course, there will be various opinions on the method, but if I were to say one aspect of my opinion, I think it would be good for schools with sufficient foundations to implement it with school expenses, or in cases where this is not possible, to have a support association (후원회) in the school and receive donations from wealthy parents and conduct it for everyone, regardless of grade or class. To do this, I think it will require more sincerity and effort from the school authorities, and if the current method is not revised, I think that proletarian students and parents will inevitably oppose it. <121>

修學旅行 問題 論議
廢止까지는 생각할 問題

정신여학교 千潤植

「남의 학교에서 가닛가 우리들도 가야 하겟다. 그 학교서 그러한 곳으로 갓스닛가 우리도 꼭 거긔까지 가야지!』해서 다만 형식에 취하여서만 수학여행을 가는 것이 近年의 경향이 안인가 함니다. 거듭 말하면 『安東縣이나 南滿洲 방면으로 남자학교와 상업학교가튼대서 가는대 우리들도 가야한다. 』 하면서 普通女學校에서도 그곳으로 여행을 간다는 것은 큰 잘못인 듯이 생각됩니다.
자기들의 분수에 맛는 곳-여학교가트면 慶州나 扶餘가튼 곳으로 가게 할 필요는 잇겟다고 생각함니다.

一得一失

경신학교장 尹芮彬

나는 수학여행에 대하야 可하다거나 不可하다거나 그 是非를 말하고저 안이한다. 다시 말하면 수학여행은 우리에게 一得一失의 이해관계를 가지고 잇는 까닭에 한편의 이로운 점만 생각하면 해가 되는 점이 잇서 절대로 조흔 일이라고 간주하기 어럽고 또 한편의 害롭은 점만 생각하면 이로움이 잇서 절대로 납븐 일이라고 말하기는 어럽게 된다. 돈 업서지고 수업하는 시간을 소비하든 점을 보면 해롭다고만 생각할 수 잇스나 역사나 地理博物에서 배운 것을 실지로 견학하는 점에 잇서서는 불가하다고 이클기 어러운 것인즉 나는 수학여행에 대하야 可否 의견을 가지고 잇지 안는다. 돈과 시간을 존귀하게 생각하야 수학여행을 불필요하다고 하면 불필요할 것이요. 돈과 시간은 업서트려도 실지로 견문하는 것이 만하 修養에 지대한 好結果를 엇는다면 그것도 그럴 듯한 일이다.
그러나 무슨 일이던지 境遇와 시대를 參酌할 필요는 크게 잇는 것이니 교과서를 사지 못하고 月謝金을 납입하지 못하면서라도 여행비은 준비하야 여행을 가야한다는 것은 올흔 의견이라고 내세우기 어러울 것이요 학비를 풍부히 쓰면서도 수학여행엔 돈낼 수 업다고 하면 그도 容忍할 일이 안이다. 그일을 행하야 그것으로 심대한<114> 영향을 밧지안을 정도 내에서 效果 어들 立案으로 진행하면 이는 두말할 것 업시 이로운 일인 동시에 獎勵할 것이다. 그러나 처사에 잇서서 이러케 말과 가티 되기는 또한 어러운 것이다.
나는 끗흐로 내 학교 학생이거나 일반학생에게 권하고 십흔 것이 잇다. 冬期放學이나 夏期放學을 이용하야 포우쓰카우 모양으로  幕가튼 것을 준비하여 가지고 뜻맛는 동지들이 一團이 되여 徒步旅行으로 일홈잇는 곳을 탐방하야 여러가지로 견학하면 조켓다고 思惟한다. 이는 徒步로 단니니 첫재 몸이 건강하고 둘재로는 비용이 적고 셋재로는 시일에 제한을 밧지 안을 시기임으로 모든 것을 정밀히 관찰할 수 잇는 것이다.
이 일은 내 年來의 宿望이다. 여러가지 사정으로 履行을 못하여 보앗스나 금년에는 우리 학교 학생들에게 식혀보랴 하고 이일에 직접 지도할 사람은 학교 敎員들을 내세울 생각이다.

세가지로 有益

휘문고보교장 李潤柱

나 개인의 의견을 陳述한다면 수학여행은 극력 장려할 일이며 아울너 조흔 일이라고 생각함니다. 보통 수학여행을 조치 못하게 評判하며 반대하는 일부 人士도 업는 것은 안이나 그들의 반대하는 구실꺼리는 대개가 돈 문제임니다. 朝鮮人의 생활상태가 나날이 浸弱하는 형편인데 학교 당국에서는 이도 불구하고 年年히  每人에게 10여원의 비용을 수납하여 强迫的으로 학생에게 이를 실시하니 當局者의 心思를 料量하기 어럽다고 하는 이도 잇슴니다. 수학여행을 갓다왓다고 특수한 효과를 엇는 것도 안이요 공연히 학생들을 끌고 밥 사먹고 차타고 돈 쓰는데서 불과하다고 이러케 過酷하게까지 말슴하시는 이도 업는 것은 안임니다.
내 수학여행을 조타고 찬성하는 이유는 여러 가지가 잇스나 중요한 것 멧가지만 들어 말하겟슴니다.
수학여행은 첫재 학교에서 학습한 各學科를 실지로 見聞하는 것이 만흔즉 학생에게 隱然中 유익함이 만슴니다. 가령 예를 들면 南道學生을 北道 方面으로 달이고가서 평양의 錦繡江山을 구경식히고 또는 人情風俗이 달은 것을 보이고 몃천년 以來의 歷史古蹟을 參觀 식힌다면 무엇이 불가함니까. 돈 멧圓 소비하는 것을 문제삼을 바<115> 안임니다.
둘재로는 학생과 선생사이에 형식업는 친목을 할 수 잇슴니다. 普通時는 선생과 학생간에 엄격한 간격이 잇서 친근할 기회가 업스나 修學旅行時는 여관 한 방에서 寢食을 가티 하게되는 까닭에 師弟之間의 分이 자연 두터워지니까 이런 일은 今日 學界에 잇서서 美風이라고 생각함니다.
