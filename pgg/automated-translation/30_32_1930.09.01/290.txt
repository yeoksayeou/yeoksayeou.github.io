Nonsense Serial Comic Essay, New Cheongju is Guilty
First Episode: Changseok, Second Episode: Baekgwi, Third Episode: Noeul, Fourth Episode: Guseok

1, Autumn Wind Rises, New Cheongju Appears

"Hey there! Good news! New Cheongju is already out!"

"Oh, so it's already autumn."

"Does New Cheongju being out mean it's autumn, or does autumn mean New Cheongju is out?"

"Anyway, it's good news, so we should have some."

"Of course..."

When the sky, scorched by the heat, clears up as if washed and soars high, when the withered weeds (野草) shake their heads and send a cool breeze, the release of new Cheongju at that time is, according to the drinkers, more joyous than receiving a diploma, getting a job, and finding a marriage partner all at once.

The name itself is cool and refreshing. New Cheongju tastes better when you find a small alley, go into a dive bar, and sit down to eat it. Miss X, a young music teacher at OO Girls' High School, who roams the narrow alleys from noon on Saturdays. Though married to the pastor's daughter after sincere devotion in the chapel, she somehow learned the taste of seeking out New Cheongju and took the lead again today.

Sitting down in the corner room of the famous dive bar in Daokjeong, Miss X received the first glass of delicious New Cheongju after a long time and offered a grateful prayer. "Oh, merciful and compassionate Father Jesus, thank you, thank you for sending me my long-awaited lover again."

"Haha! You may be happy about New Cheongju, but if this gets out, you'll be fired from school and your father-in-law will take your wife away. Be careful." <88>

"I have nothing to fear. If the school fires me for drinking, I will expose their sins. As for my wife, what does it matter after we're married? What is marriage? It's stamping the man's seal firmly. After the seal is stamped, she can leave if she wants."

Full of spirit, she drank over 40 glasses. She was quite drunk and tried to grab her cane, but instead grabbed the patent leather toe of her shoe. Nevertheless, when she came out onto the street, her voice was somewhat nasal, making it slightly unmusical, but she was still sober enough not to stagger.

"If it's called New Cheongju, we should sit in a cool place and taste it with the autumn nature. Drinking it in that cramped room was our generation's mistake."

"That's why I insisted on going across the Han River from the beginning..."

"There's no need to be so worried. Tomorrow is Sunday, so we can sleep in. Let's go."

These brave drinkers resolutely caught a taxi and went across the Han River. How great, how truly great, is the attractive force and efficacy of New Cheongju.

However, every restaurant by the river lacked a quiet and cozy room. Due to Miss X's opposition to openly drinking in front of others, they looked for a quieter place and finally entered a bar with no customers. Coincidentally, it was a colored liquor house called Naeoe Hotel. Three women came in and sat down among four customers, immediately touching their laps. Miss X couldn't help but be nervous and closed the window a little, hiding behind it and barely feeling relieved. But then, after hearing Miss X mention that she was a music teacher, the women began to demand a song. It's typical for musicians to refuse when they're being forced, so she refused to sing in such a place. Then a rude woman grabbed Miss X's tie and clung to it.

Whether she sang or not, they ended up embracing and struggling with each other, when the door opened. The table was being cleared. In the midst of the wrestling-like commotion, a voice rang out from the yard.

"Teacher, how did you come here?" Surprised, she looked out and realized it was a disaster. It was a young man who was a member of her church and worked as a delivery man for a Western liquor company. For the young man, it was a great discovery to see something he shouldn't have seen. For Miss X, it was a secret that had been exposed.

If it were someone else, she might have called him over, given him a glass of New Cheongju, and shut him up. But since he was a devout believer, she couldn't do that. "Oh, how did you get here?" She didn't know why she was asking him how he got there; the words just slipped out in a moment of panic. "I come here often to deliver liquor," he replied. His answer was a question in itself: "I come here for work, but why are you here?" "I came out for a walk by the river...There was no place to rest my legs...The weather is so hot..." She was sweating like someone who had diarrhea in their pants. If Jesus Christ had been there, it would have been a tragic scene if he hadn't rushed to save her. <89> After the young man finally left, Miss X, whose face had turned pale, grabbed her hat and proposed that they quickly leave the place.

"Oh, you fool! Now that it's been exposed, what good will it do to leave now? Since we're here, let's just enjoy the liquor."

"No, let's get out of here. If we stay here any longer, my stomach will burst."

"Oh, do you think you're the only one who drinks secretly? Pastors and such only go to Japanese restaurants to hide from other people's eyes and are good at flirting with female servants."

"No, whatever."

This time, the women all stood up and began to grab Miss X.

"No matter what, I won't let you go. Let's get married today. Even one night is marriage...Shall we have the wedding at the chapel or at Myongwolgwan?"

"Well, goodbye, goodbye. I'll come again next time."

"No, why are you saying that when we're getting married today?"

"Alright, if you're going to leave, go ahead. I'm not giving this back."

At some point, one of the women untied Miss X's belt and wrapped it around her hand.

Unable to take it back, Miss X became angry and untied the pink silk sash that had been tied around the woman's skirt, tying it in place of her belt.

"Oh, just take it. What's mine and yours between husband and wife? Let's exchange them," she said, but Miss X pretended not to hear and ran out into the yard.

"Oh, I think that gentleman is really going to leave," two women said, chasing after her and grabbing her at the gate. As the argument over whether she should go or not lengthened, a modern couple passed by the gate. The woman, boldly arm in arm with a male student wearing a square cap, was carefully watching this side. Wasn't that a student who was good at music at Miss X's school?

If she hadn't been seen in front of a colored liquor house arguing with women, she would have scolded the student on the spot. Unfortunately, she was showing a scene that shouldn't be shown, and the student who should be scolded had her eyes widened, while Miss X was looking for a hole smaller than a hazelnut. The world is truly unpredictable.

2, Extreme Joy, What to Do With Anger

That night, two hours past midnight,

Miss X, who had gotten out of a taxi, felt the liquor she had drunk all day long vanishing and her body shivering when she stood in front of her house (to be precise, her wife's house), which was locked as firmly as ever.

Her lips turned blue, and without blinking an eye, she imagined the face of his wife, who was waiting for him to return, full of anger. She shivered once more. <90>

She leaned her head against the gate and regretfully reflected on the day's events.

-If I had left school a little earlier or a little later, I wouldn't have met those guys! If I hadn't followed them when they urged me to go, it would have been better! If I had come back after the first round, it wouldn't have come to this!... Thinking back like this, she stamped her feet at the spiteful fate.

Miss X had to overcome the difficulty of the gate, which had been locked on purpose to give her trouble for coming home late, before dealing with what was to come. But no matter what she did, there was no way.

If she shouted, even her father-in-law, the pastor, would wake up, and if she spoke softly, the house was built in a Western style, so it wouldn't reach the servants...

While she was doing this, she heard the sound of a clock striking three nearby. In her impatience, she decided to climb over the wall, but it wasn't easy. After going through all sorts of hardships, she barely managed to sit on top of the wall and let out a long sigh.

According to the ungodly principle that behavior governs the mind, after sighing, she saw herself as pathetic, and seeing herself as pathetic, she resented her wife. Even if her husband came home a little late, how could she lock the gate!

She was about to jump back outside and go to some inn, but the thought itself was frightening, so she suddenly jumped down inside.

In this way, she barely overcame the first difficulty, but the real problem was, "What am I going to do when I meet my wife?!"

She walked softly to the door, which was brightly lit and as silent as death, and hesitated, feeling even more anxious than at the gate.

Could the saying "I was brave when I climbed over the wall, but I'm trembling when I grab the doorknob" be applied to this scene?

She hesitated and hesitated, and then, encouraged by the remaining half of her drunkenness, she cleared her throat and opened the door gently. She was opening it so carefully, but suddenly, a sharp voice tearing through silk, "Who is it?" It was, without a doubt, the first cry of her wife's most extreme anger. Miss X had expected it, but she couldn't help but be startled by the sharpness of the voice.

The fight had begun.

The wife, who had been lying down with a pillow under her head, ready to fly away, glared at Miss X, who was bowing and showing her willingness to surrender with humility, with eyes more powerful than Roentgen rays. She rushed at Miss X and grabbed the top pocket of his suit. The result was, of course, that the silk pola suit, which she had made on installments this summer and had only been paying for for two months, tore beautifully, and Miss X was thrown around for a while. No words came out of Miss X's mouth...

Then, a voice even sharper and more filled with anger than before, "What is this?!" made Miss X cover her ears unconsciously with both hands. Again, the wife pointed her finger at Miss X's waist and shouted, "What is this?!"

Miss X finally looked where she was pointing. As soon as she saw it, cold sweat like being splashed with cold water ran down her whole body. What was at the place in question? It was a pink women's sash, which meant eros, instead of the belt that Miss X usually wore...and the buttons of her suit pants, which had fallen off as if in agreement!

I leave it to your imagination what Miss X's expression was when she first discovered this.

"What is it? Which woman did you exchange it with?! Huh?!" she shouted, and the wife grabbed Miss X's tie tightly. Miss X still couldn't say anything and was just shaken.

"Which woman is it?!"

"Tell me right away!"

While these two words were sharply repeated, Miss X's suit was torn to pieces like a comedian in a silent movie who had been hit by a bomb.

While they were arguing like this, her father-in-law, the pastor, woke up in surprise and ran over. The pastor, seeing the scene, couldn't say a word and stood there for a long time with his mouth wide open and his eyes wide, and then knelt down and began to pray to God.

"Holy and merciful Father God! Please forgive your sinful son."

Then the wife frantically called a servant and wrote XX-dong OOO's name on a card, telling her to send a taxi to urgently bring him.

Hearing these words, neither Miss X nor the pastor could stay still. Miss X was about to open her mouth to make an excuse for the first time when the pastor cut her off.

"My dear, what is this? What's wrong?"

"Father, don't even talk to me. I can't stand someone like that, someone like that. I hate him. He's having an affair with another woman, so why should I be frustrated..."

"Well, my dear..."

"I hate it, well...A schoolteacher says he's having a staff meeting and takes a gisaeng (妓生) to a temple..."

"What are you talking about?"

"I have proof...The school janitor came earlier and said he was going to a temple for a staff meeting...That's why he's coming home late..."

The wife couldn't contain her anger any longer, and tears poured out.

Every time a word of exposure came out of the wife's mouth, Miss X was pinched and poked somewhere.

While this commotion continued, OOO from XX-dong arrived in a taxi with the card at four o'clock after midnight, the former lover and still unforgotten protagonist of this fight. <92>

When she received the news that he had arrived, the wife, with her hair disheveled, ran down barefoot and, without allowing OOO to say a word, drove the taxi away somewhere. And the pastor and Miss X, who had chased after her to the gate, stood there blankly and sent her off.

3, Extreme Anger, Waves Arise

It was too shocking an insult.

How could someone else's wife, a pastor's daughter, do such a thing? And what time was it...Miss X, who had lost his brave daughter of God, no, wife, finally looked down at his suit, which was as tattered as a shroud torn by a grave ghost. Only then did he return to his senses, which no one could touch.

Is it retribution for what I did?

Jealousy overflowing from love?

A threat? A warning? Everything?

He tries to figure out the insult he suffered like this.

But the more he did so, the more each of those things became like the tip of a needle, stabbing his head at propeller-like speed.

His head was buzzing like someone who had been badly stung by a bee.

For Miss X, who had been striving to maintain composure as a pure Christian, it was too great a change and couldn't help but be surprising. Even a toad knew its place in the middle of the night. Who would have predicted that it would come to this!

However, having received Christian education, he sat down heavily as if falling on a rock and tried to give a Christian interpretation of what his wife had done, burying his disheveled hair in his hands. "Then," he thinks, reflecting on his own actions, "then is she punishing me for drinking and playing with women? Using my wife's hands...or was it God's will to discipline a husband who is unfaithful to his wife?"

No!

He shook his head forcefully from side to side and stood up.

"God is merciful. He forgives those who repent of their sins. And even if I drank because of unavoidable circumstances, it wouldn't be a big deal if I didn't go to extremes. Moreover, if she had examined my heart, which was anxiously beating with repentance when I pushed away the woman grabbing my sleeve and when I grabbed the doorknob while climbing over the wall, she should have forgiven me right away!"

After obtaining this miraculous answer, he shook his head again from side to side and now shuddered all over. It wasn't just because the early autumn morning breeze was cold.

At this time, the father-in-law, who had been watching his son-in-law's suffering without saying a word, as if he had no words of comfort, came closer to where Miss X was standing, looking only at the gradually brightening dawn sky and muttering a prayer, and placed his hand on his shoulder.

"Hey, don't worry too much! The merciful and holy God forgives those who repent...Let's offer a prayer of repentance soon. Your wife will soon be brought back. Now, let's pray...Father God, holy..." But now, to Miss X's ears, it was a more uninteresting sound than a scarecrow's muttering. Moreover, he was very annoyed by the fact that he was hiding his daughter's faults and telling him to repent. In this way, the old pastor's comforting words finally added fuel to Miss X's crackling anger and set it on fire. Now, Miss X's tangled emotions had finally found a decisive path to unravel. "Offer a prayer of repentance? Why should I repent when I haven't done anything wrong? Let your daughter, who violated Moses' Seven Commandments, repent. I will never again offer a prayer to an incompetent and unfair God." His voice trembled with anger, piercing the early morning five o'clock autumn sky like a sharp screw. Without even acknowledging the father-in-law, who was standing there with his mouth agape in disappointment and shock, murmuring like a goldfish opening and closing its mouth, "Oh-God-the-devil-has-invaded-your-son," he ran into his room—in fact, his wife's room.

A little later, Miss X, who had changed into drab dongbok (동복) clothes, came out with a trunk containing old underwear and ten volumes of music collections, his entire fortune, walking briskly as if to catch a train. In this situation, his mother-in-law, who had woken up from her sleep, ran after him in her undershirt.

"Oh my goodness, what's wrong with you? Well, where are you going? At this early hour...There's a daughter who's going to ruin the family. Well, hey, be patient!" He shook off the father-in-law and mother-in-law, who were holding him back and pulling him down, and burst out of the gate. Where did he get such courage?

"Your daughter ran away with another man at dusk!"

Those were the bold words Miss X shouted as he came out. Having been freed from religious patience by a sudden incident one night, Miss X, having returned to the proud spirit of an artist, had become a completely different person. He had become a dignified and bold person. Now, who would imagine that the Christian Miss X, who until recently had not been able to raise his head like a prisoner standing before a prosecutor and had his suit torn to shreds by his wife, whom he valued more than God, was now showing such a cool attitude! However, the reason why his heart had changed so suddenly and been freed from religious bondage was different. Originally, Miss X had become a believer for two purposes. First, the happiness of being able to make the old pastor's daughter, the current wife, his wife, and second, a sly purpose and ambition? to become close to and trusted by Western pastors and to study in America empty-handed was latent. In this way, Miss X, who was mistaken for a faithful Christian, had fulfilled his first desire while concealing all the licentious desires of modern youth in his heart. Now, he was working step by step towards his second purpose. Since this incident had occurred, everything was ruined. In that case, it seemed like there was no reason to hesitate in a more restrictive believer's state of mind. And he was still young. He was an artist.

When all hope and purpose were shattered in an instant (at least, in his opinion at the time), he was inevitably trying to find youth, and his long-repressed heart was trying to return to the essence of an artist.

"Huh! When I think about how I've been playing the cripple so perfectly... But now there's no way. I should show them some amazing modern style! Where can you endure it!" Lying awake all night in a cheap inn room behind Jongno, our beloved Miss X tossed and turned, thinking of a clever (in his opinion) trick to get back at his betrayed wife.

The next morning, Miss X, who had opened his eyes in the lonely inn room, took a bus to the Han River as soon as he had eaten breakfast, wondering what he was thinking. The refreshingly blue waves of the Han River seemed to be flowing, teaching Miss X the joy of life anew.

As soon as he got off the bus, he went straight into the Naeoe Hotel, where he had been playing the day before.

"Oh my goodness, what wind blew you in? What's going on from the morning?"

As if they had been close since long ago...The girls act as if they can't bear to be happy, as if it has been ten years.

As they grab his wrist, push his back, and pull him to where he wants to go, Miss X is dragged into the room.

"Should I bring you liquor, should I bring you beer?"

As soon as he sat down, the shameless girls urged him on.

"Oh, that's right, this gentleman took my sash! Huh, isn't that right?"

"Yes, yes! This gentleman! Buy her something instead."

"Yes, honey! What will you buy for the teacher? Who knows if you brought something as a souvenir...If you brought something, show it to me quickly!"

As she says this, the girl who exchanged sashes unknowingly reaches out her hand.

"Hey, do you know what a fuss that caused? You're doing this without knowing other people's feelings..."

"Oh, poor thing, you got scolded by Oksang! So you came this morning to exchange it back? Kawaii botchanne anta!"

Even today, those words didn't sound offensive to Miss X. Rather, it seemed that the lonely philosophy of life was hidden in them. He even felt affectionate, like they were his cute little sisters.

"Hehehe, you rude things. It would be nice if it was just a scolding. What if I lost my wife completely...That's why I came to you this morning to have a serious discussion...This girl said she would marry me yesterday, right? Then let's get married today! What do you say? Will you do it or not?"

"Why are you making such a joke from the morning... Okay, I'll do it." While she didn't completely believe Miss X's words from the beginning, the owner of the pink sash blushed slightly and smiled as if it were a habit.

"No, it's not a joke at all! I just want you to play the role of my wife for exactly one hour. We'll even have a wedding ceremony in front of the pastor...Take this first as payment."

To show that he was not joking at all, Miss X became serious and spoke politely, then took out his hidden ten-won coin and placed it in the hand of the pink sash. Before an hour had passed, a taxi carrying Miss X and the pink sash sped towards Jongno.

4, Waves Subside, New Stillness <95>

Misfortune falls on those you trust. The fact that his son-in-law, who was so faithful and sincere, a leading devout member of the church, had put a glass of liquor to his lips, whether the devil had possessed him or he had gone crazy, was already a thousand times more than he could bear. How much more the fact that he had flirted with a promiscuous woman and eventually committed such a heinous act. The old pastor, who occupied this spot under heaven, was no less angry than his daughter was in her indignation. However, he secretly thought it was admirable that his daughter had rebuked her husband, who had acted in a way that betrayed God, and had left in such a huff. However, the old pastor was not only a faithful messenger of God but also a kind father-in-law with human compassion. Therefore, he offered a prayer to God, seeking forgiveness in place of his beloved son-in-law, who was taking actions that betrayed God.

"Merciful Lord. How could these ignorant children have only this sin? But it is this child's sin that made him sin against the Father, O Lord! If you consider me your son, please accept the prayer I offer in place of my son-in-law. But Lord, please turn away from him. This child is an ignorant daughter, but she is a precious daughter to you. She did not want to go against your will to the end, even as she struggled with her husband who had betrayed your will. Lord! Please let X be influenced by his wife, repent again, and return to your presence."

Because he was originally a calm pastor, he didn't care that his wife was stamping her feet and finished his prayer to the end.

"Dear, there's a time for prayer. It's been several hours since the girl left and she hasn't come back, so you should think about going to find her."

After changing her socks twice and putting them on again, the old pastor finally woke the servant, who was sleeping soundly, by shouting.

"Hey, go to the taxi company and find out where she dropped off the young lady."

Only then did the old pastor, who had been somewhat impatient to go find his daughter due to his wife's constant urging, wake up the servant, who was feeling the sadness of a lodger in someone else's house, and step over the middle threshold without even hearing the master's words properly, rubbing his sleepy eyes.

"By the way, what's going on at this early hour?"

The servant, who was going out absentmindedly, suddenly came to his senses at this voice and looked up. It was the lodger of the old pastor's eldest son's house.

"Is the young master home?"

The lodger of that house asked in a hushed voice, as if he had come with a great secret mission.

"I don't know what's going on. I just woke up, but it seems like there was a commotion and everyone went somewhere."

As if relieved by the news that the young master was not home, the lodger of the son's house stepped forward without hesitation and stood before the old pastor.

"What's wrong, son? At this early hour?"

The old pastor was surprised once again to see the lodger from his son's house.

"The young lady from your house is at their house right now, but they told me to only inform you and your wife that she's at their house without the young master knowing."

The old pastor and his wife were unexpectedly relieved to hear this, <96> and they admired the actions of their daughter, which seemed quite sensible. They had been worried that she had left with another man, but now that they knew she was at her older brother's house, they ate breakfast as usual without any worries. However, as the in-laws, they were now worried and curious about their son-in-law's attitude. Just as the breakfast dishes were being placed in the sink, an unexpected special delivery arrived.

Their hearts sank at the unexpected special delivery, as if they had been frightened by a turtle, and they felt an unknown fear.

"...I regret what I have done. But I am going to leave a God who does not forgive sins. And I do not want to live with a wife who insults her husband and goes to excessive lengths, even though it is an emotion born of pure jealousy. Do not be surprised. I am going to marry another woman at ten o'clock this morning. Later, in order to prove my marriage and my relationship with your daughter, I hope that the pastor—he called his father-in-law 'pastor'—and your daughter will be witnesses. There is no more urgent and important matter than this, so please come soon. This place is Taeseogwan."

The old pastor was surprised once again. He had thought that his son-in-law would return, but suddenly he was going to marry another woman, and as important as the event was, he couldn't just stay still. He and his wife set off for his eldest son's house in a hurry.

Miss X had brought the pink sash to Taeseogwan and sent such a special delivery to his father-in-law as a trick he had planned to punish his father-in-law, or rather, that harsh wife.

The pink sash had come with Miss X because of the ten-won fee and the taste of riding in a car, without knowing why.

"Are you going to be abandoned by Oksang forever and come to a place like this from the very beginning?"

The pink sash, which lacked judgment, thought that Miss X had a crush on her and was going to try something else, so she knew that he had brought her to a restaurant at the quietest time of day. And she had received the ten-won advance payment, so she thought she would do as Miss X wanted for about an hour, and she felt somewhat satisfied to come into the restaurant room, which she had always longed for, only doing teapot errands in a musty room.

"Don't say anything and just do as I say. As promised, we're going to have a wedding ceremony here. Got it?"

The pink sash, which was still naively unsophisticated, wondered about Miss X's words, but she was proud to have come into the restaurant room anyway.

When it was less than fifteen minutes to ten o'clock, she heard the footsteps of several people walking down the hallway and going into the room next to Miss X. She thought it was the people working at the restaurant, but she knew they were guests from the polite voices of the boys.

"Who are these guests from the morning? Are they getting married like us?"

Miss X laughed at the pink sash. But inside, she was secretly waiting for her father-in-law and wife to come.

"Honey, what time is it now!"

Miss X called a boy and anxiously asked the time.

"It's ten minutes before ten." <97>

"By the way, who are the guests in the room next door from the morning? Who are they?"

"Yes, OO Pastor's daughter is getting married, and they came with only two people to keep it quiet."

OO Pastor's daughter! OO was unmistakably the name of his father-in-law. Then his daughter could only be his wife. Miss X couldn't help but be surprised to hear these words.

Next, the natural anger and the obligatory jealousy blazed like fire. He immediately clenched his fists and was about to go and grab his wife by the hair and kick her for her unexpected actions. But our educated Miss X was not such a reckless person. If OO was his father-in-law and his daughter was definitely his wife, then there was no mistake. But the thought that maybe, until now, he had trusted and loved his wife. The loving wife, who had mistaken him for having an affair with another woman, could not approve of her husband's actions. However, considering his wife's behavior that morning and the words the boy was now conveying, it was possible that his emotional and nervous wife would take such a drastic step. No, more than that, the person who had called her out of bed that morning and taken her away in a car was the man she had been in love with before she married him. Then, had she been dissatisfied with him all along? And had she been secretly waiting for such an opportunity to come? As these thoughts swirled around in his head, Miss X clenched his trembling fists with unbearably angry feelings and resentful thoughts about his wife, who had insulted and betrayed him, and stood up indignantly.

"I'm putting on this show to punish her, but she's..."

Unable to bear it any longer, our agitated Miss X grabbed the doorpost of the room next door. However, our beloved Miss X never lost face even at such a time. When he stood up and came to the room next door, he had the courage and excitement to trample on tigers and lions, but if he suddenly opened the door and it wasn't what the boy said, what if there were one or two other people with the same name, what if it was someone else. Poor Miss X sincerely hoped that they would have the same name but not be his wife and that man. He held his breath and listened to the whispering voices.

"If I marry another man without any discussion with X, what will happen? Will he be disappointed? Or will he be happy..."

It was unmistakably the voice of his wife. Gentlemen, think about it, who could have endured and listened to the end of the words in this situation? The husband, X, who loved his wife, had fire in his eyes and his heart seemed to be torn to shreds. No matter how dignified Miss X was, how could he be told to endure any longer? Miss X finally opened the door wide with both hands. However, the two men and women sitting in the room showed no different expression from our Miss X's excitement. In Miss X's blazing eyes, first, he saw his wife bowing her head. Next, he looked around at the man who was trying to steal his wife.

"Hey, X! Why are you being so rash when you're not someone who would do that?"

Along with the voice that spoke first, to our extremely excited Miss X's burning eyes, he realized that he was his wife's older brother, that is, his brother-in-law, whom he had thought was stealing his wife. Gentlemen, gentlemen, if Miss X in this situation was you, what would you have done at this moment?

The clever Miss X stood there blankly and didn't say a word.

The boys, who had been somewhat interested in this scene from the beginning, all came rushing over. The pink sash, who had unknowingly sympathized with them, thinking that she would lick a Western dish or something, widened her puzzled eyes and waited outside the door for the end of this scene.

"Hey, X, isn't it past the wedding time?"

Turning around to the person who patted his shoulder along with those words, he saw that it was his father-in-law. Miss X's mother-in-law, wearing a somewhat embarrassed expression while smiling gently, followed next to him.

"Hey, without saying anything more, let's go home quickly. Hey, you go with X too!"

Miss X's brother-in-law was a very calm self-taught scholar. He opened his mouth again, wearing a meaningful smile as if such things often happen between young couples.

"But X! You have to think about your position. 『실혀요, 글세...학교 선생놈이 직원회를 합네 하고 기생년을 끌고 절ㅅ간에 가서...』

부인은 그만 분을 참지 못하여서 울음이 쏘다저 나왓다.

3, 憤怒極兮波瀾起
그것은 너무나 긔막킨 모욕이엿다.
남의 안해로서 목사님의 딸로서 어데가 그런 짓을 할 수 잇슬가. 그리고 그 때가 어느 땐데...담대한 하느님의 딸 아니 안해를 일허 바린 X씨는 그제야 墓鬼가 찟다 내 버린 壽衣와 가티 참담하게 된 자긔 양복 된 꼴을 나려다 보앗다. 그때서야 비로소 아모도 엇적지 못할 제의식으로 돌아 왓다.
내 한 일에 대한 보복이냐?
사랑에서 넘친 질투냐?
위협이냐? 경계냐? 모던-이냐?
그는 제가 당코난 모욕을 이러케 따저본다.
그러나 그럴사록 되려 그런 것이 하나하나 바늘 끗치되야 푸로페라뎍 속도로 그의 머리를 쑤시엿다.
벌에 몹시 쏘인 사람가티 머리가 웅얼웅얼 하엿다.
오로지 퓨어. 크리스챤으로서 힘써 얌전을 사수해 온 X씨에게는 너무나 큰 變이요, 경의가 안일 수 업섯다. 아인 밤중에 홍둑개도 분수가 잇섯다. 엇지 그까지 이르리라고야 누가 예측이나 하엿랴!
그러나 크리스챤으로써의 교양을 바더 온 그는 섬ㅅ돌 우에 쓰러지는 듯이 왈칵 주저안ㅅ자 흣트러진 머리를 두 손에다 파뭇고 다시 그의 안해가 한 짓에 대하야 크리스챤뎍 해석을 내려보려 힘써도 보앗다. 그러면-하고 그는 다시 자긔 행동을 반성해 본다.-그러면 내가 술을 먹고 계집하고 놀엇다고 벌을 준 셈인가? 내 안해의 손을 빌어서...또는 안해에게 불충실한 남편을 증계하자는 하나님의 뜻이엿든가?
아니다!
그는 힘잇게 머리를 엽흐로 흔들면서 이러낫다.
「하나님은 자비하시다. 회개하는 죄 지은 자를 용서하신다. 그리고 부득이한 일로 술을 먹엇대도 란잡한 대 이르지 안으면 고만일 것이 아니냐. 더구나 옷소매를 잡고 느러지는 계집을 뿌리치고 돌아선 것이라든지 담을 뛰여넘을 때 문고리을 잡을 때 간절히 회개하는 마음에서 조마둥이 친 가삼 속을 살펴섯다면 발-세 용서되엿서야 할 것이 아니냐!」
그는 이럿케 신통한 해답을 엇고 나서 다시 한번 머리를 엽흐로 흔들고 인제는 왼몸을 부르르 떨엇다. 그것은 초가을 새벽 바람이 차가운 까닭만은 아니리라.
이때 아모 말도 못하고 사위의 괴로워하는 양을 바라보고는 참아 위로할 말이 업섯든지 차차 밝어 가는 새벽하늘만 처다보고 무엇이라 중얼중얼 긔도를 올니고 섯든 장인이 X씨 서잇는데로 갓가히 와서 억개 우에 손을 언즈면서
「여보게 과히 번민하지 말게! 자비하고 거룩하신 하나님은 회개하는 자를 사하시나니...어서 회개하는 긔도를 올니세 자네 안해는 곳 도라오게 할 것일세. 자- 긔도를 드리세나...하나님 아바지 거룩한...」 그러나 그것은 이제＜93＞ X씨의 귀에는 허수아비의 잠고대보다도 흥미가 업는 말이엿다. 더구나 자긔의 딸이 저지른 허물은 뒤에다 감추고 회개하라는 심사가 몹시 마음에 거슬니엇다. 이리하야 노목사의 위로하는 부드러운 말은 드듸어 X씨의 바삭바삭하는 분노심에 기름을 끼연고 불을 놋는 결과를 낫코 말엇든 것이다. 이제야 X씨의 얼키인 감정은 제대로 풀여 나갈 결정뎍 길을 어덧다 「회개하는 긔도를 올여요? 웨 내가 무슨 잘못이 잇서 회개를 하나요, 모세의 7 誡命을 범한 당신의 따님께옵서나 회개를 하라시오. 나는 두 번 다시 무능하고 불공평한 하나님에게 긔도를 올니진 안을 터임니다.」 그의 목소래는 분노에 떨여 새벽 가섯시 초가을 하늘을 날카롭은 나사못 가티 파고 들어갓다. 그는 그 길로 실망과 놀남에 버러진 입을 다물지 못하고 오즉 물켜는 붕어입가티 뜸뜸이 「오-하나님- 악-마-가 당신-의 아들-을 -침노햇-나이다.」 하고 섯는 장인은 본체도 하지 안코 자긔 방으로-사실 처의 방-뛰여들어갓다.
얼마 뒤에 동복을 칙칙하게 밧궈 입은 X씨는 헌 내의ㅅ가지와 음악 전집 열권의 내용을 가진 그의 전 재산인 투렁크 한 개를 들고 차 시간이나 마처 나가는 사람처름 총총 걸음으로 뛰여나왓다. 이런 판에 자다 깬 장모가 속것바람으로 뒤따라 뛰여나왓다.
「아이구머니, 자네가 이게 왼일인가? 글세, 어데로 간단 말야? 이 꼭두새벽에...도대체 집안 망할 년도 잇지. 글세, 응 여보게 자네가 참어요 점!」 하고 장인과 두리서 붓들고 느러지는것도 뿌리치고 대문을 박차고 나왓다. 어듸서 그런 용기가 생겻는지-.
「당신네 딸은 초저녁이라서 그래 남의 사내하고 다라낫슴니다 그려!」
X씨가 나오는 마당에 대담하게 부르지즌 말이다. 하로ㅅ밤 돌발뎍 사건으로 말미암아 종교뎍 忍從心에서 해탈되야 예술가의 푸라우드한 긔벽으로 돌아 온 X씨는 아조 딴 사람 가티 되엿다. 품위가 놉고 대담한 사람이 되엿다. 이제 그의 시원스런 태도를 보고 누가 악까까지 검사 압헤 나선 죄수처름 고개를 들지 못하고 안해에게 하나님보다 더 위하든 양복을 갈갈이 찟기우던 그리스챤 X씨를 상상이나 할 것인가! 그러나 그의 마음이 돌변뎍으로 종교뎍 구속에서 버서난 원인은 달니 잇섯스니 원래 X씨는 두 가지 목적으로 신자가 되엿든것이다. 첫제는, 현재의 안해인 노 목사님의 령양을 안해로 삼을 수 잇는 행복과 둘제는, 서양 목사들을 친하고 신용을 보혀서 맨주먹으로 미국 류학을 해 보자는 엉큼한 목적과 포부?가 잠재해 잇섯든 까닭이엿다. 그리하야 충실한 큰리스챤으로 誤認을 바든 X씨는 모든 현대뎍 청춘의 방종한 욕망을 가슴속에 감춘데로 제1차적 욕망을 달하엿고, 이제부터는 제2차적 목적으로 한거름 한거름 공을 닥고 잇든 터에, 이 일이 생기고 보니 인제는 萬事休矣다. 그럴 바에야 더 구속 만흔 신자뎍 심리에 머뭇그리고 잇슬 까닭이 업슬 것 가탯다. 그러고 그는 아즉도 청춘이엿다. 예술가엿다.
모든 희망과 목적이 一朝에 깨트러지고 말엇슬 때(적어도 그 때 생각으로는 그는 필연뎍 귀결로 청춘을 차즈려 하엿고 오래 억눌닌 마음은 예술가의 본질로 도라오려 하엿든 것이다.
「흥! 내가 멀정하게 병신 노릇을 하고 지내 온 일을＜94＞ 생각하면... 그러나 인제는 어림도 업다. 긔막힌 모-던 풍을 좀 보혀줄걸 어데 견듸여 보아라!」 종로 뒤ㅅ골목 엇던 불 안땐 려관방에 드러 누어서 뜬 눈으로 그 날밤을 새면서 우리 경애하는 X씨는 이제 배반한 안해를 마음껏 골여 줄 교묘한(제 생각으로는)트릭을 생각하노라고 전전반측을 하엿다.
이튿날 아침 쓸쓸한 려관방에서 눈을 뜬 X씨는 무슨 생각을 하엿는지 아침밥을 먹기가 밧부게 뻐스를 잡어 타고 한강으로 나갓다. 한강의 속시원하게 푸른 물결이 X씨에게 새삼스래 생의 환희를 아르켜 주면서 흘너가는 듯하엿다.
그는 뻐스에서 내리자 닷자곳자로 어젓게 놀던 內外酒店으로 쓱 들어섯다.
「아이구머니 무슨 바람이 부럿세요? 아침부터 이게 왼 일이세요?」
언제ㅅ적부터 친햇다고...십년구지나 되는 듯이 계집애들은 반가워 못 견듸는 신늉을 한다.
손목을 잡고 등을 밀고 잡어 다리는데로 X씨는 방으로 끄을여 들어갓다.
「약주를 가저와요, 맥주를 가저와요?」
안ㅅ기가 밧부게 얌치 모르는 계집들은 졸나대엿다.
「아이참 내 허리띄 가저 갓섯지 이 냥반이! 응 그럿치언이?」
「그래 그래! 이 냥반이야! 그 대신 뭘 사 달내라 너.」
「그래 여보! 선생님 뭘 사다 주실너오. 그래도 오미야게로 뭘 사 오섯는지 누가 아나...사왓건 내노세요 얼핏!」
하면서 허리띄 밧군 게집애는 멋도 모르고 손을 내민다.
「야 그것 때문에 무슨 야단이 낫는지 아니? 이건 괜히 남의 속도 몰으고 이러는 구나...」
「아이 가엽서라 옥상헌테 꾸중을 들으신게로군!... 그래서 아침마닥에 그걸 밧구러 오섯세요? 가와이 봇짠네 안따!」
그래도 오날의 X씨에게는 그말이 노엽게 들니지가 안엇다. 오히려 그들에게 인생의 적막한 철학이 숨어잇는 것도 갓햇다. 내 누이와 가티 귀여운 생각까지 낫다.
