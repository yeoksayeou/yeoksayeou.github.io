별건곤 제32호
1930년 09월 01일
探偵小說 人肉 속에 뭇친 夜光珠 (Detective Novel: The Gem Hidden in Human Flesh)
L.G. 삐-스톤
소설 (Novel)

Before getting to the main story, I'd like to briefly talk about my background. I was originally a jewel thief. However, after being caught by the police in a certain incident, I reformed and have been faithfully working as a subordinate of the detective "Jaeger-Mann."

Since I was originally a jewel thief, I have a lot of knowledge and experience about jewels, so I have used that to arrest many jewel thieves. This story is one of them.

Seeing the business card "Mr. Cadman" brought in by the servant, I immediately thought, "Huh, he must have come because of the Minus case." However, I didn't want to meet Cadman. To be honest, I didn't want to meet him. It was because of an unpleasant memory from three years ago... a gang of four jewel thieves. A man named Mr. Cadman was one of them, and then there was Akton. Does.

Before I was caught by the police, reformed, and became a subordinate of the detective Jaeger-Mann, I was the most depraved of the four. And, it's a bit embarrassing to say, but I had a considerable affection for Cadman's beautiful face.

In the three years that passed, I had forgotten him without realizing it. But suddenly receiving his visit felt like reopening an old wound, so I didn't want to meet him.

However, on the other hand, thinking that he must have come about the Minus diamond case, I also wanted to meet him a bit.

It was just a few weeks ago that someone had stolen the diamond secretly kept by the famous Colonel Minus.

Because the diamond was famous and valuable, the police's Jaeger-Mann detective was searching day and night and also asked me for help. But for some reason, the forgotten diamond disappeared as if it had sunk to the bottom of the endless sea. So, I was also very anxious, and although it was unpleasant, I decided to meet Cadman. <154>

When he entered the room, an erotic scent stung my nose, and his face, veiled in thin veils from the brim of his hat, was as attractive and seductive as before.

"Is there anything special?" I asked very gently and kindly. He looked back at the door cautiously and said,

"I came to see you about the Minus diamond case. You are trying to find that diamond now, aren't you? But stop that futile effort. No matter how hard you try, it will never return to your hands." His voice was filled with hostility and resistance and gradually rose. I pretended to be calm and lit a cigar that hadn't gone out, asking:

"Perhaps you are right. But it is very unexpected to hear such advice from you? Isn't it unnecessary to come all the way to an old comrade with something like that?"

"I came to advise you not to make a futile effort," he said in a nonchalant tone, looking straight at me.

"What is the reason... I mean?" I asked again.

"Yes, let me tell you... Do you know who stole the diamond? We stole it. I can't tell you all the details, but it is true that I have it in my possession. It's definitely not a lie. It took a lot of effort and I went through many difficult things to steal it."

"We? So you didn't steal it alone, but with a comrade? An old comrade..."

"I've already cut ties with my old comrades. This time, it was with a new comrade. You probably know this new comrade too: Leon. Leonsack."

Hearing this, I unconsciously frowned. Seeing that, he smiled victoriously and spoke again.

"Of course, you know Leonsack, don't you?"

"Well, I've only heard his name. If I were the former, pre-reformation Akton. Edos, I might have taken off my hat and paid my respects just by hearing his name. Anyway, you've found a good comrade. He's so skillful and bold that you could call him the King of Crime. But watch. It won't be long before that big fish gets caught in the police net."

At this, he laughed loudly. His laughter contained mockery that pinched my nerves. Not to be outdone, I spoke sarcastically.

"So you're saying that because the great enemy Leon. Leonsack participated in this case, you can't possibly find the Minus diamond? You came all the way to tell me that? How very kind of you, isn't it?"

"No. That's not it. You're not one to give up, and there's no need to. Originally, I was the one who came up with the idea to steal that diamond... By the way, you're getting married to Colonel Minus's daughter, aren't you?"

I was startled. Where on earth did this woman hear that news? I stared at his face. Strangely, a youthful complexion rose on his face.

"Tell me more. I don't know what you mean."

"Then let me tell you. We stole the diamond and felt very relieved. But hearing that you were trying to find the diamond, we couldn't just sit still. Because if it's you, you'll definitely find it. But then you'll have to die. That pains me. So I'm asking you to give up the search. Or rather, to turn the police's direction to another area. If you don't grant this request, I'll tell your fiancé, Colonel Minus's daughter, that you're a former convict."

He said this, but avoided my gaze and lowered his head. Then, he raised his head again and said in a suddenly defiant voice,

"You wouldn't care if I did that, would you?!" I made a firm decision.

"Yes, I don't care. Once I've made up my mind, I don't care about any threats."

"So you don't believe what I've said?"

"That's right. Such words don't reach my ears."

"So you won't listen even after I've said this much? But don't regret it later," he said, trying to go out the door. I hurriedly called him back and said,

"Wait a moment. I'll tell you that you've already been caught in our hands. You confessed to me that you stole the Minus diamond, didn't you? Especially in front of a detective's subordinate... What if I call the Jaeger-Mann detective right now and have you arrested?" I pressed.

"Yes, do as you please. But then you'll be in despair just like me."

"Thank you. But I am not ashamed of myself at the moment. If you're going to send a letter to Colonel Minus's house, wait until tomorrow morning and call first... Now, let me call Miss Millicent."

I called the Colonel's house and asked for my fiancé, Miss Millicent. In the meantime, which was only about thirty seconds, he was trying to appear calm, but his face was pale. In that short time, Miss Millicent came to the phone.

"Hello. Is this Miss Millicent? Yes, a woman has come to me now and says she has something to say to you, so please wait a moment." I turned to him and said,

"Miss Millicent is here. Say what you have to say." and handed him the receiver.

He took the receiver. His hand was shaking strangely. Then he put the receiver to his small ear. I quietly smoked a cigar, pressing down on my pounding chest. At that moment, a cracking sound was suddenly heard, and

"Ah! You are... You are cowardly," he cried out. He was writhing and agonizing as if he was about to collapse. I reached out to support him, but he tightly grabbed my wrist. He opened his lips, which had turned as pale as marble, and muttered to himself,

"I... I..."

In this moment of wonder—the moment when his agony and mine reached their peak—I wondered if he was just staging a play to interfere with the engagement established between me and Miss Millicent. Feeling intense confusion, I said coldly to him, who was raising himself from my arm,

"Well done. But I'm not someone who can be swayed by such a play." Then he pleaded with tears in his eyes,

"Please, please save me. Please save me and lead me to the right path like you. There's nowhere else I can turn to for help."

He pressed his neck with his gloved hands and cried out. I couldn't say anything.

I fully understood what his words meant. Thinking about it made me shudder. But he continued to mutter and speak.

"You have emerged from the pit of sin. You have left me alone in that pit of sin. Save me too. No matter how much I struggle, I can't do it with my own strength. The surrounding forces are too strong. Do you know how much I'm suffering? A past embroidered with sin, a present full of falsehood, a future without light, ah! What should I do? I'm now surrounded by constant fear, and at night, even when I'm not dreaming, I hear the sound of the prison door creaking open and the footsteps of the guards." He covered his face with both hands as if trying not to see the terrible illusion. Emotions that I had forgotten for a long time strangely began to stir again in my heart.

"Then why don't you just leave that place?" I asked. Of course, I knew it was a commonplace thing to say. <157>

"Is that... is that what you have to say? I'm drowning right now. You have to lend me a hand."

"But I can't do anything."

He looked at me with mournful eyes, then staggered and walked away, saying,

"I'm leaving." His voice was chilling and mournful in my ears.

"Goodbye. We were born under a bad star."

He shed another stream of tears down his cheeks. My heart was endlessly sad.

"But no matter how hard you try, you won't be able to find the Minus diamond," he said to me, who had gone to the door. "Something very strange has happened to that jewel. Leonsack outwardly runs a photography studio. He's very skilled in photography. I can't tell you the exact location, but it's about a mile from here. Anyway, the glass roof of Leonsack's photography studio was leaking. So the owner of the house called workers to fix the roof, and one day a guest came to find Leonsack. At that time, he was carrying the diamond on his person. But no matter how he looked at the guest, he seemed like a detective, so he didn't know where to hide it. Just then, an unexpected incident happened. A worker working on the glass roof slipped and fell into the studio, causing a large wound under a stranger's armpit with a piece of glass. As you may know, Leonsack is good at surgery. So he rushed over, laid the injured man in another room, and stitched up the wound. While stitching up the wound, he thought that the guest in the reception room was definitely a detective, and he was trying to hide the diamond. Suddenly, he thought of wrapping the diamond in gauze and putting it inside the man's wound. Of course, the man had fainted at that time."

As I listened to this story, I looked at his face with surprised and suspicious eyes.

"That's a very strange story."

"Well, I'm not saying you have to believe it. I'll leave it to your judgment whether you believe it or not..." He seemed a bit displeased that I was half-doubting him.

"Anyway, what happened next?"

"Not long after, the worker regained consciousness and was very surprised to hear that he had been hurt by glass. But because he had already been operated on, it didn't hurt too much, so he decided to come back the next day for another treatment and went home. Leonsack was finally relieved and came to the guest who was waiting in the reception room, but in reality, the guest wasn't a detective at all."

"And... the worker...?" <158>

"The worker disappeared and can't be found anywhere."

"Huh?! What happened?!"

"Either he discovered that the diamond was inside his wound... or else..."

"But is it possible for a person to disappear on their own?" I shouted unconsciously at the very strange story.

"But he really disappeared. No matter how much Leonsack turns his eyes and searches, he can't find him. But in my opinion, it seems like that person discovered the diamond. Leonsack confidently says that can't be the case. According to him, the surgery to hide the diamond in the wound was so skillful that no one would know... But the problem can only be solved if that person is found. If possible, you should try to find that person before Leonsack does."

"It's a very interesting case," I said, genuinely feeling interested. "Of course, it's not a lie, is it?"

"Of course! But Leonsack is a dangerous person, so avoid getting too close to him, and don't do what I said earlier about reporting to the police."

"I swear here that I will never do anything to trouble you."

At this, he quietly looked at my face, then left without saying a word.

Since then, I haven't been able to meet him again. His beautiful face still lingers in my eyes, but... I think he has given up the shadow of empty happiness.

The detective Jaeger-Mann clenched his fists and shouted,

"Leon. Leonsack is the king of crime. There is no one in the current police force who can skillfully catch that electric eel-like fellow."

"He's a person who slips away skillfully, just like you said," I said, smiling. "So what are you going to do?"

I told the detective everything about meeting Mattle, but I couldn't bring myself to tell him his name.

The detective thought hard and said,

"It's not an easy task. It wouldn't be easy for Leonsack either... If he were an ordinary thief, he would have been caught a long time ago, but his actions match his words. But how could such a meticulous and skillful fellow have missed that worker?!"

"He missed him because it was a dangerous situation."

"That worker must have discovered that he had a diamond like that inside his flesh... so he didn't say anything and secretly ran away, thinking it was lucky to have such a windfall..." <159>

"That's probably the case."

"But Leonsack won't give up... He'll dig up the ground and find him... He'll probably use newspaper advertisements."

"No, Leonsack wouldn't be that rash," I objected.

"But isn't there any other way? I should check the newspaper advertisements from now on... Leonsack is a bigger fish than the Minus diamond..."

"Feel free to do as you please, but it will probably be a futile effort."

In fact, Jaeger-Man detective failed. A week later, when I went to see him, he was only grumbling about failing. I told him about my plan.

"Listen to my plan. A big fish like Leonsack won't be caught without bait. So instead of waiting for their advertisement, we should place an advertisement."

"Place an advertisement?"

"It's nothing special. Just a short advertisement looking for the worker who fixed the roof... The address and name are not clear, but he disappeared on a certain day of a certain month—that is, the day the jewel was lost... And the worker was seriously injured after falling from the roof of a certain photography studio, and since then, he has disappeared. If anyone knows him, they will be rewarded for finding him... If we place an advertisement like this, Leonsack will see it, won't he? After seeing it, he'll think, 'The person who placed this advertisement is related to the Minus diamond,' and he'll come looking out of curiosity, won't he?"

"That's right, that's right... What you say makes sense... Then who is the advertiser?"

"Put it in my name."

"Okay, okay... If he sees the advertisement, he'll be curious and come looking. Now, let's put the advertisement in the evening edition starting tonight."

"Yes... So he might come looking or send a subordinate, but anyway, before the meeting, I'll call you. If we're lucky, we can arrest him right away."

"That's very clever. That's right..." the detective jumped for joy.

"You have to come right away when I call. It's a big adventure." We sat down together and worked hard to write the advertisement and put it in that day's evening edition.

Several days passed, but there was no news. We still continued to place the advertisement.

Indeed, about a week later, the person in question, Leonsack, suddenly came to find me. Fortunately, I was at home, so I suppressed my racing heart and called the Jaeger-Man detective at the police station. <160> But coincidentally, the detective was not at home and would not be back for another forty or fifty minutes.

I had no choice but to leave a message asking him to come as soon as he returned and hung up the phone. I tried to drag out the meeting time with him as much as possible.

A little later, I heard his footsteps, and as soon as his figure appeared before me, my blood was racing as if it would burst.

He was a small gentleman with a neat beard and bright eyes like a clear sky. No matter how I looked at him, there was nothing particularly different from ordinary people.

"You've waited too long," I said as politely as possible, trying to be as friendly as possible, and offered him a chair.

"Yes, that's fine," he replied politely, but didn't try to sit down. Instead, he looked at my face strangely, as if something was off. He must have been watching my movements since the first day the advertisement was published.

"I saw the advertisement you placed..." he said, touching his beard, and then abruptly stopped what he was about to say.

"Yes, did you come after seeing the advertisement?" As I was answering, the phone rang. Could it be Jaeger-Man detective? If it was him, he wouldn't hesitate to call or delay.

In that situation, I couldn't turn my back to Leonsack, so I didn't answer the phone. Noticing that, he smiled slyly and said,

"The phone is ringing," drawing my attention to it.

"It doesn't matter. Let it ring."

He smiled again.

"Is that so..." he said, putting both hands in his coat pocket and speaking as if it were nothing. "I think I've seen you somewhere before?"

"That's certainly possible," I replied.

"I think I saw you at my photography studio."

"That's probably right."

"Weren't you the one who slipped and fell from our roof back then?"

"Yes... I was very embarrassed back then."

"Yes, yes! I remember. You disguised yourself as a worker and came into my house..."

"That's right. The worker back then was me."

He said, "Uh..." as if he was out of breath. The intense ringing of the phone had already stopped at that time.

"So, how is the wound from back then?"

"Thanks to your concern, it has healed completely."

"That's good. I was very worried and went looking for you. You had a beard back then, didn't you? Looking at you now, you look 10 years younger... By the way... Does the wound not bother you at all now? Is the skin stiff at all?"

"It's slightly not, but after getting a simple surgery again, it's completely fine."

"Uh?! Completely fine!?"

"Here, look," I said, taking a piece of paper from my pocket and handing it to him.

As he lowered his gaze onto the piece of paper, the door handle moved lightly again. The detective has arrived... I thought to myself, relieved.

Leonsack suddenly raised his voice and began to read.

"I, Doctor John. Getting, at the request of Mr. Akton. Does, operated on a small wound according to the instructions of Mr. 씨 and took out one diamond from it..."

He dropped the piece of paper from his hand. His eyes were blazing with anger.

"At the request of Mr. Does... So you knew there was a diamond inside it, didn't you?"

"Yes... at one point..."

"Uh... that woman told you! It was that woman, wasn't it?"

"I absolutely cannot say who it was."

"Then I will take your life."

At that moment, I saw a pistol flash from his hand inside his pocket.

"Don't do that, calm down... Our teacher, Jaeger detective subordinate, is waiting behind you now." At these words, he growled and lunged. At the same time, eight strong arms grabbed his arms and put handcuffs on him.

An hour later, I handed the detective the yellow Minus diamond. Without Getting's certificate and the armpit wound, no one would believe my story.

In fact, I had suspected Leonsack even before Mattle came to tell me the story. Therefore, when I saw Mattle, who was working with him, come to visit, I guessed that he had come about the Minus diamond case.

Before, I had been trying to somehow search Leonsack's house, but fortunately, I heard that he was fixing the glass roof, so I disguised myself as a worker and jumped in. Then, my foot slipped and I almost died, and fortunately, the diamond ended up in my flesh. I hadn't even thought about it in my dreams until Mattle came to tell me the story.

원 줄기로 들어가기 전에 나의 내력을 잠간 이약이 하려 한다. 나는 원래 보석 도적 이엇섯다. 그런데 어느 사건으로 경찰의 손에 잽혓다가 개심을 한 뒤로부터는 도리어 『자거-맨』 탐정의 부하가 되야 츙실하게 일을 보아오는 중이다.
내가 본시 보석 도적이엇든 만큼 보석에 대하야 지식과 경험이 만흠으로 그것을 리용하야 만흔 보석 도적을 테포한 일이 잇다. 이 이약이도 그 중의 하나이다.

하인이 가지고 들어온 『마-틀.캐드맨』이라는 명함을 보고 나는 『흥 마이나스 까닭으로 왓고나』하고 즉각 하엿다. 그러나 나는 캐드맨을 맛나보지 아니 하려고 하엿다. 사실 말하면 맛나보고 십지가 아니하엿다. 그것은 불쾌한 3년전의 기억...4인조 일단의 보석 전문의 도적이엇섯다. 마-틀.케드맨 이라는 자가 그 중의 하나이엇섯고 악튼.도-에스라는 게 나엇섯다.
경찰의 손에 잽히어 개심을 하여 가지고 자거-맨 탐정의 부하가 되기 전의 나는 그 4인조의 타락한 중에도 제일 타락된 사람이엇섯다. 그리고 좀 붓그러운 말이나 나는 캐드맨의 아름다운 얼골에 적지 아니한 정을 끌니고 잇섯다.
3년이나 지나는 동안에 나는 그를 잇는줄 몰르게 이저바렷다. 그런데 갑작히 그의 방문을 밧고 나니 묵은 상처를 닷치는 것 가터서 나는 그를 맛나지 아니 하려고 한 것이다.
그러나 한편 생각하면 그는 분명히 마이나스 야광주 사건으로 왓느니라고 생각하니 좀 만나보고도 십혓다.
그 때에 한참 유명하든 마이나스 大佐가 비장하여 둔 야광주를 누구인지 훔처가 바린 것이 바로 멧주일 전 일이엇섯다.
그 야광주가 유명하고 갑시 만흔 만큼 경찰의 자거맨 탐정은 밤낫 업시 수색을 하면서 나에게도 그 조력을 식히엇다. 그러나 웬일인지 이저바린 야광주는 끗치 업는 바다미테 가려 안진 것처럼 종적이 묘연하엿다. 그래서 나도 마음이 매우 초조한든 판이라 불쾌는 하지먄 캐드맨을 맛나 보기로 햐엿다.＜154＞

『자량대로 해보십시요만은 헛수구일 것 갓슴니다.』
사실 자-거맨 탐정은 실패를 하엿다. 일주일이 지나 그를 차저가니까 실패를 햇다고 투덜거리기만하엿다. 나는 그에게 나의 게책을 이약이하엿다.
『내 게책을 들어보십시요. 레온삭크가튼 큰 고기는 믹기가 업시는 낙거지지 아니함니다. 그러니까 저편의 광고를 가다리는 것 보담도 우리가 광고를 내이는게 좃슴니다.』
『광고를 내이다니?』
『별 것이 업서요. 집웅 곳치든 인부를 찻는 짤분 광고만 내이면 그만임니다...주소 성명이 분명치 못하나 어느 달 어느 날- 즉 보석을 이저바리든 날로부터 행방불명이 된 것으로 하고...그러고 그 인부는 어느 사진관의 집웅을 고치다가 밋그러저서 큰 부상을 당햇는데 그 뒤로 간 곳이 업스니 아는 사람이 그를 차저주면 사례를 하겟다고...이러케 광고를 내여노면 리온삭크가 볼 것이 아님니까? 보고는 하하 이 광고를 내인 사람은 마이나스 야광주에 관해서 인연이 잇는 사람이로구나 생각하고 호기심으로 차저올 것이 아님니까?』
『올타 올타...자네 말이 그럴 듯 허이...그러면 그 광고주는?』

『돼서 돼서...광고를 보면 궁금해서라도 차저올테란 말이야. 자-그러면 오늘 저녁 석간부터라도 광고를 내이지』
『네...그래서 그가 차저 온다든지 부하를 보낼지도 몰느나 하여간 면회를 하기 전에 선생님한테 전화를 걸어놋키로 하지요. 잘하면 그 당장에 테포할 수도 잇슬테니까요.』
『그것 참 썩 묘-하다. 그럴거야...』하고 탐정은 조아서 날뛰엿다.
『내가 전화를 걸면 곳 오서야 함니다. 큰 모험이니까요.』 우리는 마조 안저 애를 써가며 광고문을 작성하여가지고 그 날 석간에 광고를 내엿다.
멧칠이 지나갓스나 아모런 소식도 업섯다. 우리는 그래도 게속해서 광고를 내엿다.
과연 일주일쯤해서 돌연 문제의 인물인 리온삭크가 나를 차저왓다. 다행히 나는 집에 잇섯슴으로 뛰는 피를 억제하면서 경찰서의 자-거맨 탐정에게로 전화를 걸엇다.＜160＞그런데 맛침 탐정이 집에 업고 사오십분이 지나지 아니하면 돌아오지 아니한다는 대답이 왓다.
나는 할 수 업시 돌아오는 대로 곳 와 달나는 부탁을 하여 노코 전화를 끈엇다. 나는 될 수 잇는대로 그와의 면회 시간을 길게 끌녀고 하엿다.
조곰 잇다가 그의 발자국 소리가 들니고 바로 이어 그의 정체가 내 압헤 나타날 때에는 나의 혈관에는 터질뜻이 피가 뛰놀앗다.
그는 조고만한 신사로 수염이 곱고 눈은 맑은 하눌과 가티 영롱하엿다. 아모리 보아도 보통 사람보담 특별히 달은 점이라고는 업섯다.
『넘우 오래 기다리섯슴니다.』하고 나는 될 수 잇는대로 은근히 인사를 하고나서 의자를 권하엿다.
『네 좃슴니다.』하고 그도 은근히 대답은 하나 안지려고는 아니하엿다. 다만 그러고 무엇인지 이상스러운 듯이 나의 얼골을 끄윽 바라보앗다. 그는 분명 광고가 처음나는 날부터 나의 행동을 감시하고 잇섯든게다.
『그 광고를 내신 것을 보앗는데...』하고 수염을 만지면서 하려든 말을 급히 끈헛다.
『네 그 광고를 보고 오섯슴니까?』 이러케 대답하는 판에 전화가 울엇다. 자-거맨 탐정이나 아닌가? 그일 것 가트면 전화를 건다든가 꾸물거릴 리가 업섯다.
그 때 경우에 나는 리오삭크에게 등을 향할 수가 업섯슴으로 전화를 밧지 아니하엿다. 그것을 눈치채엿는지 그는 숭글스럽게 우스면서
『전화가 왓슴니다.』하고 주의를 식혀주엇다.
『관게치 안슴니다. 두어 두십시요.』
그는 또 싱끗 우섯다.
『그럿슴니까...』하고 그는 외투 포켓 속에 두 손을 집어 너코 아모러치도 아니한 듯이 이약이를 하엿다. 『나는 어데선지 당신을 한 번 본 듯 한데요?』
『확실히 그럿슴니다.』하고 나는 대답하엿다.
『나의 사진관에서 본 듯한데요.』
『물론 그럴 것입니다.』

『네... 그때야말로 경을 톱톱히 첫슴니다.』
『그래 그래! 알엇다. 인부로 가장하고 나의 집에 들어 온...』

『그래 그 때의 상처는 엇지 되엿슴니까?』

『약간 그러치 아니한 것도 아닌데 다시 간단한 수술을 밧고 나서는 아모러치도 안슴니다.』

한 시간 뒤에 나는 탐정에게 노-란 마이나스 야광주를 내어 주엇다. 게틩 의사의 증명서와 겨드랑의 상처가 업스면 아모도 나의 이약이를 정말이라고 밋지는 아니할 것이다.
사실 나는 마-틀이 와서 이약이를 하기 전부터 레온삭크를 의심하여 왓다. 그럼으로 그와 공부하고 잇는 마-틀이 방문온 것을 보고 그가 마이나스 야광주 사건으로 온 것이라고 짐작한 것이다.
전부터 나는 어터케 해서라도 레온삭크의 집안을 뒤저 보려고 하든 차에 다행히 유리 집웅을 곳친다는 말을 듯고 인부로 가장하고 뛰여들어간 것이엇섯다. 그러다가 발이 밋그러저서 하마트면 죽을번 하엿고 그것이 또 다행으로 내 살 속에 야광주가 긔어 들어오게 된 것이 엇섯다. 그것도 마-틀이 와서 이약이를 해 주기 전에는 꿈에도 생각치를 못하엿든 것이다.＜162＞
