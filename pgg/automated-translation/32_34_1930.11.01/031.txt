Sad Comfort

When the heart aches, wealth is nothing more than a sad comfort. (Ibsen)

Fate

It is unacceptable to think of fate as only connected to death and disaster. (Maeterlinck)

Terror of Life

The terror of life in a human's lifetime is already mixed with the sacred joy of life from the early days of youth. (Baudelaire)

Labor and Thought

Labor is life. Thought is light. (Hugo)

Consumption and Right

Just as we do not have the right to consume happiness without creating it, we do not have the right to consume wealth without creating it. (Shaw)

Slave of Fate

Inferior humans are not born with the power to judge themselves. Therefore, fate delivers judgment to them. They are always slaves to a harsh fate. (Maeterlinck)

Think Separately

One must think separately about the ethics of mankind and the forms that mankind possesses in its daily life. (Gourmont)

Straight Line and Morality

A straight line is the shortest distance in morality as it is in geometry. (Rachel)

Great Comfort

At the time of his death, a poet will consider it a great comfort that he did not lift his pen against morality in his lifetime. (Nicolas Boileau)

What Tormented Me

What tormented me was not my sin, but my virtue. (Cicero)

Courage and Chastity

Vanity, shame, and among them, temperament, these three often become courage in men and chastity in women. (La Rochefoucauld)

If You Take Away Falsehood, Even Happiness

If you take away the falsehoods of an ordinary person's lifetime, you take away even their happiness at the same time. (Ibsen)

Better to Deceive Oneself Than Others

It is better to deceive oneself towards a friend than to deceive a friend. (Goethe)

Defect of Morality

Moral defect, that is a defect made by human hands. (Hugo)

Be Kinder to Your Heart Than Your Body

If you treat your heart more kindly than your body, you will not find even minor dissatisfactions distressing. (Rousseau)

One's Own False Coat

A person must wear his own clothes and tell his own lies. (Shaw)

Slaughter

We humans slaughter each other; that is certainly immoral. However, philosophy cannot remedy this. (Chekhov)

Virtue is a House on a Mountain

Virtue is like a gathering of clouds on a mountain. It cannot be hidden. We can hide our vices, even if only for a moment, if we are careful. But virtue is revealed outwardly. (Oscar Wilde) <19>

世界文豪警句集

슬푼 慰安
마음압흘 富는 한슬푼 慰安에지나지못한다.(인센)

運命
運命을 死와 災難만으로 連結해 생각하는 것은 不可하다.(메-텔링그)

生命의 恐怖
人間一生의 生活의 恐怖는 발서 靑春의 初期에서 生活의 聖스런 快樂과 混合되여잇다.
(보-드렐)

勞働과 思想
勞働은 生命이다. 思想은 光明이다.(유-고)

消費와 權利
吾人은 幸福을 만들지못하고서 그것을 消費할 權利가업는것과 가티 富를 만들지못하고 그것을 消費할 權利가업다.(쇼-)

運命의 奴隷
劣等한 人間은 自己가 自己를 審判할 힘을 타고나지 못하엿다. 그럼으로 運命이 그들에게 審判을 나리게된다. 그들은 언제든지 苛酷한 運命의 奴隷이다.(메텔링그)

로생각하라
人類의 道德論과 人類가 그 日常生活에 가지는 形式과는 로로생각하지 안으면안된다.(굴몬)

直線과 道德
直線은 幾何學에서와가티 道德에잇서서도 最短이다.(라헬)

큰 慰安
詩人은 臨終, 그의한 生涯에 道德에 反해서 붓을들지안한 것을 큰慰安으로 생각할 것이다.(니코라스ㆍ포아로-)

나를괴롭게한 것
나를괴롭게한 것은 나의 罪가아니요 나의 德이다.(시세로)

勇氣와 貞德
虛榮, 羞恥, 그中에도 氣質, 이세가지는 왕왕 男子에 잇서서는 勇氣가되고 女子에 잇서서는  貞德이된다.(라ㆍ로슈-)

虛僞를 앗스면 幸福지
普通人間에서 一生間의 虛僞을앗는다면 그와 同時에 幸福지 앗는것이된다.(입센)

남을속이는이보다 自己를
自己동무를 속이는것보다 동무에게 對하야 自己를 속이는 便이낫다.(케-테)

道德의 缺陷
道德的 缺陷, 그것은 人間의 손으로 만들어진  缺陷이다.(유-고)

몸보다마음을 親切히
몸보다도 마음을 親切히 대접해주면비록 사소한 不滿이잇드래도 조곰도괴롭게 넉이지는 안는다.(룻소)

自己의 거즈살
사람이란 제 衣服을입어야하고 제거즛말을 하여야한다.(쇼-)

殺戮
우리들 人間은 서로殺戮을한다 그것은 勿論不道德하다. 그러나 哲學으로서 이것을 救濟할수는업다.(체-홉)

美德은 山上의집
美德은 山우에서 운집과갓다. 그것은 숨길내야숨길수업다. 우리는 제 惡德을 注意하면 비록 暫間동안이라도-숨길수가잇다. 그러나 美德은 밧게들어난다. (오스카ㆍ-와일드)＜19＞
