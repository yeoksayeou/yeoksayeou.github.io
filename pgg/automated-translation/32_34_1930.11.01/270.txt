Tales of Ghosts from Around the World

German Ghost - Bed and Body with Severed Head

I. This is a story told by a doctor at the University of Freiburg in Germany. It is strange that there is such a ghost in Germany, where everything is rational.

II. The doctor said this: One summer, I went on vacation to the Black Forest with my wife. Everywhere you went, it was either a fir forest, a valley, or a place covered with large rocks. The sun seemed to set earlier than in Berlin. In fact, the woman I went with at that time was not yet my legal wife, but a female comrade, as we say in Germany. However, we were planning to get married as soon as I was settled, so it was a pleasant trip. I should also mention that the woman had an older brother who had gone missing two or three years before, and she was eager to meet him to ease her mind. Moreover, her parents had passed away early, and he was her only remaining relative.

So, one evening, as we were staying at the hotel, the hotel owner came running and said that a sick person had just arrived by car and was lying in the room below the hotel. He asked me to examine him briefly. The hotel we were staying in was in the mountains, right in front of the station. So, I took my stethoscope and went down to the room near the entrance, and there was a man lying on the bed, suffering.

He raised his trembling fingers and moved them over his head, <138> as if stroking his hair. His pale face, with a long beard, was trembling with convulsions.

Taking his pulse, I found that he had a high fever. So, I cut his hair with scissors, applied an ice pack, and gave him first aid. It seemed that he was either an alcoholic or had suffered some kind of head injury. After a while, the patient regained consciousness and opened his eyes, looking at me, who was at the head of the bed. His pupils were large and black. I explained that I was a doctor staying at the hotel and asked, "What happened?" The patient opened his eyes even wider and stared at me. Finally, he seemed relieved and said in a choked voice, "Thank you. My illness started two days ago," but I suspected he was lying. It seemed his head had been strange for much longer.

"I also came here to enjoy myself, but the other day, I suddenly lost consciousness in a forest and collapsed. Even now, I can't figure out the cause. It must be an illness. When I came to my senses, I was lying on a bed in a room that seemed to be a farmer's house. There was only a candle burning at the head of the bed, and nothing else. It was a terribly empty room. I thought that a farmer living nearby must have found me collapsed and put me in the empty room of his house. Anyway, every joint in my body ached and hurt so much that I couldn't bear it. I also had a high fever.

Then, suddenly, didn't I hear a sound like someone quietly tapping a hollow wooden crate next to my ear? It was tapping very carefully and quietly. I wondered what it was, but I didn't have the strength to pick up the candle and look. Then, the sound gradually became stronger, and the tapping became faster and faster. When I came to my senses, wasn't it the sound of someone tapping the bottom of the bed I was lying on? And it definitely sounded like someone was tapping it.

Under the bed...there's no place as unpleasant as this. Then, didn't I hear a rustling sound? I got goosebumps all over my body. It sounded like something was being pulled out from under the bed. I tried to scream, but no sound came out. Meanwhile, the strange sound became clearer and clearer. It seemed like someone was hiding under my bed and crawling out. I was so scared that my upper and lower teeth were chattering, but I managed to gather my courage and sit up. The candle at the head of the bed was burning steadily, as if it were made of wax. I held the candle in my left hand, grabbed the edge of the bed with my right hand, and leaned forward, putting my head on the dark floor and looking carefully under the bed.

And what did I see? It looked like someone was hiding there. When I looked closely, it was definitely a person.

Among them, it was the torso of a naked man. At first, I saw the shoulders. Not the head, but the shoulders without a head. It was a body with a severed head, covered in dark blue blood. I froze all over.

As I was about to lose consciousness and remain still, the headless torso gradually crawled forward. Not only the torso but also the two arms attached to it tightly were creeping towards me from under the bed like crab legs. <139> At that time, I didn't feel alive.

A torso without eyes or a mouth... Even in that deathly state, this thought came to my mind. This head might be a face I know. It might have cut off its head because it wanted to scare me so much. I don't know why I had that thought.

Then, the shoulders and arms had already crawled out from under the bed. This time, one of the arms moved invisibly, tapping the edge of the bed with its long fingers. Then, the naked torso rose up as if pulled by a string, and both arms reached out as if to embrace me, who was looking under the bed. Even if I tried to scream, I couldn't make a sound. Finally, it came up to the side of the candle I was holding, so I could clearly see where the head had been severed. The blood, which had initially looked black, was now dark red, and thick blood gushed out like a fountain as the torso rose up, streaming down over its shoulders. I screamed and threw the candle at the nape of its neck. When the light went out, the room was pitch black. Then, in the darkness, a cold, bony hand grabbed my head, and the sharp tips of those terrifying fingers pierced and dug into my head like awls!" The patient, panting and barely finishing his story, took a deep breath, as if he were thirsty, and opened his eyes wide.

"I don't remember whether I fell asleep after that or not. Anyway, when I came to my senses, it seemed to be dawn. I quietly opened the window, jumped out of the room, and ran as fast as I could to the train.... So, even on the train, my symptoms flared up, bothering the passengers, and eventually, I had to get off at a small station in the mountains."

I gave him a slightly larger dose of tranquilizers and told the hotel staff that I would examine and treat him again tomorrow. Then, I went back to my room.

I didn't tell my wife the details because I was afraid she would be scared...

The next morning, I woke up late because I was tired. Then, suddenly, my wife, who had woken up before me, burst into the room. My wife looked very flustered and said,

"Honey, about the patient we saw last night. We have to take him with us somewhere. With us. I just met him." Her face was pale, and it seemed to be a serious matter. I was annoyed and said,

"Don't talk nonsense. He's probably just a vagrant. Let the police deal with him. Why should we care?" Then, my wife started trembling.

"No. Please. Please take him with us when we leave today."

"What nonsense..."

"That-that patient is my older brother. I just heard it. That person is my older brother. He's a man who killed someone and ran away." As soon as she said this, my wife's face turned as white as porcelain and she collapsed in front of me. As she fell, she hit her forehead on the iron bedpost, and red blood gushed out of her fair forehead. <140> Three days later, I held a joint funeral for the patient, who was her brother, and my wife, who was his sister, and returned.

Chinese Ghost - The Soul of the Peerless Beauty Hwa Rye-Chun from an Old Tomb

This is a strange story that happened during the reign of Emperors Gyeongjong and Yeongjong of the Ming Dynasty in China, in the Cheonsun era.

It tells of a man named Chu-saeng from Gyeongwon County who met the soul of Hwa Rye-Chun, a concubine of King Dojong of the Song Dynasty, fell in sweet love, and vowed to be together for life. At that time, Chu-saeng was a handsome 21-year-old with exceptional talent in poetry. He had heard of the scenic beauty of Hangzhou and always hoped to visit it. One day, his wish came true, and he set out for Hangzhou with a servant.

Along the way, Chu-saeng visited famous mountains and great rivers, and toured major and minor temples, gathering inspiration for his poems. He heard that the scenery of Hoegye was also the best in the world, so he rode his horse to Hoegye. Indeed, he gazed upon the unparalleled scenery that everyone praised, and his heart was filled with satisfaction. It would have been difficult for Chu-saeng to describe the endless poetic charm and ecstatic feeling at that time.

Chu-saeng got off his horse as if in a dream, gazing at the surrounding scenery, and eventually lost his servant. Before he knew it, the sun had set, and the twilight was creeping in, as small and large birds returned to their nests. Only then did Chu-saeng realize that he had entered a deep valley, but it was too late to find his way out. As he was feeling lost and not knowing what to do, he happened to see a streak of white light flowing through the dense forest. Chu-saeng thought, "Perhaps there is a house there."

He was overjoyed and quickened his pace towards the light.

When he arrived, he unexpectedly found a splendid mansion. The house was surrounded by lush pine and bamboo, and the area was swept clean, without a speck of dust.

Chu-saeng was puzzled and stopped in front of the gate. After a while, a boy in a blue uniform appeared from the mansion and bowed politely.

Chu-saeng, thinking that there was no other way to turn back, asked the blue-clad boy for a night's lodging in the mountains. The boy told him to wait a moment, went inside, and soon came out again, leading Chu-saeng into the mansion. As Chu-saeng entered, he suddenly saw a beautiful young woman sitting in the main hall, elegantly dressed. The woman rose from her seat and greeted Chu-saeng politely. Chu-saeng was even more puzzled.

As he was about to sit down, fragrant tea was served, followed by a table of wine with delicious dishes placed before Chu-saeng.

"Who is the beautiful woman, the owner of this house, and why <141> is she welcoming me like this?" As Chu-saeng was thinking, the woman asked about Chu-saeng's origin and name. Chu-saeng answered clearly, then asked the woman about her identity.

The woman frowned slightly and told the story in detail.

"My surname is Hwa, my name is Rye-Chun, and I was born in Rimcheon. Since separating from my husband, I have been spending lonely days alone. I have made a resolution: if someone can write excellent seasonal palace poems (四季의 宮詞), I will not ask about their family background and will entrust myself to them. May I ask, do you have talent in poetry?"

"I have studied a little, but I don't know if I can write a poem that meets your expectations, but it might be good to try."

Chu-saeng readily agreed and immediately picked up a brush and effortlessly wrote seasonal palace poems.

Flowers bloom in the forbidden garden, the sun begins to shine
The long gate is deeply locked, the white day is clear
Silver screen leans sideways, spring sleep is fragrant
A nightingale sings on a willow branch

After reading these four verses, the woman was deeply moved and her face was filled with satisfaction. She smiled slightly and said,

"Your poems are truly excellent. As I said before, I want to entrust my life to you. Please have mercy on me and grant my wish."

At the woman's words, Chu-saeng felt like he was in a dream. Without time to think, he vowed his future with the woman amidst a mysterious atmosphere.

Thus, the night in the mountain villa deepened quietly. Chu-saeng was intoxicated with wine, wandering in a world of sweet dreams, and it seemed that he was hearing dreamlike music.

Thereafter, the two became inseparable, and the woman, Hwa Rye-Chun, never let Chu-saeng go out alone. Chu-saeng also could not bear to be apart from Hwa Rye-Chun for even a moment, enjoying the happiness of the mountain villa and repeating the days of pleasure. In this way, the joyful days were approaching a year.

However, one night, Rye-Chun's face was covered with a sorrow that had not been seen before, and she did not have a cheerful look and seemed to avoid even speaking. She sighed from time to time, and a sad look was evident in her eyes.

"Aha, you've already changed your mind. But there can't be another man in this mountain, so what is the reason?"

Chu-saeng was suspicious of Rye-Chun's behavior and, filled with an unknown anxiety, tried to ask her the reason. However, Rye-Chun already knew Chu-saeng's thoughts and said, "My lord! I wanted to serve you and enjoy these happy years for thousands of years, but these happy days cannot continue any longer. Heaven has punished me, and a terrible disaster is approaching. When this night turns bright, I will have to part with my lord, whom I have served for ten thousand years. If—if you think of me and stay here longer, you will suffer the same disaster as I do, so don't delay and quickly leave this mountain."

As Rye-Chun said this, tears welled up in her eyes, and her clear voice was filled with sorrow. However, Chu-saeng could not understand the meaning of her words and was simply listening blankly because it was so sudden. The destruction of joy! How could Chu-saeng let go of the woman's hand and leave? <142>

"Are you serious about what you just said? I don't understand what you mean by disaster. I am not afraid of disaster. How can I leave you alone? I want to save you from disaster, so please don't hide anything and tell me."

At Chu-saeng's words, Rye-Chun could only cry and said nothing. Chu-saeng comforted Rye-Chun with sincerity, and finally, Rye-Chun returned to her usual mood, but suppressing the unbearable sorrow, she sighed and recited a poem with a voice like tinkling silver bells, expressing her heartbreaking feelings of parting.

The beautiful qualities transform into orchids and fragrant earth
The elegance turns into a gauzy mist

At the sound of Rye-Chun's recitation, Chu-saeng wept silently. He cried and cried, and spent the night like this. It was when the light of dawn was rising from the east. Rye-Chun said to Chu-saeng, "We cannot stay here any longer. Please leave quickly," and urged him anxiously.

Should he leave? Should he stay? Chu-saeng, with a feeling of being caught and a lingering thought, bid farewell to Rye-Chun with tears and dragged his powerless legs out of the villa. He took one step, two steps, three steps, but it seemed that he was not moving forward but dragging backward. Chu-saeng looked back at the mountain villa, remembering the past pleasures.

Not long after he came down the mountain path, a mass of black clouds suddenly gathered, and the four sides became dark. Chu-saeng was surprised and hid in the thicket. Then, rain and thunder fell, and a clap of thunder seemed to pierce Chu-saeng's eardrums, and a flash of lightning blinded Chu-saeng's eyes.

After a while, when the weather cleared, Chu-saeng found his way again. He thought of Rye-Chun's house with longing. Chu-saeng hurried to the mountain villa and rushed to it, but the splendid mountain villa was gone without a trace. He could not even find a piece of tile. Only a pile of old tombs that had been destroyed by the thunder lay by the roadside, and the shattered skeletons were scattered everywhere. Only then did Chu-saeng awaken from his long dream, and a chill ran down his spine. Filled with a terrifying feeling, he rushed back to the old road.

Some time later, when Chu-saeng finally arrived home and told the story of what had happened, an old man who was listening exclaimed in surprise, "There is a grave of Hwa Rye-Chun there. She was a peerless beauty and a concubine of Emperor Dojong of the Song Dynasty. Perhaps her spirit appeared and played tricks on you."

Only then did Chu-saeng quietly think and remember that the beautiful woman had said that her former husband's surname was Jo and his name was Gi. Considering that, Emperor Dojong's name was Wi-sun, and that was the era's name.

There are tombs of the Song Dynasty in that mountain. Thinking about the events from beginning to end, it was clear that Chu-saeng had been seduced by the soul of Rye-Chun. However, Chu-saeng could not forget Rye-Chun's deep affection and resolved never to marry. He went to Cheontae Mountain and spent his life as a monk, thinking of Rye-Chun.

Russian Strange Novel - The Demon in the Earth: Gogol's "Viy"

This is a ghost story from a Russian horror novel. "Viy" is a representative work of Gogol's horror novels. "Viy" is a "Demon in the Earth" that has been passed down among the Little Russian people.

I. Three students from the Kyiv Theological Seminary - Khalyava, Khoma Brut, and Tsyberiy Gorobets - were on their way home for summer vacation. As it got dark, they asked to spend the night at a farmhouse they found late at night. The housewife, the widow, initially refused, but the three young scholars begged, saying that they would be grateful if she would let them escape the dew for one night, even in the corner, and that they would repay her sufficiently in the morning. The widow reluctantly agreed. However, she said that it would be too noisy to have young people in one room, and that her family would not be able to sleep, so she arranged for them to sleep separately. The event proceeded according to the housewife's wishes, and Khoma Brut was led to the goat shed. It didn't matter whether it was a goat shed or not. As Khoma was looking around the goat shed alone, the door opened and the old woman who had first guided him came in.

"Grandma, what's wrong?" he asked the landlady affectionately. The widow did not try to answer, but with her arms outstretched, she rushed towards Khoma. Khoma wondered what was going on and stepped back a little, but the widow kept rushing at him as if to embrace him. Khoma was at a loss and said,

"Grandma, aren't you a widow? And I'm just a young man. I can't defile myself even if you give me a thousand gold pieces." The widow still didn't say anything and grabbed Khoma's hand. Only then did Khoma realize that the widow's eyes were shining strangely, and he felt uneasy. So, he tried to send the widow back to her room, but she wouldn't move. Khoma tried to shake her off and run away, but she quickly shut the door. Then, with her eyes shining fiercely, she glared at him and rushed at him. Strangely enough, Khoma's hands and feet felt numb and wouldn't listen to him, and his voice got stuck in his throat. Just as the widow was grabbing Khoma and trying to touch his face to Khoma's, a cat jumped on Khoma's back, seemingly out of nowhere. All of this happened in an instant, and in the next moment, Khoma, with the cat on his back, was dragged out of the house by a terrifying force. Before his eyes lay a wide, flat valley, and on the other side, a forest like a screen stood in rows.

"This is a demon," Khoma realized. A crescent moon hung in the sky, casting a faint light. The forests, fields, and valleys all seemed to be asleep with their eyes open. There was not a breath of wind. Then, the moon grew as big as the sun, and the surroundings became like an ocean. He heard a strange sound that was like the sound of doves and the sound of bells. He began to recite a spell to ward off the demon. Suddenly, he felt refreshed, and the pressure of the demon on his back seemed to lessen. "I'm doing it right!" Khoma recited the spell louder. Then, the demon on his back jumped off and now sat on the widow's back. The widow was running away terribly fast, but Khoma picked up a piece of wood lying on the ground and hit the widow hard, telling her to die.

The widow groaned and collapsed on the spot. But when he looked closely, it was not the widow, but a dazzlingly young and beautiful woman. Not knowing what had happened, Khoma ran towards Kyiv as fast as he could.

II. Some time later, this rumor spread in Kyiv. The beloved daughter of a Cossack chieftain (豪族) living about 50 li from Kyiv had gone for a walk and was severely beaten by someone, which caused her death. Before she died, she had said in her will, "Call Khoma Brut, a student from the Kyiv Theological Seminary, to read scriptures for three nights."

Soon after, the Cossack chieftain sent a carriage to Kyiv to bring Khoma. Khoma heard about this from the seminary teacher and was happy that he would receive a generous amount of money from the Cossack nobleman. He set out to recite scriptures as requested in the will.

The Cossack nobleman was very pleased and greeted Khoma. "My daughter somehow knew you before she died, so she asked me to call you to recite scriptures for three nights after she died. Since when have you known my daughter?" he said with a smile. Khoma couldn't remember at all and didn't know what to say. It was a strange thing. Then, the Cossack brought Khoma to the room where his daughter's body was laid.

"I can't figure out who killed my daughter. If I knew, I would crush him immediately," he said, filled with the grief of a parent. Khoma sympathized with him deeply. Even so, what kind of woman was she? His curiosity began to stir. Taking advantage of the Cossack's absence, Khoma cast his eyes on the body lying there. It was a beauty so stunning that he doubted his own eyes. He wondered if such a beautiful woman had ever lived in the world. The contours of her face were clearly beautiful, her complexion was as white as snow, her eyebrows were as black and slender as a line drawn between the white day and the passing night, and her eyelashes cast a thin shadow on her cheeks. At the corners of her mouth, a smile like a flower bud seemed to overflow with happiness. However, as Khoma was looking at the dead woman's face, something like electricity zipped through his chest. That's right. Wasn't this woman the witch he had killed earlier? Suddenly, his face turned pale, and he recited a spell and left the room. Only then did Khoma hear from the people gathered in the house that the beautiful woman was indeed a witch and had sucked the blood of Mikita, a young child of the Cossack family.

III. The sun had set.

The coffin was moved to the temple. Three Cossacks took Khoma to the temple, left him alone in the temple, locked the door from the outside, and returned, leaving the words, "Have a safe service."

In the temple, in that wide, empty room, there was only one coffin containing the body of the beautiful witch. Night was gradually deepening. Khoma felt eerie and his whole body trembled. He lit all the candles he could find to make it brighter. The whole temple became bright, and he felt a little more secure, but he had no way to ward off the terrifying feeling that was welling up inside him.

"No, there's nothing to be afraid of. It's nothing," he tried to reassure himself several times. The witch lying in the coffin next to him was becoming more and more beautiful with each passing moment. She seemed completely alive. Ah! Tears are flowing from her eyes - flowing down to her cheeks - he thought, but it was not tears but blood. Khoma regained his senses and thought that this wouldn't do. He jumped onto the choir stand, spread out the scriptures feverishly, and began to read the scriptures in a loud voice that shook the temple. His voice strangely disturbed the quiet air.

"There's nothing to be afraid of. Demons fear the words of God. If I recite the scriptures, the witch won't be able to rise from the coffin," he told himself. He tried not to look at the coffin as much as possible, but he couldn't help but be drawn to it and glance sideways. "Look! The corpse has risen!" "No. Am I crazy? There's no way." "It might rise. What will I do if it rises?" He was burning inside with these thoughts, and his lips were parched, but the coffin didn't budge. The night grew deeper, and only the sound of the candles burning could be heard occasionally. "Ah, if the corpse rises," Khoma thought again, trying to ward off the terrifying thought that was welling up inside him. As he raised one hand and rubbed his eyes, the woman's corpse began to move in the coffin. She stood up. Then, with her eyes closed, she gradually approached Khoma's front as if searching for someone with her hands. His heart felt as if it had stopped beating. Still, he mustered all his strength and recited a spell to ward off the demon. The woman's complexion turned terribly blue, as if a person who had been dead for several days was turning blue. Her upper and lower teeth clicked together, and she opened her eyes wide, not seeing anything. She took one step, one step, one step closer to Khoma, but unable to withstand the power of the spell, she returned to the coffin. Only then did Khoma breathe out a sigh, but it was only for a moment. This time, the coffin split open with a deafening sound. It flew through the air as if blown by the wind, and at the end, it almost fell on Khoma's head, but he barely avoided it with the power of the spell.

Meanwhile, the sound of the first rooster crowing was heard in the distance. The witch went back into the coffin, and Khoma finished reciting the scriptures and returned. The same thing happened the following night. Khoma's hair had turned white in two nights.

Unable to endure any longer, he begged to stop reciting scriptures on the third night, but the Cossack insisted and refused to listen. He tried to run away but was caught. He had no choice but to perform the scripture reading on the third night. That night, the dead spirit summoned the demon in the earth, "Viy". With a thunderous sound, the temple was destroyed, and the giant goblin Viy appeared. Its face was like iron, and its hair was covered like a net. Its hands and feet were like the roots of a large tree, with lines running down them, and they were covered in dirt.

"Lift up my eyelids. I can't see."

"Viy" cried out in a voice that seemed to seep out from the ground. When the demons lifted the goblin's eyelids, "Oh, you're here!" Viy's iron claws lunged at Khoma.

Khoma lost his breath and collapsed on the spot. At that moment, the sound of a rooster crowing was heard in the distance. <146>

獨逸幽靈-寢臺 밋헤 목떨어진 몸동이
一. 이것은 獨逸 후라이뿌로그 대학연구실에서 엇던 딱터-가 한 이야기다. 모든 것이 합리적인 獨逸에도 이러한 유령이 잇다는 것은 한 기괴한 일이다.

露西亞奇怪小說-土中의 惡魔 고-고리의 「우이-」
이것은 러시아 괴긔소설 가운데 낫하난 유령이야기다. 「우이-」란 고-고리의＜143＞ 괴긔소설 가운데 대표적인 것으로 「우이-」란 소러시아 사람들 사이에 전해나려 오는 「土中의 惡魔」다.

一. 키에후 신학교 생도 세사람-하리야와 호-마·부루-와 치베리-·고로벳츠-는 여름방학이 되야 고향으로 돌아가는 길에 날이 저물어서 밤중에 차저든 엇던 농가에서 하로밤 자고 가기를 청햇다. 주부인 로부인은 처음에는 거절하엿스나 세젊은 학도들이 아구석에서나 상관이 업스니 하로ㅅ밤 우로를 피하게 해주면 답례는 내일 아침에 충분히 하겟노라고 간청함으로 로부인도 허는 수 업시 허락하고 말엇다. 그러나 쩖은 사람을 한 방에다 재웟다가는 분잡하여서 집안사람들이 잠을 잘 수 업다하야 따로따로 자게 마련하엿다. 일은 주부의 의견대로 진행이 되어서 우리 호마·부루-로는 염소깐에 안내를 바덧다. 염소깐이고 무엇이고 그 판이 문제가 아니엿다. 호-마는 혼자 떠러저서 이 염소깐을 살펴보고 잇슬 때 문이 열니면서 처음 안내해 주던 로파가 들어왓다.
「할머니 웬일이요?」하고 그는 여주인을 보고 귀염성스럽게 물엇다. 로파는 그 말대답은 하려고 하지 안코 팔을 벌인채 호-마를 향하야 닷자곳자로 달겨들엇다. 호-마는 이로파가 이게 왼일인가 하고 조곰 뒤로 물너 낫스나 로파는 그대로 달겨들어서 덥석 껴안을 듯이 함으로 호-마도 어이가 업서서
「할머니 당신은 과부가 아니요. 그러고 나는 이갓치 어린 사람이야. 천량을 준단들 더럽힐 수는 업서」 로파는 여전히 아모 말도 업시 그 손으로 호-마를 잡엇다. 호-마는 그 때야 비로소 로파의 눈이 이상하게 빗나는 것을 알고 꺼름한 생각이 낫다. 그래서 로파를 제 방으로 돌여 보내려 하나 로파는 움즉이지도 안는다. 호-마는 뿌리치고 다러나랴 하엿스나 로파는 재빠르게 문을 닷처 바렷다. 그리고는 무섭게 반작으리는 눈으로 그를 노려 보면서 달겨드는 것이엿다. 그런데 이상하게도 호-마는 손과 발이 쥐가 난 것가치 말을 듯지 안코 말소리도 목에 걸여 나오지를 안엇다. 로파가 호-마를 잡어서 그 얼골을 호-마에게다 다이고저 할 때에 어대서 뛰여 왓는지 괭이 한 마리가 호-마의 등에 뛰여 올넛다. 이런 일이 모다 일순간에 일어나서 그다음 순간에 호-마는 등에 괭이를 업은채 무서운 힘에 끄을여서 그 집을 나왓다. 눈압헤는 넓고 펑펑한 고로 산곡이 버러지고 건넌편에는 숫빗으로 평풍과 가튼 숲이 둘너싸고 잇것다.
「이것은 악마로구나」하고 호-마는 비로소 깨달엇다. 하늘에는 초생달이 걸여서 엷은 빗을 던지고 잇섯다. 숲이나 풀밧치나 산골들이 모다 눈을 뜬채 잠자고 잇는 듯 하엿다. 바람한 점 업섯다. 그러자 달이 해만치나 커지고 사방이 바다와 가티 되드니 비둘기 소리갓기도 하고 종소리갓기도 한 무슨 이상한 소리가 들니곤 하엿다. 그는 악마를 물니치는 주문을 외우기 시작하엿다. 그러자 갑작이 긔분이 경쾌해 지면서 등에 덥혀 잇는 악마의 압력이 감해지는 듯하엿다. 「올탓구나!」하고 호-마는 주문을 봅히 외엇다. 그랫더니 등에 덥힌 악마는 뚜여 나려서 인제 로파의 등에 올너 안젓다. 로파는 무섭게 빨리 도망질치랴는 것을 호-마가 길바닥에 떨어저 잇는 나무토막을 집어들고 죽어보라고 힘껏 로파를 내리 갈결다.
로파는 켁 소리를 지르고 그 자리에 잡버젓다. 그러나 자세히 보내 그것은 로파가 아니고 눈이 부시게 젋고 어엿분 미인이엿다. 호-마는 엇지된 영문을 몰나 걸음아 날 살니라고 키에후를 향해서 다름질첫다.

그러자 얼마가지 안어서 그 코싹크 호족은 호-마를 마저가려고 마차를 키에후에다 보내엿다. 호-마는 신학교 선생에게 그런 말을 듯고 코싹크 거부에게 사례의 돈이나 흠북 바들 것을 깃버하면서 그 유언대로 독경을 하려고 나섯다.
코싹크의 호족은 대단히 깃버하면서 호-마를 마젓다. 그래 「녀식이 죽을 때 엇전지 당신을 알고 잇스니 죽거던 당신을 불너서 사흘밤 독경을 하게 해 달나하엿는데 당신은 언제부터 내 딸하고 알게 되엿소」하고 웃는 것이엿다. 호-마는 도모지 생각이 나지 안어서 엇덧케 말할 바를 알지 못하엿다. 아무튼 긔괴한 일이엿다. 그러자 코싹크는 호-마를 딸의 시체가 노여 잇는 방으로 다리고 와서.
「내 딸을 죽인 놈을 도모지알 수 가 업서. 알기만 하면 그 놈을 당장 박살을 하고 십지만」하면서 비탄에 빠지는 어버이의 마음에 호-마는 저욱히 동정을 하엿섯다. 그럿타하더래도 대체 엇던 처녀일고? 그의 호긔심은 움직이기 시작하엿다. 코싹크가 밧그로 나간 틈을 타서 호-마는 그곳에 눕혀 둔 시체에 시선을 던지엿다. 그것은 제눈을 의심하리만치 아름다운 미인이엿다. 이러한 麗人이 일즉이 세상에 살어 잇섯든가 하고 의심할 지경이엿다. 얼골의 윤곽은 뚜렷이 아름다운 선을 그리고 살빗은 눈과 가티 희고 눈섭은 白日의 사이를 그으고 자나는 밤과 가치 검고 가늘고 속눈섭은 가즈런히 길게 뚜불우에 얇은 그림자를 던지고 입가장자리에서는 깃분 마음에 꼿봉우리가튼 미소가 넘처 흘으는 듯하엿다. 그러나 호-마는 그 죽은 여자의 얼골을 드려다 보고 잇는 가운데 짜릿! 하고 뎐긔와 가치 가삼밋을 쓰치고 지나가는 무엇이 잇섯다. 그럿타. 이 여자가 압서 그가 따려 죽인 마녀가 아니엿나. 그는 별안간 얼골이 파랏케 질너서 주문을 외우면서 그 방을 나왓다. 호-마는 그 뒤에야 이 집에 모힌 말을 사람들에게 그 아름다운 여자가 정말 마녀엿다는 것과 미키-타란코삭크의 어린 아히 목덕미를 물어 뜻고는 피을 빨어 먹엇다는 무서운 이야기를 들엇다.

三. 날이 저물엇다.
령관을 절(寺)로 옴기여 왓다. 세사람의 코싹크가 호-마를 절까지 다려다 주고는 혼자 그를 절에다 냅겨놋코 밧그로 문을 잡어 걸고는 「무사히 근행(勤行) 하시게」라는 인사 한마듸를 남기고 돌아가 바렷다.
절에는 그 넓우나 넓운 방에 어엿분 마녀의 시체를 담은 관 하나가 노혀 잇슬 뿐이엿다. 밤은 차차로 깁허간다. 호-마는 읏슥하고 왼몸이 떨니엿다. 밝기나 하면 나흘가하야 잇는 대로 초ㅅ불을 다 밝켓다. 왼절이 한-하게 되여 조곰은 든든한 생각도 나기는 하엿스나 무엇인지 치밀여 오는 무시무시한 마음을 물니 칠 재조가 업것다.
「아니다, 조곰도 무서울 게 업다. 아모것도 아니다.」하고 멧번이나 스사로 마음을 가다듬어도 보앗다. 엽헤 노힌 관 속에 누어 잇는＜145＞ 마녀는 볼수록에 더욱 어엿벗다. 완연히 살어 잇는 듯하엿다. 아! 눈에서 눈물이 흐르는고나-볼까지 흘너 나렷다-생각하고 보니 그것은 눈물이 아니고 피다. 호-마는 다시 정신을 밧작 차려 가지고 이래서 안되겟다고 합창 臺 우로 뛰여가서 불이낫케 경책을 펴고는 절이 떠나가게 큰 목청으로 경을 닑기 시작하엿다. 그 목소리가 고요하든 공긔를 이상하게 흔들어 노앗다.
「무서워할 것은 업다. 악마는 신의 말삼을 두려워한다. 성경을 외우고 잇스면 저 마녀가 관에서 일어날  념려는 업다」고 제마음에 일느고 될 수 잇는 대로 관을 보지 안키로 하고 경문을 내리 닑기는 하나 눈이 저절로 관에 끄을여 가서 겻눈을 팔게 되는 것을 엇지할 수가 업섯다. 「저것봐! 송장이일어낫다」 「안이다. 내가 밋첫나. 그럴 니가 잇다구」 「안이 일어날 터이지 일어나면 엇지 할고」그는 이럿케 혼자 마음을 태우고 입술이 밧삭 말나서 안저 잇스나 관은 꼼작 달삭하지도 안엇다. 밤은 점점 깁허 가는데 오즉 초ㅅ불 타는 소래가 간간 뻐지지 하고 날 뿐. 「아아 만일에 송장이 일어나면」하고 또다시 마음속에 치밀여 오르는 무서운 생각을 물니치려고 호-마가 한족 손을 들어 제눈을 부비고 잇슬 때 녀자의 송장은 관에서 움직이기 시작하엿다. 뺄덕 일어섯다. 그러고는 눈을 감은 채 손으로 누구를 찻는 듯이 차츰차츰 호-마의 압흐로 갓가히 오는 것이엿다. 그는 심장의 고동이 달깍 끈허진 듯하엿다. 그래도 잇는 힘을 다하여 악마를 물니치는 주문을 외엇다. 죽어서 멧칠 지난 사람이 푸르러 지는 것가치 녀자의 얼골빗은 무섭게 푸르럿다. 아래웃니가 똑 소리를 눈고 마조치면서 아모 것도 보이지 안는 눈을 크게 뜨고 한거름 한거름 한거름 호-마압흐로 갓가히 오다가 주문의 힘에 못익겻는지 도로 관 속에 들어가 바렷다. 호-마는 그제아 후하고 숨을 내쉬엿으나 그것도 잠간 동안이요 이번에는 귀ㅅ청이 떠리질 듯이 큰소리를 치고 관이 짝 갈너지더니 그것이 바람에 불니는 듯이 공중으로 이리 갓다 저리 갓다 하다가 나종에는 호-마의 머리 우에 떠러질 번한 것을 주문의 힘으로 간신히 면햇다.
그러는 동안에 멀니서 첫닭 우는 소리가 들니자 마녀는 관으로 들어가고 호-마는 경닑기를 맛치고 돌와 왓다. 그 잇튼 날 밤에도 꼭 갓튼 일이 반복되엿다. 호-마는 이틀 밤새 머리가 하얏게 시여바렷다.
그래서 더 견듸여 날 수 업서서 사흘ㅅ밤째에는 도경을 그만하겟다고 애원을 해 보앗스나 코싹크가 한사하고 듯지 안음으로 도망을 하랴다가 들키고 하야 헐 수 업시 第三夜의 독경을 하게 되엿다. 그날 밤 사령(死靈)은 땅 가운데 잇는 악마「우이-」를 불넛다. 우레가튼 소리가 나면서 절을 파괴하고 거대한 독개비 우이-가 낫하낫다. 그 얼골은 무쇠갓고 머리털은 그믈가치 덥혀 잇섯다. 손과 발은 큰나무 뿌리가티 줄이 죽죽 난 것이 흙이 담북 무더 잇섯다.
「눈겁을 좀 들어다오. 보이지 안는다」
「우이-」는 땅 속에서 새여 나오는 듯한 목소리로 이럿케 외첫다. 악마들이 독개비의 눈겁을 들쳣슬 때 「오-냐! 여긔잇고나」하고 「우이-」의 쇠갈쿠리가튼 것이 호-마에게 덤벼 들엇다.
호-마는 숨이 끈허저서 그 자리에 빨닥 잡버젓다. 그 때 멀니서 닭우는 소리가 들엿다.＜146＞
