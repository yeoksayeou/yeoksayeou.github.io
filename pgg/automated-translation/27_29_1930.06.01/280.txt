Thoughts on the Road in April
						Kim Hwasan

A
The street.

A cobblestone road soaked in tears - the heart of Seoul
Flags fluttering without wind at the storefront ‧ 旗‧旗‧旗
Cars running like feverish people ‧ 車‧車‧車‧車‧車
Smog - a white scarf
Powder of thick love.
A gentleman glancing at a big-bottomed woman.
People ‧ People ‧ People ‧ People……………
Oh, Seoul in the April afternoon smelling of spring,
A young man in his twenties burning with lust.

B
Spring has come without green leaves and red flowers!
Young man with long hair and a black necktie,
Do you see the brain running madly with thirty billion horsepower per second,
And the sorrow of having a one-jeon copper coin in your pocket,
And hunger,
And hatred for women,
And the crowd with thoughts as diverse as a train station?
Oh, I walk the street and
Hear the scolding of the crowd floating in the air.

C
Chandelier in the show window making blue dreams every night Marubiru
Baron Peacock - flood of cafes.
Oh, cinema advertisements scattered on the street and the newspaper reporting the great roundup of the Communist Party
April ‧ Thoughts on the Road.
Seoul embraces the refreshing breeze
××××××.
(1930‧4‧8)
