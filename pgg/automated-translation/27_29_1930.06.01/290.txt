The Tragedy of Spring
						Roa

On a soft, breezy evening
I wonder from where they were blown,
Two, three, four, five delicate flower petals
Perched on the red brick wall,
Caught in the web of a large spider, trembling.

Is the spider web hateful?
Or is it the spring breeze to blame?
The angry spider, glancing at the flower petals,
Does not know the sorrow of falling blossoms!

Oh, I can’t bear it! How can I leave them to the spider web?
It’s as if I’m looking at my own face, my own flesh.
I took a knife, tore it down with my own hands, and turned away.
(1930‧4‧20)
