기사제목	人生의 半고개 三十四十 고개 넘든 感想 諸氏 (Reflections on Life's Halfway Point: Various Perspectives on Passing Thirty and Forty)
기사형태	문예기타 (Literature and Other)

書劍無端 四十年

中東學校長 崔奎東

How old am I? In Korean age, I'm forty-seven, but my birthday is December 30th, so I'm actually forty-six.

I don't have any particular feelings, or rather, it's not that I have none, but nothing particularly remarkable. The year I turned forty, I don't know if you could call it reflecting on the past thirty-nine years, but I wondered what I had done during those thirty-nine years, and what I had accomplished. I realized I had accomplished nothing, and I wondered if I could create anything in the future. So, I wrote this poem:

書劍無端四十年. 絲靑雪白朝仍暮. 伶俜忍送恨多年. 花似人非歲又年.
(Seogeomudan sipsanyeon. Sacheongseolbaek jojeungmo. Ryeongpyeonginseong hanyeon. Hwasainbi seuyeoyeon.)

In other words, while carelessly spending precious time, I achieved neither scholarship (書) nor swordsmanship (劍), and I just spent many years filled with regret (恨). You could call it reflection or regret. The fleeting time is short and passing, and I have the ordinary, if extremely ordinary, feeling of wanting to accomplish something before I die. Even if there were any other special feelings, it's been six or seven years, so I've probably forgotten them.

What were my feelings about the education field at that time? I had some feelings about that too, but I've also forgotten them, so I don't remember well. But I wrote a line:

伽南乳蜜君林道. 吾們從玆四十年.
(Ganamyumil gunrimdo. Omeunjongja sipsanyeon.)

It was like in the Old Testament, when Moses longed for the happiness and honeyed grapes of Canaan, leading the people of Israel for forty years and wandering in the desert. That's all, nothing special.

不知老之將至

同德女高長 趙東植

What joy can we have as we greet the new year? We always say the same things and think the same thoughts <30> What special feelings could there be? I've probably forgotten them anyway! How old am I? Forty-two. Rather than feelings about turning forty, since I'm someone in the education field, and especially regarding this school, shall I tell you everything? For the past fifteen or sixteen years, this school has been like a flower blooming in a crack in a rock, not knowing when it might be knocked down by some wind. But with the support of the general public and constant effort, it was promoted in April of Taisho 15 (1926), and became a foundation in September of the same year. Now, the number of students in the two schools combined is seven hundred. Rather than my own feelings, my joy comes from the fact that our work is not declining but progressing, and that it has come this far thanks to everyone's support, and that it will continue to improve in the future. That's all. I'm sorry that I couldn't say anything better in response to your question.

사십까지는 勇氣而已

徽文學校長 李潤柱

I entered the education field at the age of twenty-six, but until then, my life was disorganized and somewhat dissolute. Moreover, since I was only at school from morning to night, I had little time to interact with real society. Therefore, I don't have any feelings to share, saying, "It was like this before, and it's like this now." Moreover, I haven't really thought about the feelings of passing thirty or forty. Rather than feelings about passing some milestone, until thirty or even forty, I had the energy to move forward bravely, but now I feel like that energy has somewhat diminished. However, recently, I don't just rush forward without carefully considering the beginning and end of what I want to do. However, since I am guiding young people, I always have some thoughts about them. First, I deeply feel that Joseon society is still immature. If you ask why, it's because I am entrusted with the great responsibility of educating young people who have great hopes for the future and grand ambitions, even though I am incompetent and lack the ability to guide them. Yet the general public does not scold me and leaves me alone. When I am with the teachers and students in one place, or when I face society, I feel ashamed and somewhat uneasy at the same time. Therefore, I try hard to keep up with new things, but no matter how hard I run, I can only chase after them. After a while, I fall behind like the tortoise and the hare, so there's nothing I can do! As for me, I am still very much steeped in old customs, so how can I catch up with the young people who are only moving forward! And people should think about the future and focus on planning ahead in order to progress and develop. But I'm already thinking more about the past than the future, and it's probably not just me, but that's probably a sign that I'm getting old! I don't remember feeling anything that I can really recall.

What about educational policy? Now, we no longer say <31> things like "the king, the teacher, and the father are one" (軍師父一體), or "those below should have mouths but no words" (在下者는 有口無言), or "I am your teacher, so you must absolutely obey what I tell you." When I first started teaching, students would be hit if they displeased the teacher, and they expected to be hit. But such a method of education is now impossible to see or do. Instead, we must theoretically instill in students' minds, "Your thoughts are like this, but my thoughts are like this. If my thoughts are right, follow them." But if only people like me, who still have many old customs, teach and guide them, we will fall too far behind. On the other hand, if only young teachers teach them, they may only have the energy to move forward and may have difficulty completing things perfectly. Therefore, in our school, there are about ten teachers over forty or fifty, and seventeen or eighteen young teachers in their thirties who have ambition and the courage to rush forward. They harmonize the old and the new, the rushing forward and the hesitating, to guide and restrain the vigorous students. And when we look at our society, some things have progressed, but we must not be satisfied with this. Unless we put in ten or a hundred times more effort than others, we don't know what state we will reach. Therefore, not only in education but in everything, I always have the thought of striving to progress forward.

마혼에 매지근

益世報社 徐世忠

It's the same for everyone, but before thirty, I had no fear, so I didn't hesitate in anything. No matter what difficult situations or hardships came my way, I didn't know they were difficult or hard, and I only wanted to rush forward, never looking back. But once I passed thirty, I became hesitant, and the courage (?) I had before thirty seemed to diminish considerably. I started thinking about the future and the past, and while the years from twenty to thirty seemed very long, the years from thirty to forty went by so quickly, as if time was suddenly running. But my thoughts are the same as they were then, but even when the courage to move forward aggressively arises, I first think about the end of the matter, so that's what has changed. Do I have any special feelings? The new year is coming, and I'll be forty! This is what I always think, but we must not allow our spirits to be taken away by the environment. If you look closely, there are many people who you thought would never do so, but they can't overcome their environment and lose the spirit they had and forget it. We are like stones in a rapid current. No matter how hard the powerful water tries to push us away, we firmly maintain our position. No matter how much society or the environment buffets us, we must not let that spirit be taken away by the environment. <32>

感慨無量

啓明俱樂部
林圭

Until now, I have lived for over sixty years without thinking about myself, so I have never thought about or remembered having any feelings when I turned forty or fifty. If I were to talk about my thoughts on passing milestones, it wouldn't be anything of substance. But first, I must tell you about the path I have taken in this world. I left my rural home (農村-내집-) when I was eighteen. The reason for that was not because my family was poor and had nothing to eat. My family had enough to eat and more. But a constant dissatisfaction filled my heart: "Why is the world so unfair?" The cruel exploitation of the poor by the rich, the harsh politics of the government towards the people, and everything in the world being unfair were keenly felt even in my young and illogical thoughts at that time. "Okay, if I only received three thousand bags of rice (三千석), I would save the people who are suffering in this situation"... But it was just a useless thought, nothing more than a fantasy, and would it ever happen? I left home on a whim, but it wasn't because I had some grand ambition or was looking for an opportunity to achieve my goals. I just came to Seoul (서울) on a whim. 转转流流, During that time I experienced all the events that happened... (omitted) During this wandering life, of course, the hardships were beyond words. For over sixty years, I have lived without achieving or learning anything. If I were to talk about my thoughts on myself, it would be, "Even though I have suffered like this, I must achieve something!" But that also was not something I desired from the bottom of my heart, and I don't know what I have been doing until now. I've talked too much about other things, but I have no thoughts about when I turned fifty or sixty, but one thing I always keep in my heart is, "We Joseon people should unite and gather our strength and move forward practically! When dealing with others, we should know what is shameful to us and not envy those who are better than us. If we help each other and hold each other up, we will surely be able to create something with our own hands." This is my unwavering thought, and it has been since the annexation, not just last year.

嗚呼歲不我廷

朝鮮日報社 文一平

I just passed the milestone, what special thoughts could I have? But when I think about the past, until I turned thirty, I was carefree and reckless, with no fear of the world, and I had the thought of trying things without hesitation, like a naked child, but I didn't know how to do it or what to do, and I just passed that milestone. When I climbed up to that milestone of thirty <33> the fields and roads ahead looked clear, and I felt like I could accomplish anything if I wanted to, and when I looked back at the road I had come, it was not only rough but also winding and clearly a roundabout way. But I don't think I have the same courage as I did then. Confucius said, "...at thirty I stood firm, and at forty I had no doubts" (三十而立, 四十而不惑), so from now on, I will set my mind on something and try to achieve something. But while I was doing that, the milestone of forty came upon me. When I looked back at the ten years I had spent, I regretted that I had come on another futile journey. Okay, I am now forty, when I am not惑 (confusion or doubt). In this steadfast time of middle age, I will grit my teeth and try to accomplish something, but I have been so burdened by the affairs of the world that I have not fulfilled my responsibility to society, so I have accumulated a great debt, but I have no way to repay it. Having spent more than half my life like this, I am now completely disillusioned and only more pessimistic. But in this new year, I will open my eyes wide, sharpen my mind, abandon useless disillusionment, get rid of unnecessary pessimism, and without regretting anything anew, I will rather be optimistic and make sure to accomplish something before I die. Even if it is not something big... <34>
