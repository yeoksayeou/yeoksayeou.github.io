발행일	1932년 06월 01일
기사제목	滄海力士(第六回) (Ch'anghae Yŏksa (Sixth Installment))
필자	月明山人(述), 金奎澤(畵) (Wŏlmyŏng Sanin (narrated), Kim Kyut'aek (illustrated))
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)

Ch'anghae Yŏksa (Sixth Installment)
						Wŏlmyŏng Sanin (narrated), Kim Kyut'aek (illustrated)

As No Yaksusa became the flag-bearer, brave warriors from across the land gathered.

No Yaksusa, originally a fish merchant, possessed extraordinary strength. He could lift the flag that others couldn't, control the wind, and move it freely. The Supreme Commander, impressed by this, asked Yaksusa:
"You are a figure from the Jianghu (江湖 - Kangho, martial arts world). There must be many warriors like you in the Jianghu. Recommend those you know to help save the nation."
Yaksusa, upon hearing this, responded:
"What if I recommend someone and they take my position as flag-bearer?"
The Supreme Commander laughed and said:
"Do you think you'll be satisfied with just being a flag-bearer? If you recommend many warriors, I will make you the vanguard commander. How about that?"
Only then did No Yaksusa speak: "I have traveled the land and met many warriors, but I have not seen anyone with the strength of my friend Hŏ Pi from my hometown. I also heard that Yŏ Minong from Myŏngsŏng is a great warrior."
The Supreme Commander asked again:
"Hŏ Pi is from your hometown, so you know his strength well. But where did you hear about Yŏ Minong's strength?"
Yaksusa replied:
"While traveling the land, I heard from someone near Odaesan that Minong encountered Sŏ Pok's (徐福 - Sŏ Pok) group in Yedudong while hunting in the mountains and fought against hundreds of them with a single club. I admire him and wished to meet him, but I haven't yet."
Hearing this, the Supreme Commander knew that Minong was undoubtedly his son and was greatly surprised. He said:
"General, you truly believe in baseless rumors. I know Yŏ Minong. He is just a scholar, a man of letters. He has no strength at all and only reads books in his room. There is no way he went hunting in the mountains."

One day, Supreme Commander Yŏ was again concerned about the lack of food supplies. While looking for someone to manage the supplies, he heard the name Hong Paengnok (洪白祿 - Hong Paengnok) and searched everywhere for him, but couldn't find him. Then, he remembered that Hong Mallok (洪末祿 - Hong Mallok), who had transported the supplies before, had a similar name. He called Hong Mallok and asked:
"Do you have a brother named Hong Paengnok?"
Mallok replied:
"I do."
The Supreme Commander was delighted and said:
"Why hasn't your elder brother responded to the call to join the volunteer army?"
Mallok replied:
"My brother and I live together. My brother has not yet been able to participate because he is organizing the household affairs, but he will answer the call soon."
Before he could finish speaking, a man named Hong Ch'ŏnbi (洪天臂 - Hong Ch'ŏnbi) from Hoeju requested to see the Supreme Commander. When he was brought in, Hong Ch'ŏnbi was none other than Hong Paengnok. The Supreme Commander immediately appointed Paengnok as the supply officer and Hong Mallok as the military adjutant.

Minong, by his father's command, recruits volunteer soldiers; Hŏ Pi and Tonghae, two warriors, arrive at the military camp.

Yŏ Minong, the son of Supreme Commander Yŏ Pang, was extraordinary and possessed exceptional strength. He followed his father's command and traveled to the countryside to recruit volunteer soldiers. He met two warriors and returned with them.
The Supreme Commander scolded him: <53>
"You have been gone for so long to recruit volunteer soldiers for the country, yet you only return with two people? Why?"
Minong replied:
"When I left, I saw that the army lacked provisions. I thought that having too many soldiers would only cause more worry. So, I brought two warriors who are capable of breaking through enemy lines with a single sword and spear."
The Supreme Commander asked for the names of the two warriors. One was Hŏ Pi and the other was No Tonghae.
The Supreme Commander was delighted and said:
"I have already heard about Hŏ General's prowess from Yanggaksin No Yaksusa, but how truly valiant is he?"
Minong replied:
"I have not actually tested his valor, but I heard from the locals that Hŏ Pi was originally a hunter who caught tigers without using any tools, so as not to damage their fur. Whenever he met a tiger, he would grab its tail and spin it around like a top, causing it to die on its own. That is why he is nicknamed General Tiger-Tail (虎尾將軍 - Homi Changgun)."
The Supreme Commander summoned the two generals. Hŏ Pi was six ch'ŏk (尺 - a traditional Korean unit of length) tall, with a bear's waist, an ape's arms, a dark face, and crystal-clear eyes, indeed the warrior he had heard of. No Tonghae was eight ch'ŏk tall, with a dignified appearance, and a neatly trimmed beard that reached his stomach, giving him the air of a scholar. His speech was refined. The Supreme Commander asked about his background. He was originally from Che (齊 - Che) State in the old Six Kingdoms. His great-grandfather resented that the Chin (秦 - Chin) Dynasty had conquered the Six Kingdoms and declared himself emperor, so he tried to cross the Eastern Sea (東海 - Tonghae) to come to Chosŏn, but died at sea. His descendants wandered and came to Chosŏn, but their livelihood was difficult, so they became hunters.
Hearing this, the Supreme Commander exclaimed:
"Isn't your great-grandfather No Chungnyŏn (魯仲連 - No Chungnyŏn)?"
Tonghae replied in surprise:
"Indeed, he is."
The Supreme Commander stood up, held his hand, and lamented:
"Who would have known that No Chungnyŏn's great-grandson would be in Chosŏn, living as a hunter in a remote mountain village? Do not miss this opportunity and achieve great things to honor your ancestor's legacy."
At that moment, No Yaksusa, having heard that Hŏ Pi had arrived, happily entered the command tent. He overheard the Supreme Commander holding No Tonghae's hand and speaking to him. A sudden burst of uncontrollable anger erupted within him. He struck the ground with his fist and shouted at the Supreme Commander:
"When you first met No Yaksusa, you treated me with such indifference. But when you meet Tonghae, you hold his hand and treat him with such respect. Why? If you admire and respect Chinese people like him, then why are you against Sŏ Pok, who is also Chinese? If you are going to choose people to serve the country, are you going to discriminate based on their ancestors or family background? What chance does someone like No Yaksusa, who is not the descendant of some great scholar or official, have to serve the country? I am unworthy of being the flag-bearer, coming from the lowly profession of a fish merchant. I will go back to selling fish and spend the rest of my life that way!"
With that, he shook his sleeves and stood up to leave. The Supreme Commander's face turned red, and he hastily stood up, grabbed Yaksusa's hand, and apologized:
"I, Yŏ Pang, have indeed made a mistake. Please, forgive me, General."
Hŏ Pi also tried to stop him. Only then did Yaksusa calm down.

After Supreme Commander Yŏ appointed his officers, he sent a letter to Sŏ Pok.

As Supreme Commander Yŏ raised his banner for the sake of the nation, warriors and heroes from across the land gathered under his flag. The Supreme Commander appointed various officers. He declared himself the Subduing-Demon Supreme Commander (伏魔大元帥 - Pongma Taewŏnsu). He appointed Hong Ch'ŏnbi as the Pacifying-the-Barbarians General (平虜將軍 - P'yŏngno Changgun), also in charge of military affairs and supply. No Yaksusa was appointed the Spreading-the-Might General (揚威將 - Yangwi Chang), serving as vanguard commander. Hŏ Pi was appointed the Scouting General (斥候將軍 - Ch'ŏk’u Changgun), in charge of assault troops. No Tonghae was appointed the Supporting-the-Water General (涉水將軍 - Sŏpsu Changgun), serving as the left-wing commander. Song Cho was appointed the Striking-Tiger General (打虎將軍 - T'aho Changgun), serving as the right-wing commander. Hong Mallok was appointed the Advisor-General (贊劃將 - Chanhwaek Chang), and Song Kon as the Gatekeeper-General (衙門將 - Amunjang). He did not give his son Minong any military position. Military Advisor Hong Ch'ŏnbi asked the Supreme Commander why. The Supreme Commander said:
"I heard from No Yaksusa that Minong killed Sŏ Pok's men in Yedudong. It is because of Minong's mistake that the Eighteen Caves fell into Sŏ Pok's hands. I should report this to the court and have him punished. How can I give him a military position?"
Hong Ch'ŏnbi said:
"That was then, and this is now. We cannot harshly punish a young man for his momentary youthful bravery. Furthermore, without Yŏ Youngnang's bravery, it will be difficult to defeat the enemy camp."
All the generals pleaded along with him. The Supreme Commander had no choice but to appoint Minong as the Capturing-the-Barbarians General (捕虜大將 - P'owŏn Taejang), making him the rear commander. After arranging the positions in this way, he immediately drafted a letter and sent it to Sŏ Pok's camp, requesting a battle at Hamap'yŏng Field on October 15th. As he was training the troops, a man named P'i T'ong from Ch'ŏkju requested to see Military Advisor Hong Ch'ŏnbi. Ch'ŏnbi summoned him. His appearance was quite humble, and his speech was also clumsy. He asked what he wanted, and he said:
"I came here because I heard that the Supreme Commander is looking for talent and I want to recommend myself."
Ch'ŏnbi asked again:
"If you want to recommend yourself to our camp, what talent do you have, and what position would you be suitable for?"
P'i T'ong replied: <54>
"I want to be a military officer."
Ch'ŏnbi said:
"The military officers have already been decided, and the day of the march is not far off. You should stay in the camp, and I will gradually report it to the Supreme Commander."

The two armies were training, and finally a battle was started at Hamap'yŏng.

After receiving Yŏ Pang's letter, Sŏ Pok led a large army and set up camp in Nohwaedong, and greatly trained his soldiers. He called Py'o Pyŏn and asked:
"I thought that the enemy general Yŏ Pang would not be able to obtain military supplies, and his soldiers would starve to death. What scheme has he been using to be able to properly supply his troops?"
Py'o Pyŏn replied:
"It is truly also doubtful to me. A man named Hong Mallok from Hoeju came to the Samjŏng Agency (三政廳 - Samjŏngch'ŏng) and asked to buy ginseng with money, but Mallok requested that since Hoeju had a famine, he wanted to exchange the ginseng for grain. So, I gave him 1,000 sŏk (石 - a traditional Korean unit of volume) of millet and 500 sŏk of potatoes. After that, I heard that the millet and potatoes went into Yŏ Pang's camp."
Hearing this, Sŏ Pok stomped his feet and felt wronged. Py'o Pyŏn said again:
"If the enemy soldiers pillage the Samjŏng Agency for grain, our soldiers will have nothing to eat. I will go to the Samjŏng Agency to defend against the bandits."
He stood up and asked Sŏ Pok for permission, saying:
"When the battle starts on October 15th, if it snows, please do not start the battle."
Sŏ Pok agreed to his request.
In this way, the two armies stood in opposition, and the feeling of war grew stronger. When October 15th arrived, the sounds of weapons clashing and flags fluttering filled Hamap'yŏng Field, and the great battle between the two armies was clear.
Sŏ Pok came out from his camp, raised the Dragon-Phoenix-Sun-Moon flag, held his T'aeak Sword (太阿劍 - T'aea Kŏm) diagonally, and rode his O Ch'o Horse (烏騅馬 - O Ch'wima) to the front of the battle. His imposing appearance was indeed dignified. T'aejawŏnilrang (太子元日郞 - T'aeja Wŏnilrang) was the vanguard commander, Yowŏnilrang (要元日郞 - Yowŏnilrang) was the left-wing commander, Princess Huch'ŏnja (公主后天子 - Kongju Huch'ŏnja) was the right-wing commander, and Hugangja (后岡子 - Hugangja) was the military advisor.
Sŏ Pok shouted from the front of the battle:
"You foolish villager Yŏ Pang in the mountain valleys, quickly come out and surrender!"
Ch'anghae Vanguard Commander No Yaksusa responded and came out, shouting:
"You rat-like Sŏ Pok, do you know Yanggaksin No Yaksusa of the great Ch'anghae nation?"
Before he could finish speaking, Sŏ Pok's left-wing commander Yowŏnilrang charged at him with his sword. No Yaksusa raised his iron club and swung it once, causing Yowŏnilrang's legs to fall off his horse like falling leaves. His wife Hugangja, seeing her husband's death, was filled with fury. She tightly closed her rouge-like lips and commanded hundreds of soldiers to attack Yaksusa. Each of the soldiers held a dagger in their hands. Yaksusa fought them off left and right, but the situation became quite dangerous. The other generals shouted to rescue him, but Yaksusa was furious and threw away his armor, helmet, and clothes. He shouted:
"How can a great man fear the daggers of women!"
He faced Hugangja naked. Hugangja fought for her life. No Yaksusa suddenly thought that even on the battlefield, it was inappropriate to undress in front of a woman. As he was putting his clothes back on, Hugangja seized the opportunity and threw two daggers, causing both of Yaksusa's ears to be cut off at the same time. Two streams of blood flowed down both shoulders. Yaksusa was furious and shouted like thunder. He raised his iron club and struck Hugangja, causing the pitiful beauty to fall off her horse and die.
At this time, the north wind blew fiercely, and the sky roared. Suddenly, white snow poured down, covering the mountains and rivers in an instant. Sŏ Pok remembered Py'o Pyŏn's earlier request and wanted to sound the gong to retreat his troops, but seeing the deaths of Wŏnilrang and Hugangja, he continued the battle to avenge them. Sŏ Pok's generals were riding tall horses and could move freely in the snow, but the Ch'anghae generals were riding native horses that were very short, and the snow reached their bellies, so they naturally had no way to use their valor. Supreme Commander Yŏ was worried and sounded the gong to retreat his troops. At this time, No Yaksusa's ears had been cut off, and he couldn't bear the pain, so he turned his horse to return. Striking-Tiger General Song Cho was protecting Yaksusa when his horse fell into a snow pit and couldn't get out. Just then, enemy general Wŏnilrang, along with Huch'ŏnja, were chasing after Yaksusa. Wŏnilrang's horse was passing over the snow pit when Song Cho mustered all his strength to jump out and stabbed Wŏnilrang's horse with his sword, causing the horse to fall and Wŏnilrang to fall into the snow. Then, Huch'ŏnja's horse also fell into the snow pit. Song Cho and Scouting General No Tonghae joined forces and captured Wŏnilrang and Huch'ŏnja, tying them up in pairs with mandarin duck-colored rope, and returned to the camp.
At this time, Sŏ Pok led his cavalry and attacked Hamap'yŏng. The Ch'anghae soldiers could not dare to face them and relied on the high hills to avoid the snow. Suddenly, dozens of snow plows came out from the Ch'anghae camp like lightning, colliding with Sŏ Pok's army. The weapons and arrows rained down like hail, causing the enemy soldiers to suffer a great defeat. The corpses lay scattered in the fields, and the blood flowed and formed a stream. Sŏ Pok gathered his defeated troops and returned to Nohwaedong. He investigated the casualties and found that Wŏnilrang and Hugangja had been killed in battle, Wŏnilrang and Huch'ŏnja had been captured, and the casualties of soldiers and horses were countless, and the loss of supplies and equipment was also beyond counting. 기사형태	문예기타

滄海力士(第六回)
						月明山人(述), 金奎澤(畵)
노약수가 집긔대장이 되니 강호상 용사가 모두 모히다.

노약수는 일개 생선장사로대 그 용력이 절인하야 남이 들지 못하든 긔를 들고 바람을 어거하며 좌우로 횡행하는지라 여원수-그것을 보고 크게 장이 여기어 약수더러 무러 가로대
「그대는 강호상 인물이라 강호에는 그대와 가튼 용사가 만흘 것인즉 아는 용사를 이 자리에 천거하야 나라일을 가치 구원하게 하라」
한대 약수- 그 말을 듯고
「만일 그러한 사람을 천거하엿다가 소인이 집긔대장의 자리를 빼앗기면 어찌하리까」
하거늘 원수-우수며 가로대
「그대는 일개 집긔대장으로 만족하게 아느냐 만일 용사를 만히 천거하면 그대로 하야금 선봉장을 삼을 것이니 어떠하뇨」
노약수- 그제야 말하야 가로대 「소장이 일즉이 천하용사를 모두 차저 맛나 보앗스되 소장의 동향친구 허비만한 용력을 보지 못하얏사오며 또 드른즉 명성따 사람 여민옹은 천하장사라 하더이다」
여원수-다시 무러가로대
「허비는 그대의 동향사람인즉 그의 용력을 자세이 알 수 잇거니와 여민옹의 옹력잇단 말은 어대서 드럿나뇨」
약수 대왈
「소장이 천하를 도라다니다가 오대산 근처 사람에게 드른즉 민옹이 산중에서 사냥하러 다니다가 예두동에서 의외에 서복의 무리를 맛나 몽둥이 한 개로 수백명을 대적한 일이 잇다 하기로 그를 사모하야 한 번 맛나보기를 원하얏스되 아직껏 맛나지 못하엿나이다」
원수-그 말을 드른즉 민옹은 자긔의 아들이 분명한지라 마음에 크게 놀래여 가로대
「장군은 과연 허망한 풍설을 미듬이로다. 여민옹은 나도 알거니와 그는 일개 백면서생이라. 본래 아모 용력이 업는 사람이요 방에 드러안저 글만 읽는 선비라 산중으로 사냥질 갓슬리도 만무하다」 하더라.
여원수-일일은 다시 군량관 업승을 근심하야 사람을 갈구하든 차에 다시 홍백록이란 사람의 명성을 듯고 사면으로 ****아러 보앗스나 업는지라 문든 전일에 군량을 운반하야온 홍말록의 이름이 그와 근사함을 깨닷고 우연이 홍말록을 불러 무러 가로대
「그대 형제중에 홍백록이라 하는 사람이 잇느냐」
말록이 대왈
「하관의 친형이로소이다」
하거늘 원수-대희하야 가로대
「그대의 백씨는 어찌하야 의용군을 모집하는데 응하지 아니하얏나뇨」
말록이 대활
「소관의 형제-한 집에 동거하옵든 바, 가형은 가사를 정리키 위하야 아직 응모치 못하얏사오나 미구에 대명할 것이로소이다」
하더니 말이 맛지 안하야 회주따 사람 홍천비라 하는 사람이 원수께 맛나기를 청하거늘 불러보니 홍천비는 곳 홍백록이라 즉시 백록으로 하야금 운량관을 삼고 홍말록으로는 군문교위를 삼다.

민옹이 친명으로 의병을 모집하니 허비 동해 두 장사 군문에 이르도다.

여민옹은 원수 여방의 아들이니 위인이 비범하고 여력이 절인한지라 일즉이 원수의 명을 바더 시골로 도라다니며 의용병을 모집하다가 두 장사를 맛나 가치 도라오거늘
원수-꾸지저 가로대<53>
「네가 나라를 위하야 의용병을 모집코저 박게 나간지 오래엿거늘 다만 두 사람을 더불고 도라옴은 무슨 까닭이뇨」
민옹이 대왈
「소자가 떠날 때에 살펴보온즉 군량이 부족하옵기로 군사가 만흐면 도리여 걱정이 될까. 생각하옵고 단칼 단창으로 능이 적진을 깨처부실만한 장사 두 사람을 마저 왓나이다」
하거늘 원수-두 장사의 성명을 무르니 하나는 허비요 또 하나는 노동해라.
원수-대희하야 가로대
「허장사의 성화는 이믜 양각신 노약수에게 드럿거니와 그 용맹이 과연 어떠하더뇨」
민옹이 대왈
「그 용맹은 실지로 시험치 못하얏사오나 그 곳 사람에게 드른즉 허비는 본래 사냥하는 포수로서 호랑이를 잡으되 그의 가죽에 상처가 날까하야 연장을 쓰지안코 언제든지 호랑이를 맛나면 그 꼬리를 붓잡고 파람가비가치 내돌리어 제절로 죽게하는 고로 별호를 호미장군이라 하더이다」
