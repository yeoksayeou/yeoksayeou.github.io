A Secret Among Secrets, A Public Account of My First Night
Song Geum-sun

My marriage happened so quickly that others would laugh if they heard about it. Anyway, only three days after the marriage proposal, I had already become someone who had to serve her husband as another's wife. It was like a dream. Or perhaps it would be more accurate to say that I felt like I was dreaming within a dream.

"Hey, Geum-sun! When did you get married?" A friend who heard the news and met me for the first time asked, her eyes wide with surprise. Then, as I touched my hair, now styled in a chignon for the first time, trying to explain how it happened, my friend interrupted me, saying, "You're so easygoing, I really envy you."

"Easygoing?"

This word somehow made my heart ache. Easygoing? I tried to think, avoiding the gaze of my friend, who was looking at my appearance, which had changed so suddenly into that of another's wife, so different from what I had expected, but I couldn't quite understand the meaning of my friend's words "easygoing" at that time. So I asked, "Am I really that easygoing?" And she replied, "Yes. Your face is so natural. You don't look like someone who has gone through a frightening first experience." She tilted her head as if it was very strange, saying, "Even if it's a formal marriage, giving a man your virginity is a big deal. Of course, when you get married, it's more than natural for a woman to give her chastity, but losing what you've been firmly guarding until now in just one night is very sad for a young woman."

Looking at my friend, who was playing with the spoon in her tea while receiving the winter sunlight streaming through the window, I said, "Oh, that—" Trying to suppress the blush rising on my face, I pondered over my feelings before going through the ordeal of that one night.

For a young woman, respecting virginity is no different from considering one's unique life as important. Even if it's a passionate relationship, one wouldn't give up one's body.

Parents, siblings, and everyone in the world talk about it, don't they? Or don't we often hear of women who even give up their lives because of it?

Such a precious pride for a woman! A woman's treasure that she has painfully endured various temptations for so long!

Losing it in a single night—it wouldn't be unreasonable to feel deep sorrow and fear close to terror, as much as it was forbidden.

Especially, my family was even more strict. It was partly because my late father was a scholar of Han studies, but also because of the great change in my mother's character, who handled all the household affairs as a single woman.

From the time I graduated from girls' school and talk of marriage gradually began, my mother's attitude towards me became even stricter. It wasn't anything unreasonable or excessively restrictive, but it was the result of her constant concern for my well-being, saying, "You're going to be married off to another family, so you must take good care of yourself." My mother never let me go out alone now that I was of age. Even if I happened to go out because of a newspaper advertisement or a friend's story, I always had my younger sibling accompany me, and if I went to a friend's house and came back late, even if my friend's father walked me home, I would still be scolded.

"A girl shouldn't do that..." Even in cases where it wouldn't have been necessary to be so strict, that's how my mother thought. So, I would be frightened as if I had committed some terrible sin and answer, "Yes, I will never go outside alone again," and stay at home as much as possible to please my mother. Even if brides who are trapped in ignorant and stubborn families aren't like that these days, there was nothing I could do about my circumstances. Living so strictly <34> without being able to leave the house and continuing a life that felt so far removed from the world, this world seemed entirely frightening.

Especially when it came to matters of the opposite sex, these strange thoughts grew even more. Even when I met the face of a student who was lodging near my house, I felt some strange emotion, as if I was ashamed.

"What should I do?"

Knowing such a cowardly feeling, I tried to correct my own heart, but in the end, it had no effect.

While I was living like this, marriage talks suddenly arose, and I ended up getting married quickly on the third day.

I first heard about the marriage proposal on December 25th, when snow was falling for the first time that year.

My mother quietly called me into the next room as I was knitting and sat me down, saying, "Such a good marriage prospect has come up, and I've decided to send you off to get married," with a look of relief on her face.

"They say next year is inauspicious for your age, and they are also urging us, so let's get married this year," she said, without any objections or complaints. My mother, who does everything on her own, only listened to what I had to say with one ear, and anyway, she decided on the marriage in a hurry.

"Another's wife!"

As I returned to my room alone, I focused all my thoughts on trying to grasp the unknown entity that fate had captured me with. However, in my mind, there was not the happiness of a splendid marriage that every woman longs for, but only the sorrow of separation (愛別感) from my virginity, which I would be bidding farewell to forever after that one night.

Even if my life were in danger, I had been taught not to let go of this one thing, the last pride I had been told about—wasn't that why I had endured any desires of my youth and spent my days?

It was like crushing a jewel in my palm, something I couldn't express in words.

"Marriage is the flower of life."

I tried to negate this thought, thinking hard, but my sorrowful thoughts became even more sorrowful, and my pillow was soaked with tears that night.

You may not understand if I talk like this, but because I only had about three days from when the marriage proposal arose until the day of the wedding, my heart must have been more troubled than others, which is an undeniable fact.

And even more than that sense of sorrowful separation, what pressed down on my small chest was the anxiety about the unknown husband with whom I would share the joys and sorrows of my life.

I had only ever thought of men as enemies of women, and just the thought of how the man who was to be my husband would treat me made that one night seem frightening.

"How happy you must be!"

"How great this must be!"

People who knew about my marriage said to me every time they met me,

"What is so happy and great about it?"

I laughed to myself.

Anyway, with such a mindset, I went to the wedding hall on the day of the ceremony, completed the wedding without any major incidents, and set off on a week-long honeymoon to Onyang Hot Springs.

The wailing sound of the train as it rushed forward sounded only like a sound urging the eternal farewell to virginity. There were more than a few times when I covered my ears so that my husband wouldn't see me while we were on the train.

When we arrived in Onyang and entered the inn, the quick-witted innkeeper seemed to have sensed our situation with his professional intuition and, as he guided us to a room that was a bit far off, chattered, "This room is very quiet and convenient."

Indeed, it was a clean and quiet room, but for me, who was about to experience my first night, it felt strange.

"Those skillful guys must have caught on right away," my husband said, pouring the bottle of alcohol he had brought, and on the one hand, he flattered the innkeeper.

"Have a little bit to drink."

He poured me a glass and urged me, his face flushed with excitement and alcohol.

"No, thank you..."

I didn't know what to do. But I couldn't just accept it so readily.

After some time, as it became nine o'clock, the large inn became quiet.

"Ah, you must be very tired."

My husband, whose face was red up to his chest from a bottle of alcohol, spoke to me, who was sitting still like a statue.

"Well, let's go to sleep now."

Those were ordinary words from a husband, but to me, they felt like needles piercing my heart.

"..."

"You'll catch a cold if you stay like this for too long."

Following the sound of my husband pressing the call button, the maid quietly spread out the bedding and left.

"Ah! What should I do!"

The final moment had finally arrived.

Although the eyes of my mind were closed, the agitation in my heart at that time was truly indescribable.

However, my anxiety and fear didn't last that long. I resigned myself to everything and entrusted my body to my husband. The next morning, my heart felt lighter and my head felt relatively clear.

I realized what life was for the first time. That doesn't mean I'm pointing to mere pleasure.

I realized then for the first time how meaningful and important it is to give one's virginity on the wedding day.

My husband sincerely said to me, "I will never forget your true heart."

"I will love you forever."

The most sincere face of a man! It was then that I saw it for the first time, and I realized its power and greatness. Our hearts were bound together by that.

I offer this foolish experience to the countless women who feel fear on their wedding night. - The End - <36>
