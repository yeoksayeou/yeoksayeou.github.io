Love's Snow Burial
Yang Sang-ho

I don't know if it's because my eyesight isn't good and my hearing is poor, but they say it's a "story picked up in the snow." Anyway, I don't have anything worth telling. Instead, I probably left some things scattered on the streets like a fool. Shall we count them?

One, two, three... There's no end to it, so let's stop. But you'll be disappointed, so just one thing! It was the winter before last, on a night when snowflakes were falling softly. It was a very bad time when Santa Claus wasn't giving toys to children but was trying to steal the socks hanging in the lower room.

What about bonuses? The next morning, I was surrounded by beggars, banging my head on the cold floor and letting out sighs full of despair. An rickshaw puller came from outside. Thinking a friend was buying me a drink, I asked, but it wasn't a restaurant, but Chongjin-dong, Chongjin-dong! After tying the torn heel of my sock a couple of times, I got into the rickshaw.

Chongjin-dong! When I got there, it was C's house. C was a kisaeng I had been dating in the form of romance. But we hadn't seen each other for about four months for no reason. I heard that C had caught a "patron (봉)" in the meantime, was making a living, and had earned about a thousand won for her family.

"...I'm going to quit being a kisaeng now and go live in the countryside. When we met, we didn't think our love would last a hundred years, did we? So, let's end our relationship cleanly tonight in this place. The longer we drag it out, the bigger the emotional wounds I'll receive. You live well with your wife. I'm going to go to the countryside and marry even a blind topknot-wearer."

C's words were natural and reasonable. But I couldn't just clap my hands and agree! I put on a face of shedding tears of blood and sympathized with her. And I filled my throat with alcohol that I didn't know how to drink, as she suggested.

When I left C's house, the street was covered with thick piles of snow. As soon as I ran into the alley, I threw up all the machang (마찬) that C had given me. And then I covered it with snow.

"That's right, C! Let's hold a snow burial (雪葬) for our love."

Of course, it might have come out because I was drunk, but I guess I was pretty sentimental in my own way.

So, I buried my love in the snow and went home.

A story thrown away in the snow! What can I do if there's nothing else?

One Night in a Mountain Cottage
Lee Hyang-chon

It was an event in my boyhood.

That year's winter had especially heavy snowfall.

The old pine tree branches were bending low, and the roof was covered with a few ja (尺 - unit of measurement) of thick snow. The world was a snowscape, and the cold wind from the northern country relentlessly whipped and scattered snowflakes.

- One morning, I insisted on following my neighbors who were going deer hunting, even though they told me not to come.

In the mountains - <57>
I struggled through the snow, which was almost up to my chest, following closely behind the hunters. Whether it was because there was a lot of snow or because it was a particularly animal-rich year, large deer ran rampant, chased by the hunters. Moreover, the hunters were all quite skilled marksmen, so the day's results were very good, and by early evening they had already shot three or four deer, both large and small.

However, the hunters' greed was not satisfied with that, so they gathered the hunted deer, tied them to the base of a tree, and went deeper into the mountains in search of animal tracks.

The sun was already setting, slowly climbing over the high silver ridge (銀嶺) of the western mountains. The scenery of the reddish sunset slanting on the white mountain peaks was truly magnificent.

I was extremely tired in all my limbs and barely managed to keep up with them. They were so focused on hunting that they paid little attention to the exhausted young boy. As night fell and twilight descended, the mountain birds, chased by the hunters, hid in the tall trees and barely whispered, and fear gradually thickened in the gaps along with the twilight, so I struggled desperately not to fall behind them. If I fell behind here, I would surely die...

In the meantime, I discovered a deer, the size of a calf, running with a tremendously fast speed through the mountain valley.

"Woah- catch it!"

The hunters shouted in unison and chased after it like birds, and some people were shooting their guns, Bang! Bang! Each time a gun was fired, a terrible echo shook the entire mountain for a long time. Each time that happened, the mountain birds, which had barely been whispering, seemed to tremble in terror and fell into a deathly silence.

I also ran quickly after them, but I slipped on the snow and fell, rolling into a deep valley. -

Soon, in my hazy consciousness, I saw a room lamp. Then I saw the face of an old man and the face of a young woman, casting furtive glances with worried expressions. I wondered if it was a dream or not, so I opened my eyes wide and gathered my senses and looked around several times. Whether it was because I was opening my eyes strangely and making a surprised expression, the old man smiled and muttered something. I couldn't understand exactly what he was saying, but it seemed he was asking if it hurt.

- I had injured my leg. Moreover, I was exhausted, and as soon as I fell, I lost consciousness. The hunters eventually carried me and found a small house in the mountains and left me there, then went down to the village.

I fully recovered my consciousness while spending one night in that lonely small mountain cottage. That night, the young woman, who was the old man's granddaughter? showed me great kindness, bringing me cold water and putting snow on my eyes with a very worried expression. I didn't feel any affection as a member of the opposite sex because I was young, but anyway, I can't forget the young woman's worried face and kind attitude that night.

The next day, when it dawned brightly, I looked outside and saw heavy snowflakes falling again, and the shadow of a person gradually approaching from the mountain valley was shouting my name loudly. As soon as I realized that the voice was my father's and uncle's, how happy I was! It is difficult to describe the joy of being revived at that time. I immediately ran out and took a couple of steps, saying, "I'm okay - I can walk like this," and answered loudly. People were getting closer and closer. Again, I waved my hand and shouted, "I'm okay - look at this." At that time, the young woman also ran out, looked at me, and smiled.

My Father's Body Temperature
Baek Reung

It was a snowy sunset after hard work.

"A zombie (殭屍) appeared in XXX."

Hearing this news, our group, who had been hunting birds with air guns, ran to XXX.

The village chief, who was the adopted son of a sick traveler, was already there, and the constable, a servant, hadn't arrived yet.

The corpse was wearing a stained overcoat and was sitting in the corner of a rice paddy below the road.

"Who is it?"

"I don't know, who knows!" This was the village chief's grumbling answer. He continued,

It seems that two people have died.

"Where's the other one?"

"Go and poke around."

A friend went down and poked the overcoat and said,

"Oh, he died holding a child!"

and clicked his tongue in disgust.

Then, suddenly surprised, he stepped back and shouted something.

"What's wrong? Is it already a ghost?"

"Oh... no, it's twitching."

"What?"

The people who had been standing together gathered around the corpse. The village chief, who had some experience, frowned and poked at the overcoat, then smiled slightly and said,

"The little one is alive."

We rushed over.

A four-year-old boy, sleeping in his father's arms, rubbed his eyes and looked around at us. And then (in the whining tone of a child raised in front of many people) he smiled brightly.

One person next to him choked up and sobbed.

The village chief picked up the child, wrapped him in the overcoat of a friend who was now sobbing, and went into a nearby house.

The snow falls and gently covers the father's corpse.

... ... ...

Now that child has grown up as a servant in the village and is a servant in someone else's house.

Snow Goblin
An Hwai-nam

When I was eleven or twelve years old - it was when I was going to study Chinese classics ("Tonggam") with a "Tonggam (通鑑)" in Araetsmal village, which was about five ri away from my hometown, Kkotmi.

Because I was young, the road was long, and it was a rural area, it was lonely, and because I was the only one accompanying me, I hated going to study.

Moreover, at that time, there were so many goblin stories that all I heard day and night, whether in the study or at home, was about goblins. There were goblins who were rolled up in straw mats and thrown into the stream, and there was an egg goblin who rolled around and stuck to people's bodies. No matter how hard you stepped on it, it wouldn't break, and even if you grabbed it, it wouldn't come off. There was also a skirt goblin that was a young woman who had died, and it would flutter in the wind and cover people and fly away with them. Because I was young and simple-minded, I not only heard those stories, but they also made a deep impression on me. Even now, I remember most of those stories, and I can imagine the goblins in various shapes. It was like seeing and experiencing them - Winter came and it snowed, and it was covered in snow for several days. At home, I was researching how to avoid going to study and just relax every day. If I think about the scene of a long mountain corner being covered with cotton-like white snow without any gaps, and me, a young child, walking there alone with a book by my side, it evokes quite sentimental memories.

One day, I went to the study and bent my back to read a book all day. Because I had a long way to go, I left before the other children and headed home over the hill. The sun hadn't set yet, but the world was covered with so much snow that my young heart was terrified, and coincidentally, the thoughts of the goblins I had heard about day and night made my hair stand on end.

There was a place called Saeteo on the way to our village, and it was when I was passing through that place. When I turned my head towards the village without thinking, there was a goblin standing at the entrance of the village, looking at me. It wasn't just six or seven cheok (尺 - unit of measurement) tall, but a huge jangseung (장승 - village guardian pole) that was twice that size. It was wearing a hat and standing with its arms outstretched on both sides. I felt like I was being swallowed into the goblin's arms. When I looked at its face, it was indescribably hideous. I felt dizzy, but in an instant, I had run quite a distance away from Saeteo village. At that time, I was about to stop, but I made up my mind and returned home in one breath. That evening, night, and the next morning, I kept thinking about the goblin and shuddered, but I didn't tell my father, mother, or friends the story. Even from a young age, I was a person who kept things to myself.

However, the next day, I didn't go to the study alone, but went with a servant who lived in my house. Of course, the snow was still piled up as it had been the day before, and it was shining brilliantly in the light of the morning sun, making my heart flutter. But unlike the previous evening, I had energy and was lively.

When I arrived at Saeteo in front, the goblin that had attacked me the day before was gone. I went with the servant to the place where the goblin had been standing. The servant was bewildered and just looked at my face.

When I went there, there was definitely a big chestnut tree with its trunk cut in the middle where the goblin had been standing and trying to attack me. There were many branches and the upper body of the chestnut tree that had been broken off all around, but they were covered in snow and not visible from afar. Only the protruding trunk was reflecting the snow as if it were wearing a white hat on its head, and coincidentally, the stump of the chestnut tree had the most bizarre shape with its two sides stretched out like a person's arms, so I immediately realized, "Ah, this is what tricked me last night." My young heart was filled with joy.

Immediately, I took the servant boy and walked out of the village, and from the point where I had met the goblin the previous evening, I looked at the chestnut tree standing there. Of course, the goblin from the previous evening was gone this morning, and as before, the cut-off trunk of the chestnut tree was now facing me like a large doll.

I stood there for a while and smiled slyly.

I told the servant boy as we headed to the study. In this way, we brought out various goblin stories again on that snowy road.

Shipwreck
Song Gye-wol

It is a memory of a snowy winter day!
It was a lonely night in the northern country, with storms roaring and heavy snow falling.

The paper windows were banging, and the sound of the waves was so loud that people woke up startled in their blankets.

"Oh dear - is this day trying to catch people again!"

An experienced old man in the village muttered with a hacking cough.

"Is it going to ruin us again! I hope the ship doesn't break!"

This was the shipowner's worry.

"Fortunately, if the ship had encountered the storm in the harbor, nothing would have happened. What will happen now? But don't worry. We have nothing but poverty!"

As the sound of the waves grew louder, those who were thinking about the whereabouts of the ship carrying their father, mother, brother, son, and relatives tried to deny the terrible reality that would unfold in the future.

While holding this anxiety, the window turned dark blue. The wind must have calmed down because the paper window was not rattling.

"The ship has capsized!"

I heard people running and shouting.

As if unrelated to this anxiety, there was something that foretold something to my ears after sleeping soundly. Without knowing it, I put on several layers of clothes and went out onto the snowy street.

A woman crying and passing by! A young girl following behind, calling her father, hitting her chest and lamenting, and old women running while shouting that it had capsized! They were running as fast as arrows, panting.

Before I knew it, I had arrived at the pier and stopped. The snow was falling so thickly like rice flour that I couldn't even see the person next to me well.

"Why are you crying?"

I looked at a girl my age who was crying sadly and asked. But she continued to look at the sea and shed tears.

I also looked at the sea.

Ah! On that sea, weren't there six sailors who were about to have the ship capsize? Even with only eight kan (間 - unit of measurement) between them, people couldn't send a rescue boat and were just clutching their chests. Waves were overflowing the ship.

They were shouting.

"Oh! God, forgive our sins. Save us."

Among them, an old man was crying and praying to the sky. Also, a young man among them was tying himself to the sail and shouting, "Save the people!" But the storm did not subside at all. Rather, the waves were getting worse.

At this moment!

Everyone on the pier involuntarily clutched their chests and shouted "Ah!" Then they closed their eyes tightly and opened them.

""

The ship capsized! People can't be seen! People were shouting like this and started to wail even louder. Tears started to well up in my eyes as well. As I stroked the back of the girl who was writhing and crying next to me and stood crying for a while, people rushed into the customs office. The girl who had been crying sadly next to me also hurried towards the crowd.

The next day, two bodies were found on the beach. On top of them, the unceasing snow had frozen about an inch thick.

A Story Picked Up in the Snow-.
-The End-

배 업허젓다! 사람이 안보인다! 사람들은 제각긔 이러케 웨치면서 더 큰 소리로 통곡하기 시작하엿다. 나의 눈에서도 눈물이 핑 돌기 시작하드니 엽헤서 몸부림하고 울고섯든 게집애 등을 어루만지면서 한참이나 울고 서 잇쓸때 사람들은 우루루 몰려 개안으로 들어가는 것이엿다. 내 엽헤서 설게 설게 늣겨 울든 게집애도 허둥거리면서 군중이 밀닌 쪽으로 가는 것이엿다.

그 이튼날 해변에는 두 사람의 시체가 나와 잇섯다. 그 우에는 끗칠줄 모르는 듯이 내리는 눈이 한치가량이나 어러부텃든 것이엿다.
