기사제목	(探偵小說) 順娥慘殺事件 (Detective Novel: The Tragic Murder of Sun-a)
필자	崔流帆
기사형태	소설 (Novel)

崔流帆 (Ch'oe Ryupam)
From Inside the Straw Stack

"Sir, sir, a big problem has occurred. Please come quickly."

It was an evening when the still-chilly early spring breeze hastened the steps of passersby. A farmer, his face pale, ran into the P police box, located in the border area between K county and C county.

"What is it? Did Wonsam's couple start fighting again?" The patrolman at the police box, who had been writing something in a diary, spoke as if scolding the farmer for his flustered state, with a very calm attitude.

"No, it's not Wonsam's fight... You know my barley field east of the Sericulture Training Center (養蠶專習所), right? I was working there and, when I was about to go home, I went to the place where I had piled up the straw to twist some straw ropes at night. When I pulled out a few bundles of straw, I caught a glimpse, sir! A woman's corpse appeared from inside the straw stack. I was so shocked that I almost broke my back, but I barely managed to come running all the way here. Sir! It must be almost dinnertime, but won't you come with me?"

"What? A woman's corpse in your barley field's straw stack?"

"Yes! It's a woman, and she seems to be a young maiden still. And the way she died..."

"Alright! Let's go together." The patrolman dusted off the lantern that had been covered in dust. This police box, located in a relatively remote area, rarely sees a major incident even once a decade. The patrolman became extremely tense and put on his leggings before leaving.

The clear sky was already twinkling with early stars here and there. The evening breeze was not very cold. It was an evening that reminded one that spring was not far off. The feet of a farmer and a patrolman stirred the early spring path between the rice paddies.

These villagers would chase after a spectacle even if it was ten or twenty li away. Two young men who were returning from work in a distant place followed behind them.

"Ah! This is serious." The patrolman exclaimed, startled, after seeing inside the straw stack that farmer Kim Sŏndal had pointed to. And at that moment, he took a step back, but realizing that wouldn't do, he went closer to the straw stack and carefully examined the gruesome dead body (變死體).

"Murder, murder, where else could there be such a terrible murder?"

What emerged from the straw stack that Kim Sŏndal had disturbed was undoubtedly the corpse of a young woman. It had been buried in this straw stack for quite some time, but because it was winter, the corpse had not decomposed much.

The still-smooth, white, and soft elastic flesh was visible through the torn clothes here and there. Moving one's gaze gradually upwards from the rather plump ankles, anyone would unconsciously turn away their head at the sight that unfolded where that gaze ended. There, a bamboo stick about seven pun in diameter protruded from between the torn skirt. The patrolman hesitated to look more closely.

He then carefully examined the upper body of the corpse, which was buried up to the waist, while pulling away the straw little by little. The woman's hair was disheveled, its black color unchanged from when she was alive, but the woman's cheeks, which were stuck like seaweed, had turned purple, and her sunken eyes made one wonder if maggots had infested them.

"This is a serious murder case. I cannot handle this alone. Kim Sŏndal, you stay here and guard this place until I return. I'll quickly go and report to the main station." This was what the patrolman was telling the discoverer, Kim Sŏndal.

"Sir, how can you tell me to stay here alone? It's already night, how can I guard a corpse by myself?"

"Hey, how can an old man be so afraid?"

In fact, the patrolman himself had come out of a sense of duty, but he didn't want to be left alone either. Eventually, he wrote a letter for the two people who had come to watch after hearing the rumors and had them report to the C police station, which was twenty li away.

With the patrolman with a lit lantern at his side, people who had come running after hearing the news gathered one by one, making the patrolman feel less lonely.

To explain the location where the body was found in more detail, it was a place called C-ri, C county, not far from the Kyŏngŭi Line's C station, and recently incorporated into C county, in a barley field not far from the Kyŏngŭi Line railroad. To the west was the Sericulture Training Center (養蠶專習所), with farmhouses nearby, and just over one hill was Changgŏri, but it was a desolate place that didn't easily catch people's eyes.

The corpse that appeared from the straw stack, as mentioned earlier, had a bamboo stick thrust into the lower abdomen (下腹部), with one end reaching up to the chest, a severely cruel sight. The result of the coroner's inquest (檢死) was, of course, recognition of homicide.

The victim was quickly identified without difficult investigation through the clothing worn, the corpse's appearance, and the belongings scattered nearby. The victim was Sun-a (順娥) (18), the daughter of Ri XX, who ran an inn in front of C station.

Among the belongings found near the straw stack were Sun-a's handbag, a novel, and a handkerchief. Also, a woman's rubber shoe that did not belong to Sun-a was found lying there.

Reputed Beauty Sun-a

With these belongings, the C police station's investigation network became extremely tense. The C police station's detective first went to the victim's home, that is, her house in front of the station, and investigated the details of Sun-a's disappearance (失踪顚末). According to that, about a month ago, Sun-a went out one afternoon saying she was going to a friend's house, but she did not return.

Sun-a was a new woman (新女性) with a reputation as a great beauty, and marriage proposals were pouring in from here and there. Even though it had been a month since their beloved daughter had gone missing, why hadn't the family submitted a search request to the authorities and left it unresolved? This led to speculation that there might be some complicated circumstances hidden within the victim's family. However, Sun-a's father's explanation was extremely simple and clear.

"My profession involves dealing with many customers, <48> and furthermore, if it became known to the world that my daughter had gone out without permission before marriage and had not returned, it would only add to the rumors already circulating. Therefore, I wanted to avoid casting a shadow on my daughter's future marriage, so I kept it hidden and secretly tried to find her." This explanation was heard as a very reasonable one by everyone.

Then, what was Sun-a's behavior like throughout her life? Sun-a had a pure and noble heart (淸白高潔), and until the age of eighteen, she was a pure maiden who had never been associated with a scandalous rumor. Even the police, who initially suspected a crime of passion (癡情), saw that suspicion gradually weaken as the investigation progressed, troubling the officials (係官).

At the Barbershop

One evening, unusually warm for a winter day, Detective M of the C police station went to the Changgŏri barbershop to get a haircut in order to clear his head, as the search for the recent beautiful woman's murder case was not going as planned. Even while getting his hair cut, Detective M was very anxious.

This case is a rare and cruel crime, and the rumors circulating about it in the world are no ordinary matter. Therefore, each neighboring county police station is competing with each other to capture the perpetrator. Each police station is outwardly maintaining a calm attitude, but in reality, they are continuing their activities without sleep, and among them, the K police station is openly confronting us, vowing that they will definitely arrest the perpetrator of this case.

Extremely excited and tired, Detective M faintly heard the snip, snip sound of the scissors cutting his hair and dozed off.

After several tens of minutes, the detective opened his eyes at the sound of a noisy commotion. Looking behind him, he saw four or five young men gathered. Whether they were dropouts from school or unemployed young men, their first impression was that of delinquent youths, loudly talking.

"Kim-san! It seems that Sun-a's murder case is going nowhere. I guess the police station is helpless. How can they not have arrested the perpetrator of such a big case by now?" When Detective M heard these words, his nerves became extremely sensitive.

He suppressed the urge to say, "You punks, don't talk nonsense," and closed his eyes again, pretending to sleep. The barbershop owner and the young men, thinking that this person was an ordinary customer and not a detective, continued talking as if nothing had happened. One of the young men said,

"I think that the perpetrator is a simple sex maniac (色情狂) who had no relationship with Sun-a. Only a madman could do such a cruel thing."

"Indeed, this guy must be a violent delinquent (不良少年)," Detective M thought. <49>

"By the way, I heard that Patttori is suspected of being involved in this case and has been arrested."

"Yes, but I heard that he was taken to the K police station, which is not under this jurisdiction."

The barbershop owner glanced at them briefly and started sharpening his razor after putting down his scissors.

"No matter how I think about it, I don't think Patttori would do such a thing."

"But I heard that Patttori was in love with Sun-a."

"Patttori isn't the only man who was in love with Sun-a. Everyone here would die for Sun-a."

"Don't say unnecessary things. Because of such rumors, even we, who have no relationship, are becoming suspects."

"Indeed, the police station will now be paying attention to us."

"Listen, I don't want to hear that. How can we become suspects?"

"But if there is someone among our friends who would do such a violent thing, who would it be?"

"It's just speculation, but I think it would only be Patttori."

"Indeed, that guy is good at doing reckless things..."

"You can guess from the way he killed Dr. Kim's dog last time."

A Consistent Crime

The barbershop owner, who started shaving Detective M's beard after sharpening his razor, was like an agent of the detective, drawing out their stories one by one.

The young men, having become absorbed in their conversation, did not care about the attention of others and loudly exchanged words.

"Sometime on a Sunday, Dr. Kim went to Seoul and his family all went to church, leaving only Sambong to guard the house. That guy put the poison in a rice cake and threw it to the dog that Dr. Kim takes with him when he goes hunting, right? While Sambong was desperately shouting, 'No! No!!' Patttori blocked his way, and the dog ate the rice cake. Then, a little later, the dog spun around two or three times and then collapsed with its legs stretched out. Sambong, who was watching this, cried and said that there would be big trouble when the owner returned, and what would he do. Patttori smiled and said, 'Don't worry, I'll take care of it.' He dragged the dead dog and buried it in the straw stack behind the house, and then said, 'When the owner comes back, tell him that the dog is gone.' You can see how daring and thick-skinned Patttori is just by looking at this."

"That's it, I got it!" Detective M shouted unintentionally. The barbershop owner and the young men, who had been engrossed in the story, were startled for a moment and stared at the detective. Detective M immediately changed his expression.

"I'm busy, so let's do the haircut and shaving later." The detective paid the barbershop fee to the barbershop owner, who didn't know what was going on, and left the place energetically. And when he came out onto the street, he laughed heartily with joy.

"The crime of killing Sun-a and burying her in the straw stack, and the crime of poisoning the dog and burying it in the straw stack... The actions that people do unconsciously are indescribably subtle." He clenched both fists.

"The perpetrator of Sun-a's murder is definitely Patttori. The K police station has already arrested that guy first, so why didn't they arrest him first?" Detective M regretted. And now, he had to wait for the day when, after the 29-day detention period expired due to insufficient evidence, he would be released and immediately arrested.

Detective M went to the house of Yun Ch'ŏmji, who made large baskets, near the murder scene. After coming out of that place, Detective M, having obtained some clue, took a train to Seoul.

An Accomplice to the Crime

Bok-sun, Yun Ch'ŏmji's daughter, whom he had brought from Seoul, began to be questioned by Detective M.

"You probably have a rough idea why you were brought here. Patttori has already confessed everything. It would be better for you to tell the truth about what happened then, as it will be better for your future. If you tell the truth, I will try to make the punishment as light as possible, even if you are guilty..."

Bok-sun, with the mind of a young girl, sobbed anew, knowing that Patttori had already been arrested as the perpetrator of that terrible incident a month ago and that Patttori had confessed everything.

"I... I was ashamed to talk about such a thing," she said, half crying and half speaking, and told the truth about the following chilling facts.

That day was an evening that was not very cold and without wind. Before dinner, Bok-sun was strolling with her four-year-old brother on her back in the back field not far from her house. At that time, she felt the urge to urinate and went into the outhouse in the middle of the field. When she came out after urinating, she saw something wriggling in the barley field over there.

"Who is doing something in the middle of that field?" Drawn by a strange curiosity, Bok-sun quietly went towards that place without making a sound. She was only about two or three kan away when that black thing suddenly stood up. And after looking around frantically, he discovered Bok-sun standing behind her... That foul guy suddenly came next to Bok-sun. That person was a young man wearing an amaga ( 아마구) on his face and a dark durumagi (두루마기).

Bok-sun was so startled by that force that she tried to run away. However, Bok-sun's slender wrist was already in the grasp of that man's strong hand.

"You saw everything I did. If you scream or tell anyone, I'll kill you right away," he said, glaring.

"... ..." Bok-sun was speechless with extreme fear. So she just nodded. The man looked around one more time with a sharp gaze and, knowing that no one was there,

"Follow me," he said, leading Bok-sun and picking up a bamboo stick that had been placed near the fence of Bok-sun's house, which was not far from there, and went back to the barley field.

Shaking as she followed behind, Bok-sun first discovered the woman lying face down next to the straw stack. She couldn't be sure whether she was dead or alive, but Bok-sun felt chills run down her spine as if someone had poured cold water down her back.

The man dragged Bok-sun to the side. And after touching the woman's neck and putting his hand over her mouth, he stood up quietly and then committed a terrible act with the bamboo stick. Bok-sun was like a person under hypnosis. She followed what the man told her to do. And after witnessing a scene that could not be allowed in human conscience, Bok-sun almost fainted.

"You must never tell anyone about this. If you say anything, I'll kill you in the same way."

Not long after that – but how long and scary that time must have been for Bok-sun – the man casually brushed off some straw from the hem of his durumagi (두루마기) and left the place. After that shadow disappeared into the distance, Bok-sun regained her senses and ran frantically to the main road. It was only after coming out onto the main road that she realized that one of her shoes had come off, but she did not dare to go back there again to find her shoe, so she just returned home.

Bok-sun said that the man was Patttori, who always flirted with the women in the village and was a good fighter. With irrefutable evidence, Patttori could not even make an excuse and confessed to the entire crime.

That evening, Patttori had followed Sun-a, who was returning from a friend's house, all the way to this place in order to satisfy his desires. However, because Sun-a resisted strongly, he satisfied himself with violence and committed this horrific crime. <51>

집덤이 속에서

『나-나리 큰일낫습니다. 얼는 오십시요』
아즉도 이른봄의 쌀쌀한 바람이 길가는 사람의 거름을 빨니하게 하는 어느 날 저녁때엿다. K군과 C군의 접경지대(接境地帶)에 잇는 P주재소에 얼골빗을 변해 가지고 뛰여온 농부 하사람이 잇섯다.
『웨그래? 또 원삼이네 내외가 싸흠을 시작하엿느냐?』 일기책에다가 무엇을 기입하고 잇든 주재소 순사는 몹시 침착한 태도로 농부의 허둥대는 것을 꾸짓는 듯이 말하엿다.
『아녜요. 원삼이네 싸흠이 아닙니다 ... . 저 양잠전습소(養蠶專習所) 동편에 잇는 저의 보리밧흘 아시지요. 제가 그곳서 일을 하다가 집으로 드러갈 때 밤에 색기를 꼬을냐고 집덤이 사하둔 곳에 가서 집을 몃단 빼여냇는데 그때 휠긋 보닛간 나리! 집덤이 속에서 녀자의 시체가 나타낫습니다. 저는 엇더케 놀낫든지 허리가 부러질번 한 것을 간신히 참고 이곳까지 뛰여왓습니다. 나리! 벌서 저녁잡수실 때가 되섯겟지만 저하고 한번 안가보시렵니까?』
『무엇? 네 보리밧 집덤이 속에서 녀자의 시체가?』
『네! 녀자인데 아즉 나 젊은 처녀가태요. 그리고 그 죽은 꼴이란 ...』
『자! 나하고 함께 가자』 순사는 먼지가 뽀얏케 안진 등을 비질하엿다. 이 비교적 궁벽한 고소에 잇는 주재소는 십년에 한번도 잇슬가 말가한 큰 사건이다. 주재소 순사는 극도로 긴장하야 각반을 차고 나섯다.
맑게 개인 하날에는 벌서 이른 별들이 이곳 저곳서 반작어리고 잇섯다. 저녁에 부는 바람은 몹시 차지는 안엇다. 벌서 봄이 머지 안엇다는 것을 생각케 하는 저녁때엿다. 이른 봄의 밧뚝길을 농부 한사람과 순사 한사람의 발길을 머물느게 하엿다.
구경이라면 십리 이십리라도 쫏처가는 이 마을 사람들이다. 장거리에 가서 일을 보고 도라가는 젊은 사람 두 사람이 그 뒤를 따라갓다.

『응! 이것은 큰일이다』 농부 김선달이 가르킨 집덤이 속을 한번보고 순사는 깜작 놀라면서 말하엿다. 그리고 그 순간 한거름 뒤로 물러스드니 이래서는 안될 것이라는 것을 깨다럿는지 집덤이 압흐로 갓가히 가서 참혹한 변사체(變死體)를 자세히 보앗다.
『살인 살인하니 이러한 끔직한 살인이 또 어듸 잇단 말이냐?』
김선달이 헤처논 집덤이 속에서 나온 것은 틀님업는 젊은 녀자의 시체엿다. 벌서 오래전부터 이 집덤이 속에 이러케 파무처 잇섯지만 때가 동절인 관계로 시체는 그닥지 부란하지 안엇다.
아즉것 밋근밋근한 희고 보드라운 탄력이 잇는 육체는 이곳 저곳 뚜러진 의복 사이로 보엿다. 야간 살찐 발목에서 점점 위로 시선을 옴겨가면 그 시선이 끗이는 곳에서 누구든지 무의식중에 고개를 돌니킬만치 참혹한 광경이 전개되여 잇섯다. 그곳에는 죽경 일곱푼쯤 되는 지우산때 가튼 대까지가 찌저진 치마사이로 낫타낫다, 주재소 순사는 더 자세히 보기를 주저하엿다.
그리고 요부(腰部)에 이르기까지 파뭇친 시체의 상체를 조곰식 집흘 헤치면서 보앗다. 난잡하게 허트러진 머리카락 그 머리카락 빗은 검은 빗이 생전의 그것과 변하지 안엇지만 해초와 가티 착 달나부튼 녀자의 양쪽 볼은 자지빗으로 변하고 굴가리 감은 두 눈은 움푹 두러가서 구데기가 나지 안엇나 하는 생각을 가지게 하엿다.
『이것은 중대한 살인 사건이다. 나 혼자 염의로 처결할 수가 업다. 김선달 자네는 내가 도라올 때까지 이곳서＜47＞ 직히고 잇게. 얼뜬 뛰여가서 본서에 보고하고 오겟네』 주재소 순사는 발견자 김선달에게 이르는 것이엿다.
『나리 엇더케 혼자 남어 잇스라고 하십니까? 벌서 밤이 되엿는데 송장을 엇더케 저 혼자서 직히고 잇스랍쇼』
『여보게 다 늙은 사람이 무슨 겁이 그럿케 만탄 말인가?』
사실은 이러케 말하는 주재소 순사 자신도 직업상 오기는 왓지만 혼자 남어잇기는 시렷든 것이다. 결국 구경온 두 사람에게 편지를 써서 그곳서 이십리나 되는 C경찰서에 보고하게 하엿‹¤.
등에다가 불을 켜논 순사 엽헤는 어느 사인지 소문을 듯고 뛰여온 사람들이 한사람 두사람 모혀들어 직히고 잇는 순사에게 그닥지 쓸쓸한 생각을 하지 안케되엿다.
시체가 발견된 장소를 좀 더 자세히 설명하자면 그곳은 경의선 C역에서 그닥지 머지 안흔 근자에 C군에 편입이 된 C里라는 곳이고, 압흐로 경의선 철도가 머지 안흔 곳에 잇는 보리밧 속이다. 서편에는 양잠전습소가 잇고 부근에는 농가가 잇고 언덕 하나만 너머가면 장거리가 잇는 곳이엿스나 얼뜬 사람의 눈에 안 띄우는 쓸쓸한 곳이엿다.
집덤이 속에서 나타난 시체는 압헤도 잠간 말햇지만 하복부(下腹部)에 대까지를 찔넛고 그 일단은 흉부까지 달한 극도로 잔학한 참상 검사(檢死)의 결과는 물논 타살노 인정이 되엿다.
피해자는 곤란한 조사를 하지 안코 입은 의복과 시체의 용모와 부근에 허터진 유류품(遺留品)으로 즉시 누구란 것이 판명이 되엿다. 피해자는 C역 압헤서 여관업을 경영하는 리XX의 딸 순아(順娥)(18)이엿다.
유류품으로 집덤이 근처에 떠러저 잇는 것은 피해자 순아의 소유물인 핸드빽과 소설책 한권과 손수건이엿다. 그리고 순아의 것이 안인 다른 녀자가 신엇든 녀자 고무신 한짝이 떠러저 잇섯다.

評判 美人 順娥
이러한 유류품을 가지고 C경찰서의 수사망은 극도로 긴장하엿다. C경찰서의 형사는 우선 피해자의 가정인 즉 역전에 잇는 그의 집에 가서 순아 실종전말(失踪顚末)을 조사하엿다. 그것에 의하면 순아는 약 한달 전 어느 날 오후 동무의 집에 갓다 온다고 하고 나가드니 도라오지 안엇다고 한다.

그 청년들은 이야기 흥을 어덧는지 남의 이목을 거리끼지 안코 큰소래로 주고 밧고 하노라니까.
『그 언젠가 일요일날 김의사가 서울 가고 그 집안 식구는 모다 례배당에 가고 오즉 삼봉이 놈만 집을 직히고 잇데 그려. 그 놈이 그 약을 떡속에다 넛코 그것을 김의사가 사냥갈때 데불고 가는 개에게다 던저주지 안켓나. 삼봉이가 기가 막혀서 『아서! 아서!!』하는 것을 박돌이 놈이 길을 막으며 잇는 동안에 개는 떡을 집어 생켯네 그려. 그러드니 조곰잇다가 개는 두세번 삥글삥글 맴을 돌드니 그대로 씨러저서 다리를 쭉-뻣데그려. 이 꼴을 보고잇든 삼봉이는 울다십히 하면서 주인이 도라오면 큰일이 날텐데 장차 엇더케 할테냐 하니깐 박돌이 놈이 빙글빙글 우스면서 『이러케 해줌세 염려말게』하드니 죽은 개를 끌고 뒤밧 집덤이 속에도 파뭇고 오드니 『주인이 오거던 개가 온데 간데가 업다고 그러게나 그려』 박돌이 놈이 얼마나 대담하고 낫작이 둑거운지 일만 보드라도 알지 안켓나』

복순이는 나어린 처녀의 마음으로 한달 전 그 끔직끔직한 사건은 벌서 박돌이가 범인으로 체포되고 박돌이가 죄다 자백을 한 줄 알고 새삼스러히＜50＞ 소리를 내여 늑겨 울엇다.
