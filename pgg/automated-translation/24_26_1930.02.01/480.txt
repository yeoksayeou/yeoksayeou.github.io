The Story of My Mistake with Someone Else's Daughter
方小波 (Bang Sopo)

I intended to gather and write down stories of my mistakes, but I don't have any amusing stories to tell.

I had been wanting to go for a long time, and finally, I went to P'yŏngyang and Chinnamp'o for lectures and to tell children's stories. Because I had been planning this trip for so long, many adults came to greet me at the train station. On the streets, many people pointed and said, "That's him, that's him." A large crowd followed me until I reached my lodgings. Many people also came to my room to greet me. I was busy returning greetings to each of them, and it took quite a while to finish. As I was bending my waist and sitting down, the door to the inner room opened, and the landlady standing outside greeted me. "My daughter always listens to Mr. Bang's stories in children's magazines. She wants to see you now," she said. Then she continued, "Come on, dear. Why are you so shy? Come and bow." But her young daughter stood behind the door, hesitating to come out. I guessed that she was shy because she was young and there were many guests present. To make it easier for her to come out without feeling embarrassed, I said, "Oh my, let's see. Oh, how cute you are. Where... oh, how cute," using a Seoul accent as if I were coaxing a baby.

At the end of my words, she came out and bowed deeply in the yard. Unexpectedly, she was not a young girl but a tall young woman attending a girls' high school.

I thought, "I've made a mistake!" Before I could even think, my face turned red, and I couldn't think of anything to say to fix the situation. My face just turned bright red.

I had assumed she was a girl around ten years old, and I, a young man, had said "Oh, how cute" to a young woman who was about to get married! How embarrassing was that? Apparently, that young woman is getting married to a Japanese student this spring. <98>
