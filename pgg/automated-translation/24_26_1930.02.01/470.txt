親舊 問病 갓다가 남의 집 內庭 突入, 내가 실수한 이약이 (Visiting a sick friend and mistakenly entering another's courtyard, the story of my mistake)

Visiting a Sick Friend and Mistakenly Entering Another's Courtyard, The Story of My Mistake
Cha Sang-chan

The late Yu Chin-ok (兪鎭億) who was a teacher at Paechae High School until four or five years ago, was not only my classmate at a certain vocational school, but also a particularly close friend. After he moved to Hwadong (花洞), he fell ill and suffered for several days. Upon hearing the news, I went to his house to inquire about his health. Because I did not know the way well, I asked at a side dish store, and they told me it was the house right across from the Women's Student Aid Society (女子苦學生相助會). If it were daytime, I would have checked the nameplate even if I knew the house, but since it was night, I couldn't see the nameplate. I simply went through the middle gate and called out. Coincidentally, the woman of the house came out and said that her husband hadn't been feeling well for several days and was lying down. Since I had heard that Yu (兪) was living in that house and that he was sick, I didn't say anything and just said that I had heard that her husband was ill and had come to inquire about his health, so please tell him. The woman went inside for a while and then came out again and told me to come in. I followed her into the inner courtyard, and the family members, who were taking care of the sick person, hastily moved to the opposite room to avoid me.

I, without a doubt, went right up to the door of the main room and was about to take off my shoes when I glanced through the crack in the glass window and saw, unexpectedly, someone wrapped in a headscarf, making "euheung euheung" noises continuously, leaning against the wall. It was someone I had never seen before, not Yu (兪) at all. I was so dumbfounded that I didn't know what to do, unable to go in or out, just standing there blankly. The family members, not knowing what was going on, just kept telling me to come in, saying it was okay to go in. This was truly a sweat-inducing situation. I thought that making up a story on the spot and coming back out was the best course of action, so I just said, "Yes, I will go in. But I have a friend who came with me to visit, so I'll go out and come in with him." Then, I ran out of the gate as fast as I could and disappeared down another alley. If it's a mistake, this is a big one. But whose house was that, and why did the store mistakenly tell me it was the right house? The next day, I went again to look at the nameplate, and it was a Yu (兪) family, but not Yu Chin-ok's (兪鎭億) house, but a Yu Chin-ok's (兪鎭億이라 하는 이) house, and they hadn't moved in long ago. So, when I asked the store if they knew where the Yu (兪) family who had recently moved into the neighborhood lived, they had misunderstood and directed me to that house.

It's already been three or four years since Yu (兪) passed away, but whenever I pass by that neighborhood, I think of Yu (兪) and also remember my mistake, and I can't help but feel both sadness and amusement. <98>
