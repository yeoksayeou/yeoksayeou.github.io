New Year's Paper Horse Racing Tournament

A Study of Terms Containing the Character "Horse"

The *Records of the Grand Historian* (史記) is sometimes called *Horse History* (馬史) because it was written by Sima Qian (司馬遷) of the Han Dynasty. The list of successful candidates in the civil service examination was called *Sima's List* (司馬榜目), and *Horse Tally* (馬牌) was the official seal of the royal inspector in the old days, used to requisition horses based on the number of horses depicted on the tally. *Baebal Horse* (擺撥馬) was another name for the express horse used to deliver urgent communications in the past. A *Stone Horse* (石馬) is a type of stone object in front of a tomb, a *Wooden Horse* (木馬) is a type of exercise equipment, *Horse Wood* (馬木) is a support for a funeral bier, and a *Bamboo Horse* (竹馬) is a child's toy. *Millipede* (馬陸) is also called *Mayu* (馬蚿) or *Manyeou* (馬蚰), commonly known as *Noraegi*, and it is true that the *Compendium of Materia Medica* states that millipedes are found in the valleys of Hyeonto (玄菟). *Gwahama* (果下馬) is an ancient Korean horse breed. Plants include potatoes (馬鈴薯), chayote (馬鈴苽), vervain (馬鞭草), *Ma Yeong* (馬纓, flower name), smartweed (馬蓼), iris (馬藺), *Mado Ran* (馬兜蘭), broom grass (馬帚, grass name), hornwort (馬尾藻), *Mareop Song* (馬鬣松, found in Xishan Ridge, China), Asiasarum heterotropoides (馬蹄香, another name for *Duheng* 杜衡), *Mageun* (馬靳, wild fennel), puffball (馬勃, a medicinal fungus found in mainland China and Mongolia), and purslane (馬齒莧). Animals include horseflies (馬蠅), horse conch (馬珂螺), *Majeon Su* (馬見愁, a beast name from the Western Regions), *Mago Fish* (馬皐魚), *Maya Chow* (馬牙硝), leeches (馬蛭), *Mabuk* (馬腹, a beast name found in the *Classic of Mountains and Seas*), horse lice (馬虱, horse neck parasites), large leeches (馬蜞, large water leeches), horse cicada (馬蟬, the *Erya Commentary* says it is the largest cicada), dragonfly (*Madu Dae* 馬頭大, a type of dragonfly), and horse-tailed wasp.

Korean Place Names Containing the Character "Horse"

One name for Mount Baekdu (白頭山) is Mount Gaemasan (蓋馬山) (the sound of "Gae" (蓋) is "Hap" (합), and "Ma" (馬) is also called "Ma" (摩), and the sound "Hap Ma" (합마) also means "White Head" (白頭)). In the *Book of the Later Han*, it is written that East Okjeo (東沃沮) is east of Mount Gaema (蓋馬大山), and the Majasu (馬訾水) is another name for the Amnok River (鴨綠江). In Jinan (鎭安), North Jeolla Province, there is Mount Maeisan (馬耳山), which was named by King Taejong (太宗) of the Joseon Dynasty when he went to Jeonju (全州) to inspect the military, saying that its shape resembled horse ears (馬耳). In South Gyeongsang Province, there are Masan-bu (馬山府) and Masan Port (馬山港), which were named after Jousan (騶山) (a mountain shaped like a hawk, commonly called Hawk Mountain, which is now Jousan Park) near Busan (附南). In Ongjin (甕津), Hwanghae Province, there is Masan (馬山) Street, famous for its hot springs, where the county office is located, and there is also Masan (馬山) in the north of Pyongyang (平壤) Fortress. Outside the East Gate of Namhansanseong (南漢山城) in Gwangju-gun (廣州郡), there is Hanmabong Peak (汗馬峯), where Qing (淸) Emperor Taizong (太宗) stationed his horses during the Byeongja Horan (병자호란). In the middle of the East Yeoju (驪州) River in Yeoju-eup (驪州邑), there is Maam Rock (馬巖), and it is said that yellow and black horses emerged from the water under this rock, so the county name is called Hwangryeo (黃驪, former name) or Yeoju (驪州), and the rock name was also derived from this. Yi Mog-eun's (李牧隱) poem "Han Su Gong Go Maam Seok (捍水功高馬巖石)" is about this. About ten li south of the old Pungdeok-eup (豐德邑), there is Mount Baengma (白馬山), where a palace was built during the reign of King Gojong (高宗) of Goryeo, called Usu. On the promontory of the Jangheung (長興) Peninsula in the south of Gangjin-gun (康津郡), facing Goheumdo (古今島), there is Madojin (馬島鎭), which was once a naval base. In the south of Hamyang-gun (咸陽郡), there is Macheon (馬淺, that is, 馬川), and in the west of Miryang-gun (密陽郡), on the banks of the Eungcheon (凝川) River, there is Maam Rock (馬巖), named because its shape resembles a horse drinking water. In Buyeo (扶餘), there is the Baengma River (白馬江), and under Pyongyang's Bubyeok Pavilion (浮碧樓), there is Immagul Cave (麟馬窟), where it is said that King Dongmyeong (東明王) of Goguryeo (高句麗) rode a unicorn horse (麟馬) into this cave and ascended to heaven through Cheonseok Rock (朝天石). Also, upstream of the Daedong River (大洞江) in Pyongyang (平壤), there is Matan (馬灘), where Kim Busik (金富軾) of Goryeo (高麗) defeated the army of Myocheong (妙淸) in the old days, and southwest of Pyongyang (平壤) Fortress, there is Mount Maeupsan (馬邑山), where Tang (唐) General Su Dingfang (蘇定方) stationed his troops in the past. <159> North of Uiju's (義州) Baengma Station (白馬驛), there is Mount Baengma (白馬山), and on the mountain is the Baengma Fortress (白馬城址). This fortress was first built by Kang Gam-chan (姜邯贊) of Goryeo (高麗), and General Im Gyeong-eop (林慶業) repaired it during the Byeongja Horan (丙子亂) of the Joseon Dynasty to defend against the Manchu invaders. There is also the Baengma River (白馬嶺河) in Uiju (義州). Under Bannyongsan Mountain (盤龍山) in the northeast of Hamheung (咸興), there is Chimaldae (馳馬臺), where Yi Taejo (李太祖) used to ride horses, and there is also Chimaldae (馳馬臺) in the south of Cheorwon-gun (鐵原郡), which is said to be where General Kim Deok-ryeong (金德齡) used to ride horses. In front of Sinpo Port (新浦港) in Bukcheong (北靑), there is Mayangdo Island (馬養島), which is about fifty li in circumference, and is the largest island in South Hamgyong Province, where the Japanese warship Kinshu Maru was wrecked during the Russo-Japanese War. Also, in the north of Gyeongwon (慶源), there is Mount Mayusan (馬乳山), named because its shape resembles a horse's udder. On the border between Hamgyong (咸鏡) and Pyeongan (平安) Provinces, there is a great pass called Masikryeong (馬息嶺), and in Bukcheong (北靑) there is Madiryeong (馬底嶺), and in Boseong (寶城), South Jeolla Province, there is Majisan (馬峙山). In Haeju (海州), there is Yaengma Pond (躍馬池), named because Yi Taejo (李太祖) was able to overcome the muddy pond when he defeated the Japanese pirates. There is a Victory Stele (勝捷碑) erected during the reign of King Yeongjo (英祖). In the east of Gangneung-gun (江陵郡), there is Yongma Pond (龍馬池), where it is said that a dragon horse (龍馬) emerged from the pond and the progenitor of the Gangneung Kim clan (江陵 金氏) always rode it back and forth to Gaeseong (開城), and after the Kim clan died, the dragon horse flew into the air, so it was named Yongma Pond. On Namsan Mountain (南山) in Seoul (京城), there is Daeseolmahyeon (大雪馬峴), and 소설마현, and in Samcheong-dong (三淸洞), there is 말우몰이.

Historically appearing place names with the character "Horse" include the Gaema Kingdom (蓋馬國) near Okjeo (沃沮) in the Okjeo period, and Mahan (馬韓) in the Three Han (三韓) period, which was located in Geumma-gun (金馬郡, present-day Iksan-gun 益山郡). During the reign of King Bojang (寶藏王) of Goguryeo (高句麗), a divine person appeared on Mallyeongsan Mountain (馬嶺山, location unknown) and said to the people, "Your country is extravagant and immoral, and its demise is near." King Dongseong (東城王) of Baekje (百濟) went hunting in Sajiseowon (泗沘西原) and encountered snow, sleeping in Mapo Village (馬浦村) before being assassinated by Jwapyeong Mokga (佐平苜加). (Mok 苜 is one of the Eight Clans of Baekje). Tsushima Island (對馬島) was originally territory of Silla (新羅) but was abandoned after the reign of King Jijeung (智證王) and occupied by Japan. By the Treaty of Shimonoseki (甲午 馬關條約), Joseon (朝鮮) became completely independent.

Famous Historical Figures of Joseon and Horses

Baengmagongson (白馬公孫)
Because Gija (箕子) rode a white horse when he fled to Joseon (朝鮮), Gija (箕子) is called Baengmagongja (白馬公子, White Horse Prince) in ancient records. (The people of Yin (殷) revered the color white, so they also rode white horses. As the poem says, "There is a guest, there is a guest, and his horse is also white (有客有客에 亦白其馬).")

Inma Deukgwija (因馬得貴子)
King Hae Buru (解夫婁) of Dongbuyeo (東扶餘) was childless in his old age and went around praying to mountains and rivers. One day, his horse reached Gonyeon (鯤淵), and upon seeing a large rock, it cried and refused to go. The king found it strange and rolled the rock away, revealing a golden, frog-shaped child. The king was greatly pleased and said, "Heaven has given me a son," and raised him, who became King Geumwa (金蛙王).

Damna Samsinnin's (耽羅 三神人) Deukgu (得駒)
The three divine beings of Tamna (耽羅國, present-day Jeju 濟州), Yang Eul-na (良乙那), Go Eul-na (高乙那), and Bu Eul-na (夫乙那), were hunting deer by the sea when a wooden box sealed with purple mud arrived from Japan. When they opened it, there were three women in blue clothes, a colt (駒), a calf (犢), and five grains. The three men divided one woman each, planted the five grains, and raised cattle and horses.

Silla's Sijo (新羅 始祖) and Horses
So Bal-gong (蘇伐公), the village chief of Goheo Village (高墟 村長), heard a horse crying in the Yangsan (楊山) foothills of the Najeong Forest (蘿井林間). He went to investigate and found a large egg-shaped gourd (大瓠). He found it strange and broke it open, revealing a young child who was very intelligent and precocious. The residents of the Six Departments (六部) enthroned him as king, who was none other than Park Hyeokgeose (朴赫居世), the founder of Silla (新羅 始祖). At that time, Dongokjeo (東沃沮) presented twenty fine horses (良馬) as tribute and congratulated him, saying that a sage had been born in Namhan (南韓).

Baekje's Songma (百濟 送馬)
During the reign of King Nulji (訥祗王) of Silla (新羅), Baekje (百濟) sent a fine horse (良馬) and a white falcon (白鷹), which Silla (新羅) reciprocated with gold and bright pearls.

Sojiwang's (炤智王) Jema (祭馬)
Because King Soji (炤智王) of Silla (新羅) avoided disaster thanks to a crow's letter (烏書), every year on January 15th, he offered glutinous rice (糯米飯, the origin of present-day Yakgwa 藥食) to the crow, and because horses were capable of service (馬能服務), he held a ritual for horses (馬에 祭) on the Horse Day (午日), which was called Sinil (愼日).

Using Maeui (馬戱) to Destroy Gaya (滅伽倻)
During the reign of King Talhae (脫解王) of Silla (新羅), the two countries of Gansisan (干尸山) and Geochilsan (居漆山) were adjacent to each other and constantly invaded each other. Geodo (居道) wanted to annex them, so he gathered horses (群馬) in Jangto Field (張吐野) and had his soldiers practice riding every day, which was called Maheui (馬戱). The people of the two countries regarded it as a common occurrence and did not prepare for defense, so Geodo (居道) eventually destroyed the two countries. Later, during the reign of King Jijeung (智證王), Kim Isabu (金異斯夫) used Geodo's (居道) tactics to destroy the Gaya Kingdom (伽倻國) again.

Goguryeo Dongmyeongwang (高句麗 東明王) and Horses
Haemosu (解慕漱), the son of the Heavenly Emperor (天帝 子) of Bukbuyeo (北扶餘國), seduced Yuhwa (柳花), the daughter of Habaek (河伯), in the Amnok Sil (鴨綠室) under Ungsim Mountain (熊心山下). Her parents blamed her for following him without a matchmaker and exiled her to the Ubalsu (優渤水邊) south of Taebaek Mountain (太白山 南). King Geumwa (金蛙) of Dongbuyeo (東扶餘王) met her during an outing, and she became pregnant and gave birth to an egg. Geumwa (蛙) found it strange and abandoned it on the roadside, but oxen and horses did not dare to step on it... (omitted from the original text) A boy emerged from the egg, and at the age of seven, he made his own bows and arrows and was able to hit the bullseye every time. That was Dongmyeongwang (東明王) Jumong (朱蒙). He also always rode a unicorn horse (麟馬) and ascended to heaven to report his deeds.

Danro Malyeop (斷路馬鬣)
Queen U (于氏) of Goguryeo (高句麗 山上王后) cut off the manes of the road horses (路馬의 鬣) to ingratiate herself with King Dongcheon (東川王). The King said, "The road horses have no manes, how pitiful (路馬가 鬣이 없으니 可憐하다)." Queen U (于后) realized that King Dongcheon (東川王) was virtuous and enthroned him as king.

Jeokbaengma Songjin (赭白馬 送晋)
King Jangsu (長壽王) of Goguryeo (高句麗) sent an envoy to Jin (晋), sending a reddish-white horse (赭白馬) as a local product.

Masaeng Gwaeu (馬生怪牛)
During the reign of King Onjo (溫祚王), the founder of Baekje (百濟 始祖), a horse gave birth to a cow with one head and two bodies (一首二身) in a house in Hanseong (漢城).

Baekje Song Gwaha Ma (百濟 送果下馬)
King Mu (武王) of Baekje (百濟) sent an envoy to Tang (唐), sending a Gwahama (果下馬) as a gift.

Baengma Joryong (白馬釣龍)
According to legend, Su Dingfang (蘇定方) of Tang (唐) was crossing the Baekje (百濟) River when the clouds and mist became dark, which he attributed to the work of dragons. He used a white horse (白馬) as bait to fish for the dragon, hence the name Baengma River (白馬江).

Eumma Paegang (飮馬浿江)
Gyeon Hwon (甄萱) of Later Baekje (後百濟) sent a letter to Taejo (太祖) of Goryeo (高麗) saying, "I will hang my bow in Pyongyang (掛弓平壤) and let my horse drink from the Pye River (欲馬浿江)." Nam Cheo's (南秋) poem on Mangyeongdae (萬景臺), "My lifelong ambition to let my horse drink from the Pye River (平生飮馬浿江志) was never fulfilled, leaving only an empty pavilion (未就而亡空有臺)," refers to this. Gyeon Hwon (甄萱) also sent the Jeolyeongdo Horse (絶影島馬) as a local product to Taejo (太祖) of Goryeo (高麗), but after hearing the prophecy "When the Jeolyeong horse arrives, Baekje will fall (絶影名馬至에 百濟亡)," he regretted it and requested its return. Taejo (麗太祖) of Goryeo (高麗) laughed and sent it back.

Pilma Mupan (匹馬無返)
When Kang Gam-chan (姜邯贊) of Goryeo (高麗) decisively defeated the Khitan soldiers (契丹兵), not a single horse (匹馬) remained.

Mabal Guk (馬八國)
During the reign of King Chungseon (忠宣王) of Goryeo (高麗), the Mabal Kingdom (馬八國, a type of Tubo 吐藩) sent an envoy to offer a silver silk hat (銀絲帽).

Maje Chi Sang Gukbo (馬踶傷國寶)
At the end of Goryeo (麗末), Jo Min-su (曹敏修) and others enthroned Wang Chang's (王昌) son, Wang U (王禑), as king, but on the day of his inauguration, a horse (馬) galloped and broke the lock (匣錀) on the box containing the national treasure (國寶). The treasure itself ran away (斯斯로 出走).

Kim Yu-sin's (金庾信) Jeomma (斬馬)
When Kim Yu-sin (金庾信) of Silla (新羅) was young, he frequented the house of the female entertainer Cheongwan (天官). His mother found out and strictly forbade him, and Shin (信) swore never to go again. However, one day, Shin (信) was drunk and his horse, out of habit, went to Cheongwan's house again. Yu-sin (庾信) realized this and cut off the horse's head with his sword (칼로 그 말을 베히고) and returned home, never going there again. The female entertainer composed a lament (怨歌) that was passed down in the world.

Seongma Deukh 후 (息馬得后)
While leading troops, Taejo (麗太祖) stopped in Seokju (息州, present-day Pungdeok 豐德). Resting his horse (馬) under an old willow tree (古柳下), he saw a maiden drawing water whose virtue and appearance were extraordinary. He summoned her and made her his queen, who was none other than Lady Ryu (柳氏), the daughter of Ryu Cheon-gung (柳天弓) and Queen Sinhyee (神惠王后).

Seo Pil 간 구마 유주 (徐弼諫救馬廐主)
During the reign of King Gwangjong (光宗) of Goryeo (高麗), an inner stable horse (內廐馬) died. The king wanted to punish the caretaker (主者), but Pil (弼) cited Confucius's saying about not asking about horses (孔子의 不問馬說) and argued against it, so Gwangjong (光宗) did not pursue the matter further.

Maje Tapseol Geonraedong (馬蹄踏雪乾雷動)
During the reign of King Yejong (睿宗) of Goryeo (高麗), the Liao (遼) envoy Meng Chu (孟初) came to Korea, and Kim In-cun (金仁存) was appointed as the welcoming official (接伴官). Chu (初) looked down on Kim (金) because of his young age, but the two went out to the countryside together. It was the beginning of clear snow (霽雪). Chu (孟) first sang a line, saying, "Horse hooves tread on snow, dry thunder roars (馬蹄踏雪乾雷動)." In-cun (仁存) responded, saying, "Flag tails turn in the wind, fierce fire flies (旗尾翻風烈火飛)." Chu (孟) was taken aback and did not dare to look down on him again.

Yi Gyu-bo's (李奎報) 역마 (㤼馬)
Baegun Geosa (白雲居士) Yi Gyu-bo (李奎報) of Goryeo (高麗) was afraid to ride horses (말타기를 㤼하야) when he was young, and if someone shook his riding legs even a little, he would turn pale.

Ilmasa (一馬時)
Yi Gyu-bo (李奎報) composed a poem about the extreme high tide times every day at the Jo River (祖江上) in Tongjin (通津), saying, "Three rabbits, three dragons water, three snakes, one horse time, sheep three, monkey also two, the moon is dark, it is like this (三兎三龍水, 三蛇一馬時, 羊三猿亦二, 月黑復如斯)."

Sagieui (斯紀) Sangguk Matii (傷國馬蹄)
Sagi (斯紀) of Baekje (百濟) injured the hoof of the national horse (國馬의 蹄) and fled to Goguryeo (高句麗) for fear of punishment. Later, he came and said, "Although their soldiers are many, if you break the red flag first (朱旗만 먼저 破하면), the rest will collapse on their own (그 餘는 自潰)." He then attacked Goguryeo (高句麗) with the crown prince (太子), greatly defeating them.

Choe Seok's (崔碩) Palmabi (八馬碑)
During the reign of King Chungnyeol (忠烈王) of Goryeo (高麗), Choe Seok (崔碩) was the magistrate (昇平府使) of Seungpye

新春 誌上 競馬大會
馬字 들은 名辭考
史記를 或稱 馬史라 하니 漢 司馬遷이 作한 所以오, 進士 榜目冊을 司馬榜目이라 하고, 馬牌는 전날 御史의 官印이니 그 패에 그린 馬數에 의하야 役馬를 징발하얏고 擺撥馬는 전날 급한 통신을 전하든 驛馬의 별칭이다. 石馬는 墓前 石物 중의 일종이오, 木馬는 運動具의 일종이오, 馬木은 喪轝의 支木이오, 竹馬는 아동의 玩具다. 馬陸은 일명 馬蚿 又는 馬蚿 又는 馬蚰, 속칭 노랙이니 本草綱目에 馬陸이 出 玄菟川谷이라 함이 是오, 果下馬는 조선 古馬名이다. 식물에는 馬鈴薯, 馬鈴苽, 馬鞭草, 馬纓(花名), 馬蓼, 馬藺, 馬兜蘭, 馬帚(草名), 馬尾藻, 馬鬣松(出 支那廬山西嶺), 馬蹄香(杜衡의 별칭), 馬靳(野茴香), 馬勃(藥用菌類니 出 支那本部及蒙古), 馬齒莧 등이 잇고 동물에는 馬蠅, 馬珂螺(*屬), 馬見愁(西域 獸名), 馬皐魚, 馬牙硝, 馬蛭馬腹(獸名見山海經), 馬虱(馬頸寄生蟲), 馬蜞(水蛭의 大한 者), 馬蟬(爾雅註에 云蜩中 最大者), 馬頭大(蜻蛉의 일종), 馬尾蜂.

馬字로 지은 朝鮮의 地名
白頭山의 一名은 蓋馬山이니(蓋의 音은 합이오 馬는 一稱 摩니, 音으로 합 마는 亦 白頭의 意다) 後漢書에는 東沃沮는 蓋馬大山의 東에 잇다 함이 是오 馬訾水는 鴨綠江의 별명이다. 全北 鎭安에는 馬耳山이 잇스니 李朝 太宗이 全州에 講武하러 갓슬 때에 其 形이 馬耳와 如하다 하야 命名하얏고 慶南에는 馬山附, 馬山港이 잇스니 附南에 騶山(山形이 매지와 가틈으로 속칭 매지山이니 지금에 騶山公園이 잇다.) 잇슴으로 仍名하얏다. 黃海道 甕津에는 溫泉場으로 유명한 馬山市街가 잇스니 郡廳 所在地오, 平壤城 北에도 馬山市가 잇다. 廣州郡 南漢山城 東門 外에는 汗馬峯이 잇스니 병자호란 때에 淸 太宗의 駐馬하엿든 곳이요, 驪州邑 東驪江 중에는 馬巖이 잇스니 俗傳에 黃馬와 驪馬가 이 巖下 水中에서 出한 故로 郡名을 黃驪(舊名) 또는 驪州라 칭하고 巖名도 此로 인하야 得하얏스니 李牧隱 詩에 捍水功高馬巖石이 是오, 舊 豐德邑 南方 十里許에는 白馬山이 잇스니 高麗 高宗 때에 右蘇라 칭하고 宮闕을 營하엿던 곳이오, 康津郡 南 長興半島 突角에는 古今島와 相對하야 馬島鎭이 잇스니 前日 水軍의 鎭處다. 咸陽郡 南에는 馬淺(즉 馬川)이 잇고 密陽郡 西 凝川邊에는 馬巖이 잇스니 其 形이 馬의 飮水함과 如함으로 名함이오, 扶餘에는 白馬江이 잇고 平壤 浮碧樓 下에는 麟馬窟이 잇스니 俗傳에 高句麗 東明王이 麟馬를 타고 此 窟에 入하얏다가 朝天石으로 出하야 승천하얏다 한다. 또 平壤 大洞江 상류에는 馬灘이 잇스니 녯날에 고려 金富軾이 妙淸의 軍을 破하던 곳이오, 平壤城 西南에는 馬邑山이 잇스니 전일 唐將 蘇定方의 駐兵處다. 義州 白馬驛 北方에는＜159＞ 白馬山이 잇고 山上에 白馬城址가 잇슊�니 此 城은 元 高麗 姜贊邯의 始築한 것으로 李朝 丙子亂時 林慶業 將軍이 修築하야 胡賊을 防하던 곳이다. 그리고 義州에는 또 白馬嶺河가 잇다. 咸興 東北 盤龍山 下에는 李太祖의 馳馬하던 馳馬臺가 잇고 鐵原郡 南에도 馳馬臺가 잇스니 속칭 金德齡 將軍의 馳馬하던 곳이라 한다. 北靑의 新浦港 前面에는 周圍 약 오십 리 되는 馬養島가 잇스니 咸南 제일의 大島로 日露戰爭時 日艦 金州丸의 遭難處다. 또 慶源 北方에는 馬乳山이 잇스니 其 形이 馬乳와 如한 故로 名함이오, 咸鏡·平安 兩道의 界에는 馬息嶺이란 大嶺이 잇고 北靑에는 馬底嶺, 全南 寶城에는 馬峙山이 잇다. 海州에는 躍馬池가 잇스니 李太祖가 倭寇를 격파할 때에 馬가 泥池을 能超한 故로 仍名하얏는데 英祖時에 立한 勝捷碑가 잇고 江陵郡 東에는 龍馬池가 잇스니 속칭에 其 池에서 龍馬가 出하야 江陵 金氏 始祖가 항상 타고 開城으로 왕래하다가 金 氏가 죽은 뒤에 龍馬가 공중으로 飛騰하야간 故로 龍馬池라 名하얏다 한다. 京城 南山에는 大雪馬峴, 小雪馬峴이 잇고 三淸洞에는 말우몰이 잇다.

此에 歷史上에 낫타나는 馬 字 地名으로 말하면 沃沮 시대에 其 부근에 蓋馬國이 잇섯고 三韓 시대에 馬韓이 잇스니 그것은 金馬郡(今 益山郡)에 都한 所以다. 高句麗 寶藏王 때에 神人이 馬嶺山(今 未祥)에 出하야 人에게 語하야 曰 汝國이 奢侈無道하니 亡無日矣라 云하얏고, 百濟 東城王은 泗泚西原에 出獵하다가 눈을 만나 馬浦村에서 자다가 佐平苜加에게 彼殺하얏다. (苜은 百濟 八姓의이다.) 對馬島는 본래 新羅의 領地로 智證王 이후에 放棄하야 日本이 차지를 하게 되엿고 甲午 馬關條約에 의하야 朝鮮이 完全 獨立되엿다.

朴曄이 聚妓爲風馬戱
光海朝 朴曄이 平壤監司로 잇슬 때에 晝夜로 수백의 기생을 모와 同處하며 風馬의 戱를 하얏다.

金景瑞의 名馬
壬亂時 西道 名將 金景瑞가 名馬가 잇섯는데 其 뒤 光海 己未에 景瑞가 副元帥로 姜弘立을 좃차 建州를 征伐하다가 弘立이 敵에게 降하고 景瑞는 不屈하고 敵에게 死하니 其 馬가 景瑞의 頭를 물고 本國으로 도라왓슴으로 그것을 義馬라 칭하얏다.

仁祖畵馬
宣祖가 말년에 諸王孫을 모와 가지고 書畵를 試習하는데 仁祖가 말을 그럿슴으로 宣祖가 특히 사랑하야 그 畵幅을 鰲城에게 下賜하엿더니 鰲城이 北靑에 流竄할 때에 다시 金瑬에게 전하니 瑬가 此를 無心이 壁上에 부치엿다. 仁祖가 潛邸 때에 外出하얏다가 우연이 비를 피하야 길가 집으로 드러가니 그 집은 곳 金瑬家라. 인조는 주인의 집 壁上에 자기의 畵馬가 잇는 것을 보고 괴이 생각하며 또 주인이 친절히 待接하는 것을 감사이 생각하야 그 후부터 瑬와 相親하야 遂히 反正의 大業을 이르럿다. 그것은 대개 宣祖가 먼저 知鑑이 잇서서 鰲城에게 이 畵馬를 주어 仁祖의 후일 反正할 것을 암시하고 鰲城은 또 金瑬를 反正功臣될 인물인 것을 예측하고 그 畵馬를 주어 奇緣을 지엇던 것이다. 그리고 그 때 金瑬까지는 仁祖의 如何한 인물인 것을 잘 못낫스나 그것은 그의 부인이 꿈에 大駕가 臨門한 꿈을 뀌고 仁祖의 非常한 인물인 것을 미리 짐작하야 그와 가티 厚待한 것이다. 이것은 宣祖와 鰲城의 知鑑도 知鑑이려니와 金瑬 夫人의 知鑑이 또한 어지간치 안은 것이엿다.

林慶業 將軍의 義馬
林慶業이 또한 名馬가 잇섯는데 將軍이 御命으로 淸에 拿送되다가 金郊驛에서 亡命하야 그의 愛妾 梅環은 本家로 보내고 愛馬는 加平山野에 放棄하엿더니 후에 엇지 되야 그 말이 太僕에 流入하엿섯다. 그 후 慶業이 歸國하야 金自點 誣告로 寃死하게 되니 馬가 눈물을 흘이며 三日을 먹지 안타가 또한 죽엇섯다.

燕山의 採靑使와 孝宗의 伐大驄
가튼 말이라도 사람을 따러 그 用途가 다르다. 燕山은 소위 採靑使를 보내 八道 名馬를 모와다가 날마다 질탕이 놀고 孝宗은 南漢의 원수를 가푸랴고 內廐에다 天下 名馬를 養하얏스니 謂之伐大驄이다.(끗) ＜165＞
