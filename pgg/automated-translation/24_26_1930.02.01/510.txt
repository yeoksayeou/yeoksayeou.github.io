Night Pilgrimage
						Roa

The greatest of all,
Without a master,
Neither yours nor mine,
A jewel of the night sky,
Every night it rises,
Chasing away the darkness, chasing away the sighs... ... .

Over the mountains, over the sea,
Embracing an armful of ominous energy, when it rises, here and there
Like envoys paying homage to the King,
Gathering ash-colored, white robes
Cloud by cloud... ... .

Three thousand ri (里) of land to shine upon, though the way is far,
"Is the village maiden asleep?"
Passing through mountain paths,
"Is the reed trembling?"
Passing through the woods,
Riding the clouds, it came running to Seoul.

Lights, lights, look at those lights,
Building a trap inside the building,
Look at those lights beckoning us in,
Ensnared by the five-colored suction cups,
Look at that man bleeding and collapsing,
Resentment, conflict, flames of five colors!
Smiling woman, the glory of five colors! <105>

Glaring eyes, the heart of a viper,
Bloodless face, buried bones,
Anger surging, blood vessels throbbing
Look at that outcry, those arms
Oh, this land is terrifying, noisy!

Unable to bear it, turning its head,
Its flushed face turning blue,
It fled to the ice sea of the northern country,
This ice sea where it wrestles with polar bears,
In the distance, the ice weeps, "Whirr! Whirr!"
In the moonlight, scooping up the ice with a shovel,
The heart of the wandering people digging graves weeps.

This country, this sea, with troubled hearts,
The deep heart of the moon embraced by the clouds,
Three thousand ri of land to shine upon, the way is far
The world is as still as a graveyard,
All night long, it sleeps, its weeping ceasing
If I were to shatter the sweet dreams of hateful people,
Would I fear the thunder! Would I avoid the rain!
If I cannot sleep because my heart is tormented,
I will let you sleep in the starlight of hope
I will illuminate your dark heart!

The night road is long,
The sorrow is deep,
Passing through this land, the moon's face grows thin
The night road is long,
The sorrow is deep,
Passing through this land, the moon's face grows thin <106>
(1930. 1. 14)
