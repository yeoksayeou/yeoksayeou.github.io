Annals of the Rise and Fall of Korean High Officials: The Three Royal In-Law Families Weeping at Ruin, Where is the World of Yore Now?
						Ilhyang Am

Seventy years have passed since the ascension of King Kojong in the year of Kapja (甲子). Though seventy years is not a long time, the tempests that surrounded Korea during that time were quite severe. During these repeated storms, not a few figures rose to shake the heavens. Even today, now that the waters have receded and the stones are revealed, it would be interesting to see if their former glories continue.

The Cho Royal In-Law Family (趙府院君家)

The Cho Song-ha (趙成夏) family played the biggest role in placing Kojong on the throne. Cho Song-ha's grandfather, Cho Man-yong (趙萬永)'s daughter, was Queen Dowager Cho (趙大妃). Although Queen Dowager Cho became the queen of Crown Prince Ikjong (翼宗), she did not see her family rise to power because Ikjong passed away early. Thanks to Queen Dowager Sunwon (純元王后), the mother of Ikjong, who reigned for a long time, the Kim clan monopolized power for four reigns: Sunjong, Ikjong, Honjong, and Cheoljong (純宗翼宗憲宗哲宗). Therefore, Queen Dowager Cho was always displeased. When King Cheoljong passed away, Queen Dowager Sunwon had already died, so there were three widowed elders: Queen Dowager Cho, Queen Dowager Hong, and Queen Dowager Kim, but Queen Dowager Cho was the highest in rank, so she took charge of palace affairs. The Daewongun (大院君) noticed this and adopted his second son as Queen Dowager Cho's son, intending to cut off the power of the in-laws and seize absolute power for himself. He sent Cho Song-ha to appease Queen Dowager Cho, and at that time, Cho Song-ha was like Han Xin (韓信), who always won when he fought. Thanks to Cho Song-ha's efforts, Kojong inherited the throne, so power would naturally go to Cho Song-ha, but at that time, Cho Song-ha was a young man, and the Daewongun was a seasoned general. The Daewongun tricked Cho Song-ha into going to P'yongan Province as governor and monopolized power within and without. Cho Song-ha only did good for others in vain. But if he couldn't have power, wouldn't he at least have an official position? Cho Song-ha became Minister of Personnel (吏曹判書), and his son, Cho Tong-myon (趙東冕), also became Minister of Personnel at the age of 30, making seven generations of Ministers of Personnel from Cho Om (趙曮) to Cho Tong-myon. Even in the time of King Yongjong (英宗), who valued propriety and integrity, Cho Om was notorious for corruption (貪贓), and even more so in recent years, when integrity has disappeared. So, they must have amassed a tremendous amount of wealth. Therefore, people called their family "seven generations of Ministers of Personnel and seven generations of millionaires." Not only that, but their family possessions, luxurious furnishings, and collection of calligraphy and art were so vast that even the current richest man in Korea, Min Yong-hui (閔泳徽), could not compare. Cho Tong-myon's son, Cho Myong-gu (趙命九), unfortunately died early without offspring, so a distant relative from Ch'ungju (忠州) was adopted to continue the lineage. <18> This was Cho Nam-sok (趙南奭), a millionaire of 20 years ago. The family's wealth remains, but due to successive early deaths, three widows, including Cho Myong-gu's wife, Cho Myong-gu's mother, and even Cho Myong-gu's sister-in-law, who came as a dowry, live in a newly built hundred-room mansion outside Tongdaemun. In the past, their house was a gathering place for those seeking office, but now it is a gathering place for widows.

The Min Royal In-Law Family (閔府院君家)

The Daewongun, after placing his second son on the throne, chose a daughter from a family where the father or brother held a high position to be the queen, fearing that he would lose power again. But he feared losing power again and adopted Min Song-ho (閔性鎬), the son-in-law of the Daewongun's wife, as the son of Min Ch'i-rok (閔致祿), who had died without a son as the magistrate of Kwach'on (果川縣監). The daughter of Min Ch'i-rok, a fatherless orphan (無父孤女), became the queen, who is Queen Min (閔中殿). It is difficult to predict the ways of the world. When King Kojong and King Chungjong were young and did not know the ways of the world, everything went according to the Daewongun's wishes. But as Kojong grew older, there was no father or uncle in power who could always entrust all the power in the country to the Daewongun, and the Daewongun's administration was too oppressive, so many scholars (士論) voiced their opinions, and there were many enemies among the officials. Moreover, Cho Song-ha and Cho Nyong-ha (趙寧夏) constantly opposed him, so Queen Dowager Cho's trust began to waver. As those who held power and those who sought to seize power centered on the Daewongun looked for opportunities to undermine the Daewongun's power, the Daewongun inflicted an unforgivable insult on Cho Nyong-ha, and when the news reached the palace, Queen Dowager Cho became even more displeased. Finally, one earth-shattering memorial (上疏) from Ch'oe Myeon-am Ik-hyeon (崔勉庵益鉉) exposed all the secrets. The Daewongun was somewhat embarrassed and, listening to Min Chong-muk (閔種黙)'s advice, handed over power to his son, Yi Chae-myon (李載冕), intending to continue wielding influence from behind the scenes. However, he lost his ten-year monopoly on power, which was the reason for the Daewongun's downfall and the beginning of Min Song-ho's rise to power. From this time, the Yoheung Min clan (驪興閔氏) shook the entire country. It would have been better to give the Daewongun some power, like a cornered cat that turns around and bites, but they completely alienated him, so he had no power to appoint even a low-ranking military officer (萬戶僉使). The Daewongun harbored resentment and sent a chest of explosives on the anniversary of the  funerals for the prince of Yeoyang (驪陽府院君), killing Min Seung-ho, his wife, and their son. From this incident, the Min and Yi families became irreconcilable enemies, and not only did the in-laws not meet, but the Daewongun and King Kojong were estranged for a long time. In the midst of this struggle for power, which severed even familial ties, the wealth and glory of the Min clan were at their peak. The three members of Min Seung-ho's family were killed by the explosion, and because of their deaths, all the members of the Min clan rose to power, but the first to benefit were the brothers Min T'ae-ho (閔台鎬) and Min Kyu-ho (閔奎鎬). Min T'ae-ho was the eldest grandson who presided over the ancestral rites (不遷之位), but he was swayed by power and adopted his only son, Min Yong-ik (閔泳翊), as Min Song-ho's son, thus setting up a power base. Min T'ae-ho still knew how to write and maintained some semblance of propriety, but Min Kyu-ho was even more obsessed with gain and loss. The unique product of the demise of the Yi dynasty, the so-called "Seoha Law" (書下法), also began during Min Kyu-ho's time. After Min Yong-ik became Min Sang-ho's son, the family of Min Ch'i-rok (閔致祿), the Prince of Yosong (驪城府院君), once again continued the interrupted lineage, and after taking over the Chukdong Palace (竹同宮), their house became truly large and their power grew even greater. Unfortunately, a few years after Min Yong-ik came to power, the incident of Kapsin (甲申) occurred. In the knifewinds of the Postal Administration Bureau (郵政局), Min Yong-ik's ear fell off <19> and after he fled to Shanghai, the great love was completely emptied, and the two widows, Min Song-ho's wife and Min Yong-ik's wife, held power through sealed letters (封書) in the twelve-room main hall, which led to many ugly rumors and the emergence of many power-hungry members of the Min clan. Because Min Yong-ik went to Shanghai without a son, the battle for adoption (立後) was also very intense. One after another, people came and went like rats in a burrow, but in the end, they were all rejected, and Min Yong-ik's son born in Shanghai became the heir (承嫡), and he was able to use the great love of Chukdong Palace again. That is, Min Chong-sik (閔庭植). Because Min Chong-sik was originally unfamiliar with Korean affairs and Min Chong-sik's wife tried to take control from within, it became a case of "the hen crowing at dawn" (牝鷄司晨), and on the outside, all the fake Min clan members used him as a conduit, so Min Chong-sik was squeezed from within and without and crossed over to Shanghai, where he gave up everything in just a few months. If Min Yong-ik's wife had lived longer, she might have drained some money to her own family, but it wouldn't have been so absurd. Unfortunately, she did not enjoy a long life, and now the shrines of the Princes of Yoyang and Yosong are being moved to the corner of the kitchen in dilapidated houses. Now, that family is even more miserable than the aforementioned Cho Royal In-Law family. In the end, the family, which was the result of a power struggle between a Yi man and a Min woman, is also being broken up by a Min man and a Yi woman. What kind of fate is that?

The Hong Royal In-Law Family (洪府院君家)

What happened to the family of Queen Dowager Hong (洪大妃), the second wife of King Honjong (憲宗), the daughter-in-law of Queen Dowager Cho? Queen Dowager Hong's family is now the family of Hong Sun-hyong (洪淳馨), the Minister Hong (洪判事). The Hong clan had completely declined since King Chongjong (正宗大王), but Queen Dowager Hong's father became the magistrate of Hamyol (咸悅) and gave birth to Queen Dowager Hong, after which they were able to take a deep breath again. However, Queen Dowager Hong was neglected by King Honjong, so she was not able to wield much power even during Honjong's lifetime and was completely suppressed by the Andong Kim clan. Moreover, after Honjong passed away and Cheoljong ascended the throne, Queen Dowager Kim and Queen Dowager Cho, her mother-in-law and grandmother-in-law, were still alive, so she could not do anything for her family. Even after Cheoljong passed away, Queen Dowager Cho lived a long life, and Queen Min supported her family as Kojong's queen, so Queen Dowager Hong's family could not wield power, and her nephew Hong Sun-hyong barely became a minister (判書). He had been obscure for a long time, so he must have been very greedy for money, because soon after he took office as the governor of Hwanghae Province (黃海監司), not a single decent piece of clothing was left for the people of Hwanghae Province. While he was the administrator of Songdo (松都留守), the wealthy people of Songdo could not sleep soundly. He accumulated so much money that he had ten thousand bags of rice (万石), becoming the second richest man after Min Yong-hwi. The wealthy man did not give his adopted son Hong Rin-pyo (洪麟杓) even a single penny for a pack of cigarettes and made him suffer a hard life until he died. He favored his son Hak-pyo (鶴杓), who he had through a dream of eight fairies (8선녀), but he squandered everything in gambling dens and restaurants dealing in rice futures (미두판료리집). Now, an old man in his eighties, not knowing what tomorrow will bring, is eating millet porridge in a rice cake shop in Yangju (楊州). He used to arrest people and beat them, but now he is dragged around in shackles for hundreds of miles. What kind of fate is that? <20>

						一鄕暗
갑자(甲子)년 고종(高宗)의 등극도 어느듯 70년이 되엿다. 70년이란 세월이 그리 길지 안치만은 그동안 조선을 싸고 돈 풍운은 여간 험난하지 안핫다. 풍운이 그럿틋 중첩한 동안에 하늘을 흔들뜻한 귀형락을 어든 인물도 적지 안핫다. 수락석출(水落石出)한 금일에도 판차리든 그네들의 호강은 그저 계속되는가 한번 차저보는 것도 흥미 잇는 문제일 듯하다.

趙府院君家
고종을 입승대통(入承大統)시기는 일에 가장 큰 역할을 하얏고 중대한 관건을 잡앗든 집은 조성하(趙成夏) 집이다. 조성하의 조부 조만영(趙萬永)의 따님은 곳 조대비시라. 조대비가 비록 익종(翼宗)의 왕비가 되섯스나 익종이 일즉 승하(昇遐)하신 까닭으로 조대비의 친정은 세됴맛을 보지 못하고 조대비의 시어머님 순원왕후(純元王后)께서 오래 수하신 덕택으로 순종 익종 헌종 철종(純宗翼宗憲宗哲宗) 사대동안 김씨가 독판을 첫섯다. 그럼으로 조대비는 항상 앙앙불락하시다가 철종이 승하하시니 그때는 순원왕후가 이미 돌아가신 후이라 조대비와 홍대비와 김대비의 세 과부 어른이 게시나 조대비가 제일 놉흔 항렬에 잇는 까닭으로 궁중일은 조대비가 천펀하게 되엿다. 대원군(大院君)이 이런 눈치를 채고서 자긔의 둘재 아들을 조대비의 아들로 들여 세우고 외척의 집권을 절중지하든 끗이라 자긔가 독권을 차지하고저 하야 조성하로 새이하야 안으로 조대비의 의향을 돌리게 하엿스니 그 때에 조성하는 그야말로 우투즉우승(右投則右勝)하는 한신(韓信)과 가탓다. 조성하의 노력으로 고종이 정통을 이엇스니 세도는 의레히 조성하에게로 갈것이나 당시 조성하는 나 젊은 청년이요 대원군은 로활한 투장(鬪將)이라 조성하를 꾀임하야 평안감사로 내보내고 그동안에 안팟세력을 독차지하얏스니 조성하는 그야말로 남의 조흔 일만 해주는 헛수고만 들엿다. 그러나 세도는 못할 망정 벼슬차리야 못할니 잇스랴! 조성하도 리조판서요 그 아들 조동면(趙東冕)도 30전 리조판서로 하야 조엄(趙曮) 이하 조동면까지 칠대 리조판서로 하얏다. 례의렴치가 잇다는 영종(英宗)졊� 때에도 조엄이가 탐장(貪贓)으로 말을 들엇거든 더욱이 렴치가 업서진 근년에 그러틋 현혁하게 해먹엇스니 돈인들 여간 엄창나게 모흐지 안핫다. 그럼으로 그 집을 칠대 리판(吏判)에 칠대 만석이라고 남들이 일커럿다. 그야 그뿐인가 세간집물의 화려와 서화 미술(美術)품등의 보물 만키로는 현금 조선갑부 민영희(閔泳徽)로도 못당한다고 하얏다. 조동면의 아들 조명구(趙命九)가 불행이 조사하야 사속이 업슨 후 충주(忠州) 먼 일가에서 립후를 하야 노하스니＜18＞ 그가 20년전 만석군 조남석(趙南奭)이다. 그 집은 재산은 아직도 여전하나 대대로 조사한 까닭에 조명구의 부인 조명구의 자친 소박마저 와서 잇는 조명구의 매씨까지 세 과부가 동대문밧 새로 지은 백여간 큰집을 지키고 잇다. 예전의 그집은 구사(求仕)하는 이의 도회청이더니 지금 그집은 과부의 도회청이다.

閔府院君家
대원군이 자긔의 둘재 아들로 입승대통을 시키고 외척들의 권리잡은 것을 미워하든 끗헤 아버지나 오라버니가 든 세ㅅ집 딸을 간택하야 중궁(中宮)을 삼앗다가 권리를 또 뺏길가 겁을 집어 먹고 관천현감(果川縣監)으로 아들도 업시 돌아가고 대원군의 처족하 민성호(閔性鎬)를 데려다 아들로 삼은 민치록(閔致祿)의 딸 무부고녀(無父孤女)로 왕후를 삼앗스니 이 어른이 즉 민중전(閔中殿)이다. 예정키 어려운 것은 세상일이다. 고종께서나 중종께서나 나이 어리어 세상일을 모를때에는 대원군의 마음대로 다 되엿지마은 고종도 장성하니 세력에 들어서는 부자도 업고 구부(舅婦)도 업는 것이라 전국의 권리를 꼭 대원군에게 맷길리도 업는 것이오 또는 대원군의 행정(行政)이 넘우도 재이든 만큼 사론(士論)도 만히 듯고 사환가에도 적국이 만튼
