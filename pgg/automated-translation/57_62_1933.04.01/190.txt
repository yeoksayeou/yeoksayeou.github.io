기사제목 連作漫談, 女子의 一生(第二回) 修學編 (Serialized Anecdotes, A Woman's Life (Part 2) - School Days)
필자 孤帆
기사형태 소설 (Fiction)

She was, in short, an ordinary person's daughter. She had eagerly looked forward to growing a year older with everyone else each New Year. Kindergarten at age 5, ordinary school (普通學校) at age 8, girls' high school at 16—if she was in the first grade of girls' high school (高等女學校) at 16, the years might seem a bit off, but there was a reason: she had taken a break from ordinary school for several years due to illness. Don't ask what illness it was. At the age of 16, a girl is embarrassed even by seeing butterflies. It seems there were unspeakable circumstances and worries. During calligraphy class, she practiced writing love letters; during drawing class, she drew the teacher's face; during arithmetic class, she read popular novels; during Korean language class, she asked the teacher to tell old stories; during physical education class, she pretended that her XX had burst and flirted, hugging the heater and chatting with her female classmates who were engaging in same-sex love. Mu-deon's high school days were this flowery and this stylish. She would perm, tease, and twist her hair and then, during breaks, stand in front of the blackboard like a "modern type" mannequin in a department store window, and even while being punished, she would smile. Every time she smiled, a cheerful song calling for spring flowed from her eyes, which twinkled like fireflies. Whenever the teacher's angry gaze met hers, she would lower her head slightly, and her burning lips, which briefly revealed her buck teeth, were like poppies ripening in the blazing sun.

"She is kind of cute."

It seemed that untimely spring news also reached the teacher's heart, which was as hardened as a fossil with chalk dust.

"A mischievous prankster I can't hate."

This was the final cry of the S Girls' School teachers about Mu-deon. Whether they were having snacks, eating lunch, playing in the schoolyard, or even cheating during exams, they were always together—a harmonious pair of warblers! Mu-deon made a close friend like a cherry blossom swaying on the back hill. It was the spring of a 17-year-old awakening to love! It was March when Mu-deon, after watching a tragic love story at the Danseong Theater (團成社), returned home and cried.

"Sister! I miss you. My heart flutters whenever I see you. Please play with me. Please teach me. I would be happy even if I starved if only you loved me." - Eun-bong

A letter from Eun-bong, who was one grade above Mu-deon but one year younger and lived in the dormitory, had somehow found its way onto Mu-deon's desk. Eun-bong had secretly left it there. Eun-bong was a classmate who played basketball together. She was a slender, petite lady who blushed easily. That night, Mu-deon nestled in her mother's blanket and thought about Eun-bong all night long. The next day, Mu-deon immediately wrote a reply, went to Eun-bong's desk, opened her textbook, and slipped the letter halfway into it so it would be easy to find.

"Eun-bong! Thank you. I will come visit you in the dormitory tonight. 6 PM! - Mu-deon"

How tedious it was for 6 PM to arrive! Eun-bong deliberately sent her roommates out to see the sights by giving them money and waited for Mu-deon to come. Mu-deon put on perfume, reapplied rouge, carefully wiped even her nostrils with a towel, and put on her socks so that her feet would look as small as possible, praying as she entered Eun-bong's room.

Eun: "Sister!"

Mu: "Eun-bong!"

It was like a melodramatic tragedy! An embrace that only lovers can share. They were too shy to speak, and their faces were red as they sat until curfew with the sweets and fruits that Eun-bong had thoughtfully bought.

To wear the same clothes, the same color.

To be together day and night.

To report to each other what happened each day.

To sleep together as much as possible.

As the degree of their mutual affection and longing deepened day by day, another big problem arose for Mu-deon. It was that a girl named Sun-hui in the first grade had written a letter in blood, begging for her love. But an even bigger problem arose: While going to watch a baseball game at the Gyeongseong Stadium (京城運動場), Mu-deon reached into her jacket pocket to pay the streetcar fare, and a letter envelope was caught in her hand. It turned out to be a love letter from some vocational school student who had slipped it in.

"You're so popular, sister."

Jealousy is a privilege reserved only for those who love. Eun-bong's anxiety reached its peak.

"My goodness, that child is saying all sorts of things. Does she even know who I am?"

"Then tear it up in front of me."

"Oh, it's too good to just tear up. Let's read it once and then tear it up."

Thus, they finished reading the letter as they walked from the streetcar stop in front of Gyeongseong Stadium (京城運動場) to the stop in front of the Normal School (師範學校). After reading the letter, it was, of course, torn up by Eun-bong's hand. However, the shadow of anxiety that Mu-deon could not be loved only by her was clearly cast over Eun-bong's heart.

"Sister! Are you going to forget about this younger sister in order to be embraced by the powerful, burning arms of a man in a square cap (四方帽子)?"

This was the letter that Eun-bong wrote and sent every day.

"Goodness, when did I ever admire square caps?"

Mu-deon scolded, almost angrily, and habitually pulled Eun-bong's lips into a comfortable kiss. In the meantime, Sun-hui of the blood letter incident was sending letters and presents every day, and Eun-bong's mind and body were withering and drooping like leftover japchae (雜菜). Following the square-capped letter-sender and Sun-hui of the blood letter incident, a great person emerged: physical education teacher, Miss K. This teacher was an "old miss" who had graduated from Tokyo Physical Education School (東京體育學校). She started being fond of Mu-deon from the moment she saw her, and eventually, she would take her to her inn every day, bathe together, and hug her warmly in the same blanket, cherishing her wholeheartedly.

"Teacher!"

Then Miss K would get angry and say,

"You can call me 'teacher' at school, but when we're at home, call me 'sister'!"

Mu-deon was filled with pride and joy to the point where she thought there was no one in the world more adorable and happier than her. Eun-bong, who had been taken away from Mu-deon by Miss K, was exhausted and fell ill in the dormitory. As soon as that happened, before her illness could heal, a scarlet fever patient appeared, and communication between the dormitory and the classroom was cut off. Still, Mu-deon would buy Eun-bong's favorite fruits and snacks and visit her once a day, but she lost the freedom to set foot in the dormitory again. The teacher had already noticed the relationship between Eun-bong and Mu-deon and declared that Mu-deon was absolutely forbidden from entering the dormitory.

"Sister! Sister! Meeting you once a day was my sustenance, but if you cut that off, how am I supposed to live! How can you be so cruel!"

A plaintive cry came from Eun-bong. Mu-deon didn't know what to do. She felt sorry for Eun-bong. She was afraid of the teacher who was glaring and trying to monitor and isolate the relationship between her and Eun-bong. But she stubbornly broke through the blockade like a squirrel and entered Eun-bong's room, embracing and comforting her.

"Mu-deon!"

It was Miss K's voice coming from outside the window. Mu-deon lost her mind and stepped out into the schoolyard.

"Why don't you listen to the teacher!"

Miss K made a scary face for the first time in her life. But there were tears in her eyes. As soon as she saw Mu-deon, Miss K shouted and cried. <37>
