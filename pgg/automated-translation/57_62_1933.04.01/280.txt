Detective Novel, Dr. K's Master Plan
						Choe Ryuhwan

The man did not show the slightest hint of unease as he looked down at the woman's corpse. The woman lay stretched out on a marble dais, her complexion already completely gone, but with her voluptuous body and calmly composed lips, if it weren't for the gauze covering her eyes, one could only see her as a sleeping woman.

The gazes of the two officers who had guided the man, the medical examiner Dr. K, and Prosecutor C, all turned to the man's face. However, the man's expression did not change at all, and he simply looked down at the corpse with an expressionless face.

"Hey..." Prosecutor C suddenly broke the silence and spoke.

"Do you not feel any regret even when standing before the corpse you murdered?"

"I have never killed anyone. Who is this woman anyway?"

The man approached the corpse and tried to remove the gauze covering her eyes. The prosecutor prevented him from removing it.

"Hmph," the prosecutor said with a bitter smile.

"You may pretend not to know, but the reliable testimonies of several people prove that you were close to this woman for a while before she went to America, don't they? That alone is enough, but we brought you here to get you to confess cleanly if possible. No matter how much you try to deny your crime, since you have received a considerable education and have had a special relationship with this woman for some time, if you have any conscience, you will not be able to maintain such a calm demeanor for long in front of the woman you killed with your own hands."

"Prosecutor, you may say so, but how can I confess to something I didn't do? I have never even seen this woman in my life."

"So, you still insist? After this woman returned from America and her social status rose, you heard that she rejected you and was engaged to another man, and you were extremely angry about it, unable to bear the jealousy that arose like fire. That night, you went for a final negotiation, and when the woman resisted to the end, you couldn't bear it any longer, and the thought of killing her arose, and you strangled her to death at that moment. We already know all the facts of your crime. Don't bother with useless excuses and confess your crime frankly, as it will not harm your personal safety."

"You have misunderstood me. You are saying this because you do not consider my position and my character at all. If you recognize me as a murderer, it is also related to your position as prosecutor, so please conduct a thorough investigation and arrest the real criminal." The prosecutor was so taken aback by this brazen man, who remained adamant to the end, that he stuck out his tongue.

At that moment, one of the officers spoke.

"This man will never confess the truth. Even if he were standing on the gallows (絞首臺), he would continue to deny it." At that moment, Dr. K wore a slight smile on his face, as if he had a meaningful idea.

"There is nothing we can do now. Then let's take the final measure. You have received a considerable education and have a high social standing, so you know Dr. K well. Everyone knows that Dr. K has demonstrated various new methods of crime verification (犯罪檢證法) until now.

Recently, Dr. K has newly discovered a method of searching for criminals (犯人搜索法). To put it simply in one word, it is a method of developing a person's retinal (網膜) image like a photographic plate.

A person's eye is like the camera of a photograph, and the retina acts like a dry plate. While a person is alive, an object reflected on the retina disappears in the next moment and another object is reflected. In this way, metabolism (新陳代謝) occurs continuously.

However, when a person dies, the image of the object last reflected on the retina is permanently preserved. Therefore, if it is possible to develop the retina of the corpse, the image of the object that the person saw last in life will be clearly imprinted on the retina.

For example, let's say there is a murderer here. And let's say we don't know who the criminal is. At that time, if we could develop the retina, the criminal's appearance or clothing would definitely appear. So, even if we don't dissect the corpse to determine the cause of death (死因)＜56＞, we can find out the criminal immediately by taking out and developing only the retina (眼球), and thus obtain irrefutable evidence.

Dr. K has finally discovered the retinal development method. You know how important that discovery is and how fatal (致命的) terror it will cause to criminals."

Prosecutor C finished speaking and glanced at the man. A kind of uneasy light began to flicker in the eyes of the man, who had stubbornly maintained a calm attitude until now. Whether the prosecutor's long explanation stimulated his mind or the new discovery of retinal development troubled his conscience, his eyes lost their composure and his gaze frequently turned to the corpse.

"You already know everything," the prosecutor said, showing a threatening attitude.

"This room is the autopsy room. In the past, we would start the autopsy immediately, but now there is no need to perform an autopsy. Dr. K only dissected the eyes of this corpse without performing an autopsy. That's why both eyes are covered with gauze."

The man seemed to be gradually losing his composure. Even the way he lowered and raised his head looked very unnatural. And he was glancing at Dr. K, who was silently keeping still. The man seemed to feel a kind of indescribable pressure from the scholar's face of the doctor, who had a broad forehead, sharp eyes, and a lot of mustache under his nose, and who looked intelligent at first glance.

The curiosity and fear of what Dr. K might have discovered by developing the retina seemed to torment the man.

"The retinal development has already finished and is now being kept in a separate room," the prosecutor said with a calm attitude, in contrast to the man.

"However, even Dr. K himself, who is in charge, does not yet know what kind of image has appeared. As you can guess, the retina is extremely small in area, so the image is too small to be seen with the naked eye (肉眼). Therefore, a magnifying projector (擴大投射鏡) must be used for accurate identification. The preparations are ready in the next room, so now you should go there and see what kind of image appears on this woman's retina."

At this time, an assistant came in and informed the doctor that the preparations were complete.

"Let's go," the prosecutor said to the man.

"Do I really have to go there?"

The man's voice seemed to tremble slightly.

"If you haven't committed murder, there's no need to hesitate, is there?" Stung in his vital spot, the man couldn't utter a word of protest and entered the next room with the prosecutor, the doctor, and the officers. The room was dark, with a white curtain stretched across the front and a large projector (投射機) shining pale light from an arc lamp toward the center of the curtain.

As they entered the dark room, Dr. K picked up a glass dish placed on the projector and showed it to the prosecutor and the man. Something like a membrane was floating in the water on the dish.

"This is the woman's retina. This will be attached to the glass tube and hung on the projector to be projected on that screen."

Saying this, the doctor completed the necessary preparations in about two minutes.

"Now, I'm going to project it. Please sit in that chair and watch." The prosecutor commanded the man, who was hesitant to sit in the chair, to sit down.

The sound of an arc lamp hissing was heard. And in the next moment, something like clouds appeared on the screen. Then, the shape of an object gradually appeared from within what looked like clouds.

Ah!

There! There!!

A blurry image like a photograph, but undeniably the man's face...

Suddenly, the man jumped up. He only thought he had gotten up, but

"Hehehe..." he laughed. Had the man gone mad, caught by irrefutable evidence that he could no longer escape?

"Hehehe..." the man continued to laugh.

"It's a lie. It's a lie. You are deliberately acting out a play to deceive me. My face cannot appear on the woman's retina."

"Why?" the prosecutor said in a very indignant voice.

"Because I killed her while she was asleep..."

A few hours later, the prosecutor and the doctor were talking.

"Anyway, I can't help but admire your reputation, Doctor. The 'retinal development' is truly a brilliant plan."

"No, when I examined the body, I suspected that the woman might have been murdered in her sleep, so I tried to realize something I had researched to get the man to confess without saying anything. And 'retinal development' is something I want to research, and that method may be discovered in the future. Anyway, it is fortunate that our plan was successful because the criminal confessed."

"I was surprised when that guy tried to take off the gauze covering her eyes. The eyes weren't dissected or treated with medicine. Then what was that retinal-like thing in the glass dish?"

"That was film. By putting film in warm water, it looked like that. I appropriately modified and inserted the man's photo that was taken at the police station. In the past, superstitious (迷信的) methods were used to get criminals to confess, but in modern times, especially for educated intellectual classes, we have come to realize that scientific (科學的) methods must be used," the doctor said with a smile on his face.

The prosecutor was listening to Dr. K's words with satisfaction. (March 13)＜57＞

별안간 그 남자는 벌떡 이러낫다. 이러난줄만 생각하얏드니
『허허허...』 하고 우섯다. 그 남자는 이제는 도피할 수 업는 증거를 붓잡혀서 발광을 한 것이 아닌가?
『허허허...』 하고 그 남자는 우슴을 계속하드니
『그짓말입니다. 그짓말입니다. 당신들은 나를 속일냐고 일부러 꿈인 연극입니다. 나의 얼골이 그 녀자의 망막에 나타날 리가 업습니다』
『웨?』 하고 검사는 몹시 분개한 음성으로 말하얏다.
『저는 그 녀자가 잘 때 죽엿스니깐요...』

몃 시간 뒤에 검사와 박사는 이야기 하얏다.
『하야간 선생님의 명망에는 탄복하지 안을 수 업습니다. 「망막현상」이라는 것은 참말 기발한 계책이십니다』
『아니요. 나는 시체를 검사할 때 녀자가 혹시 수민 중에 살해를 당하지나 아니햇나 하는 의심을 가젓슴으로 무언중 그 남자를 자백식히기 위하야 연구하야 본 것을 실현식혀 본 것입니다. 그리고 「망막현상」이라는 것은 내가 연구하고자 하는 것으로 장내에 그 법이 발견될지도 모르겟습니다. 하여간 범인이 자백하얏스니 우리의 계획은 성공하얏스니 다행입니다』
『나는 그 놈이 눈 덥흔 가제를 벗기랴고 할 때 깜작 놀낫습니다. 눈은 해부도 아니하고 약도 발느지 아니하얏스니깐요. 그런대 유리접시에 담은 망막과 가튼 것은 무엇입니까?』
『그것은 필님입니다. 필님을 더운물 너허슴으로 그러케 보엿습니다. 경찰서에서 박은 그 남자의 사진을 내가 적당하게 곳처 박은 것입니다. 예전에는 미신적(迷信的) 방법을 써서 범인을 자백식혓지만 현대에 더욱이 교육 밧은 지식계급은 과학적(科學的) 방법을 사용치 안흐면 안된다는 것을 절실히 깨닷게 되엿습니다』 박사는 얼골에 미소를 띄우고 이가티 말하얏다.
