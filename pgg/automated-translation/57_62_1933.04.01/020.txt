The People We Saw, Secret Records of People from All Walks of Life
What a Female Student Saw of Mr. Pak Hŭi-do
Kang Chŏng-O

In the streetcar bound for Sŏdaemun...

It's unsettling enough when people of the same gender stare, but when a man you don't know looks at you intently, you can't help but lower your head and feel your heart pounding. I wondered if I had a grain of rice stuck to my nose, so I pretended to scratch my face with my little finger and surreptitiously wiped my face, but there was nothing there.

"What an odd old man. Why is he staring at someone else's face?"

I was thinking this to myself, feeling suspicious, so I poked the friend sitting next to me.

"Hey, don't you know him? It's Pak Hŭi-do, the principal of Chungang Nursery School!"

"Oh... I didn't know."

Only after realizing who it was did a sound like a punch to the gut escape me. Otherwise, with my temper, some rude words would have come out.

With a sense of admiration and respect, I turned my head and looked carefully at the teacher, from the brim of his fedora to the tips of his shoes. This was the first time I had ever met him in person.

He was as large as the nickname "Elephant" that students gave him suggested, and his face was quite broad, making him seem like a politician. Moreover, his bulging belly seemed to be full of strategies, not unlike Napoleon's. The eyes that rolled through the colorful world behind his dark glasses, the nose that pointed down almost touching his philtrum, and the thick lips all suggested that he possessed extraordinary patience and energy. Yet, the overall harmony of his build made him seem like a gentle and affectionate animal - an elephant, a hippopotamus, or a cow. The only flaw, perhaps, was that his voice was too sentimental and weak for his physique.

Oops! I'm so engrossed, I almost missed my stop at Norikae. I'll leave the rest for next time...

What a Busboy Saw of Sŏ Ch'un
C Tong

I don't want to. What if I get in trouble later for saying something like that? That teacher is really scary when he's drunk. Then you'll have to take the beating for me.

That teacher is something else. He's short and dark-skinned, and he doesn't seem to be afraid of anything. He's really tough. But when he gets drunk, he's really scary. When he comes, he's usually very drunk. So he doesn't usually drink much more when he gets here. I guess he comes here in a high (hiya-kashi) mood from being drunk... If it were just us, he wouldn't come. But the madam is here, whether he likes her or not, so I guess that's why he comes. He comes here drunk, and he's here, so how can he sit still? If something doesn't suit his ears, he'll swear and walk around. The more he's like that, the scarier he is, and I don't dare say a word to him... Of course, the madam is scared too. Sometimes she even hides inside. Then he'll yell and curse at her for running away. And when he drinks, it's a disaster. So you have to soothe him, or something terrible will happen if you provoke him or object. The madam is pretty tough, but some inexperienced people can't stand it and end up crying. After he throws a tantrum like that and leaves, we're happier than when he arrived. As soon as we see he's about to leave, we quickly go and open the door and say, "Goodbye," and as we watch him walk away, we worry about how he'll ever get home walking like that, weaving back and forth across the road. It's such a relief when he's gone. When we close the door and come back inside, it's like the storm has passed. The madam sighs, and we sigh - everyone says he's quiet and doesn't come often when he's not drunk, but alcohol is a terrible thing. Don't tell anyone I said this, or he'll say, "You little liar!" and slap me. Don't tell him I said that. Goodbye. "So, you're glad I'm leaving? Ha..."

What a Bartender Saw of Kwŏn Tŏk-kyu
Hwang Ttol-i

He really likes his liquor. He always stops by on his way home in the afternoon. So if he doesn't come one day, we start to wonder about him. When he comes, he usually comes with two or three friends, rarely with more.

He drinks with class. He doesn't just chug it down, even when he's drunk. After about five glasses, he starts patting his short frame and telling jokes, witticisms, and bizarre stories, one after another, like snacks at a bar. And if his companions are paying, he doesn't seem to want to leave, but he doesn't say anything about leaving either. That's why we get a little nervous when he comes alone. Why? Because he doesn't drink much and leaves. Sometimes he only has one drink before leaving. What does he do then? Even then, he's still on his way home, and it's evening, and he's probably feeling like a drink. Anyway, he comes in. He comes straight to the bar. Without saying a word, he says, "A big one!" A big one means makkŏlli (막걸리). We already know and give him a big bowl. Then he gulps it down like it's nothing, picks up a piece of kimchi (김치), and says, "A handful of beans!" and holds out his hand, and then he drops a five-chŏn coin into the bowl. At the same time, the handful of beans goes into his pocket, and he takes a step out the door. What else is there to say? Sometimes, he comes with another customer, and that person pays. Then, if another customer who knows him comes in, they'll offer him a drink or two. In that case, the first customer sits quietly in a corner. If the later customer knows more people and is going to drink more, he'll just say, "Let's stop here," and the first customer will pay the bill. Then the second customer will say, "I have something to talk about with these people, excuse me," and stay at the table. There's nothing the first customer can do. But is it okay to say all this?

What a Waitress Saw of Ch'a Sang-ch'an
RS Cha

... Mr. Ch'a, well, he doesn't come around here very often. I don't know if he goes to other cafes often, but he doesn't come to ours, so there's nothing to praise or criticize. Well, when he does come, usually with some friends, he's already pretty drunk, so he doesn't drink much here. He tells all sorts of strange and eerie jokes that are both funny and sometimes vulgar. Actually, I respect him because I know he's the famous Ch'a Sang-ch'an, but otherwise, he makes such vulgar jokes and reaches out to touch women in ways that no respectable customer would... it gives me goosebumps. He won't take a drink from the customers he came with and says, "What's the point of coming to a cafe if you can't have a little fun?" If I show that I don't like it, he'll immediately curse at me. And listen to what he says: "Japanese girls are always more fun. Korean girls are boring." Then he calls a Japanese waitress over, sits her next to him, and talks to her for a while, and then he always says, "Iyana hito dekuse ga warui wa (嫌な人、出来が悪いわ)" and runs away. I've said too much; what if something bad happens to me later... Yes, yes, there is one more thing to say. Once, he came with three or four people and ordered beer. After drinking only two bottles, he said abruptly, "Kaikei (會計)" (the bill) because he didn't like something, and he took out his wallet and threw two won down for a bill of 1 won and 95 chŏn. So his tip to me was 5 chŏn. But I thought he gave it to me on purpose, so I've kept it as a souvenir. If you want to see it, I'll show you the five-chŏn coin right now... Hahaha

What a Bathhouse Owner Saw of Yun Ch'i-ho
Pak Su-ch'ŏl

Mr. Yun Ch'i-ho! Mr. Yun Ch'i-ho, who specifically comes to our bathhouse, has something unique about him compared to other customers.

Whenever he comes to the bathhouse, he always brings his young son and daughter, but many other people come with their children, so that's not particularly special.

But one thing that is different from others is that he wears a vest and carries his belongings tied to a long string.

Pocket knife, handkerchief, notebook... watch, fountain pen, key ring... wallet and other pocket items are all strung together like herring merchants' skewers, and he puts them in his pocket.

Because of that, when he comes to the bathhouse, the first thing he does is hand over that dangling bundle. And there are two more things he adds here: his glasses and a gold ring.

Anyway, he's a very meticulous person with a good personality, but if he were to run into a bad thief, I worry that he would lose everything at once.

What a Kisaeng Saw of Cho Pyŏng-ok
Li O-hong

Mr. Cho Pyŏng-ok, who was the business director of the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper! I've only gotten to know him recently, mainly because of meetings related to the newspaper business at restaurants.

I heard that he went to America and finished university and even got a doctorate, but seeing him for the first time only recently, it seems he hadn't set foot in places like restaurants because he was studying so hard, befitting his doctorate.

When I first met him, his face looked so scary, to be honest, that I didn't even want to go near him.

But after meeting him two or three times, I gradually got used to him, and even though he doesn't drink much, when he has a little, he treats us so kindly, unlike how he looks, and he really knows how to make everyone feel good. I was really impressed.

I realized that you can't really know someone's personality or heart just by looking at their face. His seemingly strict yet infinitely gentle nature gave us a very good impression from the start.

What a Restaurant Busboy Saw of Song Chin-u
Kim X-tong

Mr. Song Chin-u, the president of the Tonga Ilbo newspaper! Mr. Song sometimes comes to our restaurant, sometimes with a large group and sometimes with a few friends privately.

He is a very respectable gentleman and a prominent figure in society, so there are no particular flaws from beginning to end in any gathering, but sometimes when he has too much to drink, he tends to talk a lot in his loud voice. But it's not just Mr. Song who rambles after drinking, so that's not a problem, but there was one time when he showed something like this.

One time, a few people came to visit, and it was quite late and they had drunk quite a bit. Suddenly, I heard sobs coming from the room where Mr. Song was. At first, I was startled and tried to jump in, but I was afraid of getting scolded, so I hesitated outside and tried to listen quietly. It seemed that Mr. Song was very drunk, and he was holding onto one of the employees and crying.

"...sob...some bastard I raised betrayed me...sob...sob...that guy I really trusted...that guy..."

At that time, I was worried that other customers might hear this and realize it was Mr. Song, so I felt so sorry.

It seemed that Mr. Song had been betrayed by someone he trusted most, and it was deeply ingrained in his bones. That's why, after drinking, his resentment burst out, and he forgot about his surroundings and environment and became sad.

What a Office Assistant Saw of Cho Man-sik
X Tong

Mr. Cho Man-sik, the president of the Chosŏn Ilbo newspaper! I'm a newbie office assistant who was newly hired thanks to this newly relaunched paper, and at first, I thought the president would have a very dignified face, wear nice suits, wear gold-rimmed glasses, hang a gold watch chain, wear shiny patent leather shoes, and carry an ivory cane.

But on the day I joined the company, I was shocked when I actually met the president.

When I went into the president's office, there was a shabby old man sitting there, and he looked like a scholar who had just come from the countryside.

So at first, I thought he was probably a guest who had come to see the president, and I was about to hesitate.

"Why? Is there something wrong?"

He asked, so I stammered,

"Yes... yes... I... I... I'm here to see the president."

"Then speak up, why are you standing there..."

Only then did I realize that this old man was the president, and I greeted him.

He hadn't cut his hair properly, hadn't shaved, and was wearing a Korean cotton overcoat that was short and came up to his knees - and what was even more surprising was that he was wearing cotton socks and rubber shoes.

When I saw all of this, I couldn't help but lower my head.

I was truly impressed by the president's surprisingly simple lifestyle.

What a Western Restaurant Busboy Saw of OOO
Kim O-ch'ŏng

Mr. OOO is one of the customers who often comes to our restaurant.

He is a quiet gentleman who doesn't smoke or drink. He always has his hair cut in a high collar style, but he slightly lowers his bangs to cover half of his forehead, and he often comes to visit.

Sometimes he comes with Mr. OO, but this is rare. He is a good customer who just talks quietly and leaves.

We just want him to sell a lot of dishes, no matter who he comes with, so we just hope he comes often.

But these days, for some reason, he doesn't come to our restaurant. I don't know if he's found a new regular place, but he hasn't come since he came with Mr. OO a few months ago.

So our boss scolds us, thinking we did something wrong, but we didn't, so we have nothing to be ashamed of in our conscience, but it's true that he hasn't come.

I hope Mr. O comes again soon so we can explain ourselves to the boss and brag about it.
