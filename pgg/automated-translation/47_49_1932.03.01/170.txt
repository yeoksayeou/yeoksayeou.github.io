기사형태	시 (Poetry)

If the azaleas bloom a lot on Namsan Mountain
They say this village will have a bountiful harvest
Matchmakers are abundant on the main road.
The young bachelor from the front house carries water well
They say he will get married on horseback this fall
He's happily carrying rice cakes for the wedding.
If the cuckoo cries for a long time in the back forest
They say this village will have a bountiful harvest
Matchmakers are abundant on the main road.
The young lady from the back house weaves very well
They say she will get married in a palanquin this fall
She's happily weaving cotton cloth (布) for the groom.

Song Heard in the Morning
Eunam

Like a maiden meeting her lover in the haze
The wind bows its head
In the dreamlike sleeping morning
Flowing with a slender melody
Awakening the sleeping wind
Song! Song!
Thickly covering my window, stained with frost,
So that no holes can be seen
Awakening my soul that cried and laughed in dreams
Praising the true heart of life
A certain maiden's song!
I heard it! On a morning when the sun rises. ... ...
Ah! What a lovely song.
From a maiden's solitary heart
That earnestly seeks only the truth of life (眞理)
Flowing deeply
That song! Beautiful, lovely
Your heart that seeks only the truth!

If I Play the Flute
No Ryang-geun

If I play the flute
Please dance for me
For us who are looking forward,
Why should there be worries?
Why should there be sorrows?
Even if there are some sad things
Should we just frown?
E-hey-ya. De-hey-ya-
Nillili-kkung- Nilliri-kkung-.
If you play the flute
I will dance for you
Let's not think about the frustrating past
We should prepare for the coming future
Sometimes, we should open our hearts like this
And try to laugh.
E-hey-ya. De-hey-ya-
Nillili-kkung- Nilliri-kkung-.

Song of the Pioneers
Tsushima Island Sasuna Calf

The creeping grass and trees
Grow day and night
Our hoes wielded
Even Mt. Taishan crumbles.
Dig! Dig!
Until it becomes rice paddies.
Whether it rains or snows
Or the wind blows
The day when arms and legs rest
The hoe's blade rusts.
Dig! Dig!
Until it becomes rice paddies.
In the thorny mountain fortress
A blue snake lays its eggs
In the falling drops of sweat
Jade sprouts.
Dig! Dig!
Until it becomes rice paddies.
The grandson's grandson
Will live on this land
Throw small stones
And roll boulders.
Dig! Dig!
Until it becomes rice paddies.
-Written on the night of January 3rd-

Let's Go to Work
No Gok An Guk-tae

Comrades, come out!
Let's all go to work
Like a fiery lump in the Earth's core
Raising red fists high
Like a pouring waterfall
Springing forth.
1932.1.1

A Heart of Heat and Cold
Kim Yŏng-tae

Even in the cold moonlight, sharp as needles flowing over the snow
My burning heart does not cool down.
Even in the flames where iron melts
My cold heart will never melt.
This heat and this cold
Clashing together in my heart
How can flowers of laughter bloom? <31>
