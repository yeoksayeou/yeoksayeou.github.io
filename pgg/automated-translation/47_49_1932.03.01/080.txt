A Rare and Strange Person, Giant Paektu-san: Rather an Exploration than a Visit
						A Reporter

Shorty or Tiny Tot!
These are all unflattering nicknames given to short people. If someone is of a certain age and has a beard like chestnut burrs growing on their chin, and is barely over five *ch'ŏk* (尺, approx. 5 ft), they will not escape this nickname. However, if someone is well over five *ch'ŏk*, with five or six *ch'on* (寸, approx. inches) being of medium height, over seven *ch'on*, eight *ch'on* being tall, and reaching six *ch'ŏk*, then the word "six *ch'ŏk*" is rather a pleasant nickname. They will hear unpleasant nicknames like clothes pole, beanpole, village guardian (천하대장군, *Ch'ŏnha Taejanggun*), telephone pole, *sotdae* (솟대, a tall pole with a bird on top), etc.

Of course, if the body and bulk are proportionate to the height, it looks quite dignified, but if the physique is as thin and dry as a dried pollack and only the height is exceptionally tall, swaying as they walk, they look exactly like a bamboo clothes pole, and the nickname is truly unpleasant.

It may not be so unusual to see people over six *ch'ŏk* among Westerners, but it is rare to find such a person in Korea. If bigger is better than smaller, then such a person in Korea certainly qualifies as a distinguished person. And if there is an even greater, more enormous height than this, wouldn't they qualify as a national treasure (國寶)?

This reporter, along with Mr. Kim Pu-gwi (金富貴), hurried along Nakwŏn-dong (樂園洞) Street to find Paektu-san (白頭山), one of these nonsensical national treasures. Upon entering the gate of the acquaintance's house where he was staying, I asked the servant, "Is there a person named Paektu-san (白頭山) here?"

"Paektu-san (白頭山)??.... Yes, yes. Are you talking about the tall guest?" he asked back, then peeked through the middle gate of the detached house and said, "Judging by the shoes, he must be in." The reporter also peeked in. Sure enough, there were shoes, large enough to be an exhibit at a fair. Opening the middle gate and entering, I called out, "Is anyone here!" There was no answer. I called out again. Finally, a thick voice came from inside the room, and as the door opened, the face of a giant appeared.

"Who is it?"

Paektu-san (白頭山), whom I had only heard about! Seeing him in person, he was so enormous that I felt a sense of fear (恐怖感). Taking off my hat and adopting a polite attitude, even adding a smile (微笑), I said hesitantly, "I came to see the teacher..."

"He is inside resting," he said.

"No, I came to see you..."

"Me? What business do you have?"

His words were curt and blunt like oak firewood. The reporter strode onto the porch and entered the room, saying, "Then excuse me for a moment,". I sat on the cushion indicated and presented my business card. Mr. Paektu-san (白頭山) stared at it for a while.

"What discussion?" he asked. I could tell he was not literate, despite his height.

"Yes. I am from a magazine. I heard that you are very large, so I came to see you. If you are not too busy, may I ask you a few questions?" Then Paektu-san (白頭山), seemingly understanding the reporter's intentions, bowed once.

"Yes, is that so?" he said, his tone becoming much more affable, easing the awkward and stiff atmosphere.

"What is your real name?"

"My name is Pak Han-gyu (朴漢圭)."

"Where is your hometown?"

"Ch'ungch'ŏng Province, Ch'ŏngju (淸州)."

"How old are you this year?"

"Twenty-eight."

"Then, why the name Paektu-san (白頭山)?"

"Ah! I took the name when I was a wrestler in Japan."

I ended the police-like (警察式) uncomfortable Q&A session briefly here. Before moving on to the next questions, let me first talk about his physique. His height is 7 *ch'ŏk* 1 *ch'on*, his weight is 33 *kwan* (貫, approx. 247 lbs), the length of his foot is 1 *ch'ŏk* 3 *ch'on*, and the length of his finger is 5 *ch'on*. Mr. Kim Pu-gwi (金富貴) is thin, and only his height is exceptionally tall, making him look rather deformed, but this Mr. Paektu-san (白頭山) is not like that. Everything about him is like a normal person, only the whole thing is just an enlarged version (擴大).

"Is this your first time in Seoul?"

"I came once before. But it was when I wasn't as big as I am now."

"When did you start to become so gigantic?"

"I was fine when I was young. But I suddenly started growing when I was seventeen."

"What are your parents like?"

"They have both passed away now, but they were no different from ordinary people."

"Then, have you ever thought about what caused you to start growing?"

"Of course, I've thought about it, but I have no idea why I started growing."

"Are you still growing?"

"I'm not growing anymore."

"Then, when were you at your best physique?"

"I went to Japan and worked as a wrestler when I was twenty-three, and that was when I was the biggest. At that time, my weight was over 38 *kwan*, and my body wasn't like this now (showing his arm), but I had a lot of fat."

"I hear you're not working as a wrestler anymore."

"I injured my shoulder while wrestling and went to the hospital for about two years. The doctor told me to rest, and I wasn't interested anymore, so I quit wrestling."

"Since you are so large, you must be very strong. How much weight can you lift?"

"I haven't really tested it. When I was thirteen, I lifted 23 *kwan* with one hand and walked around."

"What are you doing in Tokyo now?"

"I am working for an electric company (電氣會社) in Tamagawa (玉川)."

"What is your job?"

"I just play around."

"How much is your monthly living expenses?"

"Just food costs about fifty or sixty won."

"Do you have a family?"

"I am alone."

"How many bowls of rice do you eat per meal?"

"Ten or six bowls."

"What is your favorite food?"

"Japanese fish. I can eat about 2 *kwan* of *fugu* (pufferfish) at a time."

"2 *kwan*?" The reporter was so taken aback that he was surprised.

"Do you like beef?"

"I don't really like it unless it's good."

"What if it's good?"

"I can only eat about 1 *kwan* (approx. 7 lbs)."

1 *kwan* per meal, so 3 *kwan* for three meals! Isn't that the diet of a zoo beast (猛獸)?

"Do you drink alcohol?"

"I drink a little."

"How much?"

"Ten *toe* (斗, approx. 5 gallons)"

"Ten *toe*? Wow - amazing!... If you drink alcohol often, you must eat less rice."

"That's not true for us. We drink alcohol and eat rice separately."

Mr. Paektu-san (白頭山) proudly lights a cigarette (금강, *Kŭmgang*).

"Do you ever get into fights while drinking?"

"No. I don't want to fight, and no one wants to fight with me, so we get along."

"What about suits?"

"I have my suits made in Kanda (神田). Even if the fabric isn't that good, it costs 90 won to have them made. Everything else, socks, shirts, shoes, also have to be custom-made."

"So you can't get by with a regular income..."

"That's true."

"Do you have a home in your hometown?"

"Yes, I do."

"Are you married?"

"Yes, I have a wife, but I don't like living with her. So, I told her to go."

"Is your wife of normal build?" <17>

"She is normal. But for some reason, I don't like her."

"How old is she?"

"She is twenty-seven this year."

"She was born in the year of *hinoeuma* (丙午生)... Do you have any children?"

"No."

"How is your livelihood?"

"I don't have much, but I don't starve. I've entrusted the little property I have to her, asking her to perform ancestral rites for my mother and father... I earn my own living."

"Living so lonely in a foreign land, you must naturally feel homesick. What do you do then? Do you sometimes go whoring?"

Mr. Paektu-san (白頭山) laughs as if he is taken aback.

"Yes, there is that. In Tokyo, if you have money..."

"You go to the red-light district."

"Yes."

"Is there anyone who can handle a giant like you?"

"Because I am big... is that so..."

Mr. Paektu-san (白頭山), as if a secret has been revealed, blushes slightly and covers up his words with a smile.

"Aren't you bored just staying in this room all day?"

"I wish I could go outside. It's miserable...."

"That must be the case. Spectators will gather."

"If I get too bored, I take a car for a spin."

"You have left money behind?"

"How can I save money?"

"You should save as much as possible... When are you planning to remarry?"

"I'll probably go after 30."

"If you get a pretty new wife and have too much fun, the giant will become a dwarf."

Paektu-san (白頭山) puts out his cigarette and laughs uncomfortably (Mr. Paektu-san's (白頭山) laugh seems to be like that).

"When are you going back to Japan?"

"I was going to go already, but the host told me to stay a little longer, so I'll stay for another two or three days."

"Will you come again?"

"I will come again in two or three years. But it costs at least two or three hundred won to come and go, so it's quite difficult."

It seems that Mr. Paektu-san's (白頭山) time for a drive around the city is near.

"Let's meet again before you go to Tokyo."

The reporter left this parting word and left the room. While taking a commemorative photo in the alley outside the door, a crowd of passersby gathered.

"Hey, look! A tall man!"

"Oh, my! Amazing."

Wow- it's a sensation. Mr. Paektu-san (白頭山) smokes a cigarette and smiles.

(The photo is of Mr. Paektu-san (白頭山) and the reporter. The reporter is 5 *ch'ŏk* 5 *ch'on* tall, so he is of medium height.) <18>

모두다 키적은 사람에게부치는 아름답지못한 별명이다. 낫살이나 알마치먹고 코아래와 턱에 밤송이가튼 수염이 2, 3분식 난 사람으로 키가 5척을 간신이 넘을락말락하면 이별명은 버서나지 못할 것이다. 그러나 키가 5척을 썩넘어 사오륙촌은 중키, 7촌을 넘어 8촌만되면 키다리, 9촌을 돌파하야 6척에 이르기까지의 키라면 6척이란 말은 오히려 향그러운별명이요. 바지랑ㅅ대 장다리 장승(天下大將軍)전봇대 솟대백이 등등의 징글마진 별명을 듯게된다.
그야 키에알맛게 몸ㅅ집이나 부대하면 상당히 어울여서 위엄잇서 보이지만 마른명태처럼 밧작말은 체격에 키만 훌적커가지고 휫청휫청 거러가는 것을 보면 역낙업시 참대바지랑ㅅ대와가태서 별명 그대로 징그럽다.
코주부 서양사람들 중에는 6척이상의 키가 그다지 신기할것이 못되겟지만 조선에 잇서서는 좀처럼 어더볼수업다. 적은것보다 큰 것이 조타면 조선에 잇서서 이런 부류의 사람은 확실히 귀인될 자격이 잇슬 것이다. 그러고 이이상의 키가 너무나 너무나 엄청난 키가 잇다면 조선의 국보(國寶)될 자격이 잇지나 안을까?―
긔자는 김부귀(金富貴)군과 함께 넌센스한 국보의 하나인 백두산(白頭山)군을 차저 락원동거리를 종종거름첫다. 그가 류숙하고잇는 친지의 집 대문을 드러스며 행랑사람에게
『백두산이란 사람잇습니까』하니까
『백두산??.... 네네. 키큰 손님말이시지요』하고 되뭇더니 사랑채 중문 틈으로 드려다보고는
『구두잇는것을보니 게신가부군요』한다. 긔자 역시 따라 드려다보앗다. 아닌게아니라 구두가 노엿는데 박람회에 출품꺼리는 넉넉히 되고도 남을만큼 큰구두다. 중문을 열고 드러스며 『이로너라!』를불럿다. 아모대답이 업다. 또한번 불럿다. 그제야 굵은 목소리가 방안에서 나며 방문이 열리는 더 거인의 얼골이 나타난다.
『누구요』
