小作法의 制定과 小作人 (The Enactment of the Farmland Tenancy Law and Tenant Farmers)
金東澈 (Kim Tong-ch'ŏl)
논설 (Essay)

I. Landlords and Tenant Farmers

Just as a great tide fearlessly bursts through embankments, invades homes, and turns fields into a watery expanse with its powerful waves, the force of capitalism has swept over Chosŏn, a nation of small farmers, commodifying everything. All means of production—land, mines (鑛山), factories (工場), machinery (機械), raw materials (原料), etc.—are monopolized by a minority, and the labor of workers is sold for meager wages. The traditional self-sufficient economy gradually disappears, and in agriculture, land becomes concentrated in the hands of a few landlords, just as capital concentrates in the hands of a few in industry. Land accumulation intensifies each year, and the number of tenant farmers increases enormously. The relationship between landlords and tenant farmers over the past decade is as follows:

II. Intensification of Tenancy Disputes (小作爭議)

Just as laborers cannot own factories, tenant farmers cannot own land. They have no choice but to cultivate the landlords' land, staking their lives and pouring their blood and sweat into it, whether they like it or not. However, in the fall, they must give more than half of their earnings to the landlords, and with the remainder, they must pay back debts for Manchurian seeds, fertilizer, and other miscellaneous expenses, including exorbitant interest rates of 7% or 5%. From the day of the harvest, they are plunged into a state of anxiety and worry about how to survive until the next year. Furthermore, as the landlords suffer from the economic downturn and struggle to pay land taxes and irrigation fees, they inevitably raise rents, leading to conflicts of interest and disputes between landlords and tenant farmers. These tenancy disputes arise because of the landlords' exploitation (着X) intensifies, and as the capitalist economic downturn deepens, they become more deeply rooted. Ultimately, farmers who cannot survive without land will eventually fight to protect their land. The number of tenancy disputes and participating tenant farmers over the past five years is as follows:

III. Why Create a Farmland Tenancy Law? (小作法令)

As tenancy disputes intensify, the authorities are greatly troubled, and the landlords realize that they need a simple way to suppress them. Some landlords try to alleviate the tenant farmers' animosity by pretending to love and care for them. However, tenant farmers, struggling for survival (生), are organizing their methods of dispute more systematically. The landlords have pleaded with the authorities to enact laws to prevent disputes. The authorities have first appointed tenant officials (小作官) to reside in several provinces (道) where disputes are most severe to prevent them from arising. However, the general trend is unstoppable, and they believe that a law with more coercive power is necessary. They have decided to enact a farmland tenancy law and are currently drafting two bills based on surveys of tenant administration (小作官行) in various regions. One bill is the Farmland Tenancy Mediation Ordinance (小作調停令), and the other is the Farmland Tenancy Law (小作令). The Farmland Tenancy Mediation Ordinance, as the name suggests, aims to mediate tenancy disputes. Although the draft has not yet been released, it is likely to be based on the Japanese model (it will certainly not be better, though it could be worse), so it may be helpful to consider it briefly.

Key Points of the Japanese Farmland Tenancy Mediation Law

1.  A mediation committee exists as a mediation body. (It can be done solely by the court or entrusted to appropriate individuals, but it is mainly done by the committee.) The committee consists of a judge and several mediation committee members (appointed by the district court judge from among those selected in advance).

2.  Mediation is, in principle, based on the parties involved. If there are many parties, they can elect a representative (總代), but they must be chosen from among the parties themselves.

3.  Administrative bodies can participate in mediation. Along with the mediation law, there is the position of tenant official (小作官), who investigates tenancy relations to prevent disputes and offers opinions during mediation. Furthermore, the heads of counties (郡), cities (市), towns (町), and villages (村) mediate and report on the progress of the case, and sometimes offer opinions. It is self-evident which side these people in such positions will favor.

The Japanese farmland tenancy law has been repeatedly attempted to be passed in the Diet (國會) but has failed. It is impossible to record all of its contents here due to space limitations. However, the tenant farmer side, specifically the peasant union (農民組合), organized a farmland tenancy law committee (小作法委員會) and created the "Key Points of the Farmland Tenancy Law," which they reported to the committee. I will conclude by introducing the reason why they created the "Key Points of the Farmland Tenancy Law."

IV. Farmland Tenancy Law and Tenant Farmers

First, establish cultivation rights (耕作權確立).
1. Even if the land is sold, the cultivation rights remain.
2. Recognize a conflicting position against land ownership (土地所有權).
3. Set the cultivation right period to ten years or more.
4. Prohibit the attachment of standing crops (立稻差押).
5. If the landlord takes back the land, they must provide reasonable compensation (賠償).
6. Provide guarantees against natural disasters (天變地災).
7. Abolish the Saŭm system (舍音制度).
Second, recognize the right to collective bargaining (團體協約權).
1. Not only should peasant unions not be restricted, but they should also be allowed to participate in all collective agreements.
Third, recognize the right to dispute (爭議權). <9>

二. 小作爭議의 激化
노동자가 공장을 가지지 못하엿슴과가티 소작인도 토지를 가지지 못하엿슴으로 지주의 토지를 경작하지 안을 수 업스니 조흐나 실흐나 거긔에 목숨을 걸고 피ㅅ땀을 흘니면서 일하는 것이다. 그러나 그 일한 소득은 가을에 가서 지주의게 절반 이상을 주고 그 남어지를 가지고 만주속 외상갑슬 물고 거름갑 외상을 물고 긔타 잡비에 쓴 묵은 빗을 물되 너푼ㅅ리자 오푼리자를 물고 나면 추수하는 그날부터 명년에 살아나갈 걱정과 근심을 하지 안으면 안되게 되는 불안한 상태에 빠지게 된다. 그 우에다가 지주는 불경긔가 되여서 지세 물기도 거북하는니 수리조합비를 부담하라느니 하야 소작료를 전보다 더 올님으로 필연적으로 리해가 충돌되야 지주와 소작인이 싸우게 되야 일으는 바 소작쟁의가 생기는 것이다. 소작쟁의는 지주의 착X가 심하면 심할사록 더 이러나는 것이요 자본주의의 불경긔가 심하면 심할사록 더 깁히 뿌리박혀서 결국 토지가 업시는 못사는 농민이 차차 토지를 직히려는 싸음까지 나가고야 말 것이다. 이제 최근 다섯해 동안의 소작쟁의 건수와 참가한 소작인 수를 보면 다음과 갓다.

三. 小作法令은 왜 만드나?
이와가티 소작쟁의가 격화하게 됨을 따라 당국은 크게 머리를 알케 되엿고 지주들은 간편하게 눌으는 방법이 잇서야 할 것을 깨달엇다. 그 중에도 엇던 지주는＜8＞ 소작인을 사랑하는 척 하고 따뜻한 정을 쓰는 척 하면서 소작인의 반감(反感)을 싯처 보랴는 수단을 쓰는 자도 잇다. 그러나 삶(生)을 위한 소작인들은 쟁의의 방법이 점점 조직적으로 되여간다. 지주들은 당국에 애원하야 쟁의를 막는 법령을 내도록 하엿다. 당국은 위선 소작관(小作官)을 두어 쟁의가 가장 심한 몃도(道)에 주재식혀서 쟁의가 못 니러나도록 하엿다. 그러나 대세는 엇절 수 업서서 좀 더 강제의힘이 잇는 법령이 아니면 안될 것이라고 생각하고 소작법령을 내기로 작정하고 위선 각지의 소작관행(小作官行)을 조사하야 두가지 법령 초안(草案)을 방금 기초(起草)중 이라는데 그 두가지 법령 중 하나는 소작조정령(小作調停令)이요 하나는 소작령(小作令)이다. 소작조정령이란 것은 글자 그대로 소작쟁의를 중재하자는 것인데 아즉 그 초안이 나오지 안엇슴으로 어떠타 말할 수는 업스나 일본에서 만들어 노흔 것을 그 골자 만들어 보면 (꼭 그와 갓다든지 그보다 못하면 못하엿지 낫다고는 할 수 업스나) 약간의 참고가 될 까 한다.
