기사형태	잡저 (Miscellany)

反省할 時間
한성의원 金鐸遠

Time for Reflection
Kim T'ak-won, Member of Hanseong Assembly

Arguments always arise from trivial matters, and especially arguments—though they might not even amount to fights—between husband and wife always stem from a desire to live well together. Moreover, because there is a very close relationship and mutual trust and love, questions of right and wrong constantly arise. If one always thinks, "I'm right, and you're wrong," then the problem only gets smaller and the emotions between neighbors only escalate. Without saying a word, one should quietly take a trip for a day or two, or in any case, separate from each other to allow time to reflect on who was right. This will prevent any major grudges from forming. Naturally, the emotions will cool down, and after a while, if you cautiously initiate conversation, even if the response is still sharp, as if the anger hasn't subsided, that's the end of it!

엄벙뗑으로
秦長燮

In a Haphazard Way
Ch'in Changsŏp

Arguing about whether personalities match or ideals align is a slightly different matter. Also, I will set aside arguments between couples who are forced to live together against their will. The marital fights I'm talking about here are the ones that erupt between couples who are otherwise living happily! The cause of other couples' arguments may be trivial, but the cause of my marital arguments is even more trivial. Originally, I can be counted among the *aeŭmga* (愛飮家, those who love to drink - though I can't quite call myself a *kŏŭm* (巨飮, heavy drinker). Incidents usually occur when I return home late or fail to keep a promise to return by a certain time. However, since neither of us is very flexible, the situation unexpectedly escalates like the ups and downs of the European war, leading to a formal declaration of war and sometimes even progressing to close combat. But, as opposed to the beginning, the end is often an armistice without any formal treaty. It's like watching chicks fight—it looks like they're always fighting. Without looking directly, I secretly slip out, buy something that my wife likes, or bring a close friend along and act haphazardly, using the excuse of having a guest to calm things down. After a little while, it's all over. She's originally a very cheerful person! However, if things get too serious, I have a close friend who can help mediate a peaceful treaty, so there's nothing to worry about. Sometimes, when I feel a sense of boredom in life, I feel like stirring things up a bit. You probably all know the fun of marital fights.

어린 아해로
方仁根

Like a Child
Pang In-gŭn

In the end, all sorts of things happen. To reveal a secret method for resolving marital fights is a peculiar thing. I hide everything from my wife, but what if my wife reads *Pyŏlgŏn'gon* (別乾坤) and says, "Aha, so you've been..." and from then on, my trick no longer works? If you take responsibility for preventing my wife from reading *Pyŏlgŏn'gon*, then it would be fine, but if not, it's a bit difficult! Well, it's not that I'm not good at it, but when it comes to marital fights, I don't lose to anyone. No matter which side initiates the challenge, we never fail to respond. Sometimes, when I'm bored, I make a fuss and shout a little, which wakes me up and makes things interesting. However, when the degree of intensity gets too high, I put on my hat, pull it down low, and storm out in a fit of rage. But no matter how angry I am, she always chases after me and grabs me. However, that doesn't mean I immediately get dragged back in. I wait for her to say, "I was wrong," and then, pretending to be unable to contain my anger, I slowly sit down. Since she has already said she was wrong, she can't say anything more. Then, the peace treaty is concluded without much fanfare!
However, for about two years after marriage, it was like the Three Kingdoms period, with fights erupting frequently! But lately, it seems to have faded, and now it's only occasional. Anyway, with this method, treaties are easily concluded, and recently, our young child has become a mediator for peace. However, even if I am clearly wrong, I never admit it first, lest it damage the authority of the husband!

降服하도록
申瀅澈

To Surrender
Shin Hyŏng-ch'ŏl

I rarely fight with my wife, but when an argument arises, regardless of who is at fault, I see it through to the end. However, I don't resort to violence like breaking furniture or engaging in hand-to-hand combat. Even if I am at fault or have done something wrong, I never bow down and surrender. I fight as much as I need to, and then I casually go out and come back. The amount of time I'm gone isn't fixed, but after spending enough time away to cool down, my wife has usually calmed down as well! However, I never initiate the conversation first. I don't open my mouth until she speaks to me first. Once someone starts talking, it's over, and everything fades away. Unless it's something that makes you truly angry!

녯兵法으로
朴瓚熙

With Old Military Tactics
Pak Ch'an-hŭi

Oh my! This is truly a *Pyŏl* of *Pyŏlgŏn'gon* (別의 別乾坤, special among the specials). There have been many珍奇 (rare and strange) articles, but now you're even asking about how to resolve marital fights. Matters between husband and wife are usually full of secrets, and if those secrets are revealed, your wife may resent you, and that may cause a fight, which could then be said to be an example of adding fuel to the fire. What if the story of how to make peace becomes the material of a fight? My wife is originally very high-strung and easily provoked. If I responded to her provocations, there would be daily fights in our home, but I have the opposite personality, very easygoing, and I mostly remain silent. When she gets angry, I smile <104> and when she raises her voice, I remain quiet. Then, before long, she smiles first and shows signs of regret. In the end, I am victorious. In terms of old military tactics, this could be called the wonderful method of *Ildaerau* (以逸待勞, awaiting the exhausted with ease) and *Paekchŏnpaeksŭng* (百戰百勝, winning every battle). Men and women who are high-strung are prone to anger but are also cheerful, so I just understand that and adjust accordingly.

武力으로 解決
李一

Resolving with Force
Yi Il

Just like international wars end and peace is quickly achieved only when one side's military power is strong and they fight decisively, domestic fights can be quickly resolved only when the husband's force is strong. Whenever we fight, I always make her surrender with force. If my wife does something that doesn't please me, I first express my anger and scold her, and she just unconditionally surrenders. This may be seen as male tyranny, but it is said that women become restless if they don't get beaten by their husbands for three days. Anyway, the only good way to treat a woman is to be strict. No matter how much you love and cherish your wife, if you aren't strict with her, she will become spoiled and only cause fights. Isn't it true that the closer you get to a cat, the more it bites you? A woman's temperament is even more bizarre than a cat's.

宣戰에 不應
朱耀翰

Not Responding to a Declaration of War
Chu Yo-han

I wonder if anyone will be jealous if I say this, but my wife and I are unusually affectionate, so even if we try to fight, we can't. Therefore, I don't have any specific methods for how to reconcile. However, if there is a misunderstanding, or for whatever reason, when there is an argument, especially when the other side declares war, I never respond to it, and I don't say whether it's right or wrong. I let them be as agitated as they want to be, and after the heat has died down, I examine the reasons. If it was a misunderstanding, I clear up the misunderstanding, and if the cause is with me, I apologize if I need to apologize, and if I need to deny it, I deny it and make sure they understand. Because when someone is agitated, they only say that they are right, even if they are doing something wrong, so I just leave them alone.

쓰다듬어 준다
趙基栗

To Stroke and Soothe
Cho Ki-ryul

Rather than it being about personality, my wife and I rarely, if ever, fight. If I ever get angry or upset and start nitpicking, instead of fighting back, my wife scolds me for nitpicking and says, "What's the point of saying such things?" So, I get embarrassed and stop. Because of this, there's no reason for us to fight, and if the other side shoots an arrow, I don't respond and, even if it's unbearable, I just stroke and soothe them. Originally, we don't fight much, so there's no special method for reconciliation either. <105>

성격이 그럿타는 것보다도 우리 부부 사이에는 닷투는 일이 별로가 안이라 아조 업다고 해도 거짓말이 안입니다. 혹 가다가 화가 나는 때라든지 비위에 틀여서 정말 싸와라도 볼야고 잔소리를 집어내이면 도리혀 안해쪽에서 대적은커녕 잔소리를 한다고 꾸짓는 격으로 『그런 말은 해 무엇하는냐』고 들은 체도 안이하닛가 도리혀 승거워저서 고만둠니다. 이러고보니 싸홈이 잇슬 까닭도 업고 혹 저편에서 篙矢를 쏘을 때에는 내가 역시 응하지 안코 좀 참을 수 업는 경우라도 쓰다듬어 주고 맘니다. 원래가 싸홈이라고는 별고 업스닛가 화해방법도 또한 別한 것이 업슴니다.＜105＞
