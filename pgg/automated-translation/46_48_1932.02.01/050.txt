別건곤 第48號
1932년 02월 01일
그들이 본 日中衝突, 中國人 移動座談會 (The Sino-Japanese Conflict as Seen by Them: A Moving Roundtable of Chinese People)
대담·좌담 (Dialogue/Roundtable)

The Sino-Japanese Conflict as Seen by Them: A Moving Roundtable of Chinese People
Participants
Swee Ah-in, Missionary
Wang Chih-chiang, Dumpling Shop
Nam Seung-bak, Dumpling Shop
Chang Sam-deok, Restaurant
Deung Cho, Carpenter
Yu Buk-tang, Hotteok Shop
One Person from the Headquarters

Reporter: "It's suddenly gotten cold."
Nam: "It's cold."
Reporter: "You're wearing such thick clothes, you probably aren't as cold as our Korean people!"
Chang: "What? It's the same!"
Reporter: "Where is your hometown?" (To Nam)
Nam: "My hometown is Shandong."
Reporter: "What about Mr. Yu?"
Yu: "The same."
Swee: "Everyone here is from Shandong."
Reporter: "Are times good these days?"
Nam: "Times?"
Reporter: "Are you making good money? Are things selling well, I mean."
Nam: "Oh! Are things selling well, you mean? They're not selling well. It's a mess."
Reporter: "What about Mr. Yu?"
Yu: "Just the same."
Reporter: "What's written on the wall?"
Swee: "Oh, that (自暴動以來現錢交易慨不*賬=벽에 써 잇는 글ㅅ귀) It's nothing."
Reporter: "After the Wanpaoshan Incident, it means that only cash transactions are accepted, no credit."
Nam: "Hehe... that's right."
Reporter: "So you do that even among Chinese people?"
Wang: "It's all the same."
Reporter: "Why?"
Yu: "If something like that happens again, we'll go back to our hometowns. Then how would we get our money back?"
Reporter: "You had a hard time back then?"
Nam: "Our bad people are separate."
Reporter: "Mr. Swee, what do you do? Have a drink."
Swee: "I can't. I'm a Christian."
Reporter: "What do you do?"
Swee: "I am a church missionary."
Reporter: "What about Mr. Deung?"
Deung: "Carpenter (木手)."
Reporter: "How many years have you been in Korea?"
Deung: "Three years."
Wang: "We've been here for one year."
Reporter: "What about Mr. Yu?"
Yu: "I've been here for ten years."
Reporter: "You're quite the dandy. You must have made a lot of money."
Yu: "Hehe... I haven't made a penny. I'm broke."
Reporter: "Mr. Chang! I heard that the Japanese bought Yeolbinru Bokhaheon (悅賓樓 福海軒)."
Chang: "I heard they did. They only sold the building."
Reporter: "How much did they sell it for?"
Chang: "Bokhaheon was 7,000 won. Yeolbinru was supposedly 130,000 won. We don't really know."
Reporter: "Why don't you return to your hometowns?"
Chang: "There won't be a war."
Reporter: "Why?"
Chang: "I don't know. Wait and see."
Reporter: "Do you hear any news from China these days?"
Wang: "We don't know anything."
Reporter: "Do you read the newspaper?"
Deung: "We don't read it."
Swee: "The authorities confiscate newspapers coming from China, and the newspapers here are all lies."
Wang: "We don't read the newspaper. We have to pay for it. And it's all lies anyway."
Reporter: "So you know that Japan and China are fighting in Manchuria?"
Wang: "We know."
Reporter: "How do you know?"
Nam: "He-che. Don't you know that?"
Reporter: "What happened after the fight?"
Nam: "We don't know."
Reporter: "What about Mr. Swee?"
Swee: "I haven't been here long, so I don't know the news."
Reporter: "How can you not know about your own country's affairs?"
Wang: "What can we do if we don't know?"
Chang: "No matter how much they fight, our China has no intention of fighting."
Reporter: "Manchuria is almost entirely occupied by Japanese soldiers, what will you do?"
Nam: "It's okay if we lose Manchuria. China is still a great country (大國)."
Reporter: "Don't talk nonsense. Japan has already occupied Jinzhou (錦州). You know Jinzhou, right? If this goes on, they'll occupy Tianjin (天津) and Beiping (北平) by tomorrow or the day after!"
Nam: "I don't know, I don't know. It's all lies!"
Reporter: "There's a Manchurian-Mongolian independence movement. Manchuria and Mongolia are separating from China and becoming independent. What do you think about that?"
Swee: "We don't know. But it's a trick. They can't become independent."
Yu: (Omitted due to illegibility in the original text)
Deung: "Anyway, this war is because of Chen Youren (陳友仁). That bad guy!"
Reporter: "Why?"
Deung: "That guy went to Japan and put on a show (演劇)."
Reporter: "It's true that Chen Youren went to Japan, but I believe he's not that kind of person. Do you know that Chen Youren is the Foreign Minister of the Guangdong government?"
Deung: "We don't know that."
Reporter: "Who among the Chinese people do you like the most?"
Wang: "Good people are good, and bad people are bad."
Reporter: "What about Zhang Xueliang (張學良)?"
Wang: "Good person."
Chang: "Zhang Xueliang is still young! It's a pity he was kicked out of Manchuria."
Reporter: "Mr. Yu! Who do you like?"
Yu: "I like Wu Peifu (吳佩孚) the most."
Reporter: "Wu Peifu? Wu Peifu doesn't have any soldiers or money now!"
Yu: "I don't know. If Feng Yuxiang (馮玉祥) hadn't betrayed Wu Peifu, Wu Peifu wouldn't have become like that. Feng Yuxiang is a bad guy."
Reporter: "Is Wu Peifu from Shandong?"
Yu: "Hehe - all Shandong people are good."
Reporter: "What about Chiang Kai-shek (張介石)?"
Wang: "Good person."
Yu: "Chiang Kai-shek is talented."
Reporter: "Because he has a lot of money and soldiers?"
Chang: "He has a lot of money and soldiers."
Reporter: "Chiang Kai-shek has already resigned (下野)."
Chang: "Lies."
Reporter: "Why?"
Chang: "Huh? He's been saying he's resigning for a while now, but he hasn't resigned."
Yu: "He resigned, but it's just words, not really."
Reporter: "What about Wang Jingwei (汪兆銘)?"
Nam: "Good person."
Wang: "An even better person than Chiang Kai-shek."
Yu: "Wang Jingwei worked with Sun Yat-sen, but Chiang Kai-shek worked under him."
Reporter: "What about Wang Tingzheng (王廷正)?"
Nam: "Good person."
Wang: "He was kicked out because of the students."
Reporter: "Do you know Ma Zhanshan (馬占山)?"
Nam: "Good person."
Reporter: "Where is he from?"
Nam: "I don't know, but he's a good person."
Yu: "He's from Heilongjiang."
Reporter: "What about Yuan Jinkai (袁金鎧)?"
Wang: "Bad guy."
Reporter: "Why?"
Wang: "I don't know. He's a bad person anyway."
Reporter: "Do you have any thoughts of going to war?"
Wang: "No. Why?"
Nam: "Even if Japan and China go to war, it has nothing to do with our hometown of Shandong."
Reporter: "I'm sorry. Thanks for talking for so long."
Everyone: "You're welcome!" <9>
