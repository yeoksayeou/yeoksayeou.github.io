별건곤 제72호
1934년 04월 01일
유모어소설, 키쓰내기 花鬪 (Humorous Novel, Kissing Hwatu/Flower Card Game)
李啞夫 (I A-bu)
소설 (Novel)

The husband came home late at night.
"You wench! Aren't you scared of me? Aren't you scared of heaven?"
It was a shout of inexplicable origin.
"Why are you so drunk..."
I quickly ran to my staggering husband and supported him as he crossed the threshold. However, my husband's attitude was completely different from when he was drunk before.

"Leave me alone! I'm tired of everything!" He pushed me, who was standing there bewildered, and shouted,
"Huh! You're one of those girls who play kissing hwatu (kissing flower cards), aren't you?"
I stood there dumbfounded for a while, just staring at my husband's face. What's wrong with him today?

"What school did you graduate from?"
"Why are you asking that all of a sudden?"
"I need to know!"
My husband plopped down on the floor, taking off his overcoat, suit, jacket, and tie without a moment's rest, scolding me as if a police officer were interrogating a thief at the police station.
I felt a little uncomfortable with my husband's behavior, even if he was drunk. It felt like he was ignoring and trampling on the fragile individuality of a woman.

"Even though the principle (理致) of heaven makes men grow beards and women carry handbags, where can one complain about such unbecoming behavior? It would be one thing if I were a kisaeng (妓生), a female entertainer, or a prostitute, but to hurl such unspeakable insults at one's own wife, what kind of outrageous thing is this?"
I, who usually considered myself the epitome of a virtuous and wise wife, felt a surge of rebellion at my husband's behavior.
My husband's bloodshot eyes were glaring at my mouth, which was pursed out like the neck of a gourd.
"What's with all the loud talk? Is it okay for students and teachers to sit together and play kissing hwatu (kissing flower cards)? Your school's XX got drunk and kissed someone, and got drunk and..."

Seeing my husband get to this point, I felt uneasy for no reason. It was clear that he had read about the principal's misconduct at my alma mater in today's C newspaper and made an absurd association to my own situation. So, even though I was not guilty in the slightest, I became unnecessarily frightened because of the unfortunate fact that I was a graduate of the school in question.
"Yŏng-ja!"
My husband called out my name as if an actor were reciting lines, then uttered the question that had been looming—like a bolt from the blue—with surprising ease.
"Yŏng-ja, do you have a guilty conscience?"

"What would I have a guilty conscience about?"
On one hand, I was overwhelmed and furious at this reckless question, but I swallowed it down and calmly retorted.
"Are you saying that Yŏng-ja did or did not play kissing hwatu (kissing flower cards) with XX?"
A battle of eyes—the eyes of a husband consumed by suspicion of his wife, and my eyes desperately defending against it—sparks were flying and thunder was rumbling.
"Why aren't you answering? Silence means you admit everything, right?"
"A-ni, what kind of thing are you saying?"

With a heavy heart I gripped my chest and glared at my husband.
"If not, then what is it? Who will vouch for Yŏng-ja's pure and untainted body? Who can guarantee Yŏng-ja's virginity before marriage?"
His tongue seemed to loosen under the influence of alcohol. My husband's tirade against women and denial of chastity (貞操否認論) went on for an hour with the eloquence of a flowing river. And finally, he concluded with:
"Tonight is the last night for Yŏng-ja and me!"
He declared the dissolution of our marriage (結婚解消), which made my heart sink and my vision blur.
Having sat there from beginning to end, unable to move under my husband's pressure, I burst into tears without a single word of protest, feeling so wronged and frustrated that my eyes swelled up.
It must have been past two in the morning. My husband, with a pale face flushed with anger, muttered as if he had discovered some slogan:
"Let's sleep today! Let's break up tomorrow!"
Then he collapsed onto the bed.

How do you think I felt that night, dear readers? Isn't a cat that has been unexpectedly attacked unable to put up any resistance and forced to surrender to the enemy? Thinking about how this terrible Y Teacher incident at Ero school caused an unexpected domestic disturbance, I felt as if the favors (師恩) of the past had turned into enemies today.
I tossed and turned all night.
...Kissing hwatu (kissing flower cards)!
...Dissolution of marriage!
(The sky is falling)

The next day-
My husband went to work without even having breakfast.
As he left, he stared at me, who had not slept a wink and had cried all night, pointed at my disheveled bangs, and left.
Until noon, I pondered how I could overcome this difficult situation. Of course, I was frustrated and resentful. Was this sweet and satisfying married life going to be shattered? But it was no time to wallow in sorrow.
An emergency in married life!
At times like these, we women have no choice but to mobilize all the cleverness we are innately born with.

When the clock struck noon, I stepped into the gate of Ŭnsuk Ŏnni's house, a former classmate from the school in question.
"Aigo, Yŏng-ja, how did you come here?" Ŭnsuk Ŏnni greeted me as if she had been wanting to see me for a long time.
"But why doesn't your face look as good as before? Have you been sick lately? Is there something worrying you at home?" <19>
"......"
"Why? Aren't you saying anything? Huh? Go on, open your mouth and tell me!"
Ŭnsuk Ŏnni was getting impatient, but I just sat on the edge of the veranda, without even the strength to reply.
"Say something. What's wrong?"
Waiting for Ŭnsuk Ŏnni's curiosity to reach 100%, I finally burst into tears in Ŭnsuk Ŏnni's arms.
"Ŏnni! What should I do?"
"Oh, did you have another lover's quarrel?"
"Well... a lover's quarrel has to be within reason..."
"Hey, you ugly thing! Alright, let's hear the story!"
Ŭnsuk Ŏnni looked a little relieved. Not knowing what I'm going through. It was annoying that she seemed to assume it was just a common lover's quarrel.
"Tell me quickly! So, how did you mess up?"
Ŭnsuk Ŏnni's tone was already starting to turn playful. I regretted coming to this house.
But Ŭnsuk Ŏnni was the only place I could discuss this matter.
"Ŏnni?"
"What?"

After hearing the whole story, Ŭnsuk Ŏnni said,
"You're still young, that's why. You haven't even been married for a year..."
Then she hung her head for a long time and said,
"In fact, I've been through the same thing as you. But as you know, I'm a bit of a smooth operator, aren't I? So, I managed to patch everything up last night..."
That was very welcome news to me. Ŏnni is resourceful! Maybe—? The thought flashed through my head that my difficult situation might be resolved with just a word from Ŏnni.
The angel of laughter was dancing on Ŭnsuk Ŏnni's lips.
"So, this morning, we both woke up and laughed and giggled! You simpletons don't know what to do when something like this happens, but that's really useless. You must always stay calm and think of a way to solve the problem. Otherwise, you'll just be playthings and objects of ridicule for men!"
"Then Ŏnni! What should I do?"
I was a little annoyed by Ŭnsuk Ŏnni's conceited attitude, but I had to ask her urgently to put out the fire on my own feet.
"The key to solving it is very simple!"
Ŭnsuk Ŏnni went into the room, wrote eight letters on a small piece of paper:
"XXXX
XXXX"
and handed it to me, saying,
"Hey, you fool! Go home and think about this!"
Then she pinched my shoulder and pushed me out.

When I got home, I started pondering this piece of paper. My fate would be decided by whether or not I could decipher it.
I had not eaten breakfast, nor could I eat lunch, until dinner—for five hours I struggled. What could this message mean? Moreover, since Chinese characters (Hanja) were a subject I had always struggled with in school, it was very difficult to decipher the meaning of the words. In the end, I even regretted not studying Chinese characters (Hanja) harder.
But as the saying goes, sincerity can move heaven, so after searching through the Kwangsa Rim (廣辭林) and flipping through the dictionary of idioms (熟語辭典), I finally understood the meaning of the message that Ŭnsuk Ŏnni had thrown at me like a riddle. At the same time, I made a plan of action to confront my husband when he came home.
-A battle to avenge the humiliation of yesterday!
-A holy war to relieve the humiliation on behalf of 10 million women!

War preparations complete!
Morale soaring!
My husband came home past midnight, carrying the scent of alcohol.
Today's Yŏng-ja did not need to be as loyal as a dog or as meek as a sheep in front of him like yesterday's Yŏng-ja.
"You're pretty drunk!"
I threw out the sharp first line of attack.
My husband, expressionless like a marble statue, retorted:
"I hit it!"
I interjected:
"Anyway, let's settle everything you said yesterday tonight... I've been racking my brain all day because of that, and it seems like the only way is to get divorced."
He probably didn't expect my attitude to change so much in one day.
"I'm glad!"
Even as he said that, he was glancing at my face with one eye and trying to read my heart with the other.

"To recklessly associate the Y Principal's misconduct with the wife who received Y Principal's education will ultimately bring turmoil to the married life of the two people. Therefore, there is no need to drag this matter out. Also, if we ignore the issue because of face and circumstances, it will only lead to resentment between us, so let's close our eyes tonight and liquidate everything!"
Even I thought I was being too bold and thorough.
"So, without any regrets for each other... what do you think?"
My husband seemed speechless at this question. Because my attitude was so firm, he couldn't help but be firm as well.
"Stop talking nonsense! I'm not going to lament your leaving!"
My husband seemed to be beside himself with anger. This was because he was more suspicious and resentful of the shocking change in my attitude between yesterday and today than the fact that I was leaving.
For a long time, silence—a heavy silence—continued between the two of us.
My husband, with a flushed face, got up as if he had resigned himself to everything, and urged me to leave with his eyes.
I reluctantly got up and said,
"But as we are separating, there is one thing I want to ask you! If it's a request, it's my last request..."
"What is it?"
"Since that damn kissing hwatu (kissing flower cards) is what broke and shattered our happy and sweet married life... let's play it one last time right now as I'm being kicked out! Who knows when and where that will become a regret after we are separated forever?"
As soon as I finished speaking, tears welled up from the pit of my stomach, whether it was fake crying or real crying, I couldn't tell, and tears poured out.
"Can't I even grant you that small wish?"
My husband, who had been silent for a while, jumped up, pulled out the hwatu (flower cards) from his desk, sat down in front of me, and pounced, saying,
"Let's play."

The next day-
It was a bright day with clear skies (雲捲靑天). My husband, who had cleared the breakfast table, looked at me, who had followed him to the gate,
smiled, and went to work with the happiest gait. And that evening, he came home early and, seeing me cooking dinner in the kitchen,
smiled and went into the room, stomping on the floor.
Isn't this a delightful scene?

Episode (Author's Note)

The exemplary home of Miss Yŏng-ae, introduced above, was on the verge of a domestic tragedy due to a temporary pathological social phenomenon (?), but it returned to a harmonious home many times better than before, thanks to the witty resourcefulness (機轉) of Mrs. Yŏng-ae.
One thing that you, the readers, may have been curious about while reading to the end is the content of the note that Miss Yŏng-ae's school classmate Ŭnsuk wrote to Miss Yŏng-ae. I will write it here. That is,
"以熱治熱-轉禍爲福 (I yŏl chi yŏl - Chŏn hwa wi pok)"
Translated, it means "Treat heat with heat. Turn misfortune into blessing," which in modern terms means, "Cure the disease caused by the kissing hwatu (kissing flower cards) incident with the means of kissing hwatu (kissing flower cards). If the incident has caused a disturbance in the family, reverse it and make it the happiness of the family."
And now, in Miss Yŏng-ae's house, these two phrases are enshrined in a scroll like affectionate brothers, as one of the family heirlooms to be passed down to future generations. (Author's Note)-End-

「이년! 내가 무섭지? 한울이 무섭지?」
곡절을 알 수 업는 호통소리이엇습니다.
「웬 약주가 저리 취하셔서...」
하고 나는 날쌔게 비틀거름을 치는 남편에게로 쫏차가서 문지방을 넘는 그의 몸을 부축하엿습니다. 그러나 남편의 태도는 전날의 술취한 때와는 아조 달럿습니다.
「고만두어! 다 구찬타!」하고 당황하고 섯는 나를 밀치더니
「흥! 너도 키쓰내기 화투한 년이지?」
하고 소리를 버럭 질음니다.
나는 한동안 어안이 벙벙하야 남편의 얼골만 처다보앗습니다. 오늘에 한하야 이이가 웨이러나? 하고-
「너 어느 학교 졸업햇늬?」
「그건 웨 새삼스러히 물어보세요?」
「알 필요가 잇스니말이지!」
하고 남편은 방바닥에 펄석 주저안저 외투, 양복, 저고리, 넥타이채 벗고 끌을 새 업시 마치 경찰서에서 순사가 도적놈을 닥다르듯키 얼너대입니다.
나는 아모리 술취한 남편의 행동이라도 이러는 데에는 좀 불쾌한 생각이 들엇습니다. 너무나 간얇힌 녀성의 개생(個性)을 무시하고 짓밟는 것 가터서-
「비록 한울의 리치(理致)가 남자는 쉬염이 나게 하고 녀자는 핸드빽을 가지고 단이게 햇기로 그 쉬염갑세 못가는 행태를 어데다 한단 말이야요? 기생이나 녀급이나 창부이나 술파는 게집가트면 몰라도 그 안해에게 입에 담지 못할 욕지걸이를 하다니 이 무슨 해괴한 일이야요?」
평소에는 현모량처로서의 전형적 녀성이라고 자임(自任)하든 나이지마는 남편의 행태에는 저윽이 반항의 불길이 솟아올랏습니다.
게슴치레하게 뜬 남편의 두 눈은 호리병가티 뾰죽이 나온 주둥이를 쏘아보고 잇습니다.
「무슨 큰소리야? 제자와 선생이 모여안저 키쓰내기 화투한 것이 잘한 노릇이란 말이냐? 너의 학교의 XX이란 자가 ......에 취해 키쓰하고 에 취해...」
남편의 말이 이쯤 나온 것을 보고 나는 공연스러히 마음속이 설네엿습니다. 이이가 이 말을 끄내는 것을 보면 오늘 저녁 C 신문에 모교의 교장 추행사건이 실린 것을 가지고 나의 신상에까지 얼토당토안은 련상(聯想)을 한 것이 분명합니다. 그러니까<18> 죄는 털끗만치도 업지만 문제되는 모교의 졸업생이라는 불행한 가다가기 때문에 쓸데업시 겁을 집어먹게되는 것입니다.
「영자!」
남편은 나의 일홈을 연극배우가 세리프를 외우는 것가티 불으더니
「영자는 찔리는 구석이 업나?」
하고 막연히 예긔하엿든-청천벽력과 가튼-질문을 그이는 퍽 용이한듯키 내놋습니다.
「찔리기는 무엇이 찔리어요?」
한편으로는 주책업시 터트려논 이 질문에 분통이 턱밋까지 치미는 나는 꿀꺽 참고 침착한 어조로 반문하얏습니다.
「영자는 그래 XX과 키쓰내기 화투는 안햇느냐는 말이야?」
두 눈과 두 눈의 싸홈- 의첫증(疑妻症)을 일으킨 남편의 두 눈총과 그것을 필사적으로 방어하는 나의 눈총에서는 전광(電光)이 번쩍번쩍 우뢰(雨雷)가 우루루할 지경입니다.
「웨 대답이 업서? 침묵은 모든 사실을 시인한는 말이지?」
「아-니 그게 무슨 말슴이라고 하시는 것이야요?」
벅차게 떨리는 가슴을 부여 안ㅅ고 나는 남편을 노려보앗습니다.
「아니면 무엇이야? 누가 영자의 몸이 순결무후하다고 변명해 줄것이야? 결혼전 영자의 처녀성을 보증할 사람이 누구야?」
술기운에 혀끗은 부드러운 모양 갓습니다. 남편의 녀성저주론과 정조부인론(貞操否認論)은 현하(懸河)의 달변으로 무려 한 시간 동안의 장광설이 계속 되엿습니다. 그리고 나종에는 결론으로
「영자와 나는 오늘밤이 마지막이다!」
라고 가슴이 뜩끔하고 눈압피 캄캄한 결혼해소(結婚解消)의 선언을 내렷습니다.
처음부터 끗까지 남편의 위압밋헤 꼼짝달삭 못하고 안저잇든 나는 한 마듸의 발악도 업시 어떠케 어이업고 분통이 터지고 서러운지 두 눈이 뚱뚱 붓도록 흐늣겨 울기만 하얏습니다.
새로 두시가 지낫나 봅니다. 침통한 얼골에 상기가된 남편은
「오늘은 자자! 래일은 헤어지자!」
무슨 슬로간이나 발견한듯키 중얼거리더니 고만 자리우에 떨어집니다.
그날밤 나의 심경은 어떠하얏겠습니까? 독자 여러분!- 불의의 습격을 당한 고양이는 암상도 피우지 못하고 적에게 백긔(白旗)를 순순히 내걸은 폭이 되지 안습니까? 그 몹슬(?) 에로교장 Y선생사건 때문에 난데업는 가정풍파가 일어난 것을 생각하면 옛날의 사은(師恩)이 돌이어 오늘의 원수갓습니다.
그 한밤을 나는 전전반측하얏습니다.

...결혼해소!

결혼생활의 비상시!
이런 때에는 우리 녀성들이 선척적으로 가진 꾀주머니의 총동원을 바래지 안흘 수 업습니다.

오정이 뚜! 하고 불 때 나는 전일의 동창생인 문제의 학교의 은숙언니집 대문안에 발을 드려노앗습니다.

하고 은숙언니는 성화가 낫스나 나는 마루 한편쪽에 안진채 댓구할 긔력조차 일헛습니다.
「말좀 해. 웬일이야?」
은숙언니의 궁금쯩이 100%에 오르기를 기다려 나는 결국 은숙언니의 품에 안ㅅ긴채 울음주머니를 터트려 노핫습니다.
