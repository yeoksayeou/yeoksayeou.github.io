Strange Stories of Professions, unbelievably bizarre true stories, the true story of saving twelve death row inmates
						辯護士 Yi In

It was about six years ago, on the 11th day of the first lunar month of Kapja year, when twenty-some innocent civilians from Dokjeong-ri, Jangan-myeon, Suwŏn were suddenly bound by judicial police officers and became inmates in a detention center. The people of the township, both high and low, were merely astonished and bewildered, not knowing what had happened, and their families and close relatives spent their days in great anxiety and fear.

Later, they gradually learned that the issue was that they were arrested as suspects in a horrific criminal case involving murder, body abandonment, and intimidation, but they knew nothing about the crime or the investigation.

The case went from the police box to the police station, from the police station to the prosecutor's office, and the prosecutor's office, deeming them guilty, requested a preliminary examination. After investigating for over a year, the preliminary examination concluded that the crime was sufficiently evidenced and ruled them guilty.

The ignorant and simple villagers, the accused, thought, "I am innocent and have committed no crime, so surely..." but still spent a year and six months in a steel cage in anxious anticipation, eventually standing trial in a court of judgment.

As the trial date approached, the defendants told me, who visited them in prison, with one voice, "Sir, I am innocent. I have never seen the facts stated in the preliminary examination decision even in my dreams." However, the records, amounting to over three thousand pages, and the numerous pieces of physical evidence were all disadvantageous to the defendants. The confessions of the defendants, which were consistent across the four places—Hwangho police box, police station, prosecutor's office, and preliminary examination—made it difficult to overturn the guilty verdict, even if there was a thousand times more evidence to the contrary. Moreover, the defendants were not only mentally confused after a long time in prison, but also half-dead due to 00 (illegible) since their imprisonment. Their families were destitute, and even if there was any evidence that would benefit the villagers, the ignorant villagers would not want to go to government offices and would avoid it individually. There was no material or personal evidence to the contrary. The result of the visit was only hearing words like, "How could that be? I don't know. There isn't any. Sir, it's really unfair and unjust," and I returned without getting to the bottom of it.

The criminal facts that appeared in the preliminary examination decision were that the defendant U 00, having married and lived with Song XX's younger sister, XXja, from Jangan-ri, Jangan-myeon, Suwŏn, fourteen years ago, was constantly pressured by her to divorce her husband, 000, because he had no future prospects. XXja often lived happily. One day in the first lunar month of Kapja year, on the 4th night, XXja ran away from home and went to her lover, Ch'oe △△△'s room, where she spent the night as if they were a married couple, enjoying a moonlit conversation. The husband, 000, discovered this on the evening of the 6th of the same month and, enraged by the belief that his wife was having an affair because she was influenced by her brother, Song XX, gathered his younger brother and about ten relatives, confined his brother-in-law, XXX, in a room, and interrogated and beat him. XXX, unable to withstand the severe torture, begged to be released, promising to bring XXja. However, the defendants, fearing that XXX would run away, did not release him. XXX had no choice but to send a written message to his relatives, saying that they must bring XXja immediately, or he would die. As there was no news even by night, the defendants became extremely angry, and the defendant 000 conspired with the defendant 00 and eleven others to beat XXX to death, abandon his body on the coast near Jangan-ri, and leave the deceased's clothes and shoes on the embankment near the coast, pretending it was a suicide. <55>

The next day, XXX's wife, children, and relatives searched everywhere for the missing XXX. They found his clothes and shoes discarded near the coastal embankment and suspected that the defendants had murdered XXX, abandoned his body in the sea, and made it look like a suicide. About ten of his relatives went to the house of the defendant 000 to inquire about XXX's life or death. The defendants, about ten of them, said, "You remarried a married woman, and XXX ran away from the house last night and returned to his home. You are deliberately trying to cause trouble, so we can't let you get away with it." They beat them mercilessly. Also, the defendants, about ten of them, thought it would be good to get a certificate stating that XXX had returned to his house on the morning of the 8th to avoid responsibility for XXX's murder. As a result of threatening to kill one of the people who came to find XXX unless they wrote a certificate, they were forced to write a certificate stating, "XXX returned on the morning of the 8th, and the defendants have nothing to do with it." <56>
*In the voluminous record, the statements of related witnesses and defendants consistently say that they beat him to death, abandoned him on the coast, and committed intimidation, giving excellent confessions naturally.

The defense attorney, feeling dejected with no room for defense, only watched the judge's mouth move in the courtroom, while still mentally alert, hoping there might be some material for acquittal.

The judge said that the case was complex, but since they had confessed everything, it would be easy. He thought the defendants would only answer as asked, but unexpectedly, the defendants denied all the criminal facts and claimed that the confession was a false statement against the truth due to the 00 (illegible) of the police station. However, they had made the same confession to the prosecutor and the preliminary examination judge as they had to the police station, so even if the 00 (illegible) of the police station were true, there was no way to avoid the fact that the defendants' denial of the criminal facts in the courtroom could only be regarded as a common characteristic of defendants.

After the factual examination, when presenting the evidence to the defendants, the judge showed the defendants a large bundle wrapped in cloth and asked if they had worn the clothes wrapped in it on the 7th and 8th of the first lunar month (i.e., the clothes worn when killing XXX). All twelve defendants answered yes. The defense attorney casually had the court attendant untie the cloth-wrapped clothes and looked at them here and there. Unexpectedly, all the dozen or so clothes were spotlessly clean, with no stains and hardly any wrinkles.

The defense attorney, who had been looking at these clothes without much thought, suddenly began to have various suspicions and thoughts.

"Even though it was around the 7th day of the first lunar month, they might have worn new clothes, but after beating someone for over ten hours and then transporting him to the coast, which is more than ten li away, in the drizzling rain at midnight, the clothes they wore were too neatly worn and not dirty at all. Furthermore, the dead person wouldn't have just sat still and taken it; he would have resisted to some extent, so it's strange that there are hardly any wrinkles on the clothes." The defense attorney spread the clothes out in front of the judge and explained, pointing out that it had rained a bit that night, that the place where XXX was beaten and the coast where the body was dumped were more than ten li apart, and that the coast was in front of the village where the dead man lived. It gradually came out from the defendants' mouths. It was even stranger to think that they had killed a person and deliberately transported and dumped the body on the coast in front of the dead man's village, which was more than ten li away, on a cold, rainy night. Meanwhile, letters started coming to the judge and the defense attorney saying that XXX, who was said to be dead, was not dead. The police box in the dead man's village also reported that XXX had fled, tired of his brother-in-law 000's harassment and beatings, and pretended to be dead to deceive his brother-in-law, and that it was rumored that he was not actually dead. Then, it was rumored that on the morning after XXX was said to have died, the dead man's wife was passing by the opposite place where the body was dumped, carrying her husband's durumagi (overcoat), beoseon (Korean socks), and banghanmo (winter hat). On the other hand, <57> when the dead man was tied up in his brother-in-law OOO's house all night, an old woman in the neighborhood got up to urinate around two o'clock in the morning and heard running sounds like a man's footsteps from OOO's house, but it seemed like only one person, meaning that the dead man had escaped alone.

However, all of this was just rumor and circumstantial evidence. Considering that the defendants beat XXX and confined him for a day and night so that he could not escape, and that on the next day, whether he was really dead or not, his clothes and shoes were left on the coastal embankment in front of the village where he lived, making it look like a suicide, and that the body floated up from the coast fourteen days after his death, and that the twelve defendants had given excellent, consistent confessions to the police box, police station, prosecutor's office, and preliminary examination for over two years, and that the defendants had intimidated XXX's relatives into giving a certificate stating that the dead XXX had come and gone the day before he died and had nothing to do with it, it was difficult to accept the defendants' thousands of words of excuses, and the defendants' fate was such that even with the special protection of the gods, it was impossible to save them. The defendants said that the police station and police box arbitrarily 00 (illegible) people and inflicted cruel 00 (illegible), causing them to be in a state of 00 (illegible) and to confess as they were told. They also said that the police officer told them, "You guys have to do the same even if you go to the prosecutor's office, or else 00 (illegible)," so they said the same thing at the prosecutor's office as they had at the police station. They said that they were going to fight to the death and say that the confession was a lie at the preliminary examination, but since the police station was used as a substitute for the prison detention center in the countryside, the police officer who had interrogated them at the police station was always behind the defendants, so they thought about what would happen when they returned to the police station and could not tell the preliminary examination judge, and were only saying it now. However, it didn't seem like the judge would listen to them.

In the end, the defendants were only saying that they were innocent and wronged, and I, as the defense attorney, had exhausted all means. There were overlapping doubts, but there was no way to defend them. When the prosecutor, in his vehement argument, demanded that the twelve defendants be sentenced to death or life imprisonment, the defendants were on the verge of collapsing in the courtroom, and I, as the defense attorney, vehemently argued for their innocence, but I had no confidence that they would be acquitted. The decision was made, and the date of the verdict was set. It was clear that the defendants' fate would be the same as the prosecutor's demand. The defense attorney could not sleep well at night, thinking of the twelve defendants whose fate was like a candle in the wind, and he had no appetite. He sat alone, saying that there were strange cases, and only smacked his lips. On the day before the verdict was to be announced (of course, the court had already prepared the verdict that was the same as the prosecutor's demand), he went to the court and reviewed the records once more.

However, there was nothing particularly unusual. "Ah, this must be the defendants' fate," he thought, and as he turned to leave, he suddenly wondered if the dead man was really XXX and looked at the records in detail once more. Of course, the police station, the prosecutor's office, and the preliminary examination, as well as <58> related witnesses and even the dead man's wife and children, had been called in by the defendants the day before and beaten. The next day, the dead man's clothes and shoes were found on the coastal embankment in front of the village, and when the body floated up fourteen days after the death, they knew it was XXX because the height, build, and face were similar. However, they had not bothered to investigate whether the body was definitely XXX or not, so they had treated the body as the dead XXX's body for two years. Therefore, there was no evidence in the record that the body was definitely the dead XXX, and there was no autopsy or appraisal of the body.

The defense attorney finally felt alive again and immediately applied for a retrial and requested witnesses. After persuading the judges, who shook their heads for two days, he managed to get the trial reopened. As a result of examining the witnesses, it was finally revealed that they had not definitely investigated whether the body was XXX or not. When a person went missing and they made it look like a suicide, it seemed like they had killed a person due to a coincidence. When the body floated up from the sea in just over ten days, they said it was the dead XXX because the height and face were similar. The defense attorney realized that the letters he had received were not lies and that the rumors were not false. As a result of having the court have the body examined by a doctor, the appraisal was that they could not tell whether the body was XXX or someone else.

As a result of the retrial, a not-guilty verdict was handed down, which was beyond imagination. The lives of the dozen or so defendants, which had been on the verge of death, were revived in a miraculous way, and they were released one by one.

However, to this day, the whereabouts of XXX, who was said to be dead, are unknown, and his family holds memorial services for him, saying he is dead. The released defendants told the defense attorney that they had truly not killed XXX. This case remains an eternal mystery and enigma in Jangan-myeon, Suwŏn. <59>
