S Dormitory Bedbug Elder's Diary

Crossing the mountain-like threshold and passing through several hole-like spaces, while also avoiding the clever gazes of pretty girls, I slowly crept from room to room. Before I knew it, the sun had long passed over the western mountains and I finally arrived. (63 lines omitted from this point in the original text)

K Dormitory Mosquito Husband's Diary

Determined to do something, I cried "Eeeeeng" more forlornly than usual, searching for K Dormitory, the closest to my birthplace. Unexpectedly, most of the female students had gone home, and only a few girls with special circumstances remained.
"Since I've come all this way," I decided to make it my hunting ground for the night.
In the early evening, I flew into a room and took a look, but what a disaster!
There was a rather pretty female student, gesticulating and gesturing as she busily applied makeup. There was no way I could dare to bother her!
"But she can't go out anywhere at this hour. Why is she like that? Ah, she's taking out a Western dress and doing a series of irregular dances in front of that small mirror to check her front and back views. Now she's changed back into a newly ironed Korean dress and wandering around the room. What's the matter?" As I was just watching the young lady's actions, the dormitory cook cautiously opened the sliding door.
"Hey, they say a hotteok vendor (호떡 장사) came to collect payment for the hotteoks." As soon as the cook finished speaking, the pretty young lady seemed to bristle for a moment.
"Shhh," she gestured, barely loud enough for me, sitting on her head, to hear, "Here, take this one-won bill and take only fifty jeon for now. Use the remaining fifty jeon as your tip. By the way, I'm going to see a movie tonight. Is there only one supervisor (사감 선생님) here now?"
The cook smiled broadly,
"Yes! Don't worry about the supervisor. I'll keep a good watch. Go out through the back door of the kitchen tonight. I'll open it for you even if it's late. But be sure to put your slippers neatly in front of this room, and leave your name tag on..."
As they were talking like this, I thought, "Now is my chance!" and lightly flew to the young lady's ear and sipped a drop of sweet wine.
"That annoying mosquito!" she exclaimed, slapping. But where was I! She only slapped her own cheek! Could such a scatterbrained woman dare to lay a hand on me! Next, I flew with a poetic "Eeeeeng" sound, sneaking into an empty room towards the best-smelling corner room.
The young lady there seemed so engrossed in writing a letter that she couldn't even look around. No matter how closely I flew by her side, buzzing "Eeeeeng," she didn't even flinch.
But I was a tenacious pest—"Yeah, perfect!"—and I just indiscriminately bit her feet, arms, nose, back, everywhere! But even then, that young lady was completely absorbed in writing!
She must have written at least ten letters, all in one go? I don't know when, but a box of chocolates that had been on the desk disappeared, but she wrote a lot of letters.
In some envelopes, she put flower petals, and in others, she probably folded in embroidered bookmarks or picture stickers?
What was even funnier was that after carefully sealing the envelopes, she would kiss them, pressing them to her lips for a long time!
At that moment, I shamelessly gave the young lady a big bite on the back of her neck.
Before I knew it, my belly was so full that I didn't even have the strength to fly. I was resting on the light cord when the clock struck twelve! The young lady seemed to be reading a novel and crying by herself in surprise!
But I was snoring... August 8th.

D Dormitory Flea Madam's Lament

Another day of starvation! Where have the young ladies in this room gone again! No matter how much I wait for the pretty young ladies in the damp papered cracks, they don't stay in the room.
No matter how much I run from room to room all day long, it's no use. It's truly frustrating! How can I live if I don't have time to eat leisurely!
The young ladies are like rain going out into the yard—what are they going out to buy! Maybe they want to go outside often, so they use it as an excuse to buy one thing at a time.
And unlike other times, it gets worse as autumn comes. On top of that, they go around in groups of fellow students, making it even harder for me.
They seem to read more books than usual, but they only sneak out under trees or to school corners, so how can my heart grow?
At this rate, I'll be in big trouble if I just stay lying down! From tomorrow, I should try to realize the adventure of lurking in the folds of the young ladies' clothes, like my husband.
I'll think about tomorrow tomorrow. Today, I'll just endure a little longer and wait for the midnight snack. While they eat their midnight snack, I'll listen to the young ladies' gossip and even more juicy secrets—the hidden stories of the young ladies' summer vacation romances—while lying quietly under the soft blanket.
But when I think about waiting until that time, I feel even hungrier—oh dear.

B Dormitory Louse Official's Boast

Firstly, it's good because it's easy to hide deep inside the soft cotton clothes of the student misses (학생 앗씨) in winter, and it's warm.
Secondly, it's good because, as the end of the school year approaches, the misses (앗씨) from distant rooms move around from room to room under the pretense of competing in studies with the commuting students, making it easy for me, who has no legs, to move.
Thirdly, even if the room was clean enough that I couldn't be satisfied with it at first, it gradually becomes messy, and several misses (앗씨) gather to bake rice cakes or cook porridge in the brazier in the room, unbeknownst to the supervisors (사감 선생님). Thanks to that, the room becomes very cluttered. As a result, I can decide on a place to give birth without spending even a penny, so I can easily expand our offspring day by day, which is truly a great blessing (萬福).
Fourthly, even the misses (앗씨) who pretended to be aloof in the summer, as winter winds blow, they don't even wash their underwear well, so in this situation, the laundry that our group fears most is reduced, so we live in a peaceful world and are infinitely happy.
Fifthly, these dormitory misses (긔숙사 앗씨) seem to be practicing heterosexual romance, and as autumn turns to winter, they become engrossed in same-sex romance. During the day, they write letters, and in the early evening, they hug and cry in this corner and that corner. At night, they carefully place their slippers in front of the door and fight for a place to sleep in other people's rooms. In the end, they hug each other and whisper their respective circumstances. Around midnight, they seem unable to endure their exhaustion and briefly fall into a state of complete silence, unaware of the situation. From this time onwards, our group unhesitatingly and very happily beat drums and hold a grand feast.
How happy our life is! It's worth shaking our heads and boasting.
