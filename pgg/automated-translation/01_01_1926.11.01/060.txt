Eight Ch'on Below the Nose

In the old days, back in the time of Emperor Wu of Han (漢武帝) in China (支那), there was a famous person known as Samch'ŏngapcha Tongbang Sak (三千甲子 東方朔), whom likely no one in our Chosŏn (朝鮮), not even children or lowly servants (兒童走卒), would fail to know. At that time, a certain physiognomist looked at Emperor Wu of Han and said, "The philtrum below your nose (鼻下) is long, measuring one *ch'on* (一寸), so living a long life of 100 years (百歲長壽) will be no difficulty." Then as now, people like to hear that they will live a long life, so Emperor Wu, overjoyed, boasted to everyone he saw that he would live to be 100, his mouth stretching wide with pride.

One day, Emperor Wu saw Tongbang Sak and, as usual, bragged again about his long life. Tongbang Sak suddenly looked up at the heavens and laughed out loud (仰天大笑). Emperor Wu became greatly angered (怒) and reprimanded him, saying, "How disrespectful (不敬) is it for a subject to laugh irreverently (無嚴하게) at the sovereign's words?" Tongbang Sak, forcibly holding his stomach and calming his laughter, replied, "In ancient times, P'aengjo (彭祖) lived for 800 years (八百 歲). If the theory holds that a philtrum of one *ch'on* means living 100 years, then P'aengjo's philtrum must have been eight *ch'on* (八寸) long. If there were ever a person in this world with a philtrum eight *ch'on* long, who wouldn't laugh upon seeing them? I was thinking about that, and laughter just came out naturally." And then he chuckled heartily again. Emperor Wu, who had been greatly angered, also burst into laughter on the spot (當場), and the whole place turned into a sea of laughter. <41>

**Summary**

The article recounts an anecdote about Tongbang Sak, a witty figure from Han Dynasty China. When Emperor Wu boasts about a prediction that his one-*ch'on*-long philtrum guarantees a 100-year lifespan, Tongbang Sak mocks the prediction by humorously calculating that the legendary 800-year-old P'aengjo must have had an absurdly long eight-*ch'on* philtrum, causing everyone, including the emperor, to laugh. From a historical perspective, this piece, published in Korea in 1926, uses a classical Chinese tale likely familiar to educated Koreans. It reflects the continued cultural currency of Chinese historical anecdotes and figures in colonial Korea, serving perhaps as light entertainment or subtle commentary on credulity and authority through humor.

鼻下八寸
녯날 녯적 支那의 漢武帝 때에 유명한 사람으로 三千甲子 東方朔이라 하면 우리 朝鮮 사람도 兒童走卒 할 것 업시 모를 사람이 업슬 것이다. 그 때에 엇던 觀相 보는 사람이 漢武帝를 보고 말하기를 迤下의 인중(鼻下)이 한치(一寸)나 되게 기니 百歲長壽는 無難다고 하얏다. 녯날이나 지금이나 사람이 오래 산다면 조와하는 法이라 武帝는 보는 사람마다 自己가 百年長壽를 하겟다고 반가워서 자랑을 입이 찌여지도록 하엿다. 하루는 武帝가 東方朔을 보고 例와 가티 또 自己가 長壽한다는 말을 자랑하니 東方朔은 별안간에 仰天大笑 함으로 武帝가 크게 怒하야 責하되 臣下가 君上의 말슴에 無嚴하게 웃는 것이 무슨 不敬한 일이냐 한즉 東方朔은 억지로 배를 붓잡고 우숨을 진뎡하면서 하는 말이 「녯적의 彭祖는 八百 歲를 사럿는데 인중이 한치가 되면 百歲를 산다는 理論으로 생각해보면 彭祖의 인중은 八寸이나 될터이나 萬一 이 世上에 잉중이 八寸이나 되는 사람이 잇다하면 누가 보고 웃지 안켓슴닛가. 저는 그 생각을 하고 우슴이 절로 나서 우섯슴니다 하고 또 깔깔 우슴을 치니 크게 怒하얏던 武帝도 當場에 우슴이 또 나서 전체가 우숨의 바다로 化하엿섯다.＜41＞
