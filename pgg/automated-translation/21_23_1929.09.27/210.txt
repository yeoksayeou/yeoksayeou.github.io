2일 동안에 서울 구경 골고로 하는 法, 시골親舊 案內할 路順......
一記者
Even a person who was born, raised, and lives in Seoul feels a stirring in their heart from Yŏngdŭngp'o or Susae Station as they approach Kyŏngsŏng (京城) on their way back from a trip. It is understandable that someone visiting for the first time or after many years would feel that way from Suwŏn or Kaesŏng, and feel inexplicably bewildered.

Because that is understandable, it is also natural that they are unable to thoroughly enjoy their long-awaited Seoul sightseeing trip, rushing around and forgetting what they wanted to see, and even losing their belongings on the way back.

So, I was ordered to write "A Method for Thoroughly Touring Seoul in Just Two Days" for those people, but what to see in Seoul will vary depending on who you ask.

Some might be satisfied if I just tell them to count the bars of the Chongno Chonggak (鍾路 鐘閣), or to just look at the gates of Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮) and Ch'angdŏk Palace (昌德宮), or to count the stone structures of the Chosŏn Shrine (朝鮮神宮), but I really have to guide them thoroughly, without missing anything worth seeing in Kyŏngsŏng. It's not difficult, but it is a bit awkward.

Whether it works or not, I will now assume that a person from my hometown has arrived at Kyŏngsŏng Station (京城驛) for a Seoul sightseeing trip and guide them. People who are not from my hometown can read this and follow it as well. Since the introduction of historic sites and relics will be in another article, I will only write about the sights here.

Kyŏngsŏng Station - Ah, don't rush out so quickly as if you're being chased out. Take some time to look around the station. Everyone puts it off until they leave, but when they leave, they don't have time to look around. After you've set foot in Kyŏngsŏng, what's the rush? Take your time, lag behind, and look around the station before going out. This building cost over 1.94 million won and was started in Im Sul year (壬戌年), taking four years to complete in Ŭl Ch'uk year (乙丑年) in October. You have to go up to the second floor via the wide stairs to find the exit to the outside, so let's go up slowly. Ah, here's the second floor. It looks like we have to go down another set of stairs to get outside, but we can get to the ground directly from the second floor. So, this is actually the first floor, and there's another second floor above us, and the place where the train arrived is the basement... In that basement, there is the train departure platform <58>, a restaurant, and an office, as well as a Western and Japanese restaurant and a warehouse. On the first floor, there is a waiting room, a VIP room, a free toilet (無料便所), and a pay toilet (有料便所). And on the second floor, there are offices and large and small restaurants that serve the most expensive Western cuisine only to customers with deep pockets.
"What is a 'pay toilet' (有料便所)?"

Ha, that's why Seoul is so great. If you're in a hurry to go to the bathroom, go to the pay toilet (有料便所) if the second-class or third-class waiting room toilets are full. To put it bluntly, it's a clean toilet decorated like a concubine's house (妾致家), and you can go in and do your business for just 3 chŏn.
Okay, let's go outside. There are lines of rickshaws and automobiles. Those automobiles are all taxis with a uniform fare within the city, but if you get in thinking it's a uniform fare, they'll know you're a country bumpkin and charge you more than 2 won with all sorts of excuses. It's best not to take one on the way in. And it's best not to entrust your luggage to the inn guides who rush at you like leeches, saying, "Come to our inn. Leave your luggage with me. I'll carry it." At first, they're grateful that you're going to their inn and bow obsequiously, but once they keep you locked up, they'll charge you whatever price they want, and you'll be trapped... Okay, let's not take the streetcar and walk to see Namdaemun (南大門). We can take it when our legs get tired.

Namdaemun - That's Namdaemun. Since the station was built on this side, this gate has become the main gateway to Kyŏngsŏng (京城). When you think about how Kyŏngsŏng, not just Kyŏngsŏng, but all of Chosŏn's new civilization and new trends have been received through this gate, this gate has a lot of stories and unique pride to match the turbulent history of Kyŏngsŏng for five hundred years... The blue vines covering it like an intentionally colored painting are nice, but building a tall brick building, whether it is an exhibition hall or not, and an even taller chimney next to it is like painting dung on this gate.
"It seems like a 'Namdaemun lookalike (南大門상)' has appeared with Namdaemun as well."

You're quite cynical. Indeed, seeing the left and right sides of this gate and even other unnecessary signboards, it's a really bad eyesore.
Okay, let's go straight along this road that branches off to the left from the front of Namdaemun (南大門). This is T'aep'yŏng Street (太平通), and that gray giant building that looks like the end of an alley is the Kyŏngsŏng City Hall (京城府廳), the county office of this town. Let's look as we go along this road. The unique thing about this street is that there are many antique shops and secondhand stores on both sides. In Kyŏngsŏng, the two antique shop districts are Hwanggeumchŏng (黃金町) and this T'aep'yŏng Street (太平通). Hwanggeumchŏng (黃金町) has a lot of used furniture, while this side specializes in used bedding, used shoes, used clothes, and used chamber pots... If there's something you want to buy and they ask for 5 won, offer only 1 won and haggle for about an hour, then buy it for about 2 won.

Chungguk Village (中國村) - This road, which connects T'aep'yŏng Street (太平通) to Sŏsomun (西小門), used to be a road for funeral processions, but now it's like a Chinese settlement. Across T'aep'yŏng Street (太平通), even across the back alley of the Public Hall (公會堂), there are gloomy brick houses without windows, giving off a Hu (胡人) smell, making passersby feel uncomfortable, and in the afternoon, even the smell of opium wafts through the air. There are opium dens here that are completely ruining Chosŏn people, and there are many traders in this neighborhood who steal young girls, buy them for 7 or 10 won, and send them to China via Inch'ŏn (仁川), making it a terrifying den of iniquity.

Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮) - You don't have to make a special trip to see it, but since it's on the way, it's a place full of memories, having weathered many storms in its final years. But since King Yi (李太王) passed away, wooden fences have been put up instead of guards, so you can't even see the front yard. The Renaissance-style stone building (石造殿) inside is both pitiable and infinitely regrettable.
"What is a Renaissance-style stone building (石造殿)?"

In the backyard inside, there is a very magnificent stone Western-style palace <59>, built by the Englishman Brown (뿌라운), the former Chief of the Customs Office (總稅務司) who was in Korea during the Korean Empire era, and its style is the famous Renaissance style (文藝復興式).

Courthouse (裁判所) - The alley next to the Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮) wall can be called either the Foreigner Village (西洋人村) or the Consulate Village (領事舘村). The newly built, grand building at the entrance is the High Court (高等法院), the District Court (地方法院), and even the Privy Council (中樞院) are located here. The trial of the 33 people in Kiminyŏn (己未年) was also held here, and now it is practically a private road for those wearing prison uniforms. Because major and minor incidents are all held here, it is crowded with spectators, and the photos that appear in the newspaper every day are all from this courtyard.... Let's walk further.

Consulate (領事舘) - In front of the main gate of the Courthouse (法院正門), you can see many places, even if it's just sightseeing. To the west, that towering red Western-style building is Paejae High School (培材高等學校), the successor to Paejae School (培材學堂), and to the northwest, that tall building is the Ewha Girls' School (梨花學堂), the Ewha Women's Kingdom (梨花女人國). These two schools were the first educational institutions established by Westerners as missionaries. Paejae has 800 students, and Ewha has 700 students, including those in the Ordinary School (普通校), High School (高等校), Specialized School (專門校), and Kindergarten Teacher Training School (幼稚師範), which is quite a lot. Let's postpone the tour of the school contents until tomorrow and just pass by today. This is the Chŏngdong Church (貞洞禮拜堂), which is uniquely thriving thanks to Ewha.
In front of it, that house with the Star-Spangled Banner (星條旗) raised high is the American Consulate (美國領事舘), and below it, that house with the Union Jack (쌀米字旗) is the British Consulate (英國領事舘). The British Consulate (英領舘) shares a wall with Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮). And up there, the white Western-style building that looks the highest, with the bright red flag raised the highest, is the Russian Consulate (榮農露國領事舘). The Russian Consulate (露領舘) doesn't have such bad habits, but if you go to the gates of the British and American Consulates (英米領事館), there's a spectacle: in offices that are smaller than a personal sarangbang (舍廊), a few staff members are idly chatting, but at the gate, they have men wearing black (Chinese-style) clothes and turban-like headgear (텬벙거지) with blue and red bands on top of their topknots, recreating the old escort guard attire. Isn't their psychology strange?
"It's good for people who haven't seen the old escort guard attire to have something to see."

That's right. You can see them wearing the old attire as it is... Okay, let's go. We have to squeeze through the walls of the British and American Consulates (英米의 領事舘).

Broadcasting Station (放送局) - We're heading out to Najugae (夜珠개) now. Look at that. That building built tall on top of that hill is the Kyŏngsŏng Broadcasting Station (京城放送局), "JODK" (쩨-오띄케). The singers and musicians in that broadcasting station stand alone in a room and sing, and their voices go up that tall ladder-like structure, spreading in all directions on the wires, carried by the wind, reaching not only the countryside but also Japan and China.
Okay, here's Najugae (夜珠개). In the old days, it was the most prosperous place after Chongno (鍾路). From here, you can take the streetcar directly to Sŏdaemun (西大門) outside or Tongdaemun (東大門) or Kwanghŭimun (光熙門), but let's walk slowly east... Okay, here's Yukcho Street (六曹). In the old days and now, it's the road for powerful officials.
The white Western-style building on the southeast corner is the Tonga Ilbo Newspaper Company (東亞日報社). The one with many billboards on the northeast corner is the Memorial Monument Pavilion (紀念碑閣), and the building at the end of the north side is the Government-General Building (總督府) built in front of Kyŏngbok Palace (景福宮). Turning around again and looking south, we see Kyŏngsŏng City Hall (京城府廳) that we saw earlier. And in front of it is Tŏksu Palace (德壽宮) that we saw earlier. Okay, let's go inside the Tonga Ilbo Newspaper Company (東亞日報社) for a moment.

Tonga Ilbo Newspaper Company (東亞日報社) - This building is said to have been built with great effort to maintain the image of a major newspaper company in Chosŏn, costing over 147,200 won in total construction. It is in the Modern Renaissance style (近代復興式). Because they are very busy, they generally do not allow individual visitors, but I know several people at this company, so they will give us a special tour. Now let's look at the Memorial Monument Pavilion (紀念碑閣).

Memorial Monument Pavilion (紀念碑閣) - It was built to commemorate the 40th anniversary of the enthronement and the 60th birthday of King Yi, Kwangmu Emperor (光武皇帝), so it is just a Celebration Monument (稱慶紀念碑) and not very grand. However, it is located next to the road, like an idle old man, so you can read the inscriptions on the monument.
This is the Postal Service Bureau (遞信局). That is the Law School (法學專門學校). In front of the Government-General Building (總督府), on the west side, is the Chosŏn Infantry Unit (朝鮮步兵隊). On the east side is the Kyŏnggi Provincial Office (京畿道廳). The Government-General Building (總督府) is what they boast as the best building in the Orient, costing over 6.34 million won to build, starting in Taishō 5 (大正5年) and completing in Taishō 15 (同15年), taking exactly 10 years or 11 years in total. It's easy to get hassled by the security guard if you get too close to the entrance, so let's look at it from this distance.

Kwanghwamun (光化門) - We can't miss Kwanghwamun (光化門), which is sitting by the Samch'ŏngdong Stream (三淸洞 냇가), surrounded by the iron fence of the Government-General Building (總督府) to the northeast. They were going to completely demolish it, but due to public opinion, they moved it here. This gate, with even the haetae (해태) gone, stands alone, like it is bearing all the sorrows of Kyŏngsŏng (京城) and crying all the time.
Facing this gate is the Medical College Hospital (醫專病院), which was pushed here after the main building was taken by the Imperial University (帝大). Let's turn around and go back. If you cross the new road hill east from Tongshipchak (東十字閣), here is Andong Yukgŏri (安洞六거리).
Because most of the Chosŏn students in the Pukch'on area (北村一帶) pass through this road to go to school, it is full of students during the morning school hours, so it is called Student Yukgŏri (學生六거리).
It is inexplicable that the residence of the Colonial Bank (殖産銀行) is located on this busy main street, and that an idle Byeolgung (別宮) is taking a nap.
Sandwiched between the two major streets, Chŏndong (典洞) and Insa-dong (仁寺洞), and suffering from headaches from the noise, is Tongdŏk Girls' School (同德女學校). With over 800 students in the Ordinary School (普通校) and High School (高普校) crowding in a narrow yard, not even the size of a personal backyard, they must be rubbing their backsides against each other.
"I heard the new school principal is rich."

Well, money is only useful if you spend it. I heard they are planning to move to a site in Samch'ŏngdong (三淸洞), but if they don't hurry up, it will just be empty talk.
Let's go out to Chŏndong main street (典洞 큰길). That pointy house is the Sicheon'gyo (侍天敎) of the late Song Pyŏng-jun (宋秉畯) and Yi Yong-gu (李容九). Inside, there is the Girls' Commercial School (女子商業學校), where the girls in green skirts with red trim are chattering. In front of it, on the street, the two-story Chosŏn-style (朝鮮式) houses are lined up like a row of runaways, looking like the abandoned sites of naengmyŏn (冷麵) restaurants that went bankrupt. However, this second floor is a magnificent second floor. First, the second floor above the Sŏkt'an (石炭) mine on this side is one room of the Chosŏn Youth General Alliance (朝鮮少年總聯盟). Next, the second floor with many signs is the Seoul Youth Association (서울靑年會).
Youth General Alliance (靑年總同盟) - Various organizations engaged in intense activities on various social movement lines have their signs attached to one place and are located in one room like that. Maybe it's too early, but even when I look up, there's no one there... ...

Chosŏn Ilbo Newspaper Company (朝鮮日報社) - Okay, here's the Chosŏn Ilbo Newspaper Company (朝鮮日報社). The building is not as good as the Tonga Ilbo Newspaper Company (東亞日報), but it's a place where they're working hard to not fall behind. The people inside are different and there's a bit of a difference in scale, but since it's the same as Tonga, there's no need to go inside... It's already a little past noon, and my legs are tired, so we should eat lunch. Okay, come this way. Let's see a couple more places and then go to Chŏndong Restaurant (典洞食堂).

Kŭnwuhwae (槿友會) - This house is a restaurant, but Kŭnwuhwae (槿友會) is in front of the main gate of this house, so it's better to go there first. Although it receives some criticism, it is the headquarters of a gathering of modern women with good intentions, so it cannot be taken lightly. Isn't it surprising that there are over 5,000 members belonging to it throughout Chosŏn?
"When I went inside, there was only a phone hanging alone in an empty room."

Maybe they've all gone out on visits. A few activists can't stay still for a day.

Taedong Printing (大東印刷) - It's next door, so let's take a look before going in. Although the building is small and cramped, it is the largest printing house on the Chosŏn side, so we can't help but see it to understand the efficiency of cultural supply. By combining three printing houses, Pogeum Printing (福音印刷所), Sŏngmunsa (誠文社), and one other, and with the support of Chosŏn books and other influential publishers in Kyŏngsŏng, they are continuing to compete with the large Japanese company. They also printed many family trees here, but most of the new Korean-language prints are done here. Since the Posŏngsa (普成社) run by the Ch'ŏndogyo Church (天道敎會) burned down in Kiminyŏn (己未年), it has been the largest printing house in Kyŏngsŏng. It is a three-story building. The lower floor is for machinery, the second floor is for typesetting, and the third floor is the office and bindery. The company has a total of 170 employees.

Restaurant (食堂) - There are many restaurants besides this Chŏndong Restaurant (典洞食堂), but this one seems to be the best, and since we're passing by this way, we have no choice but to eat here. Naengmyŏn (冷麵), bibimbap (비빔밥), sangbap (床밥), or Taegu t'angbap (大邱湯飯) are all 20 chŏn each, so 40 chŏn for two people, two plates of galbi (갈비) for 60 chŏn, and a cup of liquor for 50 chŏn. It's simple, cheap, modest, and a bit noisy... Okay, let's get out of here quickly so others can come in and sit down.

Chongno 4-ga (鍾路四街) - If your legs weren't tired, you could just stand here for an hour and see all sorts of people passing by and understand the lives of people in Kyŏngsŏng. The attire of the people waiting for the streetcar. The way the people walking walk. The kisaeng (기생) riding in rickshaws. The female students carrying books. The playboys riding in automobiles wearing female student uniforms. Detectives, reporters, teachers, con men, vagrants, bobbed-hair girls, opium dealers all pass through here... Okay, it's been more than an hour, so let's leave again.

Poshingak (普信閣) - We'll hear about the Ingyŏng story (인경殿) in detail later, so you can go and count the bars. (Ingyŏng story Separate article)

YMCA Hall (靑年會舘) - That large Western-style house standing next to the Chongno Police Station (종로경찰서) like a warship is the Central Christian YMCA Hall (中央基督敎靑年會舘). Twenty-five years ago, an American donated 80,000 won, and with the money raised in Chosŏn, they built it for 300,000 won. Inside, there is a day and night school, and not only are there separate specialized institutions for learning photography, printing, and woodworking, but there is also an auditorium that can accommodate about 5-600 people. Until the Ch'ŏndogyo New Church (天道敎新敎堂) and its Memorial Hall (紀念舘) were built, it was the only public hall for civilians in Kyŏngsŏng. Let's go inside and look around for about 30 minutes. <62>

T'apkol Park (塔洞公園) - There is nothing as worthwhile to see as T'apkol Park (탑골공원). No matter how busy you are in Seoul, this is a must-see place, making it a famous spot in Kyŏngsŏng. But what's going on? All the bums (게름방) in Kyŏngsŏng have gathered here. Take a close look at the pagoda (塔), the turtle monument (龜碑), and the Octagonal Pavilion (八角亭). Shinganhoe (新幹會) - Let's briefly visit the headquarters of Shinganhoe (新幹會), located in Tŏgwŏn Building (德元삘딍) on the second floor, next to Ch'ŏnggu Photo Studio (靑邱寫眞舘), on the corner of the alley of Kyŏngsŏng's second Chinatown (中國人村), where Taegwanwŏn (大觀園) is located across from the park. There are over 150 branches and a total of 35,000 members throughout Chosŏn. It's a pity that they don't do much because there is so much interference, but they are currently working on creating a meeting hall.

Ch'ŏndogyo Church (天道敎堂) - Go north 4-5 chŏng (丁) from the upper alley of the park, and you'll find a place with three very tall and large Western-style buildings on a very wide yard. This is the Ch'ŏndogyo Central Church (天道敎中央敎堂), the Central Chongniwon (中央宗理院), and the Memorial Hall (紀念館). Among the buildings built solely with the power of Chosŏn people in Kyŏngsŏng, it is the largest building, so isn't it ultimately the largest building in Chosŏn? With a religious order of over 3.5 million followers (道人), it's truly a magnificent building. It cost 260,000 won to build just one church in Kiminyŏn (己未年), and it comfortably accommodates 2,000 people in a field-like (曠野) wide building without a central pillar, so isn't it magnificent? Also, the tall pavilion in front of this church is like a five-story pavilion, and if you go up there and open the glass windows, you can look down and see the entire Kyŏngsŏng (京城一圓) like a small chessboard. Okay, let's go up once. If you come to Seoul and don't go up here, it's a wasted trip.
The memorial hall (紀念館) built on the street in front of it was built as a memorial project for the centenary of Suun Sŏnsaeng (水雲先生), the founder of this church, to be used for any purpose in general society. It can accommodate 1,400 people in upper and lower chairs, and the stage is so large that it is equipped for lectures, music, plays, judo, boxing, and even film screenings, so there is nothing missing. Almost all social discussions on the Chosŏn side, from any convention to any general assembly, are held in this building. It was truly a worthwhile and grateful thing to do.
Also, another building, the Chongniwŏn (宗理院), is the office of the church and the Youth Party. Including the Ch'ŏndogyo Youth Party (天道敎靑年黨), the General Headquarters of all-Chosŏn organizations, such as the Boys' United Association (少年聯合會), the Student Association Headquarters (學生會本部), the April Association Headquarters (四月會本部), the Naesudan Headquarters (內修團本部), and the Chosŏn Farmers' Society Headquarters (朝鮮農民社本部), are housed here, making all plans here. 6 or 7 types of magazines are published monthly from this building, sending out over 60,000 copies each month, so it is truly a sacred arsenal.

Library (圖書館) - The Chongno Branch (鍾路分館) of the Kyŏngsŏng Library (京城圖書館). No matter how tired you are, you must see this place because it is a very necessary institution for local culture. Let's pay 2 chŏn each and go inside. Okay, the outside is so noisy <63>, but inside, so many people are sitting quietly, not even making a cough, just reading books. One book costs 2 or 3 won on average, and expensive ones cost 6 or 7 won, and some cost over 10 won, so it's not easy to buy and read such books individually. And unless you have a sarangbang (舍廊間), you can't study quietly, so if you come here and pay just 2 chŏn, you can borrow and read any book you want. Isn't this a great institution? Local leaders should definitely see this when they come to Seoul, and shouldn't such things be created in every locality, both large and small?
It's disturbing to study even if you look for a long time, so let's get out of here quickly.
Now let's go rest, eat dinner, and go to the theater at night.
Shall we go to Kwangmudae (光武臺) or the Chosŏn Theater (朝鮮劇場)? You'll have to plan to spend 1 won per person wherever you go (admission ticket, tea, etc.).
Come out at half past eleven and look around the Naeoe Hotel (內外酒店 - a house of sake and women) behind Tunŭi-dong (敦義洞 - Myŏngwŏlgwan main branch (明月館本店)), but if you eat at one house for a long time, only the empty sake bottles will come and go, and they will charge you 2 or 3 times the price of the sake, so don't have more than 2 or 3 cups at one house and move to another house.

Second Day - On the second day, as a pre-breakfast exercise, get up early and go to Student Yukgŏri (學生六거리) around six o'clock to look around. The people coming out of this alley and that alley with lunch boxes are people going to places like printing houses, railroad bureaus, rubber factories, and tobacco companies. After seven o'clock, it's school time, so you'll see a crowd marching, mixed with male and female students, specialized students, and even kindergarten children. Isn't it true that you can't say you've seen Kyŏngsŏng (京城) unless you've seen this?
After eating breakfast, go to Hwimun (徽文) or Chungang High School (中央高普) in Pukch'on (北村), pretend to be a parent, and tour the teaching conditions and facilities. Then tour Chaedong Girls' High School (齋洞女子高普) and see the 600-year-old white pine in the backyard. Then cross Kuri Street (구리개) and see the Oriental Development Company (東洋拓殖會社). As you go up next to it, go to the former Kyŏngsŏng City Hall (京城府廳), now the Job Placement Center (職業紹介所), and hear and see the sad stories of the unemployed in Seoul. That's how you really get to see Seoul properly. Coming out of there, look around the Marut'ŏk (마르턱) of Chinkogae (진고개), but you should see this after the electric lights are turned on, so just pass by roughly. See the Science Museum (科學館) on the former site of the Government-General (總督府), and then come back down and take the streetcar that goes through Kuri Street (구리개) to tour Ch'anggyŏngwŏn (昌慶苑). Eat lunch in front of the botanical garden (식물원) and come out. Just look at the University Hospital (大學病院) and take the streetcar back to Chinkogae (진고개) to see "Shinmachi (신마찌)". Cross over to Changch'ungdan (獎忠壇) and rest your legs. Go outside the castle and look around the lives of the people in Shindang-ri (新堂里) who live in tombs surrounded by folding screens. Enter through Kwanghŭimun (光熙門) and look around the Kyŏngsŏng Stadium (京城運動場). Look at Tongdaemun (東大門) and the crooked castle wall. From here, take the Kyŏngsŏng specialty bus (뻐쓰車). Give 5 chŏn to the pretty female conductor (女車掌), and it will shake violently and run away, turning in front of the Sŏdaemun Police Station (西大門警察署) in front of the former Kyŏnggi Gamyeong (京畿監營) outside Sŏdaemun (西大門), and go to Muhwagwan (慕華館), Yŏnjumun (연주문), and behind Independence Gate (獨立門), saying, "Get off, please." Tour Independence Gate (獨立門) and peek into Sŏdaemun Prison (西大門監獄). Even if you don't want to, go to Akbagol (악박골), drink a lot of Lazoom medicine (라쥼 만흔 藥물), climb up Tŭngsongi (등송이) and look into the prison facilities. Then slowly come down and go to the School for the Blind and Deaf (盲啞學校) behind Ch'ŏn'yŏnjŏng (天然亭) next to the stone bridge and see the deaf and blind children studying. Then slowly go down, but before you reach Sŏsomunchŏng (西小門町), there is the Tobacco Monopoly Bureau (煙草專賣局). Watch the flood of male and female workers with yellowed faces pouring out when the dinner bell rings. And take the Shinryongsan streetcar (新龍山電車) to the Han River (漢江) and see the iron bridge (鐵橋) and the setting sun (落照). And look at the evening smoke from the chimneys of the Kyŏngsŏng Spinning Company (京城紡績會社) standing far across the river. Go into the Gangbyŏn Restaurant (江邊飮食店), rest your legs, and eat taro soup rice (장국밥). And at night, Chinkogae (진고개).... What about the hidden bars in Pukch'on (北村)?

2일 동안에 서울 구경 골고로 하는 法, 시골親舊 案內할 路順......
						一記者
서울서 나서 서울서 자라서 서울서 사는 사람도 객지에 잇다가 도라오는 길에는 京城이 갓가워 오는 생각하고 永登浦나 水色정거장에서부터 가슴이 울넝거려진다. 처음 오거나 여러 해ㅅ만에 오는 사람이 水原이나 開城에서부터 가슴이 울넝거려지고 공연히 황당해지는 것은 허물할 수 업는 것이다.
그것이 허물할 수 업는 일인 까닭에 못처럼 별르고 별러서 온 서울 구경을 골고로 하지 못하고 허둥지둥하다가 볼 것은 니저 버리고 가젓든 물건도 니러 버리고 도라가게 되기 쉬운 것도 당연한 일이라 할 것이다.
그럼 사람들을 위해서 『단 이틀동안에 서울 구경을 골고로 하는 법』을 쓰라는 명령이 나렷지만 서울서 구경해야 할 것이 무엇무엇일지 생각하기에 따라서 다를 것이다.
鍾路 鐘閣의 창살이나 헤여 보고 갈 사람이면 德壽宮 昌德宮 大門이나 바라보고 朝鮮神宮의 石築이나 헤여 보고 가라고 안내해 주면 만족해 할는지 모르나 정말 京城 와서 볼 것을 골고로 빼지 말고 안내하여야 할 것이니 힘들 것까지는 업스나 조곰 거북살스런 일이다.
되나 못 되나 지금 내 고향 사람 하나이 서울 구경길로 京城驛에 도착하엿다 가정하고 안내를 할 것이니 내 고향 사람 아닌 사람은 이것을 닑어 두엇다가 이대로 하면 될것이다. 名勝古蹟의 史蹟 소개는 다른 기사에 잇스닛가 여긔는 것구경만 스리기로 한다.

京城역-앗다. 그리 쫏겨 나가듯 급하게 나가지 말고 정거장 속을 구경 좀 하여요. 나려 갈 때에 본다고 아모라도 뒤로 미루지만 나려 갈 때는 시간이 급하닛가 구경할 사이가 업는 것이라네. 京城 땅을 드듼 다음에야 급할 것이 무엇 잇나. 천천히 뒤에 떠러저서 정거장 속 구경이나 하고 나갈 일이지. 이 집이 194만여 圓을 드려서 壬戌년에 起工하야 4년 걸너 乙丑년 10월에 마친 것인대 저 넓은 층계로 2층으로 올너가야 밧그러 나가는 출구가 잇스니 천천이 올너가세. 자아 여긔가 2층이니 밧그로 나가려면 층계 하나를 도로 나려가야 할 것갓지만 2층에서 그냥 땅으로 나스게 되엿스니 결국 여긔가 1층이요 2층은 이 우에 또 잇고 앗가 기차가 와서 다은 곳은 지하층이지.... 그 지하층에 기차 <58> 發着場과 식당과 사무실이 잇고 또 和洋요리점이 잇고 창고가 잇고 그러고 일층에는 대합실 귀빈실 無料便所 有料便所가 잇고 2층 우에는 사무실과 대소식당이 또 잇서서 제일 빗싼 서양요리를 주머니 큰 손님에만 대접한다네.
『有料便所란 무언가.』
흥 그러게 서울이 고맙다지. 자네도 大便이 급한 때 2등 대합실 변소나 3등 대합실 변소라도 가보아서 滿員이 되엿거던 有料변소로 가요. 안이할 말로 妾致家하듯 깻끗이 꾸민 변소인대 돈 3錢만  내면 드러가 눈다네.
자아 밧그로 나와요. 人力車自働車가 列을 지어 잇는대 자동차는 저게 모다 市內 一圓均一의 택시지만 一圓均一이라고 알고 섯불니 타면 시골뚝이인 줄 알고 이 핑게 저 핑게로 2圓이상 밧기고 예사이니 처음 올너 오는 길로는 타지 안는 것이 조코 『저의 여관으로 가시지요. 가방을 저를 맛기십시요. 제가 가지고 가지요.』하고 빗장이처럼 덤비는 여관 안내인에게도 섯불니 가방을 맛기지 안는 것이 좃타네. 처음에는 자기에게로 가 주는 것만 고마워서 굽실굽실 하지만 갓다로 묵혀만 노코는 부르는 것이 定價요 이꾀 저꾀에 꼭 붓들니는 사람이 되느니.... 자아 電車는 타지 말고 거러서 南大門 뵙고 천천히 가다가 다리가 압흔 때 타기로 하지.

南大門-저것이 南大門일세. 정거장이 이쪽에 생긴 후로 이 문은 京城의 出入大門이 되여 버렷고 京城뿐 안이라 朝鮮의 新文明 新風潮는 모다 이 문으로 바더 드린 것을 생각해 보면 京城 오백년간의 波瀾만흔 역사와 맛잡아 이 문의 서른 事情과 남다른 자랑이 만흔 것일세.... . 일부러 彩色 칠한 것가티 덥혀 잇는 푸른 덩쿨은 조흐나 이 엽헤 무슨 陳列舘인지 놉흔 벽돌집과 더 놉흔 굴둑을 세우게 하는 것은 이 문에 똥칠을 한 짓일세.
『南大門에도 樣字가 부터서 「南大門상」이 된 셈일세 그려.』
자네도 상당한 險口일세그려. 사실 이 문의 左右 엽흘 보고 쓸데 업는 딴 문패까지 다른 것을 보면 똥칠도 심한 똥칠이지.
자아 이러케 南大門 안턱에서 왼편으로 곳장 뚫닌 이 길이 太平通이고 골목 막다른 집가티 마조 보이는 회색 巨屋 저것이 이 마을 郡廳인 京城府廳이라네 이 길로 가면서 보세. 자아 이 길의 좌우 엽헤는 古物商 넉마전 만흔 것이 특색인대 京城에서 古物商거리라면 黃金町하고 이 太平通하고 두 군대인대 黃金町은 헌-家具가 만코 이 쪽은 헌 니부자리 헌 구두 헌옷 헌 요강이 전문이야.... 살것이 잇는대 한 오圓 내라 하거던 1圓만 바드라 해 가지고 한시간쯤 승강하다가 한 2圓 주고 사야 하느니.

中國村-太平通에서 西小門으로 通한 이 길은 예전이면 行喪이 다니는 길이엿던 것이 지금은 中國 사람의 居留地처럼 되야 太平通 길 건너의 公會堂 뒷골목까지 건너 걸처서 들窓도 업는 벽돌집이 우중충하게  느러 잇는 까닭에 胡人 냄새라니. 지나가는 行人의 비위가 거슬니는대 오후에는 아편 태는 냄새까지 어울녀 난다네. 朝鮮 사람을 고시란히 亡케하는 阿片窟이 여긔 잇고 나어린 少女를 훔처오면 7圓이나 10圓 주고 사서 仁川 거처 中國으로 보내는 貿易商들도 이 洞里에 만흐니 말하면 무서운 魔窟이라네.

德壽宮-일부러 차저 올 것은 업서도 지나는 길에 잇스니 末年에 風波 만히 격든 곳으로 記憶 만흔 곳이지만 李太王 도라가신 후 派守兵 대신 木柵이 둘너 잇서서 압마당조차 드려다 볼수도 업시 되엿스니 이 속에 가치여 잇는 루네싼스式 石造殿이 가엽기도 하고 앗갑기도 限이 업지.
『루네싼스式 石造殿이라니 무엇 말인가』
이 안에 뒷마당에 아조 훌륭한 石造 洋屋宮殿이<59> 잇는데 예前 韓國時代에 와 잇던 總稅務司 英人 『뿌라운』이라는 사람이 지은 것으로 그 式이 유명한 文藝復興式이라네.

裁判所-德壽宮담 엽골목이 여기가 西洋人村이래도 조코 領事舘村이래도 조흔 골목인대 이 初入에 새로은지은 저 宏大한 건물이 高等法院이요 또 地方法院이요 中樞院까지 여긔 잇다네. 己未년 33人의 公判도 여긔서 열녓고 지금은 용수 쓴 이들의 專用路라 할만큼 大小 事件이 여긔서 모다 열는 까닭으로 傍聽客 踏至하고 新聞에 날마다 나는 사진이 모다 이 마당이라네.... . 더 거러가세.

領事舘-法院正門 압 이 압헤 여긔서 것구경일망정 여러 곳을 볼 수 잇스니 자 서쪽으로 웃둑한 저 붉은 洋屋이 培材學堂의 後身인 培材高普요 西北便으로 놉흔 저 집이 梨花學堂인 梨花女人國인대 이 두 학교가 서양 사람들이 선교사업의 압잡이로 교육기관을 세운 맨 첫번의 일이엿다네. 培材에는 800명 梨花에는 普通校 高等校 專門校 幼稚師範까지 합하야 700여 명이니 무던한 일이지. 학교 내용 구경은 내일 어데던지 할 셈치고 오늘은 그냥 지나가세. 자 이것이 梨花 덕분에 남달니 번창 잘 하는 貞洞禮拜堂이라네.
이 압헤 저 星條旗 놉히 단 집 저것이 米國領事舘이요 저 아래 쌀米字旗 단 집이 英國領事舘이니 英領舘은 德壽宮과 담 하나 사이이지. 그리고 저 위에 제일 놉하 보히는 白色洋屋에 제일 놉히 샛빩안 旗를 단 곳이 저게 榮農露國의 領事舘이라네. 露領舘에는 그런 낫분 버릇이 업지만은 英米領事館에는 대문에 가 보면 장관이 잇느니 사무실이라야 개인의 舍廊만도 못한 집에 사무원 數人이 한가한 하폄을 하고 잇지만은 대문간에는 黑色(中國式)服에 상투 우에 靑紅色 테를 두른 텬벙거지를 씨워서 녯날 扈衛隊服色을 식여 느러 세운 것은 놈들의 심리가 怪惡한 것 안인가 말이야.
『옛날 扈衛隊服色을 못 본 사람에게 구경거리는 조켓네.』
그러치. 녯날 복색을 그대로 닙고 다니는 꼴을 볼 수 잇스닛가.... 자- 어서 가세. 英米의 領事舘 담 사이로 끼고 넘어가랴면.

放送局-압흐로 하야 夜珠개로 나가게 된다네. 저것 보게. 저 언덕박이 우에 놉다럿케 지은 집이 『쩨-오띄케』라는 京城放送局이라네. 저 방속에서 妓生이나 音樂家가가 브인 房에 혼자 서서 노래를 부르는 것이 저 놉다랏케 웃둑 소슨 두개의 사닥다리 사이에 가로 걸닌 鐵糸로 올너가서 저 저긔서 四面八方으로 흐터저서 바람을 타고 날너가서 시골은 물논이고 日本 中國에까지 들닌다네.

일업시 오래 구경하여도 공부를 하는데 방해이닛가 그만 어서 나가세.
이제 가서 쉬여서 저녁을 먹고 밤에는 演劇場에를 가 보아야지.

열한시 반에 나와서 敦義洞(明月館本店) 뒤의 內外酒店(色酒家) 구경을 하되 한집에서 오래 먹으면 빈주전자만 들낙날락하다가 술갑을 2,3배 밧을 것이니 한집에서 만어도 2,3巡盃 이상 안이하고 다른 집으로 올마갈 것.

第二日-잇흔날은 食前運動 삼아서 새벽에 일즉 니러나 여섯시 쯤부터 갓가운 學生六거리에 나아가 구경할 것이니 이 골목 저 골목에서 점심 그릇 끼고 나오는 사람마다 인쇄소 철도국 고무공장 煙草회사가튼 곳에 가는 사람들이요 닐곱시가 지나서는 登校時間이라 남녀 학생 專門學生 틈에 유치원 원아까지 끼여서 길이 무여지게 행진하는 것을 볼 것일세. 이것을 보아야지 京城을 보앗다 할 수 잇는 것이 안이겟나.
아츰을 먹고는 北村의 徽文이나 中央高普를 차저 학부형이라 하고 敎授狀況이며 시설을 구경하고 齋洞女子高普를 또 구경하고 그 뒤ㅅ마당에 600년된 白松을 구경하고 나서서 구리개를 건너 東洋拓殖會社를 보아 두고 그 엽흐로 올너가다가 예전 京城府廳 지금의 職業紹介所에 가서 서울의 실직자들의 서른 사정을 듯고 또 보아야 할 것일세. 그래야 정말 서울 구경을 똑똑히 하는 것이 아니겟나. 거긔서 나와서 진고개 마르턱을 구경하지만은 여긔는 電氣燈 켜진 후에 보아야 할 것이닛가 그냥 대강대강 지나서 예전 총독부 터에 科學館을 보고 그리고는 다시 나려와서 구리개를 꾀뚤는 電車로 昌慶苑 구경을 하다가 식물원 압헤서 점심을 먹고 나와서 大學病院은 바라만 보고 電車로 다시 진고개로 건너가서 『신마찌』 구경하고 獎忠壇으로 넘어서서 다리를 쉬여 가지고 城 밧그로 나아가 墓窟에 병풍을 둘너치고 사는 新堂里 살님을 구경하고 光熙門으로 드러와서 京城運動場을 구경하고 東大門과 곱은 城을 보고 나서 여긔서 京城 명물 뻐쓰車를 타고 어엽분 女車掌에게 5錢을 내여주면 몹시 흔들면서 다러나 西大門밧 예전 京畿監營이 든 西大門警察署 압헤서 꺽겨서 慕華館 연주문 獨立門 뒤에까지 가서 『나리십시요』 하느니.... 獨立門 구경하고 西大門監獄을 드려다 보자니 시려도 악박골 가서 라쥼 만흔 藥물을 퍼먹고 등송이 우에 올나서서 監獄施設을 드려다 보고 그리고 천천히 쉬염쉬염 나려 오다가 石다리 엽헤 天然亭 뒤에 盲啞學校를 드러가서 벙어리와 장님 아해들의 공부하는 것을 보고 또 천천히 나려 가면 西小門町까지 채 못가서 煙草專賣局이 잇스니 여긔서 저녁뛰-를 불 때에 밀물가티 몰녀 나오는 男女職工의 노랑꼿 핀 얼골 그리고 新龍山電車로 漢江에 나가서 鐵橋구경 落照구경 그리고 강 건너 멀-니 서 잇는 京城紡績會社 굴둑의 저녁 연기를 보고 江邊飮食店에 드러가서 다리도 수일 겸 장국밥을 먹으면 조흐이. 그리고 밤에는 진고개.... 北村의 안즌 술집.... 엇던가.
