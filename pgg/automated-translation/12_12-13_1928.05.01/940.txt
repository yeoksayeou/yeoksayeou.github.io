The Beauty of Korea Seen Horizontally from Paektu Mountain to Halla Mountain
						돌이

Some speak of Lying-Down Viewing of Kumgang, but I intend to do Sitting-Down Viewing of 3,000 li. However, this is not blind fabrication.

"Splendid Rivers and Mountains, Eastern Peninsula!" "Embroidered Rivers and Mountains, Three Thousand Li!" These are slogans that praise the beauty of Korea's mountains and rivers, regardless of whether they are used by natives or foreigners. In the past, someone from China wished to be born in the Goryeo Kingdom just to see Mount Kumgang once. In the past, someone from the Ming Dynasty said that crossing the Yalu River once felt like entering a fairyland. An American gentleman said that the scenery of Kumgang surpassed that of Switzerland, and a German gentleman praised the beauty of Korea's mountains and rivers as the best in the world.

How magnificent! How can Korea's mountains and rivers be so beautiful and picturesque, becoming the focus of the world's envy? Should we give thanks to the Creator, or should we give thanks to Tangun? Whatever the case, isn't this a blessing for our 20 million brothers and sisters?

I will omit the conclusion and embark on the path of proof. Where should we start? Should we climb Paektu Mountain and look south? Or should we climb Halla Mountain and look north? Let's go in order and look south.

Let's start by talking about the scenery along the post road from Seoul to Paektu Mountain. The paper is short, and the road is long, so we will sometimes have to take a deer's pace.

Pugak (North Mountain) resembles an unopened lotus, while Namsan (South Mountain) resembles a four-year-old silkworm! The Hanggangeul (Han River) glows in unchanging colors for a thousand years, and the sounds of fishermen's songs never cease from morning to night. The pine breeze of Cheongnyangri is bright in all seasons, and the clear stream of Uidong flows without end. Insubong (Pugak's summit) seems to represent the spirit of a Goryeo man, and Dobong Mangwol seems like the Creator's arena. Crossing over the white sand clear stream and passing by strange peaks and bizarre rocks, passing Yangju and Chestnut Village, we arrive at Dongducheon. It is said, "Left is blue mountain, right is green water," and indeed, the left is more beautiful and the right is more unique. After briefly seeing Soyosan (where there is the name Little Kumgang), looking distantly at Bogae Mountain, we pass through the Cheorwon Plain and then the Pyeonggang Plain, ascending to the Seopo Highlands, which are 2,300 feet above sea level. Reeds scatter in the wind, and a grazing horse neighs loudly. <213> Turning left and right through the Sambang special region, we arrive at the truly wonderful site of Seokwangsa Temple, where we feel the best in the world. Clear streams flow amidst lush pine and cypress trees, and crimson skirts flash in Pongnae Gwanseolbong Pavilion. After drinking 4 or 5 measures of medicinal water, listening to 3 or 4 recitations of Buddhist scriptures, buying a load of pine thatch, and taking one step forward and one step back, passing through the Anbyeon Plain, looking out at the Galma Peninsula, we arrive at Wonsan, the first port on the East Coast! Ah! The scenery is unbelievably good in this land of ten thousand ri of mountains and seas! After having a bowl of chicken soup (Baekseuk Tang 1 器) and briefly visiting Myeongsa Ten-ri, greeting the Haeundae Beach, briefly passing through Muncheon Highland, and briefly stopping at Yeongheung Junwon Hall, it seems worthy of being called a royal family relic. The Hamheung Grand Plain is so vast that even a mountain village bumpkin would not be able to distinguish east from west, and the Seongcheon River Mansero Bridge is considerably long. After resting at Pangnyong Mountain, looking out to Seaho, the large and small scenery, including Egg Island, Dung Island, Brother Island, and Couple Island, is truly the most beautiful scenery in the world. After seeing Songdo and Cheondo in Hongwon, passing the fish markets of Sinpo and Sinchang, and going up along the Namdae Stream, ascending to Huchiryong Pass, it is incomparably magnificent. After taking a rest and letting out a shout, coming down to the first village, Pungsan, passing over Eungdeongnyong Pass, crossing the Heosan River, passing Kapsan, and arriving at Hyesanjin, the Yalu River, which marks the national border, flows leisurely. Watching the border crossing, taking a shortcut, and climbing Paektu Ancestral Mountain, it is truly a famous mountain that cannot be described in words.

Looking down at Cheonji Lake and meditating in silence for 1 hour, climbing to Sangsang Peak and looking out over 3,000 ri, each strip is silk and each piece is gold. The green mountains run horizontally and the green waters run vertically, with kites flying in the mountains and fish leaping in the water. The sound of Gyeongbangga (farming songs) rises above the fertile land, and red lips and rosy cheeks wander amidst the green pines and bamboos. It is truly a paradise on earth, a living painting. The dense forests like fields of rice around the sacred mountain are the world's leading timber production area, and the abundant heavenly water of the Great Lake marks the border with the Amnok and Tuman Rivers, the two great rivers of the east and west. Watching the free enjoyment of rare birds and animals in the dense forest, carrying the thousand-mile wind on our backs, passing through Musan, arriving in Hoeryeong, and again taking the Tuman River, passing Jongseong and Onseong, stopping at Yongdang in Gyeongwon to reminisce about Yi Seonggye of that time, going around the red pond of Gyeongheung, and arriving at Unggi Port, our hearts open wide to the ten thousand li of ocean waves. Circling around the Cheongjin Strait, climbing to Wonsudae in Gyeongseong to commemorate General Yun Gwan, visiting Jueul Byeolcheonji and enjoying three hot springs, then leaping over to Danbaram Myeongcheon Chilbosan, it is truly Kumgang of Gwanbuk and the pride of Myeongcheon. After resting on Sakka Peak, passing through Kilju and arriving in Seongjin, the waves surge and the pine breeze rustles. Truly, the scenery is indescribable everywhere. After seeing the jade stones of Seocheon, seeing Haksudae of Liwon and the 10-ri Songjeong, passing Jaho Red Cliff, leaping over Sinheung, and arriving in Jangjin, the reclusive and picturesque taste does not fall short of the seaside. How delightful! How magnificent! The Hamgyeong frontier ends here! Let's also go over Pyeongan Province.

Following the Jangjin River, seeing the white snow of Heohangnyeong Pass, heading towards Sinheung Pass, and again taking the Amnok River, passing through the thick forest area of Huchang, resting at Junggangjin, having a cup at Jaseonggang Estuary, crossing Matanryeong Pass, and climbing to Ganggye Inpung Pavilion, our pure interest is provoked by the wind of the Dookru River. After sharing a drink with a Ganggye beauty, stopping at Wiwon and buying one inkstone, having a cup at Chosan Yeonghojeong, hunting once at the cold springs of Byeokdong, passing Changseong <214> enjoying a hot spring in Sakju, and leaping up to Uiju Tonggunjeong, it is incomparably magnificent. Is there such scenery anywhere else besides Korea? Placing the Janggang Daeya (long river large field) in front of us, singing and reciting poetry, and then coming down to Sinuiju, seeing the Gaepye Bridge once, taking the iron horse (train) and heading towards Baekma River, again taking a boat and arriving at Yongampo, the dews are fully loaded in the hundred-ri fertile field. Passing Cheolsan, climbing to Gyeomsan and looking down on Seoncheon-eup, stopping at Jeongju to mourn General Hong Gyeongrae, following the Sakcheon and climbing to Guseong, seeing Cheonmasan once, following the Uncheon and coming down to Taechon to see Samgaksan and Hyangjeoksan, crossing Baekbisan and going to Unsan to see the gold mines and hot springs of Bukjin, then going to Nyeongbyeon to have a cup at Yaksan Dongdae, leaping over to Myohyangsan to see Pohyonsa Temple, Sangwon Hermitage, and Tangun Cave, leaping over to Anju Baekseongnu, the Cheongcheon River tells the story of the 2,000-year-old battlefield. Briefly going to Bakcheon, crossing over to see the scenery of Daenyeong River Wonsu Peak, borrowing the back of the iron horse (train) and arriving directly in Pyongyang, it is a separate sight among the scenery. Nyeongwangjeong Cheongnyubyeok, Daedong River Ryangnado, Moranbong Bubyeoknu, Eulmilda Gijarim, and ordinary Daedong, truly a park of the world. Even the creation of the gods seems to imitate it! Entering Suncheon to see the fishing on the Suncheon River, buying one Gangna hemp cloth, going to Deokcheon to see Myeongse, and then passing Maengsan and Yangdeok, arriving in Seongcheon to see Wusan Twelve Peaks and Gangseollu, buying a handful of Seongcheon grass and going to Gangdong, the Gangdong landscape is also plausible. From here, leaping westward, having a drink of Gangseo medicinal water, going to Nampo, eating ten or so apples and seeing Samhwa Park Bibeoldo, leaping over to *Ipo to see the steel mill, going to Hwangju, the scenery of Cheokbyeokgang Wolpanu is also plausible. After seeing Jeongbangsan, passing Sariwon, seeing Jangsu Mountain, passing Jaeryeong Yeomulpyeong, enjoying a hot spring in Sincheon, climbing Guyueolsan to meditate silently at Tangun's ancestral altar, passing Eunryul and arriving in Jangyeon, the blue sea in front and the green mountains behind, the traces of the ancient volcano are also famous. Enjoying a hot spring in Ongjin, resting at Haeju Fuyongdang, seeing Suyangsan, buying rice cakes in Yeonbaek, passing Geumcheon, enjoying a hot spring in Pyeongsan, going to Singye Goksan, crossing Taeryeong Pass, passing Janggok, and going down to the Eight Views of Gwandong. Korea is the park of the world, and Gwandong is the park of Korea. Such scenery does not exist in China or Switzerland. It is difficult to draw with a pen and difficult to describe with words, so what should we do? Let's just list the well-known scenic spots.

Seeing Chokseokjeong Pavilion in Tongcheon, the representative scenic spot of the East Coast, visiting Kumgang Mountain, seeing the 12,000 peaks of Inner and Outer Kumgang, Sea Kumgang, and New Kumgang for 300 days before and after, and then seeing Samilpo in Goseong, Cheongganjeong Pavilion in Ganseong, Naksansa Temple in Yangyang, arriving in Gangneung to eat a lot of persimmons, seeing Gyeongpodae, climbing Odaesan Mountain, and then coming down to Samcheok again to see Jukseoru Pavilion, seeing Mangyangjeong Pavilion in Uljin and Wolsongjeong Pavilion in Pyeonghae, climbing to Jeongseon, passing Yeongwol and Pyeongchang, seeing the scenery of Wonju, passing Hongcheon and Hoengseong, arriving in Chuncheon, the Soryang River color is also good, and the Choyang Pavilion is also plausible. From here, leaping once, passing Gapyeong and Yangpyeong in Gyeonggi, buying wild vegetable wraps from Yongmunsan Mountain, resting at Namhansanseong Fortress in Gwangju, passing Yeoju, having a snack with self-produced rice in Icheon, and again leaping to Chungju, mourning General Sin Rip at Tangeumdae, going to Goesan <215> to mourn Song Siyeol at Hwayangdong, and without paying respects, going to Boeun to see the scenery of Songnisan Mountain, leaping over Joryeong Pass, seeing Mungyeong and Sangju, passing Yecheon, going to Andong to see the Hahoe Ryu Clan Village on the Nakdong River bank, going north to Yeongju Bonghwa to see Sotaebaek and Daetaebaek, going south again to pass Yeongyang and Yeongdeok, seeing the customs of Uiseong, going along the Wi Stream to Gunwi, coming out to Yeongcheon to see the color of Geumho River, and arriving in Gyeongju, it is truly the representative scenic spot of South Korea and the only concentration of ancient sites in Korea. Gumi Mountain, Geumo Mountain, Banwolseong Fortress, Anapji Pond, Cheomseongdae Observatory, Dabotap Pagoda, Bulguksa Temple, Seokguram Grotto, it is truly difficult to enumerate the ancient sites and scenic spots. After seeing all of this, passing Ulsan Bangojin, commemorating Choi Su-un at Cheonseongsan Mountain in Yangsan, staying overnight at Tongdosa Temple, a large temple in Samnam, passing Dongnae Hot Spring to see the scenery of Beomeosa Temple, passing Haeundae Beach, seeing Busan, the gateway of Korea, and dashing to Gimhae to see Suro Royal Tomb, going down to Masan to feel the wind on Guyeon Mountain, taking a glimpse of Jinhae's scenery, passing Goseong and going to Tongyeong, you will be able to see Admiral Yi Sun-sin's Hansando Island. After reminiscing about the events of 300 years ago, looking at bamboo furniture and mother-of-pearl chests, going to Jinju, the Nam River scenery is also good, but the Chokseokru scenery is even better. Mourning Nongae, passing Danseong Red Cliff, going to Sancheong to see the color of Gyeongho River again. From here, passing Hadong, seeing the green pines and bamboos of Jirisan Mountain and the remote ancient temples, going to Haman and arriving in Hapcheon, the scenery of Haeinsa Temple is also magnificent. After seeing the Tripitaka Koreana, passing Changnyeong to the north and arriving in Daegu, you will see the quiet and simple Dalseong Park. Going to Dongjangdae to mourn the magnificent death of Choi Su-un, going up to Gimcheon, passing Taejeon, enjoying a hot spring in Yuseong once, stopping at Gyeryongsan New Capital City, smiling at the false emperor's palace, passing Donggyesa Temple and Gapsa Temple, going to Gongju to wash your heart in the Geum River and reminisce about the Donghak Rebellion, then going to Buyeo to see the Eight Views, including the Baengma River and Nakwaam Rock, reminiscing about the ancient events of Baekje, arriving in Ganggyeong, seeing the large stone Buddha of Eun Jin Gwanchoksa Temple, crossing the Ganggyeong Plain, arriving in Iri, briefly seeing the grain distribution center of Gunsan, and then going to Jeonju again to see Dagajeong Pond and Mangyeongdae, then crossing the vast plains of Gimje and Man-gyeong, arriving at Naejangsan Mountain in Jeongeup, this is also a separate landscape of Honam. After passing Jangseong, having a snack once on a Naju small table, climbing Mudeungsan Mountain in Gwangju, it feels the same as climbing Huchijibong in the north. Carrying a large bamboo stick, passing the Gwangju Plain, crossing the Yeongsan River and going down to Mokpo, our hearts open wide to the ten thousand ri of ocean waves. Climbing Mokpo Table, seeing the Dadohae to your heart's content, taking a small boat and crossing over to Jeju Island, climbing Hallasan Mountain, and you will truly become a celestial being on earth. Looking up at the three thousand ri of embroidered rivers and mountains and looking out at the ten thousand ri of blue sea is a great joy that cannot be found again. Seeing silver scales and jade rulers in Baekrokdam Lake, coming down and taking a fine horse, entering Jeju City, the female divers on the coast are envious like celestial officials. After visiting the orange grove and visiting the Samseong Cave, again taking a small boat and crossing over to Haenam, passing Heungheung and Boseong, arriving in Suncheon, going along the Seomjin River to Gurye, climbing to Namwon to think about Chunhyang and Yi Doryeong along with Gwanghallu and Ojokgyo Bridge, stopping at Imsil, leaping far to Mujyu and Geumsan, going straight to Seoul, seeing the scenery of Seaho in Suwon, seeing the Dragon Jewel Temple Forest, then coming back to see Gwanyak Sammoksa Temple and Yeonjudae, and then falling down to Noryangjin, inviting 2 or 3 acquaintances for a cup of wine on a small boat, going down the river to Yuldo and Yanghwado, reciting one poem with one cup of wine, and the eight provinces are truly contained in your stomach.

How refreshing is it? How beautiful are the rivers and mountains of Korea?
