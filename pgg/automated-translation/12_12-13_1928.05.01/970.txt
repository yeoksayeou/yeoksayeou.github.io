발행일	1928년 05월 01일

기사형태	잡저 (Miscellaneous Essay)

Great Debate at the World's Fair
Location: On the pages of Byeol Geon-gon Magazine
Date: April
Participants: Trouble-makers from various countries

It was rumored that Byeol Geon-gon Magazine would publish a special issue praising Joseon, and the ears and eyes of people from all over the world were immediately drawn to it. So-called trouble-makers, who always had something to say, gathered and unanimously declared, "Joseon's pride! What pride does Joseon have? Especially in the current state of Joseon—whatever pride they claim, we'll make sure it shrinks away like a turtle's head!" They tore, flailed, and bickered so fiercely that even someone with a mouth as big as a megaphone wouldn't be able to utter a word, let alone boast. However, the famous Gwan-sang-ja, known as the Chief of Complaints for Joseon, fearlessly launched into a rebuttal with his eloquent tongue, defeating the trouble-makers from various countries, just as Zhuge Liang (諸葛孔明) triumphed over the Confucian scholars in Gangdong with his verbal prowess. What exactly was this debate like? Listening closely, it was truly amusing and refreshing, with a captivating interest. I will briefly record it below. (A Reporter)

(China) Pride! Pride! What is there to be proud of in Joseon? Not only is there nothing to be proud of in the current state of Joseon, but even in the past, Joseon was under the rule of our great nation in terms of politics and culture, and at best, it was only praised as a "Little China." Although the mountains and rivers are beautiful, the fields are not a hundred li wide, and the rivers are not a thousand li long, making it just a small country. They claim to be a nation of etiquette, but they only value empty formalities in ceremonies such as weddings and funerals, spending several thousands of won on a single wedding or funeral, selling land and houses. The general public lacks economic sense, spending 10 jeon and drinking 20 or 30 jeon worth of alcohol, filling the taverns day and night, and drunken people staggering on the streets. They only wear white clothes and use foreign fabrics, yet they constantly complain about poverty. What is the reason for all of this? And yet they talk about pride, which is truly laughable.

(Gwan) You can't even wipe your spit properly, and what are you saying with that foul-smelling mouth? The rule of a great nation! What is this so-called great nation? Just because the ignorant people of Joseon call you people of a great nation, do you think you are truly a great nation? In reality, it is not a great nation but a "dwae" nation, that is, the country of the "dwaenom" (되놈), in other words, a Hu (胡) country. That's why you call your rice cake "Hu" rice cake, your cabbage "Hu" cabbage, your beans "Hu" beans, and your horses "Hu" horses, isn't it? It is true that Joseon imported some culture from China in the past, and there were times in history when we were political allies, but it cannot be said that we were ever completely ruled by you. Calling another country "Little China" comes from your arrogance, doesn't it? You also received culture from India, Persia, and other foreign countries. During the Song Dynasty, Emperor Hui and Emperor Qin were captured by the Jin, and during the Yuan and Qing Dynasties, the Han people were completely conquered by foreign tribes. Moreover, the million-strong army of the Sui Dynasty was annihilated by the people of Joseon, leading to the fall of the Sui Dynasty. Tang Taizong even lost an eye at the hands of a Joseon person. If we're going to talk about the past, it's all the same, so what's the point of bringing up bad things? I can't boast about Joseon people being good at drinking, but China, which is being ruined by opium throughout the country, has no right to speak, no matter how big your mouth is. Lavish spending on weddings and funerals certainly needs improvement, but how does that compare to your custom of receiving thousands of won for a daughter and engaging in arranged marriages, and when someone dies, arbitrarily abandoning them in fields or temples for crows and dogs to devour? Wearing white clothes may be uneconomical, but there's a reason for it. In China, where there is so much dust and the city streets are so filthy, you wouldn't be able to wear white clothes for even a day, but Joseon has no dust, and the city streets, mountains, and air are all clean, so it's not as easy to get dirty as foreigners think. Just as a small diamond is a priceless treasure, even though Joseon is small, with fields not a hundred li wide and rivers not a thousand li long, the mountains are high, the water is beautiful, and the natural scenery is excellent, and the natural resources are abundant, so there is nothing to envy from other countries. Since ancient times, Chinese people have wished to be born in Goryeo and to see Geumgang Mountain once, and even now, without Joseon ginseng, you cannot maintain your health. If I say too much, it's easy to hurt feelings, so I'll stop here.

(America) Ah! Mr. Joseon Representative—listening to your words, it seems that the Chinese person has nothing to say. However, I am very disappointed to hear you speak so lowly of the Chinese person. I don't know if you will do the same to us, so from now on, please use honorifics, no matter how excited you get. I don't know much about Joseon's history or circumstances, so I can only speak from what I have seen and heard. Excuse my rudeness, but Joseon people's houses are so low and small that they look like pigsties or demon houses at first glance. Years ago, when our American tourists saw the houses of Joseon people along the road from the train, they said that livestock farming must be quite developed in Joseon. And Joseon people abuse animals. They load so much luggage on cows and horses, and even ride on them while beating them. They often catch and eat the dogs they raise at home, and there is so much discrimination between men and women that women are confined to the inner rooms day and night and have no rights. How can you boast about being a civilized nation with such practices?

(Gwan) Yes, what's so difficult about using honorifics? We are originally people who advocate using honorifics even with children, so that won't be difficult. However, you Westerners only value material civilization too much, so I can't talk with you. How can I even reconsider the idea that a house being low means it's not a civilized nation? You call our houses pigsties, but to us, your houses look like bird nests. After all, the fundamental purpose of a house is to provide a safe and comfortable place for our bodies to live, but you only think that higher is better, building 3-story, 5-story, or even dozens of stories high, making them easily toppled by earthquakes, easily struck by lightning during thunder (lightning rods are a separate issue), and difficult to evacuate during storms and fires, creating various dangers and forgetting the fundamental purpose. Didn't you see that during the great tornado in America years ago, the people living on the top floors suffered the most, and that you built underground rooms when you thought a comet was going to fall? Moreover, the houses of reasonably well-off people in Joseon are not inferior to those who boast about being civilized. To speak extremely as a joke, no matter how many hundreds of stories you build in America in the Western Hemisphere, you are still living beneath our feet in the Eastern Hemisphere. (Laughs) Animal abuse! Animal abuse! But Joseon people never abuse animals. From ancient times, Joseon has held the idea that humans and animals are equal (人馬亦同), and the country even held ancestral rites for horses at the Ministry of War (太개, 司僕), and in rural farmhouses, individuals would even give special treats like rice cakes to cows and horses when they had them. On the day of Ipchun (立春), they would paste celebratory poems on the stables, saying, "The ox plows a hundred acres of fields, the horse travels a thousand li, its horns are abundant, its ears are moist." In the past, Prime Minister Meng Si-cheng (孟思誠) even forbade discussing the merits of oxen in front of them, fearing it would hurt their feelings. In reality, you are the ones who abuse animals. Just take cows as an example—before you slaughter them, you keep them alive and milk them, and after you kill them, you eat not only the meat but also the bones, fat, and even the tail, claiming to "utilize resources for the benefit of the people" (所謂 利用厚生). There's a limit to kindness—how can you put dogs in the same room with you, sleep and eat with them, and even let women fall in love and die for them? What distinction would there be between humans and animals if you did that? It may seem like we oppress women, but in reality, it is not oppression. They may not have political freedom, but housewives are the heads of their households. In the past, there were even several queens during the Silla Dynasty, so they were equal in politics as well. Joseon women may not go out as much, but there is no pressure on their bodies, while Western women go out more but bind their waists tightly, causing physical pressure. Chinese women's foot binding, Indian women's head covering, and Western women's corsets may differ in method, but they all apply physical pressure. Didn't the West even use chastity belts on women at one point? No matter how high your nose is or how blue your eyes are, my words will make your nose flat and your eyes sunken.

(England) No-no- What pride does Joseon have? Is your navy as developed as ours in England? Do you have as much territory? Is your constitution as developed as ours? Without these nationally prideful things, what value is there in boasting?

(Gwan) Ah—this is truly the first time I've heard such words. Other countries' people only talked about things like clothes, houses, and etiquette, but you have raised substantial issues like the navy, territory, and constitution. You are indeed qualified to be a citizen of Great Britain. However, just as a frog in a well doesn't know the wide world outside the well, and a worm in a cave doesn't know the bright sun and moon outside the cave, you only know about the development of the British navy and don't know how developed the Joseon navy was. In the past, the Joseon navy was not only the most developed in the Orient, but ironclad ships were also invented earlier than in any other country in the world. You should look at the naval records of your country and find out before I explain it. Of course, the British navy is currently the strongest in the world, and you have many warships, but if the worldwide military reduction that everyone is talking about begins to be thoroughly implemented, they will all become useless objects. Having many warships is no better than having many bluefish or croaker fishing boats in Joseon. And England often boasts that it has so much territory that there is never a day without sunshine, but that is no different from a thief boasting about stolen goods. As soon as the owner comes to claim it, it's over, isn't it? England boasts that it never has sunset now, but in the future, England may never see the sun and lament. Why—because, as the world knows, the mainland of England is covered in thick fog and cannot see the sun well, so even if you have no territory, you will not be able to see the sun first. Next, the constitution! You may say it's the pride of the world, but it's also true that Joseon had an unwritten constitution like England's since ancient times. Also, isn't it ridiculous to boast about something that doesn't suit the general public now? (The British representative's nose twitched)

(Germany) Since everyone has already spoken, I don't have much to say, but in my opinion, Joseon people are too peaceful and dislike fighting with others, which is why they are always invaded by those with strong military power. They have little creativity and little research in science, and therefore seem to lack great scholars and great philosophers. If a nation is always in a weak position and has no unique culture, how can it have anything to be proud of?

(Gwan) Yes—people who don't know may think so, but Joseon is definitely not like that. You don't know Joseon's history, but there is no one who loves peace and is good at fighting like the Joseon people. We certainly do not invade other countries' territories with our own desires and destroy the peace of mankind, but when an enemy invades us, we never surrender and fight to the end to achieve victory. History proves that even if I don't explain it. You will know it by looking at this special issue. There are also no people with as much creativity as the Joseon people. You will be able to guess by seeing that we invented movable type, ironclad ships, and other things before others, and that we created the beautiful Hangeul in a unique way. We also have quite a few scholars and philosophers. People in recent times worship Marx of Germany and sing his doctrines, but in modern Joseon, there is Choe Su-un (崔水雲), who is greater than Marx, and the Innaecheon (人乃天) doctrine, which is greater than his doctrine. If this doctrine is well-proclaimed, Joseon will truly have more to be proud of in the world. See next issue for how it goes

(France) -Speaking in a light tone, stroking his pointed beard- I, a Frenchman with a quick temper, will say something. What is Joseon, after all? Where in the world is there a nation as impoverished and filthy as Joseon? Our Paris has streets as clean as if they were paved with glass, but the streets of Seoul in Joseon are scattered with feces in every alley, as if they were paved with gold. Even when it comes to infectious diseases, it is said to be the world's number one in statistics. Do you have a grand theater? Do you have a large racecourse? Do you have a large park? Do you have a large library? It seems that you have none of the so-called facilities of a civilized nation. I don't know what Joseon people live for. And yet, they only have small offspring like tadpoles, so how can poor people live? It is truly disheartening to talk about pride or anything else. How can you watch the moon in the dark night, and how can you smell the fragrance of flowers in the vast desert? Oh, stop it.

(Gwan) Hush—be careful what you say— Even if this is a place to argue openly, a big commotion will arise if you recklessly criticize Joseon. Although the Daewongun (大院君) is long gone, there is a second Daewongun in Joseon, and if he were to hear your words, he might slap both of your cheeks as the Ganghwa Island (江華島) and Ganggye (江界) riflemen once shot down the French warship, so be especially careful. Paris is indeed a splendid capital called the City of Flowers (世界花都), but if you inspect the toilets of higher and middle schools, you'll find many drawings of orchids on the walls. They weren't drawn with artistry but with the students' filth, drawn with their fingers instead of toilet paper. In that case, it's no problem to have a little bit of feces in the back alleys. It may be good to have various facilities completed and splendid, but what will be the result of extreme extravagance in various aspects? You criticize Joseon people for having many children, but can you say that the annual decrease in the population of France is a good sign for the nation? What kind of sign is it? Even though there are many infectious diseases in Joseon, Joseon people have healthy bodies, so they don't consider ordinary illnesses a problem. If this is also something to be proud of, it is. The only things we are inferior to you in are that we are not as cruel as you, not as cunning as you, and not as stingy with money as you. Oh, I almost forgot. As for the climate, it doesn't rain in the winter like it does in France. In France, it rains 27 or 28 days a month in the winter, making it impossible to live. That is also something we are inferior to you in. Haha...

萬國品評會 大爭論記
場所, 別乾坤誌上
時日, 4월  일
人物, 各國의 말성쟁이

이번 別乾坤誌에서 朝鮮자랑號를 발행한다는 소문이 한번 나자 世界 各國人의 耳目이 一時 聳動하야 소위 말마듸나 한다는 前後의 말성쟁이들은 모도 모혀들어 異口同音으로「朝鮮의 자랑! 朝鮮에 무슨 자랑이 잇서 況且 只今 형편의 朝鮮에- 아모 자랑을 하던지 그 놈의 자랑이 아주 자라의 모가지 모양으로 쑥 드러가게 맹길리라 하고 제 각기 찟코 까불고 막 뜨드러대서 좀체 사람가트면 제 아모리 입이 라팔가티 큰 입이라도 자랑은커녕 말의 댓꾸도 잘 못할 디경에 이르럿다. 그러나 朝鮮의 말성 都監으로 유명한 觀相者은 조곰도 忌憚함이 업시 長廣의 舌로 一場論駁을 하야 맛치 諸葛孔明이 江東에서 群儒를 舌戰勝利하드시 各國의 말성쟁이들을 다처서 익여버렷다. 그 논쟁이 과연 엇더하엿던가. 자서이 들어보면 참으로 우습기도 하고 통쾌하기도 하야 흥미가 자못 津津하다. 이 아래에 잠깐 기록하야 보겟다. (一記者)

(觀)  타-마나 거비-니도 잘 닥지 안코 냄새나는 입에 무슨 말이 그리 만어- 大國 支配! 소위 大國이라는 것이 다 무엇이냐. 朝鮮의 무식한 사람들이 너를 보고 대국 사람이라 하닛가 진짬 대국인줄로 안단 말이냐. 실상은 대국이 안이라 되국 즉 되놈의 나라- 다시 말하면 胡國이라는 말이다. 그리하야 너희들의 먹는 떡을 胡ㅅ떡, 배추를 胡빼추, 콩을 胡콩, 말을 胡말, 이라 하지 안느냐. 過去 朝鮮에서 中國의 文化를 다소 수입한 것도 사실이요 歷史上 或時 政治的 同盟國이 된 때도 잇섯지만은 전혀 지배를 밧엇다고 할 수는 업다. 남의 나라를 小中華라 하는 것은 너의 自尊自大에서 나온 것이 안이냐, 느도 印度나 派斯, 기타 外國의 文化를 밧기도 하고 宋시대에는 徽, 欽 二帝가 金에 잡히여 가기도 하고 元淸 兩國時代에는 漢族이 전혀 異族에게 征服을 당하얏슬 뿐만 안이라 隋의 백만 대군도 朝鮮 사람에게 전멸되야야 그로 인해 隋가 망하기까지 하고 唐太宗은 朝鮮 사람의 손에 눈이 또한 멀엇다. 과거의 그런 것을 말하자면 彼此 一般이지＜220＞ 무슨 凶 볼 것 잇스냐. 朝鮮 사람의 술 잘먹는 것을 자랑할 수는 업다만은 全國이 阿片으로 망하는 中國으로서는 입이 왜 라팔가티 크더라도 할 말이 업슬 것이다. 婚喪에 람비하는 것은 물론 개량할 점이나 딸 한아에 몃 1000圓식 밧고 賣買婚姻을 하고 사람이 죽으면 들에나 절(寺)에다 함부로 내버려 가마귀 뼈와 개떼가 막 뜨더 먹게하는 느의 風俗과 比較하면 엇더하냐. 흰옷도 불경제는 불경제지만은 그것도 까닭이 잇다. 中國처럼 먼지가 만코, 시가가 불결한 곳에는 白衣를 입고는 하루도 못 견듸겟지만은 朝鮮은 먼지도 업고 市街 山川 空氣 등이 다 깨끗하야 외국 사람이 생각하는 것처럊� 그다지 쉽게 드럽지는 안는다. 金剛石이 아모리 적다 하여도 천하의 至寶인 것과 가티 朝鮮이 비록 偏小하야 野無百里하고 水無千里하나 山高水麗하야 天然의 景致가 조흔 외에 天産物이 또한 풍부하야 아모 것도 남의 나라에 부러울 것이 업다. 中國 사람들이 녯날부터 願生高麗國하야 一見 金剛山이라 하고 지금에도 朝鮮의 人蔘이 안이면은 느의 건강을 保全치 못할 것이다. 여러 말을 하면 감정을 상하기 쉬울 터이니 이만하고 그만두자.
