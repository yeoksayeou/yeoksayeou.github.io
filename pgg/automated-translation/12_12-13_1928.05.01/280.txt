기사형태	논설 (Essay)

Things of Joseon I am Proud Of - The Daedong Yeojido Map of Gosanja, Single-Handedly Surveyed and Independently Created 70 Years Ago
						Yukdang Hak-in

If we seek the pride of Joseon in academic achievements, nothing is more brilliant than Gosanja's Daedong Yeojido. Few people know about the Daedong Yeojido, and even fewer know about Gosanja. If Gosanja is asked who he is, almost no one will know, so it may seem that this is not a big deal, but in fact, Gosanja's Daedong Yeojido has a very small exposure, and its real value is very sacred. It stands out more than other practical works and academic inventions in Joseon.

Joseon, especially modern Joseon's ideology, is characterized by being non-developmental, non-utilitarian, and non-practical scientific. Everything other than the method of being a *yangban* (兩班), the materials for the civil service examination (科擧), was regarded as insignificant, lowly, despised, and even hated, so things disappeared and those that were born were criticized. There was no way that the study of nomenclature and mathematics (名物度數之學) other than meaningless rhetoric, which was not considered a profession of scholars or the conduct of *yangban*, could arise or flourish. There was no reason for talented people to open their eyes here, and there could be no stimulus to agonize, pour blood, waste energy, or worry about such things. But Gosanja's Daedong Yeojido was born in this world. It was made by a man of practical talent and firm will, who would not fail to achieve anything, with a great sincerity that would amaze even gods and ghosts. Moreover, putting aside praise and rewards, he endured all kinds of ridicule, reprimands, and even oppression and harm, while suffering from lonely indignation and solitary worries, so it is especially rare, precious, and admirable.

Even if this Daedong Yeojido is a playful thing that is socially and culturally dispensable, if there is a great and sacred value in the brilliance of one person's life and the construction of one person's character expressed through it, then its subjective sacredness will remain intact. Also, no matter how good the subject is, if the skill is insufficient and the result is poor, no matter how hard the author has worked, the objective real value will not increase because of it. However, the Yeojido is a primary necessity (第一義的 必要物) that greatly benefits the country and the people and, on a smaller scale, promotes knowledge and benefits. Gosanja's Daedong Yeojido is a groundbreaking and great work that surpasses and excels in every aspect since the existence of the Jikbangdo in Joseon. Compared to similar products in other countries of the same era, it is a prominent work that has its own strengths and is not inferior. It encompasses all the essence of the past and guides all the paths of the future. It is the foundation of all maps in Joseon, being the collection of old maps and the origin of new maps. It is a product of experimentation and actual measurement based on the highest intelligence in Joseon at the time, and it is a kind of artistic work that combines scientific precision and moral admiration. It is a pillar that represents the culture of the era and a pagoda that shows the conscience of Joseon. How can we not admire Gosanja's Daedong Yeojido in every sense? Daedong Yeojido and its author, Gosanja, are truly one of the greatest achievements and saints of Joseon. No matter who argues, I will say so.

What is the Daedong Yeojido like? I do not have the leisure to give a detailed explanation and cartographic criticism of it here, but I will first briefly describe its contents. The entire area of Joseon is drawn, from Onseong in the north to Jeju in the south, divided into 22 layers, so that when they are connected, the entire map can be seen at a glance, and when they are separated, only the necessary parts can be viewed separately. Each layer is folded into a square book shape, so that when folded, it becomes a scroll, and when unfolded, it becomes a panel, so that all convenience is sought in combining and dividing areas, and in searching and carrying. In short, it is the most practical new idea that makes it possible to see and use the entire map of Joseon in both large and small sizes. This is the format that is now seen in maps of all countries, and even the detailed topographic maps of 1/200,000, 1/50,000, and 1/25,000 scale made by the Land Survey Bureau in Joseon all use this freely divisible format. However, it is a matter of course that this was an unprecedented new idea at the time. The Daedong Yeojido came out of this original format.

The length of each layer is about one *cheok* (尺, approx. 30cm), and the width varies depending on the shape of the territory. In places like the northern part of Hamgyeongbuk-do in the first layer, it is only about one *cheok*, and in wider places, it extends to 8 or 9 *cheok*. This is folded into a width of about 6.5 *bun* (分, approx. 2cm) and stacked, so that one side of the sectional map of about 1 *cheok* and 3 *cun* (寸, approx. 3cm) is visible. The entire map contains about 120 such map sheets. The length alone covers 4 *pyeong* (坪, approx. 3.3 square meters) when combined, and the width, including both empty and filled parts, exceeds 2 *pyeong*. If this were to be made into a single full map, it would form a large map covering more than 10 *pyeong*, including the gap between the southern coast of the peninsula and Jeju Island.

As for the graphic representation of the region, he strived to accurately represent the location, distance, topography, and facilities with thorough intention and careful design. Originally, Joseon had a long history of map development and remarkable progress, and had long known the correct shape of the entire territory. Now, when the Daedong Yeojido is connected and viewed, it will be discovered that the shape of the outline is even closer to the true shape. Therefore, without precise instruments, one will know how much great effort is hidden behind the paper. <62>

The author first made squares of 10 *li* (里, approx. 3.9km) per side on a half-panel of 1 *cheok* long and 6.5 *cun* wide, indicating that the vertical side is 12 squares, or 120 *li*, the horizontal side is 8 squares, or 80 *li*, and the diagonal is 14 *li*. He then recorded the terrain he wanted to represent on this baseline. It may be difficult to match the results of detailed surveys today, but the effort to achieve accuracy in the overall picture is sufficiently evident on the map.
Since a map is inherently a symbolic representation of terrain, it can be said that the skill and appropriateness of the symbols are a major key that determines the life of the map. Therefore, when comparing modern maps with ancient ones, one can find a great difference, especially in the density and subtlety of the symbols used. The development or evolution of mapmaking <63> is nothing more than the degree of technological progress that allows one to accurately and quickly identify geographical phenomena depicted on the map, as well as related texts and symbols. The author of the Daedong Yeojido also had a remarkable design in this regard, breaking away from the old format and devising excellent symbols that exuded novelty, achieving the success of the map.

First, Joseon is a mountainous country. It can be said that the geographical features of Joseon are almost entirely mountains, and everything else is merely an accompanying landscape. There is no part of the map where the representation of dense mountains is absent. Therefore, the appropriateness of the mountain symbol has a critical influence that determines the entire life of the Joseon map. The author keenly recognized this point. As a result, he obtained a good expression technique to create a map with vibrant energy. In traditional maps of the East, it is common to use a collection or chain of ∧ symbols to represent mountains. This may be sufficient for representing isolated mountains or sparse mountain ranges, but in a mountainous country like Joseon, with its spiderweb-like topography, the ∧ symbol chain would make the map look static, making it impossible to see clarity and vitality. Therefore, the author newly represented mountain ranges with the continuous arrangement of quasi-shaped black lines, adding thickness to the lines, varying the height of the angles, and representing the size of the bodies. He also subtly and smoothly indicated the branching and winding of the roots, trunks, and branches, so that the entire map has a sculptural impression, as well as dignity, depth, and dynamism, serving as both a drawing and a model, and exhibiting both a bird's-eye view and a realistic value. At first glance, this may seem like a slight transformation of the old style, but in terms of its real value, it is a groundbreaking success in bringing the map to life. In particular, one cannot help but feel the indescribable quaint personal movement and the artistic scent that subtly purifies the soul in the clumsy and dullness.

Try hanging the Daedong Yeojido on the wall and savor the meaning of the mountain expression throughout the entire map. The veins and bones of Great Joseon, the muscular bulges, the eruption of the round and abundant primordial energy, the undulating spirit of the vast ocean—the mountains are represented both morphologically and spiritually, containing the ultimate mystery in the simplest form! The more you look at it, the more you cannot help but admire it. The Baekdusan massif, like a peony bud, became the root, and the thousands of leaves and mountain ranges that developed intricately and profoundly throughout the three thousand *li* peninsula! The symbol that simply, directly, vividly, and decisively expressed it! Unimagined wings of imagination spontaneously spread, and the taste of poetry that is not chewed spontaneously emerges, and it is impossible to stop the lines from forcibly pulling us into the land of fairy tales and the land of art. The peninsula is the divine zither that Hanul (Heaven) loves, and countless strings are stretched out to play all the mysteries with Baekdusan as the axis. Isn't the mysterious tone of the Great "Bright" (밝) that sometimes sounds alone and sometimes resonates together always emanating from there? And isn't Gosanja's brush <64> now serving as the amplifier there? Look, Baekdusan has become Myobaeklyeon, and all the 3 parts, 13 courtyards, 414 honored, and countless great families of the entire Garbhakosa Mandala are manifested as mountains on the peninsula, and isn't Gosanja's hand stitching an incredible great mandala there? The divine tiger called Joseon, with Baekdusan as its head, is lying majestically in the center of the world, and how skillfully has Gosanja's *pil-dammyo* (筆淡描, light sketching with a brush) vividly captured and presented that brilliant and obvious pattern on that one panel? Not only that, but with Baekdusan as the foundation, the sacred tree of life, larger and more sacred than Yggdrasil, is sprouting branches in the embroidered garden, and the joyful shade is covering the world. How skillfully has Gosanja's mysterious representation technique arranged the blue waves of the Dharma milk and grace dew that enter from the *ipsa gwima nyujong* (입사 귀마뉵종 - obscure Buddhist terminology) vertically and horizontally with aqueducts and water taps! Gosanja's Daedong Yeojido has clearly touched the innermost keyboard of representational art with just the mountain symbol alone.

With the remaining strength of succeeding in expressing the skeleton of the Joseon topography, Gosanja drew the water currents, showed the road network, did not forget to calculate the distances there, and demarcated the boundaries of *do* (道, province), *bu* (府, large town), *gun* (郡, county), and *hyeon* (縣, district) with clear lines in a relief-like manner, striving to not diminish the brightness of the map and succeeding in doing so. Also, he prepared appropriate symbolic signs for "map symbols" (地圖標) such as military garrisons (營衛), *eupchi* (邑治, town centers), fortresses (城池), military strongholds (鎭堡), relay stations (驛站), warehouses (倉庫), pastures (牧所), beacon towers (烽燧), royal tombs (陵寢), neighborhoods (坊里), old *hyeon* (古縣), old military strongholds (古鎭堡), and old mountain fortresses (古山城). In some ways, this is no worse than the most advanced map symbol technology today, and he further added necessary distinctions such as whether *eupchi* had fortresses or not (有城無城), and whether fortresses were mountain fortresses or border fortresses (山城 關城), showing his thorough consideration in all aspects and further enhancing the efficiency of practical use.

He completed the entire map with such meticulous consideration, and also did not forget to make special maps of important parts. The map of the vicinity of Hanyang called Gyeongjo (京兆) is one of them. Also, the map of the neighborhoods inside Hanyang Fortress called Obu (五部) among them is one of them. All of them are accurate and appropriate. In addition, he added historical descriptions regarding the origin and effectiveness of the map, clarifying his creative position and at the same time striving to arouse general attention to the map. Here, we will see the author's keen academic conscience and sincere approach to practical use.

The Daedong Yeojido is not just proud of its large volume. It is not just about the thoroughness of the system. It is the value, the pride, the life, and the spirit of the map that it provides a fair idea of the actual state of Joseon by comprehensively viewing all geographical phenomena with detailed and accurate descriptions under such intentions, giving a more accurate knowledge of Joseon geography than other traditional maps. The system is well-organized and the content is accurate, so the Daedong Yeojido has become a tiger with wings. It is cumbersome to say this and that, but in short, until the new topographic map of the Land Survey Bureau was created, there was no map of Joseon that was more detailed or more accurate than the Daedong Yeojido.

To mention a few facts that show the value of the Daedong Yeojido <65>

A reduced map of the Hanyang area in the Daedong Yeojido
First, before the topographic map of the Land Survey Bureau was released, the maps of Joseon were all based on the Daedong Yeojido, with partial actual measurements and computational estimations added to create the general map of Joseon that was in circulation until recently. Second, during the Sino-Japanese War of 1894-1895 (甲午 乙未), the Japanese military operations were actually based on the Daedong Yeojido, achieving accurate and thorough results. Third, the Daedong Yeojido is entirely academic and modern in its method, format, production, and symbols, and even if it is presented now, there is no awkwardness at all, and in many ways, it can be seen as a precursor to modern map schemes.

However, how did the Daedong Yeojido come to have such excellent contents? It is partly due to the traditional assets that Joseon has to be proud of in terms of maps, but most of it comes from the author's personal talent, sincerity, and effort, which cannot be denied. Moreover, it was not compiled and edited on the desk with scissors and paste, but was mainly produced from actual experience and investigation, with his meticulousness and uprightness focusing on actual field verification as much as possible. The Daedong Yeojido is not a copy, not a compilation, not hearsay, and not imagination. It is truly the result of the author's long study of literature and his many years of wandering to verify it, laboriously created on the basis of actual measurement and experimentation. It was not just drawn, but painstakingly made. According to legend, the author devoted half his life to thoroughly investigating every mountain, river, and corner of the eight provinces, and climbed Baekdusan three or four times to understand the main branches of the mountain ranges. In any case, the Daedong Yeojido was conceived with the blood and sweat of this extraordinary individual, and that is why it has amazing value and sacred admiration. It cannot be said that the Daedong Yeojido has fully achieved scientific effectiveness, but it is undeniable that it was produced with a scientific spirit and scientific foundation.

In general, Joseon was originally a country favored by maps. Even if we only speak of what is seen in historical records, King Yeongnyu of Goguryeo sent a "Map of Domains" (封域圖) to Tang in 628 AD (Old Book of Tang), and King Seongjong of Goryeo sent a "Map of the Country's Geography" (本國地理圖) to Liao in 984 AD (History of Liao). Therefore, the origin of maps in Joseon can be said to be truly long and distant. How the art of maps developed in the Joseon Dynasty can be examined from the many actual objects that remain to this day—I do not see the need to add to this now. Furthermore, King Sejong also devoted himself to meteorology, sending Yun Sa-ung, Yi Mu-lin, and Choe Cheon-geo to Baekdusan, Manisan, Hallasan, and Seolhanjeom to engage in the measurement of the North Star's altitude and other things (Diary of the Royal Observatory). He also made various astronomical instruments and devoted himself to the affairs of reverence. It is certain that this had a direct and indirect influence on our map history. In the era of King Sejo, which can be said to be an extension of Sejong's culture, things called "Gyuheong" and "Indiui" were invented, and modern surveying techniques such as triangulation and leveling were implemented, which must have had some effect on the correction of maps. <68> Also, Joseon was surrounded by strong neighbors on both sides, and because of the need for national defense, it was in a position to thoroughly understand the topography of the defenses of both sides, so this is also thought to have been a factor in the development of maps. In any case, Joseon, due to various opportunities, valued the administration of maps greatly and seriously, so there were many kinds and the contents were relatively detailed. However, all the achievements of the past were concentrated in the Daedong Yeojido. In other words, it can be said that the map-making efforts of successive generations of Joseon came to fruition in this. The Daedong Yeojido has a historical background in this respect as well.

For example, as for the topography of Joseon, Goryeo already made a bottle that resembled the topography of the country out of silver during the reign of King Hyeonjong and used it as currency (History of Goryeo), so it has attracted general attention relatively early, and its true form has also been recognized early. Although the precise ancient maps show a considerable degree of accuracy, there were still imperfections and distortions. However, in the Daedong Yeojido, a very surprising degree of accuracy is exhibited, approaching the real shape almost without great differences. In other words, it can be said that the Daedong Yeojido was the first to teach us the true shape of Joseon.

In this way, the Daedong Yeojido became an unprecedented authoritative establishment on the current state of affairs in Joseon. At the same time, it is the one who inherited and unified the achievements of the past, and it is the one who accurately records and depicts the current state of affairs, so it is a standard for verifying the actual situation and a basis for estimating the past state, reaching back to that time and even earlier, making it historically valuable, important, and precious for future generations. In general, as for the cartography of Joseon, after the Seonsul (選述 - selection and description) of the Yeojiseungnam (輿地勝覽, Augmented Survey of the Eight Provinces of Korea) four centuries ago, there were almost no attempts to continue its revision and supplementation (Yi's Taekniji (擇里志) is a special old theory, and Oh's Yeojaechwaryo (輿載撮要), an unpublished manuscript of 8 volumes, is from after that, so it is naturally discussed separately). Even if there is a section on geography in the Dongguk Yeoji Seungram (東國輿地勝覽), it is not comprehensive and well-organized. Therefore, since there is no way to refer to geographical facts other than and after the Seungnam, it is fortunate that there is something like the Daedong Yeojido, which can be used as a gazetteer and has a reliability that is sufficient to be trusted more than anything else. It is truly natural for the historical community today to rely on it as a basis for all research on Joseon geography.

Who is the one who built this epoch-making, trans-generational, and valuable great undertaking, both in terms of quality and quantity, and in terms of form and use? What opportunity and power did he have to achieve this immortal great undertaking? Don't be surprised. This is the product of a nameless poor scholar, who belonged to the lowest class in society at the time, completely cutting off all desires in life, fighting against hunger, fighting against oppression, and fighting fiercely with all kinds of human tragedies, pouring out every last drop of his strength, barely singing the triumphal song that he had now conquered the world. Because of this, his family was always <69>

immersed in ice and snow, and because of this, his body was always driven by whips. As a reward for achieving this great achievement that would shine forever, imprisonment and wrongful death were his final visitors. Death's victory is the tearful relic that is the Daedong Yeojido. Protection, assistance, even empty words of consolation and encouragement—these were the ultimate dreams that the author of the Daedong Yeojido tried to see in this world but never got to see. Heaven completely made the author alone responsible for this cosmic great undertaking, separating him from all humans and human things, or rather, adding all kinds of oppression and expulsion to him, making him complete the beginning and end of this undertaking in absolute solitude. Of course, the circumstances of the person at the time must have been difficult, but thinking about it, this may have been the ultimate intention of Heaven, who loved him endlessly, and because there was no one among these humans who had the good fortune to share the sacred honor that came from the achievement, his loneliness and solitude may not have been the cup of ecstatic joy that only his lips could touch.

The Daedong Yeojido was, in its motivation, a product of an extremely patriotic, national self-esteeming, national land-defending, and sublime spirit. Joseon should also have a map as good as others. Joseon should have a better map than others. Joseon urgently needs an excellent map that is a mirror for all descriptions and a guide for development. Whether thinking of the Southern Gyeong (南梗 - Southern Border Distress) of the Imjin Year or the Northern Wan (北頑 - Northern Stubbornness) of the Byeongja Year, it was thought that the outrageous boats and strange people that were rushing in might throw Pandora's box through any crack in the door, so it was necessary to provide detailed knowledge of all defensive points to the authorities and the general public. This was the fundamental impetus that made Gosanja sacrifice everything and live only in the Daedong Yeojido. This can be clearly discerned from his not-so-abundant writings, the insignificant records of his contemporaries, and the words that those who had approached him in his lifetime carelessly conveyed. However, there is no time to explain it in detail here.

Also, the Daedong Yeojido should be praised for being scientific and modern in its method. Also, the Daedong Yeojido should be praised for the external appearance of its achievement being a complete success. Also, no one can deny that the Daedong Yeojido is an epoch-making, almost trans-temporal great undertaking that transcends all of society at the time, longitudinally cutting through the entire history of Joseon, with no similar examples. In this way, if we were to bring out the inherent merits and characteristics of the Daedong Yeojido, there are many other aspects and meanings that can be used to proclaim to others that it is the pride of Joseon, but I do not intend to bring out these things in a complicated way. I do not want to bring them out deliberately. Putting aside everything else, I am proud of the fact that the practical conscience, sincerity, and ability of the Joseon people expressed through the Daedong Yeojido are still so strong and sincere to this day. I am grateful to the Daedong Yeojido for being a source of pride in the power of the mind of the Joseon people. I want to rely on the Daedong Yeojido and boast to others of this greatness, and I want everyone to boast that the sacred power that created the Daedong Yeojido is fluttering in their hearts without them knowing. In this way, the Daedong Yeojido is something that is the pride of Joseon. In a word, the Daedong Yeojido is a great pride of modern Joseon, not so much materially but mentally.

There are many things to write about the influence of the Daedong Yeojido and the facts it induced. Also, there are many things that have been passed down about the author, not only about the Daedong Yeojido but also about his amazing achievements such as the "North and South Star Chart" (南北恒星圖), the "Earth Map" (地球圖), and the "Complete Map of the Capital" (首善全圖). Furthermore, without speaking of his moral and artistic life, there is no way to clarify the life-giving basis of the Daedong Yeojido and present the moral halo behind it. 내가 자랑하고 십흔 朝鮮 것-七十年前에 單身調査, 獨力創製한 古山子의 大東輿地圖
						六堂學人
朝鮮의 자랑을 學術的 業績의 우에 찻는다 하면 우리 古山子의 大東輿地圖와 가틈은 그 가장 光色잇는 것의 한아일 것임니다. 大東輿地圖라 하야 알 이가 이미 적고 古山子라 하면 아는 이가 더욱 듬을고 古山子가 누구냐 하야 아는 이가 거의 업슬 것으로 보면 이것이 그리 대단치 아니한 것처럼 생각될지도 모르지마는 실상인즉 古山子의 大東輿地圖란 그 들어남이 아주 적은 反比例로 그 實價는 아주 거룩함이 朝鮮에 잇는 다른 實用的 作品, 學術的 發明者보담도 더욱 두드러짐이 잇슴니다.

朝鮮, 더욱 近世朝鮮의 思潮는 非開物成務的, 非利用厚生的, 非實際科學的으로 한 주요한 특색을 삼습니다. 兩班질하는 방법, 科擧하는 材料 이외의 모든 것은 輕視, 賤視 蔑視, 심하면 惡視까지하야 잇든 것도 업서지고 나는 것은 순을 질으는 참이엿스니까 士子의 業, 양반의 행세ㅅ거리로 치지 안이한 浮文空理 이외의 다른 名物度數之學이 생길 리도 업고 왕성할 리도 업섯슴니다. 有爲한 사람, 有才能한 사람이 눈을 여긔 뜰 까닭이 업섯스며 또 이러한 일을 위하야 苦心, 瀝血, 費神, 惱思할 刺戟이 잇슬 수 업섯슴니다. 그러데 古山子의 大東輿地圖는 이러한 세상에 생겨난 것이오. 무엇이라도 해서 成就 못할 리업는 實才篤志人이 神鸄鬼嘆할 大誠力으로 做出한 것이오, 또 稱譽나 報酬는 姑舍하고 그 때문에 온갓 非笑와 叱責과 내지 逼迫, 禍害를 바드면서 孤憤獨憂, 孤思獨辨한 것이기 때문에 특히 稀罕하고 귀중하고 갸륵갸륵한 것임니다.

이 大東輿地圖란 것이 설사 사회적 문화적으로 有亦可 無亦可한 작난감 비스름한 것일지라도 그것을 인하야 표현된 一人生의 생명의 光輝, 一人格의 建築의 價値로 크고 거룩한 것이 잇스면 그 주관적 聖高性이 그대로 自在할 것임니다. 또 題目이 아모리 조하도 솜씨가 부족하고 성적이 변변치 안이하면 그 작자의 苦心致力은 아무러 하얏든지 그 때문에 그 객관적 實價가 올라가 질리 업는 것이지마는 대저 與地圖는 크게 經世治國과 작게 廣知興利로 人文的 效用이 여타 特殊한＜61＞ 第一義的 必要物이오, 우리 古山子의 大東輿地圖는 朝鮮에 職方圖 잇서온 이래의 어느 점으로나 優勝卓越한 劃期的 大業的이오, 同時代外 各國의 類品에 비하야 特長은 잇슬 법호대 손색이 업는 頭角嶄然한 일대 矜耀요, 그것이 그 이전의 모든 精華를 함축하고 그 이후의 모든 蹊逕을 啓導하야 朝鮮에 잇는 古地圖의 集成, 新地圖의 淵源이 된 전지도의 根盤이오 그것이 당시 朝鮮에 잇는 最高知能에 터잡은 실험 실측의 산물로 과학적 精微와 한가지 도덕적 崇古를 兼該한 일종의 예술적 작품이오, 그것이 시대문화를 表象하는 華柱요 그것이 朝鮮 양심을 示現하는 寶塔이매 古山子 大東輿地圖의 모든 의미로 갸륵갸륵한 것이 맛당히 어떠타 하겟슴니까. 大東輿地圖와 그 작자인 古山子는 진실로 진실로 朝鮮이 가지는 가장 큰 成業 聖人의 한아임니다. 아모가 앙탈하든지 나는 그러타 하겟슴니다.

대저 大東輿地圖란 어떠한 것인가? 여긔 대한 煩雜한 설명과 지도학적 비판의 상세는 시방 그것을 겨를하지 못함니다마는 이제 몬저 그 내용을 약설하건대, 朝鮮의 全域을 그리되 北은 穩城으로부터 南으로 濟州에 이르기까지를 22층에 난호아서 連하면 全幅이 一覽되고 分離하면 필요한 부분만은 따로 보게 하얏스며 다시 每一層식을 方冊形으로 접어서 접으면 卷이오 펴면 幅이 되게 하야 綜合及分區와 檢索及携帶에 모든 便宜를 꾀하얏스니 얼는 말하면 朝鮮全域圖를 크게도 보고 작게도 쓸 수 잇게 만든 가장 실용적의 新案임니다. 이것이 시방은 어느 나라의 지도에나 다 보는 형식이오 朝鮮으로 말하야도 土地調査局에서 만든 20만分1, 5만分1, 2만分之1 등의 세밀한 지형도가 다 이 分合自在한 방식을 써서 별로 신기한 것 업는 바로대 이것도 당시에 잇서서는 前無特創한 一新案이엿슴이 母論임니다. 大東輿地圖는 이러케 大體의 형식으로부터「오리지널」로 나왓슴니다.

그 每一層의 長은 약 1尺이오 그 幅은 疆域의 形便에 準하야 廣狹이 不一하야 제 1층의 咸鏡北道 北瑞 가튼 데는 尺여에 불과하기도 하고 넓은 데는 8,9척에 뻗치기도 호대 이것을 6寸5分許의 폭으로 접어서 재떠리면 1尺3寸許의 分圖 1面식이 보이게 하니 全圖의 內에 이러한 도면이 무릇 120張許를 포함한 셈이라. 그 長만 하야도 合하야 4평에나 걸치고 幅은 虛處實部를 幷하야서는 2坪을 지나서 이것을 1폭 全圖로 꿈인다 하면 반도 南岸 濟州島간의 空隙까지 너허서 10坪 이상의 면적을 잡는 大圖幅을 일울 것임니다.

그 지역의 圖示로 말하건대 미상불 周到한 用意와 愼密한 설계 하에 위치와 거리와 지세와 시설 등의 정확한 표시를 하기에 힘썻스니 원체 朝鮮은 지도에 잇서서 발달이 오래고 진보가 두드러저서 古來로 전역의 正形을 알아온 지가 오래기도 하거니와 이제 大東輿地圖를 連結해노코 볼진대 그 輪廓의 형세가 한층 더 眞形에 갓가움을 발견할 것이오. 그래서 정밀한 기계로 嚴査精驗 아니한 분수로 어떠케 큰 고심이＜62＞ 그 紙背에 숨어 잇슴을 알 것입니다.

이 作者는 몬저 長 1척 廣 6寸 5分의 반폭에 每方 10리의 方眼을 만드러 거긔 縱이 12 곳 120里요 橫이 8, 곳 80리임과 斜徑이 14리임을 표시하고 自己의 표시하려는 지형을 이 基線의 우에 기재하니 시방 세부측량의 성적에 吻合하기는 어렵겟지마는 대체에 잇서서 정확을 힘쓴 보람은 충분히 도면의 우에 나타낫습니다.
지도는 본대부터 지형의 상징적 표시니까 어찌 말하면 標號의 巧拙長短은 지도의 생명을 좌우하는 일대 關節이라 할 것임니다. 그럼으로 근대의 지도와 고대의 그것을 대비하야 보면 다른 것보담도 특히 記載符號의 一疎一密, 一奇一淡함에서 큰 相違點을 발견도 하는 것임니다. 지도제작의 발달 혹 沿革이란＜63＞ 것은 지도상에 描出한 지리현상과 밋 여긔 관계한 文字 及 符號를 정확하고 신속하게 식별할 수 잇게 하는 기술 진보의 정도를 일음에 不外하다함이 또한 이것임니다. 大東輿地圖의 작자는 이 점에 대하야도 퍽 出衆한 意匠을 가저서 구래의 형식을 깨치고 신미가 뚝뚝 드는 好符號를 案出하야 도면의 성공을 거두엇슴니다.

첫째 朝鮮은 山岳國입니다. 朝鮮의 지리현상이란 것은 산악이 거의 전부요 다른 것은 여긔 附隨하는 景象임에 지나지 아니한다 할 것임니다. 지도의 어느 부분이라도 산악의 稠疊한 記載를 떠나서 도면이 성립될 곳이 업슴니다. 그럼으로 산악표시 부호의 得當如何는 朝鮮 地圖의 전 생명을 좌우할만한 중대한 權能을 가지는 것임니다. 이 작자는 무론 이 점을 銳敏하게 看取하얏슴니다. 그리하야 生氣의 湧騰하는 도면을 만들어낼 만한 조흔 表現術을 어덧슴니다. 東洋에 잇는 재래지도의 산악부호는 대개 ∧字의 叢集 혹 連鎖로써 함이 통례로서 孤起한 산이나 稀少한 산악의 표시에는 그것으로도 부족을 늣기지 안이 하겟지만은 朝鮮과 가티 蛛網碁盤적 산악국에 잇서서는 ∧자 連鎖로는 도면을 沈靜케 하야 거긔 明快와 活躍을 볼 수 업시 할 것이 必矣임니다. 그리하야 이 작자는 새로이 擬形的 黑線의 廷布로써 산맥을 표시호대 그 선에 *細를 부치고 角에 高下를 지우고 體에 大小를 나타내고 또 根幹枝條의 分岐 詰屈을 어리숭하고 부드럽게 지시하야 전 도면이 彫刻的으로 또드러진 印象과 한가지 莊重味 深厚味와 飛動性 活躍을 가저서 圖繪이면서 模型을 겸한 用을 하고 俯瞰的 臨寫的 價値를 아울러 발휘하는 觀이 잇게 하얏슴니다. 이것은 一見에 古式에 사소한 轉化를 試한 것일 뿐일 듯 하지마는 그 實價에 잇서서는 거의 地圖面을 살리는 上에 획기적 성공을 거든 것임니다. 특히 어리숭하고 굼으름한 中에 말하기 어려운 古朴한 인격적의 움즉임과 뭉싯하게 사람을 薰化하는 예술적 냄새가 서려잇슴을 못내 못내 늣기웁게 함니다.

그는 이러케 周密한 思慮로써 전 지도를 완성하고 또 주요한 부분의 特圖 만들기를 니저버리지 안이하얏스니 京兆라 하야 漢陽의 近郊를 圖示한 것이 그 1이오. 또 그 중에서 五部라 하야 漢陽城內의 坊里를 細圖한 것이 그 1임니다. 모두가 정확하고 적절한 것들임니다. 그리하고 또 지도의 源委, 效用 등에 관한 고증적 기술을 添하야 자기의 제작적 입장을 밝히하는 동시에 지도에 대한 일반의 주의를 喚起하기에 힘썻슴니다. 여긔서 우리는 이 작자의 예민한 學的 양심과 篤摯한 經世的 태도를 볼 것임니다.

大東輿地圖는 그 體積의 큼만을 자랑삼는 것 아님니다. 또 體例의 周到만을 닐커를 것 아님니다. 이러한 用意의 하에 상세하고 정확한 기재로써 모든 지리현상을 通觀하야 朝鮮의 實勢에 대한 정당한 관념을 엇게 해 줌이 재래의 다른 것보담 朝鮮 地理의 정확한 지식을 엇게 해 줌이 그 가치요 그 자랑이오 또 그 생명이오 정신입니다. 體例-嚴整한데 내용이 정확하니 大東輿地圖-이에 날개 도친 범이 된 것입니다. 이러니 저러니 하기가 번거로우니 얼는 말하자면 土地調査局의 新地形圖가 생기기까지 朝鮮의 지도는 大東輿地圖보담 더 詳密한 것도 업섯고 더 정확한 것도 업섯습니다.

대채로 말하면 朝鮮은 원래가 地圖的 特寵國이엿스니 史籍에 보이는 것만으로 말하야도 高句麗의 榮留王이 西紀 628년에 「封域圖」를 唐에 보낸 일이 잇고(舊唐書) 高麗의 成宗은 西紀 984년에「本國地理圖」를 遼에 보낸 일이 잇스니(遼史) 朝鮮에 잇는 지도의 淵源은 실로 멀고 길다고 할 것이며 李朝에 들어와서 지도의 術이 어떠케 발달되엿슴은 시방까지도 남아잇는 許多한 實物에서 考驗할바-니 이제 架疊할 要를 보지 안이하며 더욱 世宗은 測候에도 힘을 써 尹士雄, 李茂林, 崔天衢 등을 白頭山, 摩尼山, 漢拏山, 雪漢岾 등에 波往하야 北極高 기타의 測驗에 從事케도 하고(觀象監日記) 또 가진 儀象의 器械를 만들어 親히 欽敬의 事에 힘쓰섯스니 이것이 직접 간접의 영향을 우리 地圖史上에 주엇슬 것도 必矣이며 世宗 文化의 延長이라 할 世祖代에는 「窺衡」이니 「印地儀」니 하는 것을 발명하야 三角, 水準 등으로서 지형을 측량하는 근대적 測地術이 실행되엿스니 이것도＜68＞ 응당 어느 程度만치의 地圖訂正上의 효력을 주엇슬 것임니다. 도 朝鮮은 左右로 强隣을 끼고 잇서 국방상의 필요가 彼我關防의 形勢를 審知하야 둘 處地에 잇는 故로 이것도 지도발달의 一 誘因을 지엇스리라고 생각되기도 함니다. 여하간 朝鮮으로 말하면 종종의 機緣을 因하야 지도의 政을 크게 重하게 녁여 그 종류도 만코 그 내용도 비교적 詳密하얏섯슴니다. 그런데 그 이전의 모든 업적은 말큼 大東輿地圖로 會萃되여 말하자면 朝鮮 역대의 지도상 노력이 이에 이르러 集大成을 하얏다고도 할 수 잇게 되엿슴니다. 大東輿地圖는 이 점에 잇서서 歷史的 背景으로도만 만치 안이함을 가진 것임니다.

닐을테면 朝鮮의 지형으로 말하야도 高麗 顯宗 시절에 이미 銀으로 본국 지형에 象한 甁을 만들어 貨幣와 가티 行用한 일이 잇는 것처럼(高麗史) 비교적 일즉부터 일반의 주의를 끄을고 딸하서 그 正體도 일즉부터 인식되여서 古地圖의 精密한 것은 어지간한 정확의 도를 보이기도 하얏지마는 그래도 어설피고 또 이그러지는 弊가 업지안이하든 것인데 大東輿地圖에 이르러서는 아주 놀랄만한 正確率을 발휘하야서 거의 실형과 大差가 업슬만치 逼近하야왓슴을 봅니다. 말하자면 大東輿地圖가 비로소 朝鮮의 正形을 우리에게 가르처 주엇다고 할 것임니다.

이러케 大東輿地圖는 朝鮮 현재 실세에 대하야 曠前無比한 權威的 建立이 되는 동시에 一은 古來의 업적을 承受統括한 者임으로써 一은 당시의 實勢를 정확히 기록화해 가지고 잇는 者임으로써 길히 후세에 대하야 그 당시 내지 그 이전에 까지 遡及하야 實勢考驗의 표준이 되고 考狀態 推定의 典據가 되는 점에서 역사적 가치로도 심히 重大尊貴함을 가젓슴니다. 대체 朝鮮의 輿地學으로 말하면 4世紀나 前의 選述인 與地勝覽 이후에 거의 續修補述의 擧를 보지 못하고(李씨의 擇里志는 특수한 古說이오. 吳씨의 與載撮要未刊本 8冊은 그 후의 것이닛가 당연히 別論한 것) 文獻備考에 與地考가 잇대야 그 또한 周徧整齊한 것이 안이매 勝覽 이외와 이후의 與地的 사실은 指徵이 未由한데 여긔 다행히 大東輿地圖와 가티 지도 그대로 地誌的의 用이 되고 또 아모 것보담도 信賴하기에 足한 확실성 가진 것이 낫스니 오늘날 史學界-朝鮮 地理에 관한 모든 考證의 典據를 이에 依仰함은 진실로 당연한 일임니다.
