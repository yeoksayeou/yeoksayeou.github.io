기사제목	平安할지어다 (May You Be at Peace)  
필자	李泰俊 (Yi Tae-jun)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

May you be at peace  
						Yi Tae-jun  
Little wave!  

Truly, are you now going to be without answers?  

I hear that you are dead, whom I thought would surely come back at once, as if you would spring forth from any place.  

Little wave!  

It is said that a genius goes forth when the time comes. Are you not a part of that example? It would be good to fall into that example! Even without that "genius," how wonderful of a person you would be!  

Little wave!  

You were poor.  

But is there anyone as rich as you?  

Little wave!  

You were slow.  

But where is there someone as agile as you, as sincere as you?  

Because of this, although you are gone, your legacy remains vividly like the bright markings of a great person. Oh, your distinct marks!  

Little wave!  

Now, even if I call you, I will no longer hear your voice. I will never again taste your face in the alleys of Flower Cave or in any room of the Kaepyuksa (開闢社).  

Is there such a frustrating fact? However...  

But to feel frustration and sorrow is the emotions of those who remain. What change could death bring us? Especially with you, Little Wave, who was known for your expansive thought, would find comfort in the realm of life and death, would you not? Now you will no longer experience the pain of difficult examinations.  

Little wave!  

Like a stream flowing behind you, we are moving along. Just ten years later, that's all it is. Is all this sorrow nothing but a crafty pretension?  

Wherever you have gone, whether it be America, Germany, Paradise, or Pure Land, may you be at peace for a long time.  

**Summary**  

The poem "May You Be at Peace" reflects on the passing of a beloved figure, whom the author deeply mourns yet celebrates. The author highlights the qualities of this person, such as their sincerity and rich spirit, contrasting their apparent shortcomings with the profound impact they had on others. The author grapples with the pain of loss while conveying a sense of peace and hope for the departed. This piece captures the sense of longing and the emotionality surrounding death, reflecting feelings that were prevalent during the colonial period in Korea. Historians might find the personal touch in this lamentation indicative of the broader cultural response to loss under colonial rule, showcasing how such emotional expressions often intertwined with national and personal identity.

잡지명	별건곤 제43호
발행일	1931년 09월 01일
기사제목	平安할지어다
필자	李泰俊
기사형태	시

平安할지어다
						李泰俊
小波!
정말 그대는 이제부터 대답이 업스려나?
멧 군데 가지 안어서 당장 차저내일 듯한 그대를 모다 죽어 업서젓다고 하네.
小波!
天才는 일즉 간다 한다. 그 例에 빠지지 안흠인가? 그까짓 例엔 빠저도 조흘 것을! 그까짓 『天才』는 떼여바려도 그대는 얼마나 훌늉한사람일 것을!
小波!
그대는 가난하엿다.
그러나 그대처럼 넉넉한 사람이 어듸 잇섯스리요.
小波!
그대는 느리엿다.
그러나 그대처럼 敏捷한 사람, 그대처럼 至誠스런 사람이 어대 잇섯스리요.
그랫기 때문에 그대 가도 그대 남긴 자최돌에 파노흔 듯 뚜럿하고나. 오오 뚜렷한 그대의 자최 빗남이여.
小波!
이제는 전화를 걸어도 그대 목소리는 들을 수 업슬 것이다. 이제는 花洞 골목에서도 開闢社 어느 房안에서도 다시는 그대 얼골을 맛나지 못할 것이다.
이런 답답한 사실이 어대 잇는가. 그러나... ...
그러나 답답한 것도 아수운 것도 우리. 남어 잇는 사람의 야튼 情. 죽엄이 무슨 逢變이리요. 더구나 小波 그대만한 요량이 깁픈 사람은 必是生死一如의 境에서 편안히 발ㅅ길을 뻐덧슬 것이 아닌가. 이젠 그대에겐 檢閱難의 고통도 업슬 것이로다.
小波!
한 골재기 물처럼 우리도 그대의 뒤를 흘러 가도다. 고작 멧십 년 뒤, 그것이로다. 슬프니 언잔으니가 모다 간사한 엄살이 아닌가.
小波 그대 간 곳이 미국이던 독일이던 천당이던 극락이던 길이길이 평안할지어다. ＜3＞
