기사제목	내가 만일 싀집을 간다면 新式 家庭으로 갈까? 舊式 家庭으로 갈까? (If I Were to Get Married, Would I Go to a New Style Family or an Old Style Family?)  
기사형태	설문 (Survey)  

As a young teacher who has just left school, I do not have much experience in society, and since I am just about to continue my school life, I have not thought about issues like marriage at all. However, if you ask me to answer, I think that if we must distinguish between new and old styles, the new style refers to a family that has a modern understanding and is more cultivated, while the old style consists of parents who are still bound by feudal thought, who prefer traditional ways. No matter how advanced education may be, parents' households can still remain unaware of how the emergence of the new age affects them. When I contemplate this, I believe the evaluation should instead focus on the character and personal cultivation of the individuals involved, rather than merely separating by new and old styles. For instance, I heard that a friend of mine who graduated from middle school went to a new style family in Seoul and felt that the difference in manners from her in-laws was that they enjoyed “nice curry” or “sweet and sour pork,” indicating modernity, whereas they criticized their daughter-in-law more harshly than our less educated mothers. On the other hand, another friend went to a rural family in Gyeongsang-do, where despite the husband being educated, the parents were farmers who lived humbly. In the end, I think there are families that are flourishing due to compatibility and those that are on the decline. However, I am drawn to new style families. Why? Because to understand the new woman and to navigate the times well, it is evident that one should emerge from a new style family. Especially concerning the issue of a daughter-in-law's employment, even if such a person cannot be hired at places like shops, banks, or companies, there is a significant tendency in traditional families for younger men to seek jobs in places with better opportunities. If I want to cultivate my economic independence, it would be difficult in traditional families. Moreover, even if permission were granted, I anticipate that it would not be sustained. For these reasons, I believe that for those of us who are literate and have aspirations for development, a new style family would be more suitable. 

After reflecting on your question, maybe you are considering me as a single woman and wanting to help me find a suitable marriage partner? Anyway, others call me an aging unmarried woman, but even at twenty-five, I haven't married yet, which might contribute to such a perception. I have experienced countless temptations and challenges as I have aged as a single woman. Regarding what you asked about the new and old family styles, I have witnessed both new style wives and old style wives participating in gossip, sometimes eager to point out flaws. Therefore, if I comprehend their psychology well, it indicates that I am somewhat clever. Naturally, I have a comparison between old and new families. Therefore, I firmly advise that educated women should pursue marriage in a new style family. 

When discussing what constitutes a new style family, some might picture a modern household with bricks, two or three stories, cooking with gas, husbands studying abroad, and daughters-in-law relaxing with piano lessons and pampering, but that is not the kind of family I refer to. Besides, families in poor Korea scarcely fit that description, and if such families do exist, I certainly do not want to marry into one. 

Therefore, my ideal new style family would be one where in-laws understand their roles and respect the presence of a daughter-in-law, leading to a harmonious household. Even if the household only has enough to eat, mutual agreement would create a more valuable environment than mere financial stability. I would like to marry into such a family.

Most new women wish to marry into a new style family. This desire is simply based on the idea that in-laws would understand them more. However, I believe that there is more to it than simply rejecting old values. As society evolves, beliefs change; conflicts may arise if a person raised with traditional views interacts with someone educated to align with modern ideas. Nevertheless, one cannot solely blame the parents for this clash. Why? Because if everyone insists on their opinions, conflict will naturally arise. If one pretends just to conform while carrying out household tasks, home conflict will inevitably cease to exist. Moreover, we stand in different positions compared to women from other countries; I believe we should not merely opt for modern families due to ignorance, but rather take the opportunity to enter traditional families that need enlightenment. By sharing knowledge related to hygiene, clothing improvement, and discussions about the world to guide in creating better parents, we can show the power of women’s education. Regardless of the obstacles, I intend to enter a traditional family to initiate a movement against illiteracy among parents in Korea, as it is a fundamental issue that needs resolution.  

**Summary**  

The article discusses the dilemma of whether to marry into a new style family or an old style family, reflecting contemporary views on family structure in colonial Korea. The responses from various authors illustrate their preferences and rationales based on personal experiences and social aspirations. It also highlights the conflicts between traditional values and modern perspectives, emphasizing the ideals of women's independence and education as influential in making such personal decisions. Historians may find the interactions portrayed in the responses significant as they reveal the shifting roles of women and the societal expectations of marriage during the colonial period, indicating a gradual departure from traditional norms.
