필자	外聞生  
기사형태	소식 (News)  

Since four years have passed since the transatlantic flight, Lindbergh's popularity remains remarkably high even today. Just going out for a little while results in being surrounded by handshaking fans and autograph seekers, and when he goes to buy something, crowds gather at the shops, causing traffic disruptions simply by being in a car. Therefore, he has to disguise himself when going out to avoid the crowd, which demonstrates the sorrows of being a popular person. A magazine in the United States conducted a popularity poll, and Lindbergh was voted first while former President Hoover came in third, suggesting that he has completely become an idol among Americans and an object of hero worship.

Lindbergh succeeded in his first nonstop flight from New York to Paris on May 21, 1927, breaking 3,600 miles in 33 hours. In Paris, he received a grand welcome, which was the largest since Napoleon, and the French president awarded him the Legion of Honor. When he arrived in Brussels, Emperor of Belgium and Empress were present to take a commemorative photo at the airfield. In London, more than 150,000 people gathered at Croydon Airport, and as soon as Lindbergh's aircraft arrived, the crowd erupted in excitement and rushed forward. 1,200 policemen formed human chains to block the crowd around the plane, and the people continuously called for "Lindbergh! Lindbergh! Speech! Speech!" The next morning, Lindbergh ran to Croydon Airport to check on his beloved aircraft, worried that the vital plane might have broken down due to the commotion of the previous night. It's natural for a pilot to love his aircraft, but he referred to the body and engine as "we." At a welcome ceremony, being alone, he spoke in "we," which the French found strange.

The hidden great mother behind the hero is Lindbergh's mother, Evangeline Lindbergh, who understood and trusted her son’s great flight. She saw him off at New York Airport before his departure and then returned to Detroit to teach. When Lindbergh received a welcome back in Washington and New York, his mother was also in the car with him. When the crowd cheered for him, she said, "This is your glory, so I cannot share in it."

One of the reasons Lindbergh’s popularity lies in his non-stop flights visiting 82 cities across 18 states in the United States from July to October, covering a distance of 22,350 miles without a single mechanical failure, landing punctually at each airport. During this period, he attended 69 official receptions and participated in a procession of over 1,200 miles, delivering 510 speeches broadcast nationwide. On December 2, he successfully completed a non-stop flight from Washington, D.C. to Mexico City, covering a distance of 2,100 miles in 27 hours and 10 minutes. At that time, the U.S. ambassador was his father-in-law, and the following spring, he and Anne got engaged, holding their wedding on May 27 of that year. Lindbergh was 27 years old, and Anne was 22. This marriage created a sensation that was rarely seen in America. As a clear Christian who declared he had no time for drinking, smoking, or gambling, it is no wonder that American society was captivated, dubbing him "Our Lovely Hero," leading to a shocking number of marriage proposals reaching 300,000! However, Lindbergh remained indifferent, solely committed to aviation. A beauty named Broadway Isherwood sensationalized newspapers by claiming, "I have gone out with Lindbergh to parties and even driven with him in a car." 

Being the darling of all America could not last in loneliness forever, the question became who would be the lucky bride. After succeeding in a non-stop flight to Mexico City at the end of 1928, he spent some time as a guest at the U.S. ambassador Dwight Morrow's residence in Mexico City, during which a romance blossomed between him and Morrow's daughter. This was something even he did not know. Then suddenly, at noon on February 12, 1929, Ambassador Morrow gathered U.S. newspaper correspondents at the embassy and presented a piece of white paper from his typewriter, announcing, "Engagement to Miss Morrow and Colonel Lindbergh has been established." This truly was an unforeseen significant event beyond the scope of U.S.-Mexico negotiations, sparking a flurry of news communication between Mexico City and New York as the telegraph and telephone lines lit up. Various cities across the United States issued extras for the news, making it a worldwide major incident. Just from this, one can gauge Lindbergh's popularity.

**Summary**

The article discusses the overwhelming popularity of aviator Charles Lindbergh following his landmark transatlantic flight. It details his engagements, public adoration, and eventual marriage to Anne Morrow, tracing how his fame affected not just his life but also public perception in the 1930s. Given that the article spotlights an American hero during a time of global upheaval, historians may find interest in how Lindbergh's fame intersected with notions of nationalism and identity during the colonial period in Korea, reflecting the diverse impacts of Western figures on Korean society's aspirations and sentiments.

大西洋 橫斷 飛行 이래 4년이 지난 금일에도 의연히 린드의 인기는 非常하야 조곰 외출만 하드라도 握手狂 「싸인」狂의 포위 공격을 바드며 물건을 사러가면 店頭에는 군중이 운집하고 자동차만 타도 사람 때문에 교통이 杜絶되는 상태이다. 그래서 구경 가튼 것을 가려도 變裝을 하고야 간다니 人氣者의 悲哀도 어지간하다고 하겟다. 年前 미국 엇던 잡지가 현재 米人의 인기투표를 하얏슬 때 第一位가 린드벅이요 후-버 대통령은 第三位엿다는 사실로만 미루어 보드라도 그가 완전히 米人間에 偶像化되여 영웅 숭배의 대상이 된 것을 알 수 잇다.
린드가 뉴-욕과 파리간 최초의 무착륙 비행에 성공하기는 1927년 5월 21일이니 3,600마일을 33시간에 飛破하얏다. 파리에서는 나폴레온 凱旋 이래 최대의 환영을 하얏고 佛大統領은 레죤.도놀의 최고훈장을 주느니 부랏셀에 갓슬 때는 白耳義 황제폐하가 황후폐하와 同列하야 비행장으로 가서 함께 기념사진을 백이느니 론돈에서는 크로이든 비행장에 15만 대군중이 운집하야 린드의 비행기가 도착하자마자 열광하야 殺到하니 1,200명 경관은 서로 팔과 팔을 엇걸어 사슬을 지여서 비행기를 둘러막는 둥 군중은 린드벅.린드벅 연설 연설하고 부르지젓다. 翌朝에 이러나자 린드는 크로이든 비행장을 달려가 愛機를 점검한 것은 生死를 가티한 비행기가 昨夜 소동 때문에 고장이나 업섯나 하고 근심함이니 飛行家가 비행기를 사랑함은 당연한 일이나 그는 機體와 發動機를 가르처  『우리』라고 부른다. 그는 엇던 환영회에서나 혼자 몸이면서 『우리』라고 말하는 것은 佛蘭西人은 이상하게 생각하얏다고 한다. 그는 아메리카를 떠날 때 紹介狀 6통을 가지고 온 巴里이라 10시 반이나 되여서 파리의 루.부루-지에 비행장에 도착하는 찰나 군중이 린드벅 만세를 高唱하며 밀려드는 것을 보고 이 밤중에 이 만흔 사람이 무엇 때문에 이러케 騷動하는지도 몰낫다는데 당시의 착륙은 전문가를 감탄케 하얏다.
영웅의 뒤에는 위대한 어머니가 숨어 잇는 것이니 린드의 어머니 에반지에린.린드벅은 아들의 大飛行에 대하야 이해와 신뢰를 가지고 출발 전 뉴-욕 비행장까지 餞送하고는 여전히 데드로로이트로 가서 敎鞭을 잡앗다. 린듸가 와싱톤.뉴-욕에서 환영을 바들 때 어머니도 가티 자동차에 탓섯든 바 군중이 만세를 불러주어도 답례를 아니하는 고로 린듸가 注意를 식히니까
『이것은 네의 光榮이니 내가 논하 가질 수 업다.』고 말하얏다.
