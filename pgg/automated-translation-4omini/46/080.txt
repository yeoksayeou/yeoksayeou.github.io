잡지명	별건곤 제48호  
발행일	1932년 02월 01일  
기사제목	祕密家庭探訪記, 變裝記者=냉면配達夫가 되여서 (Secret Family Visit Record, Disguised Reporter = Becoming a Cold Noodle Delivery Man)  
필자	夜光生 (Yagongsaeng)  
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Commentary)  

I took off my suit jacket and put on the Korean traditional pants and jacket that were lying around the room. I borrowed delivery gloves and pulled a winter hat down over my ears. Then I placed a bicycle light on my finger.  

"I guess this will do!" I thought as I looked at myself in the mirror, and indeed I appeared rather convincing. While it might be an exaggeration to say that no one would recognize me, the truth is that it was indeed hard to pinpoint my true identity.  

As I said, "Here I go," and opened the middle door, I dashed out, laughing like a fool.  

"I’m ready to go. But what’s all this fuss about?" the noodle shop owner exclaimed, perhaps worried if something had gone wrong.  

“Don’t worry about it,” I interrupted, sounding as if I were annoyed, and then continued, “Please prepare the next order.” I lit up a cigarette.  

Before I knew it, the clock pointed to eleven.  

The telephone rang.  

“Yes, yes, that’s right. Two bowls of cold noodles to 24X Gwancheoldong. Yes, yes.”  

Even before the owner had fully finished placing his order, I jumped up. While waiting for the noodle to be ready, I asked one of the delivery men about the location in Gwancheoldong.  

I turned on the bicycle light. I placed two bowls of cold noodles on a board with red grain garnishing, and added a kettle of broth and a bowl of kimchi. Surprisingly, it felt quite heavy. In addition, I felt rather uncomfortable.  

“Just place it up like this and grasp it with this hand,” one of the delivery friends, probably worried about me, showed me his technique. Following his directions, I tilted my head and grasped the board with one hand. I was worried about losing my balance and tipping over. With a cheerful spirit, I rang the bicycle bell once first and then hopped on my bicycle as the shopkeeper warned me, “Be careful; the ground is uneven; you might have an accident.”  

“I’ll be careful,” I replied curtly and headed into the alley.  

As I reached the streetcar line, the road was wide, and I felt quite comfortable riding at a good speed, even with my inexperienced driving technique. I cautiously crossed the Jongno intersection and turned right behind the Jonggak (Bell Pavilion), speeding up into a narrow alley for the cold noodle shop, barely avoiding falling into the stream while struggling not to bump into a pole. Right at the crisis point! I quickly turned the handle to the other side and suddenly jolted as the board hit the pole, causing the bowls of cold noodles to tilt backward.  

“Ah, wow!” I yelled as I stopped my bicycle and lowered the board, feeling enormously relieved when I found that the bowls were still intact, with the kimchi bowl on top and about a third of the broth spilled. Ten minutes passed, all I could think of was how mortified I would be to the person waiting for the meal. However, as there was nothing I could do about what had happened, I set the bowls back in order and pedaled towards 24X.  

When I approached the door with the bicycle light on and looked at the signboard, it was indeed an upper-class house. The big front door even had the telephone number written on it. I creaked open the middle door and stepped into the courtyard while shouting, “Cold noodles have arrived!” 

The middle door of the main room opened, and I heard a woman’s voice.  

“Come this way.”  

As I went to the veranda, I lowered down the board. The woman, likely the housekeeper, pulled over a table that had been prepared beforehand and began to neatly place the bowls of cold noodles. Meanwhile, I sneaked a look inside the room. The two spacious rooms were surrounded by silk wallpapers. Two lacquered Korean cupboards and a tall Western-style wardrobe were among the clutter.  

“Put it into a different dish,” the man who seemed to be the master of the house, appearing about 40 years old, told the woman who looked about 30. The woman replied, “If you change it to a different dish, it won’t taste good.” Then she poured the broth but suddenly exclaimed, “Aren’t you just going to eat this but how are you supposed to eat it like this?”  

“Guests don’t pour a lot of broth in the winter.”  

“What kind of nonsense is that? I’ve seen many different cold noodle shops. In winter, if you don’t pour broth, you are just going to mix it and eat it!”  

“Oh! You must be quite foolish?” she said, tapping the table. Although the spilled broth was my fault, it felt incredibly irritating.  

After spending 30 minutes on the veranda, I couldn’t complain about the cold. I shifted my butt several times as the chill crept up. I wondered if they had any decent ingredients. I was looking around when the middle door opened, and the voice said, “Stop sitting there and just come in. Please pay when you take the dishes tomorrow morning.” Then the door closed abruptly.  

“I’ve never seen a more messed-up house! Waiting for 30 minutes and making me freeze!” I grumbled to myself, nearly unable to hold back my frustration, but I refrained from voicing it out loud, reminding myself that it was someone else's business. I just took the board and hurried back to the main cold noodle shop.  

When I rang the bell and returned, the shopkeeper seemed rather concerned, saying, “Where have you been? I thought something had happened.” I managed to laugh it off, saying, “Oh, I just had to wait for 30 minutes.” After I shared the unfortunate story, the shopkeeper found it amusing and chimed in.  

“That happens very often. You delivered extremely well.”  

It was my second delivery. To Jaedong X. A longer distance than before. I strapped two bowls of dumplings, a bowl of rice cake soup, and a bowl of cold noodles and headed into the biting north wind as I climbed up the alley in Jaedong. After turning into XX School's alley, I reached a recently built tiled house.  

I creaked open the well-oiled front door and as I entered the courtyard, the room door opened abruptly from underneath. It was clear that they had become aware of the cold noodle delivery. The first thing I noticed were several pairs of men's shoes haphazardly left on the floor.  

“What kind of new young couple’s shoes are these?” I thought as I placed the board on the veranda. Just as I was about to step inside, I realized with a start that these were indeed people who had a relationship.  

Suddenly, my heart began to pressure with anxiety, knowing that my identity was potentially going to be revealed. The men appeared to be from OOO-Company and XXX-Company's Tax Investigation Department, while the lady who must be the householder had a delightful round face.  

While lingering in the background, I was waiting for the dishes but could only hear nonsensical chatter. What were they discussing? My following delivery became urgent, but if I heard any interesting information, it might be worth it to stay. A man handed out a one-won note and hurried back to the main shop. The head delivery men were again singing my praises.  

The third delivery was next. I threw away an unfinished cigarette and strapped two bowls of dumplings again. While climbing up Cheongjin-dong’s main road, I turned into a few narrow alleys that led towards the riverbank and fumbled until I finally arrived at the house.  

Cheongjin-dong 25X. Compared to the other flat houses, it was clearly a relatively tall new house. When I opened the door, two well-dressed gentlemen were standing there, gazing at me suspiciously.  

“What a sight!” I murmured to myself, passing through the outer door into the courtyard. Inside was an even taller house. It was a newly constructed building featuring glass windows in the main hall, and there was a fine dining room adjacent to the outer bedroom, showing off all the various luxuries very drastically.  

“Have the dumplings cooled?” I shouted, and an old woman came out from the inner room replying, “Yes.”  

Immediately after her, a younger woman’s voice called from within, “Grandma, bring them to the lower room.” The elderly lady hurriedly slipped on her rubber shoes before rushing downstairs.  

“Place it down here. You can bring the money tomorrow morning.”  

“Very well.”  

I placed the bowls on the porch and was about to leave with just the board when I opened the front door only to have the elderly lady lock it behind me just then. The two gentlemen were still standing by the door.  

As I led my bicycle out of the alley, something made me glance back at the suspicious gentlemen. What a strange sight! They both were leaning against the utility pole after rummaging through the dumpster and were ahead of the fence. One of them was already on the fence, dragging the other up, and before long, they hopped over the fence and disappeared into the night.  

My heart raced in bewilderment. Without realizing, I headed back toward the front door. I held the light aloft and looked at the doorplate closely; it bore a rare surname in Korea, the middle character was 啓. I thought to myself, “It must be the mistress of Doctor X (부X의원 XXX).”  

Twenty minutes later, I jiggled the door. No response came from inside. After one more attempt to knock forcefully, I heard the sound of someone slipping on their rubber shoes, before the elderly lady finally opened the door with a severe look on her face.  

“Oh my, what a mess! Why are you shaking the door without saying anything?” she scolded.  

“Why would I do that? I came to pick up the dishes.”  

“I thought the master was coming.”  

“Why would you be so surprised? Didn’t you think a master was the one who might come?”  

The elder woman seemed relieved and, sorting out some moment of humor, responded with a grin.  

“Of course, I knew! There were other guests."  

“Indeed! The guests who jumped over the fence, right? Isn’t that fun?”  

“Shh! Don’t say anything. I will just get you the dishes.”  

With that, she closed the inner door and returned with the stacked dishes, reopening the door, she added, “Now, here’s 30 jeon and this as a tip.” Just as the thank you was escaping my lips, the door slammed shut. I stuffed the 40 jeon bill into my pocket and hesitated for a moment before listening carefully towards the window of the lower room. I could hear nothing. After waiting for a while, the woman’s voice finally spoke.  

“What an absurd situation! I can’t stand it.”  

A deep male voice then replied, “What are you worried about, we are just two people, and if worse comes to worse…”  

My instinct told me something was quite off. Finally, I decided it was better to head back before my delivery time was up.  

“What terrible thieves!” I cursed under my breath before returning to the main shop, and realized it was already half past twelve.  

“Just one more delivery will do,” I thought as I sat down in a corner of the shop and lit up another cigarette.  

For my fourth delivery, it was at Seodaemunjeong 1 Jeongmok 19XX, an exceedingly tricky address. After passing the trolley road toward the broadcasting station, I turned into a back alley behind the Jeil High School (제일고등녀학교), reaching a couple of houses in quick succession. I stepped into a modest Japanese-style house, likely inhabited by Korean residents.  

Standing in the center of the small yard, I shouted, “I’ve got rice cake soup!” but was met with silence until a woman's voice hurried from the lower room.  

“Just a second!”  

For some reason, her voice struck me as peculiar. I quickly became alert, my chest tightening. A couple of minutes later, the door opened, revealing a girl about eighteen or nineteen years old in her underwear with disheveled hair. I quickly stole a glance inside the room and saw what was unmistakably a man lying sprawled in the lower corner.  

Knowing that this was likely a young student taking residence in someone else’s home, each of my suspicions began to burn within me. ‘Will he be someone’s lover?’ I mused, but there was a disquieting sense of foreboding in me.  

It might not be romantic, but one could label it as “student smuggling activities.”  

That girl, who was not unbearable to look at, went to the porch, collected the bowls, and after a brief moment turned back, handing me 30 jeon.  

“Take the bowls back tomorrow morning.”  

“Very well,” I said.  

After stealing one last glance at the girl, I exited that house. She locked the panel door behind her.  

As I rode my bicycle back to the main shop, my thoughts swirled around all the peculiar events of that night. I couldn’t shake the feeling of curiosity and concern over the student.  

“When did that young student turn out like this?” I pondered, finding the thought increasingly oppressive.  

At the main cold noodle shop, I asked the delivery men about that place.  

“Yes, yes, that house! It’s about the flour. Every evening there are men that visit incessantly.” they all chimed in unison.  

I donned my suit, adjusted my collar, and thanked the noodle shop owner and the delivery boys before stepping out. The main road was already quiet; the taxis full of revelers sped off.  

“Should I grab a drink?” I mused, looking at a café with its doors shut tightly, though the interior bustled with patrons.  

“Has it already passed two o’clock?” I thought while rushing to the Gyeonji-dong main street heading towards Anguk-dong. The chill wind blew fiercely, and I suddenly felt the ache in my neck and left arm.  

**Summary**  

In this article, the author, under the guise of a delivery man, provides a vivid commentary on societal observations through his various deliveries of cold noodles. Throughout his experiences, he reflects on the nature of class distinctions, the mundane aspects of delivery work, and discovers unspoken relationships in the houses he visits, raising poignant questions about morality and propriety. This portrayal gives a humorous yet critical snapshot of life in colonial Korea, where the interactions between the classes reveal cultural and social tensions under Japanese rule. Historians may find the secretive dynamics surrounding the deliveries a reflection of the socio-political undertones prevalent during the colonial period.

						夜光生
한참 분산하게 양복을 버서 버트리고 방구석에서 굴러다니는 군때무든 조선 바지저고리를 (책보에 싸가지고 온) 박구어 입엇다. 배달부의 장갑을 빌려끼고 방한모(防寒帽)를 푹 눌러 귀까지 덥허 썻다. 그리고 자전거 전등을 한 손가락에 뀌여 들엇다.
『이만하면 되겟지!』 하고 조고마한 체경 압헤서서 내가 내 꼴을 바라보니 아닌게 아니라 능청맛다. 내가 낸 줄 모르리만치 변장이 되엿다면 과장일지는 모르나 사실 나아는 남이 나의 정체를 아라낼 수 업스리만콤 된 것은 사실일 것이다.
『자-엇덧소.』 하고 미다지 문을 열치고 뛰여나오니 냉면집 주인 배달부 할 것 업시 박장대소다.
『됏는데요. 돼서... 그런데 엇저자고 이 야단이신지 알 수 업군요.』 주인양반은 무슨 일이나 나지 안엇나해서 악가부터 하는 걱정이다.
『이-글세 염려마시라는데 웨 이러십니까.』 나는 됩데 귀찬은 듯이 주인의 말을 가로막어버렷다. 그리고 다시
『요 다음 차레를 날 주십시오.』 하고는 담배 한 개를 피여무럿다.
어느 틈에 시계가 열한시를 가르친다.
전화가 떼르...릉 하고 울린다.
『네-네 그럿습니다. 관철동 24X 번지 냉면 두 그릇이요. 네네.』
전화 밧는 주인의 분부가 나리기도 전에 나는 벌덕 이러낫다. 국수 누르는 동안에 배달부 한사람에게 관철동 지리를 무러 두엇다.
자전거 등불을 밝히엇다. 냉면 그릇에 붉은 곡갈을 씨워 두 그릇 장국 한 주전자 소독저 두 개 김치 한 그릇을 실은 목판을 억개 우에 올려노으니 웬일인지 묵직한 것 갓다. 그뿐 아니라 거북해 못 견듸겟다.
『자- 이러케 올려 노으시고 이 손을랑 한 구통이를 잡으서요.』 배달부 친구가 걱정이 되는지 그의 기술(技術)을 공개해준다. 나는 그의 가르키는 대로 고개를 갸웃둥 꾸부리고 한손으로 목판 한 구통이를 든든이 쥐고 보니 다소 중심(重心)이 취해지는 것 갓고 가다가 뒤집어 업흘 것 갓지는 안타. 신ㅅ바람이 나게 자전거 종을 미리 한번 울리고 나서 자전거에 거러안는데 주인이 나서서
『조심하십시요. 땅이 밋그러운데 큰일 나시리다.』 하고 경고한다.
『염녀맙쇼.』 한마듸를 남기고 골목을 나섯다.
전차 길거리를 나스니 길이 넓고 훠-ㄴ한 바람에 익숙지 못한 운전(運轉)솜시나마 속력노키에 매우 편하다. 종로 네거리를 조심조심 건너 종각(鐘閣)뒤로 꼬부러 드럿다. 카폐에서 흘러나오는 레코-드 소리를 듯는둥 마는둥 다시 꼬부러저 평양루 냉면집 좁은 골목을 속력을 나추어 드러스는데 쓰러질듯 말듯 개천을 피하느라고 어리둥하다가 압헤슨 전주(電柱)에 코끗이 다을 지경이 되엿다. 위기일발! 핸들을 얼른 다른 쪽으로 들렷스나 벌서 느젓다. 목판 한 구통이가 전주와 덜컥 부다치며 냉면 그릇이 뒤로 밋그러젓다.
『앗 차차.』 소리를 치며 자전거를 멈추고 목판을 내려노코 보니 천만다행으로 냉면 그릇은 깟닥업고 김치 그릇이 뒤업허젓고 장국 국물이 삼분일은 업지러저 버렷다. 업허진 화로불을 두 손으로 주어 담드시 응겁질에 장갑 낀 손으로 김치를 주어 담고 생각하니 장차 먹을 사람에게 여간 미안한게 아니다. 그러나 이미 저지러 노은 일 할 수 업는지라 그대로 그릇을 정돈하야 가지고 24X 번지를 차젓다.
자전거 등을 놉히 드러 문패를 보니 올케는 차젓다. 큼직한 대문에 전화번호까지 부튼 것을 보아 돈양이나 잇는 집 갓다. 중문을 삐걱 열고 안마당으로 드러스면서
『냉면 가저왓습니다.』 하고 소리를 치니 안방문 미다지 문이 열리며 녀자의 목소리가
『이리 가저오.』 한다. 퇴ㅅ마루 각가히 가서 목판을 내려 노앗다. 주인마님인 듯한 그 녀자는 방웃목에 미리 준비하야 두엇든 소반을 끄러 당겨다 노코 손수 냉면 그릇을 주섬주섬 집어다 놋는다. 긔자는 슬금슬금 방속을 기웃거렷다. 칸사리 넓은 2간장방에 비단방ㅅ장이 쫙 둘러처 잇다. 자개박은 조선ㅅ장(欌)이 두 개 그 엽흐로 키 큰 양복장이 한 개 그 외에도 너저분하게＜16＞ 치장해 노은 것이 만타.
『다른 그릇에다 쏫구 그릇을 내주구려.』 주인인 듯한 40먹은 남자가 30되여 보이는 그 녀자에게 일르니까 그 녀자는
『딴 그릇에 옴기면 맛이 업서요.』 한다. 그러더니 주전자의 장국을 따루다 말고
『아-니 국물이 요고 뿐이야. 이걸 가지고 엇떠케 먹으란 말이야.』 하고 탁 쏜다.
『겨울에는 손님들이 국물을 만이 안 치니깝쇼.』
『아-니 그건 무슨 소리야. 별놈의 냉면집도 다 봣네. 그럼 더 치운 겨울에는 국물업시 비벼먹고 말겟네... ...우리 집에서 냉면을 처음 먹어보는 줄 아나봐 참!』
녀자는 미다지를 탁 다드며 『참 멍텅구리』 한다. 국물 업지른 잘못은 내게 잇지만 엇전지 아니꼽살스럽다.
툇마루 끄테서 30분을 걸터 안젓노라니 치웁기란 말할 수 업다. 볼기짝에 찬 기운이 올러와서 공연이 이러섯다 안젓다 하기를 네다섯 번은 하엿다. 조흔 재료꺼리나 잇지 안을가 하고 두리번두리번 하야 보앗스나 웬체가 시원치 못하다. 이윽고 미다지가 열리면서
『거기 서잇지 말구 그대루 가우. 내일 아츰 그릇 차저갈 때 돈 바다 가구...』 하고 문이 탁 다치어진다.
