珍奇人種의 珍風俗(二)  

In the Australian “Kaidechshu” tribe, when a woman gives a stone to her son who has reached an appropriate age, it signifies that her son has grown into a strong man. Therefore, once he receives this stone, he can no longer be treated as a child.  

On Mindanao, one of the islands in the Philippines, there is a woman from the “Bagobos” tribe who appears to be smiling, as if she is using a dagger that stretches out like a sword.  

  

**Summary**  

This article discusses unique customs practiced by specific tribal cultures, focusing on a practice from the Australian Kaidechshu tribe and detailing the customs of the Bagobos tribe in the Philippines. The ritual of giving a stone to signify a boy's transition into manhood highlights the importance of milestones in tribal societies. This reflects broader themes of cultural identity and the significance of rites of passage in colonial contexts, possibly offering historians insights into the socio-cultural frameworks of indigenous peoples during a period marked by colonial expansion and influence.
