文壇風聞  
The organ of the KAPF (Korean Artistic Literature Federation) has been launched, and it seems that there is no significant change compared to its previous iterations in terms of format and price.  

Mr. Kim Ik is advertising through the magazine "Donggwang," stating that if readers send a poem with an editorial fee of 30 jeon, it will be revised and published in "Donggwang." In this harsh world, poets have invented advanced occupational strategies to earn a living. Have they only been enjoying drinks during their interactions until now? If time allows, it might be worth holding a celebration party for drinking buddies for the business as well.  

Mr. Jeon Mujik has recently moved to a boarding house in O-dong, where the landlady is known for researching culinary methods and has even published a book on the subject. She also teaches cooking at her alma mater. However, the food there is said to be either too bland or too salty, too spicy or too bitter, and since the household professes to be vegetarian for health and longevity, the only time they taste meat is once a month. Having not eaten it for a while, ones fresh meat flavor is surprisingly strong. He lamented, saying, "It seems like for my entire life, I will never get to eat such exquisite meals!"  

Lee Kwang-soo from Chunwon recently published a children’s song in the Dong-a Ilbo (January 3 issue) on page 2, which has been called his latest masterpiece and has caused quite a stir among literary fans. The children’s song goes:  

"Let’s not speak of the radish.  
Let’s not speak of the radish.  
When it grows, it becomes rice.  
When you pound rice, it becomes cooked rice.  
When you cook rice, it becomes porridge.  
If you leave some water, it becomes gruel.  
When you filter the gruel, it becomes rice water.  
Who eats the rice?  
I do.  
Who eats the cooked rice?  
I do.  
Who eats the porridge?  
I do.  
Who eats the rice water?  
That would be me!"
