필자	無名草 (Anonymous)  
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Commentary)  

I met with a friend who had come from the countryside to Seoul. This friend had recently enrolled his younger sister in a girls’ school in Seoul during the spring, and he had come to observe her studies.

So naturally, I began to talk about female students in Seoul.

Friend: "So how are the female students these days?"

Me: "Female students? Haha, after spending some time in the countryside, I see that they have become quite sophisticated!"

Friend: "Well, whether they have become sophisticated or not, since my sister is studying in Seoul now, I am naturally curious about that."

Me: "Haha, that's true. However, the so-called sophistication often leads some to engage in primitive sexual behaviors."

Friend: "So, are you saying that female students have become depraved?"

Me: "That might be so... but rather than saying that, it would be more accurate to say that women in general are still regarded as weak. Due to deficiencies in school education and family upbringing, their rational capacities are lacking, and they are unfamiliar with the ways of the world."

Friend: "What do you mean by that?"

Me: "Don't you understand that? Then I will tell you several traps that may lead to the corruption of female students in Seoul."

Friend: "Traps? Let me hear."

Me: "First of all, there are those who support female students financially, which leads to their corruption... Do you know Yongsuk (假名)?"

Friend: "Yes, where is she now?"

Me: "Well, don’t ask... She was studying, but her family went bankrupt. Then there was a professor at XXXX school who supported her tuition. Eventually, that guy committed a shameless act."

Friend: "Is there really such a terrible person?"

Me: "Saying he is a terrible person is actually inaccurate. Even though he is a professor at XXXX school, the reason he initially paid for Yongsuk’s tuition was because he intended to engage in those acts from the beginning."

Friend: "That is true, but what happened then?"

Me: "Things took a strange turn; she ended up getting pregnant. Once that happened, Yongsuk had no choice but to become his mistress for a while, until she eventually gave birth to his child. And then everyone found out! When he returned to the countryside, she followed him, but later she came back to Seoul and is currently preparing for the female doctor’s examination."

Friend: "What’s your relation to her?"

Me: "It's complicated... But you should know, there are many instances like Yongsuk's right here in Seoul. That is one trap of financial support."

Friend: "Life is really unpredictable... What's next?"

Me: "Next is when female students come into contact with male students in medical schools where they receive treatment."

Friend: "What?... Are they sedating them or something?"

Me: "Not at all... If they did that, it would be better! This is a case of romance."

Friend: "How so?"

Me: "There is a girl named Haekyoung (假名) who is very charming. However, she went to XX Hospital suffering from digestive issues and there, she was treated very kindly, and the treatment costs were subtly covered. Eventually, they fell in love... After such romance and marriage, the guy would leave. And this girl ended up going back to her countryside, taking medicine, and it's not just her..."

Friend: "Why didn’t they get married in the first place?"

Me: "Hmph, that guy never intended to marry! It was just a silly romance… there are countless cases like this. A friend of mine, who graduated from XX Medical School and is a doctor now, claims he did this kind of thing with at least thirty girls."

Friend: "You must be joking!"

Me: "Not at all. You too have the capability. What do I mean? As a guide for student boycotts, don't go down but remain. When boycotts happen in various girls' schools, just take off your shoes and run there. You would truly be receiving something for free!"

Friend: "How ridiculous!"

Me: "Oh, go see. These student boycotts have taken place, but these girls don’t know how to fight back. If a strong leader emerges, the gratitude of those students can easily transform into romance."

Friend: "That’s a mockery of the struggle."

Me: "However, I am not saying that the struggle is worthless. The fact that many people cannot lead or understand this combat is noteworthy. This kind of activity bears fruit, and the affection that female students feel is often turned into a 'boycott service' by those who take advantage."

Friend: "How absurd! What next?"

Me: "Hehe... the next thing is that the female school teachers foster female students at boarding houses."

Friend: "Well, that's a given. Teachers fostering students to see if they're studying well is a kind thing, right?"

Me: "That's true... but if they’re not careful, I wonder if such kindness will lead to ruin. Seven out of ten teachers end up corrupting students."

Friend: "How do you corrupt them?"

Me: "They start by saying, 'Are you studying well these days?'... 'How’s your family doing?'... 'Come, let’s eat together.' They come around a few times. Then, they pick up the cards on the desk and say, 'Aren't you just playing cards?' If the student says, 'No,' they further propose, 'Then let’s play a game.' In a little while, they're playing cards and touching hands, having a drink... you understand?"

Friend: "Are there such vile people?!"

Me: "And that's not it. If it's a male teacher, he does the same and female teachers play matchmaker with good gifts or school maintenance fees."

Friend: "I read something like that in the newspaper. Isn't that XX girls' school?"

Me: "Ah, come on... but it’s not just XX girls' school."

Friend: "Really... what else?"

Me: "There’s plenty more... Next is the sports teachers. In order to teach sports, it's common for girls' schools to bring in professional school athletes."

Friend: "You don’t mean they teach kissing techniques, right? Kissing isn’t sport."

Me: "Not at all! They teach more than just that! I know a guy who is great at track and swimming. This guy is a coach at XX girls' school, and he rarely stays home. Eventually, he ended up corrupting several students, one of whom ended up committing suicide after taking 'Ocotyl' (a type of poison)."

Friend: "No way!"

Me: "But the world doesn’t know about these matters. Instead, they spread stories about the family troubles regarding the girl's parents as the reason behind her suicide! Hmph."

Friend: "So what’s next?"

Me: "Hold on... it’s coming. Let’s say, for instance, there’s a really good female student at XX girls' school."

Friend: "I don't know about that."

Me: "Let's suppose... your younger sister is enrolled there, and you introduce a suitable female student. The thing is, if your sister is propped up, she might naturally take you to meet that girl."

Friend: "When have I ever said that?"

Me: "I'm not saying it’s true. Let’s just say, haha, let’s assume. At first, you're bringing your sister along with that girl into an activity, especially to watch films, particularly tragedies and comedies, while secretly flirting... After that, that becomes an excuse to do more. Then your sister may keep in touch as you both go see plays and visit places like the Han River and even go to resorts with family. Hasn’t that been the case?"

Friend: "Oh dear, don’t talk nonsense. Though I get your point... what’s next?"

Me: "Seems like you’re well-informed... just one last thing. This is interesting. When male students from their hometown visit, they act as the liaison for local community events."

Friend: "That makes sense."

Me: "So, it means that local male students are in danger."

Friend: "Isn’t that it?"

Me: "It’s not just that, but the main thing is... to highlight how weak women still are. When males engage in such temptations, they often remain unaware and fall into their traps. In such cases, they fear that if they expose the perpetrators who robbed them of their virginity, the world will judge them, which keeps them silent…"

  
**Summary**  

The article discusses seven major traps that lead to the corruption of female students in Korean society during the early 1930s. It highlights how financial support, romantic relationships initiated by male authority figures, and informal gatherings can lead to the moral decline of young women. This commentary reveals insights into the societal norms and challenges women faced in a colonial context, illustrating the power dynamics at play and raising concerns that were significant in discussions of gender, education, and morality during this period.

Historically, this piece provides a rich perspective on the vulnerability of women under colonial rule and the societal expectations placed upon them, which historians may find illuminating in understanding gender relations and the moral landscape of colonial Korea.

나 「가만 잇서요... 그래 자네가 자네 누이동생을 사이에 너코 교제를 튼단 말이야. 응... 자네 누이동생이 자네 일이니 오즉이나 침이 말으게 자네를 선젼하겟나? 물론 자네가 그동안 여학생을 다섯이나 탈을 내엿다는 말은 입을 씻고 말을 아니할 것이고...」
친구 「내가 언제 그랫서?」
나 「아-니야. 가령 말이야. 하하하하... 자네가 정말 그런다는 게 아니라. 그래 처음에는 자네 누이동생을 가치 데리고 그 여학생과 서이서 활동사진- 특별히 비극과 희극을 구경 단이면서 쓱쓱 몸동이를 비비기도 하고... 그리다가 그것이 암호가 되야서 그 다음에는 자네 누이동생은 똑 따 바리고 둘이서만 장충단도 가고 한강으로 뽀-트도 타러 가고 정말 조흔 일본 연극장도 가고 돈이 잇스면 인천 월미도 조탕에 가서 가족탕에도 들어가고... 엇대? 한번 아니해봐?」
친구 「엑기 망할 것... 그런 롱담은 부치지 말게. 그러나 그것도 그럴 뜻허이... 다음?」＜25＞
나 「만히 고수한가 보구나... 하나만 더하지. 이것도 신통하지. 무어냐 하면... 고향에서 온 남학생이란 말이야. 엇대? 지방의 친목회가 뚜쟁이 노릇을 한단 말이야. 응. 보통 달은 사람보담은 친하기가 비교적 쉬웁지.」
친구 「그럴 뜻허이.」
나 「그것 봐라. 그러니까 고향 남학생이 위태하다는거야.」
친구 「또 업나?」
나 「또 업는 것도 아니지만 중요한 것은 다 이약이한 세음일세.」
친구 「그러니 결국 말일세. 나도 귀여운 누이동생을 서울다가 두고 수백 리 밧게서 매달 학비나 보내 주게되니 원 마음이 편해야지.」
(나) 「그러니까 결국은 여자가 아직 약하다는데 도라가고 말어요. 제일 남자가 그런 유혹을 할 때에 정신을 차리지 못해서 그냥 우물쭈물 걸니어 들어가고 말고. 그런 때 남자의 그러틋한 본색을 알어채이고도 자긔가 처녀를 빼앗겻다는 것을 세상이 알가 무서워서 감히 그런 녀석들의 죄를 폭로를 식히랴고도 못한단 말이야...」＜33＞
