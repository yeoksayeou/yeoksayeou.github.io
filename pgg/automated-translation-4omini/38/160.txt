기사제목	百花爛熳 (Splendid Blossoming of a Hundred Flowers)  
저자	싸홈동무  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

In the scene, A and B were enjoying some delicious food, having unexpectedly met on the street after a long time. 

A: “You really ate well, didn’t you? I’ve been admiring you very much lately.”  

B: “Oh really?”  

A: “Absolutely!”  

B: “What exactly have you admired about me?”  

A: “First of all, your monthly income is never below a thousand won!”  

B: “And then?”  

A: “And then, a variety of irregular incomes have been very plentiful!”  

B: “Who did you say that to?”  

A: “I told the tax office official…”  

B: “What nonsense is that?”  

As a fisherman was fishing below a bridge, a passerby on the bridge asked, “Is the fish biting well?”  

“Oh, sure! I’ve caught about forty carp right here!”  

“What nonsense! Who do you think you’re talking to? I am the district judge here, and all of this land is mine.”  

“Oh, really? Then do you know who I am?”  

“I don’t.”  

“Well, I am also a famous liar in this district.”  

One Sunday, a wife who despised alcohol went out for a picnic with her husband, and when she opened the basket, she saw a whiskey bottle inside, becoming furious.  

“What on earth is this, dear?”  

“Uh-uh-uh! That’s just an empty bottle I brought back forcibly to use as a candle holder when we get home… You don’t know what’s in other people’s minds!”  

At 3 AM, a police officer caught a drunken man and asked, “What are you doing out here so late?”  

“You see, I’m uh-uh…”  

“What’s that uh-uh?”  

“I’m on my way to listen to a sermon.”  

“What sermon? Which pastor is preaching at this hour?”  

“Oh, the sermon I’m going to listen to isn’t from a pastor; it’s my wife’s sermon.”  

**Summary**  

The article contains a collection of humorous dialogues that depict everyday interactions, reflecting on social attitudes and the absurdity of certain situations during the 1930s in Korea. It exemplifies the humor arising from misunderstandings and the cleverness of common folk. Historians might find interest in the portrayal of social classes and roles at the time, especially the nuances of authority and personal pride against the backdrop of colonial rule.
