Hundred Flowers Blooming  
Great Recognition  
A history professor asked, "Who was the person who awakened the working class the most in the 19th century?"  
One student stood up and said, "The inventor of the alarm clock."

Thoughtful Intent  
"Excuse me, sir. You always carry three umbrellas with you, why is that?"  
"This? I lose my umbrellas so often, you see. If I leave one in a streetcar and another in a restaurant, at least one will remain, so I carry three."

New Idea  
“Mother, please lend me ten yen.”  
“What on earth do you need ten yen for?”  
“Well, if you lend me ten yen—look, I’ll return five yen to you. I will borrow five yen from you, so you’ll have lent me five yen, and I’ll have given you five yen. So it’s equal, right?”  
Mother replied, ".................."

Prize  
An old man came out at the station.  
"Excuse me. How much is it to XX Inn by car?"  
"Yes, it’s one yen and fifty sen."  
"But I have this luggage..."  
"I’ll take that luggage for you for free."  
"Great! In that case, just take this luggage. I’ll follow the taxi."

Trust  
Deokbo went to borrow a donkey from Sambon.  
"Hey, Sambon. Can you lend me your donkey for a bit?"  
"Can’t do it right away. Our donkey is out in the field and is not at home, you see." As soon as Deokbo finished speaking, the donkey could be heard braying loudly from the stable.  
"Oh! The sound of the donkey is coming from the stable. I guess your donkey is at home and only its body is in the field."   
Sambon looked as if he were embarrassed.  
"What do you mean by that? Donkeys have always been known to lie a lot. So, tell me, which one of my words or that donkey's bray do you think is trustworthy? Just think about it!"

Fine  
Three people got caught and were fined fifty yen. However, unfortunately for them, they didn’t have thirty yen. So the police officer said to the judge,  
"This person cannot pay the fifty yen fine. What should we do?"  
The judge replied, "Hmm," then added, "The defendant must be immediately expelled among the crowd, but make sure he doesn't escape. So if he happens to take the wallet of a reasonably wealthy gentleman, just grab him and collect the fine."

**Summary**  

This article presents a series of humorous anecdotes and dialogues reflecting various aspects of daily life in Korea during the early 1930s. The articles comment on social behaviors, absurdities, and relationships, showcasing the difficulties and unique situations individuals face in their interactions. Historians might find the content revealing in understanding societal attitudes and humor during the colonial period, as it hints at the challenges people faced economically and socially under Japanese rule, while maintaining a sense of community and resilience.
