謝告  
◇How to Cross the Borigo Pass  
◇Mr. Sin Seok-u and the Chosun Ilbo  
◇Those Who Will Enter Prison  

The above three titles have been deleted by the authorities' censorship and thus could not be published, and I express my apologies to the readers and to myself.＜29＞  

**Summary**  

This brief notice apologizes to readers for the censorship of three articles in the magazine. It underscores the challenges faced by writers and publications under colonial rule, revealing the extent of governmental control over freedom of expression in Korea during this period. Historians may find the context of censorship particularly revealing, as it provides insight into the tensions between colonial authority and media, reflecting the broader struggles of the Korean populace for autonomy and the challenges faced by intellectuals in this era.
