Prize Contest of One Hundred Winners

The results of the previous issue have been announced below, achieving a significant success amid great popularity. This time, the approach has been slightly altered. While it may seem easy, it may not be that simple. Please take a careful look.

Questions  
1. What kind of birds are in this photograph? Write the name of the bird.  
2. How many birds are shown in this photograph? Write the number.  
3. In this issue of "Children" (어린이) from the eighth week, how many photographs of people are there? (Do not count drawings, only photographs.)  

Those who know are requested to respond with a postcard stating  
1. What kind of bird.  
2. How many birds.  
3. How many photographs of people.  
Please write briefly like this (do not add explanations or other comments), and at the end clearly write your address and name. Cut out the reader's certificate located at the back of this issue, place it in an envelope, and send it. You may also send it together with your friend's submission in one envelope. If you fold half of the envelope flap down and write "Original Included" on the envelope, a two-cent stamp will also suffice.  
The deadline is until April 25. Address: Kyŏngsŏng Kaebyŏksa, Byeolgyeonk'ŏn, Prize Division   
Grand Prize: One person will receive a durumagi (두루마기, traditional Korean coat)  
Regular Prizes: 99 people will receive useful books   
(If the number of submissions exceeds one hundred, winners will be drawn by lottery, and among them, the Grand Prize will also be drawn by lottery.)  

**Summary**  

The article announces a contest inviting readers to identify birds in a photograph and count how many are present, as well as to determine the number of photographs of people in a children’s magazine issue. This contest reflects the cultural engagement of the magazine with its audience during the colonial period. Historians might find this interaction significant as it illustrates how children's media was used for both entertainment and educational purposes under colonial rule, highlighting the dynamics of cultural expression in a challenging political environment.
