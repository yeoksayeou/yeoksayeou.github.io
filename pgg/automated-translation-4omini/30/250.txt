風聞帖  
The teacher of the Women's Art School, Kim Won-sun, had been suffering from a severe illness and was undergoing treatment at a military hospital. However, her condition has improved significantly, and she returned to her family home in Ogumi-ri on the Gyeongham Line. According to a letter received by one of her relatives, she has fully recovered and returned to her original state. We continuously bless her for her future.

  
**Summary**  

This article reports on the recovery of Kim Won-sun, a teacher at the Women's Art School, from a severe illness. It highlights her return home after treatment and expresses good wishes for her future. This brief update reflects the interest in the well-being of public figures in Korean society during the colonial period, revealing social connections and the community's concern for educators, which may provide historians insight into the dynamics of social support in colonial Korea.
