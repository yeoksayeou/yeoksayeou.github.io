Conditions in Prison  
Yu Jin-hee  
Having been sentenced to a four-year heavy prison term due to the First Communist Party Incident, Yu Jin-hee has since lived in a dark cell. Contrary to what we imagine, he is in excellent health and spends his days casting nets like a fisherman from the Chao River. Recently, he has also been working at the net factory. The work is fairly easy, and the food provided is substantial, making this role superior even to that of a bank executive in society. Moreover, since he was elected by his peers as a representative for political prisoners in the factory, the guards and inmates refer to him affectionately as the "Net Department Chairman." During his pre-trial period, he read extensively in economics, but has recently focused on natural sciences, particularly astronomy and geography, and is said to wear his glasses even better than before, earning him the nickname "Mr. Megane" (glasses) among the guards. He is expected to complete his term around mid-February next year.

Kang Dal-yeong  
Similarly, Kang Dal-yeong, who was sentenced to six years as the General Secretary of his party in the same incident, is not expected to see the light of day until September next year (Showa 7). He had been suffering from a nervous disorder even while awaiting trial, and upon sentencing, his condition worsened to the point that he once began to exhibit signs of insanity, making odd claims about catching spirits that existed in his mind. Due to this, he was transferred to the prison hospital for a time, but ultimately his treatment was ineffective. The prison authorities decided to suspend the execution of his sentence, and his family was notified, prompting his wife to travel to the capital. Fortunately or unfortunately, his condition evidently improved unexpectedly, leading to the halt of the suspension and his continued incarceration. He seems somewhat better now compared to before, but without proper treatment, even if he were released, returning to his previous activities would likely be difficult. His work also consists of net casting, and besides classical Chinese texts, he reads literature related to Buddhism and Christianity.

Kim Beom-soo  
Known for his promptness even while incarcerated, Kim Beom-soo is adept at navigating prison life with great caution. Not only is he skilled at playing tricks on guards, but he holds true to etiquette when meeting fellow prisoners, always saluting with his hand rather than merely signaling. Remarkably, it seems that he has never been caught by the guards. His work also revolves around net casting, and while he prefers reading natural sciences over economics or political science, his appearance has notably changed since his imprisonment, with new dental work giving him a distinct look. Those wanting to remember his release date only need to recall the keen note of June 6, 1931. Yu Bong-un, who has helped him with clothing and other necessities since his imprisonment, will likely remember him well.

Kim Jae-bong  
Having received a six-year sentence, Kim Jae-bong is expected to see spring again around April next year (Showa 7). Despite going through hard labor in the factory (net strands), his scholarly demeanor remains, and he hums poetic verses. Though it is unfortunate he has not yet published any of his poetry, he has reportedly created several good pieces. His health holds steady, but he becomes overly sensitive about not receiving even a single piece of correspondence or a book from relatives in the prison. At times, he feels upset enough to scold himself alone and then becomes forlorn. A mere letter of comfort from acquaintances or other comrades could greatly encourage him.

Lee Jun-tae  
Usually quiet and reserved, Lee Jun-tae remains the same in prison and is not inclined to engage in dialogues. He does not read much and is also involved in net casting. With a term of five years and a total of six months awaiting trial, it seems his release date will be around mid-May next year.

Chin Byung-gi  
He began working as a cleaner in the carpentry workshop soon after entering prison, making him one of the most comfortable inmates among those incarcerated due to this incident. He has remained in good health throughout his four-year term.

Park Hyung-bok  
Having been sentenced to four years and spent one and a half years awaiting trial, he is expected to be released around November 1932. He naturally possesses a gentle and mild temperament, which allows him to withstand the hardships of prison life while maintaining good health. He has diligently read an English book titled "World Cultural History Series," taking care not to skip any characters since his pre-trial detention, while working at the textile factory.

Lee Byung-ui  
He is expected to be released around October of the same year. His health is poor, and he has developed a growth in his throat, suffering considerably in the heat. He is also weak in his lungs and is currently under medication. His readings include teachings on the Water Cloud Mind Law of the Cheondogyo (Heavenly Way) and Western Philosophy.

Seo Tai-seok  
His health is good, and he is studying Russian, expecting to be released next October.

Lee Yeong  
He will be out in July next year, and his health is reportedly better than while he was outside. He does not read much and is also engaged in work at the textile factory.

Jeon Il  
He is not expected to be released until Showa 10, but due to his previous prison experience, he is managing well and maintaining good health. His studies focus on Russian.

Heo Hyun  
He is in very good health, consistently eating well, and even in prison is concerned about the New Revival Society, organizing meetings to delegate responsibilities while appealing to visitors through correspondence.

Kim Jun-hyeon  
He has not been able to borrow longer works due to the pending judgment, only reading shorter pieces like "Household Daily." His health is reportedly much better than last year, and despite strict lending restrictions that others face for health or dietary reasons, he continues to eat consistently without interruption, maintaining a steady diet.  

Besides these, Hong Myung-hee, Jo Byeong-uk, Lee Won-hak, and Lee Gwan-yong are also reported to be healthy.  

**Summary**

This article presents a detailed look into the lives of Korean political prisoners during the colonial period, documenting their health, reading habits, and social dynamics within the confines of prison. It is noteworthy how some prisoners managed to adapt to their circumstances, engaging in work and even forming social hierarchies despite their dire situations. Historians may find the accounts of their coping mechanisms and political engagement in the face of oppression particularly revealing for understanding resistance during this tumultuous era in Korean history.

兪鎭熙
第一次 공산당 사건으로 4箇年 重刑을 밧고 爾來 鐵窓生活을 하는 兪鎭熙. 박게 잇는 우리의 상상 이외로 신체가 매우 건강하야 楚江漁父 모양으로 날마다 그물(網)만 뜨더니, 요새에는 또 그 그물공장의 잡역이 되엿다 한다. 잡역은 밥도 잘 어더 먹거니와 일도 편하고 그 공장내의 여러 죄수를 감독하다 십히 하는 복역인 까닭에 감옥 죄수로서는 박게 사회에서 은행회사 중역보다 더 조케 녁이는 榮役이다. 그런 중에 그는 감옥 관리의 選出 卽 官選이 안이요 그 공장내 일반 정치범 및 사상범인의 公選에 의하야 피선되엿슴으로 일반 간수와 죄수들은 그를 별명하야 曰 감옥 쏘빼트 그물部 위원장이라고 한단다. 독서는 未決 때에는 經濟學書類를 만히 읽더니, 근래에는 자연과학을 만히 읽는 중, 특히 天文地理學 가튼 것에 치중한다고 하고 안경은 박게 잇슬 때보다도 더 잘 써서 간수들이 『메가네』선생이라고 稱號를 지엿다 한다. 滿期는 명년 2월 중순경.
