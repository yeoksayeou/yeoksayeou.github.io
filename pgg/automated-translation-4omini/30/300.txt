A Study on the Facial Features of the Korean Army through Phrenology  
裵相哲  
Before we can begin, one might laugh at this in the modern era of advanced science and civilization. Observing the likes of phrenology and facial evaluation might seem ridiculous at a time like 1930. Most people will scoff before even reading this writing, but if they were to understand it better, they might find it compelling. However, my intention is not to make this lengthy. In this sweltering heat of 95-96 degrees where sweat is pouring down, two reasons prevent me from accomplishing that: firstly, writing while sweating does not bode well for hygiene; and secondly, if I spend my time sweating, the reader might not be able to thoroughly engage with my scholarly work due to fear of sweating. Therefore, I will attempt to keep my discussions brief.

Even though it seems long, in a world that allows free writing and reading, I can bring this discussion back on track! It’s easy to say that if one becomes too entangled in the web of discussions, it becomes difficult to observe the core. Without any more ado, let us enter the field of phrenological evaluation.

You might find it superstitious. I myself have not studied deeply, but I believe that it transcends mere science. The good name of the learned Mr. Song Jin-woo brings to mind how even modern airplanes can be seen as superstition by those who do not understand their scientific composition. At this point, I must inform you that I intend to accept this invitation for discussion readily. However, just then, I recalled the moment my grandmother saw an airplane flying in the air for the first time and said, "How can a person perform such a trick, that it's all witchcraft and a lure for ghosts?" The memory struck me like a bolt of lightning.

Now, let us lightly scrutinize the phrenological characteristics of Mr. Song Jin-woo.

Is he a person of talent? Through phrenology, can we ascertain whether this person is indeed remarkable?

While one cannot define a person simply, if we examine his sun and moon (the eyes), spirit, eyebrows, voice, and physical form, we may find him as eminent and gifted as a carp that might become a koi, transcending ordinary fry or minnows (vaguely speaking). To put it bluntly, Song Jin-woo is like an ordinary minnow. Yet, if he were to have succeeded in past times (during the era when the White Tiger was smoking), he could well have assumed the position of a state minister or even a prominent councilor. 

If he were to charge into battle, creating a loud noise, and rise in rebellion—a time when the people would rally around him like those of the loyal Three Han states and great figures like a national founder—then such outcomes could be within easy reach. Yet...

=Where is Magu’s Three Mulberry Trees? Rather, the wind drifts through the east sky...=

When analyzed in parts, Mr. Song’s education and vast knowledge strongly hint at an unyielding spirit, not merely bending but rather having the strength to withstand even the most arduous challenges.

As for the learned scholar (also known as the learned doctor...), given that my words have already touched upon this topic, I must speak of his character more seriously. His eyes are those of a hawk, and it is believed that according to phrenological principles, he possesses creditworthiness and is well-suited for high ranks and titles. Yet his smaller mouth seems to signify that he is distinctive; as a man of high stature, the risks are great. 

His sides are filled with deep concentration, with revealing cheekbones showing signs of luck. His understanding is not to be taken lightly. His facial features appear somewhat diminutive, yet upon a closer inspection, it suggests a well-nourished mind. If he pushes hard enough, success beyond his fortunes awaits. 

His fate clearly points to a somewhat turbulent past from which he might reclaim wealth with his own hands. The aspects of his character illustrate patience and courage. However, to deal with difficulties, he must train hard or else face inevitable hardships ahead. After the age of fifty, innate luck may arise; harmony among all will lead to great accomplishments (and funny incidents).

The facial patterns tell a tale of not being able to care for parents long, which could foreshadow a deathly path ahead; thus, he might be destined to face the early loss of one among his kin. It is marked as lean brows foretell the possibility of adopting children. 

His skeletal structure distinctly aligns with the upper-class characteristics expounded by phrenologists, suggesting a potent destiny that might lead him until as late as seventy, although he may not live to see his eighties. 

There is an interesting narrative in the phonological assessment of the glorious elder Han Yong-woon, who will be touched upon next.

At this earliest chance, let’s move on to the understanding of Han Yong-woon’s phrenological insights, noting his impressive courage reflected in his features. His eyes and the characteristic markings around his vision reflect both resilience and bravery, indicating a solid disposition. 

However, at first glance, he may appear rather unassuming (in the heyday of his active political life), but it can be seen that he hardly exudes a commanding aura conducive to politics, though he could be mistaken as someone of religious insight or individualized solitude. 

Upon reaching fifty-three, he may find himself engaging in the monastic life, possibly leading to a life based on offering prayers and seeking alms, revealing a unique perspective.

On examining the unique markings on his right side, it reveals a remarkable but transient fortune in his 27-28 years, pointing to existential pains. His forehead is noticeably small, with a flat impression, bearing letters that symbolically suggest devotion or public morale, and the essence of a firm but mild personality. 

But should the learned gentlemen of the world judge, regardless of how much effort one exerts towards achievements, they could still be regarded as trivial in a grand scheme. "The prose should be revered as mere clouds," they might say. If he were to engage sincerely in politics, it might not bring him success; however, there lies the potential for great renown posthumously. 

Thus, while he may seem an underwhelming presence during his life, his legacy may flourish greatly in death—a reflection of the person he is to become with age (his fate might just overlook the generalities of their lifetime pursuits). 

I could further elaborate, but the biting bugs distract me as I write. So I will pause here.

=When will the flowers blossom? The winds granted in dreams amid the heavens...=

Now, as I consider Mr. Ha Dream before I proceed to voice my assessments about him, I initially ponder his title. This is no ordinary dream; indeed, it possesses a profound character—the figure is resplendent! 

And at that realization, it illustrates that this characteristic too raises questions; what sort of character is this? I don’t need to delve deep into his form or his stature, but it indeed leads me to grant him considerable merit, repeating without hesitation, that such composition defines him as an exceptional "peculiar character.” 

In terms of intelligence and social capabilities, he surpasses average standards. He displays zeal and ambition that might be outmatched in others, but he possesses strong inclinations towards independent thought—one might view his unyielding will as an identifier of nobility. 

Considering the dispositions of hasty men, it’s evident he will dodge pitfalls and remain resolute in his aspirations. With age, he may find himself failing to blend in with the ranks of mediocrity up until eighty or sooner, contending against obstacles.

When it comes to prosperity and wealth, he may struggle, sounding more like a laborer of the fields or a craftsman; yet he may possess the intuition to keep money close at hand, akin to the coins tucked away in coffers.

He presumably leads a family as well, and there could be no concern about strain in family relations or misfortune adversely impacting them in the long term. In retrospect, it appears he seeks noble stability for the future. The worth of his existence seems to transcend transient value.

=Where does this dream lie within fields, where the earth returns to olden truths?= This is what Dream feels.

The risks often lead to eventual fortune, and through crises arise opportunities, signifying an entrustment to the uncertainties of life.

**Summary**

This article delves into the application of phrenology as it pertains to the assessment of notable Korean figures during a period when such practices were dismissed as superstition. The cultural lens through which it analyzes prominent individuals adamantly depicts their potential and personality traits based on their physical characteristics. This mixture of phrenology and political analysis exemplifies the tension between traditional beliefs and modernity during the colonial era in Korea, highlighting the complexities of identity formation in the face of colonial suppression.

Historians may find interest in how these assessments of character reflect broader societal attitudes and struggles, particularly their implications in a colonial context where identity and personal merit were scrutinized against colonial ideologies.

刦運未脫의 宋鎭禹
시럽슨 놈 과학문명이 발달된 이때 더욱 1930의 今日 이게 무슨 수작이여. 觀相法이니 骨相學이니란...하며 우슬것이다. 이 글을 채 보기도 전에 일반은 우슬 것이다 만은, 實은 알고만 보면 반할ㅅ것이다. 허나 長論코저 안는다. 이 95,6度를 쑥 올나 갓다 내려 갓다 하는 더위에 땀을 철철 흘니며 반할 이유를 長論的으로(博士가 쓴다면 博士는 狹士의 반대로 어든 博士요)대체 두 가지로 무모함이 나타나기 때문이다. 一日 땀 빼고 쓰자면 박사 衛生에 損(막걸니나 잇스면 혹 모르지만) 二日 박사가 땀 뺄제야 독자는 張飛-ㄴ가(乞赦 險口之罪)땀 날ㅅ가 무서워 全文을 죄다 못볼 터이닛가 박사의 博學을 博聞식히지 못함이 損해서 짤막짤막 主義(에크머니)를 취하려는 것이다. 이만만 해도 길긴 길지만 허나 자유가 만은 세상에 쓰기도 자유 읽기도 자유닛가. 요만한 것은 다시 문제 외로 하자! 그 소리가 듯기 실타고 뿌리댄다면 안듯는 꼴이 보기 실타고 노리고 볼 수 잇기 때문에 말이다. 여러 말 할 것 업시 骨相評室로 드러가자!
미신이라고 우스시겟지오. 저도 전문학교 낫이나 단여서 과학도 꽤 안담니다 만은, 과학 이상의 과학이기 때문에...거짓말 참말로 혼합된 박사의 말에 古下(號) 宋社長 어른(非婟賊之丈)ㅅ께서는＜100＞ 徹笑를 띄운 낫흐로 『現代式飛行機도 그 組織 된 내용을 모르는 이에게는 미신으로 볼 수 잇지 안슴닛가?』 올아, 이만 하시면 박사의 開口를 용납해 주신다는 한 단서여 해서, 박사는 대번 신명이 낫것다. 허나 그 순간, 전ㅅ날 도라가신 할머니께서 비행기가 공중에 뜬 것을 처음 보시고 「사람이 엇지 그래늬 그것이 요술을 피워 虛神을 잡어 너흔 게지」 하시든 말슴이 뇌리에서 電起함을 깨다럿다.-
그는 그럿타고 하고 宋鎭禹씨의 골상에 나타나는 바를 조금만 그저거려 보자.
인물이냐? 宋鎭禹씨가 骨相學法上으로 보아 人物이냐?

인물이란 과연 한정이 업는 것이겟지만 그의 日月(左右目)과 神氣, 眉, 聲音, 體局을 검진해 본다면 마치 鯉魚가 못 된다면 딸치(鯉魚子)라도 될 것이지 보통의 鮒魚나 송사리류는 훨신 초월한 것이다.(비유한다면 그럿탄 말이다.) 똑바로 말한다면 송사리는 안인 송진우씨이다. 녯날(白虎吸煙時代)만 갓해서 그가 立朝事君케 된다면 崇政大夫 議政府 左贊成 하나는 무난히 할 것이오. 만약 소시랑, 고물개, 몽둥이 가지고 좌충우돌하며 一聲梛子響處에  伏軍이 大起하야 적을 섬멸하고 북을 치며 도라오는 羅末麗初에 태엿던들 推忠定難定社壁上三韓功臣 三重大匡檢校太師礪良縣(礪山의 古號니 氏가 礪山 宋氏-ㄴ가 하고 박사가 추측한 것)開國侯하나는 如探囊中之物할ㅅ번 햇다. 하지만...
=麻姑三桑何處在, 魯連氣像掛東天=
그의 彩霞月角明秀學堂廣大學堂 하나하나 해부적으로 보면 철석가튼 뜻이 百折不屈은 고사하고 千折不屈도 무난 할 것이니 장부의 志槪임을 알 수 잇다.

心德은 대단히 착하고 성품도 상당히 燥한 편이며 고집이 여간하지 안해 보이고 용단이 기맥키며 누구를 한번 거상에 벗게만 보면 어지간해서는 다시 그 눈에 드리기가 어렵ㅅ고 胸中에 太平洋이 드러 안젓스니 汽船煙氣에 가슴 탈ㅅ것도 정한 이치이다.

人中의 廣短 함을 보면 子宮이 썩 시원해 보이지 안으니 晩得의 男이나 榮華를 기대려야 하겟고 전체에 얼골이 붉으니 쇠를 불에 너흔 격이라. 단단히 단련을 밧어야 성공을 할 것이나 또한 그만치 역경에 처할 것은 불가피의 사실이다. 五十부터는 土運의 生을 엇어 萬物이 상생하고 陰陽이 配合하며 聲動天地할 大數가 當來할ㅅ것이다.(에-헤)

懸岸撤手의 韓龍雲
먼저 懸岸撤手가 무엇인가 설명부터 하고-懸岸撤手란 말은 불경의 말이다. 박사보다 15세나 더 자신 당년 52세의 中老人께 어려운 불경을 꺼냄이 엇지 생각하면 不敬일지 모르지만 실은 萬海居士 韓龍雲 선생이 佛道가 안이면 모다 不道로만 알고 게시기 때문에 꺼낸 말이다. 懸岸撤手는 말하자면 설명이 길지만 잘막히 말하자면 百尺危岩의 上에서(그 밋흔 샛파란 江물이 잇고) 손에 붓잡엇든 것도 턱 노아 바려야(노면 떠러저 신단지할 경우) 그것이 남자의 용기라고 하는 말이다. 요만 하면 알 것이닛가. 더 설명 안키로 하고.-

左耳上의 頭中에 刀痕이 과거의 痕運을 말하는 중 27,8세 시대의 비애를 무언중 明言코 잇다.
天庭이 小圓하고 印堂이 平하며 三字의 紋이 잇고 地閣이 小尖하니 불문의 引子이나 다소 공명의 心이 잇슴을 엿볼 수 잇다. 그러나 박사가 斷한다면 아모리 힘써야 世의 공명이 모다 浮雲이다. 「文章當貴總如雲」이다. 따라서 設或 정치계에 나서 활약을 하더라도 萬海씨 자신은 결코 성공치 못하리라.
