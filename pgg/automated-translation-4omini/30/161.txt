**Summary**  

This article announces the establishment of new branch offices in various regions, detailing their locations and leadership. The new branches are set up in Chuncheon, Pohang, Cheongju, and Myongchun, each led by designated branch managers and with appointed administrative personnel. This move reflects the growing organizational network of businesses or publications during this period of Korean colonial history, showcasing the increasing administrative complexity and regional outreach of enterprises under Japanese rule. Historians might find it significant as it indicates the expansion of Japanese influence and local governance structures in Korea at this time.
