발행일	1931년 11월 01일  
기사제목	劇場 紅唇曲, 女俳優들의 이 모양 저 모양 (Theater: The Shape of Female Actors)  
필자	李瑞求  
기사형태	소식 (News)  

"The cute guest has arrived, so I should offer a drink of 'Dorikono'!" he says, pouring a cup of a health tonic that his lover brought for him from a glass cabinet.

“Oh, what can I eat with such things? What if it makes my mouth burn?” 

I remember drinking a cup of sweet water that resembled honey mixed with grape juice.

"Last time, when Teacher Deokgyeon came by, I drank two cups. Why won't you have another cup?" 

When he reached for the 'Dorikono' bottle again, I shook my head.

"That Deokgyeon fellow is quite greedy!"

I chuckled.

“Miss Hyesook is coming over for the board meeting.” 

It must be the servant from the brotherhood. 

"Yes, since there is a guest from Seoul now, she will be here shortly..." 

It’s the usual response. 

The actress Bang Hyesook, the star of the day who has taken the role of a gisaeng (traditional Korean entertainer) in Incheon, left her previous life behind. Those who know him from when he was just a timid minister's daughter from Gilju until now, as a tenacious head, would surely shed tears.

After becoming a gisaeng three times – first in Kaeseong, then in Gyeongseong, and now in Incheon – he has dedicated himself to being a gisaeng. In Incheon, known for hosting foreign clients, he became the social butterfly. Moreover, exposing the misconduct of male members in the Ryongdong brotherhood, he has firmly asserted that "let us manage our brotherhood." His reputation has only risen in recent years. Rumors have recently surfaced of him having an affair with a wealthy man from Mokpo. I have not seen this happen, so I cannot claim it to be true.

"How about performing a play once again?" I said to Miss Seok Geum-seong in the office of Dansungsa (Dangsung Theatre).

“Well... But there’s a baby...” 

Her vague answer wasn’t really a no.

“Even Ai-ris has a baby and her husband Lee Kwang-ryeol is following the performance tour together with the three of them. It seems carrying a baby around is the new trend.” 

I smiled faintly.

“Oh, there’s no end to these ridiculous trends. But our dear gentleman (writer Choi Seung-il) is traveling to the provinces with the dancer Seung-hee (Choi Seung-hee is Seung-il’s sister). He hardly stays at home.” 

It makes me feel a bit lonely. 

If that’s the situation, isn't it even worse now? 

What I meant was for her to join the new cast currently getting ready for a performance at Dansungsa.

“Let’s see... Somehow, it might work out...” 

She replied while gradually retreating backstage. After he disappeared, Park Jeong-hyeon, a senior member of Dansungsa, smiled slightly and said, "It seems like she came here with a promise for the new cast." 

Now, then it seems... I chuckled alone. By the time this goes to the magazine, there is a high possibility that Miss Seok Geum-seong will become a person of the stage again.

"When will the new theater debut take place?" 

A telegram like that came from Lee Gyeong-seol, who is currently touring in Jinju. I must respond so that it reaches him. I am playing it cool because I believe the only way for the debut is to negotiate well.

“I heard that Lee Sun-sang is tired of the life of a female actor; is it time to open a little café or something?” 

I once heard such a remark. When a café called 'Rakwon' opened, women in the area began to show up as regulars, surprising the world while the theater market was going on in Gyeongseong. One night, during a meeting of the group executives, we decided to hold the meeting there, enjoying our food together. At that moment, Lee Gyeong-seol criticized me, saying, “What would the world think if I were here?” 

He pushed for an answer.

“Well, for the time being, it might sell well...” 

I gave a meaningless answer. 

“Ah, men are so foolish.” 

His attitude was quite sincere. It’s true that Lee Gyeong-seol, currently touring in the provinces, feels quite bored and disgusted with the meager points of his own troupe and the unenthusiastic life.

Is it love? Is it money? 

Lee Gyeong-seol, who is now reasonable, is wrestling with how to break through his current stagnant life. Waiting for the new theater debut might be seen as a movement to find a way out.

“Oh, my, our honor has come back.” 

Returning from Shanghai after a long time, Lee Wol-hwa made an elaborate entrance at the door of my small house in Ikseondong.

He has called me “Honor” for the past ten years. Whether it is honor as an advisor or honor as a lover, or honor as a loyal subject, I do not yet know the definition, but still, since it is an honorary position, it is clear that it brings no benefit to me.

“So, what about Shanghai?” 

I inquired, to which he waved his hands.

“Ah, don't ask... Don't ask about anything...” 

He burst into laughter, using the language and expressions of an actor to cover my mouth. He cannot bear to look at the seventy-year-old men remaining in Gyeongseong, coming back from Shanghai two or three times a year. Even this time, he stayed for about a week.

“Ah, the money that Wol-hwa has spent on this trip must be around four hundred won.” 

Someone, envious of the lavish money he spends, remarked.

“What could he do in Shanghai to earn that much?” 

Others worriedly asked. 

“I’ve been doing other business.” 

Some expressed surprise. In any case, having suddenly become a first-class actor with 'Kachusha,' he has now made his way to the pinnacle with actresses Bang Hyesook and Seok Geum-seong in the Towa Association. In Joseon (Korea), where there is no place for him anymore, he found his way to Shanghai. Who expelled him from Joseon? I know well who the person is. It was love. Leaving his homeland due to love would not merely become ordinary materials of a melodrama; it is still, to this day, a veritable truth. A young man, having dedicated his life passionately, has graduated and found employment, only to discreetly embrace the world of illicit love. 

“I cannot make a legitimate wife out of a woman disgraced.” 

He entered into the badly stained world of dark markets. 

“It is indeed true that my body is disgraced, so there is no other way.” 

Without shedding even a tear and without shouting once, he left from Incheon port, boarding a boat to Shanghai, and looking inside his heart, one feels a profound sadness.

“Ah—Shin Eun-bong and Lee Gyeong-hwan have divorced...” 

Such a conversation could be heard among the young fellows. 

“Well, I don't know if that’s true or not. The theater market and theater history are burning with several competitions; since the husband, Lee Gyeong-hwan, is in the theater market while the wife, Shin Eun-bong, is in theater history, that might explain a thing or two.” 

Such conjecturing had filled the minds of the average fans.

Eun-bong! It’s imperative that she decide quickly whether to follow her husband to the theater market or to bring her husband back to theater history. 

I made the final negotiations with Shin Eun-bong in the office of Dansungsa upstairs. 

“You must come to the theater market from now on, starting with this upcoming program.” 

He resolutely answered. 

“Then let’s prepare a welcoming program for you.” 

To implement this agreement, I had written a historical play called “Lovers of the Motherland,” but eventually, Shin Eun-bong did not come, and Lee Gyeong-seol took the lead, receiving applause in her place. 

“It seems that Lee is betraying Shin.” 

Now I started to doubt his attitude too. However, he is indeed a responsible and honorable husband, having remained considerate even of others. 

“How can I treat Lee-sensei? He is my first love and an esteemed mentor. Moreover, he is the husband I care about.” 

She was seen lamenting, having returned to the theater market once more. I received a postcard from Kyungju one time. I understand that even among those who traveled to Busan, Lee Gyeong-hwan, her husband, has decided to withdraw from the theater market and return to theater history. 

“I heard that Eun-bong went back to the theater history.” 

I was informed of that many times. However, I keep hearing her words, “first love... mentor... caring husband.” All I can do is shed tears alone.

In the case of married actors and actresses appearing on stage, especially during such a chaotic phase of Joseon’s theater, anyone with slight pride and conscience can hardly remain in the same place for long.

Duk-gun’s eyes flashed like lightning. He clearly does not greet me with words but communicates through his gaze. The Yeoknyeo (玉女) who appeared in the activity film did not seem to have anything remarkable. Following that, he grabbed the wrist of Ju Samsun (朱三孫) and suffered greatly, but he eventually had to leave the film world. He came to strong Hong-sik Kang of theater history and joined the troupe on his own initiative. 

Thanks to Kang Hong-sik’s insight and passion, he became a notable star. When we meet, we merely exchange nods. My expectations for his future as a stage actor heightened when I saw his voice and appearance while he was performing in the opening of 'Yeoryak Gyeollon' (절약결혼), a Chinese comedy played at Dansungsa. So pleasant was his voice and grace. To this very day, Kang still teaches Jeon-ok long into the night whenever she receives a role, so jealous other actresses can be heard talking. He must be happy. I believe in that. (End)  

**Summary**  

This article provides a detailed exploration of the dynamic world of performing artists during the Japanese occupation of Korea, focusing on the lives and struggles of female actors, especially highlighting Bang Hyesook's career transition from being a minister's daughter to a gisaeng. The narrative outlines the challenges and societal expectations faced by actors, reflecting on personal relationships and the relentless quest for success in the theater industry. It sheds light on the complexities of love, ambition, and the pursuit of dignity in a colonial context, which historians may find particularly significant for understanding the social fabric and cultural dynamics during this turbulent period in Korean history.

젊은이들의 입에서는 이가튼 소리가 들렷다.
『글세 따는 그런지 모르지 연극사와 연극시장과는 경성공연을 가치 여러 가지고 불이 날뜻이 경쟁이 부텃는데 남편되는 리경환은 연극시장에 잇고 안해되는 신은봉은 연극사에 잇스니 그러케 말도 낫겟는걸.』
이가튼 추측이 일반팬의 머리에 가득찬 일이 잇섯다.
은봉군! 연극시장으로 남편을 따라 오든지 숫제 남편되는 리군을 연극사로 도로 다려가든지 양단간에 작정을 하는게 급선무요.』
나는 단성사 이층 사무실 압헤서 신은봉군을 붓잡고 최후의 담판을 지엇다.
『네 꼭 요다음 프로부터는 남편을 따라 연극시장으로 오겟습니다.』
그는 이래 입살을 힘잇게 움즉엿다.
『자- 그럼 신군의 환영프로를 짜아두지.』
나는 이말에 대한 약속을 시행키 위하야서 「祖國의 愛人」이라는 역사극을 집필 하얏섯다. 그러나 오마든 신군은 오지 안코 리경설군이 대역을 하야 손에게 대 갈채를 바덧섯다.
『필경 신이 리군을 배반하는 모양인데...』
인제는 나까지 그의 태도를 의심하얏섯다. 그러나 그는 역시 남의 안해이라는 책임과 의무는 저바리지 안앗다.
『내가 리선생을 엇더케 잇습니까. 그리는 나의 첫사랑인 동시 은사이요. 또 리해잇는 남편입니다.』
울어가며 하소를 하는 것을 보왓다. 지금은 시장에서 다시 뛰어나와 연극사로 갓다. 일전에 경주에서 엽서가 왓다. 부산까지 가서는 남편되는 리경환군도 시장에서 탈퇴를 식혀 연극사로 꺼러갈 것이라고 나는 밋고 잇다.
『은봉이가 또 남편을 바리고 연극사로 갓답니다 그려.』
말하는 사람을 여러 번 보앗다. 그러나 나는
『첫사랑... 은사... 리해잇는 남편.』 이라는 소리가 귀에 남아잇서 혼자 우서바릴 뿐이엇다.
배우 남편에 배우 안해가 극단에서 다니는 이상- 더욱히 조선가티 혼란된 극게에서는 조금이라도 자존심 잇고 량심이 잇는 사람이면 도저히 한 곳에 오래부터 잇기는 어려울 일이다.

땍-군 한 두눈에 「잇트」가 번개가티 발산된다. 그는 분명히 입으로 인사를 하지 안코 눈으로 인사를 한다. 玉女라는 라운규 활동사진에 나타 낫슬때의 전옥은 그다지 취할 점이 업섯다. 뒤를 이어 그는 주삼손(朱三孫)군과 손목을 잡고 가진 고생을 만히 하얏스나 마츰내 하는 수 업시 영화게를 떠나 그는 연극사의 강홍식군의 품을 차자 극단에 슨지가 임의 수개성상...
강홍식군의 식견과 열정에 싸흰 지도에 힘입어 이제는 단연히 빗나는 일개의 헤성이 되야바렷다.
그와 나와는 오직 만나면 목례나 할 뿐이다. 내가 그의 무대인으로서의 장래를 긔대하기는 일즉이 단성사에서 공연하얏든 취성좌의 「절약결혼」이라는 중국 히가극의 서막에 나타나 「서사」를 할 때 이엇다. 그 목소리 그 맵시가 왜그리 조흔지 몰낫다. 강군은 아즉도 전옥에게 배역이 오면 밤을 새워가면서라도 연기를 가르킨다고 다른 녀배우들이 부러워하는 소리를 드럿다. 그만콤 그는 행복하다. 미듬잇다. (끗)＜20＞
