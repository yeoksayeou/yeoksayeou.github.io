Still Prohibited, Thoughts at Year-End When Looking to the New Year  
                         Sin Seok-woo  
Regarding taxation or any other matters, I have refrained from expressing myself, whether in newspapers, magazines, or on the podium, for over ten years now. As long as my own situation is not liberated (解禁), I will remain silent about everything, including matters of taxation. <69>  

 
**Summary**  

The article reflects the author's frustration with ongoing restrictions that prevent open discussion of important societal issues, particularly in the context of the colonial rule affecting Korea in the late 1920s. It hints at a broader sentiment of silence imposed on individuals under colonial governance and raises critical awareness of the struggles faced by Koreans during this period. Historians might find the reflection of internal and external restrictions on public discourse particularly revealing, exemplifying the lack of freedom during Japanese colonial rule.
