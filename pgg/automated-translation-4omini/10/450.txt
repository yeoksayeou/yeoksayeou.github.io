Discussion on the Qingjin Royal Pen  

Seongseongja wishes to express sympathy. In sympathy, it is said that the most "affectionate, resentful, blue-eyed, tearful" words must be used. Is that not the case, whether "we sympathize with those in the same predicament," or "the pain and itch are related," or "the orchids may wither while the osmanthus sighs," or "the death of the fox makes the rabbit grieve," or "the mountain rain at night," or "the empty beams shining under the moon," or "the frost on a chilly morning with rain at night," or "the flowers of dawn and the moon of evening,” or "shoulder to shoulder in the woods," or "gazing at the light by lamp," or "the mutual understanding of those who share similar feelings"? Among all of these expressions, the phrase "惺惺惜惺惺" contains the meanings of "affectionate, resentful, blue-eyed, tearful." However, when the Seongseongja cannot intimately contact another Seongseongja, that sympathy will also be weak.

In the past, the "Xinhai Commotion Society Magazine" advocated the eight characters, "The end of the world is two eyes, the four seas is one collar." Reflecting on that meaning to this day indicates that sympathy lies in close contact, and even if dispersed throughout the ends of the earth, Seongseongja will cherish another Seongseongja. Generally, if a person's whole life curses its end, ultimately wandering at the end of the road, it will be like being a grain of millet in a vast ocean. Whether the observers on the other shore of the sea view that individual with coldness or warmth cannot be easily discerned by the wanderer's assumptions. However, the Seongseongja says that if the wandering individual, with a character of "unskilled and penniless," curses the end of life and wanders at the end, they will ultimately be viewed coldly, but if that wanderer possesses "a single virtue, a single skill," they will certainly be viewed with warmth.

In any country and any time, the status of emperors is only to be discussed "politically and strategically," and it is unnecessary to discuss art. However, the last emperors, who grew up in silk and fine food, spend their long years engaging in court music and soirées, later presenting the legacy of their ancestors to others with both hands, fulfilling the pleasures of wealth and ease represented by the melodies of the posteriors. 

Recently, regarding the last emperor of China, the abdicated Emperor Puyi, it can be said that, at the age of 45, he had no wealth of knowledge regarding politics and strategy. As such, does he spend time in the Rehe Palace with "a few attendants, a few eunuchs, and a few courtesans" while knowing only how to avoid starving his soul, spending time in court music and soirées? Or do they not curse the end of their reign and lament their hopeless fate?

While reading the Zhonghua Yishi Bao, the Seongseongja could not suppress an unbidden tear for the abdicated emperor Puyi. Puyi had a peculiar habit, which was his penchant for calligraphy. He had previously written over 70 couplets, adorning them with dilapidated clothes and a broken hat, accompanied by a eunuch, and roamed various shops to sell those couplets. The following day, Puyi commanded the eunuch to purchase the sold couplets for 100 silver dollars for each pair. Of the 70 couplets, only four or five remained intact in the shops. The shopkeepers, surprised by the sudden rise in value, exclaimed that the ink had barely dried and prices increased a hundredfold. The ends of the couplet papers bore the seal inscribed with the characters "靑巾." The shopkeeper asked the eunuch about the seal's owner. The eunuch replied that the character '靑' was half of '清' and the character '巾' half of the character '帝.' This incident was rapidly propagated throughout the calligraphy community in China.

The Seongseongja remarked that while the act of selling couplets by the abdicated emperor Puyi may be a form of tragic theater, it incites a certain sentiment among Koreans. Should we have a seal named "Yanggjin" in a Korean bookstore, we could compose a couplet:

In tattered clothes and a broken hat,  
Book-selling people have already guarded the nation's rivers and mountains.  
Let me convey a message to Jiangdong:  
Why not learn from the Qingjin?  

**Summary**  

The article reflects on the plight of the abdicated Emperor Puyi of China, expressing sympathy and contemplation on the nature of human connection and cultural identity amid despair. It contrasts the emperor's trivial pursuits with the gravity of his historical significance, suggesting that even in his fall, there are lessons to be drawn for Koreans under colonial rule. Historians may find the portrayal of Puyi's final years to be a stark representation of the decline of imperial authority and the yearning reflections of a lost cultural heritage shared across East Asian societies.

靑巾御筆論(現代支那珍聞), 隨感隨想, 요새 이때에 생각되는 일
						安往居(代惜惺子作)
惺惺子는 惺惺子의게 同情을 表하고자 한다. 同情을 表하는 言詞에 대하야는 무삼 言詞를 使用하여야 가장 「多情的, 多恨的, 多靑眼的, 多熱淚的」, 同情의 言詞가 되겟나냐 혹 「同病相憐」, 혹 「痛癢相關」, 혹 「蘭焚蕙嗟」, 혹 「狐死兎悲」, 혹 「巴山夜雨」, 혹 「空樑落月」, 혹 「霜晨夜雨」, 혹 「花朝月夕」, 혹 「入林把臂」, 혹 「剔燈相看」, 혹 「惺惺惜惺惺」 이라는 諸般表情詞이 잇지마는 그 중에 제일 「惺惺惜惺惺」 이라는 句語가 「多情, 多恨, 多靑眼, 多熱淚」 인 意味를 含有하엿도다. 그러나 惺惺子이 惺惺子를 親近히 接觸치 못한 時는 또한 그 惜惺心이 薄弱할 것이다.
向來 「辛亥唫社雜誌」에 標榜한 8個字, 「萬里雙眸, 四海一衿」 이라는 그 意味를 至今까지 回想할지면 그 感想이 親近接觸에 在할뿐 아니라 비록 天涯地角에 散在할지라도 惺惺子는 惺惺子를 愛惜한다할 거이다. 大抵 人生의 一生이 末運을 呪咀하다가 畢竟 末路에 彷徨할지면 茫茫한 滄海에 그 身勢난 一粒粟과 如하도다. 滄海의 彼岸에 在한 觀望者는 그 觀念이 冷情으로 視할넌지 熱情으로 視할넌지 彷徨者의 推測으로는 알기 어려울 것이다. 그러나 惜惺子는 말하되 彼彷徨子가 「目不識丁, 囊乏一錢」 한 人格으로써 末運을 呪咀하다가 末路에 彷徨할지면 終是冷情으로 看過하겟지마는 만일 그 彷徨子가 「一善一能, 一藝一術」 을 抱有하고저가티 彷徨할지면 熱情으로 看過하겟다 하노라.
母論何國何代하고 帝王의 身分은 「政治上, 度略上」을 論할 뿐이요 藝術을 論할 必要는 업다할지나 每常末路帝王이 紈袴膏梁에 生長하여 朝歌夜絃으로 許久한 세월을 消遣하다가 一朝一夕에 祖宗의 基業을 제2인의게 雙手로 奉獻하고 後庭花한 曲調에 陳叔寶의 富貴를 安樂하는도다.
最近末路帝王의 支那廢帝宣統으로 말할진댄 冲年45세에<84> 政治가 무엇닌지 度略이 무어인지 알를 餘裕가 업도다. 그러면 熱河冷宮에 「幾個侍臣, 幾個閹者, 幾個紅袖」를 다리고 松柏餓魂을 免得한 것만 多幸히 알고 朝歌夜絃으로 세월을 消遣하는가 또는, 그러치 아니하고 末運을 呪咀하고 末路를 悲歎하는가.
惜惺子는 우연히 中華益世報를 閱覽하다가 支那廢帝宣統을 위하야 無名業淚가 한업시 흘으는 것을 禁치 못하엿다.
宣統은 一種好癖이 有한대 그 好癖은 書癖이라 向者에 柱聯 70여幅을 揮灑하여 가지고 獘衣破帽로 閹者一人을 帶同하고 鄕曲窮措大貌樣으로 各店鋪에 다니면서 柱聯을 行賣하엿다. 그 翌日에 宣統은 閹者를 命하여 行賣한 柱聯을 一對에 대하여 洋銀百圓으로 買收케 하엿다. 70여幅인 柱聯이 店鋪에 完存한 것이 4, 5幅에 지나지 못하엿다. 4, 5幅을 保有한 店主는 一時驚怪하야 墨跡未乾에 價增百倍」 라는 評論이 잇섯다. 그 柱聯紙尾에는 靑巾二字로 刻한 篆文圖章이 잇섯다. 店主는 閹者의게 圖章主人을 무럿다. 閹者는 答하되 靑字는 淸字半部이요. 巾字는 帝字半部라고 말하엿다. 그 事實이 全支那書墨界에 一時宣傳되엿다.
惜惺子는 말하되 宣統廢帝의 柱聯行賣는 一時活劇的에 母過하다 할지나 우리 高麗人에 대하야는 一種感想을 惹할만하다. 假令 高麗人書店에 羊巾이라는 圖章...... 繼吟一絶曰
鶉衣破帽鬻書人. 已把山河護國民. 寄語江東懷愍帝 靑衣何不學靑巾.<85>
