필자	崔奎東  
기사형태	논설 (Editorial)  

There may appear great people: thoughts as the year ends and the new year begins  
Choi Gyu-dong  

It is not particularly because it is the end of the year that I have these thoughts, but rather because we are, as we are, reminded of Du Fu's lines: 

"In the northeast winds of this distant land, 

I drift somewhere between the southwest skies."  

Even while living this daily life, there are times that provoke tedious thoughts, leaving me with a feeling as if I am without roots despite my stable position. 

Moreover, recalling the past and considering that there has been nothing achieved, it leads only to a sense of regret. 

In my current position, my only resolve has been to fully devote myself and to serve until death (鞠躬盡瘁, 死而後己); I must exert all my effort and dedicate my body to that aim, as there is no particular reason for my thoughts to stray elsewhere. 

However, all the misfortunes encountered this year have also managed to extinguish our interest. How to tidy up the chaotic thoughts of this bewildered era? Just as a great figure like Socrates emerged in the fading thoughts of Greece, one may hope that a great figure may also arise among us. <67>  

**Summary**  

In this editorial, Choi Gyu-dong reflects on the monotony and feelings of regret he experiences at the end of the year, despite being in a stable position. He expresses a commitment to serving his purpose diligently and contemplates the possibility of a great leader emerging in Korea, akin to historical figures from troubled times such as Socrates. This perspective is interesting in the context of Korean colonial history as it hints at a yearning for leadership and heroism amid national struggles, suggesting a society searching for hope and direction during a turbulent era.

그리고 지난 일을 도라봄애 오직 아모 한 것이 업고 압흐로도 아모런 성취가 업시 공연히 일생을 맛치게 될가 생각하면 다만 애석한 늣김만 더할 뿐이외다.
