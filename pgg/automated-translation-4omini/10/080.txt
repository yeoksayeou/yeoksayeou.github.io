기사제목	圖書舘에서 본 昨年과 今年, 今年一年 朝鮮사람의 生活은 엇더하엿나, -여러 方面으로 모아 본 昨年과 今年의 比較- (A Comparison of the Life of Koreans Last Year and This Year: A Collection from Various Aspects)  
필자	京城圖書分舘 尹芝炳  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

In the center of our Seoul, in the heart of Bukchon, if one were to mention a library where the most Koreans gather, this place would certainly be one. Here, we can ponder how many writings we, as a whole, are engaging with, what sorts of writings we are reading, and who the readers are. Is the statistics we derive from this growing or declining? However, we suddenly cannot ascertain the statistics for the entire year (because the statistical year aligns with general official regulations, running from April of this year until March of the following year). Yet, looking only at the statistics for the month of November, we find that the total number of visitors was 8,824, with 8,537 being male and only 287 female. Compared to last year, that is an increase of 1,243 overall, with 1,160 male visitors and 83 female visitors rising. A curious phenomenon is that only 45 Japanese individuals entered, regardless of whether they paid or not. Typically, the majority of readers are students, and this November was particularly during the time when both public and private high schools were experiencing a mass student strike, so we imagined that this might significantly impact the number of visitors. Still, contrary to our expectations, the reduction in actual numbers was not as severe as anticipated, as compared to 6,501 male student visitors in November last year, we see a mere decrease of 80.

The peak of readership during the year occurs every February, with this November being the following peak; an 80-person decrease is indeed a reflection of some impact, if any. Regarding the tendency of reading, since the majority of visitors are students with promising futures, it is very reassuring that the most commonly read texts are in solid scientific fields. Following that, there is also a notable increase in literary works, and as the recent fields of thought continue to change, categories in politics, law, economics, and sociology are indeed growing alongside the years. However, the least read category is military texts, where if one were to imagine there being an interest in such literature, it would appear to be scant as if no readers were present at all. Viewing the visitors by occupation, this November, including public servants, the military holds the last position among the 68 professions accounted for.

The average daily visitor count is likely around 300 people. If the seating were more spacious, there would undoubtedly be even more individuals wishing to enter, but unfortunately, since the space is limited, it is indeed a profound regret that we cannot accommodate everyone wishing to enter.</156-157>

**Summary**

This article provides an analysis of the visitor statistics and reading trends at a key library in Seoul during the final months of 1927. It highlights an increase in visitors compared to the previous year and identifies the interests of the readers, predominantly students. This information is crucial for understanding cultural literacy trends under colonial rule, as it reflects both the engagement of Koreans with educational materials and the ongoing influence of larger social dynamics such as student strikes. Historians might find it particularly interesting that despite political unrest, the interest in scientific and literary works remained robust.

讀書의 傾向으로 말슴하면 閱覽者의 大多數가 前途有望한 學生인만큼 가장 堅實한 科學的 方面의 書籍이 제일 만히 읽혀지는 것은 퍽이나 든든하고 미덤성 잇는 現狀이라 볼 수 잇고 그 다음으로는 역시 文學方面 그리고 近日의 思想界가 날로 변해 가는 만큼 政治 法律 經濟 社會學의 種類도 해를 따러 늘어가는 것도 사실인데 가장 보는 이 업는 것은 兵書등속으로서 만일<156> 書籍으로 感覺이 잇다면 차저주는 사람 업다고 冊선반 우에서 눈물을 쫄쫄 흘릴 지경이니 보기에도 너무나 가엽게 생각키어집니다. 閱覽者의 職業別로 보아도 今年 11월 중에 官公吏까지 合처서 軍人이 68인 職業別 중에서는 제일 末位를 占領햇슴니다.
