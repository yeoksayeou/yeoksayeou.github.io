기사제목	北京에서, 海外에 잇는 名士의 最近通信 (Recent Communications from Eminent Persons Connecting Beijing and Overseas)  
필자	申釆浩 (Shin Chae-ho)  
기사형태	소식 (News)  

In Beijing, connecting with eminent persons overseas  
Shin Chae-ho  
Dear Brother,  

I cannot recall when and where we tasted such flavor; the place and date escape my memory, yet the moment when you refined your style while I felt shy lingers faintly. Time flows like flesh, and I wonder if my hair has turned gray after 18 years of separation overseas. I wish to know how you have been. The reason I write is that a Chinese friend here, who understands Korean, is requesting the names of the various local branches of the Church of the Heavenly Way (天道敎) and the headquarters of the Youth Party (天道敎靑年黨). I would appreciate it if you could provide this information at your convenience. Perhaps there might be a fortunate encounter waiting for us.  

That is all.  
Your younger brother, Shin Chae-ho  
November 18  

Do you have any news from Brother Pak Gi-dong? Please mention that I am still suffering from an eye illness until now. <127>  

**Summary**  

In this correspondence from Shin Chae-ho to a friend in Beijing, he reflects on the passage of time since they last met and expresses curiosity about his friend's well-being. He requests information about local branches of a religious organization in Korea, indicating his ongoing connections and concerns with his homeland despite living abroad. This correspondence sheds light on the social and cultural dynamics of Koreans living overseas during the colonial period and highlights the interconnectedness of expatriate communities and their ties to Korea.

弟 申釆浩
11월 18일
