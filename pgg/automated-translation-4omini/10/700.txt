What is Evil Virtue?  

My impression of 別乾坤 (Byeolgeon-gon) is extremely simple. I hope it becomes more explicit than before. Since it is originally a magazine of interest, exposing the hidden aspects of life and bringing excitement is a grand and intriguing endeavor. This may sometimes be pointed out as Evil Virtue. However, this tool of exposure functions as a support for the promotion of social evolution. <109>  

**Summary**  

The article expresses a desire for more direct and revealing content in the magazine 別乾坤, while considering the significance of exposing underlying flaws in society, which the author refers to as "Evil Virtue." The writer sees this exposure as essential for social progress and engages in a critique of societal norms during the colonial era in Korea. Historians might find the focus on social evolution and the critique of morality intriguing in the context of the colonial experience, reflecting the struggles of Korean identity and societal values.
