The Young Man's "Oh Well",  
					Stone Fire  
Father: "Hey, you! As a child, shouldn’t you be looking after your aging parents and taking care of home matters? What big things can come from just going around like this? Is that the behavior of a child who has learned the new studies? Is this what modern learning is all about?"  

Young Man: "Oh well..."  

Mother: "Hey, dear. Why are you just stuck on this? Other people graduate from elementary school and go to work in offices, bringing back money. You went to study in Japan and still can’t get a salary, let alone earn even a penny, and you’re just staying at XX company. What a pity."  

Young Man: "Oh well..."  

Wife: "Others buy diamond rings and platinum hairpins. You can’t even buy me a decent umbrella. If I act like others and yearn for small things, it’s really not right. And what about love and ideals? How can we find them?"  

Young Man: "Oh well..."  

Sister: "Dad! They said at school that if I don’t bring in the tuition by tomorrow, they’ll kick me out!"  

Young Man: "Oh well..."  

Son: "Dad, please buy me a toy gun. Even the kid from the neighborhood named Soo-nam bought one. Please, Dad!"  

Young Man: "Oh well..."  

Friend: "Hey, you keep talking about nothing but dying at night. What’s your plan? If you keep crying like this, what good will it do? By the way, lend me 10 jeon for a pack of cigarettes."  

Young Man: "Oh well..."  

Creditor: "Hey, you, you’ve been owing me this 1-year debt for more than a year. What do you plan to do? Let’s settle this today. What are you going to do?"  

Young Man: "Oh well..."  

Editor: "Hey, tomorrow's the deadline, so how are you going to finish the manuscript? If you keep dancing about, it will turn into a big problem."  

Young Man: "Oh well..."  

This is part of the big work "The Young Man's Oh Well" produced by Chosun Jeongsa (朝鮮事情株式會社).<141>  

**Summary**  

This piece captures the frustrations of a young man caught between familial expectations and the harsh realities of modern life in colonial Korea. Each family member expresses discontent and criticism, reinforcing the weight of societal pressures and economic struggles. The repetitive refrain of "Oh well..." embodies a sense of resignation and hopelessness, reflecting the challenges faced by educated youth in the 1920s, a time when many sought new identities in a rapidly changing society. Historians may find the young man's interactions indicative of broader themes in colonial Korea, including the tensions between tradition and modernity, as well as the financial hardships faced by individuals in this period.

아버지 『이놈아! 자식이란 제 부모가 늙으면 공양도 좀 할 줄 알고 집일을 좀 도라보아야지. 그저 그러고만 도라다니면 무슨 큰 수가 나니. 그게 신학문 배운 자식의 행동이냐? 신학문은 다 그런 것이냐?』
靑年 『글세올시다......』

어머니 『이애야. 너는 엇저자고 그러고만 매달려 잇니. 다른 사람들은 보통학교만 졸업하고도 회사관청에 가서 돈만 곳들 버러오더라. 너는 일본유학까지하고 와서도, 그러케 월급자리가 업서서 월급 한푼도 못 밧는 XX사에만 잇스면 엇자잔 말이냐. 참 딱하다.
靑年 『글세올시다......』

안해 『남들은 보석반지 백금비녀도 사줍듸다. 그만한 양산 한아도, 못 사준단 말이요. 내가 남과 가티 허영이나 만한 사람 가트면 참 말 못 되겟소. 그러고서 사랑이니 리상이니는 무엇으로 찻소?』
靑年 『글세요......』

누이 『옵바! 오늘 학교에서 그러는데 래일까지 월사금 안 가저오면 쫏차낸다고 하더만. 응!』
靑年 『글세......』

아들 『압바 나 딱총 사주어. 이 위 수남이도 사가젓더만. 압바 꼭 사주어. 엉!』
靑年 『글세......』

친구 『여보게 자네는 밤낫 죽는소리만 하고 잇스니 엇전 셈인가? 작고만 울고만 잇스면 무슨 소용잇나. 그러고 저러고 담배ㅅ갑하게 돈 10전만 취해주게.』
靑年 『글세......』

債權者 『여보 당신은 밤낫 오늘 내일하고 민 것이 1년이 넘엇스니 엇더케 할 셈이요. 가부간 오늘은 끗냅시다. 엇더케 할테요.』
靑年 『글세요......』

編輯長 『여보 시메끼리는 내일인데 원고를 엇더케 끗내야 되지 안소? 그러고만 춤 춤 하면 또 큰일낫소.
靑年 『글세올시다......』
