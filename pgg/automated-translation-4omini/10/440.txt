Saying of the Notables, Saying of the Great, Reflections and Thoughts, Matters of Recent Contemplation  
						Kim Yeong-pal  

Philosopher: What use could that possibly serve me? If you wish to know, you ought to leave this filthy human realm to comprehend the ultimate philosophy.

Historian: I don't know. What is historical inevitability? No matter how the Western theories shake the world, we have a history of five thousand years, so what do we have to bear? I find that hard to believe.

Literary Scholar: Well, of course there is. There must be something that exists, but... (scratches head).

Politician: While people are chattering this and that, we have no choice. We must compromise; without compromise, we cannot do anything. Of course, it is the local elites.

Religious Leader: Absolutely not. That would be a valid point. Nevertheless, for humans, a life of faith is the most important. Especially situated in Korea, (continues speaking).

Educator: Don't say that. It is troublesome. They keep going on strikes so often that I see it as part of the flow of the 20th century. - That is entirely baseless talk. Should an educator really have to step back? What is that? Don’t say such words lightly.

Businessman: I believe the most difficult thing in Korean society is social enterprise. However, our beneficial endeavor needs to be recognized. I get disheartened. - How could that be the case, selfish desire? There might be some misappropriation or fraud, but we... (face turns red).

Novelist: Because of that, something grave is on the horizon. Don't you know? Of course, it is the stagnation of the literary world. Do you not know about Korea's economic situation?

Playwright: Hey, do you have any spirit left? I wouldn't even know what a script is if I didn't know that it existed (mocking). I have no idea (I have no idea. He rolls his eyes and doesn’t turn back).

Film Producer: Yes, that is true. Even if we offered it to the world market, the shabby mountain fortresses, the phoenixes’ crown, and the true identity of monsters, and various other things are still there - money? Of course, it is disparate. It takes two or three thousand won at once - because of that actress? Well, where else is there a female actor like that in Korea?  

 

**Summary**

In this article, Kim Yeong-pal engages various professionals, including philosophers, historians, and politicians, in a dialogue about the state of Korean society in the 1920s. Each character expresses their frustrations and opinions about the issues facing Korea, such as historical contexts, the arts, and social responsibility. Notably, the dialogue reflects the complexities of a colonial society grappling with its identity and resistance to Western influences. Historians might find the commentary on the stagnation of literature and the challenges of social enterprises particularly interesting, as it sheds light on the cultural identity crisis during Japan's colonial rule over Korea.

哲學者. 당신은 그러한 것을 나에게 무러서 무슨 所用이 됨닛가. 만약에 알고 십거든 더러운 人間界를 떠나야 究境의 哲理를 깨닷소이다.
歷史家. 나는 모름니다. 歷史的 必然이구 무엇이구. 西洋學說이 제아무리 世界를 뒤흔든다고 하드라도 5천년 歷史를 가진 우리야 當할나구? 나는 그것을 밋소이다.
文學者. 글세. 勿論 잇기야 잇겟는대 朝鮮固有의 文學? 글세 잇서야 할 것은 할 것이지만......(머리를 긁는다.)
政治家. 요세 이러니 저러니 떠들어두 우리는 별수 업슴내다. 妥協해야지 妥協아니하고는 할 수 업지요 - 勿論 內地兩班들이지요.
宗敎家. 천만에. 그것이야 될 말임니가. 그래두 사람이란 信仰生活이 제일이지요. 더욱이 朝鮮에 잇서서, (說敎繼續)
敎育家. 말마시요. 긔막힘니다. 툭하면 同盟休學을 하니까 20세기 流行으로 나는 봅니다.-그것은 全然無根之說임니다. 敎育者의 處地로 카폐? 葛甫가 다 무엇님닛가. 그런 말은 입 밧 게도 내지마십쇼.
事業家. 朝鮮社會에 잇서서 가장 하기 어려운 것을 나는 社會事業으로 암니다. 그런데 우리들의 이 有益한 事業을 알아주어야지요. 寒心함니다.-그럴 리가 잇나요, 私慾이라니. 혹 잇기는 橫領詐欺가 잇지만 우리야.(얼골이 붉다.)
小說家. 체 때문에 큰일이올시다. 모르심닛가? 勿論 文壇의 沈滯이지요. 왜 그럿타니. 朝鮮의 經濟狀況을 모르심니까?
劇作家. 여보 당신은 精神이 잇소? 劇이란 存在도 모르겟는대<83> 脚本이 다 무엇이요. 몰나요.(몰나요. 다라난다. 뒤도 안 도라보고.)
