Necessary for the Purification of Morality: Critique and Requests Regarding Byeolgeongon  
                                By Jeong Jong-myeong of the Kimyo Association  

Since Byeolgeongon is a magazine focused on hobbies and practical benefits, it is unreasonable to request that its content address philosophical or political issues. Therefore, I refrain from speaking on that matter. Although Byeolgeongon has only been established for a year, it has contributed significantly to the purification of our social morals by providing candid and forthright critiques of figures and other satire. This is not only necessary but also gratifying.  

However, as Joseon (Korea) remains Korea and its surrounding environment is different from other countries, considerations should be made when criticizing individuals or satirizing their deeds. We should wield our pens with caution, especially when it comes to criticisms regarding women involved in the early movement of reform. It would be wise for us to exercise restraint in those matters.<110>  

**Summary**  

This article discusses the role of Byeolgeongon in addressing social morals through criticism and satire, emphasizing that while such critiques are necessary, one must also consider the unique context of Korea and approach sensitive topics, particularly those involving women, with care. This reflection on how critiques should be tailored to local customs reveals the challenges faced by Korean intellectuals during colonial rule, hinting at tensions between cultural understandings and the dynamics of colonial criticism.

별건곤은 취미와 실익을 중심으로 하는 잡지이닛가 그 내용에 대하야 사상문데나 정치시사문데를 긔재하야 달나고 하는 것은 도로혀 무리한 요구일 것입니다. 그럼으로 거긔에 대하야는 나는 아모 말도 하고 십지 안슴니다. 별건곤이 생긴지가 불과 일년이지만은 인물비평이라든지 기타 풍자의 긔사를 가장 솔직하고 적라라(赤裸裸)하게 써서 여러 사람의 경성을 만히 식히고 공포를 만히 놋기게 하야 우리 사회의 풍긔숙쳥(風紀肅淸)에 만흔 영향을 준 것은 절대로 필요하고 또 통쾌한 일임니다.
그러나 조선이 아즉 조선이요 주위의 환경이 또한 다른 나라와 다른 터이닛가 사람을 비평한다든지 누구의 비행을 풍자 공격하는 데에도 다소 참작을 하야 이 압흐로는 될 수 잇는데까지 좀 필봉을 나취고 특히 초긔운동에 잇는 녀성(女性)에 관한 긔사에 대하야 만흔 고려를 하야 주섯스면 조흘 것 갓슴니다.<110>
