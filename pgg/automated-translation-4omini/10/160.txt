Mackerel Fishing, Kangwŏn-do Yangyang-gun, Local Color  
						Yangyang Park Kwan-uk  
At the time of the mackerel season, I would like to make a request. A lot of mackerel is caught in Yangyang, along the coast of Kangwŏn-do, and I would like to describe an event from our area.

This happened a few days ago. After eating breakfast, I went out to play in the yard when I heard a hurried sound coming from a distance. Listening closely, the urgent voice was calling: 

"Fisherman, come out, come out!" 

Since I am also a fisherman, I thought, let’s go! At the order of the Governor, all fishermen hurriedly went to the seaside to load the nets onto the boats. The Commander signaled with a blue flag towards the sea, indicating to come out quickly. Our subordinates were loading the nets while singing a song.

"Fisherman, be alert, hey,  
Fish are swimming in the sea, hey!"  

As all the nets were loaded, the Commander waved a signal with the red flag toward the land. That was a signal to pull the nets in quickly. The fishermen, sweating profusely, gave their all to pull the nets, and by the time we reached the land, about eight bushels had been caught from the nets. The boats raised long white flags, signifying that they had caught fish. The Commander frantically waved the blue and red flags, commanding us to haul in the catch to the land. 

Upon receiving the order, we took the scoop nets and cried out again. 

"With the scoop nets, hey,  
With the scoop nets, hey!"  

Then there was something strange happening. The men and women, as well as the young and old from Andong-ri, came out with crooked sticks to scoop up fish. More women than men took part, and there was great activity in gathering fish. Being one of the fishermen, I couldn’t watch where I was going while trying to pick fish. Whether I fell into the water or got muddy, I hardly noticed as gathering fish was so enjoyable.

We made a fuss as if we had done something great. The thought of "makgeolli" (rice wine) made us call out, "Come here, come here!" from all around.

The Commander said if we continued, we would get a round of fresh makgeolli to drink. Washing it down with mackerel soup and a cup of makgeolli was truly a great delight. 

This may also be called a characteristic of our fishing village.

**Summary**

The article describes the experience of fishermen in Yangyang, Kangwŏn-do during the mackerel fishing season. It depicts the team effort of fishermen during a busy day of catching fish, the frenzy of local villagers joining in, and highlights the communal joy celebrated with food and drink afterwards. Interestingly, it illustrates how fishing traditions and community interactions reflect local culture and resilience in a period marked by colonial challenges, suggesting a strong sense of identity among the fishermen despite external pressures.
