OnConcealing Evil and Promoting Good, Critique and Request Regarding the Separate Cosmos  
女子基督靑年會 兪珏卿  

Is it appropriate for me to critique your magazine? As a mere individual, what kind of critique could I possibly make? However, after reading your magazine over the past year, I would like to express my honest feelings on some matters. First, I have taken great interest in the discussions of historical figures and politics which I have not seen in other magazines.

At times, regarding evaluations of individuals, it is important to remember that while we discuss past and present, people are indeed just people and not deities. Thus, everyone has not only strengths but also shortcomings. When it comes to discussing a person's weaknesses, unless the circumstances are such that it serves to admonish the individual for their own good or serves as a warning to a third party, it does not contribute to the happiness of our community to expose their faults and provoke negative feelings among the general public. Moreover, it may inevitably lead to a decrease in your magazine's readership and hinder its reputation. 

As Confucius said, "Conceal the evil and promote the good," and advocating for the Christian principle of loving others, I believe that even if there are countless points of dissatisfaction, recording people's strengths will cause our youth, both male and female, to admire the beautiful aspects of our community and encourage them to advance and strive forward with vitality. I sincerely hope that your magazine's reputation flourishes and that it becomes a publication that brings fulfillment to our youth who are spiritually starving.  

 

**Summary**  

The article expresses a request for the magazine to focus on promoting good and concealing evil rather than exposing individuals' shortcomings, which may harm community well-being and the magazine's reputation. This reflects a broader commentary on the importance of fostering positive discourse among youth, particularly in a time of colonial tension in Korea. Historians might find it interesting as it shows a collective yearning for a more positive representation of society amidst the complexities of colonial rule.
