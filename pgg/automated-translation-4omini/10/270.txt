Let's Properly Implement the Solar Calendar, Thoughts When Looking Forward to the New Year at Year-End  
朴勝彬  
In today's context, saying that we should properly implement the solar calendar seems like a very commonplace and outdated remark. However, in reality, the Korean people still struggle to properly follow it. Among the traditionalists and other less educated classes, the solar calendar is referred to as the "Japanese calendar," and the lunar calendar is seen as the "Korean calendar." As a Korean, it is unacceptable to regard the solar calendar as a holiday, and blindly opposing it is common among those with little education. Even among the so-called enlightened intellectuals, they may talk about the solar calendar in conversations or writings while superficially acting as though they are implementing it, yet in daily life, they do not do so. This contradiction is indeed astonishing. Moreover, when it comes to media organizations or youth societies, they may celebrate events during the solar calendar but then, upon the arrival of the lunar calendar, hold events like kite-flying (紙鳶) rallies or other celebrations, which causes great lamentation.

It has been over thirty years since we began to implement the solar calendar formally from the first year of Gyeonyang (建陽元年). Even today, it seems like we are in the same situation as we were back then. From this, one can infer that the ability to implement even one thing effectively has decreased among the Korean people compared to others. Of course, there are some obstacles that hinder execution, but I believe that even from the seventh year of Gwangmu (光武七年) onward, implementing it hasn’t been that challenging. Even though the past was difficult, it would have been better to uniformly implement the solar calendar from next year onward. There are other various tasks to carry out, but if we fail to carry out something as basic as this, it will likely be difficult to execute other things well. <66> <67>  

**Summary**  

The article emphasizes the importance of adhering to the solar calendar in Korea, reflecting on the difficulties and contradictions faced in its implementation amid traditional lunar calendar practices. The author notes the utopian expectations for complete adherence to the solar calendar but recognizes that entrenched practices and cultural resistance hinder progress. This reveals broader themes within Korean colonial history, illustrating the tension between modernity and tradition, and offering historians insights into the challenges of cultural assimilation and identity during the colonial period.

						朴勝彬
오늘에 잇서서 양력을 잘 실행하자는 말은 너무나 평범하고 시대에 뒤진 말 갓슴니다. 그러나 실제에 우리 조선사람은 그것이나마 잘 실행을 못함니다. 구식가뎡이나 기타 무식계급에서 양력슬은 왜슬이라 하고 음력슬은 조선슬이닛가 조선사람으로서 왜슬은 명절로 녁일 수가 업다고 하고 맹목뎍으로 반대하는 사람이야 다시 문뎨도 업지만은 소위 지식계급의 선진인물들도 입이나 글로는 양력이 올호니 엇저니 떠들며 또 형식으로는 다 실행을 하면서도 실제 가뎡에서는 실행을 하지 안으니 그것이 무슨 모순된 일임닛가. 더구나 언론긔관이나 쳥년단테 가튼데서 양력쓸 때에는 심심히 지내다가 음력슬 때가 되면  쳑사대회(擲柶大會)나 지연(紙鳶)날리기 가튼 대회를 주최하는 것을 보면 참으로 통탄함을 마지 안슴니다. 우리가 형식이나마 양력을 실행한지가 벌서 몃해 임닛가. 건양원년(建陽元年)서부터 치면 삼십여 년이나 되는 오늘날에도 그저 그때나 한가지일 뿐 안이라 엇지보면 도로혀 실행하는 사람이 감축한 것 가트니 그 한가지 일을 본다 하야도 우리 조선사람이 남보다 실행력이 적은 것을 짐작할 수 잇슴니다. 거긔에는 물론 실행하랴 하야도 못하는 난관이 더러 잇슬터이나 내가 광무칠년(光武七年)부터 이래 실행하야 보와도 그다지 곤난한 것은 업습듸다. 과거는 엇지 되엿든 명년부터는 그것이나마 일치하게 실행하엿스면 조흘가함니다. 그외에도 여러 가지로 실행할 일이야 만치마는 이러한 일을 잘 실행하지 못한다면 다른 일도 역시 실행하기가<66> 어려울 줄로 암니다.<67>
