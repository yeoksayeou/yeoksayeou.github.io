**Summary**  

This article announces a prize contest in which participants are asked to identify the location of a current conflict between Japan and China. The rewards include a tabletop clock for the first prize and subscriptions to the magazine for the others. The article also lists winners from the previous month's contest, highlighting popular participants and their voting tallies. It illustrates the engagement of the Korean populace with ongoing political situations during the colonial era, reflecting a blend of curiosity and national identity amidst turmoil.

Historians might find it interesting that even in colonial times, the medium of contests and prizes was utilized to foster engagement among the Korean people, revealing cultural dynamics and responses to colonial pressures in the interwar period.
