기사제목	女學生으로 電車 車掌과 運轉手에게, 讀者欄 (To the Tram Conductor and Driver as a Female Student, Reader's Column)  
필자	XX여학교생 △△石  
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Criticism)  

As a female student, I have been commuting by tram for four years now. Moreover, since it is not in the city but rather out of town, I have to stamp four tickets a day. 

However, when I ask for a transfer ticket and clearly present my ticket, some conductors hand me tickets for the wrong direction, and some even treat me mockingly two or three times when I request for a ticket. When I ask for a ticket, they not only give me the ticket but also take my hand. What could be the reason for this? Does holding my hand give them any satisfaction? 

There are also many drivers who, when handing me a ticket, firmly grip my hand. What is the point of this? Only my feelings get hurt, and I wouldn’t mind cursing them a little. … … 

Such behavior is absolutely intolerable! <30>  

**Summary**

The article expresses a female student's frustrations concerning her experiences with tram conductors and drivers while commuting. She highlights the inappropriate behavior of some male employees, who not only mismanage her ticket requests but also engage in unwanted physical contact. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this piece reveals the everyday challenges faced by women during this era, particularly regarding their status and safety in public spaces, signaling broader themes of gender inequality and societal norms.
