A patient with male pair tenderness and a Cupid syndrome patient – Jo Nam-hee, Park Hee-do (Central Nursery), Ahn Wae-jung (Ewha Women's High School), Kwon Hyuk-che, Shin Woo-young (lawyers), Yoo Il-seon, Kim Jung-gyeon (lawyers), Park Seung-hui. 

Similar patients – Choi Seung-won, Park Seung-mo, Seo Sang-cheon, Seo Sang-seop. 

The cause is the accumulation of subcutaneous fat, which causes the cheeks to bulge and the jaw to protrude, similar to a pregnant woman's belly. If one were to undress and observe, one would find it strikingly similar to Cupid, and the symptoms include difficulty breathing, struggling to move, and excessive sweating, having to carry a cooling fan in summer, while the areas under the armpits and groin become intensely sweaty and irritated. Furthermore, while the stomach shows some fullness, for those who are habitually lethargic, they would be referred to with unattractive epithets such as “the jug of fat” and “plebeian fat,” leading to potential risks of being bullied by those with poor temperaments.

Prescription: 
1. Grams of raw gluten 
2. Grams of lard 
Combine the two and take three times a day, particularly paying attention to food, such as allowing only unnutritious foods like radishes and other vegetables. Moreover, one should eat in moderation and avoid meats, and as a superstition, one should hang a well-dried cod on the wall and cultivate it in water.

Thinness Disease – A Patient Who Looks Like a Dried Fish – A Biological Specimen Patient: Lim Gye, Nam Hyeong-woo, Jeong An-seok, Kang Mai, Shin Seong-kyun, Shin Byeok-cheol, Baek Nam-won, Lee Gi-yeong, Yun Gi-dong, Han Won-bin. 

Similar patient – Moon Il-pyeong. 

This disease is the opposite of obesity, characterized by a lack of subcutaneous fat and an appearance resembling a dried fish. One can clearly understand its structural issues without X-ray examination. 

As for treatment, there are various methods, but without the patient's significant effort, it is hard to treat this disease. Recently, there have been reports of a “subcutaneous air treatment” which involves infusing air into the subcutaneous area to create a puffy appearance; however, this is a temporary method and does not last long. 

Those suffering from this condition typically have nicknames such as “a pitiable donkey” and “a laboratory specimen of biological origin,” making it essential to treat promptly.

Prescription: 
1. Two ounces of lard 
2. Four portions of meat candles 
3. Ten plates of tofu 
4. Three hundred grams of bone broth 

Combine these and take five meals a day. For food, one should eat plenty of greasy foods such as beef, pork, chicken, and broth rich in fat. One must maintain a calm mind, avoiding haste and not angering one’s spouse, engage in happy conversations, and refrain from socializing with thin people. 

Anal Gas Proliferation Syndrome – Patients: Min Daekwon (Dongil Bank), Shin Pil-ho (physician), Shin Woo-young, Park Ho-jin, Lee Gwang-soo. 

Gas is a physiological phenomenon that occurs in the digestive system, emitting ammonia-like odors (commonly known as foul odors), at the same time producing bizarre melodic sounds akin to soprano and tenor. A healthy individual usually defecates around two to three times a day, but exceeding this frequency may indicate a case of “excessive gas release syndrome.” 

Symptoms include incessant gas that occurs despite absence of urgency, especially surrounding stressful situations, causing even more frequent occurrences. If one tries to hold it in, it leads to abdominal distension and a feeling of tension in the lower abdomen, resulting in significant discomfort. 

In any case, this disease is a form of an embarrassing illness, especially for children; if someone is affected in a crowded area, it causes discomfort, leading to a risk of fainting from toxicity due to the bad air they produce. 

If it becomes chronic, the characteristic can be a silent effect where the patient silently releases gas. This could lead to the falsely healthy being labeled as the patient. There is also a phenomenon aptly called the bubble bath phenomenon, where bubbles originate from the bathtub floor, quite characteristic of those suffering from excessive gas release syndrome.

Emergency Treatment: 

Every morning, use a gas vacuum pump to expel all the intestinal gas. Afterward, seal the outlet with a special rubber plug from this hospital's specialty.

Foods to avoid include: rice porridge, barley rice, eggs, beer, soda, and other highly stimulating sounds, such as the sounds from an automatic bicycle.

Toilet Delay Disease - A Patient Who Takes a Long Time to Use the Restroom: Hong Myeong-mak, Kim Jin-kyu. 

This disease is unique as it involves a tendency to take a long time in the restroom. The foul odor in the toilet permeates, taking twenty minutes, and possibly up to thirty or forty minutes, and even an hour comes with leg cramps and a sore backside, resulting in an inability to finish defecating before leaving the restroom. 

Spending an hour on the toilet in 1932 is quite a significant waste of time. For the unoccupied, it may be possible to set up a drink table under their house, but for those who have valuable time, delaying the call of nature must be dealt with properly, or else it would be rather troublesome.

Prescription:
Tiger claw (from certain animals) 1000 grams 
The above prescription should be taken three times a day before meals, and after meals, a suppository (a type of medicinal capsule prescribed by the Modern University Hospital) is to be inserted. 

As for food, only fluids should be taken, while solid substances like pork, high-fat foods, and certain vegetables should be avoided.

Activity Sleep Disorder – Patients: Lee Dun-hwa. 

People who snore during sleep or exhibit unusual movement while sleeping are milder cases, while more severe patients exhibit violent behaviors that could harm others or themselves. 

If not isolated in a sleep room, such patients can disturb others easily and may lead their sleeping children to suffer from convulsions. However, a special trait of these patients is their immunity from theft during the night.

Prescription:
Makgeolli (Korean rice wine) 2000 grams 
Beef bone 400 grams 

Combine the above medication and take thirty minutes before sleep; however, extreme care must be taken as these medications are all of high potency, and overuse could lead to unwarranted chaos at home.

Stuttering Disease – Patients: Ahn Seok-joo, Bang Han-seung, Jeong Eung-jang, Moon Il-pyeong. 

This condition involves difficulty in speaking, resulting in pauses and breathlessness. It is troublesome not just for the speaker but for the listeners as well. In severe cases, breaths are held before speaking, leading to instances where only the eyes are dramatically observed.

Therefore, this disease must be treated swiftly.

Prescription:
Cold water 2000 grams 
Sesame oil 20 grams 
Acetic acid 5 grams 
Mint 2 grams 

This prescription should be taken as needed to soothe the chest and lubricate the tongue’s movements. Foods to avoid include spicy or hot dishes.

**Summary**

The article details a peculiar case of free hospitalization of 200 patients suffering from various uncommon ailments at a modern university hospital in Korea, with specific mention of two starkly contrasting conditions: excessive thinness and excessive fat in the patients. It illustrates the attitudes toward health issues during the colonial period, reflecting the medical practices of the 1930s. The use of food prescriptions and superstitions like air treatment highlights the blend of traditional and modern medicine at that time. Historians may find the interplay of medicine and social issues particularly interesting, as it underscores public health challenges in a colonial context where medical knowledge was evolving.
