Literature, Reader's Column

If it is a good year

When azaleas bloom abundantly on Namsan,
It is said that there will be a good year in the village fields.
In the big streets, matchmakers do not cease their activities.
The bachelor from the back house does well with his duties,
This autumn he will ride a horse to get married,
And has well prepared ingredients for the rice cake.
If the wild garlic blooms for a long time,
It is said that there will be a good year in the village fields.
In the big streets, matchmakers do not cease their activities.
The girl from the back house is skillfully weaving,
This autumn she will ride in a palanquin to get married,
And the groom is diligently preparing the fabric.

Morning Songs

In the early morning, like a maiden meeting her beloved,
The wind bows its head,
As the sun sleeps far away,
It flows through the thin outer line,
Awakening the sleeping wind,
The sound of a song! The sound of a song!
My room, drenched in the early frost,
Becomes beautifully adorned so that not even a hole can be seen,
Awakening my soul which was weeping and laughing in a dream,
Praising the true heart of life,
The sound of the maiden's song!
I have awoken! In the dawn!

Ah! What a lovely song it is.
The maiden’s heartfelt desire seeking only the truth of life
Secretly flows forth,
That song! Beautiful, lovely,
O heart that seeks only the truth!

When I play the flute

When I play the flute,
You shall dance for me.
To us departing with the hope of a good day,
Worries wait for us,
Sorrow waits for us too.
Even if there are sad things, let us not
Just frown and scowl at them.
Eh-he-ya! De-he-ya! 
Nil-liri-kung! Nil-li-ri-kung!

If you play the flute,
I will dance for you.
Do not think of the anxious future,
We need to prepare to live well with the coming day.
As we go, let us not be burdened in mind,
And we must pay attention too.
Eh-he-ya! De-he-ya!
Nil-liri-kung! Nil-li-ri-kung!

Pioneer’s Song

The grass grows from the ground
With scythes coming to rise,
Our fields bloom,
Even the great mountains become covered.
Let us go! Let us go!
Until we become fields.
Whether it rains
Or the wind blows,
On days when our limbs are weary,
The hoe holds rust.
Let us go! Let us go!
Until we become fields.

At Kashi Mountain Castle,
The green snake lays its eggs,
In the falling drops of sweat
A fight is breaking out.
Let us go! Let us go!
Until we become fields.

Let the grandchildren
Live upon this land,
Throwing small stones
And rolling pebbles.
Let us go! Let us go!
Until we become fields.

- January 3rd night, composed -

Let’s Go to Work

Comrades, come out.
Let us all go to the workplace,
Like a fiery core in the earth,
With a red fist held high,
Like a crashing waterfall,
Let us come out!

The heart of cold and heat
Even as the cold rain flows like snow,
My burning chest does not cool down.
Even in the blazing fire,
My cold heart will never melt away.
This heat, this cold
Merges and wraps around the heart,
Ah! Where will that lead my soul? <31>  

**Summary**

This collection of poems reflects the themes of hope and perseverance in the face of adversity during the 1930s in Korea. It addresses the seasonal cycles of life, aspirations for marriage, and an eagerness to toil for a brighter future, which resonates deeply within the context of colonial Korean society. This article may interest historians as it illustrates the cultural expressions of daily life and the sentiments during a turbulent era under Japanese colonial rule, revealing the underlying social dynamics and aspirations among the Korean populace.
