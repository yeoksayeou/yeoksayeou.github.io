泰西性的 奇聞集  
						姜博聞  
The saying that "a large nose indicates extreme sexual vigor" appears to apply not only in Korea but also in the West. 

The former King of France, François I (1494-1547), had the nickname "the King with the Big Nose." It is uncertain how large his nose was, but it is undeniable that he was a notorious womanizer. 

The king's view of women was quite simple: women were merely tools for his pleasure. Consequently, the women in his court were all subservient instruments for the king's enjoyment. 

However, since these sexual tools were so submissive, the king eventually became bored. As a result, he secretly began to sneak out into the streets, particularly in love with a few of his courtiers. He sought out not only professional prostitutes but also the wives of respectable households. 

Therefore, it was not uncommon for him to be caught by unsuspecting husbands, leading to some alarming incidents. In one case, a husband nearly brought about the king's demise by brandishing a long knife when he overheard a loyal subject trying to rescue the king. However, the king managed to save himself from that perilous moment.

At that time, in Paris, there lived a woman known as "La Vérine," referred to as "the beautiful Flower" everywhere. As is common throughout history, a woman of such beauty had a jealous husband. This husband, who was a lawyer, had recently suspected that a romantic thief had been sneaking into his wife’s chamber at night. 

One night, he made a serious decision to ambush the thief. When he confronted the intruder, he was shocked to discover that the thief was none other than King François I. Stricken with rage and unable to confront him, he could only bite his lip and grind his teeth in anger. Although the law permitted him to sue for adultery, the lawyer had no choice but to hand over the key to his wife's bedroom to the king and leave his home calmly. 

He wandered aimlessly through the narrow streets of Paris, where he thought, "The fault lies with the king." He attempted to calm down but found it impossible to extinguish the fiery flames of jealousy burning in his heart. 

Eventually, he resolved to seek revenge against the French king, the absolute envoy of God. This demonstrates how jealousy can stir up the greatest human emotions. 

Additionally, one can be astounded by how an ordinary citizen, lacking creativity and imagination, could devise an unprecedented form of revenge that would affect the course of syphilis history. That very night, the lawyer secretly entered the lowest and filthiest house of ill repute in Paris, selecting the dirtiest prostitute available and slept with her. For several days afterward, the great king was in her presence, oblivious to the lawyer's intentions. 

Several days later, the lawyer finally returned home. By then, the syphilis bacteria had already begun to spread in his body as planned. Whistling, he greeted his wife as he entered, then headed directly into her bedroom. The syphilis he had contracted quickly infected his wife, and it did not take long for it to return to the king as well. 

By 1939, one day the king gazed into his wife’s eyes and lamented, "I have sinned in this place," indicating the punishment he felt he was receiving for his infidelity. The disease he had carried spread throughout the French court.

The Terrible Forms of Punishment for Crimes of Passion

In cases where a woman killed her newborn child, the punishment was horrific: 

"Dig a deep trench underground, lay thorns in it, and place the naked woman inside. Then cover her with thorns and dirt, leaving only a breathing tube, and continuously pour milk into it. This way, the woman’s dying agony is deliberately prolonged for several days or hours." 

This was an example of brutal medieval punishments practiced in Germany. 

Additionally, women who killed their newborns and men accused of rape were also punished with piercing stakes. In the case of women, they were put into coffins, with a stake driven through their breasts; for men, they would be stripped naked, bound to four stakes in the ground, and the stake would be driven into their navel area.

In Switzerland, those who violated a virgin were punished by being staked, and if the victim requested it, the stakes would be driven by the victim's own hands. However, if the perpetrator had raped a married woman or someone who had claimed their virginity before, they could be acquitted or receive only a minor punishment.

Such brutal punishments continued, with those who killed newborns being tied up and thrown into rivers from bridges. If they survived until a certain distance from the shore, they would be declared innocent; however, this form of punishment evolved into beheading over time. 

In Switzerland, for the crimes of killing a newborn or bigamy, punishments involved having the perpetrators thrown into the water with animals such as dogs, monkeys, or cats.

Moreover, individuals committing adultery were put into iron baskets, submerged in water, and left to suffer before drowning. Those indulging in bestiality would be burned to death in a similar way.

The history of using castration as a sexual punishment dates back to ancient Egypt, where offenders who violated a virgin faced this fate. In Roman times, virgins were never to be executed, but eventually, executions were allowed, demanding that the executioner check the virginity of the woman before her execution.

In Poland, an adulterous man would have his genitals nailed to a pillar on the street, while he was made to choose between castration and death, being forced to do it himself to save his life. 

In the Netherlands, both castration and burning were options for those caught in the act for three days running, and it was up to the executioner to choose.

During the 13th century in France, customary laws regarding adulterers included such severe punishments as castration for the first offense, outright removal of genitals for the second, and burning at the stake for a third offense. The same applied to women.

Lastly, one of the most bizarre forms of sexual punishment was recorded around 1400 in one region of Austria, where the judge was mandated to command the executioner's wife to perform acts with the accused. If the accused was sentenced to a fine, the judge's wife must comply. However, if the executioner's wife was displeasing to the judge, he could delegate this responsibility to the clerk or another certain person in court. Should none of them suffice, the obligation inevitably fell back upon the judge, with the entire courtroom compelled to witness.

The previously mentioned François I was fascinated by this outrageous law and inspired to enact a similarly outrageous law, allowing women to sacrifice their honor to save their fathers, husbands, or brothers. What remains unknown is how many women managed to secure the lives of their relatives in that era. <23>

  

**Summary**  

This article discusses bizarre tales from the West, particularly highlighting the sexual escapades of King François I of France and the severe punishments for crimes related to infidelity and sexual misconduct in medieval Europe. It illustrates the extreme lengths to which one lawyer went for revenge against the king, showing how personal grievances intertwined with societal consequences. The narrative also sheds light on the harsh legal systems of the past regarding sexual crimes, revealing stark contrasts to modern views. Historians may find interest in how issues of jealousy, masculinity, and power dynamics are echoed in colonial contexts, reflecting traditional gender roles imposed during Korea's own colonial experience.

녯적 불란서왕「프랑소아」1세(西曆1949-1547)는 별명이 비대왕(鼻大王)이엿다. 얼마나 코가 커서 비대왕인지는 모를 일이로되 여하간 호색대왕인 것만은 사실이엿다.
비대왕 즉 코큰왕의 녀성관(女性觀)이라는 것은 지극히 간단한데 「녀자는 도구(道具) 이한마듸에 그치고마는 것이다. 그럼으로 궁정안에 잇는 녀자란 녀자는 모두가 비대왕에 대하야 종순한 도구들이엿섯다.
그러나 이모든 성적도구들이 너무나 종순하기 때문에 왕도 나종에는 실증이낫다. 그래서 왕은 특히 사랑하는 몃사람의 신하를 다리고 슬금살작 남모르게 가두진출(街頭進出)을 시작하게 되엿다. 직업적으로 매음하는 녀자는 물론 딱딱한 여염집부인네까지 후려내고 도라단엿다. 그러기 때문에 비대왕인 자기를 몰라보는 본남편에게 걸려 경을 잡수신 일이 적지아니하엿다.
어떤 해는 새파라케 노한 한 녀자의 남편에게 길다란 칼의 날카로운 끗을 바다 하마트면 붕어(崩御)하실 것을 왕의 사람살리라는 소리를 듯고 쪼처온 신하에게 구원을 바더 사지를 면하신 거룩한 경험까지 게시엇다. 그런데.
당시 파리(巴里)장안에는 「라·베르」(名花)라는 칭호를 모든 사람에게 듯는「페로니에-르」라는 어엽분 녀자가 잇섯다. 이러한 녀자에게일수록 질투심 강한 남편이 따르는 것은 고금이 매한가지. 그 녀자에게도 이가튼 남편이 잇는데 해먹는 장사까지도 마침으로 변호사엿다.
그런데 이 남편은 몃칠전부터 밤마다 밤마다 자긔의 안해의 방에 어떤 사랑의 도적이 숨어드는 것을 아라채렷다. 그래서 그는 어떤날밤 심각한 결심을 하고나서 도적을 잡기위하야 매복을 하고잇섯다. 그러나 도적과 맛닥듸릴 때 종자(從者)로부터 코큰도적이「프랑소아」1세인 것을 알고 꿈이아닌가하고 놀래일뿐이엿다. 분하고 또 분해서 입솔을 깨물고 니를 갈고햇스나 대항할 도리가 업섯다. 법률에는 간통죄가 잇는 것이지만 변호사양반 할수업시 안해의 침실 열쇠를 왕에게 바치고 초연히 집을 나왓다.
