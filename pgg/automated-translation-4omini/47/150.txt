A Blind Man's Suspicion  
A blind man is standing on the street with his hat tilted back, looking behind him.  
A passerby says, "Hey, if you throw away that hat, you won't be able to see where you're going." The blind man smiles and responds, "If I throw away my hat, it seems you will be the one to go."  

Watching Wrestling with a Blind Man  
A friend says, "Come on. You can't see, so if I poke you in the ribs, just say that guy is good at wrestling." So after watching the wrestling, on the way home, he poked him in the ribs and said, "Wow, that guy is good at wrestling."  

Selling Cucumbers  
Not long ago, a poor gentleman went out with a few cucumbers to sell but couldn’t shout, "Cucumbers for sale." So, he carried them around aimlessly until he entered a neighborhood where a snake merchant was shouting, "Snakes for sale! Snakes for sale!" The cucumber seller thought, "Now it seems it’s time." Trembling slightly, he half-forced himself to follow the snake merchant and said, "Cucumbers, cucumbers." Ha ha, “cucumbers” must be the name!  

A Question  
On a very hot day, after a long sermon at Sunday school, the teacher asked, "Everyone! The sermon is over, do you have any questions?" A student replied, "Yes! Teacher, what time is it now?"  

Arithmetic Problem  
One student went to a store and said, "I want to order: 10 pencils at 2 jeon each, 2 brushes at 8 jeon each, and 4 notebooks at 5 jeon each, how much is it all?" The clerk calculated on an abacus and said, "It totals 56 jeon." The student replied, "Thank you. Now I understand my arithmetic homework," and left.  

Not Speaking  
Jeongdong suggested, "Let’s make a bet that we won’t speak for an hour." Bokdong said, "That sounds good." Sudong added, "If anyone speaks, he is a dog." So, they decided not to speak. At that moment, Sudong's father came and said, "Sudong, are you here?" Sudong replied, "Yes." Jeongdong said, "Now you are a dog." Bokdong added, "Only one person is not speaking; the rest of you are all dogs."  

"Please"  
One person went to a vendor selling nuts and asked, "What is that?" The vendor replied, "Please." The person responded, "Thank you, but I can’t eat 'please.'" The vendor said, "It’s not about money, it’s 'please.'"  

**Summary**  

This article presents a collection of humorous anecdotes featuring blind individuals and playful social interactions, showcasing everyday life and the use of wit during the 1930s in Korea. These jokes reflect not only humor but also the societal perceptions and attitudes towards disability and social communication during the colonial period, providing insight into how humor was used in the face of adversity, and may reveal historical context of social relations under Japanese rule.

고깜두- 고깜두
