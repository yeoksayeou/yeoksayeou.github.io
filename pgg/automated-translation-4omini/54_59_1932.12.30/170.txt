臨時自殺  

The world has become unbearable. It is hard to live.  

There is a lack of food, and I am dozing off in a fog.  

Oh, I might as well commit suicide.  

Having made this decision, when the father attempts to commit suicide,  

his son sheds tears and says:  

As a child, it is only natural to dissuade a father from committing suicide.  

However, the reason for discouraging it is quite absurd, saying,  

"Father! If you commit suicide, I will have to bear the burden of that fate, so please, rather have a temporary suicide!"  

<53>  

**Summary**  

The article presents a somber exploration of despair and the twisted logic that arises in desperate circumstances. Through the dialogue between a father and son, it highlights how extreme situations can lead to absurd conclusions about life and death, reflecting the broader societal struggles of the time. Historians might find this piece interesting as it encapsulates the psychological impact of colonial adversity on individuals, showcasing the era's anxieties and the response to suffering in a colonial context.
