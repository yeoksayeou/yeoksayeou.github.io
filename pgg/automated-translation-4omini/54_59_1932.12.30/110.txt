Indian Woman Injects English Woman  

Recently, the movement to reject British rule has been vehemently underway in India, as one can easily understand by reading the newspapers. Now, news has come that Indian people carry around injections of a toxic germ (菌), and when they see a gathering place or a place where many people are, particularly if there is a weak English woman present, they sneak in and subtly inject her, then swiftly escape. The women who have received these injections are unaware and think they merely experienced some unfortunate stinging, and within just twenty to thirty minutes, they die, with the number reaching into the dozens. If Indians do such things, it would be quite troublesome for the British as well.  

Beauty Police Officers of Beiping  

People who are unfamiliar with the current situation in China might easily assume that Chinese women are typically confined to their homes, bound and only able to participate in menial tasks. However, seeing the awakening and active participation of Chinese women recently is truly astonishing. During the Shanghai incident last year, a group of around twenty female students formed a militia and bravely took to the streets, resulting in dozens or even hundreds perishing all at once. Nowadays, in Beiping, the Public Security Bureau is recruiting one thousand female police officers, all with short haircuts, most of whom are studying abroad and speak English fluently, surpassing men in various other knowledge to the extent that both domestic and foreign people are taken aback. Looking at these Chinese women, how could one not feel embarrassed for the Korean women? However, it is not necessary for Korean women to serve as police officers, so that point should be noted.  

Election spending for the Presidency reaches Five Million Dollars  

During the recent presidential elections in the United States, among the Republican, Socialist, and Democratic parties, the competition to elect a presidential candidate was so intense that the total amount spent on broadcasting advertisements by the three parties reportedly reached five million dollars. In the current economic climate, five million dollars is indeed a large sum. However, considering that in Korea, there have been cases where the campaign for a mere district council member involved hundreds of cigarettes, this hardly seems extraordinary.  

Second, Ma Zhan-shan  

In northern Manchuria, the fate of Ma Zhan-shan is uncertain — he is said to be both dead and alive, and people struggle to ascertain the truth of his existence. Recently, it has come to light that Zhou Bingwen was defeated and fled to Arasaka, where it was reported that they captured him and handed him over to the Japanese Manchurian Army, but upon further investigation, it is said that he was released and returned to roam in the area another time. This is reminiscent of the confusion surrounding the reports of Ma Zhan-shan's existence — whether he is alive or dead is unclear. <42>  

**Summary**  

This article details various events, including the disturbing practice of Indian citizens injecting toxic substances into unsuspecting British women, highlighting rising tensions in colonial India, as well as the empowerment of Chinese women illustrated by the formation of police forces and increased participation in public life. Particularly compelling are the comparisons drawn between the progress of women in China and the stagnation of women's roles in Korea, providing insights into the social dynamics and nationalistic movements of the time.
