기사형태	문예기타 (Other Literary)  

The New Year Has Come  

The New Year has come. Those fortunate ones who have good fortune are surely rejoicing and dancing in welcome of this new year.  

However, our Korean people have yet to receive the happiness of good fortune. Given the hardships of life and toil, and the fact that everything about us lags behind others, we cannot exhibit the kind of joyful spirit one would hope for as we enter the new year, akin to the saying "spring has come, yet it does not feel like spring."  

Nevertheless, the arrival of the new year is undeniable. The eight provinces of Gyeonggido (계림팔도) welcome the new year. The sleeping rooster flaps its wings and releases a long, clear call, awakening millions of slumbering individuals, while the brilliantly shining auspicious sun rises in the eastern sky.  

The earth revolves, the world stirs; Korea too feels this movement. Let us not remain dormant like the sleeping ones in winter, but rise together and be lively!  

This year, particularly as the Year of the Rooster—a year of good fortune for our Gyeonggi-do people—is upon us. In ancient times, the figure Liu Kun (류곤) from the Jin dynasty (진나라) is said to have heard the crowing of a rooster and awakened from his sleep, asserting that it was not an evil sound but rather danced joyfully.  

At the dawn of this new year, let us, upon hearing the rooster's crow, shed our worries, lamentations, and degeneracy and rise up with the most positive and joyful hearts to harmonize with that sound and dance. The sound of our Korean rooster is not an evil sound, but surely an auspicious one.  

Friends! Are you listening closely to this rooster's call, or are you not?  

**Summary**  

The editorial reflects on the arrival of the New Year and contrasts the joyful celebrations typically associated with it with the real struggles faced by the Korean people during the colonial period. It urges Koreans to awaken from their hardships and welcome the new year with hope, using the metaphor of the rooster's crowing as a symbol of awakening and auspicious beginnings. Historians might find it notable as it encapsulates the sentiment and morale of Koreans living under Japanese rule, expressing a desire for renewal and resilience despite contextual challenges.

기사형태	문예기타

卷頭 새해는왓습니다
◇
새해는왓습니다 팔자 조흔사람들이야 이새해를 당하야 그 얼마나 깃버서 고 노래하고 춤추겟습니.
◇
그러나 우리조선사람들은 아즉것 불행이조흔복을 타지못하얏습니다 생활에 들이고 일에 들이고 그 외모든것이 남과갓지못한 닭에 새해를 당해도 소위 춘래불사춘(春來不似春)격으로 남들처럼 그러케 깃분긔색이 보이지안습니다.
◇
그러나 새해가오기는 분명이 왓습니다 계림팔도(鷄林八道)에도 새해가왓습니다 잠자던 닭이 두나래를 치고 청아한목소리를 길게 여 잠자던 수천만인간을 우치고 광휘찬란한 서일(瑞日)이 동천에 올낫습니다.＜2＞
지구는 도라갑니다 세게는 움즉입니다 조선도 한 움직임니다 잠자던 사람들은 겨을니하지말고 다가티 여 일어납시다 부즈런이 일합시다.
◇
금년은 특히 닭의해-우리계림나라사람들의 조흔운을 단해임니다 옛적지나의 진나라(支那晋國) 조적(祖迹)이란 사람은 닭의 소리를 듯고 잠자는 류곤(劉琨)을 워이르키며 이것이 악한소리가 아니라하고 이러나 춤을 추엇다고합니다.
◇
우리도 이신년벽두에 첫닭의 소리를 드를 에 락망과 비관과 타락을 다버리고 가장용긔잇게 가장깃분마음으로 이러나 그소리를 맛추워 춤을 추기로합시다 조선의 닭소리도 그러케악한소리가아니요 길한소리일것입니다.
◇
여러분! 이닭의 소리를 잘드르십니 못드르십니.＜3＞
＜2-3＞
