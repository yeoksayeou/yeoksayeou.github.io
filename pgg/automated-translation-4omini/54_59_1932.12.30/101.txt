**Summary**  

The article focuses on the necessity for waitresses in modern dining establishments to provide quick and attentive service, particularly referencing the challenges they face when serving customers. It emphasizes that they are expected to maintain records and respond quickly to customer demands, highlighting the pressures of service work during this time.

This reflects the broader social and economic contexts of colonial Korea, where the expectations placed on workers, particularly in the service industry, may have mirrored the oppressive and demanding circumstances they navigated under colonial rule. Historians may find the article's insights into service work representative of the evolving social dynamics of the era.
