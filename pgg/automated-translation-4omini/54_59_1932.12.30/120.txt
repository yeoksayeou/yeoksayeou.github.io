Gold, Gold, Gold, Gold, Gold: Sea Gold Story  
						Jeong Oesang  
The 1.5 billion people living on Earth can each become wealthy with 4,016,000 yen—if you say this, people will think you're crazy and laugh.  

Really—  
Really—  
It's true.  

“How can that be?”  

It's sudden, but please be patient and quietly listen to this story.  

It was discovered in 1872 by an Englishman named "Harcourt" that gold and silver dissolve in seawater. He analyzed the seawater off the coast of Ireland and found that thirteen milligrams of gold is contained in one cubic meter of seawater, which is called marine gold (海洋金), while marine silver (海洋銀) was discovered earlier by the French chemist "Maragisme."  

Once, a ship sailed the Pacific Ocean for seven years and, while repairing the vessel, accidentally saw that the copper plates had turned a silvery color, confirming the presence of silver dissolved in seawater.  

As a result, scholars from various countries worked hard to analyze seawater, and in some places, only five milligrams of gold was found per cubic meter, while in others, twice that amount was found, with records showing that in one cubic meter of water in the Atlantic Ocean, 267 milligrams (53 money and 4 ri) of gold was contained.  

So, considering how much gold and silver are dissolved in the seawater that occupies three-quarters of the Earth's surface, it is estimated that there are 40 billion tons of gold and 13 billion tons of silver. Isn't that amazing?  

Then, if we convert the value of this marine gold and marine silver into the Japanese currency we use today, the amount is quite enormous; if we distribute it to the 1.5 billion people living on Earth, each person would receive 28 tons of gold and 9 tons of silver, amounting to 40 million yen in gold and 16 million yen in silver, totaling 46 million yen.  

Thus, if we were to completely extract the gold and silver from the ocean, every person in the world could possess gold worth 46 million yen, and the method of extraction is currently under research by renowned scholars. I hope we can succeed together.  

Now, it seems that seawater is not mere water but water filled with gold. The phrase “gold waves and silver waves are calm!” is not a nonsensical expression.  

Now, how much gold is actually out there? Banks have looked everywhere and found only about 30,000 tons of gold and 300,000 tons of silver—it is nothing compared to marine gold and silver.  

If all the marine gold were extracted and made into the pyramidal shape like those in Egypt, the volume would be 220 million cubic meters; if we extracted all the marine silver and made it into the pyramidal shape, the total volume would be about 12,900,000 cubic meters, which is not even the equivalent of a fingernail compared to the pyramids.  

And if we gathered all the gold currently in circulation in the world, this amount would be so minuscule it could hardly be seen; if we were to represent it in terms of volume, it would only be 167 cubic meters—so small that your naked eye might not even recognize it. The silver would amount to a volume of 30,000 cubic meters.  

With gold and silver, your eyes would pop out in disbelief; it cannot exist merely as fantasy. Since ancient times, so-called entrepreneurs have based their efforts on incomplete reports from scholars, waiting for good fortune to establish marine gold extraction companies, which ultimately ended in failure, yet you continue to gaze at the endless waves of the sea and think, "Will this water turn into silver or gold?"  

Please refrain from purchasing shares in such companies or organizing similar ventures just yet. Instead, focus on studying and researching to perfect the method of extraction so we can make this immense wealth of gold the possession of humanity.  

However, I must mention that even if we succeed, certain problems will inevitably arise. This is because, if gold and silver become so abundant, they will naturally lose their value, and commodity prices will skyrocket—eventually, who knows, a single grain of rice might cost hundreds of yen.  

What do you think, everyone? Isn't that right?  

**Summary**  

This article discusses the potential wealth that could be derived from the gold and silver present in seawater, estimating vast quantities containable within the ocean. The author highlights the historical context of discoveries around marine gold, emphasizing the ongoing research into viable extraction methods and expressing a mix of optimism and caution regarding the implications of such discoveries on global economies. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article illustrates the scientific interest and economic aspirations during the 1930s, a time of great socio-economic struggle under colonial rule, reflecting broader themes of resource extraction and wealth distribution.

한번은 태평양(太平洋)을 칠년간이나 도라다닌 배를 수선하랴고 하다가 우연히도 배밋동판(銅版)이 전부은색(銀色)으로 변하여진 것을 본대로 바다ㅅ물속에 은이풀어저잇다구하는 것을 알수가잇섯습니다.
이러구보니 각국의 한다하는 학자(學者)양반들은 긔를쓰고 싸다니며 해수분석에 전력을하엿는바 어곳에서는 일임방메틀에 다섯미리그람(時價一錢强) 어곳에서는 그두배 제일만히 포함되여잇는 레코드는 대서양(大西洋) 한구통이 일입방메틀의 물속에 二百六十七미리그람(五十三錢四厘)의 금이 들어잇는것이엿습니다.
그래서 어사람이 지구표면(地球表面)의 四分의 三이나 차지하고잇는 바다ㅅ물속에 얼마만치나 금과은이 녹아저잇나를 게산해본결과 금은 四百億噸 은은 百三十億噸 여러분하품나오지안습니?

그것을 지구에 살고잇는 전인류 十五億人에게 난호아준다면 한사람암헤올목이 금 二十八噸 은 九噸 이것을 돈으로 저본다면 금갑 四千萬圓은갑 十六萬圓 합게四千六萬圓也라입니다.

「금파(金波)은파(銀波)잔잔한데!」하는말은 엉터리엄는 형용사(形容詞)가아닙니다.
