기사제목	男女平等國과 不平等國 (On the Equal Rights of Men and Women and Inequality)  
필자	講師 金俊淵 (Lecturer Kim Jun-yeon)  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

Recently, the issues of gender equality and women's liberation have been much discussed. Then what is it that makes men and women unequal that we call for equality, and how are women bound that the issue of liberation arises? I will speak on this mainly regarding the "Legal Status of Women."

From a legal perspective, generally, the status of women is inferior to that of men; this can be divided into public law and private law, with examples from Japan that are directly related to us. 

First, in terms of public law, under criminal law and general national law, women are placed in a lower position. Even constitutionally, the imperial throne is inherited by male heirs only, and therefore women cannot inherit the throne. In regard to electoral rights, laws state that women do not possess the right to vote or to be elected in elections for members of the House of Representatives or local governance bodies, such as governments, counties, cities, towns, and villages. 

According to the current House of Representatives Election Law, Article 5: 

"Male subjects of the empire who have reached the age of 25 or older possess the right to vote."

"Male subjects of the empire who have reached the age of 30 or older possess the right to be elected."

Moreover, there are provisions stating that women are not qualified to participate in higher examinations or ordinary examinations, thus they cannot be appointed to positions requiring such examinations. However, it is possible for them to hold positions as teachers or technical officials where the qualifications are not strictly examined. Just from this, we can see that the status of women is indeed inferior. 

Furthermore, there is the crime of adultery. According to Article 183 of the criminal law:

"When a married couple commits adultery, they shall be punished with imprisonment for not more than 2 years."

In contrast, there is no clause regarding the punishment of a husband for committing adultery. This heavily restricts women and exemplifies the preferential treatment provided to men with regard to sexual matters.

Next, I will speak about private law. The law regarding others' wives places women on par with minors or the incapacitated under Articles 14 and below of civil law, categorizing them generally as incapable individuals. Thus, any legal act requires the consent of the husband to be effective. 

Additionally, when it comes to inheritance, women generally lack rights. In circumstances where men are not, women may become the head of the household. However, if the woman enters into a marriage, any rights must be entrusted to the man. This demonstrates that the status of women has markedly been lower than that of men in terms of constitutional, administrative, criminal, and civil law. 

In contrast, the examples of countries like Russia and Germany illustrate gender equality. In Germany, both men and women who reach the age of 20 possess the right to vote, and those over 25 possess the right to be elected. In presidential elections, men and women similarly have equal rights. Other laws also show that there is no distinction between genders and are treated equally.

England, too, generally possesses gender equality in terms of law similar to that in Germany. 

Additionally, in Russia, the legal status of men and women is fully equal. 

Overall, the demand for gender equality includes a call for legal equality, along with calls for educational and occupational equality. There are two forms of equality to consider: political and economic. Even if women achieve political parity with men, if they do not have equivalent economic standing, that equality is only superficial. This can be likened to the situation in democratic countries where workers and capitalists may be politically equal but lack economic equality, rendering that political equality useless.

Therefore, to truly realize gender equality, it is essential to elevate the political status of women while simultaneously enhancing their economic status. It is not sufficient to simply have equality on a legal basis; efforts must be made to specifically support and actively collaborate with women who have lower intellectual and other abilities to substantiate gender equality. In Russia, where the status of men and women is equal, Lenin stated, "One must not rely on the fact that women have attained equality. Women, having historically held a subjugated position, require special protection to ensure that they are truly treated equally to men; thus, efforts must be made towards the development and empowerment of women through special organizations." Hence, in Korea, alongside remedying institutional flaws, it is crucial for women to strive for self-improvement and vigorously promote the healthy development of our nation, as women comprise half of our ethnicity. This necessitates a consideration of women's advancement to ensure that there is intention and effort towards improving their status. 

 

**Summary**

This article discusses the legal disparities between men and women during colonial Korea, particularly focusing on the limitations placed on women’s rights under various laws related to marriage, criminal justice, and civil law. It draws comparisons with European countries, highlighting the significant absence of equality in Korean laws. Historians may find it interesting how the legal framework underpinning gender inequality was entrenched in colonial policies while recognizing emerging conversations about women's rights and roles during this period, which were influenced by global movements for gender equality.

男女平等國과 不平等國
						講師 金俊淵
近日에 男女가 平等이니 女子解放이니 하는 問題가 퍽 만히 떠들게 되엿슴니다. 그러면 男子와 女子는 무엇이 不平等이기에 平等을 부르짓게되며 女子는 어더케 束縛되엇기에 解放問題가 이러남닛가. 나는 이것을 主로 『法律上으로 본 女子의 地位』에 對하야 말슴하겟슴니다.
法律上으로 보면 大體로 女子의 地位가 男子보다 低劣하니 이를 公法上과 私法上으로 나누어 보되 우리와 直接關係 잇는 日本의 例를 들어 말하겟슴니다.
첫재 公法上으로는 刑法上으로나 一般國法學上으로 女子가 低劣한 地位에 잇스니 憲法上으로 보아도 皇位는 男系의 男子가 繼承한다 하엿슴으로 皇位를 女子가 繼承할 수는 업게 되엇고 選擧權에도 衆議院이나 地位自治團體=卽 府, 縣, 市, 町, 村의 議員選擧에 對하야 女子는 選擧權이나 被選擧權이 업다고 거긔 關한 法規에 記錄되어 잇슴니다.
現行 衆議院選擧法 第5條에
帝國臣民인 男子로서 年齡 25歲 以上에 達한 者는 選擧權을 有함.
또 帝國臣民인 男子로서 年齡 30歲 以上에 達한 者는 被選擧權을 有함.
이라 하엿고 또 女子는 高等試驗이나 普通試驗에는 應할 資格이 업다고 規程되엇슴으로 採用上試驗을 必要로 하는 官吏에는 任用될 수 업슴니다. 그러나 敎員이나 技術官 가튼=任用資格을 嚴重히 보지 안는 것은 될 수 잇슴니다. 이 우만 보아도 女子의 地位가 低劣하게 되엇스며

女子난 姦通罪로
또 刑法上으로는 姦通罪라는 것이 잇슴니다. 刑法 第183條에
『有夫婦가 姦通할 때에는 2年 以下의 懲役에 處함』이라는 條文이 잇서서 女子에게 對한 結婚 後의 拘束이 퍽＜110＞-嚴重하나 男子에게는
『有妻의 夫가 姦通할 때는 懲役에 處한다』
는 條文이 업슴니다. 이것은 女子를 甚히 束縛하고 性問題에 잇서서 男子를 如何히 優待한 것인지를 볼 수 잇슴니다.
다음 私法上으로 이약이 하겟슴니다. 남의 妻된 女子는 民法 第14條 以下의 規程과 가티 未成年者나 無能力者와 가티 一般的으로 無能力者의 地位에 두어서 그가 무슨 法律行爲를 할 때에는 大槪 夫된 男子의 同意를 엇지 안으면 完全한 效力이 잇는 行爲를 할 수 업게 만드럿슴니다.
또 相續에 關한 問題로 보드래도 女子는 大槪 權利가 업는데 男子가 다- 업는 境遇에는 女子가 家督相續人이 되어 戶主가 될 수 잇슴니다. 그러나 萬一 그 女子가 入夫婚姻„ 하야 男子를 마저 드리게되면 모든 權利의 行使를 男子에게 委託하게 됨니다. 이 우에 말한 것이 女子의 地位가 男子보다 憲法上, 行政法上, 刑法上, 民法上으로 現著히 低劣하게 된 것임니다.

赤露와 獨逸은 男女平等
이것을 歐洲로 가서 獨逸 가튼데의 例를 보면 女子나 男子가 滿 20歲 以上이 되면 國會議員의 選擧權을 가지고 25歲 以上이 되면 被選擧權을 가지며 大統領選擧에 對하야도 男子나 女子나 다가티 選擧權, 被選擧權을 가젓스며 其他 다른 法律上에도 男女間 조곰도 區別이 업시 同一하게 되어 잇슴니다. 英國도 大槪 獨逸과 가티 法律上으로 男女同等임니다.
露西亞에도 法律上으로 男女의 地位가 完全히 同等임니다.
大槪 男女가 平等을 求하는데는 첫재 法律上 平等을 求하게 되겟고 其他 敎育上, 職業上으로 求하게 됨니다. 또 政治上 平等과 經濟上 平等 두 가지로 볼 수 잇는데 政治上으로 女子가 男子와 同一하게 되어도 經濟的 實力이 男子와 가티 되지 못하면 그 平等은 實相 업는 껍데기 平等임니다. 이것을 比喩하야 말하면 民主主義國家에서 勞働者와 資本家가 政治上은 平等이나 經濟上 平等이 업스면 無所用인 것과 갓슴니다. 그럼으로 女子의 政治上 地位를 向上 식히는 同時에 經濟上 地位도 向上 식히어야 男女平等이 實現하나니 要컨대 制度上으로만 안이라 智識이나 其他 能力이 低劣한 女子에게 特別히 더 힘써서 積極的으로 協力하여야 男女平等의 實을 어들 수 잇슴니다. 露西亞에서는 男女의 地位가 다 平等이나 『레닌』은 말하기를
『女子는 平等이 되엇다고 放任하여서는 안된다. 女子는 從來 奴隷的 地位에 잇섯기 때문에 참으로 男子와 同等케 하려면 特別히 保護하야 女子만의 特別會合으로 女子의 能力發達에 努力하고 女子特別保護의 形式을 取해야 한다』고 하엿슴니다.
그럼으로 우리 朝鮮에서도 一邊 制度上 缺陷을 곳치는 同時에 女子 自身의 向上的 努力이 必要하겟고 또는 民族의 健全한 發達을 爲하야 民族의 半分을 차지한 女子의 充分한 發達을 期成하여야겟고 그리자면 一般的으로 特히 女子의 地位 向上에 對하야 用意하는 바가 잇지 안으면 안되겟슴니다.＜111＞
