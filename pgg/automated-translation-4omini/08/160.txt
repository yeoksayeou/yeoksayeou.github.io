A Plausible Matter

Father: "Ah! You ought to be a bit more mature. Is this how you're going to be every night? Look at Abraham Lincoln in America. By the time he was your age, he had already set up a good basis for independence."

Son: "That's true. And Lincoln had already become the President of the United States and fought the Civil War for the emancipation of slaves by the time he was my age."

A newly returned gentleman, who had studied in Europe and the U.S. for three or four years, came to visit and suddenly embraced the twenty-two-year-old daughter, giving her a kiss. His wife, who was watching from the side, found it so absurd that she exclaimed,

Wife: "Dear, what kind of disgraceful thing is that? She’s not a child, and at her age of twenty-two, how can you just do something like that?"

Gentleman: "What nonsense are you talking about? You can’t reason with old-fashioned women. First, you need to know what’s acceptable. In the West, that’s just how it is."

Wife: "..." (In truth) <73>  

**Summary**

This article discusses the generational misunderstandings between a father and son regarding maturity and historical accomplishments, juxtaposed with a humorous yet critical depiction of a man’s behavior towards a woman in a societal context influenced by Western norms. The responses of the father and son highlight themes of societal expectations and the conflict between traditional values and modern practices. The incident involving the gentleman and the daughter reflects the cultural tensions during the colonial period, suggesting that Western influences were challenging traditional Korean social norms.
