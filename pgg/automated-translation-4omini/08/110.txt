잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1927년 08월 17일  
기사제목	장마·洪水·死傷 그 때에 우리는 이렇케 活動하엿다, 救護所까지 잠겨서 決死隊組織 (The Monsoon, Flood, and Deaths: How We Worked During That Time, Organizing a Death-Defying Rescue Team)  
필자	朴敦緖  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

When I recall the flood, the memories of the massive flood from two years ago spring to mind. It was probably on the afternoon of July 13 of that year. The heavy rain had been incessant, the Han River was rising, and reports in the special editions of newspapers indicated a water level of 34 to 35 feet. I went to Sin-ryong-san with a few colleagues. Already, the vicinity of the Han River was flooded; the West Village No. 2 was completely submerged, and men, women, and children who had lost their homes were shivering on the dike.

We negotiated with the railway authorities and managed to bring in abandoned passenger cars to house those victims who were trembling without shelter.

After a moment, we returned to the city and noticed that the water was continually rising. In the eastern part of the East Village No. 2, houses were submerged, and cries for help from people stranded on rooftops filled the air. We immediately rowed a boat to rescue them, accommodating them in the prepared train cars for the night.

As dawn broke on the 14th, the heavy rain continued, the Han River swelled further, and the danger faced by residents along the river was mounting every second. Realizing the urgency of the situation, the three major newspapers in the city—Dong-A, Chosun, and Shidae—concluded that if we let things deteriorate further while rescuing at our respective organizations, we could not achieve the intended rescue. Thus, they drew lots to decide which newspaper would take charge of the area for relief, with the Chosun Ilbo being assigned to Dot Island (纛島).

As the world indicated, the Dot Island area was the worst-hit compared to other places. The refugees from Dot Island were housed in the Dot Island Public School, where relief efforts were conducted. The rain fell continuously day and night, and by the 16th the water level of the Han River had reached nearly 40 feet. By the morning of the 16th, all of Dot Island was underwater, with boats sailing in and out like wooden ducks. While on the boat, people fished for apples floating on the water. When we arrived at the Dot Island Public School, the rising water had already reached the children’s necks and nearly submerged the adults completely.

Faced with this imminent danger, we decided to retrieve a rescue boat. As we approached the Dot Island Public School with the rescue boat, we were met with grave danger! Floodwaters had completely engulfed the Dot Island Public School, putting the lives of hundreds of refugees at stake. Confronted with this reality, we immediately organized a death-defying team. At that point, the Han River’s level was at 43 feet, and our team, taking the ultimate risk, rowed the rescue boat to the Dot Island Public School while dead bodies floated away with the current. We quickly loaded the remaining people onto the rescue boat and transported them across ten ri (approximately 4 kilometers) to the Wŏngsŏm Station where they were sheltered before being moved to Wŏngsŏm Public School. We stayed there for five or six days, cooking and providing food for them.

The flood at Dot Island was the largest catastrophe since 70 years, resulting in hundreds of deaths and villages being completely obliterated. The changes were literally cataclysmic, and the sight of dead bodies and cattle and dogs floating in the flood was horrifying.

Not only was that the case, but the entire city was also shrouded in darkness as electricity was cut off, with tramways out of service for several days.

In the crowded shelters where men and women of all ages gathered, the scenes of families mourning those lost to the flood were heart-wrenching. Among them was a woman who had lost both her husband and child, completely becoming unhinged with grief, alternating between laughter and tears, even causing a fire in the shelter late at night which led to chaos.

However, the response from society was truly remarkable. As nature unleashed its fury, the human spirit showed great resilience. Various civic and youth organizations engaged in relief efforts with unwavering determination, with each person sacrificing their own safety to rescue others, collectively overwhelmed with emotion.

Representatives from various schools sent assistance and relief efforts while even those who seldom cooked came forward to prepare meals. Contributions from various social figures amounting to 28,000 won were raised, along with over a hundred sacks of white rice, demonstrating the communal spirit and fervor during the simultaneous disaster response efforts.

**Summary**

This article recounts the harrowing experience of the author during a devastating flood in Korea two years before its publication. It describes the collective effort of the community to rescue those affected, their struggles to provide shelter, and how society came together in the face of disaster. Historians might find this poignant account insightful for understanding social solidarity and resilience among Koreans during the colonial period, while also highlighting the cruelty of natural disasters and their impacts.

필자	朴敦緖
기사형태	회고·수기

장마·洪水·死傷 그 때에 우리는 이렇케 活動하엿다, 救護所까지 잠겨서 決死隊組織
						朴敦緖
洪水 회고를 하자면 자연 머리에는 재작년 대홍수 때의 생각이 난다. 그것이 아마 재작년 7월 13일 오후인가보다. 연일 내리는 폭우는 조곰도 쉬지 안코 漢江 增水는 시시각각으로 느러서 34척=35척이 되엇다는 보도가 각 신문사 호외로 비빨가티 쏘다지든 때이다. 나는 동료 幾人과 新龍山으로 갓다. 벌서 漢江 부근에는 홍수가 漲溢하야 西部二村洞은 모다 침수되고 집을 일코 쫏기어 난 남녀노소가 제방 뚝에서 떨고 잇섯다. 우리는 곳 철도당국에 교섭하야 폐물된 객차를 끄러다 노코 해는 지는데 숙소 업서 떠는 이재민을 수용하엿다.

잠간 시내로 다시 드러왓다가 나가보니 물은 점점더 늘어 東部二村東 일대에도 집이 잠기고 옥상에서 사람살리라는 哀號聲이 처량하엿다. 우리는 즉시 배를 저어가서 그들을 구하여 준비한 열차 안에 수용하고 밤을 지냇다.

날이 밝아 14일이 되엿스나 폭우는 여전히 폭주하고 漢江은 각각 增水하야 沿江 주민의 위험은 刻一刻 迫到한다. 此 위급을 당한 시내 三신문사 즉 東亞·朝鮮·時代 삼사에서는 이대로 두고 삼사에서 무질서한 상태로 구제하다가는 소기의 구제를 달치 못하겟스니 각각 떼어 맛자하야 삼사협정으로 제비를 뽑아 구제할 곳이 결정되엇는데 그 때 朝鮮日報社에서는 纛島를 맛게 되엿다.＜33＞
세상이 주지함과 如히 그 때 纛島 일대는 타처에 비하야 제일 참해가 심하든 곳이다. 纛島의 피란민은 纛島公立普通學校에 수용하고 구제하엿다. 연일 폭우는 주야 不休하야 16일에는 漢江증수가 근 40척이 되엿다. 16일 오전에 벌서 纛島의 인가가 전부 수중에 들고 배가 집웅으로 포푸리나무 우으로 왕래하게 되여 배타고 가면서 船底에 침수된 林檎을 따먹는 현상이엇다. 피란민이 수용된 纛島公普에 가니 刻刻 증수되는 물은 벌서 아이들께는 길이 넘고 어른께는 불두덩이에 찻섯다. 위험이 경각에 잇슴으로 우리는 곳 배를 되집어 드러가 구조선을 징발하려 하엿다.

우리가 구조선을 어더 가지고 纛島公普를 바라볼 때에는 아-위험하다! 수백 피란민의 생명을 의탁한 纛島公普의 집웅으로 홍수가 꼴닥 넘엇다. 수백 생명은 죽엇구나! 전률할 此사실에 직면한 우리는 즉시 결사대를 조직하얏다. 당시 漢江 증수43척=五六인의 결사대는 최후로 구명선을 저어 纛島公普에 당도하니 시체는 뒤를 이어 떠나려 간다. 즉시 남은 사람을 구조선에 실고 往十里로 건너와서 往十里역에 수용하엿다가 다시 往十里公普로 이전하엿다. 이곳에서 五六일간을 밥을 지어 먹이며 지냇다.

당시 纛島의 수해는 70년 이래 처음되는 대수해니 수백명이 죽고 數村은 흔적 업시 뿌리가 빠저 다라나서 문자 그대로 滄桑之變이엿고 홍수의 광란노도 우에 시체는 물론 牛馬鷄犬 떠나려가며 비명하는 것이 참혹하엿다. 어찌 그뿐이랴. 시내에도 전기가 끈허저서 全市가 암흑하고 전차가 불통하기를 몃칠이엇다.

수용소에는 남녀노소가 복잡한 중에 가족을 홍수에 일코 사라남은 가족이 비애통곡하는 모양은 目不忍見이요 그 중 남편과 자식을 일흔 부인하나는 失眞成狂하야 울고 웃고 별짓을 다하다가 필경 中夜에 수용소에 불을 노아 일대 소동을 이르킨 일까지 잇섯다.

그러나 이에 대한 사회의 구제도 실로 경탄할 만하엿다. 자연이 暴威를 부릴사록 인간의 반발력도 강한 것이다. 각 사회단체, 청년단체에서는 寢食을 닛고 실로 결사적 구제를 하엿스니 이 때 各人은 모다 깁흔 감격 속에서 자기 생명은 내여노코 남을 구제하기에 열광하엿섯다. 시내 각 남녀학교는 대표를 보내 위문 구제하고 평소에 저의 집에서는 밥짓는 근처도 안가든 기생들이 와서 밥을 지어 供饋하엿다. 朝鮮日報社로 드러온 각 사회인사의 동정금이 2만8천 원이요 白米가 백여 석이니 왼 사회는 모다 감격과 열광 속에 싸히엇든 것이다.＜34＞
