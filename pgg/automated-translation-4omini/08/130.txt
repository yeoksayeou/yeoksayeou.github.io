기사제목	義憤公憤心膽俱爽 痛快!! 가장 痛快하엿든 일 (Righteous Indignation and Public Anger: The Most Refreshing Experience)  
저자	羅龍煥 (Ra Yong-hwan)  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

Is it refreshing? It seems that there are hardly any refreshing experiences for Koreans, no matter who you are. In the situation of Koreans being pushed and chased around, what freshness can there be? Up until now, there have been very few times when I have felt refreshed, and I am still hoping for a refreshing experience.

However, even if there has not been an extraordinary freshness, there have indeed been times when there was some degree of refreshment. I mentioned once before in a different context, but at the time of the Gaehwa (甲辰) in the Year of Gye-mi, it was so refreshing to take the Daedong River (大洞江) as the police headquarters and to catch the corrupt official scoundrels and drag them down. The vigor of the common people, who had endured oppression and disdain, rising up together at that time and establishing institutions to properly investigate and persecute governors and magistrates, and also throwing them in the river, was certainly refreshing.

Moreover, even before that time, when the late King Lee (李太王) issued a decree related to the Donghak (Eastern Learning) army, we proclaimed it to the 13 provinces without going through the Ministry of Justice, and, moreover, we took documents that had been used to condemn Daesangsa (大神師) as a heretic and executed them, as well as those used to condemn Haeyulsa (海月神師) under false pretenses, creating quite a refreshing scene of shouting Long Live the Donghak army while freely roaming under the blue sky and bright sun. There was another big refreshing experience after that, but I cannot speak about this one. At that time, it really was refreshing...

My First Refreshing Experience  
崔南善 (Choi Nam-seon)  

It is indeed refreshing when you recognize what has been very frustrating and that cannot easily be understood. This experience is not the same for everyone. I have been trying hard to understand what Korea is, especially the very basics of it, but I have had some difficulties since the original texts are lacking, the artifacts are precious, and Korean history is almost unexplored. I have felt like wandering in a dark wilderness without getting anywhere, oblivious to the fact that no one else seems to be as troubled as me. It means, for example, not knowing the meaning of the word "Korea," how the Korean people originated, what the starting point of Korean culture is and its characteristics—those major issues were completely submerged in darkness for me. Although others have been leading ordinary lives, I have felt such great frustration in not knowing even these fundamental issues that I have lamented over my dullness for a long time. 

After many years of worrying over such issues—about ten years—I finally gained the opportunity for personal reflection during the year of Gi-mi while I was living separately for a long time. I began to organize what I had read and thought about Korea in a concentrated manner. After being engaged in this endeavor for about a year and a half, on the morning of August 24 in the year of Gyeongsin, while practicing my usual meditation in the cell of Gyeongseong Prison, I thought of the notable mountains of Korea (高山, 大山, 名山) and the legends of the national ancestors such as Dangun (檀君) and Gijeol (箕子), realizing similar geographical names correspond with those legends. As a result, I deduced various principles throughout ancient Korea, and through the foundation of this myth, I understood that one became history and another became religion. When I realized this, it was like light breaking through the darkness of night—it was a refreshing sensation seeing a new dawn in the world. 

This psyche is hard to express adequately, but it was clearly a level of refreshment greater than what is ordinarily called refreshment. From the moment I got to grab onto "light," there began a process of substantial understanding of ancient Korea, and not only of Korea, but also of a significant cultural system centered around Korea. I have thought it indeed a significant fact in the history of human culture and essential aspect that academia has neglected all this time. Thinking that it is my mission to manage this abandoned land of academia, I have always lived in some kind of refreshing ease of mind. 

What I Think of as Refreshing Experiences  
韓龍雲 (Han Yong-un)   

When have I ever had a refreshing experience? As a person known for his integrity and sharp debates, I believe I have never encountered anything refreshing in my life. Since I have never experienced freshness myself, how could I say anything about refreshment? 

But the reporter asks, “What about in the books you’ve read?”

So I point to the "Water Margin" (水滸誌) I have on my desk and say, “There are some refreshing moments in this book too. When Wu Song (武松) killed the tiger on the mountain, that was refreshing. And when Lu Zhishen (魯智深) gave a pig's foot to the Zen monks, that was refreshing too.” 

Master Han Yong-un, a disciple of Shakyamuni who strictly forbids killing, expresses the irony of his own humor in saying that he finds the killing of a tiger refreshing, and also the humorous act of giving a pig's foot to the Zen monks, his ease revealing itself. 

"You mentioned the actions of Buddhist monks in the past as refreshing, though they were certainly not young. Do you find the historical figures from the Imjin War (壬辰倭亂) refreshing as well?" I ask.

“Yes, perhaps some of those are refreshing too. There’s the story of a certain Grand Master from those times whose sword light covered the sun as he cut down the invading Japanese general for his men. When the samurai asked him, 'Do you still have treasures in your country?' the Grand Master responded, 'Yes, there is.' 'What is this treasure?' to which he replied, ‘Your head is the treasure.' The samurai was astonished and asked, 'What do you mean?' Thus, he coolly stated, 'If you bring your head to my country, we will give you a thousand bags of gold. Is that not a treasure?’" 

Though this can be deemed somewhat refreshing, compared to mere self-interest in the modern era when a person is shot down ruthlessly on the spot, this hardly reaches the level of refreshment. 

To conclude the interview, I ask, "What about your personal refreshing experiences?" 

He showcases his comedic side and says, “Well, once a long time ago at a certain place, I gave a speech, but I can't quite talk about it!” His expression changes, revealing a hint of worry.  

A Refreshing Event in Modern Korean History  
權悳奎 (Kwon Deok-kye)  

Looking back at the old days, in recent history, the abolition of the Seowon (서원) by Daewon-gun (大院君) seemed very refreshing to me. During that time, Confucianism and Confucian scholars were feared by the common people like idols, but after Daewon-gun assumed control, he swiftly abolished the Seowon. Upon encountering criticism for this, he remarked, “I cannot leave the cave of hypocrisy that relies on selling off the progenitors. Even if Confucius were to be resurrected here, it would not matter.”

This matter prompted thousands of Confucian scholars across the country to gather in Gyeongseong for protests against this decree, but Daewon-gun confronted them powerfully with just a word of rebuke, driving hundreds of Confucian scholars all the way to Mapo using soldiers. Ultimately, he carried out his initial plans to abolish the Confucian elite system, and breaking down the ideology of the yangban (양반) system was indeed a refreshing affair.

The Day I Submitted My Doctoral Thesis  
朴昌薰 (Park Chang-hoon)  

The most refreshing event in my life was when I completed my doctoral thesis. After graduating from the Gyeongseong Medical School, when I became employed as a doctor, I could say with certainty that among my Korean colleagues, there was almost no enthusiasm for academic research. At that time, I began my research by working at the hospital’s research lab during my free hours, even if it may sound like self-praise to some readers, as my co-workers regarded me as a fool.

There were a few reasons for this misunderstanding. Firstly, it may have stemmed from my genuine lack of talent. Secondly, during that time, Korea's educational background made it almost impossible for someone like me to expect to write a doctoral thesis. Thirdly, the circumstances surrounding this issue made it challenging to pursue. However, I completely disregarded all those factors, persevering without compromise to move forward. Eventually, I started working on my doctoral thesis, immersing myself in it for four days and nights without sleep while neglecting eating or resting, and eventually I completed it ahead of others. At that moment, I felt a kind of indescribable refreshment.  

The Two Women Who Were Convicted  
金炳魯 (Kim Byeong-ro)  

As a defense attorney, it is not uncommon to observe refreshing outcomes, whether viewed from a legal perspective or as a means to fulfill the grievances of the parties involved. For instance, I once dealt with a matter relating to a family that had fallen from grace. The family had a former judge during the Korean period named Yi Yong-sik (이용식) whose eldest son passed away young, leaving two grandsons: Yi Hyeong-gu (이형구) and Yi Jeong-gu (이정보). The elder brother, Yi Hyeong-gu, was typically unreliable and indulged in a hedonistic lifestyle. The lands, worth several hundred jeong (천석), that he inherited from his grandfather would have perished within days had they been left in his hands. 

Therefore, the family collectively decided to entrust those lands to the younger brother's wife, Park Jae-ok (朴次玉), and the transfer deeds were made under her name. However, the younger brother Yi Jeong-gu, who was also leading a disordered life, was rumored to be forging his wife’s seal to sell that land. As a result, Yi Hyeong-gu and Park Jae-ok consulted with each other again and annulled the trust, reinstating land ownership to Yi Hyeong-gu's wife, Kim Jin-wook (금진옥). This led to an occurrence where both brothers were labeled as irresponsible and disbelieving individuals, and a property worth over a thousand seoks was transferred to the two women.

Before the transfer deed was processed, Yi Jeong-gu actually forged his wife's seal and, taking advantage of the landlord’s boredom, sold land worth over 100,000 won for just 50,000 won, receiving only a fraction of that amount after all the middlemen got their cuts. 

Zhang Qiang-han (장광한), who initially had malicious intentions to deceive Yi Jeong-gu, fact went ahead and sued the four individuals, Yi Hyeong-gu, Park Jae-ok, Kim Jin-wook, and Yi Jeong-gu, for forgery of documents. However, during the preliminary examination, Yi Jeong-gu was recognized as the only perpetrator for selling the land without consent from Park Jae-ok, and thus he was the only one who was subjected to trial, while the others were discharged.

Later, Yi Hyeong-gu tragically passed away in prison. Afterward, Yi Jeong-gu was tried and found guilty of not being detained. Zhang Qiang-han subsequently filed a civil lawsuit for the annulment of the land transfer deeds from Park Jae-ok to Kim Jin-wook. In the initial trial, Park Jae-ok and Kim Jin-wook lost and appealed; however, during the appeal, they prepared a new deed stating that the transfer to Kim Jin-wook was without charge to show that it was the rightful transfer with the consent of Yi Hyeong-gu's mother.

Afterward, it turned out that since Yi Hyeong-gu had died, the land’s ownership automatically shifted to his real mother. Thus, since Yi Hyeong-gu’s mother did not give consent, the formal document prepared between only Park Jae-ok and Kim Jin-wook became illegal, leading to a criminal case being opened for forging official documents.

When the two women confessed about signing those agreements, the trial judge and prosecutors concluded that it didn't matter who owned the land. The document that they previously signed only had the asset transfer deed without ownership realization, implying that they could not be guilty of forgery. However, I, as their attorney, believed the judgment was unfair and promptly submitted an appeal, arguing that since the land was trust-owned for Yi Hyeong-gu and was later transferred to his wife Kim Jin-wook on request, the finding of guilt was misleading. The appeals court indeed accepted my reasoning, overturning the previous verdict and redeliberating the facts, ultimately acquitting Park Jae-ok and Kim Jin-wook, thus allowing them to see bright days again under the blue sky, and winning civil cases as well. Whether for the parties involved or for me, engaging in a battle of logic against the six judges during the trials and seizing the final victory was incredibly refreshing.  

A Small Refreshing Experience  
朴八陽 (Park Pal-yang)  

- "You are a journalist. Have you ever had any refreshing experiences while working as a journalist? If so, please write something about it."  

I received such a request from the editor-in-chief of Byeolgeon-gon, and I sat back to think. “Let’s see—has there ever been a single refreshing experience during my career as a reporter?” 

I pondered deeply, evaluating my years of work. 

“None.” 

This is a candid confession of mine. Such a statement reflects how thankless our profession can be—responsibility, tension, and stress.
  
Nevertheless, I cannot say that my beloved profession lacks entirety in joy and flavor. There's somewhat of it, but can it truly reach a level of refreshment? 

Reporting the facts about the oppressed and their movements, to a permit-ready extent, is one of the enjoyable aspects of my job. When we cannot report the truths that must be shared with the public, that is where our agony lies. Thus, when we can accurately report the struggles of the oppressed within that allowed range, we feel some refreshing joy. This is a natural sentiment for one who thinks of themselves as one of the oppressed. However, enjoyment and real refreshment are two different matters; it is nice to enjoy, yet it does not feel genuinely refreshing.

A part of the oppressed—acting not in organized rebellion but rather in a hysterical outburst—can sometimes lead to spontaneous violent reactions to unjust oppression. Behaviors borne from "righteous indignation" can indeed evoke a sense of refreshment within anyone involved. 

Reading the social movement history of 19th-century Russia reveals that speeches and actions made by the defendants in courts against the government and authorities evoke a splendid sensation of refreshment irrespective of our perspectives. 

Among the incidents I have reported or witnessed, I suspect many could be refreshing, yet I refrain from elaborating due to censorship issues. Nonetheless, during certain trials, asking for formal respect while refusing to rise in response to questioning or swearing by saying “You!” as a term of address demonstrates a refusal of any submissive power, which could feel somewhat delightful as one reflects.  

A Refreshing Voice  
劉洪鍾 (Ryu Hong-jung)  

As a film commentator, I would like to write about refreshing moments in cinema. Throughout my eight years and a few months of experience watching films, there is no question that the work by the holy master D.W. Griffith titled East Road (東道) provides one of the most refreshing scenes. 

I have narrated this movie for the first instance for the Danseungsa (團成社), next at the Chosun Theatre (朝鮮劇場), and for this third release, due to local tours, I was about to narrate again—yet what a shame! Repeatedly whispering my description while feeling exasperated multiple times ignites a lingering anticipation in my heart.

When the movie screened four times over, the month of March 15, an ardently refreshing scene does indeed come to mind—those who witnessed would well figure out. 

This film portrays the aristocratic lifestyle of American college students and their countryside lives, manifested through a girl named Anna, who, after connects with the debauched man Santa, becomes a tragic victim of false marriage. Eventually, after losing her mother, she finds herself wandering the streets drunkenly. 

As fate would have it, the young gentleman David eventually developed feelings for Anna. However, bewildered by the depths of her past and fearing the world, Anna is unable to accept his love, even with splendid opportunities cascading as her fate compels her actions against the backdrop of dire circumstances.

Griffith heralds an earnest alarm against the chilling realities of interactions between the sexes. What refreshing scenes are captured? The fight over Anna's essence reveals that, despite her tears, the embodiment of this very conflict sheds light on the fate of distress, violence, and the duo's destinies through a tempestuous entry as a metaphorical awakening.

“Why don’t you speak out, Anna? Why didn’t you speak up to clarify and illuminate?” That moment of triumph feels so lush, and I too found joy in sharing such narratives. 

What refreshing moments transcend the emotions, and at the peak of Anna’s actions, an exuberant cheer resonates from all corners of the audience. It reflects the pride of those who resonated with her story, and in moments like that, true refreshment lingers victorious amidst the tales of humanity intertwined in the performances.  

A Night Encounter with Robbers  
申瑩澈 (Sin Yeong-cheol)  

I am unsure of the exact year, but it must have been around twenty years ago on one of the early spring nights. I was about ten years old then. In my village, there were merely ten households, and just beside the First Mountain (第一山) was our home, which at night turned into a rather scary place for a young child.

Late one night, chaos erupted in our small village with a loud gunshot that had everyone trembling in fear—even the children. My young mind quickly connected “robbers” to the commotion.

Yet my father, unfazed, boldly dashed out, standing barefoot and shouting, “It’s robbers!” 

In response, a wave of villagers swarmed over the hill, banging gongs as they charged towards our village. The commotion in our town was deafening.

At this moment, it seemed the band of robbers, realizing they had no choice, fled without stealing anything or harming anyone, darting through the dark as they made their escape.

Thinking back, it was an exhilarating feeling. The voices of the crowd roaring and the sight of thieves making off was etched in my memory, resonant even now. The camaraderie and unity of the villagers creating that chaos to frighten off the armed robbers created an uplifting collective spirit that certainly stays with me.  

Miscellaneous Refreshments  
朴達成 (Park Daseong)  

“Refreshing!” If only I had experienced any in life, short and simple as it is, or if the societal environment hadn’t deprived me of that thrill! Still, I think about the past and the present, summing up comprehensive moments of delight. 

What experiences seem refreshing? Ah, what about Xiang Yu (項羽) torching the Efang Palace (阿房宮) until it burned for three months? Or Wu Zixu (伍子胥) digging up the remains of the Chu King (楚王) and beating them with a lash—what a thrill it evokes upon reflection! 

It's not limited to these. The tale of the sea hero striking the first Emperor of Qin (秦始皇) is exciting, or the decision of Fan Yuchi (樊於期) to cut off his own head in loyalty, and the sight of Zhang Fei (張飛) single-handedly confronting Cao Cao’s army at Changban Bridge (長板橋) evokes a refreshing spirit. And doesn’t the moment when Guo Songling (郭松齡) took Zhang Zuolin (張作霖) resonate deeply in their epic? 

In turbulent times, big calamities always appear refreshing; however, when a major disaster (floods, fires, etc.) happens resulting in the frenzy of urgent newsbulletins coming rapidly, things seem most invigorating.

Yet I have also had my own refreshing ways of feeling, even in nightmares, like when facing adversaries; how thrilling it was to escape a precarious situation by a wisp of fate! 

Grabbing a Gentleman’s Collar  
金末奉 (Kim Mal-bong)  

As a rickshaw puller, I’ve not truly engagingly refreshing experiences to speak of. To be honest, since I’ve been pulling rickshaws for a decade, there was one particular time that I clearly remember when someone from OO-dong kept trying to cut my already determined fare. 

This person would constantly try to reduce what they owed to an unreasonable degree, offering me 30-40 jeong despite the demanded fee being around 10 won. Still, they avoided paying even that. 

Initially, with every ounce of politeness, I begged persistently, “Is there no such thing as virtue anymore? I need to pay for my children to eat,” but this did not sway him. Finally, unable to hold back my frustration, I seized that character by the collar, lifted him, and tightly grasped him around the throat, shouting, “You’re not a human being! I’m a person too!”

Startled by my outburst, he finally recoiled, looking at me with wide eyes. 

“What’s the meaning of this? I’ll see you off tomorrow." 

Seizing the moment, I vividly remembered the anger that nestled in my heart: “If it gets down to it, I will not only harass but really exert pressure on you.” In the hustle of the marketplace, I called out loudly with his name, throwing him into public scorn. 

That night felt so refreshing. Sure, I could chase after my owing fare promptly the very next day! Hahaha…  

The Time I Fled My School on a Palanquin  
趙元淑 (Jo Won-suk)  

My life has been so simple that I cannot recall any of the bigger struggles or anything refreshing. But, I could say that one thing that I felt was refreshing occurred when I ran away from home to pursue my studies in Seoul. 

My hometown is Yang-yang (양양) in Gangwon Province (강원도), where transportation was particularly inconvenient and rather archaic. If male students were not granted the opportunity easily, how could a female student possibly think of going? 

However, for some mysterious reason, my desire to study burned so ardently that I would spend sleepless nights yearning for it. 

Then, on May 14, at the age of fifteen, I resolved to leave, preparing myself in stealth like a fugitive, collecting my belongings without letting my parents, relatives, or any villagers know. I hired a palanquin driver to take me, arriving at the least used boundary between Yang-yang and Gangneung. 

It was one of those perfect spring nights, where the moon was glorious in the sky, and the hills filled with lush greenery, making my heart feel utterly liberated, like an escaped prisoner or a liberated bird. It seemed hard to express just how refreshing an experience that was for me.  

The Day I Rode a Horse  
丁七星 (Jeong Chil-seong)  

You all know that I am a woman with a rather extravagant lifestyle. Thus, there have been many tearful experiences compared to others. However, the most uplifting experience I have ever had is riding a horse when I was seventeen years old while still immersed in the world of flowers and willow branches. 

In those early days, I did not learn to ride my horse out of mere playfulness or for amusement. I saw in motion pictures and novels how foreign women rode out to the battlefield in a manner bolder than any man, and I thought to myself I too would like to ride well like those strong women in order to make a name for myself in Joseon. 

With that mindset, I trained to ride and, within a short couple of months, became quite accomplished. Dressed as a man in men's clothing, I rode around the city smiling joyfully about my newfound skill. Reflecting on that time—the excitement was truly refreshing. Although today it may seem useless compared to the burdens life has placed upon me, the joy I felt from those times still remains alive in memory.  

Summary  

This collection of experiences reflects deeply on what individuals in Korea found refreshing during the period of oppressive colonial rule. Each contributor shares their moments of triumph against adversity, whether through personal realization, social upheaval, or small victories against societal norms. These sentiments and instances reveal the underlying desire for liberation and acknowledgment of their identity, which historians may find significant as they map cultural resistance and resilience during Japanese colonization in Korea.

義憤公憤心膽俱爽 痛快!! 가장 痛快하엿든 일
甲己之年의 二大痛快
羅龍煥

통쾌 말임닛가? 朝鮮사람에게는 너나 할 것 업시 통쾌한 일이 별로 업섯슬 듯함니다. 이리밀니고 저리 쫏기는 朝鮮사람의 처지에 통쾌가 무슨 통쾌겟슴닛가? 아직것 통쾌을 늣긴 적이 별로 업고 압흐로 통쾌를 바라보고 잇슴니다.
그러나 아조 무쌍의 통쾌는 안이지만 다소간 통쾌는 엇은 때가 잇지오. 먼저도 別乾坤에 한번 말하얏지만 甲辰 개회당시 大洞江을 경무청 삼고 탐관오리놈들 막 잡아다 쓰러 넛튼 그때가 무엇보다 통쾌하얏섯소. 압박과 천대를 여지업시 밧든 그들의 니른바 常民 賤民의 무리가 일시에 봉기해 가지고 제법한 기관을 설해 늣코 관찰군수 등속을 잡아다 問討를 하고 또한 江中에 집어넛튼 그 때의 기세야말로 실로 통쾌하얏소.
그보다 선하야 故 李太王이 東學軍 자유신장에 대한 조서가 내리자 그것을 언제 法部의 손을 것칠 것 업시 우리의 손으로 13부에 선포하고 그리고 니여 大神師의 邪道로 몰녀 사형 당한 죄명 실흔 文帖과 海月神師의 左道亂正律로 몰녀 사형 당한 죄명 실흔 文帖을 우리의 손으로 집어내다 불살우고 동학군만세를 高唱하면서 靑天白日의 하에 자유활보하든 때가 제일 통쾌하얏소. 그리고 그 뒤에도 한가지 큰 통쾌가 잇섯슴니다만은 이 통쾌는 말해야 될 수가 업겟지오. 그 때야말로 정말 통쾌하얏슴니다만은...

내가 經驗한 第一痛快
崔南善

자기가 제일 답답한 것이 아라질 때와 도저히 容易히는 알 수 업스리라고 하엿든 것이 아라질 때에 통쾌함을 늣길 때가 잇는데 그것이 사람을 따라서 각각 다를 수 밧게 업다. ＜38＞나는 어찌하야 朝鮮이란 것을 알려하고 특별히 朝鮮의 맨 근본되는 것을 알려 하야 진작부터 노력하엿스나 원래 문헌도 부족하고 유물도 귀하며 또 朝鮮의 史野가 처녀지로 잇기 때문에 밤낫 광야에서 헤매는 것 가틀 뿐이요, 도모지 붓들리는 것이 업서서 남이 답답해하지 안는 일에 남모르는 답답을 가젓섯다. 이를테면 朝鮮이라고 하는 말뜻부터 무엇인지 朝鮮민족의 연원이 엇더케 생기엇는지 朝鮮문화의 출발과 그 특점이 무엇인지 그런 큰 문제부터도 아조 캄캄한 가운데 잠겨잇는데 남들은 尋常하게 지냇드리고 말른지 모르나 이런 대두리 되는 문제부터도 몰라서 되겟느냐 하야 오래 답답한 중에 사라왓섯다. 또 朝鮮역사의 開卷 제일에 나오는 檀君이니 箕子이니 하는 데 대하야도 다수굿하고 시인하는 사람이나 똑똑한 체하고 부인하려는 사람이나 우리로서 보면 맹랑하기는 일반이요 도저히 이성에 만족한 해결을 어들 것갓지 아니하야 퍽-큰 걱정으로 사라왓섯다. 이러케 저러케 이런 문제를 가지고 고심하기를 십여년이 되어도 남더러 말하야 긍정을 엇기는 고사하고 자기 스사로 위로할만한 대답도 엇지 못하는 형편이매 무엇이니 무엇이니 하는 것보다 자기의 魯鈍한 것을 퍽 한탄해왓다. 그래서 작은 득의와 큰 실망의 사이에서 오락가락하는 중 공연한 세월을 보냇더니 지금 당하야는 벌서 10년 갓가운 전의 일이지만 己未年 일로 해서 오래 다른 인연을 끈코 獨處생활을 하는 동안에 자연히 이 때까지 책으로 보고 생각으로 엇든 것을 專一하게 정리할 기회를 어더서 자나깨나 朝鮮에 관한 지식의 통일적 방면, 근본적 방면을 차젓섯다. 그러케하기를 凡 일년반만인 庚申년 8월24일 새벽에 京城감옥 獄窓에서 다른 때와 가티 靜坐法을 행하고 잇는 가운데 朝鮮에 잇는 高山, 大山, 名山에 白字 붓는 일홈이 만코 檀君이니 解夫婁니 하는 國祖들의 전설지에도 그 비슷한 지명이 잇는 것을 생각하게 되어 그것이 기초되어 여러가지로 연역도 하고 귀납도 하고 분류도 하고 종합도 한 결과 朝鮮 고대에 『밝』이라 하는 일대 원리가 잇서서 그것을 기초로 하야 신화도 생기고 신화의 쌍생자로 하나는 역사가 되고 하나는 종교가 된 것을 알게된 때에 캄캄하든 밤에서 白晝가 나온 것가티 온 세계에 새로운 빗을 보게 되엇다. 이 때의 심리는 무엇이라 말하는 것이 적당할는지 모르나 그 가운데는 보통으로 말하는 통쾌 이상의 통쾌가 드러잇슴이 분명하엿다. 이 『밝』이란 손잽이를 붓든 뒤부터는 朝鮮 고대의 상태에 대하야 모르드래도 모르는 대로의 알 듯한 것이 잇고 朝鮮에 뿐 아니라 朝鮮을 중심으로 하는 一大文化系統이 존재한 것을 알고 이것이 실상 인류문화사 상의 중대한 사실이요 요긴한 部面이엇것만은 이때까지 학계가 이를 閑却하는 것이 도로혀 괴이하다고 생각하는 일면으로 변변치 아니한 힘이라도 학계의 버린 땅이 된 이것을 다스리는 것이 내 사명인 것을 생각하고는 항상 일종의 쾌심적 생활을 하고 잇섯다.＜39＞

내가 생각하는 痛快二三
韓龍雲

何時에 맛나든지 강직한 그의 성격과 辛烈한 그의 논쟁이 盛夏에도 凉味를 주는 韓龍雲씨를 맛나니
『통쾌! 내 평생에 통쾌한 일이라고 보지를 못하엿소. 통쾌한 일을 당하여보지 못한 나로서 통쾌에 대한 말을 어떠케 하겟소』
한다. 기자는 다시
『보신 서적 중에서라도...』
하니 그는 案頭에 노힌 水滸誌를 가르치며
『그런 통쾌이면 이 책 속에도 만치요. 武松이가 山上에서 호랑이 따려 죽인 것도 통쾌하고 魯智深이가 禪客들 참선하는 데 가서 禪客의 집에 도야지 고기 문지러 준 것도 통쾌한 일이요.』
韓龍雲師는 살생을 嚴戒하는 釋迦牟尼의 제자인데 호랑이 따려 죽인 것을 통쾌하다 하고 師 자신이 참선을 만히 한 터이면서 禪客의 입에 도야지 고기 문지러 준 것을 통쾌하다 함이 氏의 自在한 풍자해학의 묘미를 발휘한다.
『그 외로 불가의 행적 중에는...?』
하고 무르니 師는 다시
『壬辰倭亂 때에 四溟大師가 劍光이 天日을 가리우고 생명 죽이기를 刈草하듯 하는 倭將 淸正이를 맛나서 淸正이가 뭇기를 『너희 나라에 보배가 잇느냐.』 무르니 大師는 『보배가 잇다.』 『무엇이 보배이냐.』 『네 대가리가 보배이다.』 淸正이도 깜짝 놀라며 『어쩐 말움´냐?』하니 大師는 침착히 『네 대가리를 가저오면 우리나라에서 천금상을 준다 하엿스니 보배가 아니고 무엇이냐.』하엿다는 것이 좀 통쾌한 것이나 近世에 자기주의를 위하야 압헤 사람이 작고 銃殺이 되는데 송장을 드듸고 넘으며 欣然就死하는 것에 비하면 별로 통쾌하달 것이 못되지요』
끄트로
『정말 師 자신의 경험으로는?』
師도 해학에서 차차 본면목을 발휘하야
『내가 평생에 좀 통쾌한 일로는 년전 00館에서 연설하든 때 일이나 그것은 말할 것이 못되오!』
하고 陽明한 그의 얼골에 일편 愁雲이 덥는다.(記者)

博士論文 맛치던 날
朴昌薰

내가 제일 통쾌하게 생각하기는 박사논문 완료하든 때이다. 내가 京城醫學專門學校를 졸업하고 의사로 취직하든 때에 우리 朝鮮人 동료 간에는 學理硏究에 대한 열성이 殆無라고 할 수가 잇섯다. 당시에 나는 (이러케 말하면 독자 중에는 或 自矜이라고 꾸지람 하실 분이 업지 아니 할 터이나) 진찰에 종사하는 시간 외에 여가를 이용하야 병원연구실에 潛在하야 연구를 시작하엿다. 그 당시 朝鮮인 동료들은 나를 밋진 사람이라고 조소하는 사람까지 잇섯스니 그 이유는
첫재로는 내가 淺才鈍腦인 까닭으로 혹 남이 나의 역량을 너무 과소시하엿든 까닭이엇섯겟고.
둘재로는 당시에 박사논문 작성이라든지 논문제출이 전문학교 출신인 朝鮮인에게는 도저히 期望키 難하엿든 까닭이엇섯겟고.
셋재로는 其外의 환경이 이것을 深히 욕망키 어려웟든 까닭이엇겟다.
그러나 나는 이 여러가지를 전혀 不顧하고 不屈不撓하며 전혀 압흐로만 나가려 하엿섯다. 그리하여 나의 기대의 一小 부분인 박사논문 작성에 착수하엿는데 나는 거의 열광적으로 4일간 전후 8주야를 안진 자리에 고대로 안저서 밥먹기도 닛고 자기도 닛고 필경 남보다 압서서 완성하엿다. 이때에 나는 일종 형언할 수 업는 통쾌를 늣기엇섯다.

金炳魯
