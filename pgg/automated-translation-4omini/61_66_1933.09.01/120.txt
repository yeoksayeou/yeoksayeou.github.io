Humor

A person: "Ah! Your lips! Your lips are sweeter than honey!"  
Lover: "Oh no, I just didn't eat any candy when I got home..."  

Judge: "How did you get here?"  
Criminal: "Yes, yes, just for the same reason as you, my lord... I'm just so sorry..."  
Judge: "What do you mean by the same reason?"  
Criminal: "Yes, yes, I was just craving rice."  

Moko: "It’s foolish for a man who doesn’t enjoy night outings to buy tickets to a play or a movie!"  
Mokke: "That’s all right. While I’m enjoying the movie or play, that man is also looking at me, so to speak, that’s part of my job too."  

When a fat man went to a tailor's to get fitted for summer clothes, the tailor asked, "Would you like it made for wearing before your meal or after your meal?"  

Child A: "Ah, my father has bought a new suit... it's truly nice!"  
Child B: "Pah! That’s made by our father; even if it looks good, it was made with very bad materials inside!"  

Personnel Department Chief: "Do you have anyone you know at this company?"  
New Employee: "Yes, the owner here is my uncle's cousin's cousin's husband’s friend and classmate."  

Munjik: "What do you know to come here?"  
That person: "Isn't this the American Consulate?"  
Munjik: "It is."  
That person: "Then I came here to buy rice."

Seo: "I would just go back to sleep to turn it into twenty thousand won."

Reason for Not having a Woman  
Kim: "What is it that you cannot attain? Is there only one woman in the world? If you fall in love, that’s enough..."  
Lee: "No..."  
Kim: "What do you mean no?"  
Lee: "All the things I bought for her were bought on credit, and I still have four months left to pay..."  

Degree of Unhappiness  
-Two middle-aged gentlemen-  
A: "Of course, my wife loves me... but I wish she wouldn’t keep mentioning that ex-husband, Yiyagi, when we're out."  
B: "That’s fine... My wife is still thinking about my next husband, so she often brings him up..." <29>  

**Summary**  

This piece consists of humorous dialogues that showcase various social situations and personal relationships in 1930s Korea. The conversations reflect a mix of trivial concerns, familial connections, critical observations about love and relationships, and societal norms, providing insight into the era's culture. Historians might find the comedic portrayal of personal relationships and societal expectations particularly interesting, as it reflects the complexities of life under colonial rule and the shifting dynamics of social interactions during that time.
