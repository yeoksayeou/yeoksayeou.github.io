懸賞問題  
Who is the culprit who killed the woman "Welson" in the detective novel "The Murder in Room 23"? Is it "Horton"? Is it "Robard"? Please read the text carefully and simply write down the name of the culprit and send it to us (as sending it via postcard may cause processing difficulties, refer to the following conditions and send it as specified).  

Prize Sending Conditions  
It must arrive at our office by September 30 of this month.  

Please write your answer concisely on a card-sized paper, making sure to clearly write your address and name. You must attach the reader's coupon found on the last page of this paper to your answer sheet. If you do not include it, your submission will be invalid.  

On the envelope, write "Gyeongseong Kaepiksa Byeolgeongon Prize Section" and also write "Manuscript Inside" in blue ink, cut one side, and affix a 2-jeon stamp before sending it. If "Manuscript Inside" is not written, you will incur a penalty for insufficient postage.  

Grand prize for 1 person: specially made artistic desktop clock  
99 runner-ups: 1 copy of a book

**Summary**  

This article presents a prize question related to a murder mystery, inviting readers to guess the identity of the murderer by submitting their answers. The contest highlights audience engagement with the mystery genre prevalent during the 1930s, showing how popular culture intertwined with the interest in detective stories. Historians may find interest in the print culture of colonial Korea and the ways in which cultural products served as both entertainment and a form of intellectual engagement among the populace.
