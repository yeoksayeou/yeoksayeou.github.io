발행일	1933년 09월 01일  
기사제목	當代名女優, 李月華孃의 最後, -타락한 女子라 비웃지 마시요- (The Last of the Contemporary Famous Actress, Lee Wolhwa: -Do Not Laugh at Her as a Fallen Woman-)  
필자	卜惠淑 (Park Hye-suk)  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

Lee Wolhwa was born in Jukyojeong (舟橋町), Seoul. From the moment she entered this world with a bright appearance, she was already unfortunate - for her mother passed away from postpartum illness, and her father also left this world less than a year later, leaving her behind. Her father was said to be a member of the Shin family. At that time, Lee Wolhwa had an aunt, who took her in, but as her financial situation was difficult, she ended up giving young Wolhwa to a widowed woman of the Lee family without children. This is how Wolhwa adopted the Lee surname. It is said that this widowed woman also passed away shortly after Wolhwa began to walk. 

Afterward, she was taken in by a widow from Changseong-dong, and until her death, Wolhwa believed this widow was her true mother and devotedly cared for her. It seems one can reasonably guess the kind of household education she received, since the woman lived in a very challenging situation. Until her death, Wolhwa herself did not know her original surname or her date of birth. Isn't that pitiful?

Wolhwa attended school until the third grade of Jinmyeong Elementary School, but that too was merely a formality, and she was expelled for not understanding anything. At the age of sixteen, she joined the theater company Shin-geuk-jo (新極座) led by Kim Dosan, which marked the beginning of her acting career, and it was the first time that female actresses appeared in Korean theater thanks to Wolhwa. Later, she moved to the Minhaeng Theater Company (民衆劇團) under Yoon Baik-nam and truly became acknowledged as a female actor at this time, gaining recognition through her roles in "Eongyeok's Wife" as Olga and in "Carmen".

The name "Wolhwa" was given to her by Mr. Yoon Baik-nam. Subsequently, she participated in the "Doyulhoe" and appeared in "Katyusha" and "Calvin," becoming increasingly famous as a female actor. After finishing seven performances, she went down to Busan and became the first female actor of the newly established Joseon Cinema Company.

Before she quit Towolhoe (토월회) about twelve years ago, I first met Wolhwa when I came from Tokyo. At that time, she seemed completely naïve and clueless about life. I was astonished to think how such a girl could be a precious female actress, considering the hardships she would later go through as she became mocked by the world as a "fallen woman." 

Wolhwa starred in movies such as "The Sea's Lament" (海의 悲曲), "Secrets of the Chinese Street" (支那街의 秘密), and "The Hornless Bull" (뿔빠진 황소), but acting and films did not guarantee a living, so she became a gisaeng (妓生) in Hongseong to make ends meet. 

There is a heart-wrenching episode hidden here. During this time, Wolhwa had a passionate love for a certain man, known only as "K." He was a friend attending a law school and is now believed to be in a prosecutor's office. They seemed to have a fervent love. They even had a mutual promise, considering their situation, neither expected to marry the other, but they agreed to inform one another if they were to marry. However, hearts can be fickle. During the months Wolhwa was in Hongseong, "K" became engaged to another woman and held his wedding while she was returning to Seoul. 

When Wolhwa reached the station, eager to present K with the eggs she bought for him, she couldn't find his shadow. Disappointed, she returned home only to learn that it was K's wedding day. In anger and despair, somehow she managed to ensnare him, holding him for three days without letting him go until the wedding night. This somewhat tragicomic episode paradoxically caused Wolhwa to fall into a pit of depravity from which she might never escape. 

I believe that if K had returned Wolhwa's true love with pure affection, perhaps there would have been hope in her life. 

Let me mention briefly about Wolhwa's life in Shanghai. I can divide Wolhwa's life in Shanghai into four periods. The exact age when she first arrived in Shanghai is unclear, but when she seemed close to being trafficked at the human meat market (人肉市場), a Korean took her under his protection. In the second period, she lived in the Foreign Concession (不租界) with someone nicknamed Kim (whom they called "Banana"). The third phase saw Wolhwa managing alone in a dance hall until moving on to her role as a "dancer." Upon her most recent arrival, she organized the Ohyang Opera Troupe (五洋歌劇團) and became its director, embarking on a regional tour. 

There is a story that happened while performing in Daegu. Due to poor box office results, they were unable to pay for their lodgings, leading to the inability of the troupe members to eat. At such a time, a young man named Huh from Daegu generously covered the bill, thus saving the troupe from distress. However, the troupe disbanded afterward, and Wolhwa went over to Shanghai with this young man, Huh. 

A few years ago, she continued to live as a dancer. Tragically, young Huh, who was about twenty-two, committed suicide just a few months after he moved to Shanghai. 

The reason for his suicide seems to stem from this: Wolhwa being a dancer was frequently out with various men, returning home late, leaving Huh alone with his despair while he was continuously expecting her support. Despite having money, he felt his wealth only added to his misery. Ultimately, feeling abandoned and lost, he took his own life. 

As time passed, she met a young man named Lee Chun-rae, who was around twenty-two, in a dance hall two years ago. His father was Chinese, and his mother Japanese, a product of society at that time, and he studied at Tongmun Academy (同文書院). Attending this academy required a hefty enrollment fee of ten won, which indicates it was a prestigious institution. Their relationship blossomed, and Lee, disillusioned with his family, found comfort in Wolhwa. Eventually, he left school, took his enrollment fee, and they came back to Korea together, settling in Suwon, where they opened a fabric shop. 

Lee's family grew concerned when he went missing, suspecting he had fallen into the clutches of the "gang." They mobilized a search and eventually found out he was in Suwon. Having seen their son in such circumstances, Lee’s father returned to take him home, sending him a letter urging him to return with Wolhwa before he suffered any hardship. At that time, it was unfortunate that they had spent all their initial earnings, creating financial strain. So, Lee’s father began missing his home. 

Eventually, on July 18th, after Lee entered the granting process, Wolhwa tragically passed away from a cardiac arrest on the boat at the age of thirty-nine. Had she not died and gone back to her last beloved, Lee, maybe she would have found everlasting happiness, but thinking of how she faced such sudden misfortune at the end of her suffering and struggle is indeed pitiful.

While many in the world curse Wolhwa, it must be noted that she also cursed the world, which had been all the more vile towards her. She carried poison with her always, presumably hoping to end her life with it. 

Nevertheless, even amidst such despair, she performed her duty as a devoted daughter to her mother. It is remarkable that she only discovered a month before her death that her true mother was not the widowed lady who raised her, and she did not even know her own surname or birthday until then.

Ah, how troubling it is to think of her youth. In the end, Wolhwa could not bear her tormented heart and passed away.

Poor Wolhwa! What more is there to say, my friend? 

**Summary**

The article traces the life of Lee Wolhwa, a prominent actress in early 20th-century Korea, detailing her tumultuous upbringing, her rise to fame in theater and film, and her tragic demise amidst a cycle of love, loss, and societal scorn. Historically, it exemplifies the struggles of women entertainers during the colonial era, who faced both exploitation and judgment from society. This narrative highlights the intersections of love, ambition, and societal expectations within the broader context of Korean history, revealing the personal toll of colonialism and patriarchy on women's lives.

李春來라는 두물두 살 난 도련님을 딴스홀에서 맛나게 된 것은 재작년이엿다든가. 李는 아버지는 중국인 어머니는 일본인 사회의 난 아이노꼬인대 자긔 아버지가 일본에 건너와서 20여년 동안 장사하야 상당히 재산을 모흔 덕택에 李는 상해 同文書院에 다니는 몸이엿슴니다. 同文書院에 드러가는 데에는 만원의 보증금을 내여 노화야 한다고 합니다. 그만큼 동문서원은 뿌루조아의 도련님만을 양성하는 곳이외다. 땐스홀에서 맛난 그들은 엇더 동긔로 떼일 수 업는 사희가 되엿든가. 李는 가정에 대한한 환멸을 늣기는 때엿습니다. 돈이 잇스나 고적과 우울이 그를 괴롭히엿습니다. 그럴 때마다 그는 땐스홀에서 밤을 새워든 것입니다. 엇던 날 月華는 李에게 술이 취하야
「학생 신분에 딴스홀에 당길 자격이 업다」고 꾸지즌 일이 잇섯다고 합니다. 이것이 동긔가 되여 李와 月華는 서로이 늘 사사로히 맛나게 되는 동시에 서로의 처지를 동정하든 끗헤 李는 공부하든 것을 집어치우고 학교에 막기엿든 보증금 만원을 차저가지고 月華와 조선을 나아오게 된 것이랍니다. 그들은 水原에다 집을 사고 그리고 포목상을 내엿습니다. 李가 행방불명된 줄을 안 그의 집에서는 李가 행여나 「깽」들에게 붓들려 갓는가 습허서 사방에 수사망을 느리고 차저본 결과 조선에 와잇는 것을 알고 李의 아버지를 비롯하야 온 집안이 모다 水原을 단여 갓다고 합니다. 장사를 하고 잇는 것을 보고 매우 만족하야 李의 아버지는 문사로 도라가서 자긔 아들에게 타관에서 고생할 것 업시 月華를 더리고 집으로 도라오라고 편지를 하엿다고 합니다. 그 때는 마침 장사라고 버려 노왓든 미천은 다 드러먹다습히 되엿든 판이라 올타꾸나 하고 李는 문사로 뛰여 들어간 것이엿습니다. 그 뒤 李에게서 드러와 달나는 편지를 밧고 月華가 지난 7월 18일 문사를 들어가자 배에서 심장마비(心臟痲痺)로 수물아홉 살을 일긔로 하고 그 긔구한 그 생애를 마춘 것이외다.
만일 月華가 죽지 안코 자긔의 마지막 애인 李에게 갓다라면 그것만은 과연 길이 길이 행복스러웟슬 것이라고 뉘 단연할 수 잇스랴만은 가진 고생을 할대로 한 그가 애인의 품을 향하여 가다가 그러케 벼란간 불행하게 된 것 생각을 하면 할수록 가엽습니다.
세상사람들은 月華를 욕하는 사람이 만흐나 月華 역시 그만큼은 세상사람을 욕하고 과연 몃 배나 몃 배나 더 세상은 그에게 고약햇고 참악햇든 것입니다. 그는 늘 몸에다 독약을 지니고 다니엿답니다. 몰론 끗헤 가서 자살을 할랴고 그랫지요. 술을 먹고 울기도 햇습니다.
그러나 그런 중에도 그는 어머니에게는 무섭게 효녀노릇을 햇습니다. 그게 정말 어머니가 아니라는 것 자긔는 성도 생일도 알 수 업는 고하엿다는 것은 그가 죽기 전 바로 한 달 전에 알엇습니다.
아- 그의 앳끗는 가삼이 어떠하엿겟습니까. 그 애끗는 가슴이 고만 참지 못하고 터저서 月華는 죽엇든 것입니다.
