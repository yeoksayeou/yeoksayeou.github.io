기사제목	尾行꾼 골려준 이야기 (A Story of Fooling a Tailgater)  
필자	尹梧月 (Yun O-yul)  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

This tale took place in the spring of last year, a year that has now passed. The spring day in April, with the light rain, was one of those marvelously vibrant days when everything seems to bloom. The cherry blossoms had been in full bloom for a while, but it was only around mid-April that they were at their prime.

The phrase “when the cherry blossoms are at their best” evokes images of young men and women frolicking among the flowers like butterflies, and I can’t help but reminisce that I too might have joined them. On the night of April 20th, I went with three friends to see the cherry blossoms in Changyeongwon (昌慶苑), despite the crowded tram from Seodaemun to Changyeongwon, which was almost bursting with passengers.

From the entrance of Changyeongwon, it was so crowded that we couldn’t move an inch. Overpowered by the delightful floral fragrance, I couldn’t tell whether I was drunk on the flower scents or simply drunk on the crowd. As we pushed our way in, the flowers we came to see were obscured, and all I could see was an overwhelming wave of people.

Carried along by the ebb and flow of the crowd, we found ourselves near a performance area where gisaeng (妓生) were dancing. With so many people gathered there, we couldn’t see a thing, so we ended up jumping a bit to get a glimpse. Among us were some students in square hats who had formed a block, making it difficult for us to see. They were quite tall, wearing their school uniforms and brandishing sticks, which was quite frustrating for us. In a playful moment, we gave them a little shove and moved on, eventually turning to leave after trying to catch a glimpse.

Suddenly, I realized my foot was stuck in someone else's. Upon closer look, I discovered that it was one of those boys from the group. Then, I must have accidentally stepped on his foot. He wore a smile as if to tease me, and it turned out he had purposely put his foot in my way. Regardless, out of politeness, I apologized, saying, “I’m sorry for stepping on your foot.”

“Not at all!” he replied, laughing it off. Feeling slightly suspicious, we quietly made our way to the pond nearby.

“Stepping on someone else's foot and getting away with it?” one of my friends teased as we walked. We were enjoying ourselves and felt a little bolder, noticing that they were still trailing behind us.

After resting by the pond for about 10 minutes, one of them approached us and exclaimed, “Oh! Let’s take it easy and rest here!” He came right up to us, stepping heavily as if to claim space, which irked me. I shot him a sharp look, causing him to back away, but then he jokingly said, “Oh no! Those girls are scary!” beckoning his friends over.

It was rather amusing and made me wonder why they were all so keenly following us. Among them, one of the boys wore a hat with a white emblem on it. I figured he was the leader while the rest were like his subordinates. They looked rather foolish acting like that, making me pity them a bit.

Having left the large crowd, we headed towards the lively area where they hosted moving pictures. Those boys followed us persistently. The sight of them squeezing their way through the crowd to look for us was rather entertaining. They started to mimic us and standing still, acted as though they were simply watching.

However, soon they took on a serious look and stood in a row, pretending to watch without reacting to us. In turn, we decided to act the same, leading them on the chase as they followed us blindly through the throng. Probably, they had some plan in mind. Thinking of a little mischief, we found a tram heading to Dongdaemun and boarded it, amused by the thought of losing them.

As luck would have it, they followed us onto the same tram, chit-chatting loudly about how they were having such a good time. We were determined to find a way to shake them off. As the tram began moving, we crowded by the entrance, pretending to get off at Seodaemun.

They paused outside, waiting for us to disembark but we remained inside, concealed behind the crowd. When the tram continued on to Dongdaemun, they boarded too, looking about perplexed.

Eventually, we played with their minds, pretending to get off while continuing to observe them from the tram. They became very animated, darting around searching for us, with expressions that appeared almost humorous.

We finally got off at Samjeongmok but they continued to follow us. It felt like they were eager to be our shadows. Climbing the hill to CW-dong, we slipped into a house without being noticed.

They stood like guards at the gate, glancing in our direction while we were quiet inside. As night deepened, we were anxious but watching without a sound as they lingered outside. Their whispers came through the gap of the closed door, mixing with the night’s ambiance.

After the excitement faded, I felt more amused than angry about the ordeal with those boys. The absurdity of it all kept me laughing internally. 

The following summer night had that typical heat when P and I ventured out to buy cosmetics at the Wahsin Sanghoe (和信商會). Since it was summer break, we were in the mood to shop leisurely for various “things.”

Once we entered Wahsin, I stood at the cosmetic corner, contemplating which perfume to buy. Suddenly, two young men in formal wear approached us, asking about our selections in a friendly manner. It turned out they weren’t buying anything themselves.

As we moved up to the fabric department, they followed us again, and to my irritation, they blurred the line between attention and annoyance. They claimed they needed some sewing thread for their “wives,” which was obviously a line to keep up the charade.

After finishing our shopping at a store on Gongdeok, when we exited, those two men were still outside waiting for us. Their determination was exasperating, but amusing to witness.

Determined to teach them a lesson, we decided to play a trick. R and I hurriedly discussed going to report them to the police while I casually walked over to say hello. 

When I turned to face them, they appeared confident, thinking we were friendly. “I live in Hwadong,” one introduced himself, while the other said he was from Changseong-dong.

As we approached Gwanghwamun, R was waiting at the police station, and the unwitting boys were about to find out they’d walked into a trap.

We marched them right to the station, where they were swiftly questioned about their intentions in following us for over thirty minutes. Their face turned pale as they realized the gravity of their actions.

As the crowd gathered, they were treated like some kind of spectacle. A mix of shock and disbelief mixed with ridicule was evident among the onlookers. Consequently, the two boys ended up spending three days detained, charged for tailing women, which was perhaps a long-overdue consequence for their foolishness that night.

This amusing night served as a reminder of how the spirit of youth and folly can lead us into unexpected situations. 

**Summary**  

This article recounts an amusing encounter with two young men who followed the author and her friends during a nighttime outing to view cherry blossoms. The playful narrative describes the mix of humor and tension as they led the tailgaters on a chase through the streets of Seoul, ultimately resulting in the young men's detention by the police for their misguided behavior. This tale highlights the nuances of youth and social dynamics during a period of colonial rule in Korea, reflecting on how such lighthearted incidents also reveal the societal pressures and expectations imposed on young men in a conservative society.

날이 찌는 듯 바람 한 점 업는 여름밤입니다. 저녁밥을 먹고 화신상회(和信商會)에 화장품(化粧品)을 사러 갓습니다. 바로 P동무와 가티 방학도 하고 해서 두루두루 여러 가지 「가이모노」를 하러 서서히 나섯든 것입니다.
화신에 드러서 먼저 入口의 화장부(化粧部)에 발은 멈처 죽 진열(陳列) 해 노은 향수를 골으고 서서 이것이 조흘까 저것이 조흘까 하고 집어서 店員더러 뭇노라니 난대 업는 신사복(紳士服) 한 두 청년이 나타나 우리 엽헤 와 우리의 골으는 향수병을 가르치며 저것이 조와 우리도 하나 사세 하면서 우리 말대로 점원(店員)의 말을 가루채고 잇습니다.
우리가 웃층으로 올라가는 뒤를 따라 옵니다.
자수실을 멧개 살여고 수예부(手藝部)로 가서 푼사실 리리앙실을 골으로 섯슬 때 그 두 청년도 어느 틈에 엽헤 와서 고연히 지분대면서 아참 실 일홈은 모르지만 우리 마누라가 실 점 사 가지고 오라고 햇는데... 무슨 실인지? 매꼬모자 쓴 한 靑年은 말합니다. 도모지 우리가 가는데로 쫏차 댕기면서 (物件)을 사는 척하나 하나 사지는 안습니다.
종로(鍾路)로 나는 동모와 가티 나와 번정통(本町通)으로 뎐차를 타고 갓습니다. 먼저 선은(鮮銀) 압헤서 나리여 삼월(三越)로 드러갓습니다. 3층으로 올라가 편지지를 사가지고 나오는데 또 그 두 청년이 門 압헤서 기다리고 잇섯습니다. 너무 긔가 막혀 우리는 속으로 우스면서 두 얼골을 맘속에서 긔억하여 두고 어데까지 오나 두고 보다가 정 불량(不良)한 행동을 하면 최후(最後)의 처치(處置)를 하리라 하고 잔득 벼르고 경성부(京城府) 압흐로 천천히 거러 옵니다. 우리가 천천히 오면 저의들도 천천히 오고 우리가 빨리오면 저의들도 빨리 따라오면서 작고 수작을 붓침니다.
「E학교는 벌서 방학햇지요? 퍽 날이 더웁슴니다. 이제는 한가하실 터이니 빙수(氷水)나 갓치 가 잡수십시다」
웨 그런지 친절히 굽니다. 그리고 뒤따라 오면서 수군거리면서 킥킥 우슴소리가 들여올 제 꾀를 내여 우리는 한＜27＞ 가면(假面)을 쓰고 언제적 모르는 사람이더냐 하고 별안간에 친절히 굴어주엇슴니다. 슬그머니 먼저 R友는 야주게 파출소(派出所)에 고소를 하러 보내고 나 혼자 걸어오면서 두 청년에게 주소 성명을 물엇습니다.
그 때에 그 두 청년은 왼 떡이야 하고 신바람 나서 저의들을 사랑이나 하는 줄 알고 반가운 목소리로
「저는 화동(花洞)서 사는 김진영(金鎭英)(假名)입니다」
「저는 창성동(昌城洞) 사는 신완성(申完成)(假名)이올시다」 하면서 야단들입니다.
어느 듯 광화문을 거처 야주게 큰 행길로 드러서게 되엿습니다. 그들이 뒤따른 제는 30분(三十分)이 훨신 너머 1시간 반이나 되어 오게 되엿습니다.
그 청년들은 이야기 바더주는 바람에 아주 댁해서 연달어 문답을 합니다.
「댁이 어데십니까?」하고 나의 주소를 뭇습니다. 그 때에 나는 사직동(社稷洞)인대 가티 가시지요 하면서 우스니까 더 흥분이 되여서 이야기를 내놉니다.
「이번 방학에 피서(避署) 가십니까」
「네 元山으로 갈가 합니다」
「그러면 거긔서 저의와 맛나게 되겟습니다. 하하하」

그 때에 나는 속력(速力)을 주어 그곳을 향하고 그 靑年과 가티 갑니다. 나는 약속이 잇서 경점 바라하고 은근히 끌고 가지만 그들은 아무 멋도 모르고 암흑(暗黑)이 고 순간에 잇는 줄도 모르고 우리 집을 지금 가는 줄만 알고 쫏차 오는 양이 어리석고도 가여운 인생들이엿습니다.
벌서 R友는 날보고 눈짓합니다. 순사도 슬슬 거닐고 잇습니다. 거진 파출소 압헤 당도(當到)하여 내동모 R이 거긔 선 것을 보고 그 두 靑年은 무엇이 점 캥기는지 쭈빗쭈빗하면서 잘 오지를 안습니다. 그러나 나는 시침 뚝 띄고 그 파출소 압흘 지나는 척하고 통과(通過)하려 할 제
