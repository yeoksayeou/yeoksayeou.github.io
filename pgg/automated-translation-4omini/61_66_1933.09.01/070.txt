戀文艶書 競賣場, 연애편지 考現學  
						史郞君  

First of all, I wish to make known to my dear readers of Byeolgeongon that starting this month, I will be opening this love letter library for free to you all. Please take advantage of it.  

To begin, I will reveal the first letter of the first section concerning love letters (戀愛片紙考現學), which is about unrequited love.  

There’s a saying, “No matter which house you look at, the house next door is the best; no matter which room you enter, the husband in your own is the best.” Such is a true sentiment. The husband... the wife... the spouse... 

However, once upon a time, the parents of both sides would take the initiative to arrange meetings, send marriage proposals, and choose a suitable partner, ultimately leading to a "the husband next door" relationship. Yet, in 1933, if one tried to pursue marriage in such a way, they would end up fading into old maids or bachelors before their grandchildren even grew facial hair.  

We must pursue love. Let’s engage in love! It’s great to have a beautiful experience of love and then marry happily.  

However, in certain cases, this beautiful love can take a terrible turn. In what circumstances? Ah, unrequited love?  

It is truly heart-wrenching. While one side is pouring out their feelings in letters, the other stands aloof like a sentry. They wish to devour affection but are left unable to even succeed in unrequited love... Thus, what remains in the end is writing letters. 

Here, I will present two letters of unrequited love as examples for your reference.

Example 1  
This is a letter from student P, a senior in the XX Technical School’s X Department, to K, who is connected to XXXXX. The envelope is quite stylish, a surprise in cream color. The stationary is plain white with blue lines and a soothing outline, making it quite aesthetic. The handwriting, while not particularly elegant, shows sincere effort. However, the envelope's lettering is excessively flamboyant.  

“While spring may pass in the land of the hibiscus peninsula, it is the spring breeze carrying love that has always been blowing towards me.”  

Mr. P seems to be a noteworthy prose poet. But the continuation is indeed surprising.  

Without any consciousness of class distinction, my high school days remain immersed until the end in a spring of unconscious abandon.  

What do you think? “Class consciousness!” Isn't it terrifying?  

What others call the spring of hope, the spring of revival, and the spring of peace, I too feigned such expressions. But I must admit, I have not truly tasted the spring of genuine life. Indeed, I have been a lonely being. Up until now, I have never given love to anyone or have been loved.  

My soul, which should blossom in the warm garden of love, has been wandering in a chilly shadow. In the vast desert of life, I have left only these lonely footprints.  

How can it be that I left such a lonely, desolate, and mournful existence for the cherished boy of another family? The unmarried women of this world are indeed all facing hardships. It’s a miracle that Mr. P has not committed suicide in such lonely and fearful conditions.  

However, on April 29! Under the golden grass at Changgyongwon, I witnessed the spring garden of children. Isn’t it the garden of happiness characterized by pure, untainted innocence? These children are the very ones who will build the true culture of happiness in humanity.  

To see those guiding such children is a truly noble endeavor, isn’t it?  

Ah—Mr. P has said that those children are “innocent beings harboring no sinful seeds.” Perhaps he thought that such innocent ones had never even conceived through unknown actions of their own.  

At that moment, your impression must have been beautiful; it would have ignited the fire of the heart to an unexplainable degree, beyond any attempts to articulate those feelings. At this moment, I recall how I may want to see you! How I may hope to know you!  

This is the excitement of unrequited love found in letters.  

Yet, I should not be misunderstood when stating that the sweet feelings of pure, sentimental love are not the utmost purpose of life! Open your eyes! Isn’t this grand? Yet greater things await, so please read on.  

Esteemed readers! To win the affection of a beloved, or to rationalize one’s romantic endeavors, our Mr. P also seeks comrades in love for the purpose of advancing the cultural movement that he believes in.  

What do you say? Isn’t the cultural movement a great concept? This is Mr. P’s theory: in life’s dreary and lonely panorama, he discovered love. But, love alone is not the greatest purpose in life; one must also engage in cultural movements. Such activities require love but also camaraderie.  

Perhaps he participated in the Brnaod movement affiliated with XX Daily?  

In moments of solitude, where one can’t find comfort or encouragement, one realizes that life’s meaning lies in battling against the dawning of a great new day. Is this not indeed the philosophy of life?  

Discovering the fundamental principles concerning existence and shaping our true view of life—this is what it means.  

Mr. P’s understanding of life is as vast as his vision for struggle. He has also discerned the philosophical principles deeply, hasn’t he?  

Ah! The profound impressions your visage leaves! When I gaze upon your face, my heart leaps beyond articulation. This is my uttermost fervent desire to be with you.  

Tasting the freshness of spring as I do now, I find no desire to linger anymore. How can the feelings I have for you be conveyed? This has been an opportunity for a fully realized love affair, and has further evoked scrutiny of my previous cynicism.  

However, well do I know that your impressions will never fade away. And you cannot find further relief from my irritating thoughts!  

My friend Kim XX first brought you to my attention while resting on a bench of a pine tree. Indeed, when he spoke of you, there was a distinct difference from the initial impressions I had. At that instant, I was overwhelmed with infinite feelings. I no longer wished to linger and returned with Kim.  

The main body of the letter concludes here and ends with a poem.  

Mr. P even embraces the identity of a poet.  

Spring has arrived,  

Fallen blossoms hit the path,  

The lonely night in which I weep alone,  

Confounds all my thoughts.  

In this vast expanse, among thousands of people!  

With none to share my plight,  

I have spent and spent…  
  
Ah—how wildly the emotions run!  

Referred to as it is, it’s just a whimsically sweet letter.  

And in conclusion, the signature is equally noteworthy.  

1933, 4, 30 XX Specialized School X4PXX  

To this, he even has signed his name in English.  

Example 2  
While the first example was not too embarrassing, the second example I must introduce fills the author with irritation. The handwriting is absurdly sloppy, leaving me unable to comprehend the language used.  

The sender has the envelope labeled “Kim Soon-hee, Suburban North Line Payment.” At first glance, it appears to be sent from a friend. However, upon reading the end of the letter, it states, “To XX Dong, XX KXX from Bukchon.” It is quite amusingly embarrassing. The sender is also K, a connected person of XXXXX.  

The envelope is a typical yellow brown type that costs one won and contains five sheets of stationary typical from Kokuyo. Now, allow me to present the main text, observe closely.  

To KXX!  

[Excerpt]  

In a world filled with lies,  

Only blossoming hearts remain true!  

Only warm hearts are genuine!  

A bearer of this unrecognized sentiment can barely acknowledge it.  

But I have seen it!  

This heart that remains unknown!  

Will my passionate affection ever extinguish?  

My longing is great but it feels as if distant as thousand li?  

I wish to understand your heart!  

I wish to see you!  

How could we ever know each other!?  

Ah, now! You must realize!  

Dear readers, indeed, it’s quite an endeavor! Such distraction would make one wish they had read Confucian classics to at least understand the erratic nature of this love letter.  

Despite that, they hope for love even while crafting such a letter, how is that not a masterpiece?  

Also, there’s more to it—the next section is poetry. The following is prose.  

[Enclosure]  

At some point! During the Great Motion at the Youth Center!  

Memories of childhood shift freshly back to my mind!  

When you transformed into the angel of a deity,  

Not once or twice, but several times, truly!  

This, however, simply speaks to my own realization in this regard.  

Finally, I send you wishes for health and joy, wishing you lovely days during the summer.  

Other wishes require a reply!  

All the same, here’s a scribble drawn; I wish you would decipher my heart: your freedom!!  

Alas, dear readers, I apologize greatly. It must have been quite the strenuous read for you. I truly hope such monumental examples are not shared again.  

**Summary**  

In this article, the author presents a light-hearted take on love letters, particularly focusing on two examples of unrequited love letters from students. Through humorous commentary, the author conveys feelings of solitude and confusion that can accompany adolescence and romantic attachments. The first letter reveals a mix of poetic aspirations and philosophical musings, while the second depicts a naive and less eloquent approach to expressing affection. The reflections on these letters provide insights into the challenges and expressions of love during a transitional period in Korean society. Historians might find the emphasis on the search for genuine emotional connection amidst rigid social structures quite revealing of the cultural currents of the 1930s.
