This contest issue attracted significant public interest, with a historic total of over 19,000 participants. However, the nature of the questions was ambiguous enough that no one was able to provide the correct answer. Regrettably, a first prize could not be awarded, but 20 individuals who came closest to the answers were selected and are announced below.

**Summary**  

This article discusses the results of a traffic survey conducted in Gyeongseong (Seoul) on November 21, 1930, which resulted in over 19,000 participants. Although the responses yielded no correct answers, it highlighted the public’s engagement with such surveys during the colonial period. Historians may find the sheer number of participants and the nature of the survey indicative of the societal dynamics and interests in colonial Korea during this era.
