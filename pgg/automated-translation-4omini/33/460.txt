風聞帖  
Mr. Ryu Byeo (柳葉) has not been heard from since he left Seoul after quitting the magazine and was recently seen briefly with his somewhat downtrodden face back in Seoul. Upon hearing from his acquaintances, it turns out he is currently teaching Buddhist scriptures to monks at Haein Temple (海印寺) in Hapcheon (陜川) and plans to go to India next spring. Life, after all, is just as it is and again just as it is.

There are rumors that Mr. Choi Sang-deok (崔象德) has divorced his wife, Ms. Ryu Yeong-sook (劉英淑). However, it turns out they haven't officially divorced; rather, they are temporarily separated and living in different lodgings. Mr. Choi has also fallen ill and was hospitalized. Originally, they were a couple known as lovebirds, but what is Mr. Choi doing all alone? 

Mr. Lee Ryong (李亮), having faced many failures in his endeavors, has decided to change tactics by organizing what he called the Christian Labor Association (基督敎勞働會) to conduct labor movements under Christian principles. Where has the fervor that once opposed religious movements gone?

In the Tohwul Society (土月會), Mr. Hong No-seong (洪露省) has returned to Seoul after a long absence and is currently working with Mr. Hong Hae-seong (洪海星) on theatrical activities.

In the Kinyu Society (槿友會), Mr. Jeong Chil-seong (丁七星) has opened a new small shop specializing in embroidery and knitting at the Seoul Women's Cooperative Society (樂園洞 京城女子消費組合). The name of the business is "Pearl Jade Handicraft Store" (粉玉手藝舍) and offers free instructions on arts and crafts to those interested. However, if the shop's name had been "Jeong's Ladies' Workshop" (丁女屋), it would have presented an excellent contrast to the owner's own name, Jeong's "Son's Workshop" (丁子屋).

Recently, Mr. Lee Gwang-su (李光洙) has been dressing in brand new suits, hats, and neckties, looking sharp, while his shoes may not be brand new but are undoubtedly polished. His wife, Ms. Heo Yeong-suk (許英肅), has been observing him and teasingly asked, “What’s up with your appearance lately? They say spring is for renewal, but it seems autumn has brought you a new love interest!” 

Once a prominent actress or popular courtesan in Seoul, Ms. Bok Hye-suk (卜惠淑) has moved her registration to Yeongdong (龍洞券番) in Incheon (仁川) after receiving 800 won. A troubled youth in Incheon, hold tightly to your wallet!
