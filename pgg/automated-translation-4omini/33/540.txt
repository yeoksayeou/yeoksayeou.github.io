필자	金岸曙 (Kim An-so)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

金장이  

If you break the ore down with the ore crusher, the "stamp" pours it out. The crushed stone powder flows down with water, and when a wooden board (tablet) does this, it captures gold with mercury.

Song of the Ore Crusher  

How absurd it is, gnawing on iron stone  
Letting my figure sag over my speech   
Crushing the iron stones with a crunch  
Is it melting away the feeling of not finding a way?  

Song of the Stamp  

Without a thought, the "stamp" rises at dawn  
And my brother next door feels miserable  
Even if everything dies, in this worldly goods  
This life doesn't let go, how raucous it seems.  

Song of the Wooden Board  

The ore crusher swallows the iron stone  
Dancing along with the stamp's butt  
No matter how it is made, it's all my count  
Let me control the gold as I wish.  
<164>

**Summary**  

The poem "Gold Seizing" and its supplementary pieces employ vivid imagery to illustrate the processes of ore extraction and commodity production. Through playful language and rhythmic verses, it reflects on the labor and struggles associated with mining and the existential awareness of life under labor exploitation. For historians studying colonial Korea, this piece highlights the socio-economic dynamics and the alienation felt by workers involved in the mining industry, which was largely driven by colonial interests.

金장이(외 3편) (押韻)
						金岸曙
金장이

어처구니가 鑛石을 깨트려 내려보내면 「스탬프」는 그것을 빙아질함니다. 방아질된 돌가루가 물과 함께 흘너내리는데 木板(테불)이 그것을 이러노흐면 水銀으로 金을 잡슴니다.

어처구니의 노래

어처구니 싱겁다 쇳돌 깨무네
말을 말게 제 身勢 하도 어굴해
와작어작 쇳돌을 모다 부싀며
풀 길 업는 심사를 녹이노나이

스탬프의 노래

無心타 「스탬프」는 밤낫 츔일세
뒷집 兄 압집 同生 민망하고나
모도 다 죽노라네 쓸인 世 품에
못 끈나니 이 목슴 엉덩춤일세

木板의 노래

어처구니 쇳돌을 화에 삼키고
엉덩춤을 스탬프 추며 놀아도
모도다 제 身數라 낸들 엇저오
金이나마 맘대로 잡아두겟소
