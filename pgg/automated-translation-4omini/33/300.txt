**Summary**  

This article provides a satirical commentary on the economic difficulties faced by people in Korea during the year-end season of 1930, illustrated through the streets bustling with people yet marked by an air of despair and resignation. Notably, the mention of popular department stores highlights how even in bleak economic times, consumer behavior remained focused on certain affluent spaces, serving as a stark reminder of colonial divisions in society. Historians might find the juxtaposition of consumerism and poverty particularly revealing in the context of Korea's struggle under Japanese colonial rule.
