Question and Answer Record (7)  
                                                Lee Tae-jun  

Question: In the newspapers, it is said that you write evaluations of various paintings. Do you also know how to paint?  

Answer: I certainly cannot paint. However, it is not necessary to know how to paint in order to evaluate paintings. Just as anyone can sing praises upon seeing the majestic creation of the universe by Nature (造化翁), anyone can evaluate a painting which is a human creation. <45>  

**Summary**  

This article presents a dialogue where the author, Lee Tae-jun, responds to a question regarding his ability to paint while also writing critiques. Lee asserts that the ability to appreciate and evaluate art does not require the skill of painting itself. This perspective highlights the accessibility of artistic commentary and reflects the broader cultural discussions on art and creation during Korea's colonial period. Historians may find this exchange interesting as it signifies a moment where aesthetic appreciation transcends artistic skill, perhaps hinting at the burgeoning intellectual discussions amidst colonial constraints.
