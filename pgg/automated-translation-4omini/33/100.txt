一問一答記(2)  

記  全校生徒 얼골을 다 알어보심닛가?  
鄭  웬걸이요 全校生徒가 한 700名 되는데 그 중에 나 마튼 시간이 잇는 반이 두 반인데 그 아희들이나 알지 그 餘外 生徒들은 그저 어렴풋해서 누가 누군지 몰으지요. <24>  

**Summary**  

In this article, a conversation is presented where the author, Jeong Dae-hyeon, is asked about recognizing all students at the school, to which he responds that he is only familiar with the students in his two classes out of about 700 in total, indicating that he does not know the others well. This reflects some aspects of school life during the colonial period in Korea, where class sizes were large, potentially impacting individual relationships between students and teachers. Historians may find interest in the dynamics of education in colonial Korea and how it shaped social interactions.
