Woman (8), A Path of Remembrance  
						Kim Dong-in  

One and a half years, if I say how long, can seem long or short; I spent that year and a half in a love that could be described as exceedingly bland. However, while it may seem bland to others, to me it was an incredibly pleasant and poignant life. In the tranquil night, in a quiet room, dismissing even the maid, I lost my young heart and spirit to the serene sound of his shamisen, and after becoming pleasantly intoxicated with a drink I could hardly consume, slumped against the "脇息" (waki-iki, a type of armrest) and closed my eyes, enjoying the experience that I felt was incomparable. It seemed as though I was sinking into a soft dream of a clear and gentle world.

"Hey, shall we sing something?" He said with his long eyes slightly upward, and I couldn’t help but want to swallow him up. My heartstrings trembled strangely with an odd impulse at times.

"葉さん (Ha-san)." 

I called out to him aimlessly, having nothing to say, which felt bland. Indeed, it was not the first or second time something like this occurred. 

Nothing to say? I distinctly said, "nothing to say." But was there really nothing to say? Countless words were crawling from my heart, urging me to express something, yet I couldn't convey a single word from my heart to his ears. A fragment of a giggle that was blankly smiling was the entirety of my feelings at that moment. <165>  

The first time I met him was during that spring when the peony hill hosted a Japanese art exhibition. Having earnestly sought an invitation to see it, I went to peony hill that day to look for Semimaru. 

It was still the gentle spring before the cherry blossoms had bloomed fully. The cherry blossom garden, described by the Japanese as a "cloud of flowers," was dreamlike. The elegant children dressed in colorful Japanese garments and a sea of giggling children who followed along spread out before me. 

Twisting my way through that dream-like garden, trying to catch a glimpse of Semimaru, I found myself unable to see him and, with a lonely heart, ended up resting my back against a cherry tree under Deokwol Tower, gently closing my eyes while looking at that dream-like scene. At that moment, a group of girls dressed in Western clothing passed by, laughing. 

"!" 

I straightened suddenly. One of the girls among them was undoubtedly Semimaru. 

"Ah—" 

Straightening up, I suddenly made a sound like a half-mute, as I was about to say something when they passed by without paying me any attention. 

"?" 

Was it Semimaru? I suddenly doubted it. Coming across girls decked in flamboyant outfits, I found it hard to accept that the girl in Western dress with her hair styled in two braids was Semimaru. 

There was a difference. The girl in Western attire could not be older than twelve or thirteen. Though she didn’t have drawn-in eyebrows or a heavily painted face like those I usually saw on Semimaru, she struck me as distinctly different from a typical courtesan. 

"There goes Semimaru." 

I looked toward her retreating figure while tilting my head. She was also shorter than the usual Semimaru I was familiar with.

However, seeing the slightly upward slant of her eyes and her curly and oddly tilted hair, I wanted to view her as Semimaru despite the differences. To do otherwise would mean concluding that Semimaru hadn’t come to Peony Hill that day. I had covered the whole hill earlier. <166>  

A little later, that girl passed me again. She continued to come and go several times. Yet not once did she turn her gaze toward me, and she merely passed by with an inviting air.

The mysterious girl frequently walking past me ignited my suspicions quietly, yet gradually grew louder.

Until then, I had only remembered the contours of her face and body but had never had the chance to look directly at her. I couldn’t decisively judge whether that mysterious girl was Semimaru. Moreover, seeing that she came and went as if accustomed and likely noticed me, it was unimaginable that if she was indeed Semimaru, she could pass by without a friendly nod. But if she wasn't Semimaru, where could she have gone? How should I interpret the strong similarities I felt between that girl and Semimaru?

Even after I shifted my resting place to the top of Bbyeokglug, the girl walked past me several times again.

Later I learned that the girl in Western attire was indeed Semimaru. 

When I questioned why she did not seem to see me, she replied with only a "たつて..." and began playing the shamisen again, without any words or melody.

In summer, Yu Ji-young came down to Pyongyang. Instead of staying at a hotel, he lodged at a friend named H's house. H's house was the one we regularly used as a clubhouse, where we spent the daytime.

However, since Ji-young's arrival, we not only met there during the day but also gathered at that place at night to spend our time together.

Having kept my feelings for Semimaru a secret, I couldn't find any excuse to go to that club after Ji-young came to visit. Thus, I spent time doing nothing and was unable to meet Semimaru.

One day, Ji-young, K, and I went for a stroll and headed toward K's house to rest for a moment.

As we approached K's house, we saw a certain Japanese girl, a bit older than us, who unexpectedly sped past us from behind. 

It was Semimaru. She turned around quickly and, I couldn't discern to whom she was speaking when she greeted someone by saying, "今日は (Konnichiwa)," and turned back, quickly entering a Japanese restaurant named "Kotobuki" about twenty steps ahead. 

Kotobuki was situated very close to K's front door. After we entered K's house, I caught a glimpse of her talking with an old woman at the entrance. Standing briefly at an angle, she appeared to be engaged in some conversation... 

When we left K's house briefly, I glanced again closer to the door and saw Semimaru leaning against the wall, staring blankly ahead out the doorway. 

Seeing that scene, Ji-young remarked, "That girl seems to be waiting for someone among us. She clearly appears to try to draw attention." 

"K, do you think it’s you?" 

"I don’t know." 

"Don’t be silly!" 

I retorted hastily. 

In the fall, I began fishing. Having disrupted the habit of meeting Semimaru due to Ji-young's presence during the summer, though my passion for Semimaru had cooled, I still found the longing for her grew more intense day by day. Thus, I started fishing. 

Fishing at Daedong River was delightful. Out of the various spots, I experienced everything from catching carp and catfish in the small streams to big mullet in the river. 

Due to the considerable development in fishing techniques over thousands of years, the Daedong River's fishing practices have become the most precise and sophisticated. Though the fish may be low in number compared to other rivers due to this development, a small catch feels truly rewarding and delightful beyond words. The thrill of feeling a gentle pull on the line when a fish bites, the excitement when watching caught fish energetically leap out of the water to escape, and the pleasant sensation felt when reeling in the line with a catch—those who have never experienced fishing simply cannot imagine such joy and bliss. <168>  

During that time, I longed for Semimaru. Even if I say I longed for her, perhaps it was merely my words. More than longing, I began sketching the shadow of Semimaru in the water. In the backdrop of that dream, Semimaru was transformed in my mind into the "person of dreams."

As I passed that autumn executing my fishing trips, Semimaru’s reality blurred into an idol in my heart, merging with Mary, who had suddenly disappeared from my childhood, making it into a beautiful idol.  

By late October, that year on a certain night, Kim Hwan visited our home on the way to Beijing. We held a small banquet that night to send him off. It was then that I met Semimaru after a long time.  

With that meeting, the passion in my heart erupted once more. Every evening, my feet carried me again toward Tamaya and Shichikan. The silence of my love began anew and continued on.  

The following early spring, due to some circumstances, I had to leave Pyongyang for about a month, and when I returned that night, I learned that Semimaru had left the city. 

Falling into utter despair, I didn't ask the innkeeper about Semimaru's whereabouts. The following day, however, I finally learned from the old woman who ran the Maruten that Semimaru had been sold to the owner's wife in Iseya in Seoul. 

"When she left, she came to our house...," the old woman said.  

"How sad. She left in tears... and kept pleading earnestly for her well-being..."  

Having heard those remarks, I couldn’t articulate my feelings and remained silent. I sighed, remembering the striking resemblance to Mary, the golden-haired girl who had also suddenly vanished from my childhood. Now, another girl with black hair had disappeared before my eyes, tying my fate in this melancholy.  

Days later, I went to Seoul and searched through the telephone directory for Iseya. After looking, I found four Iseya restaurants, including one selling Japanese food and clothing. However, I could not find any clue as to where Semimaru had gone. I was too timid to make phone calls. Even if I knew where she might be, I had no recourse to take next.  

After that, I could never meet her again. I heard later from a rumor that she had returned to Dalian to work at a restaurant, but whether that was true or false, I do not know.  

In truth, Mary, who had once fluttered my heart in childhood, had suddenly vanished, and Semimaru, who had caught me off guard in my youth, also disappeared from my life. 

One is a mixed-race girl, the other a Japanese girl. These two girls remain the most beautiful idols imprinted in my mind, allowing me to indulge in sweet and sorrowful memories forever.  

Kimura Yoko—Is she still alive now? I cannot know her fate nor where she is, causing my heart to become heavier with each reflection about our fleeting bond. (This part ends) <170>  

**Summary**  

This article portrays a nostalgic and poignant reflection on lost love and longing for unattainable desires against the backdrop of colonial Korea. Through the protagonist's reminiscences about Semimaru, a Japanese courtesan, and Mary, a childhood friend who disappeared, the story illustrates the complexities of relationship dynamics during a time of cultural intersection and personal yearning. Historians might find the depiction of these interactions significant, revealing the emotional impact of colonial rule and the differing experiences of Korean individuals interacting with Japanese culture.

가을에 낙시질을 시작하엿다. 녀름 한 철을 志永이 때문에 세미마루를 만나는 습관을 깨트려 버린 나는 세미마루에게 대한 정열이 식은 배는 안이엿섯지만 전에와 가치 하로를 못 보며 이튼 날은 입맛이 업서지도록 등이 달지는 언엇다. 그런 때에 낙시질을 시작한 것이엿섯다.
大洞江의 낙시질은 자미잇다. 열기 잔뙈기에서 붕어와 패라미뙈기 내지는 챌낙이며 소가리뙈기에 니르기까지 수천 년 간의 경험이 나흔 바의 가장 정밀하고 발달된 대동강의 낙시질은 자미잇다. 극도로 발달된 낙시질 때문에 물 속에 고기의 수효는 다른 강에 비교하여 거의 업다 하여도 조흘 만치 적지만 적으면 적으니 만치 낙거올리는 그 자미는 도저히 입으로 말 할 수가 업다. 고기가 미끼를 물은 때에 躍動하는 쫑ㅅ대의 가느다란 허리며 얼네를 통하여 감각하는 때의 고기의 비약이며 맑은 물 속에서 이리 벌낏 저리 벌낏- 죽은목숨을 엇더케던 살아보려고 펄덕거리는 낙시끄테 달린 고기며 그런 때마다 혹은 꺼브러저 드러가며 혹은 실이 더 풀려 나가는 얼레의 손맛 종내 매생이까지 끌려 올라와서 낙시를 떼일 때에 손으로 감각하는 밋그러운 맛과 생명의 약동들은 낙시질에 손을 대어 보지 못한 사람은 도저히 짐작도 못할 통쾌와 희열이 잇다.<168>
그동안 나는 세미마루를 니젓다. -안이 니젓다면 말에 페가 잇슬 지도 모르겟다. 니젓다는 것보다 물 가운데 세미마루의 그림자를 그려 노흔 뒤에 그 그림자에 나의 왼 정열을 부읏고 멀건히 안저잇는 자미에 實物 세미마루를 보고 시픈 안탁가움을 니즌 것이엿섯다. 내 마음ㅅ속에 꿈으로 잠겨 잇든 세미마루는 여기서 名實이 가즌 「꿈의 사람」이 되어 버린 것이엿섯다.
이 가을을 낙시질로 보내는 동안 세미마루는 實在性을 일코 나의 마음ㅅ속에서 한 우상으로 화하여 버리고 말엇다. 그러고 세미마루가 내 마음에서 우상화하는 동시에, 몃해 전 어린 시절에 홀연히 종적이 업서지면서부터 나의 마음ㅅ속에 아름다운 우상으로 화하여 버린 메리와 合一하여 버렷다.

그 해도 거진 다간 섯달 금음께 엇던 날 金煥이 北京으로 가는 길에 집에 들엇다. 그날 밤 煥을 보내는 뜻으로 엇던 일본 料亭에서 小宴을 열엇다. 그러고 오래간만에 세미마루를 만낫다.
그 날을 긔회로 나의 마음의 정열은 다시 폭발되엿다. 나의 발길은 저녁만 되면 또다시 たまや며 七星館으로 가게 되엿다. 또다시 침묵의 련애는 시작되여 게속되엿다.

이듬해ㅅ 이른봄 무슨 일로 한 달쯤 평양을 떠낫든 나는 평양으로 도라온 날 밤으로 세미마루의 종적이 업서진 것을 알엇다. 나의 수저운 마음은 料亭 女將에게 세미마루의 간 곳에 대하여 똑똑이 뭇지도 못하고 이튼 날 丸天 주인 노파에게 다시 물어서 겨우 서울 伊勢屋이라는 집 주인 녕감의 소실로 팔려갓다는 말을 들엇다.
「그 애가 떠나는 날 집에 왓더니...」
노파는 이런 말을 하엿다.
「可哀想ね. 울먹울먹하면서 떠나더니... 긴상 도라오시거던 よろしく 해 달라고 신신이 부탁을 하면서...」
이런 말도 하엿다.
이러한 몃 가지의 말 아페 나는 말도 못하고 믁묵히 안저 잇섯다. 그러고 몃해 전에 내 아페서 홀연히 종적이 업섯저 버린 금발의 소녀 메리-와 가튼 자최를 밟어서 여기서 또한 나의 아페서 홀연히 자최가 사라저 업서진 흑발의 소녀와 나의 새에 얼키엿든 쓸쓸하고 애연한 인연 때문에 한숨을 쉬엿다.
며츨 뒤에 서울로 올라가서 電話簿를 뒤적여서 伊勢屋을 차저본 결과 전당국 요릿집 술장사 吳服店 이러케 네 가지의 伊勢屋을 차저 내인 나는 그 어느 곳에 세미마루가 가 잇는지 판단을 나릴 수가 업시 전화부를 <169> 내여던젓다. 일일히 그네 잡을 차저 도라다니면서 가 잇는 곳을 집어 내이기에는 나는 너무도 小膽하엿다. 설혹 가 잇는 곳을 안다 할지라도 그 뒤에 행할 조처도 업섯다.

그 뒤에 나는 다시 그를 만나지 못하엿다. 그보다 썩 뒤에 大連에서 다시 ひたりづま를 잡고 나섯다는 풍문은 들엇스되 그것이 사실인지 거짓인지도 알수 업다.
다만 어린 시절에 한때 몹시도 나의 마음을 떨리게 한 뒤 홀연히 종적이 업서진 메리-와 청년긔에 들어서 한동안 또한 나로 하여금 바보가티 만드러 노흔 뒤에 또한 종적이 업서저 버린 세미마루- 하나는 混血兒 하나는 일본 소녀- 이 두 소녀는 나의 머리에 가장 아름다운 우상으로 남어서 나로 하여금 늘 즐겁고도 애연한 회상에 잠기게 한다.

木村葉子-木村葉子-그는
지금도 健在한지. 그의 生死며 行方을 알 수가 업는지라 나의 마음은 더욱 그 애연한 인연 때문을 눈물겨워진다. (此項完) <170>
