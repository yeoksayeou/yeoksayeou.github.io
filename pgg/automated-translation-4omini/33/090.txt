一問一答記(1)  
						李敦化  
記  What is the most difficult thing on stage?  
李  More than anything, it is how to make the audience listen well. If I were to say that it does not matter whether others listen or not, then there would be no difficulty at all. However, to make sure others listen well is the most troublesome thing. <23>  

**Summary**  

This article features an interview with Lee Dunhwa discussing the challenges he faces while speaking on stage, particularly emphasizing the effort required to engage the audience. This insight into the life of a speaker reveals the vulnerabilities and pressures that come with public speaking during the colonial period in Korea, shedding light on the cultural dynamics of performance and communication that historians might find intriguing, especially in terms of the role of oratory in the context of Korean identity under colonial rule.

記  演壇에서 제일 苦心되는 일이 무엇임닛가.
李  무엇보다도 엇지하면 여러 聽衆이 잘 듯게 할가 하는 그것임니다. 말을 하여서 남이 듯던 안이 듯던 그것을 不關한다면 巳어니와 남이 잘 듯도록 하자면 그처럼 苦心되는 일은 업슴니다. <23>
