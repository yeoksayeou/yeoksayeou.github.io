Q&A Record (4)  
Kim Yu-pyeong  
Q: What is the most pressing thing you feel while working in the newspaper company's research department?  
A: More than anything, I wish there were economic research bodies in our civil society. It is quite disheartening to rely solely on those of the government.

  

**Summary**  

In this brief exchange, the author expresses a desire for independent economic research organizations within civil society, emphasizing a dissatisfaction with reliance on government resources. This highlights a significant aspect of Korean colonial history regarding the limitations faced by Koreans in accessing unbiased information and analysis, reflecting the broader struggle for self-determination and the development of civil institutions during a time of colonial rule. Historians might find the longing for institutional autonomy and economic empowerment particularly revealing of the socio-economic challenges encountered under colonial governance.
