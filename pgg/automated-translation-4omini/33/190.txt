Traffic Survey Grand Prize  

Last month's traffic survey provided us with substantial benefits and interest. In that regard, we wish to conduct another traffic survey with the following regulations, hoping for many respondents.

Question  

From 8:30 AM to 9:30 AM on December 20 (Saturday), how many people will pass by the six-way intersection in Anguk-dong, Gyeongseong?  
(Counting passengers of trams, automobiles, and various vehicles)  
On that day, our company will conduct a real traffic survey.  
If it rains or if there are any breakdowns, the survey will be postponed to the same time on December 22.  

One correct respondent will receive a prize of 10 won.  
The top 10 closest to the exact number will each receive a specially prepared prize.  
Entries must be submitted by December 19.  
Reference = For expected references, please see page 177 of this issue.<52>  

**Summary**  

This article announces a traffic survey conducted in Gyeongseong (now Seoul) and offers a monetary prize for guessing the number of people passing through a specific intersection. It reflects the era's interest in urban planning and traffic management, hinting at the colonial government's focus on infrastructure development during the Japanese occupation of Korea. Historians might find it interesting that such public involvement in surveys shows an early form of civic engagement, despite the overarching political oppression of the time.

交通調査 大懸賞
前月의 交通調査는 만흔 實益과 興味를 우리에게 주엇슬 줄 밋슴니다. 그러한 意味에서 다음과 가튼 規定으로 다시 한번 交通調査를 하게 되엿스니 만흔 應募가 잇기를 바람니다.

問題

12月 20日(土曜) 오전 8시 반부터 同 9시 반까지에 京城 安國洞 六거리로 지나가는 사람이 몃이나 되겟는가.
(電車, 自動車, 諸車 乘客까지 合算)
當日 本社에서 實際 交通數를 實地 調査함
當日 雨天이거나 故障이 잇슬 시는 22日 同時刻으로 함.

정답자 일인 賞金 拾圓進呈
正數에 가장 갓갑게 맛친 10名에게 두루맥이 한 감씩 진정
期日 12月 19日까지 入亟되도록
