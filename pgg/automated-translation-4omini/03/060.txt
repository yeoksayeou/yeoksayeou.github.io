기사제목	(무제-국편)  
기사형태	잡저  

After Lee Tae-jo ascended the throne, a celebration was held at Songdu Gyeongdeok Palace. The great leader, Taejo, was so filled with joy that after drinking wine with several of his功臣 (officials), he said, "Today is a good day, so let's not differentiate between the military and civil officials and share some jokes." He then pointed to the National Master, Mu-hak Dae-sa, and said, "Your face resembles that of a pig." The National Master, without missing a beat, replied, "Your face resembles that of a person, Your Majesty." Taejo then asked, "What does that mean in the context of joking?" 

Mu-hak, continuing humorously, said, "A person sees a person, and a pig sees a pig."

  

**Summary**

This article recounts a humorous incident during a celebration held by King Lee Tae-jo of the Goryeo Dynasty, highlighting the camaraderie and humorous exchanges between the king and his officials. It reflects a cultural moment where hierarchy was temporarily set aside in favor of merriment and jesting, showcasing a more personal side of governance. Historians might find this account revealing for understanding the dynamics of power and civility during the Goryeo era and how such moments of levity might serve to strengthen bonds among rulers and their subjects.
