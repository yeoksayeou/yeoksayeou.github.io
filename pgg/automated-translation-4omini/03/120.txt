People on the Road  
Yi Gwang-su  
A distinguished gentleman! Amidst the turmoil of a crowded scene at the Gwanghwamun station of the Dong-A Ilbo (East Asia Daily), where people are coming and going, talking loudly, there he stands! Wearing a bowler hat and a gray overcoat, not even donning a full coat, he noticeably waits for the Dongdaemun-bound tram, ignoring the Gwanghun tram passing by. 

His face is broad, and his cheekbones seem a bit prominent, yet his eyes shine with brilliance, albeit imbued with a melancholy expression! If he had a slightly higher nose, one could almost mistake him for a westerner. Indeed, a passerby remarks, "That gentleman looks like a westerner yet wears a Korean overcoat." 

The response from a Seoul gentleman, seemingly his friend, explains, "No, he is quite a famous writer from Korea; that's Yi Gwang-su, the current chief editor of the Dong-A Ilbo." 

To those who do not know him, it might appear as though a foreigner in Korean attire is walking by, but in reality, this is the esteemed figure in Korean literature, now heading home to his place in Samdong near Dongdaemun as evening falls. 

Miss Shin Albert  
A lady dressed in a long skirt and wearing rubber shoes, with hair that could either be in a fashionable Western bob or tied up, making it hard to discern for a man, carries a handbag with something inside. One might hastily assume she is elderly. However, she is none other than Miss Shin Albert, who has had a long relationship with Christianity, and possibly finds it wearisome. She is the president of the Korean Young Women's Association and the head of the Korean Women's Academy, actively involved, yet she often wanders about in confusion, accompanied by her daughter, leaving a disabled son at home.

**Summary**  

The article profiles two individuals seen at the bustling Gwanghwamun station in Seoul. Yi Gwang-su is identified as a prominent Korean writer wearing traditional clothing, demonstrating the intersection of Western and Korean identities in colonial Korea. Miss Shin Albert is depicted as a Christian lady involved in social work, reflecting the active role of women in the society of that era. Historians may find interest in this depiction of the daily life and the cultural dynamics of Korea during Japanese colonial rule, showcasing how individuals navigated their identities amidst colonial tensions.

路上의 人
李光洙氏
새로 지은 東亞日報社 압 널은 마당 光化門停留場에 사람이 오락가락 올느고 나리고 밧구어 타고 대혼잡대소란 중에 안쫑다리 거름으로 빨이 오는 紳士 한 분! 중절모자에 회색 두루막이, 외투도 안 입은 채로 光熙門行 電車가 지나가도 아니타는 것을 보면 東大門行 電車을 기다리는 것이 분명하다.
얼골은 넓으데데, 광대뼈 좀 소슨 듯, 그 光이 玲瓏한 眼彩, 그러나 노로스롬한 눈! 코만 조곰 놉핫스면 서양 사림이라고 하여도 누구나 속을 만콤, 아닌게 아니라 엽해 섯는 村 사람가튼 양반 한 분의 하는 말, 「저 사람 서양 사람 가튼데 朝鮮 두루막이 입엇네.」
그의 친구인듯한 서울 양반의 하는 대답.
「아니야 저이가 朝鮮서도 유명한 文士 李光洙라네.」
모르는 사람은 서양 사람이 朝鮮 옷 입고 지나가는 줄로 헛보지만 실인즉 朝鮮文學界에 일홈이 놉고 지금은 東亞日報編輯局長으로 게신 春園 李光洙氏가 사무를 맛치고 느진 저녁때 東小門 밧 三洞 자택을 향하여 도라가시는 판.

申알베트 女史
긴 치마에 고무신 신고 트레머리인지 쪽진 머리인지 남자가 보아서는 분간 못할 머리에 빗검은 벙거지를 쓰고 무엇이 들엇는지 손가방 들고 다니는 로부인 한 분! 지레짐작하고 전도 부인인 줄 아라서는 안됨니다. 긔독교에 오래 관계햇던 이만큼 긔독교라면 지긋지긋해 하는 申알베트녀사이심니다. 조선여자청년회장으로 조선녀자학원장으로 활동이 만흐서서 불구의 아들을 집에 두고 따님 한 분 다리고 늘 길에서 갈팡질팡 몹시도 밧부게 다니심니다.＜39＞
