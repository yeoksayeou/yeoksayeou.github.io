Sudden Wealth  
                      双S  
There is an actor named Kim Ryeo-ho, who performs excellently in the theater. Because he plays the role of women so well, when he puts on a female mask, applies makeup, and wears women's clothes, no one can tell he is not truly a woman.  

One summer, while wandering around, trying to earn some money and getting caught in the rainy season, after a lot of struggle, he ended up visiting a relative living deep in the mountains.  

As he walked alone on the unfamiliar mountain path, his legs were weary, and his stomach was empty, with still a hundred miles to go. The sun began to set, and it started to get dark in the mountains.  

In a place where one would be afraid to walk alone even in daylight, nighttime had come, making it pitch dark, and he was so scared that it felt like ghosts might attack him, causing his heart to race as he strained to keep his footing.  

With trembling steps, as he climbed the mountain ridge... it was a strange occurrence. In the dead of night, on the right side of the ridge was a cave, and inside it sat a very old man.  

In his fear, he had no mind for anything else, and feeling delighted, he approached and said, "Excuse me, I do not know who you are, but may I ask you a moment?"  
The old man replied, "Who are you?"  
"I'm Kim Ryeo-ho from a mountain village in Gangwon-do."  
When the old man heard 'Kim Ryeo-ho', he laughed and said, "Oh? A black fox from the mountains of Gangwon-do? Haha, foxes are known to be good at transforming into humans, so you look like you really do know how to take on a human shape..."  
Thinking he was a fox, Kim said, "Well, my profession involves wearing masks and transforming. I can play the role of a woman or even a child."  
The old man then said, "Then there must be a way to transform into a woman here since we’re in the mountains... then transform for me now."  
Kim replied, "Yes! That would be easy. I will put on my female mask."  
And Kim Ryeo-ho took out the mask he had brought, applied makeup, and dressed in women's clothing.  

In the dark, he stood there and it looked as though he had immediately transformed into a beautiful lady.  
The old man laughed, "Wow, really impressive. You’ve done it even better than I expected... now let me tell you, I am actually a hundred-year-old great snake (large snake). I wear a human mask every day to catch people, but I can’t transform into an old person at night."  
Kim exclaimed in shock, "What? You are a great snake? Please, just spare my life. Please!"  
Unable to escape, Kim pleaded desperately.  
The great snake said, "I shall let you live. I have never eaten a fox! But how is it that you can transform into anything in this world? It seems there is nothing you fear.”  
Kim answered, “There are certainly things that frighten me. I truly believe the scariest thing in this world is money.”  
The great snake asked, “Money? How could such a thing frighten you?”  
Kim replied, “Of course it does! Truly terrifying. Even a ten-won note or a hundred-won note, even small coins are nothing compared to my fear of money. What is the scariest thing for you?”  
The great snake said, “The most frightening thing for me is one thing... but I must never speak of it aloud. I must keep it a secret.”  
Kim replied, “Yes!”  
The great snake continued, “In fact, I fear cigarette butts. Just touching that sticky cigarette butt makes my body rot... but if people found out, it would be a disaster.”  
Kim said, “Please make sure no one finds out. Well then, I shall take my leave.”  
The great snake replied, “Today you must sleep over at my place. We can share our secrets about capturing people..."  
Kim protested, “No! The road is urgent.”  
The great snake then said, “Then go safely. Do come back again.”  
Kim said, “Yes, take care.”  

Finally, he had escaped! Kim Ryeo-ho, sweating all over, hurried down the mountain and then in the distance, he saw a light flickering.  

That light was as welcoming as a home from the afterlife, and he dashed towards it to find it was indeed a house. He knocked on the door and it took a while, as if the owner had just woken up, before they answered.  
Kim said, "I’m just a traveler and have nowhere to stay, kindly grant me a place for the night."  
The owner replied, "I have no room to give. But which road did you come from?"  
Kim answered, "I just came down from that mountain ridge."  
The owner widened his eyes in shock, asking, "What? You just came from that ridge?"  
Kim confirmed, "Yes, I came that way."  
The owner exclaimed, "Oh, are you serious? That ridge has a great snake that has been known to catch and eat travelers, so no one dares to go there."  
Kim replied, "Yes, that great snake is still there. I just saw it."  
The owner, astonished, exclaimed, "Sir, are you really a person or something else?"  
Kim said, "I genuinely encountered that great snake and, after some conversation, learned what it fears. If you grant me a place to sleep tonight, I will tell you what it fears."  
The owner replied, "That would be wonderful. Please do tell, come in though, we might be dirty, but let me take you to a room."  

Inside, they had dinner, and the owner asked, “So what is that snake truly afraid of?”  
Kim replied, "It fears cigarette butts. The sticky residue found within them makes it rot."  
The owner said, "What? Simply cigarette butts? We have plenty of those!"  

From the early hours of the next day, the owner began to roam around the village, spreading the news and collecting cigarette butts from every house. Soon enough, everyone gathered together and made their way up the ridge with the cigarette butts on hand.  

The great snake was in immense trouble, thinking, “Surely that fox from Gangwon-do told them about my fear.” Filled with rage, it vanished suddenly, avoiding capture.  

After that, the villagers continued to live without fear, grateful for Kim Ryeo-ho who they feasted and celebrated while also gathering money for him.  

With that money, he returned to his home village in Gangwon-do, living without a worry.  

However, the great snake, who had left in anger, fumed and planned to take revenge. Three years later, it finally found Kim Ryeo-ho's home. The great snake, disguised as the old man, approached the house.  

As Kim came out, he sensed a great danger. It was undoubtedly the same old man from three years ago. His heart racing, he said, "How did you come to this distant place? Please, come up to the porch."  

But the great snake merely glared fiercely and shouted, "You fox!"  
Kim cried, "Oh no, please just spare my life!"  
The great snake fiercely retorted, "No. You let others know the thing I fear! But today, I also brought with me what I fear the most."  
It then held out its hands and began tossing coins like pebbles onto the porch, shouting, “Well, aren’t you frightened?”  

The great snake sped away (The End).  

**Summary**  

This tale of Kim Ryeo-ho interweaves themes of fear, transformation, and the cleverness to outwit a supernatural being. He uses his stage craft both to escape from danger and to aid the villagers. The interesting aspect for historians may be Kim's representation as an actor, possibly reflecting the evolving social roles and expectations during the colonial period, especially in how individuals maneuvered societal threats by leveraging unique skills and narratives.
