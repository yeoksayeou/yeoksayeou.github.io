Feeling at the Beginning of the Year  
						Kim Jun-yeon, Chosun Ilbo  
It is difficult to speak simply regarding such matters. As for what special things we should do at the New Year beyond our daily affairs, various thoughts can be expressed. However, rather than presenting my subjective opinions or assertions about what absolutely must be done in the New Year, I would like to discuss one thing from an objective standpoint, which is that what began in 1926 should naturally be continued into 1927 and should, to some extent, be debated or resolved. This is not a trivial matter. (The following 8 lines are omitted from the original text) <105>  

**Summary**  

The article discusses reflections and considerations surrounding the New Year, emphasizing the importance of continuity from the previous year and the need for resolutions to be made in a thoughtful manner. Notably, it illustrates the tension between personal aspirations and collective societal responsibilities in the context of Korea's colonial reality. Historians may find interest in how such discussions reflect the consciousness of the Korean populace during a time of significant political and social change under Japanese rule.
