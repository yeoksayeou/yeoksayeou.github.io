Drawing the Tokki  

I draw the Tokki. I draw the Tokki. Call the painter. Call the painter over here. I draw the Tokki. Li Jeok-seon paints the phoenix (鳳凰) on the Phoenix Terrace (鳳凰臺). The southern kingdom's emperor (南國天子) paints the sun and moon (日月) on the Lingxu Terrace (凌虛臺), the painter of King Yan Zhao (燕昭王) paints a horse on the Golden Terrace (黃金臺), in the Dongting (洞庭) blue and yellow inkstone (琉璃靑黃硯), grinding the cuttlefish (烏賊魚) for ink, releasing the brush (兩頭畵筆) with the daub, drawing here and there on the white silk (白綾) snowy flower (雪花) paper. Drawing the scenes of famous mountains and places of the world (天下 名山勝地間) with the outlines of snow.

Drawing the parrot (鸚鵡) and peacock (孔雀) like the sounds they make. In the clouds and mist (蓬萊雲霧) of the abode of immortals, I draw my taste of beauty. I draw the orchids (蘭草) and medicinal herbs (芝草), with the fragrances of all kinds (香草) on my lips. I draw the feet leaping in the flower forest of harmony (萬和方暢花林). In the severe cold of mid-winter (大寒嚴冬寒), I draw the fur (防風) that protects from the wind. On the left is the blue mountain (靑山) and on the right is the green water (綠水). In the place of green water and blue mountains (綠水靑山), in the shade of the cassia tree (계수 나무), I draw the swaying clouds and mist. My ears are pointed, my eyes are round. My waist is slender. My tail is bushy. My legs are truncated. My rear legs are long. What else is there to add beyond this, oh, will it be the moon of Mt. Emei (峨眉山) and the half moon (半輪兎)? Oh, ancient one. It is you, the master clerk (主簿).

**Summary**

This article presents a whimsical and imaginative portrayal of an artistic endeavor to draw the Tokki (a mythical creature in Korean folklore) using vivid descriptions of various elements of nature, traditional landscapes, and cultural motifs. It captures the spirit of creativity and the fondness for artistic representation prevalent during the 1920s Korea. Historians might find this work interesting for its reflection of cultural identity and artistic expression during the period of Japanese colonial rule, as it hints at the ways in which traditional Korean motifs were held dear despite external influences.

톡기화상
톡기 화상을 그린다. 톡기 화상을 그린다. 화공을 불너라. 화공을 불넛소. 톡기 화상을 그린다. 李謫仙鳳凰臺에 鳳 그리든 환쟁이. 南國天子凌虛臺에 日月 그리든 환쟁이, 燕昭王의 黃金臺에 말 그리든 환쟁이, 洞庭琉璃靑黃硯, 錦水秋波거북硯滴, 烏賊魚 불너 먹을 갈아, 兩頭畵筆 다북 풀어, 白綾雪花簡紙上에 이리 저리 그린다. 天下 名山勝地間에 景槪보든 눈 그리고. 鸚鵡 孔雀 지적울 제 소리 듯든 귀 그리고. 方丈蓬萊雲霧 중에 내 잘 맛든 코 그리고. 蘭草芝草 온갓 香草 꼿따 먹든 입 그리고. 萬和方暢花林 중에 펄펄 뛰든 발 그리고. 大寒嚴冬寒 중에 防風하든 털 그리고. 좌편은 靑山이요 우편은 綠水로다. 綠水靑山 깁흔 곳에 계수 나무 그늘 속 앙금조춤 펄펄 뛰여. 두귀는 쫑굿, 두 눈은 도리. 허리는 잘눅. 꽁지는 몽툭. 압발은 짜르고. 뒤발은 길어. 깡청. 뛰여가는 峨眉山月半輪兎인들 이에서 더할소냐. 아나 옛다. 鱉主簿야. 네가 지고 잇거라.＜125＞
