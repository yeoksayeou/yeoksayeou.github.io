The Clever Wife

One day, in a household where a married couple lived, the husband returned home after working hard all day. He expected his wife to be waiting for him with a warm meal, but upon arriving, he found the door firmly locked. The usually composed husband, unable to endure his frustration, broke the lock with a stone and entered the house.

He stood there, staring blankly at the ceiling. Glancing over, he spotted a note on the writing desk in his wife’s handwriting. It read, “I am going out to watch a moving picture and have locked the door. I have hidden the key under the mat by the door.”

The husband's reaction was, “What a clever wife she is.” <139> 

**Summary**

This short narrative humorously illustrates the cleverness of a wife who prioritizes her own entertainment over immediately welcoming her husband home. It reflects the changing dynamics in marital relationships during the 1920s in Korea, where women's agency begins to emerge within the domestic sphere. Historians may find interest in the depiction of household life during colonial times, highlighting the slow but significant shifts in gender roles and personal independence among women.

천정만 처다보고 멍-하니 섯것다.
