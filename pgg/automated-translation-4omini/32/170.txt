Autumn Streetscapes of Men and Women  
Choi Yi-sun  

Time is indeed flying fast. The greenery is gone before we know it, and the autumn leaves have already taken on their colors, so it is undoubtedly autumn.

However, before the leaves in the mountains change color, it is the clothing of the elegantly dressed men in the city that has already turned to autumn. Where have those charcoal-colored overcoats and fresh white felt hats gone? If they try to push their summer hats down, they have nothing but a ragged hat that would suit an opium addict or a beggar. 

In recent years, in Seoul and indeed in Joseon… one can often see young men of the so-called "mopo" style, hurriedly wiping their sweat while donning winter hats. However, despite all the talk about the fashion of 1930, it seems that the truly extraordinary (or "out of the ordinary") sight of men wearing autumn-winter suits and white felt hats does not happen yet. For some reason, the entire appearance of men's clothing suddenly changed in unison within just 2 or 3 days. From wide-brimmed hats to shoes, everything changed from light to dark. There is no controversy about the seasonal change in clothing; rather, it is hard to see many men trying to dress well, exceeding their means (i.e., not fitting their economic status).

In a newly tailored suit, with a handkerchief hanging loosely from one pocket, a latest style winter hat perched on their head, and a red spotted tie on a clear blue background, the sight of them strutting down the street is absolutely nauseating. You see them staring at the display in department store shop windows, mesmerized by the fresh neckties, and at night running between bars and cafes like rabid dogs—this pseudo-intellectual look is even worse.

However, they are not the only performers adorning the streets in the mid-autumn season. There are also the earnest young men, in their worn but neatly pressed suits, heading off to work, giving off a sort of attachment like a pearl in the sand, and above all, they add a certain freshness to the autumn streets.

The autumn breeze brightens the procession on the streets: the chaotic dance of fashionable hats, the stumble of a beggar born of destitution, and the cheerful gait of strong laborers. All of this describes a mixed parade as the autumn urban street quietly deepens.

In the countryside, women…  
Kim An-soo

This is a scene that can frequently be seen on the streets of Seoul, but it is rather scarce in the fields, depicted in the female theater episode that occurred on the way back from the station in Jeongju, Pyeongbuk. 

I saw a woman who was barely taller than average. From the beginning, her long face, coupled with her "kkodori" hairstyle, stretched her face longer. The "kkodori" hairstyle is so extravagant that it could cover heaven-kissing nostrils and yet is not an over-exaggeration. The jeogori is hardly to be evaluated as just a jeogori; it is so clumsily worn that it hangs about her waist like a mischievous child teasing the wind. The short skirt that barely covers her knees and the high stockings flaunt her long legs, and her ungainly black rubber shoes create a shocking contrast.

With her uneven hand, she fiddles with her "kkodori", showing care for her hairstyle, moving to a place with an awkward gait while speaking in charming Japanese notes laced with the phrase "Pingyang, one piece, please." 

In the 1930s, I witnessed the beauty of Joseon here. And frankly speaking, seeing women in the streets of Seoul should not seem strange in any way, regardless of their differences.

"The more one longs, the more one hates, like the ancient saying." I cannot help but think about "modenizumu" (modernism).  

High Skies, Plump Women!  
Lee Tae-jun  

They say "the sky is high, and the horse is fat," which is a humble saying from the old days, and perhaps it refers to nurturing the home. In autumn, one tends to gain weight. Horses grow fatter, and mackerels swimming in the sea get fat with the thick spread of oil.  

However, as for the people on the streets, we cannot notice the weight gain of the horses or taste the savory oil flavor of the mackerel. All we see are the women soaring fresh autumn breezes, around whom, on evening promenades, we catch glimpses of their legs that might break their stockings.  

A New Trend in Women’s Scenery  
Ro-ah  

In the city of Seoul, where autumn’s sentiment unfurls.. Jongno. The sickly leaves of lining trees groan as they mock their demise and flutter down to the heads of passersby, but there are few who grasp the sadness. Is there no neurotic poet capturing the sorrow of falling leaves and autumnal moonlight in this city? 

The fields have ripened grain, and the mountains are ablaze with colors, yet the city dwellers have no need to know about these autumn blessings. Look! The winter hats have flooded the streets all at once, and the scent exuding from office workers’ spring and autumn clothing reminds one of things locked away in the vaults of the pawnshop.

Spring hats are merely stuck atop the heads of ill-fated alter egos. The uneven hemlines of skirts flutter like dandelion seeds. Light colors symbolize the fallen leaves, but is it an act of artistic harmony? A lively woman has come out wearing a fur neck warmer at night. Soon… who knows, it will be autumn again. 

Recently, the presence of new women in the streets of Seoul seems to have increased. Among them, the lively silken colors and patterns of their garments catch public attention, radiating an overwhelming charm as they pass by! In their anonymity lies an air of elegance stemming from ignorance. 

Flesh-colored shoes. Soft silk stockings. Their hair done up in short layers. Big colorful bows on the back of their heads. Well-formed calves like doe legs. Carrying large handbags nestled at their chests.  

I wonder if this girl is around seventeen or eighteen. In the splendor of her haute couture, she leans against a tree while flirting with someone, or perhaps with a friend. Her expression, perfectly aligned with her outfit, suggests she has refined her technique with decades of gazing into mirrors. What type of unclassified beauty might this youthful woman represent? 

Such mixed emotions stir within the minds of boys flipping out their fantasies upon seeing four or five beautiful girls walking by.

Here comes a gisaeng. A siren reminiscent of Mephistopheles. What homages will descend next? Yet, I can only bear witness to the manifestation of this new trend in women's allure. 

Rubber shoes shining white like snow that has been soap-bubbled are appropriately fitted to her dainty tree-like feet, leaving behind a faint love sound with each step. They are the most unrestrained and free women in Joseon. They are the rulers of fashion. Behold! Now they employ their art of eroticism! Pulling back their skirts to reveal a brash pair of shorts may add a liberal view on sexuality. This logic of flaret-blooming hems frequently employed by gisaengs is probably just a ploy of clever trickery.

Yet it is still abstract as "ero" (eroticism). It is sorta remaining in the deep traditions of Joseon, teeming with "twilight ero."

Women graduates donning rubber shoes, head bobbed, return to graceful homes alongside their suited husbands, strolling through Jongno with a gentle demeanor. The lingering sentiment of Joseon is persistent.

It is quite possible to see brave beauty in such scenarios. There are splendid women pulling rickshaws like dogs in a gutter, clad in chic furs, radiating robust glossiness. With every color of their hair neatly coiffed, their locks of thick curly hair bring to mind the bounds of rampant desire. Ample outlines of their visages lure the eyes! Whose concubines might they be? Are they the ones of Han River? The rickshaw where they ride is unable to rejoice in the honor of carrying such an enchanting sight. They forever miss their chance, just like the cars heading to Seodaemun or Dongdaemun, shrieking in frustration as they pass. 

After abandoning five rickshaws, as I turned to take another look, I glimpsed the masterful sorrow turning into a mischievous smile in the lady’s face, full of longing and yearning. Perhaps she could not grasp the charming laughter, her waist encircled in a high-priced summer skirt revealing trouble with the steam brought up by the sun, fluttering amidst the thinning autumn days.

And yet the season holds chilly sentiments!  

The Operator's Terminal  
Park Jin  

Heroine  
In Joseon, around twenty, short cut hair, glasses, wearing a fur scarf, colorfully patterned hat, outright stylish overcoat, silk socks, patent leather shoes. Her legs were thin, her figure slim, so much so that her body was insipid enough to bring forth no erotic appeal. The stockings were twisted.  

Her complexion, rated fifty-five, with a vastly oiled face only added to her unattractiveness. She was devoid of knowledge and her speech was also average. She did indeed wear the hat tilting low. An unidentified young lady.

Street Fashion of Tailors  
P.S.S  

As evening descends, Jongno Street appears incomparably vibrant.  
Tailor shops specializing in Korean attire have vanished, while those specializing in Western suits seem to sparkle. 

The charred, rich brown of tweed jackets hugs the figures like a suit freshly made in deep blue fabric, tracing out the shapes they form.

When you see the well-tailored suit-wearing men, you might wonder if they mistakenly believe they are clad in the best, most impressive suits in the world. If I were a beauty in my prime and if these men would strictly carry out my command with their very “fine suits,” couldn’t I decree? “My friends! If any of you can now bear to boast of that suit you’re talismaned with, then strip off it and walk out in your flesh!” Would Jongno transform immediately into a flood of naked bodies?  

At Anguk-dong Six Street  
Song-hui  

Alive with the morning freshness, I saunter through Anguk-dong intersection.  

Upon a small faded purple fedora, there are telltale signs of sweat. With splotchy skin and dull features, a not-so-youthful fellow pupils from this school.  

In a junior high uniform that’s reminiscent of a patterned suit, a XX student is leaning on the sidewalk, hands plunged into his pocket, while his left pants pocket bulges uncontrollably due to its stylish fit—overly shiny hair showcases the remnants of last night’s wash!

The flat bottomed, wider cut coat of an XX student, with a babyish smile appearing odd. Is he heading to a serious class? Perhaps someone is about to ask if he will be hired looking for the spring entrance exam to hit the market?

With the warm sun on their faces, the girls’ laughter in their vibrant skirts they combed with dazzling red lines, causing all boys passing by to pay admiration and even expectation to the richest ones amongst them.

The rickshaws driven by gentleman-like dudes high up past their younger twenties glide smoothly down the street, yet they seem not to catch everyone’s eyes, especially in places like Jongno.

Causes of the Pawnshop  
Kim Kyungsun  

As autumn drags on slightly, men in their winter suits, wrinkled and poorly folding, lurk on the streets. There may be a lack of tailoring on their part but could it instead be the cause of the pawnshop? Seeing men wearing wrinkled suits escorting women creeping down the streets like treasures adds a pitiful dimension to the scenery. Others must have witnessed such settings?

The Odd Girl Behind the Zizhong  
Baek Ryung  

Just past midnight, as I stepped out to bid farewell to the guests, the fresh nightly breeze shivered against my skin. After a car zoomed by, the cool lamplight moved in hazy patterns, illuminating shadows that danced around me.

“Take care!” 

“Farewell!” 

As I turned back, the sound of soft footsteps echoed, and suddenly, I caught a girl moving gracefully past me and into the alley.

This was the third time seeing her.  

Once by the Han River...  

As she approached the boat, she gathered her clothes, but there was something familiar about her. She wore glasses, robustly framing a round face that belied solemn expressions under change yet seemed too attractive to keep up.

Standing nearby was a middle-aged man with a chubby appearance speaking with an accent of Chungcheong Province.

The two of them were not insinuated couples to my uninterested eye, but appeared like a playful, cheerful couple casually indulging in treats like beer, fruit, or sodas with not a care in their hearts.

Seeing her again in a dim lighted cafe, she appeared as if flitting away with a delicate gait towards the Temple Street while he reluctantly peeled off to another pathway, sounding somewhat anxious, “Are you coming tomorrow?” 

Her nonchalant response trailed off, whispering, “We’ll see.” I had thought they were a married couple, yet they must have got unresolved plans.

This time, it’s the night. She was cloaked in an appealing long coat that wrapped around her lush figure, revealing enviable curves. 

What has gone of that man? I pondered, and my intrigue soon shifted to the mystery of who she could be? What’s her story, and where has she been? 

You can’t be too starry-eyed and bouncy, but something is still nasty captivating me about her elusive identity!

Attire in Various Colors  
Bang Chun-hae  

The autumn streets’ female scenery—a mysterious title. Easy yet difficult. I can hardly muster to write just as simply as standing on Jongno street and peering into the fashion of women.  
In summary, autumn rides on artificial silk waves. Scattered after a rain shower, they sparkle in the mid-afternoon light like dragonflies soaring in the summer sky.  

Thus has come the age of synthetic silk.  

Though I may not have spotted beautiful women’s faces, I often encountered the dazzling artificial silk that overpowers the eye. When I finally bore witness to the woman draped in sturdy native attire, the contrast painted a vivid picture akin to a pinned red flower within a sea of green, revealing her greater beauty than one presumed. This, I suspect, is traditionally better at wooing men, covering any curiosity to flaunt personal beauty. 

Dance is no longer performed alone in theaters; it too has invaded the streets. With outrageously short skirts pinned to the air, she dances with no hesitation, as if the sounds from either gramophone or radio inspire her to follow suit. 

However, in some respects, the attention falls on those women who unfold their beautiful figures within classical attire as if reduced to a short skirt, yet this proves more riveting than if they walk with short skirts.

Why can’t they find a suitable medium in the lengths of dresses or skirts? If I were to take on the role of a woman, I would return to the roots and question the lot of them tirelessly! 

There is a trend for bizarre styles in jeogori these days, making the traditions all the more distorted. It is customary to see deep levels of gaping fabric, exposing seamlines at the shoulders of a traditionally composed beneath garment; could women be rising to forsake the jeogori, leaving only a skirt in fashion? 

Amidst the varied methods of color usage, there’s always a bright streak, akin to festive blooms, as educated girls style headwear together with striking medleys of color in tandem with fragrant scents to brush about them.

It is a remarkable hustle about the street of diverse fashions, coupled with boys left bewildered by any hopes of maturity among the countless changes abound.  

**Summary**

In this piece, the author paints a vivid picture of autumn street life in the city, reflecting on the changing fashions of men and women during the 1930s in Korea. It captures a societal transition where the western-style clothing becomes increasingly prevalent, juxtaposed against traditional attire, revealing social status and cultural shifts. The description of young men and women navigating their fashion choices amidst the constraints of economics and contemporary standards is particularly insightful, showcasing the struggles and aspirations during this period of colonial rule in Korea. Historians may find the commentary on clothing and the fashion narrative significant as it reflects broader themes of modernization and western influence on Korean society.

가을거리의 男女風景
口逆질 나는 男性
崔義順

속절업는 세월 빠르기도 하지.
녹음은 어느 듯 어데로 가고 단풍이 이미 물드럿스니 가을이 지텃슴은 분명하고나.

그러나 산속의 단풍이 물들기도 전에 도시의 가로에는 먼저 물드른 것이 잇스니 양장 남성들의 겨울을 재촉하는 복장들이 그것이다.
연탄색 포라나 새하얀 아사 양복에 그 새뜻한 맥고들은 다-어데로 갓는지? 억지로 여름 모자를 차저내랴면 이제는 아편 중독자와 거지의 두상에 언친 밋구녕 빠진 맥고(?)밧게는 업다.
근년에 와서 서울에도-아니조선에도...라 할가-땀훔치노라 분주하면서도 伏中에 겨울 모자 쓰고 다니는 소위 모뽀라는 도련님을 흔히 볼 수 잇섯다.
그러나 제아무리 1930년식이라고 떠들어도 秋冬洋服을 닙고 하얀 맥고를 쓰고 다니는 비범(?)한 것은 아즉 연출되지 안는 모양이니 웬일인지?
무슨 약속이나 한 듯이 겨우 2, 3일을 전후로 하고 일제히 남자들의 복장전체는 물들고 말엇다.
우으로 모자부터 알애로 구두까지 모다가 淡에서 濃으로 변하고 말엇다. 시절딸하 옷갈어 닙는데 시비가 무엇이냐면 할 말업지만 조선남자처럼 분에 넘치게(즉 경제상태에 맛지 안케)까지 철을 차저 닙으려고 애쓰는 이는 적을 것이다.

新調의 양복, 옷포켓에 느러트린 무새손수건 한쪽으로 기우러질 듯하게 가만히 언즌 최신형 겨울 모자 청천바탕에 붉은 점박힌 넥타이 이만하면 되엇다는 듯키 대로로 橫行闊步하는 꼴이야 구역이 나서 못 볼지경이다.
무엇을 차지랴는 듯키 百貨店쇼-윈도 아페 멀건히 서서 새틋한 넥타이 진렬에 취한 것가티 드려다 보는 꼴도 볼 수 업거니와 밤이면 이 술집에서 저 카페로 마치 狂犬과 가티 싸다니는 似而非 인테리겐짜의 꼴은 더 말할 것도 업다.
그러나 이것만이 결코 仲秋의 街頭를＜90＞ 장식하는 출연배우들은 아닐 것이다.
정신이 나는 요사이 아츰결에 비록 퇴색한 양복이나마 반듯하게 다려닙고 자긔의 일터로 향하야 거러가는 성실한 청년의 그 모양은 마치 모래 속의 한알의 진주와 가티 일종의 애착을 늣기게 하며 무엇보다도 가을철 거리를 푸래쉬하게 장식하야 주는 것 갓다. 더욱이 가을해가 쌀쌀하게 저무러갈 때 김이 마락마락나는 군밤 두어푼어치를 사들고 도라가는 로동자들의 모습과 어덴지 싱싱해 보이는 팔과 다리는 그 어떠한 미듬을 갓게 하며 街路의 가을맛을 한층 도두어 주는 듯하다.
과연 이들은 가을 기분으로 가득찬 거리를 장식하는 미래의 주인공가티도 보인다.

秋風에 생기띄운 街頭의 행렬 가증한 모뽀의 亂舞, 부량자의 前身을 가진 아편거지의 비틀거름, 밉살마진 중학생들의 거츠른 거름에 아울너 건장한 노동자의 씩씩한 보조 이 모든 것들의 雜然한 행진측에 가을 도시의 街頭는 고요히 저므로 간다.(끗)


시골 女人의...,
金岸曙

이것은 보고 듯기가 빈번한 서울거리서는 듬울게 구경할 수 잇는 싀골하고도 平北定州停車場에 나타난 엇던 女性劇의 한 장면이외다.
나희는 설흔이 될락말락한 키는 중이상되는 여성이 잇슴니다. 본시부터 긴 얼골에다가 평안도식 머리로 소위 「꼬도리채」를 하엿스니 얼골이 더 길어 보일 뿐 아니고 「꼬도리채」야말로 하늘님 코구멍을 다처 재체기를 니르킬 지경이라 한대도 과한 形容이라 할 수 업는 것이 잇슴니다. 저고리는 저고리로의 평가를 일허 周衣代用이라 할만치 기-막케 허리에 느러저 지내가는 바람과 한가지 희롱을 하는 에로틱한 광경은 암만해도 類다른 주의를 끌만하엿슴니다. 무릅을 가리울가 말가한 짤막한 時體치마와 하이한 洋襪 속에 파무친 기다란 다리의 굽으러진 양은 검은 고무신과는 눈부싀게 별다른 대조를 주엇슴니다. 각금 눌헌 손으로 「꼬로도채」를 이리 만지고 저리주무르는 양은 무던히도 머리에 대한 주의를 小心하는 듯이보엿슴니다.

두남녀는 부부간이 아니라고 할 아무런 거림새도 업시 맥주에 과실에 사이다에 담배에...를 사서 실코 뽀트를 저어 나갓다. 유쾌한 모던 부부라고 생각하엿다.
두 번재는 카페·뻬-비압 어둠침침한 첨아미테서.
번삽한 거리라 그러한지 여자는 비실비실하며 寺洞길로 올나 가려하고 남자는 (漢江에 갓치왓든 그 남자) 典洞길로 발을 돌으키면서 무엇인지 미진한 것이 잇는 듯이 안달은 소리로 「내일와요?」한다.
여자는 던지는 말을 뒷꼭지로 바드며 「봐야 알어요」해 바린다. 부부인 줄 알엇드니 아직 멀은 모양이다.
세 번재가 오늘밤이다.
그러치 아니하여도 풍더분한 육체에 푹덥히는 외투를 입고 압흘 서서 가는 륜곽 더구나 不絶히 동요되는 中半身은 유혹이요, 에로다.
그 남자는 어데 두엇슬가? 하는 궁금쯩이 나매 뒤이어 대관절 엇던 여자가? 하는 호기심이 밧삭 난다.
그러타고 쪼차가서 무러볼 수도 업고...압흐로도 여러 번 맛나게 될 터인데 아주 깨림직하게 마음구석을 차지하고 잇는 불가해한 존재다.

仲秋의 街頭 여인풍경-묘한 제목이다. 쉽고도 어려운 제목이다. 이것을 쓰랴고 일부러 종로네거리에 가 서서＜97＞ 볼 수도업고 그러타고 여인을 그다지 세밀하게 처다보기를 실혀한다는 이보다 못하는 나로서 또 각금 각금 본 것을 상상해 쓰기도 어렵고 실로 난문제이다.
좌우간 본론으로 돌격 해보자.
가을볏헤 人造絹이 물결친다. 비오다가 개인 여름 석양에 쏘다저 나와 중천에서 진치고 날어 다니는 잠자리들의 날개보다도더 뻔적거린다.
-人造絹汎濫時代다-
나는 여인의 얼골은 못보앗서도 번줄거리는 人造絹은 눈이 시도록 만히 보앗노라. 먼저 인조견 뻔적거리는 바람에 거긔 눈이 부시고 眼力이 상하여 일즉이 정작 여자의 아름다운 얼골이 보히지 안엇노라.
튼튼한 무명옷 조선물산을 깨굿히 얌전히 해 닙은 여자를 맛나면 萬綠叢中一點紅으로 눈이 뻔적 띄워 그 여자의 얼골을 자세히 드려다 볼 때 그 여자는 자기소유의 美 몃배이상으로 아름다워 뵈인다. 그런 고로 남자에게 아양을 부리고 호기심을 끌고 환심을 사랴고 하더라도 인조견보다는 또 사치보다는 무명옷 극도로 검소한 것이 조흠이다.

지금은 딴스(舞踊)를 극장에서만 하지안코 街頭에서도 한다. 엄청나게도 짜른 치마에 온몸을 화장이상의 화장을 하고 겅중겅중 읏슥읏슥하며 걸어가는 것은 꼭 딴스식 거름거리다. 만일 유성기나 라듸오에서 마-취 곡조만 나온다면 당장 춤을 출 기세이다.
그러나 한엽헤서는 고전극에 나오드시 신여성으로 엄청나게 긴 치마를 발꿈치에 칠드럭거리며 철저히 가장나는 모던걸이 아니라고 걸어가는 것은 확실히 짧은 치마 이상으로 보기 실타. 왜 보기조게 길게도 짧게도 중용을 취하지 못하는지. 내가 여자가트면 길에서 일일이 붓잡어세노코 질문, 항의, 권고해 볼 일이다.
저고리도 마찬가지. 기형적 타입이 유행된다. 두루막이가튼 저고리가 잇더니 근자는 데쩍보다도 더 짧은 겨드랑 바로 밋까지 기여올러 가는 저고리를 新女子?가 태연히 입고 다닌다. 남 아니하는 것을 거슬러 눈에 띄우라고 하는 모양인데 그러다가는 아조 洋女처럼 저고리를 닙지안코 억개달은 치마만 닙고 다니게 될 것이다.
가지각색 치마빗에 여학생들은 붉은 줄 힌 줄까지 달고 가지각색 머리를 틀고 흰분 붉은 분을 얼골에 발느고 가지각색 향수냄새를 풍기며 천길만길되는 굽 놉흔 구두에 또는 사니희 구두 이상으로 평퍼짐한 구두에 또 격에 맛지 안는 어더 입은 것가튼 양장미인들--또 왱청다르게 부인네들은 햇ㅅ빗에 거울처럼 뻔적이는 기름바른 반즈르르한 머리에 누런 비녀 흰비녀 검은 비녀를 꼿고 또 아래에는 흰 고무신, 깜장 고무신, 누런 고무신을 신고 거긔다가 빨강 우산, 파랑 우산, 알낙달낙한 우산을 밧고 이 千態萬狀의 여인풍경에 갓득이나 얼빠진 조선청년남자를 정신 못차리게 한다.
