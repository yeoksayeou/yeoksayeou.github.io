발행일	1930년 11월 01일  
기사제목	바다로 나아가는 사람들 (People Going to the Sea)  
필자	씽그  
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Script)  

바다로 나아가는 사람들  
 씽그  
Moriah (老人)  
Patrick (아들)  
Catherine (딸)  
Nora (자근 딸)  
Several Men and Women  
An island cast away on the West Coast of Ireland  

Inside a fishing household, with a fire and a spinning wheel, there are remnants of fishing gear belonging to the fishermen. On the wall, a wooden plank leans against it. Catherine, around twenty, is kneading flour and cleaning her hands with her apron as she begins to spin. Innocent little Nora quietly opens the door and peeks in.  

Nora (in a timid voice) Where has mother gone?  

Catherine (calm until the end) She must be sleeping. Poor mother. She probably worked hard all day and has finally fallen asleep.  

(Nora quietly approaches and pulls out a piece of clothing from her skirt)  

Catherine (distracted by the spinning wheel) What is that?  

Nora It's a shirt and socks salvaged from a corpse in Donegal. The young minister brought it back for me. (Catherine's attention is caught, she stops spinning and listens carefully)  

Nora Make sure to check if it belongs to Michael O'Brien when you take it out. But wait until mother goes to the sea.  

Catherine But it couldn’t belong to Michael. He must have perished far away, up north.  

Nora Yet that’s what the minister said. These things do happen. And if it happens to be Michael’s, tell mother. Thanks to God’s grace, Michael was given a decent burial. But if it isn’t Michael’s, don’t say anything to mother or to anyone else. If she finds out, she will cry and thrash about. Father is already weak. Don’t say a word.  

(The door that Nora half-opened is pushed violently by the wind)  

Catherine (worriedly looking at the door) Didn’t you hear that Patrick was told not to let anyone stop him from going to the fishing port?  

Nora That’s what the minister said. I didn’t tell him that I couldn’t stop Patrick from going. But really, there’s nothing to fear. God will surely not let mother’s only son, Patrick, be lost to the sea.  

Catherine Are the waves rough over there by that white rock?  

Nora Oh yes, the waves from the west sound fearsome. And when the tide comes in against the wind, it will probably be worse. (Nora carries the bundle over to the table)  

Nora Should we take a look?  

Catherine We should wait until mother comes out, otherwise... (goes toward the table) It will probably take a long time if we start to cry while we look.  

Nora (going to the door leading to the other room) She is coming out now from upstairs, where she can pass through the floor above.  

Catherine Let's bring the ladder up, so she won’t see. And when the tide comes in, mother will probably go to the sea to see if Michael is rising from the east. Then we can look and cry as much as we want. (They set the ladder up toward the ceiling, and Catherine climbs two or three steps to hide the bundle in a corner. Moriah comes out from the other room)  

Moriah (looking at Catherine with a tone of reprimand) I thought I could manage until tonight.  

Catherine I’m just preparing the cakes for a moment. (throws peat) When the tide goes out, Patrick will go to Connemara for fishing. I thought I should send him off nicely.  

(Nora takes peat from the ground)  

Moriah (hanging onto a chair near the fire) If the southwest wind starts blowing, Patrick won’t go. He won’t leave today for sure. And the minister will surely support him properly.  

Nora Mother, the minister probably won’t help him. Besides, Aemon, Simon, Stephen, and the others say Patrick will definitely leave.  

Moriah Where is that fellow going?  

Nora He went to check if a ship is going out again this week. He should return from there soon. Look! The tide is coming in over there, and fishing boats from the east are setting sail.  

Catherine Someone is climbing the stairs.  

Nora (looking) Patrick is coming up quickly.  

Patrick (coming in and looking around the room, picks up a handful of dirt from the floor) What kind of a thing did you bring from Connemara?  

Catherine (coming down) Nora, give him the thing hanging on the wooden plank. This morning, the dog must have bitten the leash from its legs, so I hung it up to dry.  

Nora (hands the item to Patrick) Here, it is yours.  

Moriah Patrick, it would have been better to just leave that there untouched. (Patrick takes hold of the item like this) When Michael's body eventually washes up, the Lord will probably allow us to provide a decent burial. We could then honor him with the things from that place.  

Patrick (muttering) I’ll ride that beast. The cost might be high but if we leave in a hurry, who knows how long it will take to find a ship again. They say the prices in the market will be significant.  

Moriah But if Michael's body is washed up, we won't have any one to construct his coffin properly. Then the village people will mock. Your precious coffin is already there waiting because of Michael. (Moriah turns to glance at the leaning coffin against the wall)  

Patrick What’s the matter with the tides? Please make the price reasonable. We should have food in the making. Each day for a week, I will even give them my last penny. But as it is, the southwest winds are violent, so on my way I could get lost and never return.  

Moriah Even if Michael’s body is washed up, if they come here to take away his body, they won’t be able to wash my corpse out. But, you— just look, my boy would never return to me!  

Catherine My word! Mother, while seeing him off, why don’t you say one good word? Just once. That's all. So you don’t distress his heart as he leaves. What’s wrong with such words?  

(Nora, pointing to the entrance) Mother, the fire is now clipping!  

Catherine (suddenly startled) Oh! We let Father leave without giving him anything!  

Nora He got some bread this morning, and if he stays out till night he will surely die of hunger as well.  

Catherine (taking the cakes out of the oven) He could suffer from hunger. All of this would cause mother to lose herself entirely, and everyone else would also be consumed with despair. (Moriah, while sitting on the chair, is trembling her upper body mildly)  

Catherine (to the coal) Are you going to just let him go?  

Moriah I’ll just get out to love him.  

Catherine (looking worriedly) Nora, bring Mother some food. If she doesn’t come back, it will be trouble.  

Nora What food?  

Catherine The garment that Michael brought from Connemara.  

Moriah (holding the fabric as Nora passes it) The world has different fates. Why then, should the child leave behind treasures when they pass away?  

Moriah (slowly walks out)  

Nora (hurries back up the ladder)  

Catherine Wait a moment. If Mother comes back in, oh dear! The poor thing looks so weak; I wonder if something has happened.  

Nora Do you really think she won’t be able to see us?  

Catherine (looking out the door) I can’t see, but maybe she will return.  

Nora (pulls the garment out) Tomorrow, the minister said he would be sending some things back. If it happens to belong to Michael O'Brien, then the minister should be informed.  

Catherine (taking a piece of clothing) How can the minister know who this mess belongs to?  

Nora (coming down the ladder) You see! I saw two men loading illegal liquor on the boat north before the crows have started to crow. But one of them got caught up with the rowboat!  

Catherine (getting the clothing ready) Must be to Donegal, I suppose?  

Nora (the two keenly look together)  

Catherine (in a low voice) Nora, it could just very well belong to Michael.  

Nora Is that a shirt with cuffs?  

They are similiar! But if they go wherever they want, they might all have the same shirts sold in many shops. So what makes you so sure it is Michael’s?  

Nora (pointing to the raft) Ah! No! It looks very much like Michael’s shirt. Pray to God! My God! Let your grace bless Michael even if he is dead! Oh mother! If you find out about this, what kind of sorrow will happen?  

Catherine Then did you knit this?  

Nora I knitted two pairs out of three. The one you are holding has been knitted with sixty yarns to save it for only you!  

Catherine (counting) Truly you did well, Nora.  

Nora Michael is lost in the far north, perhaps God led him there to be safe. How sad!  

Catherine (after waiting a moment) Nora! Mother’s going to be coming in. There’s a sound like footsteps up the steps.  

Nora (listening intently) Seems she is almost at the door!  

Catherine Before she comes in, let’s keep this hidden. Maybe after Patrick has got his blessing, she will be feeling a little better. We shall wait until the only son comes home safely from the sea. Until then, let’s not speak of anything.  

Nora (together with Catherine) Let’s hide it in the corner. (They hurry and hide the bundle in the fireplace. Catherine goes back to spin.)  

Nora I can see she’s going to cry again!  

Catherine (turning to look)  

Moriah (slowly comes in, without looking at her daughters, and sits on the side. In her hand, she holds a tuft of hair)  

Catherine (while spinning) Did you get to really see Patrick?  

Moriah (without turning) I saw something frightening!  

Catherine What did you see?  

Moriah This morning; did you see when I saw that dead man holding a child?  

Catherine and Nora What?!  

(Both embrace Moriah tightly)  

Nora Mother, do tell us what you saw!  

Moriah I had come up to the well, and I saw him dressed in a white robe over there. Something was terribly frightening!  

Catherine (in tears) My God! How could it happen; no more hope is left for us!  

Nora The minister said that God wouldn’t steal the only son left to Mother.  

Moriah (in a softened voice, but nonetheless clearly) But the minister doesn’t know what the sea has to do! Patrick is surely dead. Go to the shed and ask Aemon to come watch over the land. I’ll wait to die here! How many have died? Six were born! Six strong sons left alive, and every day I bore them in pain. One of them can die and real pain befall, this would be no sooner than the sea washes over him.  

Catherine (what Moriah just spoke seems frightening, and she has a horrifying expression)  

Nora (listening intently) What’s that sound?  

Catherine It’s the sound from the sea!  

Moriah (as if to explain all her previous thoughts) Then your father, my father, everyone was gone! All disappeared without a trace; not even a scrap of their clothing was left behind. The next day, they said the boats had capsized; I didn’t even know whether our husbands, Stephen or Sean had vanished and gone around the world with a watery grave. I got this from the local folk. Little ones who didn’t even wish to tell me about this, came running one after another to comfort me. “Your husband, Stephen, is gone, and Sean loves another beyond the world.” I left all of these thoughts behind.  

Moriah (pointing to the door while she tells)  

Moriah (her face reflecting all the hardship) No worse off than “Have you found my son?”  

Catherine (looking sadly) My Patrick! Patrick's gone for good!  

Nora (on guard, holds her breath) 

Catherine (turning to her, as if to reiterate)  

Nora

Catherine (in tears)

Moriah What mystery has death brought me? My love left this world instantly and tragically! Everything is lost; no hope left for us…  

(While many tears roll down their cheeks, the curtain slowly falls down)  

**Summary**

This play, titled "People Going to the Sea," portrays the heartfelt tragedy of a family living on an isolated Irish island during the 1930s. The main characters, who are part of a fisherman’s household, grapple with the fear and sorrow associated with both their loved ones potentially lost at sea and the memories of those already taken. The poignant dialogue and reflections encapsulate the harsh realities of life at the periphery of society, defined by poverty and uncertainty, particularly the role of women in dealing with loss during an era marked by colonial struggles. Historians may find this representation significant as it captures the poignant intersection of culture, survival, and the impact of external forces on island communities during a time of socio-political upheaval.

파-트레 (색기를 뭉등거리며) 그놈의 말을 타고 갈텐데. 곱비가 잇서야지 얼는 서두러서 떠나지 안코 어름어름하다가 이 배도 또 노치면 일주일이고 이주일이고 혹은 더 오래도록 다시는 떠나는 배가 업거든요. 이번 장은 말시세가 상당하리라고들 하든데 노처서 되겟수.
