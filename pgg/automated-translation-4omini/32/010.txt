기사형태	만화 (Cartoon)  

增收 600萬石  
Due to a bountiful year, 6 million stones were harvested, but there is no market for sale. A friend who traded until last year comes with a sack as big as a young girl's waist and says he wants to buy it. But when he sees it, he proclaims, "I got it!" Seeing this, the village elder sighs and says, "In a bountiful year, even the multiplication of goods does not matter. This is the beginning of the end times!" <1>

**Summary**

The article discusses the paradox of agricultural abundance resulting in a surplus harvest of 6 million stones, yet there is no market for selling this surplus. The sentiments expressed reflect a sense of despair and futility among the villagers, capturing the economic struggles faced during this period in colonial Korea. Historians might find the commentary on abundance juxtaposed with lack of opportunity revealing of the broader socio-economic issues under Japanese rule, illustrating how colonial policies impacted local agricultural markets.
