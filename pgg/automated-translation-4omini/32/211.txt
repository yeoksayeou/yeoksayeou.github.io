**Summary**  

The article provides details on newly established branch offices of a publication, including their locations and branch managers. Each branch is set up in different cities, with a particular focus on accessibility and representation. This information highlights the expanding network of media in colonial Korea, reflecting how Korean society was evolving despite the restrictive Japanese governance. Historians may find the emergence of these branches as indicative of a growing Korean identity and the attempts of Koreans to assert their presence in various urban centers.
