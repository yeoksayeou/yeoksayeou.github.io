Symptoms of Gold Shortage  
A doctor is smiling gently while placing a stethoscope on a woman's handbag.  

The contents include one compact, three train tickets, two books, one stuffed animal, and a single hole-punched train ticket.  

Woman: Doctor, what illness is this?  
Doctor: It's a hysteria caused by the ongoing recession and the symptoms of a lack of gold.  

<84>  

**Summary**  

The article humorously depicts a woman's predicament as she consults a doctor about her financial troubles symbolized by her handbag's meager contents. The doctor diagnoses her with "gold shortage hysteria," a witty commentary on the economic struggles of the time, reflecting how the Great Depression may have affected personal finances and societal perceptions in colonial Korea. Historians might find this perceptive of the psychological impact of economic hardship during this period of Japanese rule, highlighting the intersection of personal and collective anxieties.
