발행일	1930년 11월 01일  
기사제목	華西門의 廢墟에서 (In the Ruins of Hwach'ŏn Gate)  
필자	로아 (Ro-a)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

In the ruins of the ancient city where even humans have vanished,  
The drifting clouds also wipe away tears.  

Where have all the green grasses that were once lush gone?  
Only the wild weeds have grown fiercely.  

On the four pillars of the Aramdori, the colors are painted,  
Every beam adorned with blue dragons and yellow dragons.  

The stone walls that stood against bullets crumble away,  
And the letters left on the plaque mourn in the dirt.  

The shepherds who ride their horses on the sunset road,  
Swing the whip and circle while passing by,  
Do they not know the sorrow of their beloved?  
Only the stones that supported their heads are left behind.  

Speak, Hwach'ŏn Gate. What has become of you?  
For five hundred years, we have dedicated ourselves to serve one master,  
Sacrificing money, our lives, living and dying,  
Now the country becomes a place for the people, what can you do?  
-1930. 9. 30- (On the road to Suwon) <152>  

**Summary**

This poem reflects on the desolation of Hwach'ŏn Gate, symbolizing the ruins of a once-great city representative of Korea's heritage. The author laments the loss of beauty and life amid the grim realities of colonial oppression in 1930s Korea. Historians might find the work interesting as it poignantly addresses the disillusionment and grief experienced by Koreans during the Japanese colonial period, underscoring the cultural and emotional impacts of colonization.

華西門의 廢墟에서
						로아
인적조차 끈허진 古城의 폐허에
떠도는 구름도 눈물지우네
덥혓든 청개와 다 어데 가고
잡풀만 길길이 자랏단 말가

아람도리 네기둥에 단청올니고
들보마다 청룡황룡 구비치더니
총알 막든 돌담마자 허무러지고
현판에 남은 글씨 흙안고 우는고나

석양길 소타고 자나는 목동들은
일산밧고 연타시고 자로거동하시압든
님금님 비탄을 녯일이라 모르는가
지지대 섯든 고개 비ㅅ돌만 남엇거늘

화서문아 말해라. 네 웨 이 꼴 되엿는가
오백년 나려 오며 님군 한분 모시자고
돈바치고 몸바치고 죽어 살어 왓건만
나라 일흔 백성되니 네직힌들 뭘하느뇨
-1930. 9. 30-(水原갓든 길에) ＜152＞
