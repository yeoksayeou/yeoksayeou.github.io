Three-Year Separation  
						Kim An-so  
Three-Year Separation  
Ah, how far you have gone, it has already been three years.  
Longing emotions rise, the moon shines brightly.  
Without a care, I gaze upon the bright moon,  
Wishing for the sixth time, I lament my situation.  

Original Poem  

悠悠一別己三年  
相望相思明月天  
膓斷靑天望明月  
別來三十六回圓  
- Bai Juyi  

White Heron  
In this life, shall I grow old in vain?  
Endlessly, my worries cause all my hair to turn white.  
Why, is it that by the water, the pair of white herons  
Are free of worries, while my hair is turning gray?  

Original Poem  
人生四十未全衰  
我爲愁多白髮垂  
何故水邊雙白鷺  
無愁頭上亦垂絲  
- Bai Juyi  

Staying at the Zhuang Pavilion Station  
In and out of the station, the night seems long.  
How can this body not rise in sleep, longing for home?  
What will I see under the bright moon?  
Only the white sea water shines in the sky.  

Original Poem  
夜半樟亭驛  
愁人起望鄕  
月明何所見  
潮水白茫茫  
- Bai Juyi  

Untitled  
You, with the evening glow, appear in red.  
The marble is pure white, reflecting my heart.  
The marble remains white forever,  
Alas, my heart turns to the evening glow.  

Original Poem  
君心如紅雲  
妾思如白石  
白石長**  
紅雲不待夕  
- Unknown  

At the Love-Lost Temple  
Feeling bored by the stream, I play around.  
Lazily, I stroll around the temple.  
How delightful to hear the birds sing occasionally,  
Everywhere, the streams flow down.  

Original Poem  
弄日臨溪坐  
花尋繞寺行  
時時聞鳥語  
處處是泉聲  
- Bai Juyi  
  

**Summary**  

The poems reflect deep emotions of longing and separation, illustrating the timeless themes of love and solitude. Particularly, the piece titled "Three-Year Separation" conveys a poignant sense of yearning for a beloved after a long separation, showcasing feelings that are universally relatable across time and culture. Historians might find it noteworthy as it relates to the colonial experience in Korea, where feelings of displacement and nostalgia were prevalent among the population under foreign rule. The reflections in the poems offer insight into the emotional landscape of Koreans during the 1930s.
