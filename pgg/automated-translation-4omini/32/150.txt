Magazine Title: 별건곤

Article Title: 시골 아버지의 편지 (Letter from a Country Father)  
Author: Unknown  
Article Type: 만화 (Cartoon)  

He had thus been subjected to property foreclosure four times due to gambling debts, expenses for fertilizers, tax payments, and loans from the credit cooperative. And the remaining debt was like a mountain. Oh dear, it’s tough to live in Bukgando (北間道). If it has to be, let’s go somewhere tough to live even if it means dying!

  

**Summary**  

This article reflects the hardships faced by farmers in rural Korea during the colonial period, highlighting the economic struggles tied to debts and the oppressive financial systems in place. The sentiment of despair and the determination to seek a better life despite overwhelming challenges can be seen as emblematic of the broader social issues of the time. Historians may find the portrayal of rural misery and the specific mention of Bukgando significant, as it sheds light on regional disparities in colonial Korea and the effects of economic exploitation.
