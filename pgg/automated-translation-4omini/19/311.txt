**Summary**  

This article reports on the establishment of various branch offices, specifying their locations and leadership. Branches were established in places such as Seongjin, Shinhong, and Rajin, with detailed management roles listed. This reflects the expanding administrative framework in Korea under Japanese colonization, emphasizing the structuring of local governance. Historians might find the meticulous documentation of administrative expansion significant as it illustrates the colonial government's efforts to increase control and organization in the region.
