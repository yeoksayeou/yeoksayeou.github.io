기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Azaleas  
――Jiangxi Menari――  
When Azaleas bloom,  
It is said to be spring  
不如歸不如歸  
The national bird sings  
Alas, alas,  
It is a holy fire――.  

On the mountains of my hometown,  
Azaleas bloom  
不如歸不如歸  
The national bird sings  
Alas, alas,  
It is a holy fire――.＜160＞  

**Summary**

This poem reflects on the beauty of azaleas and evokes a sense of longing for the poet's hometown. The imagery of the blooming azaleas signifies the arrival of spring, intertwined with a sense of nostalgia and emotional resonance connected to one’s roots. For historians, the poem encapsulates the cultural significance of nature and the feelings of displacement common among Koreans during the colonial period, as the longing for home often related to identity amid changing circumstances.
