잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1929년 06월 23일  
기사제목	靑春의 寃恨 尹○悳의 넉두리 (The Grievances of Youth: A Lament by Yun ○deok)  
필자	冠岳山人 (Gwangak Sanin)  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

I am so weary! It is painful and lamentable, oh, Yun ○deok!

On the eighth day of the eighth month in the Year of Gyeongy (丙寅), I plunged into the deep waters of Hyeonhae (玄海水)!

In my household, there is disobedience, and in the world, they call me “a fallen woman,” rendering me without a place to rest my heart or body. I am this broken woman, Yun ○deok!

What has happened to me, facing my fate, remembering my past? I must confront the joys and sorrows, but the taste of life eludes me. I was never able to boldly enter marriage nor raise a child. No one comes to mourn my distorted death with a cup of wine poured for me.

In life, I had friends and acquaintances, both male and female, but in death, there isn’t anyone to remember me past my swirling and troublesome end, and there is no one to truly understand my despondency.

The world says I had an affair with Kim ○—! However, if someone examines my history with him, they would see that he was never someone I would have chosen to entwine my life and body with.

By diving into the waters, although my spirit has merged with theirs, I feel resentful and have reached the end of my suffering.

That man is simply a friend from seven years ago in Tokyo... the closest way friend I had, in the so-called Dongwoo Association (同友會), organized a performance for Korean students in Tokyo to come back and perform in Korea. He served as the stage manager, and I, as just a singer, joined his troupe from that time.

After that, I was devoted to Korean performances and threw myself into struggles, unable to settle anywhere. If I had embraced Kim as my lifeblood, would my life have been as glorious as a diamond? If so, I cannot express how happy I would have been.

When I receive passionate letters from him, I cannot help but think, “Will this man ever be mine?” Answering him in detail would feel like foolishness, so I put it off and wandered out as if it didn’t concern me, hastening down the road to my grave instead.

Each thought comes back to grieve me ten, twenty times! However, the beauty of youth fades, and does Yun ○deok have to lament at all times? Under the pressure from Li ○○, when I returned after braving the Siberian winds, I resorted to giving up.

What is love, what is man, what is money, anyway? Let me simply enjoy life as it comes! Meanwhile, I took my place in Suheun Dong (水銀洞), where I engaged with all the men and initiated elaborate exchanges.

As long as there was money, I noticed everything was enjoyable. Even what I thought about music was trivial, I rode in automobiles, dined with rich young men, and I am stylefully known as the singer Yun ○deok. But as time passed and night fell, the roots of the outlook “the world is vain” grew deeper.

With my mind dulled, I stumbled on the vast plain, as if I had fallen into a bog. When I came to my senses, I was in the midst of young Yun ○deok, stained completely in black.

In this way, my younger brother, ×deok, received money from Lingua Academy and stated he would go to study in America, while I hold no manner of reproach against others.

I was supposed to be with my brother, yet how many nights did we cry in front of the theater together? 

If I had taken only the true way, I would have contributed even more than my brother, having earned esteem in the Korean music circle. However, as regrets crowd me, how much more have I lamented through the night? The feeling of desiring to die became overwhelming, and I resolved that if I were to die, I might as well leave a song instead of a farewell letter.

Thus, I solicited the Nihon Tokuyōki (日東蓄音器) company to record “Sayi Chant” and over twenty other songs.

One thought lingered heavily in two ways: If I reached Italy safely and studied vocal music for a while, I would stand once more with renewed glory. If not, I might as well die.

However, since Kim is without money or freedom, if I can’t go, then death is a better choice. It would wash away my suffering and misfortune, so I entrusted my fate to the waters of Hyeonhae, leaving the tragic lament of my death behind.

Dear siblings with kind hearts and soft spirits! After Yun ○deok’s passing, I understand the state of affairs. Affairs are sacred; however, my beliefs have turned to emptiness. Death may be sacred, yet after one dies, everything crumbles! I tried to escape to the Dragon Palace, only to find it’s hopeless—my cruel fate leads me there, but let the wretched forever remain.

I cannot blame Kim—no matter how much I loved him, it is indeed my fault for having embraced him!

Yet lifetimes of separation with him have left an indelible mark. Certainly, there are reasons he would have died too... While I do not vow vengeance against the men who brought me ruin, with Kim’s death by drowning, it has fully completed my desire for vengeance.

Bearing malice against men and allowing them to fade away, I find it impossible to voice how curious I am that having persisted, despite my weak determination, my passionate nature remained persistent. For that reason, my entire life has become a confusion, and now my lament is a tangle without order.

Love is a temporal art. When we listen to “Elman” or the music of “Kreesus,” we feel intoxicated in a similar way with love kindling in our hearts. Just as a music concert ends, the echoes of the song linger in the heart; love can also revisit and recede, leaving only ashes in its trace.

In this age, where male debauchery becomes insouciance and female mistakes are ostracized, we need to take heed to safeguard our spirit—the opportunity to engage with men can only harmonize with our preciousness.

Love is a temporal art. For it, love may lead to an ambrosial life. 

It is foolish to die only for love; there are redemptive paths in love. Therefore, in this weakened state, Kim wanders, always striking me as a burden.  

However, how can one even try? I believed since I was already doomed, I might as well drift toward death in the sea.... Yet Kim merely bathed in pleasures, superior to my own misfortune.

Alas, my unfortunate state reflects a grand truth; were I to live while properly engaged in the world of music and noble deeds, perhaps this predicament would not arise... Alas, my life of passing into the currents of Hyeonhae carries no place to rest my head. 

**Summary**

This piece reflects the profound introspection of a woman named Yun ○deok as she expresses her profound grievances regarding love and societal expectations. Her thoughts delve into regrets of unfulfilled potential and the true cost of emotional entanglements against the backdrop of a patriarchal society. The narrative resonates with the historical context of colonial Korea, as it showcases the struggles of women in expressing their desires and navigating oppressive social norms, revealing the broader societal challenges faced by women during this period.

Historians might find it remarkable how Yun’s lament encapsulates the emotional turmoil of many women in colonial Korea, reflecting the intersection of personal desires and societal pressures. The sentiments of despair and loss echo the ongoing struggles for identity and agency within a conservative framework, marking a significant narrative in the context of Korean women's history during colonization.

원수를갑는다는것은 원수를짓지안는다는데밋치지못한다 나는왜모―든남성들에게 그가튼원수를지엇슬가, 그것을오직남성들의죄책으로만돌닐가 그것은못될말이다 내가아모리죽엇다하기로그런뻔뻔스런 소리는못하겟다 모도가내가 제법의지가굿세인체하기는하얏스나 그실은너모나의사가몽롱한열정적녀성이엇든탓이다 그닭에 나의일생은뒤죽박죽이요 지금드는넉두리도뒤죽박죽갈피가없다.

사랑은 시간적예술이다 우리가「엘만」이나「크리이슬너」의음악을드를에도취되는그와똑가튼의미로사랑에 가슴을태오는것이다 그러함으로음악회가끗나면 가슴에는 노래나음곡의여운이남는다시 사랑도반다시식는가잇고식고나면가슴에는재만남는것이다 남자의방탕은묵인이되고 여자의실수는뒤처내게되는이세태에잇서 신녀셩된우리―남자와접촉하는긔회가만흔우리는 매오김히생각지안으면안이된다 사랑은시간적예술이다 그러함으로 사랑은밤이안이요과자이다 과자만사다주는 애인에게는끗끗내미들맛이적다 우리는과자도먹으려니와밥을먹어야한다 그러나감성의지배를밧는청춘녀성은 시간예술에서공간적리상을찻고과자에서 일생의식량을구하는페단이만타.
◇
죽는날지 가즌짓을다하든윤○덕이의죽은뒤 넉두리가얼마나비창하냐 이것이곳내가죽은뒤에야비로소깨다른나의인생관이다 그러나한번죽은뒤에야 무슨소용잇스랴 내대신세상에만은 동생이나 동무들이나마 인격과리지를버서나추잡한사랑의길에러지지안키를바라빌이다 처녀의일생으니운명은 남자에게비로소마음을허락하는그순간에결정되는것이다. 이것은아모도모르는곳에서 아모에게의론도못하고하게되는일인만콤 죽은윤○덕의넉두리가쇠머리나 시루에주려 무당의집을 비러하는넉두리보다는 갑시잇슬것갓다.
◇
사랑만을위하야서야죽는것은어리석다 얼마든지사랑을살날길이잇슬터이닛가 그러함으로나보다는죽을여유가 빈약한김씨는 수중고혼된탓을걸핏하면빗치는터이지 그러나할수잇나이왕가티죽은몸이라가티물속에다니는수밧게……그래도김씨는소대상을배가모자라도록엇더먹고나왓스니 그것만해도나보다는신세가조혼편이지.
아이구나설허라 내신세야 산놈의 세상도허무도하지 이태리로 갓스려니하고밋고잇는 내동생이 미국서 나죽엇다는통지를듯고 꿈가티도허무하야『안이나하고작별할  언니는 이태리로간다고햇스닛가 혹시이태리로간것을집에서모르고 죽은줄생각하고그러지안는가』한편지를보고 윤○덕가이태리에잇단다하고 자긔가아는갓가티 야단들이지.
아이그머니 내넉시야 다시살지는 못할몸이 그런소리는아는체해무엇하리 물속고혼이 외롭구나.
아이구설허라 내신세야 아모남자나 붓잡아살면서 음악회출입이나하고잇섯드면 이런지경은 안되엿슬것을……아이구머니 이내신세 현해탄깁흔물에몸붓칠곳바이업네…….＜117＞
