기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)  

Early summer essays

Was it from the mountain or the river? Whether it was from there or somewhere else, the spring that had come along the path was delightful, whether it came from the mountain or the river, the path was abandoned.

The "Golden Robe"— "Weeping Willow"— "Green Shade"—  
Just calling out those names alone makes me feel embraced by the fragrance that comforts the body tormented by the red dust of the world— that serene happiness, that purity, that mystery...

It is said that Emperor Xuanzong of Tang, upon seeing the weeping willow in the imperial garden, called it "The Golden Robed Young Man." How rich, noble, joyous, and peaceful that name is. 

Were those willows in the imperial garden the only ones that appeared thus? Did they only appear so to Emperor Xuanzong? 

No, to anyone, anywhere, the willows' gentle branches in the green shade surely must appear as "The Golden Robed Young Man." 

Oh, the happiness that is eternally enjoyed without change! 

In the past, when someone looked at the joyful little bird playing among the willows—  
the willow becomes thread,  
and the little bird becomes a drum,  
in the ninety-third spring,  
my worries (cut off)  

were something to muse over! That was his sentiment and sorrow, yet there was no reason not to dance; the willows sway as willows, and the little birds play as little birds— this must be their pure joy, that eternal joy! 

In the Tang song "Shangdiao" there is such a song. 

Little bird, awakening,  
Do not rouse the branches,  
For I cannot wake from my dream,  
Thinking of the warrior far away in Liaoxi (辽西). 

It is a song sung by a girl in an empty chamber who thinks about the soldier in Liaoxi. But I wish to sing a song in place of that sleeping little bird. 

With that, close the house's mouth,  
So it does not bring forth a sprout on the tree,  
May the dream of our beloved not break,  
For fear it may shatter...  

Indeed! In the willows! O little bird! What does it matter whether you sleep or awaken? That serenity, that purity, that mystery fills the person with comfort... (×day, in the diary)  

**Summary**

This piece reflects the beauty of nature, particularly focusing on the poignant imagery of the little bird and the weeping willow, symbolizing innocence and peacefulness amidst life's turmoil. It draws from historical references, like the name “The Golden Robed Young Man,” to enhance its aesthetic qualities. From a historical perspective, the author utilizes nature as a way to cope with the struggles of contemporary life during the colonial era, highlighting how cultural heritage and the past provide solace in an oppressive present.
