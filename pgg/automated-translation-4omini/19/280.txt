잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1929년 06월 23일  
기사제목	나의 젊엇든 時節 第一痛快하엿던 일, 十九歲때에 獨立協會에서 十部大臣을 罵倒하든 일 (The First Joyful Incident of My Youth, When at Nineteen, I Insulted the Ten Ministers at the Independence Association)  
필자	張膺震  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)

The most joyful event in my youth, indeed the most exhilarating in my whole life, was when, at the age of 19, I insulted the ten ministers in a speech at the Independence Association. 

To contextualize this event, I should first briefly explain the background of the Independence Association. After the First Sino-Japanese War in the year of Gapsin (甲午), Korea lost the Liaodong Peninsula, which it had acquired through victory, due to the pressure of the Triple Intervention led by Russia. Thus, humiliated, Japan had to relinquish the Liaodong Peninsula, causing a severe loss of national prestige.

As a result, Japan's influence over Korea began to wane, while Russia's power expanded. This led to a continuous struggle between the pro-Japanese and pro-Russian factions within the Korean political landscape. In 1904, following the collapse of the Kim Hong-jip cabinet, the pro-Russian leaders, including Lee Beom-jin and Lee Yun-yong, orchestrated the transfer of the Russian legation for over a year, resulting in a pro-Russian dominance in the court. All national resources, such as railway and mining rights, were delegated to foreign powers, preventing the common people from witnessing this rampant foreign occupation. 

Subsequently, notable patriots like Namgung Yeong and Yoo Mang gathered to establish the Gyeongyang Association (建陽協會) in 1904. However, due to the general lack of understanding of what reform or civil rights movements entailed, the population showed slightly more enthusiasm than participation, resulting merely in having a signboard in a corner of the Gyeongyang area. 

Later, with the arrival of Seo Jae-bok from the United States, the organization was renewed and renamed the Independence Association, utilizing the Mowha Hall (慕華館) at the West Gate to establish the Independence Hall and subsequently recruit members. The association became large almost instantly, securing thousands of members from students, merchants, laborers, women, the blind, scholars, and officials.

At that time, they began publishing the Independence Newspaper, with Seo Jae-bok presiding over it and promoting the spirit of independence. However, as the association was actually an amalgamation of various political beliefs, the second president, Lee Wan-yong, faced opposition from new members who argued vehemently against delegating mines and railways to foreigners. In response, the previous president and executive members, who claimed that commoners had no right to interfere with governmental matters, were ousted by the members collectively who expressed their discontent.

As a result, a joint assembly was held at Jongno Intersection (now Kim Yun-myeon, located before the Bu-mok store), where prominent figures like Namgung Yeong, Yoo Mang, and student representatives attacked the government vehemently in speeches. 

I was a student in the English school at the time, and students from various foreign language and medical schools participated collectively, with each school sending representatives to support the president and deliver speeches. The ten ministers were invited to the meeting, and I, as a representative of the English school, took part. It was a thrilling experience to see fellow members denounce the ministers without mercy. 

Until that point, under centuries of imperial autocratic rule, commoners had to bow deeply and act submissively, even when meeting the high ministers. However, at this assembly, as a 19-year-old student, to proclaim openly without fear the government's treasonous actions, while thousands cheered and roared, was exhilarating beyond compare. The ministers found themselves helpless against the momentum of the gathering.

Thus, standing before the ministers and declaring, "Do not ever grant mining and railway concessions to foreigners," along with a dozen other resolutions, was immensely satisfying. This was not only the peak of my youthful spirit; it was a moment forever etched in my life that filled me with relief and pride.

**Summary**

The article recounts the author's exhilarating experience at the age of 19 when he delivered a passionate and scathing speech against the ten ministers of Korea's government during a public assembly organized by the Independence Association. This gathering was significant in the context of Korea's struggle against colonial oppression, embodying a moment of youthful defiance and national spirit amidst foreign manipulation. Historians may find particular interest in the ways young activists mobilized public sentiment against governmental collaboration with foreign interests, showcasing the seeds of Korean nationalism during the colonial period.

그럼으로 말미암어 朝鮮에 대한 日本 세력도 위축하는 동시에 露西亞의 세력이 팽창함으로 朝鮮政界에도 親日派對親露派의 양대 세력이 항상 軋轢해 나려오다가 乙未년에 와서 金弘集 내각이 와해되고 李範晋
李允用
李完用 등의 親露巨頭의 斡旋으로 李太王의 俄館播遷이 一箇년이나 계속되고 그 후로도 朝廷에는 親露派의 천하가 되는 동시에 국가의 大利權 鑛山 鐵道가튼 것은 죄다 露 米 英 佛 獨등 外人에게 委任하는 등 피 잇고 씰개 잇는 사람으로는 도뎌히 이런 外人의 跋扈를 볼 수 업섯다. 그리다가 南宮櫶
劉猛
尹致昊 등 志士들이 모여서 建陽協會라는 民黨을 조직한 것이 建陽元年(삼십 사년 전) 이 월이엿다. 그러나 워낙 民智가 幼稚함으로 致治運動이 무엇인지 民權運動이 무엇인지 알지 못하는 민중은 피리는 볼으나 춤추고 나오는 사람이 적어서 會는 겨우 桂洞 한 구석에 간판만 지지해 나려오다가 맛츰 米國으로서 徐載弼이 귀국하야 면목을 一新하엿다. 會名을 獨立協會라 改稱하고 西大門밧 慕華館을 빌어 獨立館이라 곳치고 거긔다 회관을 둔 연후에 회원을 모집하는대 학생, 商民, 노동자, 부인, 맹인, 유생, 관리까지 입회하야 대번에 數萬名 대회가 되어 버렷다. 한엽흐로 獨立新聞을 발행하는대 徐載弼이 몸소 주재하야 독립사상을 고취하엿다.
