靑春  
						春園  

Four days into April, on a certain day. I went to Chang Gyeongwon to see the cherry blossoms. It seems like it has been five years, perhaps due to being bedridden for so long. The flowers are at their peak dying day, fluttering about like soft snowflakes falling in a light rain, and the flowers are swayed by the sickly body.

The flowers that flutter and dance evoke the passing spring, as my sick body laments, yet youth feels like just yesterday, and I cannot help but be heedless to it.

On this day, being a holiday for schools and companies, many young men and women came to see the blossoming flowers. The bright faces, tinged with the joy of spring, trample carelessly upon the flowers, the daughters of spring. They are the masters of life, and also the masters in spring.  

Those in a fleeting life, in an encounter with the young one's red faces, even amidst the spring, they are still masters, and those who tread easily upon the flowers do not seem to show disrespect.  

For someone like me with a sick body to trample upon the fallen flowers seems quite disrespectful indeed. However, for the beautiful children of life to tread upon them would surely be the wish of the fallen flowers. After all, how many among multi-thousands of blossoms could be compared to the one who gazes at their rosy cheeks?  

Yet, youth passes. Young people grow old, beauty fades, and what was once strong becomes weak. All businesses and joys of life belong to youth, and it slips away in dreams or in the blink of an eye.  

After a while, I lay down in the shade of spring,  

and when I opened my eyes from a light slumber, all the flowers in the garden had fallen.  

I wish for the passing spring to go away, and for the new spring to come.  

The conclusion is unexpectedly rebellious.  

"I am still here while everything is withering,"  
I might say in a boastful tone just once.  

  

**Summary**  

The article reflects on the fleeting nature of youth and beauty, as experienced by the author during a visit to see cherry blossoms after a prolonged illness. It contrasts the vitality of the young, who tread upon the flowers of spring, with the realization of the passage of time and the inevitability of aging. From a historical perspective, this piece echoes sentiments that might resonate within the context of colonial Korea, capturing a struggle against the backdrop of lost vitality during oppressive times, as well as the nostalgic longing for the vitality of youth.
