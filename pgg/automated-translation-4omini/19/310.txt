기사제목	내가 十八歲時節에 滿洲曠野를 밟고 高喊칠 때 (When I Stood on the Vast Plains of Manchuria at Eighteen)  
필자	朴熙道 (Park Hee-do)  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

When I stood on the vast plains of Manchuria at eighteen, how exhilarating that time was! How exhilarating! It’s not about squeezing the cheeks of Lee Ji-geun. What is exhilarating about it doesn’t add up, nor is it the essence of what I’m saying! Then, what about our youth? In our youth, without a social background, were there exhilarating moments that could be called exhilarating? Rather, there might be plenty of painful memories... However, if I had to capture just one by force, I would mention one exhilarating moment!

When I was seventeen or eighteen, I had embarked without any particular aspiration, perhaps carried by the wind, on the way to some destination, and I found myself stepping on the vast plains of Manchuria. When I crossed the Yalu River and stood on the boundless plains of Manchuria - just as the sun was sinking below the horizon, the sky was red and the earth was red!

When I stepped on the ground of the vast land that I had yearned for as I grew up in a narrow country, I unconsciously, or rather consciously, clenched my fists and shouted until my throat was raw. My heart, or rather my spirit, was far wider than the vast prairie. When I opened my hands and then clenched them tightly again, it felt as if the plain was grasped in my hands and that the vast land was in my mouth. At that moment, I couldn’t move, and my heart expanded with exhilaration until it got darker with the setting sun over the expanding land. Other than that, if I were to mention anything exhilarating, it would probably only be political in meaning, and I hesitate to do so.

If you ask me to close my eyes and say one thing, I would say that, given those words, there will be controversies from both sides, but anyhow, those who ruin will ruin, and those who prosper will prosper, and that moment will be the outcome of fate. If I dare to say, the likelihood of prospering was none, and the fate was destined to decline because of the stubbornness of the Yi dynasty (李朝). It’s amusing to say that the fall of my country is exhilarating; that is a great treason. Ha ha... 

   
**Summary**  

This article reflects on the author’s vibrant memories of youth and his feelings of exhilaration while experiencing the vastness of Manchuria at eighteen. The author contrasts youthful aspirations with the painful reality of losing his homeland under colonial rule. Notably, it reveals the tension between personal exhilaration and the collective grief of a nation facing decline. Historians may find the author's sentiments to be emblematic of the emotional struggles of Korean youth during the colonial period, illustrating how personal experiences are entwined with the national experience of loss and dislocation.
