Young Ones Play  
                       李秉岐  
We too, in due course with strong bodies,  
With healthy pulses that are vigorous,  
Step by step we advance on this earth,  
One foot in front of the other.  

No matter how fierce the winds of this world may blow,  
Regardless of what fate may usher in,  
Whether it be hundred miles or a thousand miles,  
Even if far, we must continue on our path.  

With hopes unlike any other’s we do possess,  
With courage and effort multiplied,  
With happiness and fulfillment distinct,  
That day shall surely come upon us.  

When fallen and despairing and all illusions come,  
Whenever they attempt to ensnare our feet,  
We must break free and go forth,  
For we are the young warriors of life.  

**Summary**  

This poem speaks to the resilience and determination of youth, highlighting themes of hope and courage against adversities. It reflects the spirit of striving for a better future even amid despair, emphasizing the unique strengths and aspirations of young individuals. From a historical perspective, it may offer insights into the sentiments of youth during Korea's colonial period, where the quest for identity and future amidst adversity was pronounced, making it a significant text for understanding the era's youth culture and aspirations.
