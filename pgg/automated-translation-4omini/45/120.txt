The father and the child  
Teacher: "Do you know the proverb 'Better to give than to receive'?"  
Student: "Yes, my father always says that."  
Teacher: "Ah- what a wonderful father! What kind of business does he run?"  
Student: "He’s a boxer."  

Difficult Hopes  
The prison warden called in a new prisoner and said, "Everyone who comes here must learn a trade to earn a living after their release. What trade do you want to learn?"  
Prisoner: "I would like to become a peddler."  

Resonance  
A: "What is a strike?"  
B: "It means to shorten the time."  
A: "It may be that in reality, a hour is not a very long time when you are in a 60-minute situation."  

Living More than in Heaven  
The pastor gave a wonderful sermon and said, "Would everyone who wishes to go to heaven please raise their hands?" Everyone present raised their hands, but there was just one person who did not.  
Pastor: "Don't you want to go to heaven?"  
That person: "Yes – of course I want to go, but I thought I shouldn’t go because my child is still young."  

A Minor Issue  
A: "You have a very good memory, so once something is in your head, it won't slip away, right?"  
B: "That's true; once it's in my head, it won't be forgotten."  
A: "Oh really? Then you wouldn't forget that you owe me five won, right?"  
B: "Uh, indeed, that is the case. But that is a minor issue; it's not in my head; it's in my pocket."  

Sad to Fall Behind  
A: "Hey, do you know who is the stingiest among your acquaintances?"  
B: "Old Gobdol. When everyone is waiting for their turn to buy tickets at the station, that man always goes to the very end."  
A: "So is he hoping to buy the tickets cheaper if there is one left over?"  
B: "Not at all; he is keeping his money in his pocket for as long as possible."  

Later, a letter came from the owner of the car shop.  
"In regard to the car I purchased earlier, my fiancée finds it uncomfortable to drive. Please exchange it for another one."  
The owner promptly replied,  
"In regard to that car, it is absolutely excellent, so I humbly request that you do not exchange it."  

A Helpless Child  
Mother: "Why aren't you talking, you rascal! Please go fetch me that thing there! Beat it!" The child hurried away. Thinking he had run away, but shortly after, he came back and said, "Mother, no matter how many times I look for it, I can’t find it with this stone."  

**Summary**  

The article captures a series of humorous anecdotes and dialogues reflecting life in early 20th-century Korea, showcasing the challenges and quirks of everyday interactions. These vignettes highlight social themes such as the importance of work ethics, family obligations, and the generational wisdom passed down through proverbs. Historians might find the emphasis on humor and resilience indicative of the societal struggles during the colonial era and how people coped with the constraints of their circumstances.

天國보다 살림
목사 영감이 고마운 설교를 한바탕 하든 끗헤 『어느 분이던지 천당에 가고 시푼 이는 손을 드십시요.』하니까 듯고 잇던 사람들이 전부 손을 드럿다. 그런데 꼭 한 사람만이 손을 안들엇다. 목사 『당신은 천당에 가고 십지 안습내까?』 그 사람 『네- 물론 가고 십지요만은 집에 애놈이 어려서 가지 안키로 생각 햇습지요.』

별문제
A 『자네는 기억력이 매우 조타는대 일단 머리 속에 드러만 가면 이저버리는 일이 업겟네 그려?』
B 『암 그러치 머리 속에 한 번만 드러오면 이저버리는 일이 업지.』
A 『응 그런가. 그러면 벌서 전에 자네가 나에게 돈 오원 뀐 것을 닛지 안엇겟지?』
B 『으응 그랫지. 그러나 그것은 별문제ㄹ세. 돈 오원을 머리 속에 너흔 것이 아니라 포켓트에 너엇스닛가.』

떠러지기 서러워
A 『여봐. 자네 아는 놈 중에 누가 제일 인색한가?』
B 『곱돌 영감일세. 정거장 표 파는 데서 여러 사람이 자기 차래를 기다리고 잇지 안켓나. 그러면 영감은 번번히 맨 뒤 꽁문이로 도라 가 서네.』
A 『올치 필요 업시 된 표가 잇스면 싸게 사겟다는 배ㅅ심이지?』
B 『천만에 될수 잇는대로 오래동안 표 살 돈을 주머니에 너어 두고 십흐니까 그러는 것이야』 ＜18＞
후에 자동차 상점 주인에게 편지가 왓다.
「제번 하옵고 일전에 사온 자동차로 말하면 나의 약혼녀자가 조종하기에 거북하다 하오니 다른 차로 박구어 주시옵...」.
주인 영갑 얼른 답장하기를
「경복자 그 차로 말슴하오면 절대 완전하오니 어부인을 박구시기를 절앙절앙 하옵나이다.」 하얏다.
