기사제목	別有天地·不具者의 人世觀, 안진뱅이가 旅行한 이야기 (There Is Another World: The View of Life of the Disabled, A Story of a Cripple's Journey)  
필자	朴南圭  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

There was a time when I wondered if my legs would ever heal. It was not as though I had become disabled in middle age. I was born with a disability, so I foolishly thought that, through the grace of God, I might eventually be healed. This was always my hope. Many years ago, there was a pastor named Kim Ik-du who claimed to heal any disease through faith, so I decided to travel to Sinchon (信川) in Hwanghae Province to seek his help. At that time, there were no trains to Sinchon. Holding onto the hope that, through God's holy grace, my legs would eventually be able to walk, I chose to walk there without riding horses or cars. I thought that my sincerity alone would be enough to cure my illness; it seemed to me that it would be too ostentatious to travel in luxury while seeking divine intervention. 

The journey from Seoul to Sinchon was over five hundred ri (approximately 200 kilometers). It was easy to say I would walk there, but those who have not experienced the difficulty themselves cannot truly understand it. The road was rugged, and the mountains were steep as I passed through Kaesŏng and made my way into Hwanghaedo near Geummyeon. Although travelers might have pitied me for my disability when I stopped at guesthouses along the way, the world was unkind, and instead of pity, I faced scorn. Why should I be treated harsher because I was disabled, despite having the same amount of money as anyone else? At that time, I prayed. “God, please heal my legs. And grant compassion to these people. Amen.”

After a month, I arrived at Pastor Kim’s home in Sinchon. Despite my deep devotion and earnestness in traveling so far, he treated me with disdain. When he realized I had money in my pocket, his attitude changed as well. However, all my efforts were in vain. The strenuous journey I undertook ended up injuring my back, and even now, I cannot use my back properly. Indeed, I was foolish back then, and the world around me was equally foolish. That a person like Kim Ik-du would engage in such deception, while in every place the church welcomed him with open arms believing his power to heal through Jesus, is quite astonishing.

**Summary**  

The article recounts the personal journey of the author, Park Nam-kyu, who, born with a disability, embarked on a grueling journey to seek healing from a pastor claiming to have divine powers. His unwavering faith and the hardships he encountered during the journey reflect the challenges faced by disabled individuals in a society lacking compassion. The narrative highlights both the author’s naïveté and the societal attitudes towards disability during this period, offering significant insights into the socio-religious landscape of Korea in the early 1930s.
