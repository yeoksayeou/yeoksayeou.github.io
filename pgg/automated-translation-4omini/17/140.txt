기사형태	논설 (Editorial)  

How to Guide Youth Interactions Between Men and Women  
My Three Proposals  
Kim Gi-dan  

First, the interaction between men and women, or more accurately speaking, the communication and engagement between all men and women, should be made freer and more natural. This is the first step in correcting the inappropriate customs between men and women. It is unavoidable to hide or block this natural instinct. Therefore, we must remove the veils and barriers that have been set up. In other words, in families, societies, research environments, and offices, we ought to engage openly and freely among ourselves, just as men interact with men or women with women. If, hence, men and women, including single young men and women, develop closeness and if this closeness leads to disputes, those interactions should become extremely natural and eloquent.

If these days, communication between men and women is not appropriate, most ordinary people would say, “That’s because the interaction between men and women is restricted, so to correct this issue, we must cut off their contact completely.” However, how unrefined is this thinking? How can one straightened nature by obstructing the flow of water? How can one purify human emotions by severing interactions?

Based on our experiences, we have spent thousands of years where men and women could not interact with each other. Do not be surprised. The outcome is that our men and women have lost the understanding of genuine interactions and have instead turned into licentious people. Look at the present day; is there any man who does not drool over women, or any woman who does not yearn for men? It is certainly a reality that, just due to laws and moral codes, the natural inclinations are suppressed. If these two elements were absent, society would be a creature of licentious debauchery. As Jesus said, “To lust in the heart is to commit adultery.” If we apply this saying to our current situation, who among us can deny that he is not an adulterer? This is indeed a filthy and pathetic state of affairs. Why is this so? It is because we have enforced the prohibition of natural interactions and placed gender-specific clothing and barriers, making it so that while men and women yearn for connection, they can only exhibit these desires in a twisted manner. How can we, then, correct the customs between men and women? 

Many often think that the issues of customs between men and women arise only in their interactions, yet this is also a narrow observation. Why is that? Because the psychological chaos stemming from couples who cannot interact is even more severe. We must observe that while we see decency in those situations where men and women do engage, there is a decay occurring within that space itself among those who have not. Moreover, today’s issues revolving around interaction between men and women do not derive from their newfound freedom, but rather that the suppressed emotions of past generations are exploding today under this guise of ‘freedom.’ Therefore, the ultimate solution to this problem is to facilitate more natural interactions between men and women, thus helping people train their consciousness to recognize humanity rather than merely seeing through the lens of gender.

Secondly, men should perceive women, and women should perceive men, acknowledging each other’s humanity. It is common in society to speak of human character, implying that men should treat women with character and vice versa. However, in my observation, we have not experienced the kind of respect towards women, let alone towards men, when recognizing their humanity in a serious way. Why is this so? Because character fundamentally refers to a genuinely human state. Yet who among us has truly lived with a consciousness of being a pure human being, or has seen others in such a manner? In other words, we have only viewed each other through the prism of social status, wealth, age, or gender, focusing instead upon superficial characteristics, and as such, projections of our identities have led us to fatalities in behaviors and thoughts. 

All matters aside, if we initialize the conversation about gender relations, then we indeed have historically utilized discriminatory policies towards gender to reach this historical moment. Consequently, to correct this fundamentally, we must first impart the essence of the sacredness of humanity to each person, compelling them to recognize the weighty responsibilities that come with such acknowledgment. This sacredness is what? To mention at length would take much discussion, but to put it briefly, it relates to the potential of each individual that stretches beyond this moment into the infinite future, necessitating evolution and refinement. This realization dominates humanity today, governing our lives as well as the structure of the universe. Under such reasoning, how can we belittle or disrespect a fellow being?

Thirdly, men and women both should assume the corresponding responsibilities in their individual instances. As considered earlier, every individual carries an absolute responsibility as a person while also being responsible for the roles corresponding to their individual situations. This means that among people, a Korean is responsible as a Korean, a student has responsibilities as a student, a young woman possesses duties particularly linked to her gender, and a married person has obligations associated with their marital status. If one is oblivious to these responsibilities, they might as well be a frivolous child without direction. Today's young men and women seem to lack a sense of such responsibilities more than they should. Many of them seem very carefree, taking the pocket money given by their parents and doing little more than playing around, focusing on leisure and false pursuits. These are the roots of moral decay, personal degradation, and a deviation from their familial duties. Consider, as individuals who are capable of influencing the fabric of the universe, can we afford such trivial behaviors? Young friends of this age should never dismiss such trivial behaviors as fleeting mistakes of youthful exuberance, for as the saying goes, a habit developed at three years old follows one even to eighty.

Three Proposals  

Zhang Yung-jin  

According to traditional customs, once a boy and a girl turn seven years old, they are separated, and thus, personal interactions are prohibited. However, the trends of the world and surrounding circumstances do not allow the relationship between men and women to remain in such states. In the future, human culture will necessitate that men and women unite in all aspects and mutually complement one another, progressing in harmony. Moreover, to solve future marital issues, gender equality matters, women’s labor issues, and various other concerns, men and women must undoubtedly have opportunities to interact and build mutual understanding. Each cultural region has already implemented co-educational systems for some time. Additionally, multiple social sectors have established organizations that facilitate the engagement of men and women, making such localities less peculiar, without any unusual attitudes when such interactions occur. However, in Korea, when young men and women come to meet, they tend more to regard the other in terms of sexual objectification rather than as individuals. Consequently, if the young start solely interacting, odd rumors often circulate, alongside various negative consequences.

The passive position occupied by women along with their natural predisposition to emotional weaknesses renders them vulnerable to temptation easily, especially when男子 (men) lacking sexual morality and principles come into the picture. This scenario depicts a terrible phenomenon facing our society; hence, there arises an aversion for parents or educators to allow freedom of social engagement for women. In our Korea, it appears that the chance for having co-educational systems will not arise anytime soon, nor is there any organization in society to facilitate such interactions; thus, it is critically challenging to explore even trial methods to open paths for socializing, which is why I propose a few experimental ideas:

1. Those men and women who receive education at or above vocational school should be allowed considerable freedom in socializing. 

2. For general male and female students, since we still cannot open the social gate freely, schools should collaborate with other schools, engaging in activities like joint competitions or music festivals under strict supervision—thus, students may coexist in public environments, forming connections. Alternatively, we could leverage trusted organizations such as religious institutions to jointly host various cultivation or lecture assemblies, offering a chance to interact. 

3. While public social interactions between men and women are acceptable, private visits to each other’s homes, as well as unnecessary exchanging of letters, should not be permitted. Even in the West, where free socializing is possible, unmarried men and women rarely exchange letters without significant purpose.

In summary, until women elevate their education and status, and until men develop a deep respect for women, unrestricted interaction between young men and women in Korea cannot be considered feasible. I wish to see young men and women exert effort towards their self-cultivation and advancement until the day comes when they can freely interact, in line with recent developments in the West.

Anti-Strategy to Strategy  
Kim Mi-ri  

"Girls from the Ginkgo School often appear to be at the threshold of prostitution." 

It might be true. However, if such individuals show up at our gates, would they be welcomed? Indeed, that is the case. My primary confrontation is the various undesirable interactions observed between young men and women. There are even instances where connections proceed to inappropriate levels. Yet, seeing such depravity and distressing situations prompts me to contemplate shutting our gates entirely.

Still, when I consider the matter, can I deny my own children? Can I restrict them and dictate their innate nature?

No! Keep the gates open. Even if we must open the innermost door, open it wide! We must ensure they engage in those interactions while advising them, "As a girl, do not forget these cautions while interacting with boys; and as a boy, remember the right thoughts while engaging with girls." I believe such admonitions are futile. Rather, let them discover for themselves. 

Ending the Word Guidance  
Seo Jeong-hui  

The attempts to guide interactions between men and women stem from the belief that through interaction, harmful situations can be avoided. However, I do not believe there are effective solutions to this issue. If we aim merely to censor misconduct, we might as well revert to older customs by restricting women's movements and prohibiting contact with men altogether. Nonetheless, the times have rendered such customs intolerable. No authority's intervention can block interactions between men and women; thus, prohibiting relations altogether is unfeasible. 

Similarly, we must accept that such negative consequences will arise from every interaction, which is why it seems we perceive such consequences through outdated perspectives and methodologies. Consider that human instincts, just like our drive for hunger, are naturally occurring drives and it is common for a human to seek relationships. Denying this natural inclination is precisely what generates harmful outcomes. If we allow individuals a delectable feast of experiences from every relationship and grant them the freedom to love and pursue marriage without defect, our homes and social fabric might reach a state of harmony and comfort.

Today’s so-called negative consequences originate from these unnatural situations; thus, we must break free from these restraints and promote interactions more freely, ensuring that the concept of respect for gender does not become a barrier and that classifications dissolve in favor of perceiving each participant as an individual worthy of mutual respect.  

Opportunities for Public Interactions Between Men and Women  
Park Hee-do  

There is no need to argue that social interactions between men and women should exist. However, the emergence of negative consequences clearly stems from secrecy and lack of public space. From a young age, I believe we should create opportunities for interaction not as private matters but in collective forms, where gatherings occur without secretive personal meetings. Schools for boys can collaborate with schools for girls, organizing events like academic or musical festivals under proper supervision. This facilitates familiarity and understanding among each other rather than creating hasty blind love during clandestine meetings. 

In church settings, lately, it seems there is a tendency for young men and women to attend mainly to meet their romantic partners rather than to worship. The number of rumors and disgrace from this overlap leaves much to be desired. This, too, originates from secrecy in interactions. If groups could be similarly established in churches or schools for natural congregation and interaction, there would be far fewer occasions for these awkward and embarrassing expressions of affection in sacred spaces. Therefore, whether it’s in churches, schools, or various community structures; allowing men and women to meet openly and freely should reduce the multiple negative outcomes that come from secrecy. 

Mutual Respect  
Baek Nam-gye  

Today, we find ourselves in a society that lacks rituals. This absence leads to a decline in the ethical consciousness regarding relationships between genders. Hence, even in public engagements, there exists the propensity to overlook that natural feeling of respect towards one another. Thus, despite the opportunities for social interaction, it may nurture private desires.

Emphasizing mutual respect between genders is my advocacy. When we relate with others, whether of the same or opposite gender, it is vital to regard one another’s dignity without the arbitrary markers that may result from societal appearances. I commend the notion that, in our Korea, men and women should cultivate respect towards each other as a priority.

Let Loose with Freedom  
Seon Yu-jeon  

There are times when I genuinely hope that, in observing all aspects of Korean society, everything could revert back to a state of natural necessary selection. For example, underfed individuals are not coddled; frail bodies are not nurtured indiscriminately, allowing natural selection to occur so that only the healthy can thrive. If students who struggle with learning are allowed to fall behind, leaving only worthwhile individuals in the educational system, then we can eliminate the distractions caused by failures. 

In light of this, I wonder if we should leave the unsatisfactory aspects of interaction between men and women without intervention, letting those who are unsuited to relationships recognize their shortcomings and fail. This, too, may lead to a realization of self-awareness, as well as personal accountability, thus eliminating those negative manifestations. 

In today's society, it appears that everything is orchestrated by those who wish to present themselves as wise or principled individuals bearing ideas. However, it seems that when it comes to eliminating the existing situation concerning male-female relationships, we have no room for serious contemplation. We are left with what seems solely like mere rhetoric.

Summary  

This article explores the dynamics and customs surrounding interactions between young men and women in Korean society during the late 1920s, emphasizing the need for more natural and respectful engagements. The author, Kim Gi-dan, proposes shedding the hidden barriers and fostering open communication free of the strictures of outdated norms. The discourse brings attention to a significant cultural transformation, reflecting the changing attitudes toward gender interactions in colonial Korea. Historians may find it notable how early discourses on gender roles and social behaviors hint at broader societal shifts towards modernization and individualism.

靑年 男女交際의 善導策 如何
나의 세가지 獻策
金起田

前略 첫재 남녀간의 교제, 아니 교제라하기보다도 모든 남자와 모든 녀자 사이에 잇서서 서로 대하고 서로 말하고 서로 석기여 나아가는 그것을 한층 더 자유스럽게 태연스럽게 하여라. 그럿타. 남녀간의 풍긔를 어즈럽게 아니하고 정말로 꼭 바르게 하는 데에는 이것이 첫재이다. 그 새를 가리워노코는 막어노코는 정말 엇지 할 수가 업는 것이다. 그러닛가 위선 그 가리워진 휘장을 맥혀진 장벽을 거더 치우고 볼 말이다. 다시 말하면 가정에서나 사회에서나 연구실에서나 사무실에서나 어대까지 자유스럽게 태연스럽게 교제하되 오즉 우리들의 남자 남자끼리 또는 녀자 녀자끼리가 서로 대하는 것과 가티 하도록 하여야 할 것이다. 그래서 오늘의 남자와 남자 또는 녀자와 녀자새에서 그리함과 가티 남자와 녀자 아니 처녀와 총각새에 잇서서까지도 서로 친하게 되면 친하고 (친한다는 말을 잘못 해석해서는 탈이다.) 서로 다투게 되면 다투어서 그 친하고 성기며 맛나고 떠남이 극히 례사롭게 되지 아느면 안될 것이다.
요새 남녀간에 풍긔가 조치 못하다 하면 이 말을 듯는 보통 사람은 보통으로 말하되 「그것은 모다 남녀간의 접촉이 자즌 데에서 생기는 것인즉 그 풍긔를 바로 잡으려면 근본뎍으로 남녀간의 접촉을 끈어버릴 수 밧게 업다.」고 하나니 이 과연 얼마나 철뎌치 못한 생각이냐. 흐르는 물을 막어서 그물길을 바로 잡을 수가 어대 잇스며 사람과 사람새의 교통을 끈어서 사람의 감정을 순화(純化)할 수가 잇슬가.
뎨일 우리들이 경험해 본 일로써 말한다 할지라도 우리는 몃천년동안을 두고 남녀간에는 서로 교통을 못하게 하고 사라왓섯다.＜46＞ 놀내지 마러라. 그 결과는 우리네의 남자란 남자와 녀자란 녀자의 거의 전부를 모라서 음부 탕녀를 지여노코 마럿다. 보아라. 오늘날에 우리 사람으로서 엇던 남자치고 녀자에게 침을 흘리지 안는 자가 잇스며 엇던 녀자치고 남자를 그리워 아니하는 자가 잇는가를 법률때문에 톄면때문에 그럿치 만일 그 두놈만 업다하고 보면 이 사회는 당장에 음부 탕녀의 수라장이 되고 말 것이다. 예수의 말에 「마음으로써 간음한다.」는 말이 잇다. 이 말을 우리 사람들에게 뎍용하면 오늘의 우리가 누가 姦夫姦婦 아니리요. 과연 더러웁고 측측하고 야비한 꼴이다. 이것이 모다 무슨 까닭이냐 하면 남녀간의 평소의 교통을 끈코 장옷을 격하야 중문(中門)을 격하야 서로 그리워서만 지내게 한 결과 그 정회가 싸이고 또 싸이고 막히고 또 맥히여서 그 다음에는 요행 녀자(혹은 남자) 그림자만 보아도 남자(혹은 녀자)의 음성만 드러도 공연히 신경이 흥분되여서 츰을 째- 흘리게 되는 것이다. 이러고 엇지 남녀간의 풍긔를 바로 잡을 수가 잇스리요.
