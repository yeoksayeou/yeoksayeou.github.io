Constitution of the Alcoholic Nation  
To enhance the drinking happiness of the general populace and to promote the prosperity of alcohol prices, and to eternally maintain world peace, hereby I proclaim the Constitution of the Alcoholic Nation.  
Established on February 1, 1929,  
Great Alcohol Nation's Lowly Servant  
* Each Minister is to sign below.

1. Anyone who violates this Constitution shall be exiled to the Prohibition Land for one year.  

2. This Constitution shall take effect from the date of publication.  

Article 1: The Alcoholic Nation shall be forever maintained by the general populace.  

Article 2: The territory of the Alcoholic Nation shall encompass the entire world, but the United States and other Prohibition Lands shall be treated as special colonies.  

Article 3: The Alcoholic Nation shall have no Heavenly Sovereign or General (in drunkenness, there is no sovereign, thus the origin of the saying).  

Article 4: Those with physical or mental impairments, minors, and Christians are not permitted to join the Alcoholic Nation.  

Article 5: The capital of the Alcoholic Nation shall be designated as Iron Jar City.  

Article 6: Any individual who has the qualification to consume more than three cups and pays a tax (alcohol cost) to the Alcoholic Nation shall become a rightful drinker.  

Article 7: Members of the Alcoholic Nation shall be given ranks and titles according to their consumption level: Alcoholic Sovereign, Alcoholic President, Alcoholic General, Alcoholic Scholar, Alcoholic Soldier, Alcoholic Doctor, Alchol Craftsman, etc.  

Article 8: The ranks within the Alcoholic Nation shall be classified into five levels: Gong, Hu, Baek, Ja, Ran, whereby those who drink well from empty cups shall be called Dukes, those who drink plentifully from large cups shall be called Marquises, those who can drink a hundred cups shall be called Earls, those who pour and drink for themselves shall be called Barons, and those who carelessly pour for others shall be called Rascals.  

Article 9: Members of the Alcoholic Nation have the obligation to possess a steaming container (alcohol bottle).  

Article 10: Members of the Alcoholic Nation are obliged to compensate for drinking someone else's alcohol.  

Article 11: The number of cups for drinking shall adhere to the principle of odd numbers, specifically one being inadequate, three being small, five being appropriate, seven being allowable, and nine being sufficient; anything above is left free according to an individual's constitution, financial capabilities, and tastes.  

Article 12: The appropriate times for drinking are defined as follows:  
1. When meeting with friends in distant lands  
2. Under the slanting wind and fine rain at sunset  
3. During idle moments in a cold inn with dim lights  
4. On clear nights with white moons and snow  
5. When flowers bloom and leaves fall  
6. In times of sadness and melancholy  
7. In times of joy and excitement  
(However, drinking for meals or sobering up is allowed at any time.)  

Article 13: People should drink but must not be consumed by alcohol.  

Article 14: Those with ample facial hair must clean their mouths thoroughly with a cloth after finishing drinks.  

Article 15: Those with lovers must brush their teeth thoroughly after drinking before kissing.  

Article 16: Individuals who do not pour drinks shall be punished for the crime of non-pouring.  

Article 17: Those caught selling alcohol alone shall be permanently banned from participating in communal drinking sessions. However, if they demonstrate humility, penalties may be reduced.  

Article 18: Anyone engaging in arguments or duels under the influence of alcohol that disrupts public order or endangers public health shall be immediately removed from the drinking venue.  

Article 19: Those arriving late to a drinking meeting shall be punished in accordance with the customary law of three cups for latecomers. However, this law shall be abolished from the date of this law's proclamation, as it is considered too harsh in certain regions where latecomers are obliged to drink three cups for each seat.  

Article 20: Those who sing, dance, or engage in excellent merrymaking after drinking will be awarded a large cup.  

Article 21: Those belonging to the Alcoholic Nation shall be recognized as prohibiting ten behaviors:  
1. Not drinking well and only consuming food.  
2. Taking credit for others' drinking.  
3. Only providing chatter while holding a cup.  
4. Moving to a different seat while drinking.  
5. Not following after drinking.  
6. Singing at a funeral gathering while drinking.  
7. Crying while drinking at a festive gathering.  
8. Only consuming others’ alcohol while not offering their own.  
9. Sneaking into others' drinking gatherings.  
10. Making lengthy speeches at banquet drinking gatherings.  

Article 22: The Alcoholic Nation members may walk either in a Zhi (之) or Hyeon (玄) fashion, provided they do not obstruct public transport.  

Article 23: The Alcoholic Nation members may have their hats tilted leftwards but will not be restricted specifically.  

Article 24: The facial complexion of the Alcoholic Nation members may turn red, but will not be restricted.  

Article 25: Members of the Alcoholic Nation should prepare at least one gold piece and fifty coins in their pockets (to avoid the risk of being penalized for public urination).  

Article 26: To foster culture, members of the Alcoholic Nation must recite the Drinking History, Drink Eight Immortals Song, and Ode to Alcohol at least once a day.  

Article 27: Since alcohol is a crazy medicine, drinkers shall be treated as possessing a passionate madness, and those who do not allow this understanding shall be recognized as chaos bandits of this school.  

Article 28: Other than the Alcoholic Party and the Alcoholic Water Party, establishing other parties is prohibited.  

Article 29: This law may not be changed without the majority decision of the Alcoholic Party members.  

**Summary**

This document is a humorous and satirical constitution for a fictional "Alcoholic Nation," reflecting Korean drinking culture in the 1920s. The article outlines various laws and social norms about drinking, showcasing a blend of traditional societal values and a playful take on governance. From a historical perspective, the existence of such writings indicates the complex relationship between alcohol consumption, societal behavior, and the broader context of colonial restrictions during this period in Korea, illustrating how cultural practices were shaped in part by the challenges of colonial rule.

酒國憲法
余가 一般麴民의 飮福을 增進하고 麴價의 隆昌을 圖하며 世界平和를 永遠維持하기 爲하야 玆에 酒國憲法을 發布하노라.
酒降生一千九百二十久年二月一日
大酒國賤者御酩
*務各大臣署名

1. 이 憲法에 違反하는 者는 一年間 禁酒國에 流配함.
1. 이 憲法은 發布日로부터 施行함.

第1條 酒國은 一般麴民으로 永遠維持함.
第2條 酒國의 영토는 전세계로 하되 米國과 가튼 禁酒國은 특별식민지로 함.
第3條 酒國은 天子, 大將이 無함(醉中無天子, 酒無大將이라는 由來語에 의하야).
第4條 심신에 故障이 잇는 자, 미성년 남녀, 기독교 신자는 酒國에 입적을 불허함.
第5條 酒國의 國都는 鐵甕城으로 정함.
第6條 三盃以上을 먹을 자격이 잇는 자로 酒國에 세금(酒代)을 납입한 자는 누구나 酒權者가 됨.
第7條 酒國에 입적한 자는 그 정도여하를 따라서 酒天子, 酒大統領, 酒大將, 酒*知, 謀酒兵丁酒, 알콜박사, 酒太白 등 榮位, 雅號를 與함.
第8條 酒國의 爵位는 空, 厚, 百, 自, 濫 5等으로 하야 술잔을 잘 비도록 먹는 사람은 공작(公爵), 큰잔으로 두둑히 먹는 사람은 후작(厚爵), 百盃를 능히 먹는 사람은 백작(百爵), 자기 손으로 부어 먹는 사람은 자작(自爵) 함부로 부어 먹는 사람을 람작(濫爵)이라 칭함.
第9條 麴民은 蒸甁(술병)의 의무가 有함.
第10條 麴民은 남의 술을 먹고 반듯이 보상할 의무가 有함.
第11條 술의 盃數는 酒不護盃의 원칙에 의하야 반듯이 奇數로 하되 一不若, 三小, 五宜, 七可, 九足으로 하고 此 以上은 麴民의 체질, 金力, 嗜好에 의하야 자유방임함.
第12條 음주의 適宜한 시기는 左와 如히 정함.
1. 千里他鄕逢友時
2. 斜風細雨夕陽天
3. 旅館寒燈無聊時＜160＞
4. 月白雪白夜
5. 花開葉落時
6. 憂鬱悲哀時
7. 痛快興奮時
(단, 飯酒 及 解酲은 隋時 실행함.)
第13條 사람이 술을 먹되 술이 사람을 먹지 안케 할 事.
第14條 수염이 만흔 사람은 잔을 畢한 때마다 반듯이 수건으로 口頭 大淸潔을 할 事.
第15條 애인이 잇는 사람은 酒後에 반듯이 양치를 잘하고 『키스』할 事.
第16條 술을 붓지 안는 사람은 不傾罪에 처함.
第17條 술을 혼자 밀매음한 자는 無期로 공동 酒會에 참가를 불허함.
단 改悛의 事가 잇는 때에는 酌量減輕함을 得함.
第18條 술을 먹은 뒤에, 언쟁, 결투를 하야 酒國의 공안을 방해하거나 *逆케을 하야 공중위생을 방해하는 자는 즉시 酒會席에서 퇴장을 명함.
第19條 酒會에 遲參한 자는 後來者三盃란 관습법에 의하야 처벌함.
단, 특수 지방에 시행하는 소위 谷山 사돈복이 이法은 너무 가혹함으로 본 법 발포일로부터 폐지함.(谷山에서는 後來者에게 座客마다 三盃식을 주는 일이 잇다.)
第20條 酒後에 노래나 춤 기타 작란을 잘하는 자는 大盃를 賞與함.
第21條 左記에 해당한 자는 酒國의 十不出로 認함.
1. 술 잘 안먹고 안주만 먹는 자.
2. 남의 술에 제 생색내는 자.
3. 술잔 잡고 잔소리만 하는 자.
4. 술 먹다가 딴 좌석에 가는 자.
5. 술 먹고 따를 줄 몰으는 자.
6. 상가집 술 먹고 노래하는 자.
7. 잔치집 술 먹고 우는 자.
8. 남의 술만 먹고 제 술 안내는 자.
9. 남의 酒席에 제친고 다리고 가는 자.
10. 宴會酒席에서 축사 오래하는 자.
第22條 麴民黨員의 步法은 之자 혹은 玄자 식으로 하야도 무방하되 공중 교통을 방해치 말 事.
第23條 酒國麴民의 모자는 左傾하야도 특별히 取締치 안이함.
第24條 酒國麴民의 顔面은 赤化하야도 取締치 안이함.
第25條 麴民黨員은 少하야도 懷中에 一金 五十錢也를 준비함이 可함.(소변 濫放의 科料 처벌을 당하기가 쉬우닛가)
第26條 酒國麴民은 교양의 필요상 長進酒, 飮中八仙歌, 酒德頌 등을 一日一回式 낭독할 事.
第27條 酒者는 伐性之狂藥也라는 녯 해석을 곳치여서 酒者는 伐性之狂하니 藥也다 解하되 此를 不準하는 者는 斯門亂賊으로 認함.
第28條 酒國의 정당은 麴民黨麴水黨 이외에 他黨의 설치를 불허함.
第29條 본 법은 麴民黨員의 다수결의가 안이면 변경함을 不得함.＜161＞
