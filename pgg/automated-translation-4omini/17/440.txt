기사제목	新神話 「開闢以前」 (New Myth "Before Creation")  
필자	夜雷 (Yarae)  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Other)  

One day, an old man called Mukha-ong came to me and insisted on revealing something to me that was supposed to be important. I explained that I did not have the ability to interpret what he wanted and did not even have any relevant knowledge. The old man continued his insistence, saying that there was a secret within me that I had never told anyone, and that it was essential for my very existence. I acknowledged that there had to be such a secret within me, but I was hesitant to share it because it was so terrifying that if anyone heard it, they would think it was nonsense, and so I was afraid of being called a liar. However, Mukha-ong insisted that he needed to hear it to avoid despair, and I reluctantly agreed to share a portion of my secret.

I warned Mukha-ong that if he perceived my words as a lie, it would lead to grave consequences. I reiterated that the secret I was about to share had an immense gravity; the moment he doubted its truth, he would face dire repercussions. Mukha-ong, however, urged me not to worry and pressed further for my revelation. So, I began to share the prologue of this knowledge.

I told him that this knowledge was beyond normal understanding; it transcended past, present, and future. To understand it, he must first grasp the philosophical notion of eternal recurrence proposed by Nietzsche, suggesting that the universe experiences endless cycles where everything repeats itself. I explained that our current understanding of this world is just a fraction of an unfathomable continuum of existence.

Mukha-ong seemed to be deeply pondering my words, affirming their significance. 

I explained further that our world, viewed through this lens, was merely one in countless iterations where similar events had occurred across eons. The essence of what I was about to share existed before the cosmic upheaval we now call creation.

I continued,

"Imagine a time before the current universe had fully formed—a primeval state of energy and potential where beings lived differently than they do now. The advancements we experience today are vastly inferior to what existed in that time."

I compared past innovations with modern progress, highlighting transportation advancements, such as how we would regard current machines as primitive compared to what existed before the upheaval. For instance, I might suggest the existence of a remarkable vehicle that traveled not through conventional means but through an energy akin to radio waves, allowing for travel across the cosmos effortlessly.

Mukha-ong was amazed by the description of this 'radiocar' and sought to understand how it functioned. I clarified that it did not rely on air or conventional physics but utilized energy transmissions, making travel between planets an everyday occurrence. 

He expressed wonder at the implications of such advancements and asked about social structures, inquiring if nations existed in the same way they do now. I replied that in the vastness of that world, where boundaries ceased to matter, distinctions between countries were irrelevant.

I offered an example of interplanetary unions among beings that would reflect a more unified existence compared to our current divisions.

**Summary**  

The article presents a philosophical exploration of the universe, rooted in a blend of mythical storytelling and Nietzsche's concepts of eternal recurrence. Key themes include the insignificance of our current societal constructs in the grand scale of existence, and the transformative evolution of beings that transcended the limitations of air and borders. For historians looking into the Korean colonial period, the discussion illustrates a yearning for understanding and contemplating existence beyond constraints imposed by colonial power, illustrating a bridge between traditional Korean thought and western philosophy.

「여보 령감- 사람이 비위가 조와도 분수가 잇지 平安監司도 저 하기 실으면 고만둔다는 말이 안잇소. 남이 이약이 하기 실타는 것을 그러케 졸으면 엇저잔 말이요.」

「여보 영감 그러면 내가 영감의 간청에 못익여 말은 조곰 하겟는데 영감이 만약 이 말을 듯다가 거즛말로 알면, 안됨니다. 만약 마음으로라도 거즛말이거니 하면 영감의 생명이 업서지는 것이니까 꼭 實말로만 알고 듯겟소. 단단히 알아챌이우, 정말 위엄합니다. 말 자체가 위엄하다는 것이 안이라 이 말을 거즛말로 아는 날이면 그날부터 영낙업시 탈이 납니다. 그 탈은 화태편작이라도 곳칠 길이 업스니까 하는 말이오. 그래서 내가 이 말을 함부로 못하는 것이오. 그래도 말을 하란 말이오. 단단히 말슴하시오.」
無何翁은 내 말을 듯더니 다시 허허하고 웃고 나서
「여보 딱한 말슴 마시고 어서 들여주오. 말을 듯지 못해서 자살하겟는 놈이 들어서 죽을 리가 잇소. 설사 죽는다 할지라도 듯고 죽는 것이 안듯고 죽너니보다 나은 것이 안이오. 가슴이 답답하야 견딀 수 업소. 어서 들여주오. 어서 어서 금방 죽을 디경이오. 어서 어서?」
