男子, 내가 조케 생각하는 男子  
여성적인 남자를  

What is disliked and pleasant is based on one's perspective. Just as someone may find a scruffy dog delightful, some women admire certain types of men while others may think differently. Some women might prefer a man with a robust build and cheerful personality, embodying the spirit of a true gentleman, while others might be drawn to a man who is more refined, feminine in nature, with a demure appearance resembling a gentle figure. There are also women who appreciate scholars in law or literature, and even artists or theater practitioners. Just as each person's appearance differs, so too do perceptions of others. Therefore, how one evaluates a man cannot be stated objectively; one can only speak subjectively based on personal preference. 

Whether I was single before or married now, I have always admired and yearned for such men. They should be of medium build, not too skinny or too overweight, as perhaps my own skinny body influences this view. 

A man should be tall but not excessively so; ideally, one who is neither too short nor too tall will do. 

His personality should be lively and warm-hearted. 

Moreover, he should be responsible, thoroughly completing his tasks diligently. 

Additionally, he should engage well with the community and not isolate himself. 

The most detestable type of man is the one who is ugly yet acts haughty, who, upon acquiring some wealth or status, forgets his comrades and disregards loyalty and ethics. No matter how much money he might share, I would never consider him worthy. To think of someone like that as a lifelong partner is unthinkable. 

Is there such a man who possesses these qualities?  

나의 조와하는 五色男  

If one has a similar mindset and ideals, they will regard any man affectionately. However, among those of the same viewpoint, we naturally tend to admire individuals who exhibit unique qualities, virtues, personality, or character that stand out. But how many such well-rounded individuals exist in this world? It’s often said that beautiful men might also have undesirable traits, and a good-natured man might be weak or insecure in other ways. Humans, like the scattered fruit, have both good looks and bad disposition, so preference can vary widely. 

Thus, who someone finds appealing is ultimately dictated by their personal views. I might not be aware of how others feel, but such a man is always in my thoughts as I look for a husband. 

Body-wise, he shouldn't be overly plump, though some flesh is desirable; perhaps because I am too thin. 

A tall man is appreciated as long as he's not extraordinarily tall; someone of average height would suffice. 

He should have a cheerful yet kind disposition. 

He must have a strong sense of responsibility, dedicating himself fully to any task with diligence. 

He should also maintain communication with others rather than bury himself away. 

The most detestable quality is in a person who lacks humility, one who brags despite their shortcomings and who, when wealth comes, casts aside their previous companions and misunderstands values such as loyalty and morality. 

Such a man is not worthy of my regard, no matter how wealthy they become or how long one were to live together. 

이런 男子가 잇슬가  

I have never experienced romance nor do I have the qualifications to speak on matters of men who I admire or those who do not meet my standards. But I would like to share some thoughts I usually hold dear. 

Firstly, a man should be either good-looking or not particularly striking, yet he must be outgoing, courageous, and competent. 

Secondly, whether he possesses wealth or scholarship, his character must be noble above all. 

Thirdly, he should be energetic about his work and diligent in execution. 

Fourthly, he should not have a conformist or complacent mentality; instead, he must possess a spirit for innovation and reform. 

Fifth, he ought to be generous and accepting of others. 

Sixth, he should always think about and understand Korea. 

Seventh, he should not expect rest in the comfort of his spouse. 

Such are the traits I admire in men; however, I do wonder how many of them truly exist in this world.  

義俠心 만코 씩씩한 男子  

While the topic at hand is quite interesting, I speak boldly as I understand the potential for misunderstanding if I am not careful. While I may not have a clear idea of my ideal man, if I were to become one or choose one, I would desire such qualities in a male companion. 

Firstly, in terms of character, he should be spirited and genial. A man with a petty disposition who merely pretends to be meek is quite unbearable, even to a stranger; I would find him insufferable. 

He should possess courage and altruism, helping others without hesitation in matters requiring justice. 

Should he be too dour yet consider himself worthwhile, that is sometimes hard to endure; a sharp-minded man is essential. 

Regarding appearance, he shouldn't be too ugly, nor overly ill-looking; it's more about being neither hideously unattractive nor dazzlingly handsome. Just being reasonably masculine in looks suffices. 

As for stature, he should be neither excessively tall nor too diminutive; average height is acceptable. 

In terms of knowledge, a man ought to have broad knowledge without being pompous, while it is also undesirable to possess mediocre knowledge and speak presumptuously of subjects he knows little about. 

Experience is invaluable; men in particular should face life's challenges, as they not only bring value to life but also develop empathy for others through their struggles. 

Such a man is indeed admirable, yet I must consider if I fit such criteria myself! If I were a man, I would aspire to be just like that.  

**Summary**  

This article consists of personal reflections on the characteristics and qualities idealized in men by the authors, showcasing distinct preferences while revealing deeper cultural notions of masculinity at the time. Each contributor expresses a desire for balanced traits: kindness, responsibility, emotional intelligence, and a good character against materialism. Notably, it highlights the influence of societal standards on gender roles during Korea's colonial era, illustrating how women's expectations of men intertwine with aspirations for social progress and personal fulfillment, an aspect that could be valuable in studies of gender dynamics under colonial rule.
