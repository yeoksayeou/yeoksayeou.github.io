기사제목	李太王俄館播遷事件 丙申二月大政變記, 建陽元年二月十一日 (The Incident of King Li's Transfer to the Russian Legation: A Record of the Coup d'État on the 2nd of February in the Year of Byeongshin, 11th of February in the Year of Gyeongyang)  
필자	車相瓚 (Ch'a Sang-ch'an)  
기사형태	문예기타 (Other Literary Forms)  

The incident involving King Li's transfer to the Russian legation must be observed within the context of the political upheaval caused by the assassination of Queen Min on October 8, 1895 (lunar calendar, August 20). This event is recorded as one of the most tragic incidents in the late Yi Dynasty's history, leading to a significant disruption in the politics of Hanseong (Seoul). Following the Sino-Japanese War, a pro-Russian faction emerged, led by individuals such as Lee Beom-jin and Lee Eun-yong, whose influence caused a temporary disorganization of the political environment.

Old Grand Duke Lee Geun-taek, who had been retreating to a secluded life full of complaints and poetry while distancing himself from the political turmoil, was suddenly thrust back into prominence through the influence of Japanese Minister Miura, returning to the throne as a regent for the third time. He reorganized the cabinet, appointing his first son, Lee Jae-myeong, as the head minister and appointing various other figures to key positions. A public statement was issued on behalf of the Grand Duke, expressing concerns about the nation's direction under the influence of corrupt officials, asserting that he could no longer watch his family's destiny unfold in such a way. Thus, the political reform mechanisms were triggered.

However, the assassination of Queen Min brought about a profound shock within the nation, leading to growing resentment among the populace against both the Japanese government and the new cabinet. The international implications were too significant to ignore. The Japanese government, concerned about its face and the turmoil in Korea, sent Koemura Suetaro as an envoy to investigate. On October 20 of the same year, he withdrew Minister Miura and arrested over 40 individuals involved to ensure political stability. The new cabinet was pressured to reorganize the military and disband the training corps, transitioning the army's structure to ensure loyalty to the new regime.

The first conspiracy of the pro-Russian faction failed, leading to significant consequences. Political instability continued to instigate precarious sentiments among the population, particularly following the abrupt decree of a haircut issued by the Grand Duke on December 30, which aimed to enforce Western-style haircuts and clothing among the populace. The backlash was fierce, with many local leaders in the provinces enforcing the decree, leading to widespread dissent and eventually sparking movements against the authority. 

Jeong In-hoe from Chuncheon was propelled into action, gathering supporters to resist the authorities in a bid for the restoration of the queen's dignity. This resulted in attacks on government offices and mobilization even amidst government disarray. The movement escalated rapidly, culminating in assaults in the capital, which terrified the people of Hanseong.

During these tumultuous times, we see the manipulative actions of the Russian envoy, who sought to exploit the chaos to establish stronger Russian influence in Korea. They took control of the Korean monarchy after persuading King Li to relocate to the Russian legation for safety as the pro-Russian faction regained strength and moved to consolidate their power. This chain of events marked a stark loss of sovereignty for Korea, culminating in further exploitation and the ceding of significant resources and political rights to foreign powers, particularly Russia and other countries. This period of chaos would lead to increasing foreign grip over Korean affairs and set the stage for significant exploitation wherein rights over critical resources were designated to foreigners during King Li's coerced stay in the Russian legation.

Ultimately, this transition marked not just a temporary setback of authority but the beginning of Asian and international dominance, wherein Korea's political landscape would eventually be exploited by colonial powers seeking favorable outcomes at the expense of the sovereign rights of the Korean state.

Summary  

This article delineates the political turmoil in Korea during the early 20th century, emphasizing the implications of the assassination of Queen Min and the subsequent realignment of power, which ultimately facilitated increased foreign control. The provocative actions of the pro-Russian faction and the Russian envoy, along with the domestic unrest following the controversial haircut decree, illustrate the extreme vulnerability of Korea during this period of colonial encroachment. Historians would find the details of this episode particularly significant as they reveal early indicators of the impactful foreign intervention that would define Korea's colonial experience.

李斗璜은 機를 알고 먼저 도망하며 兪吉濬은 체포되야 光化門앞 총각있는데 까지 끌여가다가 幸히 三軍府(現 朝鮮 步兵隊)를 派守하던 日兵의 救護로 僅히 생명을 保하야 日本에 망명하고 기타 趙熙淵, 李軫鎬, 李範來, 張博 등도 또한 日本으로 망명하니 金宏集의 내각은 그만 운명을 맛치고 마럿다. 이에 俄國 公使는 다시 仁川에 정박한 자국 군함에서 수군 200명을 증원하야 貞洞 일대를 엄중이 警衛하고 李範晋의 認狀이 無하면 비록 大官도 출입을 자유로 못하게 하며 일방으로 李太王에게 秦請하야 俄館 內에서 정식의 詔를 下하야 11월 28일 사건으로 처형된 李敏宏, 安駉壽, 南萬里 등의 죄를 特赦하고 安駉壽는 다시 警務使, 李範晋은 法大, 李完用은 外大 겸 學大, 金炳始는 摠理로 朴定陽은 임시 그 署理를 보게 되니 이로부터 朝鮮의 천하는 全然이 親露派의 수중으로 도라가고 萬般의 政事는 俄公使의 鼻息 알에 좌우하며 君上의 父子는 일개 流寓의 客과 가티 俄館 一隅에서 起居動作하니 당시 국사는 실로 寒心冷膽하야 寧히 言치 아는 것이 可하게 되엿다.
