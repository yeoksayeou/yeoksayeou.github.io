기사제목	敎壇에서 본 男學生과 女學生. 男女는 學生時代부터 엇더케 다른가 (Observations on Male and Female Students from the Teaching Platform. How Are Boys and Girls Different from Their Student Days?)  
저자	曺在浩 (Jo Jaehong)  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

Male and female students are alike in that they are students, but differences arise from their gender. 

Thus, by clarifying the fundamental reasons for gender differences from physiological or psychological standpoints, it is assumed that the differences between boys and girls will also become clear. However, I do not intend to explain the similarities or differences between male and female students either deductively or inductively starting from such reasons. I simply aim to reveal a few observable phenomena that might occur on the surface. 

Both male and female students, regardless of their grade level, seem unable to escape from the four broad issues: study problems, ideological issues, romantic problems, and employment problems. It seems that the central issues shift according to age and grade. Until about the first and second years of secondary school, the lives of both genders seem to focus solely on study issues. However, as they begin to confront the complexities of the world, curiosity and doubt arise, leading to the embryonic development of ideological issues. At the same time, as their age triggers primal instincts, romantic problems begin to disturb them—this seems to occur predominantly in the third and fourth years of secondary school. 

Once these ideological and romantic issues begin to impact the lives of male and female students, subtle differences in focus begin to emerge. Male students appear to concentrate more on ideological issues with a self-centered attitude rather than on romantic problems. Consequently, this slowly grows to encompass self-reflection and the contemplation of their vocations, gradually transitioning into matters of career. In contrast, female students seem to be more preoccupied with romantic issues than with ideological concerns, as they start to become aware of their paths as women, leading to an eventual focus on family. 

This tendency becomes markedly evident in the upper secondary level. Regardless of gender, those students who view their futures more long-term may temporarily concentrate all their lives on issues such as admission to senior schools, but students who consider real society tend to prioritize employment issues for males and marriage problems for females. Of course, this merely points out the main issues and does not negate all other problems. This describes what I have observed as general tendencies of male and female students at a superficial level. 

As an educator, I also see many interesting issues regarding male and female students' school lives that I wish to communicate to the students and their parents. 

First, let us take a look at their learning conditions in the classroom. Female students seem to absorb all issues emotionally and submissively. If the teacher shows unpleasant feelings while teaching, they seem to reflect that unpleasantness. They learn in a manner akin to walking on thin ice, regardless of whether what they are taught is bitter or sweet. 

Of course, this may also apply to male students to some extent, but it shows more prominently in female students. Male students do not simply accept issues while subtly gauging the teacher's feelings. They tend to chew through problems critically and voice their concerns when they find things bitter or sweet. The attitude of male students appears significantly different from that of female students, as they seem to meticulously understand each lesson by examining it from various angles and digesting it deeply. 

Next, let us examine their dormitory lives and friendships. Female students appear to form friendships rooted in affection. It seems rare for them to interact purely on a utilitarian basis or on the grounds of obligation. When they deal with friends, they transcend all theories, seemingly establishing bonds based solely on mutual feelings, almost relying on their sixth sense. Consequently, even within the same classroom, the nature of friendships among female students is acutely perceived by third parties. Regarding their close friendships, one could say they would willingly sacrifice their lives for each other, forming extraordinarily close bonds that could withstand life or death situations. However, once a rift occurs, they may treat each other as enemies. It is easy to form friendships but equally easy to break them. This relationship can be defined as easily manageable but fragile. 

In contrast, among male students, friendships are not quite so straightforward. Consequently, although there may be deeply trusted friendships, they tend to appear distant to outsiders. This may stem from an innate feeling of reliance and trust. As a result, male students generally refrain from revealing their true selves to their companions. Thus, on the one hand, they seem close to everyone, yet on the other, they also seem to share a sense of disconnect with all. 

Returning to the learning attitudes and friendships, when we approach male and female students' views on employment and romance, there is much to glean for educators and other fields as well. 

Differences between Male and Female Students  
Zhang Yingzhen  

It is a commonly understood fact that men and women have various inherent differences in their temperaments, physiques, functions, and numerous other points—one that all parents raising children can intuitively grasp. However, when we compare an all-male school and an all-female school in terms of development, these differences become even more apparent.

1. Differences in Temperament  
Men are typically characterized by their courage and fortitude, while women, especially young women, are marked by their gentleness and grace. The temperament of men is aggressive, easily cheerful, and expansive. They are unrestrained in expressing their thoughts openly without hesitation. This characteristic, perhaps due to their generous nature, often leads to negligence regarding trivial matters, manifesting as somewhat rough behavior. In other words, they lack meticulous consideration. Thereby, their patience is limited, along with their capacity for sustained focus on one subject. Thus, their thoughts tend to wander easily. In contrast, women are more conservative. They are cautious, attentive, and possess acute observational skills regarding matters, showing great strength of will and patience. Actions taken by men are largely governed by reason, while women's actions are primarily swayed by their highly sensitive emotions. Their expressions of joy, anger, and sorrow are also somewhat quicker. Men, when faced with failure or resentment, are quick to abandon and forget, while women tend to cling onto their feelings and do not easily let go of past grievances. Hence, when men make mistakes, they tend to feel heavy concern, whether receiving criticism from a teacher or suffering punitive embarrassment in front of others; however, once the moment passes, they quickly forget it. It could be said that they are somewhat bold and lack a sense of shame. Women, on the other hand, are more anxious and sensitive, tending to cry and be frightened by slight admonishments. This might be what is colloquially referred to as "tears being a woman's weapon." Therefore, even if a mistake occurs, it seems more effective to privately address it with gentle words instead of publicly criticizing or blaming, even if there is a reason to emphasize the matter. Men tend to remain silent and appear indifferent, while women often speak up and feel passionately. Men are broad-minded enough to generally disregard trivial matters, while women, with narrower hearts, are more prone to jealousy and envy. For instance, if a teacher praises or shows special affection for someone in a class, it becomes easy for the teacher or that individual to unintentionally create resentment. When exam results are announced, even if the results are simply published by number rather than being shown directly, it is usually considered good practice not to disclose them among peers. Overall, this tendency highlights women's inclination toward envy and jealousy regarding those they perceive as superior to themselves. Women in particular should be mindful of this aspect.

2. Differences in Abilities  
Men generally tend to have stronger reasoning abilities. This results in superior deductive and investigative strengths. In contrast, women's intellectual capacities might be inferior to men's in this regard. Therefore, subjects like mathematics or science may not play to women's strengths. Conversely, their memory tends to be keen, so they excel in memorization-heavy subjects such as literature, music, and arts.

3. Differences in Study Diligence  
Men overall seem to exhibit a lack of successful drive to study. Of course, there are diligent individuals among them, but in general, male students may appear less committed to their studies compared to female students. Among male students, there are those who seem to only attend school due to parental encouragement, or perhaps out of a desire to conform. Female students, despite the fact that among Korean families there aren’t yet as many parents eager to promote their education, show extraordinary determination and diligence that allow them to endure countless difficulties to attend school. Therefore, if they find themselves unable to attend due to illness or other unavoidable family circumstances, it is notably painful for them. Furthermore, female students tend to obey school regulations and teachers' orders, such as completing assignments or even performing classroom chores with greater obedience than male students. 

4. Differences in Clothing and Appearance  
The issue of student frivolity, especially among female students, seems to be increasing significantly. This has become a common sentiment expressed in society. Once both genders reach the age of approximately 20, they begin to become aware of the opposite sex, and it is natural for them to focus on their appearance and try to captivate the attention of others. Among them, young women particularly intensify their desire for beauty. Aesthetic life is something that defines women's existence. Women express beauty in various aspects. Thus, even if both boys and girls wear the same cotton or textile uniforms, women tend to insist on their clothing being of a modest but stylish fit. This inclination is particularly pronounced among young women. Therefore, even when wearing clothing made of the same material, they appear to be more extravagant than their male counterparts. 

While male students typically wear uniforms at school, which creates uniformity, the fabrics used for these uniforms are predominantly sourced from Japan, resulting in both Korean and Japanese students using similar ones. Although female students may also have similar uniforms according to their schools, they often wear traditional clothing with slight modifications, such as a white jeogori and black skirt, or entirely black attire, which may not be quite suitable as high-quality materials are hardly produced domestically. As a result, today's female students wear various types of fabrics. Though silk and similar materials are generally avoided, it seems that more students are starting to wear woolen skirts. Male students, despite wearing ragged and worn-out uniforms and trousers, appear indifferent and unconcerned about their appearance. In contrast, female students lack the confidence to wear ripped skirts or torn tights in public settings. In male secondary schools, students are typically not allowed to grow their hair long. However, in an attempt to evade school rules, many male students manage to maintain slightly longer hair and groom themselves obsessively. I have never seen anyone pay 20 jeon just to shave, yet I have observed many students lately going specifically for that purpose. Especially when students reach the fifth year before graduation, they tend to start letting their hair grow in preparation for the "high collar" style. 

Additionally troubling is that, particularly in summer, the tops of men's suits are now being worn shorter than women's jackets, resulting in their waists being exposed and their undergarments visible to the elements—a real degradation of propriety. Yet students seem unconcerned, believing that it aligns with the latest trends from Japan or the United States. Female students are banned from wearing makeup at school, but they frequently apply it privately, with many slipping on ring adornments only after returning home, and recently, it's quite common for female students to wear wristwatches. This is not driven by necessity but rather stems from vanity and extravagance. In short, young male students are increasingly focused on superficial appearances. Merchants opportunistically create and sell unnecessary goods that appeal to this vanity, further intensifying students' materialistic tendencies. As an educator, I am always vigilant about this frivolity problem. Yet it is not one that can be resolved solely within school; without proactive involvement from families and general societal oversight, it would be impossible to produce effective results. 

  
**Summary**  

The article explores the differences between male and female students based on various factors such as temperament, abilities, diligence, and appearances. It suggests that while boys may focus more on ideological pursuits, girls show a greater inclination toward emotional bonds and social relationships. Notably, gender-specific behaviors and societal expectations manifest in academic engagement and personal presentation. This analysis offers insight into the gender dynamics during Korea's colonial period, providing historians a contextual view of the cultural and educational climate amidst colonial influences.

曺在浩

남학생과 여학생은 학생이라는 점에 잇서서는 일치하지만 남녀라는 점에 잇서서 차이가 생기는 것이다.
그럼으로 남녀의 차별을 생리학적으로나 심리학적으로 그 근본 所由를 明瞭히 하면 남녀의 차이도 자연히 명백하여질줄 안다. 그러나 나는 그러한 이유에서 출발하야, 演繹的으로 또는 귀납적으로 남학생 여학생의 일치 또는 차이를 설명하고저 안이한다. 다만 표면에 나타나는 우리가 항용 목도하는 몃가지를 순서업시 드러보려 한다.
남녀학생을 물론하고 상급 하급을 물론하고 그네들의 생활은 공부문제, 사상문제, 연애문제, 취직문제, 이 네가지 범위를 버서나지 못하는 듯하다. 연령과 학년을 따라 그 중심 문제는 변동이 되는 듯하다. 중등정도 1, 2년급까지는 남녀를 물론하고 공부문제에 모든 생활이 집중되는 것갓다. 그러나 복잡한 이 세상의 모든 문제에 부다치여 경이와 의혹을 늦기기 시작하면서 거긔에 사상문제가 胚胎되며 연령이 자아내는 종족본능의 충동을 늣기기 시작하면서 거기에 연애문제가 가삼을 괴롭게 하는 모양이니,  그는 대개 중등정도 3, 4년기인 듯하다. 그러나 이 사상문제와 연애문제가 남녀학생의 생활을 충격하면서부터 학생생활의 중심문제에 남녀가 조곰식 색달나지는 듯하다. 남학생은 연애문제보담도 유아독존적 기분으로 사상문제에 더 흥미가 집중되는 듯하다. 그리하야, 이것이 점점 자라나서 자기를 생각하며 자기의 천직을 사색심구하야 문제는 차차 사업으로 변하야 가는 듯하다. 그러나 여학생은 사상문제보다도 천하미인적 기분(현대적 의미에 잇서서)으로 연애문제에 흥미가 더 집중되는 듯하다.＜39＞ 그리하야 이것이 점점 자라나서 자기를 생각하며 여자의 행로를 자각하게 되야 문제는 차차 가정으로 변하야 가는 듯하다. 이러한 경향이 현저히 발현되기는 중등정도의 상급이다. 남녀를 물론하고 자기의 前途를 좀 더 長遠하게 내다보는 학생들은 일시적으로 상급 학교의 입학문제에 모든 학생생활이 집중되지만은 實社會를 내다보는 학생들에게는 남자는 취직문제를 먼저 생각하게 되고 여자는 결혼문제를 먼저 몽상하는 듯하다. 물론 이것은 그 중심문제를 지적하엿슬 뿐이요, 다른 모든 문제를 부정하는 것은 결코 안이닊�. 이것이 내가 본 표면적 일반적 남녀학생의 경향이다.
그러나 특히 교원인 나로서 남녀학생의 학창생활을 삷혀 볼 때에 여러 가지 흥미 잇는 문제와 학생 자신과 학부형모제에게 알니고 십흔 점이 不少하다.
爲先 그네들의 교실을 엿보아 그 학습상태를 살펴보자. 여학생들은 모든 문제를 감정적으로 呑下的으로 말업시 감수한다. 교수자로서 불쾌한 기분을 띄우고 교수를 하면 그네들은 그대로 본 바다. 불쾌한 기색으로 선생의 기분을 엿보면서, 如履薄氷的 태도로 주는 그대로 짠지 쓴지 맛도 모르고 呑下하듯이 학습을 하게 된다.
물론 이러한 점은 남학생에게도 다소 잇슬는지 모르겟스나 여학생에게는 이것이 현저히 나타난다. 그러나 남학생들은 모든 문제를 그대로 말업시 선생의 기분을 엿보면서 呑下하지는 안이한다. 비판적으로 咀嚼하야 짜다 쓰다는 불평을 말하면서 감수한다. 선생이 제공하는 문제를 전면으로 후면으로 측면으로 그리고 외적으로 내적으로 두다리고 십흔대로 두다리여 그것을 확실히 이해한 후에 咀嚼하야 그 맛을 알려고 하는 태도가 여학생에 비하야 현저한 듯하다.
다음에 그네들의 舍宿생활을 엿보아 우정관계를 살펴보자. 여학생들은 동무를 사귀여도 애정에서 출발하야 사귀는 듯하다.  善友 損友로는 타산적 태도로 사귀거나 事情에 억매인 의리관계로 동모를 사귀는 일은 드믄 듯하다. 동무를 대할 때에 모든 이론을 초월하고 다못마음과 마음으로 제六감으로 무조건하고 朋友를 원수를 사귀는 듯하다. 그럼으로 여학생들의 우정관계는 가튼 학급일지라도 그 親不親을 제삼자로서 명료히 의식하게 된다. 그 친한 友誼를 보면 참으로 살이라도 비여 먹일 듯하고 생사를 반듯이 한가지할만한 친교라고 할 수 잇슬 것이다. 그러나 한번 사이가 틀니기만 하면 무슨 원수를 대하듯 한다. 대스럽지 안은 일로서 한업시 친한 동모도 될 수가 잇고 다시 업는 원수도 될 수가 잇다. 참으로 타기도 쉽지만은 식기도 쉬운 우정관계라고 할 수 잇는 것이다.
그러나 남학생들은 그러치 안이하다. 그럼으로 진실로 친한 붕우간이지만은 제삼자가 볼 때 아조 당초부터 사귀여보지도 못한 사히 가티 보히는 편이 적지 안타. 그것은 아마 哀心으로 相信相依하는 데서 울어 나오는 태도인 듯하다. 그럼으로 여간하야 동모에게 자기의 정체를 보이지 안는다. 따라서 일편으로 누구에나 친한 듯 하지만은 또 일편으로는＜40＞ 누구와도 친하지 안이한 것가티도 보이기도 한다.
우에 말한 학습태도와 우정관계에서 다시 출발하야 남녀학생들의 취직관, 연애관에 들어가서 그 경향을 통찰할 때에 교육자뿐만 아니라 여러 가지 방면에 만흔 참고가 될 뜻하다.

남학생 여학생 네가지 차이
張膺震

남녀가 성질상 체질상, 기능상, 기타 종종의 점에 대하야, 여러 가지의 달은 점이 잇는 것은 가정에서 자녀를 길너보신 부모들의 다 가티 짐작하는 바이지만은 특히 남성만을 모아 노흔 남학교와 여성만을 모하 노흔 여학교에서 양성을 대조하야 볼 때에는 그 차이가 더욱 현저함을 보겟슴니다. 無論 개인 개인을 떼여노코 보면 남녀를 물론하고 개인마다 제각기 특수한 개성이 잇는 것은 말할 것도 업지만은 일반적으로 관찰한 남녀학생의 차이점을 멧가지 말하랴고 함니다.
