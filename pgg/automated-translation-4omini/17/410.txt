The name "Snake Dragon" is a type of Korean folk music. The "Snake Spear" refers to a weapon, which is mentioned in the Book of Jin where Chen An held an eight-foot Snake Spear in his right hand. Additionally, during the Three Kingdoms period, Zhang Fei also wielded an eight-foot Snake Spear. The term "Snake Doctor" is another name for the "guardian lizard," which is a dialect from the southern region. "Snake Rhyolite" is a type of mineral formed from the metamorphosis of olivine and amphibole, named because its patterns resemble snake skin. (Also known as 温石) The "Snake Mother" refers to a type of reptile, belonging to the same class but a different genus compared to lizards. "Snake Grape" is a perennial vine. There is a plant called "Snake Containing Grass," colloquially known as "Small Dragon Tooth," which is also referred to as "Purple-backed Dragon Tooth." It is said that it possesses medicinal properties; in ancient times, when a farmer witnessed a snake that had been injured, another snake brought this plant to treat the wound, and upon application, the snake healed immediately. Thus, the farmer began to use this plant on humans, leading to its name "Snake Containing Grass." "Snake Belly Pattern" refers to a specific pattern found on ancient zithers; there is an ancient poem mentioning the "Snake Belly Pattern" on a zither. (Also known as 蛇蚹) "Snake Yellow" refers to clay that snakes use to build their nests in winter, which is used as medicine. Additionally, the term "Snake Yellow" can also refer to the yellow substance found in snake bellies mixed with cow bile, which is used in medicine. "Snake Molt" refers to the shedding of skin, which is also used in medicine. In historical records, during the Jin dynasty, a pheasant that accidentally stumbled upon a snake's skin was reportedly observed by the naturalist Zhang Hua, who stated it was surely evidence of a snake's transformation because the molt was found in the place where the pheasants gathered. (According to the Book of Jin) "Snake Horn" is a name for a treasure, explained in the Commentaries on the Classic of Mountains and Seas to be "Green Rhinoceros." "Snake Berry" is a type of perennial herb, specifically a variety of bramble (commonly referred to as Snake Berry) and "Snake Bed Fruit" is also a perennial herb used in medicine. "Snake Venom" refers to the venom of the viper, the "Snake Venom Blister" refers to sores caused by contact with snake venom (commonly referred to as "stone sore"). There are also many people named with "Snake," such as "Snake Fortune," a monk from Silla (who lived during the time of Won Hyo), and there are numerous place names like "Snake Ridge," "Snake Mountain," and "Snake Island," but only a few are summarized here.  

7. Overview of Historical Records of Snakes  
The victorious snake is a dragon type capable of summoning clouds and mists, while the python is referred to as the king snake, the largest and strongest among snakes (Erya). The "Bass Snake" can swallow an elephant and will not excrete for three years before releasing its bulk (Shan Hai Jing). The viper is said to be most venomous in autumn; when its poison is released, plants perish instantly (Records of Natural History). It is said in Sunzi that when struck at the head, the tail comes first, and vice versa; thus, it is said the body of the snake can respond depending on which part is targeted. Zhuangzi tells of a centipede (지네) that boasts it moves with many legs, but the snake with no legs is not to be outdone. When faced with a snake, a centipede can reportedly kill it with its breath (Baopuzi). In the land of Fuyu, where snakes abound, the Yellow Emperor had to put on the "male yellow" to ward off snakes (same text). "Year of the Snake" is referred to in the poems of Li Shangyin as "In the year of the snake, in the month of Wuyin, I return from Liang to Qin." In the Zuo Tradition, there is mention of "Inner Snakes and Outer Snakes" battling in Zhengnan, referring to snake spirits. Sun Shuao in his youth killed a two-headed snake, and Emperor Gaozu of Han beheaded a giant snake during a midnight ceremony at the Great Marsh, leading to old spirits lamenting, stating: "My son is the child of the White Emperor, yet the child of the Red Emperor was slain." (Records of History) The Sui emperor saved a wounded snake, and later this same snake brought fine silk to him, transforming into one inch in diameter with splendid luminosity, becoming a precious object likened to the bright moon and named "Sui Pearl" or "Bright Moon Pearl." In verses by Liu Yuxi it notes: "I hold the snake pearl, and the white house, which shows itself." Emperor Taizong of Tang, while on a military expedition, was awakened from sleep by a snake that chased a rat, thus avoiding danger from enemy troops and drawing attention miraculously. The Xia Yu clan dug through Longmen Mountain, where a deity with a human body and snake head appeared, presenting a golden plate with the Eight Trigrams inscribed, giving him jade slips to measure time and space, which the deity named "Fuxi." In the chronicles of Du Yu and Zhou Xun, there are accounts of transformations into snakes during drunken dreams. Cai Jun Tan of Eastern Qi transformed into a snake in his illness and was thus named the Snake Deity (нефрит). Unnecessary actions in the proverb "Drawing a Snake and Adding Feet" suggest false compassion harming others, and in the Zuo Tradition, it is said "Feasting sheep to bury snakes are words of survival for the country." Moving onwards, a commentary by Li Bai notes "The Great Marsh is filled with dragons and snakes." In lines by Chen Hou Shan, "The mountains resound with squawking larks." In lines by Su Dongpo, "Thunder captures yellow snakes." Liu Zongyuan mentions "The poems of capturing snakes" and so forth. Furthermore, many people may find the snake's poison in the Christian scriptures oddly noteworthy and surreal; therefore, I will summarize briefly. "Poems indicate that both the snake and viper are auspicious omens for women, and in the Book of Changes, it states that the serpent-like creatures sustain life." In the writings of Yu Xin to Emperor Zhou Ming, it mentions "The Snake of the Han River delivers its treasures, a perfect pearl." In "Samdonggye," it mentions that males do not dwell alone, and females do not live in isolation, intertwining like the Black Tortoise and snake. In "Zhunanzhi," it notes that the mystical snake sings in the skies and the creature’s essence transforms beings into power. "In the rites, it is noted that sages liken dragons to men of stature and regard snakes as gentlemen." Additionally, in Zhuangzi, it mentions "There is no praise nor blame; preposterous both dragon and viper are the same." Lastly, in letters exchanged between Cao Zhi and Yang Dezu, they stated, "Everyone believes they have grasped the precious beaded snake." In "Dongmingji," it writes of "Chisongzi who took three years to consume phoenix and return to water riding a yellow snake, obtaining a pearl of golden hue." Thus, it is told: "The divine snake rides the heavens." Furthermore, in the Classic of Mountains and Seas, it states: "In the east, there are birds dancing, all riding on the snakes." In the correspondence of friends, it is noted: "General Li Shen was initially impoverished, journeyed to the heavenly palace of Tian Gong and napped on the steps; an old monk observed a black snake rising to devour the respected Li tree, cautious about the residual poison affecting people." Slowly, he descended, but the snake, looking east, entered his bosom and suddenly disappeared. Upon waking, the public was roused, leading the old monk to question whether there was indeed anything seen in a dream. The public remarked, "It seems that in dreams, they climbed a Li tree before encountering a monk." In the "Miscellaneous Records of Du Yang," it states: "At the beginning of Gyeongseong, a golden snake appeared from the treasury and wandered the courtyard; a palace servant raised coral pearls and struck the snake; consequently, the pearls were lost, but later, the keeper searched the treasury thoroughly finding both the golden snake and the coral pearls." In the "Book of the Later Han," there’s a note on a jade weapon being born alongside a snake; the snake fled into the forest, and upon the mother's death during a funeral, the great snake approached, struck the coffin, and as tears of blood flowed, its posture reflected sorrow similar to mourning. After a while, it departed, leading people to call it a herald of fortune. In Erya, it mentions that the body of the serpent follows the sun’s movement, adjusting by the month. Southern customs dictate harvesting its gallbladder for medicinal materials and stitching up the wound to avoid meeting the snares. When encountering hunters, the gallbladder’s absence would be discovered upon viewing the site; due to its passionate nature, those who capture it tend to refer to it with affectionate names like "Red Young Woman," while others cover it with a woman's underskirt to ensure it lays still, thus avoiding capture. It is noted that a subtle “feeling” emerged, later, a white-clad youth descended from his horse, expressed thanks, and welcomed together with honored gifts, but refused, instead taking thirty prescriptions from Longguang. (In poetry, it states: "Henceforth, up to here is a submission by An Jongwon.")  

8. Emergency Treatment for Snake Bites  
Before discussing emergency procedures for snake bites, let me first mention some prevention methods. Snakes prefer clean air and despise warm conditions; thus, above all else, keeping houses dry is crucial and avoiding the use of Stibnite or Chuanxiong is necessary. In ancient times, when the Emperor traveled through areas abundant in snakes, he would heed Guochengzi's advice, donning Stibnite. In Korea, mothers often carry Stibnite around their children’s heads, whereas rural farmers plant Chuanxiong near storage vessels while avoiding centipedes (지네) and cobras. Consequently, when the unfortunate event of a snake bite occurs, quickly tie off the bite site with string or hair (like from the leg) to prevent the poison from circulating into the bloodstream. Urinating on the bite can also help. The wound should be cut open to extract poison, which is certainly better, and suctioning with the mouth is also acceptable; the venom is a type of protein, which, when in contact with stomach or intestinal fluids, becomes harmless. Therefore, suctioning orally is permissible. Following that, it is necessary to consult a physician for admission and treatment. Among common remedies, the herb Yangwa (소루장이), applied as poultice, has been shown to be highly effective, as I personally experienced when bitten by a poisonous snake in my youth. During a critical moment, a monk from Geumgangsan instructed me in using this remedy, and its effects were immediate; moreover, others have tested it repeatedly with successful outcomes. Furthermore, utilizing Xixin (집신나물) also shows special efficacy. However, because I am not a specialist, I speak only from personal experience.  

9. The Thriving Retail Arena  
The manager of this retail arena is likely the most suitable person either in competence or trustworthiness as a "land sharer" (깍적이) in this matter. Regarding land sharers, I have previously announced findings regarding their digging practices in this magazine, so here I will briefly introduce some insights into the trade of snakes. The primary snakes caught by these land sharers are typically pit vipers (蝮蛇), golden snakes (金蛇), and black snakes (烏蛇). The pit viper generally sells for about 50 cents wholesale and between 1 and 2.50 won retail. The golden snake wholesales for one won and retails for 2 to 4-5 won; black snakes, which are among the finest, wholesales for 2 won and retails for 5-6 won. The white snake commands the highest price, with retail prices reaching 30-40 won. The peak time for snake catching is during May, June, September, and October, while they are less commonly caught in July and August. The land sharers utilize the seasons to their advantage since the pit vipers appear to migrate during such times. In China, the prime location for snake hunting is Guangdong, where it is customarily used in local cuisine; snake medicine is frequently sold in city markets. In Japan, Shiga and Ehime prefectures are known as the richest in catches. Annually, it’s reported approximately 300,000 striped snakes, 200,000 pit vipers, and 2,000 bird snakes are captured. Typically, a snake catcher earns about 4-5 won daily, with Korea producing about 200-300,000 snakes yearly, primarily for export to Japan and China.  

10. Entertaining Activities that Will Make Everyone Laugh  
As for the entertainment at this fair, one might expect to witness snake charming performances from Indian or Southern Pacific Islanders, easy to think in that direction. However, I will refrain from that as it seems rather dull. Instead, I will convey a humorous story related to snakes before concluding. There once was a member of the Yi royal family known as Po-seong-gyeong, who was enamored with a woman and relied on her every word as divine, cherishing even her flatulence as if it were good-smelling incense. One day, he heard the tale of a fragrant gisaeng (기생) in Namwon, Jeolla Province, and journeyed there, spending time delightfully with the woman. When it was time to return to Seoul, the gisaeng couldn't bear to part and sobbed as she held his hand tightly. Seeing his excessive display of emotion, the gisaeng cunningly remarked, "I will transmogrify into a snake and return to you," to which he completely believed. This man then firmly grasped her hands in anticipation of her promise. At that time, Pastor Jeong Hee-hyun from Gongju viewed her act as comical; when Yi arrived in Gongju, he played a prank by capturing a large snake and placing it before him, leaving just its tail exposed. After a short while, Pastor Jeong feigned surprise, directing Yi's attention to the snake's tail, exclaiming, "What is this?" Yi observed the tail and, assuming it was the gisaeng who had died and transformed into a snake, immediately burst into tears. "Alas, this is so pitiable! It’s my fault you died!" he lamented. "How could you know such sorrow? You, noble gisaeng, are the most virtuous woman in the world!" He wept loudly for a long time, ultimately tossing his attire aside to wrap the snake properly, proceeding to bury it near an inn, and even performed a ceremonial act, erecting a monument. Ultimately, the situation became the talk of the town and was shared throughout Gyeongseong.

8. 蛇咬의 應急治療室
뱀에 물인 응급치료법을 말하기 전에 먼저 驅除法을 몃가지 말하겟다. 뱀은 潔氣를 조와하고 陽氣를 시려하는 까닭에 무엇보다도 집안을 燥강하게 할 것은 물론이고 石雄黃이나 川芎을 忌하는 까닭에 녯적에 皇帝氏가 負丘(多蛇之地)를 갈때에도 廣成子의 말을 좃차서 雄黃을 차고 갓섯고 朝鮮에서도 자래로 부인 소아들이 당긔나 頭盖에 石雄黃을 달고 다니며 시골 농가에서는 川芎을 장독간에다 심으고 또 댓진(烟竹진)과 蜈蚣(지네)을 忌함으로 농군이 풀밧에 갈때에는 그것을 바로고 가긔도 한다.그리고 不幸히 뱀에게 물인 때에는 急急히 피해처를 노끈이나 머리털(다리꼭지)가튼 것으로 잔득 매여 毒液이 血液에 流通되지 안토록 하고 뱀이 사람에게 붓터서 떠러지지 안커던 더운 소변을 찌언지면 즉시 떠러진다. 피해처는 즉시 절개하고 毒血을 뽑아내는 것이 조흔 것은 물론이고 입으로 빨어내도 관계치 안으니 그것은 蛇의 毒液은 일종의 白質로서 胃液이나 腸液을 만나면 消和되야 無害物로 되는 고로 입으로 흡수하야도 무방한 所以다. 그 다음에는 醫師를 차저서 入院治療를 할 것은 물론이어니와 常藥中으로는 羊渧菜(소루장이)로 浮黃찜질(항아리에 소루장이를 두드려 늣코 보통 부황찜질하덧 함)즉일 神效가 잇다. 이것은 필자 자신이 어렷을 때에 독사에 물니여서 가장 急險한 시기에까지 이르럿는대 금강산 노승이 와서 그 약을 가르처줌으로 시험한 즉, 즉시 神效가 잇섯고 또 다른 사람도 여러번 試用하야 다 得效한 것이다. 또 細辛(집신나물)을 두드려븟처도 특효가 잇다.그러나 나는 전문가가 안인 까닭에 그저 경험상으로만 말할 뿐이다.

9. 天下大繁昌의 賣店
이 賣店의 주임은 러력으로 보나 신용으로 보나 아마 『깍적이=땅꾼』이 제일 적임자일 것이다. 땅꾼에 관한 이약이는 언제인가 이 주최자가 그 掘穴을 탐사하야 본지에 자서이 발표하얏기로 略하고 여긔에는 뱀의 賣買하는 이약이를 잠간 소개하랴 한다. 이 땅꾼들의 잡는 뱀은 주로 蝮蛇, 金蛇, 烏蛇 등인데 蝮蛇는 도매로 대개 50전 소매로 일원 내지 2원50전이고 金蛇는 도매 1원, 소매 2원 내지 4,5원이고 烏蛇는 蛇 중 색태가 제일 조흠으로 도매 2원 소매 5,6원이고 白蛇는 最貴한 자로서 소매 3,40원까지 간다고 한다.(작년 표준) 뱀의 제일 잘 잽히는 때는 5, 6, 월 9, 10월이오. 7, 8월은 잘 잽히지 안는다 한다. 蝮蛇는 5, 6월과 8, 9월이 사람으로 치면 무슨 대회시기와 가티 정기로 모혀드는 일이 잇기 때문에 『땅꾼』들은 그때를 이용하야 蝮蛇 산양을 한다. 중국에서 뱀이 맨키로는 廣東이 제일인데 그곳에서는 자래로 뱀을 식용에 쓰는 고로 시가에서 蛇藥를 파는 곳이 종종 잇고 일본은 滋賀, 愛媛兩縣이 제일 만흔데 전국에 일년에 잡는 것이 縞蛇가 약 30만匹 蝮蛇가 20만필, 鳥蛇가 2천필인데 뱀잡이꾼의 일일 평균 수입이 약 4,5원 된다 하며 조선은 경기가 제일 만코 其次는 충청, 전라인데 輸出數는 자섯치 못하나 약 2,30만匹이 되고 輸出地는 일본 及 중국이라 한다.

十. 萬人必笑의 餘興場
이 박람회의 여흥이라 하면 누구나 얼풋 생각하기를 印度 사람이나 南洋土人의 뱀노리를 소개하는 것으로 알기 쉬울 것이다. 그러나 그것은 이약이만 하기에도 너무 질글징글 하닛가 그만두고 그대신으로 뱀에 관한 우서운 이약이를 한마듸하고 폐회하랴 한다. 전일 李氏皇族 중에 坡城令이란 이가 한 분 잇섯는데 그는 엇지나 여자에게 況惑하얏던지 여자의 말이라면 그야말로 天神의 말과 가티 절대로 신용하고 방귀라도 여자의 방귀하면 향내로 녁이는 이엿다. 한번은 그가 전라도 남원이 자래 색향이라는 말을 듯고 놀너 갓섯는데 엇던 기생과 가티 數朔을 정답게 지내다가 서울로 다시 도라오게 되매 기생과 참아 떠러지지를 못하고 손으로 잡고 울며 불며 하니 그 기생은 그의 태도가 너무도 과도한 것을 보고 그짓 울며 속혀 갈오되 당신께서 저를 그와 가티 사랑하시니 제가 죽어 구렁이가 되어서라도 당신을 다시 차저 가겟다고 하니 그가 그 기생의 말이 진담인 줄만 알고 꼭 고지 들엇섯다. 그때는 공주 목사 鄭希賢이라 하는 이가 그 이약이를 듯고 마음에 항상 그 坡城令의 所爲를 鼻笑하던 바, 한번은 그 李氏가 마츰 공주에 오게 되매 鄭목사는 그를 한번 속히랴고 큰 구렁이를 한 마리 잡어다가 자리밋해다 느어두고 다만 꽁지만 조금 내밀게 한 다음에 그를 그 자리로 안지엿다. 조금 잇다가 목사는 시침이를 뚝 깨고 李씨를 향하야 그 구렁이의 꽁지를 가르치며 익크- 이것이 웬일이야 하고 그짓 놀내는 체하니 李씨가 그 구렁이의 꽁지를 보고서는 전일의 남원 기생이 죽어서 구렁이가 되여 온 줄만 꼭 알고 그만 두 눈에서 눈물이 비오듯이 뚝뚝 떠러지며 하는 말이 『아이구 불상해라. 나 때문에 네가 죽엇구나.』 네가 그리 느� 줄 엇지 알엇단 말이냐. 아모리 기생이라도 너가튼 기생은 천하에 열녀요. 信이 잇는 여자로구나.』하고 한참동안을 통곡을 하다가 자기의 속옷을 버서서 구렁이를 잘 싸가지고 客舍 근처에다 장사를 지낸 다음에 또 제를 지내고 碑까지 해 세우니 일반이 다 웃고 한참동안 京鄕에 이약이거리가 되엿섯다. 여러분! 엇덥심닛가. 여러분도 기생의 말을 잘못 드르시다가는 이 李씨 가티 죽은 구렁이를 보고 울으시게 되기 쉬을 것이니. 주의하십시요.쉬...(폐회)＜159＞
