The Famous Courtesan Hwang Jini  
						Han Yong-un  
When she explained her situation to her father and rushed out in simple clothing with no makeup, embracing the dead man’s body, only then did the corpse fall to the ground, and they were able to conduct the funeral safely. From that day on, Hwang Jini swore an oath to her father that she would become a courtesan. Originally, she was a beauty of extraordinary talent; in less than six months, she mastered singing, dancing, and playing various instruments, earning a reputation in Joseon that spread even to the Ming dynasty. Whenever envoys came from Ming, the wealthy and cultured men sought news of Jini above all else. Her beauty and skills are difficult to convey by someone who is a novice in the female world, but the reason she became a courtesan speaks volumes about her purity and nobility. Truly, she transcended all worldly desires. To relate it to our Buddhist philosophy, one might say she attained realization through meditation. 

Even while she practiced as a courtesan, she did not fall prey to greed for money or succumb to power, but rather showcased her individuality and lived her life to the fullest. The fact that she often associated with scholars like Seo Hwajeok and traveled alone to renowned places such as Geumgangsan (Geumgang Mountain) or Jirisan (Jiri Mountain) gives us insight into the depth of her character. It is also natural that Lim Baek-ho, a poet and knight of Joseon, wrote a memorial for Hwang Jini at her grave. (The End)  

**Summary**  

This article recounts the life and character of the famous courtesan Hwang Jini, emphasizing her beauty, talent, and noble spirit, as well as her commitment to her individuality despite the societal constraints of her profession. Her association with scholars and her travels to famous landmarks reflect her elevated character. Historians may find this narrative insightful as it highlights the complexities of women's roles in Korean society during the colonial period and challenges the stereotypical views of courtesans as merely entertainers.

天下名妓黃眞伊
						韓龍雲
자기 부친에게 그 사정을 말하고 素服淡粧으로 뛰여 나가 그 남자의 시체를 꿀어 안고 얼우만즈니 그제야 시체가 땅에 떠러저서 무사히 장례를 하고 그날부터 黃眞伊는 다시 부친에게 죽기를 맹서하야 기생이 되엿섯다. 그는 원래에 인물이 절색이고 天才가 비범한 까닭에 불과 엿달에 노래와 춤이며 기타 음악을 능통하니 香名에 朝鮮에만 선전할 뿐 안이라 멀니 明나라에까지 뚤치여서 明나라의 사신이 올때이면 여간의 풍류객들은 무엇보다도 眞伊의 소식을 먼저 뭇게 되엿다. 그의 인물과 재능은 여자계에 문외한인 내가 별로 이럿타고 말하기 어려우나 그가 기생된 동기로 말하면 그 얼마나 순정하고 고결한냐. 참으로 인간의 일절 진념을 해탈하얏다. 가위하겟다. 우리 佛家로 말하면 참선에 달하얏다 할 것이다. 그가 기생 노릇을 할 때에도 다른 기생과 가티 금전에 팔이거나 세력에 부쫏치 안코 자기의 개성을 그대로 잘 발휘하야 일생을 終하얏다. 그가 일개 기생으로 徐花澤과 가튼 道學者와 항상 추축을 하고 혹은 금강산 혹은 지리산 가튼 명승지를 단신으로 유람한 것을 보면 그의 인격이 얼마나 고결한한 것을 짐작할 수 잇다. 朝鮮의 시인이오 俠士인 林白湖가 黃眞伊의 묘소에 가서 祭文을 지어 吊상한 것도 또한 당연한 일일것다. (끗)＜10＞
