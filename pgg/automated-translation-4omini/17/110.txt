The Way of the Couple  
						Cho Jaehwa  
A person who goes against the path of humanity will always experience the emptiness of life. The life situation of old maids and old bachelors. 

When we consider their feelings, there is certainly much to sympathize with. They seem to be akin to a traveler wandering alone through a desert that has gradually turned into twilight. No matter how far they go, it remains a desert. They will walk all day only to find their companion is merely their own shadow. Although the sorrowful tears of old maids and old bachelors may indeed exist, they will undoubtedly experience the greatest void that nature provides for those who go against humanity's great course and reverse the path of human existence. An old saying states, "The union of two surnames is the source of all things," suggesting that the meaningful union between men and women is the grand path of human existence. Therefore, while it is common to speak of love as supreme, poet Browning expresses in his sentiment "Love is best" through the conclusion of "Love among the Ruins," and thus we are reminded of this. Truly, for those who have never tasted love or have never laid down in love's cradle, life will only be lonely, whether awake or asleep. 

Lord Avebury has stated, "Love is the light and sunshine of life." Indeed, it is not an exaggeration to say that love is the glory of human life. Experiencing this "love" is said to begin with mutual attraction between the sexes. When regulating the physiological or psychological development of a person's body and mind, it is argued that once all conditions necessary for the preservation of one's individual life have developed, the conditions necessary for the survival of the species will begin to flourish. Children and youth are inherently egocentric and self-centered, living freely according to their own desires, but in their youthful phase, especially in physical maturity, sexual differences become discernible, and socially conscious and awareness of others begin to emerge. Those in their youthful phase, looking for the source of all blessings, begin to awaken to the existence of others and start to learn cooperation and integration within society; it is said that this all begins with the love of the opposite sex. Thus, saying that the union of two surnames is the source of all things is not surprising.

Men and women are born with the fundamental condition of mutual attraction and mutual assistance. Therefore, viewing sexual desire and appetite as the threads that weave life together, one can argue that all activities and conflicts commence from that. How can a person live as an old maid or old bachelor? With the union of the halves—man and woman—there is love, marriage, and the formation of a couple. Therefore, the youth, while diligently discovering and cultivating their vocation, also dreams of finding their soulmate and establishing an ideal couple and family, which is indeed a significant matter for humanity. Although there may be exceptions like Herbert Spencer who meets his soulmate through friendship, it is impossible not to sympathize deeply with the heart of those who have crossed from Asia to Europe in search of love. 

It may be an exaggerated expression, but it is not an overstatement to say that "to live is to love." Thus, the youth actively search for their soulmate, but truly joining with their soulmate to become an ideal couple is to extol love as supreme, and marriage as the cornerstone of life, just as many praise it. However, even if they have united as an ideal couple, how many truly practice a genuine couple's life when they incorrectly manage family life and find themselves longing day by day? Therefore, I would like to reflect on some points that both parties should be aware of in the journey toward becoming a couple, and the duties they share when establishing family life.

Romance is not simply pleasure, a pastime, or even amusement. The quest for an ideal partner should be anchored in a heartfelt engagement between the most genuine personalities. It is true that romance is hardly simply sweet. At times, it can be bitter and painful. When recalling or recounting romance, it may appear blissful and nostalgic, but the actual experience of romance can be quite burdensome and bitter. Therefore, those who treat romance as mere pleasure should never envision marriage, as they are driven by transient desires, and such individuals are unsuitable in society. Persons engaged in extramarital affairs or those who “open the back door” to other partners will surely shed tears of emptiness.

However, even among those who sincerely engage in romance, many derive unhappiness from marriage and deviate from the paths of couplehood. While various circumstances can account for this, it often stems from a failure to establish clear views on romance, marriage, and couplehood. Thus, I advise passionately loving couples to uphold these counsel. 

I believe one should start from the fervent desire of, “If I have that person in my life, I will focus my life’s work no matter if I am single,” and approach with a calm mind and discerning perspective to analyze their partner’s compatibility in terms of intellect, character, and appearance. They should evaluate health and the presence of family members, as well as the respective personalities and backgrounds to understand the living environment. After analyzing past relationship experiences and social standing, they should ultimately consider their partner's attractiveness and economic capability (whether they have wealth or activity level). 

Only when compatibility is established should the heart consent, allowing harmony to develop and nourish the relationship. This I believe to be true romance. After undergoing such a relationship, rigorously comparing each other's suitability before engagement, and subsequently progressing towards marriage when financial independence is within reach, becomes a truly beautiful matter among humans. Such a marriage is undoubtedly the cornerstone of life. Couples formed on the basis of such union may cultivate an ever-refreshing and highly cherished relationship. As Lord Avebury said, "Love, true love, grows and deepens with time." 

However, even among couples formed in this manner, if they mismanage the steering of their lives, both being emotional beings, will inevitably grapple with marital monotony and, if not careful, may yield to new attractions, thus potentially triggering crises in what should be a beautiful life. Therefore, there are several matters that couples should be mindful of, such as (1) frequently revisiting their first impressions and opportunities to recall romantic memories from their relationship. Just as consuming three meals a day is necessary for maintaining physical health, recalling the days of love with gratitude should be seen as the source of harmony among couples.

In this respect, collections such as photographs and letters from the early days of romance that remain until the birth of their children can become materials for reigniting the love that might fade away. (2) Next, careful attention should be paid to each other’s living conditions and language, ensuring mutual respect. Furthermore, the husband should maintain a sincere and trustworthy demeanor towards the wife, while the wife must possess the capability and demeanor needed to weather any storm that may arise in managing all familial responsibilities. 

(3) In addition, the husband should be able to appreciate and praise his wife’s unique efforts, while the wife must be meticulous in preparing everything before her husband speaks. To put it differently, if a wife who usually styles her hair beautifully appears unkempt, he should refrain from mentioning her shortcomings and instead commend her good aspects. When the husband unpacks a bottle of cigarettes, the wife should be ready with matches. 

If even these three elements exist, the couple can continuously cultivate a fresh mindset like a spring breeze and establish a heavenly home, thus creating the driving force for all their endeavors.

**Summary**

This essay discusses the importance of love, marriage, and mutual respect in relationships, while also warning of the emptiness experienced by those who avoid these experiences, particularly old maids and bachelors. It emphasizes the natural inclination between men and women toward coupling as essential for personal and societal well-being. From a historical perspective, this article reflects the societal views on marriage in the context of the 1920s Korea, offering insights into gender roles and expectations influenced by colonial society.
