기사제목	男女結婚年齡, 男女 몃살때의 結婚이 適合한가 (The Marriage Age of Men and Women, When is the Suitable Age for Marriage?)  
필자	醫學博士 李甲秀 (Medical Doctor Lee Gapsu)  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

The institution of marriage that arises from the act of wedding is described as an industrial cooperative that Humankind creates. Therefore, a couple who marries after the male has fully matured in sexual development as a man and the female has fully matured as a woman will be able to achieve their instinctive goals satisfactorily. Consequently, complete physical development is required for marriage, and suitable ages are necessary for complete physical growth. In other words, it is extremely legitimate for men and women to marry during their mature phase of adulthood, and this will align with the natural law of humanity.

In Korea, the evil custom of child marriage has become prevalent, leading to severe cases where even children under the age of 10, who cannot yet distinguish between right and wrong, are forced into marriages that merely serve as a formality, or young boys around 10 years old are matched with women aged 17 or 18 who have reached maturity, thereby wasting the youth of women. This situation creates an indescribable human tragedy. The harms resulting from child marriage are not limited to this alone; there are many other significant repercussions. Firstly, it is extremely harmful to the health of the individuals involved. For instance, planting a seed that has not yet matured would not yield high-quality fruit. It is no different than the fact that, before budding, the seed may rot; and even if it does sprout, it will not grow into a perfect plant. 

Furthermore, the offspring born from these unions are often weak and run the risk of various ailments, posing a threat to the betterment of humanity. Thus, in ancient Greece, laws were established to ensure that men were at least 35 to 40 years old and women at least 30 years old before marrying, which resulted in the production of healthy citizens. Similarly, in Germany, laws prohibited marriage for men younger than 25 and women younger than 22 to prevent child marriages. As a result, these nations produced strong and superior populations. Moreover, in Europe, the age at which individuals actually marry is, contrary to our situation, often ten years older than the legal marrying age. This is due to the evolved sense of hygiene and the general spirit of the populace in those countries, leading to a consensus that the time for child marriage has passed, with couples now waiting for the optimal phase of fertility, which occurs during adulthood. 

To provide statistics on marriage ages in Europe, in the United Kingdom, the average age for men is 26 and for women 25; in France, it is 28 for men and 25 for women; in Norway, it is 28 for men and 26 for women; in the Netherlands, it is 29 for men and 28 for women; and in Belgium, it is 30 for men and 28 for women. The averages across these countries indicate that men marry around 28 years and women around 26 years. This suggests that the suitable marital age for men could be around 28, whereas for women, from a physiological perspective, marrying later could be inadvisable. 

So, which age is most appropriate for marriage? In my humble opinion, the age range when individuals are most capable in life is from about 20 to 38 for women and from 28 to 48 for men. Therefore, we should seek to marry within this timeframe for the best outcomes. Since women generally develop physically and sexually more rapidly than men, it is natural that there will be an age difference between spouses. 

Another issue that arises is how large this age difference should be to remain suitable. Various scholars suggest that women should be, on average, 6 to 10 years younger than men, with 8 years being the most appropriate age gap. However, couples who marry later tend to have a greater age difference, while those who marry early should have a smaller difference. This can be clearly understood by examining the marriage age compatibility chart mentioned earlier. Additionally, referring to a marriage compatibility chart by Dr. Kawai introduced in Osaka Asahi Shimbun, it's quite suitable, though in comparison, it seems that the female age is slightly delayed relative to the male age. 

What pairing would be most advantageous? Combinations like a 28-year-old man and a 20-year-old woman, a 29-year-old man and a 21-year-old woman, or a 30-year-old man and a 23-year-old woman would be the most rational. In simpler terms, men aged 28 to 30 and women aged 20 to 22 would be the most suitable age for marriage. It would ensure that one-spouse-one-partner marriage laws are fulfilled satisfactorily; besides, the likelihood of infertility in the first pregnancy would be incredibly low, leading to higher pregnancy rates, and it would also mitigate the complications often associated with pregnancies in very young and older first-time mothers. 

In conclusion, marrying earlier than the discussed age can be deemed relatively early marriage, whereas marrying later is considered relatively late marriage. Therefore, we should strive to find a partner and engage in rational marriage within this timeframe.

**Summary**  

This article discusses the appropriate ages for marriage, emphasizing the necessity of both physical maturity and health for both men and women before entering into marriage. It critiques the practice of child marriage in Korea, contrasting it with the legal restrictions placed in Western countries that favor later marriages. This perspective reveals the evolving societal norms and health considerations surrounding marriage in Korea during the colonial era, highlighting a tension between tradition and the need for reform in marriage customs. 

Historians may find it interesting how the article reflects early twentieth-century views on marriage and health, illustrating the influence of Western ideologies on Korean social practices, and points to the broader context of colonial modernity shaping these discussions.
