Big fights and little fights, the secrets to reconcile spousal quarrels, and the disclosure of secrets by notable figures

The time to reflect

Kim Tak-won, Hanseong Hospital

Arguments often arise from trivial matters, and even more so between couples - these quarrels are typically based on a desire to maintain harmony. The close relationship stemming from mutual affection makes it such that the questions of what's right and wrong often surface. In such cases, if one party feels they are in the right while the other is in the wrong, the feelings in between can become exaggerated. If, however, both partners spend some time apart—whether on a trip or otherwise—allowing time for reflection, it could prevent significant resentment from building. Naturally, feelings would cool off, and after some time apart, tentative conversation can resume. However, it's not uncommon for the responses to be a bit sharp initially, but that too is part of the process!

Indecision

Jin Jang-seop

When it comes to arguments between couples, it's a different matter than disputes regarding personal preferences, and the quarrels I've encountered in my marriage are usually based on trivial issues. I'm not necessarily a heavy drinker, but as a lover of alcohol (I can’t normally drink excessively), if I return home late or fail to uphold a promise to be home by a specific time, this becomes the centerpiece of conflict. However, there’s a tendency for neither party to be particularly gentle. Just as the chaos of European wars escalates due to misunderstandings, arguments often escalate without a formal declaration of war but can also end without a formal peace treaty quite easily. Those small, silly disputes often seem like minor squabbles at first yet, if they persist, they become pronounced issues requiring resolution. 

With children in mind

Bang In-geun

Life is filled with peculiarities. To suggest that others disclose their methods for reconciliation during spousal fights seems quite peculiar. If the wife experiences a revelation after learning a method and then says, “You did such and such,” and from that point on, the husband cannot rely on that method anymore, what then? It would certainly be brave to take on the responsibility of keeping the magazine from my wife, but if that fails, things could get difficult! Undoubtedly, out of various matters, issues between couples often remain private. 

Resistance is futile

Sin Yeong-cheol

I usually avoid quarrels with my spouse, but whenever a dispute arises, we tend to have a comprehensive showdown. While I do not violently destroy anything or engage in direct confrontations, even when it's apparent that I may be wrong, I refuse to bow down and concede. We might argue for a while, and afterwards, I usually slip away quietly. The exact time length isn’t predefined, but when I return after cooling my temper, things usually resolve by themselves. I won’t be the one to initiate dialogue afterward; I shall only speak when my partner breaks the silence. If neither party brings up the issue afterward, then it’s best to let it be.

Ancient strategies

Park Chan-hee

Ah, how curious it is! The fact that people are inquiring about techniques to reconcile spousal squabbles says a lot. Private matters between couples shouldn't be made public, as revealing such might lead to resentment from the spouse and rekindle the quarrel anew. My wife has a rather sensitive temperament, and if I respond to her provocations, we could easily wind up clamoring in conflict daily. However, my nature is quite the opposite. I am, by nature, unflappable, and when she is angry, I continue to smile quietly. When she shouts, I remain silent. Soon, her anger dissipates, and she appears to regret it. In the end, I emerge as the victorious party. This can be likened to a military strategy of ancient times, where waiting for the enemy to exhaust themselves before springing into action leads to consistent victories. Typically, those prone to anger are quick to become roused but also tend to be amenable.

Resolving with force

Lee Il

In international wars, conflicts are resolved quickly when one side demonstrates overwhelming military strength. Similarly, in domestic quarrels, the husband must exert authority to reach rapid reconciliation. Whenever arguments arise, I often resort to this forceful approach. If my wife does something displeasing, I initially express my anger, and she inevitably capitulates. While some may see this as tyranny, it is said that if women do not feel some measure of harshness from men over three days, a restlessness arises within them. Therefore, being stern is sometimes necessary, even when I love and pamper her, otherwise, it leads to more quarrels. 

Refusing to engage in conflict

Joo Yo-han

Even if I say this, I doubt anyone would be envious. My spouse and I are genuinely affectionate; we rarely find ourselves in actual disputes. If misunderstandings arise, it's often how we engage each other that leads to conflicts. In these moments, I never respond to provocations directly and prefer to let the situation cool before discussing it logically—when tempers have cooled, if misunderstandings exist, I clarify those, and if the fault lies with me, I acknowledge that openly. 

A gesture of reconciliation

Jo Gi-ryeol

Despite our differing personalities, arguments between my spouse and I are quite rare; it's almost a myth. Even during difficult times, if an argument arises, my spouse directs criticisms at me, but I counter with, “For what purpose is that being said?” Ultimately, such disagreements dissolve without resistance. I often find there is no real point to disagreements and recognize there are no distinct methods for reconciliation. 

**Summary**

This article discusses various perspectives from different contributors on how couples can handle quarrels and reconcile after disputes. Many emphasized the importance of reflection, communication, and sometimes the necessity of authority in resolving conflicts. Notably, the discussions reveal traditional gender roles and expectations in relationships during the 1920s, with some contributors asserting that men often maintain authority to prevent quarrels from escalating. This depiction of marital dynamics and societal norms surrounding marriage may interest historians studying the evolving gender roles in Korea during the colonial period.
