기사제목	카폐의 情調, 서울맛·서울情調 (The Atmosphere of Cafés, Seoul Flavor and Seoul Sentiment)  
필자	朴露兒  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Other)  

It is hot. Indeed, it is hot. It feels stifling inside the goddamn belly of despair, and it is hard to breathe. I cannot endure this; so let’s go out and find a cool and refreshing place!  

Shall we follow the honey bees over the mountains and savor sweetness? Or shall we pass by the broken-winged butterflies, seeking the scent of spring? Shall we shake the sleeping dewdrops and dance with the fireflies? What can the melodious songs of the orioles hidden in the lush greenery be like? How can we find such exquisite refreshment in the very center of the bustling city? Furthermore, such encounters are too monotonous, too languid, and unrealistic; they bear the fatigue of daydreams threaded through a colorless landscape, and whether they exist if poets recite them, or painters depict them—who knows? The youthful crowd indulges in these temptations, the ordinary revelers will certainly retort against it. Ah! I am also youthful, so let us suppress these childish notions and meticulously scrutinize everything around like a crab peering everywhere with its two eyes.  

Amid the clamorous chaos, there breathes a web-like unity and silence, while the reality feels like powerful waves smashing against the sandy hills. Yet, within it lurks a soft melancholia akin to the dreamlike night, the lively autumn air gliding past, as sharp instincts leap around like two lively eyes dancing in playful fancy, where feigned calm eyes gaze curiously, wary of external threats, savoring a peculiar fatigue, the ears ringing with sound, vibrant colors, the bizarre action of hallucinated peripheral nerves—if one seeks such new conditions full of intense stimuli on modern or prophetic highways, these may not be absent. The city is a playground for youth. Just because it is summer and hot, does it mean there isn’t modern pleasure around, or the refreshing breeze of elegant spots?  

As the fiery sun rolls lazily along the sky, blasting thousands of fire arrows as if it could kill at any moment, July’s fierce sun seems to triumph, and as it leisurely makes its way toward the moon palace, sinking behind the western sky, Seoul, plunged into the depths of heat, suddenly springs to life like a multitude of reinforcements. The cool breeze teasingly caressing the sweaty body after such a long wait, the gradually flickering electric lights, under the moonlight, amidst the peculiar bluish night sky, the march of nighttime Seoul begins to play its first tune.  

In the illuminated streets of Seoul at night, alluring smiles sway like celestial constellations, and people drift with the tides. Some hide in shadowy corners, some wait stiffly in front of the stalls, some wander into the nighttime market, disappearing into narrow alleys, pouring into the Wahsin Business Association, while friends sneak into taverns. Each moves in different directions, but amidst them, the young men in neatly tailored suits and the middle-aged gentlemen’s footsteps are like those of Kim Yu-shin, the famed general of the Silla Era, habitually heading toward the brothels while gliding along the familiar paths, making their way unknowingly toward the café district. Bukgyo-dong, Chaya-dong, Meiji-dong, Hwanggeum-dong, Yeongnak-dong, café, café, café...  

There it is! A café. The café understands the modernity of people's varied appetites and responds to their urgent needs while preparing everything for them. Now, let us step into the cozy sanctuary of happiness that is the café and take a look around.  

Do they seek the modern pleasures of women here? Here are charming and modestly adorned young women, mirroring the western maidens in their castle, with their roles as “waitresses,” carrying the weight of lives with every smile and laughter rendered, skillfully hidden behind snow-white aprons! Drumming! The piano resounds amidst the swaying flowers, where tears shower and flit around the fluttering moths—how dance-like and stirring they are as they walk among the marbled tables! When the chaotic rhythm of the “Charleston” flows from the records, their soft waists and swaying bodies temptingly stir the minds of inebriated male patrons amidst dreamy clouds of smoke swirling around the room. Uneasy secretive meetings at the tables, quick exchanges of gifts like lightning bolts, blushing eyes bursting like flower buds—the whispers of secrets, who can fathom them! Over there, an old man without a care peeks over hungrily, chuckling; should we ask him...  

As the night stretches on, the murmurs from every corner rise, and the legs of the waitresses playfully start to twist in cuteness. A group of students occupies the large table. Wine glasses celebrating youth and love clash repeatedly.  

Oh, my precious angel!  
Tonight, let your warmth embrace me in my room,  
Even if tomorrow I hold onto the gravestone,  
How beautiful is the dream of your gentle weight lingering in my arms! Oh dear! Hahaha, hohoho, what’s this!? The waitress, with peacock feathers atop her head, signals slyly toward the student.  

Using all her charm, she interacts with tipsy patrons, deftly attending to the drunk young women within their cozy fold. The scent of perfumes wafting in from the fan's breeze and whispers of smoke fill the room. Amidst the cerulean hues of electric lights and the evocative fragrance of roses, rooms are teasingly hospitable, yet curiously reminiscent of a graveyard; they don't feel lonely or mournful! Bottles and emptied containers, which crowd around the table, now lay like corpses! The glistening bottles of refined liquor with Western labels momentarily awaken the fresh tastes of the crowd.  

“Bring the brandy!” “Let’s get the vermouth!”  

However, those few scanty glasses hold mockingly the laughable price of 40 jeon. Like a stormy wave crashing, patrons surge in! Businessmen are present. There are clerks. Students, middle school teachers, journalists, and vagabonds… Each leaves behind traces upon the marble tables or the mirror reflections in walls, supposedly fulfilling their yearnings—but anyway leaving no trace at all. Some satisfied depart in taxis, while others stumble home as night falls.  

Then, where is the atmosphere of the café? Is it in the curves of the brightly colored dresses of the “waitresses”? In the exchanges around the tables full of excitement? In the thick, muddy air enveloping the entire space, blending softly with the distinct ambiance present across each table? Or is it in the tender farewells of the eight or so “waitresses” as they bustle around the handsome patrons, gliding into their cars as if they are part of a grand procession?  

Yet, it isn’t limited to that. The café's atmosphere does not lie in its furnishings. Instead, it is found in the collective moods and sentiments of its patrons. The café merely caters to this; it harmonizes the emotions and mixes the feelings, acting as a medium. Therefore, the café in student districts carries the spirit of students while the gentry district hosts a gentlemanly atmosphere, and the newly opened café in the triangular district (essentially a galboodong shop) does not allow for indulgence in liquor. Consequently, the mixed feelings and emotions of the many who gather in cafés exert influence upon all the furnishings, hence, the café's atmosphere may be regarded as that fusion!  

Dear friends! Rather than be dominated by the sophisticated settings of the café or vainly losing oneself in the “waitresses’” affections, tossing coins of 10 or 20 jeon for tips fit to fill a pocket with 1 or 2 won—or being pressed into a distorted mind with 3 or 4 won—why not, when feeling low from a setback in business or a failed ambition, tuck away tens of jeon and venture into a snug little tavern?  

Among the gathered friends shouting amicably, surrounded by people jostling, unexpectedly you might fall amongst your allies, savoring fellowship, and when you discover your leader, it could be during a moment of sheer joy. If you find it unsatisfactory, toss aside your glass and bang on the café door, let the world hear you with vivacious, lively youth and shake the café itself! There lies the atmosphere of the café!  

**Summary**

The article captures the vibrant and alluring atmosphere of cafés in 1920s Seoul, weaving together vivid descriptions of urban life amidst calls for stimulation and youthful exuberance. It paints a picture of the tensions between modern living and traditional values while emphasizing the appeal of cafés as social spaces for both the younger generation and disillusioned adults. Historians might find the examination of café culture and the language used in this article particularly intriguing, as it reflects societal norms and the emotional landscapes of people experiencing colonial rule in Korea.

그러면 카폐의 情調는 어데 잇는가? 「웨트레스」들의 색채찬란한 의복의 곡선에 잇는가. 새로 2시까지 테불을 끼고 속살기는 물거품가튼 사람에 잇나. 테불마다 기분이 다르면서도 은연중 방 전체의 鈍濁한 공기속에 녹아드는 砂塵가튼 통일에 잇느냐. 그럿치 안으면 둘너 싸는 7,8명 「웨트레스」들의 정다운 餞送을 빗발가티 바드며 귀공자와 가티 자동차에 몸을 싯고 흔들거리는데 잇느냐.
그러나 그럿치도 안타. 카폐의 情調는 카폐가 가진게 안이요 카폐에 모아드는 客들의 기분과 정서에 잇는 것이다. 카폐는 오즉 그 지배를 바들 뿐이며 그 정서를 조화하며 기분을 융화하는 매개소 밧게는 더 되지 못한다. 그럼으로 학생촌에 잇는 카폐에는 학생기분이 떠돌며 신사촌에는 신사풍이 잇고 三角町(水標町 부근)에 새로 생긴 카폐(實은갈보우동집)에서는 싸홈하지 안코 술을 먹지 못한다. 그러면 카폐에 모여드는 여러 사람들의 기분과 정서 감정들이 카폐의 모든 설비에 힘을 엇고 나래가 돗처 자유롭게 흘너 나와 다시 연기와 가티 푸른 안개와 가티, 融合된 것이 카폐의 情調라고나 할까!?
사랑하는 모뽀들이여! 부질업시 카폐의 고묘한 설비에 지배를 바드며 어엽부게 가장한 「웨트레쓰」들의 환심을 살냐고 10전 20전이면 족할 「팁」(쁫지)을 1,2圓식이나 풍풍 내던지며 3경 4경까지 畸形的 심리에 눌니여 테불만 직히고 안저서 앗가운 시간과 돈과 정력을 낭비하지만 말고 時 혹 사업에 실패하거나 큰 뜻을 일우지 못해 마음이 울적하며 腸子에서 비참의 불길이 치밀 때가 잇거던 幾10전 호주머니에 너어 가지고 점잔은 선술집을 차자 가보라.
잔치(宴)집 모양으로 서서 옥신옥신하는 사람들  틈에 끼여서 약주잔을 거듭하는 가운데 의외에 그대들의 동지를 찻고 친구를 맛나며 지도자를 어들 때가 잇슬 것이다. 그래도 시원치가 안커던 잔을 던지고 일어나서 카폐의 문을  두들겨 그 천하를 호령할 만치  쾌활하고 발랄한 청춘의 기분으로 카폐을 뒤흔들어 보라! 그 곳에 카폐의 情調는 뛰논다! <45>
