(米) P·와일드  

A Canadian shabby place under a tent  
At night, a woman lies on a bed wrapped in clumsy cotton bandages. Nearby, a man sits with a rifle laid out.  

Wife: It's about time.  
Husband: Don't say such things.  
Wife: But I know I won't survive the night. (Silence for a moment, smiling) You know your skills are undeniably impressive.  
Husband: Still, it's hard to hear.  
Wife: No matter how much I try to deny it, the world knows famous hunters. It's true—I shouldn't have been wearing the red sweater you said I wouldn't. It was a mistake to think you would shoot knowing it was me, wasn’t it? Otherwise, did I really come to be shot at 300 yards away? Truly, what a famed hunter you are!  
Husband: Please, my dear! Don't say that. (Silence) The guide will bring a doctor soon.  
Wife: When?  
Husband: I suppose by dawn...  
Wife: By dawn? It'll be too late by then—but never mind worrying me. I am not feeling any pain. I will die feeling humorously connected to you. Will the game that dies by your hand not run away with clean hearts? (The woman grasps the man's hand tightly. The man remains silent.) My love, look at me! I am truly happy.  
Husband: Is that so?  
Wife: Death! - Death is truly a delightful resolution. (Silence) There’s something I must assure you before everything is resolved.  
Husband: You’re getting weak and tired; please just rest.  
Wife: No, something like feeling tired doesn’t matter! In a while, I will be able to rest all I want. What could I possibly worry about? I must make sure to convey everything before I die. I was a terrible wife. A terrible mother. I betrayed you, a wretched wife. (Silence between both.) It was long ago. However, I will not speak the name of that man. Afterward, did I continue with another man? (Silence) I always wished for the praises of men. Eventually, I must have found that my heart did not feel at ease... So I worked hard to straighten my heart, but I could not entirely straighten it out, could you know it? I worried if the children would find out... But now, they all know well, and they no longer doubt me, thinking I am a good mother... right? Please do not say any words to the children about this.  
Husband: What words could there be to say...  
Wife: Promise me not to let them know? (The husband nods.) And you will forgive me everything? Yes? (The husband nods again.) Ah—truly, you are a good man!  
Husband: (quietly) But I have known everything since the beginning. – (The woman is startled.) – I have known everything from the start.  
Wife: Then, why did you feign ignorance?  
Husband: How could I say such a thing fearing for the children?  
Wife: Then what? (Grabs his hand.) - You don’t need to be someone as kind as you...  
Husband: Really? (Silence) From your actions, I knew everything. I thought of how to resolve this before you had to worry... (Silence... speaking faintly) So I wondered and thought about it, and this time it is the mountain we came out to! We came to this lonely place away from others, eagerly waiting for an opportunity, and when it came, I just had to carry out my skill and fire the trigger!  
It is a problem; it is a resolution.  
(End Scene)  

**Summary**  

The dialogue in "Resolution" presents a tense conversation between a couple facing death. The wife, aware of her impending demise due to an injury from her husband's gun, reflects on her troubled past and expresses a mixture of resignation and happiness about their relationship. The husband, wary of the truth of their situation, seems to grapple with both love and betrayal. This poignant interaction sheds light on themes of guilt, love, and existential acceptance, which can resonate deeply with the historical context of personal and social struggles during the colonial period.

Historians may find this exchange illuminating due to its exploration of emotional and psychological burdens during a time when Korean identities were challenged under colonial rule. The intimate dynamics reveal how personal crises also mirror larger societal issues, shedding light on the human experience amid the uncertainties of colonial life.

카나다 荒廢한 곳의 天幕內
밤 침상에는 서투른 솜시로 붕대를 감은 여자가 누어 잇다. 그 녑헤는 궤ㅅ작을 깔고 한 남자가 안저 잇다.

妻 인제는 고만이예요.
夫 글세 그런 말은 말래두.
妻 그러치만 오늘밤을 못 넘길 것을 나는 알고 잇서요. (잠간 침묵, 미소를 띄우고) 참 당신의 견양이야말노 틀님업섯서.
夫 글세 듣기 실혀.
妻 무어니 무어니 해두 세계에 유명한 포수는 다릅듸다-. 내가 당신이 이른대로 빨간 쎄-타를 입지 안코 풀숩으로 단인 것이 잘못이지 당신이 김승으로 알고 쏜 것이야 잘못이요? 엇저면 300야-드나 떠러진 곳에서 쏜 것이 드러와 맛는단 말이유! 참 명포수야!
夫 글세 여보! 제발 빌께 그러지 좀 마러요. (긴 침묵) 아마 곳 안내인이 의사를 데리고 올테지-.
妻 언제?
夫 새벽까지는 오겟지.. .
妻 새벽까지? 너머 늣는데- 그러나 아모 념려 마러요. 조곰도 괴롭지 안으니- 나는 우스면서 깃거운 마음으로 죽을 테요. 당신의 총에 마저 죽는 김승들도 다- 나가티 깨끗한 마음으로 죽을가? (여자는 남자의 손을 꽉 쥔다. 남자는 침묵.) 여보 날 좀 보아요! 나는 참 행복스러웁소.
夫 그래두.
妻 죽는 것! - 죽는 것은 참으로 유쾌한 해결이구려. (침묵) 모든 것이 해결되는 때 당신에게 꼭 한마듸 해둘 말이 잇서요.
夫 작고 피곤한데 가만히 좀 잇서요.
妻 아-니요. 피곤한 것 쯤이야 그까짓 것! 곳 인제 얼마만 지내면 오래오래 마음껏 쉬일텐데. 무슨 걱정이야요. 나는 다- 이약이를 해버려야지 그대로는 못 죽것서요. 그러고 내가 죽은 뒤라도 다 알려질 것이지만<165> 숨이 끈어지기 전에 내 입으로 다- 말해바릴 테야요. 나는 고약한 안해이엿슴니다. 고약한 에미이엿슴니다. 당신을 배반한 고약한 안해이엿서요. (兩人 침묵) 벌서 오래 전 일임니다. 그러나 그 남자의 일홈은 말하지 안겟서요. 그 뒤에 곳 다른 남자와 그랫스닛가. (침묵) 나는 언제든지 남자들의 禮讚을 밧고 십헛다우. 그러다가 나종에는 내가 도리혀 마음이 들뜨게 되여바리지 안엇겟서요?... 그래 나는 늘 마음을 바로 잡으랴고 애를 퍽 썻스나 끗끗내 바로잡지 못하고 당신이 아시지나 안을가 아희들에게 들키지나 안을가 마음이 조마조마하면서도.... 그러치만 인제는 아희들도 다 잘앗고 인제는 아희들에게도 의심을 밧지 안코 또 나를 깻끗하엿든 어미라고 생각할 것이지요.. 응? 여보 당신 아희들에게는 아모 말도 마러주서요.
夫 암 말은 무슨 말....
妻 곡 약속을 직혀주시지요? (남자는 수긍한다.) 그러고 나의 모든 것을 용서도 해주시겟지요? 네? (남자는 수긍한다.) 아- 참으로 당신은 가업슨 냥반이서요!
夫 (조용하게) 그러나 나는 다- 알엇섯서. -(여자 놀랜다.)- 모든 것을 처음부터 알엇서-.
妻 그러구서두 모른체 하섯수?
夫 엇더케 그런 말을 아희들을 념려해서도 할 수가 잇나?
妻 엇저면? (손을 잡는다.)- 당신가티 착한 냥반을 고만....
夫 글세? (침묵) 나는 마누라의 행동을 보고서 벌서 다 알고 잇섯소. 그러고 마누라가 마음이 괴로워하기 전에 엇더케 결말을 지으랴고 생각을 햇다가.... (침묵.. 목소리를 엿게) 그래 궁리궁리하야 생각난 것이 이번에 산양을 나온 것이란 말이야! 우리는 사람들과 떠러저서 외로운 곳으로 왓고 열심히 기회를 기다리고 잇다가 드듸여 기회는 와서 나는 견양을 바로 마처가지고 방아쇠를 달깍 잽아제첫슬 뿐이야-.
問題야 解決이지.
