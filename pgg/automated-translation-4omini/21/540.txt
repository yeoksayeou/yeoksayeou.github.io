MANIQURE GIRL  
                        (米) P·와일드  
The scene is set in a hotel room. A middle-aged man, who appears very affluent, enjoys a chair. A cigarette is between his fingers. A diligent manicurist is attentively clipping his nails.

Man (after a while): Is the smoke bothering you?

Manicurist: Not at all! Good cigarettes blend well with the air. I’ve seen guests smoke Five-Cent cigars, and the smell from those cigars is quite pleasant.

Man: Do guests even smoke cheap cigars at such a hotel? I would expect Havana cigars at least...

Manicurist: Not at all. Most guests hardly buy cigarettes and would rather contribute that money to their hotel bills.

Man: It’s surprising; is this hotel really that expensive?

Manicurist: Oh dear, even you have said such things? They say you’re a millionaire worth 50 million!

Man: But I don’t even know how much I own. It’s hardly been long since I started living as a rich person. Until half a year ago, all I had were just a single nickel and a bad luck that kept coming my way.

Manicurist: Would you like me to apply some polish to your nails?

Man: Oh really! Just as I thought! (Suddenly remembering) Huh! Ever since I was young, I aimed for gold mines, but after discovering copper mines, I failed to manage copper and ended up failing in gold too, and silver mines were a complete flop as well! All I had with me were hunger and loneliness, and I wandered through the mountains with wild snakes and feral cats! At that time, I was utterly disappointed with everything, thirsty enough to wish for death!

Manicurist: And then, eventually, you managed to save some money? They say you’ve saved like 5 million cash!

Man: Yeah, that’s right! None of that matters, it's from oil...

Manicurist: Such is life.

Man: What? Life? After thirty years of suffering in the mountains — after thirty years of searching — can that be called luck? (He extends his hand) Take a look! Is this the hand of a lucky man?

Manicurist (gripping his hand): Well, with hands like this, you might as well get married. I know a woman who could keep your heart locked for a moment.

Man: What nonsense are you talking?

Manicurist: (laughing) Ruby! My name is Ruby.

Man: Oh! Ruby! That’s a lovely name. But I’m already married and have two children—

Manicurist: (disappointed) Oh?

Man: My wife and I live apart, and she is well-regarded in social circles, but after thirty years of suffering together, it’s understandable to feel estranged from others!

Manicurist: (suspiciously) But you’ve already gone and done things together with someone else.

Man: (dreamily) She’ll always seem no older than twenty to me.... It wouldn’t be surprising if she mingles with other men.

Manicurist: Yet I believe you would secretly feel upset.

Man: Not at all! If my wife were to act thusly, before she even could, my right arm would have to fall off!

Manicurist: Ah! I’m done now!

Man: (pulls out a wad of cash and takes out one note) Here, five bucks. I don’t have any change, but it doesn’t matter; keep two of it.

Manicurist: That amount is not enough.

Man: What, five bucks is small?

Manicurist: Definitely.

Man: (pulls out more cash) Then how much should I give?

Manicurist: Ten thousand bucks.

Man: What? Again?

Manicurist: If it’s a one-time payment, it’s ten thousand bucks.

Man: Why are you joking like this!

Manicurist: If I’m joking, I’ll laugh! When have I ever claimed things lightly?

Man: Well, it makes sense. But for nail trimming to cost ten thousand bucks! That’s a bit expensive.

Manicurist: Still cheaper than losing an arm due to your wife!

Man: What does that mean—

Manicurist: Look here! We’ve been locked up in this room together for more than half an hour already.... We’ve violated hotel rules.

Man: I don't care about the rules.

Manicurist: (locking the door) Moreover, with the door locked — now hurry—

Man: So what does that mean?

Manicurist: Now you’re the one who initiated things!

Man: Are you crazy, woman?

Manicurist: But if I speak like this, who would dare to deny it?

Man: Yet still, it doesn’t seem like I approached you.

Manicurist: (suddenly tearing her clothes) Look! Is this not enough? (pauses) Now! It’s better for you to pay up! Otherwise, shall I inform your wife? How ridiculous it will be! Being caught by the police here, facing trial for shame in a hotel room regarding someone else's wife! The tabloids would be all over it! Your wife has spent thirty years with you. Yet, if she sees a detailed account of such disgrace in the newspapers, and then finds out you were taken away by the cops... Surely she would come to despise you. Ha ha! Which woman would endure such a cowardly act and continue to live with a man she refers to as her husband? (threateningly) Hurry and give me the money!

Man: Are you making fun of me?

Manicurist: Making fun? Why?

Man: Oh, you do as you wish. I won’t give you a single penny... Do you think I would fall for a bitch like you? Haha. (The man quietly takes a deep drag of his cigarette.)

Manicurist: What? Hmph. Bitch! You spoke well, but without yielding any money, you won’t endure it. Just you wait. (screaming) Help me! Someone help me! (tears her clothes apart) Help me! (overturns the table and throws the nail clipping tools.)

Man: (still sitting quietly) Scream a bit louder.

Manicurist: Help! Oh, someone save me—

Detective (knocking on the door): Open up! Quickly!

Manicurist: Someone’s trying to kill me—

Detective: Open the door now!

Manicurist: This guy locked it! (The woman rushes to the man, shaking the key) Coward! You won’t give it over? Oh dear... (finally, she turns the key in the lock.)

Detective: (kicking the door open) What’s going on? Huh? (Other patrons peer into the room.)

Manicurist: (madly) This man called me to trim his nails and forced me in here and locked the door—

Detective: Huh? Sounds like trouble!

Manicurist: What a coward! So, what now?

Detective: (moving over to the man) Sorry, but we’ll have to take you to the station.

Man: (still sitting reluctantly) Wait just a moment. What has this woman claimed I did to her?

Manicurist: Yes! This coward did that to me! 

Man: I haven’t moved from my seat for over half an hour. (He raises his hand holding a cigarette for others to see) Look! The cigarette was lit first. The ash is about two inches long. If it shakes even a little, it will fall off. (幕)  

**Summary**  

In this dramatized scene, a wealthy man and a manicurist engage in a dialogue that transitions from mundane interactions to a tense encounter that hints at scandal and deception. The manicurist, Ruby, attempts to leverage the situation for money, threatening to expose the man. The play reflects gender dynamics and social tensions, showcasing how economic disparity and societal norms lead to manipulative behaviors. Historians might find interest in how this example reveals colonial societal structures in Korea during this era, where personal dignity, wealth, and gender roles were deeply intertwined within the context of increasing Western influence.
