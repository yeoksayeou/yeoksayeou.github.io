The Transition of the Markets in Gyeongseong  
						Lee Chong-hwa  

I. The Origin of the Markets  

The terms "left temple and right society" and "front morning and back market" were certain principles in the establishment of a country and a capital in the East. The market was not only related to the people's trade but also served the needs of the nation. In Korea, markets were established in the capital since the Three Kingdoms period to secure trade and facilitate transactions in the countryside, where markets were opened on the 1st and 6th, 2nd and 7th, 3rd and 8th, 4th and 9th, and 5th and 10th days of each month, known as "one month and six days."  

Considering the opening of markets now, it might be unrecorded in Goguryeo and Baekje, but Silla began to open markets in the 2nd year of King Beopheung (AD 490) in the capital for the exchange of goods from various regions. This was the establishment of the market in Silla at that time, leading to the later establishment of Pyeongyang and Gyeongseong.  

Later in the 10th year of King Jijeung, in the spring of the first month (AD 509), the East Market was established in the capital, and an agency called the East Market Office was set up to manage it with two inspectors, two chief merchants, three scholars, and four historians. In the 4th year of King Gyeongdeok (AD 695), two markets to the southwest were established, and just like the East Market, West Market and South Market Offices were established with similar officials for each.  

Based on this, it can be seen that the 10th year of King Jijeung is merely 20 years after the 12th year of King Beopheung, meaning that after Silla opened its markets, the market situation developed rapidly, and in order to facilitate its flourishing, it became necessary to organize it. Therefore, King Jijeung selected the eastern part of the capital centered around Gimsong and Banwolseong, where it was easy for people to gather and established markets there along with officials to protect and manage them.  

Silla's national strength surged and ultimately conquered Baekje and Goguryeo, achieving the unification that occurred 179 years after the 12th year of King Beopheung, which is in the 8th year of King Munseong (AD 668). Following this, the development of Silla's capital was extraordinary, so the East Market alone would not be able to provide enough convenience to the people. This is why, in the 4th year of King Gyeongdeok, 27 years after the unification, it was a reasonable choice to establish the two markets in the southwest, and it shows that the development of the Silla capital first progressed to the east and then to the west and south.  

Goryeo, in the 2nd year of Cheonju (AD 918), when Taejo decided the capital at Songak and established the palace and administrative offices, also held the markets and restructured the scale of the city by dividing it into five districts. Later, in the 4th year of King Hee (AD 1208), there was a major restructuring of the long corridors of the East Market, with a total of 1,008 beams stretching from the area beneath Gwanghwamun to the intersection at Jeongda Street. From this, we can know the location of the initial markets established when Taejo was determined to relocate the capital; the necessity for major renovations was evident after 291 years since Taejo's 2nd year.  

Moreover, just as Silla established markets and managed them, Goryeo established the Gyeongshi Office to administer the markets and separately appointed long corridor inspectors to supervise the construction and renovation of the markets. When King Hee was reconstructing the long corridors of the East Market, we can observe that a separate supervisory official of ministerial rank was appointed outside of the usual positions, indicating that this construction work was of great significance. However, at one time, in addition to the Gyeongshi Office, the Gyeongshisa was also established to specifically manage matters related to the great markets.

II. The Establishment of Markets  

538 years ago, in the 3rd year after the founding of Korea by Taejo, in the year AD 1394, the capital was set in Hanyang and with the construction of the ancestral shrine and palace and the city wall, the construction of the new capital progressed. The establishment of the markets occurred six years after the capital was designated, immediately after Taejo abdicated the throne.  

Accordingly, in the 1st year of King Jeongjong, in February (AD 1399), the left and right long corridors of the markets began to be established with more than 800 bays, the area of which stretched from Huijeong Bridge to Donghak Palace's entrance. This was the first facility for Gyeongseong markets, as crowds gathered from the outside, including vagrants and monks for supplies and labor.  

After 74 years, in the 3rd year of King Seongjong, during the year of Imjin (AD 1472), the designated area of the markets became narrow, and with the people increasing, chaos arose, as military horses filled the places causing many injuries to civilians. Thus, the teaching was given to extend the markets from Yeondae-dong to Dunui Gate's Stone Bridge, thereby making it easier for trade. However, the Ministry of Household Affairs recommended that the area from Samkan Seungmun to Dunui Gate's Stone Bridge become narrower due to the geographical conditions, coupled with the fact that the crowds around Changgyeong Palace, Jongmyo Shrine, and the Uigyeong Office were not appropriate for such bustling activity. Following this suggestion, the second expansion of the markets was made only to extend the original area to the east.  

However, in the 194th year since the establishment of the markets in the 45th year of King Seonjo (AD 1592), the market suffered from the Imjin War, and again, in March of the 28th year after this, in the 11th year of King Gwanghaegun (AD 1619), a terrible great fire occurred where several thousand households were burned from the Bell Tower to Dongcheon and the Southern Palace. The resulting loss of life was extremely high, marking one of the most catastrophic disasters in history since the opening of the markets.  

Then for 97 years, there were no disasters affecting the markets. However, during the 9th year of King Yeongjo, in October (AD 1733), and in September of the 13th year (AD 1737), and in September of the 20th year (AD 1744), as well as in October of the 37th year (AD 1761) and in March of the 47th year (AD 1771), the markets faced fires. King Yeongjo had the longest reign among the Joseon dynasty kings at 52 years. However, with this extended reign, the frequency of market fires increased, leading to a fire roughly every ten years.  

Originally, the establishment of the markets was to facilitate national management, and when large-scale fires occurred, the government would lend interest-free long-term construction funds to the victims so that they could rebuild and secure safety in the markets. The rules for compensation were to be repaid in installments, while waiving public services for that year to alleviate their burden. Alternatively, on special occasions, direct financial support was provided for rebuilding expenses.  

In modern examples, in April of the 5th year of King Sunjo (AD 1804), when a market suffered from a disaster in Hwaryeok, funds were ordered to be released equivalently to 100 units of war fabric to the markets with a ten-year repayment period, interest-free. Furthermore, in December of the 10th year of King Heon (AD 1844), when the Myeongchum Market was affected during the same disaster, funds and construction materials were released to restore the market under the same terms as previous occasions.  

In November of the 20th year (AD 1883), when the Cotton Market, Paper Market, and Horse Market faced fires, the royal court was highly concerned about the reconstruction of the markets and offered particular financial assistance of ten thousand nyang and twenty units of timber for reconstruction. Moreover, on September 2 of the 24th year (AD 1887), when the Fabric Market, Paper Market, East Horse Market, and Cotton Market experienced a fire, it was a scene reminiscent of a city engulfed in flames, appearing as if it were a city that never sleeps.

In this instance, the king was extremely disturbed by the situation and issued notifications of condolence to the citizens affected by the disaster, emphasizing the royal treasury's financial aid. The notification was as follows:  

It is said that markets are a source of livelihood for the common people of the capital. Now, in light of this tragic disaster, where several shops have been turned to ashes, it brings great concern, especially with the winter approaching, where those without work may have no place to stay. Thus, a special aid in the form of one hundred thousand nyang and fifty units of timber is offered, to be divided reasonably among the shops for relief.  

In this manner, when disasters occurred in the markets, the state would provide aid and assist in the rebuilding of the burnt establishments so that the disaster-affected merchants could return to their former livelihoods, ensuring that the pathways of mutual dependence between the people and the state would not be severed. For the past 500 years, the markets in Gyeongseong have not changed locations, remaining in fixed areas.  

Now, according to the map of the capital's Jinju Mountain River Collection, the area from Huijeong Bridge to Yeonji-dong Bridge is related to the southern side of the large road, from Jeongro 1 Street (below Huijeong Bridge) reaching Jeongro 4 Street, and from south Jeongro 4 Street up to the small Seongyo Bridge, the names of the markets have been recorded to indicate their locations.  

To list them, starting from Huijeong Bridge to Yeonji-dong Bridge on the southern side of the main road includes from Huijeong Bridge to Saenjeon (fresh produce market) to Byeongmun (screen door) up to Jeongro 4 Street; the Fresh Produce Market, the Chicken Market, the Sand Pottery Market, the Smoke Bamboo Market, the Corner Market, the Cotton Fabric Market, the Longevity Bed Market, the White Wood Market, and from Jeongro 4 Street towards Iron Wood Bridge; the Bark Market, the Blue Cloth Market, the Straw Hat Market, the Shoe Market, the Salt Market, the Spoon and Chopsticks Market, the Horse Bed Market, the Clothing Market, the Mixed Grain Market, and so forth, leading from Iron Wood Bridge to the Cloth Market (the Fabric Market) up to Changgyeong Palace's entrance.  

On the northern side, starting from Sujinbang-dong Byeongmun up towards Jeongro 4 Street includes the Matt Market, the Honey Market, the Upper Rice Market, the Silk Market, the Ramie Cloth Market, the Royal Bed Market, the Jeong Market, leading from Jeongro 4 Street towards Iron Wood Bridge, including the Jeong Market toward the Small Seongyo Bridge and further up to the Great Seongyo Bridge, representing the White Wood Market, Paper Market, and the Cotton Market, continuing to Small Seongyo Bridge while including the Lacquer Wood Market, the Gyu Air Market, and the Nine Separation Bench Market.  

When comparing the positions noted on this current capital map with those from 36 years ago, specifically during the Gojong era (year AD 1894) at the time of the Gabo reform, the position of the Bark Market next to the Shoe Market is now above the Blue Cloth Market, along with some differences in location, such as the Upper Corner Market dropping below the Iron Wood Market. Excluding one or two locations that were shut down, the overall arrangement remains unchanged.

Summary  

This article discusses the evolution of the markets in Gyeongseong, tracing their historical origins from the Three Kingdoms period through to the late Joseon dynasty. It highlights how these markets served not just as centers of commerce but also as significant components of the state’s economic management and societal structure. The consistent location of these markets over 500 years and the interventions of the state during disasters reveal a deeply intertwined relationship between the government and the merchants. Historians might find the government’s proactive approach to market management and disaster recovery particularly interesting, reflecting the dynamics of state and society in Korea during the colonial period.

						李重華
一, 市廛의 由來

左廟右社라든지 前朝後市라 함은 동양에서 國을 立하고 都를 設함의 일정한 準則이엿스며 시장은 人民의 貿遷이 此에 係할 뿐 안이라 국가의 需用이 또한 此에 資함으로 朝鮮은 遠히 三國時代부터 首都에 市廛을 置하야 坐賈를 安하게 하고 鄕村에 시장을 設하야 행상을 便하게 하얏는대 其物貨의 取引이 도시의 市廛은 日日로 此를 행하되 鄕村은 매월의 1, 6일과 2 ,7일과 3, 8일과 4 ,9일과 5, 10일에 市를 開하니 此가 소위 一月六場이다.
今에 市廛의 開倉을 考하면 高句麗와 百濟에 在하야는 此를 未聞하겟스나 新羅는 炤智王2년(서기490년)에 京師에 始히 市肆를 開하야써 사방의 物貨를 通하얏하니 此는 新羅가 당시 수도에 市廛을 置한 開創될 뿐 안이라 實노 後日의 開城과 京城市廛의 先驅가 된다 하겟다.
然한대 其後 智證王10년 春正月(서기509년)에 京都 東市를 置하고 東市典이라 하는 官署를 設하야 監2人 大舍2人 書生3人 史4人을 置하야 此를 관리하게 하고 孝昭王4년(서기695년)에 西南兩市를 置하며 東市의 例와 如히 西市典과 南市典을 設하고 監2人 大舍2人 書生3人 史4人을 各置하얏다.
此에 의하야 觀하면 智證王10년은 炤智王12년을 後하기 20년에 불과한 즉 炤智王이 市肆를 開創한 후로 市況의 발전이 速하야 尤히 此를 繁盛하게 하랴면 此를 정리할 필요가 有함으로 智證王은 당시 人民의 聚散이 편한 金城과 半月城을 중심으로 한 國都의 東部를 택하야 市廛을 此에 集合하고 官을 設하야 市廛을 보호 관리하게 함이라 하겟다.
新羅의 국력이 澎漲하야 竟히 百濟와 高句麗를 攻하야 統合의 業을 成하기는 正히 炤智王12년을 後하기 179년인 文武王8년(서기 668년)인대 隨하야 此時 新羅國都1 의 발전이 非常하얏슬 것인즉 東市의 一로는 인민에게 便宜를 與하지 못할 것이다. 故로 統合 後 27년에 當한 孝昭王4년에 西南兩市를 置함은 時勢에 適宜한 합리적 辨法이라 할지며 또한 新羅 國都의 발달은 東에 先하야 西와 南에 及함을 知할 수가 잇다.
高麗는 天授2년(서기918년) 太祖가 都를 松岳에 定하고 궁궐을 創하며 官衙를 設하며 坊里를 辨하야 五部에 分하야 도시의 규모를 정비할 時에 또한 市廛을 立하얏스며 其後 熙宗4년 秋7월(서기 1208년)에 大市左右의 長廊을 改營한 事가 有하얏는데 廣化門2으로 自하야 十字街에 至하기 凡1008楹이라 하니 此에 由하야 太祖 定都의 初의 市廛의 位置를 知할 수 잇스며<70>太祖2년을 距하기 291년인즉 大修繕을 要하기도 必然의 勢일 것이다.
且 新羅가 市廛을 置하야 市를 管理함과 如히 高麗는 京市署를 置하야 市廛을 句檢하는 事를 掌하게 하얏스며 別히 行廊3 都監4 을 置하야 市廛의 營造와 修繕을 掌하얏섯는대 熙宗이 大市長廊을 改營할 時에는 從來의 使와 副使 등 官外에 특히 宰樞(大臣格)로 別監을 任하얏슴을 觀하면 此時 改營의 工事가 중대한 것이엇슴을 知할 것이다. 然而 一時는 京市署 외에 大市司를 置하야 名稱과 如히 특별히 大市에 관한 事를 관리한 듯하다.

二, 市廛의 設置

지금부터 538년 전에 朝鮮 太祖끠서 國을 開하고 즉위하신지 3년 즉 서기1394년에 都를 漢陽에 정하시고 太廟와 宮殿을 建하며 성벽을 築하야 新都의 건설이 次第로 進就하얏는대 市廛의 설치는 都를 정한지 제6년이오 太祖끠서 位를 禪하신 後에 在하얏다.
按하면 定宗원년 己卯 春2월(서기1399년)에 始히 市廛左右行廊 8백여 間을 置하얏는대 其 구역은 惠政橋1 로 自하야 昌德宮 洞口에 至하기까지의 大路兩側이며 外方의 游手와 僧徒를 聚하야 糧을 給하야 此를 役하게 하니 此가 京城市廛 최초의 시설이며 其後 74년을 經하야 成宗3년 壬辰(서기1472년)에 市廛의 구역은 협소하고 人은 衆하야 奸細가 攘奪하며 軍馬가 塡塞하야 人이 多히 躝傷하니 蓮地洞口로 自하야 敦義門2 石橋에 至하기까지 市肆를 分列하야 踈密을 得宜케 함이 可하다 하신 傳敎를 下하니 此는 즉 市廛의 증설한 구역을 동서로 연장케 함이다. 然하나 戶曺는 三間屛門3 으로 自하야 敦義門 石橋까지는 地勢가 窄狹하고 昌德宮과 宗廟와 義禁府4 는 人을 聚하야 雜鬧케 함이 不宜하니 日影臺5 로 自하야 蓮池洞橋6 에 至하기가지 左右에 分하야 列廛하야지이다 啓請함에 從하야 市廛의 제2차 확장은 原구역의 東으로만 연장하게 되얏섯다.
然한대 市廛을 설립한지 194년인 宣祖245년(서기1592년)에 壬辰板蕩의 亂을 經하얏고 又此를 後하기 28년인 光海11년(서기1619년) 3월에 鐘樓7 로 自하야 銅峴8 과 南別宮에 至하기까지 數千餘家가 延燒하야 人畜의 死者가 甚多한 참혹한 大火가 有하얏는대 餘孼이 未盡하얏든지 此禍를 經한지 19년인 仁祖14년(서기1637년) 丙子胡亂에 賊兵이 12월26일에 城內의 左右行廊과 公舍를 盡焚하얏나니 此는 市廛開創 이후의 空前絶後하얏든 大劫運이오 大厄會라 하겟다.
其後 97년간은 市廛의 災禍가 無하얏다. 然하나 英祖9년 10월(서기1733년)과 同13년 9월(서기1737년)과 同20년 9월(서기1744년)과 同37년 10월(서기1761년)<71>과 同47년 3월(서기1771)에 市廛이 火하얏다. 英祖는 其在位가 52년인대 朝鮮列祖列宗 중에 治世가 最히 長久하얏다. 然而 長久한 이만큼 市廛의 火가 頻繁하야 每10년간에 一度의 火가 有하얏든 比例가 되리라 하겟다.
원래 市廛의 설치가 국가의 경영이엿슬 뿐 안이라 大燒의 災가 有하면 피해 시민의게 무이자 장기의 건축 자금을 貸與하야 市廛을 改建하야 安接하게 하며 其辨償의 法은 年賦하야 상환하게 하며 當年의 公役을 면제하야 其力을 舒하게 함이 恒例이며 또는 內下錢10 을 賜하야 工費를 充用케 하는 特例도 有하얏다.

敎曰市井者는 都下民庶之資以爲生也어늘 今此鬱攸之災에 各廛이 多入於灰燼하니 惕然驚心이며 寒序將進에 失業棲遑이 實所矜念이라 內下錢十萬兩과 惠廳木五十同을 特爲助給하노니 自廟堂으로 量宜分劃하야 以市顧恤之意하여라.
