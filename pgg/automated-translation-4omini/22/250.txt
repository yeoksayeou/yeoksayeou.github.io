발행일	1929년 12월 01일  
기사제목	工場監督과 大擊鬪, 夢夢夢 (Factory Supervisors and Great Fights, Dreams)  
필자	黃信德 (Hwang Sin-deok)  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

On a very warm day in early autumn, I took our school's students on a field trip. The number of students had unexpectedly increased, amounting to over 2,000. When we set out, the line stretched for 10 miles, causing everyone on the road to stop and marvel, saying, "Wow, how many people!" Hearing such remarks made me feel proud. Our school seemed to be the best girls' school in Seoul. 

While we were visiting the impressive large factory located in the heart of the city, all the students rushed in to look around. Inside, thousands of female workers were busily attending to weaving machines. Upon closer inspection, I wondered if all these workers were students from our school. Curious, I looked back, but none of the students who had formed a line behind me were present; they seemed to have disappeared underground. My heart raced with astonishment, and as I stared directly at the faces of the female workers, I thought, "How could they possibly be doing this?" Our students were absent.

Those girls amusingly looked at me and smiled quietly without saying a word. It became unbearable for me to feel confused without understanding what was going on. Suddenly, a malicious-looking supervisor, like a hawk swooping down for a chick, dashed toward me. He grasped the arm of a second-year student from our school who was working and shouted in a thunderous voice, "Why are you just yawning? If you want to work, get out!" He slapped her and kicked her, looking as if he would kill her on the spot.

"How dare you do that?" I yelled menacingly at him. Surprised by my strength, I just grabbed his neck and lifted him effortlessly like a mouse, then threw him straight onto the courtyard.

I felt so refreshed as if I had been freed after a 30-year confinement. But then, I thought, "Oh no, what happened to that girl?" As I turned around, I found myself unable to see the factory, the female workers, or the students anymore; the place was empty. I was writhing and turned around when my eyes suddenly opened wide. It had been a dream. Where could I have found such strength?  

**Summary**

This article reflects the experiences of the author, Hwang Sin-deok, during a school trip to a factory, highlighting the harsh realities faced by female workers. It illustrates Hwang's shock and indignation at the treatment of a student-worker by a factory supervisor, epitomizing the struggles and inequalities of colonial Korea. Historians might find this account revealing, as it reflects the intersection of education, labor, and gender dynamics under colonial rule, as well as the author's personal emotional engagement with these issues.

工場監督과 大擊鬪, 夢夢夢
						黃信德
초가을 日氣가 몹시 따뜻한 날인데 나는 우리학교 生徒들을 다리고 修學旅行을 떠낫슴니다. 생도 수가 어느새 그럿케 만히 불엇는지 2천여명이나 될 것갓슴니다. 행길에 나서니 列이 10里나 뻐처서 길 가는 사람들이 죄다 발을 멈추고 「야-만키도 하다」하고 감탄을 마지 안슴니다. 그런 말을 들으니 엇지나 깃부든지요. 우리학교가 서울 여학교 중에 제일가는 것가티 생각이 들엇지오. 그래 어데서 꼭 한번 볼 듯한 도시의 한복판에 서잇는 굉장이 큰 공장에 들어가서 見學을 하는데 그 만흔 생도들이 한꺼번에 다 들어서서 구경을 함니다. 거긔는 수 만흔 女工들이 분주히 직조기계 압헤 매달여서 일을 하고 잇는데 자세히 보닛가 전부가 우리학교생도들이 안이겟슴닛가. 하도 이상하야 들여다보니 내 뒤에 列을 짓고 서잇슬 생도들이 감쭉가티 땅속으로 들어갓는지 한사람도 업슴니다. 그만 가슴이 두군거리고 엇지나 놀나운지 기계마다 달여가서 女工의 얼골들을 똑바로 처다보니 이일을 엇지함닛가. 우리학교생도들이 틀님업슴니다.
그 애들이 또 앙큼스럽게 아모 말도 하지안코 나를 보고 빙그레 웃기만 하겟지요. 그래서 엇떤 영문을 몰으고 속만 갑갑해 못견듸는데 저편 구석에 서잇든 심술사납게 생겨먹은 감독인지 한 자가 병아리 차러오는 솔개미 모양으로 우닥 대달여오드니 바로 내압헤 일을 하고잇든 우리학교 2년급인 女工의 팔을 움켜쥐고 왕방울가튼 목소래로 「웨 하품만하고 잇서! 일이 하기실우면 나갓소 조와!!」하고 그만 뺨을 치고 머리를 끄들고 차고 하는데 금방 때려죽일 것갓슴니다.
「이년석 어데 견듸여 보아라」 칼날가티 무섭게 외치고 그놈압헤 나선 나는 엇덧케 힘이 세든지 그냥 그자의 목덜미를 잡아쥐고는 쥐색기처럼 외팔로 깡중 처들엇다가 그대로 마당에다 태기지를 처바렷슴니다.
엇지나 마음이 상쾌하든지 30년 久滯가 하로 아츰에 나린 것갓슴니다. 그러다가 「앗차 이젓고나 앗가 몹시＜56＞ 어더맛든 그 女工은 엇지되엿노」하고 돌아다보니 발서 그 곳에는 공장도 女工도 생도도 아무것도 볼 수 업고 자리가 횡-하드니 몸부림을 치고 돌아눕는새 눈을 번쩍 떳슴니다. 꿈이지요. 나의게 무슨 그런 힘이 잇슬나구요.＜57＞
