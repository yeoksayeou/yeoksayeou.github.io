Using the "Dakkaki" as a Guide, All Journalists Set Out, A Record of the Night Exploration of Great Kyungsung, From Midnight 12 on November 7  
First Team Songjak, Seokchon  

Upon entering the new job, I was commanded to embark on a night exploration just two days after starting, yet I had no idea what my duties were around the Jung District. Following Songjak, who was tied by a rope like a pig, I unexpectedly found myself loitering for two hours in a shop, patiently waiting because of the winds from the fish sellers, then finally proceeded to my original destination, Gwanong-dong. Listening closely to the pounding sounds of the drums, my ears attuned only to the sounds around me, I continued my journey. Just as I reached the beginning of Gwanong-dong, I suddenly heard the pounding sounds. After some playful banter with Songjak, I ventured down an alley, where I met someone who seemed familiar. I introduced myself, explained our situation of night exploration, and asked to accompany him, which he readily accepted. We followed him through winding paths until we arrived at the beginning of Bongik-dong. In this curious setting, no one other than an innocent-looking person was to be found, as he was not knowledgeable about worthwhile places either, merely banging his merrily to and fro.

After roaming for an hour without success, I realized that further exploration would lead to more failure. Thus I excused myself at the Association of Completeness and left the “Dakkaki” behind, setting out with just Songjak for a true exploration. Guided by Songjak, we ducked into an alley behind Dong-eung-dong. Originally known for its sexual taverns, I had expected some raucous entertainment, but there too, the place was eerily silent, merely a few waitresses lingering about, with boredom evident. Progressing further brought us to the back alley behind the corner clothing store in Suwon-dong. As I navigated through another narrow path, I tripped over someone lying on the ground, and upon looking closer, noted that a beggar woman appeared to be dozing with a young child she was cradling. I felt immediate guilt but was unable to speak. However, Songjak, skilled with words, promptly apologized and gave her a gold coin, urging her to buy something for the child, prompting questions about her background and how she ended up as a beggar. 

The woman replied she was originally from Incheon and had married at twelve, becoming a widow at nineteen, eventually remarrying a man notorious for pillaging her possessions, which left her destitute with only this child. It turns out she recently remarried to a Chinese man working in a restaurant across the street, but he merely dictated her living conditions without supporting her needs, leaving her in distress. The description of her plight turned out far from the truth, showing her extreme disillusionment. We were hasty not to be fooled again, and I turned back toward Wongun-dong to investigate the dens of notorious pupils, but by then, I realized it was already three-thirty, sealing our failed night exploration with little more gained than this scant tale!  

Thus we exited the establishment of Wongun-dong and made our way toward the main street of Leongwong-dong, finally settling at a new food establishment known for its splendid meals. There, I noticed a woman in traditional Japanese clothing, hearing a raucous cheer from within. Hoping to explore while getting a drink, we entered to find five students sporting university caps tipsy and lewdly dancing. As we huddled in a corner, they tumbled out, the woman’s voice lamented how these people scorned their own homes and went around frivolously. 

I inquired of the woman how a Joseon woman came to wear such Japanese clothes and what her former occupation was. She, with a dramatic sigh, claimed she was a graduate of O-gi-dong School, and her life circumstances forced her to find employment in a Japanese store, leading her to don these clothes she still possessed. After sharing a cup of tea, I noted it was already three-something, and our bounty for the night amounted to this scarce revelation!  

**Summary**  

The article depicts the nighttime exploration in Kyungsung, showcasing both the excitement and disillusionment of the reporters as they encounter various aspects of life in the city. They meet a beggar woman who shares her heartbreaking story of hardship and exploitation. The narrative offers insights into the socio-economic struggles during the colonial period, emphasizing the plight of ordinary people caught in poverty and the complexities of identity, particularly regarding the adoption of Japanese customs by Koreans.

Historians may find this account revealing, as it provides a snapshot of daily life under colonial rule, reflecting the broader struggles of the time. The interactions and encounters demonstrate the impact of Japanese colonial policies on the social fabric of Korean society, illustrating the complicated dynamics between colonized and colonizers.

(83행 前略-원문)
못처럼 陵參奉을 하닛가 한달에 거동이 스물아홉번이라고 입사한지 단 2일에 야간탐방을 하라 명령하니 村鷄官廳으로 무엇이 무엇인지 알 수가 업다. 끈에 매달인 뒹박모양으로 松雀氏를 따러섯다가 칼치장사 쥐정 바람에 알지도 못하는 상점에서 두시간이나 팔자업는 간수노릇을 하고 다시 따러나와서 본래의 목적지인 勸農洞으로 갓다. 압못보는 장임이 북소리만 엿듯덧이 귀를 긔우리며 딱딱이 소리만 살펴드럿다. 한참 가면서 듯너라닛가 勸農洞 初入에서 마츰 딱딱 소리가 난다. 松雀氏와 가치 다름질을 처서 골목으로 드러갓다. 그 딱딱이를 언제나 아는 듯시 인사를 하고 우리의 야간탐방하는 사정을 말하고 동행을 청하니 그도 매우 좃타하고 승낙한다. 우리는 그 사람의 뒤를 따러 이리저리 도라다니는 것이 그럭저럭 鳳翼洞 初入까지 다다럿다. 그러나 어리친 개미 한마리 걸이지 안코 또 그 딱딱이도 아주 순진한 초대인 까닭에 엇던 곳이 가볼만한 곳인지 알지도 못하고 소경의 파밧두다리듯 딱딱이만 뚜다리고 다닐 뿐이다. 略 한시간이나 가치 단녀도 별수가 업는 것을 보와 더 다녀도 실패할 것을 짐작한 나는 團成社 압헤 와서는 딱딱이를 그냥 딱지식히고, 松雀氏와 단 두사람이 직접탐방의 길로 나섯다. 松雀氏의 인도로 敦義洞 뒤골목으로 드러섯다. 그곳은 원래 色酒家村인 까닭에 허다 못하야 쥐정꾼이라도 잇스랴니 하엿더니 그곳도 역시 博覽會 뒷장 모양으로 적적하기가 짝이 업고 다만 한둘의 酌婦가 행여나 지나가는 손임을 끄러볼가하고 올올뜰며서 문안깐에 지대 섯쓸뿐인데 情色이 참으로 可憐하야 뵈인다. 그럭저럭 가는 것이 授恩洞 角田洋服店 뒤골목까지 이르럿다. 깡감한 골목에서 압만 보고 가너라닛가 늬�엇인지 뭉쿨하고 발길에 채이며 아이구 소리가 낫다. 나는 깜작 놀나 살펴보니 엇던 乞人여자가 아희 한아를 안고 길가에서 곤하게 자다가 나의 발길에 잘못 걸이여서 잠을 깬 모양이다. 나는 엇지나 미안한지 밋처 말도 못하는데 말 잘부치는 松雀氏는 벌서 그에 대하야 미안하다고 말을 하고 一金拾錢을 내여 주며 배곱흔데 무엇이던지 사서 어린아희를 주라하고 그 여자의 來歷과 엇지하야 乞人이 된 것을 뭇는다. 그 여자 네-저는 본래 仁川사람으로 열두살에 시집을 갓다가  19세에 과부가＜30＞ 되고 또 改嫁를 한 것이 아주 란봉을 만나서 의복까지 다잽혀먹고 이 자식 한아만 원수로 냉겨 논 다음에 또 못된 병까지 傳染을 식혀주니 남의 집에 가서 雇傭도 못하겟고 그저 이럿케 거지노릇을 하고 단임니다. 그리고 요전에는 엇던 사람의 소개로 저건너 淸人料理집에 잇는 淸人에게 또 시집을 갓더니 그 놈이 날마다 집을 어더준다고만 하고 그저 이대로 두어 고생을 식힌다고 한다. 나는 요리집에 잇는 淸人이란 바람에 엇던 요리집 淸人인가하고 따저물은즉 웬걸요 그 여자의 수작은 참말과는 아주 딴판으로 콩팔칠팔하고 딴말을 한다. 여러분 웃지마시요. 그 여자는 알고보니 밋친 여자이다. 실패 대실패!(石村)
