저자	미제 (Unknown)  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

The Wonyang Hot Springs are now a well-known tourist attraction, but originally they were located in a very desolate village. During the reign of King Sejo of the Joseon Dynasty, while he was visiting the Mount Suri area to express gratitude, he stopped at Wonyang on his way back. A white heron flew past him, and he shot it with his bow. When he pursued it, he found it had fallen into the grass, and to his surprise, there was a hot spring with steam rising from it. The king, who had suffered from swelling ailments, was overjoyed and took a bath in the hot spring, which healed his condition completely. The king attributed the discovery of the hot spring to the white heron's intervention and spread the news of his healed injury, leading Wonyang Hot Springs to become famous worldwide.

  
**Summary**  

This article recounts the historical discovery of the Wonyang Hot Springs by King Sejo of Joseon, illustrating how a chance encounter with a heron led to the finding of a region that would later become renowned for its therapeutic properties. It highlights the intersection of natural wonder and royal favor, providing insights into the socio-cultural significance of natural landmarks in Korean history, especially during the colonial period.

溫陽溫泉의 첫發見
溫陽溫泉이라하면 지금은 누구나 모를 사람이 업시 큰 명소가 되엿지만은 본래에는 아주 蕭條한 일개 寒村이엿다. 李朝世祖大王이 報恩俗離山에 行幸하엿다가 歸路에 溫陽에 駐v蹕하얏는데 공중으로 백로 한마리가 날러감으로 활로 쏘왓더니 그 백로가 左翼을 마저서 풀 속에 떠러짐으로 쫏차가서 본즉 뜻박게 김이 물윽물윽 나는 온천이 잇는지라 例의 腫患으로 항상 고생을 하던 世祖는 크게 반가워 하야 그 온천에서 목욕을 하니 腫患이 快癒하얏다. 世祖는 그 온천을 발견하게 된 것을 백로의 덕이라 하고 그 中矢한 상처을 치료하야 放送하니 이로부터 溫陽溫泉이 세상에 유명하게 되엿다.
