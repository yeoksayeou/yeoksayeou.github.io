기사제목	無知의 苦痛과 설넝湯신세, 新舊家庭生活의 長點과 短點 (The Pain of Ignorance and the Situation of Sullungtang, The Strengths and Weaknesses of New and Old Family Life)  
필자	朴O熙  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)

The terms "new family" and "old family" are often spoken of casually, but I believe they cannot be easily distinguished. A new family should imply that the couple has a reasonable grasp of common sense about the world and is utilizing all modern conveniences as much as possible to lead a simple, economical, and cultured life. An old family, on the other hand, suggests they are mired in old morals, perhaps smoking long pipes or consuming opium, reflecting a bygone era. Even if one simply marries a woman with braids, it could be considered a new family, and if a man wields a short cane and takes his wife to the movies, that too can be seen as a new family. Where, then, is the true essence of a new family? Overall, what we often refer to as a new family is merely a transitional temporary state that possesses neither genuine new elements nor truly old ones, indicative of a life that is just a facade. This may sound harsh, but it is said that those who constitute a new family will eat two bowls of 설렁탕 (sullungtang) daily. Why? Because young couples, when newly married, dream sweet dreams and, while their finances are adequate, dine out often. However, when money is limited, they tend to eat what is easiest, perhaps ordering 설렁탕 after a lazy morning. After their meal, they might put on some makeup and eventually wander to watch a show or take a stroll in the park, returning home late in the evening. It is during this time that they cannot prepare home-cooked meals and often opt for the convenience of purchased 설렁탕. Thus, the notion of consuming two bowls of 설렁탕 every day captures the essence of a certain type of new family.

This new family, regardless of its shortfalls, is indeed a reflection of individual moral decay, the destruction of family bonds, and societal harm, yet this aspect of the so-called new family cannot simply be dismissed. An old family, then, signifies the ignorance of the first wife. Often lacking education, such women remain waiting at home, led astray by matchmakers, or they fulfill their mother-in-law's desire for a comfortable life or their father-in-law's expectation to see grandchildren, thus succumbing to societal pressures. The notion of marriage itself becomes a painful sacrifice of the bride's individual identity to satisfy the desires of two parents. Even those possessing some knowledge may find it impossible to establish an ideal living situation amidst such unreasonable unions. It is a significant demand on any woman, let alone one who knows nothing. Previously, strict morals governed these unions, compelling individuals to endure suffering, but in today's age of liberal thought, various disturbances inevitably arise. Even if one happens to endure an unreasonable marriage, domestic unhappiness cannot be avoided, and the greatest pain lies in ignorance. While some assert there is nothing more fearful than ignorance, the reality is, there is indeed nothing more challenging than ignorance—for one cannot fully comprehend ignorance. When faced with difficult circumstances, individuals often blindly accept their fate, but this uncritical compliance can also be seen as a form of virtue. Yet, isn’t such servitude an insult to one's humanity? If one were treated merely as a machine to fulfill desires or reduced to a slave whose well-being matters little, there would be no issue. However, to grow as companions who share joys and sorrows is rather a heavy burden.

Nonetheless, one can also discover various strengths within old families that may not exist in new families. In a society where industry has not developed and division of labor is not fully established, each household in Korea must produce all necessary goods. While clothing may be purchased, food must generally be prepared at home. Items such as doenjang (된장), ganjang (간장), kimchi, and the labor involved in laundry and fabric care fall squarely on the shoulders of women, alongside the tremendous responsibility of child-rearing until they come of age without imposing undue burden on their husbands. It could be argued that this capacity for self-reliance is a unique strength of old families.

Conversely, what of the so-called new family? Many believe that a new family should be a relationship of understanding between individuals, wherein married couples can not only secure happiness but also contribute positively to family and society. Ideally, this should be the case, and if it were not so, the very notion of a new family would be meaningless. Let us sift through the underlying issues of the new family. In fact, it has its own strengths not found in old families. Husbands may defy exhaustion from work and put effort into offering kind words of comfort. They might press their creased suits, wash their shirts, or provide clean clothes without them needing to visit a laundromat. One can even see embroidery that the wife made during school days displayed around the home, along with children’s simple clothes she can create with little more than some thread. Reading the newspaper and joining conversations about world affairs becomes possible, and should there be a wedding, an invite to accompany one’s spouse lends a sense of honor. Yet, on the flip side, with all these difficulties, the household must still rely on a woman to cook meals. Typically, a maid may be required, and if a child is born, there must at least be a wet nurse or a young girl to assist in child-rearing. 

While husbands may not wear formal attire, wives often find themselves incapable of sewing their own dresses or finding the motivation to perform tasks as domesticity demands, even as they must regularly attend social events like music concerts or theaters—obligations that hint at societal pressures. Remarks about a woman being ill-dressed even in our modern age carry weight, and the appearance of a warm winter coat becomes a necessity, causing financial strain on the husband, thus creating burdensome situations. Meals consist of unappetizing kimchi, devoid of flavor, while what’s deemed high culinary art on the table may consist of poorly-cut, low-quality beef mixed with basic ingredients. 

In light of these realities, what value does the idea of a new family hold? Nevertheless, it is unfair to attribute all these errors solely to the new women of society. The surrounding circumstances play a significant role as the overall level of knowledge remains limited, and societal conditions allow little room for genuine progress. Husbands should fulfill their responsibilities, and wives theirs as well, but the weight of responsibility on modern women is far too great. At some point, they take on the roles of preparing fermented condiments, raising children, handling laundry, and supporting their husbands while also engaging socially. If they were financially secure, it might be manageable, but how could one live a new family lifestyle if the surrounding environment does not permit it? Ultimately, both the new and old families have their strengths and weaknesses, and just as the development of society is underway, we must accept that both constructs are still evolving and cannot be quickly dismissed or glorified.

**Summary**

This article discusses the contrasting definitions and characteristics of new and old families in Korean society during the late 1920s. It presents a critical view of how societal expectations and economic realities burden both old and new family structures, highlighting ignorance, financial strain, and unequal responsibilities as significant issues. Historians may find it particularly interesting how this reflection dives into the roles and perceptions of women within the family, illustrating the tensions of modernization against traditional practices, while revealing the struggles of families in colonial Korea.

우리가 想像하는 신가정이라는 것이 이래서야 무슨 의의가 잇겟슴닛가. 그러타고 이 모든 과오를 신여성에게만 돌일 수가 업슴니다. 그것은 모든 주위의 정세가 그러한 것과 가티 일반적으로 智識의 정도가 淺薄하고 사회적 조건이 허락치 안는데야 엇지하겟슴닛가. 남편된 사람은 남편된 책임만 다하고 처자된 사람은 처자된 책임을 다 해야만 할 것인데 現今 가정여자에게는 너무도 책임이 중하다고 생각함니다. 어느 틈에 된장 간장을 담ㅅ고 어린애 길으고 빨내 바느질하고 그리고 남편의 할일을 도으며 與人接客을 하람닛가. 그도 돈에 여유나 만하서 하나에서부터 열가지까지 마음대로 된다면 모르지만은 주위가 허락지 안는 것을 마음으로만 신가정적 생활을 하랴면 될 수가 잇겟슴닛가. 또 대체 신가정이고 구가정이고 其特長과 缺點이 잇는 동시에 사회의 모든 것이 그러한 것과 가티 주위의 발전과정을 따러서 점차로 발달되아 나가기를 기다릴 수밧게 업슬 줄 암니다. 지금 안저서는 구가정이라고 그러케 배척할 것도 안이고 신가정이라고 그러케 환영할 것도 업겟지오.＜29＞
