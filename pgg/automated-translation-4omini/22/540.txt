The Moon and Sentimentalism  
……………I send this poem to the Moon Lake………

My heart……  
I had not been shaken even by the totality of guns and knives  
Why does it shake so much tonight!  
It is as if I have met an old lover  
Without laughing  
Without being angry  
Oh, the moon, just gazing down at me through the moonlight…  

In the clear face of the moon, I draw the branches of memories  
My heart that cannot sleep!  
With the dew drops resting on the grass, weeping,  
My heart that cannot weep!  
It does not even know how far the road is;  
That half-light only goes towards the moon,  
My heart that cannot go!  
Ah, seeing the moon, I want to sing a song loudly, but my head is groaning!  

Oh, weary heart! But sleep now  
Before the dawn breaks tomorrow  
Isn't your suffering heavy here on this land!?  
Before this day brightens  
Shed all the bullets left in your heart! Sharpen the sword!  
Before the dawn breaks tomorrow  
Isn't your suffering heavy here on this land!?  
-1929.09.15-<111>  

**Summary**  

This poem reflects deep emotional turmoil where the speaker grapples with memories of love and the burdens of existence in their contemporary reality. The moon serves as a poignant symbol of nostalgia and unfulfilled desires, contrasting with the speaker's weariness and contemplations about suffering. Historians might find interest in this poem's expression of personal anguish against the backdrop of colonial Korea, revealing insights into the emotional landscape of individuals during this tumultuous period.
