The Four Seasonal Delicacies: Pyongyang Cold Noodles, Treasures, Famous Products, and World-Famous Foods  
						Kim So-jae  

Spring  
As the spring breeze gently blows, the peonies bloom on Mokdan Mountain, and the trees sprout new leaves in the 3rd and 4th month, filled with the thrill of spring. I carry my weary body to the second floor of Dae Dong Gate, where I enjoy a hearty bowl of cold noodles, refreshing in the flowing waters of the Pahang River after a long day!  

Summer  
Under the influence of the mainland, the heat rises considerably during summer, and when the heat is intense in Pyongyang, there is nothing like cold noodles hidden beneath a pristine white bowl. The coldness relieves the summer heat, while the mustard and sourness shake off any heaviness!  

Autumn  
After years of longing for dear friends, when we meet by Pacheong, as the autumn rain sweeps down from the willow trees by Yeongra Island, reflecting on old memories while sharing our hearts, the intertwined cold noodles echo the difficulties of their friendship!  

Winter  
Just as Koreans often miss kimchi when abroad, Pyongyang residents have a profound longing for one particular flavor when they are away from home—this is the taste of cold noodles in winter. As the snow falls thickly outside, I hear my mother's soft voice inside, speaking of the Three Kingdoms. As I gradually see words blurring, her voice quiets, and I hear a loud voice proclaiming, "Noodles—" as she opens the door to bring in handmade cold noodles. Drizzle in rich kimchi soup with floating bits of fermented kimchi, mix with chopsticks, and enjoy—what a taste! Have those who haven't tasted Pyongyang cold noodles ever imagined!  

Summary  

This article praises Pyongyang cold noodles as a mainstay of Korean cuisine throughout the seasons, symbolizing comfort and nostalgia. It paints vivid images of enjoying cold noodles in different seasons while highlighting the strong connection to culture and home. This reflection during the colonial period emphasizes the importance of local culinary traditions as a source of identity and belonging amidst colonial pressures. Historians may find this narrative particularly interesting as it captures the socio-cultural landscape of Korea during this time, showcasing how food can serve as a means of cultural resilience.
