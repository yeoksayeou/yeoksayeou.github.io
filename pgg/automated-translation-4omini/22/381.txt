Summary  

This article announces the results of a prize contest from the previous issue of the magazine, highlighting the exceptional number of participants and detailing the winners. The significant response from the public reflects the magazine's popularity and the engagement of the readers during a time of great change in Korea. Historians may find the sheer number of participants indicative of a growing interest in cultural exchanges and print media during the Japanese colonial period, as well as they might analyze the socio-political contexts motivating such large participation.
