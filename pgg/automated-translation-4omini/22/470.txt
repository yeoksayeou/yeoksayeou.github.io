別의別乾坤  
◇乞人像防器  
In Amsterdam, a man named Zinke has applied for a new patent for a peculiar super bell that will not sound unless a one-cent copper coin is placed inside. It is said to be an ideal new product for repelling beggars.

  
◇Silk Hat First Generation  
The silk hat made its appearance in the world 132 years ago, in 1797. A hat seller named Sarington from London bravely wore the silk hat as he walked the streets, causing such astonishment that one noblewoman fainted. Consequently, the general public complained, saying, "It certainly must be something to give us a fright, so large and shiny!" The municipal magistrate charged Sarington with "instigating the public and disturbing public order," imposing a fine of 1,000 yen and declaring that he could no longer wear the hat.

  
◇Mobile Bathhouse  
Recently in England, a mobile bathhouse has been established that is equipped with bathing tubs, dressing tables, and sleeping rooms. It is a traveling bathhouse. The water in the bath is heated by the automobile's engine, and as it goes around, the vendors call out like in Korea, "Bathing! Bathing!"  

 
◇Thus, the Moon  
People from Dakota, America believe that dogs eat the moon. Polynesians think that the spirits of the dead eat the moon. Capillarians believe that when the moon presses down on their heads, they place their hands on their foreheads. Eskimos say that the moon goes to eat something because it is hungry. Which one is correct?  

 
◇New Style Bank Clerk  
At a large bank in England, a new clerk has been employed who operates a machine that counts money and records in the ledger. It is astonishing. If an overdraft occurs in the current account, the machine automatically stops, and only a piece of paper with the overdraft amount printed in block letters comes out.

  
◇Deaths by Animals  
In 1927, more than 22,000 people died in British India due to animals that year. Breaking it down further: ▲ 1,033 from tigers ▲ 218 from leopards ▲ 465 from jackals ▲ 78 from bears ▲ 56 from crocodiles ▲ 136 from alligators ▲ 85 from wild boars ▲ 48 from wild dogs ▲ 19,724 from poisonous snakes. Additionally, the counts of those animals includes ▲ 1,368 tigers ▲ 490 leopards ▲ 2,738 bears ▲ 2,439 jackals ▲ 57,116 poisonous snakes. <95>

  
**Summary**

This article presents various bizarre inventions and oddities from around the world, including a bell designed to repel beggars, the historical debut of silk hats, and a mobile bathhouse. It touches upon cultural perceptions of the moon and introduces modern banking technology. Of particular interest in the context of Korean colonial history is the mention of how British India’s animal-related deaths highlight the complex relationships between humans and wildlife during the colonial period, reflecting on the impact of colonial rule on indigenous populations and natural surroundings.

◇씰크해트 第一世
씰크해트가 그 姿態를 世上에나타내이기는 지금으로부터 一百三十二年前 西紀 一七九七年이다.
콘돈의 帽子장사 사링톤이라는 사람이 씰크햇트 發明하여가지고 勇敢하게 길거리로 쓰고돌아다니는데보는사람들의 놀내인것은어느 貴婦人하나가 氣絶을한것으로만도 證明을 할 수가잇다. 그리하야 一般市民은『굉장하게 키가크고 새맛게 번쩍번쩍하는것이 확실히우리를 놀네게하기위한것』이라고 불평을부르지젓다.
그리하야 治安判事는 사링톤에게
『民衆을 선동하고 治安을 妨害하엿다』
는 罪名으로 午千圓의 罰金을 물니는 同時에 다시는 그 帽子를쓰지못하도록 宣告하엿다.
◇移動沐浴집
요지음 英國에는 沐浴통 化粧臺 寢室等을 設備한 自動車가생겻다. 결어다니는 沐浴집이다.
沐浴물은 自動車의소린 불로데우고 거리로다니면서 朝鮮의『신기려』장사가『신기우려!』하듯이『沐浴허려!』하고 돌아다닌다고한다.

어느것이 正確?
◇新式銀行員
英國어느 大銀行에서는 새로운 銀員을 雇揷하엿는데돈도세이고 帳薄도 記入하는 機械라니 놀라운일이다.
當座預金의 貸越이생기면 自動的으로 機械의  運神이 停止되고 貸越金額만 불근글자로벡힌 조각만나온다고한다.
