잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1929년 12월 01일  
기사제목	키쓰의 별악, 夢夢夢 (Kiss's Strange Evil, Dream Dream Dream)  
필자	南柯生  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

In a newspaper company, I cannot tell which one it is and I went to a place that looked quite different from my current life, a place resembling a café, not a newspaper office, a so-called editorial office. 

I exclaimed "Oh!" as I sat at the table, feeling like I was about to be served a meal. While waiting for the waitress to come, the situation unexpectedly flipped. Rather than a newspaper staff, a bunch of ill-mannered reporters, not just one but around ten of them, burst out into the scene. 

Since I would never dream of being rude, I politely stood up and greeted them in a very Western manner. Immediately, laughter erupted from all directions, like a flock of chicks making a ruckus. I had no idea what was going on and looked around, only to feel a poke from behind. 

Turning around, I found a woman giving me a seductive glance. I was so startled that I couldn't respond to her charming gaze when yet another lady from behind playfully tugged at my sleeve. Again, I turned around to see her blinking at me curiously while I felt another series of playful pokes from behind. 

I felt like a cat caught in a mouse trap, trying to figure out where to look. Back and forth, I was in a state of confusion, when one fellow, after a little flirting, turned around and nonchalantly walked away, leaving me bewildered. 

I was hardly able to suppress my frustration and yelled "You foolish brutes!" only to be met with more teasing laughter that tickled my senses. I was dumbfounded, staring blankly around me, not knowing what to make of the situation and how obsessed I had become with their teasing. I felt like I would be caught like a treasonous criminal with no defense. 

Despite how much I shouted, the playful jests kept coming, and eventually, a wild burst of laughter that seemed endless continued. I felt desperate and jumped up only to find my head bumping into the desk and waking me from the dream. 

The next day, I found myself experiencing a bout of misfortune, but considering that I, the forsaken one, had tasted a lavishly imagined life that would be unattainable to me even in a hundred years, I was indeed grateful to the dream for that moment.  

**Summary**  

This article illustrates a humorous and surreal dream experience of the author in an amusing café-like setting, where he finds himself caught among playful women who tease him. It reflects the author’s thoughts on societal norms, flirtation, and confusion that men often faced in romantic pursuits during the colonial period. Historians might find it interesting to analyze how such whimsical portrayals reveal underlying frustrations and escapes from the harsh realities of life during Japanese occupation.
