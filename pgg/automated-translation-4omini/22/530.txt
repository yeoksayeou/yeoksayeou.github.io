필자	로아 (Ro-a)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Ascending to the Phoenix Terrace  
		— Ro-a  

Upon the Phoenix Terrace, covered in fallen leaves,  

I ascend, embracing the fading moon shadow beyond the ancient tomb.  

The grasses are swaying, lamenting my presence.  

×  

Night, in this night sea, mingled with dew and tears, is the cup from yesterday,  

And amidst that, the suffering bodies of the unfortunate... alas, I close my eyes against that sight.  

×  

At the ten thousand households of Chang'an, the cries of the mournful rapids, the sound of hooves resound,  

The unfortunate prince, wandering tirelessly by the wild goose pond all night!  

Even rain may stain the old robe's hem with blood tears.  

×  

Oh, the news from the past... who shall utter it! As I gather my thoughts and lift my eyes,  

The night light glimmers over the woolen house.  

The wrathful spirit of the giant stirs and sways like a plea from the joyous house.  

×  

Oh, to whom shall this land be confided?  

Shall someone be asleep now? What distress remains!  

With grains gone, only fallen leaves remain this autumn, this night,  

Unable to endure...  

I long to cry out!  

(From Gyeongju, in old manuscripts)  

  

**Summary**  

The poem captures the melancholy of a night spent at the Phoenix Terrace, filled with references to personal suffering and social despair amidst fading memories of the past. It reflects the emotions of despair and longing as the author mourns the loss of joy and connection to the land during a time of distress. Historians may find interest in the reflections on misfortune and the expressions of identity and cultural connection during the colonial period, illustrating the complex realities of individuals caught within colonization's shadow.
