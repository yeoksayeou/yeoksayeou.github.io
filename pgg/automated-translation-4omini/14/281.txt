Amazing Printing Technique

Observe how much printing techniques have progressed in Korea by viewing the three-color photograph on the cover of this issue of "Children" (어린이) (attached with a ten-won supplement) <129>  

 
**Summary**  

This article highlights the advancements in printing technologies in Korea during the late 1920s, specifically showcasing a colorful cover of a children's magazine as a testament to these improvements. The mention of a supplement suggests an effort to entice readership and improve the overall quality of publications. Historians might find it interesting as it reflects a period of modernization and the influences of Western printing techniques in Japanese-occupied Korea, indicating a shift in cultural and commercial practices during colonization.
