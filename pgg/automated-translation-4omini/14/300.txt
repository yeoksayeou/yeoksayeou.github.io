Secret Correspondence Among Old Maids Who Do Not Marry  
by Female Detective  

It is a fact that among the so-called new women nowadays, many remain unmarried even past the age of thirty-two. They seem to live indifferent to the idea of marriage, acting as if they resolve to continue living alone forever without caring about getting married. When a friend brings up the subject of marriage, they react as if it is an absurd notion, which is quite indicative of their mindset. At first glance, it may appear that they have noble ambitions and exceptional ideals, leading them to choose a single life. However, it is prescient to consider whether their inner thoughts are severely deteriorated or if they harbor unspoken worries or have some particular circumstances preventing them from marrying. 

Though I am often approached to discuss these matters, I must confess that my understanding of the single lives of these women is limited. Thankfully, I have in my possession some correspondence exchanged among several unmarried women I am acquainted with, so I will share these letters without concealing any details as observational material for everyone here. 

Rather than elaborately explaining their lives with a thousand words, I believe that letters exchanged among them will more effectively convey their everyday experiences without leaving anything unsaid. It is surprising how they confide in their letters with an intense candidness that one would not expect from them in public.

Now, I will introduce a few excerpts from these forthright letters, omitting some particularly explicit passages.


1

<133> My beloved S,  
The restless rain continues to fall throughout the night. I pick at my dinner half-heartedly and sulk in my room alone since morning, feeling increasingly melancholic, longing for the dreary rain to fall even longer. In the kitchen, the aging mother is clattering around cleaning up after supper, and my brother has put the child to bed since early evening. 

It feels as if everyone is waiting for the right time to fall asleep, and on rainy days, I feel even more impatient for night to come. S, as soon as the mother finishes cleaning, and returns to her room, it will feel like the world is sealed off, and only the rain and I will be awake. I find myself rubbing my head against my desk, feeling small enough to weep. In this empty space, I cannot find anywhere to place my troubled spirit while listening to the rain. Memories of past foolish dreams crowd my head and become more unbearable.

S, on a rainy day like this, don’t you remember how we used to blame each other for not visiting? Are you there alone, feeling sorrowful in your own home, bothered by the patter of rain? Oh, lonely S, don’t just cry; come over to my place. Before this long rainy night ends, let us be together and fortify each other's lonely hearts against this desolate night. 

Hoping for your arrival on this rainy night,  
My dear sister,  
X  X  X  


2

Dear Brother,  
It seems the final agreement has fallen apart. Why do men think this way? Even if they say they cannot remarry, how can they claim to love at all yet refuse to marry? If only I could think of it as just a fantasy feelings and move on, but I cannot—this is not mere deception, and I know he loved me as well. It’s been my misfortune that I can no longer attach myself to someone devoid of affection. 

Oh, brother, I am caught in an emotional whirlwind! I have firmly decided that if a woman resolutely refuses a man’s love, he will surely be unsatisfied. Oh dear brother! If you permit me to remain single as you do, I too shall vow to remain forever unwed. I have no intention of marrying anyone else. I will come by this weekend.  

Yours,  
OO’s younger sister  


3

Dear O,  
Why is it that I cannot see you these days? Have you developed a romantic attachment that makes you forsake us? It seems you are neglecting your friends at this point. Ha ha, I miss you greatly since I haven't seen you in a while. 

In our solitary lives, if we don’t meet even among ourselves, I feel my heart tormented by sorrow. Last night, we gathered at K’s residence. After my farewell to H, while K and P were there, friendly discussions unfolded with more plans for the future. K is planning to become a lawyer, while P intends to study medicine. Yet, they seem less concerned about those futures than about trading secrets. With four of us, we gather but without plans, waiting for opportunities to be serendipitous. 

My roommate and I may seem like true old maids, but when we are together it feels like we are all just young girls. Still, while harboring similar thoughts, we pretend to be oblivious, laughing and playing games until nightfall, and then we part ways, leaving behind that pleasant feeling of loneliness among us. Oh, “the sorrow of wishing for something and never obtaining it” as M wrote in her letter makes me feel tearful. 

I will stop here. Please come and visit.  
April 28, late night  


4

Dear OO,  
Have you heard? D is engaged! It’s quite shocking, isn’t it? Moreover, she is marrying that old clod. I was stunned at first, thinking it must be a lie. I mean, how could she marry a man who is not only old but has children and grandchildren of his own? 

Considering just recently, she had been courting a doctor. Now looking back, I suspect the rumors circulating about her and C and K involving nighttime visits to his home and eating and drinking, and indulging in all sorts of disorders might hold some truth. Therefore, how long do you think she can endure living with that old man? You should recommend she break her engagement now; she might as well enjoy the freedom of a single life instead.  
(End of text)

**Summary**  

This article explores the hidden thoughts and emotions of unmarried women residing in colonial Korea in the late 1920s, detailing their friendships and correspondences. These women, anchored by societal expectations yet yearning for personal connections, often grapple with the solitude that comes with being single. The letters reveal a candid perspective on their emotional struggles and defiance against traditional marriage norms, showcasing a moment in Korean history when women began to voice their desires and challenges more openly. Historians may find the discussion of women's autonomy and the challenges faced in a patriarchal society of particular interest.

조흔 일인지 조치 못한 일인지......엇잿든지 요사이 소위 신녀성들 중에는 나희 삼십이 넘도록 결혼하지 안는 이가 만허지는 것이 사실임니다.
그러고 그들은 겨테서 보기에 『결혼을 해야겟는데』하는 생각도 안하고 영영 결혼을 니저버리고 살 사람처럼 무관심하게 지내면서 동모가 혹시 결혼 비관 이약이를 걸면 못할 소리를 한 것처럼 특명스럽게 핀잔을 줌니다. 여긔까지는 훌륭한 포부와 남다른 리상과 주견이 잇서서 독신생활을 결심한 것갓치 보임니다. 만은 사실로 것흐로 보이는 바와 가티 그들의 속생각이 단단이 구더진 것이 잇는지 남모르는 고민을 갓지나 안는지 또는 독신생활을 아니하지 못할 무슨 특별한 사정이 잇는지 그것을 고찰할 필요가 잇다고 잘 알아서 쓰라 하는 부탁이지만 나는 그럿케 만히 독신생활을 하는 이를 알지도 못하고 또 더러 안대야 그들의 생활을 엇더케 여실히 잘 그려내일 재조가 업슴니다. 다행이 내가 직접 간접으로 아는 독신 녀자 몃 사람들 사이에 내왕하는 편지를 손에 가진 것이 잇스니 그것을 일홈만 숨기고 여긔에 여러분의 고찰 재료로 제공하겟슴니다.
천 마듸 만 마듸 말로 그들의 생활을 장황이 이약이하는 것보다 그들 자긔내들끼리만 속을 주고 긔맥을 통하는 동모들 사이에 족곰도 은휘함이 업시 주고 밧고 하는 편지가 가장 잘 그들의 생활을 말하는 것인 까닭임니다.
보시면 놀나실 일이나 남의 압헤서 우슴의 말도 잘하지 안을 듯하게 꽁하고 얌전한 이도 『과부와 가튼 사정』이랄까요- 자긔네들 사이에 주고 밧는 편지는 이외에 맹렬하게 로골한 토설을 하는 것이 다른 이들의 짐작도 못하는 일임니다.
이제 그 중에 넘어 로골한 구절을 빼이고 몃 가지를 들어 소개하겟슴니다.

一

<133>사랑하는 S야
심란한 비가 밤이 들도록 오는 구나. 먹는 둥 마는 둥 저녁밥을 맛치고 내방에 도라와 혼자 안젓스니 아침부터 울울한 심사가 무심한 비ㅅ발에 울고 십게 처량하고나. 부엌에서는 성남 어머니(오라비댁)가 덜걱덜걱 설거지를 하고 잇고 옵바는 초저녁부터 까라 노은 자리에 성남이를 재우고 누엇다.
모든 사람이 잘 시간을 기다리는 것 갓고 비 오는 날일수록 밤들기를 더 기다리는 것 갓고나.
S야. 성남 어머니도 속히 설거지를 맛치고 자긔 방으로 도라가면 방마다 문이 닷치고 비와 나만이 깨여 잇게 되겟구나. 책상에 얼골을 부비면서 작고만 울고 십다. 대일 데 업고 붓칠 곳 업는 이 마음을 아모럿케나마 위로할 것이 업서 휘덩그럿케 뷔인 방안에 뷔인 가삼을 안고 비 소리를 드르니 어렷슬 때 헛되히 꿈꾸든 왼갓 일이 지긋지긋이 머리에 떠돌아 점점 더 심하게만 되는고나.
S야. 이럿케 비 오는 날이면 『하도 젹젹하기에 놀러왓지』하고 달려들 때까지 웨 오지 안느냐. 실혀도 로처녀로 행세하게 된 우리 사이에 너라고 이제 와서 나 모를 자미가 생겻슬 리 업고 너의 집 그 뒷간 엽방에 네 몸이 혼자 안저서 구즌 비에 마음 상하는 모양이 보이노나.
아아 외로운 동생S야. 혼자서 울지만 말고 나한데로 놀러 오려므나. 비 오는 밤이 더 깁기 전에 와서 또 가티 외로운 몸을 OOOOO OOOOO OOOOO고나 외로운 불 미테 외로운 두 몸을 나란히 누이고 슬푼 혼을 쉬여나 보잣고나.

엇재서 남자의 맘은 그럴까요. 암만해도 리혼까지는 할 수 업다고 나종에는 잘러 말하니 웨 사랑하는 맘은 업다고 하면서 리혼은 못하는지요. 이때까지 내가 속에서 감정의 희롱을 바든 것이라고나 생각되면 싸홈을 하던지 분푸리나 하고 말겟는데 그래도 내가 속은 것이 아니고 그의 내게 대한 사랑이 거즛이 아니엿든 것은 미더지는 고로 단렴되지도 안코 상처바든 가삼이 압흐기만 함니다. 용긔야요 마음 착한 그에게 용긔하나 업는 탓이야요. 분명이 실혀하면서 떼여버리지 못하니 용긔가 업는 것이 아니고 무엇이겟슴닛가. 아아 형님 그는 용긔가 업서 못 떼인다 하고 나는 엇지하면 조켓슴닛가.
