잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1928년 08월 01일  
기사제목	民謠, 님의 배 (Folk Song, My Beloved's Boat)  
필자	劉道順  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

When I look at the sea from the mountain every day,  

the vast waves stretch north and south for a hundred miles,  

the boat my beloved is on has no news once again,  

and for three years I have been lonely in the river village.  

  

Ah, oh! I hear the sound of the oars,  

the rhythm floats in the air,  

the small fishing boats are all coming in,  

but why isn't my beloved's boat coming?  

  

For two or three years, I have wished for your return,  

and I shall welcome you home in fine robes.  

The boat has left for a thousand miles beyond the barriers,  

but today in the sea, I only see white cranes.  

  

Ah, oh! I hear the sound of the oars,  

the rhythm floats in the air,  

the small fishing boats all come in,  

but why isn't my beloved's boat coming?  

  

The evening sun sets over the mountain's ridge,  

and colorful clouds float in the sky.  

Where is the revered sacred mountain and grand temple?  

For my beloved, I shall pray for your safe return.  

  

Ah, oh! I hear the sound of the oars,  

the rhythm floats in the air,  

the small fishing boats all come in,  

but why isn’t my beloved’s boat coming? <1>  

  

**Summary**  

The poem expresses the longing and sorrow of a person waiting for their beloved who is away at sea. The speaker reflects on the passage of time, longing for reunion while observing the beauty of nature around them—a stark contrast to their feeling of isolation. This piece is particularly poignant within the context of Korean colonial history as it touches upon themes of separation, yearning, and the enduring hope for reunion, reflecting the emotional landscape of Koreans during a time of loss and displacement.
