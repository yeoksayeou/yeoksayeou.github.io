잡지명	별건곤 제46호  
발행일	1931년 12월 01일  
기사제목	노래하는 死刑囚 (The Singing Death Row Prisoner)  
필자	楊相浩 (Yang Sang-ho)  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)  

The region along the Heilongjiang (黑龍江) River in Northern Manchuria had long been famous for its sand gold (砂金), particularly the gold mines at Guando (觀都), Makha (漠河), Qigan (奇乾), and Yoqing (餘慶) which all boasted an impressive 80 percent quality of gold output in vast quantities.

Now, the story originates from a gold mine located in Taepyeonggou (太平溝), under the jurisdiction of the Guando gold mining authority. The miners (採金夫) working in these mines are strictly ruled by the chiefs of the “coolie” system (쿨리), and according to the regulations of the mining authority, all extracted sand gold must be sold to the mine at an official price, and they are strictly prohibited from selling privately. The division of the profits from one liang of sand gold is referred to as “saryuk bungeum” (사륙분금) where 4 parts go to the mine and 6 parts go to the miner.

However, due to their ignorance, the miners are never satisfied with this 6-part share and often attempt to smuggle the gold, sometimes swallowing it whole or hiding it away to escape and flee at the right opportunity without little equal risk. However, this escape is fraught with difficulty and danger. Until they risk their lives, it is inconceivable to think of escaping. Even if they manage to deceive the watchful eyes and escape, the methods are limited. They must traverse the rugged mountainous terrain surrounding the gold mine to reach some location along the Heilongjiang River. Nevertheless, reaching that destination is rife with obstacles. There are deep and dense forests that have not been stepped into for hundreds or thousands of years, and once they stray in that direction, they could end up wandering forever in the lingering mountains. Furthermore, fierce predators like tigers and wolves lurk in the nights, ready to pounce and make them their prey. Is that all? No. There is another looming threat: a ferocious type of bandit (馬賊) that roams these terrifying mountains, hunting down gold mine escapees.

The protagonist of this story is also one such bandit who, having previously worked as a miner in Taepyeonggou, knows well the escape routes of the fugitives and, awaiting their escape, committed cruel acts, resulting in countless miners being sacrificed until he was caught and sentenced to death.

However, more intriguing and tragic than the fact that this bandit received the death penalty is his natural ability to sing beautifully and his skill in singing. The prison guards were captivated by his voice. Eventually, the sound of his singing reached the ears of the head jailer (典獄). The jailer, known for being a connoisseur of culture and a drinker, became profoundly moved after hearing his performance and devised a strange plan for the death row inmate to perform while providing him guidance. Upon command, the plans were set in motion.

Facing impending execution, the bandit trembled in despair yet agreed to be the star of the day and sang of his past life. The song's content reflected something like this:

“Years have passed since I left my hometown in Shandong,  
Wishing to take golden sands from the Heilongjiang River  
To my parents, brothers, and my beloved,  
I left with courage, determined to journey forth.  
But behold! Dragged by the coolie chief in Harbin,  
I floated down the Songhua River to the confluence of the three rivers,  
Climbing up the winding Heilongjiang.  
In Taepyeonggou, the golden sands I sought,  
Were not mined as I wished,  
And before I knew it,  
I couldn’t resist the temptation of good spirits and sweet spots,  
I lost sight of my body and soul,  
And fled the gold mine in stealth.  
Now unable to return to my hometown,  
I must lie in the midst of the forest,  
Living like a ghost until death,  
What a pitiful state this is!  
I curse my fate.  
And yet, it’s not that I cling to home and warmth,  
For I’m not that foolish.  
As things are fated, with this beautiful voice  
I shall sing of my miserable fate,  
Sing and sing until my bitter end.  
Isn’t it a splendid death?  
Do you not envy me, oh world?  
You clever ones who mock me for my misfortune,  
Yet that is a grave mistake.  
I fear neither hell nor capital punishment,  
For I am delighted and joyful,  
And with this beautiful voice of mine,  
I shall sing until the end of execution...”

That day arrived. Before long, word spread, and the villagers gathered out of curiosity to witness this spectacle. First, a cavalry of five or six men rode ahead, followed by a line of soldiers brandishing revolvers and knives, marching in coordinated steps with the condemned man at the center. The jailer, who was the supervisor and organizer of the event, sat proudly upon his horse.

The beautiful song began to flow from the prisoner’s lips. The poignant, sorrowful melody, with echoes of a Chinese tune that seemed to weep and resent, captivated the audience, including the guard soldiers, who became silent under the enchanting performance. As the song reached a crescendo, the marching soldiers were so moved they couldn't help but cheer in admiration, eventually joining in the singing themselves.

The soldiers, caught up in the harmonious breeze, encouraged the prisoner to raise his voice even higher, lost to the artistic ecstasy and temporarily forgetting the fear of death that approached. The march of this heroic-sounding procession continued until they reached the execution site in the village. Up until this moment, enchanted by his artistic mood and transcending life and death, the artist suddenly started to writhe in agony. 

“Kill me! I’ll make you all perish! Let the ghosts take care of you…” Amidst various curses and rage, he spiraled into a frenzy, unable to be restrained.

At that moment, from behind, a revolver discharged sharply with a bang! The sound echoed through the mountains as the final breath of the great artist was silenced.  

**Summary**

This article tells the poignant story of a condemned man who, despite facing execution, finds solace and beauty in music. The tale offers a glimpse into the harsh and exploitative conditions in the gold mines of early 20th century Manchuria, highlighting the struggles of miners against a backdrop of fearsome predators and the desperation of those seeking to escape oppression. Historians may find the depiction of both the miners and the bandits revealing, illustrating the complexities of survival and resistance during the colonial era in Korea’s broader historical context.

북만주 흑룡강(黑龍江)연안은 원래부터 사금(砂金)만히 나기로 유명한 곳인데 관도(觀都) 금장, 막하(漠河) 금장, 기건(奇乾) 금장, 여경(餘慶) 금장은 80퍼-센트의 금질을 가진 실로 방대한 황금의 산출지역인 것이다.
이제 이야기에 발생지인 태평구(太平溝)라는 데는 관도 금장국 관할 하에 잇는 한 금장이다.
금장에서 일하는 채금부(採金夫)들로 말하면 각기 『쿨리』두목의 지배하에 금광국의 규정에의하야 금을 캐는데 캐여낸 사금은 일절 공정가격으로 금광국에 매수하고 절대로 사사로히 팔지는 못하는 것이다. 그리고 사금 한 량중에 대하야 사륙분금(四六分金)이라고 하야 4분은 금광국에 납부하게 하고 6분을 채금부가 차지하세 규약이 되어 잇다.
그런데 무지한 그들은 이 6분의 소득만으로는 만족이 되지 못하야 각금 엄중한 감시의 눈을 속여 사금을 목구녕으로 삼켜넘기기도 하고 혹은 숨겨두기도 하고 기외 여러 가지 수단으로 감추어 두엇다가 조흔 기회에는 가지고 탈출하여 버리는 것이다.
그러나 이 탈출이 여간 어렵고 모험인 것이 아니다. 목숨을 내걸지 안코서는 생각도 할수 업다.
글들이 엄중한 감시의 눈을 속이여 탈출한다 할지라도 방법은 하나밧게 업게된다. 즉 이 금장을 싸고돈 연련한 산악지대를 잠행하야 흑룡강가의 어느 곳으로 빠지는 것이다. 그러나 거기까지 빠져나가기에는 여러 가지의 난관잇다. 몃백년 몃천년을 두고 독기에 한번 찍혀 보지 못한 울울창창한 대삼림이 잇는지라 일단 방향을 일케 되면 영영이 깁흔 산중에서 헤매지 안을 수 업게 된다. 그뿐만 아니라 호랑이 늑대와 가튼 맹수가 낫밤을 가리지 안코 으르렁거려서 깟닥하면 맹수의 밥이 되고 만다. 그뿐인가? 아니다. 또 무서운 위협이 잇다. 그것은 맹수이상의 포악한 마적(馬賊)의 일종이니 금광탈주자를 노리고 이 무서운 산속을 횡행하는 것이다.
이 이야기의 주인공도 역시 이러한 도적의 하나인데 전에 태평구에서 채금부 노릇을 한 관게로 탈출자의 도망하는 경로를 잘 알아서 그들이 탈출하는 시기를 기다렷다가는 잔인한 범행을 하야 관현에게 붓들려 사형선고를 밧지 전까지에는 수만흔 채금부가 희생을 당하엿든 것이다.
그런대 이 도적이 사형을 바덧다는 사실보다도 더 자미잇고 우수운 이야기가 잇는 것이다.
그자는 천성으로 목청이 곱은 데다가 노래를 여간 잘하지 아니하엿다. 그래서 옥문을 직히는 경리들은 각금 그자의 노래에 반하고 마럿다. 그러다가 노래ㅅ소리가 전옥(典獄)의 귀에까지 드러가게 되엿다. 전옥 영감은 상당한 풍류랑이요 오입쟁이이라 그자의 노래소리를 한번 듯고는 크게 감동되여 드듸어 사형수를 위하야 고금에 업는 기상천외의 진기한 게획을 세웟다. 그리하야 명령이 한번 떨어지자 게획은 착착 진행되엿다.-
도적은 닥처오는 사형을 압두고 비통한 감격에 떨면서도 그날의 훌륭한 주역(主役)이 되기를 승낙하고 자긔의 과거 한생을 노래로 지엿다. 그 노래의 내용은 대개 이러한 뜻이다.
