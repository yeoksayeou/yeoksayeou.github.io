**Summary**  

The cartoon depicts a humorous exchange where the protagonist brings an astonishingly heavy object, which turns out to be a large rock with a note inside it. The note playfully addresses the person who enjoys taking what belongs to others, suggesting that even this strange gift is too heavy to drop. This article provides insight into the social commentary of the time, reflecting the sentiments around materialism and interpersonal relationships during the colonial era in Korea. Historians may find the lighthearted yet critical commentary on theft and personal morals particularly revealing amid the broader context of Japanese colonialism and its effects on Korean society.
