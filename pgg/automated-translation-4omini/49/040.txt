Establishment of Tenant Farming Law and Tenants  
						Kim Dong-cheol  

I. Landlords and Tenants  

Like a great tide (潮水) flowing forcefully, bursting a levee and inundating homes, capitalism's power is overtaking even small agricultural countries, commodifying all goods and concentrating the means of production—land, mines, factories, machinery, resources—in the hands of a few. As the laborer's strength is cheapened to sell, the previously self-sufficient economy is gradually disappearing, akin to the concentration of capital in the hands of a few in agriculture and industry. As a result, land is increasingly concentrated in the hands of a small number of landlords, with the number of tenants growing enormously. II. Intensification of Tenant Disputes  

Like workers who do not own factories, tenants do not own land and thus have no choice but to cultivate the landlord's land, risking their lives and laboring tirelessly. However, the income from their labor, come autumn, must be given more than half to the landlord, with whatever is left going to pay debts for seeds and fertilizer, and the basic living costs for the coming year, leaving them in a precarious state filled with worries and concerns. On top of this, when landlords face economic difficulties, they force higher rents and other burdens, leading to inevitable conflicts between landlords and tenants. The intensity of tenant disputes rises proportionally with the severity of the landlord's exploitation and the deep-rooted economic hardships of capitalism, ultimately pushing tenants toward wanting to seize land directly. The recent five-year period reflects the following statistics on tenant disputes and the number of participating tenants:

The yearly increase in tenant disputes will escalate even further in response to the landlords' intensified aggression.

III. Why Establish Tenant Laws?  

With the escalation of disputes, authorities were forced to pay attention, and landlords realized they needed a convenient way to suppress these issues. Some landlords pretended to care for the tenants, showing kindness in order to temper their resentment. However, the tenants, fighting for their livelihoods, are becoming increasingly organized in their methods of resistance. In response, landlords have petitioned authorities to enact laws to curb disputes. As a result, authorities established tenant officials in the most conflict-driven areas to ensure disputes did not arise. Yet the situation necessitates stronger enforcement and thus the decision to draft tenant laws was made. Currently, two drafts are being prepared: one for tenant mediation (小作調停令) and one for tenant laws (小作令). The tenant mediation law aims to mediate tenant disputes, although the exact details are still under draft, with some elements taken from Japan's existing laws, which might serve as a partial reference.

Main Points of Japan's Tenant Mediation Law  
1. There exists a mediation body, a mediation committee. (This can be a court or appropriate individuals, but primarily consists of a committee). The committee is formed by a judge and several mediators appointed from a list selected in advance by local court heads.  

2. Mediation is based on the parties involved. If there are multiple parties, representatives can be elected, also from among them.  

3. Administrative bodies may participate in mediation. There is a tenant official tasked with investigating tenant relations to prevent disputes, and during mediation, they may provide opinions. The heads of counties, cities, towns, and villages can mediate and report on the incidents during mediation, though it might be evident that such individuals will favour one side over the other.  

The tenant law in Japan has faced repeated failures in passing through their National Diet, and while discussing its contents would exceed the space here, the purpose of tenant laws, developed through the organization of tenant-tenant committees, is to ensure the liberation from the unreasonable and disadvantageous situations tenants find themselves in by establishing their farming rights. 

IV. Tenant Laws and Tenants  
Firstly, to establish farming rights.  
1. Even if the land is sold, the farming rights must remain.  
2. Acknowledgment of the opposing status regarding land ownership.  
3. The term of farming rights should be set for over ten years.  
4. Prohibition of seizure of farming profits.  
5. In the case of landlords seizing land, they must provide fair compensation.  
6. Assurance regarding contractual rights.  
7. Abolishment of the "household reputation" system.  

Secondly, to recognize collective bargaining rights.  
1. Farmers' associations should not be restricted and should be allowed to participate in all agreements collectively.  

Thirdly, to recognize the right to dispute.  

**Summary**  

This article discusses the establishment of tenant farming laws in the context of escalating conflicts between landlords and tenant farmers during the rise of capitalism in Korea. It highlights the increasing concentration of land ownership and the precarious living conditions of tenants, who often face exploitation at the hands of landlords. An important aspect is the systemic response from authorities, which included drafting laws intended to mediate disputes while reflecting a deeper struggle for farmers' rights. Historians may find it noteworthy that this document captures a critical moment in colonial history when Korean tenants were striving to assert their rights in a rapidly changing economic landscape dominated by Japanese capitalists.

二. 小作爭議의 激化
노동자가 공장을 가지지 못하엿슴과가티 소작인도 토지를 가지지 못하엿슴으로 지주의 토지를 경작하지 안을 수 업스니 조흐나 실흐나 거긔에 목숨을 걸고 피ㅅ땀을 흘니면서 일하는 것이다. 그러나 그 일한 소득은 가을에 가서 지주의게 절반 이상을 주고 그 남어지를 가지고 만주속 외상갑슬 물고 거름갑 외상을 물고 긔타 잡비에 쓴 묵은 빗을 물되 너푼ㅅ리자 오푼리자를 물고 나면 추수하는 그날부터 명년에 살아나갈 걱정과 근심을 하지 안으면 안되게 되는 불안한 상태에 빠지게 된다. 그 우에다가 지주는 불경긔가 되여서 지세 물기도 거북하는니 수리조합비를 부담하라느니 하야 소작료를 전보다 더 올님으로 필연적으로 리해가 충돌되야 지주와 소작인이 싸우게 되야 일으는 바 소작쟁의가 생기는 것이다. 소작쟁의는 지주의 착X가 심하면 심할사록 더 이러나는 것이요 자본주의의 불경긔가 심하면 심할사록 더 깁히 뿌리박혀서 결국 토지가 업시는 못사는 농민이 차차 토지를 직히려는 싸음까지 나가고야 말 것이다. 이제 최근 다섯해 동안의 소작쟁의 건수와 참가한 소작인 수를 보면 다음과 갓다.

三. 小作法令은 왜 만드나?
이와가티 소작쟁의가 격화하게 됨을 따라 당국은 크게 머리를 알케 되엿고 지주들은 간편하게 눌으는 방법이 잇서야 할 것을 깨달엇다. 그 중에도 엇던 지주는＜8＞ 소작인을 사랑하는 척 하고 따뜻한 정을 쓰는 척 하면서 소작인의 반감(反感)을 싯처 보랴는 수단을 쓰는 자도 잇다. 그러나 삶(生)을 위한 소작인들은 쟁의의 방법이 점점 조직적으로 되여간다. 지주들은 당국에 애원하야 쟁의를 막는 법령을 내도록 하엿다. 당국은 위선 소작관(小作官)을 두어 쟁의가 가장 심한 몃도(道)에 주재식혀서 쟁의가 못 니러나도록 하엿다. 그러나 대세는 엇절 수 업서서 좀 더 강제의힘이 잇는 법령이 아니면 안될 것이라고 생각하고 소작법령을 내기로 작정하고 위선 각지의 소작관행(小作官行)을 조사하야 두가지 법령 초안(草案)을 방금 기초(起草)중 이라는데 그 두가지 법령 중 하나는 소작조정령(小作調停令)이요 하나는 소작령(小作令)이다. 소작조정령이란 것은 글자 그대로 소작쟁의를 중재하자는 것인데 아즉 그 초안이 나오지 안엇슴으로 어떠타 말할 수는 업스나 일본에서 만들어 노흔 것을 그 골자 만들어 보면 (꼭 그와 갓다든지 그보다 못하면 못하엿지 낫다고는 할 수 업스나) 약간의 참고가 될 까 한다.
