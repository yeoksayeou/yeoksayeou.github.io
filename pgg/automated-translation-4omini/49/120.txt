Mopomokkŭl's Spring Recreational Economics

When socks get holes, one makes do with them. However, when one's lover is about to be met, even usable socks are thrown aside to purchase a new pair. Herein lies the development of economic matters.

Today, we are going to meet a certain beauty. Her wise husband, adorned in a suit with a golden accent, fixed his gaze at her. The young man, who had tied a new necktie to his freshly laundered shirt, felt a tingle in his chest and asked,

"What are you saying?"

He stiffened in surprise. However, his wife continued her relentless questioning.

"Then why must you wear a soiled shirt, neglect to shave that unruly beard of yours, and even ask for golden accents in your trousers?"

At this, there was nothing for him to say in response. Without a choice, he forced a laugh, dismissing her interrogative with irritation that echoed like a raging scream, and he set out from home into the streets. Under the bright spring sun, adorned in new clothes, he felt refreshed, akin to gazing into a clean mirror.

How to entertain this beauty?

This is where the planning truly begins. At that point, above all, one must consult what's in the pocket.

What's the money situation? How much do I have in hand? What is my monthly allowance? How much can I borrow from my mother's purse, and if it gets serious, how many coins have I saved with my wife?

Now, how much money is necessary? I will proudly boast a mid-level understanding of spring economics gathered from my lessons.

Part One: When You Have Just One Won 

Having a single won with a lover to enjoy a spring excursion is indeed pitiful. Even the suit you wear would need to be neglected. However, if you discard the suit, you would stand together with your lover, so despite breaking a sweat, one must plan a reasonable budget.

1. Bread: 20 chŏn (four pieces)  
2. Sugar: 5 chŏn (powder)  
3. Apple: 10 chŏn (two pieces)  
4. Banana: 20 chŏn (ten pieces)  
5. Candy: 5 chŏn (a handful)  
6. Mat: 30 chŏn (for one person)  
7. Wooden bottles: 10 chŏn (for drinks)  
8. Transport fare: 10 chŏn  

Total: 1 won  

If one wisely spends this single won, surely they could play the role of a significant figure within the country. The question, then, becomes how to enjoy this one won.

First, simply take a single piece of bread and stroll out into the streets with your lover. There, purchase some bread, sugar, apples, bananas, and candy, then head up to Namsan Park. With blossoming cherry trees dotted among brick houses, and against the backdrop of the setting spring hue of Gyeongseong, the two of you should start ascending the hill of Gyeongseong Shin-sa.

Considering the fresh air, one will likely feel breathless. At this moment, one may take out the candy and pop it in the mouths of one another. As you go up, human presence diminishes, and the city gradually shrinks away. The two will have set up a world only for itself. Singing on the grassy patches or atop rocks, as she gently devours a banana and you munch on an apple, no one would dare speak against the sweetness of such an intimate exchange.

However, do take care that you do not overindulge, for no food remains at the end! Continuing the scenic hike, one may reach the historic site at the National Treasure Shrine. From here, revel in the exhilarating view overlooking the expanse of Gyeongseong.

The Han River flows freely to the south; the blue rooftops of Changgyeonggung and Gyeongbokgung are quaintly nestled in the ground. Here, the sacred shrine lives, and the refreshing spring water is even better than the cheap soda. The great tree embraces youthful love with its generous shadow. At this point, enjoy your meal: 

Bread, 

Candy, 

While strolling about.

After quite some time enjoying at this fine spot, rehydrate with another cup of spring water, and choose the western route, following the ancient castle path leading you to Jangchungdan and its forest. For this journey, it is expected that any youthful lady would not fall ill. The path is quite smooth and effortless; anyone could manage it. Once you arrive at Jangchungdan, the children bring out sodas and candies, insisting the lovers spend their springtime together. 

With 30 chŏn remaining, splurge 20 chŏn on sodas, savoring the delightful moment together. From here on, the two can play for quite a while, as you certainly can afford the trip down to the bus stop to the West.

Part Two: When You Have Three Wons

One hundred fifty nyang is three times one won. You can indulge a little more.

1. Transport fare: 25 chŏn  
2. Entrance ticket to Changkyŏngwon: 20 chŏn  
3. Lunch: 80 chŏn  
4. A bottle of soda: 20 chŏn  
5. Two wheat snacks: 15 chŏn  
6. Entrance fee: 60 chŏn  
7. Cushion: 10 chŏn  
8. Dinner pack: 50 chŏn  
9. Coffee: 20 chŏn  

Total: 3 wons  

My dear beloved, let’s rejoice in spring.

You have 3 wons. Let’s meet at the gate of Changkyŏngwon at 2 PM this Saturday. Anyone arriving first has priority.

A letter alone can win a beautiful lady's heart. Promptly at two, a young man and a beauty arrive at the grand gates of Changkyŏngwon.

Man: "Ah!"  
Woman: "Have you been waiting long?"

The couple's gazes lock. They are determined to purchase tickets. Every item at Changkyŏngwon seems to bloom for their enjoyment. Side by side, they line up to buy their tickets, going for a stroll that only those in love may truly appreciate. Entering the zoo, they observe animals, visit the museum, and eventually enter the botanical garden, where tranquility reigns.

As they stand by the water thinking about the spring, the young woman gently grasps her man’s hand. In the scene's beauty, even without speaking, both can feel the unspoken connection.

At this point, conversations flow easily: "When we're together like this, how can we think of worries or fears?" 

"It's hard not to be hungry."

"Those in love are hungrier than usual, I hear." 

"Well then, I will bring back something to eat."

Two rice cakes for 80 chŏn, a bottle of soda for 20 chŏn, and two apple snacks for 10 chŏn, totaling 1 won and 15 chŏn have been spent. The transport fee to Changkyŏngwon was paid for out of their respective coffers, with the entrance fee also covered. They still have 1 won and 60 chŏn remaining.

With the mat laid out, they begin to feast on their treats and share a soda.

"Oh, my legs!" exclaimed the young woman as she stretched her feet.

Then they noticed that her socks were developing holes, and they laughed together, finding it humorous. Although such a trivial matter might seem ordinary, for these bright young lovers, it became a source of endless amusement, surpassing even the laughter of Don Quixote.

Having played at Changkyŏngwon, the couple took the tram back through Main Street, yearning to savor the springtime scenery. Windowpanes were adorned with the fresh blossoms of spring. Buying stylish umbrellas, silk scarves, spring hats, and with vibrant outfits replete with new fabric from top to bottom, they used the fare to cross over to Meiji Confectionery for a refreshing glass of pop, costing 20 chŏn! They still had 1 won and 40 chŏn left over. 

As twilight approached seven o'clock, they headed towards the Joy Theater or Daegyeong Theatre. Visiting the Joseon Activities Photo Gallery might be visually engaging, but instead, they opted for a place with better images: the Japanese photo studio.

On the second floor, they could skip the shoe removal for comfort, so under the pretense of needing to soak in their time with Japan, they purchased tickets with different fare classes.

Adults third class: 40 chŏn  
Students third class: 20 chŏn 

The tickets were inscribed thus. Did the school depend on graduation, or could a Joseon woman pretend to be a student as long as her hair No. 4 was Tamara? The young man purchased a ticket for an adult while his lady took the student’s ticket.

At the gentleman’s gathering, due to no side cushions, they had to pay an extra 10 chŏn; thus remaining money was 66 chŏn after experiencing the gallery. After some time spent enjoying the scenery, they would buy a bowl of beef rice at 25 chŏn and have 10 chŏn left over when returning home.

Part Three: When Luck Comes With Ten Wons

As long as both lovers had a ten won note stuffed in their pockets, they could afford to treat themselves to a delightful time. 

"Dear, if we run out what will we do?"  

"Let’s at least buy some fabric and have a visit to the zoo."

Without consideration at hand, the devoted husband gripped the love of his life and set off for an enjoyable outing. 

Considering no money concerns would be left at home, they were initially hesitant. However, they decided it was best to have fun and make the best of it.

Onyang Oncheon, Soyo San, Gwanak San, and Ui-dong were all suggestions. Finally, they resolved to keep their focus within the city limits, meaning truly, it was the same day as always—and so, they made their way to the street corner of Jongno.

They hailed an inexpensive taxi costing 80 chŏn. The luxurious thirty-first model whisked them through a magnificent drive toward Namsan Park, directing the driver to Han-yang Park!

After stopping, they took in the grandeur of the blossoms. Here in the spacious courtyard, they had a great view of the blossoming trees of Gyeongseong. As they walked leisurely, taking pleasure in the spring breeze, feeling the air as they passed Han River’s bridge, it was an experience hard to forget.

At the Han River, they boarded a boat costing 1 won and 60 chŏn, settling in with refreshments worth 90 chŏn each for two beers, 30 chŏn worth of snacks, and 30 chŏn for soda.

Total: 1 won and 70 chŏn worth of food. The gentleman took the oars while the wife shielded herself from the sun against the sunlight's radiance. They ventured down the peaceful Han River to delight in nature's offering. 

Eventually, reaching a grassy strip by the riverside, the boat was docked. Picking wildflowers became joyous for both lovers.

"Dear, will you pour me a drink?"

"Why do you think I’m a slave to your whims?" 

"You can serve me in this serene setting."

A carefree day where they let go of their troubles, forgetting even to think of the time, but spent hours embraced by nature’s splendor.

What amount of time had passed? It was already five in the afternoon.

Leaving the boat cost 50 chŏn. 

The next cab ride had a minimum fare of 80 chŏn; thus, they contributed a won and later asked to please take them to the park. Arriving to take in another glory in this rustic environment, they moved through the city center while seeking the open flavors of fresh evening appetites. 

While motivating conversations distracted them at the scenery, the heated agreement simply became a part of their wonderful outing. The longer they explored, the better—time flew faster.

Eventually, their conversations led to reviewing a time piece—a week's worth seemed to pass in mere hours. 

"Ah—that feels wonderful. Everything here suits you well."  

"I'm so afraid to choose. I might prefer this hat instead." 

As lingering minutes combined with lovely interactions found their culmination together, they hesitated at a compelling choice to make together. It was lively harmony.

An hour spent drew them to excitement, and season events naturally unfolded. Returning to Changkyŏngwon itself was a choice made spontaneously; their total leftovers diminishing from three wons and seventy-five chŏn included an after-meal!

Summary 

The article whimsically explores the economics of enjoying springtime outings in 1930s Korea, focusing on youthful romantic encounters and the constraints of budgeting through playful discourse about the cost of various treats while courting. The writer illustrates how a single won could foster an intimate outing filled with joy and delight, a reflection of the era's socio-economic conditions under colonial rule. Historians may find the sentiments shared in love reflective of an escape from the harsh realities of life during the Japanese occupation, highlighting how economic struggles influenced social interactions and bonding.
