**Summary**  

This article presents a prize contest encouraging readers to name their most revered living male and female figures from within Korea. The prizes include an art clock for the grand prize and books for other selected winners. The contest underscores a cultural engagement with contemporary figures during a period of colonial rule in Korea. Historians may find interest in how public admiration was expressed in a time when Korean identity was challenged by Japanese colonization, reflecting both personal and national sentiments.
