필자	金奎澤 (Kim Gyujak)  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

A Distant Place Even If It’s Near  
						Kim Gyujak  
A modern young lady who only endures the annoying face of a man  
“Why must I bother in a place where there are no gentlemen? This is truly distressing.”  

“Not at all. Don't mention it. You and I are far apart.” <16>  

**Summary**  

This article reflects on the frustrations of women in the context of their societal positions during the colonial period in Korea. It highlights the struggles of the modern young lady when faced with undesirable male attention, indicating a sense of frustration and distance from the ideals of gentlemanly behavior. Historians may find the commentary on gender roles and the evolving perspectives of women during the early 20th century particularly revealing, as it captures the tensions and frustrations arising from colonial norms and traditions.
