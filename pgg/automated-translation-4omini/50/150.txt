기사제목	巡査는 六尺身長 (The Constable is Six Feet Tall)  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

The constables of London, England, are famously known for their height, and this time, they have revised the previous requirement of a height of 5 feet 9 inches, raising it to 6 feet.  

 

**Summary**  

The article discusses a change in the height requirement for police officers in London, reflecting societal values regarding authority and physicality during the early 20th century. This change could pique historians' interest as it provides insight into British colonial attitudes and standards during a period when Britain exerted influence over parts of Asia, including Korea. Such requirements may indicate a larger narrative about societal expectations and the embodiment of authority in colonial contexts.
