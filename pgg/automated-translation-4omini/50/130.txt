기사제목	朝鮮木鐸의 大出身, 交響樂의 樂器로 (The Great Origin of the Korean Moktak, as an Instrument of Symphony)  
저자	-  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

The Korean moktak (木鐸), often reverberating the sacred chant "Namu Amida Butsu," has received a considerable order from a certain music store in England, reaching the Tokyo office of the Korean Governor-General in large quantities. Consequently, the Tokyo office requested an inquiry into the production status of the moktak from the Korean Association of Commerce. It is said that this moktak has been ordered for use as one of the instruments in a symphony orchestration in the West. While Westerners are increasingly fascinated by the East, drawing interest through symbols such as "Fujiyama," "Kimono," and "Bukeye" in Japan, one wonders what indeed might capture their interest regarding Korea.

  
**Summary**  

This article discusses the surprising demand for the traditional Korean moktak as an instrument in Western symphonic music, illustrating a cross-cultural interest in Korean traditional artifacts. It highlights how elements of Korean culture, like the moktak, have found a niche in Western music contexts, reflecting broader themes of cultural exchange and colonial dynamics. Historians might find the article significant as it underscores the ways in which the colonial relationship influenced the perception and commodification of Korean cultural products in the global market.
