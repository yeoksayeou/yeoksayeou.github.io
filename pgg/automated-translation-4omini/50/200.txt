기사제목	사공의 안해 (The Wife of the Helmsman)  
필자	徐德龍 (Seo Deok-ryong)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

The waves are fierce along the crashing shore,  

Gathering the broken pieces of the boat,  

On the edge of the sea, my husband has gone,  

How much have I cried since last night?  

Though the ship may break, it can return,  

But once he has left, he will not return.  

Today, still by the shore, all alone,  

I weep, thinking of the days gone by.  

It has been three years since time passed,  

Before I knew it, I have grown a child.  

By the sea, I tell my little one,  

"Grow up soon and battle the sea".  

(1931.4.5日)<27>  

**Summary**  

The poem reflects the sorrow of a wife whose husband, a helmsman, has gone missing at sea. The imagery portrays the fierce waves and the emotional turmoil of waiting and wondering about the fate of her husband over the years while raising their child. The text provides insight into the lives of people impacted by the maritime culture and possible dangers faced by fishermen during this period, highlighting themes of loss, waiting, and the resilience of women. Historians may find it revealing of the everyday struggles faced under colonial rule, where personal stories of loss intertwine with the broader narrative of survival.

기사형태	시

사공의 안해
						徐德龍
물결조차 사나운 저바다가에
부서진 배쪼각 주서모으는
저안악내 풀랑에 남편을 일코
어제밤을 얼마나 울고나샛나

타신배는 부서저도 도라오건만
한번가신 그분은 올길업고나
오날도 바다가에 외로히서서
지나간날 생각에 울다가가네

속한 것은 세월이라 삼년이되니
어느사이 어린아해 키워가지고
바다가에 니르러 일러주는말
어서커서 바다와 싸워나다오
(1931.4.5日)<27>
