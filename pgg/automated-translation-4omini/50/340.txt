滄海力士(第六回)  

As No-yak-su became the chief of the fish market, all the heroes of the martial world gathered around him.  

No-yak-su, a mere fish vendor, possessed such immense strength that he could lift a weight beyond what others could not, swaying it while strolling left and right. Seeing this, the general, Yeowon-su, greatly admired it and said to No-yak-su, "You are a person worthy of this martial world. Since there are no heroes like you in this land, nominate those heroes here to protect the nation."  

In response, No-yak-su said, "If I recommend such a person and they take my position as chief, what will I do then?"  

Yeowon-su, feeling distressed, said, "Are you satisfied as just a mere chief of the fish market? If you recommend heroes, you will be able to make one of them the commander-in-chief. What do you think?"  

No-yak-su then spoke, "I have traveled the world and have met many heroes, but I have not seen anyone who possesses strength like that of my friend Heo-bi. Besides, I have heard that Ye-min-ong is known as the greatest warrior in the land."  

Yeowon-su probed further, "Heo-bi is your friend, so you surely know his strength well, but where did you hear about Ye-min-ong's prowess?"  

No-yak-su replied, "I have been around the world, and when I was near Odae Mountain, I heard from people that Ye-min-ong, while hunting in the mountains, once killed hundreds with a single club against a band of invaders. I admire him and have wanted to meet him, but I have not been able to see him yet."  

Upon hearing this, Yeowon-su realized that Min-ong was the son of Yeowon, and he was greatly astonished, saying, "Surely, you are misled by rumors. I know Ye-min-ong well, and he is nothing but a common scholar without strength, who spends his time reading books. It is quite impossible for him to be out hunting in the mountains."  

On another day, as Yeowon-su was worried about military supplies, he heard about a person named Hong Baek-rok and inquired around to see if anyone knew him. He then recalled that the Hong Mal-rok from the previous day, who had assisted in transporting supplies, had a similar name and thus summoned him to ask, "Is there a Hong Baek-rok among your brothers?"  

Mal-rok replied, "He is my elder brother."  

Yeowon-su rejoiced and asked him, "Why did your brother not respond to the call for volunteers?"  

Mal-rok answered, "My family lives together, and my elder brother, in order to arrange our household, has not yet volunteered, but he will soon."  

At that moment, Mal-rok's speech was interrupted when a man named Hong Cheon-bi requested to meet with Yeowon-su. Upon summoning him, he found Cheon-bi to be of unsophisticated demeanor and when he inquired, Cheon-bi stated, "The General sent for talents, so I have come here."  

Yeowon-su further asked, "If you came to our camp, what position do you seek?"  

Cheon-bi responded, "I wish to be a soldier."  

Yeowon-su said, "We have already decided on our military officers, and the marching day is near, so if you have anything to say to our General in private, please do so later."  

Each army was preparing, and eventually, they would go into battle at Hamakpyeong.  

After Yeowon-su delivered his message to the state, all of the warriors and heroes gathered under his banner. Thus, he designated himself as the Grand General while appointing Hong Cheon-bi as the Commander of the Supplying Troops. No-yak-su was made the Vanguard Commander, Heo-bi became the Scout General, and No-dong-hae was appointed as the General of the Left Wing. Song Jo was given command of the Right Wing, Hong Mal-rok as the Adjunct General, and the son of Min-ong was not given any military position.  

Seeing that Min-ong had claimed to have killed a band of invaders at Yeodoo-dong, the Grand General asked, "Since he is responsible for the deaths of eighteen of the enemy, can we actually trust his abilities to hold a position?"  

Hong Cheon-bi answered, "That was then, and this is now. You cannot judge a young man's bravery by a momentary passionate action – or else we could not proceed with the military campaigns."  

Thus, as all the soldiers backed him, Yeowon-su reluctantly appointed Min-ong as the commander of the rear forces and made some arrangements. He then sent a message to the enemy requesting they meet in battle on October 15th at Hamakpyeong, as the soldiers prepared.  

On the fifteenth of October, the air rumbled at Hamakpyeong, and banners fluttered as both armies converged for a decisive battle. Yeowon-su, standing before his troops, raised the imperial banner and wielded his grand sword while shouting at the enemy, "Foolish husband Yeobang, come out and surrender immediately!"  

At that moment, the leading warrior No-yak-su stepped forward, shouting, "You bastard, Seobok, do you know who I am, the Grand Warrior No-yak-su of Changhae?"  

Before he could finish, the left commander of Seobok, Won-il-rang, lunged at him wielding his sword, and No-yak-su, with his iron rod, struck him down, having his feet crumble beneath him. Seeing the death of her commander, the female general Hoo-gang-ja, filled with rage, commanded her troops to attack No-yak-su.  

The female soldiers wielded blades. No-yak-su, faced with this challenge, tore off his armor and shouted with all his might, "As a man, why should I fear the blades of women?"  

As he readied himself, Hoo-gang-ja seized the moment, throwing two daggers at him, which struck his ears, and blood gushed down his neck. No-yak-su, filled with fury, raised his rod again to strike Hoo-gang-ja down.  

At that moment, a sudden strong wind blew, and snow began to fall heavily, soon covering the mountains and fields. Seobok, recalling the advice from Pyo-byeon, withdrew his troops, hastening to retreat as he saw both Won-il-rang and Hoo-gang-ja dead.  

Seeing the withdrawal of the Changhae soldiers, Yeowon-su's troops swiftly maneuvered through the snow, as their horses, trained for combat, could easily navigate, while the enemy's horses were ordinary and struggled in the deep snow, thus their bravery faltered. 

Yeowon-su, who felt no remorse, struck back furiously, leading his army.  

At this moment, No-yak-su, now injured, attempted to retreat when the Tiger General Song Jo rushed to protect him. However, as they tried to flee, their horses fell into a pit covered by snow and they could not escape.  

Finally, the enemy commander the Lovely, Won-il-rang, pursued No-yak-su, but as his horse also fell into the pit, Song Jo seized the chance and stabbed the horse's belly with his sword. The horse went down, throwing Won-il-rang to the ground, and soon they captured both him and Hoo-gang-ja, tying them up securely.  

Finally, Seobok led his cavalry towards Hamakpyeong, inciting a strong attack against the Changhae soldiers who could not hold their position. A swift charge brought victory to Seobok's forces, as bodies lay thick upon the battlefield, and blood flowed like rivers.  

After gathering the troops and assessing the casualties, Seobok found that both Won-il-rang and Hoo-gang-ja had fallen while the enemy commander Yeobang had been captured, realizing the significant losses suffered by both sides was beyond count. 

**Summary**

In this chapter, No-yak-su's journey from a humble fish vendor to a central martial figure illustrates themes of valor and identity amidst the chaos of battle. The narrative underscores the dynamics of power, loyalty, and the contrast between traditional roles and martial capabilities in a historical context. Historians might find the depictions of class and heroism reflective of the socio-political conditions under Japanese rule, examining how colonial narratives shaped the aspirations and identities within the Korean resistance.

하다.

양군이 군사를 조련하다가 필경 하마평에서 개전하다.

서복이 여방의 격서를 바든 후 대병을 거느리고 노회동에 진을 치고 병마를 크게 조련할새 표변을 불러 무러 가로대
「적장 여방이 군량을 엇지 못하야 그 군사가 주려죽을 줄 아럿더니 무슨 모책으로 군량을 능이 지급하는지 알 수 업도다」
표변이 대왈
「실로 또한 의심이온 바, 회주사람 홍말록이 적합포삼정청에 와서 인삼갑슬 달라 하기에 돈으로 주고저 한즉 말록이 청하기를 회주에는 흉년이 드러서 곡식이 절핍인즉 인삼갑을 곡식으로 박구어 달라 하기에 조 천 석과 감자 500석을 주엇더니 그 뒤에 드른즉 그 조와 감자가 여방의 진중으로 드러갓다」
하더이다. 서복이 그 말을 듯고 발을 구르며 원통이 여기니 표변이 또 다시 가로대

하고 이러서며 서복에게 부탁하야 가로대

이와 가치 양군이 대립하야 전쟁의 기분이 익어가다가 10월 15일이 되매 하마평벌판에 창금이 요란하고 긧발이 휘날리니 양군의 대전이 분명하더라.
서복이 진중에 나서 용봉일월긔를 세우고 태아검을 빗겨들고 오초마를 모라 진전에 나서니 과연 위풍이 늠늠하고 태자원일랑은 선봉장이 요원이랑은 좌익장이며 공주 후천자는 우익장이요 후강자는 군사가 되엿더라.
서복이 진두에서 소리질러 가로대
「산곡간에 어리석은 지아비 여방은 속히 나와 항복하라」
하니 창해선봉장 노약수-응성출마하야 가로대

말이 맛지 못하야 서복의 좌익장 원이랑이 칼을 들고 달겨드니 노약수-철봉을 드러 한 번 두르매 원이랑의 발이 추풍낙엽과 가치 말에 떠러저 걱구러지는지라 그 처 후강자-그 남펀의 죽음을 보고 분긔 충천하야 연지가튼 입술을 독하게 다물고 여병 수백을 지휘하야 약수를 치니 여병의 손에는 낫낫치 비수라. 약수-좌우로 대적하야 형세가 자못 위태하거늘 제장이 소리를 질러 구원코저하나 약수 대로하야 갑옷과 투구와 옷을 버리고 소리를 질러 가로대

이 때에 북풍이 자고 하눌이 나자지드니 의외에 백설이 분분이 쏘다저서 삽시간에 산천을 더퍼 길이 싸이는지라. 서복이 표변의 일즉이 부탁하든 말을 생각하고 쟁을 처서 군사를 거두고저 하다가 원이랑과 후강자의 죽음을 보고 원수를 갑기 위하야 시살을 게속하니 소복의 장수는 노픈 말을 탓스매 눈 위로 자유횡행할 수 잇스나 창해장수들의 탄 말은 원래 토산말로서 키가 심이 적어 눈이 배에 차니 자연이 용맹을 쓸 길이 업는지라 여원수-염녀하야 쟁을 울리며 군사를 물리치니 이때 노약수도 귀가 떠러저 아품을 익이지 못하야 말을 돌려 도라오랴 할새 타호장군 송조 약수를 보호하야 오다가 말이 눈구렁에 빠저 소사나지 못하더니 맛침 적장 원일랑이 후천자로 더부러 약수의 뒤를 쫏다가 원일랑의 말이 눈구렁위로 지나가는지라 송조-힘을 다하야 뒤여나오며 칼로써 원일랑의 말배를 찌르니 말이 걱구러지며 원일랑이 눈 속에 떠러지고 이어서 후천자의 말이 눈구렁에 바지거늘 송조-척후장군 노동해와 합력하야 일랑과 후천자를 사로잡아 원앙색기줄로 쌍쌍이 결박하야 군중으로 도라오다.
