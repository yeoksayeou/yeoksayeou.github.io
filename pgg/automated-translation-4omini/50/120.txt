기사제목	팔다리 病身50명, 乞人團長의 成墓式擧行 (Fifty Disabled Beggars Hold Memorial for Their Leader)  
기사형태	잡저 (Magazine Article)  

In the communal cemetery of Jinaeri (池內里), Ido-Myeon, Eunyul-gun (殷栗郡 二道面), Hwanghaedo (黃海道), over fifty beggars, including those with disabilities such as missing limbs, gathered on the fifth of April to hold a memorial service for Kim Mo (金某), whose grave they honored. Kim Mo was a renowned leader among beggars across Haeseogak-gun (海西各郡), respected by many in the general beggar community. He passed away last winter and was buried in the cemetery; due to the winter season, the memorial at that time was incomplete. Therefore, on the occasion of the Chŏngmyo holiday, they decided to conduct a proper memorial service together, which has become a significant topic among the public.

  

**Summary**  

This article discusses a memorial service held by disabled beggars for their leader, Kim Mo, in Hwanghaedo. The gathering highlights the social structure and communal ties among beggars during this period, where even marginalized individuals sought to honor their deceased leader in a meaningful way. Historians may find the article interesting as it sheds light on the lives of beggars in colonial Korea, revealing their struggles, community solidarity, and the respect they held for their leaders, countering the typical narratives of poverty and marginalization.

팔다리 病身50명, 乞人團長의 成墓式擧行
황해도 은률군 이도면 지내리(黃海道 殷栗郡 二道面 池內里) 공동묘지에는 지난 사월오일에 팔병신 다리병신 등 신체가 온전한 사람이 별로 업는 남녀노소의 걸인 오십여명이 모여들어 작년 겨울에 매장한 김모분묘에 때를 주며 추모제를 거행하엿다는데 그 분묘의 주인공되는 김모는 해서각군(海西各郡)에 널려 잇는 걸인들의 단장으로 일반걸인군에게 상당한 존경을 바다오든 중 작년 겨울에 그 지방에서 사망하야 그곳 공동묘지에 매장하엿든 것인데 동절이 되기 때문에 성묘가 완전치 못하엿든 것을 이번 청명절을 당하야 그와 가티 혹 때를 하며 추도제를 행한 것이라는 바 일반에게 잇서서 큰 화제(話題)꺼리가 되어잇다.<20>
