A Medal for the Crime King?  
Everyone knows about the crime king Al Capone from Chicago, America (he has been mentioned before in Byeolgeongon). It hasn't been long since he has been eating beans in prison. However, since Capone was recently released, small gangs and smuggling groups across the United States have been scared into honestly reporting their illicit earnings, resulting in a massive increase in tax revenue at a time of economic downturn. The government is currently discussing the idea of awarding Al Capone a medal in recognition of his contributions while in prison... There are all sorts of crazy things happening in this world...<20>  

**Summary**  

This article humorously discusses how Al Capone's incarceration inadvertently led to an increase in tax revenues from organized crime, as criminals began to report their earnings honestly out of fear of Capone's influence. The government's potential decision to award Capone a medal for this unexpected outcome reflects the absurdity and complexity of crime and governance during the Great Depression era in America. For historians, this article provides insight into the intersection of crime, economy, and public policy during a tumultuous time, underscoring how individuals can influence systemic economic changes, even while imprisoned.

犯罪王에게 勳章?
미국 「쉬카고」의 범죄왕 알·카포네라면 누구나 다 아는 사실이지만 (전에 별건곤에도 긔재되엿다) 그가 감옥에서 콩밥을 먹는지도 얼마안되엿다. 그런데 카포네가 한번 붓들리게 되자 미국각지에 잇는 조고마한 폭력단·밀수입단들은 겁을 집어 먹고 부정수입(不正收入)가튼 것을 정직하게 신고하기 때문에 수세금(收稅金)이 막대하게 되어 불경기한 이때 갑작이 국고(國庫)의 수입이 격증하야 정부에서는 투옥 중의 범죄왕 알·카포네에 대하야 그 공(功)을 표창하는 의미에서 훈장을 증정하기로 목하협의중이라...세상에는 별놈의 일이 다잇다...<20>
