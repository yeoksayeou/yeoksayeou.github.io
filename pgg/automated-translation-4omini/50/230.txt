Naughty Woman Parade  

I was originally born with the kind of looks that, had I been born in America as a woman, I could have easily enjoyed something like Carnegie's feast. Even if that weren’t the case, if I were to step out just a bit into the society, I could manage to comfortably live off someone of my equal like Jeolla Governor. However, there is one flaw. It is that despite having such a robust physique, I now speak in sharp tones that are often quite pricking even in their gentleness. 

I know very well that this person isn’t someone vile or worthless, yet when I pick up the brush, I continue to issue forth words pricklier than this pen I’m using, making them more piercing than rose thorns. Knowing such a state, the editor-in-chief from Gaebyeoksa, who came asking for this piece, keeps asking me—over ten, twenty, or countless times—"Please avoid those thorny 'hīniko' remarks. Write more softly and roundly, and with gentleness, so that it doesn’t feel too prickly." 

I did answer him promptly at the time, but whatever, it’s habitual now... you only find out by writing... 

If I think about it one way, I ought to repent; I, who have crossed over thirty, with a waist dwindled and grayed hair has become an old man who only speaks harshly—moreover, to beautiful women like these—then how can I expect to escape being a ghost of singlehood? You might wonder who could guarantee that I am not destined to die a tragic, unfulfilled death due to the popular ‘red and white terror tactics’ that are being circulated these days.

That serves as a prologue, and now I need to shift into the main topic, titled “Naughty Woman Parade.” 

By now, it seems like "naughty women" need to be accounted for and turned into mannequins. Even if I have repented and write straightforwardly like the Governor-General’s house sources, due to the title of the piece and the nature of the writing, I would likely be harshly criticized. Ah, as an old bachelor who might not get married at this rate, I think it’s a joy in itself to be able to get scolded by women. 

In XXX, there is a major proctology clinic founded by a woman named P. If one were to examine her background, her family runs a well-known medicinal wine business. They made quite a fortune using the secret recipe of this medicinal wine, and now there’s a heap of gold (though with the recent banning of gold exports in this country, it would be land deeds and bankbooks) sitting in the vault. 

She married, but her husband turned out to be a notorious rogue. His main job was drinking, and his side business involved making human feces. By this standard, it could be said she got a good deal out of her life. However, looking at it from the wife’s perspective, it would be hard to believe that she, at least a graduate of XX Women’s School in her youth (while now she’s a modern woman), would tolerate such a situation. 

Eventually, she divorced and went back to Seoul, graduated again from XX Women’s School, and then crossed over to Tokyo to enter a medical school specializing in proctology. Later, she returned to her hometown, worked as an assistant at XX Hospital, and recently opened her own practice. 

Now, if I've told this much of her story, someone might exclaim, “Her husband is wandering, she divorced, studied more, became a doctor, and has established herself as an independent lady in society—what makes her a naughty woman?!” But that alone does not qualify her as a naughty woman. It’s merely that, like strands of sweet scents, the sweetness is tangled, interwoven, and what that really indicates is just naughtiness.

The readers might misunderstand, thinking that whether it's naughtiness or cruelty, the subjective moral value of that doesn't apply to this writer. I even think that if many men can be naughty, what’s wrong with women being naughty too? 

Let’s put that aside. You might wonder whether I have seen P’s naughtiness with my own eyes. But that’s a wise yet foolish question; how could one only know about life by seeing it directly? If that were the case, then all records would have to be dismissed. 

It’s somewhat unclear whether her naughtiness is truly naughtiness, but she seems somewhat beautiful in appearance. 

As alluring as a flower and with a 100% figure, she speaks fluent Japanese (Oops, not related to physical traits), has warm and gentle eyes, and though she seems older to some degree, she’s actually twenty-seven (though this twenty-seven might seem small, she will be twenty-seven also next year).

Now, seeing her physique, youth, and wealth, how could one not want to be a little naughty?

When asked, “Are you married?” she definitively replies, “Yes, I’m single.” 

When asked, “Aren’t you getting married?” she responds hesitantly, “Yes...” This in itself shows that her inclination for naughtiness and qualifications are at 100%.

The time is the year 19XX in month X, 

The location is XXX, at the boarding house of an artist named K. K appears to be lying down deeply, either conjuring up a novel or daydreaming something. 

At this moment, the door bursts open, and in comes a female artist named S (this 'S' is somewhat of a latecomer). 

Barrelling in, S whines about her jealousy. Caught between aesthetics, whether born in Korea or now, often being envious seems standard.

“I’m so jealous,” she laments.

“Why haven’t you eaten?” he replies.

“It’s not that I haven’t eaten, I just haven’t eaten well.”

“True enough!”

“Will you at least buy me a bowl of noodle soup?”

Fortunately enough, K happens to have an unspent bowl of noodle soup in his pocket (or maybe he was just planning to spend it tomorrow, who knows?), so he called over the landlady and quickly served a bowl of noodle soup. 

Not paying attention to K, watch how S acts. Stretching out the fine blanket, and oops, she declares she’ll lie down underneath it and take a nap. 

I’ve known from past interactions that S has such characteristics, but since guys are not strictly adhering to the rules against women, it can’t just be dismissed. 

Using the narrative style from old novels, one could surmise that S's naughtiness roughly resembles the following. 

Now, having gone to China, it is being said that she has come to be quite well-known. 

A woman who has graduated from the Southern music school in Tokyo and is now instructing Western music at Kyungsung Eastern Industrial Academy... 

Even a piano could cry out with her thin fare, as she feigns an air of solitude saying, “I’m so lonely!”

“Where shall we go to have fun? Let’s dream for a moment,” she pouted, unable to get through a single conversation.

Let’s delve into Mrs. M’s background. 

She married the son of a wealthy family, crossed over to Tokyo, and studied at the Southern music school. But for some reason, her husband stopped supporting her, and they ended up living apart. She entirely rejected being a wife. Naturally, every month 70 won for tuition was covered by her husband’s family, and her husband was studying something related to modernology at some school, while Mrs. M faced such hardships that she spent her days in a haze of sodden liquor, taking up side jobs.

And eventually, upon returning to Korea, she remarried! 

After her remarriage, she seemed unable to endure the ‘loneliness’ she typically spoke of, or perhaps due to her husband's juggling with solitude, she developed into quite an expert in same-sex relationships.

People may argue, saying “If her husband left and she remarried and engaged in same-sex relationships, what on earth makes that naughty?” However, if that’s the case, then same-sex relationships would also fall under my perception of sexual promiscuity, and it would be rather regrettable for me not to categorize her into that lot.

**Summary**

The article offers a critique of societal norms surrounding women and their perceived immorality in 1930s Korea through satirical anecdotes of various women, portraying their struggles and transformations. It provides a glimpse into the variations of social roles for women during the colonial period, marked by traditional expectations and modern aspirations. Historians might find the insights into women’s education and their attempts at independence particularly revealing as they reflect broader social transformations throughout Korea under colonial rule.

그것은 그와가티 풍더분한 체격에도 얼니지 아니하게 뽀족한 소리를 잘하는 것이다.
그 사람이 그러케 지겁게 미웁다거나 못쓸사람이 아닌 것을 번영히 알고잇스면서도 一단 붓을 들면 지금 쓰고 잇는 이 철필보다도 더 뽀족하고 장미가시(흥-)보다도 더 압흐게 찔으는 소리를 늘어놋쿤한다.
이러한 형을 알엇슴인지 이원고를 부탁하러온 개벽사의 긔자군이 엇잿건 열 번 스무번 골백번 더 부탁을 한다-제발 가시가튼「ヒニク」는 하지말고 둥굴하게 두루뭉실하게 솜몸둥이로 매맛는 것가티 근질근질하되 압흐지 아니하게-이러케 써달나고 부탁이다.
그 자리에서는 귀치안허서 그러마고 대답을 하엿지만 뭘내버릇 개주나...써봐야 알지...
