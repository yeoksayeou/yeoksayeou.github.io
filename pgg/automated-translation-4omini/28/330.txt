Washing My Feet  

As I roll the rough stones and dig in the sand,  

The water flowing in the stream makes a sound.  

Could my heart travel a thousand miles?  

A thousand miles back to my hometown, my mother's grave.  

Even there, the stream flows...  

Fledgling fruits lie beneath the water,  

As I pick them, the minnows turn colors as the sun sets.  

Is it hiding that the tails sway in the water?  

I wash my feet; do not play too much.  

I'll pick the ripe fruits; come out and play with me.  

6.1(From Uido-dong) <157>  

**Summary**  

The poem reflects a nostalgic longing for home and the simple joys found in nature, emphasizing the beauty of the stream, the act of washing feet, and the imagery of minnows and fruits. This thread of longing can be tied to the broader context of Korean colonial history, as many Koreans experienced separation from their homes and cultural roots during this tumultuous period. Historians may find it interesting as it captures a personal yearning amidst broader national challenges.
