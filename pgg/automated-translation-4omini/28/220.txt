발행일	1930년 07월 01일  
기사제목	朝鮮歷史日誌, 七月錄 (Korean History Journal, July Record)

Korean History Journal, July Record  
1st (6th of June)  
The Korean and Japanese Red Cross Society merged. (Gwangmu 10)  
The Mining Act is promulgated. (Same year)  
Four treaties delegating judicial authority to Japan are established. (Ryeonghe 3)  

2nd (7th of June)  
Kim Bang-gyeong once again defeats the Yuan troops at Iggyudo Island. (Goryeo, King Jongnyeol 7)  
Purchasing of over ten thousand volumes of books from Song is undertaken. (Goryeo, King Jongsuk, Gapsin)  

3rd (8th of June)  
Lee Taejo exiles Goryeo King Woon to Ganghwa.  
Lee Joon, the mastermind of the Hague Incident, commits seppuku in The Hague. (Gwangmu 11)  

4th (9th of June)  
That night, a military coup occurs. (King Lee Tae, Jeong-woo)  
The combined forces of the Yi Clan suffer a major defeat in Japan due to a typhoon. (Goryeo, King Jongnyeol, Sinmyeong)  
The National Railways Regulation is published. (Gye 505)  

5th (10th of June)  
The Red Ginseng Monopoly Act is promulgated. (Ryeonghe 2)  
Japanese envoy Hanbong Gishi returns home due to military disturbances. (King Lee Tae, Jeong-gap)  

6th (11th of June)  
King Danjong abdicates the throne in favor of King Sejo. (Byungja)  
Lee Deok-hyeon meets with Japanese envoy Hyeon-so in Daedong River. (King Seonjo, Jeongjin)  

7th (12th of June)  
King Seonjo sets out from Pyeongyang towards Yeongbyeon. (Jeongjin)  
Lee Tae acknowledges abdication. (Gwangmu 11)  

8th (13th of June)  
Korean and Japanese soldiers engage in fierce battle within West Gate. (Same year)  
Shin Jeong-hui holds negotiations with the Japanese envoy at Namsan Old Pavilion. (King Lee Tae, Gapmu)  

9th (14th of June)  
The Six Ministries and various departments are abolished and a new department minister system is established. (Gye 503)  

10th (15th of June)  
Goryeo's Founder, Taejo, ascends to the throne. (Muin)  
This day is designated as Flowing Head Festival. (Goryeo, King Myeongjong, Eulsi)  
Qing envoy Yuan Shikai withdraws. (King Lee Tae, Gapmu)  

11th (16th of June)  
Kim Bang-gyeong returns victorious from Jeju. (Wonjong, Gye-yo)  

12th (17th of June)  
Goryeo relocates the capital to Ganghwa and constructs a palace. (Gojong, Jeongjin)  

13th (18th of June)  
The Newspaper Law is enacted. (Gwangmu 10)  
The Korean-Japanese Seven Clause Treaty is established. (Ryeonghe, First Year)  

14th (19th of June)  
Lee Yi-myeong is independently appointed. (King Sukjong, Dongyu)  
The Korean-Japanese Commercial Regulations are published. (Gye 490)  

15th (20th of June)  
Sung Mun-jo and five ministers die defending the country. (King Sejo, Byungja)  
The Korean Bank Ordinance is promulgated. (Ryeonghe 3)  
The cornerstone ceremony for Gyeongbok Palace is established. (King Lee Tae, Eulcho)  

16th (21st of June)  
The Security Act is executed. (Gwangmu 10)  
The Communications Bureau is abolished. (Same Year)  
Jeong Moon-gyeong passes away. (Injo, Sinmyeong)  

17th (22nd of June)  
Silla aids Tang General Liu Ren-gui in attacking Goguryeo. (Silla, King Munmu, Mujeong)  
The detailed regulations for the Mining Act and sand mining methods are executed. (Gwangmu 10)  

18th (23rd of June)  
The Regulations for the Management of Postal Station Land are promulgated. (Ryeonghe 2)  
The Korean-Russian Land Trade Treaty is established. (Ryeonghe 2)  

19th (24th of June)  
The Reform Government’s edict is published. (King Lee Tae, Gapmu)  
The army is abolished and disbandment orders are issued. (Ryeonghe, First Year)  
The Imperial Guard Cavalry is organized. (Same Year 3)  

20th (25th of June)  
Ming General Jo Seung-hun leads troops to aid. (King Seonjo, Jeongjin)  
From the 21st (6th month of the lunar calendar) to the 25th (6th month of the lunar calendar) - summary begun.  

26th (July 1)  
King Injong has passed away. (First Year, Eulsi)  
Silla's King Munmu passes away. He is buried on the east coast. (21st Year, Sinmyeong)  

27th (2nd of July)  
The Empress Dowager of the Yuan grants 17,000 volumes of secret books from Song. (Goryeo, King Jongsuk, Gapsin)  
The era name is changed to Gwangmu. (King Lee Tae, Jeongyu)  

28th (3rd of July)  
The Uniform Labor Tax law is implemented. (Yeongcho, Gyeongmu)  

29th (4th of July)  
The Jurchens request peace and the fortresses are returned. (Goryeo, King Yuijong, Gichou)  

30th (5th of July)  
British ships dock at Hongsu Ancient Island to request trade but are denied and retreat. (Heonjeong, Jeongjin)  

31st (6th of July)  
Choi Yu threatens the king to return to Ganghwa. (Goryeo, King Gojong, Jeongjin)  

**Summary**  

This article provides a detailed chronicle of historical events in Korea throughout July, focusing on political maneuvers, wars, and legislative changes. It highlights significant events such as military defeats, treaties, and the establishment of various laws affecting governance and trade. Historians may find interest in the interactions between Korea and Japan during this period, illustrating the complexities of colonial rule and the political tensions that defined the time.
