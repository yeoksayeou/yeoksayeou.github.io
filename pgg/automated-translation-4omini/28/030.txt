開闢社略史  
The 3rd of September in the year of the Earth Pig (1919) during the Taisho era was when the Mansei Incident occurred. Li Tunhwa, Bak Dacheong, Li Duseong, and other individuals, who had the desire to initiate a New Culture Movement in Joseon, thought it essential to first establish a platform for public discourse. Thus, they proposed the founding of the Kaebyuksa and began preparations, but due to a lack of funds, they faced difficulties. Fortunately, Choijeongyeong, a philanthropist from the Cheondogyo community in Bokcheon, donated 1,000 won, and Byeon Gunhaeng donated 500 won. With this as the foundation, they submitted an application for permission to publish a magazine under the Press Law on December 20 of the same year. This was approved on May 20 of the following year, 1920, and the first issue was published on June 25 of that year, with Li Tunhwa as the editor, Li Duseong as the publisher, Min Yeongsun as the printer, and Choijeongyeong as the president. The office was located at 34 Songhyeondong (currently the upstairs of the Busan College), where they borrowed a small heated room from the Cheondogyo community, which at that time was the headquarters in Seoul. The decision to mark July as the founding month was because the first issue of the magazine was published in July. In November of that year, while publishing the 5th issue, it was changed from being published on the 25th of every month to the 1st of every month. In December, an article by Yu Jinhee (the article featured in the December issue) became controversial, resulting in a 50 won fine from the Seoul District Court.

On June 1 of the 3rd year, 1922, the women's magazine "Woman" was launched, with Li Tunhwa as the publisher and Min Yeongsun as the printer. In September, a publishing department was newly established to begin publishing general ideational literature, and from October, they were granted permission for the inclusion of current affairs documentation in the Kaebyuk magazine (initially limited to religion, academia, and literature).

In the 4th year, 1923, on March 1, the children's magazine "Eorini" was launched (publisher: Bang Jeonghwan, printer: Min Yeongsun). Additionally, in order to connect with their fellow comrades, a system of society membership was devised, and on September 1 of that year, the women's magazine was renamed "New Woman," with the first issue being published. The publisher was Bak Dacheong, and Min Yeongsun printed it — the publisher changed to Bang Jeonghwan from the 3rd edition onward.

In the 6th year, 1925, on August 1, the publication of Kaebyuk magazine was suspended, which lasted for two and a half months, being lifted on October 5.

In the 7th year, 1926, on August 1, the publication of Kaebyuk magazine was prohibited. In October of that year, "New Woman" ceased publication, and on November 1, the first issue of the hobby magazine "Byeolgeongon" was published — publisher: Li Il, printer: Min Yeongsun. From the 14th issue (Showa 3, July 1), the publisher changed to Cha Sangchan, and from the 24th issue (Showa 4, December) onward, the printer changed to Jeon Junseong.

In the 10th year, 1929, on March 1, a students' magazine was launched — publisher: Bang Jeonghwan, printer: Jeon Junseong.

Between the 4th year, 1923, from March to the 6th year, 1925, in November, an investigation was conducted in all 13 provinces of Korea to disseminate basic reference knowledge for all movements and to promote public culture via lectures and exhibitions held at various times.

**Summary**

This article presents a detailed history of the Kaebyuksa, highlighting its founding, publications, and societal contributions during the Japanese colonial period in Korea. The establishment of the Kaebyuksa was significant in promoting discourse through various publications, especially targeting underrepresented groups such as women and children. Historians may find it particularly intriguing that despite the restrictive colonial environment, there was a concerted effort to foster cultural and intellectual growth among the populace, evidenced by the launch of various magazines and efforts toward public education.

開闢社略史
萬歲 事件이 이러나던 己未年(大正8年-西紀1919) 9月 2月이다. 먼저부터 朝鮮에 新文化運動을 이르키기로 뜻을 두엇던 李敦化, 朴達成, 李斗星 外 某某氏는 그 運動을 이르키는데는 무엇보다도 먼저 言論機關을 設置하는 것이 必要하다 생각하고 伊日에 開闢社의 創立을 發議하고 萬般의 準備에 着手하얏스나 資金이 업슴으로 困難을 늣기던 中 마츰 博川에 잇는 天道敎人 中 篤志家 崔宗楨氏는 一金 一千圓也, 邊君恒氏는 一金 五百圓也를 寄附함으로 此를 基本으로 하고 同年 12月 20日에 第一番 事業으로 新聞紙法에 依한 言論雜誌 『開闢』의 發行 許可願을 當局에 提出하엿던 바 翌2年 庚申(1920) 5月 20日에 此가 許可되야 同年 6月 25日에 創刊號를 發行하얏스니 그때에 編輯人이 李敦化, 發行人은 李斗星, 印刷人은 閔泳純, 社長은 崔宗楨이엿고 位置는 松峴洞 34番地이엿스며(지금 普專校 바루 웃집인데 當時 天道敎 京城 敎區로서 本社에서 事務室로 借用한 것은 僅一間 半溫突房이엿다.) 本社의 創立을 七月로 定한 것은 이 開闢의 創刊號가 7月로 發行된 까닭이엿다. 그 해 11月에 第5號를 發行하며 從來 每月 25日에 發行하엿던 것을 每月 1日 發行으로 變更하고 同 12月에 兪鎭熙氏의 記事(卽 12月號 記事)가 問題되야 京城地法院에서 罰金 50圓의 判決을 밧다.
第3年 壬戌(大正11年) 6月 1日에는 女性雜誌 『婦人』을 創刊하고, 發行人 李敦化, 印刷人 閔泳純, 同 9月에는 出版部를 新設하야 一般 思想書類의 出版을 開始하고 同 10月부터 開闢誌에 時事 記載의 許可를 엇다.(發行 當時에는 宗敎, 學術, 文藝에 限함)
第4年 癸亥(大正12年) 3月 1日에는 少年雜誌 『어린이』를 創刊하고(發行人 方定煥, 印刷人 閔泳純) 또 널이 同志를 連絡키 爲하야 社友制를 創案하고 同年 9月 1日에는 婦人雜誌를(23號까지 發行) 新女性으로 改題하야 第一號를 發行하다. 發行人 朴達成, 印刷人 閔泳純-第3號부터 方定煥으로 發行人 變更-
第6年 乙丑(大正14年) 8月 1日에는 開闢誌의 發行停止處分을 當하야 2個月 半되던 10月 5日에 解停되다.
第7年 丙寅(大正15年) 8月 1日에 開闢誌의 發行禁止를 當하다. 同年 10月에 新女性을 休刊하고 同 11月에 1日에 趣味雜誌 『別乾坤』 創刊號를 發行하다. -發行人 李乙, 印刷人 閔泳純-第14號(昭和3年 7月 1日)부터 發行人은 車相瓚, 第24號(昭和4年 12月)부터＜8＞ 印刷人은 田畯成으로 變更함-
第10年 己巳(昭和4年) 3月 1日에는 學生雜誌를 創刊하다-發行人 方定煥, 印刷人 田畯成-
尙又 第4年 癸亥(大正12年) 3月부터 第6年 乙丑(大正14年) 11月까지 3年間 朝鮮 13道 道勢를 調査하야 모든 運動의 基本 參考 智識을 普及케 하고 民衆文化 向上을 爲하야 巡廻講演, 展覽會 等을 隨時 開催하얏다.
