World Night Talks  
Seoul Night Talks  
Kim Il-han  

With only 4 or 50 jeon, one can find comfort in a theater, and with just a single 10-jeon brass coin, one can enjoy coffee from "Brazil" while gazing at a beautiful waitress in a cafe. If such options did not exist, how much longer would the youth of Seoul endure their mundane and tasteless lives filled with isolation and boredom? 

It goes without saying that there is no freedom of assembly comparable to that of Western so-called "clubs" or even what neighboring countries may provide. For the young people of this land, who can barely attend a single lecture or concert throughout the entire year, theaters and cafes serve truly as oases in a desert of despair, functioning even as social facilities at times.

That is why I frequently visit cafes.

It was a year ago, last summer. Like any other day, after a tiring day at the newspaper office, I returned to my boarding house around five in the afternoon, having done little more than eat dinner, and lay down quietly on the floor. Suddenly, the door burst open with the sound of "Mr. Kim!" and in came the broad, large face of Liu-kun. 

"Wow! It's been a while!"  

"It has really been a long time."  

"So, how's it been?"  

"Nothing out of the ordinary."  

"But, Kim-kun! Aren't you going out anywhere?"  

"Where would I go if there's nowhere to go?"  

"Isn't there even a place to go?"  

"Of course there is."  

"Then hurry up and let's go. If you go to Honmachi 3-chome, there's a cafe called 'Wistaria' where there's a splendid 'moga' (modern girl). It seems she recently came from Tokyo. Not only is she pretty, but I hear she speaks very well too. Her name is Mori Mitsuko, or something like that... Now, let's go quickly! What are you doing just lying around like this?"  

Liu-kun, a close friend of mine, has a cheerful and vibrant personality, which is always his strong point. However, at times he can chatter away nonsensically, earning him the nickname Liu Cannon, which is a small flaw, but not a significant one.

Still, at that moment, I had nowhere to go and no specific reason to be involved. So I decided to leave the boarding house with Liu-kun.

The cafe 'Wistaria' is located just a bit southward from the Honmachi 3-chome intersection, near the former governor's office, in a stately three-story Western-style building, which already looked very impressive from the outside.

We had made a detour on purpose to stroll around the Jongno night market toward Honmachi, but when we arrived at the cafe, it was still not quite nine o'clock, so because it was too early, there were hardly any customers—only two Japanese men who looked as if they had just come in after bathing, holding towels, and were drinking soda and the like. 

As we settled at one of the corner tables, a waitress who had apparently been waiting for us came over with a lovely smile, saying "Irasshai" (welcome). Liu-kun, being familiar with her, remarked, "Hikari-chan! Today is quite a rare occasion, isn’t it?" She smiled back, asking, "Why?" When Liu-kun replied, "It seems like we’ve been able to meet sooner than usual," she responded with "Well, it’s still early, isn’t it?" While this happened, she shyly looked away as if hiding her face behind her hands. 

"Ah! So this is the waitress 'Mori Mitsuko!'” I thought to myself, looking closely. The girl wore a short black skirt, a fluttering white blouse, a bright red necktie visible on her neck, and elegant high-heeled shoes, with her hair styled just so that it looked much like that of a primary school teacher or perhaps a normal college student. 

To describe her looks, rather than calling her a beauty, it would be more fitting to say she resembled a refined and charming young lady rather than someone from the cafe. Her semi-ordinary appearance carried a sense of elegance and delicacy.

At first glance, I was utterly taken by her. Leaving aside everything else, in the cafes one typically sees hardly anyone good-looking, their faces smothered with layers of thick white powder. This one girl, however, had made no effort to cover her skin with makeup, which could have been a reason enough for me to like her.

Liu-kun would boastfully say, "See? My taste is certainly refined!" and then he ordered a bottle of beer.

From that moment on, I found myself returning to Wistaria whenever there was an opportunity, always in the same red skirt. In addition to my delightful banter with Mitsuko, I also lamented the long summer nights on occasion. Once, I had savored an ice cream infused with delightful scents while listening to the lively sounds of jazz from the café and spent another month with her under a moonlit sky, intentionally dimming the lights to hear her sing melancholic melodies like 'Sōkyōkyoku' and other songs.

However, I had no idea about her circumstances regarding why she had come to Korea, which I later discovered was related. So, once in a while, when I asked her, "You don't seem like a typical waitress. What brings you to Korea? Could you tell me?" she would smile coyly and reply, "Don't be so impatient. I'm sure I will return to Tokyo soon. When I do, I will tell you everything," finishing with a sly grin. 

Also, typically, waitresses in cafes are usually quite keen on selling something—whether at a loss to themselves or not—but Mitsuko was absolutely different in that regard. When I ordered a beer, she'd say, "What would a person who doesn't drink medicinal liquor want with beer?" and when I voiced any desire to eat or drink, she would often insist, "I really won't have anything; please don't waste money on me." 

I particularly liked her elegant and non-materialistic character. Thus, while I always kept the thought of her being an "unknown woman" in my mind, I remained strangely drawn to visiting Wistaria more and more.

As Seoul is a small place, Mitsuko soon became famous. Everyone who frequented cafes—club members, bank clerks, government officials, newspaper reporters, writers, school teachers, etc.—flocked to Wistaria. There were times when a single 'moga' would have a cluster of handshaking patrons around her. However, Mitsuko would effectively cater to all, leaving a good impression on everyone who came to see her. Among those who came to see Mitsuko were not a few friends of mine, and sometimes I would find myself coincidentally sharing a space with Mitsuko and one of my friends at the same table, at which point they'd quip, "Hey, Mr. Kim! What a coincidence this is!" 

In these moments, Mitsuko would often quietly express her discomfort, whispering, "I’m ashamed to have such guests at my table..." while licking her lips somewhat uncomfortably.

One night, just the two of us were having tea, and a man wearing a suit unexpectedly approached us, taking a seat at the table beside us. He looked to be around 40, with beady eyes and a rather rough appearance. 

After observing him for a while, Mitsuko suddenly seemed to have had a realization. She quietly took a pencil from my coat pocket and wrote something on the table, glancing mischievously at me. The message read:  

S P Y!  

Upon seeing that, I looked back at the man in the suit, and judging by his demeanor, he seemed either to be a detective or, at the very least, someone involved in law enforcement.

Later, Mitsuko asked me various questions about the state of social movements in Korea and inquired about women's movements, as well as the lifestyle and culture of Koreans in detail. 

"When will our paradise be built? Of course, it’s vague, but don’t despair. Yes, indeed! Let’s persevere!" she said, concluding with a passionate tone. 

At that moment, I clearly realized that Mitsuko was truly not an ordinary woman, not only as a waitress but as an individual. 

After an unusual event, Mitsuko suddenly disappeared from Wistaria without a trace. When I asked other waitresses, they said they didn’t know where she had gone but seemed to believe she lived in Meiji-chō.

While I was curious about this strange turn of events, about a week later, I received a phone call from Mitsuko. 

"I’m really sorry. Something urgent came up, and I had to move to a cafe named 'Winsol' inside the Namdaemun gate. Please come by; I'll explain everything when you arrive." 

That night at Winsol, as we talked, she explained that a spy incident had occurred at Wistaria, and thus the owner had to relocate there.

She smiled widely, saying, "It was quite a troubling place, you know!"  

Then she grew serious and continued, "But ultimately, I must return to Tokyo. Though the date isn’t set, I might leave suddenly, and it’s possible that I won’t be able to say goodbye." 

Her expression was weary.

Indeed, after a month or so, Mitsuko vanished forever from the cafe 'Winsol'. 

Therefore, I thought I would never comprehend Mitsuko's true identity and attempted to forget the strange events that had happened in the past. But shortly after she had disappeared from Winsol, I unexpectedly learned about her identity.

This happened by chance when I heard from a person from the OO Military Police, who was aware of the operations being conducted in the area. He explained that "Mori Mitsuko" was a former lover of SN, a key leader of the Japanese OO Party involved in a 5.3 incident last year, who had sought refuge in Korea for a while and had masqueraded as a waitress. It appears that as her true identity was becoming clearer, she had to escape back to Japan once again, and "Mori Mitsuko" was simply a pseudonym. 

Upon hearing this, I reflected and realized that various hints about her past behavior might have indicated something of that nature. 

After Mitsuko's departure, I received a postcard that simply said Mengshi Oji in Tokyo. It expressed her regrets and that she was now safely in Tokyo after a brief visit to Nara, while apologizing for all the trouble she had caused while in Seoul. 

I showed this postcard to Liu-kun, and he responded, "I knew it from the start. The most troublesome part is how she observed me while leaving and thought to herself about the disappointment in the Korean 'intelligentsia'... They must have acted rather frivolously..." 

Her remark about the Korean 'intelligentsia' left me pondering about how this applied to myself, turning my face hot with embarrassment. 

Every evening now, the sound of the 'pentora's* lively jazz music still drifts out from various cafes throughout the city.  

"When red lights and blue lights come together," 

When I hear such songs from these cafes, memories of past events and the fantasy of the missing 'Mori Mitsuko' come back to mind. 

However, I still have no idea where the revolutionary’s beloved is now or how to reach her.

Moscow Night Talks   
Pak Hyung-saeng  

The white nights, the parks… 

In this winter wonderland called Seoul, all forms of joy seem to fade under the long winter nights, yet only when I can step outside and drop the heavy shuba (sheepskin coat) off my shoulders can I feel vitality and the zest for life flow in me as I embrace nature. 

As spring breezes through like a condiment, summer peeks its head out between the end of April and the beginning of May, when I find myself on the streetcars alongside fashionable women in airy summer dresses, displaying their arms covered in pure white snow. 

At first, the peculiar scent of their underarms overwhelms my unaccustomed Eastern nose, but slowly I become accustomed to it as I understand the connection between the musky odor of nature and the young girls’ subtle warmth; just as the splendid summer winds carry sweet fragrances in the air.

When summer arrives, the white nights follow. These white nights are like the rich treasure chests of fleeting summer romance, encapsulating the dreams of youth, the very embodiment of love, coupling youth with sweetness! 

Right before summer fades, in the midst of the shimmering stars, the remaining glimmers of dusk merge with the first dawn, creating an enchanting ambiance, causing an insatiable restlessness in the young souls.

Step out to the park! You will come to understand the pains of deserted, lonely Eastern men wandering through the backstreets of Moscow like shadows. 

Lend me your ears! The rhythm from kisses, the sounds of clutter echo from the depths of the shadows—a joiful joyousness!

In the soft murmur of the moonlight, shadows throw themselves against delicate fabric intertwined with whispers, there they are couples nestled close together under the faintly lit trees.

While immersed in sweet, innocent dreams in the atmosphere of the night, surrounded by the lingering scent of spirits, I find joy as I stroll through these moonlit nights. Young Russians, braving the biting cold of winter, roam the streets and enjoy their leisure, even amidst howling winds.

"Kitaits!" 

One woman, addressing me with curiosity shining in her eyes, smiles at me and points. 

"Ho-za! A Chink!" (An expression sometimes used in a derogatory sense for Chinese people). 

The other girl sneers. She wears a bright red lipstick while her expression betrays blatant disdain yet her eyes glimmer with curiosity sending waves of stimuli to my insides. 

With this intrigue beginning to wane, I faced her bravely and joined her on the following trail. 

"Let me befriend you!" 

Having learned their customs, I drew nearer. 

"Is that your answer? Are you a passing Chinese?" 

"No. I just want to know you better!"  

"You’re quite bold for a Chinese." 

I shot back saying, "And you’re a Tatars’ girl, aren’t you?"  

"No, I am a Russian girl!" 

Knowing the distaste they have for Tatars, I decided to counter their assumptions, and the effect was instantaneous. They understood that being labeled Tatars was derogatory, eliciting gasps. 

"You see me as one who will still rely on such labels?" 

"That is just words! Are you really a Chinese? Do you know Sun Yat-sen?" 

"If I must—though I prefer a different topic. Let's link arms and stroll about!" 

"You, Chink, don't you dare!" 

Hearing that from a fellow, this phrase struck me: "Oh, should I not be walking side by side with a gorgeous Russian being held with such disdain?" 

With the air of arrogance dissipated, I decided calmly to seek her house instead. 

"Don't be strange. Why don’t we head to my place? I would like to invite you!"

Unnoticeably, I can sense her urgency; by dawn we could have danced together for hours meaninglessly if I had not finally spoken up declaring, "Unfortunately, I can’t tonight since I have a student council meeting!" 

The girl… she turned out to be a common kind. Without any more words, she drew up my arm, wanting to guide me to her doorway. 

I dashed off from them quickly abandoning our secluded corner of the park and, not wishing to attract attention from her acquaintances, the next group hurled insults at me. 

"Chink! A penniless beggar!" 

Russians do have a notorious reputation for drink-related excess. The frigid cold in the cold tundra leads to an insatiable thirst for alcohol, mirroring the frenzy that emerges in their spirits. 

Following the revolution, they entered a phase of abstinence; however, with the establishment of the NEP, breweries began operating again, showcasing a resurgence in their alcoholic pleasures. Patrons gather at the vodka house with pennies to quench their thirst during times of adverse weather. 

Even in times of relatively recent alcohol prohibition, I had the common sight of long lines of cold people waiting outside liquor stores since the Russian winter does not stop the desire to partake. 

For hours young and old would bear the frost in the early morning, waiting to buy just one bottle. 

Yet this is not the sole image of Moscow. More vividly displayed are the struggles of its alcoholic patrons. Crude bravery reveals those seeking connection and companionship along the roads of this mysterious festive commune.

Some even manage to end life, sacrificing all of their struggles merely seeking solace. "Should I succumb to my anger, it is clear you foolish child bear no knowledge of my tumult—your apologies are thin and insincere!" 

Instead of pitying themselves, intoxicated souls drown in despair. 

The process of building up beacons of hope, revolutionary sentiments, and social connections rises above the whispers that existed in these alleys, as if hoping to care for the riches they uncover buried in their hearts.

So what comes after each crack of opportunity? These countless souls venture between vibrant displays in the facets and corners of the city, the smell of fragrant delights.

And all these shades dwell within the vast spaces enchanting people departing through despair contrasting in the expanses of Moscow.

Beggar

In Russia, beggars are still numerous. In old imperial times, they were even more prevalent. During times of famine in southern Russia and Ukraine, desperate impoverished souls flocked to the cities. Women were often forced to consort with greedy revelers echoing familiar tales of despair. 

It appears some such customs to this day still linger. 

Beggars abound, and so do those who throw coins to charitable souls. They share a strong sense of community. 

This might be attributed to the advanced mutual assistance customs and fundamental philosophies instilled within their culture. 

To dismantle the web of their rationale is challenging. They would rather stand beside each other and proffer assistance rather to suppress another’s desperation, all thanks to their ingrained religious sentiments against selfish individuals.

These beggars set up their characters before temples (Moscow itself boasts over 3,000 churches), actively calling out unsolicited offers even while on streetcars where they are often met with hostility. 

Their careless attitude relies on the hope of kindness from those passing for survival. 

Should anybody witness this foul-mouthed beggar barely clinging to existence under their whiff of arrogance from a hapless elderly individual shout, "Hey! Mister! Could you spare a penny? Just a penny?" 

They ask me with their earnest desperation, from elder to infant, yet when they hear nothing they drip disdain for the ones who turn away devoid of sympathy.

Moscow

This is the city wherein the Third International exists, where the Comintern operates, where the Kremlin stands, and where Lenin's tomb rests—it’s not a city consumed by pleasure. 

There thrives the world's proletariat’s heart beset by capitalist imperialists. 

Meanwhile, the will and cunning of the Soviet laborers and peasants overflow within this bastion of resilience. 

To seek a trivial form of recreation here is a grave mistake. Such leisure is not permitted to flourish; it has become a salient yet punishable affair amongst the youth of the party and its comrades to squander vitality, oftentimes forbidden altogether. 

What of dance floors, those again bereft of music and pleasures, must one engage gallantly amongst the decadent disposal so rampant? 

Better would it be to go toward Harbin, that city of pleasures, a city flaring in chaos. 

Moscow has its beauty in abundance. Unsurpassed beauty embedded in glorious, charismatic females wrapped effortlessly within the spaces of artistic intimacy—one’s heart might ignite with warmth in these pockets of society. 

Experience the performances that ensnare both body and spirit on a summer's night while witnessing wonders orchestrated by talented comrades. Visit the reputable establishments participating in fostering aspirations and encouraging vibrant discussions filled with exhilarating sensations of joy and wonder!

For those who do find themselves in Moscow, expect indeed an influx of fascination and profound connections during these fleeting visits.  

Shanghai Night Talks  
A Shanghai Resident  

Shanghai is vast and extravagant. With massive arches and a wide-open doorway, the Customs House rises high like a twelve-story palace, gently washing across the skyline, and the clock tower reaches toward the sky. The bright, white exterior of the Hong Kong-Shanghai Bank constructs a mile-long snaking formation of luxury passenger cars. The harbor and Huangpu River bustling with vibrant ships and vessels; on both land and water, banners and symbols, with the Union jack, the French tricolor, and the flags of all the nations present all dapple the scenery—all showcasing the cosmopolitan beauty of Shanghai! 

The glimmering cafes boast vibrant lights casting shadows upon the street, with girls adorned in fashionable dresses adorned with newfound elegance. The sense of excitement embodies the spirit of Shanghai as two sailors regroup at the helm of opportunity; meanwhile, dimly lit dark alleyways whisper tales shared amongst addicts and stragglers under the careful watch of the dogs double tasked with drink searching. 

The city of demons, Shanghai! 

Hostages! The Communist Party! Pirates! Assassins! Kidnappers! Drug traffickers! Corrupt politicians! 

All of them tread paths deeply embedded within Shanghai nightly, scheming and plotting both out of sight and mild winds fluttering forcefully. 

Then comes the avalanche of 160,000 unemployed citizens and their hungry families! 

Dignitaries and wealthy merchants fear walking alone. If the officials must venture outdoors, they must summon forces of arms and guards to accompany them on their route! 

The common journal might announce, "On a certain date, at a certain hour, a significant commander, a prominent chief, will arrive at a station." Yet, upon arrival, he is nowhere to be found, emerging only from freight cars or out from behind a curtain as a mere illusion. 

Should you happen to discover an amiable host, they may indulge extra dollars prior to stowing away on the appointed express, a common game for dignitaries, but the ordinary man cannot bear the emboldened shadows dancing outside clubs.

Murderous power resides within assassin's grasp!

Countless threats of inhumanity loom above one’s head! 

Men seize victimized gentlemen while on their way to work, abducting them in broad daylight.

Children chase after classmates; they’re oblivious to the lurking dangers that arrest the wealthy bureaucrat's son while walking about during school hours. 

Everyone connected to the high echelons of Shanghai lives in anticipatory terror. 

The sister company, the South Sea Brother Tobacco Company located within Shanghai's east section, has a vast estate supported by capital, yet they now function with difficulty resembling a tortuous endeavor to stay afloat. Dozens of factories have been rendered inactive, placing thousands of laborers out of work!

From August 1928 to December 1929, there were a staggering 378 labor disputes recorded! 

Needing to survive, individuals turn to the last resort. The laborers, overcome by the crushing weight of their sorrowful plight, become soldiers bound to any criminal act. 

If they cannot, they join the hordes of beggars lining the streets, existing beyond despair, sinking into misery. 

Silks and stockings are sold alongside rumors of fortune—the glittering façade of Shanghai. 

Golden faces illuminate across families, yet families have now diminished to bundles of misery. 

Amidst this turbulence lies the abyss. Countless businesses are hidden under the haze of oppression, flowing over with woes while blood and sacrifices writhed beneath the bricks of the expired market. 

Feats of unimaginable pain emerge alongside brutal assaults! 

The chaos pouring into Shanghai inundates the realm with incessant noise, fluctuating between screams of lamentation of friends against animals and tricksters. 

Moments of fleeting beauty might reflect from a sliver of opportunity—a belief in what is truly seen aboard the steamer, for the magnified failures are obscured behind grandiose specters of shimmering joys wherein poignancy awaits those treaded endless paths.

The Yangtze river churns with torrents of muddied waters within Shanghai. 

From East and West, across the city, time and tide combine as a wave of myriad rooftops collide. 

Beauty, ugliness, wickedness, and poverty all submerged into the Huangpu and Suzhou rivers—a deluge of cacophony! 

Squalid homes muddied with neglect fuse within divisions of awful dispensations—a fortification built through time and illness remorselessly trained upon them.

By sheer statistics, one family may dwell amongst a single tenement, or possibly four families trash one dwelling, haunts cast within the crowded houses—burdens deepening and rotting in despair.

They pay merely a sixth of their earnings for rent, yet their debts loom, a distressing struggle to face the kindling flames of oblivion.  

The city intends to establish order while mercilessly resolving to eradicate shantytowns—issuing orders for removal; refusing to comply, they reignite the flames of threatening measures—but stubborn they remain, always moving from site A to B disguised as unwanted dissidents. 

Authorities surveil daily expenses that embolden lies!  

Records show that 25,615 people scrape together a rife income—of which 113,500 individuals endure in those conditions.  

Period from 24 pennies to even 28 pennies into 8 in cash—silver notes have literally plummeted in value.

At once fortunes dwindled by half, while external debts burgeoned an unthinkable ten million. 

Once a millionaire's team dismissed 450,000 but lost it shortly after.

China now dressing itself in the cloak of extreme poverty—the most base among nations.  

The latest report of extravagance arises along with heightened trepidation of catastrophic waves—the turmoil of misfortune casts shadows unveiling dread covering the existing legacy.

In this tumultuous environment, expectations soar while anxiety burdens many.

Those who once lived lavishly plunge themselves beyond their shores toward their upon probabilities leaving cities. 

All bulges uprooted yet hope remains!

Music, gambling, home, rebellion!

Life in Shanghai has undergone a grim metamorphosis through cycles of violence steadily amplifying, leaving disappointed whispers in the mature plazas resounding!

Paris Night Talks   
Lee Jeong-seop  

Those familiar with the life of crackling Paris praise the vibrant night life even more than daylight Paris. 

The day reveals high art and exquisite delicacies; however, night Paris blooms splendidly in a myriad of colors under the Champlain's bright lights. The night overflows with "jazz" as the city dances and revels.

Is Paris a city devoid of sleep? 

Indeed, amidst nocturnal pleasures, if Paris lacks its essence, what lies beneath? 

Of the many sleepless cities, a particular place known as "Place de la Concorde" shines above all others. 

The assembly of thousands of candles beaming brilliantly forms an elaborate city as dazzling as the sun. On Sunday evenings, a night speaker walks through the gatherings of English friends enchanted by this radiance, attempting to elicit my take. 

"Fantastic, however, is it not the extravagance of French indulgence?" 

I respond, "Indeed, yet your saying is a display of the British obsession with practicality. Life itself can never mimic the rigid abstractions of cubism and must hold the Kreutzer sonata entangled in wild curves as its melody flows forth." 

I elaborated on the philosophical implications of one's daily existence.

As one wanders through the poetically plentiful Place de la Concorde to the northeast, one finds themselves within the famed "Montmartre," a richly provocative sight—invitingly convened in the heart of night-time Paris, beckoning all to participate in lively revelries of modern nightlife. 

Each step at the dance hall, with the stunning modern Parisian women brushing against the figurines of notoriety, remarking in flight of delight, all engaged in intense conversation with the sharp reds against the glitter. 

"Our visits night and day cost twenty francs!" 

To enjoy chic vulgarity is to simply revel, step aspiring through this lively Parisian atmosphere, all at a frivolous twenty francs—a fiscal leisure in harmonious contrast to other forms of entertainment back home wrapped in the idea of grandiosity.

The notable "Moulin Rouge" lies entrenched here, beckoning artistic exposure with the famed "Josephine Baker" showcasing her talents with gilded décor adorned with prowess in each pour of passion. 

Not only the "Moulin Rouge" calls—places like "Casino de Paris" and "Folies Bergère" shine, resplendent with opulence decorating monologues at the forefront. 

Costs of scenes can transcend from one to two million dollars in extravagant shows, revealing just how breath-taking these facilities really are. Without a doubt, "Follies Bergère" is stanchioned with performances that extend for six months, or perhaps even an entire year, yielding wonders that promise perpetual capture.

Consequently, amidst the typical theaters and grand opera halls, there awaits lavish shows and singing beauties fawning over widespread beauty’s signature performances, awaiting the true splendors of the nocturnal visage of delightful Paris. 

Such extravagant theaters do possess a plethora of elusive beauties enchanted and adorned, calling attention to aspects far less seen—that remains in which revelers lose themselves amidst the layers and flows of melody.

However, there remains in every corner unknown to many, an unremarkable old building clinging faintly beside the renowned "Sacré-Cœur." 

Upon first glance seeming to lean into oblivion, within its interior an ordinary lone table filled with mismatched chairs reveals the antiquated decor charms, whispering songs of forgotten times, saturating the festively joyous parties within these spaces—the gathering unwinds, unfurling in the warmth of gathering bonds.

An indisputable delight lingers amongst strangers; conversing through laughter, sharing songs, bridging gaps of social distance weaving through the city—those who have been for long in Paris may well remain unaware of this nearest corner found nearby.

京城夜話
金乙漢

4,50錢만 가저도 하로 저녁의 위안을 어들 수 잇는 劇場과 10錢짜리 白銅錢 한푼만 잇서도 「뿌라질」에서 온 「커피」에 兼하야 美人 「웨이트레스」까지 볼 수 잇는 「카페-」조차 업다면 서울의 젊은이들은 갓득이나 固塞하고 乾燥無味한 생활에 얼마나 더 寂寞을 늣길 것인가?
西洋 사람들의 所謂 『俱樂部』가튼 것은 말할 것도 업거니와 이웃나라 사람만큼도 集會의 自由가 업서서 일년 가야 講演 가튼 講演 하나 들을 수 업고 음악회 가튼 음악회 하나 볼 수 업는 이 곳 이 따의 젊은 사람에게 잇서서 극장과 「카페-」는 실로 사막 중의 「오아시쓰」와 가티 다시 업는 위안꺼리가 되는 것이며 따러서 혹 엇던 때에는 일종의 社交機關까지도 되는 것이다.
그러기에 나는 종종 「카페-」를 간다.

별서 일년 전인 작년 녀름 일이다. 그 날도 다른 날과 맛찬가지로 온 종일을 신문사에서 쪼들이다가 오후 다섯 시나 되어 하숙에 돌아와 저녁이라고 먹는 둥 마는 둥하고 방바닥을 자리 삼어 가만히 누어 잇스려니까 「미스터 김!」소리와 함께 방문이 와락 열리며 柳君의 넓적하고 커다란 얼골이 쑥 들어온다.
『여- 이게 얼마 만인가?』
『참 오래간만일세』
『그래 재미가 엇대』

柳君은 나의 親友의 한 사람으로 서글서글하고 快活한 성격이 항상 그의 장점이다. 그러나 또 때로는 엉뚱하게 헛풍을 잘처서 柳大砲라는 별명까지 듯는 결점이 아조 업지도 안엇다.
그러치만 그때의 나는 가려 해야 갈 데도 업섯거니와 또 별로히 볼일도 업섯슴으로 엇잿던 柳君과 함에 하숙을 나서기로 하엿다.

카페- 「위스타리아」는 本町 3丁目 네거리에서 남쪽으로 前 統監府가 잇는 편으로 조곰을 나가자면 바로 동쪽에 잇는 삼층 양옥의 카페-로, 외관부터도 매우 당당한 것이엿다.

柳君은 아조 구면인드시 「光ちやん! けふは 大分珍らしいねー」(오날온 참 희안하구려-」라고 우스며 말을 건느니 그 여자도 역시 얼골에 미소를 띄우며 「どうして?」(왜요?」라고 말한다. 그리고 柳君이 다시 「いつになく直ぐ會へたかう」(전애 업시 곳 맛날 수 잇스니」라고 말하자 「だつて又早いもの」(무얼요, 아직 일러 그러치요」라 하며 이번에는 또 볼타구니가 쏙 드러갈 만큼 어업비 우서 보힌다.
「오-ㄹ치, 이 웨트레스가 「모리, 미쓰꼬」로구나! 나는 속으로 이러케 생각하며 다시 한번 자세히 보니 그 녀자는 「스카-트」가 짜른 검정 치마에 하늘하늘하는 흰 웃통을 닙고 그 우에는 샛빨간 「넥타이」를 보기좃케 매엿스며 발에는 굽 놉흔 구두, 머리는 그럴 듯이 보이는 7분 3분- 어듸로 보든지 차림차림이 소학교 선생이나 그러치 안으며 사범학교 학생의 「타입」이지 아무리 보아도 路柳墻花의 신세 가튼 카페-의 「웨이트레스」 갓지는 안엇다.
더욱히 그 여자의 좁지도 넓지도 안은 보기 조흔 이마와 영리한 두 눈과 균형된 코와 밋 애교 잇는 두 입술을 가진 둥글 넙적한 얼골은 미인이라느니 보다는 차라리 남의 집 맛며나리감이라는 말이 더 적당할 만치 고상하고 귀염성스러웁고 또 婦德이 잇서 보혓다.
나는 첫눈에 그 여자가 마음에 들엇다. 다른 것은 다 고만두고라도 카페-에 잇는 게집이라는 게집은 모조리 그리 잘생기지도 못한 얼골에 회빡가치 粉을 뒤집어 쓰건만은 다만 한 사람 이 여자만은 도모지 분을 발른 형적이 업서서 끗업시 검소하고 또 자연스러웁게 보히는 한가지 사실만 가지고도 내가 그 여자를 조와하는 이유가 되기에 충분하엿다.
柳君은 여봐란드시 「엇대? 나의 鑑賞眼이 상당하지!」하고 자랑하드시 말하며 우선 麥酒 한 병을 청하엿다.

그 후의 나는 똑가튼 갑이면 당홍치마라고 카페를 갈 만한 기회만 잇스면 반드시 위스다리아를 차저갓다.
그래서 「모리 미쓰코」를 맛나서 쓸데업는 雜談을 하기에 녀름 밤이 짜름을 한탄한 적도 한두 번이 안이엿다.
어느 때는 「삑토라」의 울멍찬 「째즈」소리를 들으며 香氣 놉흔 아이스크림에 입맛을 다신 적도 잇섯스며 또 어느 달 밝은 밤에는 일부러 電燈불을 끄고 그의 입으로부터＜87＞ 「思鄕曲」의 애처로운 「멜로듸-」와 XX歌를 들은 적도 업지 안엇다.
그러치만 나는 그가 東京 사람으로 엇더한 事情이 잇서서 一時 朝鮮에까지 온 것이라는 것 박게는 도모지 그에 대하야 아는 것이 업섯다.

그러나 혁명가의 안해는 지금에 어듸 잇는지 소식조차 알 길이 도모지 막연하구나...(끗)

白夜, 公園
겨울의 나라 눈 나라의 서울이고 보니 온갖 歡樂은 기나긴 겨울밤을 마자 버러지는 것이지만 아모래도 아득한 지평선을 한계로 속 시원히 퍼진 광야에 두 억개를 눌으는 무거운 슈-바를 버서 바리고 마음놋코 뛰여나와서 자연의 품에 안길 수 잇는 때라야 生氣도 나고 生의 깃붐도 늣기게 된다. 봄이 양념처름 지나가고 뒤를 밧작 따라 여름이 머리를 내밀 때는 南國에서 아즉도 꼿소식이 한창인 4월말 5월초다.

조름 가득한 별들의 殘光에 훠-ㄴ하니 떠올너오는 첫새벽과 가티 앳구진 밤에 界線을 거두어 업새고 깬 듯 잠든 꿈속 가티 甘美한 피로를 살작 잡어 닛그는 慌怳惚境을 잠 못 이루는 젊은 사람들 압헤 펼처주는 것이 백야다. 공원에를 나가보라! 모스크바의 뒷골목을 슬적 둘너 아렉산돌슬카야, 부리발(公園)이나 뿌-찐콥스카야, 부리발에 발을 돌여 노흐면 위선 오나가나 짝 업는 홀애비 東洋人의 비애를 痛切히 늣길 것이다.
이것을 들으라는 듯이 쪽쪽쪽! 그 충동적 리즘이칼한 키쓰 소래, 키쓰의 爆音!
이 잔띄 저 수풀 사이 꿈결가치 아득한 微光에 싸혀 쌍쌍이 陳을 치고 안즌 남녀의 그림자가 엷운 망사를 통해서 보는 것가티 눈에 띄운다.

「怒하섯소. 당신?」
여인은 애수 가득한 눈을 내 코밋해 가저와서 내 얼골을 주시하는 것이엿다.

「괸이 남 놀니지 말어요. 난 학교에 단이지 안어요... ...」
「그럼?」
『뭘 그래 사람두... ... 그러지 말구 우리 집엘 가요 나허구... ... 마차를 불너요.. ... 뭐 멀지두 안어 10환 가젓서 응, 가요. 가지 응?!」

「그럼 5환은 잇서요. 5환말이야!」
나는 아모 말도 못하고 고개를 엽흐로 저으면서
「다시 맛납시다. 오늘은 학교에서 학생 회의가 잇서서 안되... ...」
여인... ... 그도 결국은 그러한 여자엿다... ... 은 無昧한 듯이 자긔 팔에서 나를 노와주엇다.
나는 다라나듯이 황망히 공원 밧그로 달여 나왓다. 동무의 영업을 방해하지 안으려고 저만치 떨어저 잇섯든 그 여자의 동무들이 나의 뒤꼴에다 노-란 우슴을 던젓다.
「춀트! 가난뱅이 기따이츠!」
「워드카」 「삐브나야」
로서아 사람들이 好酒癖이란 말할 수가 *다. 寒帶地方의 혹독한 冷寒이 엇절 수 업시 알콜의 열과 흥을 찻게 한데서 기인한 것이 안인지. 이럿틋 술을 조와하는 로서아 사람들이 혁명 이후 한동안 금주 시기를 지내오느라 그 생몸부림을 치다가 신경제 정책이 實* 된 뒤로 비로소 국가에서 주류를 양조하야 市場에 내여놋케 되자 老朽酒黨들의 깃븜이란 젊은 사람들의 혁명에 대한 狂悅보담도 더 컷섯다. 정부는 주류 판매소를 내여 거긔서 나는 잔돈푼으로 용돈에 보태여 쓰는 것이다. 帝政 時代에도 술만은 國營이엿고 거긔서 나오는 소득은 촬-의 유일 최대한 용돈이엿섯다. 그리하야 1905년 혁명 당시에 로서아 민중은 제일 먼저 촬-의 주류 창고를 파괴하야 촬-에 대한 復讐를 하엿다 한다.
그러나 국영 주류 판매소는 그리 만치 못할 뿐 안이라 하로 판매하는 양에 한도가 잇슴으로 판매소 문전에는 꼭두새벽부터 사람들이 모혀들어 행렬을 整齊하고 문열기를 긔다린다. 그 列이 도로에까지 뻐더저 나와 가지고 네가 먼저 왓느니 내가 먼저 왓느니 하야 승강이가 생길 때도 잇서 경관까지 나와 행렬을 감시하는 것이다. 한기가 영하 20도를 나려와 입김이 외투 깃에 허엿케 얼어 붓는＜92＞ 일흔 아침에도 나는 트웰스카야 街에 잇는 판매소 압헤서 여러 번 이런 행렬을 보앗다. 여러 시간을 남녀노소들이 찬 공기 가운데서 겨우 워트카(火酒-알콜분 40-%) 한 병을 살여고 긔다리고 섯다가 그만 행렬 중간에서 술이 끈어지고 보면 남어지 後半列은 헛되히 뷘 손 치고 돌아서게 되는 때가 만타. 한껏해야 침을 택 벳고 춀트 .욕토요마-치(제-밀부틀... ...)나 한바탕 늘어놀 뿐이다. 그 중에도 우수운 것은 로인 하나가 압헤서 간신히 워드카 한 병을 사들고 나와 그 자리에 서서 벌덕벌덕 해바리고는 입을 싹 씻고 다시 끄덕끄덕 걸어가서 열 꽁문이에 대어서는 것이다.
이와 가티 엇기 어려운 火酒 대신 到處에 「삐-브다야」라 하야 맥주만을 파는 삐-어홀이 잇다. 酒黨들의 미흡한 주량을 여긔서 채운다. 맥주 외에도 간단한 정식을 먹을 수 잇는 곳이다. 시험삼아 한번 들어서서 위선 방 모양을 살펴보면 천정은 얏고 매연에 그으럿스나 방이 꽤 넓다. 두리 마조 안줄 수 잇는 자그마한 테블이 3,40개는 벌어저 잇다. 테블 우에는 얄편한 대리석을 깔기도 하야 호기로운 맛이 잇스나 의자는 형편이 업다. 손때며 기름이 올나 밋긴하야 안즐가 말가 주저하게 된다.
이 곳 저 곳 테블을 점령하고 잇는 손들은 대개가 노동자들로 헐물이한 양복 주머니에 한 손을 수서 박고 다른 팔굼치로 테블을 집고 맥주를 드리키는 사람 집수제가티 된 컵을 아무럿케나 언저쓰고 醉眼움´ 몽롱하야 뷘 컵을 드려다보고 안젓는 사람 한 손으로 뷘 병을 어루만지면서 다른 손으로 마홀카(설은 담배)를 끄내여 신문지쪽을 뜨더서 卷煙을 말기 시작하는 늙은이들 이러한 사람들이 눈에 띄운다.

巴里夜話
李晶燮

巴里 생활의 깁흔 맛 아는 사람은 그 누가 晝巴里보다도 夜巴里를 예찬하지 안을 이 잇슬 것이냐. 晝巴里는 高雅한 藝術에 잠겻스되 夜巴里는 五色燦爛한 『일류미네-슌』 가운데 放奔한 『쟈즈』曲 맞추어 全市가 춤추고 날뛰고 노래한다. 不夜城의 巴里! 放奔한 巴里! 이 두 가지가 巴里를 특징하니만큼 이것을 巴里에서 제거하면 巴里다울 것 그 무엇 잇스랴. 不夜城 중에 有不夜城하니 그는 곳 巴里의 中央에 잇는 『프라쓰, 되, 라, 콩코르도』라는 광장이다. 數百燭光이라 짐작되는 장영燈이 문득 燈林을 이루어 整齊하게 나열한 그 景槪 태양님도 붓구럴 만큼 輝煌爀爀하다. 어느 일요일 저녁 夜話者가 英國人 동무와 不夜廣場을 지내치다가 英人 『찬란하다. 그러나 이것이 佛人의 사치성의 발로이다.』 하야 호을로 하는 말로써 나의 의견을 구하는 듯하기에 나 『그럿소이다. 그러나 당신의 말은 영국인의 實益癖의 발로가 안일까요. 사람 생활은 결코 『큐비스트』派의 繪畵와 가튼 것이 안임니다. 각금 가다가 放奔的 狂的 곡선도 가미하여야 생활의 그윽한 늣김을 어들 수 잇슴니다. 이 불야성을 일종의 狂으로 보면 그만이오.』 하야 생활 철학의 一面을 말한 일도 잇다.

詩的 情趣 잇는 不夜廣場에서 東北 또 東北으로 작구 가면 거긔에는 음탕하기로 유명한 『몽마르트르』가 잇다. 이 『몽마르트르』야말로 夜巴里의 중심지이어서 放奔生活의 첨단을 가는 『모던』 巴里兒의 천당이라 할가. 大小無數의 무도장이 즐비하야 어데나 쓱 드러가면 최신 유행으로 몸 감은 美麗한 『파리지엔느』가 여게 저게 척척 걸안저 조그마한 紅脣으로 담소하는 그 자태. 개볍고 부드러운 날개 가진 蝶群이라 하면 비유의 말이나 선녀와 갓다 하면 사실을 과장하나마한 정도의 말일 것이다. 밤 열두 시로부터 아츰 네 시 다섯 시까지 춤추고 십흔대로 하고 조금 사치를 부려 『잠파인』 한 병쯤 뗀다 하여야 一夜의 유흥비로 우리나라 돈 20圓만 쓰면 이 세상이 혹 나를 위하야 맨드러진 세상이 안이냐고 의심하리만큼 쟈즈에 도취하여 놀 수 잇스니 우리나라 요리점 유흥과＜98＞ 비교하야 적은 돈으로 잘 노는 점은 어데까지 경제적 의미에 則한 유흥이라 할 것이다.
영화에 자조 나오는 유명한 『무-랑, 루-지』는 바로 이 『몽마르트르』에 잇스니 『레뷰-』의 최첨단을 가는 黑女로서 巴里兒를 惱殺하는 『죠세핀, 삐조-커-』가 그 妙技를 演하는 곳인 줄은 보통 팬의 다 알 바이다.

엇지 『무-랑, 루-즈』뿐일 것이냐. 『카지노, 되, 파리』도 잇고 『포리-, 베리제-르』는 『레뷰-』에 잇서 세계 유일의 稱이 잇는 극장이다. 一場面을 꾸미는데 백만弗郞으로부터 2백만弗郞의 거대한 비용이 든다 하니 그 『레뷰-』가 얼마나 화려하고 精妙한 것인가를 알 수 잇슬 것 안인가. 그리기 때문에 『포리-, 삐르제-르』에서는 一場面을 꾸며 가지고 혹은 6개월 간 혹은 1년 간을 연주하게 된다. 그러지 안이하고 그 갑비싼 『레뷰-』를 자조 가러댄다고 해서는 극장은 파산하고 말 것이다. -다만 보통 극장이나 大『오페라』 극장 이외에 이러틋한 극장에는 巧言令色하는 怪美女가 가득가득 드러찻다는 것만은 주의할 필요가 잇슬 것이다.

그러나 이상 말한 夜巴里는 누구나 아는 夜巴里이어서 별로 이러타 할 것이 업겟지만 여긔에 누구나 잘 몰르는 夜巴里의 한 모퉁이가 잇스니 그는 巴里하고도 白眉라 하는 『싸크레, 괴-르』 사원 근처에 잇는 18세기의 허름한 건물이다. 표면으로 보아 찌부러저 너머지는 듯한 古建物인 餘에 내부에 드러가 보아도 의자 탁자로부터 일반 가구가 허름한 골동품 비슷하다는 것 밧게 다시 아무것도 업다. 그런데 무슨 巴里夜話가 潛在하여 잇슬가. 첫재로 이 집에 드러오면 누구를 물론하고 형제 자매가 되어 당신이란 말은 집어치고 너 나 하야 아무리 처음 보는 사람이라도 서로 아모 숭허물 업다는 것과 주인의 노부부가 18세기 시대의 의복을 닙고 당시에 유행하든 노래를 하면서 一夜를 談笑裡에 보낸다는 그것이다. 서울만 하여도 이웃 사이에 서로 모르고 지내거든 況 巴里가튼 데서 全然히 모르는 사람기리 형제 자매의 情誼로 녯노래를 드르며 먹으며 웃는다는 것이 그 얼마나 滋味스러운 일일가. 다년간 파리에 잇는 사람도 夜巴里의 이 조그마한 秘密은 잘 모르는 이가 만타.＜99＞
