기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Pagoda is the Land of Fairy Tales  
by Roah  

As the flames flicker in the daytime,  

even if a train blows past with the hum of a whistle,  

the people sleeping under trees and grass do not stir.  

  

Here, does the evening dew not wet the grass?  

The dimming shadows cannot hold their wings;  

in the quiet slumber, they call on the clouds in the sky with mouths wide open.  

  

What to do with the sound of green rain?  

The old folks, turning their backs on the world, gather daily near the old pagoda,  

unaware that time is passing with tales of the past.  

  

In the pond bubbling with muddy water,  

a piece of blue sky jumps up like a goldfish.  

Isn’t it you who lives here!... ... This place is a dreamland of fairytales... ... .  

  

But I have seen it.  

A dark statue smeared with bird droppings stands smiling!  

1930.6.13. (at Pagoda Park) <158>  

**Summary**  

This poem reflects on the whimsical and serene imagery of a place referred to as "Pagoda," which symbolizes an enchanting land filled with dreams and fairy tales. The juxtaposition of tranquility in nature with a sudden reminder of the harsh realities, represented by the dark statue, highlights a tension between nostalgia and the present. Historians might find this piece particularly interesting as it encapsulates the contrasting sentiments of escapism and the stark reminders of colonial life in Korea during the 1930s.

「파고다」는 童話의 나라
						로아
불볏 나리는 대낫에
뎐차가 홰파람 불고 지나가도
숨소래 업시 잠자는 나무 풀 사람 사람

이곳엔 풀닙 적시는 밤이슬도 업든가
나래돗친 몬지라 막지를 못해
낫잠이 작난삼아 벌인 입으로 하늘에 구름을 부르고 잇구나

록음의 빗을 분별해 뭘하랴
세상 등진 로인네들 녯탑 엽헤 나날이 모혀
허물업는 녯이야기에 날가는줄 몰으네

부글부글 흙물 괴는 련못 속에서
한조각 푸른하늘 차자 뛰여오르는 금붕어
네나 살엇달가!... ... 이 곳은 어룬의 꿈나라 童話의 나라... ... .

그러나 나는 보앗네
얼골에 새똥칠한 식커먼 동상이 웃고 섯는걸!
