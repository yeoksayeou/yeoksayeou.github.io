기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Screenplay)  

유모어 寸劇 內助의 功(全四幕)  

제일막  

A young husband and wife at home.  
Wife: "Honey... money!"  
Husband: "Why do you want money?"  
Wife: "Just come here for a moment."  
Husband: "Is something good going to happen?"  
Wife: "My uncle sent me two baby chicks from my parents' home!"  
Husband: "Oh, that’s perfect! Let’s catch one and make it for dinner tonight."  
Wife: "Ah, you’re so hasty… But look at this chick. It must have gone bad during the long journey…"  
Husband: "Well, it does look a bit flat, but it’s still edible."  
Wife: "But if we eat this and get a stomachache, what will we do?"  
Husband: "True, but we can’t just throw it away after it was sent so kindly..."  
Wife: "Then, honey, how about this? Let’s do this: you give it as a present to your manager."  
Husband: "How do you mean?"  
Wife: "Like this, you know, you’ve been coming late to the office lately and they’re thinking you’re a bad husband, right?"  
Husband: "Yes..."  
Wife: "So, you can give him this and maybe soften his heart a bit."  
Husband: "That’s quite clever!"  
Wife: "That’s what I call the功 of the inner assistance (內助의 功)!"  

제이막  

An old section chief and his wife. The next day.  
Wife: "Dear, do you know Kim Sang-ki?"  
Husband: "Yes, I know him well. He’s a young man at the office."  
Wife: "He sent us two baby chicks by parcel!"  
Husband: "Oh, how nice of him! Let’s try to make some dish with these for dinner."  
Wife: "But why does the chick look a bit unwell?"  
Husband: "Well, it’s a thoughtful gift, so let’s take a bottle of the very special old wine from our wedding in 1888 for tonight’s dinner."  
Wife: "What do you mean? Aren’t we being a bit too extravagant? We promised to save that for our golden wedding anniversary."  
Husband: "But that wine is at its best right now, you know… If you make your famous ‘chicken cutlet’,"  
Wife: "Whether it’s cutlet or anything else, if it disagrees with me, what shall we do? It’s not just about our family’s face."  
Husband: "Still, considering the kindness of my subordinate, we can’t just reject this gift."  
Wife: "Then let’s do this: let’s send these chicks to the artist Lee Jung-geun instead. After painting the bedroom wall, he hasn’t received anything for his trouble and it would be a nice gift for that poor painter."  

제삼막  

A young artist and his wife. The next day.  
Wife: "Oh, a parcel has arrived. It’s from Section Chief Park—what could it be? Oh, it’s baby chicks!"  
Husband: "Baby chicks! How nice! Let’s make a ‘sichu’ with these. It’ll be the first nutritious meal in ages!"  
Wife: "How ridiculous... Honey, we need to earn some money instead. No matter how exceptional these chicks are, we need to send that money to Jung Jusa."  
Husband: "How can we give baby chicks to that scary old man?"  

제사막  

Still the artist couple. The next day.  
Husband: "Honey, we got a thank-you letter from Jung Jusa!"  
"Wife: "Seriously? After the chicks you sent yesterday? Thank you very much for the chicks. They truly are the best kind of chicken. As soon as I received them, I prepared a dish and two wings each yielded a twenty won note! I was initially quite surprised, but I marveled at the ingenious idea of a true genius! The original value of what you sent was thirty won, and I suppose you must have sent me interest of ten won as well! I am returning this to you. I wanted to express my gratitude even if just in writing."  
Jung Sang-jin: "Received sincerely, Lee Jung-geun"  
This is written to make it easier for you to recognize the figure, using only Korean names.  

**Summary**  

This play humorously portrays the interactions and misunderstandings between different couples regarding gifts and the dynamics of social hierarchy in a colonial setting. It highlights the intricate social actions that individuals engaged in to navigate gifts and relationships under Japanese colonial rule. Historians might find the depiction of these interpersonal relationships particularly revealing of the social habits and economic realities faced by Koreans at the time, as well as how individuals creatively addressed their complicated social situations.
