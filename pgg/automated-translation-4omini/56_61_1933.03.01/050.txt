Oh, how I long for that spring, my unfading spring scene  
朴祥燁  
"We lie on the soft sand of the beach, throwing off our wings, gazing up at the clear blue sky while below, the sound of a child playing in the sandbox mingles with the whispering waves. As I lie here, all the troubles and worries of the world dissipate like morning fog, carried away by the water droplets. Ah! The natural music! The beautiful spring melody of the sound of the waves! Worries that trouble the hearts of youth are washed away... Ah! Sweet spring waves!"

Six years ago!  
A line from one of my days, celebrating my nineteenth spring.

Feeling nostalgic for my spring at nineteen, tears well up in my heart just thinking about it. Oh, how I long for that spring! The memory of my shadow from that spring, washed away by the waves, disappears somewhere! Like water... Like dew...

At that time, I poured all my passion into loving one person. Just like a young mother holding a baby in her warm embrace, comforting me with tender and soft words, singing beautiful songs, that love has not yet vanished from my heart.

The lighthouse on C Island sways gently, and a lonely rock with a smile welcomes me. This was the one I loved and the one who loved me without any change.

At that time, I lived in A Port. My heart was filled with gloom and sentimentality. I had just come down from completing middle school (P Middle School) in Seoul. Realizing my family's financial troubles, I could hardly muster the courage to aspire to high school. When I thought of my father, mother, and siblings, all with yellowed faces from malnutrition, I understood that I had to become a beautiful sacrifice for them. To do that, I needed to find a job, even if it paid just a few pennies. Finding a job as a middle school graduate was harder than catching a star in the sky.

When I returned home, I felt like a "Raskolnikov" afflicted by hypochondria. When I got home, I faced the harsh reality of poverty each night. After finishing middle school, I could only lie on the cold floor, staring at the ceiling.

In those moments, a fierce flame would ignite in my heart—one that seemed to want to burn the whole world. I instinctively ran out of the house toward C Port. The sound of the ship's whistle echoed, mingling with the sounds of the laboring crowd on the docks—this scenery consumed me. I turned back towards the breakwater with a heavy head, my chin low. The waves crashing and breaking against the breakwater was the sight I had to face! After walking for about thirty minutes, I could see C Island and that external lonely rock welcoming me.

I lay on that welcoming rock, like a mother who wipes away the tears of her child after being away for some time, and at the sound of waves splashing at my feet, tears flowed from my eyes, wetting my cheeks. The flame that ignited in my heart from loneliness and gloom burned fiercely... Oh! Those warm tears flowed and flowed.

Oh my beloved! If it weren't for the comfort of that rock... The intoxication of the waves, who could assure that my body wouldn't have become a sacrifice to the fish?

Until the sunset plays hide-and-seek upon the distant horizon, I sang in harmony with the splendid sound of the waves, resonating through the blue earth!

Sea! Sea! I call upon you, calling it the mourning of youth - Night of February 11  

<9-10>  

**Summary**  

The author reflects on his poignant memories of youth during a spring spent at C Port, where his feelings of loneliness, despair, and longing are intertwined with love and nature. The piece encapsulates a deep nostalgia for the past, marked by the struggles of a family grappling with poverty and the weight of responsibilities on a young man's heart. This article is revealing in its depiction of the emotional burden young Koreans faced under colonial rule, where aspirations and dreams were often sacrificed for familial duty, offering historians insight into the broader social context of the period.

아기를 안은 젊은 어머니가티 따스한 품에 나를 안고 정다웁고 부드러운 말로 위로하여 주고 아름다운 목소리로 노래하여 주는 아즉도 내의 하트에서 그 자최가 사라저 가지 아니한 애인을 가지고 잇섯다.

나는 그때 A항에 살고 잇섯다.
그때의 나의 가숨에는 우울과 감상(感傷)으로 가득이 차 잇섯다.
그때 나는 서울에서 P중학을 마추고 나려 온 때엿다. 구차한 집안형편을 빤히 드려다 보고 잇는 나로서는 부덕부덕 상급학교에 가겟다는 용긔가 아니 염치가 차마 나아오지 못하엿섯다. 영양부족으로 누런 얼골을 한 아버지 어머니 동생들을 생각할 때에는 내 자신이 그들에게 고운 희생이 되지 안으면 아니 될 것을 깨달앗섯다.
그리하기 위하야는 나는 다만 서푼짜리라도 취직을 하지 아니하면 아니 되엿섯다. 하늘에 별따기 보다 더-어려운 취직이 한 개의 중학졸업생에게 그 고마운 차례가 도와올 리가 업섯다.
집에 도라온 나는 히포콘드리아(憂鬱病)에 걸린 한 개의 「라스콜니코푸」이엿다. 집에 도라왓대야 그날 그날의 밤거리가 업는 빈한한 형편이엿다. 중학이라고 마추고 왓대야 펀둥펀둥 찬방바닥에 누워서 천장만 치여다 보고 잇지 아니하면 아니 되엿섯다.
그러할 때마다 가숨에 확확 타오르는 불길-온 세상을 태워버릴듯한 -을 안고 집을 튀여나왓다. 나의 발ㅅ길은 거의 본능적으로 C항부두로 나아갓다. 긔선의 다ㅅ감는 소리 항구를 울리는 긔적소리 부두에 오락가락하는 로동의 무리- 이 풍경에 나는 정신업시 취하는 것이엿다. 나는 다시 발ㅅ길을 도리키여 기ㄴ 방축을 거러가는 것이엿다. 더부룩한 머리를 하고 고개를 푹 떠러트리고 물결이 몰려와 부닥처 깨여지는 방축을 가는 그 모양! 한 삼십분 거러가면 C섬이 보히고 그 엽헤 외로히 소슨 바위가 내가 오는 것을 반기우는 듯이 해즉이 우서 보이는 듯 십헛다.
몃햇동안 떠러저 잇든 아들을 마지 하여 주는 어머니의 뜨거운 눈물가티 그 웃득 소슨 바위우에 누어서 발아래에 와 솨-솨-하고 깨여지는 물결소리 흰돗대를 달고 푸른 물결을 저어가는 뱃사공의 노래소리를 듯고 잇슬 때 내 눈에는 뜨거운 눈물이 흘러나리여 내뺨을 적시여 주웟다. 내 가슴에 타오르든 불ㅅ길이 외로움이 우울이...
