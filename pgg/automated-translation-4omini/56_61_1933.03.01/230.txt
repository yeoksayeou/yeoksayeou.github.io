Oh weak one! Your station is as a man - portraits of men living under their wives  
Alas! What a wonderful plan! This is the secret record of K’s drunken escapades.

Mr. K, who serialized sweet long novels in XX Daily and whose wife, Ms. H, is a refined woman who even studied in Tokyo, has a household that others would envy for its considerable wealth.  

However, while everyone knows this outwardly, it is quite unfortunate that rumors of discord have leaked out from between the two.  

Mr. K! He is originally a literary man and businessman and there are sometimes occasions when he drinks alcohol with friends.  

However, it is not so serious as to warrant inclusion in the newspaper, and it is extremely unlikely that he would engage in irresponsible behavior after returning home (one could surely vouch for it considering his usual conduct), so he is trusted as a decent husband.  

But for some reason, Ms. H (to be sure, there must be contrary reasons) has a major complaint about Mr. K going drinking and frequently storms arise between them.  

It is very lamentable for those who are familiar with their family situation that she harbors arbitrary suspicions of Mr. K, a father with children, resulting in extramarital concerns and jealousy.

At its worst, there was even a story that he was forcibly dragged away by friends to a certain restaurant *one night, which serves as quite the proof.

In such an ambiance, Mr. K always lived with a sense of grievances! For a long time, he was unable to drink his usual drinks under the watchfulness and scrutiny of Ms. H and thus sat gloomily.  

Then he devised a clever way to enjoy a drink.  
“Honey, today I must go to Kaesong for an urgent matter.”  
“What do you mean urgent?”  
“Well, I must go because it is a matter related to my business…”  
“How is it that a person from the business department can’t go?”  
“No, but this case is such that it cannot be handled that way; being the editor, I have to go myself!”  
“In that case, what time are you thinking of leaving?”  
“Let me see. It’s almost 8 AM now, so I could take the express train at 9 AM and return on the express train at 7 AM tomorrow…”

Thus Mr. K received Ms. H’s permission!  

In his heart, he sang a song, filled with joy, thinking to ease the suspicions and packed his washcloth and toothbrush as he left home.  

“Ah, today I will surely enjoy a drink.”  

Muttering like this, he casually went out, running various errands in the town, and in the afternoon, he headed towards the new bar in X-dong after pre-arranging with some friends and took the streetcar on XX road.  

At that moment, coincidentally, he met a friend on the streetcar, and while exchanging greetings and lively conversation, soon the car reached the intended destination, and so he parted ways with that friend.

If matters had gone this smoothly, the situation would have turned out just as he had planned. Unfortunately, however, an unfortunate event occurred - this friend who met him on the streetcar came to visit Mr. K’s home that night, which threw everything into disarray.

He thought he would meet Mr. K because he had an urgent matter to discuss, and was quite pleased he had encountered him on the streetcar, yet due to this unexpected visit, he could not accomplish anything substantial by way of meeting.  

Therefore, assuming that Mr. K had gone to drink with his friends, he returned home that night.

“Mr. K went to Kaesong this morning. He probably won’t return until the express train tomorrow morning.” 

This was the honest statement from Ms. H. Then, how could the friend, having enjoyed a drink with Mr. K that afternoon, possibly have imagined such a hidden charade?   

“Kaesong? Didn’t I see him drinking with friends around 3 PM today?” he retorted authentically.

“Yes, yes, on the streetcar… around 3 PM…” 

Ms. H’s face flushed bright red with rage; she couldn’t contain her trembling lips and the twitching of her eyes.  

“Humph, let’s see about this!” 

In her heart, Ms. H planned some kind of retaliation.  

However, the friend who had gone to the bar did not notice her changed complexion and simply left, unaware of the storm he had just ignited.  

On the morning of the following day, the matter I will not discuss further still caused Mr. K to suffer immensely from the severe wrath and emotional upheaval inflicted by his wife.

Oh weak one! The dramatic account of B, the director of XX Newspaper, who was caught in a trap by a hawk.

Mr. B of the XX Department at A Newspaper Company is famous for his impressive groaning, while his wife, Ms. C, is likewise known for her prowess in entertainment.  

Around 3 PM, when it’s time to leave the office, the phone rings; it’s Ms. C calling from home.  
“Argh, when shall you be coming home?”  

Upon being asked what time he would return, he answered that he would be home by 4 PM.  

If he agreed he would be home at 4 PM, he should certainly arrive without delay at that time, yet unexpected incidents would erupt, igniting a tragic drama and possibly even terrorism against a performer.  

His wife had anticipated 4 PM, yet circumstances arose where he found it impossible to leave as planned, and so he was delayed by some friends at Myungwolgwan main location while having a drink.  

As he drank moderately and the entertainment was lively, his spirits rose to the point where he was enjoying himself thoroughly when suddenly a servant dashed in, saying “Mr. B, you have a guest!”  

“A guest!?”  

He knew no one who would be visiting him at such a place.  
If it were a colleague or a friend, they would have just walked in quietly anyway.  

“A guest!? Who is it?”  
“Just a young lady who wishes to see you.”  
“Hmm!”  

Mr. B’s heart raced. Ms. C, who had been waiting for him, finally managed to find out he was at A Newspaper Company after he did not return by 4 PM, and thus she had rushed over. This was quite the dire situation.  

Without a moment to think,  
“Where’s my suit jacket? Where’s my hat? Oh gosh! They will think I’m so rude! I must hurry!”  

So, flustered, Mr. B rushed out. In all his hurry, he did not even notice that his waistcoat had unbuttoned and was hanging loosely.  

At the entrance of Myungwolgwan, Ms. C was waiting with her mother, and she grabbed Mr. B like a chick caught by a hawk and took him home.  

What transpired at home is a story for a silent film that I will not disclose here.

  
**Summary**  

The article presents comedic portrayals of men grappling with the realities of domestic life under the watchful eye of their wives, illustrating the conflicts arising from jealousy and suspicion. It amusingly contrasts their desires for freedom with the constraints imposed by their marital relationships. The situations depicted reflect societal attitudes towards gender roles and marriage in colonial Korea, revealing the tension between traditional expectations and personal autonomy. Historians might be interested in how these narratives reflect the changing dynamics of familial roles during the colonial period, especially regarding masculinity and societal expectations.
