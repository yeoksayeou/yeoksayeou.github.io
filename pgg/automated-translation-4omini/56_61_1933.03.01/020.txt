Survey on the Psychological State of Modern People  

1) If you could be born again, what kind of person would you want to be and what work would you like to do?  

2) What kind of person (man or woman) do you prefer?  

3) What has cinema given you?  

4) How do you envision Korea in ten years?  

5) If you knew your lifespan in advance, how would you live?  

6) How would you express romance in one sentence?  

7) Which book has influenced your youth?  

8) Among things that are no longer there, what do you miss the most about Korean spirit and sentiment?  

9) What is the first thing you think of and want to preserve in Korea?  

10) What would be the most difficult thing to do without?  

Novelist … Lee Tae-jun  

1) I'm still not old, so I can still do whatever kind of work and be whatever kind of person I desire.  

2) A person who doesn't apply policies to every matter.  

3) The time is still not enough compared to what I have now.  

4) If I knew this, I wouldn't be holding this brush right now.  

5) I would prepare for my last moments.  

6) It's hard to summarize romance in one sentence.  

7) The book "Confession of Death" by Koeda Taijiro.  

8) The feeling of a young girl, whose heart no longer exists due to the lack of rubber shoes.  

9) Our linguistic movement.  

10) The loss of the Dongseomun (東小門) town hall would be the hardest.  

Journalist … Shin Gyeong-soon  

1) I want to become a mighty sea warrior and crush Qin Shi Huang's cart with an iron staff.  

2) I like people, regardless of gender, who are humorous and flexible. I do not enjoy people who are excessively strict about rules. I would also not welcome a woman who is reserved.  

3) I saw a film called "Lullaby" in the cheap public theater, which deeply moved me with its highlighted maternal love. Perhaps it's because I've been thirsty for maternal love since childhood.  

4) I have seen Korea from ten years ago, and I am observing it today, so surely there is no one who cannot imagine what it will be like in ten years. However, what use is it to speak in grand terms?  

5) I may have no choice but to live with what I have.  

6) It is a natural procedure of life.  

7) The biography of the general Hannibal stirred my heart during my student days and made me passionate.  

8) I can appreciate the Korean aesthetic and sentiment from the back view of a young girl dressed in a red traditional dress and white hanbok, carrying a water jug.  

9) Mainly issues of public health for the people.  

10) It would certainly be very difficult if a prohibition on alcohol were to arise, causing bars to close.  

Poet … Park Pal-yang  

1) I want to be born as a genius artist, creating beautiful art or, alternatively, someone who can make life more enjoyable through any endeavor.  

2) I prefer people, regardless of gender, who are sincere, earnest, healthy, diligent, cheerful, and optimistic. Among women, I also wish for beauty; ideally, a combination of both would be the best.  

3) Movies have become a necessity for modern city dwellers. While watching, we wander in a world separate from reality. However, what has it given us? I can only shrug and ponder this. Certainly, future movies will bring us many benefits. However, the kind of unrealistic, low-quality films we've seen thus far have given us nothing. I wonder when films that fit our desires will be presented before our eyes.  

4) This is a very difficult question. I find it hard to speak about.  

5) There are no big events that require specific knowledge of the end of my life; I would rather live until the day I die. "Once or twice might be worth it, but it's okay not to do so from the third time on."  

6) Regarding romance, it is a gamble! You risk even your life, especially in Korea! If discovered, it becomes a crime.  

7) Works by Nietzsche and writings from Ko-san Chogyu have impacted me; I once had a tendency towards pessimism, but since then I have not encountered significantly influential works.  

8) I have not particularly missed anything.  

9) The only thing I can think of is the "Public Education Movement."  

10) From a mental perspective, it’s belief. From a material perspective, it is money (though this is true in today’s society).  

Journalist … Yun Baek-nam  

1) If I could reincarnate, I would prefer to be something other than a person. I'm tired of being human. I might want to become a lion and roar against the great outdoors.  

2) Who could endure the words of a man who is sick of people?  

3) There is no particular experimental interest.  

4) I cannot even know about tomorrow, let alone the next ten years.  

5) Death is a certainty, so the duration does not really matter.  

6) It’s a matter better left unspoken.  

7) The book "Nature and Life" by Rohwa.  

8) The silk shoes of women, the luck banner for children.  

9) I find it quite troublesome to keep track of things. Perhaps a theater could be a good idea.  

10) Without food, and without women.  

Woman journalist … Kim Won-joo  

1) I want to be born as Kim Won-joo again, but just wishing for better brains. As for work? Well...  

2) I prefer people with strong loyalty to one another.  

3) Aside from entertainment, it hasn’t given me much else.  

4) I can’t imagine the future properly as I just got here.  

5) I can’t think about the day I will die, let alone do anything.  

6) Perhaps I would label it "an anxious affair."  

7) Reading Nietzsche and writings of Ko-san Chogyu led me toward a somewhat pessimistic nihilism, but since then I have not encountered any works that have significantly impacted me.  

8) Young women's trimming willows along the road in spring.  

9) On this issue, I would like to listen to your thoughts as a reference.  

10) This question is too vague, making it difficult to answer.  

Poet … Lee Eun-sang  

1) It's said that lifeless objects bear no worries. I would prefer to be a lifeless object.  

2) Someone who knows only how to smile or only how to cry.  

3) I enjoy films, but I always forget the actors' names or their pictures.  

4) I imagine things will not go as I desire.  

5) When I think about it, I perceive something grand, yet upon further reflection, it seems quite insignificant.  

6) Love is a form of gambling! You could even bet your life on it. In Korea, it is even more a gamble! If discovered, it becomes a sin.  

7) Buddhist scriptures, writings from Zen masters. Therefore, I aged my youth rather quickly.  

8) I miss the old-fashioned clothes and the chilliness of the open fields.  

9) - (Omitted) -  

10) The streetcars outside the market are a great help.  

Novelist … Lee Ik-sang  

1) I want to be a simple farmer or laborer, someone who can be satisfied with one bowl of rice or one bowl of soup, possessing a simple character that seeks to show off their simple clothing in the market.  

2) A person who does not lie, a person with a sense of justice.  

3) None. Mostly, they are dull distractions.  

4) Knowing my lifespan does not change my reality. People simply fade away as they go.  

5) A distorted form of religious belief.  

7) The novel "The Fire Pillar" by Kinoshita Ikka.  

8) I do not miss much; there isn’t anything particularly precious.  

9) The status, capabilities, and weaknesses of Koreans. Significant improvements are necessary in all areas.  

10) Without consciousness, it would be extremely difficult.  

Journalist … Hong Jong-in  

1) A politician. However, referring to myself as merely a politician wouldn’t be appropriate; I aspire to be a person filled with more passion, possessing a side similar to Byron.  

2) A person with warmth and integrity, someone who is humble and strong. Especially in women, I prefer someone without vanity.  

3) The impact of cinema on me has been minimal.  

4) Regarding Korea ten years from now, I can’t speak exclusively of Korea; it should be seen in conjunction with the movements in Japan, China, Manchuria, etc. It is certain that we will all progress together in the Far East One (極東). Beyond that, there isn't much else to say.  

5) Foreknowing the end of one's life doesn't lead to despair. Knowing when it will end does not yield a more optimistic view either. That said, if I were assured of living more than twenty years, I might feel compelled to complete projects a bit sooner. I view life similarly to how I view work.  

6) The love between Goethe's "Werther" and "Lotte" is a brilliant record of life.  

7) The works of Natsume Sōseki, "Bōchan," and later Thomas Carlyle, followed by historical materialism.  

8) Regarding the longing for the spirit of Korea, I envision sentimental artistic scenes produced with the blooming azaleas in the spring, while the folk songs of farmers in Pyeongan-do permeate my soul with nostalgia.  

9) The foremost questions to consider are: where are Koreans heading? What is happening along the journey of many? Observing the numerical average can lead to discussions on what we ought to do as individuals.  

10) -(Omitted)-  

**Summary**  

This article features a survey conducted among various intellectuals of the time, revealing their thoughts on life, the future of Korea, and the essence of romance. The responses reflect a blend of aspirations, nostalgic sentiments, and the impact of cinema on modern sensibilities. Historians might find the perspectives on identity, cultural heritage, and the longing for a possible future engaging, particularly against the backdrop of Korea's colonial struggle.

1) 정치가(政治家) 그러나 시속 정치가라는 말로서는 당치 안을 것 갓습니다만은 좀더 정열로 뭉친 「바이론」의 일면을 가진 남아(男兒).
2) 열이 잇고 순직한 사람 돈과 게집에 담박한 사람 온후하면서도 강한 사람. 특히 녀자는 허영이 업는 사람.
3) 특히 영화가 끼처 주엇다 할 영향은 적습니다.
4) 십년후의 朝鮮? 조선만을 따로히 말 할 수 업슬줄 압니다. 일본, 중국, 만주 등등 극동(極東)<12>의 움즉임에 딸아 꼭가티 움즉일 것만은 확실성이 만타고 보나 그외는 더 말 할 수 업습니다.
5) 생명이 끈어지는 날을 미리 알엇다고 더 비관하고 생명이 끗날 날을 몰으고 막연한 미래를 상상하면서 산다해서 좀 더 락관적일리는 만무합니다. 딸아서 수명이 진할 시일을 미리 알고 몰은다는 것은 현재생활에 조금도 영향할 바 업슬 것 갓습니다. 다소 심리적 영향이 잇슬는지 몰으나 만일 십년이상 더 살 수 업다는 것이 확정적이라면 이십년 예정하엿든 일을 좀더 빨리 其內에 성취하여야 되겟다는 마음을 가지게 될 뜻 합니다. 일생도 사무와 가티 볼 바가 크다고 생각함으로.
6) 꾀-테의 「베르테르」와 「롯테」의 사랑은 인생의 찬란한 긔록이요 꼿입니다.
7) 夏目瀨石의 「坊ちやん」, 그 다음은 「타고어」. 다음은 유물변증법론으로.
8) 조선의 정조 가장 그리운 것? 봄 아즈랑이 가티 눈 속에서는 센틔멘탈한 회화적 장면이 만히 뒤박긔고 잇스나... 平安道 龍岡, 江西 방면에 듯든 농민의 애끗는 기나리(民謠)는 닛기지 안는 한 긔억이 되어 잇습니다.
9) 몬저 생각해야 할 것은 조선 사람은 어데로 가고 잇는가? 엇떠케 되여가고 잇는가? 多大數라는 표준점에 어데서부터 어데로 가고 잇는가를 주목하면 우리는 무엇을 하여야 할 것인가를 각자 방면을 딸아 생각할 수 잇슬 것입니다,
