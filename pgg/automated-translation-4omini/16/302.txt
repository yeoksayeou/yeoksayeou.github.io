**Summary**  

This article outlines the establishment of new branch offices for the publication, detailing their locations, zones, and appointed branch managers across various regions. It mentions the localities including Sincheon, Yongampo, Ranan, Chungju, and a children's branch in Gyunghyang. The establishment of these offices illustrates the expanding influence and organizational efforts of the publication during this period. Historians may find the geographic spread relevant to understanding the media landscape and its relationship to local communities under colonial rule in Korea.
