기사형태	잡저 (Essay)	

戀愛憲法  
1. Those who do not read and laugh at this Constitution shall be subjected to severe punishment.  

2. Men and women who do not follow this Constitution shall be subjected to a love sentence of no less than one year.  

3. This Constitution shall be enforced from the date of publication.  

Chapter 1: Love  
Article 1: Love is a fever that occurs as an instinctive impulse.  

Article 2: Love will be succeeded by the offspring of billions of lives.  

Article 3: Love is sacred and cannot be infringed upon by a third party.  

Article 4: Love is ideal when it is observed in accordance with this Constitution.  

Article 5: Love entails the obligation of marriage.  

Article 6: Love should occur only once in a lifetime. While cats are said to have love twice a year, should humans have more? True love occurs only once in a lifetime.  

Article 7: Love must be on an equal maturity level; thus, do not engage in unrequited love, as it can ruin oneself.  

Article 8: Love thrives on silence; there is a charm in restraint. Love that is conducted as if within a glass box cannot grasp its true flavor.  

Article 9: Love cannot be double-touched or betrayed. The source of tragic love is found here.  

Article 10: The value of love is determined by chastity.  

Article 11: The motivation of love is not necessarily pure; instinctual love is vulgar. After patience, cultivation, and trials, love becomes noble.  

Article 12: Love is freedom. However, in some ways, it must also be constrained.  

Article 13: Love is blind; yet, there must be reason.  

Article 14: Love must continue throughout a lifetime.  

Article 15: Love is not sympathy.  

Senseless pity or compassion can easily transform into love, but such love is short-lived. It must not be mixed with the responsibilities of love typical in marriage.  

Article 16: Love must have an equal age difference. Age does not matter in love, as seen when a person over 80 loves a girl. However, a happy love occurs between those of similar age.  

Article 17: Love that is easily achieved is also easily broken.  

Article 18: True love exists with a true person.  

Article 19: Love is an unending trap.  

Article 20: The taste of love is like honey, yet can be very bitter.  

Article 21: First love is a flame ignited during the springtime when instincts manifest, igniting the heart with longing for the opposite sex.  

Article 22: Although one might feel immune to love after one experience, in fact, one can easily fall in love multiple times. In an age where love immunity methods have not been discovered, do not approach members of the opposite sex carelessly. Danger is great!  

Article 23: Love can easily become warm and can just as easily become cold.  

Article 24: Love is a beautiful novel, while marriage is a flavorless dissertation.  

Chapter 2: Attention to Lovers  
Article 1: Those who speak much are less genuine.  

Choose a person who can express love through actions rather than words. Beware of pretenses!  

Article 2: Those who cater to your digestion.  

A lover who tends to your needs, such as burdening you with heavy bags, or caring for you during sickness, may be caring. However, such individuals often become tyrants in marriage.  

Article 3: Recidivists.  

In the realm of love, habitual offenders exist who have committed repeated infractions, enticing others by the means they used on you. What was tasted on my lips may also be tasted by another. Do not love, but rather cherish what is truly valuable!  

Article 4: Liars.  

Compared to the honest person, deceivers generally gain fame as lovers. A woman's betrayal is less than a man's. Those who make sensational headlines usually lack the insight to see these deceptive individuals.  

Article 5: Those with inflated egos.  

Those who seek to dominate others are foolish, but those with pride are even more foolhardy. It is impossible to be happy with someone who has a superiority complex.  

Article 6: Those who are too kind.  

While a man's kindness can be deceptive, true kindness is even rarer. A genuine kind man may treat the opposite sex coldly outside of the love context. It is a dangerous thing for a woman in love to take a man’s kindness for safety.  

Chapter 3: Forbidden Love  
Article 1: Impossible love.  

A woman who is married may become enchanted by a man's looks, wealth, or art apart from her husband, dreaming happily while forsaking her family and children. This may seem delightful at the moment but will always lead to unrelenting guilt in the conscience. The pain of such love is monumental, and even the greatest of loves cannot ignore this! Regret comes too late!  

Article 2: Contemptible love.  

The market vendor of Hamgyeong Province who sells fabric, can mislead one into thinking they cannot live without it during autumn. If they fail to lure customers in autumn, they lose significantly. Many individuals engage in this kind of trapping love with half-hearted desire and half-hearted coercion. Unless one seeks to be a partner in misery, this situational love is best avoided.  

Article 3: Hidden love.  

Whether it’s merely hearsay or charm by an artist, clinging desperately to such love can lead to dire consequences. The contrast between hopes and outcomes can create unfortunate examples from these naïve endeavors. Remember!  

Article 5: Unfortunate engagement over love.  

"For the sake of love, wherever you are!" may symbolize the greatest ideals of lovers; however, they will often find themselves sighing when reality strikes.  

After intertwining lives, repeatedly losing something precious will lead to disappointment.  

Article 6: Love between teacher and student.  

There may be no class distinctions in love; however, if love is improper, not only humans will find it difficult to forgive, but heaven will not forgive. Friends engaging in love often suffer the same pitiful fates.  

Article 7: Love does not equate to marriage.  

Love related to the sweetness of honey is quickly consumed by all. However, restraint is necessary. If love is experienced too early, weariness follows. Marriage, defined by the preservation of chastity, can only be secured through overcoming temptations.  

Article 8: Speak boldly about love.  

Those who are confident in love often fail; indulging in status and reputation leads to failure. Cautious lovers triumph.  

Article 9: Do not treat love lightly.  

Article 10: Experience counts when it comes to love.  

Experiences are valuable, and through them, various aspects of life can be enhanced. When it comes to love, misfortune often accompanies naïveté, more so than even a Roman potter.  

Article 11: Do not issue love's bad checks.  

Feigned warmth and affection will lead to destruction when the time arrives for resolution.  

Article 12: Do not become addicted to love.  

Love's power is great, but there are greater forces in life. Those who fill their lives solely with love may find themselves tragically alone in middle age. A love addict is more pitiful than even a heroin addict.  

Article 13: Do not vie for love.  

A loss results in multiplied grief, while a win invites equal troubles.  

Chapter 4: Love Regulations  
Article 1: Treat love as one does eating buns.  

Delightful and sweet passion should be wrapped in the shell of reason like a bun. The essence of deliciousness does not come from solely eating the outside. Lovers ought to remember not to consume only the exterior.  

Article 2: Walk the path of clean love.  

Being lost solely in sensual desires is not true love. "Beautified love" is what this Constitution defines and aims for; it is the kind of love suitable for advising sons and daughters.  

Article 3: Love should not stray like a kite.  

When friction exists, wisely tighten the thread of love, and when it weakens, loosen it. Skill is required to prevent the love string from breaking in the autumn wind.  

Article 4: Love should be uncalculated.  

Love with ulterior motives will disappear with the objectives if those objectives do not exist.  

Article 5: Know the strengths and weaknesses of your partner in advance.  

Article 6: Do not respond hastily to love proposals.  

Even when purchasing a pair of shoes, one usually does not agree immediately based on the first offer. Treat love with the same caution.  

Article 7: Love should be accompanied by life energy.  

The success of love revolves around the existence of vitality in life. A beautiful boat is of little worth without the strength to navigate it.  

Article 8: Love must maintain consistent warmth.  

Merely having charming faces is not enough for happiness. Living solely for unadulterated love can lead to unacknowledged loneliness. The strength of enduring love is vital for all, especially between couples.  

Article 9: Choose someone who adores children as your lover.  

Anyone who genuinely loves children is likely not a bad person, though one should be cautious of those who may use it as a means to win affection.  

Article 10: Do not forsake appearances even in love.  

As love endures, it is easy to become careless about appearances. For a while, "pettiness can be forgiven" may apply, but over time it may breed contempt. Watch out!  

**Summary**  

The "Constitution of Love" outlines various articles concerning the nature, obligations, and pitfalls of love. It outlines love's ideal qualities and rules, emphasizing the serious commitment involved in romantic relationships and the importance of marriage. The document reflects societal norms and gender expectations of the 1920s in Korea, illustrating how romantic notions were intertwined with traditional values such as chastity and duty. Historians may find it revealing how these constructed ideals under colonial rule influenced personal relationships while serving as a critique against superficial romances and unfaithful conduct.

第4章 戀愛典
第1條 戀愛는 饅頭먹듯 하여라.
감칠맛 잇고 달큼한 정열이란 속을 理性이란 밀가루껍풀이 싸고 잇다. 속부터 먹는 것이 饅頭를 먹는 것이 아니다. 戀愛子는 모름직이 속부터 먹지 말라.
第2條 戀愛는 淸潔한 길을 걸어라.
情慾에만 빠저서 허덕어리는 것은 참 戀愛가 아니다. 아들과 딸에게도 일러 줄만한 『악름다운 戀愛』 이것이 이 憲法이 規定하고 바라는 바 上等戀愛이다.
第3條 戀愛는 연(紙鳶)날이 못하여라.
저 便에서 猛熱하거든 戀糸를 늣구어 주고, 저 便이 느러지거든 戀糸를 켱기여라. 가을바람에 끈허지지 안토록 잘 操縱하라.
第4條 戀愛는 非打算的으로 하여라.
利益을 目的한 戀愛는 그 目的物이 업서서는 同時에 사라진다.<155>
第5條 戀人의 長處와 短點을 미리 알아 두어라.
第6條 戀愛는 請求에 빨리 應치 말라.
질뚝백이 하나를 사는 데도 부르는 대로 밧고 팔면 도리혀 破損이 나지 안엇나 하고 疑心하는 것이다. 戀愛를 重히 녁이거든 輕率히 應치 말라.
第7條 戀愛는 生活力이 한가지 하여야 한다. 戀愛의 成否도 生活力 有無에 좌우되는 世上이다. 배가 곱하 가지고는 戀愛할 거룩한 詩이다. 그러나 生活力이 업는 戀愛는 몸을 亡치 나니라.
第8條 戀愛는 恒常熱度가 가타야 한다.
人形과 갓치 귀엽만 밧고 잇는 것이 幸福이 아니다. 單純한 사랑만 밧고 잇는 안해가 남모르는 寂寞이 얼마나 만흔지 모른다. 바든 사랑을 돌녀보내 일만한 사랑의 힘이 핑핑히 켱겨잇서야-더구나 夫婦에 잇서서 더욱 그럿타.
第9條 어린아희를 귀여워하는 사람을 戀人으로 하라.
어린이를 귀여워하는 사람에게 惡人은 업다. 그러나 歡心을 사기 爲한 手段으로 쓰는 者가 잇다. 嚴密한 鑑別이 必要하다.
第10條 戀愛人끼리도 體面은 차려다.
