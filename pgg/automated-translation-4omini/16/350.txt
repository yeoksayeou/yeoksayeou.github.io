“Yesterday, did one ever speak like that!”  
“When looking at the groom, what a pity!”  
“They say the bride is two years older!”  
“What good are mere relations? They say the widows' household offered as much as fifty thousand nyang to the groom's family already!”  
Various gossips and chatter from the townsfolk have concluded that Gwae-dong’s wedding ceremony is done. Once again, the troublemakers from the village have dragged Gwae-dong into a tavern.  

Gwae-dong, who has been mocked and derided by drunken rascals for having a bride who is better looking and more intelligent than himself, has begun to develop feelings of resentment and hatred towards his wife. Thus, he has disgraced her with verbal abuse, resolving to express his discontent through grumbling. Nevertheless, the gentle wife, while shedding tears alone, bears no resistance. Having grown up undisciplined, and being a petulant Gwae-dong has ultimately destroyed the family’s harmony and joy. <131>

**Summary**

The article narrates the story of Gwae-dong, whose marriage is subject to gossip and mockery from the townsfolk due to perceived inequalities with his bride. His resentment leads to mistreatment of her, despite her silent suffering. This narrative reflects deeper social dynamics and pressures of the time, particularly how public perception influenced personal relationships. Historians might find the depiction of marriage dynamics and societal expectations in colonial Korean society revealing, highlighting the struggles between personal emotions and external pressures.

『엇저면 저러케 입블까!』
『신랑보아서는 앗가운 걸!』
『신부는 두 살이나 더 먹엇대!』
『아모리 입분들 소용이 잇나. 과부집 살림이라 벌서 신랑집에서 五萬兩이나 주엇다는 데!』
이런흉 저런흉와 말 만흔 동리사람 입술끗헤서 貴童이의 婚姻式은 끗이 낫다. 다시 업슬 그희를 어든 동리의 작란 패들은 貴童이를 잡아내여 술집으로 끄을고 갓다.
自己보다도 신부가 잘 생기고 똑똑하다고 비웃고 조롱하는 술취한 작란패의 말에 철업는 貴童이는 신부에게 대한 분한 마음과 憎惡가 생겻다. 그래서 한방에 잇기를 실혀하고 트집과 심술로 안해를 구박하얏스나 온순한 안해는 남모르게 홀로 눈물은 흘릴지언정 일언의 반항이 업섯다. 버릇업시 자라낫고 철업는 貴童이가 안해를 미워하는 것이 결국은 집안의 和氣와 우슴을 쫏차바렷다.<131>
