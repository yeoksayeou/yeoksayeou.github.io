기사제목	새 正月에 생각나는 사람들 (People Who Come to Mind at the New Year)  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

New Year Thoughts  
Farewell to Kim Ok-kyun at the Shanghai Port  

Yoon Ji-ho  

I first met Kim Ok-kyun 48 years ago, in the autumn of the year of the new century (신사) 1889. At that time, I was a young man of merely eighteen years, accompanying Mr. Yoo Yoon-joong, one of the twelve great men of Joseon, to Japan. It was around that time that Kim Ok-kyun arrived in Japan, and we met in Tokyo. Due to my youth, I received much affection from him. Afterwards, I went to Shanghai for studies, while he stayed in Japan, resulting in our only communication being through letters, and we seldom had the opportunity to meet face-to-face. Subsequently, I transferred to the United States and returned from Japan in June of 1893, where I briefly met him again.  

Later, I went back to Shanghai where I taught English at a Chinese school, the Sino-Western Academy. On the morning of March 27, 1894, I received a telegram from him stating, “The Seikyo Maru has arrived in Shanghai.” Since I had a great admiration for him, I was quite pleased and arrived at the dock early that afternoon to meet him. He was heading directly to a Japanese hotel called Donghwa Yanghang. That night, when I visited his hotel room, I found him in room 1 on the second floor, while in the room next to him was a tall man wearing a brown Joseon robe and a Joseon hat. I asked him who that person was, and he replied that it was Hong Jong-u, someone he had met a few times in Japan, who had come on the same ship and was now staying at the hotel. At that moment, I had no idea that he was an assassin.  

On the next day, March 28, the very day Kim suffered his tragic fate, he sent me this letter: “You must plan carefully today; how about coming to visit around one or two in the afternoon? If it becomes impossible, then it's better not to come hastily, but I hope you can come early if possible.” The letter, of course, was his invitation for me to be his guide to tour Shanghai that afternoon. Thus, I hurriedly finished my work at school and headed to his hotel at the appointed time.  

As I approached his room, much to my shock and grief, Kim had already been attacked by an assassin, and the hotel had turned into a scene of chaos filled with police and other people. I didn't know what to make of it and was suddenly seized with panic. Rushing to his room, I found him lying on the bed, turned toward the wall, lifeless, with blood all over his face. His Japanese attendant, Wada, was in despair, crying uncontrollably. Unintentionally, tears streamed down my face as well. Oh, that moment was filled with despair and horror! Even now, I tremble at the memory, and my eyes widen with sorrow.  

Having fought tirelessly to revive Joseon and dying before he could achieve his goals, how can I ever hope to resolve the lingering regrets of that teacher? Time is merciless; it has already been 36 years since he left us. The world has changed repeatedly, and now the young man who was once bright and vigorous has turned gray. Looking back at the past and observing the present, how can I suppress my profound sentiments? Raising a cup of Tusu liquor, I wish to honor his spirit; all I can do is let my tears flow abundantly.  

Kim Hong-jip, who feared nothing at death  

Kwon Dong-jin  

Kim Hong-jip was significantly older than I, so I did not have many opportunities to converse with him. However, since he was the Prime Minister during the Gabo Reform era and my older brother, Kwon Yeong-jin, was with the police then, I frequently heard his words. Among various figures at that time, he was undoubtedly the most learned and possessed great virtues, making him truly qualified for the position of Prime Minister. Whenever he presented a proposal, no one dared to oppose him, and everyone complied with his directives. This was surely due to his extraordinary insight, but also because he had a knack for deeply influencing the public. His bold reforms during the tumultuous Gabo year could not have proceeded without him.  

Unfortunately, due to the scheming of pro-Russian factions, an incident occurred in which King Gojong fled to the Russian legation. He also fell victim to a violent death. If things had progressed more favorably, Kim Hong-jip's aspirations could have transformed Korea into what it is today. Even when he was captured along with Jeong Byeong-ha while trying to enter Gyeongbokgung Palace, he showed no change in expression, maintaining his composure. This suggests how extraordinary his character was. In today’s tumultuous era, as we welcomes a new year, my thoughts on such predecessors become even more earnest. Indeed, our society is in dire need of better individuals now.  

Remembering the beloved Sun Ui-an  

Choi In  

Sun Ui-an is a person who represents both my instructor and my stern father in terms of affection. My admiration and longing for him is always deep-seated, but during the New Year, it is several times more intense. I have associated and debated matters with various renowned individuals both at home and abroad, but I have yet to meet anyone on par with this Sun Ui-an.  

His capacity for inclusion is extraordinary. Regardless of rank or status, he viewed everyone equally and embraced them with love. Even his enemies, he never regarded as such and always showed them tolerance. To put it another way, he is like a great river that does not discriminate and a spring breeze that nurtures all things alike. He had a unique ability to touch the hearts of everyone around him.  

Secondly, he had an unwavering sense of responsibility. No matter with whom or what he was dealing, once he made a commitment, he would fulfill it to the end without faltering, unaffected by the opinions of others. This made it easy to collaborate with him; once you gained his approval, you could proceed with peace of mind.  

Thirdly, he possessed immense courage. Whenever he contemplated any matter, he would thoroughly reflect on it without rushing, but once a decision was made, he immediately acted with determination. His courage could withstand even the might of a thousand armies. Most of his accomplishments were attributed to this relentless courage. These three qualities are distinctive traits, and everyone could learn from them.  

Although we live in an age where public opinion and trends dominate, a public opinion without central figures ultimately struggles to achieve meaningful results. Especially in a time of great unrest and confusion, the thoughts of figures like him become more pronounced.  

Forever admiring Jeong Po-in  

Song Jin-woo  

Though it is New Year’s, one might question who specifically comes to mind, but I always think of Jeong Po-in of Goryeo because of my deep admiration for him. Jeong Po-in is widely acknowledged as the greatest political and diplomatic figure, as well as a scholar, during the 475 years of Goryeo. As noted in the poet Songdu's nostalgic poem, “The spirit of mountains and rivers is extinguished with Jiang Han-chan, while the sun and moon shine upon Jeong Mong-joo,” he is unparalleled among military figures or civil servants throughout the entire history of Goryeo. His presence was vital in preserving Goryeo, and his death marked its decline.  

In an era when Goryeo's national power was waning, he served in the political arena to educate and assist the foolish monarch above while repressing the rabble of military leaders and the licentiousness of Buddhism below. He countered foreign invasions from both Japan and the Yuan dynasty, while also actively participating in international matters that helped strengthen Goryeo’s position.  

He was pivotal in shaping Goryeo's fate; had he not left the country, it is likely that the throne would not or could not have fallen to Lee Seong-gye as easily. The issue of his success or failure is another matter, but I truly admire and respect his character. As I contemplate new thoughts this year, my longing for such great individuals becomes ever more profound.  

Supporting the Joseon Heart, Kim Dae-mun  

Choi Nam-seon  

Every nation and ethnicity, without exception, has its own unique thought and culture. In the late Silla period, the thought and culture of the Tang dynasty invaded like waves, progressively subjugating Silla’s quiet culture and native thought during the most perilous time. Although scholars like Choi Chi-won were around, being Tang’s students, even they did not escape the Tang atmosphere. However, Kim Dae-mun was remarkable in maintaining his defiance and dignity, intermingling Silla’s thought and culture amidst the chaos.  

While he was the governor of Hansanju, his writings, such as Han Mountain Record and others related to the Silla's Flower Knights era, were works that ordinary scholars and practitioners at the time would not dream of composing. These writings are not grand or elaborate but serve as critical records. His Han Mountain Record elucidates the geography and history of Hansando, which was of great significance to the national borders, while the Flower Knights’ era documents the essence of Silla's noble youth system.  

In summary, Kim Dae-mun was an exemplary supporter of Joseon’s heart and thought. Even though it may seem somewhat odd to think of someone from thousands of years ago at the New Year, in a time when the culture and thought of Joseon are gradually declining, recalling him does not stem merely from the curiosity of an archaeologist.  

Forever Remembered, Jeong Su-un  

Ra Yong-hwan  

As a Cheondoist, recalling Jeong Su-un is deemed quite commonplace. However, apart from identifying as a Cheondoist, when thinking of Korea and the world, I cannot help but hold him in high regard. His espoused principle of “Humanity is the Lord of Heaven” alongside his advocated goals—protecting the country, securing the people, and broadly aiding the masses—are more timely and fitting than any other ideology or religion in history. As time moves on and countless people resonate with his teachings of “The major currents of mountains and rivers return to this path,” it is evident and undeniable that he was indeed prophetic.  

Thanks to him, Koreans have thrived, and there lies hope for the world’s prosperity, too, which makes it all the more essential to think of him. While others lament another year of their lives passing, we cherish each passing year as we witness the growth and expansion of the Su-un doctrine, leaving no room for dismay. Thus, even at the New Year, I bear no less than a fervent thought toward Su-un.  

The Famous Gisaeng, Hwang Jin-hee  

Han Dong-woon  

It might be difficult for some to understand why I first think of Hwang Jin-hee, a figure from hundreds of years past, at the beginning of the New Year, but my consideration is not because she was a celebrated gisaeng, nor for her extraordinary voice and dance considered among the greatest, nor for her poetry and writing. Rather, I am inspired by the circumstances that led to her emergence as a gisaeng. Everyone knows she was a renowned gisaeng during the reign of King Jungjong of the Joseon Dynasty and the daughter of a respectable man named Hwang Jinssi from Kaesong, who was renowned for her beauty and remarkable talent.  

By the age of sixteen, she had already captured the hearts of the nation, but due to the rigid societal norms of that time, it was extremely difficult for young men to meet her, especially given her strict family upbringing. An admirer even fell gravely ill from longing for her, ultimately passing away. When the funeral procession passed by Hwang Jin-hee’s house, remarkably, the carriage carrying the bier strangely did not move. Everyone stood in shock not knowing how to react. Someone informed Hwang Jin-hee of the man's tragic story, which deeply moved her.  

She reflected, thinking that while she might not have been able to save the soul of a lost person, it would be a great sin if her beauty were to cause the death of others. Hence, she decided to forsake her familial reputation and embrace a life of freedom.  

Summary  

The article reflects on the significant lives and contributions of various historical figures regarded by the author with admiration on the occasion of the New Year, including Kim Ok-kyun, Kim Hong-jip, Sun Ui-an, Jeong Po-in, Kim Dae-mun, Jeong Su-un, and Hwang Jin-hee. Through their narratives, it highlights the enduring impact of these individuals on Korean identity and the continuous pursuit of justice, integrity, and cultural heritage despite historical adversities. Historians may find interest in these emotive recollections as they illustrate the complexities of Korean nationalism and the challenge of maintaining cultural consciousness during a period of colonial oppression.

臨死不懼하던 金弘集

權東鎭

金弘集씨는 나보다 연령이 휠신 선배임으로 그자지 친하게 본 일은 업섯슴니다. 그러나 그가 갑오경장 시대에 내각총리대신으로 잇슬때에 우리 형님(權瀅鎭)은 경찰사로 잇섯기 때문에 그의 말슴을 만히 드럿슴니다. 그는 당시 여러 인물중에 학식이 제일 풍부함은 물론이고 덕량이 또한 커서 그야말로 당당한 일국의 총리대신 자격이 잇섯드람니다. 그가 무슨 <39> 議案을 내면 누구나 반대를 못하고 다 唯唯 복종하얏담니다. 그것은 물론 그의 식견이 초월한 것도 이유이겟지만은 평소에 일반을 감화를 잘 시키는 덕량이 잇는 까닭일 것임니다. 갑오년에 그 난관에 처하야 여러가지 일을 용단스럽게 개혁한 것도 전혀 그가 안이면 될 수 업섯슴니다. 불행히 친러파의 蠢動으로 高宗끠서 아관파천하는 사변이 생기고 그도 비명의 橫死를 하엿기에 그럿치 만일에 일이 수조로 진행되야 그의 雄志를 잘폇다면 조선이 오늘의 조선은 되지 안엇슬것임니다. 그가 變을 듯고 農大鄭秉夏씨와 가티 경복궁으로 드러가다가 반대당에 잡히여 경무청 문압헤서 참살을 당할 때에도 조곰도 神色이 변치 안이하고 태연자약하엿던 것을 보면 그의 인격이 얼마나 초월한 것을 짐작할 수 잇슴니다. 지금에 그러한 동지가 잇다면 일을 하기에 얼마나 조켓슴닛가. 多事多忙한 시대에 또한 새해를 맛고 보니 그런 선배의 생각이 더욱 간절함니다. 우리 사회에는 무엇보다도 조흔 인물을 더 요구하는 때이닛가요.

갈사록 思慕되는 孫義菴선생
