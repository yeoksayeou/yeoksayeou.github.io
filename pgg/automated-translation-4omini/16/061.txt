Announcement of Rice Prize Winners  
In the December issue of this magazine, the prize question regarding the photo of Yeomnaehimachi received significant popularity, generating considerable buzz wherever it was discussed. A total of three thousand two hundred ninety-three responses were submitted, but only forty individuals completed the task correctly. Most of the correctly submitted responses were from women. Among these forty, names were drawn from a lottery conducted by having a young boy from the printing house select the winning entries, and the following individual will receive a bag of rice:  

Han Eungyu, 27, Yeonchi-dong, Gyeongseong  

Additionally, prizes will also be sent to the following thirty-nine individuals.

Summary  

This article announces the results of a prize contest held in the previous month's issue of the magazine, where readers were asked to answer a question related to a photo puzzle. A significant response was received, illustrating the popularity of puzzles among the readership. The winners, primarily women, reflect cultural trends of participation in leisure activities during the colonial period. For historians, the number of participants and the nature of the responses can shed light on gender roles and social engagement in Korea under Japanese colonial rule.

白米한섬 懸賞當選發表
本誌十二月호에 낫든 懸賞問題 寫眞나희마치기는 참으로 人氣가 大端하야 가는곳마다 그이약이로수근거리고 모다드러온 대답이 三千二百九十三枚엿는데 틀닌 것은 다마치엿스나 나희를 마친이가 겨우 四十人밧게 되지안엇슴니다. 大槪는 女子의 나희를 더만히처서틀엿슴니다. 마침 이 四十名에서 제비아서(제비는 印刷所에서 第一적은 少年한분을 請하야 게하엿슴니다)左記韓氏에게 白米한섬을 보내드리게되엿슴니다. 그리고 下記三十九人도 어엽분 賞品을 보냄니다.
京城府禮智洞二十七番地 韓應裕氏
