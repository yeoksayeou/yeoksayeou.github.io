各都市探査記歡迎  
As the winds of civilization blow ever more strongly, the so-called urban areas are steadily increasing in number. We have investigated the various magical dens in the capital area, but now we are making efforts to explore local cities with great resolve. Those who are willing should provide substantial materials responsibly. <133>  

   
**Summary**  

This article discusses the increasing number of urban areas in Korea during the colonial period and the importance of exploring local cities beyond the capital. It calls upon engaged individuals to contribute reliable information for this initiative. Historians may find the emphasis on local urban development intriguing, as it reflects the broader changes in society during Japanese colonial rule, indicating a growing interest in the dynamics of urbanization and the colonial administration's approach to managing and documenting these changes.
