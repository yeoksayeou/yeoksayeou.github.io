잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1929년 01월 01일  
기사제목	新春誌上 男女大討論, 現下의 朝鮮家庭 한집에 姑婦同居가 可한가 否한가 (New Year Magazine: Major Debate Between Men and Women - Is It Possible or Not for a Daughter-in-Law to Live Together in One Household?)  
기사형태	논설 (Editorial)  

可便  

吳華英  

(Audience applause) The topic of discussion must always be something that has both sides of the argument and is interesting enough for people to express their opinions. If a problem is raised just to pull it out carelessly, is there any way for people to be engaged? The question is: Should a daughter-in-law live separately or together? I don’t believe we need to discuss it; certainly, it would be better for them to live together. I assume that you all think the same way. Of course, the essence of this question is indeed that way, but please let me clarify that I am not advocating for the big family system!  

Living together in one house with multiple generations means that the relationship becomes complicated; if even a distant relative causes a slight problem, it may lead to everyone unconditionally quarrelling for weeks or months. This large family system fosters laziness and nurtures dependence, as you all know well. I don’t need to elaborate further because everybody understands this.  

Now, I don't know where it is reasonable to say that a daughter-in-law living in the same house is not okay. Let's examine the fundamental idea of a daughter-in-law. A father and son live together, and it's not the case that a mother-in-law and daughter-in-law can live separately. Then the mother-in-law and daughter-in-law have names and relationships stemming from being attached to the father and son. Wouldn't it end up saying that a father and son living together are not okay?  

Anyway, if we abandon customs and traditions and break everything down, we might be able to shatter old habits, but can we break down human relationships and morals? But the youth, who are swayed by the new ideas, ask what morality is and what law is, suggesting that they are just dreams created by themselves! Even if we were to disregard ethics, what then? Will we destroy human relations?  

Moreover, world history proves that the Eastern ethics and morals that humanity esteems, especially in our case, are referred to as the land of the virtuous gentleman. What is called the land of the virtuous gentleman? Is it not because we have consistently upheld ethics and morals? Everything we have—no house, no land—is nowhere to be found! Yet we clutch onto the remnants of ethics and morals that bloom like a single flower in a desolate wasteland, trying with our hands to crush them and stomp on them! Are we really trying to discard that beautiful homemaker in the land of rites?  

Is this era truly the era we live in...? However, amid it all, why do we seek to bring forth a revolution in our precious morals and ethics? Just think! We have familial obligations of loyalty that are ranked above paternal affection. Shouldn’t a son be away from his father and live separately? But the reason this separateness is brought up is not due to the necessity of it—such as having to move away for work or due to economic hardships—but stems from the stubbornness of parents or the nagging of a mother-in-law!  

Because of their conflicting ideologies and differing opinions, the new young couple finds it impossible to live together with such stubborn parents. If living with such stubborn parents means that all the ideals and doctrines they carry are rendered meaningless, then it would be completely natural for them to want to live separately, and we should not bring this matter into question. However, there remains the consequence of crushing our beautiful ethics and morals along their path.   

Still, dismissing the rigidity of that—thinking only of ethics and morals—why not just live separately as you wish? Even in doing so, some reason lies behind it all. It is the basic premise that a human being must not trample on the fundamental issues that make someone human. If you trample the fundamental problems of humankind, then you cease to be a human being yourself.  

But is this problem something that will continue forever, rooted since the past and lasting into the future? Absolutely not. There were certainly times when this issue was virtually invisible or unheard of. It may seem that this is merely an issue confined to this transitional period, currently existing in this exact moment. It is a struggle between new thoughts and old ideologies that exist right now. And it is certainly a question that has to be addressed.  

Has the generation come this far? But don’t forget that your parents and mothers spent half their lives raising you, and what is meant by raising a son and marrying off a daughter? Is it really to simply exploit them? If they strive to match you up with someone and then have you find joy in life, will any mother-in-law truly resent or hate that?  

Do not blame the stubbornness or misunderstandings of mothers-in-law but rather resent the fact that you are in a transitional period. How much does their nagging and misunderstandings truly affect you? Don’t push away the stubborn father or dislike the nagging mother-in-law! In the midst of these tumultuous affairs, you may find something worthwhile.  

Do not focus only on their shortcomings but rather seek out their strengths! What is new today will become the norm tomorrow. Remember that when you resent your mother-in-law, it is possible that you too will become a stubborn mother-in-law in your daughter-in-law’s eyes. Simply saying one's thoughts are new may mean the same words will return to you again when you have created a family. If you cannot endure this kind of situation, then what are the claims of a new ideology or enterprise worth?  

Thus, I hope we do not tread upon the morals and ethics we have inherited all this while as I leave the stage now. (Applause)  

否便  

방정환  

Even if a son reaches the age of seventy or eighty, parents still see them as individuals who need to be cared for, incapable of living independently; however, when they marry a new bride, that creates a new household for the two peers.  

The newlywed couple must certainly put in considerable effort to harmonize their wishes in establishing their home, and with other people around, that cannot happen smoothly; thus, it becomes difficult for the couple to manage themselves freely with someone above them to follow. Hence, if there are multiple couples all in one household, it does not belong to either party.  

Moreover, having parents-in-law who mandate absolute obedience is exceedingly unreasonable.  

Firstly, what modern people seek from their families is relaxation and rest. Be it at a factory, office, or study, as one fatigued by the intense labor of society, the home is where one seeks adequate relaxation and rest. In the traditional large family system of Korea, returning home often means confronting the complex family relations that do not provide that rest but instead create more stress.  

The wife at home thinks primarily about one man, understanding her husband's profession and striving to create a space for his relaxation according to his occupational needs. But with stubborn parents above and siblings, a wife’s attention must go in various directions, making it impossible for her to focus on one husband.  

As a result, work and home life become entirely two separate worlds, influenced deeply by the stresses of their jobs and worries about home affairs that make them feel like they are running a boarding house.  

Thus, all family members suffer under the dual burden of professional and family obligations.

Secondly, it gets vague as to whether the center of the family lies with the parents or the young couples, or among three-generation systems or sibling families; families then find themselves caught up in maintaining appearances without tangible benefits, leading to significant detriments—one of which happens to be the upbringing of children. 

Increased concerns about the children being brought up become even more problematic under these circumstances. When there are familial grievances against the children, it might stem from unexpected teacher or friend criticism, and every minor reprimand can falsely project an awkward relationship.  

The principles of establishment become unclear, leading to harmful outcomes during the growth phase. The more filial the child, the less likely they are to scold. Sometimes to help the child realize their mistake, the elders must step in; if they don’t, instead of punishing the child, they may end up comforting them.

The greatest sacrifice of the large family system falls on the youth.

Thirdly, it is a commonly repeated immense harm. The dependency lifestyle becomes entrenched whether the son and daughter-in-law depend on the parents or vice versa, leading to drastic reductions in efficiency for the whole Korean race, consequently accelerating the decrease of our economic life.

Young individuals, due to their lack of experience in life, when met with even minor issues, often yield to panic; for instance, if a child falls ill, they may act impulsively, creating major problems in their lives. Hence arises the belief that an elderly parent must be nearby to mitigate distressing situations.

However, that rationale cannot justify the claim that the parents must live together. In those moments, rather than having a mother-in-law imposing herself in the household, it’s more viable to have an experienced doctor or someone older available during regular times. A modern mother-in-law with a proper understanding should not interfere unless necessary, participating only when it is advantageous. (Audience applause)

可便  

金美理士  

If I say such things, I wonder if the young ones who happen to be in my position will consider me as reflecting outdated thinking of an era gone by. (Audience laugh) The saying goes that 'the reason one puts effort into their rice paddy is to eat the resulting rice' and similarly, the affection a parent has for their child is often due to the hope that when they grow up, they will inherit and perpetuate the family name and care for them when they can no longer be independent.

Given that parents invest such immense effort in rearing their children, why should they not be able to coexist amicably when the children become their spouses? If a daughter-in-law lives with a used-to-be family-in-law yet retains a separate lifestyle, living separately would not be any different from others in society—what makes it beneficial or worthy?

Besides Western countries that have developed individual systems and social structures, countries like Korea that have evolved around family-centered ideologies over hundreds of years cement these familial traditions.

If such thinking leads to a discourse on separation, it raises a major issue for us all! While cohabitating with a mother-in-law may have its own challenges, it does not mean similar challenges are absent elsewhere. Is separation from family truly free of conflict?  

Why would two people who typically understand each other become distant and face separation? When there are issues, they must be actively mitigated—not disconnected.  

To claim dependence and expectations undermine the familial system does not necessarily mean separation is the solution! When your spouse's actions are criticized, who could rightfully claim that dependence emerges simply from living with a mother-in-law?  

There’s no need for pressure in living together, and adding more barriers complicates relationships.  

Being with a mother-in-law does not negate the urgency of a harmonious, diligent household. Rather, a cohesive view of moral traits can be critical for the upbringing of the new generation—many children raised under grandparent care demonstrated that they themselves may not exhibit proper decorum.  

Upon marriage, a couple typically retains individual thought patterns for child-rearing but finds it challenging to uphold those beliefs under the influence of the older generations. This ultimately leads to issues where children may be nurtured in disorder or may negligible education has detrimental consequences.  

Ultimately, the fundamental conflicts undermine harmony between the daughter-in-law and mother-in-law, suggesting that cohabitation is practically impractical in modern life. Separation becomes necessary to maintain individual ideology and optimally live according to one’s aspirations; even if in close proximity, it is essential to maintain some distance to prevent interference in personal matters.  
  
Thus, I assert that living together with a daughter-in-law is unacceptable.  

**Summary**  

This article presents a significant debate regarding whether a daughter-in-law should live with her in-laws in a traditional Korean family structure. The editors lay out arguments both in favor of and against cohabitation, emphasizing changing societal expectations and the impact of extended family relations on personal independence and marital harmony. A particularly interesting aspect for historians of Korean colonial history is the exploration of traditional familial roles and the resistance to change in a modernizing society. This dialogue hints at the cultural tensions experienced during the colonial period, reflecting how historical contexts shaped and sometimes challenged existing family norms.

(滿場拍手) 討論問題라는 것은 항상 兩便이 어지간하게 뺏딍길 힘이 잇서야 말하는 사람이 열도 나고 滋味도 나는 것이지 의레히 그래야만 할 것을 꺼집어 내다가 討論을 한다면 亦是 듯는 사람도 흥미가 업슬 것임니다. 問題가 姑婦가 別居를 해야 올켓느냐? 同居를 해야 올켓느냐? 내가 말할 것도 업시 물론 同居를 해야 올치요. 아마 여러분도 그러케 생각하실 줄 암니다. 물론 이 문제의 本意도 그럿켓지만 내가 말하는 것이 또한 大家族制度를 주장하는 것이 아닌 것을 미리 아러 주십시오! 二代三代식 한집에서 들끌코 어서케 遠戚이라도 치(寸)수에 맛기만 하면 덤허놋코 두달 석달 일년잇해식 무조건하고 우둑허니 안저서 뜨더만 먹고 잇다든지 애비 할애비에게만 의로하고 지낸다든지 이 대가족제도라는 것은 惰怠를 기르고 依賴心을 북도다 주는 큰 惡習인 것은 내가 말하지 안어도 여러분이 더 잘 아실 것이 아닌가 더말 하고자 안슴니다만은 姑婦가 한집에서 사는 것이 올치 안타는 것은 어듸 當한 말인지 모르겟슴니다. 姑婦라는 것의 根本을 캐여봅시다. 父子는 한집에서 살고 싀어미와 며누리만 각각 산다는 것이 아니겟지요. 그러면 싀어미와 며누리는 애비와 아들에게 딸려서 그러한 名稱과 關係가<62> 잇는 것이 아님닛가 그러면 결국은 부자가 한집에서 사는 것이 올치 안타는 말이 되지 안슴닛가. 그러니-習慣도 고만두고 因襲도 고만두고 다 그까지 묵은 것은 깨트려버린다 하드라도 人倫과 道德을 깨트릴 수야 잇슴닛가 그러나 이새 新思潮에 젓고 破壞性을 가즌 靑年들의 말은 道德이란 무어며 法律이란 무엇이냐 저희끼리 저희마음대로 꿈이고 만든 것이 아니냐? 설사 道德은 그러타고 합시다. 그러면 人倫이란 것도 깨트려버리겟슴닛가. 더구나 世界歷史가 證明하고 世界人類가 崇尙하는 東洋의 道德과 倫理- 그 中에도 우리 됴선으로 말하면 君子의 나라라고 하지 안슴닛가. 무엇으로 君子의 나라라고 함닛가. 倫理와 道德을 꾸준히 직혀온 까닭이 아니고 무엇임닛가. 우리의 것이라고는 다-업서젓슴니다. 집도 터전도 다-업서젓서요! 그러나 웅켜쥐고 나려오는 것이알고는 이것밧게 업슴니다. 荒廢한 曠野에 다-씨려지고 부러진 그가 온대에서 한낫 홀로 피여잇는 이 倫理와 道德의 꼿마자 우리손으로 꺽고 우리 발로 밟아버리려고 함닛가? 東方의 禮儀之國이라는 그 알뜰한 일홈마자 떼여버리려고 함닛가? 時代가 時代이닛가...그러나 하고만흔 中에 아름다웁고 보배스러운 우리의 倫理와 道德에 革命을 이르키랴함닛가 생각해보십시오! 우리는 君臣有義보다도 父子有親이 한자리 上座에 잇지 안슴닛가. 그 아들이 그 아바지를 떼여버리고 따로 저희끼리 살어야을하요? 그러나 여긔에 問題삼는 別居라는 것은 職業을 따라서 먼 地方으로 가게 된다든지 經濟의 打拍 즉 生活活難으로 到底히 한집에서는 살 수가 업슴으로 눈물을 먹음고 東分西離한다든지, 아버지가 아들에게 財産을 分配해 주어서 分居를 식여준다든지, 其餘他의 할수업는 事情으로 別居할 수 밧게 업는 형편에 잇서서의 別居가 아니라 아버지는 頑固요 싀어머니는 잔소리꾼이요! 思想이 틀리고 주의가 맛지 안코 이상이 다른 까닭에 항상 衝突과 葛藤이 생김으로 도저히 한집에서는 살수가 업다하는 젊은 아들과 젊은 며누리가 끄을어 내어인 問題이라고 생각함니다. 그러면 그러한 頑固한 父母와 갓치 살다가는 일껏 가젓든 主義와 품은 理想이 헛것이 되겟슴으로 別居를 하는 것이 當然한 일이라고 하면 여긔에 올흐냐 글흐냐 問題삼을 것도 업슬 것이 아님닛가 그러나 그 문뎨를 끄을어 내인 그들이 亦是이 우리가 가지고 잇는 이 아름다운 倫理와 道德을 밟고 넘어 스기가 어려움으로 그런 것인가 생각함니다. 그러나 그까지 頑固뗑이-그까지 倫理道德다-골치압흔 것 생각치말고 자기 네마음대로 따로 살면고만이겟지만 여긔에서 사람이 사람된 갑이 이슨 것임니다. 만물의 영장이라는 사람에게는 다른 동물게서 차저 줄 수 업는 그것을 가젓기 때문이요. 여긔에 문뎨삼는 것도 버서나지 못할 그것이 잇는 까닭임니다.그러나 人道에 버서나서까지 사람된 근본문제를 짓밟아 바린다면 그는 벌서 사람이라는 사람이 아닐 것임니다. 그러나 이 문제가 예전브터이섯고 장래에도 끗끗내 잇슬 것이냐 하면 결코 안임니다. 前에는 물론 보지도 듯지도 못한 문뎨이고 압흐로도 들을 수 업슬 것이라고 생각함니다. 다만 요때! 요 過渡期대에만<63> 잇슬 것이고 現在에 잇는 문뎨임니다. 新思潮와 舊思想이 엇밧귀이는 요 現在에만 잇슬문제임니다. 그러나 당연히 잇슬 문뎨임니다. 세대가 이쯤되엿스닛가!그러나 당신네의 아버지와 싀어머니가 당신네를 기르기에 半平生을 虛費햇고 머리가 허-엿케 된 것을 생각해 보시오! 아들나서 좃타는 것이 무엇이고 며누리 보아서 좃타는 것이 무엇이겟슴닛가 아들 낫코 며누리 보아 부려먹자는 것이 아임니다. 長成한 아들을 짝을 맛추어 놋코 半平生의 苦生한 것도 이저바리고 아들 며누리가 滋味잇게 사는 것을 볼때에 어느 쉬어머니가 실혀하고 미워할 것임닛가. 싀어머니의 頑固와 沒理解한 것을 원망하지 말고 당신네가 過渡期에 處한 것을 恨하시오. 그들의 잔소리말고 당신네가 過渡期에 處한 것을 恨하시오. 그들의 잔소리와 沒理解가 얼마나 가릿가? 頑固한 아버지를 그 아들이 排斥하고 잔소리 만흔 싀어머니를 실혀하지마시오! 그 中에 도배흘 것이 만슴뉜다. 短處만을 집어내지 말고 長處뿐을 골나 쓰시오! 오늘의 새것은 내일의 현 것이 될것임니다. 싀어머니를 실혀하든 며누리가 排斥밧는 싀어머니가 될 것을 생각시오! 당신의 思想이 지금은 새것이지만 당신의 며누리때에는 다시 頑固소리를 들을 것을 생각해 보시고 참고 계십시오! 그만한 것을 못참고서야 무슨 新主義니 事業이니 하고 떠든단 말이요! 굿해여 우리가 이때껏 가지고 오든 道德과 人倫을 밟아바리랴고 마시기를 바라며 壇을 나려감니다.(박수)
