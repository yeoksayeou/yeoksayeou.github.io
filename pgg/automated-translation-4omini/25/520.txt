Laughing University  
Woman: "No matter what medicine they take, men will always listen with one ear and let it flow out the other."  
Man: "When women hear something, they listen with one ear and spit it out with their mouths."  

Thinking  
Mother: "Aren't you thinking of your younger sibling, just eating by yourself?"  
Child: "Why wouldn't I think? I was thinking that if 순길 (Sungil) didn't come, it wouldn't be good."  

Praise  
Woman: "I went to the exhibition and only saw your painting."  
Artist: "That is too much of a compliment."  
Woman: "In other drawings, a lot of people gathered, but I couldn't see your painting."  

Indeed  
Teacher: "A transparent body is something that can be seen through, like glass. Can you give an example of a transparent body?"  
Students together: "Teacher! That ladder (梯) over there!" <158>  

**Summary**  

This article provides humorous dialogues that reflect on gender perceptions and family dynamics in early 20th-century Korea. It captures moments between men and women discussing listening behaviors and a child’s reflection on family responsibilities. The exchanges also highlight social gatherings and the appreciation of art, showcasing the cultural values of the time. Historians may find the portrayal of gender roles and family relationships particularly insightful within the context of Korea's colonial period, as they reflect shifting societal norms and expectations.
