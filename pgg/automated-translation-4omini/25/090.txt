One person, one article, Mr. Zhang does not know

"Here, there is a letter addressed to Mr. Zhang, and I wrote a note stating, 'Mr. Zhang does not know.' I will return it to the brush."  

"That was well done."  

"It seems that Mr. Zhang is not here."  

"Then!"  

To say that, he was an ignorant person. However, to that extent, he had written using his knowledge. His note stating "Mr. Zhang does not know" would have possibly sufficed.  

If we write something simply so that others can understand, that would be the ideal of writing. I learned this from "Mr. Zhang does not know."  

<28>  

**Summary**  

The article reflects on the simplicity and clarity of writing, using a fictional letter as a device to convey the message. It discusses how writing should remain within one's limits of understanding, promoting the idea that effective communication prioritizes accessibility. This reflection may reveal attitudes during the colonial era in Korea regarding the use of language and expression in a time of cultural suppression, offering insight into how artists and writers navigated their identities and societal roles.

一人一文, 張主事不知
						李泰俊
「여기계시든 張主事어른께 편지가 옷사와요. 그래 제가 張主事不知ㄹ고 쪽지를 써 붓처 도로 붓첫지요.」
「그거 잘 햇구료.」
「張主事어른 안계시단말이 됏겟습지요.」
「그럼!」
말하자면 그는 無識軍이엿섯다. 그러나 그는 그만치 자신이 잇시 글을 써먹엇다. 그의 「張主事不知」라는 쪽지는 휼늉히 파쓰하엿슬 것이다.

무슨글이고 쉽게 쓰자 그것이 글의 理想일 것이다. 자기가 아는 그 범위안에서 자신이 잇시 활약하는 문자래야 할 것을 나는 이 「張主事不知」에서 배웟다.＜28＞
