When comparing trains and streetcars, the grand form of trains is indeed superior. However, despite the majesty of trains themselves, they appear somewhat monotonous to a spectator. There is no one who would see a train passing through a wide field and find it grand. 

In contrast, streetcars may be smaller, but they rumble and dash down the streets with great vigor.

Automobiles... for those riding in them, they are indeed comfortable and cuter in appearance than trains or streetcars, but from a distance or from afar, they look like a little thief just quietly sneaking along.

  

**Summary**

This article explores the perception of different modes of transportation—trains, streetcars, and automobiles—emphasizing their visual and experiential differences. It reflects a nostalgic admiration for the grandeur of trains while acknowledging the dynamic nature of streetcars and the more personal experience provided by automobiles. From the perspective of colonial history, the discussion showcases the evolving modernity and technological advancements within Korea during the Japanese occupation, highlighting the cultural shifts and changes in transportation that accompanied modernization in the early 20th century.
