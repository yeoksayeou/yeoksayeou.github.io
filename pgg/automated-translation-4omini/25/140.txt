기사제목	一人一文, 고무신 (One Person, One Article: Rubber Shoes)  
필자	尹聖相  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Other)  

As I was passing through an alley in T-dong, I saw a female student wearing somewhat decent rubber shoes. At first glance from a distance, I thought she was just an ordinary girl and felt a pang of sympathy, thinking, "What an unfortunate maiden!" However, as I approached and examined her more closely, I wondered, "Is this a girl who has nothing at all?"  

When I returned home and discussed this with Mr. C, the doubts I had felt earlier were not resolved immediately. Mr. C asked if I did not know that there was a reason for her wearing those rubber shoes.  

"She believes that she is not qualified to wear regular shoes. Although she has decent shoes at home, she put on whatever was available at hand just for a moment when going out," he explained.  

Thinking about it, that seems plausible.  

Though this is a common fact, it is something we often overlook, and we ourselves unknowingly engage in such behavior. <29>  

**Summary**  

The article reflects on the societal perceptions of women in Korea during the colonial period, using the example of a female student who chooses to wear rubber shoes instead of more respectable footwear. It illustrates the internalized class distinctions and practices of modesty among young women at the time. Historians may find this insight into social norms and expectations particularly revealing, as it highlights the struggles and social pressures faced by individuals in colonial Korea while navigating identity and class.

집에 도라왓다. C군과 그런 이야기를 할때고 아까 품엇든 疑訝가 풀니지 안이하엿다. C군은 그것도 몰르느냐고하면서 그의 고무신 끄으는 이유를 말햇다.
「제깐엔 제가 고무신 신을 자격이 아니다 제게는 휼늉한 구두가 잇지만 잠간 나오는 길에 발에 걸니는대로 집어 신은 것이 이것이다」를 표시하기 위한 것이라고 ... 듯고보니 그럴듯 햇다.
평범한 이 사실이연만 우리는 각금 발견하는 일이요 또 우리도 각금 不知不識間 이러한 행동을 하는 일이 만타.＜29＞
