What has made her so cold? The Record of Life that has come to pass, a 31-year-old Chaste Old Maid  
                                             松雀生  
In this world, there are many chaste widows, but if there is a chaste old maid, everyone would likely say it is merely a lie. However, that is not a lie; it is indeed a truth. K, a nurse working at S Hospital in Seoul, is a 31-year-old old maid who, despite numerous good-looking men, wealthy young masters, doctors, writers, and lawyers proposing marriage, absolutely refuses. Those who do not know assume that she intends to remain single for her entire life, but behind her refusal to marry lies a secret shrouded in a cloud like a fog billowing across the sky.

Now, although she is already over 50, standing like a beautiful rose facing a stormy spring mountain, she was quite the beauty about ten years ago when she first began working as a nurse at a certain hospital in Pyongyang during her 20s. The patients at her hospital looked forward to seeing her even more than waiting for the doctors, and if she did not come to the hospital for just a single day, it could seem as if their illness worsened. There were even those reckless young men who, having no ailments, became inpatients simply to see her beauty. 

Among the many patients who were captivated by her, there was a young man named P, who loved her profoundly and, moved by her kind nursing, pursued her not only while they were both at the hospital but even after his discharge, showering her with affection in both spirit and material offerings.

However, despite the deep affection P showed her, she did not waver even slightly in her heart. Though she cared for patients with utmost sincerity, she remained a pure maiden inside and could not bring herself to entertain love for another man. The persistence of P, who pursued her dedicatedly, caused her distress and sorrow, and forced her to resign from her post at the hospital and transfer to another place without his knowledge. Yet how could P, who had fallen so deeply in love, fail to track down where K was? 

Just a few days later, he found out where she was working again and continued to visit her hospital day after day, compelling her with his attention, and even writing letters whenever he could not go see her in person. After about a year of such devotion, the once steadfast heart of K, which had been like rock, began to thaw like spring snow, and the threads of their love tangled together.

However, there was one obstacle; P had already married and had children with his wife. Although they could not marry officially due to the constraints of their circumstances, they were nonetheless compelled by the intensity of their feelings to come together in love. While normal women would demand that the man divorce his wife or, at the very least, end their relationship and seek new love, the pure-hearted maiden had vowed to sacrifice herself entirely for P once she had engaged in love with him; she could not entertain feelings for another man. 

Whether this was right or wrong may be subject to public opinion, yet I merely wish to introduce the fact that such realities do exist in this world.

  
**Summary**  

This article presents the story of K, a 31-year-old nurse who refuses marriage proposals from various suitors despite being beautiful and popular, choosing instead to remain chaste and loyal to a married man, P. Their relationship develops over time, marked by P's perseverance and her inner struggle between love and purity. The narrative touches upon themes of loyalty, societal expectations regarding love, and the sacrifices made for personal convictions. Historians might find this article revealing as it highlights the complexities of romance and relationships under colonial rule where individual desires often clashed with societal norms and expectations, particularly within the context of gender roles in 1930s Korea.
