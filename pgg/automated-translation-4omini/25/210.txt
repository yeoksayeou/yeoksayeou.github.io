기사제목	朝鮮歷史日誌 (Korean Historical Diary)  
저자	없음  
기사형태	잡저 (Periodical)  

Korean Historical Diary  

1st (Old 2nd February)  
The Treaty of Protectorate with Japan, known as the Jae-mul-pu Treaty, is established. (Lee Tae-wang Byeongja)  

2nd (Same 3rd)  
Gwon Ryul, the Military Instructor, defeats the Japanese army greatly in Hyeong-ju. (Seonjo Gyesi)  

3rd (Same 4th)  
The system of the shrine, hall, and royal tombs is revised.  

4th (Same 5th)  
Military conscription decree is issued. (Gwangmu 7th year)  
The Commercial Company is established. (Lee Tae-wang Gihai)  

5th (Same 6th)  
Kang Han-jang greatly defeats the Khitan army and returns in triumph today. (Goryeo Hyunjo Gimae)  
The contract for loan with the Japanese Industrial Bank is established. (Gwangmu 10th year)  

6th (Same 7th)  
The prohibition of the one-yuan silver currency is enacted. (Ryeonghee 2nd year)  
Kim Yu-shin defeats the Goguryeo army at Pyohyeon River. (Silla Munmu Wang 2nd year Gyeongmyo)  

7th (Same 8th)  
Sunjong is born. (Lee Tae-wang Gapso)  
Crown Prince So-hyeon and Prince Bongrim are taken as hostages to Qing and go to Pan-yang with Qing's 9th King. (Injo Byeongja)  

8th (Same 9th)  
Envoy Liu Hai asks for peace at Ganghwa with Gang Hong-rip. (Injo Jeongma)  

9th (Same 10th)  
Lee Sip occupies Gyeongseong. (Injo Gyeongja)  
The father of Gang Hong-rip, Gang * is sent from the enemy camp for peace talks. (Injo Jeongma)  
Japanese patrol police are first established in the five districts of Hanseong. (Gwangmu 9th year)  

10th (Same 11th)  
Jeong Jong-sin and Jang Ban, among others, defeat Lee Sip at An-hyeon. (Injo Gyeongja)  
Banking regulations and agricultural banking regulations are published. (Gwangmu 10th year)  

11th (Same 12th)  
Injo relocates from Gongju. (Injo Gyeongja)  
The Three Scholars are sent to Pan-yang. (Injo Byeongja)  
Lee Han-eum, the virtuous teacher, is born. (Myeongjong Sinmi)  

12th (Same 13th)  
The Korea-US Trade Treaty is established. (Gwangmu 5th year)  
Two Korean individuals, Jeon Yun-myeong and Jang In-hwan, are killed by an American official in Sanggang.  

13th (Same 14th)  
An Yu, Lee Bang-sil, and others greatly defeat the Red Robes. (Goryeo Gongmin Wang Gyeongja)  
The Central Bank regulations and exchange voucher regulations are published. (Gwangmu 7th year)  

14th (Same 15th)  
Seedling farms are established in Jin-gam and Yang-ju. (Ryeonghee 2nd year)  

15th (Same 16th)  
The Water Resources Association regulation is published. (Gwangmu 11th year)  

16th (Same 17th)  
Sejong passes away. (Gyeongmu)  

17th (Same 18th)  
Lee Tae-jo defeats the Eastern Ning Prefecture and returns. (Goryeo Gongmin Wang 19th year)  
Japanese troops defeat Russian troops at Jeongju. (Gwangmu 8th year)  
The Governor-General Office opens. (Same as above)  

18th (Same 19th)  
Count Jin Yeong-heo leads the Jin army across the Liao River. (Gonghae Dosi)  
Kim In-mun allies with Liu In-won and Buyeo Yong at Hyung-jun. (Silla Munmu Wang Eulchul)  

19th (Same 20th)  
Kim Bu-sik brings peace to Myeolcheong at Seogyeong. (Goryeo Wonjong 14th year Byeongjin)  

20th (Same 21st)  
Silla grants amnesty to the whole world and halves the taxes. (Munmu Wang Jeongchul)  

21st (Same 22nd)  
Funyeong and Cheongjin are opened as ports. (Gwangmu 9th year)  
Injo returns from Gongju to Gyeongseong. (Injo Gyeongja)  
The tomb of Queen Sin-deok Kang is relocated from Jeong-dong in Seoul to Yangju. (Taejong Gichul)  

22nd (Same 23rd)  
Great Master Seosan Hyu-jeong passes away at Myohyang Temple in the capital. (Seonjo Gachin)  

23rd (Same 24th)  
Goryeo Gongmin Wang avoids the Red Rebellion in Fuzhou (Geum An-dong) and returns to the capital. (Gyeongjin)  

24th (Same 25th)  
The regulations on the disposal of national forests and mountain lands are promulgated. (Ryeonghee 3rd year)  

25th (Same 26th)  
Lee Soon-shin is captured. (Seonjo Jeongyu)  
The Hongmun-gwan is abolished. (Gae-guk 56)  
Sunjong farms directly in Dongjeok-cheon. (Ryeonghee 3rd year)  

26th (Same 27th)  
Lee Gwan presents a great defeat against the Jurchen with the offering of tablets. (Goryeo Yuisong 3rd year Musi)  

27th (Same 28th)  
Lee Gwan erects a great monument to the Jurchen in Gongheung. (Same as above)  

28th (Same 29th)  
The iron pillar of Yongdu Temple in Cheongju is cast. (Silla Seondeok Wang 3rd year Gyeongmu)  

29th (Same 30th)  
The taxation income ledger and office regulations are implemented. (Ryeonghee 2nd year)  

30th (Same 1st March)  
The righteous woman Yi Gi-lon, at the foot of the rock tower in Jinju, embraces the enemy commander and sacrifices herself for her loyalty. (Seonjo Gyesi)  
The tomb of Danjong is posthumously honored to Jangseong. (Sukjong Gihuo)  

31st (Same 2nd)  
The army of Liao's Yelü Ning brings Wu Yuan and Bao * back to our country. (Goryeo Yuisong Jeongyu)  

**Summary**  

The "Korean Historical Diary" chronicles significant historical events, focusing on military actions, political changes, and royal matters over a month. It highlights battles, treaties, and administrative reforms spanning various periods, providing a succinct view of Korea's evolving history. Historians may find the records of military engagements and diplomatic treaties particularly revealing, as they reflect Korea's struggles and relations with Japan during a time of colonial influence.
