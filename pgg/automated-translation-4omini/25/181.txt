**Summary**  

This article announces the results of a prize contest from a previous issue, listing the winners and the correct answer to a question posed. The interest in the contest, demonstrated by over 23,000 participants, reflects a keen engagement with popular culture at the time. For historians, the high level of participation hints at the social dynamics and the cultural climate during the colonial period in Korea, exemplifying the allure of print media and public contests as means of engaging a population under colonial rule.
