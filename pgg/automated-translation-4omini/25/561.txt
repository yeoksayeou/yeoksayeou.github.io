The text discusses newly established branch offices across different regions, highlighting their locations and leadership. The branches are in Gwangju, Sinchung, and Yongin, detailing their jurisdictions and heads. The Gwangju branch is led by Jeong Tae-ho, located in Sukiwon Town, while the Sinchung branch is managed by Bae In-sik in Dalksu-ri. Meanwhile, the Yongin branch is directed by Choi Sang-u in Geumyangjangri.

  

**Summary**

This article presents an update on the establishment and organization of branch offices in various locations, emphasizing local leadership and their areas of influence. It reflects the administrative developments of the time, highlighting the expansion of governmental or commercial oversight during the Japanese colonization of Korea. Historians may find the designation of these branches significant, as it reveals the expanding infrastructure the colonial regime employed to facilitate its governance and economic control over Korea.
