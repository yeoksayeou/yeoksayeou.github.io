잡지명	별건곤 제27호  
발행일	1930년 03월 01일  
기사제목	窃盜病患者 (Cleptomania Patients)  
필자	迫眞  
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Screenplay)  

窃盜病患者  
						迫眞  
(Explanation) Cleptomania (窃盜病) is a condition characterized by sudden urges to steal various objects, often without the intent to keep them. This is known scientifically as Cleptomania. According to specialists, a peculiar aspect of this disorder is that a sharp slap to one's cheek can often bring the person back to their senses during the episode. The following piece will be presented as a Nonsense drama, akin to a revue, filled with meaning.

First Scene: Inside the Doctor's Office  
(Explanation) The entrance and the examination room door are facing the audience. Chairs, tables, a hanging board, and various decorative items are comfortably arranged. Several patients suffering from cleptomania, resembling modern men, workers, courtesans, and merchants, are eagerly awaiting the arrival of a nurse. It is around 10 AM. A young man enters, leading his wife.  

A nurse comes out of the examination room.  
Nurse (standing between the two) Please detail the progression of the illness first.  
Man (muttering) You want me to tell about my wife’s experiences one by one? Is that possible! ... Um! Since I've never had an experience at a department store … once at the theater ... and twice on the streetcar (a chuckle is heard) ... and several times in the reception room while hosting guests - that’s all. (The nurse writes it down one by one.)  

Worker (suddenly and loudly waving all nine fingers) Hey you guys, give me my fingers! Hey you all, hand them over quickly! (madly) Oh my fingers, my fingers!  
(Explanation) Everyone turns pale and hides their hands behind their backs.  

A merchant-like man (growing more flustered) This is a serious problem! I just brought a dissecting knife for surgery into the room, and if I lose that, it will be a big deal. (hastily puts his bag under the chair.)  
(Explanation) A medical doctor, the young man’s friend, comes out of the examination room.  
Doctor (seeing the worker's wife) This worker had an accident where he lost a finger while working in a factory, so each time he experiences a cleptomania attack, he subconsciously seeks to find his severed finger. (He extends his hand to the worker.) Look here. Hold onto my finger. (He slaps the worker on the cheek to bring him back to his senses.) Within cleptomania, there are special things that do only exist singularly. Now, look at that long chair.  

See how that modern man’s hand is smoothly sneaking into the pocket of that modern-price girl’s dress. Look there! That man’s hand already has a pack of cigarettes taken out from the modern girl’s inner pocket.  
Man (suddenly checking his pockets) Look, it just came out ... Did I leave it at home?  
Other man (naturally) What are you saying? Is it the cigarettes? (Offering the pack he took) If it’s nothing too serious, please have one.  
Man Sure, thank you. But I’m sorry. (Reaching for the cigarette) It’s peculiar - isn’t it? I must have left mine at home too as it looks exactly the same. This one also - right down to the contents - just like yours, a high-class fellow. Mako mako mako ma-ko  
(Condition of the attack)  
(Explanation) The modern man's hand is slowly reaching towards the cigarette pack again.  

Doctor Isn’t it curious? The pack of cigarettes has returned to its original place. He can only rely on the words of the doctor’s wife and be sure of his treatment. And he asked to be seen before these people.  
Man I’m very sorry.  
Nurse (peeking her head from the examination room) Number 11, please.  
Man Hurry in! I will just run back to the office, so be sure to greet the doctor properly.  
Doctor Then I must be going as I have matters elsewhere. Excuse me.  
Wife Excuse me. (enters the examination room.)  
Merchant-like man (blocking the doctor's exit) Excuse me, since you are a doctor, shouldn't this wonderfully sharp scalpel be useful to you at all? Don't you have any reservations about it? I am a salesman from XX Medical Equipment Company.  
Doctor Why are you here if you’re not a patient? I’m not a surgeon, so I have no need for that. (Exits with his friend.)  
Merchant-like man Hmph, if you don’t want to buy, that’s your choice, but I’d like to see some more of the antics these madmen are performing here before I leave.  
Worker I’ve been waiting the whole time, and they still call this last lady first; damn it.  
Courtesan It’s already been hours. Who will pay for my time?  
Other man If it’s about the courtesy of attending the ladies, naturally I deserve priority; what’s going on here? The jerk of a doctor!  
Other man Waiting like this is without end (looking at the other man) Honey, shall we take a stroll somewhere else?  
(Explanation) The waiting room is growing increasingly chaotic.  

Second Scene: Examination Room  
(Explanation) Various medical equipment painted white are noticeable. An elderly doctor with a warm demeanor and a flourishing white beard is nodding as he reads through a book. The wife is positioned on the treatment table.  
Doctor Hmmm. (still nodding while looking at the book, approaches the treatment table) Where is the item of the guest brought to the house ... show me your tongue. Hmmm, let me see your eyes a bit ... (Turns back the wife’s eyelids.) Oh dear! (Hearing the uproar from the waiting room) It’s very noisy. I should go out. (sends the nurse out) Now let me check your pulse. (Grabs the wife's hand.) Oh- how splendid! It shines brilliantly with a platinum ring - platinum, platinum, platinum - oh dear, I made a mistake – oh dear, my eyes are getting hazy - how strange - platinum ... (unconsciously tries to put the ring into his pocket.)  
Wife (snatching the ring back) No, sir ... now I see - you too are a cleptomania patient. What is this? (The doctor looks dazed.) What a big deal has happened! Sweetheart - (shouting) Slap him!  

Doctor (coming back to a state of fear) Oh dear, what have I done! - Ah! (For a while, speechless, rubbing his hands, wandering about in the room.) The prestige of my scientific dignity, which I have maintained for decades, has collapsed in a moment due to a lady's ring! How can this happen! (Rubbing his hands, wandering around left and right)  

Third Scene: First and Main Room  
(Explanation) There isn’t even a shadow of people, and the only interior decorations consist of a ceiling light. The husband enters.  
Man (suspiciously) Did I enter wrongly? Nah, but the door has the examination room’s sign on it. Where have all the patients and the furniture gone?  
Doctor (nearing) What’s happening here?  
Man You see, I came in because my heart felt uneasy, and this is what I find!  
Doctor (slapping his hands) All of the people who were here must be cleptomania patients. So it’s clear that while all were waiting for a long time, they ended up taking something one by one as their condition emerged.  
Man Yes, indeed.  
Doctor Absolutely! What about you?  
Man I came since my wife was feeling faint, but why have you come back?  
Doctor I have only the chain left from my pocket watch, and nothing else is there. When I was here, I adequately guarded against the other patients, not to mention your wife, but when they whisked it away, it’s incredibly puzzling. In truth, it is worse than you believe, and I cannot find reason behind it.  
Man So what happened to my wife?  
Wife (coming out of the examination room.) Oh honey. (Clinging to her husband’s chest) I was so scared, I lost my mind. Who would have thought that the doctor himself was a cleptomania patient! (The two are surprised.) So if that happens, he could have stolen this ring!  
Man I can’t tell whose word is true; how can I trust anyone? We shouldn’t stay in this place any longer; let’s hurry and leave.  
Doctor Please wait a moment; it’s very bothersome, but would you check if my watch is with your wife? Could you please take a look?  
Man Oh no, then are you saying you will slap my wife? Go ahead and try it; however, you should be ready to take your coat off (as he starts to take his jacket off) and prepare for battle!  
(Explanation) At this moment, the doctor quietly opens a path. The doctor exits from the examination room, looking dazed and rubbing his hands, and the couple leaves.  
Doctor Please come to your senses. This is a big deal! (He goes back into the examination room, pulling away.)  

Fourth Scene: A Busy Intersection  
(Explanation) At a large busy intersection, the traffic cop’s regulation is ineffective, and a crowd has gathered from all directions around the intersection. In the shop windows, many observe as if the sticky and creeping heads from a dead fly are looking through. … …  
Led by the worker, various courtesans, modern men, modern ladies, and nurses are all carrying various items gathered from the waiting room, marching through the crowd. The merchant-like man is last in line, and just as he sees this parade, he starts to look frightened and hastily turns to leave.  

A detective in traditional clothing follows him closely.  

Fifth Scene: Again, First and Main Room  
(Explanation) The doctor appears from the examination room while the detective and merchant-like man appear simultaneously from the entrance.  
Detective (holding up the watch to the doctor) Do you know whose this is?  
Doctor (gladly) It’s mine. I was really about to lose hope here.  
Detective (looking at the merchant-like man) Is that so? (Handing the watch back to the doctor) This man is a thief disguised as a merchant, wandering around hospitals.  
Doctor Absolutely! Absolutely! I was told to buy a scalpel for my friend, and you slipped it away then. Well, thank you! Now, any suspicion against my friend’s wife and the doctor is cleared up, and the old justice will be restored again.  
Merchant-like man But what will happen to those guys making such a ruckus on the streets? Hmph, grateful? Part of their crime of creating chaos amongst the streets is greatly yours. But if they hadn’t shown up, you wouldn’t have caught it.  
Detective Well, a few men and women were caught in the chaos at that intersection, and in their statements, there was some relation to this examination room, so I was sent to protect the premise and came back with this. But what exactly happened?  
Doctor Yes, those individuals are all patients from this office. I will tell you in detail. Please let’s go.  
- END -  

**Summary**

The article is a satirical play centered around patients suffering from cleptomania visiting a doctor's office. The humorous chaos of the characters exemplifies their compulsions to steal various items, leading to a series of misunderstandings and absurdities. This piece provides insight into societal attitudes during the colonial period, reflecting the psychological struggles faced by individuals and the light-hearted approach to mental health issues, which may have been stigmatized in that era.
