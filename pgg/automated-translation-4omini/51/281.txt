Prize Question

Prize  
Special prize for one person: Decorative desk clock  
Normal prizes for ninety-nine people: One volume of books  

Regulations  
◇ Write your answers on a postcard-sized inquiry, clearly stating your name and address.  
◇ Be sure to cut and attach the reader certificate from this month's issue.  
◇ On the envelope, write "京城開闢社別乾坤懸賞界".  
◇ On the envelope, write "Original Manuscript Inside" and affix a two-cheon stamp on the corner of the envelope.  
◇ If possible, you can send multiple entries from different individuals in one envelope for convenience and save on postage. However, each individual’s answer must have its own reader certificate attached.

**Summary**  

This article presents a prize question and invites readers to suggest what kinds of articles they would like to see in future issues. It lists prizes for participating in the questionnaire, which include a decorative desk clock and various books. The response rate for the previous month's question was notably high, indicating active reader engagement. This reflects a vibrant reader community during the colonial period and highlights the magazine's effort to foster interaction with its audience. Historians might find it interesting how such endeavors reveal the cultural and literary pursuits under colonial rule, contributing to an understanding of public discourse and consumer interests in the 1930s.
