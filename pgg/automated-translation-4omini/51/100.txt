Animals Are the Same  
The indoor concert held at Mrs. A's cultural house in Dongdaemun was quite successful. Mrs. A asked the vocal artist Mr. P, “Could you sing just one more piece for us?”  
Mr. P replied, “Well, if you ask for one more, I can do it, but it’s getting late, and would it disturb the neighbors?”  
“Not at all, it’s perfectly fine. Everyone knows about it. Besides, the dogs in the area bark all night anyway.” <17>  

**Summary**  

This article presents a lighthearted exchange at a social gathering, where the host encourages a performer to continue despite the late hour, humorously noting that neighborhood dogs create more noise than the concert could. This reflects the intimate and familiar social dynamics among people during the colonial period in Korea, as public concerts represented both cultural engagement and a form of resistance to colonization by fostering a sense of community and identity.
