The Manuscript of Sōha  
故 方定煥 (deceased Bang Jeong-hwan)  
Every time that wretched bug called "bedbug" comes around, I think of two things.  

The first thought is that the greatest insult one can throw at a person is to say they are as worthless as a bedbug.  

The second thought is whether there exists anyone among the Korean people who has ever considered the idea of a remedy for bedbugs.  

If someone could invent a miraculous drug to kill bedbugs, wealth of one million won would instantly come pouring in—this is a certainty even without consulting heavenly deities. 

One million! In a world where beauties are sold for just a few won and where a murder case can arise for a mere one and a half won, just imagine the riches one could amass! This contemplation makes me lament not having studied medicine or pharmacology.  

Even a non-pharmacologist like me, who ponders with a puzzled mind, can see there are two clear paths for dealing with bedbugs. The first method involves discovering the most potent poison for humans and using it against bedbugs by spraying it wherever they might be found. The second involves finding the scent that attracts bedbugs so irresistibly that they cannot help but swarm towards it, much like a rich man’s child can’t resist rushing to grab land deeds. 

Each of these methods is likely to be more certain than others. However, exploring poisons may lead one to more potent solutions than the ones already experimented with, as I have found that spraying kerosene or gasoline has proven ineffective. I think it might be more fruitful to discover the scent that bedbugs are drawn to.  

But what scent could that be? An expert in pharmacology should spend time conducting real research to know. Nevertheless, it’s evident even to an ignorant person like me that bedbugs are attracted to human sweat. Once they catch a whiff of a human's body odor, they cannot help but emerge, even from a three-year old shell. Thus, it is indeed true that bedbugs are drawn to the smells of human sweat and blood.  

So, might it not be a good idea to utilize the dirty water from a bathhouse? Instead of discarding it, one could strain it to create a concentrate that captures the grime (sweat and dirt) and use it to bait bedbugs, luring them into a chamber pot or a can of oil? 

In a single ondol room (a traditional Korean heated floor room) where bedbugs are caught for as little as thirty jeon, if one household had just three rooms and one million households did this just once, that would amount to one million won. 

Who hasn’t had such thoughts? Ha ha...

July 23rd marks the first anniversary of the late Sōha Bang Jeong-hwan’s death. The editor has chosen to publish this manuscript in remembrance of him. Dear readers who think of Bang, please take care in reading this. Especially during this summer when bedbugs abound—  

 
**Summary**  

This article presents a humorous yet thoughtful reflection by the deceased writer Bang Jeong-hwan on the ubiquitous problem of bedbugs during the summer of 1932. He explores both the potential for wealth through the invention of a remedy for bedbugs and the science behind the creatures' attraction to human sweat. The article offers a glimpse into the everyday concerns during the Japanese colonial period in Korea, highlighting the economic aspirations and societal issues of the time, while also revealing a common cultural frustration with pest problems which persists across generations.

小波遺稿, 임자 찻는 百萬圓
						故 方定煥
숭얼숭얼숭얼 싯뻘건 『빈대』란 놈이 잡아도 나올 때마다 생각하는 일이 두 가지 잇다.
『사람 중에 낫분 놈은 빈대가튼 놈이라고 욕을 하는 것이 제일 큰 욕이겟다』하는 생각하고
『조선사람 중에는 빈대 잡을 약을 생각해 보는 이가 그러케 한 사람도 업슬가』하는 생각이다.
빈대 잡는 신통한 약을 발명만 하면 단번에 一百萬圓씩은 굴너 도러올 것이 천지신명께 점쳐보지 안트래도 확실한 일이다.
백만원! - ! 3, 4원에도 미인이 끌니고, 1원 50전에 살인사건도 이러나는 세상에 백만원! 백만원이 생긴다면. 이런 생각을 할 때 나는 의학이나, 약학을 배호지 못한 것을 한탄한다.
약학자 아닌 사람이 갑갑한 생각으로 따저 본대도 빈대를 잡는데는 두 가지 길이 잇슬 것이다. 사람에게 비상이 독약인 것처럼 빈대에게 제일 독한 독약을 발견하야 빈대 잇슴즉한 곳에 뿌리던지 아니면 빈대가 제일 조와하야 어엽분 게집보고 부자ㅅ집 자식이 땅문서 들고 대여들 듯 참지 못하고 달겨들 만큼 빈대가 조와서 냄새낼 물건을 발견하야 그 냄새로 빈대란 빈대를 모조리 요강 속으로 꼬여드리던지 석유통 속으로<50> 꼬여드리던지 이 두 가지 밧게 더 확실한 방법이 업슬 것이다.
그러나 독약을 생각하는 길은 석유를 뿌려보아 휘발유, 양잿물을 뿌려보아 그래도 안 되니가 더 독한 것은 생각하기 당연하겟고, 빈대가 조와 못견대고 달겨들 냄새를 발견할 도리를 하는 것이 올흘 것 갓다.
빈대가 대체 무슨 냄새를 조와하는가 그것은 약학자가 추근추근하게 급히 굴지 말고 천천히 실지 연구를 해보아야 알 것이다. 그러나 우리 따위 무식배의 생각으로도 빈대가 사람의 땀내를 조와하는 것은 분명하다. 어두운 속에서 그 좁은 틈바귀에 끼여 잇다가도 땀흘리는 사람의 고기냄새를 맛기만 하면 3년 묵은 *껍질도 긔가 나서 긔여 나오는 것으로 보아 사람의 땀내와 피 냄새를 조와하는 것만은 사실이다.
그러니까 말이지 목욕탕에서 목욕하고 버리는 더러운 물, 그 물을 그저 버릴것이 아니라 그것을 체에 쳐서 건데기(땀과 때)만 남기던지 어떠케 그려서 『엑기스』를 맨들든지 하면 어떨가. 그래서 그것을 요강속 바닥에 노코 빈대들을 꼬드리거나 석유통 속에 노코 꼬여드리거나 하면 묘하지 안흔가 말이다.
온돌방 한간치에 30전씩 적게 적게 *잡아 한 집에서 3간치씩만 백만 집에서 한번씩 사도 百萬圓.
