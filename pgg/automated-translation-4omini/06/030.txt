Prediction: What Will Korea Be Like in Ten Years?  
If one says they don't know, they wouldn't even know what tomorrow or the day after will bring. If they say they do know, then a hundred years into the future is also something humans can comprehend. The question “What will Korea be like in ten years?” at first glance seems vague. At the same time, it can also be seen as irresponsible. However, upon further reflection, it is neither as vague nor as irresponsible as it seems. For modern individuals, a ten-year foresight is something anyone can achieve. There might be doubts about matching it with reality for a moment, but in general, I can confidently say that it will mostly be accurate. Here, I will share my ten-year forecast with the readers and introduce the thoughts of those who foresee the next decade. I will temporarily set aside political issues and ask for your understanding. Also, opinions will vary individually, so I hope you will not blame others if their opinions do not match yours. (Editor)

Surely, the Korean spirit will emerge.  
Choi Namseon

Not being able to see through a single layer is a sign of a dull mind; yet in Korea, where eternity and infinity are visible in dreams, there are also divine treasures, sacred literature, historical records, and folklore. It is said that predictions of the next ten years may feel as if they are merely a repetition of the past. However, I am not that person.  

If a fool dreams and a primitive man has no distinction between reality and concept, no matter how I try to gather and process the remnants of the dreams left in my head, it is still difficult to reach clarity. I might know honest truths, yet whether such honesty is accepted by this "別乾坤" (Separate Universe) remains questionable. It has been a long time since honesty was no longer accepted, and it is uncertain whether "別乾坤" can even accommodate this honesty.  

So, what about ten years ahead? From the perspective of ideology and academia, if I may speak, it appears that the heart of the matter easily materializes in the palm of one's hand, while the lost sheep of the nine paths must be visibly cared for! As I ponder, the accumulated thoughts and intentions of the last decade will find an outlet ten years later, transforming in value. If the three ages are interconnected, could it be said that the future and the past are originally not two separate entities?  

Surely, in ten years, there should be some resolution in the ideological sphere.  

Overall, by looking back on the past ten years of joys and sorrows, the era's movements will, of their own accord, transform into a stable and organized path, definitively establishing the main current of the times. Those who can compose words smoothly for business, those adept at speaking ill, those skilled in sophistry, those who thrive on flatteries, those who can mimic a parrot, those who deceive with grace, and those who can manipulate well will be out of the picture by then. Since many have already been misled, now there has arisen a need for firm foundations, sincerity, and vigilant realities; without these, one cannot gain general approval.  

The era will be handled based on practical economic theories rather than by philosophy or abstract ideals. The coherent and practical thoughts that guide and innovate reality will be the prevailing ideology by that time.  

Conceptual ideologies, movements centered on banners, and strategic plans will be the concerns of the past. It is likely that the remnants of those experiences will be spoken with bitterness by the heroes of those times. For they will be laughed at by how unaccommodating they were, even if time has passed; the opposing parties, within their own factions, will be full of countless counter-revolutionary elements that have failed to unify themselves, demonstrating the futility of their struggles.  

The only absolute reality for Koreans will be that everyone takes a stand, and if one does not break this iron wall of pretentiousness, then all else will be mere illusions. They will understand how amusing it is to tear each other apart, bickering and engaging in disputes while failing to realize that they share the same underlying reality. Regardless of perspectives, while opinions might vary, the visceral connection through shared realities will seem to paint different pictures when viewed from different depths, and yet there can be no fundamentally different views or theories.  

Why has this become so confusing? What is the reason for wandering down an illuminated path while turning one’s back amidst struggled walks along the same route? Life is practical. Why has it been forced into abstraction, amusement, and dreaming? Tomorrow will be tomorrow, and whether I succeed or fail, ultimately hinges solely on my own actions.  

Why, when expecting something from the southern neighbors in the mornings and relying on the northern neighbors in the evenings, has the focus on "myself for myself" perpetually failed to establish itself as the center of understanding? 

Through the strife that has chipped away at brotherhood, we have failed to see the roots being uprooted as we watch from the heavens, while the ruins grew ever more desolate, allowing fertile lands to rot. What is the reason for this negligence?  

Amidst everything that has been shattered, things seem nothing more than chaos without the slightest wish for unity. Even as cries of progress and grand successes emerge, it feels the opposite; one might wonder why such times have stretched out endlessly.  

When looking back, the stark clarity of time reveals just how belabored our thoughts have become. One may wonder why, despite experiencing such trivial matters, there appears to be a sudden darkening of our understanding and why even in times like these, there seems to be no reflection on such matters. 

However, as I have come to a delayed yet profound awakening, I can say I am beginning to realize that I will be among those who have wasted what should have been more productive times from this ten-year span.  

The heart of ideation at that time will be a uniquely Korean essence. This will dominate various aspects through its formidable penetrative power and universality. Finally, the dawn that has long beckoned will shine forth and spread universally.  

Of course, amidst this, various elements will persist, and some may even emanate strangeness. Yet the core ideology will undeniably emerge clearly centered around being distinctly Korean.  In earlier times, figures like "Steiner," "Marx," and "Huxley" served sharp transitional roles, but eventually, they too will integrate entirely with the "Korea" of the revisited melting pot we are in, revealing nothing but the cyclical nature of Taebaeksan’s **(太白山)** lunar eclipse in the distance.  

In general, words will diminish, and actions will multiply. Now that there is a recognition that merely complaining about others will not bring harmony, actions and efforts will become more significant and prevalent, thus chipping away at the phrases and searches for communication that once typified the era we were once lost in.  

The influences of ignorance, absurdity, and misunderstandings will unfortunately diminish. The overly simplistic theories of world trends, as well as human justice arguments will significantly lessen. The awareness that one’s success is inherently tied to one’s merit and efforts will begin to root within everyone’s collective consciousness.  

Thus, the emergence of a new Korea will begin to take shape within this environment, reflecting an essence that will blossom forth, free and liberated. The first expression of this will manifest in the shape of “Let us create something together.”  

This will take the shape of rolling with the proverbial punches in this transitional phase. Shadows of moderation shall dwindle, and amid this shifting state, we will begin to see at least incremental achievements—building to further development. Instances of upheaval like those in the Gapsin year will serve as transitions while everything around us continues to blend, creating a mishmash of experiences, and the shadows of past upheavals will fully disappear in the hindsight.  

At that time, figures like "Marx" and "Lenin" might take on less importance compared to "Dangun," who will emerge as a leading figure embodying the synthesis of collective human values from experiences and the special mission of Korea, filling this unique role satisfactorily.  

To summarize, while scientific atmospheres are said to be evolving, this is merely a simplistic assertion, given that many complexities in academic discourse might also get lost within this mélange.  

Finally, the past is often said to be the mirror of the future. Thus, if that is indeed the case, then anyone without amnesia would almost certainly be regarded as a prophet. Whether our observation of the smooth reflection is purely practical remains open to question, and engaging in reflective discussions might entail anything from absurdities to the heartfelt validations within the pages of the "別乾坤's" undertaking.  

Religious Changes and Civil Law Reforms  
Ro Yong-hwan  

What will Korea be like in ten years? At first glance, this seems vague, but who can predict ten years ahead? I am confident that nothing from today will remain in ten years. While ten years do not seem too long, they can feel as extensive as a century against the backdrop of our history. The current year would be akin to a century from the past. I assert that all aspects of thought, logic, customs, habits, and all manner of phenomena will dramatically change in ten years.  

The separations of old and young, of senior and junior, of male and female, and all other forms of differentiation will vanish, producing an atmosphere of uniformity and equality. Topknots will be things of the past, while white garments will become commonplace. The pretentiousness of the yangban (aristocratic class) and the influence of the elderly, as well as the internal and external roles of women will all shift radically.  

The reason for this is that the religion controlling the minds of everyone and governing societal customs is transitioning. Religions unquestionably evolve in response to societal needs and universal favor, which leads to transformations in customs and institutions. Religion serves as a key pillar of civil law.  

Historically speaking, during the Buddhist era, societal customs aligned with Buddhist teachings; in the Confucian era, customs adhered to Confucian ideals; and in a Christian society, customs reflected Christian values. Likewise, in Korea, the eras of Confucianism and Christianity have become outdated. New religions adapted to the times are increasingly sought. As a result, societal customs and institutions will also evolve.  

During the Donghak (Eastern Learning) era, the heads of corrupt officials were severed, and well-to-do yangbans suffered in equal measure. In the Gapsin era, a mass head-shaving movement began, followed by the widespread adoption of black garments. Such power of religion is truly astonishing. In ten years, a new religion will surely dominate Korean society. Currently, with an estimated three million, the number of adherents has risen by five or six thousand just this year. In ten years, at least half of Korea will be followers of Cheondogyo (Heavenly Way).  

Its core tenet states, “Humanity is Heaven," and its goals are to protect the nation, maintain peace, and promote virtue worldwide. The doctrine it teaches is one that aligns with the demands of this era. Additionally, the historical background of this religion already possesses a strong collective societal backbone.  

Under the influences of “people like the sky” and “inequality amongst all people” teachings, the differentiation between men and women, as well as divisions of age and class, will fade away. Superstitions and debauchery will vanish too, alongside the obliteration of topknots and white garments. We will approach new societal institutions based on a new system of equality.  

The changes in religion will undoubtedly dictate shifts in civil law, or the customs and habits of the people will hardly change. The power of the state alone cannot accomplish this. Even if politics and law bear considerable might, they cannot ultimately govern societal morals or customs as religion does.  

I confidently declare this before everyone, without hesitation, whether addressing spirits or questioning the heavens. I reiterate that, in ten years, due to the fluctuations in religion, everything will transform.  

The Status of Korean Women in Ten Years  
I Gyeon-won  

1  
It is quite unreasonable to expect prophecies from non-prophets. Likewise, attempting to address the question of what the status of women in Korea will be in ten years is a daunting undertaking for anyone who is not a seer. 

However, considering that all things change without exception, it is impossible to deny that the status of women in ten years will bear no resemblance to that of women today. So in what direction and to what extent will this change occur? In this respect, social sciences afford us a degree of predictive capacity.  

2  
The Korean women’s landscape is reliant on the Korean society as a whole. Moreover, the direction of change within Korean society is intertwined with global social shifts. Therefore, any discussion on the transition of Korean women must be anchored within the broader context of worldwide transformations.  

Globally, feudal structures have nearly been entirely conquered by capitalist reforms. However, the capitalist modern paradigm has accrued sufficient experience and is beginning to aim swiftly toward an even more progressive order. This reflects the inevitable laws of historical evolution and the currents of global trends.   

In Korea, while the feudal order has yet to be completely dissolved, we remain unsuccessful in aligning with this larger global trend, thus having fallen into a “bibimbap-esque” state of muddled conditions. Yet, the elements within this “bibimbap” represent diverse historical processes—feudal elements mixed with capitalist influences, replaying opposing narratives whereby the capitalist forces assert forward movement against the backdrop of feudalist decline.  

From this perspective, the future change of the Korean women’s narrative becomes apparent. The feudal iron chains binding Korean women will gradually loosen, and the influx of capitalist individuality will increase. In parallel, the influences of the working-class elements will become more pronounced.  

3  
Now, how much transformation can we anticipate within the next decade? We can consider two scenarios: first, if significant paradigm shifts occur within society during this period; second, if no such transformations take place, imposing only standard everyday changes on the current state. If the first scenario unfolds, the status of women in Korea will entirely renew alongside that of society. All unreasonable shackles—inclusive of both feudal and modern impositions—will have lost their power, allowing women to engage actively in the true construction of newfound liberties.  

The pains of gender and class oppression will dissipate rapidly. However, if the second scenario arises, the changes in ten years might merely reflect an expanded version of the current state. For instance, if the number of educated individuals rises, so may the number of those conscious of individual freedoms, or if the comprehension of the working women emerges regarding class struggles, then the socialist women’s movement may gain momentum, but the fundamental circumstances may not undergo profound changes.  

Yet regarding whether or not Korean society will witness significant transformations over the next decade, I remain uncertain.  

The Economic State of Koreans in Ten Years  
Sŏn'u Chŏn  

If people cannot foresee the near future, how can we predict what will happen in ten years? Just as individuals adjust their thoughts, beliefs, and policies based on expectations of their personal futures, so too do social entities represented by individuals change with the fluctuations of the times. However, the outcomes resulting from changes in thought, beliefs, and policies—all of which connote an unknown culmination—cannot be foretold, whether as personal views or societal realities.  

The thought that was once square can become round; that which was once round can become square. Within such unpredictable results, both optimism may turn against itself, and pessimism, under certain conditions, can pivot to become optimistic.  

Thus, in a time of volatile transitions, we appreciate our current situation while exercising caution in response to changes. Instead of succumbing to pessimism regarding our current state, we must remain vigilant to opportunities for optimism. Additionally, it ought to be noted that taking sufficient action toward legitimate goals—even amidst adversity—will invariably yield fortunate outcomes, which will later become points of pride for ourselves and sources of contentment.  

Based on both the movements of the times and the evolution of thoughts and ideologies, we, as individuals existing in this turbulent period, must remain attentive to our ideals, as such vigilance is likely to lead toward achieving economic happiness.  

**Summary**  

The article presents varied predictions from several authors on what Korea will be like in ten years. The overall tone reflects optimism for societal change, particularly in ideology, gender equality, and religion. Contributors like Choi Namseon envision a uniquely Korean essence taking shape and becoming prominent, alongside the dissolution of outdated hierarchies and customs. This optimism in discussing potential reforms and the rising influence of new religious thoughts contrast starkly with the realities of colonial subjugation, suggesting a yearning for transformation amidst a historical backdrop of oppression. Historians may find it compelling to explore these aspirations as reflections of national identity formation during the colonial period.

預言 十年後의 朝鮮如何?
모른다 하면 내일일 모레일도 모를 것이요. 안다 하면 십 년 백년 후도 알 수 있는 것이 인간의 머리이다. 『십 년 후의 조선 如何?』얼핏 생각하면 막연한 문제요. 동시에 무책임한 일 갓트나 그러나 다시 생각하면 그닥 막연치도 안코 무책임한 즛도 안이다. 현대인으로 10년 預見은 누구든지 알 수 잇는 일이다. 사실과 부합될넌지는 잠간 의문이나 그러나 대개는 적중 될 것을 확신한다. 玆에 讀者 諸氏와 10년을 預約하기로 하고 先見 諸氏의 십년관을 소개한다. 정치 문제는 잠깐 피하야스니 諒解하라. 그리고 사람에 따라 보는 바도 母論 각각일 것이니 각자의 의견에 맛지 안는다고 그는 責하지 말기를 바란다. (編者)

반듯이 朝鮮心이 생길 줄 아오
崔南善

조희 한 겹으로 내다보지 못하는 것이 사람의 鈍眼이라고 하지마는 천년만년도 눈압헤 닥아서 보이든 神誌도 잇고 道誌도 잇고 鄭鑑도 잇고 土亭도 잇는 조선에서는 십 년쯤 압서의 일은 지난 일갓치 안다고 할 이도 잇기야 하겠지.＜14＞ 다만 내가 그이가 아닐 따름.
癡人에게 꿈이 만코 원시인에게는 현실과 관념과의 경계가 업다는 셈으로 내 머리엔들 살아지다가 남은 꿈ㅅ부스럭이가 업술가하야 뒤지고 끍어 모아도 의연히 걸리는 것이 업다. 업는 것을 업다고 말미 정직한 줄이야 알건마는 이러한 정직을 『別乾坤』이 용납하야서 맛긴 문제의 책임을 면해 줄는지가 의문이다. 정직이 통용 아니 된지도 오랜 세월이매 『別乾坤』이라고 꼭 이 정직을 조하하는지 未可必이다.
글세 십 년 압? 사상이나 학술상으로 보는 바만은 말슴하라 하시마는 六窓의 心猿이 만만히 손에 잡히며 九逵의 亡羊이 얼는 눈에 띄워야 말이지! 이리 굴이고 저래 뭉치매 결국은 지난 십 년 동안의 心念意圖가 그대로 십 년 압흐로 가치를 전환하야 나옴을 본다. 三世가 圓通이라 하면 미래와 과거가 原來不二리라고나 할가.
설마 십 년 후에는 사상계도 좀 歸端이 잇겟지.
대체로 보아서 回歸熱시대라고 할 其間 십 수년의 甘苦 여러 가지 경험이 저절로 沈靜과 整調를 나하서 줄잡아도 시대 사조의 本流正幹이랄 것만은 確立하겟지. 떼 잘 쓰는 자가 장사요, 욕 잘하는 자가 장사요, 억설 잘하는 자가 장사요, 숭내 잘 내는 자가 장사요, 鸚鵡부리 가진 자가 장사요, 言足사이비하는 자가 장사요, 영합 잘하고 속임수 조코 발라마치기에 得妙한 자가 장사일 시절은 벌서부터 아니겟지. 원체 만히 속아 본지라 인제는 깁히와 탄탄과 알심과 실사커 잇는 것이 아니면 일반의 승인을 바들 수 업습으로 뷘거플的 허수아비的 사상은 아모리 남의 혼인 의복을 빌어 닙고 造瑪瑙假眞珠의 가진 노리개을 차고 나서도 그제는 다시 남의 눈을 끄을지 못하게 되겟지. 生活本位, 實生活本位, 自己生活本位로 모든 것이 처리되는 철이매 시 보담 철학 보담 이상론 보담 압서서 요구되는 것이 현실적 經論-그 야무른 久遠의 이짝끗친 現實改化를 밝히 또 바로 지시하고 導化하는 사상이 그때의 중추 사상으로 권위와 세력을 가진 것이다.
槪念的의 主義니 경향이니 看板至上의 운동이니 方略이니 하든 일을 回顧하고는 넘어 과한 遊戱 기분을 애닯어하는 소리를 當年의 용사들에게 골고로 드를 것이다. 그만큼 지내보아도 不相容할 것가치 彼我가 대립하고 또 黨中에 입당하고 또 각개 스스로의 속에도 百千無量의 反撥分子를 지녀서 自己 統一부터 못하야 애쓰든 일이 실로 우스운 것을 깨다랏슬 것이다. 조선인에게는 유일한 大現實이 압을 꽉 막은 바에 ＜15＞ 누구든지, 무엇으로든지, 다 한가지, 그저 외골스로 위선 그 철벽을 깨터러지 아니하면 다른 모든 것이 虛幻空妄인 것을, 이것을 아니라 하야 서로 다토고 찟고 발기고 하든 일이 어떠케 우스운 지를 잘 알앗슬 것이다. 그남아 서로 소견이 아조 틀렷으면 어쩌리오 마는 실상은 동일한 현실을 동시에 對觸하는 바에 或前或後하야 보는 당면은 다를지 모르고 或深或淺하야 데미다보는 정도는 갓지 아니할지 모르되 아모에게고 아주 다른 對象觀과 아주 딴판의 經論策이 잇슬 수 업슴이 自然한 약속이어늘. 다만 或此或彼라하야 形喩하는 言辭가 다를 뿐으로 不共戴天할듯키 으르렁거리든 일을 들이보는 족족 입슐을 깸을 것이다.

턱업슴과 엉터리업슴과 주착업슴과 가늠업슴 등의 세력권은 불상하게도 축소될 거이다. 휑뎅그렁한 世界大勢論, 얼레빗살 가튼 人道正義論도 안색이 퍽 減損될 것이다. 어떠한 세계에서든지 제가 잘나고 제가 잘해야 제가 잘된다는 의식이 퍽 선명하게 만인의 중심에 보금자리 칠 것이다. 닐은바 新朝鮮이란 그 속으로서 비로서 胎盤을 지을 것이다. 그런데 그 第一表現―멘 몬처의 努力形式은 『무엇이든지 한아씩 만들자』로 나타날 것이다. 남의 세계에는 무슨 야단이 낫든지, 먼 압헤는 무슨 영상이 보이든지, 우리는 위선 『시방 여긔서 한 무엇이 되자』를 생각하고 또 힘 쓸 것이다. 밤낫 과도기라고 스스로 寬怒하는 중 밤낫 아모 것도 몽킈고 어울리는 것이 업는 어섧힌 상태로부터 차차 대단치 아니헌 것으로라도 무엇 하나식이 되고 그것이 계단이 되어 그 우에 또 다른 建造가 잇는 修理 잇는 국면을 보게 될 것이다. 甲申年도 過渡, 甲午年도 過渡, 이때 이것도 過渡, 저때 저것도 過渡하는 동안에 모든 것이 뭉그러지고 얼크터지든 일을 생각하고는 過渡自怒論은 완전히 그림자를 거둘 것이다.

예언자 아닌 사람의게 예언을 기대하는 것은 무슨 방면으로 보든지, 우서운 일이요. 또 예언자 아닌 자로서 예언을 해보라는 부탁에 응하는 것도 똑가티 무슨 방면으로 보든지, 무서운 일이라. 고로, 십 년 후의 조선 여성계가 엇지 되겟느냐는 물음에 대해서도, 자세히 알알수업다고 예언하는 것이 예언자 아닌 자의게 잇서서는 가장 정당한 예언일 줄 안다.

조선 여성계는 조선 사회에 의존하여 잇다. 그리고 또 조선 사회의 변천의 방향은 세계적 사회의 그것과 합류하여 잇다. 그러닛가 조선 여성계의 변천의 방향을 의논하려면, 우리의 시각은 爲先 세계 전반에 떠러지지 아니할 수 업다.
전 세계에 亙하야, 봉건적 모든 질서는 벌서 자본가적 신질서에 의하야 거진 완전히 정복되엇다. 그러나 자본적 현대 질서도, 벌서 界根점에 달하야 이제 바야흐로, 급속히 그보다 더 나흔 질서로 향하야 간다. 이것이 역사 진화의 必然要則에 따르는, 세계 경향이다. 그런데, 조선 사회는 봉건적 구질서를 미처 완전히 청산하지 못하엿슴에도 불구하고, 세계 대세에 합류하지 아니하지 못하게 되엇나니 조선 사회는 방금 비빔밥적 상태에 빠저 잇다.＜19＞ 그러나 그 『비빔밥』의 제요소는 다 각각 다른 역사적 과정을 대표한 자이니 봉건적 요소와 자본가적 요소는 다가티 다음 요소에 대하야 물러가는 요소에 불과하지마는 자본가적 요소는 봉건적 요소에 대하여는 도로혀 일보 전진의 가치를 가지고 잇는 것이다.
如斯한 의견에서 생각하면, 조선 여성계 금후 변천의 방향은 명백한 일이다. 조선 여성계를 아즉 강하게 구속하고 잇는 봉건적 鐵鎖는 점점 끈어지고 자본가적 個性主義가 점점 발달될 것이다. 그러나, 그것과 동시에, 무산 계급적 요소가 점점 우세를 가질 것이다.

그러면, 그러한 변천이 금후 십 년이면 얼마만한 정도에 달할 게이냐? 우리는 그것에 대하야 두 가지로 생각할 수 잇스니 第一은, 그 동안에 사회의 劃時期적 일대 변동이 잇슬 경우 第二는, 그것이 업시, 일상에 보는 보통 정도로 변천헐 경우이다. 만일 第一의 경우가 실현된다 하면 조선 여성계는 일반 사회와 한가지 面目一新해 질 것이다. 봉건적, 현대적 모든 불합리한 구속력은 벌서 위력을 일코 자매들은 진정한 새 자유의 건설에 奔忙할 것이다. 性의 의한 性의 설음, 계급의 의한 계급의 설음 등은 가속도로 사라질 것이다. 그러나 第二의 경우이면 십 년 후의 상태는 금일의 상태의 양적 확장에 불과할 것이다. 교육바든 자의 수가 는다든지 따라서 개성의 자유에 대하야 눈뜬 자의 수가 는다든지 또는 無産婦人의 의식이 발달된다든지, 따라서 사회주의적 부인 운동이 활기를 가지게 된다든지 등은 물론 상상할 수 잇는 일이지마는 그러나 기본적 상태에 잇서서는 금일과 별 질적 변동이 업슬 것이다.
그런데 금후 십 년 동안에 조선 사회가 일대 비약적 변동을 가질 수 잇슬가 업슬가에 대하여는 나는 몰으겟다.
