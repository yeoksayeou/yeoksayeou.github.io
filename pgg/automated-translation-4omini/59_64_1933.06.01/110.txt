기사제목	百萬圓이 생긴다면 우리는 어떠케 쓸가? (If We Had One Million Won, How Would We Spend It?)  
필자	B記者(調査)  
기사형태	설문 (Survey)  

If we had one million won, how would we spend it?  
B Reporter (Investigation)  
Anjamjaegi An-sin  
Reporter: If you had one million won, how would you spend it?  
An: How much is one million won?  
Reporter: One million won is how much? Then you know one hundred won, right?  
An: Yes, one hundred won is one hundred won. I know.  
Reporter: One hundred won multiplied by ten thousand is one million won.  
An: So then, that's quite a lot?  
(Anjamjaegi seems not very impressed. She merely understands that it's a lot but not precisely how much.)  
Reporter: Quite a lot... So how would you spend one million won if you had it?  
An: Goodness! How could such a large amount of money come about?  
Reporter: Ah, I mean if hypothetically you received it…  
An: Haha… Well… I suppose I would stop being a 잠재기 (my occupation).  
Reporter: Of course, that's true… But what would you buy?  
An: I should get some medicine for my sick child at home.  
Reporter: And?  
An: I would buy a phonograph. Hohoho…  

Tteok Saleswoman Old Lady Choi  
Choi: Would you like to buy some delicious rice cakes?  
Reporter: How much for one piece of rice cake?  
Choi: One jeon each… If you buy ten, I give you eleven…  
Reporter: How much do you earn in a day?  
Choi: What does it matter? I do this just to get by.  
Reporter: But if you even earn one penny, that’s something, isn’t it?  
Choi: I’d be lucky to earn that much a day.  
Reporter: If you had one million won, how would you spend it?  
Choi: What is one million won?  
Reporter: That’s money, you know. Money… If you received one million won, how would you spend it?  
Choi: How many is one million won?  
Reporter: It's one hundred won multiplied by ten thousand.  
Choi: Oh my… That's quite a lot! Where would such money come from?  
Reporter: Ah- I mean, hypothetically if it became yours.  
Choi: Stop talking nonsense! Just buy some rice cakes.  
Reporter: Well, I would buy the rice cakes… Just give me your answer.  
Choi: I would marry off my son.  
Reporter: You would still keep selling rice cakes?  
Choi: I would die without this job!  
Reporter: Even if you had one million won?  
Choi: One million won is as likely as rat droppings!

Ice Cream Seller O-bok  
O: Hey- Ice cream—  
Reporter: How about ice cream—  
O: Yes- how much do you want?  
Reporter: I probably wouldn’t eat it anyway…  
O: I beg your pardon… Would you like ten jeon?  
Reporter: If you got one million won, what would you spend it on?  
O: One million won? If one million won…  
Reporter: That would be ten thousand one-won notes, you see.  
O: Ah… Where would such money come from?  
Reporter: Ah- I mean if you were to receive it.  
O: If I had it, I would spend it all.  
Reporter: Well then, what would you buy first?  
O: I would buy a proper ice cream machine.  
Reporter: And?  
O: Ah- No, I would probably stop being an ice cream seller.  
Reporter: After that?  
O: I would buy a store.  
Reporter: Why?  
O: So that I wouldn’t starve when I have no money.  
Reporter: Next?  
O: I would pay for my utilities.  
Reporter: More?  
O: I would build a house.  
Reporter: And more?  
O: Hehe… I would like to buy a suit like yours and sell some ice cream!  
Reporter: Am I being sold?  
O: I’m nineteen.  
Reporter: Would you secretly spend your wealth if you got one million won?  
O: Hehe… I would hope so.

Fish Seller Mr. Oh  
Reporter: Excuse me, Mr. Fish Seller.  
Oh: Yes?  
Reporter: If you had one million won, how would you spend it?  
Oh: One million won? Haha… Please, don’t speak nonsense and sell some fish! They’re jumping fresh.  
Reporter: I intend to buy fish… But wouldn’t you entertain the thoughts of spending one million won? Do you know how much that is?  
Oh: Haha, of course, I do.  
(Despite his declarations, he still does not have a grasp of what one million won means.)  
Reporter: Then… What would you buy first?  
Oh: I am currently considering buying a fish stall near Jeonggogae.  
Reporter: So you’re saying you want to be the owner of a fish stall?  
Oh: Yes.  
Reporter: With one million won, what else would you do?  
Oh: That’s an immense amount when you think about it. I could make over a hundred won daily.  
Reporter: After that?  
Oh: Then I would gather our local men and treat them to a round of drinks.  
Reporter: And?  
Oh: I would then spend it randomly.

Boat Vendor Mr. Kim  
(He is a boat vendor from Wangsimni. He was born into a long-established family of boat vendors.)  
Reporter: If you were to bring in a load right now, how much would be left over?  
Kim: I’d be lucky to earn thirty to forty jeon.  
Reporter: How do you feel if you suddenly came into a million won?  
Kim: One million? You mean money?  
Reporter: Yes, if you had ten thousand one-won notes right in your hand.  
Kim: Ten thousand one-won notes? Oh- that’s a lot.  
Reporter: Yes.  
Kim: Hehe… Who would give me such a huge sum?  
Reporter: I mean if hypothetically it were to come to you…  
Kim: I would spend it.  
Reporter: Well, how would you spend one million won to begin with?  
Kim: There would be many uses for one million won. I could buy a car… And then…  
Reporter: And?  
Kim: Well… Hehe… I suppose I would see what else I could do.

Rickshaw Man Mr. Lee  
Reporter: What would you do if one million won came into your hands?  
Lee: Huh? Such a sudden question has perplexed me.  
(He seems confused about what this means.)  
Reporter: I mean money, you know. If you had ten thousand one-won notes right now, how would you spend it?  
Lee: (He stares blankly. It seems he doubts whether he has just come out from the Governor-General’s Hospital with such a question.) Where would such money come from?  
Reporter: I mean, if it were to come…  
Lee: What kind of fate would permit me such money?  
Reporter: If, hypothetically, that were to happen…  
Lee: I would spend it significantly.  
Reporter: How?  
Lee: I would be able to, yes.  
Reporter: What would you spend it on first?  
Lee: I would buy all the cars in Seoul.  
Reporter: (Feigning surprise) Why?  
Lee: Because they take away the profits from me.  
Reporter: But with one million won, you wouldn’t need to pull a rickshaw anymore, right?  
Lee: That’s a fair point.  
Reporter: What else would you do?  
Lee: I would buy rickshaws for all my friends.  
Reporter: And?  
Lee: I would have a harem and drink and enjoy life.  
  
Bag Seller Mr. Moon  
Reporter: Excuse me, where did you go?  
Moon: Yes. I went out after breakfast.  
Reporter: How many family members do you have?  
Moon: There are three of us.  
Reporter: Do you all support yourselves by this?  
Moon: Yes.  
Reporter: Now, if you had one million won, how would you spend it?  
Moon: Huh? What is one million won?  
Reporter: Money, you know… If one million won came to you…  
Moon: Oh dear! How could money appear so suddenly!  
Reporter: I mean if it came to you, hypothetically…  
Moon: Hehe, one… Well, how could one million be spent?  
Reporter: You would need to spend some, wouldn’t you?  
Moon: If so, then… Hmm… I suppose I would get some pots for the kitchen.  
Reporter: Why is that?  
Moon: Hehe… It’s not easy. My old back is getting worse carrying water morning and evening.  
Reporter: But even if you had one million won, wouldn’t you still keep selling bags?  
Moon: Hehe. Even so, I would also think about getting summer clothes for my wife—perhaps something in synthetic silk.  
Reporter: What is your ultimate dream?  
Moon: What would that be? I would simply take the car back home to farm and eat.

**Summary**  

This article presents a survey where various individuals give their opinions on how they would spend one million won. The responses reflect a mix of skepticism and aspiration, often highlighting everyday needs or simple desires. Several respondents expressed disbelief in the notion of receiving such a sum, demonstrating the harsh economic realities of the time. The article gives historians interesting insights into the socio-economic conditions during Korea's colonial period, showing the gap between wealthy aspirations and the grim realities faced by ordinary citizens.
