**Summary**  

This article presents a collection of foreign comic strips that humorously depict various situations involving men and women, often highlighting societal norms and human folly. The humor of the comics reflects cultural attitudes and illustrates the interaction between everyday life and comedic elements that were likely appealing to the readers of the time. Historians might find interest in the portrayal of gender roles and societal dynamics typical of the 1930s, as well as the influence of Western comic styles on Korean entertainment during the colonial period.
