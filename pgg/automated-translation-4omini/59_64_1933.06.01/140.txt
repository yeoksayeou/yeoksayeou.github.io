기사제목	잡어먹고 십흔 이야기(2) (Stories of Eating Junk)  
필자	浩然堂人  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

잡어먹고 십흔 이야기  
浩然堂人  
인물 - 안해, 남편, 갓난애기  

Wife:  Honey.  
Husband:  Yes.  
Wife:  Can you stop doing this?  
Husband:  Yes, yes.  
Wife:  Come on, get up. I can't take it anymore with that smell of liquor until I pass out.  
Husband:  (snoring)  
Wife:  Where did you go and what were you doing that your suit is in this state? You didn't even take it off, just collapsed here… Hey.  
Husband:  (snoring)  
Wife:  Seriously, honey.  
Husband:  Yes, yes.  
Wife:  You really need to get up.  
Husband:  Just let me sleep a little longer...  
Baby:  Waaaaah! Waaaaah!  
Wife:  ………  
Husband:  Yes, yes.  
Baby:  Waaaaah! Waaaaah!  
Husband:  Why is the baby crying before breakfast? You can't let me sleep in peace!?  
Wife:  What's breakfast?  
Husband:  Enough with the talk, just go do your work. Get some soup ready.  
Wife:  Really! There’s no breakfast at all… What about the wage you were supposed to bring home?  
Husband:  If there’s nothing for breakfast, why didn’t you say so earlier?  
Wife:  Didn’t I tell you? I said there was no dinner yesterday when you left in the morning.  
Husband:  I didn’t hear that...  
Wife:  Oh my, what do I do… You eat well outside but I didn’t even get dinner yesterday… I should be producing milk! Can’t you see how the baby is crying from hunger?  
Baby:  Waaaaah! Waaaaah!  
Husband:  Ugh.  
Wife:  How are you going to go to work today looking like this?  
Husband:  Don’t worry.  
Wife:  I’m not worried, just give me the wage you brought home... Really, I’m going to starve with the baby…  
Husband:  Just take everything.  
Wife:  How much is this?  
Husband:  How would I know how much it is!  
Wife:  You spent 60 won, and you’re only leaving me this little?  
Husband:  If there’s a place to spend it, then that’s how it is.  
Wife:  So what am I supposed to do with 1 won and a few dimes for a month’s living? What about the rent?  
Husband:  Just postpone it for a bit.  
Wife:  How can I postpone the rent?  
Husband:  Just postpone it for a few days.  
Wife:  The rice payment is already two months overdue.  
Husband:  I told you I’ll manage it in a few days.  
Wife:  The gas bill has gone up to 18 won, and now I can’t even buy vegetables.  
Husband:  Then don’t eat.  
Wife:  I have to pay the interest too.  
Husband:  You won't starve for a month.  
Wife:  The tailor will now send everything to the house.  
Husband:  Send it to the company.  
Wife:  What will you do since you have no clothes?  
Husband:  Don’t worry about that.  
Wife:  I have nothing left to wear, what should I do?  
Husband:  Take that and wear it.  
Wife:  What am I supposed to buy breakfast with!?  
Husband:  Just buy some seolleongtang (설렁탕) to eat.  
Wife:  Who’s going to give that to you for free if you don’t pay?  
Husband:  Then I guess you’ll just have to starve.  
Wife:  Aren’t you hungry too?  
Husband:  Isn’t there anyone who eats and isn’t hungry!  
Wife:  What did you leave me this for? Should I use it all up?  
Husband:  Give it here... go and get some food.  
Wife:  Oh my!  

**Summary**  

This piece depicts a typical morning in a struggling household during the 1930s in Korea, highlighting the tension and everyday struggles of a family dealing with financial hardship and the responsibilities of parenthood. The husband is depicted as indifferent to the dire situation, while the wife is acutely aware of their precarious circumstances. The dialogue reflects the broader socio-economic challenges faced by families during colonial times, exposing issues of poverty, labor struggles, and gender roles in managing household finances. Historians might find this portrayal revealing about domestic life under colonial rule, reflecting how economic pressures affected family dynamics.
