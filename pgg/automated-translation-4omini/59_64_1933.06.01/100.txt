百萬圓이 생긴다면 우리는 어떠케 쓸가?, 그들의 엉뚱한 리상  
A記者(調査)  
Human-powered vehicle worker Song U-ryang  

One million won? Oh dear! That's money we can't even imagine. But you know, money itself isn't burdensome. If you spend, the limit is endless, and if you don't spend, you can easily let it be.  

First of all, we need to save the women who live worse than livestock. Even if they have a house, they can't live without worrying about their means of living. If someone were to say that I was gambling the night before, who would care?  

Living without being criticized or looked down upon is the best thing. If you can send your children to school and have them educated, they will go into society and in time become gentlemen with knowledge and dignity, right?  

And if you are really obliged to spend some money, it’s easy. What can't you do with money? Am I supposed to take a night trip and waste that money on some gisaeng (female entertainers)?  

Look at these folks. No matter how we see them from wherever, we still can't afford to be looked down upon. But they still do despicable things! They act like bourgeois to those who have even a bit of money, while treating us with derogatory remarks like,  

“Hey there!”  
“Look here!”  
“Go slowly. Hurry up!”

They throw around their educated words. So sometimes I hear,  
“What kind of dialect are you speaking?”  
And only then do they realize,  
“Oh dear, there are such things as this and that…”  
How cunning!  

I'm really quite embarrassed to be associated with them. How could I face that ridiculous woman? And is one million won something that ghosts would eat? Just picking a pretty girl, enjoying delicious food, and spending a night is enough, right?  
“Human-powered vehicle!”  
Yes— I need to go now.  

Bank employee Jo Gyu-sik  

One million won? If I had a million won, it would be exactly the same as our bank's capital. We could establish a large company (of course I'd be an executive), and just by keeping it quiet and collecting interest, I would be rich enough to burst!  

What would I do? One million won! One million won!  
Oh dear, I would be satisfied living off that money quietly. When I think of all the troubles of my current salary, I can't even think about how much I would have left after every expense. Today is payday, so I have no idea how much will come out after my various payments.  

I still have my three-month installment on my tailored suit left! And summer is almost here, I need a new straw hat! As a salaried person, I have no chance to avoid these problems!  

You will see, but the situation of being pursued by the hat salespeople is something I've carried on for the last three years. Each payday, I am filled with dread!  

If I were to save a few won, at home, it's all about my wife! And I would eventually just want to hand over my entire salary in one go. The moment I hand over the paycheck, I would also want to capture my wife's facial expression in a photograph!  

Freelancer Kim Gyeong-seok  

What is this one million won? How much money is that? Is it ten thousand at a time making one hundred? Or is it one hundred making ten thousand? Oh dear!  

What can folks like us do if we had that money? We would go mad. Even having one ten-thousandth of it would be something to be grateful for.  

If money were abundant, even we uneducated and ignorant folks could run one business or another. The trouble is, my hunger is so severe that eating is a daunting task, and I can't even talk about it!  

How often do we encounter such facility issues as toilets? How could we go inside the wealthy’s homes? No, when it’s raining or storming, we have to tread ten li just to find a public toilet! So, what can we do?  

Just imagine if we had ten thousand or a hundred or a million!  

Do you see this?  

We'd need a public toilet built at Jongno! How about that?  

Waitress Baek Ae-young  

What? In this ecomomic downturn, are there people who give such big tips? Ho ho ho! We simply live off the tips we receive!  

But if we had one million won, we would stop being a waitress and living off tips. What would I spend it on?  

First, I would build a cultural residence. Somewhere outside of town, I'd lay out good roads with a private car!  

I would buy a piano, make a nice yard, and install a radio… Oh! I would make a dance hall too!  

When my friends come at night, we could go for a drive in the car. We could dine at restaurants, go shopping at department stores, and visit beauty salons.  

What is this dress I’m wearing? These shoes? Of course, I would buy the most luxurious jewels too!  

Summer is coming. Ah, I will call all my friends over for a trip to the beach. We will frolic like the Western women in movies, spending days at sea on a yacht.  

Having a great meal and a drink, we would enjoy ourselves leisurely.  

Why, I could even think about marriage!  

Oh goodness! Only a handsome man, not someone who slips on a banana peel, would do. Someone good-looking, knowledgeable, kind-hearted, and lively who loves me deeply. That kind of man is ultimately the one I would engage in marriage with. Then we would take a grand honeymoon around the world!  

How about that? This is how I would spend one million won.  

Wealthy person Min Ho-kyu  

What? One million won comes for free? Even a joke can’t get more absurd than that. Until now, I have crawled to save up this little money, presumably because I have been thrifty and careful—and yet, how is it supposed to seem plausible that one would just stumble into one million won?  

What do I need that money for? What would I even spend it on? 

If I had one million won or even just a hundred or a single coin, no one would frivolously spend even that!  

It is absurd to think that I would freely squander a valuable amount of money like one million won if it just came out of nowhere. Even if it did come, I’d preserve it well.  

With my current money and a million more, wouldn’t I finally sound like a wealthy person just once in my life?  

Student Jo Geum-yeong  

Just previously, I saw one of my former classmates investing twenty thousand won into running a café. That person, like many of our peers attending technical schools, can be found easily today.  

If one is to buy a book, they can refrain but never hesitate to buy alcohol. Some even tend to prioritize drinking over their studies for months.  

We can see business owners behaving this way too.  

We are young individuals in development; our future is incredibly bright. It seems incomprehensible that such youths use their paternal inheritance for superficial pursuits.  

When confronted with the question of accumulating wealth, they may be indulging in activities detrimental to society.  

Even if such youth, who frivolously partake in drinking or visiting brothels, were to have one hundred thousand won, what good would come of it?  

If I were to have one million won, I’d rather help my unfortunate peers who have the potential but are suffering from poverty!  

I would provide them with school fees so they could graduate and even go abroad for further studies! It would greatly contribute to our achievement of a cultural standard in the future!  

**Summary**  

This article presents a survey of various individuals sharing their thoughts on how they would spend a hypothetical amount of one million won. Ranging from dreams of providing for their families and helping others to aspirations of extravagant lifestyles, the responses reflect the distinct socio-economic realities and challenges of the time, particularly under colonial rule. It highlights the stark differences in perspectives based on societal roles, from laborers and waitstaff to bankers and affluent individuals. Historians might find this revealing of the gap in experiences and aspirations shaped by the socio-political landscape in Korea during the colonial period.

당정 어떠케 쓰겟느냐고요? 오날이 마침 월급날인데 머니머니 모다 제하고 몃푼이나 남어서 나올른지 몰으겟습니다. 생각하면 이러케 안저서 백만 원이 생기면 어떠케 쓰겟다고 팔자느러진 시간을 보내고 잇슬 형편이 못됩니다.＜25＞

요전번 나의 보통학교적 동창생 하나이 돈 이만원을 드려서 카페경영하는 것을 보앗습니다. 그 사람 뿐 아니라 현재 우리가 소위 전문학교를 가티 다니며 가튼 공부를 연구하고 잇는 그들 동무 속에서도 전자와 가튼 사람을 만히 발견합니다.
책 한 권 사자면 안사도 술 한잔 내라면 곳잘 내놋습니다. 심한 사람은 학교를 멀하러 다니며 공부를 어떠케 하는지는 몰라도 일년 열두달을 가야 술은 잘먹고 놀러는 날마다 다니지마는 책은 한 권을 사는 것을 못보앗습니다.
카페를 경영하는 놈도 그러쵸. 우리는 지금 한참 발전해 나가는 청춘이며 우리의 압길이야말로 구만리장천 갓지가 안습니까? 그러한 젊은 놈이 할 것이 업서서 애비에게 물려가진 돈을 가지고 그 따위 것을 하고 잡바젓서야 합니까? 사회를 위하야 좀더 자긔사업으로 잡고 나갈만한 것이 업슬가요?
돈을 몬다고요? 사회를 해치는 그러한 것을 해서까지 잇는 돈도 만흔대 더 모아 무엇하며 또 그까짓 쓸데업는 놈들이 돈을 모면 나종에 무엇에다 쓰겟습니까? 소용업는 노릇입니다. 또 젊은 놈이 술과 게집의 아양이 조와서 허영적으로 그러한 것을 하고 잇다면은 그놈은 저능아(低能兒)일 것에 틀림이 업스니까 돈을 모기는 커녕 잇는 것도 모다 업새고 말 것입니다.
만일 나에게 백만 원이라는 큰 돈이 잇다면 이런 재조잇고 열성이 잇스며 총명하되 돈업서 그대로 석어버리는 불행한 동무들에게 밧치겟습니다.
그들에게 학비를 주어 학교를 졸업해 나오게 하고 외국으로 더 나아가 연구를 싸케하고 오날날의 우리가 세게의 문화수준(文化水準)에 도달할랴는 데 큰 도움이 잇는 것을 보겟습니다.＜29＞
