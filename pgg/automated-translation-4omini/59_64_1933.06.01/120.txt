連作漫談, 女子의 一生  
朴愚石  
After Mudeon-yi got a job as a saleswoman at XX department store, she became not just a mere saleswoman. Beautiful Mudeon, charming Mudeon, stylish Mudeon, clever Mudeon, and kind-hearted Mudeon—there’s nothing wrong with our Mudeon who, in just less than a month, gained not only the trust and favor of the executives at the department store but also the affection of hundreds of fellow employees. Hence, within three months, the charm and energy from within resonated throughout our Mudeon-yi, making it seem as if the whole of Seoul was ignited by the focus of a fire mirror on a summer day. But it wasn't just Seoul. From the day Mudeon entered XX department store, every newspaper in Seoul began to feature her with photos, and the XX newspaper published a lengthy headline, "Our Mudeon-yi, the beauty of Korea, until she became a saleswoman at XX department store," serialized alongside stories for thirty days. The attention she garnered was boundless, not only in Korea but also in cities like Berlin, Paris, London, and New York. Our pride, Mudeon, was recognized globally, and in the recent global beauty contest, she outshone the famed Orion of Persia by 6 votes, winning the title of the most beautiful woman in the world, garnering praise from the director of the World Beauty Research Institute. 

However, one regret lingered—because of this "global" fame, our humble Mudeon, who had been held high in esteem and maintained her modesty until then, was unknowingly driven to vanity, which stifled her pride and transformed her character. It's no wonder that, after serving only half a year as a saleswoman with a monthly salary of 60 won, she started feeling that this was insufficient. But dear friends, is it true that Mudeon has fallen? Absolutely not! Even if every woman in the world were to fall down the wrong path, our Mudeon is like a lone lily blooming in a garden, untouched by weeds. Such is her upbringing and innate character that even a little ego could never overshadow her constitution—she possesses a strong will and clear reasoning. 

Yet in her heart, there has blossomed the flower of youth, and on her face, indeed, on her chest, her entire body could not resist the invading spring breeze, which is inevitable. For quite some time, she has been receiving dozens of love letters every day from hundreds, if not thousands, of men. It has been half a year of spring for Mudeon, the season of love at its peak. Up until now, she had anxiously pondered over a total of 126,578 letters, and if one were to convert them into postage, it amounts to about 5,063 won and 12 jeon, just from the average of a third of the friends who sent letters by parcel mail. 

However, Mudeon-yi would never be swayed by superficial love letters drenched in cheap perfume. Yet, her heart wasn't hardened; no, not at all. The flood of love letters that she tossed into her mailbox every day began to choke her heart with a hidden suffering and unrest that she could not express, for there was one man among them she genuinely loved. After concluding dinner with her mother, who did not understand her feelings, Mudeon found herself caught in a nagging discussion from her mother.

"Mudeon, until now, you've been doing as you please, and I haven’t interfered with your affairs. However, at the tender age of twenty-one, it's time for you to get married and create a household, living comfortably without envy. Times have changed, even if we still consider ourselves to be of the same lineage that has diminished, but surely this isn't a time to just be lost in your whims. Nowadays, getting married is more difficult than ever, but with your charm being widely recognized, finding a suitor for you would be easier than picking from a marketplace. So go ahead and choose a husband you like and start a family; please consider this, my dear." 

As her mother continued with her lengthy speech, she casually puffed on a cigarette, swallowing her saliva while glancing at Mudeon, who was deep in thought, pondering her response. The more she listened, the more Mudeon's heart felt heavy and restless. Her mother’s words made her realize her own privilege and desire for comfort in the future, yet her heart burned with anxiety. How should she respond? She waited so long that she found herself rubbing her fingers on the floor.

Finally, feeling overwhelmed by her mother's pleading gaze, she uttered, "I don’t want to marry a rich or noble man at all. I simply refuse to marry." This outburst was not a deliberate decision but rather a spur-of-the-moment expression from her troubled heart. However, immediately regretting her outburst, she felt an inexplicable amount of pressure, as if a resolution was being suffocated inside her. Yet, her oblivious mother continued calmly as if the weight of Mudeon’s words was not a surprise. After all, in her mother's straightforward and inexperienced thoughts, she just could not conceive the notion that her daughter could feel such deep marital reluctance. 

As the night went on, Mudeon found herself unable to suppress her sorrows, moving to her room to bury her head under the covers, crying quietly through the night. She felt both pity for her unaware mother and sadness for herself. More than that, she felt her situation was too tragic and overwhelming, leaving no room for new thoughts. It was then that there was the man K, who was the eldest son of a strict family. 

By some twist of fate, Mudeon and K fell in love, constructing a bond that seemed to tie them together deeply, promising, "If fate has led us here, let us swear a pledge together." When Mudeon loved K, it was never for money, and when K loved Mudeon, it was for her character, not her beauty. K was indeed a sincere young man, a man of strength. 

"What would your father do if he opposed our marriage?" 

"By no means! My father is quite resolute and strict, but as I’m his only son, there’s no way he could refuse me outright. However, if he doesn’t permit it, perhaps I will have to struggle and suffer like a field mouse." 

"I never loved you for your wealth. I respect you as a person and I love you for that alone. What does it matter if we have to live on just cold water? If it's together with you, I would not hesitate." 

This ironclad promise and unwavering sentiment made them commit to each other deeply. While the world continued to remain unknowing, Mudeon was not her usual self. Nonetheless, from that point, she felt she had greater hopes and convictions, merely waiting with a tense heart for the day she would walk into the wedding hall as K's bride. In the dim light of the moonlit night, she imagined her future home, whether that be a villa or a humble cottage, dreaming of marital bliss. But alas, all those promises and dreams withered away, vanishing as mere dreams, leaving one to wonder about the cruelty of fate and the harshness of life. But whose fault was this? It was neither K’s fault nor Mudeon’s fault; the outcome lay solely with K’s stern parental influence and his penetrative yet fragile will. Yet Mudeon did not blame K. 

Unfortunately, her heart shrank painfully, overwhelming her, and imparting an almost nihilistic essence to her life. This was Mudeon’s first love—a long-lasting tragedy that would mark her as the protagonist in her journey as a woman. <31>  

**Summary**  

This serialized article delves into the life of a young woman named Mudeon as she navigates the expectations surrounding love and marriage in early 20th century Korea. Though initially celebrated for her beauty and charm, she finds herself torn between societal pressures and her own desires, reflecting a deep conflict between traditional expectations and individual longing. Historians may find this narrative revealing in its portrayal of the struggles faced by women in colonial Korea, highlighting issues of autonomy and identity during a period of significant social transition.
