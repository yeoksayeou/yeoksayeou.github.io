기사형태	대담·좌담 (Interview and Discussion)  

各界男女 逢變 誌上 座談會

Out of appreciation for your presence here, I would like to thank you for attending despite the potential inconvenience. If you brought any cigarettes, feel free to smoke them. Initially, I planned to host this discussion at a place like Myongwolgwan, but this gathering has turned somewhat awkward. Most of you enjoy drinking, and if we were to serve you drinks here and it ends up with people drunkenly misbehaving, it would be embarrassing. So, I have invited you through this manuscript. 

Now, let us stop with the pleasantries and get to the point. Please share any embarrassing or amusing stories you have, one by one. If someone particularly excels, perhaps we might reward you with a drink later when you leave.

卞: Oh, I don't drink.

徐: Then I will take the lead. A man who can’t drink isn’t much of a man!

廉, 車, 李: (in unison) Yes!

尹(聖), 許, 姜: There’s no such thing as that!

薛: When I see someone drinking, it doesn’t sit right with me…

双S: Oh, why the discord? I was thinking of maybe bringing some makgeolli if we got into talking about drinking, but not at all! This isn’t the time for such things… So let’s start with you, Seon-chun, and please tell a story.

徐: Sure, I will. But I ask that if I receive a gift for this story, it must be on my end… It’s been quite some time since I drank. 

I once drank a lot. I got quite drunk almost three years ago. 

I had been at the Asia Café in Insadong. While there, I asked the waiter to call a taxi for me, but I didn’t end up going home in it. 

許: How frustrating!

徐: Honestly, listen to this. When I got home, I wanted to pay the fare, but I had no money... So, as a last resort, I gave my business card, but they didn’t accept it.

薛: Is your business card that prominent?

徐: Just listen… The driver insisted that he wouldn’t return my card until I was taken home, so he brought me to the police station. I got into an argument at the police station… In the midst of all that commotion, I ended up urinating inside the police station… What an embarrassing moment!

一同: (laughing heartily. How embarrassing!)

徐: But here’s the kicker… The officer asked why the driver hadn’t taken me home and when I showed him my wallet, it was empty, and he suddenly changed his attitude and told the driver that I was a member of the Dong-a Ilbo and that I was not someone who couldn’t afford a fare of 1 won. Hence, he commanded him to take me home immediately.

一同: (laughing heartily.)

卞: I also have a story about getting drunk and embarrassed. Nowadays, even looking at a liquor company is a reminder of the good times, but years ago, there was a person known for getting notoriously drunk, Byun Yeong-ro. One day, I went out with a friend and we ended up drinking all night long till I was completely beside myself! 

徐: What a shocker!

卞: No, no… It seems I crawled under a mosquito net to try and sleep!

徐: A truly absurd situation!

卞: I must have been bitten by mosquitoes while trying to sleep. From that incident onwards, I've tried to hold back on drinking. 

徐: What a weakling! If that was all you had, how could you call yourself a man?

双S: Yes, let’s hear about Seol-ui-sik's story.

薛: What can I say? Since I don’t drink, it might be hard to share a fun story… But perhaps I can recount a time when I faced embarrassment after going out to Yeongdosan. 

That was, you see, after the return of Li Wang and the day following Seongbok. We had all been staying up through the night preparing a special bulletin...

徐: Was that when you were working as the head of the social section?

薛: Yes… back then, though there weren’t many of us, I ended up going to Yeongdosan with Kim Yong-bo, Chae Man-sik, Park Man-seo, and a couple of entertainers… We drank and sang… Well, we were in the middle of some fun when some guy came in, looking quite nasty and ordered a bowl of cold water and left. 

Initially, we thought it was nothing, but it quickly spiraled out when we decided to leave this specific bar at Yeongdosan where we had some fun with the entertainers. 

While looking back, I suddenly spotted about twenty of them standing there! We were only five or six! I tried to step in and de-escalate, but before I knew it, someone slapped me out of nowhere!

Honestly, I felt like I could just about die! I was livid, but without thinking of the aftermath, I ended up crying.

徐: You seem to enjoy crying in such situations!

李: That reminds me of a time when Cha Sang-chan got into a drunken scuffle.

徐: How so? 

李: Once while walking in Gwancheoldong, he drunkenly bumped into someone. The guy was furious and looked ready to throw down, but Cha was so quick-witted and said, "Excuse me, I’m inebriated! Don’t take it personally." That the other guy found it amusing and just walked away. 

尹(聖): So he is indeed quite the quick thinker... If you just ease their anger, you can usually sidestep trouble. My, oh my!

車: What’s all this quick thinking, though! When one suddenly faces an unavoidable embarrassment, there’s no way out. I received quite the tongue-lashing from some engineering students for an article I wrote in Byeolgeongon.

李: Did you get physically attacked or just verbally?

車: Mostly verbal, but still, it felt like harassment. They just crowded around, lobbing insults without fact-checking and even pushing me around shortly afterwards… What a humiliation!

徐: During that time, if you had just fought back a little, maybe the embarrassment wouldn't have stuck as bad. 

車: Perhaps, but who knows how things might have played out at the courthouse after? 

許: Why not share your story from Dongdaemun Police Station?

尹(聖): But that’s something people all know well… On that occasion, you see, a rather brash young man came up to me, trying to propose marriage in the middle of the afternoon! As far as embarrassing moments go, that takes the cake! Eventually, I took him to Dongdaemun Police Station. Later, I heard he had graduated from university in Japan. 

許: Yes, it even made the newspapers.

尹(聖): The newspaper reported about a "certain man" harassing a lady on the street. 

車: Climbing onto the stage, calling the lady that – that’s quite bold of you!

尹(聖): Why are you bringing others into this?

車: I didn’t drag anyone in! 

許: I suppose whether it's being humiliated or however else, I must confess, I have been through quite an ordeal. When I was setting up my new practice in Dongju-dong, over time visitors started coming for treatment, but I tell you back then, it was challenging to find women specifically in Korea. 

徐: Of course, you would draw the attention like a beacon! 

許: Well, one night, someone knocked on the door late. Pressed, we needed to arrive to an immediate request for care. So, following the guide, I hopped on my horse carriage without a second thought, ending up at a rich homeowner’s place. 

There, I was greeted by the owner’s son, who welcomed me as the patient was a woman and was expected to be taken to care, so while I was waiting, I was asked about my progress. After that, the man tried to hold my wrist!

Honestly—how I managed to escape that incident? I probably aged ten years from fright! 

徐: So then, you think you can’t live to see ninety?

허: What do you mean no one lives beyond a hundred?

徐: But then, there’s yet another incident you might recall. When XXX was new in Tokyo, he once rushed into a shop on Jongno…

허: It’s about XXX? Let's not discuss this. 

廉: I’ve had international humiliation when I was drunk. 

卞: Humiliation certainly pairs with odd stories. 

廉: I ended up at the British Consulate, utterly plastered. When I got there, I banged on the locked door shouting, and ultimately a servant came out. I just wanted a glass of water. My approach made no sense to them, and they asked me if I knew where I was! I couldn’t believe I shouted out it was the British Consulate!

一同: (laughing heartily.)

尹(益): Although it happened quite a while ago, I took my wife out for a stroll in Cheongnyangni, and I got quite the embarrassment. It was common practice back then, but there were still folks who looked down on couples walking together – they’d assume you were just a couple of good-for-nothings. Amazingly unaware of the finer points of the time, we happened to pass a group of thugs who started hurling insults at us, and it felt genuinely disheartening, leaving us feeling ridiculed. 

李: I’ll share my mishap: one rather unpleasant story. 

車: A stink to that one! 

李: No, it’s in the past. 

徐: How rude! Imagine showing so uncouthly at first meeting, but do you mean you literally can’t keep your bowels together? 

李: It happened so quickly! 

徐: Oh dear! 

李: I was minding my own business but when nature called, the little one couldn’t hold it. So, I hurried off to the restroom… I was in there when the other one came right along, timing to outdo the big fellow instead... 

一同: (laughing heartily.) 

李: And what was I supposed to do about that? I exited to find guests lingering nearby. Moreover, Kim Myung-soon, upon arriving, offered me a seat at the gathering. The odor was overwhelming with nobody making the shift to change positions for a minute, which surely, made for a dilemma! 

車: Your embarrassing tale now seems fun, but have you heard of Lidu Seong, a former publisher from Gaebyeok during the summer who found himself in a tough spot on the Han River, returning from a boat ride? One summer, he had an accident and ended up on a tram ride, releasing right there! He was in a panic while realizing what it meant when he caught sight of blood all over his attire… When he looked, it was supposedly water pooling from his mouth, suddenly spilling. 

姜: Now, I’ll share an embarrassing story for a change about humiliating someone else. 

徐: That’s different. 

姜: There was a student who kept sending letters, acting very coy and befriending others, and one time we bumped into one another. Out of the blue, he cheerfully greeted me and nonsensically chatted. We kept walking straight on, while the lady behind just kept mildly apologizing without losing her composure. Though I carried out my duties in silence, before making a sudden move, I threw a bucket of water over his head! 

一同: (laughing heartily.) 

双S: Well then, as I realize I can no longer follow the nonsensical tales, we’ll wrap things up here. So, as we part, why not treat yourselves to a drink of makgeolli as a parting gift!

  

**Summary**

This article presents a lively and humorous discussion among various individuals who share their embarrassing experiences, mostly related to drinking incidents and social faux pas. The gathering shows camaraderie among intellectuals in colonial Korea, emphasizing both the norms and pressures of social behaviors, especially around drinking, which was common among men during this period while highlighting the frequency of public grievances and misadventures. Historians might find the laid-back yet revealing nature of these anecdotes to illustrate the social fabric of Korea during colonization—the mix of humor and shame in lived experiences serves as a cultural lens into Korean societal pressures.
