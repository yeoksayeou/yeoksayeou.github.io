非常時  
A: "I dropped my gun while caught on a tree stump, and at that moment, two big tigers came charging in from either side. Before I could grab my gun, I had to catch one tiger with each hand..."  

B: "Wait a minute... doesn't that mean you were losing an arm before you went to deal with those ferocious beasts?"  

A: "Yes, that's right... but in such an emergency, do you have the luxury to think about losing an arm? I just grabbed the beasts, one on each side, and threw them away!"  

  

**Summary**  

The article discusses an exaggerated hypothetical scenario in which a person, under extreme pressure, must deal with dangerous situations without concern for personal loss. The dialogue emphasizes the urgency and instinctive reactions individuals may face during emergencies. This reflects on human psychology, showcasing how crises force individuals to act rather than deliberate. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, such narratives could suggest a critique of the oppressive circumstances under which people lived, possibly alluding to the struggles faced by Koreans during the Japanese colonial period.
