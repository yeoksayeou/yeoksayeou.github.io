기사제목	出發 (Departure)  
필자	金華山 (Kim Hwa-san)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Departure  
						Kim Hwa-san  

Fierce wind.........  
Snow............  

Oh, sharp wind. As if enraged, fiercely blow upon me, upon this city, upon this land.

Snow. Pure white snow. Gently, gently come upon me, upon this city, upon this land.

Your face is red like a young calf's in the cruel, bitter cold.  

Your hands are cold like ice. But your eyes shine like black pearls.  

Oh, you are as white as snow.  
You are as fierce as the wind.  

Your hair flutters like a frayed string.  
From your cheeks, white snow falls in uncountable flakes.  

You weep thinking of our comrades,   
Those comrades who, with thin blankets,  
Barefoot, unable to sleep, shiver through the cold that penetrates to their bones!  

Oh, how many countless comrades are sacrificed in this cold?  
How many countless comrades are to become invalids in this cold?  

But my person. Overcome this sadness.  
This harsh wind and the swirling snow awaken our sleeping souls.  
They give strength to our fatigued veins.  

Our predecessors marched onward overcoming this storm and bitter cold.  
Their iron will. Their combined strength. Their burning passion!  

Oh, my person.  
In the footsteps they walked!  
Underneath their waving flags!  
A brave departure must happen.  

It is now winter. The sharp wind shouts like a cry.  
Oh, fierce person. Blow with all your might. Fiercely blow upon me, upon this city.  
Snow. Pure white snow. Snow. Gently, gently come upon me, upon this city, upon this land.

Oh, charge forward!  
Charge forward!  

Amidst the wind and snow, your face is truly beautiful.  
-1931.1.15-  

**Summary**  

This poem captures the spirit of resilience and camaraderie amidst harsh winter conditions, reflecting the struggles faced by the poet and their comrades. It calls for awakening the spirit and strength of individuals in the face of adversity, evoking a sense of shared hardship. Historians might find this work significant as it embodies the national sentiment and resistance present during Korea's colonial period, showcasing how such artistic expressions served as a rallying cry against oppression.
