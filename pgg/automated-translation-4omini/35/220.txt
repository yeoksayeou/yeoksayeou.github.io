京城慶雲洞開闢社 葉書通信  

As I passed through Hamnam and visited places like Hamhung, Heungnam, Yeongmu, Sinpo, Sinbukcheong, Dancheon, Seongjin, and Bukcheong, I found that in this time of recent economic downturn and austerity, the only things coming from the dining establishment (Nakai) were the sounds of song and the lonely voice of a beautiful girl singing a sad poem. While some people are urging to enjoy eating as if it were a matter of life and death, I wonder where on earth do they get the funds for such entertainment? Can we have sympathy for those who indulge? What, I wonder, is the true conscience of these individuals?  
-(Hamnam A Drifter)-  

As the year ends, they use the Nightwatch Corps to guard against others sleeping at night, standing vigil.  

Nightwatch Corps! The leads are on the lookout. However, they are hitting the drum loudly, and so unless a person is awake long enough, it becomes difficult to sleep due to this incessant drumming. If the drum is meant to deter thieves, could it be that thieves would hear that sound and take the opportunity to commit robbery?  

Can they not stop the drumming and still conduct their night watch?  
-(Jaeheung K.C.Y Student)-  

Patients of Hanmun Addiction  

In this world, there exist wretched individuals who can’t properly write but only line up Chinese characters (漢字) to express ideas. Certain esteemed doctors and scholars with grand titles, who publish long articles about Japanese social theory, Japanese literature, or research on various Korean institutions in newspapers and magazines, are included in this group. Do they think reading true prose and writing in the vernacular (언문) would damage their prestige? Please be cautious! Such devotees of classical Chinese (漢文) ...ugh!  
-(Sincheong Student)-  

We Highly Welcome Contributions  

Regardless of what it may be, if there is a fact that can be published in this column, please send it in as a contribution. It doesn’t have to be signed, nor does it need to be anonymous. The article should be concise enough to fit on a postcard. <89>  

**Summary**  

This article presents a snapshot of life in colonial Korea and touches on various social issues, including the economic struggles faced by individuals. Through the perspectives shared by various writers, it reveals a culture grappling with traditional values and modern societal challenges. 

Historians might find the reflection of economic hardship, social commentary, and cultural identity particularly revealing, as it showcases the struggles of people beneath colonial rule while also critiquing the societal standards of that particular era.

연말이 되면 夜警團을 내세워서 다른 사람이 자는 밤을 경게하며 단인다.
夜警團! 조키는 조타. 그러나 그 사람들이 딱딱이를 가지고 딱딱딱딱 치며 도라단이는고로 엥간히 잠이 깁히들기 전에는 이 딱딱이 소래로 인하야 잘수가 업고 또 도적을 경게하는 딱딱이라면 도적은 그 소래를 듯고 도적질 하기에 더욱 조흔 긔 기회를 만드러 줄 것이다.
딱딱이! 그것을 좀 그만두고는 夜警을 못하는겟는지?
