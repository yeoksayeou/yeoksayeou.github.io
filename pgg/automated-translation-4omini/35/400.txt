잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1931년 02월 01일  
기사제목	연통갓흔 내마음은 닥지 못하나 (The Echo of My Heart Cannot Be Suppressed)  
필자	하윤  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

The echo of my heart cannot be suppressed  
When it clangs... my heart weeps.  
Even if they say to extinguish the chimney,  
When it booms... my heart aches even more.

When it clangs... my heart grows despondent.  
The echo of my heart cannot be suppressed,  
When it clangs... only my heart grows despondent.

The unfamiliar hands passing by the street,  
As the sun goes down over Namdaemun (남대문)  
At the café in Jingo (진고개), the lights turn on,  
Inside the train bound for the border,  
They have fares for the night journey under the moon.

When the whistle of the mountain (산) sounds louder,  
A traveler crossing the asphalt road,  
Not even knowing the destination or the way,  
Feels as if there is work for this nation amidst the chill.

In this nation, it seems that there is work to be done,  
Not seeing anything, not speaking a word,  
I walk through the small, narrow, night streets.

- Old Manuscript -  

Summary  

This poem expresses a profound sense of longing and lamentation intertwined with the imagery of daily life and travel against a backdrop of national troubles. The author, Ha Yun, reflects on the struggles of both the individual and the collective, evoking a feeling of helplessness amidst the changes occurring in Korea during the 1930s under Japanese colonial rule. Historians might find this piece significant as it encapsulates the emotional landscape of colonial Korea, reflecting both personal and national identity complexities.
