잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1931년 02월 01일  
기사제목	閻羅國 審判傍聽記 (The Record of Observing the Trial in the Land of Yama)  
필자	金振九 (Kim Jin-ku)  
기사형태	문예기타 (Other Literary Genre)  

In the first month of the lunar calendar, intoxicated by the liquor of the New Year, my heart and mind are rejuvenated. As I lay my full stomach on a bamboo mat, I recite a divine song with my lips and mind, and suddenly my body becomes like that of a butterfly, soaring into the void and ascending to the realm of immortals! I visit the Northern Mountain and, having passed the bright fragrance of the ninth month, I arrive at the White Head Divine Realm. Beneath the pine and crane, I encounter two immortal officials. Bowing deeply, I inquire of their esteemed names; the elder is the Supreme Lord Laozi, and the other is the True Yang Person. Lifting their cane, they guide me to the Jade Capital.

Riding on the back of a blue crane, riding the rainbow and splitting the clouds, following the True Yang Person, we arrive at the palace. The Twelve Great Gates are like iron barrels, guarded by demon-faced, blue-bodied gatekeepers who seize me and demand that I write my circumstances. Personal feelings and bribery also exist in this land. As a destitute wanderer, no matter how much I think, I cannot afford to use money for relationships. After much thought, I toss aside my outer robe. When I raise my arms, they ridiculed me, and the Twelve Gates opened.

The Pearl Palace and Shell Summit are resplendent, with enchanting beauty, where five hundred celestial maidens dressed in dragon robes and adorned with splendid jewels are clustered in different chambers. The Jade Emperor, wearing the heavenly cap and the robe of sun and moon patterns, commands from the royal seat.

The Supreme Lord Laozi, positioned near the Emperor, steps forth and bows again, saying, “Honoring the Emperor by singing a divine song, I have brought this humble mortal to seek your grace. Please grant me an audience.”

The Emperor smiles kindly, saying, “Your words are commendable. Since you have come with such sincerity, make haste to the domain of Yama and observe the trial, but be advised that the heavenly secrets must never be revealed.”

As the words finished, he summoned Judge Choi and ordered him to grant me permission to observe the trial in the court of Yama. When Judge Choi raised his hand, the emissaries of Yama welcomed me into the court. Stepping forward, the oppressive atmosphere is completely different from the celestial realm. The chilling air of autumn and winter surrounds me, oppressive and deadly.

Wearing the Fire Crown and holding a white jade scepter, the King of Hell sits on a golden throne, surrounded by divine generals who have dragon faces and beast bodies. Beneath the canopy, the flags and weapons are fearsome, along with the instruments of punishment like axes and clamps lining the place. It is a sight that sends shivers down the spine.

I asked Judge Choi, “Has the King of Hell been the same since the creation of heaven and earth?”

Looking at me, the judge shook his head. “No, the King is changed every five hundred years. The current King is an incarnation of the governor of Gangwon, who rose to heaven after completing his duties in the region, succeeding the previous King, Yun Kwan of Goryeo.”

“Then, who orders the dismissal?”

“What an ignorant question! It is by the command of the Jade Emperor, who else?”

I settled into my designated seat and observed the proceedings. After a while, the announcement came that it was time for the trial. The gates opened, and the day’s emissaries, holding badges, entered, followed by a procession of five hundred Arhats, bringing with them ten guilty humans shackled in chains.

Trial of the Wealthy

They brought them forth and said, “The ten guilty humans have been brought all at once.”

Judge Choi opened his record, questioning each one individually. After confirming their crimes, the voice of the King of Hell resounded, “You have lived as a wealthy man among humans, experiencing joys that no one else can enjoy. With the fortune you amassed, what virtue or good deeds have you done? Have you been loyal to your country? Have you been filial to your parents? Have you maintained harmony with your siblings and relatives? Have you given food to the starving? Have you clothe the naked? Have you built lodgings for travelers? Have you built bridges over rivers or ships on seas for crossing? Have you helped save lives? Have you done charitable deeds like chanting Buddha’s name? Have you built temples to honor the sages? Have you established schools for your descendants? Have you started factories to relieve unemployment? Have you supported media for public opinion? Have you taken care of the elderly and the orphans? If you have done any of these, you will go to the Pure Land, Lotus Seat, achieve divine honors and fame, and be mighty like a commander in chief or a prime minister. But if you have only indulged in pleasures, you shall descend to hell.”

The first, an estate-owning official, was called forward and raised in the light of dawn, judged and sentenced at once. “You have flourished only by the power of your noble lineage and have exploited land and laborers for your own gain, causing their suffering. You will be sent to the hell of the damned.”

They dragged the official away in chains; he screamed as his body fell like a mountain.

The second, a greedy merchant, was brought forward. “You used your wealth to save the wretched while taking advantage of the vulnerable. You have misused your money for bribes to those in power, allowing you to indulge in gambling and debauchery. You will go to the centipede hell.”

The executioners grabbed him and tossed him into the dark abyss.

The third, a usurer, was also brought forth. “You have exploited the interest left by your forefathers, harassing the poor and forcing their children into debt. You will be sent to the snake hell.”

They discarded him into a dark pit where poisonous snakes swarmed.

The fourth, a landlord who exploits tenants, was next. They stripped him of his coat and caused him to suffer in a boiling vat of oil.

The fifth, a hardworking person who became wealthy through diligence, was called next but was judged for not using his wealth for societal benefits, and he became a pig, squealing away into the pigsty.

The sixth, a deceitful thief, was judged for luring a wealthy man's child and squandering his fortune. “You evaded punishment but will suffer in hell.”

The seventh was a noble elder, praised for honoring his ancestors and helping subsequent generations. “You have achieved success and will serve in the heavenly ranks.”

The eighth was called, an educationalist praised for establishing schools.

The ninth was a kind elder who devoted their life to charitable works and would be reborn as the wife of a global leader.

The tenth was another elderly woman, recognized for helping the abandoned.

After the trials concluded, I was invited to partake in the wine and peaches prepared by the celestial maidens. Initially, I refused several times but finally thought it appropriate to enjoy a taste of the heaven in the palace. As I relished it, I found the flavor exquisite and the taste profound, and in my revelry sang the popular song, “Kaeji Nachingching,” provoking laughter around me.

My eyes opened, and it was R. K. B. there.

“This man thinks it is still Kaeji Nachingching.”

After witnessing such extraordinary events, I was cautioned not to reveal the heavenly secrets while feeling the weight of awaiting judgment.

**Summary**

The article primarily relays a fantastical courtroom drama set in the mythical afterlife realm of Yama, detailing the trials of various figures based on their earthly deeds. Each character's fate is determined by their accumulated virtues or vices, reflecting moral teachings prevalent during the time of colonial Korea. This narrative serves as a critique of societal structures, particularly how wealth and power corrupt morality. Historians might find it revealing how moralistic themes intertwined with social commentary were articulated during Korea's colonial period, as it subtly reflects discontent with class exploitation and an aspiration for moral integrity amidst oppression.

五. 勞力致富. 한 사람 잡어내어

六. 詐欺取財. 말속한 양복쨍이 한 사람 잡어내어
뻔뻔스럽고 간교한 自晝强盜 가튼 놈아 어리석은 부호의 자식을 꾀어내여 그 사람의 祖父業을 탕진식히고 그것을 가지고 好酒美女의 유락으로 소비하엿스나 네 지혜 영리하야 인간지옥 면햇지만 염라신부에서는 면할 일이 바이업다. 그리알고 원망마라 그놈의 코를 꾀여 철강속에 가두엇다.

九. 慈善家. 白髮風身 넉으럽게 된 한 노파를 불러내어 금방석에 안친 뒤에

그 노파 조와라고 춤을 너울너울 추며 선녀의 인도바더 대문으로 나아간다.
