필자	平壌 孫榮烈(寄)  
기사형태	희곡·시나리오 (Play/Script)  

寸劇, 入院한 女子  

Woman: "Doctor! If it's pleurisy, I will be hospitalized!"  

Doctor: "Ah, no. Even if it's pleurisy, it’s not that serious, so there's no need for hospitalization. Just go home and rest for a while."  

Woman: "No! I absolutely will be staying here!"  

Doctor: "Well! If you insist on being hospitalized when I've said it’s unnecessary… do as you please."  

Woman: "Oh dear, please bring me a wet towel!"  

Pharmacist: "Yes, I’m coming in. We need to finish this prescription first."  

Woman: "Could you please hand over this 5-won for me?"  

Pharmacist: "Is this money what you wanted?"  

Woman: "I brought 200 won from home and I’m sending this amount back to my mother since I have some left."  

Pharmacist: "What? There’s a way to have leftover money? By the way, doesn’t your father work?"  

Woman: "How can you say such things! My father passed away when I was young."  

Pharmacist: "I’m sorry, but since you asked, can I ask one more thing? Who was the person you called on the phone?"  

Woman: "What kind of question is that?!"  

Pharmacist: "What? Is there anything wrong with asking?"  

Woman: "Honestly, it’s just that XX transportation point is my father’s... and the XX bus driver is my sixth uncle."  

Pharmacist: "Oh! This is quite a story!"  

Woman: "Hey, maiden! Is the person working at the pharmacy at the present?"  

Maiden (in the inpatient room with a meal): "Not at all!"  

Woman: "Do I have any money at home?"  

Maiden: "I don't have any!"  

Woman: "That high school student over there... where are they from?"  

Maiden: "It’s from the principal's office!"  

Woman: "Oh! So that’s it? Where's my home, then?"  

Maiden: "It should be somewhere within the city."  

Woman: "In the city?..... Is the principal’s wife around?"  

Maiden: "Oh, how ridiculous! You’re asking about everything under the sun! What do you mean, 'wife'?!"

Woman: "............"  

Woman: "How old do you think the person who just left is?"  

Pharmacist: "They must be around twenty-four or twenty-five."  

Woman: "That’s quite accurate! You know your stuff!"  

Pharmacist: "Of course! I’d know to some extent... Is that person your father?"  

Woman: "That's right! He is my father's child."  

Pharmacist: "What does he do?"  

Woman: "Him? He’s a teacher at XX Public Elementary School. He’ll be back later, I believe."  

Pharmacist: "So you said he has a big family, right?"  

Maiden (previously mentioned): "Hey, have you heard of something interesting?!"

Child watching the director: "What?"  

Maiden: "Oh! The girl who was just hospitalized is now playing with her sleepy friends!"  

Child: "Really?"  

Maiden: "Didn't you see?!..... I’ll fetch some water and come back, so please turn off the lights. I don’t know why that will turn off the lights... but... I still can’t say who it is...?!"  

Child: "Hey, did they say that she’s my father?"  

Maiden: "Really?! Well?!"  

Woman: "Oh no! I have to give this money back. How can I spend it?"  

Man (former teacher, discussing with the children): "Didn't she not give you 5 won yesterday?"  

Woman: "That money covered the medication and all!"  

Man: "Do you only have this little money?"  

Woman: "Huh! Where am I supposed to spend it?"  

Man: "Then, at least use the savings you have?"  

Woman: "Ah, I see! If I eat the 200 won, what will I do then?"  

Man: “Ah! Don’t say that, money will eventually come by again.”  

Woman (pouting)  

Man (former teacher): "Hey! Where did the woman who was just hospitalized go?"  

Pharmacist: "She got discharged last night."  

Man: "What?"  

Pharmacist: "Yes! Last night... and who did she leave with?"  

Man (licking their lips): "Hmm…"  

-(1931. 1. 1)-  

**Summary**  

The dialogue reveals a scenario involving a woman determined to be hospitalized even for a minor medical issue, showcasing her interactions with a doctor and pharmacist as she tries to manage her finances and family connections. The exchange highlights the social dynamics and attitudes towards women and health during Korean colonial times. Historians might find the depictions of female agency and societal expectations particularly interesting, as they reflect the struggles faced by women in that era of Korean history.
