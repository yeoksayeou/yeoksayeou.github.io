Editor's Note  
As we welcome the New Year, our publication has taken bold steps to further refresh and expand its operations, continuing the publication of the women's magazine Sinyeoseong while also moving to a newly constructed space on the upper levels of the Cheondogyo Memorial Hall. We ask everyone to keep a watchful eye on the ongoing progress of our publication.  

This February issue was intended to be published earlier than the New Year's edition; however, due to the busy circumstances surrounding the year-end and the moving of our editorial office, the submission of manuscripts was delayed, resulting in a slightly later release than planned.  

Nevertheless, this issue covers significant current issues in Korea, such as the debate surrounding the dissolution of the Sin-Gan-hoe (New Korean Society), major unity issues concerning Christianity, Cheondogyo, and the Kyŏngin province, as well as protests against the Nanjing government by the Korean diaspora in Manchuria... The content is substantial, making it a must-read for anyone wishing to understand and be concerned about Korea.  

As for international articles, we delve into the activities of the Chinese Communist Party, which is again stirring up trouble in the now-unified China, a report on the Lamasery Head's meeting, and movements within the Japanese administration... Additionally, we recommend the How-To articles on various topics as recreational reading.  

Furthermore, articles that have remained unresolved since last year, such as the issues surrounding the daily newspaper, practical education for women, suburban transportation issues, and employment strategies for this spring’s graduates, are also important reads.  

Acknowledgment  
Due to unavoidable circumstances, we regret not being able to publish the full versions of Mr. Yang Hyun-soo's 'Critique on the Dissolution of the Sin-Gan-hoe' and Mr. Buk Hongsaeng's 'A Ballad of the Korean Infantry,' and we extend our apologies to our writers and readers for this.

**Summary**  

This article serves as an editorial note reflecting on the transition of the magazine, its thematic coverage, and the challenges faced in publishing. It underscores the commitment to addressing significant national issues and highlights the ongoing tension around Korean identity during colonial rule. Historians might find it interesting how the magazine positions itself as a crucial platform for discussions about Korean autonomy and socio-political concerns during a politically turbulent period.

編輯落書
新年을 마지하며 본사는 사업을 일층 刷新확장하야 여성잡지 신여성을 또다시 續刊하는 동시에 사옥도 構內新築한 天道敎紀念館 上層으로 이전하게 되엿슴니다. 여러분도 다갓치 본삿 社運의 日益進展됨을 깃버하야 주시옵소서.

이번 2월호는 신년호보다도 발행을 좀 더 일즉이 하랴고 하얏더니 연말연시에 필자 여러분의 多忙한 관계로 원고도 느저지고 우에 말슴한 바와 가티 사옥의 이전관계로 편집 시일도 조금 느저저서 여의하게 되지 못하얏슴니다.

그러나 이번호는 『現下朝鮮에서 一大문제되는 신간회의 解消贊否論을 비로하야 기독교, 천도교, 京仁取等 三大합동문제와 在滿백만동포를 위하야 중국 남경정부에 항의 등등.... 朝鮮實事情記事를 만히 취급하니만큼 내용이 자못 충실하야 조선을 알고자하고 조선을 걱정하는 이로는 누구나 한번 꼭 일어야만하게 되엿슴니다.(車)

국제기사로서 現下南北통일된 중국의 천하를 재차 소란케하는 중국공산당의 활약과 라마법왕회견기, 일본민정당내각의 운동...은 누구나 또 일을만하고 그 외 넷썬스 실화집, 넷썬스 좌담회는 취미기사로 또한 일독을 권하고 십슴니다.(蔡)

그리고 昨年來로 미해결하야 누구나 궁금하게 녁이는 中外日報문제, 實踐여학교문제, 교외전차문제 등과 今春의 졸업생을 위하야 쓴 就職前哨戰에 관한 문제, 생활개선문제 등도 또한 한번 읽을 필요가 잇는 기사임니다.(朴)
