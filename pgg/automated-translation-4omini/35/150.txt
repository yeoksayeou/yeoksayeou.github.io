誌上顧問蘭  
How can one endure苦學 (hard study) after going to Tokyo? (Haeju K)  
You can do it—if you have the resolve to overcome a hundred difficulties.  

(Excerpt omitted) So, how must both I and Eo Il proceed to improve? (A worker at a factory in the city)  
(Answer: omitted)  

If the era of Goguryeo (高句麗) returns? (Pyongyang XY)  
If the arrow of Yang Manchun (梁萬春) could pluck out the eyes of Tang Taizong (唐太宗)...  

I was heartbroken a year ago. Is there any way to avoid heartbreak? (Gwangju P)  
Do not engage in romance.  

My lover insists I am married and rejects me. What should I do? (A resident in the city, W.K.S)  
(1) Get a divorce, (2) return to your spouse, (3) find a lover who will accept you, or (4) consider a suicide attempt that is not life-threatening.  

While reading articles in Byeolgeongon, I sometimes get so frustrated with parts like "This paragraph omitted" or "The following is omitted" that I feel like throwing the book away. What on earth is going on? (A reader in Busan)  
Please understand my inability to answer even that. It is a situation akin to eating mustard while crying.  

There is a saying to send one's loved one (말색기) to Jeju while sending a person (사람의 색기) to Seoul. Should I go to Seoul once? (A student from Gwangju who has never been to Seoul)  
If you are not a naive friend like the Buddha, you would be the biggest fool in the world. Either way, if you can manage this autumn, please come here, as it is a bit crowded.  

Miss A is cold to me, yet I like her, while Miss B follows me, but I feel sad. What gives? (Triangle student in the city)  
This is the timeless riddle of static entanglements between the two genders. It would be best to stop concerning yourself with A and B and instead find a C who admires you and follows you.  

Is it true that men should maintain their chastity? (A student from Geumgang)  
The notion of chastity varies by time and class.  

How many readers does Byeolgeongon have? (A student from Pyongyang)  
It is usually more than the printed circulation.  

What is the current resting status of Jeong No-bong, the proponent of "National Socialist Literature"? (A student from Anseong)  
One rumor states he is preparing for the “Korean literature” exam, while another states he is employed at a certain provincial office.  

(1) Address of Dr. Lee Gapsu, (2) a well-known ear doctor in Gyeongseong, (3) is there a special course at the Middle Eastern School? (Gil Bogeok from Jangheung)  
(1) 64 Susong-dong, Gyeongseong, (2) Ear-nose-throat department at Gyeongseong Séburanùs Hospital, Dr. Hong Seok-ho, (3) not available.  

(Excerpt omitted) Will you consider printing a play in each issue starting from the new year, and also adding a Korean language section? (Kim Young-su from Busan)  
While we haven't printed a play in every issue, we have done so several times. The Korean language section is under consideration.
