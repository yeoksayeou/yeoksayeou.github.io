잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1929년 08월 01일  
기사제목	痛快無雙奇人篇, 朝鮮의 諸葛亮인, 天下奇人 먹젹골 許生員 (Exciting and Unsurpassable Remarkable Person: Korea's Zhuge Liang, The World’s Remarkable Person, Heo Saengwon of Meokjeokgol)  
필자	壽春學人 (Suh Chun Hak-in)  
기사형태	소식 (News)  

 

Heo Saengwon of Meokjeokgol (also known as Meokjeok-dong, now Daiwa-cho 3-chome) is a person whose background and name remain largely unknown. People simply refer to him as Heo Saengwon of Meokjeokgol because he resides in Meokjeokgol and has the surname Heo. 

He lived his life devoted to reading, neglecting worldly affairs, which led his wife to struggle to make ends meet by working as a maid in someone else's house. However, even that income was insufficient, and he often went half a month without eating in a month. 

One day, unable to endure the hunger any longer, his wife tearfully said, "My dear, if you're only going to read books for your entire life, what will we do?" Heo Saengwon smiled and replied that he still had more reading to do. His wife, furious, scolded him, questioning how he expected to provide food if he didn't engage in any trade or farming but only read all night. She suggested that it would be better for him to steal if that was the case. Heo Saengwon, taken aback, closed the book he was reading, lamenting, "How unfortunate that I had planned to read for ten years, and now I can hardly manage to read for seven." 

He left the house and wandered around Jongno, inquiring among the townspeople about the wealthiest merchants in the capital. Eventually, he learned that the Byun family was the wealthiest. Although he had never met Mr. Byun, he mustered up his courage and approached him, explaining that he was very poor and requested to borrow a sum of one hundred thousand yang (a currency). Mr. Byun, being a wise man, sensed that Heo Saengwon was an extraordinary individual upon meeting him and immediately granted his request, giving him the one hundred thousand yang he asked for. The family was astounded and questioned their master as to why he gave such a large sum to a beggar he did not know, but Mr. Byun simply replied that they would not understand.

 

Heo Saengwon took the one hundred thousand yang and traveled to Ansung, where he traded various fruits such as jujubes, chestnuts, pears, and mandarins. Before long, there was a nationwide harvest failure, and he gained tenfold profit from his fruits. Following that, he ventured to Jeju to trade horsehair (馬*), again securing a tenfold profit.

Suddenly possessing a substantial amount of money, he sought information from an old boatman and discovered a large uninhabited island in the South Sea. He then led thousands of men from the dock at Byeonsan to relocate to that island, investing three hundred thousand yang into establishing an industry, cultivating barren land, and planting various crops. Within a few years, he managed to accumulate millions of sacks of grain. 

At that time, a severe famine struck Japan, so he transported his grain there and sold it at a high price, earning one million yang all at once. Heo Saengwon then sighed and said, "I have already demonstrated a small test." He distributed significant sums of money to others and, when returning, lost five hundred thousand yang in the sea but still had ten thousand yang left after sharing with the many poor people he encountered. 

He then returned to Seoul to meet Mr. Byun, who welcomed him joyfully, asking if he had experienced any significant failures in the meantime. Heo Saengwon answered with a smile that he had lost ten thousand yang. Mr. Byun was astonished and proposed to take only the interest on his principal, but Heo Saengwon grew angry, saying, "Do you view me as just a mere merchant?" He rushed back home, where his wife mistook him for dead and had been mourning him for several years, only to be surprised and delighted by his return. 

Afterward, Mr. Byun continued to feel grateful and, when there was a shortage of supplies, personally provided for Heo Saengwon's family. Their friendship deepened to the point where they became very close friends.

One day, seizing an opportunity while Mr. Byun was urging him, Heo Saengwon remarked that the high-ranking officials were aiming to resolve the national crisis (referring to the Byeongja Horan incident). He encouraged Mr. Byun to make use of his talents instead of suffering without recognition of his capabilities. Heo Saengwon expressed concern, lamenting that even now Zhao Shengqi, who possesses extraordinary abilities, remained a recluse despite being capable of revitalizing the nation, while Yu Hyung-won could sufficiently supply military provisions but is wasting his life away. 

At that time, Li Wan, a general, was on the lookout in various regions for talents to execute the king’s ambition to invade the north during the reign of King Hyojong. Mr. Byun, being acquainted with Li Wan, introduced Heo Saengwon's talents. Li Wan was greatly surprised and remarked that indeed, if such a person existed, he would be a remarkable figure in the world. He asked for his name, to which Heo Saengwon replied that he had been with Mr. Byun for three years but did not know his own name.

Li Wan then conveyed that he had come because the king was seeking capable individuals for his campaign against the north. Heo Saengwon replied, "If you are a general, what is the meaning of seeking me?" 

Li Wan then explained that the monarch wished to find talents who could be put to use for this purpose. Heo Saengwon responded, "If so, you understand my capabilities and I will act like Zhuge Liang, but you need to persuade the monarch to employ me like Liu Bei did in seeking Zhuge Liang." 

Li Wan bowed for a while and expressed that it was too difficult, asking if Heo had anything else to add. Heo shook his head, saying he did not know the next thing. 

Li Wan repeatedly pressed for more, but Heo continued, "Since the Ming merchants have ties with Joseon, their descendants have secretly arrived and are hiding in Joseon. If you could ask the king to wed his daughter to those who are wandering and helpless, and if you could take current influential figures like Kim Jaem and Jang Yu and provide them with a place to live, you could manage that." 

Li Wan thought a long while but ultimately noted that that too was challenging. 

Heo then remarked, "If both this and that are difficult, what can be done?" He further stated, "Certainly, the easiest task lies in uniting the heroes across the land." He suggested that before seeking to invade another country, intelligence must be gathered, else there would be no success. 

As the Jurchens suddenly rose to dominate the world, Heo cautioned that if Joseon would take the initiative to show friendliness toward them, they might come to trust Joseon. Seizing opportunities, Joseon could request the young and virtuous descendants from the Ming to travel and study in Joseon, enabling merchants to travel freely. 

If this were accomplished, those who went would shave their heads and wear the appropriate clothing, thus interacting with prominent people and gaining access to national affairs. If they form bonds with the fallen heroes of the Ming, they could potentially reclaim their own honor. He argued, as they sought a way out, if they could not heed the requests of noble families, uniting the surviving kings and carefully selecting individuals could elevate them to the status of national advisors, even if retreating they would not fall out of alignment. 

Li Wan remarked that although government proprieties held precedence, few would agree to shave their heads and wear the appropriate attire. Heo reproved him fiercely, questioning what the nobility really were if they were merely parasites. In pursuit of personal vendettas, those such as Fan Yuqi even refrained from cutting their hair, and Zhao Wuling Wang did not hesitate to dress properly in preparation for the affairs of state. 

Now, if one speaks of redeeming the honor of a nation, how is it fitting to remain so preoccupied with respect that they cannot act decisively? If you cannot heed what I say, what worth does such a character hold? Such people should promptly be relieved of their existence. He scanned his surroundings and grasped his sword, making Li Wan flee in a panic. 

The next day, Li Wan sought to meet him again, only to find that Heo's residence was already abandoned, and it was impossible to trace his whereabouts thereafter.  





**Summary**

This article portrays the remarkable life of Heo Saengwon, a figure who transitioned from a life of poverty and seclusion dedicated solely to reading to becoming a wealthy trader and a prominent individual with significant influence. His interactions with Mr. Byun highlight the collaborative nature of relationships in overcoming hardship, while his conversations with Li Wan reflect the broader social and political issues of his time, particularly concerning national identity and governance during a turbulent era in Korean history. Historians might find it interesting to explore how Heo Saengwon's character serves as a critique of the traditional elite's indecision and the ongoing challenges faced by Korea during colonial rule.

먹적골(즉 墨積洞이니 지금 大和町 三丁目) 허생원은 내력도 잘 알수 업고 이름도 또한 알 수 업는 이다. 다만 그의 집이 먹적골에 잇고 성이 허씨인 까닭에 세상 사람들이 그저 먹적골 허생원이라고 부를 뿐이엿섯다. 그는 평생에 글읽기만 조와 하고 살임사리는 돌보지 안음으로 그의 夫人이 남의 집 삭바누질을 하야 억지로 생활을 하얏다. 그러나 그것만으로는 도저이 살 수가 업서서 한 달에도 보름식은 굼기가 례사엿다. 그의 부인은 飢寒을 참다참다 못하야 하루는 울며 말하되 여보 당신이 평생에 글만 읽고 잇스면 엇지하잔 말이요 하니 허생원이 웃스며 대답하되 내가 아즉도 글을 더 읽어야 한다 하니 그 부인은 승이 나서 꾸지지되 남자가 장사도 아니하고 농공업도 아니하며 밤낫 글만 읽으면 밥은 누가 먹이여 준단 말이오. 그러할진대 찰아리 도적질이라도 하는 것이 좃치아느냐고 하니 허생원은 하드 기가 막히여서 읽던 책을 탁 덥고 일어나며 탄식하야 갈오되 악갑드다 내가 원래에 십 년 동안을 글읽기로 작뎡하엿더니 이제 겨우 칠 년박게는 못 읽게 되엿다 하고 문을 나서서 종로 거리로 한 바휘 도라다니며 시정 사람들에게 장안에 제일 갑부가 누구인 것을 탐문하다가 필경 변씨라는 이가 큰 부자인 것을 알엇다. 그는 본래 변씨를 한 번도 만나 본 일도 업지만은 비위 좃케 차저가서 변씨를 보고 말하기를 내가 집이 매우 가난한 까닭에 무엇을 시흠 삼아 좀 하야 보겟스니 돈 만 량만 취하야 달나고<2> 하얏다. 그 변씨는 또한 원래 知鑑이 잇는 사람이라 허생원을 한 번 보고 비범한 인물인 것을 짐작하고 당장에 쾌락하고 돈 만 량을 청구한대로 주니 그 집의 자제와 손들이 모도 놀내며 주인에게 무슨 까닭으로 알지도 못하는 한 걸인에게 만 량을 그럭케 허수이 주느냐 하니 변씨는 그저 너히들이 알배 아니라고만 하니 여러 사람들이 더욱 괴이하게 녁일 뿐이러라.
