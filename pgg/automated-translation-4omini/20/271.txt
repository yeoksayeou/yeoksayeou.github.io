Announcement of Wedding Ceremony Selection

As traditional practices fade away without a clear substitute emerging, many find themselves perplexed when facing weddings. Thus, our organization initiated an open call for proposals for new wedding ceremony ideas, resulting in a collection of seventy-nine entries. After careful consideration and selection, we have decided upon the following winners:

Second Prize (Prize: 50 yen)	High Gwangsan (高光山)	서울평동 (Kyŏngsŏng P'yŏngdong) 20

Second Prize (Prize: 50 yen)	Yu Yeongseon (魚永善)	단양 (Danyang) (Please notify address in handwriting)

Third Prize (Prize: 30 yen)	Park Hyun-gyeok (朴鉉珏)	선천군선천면천북동 (Seoncheon-gun Seoncheon-myeon Cheonbuk-dong)

Third Prize (Prize: 30 yen)	Kim Sang-ho (金相鎬)

Non-awarded	Lee Seung-gang (李承綱)	청도군화양면유등동 (Cheongdo-gun Hwayang-myeon Yudong)

Non-awarded	Lee Myeong-uk (李明瑀)	평양경상리 (P'yŏngyang Kyŏngsangri)

With these selections made, the reason for not having a first prize winner is that any first prize selection must be a completely feasible proposal that everyone can practically implement. Since each proposal presented has its unique strengths and weaknesses, we intend to extract the best features from each and compile them into a comprehensive and practical proposal that we can recommend universally.

Thus, the editorial staff will consider the merits of each entry and, based on public feedback, will compile a common proposal to be released in the next issue of this magazine. Moreover, we will contribute a cash prize of one hundred yen to the first family to implement this proposal.

Please look forward to further details in the next issue of this magazine.

The winners of the five hundred yen literature prize for youth have been published in the August issue for children, while the student cover winners were published in the June issue for students  <97>

**Summary**

This article announces the results of a competition for new wedding ceremony proposals aimed at modernizing traditional practices. It suggests a collective effort to produce a comprehensive wedding plan by merging the strengths of various submissions. The absence of a first-place winner highlights the expectation for thorough, practical proposals accessible to everyone. For historians, this reflects the societal shifts during the colonial period in Korea, where traditional norms were challenged and redefined under Japanese influence.
