잡지명	별건곤 제63호  
발행일	1933년 05월 01일  
기사제목	連載探偵小說, 湖底의 秘密 (Serial Detective Novel, The Secret of the Lake)  
필자	모-리스. 루부랑(원작자), 尹成學(번역자)  
기사형태	소설 (Novel)  

In the splendid spring of March in Seoul, with warm spring rain falling, the lush green grass emerged, mirroring the enthusiasm of youth engaged in vibrant life. A young man named Ryu Do (리우도) was wandering through the city with a leisurely heart. 

He was a modest gentleman of unremarkable height, neither too fat nor too thin, giving him an even more appealing and vigorous appearance. His skin appeared bright and handsome, exuding the masculinity of a young man dressed impeccably in the latest fashion. Even upon first glance, it was evident that he was one of the modern representatives of stylish men. 

As he was crossing a park, he suddenly noticed a gentleman stealthily following a woman. This strange occurrence soon became a confirmed fact. Watching a man tail a woman was neither amusing nor surprising. 

“Hey— that guy is following a woman. How interesting! I might as well follow him…” Drawn by curiosity, he started trailing the man who was pursuing the woman. 

The man following the woman, and the young man trailing the man — this could be described as a love triangle of sorts! 

All three maintained a suitable distance as they strolled through the bustling streets of Seoul. The strange man behaved with such gentlemanly composure that the woman remained unaware of his presence. 

Ryu Do was at least experienced in such matters. He pretended not to be watching while smoothly passing pedestrians, thinking, “I need to get a glimpse of both their faces.” 

The figure of the strange man was tall and wore a well-fitted suit and a black bowler hat. His hair was shiny and slicked back. Judging from his pronounced features, he seemed to be in his thirties. He appeared cheerful, yet forced himself to act serious, his demeanor somehow unrefined. He wore several rings and had a cigarette in his mouth. 

“This time, I need to see the woman’s face,” Ryu Do thought, quickening his pace. The woman was slender and walked in a lively manner, immediately reminding one of British ladies. 

She had an exquisite curve that looked almost fragile, and a rounded, soft ankle that seemed delicate to the touch. Her face was incredibly beautiful, reminiscent of a classical beauty, enchanting enough to catch the eye of any passerby. Her sparkling eyes shone even brighter alongside her golden hair. 

As the throngs of people exchanged views, she seemingly paid little attention to the gazes directed her way. 

“Oh! A beauty resembling an angel,” thought the young man in astonishment. 

“A truly exquisite classical beauty. How can such a stunning woman be tailed by that man? Could he be some jealous detective? Or is he a broken-hearted handsome fellow? Otherwise, is he merely a love-sick fool? Perhaps he won’t be overly intrusive.” 

“It’s not certain to say he is indeed those things. Yet it’s very likely, with money and women around, that it’s just pure instinct guiding him.” 

She walked gracefully into an opera house filled with carriages, and as soon as she entered, a carriage blocked her path. Seeing the obstruction, she silently reached out to catch the reins of the startled horse, which halted in its tracks. However, this angered the horse’s owner, who emerged from the carriage. 

“What is the meaning of this! This is outrageous!” he exclaimed, and no sooner had he shouted than the woman unleashed a punch that drew blood from his nose, splattering on the ground. 

People gathered around, while policemen rushed in to mediate amidst cries and confusion as she, ignoring any looks cast her way, swiftly passed by as if nothing had happened. Meanwhile, two boys came fighting near the youth. 

Without concern for the commotion, the woman grabbed the two boys by their collars and, with a swift motion, threw them aside as if they were mere kittens, then tossed out two coins. 

“Oh, what a frightening woman!” Ryu Do thought, his eyes wide in surprise.

When they arrived at Jongno Street, she entered a certain shop. The man who had followed her sat not in the restaurant but, cleverly hidden, took a corner seat where she couldn’t see him. 

The woman ordered tea and a piece of bread, beginning to eat with her pristine white teeth. 

As people came and went, they looked at her, but she seemed oblivious to their attention. Again, she ordered several pieces of bread. 

At that moment, a slightly shabby woman sitting at a nearby table caught Ryu Do's attention. She too had golden locks and dressed simply in fashionable attire typical of Seoul. 

She had three children with her, who, partaking of cookies provided by the woman, seemed cautiously excited, mumbling to one another, unaware of the woman’s intentions. 

“Such a smart face and a charming stance,” Ryu Do mused, captivated by her beauty. He glanced back at the woman he had been trailing. That woman was indeed striking. 

She was very elegant and lovely, but the blue-eyed woman surpassed her in allure. 

As the woman paid for her meal and stood up with her children, he debated whether to follow the blue-eyed woman or the British woman. Which one should he choose? 

He had no time to decide, so as soon as she stood up, he hurriedly left the café. In an instant, he saw the blue-eyed maiden standing close to the British woman, and the strange man was speaking to her. However, their conversation hurriedly turned into a heated discussion, and it appeared that the woman was blocking the man from passing. 

“Interesting. Here’s a great opportunity; I can use this situation to intervene and resolve their conflict,” he thought, charging ahead. Just then, a car arrived at the cafe's entrance. 

As a man got out, he noticed the scene and immediately strode over, striking the hat off the strange man’s head. 

“Hey!” the man cried, stepping back in surprise. 

“What are you doing?” the gentleman exclaimed. He appeared roughly fifty, with a wild demeanor, shaking his fist. 

“I am her father! Don’t behave so recklessly!” the furious father shouted. 

The two men glared at one another, and the girl tugged at her father's arm, trying to pull him back toward the car. 

“Let go of me!” 

Then suddenly, the strange man lunged forward and shoved the girl aside, grabbing the father's hat. 

As he yanked it away, a young man unexpectedly stepped between them, blinking nervously while holding a cigarette. It was Ryu Do, who spoke in a loud voice. 

“Excuse me, but could I borrow a light for my cigarette?” 

Caught off guard, the strange man became dazed for a moment. 

“Why are you interfering?” 

“Actually, I just needed to borrow a light for your cigarette,” Ryu Do insisted, pushing against him. 

But strangely, both his arms and entire body seemed frozen and unable to move. He suddenly bent over, letting out a sigh from his mouth. A sound of discomfort escaped him. 

“Ah…” Ryu Do, thinking swiftly, tightened his grip even more to wrestle him down while still smiling. 

“Excuse me, would you mind lending me a light? It would be much obliged if you could…” He was met with laughter from the curious crowd surrounding them. 

After some mockery, Ryu Do released the man and pulled him away from the crowd. But just then, the blue-eyed girl and her father’s car sped away. 

“The tables have turned. What a stroke of luck!” Ryu Do thought as he looked back at the British woman, who remained watching the unfolding drama, now moving through the crowd.

Ryu Do found himself intertwined with notions of his past and future. His past was filled with strange businesses and adventures, and the future seemingly guaranteed not to stray from this path. But for now, he lacked direction. 

In the fleeting moment when the blue-eyed maiden evaded him, he chose to follow the British lady, embarking on a path that might uncover his destiny. Before long, he realized he had turned back again, catching sight of the original target of his pursuit. 

The love triangle continued to unfold.

Many people remained bustling about as the British lady entered a hotel. 

The waiting crowd halted. 

After a moment, a short while passed, and extricating himself from the commotion, Ryu Do purchased a cigarette pack and entered the hotel. After about thirty minutes of conversing with the doorman, he exited again. 

Ryu Do approached the doorman to inquire about the British lady. 

No sooner had they exchanged words when the British woman appeared and climbed into a lavish car. Ryu Do, with a bag in hand, seemed to deduce that she was about to travel somewhere. 

“Driver, please follow that car,” Ryu Do called out, climbing into the adjacent vehicle. 

The British woman, after leaving the station, dined at a restaurant and Ryu Do sat at a slightly distant table. 

She finished her meal and lit a cigarette. 

After a while, at about nine-thirty, a ticket collector arrived with tickets for the fast train scheduled for nine-thirty. 

“Hey, ticket man!” Ryu Do swiftly handed him a coin. 

“If you can tell me about that woman, I’ll give you five won…” 

“Her name is Baek Hwa Son (백화손양).” 

“Where is she going? You must know?” 

“She has a ticket to Bongcheon, just got a ticket for car number five.” 

Ryu Do made his decision, purchasing a ticket for Bongcheon and hastening to the platform. 

The platform was relatively crowded, and she appeared to have already boarded. 

Car number five was nearly empty, with only two passengers. Ryu Do boarded the train from a distance while deliberately browsing through newspapers and magazines. 

Before long, the train’s whistle blew. 

He hurried into the swiftly moving train, securing a seat in the three-person section. The British woman occupied the window seat of the car, and she glanced at him, clearly astonished to find someone without luggage or belongings. 

For a while, the train sped through vast fields, and the bright outlines of glamorous Seoul began to fade away. Ryu Do flipped through newspapers absentmindedly. 

He began to feel bored. 

Gathering courage, he approached her seat. The British lady looked at him, seemingly refusing to engage in conversation. 

He took it upon himself to speak, knowing he had nothing to lose. 

“Excuse me, I apologize for bothering you, but I find myself in a rather particular situation and seek your permission to discuss an important matter concerning you. Would you mind?” 

The woman, now looking up, replied coldly, “Your behavior is quite rude.” 

“Pardon me, however, there is a troubling matter regarding a peculiar man I noticed following you earlier…” 

“Why are you following me?” she shot back.

“It’s nothing personal; the man has been acting strangely…” 

“If you know him, then I’m well aware. He’s a man named Ma Taek. While he lacks any form of basic civility—and while he exists in my personal life—why then would you involve yourself in that?” 

The woman released her chilling response was so intense that Ryu Do felt a sharp stir of discomfort.

“I sincerely apologize, I just couldn’t ignore my sense of concern…” 

“I understand. Now please exit the train at the next station.” 

“I’m sorry, but I need to travel to Bongcheon; I cannot comply.” 

“After noticing that I was going there you probably found a reason to follow me here.” 

“No, I only saw you at the station; that was when I decided.” Ryu Do finally raised his voice. 

“It seems you viewed me with admiration, and if it were to be without your efforts, perhaps you would let the blue-eyed woman be.” 

That’s when you chose to follow either of us?” 

“I see I have made a blunder; I should have welcomed your pursuits before this interaction.” 

“I certainly appreciate your keen observation skills regarding personal affairs." 

The woman now regarded him with renewed interest, glancing around the carriage. 

Ryu Do felt momentarily mute as she had seen through him, asking a question. 

“It’s quite easily ascertainable, considering the details of where I reside.” 

“It’s hardly noteworthy since you stand out very notably.” 

As the stunning blue-eyed maiden had been isolated from the crowd, she noticed the hat with the initials 'H.V' adorned upon his head. 

“Surely, you’re not claiming…?”

“Of course, I’m none other than Li Do-wu (리도우) the explorer of the east and central Asia.” 

Ryu Do was taken aback. 

“What fine information indeed! But how did you come across so many details?” 

“I didn’t need anything besides observation. The fact you entered this train with no belongings like a traveling vagabond is apparent to any observer. Furthermore, no bag or even carry-on luggage notices.” 

“Truly, anyone would realize that too.” 

In the ensuing dialogue, Ryu Do became dumbfounded by her insights, realizing she was deliberately teasing him with irony. 

“Now for those initials 'H.V,' what do they signify?” 

“It refers to the uniqueness of your name simply shared by a few—rare initials few possess.” 

After noticing the H and V letters on his hat, she appeared to understand. 

“I am perplexed as to why you presume that…” 

“Is it not clear that you are also of the famous, Lupin?” 

Ryu Do clapped his hands in mirth, caught with delight. 

“Hahaha, so you mean I am Lupin, huh?”  

**Summary**  

The story centers around Ryu Do, a young man entangled in a love triangle as he spies on a mysterious blue-eyed woman and a gentleman following her. This narrative unfolds against the backdrop of 1930s Seoul, reflecting the intersections of individual pursuits, societal norms, and budding relationships. Its portrayal of the dynamics of jealousy and amorous interest, coupled with the incorporation of modern themes of identity and observation, provides insight into the complexity of human relationships during the colonial era, highlighting how such interactions serve as microcosms of broader sociopolitical narratives.

						모-리스. 루부랑(원작자), 尹成學(번역자)
1
화려한 서울의 춘삼월 따뜻한 봄빗흘 바더 푸릇푸릇이 도다 오르는 고흔 잔디와 가티 생(生)에 열중한 절문 청춘의 부드러운 가삼과도 가티 청년 리우도(李愚道)는 한가한 마음으로 시가(市街)를 건일고 잇섯다.
키는 그리 크도적도 안한 아담한 신사 그다지 뚱뚱하도 야위도 안한 알마즌 테격 그것이 더한층 긔잇고 강긔잇게 보인다.
그 모든 것에 영민하고 단아하게 보이는 피부가 터질 듯이 힘잇서 보이는 두억개 넓직한 가삼의 남성미 그런 중에도 최신 류행의 줄선 양복을 몸에 꼭 맛도록 입은 그 남자 아모리 처음보는 사람일지라도 현대 대표적 신식남성의 한사람이란 것을 잘 추단할 수가 잇다.
그가 맛츰 공원 압흘 지날 때 불의에 자긔와 가티 거러가는 신사가 엇더한 녀자의 뒤를 딸은다는 것을 발견햇다.
그럴덧하든 추칙은 발서 어느 사이에 확실한 사실이 되고 마랏다. 남자가 녀자의 뒤를 딸른 것을 구경하는 것가티 자미잇고 우수운 일은 업다.
「야- 녀석이 녀자의 뒤를 딸아간다. 자미잇구나! 그러면 나는 저자의 뒤를 딸어볼가. ...」
그는 만흔 호긔심에 끌녀 곳 녀자의 뒤를 쫏튼 남자의 뒤를 쫏기시작햇다.
녀자의 뒤를 따른 남자, 남자의 뒤를 따른 남자. 말하자면 이것이 삼각관계라고 할까!
세사람이 다 각긔 적당한 거리를 띄워가며 번화한 서울 시가를 거러갓다.
이상한 남자는 녀자가 눈치를 채이지 못할만치 가장 신사인 태도를 일치안엇다.
리도우는 적어도 그런 방면에 경험자이엿다. 그는 또한 아모것도 몰으는체 하고 만흔 행인을 슬적슬적 지나치며 「엇더케 하든지 남녀의 얼골을 조곰 보야야겟다」하고 생각햇다.
그 이상한 남자의 뒷모양은 줄진 양복에 검은 중절모 모자 밋헤는 머리는 기름을 발너 광채가 나고 역시 억개는 넓직한대 키는 훨신 크고 도라선 정면을 보니 아조 얼골이 잘생겻고 수염은 팔자(八字)로 버러젓스며, 윤택잇는 살빗, 나희는 한 삼십세 가량이나 되어보엿다. 활발스러운 거름거리 억지로 점잔은체 하는 태도 그러나 어대인지 상스려운 풍채가 뵌다. 손에는 두셋의 반지(指環)을 끼고 입에는 여송연을 피우고 잇섯다.
「이번에는 녀자의 얼골을 보아야겟다」하고 리우도는 다리를 빨니 옴겻다. 녀자는 키가 호리호리한데 삽분삽분거러가는 거름거리는 대번에 영국부인들을 련상할만하다. 어엽븐 곡선미(曲線美) 그리고 만지면 부서질 듯이 연(軟)해 보이는 동글고도 볼통한 두복상씨(踝) 그리고 얼골은 경국의 미를 생각할 만치 고옵고도 아름다웟다. 샛별가티 반짝이는 두 눈동자가 금실머리 밋헤서 더 한층 반짝이고 잇섯다.
오고가고 하는 만흔 사람들이 겻을 지날 때마다 흘금흘금 처다보앗스나 그녀자는 처다보는 행인들에게 별로 유의하지 안는듯이 보엿다.
「오오! 천사가튼 미인이다.」하고 리청년은 불식중 속으로 부르지젓다.
「참으로 훌늉한 고전적(古典) 미인이다. 뒤를 따른 여드럼장이 한테는 너무나 과한 미인이다.... 그러나 저자가 엇떠케 할 적정인가? 질투만흔 나리님(刑事)인가? 실연한 자층 미남일가. 그러치 안으면 사랑을 주으랴고 도라단이는 색광마(色狂魔)일까? 그러타 과연 그가튼대서 별로 버서나지 안으리라.」
「꼭 맛는다고는 할수 업서도 하여간 이 방면에서 멀지 안타. 또는 돈이 잇고, 여자가 잇고, 다만 본능적으로 성욕에 넘쳐 도라단이는 놈인 듯하다.」
그녀자는 마차가 들어찬 오페라(극장) 압 널은 마당으로 삽분삽분＜56＞ 거러들어갓다. 막 들어가자말자 한채의 마차가 녀자이 압길을 막엇다. 압 길을 막은 것을 본 녀자는 두말 업시 고흔 팔을 느려 말 곱비를 잡업다. 곱히를 잡힌 말(馬)은 놀나 멈춧섯다. 그러나 성낸 것은 말 임자이엿다. 말 임자는 곳 마차에서 나려왓다.
「이게 무슨 짓이야! 큰일 나겟네!」하고 욋치자 말자 말 임자는 「악」 소리를 첫다. 어느사에 그 녀자의 주먹은 말 임자의 코를 후려갈겨 시뻘건 피가 땅에 두닥두닥 떨어젓다. 왕래하든 사람들은 끄러모이고 순사들이 달녀와서 이런이 저런이 하고 야단을 하나 그 녀자는 코를 움켜쥐고 잇는 말 임자는 도라보도 안하고 몸을 가볍게 돌녀 아모치도 안한 듯이 빨리 거러갓다. 청년회 압헤서 두 아해가 싸옴을 하고 잇섯다.
그 녀자는 뭇는말도 업시 댓듬 두 아해를 덜미를 움켜쥐며 맛치 고양이색기나 집어던지는 듯이 던지고는 금전 두푼을 내던저 주엇다.
허허허 참 끔직한 녀자다! 하며 리도우는 눈이 둥그래젓다.
그리하야 종로 네거리에 왓슬 때 그녀자는 엇던 차점에 들어갓다. 리청년은 녀자가 의자에 걸어 안는 것을 바라보앗다.
그 녀자를 미행하여 온 그 남자는 식당 한 가운대는 들어가랴고도 하지 안하고 녀자가 못볼만치 한편 구석에 자리를 잡고 잇섯다.
그 녀자는 차와 빵 넷쪼각을 주문하야 백설갓치 흰니로 먹기 시작햇다.
사방에 들너안즌 만흔 사람들이 각금 시선을 그녀자의 편으로 돌엿스나 그 녀자는 예사이엿다. 또 다시 빵 네조작을 주문햇다.
그때 거기서 조곰 떠러저 잇는 탁자 압헤 안즌 한 녀자가 리청년의 주의를 끌엇다.
그도 또한 영국녀자와 가티 푸론드 금빗나는 머리털과 그다지 화려한 의복이라고는 할 수 업스나 서울에서도 류행되는 옷을 색맛추어 입엇다.
그녀자의 엽헤는 다 떠러진 옷을 입은 아해를 셋이서 잇섯다. 아해들은 그 녀자에게 과자를 바더 먹고 잇섯다.
아해들은 알지도 못하는 녀자에게 과자를 어더 왼 영문인줄 몰으고 겁도 나는 듯하나 질거운 듯이 무엇이라고 짓거리고 잇섯다.
「영민한 얼골과 어엽분 자태다.」하고 리청년은 아조 그 녀자에게 미혹되고 마랏다. 그는 고개를 돌녀 앗가 따라온 녀자를 바라보앗다. 그도 또한 미인이다.
너무나 어엽버서 놀날만치 단려하다. 그러치만 눈푸런 녀자는(그는 혼자서 자긔 마음대로 눈푸린 녀자라고 지엇다) 영국녀자 이상이다.
