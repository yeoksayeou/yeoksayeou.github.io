Prize Question  
"If a person consumes a certain elixir from a deity, they can accomplish any one desire. What might that person have wished for after taking the elixir?"  

Announcement of winners from last month's prize question  
Question: "What is the highest city in Korea?"  
Answer: "The Capital (京城)."  
This can be understood, considering how people from all thirteen provinces tend to go up to Seoul.  

This time, the number of applicants was 53,027, and almost all answers were correct. Following a draw, the rankings were decided as follows.

**Summary**  

This section provided a fascinating glimpse into early 20th-century Korean culture through its engagement with a public prize question. The emphasis on desires and wishes signals underlying societal aspirations. Historical context reveals how these contests served as both entertainment and a means of engagement for a populace navigating the complexities of colonial rule. The remarkable number of participants highlights a thirst for knowledge and connection amid the challenging realities of the Korean colonial experience, which historians might find worthy of further exploration.
