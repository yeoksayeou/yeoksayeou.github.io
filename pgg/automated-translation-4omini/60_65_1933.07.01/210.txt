The Superstitious Nation: America  

Even in America, which prides itself as the center of world culture, there are indeed many superstitious people. This was evident when the mayor of Chicago, Anton Cermak, was mistakenly identified as the new President Roosevelt and was shot at while arriving in Miami. He was treated in the hospital for several days but ultimately succumbed to his injuries. During this time, a certain woman from Chicago wrapped a certain flower in delicate cloth and sent it to the hospital with a letter saying, "Please place this flower on Mayor Cermak's chest for about thirty minutes. He will surely recover. This has been a family heirloom of mine for 200 years and has worked wonders on the gravely ill and injured." 

Furthermore, a strange letter was received from someone in Wisconsin, stating, "Every day at 9 AM, please turn Mayor Cermak’s face toward the east and lay him on his left side. Please concentrate your thoughts on him. I will also focus my energies at that time here so that the mayor may recover." 

Additionally, a certain woman even insisted on traveling to the hospital to ask the attending physician, "Please place my hand on Mayor Cermak’s forehead for about thirty minutes. Under my guidance, he will definitely recover." 

Indeed, a myriad of superstitious beliefs abounded. However, the doctor absolutely refused to employ any such "mysterious" methods. While this was certainly out of kindness towards the mayor, the atmosphere of superstition surrounding Mayor Cermak ultimately led him to become a permanent guest of the underworld. <57>

**Summary**  

The article discusses the prevalence of superstitions in America, highlighting the case of Chicago's Mayor Anton Cermak, who, mistakingly identified as President Roosevelt, was shot and died subsequently. Various bizarre superstitions, such as using flowers and hand placements for healing, were presented as attempts to aid his recovery. This article reveals the contradiction between America's self-image as a rational and modern culture while showcasing how deep-rooted superstitions still persisted in society. Historians might find interest in highlighting the cultural dissonance of American society in the early 20th century, particularly in the context of its global influence and modernization versus traditional belief systems.

迷信國 米國
세게문화의 중심지로 자임하는 아메리카에도 미신가(迷信家)가 대단히 만타. 이는 과반 「쉬카고」 시장 「안던. 서막」씨가 「푸러리더」주 「마이아미」에서 신 대통령 「루스벨트」씨로 오인(誤認)되야 저격(狙擊) 당한 바 수일동안 병원에서 치료를 밧다가 마츰내 사망하엿는데 그때에 「쉬카고」의 엇던 부말인은 말니운 꼿씨를 엷은 조희에 싸서 병원에 보래엿다. 그리고 편지 한 장을 가티 보내엿는데 그 내용에는 「이 꼿시를 「서-막」 시장의 가슴우에 30분간쯤 노아두고 보십시오. 반듯이 전쾌될 것입니다. 이것은 200년 전부터 저의 집 가보(家寶)로 전래(傳來)되는 것으로 중병과 부상된 자를 만히 곳치엿습니다 라고 하엿다.
또 「위슨컨싱」주의 모씨로부터는 아래와 가튼 괴상한 편지가 왓다.
「매일 오전 9시가 되거든 서-막시장의 얼골을 동편으 향해 놋코 왼편으로 눕혀 자게 하시오. 그리하야 정신을 집중식혀 주십시오. 그러면 나도 또한 가튼 시간에 이곳에서 정신통일을 하야 시장이 전쾌되도록 해 보겟습니다.」
또 엇던 부인은 일부러 병원까지 차저와서 주치의사에게 부탁하기를
「나의 올은 손을 30분 동안쯤 「서-막」시장의 얼골우에 노와 주십시오. 나의 긔도로 시장은 반듯이 쾌복될 것입니다.
그박게도 별별 미신의 소리가 얼마이고 쏘다져 들어온다. 그러치만 원장은 절대로 그러한 「인찌끼」 방법을 채용함을 허락지 안엇다. 그는 물론 시장을 생각하는 친절한 마음으로부터 이엇겟지만 그러한 미신의 분위긔에서 신음하든 「서-막」시장은 결국 불귀의 객이 되고 말엇다.＜57＞
