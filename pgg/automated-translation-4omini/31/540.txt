기사형태	시 (Poem)  

The Bridge of Good Bamboo  
						Lee Yu-sook  
Could I snatch away this death while serving you?  
At the head of the bridge, would it become my soul?  
Now, with my heart, my hand reaches out.  

Though I hold onto my grudge, bleeding in the land,  
The indifferent woman’s red shawl is a burden.  
Oh bridge, do you not know, when is the time for flourishing?  

As the sunset rises, it’s the path of the shepherd.  
The remnants left behind by the loyal subjects are far and unreachable.  
Ah, do not come on purpose, for my heart's eye sees.  
-26. 9-<163>  

**Summary**  

This poem reflects deep emotions centered around loyalty and loss, connecting personal grief to a broader historical context. The bridge serves as a metaphor for the connection between the living and the dead, as well as the struggles faced by loyal subjects during tumultuous times in Korean history. Historians might find this depiction of loyalty and personal reflection particularly interesting in understanding the emotional landscape of Koreans during the colonial era, highlighting the resonance of sorrow and patriotism.

善竹橋(時調)
						李裕宿
임군을 섬기는 이 죽음을 앗기랴만
문허진 다리머리 외론 혼이 되단말가
지금히 마음잇는 손이 가삼압허 하노라

원한을 기리 품고 피흘닌 자리련만
무심한 아낙네의 빨나너는 터이로다
다리야 너는 알니라 호강철이 어느때냐

석양이 빅겨지니 목동의 길이로다.
충신의 남긴 자취 차적볼 길 아득하네.
아해야 일부러 닥지마라 마음눈이 보느니.
-26. 9-<163>
