大邱行進曲  

The hills behind are Bisham, and in front is Bagong Mountain,  

In the middle flows the waters of Geumho River.  

How bitterly do tears fall and sighs long escape,  

At night it cries like a sickle through the night.  

At the empty field of Dalku Castle in Ban-nam,  

In the dark shade lies the Doso Garden, a place to play.  

Though people come and go, it seems to be countless,  

Like an old tree on the embankment of Bangcheon.  

Even if Daegu (大邱) is so broad and abundant,  

For us, who have lost both laughter and hope,  

With a lonely body that has even lost my beloved,  

Though I wander in the fields behind, my heart is heavy.  

On an autumn night, if the stars brightly shine,  

I wish to pour sweet and lovely wine,  

And let this one night breathe without sleep,  

And be drunk until the sun rises and the moon sets. <66>  

**Summary**  

The poem "Daegu March" reflects a deep sense of longing and sadness against the backdrop of the city of Daegu. The author expresses feelings of loss and isolation in a landscape marked by natural beauty but overshadowed by personal grief. The imagery of wandering and yearning hints at broader social and emotional struggles faced during the colonial period. Historians might find interest in how such sentiments illustrate the impacts of colonial rule on personal identities and community bonds in Korea at the time.
