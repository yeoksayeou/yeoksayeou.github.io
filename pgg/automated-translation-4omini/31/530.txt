Thoughts of a Friend

Once when I fell into injustice  
My friend looked and smiled softly.  
More than the words, that smile  
How afraid I was.

As my friend showed the way,  
Holding my hands, I spoke,  
But my friend pretended not to hear.  
Without hesitation, they went their way.

My friend said they would live in Baehwa,  
Crossing the sea, they laughed and left,  
Then I received word that they were ill,  
And fetched the medicine to send.

Today, my friend came back with great ambition,  
Calling me in a loud voice,  
Knowing my state only brings me distress.  
Tears come from old memories.

Another night when the stars shine brightly,  
This dream takes me to my friend's home.  
Three family members singing the song of peace,  
Only after finishing, I turn back.

Are friends truly all like this?  
Even though I miss them, unable to see.  
I hope they might come out, 
Longing, I open the door to welcome.

<163>  

**Summary**

This poem expresses deep feelings of friendship, longing, and the complex emotions tied to separation and hope. The poet reflects on moments of distress where a friend offered support but now feels the pain of distance and illness. The mention of the friend planning to settle in a distant place and the consistent yearning highlights the human experience of connectedness amidst physical separation. From a historical perspective, this poem offers insight into the emotional landscape of individuals during the Japanese colonial period in Korea, revealing how personal relationships were impacted by broader societal changes.

잡지명	별건곤 제33호
발행일	1930년 10월 01일
기사제목	벗생각
필자	李裕宿
기사형태	시

벗생각
						李裕宿
언젠가 내가 불의에 빠젓슬 때
벗은 보시고 빙그래 웃더이다.
말드름보담 그 우슴밧고
얼마나 내가 겁내엿든지

벗이 엇길노 거르시기로
두손목 잡고 말여봣스나
벗은 안이 드른체 하시더이다.
서슴안코 엇길노 가시더이다.

벗이 배화야 사시겟다고
바다 건너서 웃고가더니
병드러 누웟다는 기별잇기로
왼갓약을 구하여 밧처드렷네

오늘날 벗이 큰 뜻을 품고 와서
깁분소리로 나를 작고 부르되
알는 몸이라 애만탐니다.
지난 생각에 눈물짐니다.

철리 머다하리 밤만 오면은
이 꿈은 벗님의 집 가고 맘니다.
세식구 불은 평화의 노래
듯고 나서야 돌아듬니다.

벗인들 아주 이즈시랴만
일매여서 자조 못뵙네
하마 나오실까봐 기대림니다.
그리워 살압열고 마중감니다.
(살압은 삽작문)-30. 1-
