기사제목	探偵小說, 凶家의 祕密(一), 原名 綠色의 門 (Detective Fiction, The Secret of the Haunted House (I), Original Title: The Green Door)  
필자	H·랜돈  
기사형태	소설 (Novel)  

On February 17th, an advertisement appeared in the personnel section of the Sentinel newspaper in New York, published by Silverstar Trent. 

"I am a researcher of true spiritual phenomena, seeking to rent a haunted house. I hope those who have one will notify me. However, if possible, I prefer houses outside the city."

Some time later, a rather modestly dressed man emerged from a vehicle near 50th Avenue and wandered around before entering the Aryan Hotel. This man was Silverstar Trent. 

He entered the office, provided his name, and took the elevator up to the sixth floor, where he pressed the bell at room 612. A rather sullen-looking man opened the door. His cheeks were sunken, his tie was neatly adjusted, and his penetrating gaze was icy cold. 

As Trent met the man's gaze, he hesitated, feeling a strange discomfort, and asked, "Are you Mr. Maltie Verfel?" The man nodded slightly as he gazed earnestly at Trent. They both entered the room. 

"Please have a seat," the host, Verfel, finally spoke. "I understand you're looking to rent a house..." Trent quietly smiled as he replied, "I wanted to consult with you about the advertisement I placed in the Sentinel." 

"Yes, that advertisement was rather unusual..." Verfel said with a cold tone that seemed devoid of any emotion. "But what exactly is it that you wish to do in such a house?" 

"Well, as mentioned in the advertisement, I want to study spiritual phenomena..." 

"So you mean the ghosts and things like that?" 

"Yes, that's how people generally refer to it. Since newspapers aren't usually read by scientifically literate individuals, I wrote the ad that way." 

Verfel's lips moved slightly, forming a thin smile. The room was richly decorated but also filled with an eerie atmosphere. Half-closed curtains let dim light filter in, and although his attire appeared tidy, it was somewhat threadbare. This suggested that while Verfel was affluent, he had peculiar tastes.

"So, you study mysterious things, hmm?" Verfel inquired. 

"Yes," Trent replied, seeming modest. "I have written several papers on the subject. If you have read those magazines, you would know my name..." 

"However, I don't read magazines at all... That said, have you received many replies regarding my advertisement?" 

"I received three responses. However, two of them seemed quite trivial, so I couldn't consider them..." 

"Why not?" Verfel asked, as if sensing some hidden meaning in what Trent was concealing. At that moment, a clear expression of inquiry rose on Trent’s face. 

"They didn't understand what I wanted to research." He appeared to study Verfel's expression, wanting to understand the effect of his explanation. Verfel seemed to take this in and nodded, "Such people are generally like that... But how do you plan to live in such a house?" 

"I plan to spend most of my time reading and writing. I need to be alone in my work, so I don't plan to employ any servants. I can manage quite well on my own in terms of living." 

"In that case, do you have any identification?" 

Trent pulled out some documents from his small wallet. Verfel examined them carefully and, looking satisfied, said, "This is fine." He seemed to soften his previous cold demeanor slightly. "People can be overly cautious, you see... Don't mind my insistence on thoroughness... I believe the house I own might suit your needs." 

It is a house that once belonged to a distant relative of mine, but I currently hold onto it. The actual owner is a woman who went abroad for treatment due to illness... This house is in Westchester, and it offers the best scenery. It was constructed sturdily. However, shortly after I purchased it, strange occurrences began at night, prompting me to move out immediately." 

"Really? Do you not know more details?" Trent asked eagerly. Verfel’s expression was again one of doubt. 

"I cannot be absolutely certain. It's hard to declare a house haunted based solely on one person's experience. The owner, Miss Arnold, is not someone who enjoys excessive speculation, so I don't have much information about her... But as far as I know, if you're looking for a haunted house, I think ‘Sedro’ would be a suitable choice." 

"Sedro?" Trent asked, almost taken aback. Verfel squinted suspiciously and continued to explain. 

"Yes, Sedro. It used to be a famous gambling house where people from New York's high society often visited for games. After it closed down, however, I heard it was reputed for its solid construction. I thought if its reputation lasted long enough, it would surely profit as a rental, so I encouraged Miss Arnold to buy it, only to find out that it is a haunted house." 

"How was it built?" 

"Mostly with stone, and there is a tall tower. Originally, the house was located on a high ground, allowing the tower to be visible for miles on clear days. The interiors were decorated in an antique style with grand doors and old French windows." 

Verfel paused, glancing at Trent. When he mentioned the tower, excitement was visibly apparent on Trent’s face, though he attempted to conceal it, while Verfel picked up on it. 

"It's a rather interesting house. However, typically in haunted houses, there are always some dark rumors associated. Does the Sedro have any?" 

"Yes, there are rumors. The owner who managed the gambling games, Mr. Arnold Ward, had issues with the police shortly before his death and was unable to continue operations. He lived there until he died." 

"What do you mean by 'died'?" 

Verfel wore a wry smile and answered enigmatically, "Well, people say he died, but no one knows whether he was actually dead or alive. A year before, he disappeared one night without a trace, and no one has heard from him since." 

"Just speculation then?" Trent asked. Verfel's icy gaze seemed to bore into Trent’s face. 

"When the police investigated, they found the house in a chaotic state. There were signs of a violent scuffle in Ward's bedroom, and a pool of dark red blood had gathered on the floor, not even dried yet. After that, they did search, but neither hide nor hair of Ward turned up, and if he was indeed murdered, the murderer must have hidden the body extremely well." 

"That is a chilling revelation... So no one has managed to solve that case? Was there any motive for Ward's murder?" 

"Ward was known as a reserved man, and while some claim he was distressed by unfortunate events, it’s hard to determine the sum of truth in that matter. After he closed the gambling house, he would sometimes take strolls in the neighboring gardens and read in his study, and once he even went on a week-long trip. The more he did this, the more taciturn he became, to the point where one old man living in the village tried speaking to him but only received a cold glare in response." 

Excitement was clear on Trent’s face as he looked at Verfel and asked, "So does Ward not have any relatives?" Verfel suddenly looked taken aback, glaring at Trent. 

"Trent! I cannot fathom why you would ask such a question!" 

"It's nothing serious; I just wondered casually. It's merely a chance remark." Trent turned his face away, seemingly displeased, and continued, "So, is the ghost that appears in Sedro ultimately Ward's ghost?" 

"You're asking about things that only you could figure out for yourself! I am not knowledgeable in matters of this sort." 

"Indeed, that's true... Thank you for sharing all these intriguing rumors with me. Now, shall you rent the house to me? How much is the rent?" 

Verfel hesitated before naming the amount. Trent retrieved cash from his pocket and handed over the payment receipt. 

"Thank you. I apologize for any inconvenience," Trent said pleasantly, tipping his hat, and quietly made his way out. At that moment, he remained unaware of the keen and watchful gaze Verfel had on him. 

After Trent left, Verfel peered out the door. Seeing no one was around, he absently tapped on his desk and mused deeply. While chewing on the extinguished end of a cigar, his expression turned bewildered as he muttered to himself, "Silverstar Trent, you're either a completely useless fool or a dangerous villain." 

**Summary**

In this detective story, Silverstar Trent, a researcher of spiritual phenomena, seeks a haunted house to conduct his studies. He meets Mr. Verfel, who tells him about a property known as Sedro, which has a murky past involving the mysterious disappearance of its last owner, Arnold Ward. This encounter reveals intriguing dynamics of fear and superstition surrounding ghost stories, particularly the notion of haunted locations reflecting past tragedies. Historians might find this narrative significant as it hints at the societal anxieties during the colonial era, illustrating how the public's imagination connected to issues of fate, wealth, and misfortune in a colonized society.

『경관들이 들아가 보니까 집안이 요란하게 어즈러젓더람니다. 와-드로-의 침실에는 격렬한 격투의 자최가 남어잇고 그리고 방바닥에는 검붉은 피가 흔근히 고여잇는데 그것이 채 말으지도 아니햇더라나요. 그뒤에 아모리 수사를 하엿스나 생사간 와-드로-가 나오지도 아니하고 하여간 와-드로-가 살해를 당햇다고 하면 살해자가 썩 교묘하게 시체를 숨겨바린 것이겟지요.』
『거참 무서운 이약이임니다... 그래 아무도 그 사건을 해결하지 못햇슴니까? 와-드로-가 살해를 당할 동긔가튼 것도 발견이 아니되엿나요?』
『와-드로-는 냉담하고 입이 묵어운 사람이엇슴니다. 누구는 그가 불행한 사건으로 번민을 하고 잇섯다고도 하나 어데까지 신용을 하여야 조흘지 모를 말이지요. 도박장을 페업한 뒤로는 집뒤에 잇는 화원으로 산보도 하고 서재에서 독서도 하고 어는 때는 멧 주일식 려행을 한 일도 잇섯슴니다. 그럴수록 그는 입이 묵어워지고 햬서 언젠가 촌에 사는 로인 하나가 말을 붓처보앗더라나요. 그러나 역시 아모 대답도 하지 아니하고 험한 얼골로 그 로인을 끄윽 바라보기만 햇다고요.』
트렌트의 얼골에는 흥분이 완연히 나타낫다. 그는 펠스의 얼골을 바라보며 이약이를 듯다가
『그러면 드와-로-에게는 일가 친척도 업섯나요?』하고 물엇다. 이 말에 펠스는 깜짝 놀내어 트렌트를 노리어 보앗다.
『트런트씨! 당신이 엇지 그런 말까지 물으시는지 나는 알 수가 업슴니다?』
『엇지가 아니라 뭣 깁흔 리유는 업슴니다. 그저 우연히 나온 말이지요.』하고 트렌트는 매우 불쾌한 듯이 화두를 돌니엇다. 『그래 세-드로-에 나오는 유령은 결국 와-드로-의 유령인가요.』
『내가 몰으는 것만 당신은 물으심니다 그려! 그런 것은 당신이 스사로 알어내서야 할 것이 아님니까? 나는 그런 데 대해서는 문외한이니까요.』
『네 그건 그럿슴니다. ...여러 가지 재미잇는 이약이를 만히 들녀주서서 대단 고맙슴니다. 그러면 집을 빌녀주십시요. 집세는 얼마나 됨니까?』
펠스는 주저하면서 금액을 말하엿다. 트랜트는 포켓속에서 지젼을 내여주고 령수증을 바덧다.
『고맙슴니다. 괴롬을 끼처서 미안함니다.』하고 트렌트는 유쾌하게 우스면서 모자를 집어들고 고요히 걸어서 밧그로 나아갓다. 그때에 그는 펜스의 자긔에게 대하여 경게와 주의를 하는 눈치를 조곰도 알지 못하엿다.
트렌트가 나간 뒤에 펜스는 밧갓을 내어다 보앗다. 그는 아모도 업는 것을 보고 나서 무심히 책상을 똑똑 두드리며 깁히 생각을 하엿다. 그는 불이 꺼진 여송연을 니끄트로 씹으면서 의혹에 가득한 표정으로  『실버스터- 트렌트군 자네는 아주 못생긴 바보가 아니면 무서운 악한일세.』하고 혼자말로 두덜거럿다.(續)<175>
