Have you had the experience of being a schoolboy in the United States?  
Yes, I have. In a country like the United States, it is virtually impossible to work as a self-funded student. If you just get in as a schoolboy, they provide you with a room and also pay you around 15 or 16 dollars a month. Then, in the morning, they might provide you with some tea, and on Saturdays, if you do half a day's work around the house, you have your freedom. It is a very convenient and simple occupation for studying.  

**Summary**  

This article features an interview with Kim Lee-shik, who describes his experience as a schoolboy in the United States during the early 20th century. He highlights the financial aid offered to students and the minimal responsibilities expected of them in exchange for accommodation and a stipend, showcasing the opportunities available in America compared to colonial Korea. Historians might find it intriguing as it reflects the perceptions and realities of study abroad at that time, as well as the aspirations of Korean youth amidst colonial rule.

金麗植

記者, 米國서 스쿨보이(school boy)되여 보신 일이 잇슴닛가?
金, 잇지요. 米國가튼 나라에서는 도저히 自費로 工夫하기는 어려우닛가요. 스쿨보이로 만 들어가면 房 한間 주고 먹여주는 外에 매달 15,6弗은 바들 수 잇스닛가요. 그러고 아침때 茶물이나 좀 끌여주고 土曜日가튼 날 半日쯤 집안 일을 해주면 自由임니다. 공부하기에는 썩 便利하고 간단한 職業이지요.
