Q&A Record (5)  

Reporter: When are you the happiest and the most unhappy as a doctor?  
Lee: The happiest moment as a doctor is when I can successfully treat a difficult illness while examining someone else’s condition. However, since doctors always deal with unfortunate patients, I often find myself in an unhappy state. Still, whether I feel good or bad each day depends on my mood or the mood of the person I’m treating, so it’s hard to pinpoint definitively when I am the most unhappy.  

<19>  

**Summary**  

In this article, Dr. Lee Deog-ya shares reflections on the emotional experiences faced by doctors, emphasizing the dual nature of joy and sorrow in their profession. He describes the satisfaction that comes from treating difficult illnesses and acknowledges the inherent sadness in dealing with patients' misfortunes. This piece offers insight into the psychological burdens of healthcare professionals during the colonial period in Korea, shedding light on how the socio-political atmosphere may have influenced medical practices and emotional well-being among doctors.

一問一答記(5)
李德耀

記者, 醫師로서 제일 愉快할 때와 제일 不快할 때가 언제임닛가.
李, 醫師로서 第一 愉快한 때는 自己가 남의 病을 보다가 難治의 病을 잘 곳치게 되는 그때임니다. 그러나 醫師는 相對者가 항상 不幸한 患者들이기 때문에 언제이나 不快할 때가 만슴니다. 그럿치만은 그것도 그날그날 自己의 氣分如何 또는 相對者의 氣分如何를 따러서 다른 것이닛가 어떤 때가 제일 不快하다고는 꾀집어 말슴하기가 어럽슴니다.
