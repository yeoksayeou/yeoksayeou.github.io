Recollections of the Chinese Revolution's Involvement in the Guangdong Incident of 1927, Miscellaneous Reports of China's Internal Turmoil  
						滬上 居人  
First,  

When one thinks of war, one cannot help but associate it with figures such as Alexander the Great, Napoleon, and Kaiser Wilhelm of modern times. However, the wars fought by these figures were conflicts between imperialism and imperialism, while the war I am discussing signifies the class struggle between imperialism and the proletarian movement. As one who participated directly in this war, I must say that regardless of its scale, war is a barbaric tragedy that humanity should engage in. However, when one enters into fierce battle with the enemy and faces in direct combat, it seems the value of existence for humanity lies in that struggle. Neglecting one's own life and prioritizing the duty of self-sacrifice is indeed the greatest obligation. At the same time, witnessing the enemy crumple under the impact of one's fired bullet can evoke a sense of satisfaction, unlike the gruesome sensation of killing insects. Now I wish to discuss the general causes and situation surrounding the Guangdong Incident, along with the motives that led me to participate directly in the front lines, sharing these insights with my esteemed readers.

The Political Situation in Guangdong at the Outbreak of the Riot  

The outbreak of the riot occurred at early dawn on December 12, 1927, around 3 or 4 o'clock. This day remains vividly etched in my memory, and the torturous scenes I witnessed still loom large. Having narrowly escaped death, I am persistently reminded of the feeling of rebirth. Now, to elaborate in order, the political situation in Guangdong at the time of the riot was exceedingly complicated, making it difficult to describe accurately. However, in general terms, Guangdong was suffering under the rule of Li Jichen, where the major warlord's governance led to the oppression of the populace, akin to a lawless land, executing dozens of laborers and farmers daily. I directly witnessed such inhuman acts of brutality. This extreme and unprecedented behavior inevitably stirred the resentment of the masses. At that time, as the Northern Expedition army, led by Zhang Fakui (a leftist of the Kuomintang), was set to return to Guangdong around October, this army was renowned in China as the "Iron Troops". Entering with the banner of success of the Northern Expedition, Zhang Fakui appeared as fierce as a tiger. Under such pressure, Li Jichen retreated, and Guangdong City essentially became the stage for Zhang’s solo performance. Meanwhile, the masses gathered in various locations, expressing that Zhang was indeed better than Li, being a leftist... Public opinion was fervent. The mentality of the populace shifted towards Zhang, creating a strong inclination to welcome him. As the sentiments of the masses shifted in such a manner, Guangdong City rapidly transformed into Zhang's bustling stage. Unable to raise his head in the rear, Li Jichen quietly pondered, feeling quite outraged at having lost power to Zhang. Thus, he reorganized his army internally while calling for recruits externally to expand his forces and prepare to suppress Zhang once more. However, the expectations of the masses did not align with reality, and Zhang, just like Li, also became regarded as a warlord. 

It became evident that Zhang would grasp any opportunity to reclaim the political power he had lost to Li and perform again as the sole actor in Guangdong's political scene. While scheming in this manner, the opportunity arose as Wang Jingwei (leader of the left faction of the Kuomintang) entered Guangdong via Peking. Taking this chance, Zhang conspired with Wang, and as they left for Nanjing, arriving in Hong Kong, the next morning around 4 o'clock, Zhang Fakui's army attacked the entire city in a coordinated cannon assault, capturing the entire city within 3 to 4 hours and gradually transferring political power to Zhang. At this time, Li Jichen, located in Hong Kong, received the telegram immediately and fled, leading the army stationed outside Guangdong City to counterattack, with Zhang’s forces departing to engage. At that moment, public sentiment was highly chaotic, and the city was in a state of anarchy. This riot directly arose during this turmoil.

Overview of the Causes of the Riot  

1927 was a year of much political upheaval in China. In that spring, the Kuomintang finally experienced a core division between the Communist and non-Communist factions, resulting in open conflict between the Wuhan government and the Nanjing government, followed by a second schism within the Wuhan government itself, stemming from a “coup” against the Communist Party. The brutal killing of dozens and even thousands of Communist Party members, led by figures such as Chiang Kai-shek and various warlords, marked an unprecedented tragedy in history. The Communist members among this persecution boldly proclaimed slogans echoing the sentiment: “The cities thriving yesterday will become wastelands today.”... They vigorously asserted that the blood spilled by our proletarian masses would be utilized as a weapon, flowing into the bourgeoisie to bring about its downfall.

In reaction to such oppression, soon thereafter in November of 1927, 11 members of the Communist Party were brutally executed in Guangdong City. Taking advantage of the ongoing conflict between Li Jichen and Zhang Fakui, Ye Ting, who had actively been working in the Soviet regime, arrived in Guangdong in December of that year. It should be noted that Zhang Fakui's organized Corps student body of 1,500 members (including over 50 Korean students) consisted entirely of Communist Party members and had repeatedly requested disarmament from Zhang. However, Zhang, who had faced them with increasing disdain, chose not to disband again, primarily due to his inability to adhere to his own preceding policies, which led him to underestimate the consequences of his greed and ultimately plunging into a major upheaval. The negotiators that Ye Ting had previously discussed with were incorporated into the Corps to execute a program for vigorous combat, rallying the main force of 1,500 students along with over 200,000 farmers rising up with nothing but sticks at dawn on December 12 of the same year, launching a massive revolt.

The Two-Day Rule of the Soviet Government  

At that time, I happened to be residing in the dormitories of Sun Yat-sen University in Guangdong with over 20 Korean comrades. Awakened by the sound of gunfire, as we rubbed our eyes, many of our comrades gathered together, exclaiming, “Ah, the uprising we have awaited!” Indeed, our time has come! As excitement rang out, Korean soldiers returning from the front lined us up in our dormitory and conveyed detailed news, bringing us greater reassurance as we opened the door. The entire city was welcoming the People's Army, with cheers shaking the heavens and gunfire echoing throughout the earth. Fighting continued, with the Armament Factory and other buildings being set ablaze, and the whole city was enveloped in flames. 

The next day, the 13th, around 10 am, I remember this date as a monumental moment, one unfading even four years later. It marked the first encounter with profound difficulties, the day we found the essence of humanity in hardship. Alongside over 20 comrades of mine at Sun Yat-sen University, we harbored deep ambitions. Departing from the warmth of our motherland, we faced mountains and thousands of miles for a purpose. Were we not here to put our ideals into action, acquiring practical experience out on the battlefield rather than engaging in mere empty discussions? If even one of us among dozens survives, wouldn't it surpass the worth of tens of theoreticians? Filled with such grand aspirations, we loaded makeshift weapons and surged toward the Public Security Office, where our farmer comrades were stationed in disarray, each unable to make a move. 

As we approached the front of the Public Security Office, many bodies lay as the aftermath of artillery bombardment. Entering, we found arms scattered everywhere. Those stationed there were all Koreans. Our contingent also joined the workforce amidst the chaos. Wherever we went, Koreans showcased astonishing adeptness; in this upheaval, all artillery technicians were Koreans, while tactics and methods employed surpassed those of the Chinese. At the Public Security Office, my group assumed tactical oversight, distributing weapons and ammunition to soldiers advancing from the front as well as arming farmers to send them into battle. When the brutal killings of landlords occurred, we apprehended fleeing landlords and executed one out of a line of ten individuals, taking the corpses outside. After executing over ten of them, the final remaining body was carried out by the fetching perpetrators. Witnessing such harsh realities, the immediate feeling among us was of an inexplicable satisfaction instead of pity for the victims. The moment these landlords fell, feelings emerged; ah, until such vermin are eradicated, paradise on this earth remains unattainable.

After these executions, as if affirming the remark of fatalism, a sudden upheaval swept in. Alerts came in informing us of the advancing White Army into the city, and cannon sounds reverberated through the Public Security Office, as bullets rained down continuously. At this chaos, every man scrambled to find a safe hiding place, transforming the previous resolute spirits into a desperate scramble for survival. At this moment, our soldiers began vacating toward the frontline, leaving only the poorly-trained among us and farmers behind in a situation where death loomed closer. 

As the evening sun lowered, messages of reassurance spread among our comrades, revitalizing us from the hellish nightmare we had confronted. Tonight could have been our homeward journey unless the danger moderated. Torn between hope and despair, we shuttled through perilous artillery fire and finally made it to the university dorms safe and sound.

The next day, the entire city turned into a battleground dominated by the White Army. The common soldiers were utterly in the dark about such developments until dawn. On the previous day, those of us who had gathered there were confronted by a turbulent scene of surprise. The moment we peeked out, fear gripped us deeply, but despite being foreign nationals, it turned out we were not immediately targeted for execution as the Koreans within continued to resonate amid gatherings at the university. 

Yet, enduring two to three days felt akin to enduring decades of hardship. As decrees of extermination were ordered during these days, the fleeting facade of Soviet governance began fragmenting, leading to a complete break of the military warlords in the end.

The Cause of the Communists' Defeat  

1. Zhang Fakui and Li Jichen engaged in fierce battle, receiving news of the uprising and compromising sporadically during transit.  

2. There was a lack of mobilization as apart from the 1,500 student Corps, most were untrained farmers.  

Scenes of Devastation after the Defeat of the Communists  

The brutal soldiers of the warlords roamed Guangdong City, mercilessly slaughtering powerless farmers and laborers. Even small-time merchants, acting as minions for the warlords, took up stones and clubs, indiscriminately beating any they encountered to death. Many were captured and executed in large numbers, their bodies piled up everywhere in the streets. Among these bloodied corpses, limbs lay contorted, evoking the profound sorrow of this ruthless play—seemingly moving even the heavens to tears. Estimates suggest that during this event, around 5,000 to 6,000 were killed (including over 20 Koreans).  

**Summary**  

The article details the author's recollections of the 1927 Guangdong Incident amidst the Chinese Revolution, highlighting the complex political climate, the various factions involved, and the brutal consequences of the uprising. It sheds light on the sense of urgency among Korean comrades who saw this turmoil as a chance to join in the revolutionary efforts. Historians might find notable the implications of Korean participation in the Chinese revolutionary movement, indicating a moment of solidarity against imperialism, and how deeply intertwined Korean and Chinese revolutionary histories are at this time.

						滬上 居人
先言

戰爭이라 하면 옛날 알렉산더와 近世에 나폴레옹이며 崔近世에 카이제루를 聯想하지 안흘 수 업다. 그러나 그들의 戰爭은 帝國主義 對 帝國主義 戰爭이거니와 筆者가 말코저하는 戰爭은 帝國主義 對 無産運動의 階級 戰爭을 意味한다. 筆者도 直接 이 戰爭에 參戰하야 보왓거니와 戰爭은 大小 莫論하고 人類는 하지 못할 動物的 惡戱라고 밧게 할 말이 업다. 그러나 敵軍과 接戰하야 激戰할 때에는 人類의 存在 價値는 鬪爭에 잇는 것갓다. 自己의 生命存亡을 不顧하고 도리혀 自己의 命을 밧치는 것이 最大 義務인 것갓다. 同時에 自己가 放射한 彈丸에 敵軍이 마자 꺽구러지는 것을 볼 때에 昆蟲을 죽이는 것은 醜態의 感을 일으키거니와 敵軍이 꺽구러질 때에는 도리혀 愉快感을 일으키게 된다. 이제 몬저 廣東 事變의 이 일어난 大體의 原因과 戰況을 들어 말한 後에 筆者가 直接 戰線에 參戰한 動機와 그 瞬間에 늣겨진 바를 讀者 諸兄에 公開코저 한다.

暴動勃發 當時의 廣東 政局

이 暴動이 勃發한 해는 距今 4年 前 1927年 12月12日 새벽 3,4時 頃이엿다. 이날은 아직까지 筆者의 머리의 記憶에서 사러지지 안코 그 지긋지긋한 光景이 눈압헤 어른어른할 때에 九死一生으로 사라난 나는 항상 再生의 感이 이르켜진다. 이제 順次로 이야기하면 暴動 當時의 廣東 政局은 複雜多端하야 무엇이라고 形言키 어려웟다. 그러나 大體로 말하자면 그때 廣東은 李濟琛의 統治 下에서 신음하고 잇섯는데 그 大軍閥인 李氏의 統治 下에 民衆의 抑壓은 無人之境과 가타서 農工人을 매일 數十名式 銃殺을 식히엿다. 그 光景은 筆者도 直接目見한 바이다. 이런 暴惡無雙한 非人間的 行爲야말노 民衆의 反感을 必然으로 이르키게 되엿다. 이때에 맛츰 北伐을 나가던 張發奎(國民黨 左派)가 10月頃에 軍隊를 引率하고 廣東으로 돌어오게 되니 이 軍隊는 中國에서 鐵兵이라고 別名을 듯는 强兵이다. 北伐에 成功의 깃발을 날니고 들어오는 張發奎의 威風이야말노 猛虎와 가텃다. 그 威風에 허덕이는 李濟琛은 뒤방으로 들어가고 廣東 市街는 張氏의 獨舞臺가 된 感이 잇섯다. 그 中間에 잇는 民衆들은 여긔저긔 모와서서 입안으로 하는 말이 그래도 張놈이 李놈보다 낫겟지. 左系니까... 여긔저긔서 輿論이 沸騰하엿다. 그때에 民衆의 心理는 張氏에게로 올마가서 張氏를 歡迎하는 氣分이 濃厚하엿다. 民衆의 心理가 이럿케 돌아가게 되니 暫時間에 廣東 市街는 張氏의 活舞臺가 되고 말앗다. 뒤방에서 머리를 들지 못하던 李氏는 가만히 안저 생각하야 본즉 張氏에게 政權을 빼앗긴 것이 퍽이나 憤하엿다. 그래서 內로 自己의 軍隊를 整頓하고 外로 徵兵을 募集하야 自己의 勢力을 擴張하면서 다시 張氏를 抑壓하려 하엿다. 그때에 民衆들은 自己들이 企待하던 생각과는 全然 딴길노 들어가고 張氏도 亦是 李氏와 가튼<150> 軍閥로 取扱을 밧게 되엿다. 그러고보니 張氏의 威信은 李氏의 抑壓에 다시 餘地업시 地下로 떠러지고 말앗다.
그러나 張은 엇던 機會만 잇스면 도로 李氏의 政權을 奪回하야 가지고 自己가 廣東 政局에 獨舞臺의 俳優로 出演코저 하엿다. 틈을 탈녀고 東走西走하던 판에 맛츰 汪兆銘(國民黨 左派의 首領)이 北平을 걸처 廣東으로 들어오게 되엿다. 틈을 탄 張氏는 그 翌日부터 汪氏와 前後事를 陰謀하야 놋코 汪氏가 李氏를 꾀여가지고 3人이 作伴하야 南京으로 가는 途中 香港에 到着되자 그 翌日 새벽 4時頃에 張發奎 軍隊가 全 市街를 一齊히 砲圍 攻擊하야 3,4時間內에 全市를 다 占領하고 政權은 張氏의 掌中에 漸間 轉嫁하게 되엿다. 이 때에 香港에 잇는 李濟琛은 그 電報를 接하고는 卽時로 떠나 廣東 市外에 住頓하야 잇는 軍隊를 引率하고 廣東市를 攻擊하야 들어오게 되니 張氏는 그것을 追擊하기 爲하야 市內에 잇는 左鄕 軍人 數千名과 自己 經營하는 敎導團(軍官 學校 學生) 1,500名을 남기고는 全軍을 統率하고 戰線으로 나갓다. 이 때에 人心은 몹시 一騷亂하고 市街는 無政府 狀態이엿다. 이틈을 타서 일어난 것이 廣東 事變에 直接 動機라 하겟다.

暴動勃發의 大體原因

1927年은 中國 政局의 多事秋이엿다. 同年 봄 國民黨은 드듸여 共産黨 非共産黨으로 난호어 武漢政府와 南京政府가 對立케 되엿고 또 그담에는 武漢政府 自身의 共産黨에 對한 『쿠데타』로 因하야 第2次 分解作用이 일어낫스니 國民黨 中央執行委員의 除名處分 等 蔣介石 以下 여러 軍閥들이 共産黨員 數십명 乃至 수천명 을 每日 虐殺식힌 事實은  前古未曾有의 慘劇이라 하겟다. 이 剝害를 當한 共産黨員들은 羅馬格言에 『昨日에 繁榮하던 都市는 今日에 荒蕪地가 되리로다.』... ... 標語를 압세우고 우리 無産 大衆이 흘닌 피는 血色업시 搾取 當한 無産 大衆의 血脉으로 흘너가서 부르조아지의 沒落을 招來하는 武器가 되여지라고 힘잇게 웨첫다.
그런한 反動으로 얼마 안이되야 廣東市에서 1927年 11月 頃에 共産黨員 11名을 銃殺한 事件을 아울너 李濟琛과 張發奎 軍閥이 싸우는 틈을 타서 海量陸量 方面에 쇼베트政府를 建設하고 活躍하던 葉挺이 同年 12月에 廣東으로 들어오게 되엿다. 여기에 몬저 말하야 둘 것은 張發奎가 經營하는 敎導團 學生 1,500名(內에 朝鮮人 學生 50餘名)은 全部 共産黨員이엿슴으로 張氏에게 武裝 解除를 여러 번 當하엿스나  張氏가 다시 武裝을 식히고 解散을 식히지 안한 原因은 自己의 政策上 할 수 업시 그냥 두고 내려왓던 것이엿는데 맛치 뒤를 잡으려다가 독을 깨친 格으로 自己의 野慾을 채우려다가 結局에는 大逢變을 當하게 된 것이다. 임의 約束하야 두엇던 葉挺은 바로 敎導團에 들어가서 激戰할 프로그람을 짜가지고 主力 部隊인 學生1,500과 赤手空拳으로 다못 棍棒만 들고 蹶起한 農工人 20餘萬名을 引率하고 同年 同月 12日 새벽 3,4時 頃에 靑天霹靂과 가티 大反亂을 일으켯다.

쇼베트政府의 二日天下

이 때에 筆者는 바로 廣東國立 中山大學 內 寄宿舍에 朝鮮 同志 20餘名과 함께 잇슬 때엿다. 銃소리에 놀내 깨여 일어나니 발서 여러 同侔들은 눈을 비비며 한 곳으로 몰니여 오-라. 近日에 暴動이 일어나겟다더니 今日이야 爆發되엿구나. 아- 우리의 世上이 왓구나. 여러 同侔들은 좃타고 날뛰는 판에 戰線에 나갓던 朝鮮 軍人이 우리들을 차저 기숙사에<151>
