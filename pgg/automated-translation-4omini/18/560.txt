잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1929년 04월 01일  
기사제목	離婚한 男女가 다시 만나 結婚 - 監獄에서 離婚한 夫婦 外國서 맛나 再次 結婚 (Divorced Man and Woman Meet Again and Marry - A Former Couple Divorced in Prison Remarry in a Foreign Country)  
필자	方仁根  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

My friend K conducted a thorough love marriage, even amidst the culture of love marriages at the time. His partner S was a girl studying in the third year of high school. K was attending a vocational school in the second year. Usually, if a girl is somewhat clever and attractive, she will get engaged before graduating high school, and S was also pursued by many boys in her third year, resulting in a fiercely competitive selection; however, K emerged victorious. S was quite gentle and kind, while K was cheerful and spirited. They got engaged and decided to marry.

Even knowing S was engaged, mischievous and devilish young men tried hard to steal her away from K. Knowing this, K impulsively married her, realizing the danger of the situation. They were both too loving and weak-hearted to wait until graduation. 

The time after their marriage was genuinely sweet. "Let's laugh together, oh flowers, let's sing together, oh birds," they continued their happy life. A few friends of ours were immensely envious of their household and found it quite fun to visit them. 

K graduated from vocational school and began working in a company. Finally, with a steady income, S started decorating their home little by little. Aesthetically and artistically, when anyone visited, it was as refreshing as a clean breeze. During the times when her husband was away at work, S immersed herself in household chores, preparing delicious dishes, cleaning the rooms meticulously, and spending every day in anticipation of her husband’s return. 

If life continued like this, it would have been great, but a terrible storm approached them. One day, while waiting for her husband, S found that he did not come home even after it grew late into the night. Worrying, she repeatedly checked the dining table and re-heated the stew several times. Even after midnight, he still had not come. It was the first time after their marriage that she felt such intense worry, fear, and suspicion, curling up alone in the house with her heart burning. On the previous day, when she checked with the company over the phone, they mentioned he had left and had not returned. 

S felt as if the sky was about to break. She returned home, crying, when suddenly K’s friend came to deliver some news carelessly. 

It turned out that K was caught during a secret society movement, leading to his arrest along with his comrades. S wept and trembled. Afterward, K received a sentence of five years and eight months and began his life in prison. S was left alone without her parents or relatives. More than missing her husband, the immediate difficulties of survival pressed heavily upon her. 

Experiencing such a bitter reality for the first time was excruciating for the delicate S. One by one, she had to hock her belongings and eventually ended up renting a small room, struggling desperately to find work. While trying to find employment, no one was willing to help her. S could only sigh and focus all her energy on barely scraping by. The world seemed exceedingly lonely and devoid of fun. 

Unfortunately, an unscrupulous person, a vagrant, discovered her situation and began to take advantage of her. Initially, S did not even consider him as a viable option, but gradually, she succumbed to his persistent advances. Eventually, she became his mistress. S’s destruction was due not only to society's faults but also to the faults of us friends of K. Of course, S also had her part of culpability. Nonetheless, S endured a luxurious life while suffering internally. K always haunted her mind and heart, although in time, that began to fade, and the lavish pleasures dimmed her vision and dulled her senses. 

At this stage, she decided to live with this man forever, thinking that by doing so, she'd tie up her relationship with him completely. The man, meanwhile, threatened that if S divorced her legitimate husband, he would divorce too, leading to S filing for divorce three years after K was imprisoned. 

K, who was in prison, could not legally agree to the divorce petition, and consequently, S divorced him. K, consumed by rage, felt the overwhelming urge to kill S when he found out. Everything felt lost, and eventually, he thought of dying. However, he resolved to completely separate himself from society because of a woman! After this resolution, he was granted a reduction in his sentence, and after five years in prison, he was released into a world where he longed for and loathed Korea at the same time. 

Wandering into freedom, he was deeply disappointed as to where he could return on the first night after his release. Meeting with friends who picked him up from prison, he was grateful yet lost. K learned of S’s excitement upon hearing he had been released, and while she felt frightened, she also felt overjoyed. Tears streamed down her face, which she had not noticed before. K felt deeply pitying for her and realized the extent of his own guilt. He missed her and ached for their time together. 

After contemplating various scenarios day and night, he suggested to her to escape to a distant land. But S only responded with distress. S tracked down K's whereabouts and started visiting him late at night. When S entered, she only cried without being able to speak. Faced with anger and frustration over their situation, K thought about hitting or even killing her, but no, he couldn’t bring himself to act that way. 

He resolved to show indifference, although his heart would burst with sorrow. “Go away, dirty woman!” he would lament fiercely inside, and yet, when faced with S's sorrowful, tear-stained face, the urge to forgive her became stronger. 

After a while, S stopped visiting altogether, and K, while feeling relieved, wondered and longed for her. Months later, he saw S by chance on a back street of Jongno, but she looked entirely different; her clothing was different, and her appearance seemed in a hurry. 

K asked her curiously what had happened. S responded that she had left that man, discarded her mistress lifestyle, and decided to work alone as a woman worker in a factory, building a new life for herself. 

She solemnly conveyed her determination to turn over a new leaf, burying all past commitments and living as a honest citizen. K felt moved by this, yet S showed no willingness to suggest living together or rekindle their past. This left a deep pain in K's heart and evoked feelings of admiration within him. Ultimately, they parted ways that day. 

Neither had noted each other's addresses and there was no means to meet again. Yet, a shadow trailed closely behind K as he retraced his steps back. “I must take care of S! Rebuild our previous life together to bring back joy,” he thought. 

If a weak-hearted woman could undergo such change, it wouldn't be unforgivable—there is guilt within me and society is guilty too. Moreover, S had repented of everything and had become a new person. With this in mind, K’s strange pride and stubbornness would not permit him to return to his previous life. He departed for another place to find work. 

As he boarded the train at Nowon Station, his heart ached while crossing the Aeo-gye-gully, longing to return. “I will end everything! I will break through all barriers with my steel resolve and start anew tomorrow,” he vowed to himself, gritting his teeth and affirmatively closing his eyes to abandon all thoughts of S. 

After some time, an acquaintance approached K with bright smiles. It was S, looking strikingly cheerful. “I came to follow you and work with you,” she told him. 

“Where did you learn this?” K asked.

“Oh, I’ve been on your trail this whole time, carefully watching over you!” 

They embraced each other. 

They went to a faraway land together. Indeed, they had missed their honeymoon due to their earlier troubles but now they had a proper wedding celebration abroad, among the company of friends who had only heard rumors of their remarrying. Afterward, it was said that they lived joyfully as a family, even welcoming a son, and they were working successfully in their roles there. For a while, their updates ceased.

Though this may be a story, it is based on true events. If they are still in Korea, I trust they will not take offense at being mentioned in this article, as it was written at the request of “Byeolgeongon” to recount a tale of those who divorced and rekindled their relationship. I would have liked to write with more detail.

I hope to meet you both again, as the light shines brightly for you now. 

**Summary**

This narrative recounts the tumultuous love story between K and S, who marry young, face separation due to imprisonment, and later divorce during K's incarceration. After a series of struggles and external influences lead S into a troubling lifestyle, they surprisingly reunite with changed perspectives and ambitions. The article highlights the societal pressures and the impact of tumultuous historical contexts on personal relationships during Korea's colonization, illuminating the profound struggles individuals faced during this era.

The fascinating element for historians lies in the portrayal of the struggles under colonial society, emphasizing early feminist themes, personal resilience, and the consequences of the socio-political environment. The shifting dynamics between former spouses show how personal and socio-political narratives intertwine during Korea's complicated historical timeline.
