Submission Welcome

If you write and send in local anecdotes, unique emotions, and customs or general news that are specific to your area, we will revise and edit the text for publication.

We welcome postcards and communications, and you may send in your sarcastic feelings or discoveries about local happenings—we will publish them verbatim (as is, on a postcard).

We also welcome both original folk songs and adaptations of existing ones. We are open to publishing any original folk songs or those where only the words have been changed from traditional folk songs.

There is no deadline; feel free to send your contributions at any time for inclusion in next month's issue. <36>

**Summary**

This article invites readers to submit local anecdotes, personal feelings, and folk songs for publication, emphasizing regional uniqueness and creativity. It reflects the cultural engagement of the magazine at the time, providing a platform for voices from various demographics under colonial rule. Historians may find the encouragement of local expression particularly interesting, as it suggests a subtle resistance to assimilation and a drive to celebrate local culture amidst the pressures of colonialism.
