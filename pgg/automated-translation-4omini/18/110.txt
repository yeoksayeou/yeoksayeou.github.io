Exciting! Great Excitement! General Kwon Ryu's Victory at Hwangju - Seonjo Year of Gyeesi, March 13  
Li Yunjae  
(......the rest of the 22 lines omitted from the original text......)  
The battle of Hwangju began on February 12 of the Gyeesi year (26th year of the Seonjo reign), ten months after the start of the Imjin War. On this day, Kwon Ryu, who was the special envoy of Gwangju, had returned to his hometown after suffering a tremendous defeat at Yongin the previous year. He concentrated on training over a thousand young men from his territory and nearby towns. Because of this achievement in defeating the Japanese army coming from Gyeongju to Jeonju at Iji, he was promoted to governor of Jeolla. By this time, thanks to this victory, Hunan was able to avoid the Japanese army’s devastation.

Upon receiving this imperial order, Ryu went to Jeonju to organize military discipline. As recovering the capital was a matter of urgency, he led an army of 20,000 troops along with monks under his command and made his way north to support the king. Reaching Suwon, they took a stronghold at Todeungsan and defeated the Japanese troops, then advanced across the Han River to establish a camp at Hwangju Mountain Fortress, which is about 30 ri from the capital.

At this time, the Japanese forces were gathering around the capital in hopes of reinforcements from Ming troops, making the situation extremely severe. In the early hours of the 12th, tens of thousands of Japanese troops came and surrounded the mountain fortress. Ryu issued commands to the troops to remain calm and prepared as the Japanese launched an attack from three directions, making the confrontation extremely dire. Ryu himself stepped forward, overseeing the battle amidst a torrential rain of arrows and the booming of cannon fire, staging a fierce battle.

As the Japanese soldiers set fire to the grass to create smoke, intending to breach the fortress, the defenders responded by dousing the flames with water. When the Japanese breached the northwest corner of the fortress, some monks began to retreat. Ryu personally executed a few retreating soldiers to rally the others; he encouraged the generals, leading to a significant boost in morale. Shortly after, the arrows were nearly exhausted, preventing further combat, but then a water supplier named Li Pin sent several tens of thousands of arrows on boats, allowing them to continue fighting without setbacks. Thus, from sunrise to sunset, fierce fighting continued, and the Japanese army suffered a great defeat; dead bodies filled the mountains, and blood flowed like streams.

As the Japanese troops began to flee, Ryu led over ten thousand men to pursue them, with corpses lining the road, and the remnants of the enemy crying as they retreated. They captured a total of 727 items, including the enemies' bows, arrows, armor, swords, and rifles. They gathered the corpses into four heaps, gathered firewood to burn them, and the smell of the burning flesh wafted for ten ri.

Ryu then moved his army to Imjin and camped at Paju Mountain Fortress. When the Japanese commanders were shocked by the news of their defeat at Hwangju, they hurriedly sent their forces led by Ko Chou Seijō with 40,000 troops to retaliate. However, Ryu's forces had already moved to Paju, and they burnt the mountain fortress and returned.

This victory at Hwangju was the most glorious naval battle amidst the Imjin War. The day after this victory, Ming General Cha Dae-soo came to see the battlefront and was astonished, saying, "Indeed, there are true generals in Korea." Ming Emperor, upon hearing the news of this victory, remarked, "Given the strength of Korea, they are indeed a robust nation," and noted Ryu's achievements, indicating that the Korean people would surely become invigorated. The commander of the reinforcements, Li Yu-song, who had fled far away to Pyongan after suffering a defeat at Boteguan, regretted his haste whilst hearing the news of this victory and quickly returned to Kaesong to plot anew.

    

**Summary**

The article details the significant victory at Hwangju by General Kwon Ryu during the Imjin War, showcasing the resilience of the Korean forces against the Japanese invaders. This battle not only marked a pivotal moment in the conflict but also represented a rare instance of successful resistance during a time of colonial oppression. Historians might find the dynamics of leadership, martial spirit, and strategies employed by Korean forces particularly interesting as they reflect both the challenges faced under colonial rule and the capacity for mobilization and resistance.

痛快! 大痛快! 權慄都元帥 幸州大捷 - 宣祖 癸巳 三月 十三日
						李允宰
(......이상 22줄 삭제-원문......)
幸州役은 任亂이 시작된 지 10개월을 지난 癸巳年(宣祖 26년) 2월 12일이다. (陽 3월 13일)이 役의 主人公인 權慄은 光州特使로 잇스면서 지난 해에 龍仁에서 慘敗를 보고 자긔 고향에 돌아와 境內와 近邑의 子弟 千餘人을 어더서 훈련하기에 열중하더니 그후에 錦山으로부터 全州로 들어오는 日兵을 梨峙에 싸우어 물러치엇다는 功으로써 全羅監司로 陞進되엇다. 이때만 해도 이 勝捷으로 말미암아 湖南은 오로지 敵의 遍蹂를 면하게 된 것이다. 慄이 이 恩命을 밧고 全州에 가서 軍紀를 整頓하고 하로 밧비 京城을 收復함이 가장 急務라하여 군사 二萬과 僧將處英을 거느리고 곳 北上하여 勤王에 힘썻다. 水原에 이르러 禿城山城을 웅거하여 日兵을 破하고 다시 전진하여 漢江을 건너 高陽郡 幸州山城(京城을 距하기 30里)에 陳첫다. 이때는 敵의 諸路軍이 明軍의 來援에 둘려서 바양흐로 京城으로 集注하는 판이매 그 形勢가 자못 큰지라. 12일 새벽에 日兵 數萬騎가 와서 山城을 포위하엿다. 慄이 軍中에 令을 내리어 鎭靜히 待하라 하더니 日兵이 三路로 난우어 來攻하여 형세가 심히 逼切한지라 慄이 陳頭에 나서서 督戰하니 矢石이 비오듯 하고 砲聲이  천지를 움즉이어 一大激戰을 演하엿다. 日兵이 束草에 불을 붓치어 바람껼에 노아서 城柵을 살오려거늘 城中이 물을 뿌리어 이를 구하고 日兵이 城柵의 西北隅를 헐어버리니 僧軍이 조곰 퇴각하거늘 慄이 당장에 퇴각한 자 數人을 親斬하고 諸將을 督하니 士氣가 大振이라. 조곰 뒤에 화살이 다하여<17> 더 싸울수 업게 되더니 水使李蘋이 화살 數萬을 배에 실어 보내어 왓슴으로 조곰도 挫折되는 일이 업시 더욱 奮戰하엿다. 이와 가티 日出로부터 日沒까지 彼我力戰하여 드듸어 日兵이 大敗하니 死屍가 산에 가득하고 流血이 내를 일우엇다. 日兵이 潰走할 새 慄이 萬餘人을 거느리고 日兵의 뒤를 추격하니 伏屍가 길에 싸히고 殘兵은 통곡하고 돌아간지라 . 적의 弓箭盔甲刀銃倭器 등을 奪獲한 것이 모도 7백 27件이요 시체를 싸하 四堆로 난우고 柴草를 모아서 불에 살오니 타는 냄새가 十里박게까지 밋치엇다. 慄이 군사를 臨津으로 옴기고 坡州山城에 陳쳣더니 日陳이 幸州의 敗報를 듯고 크게 놀라서 이를 復讎하려고 行長淸正 등이 四萬兵을 거느리고 급히 달아 幸州로 들어온즉 慄의 군사는 이미 坡州로 옴기어간지라 山城을 燒火하고 돌아갓다.
이 幸州大捷은 任亂중에 陸戰으로 가장 壯烈한 것이다. 이 戰捷이 잇은 翌日에 明將査大受가 와서 실지로 戰蹟을 보고 놀래어 말하기를 朝鮮에 과연 眞將軍이 잇도다 하엿스며 明帝는 이 捷報를 듯고 말하기를 朝鮮을 본대 强國이라 일컬엇더니 이제 權慄의 斬獲한 功을 보니 朝鮮人民은 오히려 가히 振作하겟도다 하엿스며 碧蹄館에서 敗하여 혼이 나서 멀리 平壤으로 뛰엇던 援將 李如松도 이 捷報를 듯고 嚮時에 넘우 급거히 回軍하엿슴을 후회하고 빨리 開城으로 돌아와 再圖를 꾀하는 일까지 잇섯다.
