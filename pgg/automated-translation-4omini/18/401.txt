**Summary**

This article discusses Choi Su-won as a significant figure in the development of the Philosophy of Humanity (人乃天主義). It reflects on his contributions and thoughts regarding the ideology during a time when Korea was under Japanese colonial rule. Historians might find this particularly interesting as it highlights the emergence of new philosophical movements that sought to define Korean identity during the colonial period, reflecting resistance against cultural oppression.
