Discussion on the Issue of School Trips  
Is it something to think about until abolition?

Jeongsin Women's School Cheon Yun-sik

"There is a growing tendency that ‘if other schools are going on a trip, we must go too.’ Just because other schools are going to a certain place, we feel compelled to go there as well! This seems to be the tendency in recent years. It seems to me that it is a major mistake for normal girls' schools to follow boys' schools and commercial schools to travel to places like Andong County or Southern Manchuria. I think it is necessary that girls’ schools be sent to places that suit their circumstances, such as Gyeongju or Buyeo.

Advantages and Disadvantages

Principal of Gyeongsin School Yun Ye-bin

I do not wish to state whether school trips are good or bad outright, because school trips have both pros and cons. To put it simply, thinking only of the benefits can be harmful; likewise, considering only the drawbacks does not allow us to view them as entirely negative. Although some may argue that it is a waste of money and time, it is also undeniable that experiencing what we learn in history or geography firsthand is invaluable. Therefore, I do not have a definitive opinion on whether school trips are beneficial or not. If one thinks of the costs and time as insignificant, then school trips may be worthwhile; however, if one deems those costs to place a heavy burden, they become unnecessary.

However, it is crucial to consider the circumstances and era. Advocating for a trip while unable to afford textbooks or tuition fees is hardly a sound argument. It is not a reasonable proposition to spend lavishly on school fees while then refusing to pay for a school trip. If a school trip can be executed with an appropriate budget and has a considerable impact on students, it should undoubtedly be favored. However, the execution remains complicated in practice.

I would recommend to seniors of my school and general students this year that during winter or summer breaks, they consider organizing a group to hike to nearby areas. Hiking has many benefits; it is good for physical health, costs less, and allows for ample time to observe closely.

This has been my hope for many years. I have not been able to realize this due to various circumstances, but I hope to be able to implement it for students at our school this year, with staff members leading the initiative.

Threefold Benefits

Principal of Whimoon High School Lee Yun-joo

In my personal view, I would vigorously advocate for school trips as a commendable undertaking. Some people who oppose or criticize school trips primarily raise concerns about the cost. Given that Korean people's living conditions are deteriorating, how can school authorities demand a forced fee of about 10 won per student annually for such trips? Not everyone agrees that trips yield noticeable benefits, while some cynically suggest they are about spending money on food, snacks, and transportation.

My reasons for advocating school trips revolve around several key points. 

First, they provide students with a means to directly observe various subjects learned at school, which can lead to unquantifiable educational benefits. For example, if southern students travel to the northern regions to view the natural beauty of Pyongyang or observe different customs and historical sites that date back thousands of years, what harm is there in that? We should not view the monetary aspect of spending a little money as an impediment.

Second, school trips allow for informal bonding between students and teachers. In regular circumstances, there exists a strict separation between teachers and students, leaving little opportunity for genuine friendship. However, during school trips, because of the shared space and meals, the distance between student and teacher naturally lessens, promoting camaraderie. This is viewed as a beneficial aspect within today’s educational environment.

Thirdly, students in Korea often reside in cramped conditions where they experience poor air quality and mental strain as a result. Hence, traveling outdoors to experience the vastness of nature has significant physical and mental health benefits. For example, viewing the beautiful mountains and expansive fields can invigorate the spirit, awakening feelings of courage and clarity.

Therefore, I hope that at least once a week we can incorporate outings under the guidance of teachers within school hours, allowing students to nurture both body and mind.

In any case, I firmly believe that school trips are a necessity despite the costs involved.

Unnecessary Travel is Not Required

Central School Choi Jin-soon

School trips are an essential extracurricular item. Experiencing the various things learned in class firsthand, especially in history, geography, and natural sciences, is invaluable. The saying "seeing is believing" has existed since ancient times, and seeing real examples of what has been taught in a classroom leads to profound astonishment and much more meaningful learning.

However, it appears that various schools undertake school trips as an annual tradition. If such trips are merely a frivolous excursion similar to a group of country folk visiting Seoul for sightseeing, then I think such behavior deserves criticism.

Before school trips, preparation is vital. For instance, when visiting historical sites, there must be preparatory education about those histories. If the destination is of geographical significance, prior education must also be provided. Failing to prepare adequately for a trip, with no clear plan or objective, results in wastage of time and resources.

Currently, most school trips lack sufficient preparatory education prior to the trip. Most of these trips seem to amount to abandoning the day’s class without a meaningful educational outcome, a belief I hold firmly.

Another significant benefit of school trips is the opportunity it affords for fostering camaraderie among students and teachers. 

Unlike the past, the present-day school operates as a formal structure, where only children from within the same village come together for study. Even in schools located in the capital, students come from diverse regions throughout the country. Thus, while students may be together in the same classroom, they remain distant acquaintances. Moreover, the relationship between students and teachers often resembles that of parents and children, full of affectionate bonds that for some reason fail to develop; it is regretful. School trips provide opportunities to break down these barriers.

Critics of school trips often highlight the financial aspect. This approach seems entirely foolish. The yearly cost of a trip averages around 10 won per student; spending that represents a significant financial burden for families with limited means. To assert that merely averting this small expenditure will alleviate financial strain is unreasonable. 

In any case, I firmly maintain that school trips must be acknowledged as essential for students in terms of physical education and psychological growth, regardless of the costs.

More Meaningful Trips

Principal of Central High School Choi Do-seon

Until a few years ago, the question of whether to continue school trips did not arise. However, in recent years, it has increasingly become a topic of contention among both school authorities and students; public sentiment regarding this matter is also strong. Our school was unable to undertake school trips two years ago and was halted again last year. This should not be taken as outright denial of the need for school trips. The decision last year was primarily due to the issue of organizing classwork alongside internal school management. 

Moreover, given the severe droughts in South Korea and floods in North Korea, how can we justify spending precious resources during such dire hardship, even on school trips? 

For these reasons, both last year and this year have seen the cancellation of such trips across all grades, only allowing for some local outings this spring.

My personal stance on the necessity of school trips cannot be presented without the broader consensus of the institution. Even if the school decides that school trips are absolutely necessary, we must strive for more meaningful and productive experiences than previously provided. If a complete cancellation becomes necessary, we will thoroughly communicate to both parents and students to ensure mutual understanding.

Our school has even engaged in complete cancellation

Principal of Baejae High School Ah Ban Seolra

Rather than discussing theories or ideals concerning school trips, I will outline what has been practically implemented at our school. 

We have not considered school trips necessary for the past two years, leading to a complete halt of such activities. Suddenly suspending an annual event left students feeling disappointed, and our administration faced criticism for taking such extreme measures. After careful deliberation, we decided that only the fifth-year graduating students would go on a single trip each year. Thus, we have been pleased with the outcomes we have observed. 

This is not limited to the students, but relates to the overall state of Korean society. Financially, Koreans endure significant hardships. Many families find it nearly impossible to send their children to urban centers for education. I believe it is crucial for educational authorities to refrain from imposing excessive burdens on families already facing economic difficulties.

Therefore, our school has designated a budget of 3 won for annual travel expenses. For fifth-year students, upon accumulating 15 won, they are taken to the beautiful Geumgangsan Mountain, a site worthy of global pride. 

To visit a place like Geumgangsan independently, one would require at least 100 won. Moreover, the terrain there is steep; without proper preparation, one cannot fully appreciate the vastness of the mountain.

In this regard, I believe we are making a significant contribution to the personal development of our students. 

Although school trips are often lauded as educational activities, it is not uncommon for students to engage in behaviors that may harm the school's reputation during these excursions. The time and resources spent on school trips often do not yield crucial educational outcomes, thus leading me to propose that we should limit such trips to a minimal capacity.

The Increasing Lavishness of School Trips

Kim Palbong

Traveling during spring and autumn has become a standard annual event in schools. Is this beneficial or harmful? I would say it can be both good and bad.

First, school trips can be advantageous because they provide opportunities to expand knowledge of geography, history, and customs, thus facilitating practical learning. In addition, these trips serve as a temporary refreshment from the routine of school life, which can positively impact health.

Conversely, school trips can also have drawbacks as they provide students excessive opportunities to spend money freely and perhaps foster a desire for casual enjoyment rather than tangible learning. Another issue is that many families may be struggling financially, meaning that often students are pressured to scrape together funds to cover travel costs. Thus, the affordability of school trips can lead to a situation where only students from wealthier families can participate, rendering these trips a form of luxury that creates division among students.

In short, the responsibility lies with school authorities and parents. They must research ways to ensure that students can connect with nature while also gaining meaningful knowledge about geography, history, social customs, and trends, working diligently to steer school trips away from becoming overly extravagant. 

If changes to the approach cannot be made, then it may be preferable to consider abolition.

Lee Seok

In any endeavor, no matter how noble the goal, the means to achieve it must not be negative. Therefore, regarding the issue of school trips, while the intention may be to offer students a well-deserved financial break after a year of toiling in studies while also enriching their geographic and scientific knowledge by exploring famous sites, if the process merely adds to their burdens or causes undue stress, it might be better to terminate school trips altogether.

Most Koreans struggle financially; sending one son or one daughter to school already stretches their budgets thin, and to impose additional costs for school trips adds an unbearable burden. The required funds for trips, typically ranging from 5 to 10 won, can be significant, driving families deeper into financial hardship when considering the additional expenses for clothing and travel gear.

For example, I recall my own experience from fourteen or fifteen years ago while attending a local agricultural school, when our class's designated school trip to a conference in Kyungsung was arranged. Unfortunately, my family's poverty did not allow me to contribute the necessary funds, compelling me to forfeit my trip. This left a lasting sense of shame and resentment towards both my family's financial struggles and the school administration.

I have attempted to provide my own child with a better experience than I had, but several years ago, when my child asked for sports clothes ahead of a school outing, I felt the burden of money again when I couldn’t afford to respond positively. Unfortunately, shortly thereafter, my child passed away, leaving me to grieve not just for their loss but also for not being able to fulfill that small wish, etching the pain of regret deep into my heart.

Overall, while I recognize that school trips themselves are not inherently problematic, I firmly believe that if they cause such severe hardship for even a minority of students and families, there are grounds for abolition. Rather than eliminating these trips entirely, I advocate for reforming the methods of execution. For schools with ample resources, utilizing school funds for such purposes seems sensible. In instances where financial support is lacking, establishing a sponsorship system among wealthier families for the benefit of the whole student body would be beneficial. Implementing such solutions requires the goodwill and effort of school authorities. If current methods cannot be adjusted accordingly, it is likely that impoverished students and their families will voice their opposition.

**Summary**

The article presents various perspectives on the issue of school trips in Korea during the late 1920s. While some educators advocate for school trips as essential educational experiences, others highlight the financial burden they impose on families, particularly in a period of hardship. The discussions reflect the complexities surrounding the educational practices within a colonial context, illustrating the tensions between modern educational aspirations and pressing economic realities faced by the Korean populace under Japanese rule. Historians may find it significant that the debates around children's education intersect with broader societal struggles during this period of colonial Korea.

修學旅行 問題 論議
廢止까지는 생각할 問題

정신여학교 千潤植

「남의 학교에서 가닛가 우리들도 가야 하겟다. 그 학교서 그러한 곳으로 갓스닛가 우리도 꼭 거긔까지 가야지!』해서 다만 형식에 취하여서만 수학여행을 가는 것이 近年의 경향이 안인가 함니다. 거듭 말하면 『安東縣이나 南滿洲 방면으로 남자학교와 상업학교가튼대서 가는대 우리들도 가야한다. 』 하면서 普通女學校에서도 그곳으로 여행을 간다는 것은 큰 잘못인 듯이 생각됩니다.
자기들의 분수에 맛는 곳-여학교가트면 慶州나 扶餘가튼 곳으로 가게 할 필요는 잇겟다고 생각함니다.

一得一失

경신학교장 尹芮彬

나는 수학여행에 대하야 可하다거나 不可하다거나 그 是非를 말하고저 안이한다. 다시 말하면 수학여행은 우리에게 一得一失의 이해관계를 가지고 잇는 까닭에 한편의 이로운 점만 생각하면 해가 되는 점이 잇서 절대로 조흔 일이라고 간주하기 어럽고 또 한편의 害롭은 점만 생각하면 이로움이 잇서 절대로 납븐 일이라고 말하기는 어럽게 된다. 돈 업서지고 수업하는 시간을 소비하든 점을 보면 해롭다고만 생각할 수 잇스나 역사나 地理博物에서 배운 것을 실지로 견학하는 점에 잇서서는 불가하다고 이클기 어러운 것인즉 나는 수학여행에 대하야 可否 의견을 가지고 잇지 안는다. 돈과 시간을 존귀하게 생각하야 수학여행을 불필요하다고 하면 불필요할 것이요. 돈과 시간은 업서트려도 실지로 견문하는 것이 만하 修養에 지대한 好結果를 엇는다면 그것도 그럴 듯한 일이다.
그러나 무슨 일이던지 境遇와 시대를 參酌할 필요는 크게 잇는 것이니 교과서를 사지 못하고 月謝金을 납입하지 못하면서라도 여행비은 준비하야 여행을 가야한다는 것은 올흔 의견이라고 내세우기 어러울 것이요 학비를 풍부히 쓰면서도 수학여행엔 돈낼 수 업다고 하면 그도 容忍할 일이 안이다. 그일을 행하야 그것으로 심대한<114> 영향을 밧지안을 정도 내에서 效果 어들 立案으로 진행하면 이는 두말할 것 업시 이로운 일인 동시에 獎勵할 것이다. 그러나 처사에 잇서서 이러케 말과 가티 되기는 또한 어러운 것이다.
나는 끗흐로 내 학교 학생이거나 일반학생에게 권하고 십흔 것이 잇다. 冬期放學이나 夏期放學을 이용하야 포우쓰카우 모양으로  幕가튼 것을 준비하여 가지고 뜻맛는 동지들이 一團이 되여 徒步旅行으로 일홈잇는 곳을 탐방하야 여러가지로 견학하면 조켓다고 思惟한다. 이는 徒步로 단니니 첫재 몸이 건강하고 둘재로는 비용이 적고 셋재로는 시일에 제한을 밧지 안을 시기임으로 모든 것을 정밀히 관찰할 수 잇는 것이다.
이 일은 내 年來의 宿望이다. 여러가지 사정으로 履行을 못하여 보앗스나 금년에는 우리 학교 학생들에게 식혀보랴 하고 이일에 직접 지도할 사람은 학교 敎員들을 내세울 생각이다.

세가지로 有益

휘문고보교장 李潤柱

나 개인의 의견을 陳述한다면 수학여행은 극력 장려할 일이며 아울너 조흔 일이라고 생각함니다. 보통 수학여행을 조치 못하게 評判하며 반대하는 일부 人士도 업는 것은 안이나 그들의 반대하는 구실꺼리는 대개가 돈 문제임니다. 朝鮮人의 생활상태가 나날이 浸弱하는 형편인데 학교 당국에서는 이도 불구하고 年年히  每人에게 10여원의 비용을 수납하여 强迫的으로 학생에게 이를 실시하니 當局者의 心思를 料量하기 어럽다고 하는 이도 잇슴니다. 수학여행을 갓다왓다고 특수한 효과를 엇는 것도 안이요 공연히 학생들을 끌고 밥 사먹고 차타고 돈 쓰는데서 불과하다고 이러케 過酷하게까지 말슴하시는 이도 업는 것은 안임니다.
내 수학여행을 조타고 찬성하는 이유는 여러 가지가 잇스나 중요한 것 멧가지만 들어 말하겟슴니다.
수학여행은 첫재 학교에서 학습한 各學科를 실지로 見聞하는 것이 만흔즉 학생에게 隱然中 유익함이 만슴니다. 가령 예를 들면 南道學生을 北道 方面으로 달이고가서 평양의 錦繡江山을 구경식히고 또는 人情風俗이 달은 것을 보이고 몃천년 以來의 歷史古蹟을 參觀 식힌다면 무엇이 불가함니까. 돈 멧圓 소비하는 것을 문제삼을 바<115> 안임니다.
둘재로는 학생과 선생사이에 형식업는 친목을 할 수 잇슴니다. 普通時는 선생과 학생간에 엄격한 간격이 잇서 친근할 기회가 업스나 修學旅行時는 여관 한 방에서 寢食을 가티 하게되는 까닭에 師弟之間의 分이 자연 두터워지니까 이런 일은 今日 學界에 잇서서 美風이라고 생각함니다.
셋재는 조선학생들은 항상 草屋속에 뭇처 잇는 것이 보통인데 자연 공기도 불결하고 또는 정신상에도 不少한 영향이 밋슴니다. 이런 형편인 고로 단체로 광활한 자연을 찻는 일은 육체와 정신에 실노 조흔 效得이 잇슴니다. 예를 들면 경치조흔 산에 올나 一望無際의  들을 바라볼 적에 胸襟이 豁然하여지는 것도 爽快한 일이거니와 천하가 한 眼界에 들적에 내 정신에는 名命하기 어러운 雄敢의 기세가 湧出함니다.
그럼으로 내 생각 갓해서는 每週日에 一次式 학과시간에 방해되지 안을 범위 내에서 선생이 인솔하고 遠足가튼 것을 하야 학생들의 心身을 涵養식히는 데 한 방법을 삼기를 바라는 마음도 업지 안슴니다.
하여간 수학여행은 돈을 쓸지언정 잇서야 할 일이라고 思惟하야 단언하기를 마지 안슴니다.

無意味의 旅行은 不必要

중앙학교 崔瑨淳

수학여행은 課外項目으로 必須科程이다. 학과에서 배운 여러가지 일을 실지로 보는 것이니 그 중에도 특히 歷史 地理 博物에 니르서는 不可缺하리만 유익함을 준다. 古來로부터 百聞이 不如一見이라는 말도 잇거니와 狹搾한 교실 안에서 불완전한 표본 등을  보고 배운 것을 실제로 本物을 보게되니 학생들의 驚異와 眞味는 둘재로 치고 얼마나 修得에 효과가 잇슬 것인가.
그런데 우리 사회에서 각종 학교가 年中行事로 수학여행을 하는 모양인데 이와가티 싀골사람이 서을 구경하는 격으로 떼를 지여 가서는 길거리로 도라단니며 색다른 것이나 보고 온다면 이는 반대할 일이라고 생각한다.
우리는 수학여행을 가기 전에 여기에 대한 준비가 잇서야 한다. 實例를 들면 역사에 관한 古蹟이 만흔 곳으로 갈적엔 역사에 대한 準備敎育이 잇서야 하고 地理에 참고가 될만한 곳이면 여기에 해당한 가르킴이 잇서야 할 것이다. 그러치 안이하고 어느 곳이 名勝地이니 今年엔 그리로 가자 하야 막연하게 떠나갓다 오면 이는 돈과 시간만 허비하는 일이 되고만다.
現今 일반학교에서 실시하고 잇는 수학여행은<116> 대개가 준비교육이 업시 막연하게 행하는 것이 태반이다. 이 종류의 수학여행은 하루밥비 폐지하여 버릴 필요가 잇슬는지 모르나 근본적으로 교육적 見地에서 관찰한다면 안이될 일이라고 信念한다.
