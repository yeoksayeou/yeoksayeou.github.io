필자	韓晶東  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Folk Song: Water Mill Song

This body is a water mill color used for grinding rice, 

Each grain of rice that is ground 

Is my autumn heart.

Alas, alas, what a pity.

The grains of rice are ground 

And pounded sweetly, 

Yet my heart keeps pounding 

With yearning only.

Alas, alas, what a pity.

When evening comes, 

The water mill does not rest. 

At night my heart 

Beats even more.

Alas, alas, what a pity.

The water mill turns and turns, 

Will I die from longing? 

This body is a water mill 

Longing to death.

Alas, alas, what a pity. 

**Summary** 

The poem reflects the emotional turmoil of longing and love, using the metaphor of a water mill grinding rice. The imagery evokes a deep sense of yearning as the speaker connects their heartache with the rhythmic and laborious process of the mill. This piece is particularly interesting to historians as it encapsulates the cultural and emotional landscape of Korea during the colonial period, highlighting the fusion of everyday life with profound emotional expression, common in folk songs.

**Named Entities**  

江西		Jiangxi

**Glossary**  

物방아색	물방아색	Water mill color; refers to the hue associated with a water mill, symbolizing labor and longing.  
찟다	찢다	To grind, to crush, an older usage in poems.  
성화로구나	성화로구나	Alas, a lamentation expression often found in folk songs.  
부대켜	부대켜	To pound; an older form of the verb used in folk contexts.
