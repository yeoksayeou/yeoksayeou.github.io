Eight Province Song - Gaksali  

To dismiss the Songtaeryeong (장타령) as merely the foolish ramblings of a beggar is ignorant. Originally, the Songtaeryeong is a regional song, so even though it may be short in verse, it embodies the unique characteristics of local specialties, human feelings, and cultural customs of each province, making it something to savor.  

The first lament of "Gaksali"  

Oh, indeed, well done.  
You do well as a Pumba (품바, a traditional entertainer).  
Gaksali who came last year,  
Has returned again without dying.  
Oh, this fellow, despite all this,  
The son of a high official,  
As the provincial governor,  
Sold for a penny and  
Met as Gaksali.  
Indeed, indeed, well done.  
You do well as a Pumba.  
Who is your teacher?  
You do better than I do.  
Did you read the Poems and Books,  
So artfully and well?  
Did you read the Analects and Mencius?  
Well done, well done.  
Did you drink cool water?  
So refreshingly well.  
Did you drink hot water?  
Directly, directly well.  
Did you eat oil?  
So thickly, thickly well.  
If one doesn't see the carpenter,  
They'll shed winter's misery.  
Indeed, indeed, well done.  
You do well as a Pumba.  
  

With one character, I contemplate  
With a heart of pure sincerity:  

If I die, so be it, I cannot endure.  

With two characters, I contemplate  
In two rivers divided, at the white heron (白鷺洲)  
The white heron flies cheerfully away.  

With three characters, I contemplate  
In the third month, on the third day,  
A pair of swallows come flying in.  

With four characters, I contemplate  
In the fourth month, on the eighth day,  
The lantern Festival (觀燈) is delightful.  

With five characters, I contemplate  
In May, on the Dano (단오) day,  
Maiden and youth gather together,  
The swing party (鞦韆) is delightful.  

With six characters, I contemplate  
In June, on the day of the flow of heads,  
The foot washing party (濯足) is delightful.  

With seven characters, I contemplate  
In July, on the day of Chilseok (七夕),  
The Cowherd and Weaver Girl (牽牛織女) are delightful.  

With eight characters, I contemplate  
In August, on the harvest day,  
The five-grain rice cakes (송편) are delightful.  

With nine characters, I contemplate  
In September, on the ninth day,  
Chrysanthemum wine (菊花酒) is delightful.  

With ten characters, I contemplate  
In October, on the day of 무오 (戊午),  
The ancestral rituals (고사사당) are delightful.  

With one hundred characters, I contemplate  
In the city of one million, in a billion families,  
The peace song (泰平歌) is delightful.  

With ten thousand characters, I contemplate  
Long live, long live our nation,  
The country is peaceful, and the people are secure.  

With one hundred million characters, I contemplate  
In countless living beings,  
They are all full and content.  
Indeed, indeed, well done.  
You do well as a Pumba.

**Summary**  

The article presents the "Eight Province Song," a traditional Korean performance piece known as "Songtaeryeong." It illustrates distinct characteristics and cultural aspects from each of the eight provinces in Korea during the colonial period. The work conveys both humor and regional pride while highlighting social issues like poverty, which were prevalent during the Japanese occupation. Historians might find the vernacular and social observations pivotal in understanding the Korean identity amidst colonial oppression.
