Recruitment New Path: If I Were to Conduct a Job Acquisition Movement 

Author: Kim Ki-jin  

No matter what kind of job one seeks, it is essential to have one's skills first and foremost. Particularly, newspaper reporters should not be wrestling with ledgers like bank employees or clerks, nor struggling with chalkboards like school teachers; since they are engaged in a profession that requires writing on manuscript paper with a pen, one must first know how to write well to secure such a position. It would be beneficial to submit many articles to newspapers or magazines so that the publishers or the general public can become well aware of one's abilities. However, even with the right skills, finding a job is not so easy in this time when society's structure and the economy are still imperfect. There are many people in this world who possess significant abilities but are unable to find employment due to a lack of influence, causing them to live in unfortunate circumstances. Even someone with the necessary skills will find it much easier to get hired if they have connections or relationships with the company president or other executives. Conversely, for those without such connections, securing a position will be difficult. Ultimately, it can be said that both skills and influence are required to attain employment. Based on my past experiences, even if I were to look for a job again today, I believe I could only do so if I possessed both of these powers. Just as the earth rotates with both centrifugal and centripetal forces, individuals navigate through all matters of life with the combination of skills and influence. Those currently pursuing job acquisition must prepare themselves accordingly. 

Two Essentials  

Author: Lee Seo-kyu  

First, one must read newspapers and magazines extensively. Just as someone aspiring to be a general should read military texts and someone wanting to become a doctor should read medical books, one aspiring to be a newspaper reporter must begin by thoroughly reading newspapers and magazines. This will increase their knowledge about the newspapers and magazines, including how articles are treated and how to write them, and the various formats that exist. While it would be ideal to read foreign newspapers and magazines, if that is not possible, at least diligently reading those published in Korea is essential. 

Second, it is necessary to submit many articles to various newspapers and magazines. In the past, it was understood that "whether one exists or not, it matters not," but that era has passed. Now is the time for both individuals and organizations to promote themselves and advertise. To seek employment, particularly for newspaper and magazine reporters, one must publish many writings so that the public reads them, understands one's level of skill, and recognizes them. Without this, one will surely have to go to the editorial department or the upper management with a rickshaw or car to seek jobs. To not do so, while lacking the necessary skills, and still seeking to persuade editorial managers or executives will lead nowhere. Even if hired, they might find the work too demanding and resign within days, or the company may find them to be of no use whatsoever. Compared to banks or companies, where there might be some leeway with minimal efficiency, journalism demands complete attention since reporters represent the entire company. In summary, fortifying one's own abilities is the foundational issue. 

If Conducting a Teacher Movement  

Author: Park Dal-seung  

If I were to engage in a teacher movement, I would attempt to do so through three methods. Of course, these are three different ways of conducting one movement. 

First, I would familiarize myself with the most powerful authorities within the school, to the extent that, at least, I could recognize their names in written form, while making every opportunity to demonstrate my sincerity to them. 

Second, using indirect methods through friends is one way; this is similar to the first point but involves indirect communication. 

Third, while this may seem the most base, it is actually the strongest method: by conducting thorough research on the school's most influential authorities (the principal or the head of education), I would go to them prepared to flatter or threaten as circumstances dictate.  

Agricultural Night School Teacher and Cooperative Director  

Author: Kang Yu  

If I were to seek a job now, I would not wish to be in a position that provides a salary in Seoul or other urban areas. It is true that one must eat to survive, but eating does not solely rely on receiving a paycheck. From my experience, whether one receives 10 won or 100 won is essentially the same. Especially in urban areas like Seoul, where the social dynamics are harsh and the prices are soaring, it will be quite difficult to maintain a living on a modest salary. Even if the salary is seemingly adequate, it often amounts to personal luxuries with no benefit to the common people. Therefore, I intend to return to the countryside and establish night schools to awaken the ignorant farmers and the underprivileged children. By collaborating with farmers, I would work towards starting small cooperatives or consumer associations to support the living conditions of the dispirited farmers and invigorate the declining rural areas. How beneficial and fulfilling that would be! In doing this, though one may be an unknown person in the city, they would naturally become a teacher or a cooperative leader in the countryside, embodying a sense of ownership in every matter—what an honor that would be! Thus, in turn, they would find sustenance and necessities for themselves. That sense of natural freedom far surpasses the limitations and inequalities of a salary-based life. Every year, hundreds of graduates emerge while job opportunities remain scarce, and witnessing capable young men wasting precious time and money wandering in the city is indeed disheartening. If one truly possesses the will to work for Korea, they should inevitably go rural to alleviate their parents' burdens and enlighten the farmers. Thus, I conclude that I would choose to become an agricultural night school teacher or a cooperative director in the countryside instead of living a salaried life in the city.  

**Summary**  

The article discusses the importance of both skills and connections in securing employment in a colonial context where opportunities are limited. Kim Ki-jin emphasizes the need for foundational writing skills in journalism as well as the importance of knowing influential people for securing jobs. Lee Seo-kyu advocates for extensive reading and article submissions as crucial steps in becoming a reporter. Park Dal-seung and Kang Yu reflect alternative pathways, highlighting educational and community development roles over conventional salaried urban jobs. 

This reflects the social dynamics of colonial Korea, where traditional pathways to success often overlook the abilities of ordinary individuals, revealing the complex relationship between meritocracy and social connections during this period. Historians might find it revealing how these reflections underline the struggle for agency among young Koreans in a time of colonial oppression.

新聞記者될 運動을 한다면

金基鎭

무슨 직업을 구하던지 먼저 자기의 실력이 잇서야 될 것은 물론이다. 특히 신문기자는 은행,회사원과 가티 帳簿나 珠盤을 가지고 씨름하는 것도 안이요 學校敎員과 가티 白墨漆板과 씨름을 하는 것이 안이고 원고지와 鐵筆을 가지고 씨름하는 직업인 까닭에 그 직업을 구하는 데는 먼저 글을 쓸줄 알어야 한다. 그리하야 그것을 신문 혹은 잡지에 投稿를 만히 하야 그 社의 책임자나 또 일반이 그 사람의 실력을 잘 알도록 하는 것이 조흘 것이다. 그러나 아즉까지 사회의 *度와<97> 경제의 조직이 불완전한 이때에 잇서서는 다만 자기의 실력만 잇다고 취직이 그럿케 용이한 것은 안이다. 이 세상에는 상당한 실력이 잇스면서도 勢力이 업는 까닭에 취직을 못하고 不遇落拓하는 사람이 만치 안으냐. 가튼 실력이 잇는 사람이라도 그 사람이 勢力이 잇서서 직접이나 혹은 간접으로 그 社의 社長 기타 중역과 친분이 잇는 사람이면 물론 입사하기가 용이할 것이오 그럿치 못한 사람은 또 입사하기가 어려울 것이다. 결국 말하자면 실력과 勢力 두 가지가 모다 구비하여야 취직하게 될 것이다. 내가 과거 경험하야본 일도 그럿치만은 지금에 만일 다시 취직을 구한다 가정하야도 또한 이 두가지 力을 가지고 운동하는 수 밧게는 업슬 것갓다. 지구가 원심력과 구심력을 가지고 회전하는 것과 가티 사람은 이 실력과 세력으로 모든 일을 하야가는 것이다. 지금 취직운동을 하는 이도 이것을 각오하여야 될 것이다.

두가지가 必要

李瑞求

第一은 신문과 잡지를 정독하여야 될 것이다. 將帥가 되고자 하는 사람은 兵書를 잘 읽어야 하고 의사가 되고자 하는 사람은 醫書를 잘 읽어야 되는 것과 가티 신문기자가 되고자 하는 사람은 먼저 신문과 잡지를 정독하여야 될 것이다. 그리하여야 자기가 그 신문 잡지에 대한 지식이 늘어서 기사취급하는 방법이라던지, 기사 쓰는 것이라던지 體裁, 기타 여러가지를 다 알게 될 것이다. 신문잡지는 물론 외국 것까지라도 정독하엿스면 조켓지만 그럿치 못하다면 少하야도 조선에서 발행하는 신문잡지만이라도 정독하는 것이 필요하다. 第二는 각 신문과 잡지에 投稿를 만히 할 필요가 잇다. 前日에는 有若無實若虛라는 그것을 한 처세의 묘법으로 알엇지만은 지금은 벌서 그 시대는 지나고 단체나 개인이나 모도가 선전하는 시대요 광고하는 시대다. 우리가 취업을 구하는 데도 특히 신문 잡지 기자는 그 무기인 글을 만히 발표하야 여러 사람이 그 사람의 글을 잘 읽고 따러서 그 사람의 실력 여하를 알어서 일반이 인정하게 된다면 자기가 기자될 운동을 하기 전에 신문이나 잡지사에서 人力車나 자동차를 가지고 가서 제발 오서달나고 요구를 할 것이다. 그럿치 안이하고 자기가 별 실력이 업스면서 편집국장이나 기타 중역에게 운동을 한다면 채용할 리도 만무하고 설혹 채용을 한다 하여도 그 일을 堪耐치 못하야 불과 몃칠에 자기가 自退를 하던지 그럿치 안으면 社로서 미역국을 먹일 것이다. 은행과 회사 가튼 데는 多少 能率이 부족하더라도 일이 단순한 까닭에 조금 연습하야 그 맛튼 부분의 일만 잘하면 문제도 업지만은 기자란 것은 언제이나<98> 그 社 전체를 대표하야 활동하는 까닭에 원래의 能率이 부족하여 가지고는 도저히 그 임무를 다 할 수 업슬 것이다. 總而言之하면 먼저 自力을 충실케 하는 것이 근본문제일 것이다.
