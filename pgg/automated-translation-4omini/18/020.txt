필자	韓晶東  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Western Folk Songs: May Girl’s Lament - Set to the Melody of Menari from Jiangxi  
Han Jeong-dong  
Oh, this sorrowful body,  

In the morning I gather water,  
In the evening I pound grain.  
Oh my, oh my,  
What a hardship it is!  

In the harsh rain and wind,  
Suffering in my late forties,  
Don’t be sad, my heart is red.  
Oh my, oh my,  
What a hardship it is!  

So ugly, yet so beautiful,  
For the sake of my face,  
What is the urgency?  
Even mischievousness has come.  
Oh my, oh my,  
What a hardship it is!  

Mother’s wrapping clothes,  
How lovely they are when I wear them.  
In the Dongzhi (端午) festival,  
I will go to the shrine.  
Oh my, oh my,  
What a hardship it is!  

**Summary**  

The poem reflects the hardships and emotions of a woman navigating her daily struggles, particularly emphasizing the sorrow connected with her gendered labor and societal expectations. It evokes a strong sense of cultural identity through its reference to specific regional practices such as the Dongzhi festival. Historians may find this article revealing as it encapsulates the lived experiences of women during Korea's colonial period, illustrating their resilience amid societal constraints, and providing insight into the gender dynamics and cultural continuity in colonial Korea.
