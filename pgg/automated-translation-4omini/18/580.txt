잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1929년 04월 01일  
기사제목	乞人으로 5千圓을 모와 10年前에 改嫁해 간 本妻를 다시 차저 (A Beggar Gathers 5,000 Won and Reclaims His Original Wife Who Remarried 10 Years Ago)  
필자	金秉濟  
기사형태	문예기타 (Other Literary Forms)  

More than 20 years ago, in Yongheung, among the sea and at a house resembling a tent, lived a young couple by the names of Kim Sang-nok (pseudonym) and Choi Bok-soon (pseudonym). As the lunar calendar turned, they were so consumed by worries about food and clothing that they lost track of time, and while their bodies wasted away from lack of sustenance, they were additionally pressured by relentless debt collectors. Bok-soon, with a stomach gnawing from three days of hunger, worried about her husband’s well-being. One day she fell asleep and, upon waking, found her husband gone. Initially thinking he had gone out to fetch something, she waited, hour by hour, until dawn broke, yet there was still no sign of him. Panic stricken, she began to suspect the worst—perhaps he had encountered some wild beast. She searched the yard, the back, and even the outhouse, but no trace could be found. 

For a husband to vanish completely, this was unfathomable. They could not have gone far as they had no means. Bok-soon’s heart sank deeper as time passed. After spending the day in tears, she resolved to carry on alone, doing odd jobs washing and cooking to sustain herself, continuing this way for three months. As spring went by, the summer heat bore down on them, and it became increasingly unbearable to stay in that village; therefore, Bok-soon felt forced to move elsewhere. 

Traveling about ten li to the coast, she came across a saltworks where a man named Kim Jwa-su was the overseer. Fortune found her there as she stayed at Kim Jwa-su's place where she was provided shelter in exchange for her work. After about ten days of quiet living with his family, Jwa-su said to her, “I originally intended to help you stay here longer, cooking and providing for you as a relative, but due to certain circumstances, you can no longer stay here. It's best for you to move on.” Upon hearing this, Bok-soon was perplexed, wondering if she had somehow wronged him. She considered this with confusion and after pondering for a moment replied, “I do not understand, but I thought of you as a kind benefactor during my hardships. Why share such words? Please, honor me with kindness instead.” 

Kim Jwa-su, seeing her sincerity, replied, “I didn’t mean to hurt your feelings. I didn’t know your situation was so dire; the coming summer season sees many unsavory characters flock to the area and I wouldn’t want you to be caught up in that. I’ve turned down many requests, but they are persistent. Thus, it has become impossible for me to keep you here. You should look for a safer place to stay.” 

Bok-soon, being a simple-minded woman and holding strong to her sense of fidelity, realized the intent of his words. Although her husband might have perished, he was still alive somewhere and if he found her, she had resolved to remain pure for him. After resolving to sacrifice her own integrity to stay alive until they reunited, she thought, “Perhaps in a year or so I can meet him again and discuss this. If we cannot, then I might as well end my life.” 

With her resolve set, she looked at Jwa-su and said, “If you really wish for me to leave, I have nowhere else to turn. I cannot live with another man, nor can I resettle. Kindly find it in your heart to keep me here.” Jwa-su, initially reluctant, felt her sincerity and within days, Bok-soon became a maid in Jwa-su's household. 

As the years unfolded, Bok-soon lived ten years under Jwa-su’s roof, earning her keep comfortably. One autumn day, Jwa-su went for a stroll as the sun set when he encountered a beggar sleeping by a willow tree. Overcome with pity, he approached the man and said, “The sun is setting; aren’t you going to prepare yourself an evening meal?” The beggar expressed his gratitude but then asked, “Excuse me, I have a question. It’s been ten years since there was a certain man named Kim Sang-nok who disappeared from a village about ten li from here. Do you know if he still lives in your house?” 

Jwa-su was taken aback by the beggar’s inquiry, contemplating how he might know about the situation. But he responded, “Indeed, I know him well and can even recognize his face. I’ll arrange for you to meet with him.” Upon returning home, he informed Bok-soon of the beggar’s inquiry. 

Astonished, Bok-soon realized her husband must still be alive. She rushed out, and as she approached him, she felt a rush of emotions. After ten years, she called out to him, “Have you really returned in that guise?” The beggar sighed heavily before replying, “My fate has led me to many places, and always in hardship. Now that I have returned, I wanted to find you, yet I appear like this. It is your choice whether to follow me or stay with Jwa-su. Make your decision as you see fit.” 

Bok-soon had bottled up fervent emotions for ten years and, feeling an overwhelming surge, she responded, “I have borne much shame all this time, waiting for you. I resolved to share my grievances with you and follow you, or end my life in the sea if you reject me. But your words to leave it up to me feel like a test. As long as you don’t find me beneath your dignity, I will follow you.” 

Finally, the beggar found some comfort in her words and reassured her, “The troubles have been of my own making, and all these years of suffering were my fault. We must show our gratitude to Jwa-su, for he has been a great benefactor to us.” That night, the couple expressed their appreciation towards Jwa-su and, shortly after, they returned to the village they once called home. 

While there, Bok-soon learned of a large farm available for sale. She immediately sought out an acquaintance to assist her in negotiating the purchase. Her acquaintance expressed doubt, “How can you, after all the difficulties you faced, afford to buy such a grand farm?” To this Kim Sang-nok reassured him that during his journey back home, he had met a friend in Wonsan who promised to help them with the funds, so do not worry. Therefore, he encouraged his acquaintance to begin negotiations for the farm. 

Eventually, the friend pestered him incessantly, suspecting Sang-nok's intentions to be dubious since he had appropriated funds without evidence of wealth. As days passed, the friend grew more worried. The day came for the farm’s purchase and, upon arrival, Sang-nok who had appeared to be a beggar, revealed a hidden stash containing five thousand won. The astonishment extended beyond his friend to Bok-soon, who was taken aback to see the wealth that had been invisibly carried alongside his impoverished appearance, realizing that even in hardship, love and faith can transform lives.

**Summary**

This article tells the tale of a couple separated by hardship and fortune’s cruelty, showcasing their reunion after a decade of trials. The narrative highlights themes of fidelity, loyalty, and the social struggles of the time, shedding light on the experiences of individuals during the colonial period. Historians may find interest in the societal norms of fidelity and marriage during this era, as well as insights into the economic conditions faced by the working class in Korea.

하는 외마듸 말을 던저슴니다. 그 때 그 걸인 남편은 후유 한숨을 지으면서 하는 말이
본래 팔자가 사나운 놈이 아무데를 가면 별수가 잇스며 암만 오래 지내면 별수가 잇겟나. 10년이 하로가티 이 모양으로 도라 단이다가 그래도 제 살든 곳이니 고향이나 차저 가본다고 오는 길에 자네가 그러케 신세곳친 말까지 듯고 左右間 한번 만나서 이약이나 한마듸 하여보고 어듸든지 가보려고 온 것인데 蔽一言 길게 말할 것 업시 자네는 그 동안 10년이나 金座首의 신세를 지고 몸 편안히 잘 지나는 터이오, 나는 이 꼴을 가지고 왓스니 이저는 나를 따르든지 金座首를 따르든지 자네 맘나는대로 결정하게. 나는 그 말이나 듯자는 것 뿐일세. 하고 崔氏를 치어다 보겟지오. 이 때 崔氏는 10년 동안이나 하소연할 곳이 업서 九曲肝腸에 매치고 맷친 분하고 억울하고 쓰라린 설음이 한껍에 용소슴 처서 고만 걸인 남편의 무릅헤 업드러저서 한참이나 흑흑 늣겨가며 울다가 겨우 울음을 진정하고
여보시오 내가 이 때까지 모든 屈辱을 다 참고 살아 잇는 것이 언제든지 당신을 만나서 그 동안 지난 사정이나 하소연하고 당신이 나를 거두면 따러가고 그럿치 못하면 바다에 몸을 던저 죽기로서 결정하엿사온데 당신의 말삼이 네 맘대로 하라는 것은 나를 시험하는 말이 오니까. 당신만 나를 더럽다 하지 안흐면 어듸라도 따러 가겟슴니다.
고 하니까 그제야 그 남편은 이왕 모든 고생은 다 자기의 허물이라고 하야 崔氏를 慰勞하고 여하튼 金座首는 우리의 큰 은인이니 그에게 感謝의 뜻을 표하여야 된다고 그날 밤은 金座首 집에 가서 여러 가지 이약이로 金座首끠 致賀하고 그 잇흔날 崔氏와 가티 이왕 살든 그 洞里로 가게 되엿슴니다. 그리하야 어느 親故의 집에 가서 몃츨동안 엇어 먹고 잇다가 하로는 그 洞里 안에 잇는 큰 田庄이 팔일 것이 잇다는 말을 듯고는 엇든 親故 한 사람을 차저 가서 내가 그 田庄을 살터이니 자네가 周旋이나 좀 하여주게 하고 청탁 하겟지오. 그래 그 親故는 하는 말이 자네가 그러케 비러 먹으면서 고향에 차저 온 사람이 무슨 돈이 잇서서 그런 큰 田庄을 사겟나 하니까, 金尙祿의 말이 그런 것이 안이라 내가 이번 고향으로 오드 길에 元山 잇는 어느 親舊를 만나서<171>내의 신세를 이약이 한 즉 그가 그러면 어듸든지 조흔 田庄이 나 잇스면 사서 줄 터이니 탐지하여 보라고 하엿슨 즉 여하튼 돈은 대라는 대로 댈 터이니 念慮말고 田庄이나 흥정하여 달나고 함니다. 그래서 그 친구는 그 말을 딱이 밋지는 안흐면서도 한번 지나 보랴는 생각과 金尙祿의 조르는 바람에 할 수 업서 멧츨동안 도라 단이면서 그 田庄을 5천원에 흥정을 떼여 가지고 金尙祿을 차저 와서 꼭 열흘만에 대금을 다 주어야 될 터이니 그리 알고 잘 처리하야 보라고 하엿슴니다. 그리고도 그 친구는 늘 미심하야 날마다 金尙祿의 출입을 엿보고 잇슴니다. 元山 사람이 돈을 대여 준다니까 물론 元山은 갓다 와야 하겟는데 하로가 지나도 그냥 능청스럽게 잇슴니다. 그 친구는 하도 우습고 답답하야 金尙祿을 차저와서 자네가 나까지 無信用한 사람을 맨들랴고 그러케 무심하고 잇단 말인가. 元山 친구가 돈을 준다니 元山을 가야 하겟는데 내일이 기한인데도 尙今도 가지 안흐니 웬일인가 하고 책망을 하엿슴니다. 그래도 金尙祿이는 泰然스럽게 우스면서 엇지 하든지 期日에 돈만 내엿스면 될 일인데 웨인 걱정을 하나 念慮말게하고 늘어저 잇드니 그 잇흔날 지주와 그 친구갸 가티와서 田庄 갑을 달나고 할 때 올시다. 그제야 金尙祿이는 자기가 고향으로 올 때 가지고 은 단 한가지의 物件인 속새 오작이를 끄내드니 그 속에 가득한 紙幣뭉치에서 일금 5천원을 세여서 내여 노핫슴니다. 그것을 보고 그 지주나 친구가 놀낸 것은 말할 것도 업거니와 안해 崔氏가 더욱 놀냇슴니다. 저러한 돈뭉치를 가지고도 걸인의 행색을 하고 온 것이라든지 만난 후 한 달이 다 지나도록 돈 잇는 내곳도 하지 안코 잇섯든 것이든지 모도가 뜻밧기오 꿈박긴 同時 저 양반이 나의 心志를 저울질하여 본 것이로고나 할 때에 더욱 놀냇슴니다.
