Strange Diagnosis  
Judge: "What do you think about the injuries of the victim?"  
Doctor: "Well... two of the wounds are fatal, but with proper treatment, the other one might recover in about three months." <153>  

**Summary**  

This article presents a brief exchange between a judge and a doctor concerning the injuries of a victim. The doctor describes the severity of the injuries, indicating that while two are fatal, there is hope for recovery for another wound. This dialogue offers insight into the medical assessments made during the period, reflecting on the societal conditions of colonial Korea where issues of health and mortality were closely intertwined with justice. Historians might find the implications of medical knowledge and its authority in legal contexts particularly interesting, revealing how colonial governance influenced perceptions of health and justice.
