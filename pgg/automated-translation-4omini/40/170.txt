The loss of a young man's life is a heartbreaking event, one that our senses instinctively recognize as one of the most tragic occurrences. The death of a young person always leaves a distinct mark of overflowing tears. This particular incident occurred in the southern region of Korea, in Tongyeong’s quaint cottage, Tondeok Mountain, during the flourishing summer season of July, where a 23-year-old youth, filled with hopes and aspirations, tragically departed from this world. Although he was known for his excellent scholarship, great ambitions, and being an exemplary young man, his death is undoubtedly a sorrowful and pitying circumstance. 

Purity—this brings even the heavens to shudder. Not even the worst criminal could remain unaffected. Among all forms of purity, the untouched heart of a young lady stands out as one of the purest and cleanest things in humanity. Who could deny that? Now, let us consider a maiden who deeply loves someone, cherishing him with all her heart and praying earnestly for his recovery upon hearing of his sickness. Each night, she gazes up at the countless stars and prays to the heavens, hoping for the healing of her beloved. Anyone observing her heartfelt sincerity would undoubtedly feel sympathy for her, and be moved by her genuine emotions. However, the grim reality disrupts her delicate dreams. If she were to shatter those dreams and extinguish her life upon the news of his passing, who among us would remain unaffected, or who would not shed tears over such a tragic narrative? Is this not a tragedy surpassing even “Romeo and Juliet”?

This heartbreaking event unfolded in the southern Tongyeong's quaint cottage, Tondeok Mountain, where a 23-year-old man named Kim Du-hong had just passed away. He had graduated with outstanding grades from the Tongyeong Fisheries School and was admitted to the Shimane Prefectural Maritime School in Japan, but due to financial issues he could no longer attend. His aptitude was recognized by the school's principal, who offered to cover his tuition. He resumed his studies with determination, but unfortunately, he succumbed to a nervous breakdown and was unable to return home for treatment. Despite his efforts, he tragically passed away on July 15th. At that moment, he was engaged to a 17-year-old girl named Yun Sun-yi, who lived in the neighboring village of Okusan, Gyeongju. Upon hearing that her fiancé was suffering from an illness, she secretly tormented herself, praying fervently for his recovery. However, upon receiving the news of his death, she left a message— “I will go to him”—and committed suicide the very next day. This heartbreaking truth profoundly affected their families and the community, leading to the two families conducting a funeral union for the two young corpses on July 17th as a form of consolation. 

This is the truth we now know. Beyond this, we are yet unaware of further details. However, this profound narrative, filled with warmth, will surely be transmitted from mouth to mouth over the lush southern island, where these lovers—if they exist in the afterlife—might rejoice together in eternal bliss.

 

**Summary** 

This article recounts a tragic love story from Tongyeong, Korea, focusing on the hearts of two young people whose lives were cut short by illness and despair. After the young man died, his fiancée followed him in death as a demonstration of her devoted love. Their bodies were united in a ceremonial wedding, symbolizing their undying bond. The story highlights themes of love, loss, and the societal reactions to such events during the colonial period. Historians may find this account reveals the emotional struggles and cultural practices surrounding death during a time of significant socio-political upheaval in Korea.
