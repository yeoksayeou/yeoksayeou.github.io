기사형태	설문 (Survey)  

I see myself, the perspective of a notable person  
Kim Mal-pung  
1. I often pretend to be lively, but in reality, I frequently stew over trivial matters alone for several days.  

2. My emotional sensitivity is overwhelming. Because of this, while there are pleasant moments, there are also many situations that lead to regret.

3. When it comes to others, I am thoroughly exhausted. Even if someone is a mass of flaws and errors, I want to defend and support them till the end. However, when someone seems to deliberately cover their own shortcomings, I find it impossible to grant them true friendship.  

4. Lastly, I tend to think only of my own faults, and I feel a lack of hope and courage about what to do. Therefore, every day I intentionally try to struggle against this, and I am well aware of it.  

Kim Mi-ri  
People live indulging in their own sense of superiority. We don't like to speak of our own flaws. I feel the same way.  

Bae In-sun  
I have a sudden stubbornness. While I see myself or hear what others say about me and think, “I am very kind and gentle-hearted,” in reality, I am indifferent and do not even shed tears over trivial matters.  

Yoo Young-jun  
First, I am biased. When facing more than two people, I tend to prefer the somewhat better one among them.  

Second, I have an untamed nervousness.  

Third, I am always troubled by my lack of knowledge.  

Fourth, my mind is quite dull.  

Fifth, I pretend to know things out of concerns for my image even when I don't.  

Lee Deok-yo  
I see myself as an extraordinarily clever but very foolish, ordinary woman. However, people from the past would recommend such a personality type as cynical, which makes me greatly pessimistic.  

Heo Young-suk  
I see many weaknesses in myself, but if I mention all of them, it may offend others. I'll mention just a couple of things.  

First, I am very consumed by family and children. Although I don't feel too lacking in my education from elementary to secondary schools, since I cannot write or engage in my field of medicine, I find myself in a bind.  

Moreover, I do not handle household matters well either. Knowing how women in Korea need to be active, I feel significant weakness that I am unable to do so.  

If I were a man, I would not seek marriage with a woman like myself. It's a trivial matter, but once, I rode a streetcar going somewhere with a well-dressed person, and I couldn't help but feel embarrassed because I, poorly dressed, was looking at him.  

If I had to find a positive aspect, it would be my indifference to trends or luxurious living. Recently, while trends and luxuries have become very significant, I am still wearing old shoes I bought three years ago, and I have only two new fur coats to show for it.  

Lim Hyun-jeong  
Others see me as reliable, lively, and persistent, but in reality, my reliability is not so strong, and I lack liveliness and stubbornness to some degree; I am a type of fool.  

I sometimes cry with unbearable feelings when someone says something resentful or unfair that no one else could even imagine.  

My idealistic plans are plentiful, but I am not strong in execution and am unable to act, so I lack competency.  

If there is a strength, it is that I do not think of harming others for my own honor, status, or gain, and I do not take pleasure in flattering others, and I am resolved to keep striving until my thoughts are realized.  

Shin Albert  
In Korea, every person possesses a bitter destiny. This person is also someone born in Korea, thus nothing special about their existence can be argued. For those who don't exist (like me), it’s pointless to talk about what could be utilized.  

If I were to express my thoughts, I would recall my life journey that brings me tears and plenty of sad moments.  

Accepting the fact that my hair has turned white until today, holding onto my pen, is a school I am currently dedicated to.  

Reflecting on my time spent with our housewives and women past school age, my first thought is that there is no money comparable to others, making it difficult for me to find a foothold - I wonder how I can make a little progress.  

Forcing myself to move forward in my thoughts brings about laughing moments but along with recognizing that my foundation is not strong and that I should be direct and clear, yet I only take small steps, all these lead to self-despair.  

From my perspective, I could be of use to society in some ways, but with my original unclear existence, my work with literature is futile.  

Choi Gyu-dong  
I feel dissatisfaction with my self in many areas. While I cannot enumerate everything, one thing I can say is that my temperament is overly impulsive. I don’t know if this is considered a strength or not, but when others wrong me or I experience mishaps, I often blame it on myself and admonish with self-reflection.  

Jo Dong-sik  
In everything I do, I feel a lack of sincerity. However, there is perseverance in all matters. When enduring and being subjected to difficulties, I sometimes find foolishness in that, but later on, I realize that it turned out to be a strength in the end.  

An Il-young  
I possess numerous shortcomings, but above all, I tend to have a hasty temperament. I have attempted to cultivate it through discipline, yet perhaps it is innate, so it has not completely improved.  

When I start something after deciding in my mind, I finish it stubbornly, exhibiting some perseverance.  

Kwon Deok-kyu  
If there is money, I buy alcohol. I do not interact with less fortunate people.  

Choi Du-seon  
I am not at a time where I can disclose what I see as “my self,” nor have I clearly grasped my own self yet (I am not totally ignorant, but...).  

Lee Byeong-ki  
Without any merits or faults, if I have to mention a characteristic, it would be that I do not easily express emotions externally. Possibly this trait did not exist, but if ever I were to engage in a romantic relationship with a woman, I would take a long time to express feelings, while others might reach it in only three or four months.  

Also, if I have someone in my house, I tend to choose someone who no one else would choose, always looking out for them instead.  

I have no interest in socializing or leisure, but I only persistently dedicate my heart and effort to what I have set in my mind.  

Yoo Jin-tae  
Others say that I cannot attain success. I also think the same about myself. In reality, I haven't done anything that one would call a business endeavor.  

In terms of temperament, I am very hasty and impetuous. Thus, when attempting to suppress this standard behavior for urgent matters, I naturally end up feeling stifled and frustrated. However, while I suffer internally, I’ve never made mistakes or failed at anything because of this hasty temperament.  

Shin Myeong-kyun  
I typically do not engage in arguments with others. This might be called humility, or it could be my fault. However, when I have decisively thought something through, I dedicate all my sincerity and effort towards it.  

Thus, I tend to get too obsessive over a particular issue while being unable to let go, which could turn into a flaw.  

I also often hear remarks about being indifferent towards others.  

In fact, I have been completely void of any knowledge regarding home economics or everyday living. Speaking of hobbies, I cannot name even one related to daily living or leisure. I only find pleasure in my own research without anyone else knowing it. In any case, I am a fool.  

Kim Yi-seok  
With no remarkable talent and even a feeble constitution, I have nothing to boast about before others. However, one thing I can assert is that I have lived honestly and with integrity.  

And I consistently possess enthusiasm and perseverance in my work, which I can mention freely. For this reason, I do not have initiative in engaging in diverse activities or endeavors.  

To undertake significant endeavors in this world, one seemingly needs ambition or some cunning approaches, and with my personality, I find it impossible to aspire to such lofty endeavors. Therefore, there are moments when my work in education, which I currently pursue, does not progress smoothly.  

In business, having some ambition and employing various means is essential; simply being honest is not enough.  

Lee Il  
Even a great sage like Socrates was extremely dull at self-reflection and threw the riddle, “Know thyself.” Even if one knows oneself, if they don’t trust themselves, how would one’s benefactor recognize them?  

In this world, more than anything, I know myself the best. I worry so much because I know myself too well.  

Realizing I am well aware of my good traits is quite frustrating, but knowing my faults in detail is what troubles me the most.  

From the outside, I do not look like one of the shorter Koreans, nor am I petite but I am rather average. While my appearance is somewhat ordinary, to be honest, it is less appealing and less versatile.  

Additionally, I face the perplexity of being unable to find any of my attractive features that could even be useful medicinally.  

Furthermore, when I observe noble characteristics, my vision becomes critical, leading to resentment against others without needing to pay; I develop distaste for others.  

In any case, both my outer appearance and inner self fit comfortably the descriptions of foolish folk, lowly beings, and inferior people—truly this is a fitting description for the bottommost of human existence.  

**Summary**  

This article features a survey of notable individuals reflecting on their sense of self and personal flaws. The perspectives reveal a wide range of emotions—self-doubt, introspection, and the struggle to balance personal ambitions with societal expectations. What stands out is the consistent theme of individuals feeling inadequate or incapable due to the pressures of their circumstances, such as socio-economic limitations, the expectations of gender roles, and the impact of colonial rule. Historians may find it interesting to note how these sentiments reflect the struggles of personal identity during colonial Korea, capturing the conflict between individual aspirations and the societal constraints imposed by the period.
