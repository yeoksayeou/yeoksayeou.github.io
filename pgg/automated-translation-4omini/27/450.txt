잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1930년 06월 01일  
기사제목	李忠武公時調 (Songs of the Loyal Military Minister Lee Chung-mu)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

In the quiet mountain island under the bright moonlight,  

Alone in the watchtower,  

Wearing a big sword on my belt, I sigh deeply,  

From somewhere, a sound of a hujia is heard, which is someone else's sorrow.  

  

**Summary**  

This poem reflects on themes of loneliness and duty, using the imagery of a moonlit night and the solitude of a watchtower. It evokes a sense of longing and melancholy through the mention of the hujia, an instrument often associated with the emotions of the past or of battles that were fought. Historians might find it interesting as it encapsulates the spirit of the time, possibly reflecting the sentiments of Koreans under Japanese rule in the 1930s, hinting at feelings of loss and nostalgia intertwined with a sense of national identity.
