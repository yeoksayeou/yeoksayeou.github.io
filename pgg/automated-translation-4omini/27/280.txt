기사형태	시 (Poem)  

On the Road in April  
						Kim Hwa-san  

A  
The road.  

A cobblestone path stained with tears - the heart of Seoul  

Flags fluttering aimlessly at shopfronts,  

Cars racing like a fevered person - cars, cars, cars, cars, cars  

Coal smoke - white scattered,  

Dense powder of love.  

A large woman at the palace,  

A gentleman with an attentive gaze towards the ladies,  

People, people, people, people……………  

Oh, in April's warmth,  
Seoul wafts the scent of life,  
A young man caught in the tumult of desire in his twenties.  

B  
Without the green trees and red flowers, spring is not complete!  

Oh young man with long hair and a black tie,  

With a mind racing like thirty billion horsepower,  
a tragic woe possessing only a single coin in your pocket,  
hunger,  
hatred towards women,  

Do you see the diverse thoughts of the crowd in the parking lot?  

Oh, as I walk the street,  
I hear the shouts of the floating crowd in the air.  

C  
Night after night in the shop windows, Chandeliers bathed in green,  

Baron Peacock - a flood in the café.  

Oh, strewn along the road, cinema ads and newspapers  
reporting the great examination of the Communist Party,  
On the Road in April.  
Seoul, the idol of light and wind,  
××××××.  
(1930, 4, 8)  

**Summary**  

This poem captures the bustling life and vibrant energy of Seoul in the spring of April 1930, reflecting on the myriad emotions and chaotic experiences of its citizens. It paints a vivid picture of the sights and smells of the city, intertwining themes of desire, sorrow, and social observation. The piece offers insight into the cultural and emotional landscape of Korea during its colonial period, which historians may find significant as it evidences the struggles and aspirations of youth amidst the backdrop of political tension and societal change.
