야릇한 남녀  
Bearded Man: "I only attend your sermons on the days you preach."  
Elder (thinking that he means he only listens to sermons worth hearing, laughingly): "Yes, thank you for your words, but you mean you only come on the days I preach, not every Sunday."  
Bearded Man: "Is there an empty seat available for me to lie down and snore?"  
Elder: "......." <104>  

**Summary**  

The article features a humorous exchange between a bearded man and an elder, where the former expresses only attending sermons on the days the elder preaches, leading to a comical misunderstanding. The dynamics suggest a social critique of religious attendance and engagement, reflecting broader themes in Korean society during the colonial period. Historians might find the representation of religious life amidst colonial constraints intriguing, as it sheds light on individual attitudes toward authority and spirituality during this time.
