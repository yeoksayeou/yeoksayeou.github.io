Special Prize Beetle Extermination Experiment Report, Urgent Recruitment for 50 Won Reward

It is a shameful matter, but Joseon is a country infested with bedbugs. There are no insects that can bother us as much as bedbugs. The exhausted body after a long day cannot enjoy a peaceful sleep at night due to bedbugs, leading to a tremendous loss of energy and ability for those afflicted. When accounting for the losses suffered by numerous people, it becomes clear how significant the overall impact is. To have endured such troubling assaults for 10 years, or even 100 years, without having researched one effective remedy or method to eradicate them is indeed a disappointing matter.

We invite those among our readers who have succeeded in eliminating bedbugs to send us their methods. We will gladly publicize your approach.

1. If it is a remedy, please detail the ingredients, quantities, and timing so that anyone can execute it (taking into consideration those without money). 

Reward: 50 Won for the best entry, and 5 Won for regular selections.  
The deadline for submissions is July 31. (Winners will be announced in the August issue) <51>

**Summary**

This article addresses the significant issue of bedbug infestations in Korea during the colonial period, expressing concern over the lack of effective remedies to combat the problem. The call for solutions highlights the communal struggles faced by individuals under Japanese rule and reflects the socio-economic challenges of the time, such as the burden of pest infestations impacting daily life, sleep, and productivity. Historians may find interest in how pest issues were tied to broader themes of public health and welfare during the colonial period, illustrating the material struggles of Koreans under colonial rule.
