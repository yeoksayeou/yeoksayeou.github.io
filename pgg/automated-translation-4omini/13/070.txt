同志로서 同志에게  
K, my friend. It has been about 10 years since you and I have wandered haphazardly through this murky stream while carrying the banner of our so-called journalistic life! How much of this sweet and bitter world have you tasted during that time? Or how many profits and losses have you experienced? Although I cannot easily know the measure you hold, I candidly admit that at the same time everything is reduced to zero, my past life has been nothing but regret. This is a matter that I must address not only in the context of the social environment but also personally concerning myself.

Now, K, speaking of regret, what use would it be? Regardless of whether we hold each other as enemies or if we are bound by the times, we find ourselves in a position where we carry a signboard of truth and falsehood without any real substance, and when we think about all our comrades around us, we cannot help but see them wandering down the same path as us. Thus, I cannot express my regret or convey your regret without also acknowledging that our friendship is our most fundamental bond.

K, I truly tell you that everything in the past is nothing but regret for me. To put it simply, I admit that I have never had any qualifications worthy of being called a journalist in the first place. As you have experienced, the news organizations (newspapers and magazines) are the ears and eyes of society and must reflect the times in a mirror; as a part of that organization, a journalist should also embody the eyes of society and the times. Isn't it the duty of a journalist to observe all moral and immoral, beautiful and ugly aspects of that society or time and report them under strict critique to serve as a standard for the general public?

So, K, what qualifications do we need to fulfill this duty? You have likewise experienced it—ideally, one must be knowledgeable, observant, and adept at critical thinking and appreciation while being sincere, diligent, honest, meticulous, calm, and decisive. Yet, K, I find myself devoid of such qualities. Truly, I entered journalism without understanding its meaning, wandering haphazardly with the whims of the generation. Although it is embarrassing to say, I believe you, like me, will not be far removed from this situation.

K, I ponder how shameful it is for someone like me, of almost zero qualifications in knowledge and character, to have masqueraded as a journalist for eight or nine years. The guilt toward my conscience and the crimes toward society weigh heavily upon me. Even if I were to assess myself and say you too share this guilt, I wouldn’t find the matter significant if I believed I had been faithful to my duty. However, I have faltered in my fidelity. I should have been diligent, but laziness prevailed more often; I should have been honest, but there were many instances of vulgar deception; I should have been meticulous and thorough but was merely complacent; I failed to remain calm when I ought to have and lacked kindness when needed; I hesitated when I needed courage and was not composed when I should have been; I mixed public and private matters; I could not distinguish between right and wrong, good and bad, beauty and ugliness; I was seduced by a mere drink and succumbed to a single envelope of money; I allowed myself to be deceived by a single victory and succumbed to threats; and when I look at those in green clothes and crimson garments, I remember how I once sacrificed my true self, leading me to profound embarrassment. If you reflect on these, K, you too will likely find yourself in a position to feel the flames of shame on your face.

K, since this matter is unrelated to you, I will speak plainly. Until the day you stop feigning virtue, please remain true to your duties. This was how it was six or seven years ago. Why did you insist on saying "yes" to the 0氏00葬 issue, which everyone else is denying? At that time, you were as good as toasting drinks with his subordinates, weren’t you? Now, reflecting on that, how shameful is it? I recall those six or seven years ago. One of our own traveled abroad but toiled away in some minor position; was that supposed to be some significant national glory, leading to talk of it for three or four months and causing nationwide sorrow? The person who held that minor position later returned and was toasting drinks again, wasn’t he? Even now, looking back, isn’t it a matter that makes one's heart ache?

You may not remember events from a long time ago. Just a few years back, when the entire world despised the 000 incident, why did you insist on emphasizing it as a significant topic? Wasn’t that the case as well? Moreover, recently, from the newspaper that you personally sponsored, didn't you hype a certain movement article across two full pages for three consecutive days? Was that particularly enticing for women? As a journalist wielding a public pen, let alone this, one cannot even dare to do such things.

Is this all to say? In this world, those so-called three thieves are indeed swayed for a mere drink, introducing some place under dubious fetters. A person everyone refers to as an infamous person, too, will praise someone when a mere envelope of money touches them. After all, as journalism’s ethical basis has declined in this world where even major news stories are neglected in favor of personal or peer-focused matters, there seems to be no need to talk about journalistic integrity.

K, from here on, relying on your conscience, outwardly, you must diligently fulfill your duties. Only then can all the regrets of the past be atoned for. Do not speak about it being slanderous or too harsh. I have shared my whole regret with you in the spirit of friendship.

My Hope (Between Lawyers)

Kim Byeong-ru

Regardless of our strength, as members of the legal community whose duty it is to advocate for and uphold the rights of the public, we must assert our legal claims as far as necessary, and we must reject any unlawful acts without reservation.

However, it is true that one or two individuals alone cannot effectively put such principles into action. The circumstances we face as Koreans, particularly those in the marginalized legal community—though numerous, we might not be capable of acting collectively—still, when including both Seoul and the provinces, we are a large consociation.

If we could unite and take legal actions, the impact would surely be great, thereby needing no further explanation. Often, for those in the provinces who are not well-versed in law, they suffer unjustly in matters of property, reputation, or physical harm without knowing how to seek redress; even if they are aware, due to specific circumstances, they remain in the corner of obscurity, only feeling sorrow and lamentation. Individuals who are meant to oversee and regulate often misinterpret or misappli the law, causing unnecessary pain to others and infringing upon rights. 

If we were to have some robust organization establishing legal knowledge and promoting awareness for the public good or perhaps advocating for the reform of past inadequate laws, that seems most necessary to me. Moreover, publishing legal newspapers or journals to provide the public with legal knowledge and commentaries on pressing legal issues is also something I deem necessary.

Two Requests (Between Physicians)

Park Chang-hun

Making remarks to someone in a similar position is a meaningful and beneficial occurrence. In this respect, I feel it’s a great privilege to share my thoughts with a fellow comrade, and I want to simply express two requests. I apologize for not being able to elaborate more due to time constraints.

1. While being a physician is of course a profession, one should not become overly professionalized. The origin of medical practice dates back to the beginning of human history and is among the necessary parts of human life; thus, a physician must possess a strong sense of awareness. That is, it is a grave offense if a physician treats patients merely as one would treat machinery, equating their practice to that of a mechanic with no moral or ethical concern. It is essential to adhere to both the moral laws of humanity and the ethical standards of the medical profession.

Although western medicine has been imported to our country for some time now, the general public still holds a deep-seated suspicion towards it, reflecting poorly upon our profession. Furthermore, to recover and enhance the trust and credit that have been lacking thus far requires diligent effort on our part. The fact that people regard the medical profession as mere business and doctors as mere merchants is nothing short of regrettable. 

2. One should engage with others with kindness and a sense of ambition and self-confidence. The medical sage Hippocrates famously proclaimed, “Nature (God) causes illness; the physician merely cares for the patient.” This means it is not by the physician's own strength that illness is cured; rather, similar to a machine operator using the energy produced by the machine, the physician merely nurses the patient by borrowing the power of nature. Contemporary medicine and pharmacology, therefore, can treat illness only as they borrow natural forces to guide the course of a patient’s health, merely facilitating the course of nature without direct intervention.

Therefore, physicians must conduct themselves with heightened kindness and gentleness, and at times of dire illness, they should be even more attentive and considerate. When patients begin to recover, physicians should humbly express their gratitude by stating, “I am grateful for your recovery.” True humility arises from such genuine feelings; otherwise, if such kindness is not sincere, it becomes insincere; thus, the physician is not functioning as a true doctor but rather operates as a merchant. During this time when Korean economic circumstances are floundering, kindness towards people suffering hardships devoid of economic care leads to inequitable treatment based on social standing or wealth of the patients, and I can’t help but reflect on the implications.

Ten Thoughts (Between Female Educators)

Son Jeong-kyu

1. Let us live diligently even without substantial returns.

2. Let us maintain a self-sacrificing spirit in everything.

3. Let us expand our knowledge while constantly reflecting on our positions.

4. Let us not think of teaching merely as a way to earn money.

5. Teachers with new experiences are often far from practical knowledge, while those seasoned may be unclear about theory. In other words, we should always be aware of our shortcomings and strive to improve.

6. When feeling upset, do not display it excessively. Be aware enough to let the other person know and to exert a proper influence.

7. Let us prioritize moral character over prowess in teaching.

8. Especially as female educators, let us be observant of our students’ inner conditions. There can be many undesirable situations that need serious meetings.

9. While teaching subjects, let’s create connections in the hearts of our students.

10. When feeling lonely, reading would be the best companion. Even reading lesser works, in time, could lead to great inspiration.

As a Christian (Between Believers)

Buyeo Yeonae-ra

Observing and understanding the current conditions of our Korean Christian church, I wish to express a few thoughts.

As a woman from a humble background, my understanding of Christian faith is still scant, and my theological knowledge is quite limited; therefore, I do not wish to provoke the ire of pastors, evangelists, or elders with my observations. However, I trust that learned scholars known for their broad tolerance can admonish me if I make any errors. 

First, taking a glance at the organizational practices of churches across Korea, it is evident that, due to the current times, there are distinct differences between the practices of Western missionaries and Korean pastors. Some of them are young disciples dressed sprightly in western clothing, while there are others, elderly gentlemen with wide sleeves in traditional garments. 

The fact that I, in my youth, now wear a suit, is it indicative of modernity? Yet, those men are addressed as "pastor," "evangelist," and "elder," perhaps only out of reverence for those honorable figures. Upon looking at the annual reports of the church regarding significant issues of tenure, it seems to echo a sentiment that churches are merely the dining halls for these pastors and evangelists.  

It is a problem indeed that pastors have monthly salaries as low as 30 to 40 won (some high salaries reach 80 or 90 won), which might reflect the influence of economic hardships even seeping into the sanctity of churches. 

These supposed representatives of God must remain untouched by worldly desires, yet they seem to be woefully concerned about bread and butter. I wonder if they think about their future, contemplating the heavenly rewards that are expected of their mortal existence, and wonder if they ignore the everyday struggles of survival.

I once heard of a pastor full of vitality who preached energetically to his congregation, saying, "Look at the birds of the air; they do not sow nor reap; yet they do not perish" or “Look at the field flowers; they neither toil nor spin.” Are you, inquiring about their welfare, suggesting that God will care for them? Is it not contradictory that they preach such dependency while ignoring the bread they must provide in their households?

The issue seems to hinge on the precarious matter of economics, yet in matters of spiritual necessity, they seem to be far removed from those concerns. 

They might complain about the burden of charitable duties, yet remain silent on the issue of churches lending their facilities to social organizations. Although they claim that their churches are sanctuaries for God, one wonders what they think when they see those who lend their sanctuaries to facilitate secular organizations.

Additionally, many of these churches face difficulties, such as issues involving church properties, which remain burdensome to churchgoers. Nevertheless, they often adhere stubbornly to outdated customs, constraining their ability to reform. 

Christianity imports from America, yet the American practitioners rent out buildings that often host grandiose dances and plays, managing to balance secular activities with spiritual values. On the contrary, when considering the possibility of renting out a church to a social organization, they seem unwilling to bend on this point. Would they also believe that God would depart from such spaces where interactions of learning and laughing occur? 

Individual church members indeed need to clear their minds by understanding that the heart itself is the genuine temple where God reigns. Modern religion must make fervent efforts to encourage thinkers' freedom of spirit. 

Without placing too much emphasis on rigidity in conditions and constitutions, overlooking the freedom of thought amongst churchgoers signals a trap. 

Efforts to address the root of sinful behavior rather than merely addressing superficial issues like punishment can lead one down the path of outdated beliefs. Conclusively, it all comes down to needs; it is essential to consider how economic and cultural currents impact the future of Korean Christianity. 

It is unreasonable to merely peg the struggles of church finances down to a lack of faith among church friends. If we proceed with such stubborn ideology, Christianity may face ruin within a decade, and believers could find themselves starving on the cold roads.

Therefore, let us shed the conventional and outmoded mindsets, and fervently work towards restoring and maintaining honorable conduct in our beliefs, ensuring that we actively engage in conscientious pursuits.

Listening to Mr. S’s Letter (Between Female Students)

Yi-hwa Specialized School, Mo Yun-sook

Dear Mr. S, among the contents of your recent correspondence, you wrote that the so-called education of new women, by venturing to step into society, exposes contradictions inherent in the education they received. They act on the belief that they have the right to carry out their wishes at the expense of others, like pressing a genuine wife away while being enthralled by luxurious attire and the sounds of a piano. 

Although this does not encompass them entirely, it masquerades as entirely their right, which, to me, appears to be utterly detrimental to thousands of women’s futures. If their education results solely in vanity and material desires, it could become a weapon of misfortune for women. 

To women in学窓, take heed! Maintain a keen awareness! Open your eyes to your responsibilities towards the future of all ten million lives in society, as your education is not only for personal happiness but also for collective growth. Your identity does not end with self-happiness.

Your writing reflects concern regarding the societal actions of educated women and provides pointed advice. While I truly appreciate this, I must address my disagreement with your assessment of the so-called new women. I do not believe that education alone is to blame for their missteps. Education always provides proper guidance, and I believe there are inherent social responsibilities in this context as well. 

In discussing your fifth point regarding the notion that women have been inherently endowed with fragility from the very beginning, we find ourselves questioning the limitations on women's capacity for activity. The societal fabric of women trapping themselves in the simplistic notions of women's purpose in tying themselves to men leaves one wondering when, if ever, gene expands its consciousness and wrests away the shackles of dependency. The feelings of inadequacy echoed in Schopenhauer's words and Boileau's poem wherein "to be human means to become female and thus relinquishing all earthly joys and uncertainties to someone else," resonate deeply. 

Still, regarding the observation that women were historically dominant during the Stone Age or in the Shilla dynasty, it is vital to remember that over time, certain structures of society progressed towards male-dominated narratives, often culminating in the marginalization of women in various spheres. 

Mr. S, we must acknowledge that however deeply invasive societal transformation might feel today, current times still mask the innate strength and potential of women. Thus, let us pursue economic independence resolutely, so we may secure our identity in the framework of modern society, promoting activities that embody the empowerment of every woman.

Please let this yearning for reformation not be an ordinary pursuit but a robust bastion of women’s rights. Let us strive profoundly to improve ourselves, ensuring that as Korean women, we hold our unique identity and plight firmly in our hearts as we aim for true fulfillment.

Farmer M in the Orchard (Between Male Students)

Choi Byeong-hwa

Dear M, I previously wrote to you with a sorrowful note regarding a certain lament, but even now, it seems unworthy of your attention, leaving me with no choice but to respond again. If you are my cherished comrade and friend, take heed of my words.

The most delightful place to call home in the world is surely where I nourished my roots. Among all humanity, the most amiable people are those from my homeland. If we could spend our lives drinking the waters of our native land, honoring our parents and living alongside our siblings, happiness should be at hand in life’s journey. Hence, I strive never to leave the earth that nourished me.

M, you must be astounded by the twist that S has taken in his ideas. He who always yearned for overseas glory has now made a complete about-face. Clearly, there must remain some romantic interest behind this drastic change in behavior. It is indeed challenging for someone like him, an active personality, to become passive and wholly retreat from prior ambitions.

Why are young people en masse drawn to cities today? While the thirst for knowledge is a primary factor, other contributing elements also abound. Yet many flock to the alluring facets of urban living without purpose or plans to contribute back to their hometowns, a phenomenon worthy of concern. 

When scrutinizing the current situation in agriculture, we face a disheartening trend: educated, resourceful youth no longer pursue professional pathways in the countryside, seeking jobs instead in urban settings. We should work tirelessly from our home bases for the development of rural places, reducing illiteracy, and addressing the pressing needs of our communities. 

If there is any admirable force among us, it is hope—a hope for the conservation and enhancement of our rural upbringing. I sincerely wish that S’s enthusiastic embrace of his hometown will yield fruitful effects.

M, regarding the emotional state of K after his heartbreak with Y, it is said he wandered in despair, lamenting that “the rose is charming and queenly among flowers, yet its very essence is difficult, encased by thorns.” In his moments of misery, it seems he builds contempt towards women in general, even distancing himself from his own kin. But now it appears that K has recovered somewhat; he is on a path of self-renewal.

Found among friends, K expressed an interest in pursuing natural science, veering away from his previous literary inclinations. Hindered by past friendships, there was a moment where I too faced stark opposition upon choosing to drop literature for commerce, but alas, here we stand! 

M, in Korea’s current context, what science can we do without? What knowledge can we regard as precious? The guidance around the field of natural science is more crucial than ever; without such guidance, we risk remaining drifted in a sea of ignorance. 

Today’s discussion on natural science reveals that true human progress stemmed only from its flourishment; no other academic field can assert its worth alone amid the advancements seen in nature. It must be said, for our nation’s cultural and social renaissance, propelling developments in natural science will surely yield higher societal fortunes. 

Were we to turn a blind eye to this crucial field of study, we might find ourselves falling to the deep chasm of oblivion. Let’s strive for a comprehensive understanding of these fundamental currents to create progress for our beloved nation.

Dreaming of the City  

Chunpo  

Dear Buk Nong, I expressed a somber melody concerning your recent correspondence. Yet, I find it lacking in sincerity—therefore, compelled by friendship, I take this final opportunity to convey another thought, which I hope you will regard dearly if you truly consider me your dear friend.

Life in the world seldom aligns with expectations, yet in your recent lament, one must believe that city life is not as one expects. While I’m hesitant to call it a farce, I must indeed declare that city life is decidedly ill-fitting for you. 

Dear Buk Nong, understanding these sentiments again, it remains evident that if you, a rural-born individual, intend to navigate city life, you must first possess certain qualifications. Namely, money, knowledge, and a profession are pivotal. If any of these elements were to escape you, what might remain? Beyond cunning skills and fluency in manipulation, or expertise in subterfuge, you would merely find yourself getting lost in the confounding complexities of urban existence, deprived of purpose.

Nevertheless, you belong to a setting where none of these conditions apply. Financially strapped and engaged in agriculture, your city aspirations would dissipate quickly, likely within the span of a year.

How much might one need just to survive? To say the least, let’s estimate it might cost at least 50 won a month, which accumulates to 600 won annually. Should we factor in transport, attire, meals, and perhaps even socializing, one should honestly anticipate 1,000 to 2,000 won being spent in such persistence. This would urge a need to mortgage one's farmland and property back home before even catching a breath.

Even if such a dip into urban life serves as a mere experience, all that remains at the end could be tears, sighs, and regrets—such is the dark entrapment awaiting.

You have lamented that simply “dwelling at home is akin to rotting alongside the flora.” It appears that your noble mindset carries perhaps an echo of various inherited ideologies, yet such are not at play. 

There is indeed a truth to opposite eventualities—those who recognize their inherent worth, irrespective of societal twists. The idealized notion of “slumming it” in the city is misleading; the precious life you harbor, lamented in its dormancy, should rather thrive in the beauty of your pastoral setting.

As the modern world vibrates with noise, though there runs temptation in its urban allure, one must question why this natural and serene existence be neglected. The urban attractions have surely led no one towards decency in their character, so why should we forsake our roots for fleeting vanity?

Turning instead inward, every corner of nature remains a profound teacher. Is it not the vista overlooking Dongshan and the serene view of Xihai? 

To overlook the currents of nature will lead to a monochromatic existence—those who learn from nature's grand lessons will reveal the larger illuminations. 

My heartfelt intention rests in not seeking to belittle you, but to earnestly present this dedication for you and your family. I hold a respect for what’s divinely entrusted to you.

My dear friend, rest assured, as you cultivate your land and reap your harvested joy, we’ll enjoy the fruits along together. Flee not to the temptations of the city, for it holds naught, save for regret.

P.C.生

K군. 그대와 나의 소위 기자생활이란 간판을 걸머지고 過渡라는 이 濁流 중에서 之東之西 함부러 헤매인지도 어언 10년이 갓가 왓구려! 그동안 그대는 달고 쓴 이 세상 味를 얼마나 만히 맛보앗는지? 또는 얼마나 만흔 損益을 보앗는지? 그대의 所持質量은 그대 안인 내가 容易이 알수는 업네만은 나는 露骨로 自白한다면 모든 것이 제로인 同時에 過去 平生이 전혀 悔恨뿐일세. 그는 母論-時代環境에도 訴할 문제이지만 내 자신에도 안이 訴할 수 업는 問題일세.

K군. 이제와서 悔恨의 一節을 말한다면 무엇이 所用이겟나만은 그대나 내나 목구멍이 원수이엿든지? 時相에 억매엿든지? 自動被動間 如何間이 일홈좃코 실속 업는 眞也假也의 看板을 그대로 지고 잇는 처지이요, 또한 주위의 모든 동무 明日의 모든 동무가 同轍에서 헤매일 것을 생각하면 한 마뒤 안이 할 수 업네. 同氣相求라고 K군과 더부러 더구나 한마듸 안이 할 수 업네. 따라서 나의 悔恨과 아울너 그대의 회한거리도 안이 말해 줄 수 업네. 이것은 友道의 至當으로만 아러주게.

K군.나는 실로 말이지 과거의 모든 것이 회한 뿐일세. 爲先 말하면 나는 記者란 看板을 질머질 만한 아무런 자격부터 업섯든 것이 事實로의 自白일세. 그대도 실험한 바와 가티 報道機關(신문이나 잡지간에)이란 社會의 耳目인 동시에 시대의 거울(鏡)이 되야 잇는 것과 가티 그 기관 구성분자의 一人인 기자란 그 者가 역시 사회의 이목이 되고 시대의 거울이 되어야 할 것은 母論안인가? 이것을 己任으로 하고 나선 것이 기자가 안인가. 다시 말하면 기자란 그 시대 그 사회에 대한 善惡美醜間穩的陽的의<26> 大小事를 일일히 觀察하야 嚴正한 批判下에서 여실히 報道하야써 일반 世人의 標準報標準鏡이 되게 하는 것이 기자의 職責이 안인가?
그러면 K군. 이 직무를 能當함에는 엇더한 자격을 가지고 엇더케 處事해 가야 되겟는가? 이것을 함에는 그대도 實驗實感한 바와 가티-母論-學識이 만코 聞見이 만코 경험이 만코 鑑賞에 밝고 批判에 강헤야 되지 안는가? 이러한 知識에다가 誠勤하고 정직하고 周到細密하고 냉정하고도 勇斷스러운 인격을 가저야 되지 안는가?

世上事不如心은 세인의 보통 嘆詞이지만 都市生活不如心은 그대의 近日 嘆詞가 되야 잇겟지? 不如心은 그만두고 不可能을 나는 말해두네. 그대에 잇서서 도시생활은 千不當萬不當이라고 아주 斷言하고 마네. 인격 모욕이라고 감정을 發할넌지 모르나 그대는 도시생활의<37> 資格부터 업는 것 일세.(나도 물론 無資格하다만은)
여보게 北農君. 再演갓지만 또드러 보게. 향촌 生長者로서 도시에 나오랴면 도시생활에 가능할 만한 여러 가지 조건이 붓터야 되네. 그것이 무엇인지 알겟나? 첫재는 돈이요 둘재는 지식이요 셋재는 직업일세. 이것이 업다하면 수단이 조와 挾雜을 잘하든지 비위가 조와 侮辱을 잘밧든지 속히기를 잘하든지 도적질을 잘하든지 그도 못하면 쓰러기통이 라도 두지고 留置場을 안방가티 아는 이가 안이면 안이 되는 것 일세. 그런데 그대는 조건 중에 하나도 해당한 것이 업네. 돈으로 말해도 게오 養牛作農하는 처지니 도회에 온다면 일년이 못가서 톡톡터러 밧칠테고 지식이라야 게오 中等에 不越하니 專門家大學家輩出의 금일에 列班부터 到底不能이 안인가? 더구나 就職難 生活難에 견대다 못하야 境外로 死地로 出沒이 無常한 금일이 안인가? 그대는 순진한 農村生長이니 만치 挾雜手段도 업고 속키고 빼앗는 방법도 모르지 안는가? 그러면 무엇으로써 도시에 나오겟나? 父與祖의 血汗으로써 간신히 싸아 노은 고 農土마직이나 도시인에게 善事하랴면 오는 것도 加하네.
여보게 北農君. 鄕村生長者로 도시에 나오는 者들의 類別을 들께 드러보겟나? 첫재는 父與祖의 힘에 의하야 배우기 위하야 學生身分으로 오는 류가 잇고(그는 여기서 말할 階梯가 안이다) 둘재는 富者의 자식으로 돈쓰기 위하야 오는 류가 잇고(官公間就職에 위하야 오는 者는 제외)셋재는 挾雜性 浮浪性을 가지고 도시와 농촌의 仲媒業으로 오는 류가 잇고 넷재는 농토가 업슴애 노동하기 위하야 오는 류가 잇고 그 다음은 無依無家의 孤兒病身들이 절도나 구걸을 목적하고 오는 류가 잇고(自由業도 잇지만)그 다음은 도시가 좃타닛가 일시 구경으로 왓다가 널너붓는 류가 잇다. 대개 이런 류들인데 그대는 이 列類에 하나도 해당한 류가 못되는 것 일세.

北農君. 그대의 인격을 너무도 侮蔑한 듯 하이. 그러나 그대를 위한 진정의 一團일세. 그대 일신보다도 그대의 가족 더구나 그대의 그 귀여운 기림내 살내 나는 땅을 위하야 이런 말을 안이 할 수 업네. 그대의 자손을 위하야 그대의 隣里親戚을 위하야 이런 말을 안이 해 줄 수 업네. 그대도 그닥 埋沒한 친구가 안이거니 엇지 나를 야속타하겟나? 그때도 눈이 잇고 귀가 잇서 보고듯지 안는가? 현대인이 떠드러 부르짓는 소리가 무엇인가? 눈방울이나 바루박킨 놈은 대개가 떠들기를.
「농촌으로 가거라. 장래의 세상은 농민의 것!」하고 부르짓지 안는가? 그리하야 다시 부르짓기를「도시문명으로 농촌문명에! 도시생활로 농촌생활에!」
하고 도시를 저주하는 동시에 농촌을 동경하는 것이 현대인의 보통심정이 안이가? 一步라도 進한 者 一事라도 知하는<38> 者는 擧皆가 주장하는데 그닥 漠漠지 안은 그대로서 왜이 天然의 행복을 逆逐하려 하는가? 왜 이 自然의 順境에서 逆轉하는가?
그대는 말하기를 「경험삼아 잠간 1년간이라도 도시의 맛을 보앗스면 조켓다」하얏지 또 말하기를 「귀여운 인생이 못처럼 세상에 낫다가 窮村僻鄕에만 蟄居하다가 與草木同腐는 極키 哀情하다고 하얏지? 얼핏 생각하면 그럴듯도 하이. 그러나 이 말은 전부가 無計劃 無住着 無目的의 아무런 뼈가 업는 말일세.
여보게 군. 드러보게 경험 삼아 1년만 지내왓스면 조켓다 하지 이런 節不知의 소리가 怜悧한 그대의 입에서 나올 줄은 참말 몰낫네. 도시 1년이 시골 100년이란 말이 잇네. 이것은 古老의 말이지만 實相 物的 損益에 잇서서 이 말은 꼭 適用되는 말일세. 爲先 일년간 농업의 休止는 말할 것도 업지만 1년간 도시에서의 消費가 얼마인지 알겟나? 적게 잡아 일개월 50원치고 1년이면 600원 안인가? 게다가 내왕 車費넛코 洋服代, 料理代, 眼鏡, 구두, 기생, 갈보까지 구경한다면(경험을 주로 한다면)적어도 1000원 이상 2000원은 들 것 일세. 자- 그런다면 압논 팔고 뒷밧 팔어도 부족이지. 農牛까지 家垈까지 並賣하여야 거의 될가 말가 하지 안은가? 내말이 거즛이면 경험 삼아 한번 해볼테면 해도 보게. 그러나 결국 남는 것은 눈물 뿐이요 한숨 뿐이요 후회분이요 타락뿐이요 밥박아지 뿐이요,구걸뿐이요 심하다면 자살 안이면 發狂일 것 일세.
또 그대는 귀한 인생이 與草木同腐 云云을 말하엿지? 그대에게도 아마 봉건적 遺傳인 영웅 심리가 잇는가 보이. 그럿치 안으면 末世不爲兒의 放浪的 根性이 잇는가 보이. 그러나 그런 것이 안일세. 貴한 人生일사록 농촌에 蟄居해야 되며 英雄일사록 與草木同腐를 해야 되네. 또 지금은 고귀한 인생이 별로 업고 영웅이 또한 업는 세상일세. 만약 잇다면 그에서 더 천한 것은 업는 것 일세.
설사 그것이 잇다해도 결코 도시에만 잇는 것이 안일세. 「서울을 가야 科擧를 준다.」는 말도 발서 진부한 녯 말이요 李斯의 소위 「똥간쥐 庫간쥐」도 그대로 이용해서는 안되는 것일세. 지금은 귀인일사록 농촌으로 드러가고 영웅적 대사업가일수록 농촌에 立脚해야 되네.
이것이 엇지 지금 뿐이겟나? 자고로 偉人烈士가 대개 다 그런 것 일세. 자내도 역사를 아니 말이지 자고로 위인치고 세인의 존경을 밧는 대사업가치고 누가 향촌인이 아이엿나? 煩多하니 외국의 예는 그만두고라도 爲先 우리의 대학자 李退溪, 李栗谷이 다 향촌인이요, 우리의 대장군 李舜臣
