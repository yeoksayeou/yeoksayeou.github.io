**Summary**

The article "Love Calendar" presents a whimsical comic depiction of the different phases and events associated with love throughout the year. Each month highlights various romantic activities or feelings typical for that time of year, such as the excitement of new love in spring or the warmth of companionship in winter. This piece reflects not only the personal experiences of love but also hints at societal expectations and cultural constructs surrounding romance during the period of Japanese colonial rule in Korea, showcasing how romance was influenced by shifting social norms and the complexities of colonial identity.

Historians may find it interesting to note the interplay between traditional romantic ideals and the adaptations made in the context of colonial life, particularly in how these ideals reflect broader cultural changes occurring in Korea during the 1920s.
