발행일	1930년 08월 01일  
기사제목	朝鮮各地 瀑布館 (Waterfalls of Korea)  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellanea)  

Korea is a mountainous country. If we were to exhibit every one of its countless mountains, it would be more convenient to simply make a model of the entire country and present it in that manner. However, the purpose of this exhibition is to introduce the features of the mountains, their products, views, and so on, to the public, and since this exhibition is named a "cooling summer exhibition," it would be most welcome to introduce only those areas of the mountains that are the coolest, where even a glimpse would alleviate the summer heat. Just from looking at photographs, visitors would surely want to escape the sweltering heat that leaves them drenched. Speaking of refreshing places in the mountains, one could mention the pristine Chonwangbong peak of Jirisan, which is still covered in white snow even in June, or the heavenly site of Baengdusan where cold sleet and chilly winds can be felt even in the peak of summer. There is also the recently discovered site called underground Geumgang, which is the Bugok Cave in Yeongbyeon, along with several others such as the Mujeongku Crystal Cave, the Wind Cave in Uijungbu, the magnificent Seoraksan in Inje, Seolbongsan in Anbyeon, and Seolhanbong in Samsu, among others.

However, one of the most refreshing attractions would be to feature the waterfalls found amidst the mountains. So, dear visitors, please pay special attention. Even on the hottest summer days, being careless may lead to catching a cold. From now on, we will present the 25 major waterfalls located in the famous mountains across the nation in order. How refreshing they must be!

Bakyeon Waterfall (Gyeonggi Kaesong)  
"The flowing water of Bakyeon Waterfall winds down to Beomsajeong." This waterfall, known even from the songs of youth! It's one of the most recognizable waterfalls. It takes only about 60 ri from Kaesong, and one can reach it in the blink of an eye by car. However, to truly appreciate the refreshing atmosphere of the waterfall, it is better to set off on foot, wearing traditional shoes and carrying a walking stick, traveling through rugged trails and the valley between the sacred mountains. To add a note, it drops from a height that could be roughly 3,000 feet, but in reality, it cascades from about 100 feet into a spectacular stone jar-like bowl, creating a stunning sight where the water churns and splashes, inducing goosebumps and leaving visitors awestruck. There are legendary tales of Bakjeon from the past, such as Bak Jin-chul who reportedly blew his flute here and influenced the Dragon King's daughter, or from the Goryeo period, there is a story about Lee Yeonggan who composed poems praising the dragon here. But let's set aside these elaborate tales; the incredible rock formations and pines create a major scenic spot that, along with the historical remnants of Songdo, has earned its fame. Many visitors flock here to witness its beauty in every season—spring blossoms, summer greenery, and autumn foliage.

Sinjeong Waterfall (Chungbuk Yeongdong)  
If you take the Gyeongbu Line train and pass Daejeon, you will find small mountains which lead you to a quaint rural station called Sincheon. Walking about 10 ri westward along the mountain path will bring you to a spot named Sinjeongdong, nestled in the well-named Yuksan. Even though it isn't well-known, this waterfall is not to be neglected. The breathtaking scene of the water flying over the steep rock walls, reaching a height of 60 feet on both banks, is truly magnificent. The gorge is filled with unique vegetation, making it a popular gathering spot for plant collectors, with rumors saying it has even changed names to the Yuksuh Waterfall.

If you are determined to see waterfalls in Chungcheongnam-do, I would recommend visiting the East and West waterfalls in Gyeryong Mountain, located in the valleys west of Seokgye-ri.

Baekyang Waterfall (Jeonbuk Jangseong)  
As autumn approaches, when the rice stalks in the wide fields of Gimje-Man-gyeong start to bend, the trains from Gunsan to Jeonju and the Honam main line are packed with passengers. These are all visitors from Honam who come to catch the autumn foliage at Baekyangsan. Just 20 ri east of Jangseong's 4th highway station is a gorge thick with old pines and the landscape is quite beautiful. While visiting Baekyangsa and Yeongmunam's towering plants is worthwhile, I recommend venturing a little further east of Yangsujeong to view this waterfall, as it should not be underestimated. Especially since there exists a miraculous spring nearby believed to cure all ailments.

Yongmun Waterfall (Jeonnam Gwangyang)  
In the west of Jeollanam-do flows the clear waters of the Seomjin River around Gwangyang! Its name is beautiful, and its scenery is absolutely exquisite. Hidden in the western region of Baegwansan is another waterfall called Yongmun which, to speak of it, is a virgin waterfall where the water cascades a good height of about 100 feet. The sight is truly a wonder that makes this a great scenic spot worthy of being depicted in painting.

Jeongbang Waterfall (Jeju Island)  
Continuing southward across the sea, we arrive at Jeju Island! The golden tangerines ripen abundantly, and the diving women gather shellfish, consisting of various sea creatures while wild horses casually roam the shores. However, one might wonder if any waterfalls exist here, as one could similarly question if any mountains are present. Nevertheless, there indeed is a waterfall nestled within the high peaks of Hallasan. Found at Jeongchong Mountain, this waterfall seems to feature among Jeju's ten scenic wonders—a must-see that I highly recommend to visitors.

Cheonjiyeon Waterfall  
While introducing Jeongbang Waterfall, I must also mention Jeju's famous Cheonji Waterfall! Heading northwest from Seogwipo will lead you to an area where an ancient tree stands on a rugged cliff; this grand waterfall reaches about 72 feet high and 30 feet wide! It is famous as a waterfall whose lower pool is also known for the silver fish and eel caught here, making it a great spot for both escaping the heat and enjoying a delicious meal.

Yongdam Waterfall (Gyeongbuk Mungyeong)  
Mungyeongsaejae (Bird Ridge)! Just the thought of it looks steep and majestic. To the northwest of Tonghwa Temple lies this waterfall. When viewed from the great height, the pond below is completely filled with stones, and stories passed down tell of its origin from where a dragon scaled up. Whether the dragon ascended or just floaty, picking mushrooms at the Saejae does not make visiting this waterfall a dull task. 

Flying Waterfall (Gyeongbuk Cheongsong)  
What could be in such a mountain area? There’s a grand surprise. Just 30 ri southeast of Cheongsong, there’s an unnamed waterfall that cascades thunderously from a 30-foot precipice, and even in summer, visitors find a refreshing experience there away from the sweltering heat. 

Mak-am Waterfall (Gyeongbuk Gunwi)  
In a small county of Gyeongsangbuk-do, the fine surrounded Palgongsan (Eight Peaks Mountain) stands tall with its stone walls reaching over 30 feet. On the inside lies a waterfall known as Mak-am because its form resembles a tent and features over 20 feet of cascading water.

Naeon Waterfall (Gyeongbuk Yeongil)  
Traveling north from Pohang for 60 ri, you will reach Bogyeongsa Temple and nestled nearby are the Seogyeongam and Seogungam hermitages. The scenic beauty of the area is serene, and moving into the deeper valley uncovers 12 waterfalls flowing neatly over rugged rocks. Among them, Naeon Waterfall falls at a dramatic height of more than 40 feet, producing a majestic view that makes one feel as if they had stepped into a heavenly realm, and the middle waterfall forms a natural wonder along with rare stones and verdant forests, creating a beauty to admire. This is a spot not to be missed when planning a trip along the east coast.

Yongchung Waterfall (Gyeongbuk Gyeongju)  
This is the spot where Lee Min-je, a scholar from Gyeongbuk, often visited. At Okseong Seowon's hill, there is a large rock on the southern face marked with the inscription "Seongshin Taa" and has the name "Yongchung" inscribed in the scholar's handwriting. Here, the winding waters of the ravine cascade down to create beautiful waterfalls and deep green pools from where they rush down, presenting a stunning landscape typical of a great tourist site.

Suhong Waterfall (Gyeongnam Gimhae)  
If you get off at the station just three stops from Busan, be sure to stop by. However, if you come from Gyeongseong (Seoul) it would be best to get off at Gupo Station, and from there you can reach the top of Subong Mountain. Upon venturing into the mountain, you can meet this waterfall that pours down, with towering cliffs rising on both sides, as the white waves spill forth creating a wondrous sight.

Yonghwa Waterfall (Gangwon Taepyeong)  
Taking the Gyeongwon Line train while passing through Cheorwon, one wouldn't be surprised if they find it not much different from rushing rivers. However, very few visit Yonghwa Waterfall after getting off. It is said to have a drop of about 210 feet.

Geumgangsan Waterfall  
Geumgangsan is indeed a mountain of rocks, trees, rare plants, and streams, as well as waterfalls. Indeed, Korea is a land of few waterfalls. Consequently, we as a people find ourselves not very familiar with them, but upon entering the gates of Geumgangsan, one cannot help but be moved by the sound of waterfalls cascading from every direction—thundering and resonating through each corner of the mountain, it is truly an indescribable feeling. While naming only a few would be insufficient, the Manpokdong in Geumgang and the Goryeong Waterfall in Oegeumgang certainly command admiration and awe.

Bangok Nine Curves Waterfall (Chuncheon Gun Nangsan Myun Bangok)  
Oh dear! Speaking of waterfalls in Gangwon-do, we cannot omit the Bangok Waterfall in Chuncheon. Chuncheon is not only the capital of Gwandong but also an ancient capital of the Myeokguk making its historic sites abundant, including this waterfall, known for its scenic beauty. The waterfall drops about 12 feet and is 5–6 feet wide, and due to the winding stream making about nine turns, it is referred to as the Nine Curves Waterfall. Because the cave's name is Bangok, its scenery is exceptional, and poets often compare it to the famous Mount Wuyi in China. Additionally, the Samhak Waterfall (also known as Gyeongchog Waterfall) near Deok 10th Institute in Chuncheon is not as grand but is located at the foot of Samhak Mountain, a well-known peak in Chuncheon, making it a serene and secluded valley. Above the waterfall lies the ancient temple Sangwonam, and a natural spring, and nearby is a large cave, Tongsagung, where visiting the ancient remnants of the Southern Gate of Myeokguk's heritage alongside seasonal visitors is commonplace, especially in summer when there are large crowds seeking to escape the heat.

Geumgangyeon (Gangwon Gangneung)  
Do not confuse the term Geumgang as referring to the waterfalls of Geumgangsan and instead, it refers to a waterfall located separately in Gangneung, close to Odaesan's Woljeongsa Temple. Recovering from the mountain reduces to steep glimmering seas and at a height of about 10 feet, the bottom is filled with splashing fish. Traditionally, it is said there lives a divine dragon here, and during the flourishing spring season, schools of unknown fish emerge to visit the pond, creating a great spectacle as they dart around, leaping up while climbing the cliff or sometimes falling back down.

Gu October Mountain Waterfall (Hwanghae Anyang)  
According to legends, the ancestor of Korea, Dangun, ascended this mountain before declaring his divinity, making Gu October Mountain a sacred mountain of Korea. Within this mountain site lies a historic fortress resembling a large ship, with no paths leading north or south, yet a single trail allowing access east to west. The waters from all surrounding valleys flow into the river, which cascades down to become a waterfall west of the fortress. While not remarkably grand, it is certainly not meaningless to travel this distance to pay homage to the historic remnants of our forefathers.

Dongrim Waterfall (Pyeongbuk Seoncheon)  
The town is Seoncheon, but the next station after passing Seoncheon is Dongrim Station. Along the Gyeonggi Line, for those who have traveled from Sinui to Shinij, it’s about 6-7 stops to Dongrim. The defense fortress constructed by General Im Gyeong-eup to combat enemies was built with an area of 18,000 axes. The Dongrim Waterfall, located just about 10 ri north from Dongrim Station, offers a delightful experience inferior only to the sense of national sentiment that the fortress evokes, with its beautiful 6-foot wide, 3-foot high waterfalls, creating a perfect backdrop for summer relaxation in the calming forest and vibrant scenery of pears, apples, peaches, and nuts appealing to visitors.

Sambang Waterfall (Gangwon Pyeonggang)  
By taking the Gyeongwon Line and barely passing over the valley of Geumbyeong and Seopho, you will reach the area called Sambang, situated about 10 ri to the northwest. This site is always filled with refreshing sound, regardless of the season as it thunders down from a height of about 140 feet! The sight of it is reminiscent of a million silk threads fluttering in the breeze, and amid the tranquil serenity, the distant sound of thunder accompanies the remains of a beautiful summer day. 

Go-eum Waterfall (Hamnyeong Anbyeon)  
This waterfall, which has been hidden for a long time now, becomes known thanks to the arrival of Cheongwon Line train that leads to Dongcheon. About 20 ri south of the station lies the waterfall where all the water converges before pouring over rocks, transforming into rapids and subsequently forming a pond. 

Unrim Waterfall (Hamnyeong Muncheon)  
In the huge mountain of Duryu, a mighty stone cliff resembling a blade has a cave interior; within it echoes the thunderous sounds continuously. Roughly 10 feet wide and around 30 feet high, the cascade's silhouette cannot be penetrated even by the heat of summer. The sound of the waterfall creates an atmosphere reminiscent of worlds beyond; the rocks are smoothed by years of cold water, and a great stone makes an ideal gathering spot for people to enjoy themselves. With natural wonders surrounding it, in spring flowers bloom while in autumn, the leaves turn, creating a beautiful scene. Although the site is somewhat remote and the trails can be difficult, for those seeking adventure, that would be no real issue.  

**Summary**  

The article presents a picturesque overview of the numerous waterfalls across Korea, highlighting their natural beauty and significance amid the mountainous terrains. It serves as an invitation for locals and tourists alike to connect with the serene and refreshing settings, especially during the summer months. Through descriptions of various waterfalls, the work illustrates how these natural landmarks have been culturally integrated into the landscape and history of Korea.  

Historians might find interest in how the article reflects colonial attitudes towards natural beauty and tourism. It highlights the attempts to engage a Korean identity rooted in its landscapes and folklore, coupled with the blending of traditional practices such as walking to these sites while navigating the modernity introduced during the colonial period.

朝鮮各地 瀑布館
조선은 山國이다. 이 수 만흔 산악을 다 출품하자면 차라리 조선 전국의 모형을 만드러서 그대로 출품하는 것이 가장 편리할 것이다. 그러나 이 박람회는 보통 박람회와 가티 산악의 형세라던지 산물, 경치 등등을 일반에게 소개코자 하는 것이 안이요 이름이 납량박람회 이니만치 산악 중에 가장 시원하야 이약이만 드러도 삼복의 炎蒸이 저절로 살어저 업서지고 사진만 보와도 등골의 땀이 아주 숙 드러 갈 만한 부분만 드러서 소개하면 입장자에게 무엇보다도 대환영을 바들 것이다. 산악의 시원한 곳으로 말하자면 5,6월에도 백설이 皚皚한 지리산의 천왕봉 소개던지 삼복 중에 冷雹寒風이 뼈를 자리게 하는 백두산 천지가의 이약이라던지 근래에 신발견한 소위 지하 금강이란 영변의 蝀龍窟이라던지 其외 茂朱九天洞, 義城의 風穴, 이름 좃차 시원한 麟蹄의 설악산, 安邊의 설봉산, 三水의 설한봉, 설매령 가튼 것을 소개하는 것도 조흘 것이다.
그러나 보다도 더 시원한 것은 산악 중에 잇는 폭포를 출품하는 것이 제일 조흘 것 갓다. 자- 입장자 여러분 특별 주의하십시요. 아모리 이 찌는 듯시 더운 삼복중이라도 잘못하시면 감기가 드실 것이외다. 지금부터는 조선팔도 명산 중에 잇는 25대 폭포가 순차로 나옵니다. 여러분 얼마나 시원하십닛가.

朴淵瀑布(京畿 開城)
『박연폭포 흐르는 물은 범사정(泛槎亭)으로 감도라든다.』 草童牧竪의 노래에 까지 귀익은 폭포! 누구나 알기 쉬운 폭포가 이 폭포다. 개성에서 60리, 자동차로 가면 눈 깜작할 새이에도 갈 수 잇지만 정말 시원한 폭포 맛을 알랴하면 집신 신고 막대 들고 변또 싸가지고 산길로 산길로 天磨聖居 두 산 틈으로 이 폭포를 차즈라. 말을 좀 보태면 三千尺도 可겟지만 정말로는 한 백척되는 언덕에서 떠러지는 물이 웅성 깁흔 돌 항아리 가튼 속에 부드치여 시이커멋케 뒤집히는 광경은 보기에도 무시무시 소름이 제절로 끼치고 눈이 우리 수리하여 질 것이다. 녜전에 朴進七이라는 이가 여긔서 젓ㅅ대를 불어 용왕의 따님을 흘렷다는 니약이도 잇고 고려때에 李靈幹이라는 어른이 글을 지어 용을 종아리첫다는 전설도 잇지만 장황하니 그것은 그만두고 엇잿든 기암괴석과 솔나무 풍나무가 가티 어울리어 一大 名勝를 이룬 것도 송도의 고적과 아울러 세상에 擅名하는 것도 그만한 갑슬 질머지고 잇는 까닭이지만 봄꼿, 녀름 녹음과 가을 단풍에 이곳을 찻는 遊人行客이 끈칠새이 업시 만흔 것드 또한 까닭이＜134＞잇서 그러는 것이란다.

新增瀑布(忠北 永同)

경부선 차를 타고 대전을 지나 조그만 산골정 두셋을 것치면 아조 조그만 심천이라는 시골 정거장에서 나리어 한 10리쯤 서쪽으로 산길을 거르면 이름부터 고흔 玉山이라는 산 속에 層岩絶壁 깍가 세운 듯한 곳에 新增東坮라는 명소가 잇는 것도 차저야겟지만 그러케 이름나지 못한 폭포나마 이 폭포가 잇는 곳을 니저버려서는 아니될 것이다. 兩岸石壁60척을 飛流하는 悽絶한 광경도 조흐려니와 그 골작이에는 기이한 화초가 만해서 식물 채집하는 선생들이 만히  모혀 들어 춤을 흘리는 이가 만타는데 요점에는 웬 까닭인지 玉吉폭포라고 變姓名까지 햇다는 소문이다.
부득이 충남 산천에가 폭포를 차저보랴거던 수수꺽기 계룡산 속에 가서 동폭포, 서폭포의 勝景을 석계리 서쪽 山谷 중에 가 방문하기를 부탁한다.

白羊瀑布(全北 長城)
가을이 되야 김제 만경 넓은 들에 볏ㅅ목이 척척 느러지면 군산, 전주, 호남 본선의 기차 속이 톡 터지게 승객이 만흔데 그것은 뭇지도 마라. 백양산의 단풍을 차저 하로를 소일하려는 호남의 손님들이란다. 장성 4街里驛에서 내리어 동으로 한 20리만 가면 계곡이＜135＞ 깁숙하고 노송이 빽빽하야 풍광이 자못 佳麗한데 백양사, 영문암의 천고식물도 보기는 보아야하지만 약수정 동편으로 족금만 더 가서 이 폭포 잇는 곳을 차저보라. 적다고 깔보아서는 아니 될 것이다. 만병통치의 약수랭천이 거긔 잇는 바에야 엇지 하느냐.

龍門瀑布(全南 光陽)
전라남도 동서쪽으로 축츠저서 섬진강 맑은 물이 구비처 흘러 도라가는 광양의 백운산! 이름도 조타마는 풍광도 絶佳 백운산 송천 서쪽에는 또 다시 이 폭포가 숨어잇서 말하자면 처녀폭이니 비류백척 그 광경은 실로 용성다을 奇觀이 이곳 勝景 되기에 붓그림이 업슬듯.

正方瀑布(濟州島)
