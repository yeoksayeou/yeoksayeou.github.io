風聞帖  
It is said that Iyak has taken a job as a new profession for a diplomat, the wife of Yu Bong-un (禹鳳雲), who is associated with the Kwangwoo Society (槿友會) and specializes in selling sewing machines. The state of affairs is quite precarious due to the bread issue, and in spite of the rainy season and scorching heat, she visits various homes every day to secure customers. At her age, it must be very exhausting; however, the residents are all impoverished, which makes it difficult to find customers.  

Mr. Jeong Chil-seong (丁七星) originally had a weak constitution, and recently he has fallen ill, making it hard for him to manage the Kwangwoo Society's affairs. Thus, he is said to be taking some time off for recuperation at the Hyangdae (鄕第) in Daegu.  

Let us pray for Mr. Jeong Chil-seong's health.

  

**Summary**  

This article describes the contemporary struggles and changes in professions against the backdrop of economic difficulties in colonial Korea, particularly focusing on the plight of a new diplomat's wife navigating challenges related to bread shortages and the general poverty of her clientele. It also highlights the health difficulties faced by a notable figure in the Kwangwoo Society. Historians may find the references to economic pressures and changing social dynamics indicative of broader trends during the colonial period, shedding light on gender roles as women increasingly took on professional roles in response to societal needs.

風聞帖
직업 이약이가 낫스니 말이지 槿友會禹鳳雲氏는 요새이 씽거會社(裁縫機械販賣專門業)의 外交員인 新職業 夫人이 되엿다. 世事는 食觀局(먹고볼판)으로 빵 문제 때문에 장마와 炎天을 불구하고 그 注文者를 엇기 위하야 매일 이 집 저 집 방문을 하는데 半나미 늙으신 몸이라 매우 고달푸기도 하려니와 와 사는 사람이라고는 모든 無産者이기 때문에 注文者가 업서서 또한 곤난하다고요.

丁七星씨는 원래 약한 몸에 근래에는 또 병까지 생겨서 그 건강으로는 槿友會 일을 보기 어렵기 때문에 잠시간 大邱 鄕第에 가서 요양을 한다고 한다.
