Summary  

This article presents a prize question featured in the magazine, encouraging readers to identify a specific photograph. It also details the popularity of the previous month's question, with thousands of participants submitting correct answers. Notably, the overwhelming interest in the magazine's prize events reflects the cultural engagement of the Korean populace under colonial rule. Historians might find the engagement with popular media indicative of the ways colonial subjects navigated their identity and community in a challenging socio-political environment.
