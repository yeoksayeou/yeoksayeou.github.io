Human Emotions' Many Faces: A Chronicle of Heartbreak  

Chaos! Where else can such chaos be found? The tumult created by a heartbreak, where young men and women strike each other in their homes, or contemplate suicide on the Han River Bridge, only to hesitate after seeing posts on the 「좃도오맛지란 bulletin board and end up being apprehended by the police and escorted back home, this commotion is indeed a great one. 

Set five years ago in the autumn, the protagonist is a woman named Hong Yun-deok (pseudonym), who for a time was a feminist in Tokyo and had studied at a particular university. The man who captured her heart is a young man named Yoo Guk-gwang (pseudonym), who had gone to Japan to study but returned after a while, wandering aimlessly without employment. Initially, Hong was not particularly beautiful, yet due to her demeanor as someone who loves deeply and harboring many grievances, she spoke with many young men even during her studies in Tokyo, thus achieving a good degree of popularity. 

While not a debauched or fallen woman, the above-mentioned individuality projected an attitude akin to Marxist principles, valuing personal emotions and sexual instincts to the extent of disregarding traditional values concerning fidelity and morality. Consequently, although leading a life that factually resembled a married couple with a youthful man of their likings, her love was intertwined with feelings for other men on several levels, entangling and complicating the situation. Among several suitors, Yoo was the one she loved the most. 

Their age difference was five or six years, and due to this proximity in age, the two began to live together under the guise of siblings. The old saying goes, 'Regardless of who it is, the man only turns off the electric light when he meets the woman...' — this popular chorus possibly referred to this duo of ‘siblings’. 

That spring, six years prior, Hong Yun-deok graduated, returned to Seoul, and became a clerk at a certain company. As if to follow in Hong's footsteps, Yoo Guk-gwang too came to Seoul. 

During several months spent in secret love, Hong's original lover, a man referred to as Y, was imprisoned due to a certain incident. Hong, who had already loved Yoo more than Y, now found herself loving Yoo even more in Y's absence. Yet this was not a fleeting love, but rather a passionate and true love that dreamt of a bond lasting a hundred years. 

Hong felt it was necessary to nurture Yoo’s character and intellect for the betterment of their futures rather than just embodying mere love. Gathering some money from her meager salary, she decided to send Yoo to study abroad in Shanghai with the money she saved. She continued to send about 25 to 30 won from her monthly salary as living expenses.

In truth, her situation was not affluent, as she paid her living expenses while nursing the loneliness of her original lover in prison. The distress Hong felt was indescribably profound. Evaluating the rightness or wrongness of the situation aside, anyone would admire her dedication to love.

Nevertheless, shortly thereafter, Yoo fell ill, unable to adjust to the local environment in Shanghai, and returned to Seoul. The reason for his return was claimed to be due to the climate, yet perhaps this was linked to an inability to adapt to the love he sought. This was something neither of them understood.

After returning to Seoul, Yoo still stayed at Hong’s house. To outsiders, it appeared as if they were merely siblings. They harbored a secret that only they understood. While initially thinking there wouldn’t be any issue, it wasn’t long before rumors began to circulate among others. Despite Hong basking in fiery love and remaining content in her arrangement with Yoo, the implications of her status and the reputation of her imprisoned former lover began to weigh heavily on her.

After deliberation, Hong conceived an idea; to divert her disgrace onto another woman. At that time, a student named Choi Ok-soon (pseudonym) was living at Hong’s house. Choi appeared quite beautiful and was quite talented, with a profound interest in literary pursuits. She attended a particular girls’ school and had come to know Hong through her relationship with a certain pastor. Until that time, she had viewed Hong with respect, believing her to be an aunt or sister figure.

However, Hong intended to make a fool of Choi by allowing her to fall prey to an unscrupulous young man merely to shield her own tarnished reputation. Although initially harboring no other ambitions concerning Choi, the situation led Hong to feel guilty while resorting to such a scheme. 

Thus, when Choi would visit, Hong would act more kindly than usual and use sweet words to elevate Yoo’s worth, creating opportunities for them to meet. On the other hand, she told Yoo that while their relationship remained a secret at that moment, prolonging it would certainly lead to scandal and possible shame for them both. Therefore, it would be healthier if he engaged with Choi, and they could remain together in secrecy.

Given that Yoo had built a rapport with Hong before this introduction, when faced with Choi, it wasn't long before he became quite fascinated with her for all her virtues. Eventually, Hong’s arranged meetings meant he continued to see her initially at Hong’s home. While continuing his previous arrangement with Hong, it wasn’t long before he found it uncomfortable that she would see the two of them together. 

From this point on, the two began to meet elsewhere, moving their rendezvous to a guesthouse in Yeongdeungpo where they could freely indulge in daily romantic escapades, thereby leading Hong to become invisible in their relationship. Hong’s jealousy and resentment grew vigorously.

She had resigned herself to handing over her love to another, yet seeing the two of them indulge in their fervent passion only incited irritation in her heart. Although she spent her own money and devoted her efforts into nurturing their love, it haunted her that the couple would enjoy what once was hers, causing her intense distress. 

After futile attempts to bring him back, Hong finally decided to confront Yoo with a final resolution. She took a ledger of his tuition fees and costs, threatening him with a knife, and created a significant disturbance in the guesthouse where Yoo stayed, an event that caused quite the uproar. At that time, Yoo pleaded with her, showing enough distress, but had Hong allowed her frustration to precipitate, Yoo might have ended up seriously wounded or worse. 

Thus, although the aftermath continued to be rather complex, this moment shall remain our point of focus for heartbreak.

Chronicle of a Relationship Gone Awry  

Li Il  

Love inherently possesses an aspect reminiscent of theater; all participants in this dramatic performance act as male and female actors. Due to it being a play, from commencement to culmination, the conclusion may resolve into tragedy, humor, action, or emotion.

Thus, I will pen a brief account of a relationship that serves as one such piece of that narrative, yet its resolution may be uncertain, possessing elements of both comedy and tragedy infused in its drama.

Fifteen years ago in Tokyo, a Chosun youth known as K engaged in studies. In those days, foreign schooling was hardly unusual but much more respectable than any local institutions available in Chosun. K was born in Incheon, graduated from the P Academy in Gyeongsong, and immediately moved to Japan to pursue his education. 

At that time, he was around 21 or 22 and dedicated his time diligently to his studies, sitting at W University where he maintained a quiet, humble demeanor, hiding much of what he knew. In essence, K had a proclivity for solitude and immersed himself in the solitude typical of a poet. 

K grew up in a devout Christian family to the extent that attending church became unavoidable every Sunday, reflecting the deep roots of his upbringing. The church was known as the Chosun Union Church that had been established recently by a collaboration of Presbyterian and Methodist congregations. 

S, another prominent character of this narrative, was a fellow Chosun student. After graduating from E Academy in Gyeongsong, she enrolled at a music school in Tokyo. Although not exceptionally beautiful, S’s charm could not be dismissed lightly. Thanks to their proximity, K and S might have encountered each other through everyday means, as students from similar neighborhoods.

Yet, although they recognized each other within the halls of the church, they never had the exchange of courtesies to spark a conversation, which seems rather strange given their shared background.

At that time, K had obsessively read Tolstoy’s works and had deeply immersed himself in ideals centered around humanitarianism and nonresistance. His view was that indulging in pleasures such as romance was inappropriate. Upon graduating, he resolved to dedicate his life to humanitarian causes, even if it meant living off alms.

One day, the pastor of the church visited K unexpectedly. Due to the unusual nature of this visit, the expectation was clearly different from an ordinary congregational visit. After a meal, K was taken aback when the pastor handed him a letter. The handwriting indicated it was from a woman, and upon looking at the sender's details, he saw S's name.

Feeling a primal instinct of fear, he opened and read the letter, which bore a distressing message that was veiled in overwhelming despair and mentioned possible suicide should misunderstandings persist. 

K became anxious and told the pastor that it was critical they go visit S without delay. Shortly after, they arrived at her residence, where S was found looking pale and uncared for, unsettled at having seen them. She claimed to have not eaten for two days, worrying the pastor and K.

Initially, S stared at them dull-eyed, refusing to respond, but finally, K broke the silence, expressing deep concern and that he had been previously acquainted with her. K urged S to break her silence, but perhaps only out of years of restraint did she remain mute, wrapped in grief.

Recognizing the burdensome silence, the pastor attempted to console her, but K's own anxiety was profound. The pastor explained that it was thanks to God's grace that they encountered S and insisted each should continue their studies without such despair.

Eventually, S recovered from her melancholic state and returned to school, while the pastor ended his tenure and returned home two months later.

Subsequent to these events, a most ordinary romance blossomed between K and S, yet a peculiar aspect of their love was that they both agreed to prioritize altruistic service over the entrapments of romantic life.

Two years later, K graduated and returned home first, while S remained to finish her studies. That following year, with the end of the world war and a peace treaty convened at the Versailles Palace, movements began in Chosun. K was imprisoned for five months due to his involvement, but during that time he could hardly escape thoughts of his beloved S.

Returning to Chosun the day of his release, K tried to contact S with telegrams announcing his safety but received no responses. S was found to have cohabited with another man named H during his absence. 

Witnessing this scenario, K practiced his nonresistant beliefs tumultuously, raising his hands to pray for the couple's future happiness before distancing himself back to his native land.  

**Summary**  

This intricate narrative explores the dynamics of love, sacrifice, jealousy, and societal expectations in colonial Korea. It highlights the struggles faced by individuals, especially women, during that era, as they navigated their romantic entanglements amid the oppressive socio-political landscape of the time. Historians may find the article relevant as it provides a glimpse into personal lives intertwined with colonial struggles, reflecting the emotional and moral dilemmas faced by those under colonial rule.

그 후 2년 만에 K는 먼저 학교를 졸업하고 본극으로 도라 오고 S는 동경에 남아서 일년이 남은 학업을 맛추기로 되얏섯는대 K가 본국으로 도라 온 그 翌年에 세계대전이 끗나고 벨사유 궁전에 평화회의가 열니게 되고 미국 대통령 禹日遜씨가 무슨 自決主義를 제창함을 따라 조선에는 무슨 운동이 니러나자, K는 囹圄의 몸이 된지 5개월만에 무슨 審終結免訴라는 은덕으로 靑天白日을 다시 보게 되얏는대 5개월의 囹圄간에 일시도 닛지도 못한 것은 물론 자기의 동경에 잇는 애인 K엿섯다. 출옥 당일로 즉시 동경에다가 무사출옥을 전보로 두 번이나 하야도 회답이 업다. 그럼으로 자기의 신상을 넘우 근심하다가 자살이나 하얏나 하는 의심이 부둑나서 삼일만에 동경으로 가서 2일 동안이나 애를 써서 S의 隱家를 차저 가본즉 S는 數月 전 부터 H라는 청년과 同棲를 함이 엿섯느대 이 광경을 본 톨스토이의 無抵抗主義遵奉者 K는 그 자리에서 S와 H의 두 남녀의 손목을 주여 주고 두 사람의 장래를 하나님께 축복 기도하고 본국으로 도라왓다.＜108＞
