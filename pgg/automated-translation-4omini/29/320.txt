잡지명	별건곤

발행일	1930년 08월 01일

기사제목	女人, 追憶의 더름길 (Woman, The Road of Remembrance) 

필자	金東仁 (Kim Dong-in) 

기사형태	소설 (Novel) 

The night before, having wandered aimlessly all day, I went to rest at the lodgings of Ganso. As was customary, Ganso was present at the lodging. I entered my room and threw myself down heavily. Then, I fell asleep. However, I could not remain undisturbed for long in my troubled slumber. Gradually, I came to realize that I was hearing some noise, and as I became aware, I recognized it was Ganso's voice. The night had grown long, and he returned drunk, apparently unaware that he had entered my room, muttering some incoherent things about the cramped space. I shivered. I was extremely sensitive even to trivial matters, and I held my breath, trying to contain my simmering anger. I pretended to continue sleeping.

Ganso mumbled something else, then collapsed and fell asleep. However, wounded pride and furious indignation kept me from finding rest. I tried to contain my anger, clenching my fists, but ultimately, unable to bear it any longer, I quietly pulled on my clothes and fled the house.

But where was I to go? With no money in my pocket on that late night, this young man found it impossible to find a place to rest. Wandering about, I ended up at Namsan Park.

It was a moonlit night. The streets were deathly quiet. The earth seemed to be in deep slumber. Amidst this, I trembled with rage and excitement, and my back dripped with sweat as I glanced disdainfully at the sleeping city. At that moment, I didn’t think of the spacious room in my home. I didn’t think of Okyeob. My overwhelming anger at Ganso made me cast aside all other thoughts.

The long night felt endlessly stretched. Waiting for dawn to break, I steadily made my way to Cheongjin-dong. During the night, my mind remained engulfed in agitation and anger, but with the morning light, I began to collect my thoughts. I had to settle matters with Ganso. The humiliation I had endured for the first time in my life triggered my proud nature to reveal itself.

At the entrance of his lodging, I saw Ganso.

“Ah, where did you go last night? It seems you weren’t around when I woke up. Now, come on out!”

Ganso spread his arms wide and beckoned me. I glared at him with eyes blazing with hatred.

“Think about it, you bastard.”

“Well, I must have been mumbling something while drunk, right? Are you angry about that? Well, well, it’s due to the liquor, you know. Have you eaten breakfast? Come on out.”

His open and honest words, brimming with friendship, were met with bitter laughter as I declined to respond and turned away.

In truth, at that moment, my nerves were frayed, and I was easily irritated. Even ordinary greetings from friends felt like insults to me.

“Have you had lunch?”

Even such a common greeting felt like an insult. In order to avoid the unpleasant feeling that arose from this situation, I tried to avoid him as much as I could.

At the same time, my feelings toward Okyeob became increasingly odd. Strange waves of resentment and bizarre emotional turmoil swelled within me, combining the unpleasantness and animosity building in my heart into a heavy burden. Whenever Ganso was to be introduced somewhere, I was deeply troubled. It was nothing short of torturous as it humiliated my pride.

“Have a good time.”

As I sent him off in a rickshaw, I did so with these words, but my heart was pained and bitter. If he were to jump out of the rickshaw and come running to me, I would not know how I might feel. Above all, thinking about him entertaining himself at a place where men gathered, my blood rushed straight to my face. I looked upon him as he went to such a place.

That day, my friends and I had gone to Cheongnyangni for some entertainment. It was my responsibility to bring Okyeob to M Automobile Depot. When I informed Okyeob of this, he became very excited and immediately began to prepare his makeup. After seeing him start, I asked him to come to M Automobile Depot as soon as he finished, then left his house.

After leaving his house, I intended to head to the depot, but I felt a strange impulse, diverting me instead to the pharmacy in Apbapgol. There, with a heart burdened with sorrow akin to that of a martyr, I contemplated all sorts of miserable thoughts. After a long time of eagerly anticipating some fun with Okyeob, if he ended up not finding me, he would surely be disappointed. Or he might just be enjoying himself with my friends instead. Perhaps he might wonder why I hadn’t returned. Festering jealousy mixed with feelings of satisfaction brought me deep emotional turmoil that clouded my mind and weighed heavily on my heart.

After enduring two hours under the sway of such strange emotions, I went to Okyeob’s house. There, I was relieved to see Okyeob had already returned.

“Are you done having fun already?”

With a feigned casualness, I asked that question, and his small eyes perked up even more.

“I came back first.” “Really?” “It was boring..."

If all things in this world proceeded according to "theory," I should have run to him and embraced him then. However, in the strangeness of my tangled emotions, I suddenly resisted his answer. My heart filled with joy, yet the words that escaped my mouth were the complete opposite. I berated him. Okyeob's actions seemed a disregard for my friends; hence, it sullied my own dignity. Even while laughing, I secretly longed for one response from Okyeob.

“How could I have fun alone while friends were enjoying themselves? If I didn’t know the visitor at all, how could I entertain myself amidst your friends?”

How much I yearned for one single sentence like this from him. But the lonely words of connection I sought from him that day were the most needed nourishment to my desolate heart. Moreover, such precious nourishment could not be gleaned from home or relatives; at that moment, I could only seek it from Okyeob.

Yet how could I hope to elicit in him the response I so desired, when he was displeased with my unacceptable behavior? I made no excuse; nor did I admit my fault. Ignoring my words completely, he concluded this was his only defense.

After an hour of discussion, he fortunately caught a rickshaw and got aboard. I left the house, unable to utter a single parting word. By then, darkness had fallen. I went to Gwangikseowon. There, leaning against the wall, I watched the ebb and flow of the night market without any clarity of thought. Even amidst a million people swarming in the bustling night market, I felt an intense solitude as if I were alone atop a high mountain.

Eventually, I decided to withdraw into myself. I then asked the owner of Gwangikseowon, Go-gun, to lend me ten won. This was the first time in my twenty-year life that I uttered such a shameful request. Until then, I had never borrowed even a single coin from anyone. How proud I was! Even in such dire circumstances, I had never brought any jewelry to a pawnshop.

My request made, Go-gun appeared very apologetic and confessed that he had no money. I asked again, requesting a small payment order (even if there was no deposit), offering to return it later.

Go-gun looked at my face and, after an uncertain watch, handed me the payment order while urging me several times that there was no deposit. After thanking him, I left Gwangikseowon and headed toward the restaurant where Okyeob was said to be.

Soon he emerged. I took him to a quiet corner outside the entrance.

“Let’s go for a walk.”

It took a long time for my request to register with him.

“Hey, don’t you know my situation?”

“Well, just a moment—there's a reason you’re here, you know.”

“I was called here, wasn’t I?”

I quietly grew dramatic in tone and revealed the payment order. “You mean money? I have it too. If I’m going to a bar with a woman, of course I’d prepare the money.” He took the payment order to scrutinize it in the light before casting a glance at my face and tearing it to pieces.

“Let’s go. Let’s go.”

“Well, we’ll have to announce it to the office, right?” 

“If I said that, I might be sent off. We need to sneak out.”

And he dashed away without waiting for my reply. I lowered my head and followed behind him.

At Cheongmok-dang, he turned to wait for me. Then he suggested that we have a beer.

“I don’t have money.” “I have it.”

We climbed up to Cheongmok-dang. Originally, I didn’t enjoy drinking, so I ordered just a few side dishes while observing his consumption. He polished off three bottles of beer in one go, then exhaled deeply, pulling out his wallet to offer payment.

We didn’t exchange a word during the meal. After settling the bill, we made for Namsan.

We went to a quiet place at the top of Namsan, sitting together. It was very dark, and by this time, it was already past eleven o'clock. Atop Namsan, we were the only two present, devoid of any shadows.

Suddenly, we heard an unusual song in the distance.

“Yōi ni ushiyō” 

As one person sang like this, several others joined in a chorus, and then a loud thud followed. Glancing towards that direction, beyond the pines, there was a bright space shining through. It seemed as if a house was being newly built, and workers were laboring through the night, singing a song while leveling the ground as they worked.

As I listened quietly, a gradually sentimental feeling began to rise in my heart. The song shifted into something sorrowful.

I closed my eyes and suddenly thought of death—just three steps away from where we sat was a cliff, which, if I crossed, I would plummet down to become a corpse in that next moment. I glanced at Okyeob. At that moment, should he even hint at leading me to the path of death, I would not hesitate to close my eyes and take those three steps to jump off.

Suddenly Okyeob opened his mouth.

“Hey, why do you treat me like this?”

I did not answer, but in my heart, I replied to him,

“Why would I want to treat you badly? The words I throw your way are all born from my own pain and discomfort.”

Through this conflict and jealousy, amidst the hidden, bizarre attachment, while experiencing various hardships, my mind was anything but calm. This led me to a yearning for a release from that weariness. Yet, I could not let go of Okyeob.

I had evidently put off returning home, having waited for news from my mother.

One day, my younger brother Dong-pyeong came to Seoul at my mother’s command, and conveyed her message that I should return home.

The next day, I went down to Pyongyang. Instead of returning home, I went straight to my mother’s house. Between my mother and me, an agreement was quickly reached.

1. I will remain in Pyongyang.

2. I will not abandon my wife and children.

3. I will not bring Okyeob into the family.

Under these conditions, I was permitted to take Okyeob as a concubine. That night, as I again set out for Seoul, my heart swelled with hope.

Okyeob also expressed her elation. After giving her some departure funds, I arranged for her to come down to Pyongyang within two days, and when we dined together after a long time at Cheongsong-gwan, both of our faces glowed with hope.

That night, I returned to Pyongyang and went to the station on the morning train two days later to wait for her. However, her shadow didn’t appear. I waited on the next train too. Again, there was no sign of her.

What was she doing? Why hadn’t she come down? Uncontrollable doubts began to plague my heart.

The next day, I received news from Liu Jiyong in Seoul—

“I happened to visit Okyeob’s home around midnight the night you left. I found someone unknown lying in the front room.”

Then, that afternoon, I received a report from friend T that he thought he saw Okyeob at a guesthouse near the Pyongyang station.

The fury of potential betrayal and the crushing of my pride created a furious blaze within me. I couldn’t spare a moment to assess the truth of those words. I wanted to inquire again, but my rationality would not afford me the frame to ponder them. In my heart, I severed the root of “Okyeob” rather brutally.

It was only five or six days later that Okyeob came. She had sent a servant to the house. I happened to meet that servant.

“Is Kim Dong-in here?” the servant asked me.

“He’s not here. Where are you from?”

“I’m from over there. When will he return?”

“I do not know. Why do you ask?”

The servant didn’t offer a clear answer and left. That day, the servant came six times, and I went out to tell him Kim Dong-in was not there each time. The next day, the same thing repeated itself. After about four or five days, Okyeob must have left Pyongyang for good as the servant stopped coming.

I managed to send Okyeob away, but I couldn’t quell the heartbreaking anguish and bitterness within me. Many times I found myself looking at Mt. Myohyang and laughing wryly. At night, I tossed about, unable to sleep.

My body and heart became extremely weak amidst torment and pain, added to the lingering attachments I felt toward Okyeob. My wife suggested I go to a hot spring for some relaxation. Accordingly, I resolved to go to Yonggang Hot Spring and finally set out for Jin-nam-po after many months.

That night, in a state of excitement, I couldn’t fall asleep. This land, this Jin-nam-po, caused me to constantly recall new memories of Okyeob. The places where I had enjoyed happy moments with Okyeob, such as Yukcheondae, Boryeong Temple, and the Grape Orchard, echoed in my mind.

And from among those various memories, a line from a song that she had sung to me kept ringing in my ears—

“In the valley of the three streams, there lies a rock.”

“So much so, where do the stones disappear?” 

“Old men speak of words of wisdom, oh stone.”

I repeated the lines of that song in my mind several times.

The next day, I met a friend named K. He stopped me from heading toward Yonggang, urging me that we should go together to Woryongbong Hot Spring. 

“Wherever it is,” since I was not particularly attached to Yonggang, I accepted his suggestion and decided to go to Woryong.

That evening, K and I boarded a train bound for Pyongyang from Jin-nam-po. Just as the train was about to depart, Okyeob appeared at the door of our carriage, rushing in and saying, “Let me get on, please!” 

My heart leapt with delight, but I did not get on. I momentarily locked my gaze on her beauty before slowly turning my head away.

The train conductor’s whistle blew.

“Hey, where are you going? Do tell me before you leave.”

She trembled in surprise, pulling me toward her. I also did not budge.

As the train whistle blew, she chatted away, then eventually exited the train. I turned my head to the window, and she looked back with a pained face as the train pulled away. 

The next day, K and I left for Andong County. That night, we began a spell of debauched fun in a restaurant named Maruko. The next day was spent in Yunosuke. During this time, we abandoned our initial plan of going to Woryong Hot Spring and engaged in euphoric enjoyment instead—one day at Maruko, and the next at Yunosuke, spending the days immersed in wine and revelry.

At that time, I tasted liquor for the first time. All sufferings and worries seemed to vanish whenever I drank—at least the bitter pain of my heart melted away, swept away, allowing me to enjoy fleeting moments of sentimental joy. While my longing for Okyeob lingered in my heart, I imbibed my way through the days, savoring transient pleasures.

A month passed. Even in Andong, excitement began to wane. One day, I abruptly collected my belongings, deciding not to return to Pyongyang but to head up to Seoul. I booked myself into a family hotel. 

In that family hotel, Kim Chang-young was also staying there.

From that moment on, my escapades resumed in Seoul. The deceased Nam Gyu-pil, Kim Chang-young, Liu Jiyong, and I formed a group, making our presence known at the restaurant starting from the day I returned to Seoul. 

In the midst of our indulgence, one day the suggestion came to go see the autumn leaves at Mt. Sogyo. We agreed to take along a courtesan each. However, I had no courtesan. While I enjoyed drink after drink to bury the pain, no other courtesans seemed to have appeared in my sight like Okyeob. I felt blank about the conversation.

At that point, Kim Chang-young had consulted with his then-lover Kim Sam-joo and proposed for me that he recommended a relatively simple and young courtesan named Hwang Gyeong-ok. Thus began my prospective entanglement with Hwang Gyeong-ok. (To be continued) 

 

**Summary** 

In this novel, the protagonist struggles with his emotions regarding unrequited love and betrayal. The narrative follows his tumultuous relationship with Ganso and Okyeob, along with reflections on his pride and feelings of humiliation. Particularly in the context of colonial Korea, the interactions and societal pressures experienced by the characters illustrate the deep struggles associated with love, identity, and personal dignity during a time of conflict and oppression, capturing the challenges faced in navigating personal and societal expectations.

Historians may find the text intriguing as it encapsulates complex emotions reflective of the struggles for identity and autonomy in colonial Korea. The story reflects divergent personal lives against a backdrop of larger historical struggles, illuminating how these experiences shape individual narratives in a colonial context.

女人, 追憶의 더름길
						金東仁
金玉葉과 黃瓊玉(續)
엇던날 밤, 이날도 하로종일의 방황에 뇌곤한 몸을 쉬이려, 비슬* 岸曙의 하숙을 차저갓다. 례에 의지하여 岸曙는 하숙에 업섯다. 나는 뷔인 방에 드러가서 몸을 커다라케 내여던젓다. 그러고, 곳 잠이 드럿다. 그러나, 나의 곤한 잠은 조금 뒤에 다시 깨이지 안을 수가 업섯다. 둥둥둥둥 무슨 사람의 소리를, 처음에는 꿈결가치 듯다가 마츰내 정신이 들면서 드르니까 그것은 岸曙의 목소리엇섯다. 밤이 깁허서 술이 취하여 도러온 그는 자긔 방에 침입하여 정신 모르고 자는 나에게 자리가 좁다고 무슨 나므렴을 하는 모양이섯다. 나는 몸을 떨엇다. 사소한 일에라도 몹시 신경질이 된 나는, 그때 폭발하려는 성을 쌕이기 위하여 숨ㅅ소리까지 죽엿다. 그리고 그냥 자는 체 하엿다.
岸曙는 몃마듸 웅얼* 나므렴을 하다가 그만 쓰러저서 잠이 들어버렷다. 그러나 자존심을 상한 노여움으로 흥분된 나는 잠이 들 수가 업섯다. 이것을 이것을 나는 몃번을 주먹을 부러쥐며 성을 내다가 종내 참지 못하여 몰래 저구리를 뒤집어쓰고 그 집을 뛰처 나왓다.
그러나 갈 곳은 어데? 기픈 밤 주머니에 한 푼의 돈도 업는 이 젊은이는 몸을 쉬일 곳을 발견할 도리가 업섯다. 이리저리 도리다니든 나는, 마즈막에 할 일 업시 남산공원으로 갓다.
달 밝은 밤이엇섯다. 거리는 죽은 듯이 고요하엿다.＜110＞
대지까지 고스란히 잠이 들어잇섯다. 이러한 가운데서 나는 노여움과 흥분으로 몸을 떨면 등으로는 땀을 벌벌 흘리며 뻰취에 걸어 안저서 잠들은 시가를 흘겨보고 잇섯다. 그때의 나에게는 나의 집의 넓고 만흔 뷔인 방도 생각안낫다. 玉葉의 일은 생각안낫다. 岸曙에게 대한 케다란 노여움은 나로 하여금 온갓 다른 일을 니저버리게 한 것이엇다.
녀름ㅅ 밤이 그다지도 길엇는지. 그 긴 밤이 밝기를 기다려서 나는 淸進洞으로 향하여 직행하엿다. 밤에는 다만 흥분과 노여움으로 다른 생각은 못 하엿지만 날이 밝는 것을 볼 때에 나는 겨우 내 정신을 수습한 것이엇섯다. 그러고 岸曙와 엇더케던 결말을 내이려 한 것이엇섯다. 나의 생애에 처음 바든 侮辱의 아페, 나의 프라우드한 성경은 마츰내 본성을 드러내인 것이엇섯다.
그의 하숙 대문깐에서, 나는 岸曙를 보앗다.
『아, 자네 어젯밤 어데 갓섯나? 깨보니까 업데 그려. 자, 드러가세』
岸曙는 두 팔을 벌리며 나를 마젓다. 그 岸曙를 나는 증오로 불 붓는 눈으로 바라보앗다.
『이자식, 생각해 봐라』
『글세말일세. 나는 취한 김에 무슨 나므렴을 한 것 가튼데, それで怒つたかね? まアま、酒のせいだちょ. 조반 먹엇나? 드러가세』
그, 岸曙의 우정에 넘치는 솔직한 말에 나는 苦笑로서 그의 말을 거절하고, 다시 발을 도리켯다.
사실 그때에 나의 신경은 여간 날카롭지 안엇다. 사소한 일에라도 역정을 내엿다. 친구들의 례사로히 하는 일도 모도 내게는 뜻잇게 보엿다.
『점심 먹엇나?』
이러한 평범한 인사조차 내게는 그것이 모욕으로 들렷다. 그러고 여기서 생겨나는 불유쾌한 모욕감을 피하기 위하여 나는 할 수 잇는대로 벗을 피하엿다.
동시에 옥엽에게 대한 나의 감정도 차차 야릇하게 되여갓다. 긔괴한 시기 긔괴한 試驗感, 긔괴한 증오-여기서 생겨나는 불유쾌, 반감, 이런 감정이 어느덧 나의 마음에 엄 도다서 자라낫다. 그가 엇지하여 客席에라도 불리는 때는 나는 몹시 괴로웟다. 그것은 자존심을 유린 바든 것과 가치 괴로움이엇다.
『잘 놀다오게』
인력거를 타고 떠나는 그에게 듯기 조케 이런 말로 보내기는 하지만 나의 마음은 여간 괴롭고 쓰리지 안엇다. 그가 만약 인력거에서 뛰여 나려서 나는 불리기 실소하면서 내게로 뛰여온다 하면 나의 마음은 얼마나 깃벗슬가. 더구나 이제 그가 요정에 가서(주머니에 돈을 드북이 너흔) 사내들하고 놀 일을 ＜111＞생각하면 나의 왼 몸의 피는 한꺼번에 얼굴로 모혀드는 것이엇섯다. 그러고 그런 장소에 깃버서 가는 그를 뮙게 녁엿다.
엇던 날, 우리(친구 몃 사람과 나)는 엇던 긔회로 문박노름으로 淸凉里를 가게 되엿다. 그때 나의 마튼 책임으로서는 옥엽을 데리고 M자동차부까지 오는 것이엇섯다. 그래서 옥엽에게 가서 그 뜻을 전하매 그는 매우 깃버서 곳 화장을 시작하엿다. 그 화장을 시작하는 것을 보고 화장이 끗나는 즉시로 M자동차부로 오라고 부탁을 한 뒤에 그 집을 나섯다.

번민과 고통 게다가 아직 끈을 수 업는 옥엽에게 대한 미련 등으로 나의 몸과 마음은 극도로 쇠약하여젓다. 안해는 할 일이 업던지 나에게 엇던 溫井이라도 좀 가잇기를 권하엿다. 이리하여 나는 龍岡溫井으로 가려고 몃 달만에 鎭南浦에 발을 드려노앗다.
그날 밤 흥분된 나는 한잠을 못 이루엇다. 이 땅 이 진남포는 더욱 나에게 아직 긔억에 새로운 玉葉의 일을 생각나게 하엇다. 玉泉台, 寶林寺, 五山果園, 옥엽의 집, たまや등, 녀름의 한 때를 옥엽과 가치 보내든 즐거운 몃 곳의 장소가 나의 머리에 왓다갓다 하엿다.

이튼 날, K라는 친구를 만낫다. 그는 나의 龍岡行을 막고 자긔와 가티 五龍背溫井을 가자고 권하엿다.
『아모 곳이라도』 특별히 룡강에 마음이 잇든 배도 안인 나는 그 말을 쪼차서 五룡배로 가기로 방침을 밧구엇다. 그날 저녁 K와 나는 鎭南浦서 平壤行 긔차에 몸을 실엇다. 그러고 그 긔차가 막 떠나렬 때에 우리가 탄 車의 문이 덜컥 열리며 거기 옥엽이 나타낫다. 그는 숨을 씩씩거리며 달려와서, 『여보, 좀 나립시다』

그는 안탁가운 듯이 몸을 떨면서 나를 끄을엇다. 나는 역시 음직이지 안엇다.

이튼 날, K와 나는 安東縣서 나렷다. 그날 밤으로 まる゛こ에서 질팡한 노리는 열렷다. 이튿날은 ゆうのす에서 놀앗다. 그러는 동안, 우리는 五룡배까지 가려든 우리의 본래의 목적은 니저버리고 오늘은 まるこ, 내일은 ゆうのすけ로, 호탕한 노리로서 날을 보내게 되엿다.
나는 그때, 처음 술맛을 아럿다. 모든 괴로움과 번민도 술이 드러만 가면-혹은 사라지거나, 그러치 안으면 녹는 듯한 센틔멘탈한 긔운을 이르켜 주지 결코 아프고 쓰린 고통 그대로는 남어잇지 안엇다. 술에 술로, 玉葉에게 대한 끗업는 미련과 애착과 분노를 그냥 마음에 품은 채로, 나는 취한ㅅ 가운데서 날을 보냇다.
한 달이 지낫다. 인전 안동현의 노리에도 염쯩이 생겻다. 엇던날 나는 갑작이 행장을 수습하여 가지고 평양은 들니지 안코 후더뎍 서울로 올라왓다. 그러고 패밀리 호텔에 투숙을 하엿다. 패밀리 호텔에는 金瓚永이 묵어잇섯다.
나의 노리는 안동현을 떠나서 다시 서울서 시작되엿다. 죽은 南宮璧, 金瓚永, 柳志永, 나 이러한 네 사람의 한 패는 내가 상경한 이튼 날부터 食道園에 몸을 나타내기 시작하엿다. 이리하여 며츨을 질탕이 놀 둥안에 우리는 엇던 날 갑자기 逍遙山 단풍 구경을 가자는 의논이 생겻다. 그러고 갈때에는 기생을 하나씩 데리고 가자는 의논이 생겻다. 그러나 내게는 기생이 업섯다. 술에서 술로 마음의 아픔을 속이기 위하여 질탕한 노리는 즐겨하지만, 玉葉밧게 다른 기생이 아직것 내 눈에 기생으로 띄여본 적이 업섯다. 나는 그 의견에 적적히 우슬 뿐이엿섯다. 그랫더니 金瓚永이 그때의 그의 애인이엿든 金三珠와 의논을 한 결과, 비교적 마음이 진하고 어린 기생으로서 나에게 추천한 것이 黃瓊玉이엿섯다. 이리하여 나와 黃瓊玉의 인연이 매저젓다(此項未完)＜117＞
