新語大辭典  
"Tr" Section  
Tomato - A red annual fruit that Westerners enjoy eating, but it doesn’t hold much appeal for Eastern people. Nevertheless, it is used to describe people who force themselves to eat it while chasing after Western styles. It is often used sarcastically to mock those who are pretentious.  

Todeshen - A German word meaning beautiful or lovely, which changed from Japanese’s **** to tode, creating a euphemism for an exceedingly beautiful woman.  

Trick (trick) - Trickery, strategy, etc. It refers to methods of deception used in film shooting.  

Torso - Also called tolso. A statue that lacks a head and limbs or only has a head or limbs.  

Trophy - A memento of victory or success. It is given as a tangible proof rather than an honorary title.  

Trypsin (trypsin) - A type of enzyme contained in digestive fluids.  

Topic - Topic, issue, title, etc. When people are silent about something, it is often successful to say, “Is the topic really like this?”  

Toe-dance (toe-dance) - A dance performed while lifting the toes. In the West, it is common for dancers to perform this toe dance during a banquet.  

Tournament - A competitive event held to determine champion status.  

Totem - A type of object that certain Arctic indigenous peoples believe has a special relationship with their tribe or family and provides protection. It is a word from the Northern native people that signifies the veneration of animals (usually bear, elephant, eagle, etc.) associated with their ancestors’ achievements, expressed through intricate and mystical carvings set before their homes. One can see such totems represented by decorative posts illustrating their mythological beliefs.  

Special Privilege Class - A class that has certain rights guaranteed not by their own strength but through institutional frameworks and customs. For instance, it includes the heirs of hereditary noble properties.  

Tonkatsu - A term that refers to a type of Western-style dish, considered generally as a representative of low-class Western food.  

Table dhote - A French term meaning a fixed meal.  

Touch off - In relay races, it refers to the action when a runner passes the baton to the next runner. For example, “He excels at touch off.”  

Tarantella - A lively 6/8 dance tune, popular in Naples, Italy.  

Tarantism - A dance sickness.  

Tammany - A term signifying a municipal disturber. It originated from Tammany Hall in New York, associated with the political corruption of a Democratic faction.  

Summary  

This article presents various words and their meanings from a new word dictionary, emphasizing their cultural significance and etymologies. The inclusion of terms like "Tonkatsu" reflects the cultural hybridization occurring in Korea during this period, showcasing the influence of Western food on Korean cuisine and social norms. Historians may find the study of language and vocabulary in colonial contexts particularly revealing, as it highlights cultural exchanges and the complexities of identity formation under colonial rule.

토-템(totem) 북극의 토인들이 자기 종족 또는 자기 가족과 특별한 관계가 잇서 보호해주는 줄 밋는 일종의 유형물. (대개는 곰, 코끼리, 독수리, **등 동물)에 대하야 미신적 崇敬을 바치는 것을 의미한 북방 토인의 말로 아라스카 古市에 가보연 이런 것을 놉흔 나무에다 교묘하고 신비로은 조각으로 표현하야 조상의 巧績을 표증하는 의미로 제집 문전(토인들의)에 세워두는 토-템.폴이란 標柱를 볼 수 잇다. 찬란한 채색과 기상천외한 조각이 제 각각 그들의 신비로운 신앙적 전설과 신화를 감초고 잇는 것이다.
