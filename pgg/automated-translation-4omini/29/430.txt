卽賣館  
1. Introduction of the Twin Maidens  
This booth is an extension of the Cooling Pavilion, and the maids are not just picked randomly like in an ordinary shop; they have been specially selected after dealings with the cold, chilly Arctic to bring the daughters of Princess Snow White.  

They have eyes like crystals, bones like ice, and skin like snow; all the traits of extraordinary beauty are present. They are dressed in a cool and appropriate manner, wearing ice gauze skirts that resemble dragonfly wings, and snow-patterned tops like cicada wings. On their heads, they wear autumn moon crowns and beautifully fitted water lily socks on their feet. As for their eating habits, they only consume ice sugar in cool water for dinner and ice sweets as a snack, so they are as clean as pure water, free from even the faintest human odor, and how could any human smell linger on such beings?  

Friendly to guests, their inherent coolness means that even the most passionate of visitors cools down just by looking at them. In this Cooling Pavilion, they are the most suitable maids surpassing any other beauty. Everyone! Please refrain from carelessly calling them beautiful maids or using the recent Japanese term "hiyakashi." Improper behavior could lead to detention in the refrigerator per the Cooling Pavilion Management Regulations, and you would suffer frostbite in the midst of summer heat, so please be cautioned ahead of time.  

2. Fan Shop  
Now, this place is the fan shop. It features products from Jeolla Province, well-known for fan production, including Jeonju, Naju, Damyang, and Namwon, as well as from Gyeongsang Province such as Daegu, Jinju, and Tongyeong.  

Among these, there are vividly colored peacock fans, jade fans, the peach-pink fan used by newlyweds, the white feather fan once held by Zhuge Liang, and the Taegeuk fan symbolizing Korea. Additionally, there are banana leaf fans, red sun fans, crescent fans, white cloud fans, ivory fans, along with others nicknamed bamboo fans, plain fans, tail fans, and various types of fans available.  

Foreign products include all kinds of Japanese fans and Chinese fans, even palm fans from the South Seas.  

From Pyongan Province, there are narrow fans and bulrush fans, known colloquially as the eight-use fan, alongside recently imported electric fans. Prices vary depending on the item and quantity; however, for those holding a Cooling Pavilion entry ticket, a special 50% discount will be offered. The high-quality bamboo fans range from 1 to 2 won, and plain fans range from 10 to 50 jeon.  

However, just because they are affordable, don’t purchase them carelessly. Last year, due to fair season windfalls, selling cattle and fields to buy fans led to considerable debt, which has caused great distress. And don't carelessly take a Taegeuk fan around; if a country bumpkin encounters you with it, you may be in danger of confiscation as it resembles the Taegeukgi.  

3. Food Shop  
Foodstuffs are displayed in a special section here, and as this is the Instant Sale Pavilion, I won't provide a detailed introduction; I will only mention the prices.  

Ice drinks cost 7 jeon per serving, while shaved ice costs between 10 and 20 jeon per bowl. Watermelons from Gwangju range from 50 jeon to 1 won 50 jeon, while those from Pyongyang range from 20 to 1 won 20 jeon, and the same goes for those from Pyeongtaek. Other items include beer, cider, melons, Korean rice wine, and bean noodles—all available at prices 20% lower than market value. Those who can pay can eat freely as long as they do not have stomach troubles; those without money will be at risk of being scolded, so there’s no need to worry about suffering in the heat while in the Cooling Pavilion.  

4. Cooling Tools Shop  
There are various cooling tools available, among which the most important are curtains, bamboo beds, sleeping mats, and bamboo pillows. The price of curtains ranges from at least 1 won to a maximum of 5 won; beds range from 7 or 8 won to 25 or 26 won; and bamboo pillows are already sold out.  

Other items like wind pillows, bamboo mats, and patterned matting tend to be sold here for over 30% lower than ordinary shops.  

5. Entertainment Venue  
Ordinary entertainment venues offer various performances that are poorly executed, and during hot weather, no one can endure the smell of sweat. However, this Cooling Pavilion has chosen only those performances that are cool, so spectators will not feel heat or discomfort at all.  

Let us briefly introduce the lineup. The first act is the bear dance, and this is no ordinary bear dance; it is the dance of the white bears that live in the bright, snowy Arctic regions. Regardless of whether the dance is good or bad, just the explanation itself will make a cool breeze sweep over everybody.  

The second act features the Jeju diving women. Dressed like flowers, these beauties boast of their naked bodies in groups, freely diving and rhythms to ocean music; anyone who sweats in a sauna would surely feel a chill down their spine from watching them.  

The third act presents real images of famous waterfalls and rivers around Korea and the world. Watching thousands of silver cascades fall from the sky, like lofty white silk threads hanging in the air; or fierce, raging waves crashing nearby; or white clouds and clear ice dispersing all around, who could fail to feel refreshed?  

The fourth act tells the tales of northern peoples hunting in the snow and provides reports from Arctic and Antarctic expedition teams, which are certainly cooler than ordinary lectures.  

Everyone! Please enjoy the performances one after the other and dine well.  

  

**Summary**  

The article details various attractions of the Instant Sale Pavilion, including unique ice-cool maidens, a wide array of fans for purchase, refreshing food items, various cooling tools, and cool entertainment acts. It presents a blend of cultural and commercial aspects during colonial times, revealing societal interests and consumer habits in Korea during Japanese rule. Historians might find the dialogue on cooling methods and entertainment interesting, showcasing how colonial influences shaped leisure activities and commerce.

3.食料店

5. 餘興場
보통의 餘興場은 아모리 자미잇게 우숩게 잘하는 餘興이라도 더운 때에는 사람의 땀 냄새에 견딜 수가 업지 만은 이 납량관은 餘興의 종목부터 서늘한 것만 선택하기 때문에 구경하는 사람이 조금도 더위와 괴롬을 알지 못할 것이다. 이제 그 종목만 잠간 먼저 소개하자면 제일막은 곰춤이니 이 곰은 보통의 곰춤이 안이라 저-빙설이 皚皚한 북극지방에 잇는 白熊의 춤이다. 춤이야 잘 추던 못 추던 설명만 드러도 누구나 몸에 찬바람이 슬슬 돌 것이다. 그 다음 제2막은 제주 해녀군의 딴쓰다. 꼿 가튼 미인들이 나체미를 자랑하너라고 삼삼오오 짝을 지여 갈막이떼 모양으로 萬頃蒼波 우에서 자유자재로 출몰하며 海洋曲을 마추어 발 장단치는 것을 보면 아마 한증막에서 땀 흘니는 사람이라도 등골이 서늘할 것이다.
제3막에는 조선팔도와 세계 각국의 유명한 瀑布大河의 實寫映畵다. 數千尺의 銀河가 九天에서 떠러지는 듯 萬丈白練이 長空에 걸닌 듯 혹은 怒濤激浪이 咫尺에서 용소슴을 치고 혹은 白雲, 晴氷이 사방으로 飛散하는 것을 목도할 때에 그 누가 爽快를 늣기지 안으랴. 그러고 제4막은 북국 사람들의 雪中狩獵하는 이야기와 南北極探險隊의 實地探査報告談이니 그 亦 보통의 강연보다는 헐신 서늘할 것이다.
