기사제목	弱한 者여 네 일홈은 男子니라 (Weak One, Your Identity is Male)  
저자	冷血洞人 (Cold-blooded Cave Man)  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literature, Other)  

Weak one, your identity is male.  
Those who lack gall are men.  

There is no excellence in being a man. Furthermore, the gall-less ones are not any better. In essence, the weak ones are women, but in reality, the weak ones are men, and moreover, those who lack gall are indeed men.  

It is clear that men are reluctant to take responsibility, as they are aware of the women who engage in illicit trade (prostitution) and still refer to each other as “flour” and “the refined gentleman” instead of rejecting those women outright. The very men who call themselves “gentlemen” are, in the evening, secretly trailing behind women.  

Of course, it’s not solely men who behave this way; rather, women also, in comparison, are stronger than men in every way. While women might not fare as well as men in terms of maternal instincts, in reality, when it comes to sexual desire or determination to achieve something, women can often be even more zealous.  

When a woman decides to part ways with a man, she does so decisively. If a man thinks he can win her back easily, he will find himself at the mercy of the consequences. Thus, when observed, it is evident that men are often defeated or scorned by women, who can end up discarding male partners as easily as a hundred men.  

When considering luxury that has proliferated in recent times, it can be said that women are behind this extravagance. They squander money as if it were a mere trifle from their husbands or lovers.  

Even more recently, the emergence of "Korontai" and "Red Love" from Russia has led women to divorce at an alarming rate.  

Despite this, men remain brazen and persistent. The worst of them allow one woman to keep him in the bedroom while another woman is kept in the garden, all while ineffectively juggling their attention and emotions, leading to confusion and derision.  

Isn’t it tragic that the men who understand this situation in contemporary youth social dynamics among men and women are few and far between? There may be friends who argue that calling it social dynamics is exaggeration, but what can be said about the reality of the situation? If exposing this behavior leads to the purification of future social interactions, it would indeed be a positive development.  

However, whether one visits cafes, inns, parks, or streets these days, it is common to find the youth of the 1930s engaging in such behaviors. They appear hesitant and restless at night, while women seem to have already cast aside the notion of chastity as a relic of the previous century.  

Even the day before this writing, around midnight, at the police box in Pagoda Park, a young male and a woman resembling a student were caught, dragging their noses on the ground and pleading for forgiveness. Men were the first to escape while the woman endured the scrutiny of the landlord and everyone else involved.  

If men are not seducing women, why is such behavior occurring? And if women are not seducing men, why is that happening? Ultimately, this reflects a mutual interaction between both genders; however, there exists an odd psychological element among men—a spineless psyche. It means that one views their wife or women closely connected to them as chaste yet contemplates engaging with other women. What kind of contradiction is this?  

Then, as they critique and scorn their partners when their hearts do not quite align, they end up slandering them. Upon reflection, it turns out that true men are notably scarce. Men uphold their pride excessively, thus being weaker than women in genuinely confronting matters.

The Addiction of Americanism  
鄭寅翼  

Modern Korea can be viewed through the conflict of two major ideologies: one being the so-called 'Americanism' and the other 'Russianism.' In terms of the relationships between the sexes, these two ideologies exhibit an alarming prevalence. Perhaps it is merely the author’s delusion, but it appears that 'Americanism' is growing more dominant compared to 'Russianism.' The young people referred to as the new generation of modern Korea, among themselves, portray this 'Americanism' sum as an inevitable historical conclusion that seems only natural.  

The 'Americanism' referred to regarding gender relations can be easily summarized as a tendency toward male superiority. Emerging from years of oppressive male dominance, Korea has displayed a reactionary stance against this, showing that the extreme outcome of inherent male superiority is not unnatural. 

It was only a few days ago that the author attended a lecture by a Japanese detective fiction writer who presented an interesting case. During the past European wars, volunteer forces in the United States suffered from poor enlistment rates. Of course, this is understandable since war is a ground for slaughter; who would willingly seek death? Unless compelled by the state, it was natural for enlistment rates to suffer.  

However, this was deemed a serious issue, leading the government to spur the nation’s enchanting women into action. They encouraged the young men to enlist out of a shared sense of national honor and for global peace, insisting they should not hesitate to respond bravely. Consequently, the young women became agents of the government, urging men to enlist.  

They even threatened that those who failed to enlist would forever be cast out from the comfort of a woman’s soft embrace. As this unfolded, a remarkable transformation occurred: those refusing to enlist found themselves quickly encouraged and taking up arms to fight for the women. All ambiguous notions of national interest or global peace faded before the compelling need to appease women.  

Thus began 'Americanism,' shaking the foundations of gender relations in Korea. Men who observed the plight of American males—all arguing about the oppression of women—became pitiful in their own societal plight. If advocating for women's liberation and the expansion of women's rights results in the oppression of men and the imprisonment of their identities, this could become problematic indeed.  

The newly empowered women might find liberation, but what becomes of the men who are left behind? To put it simply, the 'Americanism' trend in gender relations is pivoting not from the old evils of male oppression to something better but instead to a new form of injustice. It is unfortunate to see how deeply this trend has taken root among the educated youth of today. Perhaps the weak one, your identity is that of an intelligent man of Korea! Such a trend evidently arises from the intellectual knowledge of their time. The case below serves as a notable example of how this male superiority complex persists.  

P wakes up at dawn, washes, chops wood, cooks rice, and even sweeps the floor. Meanwhile, P’s wife remains in bed, engrossed in reading her scholarly references. After breakfast, when she has finished washing her hands, she brings out various Japanese-style side dishes from the cupboard. Their morning meal concludes with them being a picture of harmony, cleaning up together and then heading off to work. However, it is often the case that it is P who returns home late in the evening and ends up cleaning up and cooking. His actions are the same regardless of the weather; he shows no signs of exhaustion. Meanwhile, P's wife shows no sign of guilt or remorse. It is a routine that seems entirely ordinary to them.  

Of course, my friends and I laugh at P for this. Yet, each time he faces mockery, he defends himself with love as a shield. I ask him, “Do you alone possess love?” He responds that he does love his wife. If that’s true, then why is he the one doing all the labor? P says it is because she is the weaker woman, that he, the strong man, should take on the burden.  

Everyone, this presents a fact about the current dynamics of Korean gender relations. Because of greater knowledge and strength, men are becoming weaker. And they utilize love as a means to justify this.  

Men are weak.  

金末峰  

To say that "men are weak" seems obvious, yet it refutes Shakespeare's assertion that "the strong man is your identity." This is written in contrast to the phrase "the strong man—your identity is male." But look upon the men who have wasted years, spending both their energy and money on an education in trade or university. After investing years in studies and accumulating numerous degrees, look where their resumes end up—cluttering corners of factories, schools, or banks, and if they find no value, they are discarded as rubbish.  

Observe the young laborers who wander through narrow alleys in the metropolis, often appearing exhausted and unkempt. Even today, see the young businessman standing awkwardly, overwhelmed by the landlord’s disapproving gaze every time his wife's shadow passes his way.  

Men! Strong men! Rugged and powerful men! Yet all this reflects the conditions and environments shaped by the contradictions within modern society. The murderous recession has drained all vigor from men! It has drained the lifeblood from the veins of every young person! Oh, the weak one, your identity is the Korean man!  

**Summary**  

The article reflects on the changing dynamics of gender roles and relations in Korea in the 1930s, criticizing both men and women for their behaviors and contradictions. It argues that men are weaker for various reasons, including societal pressures and changing views on masculinity and femininity. The analysis indicates that while women assert greater independence, this also comes at the cost of elevating new forms of injustice against men. Historians might find the examination of these issues revealing, highlighting the complex interplay between colonial pressures, evolving gender norms, and societal expectations in this transitional period of Korean history.

번연히 그 녀자가 밀매음(密賣淫)을 하고 잇는 줄 알면서도 저희들끼리 「밀가루」이니 「은군자」이니 하는 층호를 주면서 그 녀자를 차저가지 아니하면 못 백이는 것이 남자이다.
그러고 제왈 「신사」라고 하는 놈이 저녁이면 녀자의 궁둥이를 슬슬 따라스는 것이다.
물론 순전히 남자만이 그런다는 것은 아니다. 말하자면 녀자는 돌이어 어느 편으로 보든지 남자보다 강하다. 녀자는 다소 모성애(母性愛)에 잇서서는 남자보다 못할는지 몰으나 실상 「성욕」(性慾)이라든지 또는 한 번 하고자 한 일은 녀자가 일층 더 하다고 할 수 잇다.
녀자는 한 번 어떠한 남자를 차버리고자 할 진댄 그만이다. 대번에 맷고 끈흔 듯이 차버린다. 섯불리 그런 줄은 몰으고 그 녀자를 차저 단이다가는 욕을 보는 것이다.
결국 어느 때든지 보면 제왈 녀자는 멧백명이든지 홀여내고 버려쥰다고 한 놈이라로 녀자 압헤 가서 굽히지 안는 놈이 별노 업다.
가만히 생각하여 보아라. 근자에 늘어가는「사치」가 죄다 누구에 돈인가 녀자가 그러케 버러서 「사치」를 하는 줄 알엇다 가는 큰 탈이다. 그것은 남편이 잇스면 남편의 손을 것처서 애인이 잇스면 애인의 손을 것처서 나온 돈이다.
더욱이 요 근자에는 「러시아」에 『코론타이』의 「붉은 사랑」이라는 것이 조선에도 쫙 퍼치자 녀자들은 離婚을 물 마시듯 밥먹듯 한다.＜72＞

그나 그 뿐인가. 그 녀자가 번연히 어느 친구하고 결혼한 것까지 알면서 슬슬 따라 단이며 『戰取』니 비러 먹을 것이니 하는 것을 하야가지고 한 놈을 고랑땡을 안긴다.
그러케 되엇스면 둘이서 잘 살엇스면 조켓는데 또 그도 그러치 못하야 얼마 후에 보면 『戰取』하얏다는 놈이 돌이어 『被戰取』을 당하야 죽느니 사는니 한강철교이니 『猫 いらず』 이니 하고 떠든다. 그러면 그 자가 또 죽는 것도 아니다. 그것은 벌서 헛풍이다.
얼마 후 이 자는 또 엇던 녀자를 『戰取』하야 데리고 단인다. 그리고 『氣高萬丈』하야 어떠니 어떠니 하고 잇다.
이것들이 내가 듯고 보고 밤낫 되푸리 하는 요새 청춘남녀의 사교상(社交相)이다. 사교상이라면 너무 과한 말이라고 노할 친구도 잇슬는지 몰으나 현실이 그런데야 무엇이라 말하랴. 또한 이러한 것을 폭로(暴露)시키어 저윽이 사교가 쟁화(淨化)한다면 고마운 일일 것이다.
그러나 요사히 『가페』와 『旅館』과 『公園』과 『街頭』 어느 곳을 가든지 이러한 일천구백 삼십년대의 청춘남녀가 만흔 것이다.
밤낫을 치고 잇지 못해 애쓰는 놋칠까바 애쓰는 놈 놋치고 설어서 덤벙거리는 놈 이것들 가티 보인다. 이러한 뎜에 잇서서 녀자들은 벌서 정조 관념(貞操觀念)이란 것은묵은 세긔(世紀)의 것으로 치고 말게 되엇다.
