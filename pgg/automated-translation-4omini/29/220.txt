기사제목	코끼리 義齒 (The Elephant Denture)  
기사형태	잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)  

客 『I purchased this short cane at this shop yesterday evening, but when I got home and looked closely, it seems it is not genuine ivory, so is it possible for this to occur?』  

店員 『Yes. That is not my fault. I do import it directly from Sarahun (세이론), but the locals there oftentimes use it to make dentures for elephants.』 <82>  

**Summary**  

The article discusses a customer's dissatisfaction with a purchased cane made of what he believed to be genuine ivory, raising questions about the quality of goods sold in the shop. The shop attendant explains that while he does procure materials directly from a region, it is common for locals to use such materials in creating dentures for elephants. This piece reveals the complexities of colonial trade practices and the blending of cultures and materials during a time of heightened economic interest in exotic goods from the colonies, offering historians insight into consumer perceptions and the imperial economy of the 1930s.
