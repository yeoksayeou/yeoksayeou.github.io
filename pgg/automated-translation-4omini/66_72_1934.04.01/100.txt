기사제목	유모어소설, 키쓰내기 花鬪 (Humorous Novel, The Kiss-Catching Flower Fight)  
필자	李啞夫  
기사형태	소설 (Novel)

The husband returned late at night.  
"Hey! Aren't you scared of me? Of the universe?"  
It was a roar that I could not understand.  
"Why are you so drunk?"  
I rushed to support my husband as he staggered across the threshold. However, his demeanor was quite different than the drunken state of the night before.  
"Stop it! It's all nonsense!" he shoved me aside, appearing flustered, and shouted,  
"Humph! You're just a kiss-catching woman, aren't you?"  
I stood there dumbfounded, staring at my husband’s face, wondering why he was acting this way today.  
"Which school did you graduate from?"  
"Why are you asking me that now?"  
"It matters, you know!"  
My husband then threw off his coat, suit, jacket, and tie as if he were a police officer interrogating a thief.  

I felt a bit offended by my husband's behavior, even though I had seen him drunk before. He was treating me as if I were a lowly woman, dismissing my individuality.  
"Though the nature of the universe makes men unreasonable and women must be graceful, how does that justify your actions? If you were to throw insults at a wife, what is this absurdity?"  
Typically, I would consider myself the archetype of a virtuous wife, yet my husband’s conduct ignited a rebellious flame within me.  

His eyes, foggy from the alcohol, glared at me.  
"What are you getting loud about? Is it right for a student and a teacher to gather and play kiss-catching flower cards? That XX from your school... was drunk and..."  
Hearing him say this, I inexplicably felt uneasy. If he was connecting this to the article about a scandal regarding my school's principal that had appeared in the C newspaper that evening, then my existence was being unjustifiably scrutinized because of my association with the unfortunate incident involving my school.  

"Youngja!"  
My husband shouted my name as if reciting a line from a play.  
"Are you feeling guilty about anything?"  
With a sense of impending doom, he tossed this question at me.  
"Why would I feel guilty?"  
Despite my fury, I maintained a calm tone in my response.  
"Are you saying you never played kiss-catching flower cards with that XX?"  
His suspicion-filled gaze clashed with my own desperate defense, and the air crackled with tension.  
"Why are you silent? Silence is an admission of guilt!"  
"What on earth are you saying?"  
Struggling against the tumult of emotion, I shot back at him.  
"Or what? Who will vouch for your purity? Who can guarantee your virginity before marriage?"  

His tongue seemed loose from the booze, and his rants against women and denial of chastity continued uninterrupted for nearly an hour. In the end, he declared,  
"You and I are going to separate tonight!"  
I felt crushed under the weight of his oppressive behavior, unable to respond or resist, only able to cry until my eyes swelled up.  

It must have been past two o'clock. With a somber face, my husband muttered,  
"Let’s sleep today. Tomorrow we will part ways!"  
He seemed to have discovered some new slogan and suddenly collapsed.  

That night, how was I supposed to feel? Dear readers!—a cat that has faced a sudden attack can hardly defend itself, just like I, a wife, could do nothing but surrender to the chaos brought on by the Principal Y's scandal.  

The entire night, I tossed and turned.  
... Kiss-catching flower cards!  
... Divorce!  
(The universe is crumbling.)  

The next day—  
My husband left for his company without even having breakfast. As he was going out, he glanced at me, who had spent the night without sleep, still sniffling, and said,  
"Your hair is a mess!"  
pointing at my disheveled hair before walking out.  

Until noon, I pondered how I could navigate this awkward situation—feelings of helplessness and frustration flooded over me. Could my blissful marriage truly fall apart? But I couldn't remain stuck in sorrow forever.  

Emergency in a married life!  
At such times, we women must call upon all our cunning.  

At noon's toll, I found myself standing at the gate of my friend, Eunsuk’s, from the school I attended.  
"Wow, Youngja, what's happened to you?" Eunsuk greeted me as if she had not seen me for ages.  
"But why do you look worse than before? What’s going on at home?"  
"..."  
"Why? No answer? Hurry and spill it out!"  
Her relentless curiosity overwhelmed me, and I could hardly find the strength to say anything while sitting in a corner of the room.  
"Just speak! What’s the matter?"  

After I finally spilled everything to Eunsuk,  
"You’re young; that’s probably it. You’ve only been married for a short time..."  
She lowered her head for a moment, then said,  
"Actually, I went through something similar too. But you know I have a way with a situation, right? So all the chaos that erupted last night—I patched it all up..."  
That statement brought me immense relief. It seemed like Eunsuk had a solution! Thoughts rushed through my head—would it be possible?  

Eunsuk had a proud smile on her face.  
"So, this morning we both got up, laughing and joking around! You girls probably panic when something happens, but that’s just foolishness. You have to keep your composure and think of ways to resolve the problem!"  
"But sister! What should I do?"  
The self-satisfied demeanor of Eunsuk did irritate me a bit, yet as I was in dire need of help, I couldn’t hold back.  

"The key to resolving this is really simple!" Eunsuk said, and went into her room to fetch a small note.  

She handed me a sheet of paper with the words  
"XXXX  
XXXX"  
written on it and said,  
"Hurry home and think about this!"  
giving me a playful pinch as she pushed me out.  

Returning home, I began to contemplate the meaning of this note. This would determine my fate. I couldn’t eat lunch or dinner; for five hours, I racked my brain over what these characters might mean.  

Eventually, I regretfully thought I should have studied Chinese characters more diligently in school, as understanding this note was proving challenging. But, as the saying goes, diligence pays off. After searching for meanings and analyzing vocabulary, I finally discerned what Eunsuk had communicated through her cryptic note. I also began to formulate a plan of attack for when my husband arrived home.  

- A Counterattack!  
- A Thousand Women to Represent the Vengeance of the Humiliation!  

Preparations were completed!  

That night my husband finally came home, well past midnight, slightly tipsy. Today, Youngja was not the same as yesterday; I had no need to show loyalty like a dog or be docile like a sheep.  

"Seems like you’ve had a good time!" I threw the first offensive remark at him.  

Taking a stoic expression, my husband replied,  
"Enough!"  

Surprised, I continued,  
"Anyway, regarding what you said yesterday, let's clear everything up tonight... I too spent all day pondering the matter, and it seems that if we simply part ways, there is no other course."  

My assertive manner surprised even me.  
"You're so reckless!" he retorted, while one eye scrutinized my face, and the other seem to probe my intentions.  

"There is no need to drag this matter since it will only lead us into conversing about my principal's scandal, all of which will increase tensions between us. Hence, let’s cut all emotional ties and settle everything right after tonight!"  

I could hardly believe how bold I was becoming.  
"Then let’s not have any lingering feelings. How about you?"  
My husband appeared stunned by my level of firm resolve.  
"Stop talking! I’m not going to bemoan your leaving!" he has got firm, but the change in my attitude surprised and obviously irritated him.  

For a while, the tense silence hung heavy between us. Embarrassed, my husband seemed prepared for anything and turned to impulsively urge me to leave.  

"Before parting, I have one last request for you! That kiss-catching flower game—because of it, our joyful marriage has been destroyed. Right now, while in the act of parting, wouldn’t it be so much better to play it at least once? Who knows when we will be apart for good, and where this resentment might take us later on?"  

As I finished speaking, whether I sobbed from pain or true sadness, everything broke loose, and tears streamed down my face.  

"Fine! I’ll grant your last wish!"  

After a moment’s silence, my husband jumped up from his seat, took out the cards from the desk, and handed them to me. "Let’s play a round."  

The next day—  
It was a clear and beautiful day.  

My husband, having finished breakfast, glanced back at me as I stepped out the door, smiled broadly, and left for work, seemingly cheerful. When he returned home later that evening, he saw me in the kitchen cooking dinner and smiled again as he walked past.  

What a delightful scene, isn’t it?  

**Summary**  

The humorous story presents a couple's marital conflict stemming from misunderstandings, jealousy, and suspicion, culminating in an exaggerated declaration of divorce. However, through wit and clever negotiation spurred by a friend's advice, the main character manages to maintain the warmth in her marriage. This reflection on gender dynamics during the colonial period reveals intriguing insights about women's agency—they are depicted not merely as passive victims but as active participants in navigating their struggles, despite the oppressive societal norms of the time.
