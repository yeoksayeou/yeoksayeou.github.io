Science, Humans and Monkeys  

The hands and feet of monkeys, as you all well know, are skillfully designed to hold and grasp objects. This is because they have lived in trees since ancient times, dating back to their primate ancestors, and their form has naturally evolved to suit that environment. 

However, the hands of humans also possess no little ability to grasp objects, as our ancestors also lived in trees in the distant past. Therefore, even our feet were used for grasping objects. As evidence, remnants of the muscles used by our ancestors for gripping with their feet still exist in our present-day feet. Yet, humans came to realize that they no longer needed to live in trees and gradually descended to the ground. Consequently, the activity of grasping objects with their feet has devolved over time. <46>  

**Summary**  

This article explores the evolutionary relationship between humans and monkeys, emphasizing the anatomical similarities that stem from their shared ancestry as tree-dwelling creatures. It details how the human foot has lost its dexterous abilities as humans adapted to a terrestrial lifestyle. The discussion provides insight into the scientific understanding of human evolution during the colonial period in Korea, revealing how notions of evolution were likely linked to broader discussions about identity and civilization amidst colonial pressures.

科學, 사람과 원숭이
						三山人
원숭이의 손발은 여러분도 아시다십히 교묘하게 무슨 물건이고 훔켜쥘 수가 잇습니다. 이것은 옛날부터 즉- 원숭이의 선조때부터 나무우에서 살어왓기 때문에 그 형태가 자연히 거기에 적당하게 진화된 것입니다.
그런데 우리 사람의 손도 물건을 훔켜잡는데 아모런 부자유가 업는 것은 역시 우리들의 선조도 먼- 옛날에는 나무우에서 살엇기 때문입니다. 그래서 발까지도 물건을 훔켜잡는데 썻든 것입니다.
그 증거로는 지금의 우리들의 발에 선조가 물건을 훔키는 데 썻든 근육의 유물(遺物)이 아즉까지도 잇습니다. 그러나 사람은 그후 나무우에서 살 필요가 업슴을 깨닷고 땅우에 나려와 살엇기 때문에 차차 발로 물건을 훔키는 활동이 퇴화된 것입니다.<46>
