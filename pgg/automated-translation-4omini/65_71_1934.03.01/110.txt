잡지명	별건곤 제71호  
발행일	1934년 03월 01일  
기사제목	金剛情話, 蝴蝶과 鈴蘭花 (The Love Tale of the Diamond Mountains, Butterfly and Lily of the Valley)  
필자	金陵人  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literature and Other)  

It was one evening on a certain day when a grim wind, symbolic of the late spring storm, began to blow! The location is at the renowned scenic spot of Geumgangsan (金剛山), specifically the Koryongyeon (九龍淵), where a tragic yet beautiful story has taken place among the cliffs that have stood for eons.

In a crevice so narrow that even a single hand could not fit since the beginning of the universe, a single lily of the valley (鈴蘭) had bloomed alone, growing quietly and unnoticed. Suddenly, one evening, it tasted the unpleasant, grim wind, and the innocent flower's face was filled with tears of sorrow.

At that moment, from somewhere unknown, a butterfly (蝴蝶) came whose wings had been worn out, and it approached the lonely lily of the valley, which had never before met a dashing young butterfly. How desperately it flapped its wings, almost ready to fall, not being able to move any closer than a foot away from the flower. Seeing this, within the heart of the young lily of the valley, a deep sympathy and concern began to rise. However, it was also the first time encountering the appearance of a young male butterfly, sending the flower’s head down in modesty and turning it slightly red, while it remained silent with a barely audible breath.

Now, the play begins here. 

Butterfly: "Excuse me, Miss Lily of the Valley! As you can see, I can hardly move an inch in this secluded place. Magnificent and beautiful Miss Lily, could you perhaps show mercy and let me stay a while longer?"

Lily: "Oh, how embarrassing…"

Butterfly: "That is understandable. It is only natural that you are surprised at a moment like this when you have blossomed unnoticed in this sacred and beautiful place. However, because you have bloomed alone and unexpectedly, Miss Lily, your body is purer than the Milky Way and whiter than a pearl!

Ah! But what an evil fate this is. Who could have known that such a pure girl would be approached by a creature battered by the waves of the world and sullied? But if everything is a matter of fate, then we must accept such unavoidable circumstances.

Miss Lily! Could you kindly bend that gleaming and fair body and allow this pitiful creature to stay in your embrace? This way, with my sick and faded body, you could provide a resting place for this weary soul and allow me to regain my spirit for new life from tomorrow onward."

Lily: "Oh, how can I!"

Butterfly: "Yes, it makes sense to hesitate, but isn’t it time for a decision? Look over there. Black clouds are gathering in the sky. Over the mountain, strong winds are rising, you see?... Now, Miss, please make your decision quickly."

Lily: "Oh, how can I!"

Butterfly: "Then it seems that my unfortunate body cannot remain. I will have to fly away with what broken wings I have left, heading nowhere. If I die along the way, so be it… Ah! Miss Lily, please be well!"

Lily: "Oh! Where are you going? Surely you can't fly far! My love! My love!"

... Thunder... 

... Rain...

Lily: "Ah! The sound of our voice. This storm! Oh, I must have been a foolish girl, too cold-hearted! To abandon such a weary one in this wild evening? Hello, my love! My love! Where are you? You definitely must not have gone far!"

Butterfly: "Yes!"

Lily: "Wait! How did you respond? Yet while I hear your voice, I cannot see your form. What is going on?"

Butterfly: "I’m stuck here on this rock."

Lily: "Is it really so? How unfortunate! You cannot fly away like this? Hello, my love! I must have been too reckless. Quickly, come over here and into my embrace!"

Butterfly: "Thank you. But I have no strength to fly."

Lily: "Oh no! We cannot have that. Quickly, come over! I will bend like this, so just move a little and come closer!"

Butterfly: "Thank you. This kindness will last even beyond death."

Lily: "Don’t say such things. Just come now! Ah! You made it. Now, I will wrap you in my garment, so don't worry at all and rest easy."

Butterfly: "Ah! Now I am saved. Since I have received your precious love, even if I cannot live on, I have no regrets."

Lily: "Rest easy. Since I have granted you this heartfelt promise, I have solemnly resolved to share both life and death!"

Butterfly: "Thank you. This must surely remain as a sacred love inscribed on this holy mountain for a thousand years."

Thus, on that stormy evening, among the grim winds, the scene of pure and sacred love was drawn. 

Ah, however, there was a sorrowful and pitiful reality. The fierce and merciless storm did not cease and continued to pour down all night long. Amidst this horrifying fate, the lily of the valley and the butterfly held each other tightly and bravely endured. But against the overwhelming force of nature, the strength of the young lily proved far too weak.

In the end, the stalk of the lily of the valley broke, and the butterfly fell to the ground from the flower’s embrace. 

However, the sacred love continued. Even as it fell, the butterfly, recalling its fleeting spirit, made an effort to burrow deeply into the roots of the lily in order to merge back with it.

The next day arrived. 

The weather was clear. 

But still, the lily of the valley remained faint. Days passed, and with the brightening light, the lily finally regained consciousness and bloomed beautifully again.

However, it mourned the loss, unable to know where its love had gone. Little did the lily know that the deceased butterfly’s broken body had nourished its roots, allowing it to blossom again.

Moreover, this fact would be known to no one for generations to come. Only the sacred mountain would stand tall for a thousand years, and the holy waters would flow for eternity.

**Summary**  

The narrative illustrates a poignant and tragic love story between a butterfly and a lily of the valley set against the backdrop of the mountains. As the butterfly, weakened by its journey, seeks comfort in the lily, they explore themes of compassion, sacrifice, and the interplay of fate. The article reflects the deep emotional connections and cultural representations of nature in the face of struggle, which may resonate with colonial sentiments of loss and separation experienced during this era in Korea.

Historians might find the representation of nature as a backdrop for love and suffering interesting, as it reflects Korea's cultural history of valuing nature amidst the challenges of colonial rule and may symbolize resilience against oppressive circumstances.
