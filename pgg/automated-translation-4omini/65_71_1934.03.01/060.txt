World's First Collection  

The world's largest flower is the Rafflesia from the South Seas, with a diameter of 3 feet.  

The world's longest bridge is the Brooklyn Bridge in the United States, measuring 6,500 feet.  

The world's fastest car is located in the United States, achieving a speed of 2.6 miles in one minute.  

The world's most accurate clock belongs to the British Parliament, losing only one second over 200 days.  

The tallest tree in the world is found in Australia, and it cannot be measured for height.  

The tallest woman in the world is an American named "Matahan," who stands 8 feet 2 inches tall.  

The longest-living tree is a cypress in Taiwan, which is over 2,000 years old.  

The largest statue in the world is the Statue of Liberty in the United States, standing at 151 feet tall.  

The fastest tram can be found in Berlin, Germany, also achieving a speed of 2.6 miles in one minute.  

The largest book in the world is a 9-foot-wide map located in London, England.  

**Summary**  

This article showcases various world records and achievements, such as the largest flower, the longest bridge, and the tallest woman. It highlights remarkable aspects of nature, engineering, and human existence up to the early 20th century. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this article reflects the fascination with Western advances and curiosities during a period of Korean vulnerability under colonial rule, providing insight into contemporary views shaped by colonial influences and the search for national identity.
