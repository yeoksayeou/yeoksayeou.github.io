발행일	1934년 03월 01일  
기사제목	종달새와 금붕어 (Lark and Goldfish)  
필자	尹石重  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Lark,  

O lark that ascends high into the sky,  

Isn’t the peak that you reach filled with joy?  

When you soar into the empty sky,  

Do the clouds not look lonely?  

Sometimes, I wish to become a goldfish,  

Floating silently in the water, glide away.  

Goldfish,  

O goldfish that swims deep into the water,  

Doesn’t your heart feel heavy?  

When the cold waves brush softly against your body,  

Still, are you really comfortable?  

Sometimes, I wish to be a lark,  

Soaring high and singing cheerfully, don’t you wish that too?  

<43>  

**Summary**  

The poem contrasts the free-spirited nature of a lark with the confined existence of a goldfish, exploring themes of longing and the desire for freedom. It reflects on the emotions of both creatures, questioning their contentment in their respective environments. This piece could interest historians as it provides insight into the emotional landscape of the era, revealing how individuals in colonial Korea might have used nature and metaphor to express their feelings of confinement and yearning for liberty amidst societal constraints.
