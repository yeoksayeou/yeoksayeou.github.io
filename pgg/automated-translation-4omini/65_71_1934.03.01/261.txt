★地方讀者  
Byeolgeongon has undergone a renewal of its system and content, and as a result, its reputation has greatly increased, leading to a readership that has more than doubled. Therefore, we are dedicating ourselves to the development of this magazine and to creating a publication that is even closer to you, our readers. From this March issue onward, we will provide a dedicated local column, seeking communication from you, our local readers. We hope that you will actively engage with this space and make it your own, using our humble efforts. Any article related to your locality will suffice. Please write and send in your contributions. (Editorial Department) <56>  

**Summary**  

This article discusses the efforts made by the magazine Byeolgeongon to revitalize its content and engage with its readers, particularly those in local areas of Korea. By introducing a local column, the publication aims to foster communication and contributions from its readership, signaling a shift towards a more interactive and community-oriented approach. Historians may find it interesting how this initiative reflects the broader socio-cultural dynamics of Korea during the colonial period, especially as it relates to increasing local engagement amidst a colonial backdrop.
