趣味잇는 통계실  
Life spans are rarely reaching seventy, a saying goes.  

The lifespan of a person is unpredictable, with abortions before birth, deaths in childhood, passing away in one’s twenties or thirties, and dying in one’s forties or fifties. A father or mother, with deep affection for their husband or wife, and children with utmost filial piety, along with sorrowful weeping siblings and close friends, may find themselves shedding bitter tears without knowledge of the reason. Since time immemorial, utterances of "Life spans are rarely reaching seventy" have flowed from people's lips, indicating that perhaps among the aged of seventy or eighty, few are able to taste life. However, it seems that surprisingly, in our Joseon, according to the census from last year, there were as many as seventeen centenarian men and twenty-two centenarian women, and thirty-nine thousand five hundred thirty-two men and women between eighty and ninety-nine years old. Generally, women tend to outlive men, yet among them, there was only one miraculous case of a man who reached one hundred ninety-nine years old. If that individual is still alive today, he would be one hundred twenty-one years old! It’s quite curious whether he remains healthy or has passed away during this time.  

Severe separation and bereavement  

This statistics survey was conducted in October of last year, revealing that in our Joseon, there are one hundred fifty-three thousand widows and widowers who have tragically lost their spouses. Out of these, there are as many as one hundred fifty thousand individuals forced into separation from their spouses. It was found that, statistically, there is about one case of separation for every sixty-six couples. Undoubtedly, the cases of separation that go unreported are countless; child marriages are the main cause. Child marriages are a blight. How many tears have been shed because of this sorrowful separation?  

Money going up in smoke within the city  

Assuming that each household spends at least ten jeon (a unit of currency) on a pack of cigarettes each day, the money going up in smoke in the city of Gyeongseong (경성府) totals at least six thousand seven hundred fifty-three won daily, which amounts to over two hundred thousand won monthly. With thirty-eight thousand six hundred people, this money could sustain them all for a meal costing three won and fifty jeon. However, assuming a household only buys a ten jeon pack of cigarettes daily seems a bit inadequate.  

One day and night of cholera germs  

If cholera (고레라) germs were to grow wildly, a single germ could multiply by fifty billion times in just one day and night, reaching a weight of two thousand kan. What a horrifying number!<57>  

**Summary**  

This article discusses the mortality rates and longevity in Korea, revealing surprising statistics about centenarians and the nearly one hundred fifty thousand cases of bereavement resulting from a spouse's death. It highlights the distress caused by child marriages, leading to early deaths and separation, while noting the considerable amount of disposable income spent on tobacco within Gyeongseong. Historians may find the exploration of societal issues surrounding family and mortality within the context of early twentieth-century Korea revealing, particularly in understanding the impact of colonial rule on social structures and personal lives.
