One Person, One Story  
						Lee Seong-hwan  
I become the focal point and make credit direct to the creditors on the left line, while beating the debtors on the right line. That person also becomes the focal point, and then squeezes the right line, and so the next person does the same, and so forth. When the right line continues, it means that there exists a continuous circle of debtors, and the creditors on the left line also essentially become focal points to secure the credit of the left line, and so the next person does the same, which indicates that there are continuously existing creditors on the left line as well. Thus, if the line signifies infinity, who could be designated as the focal point?

In this issue, would it not be the case that each person is simultaneously a focal point and both debtor and creditor? In this world, who was it that first uttered the word "credit"? When it comes to this matter, one might feel overwhelmed and unable to find a clear answer. <93>  

**Summary**  

This article reflects on the intricate web of debt and credit that characterizes financial relationships between individuals. The author uses a metaphorical approach to illustrate how each person can embody the roles of both debtor and creditor, creating a seemingly infinite cycle. This commentary is particularly revealing from the perspective of Korean colonial history, as it highlights the challenges and nuances of economic interactions under Japanese rule, where trust and credit may have been deeply affected by the societal changes and pressures of the time. Historians may find this exploration significant as it touches on larger themes of economic dependency and the shifts in social dynamics during colonization.

一人 一話
						李晟煥
내가 중점이 되어 左線의 債權者에게 신용을 직히고 右線의 債務者를 족치니 그 사람 또한 中點이 되여, 右線을 조르고 또 그 다음 사람도 그러고 그러고 하야 右線은 갈사록 債務者가 잇고 左線의 債權者도 그 실은 자기가 중점이 되여 左線의 債權을 갑기 위함이라 하고 그 다음 사람 또 그러고 그러고 하야 左線은 갈사록 債權者가 잇다 하면, 線은 無限을 의미할 때 중점이 된 자를 누구라고 할 것인가?
이 문제에서는 사람마다 中點인 동시에 債務者오 債權者라는 解答박게는 더 업지 아니할가? 이 세상에 신용이란 말을 누가 내엿는지 이 문제에 이르러서는 코가 납짝 안을 수 업슬 걸.<93>
