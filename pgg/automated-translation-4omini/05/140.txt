A Visit to the Underground Country  
玄黃堂에서 金玉均  
Although there is pain in the yearning for a new birth, how must our ancestors have viewed the present Korea amidst its deepest suffering and mystery... If there are eyes underground, if there are voices underground, what would those people tell us... We set out on a journey to visit the patriots beneath the surface.

Until about ten years ago, the majority of our twenty million compatriots would recall Kim Ok-kyun as one of the "Four Villains" or as "the Great Traitor Ok-kyun," an impression deeply ingrained in their minds. This was due to the influence of the ideologies held by the pro-Japanese and corrupt intermediaries in society.

Even after enduring the bitter tears of indignation, the vengeful spirit of the teacher, who had no place to voice his resentment, wandered without respite for over thirty years (the Korean government has made superficial gestures towards exoneration, but...) lamenting the unending decline of our nation while helplessly watching its degradation. There were many times he would have gritted his teeth and clenched his fists, feeling his blood boiling as he witnessed the relentless attacks of the wolves of ruin ready to pounce, leaving him sobbing bitterly for the suffering of our people, so hopelessly helpless.

Indeed, the teacher surely had a great deal of fiery resentment. If his spirit were alive, he would surely vomit blood at the current state of affairs. Recently, there have been glimpses in the news and magazines of his fragments, and through this, the world has come to know about him, albeit marginally. However, the few who admire him have done so merely for his greatness or remarkable intelligence, lacking an understanding of his sublime ideals and virtuous plans.

It is likely because, in his lifetime, he could not realize even a fraction of his grand ambitions. He left the world cruelly, his deep insights and plans remaining unactualized. One can see that the Gapsin Coup (甲申事變), which could be seen as part of his ambitions, became instead a perfect weapon for the authorities at the time to slander and attack him. Despite his outstanding abilities and insights, he could not seize the rare opportunity to rise and ultimately fell into the enemy's jaws, becoming a tragic and unfortunate historical figure. At that moment, as he was assassinated on the 28th of March in the 30th year of the Gapsin calendar (1892) in the East Asian Trading Company in Shanghai at 3 PM, he departed with a heart filled with infinite regret, and now he lies in the underground.

In hope of bringing some consolation to the teacher, I prepared myself and hoisted a bottle of the wine he loved most, and even now I wonder if he is aware of the pain.

Climbing mountains and crossing rivers, moving through fields and valleys, I made my way to where my eyes led and my feet took me. After some time, I suddenly arrived at a location where the sun was bright, and a bracing breeze blew. In the midst of the beautifully adorned, intricately decorated architecture of a grand hall that seemed to soar into the sky, the sounds of drums and jade symbols echoed in harmony, and the gods and people danced together while birds and beasts flitted about joyfully. Was this a celestial paradise or some other transcendental world? Regardless, it was certainly beyond human realm.

"Since I've come all the way here, I should at least take a look around," I thought, gradually drawing closer to a gate that bore the sign "Dongheung Gate" (東興門). 

Upon entering, I marveled at the vibrantly blossoming flowers and lush greenery, and after lingering in the beautiful garden for some time, I was drawn in by the ebb and flow of music. Eventually, I found the grand hall, "Hyeonhwangdang" (玄黃堂), resplendent before me.

Hyeonhwangdang Q&A

Bravely stepping inside, I saw elegantly dressed scholars gathered for a serious discussion. A person seated at the front instantly caught my eye. He was a stately figure with a regal air, wearing a garland on his head and holding a fan, seemingly engaged in profound conversation. I briefly stepped aside to observe the situation inside, and the figure at the front was undoubtedly Kim, the teacher whom I had admired for so long.

Overcome with joy, I rushed over to him and respectfully greeted him.

I said, "As a humble villager, I am honored to meet you, teacher." He responded in a very gentle tone, "You are a youth of good spirit from our homeland. Thank you for deliberately coming to this remote place, and let me introduce you to the friends seated here."

Everyone in attendance, delighted, took turns expressing their warm gestures of greeting. Notable figures included Lee Jae-won, Hong Yeong-sik, Seo Gwang-beom, Park Yeong-kyu, Shin Gi-seon, Lee Do-jae, Yu Gil-jun, and Liu Hong-gi (to name a few), all great figures who had fallen during the struggle for righteousness, as well as others who were known as valiant warriors sacrificed for loyalty like Park Ji-suk, Yun Hong-ryeol, and many others who were present that day.

The atmosphere of joy exuded from the gathering felt like meeting a close old friend, with many asking about those still living from the old days, such as Park Yeong-hyo, Seo Jae-bil, Yun Ji-ho, Ryu Hyuk-ryu, Lee Gye-wan and Shin Yeong-hee.

I said, "I regret that as a younger generation, I was unable to pay my respects to the esteemed teachers during their lifetimes. However, it is with great fortune that I now meet you, and I consider it a privilege to have this opportunity to hear the ideals of you, the teacher, while earnestly asking you to guide us in the direction of future Korea, instilling our ignorant youth with an enlightened spirit."

At this, the group collectively let out a heavy sigh that seemed to sink into the ground, exclaiming, "If only things had turned out just as we thought they would back then! It is heartbreaking to think about how, had the people back then been as enlightened as today’s Korean youth, things would not have ended up like this..."

The teacher responded, "Ah, indeed, the courage of today’s Korean youth is truly worthy of admiration... This is indeed commendable. While there were indeed no heroic souls or brave-hearted patriots in our time, we were caught in the decay of centuries of false customs, and the people were filled with a distressing lethargy, so much so that it was rare to see anyone responding even when called upon."

At this, Byun Sooil, who was the only one animated, interjected, "That is because foreign advances of free thought and ideas have entered, stirring the timid spirits that had deeply ingrained themselves in our sluggish moral values and outdated customs, while also adding in the valiant and noble spirit of the northern land. But how far can this go? The trend of pure stream...”

I stated, "In recent years, I have dedicated so much effort to collecting materials on the Gapsin Reform Movement that I believe I have developed a considerable understanding in this regard, though there are still a few points that remain perplexing. I hope you will not hold my inquiries against me, but rather help clarify my doubts."

The teacher replied, "Yes, I shall explain as best I can..."

I continued, "Forty years ago, our society was indeed a dark realm, where it was understood merely as 'A people can be led but cannot be made to know,' while officials only faked authority. The priority was to continuously bring in Western civilization and advance the people's intellect through means like education and industry, intending to foster national wealth.”

In essence, it would mean developing the populace’s capabilities first while gradually working towards political reforms and the expulsion of foreign power. However, the ambitions you held at that time, relying on the faint power of Japan while simultaneously imploding the moribund power of the Great Qing, seem to me utterly baffling, and from a practical standpoint, rather reckless, hence the failure was understandably inevitable in my view. Besides, stimulating public intellect is the proper sequence of affairs. Therefore, must we not build an alliance with the majority of the masses and dismantle the minority of the privileged class? We must also oppose the local powers' encroachment as it is the need of our time and a sure path to success, isn’t it?"

The teacher's expression brightened, and with an upbeat tone, he replied, “You speak well, indeed! Clearly, developing the concept of public knowledge through influx of Western civilization is of utmost importance. However, considering the circumstances of the times, this cannot have been the foregone conclusion. We've also sent comrades abroad to see Western nations and endeavored to indirectly imitate Japan’s civilized framework, sending talented students to Japan with Fukuzawa Yukichi’s connections, while also appointing Inoue Kaku-go and running the half-official Hanseong Sunbo newspaper. Yet none of this was satisfactory.”

He continued, "I am also aware of our limited strength and the enemies' experience, as well as the distinct differences between the Qing and Japanese powers. At the time, foreign nations, barely understanding Korea's existence, were maneuvering to benefit from both the Qing and Japanese powers deftly. Given the unfavorable situation, I was well aware that the timing was not right and, with the hope of waiting for the opportune moment, sought foreign merchants as allies, but as fate would have it, they led to my eventually resigning. After the Imo Incident was past, with Gyeongmu-jo gone, Kim Heung-sik returned to the palace after being captured by Qing troops, and since the pro-Qing faction had upended everything internally, they were siphoning away the people’s blood while the Yuan Shikai collaborated with those under him to monopolize all foreign contracts, conducting diplomacy directly. This situation was intolerable for a patriotic individual, yet what good would come from merely awaiting decay?"

He reminisced, “When considering the state of governance then, neither the provinces nor the populace had the knowledge or capability yet to discuss matters. Those at the center thought something was right to be right and something wrong as wrong, and mere followers there was. Regardless of any enterprise taken, one must first secure political power, and thus the winds would shift in favor." He continued, "Lessons learned from the Meiji Restoration of Japan illustrate that, despite facing a meager military, they resisted a formidable force and, with relentless struggle, ultimately toppled the shogunate and restored the emperor, swiftly establishing firm foundations."

He sighed deeply, saying that when viewed from our perspective, our aspirations looked so feasible. Yet knowing the timing had been fortuitous, how melancholy it is to realize that a few days more during the crises could have entailed controlling foreign diplomatic agendas while enhancing military protocols, allowing for a flourishing and liberated Korea built upon the foundation of a four-thousand-year-old nation... How lamentable it is to dwell upon such thoughts!

Then he listed several policies that could have been implemented that would, as per military matters, lead to expansive reforms in government (regarding the amnesty of the Great Prince and abolishing aristocracy) with a dozen measures that could have transformed our situation! 

The failures at the time were attributed to poor judgment and indecision, coupled with the military’s slackness that led to the divulging of military secrets and Feng's mismanagement regarding the diplomatic resolution with France. But it ought not be surprising to be expressing these sentiments now, as circumstances evolve and everything eventually irretrievably shifts…"

As he exhaled yet again with a mix of sorrow and care in his expression, all of us fell into a contemplative silence. He seemed lost in thought, then a sudden beam of light illuminated his expression as he looked at me with a majestic authority.

“However, in meeting you, I urge you to remember this and relay it faithfully to your fellow countrymen! Lately, our beloved homeland has echoed only with sorrowful laments. Although it is fully legitimate to be grieved by the grim realities, for those at the mercy of fate, such concern grows into sorrow. Reading the newspaper, each publication, or hearing the conversations, the entire nation is flooded with voices of lament and despair. This only invites disappointment among the population. Are you not aware that disillusionment leads to cowardice, shrinking back, weakness, and ultimately ruin? To inspire dreams and awaken the heart is surely a responsibility of those in control of public opinion. Despair followed by resignation is truly terrifying. Upon such a path, discontent will comprehend only bitterness. Here, among the masses, courage reigns, filled with spirit, boding bright prospects for our future. I have hoped for this steadfastly over the years. What do you think?”

I answered, “I share the same thoughts, and upon returning, I will ensure your high discourse gains wide dissemination in favorable light. Furthermore, you have endured the tragedy of your ignoble demise, with unbearable humiliation and extreme hardships throughout these decades! Thus today, allow me to see to it that I bring you what little comfort I can, refusing to turn a blind eye to your spirit as I gingerly present the wine and medicinal items I have brought, hoping they too offer you solace.” 

“Thank you! Our movement may have been considerably radical, and unfortunate outcomes encountered caused misunderstanding among those of moral standing at the time, and hence I hold no ill-will regarding being reviled by society. I feel gratitude that recent newspapers and literary associations have begun to clarify the true spirit of our reform endeavors. We comrades have been gladdened to witness some satisfaction in this regard.”

I declared, “To carry forward the teachings of our predecessors means aiming to cultivate a future generation of capable individuals, hence there is no room for modesty in that ambition. Today's youth hold nothing but the desire to thrive together with others.” 

The teacher, after hearing this, next mentioned the urgent discussions underway regarding the wider East Asian context and the Korean situation while welcoming other notable figures to engage in more discussions. As the atmosphere cheered, bottles of wine were poured and laughter filled the air as the gathering continued, with music and later celebratory dances erupting amidst the higher tones of voices where various sentiments mingled harmoniously.

As the session came to a close, the attendees stood in unison, calmly exchanging farewells. He enveloped my hand moments longer, nodding graciously as he spoke earnestly, “The medicinals you brought shall be greatly appreciated, and I hope these remedies will serve me well!”

He smiled faintly at the thought of his ailment, “We, as fellows, gather at times to deliberate on world affairs and the situation of the East Asian question, finding ways to fortify our lineage and future. Yet will this endeavor ever bear fruit? I only wish to focus on cultivating individuals and uniting our spirits as we strive—to bestow that idea I ask of you.”

With a blend of his somber disposition and a gentle smile, he squeezed my hand as I turned to leave. Looking back, I could no longer hear any more voices, thus I returned, no longer feeling disappointment nor deprivation. 

That day turned out to be the full moon of the first month as my brethren engaged in feasting and merriment, singing joyous hymns, and the sounds of our holidays echoed all around. But in my heart lingered the truth that our path must lead towards illumination and virtue where destiny awaits.  

**Summary**  

This article depicts a vivid imaginary visit to the spirit of Kim Ok-kyun and discusses his aspirations for Korea against the backdrop of the struggles faced during Japanese colonialism. The narrative interweaves poignant reflections on lost opportunities for reform and the potential for youth empowerment. Historians might find interest in this piece for its expression of national identity and aspirations during a time of oppression, highlighting the concerns of emerging youth movements and the enduring legacy of independence activists.

地下國訪問記, 現代 朝鮮靑年에게 與하노라
						玄黃堂에서 金玉均
新生하려는 苦悶일망정 가장 苦롭고 또 어즈러운 中에 잇는 只今의 朝鮮을 우리의 先人은 엇더케 보고 잇슬가... 地下에 눈이 잇다면 地下에 말이 잇다면 그 분들은 우리에게 무슨 말슴을 할가 ... 우리는 地下의 志士를 차저 訪問의 길을 나섯다.

지금으로부터 약 십년전까지도 우리 이천만 동포의 대다수는 金玉均이라면 「四凶」의 일인으로나 혹은「大逆不道 玉均」이라는 것이 누구나 얼핏 뇌리에 인상되엿섯다. 그것은 사대사상의 소지자이며 袁大人崇拜者의 本尊인 항간 부유배의 망론에 중독되엿슴으로써이다.
비분통절의 눈물을 먹음고 忍辱鬱忿의 情怨을 하소연할 곳 조차 업는 선생의 원혼은 이래 삼십여년간 (韓國政府에서 형식상으로 伸寃식힌 일은 잇섯지만은) 구천명로를 유난방황하면서<28> 국세의 日非함을 무한히 嗟歎한 적도 잇섯스며 후생의 卑㥘無氣한 것을 한ㅅ끗 애원히 생각하기도 하엿스며 반도 강산을 俎上에 올려노코 날카라운 毒牙銳瓜를 응그리고 달려드는 豺狼群의 螺集吸血을 볼 때에 그는 온 몸의 血潮 일시에 끌어 오르면서 두 주먹을 불끈 쥐고 절치통한한 적이 몃번 이엿스며 우리 대중의 귀의할 바이 업시 날로날로 빈혈위축되여가는 처참한 광경을 볼 적마다 그는 한을을 우러러 방성통곡한 일이 한두번이 안이 엿슬 것이다.
엇잿던지 선생은 절통한 설어운 冤情이 만흘 것이다. 만일 그의 영령이 잇다면 그의 입으로는 피를 토할 것이다. 최근 수삼년 동안에 신문으로 혹 잡지로 그의 편린이 간혹 발로되엿슴으로 세상에서 그의 일을 대강 알게 되엿다. 그러나 세상에서 그를 숭경하는 만흔 사람은 그의 기량이 위대하엿다든가 지능이 탁월햇다든가하는 일종 영웅숭배심에서 나오는 그것 뿐인고로 광명한 그의 대 이상과 정대한 선생의 대 경륜을 이해하는 이는 지극히 적다.
그럴 것이다. 생전에는 그의 이상과 경륜의 九牛의 一毛도 실현해 보지 못하고 滿腹經綸을 품은 그대로 무참히 죽어서 그 정체가 사실화되어 세상에 나온 것이 적은 까닭이다. 이상의 일단이라고 볼 수 잇는 甲申事變은 도리어 당시 권간에게는 선생을 毁詆공격하는데 절호무기가 되엿슴으로 공전절후한 그의 기량과 식견으로도 맛침내 조흔 기회를 엇지 못하야 필경 적진에 뛰여들어 적의 독수에 꺽구러지고만 만고에 불상한 불우지사 이엿다. 때 마침 선생의 삼십사회기(갑오 삼월이십팔일 오후 3시 於上海 東和洋行제1실 피격 于 洪鍾宇) 임으로 천고의 유한을 품고 영원히 지하에 누어잇는 선생을 조곰이라도 위로해 드릴가 하야 나는 행장을 정돈한 후 죽장집고 마혜신고 생전에 가장 조와하시든 포도주한다쓰 질머지고 지금도 그저 치질을 알으시는가? 하는 생각으로 「치질약」두어봉 사가지고 지하국으로 김선생을 차저간다.
산넘ㅅ고 물건느고 논틀로 밧틀노 빗틀빗틀 풍타죽량타죽격으로 내눈 쏘는대로 내 발 가는대로 한정업시 빗틀거름을 거러가것다. 얼마를 갓던지 가기만 하다가 홀연히 한곳을 당도하니 일월은 명랑하고 서기가 령롱한데 산고수려한 한복판에 金碧이 交暎하고 단청이 찬란한 대전각이 덩그럿케 공중에 솟으러처 잇는 그 가운대로부터 은은히 흘러나오는 鼓瑟聲佩玉聲에 神人이 和하며 飛禽走獸가 모다 춤을 너울너울 춘다, 여긔가 선경이냐? 玉京이냐? 극락이냐? 천당이냐? 엇잿든지 별유천지이요. 인간은 아니엿다.
「하여간 어대거나 기왕 왓스니 드러가 구경이나 하고 갈ㅅ일이다.」하는 생각으로 차츰차츰 드러가니 「東興門」이라는 현판이 놉드랏케 부터잇다. 그문을 들어서니 綺花謠草爛熳한데 기암괴석은 사이사이 정원을 한참 구경한 후 집행이 질질끌며 음율소래 따러드니 심*한 숩풀속에<29>「玄黃堂」이 宏傑하다.

玄黃堂 問答

나는 가장 용맹스럽게 중당에 드러서니 의관이 彬彬한 濟濟多士가 죽-느러안저서 무엇을 한참 토의중인 모양이다. 정면에 단좌한 이가 얼른 눈에 뜨인다. 軒宇당당하고 위풍이 름름한 사람이 머리에 화관 쓰고 손에 團扇들어 지금 한참 고담준론이 풍발하는 판이엿다. 나는 잠시 한엽헤서 당내의 동정을 살펴 보니 정면에 안즌 이는 사진에서 만히보고 내가 주소로 동경하며 흠모하는 김선생이 분명하다. 엇지나 반가운 지 의식을 불구하고 와락 뛰여 달녀들어 선생 압헤 나아가서 공순히 인사하얏다.

선생「군은 고국의 지기잇는 청년이다. 이가티 陋地를 일부러 차저 주시니 감사하며 여긔 동석한 여러 친구는 다- 당일 동지제군인 즉 소개하...오...」좌중의 다사들은 한ㅅ끗 반가운 빗을 띄우며 차례로 닐어나서 따뜻한 악수를 준다. 李載元, 洪英植, 徐光範, 朴泳敎, 申箕善, 李道宰, 兪吉濬, 劉鴻基 (大致) 朴齊絧, 尹雄烈 등 일대 英傑이며 邊燧, 卓挻植, 尹泳觀, 白樂雲, 李秉虎, 朴應學, 李建英, 河應善, 鄭行徵, 林殷明, 申重模, 申福模, 朴裕宏, 李宣果, 金鳳均, 黃龍澤, 尹景完, 尹景純, 李寅鍾, 李殷鍾, 高永憲, 李燦永 등 당일 충의에 순절하든 벽혈용사들이엿다.

선생「고맙기도 하오구려! 우리의 운동이 실로 과격하엿고 또 불성공으로 맛치고 말어서 당시 구도덕가에게 오해를 밧을 만치 되엿고 따러서 일시 세상에서 唾棄惡*하든 것을 나로서는 소박도 불만히 생각하지도 안는 바이며, 근년에 와서 각 신문사와 開闢社에서 개혁운동의 眞精神을 잘 규명해 주어서 우리 동지들은 항상 내심에 만족히 생각하는 바이오. 또 나의게 대한 선전은 넘우 과거가 만허서 양심에 붓그럽고 恐悚의 땀을 금치 못할 때가 만읍듸다. 이것만은 그만치 해 두엇스면 조켓든걸 ...」
필자 「先哲을 繼함은 來學을 開하려는 주지에서 선생의 근본정신을 규명하야 新進 有爲의 투사를 배양하려는 것인 즉 거긔까지는 겸손하실 것 업슬 듯 하오며 지금 청년은 엇지하면 우리도 남과가티 살어볼가 하는 일념뿐이 올시다. (중백행략-원문)」
선생은 다음으로 孫中山이 각금 차저와서 동양대세와 모 朝鮮시국이며 기타 모든 문제를 만히 토의한다는 소소수백언을 맛치고 가장 유쾌하고 득의의 빗으로 書生 和田延次郞을 불너서 선계밀도와 가진안주 차려노코 포도주 기우려서 일순이순 도라가매 담소랑랑하고 화기가 만당한다. 시가를 울프기도 하고 혹 칼을 빼들고 닐어서서 춤도 추며 한참 狼藉히 노닐 적에 토의 종료의 종소리가 동흥문으로 올려 드러온다. 현황당 만좌빈객은 일제히 닐어나서 從容히 相揖하고 차례로 헛터질 때
「우리의 이상은 군등이 실현하야 성공하는 반가운 날 다시 한번 만납시다. 」하는 따뜻하고 다정한 그네의 작별인사 내 가심을 찌르는 듯 모다 헛터 간 연후에 나도 니어 하직한 즉 선생은 내 손을 담속 쥐고 의연히 유정하며
「갓다주는 치질약은 생광스럽게 잘 쓰겟네! 나의 치질은 도모지 안이 나어서 걱정이야! 우리 동지들은 이따금 이러케 모여서 세계대세와 동양문제, 朝鮮의 장래를 토의하고 축복하야 엇더케 해서던지 우리 자손을 살리려고 한ㅅ끗애를 쓰네만은 이 끗이 엇지나 될 것인지? 다만 바라는 바 는 인물양성만히 하고 정신을 일치말고 굿세게 닷흐어 나가게 다시 군에게 부탁일세.」
처연한 빗을 띄고 슬몃이 손목노코 상읍하고 도라서서 수보를 옴긴 후에 다시한번 도라다보니 인홀불견 간곳업다, 나는 그만 실망한 것도 갓고 부족한 것도 가티 실음업시 도라왓다. 이날이 정월 대보름날 장안만호 우리 형제 삼삼오오 짝을 지어 관월하며 답교노리 희희락락 대종교당 북소리는 重光紀念祝賀소리 이리가나 저리가나 歡喜聲이 가득한 낙원뿐이엿다. 인저는 우리의 압길은 광명이오 정로가 잇슬 따름이다.(震颶生記)<34>
