필자	李殷相 (Lee Eun-sang)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

The Four Seasons of the Fields  
						Lee Eun-sang  

In the fields, the tree blossoms  
The grass is new  
In the meadows, the larks sing delightfully  
Before I knew it, spring has arrived in all the land.  

Children heading to the fields on the cow  
During the time at the curves of the hills  
Barley is ripe  
They’ve turned yellow.  

Summer  

Hey, my beans and your beans  
Look at the bean plants  
Growing secretly  
They’ve bloomed.  

Cooling the body that was warmed by the heat with the night breeze  
Farmers lie in the yard, listening to the songs of "Simcheong Jeon"  
Though the night is short, before I realize it, it passes  
The rooster crows once - twice - thrice.  

Autumn  

The house still has unharvested gourd vines  
Pumpkins piled over  
On every vine, pumpkins - big pumpkins, small pumpkins -  
One, two, counting brings autumn.  

"Plaiting the straw mat - plaiting the straw mat  
One piece, two pieces - twist them up."  
With the songs of the farmers plaiting  
The bountiful autumn is now flying away.  

Winter  

Watching inside the room with the lantern lit  
The joyful faces of children playing  
While weaving straw mats - thick straw, new straw -  
Before I knew it, winter has come.  

On a long night, sitting at the loom  
The weaver weaves this way and that way,  
After finishing a couple of rounds,  
The bountiful winter also vanishes.  

<148-149>  

**Summary**  

This poem depicts the beauty and cycles of nature through the four seasons in the countryside, bringing to life the daily activities and joys of farming life. It highlights the seasonal changes in the fields and the resilience of rural communities. Historians may find the portrayal of agrarian life during the Japanese occupation significant, as it reflects both the persistence of traditional Korean cultural practices and the socio-economic conditions of the time.
