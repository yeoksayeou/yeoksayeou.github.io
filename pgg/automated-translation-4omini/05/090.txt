잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1927년 03월 01일  
기사제목	二十年前 韓國學界이약이, 내가 入學試驗치르던 때, 當時는 無試驗 入學이 普通 (Twenty Years Ago in Korean Academia, When I Was Taking Entrance Exams, At That Time Without Entrance Exams Was Common)  
필자	崔斗善  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

When I entered middle school, I hardly have any remarkable memories. At that time, it was generally common to have admission without exams. Nowadays, when the semester begins, everyone gets stressed about entrance difficulties and examinations, but back then, there were no tests and there weren’t many students. Schools were worried about the low number of applicants for admission. Thus, anyone who wanted to enter the school, regardless of age, educational background, or financial situation, could simply apply.

I entered school for the first time twenty-one years ago, when I was fourteen years old. Until then, I had only learned the classical Chinese texts in a private school, but with the demand of new trends, I wanted to learn modern subjects, and so I finally enrolled in the Hŭi Mun Yi Ch‘uk (徽文義塾) preparatory course. After a year in the preparatory course, I entered the main course. At that time, what kind of exams did they have? Depending on one’s educational background, entry was decided. Did they teach Korean in the Sino-Korean mixed script? Even in terms of subjects, it wasn't as difficult and complicated as today; basic subjects like Japanese, arithmetic, history, geography, and drawing, among others, were taught then.

When I entered Waseda University (早稻田大學), at that time, there were hardly any exams. It was in the era of the Japanese imperial system. I had studied English at a regular English school for about a year and based on that, when I entered before the Declaration of the Joseon (조도전), the only exam I had to take was in English, which consisted of listening comprehension and composition.

This was how my middle school and university entrance experiences were. There isn’t much to elaborate on.

  
**Summary**

The article reflects on the author's experiences of entering middle school and university twenty years ago, highlighting the lack of entrance exams and the accessibility of education at that time. It contrasts this with the contemporary situation where students face significant pressure due to rigorous entrance examinations. Historians might find the depiction of educational trends and accessibility during colonial Korea significant, particularly as it draws a comparison to modern educational pressures in a colonial context, suggesting a shift in educational policies and societal expectations.

二十年前 韓國學界이약이, 내가 入學試驗치르던 때, 當時는 無試驗 入學이 普通
						崔斗善
내가 중학교에 입학하든 때 말임닛가. 별로 이럿타 할 기억이 업슴니다. 그 때는 여하간 무시험 입학이 보통이엿스닛까요. 지금은 입학난이니 시험난이니 하고 학기만 되면 모다 머리를 싸매고 덤비지만 그 때는 시험도 업고 또한 학생도 만치 안어서 학교에서는 입학지원자가 적을가봐서 염려하든 때임니다. 그래 누구나 그 학교에 입학만 하겟다 하면 년령, 학력, 금전유무를 불문하고 막 바더 드렷슴니다.
내가 학교라고 처음 입학하든 때는 이십일년전 즉 내가 열네살 나든 때임니다. 고 전 까지는 글방에 단이며 한문만 배우다가 신풍조 바람에 신학문을 배워 볼가하고 비로소 徽文義塾 예과에 입학햇슴니다. 예과에서 일년하고나서 본과에 드러갓슴니다. 그 때 시험이 무슨 시험임닛가. 자기 학력에 따라 지원대로 입학을 식켯슴니다. 국한문으로 된 朝鮮語를 일켜 보이든가요, 그 때 학과로 말해도 지금과 가티 그러케 어렵고<21> 복잡하지 안엇슴니다, 일어, 산술이 보통이엿고 역사, 지리, 리과ㅡ 도화, 창가등 그런 것이엇슴니다,
早稻田大學에 입학하든 때 말임닛가. 그 때도 별로 시험이라고는 업섯슴니다. 그 때가 바로 심팔세 때임니다. 正則英語學校에서 약 일학기간 영어전문을 하얏지오 그리하야 조도전에 입학할 때는 시험이라고 다른 것은 업고 다만 영어 뿐 시험을 보는데 서취와 작문이엿슴니다.
나의 중학 及 대학에 입학 하든 때는 이럿슴니다. 별로 이럿타 할 거리가 업슴니다.<22>
