기사제목	新春夜話, 中國의 新人物 蔣介石과 陣友仁 니약이 (New Spring Night Talk: New Figures of China, Jiang Jieshi and Chen Youren)  
필자	一記者 (One Reporter)  
기사형태	잡저 (Magazine Article)  

"Please, if you have any news to share, could you enlighten me a bit? I've been quite curious about the world's happenings these days."

"Just what I read in the newspapers; there isn't much happening. By the way, I hear you graduated with honors; congratulations."

"Whether it's honors or not, I don't know. There’s nothing to congratulate about; that girl is capable... She's better than I am."

"Why not send her to a higher school?"

"Well, it depends on having the money... I’m busy with household chores, and what’s the point in studying if she’s just going to get married?"

"Still, if she has started, she should at least go to a teacher training school; she has the aptitude."

"Really... you talk about other things, but tell me the news—it's so dull just sitting here in the room."

"There isn't anything particular. If you have something specific you’re curious about, ask away, and I will answer as best I can."

"Speaking of news, I heard that the southern part of China is quite prosperous these days. But, who is Jiang Jieshi? And what about Chen Youren? They're doing quite well, it seems... Zhang Zuolin and Wu Peifu couldn't do much, and recently even the British, who seemed like a tiger, could do nothing, which is truly quite remarkable."

"Regarding Jiang Jieshi, I don't know the specifics, but he's currently the commander-in-chief of the Southern National Army."

"I see. Then, please explain further."

"He is from Zhejiang Province, currently around forty years old. He has been a member of the Nationalist Party since the beginning, graduating from the Imperial Japanese Army Academy. After returning to China, he worked closely with Sun Yat-sen in the revolutionary movement. Just before the first Northern Expedition led by Sun, he became Sun's aide or secretary, and during Chen Jiongming's rebellion in the 11th year of the Republic, he helped support Sun in significant battles. This is why he enjoys considerable trust from Sun. In the Spring of the 12th year, when Sun returned to Guangdong and the headquarters moved to Henan, he served as the chief of staff for the Guangdong Army under Xu Chongzhi. Later that same year, he received orders from Sun and spent about a year in Russia. After returning, he became head of the Huangpu Military Academy, training numerous military officers. Today, those officers known as the National Army were all trained by him. Following Sun's passing, in accordance with Sun's wishes those became the primary objectives of the first Northern Expedition, secondly the exclusion of foreign influence, and thirdly, the implementation of the Three Principles of the People, namely the establishment of the Republic of China. His character is said to be quite steadfast and resolute, and he values friendship highly. As a party member, despite occasional sacrifices for old friendships, he is not without flaws as a businessman. His military intelligence seems limited, but his current success is likely attributed to the strategies of his military advisor, Mr. Borozhin (a Russian). In general, he has become quite an extraordinary figure."

"What about Chen Youren?"

"Well, I don't know much in detail, but he is currently the Minister of Foreign Affairs in the National Government, drawing significant attention while confronting Britain. He is, in fact, known better as You-Jen Qian. The reason is that he was not born in China (his father is Chinese) but rather on a British island in the Atlantic and later studied in Britain, so he is considered completely British. Therefore, he knows English better than Chinese and understands British matters better than Chinese ones. After returning to China from Britain, he served as a secretary for Sun, gaining considerable favor from Sun. Later during the 12th year of the Republic, he was briefly the head of the Aviation Bureau at the Guangdong headquarters. His mastery of English is incredible, but regarding the East, especially China, he may not be as familiar, yet he remains an extraordinary individual; it’s quite something to see someone raised in Britain opposing Britain itself... In any case, he is noted to possess special intelligence in diplomacy. Beyond what I just said, I'm not quite sure."

"Haha, so he wasn't born on the Chinese mainland? It doesn’t matter where he’s from... Can I have a drink? I'm feeling a bit thirsty."

"It's alright. I have to go now, as I have a schedule."

"Just have a drink with me for a moment... And whenever you have time, be sure to get me some information on Russia—there's a big issue brewing there..."

"I can't just talk about Russia. It might land me in trouble..."

"Haha... is that so? Then how about Japan... is that a sensitive subject too?"

"Well..."

  
**Summary**  

This article provides insights into Jiang Jieshi (Chiang Kai-shek) and Chen Youren (Chen Yuen-jen), key figures in early 20th-century Chinese politics. It highlights Jiang's military and leadership background and Chen's foreign affairs experience, particularly concerning Britain's relations with China. The piece illustrates the shifting power dynamics in China during a turbulent era. For historians, this article is intriguing as it sheds light on the relationships and perceptions of international powers in the context of China's modernization and struggle against colonial influence, reflecting the complexity of Chinese national identity during Japanese occupation.
