One Person, One Story  

Recently, looking at the graduates of girls' schools, it is observable that most exhibit a tendency toward self-esteem and vanity. Even possessing a little knowledge, they believe there are no other knowledgeable people in this world, and often have desires and a sense of luxury that exceed their means. As a result, many ultimately find no success and become failures, falling into degradation. This phenomenon is common in transitional periods and can be found in any country, but we must pay careful attention and awaken to the reality of our family and surrounding circumstances. We should discard all unnecessary vanity and self-esteem and engage in matters with greater composure and sincerity, which will lead to better outcomes. <40>  

**Summary**  

The article critiques the trend of vanity and excessive pride observed among female graduates of girls' schools in the late 1920s. It highlights the disconnect between their achievements and their inflated self-perception, warning of the risks of failure and degradation that come from unrealistic desires. This commentary reflects broader societal issues during the colonial period in Korea, where education was a double-edged sword; while it promised advancement, it also fostered unrealistic expectations and pressures among women in a rapidly changing society.

一人一話
						槿花女學校 金美理士
近來 女學校의 卒業生을 보면 대개가 自尊心과 虛榮心이 만흔 경향이 잇다. 卽 조고마한 智識을 어뎌가지고도 이세상에는 자기밧개는 學識이 잇는 사람이 업거니하고 또 분수이상의 慾望과 奢侈心을 가서서 결국 아모 성공도 못하고 失敗, 墮落하는 사람이 만타. 그것은 過渡期에잇서서 어느나라든지 잇는 現狀이지만은 깁히 注意하고 覺悟하야 먼저 우리의 가정과 周圍事情이 엇뎌한 것을 생각하야 無用의 허영, 自尊을 다버리고 좀더 沈着하고 진실하게 무슨일 이든지 하얏스면 조흘 것 갓다.<40>
