People on the Road  
Shin Heung-u

A middle-aged man in an elegant suit and a top hat, with gold-rimmed glasses on his eyes, a gold ring on his hand, and a shiny gold watch on his chest, strolls down the intersection of Sajikgol. To an unfamiliar person, he might easily be mistaken for a Western dandy or a dancer. However, upon closer inspection, he is a well-known Korean figure, famous as a U.S. student who speaks excellent English, known for his handsome appearance and style, and is recognized for his participation in the Pacific Educational Association and the World Christian Association. This is Shin Heung-u, the general secretary of the Christian Youth Association. He comes to the Jongno Central Christian Youth Association from his residence at the end of Sajikgol, speaking English actively with Westerners and enjoying the sounds of spring.

Choi Eun-hui

"Look, look! It’s a bear-man!" The girls hanging around Jeondong Hospital are chattering. Confused at their words, I look closely, and I see that famous reporter from the Chosun Ilbo, Choi Eun-hui, is passing by in a fur coat made of bear fur. The sickly girls at the hospital are playfully teasing. I wonder where she is headed, perhaps looking for news materials or something else. Unlike other female reporters, she does not take a rickshaw; instead, she rides on the slender legs of a bicycle, swaying her hair in the chilly spring breeze while her lively eyes move side to side like scenes from a moving picture. Readers anticipate whether there will be an article about her in the women's section of the Chosun Ilbo this time.

  
**Summary**  

The article portrays two notable individuals in the streets of Seoul during the colonial period: Shin Heung-u, a young and charismatic leader of the Christian Youth Association, and Choi Eun-hui, a prominent reporter from the Chosun Ilbo. Their depictions illustrate a blend of modernity and traditional Korean elements, showcasing the influence of Western culture in Korea. Historians may find the contrast between Westernized youth and traditional values particularly interesting, as it reflects the tensions and transformations occurring in Korean society during Japanese rule.

路上의 人
申興雨


말숙한 양복 중절모자에 눈에는 금테 안경 손에는 금반지 가슴에는 금시계가 번적번적하며 社稷골 네거리로 나려오는 하이카라 중년신사한분. 모르는 사람이 보면 서양풍류장이나 무도꾼으로 알기 쉬울 것이다. 그러나 알고 보면 米國유학생으로 영어잘하기도 유명하고 미남자로도 유명하고 모양잘 보기로도 유명하고 朝鮮사람으로 太平洋敎育會나 萬國基督敎會에 참석한 일로도 유명한 기독교 청년회총무 신흥우씨다. 그는 사직골 막바지의 자택에서 종로 中央基督敎靑年會로 오시느라고 서양사람과 억게동모를 하고「빌빌.밸밸」의 봄새소리가튼 영어를 하며 지내간다.

崔恩喜


익크-곰 보아라. 곰 중에도 사람곰이로구나 하고 전동병문에 잇는 아희들이 떠들기에 그것이 무슨 말인가 하고 자서히 보니 朝鮮日報의 유명한 여기자 선생 최은희씨가 곰의털 외투를 입고 지내가는데 고 몹슬 병문의 아희들이 작란으로 놀니는 말이다. 그는 어듸 가서 무슨 긔사재료를 또 어더가지고 오느라고 그러는지 다른 신문사 녀긔자들처럼 인력거도 타지 안코 물새 종아리가티 설멍하고도 가느다란 정강말을 타고 쌀쌀 부는 봄바람에 머리털을 흔날니며 쌍갑풀진 눈이 활동사진처럼 우로 갓다 좌로 갓다 한다. 이번에는 朝鮮日報 부인란에 무슨 기사가 날가하고 독자는 기대기대.<15>
