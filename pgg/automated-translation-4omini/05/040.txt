One Person, One Story  
Manager Li Hŭngjong, Korea-Japan Bank  

Our Korean people have a very different attitude towards making deposits or taking out loans compared to people from other countries. Specifically, when it comes to making deposits, it is usually derived from limited earnings; it mainly comes from the profits made from agricultural produce such as grain payment from land, or it may be money earned sporadically through speculation. Likewise, borrowing does not typically involve using the funds for useful business purposes; rather, it is mainly for paying off debts or spending on expenses related to weddings, funerals, and other ceremonies. While we cannot control prevailing conditions, we still hope that deposits can grow from profits made through business endeavors and that loans might be utilized productively, essentially for business expenses.  

**Summary**  

This article by Li Hŭngjong discusses the distinctive financial behaviors of Koreans during the colonial period, emphasizing their cautious approach to savings and borrowing, which often deviates from Western practices. The article highlights a cultural and economic context shaped by colonial rule, where economic activities were invariably linked to traditional customs and necessities rather than entrepreneurial ventures. Historians might find it particularly revealing of how colonial subjugation influenced local economic behaviors and attitudes towards wealth management.

一人一話
						韓日銀行 支配人 李恒鐘
우리 朝鮮사람들은 預金을 한다든지 借金하는 것이 모두가 다른 나라 사람과는 아주 딴 판이다. 즉 預金을 하는 것도 자기가 무슨 사업을 하야 그 사업으로 생기는 돈이 별로 업고 대개는 토지에서 나오는 곡물대금이라든지 그럿치 안으면 일시투기적일로 생기는 돈이요 차금도 무슨 사업에 유용하게 쓰라고 하는 것이 안이라 대개는 부채청산이나 혹은 관혼상제등 낭비에 쓰는 것이 만타. 대세로 엇지할 수 업는 것은 별 문제이지만은 될 수 잇는 데까지는 예금도 사업의 생산액으로 예금이 만히 되기를 바라고 또 차금도 유용하게 즉 사업비에 차금을 하얏스면 조흘가 한다.<14>
