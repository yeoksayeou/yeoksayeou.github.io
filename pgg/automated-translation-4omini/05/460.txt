Letters of Literary Men  
There are times when it is easier to see a person's character through their letters than through their creations. Here, I present letters from a few literary figures for your perusal, along with their handwriting and style.

To Brother Douyuseong,  
The first issue will be published as scheduled on October 1, and I will send it to you as soon as it is published. Your manuscript has come to me for the next issue. Please send me your poem, and I hope you will work harder for this magazine. I apologize for being quite busy lately and ask for your forgiveness in advance.  

September 23, younger brother Lee Gwang-su  

I have received your letter. I will also include Yeom-gun's manuscript, which is scheduled to be published in the New Year edition. Regarding the payment, I feel very sorry, but the circumstances of our magazine are somewhat sudden, and since the settlement will only occur before the end of the lunar year, I hope you can wait until then. I know this is difficult, but our situation here is also quite challenging. Please take care.  

December 21, a major message  

The fragrance of plum blossoms mocks the coldness. I hear your frail body has regained strength and is fine. I have been suffering from solitude and illness, spending my time in newspapers and magazines, sometimes feeling the headache of the maid's teasing, but perhaps the misfortune transforms into happiness as it reminds me that I still might be young.  

When will you come? If you are here, I would like to wait for the departure, and if you ask, I would open my eyes wide, hoping you stay a long time. Instead, I have two requests to make. First, my younger brother's Jinjune (진주네) asked if I could arrange for you to consult about his poetry collection if there is a chance. Please also discuss with Seohae-hyeong (서해형) so that you can try to sell it, even if just a little; and second, regarding the manuscript from Donggwangsa, even if there is a mistake about this side, there will be more on the other side that I need to send urgently based on the prior relationship discussed.  

As a first step in a regular lifestyle, I intend to enroll in a formal school but have been suffering from a swollen throat and pain recently, though I think I will try to go. I may seem ridiculous alone, asking for money like a child requesting it, but if there is anything to send, feel free to do so. I apologize for troubling you with too many requests, compounded by the long wait.  

January 7, evening, Yeom Sang-seob  

To Brother Soba,  
I came here for a little while but brought nothing with me from Seoul. It seems like something is missing, so I keep touching my pockets. It's not that, but perhaps it is because everything remains unresolved. That's how things go in the world, isn’t it?  

The poem sent to the side was also delayed. I plan to write one without haste and contribute it to your name. As I mentioned previously, I intended to depart for Tokyo today, but unexpectedly, a storm has blocked my path. However, there are urgent matters, and knowing that Yeom-gun is suffering alone from illness, I might leave as soon as it settles down. I will write to you again once I arrive, and I hope you stay healthy and continue with your work.  

February 15, year of Yeo (丁卯) younger brother Lee Eun-sang  

I came here a while ago. There were classes. I will return in about two weeks. The magazine has been approved. I apologize, but please send the promised 5 won for the magazine to the account number 12832 Kyungsung. I have asked them to take it from there. Please refer to the first issue for further details and send me the manuscript for the second issue.  

Jinnampo, Seoseon Guesthouse, Anseo  

I have moved to the great and pleasant region of Youngbyeon in Pyeongbuk. I grow weary of Seoul and think about living a rural life. I should be financially stable now. How have you been during this time? Is Hee-cheon (희천) well? Has he not given birth yet? The literary world continues, but relationships are strained, so I plan to study and write in the countryside. The friends in Seoul also think of me often. I had a grand farewell party too, but I don’t miss Seoul.  

Summary  

The article showcases letters from various early Korean literary figures, revealing their personal thoughts and struggles during a time of turmoil and colonial oppression. They discuss their commitment to literature, their health issues, and obligations regarding journal publications. This correspondence not only reflects the camaraderie within the literary community but also presents insights into the daily lives of these individuals under colonial rule, giving historians a glimpse into the emotional landscape of Korean intellectuals during the 1920s.

斗牛星兄
창간호는 예정대로 10월 1일에 발행하겟사오며 발행되는데로 곳 送呈하겟습니다. 형의 원고는 차호에 내게 되얏습니다. 시는 곳 보내어주시기 바라오며 본지를 위하야 더욱만히 힘써 주시기를 바라옵니다.
弟는 근래에 심히 忽忙하와 실례함이 만켓스니 용서하시기를 미리 청하옵니다.
9월 23일 弟 李光洙

주신글은 보앗습니다. 염군의 원고도 바덧사오며 신년호에 게재예정이오이다. 고료에 대하여서는 매우 미안한 말삼이오나 방금 본사의 형편이 창졸하오나 양력세말전으로 계정위계이오니 그때까지만 기다려 주시면 좃켓습니다. 그때는 신년게재분과 가치 올릴예정입니다. 因難하실 것을 잘 아오나 이곳 형편도 난감이올시다. 勿勿 이만. 12월 21일 주요한 拜<124>

閤裡의 향기로움이 寒梅를 비웃으려니와 형의 약한 몸이 젓잘먹고 충실하야져 오심 멀리바 있다. 弟는 고독과 병고에 시달려 신문잡지로 세월을 보내는 둥 마는 둥 가끔 하녀의 추파에 두통도 아니남은 아니나 혹시는 나도 아즉 젊은가 하는 자의식을 환기함만은 불행중 대행인가 하나이다. 언제 오시나요? 붓드는 님 계시거던 떠날날을 뭇는 것도 열적고 그님이 물으면 눈을 세로 뜨려니와 그러기에 오래 오래 계심바라오니 그 대신에 두가지 청만 드러주시오. 한아는 弟의 ]진주는 주엇스나]를 혹 형의 시집교섭하야 본대가 잇거든 무러 보아 주시고 또 서해형과 상의하야 보시어서 아모데라도 팔도록 노력하야 보아주소서. 그리고 또 한아는 동광사에서 삼습원보낸 것을 상편에 대한 것은 만핫스나 하여간 하편이 잇슨 즉 더 보낼 것이니 즉시 부송하도록 발시하야주소서. 규칙적 생활의 제일보로 정칙교에 입학코자 도하거니와 작금으로 경안간우이에 疼痛을 감하고 인후가 붓는 모양이어도 해볼까 합니다. 어린 아해가 칭병하고 돈 달라는 것가타야 혼자 우습소이다만은 하여간 보낼 것이 없다면 구타여 애걸할 것까지는 물론업고 상부히 바들것이 잇다면 시일을 닥어서 보내게만 하야줍시사함이외다. 점으록 미안한 부탁만하야 칙간하외다 위차결례 1월 7일 夕 염상섭 拜

小波大兄
