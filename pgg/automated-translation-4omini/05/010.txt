잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1927년 03월 01일  
기사제목	탈(假面) (Mask)  
필자	李秉岐 (Lee Byeong-ki)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Building a house, can one build it anywhere?  
On a sandy bar, how can it be erected?  
On a day when the wind stirs, even shadows cannot be seen.  

Nevertheless, beautifully shall it be, for others to see.  
Even if adorned with nice decorations,  
Can the eyes that cannot see it recognize its beauty?  

The monk’s robe dances as the gisaeng (妓生) performs,  
Is it the monk’s dance? With long skirts swirling,  
They say that even the village matriarchs dance.  

Why do the ups and downs vie for attention?  
Raising the banner to the heavens and falling to the ground,  
In every household, hearts are not intertwined.  

Even if the bones of a horse endure for a thousand years,  
When it turns to rust and cannot endure,  
It is then that people will abandon one another, it is a terrible thing.  

Wearing beads,  
Yet being considered less than a stone,  
Though it may be wrapped in silk,  
Whether it’s silver dust or gold dust, it is abundant.  

**Summary**  

The poem "탈(假面)" reflects on themes of facade, societal perception, and the transient nature of beauty and existence. It contrasts the superficial appearances people present to each other in society with the deeper realities of their lives and emotions. Historians might find the poem revealing of the complexities of identity during the colonial era in Korea, as it touches on the struggles of individuals caught between traditional roles and modern societal expectations.
