Each Person's Color (Part 5)  
Regarding the preferences of Yukdang Choi Nam-seon, they were manifold. Among books, he had a particular interest in historical old books, and he also had a great passion for sijo (traditional Korean poetry), not only enjoying reading the ancient sijo of others but also taking pleasure in composing his own. He would often scribble sijo while wandering the streets, mumbling in a manner similar to an old widow's lament. As for snacks, while it is common for non-drinkers to indulge in them, Yukdang was particularly skilled at snacking, so much so that his pockets were always filled with hotteok (sweet pancakes), roasted chestnuts, and various beans. In other words, one could say that he packed his pockets tightly with manuscripts of sijo alongside hotteok and fruit. It is likely that the reason his body has been gradually becoming plumper is due to his fondness for snacking. Additionally, it is a customary practice of his to close his eyes slightly and smile first when speaking. His writing is characterized by its length, resembling the Yangtze River. In the past, he had been seen wearing mituri (traditional shoes), earning him the nickname Choi Mituri, but currently, he is said to be wearing rubber shoes. <74>  

**Summary**

This article offers a glimpse into the personal interests and characteristics of Yukdang Choi Nam-seon, a prominent figure in the literary world of Korea during the early 20th century. It highlights his love for traditional poetry and snacks, presenting a light-hearted view of his life and habits. Historians might find the focus on cultural activities, such as the appreciation of sijo, significant as it reflects a resistance to colonial cultural influences and a celebration of Korean identity during a tumultuous historical period.
