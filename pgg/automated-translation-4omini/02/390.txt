Folk Customs Cartoon, New-style Daughter-in-law and Old-style Mother-in-law

The old-style mother-in-law went to visit her new-style daughter-in-law, who lived separately after tying up the handle. She wanted to see what it was like. When she reached the house and knocked on the door, through the window, she saw shadows of a man and woman embracing each other and moving about, while doing something strange. She could not even utter a sound, saying "Oh my, how outrageous!" and quickly fled back home. 

Later, when she asked her son, "What is that horrible thing you are doing?" He replied, "What do you mean?" 

"What do you mean 'what do you mean?' You were hugging each other while the door was closed!" 

"Yes, yes, that is called dancing practice." 

“Now I understand, neither the dance nor the cabbage (배추) has anything to do with it. Was it to do such things that you insisted on living separately?” <113>  

**Summary**  

The article humorously contrasts the old traditions represented by the mother-in-law with the modern practices of the daughter-in-law, particularly focusing on the embrace practiced in dances. It highlights generational gaps in cultural acceptance and the tension between traditional values and modern influences during the colonial period in Korea. Historians might find the depiction of these familial dynamics reflective of broader societal changes and the struggles between East and West during this transformative era in Korean history.
