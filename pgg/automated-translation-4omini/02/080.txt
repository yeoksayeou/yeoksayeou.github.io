各人各色  

His tastes are known to no one. He says that a warm, elegant nook is the best for an old man like me, but that is just something he commonly quips. Some say he enjoys playing Go and reading, but those are all things from his past youth, and currently, his unique preference is smoking cigarettes from a long bamboo pipe. Christian people in the countryside, who are unable to let go, would be surprised to hear this since for Christian believers, alcohol and tobacco are prohibited, but he smokes without a care. Anytime there is a moment to light his long bamboo pipe, he takes it without hesitation. Even if there is no heaven, if there were to be a paradise, the smoke from his cigarettes would surely transform it into a dark copper heaven. Furthermore, every time he speaks, he has a knack for crafting clever remarks and evoking laughter, which is one of his characteristics. His work is something the world might soon forget, so he just puts down his pen. <24>  

**Summary**  

The article discusses the peculiar personal tastes and habits of an elder figure named Lee Sang-jae (李商在), highlighting his fondness for smoking and making humorous remarks. It reflects on how personal preferences can clash with societal expectations, particularly regarding the conservative views held by certain religious communities about smoking and drinking. Historians might find it interesting as it provides a glimpse into the daily lives and struggles of individuals living in colonial Korea, as well as how personal identity and societal norms interact during this period.
