Night 1  
Host: "Living in the village makes things uncomfortable in terms of residence and food, so how do you do it? Please forgive me. This is how it is in the village at night. It’s dirty, but let me lay down a mat." (He sweeps the floor and takes out a red cotton mat to lay down.)

Villager 2: "I'm Kim Yong-ju, also living in this village. How do you do?" (A bit of an arrogant look too.)  
Guest: "Well, do take care."  

Host: "Right now, we are listening to our guest from Seoul. It’s not easy to accommodate a gentleman like him in a rural place. Let's drink some more country alcohol. Though it may be a bit challenging..."  
Villager 1: "I have spoken rudely, but as a villager, I just want to understand better and finally have the chance to meet our guest from Seoul."  
Guest: "The alcohol isn't anything special. Just ask anything you want to know and I’ll respond to the best of my knowledge."  
Villager 1: "In the village, what is the distance to the capital?"  
Guest: "It’s about 30 li (approximately 12 km)."  
Villager 1: "In Seoul, it’s said nothing is bought, right? They even buy water to use."  
Guest: "Exactly! It’s not just the water. Even pieces of firewood are often bought too."  
Villager 1: "How on earth does one buy everything... We cannot spend a day there without money to survive if that’s the case."  
Guest: "Well, it depends. I can’t say for sure. It varies with living conditions. If a household is smaller, expenditures are lesser, and if you have a larger family, costs increase, and there are different lifestyles ranging from extravagant to frugal..." (Showing some disdain, as if implying that the villager's questions are too naive.)  
Villager 1: "Then what do all those many people do to survive?"  
Host: "That’s what I too find perplexing. I think they might not survive without relying on us villagers..."  
Villager 2: "Oh brother, what do they eat? They live off of countryside produce by consuming only the villagers' resources." (Speaking firmly with a trembling voice.)  
Host: "What does this person mean? Not all city folks are so." (Glancing at the guest, as if to indicate to forgive the impolite villager’s comments.)  
Guest: "I think what this gentleman says has some truth to it. Urban dwellers do generally depend on rural produce for sustenance. However, city to country exchanges do exist too, and it's a mutual relationship."  

Villager 2: "Why would a villager even connect with Seoul? I think it sounds valid."  
Villager 1: "This gentleman is bringing up irrelevant matters. Let’s only talk about Seoul liquor." (While shifting his feet and spitting, he looks at the guest in front of him and adds,) "By the way, there’s no rice in Seoul, right? They only eat meat side dishes at every meal."  
Guest: "...?" (He expresses discomfort as if feeling insulted and just extinguishes his cigarette with a grimace.)  
Villager 1: "In Seoul, they say people don't know their neighbors, that they don’t know the individual next door or across the street, right?"  
Villager 2: "You explain things badly, don’t you? That’s only the case in a small town like ours. Villagers know each other quite well, unlike you busy urbanites." (Clearing his throat once. He continues.)  
Villager 2: "But about Zhang Zuolin, has he become the president? I’m curious about how they are managing."  
Guest: "Have you seen the newspaper? Reports say that Zhang Zuolin is indeed the President, but it seems not officially yet."

Host: "That’s something you could know only through experience."

If you live near people that are knowledgeable, you can garner an enjoyable life. Walking around Cheongnyangni and Han River Bridge while recognizing friends or having a drink would be a life like nowhere else! Nothing more to wish for, haha." (He continues glorifying Seoul until the host is nearly convinced.)  
Host: (Smiling slightly.) "Do we believe we could get a chance like that?"  
Guest: "Come on, don’t give up! I'm not coercing you at all. Don’t you wonder about that yourself?" (He says this with a hint of a smirk.)  
Host: "That’s not why I’m saying. I’m simply uncertain about it..."

Host: "You must be thirsty; have a drink." "Ah, hey big boy!" (The city guest smokes a cigarette and the host goes out to fetch drinks.)  

Night 3  
Guest: "Alright! I will go back soon. Before that, could you manage to find 1,000 won for me? I will rush to arrange matters..."

Guest: "Would 500 won do? It should not exceed that..."  
Host: "500 won? How hard would that be? How do we have that much in our village?"  
Guest: "By trying my best, I think I could manage 300 won... if there’s a chance for that."  
Host: "Well, that would work. We’ll see what comes of it." (Rubbing his eyes as he tries to consider the options.)

Guest: "Then tomorrow, I’ll head into town while you arrange for it to be settled. I will prepare my clothes and write letters for the trip back to Seoul..."  
Host: "Alright! However, you shouldn’t expect much here. In the village, there is barely a hundred won available."  
Guest: "Is there a financial association or a small establishment in town?"  
Host: "Maybe go to the financial association; they usually deal in 1 or 200 won."  
Guest: "Then let’s go together tomorrow."  
Host: "Not so fast! Who said anyone would come tomorrow?"  
Guest: "Alright, tomorrow we will certainly go." (He shifts the topic again after hearing the reply.)  
Host: "But still, would you give me a little now? I literally have no money on hand." (He looks eager while feeling increasingly embarrassed.)  
Host: "How about I lend you around 50 won for now?"  
Guest: "If that's the case..."  
Host: (Finding his money pouch with multiple coins, he counts out five 10-won pieces.) "Here you go, take this for now."  
Guest: (With a grateful expression, bows his head.) "Thank you! In that case, I will head out now. Come visit me the day after tomorrow." (He grabs the money and addresses him as 'brother'.)  
Host: "Shall we have one more drink?"  
Guest: "Well, I did have some last night..."  
Host: "We need something to eat, right? How about a chicken?"  
Guest: "We won’t deal with that now; let’s go! We won’t be able to ruin this opportunity again."  
Host: "What opportunity is there? Well, there might not be much of a chance." (The scene changes as the guests finally proceed on their way.)

Summary  

This text presents a dialogue between a city guest from Seoul and country farmers in a village, displaying the disparity between urban and rural life in 1920s Korea. Throughout their interaction, the guest tries to persuade the host to move to Seoul, emphasizing the perceived wealth and ease of city living compared to the struggles of rural life. The farmers, however, express their skepticism of urban life, questioning the sustainability and realities of such an existence. The text reflects the social dynamics and economic conditions of colonial Korea, showcasing the tension between modern aspirations and traditional values. It also highlights the perceptions urban dwellers held about their rural counterparts, as well as the fears associated with moving to the city, which historians might find interesting, as it encapsulates the period's socio-economic changes and cultural clashes.

金  「글세 나하고 가티 邑內로 가요. 이 밤중이라도 당장 갑시다. 그 놈이 혹 邑內에 숨어 잇나해도. ... ...」
主  「... ... .」 (손길을 맛맷고 天井만 처다본다.)
金  「그사람이 설사 똑똑하고 얌전하다 합시다. 아젓씨 처지로 서울살님이 다-뭐요. 서울? 敗家亡身하기 꼭 알맛소. 서울이 다 뭐요. 서울? 아젓씨 그래 서울 가시면 무엇 해먹고 사실나오?」
主  「... ... .」
金  「아뭇 생각 마시고 農村에 꽉 눌너 안고 잇서요. 서울? 세상에 난다 긴다하는 놈들도 서울살님을 감히 못하는데 아젓씨 가티 용하고 착한 이가 서울이 다 뭐요. 집신에는 제 날이 제격이라구 村뚝이는 村이 제격이애요... 돈만 한 50圓 일허 버렷소.」
主  「... ... .」
金   「주무서요. 아적에 가티 가십시다. 분해서 못 견디겟네.」 (닐어나 나간다. 主人은 한숨을 후 내쉬며 담배대를 떤다.)＜57＞
