각 사람마다 각기 특징이 있다 (Part Four)  

The ancient friend Choi In's (崔麟) skill in Go (바둑) has been well-known since his time as a student in Tokyo. While he is indeed a strong player, he can also be incredibly stubborn. In olden times, it is said that Xie An (謝安) from Jin (晋) in ancient China neglected the invasion of the enemy just to play Go, only continuing to play until he was finally informed that the enemy had retreated. One wonders whether Choi would behave similarly in a crisis, but in everyday gatherings or at home, if he finds an opponent who is also a Go enthusiast, he is utterly absorbed, caring little for the passing hours, others talking, or any disturbances, completely engrossed in the game.

Recently, he has dedicated himself to painting, and even when he is at home, whether at night or day, he rarely puts down his brush, leading to significant progress beyond what an ordinary person could imagine over the past few years. He has always struggled with gastric illness, and to treat it in mid-October this year, he went to Tokyo, perhaps with friends from the Go community or fellow painters, and it's been a concern for his relatives whether he is feeling lonely during his travels. Since he is naturally skilled in social interactions, when he speaks with others, he has a habit of covering his mouth with his right hand, showing consideration for any offensive odors, and when he does speak, he does so with the utmost seriousness, elongating the polite expression ‘yes’ (네) before beginning. This too is one of his habits.  

 

**Summary**  

This article discusses the habits and hobbies of Choi In, a notable Go player and painter, highlighting his dedication to both pursuits despite his health challenges. It offers insights into social customs and personal quirks of individuals during the colonial era, reflecting cultural traits of Koreans during that time.
