High-Class Prostitute Empress Lü, Biographies of Poisonous Women of the East and West (Part Two)  
By: Qiaoyufengren  

In the land of Shanfu, there lived a strange figure named Lü Wen, who rarely stayed home and wandered the world, making a living by reading people's appearances. The world called him a "physiognomist."  

This person was no ordinary individual. He would not look at those whom he found insignificant, and not even the offer of a fortune could make him do so. However, if he saw something unusual in a person's face, even a beggar would make him follow and, sometimes, he would examine their appearance for free, or at times, would take to drinking or giving a small amount of money in return for the opportunity. It seemed that his findings were acutely accurate and definitely seemed to bear evidence. Thus, the people of that time referred to him as "the saint of physiognomy" and "the strange person."  

In this way, this person roamed the world, making a living seeing appearances without seeking wealth, while he selected people to associate with and engaged in discussions about literature whenever he had the chance to drink wine with others.  

As he travelled through the land, he also had a wife and daughters whom he lamented about, constantly mentioning their beauty. He had remarked, "While she has the ability of an emperor, she is just a common girl! But as an empress, she has a splendid appearance. However, her eyes show poison and her face is a bit discolored and developed indecently... Ah! This is another story!"  

After having his first daughter and checking her appearance, he mused, "Although this one is a bit inferior to her older sister in qualities, she is decent as well."  

Seeing that his daughters were growing beautifully, he lamented and expressed to his wife to raise them well, while he sought a suitable husband for them and went about his travels again.  

After a period traveling and after almost a year, he returned home to find his daughters blossoming, his eldest now eighteen and his younger sixteen. As he saw them play, dressed beautifully by their mother, he marveled at their beauty and stated that they would become the empress and queen respectively.  

He compared their appearances from childhood to now, noticing they hadn't changed much but had become more exquisite. As his wife served him a cup of wine, he lay down on a pillow made by his youngest daughter and admired the beautiful faces of his daughters.  

Noticing something peculiar in his eldest daughter's gaze, he remarked lightly, indicating that her eyes seemed a bit strange, and her complexion slightly poor.  

Thus, he departed once more. This time, off to seek suitable matches for his daughters. Upon reaching Peixian, he heard of a man named Liu Ji, who had a dragon-like face and a characteristic beard.  

This Liu Ji was known for being indulgent in pleasures since he had become the head of the Si Shang Pavilion and was expected to become significant. The physiognomist, upon hearing this, decided to personally evaluate Liu Ji.  

Upon meeting Liu Ji, he was indeed as described, and seeing the potential of Liu Ji as a prominent figure, the physiognomist was delighted and wished to marry Liu Ji to his daughter Lü Yan. Initially reluctant, Liu Ji accepted the proposal.  

The physiognomist reported his findings to his wife, who expressed her disbelief in marrying their daughter to a lowly pavilion assistant, even though the marriage had been arranged. The physiognomist insisted this Liu Ji would either become a king or possibly an emperor. Thus, an agreement was cemented, leading to Lü Hou (Empress Lü) becoming consort to Liu Bang (Emperor Gao of Han).

For eight years, while Liu Bang engaged in countless wars against Xiang Yu, Empress Lü led a rather unstable life filled with tensions and fears. Despite this, she had ambitions tied to her husband’s success.

After much turmoil, through her cunning, Empress Lü gained influence after Liu Bang's ascendance to power. With the eventual establishment of comfort in her life, she indulged excessively, showing signs of heavy indulgence.

Though she initially had few scandals, her power made her immune to scrutiny, leading her to plot against her adversaries in secrecy. Empress Lü became feared for her vicious methods, desiring dominance over her husband's allies.  

Her ambition led her to eliminate those she perceived as threats to her power. She arranged the deaths of several key figures, including Han Xin and Peng Yue, who were vital to Liu Bang’s reign.

In the end, Empress Lü faced her downfall after years of treachery, ending her life as a woman whose ruthless ambition had consumed her. Yet the legacy of her cruelty marked the annals of history drastically impacted by her actions.  

Had Empress Lü's father, Lü Wen, seen the ruin of his lineage when predicting their future? That he couldn't foresee the demise of their family at its peak is a historical question worth pondering.  

**Summary**  

The article examines the life of Empress Lü, illustrating her rise from the daughter of a wandering physiognomist to the notorious empress who used ruthless tactics to seize power. Her tale embodies themes of ambition, betrayal, and the perils of excess in positions of power. Historians might find the examination of her life particularly significant within the context of Korea's colonial history, as Empress Lü's actions reflect the intricacies of power and gender dynamics that parallel the struggles of women within the colonial structure.
