旅行地에서 본 女子의 印象, 長崎旅館에셔  
여행 중에 여자로 인하야 자미잇든 이약이를 하라는 別乾坤 편집자의 부탁을 밧고 원래 규모가 몹시 書生式으로 拙한 나는 쓸만한 것이 업다고 고사하여도 『아 - 그래도 그럴듯한 것이라도 생각나는 대로 하나...... 』하고 짓 구지 추궁을 하기로 변변치 안은대로 쓰게된다.  

That was probably about five or six years ago when I was around twenty-three or twenty-four years old. At that time, I was a reporter for XX Daily. It was when Kim Ik-sang, who threw a bomb at the then Prime Minister of Japan, Tanaka Giichi, at the port of Shanghai, was arrested and a trial was held in Nagasaki. The newspaper company gave me quite a substantial travel allowance, and filled with the spirit of adventure (especially at that time), I checked into the most famous ryokan in Nagasaki and waited for the trial day.  

Nagasaki is widely known as the place where the first capitalist American goods knocked on the door of the dreamy Japanese people. It boasts a beautiful and clear blue sea, where you can hear the sound of bells in the mornings and evenings, with lush trees behind the mountains creating a truly picturesque landscape. Mitsubishi Shipyards was building warships, and there were Mitsubishi mines on the offshore islands.  

While waiting for the trial to occur, which was delayed for several days, I took a boat ride and roamed the sea all day long. The bustling modern civilization had factories belching smoke even on the remote offshore islands, and the calm sea waves saw large ships passing by, while tranquil white gulls soared around, bringing a poetic scenery that one could hardly see otherwise. Everything visible was this chaotic, and my boat was a small ‘motor’boat making noise all day as it cruised. When I returned to the ryokan in the evening, I was extremely exhausted.  

One evening, after spending the whole day roaming around, I returned, had dinner, and started reading a book. Before I knew it, I had fallen asleep without even spreading out my bedding. I called for the maid to come and prepare my bed, so I pressed the “bell”. After I pressed it once and she didn’t come, I pressed it a second time.  

After pressing it third time, rather long, I thought, "This time she must come..." and closed my eyes gently. I was in a light sleep when I saw someone gently opening the sliding door. I was startled and looked to see it wasn't the usual maid!  

She had long, flowing hair styled slightly to the side, and it looked as if her face was lightly powdered and painted. Her lotus-like eyes seemed to hold some mysterious beauty, with her white complexion and two rosy cheeks standing out against her face, making her a charming figure that captured my gaze.  

As the sliding door opened and she knelt upright with decorum, I thought she couldn't be a maid after all. She quietly asked, “Did you call?”  

I intended to reply "Do it" but realizing she wasn’t a maid, I said, "I called for a maid to set up my bed."  

“Is it a bed?” she replied, and then she simply went downstairs.  

A little while later, the maid came and laid out my bed. Being extremely tired, I fell into a deep sleep. The next day, when I awoke, the bright sunlight was already tapping at the window. After washing my face and sitting down for breakfast, the maid came over to fill my bowl as she had done before.  

After eating one bowl of rice and requesting a second, curiosity got the better of me and I asked, “The girl who came last night wasn't a maid, but who was she?”  

The silent maid turned her head and smiled. I also laughed, expecting her to talk, but the maid still held back her smile and said, “Last night I was at the brothel, and the other maids weren’t around to come up, so the young lady from the owner’s family came up. She thought you must have called urgently, and even though I wasn’t there, she came up to lay the bedding for you.”  

It turns out she graduated from a girls’ high school this past spring and stayed home, studying various subjects. Moreover, she added that the young lady was very virtuous, emphasizing her character. I also jokingly said, “Why not invite her over for dinner or breakfast sometime?”  

She said with a smile, “What are you saying? I won’t come. Ultimately, nothing like that has ever happened before, you called so urgently last night thinking you had something important.”  

I laughed again, “Well, if you don’t come, that would be unfortunate for me as well.”  

After breakfast, instead of the usual seaside sightseeing, I spent a couple more days wandering around. Upon returning in the evening, after bathing, I sat down at a table under a bright light for dinner, and the maid serving the rice laughed and said, “If she had come over to visit, that would have been great, don’t you think?”  

She continued with a twinkle in her eye, "The young lady has a penchant for Western sweets, so if you buy some sweets to send her, she might come to thank you, which could lead to a chat or visit."  

I muttered, “How does that little maid know about such calculations.”  

The following day, visiting various sights held more urgency and I didn’t have the leisure to hold such jokes anymore. The next morning while eating, I jokingly told the maid, “Have the young lady come to serve me some rice from now on.”  

The maid pretended to be startled and said, “Please don’t say such things anymore. I was scolded just now for not listening to what you said about buying sweets for the guest.”  

After that, I didn’t dare to joke with the maid anymore and continued to enjoy my coastal excursions for about two more weeks. When I left, I had no belongings other than a small bag, so I insisted that the inn's servant wouldn’t need to escort me, but as I walked along the brightly lit street to kill some time after leaving the ryokan, I noticed a girl standing by a light.  

It was the inn’s daughter. She wore simple clothes and partially arranged her hair and face. Bending her head, she said, “Safe travels, have a good trip.”  

I was surprised at her unexpected greeting, and her coming to say goodbye eased the discomfort I had in my heart. So I replied, “Yes, I’ve taken care.”  

After a while, she looked lingeringly at me with a somber expression and asked, “When will you come to Nagasaki again?”  

I replied, “I don’t know. Unless there’s something specific, I don’t think I’ll have a chance to come back.”  

“Maybe I’ll never come here again in my life,” she said.  

I replied, “Well, we cannot say for sure. Who knows when I might come back again.”  

She lowered her head, deep in thought for a moment, and then looked up again and said, “If you come back here again, please visit my house.”  

There was a shadow of regret in her expression.  

So, several years passed. I had not had the opportunity to return to Nagasaki.  

**Summary**

This article narrates the author's traveling experiences in Nagasaki during a significant political event, detailing encounters with a graceful young woman from the ryokan where he stayed. The article offers a glimpse into the daily life and social norms of Japanese women of that era. It highlights the delicate interplay of misunderstandings and flirtation, revealing aspects of colonial relationships and the impact of societal changes on personal interactions. Historians might find the contrast between the author's casual encounters and the backdrop of political tension significant in understanding the complexities of Japanese-Korean relations during this period.
