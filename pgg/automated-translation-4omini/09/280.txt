기사제목	닛지마시오 (A Note on Japan)  
기사형태	사고·편집후기 (Editorial Note)  

Foreign people read books selected by parents for their children in their homes. In Korea, the magazine that parents can trust the most to provide safe reading material for children is undoubtedly "어린이" (Children), published by our company monthly; this is widely recognized. Those who cherish their children can easily save enough to purchase it, as it only costs 15 jeon (錢), about the price of a pack of cigarettes.＜95＞  

**Summary**  

The article discusses the importance of providing children with safe and trustworthy reading materials, specifically praising the children's magazine "어린이" for its quality and reliability. It emphasizes the responsibility of parents in selecting appropriate literature for their children and suggests that even small savings can allow them to afford such publications. This article reflects the broader context of colonial Korea, where there was a growing recognition of children's education and welfare amidst Japanese rule, indicating shifts in cultural values and parental responsibilities during this period.

닛지마시오
외국 사람은 자긔의 아들이나 딸을 위하야 읽힐 책을 자긔 집 가정에서 부모가 골나주고 사줍니다.
조선에서 제일 안심하고 자녀에게 읽힐 수 잇는 잡지는 본사에서 달달이 발행하는 『어린이』뿐이라고 천하의 정평이 잇슴니다. 자녀를 귀히 아시는 이는 담배 한갑만 절약해도 그것을 사 줄 수 잇스니 갑시 겨우 15錢이기 때문입니다.＜95＞
