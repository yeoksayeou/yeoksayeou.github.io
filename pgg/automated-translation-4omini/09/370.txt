잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1927년 10월 01일  
기사제목	釋王寺藥水의 夏期經過 니약이, 避暑客도 가지가지 (The Summer Situation of the Shakwang Temple Mineral Water: Various Summer Visitors)  
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Commentary)  

I must honestly confess. While it is said to be a treasure of Joseon, in reality, the place I am in is a sort of consumer and entertainment venue, a place for debauchery and entertainment akin to a den of courtesans. Usually, a novice would come and leave without making a profit; please understand that.

This year alone, over 1,900 visitors have come (from February 25 to August 10). Adding late August and early September, it is estimated that at least 20,000 people will have passed through (20% increase compared to last year). The amount spent in my vicinity has been around 25,000 to nearly 30,000 won (which is also more than a 20% increase from last year). However, if we look at it month by month, from February 15 to April 1, there were only about 500 visitors (just 10%). And from April 2 to April 9, the eight days saw an influx of over 8,000 visitors. Do you all know why there was such a sudden increase of nearly 1,000 visitors daily? Perhaps you don’t know. The reason is that April 8 is the Buddha’s birthday (釋迦牟尼佛誕日). This shows how powerful the influence of the Buddha still is, doesn’t it? Among these 8,000, there were many women. During this time, a certain wealthy person from Seoul brought hundreds of people, men and women, to celebrate a ceremony for his concubine or something, and they made quite a scene. When I saw this, I realized that the world is still a world for the wealthy. I was almost at my wits’ end with exhaustion from the relentless amusement during those eight days.

Then, from April 10 to the end of May, only about 900 remained, perhaps because it was neither cold nor hot during this period. Among these, about 400 were students, who seemed to be on a school outing. Many young fellows came flocking in an irregular fashion, and while they did find a kind of leisure, they were healthy, vigorous boys with promising futures. I had a lot of delight in observing them during the year. Dressed in school uniforms with hats, bags, notebooks, and pencils, their sparkling eyes moved from side to side, taking in every single blade of grass and drop of water. I couldn't help but be impressed.

While observing the priests dressed in their robes or the pale and sick individuals coughing around, or the lewd courtesans, I felt an urge to embrace them right away; they seemed so delightful and endearing.

From June 1 to August 10, about 1,500 people came in total. Among them, around 15% were women, mostly entertainers like gisaeng (기생) and a few sickly women. Also, among the men, there were certainly wealthy individuals, vagabonds, poets, writers, journalists, scholars, painters, and musicians who had a fair number there. While it's hard to name them all, I can mention briefly that a certain director from the Government-General (총독부) visited with his family for a birthday celebration involving over ten members, enjoying a few days there, with one innkeeper reportedly lamenting, "With the arrival of the big official, we surely earned a lot!" but later regretted it like a dog being chased by a rooster. Recently, a gentleman named Zhao from Seoul even visited with five or six women, staying a few days before leaving. Then, there was also Choi from Seoncheon, known as the so-called King of Gold Mines, who came to negotiate some lease on temple land to build a villa within the temple grounds but eventually left empty-handed. It is difficult to name others individually due to embarrassment. As a reporter, I have not seen more than five or six distinguished figures from either the Korean or Japanese side, and particularly, the most notable new impression I got was from a lady journalist of C Company.

There were certainly many scholars and their students coming and going as well, but there wasn’t much to note there. A group of gisaeng, numbering around sixty to seventy, also came, but their behavior is well-known so they went disregarded. It seems like some of them were quite infatuated this summer.

Ah, how could I forget! There were a few women with short hairstyles rumored to be "modern beauties" as well. I don't know their names, but there were also many girls resembling students, albeit not really students, with short skirts and fashionable outfits who visited; unfortunately, I still don’t know what officially classified them.

It is difficult to mention every visitor in detail. To sum up briefly, I would say that among the many people, most of them are urban folks, and there are no rural attendees. Mostly, the visitors are wealthy people, officials, members of the elite, intellectuals, or their accompanying courtesans; in fact, there were no laborers at all.

So, while Shakwang Temple is famous, it remains a gathering place for city residents and for a special class, not a place for farmers or laborers. “The bright moon in the sky shines for them, and the flowers blooming on the green hills bloom for them!” This touching and mournful song was heard during my dealings with the guests; however, I must say that the pine wind and bright moon of Shakwang Temple still exist purely for their benefit. As for the mineral water itself, isn’t it the case that I am also here for their sake? (Continued) <120>

**Summary**

The article reflects upon the summer activities at Shakwang Temple, detailing the patronage and the nature of visitors to the mineral springs, which are regarded as a leisure destination for the wealthy and urban dwellers, leaving out the working class. The writer laments the overt socioeconomic disparities in attendance, suggesting that spiritual and natural attractions have become commodified. This commentary may interest historians examining the social hierarchies prevalent during the colonial period in Korea, highlighting how public spaces were often shaped by class and privilege.

나는 정직하게 고백함니다. 나는 조선의 명물이라 하지만 실상 말하면 나 잇는 이 곳이란 일종의 소비장이요 유흥장이요 蕩子淫婦의 오락장임니다. 여간 풋내기는 왓다가 본전도 못 거두워 가게 됨니다. 그쯤 아러 주십시오.

금년만해도 발서 1900餘人이나 왓다갓슴니다.(自2월 25일 至8월 10일 計) 8월 하순과 9월 초순을 加하면 적어도 20000명은 될 것임니다.(작년보다 2 割增) 그리하야 내 부근에 떠러진 금액이 25000餘圓 近 30000圓이라 합니다.(此亦작년보다 2 할이상 增) 그런데 이것을 월별로 본다하면 2월 15일로부터 4월 1일 꺼지는 500명 내외에 불과 함니다.(여 1할) 그리고 4월 2일로 동 9일 까지의 8일 간은 일약 8000명 이상에 달햇슴니다. 갑작이 이럿케 일일 1000명의 대다수에 달하게 된 까닭을 여러분 아시겟슴닛가? 아마 모르시겟지오? 그 까닭은 4월 8일이 釋迦牟尼佛誕日인 까닭임니다. 이것을 보면 釋迦스님의 위력이 아직도 얼마나 한 것을 능히 짐작할 수 잇지 안슴닛가? 그 때 이 8000人 중에는 여자가 더 만엇슴니다. 그리고 이때에 서울의 某富豪가 자기 첩의 齋인가 뭔가 올닌다고 男女老少 수백명을 끌고와서 한바탕 법석을 치고 간 일도 잇슴니다. 그것을 볼때에 나는 이 세상은 아직도 부자 놈의 세상인 것을 아럿슴니다. 이 8일 노름에 나는 거의 절명이 되다십히 유방을 짜내기에 몹시도 피곤햇슴니다.
그리고 4월 10일로 5월 말일까지는 900명 남즛한데 이때는 不寒不熱인때라 그런지 좀 소수이엿슴니다. 그 중에 학생이 약 400餘명이나 되얏는데 그는 엇던 학교의 수학여행인가 봅듸다. 다수의 젊은 친구가 좀 불규칙하게 우루루 드리 덥븨는데는 일시곤난도 만엇지만 엇잿든 그들은 씩씩하고 활발하고 장래 有爲의 쾌남아들 입듸다. 내가 일년 중 보든 중에 제일쾌감을 늣겻슴니다. 학모 쓰고 의복닙고 각반치고 가방메고 수첩들고 연필들고 그 - 초롱가튼 눈을 좌우로 굴니면서 一草一木一滴一粒이라도 심상히 보지 안코 지나는 것은 실로 感心햐얏슴니다.
