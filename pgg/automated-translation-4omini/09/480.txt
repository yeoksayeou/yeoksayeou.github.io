"Speech"  

Lee Kyungsun  

Long-ups, commerce, scholars.  
They each have their own music.  

Example - The rural children of tree-cutters.  

- Turtle-ah  
- That-hey  
- Tree-gar  
- Quietly  
- What are you doing?  
- With your head down  
- Are they frozen?  

Those who are closest to nature in this world, every time they speak, they naturally release music.  

Example - This time, I will present the speech of the most refined young people living in the city.  

- What's going on?  
- What?  
- Necktie  
- This?  
- Going to delicious country  
- It’s spring!  

Example - This time I will present the conversation exchanged among the members of the debate society in Joseon.  

- When have you come?  
- It's been about three days.  
- So you’ve seen a lot?  
- Yes. This time there's a heavy rain.  

Example - This time, I will present the music of the playful boys in the newly created youth sections.  

- That's crazy!  
- Where!?  
- Hey!  
- Cock-a-doodle-doo!  
- You, kid!  
- Hey, you jerk!  
- Please sit down!  
- What's up!?  

Example - This time, it’s the music of Korean elders.  

- Hey! What’s up?  
- You’re a real brat!  
- So how’s the heat?  
- Ah, I can’t take it anymore!  

Now, let's move on to another topic.  

Actions and speech.  

If the music of that elder and the youth in the new district blended, what kind of music would it create?  

- (   ) Where did the uncle go?  
- (   ) Who are you?  
- (   ) It’s me. Just from the countryside of Pyeong’an.  
- (   ) Oh, really? Oh, really!  
- (   ) When did you come back?  
- Is this Geongsam?  
- Yes, it is.  
- My eyes are…  
- Yes, indeed.  
- That’s right.  
- (     ) -  
- - (     )  

Actions would appear in such cases. The brackets must include actions. The youth would bow humbly while the elder would groan in surprise.

In this place, action is also a part of music, and that richness is found there as well. However, in this situation, the action cannot be separate.

However, even while our speech exists, the music is flowing within our bodies, and you should realize that there is no dialogue that is in complete harmony with that.

Speech and writing.  

When converting speech to writing (drama, etc.), it's often challenging to convey the meaning directly.  

For example:  

- Ah!  

1. After a long time, what’s up, ah!  
2. Suddenly, I'm shocked, ah!  
3. Suddenly, I’m surprised, ah!  
4. Even if he’s shocked to death, ah!  
5. The youth’s “Ah!” sound  
6. The elder’s “Ah!” sound  
7. The woman’s “Ah!” sound  

In such cases, only the musician themselves can express it. Therefore, whether a musician, actor, or an ordinary person, the tone of speech is terrifyingly indicative of their personality.  

Liars.  

Among diplomats, liars exist, especially among second or third-rate diplomats.  

For example, let’s assume that someone’s child is ill.  

- What’s wrong?  
- Yes.  
- What kind of medicine is this?  
- Yes, I need it.  
- Oh, what’s happening?  
- It’s a lie, truly.  

They try to follow someone else's music hastily, which causes complications.  

Speech is music.  

You all should regard each letter as your vitality.

It’s commonly thought that women around the home utter countless phrases casually, resembling a blabbering child.  

Those who pretend to be knowledgeable get lost in accumulating technical terms, thus losing their sincerity.

Lifespan.  

Life is not the yardstick of the past.  

No one who has conceptually turned into a technical term is worse off than anyone else.  
Let’s move on to another topic.

Let’s work hard.  

Make an effort to speak beautifully. Although it may seem trivial, you should recollect the moment you confessed your first love. Wouldn’t you have been sweating profusely with each word?  

Respect the opponent.  
Understand him.  

In short, when it comes to a person, knowing everything would take much longer than simply saying, “Where is my fee?”  

It only adds to the suffering for the other party. Human beings, scattered in their existence in music, would surely find it immensely difficult to stand together. Do not frown and say yes or no.  

Simply make it simple.  

What I mean is to feel free to say “what needs to be said.”

This simple and complex task is to find what is significant in saying “what needs to be said,” and one who knows how to do that could even become a playwright.  

The person with a baton of leadership.  
This is the ultimate secret.  

The baton of a conductor; it’s not just needed when playing as a group, but each must aim for their own sound, thus, “What have I expressed today?”  
That’s referred to in theater studies as “the reflection of the self.” At every moment, that reflection must be in harmony, otherwise, it would cease to be genuine words.  

For instance, this woman exists. After being married to someone, she hears some rumors about her past.  
In the meaning of “you truly are that person,” she merely replies with “Yes.” Yet she is determined to avoid saying anything more.  

1. Do I love this person? 2. If that person abandons me, would I be devastated? 3. Should I make excuses? 4. Is it better to bear the punishment myself at once? 5. How should I deal with this? 6. Am I capable of not answering? 7. What is his current scene? 8. Should I answer after considering?  

“Sure.”  

That “sure” is a very complex “sure.” But the structure of a person is even more intricate, so don’t hesitate to take risks.  

However, at that moment, such reflections must exist.  
“Sure.” “Well then.” “......Sure.” “Then.” “Sure.” Or “That’s right.” “Sure.” “Yes.” “Yes.” “Certainly.” “Yes.”  

Expressing which is truly my own voice is key to accurate expression. If such efforts are real, in such situations, it shouldn’t be difficult to express emotion freely.  

In those cases, if there's no piano at home, you can practice using a child’s crying. That’s beneficial.  

They seem to resolve everything through crying.  

“Mama!” also cries.  
“Hurry up!” also cries.  
“Feed me!” also cries.  
If mom is busy working and leaves the child alone for about twenty minutes, the child might wage war against the mother using the music of crying. Among them, a five or six-year-old might express all sorts of words through crying music.  

At that moment, you may test what that sound means.  

It’s true. You too subconsciously engage and wage warfare with that child through mere expressions. Then, could you not say, “Ah, you are exactly like your father.”  

I will stop here.  
I apologize if I seem to write something difficult and professional, but it's really not that professional. Many of you might have seen aesthetics books that say a person can express their character with something as simple as their calling card.  

Thus, they could argue that the size or style of a calling card reflects the personality of its owner.  

A much simpler example exists. In the youth sections, the way one wears a hat is often more complicated than my speech. This shows that a specific way indicates a certain character, and that presents a simpler example.  

That refers to visiting the in-laws’ home and how you should greet the mother-in-law. (1927.7.10. Ah-Cham)  

  
**Summary**  

This essay reflects on the concept of speech as music, likening different dialects and expressions to musical notes played in various social contexts. It emphasizes the innate rhythm in human communication and how physical actions complement this speech, making it a holistic expression of personality and emotion. The focus on dialect and social interaction hints at the deeper sense of identity, which could resonate profoundly with Korean colonial history, revealing how language became a means of cultural resistance and social stratification during the colonial period. Historians might find these insights valuable in the context of identity formation during Korean colonization.
