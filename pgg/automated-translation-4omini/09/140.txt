Tales of Literary Giants  
The American literary giant, Wana, had such poor penmanship that it was almost inhuman. During the Civil War, he vigorously worked as the editor of a certain newspaper in Hartford, striving to arouse the patriotic spirit of the readers. One day, a typesetter came out to him and said, “I also decided to enlist.” Wana was extremely pleased to see such a patriot emerging from his newspaper office and greatly praised the decision. The typesetter, however, with a strange expression on his face, responded, “I am not doing this for such a difficult reason. I thought that it would be better to go to the battlefield to die by a bullet rather than picking up letters of your manuscript.” <54>  

**Summary**  

The article humorously recounts how the poor penmanship of American literary figure Wana drove a typesetter to prefer joining the army over continuing to work on his manuscripts. This anecdote sheds light on the cultural perceptions of authorship and patriotism during the American Civil War era, highlighting the sometimes extreme responses to writing as an act of duty. It may reveal insights into the broader struggles faced by those in creative professions during tumultuous times, which historians could relate to the societal pressures faced in colonial Korea.

文豪逸話, 죽는 것이 나어
미국 文豪 와-나-는 아조 惡筆이여서 누구나 닑을 수가 업슬 지경이엿다. 남북전쟁때 하-토풀드市의 某新聞 주필로 격렬히 독자의 애국심을 환기하기에 힘쓰고 잇섯다. 그래 어느 날 植字職工이 주필의 압헤 나와서 『저도 兵隊를 지원하기로 햇슴니다.』함으로
와-나-는 자긔 신문사로부터 그런 애국자가 생긴 것을 너무나 깃버해서 대단히 그 결심을 칭찬한 즉 職工은 이상한 얼골로
『저는 그런 어려운 이유로 그러는 것이 아니라 선생님의 그 원고 글ㅅ자를 줍는 것보다는 차라리 兵隊로 전장에 나가서 탄알에 마저 죽는 편이 낫지 안흘가 생각한 까닭임니다.』＜54＞
