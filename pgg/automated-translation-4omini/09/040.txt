筆跡判斷  
In the land of Buddha, a literary giant named Ppalssakgeun boasted of his skills in handwriting analysis. One day, a woman brought in a sheet of calligraphy written by her 12-year-old son, asking him to predict her child's future based on it. With a deeply furrowed brow, Ppalssakgeun asked if this handwriting belonged to her child. The woman replied that it was not her son's. After a brief pause and a cough, the teacher declared, "This child is rather foolish. Moreover, he is quite rash. No matter how much he might resemble a scholar, it would be of no use; it would be wiser to have him drop out of school and cultivate the land." 

After saying this, it turned out that the calligraphy was actually Ppalssakgeun's own handwriting from his childhood, discovered in an old practice book. Following this failure, he declared that he would cease all handwriting analysis from that day onwards. <8>  

**Summary**  

This article humorously recounts an incident in which a handwriting expert fails to accurately assess a child's future, only to discover that the handwriting in question is his own from youth. The narrative reflects on themes of judgment and humility, highlighting the folly in asserting authority in matters one is not fully knowledgeable about. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the story captures the era's attitudes towards education and cultural identity, as well as the pressures individuals faced within a colonial context to excel in foreign-influenced fields.

筆跡判斷
佛國 문호 빨싹크는 필적 판단이 능난하다고 스스로 코를 놉히고 잇섯다. 어느날 한 부인이 12세 된 아이의 쓴 習字를 한 장 가지고 와서 이것으로 그 아들의 장래를 판단해 달나고 부탁하엿다. 잔뜩 찡기는 얼골을 가지고 잇던 빨싹크 선생, 이것이 당신 자제의 것이냐고 물엇다. 부인은 자긔아들의 것은 아니라고 햇다. 선생은 조금 잇다 기침을 하고 하면서 『이 아이는 아조 둔재올시다. 게다가 더구나 경솔함니다. 암만 학문가튼 것 식혀야 소용업겟스니 학교를 퇴학식히고 농사나 짓게 식히는 것이 낫겟슴니다.』
말을 마라, 그 習字는 부인의 말을 듣고 보니 무근 習字帳에서 발견한 빨싹크 선생 자신의 소년시대의 필적이엇다.
선생이 거긔에 실패를 하고는 그 후부터 일체로 筆跡判斷을 폐업해 바렷다고.＜8＞
