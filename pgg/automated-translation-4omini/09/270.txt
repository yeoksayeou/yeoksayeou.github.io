기사형태	문예기타 (Literature and Others)  

Master Musok's Journey to the New Capital, Don't Be Surprised! After 500 years, Master Musok who designated Hanyang as the capital visited Seoul today. Guided by the Heavenly Prince  

"Heavenly Prince. You probably don't recognize me; I am the monk Musok who, 500 years ago, assisted Lee Seong-gye in designating Hanyang as the capital. Don't be suspicious just because we come from different backgrounds."

"Oh! Indeed, I have heard of the great master's esteemed name for a long time, but I never imagined I would meet you here in person. Is this a real encounter, or am I dreaming? How can a master who lived 500 years ago present himself like this?"

"If one truly encounters the Buddha and attains great enlightenment, they can materialize, whether 500 years or even 5000 years later. However, if he is not present at the place of enlightenment, he will not be known..."

"How on earth do you happen to descend to this mundane world? Please have a seat and rest your legs before you proceed."

"I have been in the Lotus Realm for around 500 years, and it became tiresome. Also, I became curious about matters in the lower realms, so I came down for a brief outing and inspection. It's like how ordinary people travel in autumn..."

"Ah! I have just recently visited 釋王寺 (Sakyamuni Temple) where I stayed for a week before heading to 金剛山 (Mount Geumgang) to see 憾溪寺 (Yuseong Temple), 長安寺 (Chang'an Temple), and 表訓寺 (Paper Training Temple). After visiting 金城 (Gyeongseong), 金化 (Gyeonghwa), 鐵原 (Cheorwon), 楊州 (Yangju), I slept at 道仙寺 (Dao Seon Temple) last night, and I just stopped by 華溪寺 (Hwajeong Temple) on my way here. Ah! It’s not as if if I am bored, but I’m quite tired. What a city this has become... But 500 years is not such a long time in mundane terms, yet seeing how much has dramatically changed during this time is truly overwhelming. Everywhere, the trains are crying out, telephone wires are strung like swords, electricity and gas illuminate the night, trams and automobiles streak by, and multi-storied Western-style buildings rise up. People have changed remarkably with their minds almost like monkeys; what on earth caused such a great transformation? Truly, it’s said the world undergoes endless evolution..."

"If you're thirsty, please have a cup of tea and speak slowly. What will you have for lunch?"

"Lunch? I can do without it here. In this world, where people would complain if it was only a bowl of rice, why would I worry? I’d prefer a cup of coffee... The taste of barley tea is so refreshing."

"Here is the barley tea. We have various teas like ginseng tea, milk tea, black tea, etc., but as we are the underclass, we drink this barley tea. It has a nice flavor."

"Economical and tasty is all there is to it... Ah! By the way, can it truly have changed this much in 500 years? How can even King Lee Seong-gye feel it more than I do when he first ascended?"

"No matter how much you might feel, do not speak such things. You must be cautious."

"Cautious? What do you mean?"

"Please refrain from further discussion of such matters."

"Ha ha! To mention that is a ridiculous thought when I am in this world. By the way, when I first arrived at 釋王寺 (Sakyamuni Temple), the guards were making a fuss about someone claiming a great monk had come. They wrote down my name, and before long, some officers or guards came and imposed restrictions. I have never experienced such mockery even after living over 500 years! I raised my voice and scolded them, and they just laughed off my frustration! Was it like that when I arrived at 金剛山 (Mount Geumgang)? But now you’re implying that speaking about this is enough for me to be restrained? I will not be constrained by anyone."

"Master, we’re just living among the world’s realities, so you must be careful."

"Haha! Oh young lad, if you're so fearful, how can you survive in such a treacherous world?"

I led the master to the summit of 北岳山 (Mount Bukak).

"However, you say you designated this Hanyang capital, but actually, it was Jeong Dojeon who decided this. There are many arguments about that; since the master has come here, please elaborate on the circumstances back then."

"Um! That’s true; saying Jeong Dojeon was the one who determined it is not untrue. Let me tell the truth of that matter.

I was originally a monk in 安邊雪峰山 (Snow Peak Mountain of Anbyeon). I was requested to interpret a dream by the great figure Lee Seong-gye. He asked for my interpretation after experiencing a strange dream. I wondered, after thinking for a while, not to panic. It was a dream of becoming a king as he carried three wooden beams on his back (王字, being the shape of the character for "king"). He also had a dream about a rooster crowing at the same time—it seemed evident that greatness was in store (the rooster's crow being a symbol of noble birth). So I interpreted this as a good omen; he would become a great king!"

"Lee Seong-gye felt pleased and earnestly repeated his request for assistance. At that moment, we became close, leading to the Lee family seizing control of the Goryeo Dynasty, beginning around 536 years ago. Upon ascension, concerned about the suitability of the existing capital, a new capital needed to be selected, and he summoned me to decide on the new site. I accepted this request and began inspecting the geography. Finally, upon arriving at Hanyang, I found that the landscape was suited for a triangle of mountains and the vivid Han River. The North Mountain would serve as the rear guard, while the 仁王 would serve as the right side and 駱山 (Nak Mountain) the left, creating a fortress for at least a thousand years."

"And after considering this fine terrain, I thought more about constructing temples than a capital city, aiming for the flourishing of Buddhism. Thus, I got the idea to abandon the Hanyang capital suggestion and moved south toward 鷄龍山 (Gyeoryong Mountain), believing it to be a favorable site. After exploring Gyeoryong, I presented the case for setting up the capital here, believing this to be an excellent base for fortifying the foundation of the nation. Lee Seong-gye, always obedient to my words, accepted my suggestion without question and moved the construction to Gyeoryong."

"But during this time, the resourceful Jeong Dojeon seemed to sense my ambitions and brought forth his plan to oppose Gyeoryong, espousing the significance of establishing the capital at Hanyang. Lee Seong-gye was caught indecisive between believing either my claim or Jeong Dojeon as the construction was underway, drifting in uncertainty. Jeong Dojeon devised a clever scheme, placing a stone at the construction site saying, 'The evil monk Musok mistakenly entered Jeong's territory.' Furthermore, he sent a messenger to spread word about the mountain spirit’s guidance, singing a song in the middle of the night, saying, 'This land will belong to the Jeong clan in 500 years; Lee Seong-gye will arrive here; go back north toward Hanyang; this is your land.' Upon hearing this, Jeong Dojeon reported to Lee Seong-gye that the mountain spirit was providing instructions and urged against sticking to human decisions, insisting that the master should gravitate to Hanyang."

"Meanwhile, the laborers discovered the stone mentioning the evil monk Musok's mistake in entering Jeong's territory. This led to Lee Seong-gye deciding to follow Jeong Dojeon’s suggestion and move the capital to Hanyang. I felt utterly defeated and was resentful that the power had slipped from me."

"I then aimed to secure Hanyang by planning and constructed the capital bases in 普成 (Puseong) near now known as Wangsimni. Jeong Dojeon learned of my plan and countered with resistance, but as the circumstances were proceeding unfavorably, he pushed his arguments even more, disputing the Hwangsimni designation. Eventually, he also placed stones at the construction site, marking it with 'Wangsimni.' In the end, this stone was discovered..."

"Reflecting on all this, I realized my plans appeared pointless. Eventually, the decision to select Hanyang was finalized. Now, I had no recourse. Better to contribute genuinely to assist Lee's endeavors. I insisted on recognizing 興化門 (Heunghwa Gate) as the proper site for the royal palace. Jeong Dojeon opposed, stating, 'That site does not face the southern aspect; it must be suitable for a king to govern from the south!' He insisted on keeping the grounds at 景福宮 (Gyeongbok Palace), claiming them as the most favorable, while I objected strongly that the rear side was too steep and there would be misfortunes and calamities in the palace. Eventually, my objections fell on deaf ears as the cunning Jeong Dojeon persuaded them otherwise..."

"As I mentioned, my discussions were disregarded, and thus there have been many calamities and misfortunes experienced within the palace. After all these incidents, I retreated, feeling that worldly affairs resemble floating clouds, and it is better to release oneself from desires. So, I withdrew to 雪峰山 (Snow Peak Mountain) to meditate quietly. King Lee Seong-gye, recognizing the services of interpreting his dream, later named my hermitage 釋王寺 (Sakyamuni Temple) and invested greatly to construct it."

"So, that was the gist of events. My ambitious actions might even appear laughable to today's people."

"Is that so? Well, I have listened well. Now, let us continue sightseeing in Seoul... but please, let’s refrain from discussing the Korean-Japanese political relations; such talk might lead to imprisonment."

"Is it possible that merely being here in the mountains can lead to such a fate? Oh, such a cowardly lineage you've got there! What kind of oppression must have been so severe that it gives rise to the phrase, 'you might be taken away?'" 

"Master, regardless of how sublime one might be, there's little we can change. Now, let’s enjoy the sights of Seoul..."

"That building with the red bricks is the 京畿道廳 (Gyeonggi Provincial Office), and prior to that, one of the six offices was the Korean-style tiled house that houses the 法學專門學校 (Law Specialty School). Across from it, the white brick building is the 递信局 (Post Office), and the upper house is the Korean infantry headquarters. That grand 德壽宮大闕 (Deoksugung Palace) was where the Emperor Gojong lived until his death, and it has now been sold to the Japanese due to agreements made in the year of the 戊午 (Ahn Hwa). The grand 興化門 (Heunghwa Gate), which you, Master, once established, is currently being used as the 京城中學 (Seoul Middle School) for Japanese. The rustic place over there is now broadcasting station radio, and the place where the red flag flies is the Russian Consulate. That is 梨花女學校 (Yeoido Women's School) and behind is 培材學校 (Beizai School). The high place in the street of 義州通 (Uiju Street) is the Independence Gate (a guarantee of complete independence made by the Treaty of Shimonoseki after the First Sino-Japanese War). Behind that scarlet brick building is the 西大門刑務所 (Seodaemun Prison), also known as the Hell for mankind. As we travel south, that locale filled with smoke and noise is the 南大門停車場 (Namdaemun Transportation Center), and just to the east stretches the Japanese residency area 新龍山 (Sinryongsan), while the old 龙山 (Long Mountain) lies across the river now. The 新龍山 (Sinryongsan) accommodates the 20th Division of soldiers, a rail authority, and a gas company. Later, you will be able to observe carefully. That eagle's stone bearing the soaring sun is 崇禮門 or 南大門 (Namdaemun). Passing through is a grand stone structure that is the Korean Bank, and across from it stands the Postal Bureau of Gyeongseong, closely followed by the Commercial Bank, and then the Agricultural Bank, which is the most prominent in Korea. Beneath the feet of 崇禮門 (Namdaemun), the renowned place known as the bell-top road leads to the army memorial site. Further down is where the 水道局 (Water Supply Office) is located, close to the joint cemetery in 水鐵里 (Sutaekli) and another cemetery in I-Pyeong."

"Now, you have an idea of the general layout. Let’s go down and further observe."

"Oh, is this truly wonderful! But what about that building on the hillside with red bricks?"

"That is the 中央高等普通學校 (Central High School)."

"And that building?"

"That is 徽文高等普通學校 (Huisong High School)."

"And that one?"

"That is 京城女子高等普通學校 (Seoul Women's High School)."

"And what about that building?"

"That is an empty palace with no significance. You need not concern yourself further."

"And what about this?"

"That is the 侍天敎堂 (Shiten Church)."

"Oh, the buildings are quite numerous! Just how many people reside in this city of Hanyang?"

"Yes, the total population is approximately 342,600, including over 245,000 Koreans and about 84,000 Japanese. The household count totals 76,628, with about 55,500 Korean households and around 15,000 Japanese households."

"Wow, there are quite a number of Japanese here. How did things turn out like this?"

"It might be inevitable. After all, it’s fate."

"But does the castle remain as it was?"

"Not so much. Generally, time and natural erosion have led to its deterioration, and other alterations have brought some decline in its fortification. The western gate and the small western gate have long since collapsed, and both the north gate and south small gate are now dilapidated. Presently, the gates remaining include the 南大門 (Namdaemun), 東大門 (Dongdaemun), 小東門 (Small East Gate), 彰義門 (Changui Gate), and 屍口門 (Death Gate)."

"But didn’t we worry that the collapsing 南小門 (Small South Gate) would lead to extreme immorality in Hanyang and societal chaos? So, has that come true?"

"That seems to be the case. Reports suggest that the vice and sexual misconduct trends among the youth in Hanyang are on the rise. Even young boys and girls of ten are fascinated with love and engage in unusual relations, raising worries among more knowledgeable persons. It seems that the open small south gate leads to an increase in promiscuous activities with the new quarters, indicating its influence."

"Oh, we learned much and saw much today. Let’s head down now; you must be hungry to eat something before we explore the night view soon..."

**Summary**  

This article depicts Master Musok’s comedic yet reflective journey to modern Seoul after 500 years since he designated the city of Hanyang as the capital. As he comments on the remarkable changes in society, technology, and culture, it highlights the complexities of Korean identity and history in the context of colonial influences. The dialogue subtly reveals the duality of past glories versus contemporary realities, which historians may consider particularly insightful regarding Japan's colonial impact on Korea and the evolving nature of Korean society during that time.
