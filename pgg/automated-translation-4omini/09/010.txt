잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1927년 10월 01일  
기사제목	거지의 꿈 (The Beggar's Dream)  
필자	巴人  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

The beggar, dressed in torn hat, worn-out shoes, and old suit,  

sleeps by the roadside, dreaming of becoming a king and marrying.  

No, no, kings also get married,  

When the trumpets sound and drums beat as I march,  

I dream of waving a red flag and shouting cheers.  

 

**Summary**  

This poem captures the ironic aspirations of a beggar, revealing a deep yearning for a life of dignity and fulfillment despite his present circumstances. The dream of nobility and love showcases the universal desire for improvement and hope, even amid adversity. Historians may find it interesting as it reflects the struggles and dreams of the marginalized during the colonial period in Korea, revealing how those at the bottom of the social hierarchy hoped for a better future even when faced with grim realities.
