기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Waiting  
						Chŏng Gi-hwan  
As I wait, my footsteps grow weary  
Though the moon rises, my love doesn't come.  
Will it be at sunrise that you will crawl in?  
I feel so troubled, just waiting for that moment.  

In the garden of watercress, the sound of frogs  
Even the frogs have grown hoarse.  
Will this sound keep me awake through this night with my wishes?  
The mulberry trees are bathed in morning dew, the dew of dawn.  

Selection Commentary  
The village girl who owns neither silk garments nor a silver hairpin possesses nothing but a clean love that is on par with others. The author beautifully sings of her innocent love against the backdrop of the surrounding scenery and the primitive yearning for affection. At any time, folk songs must have the fragrance of the earth and the sentiment of the rustic people. Please forgive the slight embellishments made.  

This piece may not be complete, but I hope for your dedicated efforts, Mr. Sŏng ŭm-nyŏn and everyone else.  
Ba-in <133>  

**Summary**  

This poem expresses deep feelings of longing and innocence through the lens of a simple rural love. The imagery of nature and the sounds of frogs highlight the emotional weight of waiting for a loved one, entwined with the purity of rural life. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, the poem reflects the enduring nature of traditional values and emotions amidst the changes brought by colonization, offering insight into how rural Koreans retained their cultural identity during this period of upheaval.

入選民謠, 기다림
						淸州 鄭箕煥
기다리자니 더듸기도 할 발길
달 솟아 열자 되어도 안오니
해 뜰때야 어슬넝어슬넝 기어들텐가
장태하나 넘기 그리도 밧부오.

미나리 광에 개고리 소리
개고리조차 목이 쉬엿네
이 소리 듯자고 내 예와 이 밤을 새우는가
뽕 밧헤는 이슬이 내리네 새벽 이슬이 내리네.

選評
비단 옷도 은비녀도 업는 시골 색시에게 오직 깨끗한 사랑만은 남에 못지안케 늘 가지고 잇다. 그 순진한 사랑을 작자는 四圍의 배경과 애끗난 원시인적 戀慕를 비러 잘 노래하엿다. 언제든지 민요는 이러케 흙의 향기와 순박한 野人의 정서를 가져야 할 것이다. 다소 加筆하 엿슴을 용서하라.
이밧게 昇應順氏外 여러분 것이 잇스나 미숙하엿다. 만은 분투를 바란다.
巴人 ＜133＞
