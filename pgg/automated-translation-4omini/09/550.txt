발행일	1927년 10월 01일  
기사제목	中國秘話 旅中의 老子 (Secret Tales of China: Laozi on the Journey)  
필자	代老子  
기사형태	소설 (Novel)  

Life is full of changes. The most mysterious thing about life is its transformations. 

I approached the age of 60, and due to the misfortunes of my wealth, I adorned myself again with the young beauty Huang Ye and set out on a wandering journey once more. Sensing a desire to return to the bustling life, I began to travel towards the royal capital, Luoyang. 

We departed, and the hardships along the way are beyond description! After various forms of travel—walking, riding, and taking carriages for around three to four days, we finally arrived at a place called Rongyang. Along the way, I intended to stop at Mount Song to take a look, but instead decided to take a path north of Mount Song.

Rongyang was very lively at this time. While talking about where to find a suitable place to rest, we wandered about for a while and soon found ourselves at a well-regarded establishment called Zhao'an Building, favored for its reputation. 

As I was welcomed, I noticed a stream running through a dense grove, and chose the most secluded room within. It was not unexpected, but due to fatigue, my inexperienced companion fell ill, rendering our journey difficult. Consequently, we had to stay here for several days.

The owner was a good man around fifty named Chang Wu, and he had a handsome and well-mannered servant named Wen Liang, who was around forty and had an exceptional presence. They both treated us with kindness, for which I was sincerely grateful. Particularly at night, the two would come to our room to chat leisurely, providing comfort regarding my companion’s illness. Thus, we remained here for seven days.

Crossing the green waters, the refreshing breeze carrying the green leaves shook the hanging bells on the balcony as the setting sun signaled the beginning of the early summer days. At that moment, Wen Liang entered and said, “Sir, this truly is a fine season.” 

I replied, “Indeed! It is truly a splendid time.” 

“How is your strength?” he then asked Huang Ye.

“Thanks to your concerns, I am very well. Sorry to trouble you so much,” Huang Ye said with a bright expression. 

“Not at all! It seems to me that the worst of it is behind, but indeed... being ill while traveling is a great hardship.” 

“Certainly, traveling with a woman entails its hardships,” I interjected.

Wen Liang began to converse with me again. “Sir, excuse my impertinence, but I hear that you are from the Zheng state.” 

“Is that so...” 

“Are you from Zheng?” 

“No, I was born in the Chen state.” 

“Is that so? Yet these days, there is a great person in Zheng named Zi Chan who is quite well acclaimed.” 

“Oh, is that so? Zi Chan indeed has a good reputation.” 

“Also, there is a famous figure in Zheng named Li Dan.” 

At this, I was somewhat taken aback, but naturally responded, “Is that so?” 

“Seeing as I have spoken so freely, I must say that you do not seem like an ordinary person.” 

“Haha… What kind of person do you think I am?” 

“You seem like anything but an ordinary person. From the very first moment of meeting you, both your demeanor and speech do not suggest someone ordinary.” 

“Is that so? Regardless, if one does not open their heart and speak freely, how could they be ordinary?” 

“Ordinary people could not be otherwise. I even wonder if you eat rice like the rest of us.” 

“Oh! What a troublesome remark. Who is there that does not eat?” 

“I apologize, but you seem like one of those beings who do not engage with the worldly affairs.” 

“Of course! When it’s hot, I feel hot, and when it’s cold, I feel cold.” 

“Quite so. If good food is set before you, do you enjoy the taste?” 

“If you cook well, I certainly enjoy eating. But how on earth does good food get made? Hahaha…” 

“I apologize, but I wonder if you’ve ever loved in your youth.” 

“Haha… Of course. Even now, I have such a young and beautiful wife, do I not?” 

“Oh, I mistook you for an elderly gentleman. Apologies.” 

“Is that so? There’s no need to be surprised.” 

“No, there is no need to be surprised, but it seems you are somewhat mismatched in age. It seems inappropriate for one as aged as yourself to have such a young wife.” 

“Even that is fated; what can be done?” 

“That’s a viewpoint that cannot be avoided. As an older man with a young wife, there are at least two significant drawbacks.” 

“What are those two drawbacks?” 

“The first is that due to the poor health of the husband, there is a fear of death. The second is that elderly men are often more easily deceived. I apologize for speaking harshly.” 

“What, a drawback? To be deceived or to deceive is entirely dependent on the person.” 

“Ah, this is indeed a very embarrassing counterargument. However, I have no need to hide anything. Recently, I have had a greatly unfortunate experience of being deceived.” 

“Haha… What a very enlightening statement. Please, go ahead.” 

With that, Wen Liang began recounting his tale with a deeply contemplative expression.

I was originally born in a place called Baigao, not far from the capital of the Chu state. Due to my father’s failure in business, our family fell into great poverty, and I lived a life filled with hardship since childhood. 

In such circumstances, having grown up, I dreamed not once of establishing a family or taking a wife, as I thought it to be far too tormenting to live with a spouse and languish in poverty. Therefore, I resolved to accumulate money instead of marrying. 

At that time, there was a man named Xu Sheng living next door, who had done well for himself as an oil merchant. From the age of 20, I became employed as a laborer at this shop. Though the salary was not abundant, I refrained from spending even a penny elsewhere and saved up whatever I could. 

Thus, the resolve is a formidable one. Gradually, I managed to amass several thousand gold. Those around me learned of this and encouraged me to find a wife, but I remained unmoved and concentrated solely on accumulating wealth, even earning the nickname ‘Golden Bug’ among my peers. 

As the years passed, I turned 30, then 35, and finally 40. Hence, from the age of 20 to 40, I managed to save almost 2,500 gold. Though this is a considerable sum, I was dissatisfied and resolved to accumulate even more, aiming for several hundred thousand gold. However, reaching 40 naturally brought a sense of melancholy at times. It was difficult to understand the reasons behind this melancholy. 

One summer evening at sunset, I went to visit my friend Xu Baiyuan who lived in the suburbs. The scenery by his house was clear and pleasant, and as I gazed at the stream, I noted the figure of a woman washing clothes, capturing my attention. She was undeniably a stunning beauty. 

Having never interacted with women before in my forty years, I found myself unintentionally entranced. Curious about her visage beyond her alluring profile, I thought to approach and see her face—but a thought suddenly struck; what purpose would it serve to pursue a woman at my age? 

So I suppressed my interest and headed to Xu’s house instead. Unfortunately, he wasn’t home, so I returned, glancing back at the woman. This time, I saw clearly her face framed by the sun setting behind the Western Mountains; she was indeed a beauty of unparalleled perfection. 

I thought, “Is there such a woman in this world?” I felt as if my very being was swept away, and as the woman gradually disappeared among the trees, I found myself vacantly departing with no sense of direction. 

The next day, I visited Xu Baiyuan again, chasing the fleeting thought of seeing that woman once more. But no matter where I looked, I could not catch sight of her. Disappointment washed over me.

Just as I was about to inquire at Xu Baiyuan’s house, I heard the door from the neighboring house open. Without even thinking, I glanced in that direction; marvel of marvels, it was the very woman I sought, appearing to exit her house. I observed her graceful movement while foolishly fixated on her every detail. At that moment, I was completely lost to the world. 

On this day, Xu Baiyuan was home, and we conversed and shared thoughts, but more than anything, I was preoccupied with the details of this beautiful woman. 

“Xu, who is the newcomer next door?” I asked.  

“Oh, someone has come to visit; she is an exceptional person but probably not interesting enough for someone like you, who has no engagement with women,” he chuckled.  

“Yeah, no doubt, tell me nonetheless.” 

“Well, since you insist—there’s an exquisite beauty whom you would likely die upon first sight. She must be around thirty, emanating an aura like a blossom in full bloom, unmatched by any. Pink like a peach and more delicate than a crabapple, her eyes glisten like narcissus, and her lips resemble laughing peonies. Hahaha, I don’t have the words to fully describe her beauty. She stands under a different category of beauty than anything you would see around here.” 

“Remarkable, you have quite the poetic flair.”  

“No, no, though my descriptors may be lacking, it is honestly ineffable.” 

“But I do wonder, will you ever get married?” 

“Even to mention marriage, getting along without children is truly too tragic.” 

As we discussed, I found the beauty's image burned in my heart, and I could not rid myself of the sentiment, even as my heart seemed heavier and heavier. 

After several days, I received an unexpected visitor. A man in his sixties came to my door, leading a little girl about ten years old, and said, “Excuse me. I am here by introduction from Xu Baiyuan to meet you briefly.” 

At the sound of that, a peculiar sensation gripped my heart. 

“Is that so? Please, come in.” 

“Not at all,” he said, bringing the little girl in and taking a seat. The elderly man spoke slowly. 

“I apologize for arriving abruptly, but I heard from Xu that you live alone and are considering adopting a child. I felt embarrassed, yet I came here hoping you would consider taking this child.” 

“I see you’re introducing your child. However, I have not made any formal plans to adopt.” 

“That is understandable. But my situation is dire, and I took great courage to come here; I am currently a minister in the Chu state, yet I am finding it hard to provide security for this woman after the passing of her husband. If you would take this girl, it would also provide her mother with protection and companionship.” 

“I suppose it could work out.” 

The elderly man’s demeanor brightened as he turned to the child, saying, “Little one! From now on, this gentleman will be your father. Greet him.” 

The little girl, indeed, was delightful and charming, bowing her head three times. In a dreamlike state, I thought the situation was remarkably straightforward. The elderly man stated, “Then, tomorrow, I will return with the mother to express my gratitude after leaving her here today.” 

I was indeed in a daze, mulling over how the tale had unfolded. Yet, anticipation surged within me at the thought that later, the mother would visit. 

That night, sleep eluded me. 

The next morning, I prepared vigorously, eager for my guests to arrive. As time passed, I grew anxious waiting for them. 

By afternoon, I heard the elder’s voice announcing their return along with the woman. I rushed to greet them at the door, and lo and behold, there she stood—the exquisite beauty I had glimpsed before! 

We exchanged greetings only to discuss the care of the little girl and then took our leave. 

From that moment onward, the girl delighted me in every manner possible; I came to understand that beyond the value of money, the charm of parenting was something profound. 

In the next few days, I found myself slacking off with my work, feeling guilty with each passing day as I returned to the shop. The owner recognized my dedication over twenty years and did not reprimand me. 

This continued for some time. Eventually, the elderly man visited every few days, expressing his gratitude during each visit. However, the mother seldom came to see me, filling me with concern. 

Nearly a month passed before the elder appeared, looking somewhat distressed, and sighed loudly. Curious, I asked, “Is something troubling you?” 

Seeing my concern, he replied, “Since yesterday, yet another incident has arisen. It seems that a messenger from the Chu capital demands my return; if I do not go, it may be perilous for me. What then will happen to Laojie?” 

“Certainly, that is a difficult matter,” I responded. 

“Yet…” he hesitated, casting a careful glance my way. 

“I’d like to suggest a compromise, though I am worried it may be inappropriate. I want to ask if you would accept Laojie as your wife.” 

He continued, trembling. “This is terribly awkward to ask, but if I may be so bold, if you were to take her, both our matters could be resolved. If not, we will all be in dire straits.” 

Hearing this, I pondered deeply. Truly, it was evident that not only had I developed an affection for the girl, but also for her mother. 

Upon hearing the elder’s proposal, I had hardly any time to deliberate; I promptly agreed to marry her. The elderly man beamed with joy and said, “Then everything will work out. Please choose a date for the wedding.” 

“By all means, let’s do it soon.” 

“Whenever there’s a good day approaching, it’s best to get things done quickly. Tomorrow will be a fine day to hold the ceremony.” 

Thus, our meeting concluded. Afterward, I discovered happiness I had never known before. 

At the age of forty, I first tasted the joys of love and companionship, feeling as if I were adrift in a dream. I ceased my duties at the shop and spent day and night indulging with my new wife. The elder returned to the capital following our wedding. 

Both my employer and my friend Xu Baiyuan were taken aback by my sudden marriage, but they understood that men and women alike cannot live in solitude and were joyous for me. 

So, in the following month, we lived a lavish lifestyle. Yet my wife, even unwittingly, began to harbor some concern regarding money. “I feel bad asking, but with such spending, what will we do?” 

“Do not fret. Even at the shop, there is still over two thousand gold.” 

“Indeed! That’s incredible.” 

“Certainly, you needn’t worry.” 

“But upon consideration, it leads to another anxiety.” 

My wife's demeanor clouded a bit. “What concerns you?” 

“Well, the more I think about it, money brings its troubles whether present or absent. What if the shopkeeper were to refuse to return my money?” 

“Surely that wouldn't happen.” 

“Yet do you have any paperwork supporting that?” 

“I have no document, but I have confidence in it.” 

“That would not do. One should not handle such things without proper assurance.” 

“If you are worried, you should withdraw everything.” 

“By all means then. I know that one must keep their money tightly.” 

So I assented to her reasonable request, saying, “In a day or two, we shall withdraw it all.” 

After a few days, I approached my employer to claim my savings. He knitted his brows, saying, “You may take it. However, this gold is the result of two decades of hard work and should be spent wisely.” 

“I shall be cautious.” 

Thus, I withdrew all my funds, amounting to about 2,500 gold, a significant sum. I showed the entirety to my wife, and her face lit with joy. 

“Indeed! This is fantastic. Let us safeguard it!” 

“Is that so? Our lives then will become secure.” 

Delighted, we continued living in bliss for another month. However, my duties called, and I had to make arrangements for a temporary trip to the shop for about 20 days. 

I took care to advise my wife during my absence, but unexpectedly found more business, returning five days late—25 days in total. 

As I hurried home, filled with longing for my wife’s charming face, what could await me? To my astonishment, upon returning, I found my house locked up tight. There was no answer to my knock, and in a state of utter disbelief, I turned to a neighbor, who revealed, “Your wife took the child and left approximately ten days ago.” 

In that moment, words could not express my feelings. Enraged, I smashed open the door, rushing inside to discover an empty home; every sign of furniture and my accumulated wealth disappeared without a trace. 

In an instant, the weight of my 20-year effort shattered. In despair, I threw caution to the wind, departing on a vagabond journey without a destination. 

Wandering aimlessly, a broken soul seeking anything, I ended up in this place, where I again made a living. This was the path of my burdens. 

“Sir, please think on this. Later, I learned that she became a songstress in the capital of Ying, and it is rumored she did this to find me and seized my wealth in misguided trickery.”  

Such a long and humorous yet poignant tale concluded, and Wen Liang let out a long sigh. I found the story exceedingly fascinating and asked, “Wen Liang, when did this happen?” 

“Yes, this occurred in June of last year.” 

“Then a year has passed.” 

“While it is not monumental, the pain still lingers in my heart.” 

“Ah, then that won't do. Let it go and move forward. One must strive to understand things and treasure memories during their life’s struggles and efforts; however, one must learn to let go, for holding onto burdens can become a great affliction.” 

Wen Liang looked at me in astonishment, inquiring, “Do you have a way to let go?” 

“Certainly. It cannot be contested; one must learn to forget, as it is the weight of unyielding heart that often burdens a person's life. Life must be embraced; let go to find light.” 

“Ah, is such release possible?” 

“There is a method, certainly.” 

“You remain caught in the bonds of attachment; forgetting is crucial.” 

“Indeed, I need to void those weights to alleviate my life.” 

“True, each affair is akin to numbers. Even in matters of finances and daily life, it all adds up; your outward attachments lead to enduring thoughts.” 

“Indeed, that is true.” 

“As you observe, one must learn to refresh their hearts, seeing each number vanish from their mind.” 

As I elaborated on this with Wen Liang, he mused, “Ha!” as he pondered deeply, eyes downcast in thoughtful silence, eventually raising his gaze to me, “Are you perhaps the sage Li Dan?”  

“Haha… I am indeed Li Dan.”  

Following this exchange, Wen Liang became my disciple, just as Chang Wu, the innkeeper, also joined our fellowship. How about my traveling tales? 

**Summary**

In this narrative, a man reflects on the twists and turns of life, tracing his path from a life dedicated to accumulating wealth to unexpectedly finding love and companionship. However, his brief happiness is shattered when his wife leaves him with their possessions, prompting his melancholic wandering. This tale illustrates the theme of unattainable desires and the fleeting nature of happiness amid personal struggles. Historians might find this poignant reflection on relationships and societal norms particularly intriguing in the context of the colonial period in Korea, where individual aspirations and traditional values often clashed against the backdrop of foreign rule.

						代老子
인생이란 변화가 만흔 것이다. 생각하면 생각할사록 불가사의의 것은 인생의 변화이다.
나는 60이 近當해가지고 가산의 영낙으로 해서 젊은 안해 黃葉을 더리고 다시 유랑의 길을 떠나게 되얏다. 마음에 은연히 다시 번화의 생활을 하고 십허서 왕도 洛陽을 향해 거름을 옴기게 되얏다.
약처와가티 떠낫스니 도중의 고통이야 말해 무엇하랴! 타지안어도 可할 말도 타고 수레도 타고하야 이렁저렁 삼사일만에 榮陽이란 곳에 니르럿다. 도중에 嵩山을 좀-올나 보았으면 조켓스나 그 亦 약처땀으네 중지하고 嵩山의 북으로 길을 취햇섯다.
榮陽이란 곳은 이즈음 매우 번화한 곳이다. 나는 어디든지 좀 - 적당한 곳을 가리여 유쾌히 좀 - 쉬여가랴고 말군과 가티 한참이나 차젓다. 맛츰, 照顔樓라는 가장 평판 조흔 곳을 어더 들게 되얏다.
누는 소천을 임하야 수림이 울창한 가운데 퍽 유수하게 되얏는데 나는 그 중에서 가장 청초한 一室을 택하야 신발을 끌럿섯다. 의외라고 할 수는 업지만 여행에 경험업는 약처는 그만 피로병으로해서 다시 행로가 어렵게 되얏다. 부득이 이 곳에서 멧츨간 두유하게 되얏다.
주인은 長梧라고 하는 50 남즛한 好男子이고 거긔에 文良이라고 하는 풍채조흔 好人物이 하나 잇다는데 그는 이 樓의 使喚格으로 잇는 사람이다. 나희는 40가량 되야보이고 체격과 안광이 아울너 비범한 산아희엿다.
주인과 사환이 엇지도 우리 부부에게 친절을 주는지 나는 실로 감사함을 마지 안엇다. 그 중에＜161＞ 특히 야간이면 양인이 번가러 우리의 방에 드러가지고 세상 니약이를 하며 병처를 위로해 주는데는 여간 감사하지 안엇다. 이렁저렁 발서 7일간이나 이곳에 머물넛섯다.
녹수를 건너고 靑葉을 슷처온 청풍이 난간의 방울을 흔들고 지나간 初夏의 석양이다. 이때 文良이 드러오며.
『선생님 진실로 조흔 계절임니다.』라고 인사를 한다.
나는,
『참말! 실로 조흔 시절일세.』라고 경쾌한 대답을 건넷섯다.
『기력이 엇더심닛가?』하고 그는 黃葉에게 또 무럿섯다.
「염려하신 덕분으로 매우 좃슴니다. 넘우 수고를 끼처 미안함니다.」라고 黃葉도 매우 조흔 기색을 뵈엿다.
「천만엣! 넘우 불편하서서, 그러나 큰일은 업스신 듯 하니...... 엇잿든 旅中의 병은 큰 곤난임니다.」
『그럿치, 여자와 가티 여행하랴면 물론 고생이지.』하고 내가 말을 가루 마텃다.
文良은 또 나를 향해 말을 건너기 시작하얏다.
『선생님, 미안한 말슴이오나, 선생은 鄭나라 양반이시라구요.』
『그랴......』
『바루 鄭나라 출생이신가요.』
『안이, 나기는 陳나라에서 낫고.』
『그럿슴닛가. 그런데 이즈음 鄭나라에는 子産이라고 하는 대인물이 잇서 평판이 매우 좃타지오.
『그런가 子産, 올치 평판이 조흔 인물이지.』
『그리시고, 또 鄭나라에는 李聃이라고 하는 유명한 인물이 잇다지오.』
이때 나는 깜작 놀내엿다. 그러나 천연스럽게 『그런가.』하고 시침이를 뚝 땃다.
『그러고보니 실례올시다만은 선생님도 보통인간은 안인 듯 십슴니다.』
『허허...... 나는 엇던 인물로 생각하는가.』
『선생은 엇잿든 보통 사람은 안인 것 갓슴니다. 처음 안색을 대할때도 그러코 말슴을 드러보아도 그럿코 엇던 점으로 보든지 선생은 보통 사람은 안임니다.』
『그런가, 엇잿든 간격업시 흉금을 터놋코 니약이하게 엇재서 보통사람이 안이여.』
『엇잿든 보통 사람은 안임니다. 선생님도 밥을 잡수시는가 의문임니다.』
『아 - 이런 거북한 말 보아. 사람치고 밥 안이 먹는 사람이 어대잇서...... 』
『실례올시다만은 선생은 엇잿든 이 세상사람 갓지는 안슴니다. 선생께서도 寒暑를 감각하심닛가.』
『암! 그럿치. 더울때는 더워하고 치울때는 치워하지.』
『그럿슴닛가. 조흔 음식을 대하면 맛나시는 것을 아심닛가.＜162＞』
『그대들이 조흔 음식을 해주면 물론 맛나게 먹지. 그러나 맛나는 음식을 어대 해 주는가. 하하하하...... 』
