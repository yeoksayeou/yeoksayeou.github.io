잡지명	별건곤 제9호  
발행일	1927년 10월 01일  
기사제목	入選時調, 無題 (Selected Poems, Untitled)  
필자	開城 朱德鍾 (Kaesŏng Chu Deok-jong)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

A sickle is like a hardened iron  
Iron devours iron  
The soft iron is like foliage  
Chews on the wood.  
So our life, too,  
Goes on cutting and scraping  

評 - Jealousy, hatred, quarrel, and murder abound in this world; such lamentation cannot be avoided. <134>  

**Summary**  

The poem reflects on the harsh realities of life, drawing a parallel between the actions of a sickle devouring material to the struggles and conflicts inherent in human existence. It portrays a sense of inevitability in the cycle of cutting and scraping, emphasizing the destructive nature of jealousy, hatred, and conflict that permeates society. This work, written in the context of Japanese colonial rule, highlights the emotional turmoil and societal issues faced by Koreans during this tumultuous period, which historians could find significant in understanding the cultural sentiments of the time.

入選時調, 無題
						開城 朱德鍾
줄칼은 가튼 쇠언만
쇠가 쇠를 쓸어먹고
속 쇠는 풀이언만

評 - 猜忌, 증오, 爭鬪, 살해가 만흔 이 세상에 이런 탄식을 아니할 수 업다.＜134＞
