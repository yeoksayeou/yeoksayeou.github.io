기사제목	結婚의 迷信 (Superstitions of Marriage)  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

In some regions of the United States, it is customary for the bride and groom to have money poured on their foreheads when they come out of the church. This is said to bring wealth!

Furthermore, it is believed that if a cat sneezes on the night before the wedding, it signifies a good fate!

In Germany, on the night before the wedding, several old plates are thrown into the street. If one of them does not break, it is considered unlucky!

In Scotland, it is said that if the bride does not cry during the wedding ceremony, it is also a sign of bad luck. If she does not cry that day, it is believed she will have reasons to cry in the future!<45>  

**Summary**  

This article explores various marriage superstitions from different countries, highlighting practices believed to influence the couple's fortune and future. The customs presented reflect a blend of cultural beliefs that associate certain actions with luck and misfortune, pointing to the significance placed on marriage in society during that time. Historians may find it particularly interesting to note how these superstitions reveal cultural values and social expectations surrounding marriage within the historical context of colonial Korea, where traditional beliefs intersected with Western influences.
