Summary  

The article discusses the establishment of various branch offices (支社) within different regions, detailing their specific locations, corresponding district areas, and branch managers. The document reflects the organizational expansion during this period, hinting at the administrative developments in colonial Korea. Historians might find the establishment of these branches indicative of the operational strategies employed by colonial authorities to consolidate control and enhance their administrative networks in Korea, thereby offering insights into the government structure during the colonial era.
