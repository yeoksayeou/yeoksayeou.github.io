Visiting an Old Friend's Illness, Accidentally Intruding into Someone Else’s Home, My Mistaken Visit  
Cha Sang-chan  
Until about five years ago, Mr. Yoo Jin-eok, who passed away, was my classmate at a vocational school, and we developed a special friendship. After he moved to Hwadong, he fell ill and had been suffering for several days. Having heard the news, I decided to visit him, but unable to find his house, I asked a neighbor, who said it was right across from the Women's Suffering Student Association. Although I knew the neighborhood fairly well, I thought it was polite to at least check the nameplate. However, at night, I couldn’t see the nameplate, so I entered through the main gate without a second thought. Upon entering, a lady from that house came out and told me that her husband had been bedridden for several days. Knowing this was Mr. Yoo’s home, I hesitated to announce myself and simply expressed my concern for Mr. Yoo’s illness and said that I had come to inquire about his health.

The lady went inside and, after some time, came back out asking me to come in. As I followed her into the courtyard, the family members, while caring for the sick person, seemed to be in a frenzy, moving to another room to avoid me. I believed them implicitly and stepped further into the inner room, and at that moment, looking through the narrow opening in the door, I caught sight of someone with their head wrapped, making sounds of distress; the person was a complete stranger, and Mr. Yoo was nowhere to be seen. 

I was so taken aback that I didn’t know what to do. I couldn’t go in any further or retreat. I stood there dumbfounded while the family members were puzzled, urging me to come in further without concern. It truly was a moment that made my heart drop. I thought it would be best to simply leave, even if I had to speak awkwardly. So I said, “Well then, I’ll just leave.” At that moment, I spotted another friend who had also come to visit and, trying to keep my dignity, I decided to leave through a different alley.

This was indeed a significant blunder. The next day, when I returned to see the nameplate of that house, I noticed that it had Yoo’s family name on it, but it turned out to be the home of Mr. Yoo Jin-eok. Mr. Yoo had only recently moved in, and when I had previously asked for directions to his home, the neighbors had also misunderstood and pointed me to the wrong house. Although it has been three or four years since Mr. Yoo passed away, I still think of him every time I pass near that house, and I am struck with both sadness and amusement at my blunder.

  
**Summary**  

This article depicts a humorous yet poignant mishap involving the author, who mistakenly visits the wrong house while trying to inquire about a sick friend, Yoo Jin-eok. Through this narrative, the author reflects on the enduring memories of a lost friendship and the quirks of human interaction. 

This anecdote reveals the complexities of social relations during a time of colonial rule, providing insight into how personal connections and misunderstandings thrived amidst the backdrop of societal changes in Korea. Historians might find the author's experience illustrative of both personal and communal identity issues in colonial Korea, as it captures the everyday life and challenges prevalent during that era.
