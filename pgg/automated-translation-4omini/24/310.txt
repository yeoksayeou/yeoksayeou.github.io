기사제목	生活改善同盟을 組織, 二重過歲廢止策 (Organizing a Life Improvement Alliance, Measures to Abolish Dual New Year's Celebrations)  
필자	韓基岳 (Han Gi-yuk)  
기사형태	논설 (Editorial)  

The elderly and middle-aged people who are still attached to old customs tend to believe that they truly age only after celebrating the lunar New Year. This belief seems even more pronounced among women. It appears quite difficult to remove these long-held customs in one sweep and to universally adopt the solar calendar. However, the dual practices we are currently engaged in stem from the lingering customs which we cannot seem to abandon, but if we wish to improve our lives, the primary reform must begin with rectifying this dual observance of New Year's.

Our stance is quite unclear. While we celebrate the solar New Year with gatherings and New Year's ceremonies, when we return home, we feel compelled to still perform the lunar New Year rituals of paying respect to elders and eating rice cake soup—a responsibility placed on the home's mistress. Nowadays, some households recognize this need and prepare rice cake soup and have celebrations for the solar New Year, but once they enter the lunar New Year period, they again prepare rice cake soup and their children don new clothes as they make their rounds visiting relatives. Thus, specific families cannot simply let the lunar New Year pass by excluded from the rituals. Moreover, those visiting to pay respect will expect to see the designated offerings, the so-called “Sebesang.” Houses might not have such offerings or might discourage visitors from coming due to lack of festive preparations; inevitably, they end up observing the lunar New Year instead.

It seems too challenging to execute such changes solely within individual families. What is needed is an organized institution, perhaps called the Life Improvement Alliance, to be formed. A collectivized body should emerge that can promote an understanding of ways to encourage the change in consciousness among household leaders, teaching them to dress children in new clothes on solar New Year and engage in play to foster the celebratory spirit that comes with this day. Yet, at the same time, it seems unlikely that the lunar calendar will be completely abolished and recognized as the sole New Year celebration. 

Ultimately, what is required is an organization that can support not just the reform of tax payments but accelerate improvements in all aspects of life, which, over time, could lead to a natural enhancement in mindset and surroundings as we engage in these changes.  

 

**Summary**

The article advocates for the establishment of a Life Improvement Alliance to address the dual observance of the lunar and solar New Year celebrations in Korean society during the colonial period. The author, Han Gi-yuk, highlights the challenges posed by deeply ingrained customs and argues that such an organized effort could foster a shift in societal attitudes toward modern practices. This reflects the broader struggle of Koreans during colonial rule to negotiate their cultural identity amid imposed Japanese practices, making it of significant interest to historians studying the period.
