기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Night Pilgrimage

Who is the largest? 

With no one by your side, 

Yours and mine are also nothing. 

Every night a luminous pearl 

Rises, chasing away the darkness and sighs... .

When a flock of clouds rises 

Over the mountains and into the sea, 

At this place and that place, 

Like the envoy of the king 

Gathering white waves of raindrops, 

Building up clouds upon clouds... .

Though the land is painted for three thousand li, and the way is long, 

“Has little Village Girl fallen asleep?” 

Crossing the mountain path, 

“Is the rain crow trembling?” 

Passing through the thickets, 

I rode on the clouds and came to Seoul.

Ding, ding, look at that firelight! 

Digging a trap deep in the belly, 

That firelight beckons me to come in. 

Look at that person wrapped in the colors of the rainbow, 

Bleeding and sorrowful. 

Resentment filled with colors of flame! 

A house of tears, a movie of colors! 

Look at the tears streaming down, the heart of a viper, 

A blood-drained face, white bones buried deep. 

Anger provokes the blood to surge. 

Look at the cries and the muscles! 

Oh! This land is terrifying, distressing!

Unable to endure, I turn my head 

And my face turns red, turning pale. 

I descended to the icy sea of the northern country, 

Wrestling with polar bears in the cold waters. 

From afar, it whistles! Whistles! The cold becomes louder. 

With the nightlight extinguishing the cold on this moonlit night, 

The heart of the miner digging graves wails.

This country, this sea, with an uneasy heart, 

Wrapped in the thick clouds of a lingering moon. 

The painted land of three thousand li, the way is long. 

The world is quiet, a tomb and an altar, 

Even through the night, the wailing continues, as it awakens. 

If the hated one breaks the peaceful dream, 

Isn't it terrifying? If it rains, shall I hide? 

If my heart aches and I can't sleep, 

I will sleep in the starry light of hope, 

And the dark heart will be soothed by the paint of the night!

The night road is long and 

The melancholy is deep. 

This land passes under the pale moon. 

The night road is long and 

The melancholy is deep. 

This land passes under the pale moon. 

(1930. 1. 14)  

  

**Summary**  

This poem depicts a deep sense of longing and despair reflecting the struggles faced during the turbulent times of the 1930s in Korea. The imagery used contrasts the serene beauty of night with the harsh realities and emotional pain of the society, hinting at colonial oppression and national identity. Historians may find the poem significant as it captures the sentiments of the Korean people under Japanese occupation, highlighting themes of hope, sorrow, and cultural resilience.
