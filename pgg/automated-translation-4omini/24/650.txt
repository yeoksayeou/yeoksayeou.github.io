The Golden Age of Automobiles  
Automobiles... ...automobiles  
The modern mechanism has brought about a civilization of high speed, within which airplanes and automobiles exhibit remarkable performance. While there are still people in Korea who have never seen an automobile, in the United States, the number of automobiles is reportedly growing in proportion to the entire population, with some cities even rumored to have more automobiles than inhabitants. It seems that we are on the verge of a world where machines created by humans are beginning to dominate human beings.

So, what is the number of automobiles?

The Automobile Kingdom, the United States  
When it comes to taste and preference, the United States dominates in terms of automobiles. The statistics truly show that the U.S. reigns supreme in the automotive world. Although it may seem surprising that countries like the U.S. have a lower number of vehicles compared to mileage, this is due to the extensive development of roads and vast territory.

**Summary**  

This article provides an extensive overview of the rise of the automobile industry, particularly focusing on the growth in the United States from a few vehicles to a staggering production number within a few decades. It also highlights the disparity in automobile distribution worldwide, notably mentioning Korea's relatively low automobile count during colonial rule. Historians may find the statistical evidence intriguing as it reflects the technological advancements and economic conditions of the 1930s, particularly in colonized nations like Korea, which lacked such industrial developments.
