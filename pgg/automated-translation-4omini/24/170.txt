Mrs. Ra Hwaek-sik, wife of Kim U-young, an Impressive Woman During the Visit  
Kim Ki-t'ae  
Among the friends I visited, when I think of a person whose impression or admiration captured me, it is Mrs. Ra Hwaek-sik, the wife of Kim U-young. She has a way of making the first encounter's atmosphere become one of peace and relaxation. Thus, it gives one the feeling akin to that of being by a clear stream flowing through white sand and green pines.

Furthermore, her attitude toward her children is like that of a friend toward a friend, creating an environment in the household that seems to overflow with harmony and smoothness even in a brief experience of observing them.

  
**Summary**  

This article describes the impressive character of Mrs. Ra Hwaek-sik, wife of a notable figure, Kim U-young, highlighting her ability to create an inviting and harmonious familial atmosphere. The depiction promotes cultural ideals of maternal warmth and interpersonal grace during a time when Korean society faced significant pressures under Japanese colonial rule. Historians may find Her portrayal reveals the traditional values still cherished in the family structure, even amidst pervasive societal changes.
