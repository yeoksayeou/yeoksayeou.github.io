기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Other)  

除夜  
						  
A whirl of wind howls as it sweeps through, passing between the dry branches and tuning the strings of a stringed instrument. Like a mischievous child, it throws up dust from the courtyard against the window and scampers away. It barely leaves the home of a frail clay figure lying in a corner. On this cold night, the wind darts around, causing a commotion all on its own.

"In Manchuria, the merchants cry out again today, never pausing," but he wails in vain about the hardships of life without any results or understanding. 

The wind blows on.

Having never heard a large drumbeat beneath someone else's roof, the beggars who huddle and cuddle up will be spending this night—come the morning, who knows how many souls will pass away? Bound by the pitiful instincts of wanting to survive, they tread the mortal path laid out for them, while this wind—oh, what a tragedy it would be if it extracts their last breath.

Today is the last day of the year. I close my eyes while attempting to settle the matters from this past year. Major and minor events unfold like a panorama in my mind's eye. Gradually, my thoughts wander further back, dredging up nostalgic memories and regrets, all tangled together, stirring up emotions, and I grow resentful of the past. Even now, as I resent the past, I cannot escape from my experiences; this is my agony.

I lifted my pen from the desk to write but could only end up in confusion, unable to write a single line. Suddenly, chime! Chime! The sound of bells ringing a hundred times for the New Year echoes. With an imposing and sacred sound demanding to cast off all concerns and old thoughts, it rings out.

A jubilant sound of victory fills the air, mingling with the sound of fireworks, thrumming with hope and tension. The ringing has both a feminine freshness and a masculine assurance, ringing in a resounding chime.

A new year has dawned. From now on, it is a new year. People lean on each other out of the idea of renewal, but amidst this birth of something new, clarity is scarce; it is challenging to see what is genuinely renewed.

In any case, my heart feels anxious.

I need to draft the programs for the new year and prepare to execute my plans. I have yet to dispose of my burdens from the old year, which have piled upon me, weighty and cumbersome beneath new ones. Again, I lower my head and ponder.

The rooster crows, piercing the darkness, as the spirits who wriggle without a head or tail are warded off, urging a hopeful light that wakes anew within the night. 

The sun rises. As it ascends with a burning heat and solemn emotion, people spread their arms to embrace the light of dawn as they march forward.

  
**Summary**  

This piece reflects on the thoughts and emotions surrounding New Year's Eve, contemplating the passage of time, memories of the past year, and aspirations for the upcoming year. The author grapples with feelings of anxiety about unresolved issues from the previous year while being uplifted by the sound of the bells ringing in the New Year. Historians may find the portrayal of the hardships faced by individuals during the colonial period significant, as it illustrates the impact of societal struggles on personal experiences, highlighting both despair and hope in difficult times.

『만-쥬노 호야-호야』오늘도 여전히 만주장사는 쉬지안코 소리를 지른다. 그는 아모의 구속도 업시 인생고(人生苦)의 하소연을 제멋대로 왼텬지에 호소하고 잇다.

하여튼 마음은 초조한다.
새해의 푸로그람도 짜서 보짐속에 너어야 하겟다. 향하고 나아갈 표방도 세워서 집행이를 삼어야 하겟다. 묵은해의 짐도 청산을 못하고 지고 넘서 왓스니 그 우에 새짐좃차 덥처지는 거북하엿다. 글어나 다시 머리를 숙이고 생각한다.
닭이 홰를 치며 울음을 운다. 어두운 때만 자긔의 세상이라고 꿈틀거리는 밋도 끗도 업는 유령(幽靈)들을 물니치고 정긔잇는 희망이 사람이 밤사이에 길는 새 힘을 가지고 니러나라고 닭은 귀를 찌르는 듯 뾰족한 소리로 놉히 부른다.
