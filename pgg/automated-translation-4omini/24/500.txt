잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1930년 02월 01일  
기사제목	내안해 (My Beloved)  
필자	金華山 (Kim Hwa-san)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Gathering breath, gathering breath, I will warm it with tenderness.  
Bring forth those cold hands, stiff from hardship.  
Oh, my beloved, wilted and emaciated from poverty.  

With tangled hair, you've come to me for six years now.  
Submerging your hands in cold water, it has been six years.  
You have patiently endured the difficulties that aren't even rumored.  

Like the evening sun's rays, the days of the past fade away.  
The sorrowful matters always bring chilly winds  
In the shadow of poverty, where no sound can be heard,  
You have laughed so heartily that even the larks flew away!  

However, my beloved, wilted and emaciated from poverty,  
We must live even while biting on stones.  
Collect the rainwater, do not let out the sigh of the impoverished.  

Poverty too is but a season, and sorrows too shall pass.  
When this time is over, a new world will arrive,  
This too will be an unstable dream of a single moment!  

Steeled your heart, make it as hard as a rock.  
Be as pure as white snow!  

Oh, my beloved, wilted and emaciated from poverty,  
Your hands, which shiver in winter, are like cold stones.  
Indeed, gather! I will warm it with breath!  

(1930. 1. 8)  

**Summary**  

This poem expresses the pain of living in poverty and the emotional bond between the speaker and their beloved. It touches on themes of suffering, resilience, and the hope of better days despite hardship. The vivid imagery of cold hands and endurance resonates with the struggles faced by many during this period of colonial rule in Korea. Historians might find it interesting as it illustrates the personal and collective experiences of Koreans living under the oppression of poverty and colonialism in the 1930s.
