기사제목	密陽의 女流豪俠 高遠涉氏, 訪問 가서 感心한 婦人 (The Woman Hero of Milyang, Go Won-seop, A Woman Who Impressed Me During My Visit)  
필자	車相瓚  
기사형태	소식 (News)  

The woman I was impressed with among those I visited was Go Won-seop from Milyang. About six or seven years ago, I went to Milyang for work and at that time she was a formidable woman active as the local newspaper branch manager or the president of the women's youth association. I went to her house hoping to meet her. Typically, when common women meet someone, even if they are engaged in physical activities like sports, they usually clean themselves up and dress properly, attempting to appear graceful in their speech and behavior. However, upon meeting me, she showed no sign of put-on airs and treated me with complete ease as if we were old friends.

At first, she wore the same simple clothing she wore while doing chores at home and greeted me with a warm handshake. After exchanging pleasantries, she personally went out to buy drinks and meats, saying, "In Milyang, the best beverages are makgeolli (막걸리) and beef, and you need to taste this to know the local flavor." Then she served herself some meat and poured me a drink, saying that one cannot drink alone, especially with an unfamiliar woman. She noted it was customary to refuse at first, but she had broken that awkward habit by having a drink first.

Although I had heard beforehand that she was a female hero, I didn’t expect to be so surprised and impressed upon meeting her. Having traveled to Seoul and rural areas and mingled with many women, I had rarely seen someone as sincere and straightforward with men as she was. Afterwards, we had no chances to meet again and exchanged no letters, but to this day, even after six or seven years, whenever I think of Milyang, she is the first person who comes to mind. Every time I pass through Milyang Station on the Gyeongbu Line, I remember her. Nowadays, she reportedly does not engage in the same public activities as before but leads a warm family life. However, back then, it would not have been an exaggeration to proclaim her as one of the three wonders of Milyang, alongside Yeongnang Tower (嶺南樓) and the Milyang River silver fish (密陽江 銀魚).  

**Summary**

The article recounts the author's experiences meeting Go Won-seop, a prominent female figure in Milyang who was notable for her straightforward demeanor and hospitality. The author reflects on her unique character, which stood out amidst the typical societal norms of the time, emphasizing her role as a female hero. This portrayal is interesting from a historical perspective, as it showcases the evolving roles of women during the colonial period in Korea, highlighting both resistance and adaptation to societal expectations.
