Secret Stories, Collection of 100 People  

Taegen Saeng  

Lee Sang-jae, who passed away years ago, was not only a Christian but also a thorough Christian, however, unlike other believers, he did not refrain from smoking. When at home, he would always smoke tobacco on a long pipe. When a fellow believer asked, “Teacher, do Christians smoke?” he smiled and responded, “When did Jesus say not to smoke? Tobacco did not exist in the time of Jesus. If there had been tobacco then, he certainly would have advised older people to smoke when they were bored.” They all laughed at that.

Ryu Gyu-hyeong was known by the world merely as a great scholar of classical Chinese, but he was a man of many talents beyond traditional studies. He wrote beautifully, used rapid strokes, played Go, and was proficient in various games such as card games without there being a master in any of them. His knowledge of mathematics, especially in music, and his painting skills exceeded that of ordinary people. Particularly in painting, he kept his work under wraps, with no one knowing of the pieces he had created, and he only shared them as screens drawn in his own house.

His real name is Lee Hee-seon. He entered the examination as a scholar disguised to pass the grades during the Gapsul exam. Because of that, when Min Yeong-hwi was governor of Pyeongan province, he lost all his assets on pretext of that crime. Afterward he wandered to China or Japan, graduated from a military academy in Japan, and returned during the Russo-Japanese War. His title became a lieutenant. It is known that he regained all his property from Min, but before going abroad, he had desperately sought to claim his property even before the military governor of Chuncheon. During that time, he reportedly walked from his hometown in Sookcheon, Pyeongan province, to Chuncheon, but fell ill and ended up resting at his relative’s house, where he negotiated multiple times with Min to regain his property, ultimately failing and going abroad.

The first Korean to write on the subject of Korean-Chinese literature was Gu-dang Ryu Gyu-jun, who wrote the famous “Record of Travel in the West” post his studies abroad.

At the end of Huaiyi Island at the Qiyun Spring, a stone wall bears the words “Qinglin Dongtian” and “Land Is Hollow and Old, Spring Is Clear and Stone Is New.” I always wondered whose handwriting this was, but when I asked an elderly man, he told me it was a remnant from the famous contemporary artist for ink and painting, Byeo Jeong-min Yeong-ik.

In a vacant lot behind a well in Boku Valley near Bokju, there lies a large long stone. On the stone are vividly inscribed the words “Hand-planted Pine by Seong Seong-seng.” According to the locals, this was originally the site of Seong Sam-mun's house, and for many years there used to be his hand-planted old pine tree, which was marked with this stone. However, now the old pine is dead, and nothing remains visible. Recently, someone is said to be constructing a house on the site, causing the stone to be cleared away.

During the Eulmi Incident, Eo Yoon-jung fled back to his hometown Yongin while riding in a carriage. Upon passing a hill, he asked the coachman what the hill was called, to which the driver replied it was called Eo Sal (Fish Kill) Hill. Hearing this, Yoon-jung was shocked and said, “My surname is Eo, and Eo Sal Hill is quite ominous.” He stayed silent for a while, and when the conversation ended, the nearby peasants, knowing he was Eo Yoon-jung, plotted against him and killed him mercilessly. This is similar to the historical incident of Pang Tong of the Shu Han who was killed at the Falling Phoenix Slope.

U Beom-seon was a distinguished man, but his thoughts were rather outlandish. He always said that Korea’s land was too small, and if anyone wanted to think about the nation’s future, they must consider expanding into Manchuria. When he exiled to Japan and changed his name, he specifically named himself Bukhyeilpyeong, implying he aimed to conquer the Manchurian plains, which at that time, when no one knew where Manchuria was, reflects his remarkable vision and ambition.

The so-called champion of the New Japanese Doctrine, Eo Shin-deok, the widow of the late Min Won-sik who was shot dead by Yang Geun-hwan about ten years ago, suddenly fell ill when she read about Yang’s temporary release from prison last year and took hours to recover afterwards, since then her health has been in poor condition. Whatever the details of the incident that led to his death, there will always be lingering resentment and discontent between their marriage.

The day before, at the Chosun Ilbo, Hong Myung-hee was there along with his brother Hong Seung-hee and his son Ki-mun. Nowadays, Hong Myung-hee writes a novel about Lim Gyeok-jeong, earns a substantial salary of one hundred won a month, and Ki-mun has become a head of research, yielding significant income. It seems there is a persistent connection between the Hong family and Chosun Ilbo.

After many years of being a celebrated individual in newspapers and magazines, the prominent figure Ju Yo-han was assigned to manage the Hwasin Department Store. Rumor has it he might be called back by Dong-a Ilbo for recruitment.

He is known as Chunwon, and in nature, he is gentle and harmonious, making him one of the most genial figures among writers. However, he does possess the local zeal that matches anyone. When he overheard that his picture was not featured in Chosun Ilbo when Ando San (An Dowon) was arrested and transported, he became furious. He yelled that since An Chang-ho is from Pyongan Province, it would not have occurred that way if it had been a person from Gyeongho. This outburst truly showed whether his heated feelings stemmed from an illness or local pride.

Kim Hyung-won from Chosun Ilbo and Oh Bong-min, head of Chosun Fine Arts Museum, were teased by their peers during high school for their intensely close relationship. Recently since Kim became the director of Chosun Ilbo, a friend joked with Oh upon seeing him, “Your wife has become the director of Chosun Ilbo; you should be more supportive,” to which everyone burst into laughter.

Jeong Dae-hyeon, currently the principal of Boseong High School, and Im Gyeong-soon from Dongil Bank have been closely connected since their studies abroad in Tokyo, tying their friendships deeply. Up until now, when Jeong meets Im, he inquires if his wife is doing well, to which Im reciprocates with inquiries about whether Jeong's older gentleman is also well, leading to laughter.

Min Dae-sik is known either as a saver or a spendthrift, it’s uncertain, but he is notoriously known for not spending money. Recently he had to treat his grandson, but his friends could not persuade him to pay for the meal. Ultimately, he was taken to a soup restaurant, but he only paid for what he consumed when leaving. Others commented on his stinginess, leading to a total silence in conversation afterwards.  

Not long after, the news reached Min Yeong-hwi, and he, feeling sorry for this, called his son Min Byeong-soo and said, “How embarrassing for your father not to contribute; even if it costs a fortune, you must treat on my behalf.” So, in a rightful jovial manner, Byeong-soo called in numerous friends from the bank and managed to treat them well with three hundred won.

 Kim Seong-su is relatively clean-cut toward women, but during his studies in Tokyo, there were hidden romantic tales. When he was lodging with Moon Il-pyeong, the landowner’s daughter reportedly showed much affection towards him, which he figured out. Through some manipulation, he seized her love first, leading to Moon’s failure and his success. To this day, when Moon meets Kim, he manages to light-heartedly refer to those old grievances.

Yang Won-mo, head of Dong-a Ilbo, has a wife named Byeon Yeong-ae. The amusing coincidence is that Yeong-ae sounds remarkably like “young daughter,” which led one of Yang's gossiping friends to comment, “So, you are married to your daughter?” causing the other listeners to laugh heartily.

Choi Nam-seon and Jeong In-bo once shared a close bond but after Choi’s employment started to dwindle, they stopped communication entirely, sending letters of severance from their friendship. It is unclear why, but they seem to have resumed their friendship, gradually returning to the old ways of correspondence.

He, who had been delivering Korean language lectures on the broadcasting network recently, one day failed to broadcast in Korean but instead stuttered through an English broadcast to the amusement of listeners. However, the truth is that the drunken man had mistakenly mixed English with Korean during his broadcast, resulting in truly excessive and confusing broadcasting.

Kim Jin-gu and Yun Baek-nam are both well-known storytellers. Kim once commented on the story of Kim Ok-kyun while having his eyes closed, exclaiming that he could clearly see Kim Ok-kyun. Meanwhile, Yun Baek-nam tended to reflect long on the story of the Yeonsan Dynasty, often emphasizing this repeatedly.

The late Bang Jeong-hwan and Ryu Gwang-ryeol were not only born in the same year, but shared a mutual understanding, engaging closely with each other. After Bang had passed away, Ryu fell into deep sadness, leading him to abandon his philosophy of remainders and hurriedly marry. With each task he undertakes, he now yearns for Bang’s presence, musing that his continued life is insignificant compared to Bang’s, and thus he has cultivated a mindset of not worrying excessively about his physical self.

Bang Eung-mo has become a notable figure leading the Chosun Ilbo but in the past, several years ago, when he managed the Dong-a Ilbo's Seongju branch, he faced much hardship when unable to settle the paper dues, leading to interruptions in publication. He came to the main office with great effort over that. Some would say when he initially brought important figures from the Chosun Ilbo to his new place, it was his own form of payback—whether that was indeed the case or not remains uncertain, but upon recalling Lee Kwang-soo, who once served as an editor in the main office, he might have silently rejoiced.

Park Hee-do is known for his stature, and as such, he is also quite big in terms of food consumption; he typically eats two bowls of stone soup for lunch, enough for about six or seven people.

Song Jin-woo was once joked about for not having a beard, but he secretly took some medicine to grow facial hair, and recently it has started to sprout, possibly leading to substantial growth by the time he reaches middle age.

Lee Yoon-jae is known for his habit of walking, famously wearing a coat during winter, yet instead of shoes, he often carries a unique pair of slippers. He often walks around in these slippers while teaching, surprising his students.

Economics doctor Lee Geuk-ro is known for his new economic policy that starts from rubber shoes and is almost always seen wearing rubber shoes. However, his wife still continues to wear heels proudly.

Dokgo Hyun and Sigo Hwan, from Jungdong School and Kyungsung Child Welfare respectively, both carry compound surnames. First-year students, unaware of their compound last names, would mistakenly refer to Dokgo as “The teacher from the Independent School” and Sigo as “The teacher from the Secretary,” making others laugh.

Yoon Chi-ho is known to gather his family every Saturday to eat out, whereas Park Seung-bin imposes strict meat restrictions on his family every Thursday for dining. This is something only someone wealthy would impose!

After the dissolution of the Gyeongwoo Club, which aimed at social affairs, he has been engaging in weaving and embroidery as matters of leisure. Maybe this indicates a shift toward the ideals of socialist artistry.

Kim Ja-hye from Sin Dong-a and Kim Ja-hye from Geunhwa School share the same name, leading to frequent confusion. At times people would mistakenly approach Kim from Sin Dong-a when inquiring about news from Geunhwa, and vice versa. At one point, it became almost routine for suitors who were already enamored with Geunhwa's Kim Ja-hye to send love letters to her namesake from Sin Dong-a, obliviously.

Lee Gab-soo and Lee Gab-su are often confused because of their similar names and both being doctors. One especially humorous incident occurred last year when Lee Gab-soo was having a wedding reception at Chosun Hotel. Many guests who were unacquainted mistook him for Lee Gab-su, resulting in a humorous situation throughout the night.

Kim Gi-jeon, who has been receiving treatment free of charge at Haiju Nursing Home for many years, has seen good improvement lately. If he continues progressing well, he might be discharged either this spring or summer. Along with that, it is heartening to know that the hospital has been offering him free treatment all this time, even providing him with a special infirmary at the ocean side in recognition of his situation until he is ultimately released.

Whenever children see him, they call him the “Guri Grand Teacher.” It’s not because he is good at telling flatulent jokes, but rather that whenever he talks with children, he often recounts the old stories about Guri and its chants, leading to this nickname.

Choi Seon-ik is referred to as the Deputy Director of the Office due to his character. This doesn’t concern his character but rather as he is the youngest compared to other deputy directors from various newspapers like Lee Sang-hyup from Dong-a and Lee Kwang-soo from Chosun.  

**Summary**  

The article presents a collection of anecdotes and personal histories of notable individuals in Korea during the early 20th century. It portrays their unique quirks, relationships, and experiences amid the changing socio-political landscape of Korea under Japanese colonial rule. Particularly fascinating is the insight into how personal interactions and cultural practices reveal the broader tensions and realities of colonial life, reflecting a mix of humor, resilience, and the challenges faced by intellectuals of the time.

Historians may find interest in the way personal anecdotes intersect with political realities, revealing public sentiment and the struggles of individuals against external pressures.

						多言生
李商在
년전에 작고한 이상재씨는 예수교인으로는 물론 찰예수교인이지만은 다른 교인들 모양으로 금연(禁煙)을 하지 안코 집에 잇슬 때에 항상 장죽에 입담배를 피웟는대 어떤 교인이 질문 비슷하게 뭇기를 선생님 리교인도 담배를 먹습니까 하니까 그는 싱긋이 우스며 하는 말이 예수씨가 언제 담배먹지 말나고 말슴하엿소. 예수시대에는 담배가 업섯기에 그럿치 만일 그때에 담배가 잇섯다면 그 박애하시는 양반이 반듯이 말슴하시기를 우리가타 늙은 사람들은 심심한 때에 몃대식 먹으라고 말슴하섯슬 것이라고 하야 그 뭇던 사람도 또한 일소를 하얏다고 한다.

呂圭亨
하정(荷汀) 려규형씨는 세상 사람들이 다만 한문대가(漢文大家)로 만 아지 만은 그는 한학이 외각방면으로 다재다능한 분이다. 글씨도 물론 잘쓰는데 속필이요. 바둑도 농수요 그 외장긔 수투전 골패 등 어느 잡긔에 명수안 인 것이 업고 數學 풍류(특히 양금) 그림도 보통사람 이상의 조예(造詣)가 잇는데 특히 그림은 남모르게 혼자 그려서 세상에 전한 것이 한폭도 업고 자긔집에 병풍한 채를 그려서 전해들 뿐이엿다.

李甲
그의 본명은 이희선(李徽善)이 다 갑술과(甲戌科)에 나히를 속이고 진사(進士)한 까닭으로 민영휘(閔泳徽) 평안감사 때에 그 죄를 구실삼아 전 재산을 다 빼앗기고 그 뒤 중국으로 또는 일본으로 방낭하다가 일본사관학교를 졸업하고 일로전쟁때에 귀국하야 일략 참영(參領)이 되여 민씨에게 그 재산을 다 찻게 된 것은 세상사람이 다 아는 사실이지만은 그가 외국에 가기 이전에도 민씨에게 재산을 차지랴고 엇지애를 섯던지 민씨 춘천류수(春川留守) 시대에 그의 고향인 평안도 숙천(肅川)에서 춘천까지 도보로 거러갓섯는데 발병이나서 춘천에 잇는 그의 외족 차씨(外族車氏)의 집에 누어 잇스면서 그 돈을 차지랴고 민씨에게 여러번 교섭하다가 그만 실패를 하고 그길로 외국으로 갓섯다고 한다.

兪吉濬
조선사람으로 조한문(朝漢文) 교요의 저서(著書)를 처음 한 분은 구당유길준(矩堂兪吉濬)이니 그가 구주에 류학하고 도라온 후 력작한 유명한 서유긔견문긔(西遊見聞記)란 것이 그것이다.

閔永翊遺筆
가희동막바지 취운정(聚雲停) 약수터의 석벽에 청린동천(靑麟洞天) 네글자와 『地僻山意古, 泉淸石氣新』이란 글 한 귀를 색인 것이 잇는데 그것이 누구의 필적인지 알지 못하야 항상 궁금하얏더니 어떤 로인에게 드른즉 근대 조선청게에서 세력이 당당하고 서화로 유명하던 표정민영익(杓亭閔泳翊)의 유필이라고 한다.

成三問手植松
화동(花洞) 복주 우물 뒷골-속충비각뒤라고 하는 빈터에는 큰 장석(長石)이 한 개 누어잇고 그 장석에는 성선생수식송(成先生手植松) 이란 여섯자가 뚜렷이 색여잇다. 그 동리사람의 말을 드르면 그터는 원래 성삼문(成三問) 선생의 집터로 몃십년 전가지도 그의 수식한 노송이 잇섯고 그돌은 그것을 표시하기 위하야 세윗던 것인데 지금은 소나무도 죽어업서지고 몰만남어 잇다고 한다. 그런데 요새에는 그 터에 누가 집을 짓노라고 돌까지 엇다치워 버려서 볼 수가 업게 되엿다.

魚允中과 魚殺峴
을미사변(乙未事變) 때에 어윤중이 부인라는 교군을 타고 자긔 고향인 륭인(龍仁)으로 도망을 하얏는데 한고개를 넘어가다가 교균 꾼더러 뭇기를 이 고개가 무슨 고개냐 한즉 교군꾼이 대답하되 어살이 고개라고 하얏다. 윤중은 그 말을 듯고 대경실색하야＜18＞ 왈 내성(姓)이 어가인데 어살이고개(魚殺峴)면 대단이 불길한다 하고 교군에 나려서 한참 동안 넉을 일은 듯이 안젓더니 그말이 끗나자 그 근처에서 농사하던 무지란민이 그가 어윤중인 줄 알고 작당하야와서 함부로 란타하야 죽엿다. 그것은 맛치 녯날 촉한(蜀漢)의 봉추선생방통(鳳雛先生龐統)이 낙봉파(落鳳坡)에서 죽은 것과 사실이 비슷하다.

禹範善
