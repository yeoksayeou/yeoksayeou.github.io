기사형태	잡저 (Periodical)  

The saying "If you worry a lot, you lose weight" has been heard for a long time. However, a recent report from an American university, "A-rl" (Harvard), presented by Dr. Miles, a psychology professor, has published a detailed experimental study on how emotional disturbances affect weight changes. The method involves creating a special bed equipped with a device capable of precisely measuring even minimal weight fluctuations. A person lies on this bed while their psychological state is observed in order to detect any weight changes resulting from emotional fluctuations.

Naturally, it's obvious that the rate of weight loss in humans is lowest when they are sleeping. However, this bed reveals that even during sleep, unconscious emotional disturbances significantly impact a person's weight.

 

**Summary**

This article discusses a study that investigates the impact of emotional fluctuations on weight variation during sleep. The findings suggest that even while sleeping, a person's emotions can influence their weight, challenging preconceived notions about weight loss occurring only when one is awake. This reflects a growing interest in psychological factors affecting physical health, a topic that could illuminate the intersection of mental and physical well-being during the colonial period in Korea. Historians may find this noteworthy as it highlights early psychological research within a colonial context, potentially indicating Korea's engagement with Western scientific methods and thoughts.

感情과 體重關係
『걱정을 몹시하면 옴이 마른다』는 말은 예전부터 들어나려오는 말이다. 그런데 최근에 감정이 동요가 체중증감에 잇서 어떠한 영향을 밋치게 하는가에 대하야 미국 『에-ㄹ』 대학이 심리학 교수 『마일스』 박사에 의하야 처음으로 정밀한 실험보고가 발표되엿다. 이 방법으로는 여하히 적은 중량의 증감이라도 곳 민감하게 계산할 수 잇는 특별 장치의 침상을 만든 후 그 우에다 사람을 새우면서 부절히 그 심리상태를 관측하야 그 체충에 밋치는 변화를 침상에 장치한 긔게에 낫하나게 한다는 것이다.
물논 인간의체중의 감소율은 잠자는 동안이 가장 적은 것은 당연한 일이겟스나 이 침상에 의하면 잠자는 동안이라도 사람의 무의식적 감정의 동요가 완연히 그 체중에 영향됨을 알수가 잇섯다.＜52＞
