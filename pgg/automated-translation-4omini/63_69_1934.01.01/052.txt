**Summary**  

This article provides an extensive list of notable mountains and rivers in Korea, detailing their heights and lengths in traditional measurements. The information preserves elements of indigenous knowledge about geographical features during the colonial period, illustrating how the natural landscape was both a part of Korean identity and a point of administrative interest under Japanese rule. Historians might find the precise measurements and regional specificity revealing, as they highlight the colonial engagement with land and geography.
