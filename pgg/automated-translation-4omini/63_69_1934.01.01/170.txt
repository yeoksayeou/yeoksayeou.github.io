기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

How many days can a person survive without eating? 

Everyone experiences thoughts of hunger when they do not consume food. Initially, this feeling appears, but interestingly, after a certain period, it suddenly becomes less intense, and the urge to eat seems to grow even stronger. Eventually, this sensation subsides as well, and if fasting continues for several days, the person will lose all perception of hunger. The body becomes excessively weak, and ultimately, they lose consciousness and die. 

Then, how much food do we really need? It is stated that approximately 2,500 calories of intake is necessary to maintain normal bodily function. The ability to endure hunger varies from person to person, but if one fasts without even drinking water, it is generally believed that survival is possible for at least a week, while with just water, survival can be comfortably extended to about three weeks. <40>  

**Summary**  

The article discusses the physiological responses of humans to starvation, detailing how the sensation of hunger fluctuates over time and the body’s ability to endure fasting. It notes a general caloric requirement for maintenance of normal health and offers insights into survival durations under extreme conditions. This topic reflects broader issues related to food scarcity and physical endurance relevant to the historical context of colonial Korea, offering a glimpse into the lived experiences under colonial rule where access to food could be irregular or limited.

사람은 누구나 음식을 먹지 안으면 배곱흔 생각이 나는 것이 원측이나 고때가 조곰만 지나면 이상하게도 아모럿치 안타가 또다시 전보다 더 심하게 배곱흔증이 생겨지는 것이다. 그러다가 또다시 이것이 멈추어 지는데 이럿케 하기를 몃칠 동안 게속하면 전연 이에대한 감각이 업서지고 전신이 무긔력하게 되며 몸이 수척해 지고 나중에는 의식 불명이 되야 죽어버리는 것이다.
그러면 우리들에게 필요한 음식이 량은 얼마나 되겟느냐 하면 대략하로에 2천5백카로리의 보급이 잇서야 평소 현상대로 유지 될 수 잇다는 것이다.
배곱흔 것을 참는 것은 그 사람의 체질여하에 따라 일정치는 안으나 대개 물도 먹지 안코 그냥 그대로 단식을 한다면 적어도 일주간 동안은 생명을 부지 할 수 잇스며 물만 이라도 먹는다면 삼주간 동안은 넉넉히 살수 잇다 한다.＜40＞
