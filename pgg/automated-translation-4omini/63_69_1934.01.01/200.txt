**Summary**  

The poem reflects a child's perspective on a snowman made the day before, filled with a sense of wonder and innocent questioning about its fate. The child speculates whether the snowman has melted away due to warmth or if someone has taken it to warm up by the fire. This playful imagination captures the essence of childhood in the stark realities of a winter landscape. Historians might find this article interesting because it offers a glimpse into the cultural reflections and everyday life of Korea during the colonial era, seen through the eyes of a child, highlighting how colonial influences seep into even the simplest expressions of creativity.
