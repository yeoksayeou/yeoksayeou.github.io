Humorous Orchestra, A Talk  
Author: Yoon Bak-nam  

A woman is the embodiment of vanity.  
While traveling at night, I, the author, do not hesitate to present such unpleasant traits. However, who can deny that women, more than men, are driven by vanity? This undeniable fact is a predicament we must all face.  
"Hey..."  
Upon seeing her husband’s face the moment he returned after resigning from his job, she seemed to be someone who had just sent multiple telegrams with worries.   
"Why so?"  
"Look at this! An invitation has arrived!"  
The invitation stated that a soirée would be held on a certain day, and that they would like the honored guests to attend. It was signed by the name of a well-known businessman in society at that time.  
"Dear, you should have an acquaintance with many distinguished people at events like this. How could such a fine invitation have reached us? I wonder who referred us... but I..." The woman, with a slightly worried expression on her face, was troubled.  
"But... I have is one set of outing clothes, but for a soirée, wouldn't I have to wear a necklace at least? Don't you think?"  
This couple, who had just recently married, were experiencing marriage-related conflicts due to financial issues despite the love they had for each other. At such times, the man often felt a pang of guilt, especially when the mention of "necklace" was brought up, and for a moment, he seemed lost.  
"And how do you expect me to suddenly buy you a necklace? We don't even have the means!"  
Ignoring the thought of dinner, they pondered how they could possibly attend an event without being outshined by others. They immediately thought of Mrs. O, who lived nearby.  
Mrs. O was a wealthy widow, and having seen her with exquisite necklaces worth thousands, they hoped she might lend something.  
Indeed, Mrs. O showed compassion. Therefore, she readily lent a dazzling diamond necklace worth five thousand won without hesitation.  
The woman took it home and eagerly counted the days leading to the soirée, flipping through the calendar multiple times.  
The anticipated evening finally arrived.  
The couple attended the event, feeling delighted, enjoyed eating and dancing. The woman consumed a large amount of alcohol, to the point of losing her senses and feeling embarrassed, so she stepped outside for fresh air, leant against a wall, and eventually fell asleep.  
When her husband came out after some time, he found her sleeping soundly with a slight snore.  
"Dear, what's with this sleep? How embarrassing!"  
As he shook her gently, his wife opened her eyes in surprise.  
"Oh, where's the necklace?"  
Seeing that his wife did not wear the necklace anymore, he was shocked.  
"What? Oh my goodness, what's happened?!"  
The woman's face immediately turned pale, and the husband had a meal of cold sweat as the fear of theft struck them.  
Defeated, they returned home, dazed.  
"What should we do?"  
"Five thousand won—five thousand won—oh, our lives seem to be over."  
The couple debated long into the night but could find no solution. They eventually packed their belongings and fled their hometown that night.  

Ten years passed.  
On a stormy day, that evening was reminiscent of the event that took place a decade before.  
A shabby couple stood before Mrs. O's house.  
"Who are you?"  
"I might not be recognizable. We are the couple who fled ten years ago after borrowing the necklace."  
"Oh, indeed, so it seems... But why did you run away that night?"  
With tears in their eyes, they recounted their story.   
"On that fated night, the necklace was stolen from us. How could we have imagined such a dreadful fate? Of course, we had no means to repay the five thousand won... We debated between us, and together, we decided to leave that very night. Until today, we have scrimped and saved, hoping to repay even a fraction of that, and we have managed to save only twenty thousand won."  
"It's not the five thousand, but we humbly ask for your forgiveness for the burden on our conscience."  
So saying, they took out two thousand won from their bag.  
The widow gazed at the money and the couple with tearful eyes, then suddenly began to sob uncontrollably.  
"Why are you doing this?"  
"Please forgive me! The necklace was not worth five thousand won; in reality, it was valued at only a few hundred won. Thinking about my excessive vanity leading to such precious ten years of youth wasted for the young couple makes me feel I must come to your feet to confess my wrongdoing. How can I atone for this sin? Now, I am but a single woman, with no means to offer you."  
Regardless of the intentions, the couple and the widow embraced and sobbed together.  

Twenty-eight thousand winds  
The solitary sail rises  

(A sound of a radio that is vibrating in the wind from beginning to end)  
Man: "Is this ugly thing, the ring made of diamond from Kim Jun-su, what has you envious? What are riches? What is a diamond? We have only pure, unwavering love. So you are thinking of going to that batty fellow, Kim Joong-bae? Wouldn't that make your mind at ease?"  
Woman: "But I won’t be full."  
Man: "If being full is what you desire, come with me down this road, for I am a capable man. In just two months, I can have your belly round."  
Woman: "But you need money to live."  
Man: "Don't worry! I will save a million won with your charm."  
Woman: "What? A million won?"  
Man: "Yes, a million won! The diamond necklace of the Queen of Holland can be bought for twenty thousand. Right away, I'll put it around your neck, take you to Geumgangsan and build a villa wherever you please."  
Woman: "But isn’t the countryside lonely?"  
Man: "If the countryside is lonely, we can go to Paris, to New York."  
Woman: "I might get seasick."  
Man: "If you get seasick, I will carry you if the airplane crashes. Paris is a rustic charm. New York is a golden bee trap."  
Woman: "I'd get homesick."  
Man: "If you miss home, you can come back to Joseon."  
Woman: "But there's no home."  
Man: "Why wouldn’t you have one? I would build a spacious area around Samgaksan and construct a cultural home with a fragrant bathhouse and plenty for you to enjoy. You can feast on rice prepared with golden rice, and sweet treats whenever you desire."  
Woman: "Just be giving."  
Man: "Why don’t we share? You can ride my car, and I ride yours. The greenhouse always has your favorite white camellia blooming, and in the reception room, the floor is covered with cash and bank merchants paying homage to you, my dear."  
Woman: "What if a thief breaks in?"  
Man: "What’s there to worry about, they are not able to touch their owner."  
Woman: "Uh, the ghastly ugly man will just see me dead."  
Man: "Why would that ugly man harm his owner?"  
Woman: "Nothing’s appealing."  
Man: "You are starving so acutely for food? Don't worry! The kitchen is stocked with chefs from across the world: English stews, American spaghetti, German beans, Indian cold noodles, Brazilian fried rice—everything to satisfy your desires."  
Woman: "But, I think I’m missing kimchi."  
Man: "Ah! Your lifelong wish is to have kimchi stew, what a delightful aspiration!"  

The Crushed Pot  
Lee Seo-kyu  

In the middle of Jongno, a trolley and a car collided. Inside the car sat a young lady of about eighteen. The impact caused her to lose a tooth and her posterior to become as flat as a pan. Even further, the trolley driver inadvertently bumped into the young lady’s flattened posterior, trying to catch his breath with his nose stuck in the clothes. Truly a scenario that can’t be believed. Onlookers flocked, unaware of the undignified circumstances, jeering that the driver and the young lady were having an affair.  

In the cheerful May sunlight, the oil-stained driver’s gloves and the young lady’s scorched asphalt were mixed in a peculiar exposure capturing this unusual class struggle. Yet, the ticket from the young lady's handbag and the driver’s notebook showed the unfortunate scene of a crushed relationship on that lovely afternoon.  
Woman: "So that’s why money is indeed necessary."  
Man: "That’s not the reason. Apparently, from those forms of savings and ticket together, the same printing house had birthed love on the very same day."  
Woman: "But how did the collision happen?"  
Man: "Oh, do you really want to know? The car was trying to cross the trolley tracks when an old man from the other side rushed in hurriedly. The driver panicked and swerved, colliding soundly into the trolley."  
Woman: "And why was the old man in such a hurry?"  
Man: "Ah! A beloved child of his—like jade—had gotten drunk the previous night after celebrating the birth of the first son for the company after ten years, leading to this distressing situation."  
Woman: "What a miraculous tale! How did they manage to have a first son?"  
Man: "Well, they had a housemaid at the management for over a year, who claimed that taking a dip in the warm spring will help conceive, prompting his wife to visit the hot springs. It seemed the heavens as they were destined, blessed them with a child."  
Woman: "Then what of the housemaid?"  
Man: "Well, she was introduced by the wife of the manager of the housemaid's friend who made this connection."  
Woman: "Then how did the friend get to the lawyer's home?"  
Man: "Ah, that's a long story! The husband of that friend has recently been involved with a café beauty, always in trouble with such a raucous life. She, tired of his antics, had decided to seek a divorce, which led to hiring the lawyer’s wife."  
Woman: "Then what of the café beauty's past?"  
Man: "Her name is Seomseom; in reality, her common name is Kim Seong-nyeo. The café girl was known as 'Raygo.'"  
Woman: "Then Raygo's appearance was what led to the young lady’s backside being squashed, right?"  

That Night  
Wang Byeong  

A long night! The wife, waiting for her husband to return, said,  
"Woe is me! My husband squandered his meager salary in a bar and brought back just two cents! Are we to starve in this house?"  

"Don't die just yet! At least he brought those two cents!"  

"What do you mean?"  

"Oh, during a long night like this, let me have a drink. A woman’s voice calls outside—our household’s doomed!"  

“Yet he managed to come home with any salary this month...”  

"Even if that’s the case, is that any excuse to squander all your salary in a single evening?"  

"I mean, after laboring for an entire month, payday comes, and I have to celebrate!"  

"Celebration has a limit, especially for you to spend all your salary!"  

"Well, just eat if you are to eat!"  

"And how do you expect us to eat?”  

"There’s no grain for tomorrow!”  

"Then why don’t we settle with breakfast?"  

"All food is gone!"  

"No porridge?"  

"That’s right! Without cooking, how will I cook rice?"  

"Without an ungrateful mess, let’s eat seolleongtang!"  

"We can't buy that with the meager amount you brought home!"  

"Then it must be the delay in payment!"  

"You know I can’t!"  

"Surely, that bank isn't watching a dying man."  

"Then what do I care?"  

"Well then, that's how we’ll lead our lives; who’s afraid?"  

"Oh, this foolish husband of mine!"  

"Hey, I don’t care about it at all!"  

"Honestly, it’s a tragedy when we don’t eat! What shall I do?"  

"Next month, we can manage it all at once!"  

"What? You are suggesting we should starve for a whole month?"  

"Eat if you are hungry!"  

"And what use is that?"  

"Eat until this means there is nothing left."  

"Oh, I can’t take this anymore. I might have to leave!"  

"But where will you go? Aren't you aware of what will become of you at 40?"  

"Hey, I need to leave here sometime!"  

"At your age? Who’s going to look for you?"  

"Ah, I’m not old!" (Crying)  

"Isn't it a shame? I was preparing a home for you on top of all the trees, complete with a stove that thickened your living space and breakfast for your fancies."  

"Is that true?"  

"I just had a dream about building twelve homes for you."  

"Really? Since when did you say that?"  

"You know what? I believe that was during a dream I had last night."  

"What are you saying today?! Am I a joke?"  

"Don't get upset; it was just a jest about the future!"  

"That must be a dream in our lives."

목거리
尹白南

녀자는 허영의 화신이외다.
밤길을 다니지만 이하는 필자는 이런 밉살을 서슴지 안코 내놋는다. 그러나 어째떤 허영심이 만키는 남자보다 녀자이니 이 엄연한 사실이야 뉜들 어쩔수 잇스랴.
『여봐요...』
회사에서 사퇴해 도라온 남편의 얼골을 보자마자 지금 전보를 대여섯장이나 겁허바든 사람가티 허겁을 해서 남편을 부른다.
『왜 그리우』
『이걸좀 보서요. 초대ㅅ장이 왓다우』
그 초대ㅅ장에는 오는 XX날 야회를 열겟사오니 동부인하서서 래임하시기를 바란다는 뜻이 적혀 잇고 당시 사회에서 이름이 놉흔 모 실업가의 이름으로 서명이 되어잇다.
『여보 이런 긔회에 당신도 여러 명사들과 지면을 해두어야하오. 참 어쩌면 이런 조흔 찬쓰가 왓슬가 대관절 누가 우리를 소개햇슬가 고마운 일야... 그러나 난』
하고 XX자는 어엽분 얼골에 눈ㅅ살을 잡으리며 난처한 빗을 띄웟다.
『난 어째』
『옷은 한 벌 나들이 옷이 잇지만은 야회라면 명류부인들이 올텐데 목거리라도 하나 걸고가야지오. 그러치 안소.』
신혼한 지 얼마 아나되는 이 한쌍 부부는 련애결혼임에도 불구하고 잇다금 경제무제로 하야 족금한 다툼이 각금 이러나는 터이다. 그럴 때마닥 남편되는 위인의 가슴은 미상불찔-한적이 만은대 지금 또 『목거리』 말을 드르매 눈압히 일순간 캄캄해젓것다.
『그러타고 지금 별안간 안이 별안간이 안이라 우리 살님에 무슨 수로 『목거리』를 산단 말요.』
내외는 저녁밥 먹을 생각도 제처노코, 엇지하면 남에게 빠지지 안는 차림차림으로 야회에 참석할 수 잇슬가 하는 문제에 업는 지헤를 짜내여 본 결과 - 두 사람은 바로 이웃에 사는 OO부인을 차저갓다.
OO부인은 백만장자 미망인으로 『목거리』 가튼 것도 대엿섯개 잇는 것은 보앗스니까 렴치불고하고 하나 어더보려는 작정이엇다.
과연 OO부인은 동정을 햇다. 그래서 눈이 부신 따이아몬드 목거리 갑시 5만원이라는 것을 두말안코 빌려주엇다.
XX자는 그것을 빌어가지고 와서 일력(日曆)을 하로도 몃번씩 뒤집어 보며 야회ㅅ날이 오기를 기대렷다.
고대한던 야회날은 왓다.
내외는 의긔양양해서 그 연회에 참석하고 마시고 먹고 춤추기 시작햇다.
XX자는 깃분김에 먹지 못하는 술을 다량으로 마시고 정신이 오락가락하게 되고 나서야 비로소 부끄럽기도 해서 빨코니-로 찬바람을 쏘이러 나가서 그란간에 기대여 안잣다가 잠이 들엇다.
남편이 차자서 나온 때에는 XX자는 가느다란 코를 골고잇섯다.
『여보 이게 무슨 잠요. 창피하게』
XX자의 억개를 흔들며 남편은 나즈막히 꾸지젓다. 안해가 깜작놀내서 눈을 떳다.
『아 그런데 여보 목거리는 엇째ㅅ소.』
남편은 안해의 목에 목거리가 업는 것을 보고 놀내서 무럿다.
『네? 에구머니나 이게 웬일요』
XX자의 얼골은 금시에 새파라케 질리고 남편의 얼골은 침면조 뻔으로 야릇한 빗치 오르락 내리락 햇다. 다이아몬드 목거리는 확실히 도적마젓다.
내외는 망연자실해서 제집으로 도라왓다.
『이 일을 엇짜면 좃소』
『5만원-5만원- 아아 우리의 생활은 끗치 왓나보우』
내외는 밤이 깁도록 의론을 햇지만 별도리가 업섯다.
둘은 여간 보ㅅ잠을 싸가지고 그밤으로 고향을 도망해 나왓다.

십년이 지낫다.
비바람 치는 날 마츰 그날은 십년 전에야 회가 잇섯던 날 저녁이다.
락씨-를란 허푸수수한 남녀가 백만장자 미망인의 집 문압헤 서나렷다.

『누구신지 생각이 아니남니다.』
『그럴런지도 모르겟습니다. 십년전에 목거리를 비러간 채로 도망해간 내외올시다.』
『오-참 그럿습니다 그려... 그런대 어째 그밤으로 도망을 하섯드란말요.』
그 내외는 눈물과 함께 아래와 가튼 이야기를 시작햇습니다.
『그만 찬회ㅅ날 밤에 목거리를 도적 마젓습니다. 그러라고 무슨 염의로 이러버렷다고 하겟습니가. 물론 5만원을 보상할 돈은 업고... 내외가 의론한 결과 부부가 그 밤으로 이곳을 떠낫습니다. 그래서 오늘까지 십년동안을 먹는 것＜8＞ 입는 것을 절약해가며 일ㅅ단 그 돈을 갑흘 결심으로 모으고 모앗다는 것이 겨우 2만원 밧게 못됩니다. 5만원은 못되오나 우리의 성심에 용서하서서 우리의 죄를 더 책하지 마십시오.』
하며 가방속에서 2만원 돈을 내노앗다.
미망인은 돈과 내외를 격금내기로 물끄럼이 보고 잇더니 만은 고만 그 자리에 업대서 통곡을 하기 시작햇습니다.
『왜 이러십니까』
『안이오 내 죄를 용서해 주서요. 그 목거리는 5만원 가치가 잇는 것이 안이라 실상은 몃원 자리의 가ㅅ자 따이야올시다. 나의 족으마한 허영심이 그런 말을 엿줍지 안케해서 앗가운 청춘남녀의 금가티 귀한 십년세월을 허비케 한 일을 생각하니 내야 말로 당신 발아래 업대서 사죄해야만 올습니다. 나는 무엇으로 이 죄를 갑하야 올습니까. 인제는 나는 단신네 내외분이 처분만 바랄 수밧게 업습니다.』
어떠한 뜻이 엇던지 그 내외는 역시 미망인에게로 당녀들어서 세 사람이 서로 엉크러저서 통곡을 햇다합니다.

二十八萬風
孤帆生

(처음부터 끗까지 바람에 울니는 電波소리)
男 『이 못생긴 것아 김준의 금강석 반지가 그럿케도 탐이 나드냐! 돈이 머냐! 금강석이 무엇이냐 오직 우리에게는 순결무후한 사랑이 잇슬 뿐이다. 그래서 너는 긔어코 그 도야지 가튼 김중배 놈에게로 싀집을 가야 네 마음이 편하겟느냐』
女 『그럿치만 배는 부르지』
男 『배부르기가 소원이면 이 길로 나하고 다라나자 이수일이도 당당한 대장부이다. 두달이다 못가서 네 배가 올창이 배가티 불느게 해주마』
女 『돈이 잇서야 살지』
男 『걱정마라 위선 네 일흠으로 백만원만 저금을 해주마』
女 『머-백만원』
男 『암 백만원이지 화란녀왕의 목에 걸닌 금강석 진주목도리가 이천원만 주면 살수가 잇다드라. 당장에 네 목에다가 거러주마 금강산을 사가지고 가시성을 쌋코 일만 이천봉 봉이봉이 마다 별장을 지어주마』
女 『시골은 쓸쓸하지』
