기사제목	新春의 새 希望 (New Spring's New Hope)  
기사형태	설문 (Survey)  

New Spring's New Hope  
As we enter the New Spring, I appeal to the students.  

Choe Kyu-dong  

This is not a sermon but a heartfelt plea.  

Everyone! Just because it's a new year, what new words can I share that would be meaningful for elementary school children, vocational students, or even the middle school students in between? Honestly, I had an urgent request, but I hesitated to speak because I know the psychological state and ideological trends of students quite well, and I thought I had nothing to say.  

Although my request is urgent, I continually apologized for the repeated refusals, thinking I had nothing to say to you.  

The subject of self-cultivation seems to serve no purpose; preaching belongs to the instructors of the bygone days, so what is the point of issuing a moralistic sermon in this advanced and new era? We know this well. It is the role of those with decayed and outdated minds to engage in those discussions. You students already have profound fixed prejudices in your minds. When a teacher opens their mouth, you turn your heads suddenly and pout your lips, sneering, "Another boring talk." This has become the modern student.  

Indeed, we speak as if we are merely old and decayed objects. You possess superior talents and more progressive thoughts than we do; we are well aware of it, and thus, we naturally hold hope and expectations. We are filled with respect and admiration for you.  

As educators who have fallen behind the times, what on earth could we possibly have to offer you? If there were anything to say, it would merely echo the stale, worn-out moral platitudes typical of old-school teachers, and we know there is nothing you would even pay mind to, so it follows that there is no fresh plea I could make to you for this New Spring. This is the reason I have repeatedly declined and begged off.  

And yet, because my request is urgent, what I will share now is merely a fraction, a small portion that I have observed, as a sort of earnest plea, similar to an appeal, to you.  

Embrace Between Teachers and Students  
Recently, observing the climate among students, the theory of personality has been greatly promoted. It is a matter of character between teachers and students. In other words, both teachers and students are human beings. However, it seems that teachers do not treat students as individuals but think of them as mere dolls, objects, or slaves, which fills many righteous students with indignation.  

Indeed, both teachers and students possess personal dignity. Yet, I think it is a serious misunderstanding to say that teachers regard students as mere objects or slaves. Can any teacher regard their students simply as dolls or material possessions? Though individuals can be profoundly flawed in various ways, I find it hard to believe that even a teacher, even if they are behind the times, would display such shallow thinking.  

Regardless, since every individual has their personal dignity, it is only natural that we should respect one another’s dignity. However, at least within the school, there needs to be real love between the teacher and student; that is, the teacher should embrace the students, and the students should feel embraced by the teacher with genuine love.


There should be a feeling of being enveloped in a warm embrace, and during those years in the school, students should feel they are being cared for like the teacher’s child and, in turn, should feel a sense of reassurance and comfort as they rely on the teacher. However, in actuality, few teachers seem to possess a shared sense of responsibility for the students, while students are often too stiff, merely trying to escape from the guidelines set by teachers and parents.  

As children grow up, they cease to rely on their mothers the way they did as infants. They move from being bottle-fed to eating solid food, become capable of walking freely instead of just crying, and stop being oblivious to their surroundings and start to understand the world. When the young child is pulled away from their mother's nurturing embrace, they might cry out of hunger, and when children who cannot even properly walk embark out of their family’s embrace into the streets, they face risks from cars and bicycles.  

I cannot help but suspect that we mistakenly perceive students merely as infants who cannot rise to maturity. Educators often carry the superiority and pride of being teachers or parents. However, as acknowledged earlier, there are students who possess talents even superior to ours and hold advanced thoughts.   

Within the school, while in an instructor-student relationship, we must share a bond resembling familial love. It is crucial that, during the four or five years, or even permanently ongoing years, it becomes hard for individuals to cultivate and establish a lasting relationship.  

Lack of Gratefulness  
Schools are not marketplaces.  

Every school charges tuition fees for students, while teachers receive salaries. Therefore, teachers are paid for tuition and in turn sell education as a product. Thus, we have commercialized education, turning schools into businesses. Teachers are seen as an exploiting class, merely a paw of the capitalist system.  

This prevalent thought in today’s ideological winds and panaceas offers no end of frustration, rendering me speechless. So, are teachers merely to forfeit their salaries, and should students cease to pay tuition fees? If schools are managed by capitalists and thus should be abolished, as claimed by various ideologies floating around, it becomes painfully apparent. However, considering whether such an advanced society could even allow for these fully executed ideas is indeed something that even progressive thinkers cannot ignore.  

If we look at the schools now as mere merchants selling educational commodities, are we not painfully mistaken? Realistically, if we understand the efforts of those running these institutions, it becomes undeniably impossible for you to harbor even an ounce of misunderstanding toward them.  

For us, to speak in this manner serves merely as self-justification and self-affirmation, yet I urge you not to misinterpret the true situation. The real underclass, the ones who are truly without assets, are none other than us—us teachers. Truthfully, we are the true underclass, while you are often from the rural or upper-middle-class families.  

This statement of ours may sound trivial, yet if you interpret the world this single narrow way, it is easy to draw conclusions. However, in more public and broader reflections, it could give birth to many goods. The question remains—whether a school is indeed a marketplace or not matters little; yet, if we emphasize the distant future and the vast potential for who gains from this education, and if you genuinely understand this, I confidently believe you will diligently strive for learning.  

Do not prioritize knowledge alone, but self-reflect instead and grasp the Truth of Great Life Extension.  
To this point, if educational training yields flaws, then we can consider that it is due to a heavy emphasis on knowledge.  

Entering school, education becomes obsessed with providing merely mechanical knowledge to prepare for upper-level schools, ignoring any moral or ethical training or personal development. This neglect has lately left students acting purely mechanically, with no understanding or attachment, merely forgoing the issue. When this flaw creates an emptiness and turmoil, the flames of conflict emerge.  

Yet it is evident to anyone that human beings cannot merely be defined by knowledge. It should not be surprising that students have begun just now to comprehend and feel astonished and shaken by this reality. 

But students! You must remember that people are neither gods nor divine beings; indeed, educators are also not gods or divine beings but must recognize their limitations and human nature.  

So, students! What then shall you seek? It should begin with self-reflection. Instead of blaming others, you should blame yourselves and, rather than scolding others, turn the mirror on yourselves.  

When you see others’ shortcomings, reflect on yourselves first. You should strive to embrace the strengths of others rather than expose their faults, as that will not lead to being someone’s slave.  

This attitude must not only apply within the education system but carry over into society as well, whereby the noble spirit of self-sacrifice may blossom. It would be difficult to predict that such a true educational figure could appear among you, yet we deeply desire that those among you emerge who fit this ideal. Thus, it is not far-fetched to expect this of you in the near future.   

Therefore, we place our hopes on you.  

Students! What you gain from your education is not merely a return for the learning you receive; it represents the extension of the Great Life of humanity. The educator extends their life to the next student, and likewise, the recipient of that education then extends it further down the line, continuously passing on life.  

If we all recognize this substantial burden and responsibility, if we realize this Truth, I shall conclude my words here.  

A Plea to New Women: The present age has no other time like this for women; New Spring’s New Hope.  
Sin Albert  

The sun has risen anew. While many people are celebrating, do we really see any transformations around us?  

For us, who must continually renew ourselves, if nothing has changed, ultimately, there is evidence that we are dwindling and degenerating.  

This applies not just to society and men, but also to women around the globe—while even our traditional women seem not to be evolving at all. After the advent of education for women in Korea, we now have ‘New Educated Women’ among us. I wish to address these newly educated women, even though I am just a dilapidated relic from past times, regrettably with less knowledge than you modern educated women.  

Regardless, if you are indeed a new woman, you must surely have some points of merit or something new that surpasses your traditional predecessors. However, if dressing fashionably, putting on makeup, and maintaining appearances become the sole advancements you can offer, I cannot help but question how genuine such changes are.  

You may often hear that women should step into society, while faring poorly in domestic roles. Yet, after a thorough analysis, I find that even the traditional women perform domestic duties better than you, our new women. Thus, as a modern woman, if you merely step forth into the public without fulfilling your domestic roles, how can you defend yourself against criticism that you cannot even compare to your traditional counterparts?  

After all, cooking meals, doing laundry, cleaning houses, taking care of husbands, and raising children fall within the tradiial woman’s duties. As new women, we should at least manage to fulfill a third or a tenth of those duties if we are to carry the name of ‘New Woman.’  

If you want to label yourselves as ‘new’, you must manage to conduct yourselves better than the traditional women who once created comfort for husbands and households.  

There are women today who, despite claiming to be new, display behaviors that demean our status. Numerous males boast about how readily they could have women like us adhered to them, whether through appearance or intellect, while proclaiming that if we continue to decline in status, it will only serve to deepen societal inequalities between genders.  

As women, when we look at ourselves, should we not reflect on our perceived ennoblement versus our current embarrassments?  

One cannot forget that without a solid education, new women must not only express a lighthearted interest in romance letters and romance; we should also aim for a deep understanding of the world around us. Yet, we fail to uphold basic responsibilities at home, such as dressing our children or maintaining cleanliness; no one will commend you for demonstrations of knowledge you portray from your years in school.  

When your children do not even possess clean clothes or are given basic care, I cannot guarantee that your husbands will appreciate your academic achievements, which have taken you three to four years to acquire.  

In an era where happiness stems from marriage, it is indeed a difficult progression for men to have wives, let alone multiple wives. In the past, it was incredibly challenging to marry once more. Traditional women held significant importance, and that should ideally extend to new women.  

Nonetheless, do new women rise above traditional women, consequently presentworthiness commensurate with men? It fulfills every modern woman’s expectations.  

One will hear how women regard one another as these foolish individuals capable of beautiful speeches. Yet, when we witness new women act as lavishly as gentry, what is one then to infer about such individuals?  

While the notion of women working for their worth is championed, have new women effectively assumed traditional work roles? Shouldn't our self-awareness compel us to dedicate ourselves to our families and society?  

If you desire not just to be called 'New Women' but actually embody that title, embrace the challenge of improving your worth on your own efforts. Education should be sought with genuine zeal; dedication to family and society should be undertaken earnestly.  

Nevertheless, we still face the challenge that new women from the past seem insufficient as compared to the traditional women in executing even their normalized roles and relationships within families.  

In short, if we are to exist within the realm of ‘New Women,’ we must first assume the responsibilities of all roles that were undertaken by our predecessors, meeting our needs and working on behalf and for our own growth.  

For Practitioners in the Industry: Listening to Hopes from Experiences  
Hyeon Jeong-joon  

The idea of a new life! A new hope! Yet without prior experience, it is impossible to give birth to new life or sustain hope.  

It may seem trivial to you, but I wish, based on my 56 years of experience in the industry, to offer even a tiny glimpse into some hopes and performative insights to align with potential outcomes in the future.  

Korea's economy has been declining, and there are various reasons for this which hardly need reiterating. Yet it is evident that the inability to support oneself through one's own knowledge base and decisions outweighs these external causes.  

The overwhelming obsession with getting rich quickly, along with hastily starting businesses without any plan creates more complexities. Young people with land inheritance may amount to nothing after having spent some time overseas in big cities, only to hastily abandon their ventures to head back home and pursue farming duties—this is a danger we see among many.  

The young person will have parents cheering them on while they pull out resources accumulated painstakingly to support their desires to start anew without any real wisdom. They might buy expensive racehorses or farm animals to boost their apparent status, only to fail miserably, as they lack even the most basic understanding of the farm, leading to losses after just a few years in the industry.  

At last, their disillusionment is brought forth. "Agriculture fails; can I truly make a living this way?” they complain, then hastily turn back to the urban life they left behind, where they had experienced superficial fortune.  

Then another letter arrives, expert managers advise easy avenues towards farming success contradictorily popularized by industry pamphlets. Imagine jets flying to the new horizons: they trust in such false hope. 

This cycle continues, encouraging individuals to step away from viable plans.  

Opening a business, it is as if structured variants and emerging plans simply disappear, merely replacing those notions with a new veneer. All too often, even savvy entrepreneurs omit necessary foundations.  

Are not the three, four, or five years of communications with potential suppliers, discerning various merchandise, or educating oneself on market conditions the basis inherent in your next stages? The rich and the capable do not find opportunity via shortcuts; they succeed only when consistent efforts towards their labor yield results.  Therefore, my appeal in working is towards but not against that which seeks abiding success.  

As entrepreneurs, considerations should not phase you, as they illustrate that true success is rarely a chance event. Rather, it involves diligence, sustained focus, and maintaining professional know-how over many years.  

Establish a strong foundation and earn your way up.  Fostering this path will lead you not only to success but also ensure this success is enduring—year after year—for at least a decade.  

New forms of labor can emerge as minerals might flow; they simply require resolution as we establish connections.  

Thus, entrepreneurs should forego the impulsiveness of immediate returns, focusing instead on firm strategies to create robust infrastructures towards eventual triumph.  

Let us embark cautiously, evaluating moves with grace, cause, and opportunity.  

Sisters Uniting at the Meeting  
Yoo Young-jun  

I am somewhat of a middle-ground woman. I extend my hand to old-style women and, in turn, to progressive young women as well. Some may say I behave like a bat, not fitting into either camp.  

Nevertheless, I do not see myself that way at all. In the unique situation of Korea, I feel compelled to act in this manner. I indeed wish to advance alongside other progressive women, yet the majority of traditional women still dream their old dreams, thus representing a vast contingent.  

Hence, I feel the need to awaken their consciousness; for if these women remain uninformed, the shoutings of the progressive women will resonate lightly, unsure of true action.  

My main consideration is requesting traditional women to attend any and every meeting possible. They should gather together to not just listen but also to witness, provided there will be many beneficial outcomes.  

Moreover, the husbands of these traditional women should also understand this necessity and allow their wives to attend. Progressive women should also pursue gatherings with them, extending invitations for their attendance.  

This is, therefore, my New Spring hope.  

To My Mother  
Kim Chun-hwa  

Whether as a mother-in-law or daughter-in-law, you are both merely people and can still consider the place of women. Thus, there exists not a dime’s worth of difference between being a person and fulfilling the responsibilities within the female role.  

While there may be distinctions according to age—between the old and young—there exists no difference in the duties required of an individual as a person and as a woman. Yet many present-day mothers do not seem to understand their obligations. They mistakenly think that once a daughter-in-law enters into their lives, their status should surpass that of mere housewives, leading to arrogance and nagging.  

In household or societal matters, just because one carries a certain responsibility, does not allow them to abandon their duty as a woman—for they neglect the essential accountability.  

As mothers, their responsibilities should grow, working even more diligently while educating their sons and daughters-in-law with knowledge and authoritative parenting.  

Each individual deserves respect, regardless of their roles as daughters-in-law and mothers, with expectations to be maintained.  

In addition to my words, should mothers treat daughters-in-law like mere servants and execute their commands or even abuse them, it often reflects how they themselves were treated during their own time as daughters-in-law. The cycle of this behavior can be vicious and ought to be recognized.  

So, we need to grasp that these dynamics are perpetuated down the line.  

A Call to Tear Down Barriers Between Teachers and Students = A Hope Addressed to Teachers =  
Choe In-seong  

Entering middle school, I find myself increasingly nostalgic for experiences in primary school.  

In classrooms, the warmth felt while sharing laughter with beloved teachers feels like embracing that sense of security, akin to returning to a mother’s nurturing care.  

When we would follow those teachers, eager to forage for food or gather plants together, we felt a companionship free from the barriers of teacher-student relations.  

However, it appears that once we transitioned to secondary schools, our experience dramatically shifted. The separation between teacher and student has indeed widened, creating immense distance. In the classroom, one may find themselves act accordingly, yet upon leaving, camaraderie darkens, and the boundary has only expanded.  

With each passing day, we find those rigid perceptions distort much of what we once knew, becoming almost unbearably constricted. Our sincere interactions feel diminished, consumed by the formalities of detachment.  

In weaming formal education, I plead as a student to break down that barrier where once, we might have known joy in earnest companionship—the warmth of teacher and student resembling family banners celebrated together—if only I could suggest ways to restore that cherished bond, revitalizing genuine affection and relief within that relationship once more.  

As we face the growing anonymity of distance, ensure that the bonds of education reignite not only the flames of learning but also genuine connection, dissolving the artificial barriers that arise in classroom coordination. Urging an embrace, allowing personal relationships to flourish, and fostering open communication persist as essential elements to reconnecting these two intrinsic branches of authentic learning.  

The Patient to the Physician  
Park In-seok  

There is an ancient saying which speaks of physicians’ art as benevolent medicine (仁術). As once argued, if one cannot rise to greatness within life, then one might as well endeavor to become a healing practitioner, thus offering redemption to the unfortunate through compassionate care.  

For a patient, what hopes might one bear when approaching a physician? At the very least, they hope to be cured swiftly from their ailment.  

Doctors, in the view of a sick person, remain more deserving of respect than even a saint. One's doctor’s word carries the potential to soothe pain while providing faith, leaving the patient indebted with hopes of recovery.  

Yet, somehow, alongside their grievances or frustrations during recovery, patients often develop grievances against physicians for their impassive attitudes.  

Firstly, it is all about a lack of kindness. A sick individual may already feel that they bear the weight of the world with both their health and spirit devastated. The physician should show compassion through their discourse as well as respect for the patient; consequently, traversing voids of fear without resorting to derogatory behavior toward nurses or companions simply because someone is paying, and seeing patients as mere transactions is a bitter pill to swallow for anyone enduring illness.  

And medicines are prescribed with exorbitant prices for those who cannot afford them; doctors must not decree that care charges for patients be overwhelming under the pretense of treating them. It’s folly to treat Korean society like Germany or America, for they only present failure.  

Should that indeed occur—it may well cut their own credibility and hidden fortunes—one becomes fully aware of the distress impacting Tories’ (托元).  

Sick patients often feel hopeless; a patient stricken with poverty bears the most regretful badge of all. If a physician becomes more transactional, manipulating desperate patients for increasing profits, one may doubt the essence behind their oath.  

If the physician were to limit their gain to appropriate percentages from actual costs, with clear integrity evident in their effort—perhaps such ideals may be attainable by some doctors, exhibiting approaches that carefully examine each patient's situation humanely.  

And in today’s practice, we are all aware of the expectations held toward doctors. In truth, concerns are not based upon whether they possess advanced qualifications; it is the expectation that they knew how to treat patients expertly.  

Ultimately, we clearly define the essence of healing through connection rather than elevation of status.  

Summary  

In the article "New Spring's New Hope," Choe Kyu-dong implores students to embrace a progressive relationship with their teachers, rather than the historic distant and transactional model of education. He emphasizes mutual respect and understanding and warns against the dangers of viewing education solely as a commoditized transaction. Through reiterating communal bonds within education, he paints a vision of a future where both students and teachers nurture each other, ultimately fostering an enriching atmosphere. The exploration of these perspectives reflects the era's social discourse surrounding education and the hope for unity amid the oppressive colonial framework. Historians may find interesting contrasts with traditional American or European educational discourses in terms of the emphasis on mutual respect and emotional connection.

新春의 새 希望
新春에 際하야 學生諸君에게 訴하노라

崔奎東

訓話가 아니외다 懇告외다
여러분! 이번이 새해라고 무슨 새로운 말로 소학생도 그만두고 전문 이상의 학생도 그만두고 오즉 그 중간에 잇는 중학생 제군에게 들려 줄 말슴이 업겟느냐고 실은 긴요한 부탁이엇슴니다.
부탁은 긴요한 부탁이나 나는 제군의 심리와 사상경향을 잘 알고 잇기 때문에 할말이 업다고 그만두자고 몃 번이나 謝絶하엿슴니다.
修身과목은 소용업는 것. 훈화는 녜전 서당의 훈장이나 하는 것이지 이와 가티 진보한 시대에 이와 가티 새로운 시대에 수신서나 훈화가 다 무엇이냐. 그만 것은 우리도 알고 잇다. 그것은 썩고 묵은 頭腦의 소유자가 할 일이요 할말이라고 그러케 제군의 머리에는 벌서 確乎不拔의 선입견이 들어 잇슴니다. 선생이 입을 열으면 문득 머리를 돌리고 입을 삐죽이며 「또 썩은 소리」하고 빈정거리는 것이 近日의 학생이외다.
그럿슴니다. 우리는 말하자면 썩은 물건이요 묵은 물건이외다. 제군은 우리보다 오히려 優越한 才操가 잇고 우리보다 오히려 진보한 사상을 가진이가 만흔 줄을 우리로서도＜16＞ 잘 알고 잇는 동시에 따라서 희망을 두고 기대를 하는 바가 만흠을 아울러 또는 敬愛之感服之하는 바외다.
시대에 뒤떠러진 우리 소위 교육자로서 진보사상의 소유자인 제군에게 무슨 훈화가 잇겟슴닛가. 잇다면 서당 훈장식의 陳腐한 倫理道德說가티만 해석되는 그것이 제군의 귀 한 갓에나 들어갈 이치가 업고 제군의 가슴 한편에라도 울리는 바가 업슬 것을 아는 나로서 제군에게 무슨 새삼스러운 新春의 부탁이 잇겟슴닛가. 그것이 謝絶 또 謝絶한 이유임니다.
그터나 긔어코 한 말슴을 부탁하여 달나고 記者의 부탁이 정말 至再至三임으로 하다가 못하야 한 말슴을 드리게 된 것입니다.
그러나 훈장식 훈화는 될 수 잇는대로 피하고서 내가 보고 늣기고 한 一端 극히 一端인 (전체는 일일이 말할 수가 업스닛가) 몃 부분을 들어 제군에게 懇告 비슷 哀訴 비슷한 이것을 드리랴 하는 것입니다.

師生間의 互相抱擁
근일에 학생의 기풍을 보면 인격론이 非常히 고조되어 잇슴니다. 선생과 생도도 인격 대 인격이다. 즉 선생도 사람이요 생도도 사람이다. 그런데 선생은 생도를 인격적으로 대하지 아니하고 한 인형이나 물품이나 노예가티 안다고 심히 분개하는 志士적 학생이 만흔 모양입니다.

병든 사람에게는 의사야말로 구주(救主)이상으로 존경하고 십고 한우님 이상으로 처다뵈는 것임니다. 의사의 말한마듸가 병자에게는 얼마만한 위안을 주고 얼마만한 신뢰를 니르키는지 아십닛가. 돈을 주고 병을 곳치는 것이지만 병 나은 그 날에는 그야말로 재생(再生)의 은혜를 못 니저하는 것이 보통의 인정일 것임니다.
그런데 병자로서 의원을 차저 갈 때나 병자로서 병원에 입원하여 잇슬때나 혹은 병을 치료하고 퇴원한 후까지도 그와 가티 구주이상 천사이상으로 울어러 보고 밋고하는 의사에게 불평을 품게 하는 것이 무슨 까닭임닛가.
첫재로 불친절 그것임니다. 병든 자가 불행한 몸으로 그러지 안이해도 세상을 비관하는 일이 만흔 것인데 의사의 그 특명스럽고 함부로 하는 말ㅅ세나하며 조수 간호부에게 대한 비루한 태도나 하며 돈 잇고 업는 정도를 보아 사람대우하는 심리의며 그저 돈만 글거드리면 그만이라 병이 늣게 낫고 일즉 낫는 것은 나의 상관이 안이다 하고 아모런 성이하나 업시 병인을 취급하는 고얄미운 심정은 병든 가슴에 칼을 차게 하고 압흔 몸에 울화를 나게 하는 일이 한 두 가지가 안임니다. 그리고 병이 잇서도 돈이 업서서 곳칠 수 업는 것이 우리네의 살림형편인데 웬 턱에도 닷지안는 진찰료, 치료비, 약갑을 막 떼여먹기로듭닛가. 조선을 독일이나 미국으로 알어서는 큰 실패임니다. 만일 그럿타면 간판을 얼는 떼여 질머지고 백림이나 뉴욕을 향하시는 것이 돈버리도 되고 명예유지도 될 것이 안임닛가.
병든 자는 불행한 자외다. 병든 중에도 가난한 병자는 불행중 불행한 자외다. 자긔의 자본이나 성력을 드려 자선사업은 못할 망정 불행자의 약점을 긔회 삼어 부정한 리익을 도모하고 사복을 채움은 인술을 행하는 의원 량심으로 과연 허락할 바가 안인가 함니다. 실비를 제한 상당함 범위의 리익 자긔의 로력을 판 대상이야 누가 죄악이라 하겟슴닛가만은 10배, 20배의 폭리만은 가난한 병자에게 부담식히지 안혀도 조흘 것 갓슴니다. 그리고 병자에게 성의를 쓰고 친절히 구는 것만은 실비 들 일도 아니요 로력 허비하는 것도 안일 것 가튼 그런 미덕을 가질 수 잇는 의사가 되어 볼 수는 업슬까요. 최소한도로 바란다면 그것쯤은 당신들도 실행하여 과히 해로운 일은 안인가 함니다.
그리고 의사게에는 엇지 그리 박사가 만슴닛가. 박사가 만허서 걱정될 것은 업는 일임니다만은 박사 안인 이까지 모다 박사행세를 하게 되니 정말 박사에게 좀 「메이와구」가 될 것 갓소이다. 그러나 그거야 우리네 환자에게는 병만 잘 곳처 주면 박사이지 별수 잇슴닛가. 직접 관게가 그러케 크지 안이하니 그것은 그만큼 말슴해 둡니다.＜31＞
