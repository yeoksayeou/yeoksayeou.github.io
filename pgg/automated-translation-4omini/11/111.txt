**Summary**  

This article reports on the establishment of several branch offices of the magazine, detailing their locations, personnel, and area of coverage. It indicates a potential expansion of the magazine's reach into various regions, which may reflect a strategic attempt to increase influence and communicate more broadly during a time of significant political and social change under Japanese rule in Korea. Historians might find it interesting to scrutinize how media networks were forming in the colonial period, as it sheds light on the channels of communication and dissemination of information during that era.
