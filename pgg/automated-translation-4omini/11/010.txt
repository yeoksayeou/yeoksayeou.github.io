발행일	1928년 02월 01일  
기사제목	기다림(민요) (Waiting (Folk Song))  
필자	申東植(作곡), 鄭箕煥(作謠)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Waiting  
						Shin Dong-sik (Composer), Jeong Gi-hwan (Lyricist)  
As I wait, the footsteps move slowly,  
Even when the moon rises and the door opens, you do not come.  
When the sun rises, will you saunter back,  
After waiting through all of this?  

In the minari garden,  
The sound of the dog's bark,  
Even the bark has gone hoarse.  
Is this night spent waiting here  
To hear that sound?  
The mulberry leaves  
Are falling with the dew,  
The morning dew is falling. <1>  

**Summary**  

This poem conveys a deep sense of longing and anticipation, capturing the emotional weight of waiting for someone who is absent. The imagery of nature, such as the rising moon and morning dew, connects the speaker's feelings to the passage of time, evoking nostalgia and yearning. From the perspective of Korean colonial history, this poem reflects a common theme of waiting in both personal and collective contexts, highlighting the emotional struggles during a time of uncertainty and loss under colonial rule.

**Named Entities**  

개고리		gaegori	Dog's bark  
미나리광		minari-gwang	Minari garden  

**Glossary**  

기다리자니	기다리다	To wait, in an older form; expresses the act of waiting.  
발길			발길	Footsteps; refers to the movement of someone expected.  
긔여들텐가		여들다	To come along; an older expression indicating arrival.  
미나리광		미나리광	Minari garden; a garden where water celery grows, symbolizing freshness.  
뽕밧헤는		뽕밭		Mulberry leaves; signifies nature and the connection to the speaker's emotions.  
이슬이 나리네	이슬이 나리다	Dew falls; used to depict morning and the passage of time.
