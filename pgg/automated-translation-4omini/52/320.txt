기자선생님	기자 (Reporter)  
기사형태	사고·편집후기 (Editorial Notes)  

Dear reporter! How immersed you must be in your work on this hot day. I wish I could send you a drink of our fine local rice wine, but I'm unable to do so. Isn't it just a short journey nowadays with just a thousand miles? (Miryang Kim Soon-yam)

Please, do not speak like that. In this cold time of the negotiation incident in Gyeongseong (Seoul), the crime of bribery is such that one might lose their head on the chopping block from Gyeongseong. I am truly moved by your intention, but it's a situation in which I cannot afford to oblige. (Reporter)

Reporter! I come in for the first time. I did not expect the 55th issue to be this good. Henceforth, I vow to become a permanent reader and will gather some companions from our village to create a readers' club for the 55th issue and promote reading together, so please support us generously. (Haeun Kim In-yeong) <60>  

**Summary**  

This column features letters from various readers expressing their thoughts and support for the magazine, with a mix of gratitude and calls for community engagement. One reader, reflecting on the local climate of dishonesty involving bribery, shows a desire for truth and accountability, while another enthusiastically commits to forming a readers' club. The article highlights the connection between the magazine and its readers amid the tense atmosphere of colonial Korea, where journalism served both as a platform for community discourse and a means of challenging the status quo. Historians might find the readers' commitment to self-organization and promotion of local culture particularly interesting against the backdrop of Japanese colonial rule.

쉬- 그런 말슴 마십시오. 京城府의 談合사건으로 식그러운 이때 賄賂罪로 개벽사로부터 머리를 잘리올 일을 식히십니까. 順岩씨의 뜻에 감격불이 하오만 만부득이 헐 수 업는 형편이외다. 쌉쌉(기자)
