기사제목	애기陵을지나서 (Passing Through the Princess's Tomb)  
필자	尹風臺  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Through the woods, I wandered, confused and lost,  

As I dropped the leaves, I wondered where to go next.  

Stopping in my tracks, I found the Princess's Tomb.  

  

In the place where my heart is heavy, how could I cross the bridge?  

When I recall the old stories,  

I see the children crying more and more,  

As the setting sun gently touches the grass.  

一九三二, 六, 二五,(in a quiet street)＜12＞  

**Summary**  

The poem reflects on the emotions of nostalgia and sorrow as the speaker traverses through a wooded area, ultimately reaching the Princess's Tomb. The imagery evokes a moment of introspection, highlighting memories of childhood and the indelible marks of the past. It resonates deeply with the psychological strain of colonial rule, illustrating how historical places evoke both personal and collective memories that are shaped by a shared cultural heritage, making it a poignant reflection on the identity crisis faced during Korea's colonial era.
