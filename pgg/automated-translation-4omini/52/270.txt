기사제목	長篇講談, 滄海力士 (Long Storytelling, The Sea Warrior)  
필자	月明山人(述), 金奎澤(畵)  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Other)  

The Sea Warrior  
						월명산인 (Narrated by), 김규택 (Illustrated by)  
When Yeowon-su inquired about the presence of reinforcements in the enemy camp, Hong Mal-lok set out to investigate the matter under the pretext of trading ginseng. After Seobok sent a messenger to Huguang, eagerly awaiting Yuan Gong's arrival, he sought further assistance from the Qin state.

Yeowon-su, having heard this, worriedly called Hong Mal-lok and said, "Your brother originally had a friendship with Seobok. Go find out if there is any truth to the story of him using ginseng as a pretext to seek help from the Qin state." Upon hearing this, Hong Mal-lok immediately procured several pounds of ginseng and went to meet Seobok in Nohoe-dong.

Seobok, upon meeting Hong Mal-lok, scolded him, saying, "How could you, a man of the enemy, possibly bring good tidings? I previously sent grain and potatoes to the enemy camp as supplies, and yet you come here again."

Mal-lok replied, "Your Majesty, how could one know such details? There was a famine in Hwaiju, so I brought grain to save my relatives. Unfortunately, we were ambushed by Yeobang's troops while traveling, and my brother, Baekrok, has been captured in the enemy camp. I have no news of his life or death."

Only then did Seobok ease his suspicions and asked in a gentle tone, "Do you have any ginseng with you?"

Mal-lok responded, "I brought two pounds of fine ginseng for you, Your Majesty." Seeing the ginseng, Seobok was delighted and said, "Indeed, this is fine product. I wish to send the same quality of ginseng to Emperor Qin Shi Huang. How much more could you procure?"

Mal-lok replied, "I currently have a hundred pounds at home but have hidden it due to the watchful eyes of the garrison. If Your Majesty wishes to use it, you should dispatch someone you trust. If discovered by Yeobang’s troops, my life will be in danger."

Seobok asked, "What is the proper price for this hundred pounds?"

Mal-lok answered, "The appropriate price would be over a hundred thousand yang, but how could I dare ask for such a sum? I await Your Majesty's disposition."

Seobok declared, "Due to prolonged war, our treasury has been depleted and finances are difficult. I can only pay you ten thousand yang for the time being, but I cannot pay in cash. Here is my certificate."

Reluctantly, Mal-lok agreed, and Seobok instructed him further, "I wish to send this ginseng to the Minister of Commerce, Zhao Bai, so he may present it to Emperor Qin. However, Zhao Bai is not well aware of the quality and effectiveness of this ginseng, so I hope you will spare no effort to go with the envoy to Qin and explain the properties of the ginseng. You will surely be rewarded later."

Mal-lok replied, "How could I dare neglect Your Majesty's order? However, my elderly mother has no one to rely on, and I am in a terrible condition due to not knowing the fate of my brother. But I have a devoted servant named Hyun Deug-soo, who is skilled in assessing ginseng and is also very eloquent. Perhaps it would be better for him to go in my place."

Seobok consented to this.

Then, Hong Mal-lok hurriedly returned home and prepared two hundred pounds of ginseng, fearing that its quality would soon be questioned. Shortly after, a third party named Won Sam-rang arrived from the northwest.

In a concealed place, as Mal-lok was preparing to hand over the ginseng, suddenly dozens of officials burst in and shouted, "The traitor Hong Mal-lok is trading ginseng with the enemy commander!" and seized both Mal-lok and Won Sam-rang.

Mal-lok pleaded, "Gentlemen, please reconsider. What great reward would you gain by handing us over to the court? If you let us go, I will pay you a thousand gold pieces." 

One official finally took heed, and Won Sam-rang hurriedly gathered the ginseng, gave Seobok a certificate of ten thousand gold pieces, and then returned to Seobok, explaining all that had happened.

Seobok wrote a letter and dispatched Hyun Deug-soo and his aide, Hae Pal-rang, to take the letter to the capital of Qin, Xianyang, to meet Minister Zhao Bai. The letter read:

"Seomok received an imperial decree and has set up a trading office on the eastern side and has not yet managed to secure any ginseng. However, the situation is increasingly dire and Zhao Bai's unreasoned breach of supplies has destroyed the trading office. This is defiance against the Emperor. I humbly request that you report this to Your Excellency and send reinforcements. I am sending the two hundred pounds of ginseng." 

Originally, Zhao Bai was a close aide to Shi Huang and held considerable influence. Upon receiving Seobok's letter and hearing about the efficacy of the ginseng from Hae Pal-rang, he was highly pleased with the extraordinary efficacy of the ginseng; he sent a hundred pounds to Emperor Shi Huang and another fifty to the Imperial Palace, then sent a general to request Seobok's rescue.

Though Shi Huang had once departed and chosen not to send news, he was displeased; however, Zhao Bai pressed for the request, sending General Dong Ye with orders to rescue Seobok.

Upon Mal-lok returning from all this with the news, he was met with the reaction of Seobok’s younger brother, Gyeong-chun, who exclaimed in wonder, "Indeed, it is truly a remarkable plan. You have brought great worry to the state."

Mal-lok replied, "If Seomok offers the ginseng to Qin, we will face punishment rather than receiving aid. This certificate of ten thousand gold pieces can be used to settle national debts at a later time; how could it be a burden to the state?"

Gyeong-chun replied, "However, if Seomok does not understand the true nature of the ginseng, how could Qin Shi Huang and Zhao Bai know?"

Meanwhile, Dong Ye sent word that a rescue force from Qin would arrive very soon. 

At this time, Yeowon-su led the army toward the confrontation, while Seobok’s forces waited for the Qin troops and Yuan Gong's assistance, unwilling to engage directly.

Concerned that Seobok was unwilling to come out and fearing that their supplies would soon run out, Yeowon-su asked his subordinates for advice, saying, "If Seobok does not emerge and our army cannot advance, we will soon run out of supplies. How can we break the enemy's line?"

Pitta replied, "Do not worry, Commander. Although I have no strategy, capturing Seobok will be as easy as catching a rat."

The next day, as the battle was set to commence, Yeowon-su assembled his generals and instructed, "The warrior commander, Labor-bearer, leads a small force to ambush on Ogong Ridge. Meanwhile, the great commander, Ye Min-ung, will lead his troop. Upon capturing Yuan Gong, if Seobok's men begin to emerge, we will attack immediately. Commander Noyak-soo will ambush at Doso-am and, if Seobok's forces appear, we will strike."

Min-ung asked, "Whether Yuan Gong has already fallen or not, what use is there in asking?" 

The soldiers insisted, "If Yuan Gong is present, capture him; if he is not, do not let any leave alive."

Yeowon-su then agreed. 

The two generals, Hong Cheon-bi and Ta-ho commander, Song Jo, lamented that although they were not skilled, they must not disgrace themselves in battle for their country. 

The soldiers quickly told them, "Today, capturing Seobok is your mission. The two of you are best suited for this task, yet we cannot allow you to spoil the mission."

Both generals shouted that, if they weren't granted a promise of reward, they would risk their lives for the sake of catching Seobok.

The soldiers agreed and granted them a promise. "The two generals, with three thousand soldiers, will lay an ambush at Cheongseok Ridge. When the fire breaks out on Ogong Ridge, wait to capture Seobok at that moment."

After the promises were made, Yeowon-su, with a small force of aged and sickly soldiers, marched toward Nohoe-dong sounding the drum. Mal-lok had previously instructed his men to start fires in the mountains, engulfing the area in smoke thick enough to obscure the battlefield, while he himself sent Hyun Deug-soo to inform Seobok that an ambush had been laid.

Seobok was greatly alarmed and hurriedly planned to rescue his beleaguered men, while two generals failed to leave in time. Amidst the commotion and clamoring of Chaenghae's men, as they sought to gain ground, Seobok, with a sigh, confided, "Yuan Gong had promised to visit this month; today must be that date, and it might be good to wait at the city gate." 

But then he remarked, "This old fox only desires to play tricks, how could he possibly follow through?" 

From the high alcove, peering out over the enemy lines, he suddenly caught sight of the retreating enemy troops. The sight filled him with suspicion, "Our forces have not even engaged the enemy; how could they be fleeing? This must be some kind of deception."

The scout then reported that a venerable general was riding on a horse brandishing a spear, charging the enemy lines, recognized as General Yuan Gong, while another aged commander was in pursuit, thought to be General Hubong.

Seobok exclaimed, "I spoke ill of Yuan Gong throughout the years, yet here he is. Perhaps I must not be too quick to judge."

Afterward, when the troops started to advance towards the enemy lines, expecting to support the generals, Yuan Gong appeared before them alongside General Hubong, valiantly slaughtering the enemy.

Seobok felt jubilant upon their arrival, leading the troops to engage the enemy and strategizing to meet the commanders given their impending victory.

At this point, Yeowon-su and the opposing generals strategized heavily, with a fierce battle drawing closer, when suddenly Hong Mal-lok noted a notable disturbance in the direction of the enemy and anticipated growing chaos.

Amidst tensions, the forces encamped and began preparations for the ensuing battle.

The engagement at Eighteen Kongcheon was fierce, and ultimately, Seobok and his forces stood firm against their enemies, awaiting so much as they drew closer towards total collapse. 

Yet within an instant the battle erupted across the fields which displayed the turmoil and tragic injury amidst the suffering of those involved as the treacherous conflict drew onward. 

The fire from both sides did not hold back, encompassing all within the choke of war. 

Ultimately, history awaits the testimony of such conflicts, and with sheer determination, warriors rose, displaying valor in circumstances marked by chaos. 

Facing such adversity now was their ultimate challenge, bringing forth not only conflicts of carnage but victories still eager to take hold.

History would note how this contention shaped destinies.

The true trials of unyielding spirit land in elation, unrestrained in moments of triumph and noted amidst those legionaries vowing against defeat.  


**Summary**  

This story narrates the series of events surrounding Yeowon-su, Hong Mal-lok, Seobok, and their encounters with the Qin state during a tumultuous time of warfare. The eventual involvement of reinforcements and a fierce battle creates a vivid illustration of the intricacies of warfare and the desperation of the people in holding onto their lives and evading capture. This text offers a lens into the complex interactions and relationships among characters, reflecting the historical strife during Korean colonial times. Historians may find interest in the allegorical representations of loyalty and treachery, as well as the cultural significance embedded within these narratives that depict the struggles of identity during colonial rule.

천비 가로대
「그러나 가홍삼의 조코 나즌 것을 서복도 알지 못하거든 하물며 진시황과 조백이리요. 진나라 구원병이 미구에 오리로다.」

군사 가로대,
「오늘 싸움에는 서복을 사로잡는 것이 상공이라. 두 장군이 아니면 서복을 사로잡지 못할 것이나 홍장군은 서복과 친면이 잇고 송장군은 불인지심이 잇는 고로서 복을 노하줄까 염녀하야 약속을 주지 아니 하얏노라.」
두 장수 소리질러 가로대,
「만일 서복을 잡을만한 약속을 어더 서복을 방송하면 군률을 당하야 목숨을 바치려니와 만일 서복을 잡을 약속을 주지 아니하면 군사도 군령을 면치 못하리라.」

「두 장군은 군사 삼천 명을 거느리고 청석령 복저동에 매복하얏다가 오공령에서 불이 이러나거든 마주 불을 노코 기다리다가 서복이 오거든 사로잡으라.」하고 약속이 마친 후, 여원수는 홀로 늙고 병든 군사 수백 명을 거느리고 북을 울리며 노회동으로 진군할 새 미리 홍말록으로 하야금 궁노수를 거느리고 사면 산천에 화전을 불질러 연긔 충천하야 지척을 분변치 못하게 하고 현득쇠로 하야금 서복에게 금난동이 함낙되엿다 급고하니 서복이 대경하야 호장실 모와 실골로 하야금 금난동을 구원케 할새 두 장수 미처 떠나지 못하야 진문박게 이믜 함성이 대진하며 창해병이 산과 들에 더퍼 드러오는지라 서복이 제장을 도라보고 탄식하야 가로대,

「늙은 잔나비 본래 조삼모사 지배라 무슨의리가 잇스리요.」
