기사제목	啓蒙運動是非論 (On the Pros and Cons of the Enlightenment Movement)  
필자	漢陽學人 (Hanyang Scholar)  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

Is there a need for the enlightenment movement in Joseon? If posed with such a question, I would unhesitatingly answer that it is indeed necessary. 

However, if I were to be asked—does the enlightenment movement being carried out currently by the Dong-A Ilbo (East Asia Daily) really benefit Joseon? Regrettably, I must say that it seems more harmful than beneficial, leaving me with a rather unpleasant feeling as I shake my head in disagreement. 

In general terms, one might argue that an enlightenment movement is needed in Joseon, but why then is the Dong-A Ilbo's movement considered unnecessary? Clarifying this is the crucial objective of this article.

Therefore, it would be essential to specifically understand what the Dong-A Ilbo's so-called "Brna Rod" movement entails. So what exactly is the Brna Rod movement that involves students?

Firstly, they issue a mobilization order stating that “All students are to be mobilized!” alongside the slogan “Let us provide literacy to the 13 million illiterate!” prominently displayed.

What plans, content, and steps do they have in mind to realize this slogan? 

First, looking at what could be considered a concrete action guideline, the so-called “Five Principles of Practical Action” shows us the following:

1. Collaborate with comrades in the provinces to ensure the movement is solid.

2. No mixture of anything other than literacy and numeracy in this movement. 

3. Activities should only commence after securing the approval of local branches from governmental authorities.

4. As this movement serves the compatriots, the spirit behind it is the foundation of the popular spirit, thus special attention should be paid to conduct.

5. Pay attention to health.

These points summarize the specifics of the enlightenment movement they are conducting. By carefully examining and critiquing their Brna Rod movement, we can uncover its true identity.

First, what do they mean by so-called collaboration with local comrades? In this regard, their “mobilization order” states—this Brna Rod movement is centered on students, but to thoroughly disseminate this movement, the cooperation of general members is welcome. Especially the cooperation of interested parties in local religious organizations, self-cultivation groups, and cultural organizations! Furthermore, the editorial of the Dong-A Ilbo expresses concerns about governmental hypersensitivity towards the Brna Rod movement while seeking assistance. 

Here it becomes evident that their so-called local comrades consist of various religious organizations, self-cultivation associations, cultural groups, and the “authorities.” How truly devoted are they to working for the Joseon populace? Dong-A Ilbo, one of the most openly reactionary cultural entities in Joseon, claims to hold hands with their most cherished comrades while conducting the enlightenment movement in Joseon. Can the Joseon populace realistically expect any benefits from such a movement? — Oh, Joseon people! We must duly pay attention to how systematically they intend to operate their reactionary roles even in this seemingly ordinary movement!

But let me go back to understanding their claims in more detail! 

Secondly, they insist—do not mix anything other than literacy and numeracy into this movement! Within this main point, the specific nature of their movement becomes clear. It seems they treat this point as the most important item. Why? Because they frequently echo similar sentiments throughout their discussions, which are elaborated upon in their editorials—ultimately, the main message to the members of the Brna Rod team is to realize that this task should simultaneously serve the public’s literacy while also being a practical training exercise for the members themselves. That is to say, they persist in advocating that any deviation from “literacy and numeracy” should be strictly avoided! 

Moreover, Ms. Song Jin-u has also conveyed similar advice on this matter in discussing their so-called “serious mobilization ceremony”—to face the task with a humble and gracious attitude! What a surprise it is to see the echoes of the speeches made by the principal of a normal school, Mr. Song, in their guidance! 

We must be cautious and attentive regarding this. We need to critically discern the reactions hidden within this central issue of their enlightenment movement. 

Initially, I mentioned that while enlightenment movement in Joseon is generally necessary, that of the Dong-A Ilbo is indeed unnecessary; it is time to ponder this relationship deeply. 

When we think about the enlightenment movement, can we view it purely as an economic and political endeavor or just an enlightenment movement for its own sake? Any individual concerned for the public cannot treat such a meaningless enlightenment movement as valid! It must be closely linked to concrete political significance. Despite this, the Dong-A Ilbo's approach is contrary to this; do they not state? Do not mix anything other than literacy and numeracy in this movement! Additionally, you must absolutely refrain from engaging in any behavior that deviates from literacy and numeracy! In other words, they consistently argue that explaining the need for enlightenment movements to the populace from an economic and political perspective is unnecessary or should not be done at all. They maintain that merely teaching “literacy and numeracy” will ensure that Joseon people become prosperous and that the “prosperity of rural areas can be guaranteed!” (as stated in a speech by Yun Chi-ho). 

In their minds, those in the enlightenment movement assume that simply imparting knowledge of literacy and numeracy, as exemplified by the German populace who, despite being wealthy in knowledge, still suffer as the poorest of the poor, is never considered. 

Such ineffective enlightenment movement is not necessary in Joseon; as I stated initially, it is not only pointless but also potentially harmful. 

Thirdly, what does their guideline regarding local media branches imply? It simply indicates that their aim is to promote their own media interests and gain commercial benefits while simultaneously enhancing their political power and influence amid this so-called Brna Rod movement. To put it plainly, the focus of their Brna Rod movement is not on enlightenment but rather on securing these objectives.

Concerning their fourth guideline, especially caution regarding conduct, it must again be understood in the context of the second guideline. When they say the spirit is foundational for the populace, we should not simply interpret this as a warning to avoid moral laxity; rather, it subtly suggests that adhering to conduct should also be viewed through the lens of political and ideological implications, pointing towards their reactionary leadership's significant objectives.

In summary, we must critically understand and evaluate this Brna Rod movement from the Dong-A Ilbo. Specifically, it represents the most hypocritical type of reactionary enlightenment movement.

Meanwhile, their esteemed comrades, such as Yun Chi-ho (noted to be a key religious leader!) and Hyun Sang-yun, are energetically applauding and supporting it as if it were a planned endeavor. They hide behind bourgeois phrases such as “only through enlightenment can rural areas prosper” or “the great power of knowledge for sustainable living,” while they are just expressing beautiful praises. This reveals the significant collusion among their “comrades” in executing this reactionary movement.

Ultimately, to finalize, the Dong-A Ilbo's Brna Rod movement conceals its reactionary nature behind beautiful phrases, with statements like, “More than 2,071 young brothers and sisters have set forth on this sacred task, braving the fervor and fatigue!” This illustrates the potential manipulation of the subconscious mind of the general, unassuming student public towards their own reactionary crusade. As part of their first significant offense, the mobilization of these idealistic corps presents a hidden danger, while their largest sin lies in conducting this most reactionary enlightenment movement while portraying it as just and virtuous to the unaware populace, obliterating their concerns and attention towards reality and life while distorting the rightful direction of the popular movement. 

In this context, we should approach and critique this Brna Rod movement with great caution and vigilance.  

**Summary**

This article critiques the enlightenment movement led by the Dong-A Ilbo in the early 1930s, arguing that, while a legitimate enlightenment movement is necessary for Joseon, the Dong-A Ilbo's approach is fundamentally flawed and harmful. The author expresses concern that the movement focuses solely on literacy and numeracy without addressing the political and economic context necessary for genuine empowerment of the populace. Historians might find it interesting how this reflects the broader dynamics of colonial rule in Korea, where cultural movements often intersected with national identity and resistance against oppressive policies.

**Glossary**

게몽운동	enlightenment movement	An effort to increase literacy and education among the populace, often in a political context.  
글모르는이	illiterate	Refers to those who cannot read or write.  
총동원령	mobilization order	A directive aimed at gathering people for a specific cause or movement.  
정신	spirit	The underlying essence or ideological basis of a movement.  
품행	conduct	Morality or behavior expected in the context of the movement.

그럼에도 불구하고 그들의 가장 경애하는 동지들 윤치호(尹致昊)씨 (긔독교회의 거두임에주목하라!)밋현상윤(玄相允)씨등은 「작정이나 한듯이」동지적 박수와 갈채를 보내고잇다. 그들은 다가티「게몽후라야 농촌은번영」한다든가「생활을위한지식의큰힘」이라든가의 부르조아적 미문구(美文句)두에숨어서 아름다운찬성을표하고 잇는 것이다. 여긔에 그들의 동지(?)적결탁아래서 대대적으로 ×동운동을 리행하는사실이 드러나잇는 것이다.
여긔서 맨나종으로 최후적 결론을 말하기로하자!
이번 동아일보의 브나로드운동은 그것의 반동적내막을 가즌 미구(美句)안에 감추면서 즉 「二千七十一名의 젋은 형제자매는 염렬(炎熱)과 피로(疲勞)를무릅쓰고 이 성(聖)된 사엄을 향하야 경건한 길을 나선것이다!」라는 十字軍에대한 로-마법왕(法王)의 설교적문구(文句)를 버러논는데서 일반무의식학생대중을 그릇된방향에 그들의 ×동적십자군十字軍을 동원식힌데 그들의 첫재 ×동적죄악이 잠재되여잇고 둘재로 가장 ×동적게몽운동을 민중에게 리행하면서 그것이 가장 정당한것인것가티 일반무의식한대중에게 선전하면 선전하는데서 그들의 현실과 생활에대한 관심과 주의를 말살(抹殺) 식힌데 라서 전면적으로 민중운동의 정당한방향을 그르친데 그들의 최대의 죄악이 숨어잇는 것이다. 이런의미에서 이번 브나로드운동을 가준 경게와 주목밋헤서 생각하며 비판해야할 것이다.＜16＞
