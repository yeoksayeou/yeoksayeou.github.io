Readers' Column

The meaning of this column is to promote friendship between readers and between readers and the editors. Please use this space. The medium is limited to postcards.

How are you, dear teachers? These days, the state of world affairs is beyond words. Thank you very much. I apologize for my rude words, but please try to ensure that the English in the articles is understandable for those of us who are not familiar with it. We just look forward to the strokes of your pens. (Munam Ch'oe Ch'ong-nong)

In cases where foreign words appear, please provide their meanings in parentheses below.

Dear, would you please open the door to the discussion room soon? I want to say something directly. Are only weak teachers gathered at Kaebyŏksa? Isn't it shameless for some teacher in the dalmadang to say that he couldn't publish some articles because of his illness? Don't do that, or you will be punished. (Samsuseng)

Thank you for your words, but do you think it's good for children? I don't want to get involved in anything troublesome. If I can do that, I will come out and talk. (Byŏnggisa)

Mr. Kim, I have a question. If the magazine Byeolgeonkŏn is priced at 10 jeon, while it was 5 jeon it had 40 pages, and now at 10 jeon it has 60 pages, has 20 pages gone to heaven? Besides, isn't Byeolgeonkŏn a magazine only for the bourgeois? As a rural worker, I wonder how it appears easy to us. Mr. Teacher, you have mistaken the purpose of Byeolgeonkŏn. Please create a magazine that serves the rural workers and factory laborers. (Tongch'ŏn Yi Ch'ŏkkwŏn)

Mr. Ch'ŏkkwŏn, your criticism is quite strong. I almost got knocked out. Just remember that there were numerous advertisements when it was priced at 5 jeon. Although there are currently 60 pages, compared to before, the articles have increased significantly. In any case, if converted to the size of the children's magazines (as in the children's Shin Yŏsŏng edition), it would come out to about 130 pages, and in regular size (1st line size), it would be around 80 to 90 pages worth of content. (Kim)

After reading the article "Living on 3 won per month" about Mr. Yun Seong-hak in the May issue, I was moved to tears. Even if it’s just a small amount, may I sympathize with him? Please publish his address. (Ganghwa Hwang Ch'ŏlju)

I appreciate your kind words. Mr. Yun's address can be said to be in Kyŏngbuk Uiseong-eup. (Reporter)

Mr. Kim, how have you been? The Byeolgeonkŏn August issue was very good. I am waiting for the September issue so please make it come out soon. Also, please create a question and answer section in Byeolgeonkŏn. (Daegu Park Yŏngsan)

Please use this column for questions.

How many reporters do you have? Is there any chance I could see their faces through the magazine?

Seeing those faces of the reporters? However, if you really want to, I could consider it. (Reporter)

Dear editor! I have submitted articles five times, yet none have been published, what is going on? Is there even such a law in this world? Although it may be due to my lack of skill, it makes me very pessimistic… (Pyŏngyang Lohwol)

Do not write too long. Please do not just write too many love stories, but send brief interesting stories about rural life these days. (Reporter)

Please submit many interesting letters to Byeolgeonkŏn. The illustrations by T'anghaerĭngsŭ have been omitted several times recently, what’s going on? (Kyongseong A Reader)

It seems I wasn't mentally prepared and made a mistake. I will endeavor to correct this. (Kim)

Our village is extremely backward and rural. We hardly have any people attending elementary school, and the only paper read is Dong-A Ilbo, as not many know about magazines. I graduated from elementary school and couldn't go on to study anywhere else and have been working at home. However, I have always been curious about news from the world. It was by someone’s introduction that I began reading Byeolgeonkŏn, and since then, I have come to know the news and enjoy reading it each month. Whenever the magazine arrives, the people from the village gather around and ask to read it out loud. Even while working, the servants talk about Byeolgeonkŏn stories. As that month approaches, everyone in the village waits for the next month's issue to come. Could it be possible to outline the significant events of the month for us, who can't read the newspaper? It would be very useful for us. (Gap-san Yi Dŏkjae)

Thank you very much. Having a book that the whole village enjoys when it arrives is indeed heartwarming. The idea of summarizing essential news in a simple manner is something we at the editorial office have been considering, and we will try to implement it in the future. (Reporter)

It is a good thing that Byeolgeonkŏn has increased in price and pages. If possible, please also publish some nonsensical articles as they would be very beneficial and informative for our farmers and workers. This is my sincere wish. (Kŏngch'in Park Dong-su)

In our factory, comrades come together to order and read Byeolgeonkŏn all at once. Thus, they read it in the factory and at home as well. While reading, it is pleasurable, and there are many topics, but afterward, there is a feeling of lacking any substantial benefit, which makes one feel somewhat down. Is there a way to include more vigorous and intellectually stimulating articles? Truly, if Byeolgeonkŏn could appeal to such a wide readership, it would be the best magazine in Joseon. (In the city, Hwang Wansoo) <57> 

**Summary**

This article presents various reader letters expressing gratitude and concerns about the magazine Byeolgeonkŏn and its content. Readers share their experiences and suggestions, emphasizing the importance of the magazine in their lives and calling for a more accessible format, especially for workers and rural audiences. Historians may find the readers' criticisms representative of the broader sentiment in colonial Korea, where there was a growing demand for media that reflected the realities of life for the lower classes, highlighting the inequities of the colonial system.

지난 5월호 32頁에 기재된 「매월 3원으로 새식구 살니는 사람」 義城尹成學씨의 기사를 읽고 눈물이 납니다. 약소한 금전이나마 동정하고 십흐오니 분주하시지만 氏의 주소를 발표하여 주십시요.(江華 黃哲周)

미처 정신을 못채려 그럿습니다. 되도록 노력하지요.(金)

우리 동리는 아조 궁벽한 시골입니다. 보통학교에 다니는 사람까지 별로 업고 신문이라고는 재 넘어 청년회장 리모씨가 동아일보를 보는 외에는 잡지라면 이름도 모르는 사람이 대부분입니다. 나도 보통학교를 졸업하고는 다른 데로 더 공부하러 가지도 못하고 집에서 일을 하고 잇습니다. 그러나 세상소식이 늘 궁금하야 하든 차에 누구의 소개로 「별건곤」을 읽기 시작하면서부터는 세상 소식을 알게되고 또 무슨 이야기가 만하야 퍽 자미잇게 매달 읽습니다. 그리하야 책이 오기만 하면 동리사람들까지 쪼차와서 읽어 달나고 쟀°르고 일청에만 나가면 모슴들까지 일하면서도 별건곤 이야기로 꼿치 핍니다. 그리하야 그 달이 다가면 다시 다음달 잡지가 내려오기를 왼 동리 사람들이 다 가티 기다리고 잇습니다. 신문긔사에서 중요한 것만 따서 한달 동안 지난 상황을 간단이 긔재할 수는 업슬까요. 그런 것이 잇스면 신문 못 보는 대신 잘 읽겟고, 퍽 유익할 것 갓습니다.(甲山 李德載)

대단이 고맙습니다. 책하나를 가지고 왼 동리가 자미잇게 지난다는 것은 참말 듯기에도 깃븝니다. 신문긔사 가튼 것은 간단이 큰 사건만 따서 게재하랴고 편즙국에서도 늘 생각하고 잇는 일이니 장차 시행하겟습니다.(기자)

별건곤이 십전 되고 패지ㅅ 수가 늘은 것은 조흔 일입니다. 될 수 잇스면 넌센스한 긔사도  실는 것이 조치만 우리농민과 노동자들에게도 유익하고 참고 될 긔사를 아조 쉽게 써주십시오. 그것이 간절한 소원입니다.(庚津 朴東洙)

우리 공장에서는 동무들이 한꺼번에 모하서 별건곤을 주문해 읽습니다. 그래서 공장에서도 읽고 집에도 도라가서도 읽습니다. 읽을 때는 자미잇고 이야기ㅅ 거리가 만흐나 읽고 난 뒤에는 무슨 소득이 업는 것 가태서 마음에 어떤지 섭섭한 생각이 안납니다. 좀더 지금보다 힘차고 머리에 읏슥읏슥해질 긔사를 실을 수는 업슬까요. 참말 별건곤은 독자가 그러케 만타니 만흔 사람에게 그런 조흔 글을 읽힌다면 그는 별건곤의 공이 참말 조선에서 어느 잡지보다도 클 것입니다.(市內 黃完秀)＜57＞
