접시가 그립다  
A customer is having a meal at a Western-style restaurant, and a cat is crouched in one corner, looking up at them.  
Customer: "Hey, why is that cat only staring at me since I picked up that plate?"  
Servant: "Yes, it seems that the dish you are eating from must have something that piqued its interest."  

우는 愛嬌  
There seems to be some argument between a married couple, and the wife is just quietly crying.  
Husband: "Honey, if you’ve had enough, just stop crying. Why do you keep crying?"  
Suddenly, his wife raises her head and says, "What’s wrong with me crying? Don't I have any charm?"  

염녀업지요  
The reason for the husband’s illness was unusual, and the condition seemed serious. The doctor cautioned the patient’s wife,  
"Please, absolutely do not use the same room as Mr. Park. I earnestly advise you to take care."  
"Yes, yes, dear. Is there any other way to manage that while being in bed together?"  

아십니  
The magazine "어린이" (Children) will change its format and content from next month. Above all, there will be dozens of pictures and illustrations over ten pages, and the writing will be simplified so that anyone from the second or third grade of elementary school can read it. Nevertheless, the standard price is ten jeon. <16>  

**Summary**  

This article contains a collection of humorous vignettes exploring everyday situations among couples, customers, and a humorous commentary on feline behavior, as well as the changes planned for a children's magazine. The humorous exchanges encapsulate the dynamics of relationships during the 1930s in Korea, reflecting social norms and the impact of modernization. Historians might find interest in how humor was portrayed as a coping mechanism in a society under colonial rule, offering insights into cultural resilience and everyday life during this period.
