A Short Tune, Women of the Farming Family  
									崔吉龍  

1. On a day when the spring rain lightly falls,  

The eyes of a farming woman are full of tears,  

As she mends the hem of a garment,  

Worn in patches,  

A single outfit indeed.  

2. Even that torn garment,  

Is hard to mend  

Except on a rainy day during spring's season.  

Without rest for her weary body,  

She mends it earnestly.＜40＞  

**Summary**  

The poem paints a vivid picture of a farming woman engaged in the laborious task of mending her damaged clothing amidst the backdrop of spring rain. It reflects the struggles and resilience of rural women during this period, capturing both the physical and emotional weight of their work. From a historical perspective, the portrayal of women's labor emphasizes the socio-economic challenges faced by families in Korea under colonial rule, highlighting the persistence and resourcefulness of women as they navigate adversity.
