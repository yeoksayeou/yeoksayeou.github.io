Viewing Great Buildings (Part 1) - Cheondogyo Central Church (Seoul)  
A spacious floor that can accommodate up to 2,500 people, with nearly 3,000 able to fit in, is found only in this building in Korea, which has no mid-support pillars.  

When you go up to the rooftop of this four-story building, you can overlook not just this church but also check out the rear courtyards of the houses throughout the city.  

The total cost was 360,000 won, and it was constructed in the last year. There are two other large buildings in this courtyard.<9>  

**Summary**  

The article describes the Cheondogyo Central Church in Seoul, emphasizing its size and architectural significance as a unique structure in Korea. The church, notable for its lack of mid-support pillars and capacity for hosting large numbers of people, reflects the growth of religious movements and architectural ambition during the colonial period in Korea. Historians may find the investment in such a prominent building indicative of the cultural and social aspirations of the Korean people amidst colonial oppression.
