기사제목	안잠재기가 본 모보·모껄 新家庭 風景 (Anjamjaegi's View on the New Family Scene)  
기사형태	세태비평 (Social Commentary)  

Anjamjaegi says: "Our mistress has a quick temper and is quite modern, but she has a very kind heart. Her husband is even kinder. The baby has just passed the one-year mark. Our mistress knows that the sun rises in the east but doesn’t know where it sets. In the morning, she barely rises before I prepare the fire in the two hearths in the room so that the rice will be cooked by the time she wakes up. 

It is only when her husband is about to leave for the bank that he washes his face and comes to the room. Then I prepare water for her to brush her teeth and then she brushes her teeth, after which she fusses about breakfast. I don’t know much about modern life. I’m told to do this and that as she lies in bed, calling me numerous times, and I am exhausted trying to please her, and that doesn’t always work out well.

Breakfast is eaten by the two of them together in the room. The husband manages to eat about half, but the mistress barely eats a couple of bites, choosing instead to indulge in fresh eggs and milk. Therefore, I have to eat the leftovers from the rice I served them, as there's no way to scoop up the rice that's cooled down. In the winter, I find it hard to cope with such cold rice for days on end.

Certainly, if one has money and lives modernly, there is nothing better in the world. But when young people get married, shouldn't they serve their in-laws and manage the household? However, our mistress does not have in-laws. Is her husband truly that strict? Instead, he seems to serve her.

Look, after they have breakfast in such a manner, once her husband goes off to the bank, she has a nice long sleep by the warm floor. After dozing till late morning, she washes her face. Her washing up must be quite a spectacle. It seems her beauty routine involves heating up some metal implement and scorching her forehead, and shaving with a straight razor. I’ve seen women plucking hairs with tweezers, but never seen a modern lady shave with a razor. 

She usually does it herself on normal days, but on holidays, her husband helps her out. When he shaves her, she leans her head on his lap and takes a nap.

After her heating and shaving, she puts powder on her face, and it seems she can hardly apply it right, no matter how many varieties she has. After that, she gets fully dressed.

Her clothes, what a luxury they are! She tries on this outfit and that outfit; it’s all such a commotion. Then she spends the day chatting with her friends and having a party, buying things like food that I cannot eat for my life—things that she advises me to eat only a little bit of—and sings songs accompanied by some instrument making noise. I tell you, I can hardly bear the noise day or night due to that ruckus.

When she goes out to have fun, I'm left to deal with any of the baby’s crying or when her husband returns home late from the bank, which is chaotic. Then I see them getting active together again.

There was a time when she was preparing to leave with a bag, and I felt scared, rushing to stop her, after which she feigned not to go out and returned. While our mistress goes about having fun, her husband seems to be unable to do anything.

On holidays, her husband shaves her and then they both go to the bathhouse. On such days, I don’t even prepare dinner, since they go out to eat elsewhere and come back full. By the way, I've heard that those dinners are extravagantly priced, nearly one hundred fifty yang each. 

It seems on typical nights, the husband sleeps in the side room, while the mistress and the baby sleep in the room, but she intentionally leaves space for herself and pretends not to hear when the baby cries.

Instead of such, would it not be better to have her occupy the side room so that I could take care of the baby? Once, her in-laws came to visit. Such old folks! Perhaps it’s their first time seeing a daughter-in-law. 

My, the sight was quite amusing! Although she's a modern lady, she really struggles to fit in her role as a daughter-in-law. Seeing her wearing a short skirt, running around between the kitchen and the room, it must have been embarrassing for her mother-in-law, who might have said, 'Dear, although I know you live modernly, while your father is still around, at least wear a lengthier skirt to cover your legs. It's quite troublesome to see.'

The mistress must not have said anything in that moment, but later, she fussed to her husband about it. Still, her mother-in-law dotes on her daughter-in-law and is quite fond of the grandchild, not wanting to let him out of her sight. 

If the baby cries just a little, she calls for the daughter-in-law to breastfeed him, and afterwards, she cuddles him and strokes his head, smiling widely at her. 

When it’s time to leave, she even gives them fabric for clothing in silk. Later on, I found out that it was because our husband lived alone before marriage but the mistress opposed it and said she would not raise her in-laws. 

These days, our household has become quite the spectacle. They brought home a gramophone and a man who teaches the mistress to dance, who presumably earns a salary of fifteen hundred yang a month, which is more than I earn. 

This man enters our home and teaches her dance while embracing her waist, circling the room in merriment. It’s an atrocious sight! 

In my opinion, dance is something only entertainers or jesters should do, not what a lady does. 

And what’s more? Now they fill the room with pieces of music and dance together every night. I find myself driven out into a corner to bear another sleepless night. I’m at my wit’s end.

The husband supposedly earns two thousand five hundred yang a month, and it looks to me like they spend more than ten thousand yang monthly. Then I wondered where all this money came from, and it turns out the mistress had brought a sum when she got married. 

So she seems to be quietly spending that down little by little. After that runs out, I worry about how they’ll continue to maintain this lavish lifestyle. If our mistress gets worried, she could just leave me out of such talks. I’m barely scraping by with three yang a month while enduring cold rice, and if I got kicked out, I wouldn’t even know where to go."  

**Summary**

This article provides a critical social commentary on the nuances of modern family life in Korea during the colonial period, as seen through the eyes of a housemaid. It highlights the disparities between the wealthy households of modern women who enjoy their luxuries and the hard labor of domestic workers. The maid’s reflections convey a sense of frustration and irony, showcasing the growing consumer culture juxtaposed against the traditional familial obligations that continue to persist. Historians may find value in examining how such domestic dynamics provide insight into larger societal shifts during the Korean colonial era, revealing class structures and the complexities of modernity.

그래저래 나는 식은 밥으로만 살어가지요. 그것도 여름이면 모르지 다만 치운 겨울에 그것을 하루 이틀이 안이고 터 엇케 견대냄니까.
