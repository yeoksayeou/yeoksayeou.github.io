유-모어 집  

A 女: "You are indeed getting married."  

B 女: "How do you know that?"  

A 女: "Such things are obvious. I can tell just by reading your palm! You are engaged to Mr. K, right?"  

B 女: "How can you tell from reading palms?"  

B 女: "Oh… it's not about palm reading, I meant my fingers! The ring I sent to Mr. K the other day was on my finger, you know!"  

  

수가 업서  

先生: "Don't you understand? If you add one to three, you can't not know what the total is."  

生徒: "..."  

先生: "Alright- let's say someone brought three chicken eggs to your house and then brought one more! How many do you have now?"  

生徒: "I can't count."  

先生: "Why not?"  

生徒: "Because the egg seller in this household might not know how many eggs there are."

醫師: "Sure- you are suffering from Alkoholismus."

X: "Excuse me, shall I open the window?"  

  

나릴 줄 몰나  

Seeing a girl who had fallen off a bicycle, a police officer said: "Why are you riding on the main road when you don't even know how to ride?"  

The girl replied: "No, I can ride; I just don’t know how to get down."  

  

그러나 저러나  

"From the looks of the opposition to this case, it seems that half of you must be fools without discernment."  

The audience murmured in discontent.  

"It was an error in my speech. Take it back! Fools?!"  

"Yes! I made a mistake. I take that comment back! Half of you are not fools without discernment."  

  

平生所願  

A: "Have you ever had one of your lifelong wishes come true?"  

B: "Yes, one thing. When I was a child, I wished for someone to wash my hair. Now I am told, 'You better not have any hair left soon.'"  

  

相對가 업스니  

A professor of physiology asked: "What are some of the reasons a woman might not be able to conceive?"  

A student replied: "Yes, there are several reasons. One of the main ones is her not being attractive."  

  

相對性原理  

There are numbers that, when you add one to one, can equal zero, two, or even three; what is that equation?  

男性 + 女性 <27>  

**Summary**  

This article consists of a series of comic dialogues reflecting everyday life and social norms in colonial Korea. Each dialogue portrays humorous misunderstandings or social critiques among diverse characters. The recurrent theme of misunderstanding often reflects the struggles of identity and social roles in a colonized society. Historians might find the satire of gender norms and societal expectations particularly revealing, as they showcase the tension and complexities of life under colonial rule.
