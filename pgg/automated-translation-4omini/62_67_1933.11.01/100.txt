기사제목	朝鮮日報對 東亞日報 爭覇戰, =新聞戰線에 異狀잇다 (The Struggle for Supremacy Between Joseon Ilbo and Dong-A Ilbo, = Abnormal Situation on the News Front)  
필자	벽상생 (Byeog Sang-saeng)  
기사형태	소식 (News)  

The jade dew (옥로) is not far off, trying to catch the frost and the golden wind (금풍) that lost its way is flaring up as if to hasten the heavenly season. The stirrings of the times feel unpredictable as the expansion of the U.S. Navy follows Japan (日本) and Britain (英國) expanding their national budgets, the Anti-Chiang movement (반장운동) breaking out in North China (북중), the Russian (로서아) military preparations in the Far East (극동), and the realization of alliances between Germany (독일) and Austria (오국). All of this suggests a looming chaos, bringing forth a sense of impending storm—a feeling of onset as strong as rain on the mountains while the winds fill the building. The only ones left out in all this movement are the Koreans, who may consider their own happiness or unhappiness trivial compared to the rest, spending time sleeping and eating, as if to embrace the possibility of merely crossing over into a quiet region devoid of any wind. However, as competition between the newspapers emerged, the clattering of the footsteps of people has begun to stir the stillness of Joseon society, creating material for various discussions. Thus, it wouldn't be meaningless to expose the truths beneath the surface of each newspaper and to share such a peculiar interest with the readers.

If the Dong-A Ilbo (東亞日報) represents a formidable bulwark, then the emerging force of the Joseon Ilbo (朝鮮日報) can be likened to the rising sun. For the relatively new Joseon Ilbo to compete with the sturdy established force of Dong-A Ilbo, it must rattle the foundations of Dong-A Ilbo through personnel organization and business policy. If it doesn’t, and if it aims to shake things up, it must realize that swiftly dragging out pivotal figures that hold sway in Dong-A Ilbo would be quicker. This is why the significant personnel reshuffle at Dong-A Ilbo occurred, pulling out figures such as Lee Kwang-su (李光洙), Seo Chun (徐椿), and Kim Dong-jin (金東進) which led to changes in the organization; these individuals were pulled by personal connections, while Shin Tae-ik (申泰翊) and Ham Sang-hun (咸尙勳) were moved purely by interests, shifting for salaries and status.

Essentially, newspaper companies are places where intellectuals and schemers gather, and it stands out that the turbulence has been particularly notable in Joseon compared to other social institutions. Although Dong-A Ilbo has been the most stable private newspaper, the management personnel have seen numerous changes despite the founders remaining in place. Dong-A Ilbo was born from the passionate heartbeat of the Korean people, and during its founding, many prominent figures flocked to it. The groundwork has become tattered, and management has been critically burdened, leaving little room for conflicts or schemes. However, as the fervent spirit gradually cooled, management struggles eased slightly, leading to a gradual clash between the pro-Yun (긔호파) and pro-Jeolla (전라도파) factions, namely the management and employees, culminating in a vote of no confidence from some members around a certain figure. 

The individuals associated with these factions, especially those in the privileged class, were finding themselves wanting, seeking merely leadership positions; if their commands were not to their liking, they often resorted to destructive measures. Such division has a long history, and the affliction of acquiring and losing remains insatiable even today. When executives and the major stockholders leaned towards the pro-Yun faction, listening solely to their opinions, it prevented conflicts from surfacing. However, when individuals with true abilities began to exert authority, it became clear there was limited scope for strategists, and due to minimal financial contributions, those who barely attended meetings and fulfilled their duties solely as shareholders could easily lose their rights.

The friction among pro-Korean individuals during these confrontations showed signs of being detrimental, with the management faction, mainly from Honam (호남), seeming to prevail but at a significant cost—providing an opportunity to reevaluate their strategies. Following these experiences, reorganizing strictly along pro-Honam lines proved too narrow-minded, inciting ridicule. Thus, there was an unavoidable need to recruit non-Honam figures who had lesser political acumen, leading to the preeminence of the pro-North faction within important management positions at Dong-A Ilbo for a time. However, despite lacking real power, the dictatorship of Song (송) became excessive, leading to serious discontent, particularly among the staff.

The financial backing of the gold mine king (금광왕) helped to manage the Joseon Ilbo, ensuring it could withstand Dong-A Ilbo in competition, and the need to attract those feeling left out from Dong-A Ilbo became imperative to strengthen their foundation and inflict serious damage on their competitor. The moves made by figures like Lee Kwang-su and Seo Chun reflect this motivation, even if their primary interest is in gaining more influence. Yet, the scheme of luring talent away was heavily accompanied by incentives, with offers increasing from a hundred to a hundred and fifty won in salary, leading to what felt like the dubious practices of a cabinet minister increasing positions before retirement.

Despite these upheavals, it proved to be a significant tonic for Dong-A Ilbo. Firstly, President Song realized that the narrow-mindedness stemming from connections was detrimental. Secondly, after failing with both pro-Yun and pro-North endeavors, they found it better to focus on a clearly Jeolla-centered approach. Undoubtedly, Honam localism (몰로주의) would become stronger. Thirdly, removing high-salary veterans to employ fresh talents at lower wages clarified an understanding of benefits from efficiency and economy.

Even after separating from Song Byeong-jun, Joseon Ilbo managed to present itself adequately, gaining respect in society. However, the management faced internal strife, and chaos loomed. The financial support from the gold mine kingdom led to lavish management efforts, but rumors of throngs of staff also hint at substantial risks. Should these matters hold true, it illustrates a necessary awakening. With departments competing fiercely, the Morning edition (조간) has nearly lost meaning, while excessive issuing of evening extras (호외) appears to undermine the prestige of the newspaper. Dong-A's blatant local-centric practices attract prevalent criticism, and Fang (方), by mimicking the practices, focuses on hiring only from Pyongan-do (평안도), showing a strong local bias despite acknowledging the connections.

Should Dong-A and Joseon continue facing each other in this manner, in the end, they might solely become known as Honam Ilbo (湖南日報) and Kansai Ilbo (關西日報). Regardless, as the two newspapers fiercely compete with one another, it is uncertain who will ultimately prevail; however, readers may simply gain an extra page of photos or supplements, finding themselves helplessly watching.

In the midst of this, the privately managed Joongang Ilbo (中央日報), despite its insufficient scale and capital compared to both newspapers, finds itself less able to compete. It remains uncertain who will act as Zhuge Liang (諸葛亮) guiding it through this tumultuous time.

  

**Summary**  

This article discusses the intense competition and struggles between the Joseon Ilbo and Dong-A Ilbo during a tumultuous time in Korea's colonial history. It highlights the shifting personnel dynamics and the influence of regional biases, particularly the conflict between factions based on geographic affiliations. Historians might find the detailed depiction of newspaper politics and the reflection of broader societal struggles during the colonial period particularly revealing, as they illustrate how media played a crucial role in shaping public discourse at the time.

이번 일이 동아일보에는 큰 약이 되엿스니 첫재, 송사장으로 하야금 어인접물(御人接物)의 편협이 못된 것인 것을 절실히 깨닷게 하얏고. 둘재로는, 긔호인물에 실패하고 서북인물에 또 실패하야 차라리 편협할지언정 순 전라도 중심으로 하는 것이 낫다는 것을 알앗슬 것이니 이 압흐로는 전라도의 몰로주의가 더욱 강할 것이오. 셋재, 타긔가 잇든 돈 만히 먹든 고급자를 내보내고 4, 50원의 신진인물을 쓰게되니 능률과 경제상으로 도로혀 이익이 될는지도 알 수 업다.
조선일보가 송병준의 손을 떠나 신문다운 면목으로 세상에 행세도 하얏고 남의 대접도 상당히 바닷지만은 경영자들이 긔호인물이든 만큼 역시 파쟁과 알륵으로 종시하야 호강은 짤럿고 가진 욕은 형용키 어럽게 격다가 지금 평안도 금광 왕국으로 들어가 풍성풍성한 돈으로 호화로운 경영을 하야가나 밧그로 숨어 나오는 풍문을 드르면 그곳에서도 상자가 넘우 여럿이어서 잘못하면 가마솟깰 념녀가 업지 안흔 모양이라니 그것이 사실이라면 그네들도 한번 맹성할 일이요. 편즙은 넘어 별안간에 대시문 노릇을 하랴는 바람에 재료선택보다 큰 활자를 만히 쓰고 조간(朝刊)은 남타경쟁을 하너라고 저녁에 먼저 돌이니 석간이 회라고 할지언정 조간의 의미가 업서뵈며 호외(號外)는 변변치 안은 일에도 너무 람발을 하야 엇지 도로혀 신문호외의 위신을 일케 하는 듯하다. 그리고 동아가 너무 전라도 몬로주의를 써서 일반의 비난이 만흔 이때에 방씨(方氏)가 도로혀 그것을 모방하야 사원을 평안도 중심으로만 채용하는 것은 정실관계에 할 수 업는 일이라 할지라도 너무 지방색이 농후한 것 갓다. 동아, 조선 두 신문이 그대로 만나아 간다면 최후에는 명의까지도 동아는 왈 호남일보(湖南日報), 조선은 왈 관서일보(關西日報)가 될는지도 알수 업다. 하여간 두 신문이 그러케 경쟁을 하야 최후에 누가 패권을 잡을지 알 수 업스나 독자로서는 사진 한 장을 더 어더 보던지 부록 한 장을 더 엇게 되여도 손은 업슬 것이니 벽 상에 관초건격으로 그저 구경만 할 수 밧게 업다. 그 틈에 긔호인물을 중심하야 가지고 경영하는 중앙일보(中央日報)는 자본의 힘으로나 규모의 적은 것으로나 아즉 형편으로는 도저히 두 신문과 경쟁을 하지 못할 것 가터 서 맛치 삼분천하에 익주가피폐(三分天下益州疲廢)한 감이 잇스니 아지 못거라 중앙일보에는 과연 누가 제갈양(諸葛亮) 노릇을 하며 국궁진췌할고.＜29＞
