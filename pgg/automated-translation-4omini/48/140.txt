最善의 法  
A person asks a passerby, "Excuse me, how do I get to the police station?"  
"Yes, the police station. It's very simple. Go to that shop you see over there and ask them something. They'll take you there..."  

The Heart of a Child  
A child says, "Hey, have you seen my mother's hairpin? That’s a pure gold hairpin!"  
The son of a silversmith responds, "Don’t lie. That hairpin is one that your father bought at our house. It's gold-plated."  

Let It Be  
The goat herder asks, "Excuse me, is there anywhere nearby to get something to eat?"  
The passerby replies, "Of course! If you go along this road and take the tram to Dongdaemun, you'll go towards Dongsoemun. Then when you get off at the terminal, there’s a big gate, you see..."  
The goat herder inquires, "A big gate? So that's a way into someone else's house?"  
The passerby clarifies, "No, it’s the zoo I'm talking about."  

Suspicious Sir  
In the dark of the night—  
A strange noise comes from the yard.  
A scared mother asks, "Is that you, sir?"  
A voice responds, "That's right. It's me."  

Please Give Me Some Rain  
The policeman says, "Hey, you! Why are you acting like a beggar?"  
The beggar replies, "When have I ever asked for anything?"  
The policeman questions, "Then why are you holding out your hand?"  
The beggar explains, "It's this—wondering if it will rain or not."  

Thus, Remain Calm  
The doctor says, "You must absolutely remain calm, sir. Here, there’s a sleeping pill as well..."  
The wife asks, "Then when should he take it?"  
The doctor responds, "Not for him; I mean for you to take it."  

Solo Performance  
A father returns from the battlefield and tells his young son,  
"I always say this, but my actions in that war were remarkable."  
The son replies, "Then, Dad, what were the other soldiers doing there at the war?"  

A Great Adventure  
The adventurer says, "...there were many times I almost died from the claws of that terrible tiger! Truly, what a dull life it would be without adventures."  
A listener remarks, "Oh, that’s not the case. I also go on adventures several times a year by forgetting my umbrella on days when it seems like it might rain."  

**Summary**  

This text is a collection of humorous anecdotes and dialogues reflecting the everyday life and social interactions in 1930s Korea. Each vignette touches on themes of misunderstanding, innocence, and the absurdities of life. The pieces showcase the common man's experiences during the colonial period, shedding light on societal norms and the blending of traditional values with modern life as Korea underwent significant changes. Historians might find the humor and language used in these anecdotes a valuable insight into the cultural psyche of Koreans during the Japanese occupation.

잇고 말고
