No. 6 Freedom March  

On a certain Sunday afternoon, I took the tram to Dongdaemun. The day was a Sunday, and the tram was quite crowded with passengers. Among them was a drunken young man who deliberately placed his legs on the knees of a female student whenever the tram shook. After doing this two or three times, he suddenly cried out, "Oh, my leg!" and fell over. Even he did not know the reason, and neither did the others around him. Eventually, we arrived at the Jeongnyu station, and the drunken man got off and began to walk away, while the female student who had been harassed poked her friend in the side, giggling and saying, “That guy got what he deserved!” It turned out that recently, because there were such rude men when female students went to public places or took the tram, they had started to carry sewing needles in their skirts and would prick the offending man with them whenever he acted improperly. To the rowdy young men out there! If you recklessly trouble weak female students, you’ll be pricked by a hidden needle, so be careful. And for female students who have just come from the countryside, when you're buying shoes, dresses, and other such things, remember to also buy a few needles.

  

The Bitter Life  
  

“Hey! Do you want to try some taffy? This is how sweet it is.”  

“Okay, I’ll try it. It is sweet; delicious! Eat a lot!”  

“Now that you’ve had some taffy, give me what you have in your hand. Then I’ll give you more taffy later.”  

“Look at that! I’m not going to give you anything; you’re a greedy little brat! Don’t think that just because you’ve eaten my taffy, I’ll give you that!”  

“If you won’t give it to me, then bear with it.”  

“How can it be fair? You’re really a greedy little brat; I think you’re going to keep trying to pull this stunt."  

“Listen now, it’s still in your hand. It’s mine as long as it’s with me. You just know how to eat taffy.”  

This is the grim reality of life.  

  

Visiting Marriage  

The night of Jongno! The Jongno at night!! The roaring automobiles and buses, the sound of trams clashing, the cries of the trams, the neon signs adorned with red, yellow, and white from the cafes flowing with seductive atmospheres, and the drunken rants of drunken people, all blended in a symphony of noise! Captivated by the chaos of this hub of Seoul, I wandered toward the neighborhood without purpose.  

As I arrived at the department store, I bumped into a friend who said, “Hey! Long time no see!” and together we climbed the stairs to a restaurant on the third floor. At a corner of the restaurant, I was listening to a conversation about the modern forms of love, particularly that “visiting marriage is a new type of modern love,” in response to a question from a hostess. My friend and I involuntarily repeated the young man's words: "Visiting marriage!?..."

Marriage in 1932 is now defined by visiting marriages!!  

  

The Comedic Actor (Aikyo)  

When I went to the Joseon Theater to watch Paramount News, Aikyo came out. It was a parade led by the Black Shirts youth group, where he stood with his chest puffed up commanding attention. The Italian soldiers marched in with the blaring sound of drums. They rode their bicycles steadily, and just then, with a bang, came the motorcycle and machine gunners behind the Black Shirts youth. Aikyo was seen talking in Italian to a soldier in uniform, whose expression was fatigue despite delivering commands imperiously.  

After the parade ended, he called a subordinate, and one-by-one they stepped up, and as the lieutenant affixed medals on the chests of the honored Black Shirts youth, Aikyo looked at them with a strangely soft face. His stiff expression looked overly rigid; whenever he adorned them, the expression on the faces of the honored was such that they felt uncomfortable and looked awkward. Seeing this made me laugh somewhat similarly to a person who couldn’t control their emotions. Those who did not understand the meaning of that expression stared at us as if we were peculiar. The more they tried to be serious, the more ridiculous it became. Without a doubt, Aikyo is a comedic genius.  

  

Polishing and Grinding Teeth  

My brother used to steal my toothbrush toothpaste (contained in a large tube). We were two who shared what was supposed to last for twenty days, and we couldn’t even last for ten days! The person who bought it was angry.  

"This time my brother will surely buy it," my brother thought. And in turn, the younger brother thought, "Surely, my elder brother will buy it." They awaited each other's actions, but it was in vain.  

“Hey! That guy!” my elder brother shouted and decided to replace the toothbrush with a new one. The younger brother had no choice and did the same.  

One time, the younger brother discovered the toothbrush toothpaste while rummaging through the elder brother's room. He thought about how he had bought it for himself and, with tricks, had even returned the tube. Feeling quite disheartened, he finally found the time to use it after a long time. He squirted a bit on the brush and rubbed it on his teeth two or three times, "Oh! What a pity, it was medication for his skin!"  

  

Spring Has Come  

The cold, fierce winter has passed, and the flowers have bloomed, thus filling the mountains and fields. The sounds of birds chirping blend with the warm air of spring. This spring has come to this land without reservation, yet it seems it has not returned to me.  

Every day I hear the sounds of complaints that there is nothing to farm, and where to gather something to eat will matter to the farmers. The spectacle of moneyed folk strolling in the suburbs and the students ambling around in leisure—these sights all stem from the arrival of spring. If spring had not come, I wouldn’t have had to see these sighted scenes, wouldn’t be filled with despair, would have better lived from this land.  

Spring, initially meant to revive the spirit of all beings on this earth, only leads us to face this bleak reality and rob us rather than animate our spirits. Is there any joy in saying that spring has come?  

**Summary**  

This collection of pieces showcases the diverse experiences in early 1930s Korea, blending societal observations with humor. It highlights incidents of harassment towards female students, social interactions, and the realities of everyday life during a time of colonial rule. The noteworthy aspect is the resilience depicted in women’s responses to social challenges, hinting at emerging feminist sentiments despite societal constraints. These narratives provide historians with insight into the changing social dynamics and the evolving roles of gender within Korea during this period.

喜劇俳優 뭇소리니
鏡花

조선극장에 가서 파라마운트 뉴-스 영록을 보니까 못소리니 각하께서 나옵섯다. 흑샤스청년단을 주로한 열병식인가 분데 각하는 가슴을 쓱 내밀고 놉흔데 서서 커단 상판을 하고 잇다.
이태리명물 벨사리에리 구보로 보병이 달려온다. 자전거대가 용케 맛부드치지도 안코 가즈런히 달려온다. 그리더니 탕탕탕 소리를 내며 달러오는 오토바이 기관총대 뒤으로 흑샤쓰청년단의 분렬행진-대상의 뭇소리니각하는 겻헤 잇는 군복쟁이에게 뭐라고 알지 못할 이태리 말로 주절거리더니 분렬행진이 끗나자 정렬한 각 부대 앞흐로 아조 거만한 거름으로＜27＞ 뚜벅뚜벅 거럭간다. 실로 뭇소리니처럼 껑충껑충 것는 사람은 드물리라. 이번에는 *장수*식인 모양이다.
각하가 부관 한 사람을 불너내인다. 렬중으로부터 한사람씩 나스는 흑샤쓰 청년공로자의 가슴에 부관은 훈장을 꼬자준다. 그때 각하의 상판이라니 지나치게 검잔탈까 너무 시침이를 뚝땃다고 할까 도모지 보드러운 기색업는 표정이다. 그 상판도 상판이려니와 그 보다도 훈장찬 흑샤쓰청년의 두 억개를 두 손으로 턱누르고 독꼅이 주둥이 가티 두터운 입술을 청년 두 뺨에 갓다 부칠때는 그것을 밧고 경래를 붓치고 드러스는 청년의 얼골에는 깃분 빗보다 끔찍스럽다는 듯한 표정을 한다. 그것을 보니까 엇더케 우수운지 가티 갓든 R군하고 그냥 우서버렷다. 그 우슴의 의미를 모르는 사람들은 우리를 이상하다는 듯이 처다본다. 점잔빼면 뺄사록 턱제키면 제킬수록 우습게 보인다. 영사막 우에서는 뭇소리니각하는 확실이 희극의 천재이시다.
