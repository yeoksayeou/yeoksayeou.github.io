기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Spring Rain  
The rain that comes to this land  
Is spring rain.  

You and I  
Let us say our goodbyes.  

The woodsman withered in spring  
Even if I am to grow anew.  
(End)  

Folk Song - Farewell  
盧良根 (Roh Yanggeon)  

Before leaving, before leaving  
I must tell you what will happen afterward.  
Do not linger; hurry up and go.  
When you step over the threshold  
Why then can’t I cry out in sorrow?  

Having gone and returned,  
Carrying a load of flowers and a gift,  
When coming back, whether it be  
Laughing or dancing, come out to greet me.  
Let me be resolute; hurry now and go.  

While passing the Lion Gate,  
I had to climb up to Hongryu Cave, coming for a while.  
Standing by the stone pillar, I put up my hands at the door.  
My friend, they say this is Lion Gate.  
How many guests are coming and going?  
Can you imagine the number of people passing through there?  
*Only we know you, as we go.  
On the lion's back, I have a part of my mind set.  
From the 80,000 Tripitaka, who would know your place?  
It’s a place for two Zen schools, a great hall.  
My body stays here.  
In Gaya Mountain, at Haein Temple, there is a part of my mind stuck.  
For forty-nine years, what does it say distinctly?  
In the words spoken by the Buddha, the Lion’s seat is here, it seems.  
The water under Dragon Boat Bridge flows gently.  
At Purple Cloud Cave, the stones and water float gently.  
In one place, three views are seen, how exciting!  

Folk Song - Let’s Dance  
金樂煥 (Kim Neunghwan)  

The chubby fellow, the fat thing, old dogfather,  
Look at that squashed pot!  
I stayed awake all night guarding it,  
And now, I burst into flames as I revealed it.  
Let’s dance!  

Even now, there’s nothing to it;  
The pudgy nose stuffed,  
The oily belly skin stretching,  
Will I not see a dreadful sight?  
Let’s dance!  

I am water.  
安國泰 (An Guktae)  

I am the life water of all beings, but beyond that, I am water that doesn’t speak.  
When summer days bring heat to my weary body, as I rest under the sun,  
I panic and yell that I could die at any moment,  
Yet when the clouds come down, I scuffle away, then I let go of that time.  
I know how human hearts feel from this.  

Look at the waves of red blood.  
金野笛 (Kim Yeodeuk)  

The stars in the sky are dark,  
And the waves of the sea are singing.  
My friend, are you watching?  
Like the eyes of a raider, shining bright  
In the city’s night.  

In the delight of joy overflowing in the festival…  
The fraying ends of the revue swirling wildly,  
- In that fragrant valley  
Of sweet sorrow, the marching tune  
My friend, can you hear it?  

However, look at the sour cries  
That roam under the city’s facade.  
Oh, sir, life is accompanied  
By the bourgeois's deranged dreams and cries.  

Beautifully to burn our  
Fervent passion's goblet is  
Slowly appearing in that red wave.  
My friend, can you see it?  
1932. 2 (From Wonsan Port) <33>  

**Summary**  

This collection of poems and folk songs reflects the emotional landscape of Korean society in the early 1930s, highlighting themes of nature, separation, and longing mixed with a deep yearning for connection. The works express sentiments of seasonal change and personal reflection, indicative of the broader struggles faced by Koreans during the colonial period. The poignant depiction of farewells and the interweaving of traditional folk forms illustrate not only personal but collective cultural sentiment, offering historians insight into the cultural responses to colonial oppression.
