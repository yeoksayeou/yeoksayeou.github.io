기사제목	우체통과 거지 (The Postbox and the Beggar)  
필자	尹石重  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

The postbox

At the crossroads, the postbox  
Big letters and small letters  
The postbox that eats them up.

The beggar is near the postbox  
With hands outstretched, begging  
"Please, lady, give me a penny  
Please, sir, grant me a penny."

The mouth of the postbox is wide  
Food keeps disappearing inside  
But in the beggar child's hand,  
Not a single coin all day long.  
- From Ilheobarin Daenggi - <41>  

**Summary**

The poem depicts the stark contrast between the postbox, a symbol of communication and connection, and the beggar, representing poverty and desperation. It illustrates the disconnect in society where the postbox is filled with letters while the beggar struggles for basic sustenance, highlighting social inequalities. Historians may find the imagery significant as it reflects the economic struggles during the colonial period in Korea, showcasing the plight of the marginalized against the backdrop of colonial administration.
