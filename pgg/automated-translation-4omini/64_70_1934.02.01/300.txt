필자	李啞夫  
기사형태	소설 (Novel)  

An educator, Mr. An, who had been chased into the counseling room, slumped down on the chair, and as soon as he did, he let out a wail that rose to his chin. “Those bastards' mouths! Those bastards' mouths! It’s like they’re snatching me away!" He cried alone, bewildered, and wept pitifully.

The truth is, he had attended a nickname-giving ceremony hosted jointly by male and female teachers at K Girls’ School that afternoon. There, he endured an unbearable humiliation, which drove him to this state of despair.

The humiliation he couldn't tolerate from his colleagues went something like this: During the naming ceremony, he was given five nicknames. 

First, since it was a sunny day, he was called Rain Stop Teacher.  
His body was heavy, so they called him Gagged Shit Teacher.  
He was always smiling, so they named him Smiley Teacher.  
Because he was short and plump, they called him Fat Teacher.  
And since he enjoyed eating hotteok (sweet Korean pancakes), he was named Hotteok Teacher.  

“I am such a pity! If I had just left when I graduated, I wouldn't have had any of this! Why did I bother to enclose myself with this meaningless crap, only to be humiliated like this?” His sobbing grew louder.

“Oh! Father, Mother! What wrong have you done? How did you create me this way? I hardly got the top part right, and yet you fed me greedily, how could you make me grow this fat?” 

“From tomorrow on, how will I enter the school with such hues? If this rumor spreads, the students will treat me even worse! Look at how comically shaped I’ve been set up; I won’t get married for the rest of my life, and they'll ridicule me!”  

“Oh! Sister! I beg you! Mother! I beg you! What sin do I have that you gave me this bitter life?”  

“Oh! I am the ‘Suffering Rain Stop Teacher!’ Is it not a tragically loaded woman’s lifetime?” His cries continued, reverberating hopelessly.

After twenty, thirty, forty minutes, his cries began to subside. With swollen eyelids, Mr. An stroked his head and got up from the chair. He turned towards the wall, pondering. “No! This isn't the time to dwell on self-pity! They are all mocking me for being a woman who has never been married!”  

"... ... ..."

“Ha! If she were a married woman, would they ever speak such degrading words? If one said something disrespectful toward a woman who has a husband, they would have their legs broken! How could this be my fault for not getting married?”  

"... ... ..."

“This ugly thing! It’s no time to cry! It’s no time to stand idle! Now is the moment to raise the banner of male conquest! You’re right! This is an emergency during my youth. I will not pass on my feminine status lightly! I will fight till the end! I must capture a man!”  

"... ... ..."

“But – there must be a counterpart, right? Oh! Yes! Surely among the hundreds of millions of men, there won’t be anyone coming my way.”  

Thus, Mr. An, who had been lingering in despair, stood up decisively. He returned to the earlier cheerful mood, welcoming the youthful students flowing into the school that night.

One night - it's snowing in the forest of Namsan.

Mr. An, having graduated from a women's physical education school in Tokyo and currently serving as a gymnastics teacher at a local school, wasn't much concerned about the weather, whether clear, cloudy, or rainy. That night, he was actively engaging with another teacher.

“Don't you think, ultimately, life is a tragedy?” he asked.

The counterpart, a young man named Choi from the same school, aged twenty-five, without the handsome face of actors, was surprised. 

“Why? Do you feel so pessimistic? Then has it ever been a comedy?” 

“Who said it was a comedy?” 

“Then?” 

“It just occurs to me that since everything doesn’t go as I wish, I feel this way!”  

“... ... ...”   

“I came to meet you tonight for not just any purpose. I hope to ask for your help in making something happen that doesn’t go according to my will!”  

“Your ambitions seem high! Where is there anything that goes as we wish?”  

“Please, tell me what are you proposing?”  

“Teacher, are you married?”  

“No!”  

“Why?”  

“Why do you ask?”   

“I need to know so I can gather the courage to speak.”  

“... ... ...”  

“Why are you silent?”  

“Well... I want to remain single.”  

“Hohoho! Isn't that a bold statement?”  

“However, what I’m trying to address isn’t anything else… it’s about marriage…”  

“What about marriage?”  

“It’s about your marriage.”  

“Then, could you perhaps introduce someone suitable?”  

“No! Absolutely not! … ... I’m saying this to stimulate a shift in thought that in the past the woman was passive and the man actively considered and decided on marriage; but tonight, I want to think the other way around…”  

“Teacher? ... Teacher?”  

“... ... ...”  

“Why don’t you speak?”  

“Huh?”  

“What’s wrong?”  

“I still... want to be single.”  

“Hohoho! How embarrassing it is to say that.”  

“Yet, what I want to talk about isn’t anything else... um... indeed regarding the marriage matter…”  

“What?”  

“It is about your marriage matter!”  

“Could we possibly meet somewhere suitable?”   

“No! Never! ... ... I mean, until now, women have always regarded marriage as something men take the initiative upon, but this time, I hope to suggest, from the other end, that we could consider marriage.”  

“Teacher!”  

“How about you marrying me?”  

“? ?”  

“Surely, you would consider my suggestion strange, wouldn’t you! But this is for both your happiness and mine...”  

“Both our happiness?”  

Until now, Mr. An’s questions had been only directed toward the actions of the other teacher, Lee, when finally, he opened up and responded.  

“Do you reckon our union would bring happiness? A woman older by a year and a twenty-five-year-old man should unite for happiness?”  

“Yes! I frankly declare and guarantee it.”  

“In conclusion, should I be merely a perplexing situation for that?”  

“Not at all! What absurdity is that? Looking at it from a eugenics perspective, that would make sense!”  

“Huh! Just because both are gymnastics teachers, does that mean the union of healthy bodies guarantees healthy offspring?”  

“And then!”  

“What do you mean by ‘and then’? After ten to twenty years, what will that lead to?”  

“Ah! That point!”  

“What! The unlucky after several decades, staying married?”  

“That’s unfair!”  

“Why? Is it unlucky to live together until our hair turns gray?”  

“Honestly, you lack insight, Mr. An. You don't understand men. Men do not wish to marry just any woman.”  

“Why?”  

“Women tend to age more quickly than men!”  

“Does that mean we should deliberately withhold the union of our healthy bodies because of the noticeable age difference?”  

With that, Mr. An's criticisms and Lee's retorts reached a climax. “Mr. An, your claims from the very beginning are unreasonable. You contemplate marriage with a very simplistic mindset. It's not something that straightforward. Feelings, emotions, age, integrity, appearance, and so on...” 

“Let’s cut that off! I understand much from what you’re saying. Yet I never realized that you and I had such different views on marriage! I’ve cherished the thought of you fondly...”

At this time, Mr. An, unable to hold back anymore, burst into tears, as though opening a floodgate of sorrow.  

“Mr. An! What on earth is this? Huh?”  

“I do not know! I am now a discarded person!"  

“Discarded? Who was it said so?”  

“If even you, Mr. Lee, shun me, can I count myself as anything other than the dead?” His cries continued without restraint.

“Then how shall we proceed?”  

“Love me!”  

“I cannot love you!”  

“Then kill me!”  

“You wish to make me a murderer?”  

“Then do as you please! What shall I do living this wretched life?”  

“Stop being so dramatic; let’s just go home. If you have any important matters to write in the letter, do so. Right now, what is this?” 

“Integrity? Dignity? Will those concern me when making my ultimate decision?”  

At that moment, the situation was indeed very awkward for both parties. Lee became concerned about how to appease Mr. An, who had been sobbing hard for over two hours, while Mr. An, too, felt he ought to keep up his demanding position. But Lee couldn't withstand it any longer. 

At last, he reached into his wallet of evasion. “Mr. An?”  

“Yes?”  

“I can’t stay here any longer.”  

“Why?”  

“Firstly, I feel so uneasy, secondly, I cannot bear it any longer, and thirdly, I’m acutely aware of the rôle of a member of the Youth Protection League.”  

“... ... ...”  

“If we are discovered here, just the two of us!”  

“What? Why are members of the Youth Protection League coming here?”  

“What do you mean? They come every so often to monitor these places! Did you not read it in the newspapers last summer about the police cracking down on these sorts of meetings?” 

“Those cowards! Who would believe that?” 

“If you don’t believe that, how can you stay here through the night?”  

“Not until I receive your approval.”  

At Lee's last remark, he was utterly dumbfounded. He gazed at Mr. An, who had been crying heavily, and stated, “Then I have to go. I am taking my leave.” Thus, he faded away into the darkness. 

Mr. An stared vacantly at the spot where he had hurriedly disappeared, thinking to himself… 

“How foolish I’ve been...”

“Men are all such cowards!"  

“Surely another name will soon be added to my list!”   

Unrequited Love Teacher!  

**Summary**

This story depicts the emotional turmoil of a teacher, Mr. An, who faces humiliation at a teachers' meeting and grapples with his self-image and societal expectations as an unmarried woman. His interactions with a male counterpart, Mr. Lee, reveal tensions around gender roles and the expectations of marriage during the colonial period in Korea. The narrative highlights the absurdity of societal pressures and the complexities of personal aspirations against the backdrop of societal norms, reflecting the struggles many women faced during this era.

Historians might find this article revealing for its representation of gender dynamics in colonial Korea, particularly the societal ridicule faced by women who remained unmarried and the limited agency they experienced in matters of love and marriage.
