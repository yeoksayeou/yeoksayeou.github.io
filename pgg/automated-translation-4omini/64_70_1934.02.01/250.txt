Long Children's Picture, Aryn's Adventure  
Yeon Seong-gyeom  

Aryn woke up in a strange place, the valley of Wadden Mountain, with her uncle, a wizard. Not noticing the heavy gems weighing down her pockets and pant legs, she ran home, thinking only of the stories she had to tell her mother. 

On the morning of the next day, Aryn woke up starving. She kicked off the blanket and called out, "Mother! I’m so hungry, please give me some rice!" Her mother replied weakly, "Oh dear, we've run out of money for rice and firewood since we’ve been hungry since yesterday..." After sighing deeply, her mother looked at Aryn with tear-filled eyes and said, "Even if I were to sell everything, I must save you..." 

Seeing her mother in such distress, Aryn suggested, "Mother! Forget the clothes; please bring the lamp I found. Perhaps it can be sold for money to buy rice..." Her mother picked up the old lamp and began to rub it diligently to see if it had any value. Suddenly, a terrifying spirit appeared, with two horns and bulging eyes, and said in a thunderous voice, "Why have you summoned me? I am a servant of the lamp. Whatever you command, I will obey." 

Aryn's mother fainted in fright. But Aryn, having previously dealt with such spirits in the cave, stood up calmly and ordered the spirit, "We are starving; bring us food!" At her command, the spirit bowed and disappeared in a puff of smoke. 

Aryn quickly went to the kitchen, wet a towel with cold water, and cared for her mother's head. As she tended to her, the spirit returned with a large silver tray filled with delicious dishes including stewed ribs, grilled pork, and a variety of rice cakes and dumplings. 

When Aryn's mother regained consciousness, she was astonished by the abundance of food and couldn't believe it was real. Aryn laughed heartily, explaining that the lamp was magical; whenever a difficult situation arose, she could simply rub it to summon the spirit who would help them. 

After eating their fill, Aryn's mother, still uneasy, suggested selling the lamp so as not to summon the frightening spirit again. But Aryn dismissed that idea. For a week, they lived comfortably by selling the silver and bronze dishes provided by the spirit, until finally, they commanded the spirit to provide food whenever they were hungry, with neither of them wanting for anything over a period of two years. 

One day, Aryn heard that the country’s princess was passing through their village to visit a famous hot spring. Having never seen the princess, Aryn was eager to catch a glimpse of her. 

When the princess arrived, Aryn saw her lovely face and was left in awe. Days later, Aryn approached her mother with an unusual request. "Mother! I saw the princess, and she is so beautiful. I wish to marry her! Please speak to the king on my behalf." 

Her mother replied, astonished, "Aryn, is it really right to speak such nonsense? How could you dare!" Although her mother found it ridiculous, she was taken aback by Aryn's sincerity. 

Aryn insisted, "Why would I be mad? The spirit of the lamp can help me! Plus, I’ve still got some jewels left from the cave to offer to the king as a present. Please, can you fetch me a large silver bowl to hold the jewels?" 

Reluctantly, her mother complied and brought back a large bowl. Aryn filled it with sparkling jewels, their brilliance dazzling even them as they marveled at their beauty. 

"Take this to the palace and present it to the king. If he sees this, my wish will be fulfilled!" 

The next morning, Aryn's mother set off toward the palace with the bowl of jewels. Upon arriving, the guard stopped her, saying, "You cannot enter the palace; state your business!" 

Aryn's mother explained that she had brought a rare precious offering for the king. The guard relayed her words to a higher-ranking officer, who immediately made arrangements for her entry. Soon, an official led Aryn's mother into the royal presence. 

Upon kneeling before the king, he commanded her to speak. Overwhelmed by the honor, she hesitated at first and then stammered, "Your Majesty, please forgive me for my humble appearance as I present this gift. But please allow me to express my wish for my son..." 

The king, listening patiently, inquired about her offering. Aryn's mother revealed the bowl filled with rare jewels, saying, "This treasure is something that cannot be found in this world. I present it to Your Majesty."

**Summary**  

The story recounts Aryn's adventure with a magical lamp that grants her and her mother the ability to overcome hardship through the assistance of a spirit. Using the lamp, they create a life of comfort, and eventually, Aryn hopes to marry a princess, believing that the spirit can help fulfill her wish. The narrative reflects themes of resilience, hope, and the dynamics of wealth in a colonial context, indicating the challenges faced by common people in Korea during the 1930s. Historians might find the portrayal of folkloric elements juxtaposed with desires for social mobility particularly interesting, shedding light on the cultural aspirations of Koreans under colonial rule.

그 이튼날 아츰이엇습니다. 아련이 어머니가 그 전날 접시에 담어두엇든 보석을 싸 가지고 대궐노 향햇습니다. 대궐문 압헤 일으러 대궐 안으로 드러가랴 하니까 파수 직히든 병정이 압흘 막어서면서
『여기는 한만이 들어가지 못하는 대궐인데 무엇하러 들어가랴 하시오.』
하고 물엇습니다. 아련이 어머니는 보석 담은 접시 보퉁이를 병정에게 내어 밀면서 『이것은 이 세상에선 어더볼 수 업는 훌령한 보석입니다. 이것을 임금님께 진상하러 온 길입니다.』
하고 대답하니까 그 파수병이 다른 병정 한 사람을 불너 가지고 무엇이라고 수군거리드니 병정 한 사람이 불이나케 대궐 안으로 뛰여들어갓습니다. 얼마 만에 두 팔에 금줄을 만히 둘는 군인(軍人) 한 사람이 나와서 아련이 어머니를 다리고 대궐 안으로 들어갓습니다. 그러니까 그 나라 총리대신(總理大臣)이 임금님 엽헤서 시중드는 벼슬아치 한 사람을 불너드려서
『이 부인을 상감님께로 인도해라』
하고 명령을 나렷습니다. 아련이 어머니는 그 벼슬아치의 뒤를 딸어서 임금님 룡상(龍床) 압헤까지 일으자마자 마루바닥에 코가 닷도록 업드려 절을 하니까
『일어나서 그대의 소원을 말하라』
하는 임금님의 분부가 나렷습니다. 아련이 어머니는 황송해서 읏지할 줄 몰나 허리는 굽힌채 『상감님! 천한 게집의 몸으로 여기에까지 일으른 것을 용서해 주십시오. 먼저 그것을 용서해 주실는지 그말슴 먼저 듯고자 합니다.』하고 말햇습니다. 그러니까 임금님은 『그대의 소원이 무엇인지 속히 말하라 모든 것은 다- 용서하는 것이니 여기에까지 일으른 그 목적(目的)을 숨기지 말고 말하라』
하고 말슴햇습니다. 그제야 아련이 어머니는 마음이 저윽이 노여서 아련이의 말대로 자기의 하나박게 업는 아들 아련을 가엽시 생각하야 사위를 삼어 달나고 애걸애걸 햇습니다.
아련이 어머니의 말을 들으시고도 임금님은 조곰도 성내시는 기색이 업시 물끄럼이 아련이 어머니를 나려다 보시드니
『그 보퉁이는 무엇인가?』
하고 물으섯습니다. 아련이 어머니는 보자기 속에서 보석이 수북하게 담긴 은접시를 끄내들고
『이것은 이 세상에서는 어더 볼 수 업시 귀한 보석이옵기로 상감님께 진상하랴고 가져왓습니다.』
하고 임금님께 바첫습니다.(게속)＜43＞
