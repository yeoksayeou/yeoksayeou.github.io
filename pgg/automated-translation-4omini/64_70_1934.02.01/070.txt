The term 'Kwandong' refers specifically to Gangwon-do, as it is a name derived from the region being adjacent to the Tumen River, which borders Hamgyeong-do at the Iron Ridge (철령). There is another explanation that suggests the majority of Gangwon-do lying east of Daegwanling leads to this name, but the former explanation seems more common.

'Kwan-seo' is an alternate name for Pyeongan-do, which also originates from its location to the west of Iron Ridge. Some say it is called this due to its proximity to Cheongseok Kwan-seo.

'Ryeong-dong' and 'Ryeong-seo' refer to the regions to the east and west of the Taebaek Mountain Range (脊梁山) in Gangwon-do. Ryeong-dong encompasses cities such as Gangneung, Yangyang, Wŏnju, Samcheok, Goseong, Tongch'ŏn, Pyeonghae, and various valleys, known as the 'Nine Cities of Ryeong-dong' previously. Ryeong-seo refers to the area west of the Taebaek Mountains, which includes cities like Chuncheon, Wonju, Hoengseong, Hongch'ŏn, Hwacheon, Inje, Geumhwa, Cheorwon, Pyeonggang, and others.

'Ryeong-nam' is another name for Gyeongsang-do, as the region's landscape is predominantly in the southern stretches of the Taebaek Mountain Range, including Autumn Wind Ridge, Island Ridge, and Bamboo Ridge. It is also sometimes referred to as 'Gyaom-nam.'

'Kwanbuk' is an alternate name for Hamgyeong-do. In Seoul, this term refers to the boundary marked by Iron Ridge, while within Hamgyeong-do, it refers to the area north of Hamgyeong Ridge, hence Kwanbuk for the north and Kwan-nam for the south.

'Hae-seo' serves as another name for Hwanghae-do, referring to the western part of Korea, meaning both the western part of the country itself and more narrowly, the western part of Gyeonggi-do.

'Hunan' is an alternate name for Jeolla-do, derived from the old name of the Geum River (錦江) being Lake River (湖江). There is another explanation suggesting it refers to the southern part of the Pyeongbuk Pool (碧骨池).

'Hoseo' is an alternate name for Chungcheong-do, referring specifically to areas around the Daechong Reservoir.

'Two Lakes' and 'Three Nams' refer to the two lakes mentioned above, 'Hunan' and 'Hoseo,' while 'Three Nams' represents the three provinces of Chungcheong, Jeolla, and Gyeongsang.

**Summary**  

The article provides a detailed account of various alternate names for the provinces in Korea during the colonial period, highlighting local geographical and cultural references contributing to these names. It reveals a rich tapestry of names grounded in the historical and geographical realities of Korea, showing how the landscapes and prominent features influenced local vernacular. Historians might find this interesting as it reflects the way Koreans engaged with their environment and how they shaped their identity, particularly in a time of colonial rule.

朝鮮各道의 別稱
關東=江原道를 別稱關東이라 하니 그것은 그 땅이 咸鏡道와 道界되는 鐵嶺關東에 잇는 까닭에 생긴 이름이다. 또 일설에는 江原道의 대부분이 大關嶺東에 잇는 까닭에 그리 부른다고도 하나 前說이 올흔것 같다.
關西=平安道의 別稱이니 역시 鐵嶺關西에 잇서서 하는 말이다. 혹은 靑石關西에 잇서서 그리 부른다고도 한다.
嶺東과 嶺西=江原道의 脊梁山大嶺(關嶺等) 以東을 嶺東이라 하니 卽 江陵, 襄陽, 蔚珍, 三陟, 高城, 通川, 平海, 峽谷 등은 전날에 소위 嶺東 九邑이요. 嶺西는 脊梁山嶺 以西의 地니 卽 春川, 原州, 橫城, 洪川, 華川, 麟蹄, 金化, 鐵原, 平康, 伊川 등등이다.
嶺南=慶尙道의 別稱이니 그 땅이 대개 秋風嶺 島嶺 竹嶺 등 大嶺南에 잇는 까닭이다. 또 혹은 嶠南이라고도 한다.
關北=咸鏡道의 別稱이니 서울에서는 대개 鐵嶺關을 標準하야 말하나 咸鏡道에서는 咸關嶺을 標準하야 그 北을 關北 그 南을 關南이라 한다.
海西=黃海道의 別稱이니 朝鮮의 西部 즉 海東國의 西部란 意味도 잇고 또는 좁게 말하면 京畿道海의 西部란 意味도 된다.
湖南=全羅道의 別稱이니 錦江의 舊名이 湖江인 까닭에 그리 말하는 것이다. 일설에는 또는 碧骨池의 南을 云함이라고도 한다.
湖西=忠淸道의 別稱이니 堤川義林湖를 標準하야 말하는 것이다.
兩湖와 三南=兩湖는 前記한 湖南湖西요, 三南은 忠淸, 全羅, 慶尙三道.＜9＞
