기사제목	유-모어掌篇, 電話 四四七一番 (Yū-mō-ā-chōpen, Telephone 4471)  
필자	崔公瑗 (Choi Gongwon)  
기사형태	소설 (Novel)  

He had seen others use the telephone many times, but this was his first experience doing so himself since birth. 

Confidence arises from experience - he had seen others handle the phone effortlessly, but when he was faced with it, he felt awkward and bewildered without any self-assurance. 

Before picking up the receiver, he pursed his lips and prepared to speak into the transmitter, as if about to pour out a stream of words. This was not because there were crucial words he needed to convey, but to ensure that he did not make a mistake in the order of the call.

Then, in order to clear his throat properly and facilitate the sound waves produced by the vibrations of his vocal cords to pass through the vast expanse of the universe, he gave a loud cough. Only after this did he finally lift the receiver to his ear.

"Bang..." 

"Oh, is anyone there?" 

No answer.

"Oh, is anyone there?" 

Then, a clicking sound came from the receiver, and his ears perceived a voice fluttering like a mosquito buzzing near him.

"Is this Nanbang (南方)?" 

In the midst of this transmission, his remarkable imagination stirred, and he thought that the clicking sound was made by the exchange operator entering the exchange room. 

"Ah, yes, this is 4471." 

"What is 'Dottle'? (どちら)" 

He was taken aback by the unfamiliarity of the word that seemed rather straightforward to him. 

"Is this 4471 you are calling?" 

The operator’s tone grew increasingly sharp compared to the earlier tones. 

"Is this 4471?" 

The thought crossed his mind that the operator must have misunderstood, thinking he was angry and quieted down. He scratched his head, wondering why the operator could not get it right while he looked again at the written number in his notebook.

-What does 'Dottle' refer to? The number is definitely 4471...- 

As a young man raised in a particular focus area, he was not so tone-deaf to Japanese; however, the simple word 'Dottle' puzzled him.

-Why isn't there any meaning in calling regarding 'Dottle'?-

No matter how he thought logically, the meaning of 'Dottle' in calling something did not exist for him. Thus, he pushed all the mistakes and misunderstandings onto the telephone operator and attempted to make a second call.

"Ah, is anyone there?" 

"Were you calling?" 

It was a different operator's voice.

"I'm calling from Yonsen (四仙), 4471." 

"What is 'Dottle'?" 

His back tightened again, but this time he was determined to find out what 'Dottle' meant.

"Since we have been calling from Yonsen (四仙), who is 'Dottle' in that case?" 

"Is this 4471 you are calling?" 

"Ah, I see, I see. The inner room (内室) then." 

Finally, he seemed to have understood the reason behind 'Dottle' with great satisfaction. Inside, he thought, 

-Then indeed, there wasn’t anything else.- 

"Eh?" 

"The inner room, Meishuku (明淑), the room!" 

"Where is that? Is it a district near Nanbaku (南博)? " 

And suddenly he felt profound dissatisfaction. How could anyone be so obtuse? 

"What do you mean?" 

There were no words to express his feelings. Not even a sound could be heard. He shook the receiver furiously, 

"Ah, is anyone there?" 

A faint sound came through again, a voice that resembled the one from before. 

"Were you there?" 

"Is this Nanbang (南方) again? What is the meaning of Yonsen (四仙) calling 4471? " 

"Ah, I finally understand now. I'll call again. Ah, Moshi moshi, Kan'tetsu-dō (貫鐵洞), 4471." 

"Yes!" 

"Yes, that is 4474 (四四七一)!" -End- <51>

**Summary**  

The protagonist experiences the complexities and frustrations of using a telephone for the first time, revealing his discomfort with language and communication. His struggle highlights the barriers of language for those in colonial contexts, especially as a Korean interacting with Japanese operators. This reflection on miscommunication speaks to the power dynamics and alienation experienced by Koreans during this period, providing historians with insight into the everyday challenges faced under colonial rule.
