艶福疾走記  
						金陵人  
... The place is the clean cultural house at Bukhansan in the great city of Korea, "Kyungsung"! It is the early modern spring afternoon of a clear day. In one corner of Seoul, unaware that this small play is taking place, cars are honking and streetcars are ringing as they go on their way.  
Well! The play begins in the inner room of this cultural house, where a young housewife welcomes a man who is coming back from a rural area.  

Woman: "Come on, take off your hat and settle in here."  

Man: "Is it all right to settle in?"  

Woman: "Yes. Just relax and you can consider it your home."  

Man: "But!"  

Woman: "What's holding you back?"  

Man: "Then where can I sit?"  

... The man, not knowing what to do, feels perplexed and finally squats down.  

Woman: "How do you find the room?"  

Man: "It's really nice. I've never been in such a room before."  

Woman: "Do you like the room?"  

Man: "Yes, it's very nice. I heard that rooms in Seoul are all narrow and cramped, but I never expected it to be so spacious and splendid."  

Woman: "So, you just came to Seoul?"  

Man: "Yes. I just came up from the countryside this morning."  

Woman: "Oh, is that so?"  

... The girl looks surprised and her bright eyes sparkle. Then, dropping her coat, she reveals her curves wearing only a thin and simple dress, while the man finds himself entranced.  

Woman: "Oh my! But how can you be so daring?"  

Man: "I... ...?"  

Woman: "Go on, say it."  

Man: "Am I being daring?"  

... The man appears quite naive.  

Woman: "Oh my! Isn't everything you are doing now daring?"  

Man: "Is this daring?"  

Woman: "Indeed."  

Man: "I was afraid I'd act clumsily."  

Woman: "No, no. It seems you don't understand what I'm saying. How can someone who is new to Seoul act so boldly and approach me in the middle of Jongno?"  

Man: "But I hesitated a long time before speaking..."  

Woman: "Still, these days it is rare to see a man with such daring, and I must say I am intrigued by your boldness. In other words, I must admit I've fallen for you."  

Man: "Haha."  

Woman: "Why are you laughing?"  

Man: "Oh, you are just kidding, right?"  

Woman: "What do you mean?"  

Man: "How could someone as beautiful and charming as you fall for a simple countryside fellow like me? That’s impossible."  

Woman: "Oh, but I mean it."  

Man: "Is it really true?"  

Woman: "Well, then what about it?"  

Man: "If I had known this, I would have spoken to you sooner! I was worried I might offend a lady."  

Woman: "Well then, do you think I’m a lady?"  

... The young woman becomes quite annoyed.  

Man: "A lady, you say? Haha."  

Woman: "Well then, what kind of person do you think I am?"  

Man: "Oh, I must have misunderstood. Then, um, pardon me."  

... The man starts to leave.  

Woman: "Where are you going?"  

Man: "I can't risk offending someone as refined as you. I should go."  

Woman: "Oh no! Stay and play with me! I sympathize with you."  

Man: "But..."  

Woman: "Why are you acting so stupidly? Just relax and stay here! Am I pretending to do this?"  

Man: "Oh, of course not."  

Woman: "Then why act like that?"  

Man: "If so, you really are that kind of woman?"  

Woman: "What kind?"  

Man: "Just that kind."  

Woman: "Hey, that's a disrespectful comment to make to a lady."  

... The man looks confused and moves toward the door again.  

Woman: "Where are you going?"  

Man: "I must take my leave! If you’re really a lady, I cannot stay here."  

Woman: "Haha, you really are a simple country bumpkin."  

... Suddenly, the woman’s anger melts away.  

... The man stands there, utterly bewildered.  

Woman: "Now listen, think of this as your home. But there is a condition. Until six o'clock, you can stay like this. My husband will come back from work then."  

Man: "Well, then I can think of you as my wife, right?"  

Woman: "Haha! Look at that! You really are bold."  

Man: "So until then, is it all right?"  

Woman: "Korean women nowadays are quite strange. To be so generous to a man I just met!"  

Man: "But..."  

Woman: "What is it?"  

Man: "But, you aren’t pretending, right?"  

Woman: "What do you mean? Haha."  

... The woman giggles as she playfully pokes the man.  

Woman: "Now, stop being like this and let's talk."  

Man: "Talk about what?"  

Woman: "How could you be so daring to talk to me, whom you met for the first time in the middle of Jongno?"  

Man: "Ah, that story. I can tell that story well."  

Woman: "Then hurry up and tell me."  

Man: "This morning, when I arrived at the Kyŏngsŏng station and rode the streetcar, there weren't many passengers. A gentleman approached me."  

Woman: "What did he say?"  

Man: "He asked, ‘Where can I see beautiful Seoul women?'"  

Woman: "What did he say?"  

Man: "He advised me to go to the middle of Jongno and just wait there for a few hours."  

Woman: "Oh, that’s quite funny."  

Man: "So I thought, why not ask again. ‘Actually, I want to flirt with beautiful women.’"  

Woman: "Wow, you're really bold."  

Man: "He then said, ‘Go right to Jongno and approach any woman who looks particularly exquisite, don’t hesitate.’ He said the ones with great hair and are dressed up are likely available.’”  

Woman: "And that’s how you ended up talking to me."  

Man: "Yes."  

Woman: "Haha, how amusing."  

Man: "So, is this what you call daring?"  

Woman: "You have succeeded in the first requirement that a woman seeks from a man."  

Man: "What success?"  

Woman: "Indeed a brilliant success! What kind of prize do you want?"  

Man: "Well..."  

Woman: "Yes. What is it?"  

Man: "How about a drink, poured by your hand?"  

Woman: "Why do you ask so timidly? Is it just a drink?"  

Man: "Ah, am I dreaming?"  

Woman: "Please don’t act foolishly, just come over here."  

... The young woman smiles sweetly, brightening her alluring lips as she beckons the man.  

Suddenly, they hear the sound of footsteps.  

Woman: "Oh no, it seems your husband is here, this is a big problem."  

Man: "Then what should we do?"  

... Imagine the look of utter bewilderment on the rustic man’s face at that moment.  

Woman: "Hurry and come to this table."  

Man: "Why?"  

Woman: "Hurry. Then pretend you are fixing the light. Act like you just came from the electric company..."  

Flustered, the man awkwardly approaches the table.  

Meanwhile, the husband enters the room.  

Woman: "Why are you here so early? You came back much sooner than usual."  

Husband: "Yes, I finished my work early today. Who is this person?"  

Woman: "He is from the electric company. I called because the light was dim and malfunctioning, I guess he just arrived."  

Husband: "Well done. That darn light has been flickering so much and it breaks easily."  

Woman: "Yes. So, today I called for service."  

... Up to this point, everything goes smoothly. But after this, it becomes chaos.  

Husband: "But..."  

Woman: "What is it?"  

Husband: "Why is he acting that way?"  

Woman: "Oh, what do you mean?"  

... The woman seems very nervous as if she has a guilty conscience.  

Husband: "Something still seems strange."  

Woman: "What do you mean?"  

Husband: "Come now, really take a good look."  

Woman: "Yes."  

Husband: "From the start, he has merely been fidgeting with the electric light."  

... At this moment, the rustic man’s heart drops, and without thinking, he raises his eyes to look at the husband’s face.  

Then, their eyes meet, with one man looking down and the other looking up.  

Man: "Ah?"  

Husband: "Oh?"  

Woman: "Oh, my, what’s going on?"  

Husband: "It seems this person is the same one I saw on the streetcar when he came up from the countryside."  

Woman: "What?"  

... In that moment, the rustic man bolts for the door. The woman’s face turns pale, and she breathes heavily, standing there still.  

Husband: "Oh, so that is how it turned out."  
 
Summary  

This play illustrates a humorous encounter between a naive young man from the countryside and a modern woman in Seoul. The man, intrigued by the woman's boldness, engages in witty banter, ultimately revealing societal contrasts. It highlights the evolving gender dynamics and societal expectations of the time in Korea under colonial rule, providing insights into the cultural changes and social interactions of the 1930s. Historians may find the interplay of traditional values and modern behaviors depicted in the dialogue particularly revealing of the complexities during this period of transition in Korean society.
