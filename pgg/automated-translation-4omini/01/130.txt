A Bug’s Words

Ignorant humans, in trying to imitate me, waste their money on flight practice and end up making mistakes that cost them their lives; it is truly ridiculous. Buzz buzz! Oh, you fake buzz! What shall military flights accomplish? (dragonfly)

On long nights of October, without even a grain of rice, you sleep soundly while suffering the cold winds and snow in winter. Alas, how pitiful you are! (cricket)

In the sweltering heat of June, unfeeling humans hate me like enemies, carelessly using fly traps and poisons; while I have no fort to protect myself, my little offspring cry out as their hair is pulled away, and their hands and feet are cleared away. Truly pitiful. Will these people now consider my thoughts? (fly)

What foolishness there is, tying up and playing around with nothing but the rooftop while the common folks live. The so-called patriots and writers have no house of their own and only roam about suggesting ideas from others; indeed, it is quite pitiful. (snail)

Sucking the blood of the impoverished like a voracious predator, even the fat rich folks, upon being bitten once, call me a blood-sucking bug and a vile bug, making a fuss without the slightest hint of shame. Do they not possess even a small sense of moral embarrassment? Slyly this way! Slyly that way! (bloodsucker)

Dressed in fine French garments, at a time when I walk gracefully in the slanting sunlight after the rain, the prostitutes, who appear for a moment to resemble me with a refined demeanor, upon closer inspection, engage in all sorts of shameful activities. Oh, don’t! Don’t say that! Prostitution! Prostitution! (cicada)

It is common for people to trap others in the name of law, but when people set traps with silk to catch me, they are horrified, thinking it vile and trying to hinder it. In human society, there seems to be no original justice or public opinion. It is forbidden this way! It is forbidden that way! (spider)

If people fail to utilize nature, such as electricity or oil, they cannot discern even a foot in front of them at night, and yet those who claim, “Among all things, humans are the most precious,” are indeed the most foolish creatures. Will they praise me when they see me? Here a flicker! There a flicker! (firefly)

Those city dwellers who live with food and clothing made from the kindness of farmers yet look down on them are indeed detestable and pitiful. If you do not wish to perish, try at least to spend a day alongside me, running along the rice paddies, mingling among the grass. Return to the land, back to farming! (cricket)  

<76>  

**Summary**

This article presents a series of criticisms and observations articulated from the perspective of various insects, showcasing societal issues and human folly in colonial Korea. Through the metaphor of bugs, the author critiques human behavior regarding exploitation, moral indifference, and the relationship between the city and rural life. Historians may find the commentary on colonial attitudes toward both social class and rural labor particularly revealing during this era of social upheaval and cultural change.

밤낫으로 貧民의 피만 빠러 먹고 배가 南山 가티 불은 부자 놈도 내가 한번 저를 물면 吸血虫이니 惡虫이니 하고 야단법석을 함부로 치며 양철홈도 대고 除虫藥을 뿌리니 그 놈에게는 良心의 부끄러움이 조곰도 업슬가 이리 살살! 저리 살살! (貧代)

불란사 겹저고리에 한산 모시 치마를 멥시잇게 떨처입고 雨後 斜陽에 행똥거리고 情夫를 차저 돌아 다니는 틀에머리 蕩女子는 잠간보면 나와 가튼 仙風道骨의 態가 잇다만은 내막을 조사하면 별별 소행이 다 잇구나. 아서라-마러라-네 그리 마러라-賣淫! 賣淫! (蟬)

법으로 그물을 처서 사람이 사람을 잡는 것은 尋常히 넉이지만은 실(絲)로 그물을 처서 벌어지를 잡아 먹는 것은 사람놈들이 흔이 惡하게 생각하야 방해를 만히 하니 인간 사회에는 원래 正義 公論이 다 업는 것 갓다. 이리 엄금! 저리 엄금! (蜘蛛)

전기나 석유 등의 자연을 이용치 못하면 밤즁에는 지척도 不辨하는 인생들이 자칭왈 萬物之中에 惟人最貴라고 입으로만 떠드는 것을 보면 사람이란 참으로 우순 동물이다. 나를 볼때에 그들이 불어워 하지나 안을가. 여긔서도 번젹! 저긔서도 번적! (螢)
