기사제목	對話 暗室鶯歌, 『輿論』社 編輯室의 一日生活記 (Dialogue: The Song of the Nightingale in the Dark Room, A Day in the Editorial Office of "Opinion")

기사형태	대담·좌담 (Dialogue)

Dialogue: The Song of the Nightingale in the Dark Room, A Day in the Editorial Office of "Opinion"  
Stenographer  
(Location - indoor)  
(Charachters - all male and female reporters)  

Pyeongseok. (Sighs heavily, while editing a manuscript) Ah, autumn has already come!  

(Silence)  

Chunsu. (Glancing at the female reporter while writing) Ah! I can already hear the cry of the crickets.  

Hwaseong. (In a calm tone) Why is it that they start chirping early in the morning today? Don't they usually stay quiet?  

Chunsu. Well, they are male crickets, so they chirp. If widows cried like that, wouldn’t it stir the family up?  

Hwaseong. What the heck? It’s concerning because they’re widowers. What do we care if it’s the same with widows? If a man dies, that’s it, isn’t it? It’s the widowers that cannot be dealt with, you see.  

Chunsu. Well, if we can't handle widowers, how will widows be handled? For a pair to come together, there need to be both widows and widowers, right? (Glances subtly at the female reporter)  

Hwaseong. You are right. Let's stop this.  

Pyeongseok. (Laughing) Was it a lingering resentment today? What's up with this?  

Hwaseong. Chunsu claims the theory, but whether right or wrong, he’s adamant he's the victor here. What’s the big deal if I lose?  

Akhwa. (Waking up halfway) Haha!  

On this day, Hwaseong, you're in a bad spot.  

Female Reporter. (Smiling) What's the matter with you today, Hwaseong?  

Hwaseong. (Now quite stirred) Oh no, I’m always in pain. Why do these people try so hard to be victorious in a casual chat?  

Female Reporter. (Looking a bit surprised) Why do you say that? Is there something unusual... ?  

Hwaseong. If you're asking that much... If you think about the life of a Korean person—  

Chunsu. (In a mocking tone) Another ‘patriot’ has emerged. Such patriots will act in love wickedly, I'm sure.  

Pyeongseok. That’s not it. Generally, when women start to talk, society spirals into chaos because they don’t know what they’re doing, causing pointless topics to arise. Just like a patriot... Is that how love is going to go well?  

Female Reporter. (In silence and deep thought)  

Hwaseong. (Growing increasingly excited) You people! Are you really insulting me like this?  

Chunsu. (Looking at the female reporter again) Tsk. If they take it as an insult, they really are narrow-minded. Is there a rule against falling in love?  

Female Reporter. (Her face reddening a bit) So what does that mean? Do you think women are mere things without knowing anything, thinking nothing, because they act like patriots? What do you mean by that?  

Hwaseong. (Calming down a bit) Right. Those who are concerned with the present condition tend to look down on women.  

Chunsu. (Having gotten excited owing to his prevailing emotions without knowing what to say) Do you feel empowered now? It's all because of that, you see! You can see clearly the results it produces!  

Female Reporter. Is it that exciting? Why would you become so agitated?  

Chunsu. Well, it’s really the man who gets riled up, not I. Wasn’t I just saying how the crickets are chirping? He’s just randomly got riled up, acting like a patriot! Don’t sympathize with that kind of person more, please. That just fuels the fire.  

Hwaseong. Well, what do you mean by that? What more is there for me to do?  

Akhwa. Oh! I could eat more! The food was just good! Seriously, you people are so good-natured. Isn’t life better than this, even if it’s just a little bit?  

Everyone bursts out laughing.  

Gaptuk. Hwaseong, please answer the phone.  

Hwaseong. Oh! Did someone call for me? (Stepping forward)  

Pyeongseok. Oh, Chunsu! This has gone too far, hasn’t it?  

Female Reporter. Oh! You too, dear! Losing or winning, does it matter?  

Santang. (Coming back into the room) Oh, indeed today something is off!  

Pyeongseok. What do you mean? Is it about the conditions?  

Santang. The money condition is completely off! Oh! That’s it! Did I miss any wood or rice? Ah!  

Hwaseong. (Returning and sitting in his seat, with a gloomy demeanor)  

Pyeongseok. What's wrong with you, Hwaseong? It looks like you're feeling down today.  

Hwaseong. Ah! Please! Don’t tease me by calling me a patriot anymore! I heard that A is dead in prison. And N is in critical condition...  

Everyone is shocked.  

Everyone. What? A has died? Oh! What a terrible person has died.  

Hwaseong. That’s right! The unfortunate has died, and the smart ones are caught! All that remains are the ill-fated ones!  

Chunsu. (Feeling touched) Now, I truly agree with that patriot's words!  

Akhwa. Ah! It’s so good!  

Hwaseong. They are all gone. Those who are remaining are just left to die--.  

Chunsu. Still, did you at least eat before coming this morning?  

Hwaseong. What good is that?  

Chunsu. What’s the point in making ourselves feel poor through talk like that? Come! Let’s have a chat. (Looks at the female reporter) Isn't that so?  

Female Reporter. (Repulsively) Yes, it is so. But for some reason, I feel like talking about absolutely nothing lately. My body is terribly tired. (Yawns) Ah! So tired!  

Chunsu. Then why not get some exercise?  

Pyeongseok. No way. Because you’re saying get some mountain exercise--what you need is to rest a bit!  

Chunsu. (Turning a bit red) Well, that is true! If you exercise, you’ll be much better off.  

Hwaseong. Oh! This is agonizing! Nothing is going well!  

Chunsu. Rather, you need to do it more! You must fight!—  

Hwaseong. Looks like you have set your mind right! Why can one get more of this or that?  

Chunsu. What’s anything at all? Exercise, love or whatever is necessary!  

Pyeongseok. This friend only talks love, leaving everything else aside.  

Female Reporter. Ah! I must go!  

Chunsu. Why are you hurrying to leave? We’re all bored, isn't it okay to just chat?  

Female Reporter. I truly have nothing to talk about anyways.  

Chunsu. I’ll give you something to talk about!  

Female Reporter. (Reluctantly) Fine.  

Pyeongseok. Ah! It’s so boring.  

Hwaseong. (Sighs heavily)  

Santang. (Just puffing smoke from a cigarette without saying anything.)  

Gaptuk and Akhwa are still napping without waking up.  

(Silence)  

Female Reporter. (Quietly whistles a mournful tune)  

Hwaseong. Teacher!  

Female Reporter. Yes?  

Hwaseong. The world is like this...  

Chunsu. ... this lonely...?  

Pyeongseok. ... this lonely on this vast plain...?  

Santang. When in solitude...  

Hwaseong. When in solitude, the person I long for...  

Pyeongseok. Haha... that ‘person I long for’ is coming out, I see.  

Hwaseong. By the way, why are you all so quick to talk and laugh at what others say?  

Female Reporter. Hwaseong, please say it again.  

Hwaseong. Yes! What did I say?  

Female Reporter. 'The world is like this...' you said.  

Hwaseong. Yes, yes! That’s right. When the world is so lonely...  

Female Reporter. Yes—  

Hwaseong. People who know the heart—  

Female Reporter. Yes—  

Hwaseong. And take comfort in each other—  

Female Reporter. Yes—  

Hwaseong. ‘... ... ... ...’  

Female Reporter. Yes?  

Hwaseong. I have an urge.  

Female Reporter. Are you speaking to me?  

Hwaseong. Yes, it’s for you.  

Female Reporter. ‘... ... ... ...’  

Hwaseong. If nothing else, please sing us one song. It’s too quiet--.  

Female Reporter. You’d have to know how to do it well.  

Chunsu. (Suddenly stepping out) The music speaks, so even if you do it poorly, it would be fine—  

Female Reporter. Oh are you sure about that?  

Hwaseong. Shh! Don’t chatter. Sing—  

Female Reporter. (Her face getting a bit red) If you’re so bored, well then, I’ll try even if it’s a bad sound.  

Everyone is tense.  

(Quietly, the sound comes out.)  
  
“In the tranquil night, beneath the stars,  
When my lonely heart sways in the breeze,  
The distant past comes back to me once again.”  

Hwaseong. How beautiful! That indeed reminds me of the song of the nightingale in the dark room! Please continue!  

Female Reporter. I can’t! I’m too exhausted.  

Hwaseong. I see, I see.  

Female Reporter. I must go. That’s enough. (Tries to leave)  

Chunsu. But I have a complaint for the teacher.  

Female Reporter. Yes?  

Chunsu. For some reason, when I asked you yesterday, you refused, but now you agree to Hwaseong’s request, is it because of your patriot act?  

Female Reporter. Haha! Please forgive me. I refused because there was someone I didn’t know there yesterday! But you shouldn't misunderstand.  

Chunsu. What if it’s just between us? Isn't it alright to misunderstand a bit?  

(Female Reporter exits)  

Hwaseong. Ah! Once again today, time has flown by. The day has passed so well.  

Pyeongseok. Come! Why are you all here, waiting about?  

Hwaseong. This is how we spend our time, idling away!  

Pyeongseok. When it’s time, it will come! Oh what an unending day!  

Santang. Let’s go then.  

Hwaseong. I’m going too—  

Chunsu. Hwaseong, don’t misunderstand now.  

Hwaseong. Haha! We’re just joking here! However, even if misunderstood, there wouldn’t be any fierce fight! Haha! Talking about this, Gaptuk! Just a little while longer, wake up and go! You too, Akhwa, please just go home and sleep.  

Everyone exits.  

**Summary**  

This dialogue showcases the interactions among journalists during a quiet day in their office, displaying their perspectives on life under colonial rule and the poignant issues faced by their contemporaries. Their conversations oscillate from humorous banter to serious reflections on societal issues, including the fates of political activists, revealing the emotional toll of colonial oppression, and their profound sense of loss regarding friends and fellow patriots. Historians might find this exchange significant as it encapsulates the anxieties and cultural exchanges of intellectuals in colonial Korea, portraying their struggles with identity and societal expectations in a changing world that is aware of the tragic realities around them.
