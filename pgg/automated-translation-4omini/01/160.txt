統計室  
As of the end of last year, the cultivated land area in Korea is 1,563,736 chō for rice paddies (畓) and 2,784,618 chō for fields (田), totaling 4,348,354 chō. When distinguishing between self-cultivated land and rented land, there are 548,208 chō of self-cultivated rice paddies and 1,015,527 chō of rented rice paddies, while in fields there are 1,601,417 chō of self-cultivated and 1,183,200 chō of rented land. This means that in fields that produce poor quality crops, self-cultivated and rented land are roughly equal; however, in the rice paddies that yield the best quality rice, about two-thirds of the land belongs to landlords.

As is generally known, the total area of Korea is 143,124 square ri (it should be understood that one square ri refers to a square 1 ri). This is divided into 13 administrative divisions, which vary in size. For example, the North Hamgyeong Province occupies 2,073 square ri as a single province, whereas South Chungcheong Province is only 481 square ri. Currently, there are 12 districts (府), 2 islands (島), 218 counties (郡), 2,504 townships (面), and 28,300 villages (洞 and 里) in all of Korea. However, the exact number of villages is unknown, but it is important to remember the number of districts and counties.

In terms of land composition regarding Korea's total area of 14,312 square ri, forestry occupies 15,883,000 chō, cultivated land is 4,348,344 chō, and the area of miscellaneous land is 2,053,000 chō. Uncultivated land comprises approximately 70,000 chō. In comparison to the total area, forests account for about 91.4%, rice paddies 7% (i.e., 7/100), fields 12.4%, and miscellaneous land 9.2%. The cultivated area per farming household is 1 chō 6 dan, which is 5 dan more than Japan's 1 chō 1 dan. However, considering the current agricultural situation, as of the end of last year, out of the 2,693,241 farming households in Korea, 1,135,244 households are impoverished tenant farmers who do not even own a pole to plant.

So, what is the agricultural condition in Korea? According to the number of farming households, there are 2,731,177 households of Koreans with a population of 14,601,269, while 9,483 households belong to non-Koreans with a population of 39,560. In terms of self-cultivated and rented land: there are 103,567 landlord households, 544,536 self-cultivating households, 910,178 households that both self-cultivate and rent, and 1,184,422 tenant farming households. This indicates that there are nearly 40,000 non-Korean farmers in Korea, and it is evident that tenancy is most prevalent.

  
**Summary**  

This article presents statistical data regarding agricultural land distribution and farming households in Korea as of the end of the previous year, highlighting the disparities between self-cultivated and rented land. A significant portion of the farmers are tenants struggling to make a living, which showcases the pressing difficulties faced by the rural population under Japanese colonial rule. Historians may find the ratio of tenant farmers compared to landlords particularly revealing of the socio-economic conditions during this period, indicating a significant level of exploitation.
