발행일	1930년 05월 01일  
기사제목	結婚의 最尖端 友愛結婚 이약이 (The Cutting Edge of Marriage: Companionate Marriage)  
기사형태	문예기타 (Other Literature)  

The term "Companionate Marriage" often comes with a sense of self-mockery. In Korea, even disregarding whether it is actually implemented, understanding this new trend could be valuable for future reference. Now, I would like to introduce part of a book called *Companionate Marriage*, authored by the American juvenile judge Linze, who serves as the main advocate and promoter of Companionate Marriage.

One day, while enjoying the sunset, I was taking a quiet break when my friend C arrived. He seemed disturbed, as if some significant event had occurred, and the moment he entered, he immediately spoke.  
"B, Agnes got married without telling us today! So I came to consult you... what can we do about it?"

Agnes is C's seventeen-year-old daughter, attending her first year at the nearby coeducational university. I have known her since she was young, and as she grew up, she started showing interest in court cases, often coming to chat with me. 

She was a girl with considerable charm, full of vitality and health, blending well with her surroundings.  

I took C into the study to hear the details of the situation.  
"About an hour ago, she called to say she got married this afternoon. I don’t think it’s in this district, but I don’t know where."  

"So... what did she say on the phone?"  

"She didn’t say much! I was so shocked that I couldn’t even speak. But she didn’t really ask for permission... I told her, ‘It’s regrettable that you didn’t consult me beforehand, but if you are happy, then I’ll be happy too,’ and she seemed quite proud of it. But B, the man she married is just a third-year student, a very young kid! I don’t know the details, but he could be a delinquent. Do you think she has the ability to choose a man rightly at the age of seventeen? If he turns out to be useless, can she get a divorce?"

"Divorce is easy, but there’s no need to be overly anxious. I understand your daughter better than you do. What Agnes did might actually be very wise. It’s clear that she is very rational. When a girl like her feels the instinct for marriage, it’s better to let her follow it. Remaining single isn't always the best solution and can lead to various troubles, even affecting her morals."  

(Here, Linze further explained something.)  
I had heard from the principal of the coeducational university that young couples who get married perform better academically compared to their unmarried counterparts, and that the rising marriage rate among young students is viewed as the best solution to problems arising from societal pressures on sexuality. Linze also agreed with this principal, stating that modern civilization has kept men and women single for too long, which has led to many troubles. Those from wealthy families may have no trouble getting married, while the poor cannot. If they don't climb the social ladder step by step, they won’t be able to do so. So if Agnes marries the man she loves, they will have to wait five or six years until they can live independently after graduation. Can they really last together happily and support each other every day for that long? Is that rational? Obviously not. Therefore, what Agnes did is justified.  

By doing so, both will improve their health, intellect, and efficiency at work... According to psychoanalysts, there’s a concept called the "Father Image," which suggests women choose lovers who resemble their fathers, so Agnes must have chosen a guy who shares little with C. Waiting to see the outcome and then acting slowly is better.  

After hearing this, C remained uneasy and said,  
"Perhaps the young man just married Agnes because he failed to seduce her. Maybe he took advantage of her youth and inexperience like this?! If we wait to make a proper judgment, it might take a long time, and this situation might ruin my daughter’s life, which would break my heart."

"But getting divorced is surely straightforward. If they claim legal invalidity of the marriage until she is twenty-one, it can always be annulled, so there's no need to worry so much... But what does your wife think?"  

"She's almost in shock. Today she probably planned to consult with you, but it seems she couldn’t since she has always opposed your idea of Companionate Marriage."  

"Oh, really?"  

"It doesn’t really concern me whether it’s your concept of Companionate Marriage or not, but I must worry about how it has come to this."  

"I hope you can look into this and see what's going on. Try to bring her along."  

"That sounds good."  

"Another difficult issue is... how are they going to live? Agnes mentioned on the phone that the young man seems to be living off what his parents send him and whatever he earns. Is Agnes going to care for him like she did while she was single? Isn’t it unreasonable for a guy to marry someone’s daughter without being able to provide for them? If Agnes is small, though she gave her consent, it’s concerning."  

"That’s true; they should be fine."  

At this response, C appeared stunned, looking at me with wide eyes, and continued speaking.  
"How can that be fine? What if they have a child next year? Shouldn’t I be the one to support them then?"  

"That’s not a problem. They surely know about family planning laws."  

"When you go home, make sure to take her to the doctor to learn the methods well. If no children come while they are establishing their lives together, this marriage can’t be considered a failure. And if their relationship doesn’t work out, they can simply divorce. There is only an issue of children if children are born."  

"So that’s fine then. Why don’t they create such laws?"  

"That’s precisely Companionate Marriage."  

"Huh? I thought all along that it was called trial marriage."  

"Of course, it can be easy to view it as trial marriage. However, comparing the original essence of Companionate Marriage with what Agnes did, the differences would be the absence of a safe mechanism for mutual divorce, and it does not allow legal family planning. Additionally, there is no medical examination of the health of the marriage partners. However, I am not advocating for the establishment of this new system called Companionate Marriage, rather I am proposing to legalize the existing trial marriages. Socially, we need legal recognition, and between couples, we must agree not to have children during the designated period. In humanity's primitive times, there were no marriages in which couples consciously decided not to have children, but in modern times, marriage systems that avoid childbearing should be acknowledged."  

"Haha. I see... After being married for three years, I couldn’t raise children, so I practiced family planning. That is Companionate Marriage! Then I should tell my wife to hurry and have a proper discussion."  

With that, C left cheerfully, satisfied with the response. After a couple of days, Linze met with the couple, offering various advice.  

The above describes an example of Linze's Companionate Marriage, and he emphasized in his book the distinction between Companionate Marriage and trial marriage. That is, trial marriage is merely a temporary union resulting from careless free love, while Companionate Marriage aims for permanent union from the beginning.  

**Summary**  

This article discusses the concept of Companionate Marriage, as highlighted through the experiences of a father, C, who learns that his daughter Agnes has married a young man without prior consultation. Linze's explanation embraces the idea of rationality in marriage decisions and breaks down the relationship between education, economic viability, and societal pressures on youth. What may intrigue historians studying Korean colonial history is the intersection of modern ideas of marriage and family within a colonial context, reflecting societal changes in the face of Western influence during the 1930s.

어느날 夕陽에 나는 하로일을 맛치고 잠잠ㅅ간 쉬이느라 닛가 친구 C군이 차저왓다. 그는 무슨 중대한 사건이 생겻는지 顔色이 매우 당황하여 가지고 방안에 들어서자 마자 대번 말을 끄냇다.
『B군(린제-) 아그네스가 오늘 제멋대로 결혼을 해바렷네 그려! 그래 자네한톄 相議해서 ... 무슨 도리가 잇슬가 하고 온겐데. ... ...』

남의 정을 끄를 만큼 꽤 風致가 잇는 소녀로 잘 調和된 건강미와 活潑한 生氣를 가추어 잇섯다.

이러케 C君은 만족한 대답을 엇고 빙글빙글 우스며 도라갓다. 그뒤 2,3일이 지나서 린제-는 아그네스 夫婦를 맛나 여러가지 注意를 들녀주엇다.
