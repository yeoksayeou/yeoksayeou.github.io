공연히 그리심니다  
Very fat Judge asked the names of three criminals, A, B, and C.  
Judge: "A, what is your name?" Though he asked A, his gaze was directed at B.  
B: "I am XXX."  
The judge angrily responded, "You rascal, I didn't ask you!" and shouted. Then C said, "It's irrelevant for me to talk; I haven't said anything." <63>  

**Summary**  

This article features a short humorous exchange in a courtroom setting, highlighting the absurdities that can occur in communication and authority figures. The portrayal of the judge and the defendants could reflect the social dynamics under colonial rule, emphasizing both the confusion and tension in judicial interactions of the time, which historians might find illuminating about societal attitudes and the colonial legal system.
