개똥 『알기는 하지만 말은 아니 합니다.』  
선생 『왜?』  
개똥 『나는 배우러 왓지 선생님을 가르쳐 드리러 오진 안했으니까요.』<115>  

**Summary**  

This article presents a humorous exchange between a teacher and student during class, illustrating the student's reluctance to answer a question about the highest mountain in Korea. The student's response reflects a societal attitude of prioritizing learning over simply recalling information for the teacher. The humor intertwined with the lesson underscores the nuances of education during the colonial era, hinting at the resistance to authoritarian educational approaches prevalent at the time. Historians might find this an interesting snapshot of the attitudes towards education in Korea under Japanese colonial rule, highlighting a young generation's pursuit of knowledge amidst external pressures.
