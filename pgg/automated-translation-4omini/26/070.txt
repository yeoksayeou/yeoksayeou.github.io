메리듸트의 結婚觀  
George Meredith (1838-1907), a British poet and novelist, believes that marriage should be contracted for a period of five years. After living together during this first five years, if both parties think they can continue living together, they may renew their contract for another five years, thus forming a second term. By continuing this process of renewing the contract every five years, when both spouses reach the age of 50, they would not be allowed to separate from each other.  

  
**Summary**  

In this article, the author presents George Meredith's unconventional perspective on marriage, proposing a contract-based system that allows couples to reevaluate their relationships every five years. This notion of temporary marriage contracts rather than lifelong commitments highlights shifting attitudes toward marriage in the early 20th century. Historians may find it interesting as it reflects broader themes of personal autonomy and evolving familial structures during the time of colonial Korea and the changing social landscape influenced by Western thought.
