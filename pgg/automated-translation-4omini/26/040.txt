기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)  

Now, regarding the new students who have recently graduated, having spent a long time in the school where they were burdened by studies and held grand aspirations, they are finally stepping out into society as workers. Upon leaving the school gate, the first issues they all grapple with are employment and marriage. While many have found solutions to job-related concerns, the question of marriage is one that they will have to resolve themselves in time. What ideals do they hold regarding marriage?

Understanding male perspective   
Anbyeong Dental College, An Bok-hee  

Though I have not yet seriously thought about the marriage problem, it is undoubtedly a matter that will soon arise. As for a spouse, perhaps the fundamental condition is mutual understanding. Since we will be together, it wouldn't be realistic to have unreasonable demands or expectations.  

Of course, since I am a dentist, it would also be good for my partner to have a relevant profession. A joyful personality would be ideal, and I wouldn’t mind too much about their living standards. I think it would be better if the man is a bit older.  

As for the marriage ceremony, my family still follows traditional customs, so I suppose we would proceed in that old-fashioned manner. Though it may seem cumbersome, I enjoy that classical flavor it brings. Whether my partner has been married before or not doesn’t matter. If we get too hung up on those things, we might end up without getting married at all!  

Modern women are a big no-no  
Anbyeong Dental College, Eun Joong-ki  

Well, I wonder how on earth one would eat, so it seems I have to get married. ... I am an ordinary fellow, and I would need a wife who knows how to manage household matters well – in other words, someone not too modern.  

If I were to enter a family where they have a modern woman, that could lead to all sorts of trouble.  

The age? Just around 19 or 20 would be fine. As for the ceremony, since I am not a child of heaven, I cannot go to a church, and traditional customs seem like silly games, so I wonder if I should choose a social wedding or something like that. If there are other good customs to follow, I'll consider those.  

I want to marry a female teacher  
Yeonhee College Student  

Though there are those who say that writing students are full of love affairs, I have never had any experience in love to date. School is finished, and it’d be nice to get married at least once, but I’m not even inclined towards that idea. While one must marry (it is necessary), it feels like separating a mother from her daughter when sending her off.  

Well... if I were to choose a partner, I would prefer to marry a female teacher from a girls' school.  

Moreover, they shouldn't be too good-looking. If they are beautiful, they would mock me for my own looks. I wish they would go a path above mine.  

The feelings that a woman who takes care of me well brings are indeed wonderful when I think about it. Regarding the ceremony, I will go with whatever she prefers, but if it comes down to my choice, I would want to get married in a church as a Christian.  

Flexible women  
Law College, Ha In-yong  

I haven’t really thought about marriage yet. ... Well, my partner should be an active and flexible woman. Not a modern one, heavens... with my appearance, I doubt a modern woman would seek me out! ...  

I prefer a gentle woman who has a resilient spirit when challenged.  

As for the age, I think someone a year or two younger, around 21 or 22, would be appropriate.  

Regarding the ceremony... Well, while I seem classic on the outside, I inherently lean towards modernity and cannot keep doing what everyone else does. If I ever get married, I would create an innovative and surreal marriage ceremony.  

But genuinely, are you asking if I would like an introduction arranged for me?  

Country maidens  
Advanced Industrial College, S.K.Y  

It is said that humans are motivated by food and sex; thus, I think the problem of marriage is not so peculiar. I mean, I haven't given it much serious consideration.  

If it were my choice, I would seek a countryside maiden as my wife. Although these modern girls act so brazenly, the spouse of a regular working-class man – that is to say, my wife, would surely be a countryside maiden.  

If she is honest, decent, and has a lovely appearance, that would be perfect! I briefly dated some students, but because I was not modern enough, I lacked the ambition.  

In fact, women in this transitional period of liberation seem rather brash and, truthfully, resemble hollow balloons inside.  

As for age, a countryside maiden would likely be under 20.  

For the ceremony? Since I am not a churchgoer, it feels uncomfortable to attend a service, and a social wedding feels awkward. I suppose I would prefer a classical Korean style rich with tradition. Marriage ceremonies should be conducted with solemnity.  

This is merely my current ideal (and being an ideal, I'm not sure if it will turn out that way).  

A 100% modern woman  
Busan College Student, R  

As for a spouse? It’s rather contradictory to even speak of such!  

A young person dating shouldn’t be constrained to pick a marriage ideal target, right?  

But if I must choose...   

I will go for a 100% modern woman. Of course, by modern, I don’t mean an unhealthy kind; it means a wholesome modern.  

She will undoubtedly be clear-minded, cheerful, and lively, and can live every day with a bright perspective, free from gloomy feelings.  

Regarding age, around 18 to 21 is the best fit.  

As for the ceremony? The ceremony isn't even a matter to consider.  

I reject conventions and deny all traditions that tie me down. I can’t picture myself dragging myself to a church to illustrate a God I don’t believe in, or participating in old-style Korean weddings.  

These days, there are social weddings, but those are just ridiculous. Perhaps a wedding in the woods like Hwang Seok-yu’s stylized wedding? Ha ha... that’s just a joke. I don’t plan on having a ceremony at all.  

My marriage ideal  
Pharmacy College, KCH  

If all the conditions I desire are met along with mutual affection and feelings, I would prefer to engage in a normal relationship for over a year. I would look for the strong and weak points of my partner, and if I have qualities to compensate for those weaknesses, I might go further with love if it arises.  

Marriage, being a significant aspect of a person’s life, deserves serious consideration, and making a swift decision seems implausible.  

As for the marriage age, it’s tough to determine; if there are no candidates, I might remain single for life. Medicine suggests a woman is ideal around 25 years or older, while those who value beauty may find an older bride lack fit. As a compromise, how about 22 or 23?  

I think it would be nice to reform traditional customs for the ceremony.  

During the courtship phase, I would want to assess how understanding they are and how well they observe etiquette, especially towards the rich and the poor alike.  

Knowledge does not matter whether they graduated from school or are self-taught; all I expect is common sense and competency. I don't necessarily need a scholar, but they should understand literature and be able to express their feelings.  

As for character, I prefer a woman to have a spirited nature. Energetic while remaining reflective seems perfect. I dislike criticism while remaining lively.  

In terms of appearance, I tend to seek someone to supplement my own shortcomings. As for the type of profession, it doesn’t matter much, but they should have a steady job without being a burden.  

**Summary**  

The article explores the marriage ideals of a group of graduates in 1930s Korea, revealing a diversity of views among modern men and women regarding their future partners. While some wish for traditional values and roles, others seek out modernity and individuality, highlighting the tensions between old customs and emerging modern ideals. The reflections provide a window into the social dynamics and values of the time, revealing how the colonial period impacted personal choices as Koreans navigated their identity amid change.

Historians might find it interesting to note how these ideals reflect broader societal changes during the colonial period, particularly in relation to gender roles and the tension between tradition and modernity, which were significant themes in Korean society under Japanese rule.
