The Most Glorious Page in Korean History - The Military Might of Goguryeo  
(Kim Do-tae)  

In discussing the most glorious pages of our history, referring to our current situation is like talking about dreams in a dream. 

The 4,000 years of Korean history can generally be regarded as unfortunate for our ancestors. The reason being, Korea's geographical position is such that it is bordered to the west by China and to the east by Japan, just a narrow stream apart. In other words, we find ourselves nestled between the great peoples of China to the west and Japan to the east. Caught between these two powerful nations, we have often found ourselves in the precarious situation of having to flee and cope with the harsh fate of suffering from exposure to the elements. Multiple times, we have had to be vigilant, fearing incursions that might arise from either side, while also attempting to win their favor through various means. While it would be an exaggeration to say that this makes up the entirety of Korean history, it likely accounts for more than half. We have been unable to freely showcase our talents or undertake any significant endeavors due to being sandwiched between these two powerful neighbors.

Despite the misfortunes, our ancestors still managed to labor and strive within any small gaps they found. This serves as a great lesson for our descendants, and it can be said that in comparison to other global nations, their efforts were not at all inferior; rather, they can even be regarded as superior. Therefore, I would like to introduce the most glorious page of our history. 

One might argue that Koreans lived freely until the early Goryeo (高麗) period. The ancient history is not well documented, but one could assert that from the Three Kingdoms period to the early Goryeo period was the peninsula's most golden age. The military prowess of Goguryeo and the culture of Silla hold comparisons on a global scale without any shortcomings. Among these, I would like to write about the military might of Goguryeo, considering it the most glorious page in our history. The reason being, despite being caught between two great nations, Goguryeo demonstrated extraordinary military power as a "weaker" nation – an undeniable point of admiration.

Even looking at Western history, was it not the might of the Spartans that defended Greece against the formidable Persian enemies? Had the Persians trampled over them at that time, the history of Greek culture might have never become the dawn of world culture. It is because of such military might that Greek culture existed, which consequently birthed world culture.

Today, the remnants of our strength can be traced back to the might of Goguryeo. Why did the culture of Silla emerge? It was because Goguryeo repelled the strong enemies from China, allowing for safe cultural development. Therefore, I will document this most splendid page from the Records of the Three Kingdoms (三國史記).

In the twenty-third year of King Yeongyang in June, the Sui emperor, at the south of Liaodong Fortress, observed the form of the city and summoned the generals, questioning them. He said, "Do you think that you may treat me with the same disdain and fear simply because you have powerful families and high-ranking officials? When I came to the capital, did you fear seeing me because you were afraid of defeat? I have come here primarily to see your capabilities. Do you fear death so much that you do not wish to exert yourselves thinking that I cannot kill you? All the generals felt shame and disgrace, and the emperor remained at the western side of the city and ordered the troops to hold off."

Zuo Yiwai, the great general, took the naval forces from Jianghuai to proceed into the waters, engaging the army sixty li from Pyongyang and won a great defeat. After this victory, he hastened toward the city with the intention to seize it, but his deputy commander argued for a more cautious approach. While the various generals reached the city, Zuo did not pay attention to the various military preparations directly down to the city. We set ambush troops in empty cities and struck against Zuo's men, deceiving Zuo and causing them to retreat into the city, letting soldiers plunder, with no remaining resistance. Our army pursued to the point where Zuo's troops faced a major defeat, returning with only a few thousand soldiers out of three hundred thousand. 

Our army managed to break through after severe losses and even retreated to Liaodong, returning with only about two thousand seven hundred men.  

**Summary**  

This article highlights the remarkable achievements of Goguryeo during its peak, emphasizing the military capabilities that allowed it not only to survive between two powerful nations, China and Japan, but also to flourish culturally. The author draws parallels between Goguryeo's military actions and those of historical Western figures, suggesting that the historical narrative of Korea deserves recognition and respect in the context of world history. Historians studying this piece may find the social and cultural implications of the interactions between Korea, China, and Japan particularly revealing, especially regarding the influences and characteristics of identity formation in the colonial context.

朝鮮歷史上 가장 光輝잇는 페ㅡ지- 高句麗의 武威가 發揚된 곳
						金道泰
(7行 削除-原文)
이러한 現狀에 잇는 우리로서 가장 光輝잇는 歷史의「페지」를 말하는 것이 맛치 꿈속에 꿈이야기 하는 것과 가튼 늣김이 업지 안타.

2
우리 朝鮮의 4000년 歷史를 一括하건대 우리 祖先들의 處地가 참말로 불상하다고 볼 수 밧게 업다. 그 理由로 말하면 우리 朝鮮의 地理上 位置가 西으로 支那와 相接하고 東으로 日本과 一葦水를 격하야 잇다. 다시 말하면 西으로 支那의 大民族과 東으로 日本의 大民族과의 틈에 끼여 잇다. 그 두 民族의＜15＞ 틈에 끼여 잇서 今日엔 支那의 明日엔 OOOOOO當하야 이리로 避하고 저리로 몰녀 風餐露宿의 괴로운 운명이 그들을 나리누루게 된 일이 한두 번이 안이엇다. 그러다가 엇지 엇지하야 그를 막아 놋코도 또다시 침입하지나 안을가 하야 恐懼의 염려를 가지는 동시에 그들의 歡心을 사기 위하야 온갓 수단을 다하엿다. 이 사실이 朝鮮史의 전부라면 좀 과언일 것 가트나 아마 過半은 된 것 갓다. 이와가티 兩個 强隣의 틈에서 마음대로 자기네의 天才를 發揮하여 보지 못하엿스며 또한 무슨 큰 事業을 經營하기가 곤란하엿섯다.
그와 가티 불상한 우리 祖先들이 그래도 틈이 잇스면 自己네의 힘 자라는 데까지는 勞力하여 노은 것이엇다. 이것이야 말로 우리 後生에게 주는 큰 교훈이며 세계 다른 민족들의 한 일에 비하야도 조곰도 遜色이 업슬 뿐 아니라 도리여 우수하다 할만한 즉 가장 光輝잇는 「페ㅡ지」를 아래 소개코저 한다.

3
우리 朝鮮 사람으로 남과 가티 맘 노코 살아보기는 高麗초 까지라고 볼 수 밧게 업다. 上古史는 記證이 적으닛가 仔細히 알 수 업지만은 三國時代로부터 高麗初까지는 우리 半島에 가장 黃金時代엿다고 할 수 잇다.
高句麗의 무위라든지 新羅의 文化는 世界史上에 어느「페ㅡ지」에 비교하더라도 손색이 업슬 것이다.
그 중에도 나는 高句麗의 武威를 가지고 우리 歷史上에 가장 光輝잇는「페ㅡ지」라고 쓰려 한다.
그 理由로 말하면 몬저 말한 것과 가티 兩强國의 틈에 끼여 잇스면서 그 중에서 弱者로서 그만한 武力을 발휘하엿다는 것은 누구나 歎服안을 수 없는 것이다.
西洋史를 닑더라도 그리쓰의 적은 半島에서 波斯의 强敵을 막아 쪼츤 것은 오즉 스파르타人의 무력이 아니고 무엇이냐?
만일 그 당시에 波斯사람의 蹂躝을 당하엿다 하면 그리쓰의 文化가 世界文化의 始朝라는 일흠이 남아 잇슬 수가 업섯다. 그 무위가 잇섯기 때문에 希臘의 文化가 잇섯고 世界文化가 생겨난 것이다.
오늘날 우리들이 이와가치 나마 殘在한 것도 高句麗의 무위의 힘이 잇섯기 때문이다. 新羅의 文化가 무엇 때문에 생겻는가? 高句麗의 무위로 支那의 强敵을 막엇기 때문에 안전하게 발달된 것이다.
그럼으로 지금 가장 광휘잇는 三國史記의 한「페ㅡ지」를 적는다.
嬰陽王二十三年六月己未隋帝辛遼東城南, 觀基城池形勢, 因召諸將詰責之曰,公等自以官高侍家世欲以暗懦待我耶在都之日公等皆不願我來恐見病敗耳我今來此正欲觀公等所爲斬公輩爾公今畏死莫盡力謂我不能殺公耶諸將咸戰愧失色帝因留止城西數里御六合城我諸城堅守不下.＜16＞
佐翊衛大將軍. 來護兒帥江淮水軍舳艫數百里浮海先進入自浿水去平壤六十里與我軍相遇進擊大破之護兒欲乘勝趣其城副摠管周法尙止之諸候諸軍至俱進護兒不廳簡精甲數萬直造城下我將伏兵於羅郭內空寺中出兵與護兒戰而僞敗護兒逐之入城縱兵俘掠無復部伍伏兵發護兒大敗僅而獲兔士卒還者不過數千人我軍追至船所周法尙整陣待之我軍乃退護兒引兵還屯海浦不敢復留應接諸軍.
