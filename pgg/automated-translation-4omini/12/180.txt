발행일	1928년 05월 01일  
기사제목	내가 자랑하고 십흔 朝鮮 것-나는 天道敎를 (I Am Proud of the Korean Thing - I Am the Teachings of Heaven)  
필자	鄭廣朝  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

As a person of the Teachings of Heaven, to proudly present the Teachings of Heaven is not at all like praising myself. However, just as one would acknowledge the good results of one's own crops and criticize one's own flaws, it is proper and correct to speak honestly. Our Korean history spans over 4,000 years, during which many great figures have emerged, and we have much splendid culture to take pride in. However, spiritual culture has largely been dominated by foreign influences. Confucianism, Buddhism, Taoism, and even Christianity in recent times have all had a significant impact. Up to this point, there has been no religion founded by Koreans that can govern the fundamental spirit of the entire nation. However, the Teachings of Heaven, established 69 years ago, were created by pure Koreans, reflecting the contemporary spirit and thought of the Korean people, and have the potential to dominate the spirits of peoples worldwide. 

I won’t delve into the specific doctrines here, but it can surely be said that no other invention is greater than this. It is a fact that our written language was invented by us, and that we were the first to invent ironclad ships and movable type ahead of any other nation. Nevertheless, the invention of a new religion that can govern the fundamental spirit of all people is perhaps the Teachings of Heaven. Furthermore, as for doctrine, the Teachings of Heaven transcends the old shells of past religions and embraces contemporary thought while rejecting the old morals and ethics. It promotes new morals and new ethics, denies the current system, and proposes a new system with the aim of first saving the Korean people and then saving the world. 

Because of the existence of the Teachings of Heaven in Korea, new unity and new life are evident. This can be proven sufficiently by both past history and current facts. What we Koreans can take pride in above all else is the fact that this new life is continuously growing. Regardless of how splendid past cultures or how prolonged past histories may have been, if our new life were to cease, there would be no more tragic or deplorable situation than that. Therefore, the pride we take in the Teachings of Heaven is not solely confined to those of us who follow it, but should be something to be celebrated by the entire nation. <45>  

**Summary**  

The article expresses pride in the Teachings of Heaven (천도교) as a uniquely Korean religion that emerged to reflect the contemporary spirit of Koreans and has the potential to lead globally. The author critiques the dominance of foreign religions throughout Korean history and emphasizes the importance of a spiritual identity that can unify and uplift the Korean people. Historians might find this perspective revealing as it illustrates an attempt to assert Korean identity and resilience during the colonial period, highlighting cultural movements that resisted foreign domination.
