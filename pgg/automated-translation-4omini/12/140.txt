기사제목	내가 자랑하고 십흔 朝鮮 것-德性과 才質 (I Proudly Speak of the Korean Traits - Virtue and Talent)  
필자	朴瓚熙  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

I do not know whether I can accurately speak about the exceptional traits of the Korean people as a Korean myself. However, even from the perspective of a neutral third party, I believe I can acknowledge these two points. 

1. The richness of virtue. This aligns with our identity as the "land of Eastern propriety and morality," which is greatly influenced by the customs passed down from our ancestors. Whether in major or minor matters, one can clearly see the inherent morality of our people. Even when emotions are heightened between individuals, we can remarkably maintain composure and the ability to sway our adversaries. In matters of international relations, as our history has shown, we have always favored peace over conflict, which is a testament to our national virtue. In a society that often invokes the principle of “the weak being consumed by the strong,” it may not be an issue of national interests being beneficial or harmful. 

2. The intellect is bright. From a physiological perspective, the structure of the brain differs, with the weight being comparatively heavy; nevertheless, it seems that, generally, our brains are sharp. It appears that we possess the potential for development through the use of our intellect. Certainly, the current Korean people may lag behind others, but that was a consequence of the cultural stagnation during the closed-door era. At the very least, after the doors are opened, we are likely to build a new culture without significant shortcomings. Possessing a brain sufficient for understanding, researching, and utilizing knowledge, there should be no worries. This is clearly evidenced by the superior grades that Koreans achieve in schools that are jointly attended with Japanese, despite the various disadvantages positioned against us in terms of language and customs compared to Westerners. 

  
**Summary**  

The article presents a subjective reflection by Park Chan-hee on the exceptional traits of Koreans, particularly emphasizing their rich moral virtue and intellectual capabilities. It highlights the belief that, despite colonial challenges, Koreans possess an inherent quality that enables them to excel academically and culturally. Historians may find interest in the assertions made about Korean identity and virtue during a period of colonial rule, underscoring the resilience and potential of the Korean populace in the face of adversity.

1. 頭腦가 明晳 生理學上으로는 뇌의 구조가 달으니 뇌의 重量이 무거우니 하지만은 그것은 하여간 일반적으로 뇌가 명철한 것이 사실인 듯 함니다. 뇌를 쓰면 얼마든지 발달할 수 잇는 소질을 가진 듯 함니다. 물론 현재의 朝鮮 사람은 다른 나라 사람에 비하야 낙오자가 되어잇지만은 그것은 鎖國時代의 文化思潮가 밋치지 못할 때의 일이오. 적어도 門戶가 개방된 금후에 잇서서는 新文化的 건설을 하기에 조곰도 손색이 업슬 줄 암니다. 그를 이해하고 연구하고 이용하기에 족할 만한 두뇌를 소유한 이상에 무엇이 걱정되겟슴니가. 이것은 무엇보다도 朝鮮 사람과 日本人과의 共學하는 학교에서 그의 성적이 도처에서 우수한 것으로 보아 잘 증명되는 바이며 구미에 퍼저 잇는 조선인으로서 言語 風俗 여러 가지가 불리한 입장에 잇슴에 불구하고 歐米人에 비하야 만이는 우수한 성적을 내게 되는 것으로 보아 잘 알 수 잇슴니다. ＜42＞
