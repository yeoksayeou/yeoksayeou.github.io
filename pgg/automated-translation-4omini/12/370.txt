기사제목	開闢社마-크 圖案募集, 讀者考案을 募集합니다. (Call for Designs for the Opening Society's Mark, Seeking Readers' Proposals)  
기사형태	사고·편집후기 (Advertisement/Editorial Note)  

開闢社마-크 圖案募集, 讀者考案을 募集합니다. 

In the summer of the year of Gyeongshin (庚申年), the Opening Society was founded, and after struggling for eight years with women's and children's publications, various magazines of Byeolgeon-gwon, alongside necessary book publishing, lectures, and other activities, it is now even more determined to fight forward. We have decided to publicly solicit a "mark" for the Opening Society to be recognized throughout the land, from which excellent designs will be selected. We hope you will send us many beautiful designs based on your special thoughts, and a specially made gold watch and a two-year subscription to this magazine will be awarded to the chosen design.

Referencing points: 
The content to be depicted in the mark is unrestricted; it is completely free. The shape can be circular, octagonal, hexagonal, etc. (the size can be enlarged later, so it can be drawn large). The color can be monochrome; if absolutely necessary, two colors are acceptable (if monochrome, it must be black). 

Each person can submit various types, but each submission must be one type only per sheet, and the address and name must be entered on each sheet. 

Deadline: By June 20.

Wishes: Please express the spirit of the Opening Society as much as possible, and let it be easy to recognize when creating the flag; it should be easily visible from a distance, and ideally be drawn simply with modest lines. <85>

<85-85>  

**Summary**

This article is a call for design submissions for a mark representing the Opening Society, offering prizes for selected designs. It reflects the society's aim to enhance its visibility and engagement within society and highlights the importance of artistic contribution from the public during a time of cultural awakening in Korea. Historians might find it significant as it showcases grassroots participation and the evolving role of women's organizations in colonial Korea during the late 1920s.
