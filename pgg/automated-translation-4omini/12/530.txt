기사제목	乙支公石像과 基祠宇 (Eulji's Stone Statue and Shrine)  
기사형태	잡저 (Journal Article)  

In Seongnam, in the region of Yangju, there is a military cemetery overlooking the Cheongcheon River. In that hilly area, there is a stone statue and a stele along with several remnants of a shrine. The locals refer to this as Eulji Gong's stone statue or shrine, commonly called "Eulji Gongter." There is hardly anyone, even children and workers, who does not know about Eulji Gongter. 

In around the past 100 years, the statue has fallen down, and the shrine is without any traces. The stele has been thrown into the nearby Yongdam stream by a ruffian after his child got hurt while playing on it (this incident occurred during the year of Eulhai Gyeongja). During the year of Yongheung, when the Anheung School was established in Anju, the An Chang-ho family came to that place, and upon hearing about it, the school students went together to search for the stone statue at today's Eulji Gongter. They found the upper part of the stele in Yongdam stream, and during that time, it was placed in the eastern corner of the courtyard of the Anheung School, which is now the Nongsik School. 

The statue is about 4 feet high and approximately 1 foot 4 inches wide, fully armored. Of course, it has been worn down by wind and rain to the point that only the outlines can barely be recognized, while the backside remains untouched, showing the natural stone surface. Even upon casual observation, one can recognize the ancient and simple charm from over a thousand years ago. The stele measures around 1 foot 5 inches at its top, inscribed with Eulji Gong's achievements, while the lower half is missing, making it impossible to fully capture its contents. It is said that it was erected in the 224th year of the Chongzhen era, which likely reflects a modern individual's sentiments upon seeing the abandoned statue.

  
**Summary**  

This article discusses the historical significance of the stone statue and shrine dedicated to Eulji Gong, located in Anju. It highlights the neglect and vandalism the site has faced, alongside the local traditions and remembrances associated with it. Historians may find the transformative societal views regarding cultural relics and the way local history and community knowledge are transmitted through generations particularly intriguing, especially in the context of colonial history where such monuments were often overlooked or disregarded.

乙支公石像과 基祠宇
安州의 西 龍峴里 龍潭浦의 上 安州의 城 밋혜 멀니 淸川江을 瞰下할만한 兵陵이 잇고 그 구릉에는 一片 石像과 一片 碑碣 또는 數間의 廢址가 잇서 古老가 전하되 이를 乙支公의 石像 또는 祠宇라 하며 그 지명을 속칭「乙支公터」라 하야 兒童 走卒이라도「乙支公터」를 모르는 이 업섯는데 挽近 100년 내외에 석상은 땅에 믓침이 되고 그 祠宇는 터좃차 업서지고 碑碣은 그 近家의 엇던 頑夫가 자기 집 兒孩가 그 碑石우에서 놀다가 다리를 傷하엿다는 이유로 그것을 꺽거서 그 압 龍潭浦에 던저버렷는데(지난 乙亥 庚子 年間의 일) 往昔 隆熙 年間에 安州에 安興學校가 설립하며 安昌鎬氏가 그곳에 왓다가 그 말을 듯고 學校生徒들과 가티 지금의 乙支公터에서 그 石像을 찻고 龍潭浦에서 그 碑의 上部를 어더 당시의 安興學校이오 현재의 農學校의 뜰 東便 구석에 置하엿는데 石像은 高 약 4尺 幅 약 1척 4寸의 全身甲冑像으로서 물론 風磨雨洗하야 간신히 그 輪廓을 認할 뿐이오 그 後面은 조곰도 琢磨를 不加한 天然 石面 그대로 인바 暫間 보아도 1,000년 以前의 古朴한 녯 風韻을 認하겟스며 碑碣은 약 1척 5촌의 上部 折面으로서 乙支公의 功績을 기록한 것인대 下半部가 업슴으로 그 내용을 닑을 수는 업스나 崇禎 紀元 224년 丁未라 云云하엿슨 즉 아마 그 廢棄된 石像을 보고 感慕의 情으로 근대에 엇던 이가 策立한 것에 분명하다. ＜131＞
