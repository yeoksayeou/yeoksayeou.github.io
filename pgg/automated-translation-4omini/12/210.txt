기사제목	내가 자랑하고 십흔 朝鮮 것-家族制度는 世界에 模範 (I Proudly Declare That Korean Family Systems Are a Model for the World) 
필자	崔誠愚 (Choi Seong-u) 
기사형태	논설 (Editorial) 

I am proud to declare that Korean family systems are a model for the world. 

In today’s era, which fundamentally denies family systems, boasting about it seems too much influenced by contemporary thinking. However, if we can talk about boasts from several thousand years ago, then how can we entirely deny the fact that this system has vividly persisted to this day? It is a different matter as to whether this system aligns with today’s ideologies or not, but comparing it to a few Eastern countries that still implement family systems from their historical backgrounds, one can hardly deny that the Korean family system indeed serves as a model for the world.

For instance, to revere ancestors, even for ten or a hundred generations back, maintaining utmost respect and sincerity in rituals and safeguarding the gravesites is a significant aspect of the Korean tradition. Moreover, the concept of the same clan is regarded as kinship for a hundred generations; even those who lived a century ago in the same household are particularly cherished and loved, especially between kin and extended family like siblings and nephews. Also, the clan lineage system of Koreans is deemed a beautiful custom, and such beauty may not exist elsewhere. Regardless of how many hundreds of generations have passed, the lineage remains orderly without mixing bloodlines, and the relationships within a family or clan become easily recognizable at a glance. This will also serve as crucial reference material for future scholars studying genealogy or historians.

Although there are certainly some downsides in this cultural transmission, no system can be declared absolutely perfect and everlasting. Even the socialist systems currently applauded and celebrated worldwide will eventually encounter their own drawbacks and fade away. Even now, in this new ideology, anarchists are already rejecting the socialist structure, aren’t they? When viewed from any angle, our historical Korean family system was notably developed and thus can be considered a model for the world.

**Summary**

The article by Choi Seong-u extols the traditional Korean family system as a model worthy of admiration, contrasting it with contemporary rejections of such systems. It emphasizes the reverence for ancestors and familial ties in Korea, positing that these values are both historically significant and positively unique when compared to other cultures. Historians might find this perspective interesting as it illuminates the cultural pride among Koreans during a time of colonial oppression, highlighting attempts to preserve and valorize traditional values.
