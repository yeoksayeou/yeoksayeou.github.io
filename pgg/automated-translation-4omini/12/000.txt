잡지명	별건곤  
발행일	1928년 05월 01일  
기사제목	東西無比 朝鮮人情 美談集-남편 위하야 호랑이 잡고 殉死한 山中佳人 (A Tale of Unmatched Humanity: The Mountain Maiden Who Died for Her Husband After Catching a Tiger)  
필자	夜雷  
기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Works, Others)  

It has already been about 30 years. There was a farmer named Yi Min (이민) in a valley called Tongcheon (통천) in Gangwon-do (강원도). His occupation was trading bear cubs, and he carried a load of bear cubs on his back to the mountain areas to gather grains. To harvest grains, he had to pass over a high and dangerous ridge known as Jangdaeryeong (장대령). Even just crossing this ridge was terrifying, especially as the sun began to set and darkness enveloped the mountains.     

Despite his fear, he had no choice but to press on, for he had nowhere else to go. With a heart full of dread, he somehow managed to climb the ridge and soon came across a sack lying abandoned on the roadside. “How can a sack be lying here in such a deep mountain?” he wondered, but he didn’t give it much thought and carelessly picked it up and took it with him. As he continued, he eventually came upon a small house that looked as though it had been placed there by a stroke of fortune. He felt as if he had regained his life. 

He stopped to rest and knocked on the door of the house. A woman's voice, pleasant and youthful, asked, “Who is it? What brings you here?” As she spoke, a lovely woman who looked to be around thirty emerged. He requested, “Please let me stay the night, as it is getting dark in this deep mountain.” Without hesitation, the woman replied, “Of course. It is very dangerous to travel farther at this hour.” She kindly invited him in and prepared a place for him to sleep.

As the bear trader entered the house, the woman busily moved around preparing dinner. During this time, she noticed the sack he had carried and, suddenly startled, exclaimed, “Sir! Is this sack yours?” The bear trader, perplexed, replied honestly, “No, I found it on the way here.” Hearing this, the woman’s eyes filled with tears as she said, “Oh no! This is terrible! Our master has fallen prey to a tiger!” She spoke without taking a breath, “Our master went over the mountain around five o'clock to take rice in that sack to help out a family that is in need. It is certain he has been caught by a tiger on the way back. What can we do?”

The bear trader felt a mix of fear and pity, unsure of what to do. The woman quickly pulled herself together and said, “Crying will not help now that it has come to this. We must retrieve his body!” She then urged, “Please, Sir! Since you came on this unfortunate day, you must help me.” The bear trader, paralyzed with fear, stammered that he could not go out at night. 

The woman, gripped by desperation, said, “There is no choice. If we cannot retrieve the corpse, I will kill you, and I will die too.” She ran to the kitchen to grab a knife. Terrified, the bear trader thought that if he had to die, he might as well go with her to look. He gathered his courage and, taking a torch, followed as they retraced their steps toward where the sack had been found. 

Arriving there, they indeed found a bloody trail leading into the dense forest. Following the trail, the woman dashed ahead, not heeding the grim sight of her master’s body under a large rock where a tiger was guarding it. She charged forward and leapt at the tiger, lifting her master’s body and trying to rescue it, leaving the tiger behind.

The bear trader, terrified, gave a half-hearted promise to hold the torch and follow her with the corpse. He was apprehensive but finally also took the body on his back and the two of them made their way back home as quickly as they could. The woman reached her house first and, in a tone of desperation, locked the bear trader in a room, taking her husband’s body into the cave.

Sitting in the cave and waiting for the tiger to come, she suddenly struck its head with the knife as it attempted to drag her husband's body away. The tiger, startled, roared, and the struggle ended with it dead. The bear trader, terrified but relieved, departed from the house, leaving behind the sack and his previous fears. The woman, full of grief, insisted he take a ginseng root and a piece of cloth as gratitude. Ultimately, unable to refuse, he took his leave. As he walked away, he glanced back and saw the woman set fire to her house, embracing her husband's body as she entered the flames. 

Summary  

This article tells the poignant tale of a woman who bravely risked her life to recover her husband's body from a tiger, ultimately sacrificing herself in the process. The story highlights the fierce loyalty and love within the confines of a deeply traditional society. From a historical perspective, this narrative illustrates the gender roles and expectations of women in colonial Korea, where loyalty and sacrifice were often romanticized in literature, reflecting the values imposed upon them during a time of struggle.
