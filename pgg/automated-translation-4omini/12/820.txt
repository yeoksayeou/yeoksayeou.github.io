기사제목	畵家가 본 朝鮮女子美(二) (The Beauties of Korean Women as Seen by an Artist (Part 2))  
필자	虛心汕 (Hyosinsam)  
기사형태	잡저 (Journal)  

An artist inevitably observes that no matter if they are dressed in traditional hanbok, in Western-style clothing, or in Japanese dress, there is something that cannot be adequately expressed. If there were a fairy, her attire might resemble this. A layer of rich skirts worth tens of thousands. Compared to this beauty, when observing a woman in a suit resembling a rice sack, she appears to have a foul air about her, and when seeing someone carelessly lengthen their top to cover the beauty of their waist, I just want to scold them. (Hyosinsam)  

**Summary**  

In this article, the author draws attention to the ways in which Korean women's beauty is presented through different attire including traditional, Western, and Japanese clothing. The author expresses a strong preference for the traditional hanbok, celebrating its elegance and depth, while critiquing the inadequacies of other fashions that obscure the natural beauty of women. This text offers a window into the cultural tensions of the colonial period in Korea, highlighting the clash between traditional values and the influence of Western and Japanese fashions. Historians may find it revealing how such discussions reflect broader societal anxieties about cultural identity and imperial pressures during Japanese rule.
