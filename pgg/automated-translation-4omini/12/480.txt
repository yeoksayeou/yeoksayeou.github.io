기사제목	畵家가 본 朝鮮女子의 美(二) (The Beauty of Korean Women as Seen by an Artist (Part 2))  
필자	李靑田 (Lee Cheong-dan)  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

The beauty of a demure maiden, with her neatly pleated skirt and elegant jeogori (저고리), is beyond words in how it enhances her delicate, soft, and precious appearance. Moreover, the beauty emanating from her jeogori, along with the black hair and the fresh complexion adorning her, is indescribable in letters. (李靑田)  

**Summary**  

This article reflects on the aesthetic qualities of Korean women as viewed through the lens of an artist, emphasizing traditional attire's impact on beauty perception. It points towards the cultural appreciation of femininity in the colonial period, showcasing a fusion of national identity with gendered representations. Historians may find interest in how such artistic depictions acted as reflections or critiques of societal values during the Japanese colonization of Korea, highlighting issues of identity and cultural coercion.
