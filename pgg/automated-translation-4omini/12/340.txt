기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

This is a story about a miserly person who, while buying a train ticket, stubbornly tries to bargain over a small amount of change. “Why are you so worked up over a mere fraction of a penny? Isn’t it a common practice in stores to offer a discount?” In the meantime, the train begins to whistle as it prepares to leave. This person, feeling anxious, runs after it shouting, “This is a big deal, please hold the train for just a moment while I pay you the change!”

A young man with a loose tongue boards a streetcar. He sees an advertisement on the window saying “No Smoking” and remarks, “Those damn idiots, if they say don’t eat it, then of course they’ll want you to buy more, no doubt a ploy by the tobacco sellers.” 

On the other side, another passenger reads, “Be careful as it may be dangerous to lean out with your hands or head.” The fast-talking young man jumps in with, “Well, that must be a reverse advertisement from the tobacco companies!” <76>  

  

**Summary**  

The article humorously illustrates the behavior of individuals during the colonial period, capturing a moment of absurdity where a petty desire to save money conflicts with the urgency of travel. This scene reflects broader themes of colonial life, such as the economic struggles and societal norms influencing personal behavior. Historians may find the portrayal of people's interactions especially fascinating as it highlights the psychological state of individuals under colonial rule, where both humor and desperation coexist.

이것저것 다 글넛다
이것저것 다 글넛다
엇던 욕심쟁이고 인색한 사람이 차표를 사면서 작고만 닷돈을 깍거날라고 졸낫슴니다.
「무엇 그러심니까 고까짓 닷돈에 고다지 그럴 것 잇슴닛가. 어늬 상점에서나 우수리는 감해주는 걸요.」
그러는 동안에 긔차는 고동을 뛰기면서 떠나기를 시작하엿슴니다. 이 사람은 속이 달아서 쫓처나가며
「이것 큰일낫구나 여… 여보 닷돈 마자 냇게 긔차 좀 머물너 주오!」 ＜76＞

뚱딴지 광고
입 빠른 젊은 사람이 전차를 탓슴니다. 차창 위마다에「담배 먹지 마시오」하고 한 광고가 부터 잇는 것을 보고「망할 놈들 먹지 말나면 붓석 더 먹을 터니까 하여간 담배 파는 놈이 광소술은 용한데」
