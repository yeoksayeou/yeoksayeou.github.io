기사제목	내가 자랑하고 십흔 朝鮮 것-人物과 山水 (I Proudly Speak of Korean Things - People and Landscapes)  
필자	張膺震  
기사형태	논설 (Essay)  

I have spent many years in the field of education, and through my various experiences, I can say that there is no ethnic group as outstanding as the Korean people. First, the talent is remarkable; in any study, when compared to foreigners, the performance of Koreans invariably ranks first. It is globally recognized that Koreans have a natural talent for language learning. However, it is not limited to language alone. Mathematics, science, craftsmanship, and all other subjects follow suit. Even in schools where Japanese and Koreans study together, Korean students learn the Japanese language and study subjects in Japanese, bearing the burden of a double curriculum. Despite the teaching language and textbooks being entirely in Japanese, they understand and grasp the material well, performing as well as, if not better than, Japanese students in all subjects.

If Korean students were given the opportunity to follow an orderly educational path from a young age like students from other countries, and if they were provided with sufficient economic resources for substantial research, there would be no ethnic group in the world that could outperform them; they would certainly excel. Secondly, our nature is quite generous. We are capable of accommodating and forgiving others, and we seldom show our anger or joy in our expressions. This trait may be a strong factor for our nation’s future development. Moreover, we possess resilience. We are flexible yet strong; no matter what difficulties or adversities we face, we endure and remain silent. When we choose to be patient and silent, within us lies a powerful strength, which, when revealed, allows for significant activity and achievement without others being aware of it. Foreigners refer to this characteristic as the “conspiratorial nature” of Koreans. Regardless of how others evaluate it, this is a unique quality and strength of our national character.

Thirdly, our bodies are resilient and healthy, showing strong resistance to infectious diseases and possessing the ability to be actively engaged in various endeavors.  

Aside from that, regarding the beautiful natural landscapes, before we boast, it is the foreigners who commend them first. Just looking at Geumgangsan (金剛山), historically, Chinese people expressed their desire to be born in Goryeo (高麗) to see Geumgangsan. Recently, Westerners have also shown an increasing interest, with more people visiting each year and praising Geumgangsan as a future paradise of the world. It is not only Geumgangsan; throughout the picturesque landscape of our land, there are no regions where the scenery is not superb. I have visited many famous places in Japan and America, but I believe they cannot be compared to the natural beauty of Korea.

  
**Summary**  

This article by Zhang Eung-jeon emphasizes the exceptional qualities of the Korean people and their beautiful natural landscape, particularly highlighting Geumgangsan. He argues for the potential of Koreans in education and resilience, asserting that with proper support, Koreans could outperform others globally. Historians may find this text intriguing as it captures the national pride and identity during the colonial period, reflecting a push for recognition and a desire to prove the capability of the Korean people under colonial rule.
