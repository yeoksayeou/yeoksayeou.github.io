**Summary**

This article presents a series of reflections highlighting the talents and cultural contributions of Koreans under Japanese colonial rule. Each entry emphasizes various aspects of Korean identity, creativity, and philosophy, countering narratives that sought to diminish their cultural significance. Historians may find this article of interest as it encapsulates the resilience of Korean intellectual thought and art amidst oppression, offering insight into how colonial subjects articulated their identity during this tumultuous period.
