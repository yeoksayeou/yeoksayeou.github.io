기사제목	畵家가 본 朝鮮女子의 美(三) (The Beauty of Korean Women as Seen by an Artist (Part Three))  
필자	金圭澤 (Kim Kyuchak)  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

The appearance of women often gives satisfaction to us, even when they may be lacking in other aspects. The beautifully balanced upper body and the neatly tailored jeogori (저고리) paired with the long chima (치마) maintain perfect harmony. When the white attire is complemented by dark hair flowing gracefully, and the bright red ribbon catches the wind, it charms the heart of those who look upon it. 

Though perhaps not overly lively, there is a prominent characteristic of these women that conveys an air of quiet dignity and restraint. The symbol of an ideal maiden is embodied in their posture and the way their hair is tied with a ribbon... (Kim Kyuchak)  
<219>  

**Summary**  

This article presents an artistic perspective on the beauty of Korean women during the colonial period, focusing on the aesthetics of traditional clothing and the dignified presence associated with them. It reflects on the societal expectations of women, portraying them as symbols of purity and restraint. Historians might find it interesting to explore how such representations were impacted by colonial influences and the tension between traditional values and modernity.

그리 활발치는 못하나마 능쳥스럽게도 얌전한 녀자다운 큰 특징이다. 됴선 처녀의 정숙하고 결백한 표상이 이 뒤 태도과 당기 꼬리에 잇다할지…… (金圭澤)
