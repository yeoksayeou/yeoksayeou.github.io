기사제목	善德女王의 三知 (The Three Knowledges of Queen Seondeok)  
기사형태	논설 (Editorial)  

The perception in Korea has long been that women had no influence in politics. However, it is noteworthy that several millennia ago, in the 7th century (AD 632, which is 1,286 years ago from now), there was a woman in Silla who became the ruler of a nation; she was Queen Seondeok, the daughter of King Jinpyeong, known as the Holy King of Silla. Her name was Deokman, and her nature was characterized by great kindness and intelligence; she was able to predict events without having to see them. At that time, there were no peonies in Silla, and they had to be requested from the Tang Dynasty. Upon seeing a painting of a peony (牡丹), she noted that the flower bore no fragrance. When her father, King Jinpyeong, smiled and asked how she could tell that the flower had no scent, she replied that flowers are typically depicted with fragrance, and since the painting lacked any such indication, it follows that the flower depicted must not have one. When the peony was actually planted later, it indeed bore no fragrance. 

On another occasion, she observed a large number of frogs fighting in a pond to the west of the palace called the Okmunji (玉門池) and stated that this was a portent of military conflict, suggesting that there would be external threats near Okmun. She immediately sent out troops to investigate, and sure enough, forces from Baekje invaded, but they were repelled. Additionally, she was able to predict her own death, which contributed to her being regarded as possessing the Three Knowledges (三知). Moreover, the observatory known as Cheomsongdae (瞻星臺), which is the oldest astronomical observatory in the world, was also constructed during her reign.  

**Summary**  

This article discusses the historical figure of Queen Seondeok of Silla, highlighting her intellect and precognitive abilities, as well as her accomplishments, such as the construction of the Cheomsongdae observatory. The article illustrates a significant acknowledgment of women's roles in leadership during the Three Kingdoms period, which may surprise those who regard Korean history as traditionally male-dominated. Historians might find this representation relevant as it provides counter-narratives to the general thoughts on women's invisibility in Korean historical texts, suggesting a more nuanced understanding of gender roles in ancient Korea.

善德女王의 三知
朝鮮에서는 아즉까지 女子가 政治上에 아무 상관도 못한 줄로 생각하는 사람이 만치만은 알고 보면 벌서 몇 1,000년 이전 서양에서는 여자 參政權이라는 말도 알지 못하던 그때에(西紀 632년 距今 1286년) 新羅에는 여자로서 一國의 皇帝까지 된 이가 잇섯스니 그는 新羅聖君으로 이름이 놉흐신 眞平王의 맛따님 善德女王이다. 그의 이름은 德曼이니 天性이 寬仁하고도 明敏하야 무슨 일이던지 보지 안코도 미리 아는 일이 만헛섯다. 그때 新羅에는 아즉까지 모란(牧丹) 꼿이 업서서 唐나라에서 츠음으로 구하야 왓섯는데 그는 모란꼿의 그림(牧丹圖)을 보고 말하기를 그 꼿은 향긔가 업는 꼿이라 하니 황부(皇父)되시는 眞平王이 웃스며 뭇되 네가 엇지 그 꼿에 향긔가 업는 것을 아느냐 한 즉 그는 대답하기를 꼿에는 반듯이 나븨를 그리는데 이 꼿의 그림에는 나븨가 업스니 필경은 향긔가 업는 꼿이라 하얏다. 그런데 그 뒤에 그 꼿을 실디로 심어보니 과연 향긔가 업섯다. 그 뒤에 한번은 또 대궐 서편에 잇는 玉門池라는 연못에서 개고리가 만히 모혀 싸우는 것을 보고 가로되 이것은 병란(兵亂)이 잇슬 증조인대 국경에 잇는 玉門땅에 外敵이 잇슬 듯 하다 하고 즉시 군사를 보내 수탐하니 과연 百濟의 군사가 침입함으로 곳 처서 물리치고 또 그 뒤에는 자긔의 죽을 말을 미리 말한 고로 그 女皇은 三知가 잇섯다 한다. 그리고 세계 천문대 중에 가장 먼저 생긴 천문대라는 慶州의 瞻星臺도 이 女皇時代에 싸흔 것이다.＜53＞
