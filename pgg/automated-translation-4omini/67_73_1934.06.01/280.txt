기사제목	옴겨진 대궐, 童話 (The Transferred Palace, A Fairy Tale)  
필자	延星欽  
기사형태	소설 (Novel)  

However, until today, the people who have been continuously grateful for Aryun's help are making such a scene out of this rumor, not knowing the details of what has happened, that it would be a great trouble for the king too if the people’s commotion (騷擾) were to occur; thus, the execution of Aryun is not yet carried out. 

Aryun was brought out before the king and, while kneeling, asked, "Your Majesty! What crime have I committed?" The king responded, 

"What crime do you say? If you know what crime you have committed, come here and take a look." 

He then pulled Aryun to the window. Usually, from this window, Aryun could see the magnificent palace clearly; but now, there was nothing but a vast plain. Looking at this, Aryun was astonished and was almost stunned to the spot. 

After agreeing with the king, Aryun had come out of the palace and had been anxious for three days and nights about where the palace had drifted to. However, he couldn't discern any clues. So, with no grass beneath him, he wandered around until he reached the beach, when he tripped over a stone and, in a daze, fell to a rock nearby. At that moment, the ring he had received many years ago from the magician of "Africa" rubbed hard against the rock. Suddenly, the ring spirit appeared and asked, 

"Why are you distressed?" 

Seeing the ring spirit, which he had completely forgotten up until now, Aryun was overwhelmed with joy and bewilderment; after some time, he said, 

"Can you bring me to where the palace has gone? I cannot find it." 

"The only command I cannot obey is that. I am the spirit linked to this ring. You must ask that command of the Lamphu spirit." 

The ring spirit responded. Aryun thought that perhaps the ring spirit didn't have enough power to obey this command and, after contemplating, he asked, "Then can you take me to the place where the palace has been moved?" 

Immediately, the ring spirit lifted Aryun onto its back and flew like a bird, taking him away. 

After a while, Aryun regained his senses and saw in front of him the splendid palace standing tall on a wide plain. Upon closer inspection, it was undeniably the very palace that he had built with Lamphu's power. Aryun was overjoyed and, while continuously expressing his gratitude to the ring spirit, he stood not leaving the palace fence, keen on observing any movements inside. 

At that moment, it happened that one of the princess's maids, brought there by the magician while the events unfolded, mindlessly looked out through the window and discovered Aryun wandering near the palace fence. The maid quickly reported this to the princess. The princess, upon hearing this shocking news, at first couldn't believe it. So she hurriedly came to the window and, upon seeing Aryun outside just like the maid said, opened the window. 

When Aryun looked up and saw the beautiful appearance of the princess, he was so delighted he raised both hands and exclaimed "Ah!" numerous times. The princess then called out to Aryun, saying, "Please, if you keep wandering around like this, it will cause a big problem. I will open the back door for you shortly, so please come in quickly." 

A little later, as the back door leading to the princess's room opened, Aryun entered at once.

The two of them were overwhelmed with joy, shedding tears as they greeted each other after such a long time apart. Aryun first asked how Lamphu had fared. 

"How have you been able to manage without the lamp I entrusted to you?" 

"That lamp you mentioned? I have gone through hardships that you could not imagine all because that lamp was lost. I truly have no face to show you. I believed the lamp had been completely destroyed, and thought it didn’t have anything to do with another lamp. Therefore, when it was taken to some land I couldn't possibly know, I was brought here without even knowing what happened. I asked where I was. They said, 'Africa.'." The princess answered, her voice getting thick with emotion. 

After comforting the princess, Aryun said, "Now we have to think of a way to live safely from here; I will bravely go out into the streets. By the time it is noon, I will return, so let’s discuss our plan then. In the meantime, I will alter my appearance and clothing. When I come back and knock at the door, please open it." 

After Aryun left the palace and entered the main street, he encountered a farmer. Aryun exchanged clothes with that farmer. Then, urgently, he headed toward the market. 

Aryun entered one of the biggest pharmacies in the street and requested sleeping medicine. The pharmacy owner, noticing Aryun's shabby appearance, thought he was a poor man with no money and asked if he could afford such expensive medicine. So, Aryun took out a shiny gold wallet and handed over some gold coins to buy a packet of sleeping medicine. He then immediately returned to the palace and entered the princess's room. 

"We need to get rid of that wicked magician, and first, you must exert your efforts. When the magician returns, treat him kindly. Also, when serving dinner, make sure to sneak this medicine into his drink. This medicine will induce sleep, so once he drinks it, he will fall asleep, and I will take care of the rest." Aryun explained these details to the princess. 

That evening, when the magician returned, the princess welcomed him as Aryun had instructed and offered the sleeping meds mixed in the drink. As a result, the magician passed out, hardly half an hour after drinking the wine. 

The princess immediately sent a maid to inform Aryun. At that moment, Aryun silently entered the room. Immediately, all the maids, servants, and others were instructed to wait in another room, while Aryun approached the sleeping magician and, after a few tries, managed to take out the lamp tied to the magician's waist. 

As soon as the lamp spirit appeared, Aryun ordered, "Return this palace to its original position." Upon hearing this, the spirit showed respect and disappeared without a trace. 

Not long after, the palace was moved back to Chinese territory, and not a single window was damaged; it was as if it had been hidden perfectly. The palace being returned to Chinese territory was the next morning. The king, as usual, happened to glance at the spot where Aryun's palace had been standing without a trace. He thought there might be something wrong with his eyes after looking several times, but indeed, it was Aryun's palace. So, the king immediately prepared his horse and quickly rode toward Aryun's palace. 

Aryun awoke at dawn, dressed in splendid attire, and went out to see the king riding toward him. He hurried down the stone stairs to welcome the king off his horse and led him to the princess's room. 

As soon as the king saw the princess, he couldn't help but embrace her, letting tears stream down his cheeks. Those who witnessed this scene were astonished and speechless for a while. 

Afterwards, upon hearing the detailed story, the king apologized for the previous day's actions, saying, "Aryun, please forgive me for the rashness I displayed yesterday. After losing my daughter, I was so consumed with these thoughts that I lost sight of everything else." Aryun responded, "I do not have any grievances against Your Majesty. The actions you took were not unreasonable. The troubles we experienced for a while stemmed entirely from that one magician from 'Africa'." 

With this, Aryun took the captured magician and banished him from the kingdom. Several years later, the king passed away peacefully, and since there was no other child, the princess, with Aryun by her side, ruled over the kingdom well, raising future generations, ensuring that the affairs of state were well taken care of. -(End)-

**Summary**  

The story narrates the adventures of Aryun, who navigates the magical and perilous events surrounding a lost palace and the manipulative actions of a magician. It showcases the themes of loyalty, resilience, and the restoration of rightful order, culminating in Aryun's reunion with the princess and the king. This tale serves as a rich cultural metaphor, possibly reflecting the historical struggles against colonial powers and the restoration of sovereignty during the 1930s, a crucial time in Korea's fight against Japanese occupation. Historians might find the portrayal of the power dynamics and the themes of oppression relevant in understanding the cultural psyche during this period.

그후 얼마 지나지 안어서 대궐은 다시 중국 땅으로 옴겨저 갓는데 들창하나 상하지 안코 감쪽 가탯습니다. 아련이의 대궐이 중국땅으로 옴겨져 가든 그 이튼날 아침이엇습니다. 임금님은 다른 날과 가티 아련이의 대궐이 서 잇든 자리를 무심코 바라보시랴니까 어제까지도 주초돌 하나 업든 아련이의 대궐터전에 홀륭한 대궐이 웃둑 소사 잇지 안켓습니까? 임금님은 처음에는 내눈에 무슨 병통이 생겻나 보다 하야 몃 번이나 보고 자세히 바라보섯지만은 틀림업는 아련이의 대궐입니다. 그래서 임금님은 그 즉시 말준비를 시켜 가지고 말을 타고 아련이의 대궐로 살가티 달렷습니다.
아련이는 새벽 일즉이 일어나서 훌륭한 옷으로 가러 입고 박글 내여다 보랴니까 임금님이 말을 타고 달려오는 것이 눈에 띄엿습니다. 그래 돌층게 아래에 까지 달려나려가서 임금님을 말우에서 부측해 내렷습니다. 그리고 임금님을 왕녀 방으로 안내햇습니다.
임금님은 왕녀를 보시자마자 가슴에 끼어 안드니 깁분 눈물을 끄칠줄 몰으고 흘리섯습니다. 이 광경을 보는 사람마다 얼마동안은 어안이 벙벙하야 말도 잘못햇습니다.
임금님은 그후의 이야기를 자세히 들으시드니 전날 당신의 란폭(亂暴)하엿든 것을 봇내 사과하시면서
「아련이 여보게. 내가 전날 자네에게 몹시 햇든 것을 용서해 주게. 나는 딸 하나 일흔 생각에 이 일 저 일을 생각할 겨를이 업섯섯네.」
하고 말슴햇습니다.
「임금께 대하야서는 조고마한 불평도 저는 업습니다. 임금님께서 하신 처사도 무리는 아니라고 생각합니다. 저이들을 한동안 그가티 못살게 군 것은 「아푸리카」의 요술쟁이 그 한놈이엇습니다.」
아련이는 이가티 대답햇습니다.
아련이 손에 붓잡힌 요술쟁이는 람푸만 빼앗고 나라 박그로 쪼차 내버렷습니다. 그후 대여섯 해 후에 임금님은 나히만하 세상을 떠나시고 그 슬하에 한 분 박게 업는 왕녀가 여왕으로 왕위에 나아가게 되어 아련이와 함께 두 내외가 나라 일을 잘 다스려 간 것은 말할 것도 업고 자손도 만하서 대대로 나라 정사(政事)가 잘 다려젓다 합니다. -(끗)-＜42＞
