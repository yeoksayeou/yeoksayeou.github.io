Prize Questions  
1. Even if you try to stop it from coming, it will still come, and even if you try to hold it back, it will still go. What is this?  

2. On this Earth where we live, there is a place that is the farthest and most unreachable. Where is that?  

* This time, I’ve presented somewhat easier questions. Last month’s edition had questions that seemed easier yet were surprisingly difficult, so the number of submissions dropped significantly. Therefore, this time I selected problems that anyone reading this book would be able to solve. I hope everyone who has not submitted anything before will take the lead this time and send in their answers. (Prize Section)  

**Note Regarding Prize Submissions**  
The submissions must arrive at the headquarters by June 28.  

Please write your answers concisely on a postcard and be sure to include your address and name.  

Attach the reader’s certificate found on this page to the answer sheet. If not attached, it will be invalid.  

On the envelope, be sure to write "Kyŏngsŏng Kaebiksa Separate Universe Prize Division" and on the back, write "Manuscript Inside" in ink, sealing it securely and affixing two won stamps. If "Manuscript Inside" is not written, a penalty will be incurred. <66>  

**Summary**  

The article presents two prize questions for readers to solve and submit for a chance to win, encouraging wider participation after a previous issue saw a reduced response. The article reflects the magazine's attempt to engage its audience in enjoyable intellectual activities during the colonial period, showcasing the cultural dimensions of life under Japanese rule. Historians might find the format and content illustrative of the ways that cultural publications navigated colonial realities while fostering community among readers.
