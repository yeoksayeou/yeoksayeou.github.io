기사제목	제5회 「新流行小曲」 大懸常募集!! (The 5th "New Trend Song" Grand Prize Recruitment!!)  
기사형태	사고·편집후기 (Advertisement/Editorial Note)  

The 5th "New Trend Song" Grand Prize Recruitment!!  
Winning entries will be immediately composed and recorded in this magazine and on records!!  
The prize is 10 won for each selected entry and 5 won for those that are chosen for participation; submissions are due by June 25.  
Writers have the freedom to choose the content of their songs; however, anything too vulgar must be absolutely avoided. It is preferred to use simple language and straightforward content that anyone can understand.  
Songs should be composed in 345 or 435, 44, 334, or 3443 rhythms, consisting of 3 to 4 lines per stanza, and no more than 3 stanzas in total (however, 44 rhythm can go up to 4 stanzas).  
Submissions for this 5th recruitment must arrive at our headquarters by June 25; any submissions afterward will be held for the 6th recruitment.  
The results of the 4th recruitment will be published in next month's issue, and the winning songs will be composed in collaboration with professionals, ensuring placement in this magazine as well as on recorded media for promotion.  
There is no limit on the number of selected works; any number of quality entries will be considered. The awards will be as stated: 10 won (cash) for each selected entry, and 5 won (cash) for those receiving participation credit. Prizes will be sent within two weeks after the announcement (the rights to published and recorded works are completely held by the magazine).  
The selection will be handled by the magazine's editorial staff and esteemed individuals in the field. When sending your submissions, please write on the envelope "Contest Song Manuscript" and send it to Gyeongun-dong, Gyeongseong, Han'byeolguk, Editorial Department of Byeolgeunkon.  
Submitted manuscripts will not be returned.＜25＞

**Summary**

This advertisement announces the 5th recruitment for the "New Trend Song" contest, aimed at encouraging songwriters to submit simple and accessible lyrics for public consumption. The emphasis on easy language reflects a period of cultural expression during colonial Korea, where the importance of popular culture was rising. Historians might find the contest indicative of attempts to foster a national identity through popular music in the face of colonial pressures.
