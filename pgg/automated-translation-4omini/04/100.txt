People on the Road  
An Jae-hong  

Although he seems peaceful on the inside, he constantly expresses complaints on the outside. A gentleman with a handsome face, wearing horn-rimmed glasses and a luxurious overcoat made from otter fur, is quietly disembarking from a rickshaw. He appears to be a senior official in the administration, or perhaps a prosperous middle-aged man with money; this gentleman is An Jae-hong, the editor-in-chief of the Chosun Ilbo. Whether he is writing an editorial, critiquing a poem, leading the Unnamed Association, or arranging for the New Society, wherever he goes, he is often seen hurriedly riding a rickshaw and bustling about. However, even while he is so busy, he can still invent nonsense, which shows just how busy he must be.

Park Won-hee  

In a white jeogori (top) and white chima (skirt), carrying a baby like a mortar out of a traditional Korean mill, with disheveled hair, a woman is making her way through the streets of Jaedong neighborhood. Sometimes, she looks like a woman taking care of someone else's child, and other times, like a woman returning home after moving to Bukgando. However, upon closer inspection, this is the well-known Park Won-hee, famous for her contributions to the Korean women's movement. Last year, she bid farewell to her beloved husband, Kim Sa-guk, on the eternal road, and now she lives alone in the cold workshop, cherishing her only child, a fragile daughter, and despite the harsh winter winds, she goes to Andong Hospital to take care of her sick child. Who among those who know her would not shed tears of sympathy?

  
**Summary**  

This article paints a picture of two individuals—An Jae-hong, a prominent editor, and Park Won-hee, a notable figure in the women's movement—who are navigating the challenges of life in colonial Korea. An is depicted as a busy man immersed in his professional engagements while Park's narrative reflects the struggles of a single mother facing adversity after the loss of her husband. The article highlights the different social roles and challenges faced by men and women in the colonial context, revealing the personal and societal struggles of individuals during this period, which could intrigue historians exploring gender roles and social dynamics in colonial Korea.

路上의 人
安在鴻

속은 화평하야도 입에는 항상 불평이 만흔 듯. 준수한 얼골에 뿔테 안경을 쓰고, 수달피털 대인 외투입고 그리고 인력거 우에 점잔히 안저 오는 신사한분. 관리면 고등관이요, 안이면 돈만흔 중년신사로 보히는 조흔신수 그이가 朝鮮日報의 主筆로 게신 民世 安在鴻씨이다. 五字백이 제목 시평 쓰시랴 사설 쓰시랴 無名會일 보시랴 新幹會주선하시랴 가시는 곳 보시는 일이 만하서 어느 때던지 저러케 밧브게 인력거를 타시고 분주히 다니신다. 그러나 그러케 밧브신 중에도 멍텅구리까지 고안해 내신다니 참말 밧브실 것이다.


朴元熙

흰저고리 흰치마에 어린 아기를 절구통가티 들처업고 부수숙한 트레머리로 덥펄덥펄하며 재동네 거리로 지내가는 女子한분-엇지보면 남의 집 아희 보아주는 부인도 갓고 엇지 보면 북간도에 이사갓다가 고국으로 도라오는 부인도 갓다. 그러나 자서이 보면 朝鮮녀성운동에 이름이 놉흔 박원희씨다. 그는 작년에 사랑하는 남편 김사국씨를 영원한 길로 이별하고 다만 일뎜의 혈육인 가련한 따님 한 분을 장중 보옥가티 사랑하며 쓸쓸한 공방에서 홀로 세월을 보내는 터에 그 따님이 병이 나서 요새이 그 모진 눈바람을 무릅쓰고 안동병원으로 다니시는 터이다. 그의 사정을 아는 사람-그 누가 동정의 눈물을 흘리지 안이하랴.<39>
