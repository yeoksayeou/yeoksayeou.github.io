Marriage Costs in Various Countries  

Country	1 Year 10,000 Won Family	1 Year 2,000 Won Family  
England	8/10	10%  
France	10%	10%  
Germany	10%	10%  
United States	20%	20%  
Italy	40%	40%  
Spain	50%	70%  
Russia	80%	80%  
Japan	200%	250%  
China	300%	300%  

From this, it can be seen that countries that spend less on wedding costs tend to have lower financial demands, while Eastern people are spending several times more than Westerners. The marriage costs for our Korean families are not well understood due to insufficient research, but originally our customs imitated those of China. Furthermore, recently, the youth, both men and women, obliviously chant "new style! new style!" and take two, three, or even four cars to various locations such as churches (sacred places) to show off to many people, feeling a form of envy. They hold receptions at places like gardens or halls and feel that a wedding is not complete unless the images of the bride and groom are advertised in the newspaper. Their hopes and demands are far stronger from women than from men, and the so-called modern girls are more insistent than the old-fashioned ones. 

It seems that they must be satisfied with the sight of the bride and groom adorned with extravagant gold and silver ornaments at the wedding ceremony, as they likely find joy in such appearances. I heard from an acquaintance that a certain student spent 200 won for his wedding to a female high school student, while a gentleman working at a factory had a modern wedding for just 600 won. I do not know how much income these families had, but it seems that they may have merely taken from their parents’ pockets or borrowed from others.

However, even if the results are good, perhaps those who spent less may suffer more. From the very next day after the wedding, the pressure from creditors for payment intensifies, and the new wife’s demands increasingly multiply to a point where it becomes unsustainable. As a result, the husband finds it natural that pleasant words will not come forth, leading to the disruption of family peace. 

Should one spend less on wedding costs to maintain everlasting family harmony? Or should one seek superficial appearances at the ceremony and face distress thereafter? Those wishing to marry should take time to reflect on these matters. <51>  

**Summary**  

This article explores the costs associated with weddings across different countries, illustrating the significant disparities between Eastern and Western cultures. It reflects on the increasing demands imposed on newlyweds, particularly pointing out how Korean customs have evolved often by imitating Chinese traditions while also critiquing modern trends that prioritize extravagance. Historians might find the changing dynamics of marital expectations during the colonial period particularly revealing, especially concerning class differences and the pressures exerted on families in a rapidly modernizing society.

결혼비를 조금 들 쓰고 가정의 평화를 영원히 유지할 것인가? 결혼시의 외모만 뵈고 그 이튼날부터 괴롬을 당하여야 오를 것인가? 결혼하랴는 남녀는 한번 숙려할 필요가 잇는가 한다.<51>
