趣味잇는 統計室  

According to recent investigations, the number of chickens raised in the territory of the United States is five hundred and fifty-nine million, and the eggs laid by these chickens in a year amount to approximately one billion nine hundred and twenty-one million dozen (a dozen consists of twelve). This is worth about one billion seven hundred and ninety million dollars (one dollar equals about two won). 

When this is distributed among the entire population of the United States, it breaks down to about half an egg per person per day. Alternatively, the price of these eggs corresponds to one-third of the annual production value of corn (玉蜀黍), one-half of that of wheat (小麥), and is nearly equivalent to the annual production value of cotton (木棉). If we were to distribute this amount of eggs among the Korean population, assuming a population of twenty million, each individual could eat thirty-one eggs per day, and still have over two hundred left by the year's end. 

It seems that in a rich country, there is an astonishing abundance of eggs, yet there are still impoverished individuals in the U.S. who cannot afford even one egg throughout the year. Indeed, the world is remarkably unequal!

The Actual Life Expectancy of Men and Women  

Recent statistics indicate that the average life expectancy is forty-four years and five months for men and forty-four years and seven months for women. It appears that women tend to live longer than men on average.

The Muscles Used in a Smile  

It is said that to frown, a person uses sixty-four muscles, while only thirteen muscles are used to smile. Thus, it seems that frowning requires about five times more effort than smiling, which implies that people who can't help but frown tend to age faster.  

**Summary**  

This article presents various interesting statistics regarding chickens and eggs in the United States, emphasizing the abundance of resources in wealthier nations compared to the scarcity faced by some individuals, even in those rich countries. It also touches upon life expectancy differences between genders and physical aspects of smiling versus frowning. From a historical perspective, this reflects the socioeconomic disparities during the colonial period, highlighting how even in an era of abundance, inequality persisted, something of potential interest to historians studying experiences of colonial Korea and global disparities of wealth.
