발행일	1927년 02월 01일  
기사제목	무슨 까닭 (What is the Reason)  
기사형태	잡저 (Miscellaneous Writing)  

What is the reason that while criticizing the yangban (literati class), if one themselves is yangban, they are respected, but if they are a commoner, they are despised?  

What is the reason that while wearing the clothing of the dead is considered disgraceful, they flaunt a dead kim's (김) fur coat around their neck as if it were a fashionable ornament?  

What is the reason that while people are reluctant to eat leftover food from others, they do not mind living with a husband or wife who belongs to others?  

What is the reason that those who don the bobbed hairstyle think of themselves as modern women and mock others, yet all girls end up sporting long hair in the end?  

What is the reason that those who call themselves poor wear suits better than others?  

What is the reason that women who profess to live single quietly have a romantic partner on the side? <94>  

**Summary**  

This article poses a series of rhetorical questions that challenge societal norms and double standards in early 20th-century Korean society. It explores themes of class, integrity, modernity, and hypocrisy, revealing contradictions in behavior and attitudes among different social classes. Historians might find the article interesting as it reflects the complexities of identity and societal expectations during a time of significant change under Japanese colonial rule, highlighting tensions between traditional values and the emerging modern era.
