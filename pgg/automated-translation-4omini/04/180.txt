Nine Women’s School Bazaar Nine Scenes  
						Sang S, Dori  
The Kyosun Daily has been causing a stir saying that the Nine School Union Bazaar ("빠사희") hosted by the school is something great to look at, so on this day, I took my "Dori" and went to see how amazing it is. On the day before the major cold, the chilly wind made a slight sting against my ears and cheeks. "Get away! If it's not a competition among the girls' schools, I'm not going to see it," I thought angrily, as I encountered the tall teacher from the sales department. It was 1:30 PM! (Sang S)

As we pushed through the crowd on Jongno's busy street, we sneaked in through the main entrance of the Jongno Youth Club and pretended not to see the people selling tickets as we went up the stairs. They were guiding us to the auditorium on the right side there. There were pretty much no decorations on the doors or windows.

Kyungnyo

Upon entering, the very first thing on the right side was the Kyungsung Women’s High School. It is a rigid public school with a name that sounds hard, at least in that aspect. Even before noticing the sign above the display saying "Kyungsung Public Women’s High School," the large and imposing face of Ms. Son Jeong-gye was easily recognizable.

Among the products were women's handbags and various items made with care, as well as handkerchiefs and children's clothes which caught my attention. The effort to avoid flashy combs and pick practical and modest ones is commendable, but the Japanese style that shows up here and there is perhaps unavoidable for this school... still, Ms. Son's courteous demeanor seemed to charm many visitors (Dori).

Rihwa

Next up, taking a position in the southeast corner with a display arranged neatly was Rihwa School. Much talked about as a high-class school for the past ten years, it attracted even more attention. The prominently displayed knitted caps caught my eye, and there were many clean aprons and handkerchiefs, but what stood out more than anything else was a sort of boys' traditional outfit (improved dress) and children's underwear. It was thoughtful of the school to provide these items especially since these adaptations have shown improvements over time. Last year they merged pants and jackets into one piece, but complaints arose that the pants were uncomfortable when sleeping, so this year, they have been separated again. The boys of this school, which is known as a haven for girls, have received gratitude from men for this consideration. It is rumored that many boys wore these outfits... While I was standing there, one gentleman sneered, saying, "Those outfits were made by the girls of Rihwa, so if you wear them, you should appreciate them more." This seems somewhat excessive. The set is indeed a necessity worth 250 won. (Sang S)

Jeongsin

In the northeast corner, arranged in the shape of a 'ㄱ' was the Jeongsin School with the faces of Ms. Kim Young-sun and Ms. Bang Shin-young visible. Their main products were knitted items and ladies' handkerchiefs, but their more notable feature was traditional garments. The specially crafted and eye-catching jackets and skirts that are hand-made for the ladies spark envy amongst many children visiting: "I want that jacket too," they would exclaim. It seems these were being sold particularly well, as westerners and Japanese seem to have bought them quite a bit. This Quirky traditional clothing made quite the exhibit from this school, alongside aprons, caps, and ties. Ms. Bang Shin-young’s pastries and sweets added to the display's allure, shining bright objects that would make fine gifts for ladies visiting the bazaar (Dori).

Sookmyung

There were three schools showcased in this room and the auditorium seems to be the main venue for the gathering, where students visiting looked to innovate while squeezing through the narrow entrance heading toward the auditorium. On the east wall was Sookmyung School—which immediately attracts attentive glances. Adorned with a pink backdrop and blue and white combs painting delicate lily of the valley, Sookmyung's emblem—one character at a time—beautifully engages the soul. During this temporary sale event, these schools collectively gathered while the public crowded in, and however much effort they put into showcasing their products, Sookmyung's superior organization stood out. It evidently required dedication to display their products, taking advantage of an event that draws the largest crowd.

There is also another notable feat, albeit minor; the event could be more impressive. Based on their sold products last year, they seem to prominently favor useful items for the bazaar. Moreover, though many textile products were displayed, they had thoughtfully arranged an array of ties marked as “Tie Collection,” making it catch eyes more than usual. It may not seem like a big deal, but looking at the bazaar, this seemed unique since it was the only item showcased across all nine schools. Interestingly, Sookmyung had prepared a script and answers beforehand, asking, “How do you manage such a good presentation?” and wishing the situation would remain similar (Sang S).

Baehwa

Following Sookmyung, taking the northeast corner was Baehwa School, quietly operating like “a hidden rose in the flower garden.” They showcased a variety of knitted items but what stood out was a hand-painted landscape frame. The artwork was from the renowned artist Kim Ui-sik; however, it had to be highlighted that part of what they presented appeared to be older Japanese-style prints, which was disappointing. Nevertheless, the beauty in the details was evident, and it is said, "If you need a skilled embroiderer, go to Baehwa School." (Dori)

Jinmyung

In the middle of the Sookmyung auditorium stood the serene Jinmyung Women’s School. It gives off a distinctly Korean aroma, reminding us of the tender, diligent efforts of the teachers in making gradual but meticulous improvements. Influential phrases like “one should not be complacent” echoed amidst their displayed products. Beautifully crafted traditional footgear—the signature of Korean clothing—left an impression of authenticity. What charms this school is that it displays the essence of traditional Korean artistry. Interestingly, among the items were simple yet polite knapsacks made in black fabric by elementary school students which communicated far more than mere decorative items. The urge to embrace these traditional dresses speaks volumes about one's roots. As they say, "If your husband wears a suit, one can find splendidly crafted neckties too” and sweet, humble aprons fit for children further maintain interest at the bazaar. "What? It’s all stunning?" I laughed, noticing well-made waisak and welcoming the recognition each display received (Sang S).

Songgo

In the heart of the auditorium facing the main stage, right at the more prominent spot was indeed where the luxurious wonders of Songgo School flourished. Displaying woven textiles in splendid hues, its place was considerably bedecked with various goods, incomparable to other schools. The superior embroidery here made me assume that it could resonate from some north-eastern high school, until later learning from the teachers that it was not a women’s school at all. A man’s place indeed held a true surprise.

However, it wasn’t my fault if my vision misled me. As the saying goes, “While both genders cherish the gifted women, the attraction to the school’s display has seemingly made them fall in love with it.” If permitted, the commercial goods should certainly thrive there. Nevertheless, the promotion efforts of Songgo made intriguing steps, likely gaining admiration not only from their school but also from the youthful community. (Dori)

Guhwa

In the northwest corner tucked away by an alley arranged in a 'ㄱ' shape was Kunhwa School. Many assume the name Guk Jeong was created due to their ongoing theatrical and musical activities that often prompt sight. However, within the diverse items they showcased, they equally made substantial contributions as the school had a unique dedication to aesthetics, gracefully presenting everything from bamboo frames to lovingly crafted traditional vessel designs. It seems that in the niche products they sold, the children's clogs and fine shoes offered a blend of the traditional but, surprisingly, with intensely vibrant colors and less practicality that offered mere decorative merits rather than utility.

But aside from those, the kimchi and traditional textiles bewitched more as those represented the unique essence of Korea, abundantly contributing to the beauty that encapsulated their space, including the especially notable embroidered dresses by Kim Ga-i, commonly referred to as the school’s pride (Sang S). 

Dongdeok

The Dongdeok School took position midway between Jinmyung School and another esteemed establishment and had a rich assembly of items, boasting numerous unique goods. Among their showcase were knitted hats aplenty; beautiful foot attractscloths; plus much more like pockets and bags, all laid out in a variety of colors, evidently alluring many visitors. Each had been crafted with sincere attention to detail, attracting attention across class of folk. Their approach in showcasing picturesque frames in contrast to that of competitors, favoring scenic landscapes, pointed out the depth of their artistic ambition. The dedicative framework of backgrounds, paired with the nuanced art, presented between traditional goods highlighted the emphasis of the teachers here.

As they instilled the sentiment to "Become a new woman, a new Korean woman," which highlighted their efforts in nurturing future responsible citizens, one finds evidence of their well-polished communication streams that can only be attributed to much hard work throughout the year. Today, I would like to offer each future Korean mother a simple yet profound blessing as they pave the professional routes to success. (Sang S)

General Observations

Having wrapped up my viewing of all nine schools, I am left with nothing else to see except the dining area; however, this space is merely an empty shell, having no trace of human life, making everything feel hollow... I now consider what I should write about... 

A tall male student with a short jacket and coarse shoes stood in line, while two males were joking about, alternately pinching one another. "They’re not even looking to buy anything, instead just looking at the people. Stop eyeing what’s of no use, will you?" "Ah, come on! It seems they’re doing all that to score some girls, isn’t that idiotic?" 

As the comical exchanges carried on, I noticed they were mere acquaintances mounting a plummeting joke awaiting their right moment rather than having any intent to shop around. 

Men and women entered and exited, mingling while students in hairstyling uniform demeaned themselves as they walked through, two discussing the disparity in costs for handmade clothes purchased from various students. "Look! The one I got goes for seven won while hers is six!” one joked while the other proudly retorted from a southern school. 

“Eh, it’s just a game; neither can claim they made theirs," they laughed in pure fun amidst their comparisons, making for a light-hearted view on a normally serious affair. Such little moments drew perhaps the most genuine of smiles amidst the otherwise weighed themes of commerce. (Dori)

Additional Remarks

I’m not sure if it’s a frivolous statement, but one thought has lingered with me after having witnessed this event. The Kyosun Daily has mused about schools displaying and competing against each other; while some boast of their merits, many shed silent tears from behind. 

“Mother! Give me money for the sewing kit!” A girl, bundled with her books, adjusted atop her tense brow for a moment's observation as she sought help from her struggling mother. 

“Didn’t you buy it the other day? What more do you need?” The mother's eye held a painful frown, perhaps from many elements surrounding their conversations. 

“We need to prepare bedding for the fast if we want to enter the bazaar school, as it always fared better at every showcase," this year, they were told, everyone needed to put together the best offerings. 

The girl held back her tears, adding, “But today is the sewing time, and if I don’t arrive carrying something special, I’d receive a score deduction!” 

The time pressed down on them, and the daughter found her eyes watering as the mother reinforced to comfort. This is a true story.   

However, as candid as the calibre might seem, it is crucial for teachers as well as the shimmering students not only to worry about competition but more so concerning the essence of nurturing relations to form growth that profits students from within their backgrounds. Those burdened by circumstance deserve careful attention as opposed to the demand for money to keep up—transcendence should not be bound by wealth alone. (Sang S)  

In this bazaar, the Dong-A Women’s Association generously took charge, ensuring every school had wrapped their goods using freely distributed paper on which they printed their promo marks for their showcases as a subtle yet strong advertising tool. (Dori)

To conclude, I would like to leave a note for each school; I alluded to this briefly, however introducing new items and including brief descriptions effectively captivates attention. Although it is indeed essential for awareness of newly unveiled wallet designs to get recognition, even if individuals have seen those, detailing how they were crafted, supplies used, or what they may facilitate usefulness aids in binding connections with the audience, thus enhancing purchase inclinations.

Another point to mention, knitted products, gloves, and the like were undoubtedly not traditionally Korean. However, it seems the bags, mats, knitting products, etc., presented by a new generation convey a surprising consistency back to decades past, where authenticity prided itself in the past. 

While some efforts appeared commendable in displaying products such as the illustration of a deer or phoenix waving across patterns, the major elements hadn't innovatively distinguished themselves from past forms. What I observed mostly remained unchanged—a hard truth calling into question the students' innovative spirit.

Although the Baehwa school did stand out with innovative concepts, still I did not spot other efforts qualifying contemporary vibrations despite valuable lessons and considerations for broader ideas permeating. Thus, I urge for progressively creative thinkers helping with sewing and embroidery crafts who can straightforwardly apply their minds into these present reminders. (Sang S)

This article is indeed lengthy, thus I’ll draw to a close. However, such events should be appreciated yearly or at notable intervals when exams take a backseat to popular gatherings, as the lunar year simply doesn’t hold value against quantity held within sincere effort. I conclude my reflections here (Dori).  

Summary  

This article provides a rich description of the products displayed at a women’s school bazaar in 1927 Korea. It highlights nine different schools, each showcasing their unique craftsmanship from traditional clothing to practical items, reflecting the intersection of modernization and tradition during the colonial period. Historians may find it interesting how this event served as a microcosm of the educational capabilities of women during this time, illustrating both the cultural aspirations and economic struggles underlying their education.

종로 큰 길로 연긔가티 니러나는 몬지 속을 휘젓고 가서 종로청년회관 정문으로 쑥 드러서서 표파는 사람은 본체만체 하고 층계 우로 올러가니 대강당 압 동편긔도 실로 드러 가라고 안내를 하는대. 방문이나 텬뎡이나 아모 별다른 장식도 업섯다.

京女

드러서자 바른 편으로 맨먼저 느러놋코 안츤데가 경성녀자고등보통학교. 이름에 단하나 딱딱하게 석기인 공립학교이다.
진렬선 반 우에 힁으로 경성공립녀자고등보통학교라고 써붓친것보다도 孫貞奎씨의 크고 어글어글한 얼골이 더 먼저 눈에 잘 뜨인다.
수 노흔 女子의 손가방 뎐등덥개 가튼 것은 대롱대롱 달어놋코 손수건 어린애 압치마도 만흐나 수노은 그림과 방석이 뎨일 눈에 뜨이는데 빗을 화려한 것을 피하고 실용뎍이요 검박한 빗을 고르기에 마음을 쓴 것은 층찬할 일이지만 군데군데에 일본냄새가 만흔 것은 이 학교로서는 엇절수 업는 일이라 할넌지...그래도 손선생의 억구수한 응대에 손님의 조흔 마음을 적지 안히 사는 것 갓다(돌)

梨花

고다음 동남구석을 차지하고 류리창 압헤 진렬한 곳이 리화학교이다. 십년전부터 하이카라학교라고 소문만턴 학교니만큼 주목을 더 끈다. 털실편물로 된 짠겟트 모자가튼 것을 힘써 만히 내인 것이 눈에 뜨이고 깻끗한 에푸롱과 손수건도 만히 잇스나<52>아모것 보다도 남자의 자리옷(개량침대)과 어린이들의 내의를 불소히 내여 노은 것은 조흔 생각이다. 더구나 남자의 자리옷 가튼 것은 개량에 또 개량을 더한 것이라는데 작년에는 바지저고리를 한데 붓처지엇더니 잘 때에 바지가 긔여 올나서 불편하다고 금년에는 따로 떼여 맨들엇다 하니 남자 금접(禁接)의 처녀 나라인 이 학교에서 무던히 마음을 써 준 것을 세상 남자들은 감사해야 할 것이다. 팔니기도 남자의 자리옷이 만히 팔니엿다고...내 엽헤 서서 듯고 잇던 양복장이 한분이 흥!하고 도라스면서 『리화학당처녀가 맨들어 준 것이니 하고 입고자면 더 땃듯할 줄 아는게지...』하는 것은 지나친 말이다. 갑은 상하 이원오십전 조흔 필요한 물건이다.(双S)

貞信

그 엽헤 동북 구석에 역시 ㄱ자로 진렬한 곳에 김영순
방신영 두분의 얼골이 보이니 뭇지 안어도 뎡신녀학교이다. 털실편물과 아씨네 손수건 만흔 것이 뎨일 특색인데 그보다도 더 유표한 특색은 각시옷이다. 족-고만 족-고만 손바닥보다 더 족고만 색저고리와 색치마 아이그머니 그 엽헤 어린이 토수짝만한 이부자리까지 펴노앗네...아가들이 보면 『각씨 맨들어서 닙히겟다하고 저마다 욕심 낼 것이다. 팔니기도 벌서 만히 팔렷는데 서양사람과 일본사람에게 만히 팔렷다하니 아마도 朝鮮의복견본으로 구해가는 모양이다. 에푸롱과 턱밧기 모자와 넥타이도 만치만 왜 방신영씨의 약식이나 과자를 니저 버렷는지...이학교의 가장 특별한 자랑거리가 될 뿐 아니라 빠사회 왓다가는 부인네의 선물거리로도 훌륭하엿슬터인데..(돌)

淑明

이방에는 세 학교뿐이고 대강당이 본 회댱인 모양인데 구경온 여학생들과 마조치면서 좁은 문을 베집고 대강당으로 건너 스닛가 동편벽을 길게 차지한 곳이 숙명학교이다. 출품한 물건을 보기 전에 먼저 층찬할 것이 잇다. 분홍바탕에 푸른 빗 흰 빗으로 고어엽본 방울꼿(鈴蘭)을 그린 숙명다운 숙명의 마-크우에 학교 일홈을 한자씩 한자씩 써븟친 것이 엇더케 정답고도 부드럽고도 강하게 사람의 마음을 꺼는지...한때 림시일망정 물건 파는 이의 맵시인는 솜씨로나 이럿케 각 여학교가 모다 한 자리에 모히고 시민이 가장 만히 꼬이는 긔회를 리용하는 학교선전으로나 누구나 해야 할 것을 아모도 못하얏는데 숙명의 녑녑한 사무실은 그것을 닛지 안엇다. 분명히 층찬밧을만한 일이다.
그리고 또 한가지 그리 대단한 것은 아니나 족곰쯤은 층찬해도 조흔 일이 잇다. 출품한 물건으로 보아 작년에 가장 만히 팔은 경험도 잇섯겟지만은 손쉽게 팔닐 실용품만 힘써 출품한 것이 잘 생각한 일이요 터레버선 턱밧기도 만치만은 편물 중심인 것이 한 특색이라 하겟는데 따로히 족곰쯤 층찬해 주어도 좃타 할 일은 그리 눈에 뜨이는 일은 아니나 넥타이 거는 것 전에 그리 흔히 보이지 안튼 것을 걸어놋코 그 엽 <53>헤 『넥타이 거는것』이라고 써 뭇처서 손의 눈을 꺼은 것이다. 그닥지 신통할 것은 못되나 그러나 이번 빠사회에서는 아홉학교를 통하야 이것이 단 한 장 뿐인 뎜으로 보아 그냥 지나처 버릴 것이 아니라고 생각한다. 이상하게 숙명치가 내차레가 되어서 『너는 엇재 그리 속명을 몹시 층찬하느냐』 겟스니 고만두자 설명 써붓치는가 부는 잇다가 다시 이 약이 할 셈하고.(双S)

培花

가난한 어머니의 가슴이 찐-하야 될 수만 잇스면 구해주려고 리웃집에 가서 취해 보려다가 그것도 못엇고 빗진 죄인갓치 우는 듯한 소리로
『이애야. 시간 늣겟스니 오늘은 그냥 가렴으나. 잇다가라도 어더 보아서 내일 가지고 가렴으나』
『아이 오늘이 재봉시간이야요. 오늘 안가지고 오는 사람은 뎜ㅅ수를 깍는다는데 그냥 가면 엇더케 해요』
시간은 느저지고 그냥 갈 수는 업고 소녀의 눈에는 눈물이 글성글성하고 그것을 보는 가난한 어머니도 도라서서 눈물을 씻는다. 이것은 사실담이다.
그러지 안하도 생각적은 녀선생님은 갓흔 학생도 무명감보다는 비단이라야 먼저 번을 그려주는 고로 가난한 어머니와 불상한 소녀들이 눈물을 짓는데 월사금도 못내서 학교에 못 갈 디경인 학생에게 얼마나 못할 것인 것을 간혹 가다가라도 생각하는 때가 잇서야 한다. 돈으로 괴롭게 사는 사람에게 학교에서쯤은 돈으로 덜을 렬도 좃치 안흐냐(双S)
이번 빠사회에 동아부인상회가 약은 짓 하엿다. 각 학교에서 물건 싸는데에 쓸 싸개지를 전부 동아부인상회에서 자담하야 자긔네 광고박인 종의를 주어 그것으로 모다 싸 보내게 한 것은 령리한 광고수단이다.(돌)
끗흐로 각 학교에 한 말슴 할 말이 잇다. 앗가도 잠간 말하다가 말엇지만은 새로운 물건 엽헤 간단한 소개의 말을 써서 븟치는 것은 대단히 중요한 일이다.
새로운 물건을 소개하야 알니우는 데에도 물론 필요한 일이지만은 설사 다-아는 것이라 하드래도 이것은 무엇으로 엇더케 맨든 것이라던지 어느 뎜을 개량하야 어데에 필요한 것이라던지 간단하게 조흔 말로 소개하는 것은 그 물건과 보는 이의 사이에 친밀성을 더 돗탑게 하고 사고십은 마음을 충동하는 데에도 대단히 효과 잇는 일이다.
그 다음에 한가지 할 말은 편물, 뎐등덥개 장갑갓흔 것은 원래 전에 朝鮮가뎡에 업던 것이닛가 문뎨가 아니지만은 수저집 방석 타래버선 주머니 벼개모 갓흔 것은 삼십이십년 전 옛날 색씨가 맨든 것이나 지금 월사금 내고 학교에 가서 배흔 것이나 조곰도 다른 것 새로운 것이 업다는 것이다.<58>
타래버선에 수 노은 것은 아가의 버섯으로는 대단히 조흔 것이다. 그러나 지금 녀학교에서 맨든 꼿모양이나...십년전 것이나 밤낫 그 모양 그 본이요 벼개모 괴불도 밤낫 그 모양 그 본 인 것은 녀학교의 자수선생님이 넘어도 이 뎜에 무심한 것을 폭로시킨 것이라 할 것이다. 다행히 배화녀학교 출품중에 벼개모에 사슴 봉황을 안놋코 그대신 디구(地球)동반구 서반구를 그리고 세계디도를 수노은 것 하나를 본 것 외에는 하나도 예전 것을 예전 것 본틀대로 그대로 두면서 더 좃케 고처 보느라고 마음 쓴 것을 보지 못한 것은 섭섭한 일이다. 좀더 손ㅅ재조외에 머리(頭腦)를 쓰는 재봉 자수선생님이 생겨주기를 바란다.(双S)
