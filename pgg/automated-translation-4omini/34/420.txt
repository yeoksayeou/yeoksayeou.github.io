探偵小說 凶家의 秘密(4), (原名...綠色의 門)  

The outline of the previous issue: A person named Silverstern Trent learned of a sinister house in a place called Sedron (세드로-ㄴ) when he accidentally overheard words from Claude Norton, an ailing traveler he was rescuing. The next night, a mysterious woman named Henry Etta West came to warn Trent that the house was dangerous. One of Koes' subordinates, who was monitoring the actions of these two, reported the situation to Koes. Koes then ordered another subordinate named Franklin to keep an eye on them.

Getting out of the car on the hillside above Sedron, Franklin took steps toward the sinister house. The night air was very chilly, and a pale crescent moon hung in the western sky. He went up the hill and looked around. The surrounding woods were thick, and there were no signs of life from the shadows. 

Having received orders to monitor West’s actions from Koes, he hesitated on how to begin his task. While buying cigarettes in the neighboring town, he had tried to gather some information, but nothing significant came up. It was only known among the townsfolk that West was a suspicious woman who stayed at the government office, avoiding socializing and being apparently engrossed in some business.

Franklin approached Trent's sinister house. Inside, there were no visible lights, and it was dark as he entered, stealthily like an animal. He stood still for a moment, holding his breath to listen. No sounds were heard. He took out a pocket watch, shining the pale face around.

He quietly ascended the stairs past the hall, the place where he had encountered a ghost the previous night. He wanted to gather his mind to think about what had happened the night before, but his head was buzzing and nerves were frayed. Cold from the wind, he gripped the dagger handle in his pocket and wandered about, but found nothing. 

Finally, he looking around Trent’s bedroom and then went back down to the study, contemplating waiting there when he heard a faint sound from inside. Rising from his chair, he strained to understand where the sound came from. It seemed to come from behind the hall door, but surely there was no one inside. He waited longer, the events of the previous night swirling in his thoughts, and fear began to take over. He stood up, ready to act if need be, and quietly grasped the doorknob.

Suddenly a chilling breeze brushed past him. The door was firmly locked; where was this cold air coming from?! He was shocked. Then, footsteps were heard approaching from where he stood. The soft sound was like someone tapping lightly on the floorboards. Franklin instinctively took a step back. He was almost certain that the owner of the footsteps was a woman. 

As he prepared to call out to whoever was approaching, the footsteps stopped. It seemed that the person on the other side had realized someone was present. Then a clear voice called out, 

"Who are you?" 

It was an urgent and hurried female voice. A figure, blurry in the dark, was leaning against a table about six steps away from where Franklin stood. He tightened his grip on the pistol in his pocket. 

"Is this Mr. Trent?" the woman asked again. After a brief pause, she waited for an answer.

"Why are you not responding, Mr. Trent? Is that not your true name? And why are all the lights off?" 

As the woman spoke, her voice became increasingly soft and gentle. Franklin was at a loss for words when suddenly the light burst on, making him squint as he saw the woman reaching for the switch.

"Oh no!" she exclaimed, stepping back.

"Are you not Mr. Trent!?!" 

Franklin, noticing that the woman carried nothing in her hands, approached her cautiously.

"How did you get here?"

"By walking," she answered, with a teasing tone, glancing at the pistol in Franklin’s hand.

"The doors are all locked; there's no way you could have flown in… How did you come here?" 

"I'd like to ask you the same thing." 

At that moment, West took a quick breath and darted toward the door. Seeing her, Franklin, like a cat leaping to its prey, swiftly moved to block her and tried to grip her arm. However, West swiftly evaded him and dashed toward the door. As a shadow, Franklin pursued her but the woman grabbed the door and opened it, trying to escape. Franklin had to stop just short of the door and shouted a curse as he pushed the door.

The woman struggled desperately to lock the door, exerting all her strength to keep it shut, but she was unable to resist the man's strength and fled again. Franklin cursed again and chased after her.

In the dark room, they stumbled together, flailing, until West grabbed a handle and managed to flee upstairs. Franklin followed her.

At the very top, West, seeing no more escape, collapsed in place. 

Franklin pressed the switch to turn on the light and walked over to West, carefully observing her. He examined the walls and the rounded ceiling before aiming the pistol at West. She seemed to glow against the light.

Franklin considered pulling the trigger, but after some thought, he pocketed the pistol and proceeded to bind West with his coat, pulling the belt tight around her neck from behind. Satisfied with his actions, he felt a breath of relief. Thus West had become the captive of an unyielding predator.

Franklin pressed the switch and went downstairs. He felt a pang of regret for the failure of the previous night and was curious about why Trent was still not visible in the house.

As these thoughts crossed his mind, he suddenly noticed that the staircase had become narrow and was made of railroads instead of the wooden steps he had used before. He realized that the staircase he had used to follow West was not the same as the one he had climbed before.

His curious thoughts sparked a sense of dread. Also, a faint light seemed to beckon from above.

After a moment's hesitation, he went down a couple more steps, the wooden floor creaking underfoot. He found a door slightly ajar with light spilling out. Cautiously, he peeked through the crack and heard a faint sound. There was a narrow hallway lit by a ceiling lamp, and in that hallway was the dark green door (暗綠色の門).

"Green Door!" Trent called out, his heart racing.

  

**Summary**  

This excerpt presents a dramatic confrontation between Franklin and West within a sinister house as part of a detective novel. The narrative reflects the societal context of fear and suspicion, common themes in detective fiction of the time. The mention of characters' anxiety and the mysterious atmosphere captures the essence of pre-war anxieties, resonating with the experiences of Koreans during the colonial period—at once exploring individual psychology while hinting at broader societal undercurrents.

그것은 크라이드ㆍ놀톤이 남기고 죽은말가운데「세-드르-」이니 「탐」이니 「푸른문」이니 「비오라ㆍ인이니한말로미루어 탑에는 비밀의 관련이 반듯이 숨어잇스리라는 그러나 자긔스라로도 그닥지미덥지는 못한 생각으로 엇잿거나 한번 둘러보기위하야 올나간것이엿섯다.
그는 올나가서 이리저리 차저는보앗스나 결국 아모것도 발견하지못하고 다시 내려오기시작하엿다.
다내려와서 맨 아랫치에서 발을 내려놋느라고하는대 그순간에 우연히 발이 긋하야 마루바닥을 쿵하고 밟엇다 그서슬에 그는 전신의 균형을 이러바리고 마즌편벽에다 이마를  부드첫다. 압흔이마를 만지면서 비틀비틀하엿스나 동시에 무언지 이상하다는 생각이 몽롱하게 돌앗다. 머리를 부듯기는 하엿스나 그러타고 그러케 벽이 단단할리는 업는데!? 자긔가 이마를 부드친자리를  드려보앗다. 과연 속이울니는 소리가 들니엇다.
그의 등에는 가가 아마를 부드친곳과는 즉각의 방향으로 이집의 중요한장소로 통한 문이 잇섯다. 누구나 칭게를 올나가는 사람은 이번의 트렌트과가튼 실수를 해서 근방을 주의하지안이하는이상 이러한 곳이 잇스리라고는 생각지 못하게되엿다.
다시 여긔저긔 더듬어보는동안에 그의 손에는 족한 무엇이 걸니엿다 동시에 그가 이마를 보드친부분의 벽이슬뭇이 뒤로물너서며 문하나만큼 틈이버러젓다.
그리고 그곳으로부터는 칭게가 잇는데 그러나 지금내려오든것고는 달너 나무가안이요 철로되엿스며 훨신좁앗다. 그는 칭게를 여내려갓다. 칭게가 다한곳에 다시 문이잇고 그것은 미는대로 열니어젓다. 속은 어둡고 썩은공긔가 얼골을 스치엿다 그는 석냥을 그어가지고 스위치를눌넛다.
내부가 환히밝어지며 그의 압헤서는 좁은향하로되엿다 약두어간되는곳에는「푸른문」(暗綠色門)이 선연히잇는것이엿섯다. 「푸른문!」하고 트렌트는 부르지젓다.＜161＞
