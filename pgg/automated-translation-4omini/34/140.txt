Question and Answer Record 7

Reporter: What is currently an issue in the medical field?  

Yu: A scholar from Bulgaria, named Calmette, has recently released research suggesting a new preventive method for tuberculosis that has stirred quite a sensation in the medical community. This method involves administering the tuberculosis bacteria to humans after animal tests have yielded fairly good results. It seems this method may also be applied to prevent other diseases such as typhoid and dysentery by ingesting the bacteria instead of using vaccinations.  

**Summary**  

This article discusses a novel tuberculosis prevention method proposed by a Bulgarian scholar, Calmette, that involves the ingestion of live tuberculosis bacteria after successful animal testing. The method's potential applicability to other diseases raises medical and ethical questions. This reflects the period's experimental attitudes towards medicine, particularly within colonial Korea, where new medical practices often faced scrutiny amid broader societal challenges. Historians may find the intersection of colonial health policy and emerging medical research noteworthy, indicating changing perspectives on disease prevention during this era.
