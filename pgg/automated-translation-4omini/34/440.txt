Editor's Notes

Everyone! How did you spend the past year? Thanks to your unwavering support and encouragement in the past, our publication has developed to this point, but in the new year, please continue to support and encourage us so that we can achieve our aspirations.

This New Year issue has been immensely filled with content as the entire staff worked tirelessly for a month. Especially, the appendix of "Complete Home Remedies" is a gem in the Korean magazine industry, offering comprehensive references for every household.

Furthermore, regarding the historical accounts of Hong Gyeongnae and Lee Pil in the accounts of Sinmi (辛未), this is the first publication of its kind in Korea. Notably, details such as Hong Gyeongnae's proclamation of the Yan Kingdom and other members of their faction's ages are previously unknown facts, which will serve as significant references in historical studies. Several academic papers are also a must-read.

As we usher in a new year, the surrounding circumstances may not immediately change. However, as we often live vaguely without plans and goals, it is common for us Koreans to feel that time, along with the past events, slips away. Let’s approach this new year with fresh enthusiasm and plans to re-engage in our struggles.

With that in mind, the article "Three Thousand Li of Front Lines" aims to convey news from comrades scattered across the capital to strengthen new commitments.

As we divide the year into twelve months, we are barely aware of how each month slips away, and we find ourselves celebrating the New Year. It is truly chilling to think about how to spend this New Year properly.

However, I hope you all have celebrated the New Year joyfully, fulfilling your wishes.

In our publication company, we experienced marital bliss last year. In the summer, Mr. Park Seungjin got married, in the fall, Mr. Park Rorye also wed, and just before the year ended, Mr. Yeonguk Gwon Samsik likewise got married. Now, the only bachelor remaining is Mr. Choi Gyeonghwa. What will happen to him in the new year?

Reporter Kim Wonju has left the company, and Mr. Seong has gone to Jinjoo due to illness, making the office rather desolate even as we welcome the new year. I feel like I'm growing a year older.

Summary

The editorial notes reflect on the achievements and developments of the publication over the past year, encouraging readers for continued support moving forward. It stresses the importance of renewed commitment and planning for the future despite the challenges faced. The article also mentions significant historical content, notably the accounts related to Hong Gyeongnae, illustrating the importance of historical documentation amid the struggles of Korean society under colonial rule. Historians might find the reflections on personal milestones alongside the broader societal challenges particularly revealing.
