戱曲 그의 家庭風景(全一幕)  
					Cai Wanjik  
[Characters]  
He		about 30 years old  
His wife	about 20 years old  
His son	about 13 years old  
House owner	Old woman  

[Time]  
Modern early autumn evening sun  

[Place]  
Kyongsong  

[Stage]  
The front of a half room of a thatched house with a dim kitchen on the right side. In the background, through the double windows, the inner room is barely visible through the back door. The narrow yard extends from the porch, and the middle gate leans to the left. There are no noticeable furnishings in the house, and a poor-looking pile of firewood is in the corner of the porch, on which the couple's old shoes are left. As the curtain rises, he leans against the door frame, reading a book while his wife is fiddling with her sewing beside him.

Wife (after a long time of sewing)  Did you say to wait until five o'clock?  
He (distracted by the book)  Yes.  
Wife  It's already past five o'clock…  
He (looks up slowly)  Is it?  
Wife  Yes, five o'clock has passed.  
He (looking around)  Has it really?  
Wife  Then that can't be good.  
He  What are you going to do? (looks at her)  
Wife  What can we do? We can’t rely on anyone else to help us.  

He (murmuring to herself)  This one is getting a bit too complicated...  
He  If he isn't coming with the money, what obligation does he have to us? Isn’t it enough that he at least seems to care?  
Wife  It’s no use thanking him or anyone else when we can’t bring the money in as expected.  

He  Well, still...  
Wife  What do you mean, "still"?  
He (irritated)  You don’t know, huh?  
Wife  I don’t know.  
He (sighs)  Then don’t act like you do.  

(The house owner enters from the kitchen on the right)  
Wife  (leans over her sewing)  
He  (putting the book down, straightens up)  
House Owner  Aren't they coming with that money around five?  
Wife  (hesitant)  Someone said they would bring the money at five.  
House Owner  So, if that person can't bring the money, we won’t see it today?  
Wife  Oh, no! There’s no way! The one who said they would bring it will come.  
House Owner  So you say, but who knows? I need that money no matter what!  

Wife  Please don’t say such things! Even if he can’t come today, he could come tomorrow.  
House Owner  (yelling)  Tomorrow? Why would that matter? If you’re renting, you’ve got to pay. You could rent a house, but how can you eat when you don’t have money?  

(The scene escalating builds tension)  
Wife  Just let me handle it…  
House Owner  If you don’t provide the money, I have no obligation…  

(The conflict continues, revealing their struggles as the conversation progresses)

**Summary**

In this one-act play, the interactions among a struggling family highlight their dire financial conditions within a modern setting. The dialogue uncovers the pressures they endure from their landlord regarding overdue rent and their efforts to manage personal responsibilities despite poverty. The portrayal of familial strife amidst economic hardship reveals the broader societal issues faced during the colonial period in Korea; historians may find interest in how this reflects the struggles of working-class families under colonial rule.
