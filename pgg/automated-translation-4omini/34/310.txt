기사제목	京城 五十八雙童錄 (Record of Fifty-Eight Twin Names in Gyeongseong)  
저자	관상자 (Gwan-sang-ja)  
기사형태	잡저 (Miscellaneous)  

Ladies and gentlemen! Don’t misunderstand "twin names" as merely being twin brothers born at the same time. Here, "twin" refers not only to biological twins but also to people whose names are similar. While it might be a presumption to refer to respectable and mature individuals as twins, it would be appreciated if you would view "twin" merely as a term of endearment.  

Mr. Song Jin-woo and Mr. Song Jeong-woo are fundamentally different and have no family ties. However, if Mr. Song Jeong-woo were to join the Dong-A Ilbo, people might mistakenly think that he is the younger brother of Mr. Song Jin-woo or perhaps even a distant relative due to the similarity in their names.  

If Mr. Ahn Jae-hong from the Joseon Ilbo were to be at the Dong-A Ilbo while Mr. Yang Jae-hong from the Dong-A Ilbo were in the Joseon Ilbo, people would surely mix them up, considering the similarity in their names would imply they are twins.

In the Cheondogyo Youth Party, Mr. Kim Gi-cheol and Mr. Kim Ki-jin from the Joseon Ilbo, although unrelated, would often share a laugh when their names were called out at events due to the similar sounds: "Kim Ki-jin" and "Kim Gi-cheol" become a case of name twins.

Mr. Lee Seok-gu and Mr. Shin Seok-u have completely different names, yet because "Seok" in Seok-u and "Seok" in Seok-gu sound alike, should Mr. Lee Seok-gu enter the Joseon Ilbo, his close friends calling him "Seok-gu" could lead Mr. Shin Seok-u to wonder who is calling him in the office, listening intently for his name. This is another case of name twins.

Mr. Kim Ri-su and Ms. Kim Ri-sun share similar names such that people reading the magazine might easily mistake them for sibling pairs. However, Mr. Kim Ri-sue is actually the pseudonym of Mr. Park Pal-yang from the Joseon Ilbo; while Ms. Kim Ri-sun is the daughter of Mr. Kim Byeong-chan, a Presbyterian elder running Shin Hangnak.

The day before, there were two people named Lee Bong-su at the Dong-A Ilbo and the Joseon Ilbo, leading to numerous confusions in various matters.

Interestingly, both of them were imprisoned due to a Communist Party incident, and when their families went to visit them, or when there were any items sent in, the guards often got confused and would send the wrong person in, resulting in laughs like "Look who's confused!"

In the Jeongmun High School (徵文高普), there are three teachers: Lee Byeong-gye, Lee Byeong-kyu, and Lee Byeong-ki whose names sound alike. If people don’t distinguish between Hanja, "Lee Byeong-gye" and "Guk-seo’s Lee Byeong-gye," and the Korean "Lee Byeong-ki," misunderstandings frequently happen. Recently, as Lee Byeong-kyu moved to Yangjeong High School, such mix-ups have lessened. 

However, when making phone calls, there are still occasions where Lee Byeong-gye and Lee Byeong-ki get mixed up. 

The actress Kim Myung-sun and the female writer Kim Myung-sun have identical names, which led to misunderstandings where people thought the author had become an actress for quite some time.

Mr. Park Young-hee and Mr. Park Young-hee from the Kunwoo Club also share identical names, so should someone in Kunwoo give a lecture, many might mistakenly believe it is Mr. Park Young-hee from Hoewon.

Similar sounding names frequently lead to confusion. The other day, Lee Jung-hwa was at Baejai High School while there was also a Lee Jung-hwa at Jungang High School. Thus, during the spring entrance exam period, parents of students applying to Baejai sometimes mistakenly sought out the Central School's Lee Jung-hwa, requesting for entrance ceremonies.

Moreover, when schools hold faculty meetings, to distinguish between them, they referred to Baejai's Lee Jung-hwa as "the chubby Lee Jung-hwa" or "the round-faced Lee Jung-hwa" (though the term might seem a stretch) while calling Jungang's Lee Jung-hwa "the small Lee Jung-hwa."

At Jungang High School, Mr. Choi Do-seon and Mr. Choi Do-seon from the Joseon Farmers' Association may use different characters, but due to their similar sounds, whenever Mr. Choi Do-seon goes somewhere on association business, people often mix him up with the Jungang High School's Choi.

Same for the lawyer Kim Chan-yong and Mr. Kim Chan-yong from Ki Shin Trading Company, confusion arises due to their similar names. Similarly, there were Kim Won-joo, a former reporter at this office and Ms. Ha Yoon-sil, whose name also sounded alike, both from the Jinampo area. 

Mr. Kim Dong-hwan from Samcheongri Commerce and Mr. Kim Dong-han from the Russian Consulate had a similar sounding name too.

Ms. Kim Young-soon from Jeongsin Girls' School and Ms. Kim Soon-young from Kaesong Girls' School, if correction is neglected by newspapers or magazine editors, there is a risk that Kim of Jeongsin ends up being referred to as Kim Soon-young, thereby misleading the public.

Ms. Heo Jeong-suk and Ms. Heo Young-suk sound like they could be siblings, while Ms. Kang Jeong-hee and Ms. Kang Jeong-im from the Kunwoo Association, together with the former reporter Choi Eun-hee and the female dancer Choi Seung-hee all bear names that at first glance could easily lead one to believe they are related.

The firm name of Jinmyeong Girls' High, Nam Sang-jam and our own Seo Sang-jam have similar names leading to confusion when they meet, as everyone believes they are from a different family. 

Lee Chun-won and Lee Dong-won are notoriously seen as adopted siblings, with their names creating rumors, while a rural person mistook Mr. Dong-won during a visit to the Lee family saying, "The Lee family and their brothers cannot be together." However, Mr. Lee Kwang-soo and Mr. Lee Il are not flesh and blood brothers.

In Cheondogyo, Wu Se-chang and Oh Yong-chang share similar names so some presume they are siblings. Similarly, there have been misunderstandings with Mr. Kwon Dong and Kwon Dong-jin, being perceived as brothers. 

However, there isn't any familial connection at all.

People have even asked Park Dal-seong if he was brother of Park Dal-hyeon from the Joséon Ilbo's Chuncheon branch out of habitual curiosity.  

On Flower Street, which is quite close to the offices of the Dong-A Ilbo and the Chungang Ilbo, there is a name sign similar to Ha Myeong and Lee Sang-hyup. Many people often mistake the house for belonging to Ha Myeong, while in reality, it’s entirely a different individual’s household.  

Shanhai’s Heo Gang University student, recently in the U.S., Shin Hwa-cheol has had to deal with the fact that his name is similar to the Shin Hwa-cheol who was a reporter for our office, causing confusion where friends of one attempt to send letters to the other, often leading to amusing results.

Mr. Kim Dong-seong was sent as a vice president representative to the International Journalists’ Conference, and at that time, when the Dong-A Ilbo was alerting everyone about it, fellow reporter Kim Dong-cheol would invariably ask where he could find his brother, causing a rather awkward situation for him.  

Dongdeok Girls’ School principal Mr. Cho Dong-sik and master archers Mr. Cho Dong-suk also share similar characters and sounds, leading to people confusing the principal with an archer’s affliation as well.  

Summary  

This article humorously records the prevalence of name similarities among prominent individuals in Gyeongseong, leading to misunderstandings and confusions. It highlights the intricacies and social implications of shared or similar names within professional contexts, particularly in the early 20th-century Korean society. Historians might find this exploration of dual identities significant, illuminating the social interactions and the interconnectedness of personal identities under colonial rule.
