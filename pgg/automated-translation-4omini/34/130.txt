Question and Answer Record 6  
Bang Sin-yeong  
Q: Is there no way for our Korean people to eat less rice and live?  
A: The habit of only eating rice has become so entrenched that if we change that habit, it doesn’t matter what we eat as long as it is nutritious. You can eat less rice and instead eat a balanced amount of vegetables, and that would be sufficient.  

**Summary**  

This article discusses dietary habits among Koreans during the colonial period, particularly the over-reliance on rice. The author suggests that a change in eating habits towards a more balanced diet, including vegetables, could promote better health. This perspective reflects early 20th-century viewpoints on nutrition in Korea and highlights the influences of colonial life on traditional practices. Historians may find it interesting as it indicates a shift in public health discourse during a time of colonial rule, where the diet was shaped significantly by socio-economic factors.
