Magazine Title: 별건곤  
Publication Date: 1931년 01월 01일  
Article Title: 고요한 밤거리를 거러감니다 (Quietly Walking the Night Streets)  
Author: 하윤 (Ha Yun)  
Article Type: 시 (Poem)  

I walk quietly through the night streets.

As it has been intoxicating, I cautiously follow the narrow backstreets.

I walk quietly through the night streets.

Resting on a stone, my hand gingerly lifts, whether it was considered a joy or not.

Autumn is gone, and winter is upon us, yet the place where the girl used to sleep is missing.

Even the footsteps make a sound on this chilly night, causing autumn leaves to stir.

Do not neglect the one who is special to me.

With a repeated sound ringing softly,

I am passing through the quiet night streets.

When will I come to this street again?

This body too must tread on a path that is so distant.

Without stopping, I hasten my steps.

In the heart of winter, I must go to the riverside.

Though it might have been the final sight of the serene landscape, regrets linger in the heart's core.

Abandoning the road I must take, I will linger instead,

I am passing through the quiet night streets.  
(1930ㆍ8ㆍ29)<150>  

**Summary**  

The poem reflects a melancholic solitude experienced while walking through quiet night streets, blending seasonal imagery of autumn and winter with feelings of longing and introspection. The speaker vividly conveys a sense of unease yet beauty found in solitude, as well as hints of a personal connection shaped by lost love or memories. From a historical perspective, this poem's longing for connection amid isolation resonates with the broader experiences of Koreans during the colonial period, particularly regarding the loss of home and identity.
