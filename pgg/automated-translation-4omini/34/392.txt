필자	劉道順 (Yu Do-sun)  
기사형태	시 (Poem)  

Year-end Praise  
						Yu Do-sun  

Amid the chrysanthemums, seeing one’s blurry tears,  
As I eat, my inability to work brings forth even more sorrow.  
In the troubled world, when did the new year arrive?  

Even in the decayed heart, spring does not seem to come.  
Thinking of the swallow's water, new sorrow rises.  
In this burdened body of silent suffering, tears do well up.  

Still, thinking I may live what I cannot live,  
In the struggles of surviving, I feel even more captivated.  
With a heavy heart, I keep trying to push forward, taking steps.  
---On the morning of the first day of the new year---<150>  

**Summary**  

In this poem, Yu Do-sun expresses deep emotional turmoil tied to the arrival of the new year, using imagery of chrysanthemums and tears. It reflects a sadness rooted in personal and societal struggles, questioning the essence of renewal in an oppressive environment. Historians may find the work significant as it subtly captures the despair and hope within Korea during colonial rule, highlighting the conflicts between personal longing and the collective suffering of the nation.
