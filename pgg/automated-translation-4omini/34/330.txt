風聞帖  
The former criminal who poisoned beauties, Kim Jeong-bil, has been discussed in newspapers and magazines for a long time. Recently, he was hospitalized twice and has now taken on the role of a nurse, monitoring female patients. If luck is in his favor this New Year, he might even get released from prison.  

The mother of Yonsan Women's High School principal, Albert Shin, passed away at the age of 77 on December 7 of last year, curiously at 7 PM. Some say her fortune, based on her birth year, was perhaps of the number seven.  

Former Junggye Ilbo reporter, Kim Mal-bong, plans to study abroad in Hawaii. After returning to her hometown in Busan, she unexpectedly got married to a local, Mr. Jeon Sang-beom, and sent out the latest traditional-style wedding invitations to her relatives. We hope there will soon be news of her giving birth to a son or daughter; we are also curious whether they are going to the U.S. for their honeymoon.  

Dear respected family, on November 26 at 11 AM, the marriage ceremony was held at my home in 525 Yeongju-dong. I hereby inform you of this. November 29, 1930, Jeon Sang-beom, Kim Mal-bong.  

The day before, reporter Seo-hee from Joseon Ilbo is expected to return to work from the New Year and is ready to come back immediately if a good job opportunity arises. She is just waiting with her dust-covered military-like overcoat, which feels like a heavy burden.  

Lady aviator, Lee Jeong-gi, was briefly mentioned before in this publication, studying to drive a car. However, she was successful in qualifying for the driving test at Hamnam Provincial Office last November and received her driving license on the 24th. It is said she is currently raising funds to establish a women’s car company.  

The previous day, Mr. Kwon Deok-gwi passed by a road in Songhyeondong, and someone from a nearby "Namsan" tteokbokki shop got into a quarrel. Upon seeing Mr. Kwon, he suddenly ran over and exclaimed, "Sir!" then threw off his outer garment, asking Mr. Kwon to help him resolve the conflict. Mr. Kwon, just passing by, found no need to intervene and declined the request. The man, not caring about anything else, put on his outer garment again, only to be joke-rebuked the next day, saying, "Is it because I’m short that I have to endure such humiliation?" Ha ha... being short is indeed something to be blamed.  

Mr. Kim Dong-hwan, who was close to someone from Samcheolli, one day, in a certain seat, saw Kim and exclaimed, "You, you've gotten yourself into big trouble! People are calling you a traitor!" Only then did Kim realize the absurdity of the situation and was temporarily taken aback. Then he responded, "You fool, should I sell out the Samcheolli every month, then you would think that’s acceptable?" Finally, Kim was relieved and laughed it off, having realized the misunderstanding. After all, "Samcheolli" refers to Korean territory.<129>
