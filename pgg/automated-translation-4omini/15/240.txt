기사제목	조흔 經驗 新試驗失敗談 (Report on the Failed Experiences in New Attempts)  
저자	方仁根 (Bang In-geun)  
기사형태	설문 (Survey)  

I have lived in the so-called literary world for three years. It can be long or short, depending on one's perspective. Of course, I am still connected to the literary world and do not wish to leave it until death. Regardless of the changes in lifestyle, my devotion and essence remain unchanged and may even be stronger.

When I devoted myself to the magazine "朝鮮文壇" (Joseon Literary World), I managed the editorial process with the help of Chunwon, Neulbom, and Johan while managing all economic matters independently. My goal was to "start small and grow large!" Therefore, I eliminated all staff and servants typical of other magazine firms and handled everything alone—from editing, soliciting advertisements, proofreading, to mailing copies. I would ride my bicycle to post advertisements at various stores and deliver books to different bookstores, even collecting payment until the end of the month. At each bookstore, I became known as the "servant of the Joseon Literary Society." 

Of course, after a year, I had reporters and collectors helping me, but... I struggled in the whirlwind of activity like a flash of lightning, without knowing the pain until the magazine went bankrupt due to economic pressures and various circumstances. My suffering and despair at that time were hardly expressible. There’s no need to discuss it further here. It’s simply true that when one puts their whole heart into work, the pain of losing something precious feels worse than the loss of a beloved child. Hence, I thought, "Oh! Let's go to the countryside!" and fled to rural areas. I planned to write novels and maybe establish an agricultural business. However, a friend from Seoul sent me a letter saying, "Something has come up for us to pursue our objectives!" My enthusiasm was rekindled, and I crawled back to Seoul.

"Ah, it’s useless. What can I do with others? I cannot rely on them. Rather than depending on others for our work, I would prefer to labor by myself!" This excitement led me to dive into business management quickly. What kind of business should I undertake? I wandered the streets of Seoul for two months.

If I had a wealthy capital, I would have continued the Joseon Literary World despite bankruptcy. But due to unavoidable circumstances and the desperate need for stable living, I put an immense effort into choosing the most secure and promising business. Ultimately, it seemed destined or inspired, and I decided to open a grocery store, starting the next day near Independence Gate. 

The motives behind this lifestyle change were generally as follows:

First, the excessive labor was taxing on my physique. I had to wake up at dawn and stay at the shop until late at night, regardless of the weather (I hired one person but had to oversee even small orders over the phone).

Second, I had to go to Namdaemun Market to purchase goods several times a week. You might think buying goods is not difficult, but choosing sellable items from mountains of cheap goods and ensuring freshness while comparing prices amid the noise of thousands of people is truly overwhelming. 

Especially at the auction market, amidst the chaos of thousands of Chinese, Japanese, and Koreans, trying to bargain and buy goods proved to be immensely exhausting. Is survival competition this severe? After shopping and returning home, my whole body felt drained and my head ached.

Third, there are economic issues. If I had sufficient capital, there would be no pain, but I had to borrow money at night to manage immediate costs and buy goods on credit (delivering to over 30 households and lodgers). 

Lastly, my ingrained artistic habits made it hard to concentrate solely on my business; I longed to read books and write while managing the shop, which left me little time and a constant sense of pain in my heart. While it’s common to advise to focus on one task, saying, "Just do business," I feel like death would come without my artistic pursuits. The first three issues are physical pains; the last one is a mental torment.

In summary, these observations reflect my shift in lifestyle. Yet ultimately, it sadly ended in failure. I attribute this failure primarily to a lack of capital and a deficiency of experience in commerce.

실施 못한 改良帽子 (Unrealized Improved Hat)
金美理士 (Kim Mi-ri)

It has been ten years since I began efforts to spread my improved hats among women. My efforts seem to have dissipated, and it truly feels regrettable that the product never gained popularity. However, reflecting on it today evokes profound emotion. The women I sought to market the improved hats to have not changed, and I too have aged and become weary, altering my attitude towards the endeavor.

Life is like a dream; yesterday's events are as vague as morning mist. While it is said that heroes fail to achieve fame if the time is not right, similar is true for my improved hats, which were never implemented and left discarded by society’s standards. 

The improved hats were designed to complement women's garments while preserving the beauty of their hairdos. Regrettably, the hats never reached a broader audience, and this remains a lingering sorrow.

Trends among young men and women are often influenced by a myriad of factors beyond the abilities of an individual. Especially for young men and women lacking experience and sophistication, timing plays a crucial role in their hair and fashion choices. When actors and cafe waitresses don new forms of clothing or hairstyles, it stirs the vanity and curiosity of the youth, prompting them to adopt those styles.

As such, trends in society must sometimes be acknowledged within a specific timeframe, lest one becomes irrelevant.

For example, if educators attempt to suppress trends among students, it merely cultivates deceitful behavior among students. As for me, with my life nearing its end, I ponder what further contributions I can make to society. In my heart lies the fervent hope that capable individuals will emerge to take up necessary work in improving women's fashion.

단발햇다가 長髮된 까닭 (The Reason for Returning to Long Hair After a Short Cut)
許貞淑 (Heo Jeong-suk)

When a monk returns to shaving their head, people laugh, calling it "returning to Amitābha." Similarly, when women who had their hair cut short decide to grow it long again, they might receive similar mockery. However, whether people ridicule us for our choices in short or long hair is irrelevant, as we are guided by our needs. The question remains: which style serves our purposes better?

From my experience, a woman's short hair can be advantageous in terms of time, hygiene, and aesthetics, but it presents many social challenges. Walking down the street, I would be subject to ridicule from those lacking understanding, being called a "short-haired woman." 

The issue is trivial, but when mingling in society and working with others, a short hairstyle can disadvantage women in various situations. For female students or women in specific professions such as teaching, short hair poses no problem, but for those engaged in diverse fields, it can create many difficulties. 

This is why when I returned to Korea from the U.S., I grew my hair long again. While I live in Korea now, if I were to go abroad again, I would likely cut it short once more. Yet, from experience, I can view our past decision for short hair as a failed experiment. I am not alone, as I believe many of my peers who opted for short hair experienced similar outcomes.

**Summary**

This article reflects on the author's experiences in the literary world and the challenging transition to managing a grocery store due to financial struggles. The author describes the physical and mental toll of this new lifestyle, emphasizing the clash between creative pursuits and commercial demands. The accounts highlight the struggles faced by individuals during Korea's colonial period, revealing how economic hardships shaped creative endeavors and personal choices, offering a poignant reflection on the intersection of art, commerce, and survival.

물론 자본이 넉넉하면 朝鮮文壇을 망하더래도 계속하겟지만은 그럿치도 못한 부득이한 사정이 만코 첫재 생활불안정의 곤경을 면해 노코 볼 일이하는 뜻아래서 가장 안전하고 유망할 상업을 골느기에 무한히 애를 썼습니다. 궁리하든 끗에 운명의 所爲인지 靈感이나 바든드시 식료품상을 하기로 결정하고 이튼날로 독립문 못미처 집을 어더 시작하게 된 것임니다. 동기로 말하면 대개 이것임니다.
다음 이런 생활로 드러오니 친구들은 웃기도 하고 놀래기조 하고 조타고도 하지마는 고통은 네가지 밧게 업슴니다.

實施 못한 改良帽子
金美理士

내가 개량모자(改良帽子)를 맨들어 가지고 일반녀자들에게 널니 보급하여 보려고 애를 쓰든 일도 벌서 십년이 되엿슴니다. 한낫 노력이 물거품가치 살어저 버리고 아모려한 갑흠이 업섯든 일이라고 하나 오늘 와서 생각하면 실로 감개가 무량합니다. 개량모자를 씨우려고 하든 상대의 녀자들도 오늘에 니르러서는 그대의 녀자들이 안이요. 내 자신도 두뺨에 주름살이 지고 기력이 쇠진하야 일에 대한 태도도 퍽 변하엿슴니다. 원래 사람이 사는 것이 꿈가튼 일이니까 지나간 일도 하루 아츰의 안개가치 흐릿할 뿐 임니다.
영웅도 때를 만나지 못하면 뜻을 페지 못하는 것과 가치 아모리 됴흔 일이라고 하나 시절을 만나지 못하면 그 일이 듸데여 실시되지 못하고 사회로부터 한줌의 진끌가치 버림을 밧고 말게 됨니다.
내가 맨들엇든 개량모자는 됴선녀자들의 의복전톄에 됴화(調和)가 되고 더구나 진끌을 함부로 뒤집어 쓰는 녀자즐의 머리털에 대한 미(美)를 보존하기 위하야 가장 간이하게 맨들어섯스나 한때라도 녀자들의게 보급이 되지 못하고 만일은 영원히 애석하는 일임니다.＜112＞
문명의 풍자와 시세에 딸아 청년남녀에게 유행하는 여러 가지일은 일개인의 능력으로 자우하지 못하는 일임니다. 더구나 사회와 생활에 대하야 톄험과 세련을 가지지 못한 청년남녀에게 잇서서는 어느 정도와 시기가 지나가지 안코는 엇지할 수 업는 일임니다.
활동사진 배우라던지 「가페」의 녀하인이라던지 심지어 우슴을 파는 기생 가튼 것들이 새로운 모형의 의복이나 처음 보는 빗의 것을 입고들고 나세면 그것은 청년남녀의 허영심과 호기심을 이상히 자극하야 누가 식히지를 안어도 곳 본을 뜨게 됨니다.
그럼으로 시대를 딸아 니러나는 여러 가지 유행이라는 것은 어느 시기안에서는 묵인하여 두지 안으면 도리여 피해를 보는 때가 업지 안슴니다.
실례를 들면 교육에 임의 향한 이로써 학생사이에 유행하는 일을 억지로 막으려고 하면 그 학생들은 선생의 눈을 속이는 품성에 납분 관습을 뿌리박게 하는 것 뿐임니다.
나는 나도 만코 내 여생이 얼마 남지 안엇스니 내 이제 무슨 일을 다시 사회뎍으로 나서서 하겟슴니까만은 됴선녀자게에서 됴흔 인물이 나서 됴흔 일을 만히 하여주엇스면하는 속마음의 긔원밧게 더 가지지 안엇슴니다.

지내갈 때에 그까진 철모르는 희들이나 이해업는 사람들이 익크-斷髮娘 보아라. 단발미인 보와라 하고 조롱하는 것 갓흔 것이야. 문제도 업지마는 적어도 여러 민중속에 드러가서 서로 손을 잡고 물에 물분듯시 술에 술분듯시 아모 간격과 색채와 파벌이 업시 일을 하자면 아즉까지는 단발한 것이 여러 가지로 불리한 점이 만흔 것 갓다. 그 중에도 엇던 ... 잇슬 때에는 단발한 사람은 더욱 곤란한 때가 만타. 현재 朝鮮에 잇서서도 학굘던지 엇던 교회라던지 일정한 界限이 잇는 직업 부인 가트면 단발을 하야도 아모 문제도 업겟지만은 다방면으로 활동하는 사람은 아즉까지 곤란한 점이 만흔 것이 사실이다. 그리하야 나도 米國에서 귀국할 때에 머리를 다시 길으게 되엿다. 지금도 朝鮮에 잇기에 그럿치 만일 외국에 간다면 다시 단발을 하랴고 한다. 그러나 경험상으로 보면 과거 우리의 단발은 첫 시험중에 한 실패라고 볼 수밧게 업다. 그것은 나 한사람 뿐이 안이라 우리 동무중에 단발로써 장발한 사람은 모도 그럿타고 하겟지요.＜121＞
