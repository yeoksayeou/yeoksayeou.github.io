기사형태	문예기타 (Literary Miscellaneous)  

민요 두 곡

살고지자  
In the place of living,  

There, on the calm and vast road, does another day fade away.  

In the alleys behind the hills, shadows dwell, and the fields weep.  

Oh young maiden, in the fallen place of bracken,   

Do you hear the songs of the stones?  

At the sharp peak of the mountain, the golden sunset grows dim.  

In every home, the smoke from cooking rises and sways.  

The stone head, drenched in colors, is delicately seasoned with the food prepared by my beloved.  

During the season of the sublime moment, it feels like a hundred years pass.  

Where can I find the three thousand maidens, those elusive delights?  

Grasping the brush in my youthful days, warding off the white-haired arrival,  

Let us live for thousands and ten thousands of years. Oh beloved, oh beloved, let us live.  

못닛는 님  
The beloved who left after two, three, and five years shows no signs.  

Yearning thoughts are futile, and this foolish girl misses her beloved.  

When the travelers cross the hilltop, delighting in their wings,  

Though I know you will not return, I still hope for your arrival.  

As fleeting as dust passing by, embracing the fields and the skies.  

Why, oh enemy, have you come to stir up my sorrows?  

Whether in life or death, this body will yearn for you.  

Even if it must be dragged along by a leash, pull me close and take me away.  

In this world, wherever I go, a body unworthy,  

For the beloved whom I cannot see, I shall shed tears in all places.  

The taste of spicy pepper has always been the same since time immemorial,  

But those who cherish deeply, oh, their hearts go awry.  

**Summary**  

The piece presents two songs that express longing and the bittersweet nature of love as rooted in rural life. They illustrate the themes of enduring love, the passage of time, and the deep emotional connection to the land. From a historical perspective, these lyrics reflect the cultural context of Korea during the colonial period, highlighting the struggles and sentiments of individuals as they negotiate identity amid colonial pressures.
