Seeing the First Joke Abroad, We Can Also Easily Imitate It  
Kim Beoprin  

The thing I was most impressed with while in France was how passionate the people were about reading. In our Korea, we rarely see anyone reading newspapers or magazines on trams or trains, but in that country, people bring books to read not only on public transport but also in parks and recreational areas. Even when traveling, they often leave behind cigarettes and short canes, but never their books. The citizens of that country demonstrate such enthusiasm for reading, which is reflected in their numerous public and private libraries; it is no exaggeration to say that in Paris, the capital, there are establishments such as libraries even in impoverished rural areas—areas that would be akin to the mountain valleys of Korea where they rely solely on potatoes or beans for sustenance. These libraries are even more common than the traditional Korean makgeolli (rice wine) houses.

What I find even more commendable is that elderly men, around 60 or 70 years old, go to libraries every day and read diligently, just like the younger generation. In our Korea, the reason is inexplicable, but our reading capacity is still weak. Even if there are people who want to read, they cannot do so as there are no libraries available. Though there are a few libraries in Seoul, there are still very few even in major cities in the countryside. Just as our bodies require food to sustain life, our minds need nourishment to live; books are the nourishment of our spirits. Judging a nation's wealth by the amount of money it possesses is similar to judging its culture by its capacity for reading. It is truly unfortunate and shameful to think that while Koreans struggle with poverty, our reading ability is even more deficient. I believe that establishing libraries is an essential task that we must address. I wish to see many libraries established in rural areas, not only by public institutions but also by private initiatives.

Even if they cannot be large, how wonderful it would be if small-scale libraries could be established in various locations. Since it is difficult for individuals to manage them, it would be beneficial for religious or youth organizations to take the lead and create at least some simple libraries.

The improvements we wish for in the Korean family system  
Park Seok-un  

The various customs handed down in traditional Korean families may not need to be elaborated on in terms of the necessity of our lives evolving rapidly, whether on a global scale or in a more gradual manner, as this has naturally gained growing public interest. It is evident that the traditional morals underlying Korean households are being shaken to their foundations and are collapsing at an accelerated rate. As a result, people see confusion and disorder in the absence of a new framework to replace the old ones that have already crumbled. This has also led to a situation where societal morals, which should have authority, have been compromised.

Currently, the decline of moral authority in Korean society stems from many complex issues; however, one significant factor is the lack of established norms within households. While we must develop autonomous methods of self-regulation in social life, it is also imperative to renounce old structures in family life and establish practical standards to solidify the foundations of domestic life. Our previously cherished moral values seem to have lost the grounding influences they once had, which is true not only for Korea but can be said for Eastern morals.

The roots of our moral values and the strength of the social power governing them are not suitable to meet humanity's fundamental needs. However, if today's situation lacks the bonding of sustained structure for those moral values, we must expect to experience chaotic transformations. The absence of old constraints will also grant us the opportunity for freedom of thought to create a uniquely Korean tradition fitting our national lifestyle. 

The issue of improving households seems to lie in the methodology. To enhance our situation, it is not only necessary to incrementally implement superior aspects but also to conduct an essential examination of the very foundations of household morality. Our cherished Korean heart is a precious possession; thus, any new household and social ethics must originate from this Korean heart. However, this remains a rather abstract concept that may take significant time, interpretation, and faith in order to be realized in our daily conscience.

I do not intend to debate the foundation of future household morals in Korea nor the historical viewpoints towards households. There are always notions questioning the very existence of families, and I am sure there are many in Korea today doubting the reasons for family structures. Similarly, while the necessity for family improvements exists, the absence of definitive moral-based directions leads to a lack of strong regulatory social powers, making the opinions regarding execution diverse and individualistic.

The current mishmash of education regarding housing or the half-Westernized and half-traditional homes, along with similar intercultural themes, all illustrate how the influences of outside values manifest in various forms. Thus, we must accept these influences as natural phenomena. I firmly believe that through this transition, a durable household tradition will eventually be founded. Therefore, our approach to this issue should be to allow each individual to strive for improvement in their own way. In other words, as long as someone's actions do not directly harm others' societal lives, we should adopt a tolerant stance and refrain from intervening.

The greatness of the British nation is attributed to various factors, but one that the French scorn is the British's commitment to their homes. For the British, home is their fortress; it is their territory where they exercise absolute sovereignty. British people strongly uphold their rights to their own homes while simultaneously granting others the same respect regarding theirs. This leads to the British expression "Mind your own business," which indicates a cultured respect for personal boundaries. This attitude, arising from the family unit, has empowered the British populace to freely develop.

We are often overly sensitive to the faults of others. Let us build our homes as our fortresses, meaning that we should turn a blind eye to others and allow each individual to pursue their path. While the British remain distinctly British, we should return to our own Korean hearts. It is through this freedom that we can develop our unique traditional values, and I believe that by maintaining such an attitude, we can introduce distinctive traditional values into our society more swiftly.

Reflections from my Visit to Denmark  
Shin Heung-woo  

Having traveled through various European and American countries several times, I previously only did so for church-related matters, thus not observing or understanding their lives beyond those aspects. Recently, with our Christian Church placing great emphasis on Korean rural issues, I have also begun to pay attention, leading to my undertaking a survey of around 20 countries in Europe and America this past summer with a fellow church member. 

While I have learned many things and faced delays, what impressed me most was Denmark's agricultural development. As we know, Denmark is a small nation at the northwestern tip of Europe, yet it excels in agriculture to a degree that it can rival any powerful and civilized nation in Europe or America. This is due not only to the Danish people’s effort and dedication to agricultural improvement and development but also fundamentally because their agricultural leaders first teach life’s truths.

Human beings, despite their strong aspirations, cannot venture toward their desires without understanding life's truths, leading them to despair and ruin if they fail to do so. Denmark, being a nation of Christians, has agricultural instructors who are Christian scholars as well. Their perspective is that, as long as the nation exists, the wellbeing of the citizens is essential for Christianity to thrive, so both individually and as a church, they constantly teach the truths of life, believing that as their citizens prosper, so does Christianity.

What captivated me about Denmark’s agricultural progress was that their citizens have an understanding of life's truths. In contrast, analyzing the lives of our fellow Koreans in locations like Busan or Uiju leaves me feeling deeply troubled. It is regrettable to express that I cannot help but wonder why our people are so unaware of life's truths. With the right understanding, how could they not find a way to live better?

Considering the issues of our rural situations must not be seen as trivial failures. I believe that our Christianity is significantly lagging behind other religions in this regard. Despite our affiliations, we are all Koreans, so we must research and explore ways for all Koreans to survive and thrive.

Four Aspects of Life in Germany  
Lee Seung-yong  

During my time in Germany, I noted various aspects of their lives that I believed we could emulate with little effort if we were willing. Among these, particularly noteworthy were cleanliness, orderliness, measured action, and the way they raise their children.

Germans, by nature, do not know how to tidy up. Thus, stepping into a German household, you may find furniture scattered around haphazardly, much like leaves falling on a day of autumn. What is on the floor in the morning may be placed on shelves by evening, lacking any designated spots, resulting in a waste of time when one needs to find something, and ensuring items are often damaged.

Conversely, Germans, including their children and the elderly, consistently put away the items they use. Hence, their homes tend to be tidy, which is pleasing for anyone who enters. As a result, time is conserved, and it leads to a more enjoyable life, requiring neither significant expenditure nor effort. Thus, we should learn from their inclination to keep things in order.

In Germany, households conduct thorough cleanings at least once a week. Each day, houses are swept and tidied, but a comprehensive cleaning is performed weekly. Because of this, entering a German home, even touching the edges of windows or corners, reveals no dust or dirt. Thus, while their places may not be decorated flamboyantly, they are clean and refreshing, benefiting the residents’ health substantially. 

In examining our homes, it is essential to reflect on how we organize household items, which is significant for maintaining cleanliness, also a critical ideal alongside diet. Cleanliness is not only about acquiring better items; it has as much to do with maintaining a dirt-free condition, which is a task only second to eating.

Orderliness is increasingly important as societies have evolved. While households are individual units, society is the aggregate of those families. Perhaps in ancient times this was not a concern, but today it is impossible to live without it. Therefore, honoring regulations is critical.

Germans practice orderly behavior consistently. They plan ahead in all endeavors. Thus, they do not encounter foolish errors or unexpected failures. Even when visiting others, they will check in advance to agree on a meeting time, discussing essential details when they arrive. In comparison, our lives often lack precision: waking, meal times, and various activities are all quite erratic. This irregularity results in losses for individuals and broader societal challenges. 

Therefore, we must strive for orderly behavior as a fundamental practice starting today. 

Lastly, concerning child-rearing, Germans do not resort to corporal punishment. While they may reprimand or scold their children, they avoid using force. Striking a child may yield immediate compliance but can lead to the development of a violent temperament over time. Instead, Germans encourage children by using methods such as denying them meals or encouraging self-reflection on their mistakes, which promotes good behavior. 

Those of us who raise children must wisely consider that educational responsibility lies primarily within the family setting.

Regarding the thoughts I've shared, they are practical steps that can be acted upon, and I hope such actions will be taken.

Education of Children as a Model  
Kim Hap-ra  

During my time in the United States, I personally experienced and reflected on various aspects of their lifestyle, but what stands out most as a vital lesson for us is the attention paid to raising children.

Koreans do not lack love for their children. Everyone wishes to provide good food and clothing, hoping that their children grow up well. 

Koreans feel that as long as they send children to school after providing them with food and clothes, they have fulfilled their parental duties. In this regard, Koreans and Americans are no different.

However, Americans place deep importance on all aspects concerning young children, not only providing them with good food and clothing but also with careful consideration and thoughtfulness.

Americans see young children as future citizens who will enhance and support societal progress. This belief leads them to ensure that all their actions do not harm the child’s development.

While Koreans regard drinking and smoking as inappropriate, they often perform these activities in front of their children. To put it simply, Koreans typically believe that schooling is sufficient for their children’s education but overlook essential moral teachings that go hand-in-hand with education.

The home is the most significant place for a child’s upbringing; thus, parents must set a good example, as they have the primary teaching role.

Growing adults should be careful and considerate in their actions. Firstly, Americans never lie to their young children. Secondly, when they chastise them, they avoid words that might harm the child's emotional or mental state, instead encouraging the child to understand right from wrong. Thirdly, they acknowledge children's rights in the household. If children's opinions are beneficial, parents will prioritize those views over their own.

Such considerations significantly contribute to cultivating individuality and self-esteem in children. In both family and school settings, various actions taken cannot be efficacious unless we not only love our children but also learn to respect them. Acknowledging children as individuals deserving honor within the family, society, and nation is something we must strive for.

Diligence and Savings among the Chinese  
Seo Se-jung  

During my time in China, the most striking observation was the diligence of their people (excluding the upper-class playboys) and their strong sense of saving. As you may have witnessed in Korea, Chinese workers labor diligently from dawn to dusk, exerting themselves without complaint.

Whether in agriculture or industry, they are industrious in every field. Particularly in commerce, among all nations worldwide, one can see their remarkable presence due to their natural aptitude paired with hard work. Moreover, the Chinese are unique in their strong saving mentality, as they view every penny as sacred as their very existence. Even when they appear shabby—like a destitute vendor—most Chinese will have saved a few ten or hundred won.

Now, regarding the clothing they wear: unlike Koreans, who alter their attire depending on where they are—whether it be Japan, China, or the West—Chinese people retain their traditional style regardless of where they go. This firm resolve becomes one of the factors hindering the development of China.

What I have mentioned illustrates their national character briefly, but in terms of lifestyle, I have also noted that Chinese people, unlike Koreans who only eat rice, often consume "tteok" (rice cakes), which is cheaper and requires less complex preparation, allowing them to dedicate more time to work in the morning.

Regarding clothing, the Chinese prefer durable items that do not require frequent cleaning, unlike Koreans, for whom laundry becomes time-consuming. When it comes to housing, though large Korean homes contain much space, they are often less practical. Conversely, not utilizing ondol (Korean heated floors), Chinese walls are built well to conserve warmth, requiring less fuel. 

Thus, there are few housing issues in China compared to Korea. While I'm not suggesting we ought to imitate the Chinese in all aspects, should we seek to enhance our own lifestyles, studying their ways could be beneficial.

American System Adopted  
Kim Yu-pyeong  

Upon receiving a request to share observations from my experiences in foreign school systems that could be adapted to our lifestyle, I would like to discuss any applicable insights.

In our social life, it is no exaggeration to say that almost nothing progresses according to our will. In facing such a dire reality, we encounter various difficulties, often cursing our discontent as we fall into mundane routines.

The vibrant urban life of American cities, noted for their exquisite order and grand architecture, along with impressive skyscrapers and extensive manufacturing systems, brings envy. This way of life, however, is not solely reserved for Americans; if we had comparable opportunities for labor, patience, and investment of time, we might even surpass their technological achievements.

However, setting aside these difficult perspectives, what can be implemented immediately in our current situation?

I propose that we consider the American budgeting system (가계제도). The United States is a country where modern mechanical civilization flourishes, and their lifestyles are marked by simplicity and order. A core aspect of this is their application of the "Teamwork" principle within households.

We are lacking in consciousness towards "Teamwork." Let us first adopt this principle, focusing on a unified approach and control.

Our home lives are typically characterized by a weak division of responsibilities, with ambiguity surrounding whether the husband or wife is the head, leading to a chaotic and disorganized atmosphere. Thus, I propose that we strive to implement a family budgeting system within our homes.

For example, if a family's monthly income is 150 won, and the total monthly expenses are 100 won, then each spouse could allocate 75 won, thus each taking on 50 won of the costs. This method fosters a stronger sense of responsibility for both partners while allowing for each to maintain material independence.

Recognizing that a family’s income does not solely stem from the man’s labor underscores the contributions of the spouse in organizing household affairs, thus enhancing the man's ability to work efficiently. Furthermore, it means the wife holds equal rights to half of the household income.

By promoting such a system, we can cultivate responsible spending habits, instill savings culture in children, and prepare them for orderly living as well as cooperative life.

This proposed way of life can be executed regardless of household income. Improving living conditions does not mean only constructing modern cultural houses; even in a simple one-room thatched roof, we can first actualize the family budgeting system.

내가 佛蘭西에 가서 제일 感心한 일은 그 나라 사람들의 독서열이 만흔 것이 엿슴니다. 우리 朝鮮에서는 전차나 기차 속에서도 신문 잡지 가튼 것을 보는 사람을 별로 볼 수가 업슴니다만 그 나라에는 전차 기차 속은 물론이고 공원 기타 오락장 가튼 곳까지도 책을 가지고 가서 보는 사람이 만슴니다. 여행을 할 때에도 찰아리 담배와 短杖 가튼 것은 이저 버리고 갈지언정 책은 이저버리고 가지 안슴니다. 그 나라 국민은 그와 가티 독서열이 만흐니 만큼 公私立의 도서관이 또한 만허서 巴里 가튼 大都會에는 말할 것도 업거니와 아주 빈약한 향촌-朝鮮으로 치면 감자나 강낭이만 먹고 사는 산꼴두메 가튼 것이라도 반듯이 도서관이 잇슴니다. 엇지 말하자면 朝鮮의 막걸리(濁酒)집보다도 더 흔함니다. 그 중에 더욱 감탄할 것은 나희 6,70 식 된 백발노옹까지도 젊은 청년들과 조금도 다름이 업시 날마다 도서관에 가서 열심으로 책을 보는 것이올시다. 우리 朝鮮은 엇진 까닭인지 독서력이 아즉 簿弱한 것 갓슴니다. 비록 독서를 하고 십흔＜68＞ 사람이 잇다하야도 도서관 가튼 것이 업서서 독서를 하지 못하는 것 갓슴니다. 그래도 서울에는 몃개의 도서관이 잇지만은 시골에는 비록 대도회라도 아즉까지 도서관이 별로 업는 것 갓슴니다. 우리의 육신은 양식이 잇서야 생명을 유지하는 것과 가티 우리 정신에도 양식이 잇서야 생활을 할 것이 안임닛가. 서적은 곳 우리 정신의 양식이올시다. 민족의 貧富를 金力의 여하로 판단하는 것과 가티 민족의 文野는 그 민족의 독서력의 여하로 판단하는 것이올시다. 우리 朝鮮사람이 전력으로도 물론 貧弱하지만은 나는 그보다도 독서력이 簿弱한 것은 가장 유감으로 생각하고 또한 羞恥로 생각함니다. 우리의 施設할 것이 퍽도 만치만은 무엇보다도 도서관의 시설이 더욱 필요할 줄로 생각함니다. 官公立보다도 民立 都會보다도 향촌에 만흔 도서관이 생기기를 바람니다.
그리 큰 규모로는 못할지라도 소규모나마 곳곳이 설시된다면 그 얼마나 반가운 일이겟슴닛가. 그것은 물론 개인으로 경영하기가 어려운 것인즉 종교단체나 청년단체가튼데서 率先하야  簡易한 것이라도 시설을 하얏스면 조흘가 합니다.

朝鮮에도 實行하고 십흔 英國人의 家庭制度第
朴錫胤

재래 朝鮮가정에서 직혀 내려오는 여러 가지 습속이 적어도 우리의 생활이 세계회하야 가는 정도를 딸아서 급속히 혹은 서서히 변천하야 갈 것은 새삼스레 편론할 것도 업는 바이지마는 적어도 실질적으로 공리적으로 朝鮮人의 가정이 개조되여 가야 할 필요가 절실하니 만큼 일반의 이 문제에 대한 관심도 점점 커지는 것이 사실이다.
朝鮮의 가정 내에서 구지 직혀 내려오든 전통은 다시 말하면 朝鮮구식의 도덕율을 모조리 포함한 소위 구가정은 이제는 그 기초로부터 흔들려서 차라리 어느편에 잇서서는 너무 가속도적으로 붕괴하는 途上에 잇스며 딸하서 미처 崩潰된 구도덕율을 대신할 신규범이 아즉 질서잇게 자리 잽히지 못한 까닭에 그곳에 혼돈과 난잡을 발견하는 이에는 다른＜69＞ 새로운 광명을 볼 수가 업슬 뿐만 아니라 흔들린 가정의 토대가 사회일반에까지 미처 사회생활에 잇서서의 개인이 도덕이 그 권위조차 일케 된 것이 금일의 현상이다.
現下의 朝鮮이 사회적으로 도덕의 권위를 일허가는 것에 대하야는 여러 가지 몹씨 복잡한 이유가 만치마는 한편으로 가정에 잇서서 건실한 새 규범이 서지 못한 사실이 적지 아니한 기여가 될 것이다. 물론 사회생활을 우리 스스로가 자율하는 방도를 세우는 것이 아무것 보다도 捷徑이며 우리의 당연히 희구할 機軸이지마는 동시에 우리의 가정생활에 잇서서도 하로 밧비 舊穀을 버서나서 실질적 규범을 세워 가정생활이 토대를 든든히 싸아야 할 것이다.
그 전에는 우리의 직히든 도덕율이 흘러오는 本據를 가젓섯다. 이것은 朝鮮 뿐만 아니라 어느 의미에 잇서서 동양도덕의 淵源이 그곳에 잇섯다고 할 것이다. 또한 그 도덕율에 복종시키는 통일된 사회력도 가젓섯다. 그러나 우리의 가젓든 도덕율의 연원과 사회력이 인류의 본연한 요구에 적합한 것이고 아닌 것은 지금 불문에 부치고라도 오늘날은 우리에게 그와 가튼 연원도 그와 가튼 사회력도 업다. 그러함으로 전과 가튼 통제가 업는 만큼 일시의 과도기적 變態를 면치 못하겟지마는 그 대신 자유스러운 사상의 배경을 가지고 우리의 민족생활에 가장 적당한 독창적 신전통을 낫케 될 행운을 가젓다고 아니할 수 업다.

獨逸 살림 네 가지
李星鎔

내가 독일 가서 잇는 동안 그네들의 생활 가온대에서 됴라고 생각하고 올흔 것이라고 인정한 것은 여러 가지가 잇다. 그 중에도 특히 우리가 적은 시간과 만치 안은 노력으로 마음만 잇스면 우리의 살림에 옴겨다 노을 수 잇다고 생각한 것은 청결과 정돈과 규측뎍 행동과 어린 아희 기루는 것이라고 간주한다.
됴선 사람은 자고 이래로 정돈할 줄을 모른다. 그럼으로 됴선 사람의 가뎡안에 들어가 보면 가구가 맛치 가을날 락엽 가티 여긔 저기 흣터저 잇다. 아츰엔 방바닥에 노혀 잇든 것이 저녁엔 선반에 언치워 잇다든지 하야 일정한 장소가 업시 이러저리 굴러 단이는 형편이다. 이럼으로 물품하나를 차즐야면 만흔 시간을 허비하게 되며 물품도 적지안케 파손되는 것이다.
이와 반대로 독일 사람은 어린 아희를 비롯하야 늙은이에 니르기까지 물품이 나기구 정돈하는 일은 일치되여 잇다. 그네들은 한번 사용한 물품은 꼭-제자리에 가저다 노을 줄 안다. 그럼으로 독일 사람의 가뎡안은 항상 정연하야 보는 사람으로 하여금 불쾌케 하지 안는다. 그리고 우리네와＜73＞ 가티 쓸데업시 시간을 허비하지 안코 마음 씨우는 일도 적다. 돈도 안 들고 힘도 안들고 마음만 잇으면 행할 일이니 우리는 곳 정돈하는 일을 본떠야 할 것이다.
독일 사람은 일주일에 한번식은 가내 대소제를 한다. 아츰과 저녁에 방안이나 마루 가튼데는 늘-쓸고 닥고 하지만 특히 일주일에 한번식은 꼭 대청결을 한다. 그럼으로 독일 사람의 집안에 들어가면 문틈 가튼 것을 손끗으로 만저보아도 몬지 하나 뭇는 법이 업다. 그럼으로 방안은 화려하게 장식하지 안엇서도 깨끗하고 청결하야 마음이 항상 상쾌하고 딸아서 몸에 큰 유익을 엇게 된다. 우리의 가뎡안을 살펴볼 때에 가구라더니 집제도 가튼 것을 곳처야 할 것은 하나 둘이 안이지만 이는 첫재로 돈이 잇서야 할 일이니 손쉬웁게 착수치는 못하나 방안을 자주 쓴다던지 물걸네를 친다든지 독일 사람 가티 한 주일에 한번식 대소제를 하야 청결케 하여야 할 것이다. 청결케 한다는 일은 반다시 조흔 물품을 장만하여야 하는 것이 안이라 물품은 됴치 못할지라도 그 물품에 더러운 것이 무더잇지 안토록 하는 것이니 이 일은 먹는 것 다음으로 가는 귀중한 일이라고 할 것이다.
