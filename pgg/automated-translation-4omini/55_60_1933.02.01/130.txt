기사제목	送郞謠와 最卿, 民謠에 숨켜진 斷腸哀話 (Song of the Messenger and Choi-gyeong, the Lament Hidden in Folk Songs)  
필자	靑吾  
기사형태	문예기타 (Other Literary Forms)  

Once, during the Imjin War (壬辰亂) under King Seonjo, there was a young boy named Lee Gyeong-pil (李敬弼) who lived in a village in Gyeongsangbuk-do. He was not from a particularly difficult family, and his parents and siblings were all alive, allowing him to live a life without much envy from others. His brother-in-law, Ok Dong-pil (玉東弼), was similar—growing up in the same neighborhood as Lee and sharing a close sibling relationship with him. At the age of 20, Lee Gyeong-pil had a joyous occasion in his family, as he married a girl named Choi-gyeong (最卿) from nearby. The marital union brought happiness to their families, and the newlyweds lived together sweetly like a pair of mandarin ducks.

However, within three days, calamity struck. On the night of the third day, a tremendous storm fell upon them, signaling the onset of the devastating Imjin War, which forced the mobilization of every able-bodied man over eighteen across the nation, leaving no man behind. Both Lee Gyeong-pil and Ok Dong-pil were drafted into the military. No one dared to defy the command. Their family worked tirelessly through the night, sewing clothes and preparing food to send them off to war. Having lived sheltered under their parents’ care until then, Lee Gyeong-pil’s heart could only be imagined as he was suddenly sent off to battle after just three days of marriage.

Meanwhile, the emotions of Choi-gyeong, who had just moved to her husband’s house little knowing her husband’s name, must have been even more heartbreaking. Alas, if they had gone to the battlefield, fought bravely against the enemy, and returned home victoriously, it would have been a grateful and glorious occurrence. Yet, tragically, while crossing the Nakdong River at night with troops, Gyeong-pil’s horse faltered, causing him to fall into the water and drown. When Choi-gyeong heard the news, she made her way to the Nakdong River, searching for her husband's body. After several days of seeking in vain, she could not recover it. In despair, she decided to end her own life and jumped into the very waters where her husband had perished.

It is said that the local villagers, moved by her sorrow, created a song in her memory:

As I look at the sky  
I see no stars shining  
The order of conscription  
Has caught the country unawares.  

Uruma’s poet,  
Having died ten times over,  
Not even ten curses  
Can heal our wounds.  

Ten times over, my dear Choi-gyeong,  
My wife’s socks have gone threadbare,  
Wishing to part, I take leave.  

With the grace of a noble horse,  
Carrying burdens with ease,  
I bid farewell to my father,  
My son, worth a thousand gold.  

May you tread lightly on this path,  
Bidding farewell to my mother,  
My son, worth ten thousand gold.  

May you tread lightly on this path,  
Bidding farewell to my brother,  
My dear brother, worth a thousand gold.  

As for Choi-gyeong,  
May she go in a maid’s attire,  
Naming herself Ok Dong-pil,  
While I name myself Lee Gyeong-pil.  

As you reach the home,  
Please send a letter to my family.  
If they attain a son,  
Tell them to rise on the next hill.  

If you reach Uruma,  
Tell them to ascend the back hill.  
When reaching those I love,  
Do not forget my long hair.  

Tears well up in my eyes,  
If Choi-gyeong comes to request,  
Having dressed in my gown,  
In a seungmu outfit,  
To be buried with my beloved,  
While the bamboo forest grows lush.  

May the mountains be our stillness,  
As the years continue to unfold,  
Lee Gyeong-pil will blossom anew.  
Should I not flourish, let it be in sorrow,  
Should I flourish, it would be in cultivation  
'{Combining joy and lament}'.

 

**Summary**

This article tells the poignant story of Lee Gyeong-pil and Choi-gyeong amidst the devastation of the Imjin War. Their brief happiness and subsequent tragic fate illustrate the personal toll of historical conflicts on individual lives. The lament written in the song captures the cultural grief and loss brought on by war, showcasing the deeply personal side of Korea's colonial history. Historians may find the emotional resonance of this folk song significant, as it reflects the suffering endured by everyday people during tumultuous times.

						靑吾
때는 바로 선조임진란(宣祖壬辰亂)때이라 한다. 경상북도 고령땅(慶北高靈) 어떤 촌에 리경필(李敬弼)이라는 젊은 소년이 잇섯다. 그 소년은 집안 형세가 그럿케 어렵지도 안코 부모형제가 다 구존하야 과히 남부럽지안케 사는 팔자조흔 소년이엇다. 그리고 그의 매부되는 옥동필(玉東弼)이란 사람도 또한 그와 비슷하게 자라난 사람으로 한동리에 살며 피차 남매의 관게까지 매저가지고 날마다의 조케 지내섯다. 리경필은 또 나히 20세 청년시대에 그 근처에서 최경(最卿)이란 처녀와 결혼을 하야 왼 집안이 경사스럽게 잔채를 하고 두 신랑 신부는 어린 원앙의 무리 모양으로 새 정을 부처서 자미스럽게 지냇섯다. 이러케 하루 이틀을 지내고 아모 고장도 업시 내지 백년까지 해로하게 되엿다면 우리 인간으로써 그 얼마나 행복스러웟스랴. 그러나 조화옹의 작희라 할지 우리 인간에 불행이라 할지 제삼일이 되던 날 밤에 그 두 사람의 머리 우에는 청천벽력보다도 더 무서운 벼락불이 떠러젓스니 그것은 그때에 임진란이란 그 무시무시한 큰 란리가 이러나서 강제로 증병령을 나리여 전국에 십팔세 이상의 장정이란 한아도 남기지 아니하고 모조리 뽑아서 적군과 싸호는 전지로 보내게 되엿는데 이 리경필과 옥동필 남매 두 사람도 역시 병정에 뽑히여 가게 된 그것이엿다. 누구의명령이라 감히 아니갈 수 잇스랴. 그의 부모이하 왼 집안 사람들은 건지로 보내는데 밤을 새워가며 의복을 짓고 가다 먹을 음식도 작만하여 일방으로 전지를 파라서 마필을 작만하야 주엇섯다. 부모의 슬하에서 그때까지 아모 향방도 모르고 편히 살고 그중에도 신혼지초라 따뜻한 사랑의 꿈을 쉬다가 별안간 죽을 땅으로 가는 경필의 심정도 어떠할 것을 짐작 하겠지만은 물설은 곳으로 다만 신랑 하나를 바라고 시집을 왓다가 남편의 이름도 잘 모르고 단 사흘되던 밤에 뜻박게 그 남편을 죽을 땅으로 보내게 된 신부의 심정이야 더구나 어떠하다고 말할 수 잇스랴. 그나마 그들의 전지에 가서 적군과 잘 싸워 승전고를 울니고 개선가를 부르며 집에를 잘 도라왓다면 그러한 고맙고 영광그러운 일이 업겟지만은 불행히 이 경필은 밤중에 말을 타고 복잡한 중에 여러 사람들과 가티 낙동강을 건느다가 말이 잘못 실수하야 가엽게 물에 빠저 죽엇섯다. 그의 신부되는 최경은 그 소문을 듯고 자긔 남편의 시체를 차즈려고 도보로 낙동강까지 와서 몃칠 동안을 애를 썻스나 그 시체는 도모지 차즐수가 업섯다. 그리하야 그는 최후에 죽기도 결심하고 그 남편이 죽던 물로 뛰여드러가서 빠저 죽엇스니 그 지방사람들이 그를 불상이 녁여 이 노래를 지엿다고 한다.
