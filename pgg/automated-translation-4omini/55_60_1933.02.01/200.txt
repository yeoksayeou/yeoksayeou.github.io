Excessive Kindness  
Store Owner: "If you wear this to make a dress, you will probably look ten years younger."  
Female Customer: "What do you mean? ... I am only twenty now, so if I look ten years younger, that would make me look like a ten-year-old child!"

Due to Being Short  
In a circus, a dwarf named Gati, who is quite short, applied as an animal trainer.  
Staff: "How could someone as short as you possibly train that huge lion?"  
Gati: "That’s right. Being short will actually help."  
Staff: "How is that?"  
Gati: "Because the lion will see me as a child and will think of me as less of a threat and will lead it to think of me as food!"

Ignorant Scheme  
A: "What’s this... you’re not being straightforward!"  
B: "Well, I thought I might try a bit of traveling..."  
A: "Then why don't you look straight ahead instead of side-glancing?"  
B: "No, no... I’m just trying to find the route for the journey now..."

The Father's Fondness for Math  
Child: "Dad! Please help me with this math homework."  
Father: "What now... Is it that a hotel sells liquor at 1 won 50 jeon and if you mix it with five gongs of water, how much will one serving of liquor cost? Damn it, you little brat! If there’s a bar that sells liquor like that, don’t go there and get your liquor; you should have learned that in school first!"

White Labor  
Manager: "Do you have prior experience in labor?"  
Job Seeker: "Yes. Of course, I worked doing labor that made me turn completely white!"  
Manager: "I see - labor that makes you entirely white? You must be misusing that term!"  
Job Seeker: "No, not at all. I worked at a rice mill before."

Nonsensical Treatment  
Male Customer: "Honey, if guests come over and I want to serve snacks, wouldn't it be better to buy something better instead of this?"  
Wife's face turns red as she stays silent...  
This isn't unreasonable, considering that the snacks were just brought by the recent guests.  

**Summary**  

The article showcases a collection of humorous anecdotes reflecting daily life in the early 1930s in Korea. Each piece highlights societal quirks and interpersonal relations, infused with a blend of irony and wit characteristic of that era. The interactions often reveal the underlying social dynamics of a colonial society where slight confusion and humor provided light-hearted moments amidst the difficulties of life under Japanese rule. Historians might find interest in how humor reflects societal norms and coping mechanisms in a colonized context.
