漫談, 公設 카페 出現  

Recently, as many cafes have sprung up due to the issue of "ero. service" causing problems here and there, the authorities have researched and deliberated on various matters, resulting in the invention of the "Public Cafe".  

Consequently, the intimidating sign "Public Hanyang Cafe" was proudly put up at a corner of Jongno, the busiest intersection in Seoul.  

The three enthusiastic café-goers, seeing this brilliant invention, were the first to set foot in the café.  

门 "Hey hey, what brings you here?"  

A "What? What do you mean what brings us here... Hey, things seem a bit different here... Yes, I came to get some coffee."  

门 "Then please take this entrance ticket and go to the reception to register."  

B "This is kind of troublesome."  

Upon reaching the reception, they saw a female staff member there.  

C "Um, I came to get some coffee..."  

女 "Did you come alone?"  

C "No, I came with two others."  

女 "Then please present this entrance ticket. Also, you need to fill out this registration form with your address, name, job, age, etc., and affix your seal."  

A "This is too strict. I have to seal... But what if I don’t have a seal?"  

女 "If you don't have a seal, you must still make a thumbprint."  

B "A thumbprint! This is just like a court trial..."  

C "Okay, let’s do this..."  

女 "Is coffee all you plan to consume?"  

C "Well, how should we do this? After all this registration for just a cup of coffee seems a bit unfair... Ugh, I might just opt for some whiskey."  

AB "Then we’ll do the same..."  

女 "However, whiskey cannot be served to individuals ordering more than three drinks at once. So please keep that in mind."  

C "Well, that’s quite a process... At this rate, are we going to be able to drink even a cup of coffee today... Well, anyway, let’s follow the instructions here. Oh, look, there is another female staff here..."  

女 "Yes... But are you all the individuals mentioned here?"  

B "Yes, it’s all of us."  

女 "Are you certain?"  

C "If we're drinking whiskey, don’t you think it’s a bit odd to ask if our names match? Just give us some water."  

女 "Then, you must not carry any firearms or other weapons, I assume?"  

B "Come on, we’re not gangsters so don’t worry."  

女 "In that case, please head to the left where the supply office is, and submit this there."  

A "This is quite a complicated procedure... Well, let’s see if we can manage to drink a cup of coffee today... Oh, hey, there’s a female staff member here as well..."  

女 "Now... are you all the individuals listed?"  

B "That’s right. It’s all of us."  

女 "Are you confirming that?"  

C "When we’re drinking whiskey, what does it matter where the others are? Just give me some water!"  

女 "Then please enter that room. Then, you will be called one by one to receive your coffee."  

A "Wait, then where do we drink the whiskey?"  

女 "Whiskey is served in this separate room, one person at a time."  

B "But three of us are not drinking at the same time..."  

女 "No. It is absolutely not permitted for more than one person to drink alcohol together, as this can lead to excitement or accidents; hence, this is a regulation here!"  

女 "Alright, now I will call names, so please wait in the smoking area... Mr. Kim Bo-ra from Anguk-dong!"  

B "Hey, they’re calling Kim."  

C "Yes, that’s me."  

女 "You are Mr. Kim Bo-ra?"  

C "Yes, that’s correct."  

女 "Then why didn’t you respond the first time I called your name, making me call you multiple times? Maybe your hearing isn’t so good, shall I send you to the ear doctor?"  

C "Absolutely not, my hearing is fine!"  

女 "You will now be served whiskey, please come this way."  

As C entered the room, the door was abruptly closed and locked.  

C "Hey, this feels like they’re locking me away without serving any drinks..."  

At that moment, a female staff member came in with whiskey and snacks.  

C "Oh, I see, this is a café after all... But why does this whiskey taste so bland, like it’s been watered down?"  

女 "No, it’s not watered down; it’s just that the alcohol content has been lowered for safety reasons."  

C "So this is three won per glass... Well then, I guess that’s fair. By the way, miss, let’s shake hands."  

女 "That sort of thing is absolutely against the regulations here."  

C "What does it matter with those regulations... I’m not afraid of that."  

Just as he attempted to grab the female staff's wrist, an officer unexpectedly appeared behind him, glaring fiercely.  

警 "No, no, you cannot behave that way here. Just drink your allocated drink and leave..."  

C "Yes, yes, yes."  

警 "This is your first time, so I’ll let it slide. However, if it happens again, you will be detained immediately."  

C "Yes, of course. It’s been my first experience drinking under scrutiny like this!"  

Thus, all three of them suffered such indignities upon leaving the front door, when the doorman asked,  

"May I ask what you had to drink?"  

C "Well, to say the least, we drank three glasses of whiskey, but it was so weak that we left feeling just as sober as we came."  

门 "Hmph, that is the regulation for this café, you shouldn’t be surprised!"  

Dear reader, what do you think? If such cafés were to emerge (which may happen in the future), what would that be like?  

**Summary**  

The article presents a satirical commentary on the emergence of public cafés in colonial Korea as a response to issues with unregulated cafés. The narrative dives into the absurdities and bureaucratic hurdles faced by customers merely trying to enjoy a drink, reflecting broader themes of social control and regulation during the colonial period. Historians might find the commentary revealing of societal attitudes towards alcohol consumption and public morality, illustrating the colonial government's attempts to manage civic life tightly.
