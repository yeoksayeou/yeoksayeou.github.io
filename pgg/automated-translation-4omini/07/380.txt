기사제목	編輯局員總出 내가 第一 창피하얏든 일, 記者 1人 1話, 外三寸 小祥에 (The First Embarrassing Incident I Experienced as a Member of the Editorial Bureau, One Story per Reporter, About a Small Blessing of the Exterior Three Cuns)  
필자	李敦化  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Recollections and Personal Stories)  

This is a story from when I was 12 years old. I had become a vagrant and one day wandered into a house that was said to be the home of an exterior three cun family, following my father's orders to attend to a ritual. More than the desire to perform the ritual, I was motivated by a craving to drink alcohol and thus I went to the exterior three cun house. 

The day of the ritual arrived. However, having never even seen a ritual in my life, as a vagrant, I had no idea how to conduct one. It appeared that there were many rites involved, and I was assigned a specific role. My task was to place the ritual foods on the ceremonial table, but it was not just a matter of placing the bowls anywhere; there were proper ceremonial customs I had to follow. Not knowing these customs, I placed the food bowls wherever I thought was appropriate. As a result, an elderly man berated me each time I placed a bowl, saying, “Ah! A person who can read doesn’t even know how to conduct a ritual…” and chastised me repeatedly. The foods were not just a couple of items but a multitude of dishes, and with each dish I placed, the elder's admonishments grew louder and more pronounced. It became such that he threw back his head, puffed out his belly, and while standing, he would repeatedly say, “Ah! A literate person – ah – a knowledgeable person…”

This constant berating made me furious. Out of habit due to being a vagrant, I snapped and said, “You – you come do this! You handle it!” and threw the bowls onto the table before running out. 

A major issue arose among the attendees at the ritual. Because of this, I could not even drink the ritual wine and returned home that night without having participated.

  

**Summary**  

This article describes a humorous and embarrassing personal experience of the author during a traditional ritual, where his unfamiliarity with the customs leads to a scandal at the event. It reflects the social dynamics of the time, revealing how traditional customs clashed with the realities of life for the impoverished and marginalized, such as vagrants. Historians might find the writer's struggle between traditional roles and personal identity significant in understanding societal expectations in colonial Korea.

編輯局員總出 내가 第一 창피하얏든 일, 記者 1人 1話, 外三寸 小祥에
						李敦化
12세 시절 일임니다. 부랑자로 돌아 단기다가 어느 핸가 집이라고 드러 간즉 外三寸되는 이의 小祥이 되얏다고 祭祀보라 가라는 아부지의 명령을 밧고 제사 보겟다는 인사보다도 술 엇어 먹을 욕심으로 죠와라고 한 거름에 外三寸宅을 갓슴니다. 제사 날이 되얏슴니다. 하나 평생에 제사라고는 구경도 못하든 부랑자의 몸이라 제사를 엇더케 지내는지를 알 수 잇겟슴니까. 그런데 제사에는 여러 가지 執禮가 잇는 모양인데 나 한데도 무슨 집례와 갓흔 任目하나이 지정되엿슴니다. 그 任目은 제사음식을 祭床에다 바다놋는 任目인데 그것도 음식 그릇을 아모케나 놋는 것이 아니라 노흘데 꼭 놋는 제례가 잇는 것인데 나는 그 제례를 알지 못한 까닭에 음식 그릇을 아무데나 제 생각 나는대로 노앗슴니다. 한즉 겻해 엇든 노인 한 분이 그릇을 놀적 마다 「하! 글읽은 사람이 제사도 몰느고……」하면서 책망을 함니다. 음식도 한 두가지가 아니오. 멧 수십가지 만흔 음식이라 한 가지를 노흘 젹 마다 노인의 책망은 한 번式 더 하야 짐니다. 책망도 거저 하는 것이 아니라 별하게 고개를 뒤로 잔덕 잭기고 배를 압흐로 내밀고 서서 「하! 글 읽은 사람이-하-일 아는 사람이……」하고 連해 책망을 하는 바람에 나는 골이 상투 끝까지 올나 갓슴니다. 게다가 부랑자의 버릇이라 화가 벌컥 나서 「자-내 집례를 자네가 맛하 하소. 엑이 자네 실큰 혼자 하소.」하고 그릇을 상에다 메여치고 달어 나왓슴니다. 祭客中에서 큰 문제가 일어 낫슴니다. 그래서 나는 제사 술도 엇더 먹지 못하고 그 밤으로 빽손이를 대고 말앗슴니다.＜134＞
