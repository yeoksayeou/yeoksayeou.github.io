In both private schools and even in public schools of Kyŏnggi (京鄕), there exists a monthly published writing that is copied and distributed to all students. This writing is found in the magazine "어린이" (Children), which is the highest and largest circulated magazine in the Korean magazine industry. It features the "Reading Book for Children" by Bangŏng-hwan (方定煥). As it consists of less than 50 lines of short prose that instills excellent thoughts and strong abilities in children, it receives absolute acclaim not only from young boys and girls but also from teachers and parents. Such pieces as "Cute Sacrifice" and "Young Drummer" are invaluable writings akin to precious gold. Even foreign teachers have been moved to copy them by hand to share with local children.

The July issue of "어린이" includes more than 20 specially selected new articles in addition to the reading book, and it comes with a supplement of "Children’s Newspaper and Photographic Reports on Strange Customs and Habits Around the World." The price for this book is merely 15 jeon (錢). Dear reader, please send 15 one-yen stamps instead of purchasing a pack of cigarettes to Kaep'ilsa (開闢社) in Kyŏngsŏng (京城). You will not only enjoy reading yourself but also be able to share it with your neighbors. <152>  

**Summary**

The article highlights the impact of the magazine "어린이" and its educational content on children in Korea during the colonial period, emphasizing the importance of literature that promotes strong character and moral values. Notably, it indicates how foreign educators were inspired by this Korean literary output, showcasing an engagement with cultural exchange even in a time of colonial rule. This points to the resilience of Korean cultural identity amidst foreign dominance, which historians may find significant in understanding educational practices of the time.
