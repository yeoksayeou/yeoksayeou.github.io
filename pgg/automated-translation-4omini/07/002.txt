기사제목	새벽길 (Dawn Road)  

필자	李秉岐 (Lee Byeong-gi)  

기사형태	시 (Poem)  

  

Dawn Road  

  

I will walk the path I could not tread at night  

What shall I do once I have slept well?  

Whether others go or not, I will go my own way.  

  

The village rooster crows, does it awaken the sleeping Seoul?  

Even the small stream at the edge flows gently and softly,  

Who will lend an ear to listen to it?  

  

Those who go to the market through the open gate,  

Those who stagger with burdens, swaying as they walk,  

Becoming companions on this dawn road, they all seem to follow.  

  

**Summary**  

The poem "Dawn Road" by Lee Byeong-gi reflects the feelings of an individual traversing the quiet early morning streets, contemplating both solitude and companionship. It paints a vivid landscape where life stirs awake, yet maintains a sense of introspective solitude. This piece captures the essence of the moment when the city is waking, illustrating a transitional period reflective of larger societal changes. Historians might find interest in the poem’s subtle commentary on the everyday lives of people during the colonial period, hinting at both a collective experience and personal isolation.

새벽길
						李秉岐
밤에 못걸은 길을 새벽에나 걸으리라
잘잠을 다자고야 밧븐일 어이하리
남이야 가든 마든가 내갈대로 가리라

마을 닭이 운들 잠든 서울 깨우드냐
담디테 실개천도 졸졸잘잘 흐르건만
뉘그리 귀를 기울여 들어줄이 잇느냐

가개ㅅ문 열린대로 저자보러 가는이도
물지게 흔들흔들 비틀걸음 하는 이도
새벽의 길동무되여 간데족족 따르노나
