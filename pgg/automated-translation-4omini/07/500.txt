Local Flavor, Mushroom Picking and Hunting, Gangwon-do Wonju  
“Is that the Sacred Seven?  
“Yeah, it’s the 원삼 (Wonsam), isn’t it?”  
“How many did you pick?”  
“I couldn’t even pick ten with one hand.”  
“I picked more than you!”  
“How many did you pick?”  
“I couldn’t even pick five or six.”  
“Come on, don’t lie.”  
“Truly, my mushrooms are overripe!”  
“Indeed. Last year, I picked more than a hundred!”  
“I did the same last year.”  
Even now, in the early morning when the mood is flowing and the dew drops rest quietly on the grass, adults and children meet and exchange such conversations upon meeting in the valleys. This scene occurs during the mid-eighth month of the lunar calendar in our town, Wonju, while picking mushrooms. This really is like a play of celestial beings in a fairyland. The farmers, going around to pick the fragrant mushrooms, resemble those playing in a heavenly realm amidst the jewels of dew and the fresh-tinged mushrooms. By this time, people flock to the area to pick mushrooms. As soon as dawn breaks, they head up the mountain, spending two to three hours rummaging through the hills to collect them. By early afternoon, they come down, eat lunch, and then head out to work in the fields. This is the routine during mushroom-picking season. The collected mushrooms are gathered and sold at the market. When selling them at the market, just saying they come from 원주安昌 (Anchang, located in the area where the mushrooms grow) suffices to sell them without worrying about quality. Generally, the prices can vary, but a bunch (20 pieces) can range from thirty to forty to sixty cents.

There’s one more thing, which is a gathering on a designated day at one household where everyone comes together to cook a dish from the mushrooms they picked to share and enjoy as they cheerfully spend the day. As the sun sets in the west and the moon rises over the eastern ridge, they return singing songs in groups of three or five. During these hunts, men, women, the old, and the young all go without exception. (留京城 松風生)＜145＞  

**Summary**  

The article paints a vivid picture of a cultural tradition in early 20th-century Korea centered around mushroom picking in the region of Wonju, Gangwon-do. It captures the community spirit, the joy of gathering, and the interactions among people during this activity, showcasing both the labor and leisure aspects of rural life. From a historical perspective, this glimmer of pre-modern rural lifestyle under Japanese rule reveals nuances of Korean identity and communal activities, which may interest historians looking into cultural resilience and adaptation during colonial times.

아즉도 새벽에 기분이 흐르고 넘치며 풀 끗에 이슬방울이 고요히 빗나고 잇는 이른 새벽에 이 산 골작이 저 산 골작이에서 어른이나 아이들이나 만나기만 하면 이러한 대화를 끄집어 낸다. 음력 8월 중순에 우리 고을 원주에서 송이를 따면서 짓거리는 한 장면이다. 이야말노 仙境에 神仙노름이다. 풀 끗에 맷친 진주와 갓흔 이슬방울을 바스트리며 향기가 나는 송이 이것을 따러 다니는 농부의 모양 참으로 仙境에서 노는 듯하다. 이 때가 되면 그 근방에서 송이를 따러 묵거드러 온다. 훤하게 먼동이 트랴하면 벌서 산으로 올라 가서 二三時間 산을 휘 더듬어 따 가지고 이른 아츰 때면 나려와 아츰을 먹고 또 들로 일을 하러 나간다. 이것이 송이때에 하로의 푸로크람이다. 이러케 따서 멧 칠이고 모아서는 장에 갓다 파는 것이다. 장에서 팔 때에 이 송이는 原州安昌(安昌은 송이나는 산에 소재지)송이임니다.』라고만 하면 갑에 高低를 혀아리지 안코 산다. 대개 갑은 일정치는 못하나 한 두름(20개)에 三四十錢 乃至 五六十錢에 이르는 것이다.
또 한 가지가 잇스니 그것은 다른게 안이라 날을 정하여 가지고 한 집에서 한 가지식 해 가지고 가서 산에서 송이를 따 가지고 그것을 국을 끄려 가지고 먹으며 하로를 유쾌히 보내고 日落西山하고 月出東嶺할 때에 三三五五 노래를 부르며 내려들 온다. 이 철렵에는 男女老少가 업시누구던지 다 가는 것이다. ( 留京城 松風生)＜145＞
