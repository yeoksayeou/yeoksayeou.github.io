기사제목	맹낭한 이약이 (A Displeasing Remedy)  
기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

A doctor in dermatology was visited by his friend, who said, “Even during your illness, you’re still practicing that disgusting skin disease of dermatology?” To which the doctor proudly replied, “Ah, to become a doctor, dermatology is the best. Firstly, the reason is that few come knocking at your door in the dead of night; secondly, because patients never die; and thirdly, because patients never recover quickly.”  

“……….  

“Lately, my house is small, so I’m planning to expand it a bit... it's a bit cramped.”  

“Expanding? That’s fortunate to hear. How long will it take?”  

“What can I say... I just blocked one room and made it into three.”  

“………  

When the elderly man was about to die, he looked at his young wife and said, “I have no hope of recovery, so when I die, please have my household affairs taken care of.”  
“I thought about that long ago, and I’ve already prepared his clothes to fit his body.”

**Summary**  

This excerpt humorously explores the advantages of being a dermatologist, emphasizing the lack of night emergencies and the slow recovery of patients. It features a dialogue between two friends, showcasing cultural attitudes towards various medical professions during the colonial period. Historians might find the casual banter reflective of the social sentiments and patient-doctor relationships of the time, revealing insights into life and death in 1920s Korea.
