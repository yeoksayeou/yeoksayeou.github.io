기사제목	編輯局員總出 내가 第一 창피하얏든 일 (The Most Embarrassing Thing I've Done as an Editorial Staff)  
필자	朴達成  
기사형태	회고·수기 (Memoir)  

Look at this disgraceful situation. Isn't it embarrassing? At the time when I was a schoolboy, I had never even ridden a horse once (because I was scared), so how could I ride a bicycle? It was about eleven years ago, during the spring of that year when I was in my third year of middle school and working as a teacher. At that time, one of my classmates, Kim Jin-hong, was using a bicycle while running a business at Bugyeong Station. I spent most of my spare time during school hours heading to Bugyeong Station to learn how to ride a bicycle. As with anything, learning something new isn’t easy, but at least I didn’t run into any other people as I practiced learning to ride a bicycle. So, whenever there was a chance, I would secretly hop onto a bicycle, wobbling back and forth as I rode back and forth, side to side.

It was Sunday, and I wore my newly made pants and jacket along with a white haori. Starting from early in the morning, I went out to practice riding the bicycle. Each day I managed to do better than the last—today was better than yesterday, and now was better than then—yet somehow I felt uneasy. Looking at my reflection, I thought, “My appearance looks decent; surely I can ride a bike well!” With that self-assurance, I joyfully rode around the roads leading to Won-san and to Pyonggang.

At that time, I thought I would easily pay ten thousand won for a bicycle.

“Look at this. ‘I think I’m doing pretty well!’” I thought as I rode with the wind blowing through my clothes. “This feels good! I’m flying!” Without really paying attention, I sped along at full speed. Suddenly, I heard the rumbling sound of a train approaching. 

“The train is coming! It always looks lively!” In the moment of distraction, my gaze turned towards the train, and before I knew it, I lost control and my bicycle flew straight off the newly constructed road into a ditch, stopping abruptly. 

Regaining my senses, I found myself in a peculiar predicament. The circumstances had thrown me into a situation I would never expect. My bicycle had crashed into the ditch, and I seemed to be stuck in the mud. I couldn’t get off or move forward, frozen on top of my bike, staring at the sky while sighing deeply, as onlookers—people passing by—burst into laughter.

What a miserable turn of events. I barely managed to free one leg, stepping into the ditch with an agonizingly deep sigh. My waist somehow ended up buried in the mud. After struggling to get myself up onto the road, I realized my pants, jacket, and haori were all covered in mud, and my bicycle remained stuck in the mud with a wretched appearance, while I laughed awkwardly, my shoes now looking grotesque from the mud. 

There was no alternative; summoning all my courage, I dragged my mud-covered self through the town square to the bicycle parking area. Women, children, pedestrians, and farmers laughed at me without restraint. What kind of shame is this for a school teacher? I went over to Kim’s house to change into another set of clothes and gathered some people to help retrieve my bicycle and shoes from the muck.  

  
**Summary**  

This piece recounts a humorous yet humiliating incident faced by the author, Park Dae-seong, as he attempted to learn to ride a bicycle near the Bugyeong Station during the 1920s. While navigating the challenges of learning something new, he finds himself in an embarrassing situation that brings laughter from passersby. The author’s experience captures the social dynamics of the time, where a young man's failure became public entertainment, reflecting societal attitudes toward education and masculinity in colonial Korea. Historians might find interest in the story as it illustrates the cultural pressures of colonialism while simultaneously revealing the personal and collective experiences of young Koreans striving for modernity.

編輯局員總出 내가 第一 창피하얏든 일, 記者 1人 1話, 自轉車만 보면
						朴達成
자-이 꼬락산이를 좀 보시요. 엇지도 창피한지요. 글세 학교에 단일 때 목마 한 번 못 타본 놈이 (무서워서)자전차가 다 뭐란 말이요. 바로 11년 전 수물두살 나든 해 느즌 봄 평강 엇던 학교에서 교사 노릇을 하고 잇든 때입니다. 그때 학교 근처에는 나의 同窓友 金鎭弘君이 잇섯는데 그는 福溪驛에서 장사를 하면서 자전차를 사용했슴니다. 나는 上學時間만 지내면 각금 福溪驛으로 나아가 자전차 타기를 배우지오. 무엇이든지 처음 배울 때는 다 그럿치만 자전차 처음 배울 때 갓치 사람을 밋치게 하는 것은 업습듸다. 그래서 틈만 잇스면 자전차 任者야 좃타거나 실타거나 비위 좃케 집어 타고 동으로 서으로 왓다 갓다 하지요.
하로는 일요일인데 맛츰 새로 만든 바지 저고리에 하얀 洋木 두루마기를 가라 입고 일즉부터 나아가 자전차 연습을 합니다. 오늘이 어제보다 낫고 지금이 아까보다 나은데 엇지도 심기가 좃턴지오. 생긴 꼬락산이 보아서는 자전차 타는 재간은 괜찬타! 고 자찬을 하면서 元山 통로로 平康 통로로 신이 나서 왓다 갓다 함니다. 이 때 갓태서는 자전차 하나에 멧 만원이라도 주고 살 것 갓습듸다.
자- 이것 보시오. 「이만 하면 어지간 하다!」라고 自贊을 하면서 平康通 新作路로 자전차 박휘를 바람이 나게 굴니임니다. 「시원하다! 잘도 간다!」하면서 별로 주의도 업시 全速力으로 내닷습니다. 맛츰 기차가 쿵쾅거리고 옴니다. 「기차 온다! 언제 보든지 저 놈은 씩씩해!」하고 기차에 左右 눈이 다 가는 刹那에 언첸가 발서 자전차는 新作路를 뚝 떠러저 한 길이나 넘는 즌탕속에 수루룩 드러 가 턱 停車를 함니다. 정신을 차려 살펴보니 아-이런 야단이 어대 잇겟소. 자전차 압 뒤 박휘가 즌탕 속에 뭇친 것은 둘재요 몸둥이 하반부가 즌탕 속에 뭇쳐 잇슴니다 그려. 자-이런 야단이 잇소. 내려 서지도 못하고 나아 가지도 물너 가지도 꼼작을 못하고 자전차 우에 그대로 안저 한울만 처다보며 한숨만 짓고 잇노라니 오는 사람 가는 사람 모다 拍掌大笑를 함니다.
일이 이러케 된 바에 엇지 함닛가. 한 발을 간신히 빼여 즌탕 속에 그냥 내려 섯습니다. 엇지도 깁흔지 배곱 이상이 즌탕 속에 잠겻슴니다. 간신히 허우적 거려 길 우에 올나 서고 보니 바지, 저고리, 두루막이가 全혀 감탕탈을 쓴 것은 물론이오 자전차는 그대로 감탕 속에 박켜 招人種만한 울을 향해 혼자 웃고 구두는 밋구리 모양으로 감탕 속에서 한숨을 짓고 잇슴니다.
아무리 해도 別道理가 업는지라. 大勇氣를 내여 그대로 停車場通 市街 복판으로 그 夜叉가 다 된 꼬락산이을 내 노앗슴니다. 婦女 兒童 行人 農軍할 것 업시 엇지도 웃든지요. 학교 선생님 체면에 이게 무슨 꼬락산이 겟소. 金의 집에 가서 옷 한 벌을 엇어 입고 사람들을 데리고 가서 자전차와 구두를 뽑아 내엿슴니다.＜136＞
