We boarded the tram bound for Seodaemun...

Between same-sex peers, it is already difficult enough, and especially when a stranger male keeps staring at you unrelentingly, it makes you feel guilty to the point your head lowers and your heart races. I was anxiously touching my face with my fingers wondering if there was rice stuck to my nose; I even casually tried to turn my head so that I could touch my face with my palms without appearing suspicious in doing so.

"Who is this strange man looking at a woman’s face?" 

Having such doubtful thoughts in my mind, I poked my friend Yeobhe (엽헤) for confirmation. 

"Don’t you know him? That’s Park Heedo, the principal of the Central Kindergarten!" 

"Ahh... I didn’t know," she replied. 

Finally recognizing him, my heart raced even more. I would have likely said something inappropriate out of embarrassment had she not nudged me.

Filled with admiration and respect, I turned my gaze to scrutinize the teacher from the top of his black bowler hat to the tip of his shoes. It was my first time encountering him in person. Known by students as "the elephant," he had a gigantic stature and a considerably broad face; indeed, he looked like the archetype of a politician. Moreover, his protruding belly seemed to contain strategies equivalent to Napoleon's. Through dark glasses, he surveyed the colorful world around him, with a nose pointed as if it were about to join the line between his upper lip and mouth. His thick lips indicated that he possessed tremendous patience and energy. At the same time, his overall shape resembled that of a gentle and friendly giant - like an elephant, hippo, or cow. However, one flaw seemed to be that his voice was surprisingly sentimental compared to his giant physique.

Oh no! The conductor said it’s time to get off. I'll catch up later... 

What B-boy Saw of Seo Ch’ung 

C童 

It’s true. Speaking like that quite often leads to trouble later. Our teacher becomes frighteningly serious when intoxicated. So, can you please substitute for me next time? 

That teacher is quite a character. Though he has a low height and a dark complexion, he is a person without fear in the world, being very robust. Yet when drunk, there’s no denying he gets terrifyingly fierce. When he arrives usually, he is already quite drunk. So he tends to hold back from drinking too much even more. I suppose that when he’s intoxicated, it’s because he automatically feels curious... After all, he only comes because there are ladies around him. 

He arrives drunk, and how could he sit still? The very moment his voice raises, he berates the attendees while pacing back and forth. During those times, it's even more terrifying, so we dare not speak... But then, the madams are also scared. They prefer to hide. In those times, he yells like a storm. So it’s a common occurrence for those who can’t handle it to cry. After he has caused a storm and leaves, his departure is happier than his arrival. The only thing we worry about is whether he will leave or stay. The moment he signals his leaving, we open the door and say, "Best wishes on your journey home" while watching him go. The road is so wide that each path makes us fear when he will leave. Once he closes the door and comes inside, it’s like a storm has finally passed. We sigh with relief. He also feels relieved, for when he isn't drunk, he is also well-mannered. Speaking of which, should I go ahead and state such things for my sake? Good day to you all. “Why do you seem happy that I'm leaving?” 

What the Tavern Owner Saw of Kwon Deok-gye 

Hwang Teok-yi 

He certainly enjoys drinking. He usually stops by on his way home in the afternoon. Therefore, if you don't see him around, you feel quite dull. Usually, he brings a small group of two or three friends with him; it’s rare to see him come with more. 

He drinks gracefully, sipping delicately. He pairs his drink with a kind of snack just before getting drunk. After around five drinks, he tends to drop jokes, entertainment, and ghost stories while trying to get more drinks from the waitstaff. To boot, he enjoys staying within the group, and it’s not often he decides to leave. If he ever comes alone, my heart sinks. Whenever he comes alone, he usually doesn't drink much and tends to leave early, which is quite a sight. During those times, it’s just that he is heading home feeling peckish after drinking in the evening. Regardless whether it's in the company of others or not, he drinks anyway. 

As he enters the tavern, he tends not to speak much and orders, "Pour me a big cup" - meaning Makgeolli (막걸리). He yanks a bucket and drinks it down with vigor, reaches for a piece of kimchi, and asks for a cup of beans. While grabbing them, the coins drop loudly into the cup. In terms of entrée and side dishes, the beans might disappear into his pockets, so he leaps toward the door. There is some confusion because often other guests arrive later, and if they come in pairs, they may perish with worry. 

What the Waitress Saw of Cha Sang-jam 

RS子 

... Mr. Cha does not come often. While I do not know about other cafes, I hardly see him here, which is quite unfortunate, as there would be no topics of conversation when he doesn’t come. That said, he only arrives when he is blissfully intoxicated, meaning he doesn’t drink any else and engages in unwanted conversation with all kinds of ridiculous, sultry, or raunchy jokes that make ears tingle. At times they can become quite inappropriate.

Honestly, he is known for his charm, being a professor whom we respect. However, when caught in the mood, he is a fearsome character, and when too much indulged in drinking, he can hurl insults at others. He tends to note that the Japanese waitress is more lovely than Korean waitresses; thus, spouting something like, "Japanese girls are cuter!" After indulging for a while during these rantings, he will leave with a sentence like, "Damn it, what a waste!" I remember an occasion where four guests sat together and drank beer, only to leave after having just two bottles. Then suddenly, he derisively paid the bill with 1.95 won and threw 2 won at it without waiting, storming out. Remarkably, he left me with only 5 jeon. Even so, it shows he thought to give me that small change, and I have cherished it since. To this day, I still guard it tightly, and if we met again, I could give him that piece. 

What the Bathhouse Owner Saw of Yoon Chih-ho 

Park Su-cheol 

Yoon Chih-ho! Specifically hired for our bathhouse, this Mr. Yoon is strikingly different from other gentlemen. 

He always arrives at the bathhouse with his young child. While this isn't strange—other fathers do the same, it doesn’t stand out. However, one unique aspect is that he wears his traditional garments with his pockets strung up on a long string. 

Everything from pocket knives, handkerchiefs, cosmetics, fountain pens, keychains... even his wallet, all are tied like those of a fish merchant carrying his catch ready to take home inside of the pocket hidden beneath his garment. 

Thus he's a complicated individual who inspects everything he brings, and accumulation consists of two additional items — his glasses and a gold ring. His character is neat and mannerly. However, I must confess, he might attract the most ridiculous thieves, worrying that all his belongings could easily be stolen in one swift move. 

What Geese's Brother Saw of Jo Byeong-uk

Lee O-hong 

Mr. Jo Byeong-uk, who served as the sales director at the Chosun Ilbo, I have become acquainted with mainly through the recent dinners set by the newspaper office at restaurants. 

I have heard he studied in the United States, earned a doctor’s degree, and returned home, but in the recent times that I finally saw his opened face, it felt a bit terrifying at first. 

However, in time, as I saw him repeatedly, and even though he doesn’t drink heavily, getting slightly inebriated does change his demeanor, and I witnessed how gentlemen who can engage friends allow body language to be shared between strangers. This reminded me that superficial judgments often don't capture character or heart. He seemed strict but openly friendly, a realization that left quite the impression on me. 

What the Restaurant Waiter Saw of Song Jin-woo 

Kim X-dong 

Mr. Song Jin-woo, the president of Dong-A Ilbo! He visits our restaurant often, sometimes in a large group and other times with a few close friends.  

He commands a dignified presence and stands out as a respected figure in society. In any gathering, he conducts himself without any blemish, yet on occasions when he may take too much, he speaks rather loudly. 

Once during a gathering of friends, later in the evening, we heard muffled sobbing from within his room, and initially, I was startled, wondering whether to jump in to check or not as I was concerned about his feelings. 

Mr. Song, appearing somewhat drunk, seemed to express sorrow over a betrayal by someone he had cared for deeply. "This child, whom I thought I had raised, betrayed me..." he lamented. My anxieties heightened since I feared other patrons may have overheard it; how embarrassing it would be if they recognized Mr. Song as he poured his heart out.

Perhaps this was due to the fact his trusted person had deeply wounded him, causing such emotions to flare up, oblivious to another's presence. 

What the Special Assistant Saw of Jo Man-sik 

X-dong 

Mr. Jo Man-sik, president of the Chosun Ilbo! I, as a new hire, was keen to meet him. Upon first impressions, he seemed formidable, wearing a fine suit with round glasses and shiny shoes while also bringing a cane embellished with ivory. 

Yet on the day I began working, upon encountering him, I was surprised to find a disheveled gentleman looking scruffy. I initially mistook him for a guest visiting the office. 

When I stepped into the President’s room, I found a rough-looking fellow, leading me to hesitate upon speaking. 

“What brings you here?” He asked. 

Having realized this was Mr. Jo Man-sik, I greeted him. 

His hair looked rumpled, and he had not shaved; he donned a modest Korean-style coat that reached his knees. Even more surprising was his donning of rubber shoes in such a scenario. 

At that moment, I could not help but bow my head in admiration and respect for Mr. Jo’s remarkable simplicity. 

What the Western Restaurant Waiter Saw of OOO 

Kim O-cheong 

Among those who frequently visit our restaurant, there is Mr. OOO. 

He is especially quiet, refraining from smoking or indulging in drinks, yet dresses elegantly with tailored suits. At times he comes with Mr. OO, although it’s rare. 

For us, whatever guests are present, we simply hope to sell meals well, regardless of the company. 

However, nowadays, it seems he hasn’t been visiting the restaurant. Perhaps he has established a new place of his own because, for months now, after he came with Mr. OO, he hasn’t returned, making our owner feel distressed, as it seems we must have wronged him in our duties. However, we have treated everyone with care, so certainly that isn’t the case. 

I wish Mr. OOO would return soon, so we could once again demonstrate ourselves to the owner.

**Summary**

This article presents a series of observational vignettes reflecting the personalities and behaviors of notable figures in 1930s Korean society from the perspectives of various individuals who encounter them in settings such as public transport, taverns, bathhouses, and restaurants. The perspectives reveal differing attitudes towards social status, vulnerability when intoxicated, and how people perceive one another across class divides during the colonial period. Historians may find interest in how public and private behaviors illuminated social norms and the intersections of individual identity within the larger narrative of colonial Korea.
