End of Pencil  
This issue has been edited especially with the efforts of two individuals, ensuring the content is substantial, but unfortunately, we could not adhere strictly to the schedule. We hope to strive harder and do not fail to seek your support and affection.  

Last month's issue received great acclaim and was sold out. Consequently, we have increased the number of copies for this issue. As the number of readers continues to grow, we trust that we will achieve more than double the excellent results within this year.  

In this issue, we have a special feature article that includes a meeting with Charles Tchangja (차천자) and Bernard Shaw (뻐너드 쇼-옹), a discussion on Korean aristocrats (조선고관), the colonies of beggars (걸인식민지), and the lamentations of failed entrance exam candidates (입학낙제생의 넉두리), all presented with genuine wit and satire, sure to become the talk of the town.  

Next month's issue will feature even more unique articles, so please spread the word to your acquaintances and place your orders in advance. Our magazine often sells out with each issue, so late orders may miss the opportunity to purchase. <62>  

**Summary**  

This editorial note emphasizes the successful reception of the previous issue and the magazine's commitment to quality content, highlighting upcoming features that will resonate with readers regarding societal issues and literary figures. The mention of satire aimed at societal norms and the struggles of colonized individuals reflects the tensions of Korean colonial history, showcasing the role of literature as a medium for criticism and commentary during this period. Historians may find the reference to Bernard Shaw particularly intriguing given his global influence and how his ideas were perceived in the context of colonial Korea.

**Named Entities**  

차천자	Charles Tchangja  
뻐너드 쇼-옹	Bernard Shaw  

**Glossary**  

몽당鉛筆	End of Pencil	A term referring to a pencil that has been used down to its stub; metaphorically implies conclusion or finishing.  
조선고관	Korean Aristocrats	The ruling elite during the Joseon Dynasty; reflects societal hierarchy and class.  
걸인식민지	Colonies of Beggars	Refers to the social issue of homelessness and poverty in colonized areas.  
입학낙제생의 넉두리	Lamentations of Failed Entrance Exam Candidates	Refers to the struggles and frustrations faced by students who failed entrance exams, highlighting educational pressures.

기사형태	사고·편집후기

몽당鉛筆
이번 호는 특히 두 사람의 힘으로 편즙되야 내용의 충실은 되엿스나 부득이 정긔에 내여 놋치 못하엿습니다. 압흐로는 좀더 분발할 것을 자긔하는 동시에 여러분의 배전성원과 애호를 바래마지 안습니다.
지난달 호도 대호평 중에 절판이 되엿습니다. 그리하야 이번 호는 붓쩍 수호를 늘려 박엇습니다. 이럿케 애독자가 늘어가면 금년 중으로 배가(倍加) 이상의 조흔 성적을 낫타내일 것을 밋고 무한 깃버하는 바입니다.
이번 호에는 특집긔사가 만흔 중 특히 차천자와 뻐너드 쇼-옹 회견긔, 조선고관 성석긔, 걸인식민지, 입학낙제생의 넉두리 등을 정말 긔발한 흥미와 풍자를 아울너 가진 조흔 독물로 단연 만인의 이야기 거리가 되리라고 밋습니다.
오는 5월호는 이보다도 더 독특한 긔사의 특별 독물노 짜여질 것이니 지금부터 아는 이에게 널니 선전해주시고 미리 주문해 주십시요. 본지는 호마다 절판을 거듭하니까 주문이 느지면 못사실 염려가 잇기 때문입니다.＜62＞
