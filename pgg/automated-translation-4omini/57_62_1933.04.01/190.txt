기사제목	連作漫談, 女子의 一生(第二回) 修學編 (Serial Talk, A Woman's Life (Second Part) Education Edition)  
필자	孤帆  
기사형태	소설 (Novel)  

A girl named Mu-dun was, after all, the daughter of a person. Each time the new year came around, she eagerly awaited the celebration of her birthday alongside others. At the age of five, she entered kindergarten, at eight she began primary school, and at sixteen she started high school. If one were to think that being a first-year high school student at sixteen was unusual, that might be because she had taken several breaks from school due to illness. As for what illness caused her breaks, no one knows. Moreover, it is said that even a pure sixteen-year-old girl has her burdens. It seems that there were unspeakable circumstances and unspeakable worries. 

During her time in school, she spent her writing class penning love letters, drew her teacher’s face in art class, read romantic novels during math, asked her teacher about the days gone by in Korean class, and during gym class, she would tremble at the thought of a boy having touched her hair while she cuddled by the heater with her best friend. Her high school days were exciting and grand in this manner. She would frantically wear her hair in different styles, toss it around, and during break time, go to the blackboard and stand like a model from a modern shop window while getting punished, all the while smiling cheerfully. In her glimmering eyes, one could hear a cheerful song heralding the arrival of spring. Whenever the teacher cast a stern glance her way, she would shyly lower her head, and her lips, slightly parted, seemed to glow like the poppies blooming in the heat of summer: 

"How adorable!" 

Even the teacher, who was covered in chalk dust from head to toe, felt an unexpected warm breeze of spring in her heart. 

"Such a lovable rascal!" 

This was the last cry of the S High School teachers regarding Mu-dun.

Be it school or lunch, playing in the gymyard or even during exam times, she was a lively and cheeky girl who would always be bubbling with energy! With the gentle breeze of spring awakening, Mu-dun at the age of seventeen finally returned home after watching a romantic tragedy at the theater during March.

"Sister! I miss you. Every time I see you, my heart races. Please play with me. If you just love me, I wouldn't even mind being a fool." 

A letter from Eun-bong, who was one grade above Mu-dun but only one year older, had unexpectedly found its way to Mu-dun’s desk. Eun-bong had perhaps hidden it away. Eun-bong, who used to play basketball with her, was a slender, almond-eyed beauty with a delightful face. That night, Mu-dun nestled herself under her mother's blanket and thought of Eun-bong all night long. 

The next day, Mu-dun quickly wrote a reply and placed it partway into Eun-bong’s textbook so it would be easily seen.

"Eun-bong! Thank you. I will come to the dormitory tonight. At 6 o'clock! Mu-dun"

How long it seemed until it was 6 o'clock! Eun-bong deliberately paid her friends to go out and waited for Mu-dun. Mu-dun, donning perfume, dressed up again, carefully wiped her face with a towel so only her nostrils would be visible, and put on socks to make her feet look a little smaller, then entered Eun-bong’s room.

"Eun-bong!" 

"Mu-dun!" 

It was like a ridiculous melodrama! A hug that only lovers could share. 

Eun-bong had lovingly brought sweets, and together they fumbled and stumbled over their words until even after the fire was out, their faces remained flushed. 

"Let’s dress the same and comb our hair the same way. 

"Let’s go out all night together. 

"Let’s always talk about what we did during the day. 

"If possible, let’s sleep together.” 

As their yearning and affection grew day by day, another major problem arose for Mu-dun in the first year: a girl named Sun-hee had sent a blood letter requesting love. However, there was an even bigger dilemma. On the way back from a baseball game at Kyungsung Stadium, Mu-dun took a seat on the train to leave, when she discovered a letter in her jacket pocket that had slipped through her fingers, which was a love letter sent by a student from another school.

"That girl is truly troublesome." 

Jealousy is a privilege given only to those in love. Eun-bong’s anxiety reached its peak.

"That girl talks nonsense; does she even know who I am?" 

"Then let her see for herself." 

"Let’s tear it apart." 

In this way, the two read the letter together while strolling back from the train to the teacher's college near the stadium. After reading the letter, Eun-bong indeed tore it up. However, in Eun-bong's heart lingered the undeniable shadow of uncertainty that Mu-dun might love only her.

"Sister! If you have the strength of men and cling to their hot embrace, will you abandon this sister?" 

This was the letter Eun-bong wrote daily to Mu-dun.

"Why would you think I've ever done that?" 

Exasperated, Mu-dun playfully scolded Eun-bong while indulging in a kiss that was full of comfort. During this time, the trouble from the blood letter incident with Sun-hee continued, causing Eun-bong’s mind and body to grow weak, resembling leftover stir-fried noodles being neglected. 

The letters and blood letter incidents led to the overarching presence of a teacher: K-nyeo, a sports teacher who had just returned from Tokyo. She started paying special attention to Mu-dun, and as time passed, she would come to the dormitory daily together for baths and simply dive into warm blankets. 

"Teacher!" 

But when K-nyeo would scold her, 

"At school, I’m your teacher, but at home, just call me older sister!" 

she would snap. 

As those remarks burned with confidence, Mu-dun felt pride and excitement flood her mind, and there was no one in the world happier and cuter than her. 

Having been brushed aside by K-nyeo, Eun-bong fell sick from exhaustion and had to stay in the dormitory. Immediately after, even before her sickness healed, a case of scarlet fever emerged, and the connection between the dormitory and the classroom was completely severed. 

Yet, Eun-bong continued sending Mu-dun treats and sweets, and every once in a while, Mu-dun would bravely pass through the barrier of ‘forbidden’ to visit and console Eun-bong. 

"Eun-bong!" 

The voice of K-nyeo echoed from the window. 

Mu-dun blushed and dashed out to the sports ground. 

"Why aren't you listening to the teacher?" 

K-nyeo exhibited fear for the first time in her life, yet tears lingered in her eyes. When she saw Mu-dun, she shouted with rage.  

**Summary**

This narrative explores the life of Mu-dun, a high school girl navigating her emotional and social journey, including friendships, romantic interests, and the societal expectations placed upon her. The letter from Eun-bong introduces a poignant element of same-sex affection and the complexities of young love during a time when such relationships were often stigmatized. Historians might find this portrayal significant for understanding the gender dynamics and cultural shifts occurring in Korea during the Japanese colonial period, reflecting the struggles and expressions of identity amidst societal constraints.
