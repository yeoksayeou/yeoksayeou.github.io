Specimen  

"Why did that person leave no excellent teeth behind after pulling them all out?"  
"What? The reason is simple; it was to save on his skirt expenses!"

Silence is golden!  
The conductor of the orchestra hired a violinist without knowing he was a mute.  
Conductor - "Harry, how about it? If you officially become a member of our orchestra, I will pay you 40 won a week."  
However, "Harry" was dissatisfied with 40 won and was trying to turn him down while mumbling. Before he could finish, the conductor interrupted.  
Conductor - "Then how about 60 won?"  
At that moment, "Harry" finally spoke in clear words, and the conductor continued speaking.  
Conductor - "Well then, let’s decide on 80 won before this goes too far."  
"Harry" - "Th-th-thank you!"  
Conductor - "Hey, who are you...?"

The Dream of Life?  
A - "Do you believe in 'dreams'?"  
B - "Hmph, I used to think so, but now, just thinking about it makes me nauseous!"  
A - "Is that so?"  
B - "Well, it’s already been 5 years since I got married!"＜20＞  

**Summary**

This article contains humorous snippets and dialogues that touch upon themes of social status, economic hardship, and the notion of dreams within the confines of married life during the 1930s in Korea. The playful exchange about the worth of a mute musician and the cynical look at dreams reflect a societal critique of the struggles faced by individuals under colonial rule. Historians might find the portrayal of these personal dilemmas interesting in understanding how colonial conditions influenced everyday life and personal relationships in Korea.
