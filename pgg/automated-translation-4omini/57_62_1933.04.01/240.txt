기사형태	잡저 (Essay)  

I have penned a letter three times to the Hollywood actress Mary Astor, hoping to receive a signed photo in return. After two months since my first letter, I was unexpectedly sent a nice reply from the U.S. Upon reading it, I was certain it came from Mary Astor, and the tone was so friendly that it made me want to dance. Since I was about to respond, I thought, isn't it better to wait? Thus, I wrote again. This time I sent her a more profound and colored letter, typed with the help of a friend, along with a photo of myself. Eventually, a response arrived, and it was even more flattering. The unknown lover wrote that my features were more beautiful than Lamon Bavona, and concluded with a message saying she would cherish a nice postcard from Joseon (Korea) in her jewelry box, which was a delightful note to read. 

Thus began our earnest correspondence, now exchanging letters every month or so with growing affection between us. In our letters, we made sure to include sweet phrases such as “To my unknown lover” and “To my love living in America.” During Christmas, we even exchanged gifts. I sent her a couple of traditional Korean flags and a pair of dancing shoes I designed, which Mary had never seen before. They were trendy and praised in Hollywood, but due to a lack of material, they had to be crafted by hand. 

Moreover, she insisted we must meet in Hawaii next autumn, either so she can visit Joseon together with me or I can go to the U.S. with her. Oh, but is it truly in my fate? I don’t even have the money for train fare to the station, let alone to go to Hawaii. 

**Summary**

This essay narrates the amusing and heartfelt correspondence between the author and the actress Mary Astor, illustrating the distance and cultural exchanges through letters during a time of limited travel. The communication is filled with affection despite the challenges posed by geography and finances. From a historical perspective, this correspondence can reflect how people engaged in personal connections across continents during the colonial period, showcasing the aspirations and dreams of individuals in a time of social and political tumult in Korea. The romantic idealism contrasts with the harsh realities of life under colonial rule, highlighting themes of longing and interconnectedness.
