						蔡萬植  
This article can be seen as a sequel to "A Conversation on a Sunday," which could not be published due to certain circumstances. (Author)  

One  
On a certain day in May—  
P, who was released from Seodaemun Prison in the morning, left with M after saying goodbye to comrades with whom he had lunch.  

Returning to society after spending more than three years—although he was sentenced to five or seven years, his sentence was reduced by three years—there was nothing extraordinary about P's feelings, unlike the first time he was released.  

The city of Gyeongseong had notably transformed during his long time in confinement, and this change was striking to his eyes.  

Brick houses tiled with various colors, apartments, electric lights, illuminated signs, show windows, aerial passenger transport airplanes, buses, the long-bodied 'poki-dancha' that looked like it was fluttering in the wind, the number of automobiles that had significantly increased, and various sirens… all of these things aimed to show a certain aesthetic beauty of a large urban environment and were rather glamorous.  

P looked around like a country boy as he walked towards Dongnip.  

Seeing P looking around so cluelessly, M chuckled and said, "You've lived in another world for four years and now you’ve become quite the country bumpkin, huh?"  

P laughed in response.  

"Haha, it's not that! You may not have noticed it as you lived in the hustle and bustle, but to me, it seems like Seoul is well on its way to modernization."  

"Modernization, you say…?"  

"Yes, indeed. Although it's modern, that’s necessary for Joseon."  

"It's necessary, just like an early-maturing child has to have the sexual function at three years old…"  

"Haha, that’s a bit extreme… Rather, like how when the wind blows over the sea, even the shores will receive some waves, that's necessary too, right? That sounds natural."  

As the two of them strolled side by side, they reached T'apgol Park.  

"Shall we go in since it has been a long time?"  

"Sure, is it still just a thousand won for the entrance?"  

"When would it be different? But you seem too tired, don’t you think?"  

"Tired? I’m not tired; I just feel a sense of ennui from not working." They entered the park.  

On the branches heavy with flowers, the light green leaves fluttered gently, and the white poplar trees that should be buoyant with spring were poised to burst into bloom.  

The evergreen trees standing there, dressed in their gloomy winter clothes like former prisoners, began to appear a little bright.  

In various corners of the park, friends who seemed to be talking quietly, as if money were flowing out from their pockets, and models who looked just like sculptures of 'thinkers' rather than 'lazy people' seemed to slowly pick at the lice from their armpits.  

P and M went to the greenhouse on the left, finally finding a bench to sit on and smoked a cigarette side by side.  

During the three years in prison, P had persistently thought about it, and from the very morning he stepped out of prison, he desperately wanted to know about K—his wife.  

While eating lunch with comrades or at the prison gate, many new and curious pieces of information had piqued his interest during those past years, but regarding K, no one had opened their mouth. It felt as though they had silently agreed not to do so. P himself had hesitated to ask.  

In fact, it wasn't that he couldn't ask, but K not having come even to the prison entrance had given him a premonition that made him reluctant to inquire.  

As they walked toward Dongnip, almost unconsciously veering to the east, it was because the house where he had lived with K was right next to Jongmyo.  

Of course, P suspected that K was not living there alone.  

"Don't wait for me and honestly…" P had advised K on the last day before his imprisonment, and after an entire year of trial proceedings, when he had just been incarcerated, he could guess from a letter he received six months before his release how K had been doing during that time.  

That letter was as follows:  
— One year after P's incarceration—  
"… My dear! I am still young. The unfavorable aspects of your character that you used to mention about me are now coming to light. I will live and die with a heart that only thinks of you, but at this moment, death is too frightening. On the other hand, the temptations of life are too great.  

My love! What should I do? I am still young. But you are here. What should I do?  

I know your character well. I understand that your promise when you left was neither false nor mere words. But just as you are this way, my worries are growing.  

The time to make a final decision is drawing near.  
Oh! What should I do?..."

— Six months before P's release—  
"… I trust you will not be angry with me or resent me. Therefore, my troubled heart is not due to that.  

I took the wrong path. The more painful the path I took, the more I miss those days.  

Now, P, you are going to be someone else forever, and this K will no longer be P’s K, thinking of that makes me want to die.  

I believe this will be our last letter. Furthermore, I cannot even think about seeing you again. How will I ever meet your gaze again…? If you have been waiting, it must already be a year—didn't your term end in relief? How wonderful that must be—I wish I could see your promising face, but because I have been living this unsatisfactory life…"

Regarding these two letters, of course P tried to respond with bright and cheerful words, suppressing the emotions surging in him.  

However, P regretted somewhat after being released, feeling that perhaps the words he had thrown away, "Don’t wait for me," which he had advised K on the last day, would not truly align with K’s gentle and perhaps simple-minded character, who might still be waiting for him.  

Thus far, with that lingering attachment, on the other hand, P worried that K had suffered terribly afterward—he could not help but recall the female troubles during the time he was troubled, comparing her to the infamous XX.  

And as they silently walked, P expected M to eventually give him some news about K. However, M deliberately avoided that topic and engaged in other conversations instead.  

Unable to endure any longer, P started the conversation himself.  

"Hey there, you must answer my question without dodging."  

M, seemingly realizing something, asked, "Why ask something like that all of a sudden?" while eyeing P.  

"It’s not sudden… About K."  

"Why bother asking about such a filthy woman?" M answered coldly.  

P’s heart sank. But since it was a rightful procedure, he asked calmly again.  

"What about her?"  

"Forget that kind of slut…"  

"Oh dear… You shouldn't be angry all alone. Do you think I don’t know my own feelings?"  

M seemed offended and answered with irritation while puffing on his cigarette.  

"What's there to be angry about!? After you were gone for a year, she said she'd wait for you but lo and behold, the first signs of spring arrived, and she got all tangled with S, the supposed 'poet,' even going around with XXX."  

While listening to M's words, P felt a pang of jealousy—the thought that K was giving the warmth and affection she had once given him to S ignited a fire of jealousy within him.  

However, on the other hand, P tried to dismiss it and gather his thoughts.  

"Haha, buddy, do you really think you should be angry about just that?"  

M looked aghast and stared at P in disbelief.  

"Why stare at me? Are you saying I can laugh happily without knowing the situation? Well, let me say this… You know that K, being simply that way and a very emotional woman, with her desire for life being very strong, how could she spend all her youth waiting for you? Isn't that a necessary demand of human nature?"  

Looking back at M, P became a bit flustered.  

"Then are you disregarding the ties of marriage and the duty of fidelity we share?"  

"Of course not… I value them greatly. But I also believe that duty and fidelity have the flexibility of circumstances and should never be absolute."  

"Listen to this. If K merely suppresses her desire and lives a disheveled life filled with unhealthy sexual needs while pretending to keep her emotions clean compared to other women, how much right does she have to call herself more decent?"  

"Do you understand what I’m saying? Isn't that right? Haha."  

"I don’t see it that way… If I were that way, I would kick that kind of woman out."  

"Exactly. If it were a man, you wouldn't be so angry, would you?"  

M had no words left.  

Seeing this, P laughed again.  

"Listen, buddy, I wonder if you haven't entirely come to grips with your thoughts yet. However, one thing I’m concerned about is—what K is doing doesn’t seem to be going well."  

M’s expression turned skeptical seeing P act so humanely.  

"Hmph, it looks like you’ve become a humanitarian after coming out of prison."  

"Humanitarian? Haha… I might’ve sounded like that to you, but I’m not trying to fabricate any reality where I sacrifice myself to make 'everyone happy.' I’m just critiquing what is reality within broader theoretical reflections. Isn't that so?"  

"And now I just want to maximize the target…"  

"What an absurd argument…"  

"Haha, you foolish fellow…"  

M also found it difficult to resist laughing.  

Standing up from the bench, P stretched and said, "Let’s go."  

M remained seated and asked, "Where are you going? Where are you supposed to wait?" with a playful smile.  

"Haha, just find a boarding house."  

"What a silly fool… Don’t tell me you can't even get a wife?"  

"Instead, arrange for me a match."  

"Another catch?"  

"Haha."  

"Haha."  

The two laughed heartily.  

As they stood up and walked away, M suddenly turned serious and asked P, "What will you do now?"  

"What?"  

"The metabolism thing…"  

"I'll work."  

"How?"  

"What are you talking about? After all, I'm okay with just getting by…"  

"Still, you've gotta eat, right?"  

"I'll manage."  

"How?"  

"I’ll wander around as a daily laborer, looking to see if a talking mouth can give me a break."  

"And what about your morals?"  

"I'll go to a boarding house and eat on credit."  

"Let’s go to my place."  

"Is there even a room?"  

"Yes."  

"Let’s go."  

As they took to the back door of the park to head to M's house, P's mind was filled with the past he shared with K like a panorama.  

"People must have cared and loved..."  

With that, P let out a light sigh and muttered to himself.  

Two  
Seven months passed.  

P, the driver of vehicle 104 for the Gyeongseong Electric Company, set off from Dongdaemun towards Jongno, confidently accelerating his 'poki' truck on the morning he had completed his apprenticeship.  

1929. 12. 8.  

**Summary**  

This story reflects the struggles of P, who has just been released from Seodaemun Prison after years of confinement, and his thoughts about his wife K. As he walks through the transformed city, P grapples with feelings of jealousy and regret related to K's life during his imprisonment, ultimately revealing the emotional turmoil faced by individuals in a changing colonial society. This narrative highlights the intersection of personal relationships and societal transformations under colonial rule, with potential insights into how attachments and expectations shifted amid the larger context of oppression and modernization in Korea during this period.

5월달 어느 날―
아침에 서대문 형무소에서 출옥한 P는 가티 모혀 점심을 먹든 동지들을 작별하고 M과 가티 종로 네거리로 나섯다.
발서 세 번채나 감옥사리를 하고 나온 P에게는 처음 때와 달너 벌노히 『출옥한 때의 특이한 감상』 가튼 것은 쳠례하지 아니하엿다.
다만 이번이 사 년―칠 년이엿스나 삼 년은 감형이 되얏다.―사 년이라는 비교뎍 긴 동안이엿기 때문에 그 동안 변천된 경성의 면모가 현저하게 그의 눈에 띄엿다.
타일 입힌 여러 칭 벽돌집, 떼파-트멘트, 삘딍, 일미네-슌, 쇼-윈도우, 그러고 려객수송 비행긔, 뻐스, 허리가 늘ㅅ신하게 호마 가티 날새여 보이는 뽀키- 뎐차, 수가 벗적 늘고 최하가 시보레-로 된 자동차, 꽤 자조 들니는 각가지의 사이렌... ...의 모든 것이 제법 규모가 큰 도회미(美)와 분잠한 긔게미(美)를 띄우려는 긔색이 보엿다.
P는 맛치 시골사람처럼 두리번두리번 하면서 발ㅅ길은 제절노 동편으로 행하엿다.
M은 P가 그러케 두리번거리는 것을 보고
『한 사년 동안 딴 세안에 가 살고 오더니 아주 시골사람이 되엿네 그려?』하고 우섯다.
P도 딸어 우섯다.
『허허. 그런게 아닐세. 자네는 그 공긔 속에서 살엇스닛가 분명허게 의식을 못햇는지 몰으겟네만 내가 보기에는 서울도 인제는 꽤 모던화를 해 가는 게 선뜻 눈에 띄우네.』
『긔형뎍 모던화 말이지... ...?』
『응 그래. 긔형뎍이야... ... 그러치만 긔형뎍은 긔형뎍이면서 그것이 조선으로써는 필연이겟지.』
『조발성치보(早發性痴*)의 세 살 먹은 어린것이 성뎍긔능(性的機能)을＜164＞ 가지는 것이 필연은 필연인 것처럼... ...』
『허허허허. 넘우 지독헌 걸... ... . 그것보다는 바다에 바람이 불어 큰 물결이 일어나면 해변 야튼 데도 딸어 물결이 일어난다구 허는 것이 필연인 것처럼...이라는 게 졈쟌허네.』
두 사람은 억개를 나란히 하고 걸어오는 사이에 탑골공원 압가지 일으럿다.
『오랫만이니 들어가 보랴나?』
『들어가 보지... ... 지금두 천냥만냥 판인가?』
『언제라구 업겟나... ... 그러치만 자네 넘우 피곤허지 안은가?』
『피곤? 피곤헌 게 아니라 일을 안허닛가 권태가 생기네.』 두 사람은 공원 안으로 들어섯다.
꼿치 지고 난 나무가지에는 엷푸른 새입들이 납폿납폿 소사나고 철 일은 백양은 발서 록음이 욱어지려고 한다.
겨우내 봄내 음산한 옷을 입은 죄수 가티 꼴사납게 서 잇든 상록수들도 조곰은 밝은 빗치 떠돌아 보인다.
이 구석 저 구석 안즐 만한 곳으로 뻰취에 쟌듸 우에는 금시 돈뎀이가 쏘다저 나오는 것 가티 소곤소곤 천냥만냥을 하는 친구들과 로단이 보앗스면 『생각하는 사람』 대신 『게을은 사람』이라는 조각을 색엿슬 모델ㅅ감들이 방금 겨드랭이 속에서 이(虱)라도 더듬어내일 듯이 느리차분하게 안저 햇벗을 쪼이고 잇다.
P와 M은 서편 온실 엽흐로 가서 겨우 뻰취 하나를 차져 나란히 안저 담배를 피어 물엇다.
사 년 동안 옥중에서 연연히 생각을 하엿고 이날 아침 옥문을 나서면서부터도 P의 마음에 간절이 소식을 알고 십흔 것은 K―그의 안해엇섯다.
감옥 압헤서 또는 동지들과 점심을 먹으면서 과거 사년ㅅ동안 일어난 새로운 소식 궁금한 소식은 만히 듯기도 하고 뭇기도 하엿스나 K에 관하여서는 누구 하나 입을 열지도 아니하고 맛치 무슨 약속이나 한 것처럼―또 참아 P 자신도 물어보려고 하지 못하엿다.
사실은 물어보지 못할 것도 아니엿스나 K가 자긔를 마지러 옥문 압까지 오지 아니한 것으로 그는 예감한 바가 잇서 뭇기를 주저하엿든 것이다.
그려나 종로로 나서서 P가 동편으로 거의 무식 중에 발ㅅ길을 옴겨노흔 것도 전날 K와 가티 살음사리를 하든 집이 종묘 압헤 잇섯든 때문이다.
물론 P는 K가 그 집에 홀노 남어 잇지 아니한 것을 짐작하고 잇섯다.
『―나를 기다리고 잇지 말고 진직히... ...』라고 P는 사년 전 마즈막 날에 K에게 부탁하여 둔 것과 또 그가 예심에서 공판에서 일년이나 끌녀 다니다가 형의 언도를 밧고 입감하든 때 바로 바든 K의 편지와 출옥하기 륙개월 전에 바든 편지로써 K가 어터케 되엿슬 것을 골ㅅ자만은 짐작을 하고 잇섯다.＜165＞
그 편지는 이러한 것이엿섯다.
―P의 입감 후 일 년만엣것―
『... ...여보! 나는 청춘임니다. 그러고 당신이 전에 노상 말하든 모든 불리한 성ㅅ격의 소유자인 것이 날노 달노 정톄가 나타남니다. 당신을 생각하는 한 마음만 가태서는 세상을 잇고 죽기라도 하겟슴니다만은 나한테 지금의 죽엄은 넘우도 무서운 것임니다. 그 반면으로 생의 유혹은 넘우도 큼니다.
여보! 어터케 할가요? 나는 청춘임니다. 그러나 당신이 잇슴니다. 어터케 하람닛가?
당신의 성ㅅ격도 잘 알고 잇슴니다. 가실 제 하신 부탁이 결코 거짓이거나 입에 부튼 말이 아닌 것도 잘 알고 잇슴니다. 그러나 당신이 그런한 만큼 나의 고민은 더함니다.
결뎡해야 할 시긔는 박두하여 옴니다.
아! 어터케 하람닛가?... ...』
―P의 출옥하기 륙개월 전 편지―
『... ...당신이 노여워하거나 저를 원망하거나는 아니하실 줄 알고 잇슴니다. 그럼으로 나의 마음은 그러한 것이 괴로운 것이 아님니다.
나는 길을 잘못 밟엇슴니다. 잘못 밟은 길이 괴로울수록 녯날이 더욱 그립슴니다.
인제는 P가 영영 남의 P가 되고 이 K가 P의 K가 아닌 것을 생각하면 죽도록 설슴니다.
이것이 우리 사이의 마지막 편지일 것 갓슴니다. 더구나 뵈우려고는 생각도 못함니다. 어터케 당신을 치어다볼 긔운이 날 리가 잇서야지요... ... 그 동안 기다리고 잇섯스면 압흐로 일 년만 잇스면―감형되덧다지요? 퍽도 반갑슴니다―당신을 번ㅅ듯한 얼굴노 맛날 수가 잇섯슬 터인데 이 탐탁치 아니한 생활을 엇느라고!... ...』
이 두 장 편지에 대하여 물론 P는 소사올으는 감정을 억제하고 밝고 쾌활한 말노써 답장을 하여 주엇다.
