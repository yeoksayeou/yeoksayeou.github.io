Ancient and Modern Techniques for Longevity  
The Reason for Youth  

The poet Longfellow was described as possessing an ageless head of hair that shone brightly and a complexion that was always radiant, with vitality that even young people could not surpass. When asked about the reason for this, he responded as follows:  
“Look at the old tree. Although the Amori tree (アモリ) is old, it has never had an aged appearance. This is because, despite being old, it still grows a little each year. I am indeed aging, but I do not neglect my growth.”

Health Practices from Germany  

1. Live in clean air.  
2. Do not neglect exercise.  
3. Eat in moderation.  
4. Keep the skin clean.  
5. Dress appropriately.  
6. Work in an orderly manner.  
7. Pay attention to air and food to prevent epidemics.  
8. Take suitable rests.  
9. Keep a cheerful mind.  

Health Practices of Long-livers  

Gwidol (95 years old) did not smoke and did regular outdoor exercise, emphasizing moderation.  

Grimsolp (88 years old) did not smoke and practiced moderation in daily life.  

Nelson (82 years old) did not smoke, woke up early, and emphasized moderation, trying to avoid medication as much as possible.  

Sir Doington (92 years old) did not smoke and regularly consumed small amounts of meat and dairy.  

Professor Mayo (81 years old) did not smoke and adhered to a vegetarian diet, waking up at four in the morning.  

Dr. Curse (86 years old) occasionally smoked, drank small amounts of wine, ate small portions of meat, and consumed large amounts of milk.  

Prince (86 years old) had two meals daily, drank three glasses of whiskey, and exercised moderately.  

Debbis (82 years old) did not smoke and exercised moderately.  

Sir Haydon (86 years old) slept seven hours daily and ate small amounts of meat and wine.  

The Relationship Between Longevity and Occupation  

According to a study conducted by Mr. Capel on a population of 10,000, the following proportions of people aged 70 and above were reported:  
Pastors 42 people  
Farmers 40 people  
Senior Officials 35 people  
Merchants 35 people  
Soldiers 31 people  

Junior Officials 32 people  
Lawyers 29 people  
Artists 28 people  
Educators 27 people  
Doctors 24 people  

Health is Associated with Moderation in Eating  

Eating too much leads to:  
1. Excessive bowel movements.  
2. Increased frequency of urination.  
3. Increased sleep demands.  
4. Sluggishness of the body.  
5. Poor digestion and unpleasant feelings.  

**Summary**  

The article discusses various ancient and modern techniques for achieving longevity, emphasizing the importance of maintaining a youthful spirit and physical condition through proper health practices and moderation in diet. Notably, it highlights the lifestyles of several centenarians and correlates longevity with certain occupations, underscoring an interest in the social determinants of health during an era of colonial rule in Korea. Historians might find its insights into health practices reflective of the broader socio-cultural context of the time, where traditional beliefs and western medical practices began to intertwine.

古今東西 不老長壽法秘術
젊은 理由

시인 롱펠러-는 나히 늙어 머리털이 눈 가치 희엿스나 얼골은 언제든지 번지르 하고 혈기가 조아 젊은 사람도 능히 당하지 못할 만큼 기력이 왕성하엿다. 그 이유를 뭇는 사람에게 그는 다음과 가치 대답하엿다.
―져 늙은 나무를 보시요. 아모리 나무는 늙엇지만 늙은 꼿치 피인 적은 업지 아니한가. 그것은 아모리 늙은 나무라도 매년 얼마쯤씩은 성장을 하는 까닭이 아니요? 나도 늙기는 하엿지만 성장에 관하야 放心을 아니하기 따문이요.

獨逸 요루데-의 衛生法

一, 청결한 공기 속에서 살을 것. 二, 운동을 게을니 하지 아니할 것. 三, 음식을 절제할 것. 四, 피부를 청결하게 할 것. 五, 의복을 적당하게 입을 것. 六, 규율 잇게 일을 할 것. 七, 공기와 음식을 주의하야 流行病을 방지할 것. 八, 적당한 휴식을 할 것. 九, 마음을 유쾌하게 가질 것.

古今長壽人에 衛生法

구이돌 卿(95세) 담배를 먹지 아니하고 戶外 운동을 자주하며 절제를 主旨로 하엿다.

그림솔프 卿(88세) 담배를 먹지 아니하고 일상생활의 凡百에 절제를 主旨로 하엿다.

넬손 伯(82세) 담배를 먹지 아니하고 早起와 절제를 主旨로 하며 될 수만 잇스면 服藥을 하지 아니하엿다.

써-·도링와-터-(92세) 담배를 먹지 아니하고 소량의 肉物과 유유를 常食하엿다.

메-요 敎授(81세) 담배를 먹지 아니하고 菜食主義를 직히며 아츰 네 시에 기상하엿다.

커-스 博士(86세) 때때로는 담배를 먹고 소량의 포도주를 먹고 소량의 肉物과 다량의 우유를 먹엇다.

프린스(86세) 매일 二食 여송연 세 개 우이스키 두 숫갈을 먹고 적당한 운동을 하엿다.

데삐스(82세) 담배를 먹지 아니하고 운동을 적당하게 하엿다.

써-헤-돈(86세) 잠은 매일 일곱 시간씩 자고 소량의 肉類와 포도주를 먹엇다.
