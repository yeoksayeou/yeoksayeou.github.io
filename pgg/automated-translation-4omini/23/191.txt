To our readers: A special request  

1. If you have any matters to inquire about this company, please make sure to send a reply fee.  
(If sending a postcard, please use a return postcard; if sending a letter, please enclose three jeon in stamps.)  

2. When sending paper or order forms to this company, please write your address and name clearly. Because we receive four to five hundred pieces of paper in a day, if you write hurriedly, it may cause difficulties and time loss for our company. In severe cases, it may result in the books or replies sent being returned to sender. <106>  

**Summary**  

This editorial note addresses readers with requests for better communication when sending inquiries or orders. It emphasizes the importance of clear writing and the inclusion of postage, as the company handles a significant volume of correspondence daily. This highlights the logistics and challenges faced by publishers during the colonial period, reflecting broader issues of communication and organization under colonial rule, which might interest historians examining the infrastructure and societal practices in 1930s Korea.
