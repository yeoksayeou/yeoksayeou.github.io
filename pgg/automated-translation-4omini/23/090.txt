Acknowledgment  
In this article, we regret to inform our readers that we could not publish the much-anticipated articles including "Words We Want to Say to the Public" under the subtitle, the discussions of Song Jinwoo, An Jaehong, Kim Yeonsu's "Ten Years of the Korean Commercial Sector," Kim Byeongro's "Ten Years of the Legal Profession," Kim Gyeongjae's "Intellectual Trends in Korea over the Past Ten Years," and Rōru's "The Life History of the Leader of All India Nationalism, Kanduk." Furthermore, revisiting last month's publication, the "Three Newspaper Company Presidents Interviewed by a Disguised Female Reporter" could not be published due to unavoidable circumstances. We apologize for this.＜68＞  

**Summary**  

This article serves as an acknowledgment, informing readers about the inability to publish several expected articles due to unforeseen circumstances. Among the expected pieces were discussions by notable figures and reflections on Korean intellectual and commercial developments during the colonial period. Historians may find this acknowledgment interesting as it reflects the limitations and challenges faced by media outlets in colonial Korea, particularly in addressing significant societal topics.
